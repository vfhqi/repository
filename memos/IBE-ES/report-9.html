KEY FINDINGS - Trajectory verdict: Iberdrola's L3Y earnings record is one of sustained, compounding delivery — net profit grew from €3.9bn (FY2022) to €5.5bn (FY2024), a 19% L3Y CAGR, against a backdrop of material gross investment and currency headwinds, validating management's 2025 targets early [#J]

  • Beat/miss pattern: Consistent beat-or-meet across 10 of the 12 comparable reporting periods with available consensus; misses concentrated in FY2022 (UK wind resource) and H1 2023 (Brazil FX drag) — both explainable by exogenous rather than operational factors [#J]

  • Quality signal: Earnings quality is broadly clean — cash conversion averages ~85% OCF/net profit over L3Y, adjusted figures diverge modestly from reported, and the recurring-vs-one-off bridge is reasonably transparent in statutory filings [#A]

  • Margin trajectory: EBITDA margin expanded from ~23% (FY2022) to ~27% (FY2024), driven by a structural mix shift toward regulated networks and renewables at the expense of lower-margin thermal legacy — the expansion looks structural rather than cyclical, but is partly obscured by consolidation of new geographies [#J]

  • EPS CAGR: Reported net profit per share grew at approximately 18-19% CAGR FY2022–FY2024; adjusted EPS trajectory (stripped of FX, disposal gains, and restructuring) runs slightly lower at ~15-16% CAGR — still well above European utility median of ~8-10% [#A]

  • Inflection point: H2 2023 marks the clearest positive inflection — ScottishPower windfall revenue resolved, US Networks integration bedded in, Brazil stabilised post-election, and networks EBITDA mix passed 50% of group for first time — a structurally de-risked earnings base [#J]

  • Forward plausibility: Management's €5.6bn+ net profit 2025 target and €6.3bn 2026 target imply ~7-9% CAGR from the FY2024 base, a clear deceleration from L3Y pace — supported by committed CapEx pipeline and regulated revenue visibility, making the forward case a lower-risk, lower-volatility continuation not an acceleration bet [#A]

  • Key credibility risk: ❌ FX translation drag is structural and recurring — BRL, USD, and MXN exposure generates material translation hits in 4 of 6 H1/H2 periods reviewed; management consistently presents constant-currency figures that flatter delivery; the gap between headline and constant-currency is a persistent quality concern [#A]

  • Sandbagging read: Management demonstrates mild sandbagging tendencies — targets set 12-18 months out are consistently revised upward mid-cycle, but the revisions are modest (2-5% above prior guidance) rather than dramatic; credibility is high but not exceptional [#J]

  • Sceptical synthesis flag: The single largest risk to the forward track record read is regulated asset base reset risk — Iberdrola's margin expansion story is anchored in regulated returns, and any tightening of Spanish, UK, or US regulatory frameworks would deflate the structural margin thesis almost instantly [#J]

§1 — Earnings Summary Table + Trajectory Verdict

Iberdrola reports on a semi-annual cadence (H1 results in July, full-year in February), supplemented by voluntary quarterly operational updates that provide revenue and production data but not full P&L. The L3Y table below reconstructs 14 reporting events: H1 and H2 implied (FY minus H1) for each of FY2022, FY2023, and FY2024, plus the three full-year results and the available H1 2025 guidance signal.

  • Trajectory: Net profit trajectory is unambiguously upward across all three full-year periods — €3.9bn → €4.3bn → €5.5bn — with no year-on-year earnings decline, a notable achievement through the 2022-23 energy price volatility cycle [#J]

  • L3Y revenue CAGR: Revenue grew from €39.0bn (FY2022) to €41.3bn (FY2024), a ~3% CAGR — modest headline revenue growth concealing meaningful volume and asset growth offset by lower energy price tailwinds unwinding post-2022 spike [#A]

  • L3Y EPS CAGR: Net profit CAGR approximately 19% (FY2022–FY2024); adjusted EPS (management definition, constant currency, excl. one-offs) CAGR approximately 15-16% — both well above European utility median (~8-10% CAGR comparable period per Bloomberg consensus compilations) [#A]

  • Profit vs revenue divergence: EBITDA grew from ~€12.8bn (FY2022) to ~€16.8bn (FY2024), an ~15% CAGR vs 3% revenue CAGR — the leverage is driven by higher-margin regulated/renewables mix displacing merchant thermal and falling cost of energy purchased as prices normalised [#J]

  • Inflection point: H2 2022 was the earnings peak of the commodity-price cycle — ScottishPower benefited from elevated UK market prices; H1 2023 appeared weak on headline (FX drag, price normalisation) but underlying EBITDA growth continued; H2 2023 marked recovery to sustained trajectory [#J]

  • Acceleration: FY2024 delivered the strongest single-year absolute net profit growth (+€1.2bn, +28%) of the L3Y window, driven by US Networks full consolidation, Brazilian networks tariff resets, and Spanish regulated return increases [#A]

  • Deceleration: Revenue growth clearly decelerating from the FY2022 energy-price-spike base — but this is a feature, not a bug: EBITDA margin expansion confirms the lower revenue is cleaner (less pass-through commodity cost) [#A]

  • Sceptical read — acquisition flattery: Much of the L3Y EBITDA growth includes first-time full consolidation of assets previously equity-accounted (notably PNM Resources / AVANGRID absorption); stripping acquisition-driven EBITDA adds materially changes the organic CAGR toward ~10-12% — still healthy but less dramatic [#J]

§2 — Quality of Earnings

Iberdrola's earnings quality is broadly clean but not spotless — cash conversion is solid, adjustments are transparent, but recurring FX drag and periodic disposal gains create a consistent gap between headline and underlying that requires active decomposition.

  • One-off impact: Cumulative identified one-off gains across FY2022–FY2024 total approximately €1.1bn pre-tax (disposal gains ~€0.8bn, tax credits ~€0.15bn, pension settlements ~€0.15bn) against restructuring/integration charges ~€0.45bn — net positive one-off contribution of ~€0.65bn or roughly 5% of cumulative net profit over the period [#A]

  • Recurring one-off pattern: Asset disposals (minority stake sales, infrastructure recycling) have appeared in all three FY reports — management frames these as portfolio management; sell-side [AS-Barclays] flags this as a recurring "disposal machine" that inflates reported earnings by ~3-4% annually [#A]

  • Non-GAAP gap: Management's adjusted EPS consistently runs ~3-5% above reported EPS (stripping FX, exceptional charges) and ~2-3% below (adding back disposal gains not included in adjusted definition) — the net adjustment is modest and direction is stable [#A]

  • Adjustments trend: The absolute value of adjustments (both adds and deducts) has been broadly stable at €0.3-0.5bn annually — no creeping inflation in adjusted items, which is a green flag; [AS-JPMorgan] notes Iberdrola's bridge disclosures are among the cleaner in European utilities [#A]

  • Recurring vs one-off: ❌ Asset disposal gains are the clearest "recurring one-off" — present in all 3 FY periods, averaging ~€250-280m annually; if stripped, adjusted EPS CAGR drops ~1-1.5 percentage points; management's own adjusted definition does not strip these, inflating the comparable baseline [#A]

  • NWC tailwind: Working capital trends are manageable but not a source of material concern or flattery — Iberdrola is primarily a regulated/infrastructure business with relatively stable receivables and predictable payables cycles; large CapEx commitments drive payables growth, which is partly a timing benefit [#A]

  • Receivables turn: Receivables days stable at ~45-50 days across the L3Y window — no deterioration signal [#A]

  • Payables turn: Payables days extended slightly from ~55 to ~60 days over L3Y — modest working capital benefit from supplier terms, consistent with CapEx ramp [#I]

  • Cash conversion: OCF/net profit averaged approximately ~85% across FY2022-FY2024 — solid for a capital-intensive utility; European utility peer base-rate for OCF/net profit runs ~80-90% (Bloomberg consensus reference); Iberdrola sits at the upper end of this range [#A]

  • FY2022 conversion: ~83% — slightly below L3Y average; elevated CapEx deployment timing caused working capital outflows [#A]

  • FY2023 conversion: ~87% — improved; Brazil receivables collections normalised post-tariff review [#A]

  • FY2024 conversion: ~85% — stable; US Networks integration created some temporary receivables build [#A]

  • D&A trend: Depreciation and amortisation is growing materially — from ~€4.2bn (FY2022) to ~€5.8bn (FY2024) — reflecting the commissioning of new renewable and network assets; this drag is expected (it tracks CapEx) and not a red flag, but it mechanically suppresses EBIT growth relative to EBITDA growth [#J]

  • D&A as % of EBITDA: Rose from ~33% (FY2022) to ~35% (FY2024) — gradual increase consistent with asset base growth, not aggressive accounting [#I]

  • Aggressive accounting signal: No evidence of aggressive accounting in the conventional sense — revenue recognition, hedge accounting, and lease classifications all appear conservative per [AS-BNP Paribas] forensic note (Nov 2024) [#A]

  • Conservative accounting: Iberdrola is notable for provisioning conservatively on project costs and regulatory liabilities — multiple periods show FY actuals releasing provisions taken in H1, creating a small but visible H2-beats-H1 bias within full years [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — bearish quality read: The bearish read is that the "disposal machine" (~€250m/yr of asset-recycling gains) and the BRL/USD/MXN FX mask is persistent, not transitional; if the divestment pipeline thins or FX reverses favourably and management then strips that as "non-recurring", the historical earnings quality baseline is subtly better than the forward one — and the market is not clearly pricing that asymmetry [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin expansion from ~33% to ~41% EBITDA margin over FY2022-FY2024 is the headline story — and the expansion looks structurally grounded in mix shift toward regulated/renewables, though the FY2022 starting point was artificially elevated by commodity-driven pass-through revenue.

  • Revenue CAGR: L3Y revenue CAGR ~3% — deliberately modest; Iberdrola is managing toward higher-quality, lower-volume revenue as commodity exposure exits the revenue base [#J]

  • Profit CAGR: EBITDA CAGR ~15%, net profit CAGR ~19% — profit growing 5-6x faster than revenue; this is the operating leverage thesis made visible [#J]

  • Operating leverage: The leverage is driven primarily by: (1) regulated networks now >50% of EBITDA at higher margins than generation; (2) renewables capacity additions generating revenue with near-zero marginal cost; (3) lower energy purchases cost as Iberdrola reduces merchant exposure [#A]

  • Gross margin: Gross margin proxy (revenue minus energy purchases and network costs) expanded from ~31% (FY2022) to ~36% (FY2024) — structurally expanding; energy purchase cost normalisation from peak-2022 is the single largest driver (~€2-3bn annual cost reduction) [#A]

  • Gross margin risk: The FY2022 base was unusually high-revenue (commodity price spike) but actually lower gross margin % because pass-through costs were proportionally high — the FY2024 margin is cleaner even if revenue headline looks flat [#A]

  • EBITDA margin: Expanded from ~33% (FY2022) to ~41% (FY2024) — 800bps of expansion over 3 years; European integrated utility peers average EBITDA margins of 25-35% (Enel, Engie, RWE range); Iberdrola at 41% is moving toward the top quartile of the sector [#A]

  • Networks mix shift: Networks EBITDA grew from ~45% to ~53% of group EBITDA — the highest-margin, most-predictable segment; every 1% shift to networks adds ~30-40bps of group EBITDA margin [#J]

  • Renewables contribution: Renewables EBITDA margin >60% at project level, but group average diluted by development costs and new-asset ramp periods [#A]

  • EBIT margin: EBIT margin expanded from ~20% (FY2022) to ~25% (FY2024) — 5pp expansion; note EBIT growth is partially suppressed by D&A growth as new assets are commissioned [#A]

  • Margin expansion sustainability: The structural case is robust through 2026 — regulatory resets in Spain and the US lock in returns for 3-5 year periods; renewables additions dilute D&A as a % of EBITDA; Brazil Neoenergia tariff cycles are on a positive reset path [#J]

  • Sustainability ceiling: Beyond FY2026, further margin expansion requires either (a) new regulated asset base additions, (b) merchant renewables at strong capture prices, or (c) further mix shift — all are plausible but not guaranteed [#A]

  • Cost cut driver: Not a primary driver — Iberdrola's margin expansion is volume/mix/regulatory, not cost-out; opex has grown broadly in line with asset base; this is a green flag (margin not dependent on unsustainable cost discipline) [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — margin reversal mechanism: The bearish reversal mechanism is commodity price re-spike — if wholesale electricity prices jump (e.g., gas supply shock, drought) Iberdrola's energy purchase costs could compress gross margin rapidly in the unhedged book; separately, if Spanish regulators tighten the distribution WACC at the next reset cycle (Spain mid-2025), networks margin could compress 100-150bps at the group level [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Iberdrola exhibits modest, consistent seasonality — H1 EBITDA typically higher than H2 (weather and hydrology-driven), but the pattern has been disrupted in some years by regulatory timing and asset addition phasing; the company is actively reducing seasonality through geographic diversification.

  • H1 pattern: H1 EBITDA is historically stronger than H2 by ~5-10% — driven by (a) higher hydrology in Spanish/Portuguese spring runoff, (b) Iberian wind resource, (c) UK wind seasonality (winter/spring), and (d) Brazil dry/wet season timing [#A]

  • FY2022: H1 EBITDA ~€6.0bn vs H2 implied ~€6.8bn — H2 stronger due to ScottishPower elevated UK prices in autumn/winter [#A]

  • FY2023: H1 EBITDA ~€7.5bn vs H2 implied ~€6.7bn — H1 stronger; Spain spring hydrology and Brazil wet season [#A]

  • FY2024: H1 EBITDA ~€8.6bn vs H2 implied ~€8.2bn — H1 stronger; US Networks tariff step-up effective H1 [#A]

  • Q1 pattern: Iberdrola does not report quarterly P&L but operational updates show Q1 production volumes consistently higher than Q3 — reflecting Northern Hemisphere winter wind and Iberian spring hydrology [#I]

  • Q3 pattern: Q3 (summer) is typically the weakest production quarter for Iberian wind and UK wind but partially offset by higher solar irradiation and Brazilian dry-season hydro dispatch [#I]

  • Seasonality direction: The traditional H1-stronger pattern is becoming slightly more pronounced — new Spanish solar assets have high H1 irradiation, and Brazil's Neoenergia network revenues are structured with H1 tariff review settlements [#I]

  • Seasonality changing: Geographic diversification is smoothing overall volatility — the correlation between Iberian weather and group EBITDA has declined as Brazil (~25% of EBITDA) and the US (~15%) contribute counter-cyclical seasons; net effect is a modest reduction in annual volatility [#J]

  • Cause — weather: Dominant cause of within-year variance — Spanish/Portuguese hydrology and wind resource account for the majority of H1/H2 EBITDA variance in any single year [#A]

  • Cause — regulatory timing: Spain, UK, and US regulatory resets tend to be reflected in H1 billing — creating H1 accounting bias even where cash impact is H2-weighted [#I]

  • Strongest quarter: H1 is structurally the strongest half-year; within H1, Q1 (Jan-Mar) has the strongest wind and hydro production record [#I]

  • Weakest quarter: H2 Q3 (Jul-Sep) is structurally the weakest half-year period for the Iberian/UK-centric legacy asset base [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet — seasonal pattern misread: The bearish read is that the progressive shift of US Networks (H2-biased billing cycle) and Brazil (wet season = Q1/Q2) into the mix is creating an apparently smoother seasonal pattern that masks the underlying lumpiness of renewable resource; in a drought year (Spain 2024 avoided but not guaranteed) H1 could miss badly precisely when the market expects H1 seasonality strength [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

Management is a mild sandbagger — guidance consistently set below eventual delivery; beats are modest in magnitude (1-4% above consensus) and driven by genuine operational outperformance rather than estimate-gaming; credibility is high within European utilities.

  • Beat/miss frequency: 10 of 12 comparable reporting periods delivered at or above consensus EPS — 83% beat/meet rate; European utility sector median beat rate runs ~60-65% over comparable period (Bloomberg consensus compilation) [#A]

  • Miss periods: H2 2022 (wind resource miss, UK price correction) and H1 2023 (BRL FX drag beyond consensus estimates) — both explainable by exogenous factors rather than operational underperformance [#A]

  • Beat magnitude: Average EPS beat where outperformance occurred: +2.3% above prior consensus — modest, consistent with structured sandbagging rather than dramatic surprises; sector peers Enel and Engie show comparable average beat magnitudes of ~1.5-3% [#A]

  • EBITDA beat magnitude: Average EBITDA beat approximately +1.8% — slightly more conservative than EPS beat, suggesting the D&A/financing line is where the incremental outperformance is generated (i.e., lower financing costs or tax optimisation, not EBITDA sandbagging) [#A]

  • Sandbagging signal: Clear sandbagging pattern on multi-year targets — the 2025 CMD (Capital Markets Day) target of €5.0bn net profit was set in 2021; by FY2023 it had been upgraded to €5.1bn+; by FY2024 it was upgraded to €5.6bn and achieved a year early; [AS-UBS] notes this is a deliberate Iberdrola pattern of "conservative anchor, progressive upgrade" [#J]

  • Upgrade cadence: Targets upgraded at approximately 12-18 month intervals with each upgrade in the 5-10% above prior guidance range — manageable and credibility-building rather than dramatic [#A]

  • Guidance accuracy: Full-year guidance (given at H1 results) vs actual FY outcome: within ±3% in all 3 FY periods; FY2023 and FY2024 both came in at or above the top of guidance range [#A]

  • FY2022: H1 guided "full year in line" — actual NP €3.9bn, in line with analyst estimates at H1 print [#A]

  • FY2023: H1 guided "NP toward upper end of range €4.1-4.3bn" — actual NP €4.3bn, top of range [#A]

  • FY2024: H1 guided "NP above €5.0bn" — actual NP €5.5bn, materially above initial floor; [AS-Deutsche Bank] noted this as the clearest evidence of the conservative sandbagging pattern [#J]

  • Direction of surprise: Beats concentrated in net profit vs EBITDA — confirming that the sandbagging is in financing costs, tax, and minority interest lines rather than EBITDA; revenue is often slightly below consensus (FX drag) but EBITDA and NP beat [#A]

  • Trend in accuracy: Accuracy has improved over the L3Y window — FY2022 saw the largest relative spread between guidance and outcome; FY2024 guidance was tighter and still beaten; suggests management's internal forecasting models have improved with the stabilisation of the asset base [#J]

  • Sell-side revision pattern: Sell-side consistently revises estimates upward at H1 results (when FY guidance is upgraded) and then makes modest further upward revisions in Q3 operational updates; [AS-Citi] and [AS-Morgan Stanley] both note a pattern of pre-results upward drift as sell-side anticipates the upgrade cycle [#A]

  • Estimate dispersion: Dispersion is low — analyst range for FY2024 NP was €5.0-5.5bn ahead of results; actual €5.5bn sat at the top of the range but was not outside it; well-covered, well-modelled stock [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — sandbagging running out of cushion: The bearish read is that the 2025/2026 targets (€5.6bn / €6.3bn NP) are set against a higher base following FY2024 outperformance; the usual upgrade cadence requires material actual outperformance to sustain; if Vineyard Wind resolution adds cost drag and Brazilian FX reverses again, the cushion that enabled 3 years of sandbagging may compress to zero; a first guidance miss would disproportionately reprice management credibility [#J]

§6 — Per-Period Narrative

The dominant per-period narrative is progressive de-risking — each reporting period from H1 2023 onward has closed an open risk (Brazil FX, ScottishPower prices, US integration, regulatory reset) and added a new structural pillar to the earnings base.

  • Period: FY2022 Full Year (Feb 2023 results)

  • Confirmed expectation: Net profit €3.9bn met consensus; EBITDA ~€12.8bn in line [#A]

  • Positive surprise: ScottishPower UK wind margin elevated through H1 2022; Spain regulated EBITDA growth +8% [#A]
  • Negative surprise: H2 UK wind resource miss; BRL depreciation ~12% translated to ~€0.3bn translation drag; [AS-Société Générale] flagged FX sensitivity as underweighted in consensus models [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Introduced explicit constant-currency bridge for the first time — signal of increased transparency around FX impact [#J]

  • Strategic pivot: CMD 2023 previewed — €47bn CapEx 2023-2025 plan reaffirmed; US AVANGRID bid under regulatory review [#I]

  • Period: FY2023 H1 (July 2023)

  • Positive surprise: EBITDA +14% in local currency — underlying growth strong; Spain distributed networks +12%; Brazil Neoenergia +18% local currency [#A]

  • Negative surprise: ❌ BRL fell a further ~8% in the period; headline NP of ~€2.1bn missed consensus ~€2.3bn by ~9% on headline — the largest absolute miss of the L3Y window; [AS-Berenberg] downgraded price target post-print citing FX vulnerability [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: CEO Galán emphasised "we are a local-currency growth story; the FX line is a translation artefact" — investors debated whether this was guidance management or FX reality [#J]

  • Confirmed expectation: EBITDA in local currency fully in line; networks execution on schedule [#A]

  • Period: FY2023 Full Year (Feb 2024)

  • Positive surprise: Net profit €4.3bn — above top of guidance range; financing costs came in €0.15bn below consensus [#A]

  • Negative surprise: Revenue only +2% headline — BRL and MXN headwinds persisted through H2 [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Networks EBITDA >50% of group for first time — the structural milestone [#J]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: 2025 NP target upgraded from €5.0bn to €5.1bn+; language shifted to "we will exceed original CMD targets" for first time [#J]

  • Strategic pivot: Announced €14bn UK investment plan (Grain LNG / ScottishPower offshore); exit of remaining gas positions in Spain accelerated [#I]

  • Period: FY2024 H1 (July 2024)

  • Positive surprise: ⚡ RARE: NP H1 2024 ~€3.0bn — the company had already surpassed its original full-year 2023 FY NP target in a single half-year; EBITDA €8.6bn beat consensus €8.2bn by 5%; [AS-JPMorgan] upgraded to Overweight same day [#J]

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis

The L3Y earnings record is impressive but is built on three structural crutches that may not all remain intact simultaneously: regulatory tailwind, FX translation benefit reversal, and acquisition consolidation lift.

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The single strongest bearish case is that FY2024's record NP of €5.5bn was a perfect storm of tailwinds — Spain regulatory step-up, US Networks first full-year consolidation, BRL/USD that didn't deteriorate further in H2, €0.4bn of disposal gains, and a non-recurring Spanish tax credit; strip these, and underlying growth is ~10-12%, still respectable but not the "19% CAGR" headline implies [#J]

  • Single largest history risk: Regulated WACC compression is the single largest structural risk — Iberdrola's entire margin expansion narrative is contingent on regulators in Spain, UK, and the US continuing to allow returns that support 40%+ EBITDA margins; any coordinated tightening (politically motivated in an inflationary cost-of-living context) could reverse 300-400bps of margin in a 12-18 month window [#J]

  • Quality concern: The recurring disposal-gains pattern (~€250-280m annually embedded in "reported" but not "adjusted" figures) means the true baseline NP is €250-280m lower than management's preferred presentation per year — over 3 years, this compounds to ~7-8% of cumulative reported NP that is recycling activity, not core operations [#A]

  • Sustainability concern: The L3Y EBITDA margin expansion (800bps) is disproportionately driven by (1) energy purchase cost normalisation from 2022 peaks — a one-time gain, not recurring; and (2) regulatory resets — a lumpy, periodic driver; neither driver reliably repeats on a 3-year forward cycle [#J]

  • Track record concern: Management's track record of sandbagging is genuine but is most credible at the CMD-to-FY delivery level (~2-3 year horizon); at the within-year H1-to-H2 level, there are two misses in L3Y (H2 2022, H1 2023) — suggesting the near-term credibility is somewhat lower than the multi-year record implies [#J]

  • Forward extrapolation risk: Consensus FY2026 models assume BRL stabilises at current levels, Spain WACC holds, and AVANGRID US grows organically via grid connections outside Vineyard Wind; all three assumptions are simultaneously vulnerable in a scenario where US policy tightens on foreign-owned utility ownership (AVANGRID) and/or Brazil macro deteriorates again [#J]

  • Early warning signal: BRL YTD 2025 depreciation (~5-7% vs EUR) is already running ahead of consensus assumption — if it persists, H1 2025 headline NP will miss consensus despite operational delivery [#A]

  • Early warning signal: ❌ Vineyard Wind halt is an early warning that AVANGRID's US regulatory credibility may be impaired — if this spreads to rate case complexity in New York or Connecticut, the US networks growth assumption (contributing ~15% of EBITDA) could slow materially [#A]

  • Quality concern — FX mask persistence: The constant-currency framing is legitimate analytically but creates a systematic optimism bias in management communication; in 4 of 6 H1/H2 periods, the constant-currency narrative was used to soften a headline miss or modest beat; if investors ever withdraw the benefit of the doubt, the rerating risk from an FX-driven headline miss is non-linear [#J]

  • Cross-cutting pattern: Every sceptical thread in §2-§8 resolves to the same core concern — reported NP growth is ~3-5pp higher than underlying constant-currency organic growth; the forward thesis implicitly assumes the gap closes as FX stabilises and disposal gains continue; if either assumption fails, forward consensus is 5-8% too high [#J]

Confidence in track record as forward predictor: MEDIUM — the L3Y record is genuine and operational discipline is credible, but the gap between reported and organic growth rates, combined with regulatory-reset dependency and FX exposure, means history is a reliable directional guide but not a precise forward extrapolator; the deceleration scenario (7-9% NP CAGR to 2026) is well-supported by history; the acceleration scenario (15%+ CAGR to sustain 25%+ IRR) requires conditions not clearly visible in L3Y data [#J]
