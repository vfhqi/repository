EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The materiality of change is profound across leadership, strategy, and financial profile. New CEO Philip Hoare replaced decade-long leader Leo Quinn in September 2025, bringing over 30 years of deep sector expertise, while CFO Phil Harrison steps down in May 2026 after a decade, completing a simultaneous turnover of the two most critical executive roles. This elevated near-term execution risk is partially offset by industry-leading employee engagement of 83%, standing 8% above the industry benchmark, and UK voluntary attrition improving to a stable 10%, down from 12% in 2023. Strategically, management launched the "Evolve, Energize, Explore" framework to drive the next chapter of profitable growth, pivoting entirely from managing legacy distressed projects to aggressively targeting high-margin expansion in UK Power transmission and US data centres. Financially, the profile has transformed dramatically, with robust structural margin expansion and exceptionally strong cash conversion supporting peer-leading capital returns, though US Civils remains a near-term drag.

Activist presence is notably absent from the shareholder register, with no active activist holding greater than 3% existing in the AS library for Balfour Beatty, indicating management operates without external pressure for accelerated strategic shifts or capital restructuring.
