Brief company descriptions
Nokia Oyj (NOKIA.FI)

Nokia is a leading global telecommunications company that provides hardware and software for mobile, fixed, and cloud networks to communications service providers (CSPs) and enterprises worldwide.

The company operates on all continents and employs over 90,000 people across more than 100 countries, with its main market segments concentrated in Europe and North America.

Nokia's core mission is to create technology that helps the world act together, emphasizing sustainability, energy-efficient network equipment, and bridging the global digital divide.

Historically, Nokia operated under four business groups: Network Infrastructure, Mobile Networks, Cloud and Network Services, and Nokia Technologies.

To better align with customer needs and accelerate innovation during the AI supercycle, Nokia is simplifying its organizational structure into two primary operating segments: Network Infrastructure and Mobile Infrastructure, effective January 1, 2026.

The Network Infrastructure segment acts as Nokia's primary growth engine and consists of the IP Networks, Optical Networks, and Fixed Networks business units.

The new Mobile Infrastructure segment consolidates the Core Software, Radio Networks, and Technology Standards units to sharpen accountability and position the business for long-term technology leadership in 6G and AI-native networks.

Nokia Technologies, now part of Mobile Infrastructure, manages an industry-leading patent portfolio of over 26,000 patent families, driving a highly profitable and predictable licensing revenue model that generates approximately EUR 1.4 billion annually.

The company's customer base is shifting; while CSPs historically made up nearly 100% of revenues, Nokia is actively pivoting toward webscalers and mission-critical enterprises.
