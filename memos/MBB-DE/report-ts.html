Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The business operates a diversified portfolio of industrial holdings focused on energy transition and IT security niches, offering defensive structural growth.

Portfolio Structure and Segment Split

1.
Infrastructure segment leader: Subsidiary Friedrich Vorwerk, in which MBB holds a 39.26% stake, specializes in pipeline and plant engineering for electricity, gas, and hydrogen networks
2.
Specialized automation niche: Listed subsidiary Aumann, representing 49.43% of voting rights, manufactures advanced winding machinery and fully automated production lines for electric vehicle powertrains
3.
Cybersecurity software provider: Private subsidiary DTS IT AG, an 80.00% owned asset, provides cloud services, proprietary security software, and 24/7 Security Operations Center monitoring for Mittelstand customers
4.
Ecological hardwood systems: Listed subsidiary Delignit, which is 56.31% owned by MBB, manufactures hardwood-based ecological materials and system solutions for automotive and industrial logistics clients
5.
Traditional consumer goods: Private subsidiaries include CT Formpolster, which produces polyurethane mattresses, and Hanke Tissue, a manufacturer of hygienic and household paper products

Competitive Position and Value Chain

6.
Strategic exit-free horizon: MBB's durable competitive advantage is anchored by its permanent capital model with no forced exit timelines, allowing subsidiaries to plan organically and build trust with Mittelstand sellers
7.
Massive debt-free advantage: Strong liquid reserves enable MBB to purchase companies entirely with equity, providing a key negotiating edge in high-interest rate environments
8.
Strong client relationships: Infrastructure projects benefit from multi-decade relationships with 14 of the 16 largest German transmission system operators, securing a profit-favored market position
9.
High customer switching costs: High customization and technical compatibility of Aumann's stator winding machines create steep switching barriers for automotive manufacturers
10.
Other side of the story: Heavy reliance on public infrastructure tenders exposes the business to severe project execution risks, where raw material inflation can dilute margins on fixed-price contracts

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for MBB SE is highly compelling, driven by structural energy transition spending and defensive IT security growth.

Primary Growth Engines

1.
German energy transition: Friedrich Vorwerk benefits from immense visibility, with Germany's network development plans dictating €61.0 billion in electricity grid investments through 2030
2.
Growing cybersecurity demand: DTS IT AG is capturing significant mid-market demand, highlighted by securing its largest-ever €10.00-to-€50.00 million five-year contract
3.
Accretive private M&A: High interest rates restrict debt-reliant private equity buyers, allowing MBB to acquire distressed SMEs at highly attractive valuations using its cash
4.
Active capital returns: Management enhances EPS value through continuous buybacks, having returned €40.40 million via share repurchases in 2024 alongside steadily rising base dividends

Valuation Re-Rating Catalysts

5.
Underpriced operating assets: The liquid portfolio and holding cash are valued at ~€220.00 per share, presenting significant upside over the late-2025 share price of €167.00
6.
High independent probabilities: The bull case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously, as the energy transition and IT security expansion operate independently
7.
Other side of the story: A prolonged downturn in the automotive sector could permanently impair Aumann's profitability, dragging down consolidated margins if next-automation diversification fails

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by automotive industry weakness and project execution delays, though partially mitigated by structural infrastructure backlogs.

Operational Transmission Risks

1.
Project margin compression: Legacy fixed-price projects can lead to cost overruns under high-inflation conditions, as labor and raw material costs pressure margins
2.
Automotive investment strike: EV capital expenditure cuts directly impact Aumann, causing a severe order intake decline of 40.4% in 2024 that restricts automated line revenues
3.
Skilled labor shortages: Engineering and pipeline projects require highly specialized staff, meaning a failure to recruit will directly cap revenue growth

Labor and Cost Risks

4.
False friend backlogs: Sizable order books can act as a false friend, hiding cost overruns and personnel inflation that only surface during project completion
5.
Early-warning indicators: Investors must closely monitor quarterly order intake at Aumann and raw material price volatility in the consumer goods segment
6.
Other side of the story: Skepticism regarding infrastructure margins is overdone because Friedrich Vorwerk operates a highly flexible cost structure and a solid €1.07 billion order backlog
