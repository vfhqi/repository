EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 7 findings

1.
Investment Case Verdict: Tryg's United Towards '27 strategy targets a combined ratio of around 81% and an insurance service result of DKK 8.0-8.4bn by fiscal year 2027, positioning the company as a structurally advantaged Nordic P&C consolidator with best-in-class capital efficiency offsetting near-term workers' compensation regulatory headwinds and moderating premium growth momentum

Materiality of Change and Strategic Positioning

Steffen Kragh's appointment as Board Chair maintains commercial continuity following governance succession, while Group CEO Johan Kirstein Brammer continues to lead Tryg, bringing nine years of industry experience, having originally joined Tryg in 2016, ensuring leadership stability during strategy execution.

United Towards '27 targets DKK 8.0-8.4bn insurance service result by leveraging Scandinavian scale and structural cost efficiencies, while DKK 1bn property divestments de-risk the free portfolio and reduce capital consumption to optimize returns.

TryghedsGruppen's 49.8% stake increases towards its 50% target as they opt out of Tryg's share buybacks, creating a unique ownership dynamic that supports aggressive capital return policies while maintaining strategic control.

Conservative capital actions and property divestments support strong capital returns of DKK 17-18bn, keeping solvency resilient despite regulatory headwinds, demonstrating management's commitment to shareholder value creation.

Tryg faces a DKK 1.2bn pre-tax charge in Q2 2026 from an unfavorable Danish Supreme Court ruling, with solvency ratio absorbing a -4%pts impact from the ruling, offset by +9%pts from capital actions, representing the most material near-term headwind to earnings trajectory.

Q1 2026 Delivery Assessment

Operating EPS reached DKK 1.85, down from DKK 2.09 in Q1 2025, but beating the consensus forecast of DKK 1.70 by 8.82% (calculated), reflecting stable operational momentum despite investment income volatility.

The Solvency II ratio came in at 192%, beating the consensus expectation of 188%, confirming balance sheet resilience ahead of the Q2 workers' compensation charge.

Forward Credibility Under 'United Towards '27' Strategy

Tryg's United Towards '27 strategy targets an insurance service result of DKK 8.0-8.4bn and a combined ratio of around 81%, with management targeting a return on own funds of 35-40% alongside DKK 17-18bn in total shareholder distributions, representing highly ambitious yet credible targets.

Tryg has a consistent history of realizing its financial targets, such as delivering within the guided DKK 7.2-7.6bn insurance service result range for 2024, with 2025 reporting an insurance service result of DKK 7.9bn and a combined ratio of 80.3%, establishing management as an honest-tracker archetype.

Management has proven to be an honest-tracker by maintaining stable guidance and transparently disclosing the DKK 1.2bn pre-tax charge in Q2 2026, reinforcing forward credibility despite near-term earnings headwinds.

A parallel 100 bps drop in interest rates leads to a 100 bps deterioration in the combined ratio due to lower claims discounting, with Morgan Stanley analyst Wilson-Omordia noting a 100 bps rate cut drops claims discounting by DKK 400m, offset by DKK 200m bond gains, representing the primary macro sensitivity risk to 2027 targets.

The total pool consists of 17 named individual analysts from major Nordic and international brokerage firms, with the current consensus rating distribution including 9 buys, 7 holds, and 1 sell as of June 2026, reflecting a moderately constructive but cautious stance.

Tryg maintains middle-tier momentum as strong private lines premium growth is offset by deliberate commercial portfolio pruning, while Gjensidige and Sampo demonstrate superior near-term momentum through aggressive Norwegian repricing and front-loaded Topdanmark integration synergies, positioning Tryg as third in the Nordic momentum ranking.
