  • Sandbagger signal: Revenue guidance shows mild sandbagger characteristics — 75% beat rate, average beat of +0.9%; consistent with management anchoring revenue targets to contracted backlog and adding limited upside from merchant/spot exposure [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Guidance credibility rests on three simultaneously-optimistic assumptions: operating leverage materialisation, energy price stability, and O&M cost containment — any one of which failing produces material EPS downside. [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: ~70-80% of revenue under long-term PPA — contracted revenue base is a genuine strength; customer churn risk on existing contracts is low over the guidance horizon [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Embedded PPA repricing at +2-4% per annum on renewals — reasonable vs current market rates for Italian and European renewable PPAs; not aggressive given energy inflation context [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: "Organic only" caveat excludes transformative M&A, but bolt-on acquisitions (~1-2% revenue contribution historically) create ambiguity in what is considered "organic" [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Management assumes renewables capacity factor at long-run historical averages — FY24 and H1-FY25 experienced below-average wind resource in Italy (-5 to -8% vs LTA); guidance appears to assume reversion to mean [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: O&M cost guidance assumes flat-to-falling per-MWh O&M from FY25 onward, based on new asset efficiency and contract renegotiations; actual L3Y trend is O&M per MWh +3-5% annually — a direct contradiction [#J]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage assumption of ~40-50% EBITDA flow-through on incremental revenue — L4-period actual flow-through is ~20-30%; management is assuming 2x the observed leverage rate [#J]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: No explicit pricing power assumption beyond PPA contracts — this is conservative and appropriate for a regulated/contracted energy business [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Guided effective tax rate ~24-26% — Italy has implemented windfall energy taxes; risk of upward tax rate revision is non-trivial and not adequately flagged in guidance [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: No share buyback factored into guidance — conservative; no dilution assumed either; share count expected stable [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Guidance embeds stable net debt/EBITDA — CapEx programme through FY26 may push leverage modestly higher, creating interest cost headwinds not fully in guidance [#J]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Southern Hemisphere (Chile, South Africa) revenues carry FX exposure — approximately 15-20% of revenue in non-EUR currency; no currency assumption explicitly disclosed in guidance [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Energy price exposure on unhedged ~20-30% merchant generation — guidance appears to embed forward price curves, but sensitivity to a 10% energy price decline is significant [#J]
