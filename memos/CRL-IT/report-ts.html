Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Outstanding structural compounder delivers highly predictable earnings growth through leadership in niche energy-efficient environmental controls.
1.
Dominant niche: Highly profitable revenue is generated by designing custom programmable controls, humidifiers, and sensors that optimize energy efficiency in HVAC/R systems. The group focuses exclusively on high-value B2B niches rather than low-margin mass-market applications
2.
Sustained compounding: Medium-term investment case relies on a rapid multi-year double-digit organic expansion fueled by hyperscaler data center cooling and European F-gas regulations
3.
Cyclical swing: Primary forward earnings swing factor is the speed of recovery in the European residential heat pump market following severe channel destocking. Clear signs of destocking normalization in late 2025 are already driving a robust order rebound in EMEA
4.
Favourable compounding: Multi-year performance has been exceptional, with Carel delivering a 12% revenue and 13% EBITDA CAGR over the 2015 to 2025 period
5.
Accelerating momentum: Q1 2026 organic revenue expanded by 19.7% to reach €170.90 million, beating the upper limit of management's guidance
6.
Improving margins: Q1 2026 EBITDA margins hit 21.50%, representing an expansion of over 300 basis points versus Q1 2025
7.
Competitive pressure: Most critical risk is localized raw material cost inflation or pricing pressure on residential heat pump components as the market consolidates
8.
Positive verdict: First-look verdict strongly warrants deeper investigation because Carel is an asset-light niche compounder whose earnings power is structurally expanding via sustainability trends

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. High-barrier software and hardware integration creates massive customer lock-in with major global original equipment manufacturers.

Core Product Segments and Revenue Models

1.
Balanced segments: Core business is divided into HVAC at 72% of 2025 revenue and Refrigeration at 28% of 2025 revenue
2.
Specialized products: Product portfolio consists of highly specialized programmable controls, isothermal and adiabatic humidifiers, inverters, and electronic expansion valves
3.
Accretive SaaS: Software-as-a-Service capabilities are expanding through Kiona, which provides digital energy optimization for commercial buildings, delivering high-margin recurring revenues
4.
Concentrated channels: Direct customers are primarily OEMs who incorporate Carel's components into their finished HVAC/R machinery. OEMs represent 66% of group sales
5.
Widespread distribution: Distribution network includes installers, contractors, value-added resellers, and end-users representing 20% of sales, plus third-party distributors representing 14%, across 75 countries
6.
Strategic niches: Strategy deliberately avoids mass-market consumer applications to focus on hyperscaler data center cooling, pharmaceutical cleanrooms, and food retail cold chains

Industry Positioning and Competitive Moats

7.
Defensible barriers: Deep software and hardware integration creates high switching costs. Once an OEM integrates Carel's custom software algorithms into their machinery, replacing them requires an expensive and time-consuming redesign
8.
Structural tailwinds: F-gas revisions and RepowerEU targets mandate a transition toward natural refrigerants, a technology where Carel holds a decades-long lead
9.
Heavy reinvestment: Over 5% of annual revenues reinvested directly into research and development, sustaining technological leadership in variable speed compressor technology and advanced sensors
10.
Favoured position: Carel increasingly engages directly with end-users like supermarket chains and hyperscalers, who then specify Carel components to their OEM suppliers, locking in sales
11.
Other side of the story: Skeptical customers observe that as Carel acquires adjacent system manufacturers, certain OEMs are becoming concerned about potential long-term competition and are preferring independent component suppliers

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Rapid organic recovery and margin expansion in Q1 2026 confirm that Carel has successfully navigated the 2024 destocking cycle.

Historical Growth and Returns

1.
Compounding trajectory: Outstanding multi-year growth highlighted by Carel's 12% revenue CAGR and 13% EBITDA CAGR over 2015-2025. Net income also compounded at a 13% CAGR
2.
Severe downturn: Revenue declined to €578.54 million in 2024 as high interest rates and regulatory delays in Germany stalled heat pump demand. Net income fell to €62.64 million
3.
Sharp rebound: Revenue expanded 8.7% to €629.03 million in 2025 with net income hitting a record €73.64 million. Constant currency growth reached 10.60% organically
4.
Excellent returns: Cash flow conversion reached an outstanding 98% in 2025, generating c.€100 million of free cash flow
5.
Leverage transformation: Balance sheet transitioned from €50.20 million net debt in 2024 to net cash of €18.40 million in 2025 (€48.30 million net cash excluding IFRS 16)

Recent Performance and Execution Profile

6.
Stellar Q1 2026: Revenue expanded 15.9% to €170.90 million, or 19.7% organically, significantly beating upper end of consensus
7.
Margin acceleration: EBITDA margin rising to 21.50% from 18.10% in Q1 2025, representing the highest quarterly profitability in several years
8.
Conservative guidance: Management consistently outperforms top-line targets – Q1 2026 organic growth of 19.7% surpassed the upper limit of the March 2026 guidance range
9.
Accretive digital: Kiona generated a robust EBITDA margin close to 30% in Q1 2026
10.
Other side of the story: US Dollar weakness imposed a negative €5.00 million impact on Q1 2026 revenues. Persistent currency headwinds could damp the reported trajectory despite strong organic gains

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Secular tailwinds in data center cooling and natural refrigerants underpin a highly predictable medium-term double-digit EPS expansion.

Primary Multi-Year Growth Drivers

1.
Data center boom: Hyperscalers specify Carel humidifiers and liquid cooling sensors to their direct HVAC suppliers. North American HVAC sales expanded above 50% organically in Q1 2026
2.
Liquid shift failure: Potential failure mechanism is a structural shift by hyperscalers away from liquid cooling to specialized air retrofits, though air-based retrofits would still require ventilation systems where Carel remains active
3.
F-gas transition: Environmental regulations mandate that commercial retailers replace traditional chemical refrigerants with CO2 and propane. North American refrigeration expanded organically by over 40% in Q1 2026
4.
Regulatory failure: Regulatory delay or weaker enforcement of F-gas milestones in the US represents the primary failure mechanism, though end-user sustainability policies are increasingly driving adoption regardless

Secondary Recovery Drivers and Bull Requirements

5.
Heat pump normalization: EMEA HVAC order intake showed visible improvement in late 2025, driving return to growth in Q1 2026, supported by RepowerEU and building energy directives
6.
Incentive failure: Sudden changes to national residential green incentives in major European markets serve as the primary failure mechanism, which would stall OEM manufacturing pipelines and freeze Carel component orders
7.
Bull case criteria: These growth drivers are highly independent – even if European residential heat pumps remain sluggish, the data center boom can carry the EPS trajectory. The compound probability of success is exceptionally high
8.
Other side of the story: Bear case maintains that mid-term margin pressure on heat pump components will intensify as Chinese and low-cost competitors enter the European residential market

Section 6 – Valuation 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Premium multiple is fair given Carel's resilient long-term compounding model and outstanding cash conversion profile.
1.
Compounding premium: Valuation multiples remain historically elevated vs. generic European industrial peers, justified by Carel's 12% revenue and 13% net income CAGR over 2015-2025
2.
Peer comparisons: Carel commands a premium EV/EBITDA multiple relative to broad refrigeration peers like Beijer Ref, though at a discount to high-margin HVAC pure-play Belimo
3.
SaaS multiple support: Kiona operates a capital-light software business with c.30% EBITDA margins, warranting a higher forward multiple than transactional hardware manufacturing
4.
FCF yield support: €100.00 million of free cash flow generated in 2025 on high cash conversion limits the need for dilutive capital raisings
5.
Other side of the story: Cyclical delays in heat pump demand recovery have historically triggered severe multiple contraction, as occurred during the 2024 destocking cycle when organic revenue fell 11%

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Inventory destocking and regulatory delays represent the primary structural risks to Carel's earnings trajectory.

Crucial Bear Risks and Failure Modes

1.
Incentive reliance: High structural exposure to European residential environmental subsidies constitutes a key risk. Changing national incentive schemes can abruptly halt customer purchasing pipelines
2.
Severe margin drag: 2024 incentive changes in Germany and Italy dragged Carel's EBITDA margin from 21.00% in 2023 to 18.30% in 2024
3.
Currency volatility: US Dollar weakness shaved more than €5.00 million from reported revenues in Q1 2026, dampening underlying organic performance
4.
Short interest: Institutional short interest remains negligible with no published short-seller reports, due to solid founding family control and long-term track record
5.
False friend: Accelerating orders from commercial HVAC distributors can temporarily mask underlying retail weakness. Inventory destocking and regulatory delay represent structural risks hidden during order book rebuilds
6.
Early warning signal: Single most critical early-warning signal is the monthly order intake trajectory in EMEA HVAC, which historically leads reported revenues by six months
7.
Other side of the story: Bulls maintain that sustainability-driven energy transitions are unstoppable and will proceed regardless of short-term national subsidy adjustments

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Project Impulse reorganization streamlined Carel's executive matrix to accelerate regional customer proximity and technical innovation.
1.
Executive transition: Project Impulse eliminated the Managing Director position following Giandomenico Lombello's retirement after 30 years of service, allowing for a modern, streamlined corporate matrix
2.
Streamlined reporting: Four newly appointed Regional CEOs (Western Europe, EEMEA, Americas, APAC) now report directly to CEO Francesco Nalini, reducing executive complexity
3.
R&D integration: A new Chief Technology Officer role was introduced, tasked with driving disruptive, long-term product innovation
4.
Tariff readiness: Streamlined regional structures allow Carel to respond dynamically, including potential deployment of inverter manufacturing in the US if trade tariffs demand it

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Work is highly recommended given Carel's exceptional structural exposure to data center cooling and F-gas transition tailwinds.
1.
Resilient growth: Outstanding long-term B2B compounding model, highlighted by the rapid V-shaped recovery and 19.7% organic revenue growth in Q1 2026
2.
Other side of the story: Potential customer pushback could emerge if major HVAC/R OEMs begin viewing Carel's expanding system and software integrations as direct competition rather than component supply
3.
Verify backlog: First action should be to verify the depth and duration of Carel's EMEA HVAC order backlog to confirm that the destocking headwind has fully cleared
4.
Quantify sensors: Second action should be to analyze the exact sensor content per liquid cooling unit in hyperscaler data centers to quantify Carel's total addressable market expansion within the AI cooling vertical
