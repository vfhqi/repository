EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Business Model + Organizational Architecture

Addtech operates 150+ independent B2B subsidiaries across 20 countries within a strictly decentralized dual- engine model , granting full operational autonomy to local managing directors while maintaining slim central oversight . The architecture targets 15% average annual earnings growth split equally between organic and acquired gains, historically delivering 20% EBITDA CAGR over 25 years through self-funded compounding without equity dilution . The group reorganized in October 2025 from five to six business areas and 15 specialized business units , explicitly designed to scale M&A search capacity and accelerate deal flow across niche clusters . Revenue generation rests on a favorable mix: 35% proprietary products carrying the highest pricing power, 30% customized solutions deeply integrated into client processes, and 35% traditional high-quality trading . Nordic markets represent 62% of customer sales , with international exposure expanding to 38% concentrated in the UK, Benelux, and DACH, though Germany has suffered from industrial capex postponements while the UK performed strongly .

Financial Profile + Capital Efficiency

Addtech achieved record EBITA margins of 16.0% in FY25/26 and expanded EBITDA margins to 17.97% in 2025 from 14.03% in 2019, driven by pricing power execution and favorable product mix shifts . Capital efficiency

metrics reach elite compounder levels: profit over working capital hit 81% in FY25/26 versus a 45% internal target, ROE of 28.90% , and post-tax ROIC of 18.30% in 2025 . The asset-light model limits tangible capex to 0.60% of sales , while operating cash flow converted 165% of net income in 2025 , fully funding 12 acquisitions in FY24/25 without balance sheet stress . Conservative gearing ended FY25/26 at 1.4x net debt/EBITDA , providing significant covenant headroom for ongoing programmatic M&A . Peer benchmarking reveals Addtech's 16.04% EBITA margin in 2025 significantly outperformed Beijer Ref at 10.60%, while NWC days of 67 versus Beijer's 90 validates superior working capital discipline. 3 4

Value Chain Position + Pricing Power

Addtech occupies a high-value-add intermediary position as a technical solutions trader, bridging specialized component manufacturers with fragmented downstream industrial end users through engineering design and bespoke customization . The group achieves 33-40% typical gross margins and 14-26% EBIT margins at its stage, materially outpacing vanilla component distributors at 15-25% gross and 5-12% EBIT . Deep technical integration enables subsidiaries to successfully pass input cost inflation directly to customers , maintaining record profitability despite elevated input costs across multiple product lines . Value-add engineering shifts customer purchase decisions from unit cost to total life-cycle value , allowing subsidiaries to deliver solutions beyond immediate geographical markets and defend pricing power . Upstream OEM manufacturing captures 12-15% EBIT margins anchored by product IP, while downstream industrial integration suffers from 4-8% thin margins due to low barriers, confirming Addtech's optimal positioning in the margin pool. 6

Competitive Landscape + M&A Discipline

Addtech competes primarily with elite Swedish serial acquirers including Indutrade, Lagercrantz, and Lifco, sharing similar decentralized organizational models and long-term track records, with individual subsidiaries targeting distinct highly specialized niches to limit direct overlap . Addtech delivered net sales growth of 11% in 2024 and 3% in 2025 , with operating profit increasing 14% in 2024 and an estimated 13% in 2025 . Peer Lagercrantz achieved the strongest momentum with 16% sales growth and 16% EBITA growth in 2024, while NCAB rebounded sharply with 22% sales growth in 2025 after a 12% decline in 2024. Bypassing competitive auctions through proprietary sourcing allows compounders to maintain M&A multiples at 5-7x EV/EBITA , with key risks including losing pricing discipline or experiencing failed integrations . Disintermediation risk exists as upstream suppliers may bypass technical traders, but Addtech focuses on demonstrating that collaboration is suppliers' most profitable sales strategy, while deep technical customization prevents easy bypass .

Disciplined deal pricing keeps acquisition entry multiples at conservative 5-7x EV/EBITA levels , protecting the balance sheet from becoming goodwill-heavy while delivering post-tax ROIC averaging 16% over ten years . Operational resilience showed through: adjusted EBITA expanded 12% in FY25/26 despite weak European industrial activity, with swift local restructuring executing overhead cuts in Automation and earn-out revaluations adding SEK 13m to operating profit . Long-Term Structural Tailwinds + Disruption Resilience

Accelerating grid electrification from national electrical network renovation and expansion provides a highly predictable decade-long runway , with high-voltage transmission products insulated from short-term interest rate fluctuations and growing electricity demand from regional data centers . Rising defense investments drive non-cyclical structural volume growth across security and communication niches, with defense applications now accounting for 15% of Automation division revenues alongside robust demand in specialized aerospace .

Sustained green transition from societal decarbonization accelerates product development cycles for energy- efficient industrial components, with sub-units benefiting from strong demand for bespoke battery packs, electric drivelines, and marine scrubbers driven by stricter environmental rules . Mitigated disintermediation threat exists because standard digital platforms cannot replicate bespoke engineering customization , with subsidiaries acting as crucial technical links rather than simple box-movers as customers increasingly outsource complex supply chain management . Clear adjacency expansion into underdeveloped European regions provides massive geographical reinvestment runways , with sourcing targets outside the Nordics prolonging the M&A runway for decades across thousands of potential small-scale acquisitions in fragmented B2B sectors .
