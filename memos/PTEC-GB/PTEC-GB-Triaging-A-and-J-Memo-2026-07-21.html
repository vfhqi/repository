B.1 Business Description 11 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Pure-play B2B gambling technology supplier. Playtech sold its B2C Snaitech retail division to Flutter for EUR 2.3bn in April 2025 and now operates almost entirely as a business-to-business software and content licensor to regulated gambling operators
2.
Core platform is PAM+. The Player Account Management platform processes player wallets, compliance and risk management for operator clients, and processed over 350 billion transactions in FY25
3.
Three commercial models. Standard revenue-share licensing on operator net gaming revenue, a fast-growing SaaS model for long-tail operators, and structured agreements with land-based "local hero" partners entering online
3.1
SaaS acceleration: Revenue +48% year-on-year to EUR 118.1m in FY25, a genuine outlier against a typical 12-15% sector growth rate
4.
Live Casino is the second-largest B2B franchise. Playtech runs over 500 live tables across 17 studios and holds roughly 25% of the European mainstream live-dealer market, well behind Evolution AB's roughly 60-70% share
5.
Financial character. FY25 group revenue EUR 763.6m, down 10% reported but up 1% underlying ex-Caliente restructuring, group Adjusted EBITDA EUR 197.0m, a 25.8% margin
5.1
De-leveraging evidence: Net cash EUR 28.5m at year-end versus EUR 142.8m net debt a year earlier, a genuinely rapid de-leveraging
6.
Two large strategic equity stakes sit on the balance sheet. A 30.8% holding in Caliente Interactive, carrying value EUR 708.7m, and a minority stake in Hard Rock Digital, carrying value EUR 178.8m
6.1
Concentration flag: Together these represent over 40% of residual sum-of-the-parts equity value, a genuine concentration risk in illiquid, non-cash-generating assets
7.
Geographic mix is now Americas-led. US and Canada B2B revenue grew roughly 71% to nearly 100% year-on-year in constant currency in FY25, offsetting a 6% UK B2B revenue decline driven by a large customer in-sourcing its own terminal technology
8.
Investment-style read. This is best read as an event-driven, self-help restructuring case layered on top of a structurally sub-scale B2B technology franchise, not a quality compounder and not a classic cyclical
9.
Governance in flux. New Chairman John Gleasure from May 2025, 19-year chief executive Mor Weizer, and a shareholder base that forced a 32.6% revolt against a EUR 100m Snaitech bonus pool in December 2024
10.
AGM friction continues into 2026. Over 55% of votes blocked directors' share-allotment powers at the May 2026 AGM, a live, current governance-friction signal rather than a historical one
