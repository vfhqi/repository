A.2 — Quarterly trajectory and quality-of-earnings notes

The FY2025 reported net profit of €445.8m is an all-time high but lower-quality than the headline implies. Approximately €39.0m — nearly 9% of reported profit — was a one-off IRAP tax reimbursement following the "Mediolanum Judgment", which will not repeat. The Q4 2025 net profit of €114m beat consensus by roughly 30%, but that beat was driven by performance-fee timing (quarterly crystallisation concentrates performance fees in Q4), the one-off tax recovery, and a lower effective tax rate — not underlying business acceleration. The correct earnings anchor for valuation is recurring net profit of €362.5m, which grew 6.8% and represents 81% of the total.

Q1 2026 is the strongest quarterly start since 2021: €1.9bn net inflows (+28% year-on-year), with €721m into AUI (+53% year-on-year), and a record March 2026 monthly inflow of €725m. The €83.4m variable net profit component (down from €92.0m in FY2024) is the cyclically exposed line — it can compress toward zero in a 20–30% equity drawdown.

A.3 — Balance-sheet and capital position

CET1 closed FY2025 at 17.1%, down from a higher prior level after absorbing a 3.8 percentage point hit from CRR3 implementation, a 2.1 percentage point deduction for the Intermonte integration, and a 1.6 percentage point absorption from operational-risk recalculation. It remains comfortably above the 8.7% SREP minimum — roughly 840 basis points of headroom. Total interest-bearing assets were €16.7bn at year-end, 77% in a conservative short-duration financial-asset portfolio (1.4-year duration); approximately 80% of the proprietary portfolio is government securities, the source of the BTP spread sensitivity discussed in Section C and Section D. Liquidity coverage ratio 337%, net stable funding ratio 245%.

A.5 — Post-corpus update: BGN Q1 2026 results (released 6 May 2026)

This subsection integrates BGN's Q1 2026 results, which were released on 6 May 2026 — after the parent research corpus (Q1–Q14) was assembled, and after the carried-forward Triaging memo was written. It was surfaced by an Investment-Analyst-initiated follow-up query during the writing of this memo (IA-initiated Report B, Files/BGN/ESA/IA-initiated/). The Q1 2026 results are material because they contain the first Alleanza disclosure, and they are integrated into the analytical sections that follow rather than left as a stale gap. The honest framing: the Q1 2026 results mildly strengthen the case and do not change the recommendation, but they change the memo's factual characterisation of the fulcrum from "entirely pre-financial" to "early proof point arrived, scale still unproven".

Q1 2026 headline (released 6 May 2026). Net profit €126.4m, up 15% year-on-year (recurring net profit €93.5m; variable net profit €32.9m). Net inflows €1.9bn, up 28% year-on-year — the best-ever first quarter — with a further €0.9bn in April. NII €82.7m, up 4%, net interest margin 2.02%. CET1 16.7%. The financial-advisor network grew to 2,451 (from roughly 2,405 at FY2025). Guidance was raised: total net inflows now guided above €6.5bn, and the NII range was raised by €5m to €335–345m (the FY2025-corpus figure used elsewhere in this memo is €330–340m; €335–345m is the current guidance). The market took the results positively — the shares rose 4.93% on the day to roughly €54.75.

Q1 2026 Alleanza disclosure — the material finding. BGN explicitly changed its presentation "to give more disclosure on the Insurbanking business". The new disclosure:

  • A new standalone line: "total assets Insurbanking of Generali clients" of €7.5bn, shown separately from BGN's own €116.7bn of client assets
  • Conto Unico current accounts: more than 1,700 opened since the February 2026 rollout (cumulative), roughly six times the approximately 270 opened in the Q4 2025 pilot
  • Insurbanking gross premiums of €170m since inception, of which 40% is already invested in unit-linked solutions and a further roughly 40% will be invested gradually over three to four years; the eventual asset allocation is intended to be roughly 20% traditional life and roughly 80% unit-linked managed by BGN. A sell-side analyst on the call described the €170m as "a very, very strong number"
  • Alleanza agents: still 2,700, unchanged
  • Management qualitative commentary: the ramp is "in line or slightly above expectation"; "not if, but when"; onboarding a client "as a main bank" takes three to six months. The deposits gathered into Conto Unico current accounts are partly "a sort of parking, waiting for the new initiatives", to be reinvested into managed solutions, with management "more confident than in the past" of strong managed-solution numbers in the coming weeks. The banking rollout to the Alleanza network "started in February, it will finish at the end of July", with impact expected "in the second half of the year"

What the Q1 disclosure does NOT do. Three honest limits. First, Alleanza is still explicitly excluded from the headline net-inflow numbers — CEO Mossa, verbatim: "At the moment, let's say that Alleanza is not included in these numbers." Second, the FY2026 Alleanza contribution is still guided as "negligible... due to the fact that we are in the ramp-up phase". Third, full Alleanza disclosure is deferred to the three-year strategic plan at the end of the second half of 2026. So the fulcrum has its first proof point, but it is an early, partial proof point, not a confirmation of conversion at scale.
