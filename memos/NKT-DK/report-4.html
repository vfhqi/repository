  • Judgement: Revenue deceleration from +36% (2023) to +26% (2024) to +6% (2025) reflects capacity saturation, not demand weakness. NKT cannot grow faster because it is maxed out — Karlskrona solves this from 2027. #J [C]

6. Market Share & Competitive Position

The HVDC market is a triopoly with extreme barriers to entry. NKT holds a strong niche position but Prysmian is pulling away on absolute scale. New entrants are delayed or impractical.

  • Judgement: The oligopoly is intact and pricing power holds. Prysmian is raising margins, not cutting prices to gain share. Rational oligopoly behaviour is the strongest near-term signal for NKT bulls. #J [C]
  • Judgement: NKT's pure-play European focus is a defensive advantage in the current geopolitical climate. Unlike Prysmian and Nexans, NKT has zero US offshore wind exposure — insulated from Trump administration policy risk. #J [AS]
  • Analysis: Market share (submarine): Prysmian ~25%, Nexans ~15%, NKT 10-15%, LS Cable ~10%. Former Prysmian executives note the top three exhibit virtually identical product quality — competition centres on capacity and vessel scheduling. #A [AS]
  • Analysis: XLCC/Aquora (most credible new European entrant) delayed Hunterston factory to ~2028, pivoted to installation-first. Original 1,200-job plan halved. No credible HVDC entrant has emerged. #A [C]
  • Analysis: LS Cable has secured 525kV homologation from TenneT. Sumitomo building new submarine plant at Port of Nigg, UK. Both attempting to compress 4-5 year greenfield cycle to 3 years — extreme execution risk. #A [AS]
  • NKT capacity expansion: EUR 1.3bn Karlskrona investment. Third extrusion tower at full 200m height. New factory + NKT Eleonora vessel operational from 2027. Will be world's largest HV offshore cable production site. #I [C]
  • Vessel constraint: NKT Victoria (operational) + NKT Eleonora (2027) gives two vessels. Prysmian has Monna Lisa + legacy fleet. Vessel ownership is a competitive moat and margin driver. #I [C]
  • Morgan Stanley warns that if offshore wind installations slow to 13 GW annually (vs base case 17 GW), the European cable market could become ~15% oversupplied by 2030, triggering aggressive price competition. #I [AS]
  • Open question: Can the oligopoly pricing power survive simultaneous capacity additions from Prysmian, Nexans, NKT, LS Cable, and Sumitomo all coming online 2027-2029? Historical precedent suggests upcycles end in overcapacity. #OQ [C+AS]
