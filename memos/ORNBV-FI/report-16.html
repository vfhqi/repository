§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

CLEAN with two structural nuances (dual-class shares + Finnish nomination committee architecture) that require investor understanding but do not constitute red flags in context

Dual-class structure:
- ⚡ OUTLIER flag: Orion has two share classes — Series A shares (ORNAV-FI, 1 vote per share, higher nominal) and Series B shares (ORNBV-FI, typically 1 vote per share but with structural differences in dividend weighting in certain years)
- Note: Finnish dual-class structures in older-established companies (Orion, Nokia-era structures) can be legacy artefacts; the A/B distinction at Orion primarily reflects historical capital structure rather than an active founder-control mechanism as seen in US tech dual-class
- A shares and B shares: in Orion's case, A shares carry higher voting rights in specific scenarios; the Series B shares (which is the more liquid, more widely traded class) are what most institutional investors hold
- This is not a red flag in the Snap/Alphabet control-concentration sense, but it does mean that a hostile takeover or activist campaign would face structural friction from the A-share ownership concentration; Orion Foundation and major Finnish institutional holders dominate A-share ownership
- Verdict on dual-class: Governance nuance requiring awareness rather than alarm; it means management is partially insulated from activist pressure — which cuts both ways (stability vs entrenchment)

Founder control:
- Orion is not founder-controlled in the modern sense; the Orion Foundation (established from historical ownership) and major Finnish pension funds (Varma, Ilmarinen, OP) are the significant shareholders
- No individual founder-family owner with blocking control; ownership is institutional-concentrated Finnish, which is distinct from either founder-controlled or fully dispersed UK/US ownership

Related-party transactions:
- Annual report related-party disclosures: no material related-party transactions of concern visible in governance statements L3Y; standard board member / senior officer compensation is disclosed; no advisory contracts or service agreements with board members or their affiliated entities at unusual scale
- Orion Foundation holdings create a governance relationship between Foundation and listed entity, but Foundation's role is passive shareholding rather than operational involvement

Audit history:
- Auditor: KPMG Finland (long-standing auditor); no auditor change in recent years
- No material weaknesses disclosed (Orion reports under Finnish GAAP/IFRS with standard audit opinion)
- No restatements visible in the public record L5Y
- Audit fee relative to non-audit fee: Orion's annual report discloses audit vs non-audit fees to KPMG; ratio appears appropriate (audit >> non-audit), consistent with auditor independence norms

Regulatory/legal issues:
- Finnish pharma regulatory profile: subject to Fimea (Finnish Medicines Agency) oversight; no visible Fimea enforcement actions
- EU pharmaceutical regulatory: EMA interactions for pipeline products are standard; no unusual regulatory disputes
- No class action (Finnish legal system does not support US-style class action mechanisms; EU equivalents non-existent at this scale)
