Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What the company does. Judges Scientific (JDG-LON) is a serial acquirer of niche scientific and industrial instrument businesses, operating 25 largely autonomous subsidiaries across materials science, vacuum technology and marine geological coring
2.
How it makes money. The group buys small, defensible niche manufacturers at 3 to 7 times EBIT and lets subsidiaries trade under decentralised management, retaining entrepreneurial cultures rather than imposing central integration
3.
Primary market and geography. Around 50% of historical group sales derive from US and global academic research demand, with the balance split across industrial, government and commercial instrumentation buyers internationally
4.
Segment mix. Materials Sciences generated GBP 71.9m and Vacuum generated GBP 73.9m of FY25 revenue; the Geotek marine coring business sits inside the group and drives material period-to-period earnings volatility
5.
Financial character, revenue scale. FY25 group revenue was GBP 145.8m, up 9.1% year on year, though organic growth excluding the Geotek coring contribution decelerated to just 2%
6.
Financial character, margin profile. Adjusted operating margin compressed from 20.9% in FY24 to 19.2% in FY25, with Shore Capital modelling further compression to 17.6% in FY26F
7.
Financial character, balance sheet. Adjusted net debt fell to GBP 42.6m (1.5x adjusted EBITDA) from GBP 51.7m, backed by a refinanced GBP 140m banking facility running through 2028
8.
Cash generation. Adjusted cash conversion reached 118% in FY25 (136% excluding Geotek prepayments), among the strongest in the peer set, though working capital bloated to 16% of sales versus a 10% historical target
9.
Investment-style implications. The case reads as a quality compounder passing through a cyclical / policy-driven trough: the long-run 19% revenue CAGR (2006-2025) and buy-and-build discipline are intact, but FY26 is explicitly guided as a trough year
