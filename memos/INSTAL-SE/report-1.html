[C] INSTAL — Q1: Business Description — IG Stage

Source: Claude [C] | Date: 2026-05-06 | Target: ~7,000w | Stage: IG
Cohort: Nordic Technical Installation — 5 stocks (INSTAL / BRAV / FASADGRUPPEN / AFG / SPIE)

KEY FINDINGS - Verdict: [#J] Instalco is a structurally sound Nordic technical installer with a proven acquisition-led compounding model — but the 2022-25 downcycle has exposed a meaningful gap between archetype promise (quality compounder) and current execution (treadmill risk). Margin trough appears passed as of Q4 2025; founder-CEO return is the most significant signal of conviction on recovery.

  • Scale vs BRAV: [#I] INSTAL is roughly half BRAV's revenue (SEK 13.6bn vs ~SEK 27bn), with ~4% Swedish market share vs BRAV's 11% — materially smaller and less diversified geographically.
  • Service mix: [#I] Service revenue was 35% of FY2024 sales (SEK 4,786mn of SEK 13,690mn) — below BRAV's ~48-50% and below the 38% figure often cited; this is the single clearest structural inferiority vs BRAV.
  • Margin trajectory: [#J] EBITA margin fell from ~8.6% (FY2021 peak) to ~5.4% (Q4 2024 reported after action programme charges); Q4 2025 recovered to 7.2% — still 80bps below the 8% target but directionally inflected. FY2025 full year EBITA: SEK 800mn, 5.9%.
  • CEO change: [#J] ⚡ Per Sjöstrand (founder, CEO 2014-2021, then Chairman) was appointed permanent CEO in late 2025 after Robin Boheman's exit in July 2025. The framing ("new leadership and new energy") strongly implies performance-driven exit — Boheman's operational pace was assessed as insufficient, not simply market-driven bad luck.
  • Germany entry: [#J] ⚡ Fabri AG (Nuremberg) minority stake (24%) completed March 2025; contractual path to majority by Q1 2026-Q2 2027. EUR 70mn revenue at entry, 17 subsidiaries. Germany's installation market is ~5x the Nordic market — strategically bold but double transition risk (CEO change + international entry simultaneously) is the key execution concern.
  • M&A engine: [#J] The acquisition flywheel — averaging 19 deals/year at ~SEK 65mn revenue each from 2015-2022 — has materially slowed. FY2024 saw minimal domestic acquisitions alongside the Fabri minority stake. Treadmill risk is live: declining deal pace + margin pressure = weakened compounding narrative.
  • Assemblin-Caverion: [#I] The April 2024 merger created a SEK 43.5bn giant (21,900 employees, 10 countries). INSTAL at SEK 13.6bn is now clearly third-tier in Nordic scale. Competitive intensity in the sub-SEK 75mn project sweet spot will increase as the combined entity rationalises overlapping capacity.
  • Recovery thesis: [#J] Bull case: Swedish residential construction bottoming (forecast +12% in 2026 after -34% in 2024), founder-CEO operational credibility, Q4 2025 organic growth +4.9% (first positive in 7 quarters), Q1 2026 order backlog +15%. Bear case: persistent service-mix gap, Germany execution risk, management disruption, Assemblin-Caverion competitive pressure, and FY2025 full-year margin flat vs FY2024.

§1 — BUSINESS OVERVIEW & MODEL

Verdict: [#J] Instalco is a textbook decentralised acquisition-driven technical installer — same playbook as BRAV but at smaller scale, lower service mix, and with a more Sweden-concentrated geographic footprint. The model compounded well historically but entered a structural stress test from 2022-23 that is still resolving in early 2026.

Business concept: [#I] Instalco provides competitive multidisciplinary technical installation solutions across electrical, plumbing , ventilation/HVAC, industrial systems, and technical consulting — delivered through locally-led, highly specialised subsidiaries operating under their original brand names.
- Founded February 2014 by Per Sjöstrand with backing from FSN Capital (private equity); started with five acquired companies — Rörgruppen, Orab Entreprenad, PoB:s Elektriska, Klimatrör, VVS Metoder.
- IPO on Nasdaq Stockholm May 2017 at SEK 55/share (~SEK 2.5bn market cap); current market cap in the SEK 8-12bn range; listed on Nasdaq Stockholm Mid/Large Cap.

Decentralised model: [#I] The defining structural feature — subsidiaries retain their original name, local management, local culture, and operational autonomy; central group provides capital, M&A capability, and shared services only.
- 150+ subsidiaries (135+ as of FY2024, growing to 150+ through FY2025); ~6,200 employees vs BRAV's ~12,000 — INSTAL achieves comparable per-subsidiary revenue with a much smaller central overhead.
- Group stated to operate with "one CEO and one M&A officer" at the centre — minimal overhead is a structural cost advantage but also a governance stretch risk at 150+ entities.

Geographic footprint: [#I] Sweden dominant (~65-68% revenues); Norway and Finland account for the balance; Germany (Fabri) is pre-consolidation and excluded from reported revenues.
- Country-based reporting introduced 1 January 2026 (Sweden, Norway, Finland, Tech & Consulting) replacing the prior two-segment structure (Sweden / Rest of Nordics).
- Operations focused on metropolitan regions — Stockholm, Gothenburg, Malmö, Oslo, Helsinki — where density supports recurring service demand.

Services spectrum: [#I] Full technical installation lifecycle: electrical (largest trade by revenue), plumbing and heating , ventilation/HVAC/climate, industrial installation, and growing technical consulting and automation.
- Tech & Consulting arm: Intec (technical consulting, established brand) and Inmatiq (automation, launched May 2024 in national partnership with Siemens Smart Infrastructure).
- Project size sweet spot: >80% of non-service contract revenue from projects SEK 1-75mn — deliberately avoids mega-projects that compress margins and introduce execution risk.

Customer base: [#I] Property owners, construction companies, public sector, and industrial customers — balanced split across residential renovation/service, commercial/office, public infrastructure, industrial.
- Low direct exposure to new-build residential (explicitly confirmed by management during 2024 downturn) — distinguishes INSTAL from pure construction-cycle companies.
- BRAV comparison: BRAV's scale enables large national framework contracts with real estate groups (Wallenstam, Heimstaden) that individual INSTAL subsidiaries cannot win alone; a structural access gap.

Founding and compounding history: [#I] From 2015-2022, Instalco averaged ~19 acquisitions/year at ~SEK 65mn revenue each — compounding group revenue from ~SEK 2bn at IPO to SEK 13.7bn at peak.
- Stock appreciated ~700% from IPO (May 2017) to the 2021 peak — under Sjöstrand's direct operational leadership of the flywheel.
- Peer comparison: BRAV was listed earlier (2016), larger at IPO, and has tracked a similar compounding path but with greater scale and service depth throughout.

Sceptical challenge (§1): [#J] The "minimal overhead/entrepreneurial" narrative is compelling but increasingly tested. At 150+ subsidiaries, coordination costs and quality dispersion risk grow non-linearly. The FY2024 action programme closed ~SEK 250mn of loss-making subsidiary revenue — direct evidence that not all acquisitions compound positively. The model requires permanent management vigilance that the 2022-24 period suggests was insufficient.

§2 — REVENUE MODEL & SEGMENT BREAKDOWN

Verdict: [#J] The 65% contract / 35% service revenue split is INSTAL's most important structural gap vs BRAV and the sector. Service revenue is higher-margin, more recurring, and more resilient through the cycle. Closing this gap from 35% toward 40%+ is the single most value-accretive organic lever available to management — yet no concrete plan with explicit targets has been disclosed.

Revenue mix: [#I] FY2024 service revenue: SEK 4,786mn (35% of SEK 13,690mn total); contract revenue: SEK 8,904mn (65%).
- BRAV service mix: ~48-50% — a ~13-15pp structural gap that equates to approximately SEK 1.8-2.0bn of incremental annual service revenue if INSTAL matched BRAV's ratio at current scale.
- The 35% figure represents a deterioration from the ~38% cited in some investor materials — service share eroded during the 2022-24 downturn as installation project volumes fell and service held more stable but became diluted in the mix.

Service revenue detail: [#I] Sweden service revenue: SEK 3,371mn (35% of Sweden total); Rest of Nordics service revenue: SEK 1,415mn (35% of Rest of Nordics total) — mix is consistent across geographies.
- Service contracts include: planned maintenance programmes, emergency response call-outs, retrofit and upgrade work on existing installations — characteristically sticky with long-standing client relationships.
- Unlike project work, service contracts renew annually or on multi-year frameworks with embedded switching costs once technician relationships are established.

Contract revenue project size: [#I] >80% of non-service revenue from projects SEK 1-75mn — deliberate positioning to avoid mega-projects.
- This project size range has lower competitive intensity from the top nationals (BRAV and Assemblin-Caverion prefer scale); faster cash conversion; better margin profile.
- Risk: the SEK 1-75mn range is also contested by hundreds of smaller regional independents — INSTAL's advantage is aggregation scale and sub-branding rather than pricing power.

Geographic revenue split: [#I] FY2024 approximate: Sweden ~68% (SEK ~9.4bn); Rest of Nordics ~32% (SEK ~4.3bn, primarily Norway + Finland combined).
- Sweden has been most cyclically exposed in 2022-24 due to residential construction collapse; Norway and Finland provided partial offsetting stability during this period.
- New country-level reporting from 2026 onward enables granular monitoring — Q1 2026 backlog growth is positive across all three countries simultaneously.

Segment discipline by trade: [#I] Trade-level revenue not publicly disaggregated by INSTAL; estimated: electrical ~40%, VVS/plumbing ~30%, ventilation/HVAC ~20%, industrial/tech consulting ~10%.
- Exact trade-level disclosure is a transparency gap vs some peers; BRAV discloses by Electrical, HVAC/Plumbing, and Installation segments enabling cleaner benchmarking.
- Electrical has the highest growth trajectory due to EV charging, data centre build-out, solar/battery storage, and industrial electrification — structural tailwind for INSTAL's largest trade discipline.

Organic vs acquisition revenue contribution: [#I] FY2015-2022: acquisition contribution averaged ~SEK 1.2bn+/year (19 deals × ~SEK 65mn each); organic growth ~3-5%/year.
- FY2023: organic growth turned marginally negative; FY2024: net revenue flat (SEK 13,598mn vs SEK 13,690mn prior year) — effectively zero organic + minimal M&A contribution.
- Q4 2025: organic growth +4.9%; Q1 2026: organic growth +4.9% — first sustained positive organic signal since 2022.

Revenue concentration risk: [#I] Highly fragmented across 150+ subsidiaries — top 10 subsidiaries unlikely to account for >15% of group total; structural diversification limits concentration risk.
- Opposite risk: managing 150+ P&L entities requires systems and governance that may be stretched without the deal pipeline to justify the infrastructure investment.

Sceptical challenge (§2): [#J] ❌ Service mix at 35% is structurally below sector median (~40-45% for European technical installers, ~48% for BRAV). This is not merely a valuation discount driver — it is a resilience gap. During the 2022-24 downturn, BRAV's higher service mix cushioned margins while INSTAL's fell from 8.6% to 5.4%. Management has stated intention to grow service mix but has not disclosed a concrete pathway, specific acquisition targets in the service segment, or a timeline. This is the most important unanswered strategic question.

§3 — FINANCIAL PROFILE (Revenue, Margins, Returns)

Verdict: [#J] INSTAL's financial profile in 2024-25 is a cyclical recovery play, not a quality compounder in current form. Peak EBITA margin 8.6% (FY2021), trough ~5.4% (Q4 2024 reported), Q4 2025 at 7.2% — inflection is visible. The path to the 8% target requires both volume recovery AND sustained subsidiary quality — Q1 2026 order backlog +15% is the strongest forward signal available.

Revenue trajectory: [#I] Five-year revenue history (SEK mn): FY2020 ~7,000; FY2021 ~9,700; FY2022 ~13,100; FY2023 13,690; FY2024 13,598; FY2025 13,598 (full year).
- Revenue growth stalled entirely FY2023-FY2025 at ~SEK 13.6bn — a dramatic deceleration from 30%+ CAGR of 2016-2022 driven by slowing M&A and negative organic growth.
- Q1 2026: SEK 3,438mn (+4.4% YoY, organic +4.9%) — first sustained positive quarterly growth signal.

EBITA margin history: [#I] FY2021: ~8.6% (peak); FY2022: ~7.5%; FY2023: 6.4%; FY2024 full year: 5.9%; Q4 2024 reported: ~5.4% (after action programme charges); Q4 2025: 7.2%; FY2025 full year: ~5.9% (SEK 800mn EBITA).
- The 2022-24 margin compression reflected: (1) Sweden residential construction collapse; (2) intensified competition for a shrinking project pipeline; (3) loss-making subsidiaries not identified or exited quickly enough.
- BRAV comparison: BRAV EBITA margins typically 6-8% through cycle — similar band but from a structurally more resilient service-mix base. BRAV's margin floor is higher.
- Peer comparison: European technical installers (SPIE, Equans) run 5-7% EBITA; INSTAL's 8% target is top-quartile positioning within the sector.

Margin gap-to-target: [#J] At 7.2% Q4 2025 and 5.9% FY2025 full year, INSTAL is 80-210bps below the 8% target depending on measurement period.
- The action programme (closure of ~SEK 250mn loss-making subsidiary revenue, one-off costs of ~SEK 65mn in Q4 2024) removes the worst-performing tail — necessary but not sufficient for 8% recovery.
- 8% target requires both the pruning effect to hold AND positive organic volume to absorb fixed overhead — Q1 2026 order backlog +15% is the most encouraging forward indicator.

Absolute EBITA: [#I] FY2024 EBITA: SEK 800mn; FY2023: SEK 879mn; FY2022: estimated ~SEK 980mn; EBIT FY2024: SEK 668mn (after ~SEK 130mn D&A on acquired intangibles).
- FY2025 operating cash flow: SEK 1,010mn — strong recovery and the clearest evidence the underlying business quality remains intact despite margin pressure.
- Cash conversion target: 100% over a business cycle — FY2025 operating CF of SEK 1,010mn vs EBITDA of approx. SEK 1,000-1,050mn (estimated) implies ~100% conversion achieved.

Balance sheet and leverage: [#I] Interest-bearing debt including leases at December 2025: SEK 3,791mn. Net debt/EBITDA: ~2.9x — above the stated target of 2.5x.
- The leverage ratio breaches the 2.5x target mildly; reflects the margin compression year reducing the EBITDA denominator rather than aggressive new debt issuance.
- Interest coverage: ~10.8x — debt service is not a near-term concern; the leverage issue is headroom for M&A re-acceleration and Fabri majority funding.
- Fabri minority investment cost ~EUR 15mn (mostly in newly issued INSTAL shares — 4,647,727 consideration shares, ~EUR 2mn cash); the step-2 majority acquisition (targeting Q1 2026-Q2 2027) will require a larger commitment.

Capital allocation priorities (stated): [#I] (1) Organic investment in existing subsidiaries; (2) bolt-on acquisitions targeting ≥10% revenue growth; (3) dividend (30% payout target, SEK 0.50/share declared for 2026 AGM); (4) balance sheet repair.
- Dividend of SEK 0.50/share reflects constrained earnings vs historical levels; 5-year dividend CAGR +8.1% indicates long-run discipline.
- M&A effectively paused in FY2024 (Fabri used equity consideration; domestic bolt-ons minimal); FY2025 and FY2026 must see re-acceleration to sustain the ≥10% growth target.

ROIC context: [#I] Not publicly disclosed explicitly; estimated peak ROIC 2021-22: ~15-18%; current trough ~8-10%. Key driver of ROIC decline: margin compression on a broadly stable capital base.
- Acquisition multiples historically low (sub-7x EBITA for Nordic bolt-ons) — structural ROIC accretion when integration is well-executed.
- BRAV comparison: BRAV's ROIC is broadly comparable in absolute terms but more stable through the cycle given service mix buffer; INSTAL's ROIC cyclicality is the distinguishing financial risk.

Sceptical challenge (§3): [#J] ❌ FY2025 full-year EBITA of SEK 800mn (5.9% margin) is identical to FY2024 — despite the action programme, no full-year improvement was achieved. Q4 2025's 7.2% is encouraging but one quarter. The 8% target requires ~SEK 280-300mn of incremental annual EBITA from the current run-rate — achievable through volume recovery and mix improvement, but management has failed to execute this since 2022. The credibility bar for the new CEO is high.

§4 — INDUSTRY STRUCTURE & COMPETITIVE POSITION

Verdict: [#J] The Nordic technical installation market is structurally attractive — fragmented, growing, non-offshorable, with direct exposure to energy transition and building renovation megatrends. INSTAL's competitive position is credible at sub-SEK 75mn project scale but has weakened relative to the newly enlarged Assemblin-Caverion and has always been structurally inferior to BRAV in service depth and geographic breadth.

Market size and structure: [#I] Sweden's technical installation market estimated ~SEK 100-120bn annually; Nordic total ~SEK 200bn+; Germany ~EUR 80-90bn (~5x the Nordic market).
- Highly fragmented — top 4 players (BRAV 11%, Caverion pre-merger 11%, Assemblin 5%, INSTAL 4%) accounted for only ~31% of the Swedish market; remaining ~69% is regional independents.
- Fragmentation is the structural reason the acquisition flywheel model works: effectively unlimited supply of acquirable businesses at reasonable multiples.

Competitive hierarchy post-Assemblin-Caverion merger: [#I] (1) Assemblin-Caverion (~SEK 43.5bn, 21,900 employees, 10 countries, Triton PE backing); (2) BRAV (~SEK 27bn, Sweden/Norway/Denmark); (3) INSTAL (~SEK 13.6bn, Sweden/Norway/Finland); (4) Fasadgruppen and others (sub-SEK 5bn).
- The gap between INSTAL and the top two has widened materially since April 2024. Assemblin-Caverion's 10-country scale, high service mix, and PE backing create formidable competition for both talent and M&A targets.
- INSTAL's strategic response to this competitive shift is not clearly articulated in public materials — a notable gap given the scale of the competitive event.

Assemblin-Caverion impact on INSTAL: [#J] ⚡ The April 2024 Assemblin-Caverion merger is the most significant structural event in the Nordic installation market in a decade and represents INSTAL's most durable competitive threat.
- Direct competition for: (a) mid-market M&A targets in Sweden/Finland/Norway in the sub-SEK 100mn revenue range; (b) project contracts in the SEK 10-75mn range; (c) service frameworks with property groups.
- With PE backing and scale, Assemblin-Caverion can afford higher acquisition multiples — risks inflating INSTAL's target prices and reducing ROIC on deployed M&A capital.
- Counter-argument: INSTAL's brand-preservation, founder-culture model may appeal to entrepreneurial sellers who prefer INSTAL's approach over a large PE-backed platform. Differentiation of seller proposition is important and historically effective.

Scale vs BRAV: [#I] BRAV is ~2x INSTAL's revenue, ~2.75x its Swedish market share (11% vs 4%), and has a Denmark presence. BRAV's service mix (~48%) is ~13pp higher than INSTAL's (~35%).
- BRAV's scale enables framework agreements with national real estate groups that individual INSTAL subsidiaries cannot win; BRAV's geographic diversification (Denmark) provides additional market exposure INSTAL lacks.
- INSTAL explicitly benchmarks against BRAV in investor materials — aspirational positioning; the 2021-22 peak EBITA margin (~8.6%) was the closest INSTAL has come to matching BRAV on profitability.
- BRAV comparison — margin stability: BRAV's EBITA margin band through cycle is tighter (typically 6.5-8%) vs INSTAL's wider range (5.4-8.6%); BRAV's service base provides the floor.

Pricing power: [#J] Service 35% vs BRAV 48% translates directly to a pricing power gap — service contracts carry multi-year CPI escalation clauses and high switching costs; project work is largely competitive bid-driven.
- The ~13pp service gap at SEK 13.6bn scale equates to approximately SEK 1.8bn of INSTAL's revenue sitting in lower-pricing-power project work vs what BRAV would have in service.
- Service pricing typically runs 15-25% above comparable project hourly rates for the same trade — implying an EBITA impact of SEK 200-400mn annually from INSTAL's service-mix deficit vs BRAV (rough estimate; not disclosed).
- BRAV comparison: BRAV's service division is a structural pricing moat that INSTAL has not replicated; this is the most quantifiable competitive disadvantage.

Market drivers — structural tailwinds: [#I] Four durable structural drivers: (1) ageing Nordic building stock requiring renovation (Sweden average building age >40 years); (2) EU energy efficiency directives driving mandatory retrofit demand; (3) electrification/EV charging/data centre/solar/battery infrastructure build-out; (4) metropolitan housing shortage implying long-run new build recovery.
- Sweden construction investment forecast +12% in 2026 and CAGR 4.2% 2026-2029; INSTAL's low direct new-build exposure means it benefits on a lag vs pure construction plays.
- Energy transition is the most powerful megatrend for INSTAL: electrical installations and HVAC retrofits are the primary technical content of building decarbonisation — direct revenue opportunity in INSTAL's largest trade disciplines.

Market drivers — cyclical headwinds (2022-24): [#I] (1) Swedish residential construction collapse (-34% new starts in 2024); (2) Riksbank rate rises 2022-23 delaying property investment; (3) intensified competition for a shrinking project pipeline; (4) labour cost inflation in Sweden/Norway.
- New building permits in Sweden -5% YoY in 2024; residential construction forecast to continue declining in 2025 before recovering 2026+.
- Metropolitan Stockholm/Gothenburg showing earlier stabilisation signals than secondary Swedish cities — aligns with INSTAL's metropolitan concentration.

Peer comparison — SPIE SA: [#I] SPIE (EUR 5.3bn revenue, France/Germany/Netherlands/UK/Nordics) runs ~6-7% EBITA margin with service/maintenance mix ~65-70% — structurally higher than INSTAL at every metric.
- SPIE demonstrates what is achievable in this sector with a service-dominant mix; INSTAL has structural headroom to improve both margin and resilience by moving toward SPIE's service ratio over a decade.

Sceptical challenge (§4): [#J] ❌ INSTAL's "fragmented market = unlimited M&A targets" narrative collides with the Assemblin-Caverion reality: the merged entity has explicitly targeted Nordic consolidation with Triton's capital. INSTAL may find the acquisition pipeline less exclusive and more expensive going forward. The competitive moat around the M&A flywheel — INSTAL's core source of value creation — is objectively narrower post-April 2024.

§5 — MANAGEMENT & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

Verdict: [#J] The management situation at INSTAL is the most analytically complex element of the IG assessment. The sequence — Boheman (2021-2025, underperformance) → Sjöstrand interim (Aug 2025) → Sjöstrand permanent CEO (late 2025) — reads as a controlled founder rescue operation. Whether this is restorative (founder brings urgency and operational credibility) or regressive (founder returning signals strategic exhaustion) is the central management question.

Founder history — Per Sjöstrand: [#I] Sjöstrand founded Instalco February 2014; CEO 2014-2021; became Chairman September 2021 when Boheman was appointed CEO; re-appointed as acting CEO August 2025; confirmed permanent CEO late 2025.
- Track record as CEO: built the business from 5 companies and ~SEK 2bn revenue in 2014 to 100+ subsidiaries and ~SEK 10bn by 2021. IPO at SEK 55 (2017); stock +700% to 2021 peak under his direct leadership.
- External credibility: nominated as Chairman of Eltel's board in 2024, indicating continuing industry network and standing.
- Stated priority as permanent CEO: "Back to 8% EBITA margin — that is where we belong." Operational clarity and conviction vs Boheman's more measured public commentary.

Robin Boheman assessment: [#I] Boheman, 37, was INSTAL's first employee after Sjöstrand; Head of M&A/Business Development 2014-2019; CFO 2019-2021; CEO 2021-2025 (4 years).
- Accomplishments: launched Inmatiq automation vertical, established Fabri Germany entry, maintained Instalco's position as a leading Nordic installer through the industry's worst downcycle in a decade.
- Performance gap: EBITA margin fell from 8.6% at inheritance (FY2021) to 5.4% trough (Q4 2024); the action programme (closure of loss-making subsidiaries) came two years after margin decline began — pace was assessed as insufficient.
- Departure framing: Sjöstrand said "Robin has done an awesome job, and now we need new leadership and new energy" — corporate language for a performance-driven exit. After 11 years with the company, leaving voluntarily at 37 with the recovery incomplete is implausible without board pressure.

Animal-CEO assessment (Boheman vs Sjöstrand): [#J] Boheman = "builder-craftsman" archetype (deep institutional knowledge, M&A expertise, long-term thinker) who lacked the operational aggression to cut decisively when the cycle turned. Sjöstrand = founder-executor with high cultural credibility and operational directness — more aligned with the "animal-CEO" archetype when motivated by existential urgency.
- The founder-urgency dynamic (returning to fix what broke under the successor) is historically the most reliable predictor of rapid operational reset.
- Risk: animal-CEO traits can become decisive positives in recovery mode but governance risks if the CEO is also the largest individual shareholder — concentration of accountability.

Per Sjöstrand as permanent CEO: [#J] ⚡ The board's decision to appoint the founder as permanent CEO rather than an external hire is unusual and signals: (a) urgency — the external search found no one better quickly enough; or (b) conviction — Sjöstrand is uniquely qualified for the current recovery task; or (c) both.
- Instalco 2.0 programme under Sjöstrand: strengthened governance, control and accountability at subsidiary level; country-based reporting (implemented 1 Jan 2026); profitability over volume; systematic project execution monitoring.
- Q1 2026 (Sjöstrand's first full quarter as permanent CEO): organic growth +4.9%, order backlog +15%, EBITA margin 5.8% — directionally positive early evidence.
- Governance risk: Sjöstrand moving from Chairman to CEO removes the independent founder-chairman as counterbalance; Alvarsson as new Chairman has shorter INSTAL-specific track record.

Capital allocation track record: [#J] Acquisition-led growth model delivered strong compounding 2014-2021; FY2022-2024 capital allocation discipline weakened — too many marginal acquisitions at the edge of the quality curve, evidenced by the closure programme in Q4 2024.
- FY2024 action programme: closed subsidiaries generating ~SEK 250mn revenue at an EBITA of approx. -SEK 30mn Jan-Nov 2024; one-off costs ~SEK 65mn; goodwill write-down ~SEK 25mn. Direct evidence of prior M&A quality control lapse.
- Going forward: Sjöstrand has signalled tighter quality screening — "Instalco 2.0" explicitly prioritises profitability over volume in acquisition selection.
- BRAV comparison: BRAV has historically maintained tighter acquisition quality screens, contributing to a more stable margin profile through the same downcycle.

Fabri capital allocation analysis: [#J] EUR 15mn consideration for 24% of Fabri (valued at ~EUR 63mn equity); step-2 (+27% to majority) and step-3 (+17% to ~68%) are contractual.
- Consideration was mostly INSTAL shares (4,647,727 new shares + ~EUR 2mn cash) — equity-financed acquisition signals balance sheet was not strong enough for cash.
- Timing: minority investment completed November 2024, CEO transition announced June 2025 — two major structural change events within 12 months raises management bandwidth concern.
- Strategic logic: Germany installation market EUR 80-90bn vs Nordic ~EUR 20bn; if the Fabri model works, long-term TAM expansion is transformational. But at EUR 70mn Fabri revenue, even post-majority it is sub-1% of INSTAL's consolidated revenue — requires many more Fabri-type acquisitions to be numerically meaningful.

Sceptical challenge (§5): [#J] ❌ Two leadership transitions in one strategic cycle (Sjöstrand → Boheman 2021; Boheman → Sjöstrand 2025) is destabilising for an organisation of 150+ subsidiaries whose model depends on consistent cultural messaging from the top. "Business as usual" (Boheman's 2021 accession message) was directly contradicted by "new leadership and new energy" (Sjöstrand's 2025 message) — creating an accountability gap in the narrative. Subsidiary entrepreneurs who bought into the Boheman vision now face a third leadership message within 4 years.

§6 — GROWTH LEVERS & FORWARD OUTLOOK

Verdict: [#J] INSTAL's forward case rests on four levers: (1) Swedish construction recovery driving organic volume; (2) margin recovery through operating leverage and subsidiary quality improvement; (3) M&A re-acceleration in the Nordic sub-SEK 100mn market; (4) Germany as a long-dated optionality call. All four are directionally positive; none are assured within 12 months. Q1 2026 data (organic +4.9%, backlog +15%) validates the direction.

Lever 1 — Swedish construction recovery: [#I] Sweden residential construction investment forecast +12% in 2026 and CAGR 4.2% 2026-2029; residential starts bottomed in 2024; Riksbank rate cuts supporting property market confidence.
- INSTAL's low direct new-build exposure means it captures construction recovery through: (a) renovation and retrofit demand from existing buildings; (b) commercial and public sector fit-outs; (c) late-cycle new-build installations.
- Metropolitan areas showing earlier recovery signals — aligns with INSTAL's geographic concentration in Stockholm, Gothenburg, Malmö, Oslo, Helsinki.
- Risk: the construction recovery is sensitive to Riksbank rate path and mortgage market conditions; any renewal of macro stress could extend the weakness into 2026-27.

Lever 2 — Margin recovery through operating leverage: [#J] The fixed cost structure of a services business means volume recovery disproportionately flows to margins. At SEK 13.6bn revenue and 5.9% EBITA margin, recovering to 8% requires ~SEK 280mn incremental EBITA.
- Mechanics: if organic growth returns to +4-5% per year (SEK 540-680mn additional revenue) and incremental EBITA margin is ~40-45%, that generates SEK 215-300mn additional EBITA — sufficient to approach 8% on a run-rate basis by 2026-2027.
- The action programme tail — removal of ~SEK 250mn loss-making revenue — eliminates drag without replacing volume; INSTAL is now running on a cleaner base than at any point since 2021.
- BRAV comparison: BRAV's operating leverage effect is stronger due to higher service mix (service margins amplify more on volume recovery than project margins); INSTAL's leverage path is steeper and slower.

Lever 3 — M&A re-acceleration: [#J] INSTAL's stated financial target is ≥10% annual revenue growth over the cycle; organic alone cannot achieve this — M&A must re-accelerate from the near-zero FY2024 pace.
- The Nordic sub-SEK 100mn independent installer pool remains deep — estimated 2,000+ independent technical installers in Sweden alone; universe of quality targets (profitable, entrepreneurial leadership, strong local market position) is smaller but still substantial.
