KEY FINDINGS - Overall verdict: SES management guidance credibility is MEDIUM, trending toward LOW — the L4-period record shows a deteriorating accuracy pattern, with revenue repeatedly over-guided and EBITDA margin assumptions that depend on a satellite-utilisation recovery management cannot fully control [#J]

  • Beat/miss asymmetry: Revenue guidance has been missed in 3 of the last 4 reported periods, averaging a –4.2% shortfall versus initial guidance; EBITDA beats have partially offset this, but the mechanism is cost-cutting rather than top-line strength, which is structurally less durable [#A]
  • Sandbagging absent: Management does not exhibit systematic conservative guidance; the over-guidance on revenue reflects genuine forecast difficulty in a contracting GEO satellite market, not deliberate sandbagging — this removes a key comfort that current guidance is achievable [#J]
  • Medium-term credibility gap: The N3Y revenue stabilisation / modest-growth case depends on MEO/LEO managed-services ramp (O3b mPOWER) and government/defence contract wins that are behind original schedule; early-delivery slippage is already embedded in the L4 record [#A]
  • Sell-side alignment: Consensus sits below management's medium-term revenue targets for FY26-FY27; the SS community has been cutting estimates for six consecutive half-year periods, reflecting accumulated scepticism about the managed-services inflection timeline [#A]
  • Key assumption fragility: The single most fragile assumption in current guidance is the O3b mPOWER utilisation ramp — management models a step-change in throughput revenue in H2-FY25/FY26; any further delay or softer-than-expected take-up directly impairs the EPS trajectory [#J]
  • EPS sensitivity: A 2pp revenue growth miss translates to approximately €0.08–0.12 EPS drag at current leverage/cost structure — meaningful against consensus EPS for FY26 in the €0.35–0.45 range; margin of safety is low [#A]
  • Credibility verdict: MEDIUM — management has maintained transparent disclosure and adjusted guidance promptly when missing, limiting credibility damage, but the pattern of optimistic initial targets is persistent and the medium-term targets carry the same structural risks [#J]
  • Monitoring priority: O3b mPOWER throughput and backlog disclosures are the single highest-signal leading indicator; a flat or declining backlog in the next two reporting periods would constitute an early-warning trigger for thesis reassessment [#J]

§1 — Current Management Guidance

Management has issued quantitative medium-term targets anchored to O3b mPOWER ramp and managed-services growth; the guidance is more directional than pinpoint, with explicit FY-level ranges for revenue and EBITDA. [#J]

  • J-front verdict: Current guidance is net optimistic relative to trajectory — FY25 revenue range implies stabilisation that the L4-period trend has not yet confirmed; EBITDA margin target is achievable but dependent on a utilisation ramp that is behind original timeline [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Management guides FY25 revenue of approximately €1.85–1.95bn, implying flat-to-modest growth from FY24 actuals of ~€1.90bn; this is the narrowest the guidance band has been since FY22, suggesting higher management confidence [#A]

  • Sub: The midpoint €1.90bn implies zero organic growth vs FY24; analysts at [AS-Berenberg] and [AS-Deutsche Bank] note this is a de-risked bar relative to original FY25 targets set at the FY23 investor day (~€2.05bn)

  • FY current — EBITDA: EBITDA margin guided at 56–59%, consistent with FY24 reported 57.1%; guidance assumes stable video revenues declining at ~mid-single-digit and O3b mPOWER contribution offsetting [#A]

  • Sub: Absolute EBITDA implied €1.05–1.12bn; the width of the band reflects acknowledged uncertainty in O3b mPOWER ramp timing

  • FY current — EBIT: No explicit EBIT guidance provided; implied from EBITDA minus D&A run-rate of ~€700-720m gives EBIT ~€330–420m — management directs analysts to EBITDA as primary operating metric [#I]

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance issued for FY25; management references "growing adjusted EPS over the medium term" without a point estimate; this is consistent with the prior three years' practice and limits direct tracking [#I]

  • Sub: SS consensus FY25 adjusted EPS ~€0.38–0.42; management's refusal to guide EPS explicitly means any EPS narrative relies on SS-constructed estimates

  • FY current — FCF: Management guides FCF of €250–300m for FY25, down from FY24 actuals of ~€310m, reflecting elevated O3b mPOWER-related CapEx tail [#A]

  • Sub: FCF guidance has been a relatively stable anchor — this is the metric with the best historical accuracy (see §2)

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided at €750–800m for FY25, primarily O3b mPOWER satellite network completion and in-orbit servicing costs; represents a step-down from peak FY23 CapEx of ~€900m [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY26): Management targets revenue growth resuming at low-to-mid single digits from FY26, driven by O3b mPOWER utilisation exceeding 30% and government/defence contracts; no hard number published post FY24 results [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY27): EBITDA margin expansion toward 60–62% targeted by FY27, contingent on managed-services revenue mix shift — this was reiterated at the Nov-24 capital markets day [#A]

  • Sub: The FY27 margin target implies ~250–500bps expansion from current levels, an achievable range if O3b ramp proceeds on schedule

  • Source — earnings call: FY24 results call (Feb-25) and Q3-FY24 trading update (Nov-24) are the most recent guidance sources; investor day material (Nov-24) provides the medium-term framework [#I]

  • Update timing: Next formal update expected at H1-FY25 results (Aug-25); management has a pattern of narrowing the full-year band at the half-year mark [#I]

  • Caveat: All revenue guidance is stated organic, excluding any M&A or further portfolio disposals; management explicitly flagged currency sensitivity (USD/EUR and GBP/EUR material given ~55% USD-denominated contracts) [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: O3b mPOWER utilisation reaching 25–30% throughput by end-FY25 is embedded in the revenue midpoint; any delay pushes revenue toward the low end of the guided range [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The apparent de-risking of FY25 guidance (narrowed band, lower midpoint vs FY23 investor-day targets) could mask that management is merely anchoring to the already-impaired trajectory rather than identifying genuine demand recovery; the "flat-to-stable" framing obscures that the original growth thesis has been deferred by ~2 years

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

The pattern is neither consistent sandbagging nor chronic over-promising, but a recurring optimism bias on revenue that management corrects mid-year; EBITDA guidance is more balanced; the overall posture is "honest tracker with upward bias on top line." [#J]

  • J-front verdict: SES management is an honest tracker with a structural optimism bias on revenue; EBITDA managed more tightly; FCF guidance most reliable; no systematic sandbagging observed [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — revenue: Revenue initial guidance has exceeded actuals in 3 of last 4 full-year periods; the magnitude is modest (1–3%) but directionally consistent — this is a pattern, not noise [#A]

  • Sub: The optimism bias appears rooted in O3b mPOWER ramp assumptions that consistently run 6–12 months ahead of actual utilisation curves

  • Honest tracker — EBITDA: EBITDA guidance is balanced (2B/2M); management uses EBITDA as the primary investor communication metric and appears to set a more careful range for it; margin guidance is typically set as a range rather than a point estimate [#A]

  • Sandbagging signal: No evidence of systematic sandbagging; SES does not consistently guide below consensus and then beat — the revenue pattern is directionally opposite [#I]

  • Beat consistency — FCF: FCF is the only metric with a mild sandbagging pattern (2 beats vs 2 narrow misses, average +0.6%); management explicitly flags CapEx timing risk and appears to build modest conservatism into FCF guidance [#A]

  • Pattern shift — FY24: The FY24 guidance band was explicitly narrowed and lowered relative to FY23 investor-day targets — management described this as "reflecting current market visibility" rather than sandbagging; the pattern shift was communicative transparency, not guidance strategy change [#J]

  • Magnitude — miss: Revenue miss magnitude has been declining (FY23: –2.7%; FY24: –1.3%); this could signal improving accuracy or successively lower initial targets; the mechanism matters for interpreting current FY25 guidance [#J]

  • Mixed pattern — EBITDA FY23: FY23 EBITDA miss (–3.3%) is the largest single-metric miss in L4 periods; it coincided with the revenue shortfall and unexpected one-time restructuring charges (~€30m); the FY23 period is the worst credibility data point in the record [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: The trajectory from FY23 (worst period) to FY24 (better) is positive, but based on a shortened base period and lower targets — not confirmed as structural improvement [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The "declining miss magnitude" pattern is potentially an artefact of management successively resetting targets downward rather than improving forecast accuracy; if FY25 guidance was set at a conservative floor, a beat would reflect bar-lowering not genuine execution recovery; the pattern requires monitoring against the absolute revenue level, not just guidance-vs-actual variance

  • J-front verdict: Accuracy recovering from FY23 trough but structurally fragile — driven by target resets, not model improvement; accuracy is best for CapEx/FCF, weakest for revenue [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — FY24: Revenue miss –1.3%, EBITDA beat +0.6%, FCF miss –3.0% — overall the closest to guidance since FY22; suggests either improved model or lower bar [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FY23: FY23 was the weakest year — revenue –2.7%, EBITDA –3.3%, FCF –1.4%; all three primary metrics missed simultaneously; this is the benchmark for "bad performance" in the L4 record [#A]

  • Accuracy improving: The trajectory is improving: FY22 (broadly on track), FY23 (worst year), FY24 (recovering toward FY22 standard); if this holds, FY25 guidance should be achievable [#J]

  • Sub: However, the FY22 accuracy was partly FX-assisted; stripping FX, underlying accuracy improvement from FY23 to FY24 is more modest

  • Volatility driver — macro: USD/EUR FX is a recurring driver — USD strengthened ~8% in FY22 (aided beats), weakened modestly in FY23 (contributed to miss); FY25 guidance appears to embed ~1.07 EUR/USD assumption, which is broadly current market [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: O3b mPOWER delivery and utilisation ramp is the primary execution-driven accuracy risk; every revenue guidance miss since FY22 has had an O3b component — this is not resolved by FY25 guidance methodology [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The accuracy improvement from FY23 to FY24 is a low-base comparison; management set a lower FY24 target after the FY23 miss, making FY24 "accuracy" partly a statistical artefact; the true test of improved forecasting will be FY25 against the current stated range

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The guidance rests on three structurally interdependent assumptions: O3b mPOWER utilisation ramp, video revenue decline stabilisation, and government/defence contract wins — any single failure is manageable; simultaneous failure is not. [#J]

  • J-front verdict: Assumption stack is moderately credible for FY25 but fragile at N2Y/N3Y; the O3b utilisation assumption is the single most contestable variable, and sensitivity analysis shows it drives the majority of EPS variance [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer (O3b mPOWER): Management assumes O3b mPOWER commercial throughput reaches 25–30% utilisation by end-FY25, generating ~€100–140m incremental revenue in FY25; current reported utilisation as of Nov-24 was ~18% [#A]

  • Sub: The 7–12pp utilisation step required in ~8 months is achievable but requires committed contract ramp; as of Feb-25, backlog disclosures did not fully confirm this trajectory

  • Revenue assumption — pricing (video): Management assumes video (legacy GEO) revenue declines at ~mid-single-digit per annum; recent video attrition has been running at ~6–8% annually; if attrition accelerates to 10%+, revenue floor is lower than guided [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market (government): Government/defence revenue assumed flat-to-growing 5% annually; NATO/US DoD contracts are cited as pipeline; no specific contract wins required to hit FY25 guidance, but FY26 step-up depends on new awards [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance is organic; any disposal (e.g., further stake sales) or acquisition affects actuals vs guidance comparison; management has been a seller of assets (e.g., stake disposals in prior periods) [#I]

  • Margin assumption — cost: EBITDA margin guidance assumes personnel and network operation costs grow <2% annually; FY24 actuals confirmed this is achievable; the cost assumption is the most credible element of guidance [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage from O3b mPOWER incremental revenue (near-zero marginal cost once satellite in orbit) is the primary margin expansion mechanism; if utilisation ramp is delayed, margin leverage is deferred, not destroyed [#J]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Video pricing is under pressure; O3b mPOWER pricing is competitively contested by Starlink and OneWeb in some segments; management assumes stable-to-slightly-rising ASPs for O3b managed services, which is contestable given competitive entrant dynamics [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided ~20–22%; Luxembourg domicile and treaty network provide structural tax efficiency; this assumption is stable and low-risk [#I]

  • EPS assumption — share count: ~497m shares outstanding; no buyback programme active; dilution risk from management equity schemes is minor (~0.3–0.5% annually) [#I]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt ~€3.6bn post-FY24; interest costs ~€160–180m annually; management assumes no significant refinancing at materially higher rates in FY25 (most debt fixed-rate to maturity); this is a credible assumption given actual debt maturity profile [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: USD/EUR at ~1.07 embedded in FY25 guidance; every 0.05 USD/EUR move = approximately ±€30–40m revenue impact (55% USD contracts); currency is a meaningful swing factor [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Launch costs and satellite manufacturing are the primary commodity exposure; FY25 CapEx is mostly committed (satellite already in orbit); commodity exposure is minimal for the guidance period [#I]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

Overall guidance credibility is MEDIUM; management is transparent and adjusts promptly, but the L4-period pattern of revenue over-guidance is structural (O3b ramp optimism) and has not been resolved by methodology change — the same assumption drives FY25 guidance as drove FY23 misses. [#J]

  • J-front verdict: MEDIUM credibility — management maintains investor communication standards, adjusts guidance when evidence warrants, but has a documented pattern of over-optimism on the single most important revenue driver (O3b mPOWER); margin of safety on current guidance is low [#J]

  • Green flag — cost management: Cost discipline is genuinely impressive — three consecutive years of EBITDA margin at or above guidance floor despite revenue misses; management has repeatedly extracted cost savings to partially offset top-line shortfalls; this is a real positive signal [#A]

  • Green flag — FCF accuracy: FCF guidance is the most reliable metric across L4 periods; average variance <2%; useful for investors needing a stable FCF anchor [#A]

  • Green flag — prompt revision: Management has not maintained stale guidance when evidence contradicted it — FY23 miss was acknowledged promptly in the FY23 H1 update and FY24 guidance was reset accordingly; this is above-peer-standard disclosure behaviour [#J]

  • Red flag — O3b ramp recurrence: ❌ O3b mPOWER utilisation has been behind guidance in every period since launch; management has revised the ramp assumption forward three times (from original H2-FY23 inflection to now H2-FY25); the same optimism is embedded in FY25 targets [#J]

  • Red flag — medium-term target degradation: ❌ The FY25 revenue target has been cut from ~€2.05bn (FY23 investor-day) to €1.88–1.93bn (current guidance) — a ~€120–170m reduction over 18 months — representing one of the largest medium-term target downgrades in the European satellite sector in recent memory [#A]

  • Red flag — no EPS guidance: ❌ Management's refusal to provide EPS guidance prevents a direct credibility test on the bottom line; the thesis rests on EPS trajectory but the metric most relevant to Richard's IRR calculation is ungoverned by management targets [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — management deserves credit for cost discipline and transparent communication; the revenue credibility gap is structural and tied to a specific (O3b) assumption that has a documented miss history; until the ramp materialises in reported actuals, revenue guidance warrants a sceptical discount [#J]

  • Margin of safety — current: If FY25 guidance misses by –3% on revenue (consistent with FY23 magnitude), EPS lands ~€0.30–0.34 vs consensus €0.38–0.42 — a ~15–20% EPS shortfall; margin of safety is LOW at any multiple above 15x forward earnings [#J]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis depends on EPS growing from ~€0.38–0.42 (FY25) toward €0.55–0.65 (FY27/28); that trajectory requires the O3b ramp to proceed broadly on schedule; if it defers again, the IRR case breaks below 25% at current prices [#J]

  • Sceptical read: Apparent credibility on EBITDA/FCF may provide false comfort — investors who anchor on "management delivers on margins" without stress-testing the revenue assumption may be systematically underweighting the single variable most likely to impair EPS; the credibility verdict of MEDIUM specifically excludes comfort on the revenue line
