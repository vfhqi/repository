Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia represents a predictable cash flow story driven by self-help operational restructuring under new leadership, though its premium valuation relative to European peers increasingly discounts this positive turnaround.
1.
Predictable business model: Telia generates highly stable revenues primarily through subscription-based fixed and mobile connectivity services to 25 million subscribers in the Nordics and Baltics. This base provides a recurring revenue stream with high barriers to entry
2.
Compelling investment thesis: The primary bull case rests on Telia's return to predictable progressive dividend growth driven by aggressive cost reductions and improved capital discipline, which should expand free cash flow from SEK 9.3 billion in 2025 to over SEK 10.0 billion by 2027
3.
Critical swing factor: The single most important driver for earnings and valuation is the successful execution of management's country-led operating model, which has already reduced headcount by 25% since the start of 2024. This restructuring must offset persistent legacy revenue declines

Robust recent execution: Telia reported solid financial results for Q1 2026, with service revenue growing 2.1% and adjusted EBITDA rising 4.0% on a like-for-like basis, confirming that the turnaround plan remains on track

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia possesses a dominant digital infrastructure footprint in the Nordics and Baltics, translating into defensive, recurring service revenues with substantial barriers to entry and strong local pricing power.

Segment Operations and Core Geographies

1.
Integrated converged footprint: Telia Company operates as a premier integrated telecommunications provider across the hyper-digitalized Nordic and Baltic societies, delivering essential services that connect individuals, families, and businesses. The company has consolidated its regional focus, shifting away from historical Eurasian markets to establish leading positions in its core geographic footprint
2.
Swedish market dominance: Sweden constitutes Telia's primary operational engine, generating approximately 45% of total group revenues and serving as the bedrock of its profitability. In Sweden, Telia commands strong brand equity, with more than one-third of the population actively willing to pay a premium price for its superior connectivity
3.
Turnaround dynamics in Finland: Finland represents 18% of group revenue, where Telia faces intense competition from Elisa and Telenor's DNA brand. Telia's Finnish consumer business has historically struggled, suffering from mobile market share losses in the business-to-consumer segment for 39 consecutive quarters. To combat this, the group is aggressively migrating subscribers to high-margin 5G standalone networks and expanding its fiber-to-the-home footprint through the Valokuitunen joint venture
4.
Fixed network recovery in Norway: Norway accounts for 17% of revenue, where Telia's business has been hampered by the loss of a major mobile wholesale contract with ice. In response, management established a separate division for fixed broadband and TV in mid-2025 and agreed on a comprehensive mobile radio access network sharing JV with ice to improve structural network economics and reduce churn
5.
Baltic engine of profitability: The Baltic countries of Lithuania (7% of revenue) and Estonia (5% of revenue) represent highly profitable and technologically advanced markets where Telia holds leading market share. In Lithuania, Telia continues to deliver exceptionally strong service revenue and EBITDA growth, leveraging automated personalized pricing to expand ARPU without triggering customer dissatisfaction

Value Chain Economics and Durable Competitive Moat

6.
Predictable recurring subscription model: Telia's business model is anchored on subscription-based service revenues, which are recognized over time and provide highly predictable monthly cash inflows. These recurring services comprise mobile end-user revenue, fixed broadband, television streaming, and business enterprise solutions, with transactional equipment sales representing a lower-margin, volatile component
7.
Physical network asset moat: Telia sits in a highly profit-favored position within the digital value chain due to its deep ownership of physical digital infrastructure, including extensive fiber optic networks and mobile towers. This infrastructure is virtually impossible for new entrants to replicate, creating high entry barriers in its core markets
8.
Diverse defensive customer base: The group maintains low customer concentration, serving 25 million mobile, broadband, and TV subscriptions across its six geographic markets. This diversified base is split between residential consumers (54% of segment revenue) and enterprise customers (46%), sheltering the business from macroeconomic shocks
9.
Durable network quality advantage: The primary mechanism of Telia's competitive moat is its unrivalled network scale and premium brand equity, which has been independently validated by its Swedish mobile network being named the nation's best for six consecutive years. This superior network quality allows Telia to defend its premium pricing agenda and maintain low churn rates even in challenging economic environments
10.
Rational oligopolistic industry structure: The Nordic telecommunication markets are highly consolidated and mature, typically dominated by three major operators in each country. This oligopolistic structure limits aggressive price wars and enables operators to focus on value-added services
11.
Other side of the story: Intense price pressure in the business-to-business sector and the steady migration of price-sensitive consumer segments to no-frills, low-cost sub-brands threaten to erode Telia's premium ARPU margins. Analysts from DNB Carnegie emphasize that these trends force the sector to rely on constant value-loading rather than raw pricing power

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia displays strong cash conversion and improving profitability following severe restructuring, with free cash flow comfortably covering its progressive dividend while maintaining leverage within its strict targets.

Revenue, Profitability, and Capital Returns Trajectory

1.
Improving organic revenue trend: Telia's top-line performance is exhibiting a stable trajectory, with service revenues growing 1.3% in 2024 and accelerating to 1.8% in 2025 on a like-for-like basis. This steady improvement has been supported by price increases across all core markets and strong consumer TV momentum
2.
Robust EBITDA expansion: Continuing operations adjusted EBITDA rose to SEK 31,920 million in 2025, up from SEK 30,892 million in 2024, representing a solid organic expansion. This growth was driven by both solid service revenue flow-through and significant operational cost savings
3.
Dramatic ROCE acceleration: Capital efficiency has improved markedly under the current executive team, with Return on Capital Employed expansion from 4.0% to 9.0% over the past two years. This substantial return expansion is a key operational metric monitored by management, who aims to bridge the remaining gap toward the industry's best-in-class target of 13.0% to 14.0%
4.
Prudent balance sheet leverage: The group's net debt to adjusted EBITDA leverage fell to 1.93x at end-2025 from 2.28x in 2024, ending safely below management's long-term target corridor of 2.0x to 2.5x. This leverage reduction provides significant financial flexibility and protects the group's Baa1 stable credit rating from Moody's
5.
Exceptional Q1 results: In Q1 2026, Telia delivered a solid performance with adjusted EBITDA of SEK 7,942 million, beating analyst consensus by 1.4%, and generating free cash flow of SEK 1.9 billion. This performance represents a strong start out of the gate, boosting confidence in full-year forecasts

Opex Restructuring and Cash Generation Quality

6.
Self-help opex reductions: The main driver of Telia's financial turnaround is its comprehensive 2024 change program, which reduced central staff by 3,000 net positions and generated SEK 2.6 billion in annual savings. This headcount reduction has successfully offset structural wage inflation
7.
High quality of cash flows: Telia's free cash flow of SEK 9.3 billion in 2025 fully covered its dividend payout for the first time in five years without relying on temporary working capital solutions such as vendor financing programs
8.
Conservative guidance quality: Management maintains a highly credible and conservative approach to financial guidance, as evidenced by its reiterated 2026 targets. Analysts from DNB Carnegie note that Telia's targets are often set conservatively, leaving room for potential positive surprises, which means actual cash flow could exceed current guidance if opex cuts run ahead
9.
Other side of the story: Despite recent successes, Telia possesses a poor historical track record of opex restructuring, having failed to achieve its cost-reduction ambitions in two out of three major efficiency programs launched over the past decade. Analysts from DNB Carnegie note that the historical opex turnaround programs at Telia have often failed to drive long-term cash flow improvements

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case strengthens based on opex cuts and portfolio simplification, but requires sustained mobile ARPU expansion to convince the market of its multi-year free cash flow trajectory.

Capital and Opex-Driven EPS Catalysts

1.
Substantial opex restructuring driver: The primary driver of Telia's 18-month EPS trajectory is the ongoing realization of SEK 2.6 billion in annual opex savings from the 2024 headcount reduction of 3,000 employees. This headcount reduction of 16% of the workforce is expected to result in superior EBITDA growth for 2025. The driver would fail if high wage inflation or duplicate country functions erode opex savings
2.
Declining capital expenditure intensity: A critical driver of free cash flow and EPS expansion is management's strict capital discipline, keeping spectrum-excluded capital expenditures below SEK 13.0 billion for 2026. This high-plausibility driver is supported by the tapering of the core 5G build-out cycle in the Nordics. It would fail if competitive pressures or regulatory requirements force Telia to accelerate network investments
3.
Streamlined business portfolio: Simplifying the group's portfolio by divesting the volatile, low-margin TV and Media business to Schibsted Media and exiting non-core assets in Latvia serves as a strong catalyst for cash predictability. This high-plausibility driver allows management to reallocate capital to high-return telecom projects. It would fail if regulatory hurdles delay or block the closing of these transactions

Commercial and Revenue-Driven EPS Catalysts

4.
Value-based pricing power: Implementing recurring front-book and back-book price increases across mobile, broadband, and TV services serves as a vital top-line catalyst to drive ARPU growth. We deem the plausibility of this driver as medium, as Telia Sweden has successfully executed broadband back-book price adjustments and mobile price revisions. The driver would fail if intensifying retail competition triggers elevated subscriber churn, forcing Telia to accept market share losses
5.
Sovereign network demand growth: Rising demand for secure, mission-critical 5G private networks from national defense and enterprise clients represents a strong strategic growth catalyst. This driver carries high plausibility due to the heightened geopolitical threat environment in the Nordic-Baltic region, which is motivating governments to expand secure communications budgets. The mechanism of failure would be delayed government procurement cycles
6.
Earnings catalyst compound probability: For Telia's bull case to deliver the desired 25%+ IRR, opex cuts and mobile ARPU growth must compound together. Cost-out initiatives alone are ultimately insufficient to sustain long-term earnings expansion without a parallel turnaround in service revenue growth. The bull case would fail if opex savings are achieved but the top-line continues to stagnate
7.
Other side of the story: The transition to a decentralized, country-led operating model risks creating administrative duplicates across geographic silos, thereby inflating Telia's structural cost base and neutralizing the opex savings. Analysts from DNB Carnegie note that management must be careful to avoid duplicate development processes in many places within the group, which would drive up costs and investments

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia shares appear demanding at current levels because the opex turnaround is increasingly discounted, pushing its multiples to a premium relative to European peers and leaving a narrow valuation buffer.
1.
Premium price-to-earnings multiples: Telia Company trades at a premium adjusted P/E multiple of 23.5x 2025E and 23.1x 2026E. Bernstein rates Telia as Underperform, highlighting that these multiples sit at a significant premium to historical averages
2.
Elevated enterprise value multiples: The stock's current valuation corresponds to an EV to EBITDA multiple of 7.5x 2025E. This valuation reflects a noticeable premium to the broader European telecommunications sector, which trades at a lower average multiple
3.
Tight free cash flow yield: At current prices, Telia trades at a demanding NTM free cash flow yield of 4.8 which represents a premium to historical averages. This yield represents a steep premium to the 20-year historical average and is significantly lower than the average European telecom sector FCF yield of 8.1%
4.
Secure progressive dividend yield: The company's proposed dividend increase translates into a visible 4.6% dividend yield for 2026E. This yield is well covered by cash flows, whereas the European sector averages a 4.2% dividend yield
5.
Consensus target and downside: Morgan Stanley sets its price target for Telia Company at SEK 44.00, representing its base-case DCF valuation. At a closing price of SEK 47.92, this implies a potential downside of 8.18%, indicating the market has over-discounted opex cuts, meaning that current levels may yield limited near-term capital appreciation unless opex targets are upgraded
6.
Other side of the story: If Telia successfully optimizes its capital structure and divests non-core Baltic assets, it could support a progressive valuation re-rating toward Elisa's historic multiples, adding up to SEK 20.00 per share in valuation. DNB Carnegie notes that valuing Telia at Elisa's premium level would significantly expand the target price

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk landscape is moderately watching-brief due to persistent price competition in Norway and Finland, alongside Swedish fiber regulations that could cap mid-term revenue growth.

Commercial and Regulatory Risks

1.
Fierce regional competitive intensity: Heavy pricing promotions and aggressive competitor marketing campaigns in Finland and Norway threaten to erode mobile end-user revenues. In Finland, Telia's consumer mobile business has struggled to expand ARPU due to intense price competition from Elisa and DNA, forcing the company to accept net customer losses. These competitive promotions might delay the expected service revenue inflection if price-matching campaigns intensify
2.
Upcoming Swedish fiber regulation: Impending electronic communications regulations in Sweden targeting fixed broadband and fiber networks in single-dwelling units could force Telia to open its networks to competitors, potentially diluting high-margin fiber revenues in its primary market
3.
Decentralisation cost duplication risk: Transitioning to a highly decentralized, country-led operating model risks creating parallel product development and administrative processes across geographic silos, thereby inflating Telia's structural cost base and neutralizing the opex savings. Analysts from DNB Carnegie note that management must be careful to avoid duplicate development processes in many places within the group, which would drive up costs
4.
Sovereign ownership regulatory risk: The Swedish state's 41.1% ownership stake exposes Telia to complex legal proceedings and potential competition law challenges from rivals alleging illegal state aid, restricting the group's strategic flexibility

Financial Vulnerabilities and Monitoring Signals

5.
Critical failure mode: The single failure mode that would most damage the investment case is a failing to reach the targeted SEK 10 billion free cash flow by 2027, which would compromise its progressive dividend floor and prompt credit rating downgrades
6.
False friend working capital signal: Telia's strong Q1 2026 free cash flow of SEK 1.9 billion represents a temporary working capital and billing cycle shift that management expects to reverse in Q2 2026, masking underlying cash flow volatility
7.
Primary early-warning signal: Postpaid mobile ARPU growth in Sweden is the single most critical early-warning signal to monitor, as any deceleration would indicate that Telia's premium pricing power is beginning to crack under competitive pressure. Deutsche Bank highlights that Telia needs sustained revenue improvements to secure mid-term EBITDA expansion
8.
Other side of the story: The risk of a dividend cut is highly overstated because Telia's free cash flow of SEK 9.3 billion already covers its dividend payout by a comfortable margin, leaving a robust buffer even under severe operational stress

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Management has become a significant catalyst for positive change under CEO Patrik Hofbauer and CFO Eric Hageman, who have established strict capital discipline and simplified the operating model.
1.
Capable turnaround leadership: CEO Patrik Hofbauer, appointed in 2024, has acted to rapidly decentralize operations to country units to drive direct commercial accountability. Morgan Stanley notes that Hofbauer spoke about building on a sound strategy and eliminating complexity to accelerate decision-making
2.
Disciplined financial stewardship: CFO Eric Hageman, appointed in September 2023, has brought intense focus on capital allocation and cash flow predictability, as demonstrated by the 50% reduction of the complex vendor financing program. Hageman's past experience as KPN CFO supports his execution of Telia's tight balance sheet management
3.
Aligned executive incentives: CEO Patrik Hofbauer maintains direct alignment with shareholders through his personal ownership of 251,200 shares in Telia, with no insider selling or executive stock liquidations reported
4.
Modernized operational leadership: The appointment of Alexandra Fürst as Chief Technology and Information Officer in March 2025 has accelerated the simplification of Telia's IT landscape, reducing centrally managed IT systems and products by 50%

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia warrants a mixed first-look investment verdict because its excellent operational turnaround and progressive cash flow growth are already fully discounted, leaving narrow upside relative to peers.
1.
Strongest bull argument: The strongest argument for investing is Telia's highly visible self-help opex restructuring and capital discipline, which provide a predictable path toward a 10% Return on Capital Employed and SEK 10 billion in free cash flow by 2027
2.
Other side of the story: The strongest argument against investing is the limited valuation margin of safety offered by its current premium multiple. DNB Carnegie highlights that Telia trades at a demanding NTM FCF yield of 4.8%, representing a steep premium to European peers
3.
Verify Swedish open fiber timeline: The primary immediate action is to verify the implementation timeline and wholesale pricing rules of upcoming Swedish fiber regulations to quantify potential high-margin revenue dilution
4.
Analyze Finnish ARPU repair: The secondary action is to audit Finnish mobile postpaid ARPU and subscriber net adds in the next quarterly earnings print to confirm if competitive repair is sustainably accelerating
