EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 32 findings

Section summary
  1. AddLife has successfully completed a critical balance-sheet repair and strategic restructuring cycle, restoring M&A capacity and elevating margins from 10.5% to 12.5% over the last three years, positioning the company to resume its serial-acquirer compounder trajectory despite modest near-term earnings volatility from FX headwinds and temporary operational disruptions.

Materiality of Change

1.
The most material internal change centres on AddLife's deliberate shift from two years of consolidation and debt reduction back toward renewed bolt-on M&A growth, fundamentally enabled by rigorous working-capital control that drove net debt to EBITDA from 3.2x down to 2.2x during 2025, decisively breaching management's strict 3.0x leverage ceiling and unlocking capital deployment
2.
The second material internal change involves heavy restructuring of the AddVision ophthalmic business through HQ closure and the complete shutdown of the digital Homecare subsidiary Camanio in Q3 2024, permanently removing SEK 15m in quarterly operating costs, which directly contributed to the structural margin expansion visible across both operating divisions
3.
Externally, the most significant positive shift stems from prolonged post-pandemic elective surgery waiting lists, including 5.7 million patients in the UK, guaranteeing sustained demand for surgical consumables and capital equipment, providing multi-year revenue visibility independent of broader macroeconomic cycles
4.
The most material external headwind emerges from severe healthcare staffing shortages actively delaying capital-intensive hospital projects and tender awards, creating temporary volume constraints despite robust underlying demand fundamentals and demographic tailwinds

Top Key Drivers

5.
Driver 1 - M&A Re-acceleration: Management confirms leverage below the 3.0x target enables a higher acquisition pace, with analysts estimating acquired growth of 10% annually in 2026-2029, supported by two successful acquisitions (BioSpectrum and CoaChrom) completed in early 2026, though execution strictly depends on sustained cash conversion to avoid breaching covenant thresholds
6.
Driver 2 - Advanced Product Mix Shift: Pivoting sales toward high-margin gene sequencing and robotic surgery consumables, with management explicitly targeting advanced products to drive structural margin expansion, resulting in Labtech margins improving sharply to 13.1% in Q1 2026, creating sustainable profitability uplift independent of volume growth
7.
Driver 3 - Elective Surgery Backlog Recovery: Sustained demand for orthopaedic and surgical consumables driven by post-pandemic waiting lists, with underlying surgery volume trends remaining clearly positive and analysts expecting approximately 5% organic sales growth, though execution timing fluctuates due to regional healthcare disruptions like Spanish doctor strikes
8.
Driver 4 - AddVision/Camanio Turnaround: Restructuring underperforming subsidiaries eliminated losses and restored baseline profitability, with Camanio closure and AddVision restructuring yielding distinct profitability results and successfully turning AddVision from negative to positive EBITA development, delivering immediate margin accretion already flowing through reported results

Driver 5 - Academic/Pharma Capex Recovery: Rebound in large laboratory instrument sales following prolonged budget uncertainty, with management noting clear increased demand for research funding and replenished order books in March 2026 indicating a clear sales pickup, though highly dependent on volatile public funding cycles and vulnerable to government austerity measures
