EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model Strengths and Defensive Positioning

Holmen manufactures premium fresh-fiber packaging paperboard under the globally recognized Invercote, Incada, and Inverform brands targeting luxury cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and electronics packaging, while specialized book paper products hold leading volume market shares in European paperback publishing . The group operates a closed-loop circular economy model , directing approximately 50% of harvested sawlogs to

sawmills , channeling residual wood chips to adjacent pulp mills, and utilizing forest residuals for bioenergy to fuel industrial drying processes . Strategic reorganization deliberately avoided commodity capacity expansions in favour of high-margin specialty niches , focusing premium Board production on cosmetic, electronic, luxury chocolate, and pharmaceutical packaging applications . Biological asset revaluations contributed SEK 895 million to group earnings during 2025 , supported by total valued forest assets of SEK 56 711 million providing sticky balance sheet support despite industrial volatility . 5 7

Financial Performance and Capital Discipline

Industry Structure and Competitive Headwinds

European paper and packaging markets face persistent overcapacity and elevated Nordic wood costs , with European graphic paper demand collapsing from 36 million tonnes in 2010 to 12.8 million tonnes in 2025 , representing a -6.5% annual CAGR . The containerboard market remains highly fragmented , with the top five players holding under 40% capacity share across 168 companies, though European virgin containerboard shows higher concentration with the top five controlling 86% market share . The Russian timber ban removed approximately 10% of regional Nordic pulpwood supply , creating structural fiber deficits that prevent Nordic wood costs from returning to historical baseline levels . External regulatory pressures include a 10% US import tariff on European consumerboard exports, EU recyclability mandates by 2030 under PPWR, and full geo-location log traceability requirements under EUDR starting December 30, 2026 .

Value Chain Position and Customer Dynamics

Holmen's integrated model captures premium product margins through specialized virgin-fiber brands like Invercote, while wholly owned hydro and wind assets act as internal cost hedges capturing intraday power price volatility, though sawmill operations operate as low-margin, highly cyclical gateways that suffer severe profit compression when log prices and construction demand decouple. The company's 50% wood self-sufficiency aligns with SCA but faces a structural geographic mismatch as 66% of forest holdings sit in Northern Sweden while sawmills are located in Central/South Sweden, forcing Southern mills to buy expensive open-market sawlogs and generating persistent margin pressure unlike SCA's closely co-located assets . In premium virginfiber paperboard, Metsä Board, Stora Enso, and MMK control a combined 72% capacity share of the 4.5 million tonne European folding boxboard market, with Stora Enso holding a 37% capacity share across FBB, SBB, and

LPB combined . Customer concentration remains exceptionally low, as the five largest customers represent only 14% of total group sales , protecting Holmen from single-buyer leverage .

Material Downside Risks

Recent performance reflects mid-single digit delivery volume declines driven by weak European consumer demand and new competitor capacity additions in folding boxboard exacerbating market oversupply , while the Wood Products segment faced material operating losses including SEK -91 million in Q1 2026 driven by elevated Southern Swedish log costs squeezing sawmill margins . Renewable Energy experienced extreme profit compression with operating profit falling to SEK 18 million in 2025 from transmission bottlenecks locking power in Northern Sweden and depressing regional SE2 prices, though cold weather and low wind boosted Q1 2026 energy EBIT to SEK 281 million . Storm Johannes in December 2025 wind-felled 10 million cubic metres of Swedish timber representing 10% of Sweden's annual harvest , with Holmen facing 300 000 cubic

metres of storm-felled timber incurring incremental handling costs of approximately SEK 100 per cubic metre alongside slower clearing and decay risks forcing sawlog downgrades to lower-value pulpwood . Asian competitors are expanding aggressively with APRIL starting up a 1 million tonne per year FBB machine in China , while China's FBB operating rates dropped to 70% fueling competitive export pressures into Europe , with a prolonged wave of low-cost Asian board imports potentially bypassing Holmen's direct-to-brand relationships and forcing industry-wide price discounting that compresses premium SBB margins . 24
