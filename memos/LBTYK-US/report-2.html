KEY FINDINGS — Change Forces (Top 10)

  • [#J] Structural corporate simplification is the dominant change force: LG is collapsing from a complex multi-JV holding company into two cleaner entities — Liberty Telecom (OpCos) and Ziggo Group (Netherlands+Belgium spin-off, H2 2027). This is a genuine value crystallisation play, not just portfolio pruning.
  • [#J] Ziggo Group spin-off (2027) is the single highest-EPS-materiality event: Acquiring Vodafone's 50% VodafoneZiggo stake for €1.0bn + 10% equity kicker, then listing/spinning Ziggo Group (Netherlands + Telenet Belgium) to LG shareholders in H2 2027, removes holding-company discount and forces market pricing of individual assets.
  • [#J] Sunrise re-listing (Nov 2024) was accretive to per-share value — but LG now has no direct operating leverage there: Sunrise distributed as a CHF 3bn tax-free dividend-in-kind. LG's Swiss exposure is closed. Sunrise standalone must now prove it can grow share against Swisscom (~45% market share) unaided.
  • [#J] VodafoneZiggo is the weakest OpCo: 2026 guidance = mid-to-high single-digit EBITDA decline, Adjusted FCF ~€100m, no distributions. DOCSIS 4.0 upgrade creating a €100m incremental investment drag in 2026. KPN's fibre overbuild is the structural threat here.
  • [#J] Telenet (Belgium) showed genuine momentum into 2026: Highest broadband net adds in 3 years in Q4 2025; BASE FMC growing; 2026 guidance = low-single digit EBITDAaL growth + return to positive Adjusted FCF ~€20m. ❌ Belgian football rights non-renewal is a revenue headwind.
  • [#J] VMO2 NetCo sale was abandoned in 2025: UK remains a 50% JV with Telefónica — not consolidated, no clean exit catalyst visible near-term. UK fixed ARPU under pressure from promotional intensity; broadband churn elevated at 28k/quarter Q3 2025.
  • [#A] Buyback cadence is material but not transformative at current pace: LG repurchased ~10% of shares in 2024; CHF 3bn Sunrise dividend-in-kind constituted the bulk of 2024 capital return. 2025 programme authorised up to 10% further reduction. Share count attrition alone will not drive re-rating without OpCo EBITDA improvement.
  • [#A] DOCSIS 4.0 is LG's chosen technology answer — not fibre replacement: Commitment to DOCSIS 4.0 across VodafoneZiggo (7m homes, 8Gbps by end-2026), Telenet/Wyre, and Sunrise. Lower capex than FTTP but creates ongoing technology credibility risk vs pure-fibre overbuilders (KPN, Proximus).
  • [#A] Liberty Growth portfolio ($300m disposals through Apr 2026) provides non-operating value but distorts NAV: ITV stake (partial exit), EdgeConneX, Plume, Formula E, AtlasEdge, Televisa Univision — collectively $500-750m targeted disposals. Real but not in the operating EPS run-rate.

Timeline of disposals (L3Y):
- 2024: Sold UPC Slovakia to O2 Slovakia (affiliate of e&PPF Telecom) — minor market, clean exit
- Nov 2024: Sunrise distributed to LG shareholders as CHF 3bn tax-free dividend-in-kind; Sunrise re-listed on SIX (SUNN) + Nasdaq (SNRE)
- Feb 2026: Agreed to acquire Vodafone's 50% in VodafoneZiggo for €1.0bn cash + 10% stake in new Ziggo Group entity
- Feb 2026: Ziggo Group announced — combines VodafoneZiggo (Netherlands) + Telenet (Belgium) into single Benelux holdco; targeted Euronext Amsterdam listing 2027; LG plans to spin-off 90% to shareholders
- Apr 2026: Exited half of 5% ITV stake (~$101m); partial EdgeConneX disposal (~$74m); combined Q1 proceeds ~$180m, $300m through April

Portfolio remaining after 2027 spin (assumed):
- Liberty Telecom: VMO2 (50% JV, UK, ~28m customers); Ireland OpCo; residual Slovakia/other
- Liberty Growth: 75+ portfolio companies (content, tech, infra) — ITV residual, Plume, AtlasEdge, EdgeConneX residual, Televisa Univision, Formula E
- Ziggo Group (spun): VodafoneZiggo (Netherlands) + Telenet (Belgium) — listed separately

Stated end-state:
- Two distinct, simplified investment profiles: "Ziggo Group" (pure-play Benelux converged telecoms) and "Liberty Global" (smaller, asset-lighter, growth-portfolio-heavy)
- Mike Fries: "building blocks for a spin-off to shareholders in H2 2027" — explicit public commitment
- ⚡ This is the clearest strategic roadmap LG has communicated in a decade; Fries has historically over-promised on M&A pace but the Ziggo structure is mechanically straightforward once Vodafone stake closes (expected July 2026)

Mike Fries credibility check:
- Hit: Sunrise spin-off delivered on time and on announced structure (Nov 2024)
- Hit: Vodafone stake acquisition agreed within guided timeline (Feb 2026 announcement, July 2026 close targeted)

§2 — Sunrise (Switzerland) — Operational Change

- Post-integration, re-listed Sunrise on SIX (SUNN) + Nasdaq (SNRE) in Nov 2024
- LG distributed 100% of Sunrise to LG shareholders as an in-kind dividend — CHF 3bn value
- LG retains no direct equity position in Sunrise post spin-off

Sunrise standalone snapshot (FY2025):
- Mobile: ~3.16m customers; Broadband: ~1.28m; TV: ~0.97m
- Market share: ~30% vs Swisscom ~45% vs Salt ~20%
- Mobile ARPU stabilised following March 2025 price increases; fixed ARPU improving as promotional repricing phase completes
- Capex: CHF 479m in 2025, with CHF 113m in Q4 — declining post-integration
- Strategy: FMC convergence (post UPC Switzerland merger) + DOCSIS 4.0 upgrade for cable network + wholesale fibre access agreements

Why this matters for LG IG analysis:

- Sunrise's FMC convergence story (broadband stickiness post-bundle) is the template Telenet is following with BASE mobile
- ❌ Sceptical view: Sunrise's market position at ~30% is structurally challenged by Swisscom's fibre FTTP rollout; Sunrise relies on cable (DOCSIS upgrade) which faces same credibility questions as VodafoneZiggo's Dutch network vs KPN fibre

§3 — Telenet (Belgium) — Competitive Pressure

Verdict: [#J] Telenet is the more defensible Benelux OpCo near-term — genuine FMC momentum, lower fibre overbuild risk than Netherlands — but the Proximus-Wyre fibre-sharing agreement creates a medium-term structural question mark that the market has not yet priced.

Telenet competitive position:
- Telenet: cable incumbent covering ~70% of Flanders and Brussels; Wyre (JV with Fluvius utility) covers remaining areas with new fibre build
- BASE brand: Telenet's mobile offering, growing FMC (fixed+mobile convergence) penetration — highest broadband net adds in 3 years in Q4 2025
- Belgian market structure: Proximus (DSL/fibre incumbent), Telenet/Wyre (cable+emerging fibre), Orange Belgium (mobile-led), Digi Belgium (new entrant, Dec 2024, 4th MNO licence)

Proximus fibre overbuild threat:
- Proximus targeting 5.5m homes passed by end-2025 (fibre) vs Telenet's Wyre targeting ~375,000 new homes passed; Proximus urban rollout covers high-ARPU Flemish cities first
- Belgium FTTP coverage: ~30.7% in 2024, targeting 50% by 2025 — among the lowest in Europe; structural room for growth but also structural risk as fibre overbuilds cable
- Proximus-Telenet (Wyre) fibre-sharing agreement signed April 2026 — covers 2.7m homes in medium/low-density areas; under Belgian Competition Authority review
- [#A] If fibre-sharing is approved, it reduces overbuild risk and effectively upgrades Telenet's network credentials without full FTTP capex — structurally positive

Digi Belgium — new entrant risk:
- Digi entered December 2024 with nationwide 4G network via Citymesh partnership
- Digi's Romanian/Spanish track record: aggressive SIM-only pricing at 30-50% below incumbents
- ❌ Mobile ARPU pressure from Digi is the clearest near-term negative for BASE brand — Telenet's FMC flywheel depends on mobile margin contribution

Telenet financial trajectory:
- 2025: Highest broadband net adds in 3Y; Q4 2025 showing momentum
- 2026 guidance: Low-single digit EBITDAaL growth; return to positive Adjusted FCF ~€20m; capex to step down significantly
- ❌ Belgian football rights non-renewal — Telenet held football broadcast rights (major consumer value driver); non-renewal will hit premium TV ARPU and churn
- Wyre: selling ~50% stake to third party (proceeds to deleverage Telenet); expected Q3 2026; Wyre capital structure to be separated from Telenet — removes infrastructure drag from Telenet P&L

LG strategic options for Telenet:
- Hold and fold into Ziggo Group (current plan): Telenet + VodafoneZiggo under single Ziggo Group entity, listed Amsterdam 2027
- ❌ Structural concern: Telenet's Belgian cable franchise and VodafoneZiggo's Dutch cable franchise are different risk profiles being bundled together — investors seeking pure Netherlands exposure get Belgian overhang and vice versa
- Sceptical bullet: The Ziggo Group bundling may suppress rather than unlock Telenet's valuation if Belgian competitive dynamics (Digi, Proximus fibre) are seen as worse than Dutch dynamics

§4 — VodafoneZiggo (Netherlands JV) — Change Forces

Verdict: [#J] VodafoneZiggo is the most structurally challenged asset in LG's portfolio — DOCSIS 4.0 vs FTTP debate, EBITDA declining in 2026, FCF barely positive. The acquisition of Vodafone's 50% simplifies governance but does not fix the competitive position; the Ziggo Group listing is a value-surfacing mechanism, not an operational fix.

JV structure change:
- Feb 2026: LG agreed to acquire Vodafone's 50% VodafoneZiggo stake for €1.0bn cash + 10% equity stake in new Ziggo Group
- Vodafone exits; LG becomes 100% owner of VodafoneZiggo operating entity within Ziggo Group structure
- Close expected July 2026; Ziggo Group listing targeted Euronext Amsterdam 2027; LG plans to spin 90% to shareholders

Dutch market dynamics:
- KPN: leads fixed connectivity, ~40% broadband share, pure FTTP rollout via Glaspoort JV (targeting ~5m homes)
- VodafoneZiggo/Ziggo: ~7m cable homes, DOCSIS 4.0 upgrade (2Gbps now available, 8Gbps targeted end-2026) — world's largest DOCSIS 4.0 network by homes passed
- Odido (T-Mobile Netherlands): third-place mobile operator; no fixed network; growing converged propositions via wholesale
- Fibre overbuild: ~526k addresses in overbuild zones (Zeeland, Friesland, South Holland); DeltaFiber/OpenDutchFiber in rationalisation (DeltaFiber selling 200k lines to Glaspoort)
- ⚡ VodafoneZiggo is the first Dutch operator to offer 2Gbps nationwide (7m homes) — DOCSIS 4.0 technology credibility is stronger in Netherlands than market perception assumes

VodafoneZiggo 2025-2026 financial trajectory:
- FY2025 revenue: €4.0bn; EBITDA: €1.7bn
- Q4 2025: Best quarterly broadband performance in over two years; largest 2Gbps provider in Netherlands
- 2026 guidance: Mid-to-high single-digit EBITDA decline; Adjusted FCF ~€100m; no distributions to shareholders
- €100m incremental investment in 2026 (split equally opex/capex) into network resilience and service reliability — one-time investment drag
- Front-book repricing headwind continuing from 2025 commercial initiatives — ARPU pressure until customer base rolls through repriced contracts

KPN competitive dynamics:
- KPN FTTP rollout: ~5m homes targeted via Glaspoort JV; KPN leads fixed broadband at ~40% share
- KPN's fibre product is structurally superior (symmetrical, lower latency) but DOCSIS 4.0 at 8Gbps exceeds consumer demand for the foreseeable future
- Key competitive battleground: SME/business market where fibre credentials matter more than consumer broadband
- ❌ KPN's superior fibre narrative allows KPN to command ARPU premium in business segment — VodafoneZiggo's cable franchise is inherently weaker positioning for business customers

JV resolution value:
- LG paying €1.0bn for Vodafone's 50% implies VodafoneZiggo EV ~€8bn (assuming 5x-6x EBITDA on €1.7bn) — at a 30-40% discount to comparable EU pure-play fibre operators
- ❌ Sceptical view: VodafoneZiggo's 2026 EBITDA decline guidance means the acquisition multiple is based on a deteriorating EBITDA base; €1.0bn could look expensive if the "How We Win Plan" does not arrest subscriber trends by late 2026

§5 — External Change Forces

Verdict: [#J] External forces are predominantly headwinds for incumbent cable — fibre policy, new entrant pricing pressure, high leverage into a rate-sensitive environment. The sole structural tailwind is DOCSIS 4.0's capex advantage over FTTP, which buys time but does not resolve the technology narrative gap.

EU broadband/fibre policy:
- European Gigabit Society: 2030 target = all EU households covered by Gigabit network; 2025 interim = 100Mbps universal
- Gigabit Infrastructure Act (GIA) entered into force May 2024, fully applicable November 2025 — streamlines permitting, mandates duct access sharing, co-deployment with public works
- EU RRF (Recovery and Resilience Facility) + CEF Digital: €42bn investment gap for 2025 targets; CEBF equity fund €420m for rural/suburban projects
- [#A] Policy tailwind is real for fibre overbuilders (KPN in NL, Proximus in BE) — GIA reduces rollout cost and timing for FTTP build vs cable upgrade; weakly positive for LG's Wyre new-build but net negative for incumbent cable operations

Regulatory pricing:
- Belgium: BIPT regulation — Proximus must provide wholesale access at regulated rates; Telenet benefits from wholesale access to Proximus network in non-Telenet areas; but the reverse — Telenet providing cable access — is not symmetrically mandated, preserving some cable pricing power in Flanders
- Netherlands: ACM (regulator) oversight of KPN Glaspoort JV; no mandated cable wholesale access — VodafoneZiggo retains pricing independence on its own network
- ❌ Where pricing pressure is most acute: (1) Belgian mobile — Digi's new entrant aggression; (2) Dutch consumer fixed — KPN fibre promotions in overbuild zones; (3) UK (VMO2) — promotional intensity cited by management as a 2026 ongoing headwind

Technology: DOCSIS 4.0 vs FTTP:
- LG strategy: CommScope selected to upgrade all European OpCo networks to DOCSIS 4.0 (VodafoneZiggo, Telenet/Wyre, Sunrise) — full-node split upgrade rather than trench-and-fibre
- DOCSIS 4.0 economics: capex intensity estimated at $100-150/home passed for upgrade vs $600-900/home for FTTP greenfield — 5-6x capex advantage
- LG CTO publicly stated DOCSIS 4.0 CPE costs "won't break the bank" — management confident in technology economics
- ⚡ VodafoneZiggo's 7m-home DOCSIS 4.0 network at 2Gbps (8Gbps by end-2026) represents the largest concentration of DOCSIS 4.0 deployment globally — creates technology learning-curve and vendor pricing advantages LG can leverage across all OpCos
- ❌ Structural scepticism: Consumers and enterprises ultimately perceive "fibre" as the premium product; DOCSIS 4.0 at equivalent speeds is functionally superior but commercially inferior as a marketing proposition — churn risk in high-fibre penetration markets (NL) is real

Macro — leverage and FX:
- Consolidated debt: $8.5bn; cash $1.9bn; net debt ~$6.6bn
- Q1 2026 Adjusted FCF: −$319m — peak capex year; management has flagged 2026 as peak investment year
- LG primarily USD-listed (LBTYK) with EUR-denominated revenues from all European OpCos — natural USD/EUR mismatch; USD strength = reported revenue headwind
- Interest rate sensitivity: most LG OpCo debt is fixed or hedged at the subsidiary level (VodafoneZiggo, Telenet carry their own credit structures); LG HoldCo debt carries floating exposure; rising rates in 2023-2024 have increased interest cost drag at OpCo level
- ❌ VodafoneZiggo's 2026 no-distributions guidance signals debt service is consuming all FCF at the Dutch JV — no upstream cash to LG from Netherlands in 2026

§7 — Change Forces Summary & EPS Transmission

Verdict: [#J] Three change forces rank above all others by EPS materiality — in order: (1) Ziggo Group spin/holding-discount unwinding, (2) VodafoneZiggo FCF inflection from DOCSIS 4.0 completion, (3) share count reduction via buybacks. Bear cases for each are non-trivial.

Force 1: Ziggo Group Spin (H2 2027) — Holding-Discount Unwinding

Transmission mechanism:
- LG's estimated NAV (per third-party analysis): ~$12bn+ in assets vs market cap ~$3.75bn (2026 YTD)
- Ziggo Group (Netherlands + Belgium) is the single largest contributor to NAV
- Listing forces analyst coverage of Ziggo Group as standalone, applying cable/telco peer multiples directly rather than discounting through a HoldCo structure
- Peer multiples for EU converged telecoms: 5x-7x EV/EBITDA (Proximus ~5.5x, Swisscom ~7x, KPN ~6x)
- VodafoneZiggo FY2025 EBITDA ~€1.7bn; Telenet EBITDA ~€1.4-1.5bn (estimated); combined Ziggo Group EBITDA ~€3.2bn
- At 5.5x EBITDA (conservative), Ziggo Group EV ~€17.5bn less net debt (~€8-9bn at OpCo level) = equity value ~€8-9bn
- LG's 90% stake = €7-8bn equity value from one spin-off vs entire current LG market cap of ~$3.75bn
- [#A] This is the most powerful single change force: if Ziggo Group lists at even a moderate multiple, the rump Liberty Global (VMO2 JV + Liberty Growth) gets valued for near-zero implying massive embedded optionality at current prices
- Bear case: Ziggo Group lists at a compressed multiple (4x-4.5x) due to: (a) DOCSIS 4.0 vs fibre technology debate, (b) VodafoneZiggo EBITDA declining through 2026, (c) Belgian competitive headwinds from Digi + Proximus; net equity value could be €4-6bn — still above LG's current market cap but significantly less than bull case

Force 2: VodafoneZiggo FCF Inflection (2027-2028)

Transmission mechanism:
- 2026 is declared peak capex at VodafoneZiggo; DOCSIS 4.0 upgrade targeted completion end-2026 (8Gbps to 7m homes)
- Post-upgrade, capex intensity should normalise from ~€900m p.a. to maintenance levels (~€600-700m); Adjusted FCF should inflect from ~€100m to €300-500m by 2027-2028 (rough estimate)
- FCF improvement of €200-400m at VodafoneZiggo would flow 100% to Ziggo Group (LG owning 90%) post spin — materialising as dividends or buyback capacity for Ziggo Group
- EPS impact on Ziggo Group: at €300m FCF, €17bn EV implies ~1.7% FCF yield — modest but improving; the trajectory matters more than the absolute level
- Bear case: KPN fibre overbuild accelerates subscriber losses beyond Q4 2025 improvement; front-book repricing creates structural ARPU erosion; €100m 2026 network investment drag becomes recurring rather than one-time; FCF inflection delayed to 2029+

Force 3: Share Count Reduction via Buybacks (~10%/year)

Transmission mechanism:
- LG started ~2024 with ~390m+ shares; ended 2024 at ~349m — ~10% reduction
- If LG maintains 10%/year buyback pace through 2026-2027 (funded by Liberty Growth disposals + holding company cash), shares outstanding fall to ~280m by end-2027
- Same absolute EBITDA/FCF distributed to fewer shares = direct EPS accretion
- Order of magnitude: if consolidated Adjusted FCF reaches €400m by 2027 (from 2025's €224m), distributable per share rises from ~€0.64 to ~€1.43 on reduced share count — 2.2x per-share FCF growth from operations + buybacks combined
- Bear case: $1bn Vodafone stake acquisition (July 2026 close) consumes buyback capacity in 2026; if Liberty Growth disposals underperform ($500m target vs $300m through April 2026), buyback pace slows to 5%/year; per-share compounding halved

§8 — Cohort CQ Responses (Change Forces Lens)

CQ1: ARPU Trajectory (L3Y) — Structural or Demand Pulse?

[#J] LG's ARPU trajectory is mixed across OpCos; the change forces suggest structural pressure is real, not cyclical.

  • VodafoneZiggo: Fixed ARPU under pressure from front-book repricing (promotional competitive response to KPN) — management classifies as tactical, not structural; but KPN fibre's quality premium in business segment is structurally eroding high-ARPU enterprise customers. Verdict: structural with cyclical amplification.
  • Telenet/BASE: ARPU improving on FMC convergence — bundle discount is real but multi-play ARPU is higher than single-play; Digi's entry is a genuine mobile ARPU headwind (structural, not cyclical). Verdict: structural FMC tailwind vs structural mobile headwind — net unclear.
  • VMO2 (UK): Q3 2025 ARPU −0.8% YoY, 28k broadband losses — management cites promotional intensity as the driver. Altnet (full-fibre) competition is structural in UK market; Openreach/BT, Sky, Virgin all competing in overlapping footprints. ❌ Verdict: demand-pulse-reversion scepticism applies fully — any broadband ARPU recovery needs to be proven structural, not simply the absence of seasonal promotions.
  • Change force read: The material change force here is the Digi Belgium entry (structural mobile ARPU compression) and KPN fibre (structural premium ARPU capture in Netherlands business market). ARPU recovery requires LG to win FMC battles, not simply pass through inflation indexation.

CQ2: Alt-net Fibre Overbuilder Competitive Intensity by Market

[#J] The Netherlands is LG's most acute overbuild market; Belgium is moderating (fibre-sharing deal); UK (VMO2) is severe but not LG-consolidated.

  • Netherlands: KPN + Glaspoort targeting ~5m FTTP homes; ~526k homes already in dual-infrastructure zones; VodafoneZiggo's cable covers ~7m homes — overbuild overlap rising. KPN fibre already creating broadband share erosion.
  • Belgium: Proximus targeting 5.5m homes by 2025 but fibre-sharing agreement with Wyre (2.7m homes in medium/low density) reduces full overbuild scenario in rural/suburban Flanders. Urban Flemish cities remain contested.
  • Switzerland (Sunrise): LG no longer exposed — Sunrise faces Swisscom FTTP rollout as standalone company.
  • UK (VMO2): altnet density highest in Europe (over 100 registered altnets); wholesale penetration growing; but VMO2 is 50% JV — LG has no direct fix here.
  • Change force: The fibre-sharing agreement in Belgium (if cleared by BCA) is the key swing factor — it converts overbuild risk into co-investment economics. The Netherlands overbuild is structural and accelerating.

CQ3: Fixed+Mobile Bundle Economics — Flywheel or Defensive?

[#J] FMC is a genuine flywheel for Telenet (BASE mobile); looks more defensive at VodafoneZiggo and VMO2.

  • Telenet/BASE: Highest broadband net adds in 3Y driven by BASE FMC growth; churn reduction demonstrably better for FMC customers than single-play — this is the flywheel dynamic (lower churn → higher LTV → supports network investment).
  • VodafoneZiggo: FMC offering (Vodafone mobile + Ziggo fixed) exists but subscriber momentum has been weak; post-Vodafone exit, LG must decide whether to retain Vodafone brand licensing or migrate to own brand — brand transition is a churn risk.
  • VMO2: Most complex — Virgin fixed + O2 mobile combined under a JV where neither parent controls branding; FMC bundle penetration growing but UK market's promotional intensity compresses FMC economics.
  • Change force read: Telenet FMC is the strongest example of flywheel — and is the empirical template for the Ziggo Group value proposition. VodafoneZiggo's FMC future depends on brand decisions post-Vodafone exit.

CQ4: Balance Sheet / FCF / Dividend Flexibility

[#J] ❌ Balance sheet is the most significant constraint on LG's change forces — high leverage at HoldCo and OpCo level limits flexibility; FCF is currently deeply negative at consolidated level.

  • Consolidated debt: $8.5bn vs cash $1.9bn; net leverage ~4-5x estimated 2026 EBITDA
  • FY2025 Adjusted FCF: €224m — thin; Q1 2026 FCF: −$319m (peak capex quarter)
