B - Executive Summary 23 findings
Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.
Judgement summary
BLUF: 1&1 AG is a binary-outcome infrastructure bet disguised as a German telecom operator: simultaneously building Europe's first cloud-native Open RAN 5G network and positioning itself as the obvious target for German mobile-market consolidation. The standalone case is mathematically challenged (DB assigns negative NPV of >EUR 20/share to the network build and only 40% probability to independent success) but the consolidation floor (DB: EUR 34-47/share, 70-140% upside) creates a structural valuation asymmetry that cannot be ignored. Q1 2026 EBITDA missed consensus by 8%, guidance requires a heavily back-end-loaded H2, and the sell-side is unusually bifurcated (EUR 10 to EUR 31 price-target range), reflecting genuinely incompatible views on strategy rather than differing margin assumptions. The case passes the Triaging gate on change-force intensity and consolidation optionality but fails on case simplicity and predictability -- this is event-driven M&A arbitrage, not a fundamental compounder.

Core transition. 1&1 AG is executing a transformational pivot from an asset-light MVNO reseller to Germany's fourth fully functional physical mobile network operator (MNO), deploying Europe's first fully virtualised cloud-native 5G network on Open RAN technology
2.

A December 2025 intra-group acquisition of 1&1 Versatel from parent United Internet (UTDI-DE, 86.46% owner) for EUR 1.3bn added a 68,500km fibre-optic network spanning 350 German cities, reorganising the group into two new segments: Consumer & Small Business and Enterprises & Networks

E1 RA1 -- Change forces present

1.

3.

4.

6.

CQ1 -- Strong external change forces / tailwinds:

7.
German mobile market consolidation logic: the loss of the 1&1 wholesale contract (~EUR 500m in annual revenues for O2D) has structurally wounded Telefonica Deutschland -- Q1 2026 showed revenues down 5.7%, EBITDA down 8.8%, FCF down 48.3%; the distress is creating consolidation pressure from within the incumbent group
8.
Vodafone wholesale partnership -- structural opex tailwind: migrating mobile customers to Vodafone national roaming eliminates the prior Telefonica agreement; as 1&1 builds its own network, traffic migrates off Vodafone and roaming payments structurally fall; 12 million customers already migrated by November 2025
9.
Spectrum regulatory inflection: the BNetzA ruling that the 2018 spectrum allocation was unlawful reopens the spectrum access question; if the regulator mandates low-band 800MHz shared access to 1&1, indoor coverage quality could match incumbents at a fraction of the additional site-density cost required in mid-band only
10.
Gigabit Infrastructure Act: new legislation classifying digital networks as being of overriding public interest accelerates local permitting -- specifically addressing the physical site-access bottleneck that has slowed the antenna rollout; a direct government-level tailwind for 1&1's build programme
11.
Geopolitical supply-chain tailwind -- Huawei exclusion: 1&1 is the only German operator to have completely excluded Huawei from its network architecture; as European governments tighten supply-chain security requirements on telecoms, 1&1's vendor-diverse, Huawei-free Open RAN architecture gains a structural competitive advantage for potential M&A buyers or JV partners
12.
Alt-net distress creating fibre acquisition opportunities: the Versatel acquisition completed in December 2025 at ~7.3x 2026E EV/EBITDA; as additional alt-nets face balance-sheet stress, further fibre consolidation at attractive multiples becomes available
