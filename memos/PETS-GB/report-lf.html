EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Bailey brings over twenty-five years of deep grocery and commercial experience, culminating in his role as Managing Director of Waitrose where he managed a massive £8.00bn retail operation and nearly doubled operating profit margins back to 3.00%. His grocery buying and commercial history directly addresses Pets at Home's critical execution gaps on the commercial side and weak supplier negotiations, which analysts specifically identified as exactly what is required to execute the Retail Turnaround Plan. Strategic congruence is demonstrated through the aggressive food price investment, which reduced prices on over 1,000 food products by an average of 12% in November 2025, drove food volumes growing 3.7% in Q4 FY26, and achieved basket pricing actually cheaper than Zooplus. However, his pure-play grocery background may not easily translate to Pets at Home's specialized vet division, and Waitrose's market share fell from 4.2% to 3.8% during his tenure under intense Marks & Spencer competition, while direct competitor Marks & Spencer grew its market share from 3.6% to 3.7% over the same period.

Senior leadership bench strength presents elevated execution risk following the simultaneous arrival of both CEO and CFO. CFO Sarah Pollard was appointed as Chief Financial Officer on March 27, 2026, following Mike Iddon's retirement after nine years, bringing robust corporate pedigree from roles as CFO of PZ Cussons plc, Finance Director at Birds Eye, and Deputy CFO at Nomad Foods, with analysts considering her a highly solid and experienced executive appointment. Pollard oversees the delivery of a £20m overhead reduction program through restructuring Pets' Support Office, maintains pre-IFRS 16 leverage of only 0.1x underlying EBITDA, and the opex base will benefit from these central cost savings starting to flow through in FY27. The executive committee includes Richard Dening-Smitherman as Managing Director of the Vet Business, Garret Turley as Independent Non-Executive Director who co-founded Pet Doctors strengthening board-level clinical oversight, and Anja Madsen as Retail COO bringing Sainsbury's and Tesco grocery experience. However, the simultaneous arrival of a new CEO and CFO risks operational disruption during a critical retail turnaround phase, adding execution risk and increasing organizational vulnerability during a restructuring.

Board governance robustness exceeds UK Corporate Governance Code standards but faces continuity stress from rapid transitions. The board comprises seven members, with four out of seven directors as independent non-executives exceeding UK Corporate Governance Code requirements, and excluding the non-executive Chair, independent directors represent 66.67% of the active board. Committee composition includes Zarin Patel chairing the Audit and Risk Committee with only independent non-executives serving, Roger Burnley chairing the Remuneration Committee, Ian Burke chairing the Nomination Committee alongside all four independent non-executive directors, and Garret Turley chairing the Sustainability Committee bringing essential veterinary and clinical insight. Women represent 42.70% of the active board, exceeding the Parker and FTSE Women Leaders targets, though ethnic minority representation remains a focus with overall non-clinical group representation at 6.90%. However, recent high velocity of board transitions may destabilize oversight if strategic execution lags during this transition, and individual director equity ownership was not fully extracted due to database permission restrictions, though no activist investors or major institutional shareholders currently hold designated seats ensuring the board retains complete strategic autonomy.
