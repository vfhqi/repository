EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Change Materiality Assessment

The Caltagirone family maintains absolute governance control through the May 2026 reconfirmation of Cav. Lav. Francesco Gaetano Caltagirone as Chairman and Azzurra Caltagirone as Vice Chairman, while Finanziaria Italia 2005 S.p.A. holds 53.91% of share capital, enabling the family holding company to dictate all board and auditor nominations. The corporate simplification advanced materially in 2025 through the execution of the Capitolium S.r.l. merger in September, streamlining the shareholding chain without diluting capital stock. Governance safeguards were significantly enhanced in December 2025 when Mr. Caltagirone waived his delegated voting powers at BMPS and Generali meetings, subjecting future votes to binding Independent Directors' Committee oversight, a direct response to Milan Public Prosecutor investigations into alleged concert party activity. The group's diversified holdings strategy remains intact across four core pillars: industrial cement through Cementir Holding, heavy civil engineering via Vianini Lavori, publishing operations under Caltagirone Editore, and strategic financial stakes in BMPS and Assicurazioni Generali. Consolidated revenues reached 2.438 billion Euros in fiscal 2025, up 13.9%, while EBITDA grew to 487.9 million Euros representing an 11% increase, driven primarily by robust domestic infrastructure project execution offsetting subdued cement volumes.

Top Seven Key Drivers (Explicitly Ranked)

Driver #3 – Generali Insurance Stake Dividends provide stable defensive financial income, with Caltagirone maintaining a 6.32% indirect stake in Assicurazioni Generali as of April 2026 under the Lifetime Partner 2025-2027 strategic plan framework.

Driver #4 – Infrastructure Order Backlog Execution stabilizes parent construction revenues, as infrastructure contracts help stabilize parent construction revenues with adequate project margins through participation in selective public tenders and the Eteria Consortium, benefiting from sustained public investment programmes that are driving solid demand for heavy building materials.

Driver #5 – Decarbonisation Capital Expenditure Programme represents both opportunity and risk, with stated plans projecting 2026 capital expenditures of approximately 128 million Euros including 32 million Euros for sustainability. Declining free ETS allowances will create an average annual CO2 shortage of 130,000 tons, while the definitive CBAM regime starting in 2026 introduces carbon border taxes to restrict cheap imports.

Driver #6 – Turkish Operations Stabilization remains a significant headwind, as Cementir Turkish operations were penalised by Turkish Lira devaluation, falling 3.4% to 341.6 million Euros, despite Turkish domestic cement volumes growing 4% during the period. The Turkish cash-generating unit reaches its break-even limit with a 2.00% reduction in its EBITDA ratio.

Driver #7 – Publishing Media Restructuring continues to absorb heavy impairments, with indefinite-lived publishing asset write-downs increasing to 19.16 million Euros in 2025, penalizing consolidated operating profit, reflecting persistent structural headwinds in traditional print media despite digital transformation efforts.
