§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The single most coherent bear case is: revenue decline accelerates beyond the modelled floor, the cost programme delivers below target, and the combination means EBITDA never reaches the €1.3-1.4bn needed to support the leveraged capital structure [#J]

  • Single largest risk: Subscriber churn in mobile outpaces the cost-out programme — the two key variables move in opposite directions simultaneously [#J]

  • Revenue: if mobile subscriber losses run at -400k per year rather than -100-200k, revenue decline is -6 to -8% per annum
  • Costs: if vendor renegotiations deliver only 50% of target, gross savings halve

  • Combined: EBITDA could stall at €1.0-1.1bn vs target of €1.3-1.4bn — a c.20% shortfall on the value-creation thesis

  • Early warning signals: Three observable lead indicators should be tracked [#A]

  • Mobile net adds/losses (quarterly disclosure) — persistent >-200k/quarter is a structural warning
  • EBITDA margin trend (each half-year) — failure to deliver +150-200bps per half suggests programme shortfall

  • Net debt / EBITDAaL ratio (semi-annual) — if leverage is not declining, interest cost erosion is worsening

  • Peer-relative downside: ZEG-GB's downside scenario is asymmetrically larger than peers due to leverage [#A]

  • A 10% EBITDA miss for Telefónica España is a modest FCF reduction
  • A 10% EBITDA miss for ZEG-GB threatens the deleveraging path and could trigger covenant or refinancing risk

  • The optionality in the bull case comes with binary downside risk in the bear case — this is intrinsic to the leveraged-buyout-style structure

  • Breaking points: Three structural breaking points in the thesis [#A]

  • DIGI Spain accelerating to >20% mobile market share — removes the pricing discipline premise
  • Spanish macro recession — consumer ARPU cuts, enterprise contract renegotiations

  • Interest rate re-acceleration above forward curve — refinancing cost increases compress FCF to near-zero
