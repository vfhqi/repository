3.
Favourable price spreads: Wide price arbitrages between Texas and Asia, historically sitting around $250 per tonne, make seaborne shipping highly economical
4.
Solid long-haul demand: Seaborne trade routes are highly beneficial, as approximately 80.00% of US VLGC LPG exports are delivered to Far East markets

Asian petchem demand: Chinese petrochemical demand remains key, with growing domestic PDH plants acting as a major consumer of imported US propane

Lasting war disruptions: Geopolitical conflicts in the Middle East have elevated shipping demand, driving extraordinary freight rates as buyers seek alternative cargoes

High fleet utilization: Global shipping efficiency is supported near term, with BW LPG achieving an impressive 92.00% fleet utilization in Q1 2026

19.
Other side of the story: Slowing US shale activity could occur if energy prices soften, directly capping LPG export growth and leaving new vessel deliveries underutilized

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is highly asymmetric to the downside as a wave of new vessel deliveries threatens to crush global fleet utilization and shipping rates.

Global Shipping Supply and Orderbook Risks

1.
Demanding supply headwinds: Massive global shipping supply represents the single largest risk, as the Very Large Gas Carrier orderbook of 130 ships reaches 28% of capacity
2.
Depressed vessel utilization: Impending vessel deliveries are expected to push global fleet utilization down from 88.00% to 86.00%, creating severe downward pressure on spot freight rates
3.
Misleading spot signals: False positive signals are present as temporary spot rate spikes, which are driven by transit inefficiencies, mask the underlying structural oversupply of the global fleet
4.
Critical tracking indicator: Tracking global vessel delivery schedules represents the single early-warning signal, as any delivery acceleration will immediately pressure shipping rates
5.
Accelerated fleet expansion: Heavy fleet deliveries are set to concentrate in 2026 and 2027, with 10% and 12% capacity growth expected respectively
6.
Questionable absorption capacity: Shipping analysts warn that terminal capacities are near maximum utilization, limiting further export absorption

Fuel Spreads and Customer Risks

7.
Adverse spread compression: Propane substitution risks are material, as narrowing price spreads between propane and competing naphtha feedstocks could cause petrochemical crackers to reduce propane demand
8.
High customer concentration: Operational risk is high, with the top customer representing $281 million of revenue in 2025, exposing the trading business to counterparty defaults
9.
OPEC supply cut threats: Shipping volumes are highly vulnerable, as prolonged OPEC crude oil production cuts could reduce Middle East LPG export capacity
10.
Adverse regulatory compliance: Environmental cap-and-trade programs like the expanded EU Emissions Trading System pose rising carbon costs for maritime operations
11.
Geographic routing liabilities: Environmental shipping rules could alter routing decisions, potentially raising transit expenses to non-EU ports
12.
Other side of the story: Significant vessel demolition could offset oversupply, as a substantial 9.00% of the active global fleet is currently older than 25 years
