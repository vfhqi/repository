EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Value Proposition and Strategic Positioning

Cloetta's broad product portfolio spans candy, chocolate, pastilles, chewing gum, and nuts, with candy accounting for 62% of group sales, chocolate at 23%, pastilles at 9%, and chewing gum at 4% . This diversification creates a critical structural hedge, as the company can rapidly shift consumer trade promotions away from expensive chocolate categories to other sugar confectionery during commodity price spikes . The business model prioritizes strict focus on ten Superbrands–each generating at least EUR 15 million in annual sales with aboveaverage profitability–and expanding these high-margin brands into new core markets . Recent execution

includes re-launching Kexchoklad in Denmark and expanding it into Finland with 90% weighted distribution .

The company maintains a strong defensive position through high emotional attachment in a relatively stable SEK 397 billion Northern European confectionery market that remains insensitive to short-term economic fluctuations and benefits from deep consumer brand loyalty tied to nostalgic trust and regional pride . However, confectionery is one of the most impulse-driven grocery categories, with up to 80% of purchasing decisions made directly at the retail shelf , creating both opportunity through in-store visibility and vulnerability to shelf-space substitution. 8

Competitive Landscape and Market Concentration

Cloetta holds a unique position as the only company with strong confectionery presence across all Nordic markets, maintaining clear market leadership in Sweden and a solid 20% market share in Finland , though Fazer dominates Finland with 39% share while Mondelez leads Norwegian chocolate with 39% value share versus Cloetta's smaller 7% position . The competitive landscape features fragmented European confectionery dominated by massive multinational conglomerates facing intense competition from established regional players , including Fazer, Mondelez, Orkla, Haribo, and rapidly expanding private label alternatives.

Strategic Risks and Structural Headwinds

The managed Pick & mix model carries heavy fixed merchandising overheads, meaning any sudden volume contraction or retail partner loss immediately de-leverages the segment . The cancellation of the greenfield plant limits long-term capacity expansion, forcing reliance on aging facilities that could suffer from operational bottlenecks . Grocery buyers can rapidly expand private label offerings to capture market share, squeezing branded margins and undermining pricing power , while complete reliance on third-party processors exposes Cloetta to severe midstream bottlenecks, potentially disrupting supply if processors restrict capacity .

Longer-term disruption forces include restrictive regulatory shifts focusing on sugar taxes and promotional marketing restrictions, alongside rising health-conscious consumer trends debating obesity and health risks that could negatively affect long-term sugar confectionery volumes . Rising GLP-1 weight-loss drug adoption in North America requires patients to adhere to lower sugar diets, though Cloetta positions its brands around selling joy to sustain indulgence demand regardless of drug adoption . The company mitigates these risks through active expansion of its permissible range, with 23% of its current portfolio being sugar-free and targeting at least 24% of consumer units to be completely sugar-free .

Growth Runway and Capital Allocation Optionality

North America represents an attractive, fast-growing geography powered by social media interest in authentic Swedish candy, with sales to the United States growing approximately 30% in 2024 and Cloetta opening its first permanent CandyKing store on Manhattan's Bleecker Street in late 2025 . Licensing partnerships generate incremental sales lift, as Tupla chocolate ice-cream licensing generated immediate 6% incremental sales growth, while the global IKEA supply agreement expands iconic brands across 14 countries with scheduled expansion

through 2027 . M&A acts as a strategic accelerator within the updated framework, with potential focus areas including distribution platforms in Germany or the UK and strategic pastille brands .
