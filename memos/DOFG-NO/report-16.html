- Education: Norwegian university background in economics/finance; no publicly disclosed MBA or international programme, consistent with senior Norwegian industrials executives

CEO tenure:
- Approximately 3 years as CEO (from ~2022 to present); CFO tenure at DOF Group adds a further ~4 years of institutional exposure to the 2022 period
- Total DOF tenure (combined CFO + CEO): approximately 7 years — meaningful institutional knowledge; not a parachuted outsider

CEO track record at DOF Group:
- Restructuring execution: The defining achievement is guiding DOF through the 2022-23 financial restructuring — c. NOK 20bn+ debt restructured, new equity injected, creditor agreement achieved; this is operationally and diplomatically complex and Ørjasæter's background made him the logical choice

- Contract portfolio: L3Y DOF has improved contract backlog quality — longer-duration contracts, higher day-rates on renewals; this is partly cycle (offshore CAPEX recovery) and partly commercial discipline; distinguishing the two is the attribution problem

CEO track record prior:

- No material weaknesses disclosed in L3Y audit reports
- No restatements identified post-restructuring

Legal / regulatory issues:

Whistleblower / governance complaint signals:

Auditor change:
- No change identified post-re-listing; stability of auditor is a positive governance signal

Material weakness:
- None disclosed; the restructuring accounting was complex but audited; no restatement

Sceptical bullet: Bearish read on governance — the surface cleanliness may reflect the compliance machinery of a freshly-relisted company (audit committees, external auditors, and Oslo Børs listing standards create formal governance discipline in year 1-2 post-listing) rather than a durable governance culture; the latent risk is Aker Capital's influence crystalising into a related-party transaction that is formally disclosed and approved but economically disadvantageous to minority shareholders; Norway's corporate governance code is strong on paper but enforcement of minority-shareholder protection against a determined major shareholder has been tested in Norwegian corporate history (REC Silicon, various Aker entities); DOF's board independence post-Aker is the structural watchpoint
