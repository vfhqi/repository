Regulatory Environment & Strategic Outlook
The DORA III Regulatory Framework (2027-2031)
Aena's Board of Directors recently approved the draft proposal for the Third Airport Regulation Document (DORA
III), outlining the operational and financial roadmap for the 2027-2031 period. This proposal aims to align
infrastructure capacity with future demand while addressing safety, quality, and environmental sustainability
requirements. To support this expansion, the proposal sets a massive regulated capital expenditure plan of
€9.99 billion out of a total network investment of €12.88 billion. Management projects a conservative passenger traffic compound annual growth rate of 1.3%, aiming for 347 million passengers by 2031.
To adequately remunerate this heavy investment cycle, Aena has proposed a pre-tax weighted average cost of
capital of 9.0%. Consequently, the framework requests an average annual tariff increase of €0.43 per passenger, equating to a +3.8% real pricing trajectory. Operating expenses are also forecast to rise
progressively from €1.98 billion in 2027 to €2.31 billion in 2031 to accommodate the expanded infrastructure,
new personnel requirements, and elevated security protocols. 63 64 67 69 • ARS 65

Macroeconomic, ESG, and Airline Risk Factors
Despite robust operational momentum, Aena faces an array of external risks ranging from macroeconomic
volatility to supply chain constraints. Geopolitical conflicts in the Middle East and Ukraine, alongside
persistent inflation and shifting monetary policies, threaten to moderate global economic growth and
subsequently impact discretionary tourism spending. Furthermore, capacity growth is partially constrained by
delays in fleet deliveries from major aircraft manufacturers, which limits the ability of airlines to rapidly increase
seat supply. The proposed tariff hikes for the DORA III period have also triggered significant pushback from
the airline industry, with associations like IATA and ALA aggressively demanding tariff reductions and challenging
the company's traffic estimates. 82 83 84 • ARS 85 • News
