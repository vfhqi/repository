Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Management delivered a massive guidance beat driven by German asset expansion while the Belgian regulator surprised the street with a highly lucrative incentive package.

Earnings and guidance delivery

1.
Earnings beat substantial: Elia reported fiscal 2025 net profit of 557 million euros easily beating the published consensus of 527 million euros and blasting past the top end of management own 540 million euro guidance range
2.
Underlying strength masked: The reported net profit actually contained a negative 99 million euro impairment on the US energyRe Giga portfolio meaning the underlying adjusted net profit was roughly 599 million euros entirely shattering street estimates
3.
Guidance upgrade massive: Management issued fiscal 2026 net profit guidance of 690 to 740 million euros establishing a 715 million euro midpoint that violently deviated from the 644 million euro consensus expectation
4.
German division outperformance: The 50Hertz German segment delivered 439 million euros in adjusted net profit versus consensus expectations of 402 million euros proving that the massive capital deployment was translating into earnings faster than modelled
5.
Capital expenditure confirmed: Management locked in the 2026 capital expenditure target at 6.8 billion euros composed of 1.7 billion euros in Belgium and 5.1 billion euros in Germany perfectly matching the aggressive growth narrative the buy-side demanded

Regulatory and strategic developments

6.
Belgian draft lucrative: On April 17 the Belgian regulator published a draft tariff methodology proposing an 8.1 percent average post-tax return on equity for the 2028-2031 period completely deviating from the consensus expectation of a flat 6.2 percent
7.
Incentive package mechanicals: The massive regulatory beat was driven by a completely unpriced 2.0 percent return on equity adder embedded in the new incentive package combined with a better pass-through mechanism for rising bond yields
8.
German network draft expansion: The second draft of the German Network Development Plan published in early 2026 indicated overall investment requirements of 365 to 392 billion euros through 2045 deviating upward from the prior 360 to 390 billion euro baseline
9.
Funding risk neutralized: On April 28 Elia successfully placed a 900 million euro hybrid bond at a 4.625 percent fixed coupon directly answering buy-side fears regarding the exact mechanism for plugging the remaining equity funding gap

Market and internal mechanics

10.
Capitalised borrowing opticals: A significant portion of the earnings beat was driven by 112 million euros in capitalised borrowing costs up 45 million euros year-over-year deviating from models that assumed a flatter interest capitalization schedule
11.
Deferred tax revaluation uplift: The German division results were uniquely bolstered by a 47 million euro positive deferred tax revaluation triggered by a planned reduction in the federal corporate tax rate from 15 percent to 10 percent
12.
Holding costs contained: Non-regulated and Nemo Link activities posted a positive 5 million euro contribution versus the consensus expectation of a 35 to 45 million euro loss driven heavily by a 24.7 million euro Belgian tax consolidation benefit
13.
Other side of the story: The aggressive upward revision in the Belgian regulatory return is only a draft methodology subject to public consultation and could face severe political pushback before the final June publication if consumer energy bills spike
