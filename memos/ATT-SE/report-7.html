  • SS consensus: Carnegie and Berenberg (public) treat quality execution as a given in their base cases — both model occupancy recovery without explicitly stress-testing quality failure; this creates a consensus blind spot [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Swedish political discourse around care quality has intensified; political visibility is a double-edged sword — creates pressure to improve but also raises consequence of any further incidents [#J]

  • Visibility: High — IVO data is public, updated quarterly; trend is visible to all market participants; no information asymmetry advantage [#J]

  • Plausibility assessment: Declining trend in enforcement actions is real; reaching Ambea/Humana quality levels requires 4-6 more quarters of consistent improvement; achievable by end-2026 but no margin for setbacks [#A]

  • Key assumptions: (i) No headline care scandal in Swedish media; (ii) IVO engagement remains constructive not adversarial; (iii) management quality investment (SEK 50-80m annual programme cost per public estimates) is sustained [#A]

  • Bear case: A high-profile care incident in a Swedish unit generates media/parliamentary attention; IVO responds with temporary capacity restrictions; occupancy in affected units drops 15-20%; municipal procurement officers freeze referrals across ALL Attendo units as precautionary measure [#J]

  • Red flags: Swedish social media and trade press carry periodic Attendo-specific care quality stories; frequency has not declined materially in L12M despite management's quality investment [#A]

§8 — Key Driver #7: Regulatory and Tender Risk (Downside Protection Driver)

  • Company guide: Management does not provide tender renewal probability disclosure; standard commentary is that Attendo has "long track record as a preferred partner" — qualitative rather than quantitative comfort [#A]
