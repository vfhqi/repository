§1 — Current Management Guidance

Zegona's current guidance framework reflects a two-horizon structure: near-term FY 2025 operational targets and a medium-term (2026-2027) steady-state ambition anchored to the post-integration Vodafone Spain platform.

  • FY current — verdict: Management has issued explicit and numerically specific FY 2025 guidance across EBITDA and FCF; revenue guidance is range-framed and conditional on postpaid stabilisation [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Revenue guidance implicitly targets ~€3.85-3.95bn for FY 2025, derived from management's stated assumption of low-single-digit decline moderating toward flat [#A]

  • Sub: No explicit published revenue number; derived from ARPU + subscriber trajectory commentary in H2 2024 results call [#A]

  • Sub: Decline rate guided as "improving sequentially" — qualitative framing rather than hard floor [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management targets €1.25-1.35bn EBITDA (aL) for FY 2025, representing year-on-year expansion driven by the €150m cost-savings programme [#A]

  • Sub: "aL" = after leases; consistent with IFRS 16 treatment across European telco peers (Vodafone, Telefónica, MásMóvil comps) [#A]

  • FY current — FCF: Management has guided to positive FCF in FY 2025 as a categorical commitment, with specific bridge commentary pointing to CapEx normalisation post-integration [#A]

  • Sub: FCF positivity is a covenant-relevant milestone given ZEG's leveraged balance sheet; credibility is therefore high-stakes [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guided at €550-600m for FY 2025, declining from elevated integration-phase levels in 2024 [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx envelope includes network modernisation spend committed under Vodafone TSA (transition service agreement) exit schedule [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No formal EPS guidance issued; EPS highly sensitive to interest cost trajectory given ~€4bn net debt inherited [#A]

  • Sub: Consensus implies breakeven-to-modest-positive EPS for FY 2025; management has not contradicted this framing [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY 2026): Medium-term target of €1.4-1.5bn EBITDA (aL) with implied margin of ~36-38% on a stabilised revenue base [#A]

  • Sub: Margin expansion assumes full realisation of cost-savings programme plus ARPU recovery in postpaid mobile [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY 2027): Management's "steady-state" case points to FCF yield of 15-20% on market cap with net debt / EBITDA below 3.0x [#A]

  • Sub: Deleveraging path depends jointly on EBITDA growth and dividend policy; both are management-controllable in the medium term [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Primary guidance issued at H2 / FY 2024 results call (March 2025) and H1 2024 results call (September 2024) [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Zegona held an Investor Day in June 2024 that remains the primary medium-term framework document; targets have not been formally updated since [#A]

  • Sub: Eight months since last formal medium-term update introduces staleness risk; next update expected at H1 2025 results [#A]

  • Caveat — churn conditional: All medium-term targets explicitly assume "stabilisation and recovery of the Spanish mobile market" — management's language, not a quantified assumption [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — M&A excluded: Guidance is organic and excludes any further M&A, consistent with Zegona's stated "operate and optimise" phase post-Vodafone Spain [#A]

  • Update timing: Next formal guidance update expected September 2025 (H1 2025 results); any material deviation from FY 2025 EBITDA trajectory likely surfaced then [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §1: The bearish read is that revenue guidance is deliberately non-numerical, allowing management to frame any EBITDA beat as "on track" even if top-line compression continues; the embedded assumption of "sequential improvement" in ARPU is unquantified and therefore unverifiable until print [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Pattern verdict: Asymmetric pattern — management demonstrates cost-side sandbagging (conservative CapEx and cost guidance) alongside revenue over-optimism; the combination produces EBITDA beats that mask revenue misses, a pattern common in post-LBO telco turnarounds [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — costs: CapEx and cost-saving guidance has been set conservatively in all observable periods; underspend versus guidance is consistent and directionally reliable [#A]

  • Sub: This is structurally rational — management controls costs more directly than it controls competitive ARPU dynamics [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — revenue: Revenue and RGU guidance has been optimistic in every observable period; management framed stabilisation as imminent at deal close (Nov 2023) and has repeatedly deferred the recovery timeline [#J]

  • Sub: The deferral pattern is a classic over-promiser signal — each period introduces a new "near-term" recovery framing without prior-period accountability [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EBITDA aggregate: EBITDA beats emerge from the combination of cost-side sandbagging absorbing revenue miss; net aggregate beats do not indicate genuine guidance conservatism across the full P&L [#A]

  • Pattern shift — none evident: No pattern shift in observable L4 periods; revenue optimism and cost conservatism have been stable throughout the post-acquisition window [#A]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA: 4/4 EBITDA beats; however all beats are narrow (+2-6%), suggesting modest cushion, not deep sandbagging [#A]

  • Sub: Compare to European telco peer sandbagging typical of +5-10% EBITDA beat range at Telefónica, BT, and Vodafone over comparable integration phases [#A]

  • Miss consistency — revenue: 3/4 revenue misses; all in the same direction (below guidance); zero instances of revenue beating or meeting the framed trajectory [#J]

  • Magnitude — miss: Revenue miss magnitude of 3-4% per period is moderate in isolation but cumulative over 4 periods implies a structural gap, not noise [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: No evidence of improving revenue accuracy; H2 2024 miss was similar magnitude to H1 2024 miss [#A]

  • Deteriorating accuracy — RGU: Postpaid RGU misses appear to be deepening relative to management's recovery framing; guidance credibility on this sub-metric is deteriorating [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §3: The bearish read is that the apparent cost-side sandbagging is running out of cushion: the €150m cost-savings programme is largely identified and pacing risk increases as easy wins are taken; future cost beats will be smaller, while revenue optimism persists, narrowing the gap between guidance and delivery and potentially turning EBITDA beats into misses by FY 2026 [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Accuracy trend verdict: No improvement in L4 periods; revenue accuracy has not converged toward guidance despite repeated management commitments to stabilisation; cost accuracy is stable but the cushion is shrinking [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: H2 2024 EBITDA beat (+2.1%) is smaller than H1 2024 beat (+2.9%); the narrowing suggests cost-savings programme is becoming more accurately guided as implementation matures [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — revenue: Revenue misses have been consistent across H1 2024, H2 2024, and FY 2024 totality; no convergence trend is visible in the data [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — costs: CapEx guidance accuracy has improved slightly; underspend narrowed from ~–5.4% in H2 2024 to a similar level, suggesting management has a tighter grip on capital allocation [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — RGU: Postpaid net add guidance has deteriorated — management's stabilisation framing was most confident at deal close (Nov 2023) and has not been validated in any subsequent period [#J]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Spanish mobile market dynamics (MásMóvil-Orange merger creating a stronger third competitor, price pressure across market) represent an exogenous driver management cannot control [#A]

  • Sub: Analyst peers at Berenberg and Citi (coverage of Spanish telco sector) have cited the MásMóvil-Orange competitive dynamic as the primary macro headwind for ARPU recovery timing [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Internal execution has been strong on cost / CapEx; the volatility is primarily market-driven, which partially mitigates management credibility concerns on the miss pattern [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx and EBITDA cost-side; consistently delivered within ±5% of guidance [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Postpaid RGU and revenue trajectory; systematic optimism bias, no instances of meeting guidance [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §4: The bearish read is that the macro excuse (competitive environment) could persist indefinitely — MásMóvil-Orange integration is a multi-year process, meaning the "temporary" headwind management implies may become structural; revenue accuracy improvement may never arrive within Richard's 18-24M hold period [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Assumptions verdict: The guidance stack contains two fragile assumptions (postpaid ARPU stabilisation + B2B revenue growth) that interact non-linearly; a simultaneous adverse move in both would produce an EBITDA outcome materially below guidance even if cost savings fully deliver [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes Spanish mobile ARPU stabilises and begins recovering in H2 2025; this rests on competitive intensity abating post-MásMóvil-Orange integration settling [#A]

  • Sub: No quantified floor on ARPU specified; management uses "stabilisation" language without defining the metric level [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Management assumes postpaid RGU net adds turn positive by mid-2025; this has been deferred each period and remains the most consistently missed assumption [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — B2B: B2B segment is assumed to grow low-to-mid single digits, partially offsetting consumer postpaid weakness; evidence of actual B2B momentum is limited in available disclosures [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Spanish broadband market assumed to remain stable (no material ARPU erosion in fixed / fibre); this is more plausible given consolidated market structure [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: €150m cost-savings programme is the primary margin driver; approximately €80m realised by FY 2024, leaving ~€70m still to be captured; pacing risk is moderate [#A]

  • Sub: Specific savings categories include headcount, vendor contracts, and network rationalisation; identified and largely de-risked [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage assumed to improve as revenue stabilises; this is mechanically correct but depends on revenue not declining further [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: No explicit price increase assumption embedded in guidance; management is not assuming ARPU recovery from price rises, only from mix (postpaid vs prepaid shift stabilising) [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Interest cost is ~€250-300m per annum on ~€4bn net debt; EPS sensitivity to rate changes is high; management's EPS bridge relies on EBITDA growth absorbing interest and depreciation [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Spain corporate tax rate (25%) assumed stable; no guidance on deferred tax asset utilisation from prior Vodafone Spain losses [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: ZEG reports in EUR; no material FX exposure given Spanish domestic operations [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: Spanish GDP growth ~2.0-2.5% (IMF 2025 forecast) provides benign consumer backdrop; this supports fixed / fibre stability but does not rescue mobile ARPU dynamics [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Credibility verdict: Management guidance credibility is MEDIUM — cost-side commitments are credible and the track record supports them; revenue-side commitments are optimistic and have a consistent miss pattern with no convergence [#J]

  • Green flag — cost delivery: Cost savings programme is ahead of initial pace; EBITDA beats in all 4 observable periods, even if narrow [#A]

  • Green flag — CapEx discipline: CapEx has consistently come in below guidance; capital allocation track record is credible and suggests financial discipline [#A]

  • Green flag — management track record (ex-ZEG): CEO Eamonn O'Hare (former Vodafone CFO, former Virgin Media) has a documented track record of telco restructuring delivery; peer comparison to other European telco turnaround cases (BT under Philip Jansen, Vodafone Italy disposal) supports credibility of cost-led thesis [#A]

  • Green flag — leverage trajectory: Net debt / EBITDA is tracking toward the guided deleveraging path; no covenant stress signals in observable periods [#A]

  • Red flag — revenue miss pattern: 3-4 consistent revenue misses with no improvement trend is a material credibility red flag; management has not produced a single quarter of revenue outperformance [#J]

  • Red flag — RGU optimism: Postpaid RGU recovery has been the most consistently and most widely missed sub-metric; management continues to frame it as "near-term" without accountability for prior deferrals [#A]

  • Red flag — medium-term gap vs SS: SS consensus is 10-15% below management's FY 2026-2027 EBITDA targets; this gap is wide enough to suggest the market is not buying the medium-term case [#A]

  • Red flag — synergy pacing: €150m cost savings target is tracking approximately €70m behind schedule as of FY 2024; completion risk in 2025 is moderate [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EBITDA: If EBITDA guidance is off by 10% (€125-135m shortfall on FY 2025 target), FCF positivity likely disappears and net debt trajectory reverses; the investment is not robust to a 10% EBITDA miss given the leverage [#J]

  • Margin of safety — revenue: If revenue declines 5% vs guidance (not a tail scenario given prior miss pattern), the EBITDA and FCF implications are severe enough to trigger a re-rating conversation [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis as typically framed (FCF yield 15-20% at steady state, deleveraging to <3x) requires near-full execution of guidance; it is not a wide-margin-of-safety thesis — it is a precise-execution thesis [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — credible on costs, optimistic on revenue, leverage amplifies any miss into a significant downside scenario [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §7: The bearish read on credibility is that it is brittle: the cost-savings programme is finite and largely identified; once the €150m is fully realised (~end-2025), the credibility crutch disappears and the thesis becomes entirely dependent on revenue recovery — precisely the area with the worst track record; apparent MEDIUM credibility degrades to LOW by FY 2026 if revenue doesn't turn [#J]
