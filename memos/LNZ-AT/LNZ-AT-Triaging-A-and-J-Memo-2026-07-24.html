B.1 Business Description 8 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Specialty cellulosic fiber manufacturer. Lenzing produces wood-based dissolving pulp and regenerated cellulosic fibers (viscose, modal, lyocell) marketed under TENCEL, VEOCEL, and LENZING ECOVERO brands, operating nine production sites globally with ~1 million ton annual fiber capacity
2.
Two-division structure with severe profitability divergence. Division Pulp delivered EUR 452m EBITDA in FY25 while Division Fiber collapsed to EUR 0.1m EBITDA, creating a business where the upstream integration asset generates all economic value and the core downstream fiber business destroys it
3.
Deep vertical integration. Approximately 95% backward integration into dissolving wood pulp through biorefineries in Austria, Czech Republic, and a 600,000-ton Brazilian joint venture with Dexco operating at 20% above design capacity
4.
Revenue scale and geographic mix. EUR 2.60bn FY25 revenue with 61% from Asia, 29% Europe, 9% Americas; two-thirds of fiber sales target the cyclical apparel market, one-third the more stable nonwovens/hygiene segment
5.
Triopoly market position. Lenzing, Sateri (2.1m tons), and Birla Cellulose collectively control over 80% of global specialty cellulosic capacity; Lenzing holds 75% modal and 99% TENCEL-branded lyocell market shares
6.
Capital structure under stress. EUR 500m subordinated perpetual hybrid bond at 9% coupon (300-400bps above peer median), EUR 545m syndicated facility with restrictive quarterly covenants, net leverage 3.3x, dividends indefinitely suspended
7.
Intense self-help program. Performance Program delivered EUR 200m+ cumulative cost savings, 600 Austrian position cuts targeting EUR 45m annual savings by 2027, but three CEO transitions in under two years (Sielaff to Aggarwal to Kasperkovitz) undermine execution credibility
8.
Sector classification. Chemicals and Specialty Materials zone, forestry and paper products sub-sector; cyclical exposure to apparel demand and commodity fiber pricing with regulatory tailwinds from EU Bioeconomy Strategy and UK single-use plastics ban
