B.1 Business Description 9 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Sovereign digital-services platform, post-NetCo. Telecom Italia completed the EUR 22bn NetCo disposal to KKR in July 2024 and now operates exclusively as an asset-light "ServCo" entity across three segments: TIM Consumer, TIM Enterprise and TIM Brasil
2.
Domestic Italian mobile and fixed operator. The Consumer segment serves Italian retail mobile and fixed-broadband customers in a hyper-competitive, historically deflationary market, with Q1 2026 domestic service revenue of EUR 2,068m
3.
Enterprise arm pivoting to sovereign cloud and AI. TIM Enterprise extended its growth streak to 15 quarters (+4.2% YoY revenue), driven by a 14.5% expansion in cloud services tied to Italy's National Strategic Hub public-sector contract
4.
TIM Brasil is the group's hard-currency growth and cash engine. Brazil delivered its eleventh consecutive quarter of EBITDAaL growth, beating local consensus by 2.6%, and contributes roughly 44% of group EBITDAaL
5.
Financial character: revenue scale versus margin structure. FactSet-blended group revenue is approximately EUR 14.5bn (LTM)
5.1
Detail: the domestic ServCo model mechanically caps EBITDAaL margins near 21% because wholesale network access fees (paid to FiberCop and other infrastructure owners) now sit inside opex rather than being internalised as capex
6.
Balance sheet materially de-risked. Post-NetCo target net debt to EBITDAaL is guided below 1.7x by 2026, group net financial debt closed at EUR 7.5bn, and Fitch upgraded the credit rating to BB+
7.
Investment-style implication: event-driven turnaround, not a quality compounder. The case sits closer to an asset-light corporate-restructuring and M&A-catalyst archetype than a steady compounder,
7.1
Detail: given the binary Poste Italiane tender offer, the CFO transition, and the still-unresolved cash-conversion gap versus vertically integrated European peers
8.
Ownership and control now consolidating around Poste Italiane. Vivendi's complete 23.75% stake exit to Poste Italiane in 2025 removed the multi-year activist overhang,
8.1
Detail: and Poste's March 2026 EUR 10.8bn voluntary tender offer for 100% of the company is the dominant near-term corporate-action driver
9.
Segment mix is shifting toward higher-multiple, lower-margin revenue. Connectivity fell from 38% of Enterprise service revenues in 2023 to a projected 29% by 2026, replaced by lower-margin cloud and security services that management frames as higher-quality and stickier
