§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

J-front verdict: Organic revenue growth is structurally bifurcated — Networks accelerating (government/data demand) while Video declining faster than guided; the combined entity creates noise but does not change this underlying dynamic [#J]

  • Reported growth: Group reported revenue growth in H2 2024 positive due to Intelsat consolidation; full-year FY2024 reported revenue ~USD 1.714bn (legacy SES) vs ~USD 1.743bn FY2023 — a slight reported decline on legacy standalone basis [#J]
  • The YoY decline reflects Video deterioration outpacing Networks growth at the legacy SES level

  • On a pro-forma combined basis (including full-year Intelsat), revenue approximately USD 3.3-3.4bn

  • Organic growth: Networks segment organic growth ~+6% in FY2024; Video segment organic decline ~-8 to -9%; group organic approximately -1 to -2% [#J]

  • Networks growth driven by government/defence (US DoD, NATO infrastructure), managed mobility, and O3b mPOWER early commercial wins

  • Video decline driven by broadcaster rationalisation, OTT substitution, platform consolidation — structural, not cyclical

  • Segment trajectory: Networks now approximately 51% of legacy SES revenue and growing; Video 49% and declining; crossover to Networks-majority happened in FY2023/FY2024 transition [#A]

  • mPOWER is the growth engine within Networks but utilisation is still sub-breakeven

  • Intelsat adds significant government/mobility networks exposure, accelerating the mix shift

  • Price contribution: Satellite pricing is contract-driven (multi-year transponder leases); price contribution is modest and largely fixed within contract periods; the growth vector is volume (new capacity sold) not pricing [#I]

  • mPOWER pricing per Mbps is premium vs legacy GEO but the relevant metric is total throughput sold vs total capacity available

  • No evidence of pricing deterioration in Networks; Video pricing under pressure as broadcasters demand discounts on renewals

  • Pricing power: Limited near-term pricing power in Video (buyer's market as broadcasters defect to OTT); strong in government/defence Networks where SES has sole-provider coverage advantages [#A]

  • Market share: Networks — SES gaining share in government MEO (mPOWER vs legacy ViaSat-3 delays); Video — SES losing share as the market shrinks; overall satellite market share picture complicated by Starlink entry into the LEO/government segment [#J]

  • End-market demand: Government/defence demand robust (geopolitical tailwinds — Ukraine conflict, NATO spending uplift); commercial video structurally declining; commercial mobility (maritime, aeronautical) recovering post-COVID but competitive [#A]

  • Forward revenue guidance: Management guided FY2025 USD ~2.0-2.1bn (reflecting first full-year combined entity contribution, rebased); organic growth target approximately +low-single-digit to +mid-single-digit within Networks [#A]

  • Sell-side revisions: Post-FY2024 revenue revisions modest positive (Intelsat contribution fuller than modelled) but EPS revisions negative (higher D&A); net consensus revenue slightly up for FY2025-26, EPS slightly down [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish revenue case is that mPOWER's government contract pipeline converts more slowly than modelled (procurement cycles 18-36M), Video declines faster than the -8% run rate (broadcaster consolidation accelerates), and Intelsat's legacy C-band revenue (post-FCC spectrum clearing payment) disappoints — these three vectors compounding would make the FY2025 guidance range look optimistic by H2 2025

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: Adjusted EBITDA margins resilient at ~57% but below-the-line pressure from D&A and integration costs is compressing reported earnings quality; the margin picture is better than EPS suggests [#J]

  • Gross margin: Satellite sector gross margins structurally high (~70%+ for pure-play operators) given near-zero marginal cost of additional transponder capacity; SES gross margins consistent with this structure [#A]
  • Gross margin not the primary analytic for SES; EBITDA and FCF conversion are the operative metrics

  • Note: post-Intelsat, managed services mix (lower gross margin than pure leasing) may gradually dilute gross margin

  • EBIT margin: Adjusted EBIT margin approximately 28-30% in FY2024; declining vs FY2022/FY2023 (~32-34%) due to D&A step-up from mPOWER fleet activation [#J]

  • This is the expected and guided trajectory — not a surprise, but a real headwind to EPS

  • D&A will remain elevated until mPOWER throughput sold generates incremental EBITDA to offset

  • EBITDA margin: Adjusted EBITDA margin ~57% in FY2024, within guided 55-58% range; stable vs FY2023 (~57-58%); satellite sector comparable — Eutelsat guided ~60%+ pre-distress, Viasat ~40-45% (more services mix) [#A]

  • EBITDA margin stability while EBIT margin declines = D&A is the gap, which is expected

  • Management maintains EBITDA margin as the primary external KPI; sell-side concurs

  • Margin direction: EBITDA margin flat-to-stable; EBIT margin declining; reported net margin under pressure — the divergence is mechanical (D&A) but real [#J]

  • Operating leverage: Negative operating leverage at EBIT level in FY2024 — revenue roughly flat while D&A rose; expected to reverse when mPOWER utilisation crosses breakeven and incremental EBITDA exceeds incremental D&A [#A]

  • The operating leverage inflection is the central thesis catalyst — timing is the debate

  • Management framing: FY2025-26 as the D&A peak years, FY2027+ as the margin recovery trajectory

  • D&A trend: D&A rising materially — mPOWER fleet (7 satellites, ~USD 2.5bn capital cost) being depreciated; expected to peak approximately FY2025-26; historically satellite D&A runs 15-20% of revenue for GEO operators, mPOWER era lifting this toward 25-28% [#I]

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A and R&D well-controlled; satellite operators have structurally low variable OpEx post-launch; cost base is relatively fixed once fleet is in orbit [#A]

  • One-off costs: FY2024 includes significant integration charges (Intelsat) and restructuring costs estimated USD 150-200M; these inflate reported cost base and depress reported margins vs adjusted [#I]

  • Adjusted vs reported gap: The adjusted-to-reported gap is at its widest in recent history — investors following only adjusted EBITDA are missing the integration cost drag that runs through cash flow [#J]

  • Non-GAAP adjustments: Key exclusions — Intelsat integration costs, fleet-related impairments, FX translation, rebranding costs; management view is these are genuinely non-recurring; sceptical view is that "non-recurring" restructuring has been recurring for 4+ years [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish margin read is that adjusted EBITDA stability is manufactured via cost control (headcount reductions, opex deferrals) rather than genuine revenue-driven leverage; the mechanism is that once integration synergies are fully realised (FY2026?), there is no further opex release and margins plateau at a level that doesn't support the bull case multiple; meanwhile D&A remains elevated through FY2027, meaning reported EPS may not recover to pre-mPOWER levels until FY2028

  • Interest burden rising post-Intelsat acquisition (additional debt assumed); expected to moderate as debt is repaid

  • Cash taxes relatively low given tax treaty structures (SES is Luxembourg-domiciled)

  • Cash conversion: 80-85% OCF/EBITDA conversion is sector-appropriate; for context, Eutelsat historically 75-85%; Viasat lower given higher services/equipment mix; SES conversion quality reasonable [#A]

  • NWC trend: Working capital is not a material swing factor for satellite operators (revenue is contract-driven, invoiced periodically); no significant WC deterioration observed in FY2024 [#I]

  • Capex: FY2024 capex approximately USD 600-700M (mPOWER final satellite payments + legacy fleet maintenance + Intelsat capex post-close); capex/revenue ratio ~35-40%, well above sector steady-state [#J]

  • This is the peak capex year expectation; management guided capex declining materially from FY2025 onward as mPOWER construction payments complete

  • Peak capex periods are normal for satellite operators post-fleet order; the question is whether the revenue ramp justifies the spend

  • Free cash flow: FCF approximately USD 200-300M in FY2024 after capex; FCF yield on USD 3bn market cap approximately 7-10%; modest but positive [#A]

  • FCF improving trajectory as capex cycle unwinds — the bull case FCF yield in FY2027 is significantly higher if mPOWER ramps

  • FCF is the ultimate validation metric for the thesis

  • Net debt: Net debt approximately USD 3.5-4.0bn post-Intelsat combination; net debt/EBITDA approximately 3.0-3.5x on combined adjusted EBITDA basis [#I]

  • Leverage within the guided range but at the higher end; covenant headroom exists but not generous

  • Intelsat C-band relocation proceeds (FCC payments) expected to contribute USD 1.3bn+ to deleveraging — this is a significant asset that reduces reported leverage over FY2024-26

  • Dividend coverage: Dividend maintained at approximately EUR 0.50/share; FCF covers dividend approximately 1.5-2.0x; sustainable at current levels but growth unlikely until leverage reduction is confirmed [#A]

  • Buyback pace: Zero buybacks in FY2024; leverage and integration uncertainty preclude buybacks; if deleverage trajectory is on track, buybacks could emerge FY2026-27 — a potential positive catalyst [#I]

  • Capital return sustainability: Dividend sustainable; buybacks deferred; the capital return story is a FY2026+ event, not near-term [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish cash flow case is that mPOWER capex overruns or Intelsat integration costs exceed guidance, pushing FCF negative for longer than guided; the mechanism is that government contract delays (the primary mPOWER customer) push throughput revenue past FY2025, meaning the FCF inflection is FY2027-28 not FY2025-26 — at which point the leverage covenant picture becomes more complex; additionally, if C-band relocation proceeds face legal or regulatory delays, the deleverage timeline slips
