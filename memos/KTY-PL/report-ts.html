Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Grupa Kety operates a highly integrated aluminium manufacturing and packaging model, combining leading domestic market shares with expanding export exposure across Europe.

Operational Segments and Revenue Drivers

1.
Dominant extrusion division: The Extruded Products Segment manufactures custom soft-alloy profiles and finished fabricated components at sites in Poland and Slovenia, acting as Poland's market leader with a 25.00% share of production
2.
High-margin architectural systems: The Architectural Systems Segment designs and sells window, door, and façade systems under the Aluprof brand, which is a major supplier to European commercial and residential builders
3.
Strategic sun-shading separation: Established as a distinct segment in 2025, the Sun-shading Systems Segment produces shutters, blinds, and pergolas, powered by the 2024 acquisition of SELT sp. z o.o. in Poland
4.
Scale flexible packaging: The Flexible Packaging Segment produces high-performance barrier plastic laminates and is the largest Polish manufacturer of BOPP films, with an annual capacity of 60,000.00 tons
5.
Order-backed revenue mix: Revenue is predominantly transactional and order-based rather than recurring, with 100.00% of Extruded Products and Flexible Packaging manufactured strictly against specific client orders
6.
Strong internal synergies: The Extruded Products segment acts as a key upstream supplier within the Group, routing 23.00% of its aluminium profile output directly into the downstream Architectural Systems division
7.
Low-carbon fabrication departments: The group operates an Advanced Aluminium Products Plant, a specialized department equipped with CNC machining, bending, and assembly robots to deliver high-margin finished components

Competitive Moats and Industry Position

8.
Low carbon advantage: The group operates its own foundry in Kety, recycling 40,000.00 tons of scrap annually and achieving a 75.00% scrap share in soft-alloy billets to lower carbon intensity
9.
Optimal chain positioning: Positioned in mid-stream processing and downstream design, the group captures high-value architectural branding via Aluprof while avoiding raw upstream mining and smelting volatility
10.
Low customer concentration: Concentration risks are remarkably low, as no single external customer exceeds 10.00% of consolidated Group sales, and its largest architectural client, Eko-Okna, was 5.00% of revenues in 2023
11.
Certified switching moats: The Architectural Systems division benefits from high switching costs, as building developers certify specific Aluprof systems during the multi-year design and permit phases of construction
12.
Dispersed industry landscape: The European aluminium systems market is highly dispersed with established players like Schuco, Reynaers, and Sapa, allowing Aluprof to capture market share through price-to-performance advantages
13.
Ukrainian manufacturing footprints: Upstream processing is supported by foreign fabrication plants, including Alupol LLC in Ukraine, which previously suspended operations but has been integrated back into supply plans
14.
Other side of the story: Intense international competition from low-cost Turkish and Chinese extruders continues to flood the European market, placing severe long-term pressure on trade margins and pricing power for basic profiles

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The primary risk to Grupa Kety's earnings trajectory is macroeconomic cyclicality in European construction, although this is partially offset by highly defensive packaging.

Macroeconomic and Commodity Risks

1.
Construction industry cyclicality: The main risk is that the group's total revenue exposure to the construction and interior design sectors is 61.00%, exposing earnings directly to macroeconomic cycles
2.
LME aluminium volatility: Direct exposure to raw material price swings is managed via hedging, but extreme spikes can rapidly inflate working capital requirements and deplete cash
3.
Geopolitical supply disruptions: Potential disruptions in logistics or raw material access, particularly regarding aluminium scrap and granulates, could threaten the group's three-month buffer stock
4.
Foreign exchange exposure: Standalone operations manage substantial derivative contracts, hedging Polish zloty and Euro swings that impact export competitiveness
5.
Billet premiums swings: Sharp increases in North Germany billet premiums, which previously peaked at USD 600.00 per ton, directly compress fabrication margins

Regulatory and Operational Challenges

6.
Carbon border adjustments: The implementation of the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) fails to fully protect the European market, exposing Grupa Kety to cheaper, carbon-intensive imports
7.
Polish wage inflation: Severe domestic labour cost inflation in Poland threatens to compress manufacturing margins across all four primary business divisions
8.
Slowing Western Europe: Western European export markets, particularly Germany, represent a persistent pocket of economic weakness that limits overall extrusion segment utilization rates
9.
Polymer raw shortages: Flexible packaging remains vulnerable to petrochemical shortages, where LDPE and PP resins are highly sensitive to logistics choke points
10.
Scope emissions regulations: Strict transition regulations require significant investments in zero-emission transport and low-carbon foundries, posing capital allocation risks
11.
Other side of the story: These risks are significantly overstated because the Group's advanced custom fabrication capabilities and recycling foundries allow it to pass through raw material costs to clients
