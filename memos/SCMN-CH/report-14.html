§2 — Key Driver: Swiss Broadband / FTTH ARPU Trajectory

  • Net verdict: Swiss broadband ARPU shows modest positive trajectory but FTTH monetisation pace is below what a bull case requires; medium plausibility that ARPU expansion reaches 3-4% over 24M [#J]

Define the driver precisely:

  • Driver definition: Swiss residential broadband ARPU expands as FTTH rollout completes and customers upsell to higher-tier packages (1Gbps+), driving Swiss segment EBITDA above consensus [#A]
  • Magnitude required: ARPU growth of approximately 3-4% per annum over FY25-27 (from a base of approximately CHF 55-60/month blended residential broadband) to contribute ~50-80bps EBITDA margin expansion in the Swiss segment [#A]
  • Baseline: Swisscom's FTTH rollout covered approximately 3.5-4 million homes passed by end-2024, with take-up rates around 30-35% of homes passed; Swisscom publicly targets ~4 million FTTH homes by 2025 [#I]
  • Target state: 35-40% FTTH take-up with meaningful proportion at premium 1Gbps+ tiers (currently estimated sub-20% of FTTH base) within 24 months [#A]
  • Required trajectory: Incremental ~5pp take-up improvement per year; tier-upgrade upsell at approximately 10-15% of existing base annually [#A]

Bull case:

  • Bull — historical: Swisscom achieved sustained broadband ARPU above CHF 50/month for the past five-plus years, demonstrating pricing power in a high-income market with limited infrastructure competition [#A]
  • Bull — recent: Swisscom's 2024 full-year results showed Swiss revenue resilience; management indicated stable-to-improving residential broadband pricing with no material price war pressure from Salt or Sunrise [#I]
  • Bull — mgmt: Management has consistently guided for FTTH as an ARPU-upgrade mechanism; 2024 CMD (Capital Markets Day) messaging emphasised premium tier penetration as a medium-term ARPU driver [#A]
  • Bull — competitive: Sunrise's own FTTH build is co-dependent on Swisscom's network in many cantons via open-access arrangements, limiting Sunrise's ability to undercut aggressively on FTTH tiers [#A]
  • Bull — industry: European incumbent FTTH monetisation data (Proximus Belgium, BT Openreach) suggests 2-3pp ARPU lift in years 2-4 post-rollout completion; Swisscom's high-income Swiss base supports upper-end of this range [#I]

Bear case:

  • Bear — historical drag: ❌ Swisscom's Swiss residential ARPU has been broadly flat in nominal terms for 2019-2022, demonstrating that infrastructure upgrade alone does not automatically translate to ARPU expansion in Swiss market [#A]
  • Bear — competitive pressure: ❌ Sunrise (post-UPC merger) has improved its network quality and competitive positioning significantly; Salt continues aggressive mobile bundling that competes on price in the residential convergence market [#A]
  • Bear — structural: ❌ Switzerland's high absolute price levels limit further upsell headroom; at CHF 60+/month, Swiss broadband is already among the most expensive in Europe, compressing willingness-to-pay for premium tiers [#I]
  • Bear — execution: Swisscom's FTTH rollout has faced some construction cost inflation and permitting delays in dense urban areas; take-up rates in newly-passed areas have been slower than in initial rollout cohorts [#I]
  • Bear — SS skeptic: European incumbent telecoms analysts generally model Swiss residential revenue growth at 0-2% p.a. for 2025-2027; above 3% would be an upside-revision scenario, not a base case [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Quarterly trajectory: Current observed ARPU growth approximately 1-2% YoY; to reach 3-4% requires acceleration in upsell activity and/or take-up rate improvement [#A]
  • Acceleration needed: Approximately 1.5-2x the current run-rate; not unprecedented for FTTH monetisation phase, but requires specific commercial execution [#I]
  • Key dependency: Management's willingness to price premium tiers above market and consumers' uptake of gigabit packages; also dependent on no competitive price response from Sunrise [#A]
  • Downside scenario: If ARPU stays flat to +1% (base-rate continuation), Swiss segment EBITDA grows at ~0-1% p.a. vs a bull assumption of 2-3%, reducing group-level EPS contribution by approximately 3-5% [#A]
  • Probability — verbal: Medium plausibility — the structural conditions exist but the pace of monetisation has historically disappointed; acceleration is possible but not the base case

Sell-side and management expectations:

  • Consensus alignment: European telecom consensus broadly models Swiss segment EBITDA at low single-digit growth; ARPU expansion above 2% would be an upside scenario [#I]
  • Management guidance alignment: Swisscom management guides for "stable Swiss market" with "gradual improvement in broadband mix"; language is cautious, not aspirational [#A]
  • Estimate revision L12M: No material upward revisions to Swiss segment ARPU estimates observed; flat-to-modest revision trend [#I]

  • Evidence gap: Premium tier (1Gbps+) penetration as a percentage of FTTH base is not separately disclosed; without this, the ARPU upsell potential is hard to size precisely [#I]
