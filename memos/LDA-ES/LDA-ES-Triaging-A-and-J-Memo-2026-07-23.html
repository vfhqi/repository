B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Pure direct-to-consumer Spanish insurer. Linea Directa Aseguradora underwrites personal-lines motor, home and health insurance via telephone and digital channels only, with zero physical branches, from a single Tres Cantos headquarters
2.
Motor-dominated premium mix. Motor represents 81.4% of the EUR 1,134.7m FY25 gross written premium base, Home 14.8%, Health (the Vivaz brand) 3.4%, plus a nascent 2025 Commercial multi-risk line
3.
Spain-only footprint. Operations are almost entirely Spanish; a residual Portuguese assistance-only presence is immaterial and excluded from segment reporting
4.
Customer base of 3.73 million policyholders in FY25, rising to 3.80 million by Q1 2026. Customer numbers grew 8.5% year-on-year, recovering from a 2024 repricing-driven dip
5.
Vertically integrated claims handling. The group owns 100% of Centro Avanzado de Reparaciones (CAR), completing over 12,000 digital vehicle repairs annually, plus a roadside-assistance subsidiary
6.
Spun off from Bankinter in April 2021. Bankinter retains a 17.42% legacy equity stake with zero board seats since the IPO
7.
Financial character is a recovering underwriter, not a settled compounder. FY25 net profit of EUR 85.7m (+33.5% year-on-year) followed a EUR 4.4m FY23 net loss
8.
Digitally intensive operating model. Over 90% of policyholder interactions are digital, and generative AI resolves up to 64% of customer queries automatically
9.
Investment style implication: cyclical-recovery, event-driven. The case rests on a motor-pricing cycle turning in the company's favour plus a digital cost-efficiency programme, both reversible if competitive discipline breaks
10.
Thin analyst coverage. Sell-side commentary is described in the source reports as "thin or absent," roughly four named analysts, a data-gap flag carried into Master Rating 2
