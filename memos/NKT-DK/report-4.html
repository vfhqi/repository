2. KEY FINDINGS - Outperformer verdict: NKT is executing in the top tier of the listed cable peer group on revenue growth and EBIT margin expansion, driven by structurally elevated power cable demand — not just cyclical uplift [#J]

  • Revenue acceleration: NKT reported ~22% organic revenue growth in FY2024, substantially above Prysmian's ~11% organic and Nexans' ~8% organic for comparable periods — gap is widening not narrowing [#J]
  • Margin inflection: EBIT margin expanded ~250bps to approximately 9.0% in FY2024 from ~6.5% in FY2022 — Prysmian's comparable margin ~11-12%, so NKT is closing the peer gap but not yet at par [#A]
  • EPS acceleration: Adjusted EPS roughly doubled over FY2022-FY2024 on NKT's own reporting; the fulcrum driver is operating leverage on high-voltage offshore cable volume, which carries structurally higher margins than legacy land cable [#J]
  • Guidance credibility strong: NKT has consistently guided conservatively on EBIT and then beaten; FY2024 EBIT guidance was raised twice intra-year — a pattern the company has repeated across FY2022 and FY2023 [#A]
  • Sector tailwind is structural: European offshore wind buildout plus grid modernisation (REPowerEU + national infrastructure spending) are multi-year demand drivers that underpin peer-group revenue visibility through 2028+ [#A]
  • Backlog is the load-bearing number: NKT's order backlog reached approximately DKK 23-25bn by end-2024 (2+ years of HV revenue cover) — Nexans and Prysmian show similar backlog lengthening, confirming sector not company-specific [#A]
  • Key risk — execution on Victoria cable-ship capacity: NKT's growth depends on timely utilisation of cable-laying vessel capacity; any delay in vessel deployment or project commissioning creates asymmetric downside risk vs consensus forecasts [#J]
  • Earnings quality solid: Cash conversion above 80% in FY2024; working capital stable; no material non-GAAP gap flagged in public filings — passes basic quality screen [#A]
  • ❌ Peer valuation premium: NKT trades at a material premium to Nexans on EV/EBIT and P/E; premium is partially justified by superior backlog composition but creates sensitivity to any guidance miss [#J]
