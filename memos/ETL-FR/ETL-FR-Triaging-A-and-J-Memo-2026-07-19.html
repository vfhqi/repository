B.1 Business Description 8 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Eutelsat is a Franco-British satellite operator combining legacy geostationary capacity leasing for broadcast video with a newly consolidated low-earth-orbit connectivity arm built on OneWeb
2.
Revenue mix. Two verticals dominate: geostationary video, which is 46% of operating vertical revenue and in secular decline, and high-speed connectivity across mobile, fixed, government and maritime, which is scaling off a smaller base
3.
Financial scale. Full-year revenue for fiscal year 2023-24 was EUR 1,213m; organic revenue grew only 0.10% like-for-like in the first half of fiscal year 2025-26 as reported revenue fell 2.40% on video declines
4.
Margin profile. Adjusted EBITDA margin has compressed from 73.0% in fiscal year 2022-23 to 52.1% in the first half of fiscal year 2025-26, a near three-year, twenty-one-hundred-basis-point decline
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt to EBITDA fell from 3.88 times to 2.00 times after a completed EUR 1.5bn capital raise, but is guided back up to 2.70 times by June 2026 on constellation capital expenditure
6.
Ownership. The French state holds 29.65% and the UK government 10.89%; strategic investors Bharti Space (17.88%) and CMA CGM Participations (7.46%) complete a concentrated shareholder register
7.
Geography and segments. Operations span global geostationary video distribution plus low-earth-orbit-enabled mobile connectivity for aviation and maritime customers, fixed connectivity, and government and sovereign services across multiple continents
8.
Investment style implication. This is an event-driven, unproven-pivot case, not a quality compounder
8.1
Supporting detail. Negative earnings per share of roughly -EUR 0.42 in the first half of fiscal year 2026, a capital-intensive multi-year build, and a management team with a flagged history of guidance resets
