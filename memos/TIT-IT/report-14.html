§2 — Key Driver: NetCo/Infrastructure Separation and Capital Structure Relief

  • Magnitude required: Gross debt reduction from ~€25-26bn (pre-close) to ~€7-9bn (post-close ServCo perimeter); net debt/EBITDA to move from ~4-5× toward ~2-3× on ServCo basis [#A]
  • Base-rate anchor: European incumbent telco median net leverage at close of major asset disposals ~2.2× (Telecom Austria, Telenor asset sales 2018-2023)
  • Baseline: Pre-disposal TIM carried ~€25.4bn gross debt (FY2023); interest burden ~€1.3bn p.a.; credit rating sub-investment-grade (BB/Ba2) [#A]
  • Target state: ServCo net debt ~€7-8bn by end-2025; interest cost reduction ~€600-700m p.a.; rating trajectory toward investment grade over 24-36M [#A]

Bull case — historical:
- Bull — precedent: Telecom Italia Brasil (TIM Brasil) separation precedent shows infrastructure carve-outs can re-rate parent equity 20-30% over 12M (2019-2021 period) [#A]
- Bull — recent: KKR transaction closed July 2024; net proceeds of ~€14.2bn confirmed by TIM; immediate reduction in consolidated gross debt confirmed in H2 2024 reporting [#A]
- Bull — mgmt: CEO Pietro Labriola has consistently framed ServCo as "asset-light, cash-generative" post-disposal; 2025 guidance includes reduced capex intensity (~12-14% of revenues vs ~18-20% pre-disposal) [#A]
- Bull — competitive: Asset-light model aligns TIM with Iliad France / Bouygues structural analogy — leased-access operators can generate higher ROIC than vertically integrated incumbents at steady state [#A]

Bear case:
- Bear — structural: ❌ ServCo now pays wholesale access fees to NetCo (KKR entity); lease cost is a fixed claim on ServCo revenues; if revenues decline, margin compression is amplified vs pre-disposal structure [#J]
- Bear — execution: ❌ ServCo EBITDA base post-disposal is structurally smaller (~€2.0-2.3bn target vs ~€4.5bn pre-disposal group EBITDA); interest coverage ratio improvement is partly illusory if EBITDA also shrinks [#J]
