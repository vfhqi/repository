§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Governance structure is conventionally CLEAN — no dual-class shares, no restatements, no material weaknesses, no significant litigation; structural simplicity of the concession model limits governance complexity risk.

Dual-class structure:
- Absent: Getlink has a single class of ordinary shares on Euronext Paris; no dual-class, no golden shares specific to management control
- No founder control mechanism: Jacques Gounon did not retain a control block; ownership is dispersed among institutional investors (Eurotunnel's restructuring legacy means no founding-family bloc)

Voting rights:
- Standard one-share-one-vote: Consistent with AFEP-MGDE and French company law for this structure
- Loyalty shares: Under French law (Florange Act 2014), listed companies can grant double voting rights to long-term shareholders (held >2 years); Getlink's URD should be checked for whether this mechanism is in use; if so, it modestly advantages long-term institutional holders but does not create a control distortion

Related-party transactions:
- Eurostar relationship: Historical equity stake in Eurostar International creates potential related-party complexity; the reduction/exit of the Eurostar equity stake under Leriche reduces this concern
- ElecLink counterparties: ElecLink's capacity contracts involve UK and French national grid operators; these are arm's-length regulatory contracts, not related-party transactions
- No material concern identified: Disclosed related-party transactions in URD appear to be administrative/transitional in nature; no outsized flow of value to insiders identified

Audit history:
- Auditor: Getlink uses two statutory auditors (standard under French corporate law for listed companies — co-commissaires aux comptes structure); major firms in rotation
- No auditor change of concern: No surprise auditor departure or non-standard rotation noted
- No restatements: No material restatement of financial statements in available disclosure history
- No material weakness: No material weakness reported; clean audit sign-offs

Legal / regulatory issues:
- Competition scrutiny: Cross-Channel transport has historically attracted EU competition scrutiny (pricing, capacity allocation between Eurostar and Le Shuttle); no current live investigation of substance identified
- Brexit regulatory complexity: ElecLink commissioning required navigating post-Brexit regulatory approvals in both OFGEM and CRE (France); Getlink successfully obtained both — resolved risk, not ongoing flag
- No class action / securities litigation: No public evidence of shareholder class action or securities fraud investigation
- Concession regulatory risk: The primary structural governance risk is the French-UK concession treaty framework — any renegotiation is a political/regulatory event, not a management governance event; this sits outside this section's scope

Whistleblower / governance complaints:
- No public signal: No public whistleblower case, no published governance complaint through AMF (French securities regulator) channels visible
- French norms: French whistleblower culture in infrastructure companies is less developed than Anglo-Saxon equivalents; absence of public signal has lower diagnostic value than for a US-listed company

Anti-takeover provisions:
- Concession as structural moat: The concession structure itself is the primary takeover defence — a hostile acquirer would need concession authority approval; this is structural, not a corporate-governance anti-takeover mechanism
- No poison pill / staggered board: No evidence of aggressive shareholder-hostile anti-takeover provisions

Sceptical bullet:
- BEAR READ on governance: The cleanliness of governance reflects in part the structural simplicity of a mature concession — there are fewer governance stress points (no complex M&A pipeline, no founder-controller, no discretionary capital allocation decisions) than in a high-growth company; the true governance test would come in a concession renegotiation scenario where management incentives (career continuation post-concession), French state interests (sovereign infrastructure asset), and shareholder interests (NPV of concession extension terms) could diverge materially, and where board independence and compensation alignment would matter most; that test is not yet live

§8 — Governance Verdict + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The single largest operator risk is not malfeasance but mediocrity — Getlink's governance and management structure is optimised for stable concession operation, not for the incremental capital-allocation and commercial judgment required to unlock ElecLink's full earnings potential.

Reverse-engineered bear case from §1-§7:
- Bear thesis architecture: If Leriche is a competent concession caretaker but not a capital-allocation force-of-nature, and if ElecLink's merchant revenue ramp disappoints because the commercial capability is outmatched by sophisticated energy market counterparties, and if the board — staffed with long-tenure infrastructure generalists — fails to challenge commercial execution, then the post-COVID structural earnings recovery story stalls and Getlink becomes a yield-driven infrastructure stock priced at its terminal concession value rather than a compound earnings growth story
- Mechanism: CEO's compensation is tied to EBITDA and TSR over 3 years; maximum rational effort on ElecLink commercial optimisation is 3-year incentive-horizon; beyond that window, rational executive behaviour shifts toward concession management continuity (career duration optimisation) rather than risk-taking on merchant market positions; the board cannot compensate for this horizon mismatch because director tenures and incentives have the same short-medium horizon
