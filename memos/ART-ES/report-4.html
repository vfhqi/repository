EBITDA margin expansion of 430bps in a single year (11.5% to 15.8%) is extraordinary for an equipment manufacturer. The H2 2025 exit rate of ~16.4% suggests further runway. Three forces — pricing power in supply-constrained markets, product mix shift toward HV/digital, and operating leverage at EUR500m+ scale — are compounding simultaneously.

  • Judgement: The margin trajectory is the strongest story — from 8.7% trough (FY22) to 15.8% (FY25) is 710bps in 3 years, with H2 still accelerating. Arteche is now at parity with Siemens Energy Grid Technologies (15.8%) and ahead of GE Vernova Electrification (14-15%). [C+AS] #J

Sector & Value Chain Context

The power grid equipment sector is in a structural super-cycle — not a standard cyclical peak. USD470bn+ global grid investment in 2025, AI data centre demand growing 17% p.a., transformer lead times at 143 weeks. The rising tide lifts all operators, but the tide is not evenly distributed.

  • Judgement: The sector tailwind is real, structural, and multi-year — driven by grid aging (70%+ US transformers >25 years old), renewable integration, electrification, and AI data centre proliferation. This is not cyclical. [C+AS] #J
  • Judgement: Arteche occupies a challenging middle ground: well-positioned in the rising tide but structurally detached from the hyper-growth vectors (AI data centres, HVDC mega-projects) that are driving explosive momentum at Tier-1 peers. [AS] #J
  • Analysis: Supply chain bottlenecks are the defining value chain characteristic. Extended lead times (12-24 months for HV ITs, up to 4.5 years for power transformers) shift power from buyers to manufacturers, supporting pricing discipline across all product categories. [C+AS] #A
  • Analysis: Chinese manufacturers are the medium-term competitive threat — offering pricing up to 40% below Western equivalents, sitting on massive domestic overcapacity, and beginning to penetrate Western supply chains via data centre clients who prioritise delivery speed over traditional procurement rules. [AS] #A
  • Arteche's defensibility rests on 1,500+ utility customer approvals (massive barrier to entry), niche leadership positions (top-2 global in HV ITs, #1 in auxiliary relays), and localised manufacturing across 12 facilities in 8 countries. [AS] #I
  • SF6 regulatory transition risk: EU phased ban by 2030 poses a medium-term execution risk to Arteche's core product portfolio. The AHIT JV with Hitachi Energy (SF6-free GIS) and ECO-X optical range are the mitigation strategies. [AS] #I
