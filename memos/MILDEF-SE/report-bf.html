EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 6 findings

MilDef operates as a highly specialized tactical IT systems integrator serving NATO defense forces, successfully transitioning from a transactional hardware supplier into a high-margin systems house anchored by multi-year framework agreements that create massive customer lock-in through deep physical integration and proprietary software ecosystems .

The company achieved strong revenue expansion in 2025 but faces near-term profitability headwinds from the roda computer acquisition integration. MilDef has successfully migrated up the value chain to capture high-margin system integration contracts, though upstream silicon stages remain highly consolidated and dictate lead times while proprietary software commands the highest margin pools. Leadership under CEO Daniel Ljunggren executes aggressive capacity expansions supported by robust regulatory and physical moats enabling mechanical earnings scale-up.

1.
Business Model and Revenue Dynamics: MilDef operates as a systems integrator supplying rugged tactical IT, combining specialized hardware capable of extreme environments with proprietary software and professional integration services
2.
The business is organized into two geographic divisions: BA Nordics covering Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland with direct government sales, and BA Europe & NA managing UK operations and US B2B partnerships

Hardware represented 78% of 2025 net sales, services and integration contributed 18%, while proprietary software represented 4% of group revenues .

Growth in framework agreements is improving long-term revenue visibility , with order backlog exceeding SEK 4.2 billion providing over 65% coverage of forecasted 2027 sales .

MilDef is evolving from a box supplier into a full systems integrator , demonstrated by securing the SEK 1.5 billion FMV agreement with structurally superior margins on integration sales .

The business model exhibits strong scalability as fixed R&D and administrative costs remain stable while sales expand, driving adjusted EBITA margin of 17.1% in Q1 2026 .

European NATO allies increased defense expenditure by approximately €120 billion over 12 months , expanding average budgets from 1.6% of GDP in 2021 to 2.3% in 2025 .

Key Risks and Limitations: The analysis reveals several material structural risks that temper the otherwise favorable fundamental picture. The business model is highly dependent on lumpy, irregular government contracts which can cause severe quarterly revenue fluctuations

MilDef's fabless model exposes it to severe upstream pricing power and component delays which can squeeze margins and delay high-value system deliveries if key suppliers prioritize larger consumer electronics or prime defense buyers. The customer base exposes MilDef to lumpy, unpredictable government purchasing cycles where budget delays can severely disrupt quarterly earnings .

Persistent reliance on Taiwan-sourced Crete components exposes the business to sudden geopolitical disruptions that cannot be quickly mitigated by European assembly.

Primes could backward-integrate by developing proprietary rugged systems , squeezing out independent sub-tier hardware specialists .

Apparent operator excellence is highly dependent on dilutive acquisitions and lumpy sovereign budgets, with confidence in foundational strength remaining high though the single largest risk is moat erosion if primes backward-integrate .

The totality of evidence suggests MilDef occupies a structurally advantaged position within a multi-decade rearmament cycle , supported by formidable regulatory moats , high switching costs , and mechanical margin expansion as software and systems integration scale.

However, execution risks around acquisition integration , supply chain concentration , and government budget volatility require continuous monitoring to validate the sustainability of competitive advantages and financial performance trajectory.
