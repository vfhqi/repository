Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The company's business model is transitioning toward high-margin engineering services and low-carbon technologies, reducing its exposure to high-risk, lump-sum turnkey construction.

Segment and Product Offerings

1.
Clean fuels refining: The upstream and refining segment provides design and construction for low-emission refining processes, generating €802.9 million or 12.4% of total FY2025 revenue compared to €691.4 million in FY2024
2.
Natural gas concentration: The natural gas segment represents the company's largest business line, reaching €4,244.1 million or 65.6% of FY2025 sales, driven by major Middle East developments
3.
Petrochemicals pipeline: Petrochemicals generated €1,238.2 million or 19.1% of FY2025 revenue, executing key units for European and Central Asian energy operators
4.
Low-carbon technology push: Low-carbon technologies, including hydrogen, carbon capture, and biofuels, generated €122.9 million in FY2025, representing a key strategic growth area
5.
Strategic transition unit: The dedicated Track strategy launched in 2023 focuses on industrial decarbonization, expanding early engineering services in cement, steel, and aviation fuels

Revenue Model and Value Chain Position

6.
Shifting contract mix: Revenue is historically transactional via lump-sum turnkey contracts, but is increasingly shifting to cost-plus, engineering-services-only, and collaborative models
7.
Asset-light value orchestrator: The company sits as an asset-light orchestrator, providing high-value engineering design while outsourcing physical construction and materials procurement to third parties
8.
High customer concentration: Customer concentration is high, with the top five clients accounting for 56.6% of total revenue in FY2025, primarily national oil companies
9.
Absolute geographic tilt: Geographically, the Middle East is the dominant market, generating €4.21 billion or 65% of total FY2025 sales, emphasizing exposure to global oil giants

Competitive Moats and Industry Structure

10.
Massive engineering scale: Moats are based on deep technical expertise and track record, with over one million engineering hours required for complex projects
11.
Optimised hub execution: Competitive advantage is maintained by using a global hub model, combining Madrid headquarters with lower-cost engineering centers in India and Turkey
12.
Industry consolidation tailwinds: Onshore engineering has consolidated due to financial distress of competitors like McDermott, Saipem, and Petrofac, improving the competitive setup
13.
Competitor cost advantages: Sizable Chinese and other low-cost Asian contractors present high barriers in core markets, requiring European firms to focus on technology and partnerships
14.
Other side of the story: Intense cost competition from low-cost Asian contractors, coupled with rising local content requirements in the Middle East, structurally limits historical margin recovery and increases bidding risk

2.
Strait of Hormuz risk: Delivery of heavy equipment behind the Strait of Hormuz remains a critical bottleneck, which could lead to project timeline overruns

Cost inflation exposure: Global supply chain inflation and material shortages can squeeze the profitability of legacy lump-sum turnkey contracts
4.
High customer concentration: High reliance on a few Middle Eastern national oil companies creates significant exposure to changes in regional capital deployment

Execution and Financial Risk

5.
Extreme weather challenges: Working in regions with extreme temperatures, such as the Middle East, poses physical risks that alter construction site schedules
6.
Margin volatility: The low-margin nature of turnkey construction means that even small execution errors can lead to material cash outflows
7.
Regulatory hurdles: Evolving carbon and taxonomy regulations in Europe could impact future project eligibility and reputational standing
8.
Short interest activity: There are no major active short-seller campaigns reported, but high institutional concentration keeps trading volumes relatively thin

Early warning signal: The key operational metric to monitor is the monthly progress of milestone certifications
10.
Other side of the story: The risks are structurally overstated because the company is actively executing projects in partnerships
