EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

HAL Trust operates as an international holding company deploying extreme decentralization across diverse global industries, providing exceptional capital flexibility while concentrating returns in cyclical maritime and energy infrastructure assets. Net asset value expanded dramatically from €15.5bn in 2024 to €17.6bn by Q1 2026, with NAV per share compounding consistently from €147.72 in 2022 to €194.97, driven by strong quoted asset

of consolidated revenues, with the massive step-up from €5.01bn in 2021 to €12.52bn in 2025 primarily driven by full consolidation of this dredging operator. The trust maintains a €2.6bn liquid portfolio providing immediate dry powder for opportunistic acquisitions, enabling large-scale all-cash takeovers during market dislocations without requiring external debt financing. Shares traded at a severe 22% discount to NAV at end-2025, structurally aligned with European peers like GBL and Sofina but wider than Nordic leaders Investor AB and Industrivärden. 1 3

Business foundation and organizational structure

HAL intentionally operates without centralized management or corporate-level risk systems, isolating operational and financial risks within each subsidiary through unique financial structures and independent accountability. Investee companies feature supervisory boards with independent majorities, successfully shielding the broader

holding company from cross-contamination while enabling operators to focus on specific market developments. The portfolio blends highly cyclical maritime markets with defensive biological agriculture and recurring storage revenues, though heavy Boskalis weighting creates significant exposure to lumpy project cycles. The €1.6bn VolkerWessels acquisition in October 2025 demonstrates aggressive willingness to recycle GrandVision proceeds, significantly increasing domestic Dutch civil engineering and residential construction exposure. 6

Financial profile and capital allocation

Operating profit spiked to €4.14bn in 2021 before normalizing to €626m in 2022 and recovering to €2.43bn in 2025, with the 2021 peak driven entirely by the €3.5bn GrandVision disposal capital gain. Cash generated from operating activities reached €1.39bn in H1 2025 alone, though dredging operations require massive sustaining capex and US real estate tied up $420m in low-yielding commitments. HAL strictly targets a cash dividend of 2.5% of the December volume-weighted average share price, increasing sequentially to €3.50 per share in 2025 from €2.50 in 2022. Impressive headline NAV growth relies heavily on mark-to-market fluctuations in cyclical quoted assets like Vopak and SBM, creating vulnerability to simultaneous maritime and energy market downturns.

Value chain position and competitive dynamics

The global dredging industry functions as an entrenched oligopoly with the top five firms controlling approximately 80% of the market, dominated by pure-play specialists capturing almost all major capital works. Global demand for oil and chemical storage accelerates with key hubs projecting terminal market growth exceeding 10% by 2027, while operators capture highly predictable margins through long-term take-or-pay agreements. The biostimulant and biocontrol market is projected to surpass $28bn by 2030, structurally guaranteed by stringent European mandates for 50% chemical pesticide reduction across a fragmented 600- company ecosystem. Vopak generates exceptional proportional EBITDA margins near 60% at the midstream storage stage, while Boskalis delivers margins near 30% versus an industry average of 15-20% at specialist execution.

Customer dynamics and pricing power

Despite facing massive downstream energy majors and governments, Boskalis and Vopak maintain strong leverage due to severe asset scarcity, with constrained global capacity enabling substantial rapid storage rate increases. Both Vopak and Boskalis heavily utilize structural contract mechanisms to pass through raw material and fuel inflation directly to downstream customers, protecting margin pools. HAL's chosen midstream stages require relentless massive capital expenditure to maintain barriers to entry, with rapid energy transition acceleration potentially stranding Vopak's fossil-heavy terminal assets permanently.

Foundation quality and operator strength

Jaap van Wiechen successfully succeeded Mel Groot in 2024, maintaining historically patient strategy while forcing individual investee companies to independently execute through decentralized governance and independent supervisory boards. The massive €2.6bn liquid portfolio enables opportunistic debt-free acquisitions, with the €2.25bn Boskalis privatization and €1.6bn VolkerWessels deal demonstrating aggressive capital recycling into strategic adjacencies. Boskalis dominates a tight 80% market-share oligopoly with a 600- vessel fleet locking out challengers, while Vopak integrates physical terminals directly into customer plants rendering relocation physically unviable. Rising global sea levels mechanically force continuous demand growth for coastal defense capabilities while energy transition drives accelerating industrial gas terminal utilization, relying on secular infrastructure deficits rather than heroic dynamism. Foundational quality depends on sustained government spending, with a sovereign debt crisis potentially stranding capital-intensive maritime assets, while accelerated fossil-fuel phase-out represents the single largest risk with Vopak occupancy below 85% as the early warning signal.

Disruption forces and paradigm alignment

The global net-zero push threatens to strand Vopak's legacy fossil-fuel assets, while European mandates force massive substitution toward Koppert's biologicals and agentic commerce disrupts Coolblue's digital traffic acquisition. Koppert targets a biostimulant market compounding to $28bn by 2030, Vopak commits €1bn by 2030 to hydrogen and ammonia infrastructure, and rising sea levels force mechanical demand growth for Boskalis's coastal defense. The core portfolio perfectly aligns with climate adaptation, energy transition, and sustainable agriculture paradigms, with HAL's €2.6bn liquid reserves enabling counter-cyclical acquisitions during transition-driven dislocations across 5-to-15 year cycles.
