§1 — Executive Summary

Ericsson entered 2023 in a deeply distressed earnings state — battered by a collapsing RAN market, a disastrous Vonage acquisition, and a multi-year margin implosion. The 2023–2025 period has been a genuine operational turnaround, but the recovery's durability at the current pace is the central analytical question.

  • Trajectory verdict: Direction is positive; adj. EBITA margins expanded for nine consecutive quarters through Q4 2025; recovery is real. [#J]
  • Starting point context: FY2023 gross margin was 39.2% — deeply below the ~46–50% range where Ericsson operated historically; by Q1 2026 it recovered to 48.1%; the magnitude of the swing makes the base period unreliable for trend extrapolation. [#A]
  • Reported vs. organic gap: Q1 2026 is the sharpest illustration — 6% organic growth coexists with -10% reported sales due to SEK appreciation; this creates significant noise in published numbers. [#A]
  • Segment divergence: Networks is the anchor; CSS is the turnaround option; Enterprise is the drag; IPR licensing is a high-margin swing variable. [#I]
  • Peer context — Nokia FY2025: Nokia delivered ~EUR 19.9bn revenue, +2% organic, comparable operating profit ~EUR 2bn (roughly 10% margin); Ericsson's adjusted margin profile is superior, Nokia's revenue base is larger. [#A]
  • Carrier capex dependency: Ericsson derives ~85%+ of revenue from carrier infrastructure spending (Networks + CSS); the group has minimal revenue insulation from a telecom capex downcycle. [#I]
  • EPS distortion warning: Reported EPS 2022–2024 is useless for trend analysis due to cumulative Vonage goodwill write-downs (~SEK 45bn+ total charges); adj. EPS or adj. EBITA per share is the correct tracking metric. [#J]
  • Current momentum — Q1 2026: First quarter with organic growth in all three segments simultaneously in several years; adj. EBITA margin 11.3% (seasonally Q1 is always the weakest quarter due to Networks mix); on a trailing basis, momentum is positive. [#A]
  • Semiconductor cost risk: CEO Börje Ekholm flagged rising semiconductor input costs in Q1 2026 partly driven by AI demand competing for chip supply; this is a new headwind that was not present in 2024 margin recovery. [#I]
  • ❌ Sceptic check: Revenue in absolute SEK terms remains ~7% below 2022 levels even after 2025 recovery; the company is growing earnings faster than revenues via cost discipline, but top-line has not returned to prior peaks. [#J]

§2 — Revenue Growth & Organic Dynamics

  • Revenue summary verdict: Two years of sharp reported declines (2023: ~-14%; 2024: ~-5%) followed by a partial reported recovery in 2025 (+3.15%); organic performance significantly better throughout, with the FX effect masking underlying share gains. [#J]

  • Absolute revenue L3Y (reported, USD): $27.9bn (2022) → ~$23.9bn (2023) → $23.4bn (2024) → $24.2bn (2025); cumulative reported decline peak-to-trough was approximately 16%. [#I]

  • Organic growth quarterly progression (2024–2026): Q4 2024: +2% organic (first positive quarter after eight consecutive negative); Q1 2025: +6%; Q2 2025: +2%; Q3 2025: flat/slight decline; Q4 2025: +6%; Q1 2026: +6% — recovery is real but uneven. [#A]

  • CQ-1 — carrier vs. enterprise spectrum: Networks segment (carrier RAN) = ~64–67% of revenue; CSS (carrier software/managed services) = ~25–27%; Enterprise (private networks, IoT, Vonage CPaaS) = ~10%; Ericsson sits firmly at the carrier end of the spectrum with limited enterprise insulation. [#A]

  • CQ-2 — cyclicality: Revenue fell 23%+ over 2022–2024 as the post-5G investment cycle normalised; there is no meaningful through-cycle revenue floor beyond IPR licensing (~SEK 14bn, ~6% of revenue, contractual multiyear deals); the carrier capex sensitivity is very high. [#J]

  • Segment breakdown 2024 (SEK bn): Networks: SEK 158.2bn (-8% YoY); Cloud Software & Services: SEK 62.6bn (-2% YoY); Enterprise: SEK 24.9bn (-3% YoY); IPR: SEK 14.0bn (+26% YoY); Other: SEK 2.2bn. [#I]

  • Q1 2026 segment growth (organic): Networks: +7%; CSS: +4%; Enterprise: +4%; all three positive simultaneously — a significant milestone. [#A]

  • Nokia peer comparison — revenue trajectory: Nokia net sales grew ~2% organically in FY2025 on a larger absolute EUR 19.9bn base; Nokia's revenue decline in 2023–2024 was similarly sharp (~-20% peak to trough), driven by mobile network weakness. [#A]

  • North America outperformance: US carriers (AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile) drove an outsized share of Ericsson's 2024 recovery; North American RAN market grew 54% in H2 2024, pulling Ericsson revenue sharply; this concentration creates a geographic risk if US spend moderates. [#I]

  • ❌ Geographic concentration risk: Europe and rest-of-world RAN markets remained weak through 2024–2025; Ericsson's organic recovery has been predominantly US-driven; a non-diversified demand recovery is lower quality than broad-based growth. [#J]

  • RAN market context: Global RAN market ~$35bn in 2025, flat vs. 2024; expected broadly flat in 2026 (Dell'Oro); Ericsson's +6% organic Q4 2025 growth in a flat market implies share gains from Nokia/Samsung, confirmed by Dell'Oro data showing Ericsson RAN share improving 1Q25–3Q25. [#A]

§3 — Margin Profile & Evolution

  • Margin verdict — remarkable recovery, but the trough was catastrophic: Adj. EBITA margin troughed at approximately 5% in FY2023 group level; recovered to 11.0% FY2024; 18.1% FY2025 (incl. iconectiv divestment gain); ex-divestment gain, underlying normalised margin is likely in the 15–16% range. [#J]

  • CQ-4 addressed — EBIT margins vs. history vs. peers: Ericsson adjusted EBITA FY2025 at 18.1% is the highest in at least a decade; Nokia comparable operating margin FY2024 was approximately 10.7%; Ericsson's current margin is materially superior to Nokia on the adj. basis. [#A]

  • Gross margin trend: 39.2% (FY2023 trough) → 47.6% (FY2024 adj.) → 48.1% (FY2025 adj.) → 48.1% (Q1 2026); the step-change from 2023 to 2024 (+8pp) reflects mix improvement (less India, more North America) and cost reductions. [#A]

  • Networks segment margins: Networks adj. EBITA margin: 16.5% (FY2023) → 21.6% (FY2024) → ~20.7% (FY2025); Q4 2025 at 23%; this segment operates at the top end of peer-set profitability for RAN equipment. [#A]

  • CSS segment margin trajectory: Deeply negative in 2022–2023; turned marginally positive in H2 2023; FY2024 progressing toward break-even; Q3 2025 reached 12.5% adj. EBITA margin (up 9.6pp YoY); Q1 2026 at 5.3% (seasonality); CSS is now a contributor, not a drag. [#A]

  • Enterprise segment margins: Chronically negative at the adj. EBITA level in 2023–2024 due to Vonage integration costs; limited improvement; Q1 2026 Enterprise showed adj. EBITA loss. [#I]

  • Operating leverage characterisation: The 2023→2025 margin expansion was driven by: (1) geographic mix shift away from low-margin India/LatAm; (2) cost restructuring (~10,000 headcount reductions); (3) CSS operational improvement; (4) IPR licensing revenue expansion (high margin). [#A]

  • Nokia margin context: Nokia comparable operating margin FY2024 ~13.1% (EUR 2.6bn on EUR 19.9bn revenues); Nokia FY2023 comparable operating margin ~10.7%; Nokia's trajectory is also expanding but from a lower absolute level and with less Networks margin leverage. [#A]

  • ⚡ RARE — nine consecutive quarters of adj. EBITA margin expansion: Ericsson achieved this through Q4 2025; this duration of sustained margin expansion is unusual in a capital equipment business exposed to carrier capex cycles; it reflects a genuine operational transformation rather than short-term variance. [#J]

  • Q1 2026 adj. EBITA margin at 11.3% vs 12.6% in Q1 2025: ❌ Sequential setback in the margin expansion trend; management attributed this to elevated restructuring charges and semiconductor cost inflation — monitoring required to see if this reverses in Q2. [#A]

  • Plausible improvement path N3Y: Management targets 15–18% long-term adj. EBITA (ex-restructuring); if CSS scales to 10–15% EBITA and Enterprise reaches break-even, group adj. EBITA can sustain in the 15–17% range through 2028 — but this assumes flat-to-positive RAN market and no semiconductor super-cycle. [#J]

§7 — Sector & Value Chain Context

  • RAN market structure verdict: The global RAN market is in a post-5G-buildout consolidation phase; the 2020–2022 boom in 5G deployments deflated sharply in 2023; the market re-stabilised at ~$35bn by 2025 and is expected to remain flat in 2026. [#J]

  • Carrier capex cycle: Major Western carriers are in capital efficiency mode, not expansion mode; 5G SA deployments are progressing but are software-driven (lower Ericsson hardware content) not radio-driven (higher content). [#I]

  • CQ-5 — Huawei competitive context: Huawei is effectively excluded from Western telecom markets; ex-China, Huawei is not a competitive threat for Ericsson; within China, Ericsson has negligible presence and generates minimal revenue. [#I]

  • Samsung competitive context: Samsung has ~7–8% global RAN share, primarily US and South Korea; Dish's bankruptcy weakened Samsung's US position; Samsung is a longer-term threat in Open RAN architectures but is not yet a material share threat to Ericsson in traditional RAN. [#A]

  • AI/6G as medium-term demand catalyst: Ericsson management consistently references AI-driven network densification and 6G as the next cycle; Nokia is more directly benefiting from AI/Cloud capex today through optical and IP networking; Ericsson's AI benefit is more distant. [#J]

  • India market — volatile overhang: India drove a significant portion of Ericsson's 2022 growth and then its 2023–2024 decline as that buildout normalised; India is now a smaller share of Ericsson revenue, reducing but not eliminating this cyclical swing factor. [#I]

  • ❌ CQ-2 — through-cycle revenue floor is limited: IPR licensing (SEK 14bn, ~6% of revenue, contractual) + managed services within CSS are the only genuinely recurring revenue streams; Nokia has a slightly better balance with stronger fixed network and enterprise exposure. [#J]

§8 — Quality of Earnings & Accounting

  • Quality verdict — FCF generation is the most credible earnings signal; reported net income is structurally distorted: Adj. EBITA is management's preferred metric; the persistently large gap between adj. and reported figures over 6+ years requires scrutiny. [#J]

  • Revenue recognition: Ericsson uses IFRS 15 percentage-of-completion for long-term managed services contracts; IPR licensing revenue includes retroactive catch-up amounts from prior periods (SEK 14.0bn in 2024 included retroactive revenue from previously unlicensed periods); this creates lumpiness in the higher-margin IPR line. [#I]

  • Cash conversion: FCF before M&A SEK 40bn in FY2024 vs adj. EBITA SEK 27.2bn — FCF conversion exceeded 100% in 2024, a positive signal; FY2025 FCF before M&A moderated to SEK 26.8bn vs adj. EBITA SEK ~42.9bn (including iconectiv gain); the operational FCF generation is real and material. [#A]

  • Working capital: 2024 FCF benefited from a structural working capital reduction driven by supply chain improvements and customer payment phasing; 2025 operating cash flow (SEK 33.0bn) was lower than 2024 (SEK 46.3bn) partly because the 2024 WC benefit did not repeat at the same scale. [#A]

  • Restructuring charges — recurrence pattern: Ericsson has booked restructuring charges in every year since at least 2017; cumulative charges are enormous; treating these as "adjustments" misrepresents the sustainable cost base. [#J]

  • Vonage impairment impact on balance sheet: Two goodwill impairments in 2023 (~SEK 31.9bn) and 2024 (~SEK 9–11bn) have significantly reduced the carrying value of the Enterprise segment; remaining goodwill and intangibles in Enterprise/Vonage still represent a potential further impairment risk. [#I]

  • IPR licensing quality: This is genuinely high-quality recurring revenue with multi-year contractual arrangements; the 2024 step-up (SEK 14.0bn vs SEK 11.1bn) included retroactive catch-up; the run-rate forward is probably closer to SEK 11–12bn, not SEK 14bn. [#A]

  • ❌ Adjustments breadth concerns: Adj. EBITA excludes: restructuring, amortisation of acquired intangibles, impairments, legal provisions; when these are excluded every year, the gap between adj. and statutory earnings becomes a red flag for quality-focused investors. [#J]

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis

  • Central bear case: Ericsson's earnings recovery is real but rests on a narrow set of conditions — a stable/growing North American RAN market, continued CSS turnaround, no semiconductor cost super-cycle, and no new M&A disasters; remove any one of these and the margin story reverses sharply. [#J]

  • CQ-2 revisited critically — cyclicality is the dominant risk: Ericsson demonstrated in 2022–2024 that a carrier capex correction can destroy 20–25% of revenues in two years; the business has no structural floor beyond ~6% of revenues in IPR licensing; the next RAN capex pause could be 2026–2027. [#J]

  • CQ-4 revisited critically — margin quality concern: FY2025 adj. EBITA 18.1% includes iconectiv divestment gain (one-time); the nine-quarter margin expansion narrative partially depends on non-recurring cost-out and favourable geographic mix; Q1 2026's adj. EBITA margin at 11.3% (vs 12.6% prior year) is the first signal that the expansion trajectory may be losing steam. [#A]

  • Semiconductor headwind is new and unquantified: CEO flagged rising chip costs from AI demand in Q1 2026; telecom equipment margins are sensitive to semiconductor pricing; if this persists, it represents a structural gross margin headwind not captured in consensus. [#I]

  • Nokia converging from below: Nokia is growing comparable operating profit faster than Ericsson on the most recent quarterly data; Nokia's optical/IP networking exposure gives it an AI capex beneficiary narrative that Ericsson currently lacks. [#A]

  • Enterprise segment is unresolved: Two Vonage impairment charges absorbed ~$4.1bn in value; the segment generates adj. EBITA losses; management has not committed to a disposal or restructuring path for the Vonage/CPaaS business. [#J]

  • ❌ "Restructuring as normal" masks true cost structure: If restructuring charges (~SEK 3–5bn/year historically) are recurring, then the true ongoing EBIT margin is 100–200bp below the adjusted figure consistently cited by management. [#J]

  • FCF moderation from 2024 peak: FCF before M&A fell from SEK 40bn (2024) to SEK 26.8bn (2025); management attributed 2024's strength to one-time working capital release that did not repeat; if 2025 represents the new normalised level, the FCF yield thesis is weaker than 2024 suggested. [#A]

AGGREGATE WEAK SIGNALS / DOWNSIDE (❌)

  • ❌ Revenue in SEK terms below 2022 peak despite recovery: Even after 2025 growth, group revenues remain ~7% below 2022 peak levels; the top-line has not returned to prior highs, raising questions about whether 5G replacement cycles can drive a new expansion wave. [Cross-ref: §2] [#J]

  • ❌ Enterprise segment adj. EBITA still negative in Q1 2026: Enterprise continues to generate adj. EBITA losses; Vonage integration has failed to produce the CPaaS revenue growth originally modelled; additional impairment risk on remaining goodwill cannot be excluded. [Cross-ref: §3, §8] [#J]

  • ❌ Nokia dramatically outperformed Ericsson in Q1 2026 earnings delivery: Nokia +54% comparable op. profit vs. Ericsson -20% adj. EBITA YoY in the same quarter; the peer comparison in the most recent period is clearly going Nokia's way. [Cross-ref: §6] [#A]

  • ❌ Geographic concentration in North American RAN: FY2024 organic recovery was disproportionately US-driven; if T-Mobile/AT&T/Verizon enter a capex digestion phase post-5G buildout, Ericsson's volume exposure is acute. [Cross-ref: §2, §7] [#J]
