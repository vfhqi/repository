§1 — Revenue Growth Benchmarking (2023–2025)

Verdict [#J]: Croda's 2023–2024 revenue trajectory was the worst in the peer group; 2025 recovery is real but starting from a deeply compressed base, with peers having recovered earlier and more cleanly.

  • CQ — Croda 2022 peak and collapse: Revenue fell from £2,090m (2022) to £1,627m (2023), a -22% reported decline driven by mRNA Covid lipid normalisation and broad destocking. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda 2023 organic performance: Consumer Care underlying volumes fell double-digits; Crop Protection destocking drove -19% in that segment; group adjusted operating profit crashed ~70% peak-to-trough. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda 2024 partial recovery: Full-year 2024 reported sales recovered modestly; H1 2024 saw Consumer Care +6% at constant currency but Life Sciences -16% and Industrial Specialties -14%, netting a -4% group CC decline. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda FY2025 recovery confirmation: Revenue £1,699m, +4.4% reported, +6.6% constant currency, with volume recovery of approximately +9.6% — the dominant driver — partly offset by negative price/mix effects. [#I]
  • RA — Volume-led recovery pattern: FY2025 growth was volume-led rather than price-led, consistent with post-destocking bounce, not pricing power recovery; this limits read-across to underlying demand strength. [#A]
  • RA — Givaudan 2024 organic growth +12.3% LFL: Givaudan delivered CHF 7,412m sales at +12.3% like-for-like in 2024 — more than double Croda's 2024 organic pace — reflecting earlier destocking completion in fragrance/flavour. [#A]
  • RA — Symrise 2024 organic growth +8.7%: Symrise achieved EUR 4,999m revenue, +5.7% reported, +8.7% organic in 2024, led by EAME +10.9% and LatAm +15.2% — a clean, broad-based recovery ahead of Croda. [#A]
  • RA — IFF 2024 organic growth low-to-mid single digit: IFF currency-neutral Scent sales +10%, Nourish +4% comparable, Health & Biosciences volume recovery underway — aggregate comparable growth mid-single digit after major 2022–2023 destocking crisis. [#A]
  • RA — Evonik 2024 volumes +4%, prices -2%: Evonik group sales EUR 15.2bn; specialty additives grew volumes considerably but selling prices fell as raw material deflation was passed through; adjusted EBITDA +25% to EUR 2.065bn. [#A]
  • RA — DSM-Firmenich 2024 organic +5%: DSM-Firmenich organic sales grew 5% (volume +3%, price +2%) in 2024; adjusted EBITDA +19%; integration synergies a tailwind unavailable to pure-play peers. [#A]
  • RA — Symrise 2025 decelerated to +2.8% organic: Symrise FY2025 organic growth moderated sharply to 2.8% (EUR 4,929m) as the post-destocking volume surge normalised — suggesting Croda may face similar deceleration risk from 2026. [#A]
  • RA — Givaudan 2025 organic +5.1% LFL: Givaudan sustained solid +5.1% LFL in 2025 at CHF 7,472m; structural fragrance premiumisation providing ongoing support versus Croda's more cyclical specialties. [#A]

Peer organic growth ranking 2024 (best to worst): Givaudan (+12.3%) > Symrise (+8.7%) > IFF (~+5%) > DSM-Firmenich (+5%) > Evonik (+~2% net) > Croda (-4% H1 CC, partially recovered H2).

Peer organic growth ranking 2025: Givaudan (+5.1%) > Croda (+6.6% CC) > DSM-Firmenich (~+5%) > IFF (+2% CN) > Symrise (+2.8%) > Evonik (flat-to-slightly negative). Croda ranks second in 2025 on this measure, but from a much lower base.

Sceptical note [#J]: Croda's 2025 ranking improvement flatters a very low base; the peer group had largely completed its recovery cycle by end-2024, meaning Croda's 2025 bounce is partly catch-up, not competitive outperformance.

§2 — Margin Trajectory

Verdict [#J]: Croda's margin compression 2022–2024 was structurally the most severe in the peer group; 2025 recovery to 17.4% is real but leaves a 10+ percentage-point gap versus 2022 peak and well below Givaudan's sustained 23–24% EBITDA margin.

  • CQ — Croda 2022 peak margin ~28% adjusted operating: Croda adjusted operating margin peaked at approximately 28% in 2022 inclusive of high-margin mRNA lipid revenues — an artificially elevated level unlikely to be fully recaptured. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda 2023 margin collapse to ~18.9%: 2023 adjusted operating profit fell sharply; by FY2023 adjusted operating margin had fallen to approximately 18.9% as volume deleveraged fixed costs against a halved profit base. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda 2024 further compression to 17.2%: FY2024 adjusted operating margin fell further to 17.2% (H1 16.6%, H2 17.7%), with adjusted PBT EUR 260m — management citing adverse mix, lower absorption and continued destocking headwinds. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda FY2025 recovery to 17.4%: Adjusted operating profit GBP 295.3m, +5.6% year-on-year; margin recovered to 17.4% — ahead of consensus — but recovery pace is slow (+20bps year-on-year). [#I]
  • RA — Givaudan EBITDA margin 24.5% (2024 comparable): Givaudan maintained EBITDA margins at 22–24% range throughout the destocking period; its diversified fragrance/flavour mix and pricing power insulated it from Croda-scale compression. [#A]
  • RA — Givaudan 2025 EBITDA margin 23.4%: Givaudan 2025 EBITDA margin slightly lower year-on-year at 23.4% (from 23.8% in 2024) due to tariff and FX headwinds — still approximately 6 percentage points above Croda's adjusted operating margin. [#A]
  • RA — Symrise EBITDA margin 20.7% (2024), 21.9% (2025): Symrise expanded EBITDA margin +160bps in 2024 and further +120bps in 2025 to 21.9% — clean progressive improvement versus Croda's compressed and slow recovery. [#A]
  • RA — Evonik adjusted EBITDA margin recovery 10.8% to 13.6%: Evonik's specialty chemicals margin recovered sharply in 2024 (+280bps) from a lower absolute base; confirms sector-wide fixed-cost leverage on volume recovery, but Evonik's absolute margin lower than Croda. [#A]
  • RA — IFF margin compressed more severely than Croda: IFF adjusted EBITDA compressed materially during 2022–2023 due to integration costs and destocking simultaneously; new CEO acknowledged synergy pursuit distracted from core business; IFF margin recovery slower relative to Symrise/Givaudan. [#A]
  • RA — Croda's fixed-cost headwind structural not temporary: Croda's 2028 framework targets margin recovery to >20%; the implied 260bps improvement from 2025 requires both volume growth and GBP 100m annualised transformation savings — neither guaranteed. [#A]
  • RA — Margin follows volume but with lag for Croda: H2 2024 showed margin improving to 17.7% as volumes recovered; FY2025 at 17.4% suggests fixed-cost absorption is the primary lever rather than pricing, limiting near-term margin momentum. [#A]

Sceptical note [#J]: Croda's margin trajectory to >20% by 2028 requires the largest recovery of any peer; Givaudan and Symrise never fell to Croda's levels and are already at their targets — Croda is running a 3-year recovery programme its peers largely completed in 12 months.

§3 — EPS Trajectory and Estimate Revisions

Verdict [#J]: Croda's EPS has stabilised and turned upward, but adjusted EPS of 146.2p in FY2025 remains far below the implied 2022 peak — and the scale of consensus downgrade cycle 2022–2024 was among the most severe in European specialty chemicals.

  • CQ — Croda FY2024 adjusted EPS 142.6p: FY2024 adjusted EPS 142.6p, which represented a miss versus consensus expectations and continued the multi-year downgrade cycle from 2022 peak levels. [#I]
  • CQ — Croda FY2025 adjusted EPS 146.2p (+2.5%): FY2025 adjusted EPS 146.2p — marginal improvement, broadly in line with the volume-led revenue recovery; 2025 actual slightly ahead of consensus, marking the first meaningful beat in the cycle. [#I]
  • CQ — Consensus forward EPS growth +16.4% pa forecast: Post-FY2025, covering analysts (12 updating estimates) forecast EPS growth of 16.4% per annum — reflecting the expected margin recovery to >20% and operating leverage on volume growth. [#I]
  • RA — Croda profit warning count 2022–2024 at least three: In June 2023, Croda warned profit would fall from GBP 780m to GBP 370–400m pretax; through 2023–2024, further guidance revisions followed as destocking proved deeper and longer than management initially guided — a minimum of 3–4 material downward revisions. [#A]
  • RA — IFF guidance cut cycle comparable or worse: IFF reduced FY2023 sales and adjusted EBITDA guidance by 7% and 18% at midpoint across multiple cuts; new CEO acknowledged in 2024 that management had lost focus on core business during DuPont integration — comparable or worse guidance credibility crisis to Croda. [#A]
  • RA — Givaudan and Symrise EPS revision cycle much milder: Givaudan maintained earnings delivery through the destocking period; Symrise EPS revision cycle was more modest; neither experienced the multi-year sequential downgrade pattern that characterised Croda and IFF 2022–2024. [#A]
  • RA — FY2025 beat signals positive revision inflection: Croda's FY2025 adjusted operating profit GBP 295.3m came in slightly ahead of consensus, and the 2028 framework publication signals management confidence — the first conditions for positive revision momentum since 2022. [#A]
  • RA — EPS recovery path requires transformation delivery: The consensus +16.4% pa EPS CAGR is contingent on margin expansion and sustained volume growth; if 2028 margin target slips, EPS estimates face risk of renewed cuts — particularly given the track record. [#A]

Sceptical note [#J]: A single consensus beat does not restore credibility after 3–4 profit warnings; the FY2025 result is necessary but not sufficient — sell-side estimates remain forecast-dependent on a transformation programme that has barely started.

§4 — Guidance Credibility Analysis

Verdict [#J]: Croda's guidance credibility is at an historic low; the 2028 framework is a strategic reframe, not a near-term reassurance — restoring credibility requires two to three years of delivery, and comparison to Givaudan/Symrise's track records is unflattering.

  • CQ — Croda profit warning timeline 2022–2024: At least three material guidance downgrades: (1) January 2023 FY2022 results revealing peak had passed; (2) June 2023 warning of approximately 50% profit halving; (3) FY2023 results February 2024 delivering -70% profit vs 2022 while guiding further compression in 2024 with management stating "recovery difficult to predict." [#I]
  • CQ — Management transition mid-cycle: Croda changed CEO during the downgrade cycle, adding execution risk and disrupting strategic continuity — a further credibility drag not seen at Givaudan or Symrise. [#I]
  • CQ — 2028 framework introduced at FY2025 results: At the February 2026 FY2025 results, management published a three-year financial framework: 3–6% organic sales CAGR, >20% adjusted operating margin, >12% free cash flow/sales ratio, 10% ROIC — explicitly positioning the company as entering a new growth phase. [#I]
  • CQ — Early 2025 transformation delivery ahead of plan: GBP 28m of transformation savings delivered in 2025, slightly ahead of the internal plan; total target GBP 100m annualised by end-2027 — early indicator but small relative to the overall task. [#I]
  • RA — Givaudan guidance track record: consistently delivered: Givaudan met its 2021–2025 five-year financial targets — average LFL growth 6.8% vs 5–7% target, FCF/sales 12.5% — rare in specialty chemicals; sets the credibility benchmark Croda must aspire to. [#A]
  • RA — Symrise 16th consecutive dividend increase in 2025: Symrise's 2025 full-year results included its 16th consecutive annual dividend increase — a direct proxy for consistent earnings delivery and management credibility that Croda cannot currently match. [#A]
  • RA — IFF CEO admission — synergy became the goal, not a tool: IFF's new CEO publicly acknowledged in 2024 that the DuPont N&B integration distracted from core business; this candid admission combined with active portfolio restructuring is IFF's credibility restoration pathway — analogous to Croda's restructuring signal. [#A]
  • RA — 2028 framework is medium-term reframe, not near-term anchor: By replacing annual guidance with a three-year framework, management is deliberately reducing short-cycle accountability — a sensible move after warnings, but investors must judge whether management have fixed the analytical problem or merely changed the reporting horizon. [#A]
  • RA — Croda 2025 FY delivery ahead of consensus is necessary first step: FY2025 adjusted operating profit GBP 295.3m came in at the upper end of the GBP 260–300m guided range — the first meaningful positive delivery signal; a single result does not restore credibility but is the foundation for it. [#A]

Sceptical note [#J]: Croda's credibility will not be restored by a framework announcement; Givaudan and Symrise built theirs over 5–10 years of consistent delivery — Croda must demonstrate sequential margin expansion across 4–6 results reporting cycles before the market will price in the >20% margin target with conviction.

§6 — Sector Context

Verdict [#J]: The specialty ingredients sector is in a broad recovery that is real but uneven; Croda entered the cycle later, with deeper exposure to mRNA normalisation — a one-off factor that distorted comparisons — and the fragrance/flavour subset recovered materially faster than pharma lipids and crop protection.

  • CQ — 2023–2024 was a sector-wide destocking supercycle: All six peer companies experienced meaningful destocking-driven volume pressure in 2023; the cycle was unusually severe due to the pandemic demand pull-forward creating multi-quarter inventory overhang across Life Sciences, Consumer Care and Crop. [#I]
  • CQ — Fragrance/flavour subset recovered faster: Givaudan's +12.3% LFL in 2024 and Symrise's +8.7% organic confirm fragrance and flavour destocking was shorter-lived — customer inventory cycles faster in consumer products than pharmaceutical supply chains. [#I]
  • RA — Croda's mRNA lipid exposure was unique structural drag: Croda derived approximately 10% of 2022 group revenues from Covid-19 mRNA lipid products (approximately GBP 120m); as pandemic vaccine orders collapsed, this created a structural revenue gap that peers with no mRNA exposure did not face. [#A]
  • RA — IFF parallel — large acquisition created comparable disruption: IFF's acquisition of DuPont Nutrition and Biosciences (USD 26bn, 2021) created a parallel crisis: overpaying at peak demand, integration complexity, destocking in functional ingredients, and guidance credibility collapse — the closest structural analogue to Croda's situation in the peer group. [#A]
  • RA — Input cost deflation a tailwind sector-wide 2024–2025: Raw material cost deflation throughout 2024 provided margin tailwind across the group; Evonik explicitly passed lower raw material costs through to customers; Croda benefited from the same dynamic in Consumer Care inputs. [#A]
  • RA — Pharma lipid recovery now underway: Croda's Life Sciences segment — the hardest hit — is beginning to recover as non-Covid mRNA therapeutic pipeline (oncology, rare disease) scales up commercial manufacturing; this is the growth engine the 2028 framework is predicated upon. [#A]
  • RA — Crop Protection segment remains challenged: Croda's Crop Protection business (part of Life Sciences) saw -19% destocking in 2023 and continued softness in 2024; this sub-segment lagged the broader consumer/pharma recovery and remains a risk for near-term Life Sciences growth. [#A]
  • RA — Givaudan's 2025 deceleration signals mid-cycle normalisation: Givaudan moderated from +12.3% (2024) to +5.1% (2025); Symrise from +8.7% to +2.8% — this normalisation pattern is likely to affect Croda's post-bounce trajectory from 2026 onwards. [#A]
  • RA — Macro tailwinds fading into 2026: Input cost deflation headroom is largely exhausted; tariff pressures in 2025 impacted Givaudan margins; pricing power remains limited with volume-led recovery; sectoral growth returns to structural 3–6% pa rather than post-destocking bounce rates. [#A]

Sceptical note [#J]: The sector recovery narrative is largely baked in for peers; Croda's remaining recovery optionality (mRNA therapeutics scaling, crop normalisation) is real but longer-dated than the consumer/fragrance recovery — investors must wait for a structural demand thesis to materialise in Life Sciences, not merely a destocking completion.
