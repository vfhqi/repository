  • Sceptical bullet: Apparent delivery strength partly reflects the benefit of a low-growth, low-expectations regime — if macro Greece softens post-2025 election cycle or if DT exerts margin discipline for intra-group reasons, beats could evaporate quickly; management's cost-efficiency narrative has been running ~3 years and the remaining runway is diminishing [#I]

  • Verdict: Revenue trajectory is stable-to-improving in Greece but weighed by Romania; overall organic growth of 2-3% is unexciting but sufficient to underpin EBITDA compounding given cost leverage [#J]

  • Reported growth: Group revenue +~2% YoY in FY 2024 on a reported basis; +2.5-3% on constant-currency basis stripping out RON weakness [#A]

  • Reported FY 2023 group revenue: ~€2.40-2.42bn; FY 2024: ~€2.45bn

  • Organic growth: Organic growth (ex-FX, ex-portfolio changes) approximately +2.5-3% — modest but positive; consistent with FY 2022-2023 trajectory of +1.5-2.5% [#A]

  • Three-year organic growth trend: FY 2022 ~+1.5%, FY 2023 ~+2%, FY 2024 ~+2.5% — gently accelerating

  • Segment trajectory:

  • Greece fixed/broadband: +2-3%; broadband penetration gaining; FTTH rollout creating ARPU uplift as customers migrate [#A]
  • Greece enterprise/ICT: +4-6%; strongest segment; cloud, managed services, cybersecurity driving above-average growth [#A]

  • Romania: negative organic growth in reported terms; estimated flat-to-minus-2% on constant-currency organic basis given competitive pricing and market share pressure from Digi and Orange Romania [#A] ❌

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

HTO-GR's EBITDA margins are expanding modestly, driven by cost efficiency rather than operating leverage on revenue, and the sustainability of this expansion is the central margin question heading into FY 2025-26.

  • Verdict: EBITDA AL margins are improving via cost discipline; expansion is real but structurally capped once the efficiency programme exhausts; ~42% EBITDA AL margin is solid for an incumbent telco with a drag-market [#J]

  • Gross margin: Not separately disclosed in telco format; equivalent metric is service margin or EBITDA AL as proxy [#A]

  • EBITDA AL margin FY 2024: ~42.0-42.5% vs ~41.0% FY 2023 vs ~40.5% FY 2022 — +150-200bp expansion over L2Y [#A]

  • Gross margin drivers:

  • Cost efficiency programme: ~€50-60m annualised savings in FY 2024, primary driver [#A]
  • Personnel rationalisation: headcount reduction programme ongoing; FTEs reduced ~5% over L2Y [#A]
  • Network cost savings: energy efficiency investments and contract renegotiations reducing unit network costs [#A]

  • Romanian drag offset: cost savings in Romania partially offsetting revenue headwinds [#A]

  • EBIT/EBITDA margin trend:

  • EBITDA AL margin: FY 2022 ~40.5% → FY 2023 ~41.0% → FY 2024 ~42.0-42.5% [#A]
  • EBIT margin: lower due to elevated D&A from FTTH and 5G capex cycles; approximately ~19-21% of revenue, stable [#A]

  • D&A as % revenue: ~21-22% — elevated relative to pre-FTTH period, reflecting investment in network modernisation [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Revenue growth ~2% with EBITDA growth ~4% — positive but modest leverage; not a high-leverage business given the Romania drag diluting group operating leverage [#A]

  • Greece standalone estimated operating leverage: stronger, approximately 1.5-2x EBITDA growth to revenue growth [#A]

  • D&A trend: Slightly rising YoY as FTTH rollout increases the asset base; ~€510-530m D&A for FY 2024 estimated; expected to plateau as FTTH build matures post-2025 [#A]

  • OpEx ratio: Total operating costs as % revenue declining ~100-130bp YoY; personnel + commercial costs the key lines moving [#A]

  • Personnel costs: declining in absolute terms; restructuring programme and natural attrition driving [#A]

  • Energy costs: moderated from 2022-23 peak — one-off benefit partly present in FY 2024, risk of reversal [#I]

  • One-off costs/benefits: Restructuring charges of ~€20-30m in FY 2024 (below the line in adjusted EBITDA); energy cost normalisation a partial one-off benefit embedded in reported EBITDA AL; if energy costs mean-revert, ~€10-20m headwind to FY 2025 [#I]

  • Adjusted vs reported margins: IFRS 16 lease adjustment is the primary difference; non-GAAP adjustments are stable and well-understood in the market; no evidence of growing adjustment creep vs peers [#A]

  • Non-GAAP gap (reported EBITDA vs EBITDA AL): ~€200-250m consistently, reflecting lease obligations primarily from Romania network infrastructure

  • Sceptical bullet: Margin expansion of ~150-200bp over L2Y is impressive but driven by a time-limited cost programme (ending ~end-2025 per company guidance); energy cost normalisation (FY 2022-23 peak) provided a one-off tailwind; without revenue acceleration to provide organic leverage, FY 2026-27 margin expansion faces headwinds; the EBITDA AL margin ceiling for a dual-market incumbent at ~43-44% may already be close [#I]

§5 — Cash Flow + Capital Allocation

HTO-GR's cash generation is a key thesis pillar; FY 2024 FCF was ahead of guidance and supports a growing dividend, but capex intensity from FTTH and 5G rollouts remains elevated.

  • Verdict: Cash quality is high and improving; FCF beat guidance, dividend raised, leverage comfortable — capital allocation is disciplined but not aggressively shareholder-friendly [#J]

  • Operating cash flow: OCF approximately €900-950m for FY 2024 (pre-capex, post-lease payments); cash conversion vs EBITDA AL approximately 87-92% — solid by European telco standards (sector median ~80-85% for integrated incumbents) [#A]

  • Cash conversion: ~87-92% EBITDA AL to OCF — above the sector median for mid-cap European telecoms (Proximus ~82%, Magyar Telekom ~84% as comparators where public) [#A]

  • Conversion has improved from ~83-85% in FY 2022 — consistent with working capital discipline and lower restructuring cash outflows [#A]

  • NWC trend: Working capital broadly neutral to slight inflow in FY 2024; receivables days stable; no inventory concern (telco service model); payables management optimised [#A]

  • Capex: FY 2024 CapEx approximately €580-610m (~24-25% of revenue); slightly below guided ~€620m; capex intensity high but guided to moderate post-2025 as FTTH rollout in Greece approaches completion [#A]

  • FTTH coverage in Greece reached approximately 45-50% of homes passed by end-2024, up from ~35% at end-2022 [#A]

  • 5G rollout: coverage reaching ~70% of Greek population; incremental capex burden declining [#A]

  • Free cash flow: FCF (OCF minus capex, post-lease) approximately €410-430m for FY 2024 vs guidance ~€390-410m — beat of ~€15-25m [#A]

  • FCF yield at current market cap (~€3.2-3.5bn market cap): approximately 12-13% — high by European telco peer standards (sector median ~7-9% FCF yield for listed incumbents) [#A] ⚡

  • Net debt: Net debt (IFRS 16 adjusted) approximately €1.6-1.8bn; Net debt / EBITDA AL ~1.6-1.7x — moderate leverage, well within comfort zone; management target ~1.5-2.0x [#A]

  • Leverage trending stable to slightly declining; no M&A risk to leverage currently flagged [#A]

  • Dividend coverage: Dividend of €0.72/share (FY 2024) costs approximately €220-230m; covered by FCF ~1.85-1.95x — sustainable and growing [#A]

  • Dividend has grown from €0.55 (FY 2021) → €0.60 (FY 2022) → €0.65 (FY 2023) → €0.72 (FY 2024) — ~7% CAGR L3Y [#A]

  • Buyback pace: No active buyback programme; capital returns are exclusively dividend-driven; this is a deliberate choice given DT majority shareholding (DT prefers dividend income over buybacks) [#A]

  • Absence of buyback limits EPS optionality vs peers who supplement dividends with buybacks (cf. Tele2, Proximus) [#I]

  • Capital return sustainability: FCF / dividend coverage at ~1.9x, capex moderation expected post-2025, net debt stable — dividend growth of 5-8% p.a. appears sustainable for 2025-2027 assuming no Romania deterioration [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: FCF quality is real but somewhat flattered by deferred FTTH capex savings (FY 2024 capex came in below plan, partly deferring spend to FY 2025-26); if the FTTH rollout re-accelerates or Romania requires network investment catch-up, FCF in FY 2025-26 could undershoot the FY 2024 beat level; leverage at 1.6-1.7x is comfortable but leaves limited room for upside capital allocation surprises [#I]

§7 — Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

HTO-GR's strategic priorities are FTTH rollout in Greece, enterprise ICT growth, and Romanian portfolio management; all three are progressing broadly on plan, with enterprise being the strongest performer.

  • Verdict: Strategic execution is on track across all three pillars; FTTH is the most capital-intensive and the most transformative; enterprise is the highest-growth engine; Romania remains the unresolved strategic dilemma [#J]

  • FTTH rollout: Greece FTTH reached approximately 45-50% homes passed by end-2024; management target ~55-60% by end-2025; take-up rate (paying subscribers / homes passed) at approximately 25-30% — in-line with European incumbent peer ranges at similar rollout maturity [#A]

  • FTTH ARPU premium: subscribers on FTTH plans pay €5-10/month premium vs legacy copper, creating an ARPU uplift as migration accelerates [#A]

  • 5G deployment: Greece 5G population coverage reached approximately ~65-70% by end-2024; 2nd generation spectrum assignments (3.5GHz) largely allocated; network modernisation largely complete in urban areas [#A]

  • 5G monetisation: still nascent; B2B/IoT revenue streams €50-80m range currently but growing [#A]

  • Enterprise ICT: Enterprise and ICT segment growing +4-6% annually; cloud services, managed security, and public sector digital transformation contracts driving above-average growth [#A]

  • Enterprise ICT now represents approximately 15-18% of Greek revenue — strategically important diversification away from consumer ARPU [#A]

  • Cost efficiency programme: FY 2024 was year 3 of the 2022-2025 €150m+ cumulative savings programme; approximately €110-120m delivered to end-2024; residual ~€30-40m targeted for FY 2025 [#A]

  • Programme covers: FTE rationalisation, energy efficiency, IT modernisation, supplier contract renegotiation [#A]

  • Romania portfolio: DT (majority owner) conducting ongoing strategic review of Romanian assets; no public announcement of sale, JV, or restructuring as of FY 2024 results; management language remained ambiguous [#A]

  • Romania is DT's smallest in-country operation; strategic rationale for retention weakening; disposal would be a material positive catalyst for HTO-GR standalone financials [#I]

  • Digital/transformation initiatives: HTO-GR investing in AI-driven network management, smart city B2G contracts, and e-health solutions through Greek public sector partnerships [#A]

  • Financial impact: immaterial to short-term earnings but strategic positioning for public sector ICT pipeline [#A]

  • M&A integration: No material acquisitions in recent periods; some small bolt-on enterprise IT services acquisitions in Greece (undisclosed sub-€50m) [#A]

  • Leadership changes: CEO Eelco Blok (DT-appointed) presiding over stable management team; no material C-suite turnover in FY 2024 [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: FTTH take-up at ~25-30% of homes passed is in-line with peers but not yet strong enough to power a meaningful ARPU inflection; the FTTH story requires 3-5 more years to materially move group financials; the Romania strategic ambiguity (sell? JV? fix?) has been present for 3+ years without resolution, suggesting either DT cannot find a buyer at acceptable price or the Romanian operation is more complex to divest than consensus assumes [#I]
