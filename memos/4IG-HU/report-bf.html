EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business model and strategic positioning

4iG operates as a strategic investment holding company managing four core pillars: telecommunications (One Magyarország, 2Connect, Western Balkan operations), space and defense technologies (CarpathiaSat, Rába, joint ventures with Lockheed Martin and L3Harris), IT systems integration (4iG Informatikai), and international digital infrastructure, with the telecommunications segment generating 74.5% of consolidated revenue and space/defense contributing 11.1% . The group separated commercial telecommunications from wholesale infrastructure through a ComCo/InfraCo split, with 2Connect managing a 42,000 km optical backbone and 15,000 microwave endpoints as a neutral wholesale provider, enabling direct monetization through multi-tenant lease agreements with competitors including Yettel Hungary. Supplier dependencies and procurement risks

4iG faces severe single-source dependencies including reliance on Northrop Grumman as the exclusive manufacturer of the HUGEO geostationary satellite using the GEOStar-3 platform (valued at hundreds of millions of dollars), TelePIX of South Korea as the sole supplier of Very High Resolution electro-optical camera systems for HULEO satellites, Vertex of Germany for exclusive ground antenna systems, MetaSensing of Italy for synthetic aperture radar, and Tatra Trucks as the sole heavy-duty chassis platform provider with exclusive domestic sales, distribution, and assembly representation rights in Hungary. ONE Macedonia partnered exclusively with Ericsson to build its greenfield 5G standalone network supplying equipment for over 500 base stations representing a EUR 100 million technology investment , while the systems integration business depends heavily on tier-one vendors operating as a certified integrator for Dell, HPE, Cisco, Oracle, and SAP .

Competitive positioning and market share stability

The Hungarian mobile market operates as a stable triopoly with incumbent Magyar Telekom maintaining a dominant 48.60% subscriber share , Yettel Hungary holding 28.80% , and 4iG trailing with 22.60% , creating substantial scale disadvantages for 4iG that are partially mitigated by the landmark infrastructure-sharing agreement with e& PPF targeting up to EUR 1 billion in network synergies . Subscriber shares in Hungary have remained highly static over the last three years with Magyar Telekom's share stable around 48.60% from 2023 through 2025 and Yettel and 4iG shares flat at 28.80% and 22.60% respectively, while One Albania maintains a market-leading mobile share exceeding 50.00% established through the merger of One Telecommunications and ALBtelecom with optical fiber reaching over 50.00% of all Albanian households . 4iG Informatikai operates as the clear market leader in the Hungarian IT systems integration sector , leveraging its scale to capture high-value domestic public contracts though business-to-government represents less than 5.00% of 4iG Távközlési Holding's revenue . 28

Long-term growth vectors and disruption exposure

The group targets high-margin SaaS and smart energy metering solutions to expand IT TAM, operating the TIER III Montenegro state data center provides a EUR 54.25 million sovereign revenue stream representing 4iG's first large-scale foreign government digital infrastructure contract, the Grid Telecom MoU establishes a terrestrial optical fiber corridor connecting Albania with Greece extending to Western Europe, and the EU-backed EAGLE project delivers a 2,000-kilometer subsea system linking Egypt, Albania, and Italy by 2028 as an open-access joint venture.
