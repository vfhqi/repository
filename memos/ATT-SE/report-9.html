§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin sustainability verdict: Margin expansion appears structural in its primary driver (occupancy) but faces a hard ceiling from regulatory pricing and labour cost sensitivity — the easy gains are largely banked [#J]

Revenue CAGR: ~8.9% revenue CAGR FY22→FY24 — driven by volume (occupancy) and pricing; organic; no major M&A distortion [#J]

Profit CAGR: EBIT ~60% CAGR FY22→FY24 from trough base — substantially above revenue CAGR, confirming real operating leverage [#J]

  • Gross margin: expanded ~4pp (FY22 ~24% → FY24 ~28%) — primary driver is staffing-cost normalisation after COVID-era premium agency labour receded [#A]
  • EBITDA margin: expanded ~1.5pp (FY22 ~5.0% → FY24 ~6.5%) — confirms gross margin gains partially offset by higher occupancy-related overhead (activities, cleaning) [#A]
  • EBIT margin: expanded ~1.9pp (FY22 ~1.7% → FY24 ~3.6%) — absolute level still thin; D&A intensity is the residual drag [#A]

Operating leverage: Classic fixed-cost leverage visible — care homes have high fixed staff floors; each occupancy percentage point gained above ~85% drops directly to margin [#J]

  • Finland occupancy driver: Finland moved from ~83% to ~90%+ occupancy FY22→FY24 — the dominant margin driver [#A]
  • Sweden: occupancy and efficiency gains smaller; Sweden margin improvement was secondary to Finland [#A]

Gross margin inflection: The single largest gross margin driver was the exit from pandemic-era agency labour costs — structural to the extent that COVID doesn't recur; not structural in the sense of pricing power improvement [#J]

EBITDA margin ceiling: European care operators with comparable models (Nordic municipally-contracted care) rarely sustain EBITDA margins above 8-9% — ATT-SE at ~6.5% has 2-3pp of potential runway, but achieving it requires both pricing pass-through and occupancy stability [#J]

Pricing driver: Swedish and Finnish municipal contracts renegotiate annually or biennially — pricing gains in FY23-24 partially reflect catch-up for historical CPI inflation; this catch-up is not infinitely repeatable [#J]

Mix shift: ATT-SE's geographic mix (Finland ~55%, Sweden ~35%, Denmark ~10%) is broadly stable — no material positive mix shift driving margins [#A]

Sustainability assessment: The structural element (occupancy normalisation) is largely complete — incremental margin expansion from here requires either pricing above inflation (politically difficult) or cost efficiency (slower, harder) [#J]

Sceptical bullet — §3: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? If Nordic municipalities face fiscal pressure post-2025 (rising care demand + constrained budgets), contract renegotiations could see fee growth lag CPI — reversing the pricing catch-up and compressing margins back toward FY22 levels within 2-3 years [#J]

Changing pattern: The narrowing H1 deficit is a positive signal — suggests underlying operational improvement, not just cyclical recovery; H1 2024 EBIT margin (~3.4%) approaching H2 historical levels [#J]

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (cross-cutting)

Cross-cutting bearish synthesis: The strongest bearish case for ATT-SE's earnings history is that L3Y delivered a textbook post-trough bounce that has now largely run its course — the easy margin gains (agency labour exit, occupancy normalisation) are banked, and the forward thesis requires harder-won incremental progress in a regulated, politically sensitive industry [#J]

Reverse-engineered bear: The bull case rests on a trough recovery that the market initially doubted; the bear case is that the market has now correctly repriced the recovery, leaving little residual upside from the multiple — if so, the remaining return depends entirely on EPS outperformance vs a consensus that has been systematically revised upward [#J]

  • Counter-scenario: If FY25 H1 shows any labour cost re-acceleration (Nordic wage inflation is running ~4-5% p.a.) without proportionate pricing offset, the L3Y narrative of improving margins would be challenged for the first time [#A]

Single largest history risk: The regulatory pricing ceiling is the single most underappreciated risk — Nordic municipalities face fiscal pressure; if 2025 contract negotiations yield pricing below CPI, ATT-SE's entire forward margin expansion thesis is compromised [#J]

  • Historical precedent: Swedish care operators experienced pricing freezes in 2011-2013 following political scrutiny of private care margins; Attendo's own history includes a period of revenue growth without margin progress [#A]
