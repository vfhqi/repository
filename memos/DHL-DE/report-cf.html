EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

express shipments plunged 20% in late 2025 due to US tariff changes . The US abolished de minimis rules in August 2025 , creating massive customs clearance backlogs, with the EU abolishing de minimis under €150 in July 2026 , introducing direct customs liability and requiring formal customs clearance for all shipments . Middle East conflicts and airspace closures forced extensive rerouting around the Cape of Good Hope , with Middle East hub groundings reducing global air capacity by 11% , driving Asia-Europe rates up. Crude oil prices surged to US$110 per barrel in May 2026 , with global jet fuel costs skyrocketing by 114% year-on-year . The counter-read warns that simultaneous volume contraction and fuel price spikes could crush margins if shippers refuse surcharges during an outright global recession. Management quantified the worst-case EBIT headwind from US de minimis regulatory changes at €200m , though Jefferies argues this maximum €200m de minimis hit remains highly unlikely due to volume mitigation actions.
