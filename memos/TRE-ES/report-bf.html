EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Técnicas Reunidas is executing a strategic transformation from high-risk turnkey engineering, procurement, and construction execution to higher-margin, asset-light engineering services under the SALTA Strategic Plan, though the business remains heavily exposed to Middle East geopolitical volatility and severe customer concentration risks . The financial profile is recovering through accelerated revenue recognition and early retirement of government bailout facilities, with group revenues reaching €6.47 billion in FY2025 and net cash expanding to €360 million in Q1 2026 following full repayment of the €271 million SEPI loan . However, the top five customers generated 56.60% of total revenue in FY2025 , creating extreme single-point failure risks that could instantly destabilize operating cash flows if any major client cancels projects or delays payments.

Business model transformation and strategic positioning

Técnicas Reunidas provides high-value engineering, procurement, and construction services for complex industrial facilities globally, with Spain serving as the primary technical hub housing a workforce of nearly 14,000 employees , predominantly engineers. The group operates through five specialized business lines–Upstream & Refining, Natural Gas, Petrochemicals, Low Carbon Technologies, and Power–utilizing an asset-light operational model that relies heavily on third-party suppliers and subcontractors to minimize fixed asset intensity. Natural Gas represents the largest revenue driver , generating €4.24 billion or 65.6% of FY2025 consolidated sales , while the Petrochemicals division delivered a strong EBIT of €180.2 million with a 14.6% margin , and the Upstream & Refining segment recorded an EBIT loss of €57.7 million . The commercial model is shifting systematically away from traditional lump-sum turnkey contracts toward lower-risk Open Book Estimation and engineering, procurement, and construction management structures, with engineering services yielding superior operating margins in the 25% to 30% range and management targeting €500 million in annual services revenue by 2028 .

Geographic concentration and Middle East dependency

The Middle East region generated €4.21 billion or 65.05% of consolidated third-party revenue in FY2025 , with regional sales led by Saudi Arabia at 26.79% and the United Arab Emirates at 15.35% , creating heavy geographical concentration in a volatile geopolitical zone. Ongoing regional conflict drove a €45 million extraordinary cost provision in Q1 2026, representing the best cost estimate of project disruptions and shipping delays through the Strait of Hormuz , assuming a reopening in Q2 2026. Europe is the second largest geographical region, generating €776.7 million or 12.01% of FY2025 revenue , with European opportunities expanding rapidly driven by industrial electrification and strict emissions regulations under the EU's net-zero transition mandates. Geopolitical tensions physically affected approximately 1,000 equipment shipments in Q1 2026, forcing heavy rerouting of critical equipment and triggering the €45 million extraordinary provision , contradicting executive commentary highlighting Middle East operational resilience. 6

SALTA strategic transformation and risk mitigation

The SALTA Strategic Plan, launched in 2024, drives structural and organizational transformation with core pillars focusing on expanding service contracts, accelerating decarbonization, and building a presence in North America, while systematically reducing construction risks by utilizing local construction partners. Under the SALTA framework, management is aggressively growing its asset-light Engineering & Services segment, targeting scaling from €70 million in 2023 to €500 million by 2028 , with the service line projected to deliver a stable 30% EBIT margin through 2028. Collaborative commercial structures establish a systematic reduction of construction risks , with contracts leveraging Open Book Estimation terms to establish flexible project contingencies and earlystage service agreements targeting high-value engineering design without direct field labor liabilities. The transformational SALTA framework drives a structural shift toward asset-light services to significantly lower historical construction execution risks, with management aiming to grow high-margin engineering services revenue to over €500 million annually by 2028 and expanding cost-competitive hubs in India, Turkey, and Saudi Arabia.

Track energy transition capabilities and low-carbon portfolio

Launched in 2023, the Track program coordinates group energy transition and decarbonization activities, focusing on green hydrogen, circular economy biofuels, and carbon capture projects, with the dedicated transition team expanded to over 70 professionals during FY2025 . The proprietary RARETECH solvent extraction technology successfully produced its first kilograms of commercial-grade neodymium/praseodymium oxide at the Madrid pilot plant, addressing the European Union's critical raw materials roadmap by enabling regional supply for permanent magnets used in aviation and wind turbines. Clients show strong willingness to pay for complex decarbonization expertise, with the group aiming to double services revenues to €500 million annually by 2028 , delivering high operating margins in the 25% to 30% range and signed O&M digitalization contracts reaching €65 million with an additional €50 million under negotiation . Sustainable Aviation Fuel demand is set to expand rapidly driven by the EU's RefuelEU mandate requiring SAF blending to rise from 2% in 2025 to 70% by 2050 , while global green hydrogen demand is projected to reach an estimated 8.3 million tons annually by 2030 .

Financial recovery trajectory and balance sheet quality

Group revenues achieved a strong turnaround to reach €6.47 billion in FY2025 , representing a 45.25% increase compared to €4.45 billion in FY2024 , underpinned by normalized accrual rates across a highly diversified project backlog, with consolidated sales continuing upward momentum to €1.58 billion in Q1 2026 , 21.12% higher than Q1 2025. Profit from operations rose 60.64% to €291.10 million in FY2025 , driving the EBIT margin up to 4.50% versus 4.07% in FY2024 , though the GAAP EBIT margin fell to 1.96% in Q1 2026 due to the extraordinary

geopolitical charge. The balance sheet was significantly simplified on December 1, 2025, through the early repayment of €271 million in SEPI loans , fully retiring the €175 million profit participating loan and €165 million ordinary loan ahead of schedule, removing equity dilution risk and eliminating all associated government operating restrictions. Following the early SEPI repayment, a new shareholder remuneration policy commits to a 30% dividend payout ratio starting from FY2026 net profit, restoring direct capital returns to equity holders after a prolonged suspension during the pandemic recovery period. 18

Operational foundation quality and structural competitive advantages

Executive Chairman Juan Lladó and CEO Eduardo San Miguel provide deep institutional experience to lead the current operational recovery, with Lladó guiding corporate strategy through multiple cycles including the severe pandemic-era downturn, and San Miguel previously serving as CFO for 17 years before stepping into the top executive position in 2022. High project execution complexity, strict digital tool mandates, and tight deadlines restrict low-cost, unproven competitors, with the patented RARETECH solvent extraction process establishing global technological leadership in critical mineral separation and successful pilot plant tests in Madrid producing commercial-grade neodymium/praseodymium oxide from Brazilian ore. Operating profits surged in FY2025, driven by normalized project execution speeds across the global backlog, with consolidated FY2025 EBIT expanding 61% year-over-year to €291.10 million , yielding a 4.50% margin , and Q4 2025 EBIT reaching a historic high of €86.60 million , illustrating strong late-year execution momentum. As the backlog shifts from turnkey EPC to services, financial outputs improve mechanically rather than relying on heroic execution, with engineering services contracts delivering stable operating margins in the 25% to 30% range and back-to-back supplier terms enabling the group to maintain a structurally negative commercial working capital balance .

Supply chain vulnerabilities and execution constraints

Total spending on purchases from materials suppliers and subcontracts rose to €4.63 billion in FY2025 , expanding significantly from €3.21 billion in FY2024 , with sourcing in Spain reaching €1.74 billion or 34.98% of total spending , growing robustly from only €425 million or 13% of spending in FY2024 . The construction subcontractor workforce assigned to active projects averaged 33,705 workers during FY2025 , spiking to a maximum of 42,647 workers at peak times , requiring a dedicated internal team of more than 3,190 construction professionals to manage this workforce amid acute shortages of qualified construction personnel . Severe geopolitical escalation in the Middle East heavily disrupted marine shipping corridors , with closure of the Strait of Hormuz directly affecting approximately 1,000 equipment shipments in Q1 2026, forcing the group to reroute equipment and generating significant transport delay costs . 27

Customer concentration risks and contractual dynamics

The group exhibits significant reliance on its largest customers, with the top five customers generating 56.60% of total revenue in FY2025 , rising from 53.64% in FY2024 , and individual clients regularly accounting for over 10% of total consolidated revenues , creating extreme single-point project failure exposure. Saudi Aramco remains a key partner awarding mega-projects like the Riyas and Jafurah gas developments, while ADNOC Offshore awarded a €3.10 billion EPC contract for Lower Zakum in February 2025, and ADNOC Onshore maintains a strong operational footprint via the Bu Hasa project in the UAE. Substantial customer switching costs restrict clients from replacing the group mid-execution, with early engineering phases locking clients into the group's proprietary processes and Digital Twin assets, while swapping contractors on a multi-billion-dollar project triggers severe commissioning delays and litigation risks .
