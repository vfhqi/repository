COMPANY DESCRIPTION

AF Gruppen ASA (Oslo: AFG.OL) is a Norwegian contracting and industrial group founded in 1985 and headquartered in Oslo. With FY2025 revenue of approximately NOK 31.99 billion (~EUR 2.9bn), it is the second-largest construction contractor in Norway behind Veidekke and competes directly with Skanska Norge, NCC, and the Swedish peers in its home markets.

Business model: AF Gruppen operates as a multi-segment construction and civil engineering contractor with six reporting divisions plus a small property development arm.

Revenue Mix by Segment (approximate, FY2024/2025):
- Civil Engineering (Anlegg): Norway roads, railways, tunnels, bridges, ports, energy plants. Largest and highest-margin segment (~NOK 9-10bn revenue, 9-10% EBIT margin). ~30% of group revenue.
- Construction (Bygg): Residential, commercial, public building — new-build and rehabilitation. Norway-focused. ~NOK 8-9bn revenue, margins recovering toward 5-6%.
- Betonmast: Acquired 2019 (66%), fully integrated by 2022-23. Building contractor, specialist concrete/masonry. NOK ~4-5bn revenue. Historically a drag; improving toward 5% margin by 2025.
- Sweden: Building and civil operations. Structurally challenged market. NOK ~3-4bn revenue. Near-zero to negative margin 2022-2024, improving in 2025.
- Energy and Environment: Demolition, remediation, energy efficiency. NOK ~2-3bn revenue, stable mid-single-digit margins.
- Offshore (AF Offshore Decom): Removal, recycling, and decommissioning of offshore oil and gas platforms. NOK ~1.5-2bn revenue. High-volatility segment — project-by-project, loss-prone on fixed-price.
- Property: Residential development (Betonmast Boligbygg). Small, loss-making, winding down. Homes sold: 216 in 2022, 53 in 2023.

Geography: ~80%+ Norway, ~15% Sweden, growing UK/International via Offshore and now Claxton (Aberdeen).

Business model characteristics: Fixed-price contracting dominates; revenue visibility via order backlog (NOK 44.7bn end-2025 = ~1.4x annual revenue); labour-intensive with significant subcontractor pass-through; negative working capital structure (advance payments from clients).

SECTION 1: CHANGE IN LEADERSHIP

L12M (May 2025 – May 2026):

  • CEO Amund Toftum (in role since August 2020) remains in post. Background is operational — former EVP Offshore, EVP Construction, project director within AF. No change signalled. Animal CEO assessment: Toftum is an insider with deep project knowledge, appropriate for a contractor navigating operational underperformance. His public language on 2024 was candidly self-critical ("gap in performance has been too wide") — a positive signal vs the obfuscation typical in construction boardrooms.

  • CFO Anny Oen confirmed in role since 2022, having joined AF in 2009 as Group Accounting Manager. Her tenure covers the full 2022-2025 underperformance period and recovery — she has complete institutional context. Some older sources reference Sverre Haerem as CFO but this appears to be a legacy listing. Oen's continuity is a positive.

  • Divisional leadership: Post-2024 strategy review, there is evidence of second-level management action, particularly in Sweden. AF Bygg Vast, AF Bygg Ost, and AF Anlaggning Vast were publicly cited as loss-making units requiring management intervention. No named changes announced, but operational restructuring at unit MD level is inferred from the candid strategy review language.

  • Board composition: Employee representation enhanced via agreement forgoing corporate assembly. PWC continues as external auditor. No major board changes flagged in 2025.

  • Claxton integration leadership: Claxton to operate as independent business unit within Offshore, with its own senior executives joining AF Gruppen. This adds an Aberdeen-based management layer (~400 employees). Cultural integration risk (UK vs Norwegian contractor cultures) is non-trivial given AF's prior Swedish integration difficulties.

  • Construction leadership: The 2024 strategy refresh ("project management and productivity as key success factors") implies accountability changes for project managers and divisional heads, even if not publicly named. This matches the Betonmast pattern — operational personnel changes driven by results, not announced proactively.

N36M outlook:
- Toftum's credibility is on a four-year runway to the 2028 targets. If Sweden and offshore are not resolved by 2026-27, board patience will be tested.
- The 2024 strategy refresh is a public self-critique acknowledging that Betonmast integration absorbed management bandwidth and degraded project controls across the group. Whether the lesson translates into changed behaviour at project manager level is the key unknown.
- No succession signals visible — key-person risk exists, but Toftum's operational background is directly relevant to the recovery task.

Degree of change: YELLOW — CEO stable, CFO has continuity, divisional management restructuring ongoing, Claxton adds international management complexity. Leadership team is known but has underdelivered significantly against its own targets for three years. Watch for Sweden unit MD changes in 2026.

What would change it to Orange/Red: A CEO departure, or a second large offshore provision within 12 months of Claxton closing.

SECTION 2: CHANGE IN STRATEGY/STRUCTURE

L12M:

  • New 2028 strategy launched (developed throughout 2024, presented at CMD/annual results Feb 2025): three pillars — project management excellence, productivity improvement, and organisational adaptability. A meaningful departure from the 2021-24 strategy, which was growth-led. The new strategy is operationally focused — a tacit acknowledgement that scale was pursued faster than management capacity allowed.

  • 2028 targets: 7% EBIT margin (unchanged from the 2024 target that was missed) and 7% annual revenue growth, plus halving climate/environmental footprint. The margin target reprieve is notable — 7% was also the 2024 target. The runway has been extended by four years without revising the ambition downward.

  • Betonmast integration largely complete: Full merger of AF Gruppen Sverige AB and Betonmast Sverige AB planned for 2023. By 2025 Betonmast Norway is reporting improved profitability (5.3% margin Q1 2025). The multi-year drag is converting to improvement, though Sweden remains challenged.

  • Offshore: strategic growth pivot via Claxton. Acquisition of Claxton Engineering Services (April 2026, GBP 63.5m for a GBP 82m average revenue / ~14% operating margin business) is the most significant strategic move in the period. AF is doubling down on offshore decommissioning and adding offshore wind installation equipment. Claxton is headquartered in Aberdeen, operates from Great Yarmouth, has ~400 employees, and owns a proprietary fleet of surface and subsea risers plus offshore wind installation tools. Transaction financed from AF's liquidity reserves. NCA approval pending May 2026.

  • Property development: de-emphasis. Homes sold collapsed from 216 (2022) to 53 (2023). No public exit announcement but the segment is visibly being run down. Removes a loss-making drag and capital commitment.

  • Sweden: restructuring, not exit. Sweden remains in the portfolio despite multi-year losses. Best-performing Swedish units (Kanonaden, AF Prefab, AF Bygg Syd, AF Harnosand) demonstrate quality within Sweden — the problem is concentrated in specific geographic units, suggesting the fix is personnel and project selection rather than market exit.

N36M:
- Claxton closes Q2 2026 and becomes the key execution watch-point. At GBP 63.5m EV for GBP 82m revenue and 14% margin, the acquisition looks financially attractive (EV ~0.8x revenue). Risk: fixed-price offshore work can deteriorate rapidly, as the Dutch shelf episode demonstrated.
- The 7% group EBIT margin by 2028 requires Sweden to exit losses, offshore to sustain profitability, Claxton to not generate a provision, and Civil Engineering to hold 9-10% margins through the NTP cycle. All achievable individually — simultaneously more demanding.

Degree of change: ORANGE — The 2028 strategy is a material reset from the failed growth playbook. Claxton is a significant structural addition. Direction is clearer, but credibility of the 7% target depends on three simultaneous recoveries.

What would change it to Red: Another large acquisition before Claxton is integrated, or a further offshore provision within 12 months.

SECTION 3: CHANGE IN PRIORITIES/AMBITION

L12M:

  • CEO tone shifted measurably. Toftum's 2024 characterisation ("gap in performance has been too wide") is more self-critical than prior year language, which tended toward blaming market conditions. This honesty is a positive signal in a sector prone to excusing underperformance.

  • Productivity and project management elevated to top priority. Replaces prior emphasis on volume growth and market share. In construction, genuine productivity improvement requires better project selection, tighter contract structuring, and stronger site management accountability. The new strategy implies AF recognises it bid too aggressively or managed too loosely on contracts that required provisions.

  • ESG and sustainability ambition raised: New goal to halve climate/environmental footprint by 2028. Consistent with Norway's regulatory trajectory and enhances positioning on public sector tenders where ESG criteria increasingly influence award decisions. Zero serious personal injuries stated as a prerequisite for profitable growth — in a sector where safety culture directly affects insurance, retention, and productivity, this is operationally material.

  • Offshore ambition accelerated despite losses. The Claxton acquisition demonstrates management has not lost conviction in the offshore growth story after three years of provisions. This is either well-founded confidence based on pipeline visibility, or under-appreciation of fixed-price execution risk. The 14% operating margin Claxton delivers (vs near-zero from Dutch shelf contracts) suggests AF is deliberately targeting the technology-differentiated, equipment-led part of offshore decom rather than the commodity execution that caused losses.

  • Dividend raised 30%: NOK 6.50 for FY2025 (vs NOK 5.00 for FY2024). At ~NOK 31-35/share, approximately 2% yield. The board is signalling financial recovery conviction. Boards typically do not raise dividends 30% if they expect near-term earnings deterioration — this is a credibility signal.

  • Capital discipline improving: ROCE of 25.6% in Q1 2025 is above the 20% strategic target. Step-change from the 6.22% ROIC cited in older data, suggesting FY2025 improvements are genuine and not solely margin arithmetic.

N36M:
- The pivot from growth to profitability must be validated through project margins over 2-3 years. False Friend risk: record order backlog does not guarantee improved margins if new contracts are mispriced.
- Simultaneous 7% growth + 7% margin targets create inherent tension — growing into a soft Swedish market and expanding offshore while rebuilding margin is a complex execution task.

Degree of change: ORANGE — Genuine shift in management rhetoric toward quality-over-quantity, supported by the Claxton acquisition rationale and 30% dividend increase. Whether behavioural change at project level matches the language remains the open question.

What would change it: Delivery of 6%+ full-year EBIT margin in FY2026 would confirm the pivot. A further offshore provision would confirm it is not.

SECTION 5: VALUE CHAIN — DEMAND

  • Norway public sector: primary driver, structurally supportive. The National Transport Plan 2025-2036, approved by Storting in spring 2024, allocates BNOK 95.2 over 12 years. Rail alone receives NKr 435bn (~US$40bn). Roads, bridges, tunnels, ports, and harbour infrastructure command substantial allocations. The E6 Roterud-Storhove contract alone (AF, Nye Veier client) is NOK 6.35bn fixed-price, execution 2025-2030.

  • Order backlog at record NOK 44.72bn (end-2025, up from NOK 40.35bn end-2024). Civil Engineering backlog specifically: NOK 17,966m at end-2025 (up from NOK 16,433m). Demand is running ahead of supply capacity in Norwegian civil — a supplier's market.

  • CQ-C1 — order book quality: Civil Engineering backlog is highest-quality: public sector client, long duration, index-linked price adjustment mechanisms protecting against input cost inflation. Construction/Sweden mix more cyclical and less predictable. Offshore backlog quality uncertain given fixed-price execution exposure.

  • CQ-C5 — recurring vs lump-sum: Almost entirely project-based (fixed-price or remeasured lump-sum). No recurring revenue streams — no O&M, no FM, no concession income. Three offshore provisions in three years are the direct structural consequence. This is sector-wide in Nordic contracting, not AF-specific, but it materially limits earnings quality.

  • Norway housing: trough-to-recovery. Dwelling permits -16.8% in 2023 and -5.9% H1 2024. Rising rates and high construction costs drove the slowdown. New home sales rose 20%+ in the year to early 2025, and Norway construction output is projected to grow 3.4% AAGR 2025-2028. AF's Construction and Betonmast residential exposure is in early recovery — meaningful upside from the cyclical turn.

  • Sweden: still weak but improving. Swedish construction remains subdued — AF's Swedish units reported near-zero margins in 2022-2024, revenue declines of -29% in Q1 2024. Recovery likely 1-2 years behind Norway. Kepler Cheuvreux's upgrade to Buy in mid-2025 was partly driven by evidence of Sweden beginning to turn.

  • Offshore decom demand: structural growth >5% CAGR. North Sea Decommissioning Market growing at more than 5% CAGR. BP Andrew field contract (awarded, through 2031-32 in JV with THREE60 Energy), multiple Ithaca Energy contracts (2026 deliveries, NOK 225-275m per platform), and the Claxton acquisition together position AF as a leading decom player. Structural demand, not cyclical — platforms must be removed by regulatory obligation.

  • Offshore wind installation: emerging option. Claxton's equipment portfolio (marine risers, subsea installation tools) is applicable to offshore wind foundation installation and servicing. North Sea offshore wind build-out accelerating through 2030. An early-stage real option unlocked by the Claxton transaction — not in AF's financials yet but material in the 5-year horizon.

  • Demand Pulse Reversion check. Record backlog is real and multi-year (NTP-backed), not a housing-cycle front-loading. However, Q4 2025 order intake (NOK 9.59bn) was below Q4 2024 (NOK 12.51bn) — a potential deceleration signal worth monitoring. Individual large NTP contracts (like E6) cluster intake into specific quarters, creating apparent volatility in the series.

  • Norwegian defence: emerging. NATO 2% target is driving Norwegian defence infrastructure investment. Military bases, runways, and fortifications represent a growing construction sub-segment where AF has relevant civil capabilities and Norwegian security clearances.

Degree of change: GREEN — Demand is genuinely strong and structurally supported. NTP is the most durable demand driver in Nordic construction. The shift from housing-led to infrastructure-led demand is positive for AF's margin mix.

SECTION 6: VALUE CHAIN — COMPETITION

  • Norwegian construction: concentrated oligopoly at the top. Contracts above NOK 500m are accessible to approximately 5-6 qualified bidders: Veidekke (~NOK 37bn revenue, largest), AF Gruppen (~NOK 32bn), Skanska Norge, NCC Norge, Eiffage Norway (international entrant), and Strabag for selective projects. This structural concentration creates rational pricing dynamics in good times and dangerous underbidding in competitive downturns.

  • Veidekke outscaling AF. Veidekke has pulled ahead in revenue (~NOK 37bn vs ~NOK 32bn) and historically maintains slightly better margins. For AF to achieve 7% margins while growing at 7%, it must outperform Veidekke on profitability — achievable given AF's Civil Engineering crown jewel but not the historical baseline.

  • Sweden: structurally disadvantaged. AF entered Sweden at scale via Betonmast into a market where Skanska, NCC, Peab, and Besqab have entrenched positions and lower-cost structures. The Swedish unit losses reflect competitive disadvantage compounded by bad contracts — not solely market conditions. This is a structural issue, not a cyclical one, and is why "restructuring not exit" is the harder path.

  • Offshore decom: specialised niche with high barriers. AF Offshore Decom competes against Aker Solutions, Wood Group, and Heerema (heavy lift, JV partner on BP Andrew). Claxton adds proprietary technology (risers, subsea equipment) that creates genuine differentiation and pricing power — the 14% Claxton operating margin vs near-zero from commodity offshore contracts makes this case explicit.

  • False Friends check — backlog growth does not equal margin growth. A record backlog is only valuable if it contains well-priced contracts. The Dutch shelf losses and Q4 2025 offshore provision occurred on contracts that were in the backlog when booked. The quality of each new contract award matters as much as its headline value.

  • International competition entering Norway. Eiffage Norway won the E18 Rugtvedt-Langangen road contract — confirming European contractors will compete on major Norwegian projects. Sustained local presence requires safety culture embed, local subcontractor relationships, and bonding capacity — meaningful but not impenetrable barriers.

  • Pricing power assessment. Norwegian civil: tight market, small bidder pool, pricing is rational to supportive — consistent with Civil Engineering holding 9-10% margins. Swedish building: pricing power is low (oversupplied with capacity). Offshore decom: fixed-price contracts standard, structurally capping upside and concentrating execution risk on the contractor.

Degree of change: YELLOW — Competitive landscape stable. AF's relative position in Sweden is structurally challenged. Offshore differentiation increasing materially via Claxton. Civil Engineering remains competitively well-positioned in a supply-constrained market.

SECTION 7: VALUE CHAIN — DISRUPTION

  • BIM/digital project management: Norwegian public clients mandate BIM on major infrastructure. AF is digitally competent but not a technology leader. Risk: more productive foreign contractors deploying AI-assisted project management erode the local advantage over 5-10 years. Not imminent.

  • Modular and prefabricated construction: AF owns AF Prefab i Mälardalen (a strong-performing Swedish unit) — demonstrating capability. Norway's heavy use of cast-in-place concrete (seismic zone, complex ground conditions) limits modular adoption pace vs UK or Germany.

  • AI in project estimation and risk management: Fixed-price contractors deploying AI-assisted cost estimation, ground condition modelling, and schedule optimisation could materially reduce provision frequency. AF's Dutch shelf and Swedish write-downs suggest current project controls are insufficient for the most complex environments. AI tooling applied to contract risk assessment could directly address this structural weakness.

  • Offshore wind installation technology: Claxton's equipment portfolio is applicable to offshore wind monopile installation and servicing. As North Sea offshore wind accelerates through 2030, demand for specialised installation equipment is growing rapidly. This is a genuine technology-adjacent growth option unlocked by the Claxton transaction.

  • Carbon and green construction: Norway's regulatory trajectory requires reducing embodied carbon. Low-carbon concrete is commercially available but more expensive. AF's 2028 climate halving goal requires investment in green construction methods — a cost headwind unless passed through to clients, and a tendering advantage on ESG-scored public contracts.

  • Labour market automation: Norwegian construction wages among Europe's highest; sector productivity flat for 20 years. Robotic formwork, autonomous plant, and 3D concrete printing remain pre-commercial in civil engineering at scale. Limited near-term disruption but a medium-term productivity opportunity.

Degree of change: YELLOW — Disruption risk is real but medium-term. The most actionable signal is the Claxton offshore wind equipment angle. AI-driven project risk management is the most relevant internal disruption watch-point given the provision history.

SECTION 8: VALUE CHAIN — POLITICAL AND REGULATORY

  • NTP 2025-2036 is the single most important political fact for AF Gruppen. BNOK 95.2 committed to transport infrastructure over 12 years. Rail alone receives NKr 435bn. Norwegian infrastructure investment has been bipartisan for decades — political reversal risk is low.

  • Nye Veier model: beneficial for contractors. Nye Veier (state-owned road developer, est. 2015) operates on design-and-build contracts with clearer risk allocation and performance incentives vs traditional Statens vegvesen road administration contracts. AF's E6 Roterud-Storhove engagement represents the best risk-adjusted contract structure available in Norwegian civil.

  • Norway: EU EEA member, not EU member. Norwegian offshore decom operates under PSA (Petroleum Safety Authority Norway) rules — stringent but well-established. UK North Sea operations (growing via Claxton) operate under NSTA — a different regulatory framework requiring navigation but well-understood in the sector.

  • UK Offshore: North Sea Transition Deal + windfall taxes. UK political environment for North Sea operators has been turbulent — windfall taxes since 2022 have reduced operator cashflows. NSTA actively enforces decom obligations. Net effect on AF/Claxton: uncertainty on individual project timing, but decommissioning cannot be permanently avoided — it is a regulatory obligation.

  • Sweden: regulatory complexity. Swedish housing market is hampered by Plan and Building Act complexity and slow planning processes — a political problem acknowledged but not resolved by successive Swedish governments. AF's Swedish recovery is partly held hostage to Swedish regulatory reform.

  • ESG regulation: CSRD compliance. AF Gruppen falls under CSRD as a large European-listed company. The climate halving goal (2028) is aligned with CSRD requirements. Management bandwidth-consuming but not a direct business risk.

  • Norwegian labour law: Strong union tradition, IA agreements, apprenticeship requirements. AF's stated ZERO serious injuries prerequisite for profitable growth reflects genuine understanding of the link between safety culture, labour retention, and long-term cost structure.

  • 🚩 RARE: NCA review of Claxton. The acquisition is subject to Norwegian Competition Authority review. Approval is expected but creates minor near-term closing uncertainty of 1-2 quarters.

Degree of change: GREEN — Political backdrop firmly supportive. NTP is multi-year, bipartisan, and legally committed. UK decom regulatory obligations are structural demand generators. Main political risks are indirect: UK windfall taxation on operators and Swedish housing regulatory inertia.

SECTION 9: VALUE CHAIN — SUPPLY SIDE

  • Labour: dominant input cost, tight in Norway. Norwegian construction wages among Europe's highest. Labour is ~35-40% of direct cost base. Construction sector labour shortage eased from 2022 peak but skilled civil engineers, tunnel workers, and offshore decom technicians remain scarce. AF's apprenticeship programmes, safety culture, and employer brand are competitive advantages in labour attraction.

  • Materials: input cost inflation materially eased. Construction cost index +3.3% YoY in Q1 2024 (materials +3.9%, labour +3.1%) — well below the 2022 peak of 8-10%. Fixed-price contractors who locked in 2021-22 contracts before the inflation spike suffered; new contracts signed in 2024-25 at post-spike input prices have significantly better cost recovery. A structural tailwind for 2025-2027 margins.

  • Subcontractors: ~40-50% of project costs on large civil contracts are subcontracted. In a tight Norwegian market, subcontractor availability and pricing directly affects margin. AF's scale gives preferential access to quality subcontractors and stronger negotiating position.

  • Equipment — Claxton adds capital intensity. Pre-Claxton, AF's capex was modest (~2-3% of revenue). Claxton adds a meaningful proprietary equipment fleet (marine risers, subsea tools, offshore wind installation equipment). This creates an equipment-leasing revenue stream within offshore and adds capital intensity — but the 14% Claxton operating margin suggests this model is highly cash-generative.

  • Energy costs: Norwegian structural advantage. Norway's abundant, cheap hydroelectric power shelters AF's Norwegian operations from European energy cost shocks. A structural input cost advantage vs Strabag, Sacyr, or Ferrovial, and vs Claxton's UK operations.

  • Swedish input cost dynamics: Sweden faced sharper input cost inflation in 2022-23 (SEK weakness, higher European energy exposure). AF's Swedish unit write-downs are partly attributable to contracts priced in 2021-22 at SEK inputs that then inflated faster. This dynamic is reversing as input costs normalise.

  • Supply concentration in offshore decom recycling. AF's ownership and operation of AF Environmental Base Vats (700,000m2 recycling and processing facility in Norway) provides vertical integration in platform disposal — a meaningful competitive moat in North Sea decom that peers cannot easily replicate.

Degree of change: YELLOW — Input cost inflation has substantially eased. Key supply-side risk is labour scarcity in Norwegian civil. Claxton adds equipment capital intensity — manageable but changes the offshore division's asset character.

SECTION 10: REVENUE/DEMAND CYCLE

  • Norwegian civil engineering: mid-cycle with structural NTP floor. NTP-driven infrastructure cycle is long (12-year plans) and relatively insensitive to short-term economic fluctuations. Peak construction activity typically lags plan launch by 3-5 years — this suggests peak civil activity for AF Gruppen is 2027-2030.

  • Norwegian building/construction: early-to-mid recovery. The 2022-2024 period was the cyclical trough driven by rising interest rates and high construction costs. Dwelling permits -16.8% in 2023. New home sales +20%+ in early 2025. Norway construction output projected to grow 3.4% AAGR 2025-2028. AF's Construction and Betonmast residential exposure is in early recovery — meaningful upside from the cyclical turn.

  • Sweden: still at or near trough. Swedish construction more severely affected than Norway. AF's Swedish units near-zero to negative margins 2022-2024. Recovery likely 1-2 years behind Norway, dependent on Swedish rate normalisation.

  • Offshore decom: secular growth, not cyclical. Decom is driven by platform age and regulatory obligation, not oil price directly (though low prices delay timing decisions). The structural aging of North Sea fields means decom volumes are accelerating. BP Andrew field contract runs to 2031-32 — a long-duration revenue commitment.

  • Order backlog as forward indicator. Record NOK 44.72bn at end-2025 (1.4x revenue) suggests demand running ahead of capacity. Historically associated with improved contract pricing — consistent with Q4 2025 Civil Engineering margin of 9.5%. However, Q4 2025 order intake (NOK 9.59bn) below Q4 2024 (NOK 12.51bn) — potential deceleration signal.

  • Demand Pulse Reversion check. Record backlog is NTP-backed and multi-year — not a housing-cycle front-loading. But individual large NTP contracts cluster intake, creating apparent series volatility. Risk: 2024-25 intake represents a cluster of major NTP awards that does not repeat at the same rate in 2026-27. This is a material risk to FY2026-27 revenue growth.

  • FCF cycle dynamics. AF's negative working capital means cash is generated ahead of revenue recognition. FY2025 FCF conversion of ~185% likely reflects a working capital inflow cycle. Sustainable FCF conversion is more likely 150-165% — still excellent by construction sector standards.

Degree of change: GREEN — Demand cycle is in a favourable phase: Norwegian civil near peak activity, Norwegian building recovering, offshore decom structurally growing. The only negative signals are Sweden (trough), Q4 2025 order intake deceleration, and potential NTP intake clustering risk in 2026-27.

SECTION 11: VALUE CHAIN — MACROECONOMIC

L18M (Nov 2024 – May 2026):
