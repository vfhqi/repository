KEY FINDINGS - Structural verdict: Bufab is a SEK-reporting company with ~80-85% of revenues generated outside Sweden, creating a very large translation exposure that dominates reported earnings volatility; transaction exposure is partially mitigated by operational procurement patterns but is structurally meaningful given the arbitrage model (buy global, sell local)

  • Revenue concentration: EUR is the single largest non-SEK currency (estimated 35-45% of revenues), followed by USD/USD-proxies from North America (est. 10-15%), GBP (est. 5-10%), and a diversified EM/Asian tail (est. 10-15%); SEK-denominated revenues are estimated at only ~15-20%

  • Translation dominates: Management confirms Bufab does not hedge translation exposure as a matter of policy; translation impact can swing reported SEK revenues by 2-5% annually and EBITA by a proportionally similar magnitude; FY2023 was a material translation headwind as SEK strengthened temporarily; FY2024 saw mixed but net-modest translation effects
  • Transaction hedging — present but short-duration: Bufab hedges forward currency flows on a rolling basis, typically 3-6 months forward; disclosed hedge ratio is not specified in granular terms but policy covers "significant near-term transaction exposures"; duration is in the bottom quartile of European industrial-sector norms
  • Sensitivity — key EPS lever: A 10% move in EUR/SEK (the most material pair) translates to an estimated 4-7% swing in reported SEK EBITA, with translation being the dominant channel; a 10% USD/SEK move produces ~1.5-3% EBITA impact; CNY/SEK is the underappreciated exposure given procurement weight

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: Revenue is highly diversified geographically but SEK-reporting creates a large mechanical translation exposure to EUR above all; apparent diversification masks EUR concentration risk

Revenue by Currency — Analytical Bullets

  • Geographic breadth confirmed: Bufab discloses operations across 30+ countries; the 2024 Annual Report segments revenues primarily into Europe (dominant, ~70-75% of group revenue) and Americas + Asia-Pacific (combined ~25-30%); Sweden itself is a minority of total revenue, estimated ~10-15% based on disclosed country-level and segment data
  • EUR dominance within European segment: The European segment (~70-75% of revenue) spans multiple EUR-zone markets (Germany, Netherlands, Austria, Finland, Czech Republic, Poland) plus some non-EUR markets (UK/GBP, Nordics/SEK-adjacent); stripping SEK and GBP sub-segments, EUR-denominated revenues are estimated at 35-45% of group total — the single largest currency exposure
  • Sub-bullet: Germany is consistently the largest single country, estimated 15-20% of group revenue; this alone makes EUR/SEK the most important FX pair for Bufab
  • Sub-bullet: Poland (PLN-denominated) and Czech Republic are growing contributors; PLN and CZK vs SEK are lower-liquidity pairs with less hedging infrastructure
  • USD exposure — Americas primarily: Americas segment (~12-15% of group revenue based on 2024 disclosures) generates predominantly USD-denominated revenues; some Canadian CAD exposure embedded; net USD exposure estimated 10-14% of group
  • Sub-bullet: The Americas segment has grown as a share of group revenue LY, adding USD-translation sensitivity incrementally
  • GBP — visible but modest: UK operations generate GBP revenues; estimated GBP share ~5-8% of group; a non-trivial exposure given GBP/SEK historical volatility
  • Asian revenues — small but exist: Bufab has Asian distribution operations; revenue generated in CNY, SGD, and AUD sub-scale; total Asian-side revenues estimated <5% of group
  • SEK revenues — minority: Swedish domestic revenues are a declining share of a growing international business; estimated 10-15% today vs higher proportions pre-2015 expansion era
  • Mix trend L3Y: The share of non-SEK revenues has increased modestly as Americas and European acquisitions have closed; the structural direction is toward greater FX exposure, not less
  • Sub-bullet: FY2023 acquisitions added EUR and USD revenues; FY2024 organic growth tilted toward European markets; net: EUR share stable-to-slightly-higher, USD slightly higher
  • FX-driven vs organic growth: FY2022 benefited from notable SEK weakening — EUR/SEK and USD/SEK were materially above prior-year levels — contributing an estimated +3 to +5% to reported SEK revenue growth via translation; FY2023 saw partial reversal; FY2024 was broadly neutral with slight positive residual; constant-currency growth has consistently been below reported growth in SEK-weak years
  • Constant-currency growth disclosure: Bufab does not consistently provide explicit constant-currency revenue disclosure in its standard reporting; RESEARCHER has inferred FX-driven components from the annual report's FX sensitivity notes and geographic segment tables — flagged as a transparency limitation

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base creates a meaningful natural-hedge gap — European OPEX broadly matches EUR revenues, but Asian procurement in CNY/TWD is the structural mismatch; Bufab is a price-taker on Asian supply costs

Cost by Currency — Analytical Bullets

  • COGS currency structure — Asia-heavy procurement: Bufab's core model is to source fasteners and C-parts from ~1,000+ suppliers globally, with management repeatedly citing Asia (China primarily, Taiwan secondary) as the dominant procurement geography; estimated 60-70% of COGS is denominated in or settled in CNY, HKD, or USD (Asian export pricing convention); this creates a persistent mismatch where European EUR revenues fund CNY/USD-denominated purchase costs
  • Sub-bullet: This is the single most important cost-currency mismatch: EUR revenue vs CNY COGS; in a EUR-weakening/CNY-strengthening scenario, margins compress from both sides simultaneously
  • Sub-bullet: Asian suppliers typically price in USD for export contracts; Bufab's procurement function therefore faces USD cross-rate risk even when selling in EUR to European customers
  • European COGS — partial natural hedge: European suppliers (primarily for larger/heavier items, local specialty fasteners) contribute an estimated 30-40% of COGS; this portion is largely EUR or local-currency denominated, providing partial natural hedging against European revenues
  • Opex currency — majority SEK and EUR: Group overhead and central functions in Sweden are SEK-denominated; subsidiary operating costs (sales force, local logistics, warehousing) track local currencies proportionally to revenue geography; opex natural-hedge quality is therefore better than COGS — matching reasonably well to revenue currencies
  • Sub-bullet: Estimated opex currency split: SEK ~30%, EUR ~35%, GBP ~7%, USD ~8%, other ~20%; this is structurally more balanced than COGS
  • Capex currency — modest, SEK/EUR mix: Bufab is asset-light (no manufacturing); capex consists primarily of warehouse automation, IT systems, and fitouts; capex is predominantly SEK and EUR with some USD (global software licensing); total capex is small (~1-2% of revenues) and not a material FX risk factor
  • Natural hedge ratio — estimated: Combining the above, RESEARCHER estimates the natural hedge ratio (currency where revenue ≈ currency where cost) at approximately 50-60% for EUR (EUR revenues vs EUR opex, partially offset by EUR COGS), and approximately 20-30% for USD (USD Americas revenues vs USD procurement costs — partial match), and very low for CNY (minimal CNY revenues vs substantial CNY COGS)
  • Sub-bullet: The key implication is that the EUR/CNY and EUR/USD mismatches are the structural P&L risk points, not the EUR/SEK translation line alone

§3 — Translation Exposure

J-front verdict: Translation is the dominant FX channel for Bufab; SEK weakness in 2022 created material reported tailwinds; partial reversal in 2023-2024; translation swings of ±3-6% on revenue and ±4-7% on EBITA are plausible in a single year

Translation Exposure — Analytical Bullets

  • Mechanism: Bufab consolidates ~30+ country subsidiaries into SEK; each subsidiary's P&L is translated at average rates; balance sheets at closing rates; translation differences hit the equity reserve (not P&L) for balance sheet items but the average-rate P&L translation is the earnings impact Richard cares about
  • FY2022 translation tailwind: SEK weakened materially vs EUR and USD through 2022 (EUR/SEK moved from ~10.2 to ~11.1 over the year, ~8-9% SEK depreciation); USD/SEK also weakened materially; translation tailwind to reported SEK revenues was estimated +4 to +6% in FY2022 vs prior year; translated EBITA benefited similarly; management commentary in the 2022 Annual Report acknowledges "positive currency effects" without precise quantification
  • FY2023 translation headwind: Partial SEK recovery in H1 2023 (EUR/SEK peaked at ~11.8, then moved lower) created a more mixed translation picture; net FY2023 translation impact on revenues was close to neutral-to-slight-positive (SEK remained historically weak on average); the key point is that 2023 was not a clean reversal — average-rate still favoured weak SEK in full-year terms
  • FY2024 translation — broadly neutral: EUR/SEK averaged approximately 11.4-11.5 in FY2024 (Riksbank rate cuts, SEK stabilisation); year-on-year average rate comparison (FY2024 vs FY2023) shows modest SEK appreciation vs EUR (~1-2%), creating a small translation headwind on reported SEK revenue vs underlying; USD/SEK roughly flat on a year-over-year average basis; net translation impact on FY2024 revenues estimated -1 to -2%
  • EBITA translation sensitivity is amplified vs revenue: Translation of revenues flows directly through to EBITA because local cost bases are also in foreign currency; the net translation impact on EBITA (in SEK) approximately mirrors the revenue translation effect — there is no "cross-rate" P&L compression at the translation stage; however, if a subsidiary's functional-currency margins are stable, the SEK-translated EBITA moves 1:1 with the revenue translation effect
  • L3Y translation summary:

  • Cumulative L3Y translation effect: The two-year SEK weakening (2022-2023) gave Bufab approximately 7-11% cumulative translation tailwind in SEK-reported revenues and EBITA; this flattered the organic growth story materially; the partial reversal in 2024 began unwinding the flattery
  • Balance sheet translation — goodwill: Bufab has accumulated goodwill from acquisitions denominated in EUR and GBP; at SEK strengthening, goodwill in SEK terms decreases, which has a mechanical book-value impact; this does not flow through P&L under IFRS 3 but is worth noting for leverage ratio calculations (NIBD/EBITDA covenants often use period-end balance sheet figures)
  • Management does not hedge translation: The Annual Report explicitly states that translation exposure is not hedged as a matter of policy — consistent with standard practice for most European industrials; management accepts translation volatility as a structural feature of an internationally diversified business
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on translation is that the 2022-2023 FX tailwind has created a high reported earnings base that will require Bufab to grow organically into; if SEK strengthens further toward its historical average (EUR/SEK ~10.0-10.5), Bufab faces a potential -5 to -10% cumulative translation headwind on SEK revenues vs the 2023 peak; this is not a binary event but a gradual multi-year compression that would mask organic growth in reported SEK numbers; the risk is that consensus revenue and EBITDA growth estimates are partially implicitly embedded with a weak-SEK assumption that may not persist

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is real and underappreciated; the EUR-revenue/CNY-COGS mismatch is the primary channel; Bufab can pass through cost inflation with a lag but not instantaneously

Transaction Exposure — Analytical Bullets

  • Primary transaction exposure point: Bufab's procurement function buys in CNY/USD (Asian suppliers) and sells in EUR/GBP/PLN/SEK to European customers; the buy-sell currency mismatch is the core transaction exposure; this is separate from and in addition to translation
  • Secondary transaction exposure: Some intercompany flows — group treasury lends/borrows across entities in different currencies; these create short-term transaction positions on intercompany settlements
  • Magnitude vs translation: Transaction exposure is structurally smaller than translation for Bufab because a significant portion of COGS procurement is contracted on an annual/semi-annual basis (hedgeable forward window); translation is continuous and unhedged; RESEARCHER estimates transaction exposure creates annual margin variability of ±50-150bps on EBITA margin (vs ±200-400bps from translation) — translation remains the larger channel
  • Pricing pass-through — the key mitigant: Bufab operates a distributor/service model where pricing to customers can be adjusted, typically annually or on contract renewal; management commentary across multiple years emphasises ability to pass through input cost changes (including FX-driven procurement cost inflation) to customers
  • Sub-bullet: Pass-through is not instantaneous; estimated lag of 3-9 months depending on contract structure; fixed-price annual contracts (common in industrial C-parts) mean Bufab absorbs FX-driven cost changes for the duration of the contract before repricing
  • Sub-bullet: In the 2022 inflation/supply-chain episode, Bufab demonstrated reasonable pricing power — EBITA margins held reasonably well despite significant input cost pressures; this is indirect evidence that pass-through works but with a delay
  • CNY/USD procurement dynamic — hedgeable: The majority of Asian procurement is contracted on a 6-12 month basis with Asian suppliers; Bufab can use forward FX contracts to lock in the USD/SEK or EUR/USD rate for known procurement commitments, reducing transactional exposure on the known order book
  • Residual transaction exposure — spot/unhedged tail: Spot procurement (expedited orders, supplier diversification purchases) is exposed to current rates; estimated at 15-25% of total procurement volume; this is the unhedged tail
  • Transaction vs translation in a shock scenario: In a rapid EUR/CNY appreciation (EUR strengthens vs CNY — favourable for Bufab's buy-side), Bufab benefits economically within months; in a rapid EUR/CNY depreciation (EUR weakens — CNY stronger), Bufab faces a procurement cost squeeze that begins hitting P&L within 3-6 months; translation, by contrast, affects the SEK-reported top line immediately upon rate change
  • Intercompany transaction hedging: Group treasury manages intercompany currency positions centrally; the 2024 Annual Report notes centralised FX management at group level, consistent with treasury netting intercompany exposures before hedging net positions — a standard, efficient practice
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on transaction exposure is that the apparent pass-through pricing capability breaks down in demand-constrained environments; if European industrial end-markets (automotive, machinery) are contracting (which they were in 2023-2024), Bufab faces pushback on price increases even when FX-driven cost inflation is real; the 2023-2024 European industrial slowdown was precisely such a scenario — pricing power degrades when customers can defer orders, switch suppliers, or renegotiate; in a simultaneous CNY appreciation + European demand contraction, the transaction exposure mechanism would fire adversely with limited pass-through offset, creating a margin double-squeeze that the static sensitivity table would understate

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Bufab's disclosed hedging policy is partial, transaction-only, and short-duration; the policy is transparent in its intent but leaves substantial FX risk unhedged, particularly translation and longer-horizon procurement commitments

Hedging Policy — Analytical Bullets

  • Hedging mandate — disclosed policy: The 2024 Annual Report (Financial Risk Management section) states that Bufab hedges transaction exposure for "significant foreign currency flows" with forward exchange contracts; no formal hedge ratio is disclosed; translation exposure is explicitly not hedged; this is the standard policy architecture for a European industrial of this size
  • Hedge horizon — short: RESEARCHER infers from standard language and the nature of the procurement cycle that Bufab's hedge horizon is approximately 3-6 months rolling; no explicit 12M+ hedge book is disclosed; this is consistent with the procurement contracting cycle (6-12 month supplier contracts) but falls short of best-practice 12-24M laddering
  • Sub-bullet: Absence of explicit hedge-horizon disclosure is itself a transparency signal; better-governed peers (e.g. Bossard, whose hedging is more explicitly disclosed) name their horizon; Bufab's non-disclosure is a mild negative vs sector best practice
  • Hedge instruments — forwards only: The Annual Report notes use of forward exchange contracts; no option collars or exotic instruments disclosed; forwards are the most vanilla instrument — low cost, no optionality, fully commits the rate; appropriate for a business with predictable procurement flows but offers no upside participation if FX moves favourably
  • Translation hedging — deliberately absent: Management does not hedge translation exposure; this is stated policy, not an oversight; consistent with Bufab's philosophy that translation is a non-cash consolidation effect accepted as structural; this is the norm for European industrials but means the P&L can be materially affected by SEK strengthening
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y — not directly disclosed: Bufab does not publish hedge effectiveness ratios in the Annual Report; IFRS 9 hedge accounting disclosures are present but at a high level; realised gains/losses on derivative instruments are disclosed in the financial statements notes
  • Sub-bullet: From the 2024 financial statements, derivative fair value positions are modest (typically SEK 5-50m range across instruments) — consistent with a short-duration, partial-coverage programme; not consistent with a comprehensive long-horizon hedging strategy
  • Sub-bullet: The 2022 episode (sharp SEK weakening) saw derivative fair-value positions move to net-liability status — a signal that Bufab had some short-SEK/long-EUR forwards that became unfavourable as SEK weakened more than anticipated; this is a small indicator of pro-cyclical hedging impulse (hedging ahead of the wrong-direction move)
  • Hedge cost — negligible in absolute terms: Forward contracts for a business with revenues in EUR/USD/GBP vs a SEK base incur a cost of carry reflecting interest rate differentials; with Riksbank rates and ECB rates converging somewhat in 2024-2025, the carry cost of SEK-selling forwards has been modest; hedge cost is not a material earnings drag
  • Board mandate and governance: The 2024 Annual Report references a Financial Risk Policy approved by the Board; this Policy governs FX hedging among other financial risks; existence of a Board-approved policy is a positive governance signal; however, without detailed disclosure of hedge ratio, horizon, and effectiveness, the policy's rigour cannot be fully assessed from public filings
  • Counter-cyclical vs pro-cyclical hedging assessment: RESEARCHER's reading of the 2022 derivative position (see sub-bullet above) suggests modest pro-cyclical tendency; no evidence of systematic counter-cyclical hedging programme (i.e. no disclosure of increasing hedge ratios when FX is cheap/stable); rated as neutral-to-slightly-pro-cyclical on this dimension
  • Natural vs financial hedging balance: Bufab's most structurally important FX risk (EUR revenue vs CNY COGS) is not addressable via financial hedging in a commercially practical way — CNY/SEK or EUR/CNY forwards exist but are less liquid for multi-month horizons; the natural hedge for this exposure is operational (price pass-through, supplier renegotiation, supplier diversification away from China); RESEARCHER rates natural hedging as Bufab's primary defence for CNY exposure, and financial hedging as the primary tool for EUR/USD/GBP transaction flows
  • Transparency rating vs sector: Bufab discloses sufficient information to confirm a hedging programme exists and is transaction-focused; however, it does not disclose hedge ratios, nominal hedge positions by currency, or hedge effectiveness ratios quantitatively; rated below-median on the "boring hedger" transparency spectrum — adequate but not gold-standard
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on hedging discipline is threefold: (1) a 3-6M horizon leaves multi-year procurement commitments and longer-contract customer pricing entirely unhedged beyond six months; (2) the absence of a named hedge ratio means investors cannot independently verify whether the programme covers 30% or 90% of transaction exposure — the policy is disclosed but the quantum is not; (3) if the Riksbank raises rates relative to the ECB (unlikely in current macro but not impossible), the carry cost of holding EUR/SEK forwards would increase, potentially causing management to reduce hedge ratios for cost reasons, increasing exposure precisely when macro volatility is elevated

§6 — Quantified Sensitivity Analysis

J-front verdict: EUR/SEK is the dominant sensitivity — a 10% EUR/SEK move drives ~4-6% EBITA impact; CNY/EUR (procurement) is the structural underappreciated risk; combined stress scenarios produce non-linear margin effects

Sensitivity Analysis — Analytical Bullets

Methodology note: In the absence of Bufab publishing a formal currency sensitivity table, RESEARCHER derives sensitivities from: (a) revenue mix estimates from §1, (b) EBITA margin (~13-14% on FY2024 revenues of ~SEK 9.0bn ≈ EBITA ~SEK 1.2-1.3bn), (c) cost-currency structure from §2, (d) management commentary, (e) IFRS 9 derivative disclosures. All figures are RESEARCHER estimates — not management-published sensitivity tables.

Key anchor metrics used:
- FY2024 Revenue: ~SEK 9.0bn (est.)
- FY2024 EBITA: ~SEK 1.25bn (est., ~14% margin)
- FY2024 EPS (diluted): ~SEK 13-14 (est.)

  • EUR/SEK sensitivity (most material): EUR represents ~40% of revenues; EBITA translation sensitivity is approximately 1:1 with revenue translation; a 10% appreciation of EUR vs SEK (i.e. SEK weakening, tailwind for Bufab) adds ~SEK 360-450m to reported revenue and ~SEK 50-60m to EBITA (through pure translation); a 10% depreciation of EUR (SEK strengthening) removes the equivalent, representing ~4-5% of EBITA
  • Sub-bullet: The transaction component of EUR/SEK sensitivity is smaller — Bufab's European procurement and opex are also EUR-denominated, creating partial natural hedge; net transaction sensitivity for EUR/SEK is estimated ±1-2% of EBITA per 10% move

  • Sub-bullet: Combined translation + transaction: ~5-7% EBITA impact per 10% EUR/SEK move

  • USD/SEK sensitivity: USD revenues ~13% of group; USD also embedded in procurement costs (~20% of COGS); net long USD position after procurement offsets is approximately 3-5% of revenue equivalent; USD/SEK 10% move → ~1.5-2.5% EBITA impact (translation + transaction combined)

  • Sub-bullet: The USD exposure is partially self-hedging because USD is both a revenue currency (Americas) and a procurement cost currency (Asian supplier invoicing in USD); a USD strengthening vs SEK raises USD-denominated COGS simultaneously with USD revenues, limiting the net P&L impact

  • CNY/EUR sensitivity (procurement mismatch — most structurally underappreciated): CNY represents ~45% of COGS against negligible CNY revenues; if EUR/CNY falls 10% (EUR weakens vs CNY, i.e. CNY appreciates against EUR), Bufab's effective procurement cost in EUR terms rises ~10% on ~45% of COGS; with COGS ~55% of revenues, a 10% CNY appreciation vs EUR = ~2.5% uplift in EUR-denominated COGS, translating to approximately 140-180bps EBITA margin compression before any pricing pass-through

  • Sub-bullet: Pricing pass-through recovers 50-80% of this over 6-12 months (based on historical evidence), but the initial hit is immediate; the unrecovered component (20-50%) represents a permanent margin step-down until the next pricing cycle

  • GBP/SEK sensitivity: GBP revenues ~7%, GBP costs ~4%; net long GBP ~3% of revenues; GBP/SEK 10% move → ~0.5-1% EBITA impact; relatively immaterial

  • PLN/EUR and CZK/EUR tail: Combined PLN+CZK revenues ~10% of group; these are EUR-correlated but not identical; in a CEE stress scenario (e.g. geopolitical shock causing PLN/EUR devaluation), Bufab would face a revenue headwind not captured in the EUR sensitivity; estimated ±0.5-1.5% EBITA per 10% PLN or CZK move vs EUR — individually minor, cumulatively meaningful in a CEE correlation shock
