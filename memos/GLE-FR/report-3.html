Section 3 — Base Formation Analysis

Seven Bases: What the Count Means

Mark Minervini's framework considers 3-4 bases as the typical productive life of a Stage 2 advance. After the 3rd or 4th base, the probability of failure on the next breakout rises significantly. By the 5th base, the advance is late stage. By the 7th base, you are watching a stock that has been in a sustained uptrend for so long that institutional profit-taking is likely well advanced.

GLE has completed 7 bases. This is the single most important number in this memo.

The counter-argument is that base #3 (22.7% depth, French political crisis) was severe enough to function as a "reset." A correction of that magnitude shakes out most of the weak hands and can reset the demand-supply dynamics. If you count from base #3 as the effective restart, bases #4-7 plus the current pullback give you 4-5 episodes — still late, but not as extreme as a raw count of 7 suggests.

The resolution depends on your framework. If you take the raw count, the stock is extremely late stage and the risk budget for a new position should be minimal. If you grant the reset, it is still late stage but perhaps one more base away from the danger zone rather than deep inside it.

Individual Base Character
