EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 3 findings

This comprehensive management quality investigation of Teqnion AB (TEQ.SE) delivers a high plausibility verdict on CEO Johan Steene's operator capabilities, validated by his twenty-year entrepreneurial track record and proven turnaround pedigree. Steene demonstrates a strong match for the Animal-CEO archetype across force, agency, dynamism, capital allocation, communication, and outlier performance standards. The operational thesis is supported by a remarkable 106% year-on-year EBITA rebound in Q1 2026, proving his hands-on execution discipline following severe 2024 setbacks. However, the assessment identifies critical structural vulnerabilities including concentrated key-person dependency on the core acquisition duo and recent integration failures that generated a SEK 73 million goodwill impairment.

1.
Governance Robustness and Structural Controls: Teqnion maintains a seven-member board with 85.71% independent directors (six of seven), and five directors are independent of major shareholders, protecting minority investor interests. The Audit Committee is composed entirely of independent directors with Helena Nathhorst serving as chair, leveraging her background as former CFO of Addnode Group. The board demonstrates strong expertise with Chair Lena Almefelt bringing extensive corporate legal experience as former General Counsel at EQT, and Henrik Joelsson contributing private equity experience as former Investment Director at Ratos. Teqnion maintains a single-share class structure with equal voting rights of one vote per share across all 17,165,756 shares, eliminating voting power concentration common among dual-class Nordic serial acquirers. However, the governance framework reveals critical weaknesses as Teqnion operates without a formal internal control function, relying on an informal non-audited matrix, and the board does not engage auditors to review quarterly reports, increasing the risk of delayed impairment detection. Independent directors Lena Almefelt and Henrik Joelsson hold very small positions of 3,000 and 2,750 shares respectively, while directors Helena Nathhorst and Boel Sjöstrand own zero shares, falling below peer-group alignment averages
2.
Insider Transaction Signals and Conviction Evidence: Insider ownership demonstrates exceptional executive alignment with CEO Johan Steene holding 861,471 shares representing 5.02% of outstanding capital and Deputy CEO Daniel Zhang holding 108,000 shares with zero option holdings. Board member Christopher Mayer holds 769,000 shares representing 4.48% of company capital, while Mikael Vaezi holds 16,202 personal shares representing Spiltan's substantial 11.41% anchor holding. Chief Controlling Officer Jonathan Alexandersson accumulated shares from 850 shares in October 2025 to 2,250 shares by March 2026, demonstrating rising confidence. Critically, no board members or executives executed any open market sales during the last 12 months, with insider activity characterized by accumulation and holding patterns rather than profit-taking. The Warrant Program 2025/2028 features 55,500 outstanding warrants among eighteen participants with a high subscription price of 255.68 SEK per share, requiring a 160% premium over the April 2025 average price. These transaction patterns validate top-quartile skin-in-the-game commitment from the founding management team and anchor institutional shareholders
3.
Single Largest Credibility Risk Identified: The single largest credibility risk is the lack of formal internal control audits, which conflicts with claims of managing a robust risk-managed corporate structure, as Teqnion operates without a formal internal control function and relies on an informal non-audited matrix. This structural weakness was exposed by the liquidation of Reward Catering and a SEK 73 million goodwill impairment in Q3 2025 following fraudulent activities that challenged Steene's underwriting quality and pre-acquisition due diligence processes. Compounding this governance gap is the extreme key-person dependency as CEO Johan Steene and Deputy CEO Daniel Zhang execute 90% of Teqnion's proprietary acquisition deals, with the parent company's central staff of only 9 employees creating a sourcing bottleneck where if either key executive departs, the proprietary broker-free deal sourcing pipeline could immediately collapse. Excessive delegation and lack of audit scaffolding may blind the board to localized financial irregularities within far-flung acquisitions, creating asymmetric downside risk as international complexity accelerates across the UK and Irish portfolio expansion
