Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Tele2 AB is a converged fixed and mobile telecommunications operator headquartered in Sweden, serving consumer and enterprise customers across Sweden and the Baltic states (Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania)
2.
Revenue model. The group earns subscription-based mobile and fixed service revenue, equipment sales, enterprise IoT connectivity and a growing digital-television and cloud-services layer, on total FY2025 sales of SEK 29.89 billion
3.
Financial character. FY2025 underlying EBITDAaL grew 11.4% with margin expansion of 340 basis points to 39.40%, and Q1 2026 equity free cash flow reached SEK 2,171m, beating consensus by 43.4%
4.
Balance sheet. Economic net debt leverage fell to 1.5x underlying EBITDAaL (from 2.4x) after the Baltic tower carve-out generated SEK 4.7bn (EUR 430m) of proceeds in Q1 2026
5.
Segment mix. Swedish consumer mobile and fixed broadband form the core, alongside Baltic converged operations, Swedish enterprise IoT connectivity, a nascent B2B sovereign cloud offering (with Scaleway) and a digital-television unit (Tele2 TV)
6.
Geography. Five core geographies (Sweden plus three Baltic states, plus a pan-Nordic peer-comparison footprint), a narrower footprint than several European peers in this cohort
7.
Ownership. Freya Investissement, the Xavier Niel and iliad Group vehicle, holds 22% of capital and 29% of votes following its February 2024 acquisition of Kinnevik's stake, and has driven an active operational agenda since
8.
Investment-style read. The case reads as a self-help cost transformation compounding into a valuation re-rating, not a classic secular growth compounder, given the 15% workforce reduction underpinning most of the recent margin delivery
9.
Leadership context. Jean Marc Harion steps down as chief executive on 30 June 2026; Nicholas Högberg, previously chief executive of Tre Sweden for nine years, becomes President and chief executive from 1 July 2026

Peer positioning. Tele2 holds the top overall momentum rank of 1 among named Nordic peers (Telia rank 2, Elisa rank 3, Telenor rank 4, Vodafone rank 5) on the source's four-dimensional fundamental-momentum grid
