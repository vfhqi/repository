§2 — Key Driver: FTTH / Fibre Monetisation (KD-1)

J-front: FTTH rollout is the most credible bull-case driver; homes-passed metrics ahead of plan, but ARPU uplift per-subscriber remains the unproven step-change that determines whether this driver is truly fulcrum [#J]

  • Driver definition: Copper-to-fibre migration drives broadband ARPU expansion from ~€18-20 (ADSL/VDSL baseline) toward €25-28 (FTTH premium tiers) in Greece domestic market over 24-36 months [#A]
  • Magnitude required: ~25-35% ARPU uplift on migrated subscribers, applied to a migrated base of ~600k-800k broadband RGUs by end-2026, to deliver meaningful EBITDA contribution (~€50-80m incremental) [#A]
  • Baseline — current state: OTE reported FTTH homes-passed ~3.8m at end-2024; broadband RGU base ~2.1m Greece; attach rate (FTTH active / homes-passed) approximately 30-32% [#A]
  • Target state: Attach rate 38-42% by end-2026; ARPU blended uplift of €4-6/month per migrated RGU; incremental EBITDA contribution €50-80m over 24M [#A]
  • Required trajectory: ~180-220k net new FTTH activations per year; ARPU step-up of ~€1.50-2.00 per migrated sub per year; consistent with ~15-20% annual fibre-base growth [#A]

Bull case:

- Bull — recent: Q3-Q4 2024 earnings calls confirmed broadband ARPU holding flat-to-modestly-positive despite ADSL erosion; suggests migration is absorbing legacy price-mix headwind [#A]
