Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Technoprobe occupies a highly profitable niche in the semiconductor value chain by dominating vertical MEMS probe testing.

Business Model and Product Segments

1.
Wafer-level testing specialization: Vertical MEMS probe cards are electromechanical interfaces used to test chip functionality at wafer sort before they are assembled into packaged systems
2.
Consumable economics: Probe cards operate as customized consumables because their lifecycle is linked to a specific chip design, meaning they cannot be reused across product generations
3.
MEMS segment leadership: Technoprobe holds over 60.00% global market share in vertical MEMS probe cards, which is the fastest-growing and most complex segment of the market
4.
Back-end final test expansion: Acquisitions of DIS Tech and Harbor Electronics expanded the company's product offering into final test interface boards and high-performance PCB manufacturing
5.
Vertical integration strategy: In-house manufacturing of PCBs and multi-layer organic substrates enhances manufacturing margins and protects technical know-how from competitive replication
6.
TPEG proprietary technology: The proprietary TPEG probe technology, enhanced by the 2019 Microfabrica acquisition, serves as the core performance driver for advanced pitch testing
7.
Customization focus: Every probe card requires a fully customized design based on specific chip layouts and pin counts, preventing commoditization of the product line

Customer Concentration and Competitive Moat

8.
TSMC dependency: Over 70.00% of direct and indirect logic sales are concentrated at TSMC, creating a key structural vulnerability to foundry allocation shifts
9.
Sole Nvidia supplier: Technoprobe acts as the sole supplier of vertical MEMS probe cards for Nvidia's AI GPUs, securing an near-100.00% market share at Nvidia
10.
Technical duopoly structure: The advanced logic probe card market is a rational duopoly shared with primary US-listed peer FormFactor, with the top three players controlling 70.00% of total share
11.
Technical switching costs: Transitioning to alternative probe card vendors requires lengthy qualification cycles and custom test re-engineering, resulting in high customer stickiness
12.
MEMS capital barriers: High capital investment barriers and complex customer evaluation processes prevent low-end competitors from entering advanced logic testing
13.
Joint developer alignment: Strategic partnerships with automatic test equipment duopolists Teradyne and Advantest secure early visibility into next-generation chip roadmaps
14.
Global customer support: Post-sales service networks located close to major semiconductor foundries secure high customer retention and rapid hardware maintenance
15.
Other side of the story: FormFactor could successfully qualify its Apollo MEMS cards at key customers like Nvidia or TSMC, eroding Technoprobe's logic market share if competitive dynamics shift in favor of the US peer

Section 5 – The possible investment case 16 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is strengthened by a structural inflection in test intensity driven by AI packaging complexity.

Logic and Custom ASIC Core Drivers

1.
Surging test intensity: Next-generation Blackwell and Rubin GPUs require extremely complex wafer probing, yielding a 15-to-20x step-up in test intensity per die compared to standard mobile SoCs
2.
Custom ASIC transition: Hyperscalers are migrating in-house AI ASICs to vertical MEMS, driving Technoprobe's projected ASIC sales to €318.00m by 2028
3.
WinWay low-end partnership: The WinWay partnership adds scalable exposure to lower-end ASIC markets where MPI currently dominates
4.
Server CPU recovery: Server CPU probe card revenues are projected to recover to €172.00m by 2028, driven by market share gains at Intel and AMD
5.
Extreme power thermals: Elevated chip thermal design power of up to 1400W drives the adoption of advanced thermal-control probe cards to prevent thermal runaways
6.
Active co-development programs: Joint R&D with Advantest and Teradyne addresses signal integrity constraints, locking in future test sockets
7.
Unprecedented logic demand: Broad adoption of chiplets increases testing demands by up to 50.00%, raising the need for known good dies before heterogeneous assembly

HBM and Silicon Photonics Options

8.
Custom HBM transition: DRAM manufacturers are migrating to vertical MEMS probe cards for pre-stack testing in custom HBM4E and HBM5
9.
HBM sales projections: Memory-related revenues are projected to scale from zero to €250.00m by 2028 upon successful qualification
10.
Silicon Photonics insertion: Co-packaged optics test demands drive the adoption of the optical-electrical Eclipse probe card, targeting €110.00m by 2028
11.
Active alignment edge: Technoprobe's active-alignment Eclipse technology provides a superior margin of error compared to competitor passive solutions
12.
Teradyne Photon100 partner: The Eclipse probe card has been qualified on Teradyne's Photon100 platform in collaboration with Marvell
13.
Ahead-of-schedule capacity: The Italian fab expansion is progressing ahead of schedule, facilitating an unconstrained volume ramp to double capacity by Q1 2027
14.
Independent driver profiles: The core investment drivers of logic GPUs, custom ASICs, and HBM are uncorrelated, providing multiple paths to success
15.
Other side of the story: If custom HBM DRAM manufacturers successfully extend cantilever probe card life, Technoprobe's memory revenues might materialize much slower than expected over the next 18 months
