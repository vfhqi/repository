Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Strategic diversification and restructuring are successfully transforming DEUTZ from a cyclical engine manufacturer into a high-margin, resilient energy and service provider.
1.
Core mechanics: DEUTZ generates revenue by selling specialized off-highway combustion engines to global manufacturers and providing highly profitable aftermarket maintenance services
2.
Investment thesis: The primary opportunity lies in accelerated earnings growth as high-margin service revenues expand and structural defense tailwinds take hold, which effectively buffers the cyclical engine business
3.
EPS swing factor: The single most critical driver for the forward 18-month EPS trajectory is the execution speed of the Future Fit cost-cutting program alongside the integration of Frerk Aggregatebau
4.
Historical backdrop: Revenues rose from €1,617.3 million in 2021 to a peak of €2,063.2 million in 2023, showing solid through-the-cycle resilience despite severe cyclical headwinds
5.
Critical risk: The investment case would break if severe supply bottlenecks or geopolitical tariffs in critical rare earth markets disrupt factory production or prevent price pass-through
6.
Initial verdict: A closer look is highly warranted because of a massive margin expansion to 7.0% in Q1 2026 and robust cash generation from restructuring initiatives

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. DEUTZ occupies a strategic, non-captive position in the off-highway value chain, moving rapidly from pure hardware production to high-margin life-cycle services and critical backup power.

Business Segments and Product Mix

1.
Classic Engines segment: Designs and produces off-highway diesel and gas engines ranging up to 620 kW for industrial, construction, and agricultural machinery
2.
Daimler IP agreement: Under a structural partnership with Daimler Truck, DEUTZ acquired medium-duty engine assets and heavy-duty IP rights to drive market consolidation
3.
John Deere partnership: Cooperative engineering highlighted by the joint development of the DEUTZ TCD 3.9 engine with John Deere, expanding its mid-power range
4.
Decentralized Energy business: The Energy unit provides emergency backup power and hybrid energy solutions, highlighted by genset manufacturer Blue Star Power Systems
5.
Global Service network: Spare parts, maintenance, and digital services across 1,250 locations, operating as a high-margin and highly stable cash generator
6.
Defense sector entry: Defense unit serves specialized propulsion systems and mobile military energy solutions, including drone propulsion packages
7.
Alternative NewTech drives: NewTech focuses on hydrogen combustion engines, electric drive systems, and battery management, though high research expenses continue to depress margins

Value Chain Position and Customer Dynamics

8.
Non-captive positioning: DEUTZ operates as an independent engine manufacturer, supplying OEMs that choose to purchase engines rather than develop proprietary powertrains
9.
Core clients: Include leading global construction and material handling OEMs such as JLG, Terex, Volvo, Atlas Copco, and Liebherr
10.
Switching costs moat: High collaborative engineering requirements create significant switching costs, as integrating specialized off-highway engines into machinery requires years of joint development
11.
Consolidating structure: DEUTZ aims to leverage structural partnerships to become a top-three independent drive producer by 2030
12.
Other side of the story: Alternative drive adoption presents a severe risk of vertical disintegration, as transitioning to electric drivetrains can reduce DEUTZ's proprietary component value-add

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Strong operational resilience during the cyclical trough of 2024 has paved the way for a sharp profitability rebound in early 2026, driven by aggressive cost-out programs.

Financial Trajectory and Profitability

1.
Structural margin expansion: Adjusted EBIT margin rose to 5.5% in 2025 from 4.2% in 2024, showing strong cost control
2.
Earnings quality: Profitability increasingly insulated from the core cycle by the resilient and growing service business, which generated €556.7 million in revenue in 2025
3.
Cash flow recovery: Free cash flow improved from minus €204.5 million in 9M 2024 to negative €119.5 million in FY 2025
4.
Sufficient liquidity: Solvency backed by a €250 million syndicated revolving credit facility running until May 2029

Recent Results and Guidance History

5.
Q1 2026 outperformance: Adjusted EBIT margin jump to 7.0% on revenue of €530.0 million in Q1 2026
6.
Order momentum: Q1 2026 new orders advanced by 41.2% year-on-year to €771.0 million, reflecting strong organic demand and Frerk Aggregatebau integration
7.
Guidance accuracy: Management delivered 4.2% adjusted EBIT margin in 2024 against its revised 4.0% to 5.0% guidance range
8.
Forward projections: Management guided 2026 revenue to €2.3-€2.5 billion and adjusted EBIT margin to 6.5%-8.0%
9.
Other side of the story: Underproduction and shift reductions at the Cologne site can severely impact fixed cost absorption, dragging down segment margins rapidly if volume falls

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case looks exceptionally strong due to a structural pivot toward higher-margin services and decentralized energy, which structurally reduces historical cyclicality.

Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
Future Fit savings: The structural cost-cutting program has exceeded its original target of €50 million by 10%, with over €40 million of savings coming from the Engines segment alone
2.
High-margin Service expansion: The Service segment targets over €1.0 billion in revenue by 2030 through multibrand capabilities, expanding its share of stable, recurring earnings
3.
Genset energy exposure: The acquisition of Frerk Aggregatebau in February 2026, which contributed around €145 million to new orders, secures high-power emergency backup exposure in data centers
4.
Defense sector penetration: Targeted investments in ARX Robotics and drone propulsion package orders for the German army establish DEUTZ in a highly lucrative, structural growth market
5.
Classic consolidation: Structural partnerships with Daimler Truck heavy-duty engine IP starting in 2028 will consolidate market share and reduce future R&D capital expenditure

Plausibility and Transmission Risks

6.
High plausibility: The Engines segment returned to profitability and Q1 2026 adjusted EBIT jumped to €37.3 million
7.
Independent driver probability: The core drivers operate independently – a slow transition to electric drives does not jeopardize the highly profitable service or energy growth
8.
Bull case criteria: DEUTZ must sustain its 7.0% adjusted EBIT margin while capturing a cyclical recovery in European agricultural machinery
9.
Other side of the story: A prolonged downturn in key European agricultural and construction end markets could trigger extreme pricing pressure from competitors, offsetting the benefits of cost savings

Section 6 – Valuation 5 findings

Section summary
  1. DEUTZ shares trade at a highly attractive discount to global peers like Cummins, failing to reflect the structural shift toward recurring service and data center power.
1.
Relative peer pricing: DEUTZ historically trades at c. 7.00x P/E, representing a massive discount to global peer Cummins which trades at c. 13.00x P/E
2.
Sell-side upside: Current price targets suggest substantial upside, with Oddo BHF targeting €12.60 and Warburg Research targeting €11.50
3.
Market mispricing: The equity market appears to price DEUTZ as a highly volatile, pure-play engine cyclical, completely discounting its massive pivot into non-cyclical backup power
4.
Other side of the story: A sudden global economic contraction could cause the market to de-rate DEUTZ back to its historical recessionary multiple floor of c. 3.00x to 4.00x P/E

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. While structural risks around engine cyclicality remain, aggressive diversification into decentralized energy and defense tech significantly mitigates potential earnings volatility.

Strategic and Operational Headwinds

1.
Procurement security: Growing international trade disputes over rare earth metals represent a moderate supply limitation risk
2.
Under-absorption risk: A decline in classic engine production volumes severely impacts factory utilization, historically dragging Engines & Services segment margins from 7.8% to 6.1%
3.
Alternative technology drag: The NewTech alternative drive segment recorded adjusted EBIT losses of €34.1 million in 2025 due to intensive R&D
4.
Tariff repercussions: US tariff policies represent an external shock that could disrupt international trade and compress export margins
5.
Short interest: No major published short-seller campaigns or elevated short interest positions exist on DEUTZ in the public domain

Warning Signals and Moats

6.
Integration risk failure: A failure to integrate the Frerk Aggregatebau acquisition would severely damage the high-growth backup power expansion narrative
7.
False friend signal: Sharp short-term jumps in new orders can act as a false friend if they represent temporary customer pre-buy effects ahead of emissions changes
8.
Single critical metric: Investors must closely monitor capacity utilization rates at the core Cologne-Porz factory as a critical operational indicator
9.
Other side of the story: Bears overstate cyclical risks because the highly profitable global service network delivers extremely steady, recurring cash flows that insulate the consolidated operating profit

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership change is actively driving the transformation with a streamlined management structure and strong insider alignment via significant share ownership.
1.
Streamlined executive board: Effective January 2026, DEUTZ implemented a new divisional structure and eliminated the COO position, resulting in Petra Mayer stepping down
2.
Transformation leadership: Katharina Krüger will join the executive board as Chief Transformation Officer effective June 1, 2026
3.
Insider transaction alignment: Board members, including Dr. Sebastian Schulte and Oliver Neu, actively disclosed market purchases of DEUTZ shares throughout the 2025 financial year
4.
Strong shareholding status: CEO Sebastian Schulte holds 190% of his Share Ownership Guideline target with €1.28 million in shares, ensuring tight alignment with equity investors
