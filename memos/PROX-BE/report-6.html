RESEARCH PLAN SUMMARY

Proximus Group's sell-side landscape is currently dominated by a "wait-and-see" approach following the February 2026 Capital Markets Day , where the company halved its dividend to €0.30 per share and reset its medium-term targets. Analysts are primarily focused on the struggling Global division and the potential for organic Free Cash Flow to eventually cover shareholder returns after years of heavy infrastructure investment.

Segment-Specific Consensus Views

Proximus Global Turnaround

Analysts are sceptical of the immediate turnaround, viewing FY26 guidance (€100-130 million EBITDA) as reflective of continued headwinds in legacy voice and messaging. While management targets a return to growth in 2027 through a product mix shift (Cloud and Cyberdefence), analysts consider the outlook "volatile" and have flagged recent non-cash impairments of €275 million on Global goodwill.

Sources: Deutsche Bank Research 23 Feb; Deutsche Bank Research 27 Feb

Domestic Belgium and FTTH Monetisation

There is general belief in the Domestic segment's stability, but the monetisation of Fibre-to-the-Home is seen as a long-term play. Proximus has successfully migrated customers to newer, higher-value plans using 3% price increases on older bundles as a catalyst.

Sources: Deutsche Bank Research 28 Apr; Deutsche Bank Research 25 Jul 25; Deutsche Bank Research 4 Nov 25

However, fibre penetration in Belgium remains relatively low at c.11% (as of late 2025), with cable subscribers holding steady.

Sources: ING Wholesale Banking 16 Sep 25; ING Wholesale Banking 14 Oct 25

Digi Belgium Impact

The "Digi risk" is currently modeled as a low-end market threat that has been "muted" or "contained within mobile" thus far.

Source: Deutsche Bank Research 28 Apr

Fixed Impact: Analysts note Digi's very limited fixed network coverage (<100k homes passed) makes a near-term broadband impact difficult, though its aggressive pricing (€10/month for 500Mbps) remains a long-term competitive watch-point.

Source: Deutsche Bank Research 28 Jan 25

Mobile Impact: Initial porting to Digi slowed significantly after launch, with recent data showing Digi captured approximately 62k mobile customers by mid-2025, a level described by analysts as lagging expectations.

Source: Deutsche Bank Research 28 Apr 25
