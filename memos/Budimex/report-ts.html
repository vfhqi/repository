Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. Budimex dominates Polish public contracting through unmatched scale, operating a highly efficient, vertically integrated model that yields premium construction margins and predictable maintenance revenues

CORE SEGMENTS AND OPERATIONAL FOOTPRINT

1.
Dominant civil builder: Civil engineering and infrastructure construction represents the largest division, accounting for 44.00% of construction sales or PLN 3.79 billion in FY2025
2.
Expanding volume construction: Cubic and building structures surged to 35.20% of FY2025 construction revenues, up from 30.20% in 2024, showing strong commercial execution
3.
Specialist railway capability: The railway division represents a specialized end-market comprising 20.80% of construction sales in FY2025, which carries higher-margin potential
4.
Defensive services pillar: The services segment, anchored by FBSerwis SA, provides municipal waste management, road maintenance, and street lighting services, contributing PLN 921.00 million in FY2025 revenue
5.
Nascent green transition: Budimex is expanding into renewable energy, owning wind and solar projects alongside Budimex Mobility which plans over 460 EV charging stations by 2026
6.
Active international expansion: Budimex is scaling operations across Germany, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, and the Baltics, securing a massive EUR 190.00 million Rail Baltica contract in Latvia
7.
Strong municipal focus: FBSerwis operates five mechanical-biological waste treatment facilities and three landfills, securing highly predictable, long-term public cash flows
8.
Broad contract exposure: Budimex SA runs more than 200 construction contracts in parallel, while FBSerwis manages over 120 contracts, providing highly diversified revenue streams

VALUE CHAIN POSITIONING AND COMPETITIVE MOATS

9.
Favoured prime contractor: Budimex sits at the apex of the construction value chain, managing over 200 concurrent contracts as a general execution and financial management hub
10.
Unrivalled procurement scale: The centralized Procurement Department pools purchasing power to hedge against raw material price volatility, ensuring no single supplier exceeds 10.00% of sales
11.
Strong public client-base: The customer base is heavily concentrated in Polish state and municipal entities, such as the General Directorate for National Roads and Motorways and PKP PLK
12.
Formidable entry barriers: Peer PORR notes that scale and pre-qualification credentials act as massive entry barriers, effectively limiting large Polish transport tenders to Budimex and Strabag
13.
Strategic regulatory protection: Poland's alignment with EU procurement rules allows the exclusion of non-reciprocal third-country bidders, primarily protecting Budimex from aggressive Chinese and Turkish competition
14.
Integrated steel fabricator: Subsidiary Mostostal Krakow SA provides in-house steel fabrication, closing a critical bottleneck in the transport and industrial infrastructure chain
15.
Durable switching costs: Public customers face high switching costs as Budimex's flawless completion record protects municipalities from costly delays and bid protests
16.
High execution complexity: Budimex optimizes designs to lower subcontractor costs, maintaining superior bargaining power that ensures its profitability remains above the sector average
17.
Solid technical credentials: Mostostal Krakow has completed strategic Western European contracts, such as the Avicii Arena modernization in Sweden, proving high-end steel assembly competency
18.
Other side of the story: Intense bidding pressure can compress margins if competitors bid aggressively in periods of low public procurement volume, forcing Budimex to choose between backlog erosion or lower returns

ACCELERATING MARGINS AND CASH CONVERSION

6.
Zero debt service risks: Low gross debt of PLN 105.15 million means persisting high interest rates do not harm earnings via rising debt service costs
7.
Negative cash conversion cycle: Budimex achieves premium capital efficiency by utilizing large upfront prepayments and progress billings to fund its subcontractor cost base

10.
Decline in Q1 earnings: Q1 2026 net profit fell 20.97% to PLN 87.23 million, primarily due to lower interest income of PLN 17.60 million versus PLN 31.70 million in Q1 2025

14.

Positive road contracting share: Budimex won approximately 42.00% of all road sector contracts signed in Poland during 2024, proving its unmatched public bidding power

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Severe litigation exposures and material-cost inflation threaten to erode operating margins and drain Budimex's pristine PLN 2.90 billion cash cushion

LITIGATION EXPOSURE AND MACRO COST PRESSURES

1.
Unfavourable lawsuit exposure: Budimex face a massive PLN 1.05 billion PGE Turow consortium lawsuit over the Turow Power Plant project, where Budimex's share is 23.58% [#r]
2.
Material Silesian Museum dispute: A pending Silesian Museum lawsuit seeks PLN 122.76 million in solidum from Budimex and Ferrovial, threatening additional cash outflows
3.
Persistent minimum wage inflation: Poland's rapid minimum wage hikes and a 7.50% year-on-year increase in construction wages exert severe, permanent pressure on labor costs
4.
Renewed commodity price spikes: Geopolitical tensions, including conflicts in the Middle East, threaten to spark sudden spikes in fuel, asphalt, and steel costs
5.
Decline in order backlog: The group's construction order book fell 9.00% to PLN 16.16 billion in FY2025, indicating a temporary slowdown in contract signing before the next EU funding wave
6.
Critical warning signal: Investors must closely monitor monthly steel and asphalt index spot prices, as sudden cost spikes immediately erode fixed-price road contracts
7.
High public budget reliance: Over 80.00% of Budimex's revenue is tied to public procurement budgets, exposing the group to political and bureaucratic capital-expenditure delays
8.
Regulatory carbon penalties: The implementation of EU CBAM and carbon-neutral policies could escalate structural cost pressures for critical materials like cement and steel
9.
Other side of the story: The negative impact of material inflation is heavily mitigated by the fact that most of Budimex's backlog consists of relatively new contracts containing robust indexation clauses
