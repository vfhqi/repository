Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Almirall has built a leading European medical dermatology franchise supported by stable cash flows from its legacy general medicine portfolio

Core Product Portfolio and Segment Dynamics

1.
Biologics product mix: Almirall operates primarily through a product sales model, distributing proprietary and licensed skin health treatments directly to European wholesalers. These commercial activities generate recurring product sales across several therapeutic areas
2.
Advanced AtD biologic: Ebglyss is Almirall's novel anti-IL-13 biologic approved for moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis, which doubled sales to €41.90M in Q1 2026. The drug is now launched in major European markets including Germany, France, and Italy
3.
Entrenched psoriasis biologic: Ilumetri is Almirall's anti-IL-23 biologic for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis, which grew 11.80% YoY to €61.60M in Q1 2026. The drug continues to deliver double-digit growth and remains Almirall's largest single product
4.
Topical dermatological treatments: Topicals like Wynzora for mild-to-moderate psoriasis and Klisyri for actinic keratosis provide a comprehensive portfolio for dermatologists. These products allow Almirall to act as a one-stop shop for diverse skin conditions
5.
Licensing value chain: Almirall occupies a profit-favoured position in the value chain by in-licensing clinical-stage assets and leveraging its established sales network. The company focuses on European commercialization, avoiding the heavy capital requirements of early-stage global discovery
6.
Diversified customer base: Customer concentration is low as sales are distributed across diverse pharmaceutical wholesalers, hospitals, and retail pharmacies. This distribution model minimizes the credit and receivables risks associated with single-purchaser models

Competitive Advantages and Industry Structure

7.
Sticky patient retention: Patients on biologics like Ebglyss and Ilumetri exhibit high retention rates, creating durable and predictable revenue streams. The long-term efficacy and safety profiles of these biologics limit the risk of patient discontinuation
8.
Expansive commercial scale: Almirall's deep relationships with European dermatologists provide unmatched commercial distribution scale compared to smaller specialty pharmaceutical competitors. This commercial excellence enables Almirall to optimize launch timelines and secure national reimbursements
9.
High regulatory barriers: Biologic approvals and complex manufacturing processes protect Almirall's main growth drivers from rapid generic erosion. These barriers create a highly defensible franchise with long patent runways
10.
Growing market structure: The European medical dermatology market is expanding quickly, consolidated around a few high-efficacy systemic biologics. Biologics are increasingly favored over older oral systemic treatments due to superior efficacy
11.
Strategic family reference: The Gallardo family reference stake ensures long-term alignment and disciplined capital allocation strategies focused on dermatology. This corporate governance structure prevents short-term pressure from disrupting clinical timelines
12.
Other side of the story: Heavy competition from Sanofi's Dupixent and emerging oral JAK inhibitors could limit Ebglyss market share gain in Europe. If payors enforce strict step-edits prioritizing Dupixent, Ebglyss will be relegated to second-line therapy

Section summary

Primary Growth Drivers and Plausibility

1.
Accelerated Ebglyss rollout: Ebglyss sales are expected to expand to over €450.00M in Europe, carrying a high plausibility based on positive early uptake in Germany. The drug's monthly dosing regimen represents a key competitive advantage over Dupixent's bi-weekly schedule
2.
Entrenched tildrakizumab growth: Ilumetri continues to gain market share in the anti-IL-23 class, on track to achieve peak European sales of over €300.00M. The launch of the high-dose 200mg presentation provides significant flexibility for treating severe patients
3.
Scaled topical margins: Wynzora and Klisyri launches provide high-margin growth, capturing leading topical market shares in key regions. These topicals diversify Almirall's product portfolio and expand patient touchpoints
4.
Progressive operational leverage: Scaling of Ebglyss over Almirall's existing dermatology salesforce is projected to expand EBITDA margins to approximately 25.00% by 2028. This commercial leverage supports rapid margin accretion as sales scale

Fail-Safe Mechanisms and Case Failure Modes

5.
Strategic co-development protection: Almirall's financial exposure is protected by cost-sharing agreements with Eli Lilly and Sun Pharma, limiting R&D risk. These partnerships insulate Almirall's cash flows from severe commercialization shocks
6.
Interdependent bull scenario: The bull case requires both Ebglyss and Ilumetri to hit peak targets to offset legacy segment erosion. If one biologic underperforms, the EBITDA margin expansion trajectory will flatten
7.
Other side of the story: Aggressive competitor pricing or safety concerns in the atopic dermatitis class could severely compromise Ebglyss peak sales. If cheaper biosimilars flood the market, Almirall's premium pricing power will be eroded

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture remains manageable but real, driven by intense competition in atopic dermatitis and mature product erosion

Core Risks and Transmission Channels

1.
Psoriasis class competition: Biosimilar competition and established IL-23 inhibitors like Skyrizi could slow tildrakizumab's market share gain. This competitive pressure directly threatens Ilumetri's peak sales targets
2.
Mature general medicine decline: Persistent generic erosion of mature drugs like Efficib and Tesavel creates a drag on Almirall's net sales. This structural decline requires constant biologics growth to maintain positive overall net sales growth
3.
US legacy segment drag: Generic erosion of Tazorac and Seysara has reduced US sales, prompting Almirall to manage this segment for cash. The US segment represents a minimal portion of total revenue and provides limited upside
4.
Pipeline binary risk: Failure of phase II assets like the anti-IL-1RAP mAb in HS would limit mid-term growth optionality. Clinical delays would increase Almirall's dependence on licensed assets

Sceptical False Friends and Early Warnings

5.
False friend margin growth: Near-term gross margin improvements may be driven by one-off licensing upfronts rather than sustainable operational improvements. Investors must adjust for these one-offs to analyze Almirall's true underlying margin expansion
6.
Single early-warning signal: Investors must monitor quarterly Ebglyss sales trends in newly launched territories like France to gauge real-world adoption. Slow uptake in France would signal payor access challenges
7.
Other side of the story: Risks are likely overstated as Ebglyss possesses a pristine safety profile that matches standard of care Dupixent. This safety profile reduces clinical hurdles and supports rapid first-line use
