B.1 Business Description 8 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Global scale distributor. Brenntag is the world's largest chemical and ingredients distributor, holding roughly 5% of a highly fragmented ~EUR 4tn global chemicals market, per Business Foundation report [AS, 2026]
2.
Physical-asset moat. The group runs ~600 sites in 70+ countries, an asset-heavy logistics and repackaging footprint that is structurally different from asset-light specialty peers
3.
Two-division structure. Essentials (commodity chemicals, ~70% of group gross profit) and Specialties (formulation-led ingredients, ~30% of gross profit) share one legal and IT backbone after management cancelled the planned full separation in November 2025
4.
Financial scale, thin margins. FY2025 revenue EUR 15.17bn (-6.6% reported, -3.7% organic constant currency) at a 6.1% operating profit margin, roughly half the conversion-margin efficiency of Azelis and IMCD
5.
Extreme customer and supplier fragmentation. Over 160,000-180,000 customers (average order EUR 4,000) and thousands of suppliers insulate Brenntag from single-counterparty pricing power, per Business Foundation report
6.
New leadership, radically slimmed governance. CEO Jens Birgersson (from Sep-2025, ex-Rockwool) and CFO Thomas Reisten (from Apr-2025, ex-Vantage Towers) now run a two-member Management Board, down from four, with both divisional CEOs departed
7.
Cyclical, commodity-tilted style. Investment style is best read as a cyclical, event-driven turnaround candidate, not a GARP compounder; ~70% of gross profit sits in commodity-linked Essentials, making Brenntag structurally more cyclical than pure-play peers Azelis and IMCD
8.
Balance sheet is a relative strength. Net debt/EBITDA of 2.0-2.2x sits below both Azelis (3.3-3.4x) and IMCD (2.8x), a genuine structural positive that is easy to lose under the weight of the bear case
