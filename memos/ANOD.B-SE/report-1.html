§1 — General (Description, Financials, Peer Comparison)

Net signal: Addnode is a mid-cap Swedish B2B software group with SEK ~6bn revenue, recurring-revenue-weighted model, and M&A-driven growth — margins structurally capped below pure-play software peers, creating a dual-thesis tension [#J]

Description

  • Description: Addnode Group operates as a specialist software and services provider to the AEC (architecture, engineering, construction), PLM (product lifecycle management), and public-sector process management verticals [#I]
  • Core operations span three divisions: Design Management (AEC/CAD/BIM), Product Lifecycle Management (engineering software), and Process Management (digitisation of public-sector workflows)

  • Business model blends value-added reseller * of third-party software (Autodesk, Siemens, PTC, Trimble) with proprietary software** development and managed services

  • Organisation: Three reporting divisions with substantial operational autonomy under a lean holding company; 3,200+ employees across Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, UK, Germany, and the US [#I]

  • Design Management (~45% of revenue): CAD/BIM software resale + services, Autodesk-heavy, Nordic and UK focus
  • PLM (~30% of revenue): Siemens/PTC resale + proprietary PLM tools, manufacturing sector, broader European footprint

  • Process Management (~25% of revenue): proprietary software for public-sector workflows, highest-margin division

  • Strategic objective: Stated goal is to be "the leading Nordic software group" in its chosen verticals through organic growth plus disciplined M&A at 4-7x EBIT [#I]

  • Secondary objective: increase proprietary software share vs VAR revenue to improve margin profile — directionally confirmed by CMD 2022 commentary [AS-Company]

  • Listing: ANOD-B traded on Nasdaq Stockholm; SEK reporting; EUR functional relevance given ~30% non-SEK revenue [#I]

§2 — Company Details (Revenue Model, Business Model, Recent Delivery, Internal Change)

Net signal: Addnode's recurring revenue base (~75%) and embedded VAR relationships create meaningful predictability, but proprietary software is still sub-50% of group revenue — the key execution test is accelerating the shift toward higher-margin owned IP [#J]

Revenue Model

  • Revenue source: Three streams — (1) software licence/maintenance/SaaS subscriptions (~55% of revenue, ~75-80% gross margin on proprietary, ~20-25% on VAR), (2) professional services/implementation (~25%, ~30-35% gross margin), (3) managed services/hosting (~20%, ~40-45% gross margin) [#A]

  • Recurring share has increased from ~60% (2018) to ~75% (2023) — directionally right, slower than peer Vitec which targets 85%+ recurring [#A]

  • Geography: ~55% Sweden, ~20% Norway, ~10% UK, ~8% Germany, ~7% other [#I]

  • Nordic concentration creates FX exposure (SEK/NOK/DKK) and limits diversification but also insulates from more competitive US/global markets

  • UK footprint (Design Management/AEC) subject to post-Brexit procurement complexity — modest risk [#A]

  • End-market: AEC (~45%) is most cyclically sensitive to construction cycles; PLM (~30%) tied to manufacturing capex; Process Management (~25%) tied to public-sector IT budgets — least cyclical [#A]

  • Public sector (Process Management) provides a natural hedge against AEC/PLM downturns; COVID-2020 demonstrated resilience: group revenue grew +5% vs Nordic peers flat/negative

  • Cyclicality: AEC/PLM resale revenue has moderate cyclicality — construction slowdown 2023-2024 visible in Design Management organic growth deceleration (est. ~4% vs ~10% in 2022) [#A]

  • Recurring subscription/maintenance revenue is sticky even in downturns; project-based professional services is the cyclical tail

  • Predictability: High for recurring revenue (~85-90% annual retention on maintenance contracts per management); moderate for professional services; lower for initial licence sales [#A]

Business Model — Customer Side

  • Customer moat: AEC/PLM customers embed Addnode's services into multi-year procurement frameworks (especially Autodesk enterprise contracts intermediated via Addnode) — switching is costly due to re-training, workflow reconfiguration, and data migration [#A]

  • Process Management customers (Nordic municipalities, government agencies) face procurement regulation requiring tender cycles for switching — effectively a 2-4 year lock-in per contract [#A]

  • Switching cost: Addnode's proprietary software (CANEA, Symetri, Technia brands) is workflow-embedded; customers average 5-8 years of tenure on proprietary products per management commentary [AS-Company CMD 2022]

  • NPS/Churn: Management cites sub-5% annual churn on maintenance/subscription contracts; no independent third-party NPS data available at IG depth — ESA depth required [#I]

  • Customer behaviour: B2B purchasing — IT managers + procurement teams in AEC firms and municipal governments; long sales cycles (~6-12 months for new logo), short renewal cycles (~3 months); upsell via new module additions [#A]

Business Model — Scale Economies

  • Scale economy: Addnode's scale in Autodesk/Siemens VAR relationships provides volume pricing advantages unavailable to smaller Nordic resellers — licensing margin uplift estimated 2-4ppts vs subscale competitors [#A]

  • Process Management proprietary software: marginal cost of adding new municipal customers is low (~15-20% incremental delivery cost) — genuine software-like operating leverage as installed base grows

  • Operating leverage: Group EBIT margin has expanded ~100-150bps over 2019-2023 — modest relative to Vitec (+500bps over same period) but directionally consistent with increasing proprietary software mix [#A]

Recent Delivery (L3Y)

  • Recent delivery: FY2021-2023 delivered SEK 1.4bn revenue growth (~+37%), adj. EBIT grew from ~SEK 390m to ~SEK 630m — solid absolute delivery though margin expansion has been limited (10.5% vs 9.5% in 2020) [#A]

  • M&A delivery: 8 acquisitions completed FY2021-2023 at disclosed multiples of 4-7x EBIT — consistent with stated discipline; integration appears smooth (no goodwill impairments, minimal management turnover post-acquisition) [#A]

Internal Change (L12M and N3Y)

  • Strategic priority shift: CMD 2022 articulated explicit goal to increase proprietary software revenue to >50% of group (from ~42% in 2022) — if achieved, would structurally re-rate margins; execution against this KPI is the key forward watch item [#J]
