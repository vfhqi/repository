  • Sub: Electricity sold to grid is invoiced on metered output — one of the least subjective revenue recognition models in corporate accounting

  • IFRS project finance complexity: SLR-ES uses non-recourse project finance extensively; IFRS treatment of these vehicles (consolidation decisions, interest capitalization, IFRS 9 fair value) can be complex and is a legitimate audit-complexity concern [#A]

  • Sub: Non-recourse debt may be excluded from headline leverage metrics, creating a cleaner-than-actual balance sheet appearance to investors who do not look through to project-level debt

  • Sub: Industry standard practice but warrants scrutiny — European renewable IPP peers (Encavis, Capital Energy) use similar structures

  • Asset revaluation / fair value: Under IFRS, SLR-ES may carry assets at fair value or revalued amounts; in a falling power-price environment, impairment risk on operating assets increases [#A]

  • Sub: Bear concern: if Iberian pool prices remain structurally lower, DCF-based asset valuations embedded in balance sheet may overstate economic value

  • Capitalisation practices: Development costs for pipeline projects are capitalized on the balance sheet; if pipeline projects are cancelled or delayed, write-downs flow through P&L [#A]

  • Sub: Pipeline of several GW implies meaningful capitalised development costs; impairment risk is non-trivial if permitting or grid conditions deteriorate

  • Non-GAAP gap: SLR-ES does not appear to present heavily adjusted non-GAAP metrics; reported EBITDA is relatively close to cash generation metrics, which is positive for earnings quality [#A]

  • Sub: Compared to peer renewable developers who sometimes exclude significant items, SLR-ES's reporting is relatively clean

§6 — Counter-Arguments to Bull Case

  • Bull thesis #1 — Growth pipeline: SLR-ES will commission multi-GW of solar capacity over 2024-28, driving significant EBITDA and earnings growth [#J]
  • Bear rebuttal #1: Pipeline-to-commission conversion has historically run below guidance; Spanish permitting delays, grid connection queues, and equipment lead times have all caused COD slippage
  • Bear rebuttal #1b: Even commissioned capacity at lower realized power prices generates lower EBITDA than the bull model assumed; GW additions without price recovery is earnings-neutral at best

  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: Spain's REE (Red Eléctrica Española) has publicly acknowledged grid congestion and connection bottlenecks in priority solar zones; SLR-ES's historical COD additions have repeatedly lagged initial guidance

  • Bull thesis #2 — Power price recovery: Iberian pool prices will recover from 2023-24 lows as hydro normalises and demand grows [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #2: Iberian power price is structurally being compressed by Spain's own solar build-out — SLR-ES's own capacity additions, plus those of Iberdrola, Acciona, and dozens of international developers, increase supply and compress the marginal price
  • Bear rebuttal #2b: The "solar cannibalisation" problem is well-documented: solar-heavy generation mixes create price cannibalism at peak solar hours, structurally lowering realised prices for solar generators

  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: Spanish solar generation share has grown from <10% to >15% of electricity mix in 3 years; OMIE pool data shows mid-day price compression during high-solar hours even in high-demand seasons

  • Bull thesis #3 — PPA coverage provides earnings visibility: SLR-ES's PPA book de-risks revenue [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #3: SLR-ES's merchant exposure remains meaningful; the PPA book covers only a portion of output, leaving significant earnings at risk from pool price moves
  • Bear rebuttal #3b: New PPAs signed in a lower power price environment lock in lower revenues than legacy PPAs; PPA roll-over risk is a structural margin headwind

  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: Company disclosures show a mix of merchant and PPA; the merchant proportion has been higher than some comparable European IPPs (e.g., Neoen's higher contracted %)

  • Bull thesis #4 — Regulatory support for renewables in Spain: Spanish government policy is supportive of solar build-out [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #4: Spanish government has demonstrated willingness to intervene against renewable sector profits when prices are high — windfall taxes and CESUR-equivalent mechanisms have been deployed
  • Bear rebuttal #4b: Permitting and grid access rules have been tightened; while the intent is to build renewables, execution-level bureaucracy creates real COD delays

  • Macro evidence: Spain's 2022 windfall tax on energy companies (Decreto-ley 17/2022) captured a portion of extraordinary profits; precedent for intervention is established

  • Bull thesis #5 — Attractive valuation after de-rating: SLR-ES trades at a discount to 2021 highs and is cheap on a normalised earnings basis [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #5: The stock is only cheap if power prices normalise AND pipeline delivers AND multiples hold; all three conditions must be simultaneously true for bull returns
  • Bear rebuttal #5b: European renewable sector re-rating may be structural, not cyclical — higher discount rates, solar cannibalism, and policy risk have permanently reduced the sector's warranted multiple

  • Competitive evidence: Encavis was taken private at a 27% premium to spot in 2024 — this is evidence of private value but also suggests public market is unwilling to re-rate renewables to prior peak multiples organically

  • Bull thesis #6 — Management quality and track record: Founder-led management team has built SLR-ES from zero to a significant Spanish solar operator [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #6: The same founder-led management team has consistently set growth targets that have not been met on schedule; the track record since the 2021 peak is one of execution disappointment
  • Bear rebuttal #6b: Founder concentration creates succession and governance risk; the company's long-term institutional quality may be tied to one individual's health and vision

  • Track record evidence: Annual reports from 2021-24 show successive downward revisions to MW commissioning guidance

  • Bear rebuttal : The weakest bear rebuttal is #5b (permanent sector multiple compression) — if ECB rate cuts in 2025-26 reduce discount rates, renewable sector multiples could partially re-rate; the argument that the de-rating is structural rather than cyclical requires a strong confidence in "higher for longer" interest rates and persistent solar cannibalism, neither of which is settled

  • Downside scenario construction: Bear scenario requires three simultaneous conditions: (1) Iberian pool prices remain structurally depressed at €55-65/MWh average, (2) GW delivery runs 30-40% below stated pipeline plan, (3) market awards EV/EBITDA multiple of 8-10x vs current implied 12-14x [#A]

  • Sub: None of these three conditions is extreme or low-probability in isolation; the bear scenario is NOT a tail risk — it is a plausible central scenario

  • EPS downside — power price: A sustained €15/MWh downward shift in realised price versus bull assumption implies €15-25mn EBITDA reduction on current ~400-600MW operational base (rough estimate, verify with actual MW and price split) [#A]

  • Sub: Percentage impact: if EBITDA runs at €120-150mn (approximate current estimate range), a €20mn downside is 13-17% EBITDA compression — material but not catastrophic

  • EPS downside — pipeline slippage: If 2024-26 GW additions come in at 50% of guidance, EBITDA growth projections (often assuming 20-30% annual growth) collapse to 8-12% growth; EPS trajectory falls commensurately [#A]

  • Sub: This is the single most deceptive bear risk — the stock's multiple is priced for high growth; a growth disappointment not only reduces absolute EBITDA but triggers multiple de-rating (double-hit)

  • Valuation re-rate: If sector EV/EBITDA reverts from ~12x to 8x (the level that implies a utility-like, low-growth multiple), the stock experiences a 35-40% de-rating even on flat EBITDA; combined with EBITDA compression, the total downside could be 40-55% [#A]

  • Sub: Peer anchor: Orsted's trough multiple in 2023 was ~7-8x forward EBITDA; Encavis was taken private at ~12-13x; SLR-ES's warranted multiple in a bear scenario likely sits at 8-10x

  • Fair value downside quantified: Bear scenario fair value: if bear assumptions play out, SLR-ES could be worth €6-9 per share versus recent trading near €12-15 (approximate), implying 35-55% downside [#A]

  • Sub: This is NOT a forecasting exercise — it is a bear scenario stress-test; the actual downside depends on power prices, execution, and market sentiment

  • Margin compression: EBITDA margins in a bear scenario could compress from ~60-65% (current) to 50-55% as lower power prices hit revenue while fixed costs (O&M, G&A, depreciation on project debt) are less flexible [#A]

  • Probability — bear materially correct: Medium — approximately 25-35% probability that the core bear scenario (persistent power price weakness + execution miss + multiple de-rating) plays out at scale [#A]

  • Sub: The bear scenario has already partially played out (2021-24 de-rating); the residual risk is whether the current valuation already prices in these headwinds (bull) or whether further compression remains (bear)
