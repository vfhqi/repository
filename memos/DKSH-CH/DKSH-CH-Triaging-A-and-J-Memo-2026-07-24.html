B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Heritage and footprint. DKSH Holding AG is a Switzerland-domiciled Market Expansion Services provider, tracing to 1865, that helps multinational manufacturers enter and expand across Asia-Pacific without owning the local infrastructure themselves
2.
Four-segment structure. The group runs four business units: Healthcare (52.6% of FY2025 sales), Consumer Goods (30.5%), Performance Materials (12.3%) and Technology (4.6%), each with a materially different margin and growth profile
3.
What it actually does. DKSH takes on sourcing, regulatory registration, marketing sales, physical distribution and after-sales service on behalf of principal brand owners (Bayer, Sanofi, Nestlé and others) across 36 Asian markets
4.
Scale. FY2025 net sales reached CHF 11,070.6m (+2.9% constant-currency, -0.2% reported on Swiss franc translation), Core EBIT CHF 349.0m (+6.7% CER), 26,840 employees
5.
Financial character. A five-year run of Core EBIT margin expansion to 3.2% sits on an extreme asset-light base
5.1
Asset-light detail. Capex just 0.3% of sales, 95.2% cash conversion (sixth straight year above the 90% target), and a 23-day cash conversion cycle
6.
Geographic concentration. Thailand alone is 30.4% of group net sales, with Greater China at 21.6% and Malaysia/Singapore at 21.8%; the group earns over 94% of revenue in Asia-Pacific while reporting in Swiss francs
7.
Investment-style read. DKSH reads as a low-margin, high-cash-conversion distribution compounder rather than a classic GARP growth name or an event-driven special situation: the change-from-current is a slow mix shift (Healthcare outsourcing), not a discrete catalyst
8.
Ownership. The Diethelm Keller family pool controls 45.0% of voting rights, a structural feature that both insulates the stock from activist pressure and removes an upside lever Richard's other holdings sometimes carry
9.
Balance sheet. Net cash of CHF 25.2m at FY2025 close, equity ratio 33.1%, net debt-to-EBITDA below 0.10x versus a 2.0x internal threshold: DKSH carries no leverage constraint on its M&A programme
10.
Recent capital deployment. 35 acquisitions completed 2019 to 2025 (versus 16 in the prior period), nine in 2025 alone adding CHF 150m of annualised sales, funded entirely from internally generated cash
