§4 Synthesis

4.1 Sector and Business Context

Trustpilot is a B2B SaaS platform monetising its consumer review marketplace: businesses pay recurring subscriptions (annual contract values typically $5,000-$150,000+) to manage their Trustpilot profile, respond to reviews, and access analytics. The structural thesis rests on two pillars: (i) the shift of consumer trust-verification online, accelerating as AI language models increasingly cite third-party review sources; and (ii) a multi-year margin expansion story — the business crossed $300m ARR in 2025, reached a 15.6% adjusted EBITDA margin (up from near-zero), and is targeting 30% margins by 2030.

The AI angle deserves specific weight. Trustpilot ranks as the fifth most-cited domain on ChatGPT globally. AI-driven click-throughs rose ~1,490% in FY2025, making Trustpilot one of the rare listed platforms that is a net beneficiary of the AI search transition rather than a victim. This drove an aggressive re-rating in the 12 months to May 2026 — the stock had rallied ~60% year-to-date before the Morgan Stanley downgrade.

The FY2025 result itself was strong: revenue of $261m (+24% year-on-year), adjusted EBITDA of $40.7m (+69% year-on-year), pre-tax profit of $14.1m (up from $5.2m in FY2024), and adjusted free cash flow of $46.6m (+173%). Net Dollar Retention of 102% and Gross Dollar Retention of 87% confirmed the subscriber base is broadly stable, though gross retention below 90% remains a mild structural concern. Bookings growth of 18% in constant currency and crossing the $300m ARR milestone were the headline beats.
