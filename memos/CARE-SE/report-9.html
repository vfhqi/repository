§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (margin sustainability)

  • Margin sustainability verdict: Margin expansion to date is real but primarily cost-sourced — sustainable only to the extent cost actions are permanent; the next ~200–300 bps of target margin expansion (to 18–20%) requires revenue growth to provide operating leverage, which is not yet demonstrated at scale [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: Revenue CAGR H1 2022 to H2 2024 (annualised) is approximately +1.5% — effectively flat in real terms; this is below the inflation rate in the Nordic countries (Sweden CPI ~5–8% in 2022–2023), implying real revenue contraction throughout most of the period [#A]

  • EBITDA CAGR: EBITDA CAGR over the same period is approximately +20% annualised from trough (H2 2022 SEK 50m to H2 2024 SEK 80m) — materially above revenue growth, confirming genuine operating leverage; the source of leverage matters critically [#A]

  • Operating leverage: The ~500 bps EBITDA margin expansion (H2 2022 ~11% to H2 2024 ~17%) decomposes approximately as: headcount reduction ~250 bps, alarm-centre lease renegotiations ~150 bps, equipment cost efficiencies ~100 bps — all cost actions, not revenue-driven [#A]

    • Sub: Revenue per subscriber has been broadly flat (modest price increases offset by mix shift to lower-ARPU product tiers in UK)
    • Sub: No evidence of material pricing power expansion in L3Y data — this is a known bear point for the forward thesis

  • Gross margin: Gross margin has been stable-to-improving: ~56% (H1 2022) to ~58% (H2 2024) — the modest 200 bps expansion reflects equipment cost reduction (chip-based alarm devices cheaper to source) and slightly better Nordic service contract terms [#A]

  • EBITDA margin: EBITDA margin target of 18–20% (management medium-term guidance, H2 2024 call) implies ~100–300 bps further expansion from H2 2024 ~17%; achieving this via cost alone is increasingly difficult — the remaining cost actions are smaller and the next tranche requires revenue growth to provide the lever [#A]

  • Cost cut driver: Careium reduced headcount from ~1,800 FTE (H1 2022) to ~1,500 FTE (H2 2024) — a ~17% reduction; the restructuring charges reflect this; further headcount reduction of equivalent magnitude appears unlikely without service quality risk [#A]

  • Scale driver: Scale benefits from organic subscriber growth are visible only in H1–H2 2024 — too early to establish as a structural driver; the management case for 18–20% EBITDA margin rest substantially on ~5% organic revenue growth per annum providing incremental operating leverage on a partially fixed cost base [#I]

  • Sustainability sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? The ~500 bps margin improvement is cost-cut-led and largely exhausted — headcount reduction headroom is limited, lease renegotiations are one-time, and equipment savings are already embedded; any revenue growth disappointment at the 4–5% required level would stall the margin expansion story at ~17% rather than reaching the 18–20% target [#J]
