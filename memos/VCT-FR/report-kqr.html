Section 4 – Value Chain Map + Vicat's Position + Margin Distribution 33 findings

Section summary
  1. high upstream quarry and kiln integration protects clinker self-sufficiency, shielding downstream activities from raw material price volatility and supply disruptions
  2. aggregates command structurally superior EBITDA margins of 31% to 35%, whereas ready-mix concrete acts as a low-margin, high-churn volume puller
  3. pure-play grinding plants remain highly exposed to clinker price swings, unlike integrated plants which enjoy localized regional monopolies and pricing power

End-to-end heavy materials value chain

1.
Upstream quarrying stage: the value chain begins with high-capital extraction where limestone and clay are blasted from permitted reserves near plants
2.
Resource access barriers: greenfield quarry permits are increasingly difficult to obtain, creating a formidable moat for existing heavy building material operators
3.
Transport distance limits: heavy limestone feedstock cannot be transported economically over long distances, restricting cement plant locations to raw deposits
4.
Industrial clinkerization stage: raw materials are heated to 1,500 degrees Celsius in massive rotary kilns to produce clinker
5.
Massive capital requirements: building a modern cement kiln requires an intensive capital investment of €150 million to €300 million
6.
Extreme energy dependence: clinkerization is highly sensitive to energy prices as kiln thermal combustion represents a substantial operational cost price component
7.
Downstream grinding stage: clinker is ground with gypsum and supplementary cementitious materials to produce finished construction cement
8.
Grinding aid usage: chemical admixtures are added during grinding to improve mill efficiency and enhance finished cement performance
9.
Low-carbon product blending: alternative materials like pozzolan and calcined clay are blended to lower the clinker-to-cement ratio
10.
Downstream concrete mixing: ready-mix concrete is manufactured locally by mixing cement, aggregates, water, and specialized chemical admixtures
11.
Perishable delivery window: ready-mix concrete is highly perishable, requiring rapid transit in rotating drum mixers within 90 minutes
12.
Localized batching networks: concrete plants must sit close to urban construction markets due to prohibitive trucking transport costs

13.

14.
Premium quarry returns: well-located quarries can achieve gross margins of 55% to 60% by capturing logistics freight savings
15.
Integrated cement margins: integrated cement plants capture high EBITDA margins near 29.07% (calculated) for Vicat
16.
Post-depreciation EBIT returns: Vicat's cement segment generated a current EBIT margin of 19.10% (calculated) in 2025
17.
Ready-mix concrete margins: the concrete segment operates on much thinner margins, generating typical EBITDA margins of only 10% to 15%
18.
Grinding station returns: non-integrated grinding facilities earn low EBIT margins of 5% to 8% due to clinker feedstock purchase costs
