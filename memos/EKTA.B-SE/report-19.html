KEY FINDINGS (BLUF) #J

  • Siemens/Varian integration is not "pending" — it has landed. Full brand unification completed August 2025. ARPA-H $83M FLASH mandate issued May 2026. The scale and innovation gap is widening, not stable. #J
  • ❌ MOSAIQ displacement is structurally underappreciated. Epic's radiation oncology module plus ARIA's installed base share a combined US market foothold that makes MOSAIQ increasingly a legacy system in the world's most lucrative market. #J
  • GE HealthCare is a credible MRI-linac entrant, not a distant threat. ESTRO 2025 portfolio expansion confirmed; iRT MR-guided solution live; Spectronic Medical MR-only workflow partnership active. The "no competitor" narrative has a shelf life. #J
  • United Imaging's overseas revenue is growing 42% YoY. Established in UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain. CT-Linac integration is a structural product advantage Elekta does not match. Price differential in APAC/EMEA standard linac is widening. #J
  • The backlog revisions are a false friend. Management framed two purges totalling SEK 7.1bn as "quality improvement." Bears should read this as demand destruction in China and US, not housekeeping. #J
  • FLASH radiotherapy funded at scale by ARPA-H for Siemens' installed linac base — not MRI-linac focused — threatens the Unity premium positioning by making adaptive delivery achievable on standard C-arm systems. #J
  • ViewRay's failure was not a vindication of Unity — it was a warning about: cash burn in niche premium segments, long installation cycles, and the brittleness of clinical evidence-dependent sales cycles. Elekta faces structurally analogous risks at smaller scale. #J
  • ❌ Operating leverage story stalled: EBIT 11.8% in FY24 only reached 16.3% in FY25 via cost-out, not revenue growth. Margin expansion driven by restructuring rather than volume/mix shift is not durable. #J
  • SEK structural weakness is not the only FX risk. With 25-30% US revenues and USD-denominated contracts in EMEA/APAC, a prolonged USD weakness scenario (USD/SEK falling) hits both revenue translation and competitive pricing power simultaneously. #J

§3 — False Friends

Definition: Evidence that investors interpret as bull confirmation but which bears should read as confirming failure. False friends are structurally more dangerous than obvious bear cases because they suppress the urgency to act on emerging deterioration. #J

False Friend 1: "FY25 EBIT margin of 16.3% proves the recovery story is working"

  • What bulls see: Margin expanded from 11.8% (FY24) to 16.3% (FY25). Target of 14%+ achieved and exceeded. Thesis on track.
  • What bears see: 16.3% was delivered via SEK 279M cost-out run-rate savings. Not via revenue growth — which was guided down mid-year. Not via mix shift — software growth rate was below target. Not via ASP improvement — linac pricing is under competitive pressure. The margin was extracted from the cost base, not generated by the business model. A cost-out driven margin is a one-time improvement that exhausts itself within 1-2 years unless the revenue engine accelerates simultaneously. In Elekta's case, revenue growth faces multiple structural headwinds.
