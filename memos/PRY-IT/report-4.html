Revenue Growth & Organic Dynamics

PRY's +5.4% organic growth on a EUR 19.65bn base represents the largest absolute revenue increment in European cables. Transmission (+28.7%) carried the load, but Q4 organic growth decelerated to 4.3%, and Specialties contracted (-2.1%).

  • Judgement: PRY is the only player with scale across all cable segments and geographies — the Encore Wire deal doubled North American footprint and structurally shifted revenue quality toward higher-margin transmission and digital solutions [C] #J
  • Judgement: Q4 Transmission organic growth of 8.4% missed the Visible Alpha consensus of 24.8% — driven by tougher YoY comps, not fundamental weakness, but a warning that headline growth rates are normalising [AS] #J
  • Analysis: FY25 reported growth of +15.4% overstates organic momentum — roughly half from perimeter change (Encore Wire annualisation + Channell). The true organic run-rate of +5.4% is respectable but dependent on Transmission continuing at +28.7% [C+AS] #A
  • Analysis: Nexans outpaced PRY on organic growth (8.3% FY, 11.8% Q4) but off a much smaller base and with unsustainable Transmission surge (+40% Q4). NKT's +6% organic decelerated sharply from +26% in FY24 [C+AS] #A
  • Analysis: Encore Wire captures ~25-30% of the USD 8-10bn US copper building wire market where PRY previously lacked domestic capacity — a synergistic top-line play validated by IRA/reshoring tailwinds [AS] #A
  • PRY segment breakdown (FY25): Transmission EUR 3,262m (+28.7% organic), Power Grid (+12.8%), Electrification I&C EUR 7,519m (+0.6%), Specialties (-2.1%), Digital Solutions EUR 1,618m (+8.4%) [C] #I
  • Nexans warned that near-30% Transmission organic growth seen over last two years is "highly unusual" and will normalise significantly downwards in 2026 [AS] #I
  • NKT Q4 organic revenue contracted -8% due to Champlain Hudson project ramp-down — the sharpest recent negative inflection in the peer group [AS] #I
  • Sumitomo Infocommunications posted +51.6% YoY revenue growth in fiscal Q3 on AI data centre demand — the strongest quarterly acceleration among all peers [AS] #I

  • Judgement: PRY's absolute EBITDA of EUR 2,398m dwarfs both peers combined (Nexans EUR 728m + NKT EUR 390m = EUR 1,118m) — PRY alone accounts for ~68% of the combined EBITDA pool across the three European cable majors [C] #J
  • Judgement: Transmission margins at 20.9% (Q4) exceeding the 2028 target raises the question of whether further expansion is achievable or whether margins plateau — while Power Grid's 180bps sequential drop to 12.1% is the bear's strongest data point [C+AS] #J
  • Analysis: Margin expansion drivers: (1) Transmission mix shift to HV/submarine (+380bps FY), (2) Encore Wire high-margin US building wire, (3) Digital Solutions margin doubled to 18.3% via Channell, (4) operating leverage on volume growth [C] #A
  • Analysis: Morgan Stanley explicitly warns 2026 may represent the absolute cyclical peak for Electrification margins — US building wire at ~15% vs 7-8% pre-pandemic historical average, making current profitability highly vulnerable to competitive mean reversion [AS] #A
  • Analysis: Industry experts note extreme HV transmission margins (~25% project margins) are a direct consequence of severe capacity shortage among top-tier European producers — this is structural but not permanent [AS] #A
  • Analysis: No concrete evidence US copper/aluminium tariffs have provided meaningful margin tailwind for Electrification or Power Grid divisions despite widespread market anticipation [AS] #A

Sceptical Assessment

The fundamental trajectory is the strongest in the peer group. The debate is valuation — whether the market has already priced the structural growth story — and normalisation risk in Electrification margins.

  • Judgement: The bearish case is concentrated on three risks: (1) Transmission margin plateau/normalisation as new capacity enters, (2) Encore Wire margin mean-reversion from ~15% to historical 7-8%, and (3) Asian competition shifting HV from deficit to oversupply by 2030 [C+AS] #J
  • Judgement: The structural demand story (grid modernisation, offshore wind, data centre electrification, US reshoring) is real, visible in backlog data, and multi-decade — the EUR 17bn+ backlog is tangible proof [C+AS] #J
  • Analysis: Sector's recent fundamental momentum has been partly inflated by an unprecedented confluence of supply shortages, project prepayments, and post-pandemic pricing abnormalities now entering a normalisation phase [AS] #A
  • Analysis: Barriers to entry remain high in submarine cables — the oligopoly structure (PRY + NEX + NKT + Sumitomo = ~50% of market) is unlikely to erode quickly. But Asian entrants are gaining credibility through homologation and major European contract wins [C+AS] #A
  • Analysis: Encore Wire is a better asset than the market appreciates — US building wire is high-ROIC with low capex needs and strong cash generation, providing earnings diversification against the project-based Transmission cycle [C] #A
  • Analysis: Submarine turnkey contracts carry immense liability clauses — minor delays or technical defects can activate crippling financial penalties. Cost variations and installation hurdles frequently cause actual project margins to deteriorate significantly from optimistic tender-phase levels [AS] #A
  • Analysis: Low-voltage construction margins remain highly tethered to copper prices — a cyclical drop in metals could instantly wipe out several percentage points of profitability. Conversely, copper/aluminium structural deficits projected by 2026 may support pricing [C+AS] #A

  • Action: Track Asian competitor European market share trajectory — any evidence of pricing pressure or contract wins from LS Cable/Sumitomo is an early warning signal [AS] #T
