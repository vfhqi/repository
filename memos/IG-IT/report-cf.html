Material Risks and plausible problem to monitor

1.
Risk 1: Regulatory efficiency clawbacks and WACC compression post-FY27 represent the most severe structural headwind. ARERA plans to introduce a totex-style regulatory framework for Italian gas distribution by approximately 2028, with Deutsche Bank expecting a €50 million efficiency clawback in 2028 and another €100 million in 2030 . This threat is compounded by the risk of moving to full cost-of-debt indexation from FY28 (which could cut WACC by 30 bps) and aligning gas distribution's 44% notional gearing with the 50% peer utility average (which would cut WACC by 20-30 bps) . The combined impact could eliminate €150 million of opex outperformance, reducing valuation to €8.00 per share from €10.00

Risk 2: Structural household gas demand decline and volume erosion threatens long-term RAB growth sustainability. Italian household gas consumption in 2024 fell 24% below 2013 levels due to energy efficiency trends and domestic household gas demand sat 21% below pre-energy crisis levels recorded in 2021 . Forward projections remain dire, with Snam scenarios projecting Italian gas demand dropping to 19.4 bcm by 2030 under the PNIEC policy plan . While regulatory revenue caps currently insulate Italgas from volume risk, sustained demand declines will eventually pressure ARERA to reduce allowed opex and potentially cut WACC parameters to reflect lower utilization and stranded asset risks. 46 plausible problem to monitor: Unresolved smart meter depreciation recovery and technology transition costs create hidden P&L risk. This regulatory ambiguity could force accelerated depreciation charges and reduce the net economic benefit of the digitalization capex program, directly undermining the €100 million revenue uplift thesis
