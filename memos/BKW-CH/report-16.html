§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Governance structure is broadly clean for a Swiss cantonal-anchor company — no dual-class, no material weaknesses, no restatements, no known regulatory probes — but the cantonal control structure is itself the dominant governance characteristic and sets a ceiling on standard-governance-framework assessments

  • Voting rights structure:
  • BKW is a conventional single-share-class company (ordinary registered shares; "Namenaktien")
  • No dual-class structure, no golden shares, no poison pill
  • However: Canton Bern + Bernese municipalities collectively hold ~52% — this is a de facto controlling block that delivers all the governance limitations of concentrated ownership without the formal dual-class mechanism; the AGM is not genuinely contested
  • Anti-takeover defence: the ~52% block is itself the anti-takeover provision — no formal shareholder rights plan needed
  • Related-party transactions:
  • BKW provides electricity distribution and grid services to Bernese municipalities and cantonal entities — these are related-party transactions by accounting definition; disclosed in annual report
  • Terms: regulated tariffs (Swiss grid tariff framework under ElCom regulation) — cannot be meaningfully abused in the direction of favouring either party; regulatory framework disciplines pricing
  • Scale: these are material revenue items (municipal/cantonal electricity delivery is a core BKW business line) — disclosed in related-party note; no evidence of non-arm's-length pricing
  • Assessment: related-party structure is structural and disclosed; not a red flag given regulatory pricing discipline, but worth noting the economic entanglement
  • Audit history:
  • Statutory auditor: PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) — long-standing relationship
  • No recent auditor change
  • No material weaknesses or significant deficiencies in internal controls noted in L3Y annual reports
  • No restatements of significance
  • Reporting quality:
  • IFRS consolidated accounts + Swiss statutory; reconciliation clean
  • Non-GAAP adjustments (adjusted EBIT, adjusted EBITDA) are reconciled in filings; adjustments appear for acquisition costs and one-off restructuring items — legitimate and bounded
  • Services segment disclosure: noted in §2 as partially opaque; not a red flag but a limitation
  • Legal/regulatory issues:
  • No known class action lawsuits
  • No FINMA (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority) investigations publicly flagged
  • No EU-level antitrust or regulatory probe visible
  • BKW's nuclear plant (Mühleberg) decommissioning is a regulatory process fully underway and disclosed; no material unexpected liability flag
  • ElCom (Swiss electricity regulator) regulatory framework governs network tariffs — standard regulatory risk, not a red flag
  • Whistleblower / governance complaints:
  • No publicly visible whistleblower cases or governance complaint signals
  • Glassdoor reviews (§7) contain standard disgruntled-employee critiques (management hierarchy, bureaucracy) but no systematic fraud/ethics allegations
  • Swiss governance code compliance:
  • BKW reports compliance with the Swiss Code of Best Practice for Corporate Governance (economiesuisse code)
  • Compensation report is separate from annual report per Swiss Ordinance Against Excessive Compensation (OaEC / Minder initiative) requirements — shareholder binding vote on compensation held at AGM
  • AGM compensation votes: no evidence of significant shareholder dissent on compensation in L3Y (cantonal anchor makes defeat of management proposals structurally impossible, but the absence of large-scale minority dissent is still signal)
  • FINMA / SIX Exchange regulatory filings:
  • Insider trading disclosures: routine and current
  • Ad hoc announcements: consistent with regulatory requirements; no evidence of delayed material disclosures
  • Sceptical bullet (governance): Bearish read: The single largest structural governance concern is not a red flag per se but a systemic constraint — the cantonal block renders conventional governance-discipline mechanisms (hostile takeover threat, contested AGM, activist pressure) functionally inoperative; this means management is accountable primarily to the cantonal shareholders' political priorities (energy transition, regional employment, Bernese energy security) rather than purely to shareholder value maximisation; if the cantonal political agenda ever diverges from minority shareholder interests (e.g. pressure to over-invest in grid for Swiss grid security at returns below cost of capital), there is no governance mechanism for minority shareholders to resist this effectively; the compliance-theatre risk is highest here — BKW governance appears exemplary precisely because it is never truly tested
