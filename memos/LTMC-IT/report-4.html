  • EBITDA margin best-in-class: 11% (FY2021) → 38% (FY2025) → 39.1% (Q1 2026). LTM's margin now aligned with Flutter (~39%), having compressed a ~28pp gap to ~1pp in four years. Entain (~20%) and Evoke (~23%) remain far behind. [#A]
  • Revenue growth decelerating naturally: +23% (FY2024, M&A-assisted) → +10-12% (FY2025, post-SKS365 consolidation) → guided +6-9% (FY2026). The step-down is structural and expected: M&A acquisition phase complete, organic growth primary driver going forward. [#A]
  • ANP outgrowing EBITDA: ANP grew +45% (FY2025) vs EBITDA +21% — driven by falling interest expense as leverage declines (3.7x → 2.4x). This leverage-to-EPS transmission dynamic continues through FY2026-FY2027. [#J]
  • Upgrade cycle in force: 14 net EPS upgrades in L12M. Post-Q1 2026, management raised FY2026 EBITDA guidance to top end of €940-980m range; Deutsche Bank raised its estimate to €979m. [#A]
  • Four consecutive guidance beats: Every quarterly guidance since IPO (May 2023) has beaten or been raised. Conservative guidance setting creates low guidance-cut risk. [#J]

  • Margin at convergence with Flutter: LTM EBITDA margin 38% (FY2025) vs Flutter ~39% — LTM has essentially closed the margin gap with the world's best-in-class online gaming operator. Entain (~20%) and Evoke (~23%) remain far behind. [#J]
  • Peer Margin Caveat: LTM's reported EBITDA margins appear higher than peers partly because LTM reports revenue NET of gaming taxes, while many international peers report gross revenue. On a comparable basis, retail gaming margins align more closely with peers. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 19 Aug 25]
  • Online EBITDA margin 57.5% (Q1 2026) — up from 53.6% (Q1 2025). The PWO/SKS365 integration delivered €87m total synergies, 34% above target. [INTRM • 7 May]
  • Guidance consistency: FY2024 guidance €700-730m → outturn €739m (+1.2% beat). FY2025 guidance originally €840-870m → raised → outturn €856m beat. FY2026 guidance €940-980m (now tracking top end). [#I]

Sceptical bullet: The EBITDA margin expansion story will eventually reach a structural ceiling. At ~50% online/50% retail equilibrium, blended EBITDA margin likely plateaus at 41-44%. Future EPS growth must increasingly come from revenue growth and leverage reduction, not margin expansion. Perhaps 3-5 percentage points of expansion remaining vs 28pp already captured. [#A]

§4 Free Cash Flow + Capital Returns

Net signal: FCF generation is growing strongly and now being returned to shareholders rather than absorbed by leverage paydown — marking the transition from PE-deleveraging story to capital-returns story. [#J]

  • Operating CF: FY2024 operating CF €556.8m (75% EBITDA conversion); Q1 2026 operating CF €196m (83% EBITDA conversion). Improving trajectory. [#A]
  • Capital return inflection: FY2024 dividend: €0.30/share (~€75m). FY2025: €0.44/share (~€111m = +47%). New €700m buyback authorised April 2026. Total returns target: €1bn across 2026-2027. [#A]
  • ROIC: "Solid high-teens" after tax per analysts — compares favourably to international peers. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 24 Oct 25]
  • Cash conversion 83.3% as of Q1 2026 — above FY2025 average of ~75%. [INTRM • 7 May]

§5 Segment Earnings Trends

Net signal: Online segment is the earnings growth engine; Gaming Franchise is the stable cash cow; Sports Franchise is the managed-decline contributor. EBITDA mix shifting rapidly toward Online. [#J]

  • Online EBITDA margin ~57.5%: Materially above blended group margin of 38-39%, confirming online mix-shift drives overall margin expansion. [#A]
  • Gaming Franchise margin ~24.8%: AWP machines have structured margin through fixed-fee ADM concession structure. Stable contributor. [#A]
  • Sports Franchise margin ~24.6% (Q1 2026, reported) vs 30.4% (Q1 2025): Low volatility from payout fluctuations; secular volume decline at retail level offsets any revenue growth at the margin line. [#A]
