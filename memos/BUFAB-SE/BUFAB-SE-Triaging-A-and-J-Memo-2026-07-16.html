B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Bufab Group is a Swedish value-added distributor of C-parts (fasteners, fixings and specialised components) serving industrial original equipment manufacturers across Europe, North America and Asia-Pacific
2.
Revenue scale. Trailing revenue runs approximately SEK 8.4 billion, built from organic growth of 2-5% per annum plus serial bolt-on acquisitions contributing a further 9-10% annually
3.
Margin profile. Adjusted EBITA margin reached a record 15.3% in the first quarter of 2026, structurally ahead of the company's own 14% 2026 target and materially above the peer range
4.
Balance sheet. Net debt to adjusted EBITDA improved to 2.4x by the first quarter of 2026, inside the company's 2.0x-3.0x target corridor, funded partly by strong cash conversion during the 2023-2024 destocking phase
5.
Strategic transition. The group divested its remaining manufacturing assets (Lann, Hallborn, the US Component Solutions Group unit) in 2024-2025, completing a pivot from mixed manufacturer-distributor to pure-play distribution
6.
Geography and segments. Operations run across a five-region model (Europe West, Europe North & East, Americas, Asia-Pacific and a fifth administrative segment), with Europe West the largest and best-performing region
7.
M&A engine. The group has completed over 50 acquisitions historically, most recently VITAL (Italy, November 2024) and novia Group (Germany, October 2025), both immediately accretive to group margins
8.
Investment style implications. The business reads as a GARP-style serial-acquirer compounder in the Addtech/Indutrade/Lifco Swedish tradition, not a pure cyclical or a pure event-driven situation, though cyclical industrial exposure is material
9.
End-market mix. Demand splits between resilient pockets (energy, defence, agriculture, digital infrastructure) and weaker cyclical pockets (automotive, construction, furniture), a genuinely mixed demand picture rather than a clean tailwind
10.
Sell-side coverage. Coverage is thin and concentrated: three brokers (DNB Carnegie, Carnegie, plus Jefferies peer-mentions), all currently rated Buy, which is a coverage-concentration flag worth carrying forward
