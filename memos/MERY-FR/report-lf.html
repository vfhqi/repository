EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 6 findings

1.
Net Verdict on CEO Quality: Vincent Ravat represents a highly credible operator-track CEO with an exceptionally stable 12-year internal trajectory at Mercialys, spanning Deputy Managing Director (2014), Deputy CEO (2016), and Chief Executive since 2022. He has delivered consistent operational performance through a disciplined portfolio refocusing from 93 to 47 shopping centers, executed the accretive Saint-Genis 2 acquisition at ~8% yield, and posted the best overall results since 2019 in FY2025. He is not a visionary capital allocator or high-profile dealmaker, but an unusually grounded execution-focused operator with deep merchant DNA from his decade in French retail
2.
Board independence and governance profile: The Board of Directors comprises nine members with an exceptional 77.80% independence ratio – significantly above Carmila (50%), Klepierre (67%), and URW (67%). Average board age of 59 years and average seniority of 7 years secure stable institutional knowledge. The Audit, Risks and Sustainable Development Committee is 100% independent with 75% female representation. No shareholder agreements or voting blocks remain in effect, and the free float represents 87.5% of outstanding capital. CEO Vincent Ravat holds 197,814 shares (0.21% of capital), while Chairman Éric Le Gentil holds 28,698 shares
3.
Governance clean and transparent: Mercialys presents a highly transparent, clean governance framework with one share, one vote enforced since Casino's full exit. No dual-class share structure, no related-party transactions, and no material audit findings
4.
CFO and compensation alignment: CEO ex-post compensation of €1.14 million in 2025 is significantly below peers. Robust clawback provisions allow the board to reclaim variable pay in cases of serious misconduct or fraud. Heavy 30% weighting of carbon-reduction targets in long-term incentives could dilute focus on absolute financial return performance
5.
Expert network – thin pool: Expert commentary reveals a highly polarized view on retail structures. Direct Mercialys expert pool is thin (1 call in 36-month window), requiring reliance on peer expert discussions
6.
Key-person risk – elevated: The December 2025 departure of Deputy CEO Elizabeth Blaise creates unprecedented single-point executive dependency. Ravat is now the sole member of senior management since January 2026
