Research Plan (AS framing)

This deep-dive question set is designed for a one-on-one meeting with Elekta's CEO, Jakob Just-Bomholt, and CFO, Tobias Hägglöv, focusing on the critical execution pivots currently underway. As Elekta navigates its "Must-Win Battles" and prepares for the June 17, 2026 Capital Markets Day, management is facing high investor scrutiny regarding the bridge from current performance (~12% adj. EBIT margin) to their 14%–15%+ medium-term targets.

Sources cited: Q3 2026 Earnings Call (05 Mar 26), Q2 2026 Earnings Call (26 Nov 25), Q3 Interim Report (05 Mar 26), Q3 Press Release (05 Mar 26), Q1 2026 Earnings Call (28 Aug 25), DNB Carnegie — "Elekta (Buy): Margin recovery has started – Q3 review" (06 Mar 26), Jefferies Research — multiple notes (17 Mar, 26 Mar, 29 Apr, 2 Feb, 28 Mar 25), Deutsche Bank Research — "Elekta: Priced for more than it can deliver?" (13 Mar), Expert Calls (29 Dec 25, 1 Jan, 22 Dec 25).

Section 1: FY27 EBIT Bridge and Structural Cost Reset

Q1. Can you deconstruct the bridge from the current ~12% adjusted EBIT margin to the 15%+ medium-term target by quantifying the specific contributions from the SEK 500 million savings program versus organic operating leverage?

Context: Management has initiated a "Must-Win Battle" to reset the operating model, targeting SEK 500 million in annual savings. On a ~SEK 17 billion sales base, this alone represents ~290 basis points of potential uplift.

Informative Answer: A clear split between cost-out benefits and the volume-driven leverage required to hit 15% would allow investors to model the "floor" of margin expansion independent of market growth.

Key AS evidence: Jefferies (17 Mar): "The path to margin expansion: deconstructing the 14%+ target... The most significant driver should be the >SEK 500 million in annual run-rate cost savings from the new operating model. On a rolling-12-month net sales base of ~SEK 17.1 billion, this alone represents a potential ~290 basis point uplift to the EBIT margin. A substantial portion of this benefit is expected to materialize starting in Q4 2025/26."

Deutsche Bank (13 Mar): "Elekta: Priced for more than it can deliver?" — Falko Friedrichs.

Q2. What is the expected quarterly cadence for the SEK 500 million run-rate savings, and should we expect any "leakage" where these savings are reinvested into commercial intensity in the U.S.?

Context: While full run-rate impact was originally expected in Q1 FY27, recent updates suggest a "substantial part" may materialize as early as Q4 FY26.

Source: Jefferies Research (17 Mar).

Q3. With the shift from a capitalization-heavy R&D model (historically ~60%) toward a more amortization-balanced approach (~40%), how does this impact the "cash EBIT" bridge over the next 24 months?

Context: Elekta is intentionally reducing R&D capitalization levels to improve "quality of earnings," which has recently created a 150-basis-point headwind to reported EBIT.

Sources: Jefferies (17 Mar), Q3 Interim Report (05 Mar).

Jefferies (17 Mar): "The path to margin expansion relies on several distinct, quantifiable levers. Restructuring savings: The most significant driver... Gross margin improvement: adjusted gross margin improved to 38.3% in Q3, up 120 bps year-over-year... Pricing: A new pricing desk has been established... Mix: Higher volumes of new, higher-margin products like Elekta Evo and Elekta ONE will improve the overall product mix. Upgrades, in particular, carry very high margins (CEO mentioned '80% plus')."

Section 2: Evo Linac Margin Profile and Product Mix

Q4. Is the Elekta Evo linac expected to be gross margin accretive to the blended Solutions segment in FY27, or does the initial manufacturing ramp-up make it dilutive in the near term?

Context: Evo is positioned as a key lever for margin expansion, reportedly carrying a ~30% price premium over similar platforms with broadly unchanged COGS.

Source: Jefferies (26 Mar).

Informative Answer: Explicit confirmation that Evo's unit economics are superior to the Versa HD/Harmony platforms from day one, rather than requiring several years of volume to reach maturity.

Q5. How much of the projected gross margin expansion to 40%+ relies on "upgrades" of existing systems to Evo versus "greenfield" installations?

Context: Management has noted that upgrades carry significantly higher margins (80%+) compared to full new system installs.

Source: Deutsche Bank (13 Mar).

Q6. Regarding the "Continuous COGS Reduction" initiative, what are the three largest bill-of-materials components you are targeting for dual-sourcing or re-engineering?

Context: Must-Win Battle 4 focuses on reducing COGS through dual-sourcing and cost-efficient suppliers.

Source: Q3 Press Release (05 Mar).

Jefferies (2 Feb): "Multisourcing, local manufacturing and AI-enabled service workflows represent the core levers, to be driven by a soon-to-be-appointed COO. With GM having shed ~300bp in the past 5Y, the >SEK500M savings program is critical."
