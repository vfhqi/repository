Research Plan: Nokia is currently navigating a pivotal transition as it shifts its focus from traditional telecom equipment markets toward high-growth AI and cloud data center infrastructure, a move that has significantly polarized sell-side sentiment. While bullish analysts point to a "re-rating story" driven by accelerating momentum in Optical and IP Networking, more cautious observers worry that the stock's valuation has run ahead of its fundamentals despite a still-sluggish global Radio Access Network market.

Key Bull vs. Bear Arguments

Bull Case: The AI Data Centre Transition

  • IP and Optical Momentum: Nokia no longer just a "telco play" — credible competitor to Ciena and Arista in data centre market. Optical+IP growth guidance raised to 18-20% for 2026. AI & Cloud SAM to grow at 27% CAGR (2025-28) vs 16% indicated at CMD. [Jefferies • 23 Apr]
  • Nvidia Partnership & AI-RAN: Strategic collaboration with Nvidia seen as "future-proofing" for 6G transition. GPU-based software-led radio solutions could allow Nokia to regain share lost in 5G cycle. [Jefferies • 26 Mar, 3 Mar]
  • Margin Expansion Potential: Shifting mix toward higher-margin software and Network Infrastructure expected to drive operating margins toward 13% by 2027-2028. [DNB Carnegie • 23 Apr]
  • Gross Margin Beat: Q1 2026 Network Infrastructure GM of 43.4% vs Jefferies estimate 41% — Infinera synergies materialising faster than expected. [Jefferies • 23 Apr]
  • Order Momentum: €1bn AI & Cloud orders in Q1 2026 alone; book-to-bill above 1 in Optical and IP. [Interim Report • 23 Apr]

Bear Case: Valuation and Structural Risks

  • Stagnant RAN Market: Core Mobile Networks business challenged by low single-digit margins and flattish global market; significant historical share losses at AT&T and Verizon. [Multiple brokers]
  • Valuation at 10-Year Highs: AI-fueled rally may have already priced in expected earnings growth; stock at premium that fundamentals do not yet fully support. [DNB Carnegie • Mar 26]
  • Geopolitical and Supply Risks: Trade tensions, optical component supply chain tightness, loss of Chinese market persistent headwinds for both Nokia and Ericsson. [Deutsche Bank • Apr]
  • AI-RAN Commercial Delay: Mass commercial AI-RAN deployment not expected until 2028 — long-term story, not near-term catalyst. [Multiple]
  • IP Networks Lumpiness: IP Networks grew only 3% in Q1 2026; hyperscaler design wins (believed to include Google) not shipping until Q2+. [Jefferies • 1 May]

Key Analyst Debates

  1. Re-rating vs. value trap: Is Nokia truly a structural AI story or a hardware vendor temporarily benefiting from AI capex cycle? Jefferies bull; DNB Carnegie cautious.
  2. IP Networks acceleration: Q1 IP growth was only 3%, but Jefferies believes a major Google switching design win will accelerate from Q2. Will it materialise on timeline?
  3. Valuation vs. Ericsson: Nokia trades at EV/Adj. EBIT 20.6x vs Ericsson 9.8x (DNB Carnegie estimates). Nokia premium reflects AI optionality — but is it sustainable?
  4. Optical margin sustainability: Q1 NI GM beat at 43.4% vs 41% estimate. Are Infinera synergies durable or front-loaded?
  5. RAN stabilisation timeline: Mobile Networks growing again in Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 — but is this genuine recovery or inventory restocking?
