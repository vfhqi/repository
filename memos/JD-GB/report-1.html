1. Segments

JD Core (Sports Fashion): Flagship brand. Premium, style-led, 16-24 year-old core demographic. UK now only 25% of Group sales.

North America Multi-Banner: JD, Finish Line (retiring), Hibbett (1,100+ stores, smaller cities), DTLR (urban streetwear), Shoe Palace (West Coast), City Gear (Mid-South). Five complementary fascias covering demographic and geographic breadth no single competitor matches.

European Expansion (Courir): Nov 2024 acquisition (€520m, 323 stores, primarily France).

Outdoor/Other: Blacks, Millets. Being rationalised. Non-core.

Specialty Boutiques: Size?, Footpatrol. Premium sneaker enthusiast market. Disproportionate brand heat and cultural credibility. Strategically significant.

3. Nike/Adidas Dependency — The Central Structural Fact (CQ4 — Own-Brand vs Third-Party)

Nike: ~45% of purchasing. Adidas: ~15-20%. Together: ~60-65% of cost of goods. JD has NO significant own-brand proposition. Margin entirely a function of brand partner terms. This is the critical CQ4 answer: JD is structurally third-party, and that is unlikely to change materially over the investment horizon.

The exclusive product dynamic is the moat. JD's superior access to Nike and Adidas exclusive colourways and limited-edition SKUs is relationship-based, not contractual. When Nike underperforms (2023-24 innovation drought), JD underperforms. Structural correlation.

4. US Business

North America: c.39% of Group revenue at £4.9bn+. Five fascias. Finish Line: retiring (converting to JD at selective locations, rest operating through Macy's concessions over next 3 years). Hibbett: $1.1bn acquisition, 1,100+ stores, $25m annualised cost synergy target on track. US margin under pressure: Finish Line promotional intensity during brand transition, Hibbett integration costs, footwear tariff uncertainty.

5. Management Quality (CQ5)

Régis Schultz (CEO Jan 2023): professional manager from Al-Futtaim Group. Priorities: JD First, omnichannel/digital, disciplined M&A, 250-350 new stores/year, rationalise non-core. Capital allocation: Hibbett ($1.1bn — complex, jury out), Courir (€520m — defensible, fills French gap), buybacks (£200-300m rolling). Execution risk: simultaneous Hibbett + Finish Line retirement + Courir + digital build + rationalisation.

Governance: post-Cowgill improvements substantive (chair/CEO separated, Andy Higginson as independent chair). Cowgill £5.5m consultancy deal (3 years, 2024) remains governance irritant. Pentland Group (founding family) retains c.53% of votes — limits institutional leverage.

6. Consumer Cycle (CQ2)

LFL sluggish in 2022-23 as real incomes squeezed. Margin stable but volume soft. Business is cyclical in volume but relatively protected in margin — quality cyclical rather than defensive compounder. FY2027 Q1 organic flat, LFL -2.3% — consumer pressure ongoing.

7. Fulcrum Driver (CQ3)

Nike DTC strategic reversal. Elliott Hill reversed Donahoe's DTC-first strategy, re-engaged wholesale partners, invested $5bn+ in marketing. Schultz confirmed Nike has specifically "accelerated" its wholesale partnership with JD. The LFL headwind from Nike product underperformance in 2023-24 should begin reversing in FY2027-28 as pipeline rebuilds. Trackable via Nike quarterly wholesale revenue trajectory, product innovation pace, and explicit management commentary.

8. Capital Allocation (CQ1)

Buybacks: £200-300m rolling. Dividends: token (0.6p final). No large M&A currently signalled. Capex £450-500m FY2027 (down from £500-600m) — genuine efficiency pivot. FCF yield at current market cap: ~14% — the single most compelling valuation metric.
