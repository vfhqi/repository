Regulatory Environment and DORA III Outlook
Aena submitted its comprehensive DORA III proposal for the 2027-2031 regulatory period to the Directorate
General of Civil Aviation and the National Commission on Markets and Competition on February
17, 2026. The framework outlines a massive €12.88 billion total investment plan, of which €9.99 billion is dedicated strictly to regulated investments. This unprecedented capital expenditure cycle is designed to
expand capacity at heavily congested hubs, enhance physical and operational security, and aggressively advance
the company's sustainability targets. 1 • ARS 2 3 • IRPRESS

progressively drive the Maximum Annual Revenue per Passenger from €10.92 in 2027 to €12.69 by the
end of 2031. 4 5 • IRPRESS 6 • ARS

International Expansion Strategy: Focus on Brazil
Aena's overarching strategic plan prioritizes the consolidation and selective expansion of its international asset
portfolio, with a soft target of increasing the international segment's contribution to 15% of the Group's total
EBITDA by 2026. The company enforces a rigorous capital allocation framework, stipulating that any new
acquisitions must offer operational control, full accounting consolidation, and attractive risk-adjusted profitability.
Consequently, Aena has strategically targeted Brazil as a core growth market due to its dynamic air traffic
potential and the opportunity to deploy Aena's established network management model. The Rio Galeão Acquisition
