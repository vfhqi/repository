§2 — Key Driver: MW Installed Capacity Growth (Build Rate)

  • J-front verdict: Constructive — Solaria's pipeline is credibly large and permitted; delivery pace is the case-defining variable and has been inconsistent, but the structural direction is right [#J]

Driver definition:
- Driver definition: Solaria grows operating solar MW from ~2.3 GW (end-2023 reported) toward a stated medium-term target of 6+ GW by 2026, implying ~1 GW per year of new capacity additions [#A]
- Sub-note: At ~€30–35/MWh EBITDA contribution per MW (European utility solar peers, 2023–24 vintage), each 1 GW adds ~€30–35m EBITDA; three-year ramp would add ~€90–105m EBITDA vs 2023 base [#A]
- Magnitude required: ~900 MW–1,000 MW per annum of new connections; Solaria's 2022–2023 delivery averaged ~600–700 MW/year — so acceleration of ~35–40% above recent run-rate is embedded in the bull case [#A]

- Target state: ~5.5–6.0 GW operating by end-2026 per management medium-term guidance [#A]
- Required trajectory: ~1 GW net annual additions 2024–2026; Q1–Q2 2024 newsflow suggests additions running below this pace [#A]

- Bull — recent: Spain's permitting framework for solar has been streamlined post-2022; the Ministerio para la Transición Ecológica has accelerated environmental assessments for projects under 50 MW [#A]
- Bull — mgmt: Management reconfirmed 2026 capacity targets at the 2023 annual results presentation; CEO Francisco Martínez-Córcoles has maintained public guidance at each quarterly update [#A]
- Bull — competitive: Solaria has one of the largest permitted land banks in Spain — a genuine moat vs greenfield competitors who face 4–6 year permitting cycles; pipeline quality is structurally above peer average [#J]
- Bull — industry: REPowerEU + Spain's National Energy and Climate Plan (PNIEC) targets imply ~20 GW/year of solar additions nationally through 2030; Solaria's 1 GW/year is a <5% share of a policy-driven demand base [#A]
- Bull — initiatives: Solaria has entered EPC framework agreements with Siemens Gamesa and multiple local civil contractors to reduce construction bottlenecks; standardised substation design reduces per-MW cycle time [#A]

- Bear — macro: European grid infrastructure capex cycle is under pressure from sovereign debt sustainability concerns; delays in REE grid investment would extend connection timelines beyond management's assumed base case [#A]
- Bear — structural: Solaria does not have a utility-scale battery storage portfolio to unlock curtailment-constrained grid slots; pure solar without storage is increasingly disadvantaged in Spain's increasingly renewables-heavy grid [#J]

- Baseline: Solaria disclosed ~60–65% of 2023 operating production was covered by long-term PPAs at ~€40–50/MWh (vintage 2020–21 contracts); residual ~35–40% exposed to spot [#A]

- Bull — industry: EU industrial electricity prices remain elevated vs pre-2021; corporate buyers have structurally higher willingness to pay for fixed-price renewables vs merchant exposure [#A]
- Bull — SS: Most sell-side models embed a gradual blended price improvement; Berenberg (per public commentary) models ~€55–58/MWh blended realised by 2026 [#A]

Bear case:

- Bear — macro: ❌ Spanish spot electricity prices fell sharply in H2 2023 and Q1 2024 on high hydro and wind generation; if this persists, merchant volumes realise below €40/MWh, dragging blended price below bull case [#A]
- Sub-note: Spain's nuclear availability + French interconnection capacity can suppress spot prices episodically; this is a structural risk, not purely cyclical [#A]

- Acceleration needed: Minimal — the driver is more about protecting existing realised price than accelerating improvement [#J]
- Probability — verbal: High plausibility for base case price protection; medium plausibility for upside price improvement scenario [#J]
- Key dependency: Corporate PPA demand remaining strong; Spanish spot prices not collapsing to sub-€30/MWh sustained [#A]
- Downside scenario: If blended realised price falls to €42–45/MWh on spot weakness + cannibalisation, EBITDA impact vs €55/MWh scenario is ~€15–20m per GW of operating fleet — material at 5–6 GW scale (~€75–120m EBITDA sensitivity) [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:

- Estimate revision L12M: Modest downward drift (−3 to −5%) on revenue per MWh reflecting 2023 spot weakness; not a major revision cycle [#A]
- Consensus dispersion: Moderate — revenue per MWh assumptions vary ~15% across brokers, primarily driven by different Spanish spot price decks [#A]

Evidence gaps:
- Evidence gap — PPA coverage on new-builds: The PPA contracted percentage for projects currently under construction is not publicly disclosed; this is the critical unknown — are new-build economics locked in or exposed to spot? [#A]
- Evidence gap — cannibalisation quantification: No public disclosure of Solaria's actual capture rate (realised price / average pool price) by year; this data is needed to track whether cannibalisation drag is accelerating [#A]

  • Plausibility verdict: High plausibility for base case power price realisation; upside price improvement scenario is medium plausibility given cannibalisation structural headwind. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: VALIDATES secondary fulcrum status; adds cannibalisation caveat that should be explicitly modelled.
