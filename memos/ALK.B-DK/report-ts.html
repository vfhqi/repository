Section 3 – What the company does 21 findings

Section summary
  1. ALK-Abelló occupies a dominant, vertically integrated position in the global allergy immunotherapy market, utilizing a high-barrier manufacturing process to deliver disease-modifying tablets and shots.

1.
Respiratory SLIT-tablets: Standardized sublingual immunotherapy tablets (ACARIZAX, ITULAZAX, GRAZAX) constitute 53.00% of total revenue and represent the primary, high-margin growth engine
2.
SCIT/SLIT-drops segment: Subcutaneous injections and sublingual drops represent 34.00% of 2025 revenue, offering stable cash flows but slower growth dynamics
3.
Anaphylaxis treatment portfolio: Emergency adrenaline treatments (Jext auto-injector and the licensed neffy nasal spray) contribute 13.00% of revenue and are growing rapidly
4.
Product focus and synergy: Products are strictly linked to respiratory allergies and acute anaphylaxis, enabling ALK to leverage its established specialist prescriber base
5.
Geographic segmentation profile: Revenue is highly concentrated in Europe at 71.00%, followed by North America at 16.00%, and International Markets at 13.00%
6.
Diagnostic test products: Allergy diagnostics (including skin prick tests) represent a small but highly complementary segment that supports clinical initiation of immunotherapy

In-licensed commercial assets: Leverages its commercial platform to distribute neffy, capturing a larger share of the fast-growing adrenaline market

Low customer concentration: No single customer or clinic holds material negotiating leverage, though regional public payers dictate local reimbursement and pricing

Structurally growing market: The global allergy population is expanding, with 50.00 million people experiencing severe, AIT-eligible respiratory symptoms globally

3.
Short interest status: No material published short-seller reports or elevated short interest exist on Copenhagen-listed ALK shares
4.
French reimbursement impacts: Reimbursement rate changes in France put downward pressure on average selling prices for sublingual drops starting early 2026
5.
Clinic capacity constraints: Growth in subcutaneous shots remains constrained by nursing capacity bottlenecks in major European and Nordic allergy clinics

Clinical and Commercial execution

6.
Physician habit inertia: The greatest commercial failure mode is the difficulty in converting US allergists from lucrative SCIT injection economics in the USA
7.
False friend signal: High wholesaler inventory stocking can act as a false friend, temporarily inflating European tablet growth before subsequent destocking cycles
8.
Early warning signal: Investors must monitor the net inflow of new adult patients as a vital leading indicator for long-term tablet volume growth
9.
Uncomfortable side effects: Patients frequently discontinue sublingual tablets due to local adverse events, including throat irritation and mouth itching
10.
US commercial execution: Direct US commercialization carries high execution risk as ALK must fund its own sales force to build the tablet market
11.
Other side of the story: The risks are highly overstated because ALK's core business lacks patent-cliff risks, ensuring unusually stable product lifecycles relative to large pharmaceutical peers
