Section 3 – What the company does 22 findings

Section summary
  1. Valeo holds unrivalled market leadership in high-growth ADAS and lighting segments, while transforming its powertrain business to capture tripled content per vehicle in electrified architectures.

Division and Product Segments

1.
Favourable Power segment: The Power division, representing 50.00% of sales, provides the entire range of thermal and powertrain solutions for conventional and electrified vehicles
2.
Leading hybrid technologies: Within Power, the portfolio spans from 48V mild-hybrid systems, where Valeo is the global leader, to 800V high-voltage silicon carbide inverters
3.
Integrated thermal systems: The thermal sub-segment offers battery thermal systems and immersive battery cooling, which are critical to maintain range and battery life
4.
Advanced Brain division: The Brain division, accounting for 24.00% of sales, is the global leader in Advanced Driver Assistance Systems, equipping over one-quarter of vehicles
5.
Centralized software architecture: Brain is leading the software-defined vehicle revolution by developing liquid-cooled central compute units that merge multiple electronic control units
6.
Differentiated Light segment: The Light division, generating 26.00% of sales, designs innovative exterior adaptive lighting and interior intuitive headlamps to improve road safety
7.
Integrated sensor cleaning: Light also designs specialized driving assistance sensor cleaning systems, combining Valeo's global leadership in wipers and automated sensors

Revenue Model and Value Chain

8.
Contractual original equipment: Revenue is primarily earned through long-term original equipment manufacturer contracts, where Valeo is nominated to supply parts for a platform
9.
Predictable revenue stream: Original equipment contracts provide multi-year revenue visibility once series production launches, making forward sales trends highly predictable
10.
Resilient aftermarket business: Valeo Service provides a high-margin, less cyclical aftermarket revenue stream representing 10.00% of total group sales to independent networks
11.
Aging vehicle benefits: The aftermarket segment benefits from the increasing number and age of vehicles on the road, supporting stable, non-cyclical profitability
12.
Volatile miscellaneous sales: Tooling and customer research and development contributions make up 7.00% of sales, including one-off contract cancellation compensations
13.
High value positioning: Acting as a master system integrator, Valeo sits in a profit-favoured position to capture higher content per vehicle
14.
Shortened development cycles: Collaborative partnerships with tech giants like Qualcomm help share development costs and shorten product time-to-market

Customer Concentration and Moats

15.
Concentrated customer base: The customer base is highly concentrated, with the top two automaker clients representing 28.00% of sales and top five representing 49.00%
16.
High platform exposure: Customer concentration exposes the business to individual platform decisions, platform cancellations, and localized regional demand swings
17.
Durable switching costs: Moats are created by deep integration into automaker software architectures and long-term development cycles, preventing mid-cycle supplier replacement
18.
Formidable intellectual property: Competitive advantages are guarded by one of the largest patent portfolios in France, filing 1,419 patents in 2025
19.
Oligopolistic industry structure: The auto parts industry is a highly consolidated, concentrated oligopoly where global players compete on system cost
20.
Fierce peer competition: Major listed peers like Continental and Forvia maintain comparable global scale and platform exposure in key regions
21.
Other side of the story: Automaker clients possess massive countervailing buyer power, allowing them to impose demanding price reduction targets or delay programs
