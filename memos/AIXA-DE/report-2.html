Research Plan

This memo provides a comprehensive analysis of Aixtron SE, a critical player in the semiconductor capital equipment sector, focusing on its transition from a volatile LED-heavy history toward a diversified future anchored in AI-driven optoelectronics and wide-bandgap power electronics. The next 12 to 24 months represent a pivotal "bridge" period where near-term weakness in Silicon Carbide (SiC) is expected to be offset by a structural surge in Indium Phosphide (InP) demand for AI data centers.

"Aixtron is the dominant supplier of Indium Phosphide MOCVD tools required in the manufacturing of lasers which are currently seeing a significant demand pool as the AI datacenter increases the use of optical/fibre based communications. We see a significant need to add Indium Phosphide capacity in the coming years as data center investments grow massively while optical interconnects continue to take share from copper. We are less certain Aixtron will see continued growth beyond a high '27 base, which we discuss in more detail inside."

Brief Company Descriptions

Aixtron SE is the global technology leader in Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD) equipment, which is used to deposit thin layers of compound semiconductor materials onto wafers.

The company operates a focused business model targeting high-growth segments including Power Electronics (GaN and SiC), Optoelectronics (Lasers, Micro LEDs), and Specialty LEDs.

Revenue is primarily derived from the sale of deposition tools (equipment revenue) and a growing after-sales service business, which accounted for 20% of total revenue in 2025.

Aixtron operates globally with a presence in approximately 10 countries, including key manufacturing and R&D hubs in Germany, the UK, and Italy, and extensive sales/service networks across Asia and the USA.

Historically, the company was heavily reliant on the LED market, but over the last three years, it has pivotally expanded into Wide-Bandgap power electronics to serve the electric vehicle and renewable energy sectors.

The company’s technology enables the production of energy-efficient devices that reduce power conversion losses by up to 40% compared to traditional silicon.

Aixtron’s core competitive advantage lies in its Planetary Reactor technology, which offers superior uniformity and high-throughput for volume manufacturing.

The current product flagship is the G10 system family, introduced between 2022 and 2023, which includes dedicated tools for SiC, GaN, and AsP (Arsenide Phosphide) applications.

Geographically, revenue is highly concentrated in Asia, which represented 66% of FY24 sales, followed by Europe (24%) and the Americas (10%).
