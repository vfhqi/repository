2. KEY FINDINGS - Verdict: FY2024 delivered MTLN-GB's strongest reported financials since the Metlen rebrand, with revenue and EBITDA both reaching records, but elevated debt and an unresolved Greek-regulatory overhang cloud the forward trajectory [#J]

  • Beat summary: Revenue of ~€5.7bn beat the company's own FY2023 baseline (+~18% YoY); EBITDA reached approximately €1.35bn, consistent with or modestly above analyst direction; EPS improvement driven by operating performance rather than buybacks [#A]

  • Key surprise: The pace of metals/aluminium earnings recovery — Aluminium of Greece (AoG) swung from near-breakeven to a meaningful positive EBITDA contributor — exceeded what most public commentary had anticipated entering 2024 [#J]

  • Guidance posture: Management reiterated a medium-term EBITDA target in the range of €1.4–1.6bn by FY2026, implying modest further growth from FY2024's base; no formal per-annum EPS guidance issued [#A]

  • Leverage concern: Net debt rose to approximately €2.8–3.0bn (ND/EBITDA ~2.0–2.2x) reflecting ongoing capex on energy transition assets and the Metka Industrial capex cycle; not yet distress-level but meaningfully above FY2022 [#A]

  • Structural positive: The energy segment's shift from pure merchant power toward regulated/contracted revenues (RES capacity additions + grid services in Greece) provides a more visible earnings floor than L3Y history implied [#J]

  • Investment implication: FY2024 results strengthen the qualitative thesis (diversified industrial-energy platform, management execution) but do not resolve the two key quantitative questions — RES ramp timing and aluminium spread sustainability — that determine whether 25%+ IRR is achievable [#J]

  • Sceptical flag: Both the aluminium recovery and the Greek energy market tailwind are partly macro/commodity-driven; if LME aluminium prices or Greek power spreads revert, the FY2024 record will prove cyclical rather than structural [#I]
