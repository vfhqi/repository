EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Puig Brands SA stands at an inflection point following the termination of transformational merger discussions with Estée Lauder in May 2026, reverting to a standalone organic trajectory anchored by niche fragrance scaling and Charlotte Tilbury geographic expansion , while confronting normalizing premium beauty demand and channel inventory rebalancing across makeup. The company delivered €5.04 billion in FY25 net revenues with a

historical double-digit fragrance growth to mid-single-digit levels.

The Puig family retains 93% of total voting rights through Class A shares, insulating management from activist pressure while maintaining strategic autonomy over capital allocation, M&A discipline, and brand portfolio pruning. The March 2026 CEO transition separated executive and chairman roles, with José Manuel Albesa assuming operational leadership after 28 years within the company, while Marc Puig continues as Executive Chairman leading strategic M&A and family culture governance.

External forces reveal a bifurcating premium beauty landscape where high-end niche fragrances maintain structural pricing power, but legacy mainstream portfolios face severe market share erosion from agile indie competitors leveraging viral dupe culture and digital-first commerce, while supply chains confront material tariff exposure from centralized European manufacturing footprints and escalating regulatory compliance costs for allergen labeling and sustainable packaging mandates.
