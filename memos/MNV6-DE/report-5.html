§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: Margins compressed sharply in FY 2024 as commodity normalisation hit supply-segment economics; regulated-segment margins held; compression appears cyclical but elevated capex will structurally pressure FCF margins through FY 2026 [#J]

  • Gross margin: Estimated ~12–14% in FY 2024 vs ~15–17% in FY 2023 and ~11–13% in FY 2021 (pre-crisis baseline); FY 2024 returns close to pre-crisis normalised levels, suggesting the compression is mean-reversion not structural deterioration [#A]

  • Sub: for context, E.ON reports blended gross margins ~15–17% and RWE supply segment ~8–10%; Mainova's ~13% midpoint is sector-appropriate

  • Gross margin drivers: Commodity supply margin compression (-3 to -4pp) partially offset by district heating volume uplift (+0.5pp) and network tariff stability (+0pp, held flat); net ~-3pp [#A]

  • EBIT margin: FY 2024 adjusted EBIT margin approximately 5.5–6.5% vs FY 2023 ~7.2% and FY 2022 ~8–9% (crisis peak); FY 2021 pre-crisis was ~5–6% — again, FY 2024 is a return to normalised baseline [#A]

  • Sub: the pre-crisis baseline comparison matters for thesis framing; this is not margin collapse, it is normalisation

  • Margin direction: Contracting in FY 2024 vs FY 2023; but the direction vs FY 2021 is flat-to-stable, confirming cyclical rather than structural compression [#A]

  • Operating leverage: ❌ Negative operating leverage in FY 2024 — revenue fell faster than costs; fixed cost base (network maintenance, staff, depreciation) is sticky; OpEx ratio (costs/revenue) rose approximately 3–4pp YoY, consistent with utility fixed-cost structures but unhelpful for margin recovery [#A]

  • D&A trend: Depreciation rising ~8–10% YoY as the energy-transition capex programme (€250–280m guided FY 2025) adds to the asset base; D&A set to continue rising through FY 2026–27 as new grid/heating infrastructure depreciates [#A]

  • Sub: RWE and E.ON similarly see rising D&A from green capex; sector-wide pattern

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A and operating costs as % of revenue increased ~3–4pp in FY 2024 vs FY 2023; primarily mechanical (fixed costs on declining revenue base) not structural cost creep [#A]

  • One-off costs: Restructuring provisions ~€8–12m in FY 2024 (workforce rationalisation and IT transformation); management excluded from adjusted EBIT; these charges have appeared in each of the last 3 years, raising the question of whether they are truly one-off [#I]

  • Sub: cumulative 3-year restructuring charges ~€25–35m; if treated as recurring, adjusted EBIT overstates true earnings by ~€8–12m p.a.

  • Adjusted vs reported: Adjusted/reported EBIT gap ~€3–5m net in FY 2024 (restructuring charges partially offset by asset disposal gains); modest; not a material distortion at current scale [#A]

Sceptical bullet: The "cyclical not structural" margin read depends on commodity prices stabilising at current levels; if gas/electricity prices decline further in FY 2025–26 (European gas glut risk, renewable oversupply), supply-segment margins could compress below FY 2021 pre-crisis levels for the first time, testing the regulatory floor's ability to sustain overall EBIT
