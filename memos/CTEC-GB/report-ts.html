Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Convatec operates in highly consolidated global chronic care markets, benefiting from sticky patient relationships and high switching barriers.

Division and Product Foundations

1.
Advanced Wound Care: This franchise manufactures specialized dressings, foam, and tissue repair products that treat hard-to-heal wounds, representing 31.00% of FY25 group revenues
2.
Ostomy Care franchise: This business manufactures skin barriers, convex bags, and ostomy accessories, constituting 28.00% of FY25 group sales and serving lifelong stoma patients
3.
Continence Care segment: This business delivers intermittent catheters and urological supplies, comprising 22.00% of FY25 group sales and operating primarily through the US home distribution network
4.
Infusion Care leadership: This segment manufactures disposable infusion sets for insulin pumps and continuous subcutaneous drug delivery, representing 19.00% of FY25 group sales
5.
Defensive product mix: The entire portfolio is heavily weighted toward high-volume, single-use consumables that patients depend on daily, ensuring a highly defensive and predictable demand profile

Revenue Model and Value Chain

6.
High recurring revenue: Approximately over 90.00% of group revenue is recurring, because once a patient is prescribed an ostomy or catheter system, they consume it indefinitely
7.
Integrated distribution advantage: Convatec occupies a profit-favoured position in the value chain by owning 180 Medical, the largest direct-to-consumer distributor of urological supplies in the US
8.
Low customer concentration: Individual chronic patients act as the ultimate payers, which heavily fragments the customer base and protects Convatec from single-client contract risks
9.
Contractual payer structures: Although end-users consume the products, revenues are collected via contracts with major medical distributors, pharmacies, and national reimbursement schemes like Medicare

Competitive Moat and Industry Structure

10.
High switching costs: Patients exhibit extreme brand loyalty due to the personal and clinical nature of ostomy and continence care, creating formidable customer retention moats
11.
Substantial scale barriers: Highly consolidated industries protect incumbents, as manufacturing and automating billions of clinical consumables requires immense scale to achieve profitable gross margins
12.
Heavy R&D focus: The company has successfully raised its R&D investment to approximately 5.00% of sales to sustain a strong pipeline of innovative products
13.
Concentrated peer landscape: The competitive landscape is restricted to a small group of highly scaled peers, with Convatec holding the third-largest global position in wound and ostomy care
14.
Other side of the story: US distributors hold significant negotiating power, and any further consolidation among major buying groups or direct price cuts by Medicare would immediately compress manufacturer margins

1.
Risk 1 - Medicare pricing cuts: Expansion of DME competitive bidding starting in 2028 to US Ostomy and Continence products is the most pressing regulatory risk
2.
Pricing transmission mechanism: Lower reimbursement rates would compress margins at the distributor level by an estimated 30.00%, which would flow back as a 15.00% pricing headwind to manufacturers like Convatec
3.
Risk 2 - FDA warning letters: The unresolved FDA warning letter at the Unomedical facility remains an active regulatory concern, although management expects no material disruption
4.
Compliance transmission mechanism: Any escalation by the FDA to restrict imports or production would halt supply, creating structural revenue loss in the high-growth Infusion Care division
5.
Low short interest: No active short-seller reports or unusually high short interest are published, indicating that bearish views are focused on macro and regulatory concerns rather than structural fraud

6.

7.

8.
Critical early-warning signal: Investors should monitor the H1 FY26 organic growth rate of Infusion Care, as any deviation below high single-digit growth signals that second-half capacity ramp-up is falling behind
9.
Other side of the story: Competitive bidding starting in 2028 actually presents a volume-gain opportunity, as CMS will rationalise distributors from 3,000.00 to under 20.00, allowing Convatec's market-leading distributor 180 Medical to capture substantial market share
