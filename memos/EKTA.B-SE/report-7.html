1. EBITA Margin Expansion

Definition

EBITA (Earnings Before Interest, Tax, and Amortisation) margin is the percentage of net sales converted to operating profit before tax and interest. For Elekta, the 24-month driver is recovery from 11.6% (FY2024/25 full-year adjusted) toward a midterm target of 14%+ and eventual 18–20% target. This is mechanically driven by (a) absolute cost reductions via restructuring programs, (b) gross margin improvement through pricing and product mix (software, services, higher-ASP products like Unity), and (c) operating leverage on growing sales. Management has committed to cost programs totalling SEK 500M+ run-rate savings beginning Q1 2026/27 (new operating model) following delivery of SEK 279M in FY2024/25.

Why It Drives Share Price: EBITA margin is a direct EPS lever. On a base of SEK 18bn net sales (FY2024/25 run rate), a 300 bps EBITA improvement (11.6% → 14.6%) adds ~SEK 540M to operating profit before tax, translating to ~20–25% EPS growth independent of revenue growth. At 2–3x forward EV/EBIT multiples (medtech), this margin re-rating can drive 40–60% share price appreciation over 24 months.

### Company Guidance & Public Communications

- Cost Program Guidance (FY2024/25): Elekta achieved SEK 264M run-rate savings by end Q3 2024/25, ahead of SEK 250M target. Full-year FY2024/25 achieved SEK 279M, driven by headcount reductions, footprint consolidation, and supply-chain improvements.
- Forward Cost Program (FY2025/26 onwards): Management announced a new operating model aiming for SEK 500M+ yearly cost savings with full effect starting Q1 2026/27. This is a significant escalation from the prior program, indicating sustained management focus on structural cost reduction.
- Margin Targets: Management explicitly reiterated guidance for "EBIT margin of 14% and higher beyond the current fiscal year," supported by price increases, product launches (Evo, Versa Reflex), and volumes.
- Gross Margin Ambition: Stated goal to move gross margin toward pre-pandemic levels (43–45%, vs. current 40.3% adjusted FY2024/25), implying 250–300 bps gross margin improvement potential.

Credibility Assessment: High. Management has delivered on prior cost targets (279M vs 250M committed). The SEK 500M forward target is material but realistic given company size (~SEK 18bn sales). However, it represents ~3.3% of current revenue, a significant number requiring sustained discipline.

4. Software/Services Mix Improvement

Definition

The mix driver is acceleration of SaaS penetration and recurring revenue as % of total sales (target 50%+ recurring by FY2027/28). Monaco and MOSAIQ are transitioning from perpetual license to subscription models, enabling 5–10 year recurring revenue contracts.

Why It Drives Share Price: Recurring revenue (subscriptions, maintenance contracts) has three economic benefits: (1) Higher margins (40–50% gross margin for software/services vs. 38–40% for hardware), (2) More predictable earnings (lower churn, multi-year contracts), (3) Higher valuation multiples (SaaS businesses trade at 8–12x EV/Revenue vs. 4–6x for hardware). A 5% shift in mix (from 42% to 47% Services) with service margin at 45% vs. hardware at 38% could add 35 bps to blended gross margin. At EBIT level, software/SaaS has 50–60% EBIT margin (vs. 14–16% blended), so even small mix shifts have leverage.

MOSAIQ (oncology informatics/EHR software) is similarly shifting to cloud-based subscription (MOSAIQ Plaza cloud offering).
- Service Contracts: Multi-year service contracts with hospitals are at 5–10 year terms, with 40%+ gross margins due to remote monitoring, parts logistics, and software upgrades bundled in.
- Cloud Platform: Elekta ONE (unified cloud platform) is new offering combining MOSAIQ, Monaco, and real-world outcomes analytics. Positioned as 10-year subscription.

Credibility Assessment: High. Industry trend (Varian, Philips, GE all shifting to recurring models) is structural.
