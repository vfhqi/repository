EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Solvay SA has emerged from its December 2023 demerger as a simplified, commodity-focused essential chemicals group operating two primary divisions–Basic Chemicals (62% of sales) and Performance Chemicals (38%)–across five global technology platforms including soda ash, peroxides, specialty silica, fluorine, and Latin American solvents. The company commands a global #2 position in soda ash with 19% capacity share and global #1 leadership in sodium bicarbonate with 16% market share within a highly consolidated oligopolistic structure where the top five ex-China producers control 70% of capacity. Customer concentration risk is exceptionally low , with no single buyer representing more than 10% of group revenue and top-ten customers historically accounting for less than 15% of sales collectively. 1

- Analyst/Investor Customer lock-in is structurally secured through high technical specification barriers , with Solvay's soda ash featuring 15-20% less iron impurities than typical market offerings and customers willingly paying up to $60 extra per container for this high-purity material critical to premium glass manufacturing. Contract structures enhance pricing visibility, with soda ash sales split 50% on annual contracts, 20-30% on multi-year agreements, and 20% on spot markets, while Solvay sells directly to end-users avoiding volatile third-party trading channels. Automatic energy pass-through mechanisms cover the majority of group sales with natural gas surcharge clauses activating above €50 per megawatt-hour, though a maximum four-month indexation lag exposes margins to temporary compression during sudden European TTF gas price spikes.
