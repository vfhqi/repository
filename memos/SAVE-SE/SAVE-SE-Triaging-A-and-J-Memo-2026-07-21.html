B.1 Business Description 9 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business: the largest pan-Nordic digital savings and brokerage platform, not a single-market challenger. Nordnet serves 2.43m active customers across four countries (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland) with SEK 1,214bn in savings capital under custody as of March 2026, more than 2x Avanza's Sweden-only customer base
2.
Revenue mix: near-even split between rate-sensitive and activity-sensitive income. 57% net commission income (brokerage, fund distribution, currency exchange) and 43% net interest income (NII) on deposits and lending; the balance shifted in Nordnet's favour in 2025 as commission income grew 27% while NII declined
3.
Geographic structure: genuinely diversified across four monetary and regulatory jurisdictions, expanding to a fifth. Sweden contributes the highest revenue-per-customer but only ~27% of group revenue on a trailing basis (down from 33% in FY2024); Norway, Denmark and Finland collectively carry the growth; Germany (H2 2026 launch) is the fifth market and the single largest strategic catalyst
4.
Financial character: a high-margin platform compounder with material NII sensitivity. Adjusted operating margin reached 70% in Q1 2026 (ahead of Avanza's 68%), full-year 2025 adjusted operating income was a record SEK 5,389m, and adjusted ROE was 42% in 2025 (41% Q1 2026 RTM) -- but the earnings base is geared to four separate central-bank rate cycles simultaneously
5.
Capital structure: well-capitalised but with a binding leverage constraint. CET1 ratio 18.4%; total capital ratio 22.4% versus a 15.7% requirement; leverage ratio compressed to 4.5% in Q1 2026 from 5.1% in Q4 2025 (upper bound of the 4.0-4.5% target corridor) as uncontrolled deposit inflows mechanically compress the binding metric
6.
Investment style: platform compounder with a live growth-versus-value-chain-risk tension. Fits the "D2C digital savings platform" archetype with recurring inflows, high retention (97.3% in 2025) and genuine network-effect elements (the Shareville social-trading community, 507,000-523,000 active users); the tension is whether commission-income growth can structurally replace NII as the earnings engine before the next down-cycle
7.
Cohort positioning: the outright growth leader, not the outright quality leader. Nordnet leads Avanza and flatexDEGIRO on customer growth (+12% vs +8% vs company-specific), assets-under-custody growth (+30% vs +22% vs +32%), and operating margin (70% vs 68% vs ~47.8% EBITDA margin), but trades at the cohort's highest forward multiple (20.0x local P/E; DNB Carnegie's own comparison puts Nordnet at 22.6x 2026e versus Avanza's 19.2x)
8.
CEO Rasmus Järborg (effective 1 April 2026): internal promotion from Chief Product Officer (since 2018) and Deputy CEO (since 2021); prior career at SEB (10+ years) and UBS London (9 years); designed the platform's product strategy that drove competitive differentiation since 2018 -- a product-led, technically literate profile, distinct in character from Avanza's more institutional/administrative CEO Gustaf Unger
9.
Germany is the single largest strategic catalyst and the single largest execution risk simultaneously. BaFin passporting confirmed January 2026, launch guided for H2 2026, opex SEK 80-90m in 2026 rising to ~SEK 100m by 2028, breakeven guided for 2029, entering a market with no structural equivalent to the Nordic ISK/ASK tax-wrapper moat and against entrenched incumbents (Trade Republic ~8m customers, Scalable Capital)
