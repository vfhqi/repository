§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: Mixed pattern — not a sandbagger; management guides at stated ambition, not conservatively; beats are macro-driven not cushion-driven; misses are execution/timing-driven [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: Absent for RES segment — management does not habitually set low bars; investor day presentations consistently lead with ambitious capacity-addition schedules that subsequently slip [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal: Partially present for distribution segment only — RAB-regulated return with URE means management knows distribution EBITDA trajectory 12-18 months ahead; conservative guidance here is structurally easy [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal: Moderate for offshore/greenfield milestones — Bałtyk Phase 1 original commercial operation date has slipped at least twice in public communications; each slip was framed as "minor delay" [#J]

  • Mixed pattern: Group-level guidance record looks acceptable on surface (3 beats in 4 years) but masks systematic over-ambition on construction timelines and a dependence on power-price tailwinds for headline beats [#J]

  • Pattern shift: Pre-FY21 (limited public data) management had a simpler business (mainly distribution); post-FY21 RES scale-up introduced construction-phase complexity that management has not yet demonstrated it can guide with precision [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Group beats averaged +10-12% above guidance midpoint in FY21-22; strip power-price contribution and underlying capacity-delivery beat was +2-4% [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: RES segment misses averaged -15% vs guidance midpoint in FY23-24; distribution beats offset ~30% of that at group level [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: No evidence of structural improvement; FY24H1 miss is the worst recent RES segment outcome; management has not changed its guidance methodology [#J]

  • Deteriorating accuracy: The increasing weight of offshore/greenfield in the business mix means guidance accuracy will deteriorate further as a structural feature unless management introduces explicit offshore timeline conditionality [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the apparent "cushion" from distribution EBITDA stability is shrinking as a share of group EBITDA; by FY27 offshore will be 30-40% of group EBITDA (management plan), meaning the historically-unreliable segment dominates — structural accuracy deterioration is baked in to the business mix shift [#J]
