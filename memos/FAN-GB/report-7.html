§8 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The dominant bearish risk is a correlated multi-driver failure — if UK RMI stalls (Driver #1), margins fail to recover (Driver #5), and M&A multiples rise (constraining Driver #2), all three large-impact drivers underperform simultaneously [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers #1 (RMI recovery) and #5 (margin expansion) are highly correlated — margin recovery is structurally contingent on volume recovery; these are not independent bets [#J]

  • Sub: If RMI stalls, Drivers #1 and #5 both deliver near-zero; combined EPS contribution drops to zero from modelled ~20-30%

  • Driver independence: Drivers #2 (M&A), #3 (regulation), and #4 (international) are largely independent of UK RMI cycle — providing a genuine floor to the thesis [#J]

  • Sub: Even in the bear case (UK RMI stuck), these three drivers still contribute ~10-15% EPS CAGR, which supports current valuation but delivers ~15-20% IRR, not 25%+

  • Correlation across drivers: The macro scenario of "UK rates staying higher for longer" would hit Driver #1 (RMI via affordability), partly constrain Driver #4 (NZ/Nordic if global rates stay elevated), and increase debt costs for Driver #2 (M&A leverage) [#A]

  • Sub: Single rate-environment scenario can simultaneously impair three drivers; this is the key correlation risk in the thesis

  • Cumulative plausibility: Assigning: Driver #1 = 55% (medium), #2 = 80% (high), #3 = 80% (high), #4 = 65% (medium), #5 = 60% (medium, contingent on #1), #6 = 40% (low-medium) [#A]

  • Sub: Joint probability of all 6 delivering at full base case = approximately 55%×80%×80%×65%×60%×40% = ~5-6% probability of full delivery — illustrating why independent-driver framing matters

  • Sub: More usefully: probability of achieving 25%+ IRR (requires ~3 of the top 4 drivers to deliver) is estimated at ~35-45%
