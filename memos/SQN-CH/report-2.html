SECTION 1 — THE SNB RATE CYCLE & NIM TRANSFORMATION

1.1 SNB Rate History and 2026-2027 Outlook

The Swiss National Bank maintained negative policy rates for nearly eight years starting in 2015, only exiting the sub-zero regime in September 2022 to reach a peak of 1.75% during the 2022-2024 hiking cycle.

As of May 2026, the SNB has concluded its cutting cycle and holds the policy rate steady at 0.00%. Forward-looking consensus for 2026 suggests the SNB will remain at a "hold" position for the remainder of the year, with Maybank forecasting the first potential 50bps of hikes toward a 0.50% floor in 2027. This stable-to-rising rate outlook in 2027 is supported by anchored medium-term inflation expectations and a nominal effective exchange rate that remains near historical highs, providing a floor for interest income normalisation.

[AS·Broker]: "Nevertheless, we expect safe-haven properties of the CHF to remain intact amid what we see to be a highly uncertain external environment that would be naturally supportive of the CHF amid two-way risks. We see the SNB holding rates steady at 0% in 2026 and pencil in 50bps of hikes to 0.50% in 2027."
— Maybank Investment Banking Group, 4 May 2026 | Saktiandi Supaat | "Macro research - 2026, Issue 4: Sticky Inflation, Fragile Growth and More Volatile Dollar Cycle"

[C]: Rate path context — The SNB trajectory matters asymmetrically for Swissquote. The hiking cycle (Sep 2022 – Sep 2023: -0.75% → +1.75%) delivered the single largest structural financial change of the past decade, adding an estimated CHF 100-150m of annual NII that was absent in the zero-rate era. The cutting cycle back to 0.0% by March 2025 partially reverses this windfall. The Maybank 2027 hike scenario (+0.50%) would be incrementally constructive. The tail risk — SNB returning to negative rates on aggressive CHF appreciation — remains a plausible but non-base-case scenario.

1.2 NIM Inflection and NII Sensitivity

The transition from negative to positive rates served as a massive earnings inflection; Swissquote's Net Interest Income skyrocketed from roughly CHF 114.4 million in the 2022 transition year to a peak of CHF 217.6 million in 2025. As of year-end 2025, Swissquote's internal sensitivity analysis quantifies the impact of a ±150bps parallel shift in CHF rates:

  • Upward Shift (+150bps): Net interest income increases by CHF 52.2 million.
  • Downward Shift (-150bps): Net interest income decreases by CHF 24.0 million.

This asymmetry — where the upside from hikes is nearly double the downside from cuts — reflects Swissquote's proactive treasury management and the presence of "sticky" client deposits that allow the bank to lower deposit rates faster than reinvestment yields fall.

[AS·Broker]: "Between FY20 and FY24, customer deposits grew by 67% and loans by 111%, allowing Swissquote to continue expanding interest-bearing assets even as policy rates decline."
— Deutsche Bank Research, 6 Aug 2025 | Miriam Killian | "Swissquote: Steady as she grows - initiating with buy"

[C]: NII asymmetry is analytically significant — this +150bps / -150bps asymmetry (+CHF 52m vs -CHF 24m) is not standard for a neobroker; it reflects the banking licence's ability to manage repricing on both the asset and liability side. Peer neobrokers without banking licences (Avanza, Nordnet, flatexDEGIRO for brokerage activities) have no analogous NIM lever. This asymmetry is a structural quality differentiator relative to the cohort.

1.3 Rate Scenario Analysis and EPS Implications

Broker analysis from Jefferies and Deutsche Bank highlights three potential paths for the SNB cycle through 2027:

  • Base Case (SNB at 0.0% to 0.5%): NII is expected to reach approximately CHF 190-210 million, as deposit and loan growth (which grew 67% and 111% respectively between 2020-2024) counterbalances lower yields. [AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank Research, 6 Aug 2025.
  • Bear Case (Back to Zero/Negative): A return to a sub-zero environment would likely trigger a 10-15% decline in NII, though Swissquote retains the "mitigation measure" of reinstituting negative interest charges on large client balances, a strategy used effectively prior to 2022. [AS·Broker]: Jefferies Research, 27 Sep 2024.
  • Bull Case (Rate floor >0.5%): If the SNB hikes more aggressively in 2027 to 1.0%+, Swissquote's high-margin interest income could see a CHF 30-50 million tailwind, potentially driving EPS 10-15% above 2026 estimates. [AS·company]: Annual Report 2025.

[C]: ❌ Weak signal — NIM + crypto revenue concentration. NIM + crypto together represent approximately 60-65% of revenues — both are cyclical or rate-sensitive. Revenue quality sits below Avanza and Nordnet, which carry more recurring commission-based income relative to total revenue. However, the NIM "floor" at zero rates is structurally higher than the zero-rate 2021 baseline due to AuC growth: even at 0.0% SNB, lombard lending spread (~2-3% above SNB rate), structured product margin, and FX bid-offer sustain a positive NIM contribution on the enlarged balance sheet.

1.4 Deposit Base and Cash Proportions

[AS·company]: Swissquote's client assets are characterised by a "mass-affluent" profile, with average assets per account reaching CHF 117,000 in 2024. Approximately 12.1% of total client assets (CHF 9.2 billion out of CHF 76.3 billion) were held in cash as of recent reporting, a proportion that tends to be lower and more stable than Nordic peers like Avanza or Nordnet, who often see higher cash drag during market downturns. This "invested" nature of the customer base makes Swissquote less sensitive to rapid cash withdrawals during rate cuts compared to discount brokers serving high-frequency retail traders.

[C]: Cohort comparison — Average AuC per client of CHF 117,000 (2024 actuals) is significantly above Avanza and Nordnet (estimated CHF 40-60k/client). This affluent-skew is confirmed by low cash as a proportion of total assets (12.1% vs typically 20-25% for mass-market platforms in rate-cut environments). Fewer clients, higher value — this is a deliberate positioning choice with structural NIM implications.

1.5 NIM Sustainability Floor

[AS·company]: Management targets a structurally stable pre-tax profit margin of 55% by 2028, implying that even at a 0.0% SNB rate, the platform has reached sufficient scale to sustain high profitability. Analysts believe NII has likely "normalised" at the CHF 210-220 million level as of 2026, which provides a durable earnings floor for the company to finance continued international expansion in Luxembourg and Singapore.

SECTION 2 — CRYPTO: SECULAR TAILWIND OR VOLATILE NOISE?

2.1 Crypto Revenue History and Correlation

Crypto-related income has evolved from a niche offering to a material revenue driver, representing 13% of total revenue in 2024 (CHF 85.5 million) before moderating slightly to 12% in 2025 (CHF 85.7 million). While the share price remains highly correlated with the Bloomberg Galaxy Bitcoin Index, the underlying financials show that crypto revenue is driven by volume rather than just price; in late 2024, a doubling of Bitcoin's market cap to over USD 100,000 drove a 353% surge in Swissquote's annual crypto income.

[C]: Crypto revenue context — the 2021 peak was estimated at CHF 80-90m; the 2022 FTX contagion trough reduced this to ~CHF 30-40m (a ~50-60% fall); the 2024 recovery to CHF 85.5m confirms near-full cycle recovery. The April 2024 Bitcoin halving is a structural positive for 2025-2026 crypto volumes given historical 12-18 month post-halving bull patterns. However, crypto revenues are inherently binary — a full bear market can compress this line by 50-60% within 12 months.

2.2 The FINMA Crypto Banking Licence Moat

Swissquote is one of the few global institutions to operate its own crypto exchange while holding a full FINMA banking licence. This dual-status provides a significant moat, enabling:

  • Bankruptcy Remoteness: Client crypto assets are legally segregated and protected under the Swiss "DLT Law," a feature unregulated platforms cannot legally guarantee. [AS·company]: Annual Report 2025.
  • Integrated Services: Swissquote offers staking, custody, and trading on 26+ cryptocurrencies directly through its banking app, facilitating seamless "fiat-to-crypto" transfers that attract institutional-grade clients. [AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank Research, 6 Aug 2025.

[C]: FINMA moat stress-tested under live conditions — in November 2022, Swissquote publicly confirmed zero exposure to FTX, BlockFi, and Celsius. FINMA custody requirements prevented co-mingling of client assets. This was a genuine moat test under maximum stress and Swissquote passed. This base-rate is rare in the crypto custody landscape and directly enhances post-FTX institutional client confidence. Peer platforms without banking licences (Coinbase EU, Kraken) cannot replicate this segregation mechanism at the same legal certainty level.

[C]: Crypto product depth — Swissquote offers 250+ cryptocurrencies, staking, and crypto lending. This breadth exceeds most regulated EU/Swiss peers. Avanza and Nordnet offer crypto via ETPs only; flatexDEGIRO has minimal crypto exposure. Swissquote's direct custody is a category differentiator. Obtaining a Swiss banking licence from scratch takes 3-5 years and CHF 50-100m+ capital commitment — this is a hard structural moat against new entrant crypto natives.

2.3 SQX Crypto Exchange and Competitive Positioning

Launched in September 2022, the SQX crypto exchange aggregates liquidity from multiple venues to provide retail and institutional clients with optimised execution. Trading volumes reached CHF 15 billion in 2024, positioning SQX as a legitimate European competitor to major players like Coinbase and Kraken, particularly for "sophisticated" European investors who prioritise Swiss regulatory safety over lowest-cost trading.

[C]: The CHF 15bn SQX 2024 trading volume figure is notably larger than what most regulated-bank crypto venues report. The combination of Swiss DLT Law asset segregation, banking-licence lombard lending integration, and broad asset coverage creates a platform that crypto natives structurally cannot replicate without a banking licence — and Coinbase Switzerland holds only a Swiss VASP registration (2023), not a full banking licence.

2.4 Institutional Adoption as Structural Tailwind

[AS·company]: The 2024 approval of Bitcoin ETFs in the US and the subsequent rise of institutional crypto interest have transitioned crypto from a speculative retail asset to a strategic pillar for Swissquote. Institutional crypto custody and trading now represent roughly 27% of Swissquote's total B2B/B2B2C revenue mix, with institutional assets under custody rising as firms seek regulated Swiss storage solutions post-FTX.

[C]: ⚡ Outlier — crypto institutionalisation secular inflection. US Bitcoin spot ETF approved January 2024; BlackRock IBIT reached USD 20bn AuM in 40 days (fastest ETF launch in history). EU institutional investors (pension funds, insurance companies) are beginning to allocate to crypto via regulated vehicles. Swissquote's FINMA custody + multi-asset platform positions it as a preferred Swiss custodian for institutional crypto allocation — a 5-10 year secular tailwind as institutional AuC in crypto grows from a near-zero base. This optionality is likely not in consensus estimates.

2.5 Regulatory Evolution: MiCA and FINMA

[AS·Broker]: The implementation of the EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation in 2024-2025 is viewed by Deutsche Bank as a net benefit rather than a threat. While MiCA allows EU competitors like Fineco or Trade Republic to more easily obtain crypto-asset service provider status, Swissquote's early-mover advantage and the "Swiss trust premium" are expected to maintain its dominance among HNWI in markets like Luxembourg. Meanwhile, FINMA issued new guidance (01/2026) on the custody of crypto-based assets to further tighten requirements, a development Swissquote is well-prepared for given its 25% capital ratio.

[C]: MiCA is a double-edged sword structurally — it raises the compliance bar across EU crypto industry, levelling the playing field upward in favour of regulated incumbents like Swissquote over unregulated crypto natives, while simultaneously creating a pathway for better-capitalised EU banks to enter the regulated crypto custody space. The FINMA Category 3 reclassification (see Section 5.2) shows regulatory burden is rising with scale — net regulatory vector is positive (moat deepening) but not costless.

2.6 Crypto Bear Market Risk and Natural Hedge

[AS·Broker]: Research suggests that a 50%+ drop in Bitcoin prices acts as a "one-year earnings event" rather than a structural impairment. During the 2022 crypto winter, Swissquote's crypto income fell 73%, yet the bank still reported a pre-tax profit margin of 38.6% due to the surge in interest income. This natural hedge — where risk-asset volatility often coincides with rate spikes — limits the downside of crypto cyclicality.

[C]: ⚡ Critical caveat on the natural hedge. The 2022 concurrent offset (crypto revenue -73%, NIM windfall from rate hikes) was partly fortuitous timing. With SNB now at 0.0%, a future crypto bear cycle would not benefit from the same simultaneous NIM windfall — the hedge is structurally weaker in a low-rate environment. This is a key IG-stage analytical risk: the 2022 resilience narrative may overstate forward resilience if rates do not rise concurrently with the next crypto bear episode.

SECTION 3 — EFOREX & FX BUSINESS DYNAMICS

3.1 eForex Revenue Trends

eForex revenue, primarily derived from leveraged FX and CFD trading, has been stable but under pressure due to a low-volatility environment in 2024-2025. Revenue from this segment decreased 3.8% YoY to roughly CHF 94 million in 2025, though management noted a significant shift in late 2025 toward precious metals like gold, which helped sustain overall activity levels.

[C]: eForex context — this segment grew from ~CHF 20m (2019) to ~CHF 70-80m range (pre-2025 estimates) as a B2B FX prime brokerage infrastructure business; however the AS data suggests the picture is more nuanced than the pure B2B growth narrative, with leveraged retail FX under regulatory and volume pressure. The pivot toward non-leveraged FX conversion revenue (see 3.2 below) is strategically coherent.

3.2 Customer Mix and the Non-Leveraged Pivot

Swissquote competes with pure-play FX brokers like Saxo Bank and IG Group but targets a more affluent "buy-and-hold" customer rather than day traders. While eForex is characterised as "leveraged FX," Swissquote has pivoted toward "non-leveraged" trading income (conversion of currencies for securities trading), which grew 52.6% in 2025 to CHF 119.5 million and is viewed as a more sustainable long-term revenue stream.

[AS·Broker]: "Swissquote has now published its full 2H25 results, following selected financials pre-released on January 16. Pre-tax profit was in line with the pre-release, while net revenues came in 1% ahead of the implied 2H25 consensus (+6% YoY), supported by stronger net interest income, eForex, and trading, partly offset by weaker crypto income decreasing 15% against a strong 2H24."
— Deutsche Bank Research, 20 Mar 2026 | Jonathan Jayarajan | "European Daily Focus: Friday, 20th March 2026"

[C]: The non-leveraged FX conversion pivot (+52.6% in 2025) is analytically important — it suggests Swissquote is successfully migrating its FX revenue base toward a more recurring, less speculative revenue stream (FX conversion tied to multi-currency investment activity), rather than relying on leveraged retail trading volumes. This is consistent with the "digital private bank" strategic direction and improves revenue quality. eForex B2B infrastructure (white-label FX for third-party banks and brokers) remains a differentiated B2B revenue stream absent from Avanza, Nordnet, and flatexDEGIRO. FX prime brokerage is relationship-intensive with high switching costs once integrated into counterparty infrastructure.

[C]: ❌ Weak signal — eForex B2B segment opacity. Swissquote does not disclose sufficient segmental margin data on the B2B eForex unit for investors to price it correctly. The opacity on B2B economics is a valuation discount factor. The 2015 CHF shock (SNB EUR/CHF floor removal) caused a one-time H1-2015 loss from short-CHF client positions — a reminder that eForex B2B carries tail FX risk exposure that is not fully visible from outside.

3.3 CHF Volatility and SNB Policy

The Swiss Franc's safe-haven status periodically boosts FX volumes, as seen during the 2022 rate pivot and the 2024 geopolitical volatility. The SNB's readiness to moderate "excessive appreciation" in the CHF creates two-way currency risks that incentivise high-volume trading among Swissquote's institutional clients.

[C]: CHF strength is a dual-effect macro factor — positive for Swiss client purchasing power and maintained consumer confidence; negative for international clients denominating wealth in EUR/USD who see relative cost increase of CHF accounts. With the SNB at 0.0% and NEER near historical highs, intervention risk is real but two-way FX volatility structurally supports eForex volumes.

3.4 Structural Outlook for Retail FX

Management considers the eForex business "stable" but acknowledges that it is a "highly regulated business that is in the process of being cleaned up" by European and Swiss regulators. Retail traders are increasingly diversifying into crypto and fractional equities, meaning eForex may represent a shrinking portion of the total revenue pie as the bank evolves into a broader wealth platform.

SECTION 4 — COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS

4.1 Yuh JV and Full Consolidation

In July 2025, Swissquote acquired the remaining 50% stake in Yuh from PostFinance, bringing the mobile-first brand fully under its control. Far from cannibalising the parent, Yuh serves as a "top-of-funnel" customer acquisition engine for younger, mass-market savers, boasting 399,201 accounts and CHF 3.65 billion in client assets as of year-end 2025.

The consolidation added approximately CHF 10 million in revenue to 2H25 results and allowed Swissquote to book a one-time gain of CHF 49.8 million from the revaluation of its original 50% stake. Total Yuh acquisition cost: CHF 90 million.

[C]: Yuh strategic logic clarified by the acquisition. The [C] framing of Yuh as a potentially cannibalistic "capture the bottom of market" strategy is superseded by the AS evidence: Yuh was already generating CHF 1.7 million profit in 2024 and is transitioning from a customer acquisition cost drag to a profit contributor. Full consolidation converts Yuh from a JV to a controlled top-of-funnel — directing mass-market demand (sub-CHF 10k AuC) to Yuh while Swissquote captures the affluent migration upward. The CHF 90m acquisition cost for 399k accounts + CHF 3.65bn AuC implies a client acquisition cost of ~CHF 225/account, reasonable by European neobroker standards.

[Analytical disagreement] [C] view: Yuh may cannibalise Swissquote's lower-end client base. [AS] view per Jefferies/Deutsche Bank: Yuh is a top-of-funnel acquisition engine, already profitable in 2024 (CHF 1.7m PTP), and the full acquisition aligns incentives cleanly. Verdict: AS evidence (2024 Yuh profitability, July 2025 acquisition completion) supersedes the earlier [C] concern — the cannibalisation risk was real during the JV phase but the acquisition resolves it.

4.2 Competitive Threats in Switzerland

Swissquote maintains a 3% market share in Switzerland, leaving significant room to capture assets from legacy institutions like UBS and the cantonal banks. While Saxo Bank and Interactive Brokers compete on pricing, Swissquote's "Swiss-regulated" brand and integrated banking services (mortgages, debit cards) allow it to maintain pricing power, particularly among the mass-affluent who hold larger portfolios.

[C]: ❌ Weak signal — IBKR competitive threat. Interactive Brokers is the most credible single competitive threat — global platform, institutional-grade tools, competitive pricing; directly targets Swissquote's active trader segment. IBKR opened its Swiss entity in 2019 and has been steadily growing. However, IBKR lacks a Swiss banking licence and cannot offer lombard lending, NIM products, structured notes, or crypto custody alongside trading — limiting its competitive overlap to the active trader segment rather than the affluent wealth management segment.

[C]: Traditional Swiss bank digital upgrades (PostFinance, ZKB, cantonal banks) create substitutes for basic brokerage but not for Swissquote's multi-currency, multi-asset, crypto-inclusive platform depth. The competitive moat is real for the existing 2026 platform, but platform parity is rising — as cantonal banks invest, the "all-in-one Swiss regulated platform" narrative may compress in value over 5+ years. This is a watch item, not an acute risk at IG stage.

4.3 EU Expansion via Luxembourg

This entity is critical for targeting expatriates and sophisticated European investors who seek an alternative to domestic banks, with 17% of group income already generated outside Switzerland.

[C]: The Luxembourg entity (formerly Internaxx, acquired 2019, renamed Swissquote Bank Europe) provides a dual strategic function: (a) EU passport for MiCA-compliant crypto services and investment products, and (b) a regulated hub for EU-resident HNWI seeking Swiss-supervised custody with EU-passported legal framework. Post-Brexit environment also creates opportunity via Swissquote's London-EU hub structure. The 17% non-Swiss revenue share is still low but growing — international diversification is strategically coherent and derisks Switzerland-specific regulatory or rate risk.

This "trust premium" is evidenced by the record CHF 8.5 billion in NNM collected in 2025, a trend analysts expect to persist as investors prioritise solvency and digital maturity.

[C]: ⚡ Outlier — Swiss financial centre post-CS repositioning. This tailwind should moderate as CS-specific wealth rotation completes, but the brand halo effect on "FINMA-regulated, not CS" client acquisition persists.

SECTION 5 — REGULATORY & STRUCTURAL CHANGE

5.1 MiCA Implementation

Full MiCA implementation in 2025 has standardised crypto regulation across Europe. For Swissquote, this simplifies the acquisition of EU-based clients through its Luxembourg subsidiary, as the MiCA framework aligns with Switzerland's own "DLT Law" and FINMA's stringent requirements.

[C]: MiCA raises the compliance bar across EU crypto industry — levelling the playing field upward in favour of regulated incumbents like Swissquote over unregulated crypto natives. MiCA compliance may be achievable via the Luxembourg entity for EU-resident clients. The "Swiss trust premium" + Luxembourg EU passport is a differentiated positioning that pure Swiss-only or pure EU-only competitors cannot replicate easily.

5.2 FINMA Bank Re-classification

[AS·company]: Due to its rapid balance sheet expansion (exceeding CHF 17 billion), Swissquote is transitioning from a "Category 4" to a "Category 3" bank under FINMA's classification. While this move among Switzerland's largest banks brings more onerous disclosure and capital requirements, Swissquote's current 25.0% capital ratio is already far above the 11.2-12.0% minimums required for either category.

[C]: FINMA Category 3 reclassification is a double-edged regulatory development — it adds compliance and disclosure cost (est. CHF 5-10m/year additional regulatory technology and staffing) but simultaneously signals that Swissquote has grown into a systemically relevant Swiss institution, reinforcing the "FINMA-regulated" trust premium for institutional and HNWI clients. The 25.0% capital ratio provides substantial headroom — excess capital above a 17% CET1 ratio expected to exceed CHF 500m by 2026.

5.3 Swiss-EU Regulatory Equivalence

Post-Brexit and in the absence of a comprehensive Swiss-EU institutional agreement, Switzerland remains "non-equivalent" in several areas of financial services. Swissquote mitigates this by routing EU operations through its Luxembourg banking licence, ensuring it remains "future-proofed" against potential regulatory frictions between Bern and Brussels.

5.4 Fund Kickback Reform and Robo-Advisory

[AS·company]: Swiss law (FIDLEG) has moved closer to MiFID II standards regarding the disclosure of fund retrocessions (kickbacks). Swissquote has proactively shifted its Invest Easy and robo-advisory offerings toward low-cost ETFs and passive themes trading to minimise reliance on kickback revenue, with "Invest" products now representing 11% of total asset class exposure.

[C]: LEON/Invest Easy robo-advisory penetration remains modest (11% of total asset class exposure; AuM est. CHF 200-400m in LEON) — peer Nordnet's robo-advisory penetration is ~3-5% of client AuC. After 8 years post-launch, LEON has not become a material standalone revenue contributor. The FIDLEG retrocession reform is structurally positive for Swissquote's low-cost positioning but limits fee expansion in the advisory segment.

5.5 Swiss DLT Act and Tokenised Assets

[AS·company]: The Swiss DLT Distributed Ledger Act (2021) creates a legal framework for tokenised securities. SIX Digital Exchange is already issuing tokenised bonds.

[C]: Tokenised asset custody is a first-mover opportunity for Swissquote — traditional private banks are moving slowly; crypto natives lack banking licences to custody equity tokens. If RWA tokenisation reaches 5-10% of global bond market in 5-10 years (BlackRock scenario), Swissquote's licensed custody infrastructure could be a key beneficiary. This optionality is not in consensus estimates at IG stage and should be flagged as a medium-term non-linear opportunity rather than a near-term earnings driver.

SECTION 6 — FINANCIAL CHANGE TRAJECTORY

6.1 Revenue and CAGR Outlook

[AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank forecasts a revenue CAGR of approximately 9% over the 2024-2027 period, outpacing the 7% consensus. This growth is driven by:
- Commissions: Expected to grow in line with NNM inflows and wallet share gains.
- NII: Projected to remain resilient at ~CHF 200m+ due to loan book expansion.
- Crypto: Modelled with conservative growth post-2025 high-water marks, yet retaining upside optionality.

2028 company targets: CHF 950m revenue, CHF 500m PTP, 53% margin.

[C]: Revenue trajectory context — 2020-2024 CAGR was approximately 17% (peer-leading: Avanza +12%, Nordnet +10%, flatexDEGIRO +8%). The Deutsche Bank 9% forward CAGR (2024-2027) represents a deliberate moderation as the NIM windfall normalises. The 2028 CHF 500m pre-tax target implies ~50% earnings growth from the 2024 base and is achievable via: (1) NIM maintenance at ~1.5-2.0% on growing AuC, (2) continued crypto revenue recovery and institutionalisation, (3) eForex B2B scale and non-leveraged FX growth. Key risk: if crypto enters another bear cycle, this target could slip by 2-4 years.

6.2 Margin and Operating History

[AS·company]: Swissquote's pre-tax profit margin expanded significantly from 48.1% in 2023 to 58.1% in 2025. Compared to Nordic peers like Avanza, which operates with PTP margins often in the 70% range due to a purely digital domestic model, Swissquote's lower margin reflects its heavier investment in international banking infrastructure and its diverse workforce where 36% are engineers.

[C]: The 48.1% → 58.1% margin expansion in two years is a strong operating leverage signal. During the NIM windfall period (2022-2024), management did not significantly increase costs — cost/income ratio improved from ~55% (2021) to ~45% (2023), with incremental revenues mostly falling to profit rather than being consumed by cost expansion. This is a positive management quality signal. The gap vs Avanza (~70%) reflects Swissquote's deliberate multi-jurisdiction banking infrastructure investment, not an operational efficiency gap per se.

6.3 Capital Generation and Dividends

[AS·company]: Swissquote maintains a policy of returning approximately 30% of net profit to shareholders via dividends. For 2025, management proposed a dividend of CHF 7.00 per share (+23% YoY). With excess capital above a 17% CET1 ratio expected to exceed CHF 500 million by 2026, analysts suggest that in the absence of material M&A, the group could consider its first major share buyback programme.

[C]: The 30% payout ratio is higher than the earlier CHF 4.00/share (2024 est.) narrative — the CHF 7.00/share (2025) vs CHF 4.00/share implies actual dividend acceleration. The potential buyback is an incremental capital return catalyst not widely discussed. Asset-light model (no physical branch network); capex ~CHF 20-30m/year; FCF conversion ~90%+ of reported earnings — consistent with Avanza's similarly high FCF profile.

6.4 AuC Trajectory

[AS·company]: Total client assets reached CHF 88.7 billion at year-end 2025 (+16.3% YoY), supported by record NNM of CHF 8.5 billion. With the total number of accounts rising to 1.16 million, the platform is benefiting from an acceleration in client acquisition that management believes will drive AuC toward the CHF 100 billion milestone by 2027.

[C]: AuC trajectory — CHF 35bn (2020) → CHF 57bn (2024 est.) → CHF 88.7bn (2025 actual) implies accelerating growth, with the 2025 jump (+16.3% YoY) notably above prior trend. CHF 100bn by 2027 is a credible milestone.
