  • Organic growth: Regulated network revenue grew in the low-single-digit % range YoY, broadly consistent with RAB inflation-linkage and asset base expansion [#A]

  • Germany regulatory revenue grew with the Regulatory Asset Base (~3–4% underlying); UK regulated revenue grew per RIIO-ED2 framework allowances [#A]

  • Segment trajectory — Networks: Networks EBIT is the core growth engine; trajectory is stable-to-improving as capex-funded RAB growth compounds [#A]

  • Cash conversion: OCF/adj. EBITDA ~80–85%; acceptable for a capital-intensive regulated utility; peer regulated utilities (National Grid, Enel, Iberdrola) typically 75–90% [#A]

  • No material deterioration in cash conversion signalled [#A]

  • Capex: ~€7.0bn capex in FY2024 — record high and up from ~€5.5bn in FY2022; capex/revenue ~8.3% on reported revenue; capex/EBITDA ~82% [#A]

  • Capex growth is deliberate: E.ON is executing on the largest network investment programme in its history to build out the RAB [#A]

  • Growth capex dominates; maintenance capex is a minority [#J]

  • Free cash flow: FCF (post-capex) ~€0–0.5bn in FY2024; essentially zero after the record capex year [#I]

  • Dividend is funded by operating cash flow before full capex commitment; this is standard practice for regulated utility investment cycles but increases balance sheet leverage [#I]

  • Net debt: Net debt ~€41–42bn by end-FY2024; up from ~€38bn in FY2022; trending upward [#I]

  • Net debt/adj. EBITDA ~4.8–5.0x; within management's stated comfort range (~5x) but at the upper boundary [#I]

  • Peer regulated utilities (National Grid, Enel) operate at similar leverage ratios; E.ON is not an outlier on this metric in sector context [#A]

  • Dividend coverage: Dividend policy: 5–10% annual DPS growth through 2027; FY2025 DPS guided €0.55 (confirmed) [#A]

  • Dividend growth is funded by regulated earnings growth, not FCF surplus; model depends on regulatory returns keeping pace with leverage cost [#I]

  • Buyback pace: No share buybacks; capital entirely directed to network investment + dividend [#A]

  • Capital return sustainability: Dividend sustainable at current regulated return levels; would come under review if net debt/EBITDA consistently exceeds 5.5x [#I]

  • Management has stated commitment to investment-grade credit rating; Moody's/S&P BBB range maintained [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: ❌ FCF after capex is essentially zero; dividend is structurally funded by debt rather than organic FCF surplus; rising interest rates increase the cost of this carry, potentially compressing the equity return; if the RAB-funded revenue uplift lags the capex cycle by 2–3 years (which is structurally the case), leverage peaks before regulated returns recover [#I]
