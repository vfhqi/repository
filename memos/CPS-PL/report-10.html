§6 — Counter-Arguments to Bull Case

J-front: The most credible bear rebuttal targets the "deleveraging flywheel" bull thesis; if FCF is structurally lower than stated, the entire re-rating case collapses [#J]

  • Bull thesis #1: "Integrated convergent model (telco + TV + broadband) creates sticky subscriber base and cross-sell upside" [#J]

    • Bear rebuttal #1: Convergence bundling has not demonstrably protected ARPU in Poland; competing mobile-only and broadband-only players (Play/PLAY SA, Netia, Orange PL) offer aggressive price competition
    • Sub-bullet: ARPU growth across disclosed KPIs has been flat to +1-2% YoY — below Polish CPI of ~5-10% over 2022-2024, implying real ARPU erosion
    • Sub-bullet: European convergence peers (BT/EE, Proximus, Swisscom) have similarly found convergence bundling to be a churn reducer but not a pricing power generator

  • Bull thesis #2: "Deleveraging pathway is credible; FCF will grow and debt-to-EBITDA will fall to ~2.5x by 2026-27" [#J]

    • Bear rebuttal #2: FCF after capex and interest is materially thinner than EBITDA implies; PLN 4.5bn EBITDA minus ~PLN 2.7bn D&A minus ~PLN 1.8bn capex minus ~PLN 0.8bn net interest = FCF of ~PLN 0.7-1.0bn — barely covers dividends and leaves minimal debt reduction
    • Sub-bullet: Bears argue that the deleveraging case requires either (a) capex reduction below reinvestment requirements or (b) EBITDA growth that is not visible in subscriber data
    • Sub-bullet: Peer anchor: BT Group deleveraging thesis was similarly made in 2018-2020; net debt barely moved due to pension obligations, capex, and interest burden

  • Bull thesis #3: "Polsat Box content assets (sports rights, Polsat Plus sports channels) are undervalued and represent hidden value" [#J]

    • Bear rebuttal #3: Sports rights are depreciating assets requiring constant renewal at escalating cost; Ekstraklasa (Polish football league) rights inflation and UEFA competitions costs are growing faster than advertising revenue
    • Sub-bullet: OTT platforms (Canal+, Viaplay) are competitive bidders for Polish sports rights, eroding Polsat's traditional exclusive position
    • Sub-bullet: Linear TV sports viewership is declining even in Poland; reach numbers for Polsat Sport channels have shown YoY decline per disclosed audience data

  • Bull thesis #4: "Management has demonstrated operational discipline and cost control since the Polkomtel acquisition integration" [#J]

    • Bear rebuttal #4: The Solorz family governance dispute reveals management succession risk and structural governance weakness that cost-cutting metrics do not capture
    • Sub-bullet: A CEO/controlling shareholder whose family is in public legal dispute over corporate assets is a management distraction risk with no easy quantification
    • Sub-bullet: Track record evidence: Polkomtel acquisition (2011) was executed at peak telco valuations; leverage has remained elevated for >12 years, suggesting the "integration complete" narrative has been told before

  • Bull thesis #5: "Polish macro tailwinds (wage growth, consumer confidence recovery post-2022) will support ARPU and subscriber growth" [#J]

    • Bear rebuttal #5: Real wage growth benefits MVNOs and OTT services more than incumbent integrated operators; rising disposable income is more likely to fund Netflix/Disney+ subscriptions than incremental Polsat Box upgrades
    • Sub-bullet: Competitive evidence: Iliad/Play SA has been the primary beneficiary of Polish mobile market growth; CPS-PL's mobile market share has been stable-to-declining

  • Sceptical bullet (BULL rebuttal): The weakest bear rebuttal is #5 (macro); Polish macro tailwinds are real and the integrated operator does benefit from household service bundling as incomes rise — the bull is probably right that macro helps, even if the magnitude is modest
