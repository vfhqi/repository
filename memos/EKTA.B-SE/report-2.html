[C] Elekta AB — IG — Change Forces @ 16-Apr-26

1. Brief Company Description

What it does: Elekta AB is a Swedish medical technology company specializing in precision radiation therapy and radiosurgery systems for the treatment of cancer and brain disorders. The company designs, manufactures, and markets linear accelerators (linacs), MRI-guided radiotherapy systems, treatment planning and delivery software, and oncology information systems to hospitals, cancer centres, and research institutions globally.

Why and how: Radiation therapy accounts for approximately 50% of cancer treatments globally. Elekta addresses hospital and cancer centre demand for increasingly precise, adaptive treatment modalities through integrated hardware-software solutions that combine imaging, planning, and delivery in unified systems. The company operates through two main segments: (1) hardware (linacs, Gamma Knife radiosurgery units, imaging systems), sold as capital equipment with multi-year lifecycles; (2) software and services (MOSAIQ oncology information system, Monaco treatment planning, Elekta ONE SaaS platform, recurring maintenance and support), delivered increasingly on subscription and software-as-a-service (SaaS) models.

Revenue streams (FY2024/25): Total revenue approximately SEK 16.3 billion (circa USD 1.55 billion). Breakdown: Hardware approximately 52% (linacs, Gamma Knife, imaging), Software & Services approximately 48% (recurring licences, cloud, maintenance, training, managed services). Within software, approximately 48% of sales now delivered on recurring subscription basis, up from ~35% three years prior.

Geographic mix (FY2024/25): North America 42%, EMEA 28%, APAC 30%. North America remains heavily concentrated: >40% of Elekta's revenue derives from US market; Medicare reimbursement changes therefore create material operating leverage.

Key products:
- Unity MR-Linac: Proprietary 1.5 Tesla MRI-guided linear accelerator; flagship product combining real-time MRI imaging with radiation therapy; >80 units ordered to date; ~500 published abstracts, 350+ peer-reviewed articles; differentiated from Varian/Siemens offering.
- Versa HD: Workhorse external beam platform; full modulation and stereotactic capabilities; latest versions with enhanced adaptive imaging (XVI 5.0.7); 2024 launch of Evo CT-Linac variant for computed tomography guidance.
- Leksell Gamma Knife: Stereotactic radiosurgery system; ~360 systems globally; >2 million patients treated to date; market leader with 3x more published articles than all competitors combined; recurring revenue from software and maintenance.
- Monaco TPS: Treatment planning software; increasingly cloud-based; embedded in Elekta ONE integrated platform.
- MOSAIQ: Oncology information system ; ranked #1 in KLAS 2024 Best in Services and Software report; multidisciplinary workflow backbone; >6,000 hospital/cancer centre installations; driving SaaS transition and recurring revenue.
- Elekta ONE: Unified SaaS software environment launched 2023; consolidates MOSAIQ, Monaco, contouring, planning, and AI modules; multi-vendor compatible (supports non-Elekta hardware); strategic pivot toward platform stickiness and recurring revenue.

8. Value Chain — Competition

Competitive structure: Duopoly-like market with Varian/Siemens Healthineers (~52% global linac market share, >55% installed base) and Elekta (~42% market share). Varian's 2018 acquisition by Siemens created unparalleled scale and integration (diagnostic imaging + linac + software ecosystem).

Varian / Siemens Healthineers advantages:
- Integrated ecosystem: Single-vendor imaging (CT, MRI, ultrasound) + linac + treatment planning + OIS; large hospital systems value simplicity and accountability from single vendor.
- Scale economies: >USD 2.8B revenue in radiotherapy; fixed costs distributed across larger installed base; pricing power in developed markets.
- Software moat: Varian's integrated software environment (Eclipse treatment planning, Aria OIS) is sticky; hospitals reluctant to rip-and-replace for competing equipment.
- Geographic breadth: Strong in North America (USA, Canada); European presence significant but secondary to Elekta.
- R&D scale: Larger R&D budget supports faster innovation cycles and broader product roadmap.

Elekta competitive advantages:
- MRI-guided radiotherapy leadership: Unity MR-Linac is differentiated; Varian's MR-Linac is less mature; this is a material competitive edge in advanced AMCS and research centres.
- Stereotactic radiosurgery: Leksell Gamma Knife market leadership (360 units, >2M patients treated, 3x more published articles than competitors); Varian has no equivalent SRS platform; high-margin niche.
- Oncology software ecosystem: MOSAIQ ranked #1 in KLAS 2024; strong workflow integration; Elekta ONE SaaS platform emerging as competitive alternative to Varian's Aria.
- Emerging market presence: Strong Sinopharm JV in China; earlier entry into India/Thailand vs Varian; positioned for APAC growth.
- Agility: Smaller scale enables faster product iteration and customer-centric development vs Siemens bureaucracy.

Secondary competitors (niche / emerging):
- IBA (Belgium): Proton therapy specialist; weak in linac market; growing but smaller scale (~USD 0.9B).
- ViewRay: MR-guided linac pioneer; smaller installed base (~20–30 units); Elekta partnership (9.9% stake option) suggests Elekta views ViewRay as potential acquisition target or technology partner rather than competitive threat.
- RaySearch Laboratories (Sweden): Treatment planning software; smaller footprint; less integrated than Elekta/Varian offerings; risk of consolidation/acquisition (private equity, other vendors).
- Accuray : CyberKnife radiosurgery; smaller market; niche positioning.

Competitive response intensity:
- Varian/Siemens: Recent moves include emphasis on integrated imaging+linac solutions; cloud-based software transition; aggressive pricing in mature markets (US, Western Europe) to defend share against Elekta's MRI-linac uptake.
- Elekta: Recent strategy emphasizes software-as-a-service and recurring revenue (Elekta ONE); geographic expansion (China, India); MRI-linac ramp and marketing.

Barriers to entry:
- High: Radiotherapy equipment requires 5–10 year regulatory approvals (FDA, EMA, PMDA); clinical validation (peer-reviewed publications, case series); hospital network relationships and service infrastructure.
- Technical: Linac engineering is complex; supply chain for key components (RF sources, magnets, accelerator tubes) concentrated among few suppliers (Elekta, Varian, IBA); new entrants face long development cycles.
- Switching costs: Hospitals lock into software ecosystems (MOSAIQ, Aria); hardware replacement driven by multi-year capital plans; once installed, linac remains in use for 5–10 years.

Assessment: Elekta is in defensible #2 position vs Varian/Siemens duopoly. Competitive threats are: (1) Varian/Siemens integrated offerings winning over hospital system consolidation (large AMCs prefer single-vendor), (2) Proton therapy growing faster than linac market (risk of longer-term demand deflection), (3) Smaller competitors (RaySearch, ViewRay) potentially gaining share in niche segments. Elekta's competitive advantages (MRI-linac, SRS, software ecosystem) are real but not sufficient to gain share in North America (Varian dominance too strong). Competitive strategy should focus on defending EMEA/APAC share and winning in niche segments (MRI-linac, SRS, emerging markets).

Change rating: ORANGE — Competitive intensity stable at duopoly level; Varian/Siemens retains structural advantage (integrated ecosystem, scale). Elekta's MRI-linac differentiation is material but serves smaller addressable market. Risk: if Varian/Siemens launches credible MR-linac product, Elekta's differentiation erodes. Opportunity: software-as-a-service transition (Elekta ONE) may create switching friction and recurring revenue defensibility that hardware alone does not provide. Monitoring: Varian product announcements, customer case studies (who is buying MRI-linac vs Varian), software adoption rates (Elekta ONE vs Aria).

9. Value Chain — Disruption

Proton therapy threat (structural):
- Market growth: Proton therapy market CAGR 13.3% (2025–2030) vs linac CAGR 10.4%; proton therapy growing 3.3 percentage points faster than traditional photon linac market.
- Clinical drivers: Lower radiation dose to healthy tissue; superior for paediatric cancers, cardiac-adjacent tumours, and certain CNS malignancies; increasing clinical consensus on outcomes vs photon therapy.
- Economics barrier: Proton centres cost 2–3x more to build (300–500M USD) and operate (higher staffing, maintenance); limited insurance coverage outside US (Medicare covers select indications). Adoption in Europe, APAC slower than US.
- Elekta exposure: Elekta has proton therapy offering (Radiance 330 synchrotron), but market share in proton much smaller than in linac (~5–10% est. vs 42% in linac). Major competitors: IBA (Belgium), Hitachi, SHI Group, Mevion. Elekta's proton presence is non-core.
- Revenue risk: If proton therapy adoption accelerates in next 5–10 years (FDA approvals, insurance coverage expansion, cost reduction), Elekta's linac-centric business model faces structural headwind. Current addressable market for Elekta's photon linac: ~USD 3–4B globally; proton growing from smaller base (USD 0.6B → USD 2B+ by 2034, per market research).
- Mitigation strategy: Elekta is investing in adaptive linac technology (MRI-linac, real-time imaging) to improve outcomes on photon side, narrowing clinical advantage of proton. Also pursuing proton partnerships/acquisition opportunities, but not a core strategic focus.

AI and machine learning disruption (emerging but significant):
- Contouring automation: AI-driven automated organ/tumour delineation reducing manual planning time 30–50%; reduces inter-observer variation; faster treatment planning.
- Dose prediction: Machine learning models predicting 3D dose distributions faster and more accurately than traditional algorithms; accelerating treatment planning cycles.
- Adaptive radiotherapy: AI enabling real-time plan adaptation during treatment (MRI-linac, CT-guided linac systems); improving outcomes in real-time vs traditional static plans.
- Elekta innovation: Elekta ONE Planning includes AI modules; partnerships with academic centres (Netherlands Cancer Institute, Oulu University Hospital) on AI research; patent portfolio in machine learning for dose prediction and adaptive workflows.
- Competitive risk: Varian also investing heavily in AI (Eclipse planning, Aria workflow); both vendors racing to embed AI into treatment planning and delivery pipelines. Risk: commodity AI (open-source, generic neural networks) reduces differentiation; vendors that can train, validate, and certify AI models faster will gain competitive edge.
- Revenue opportunity: AI modules represent new software revenue stream (SaaS modules, per-case licensing); higher margin than traditional software.

MRI-linac technology disruption (moderate positive for Elekta):
- Clinical advantage: Real-time soft tissue visualization during treatment enables adaptive radiotherapy; improves targeting precision; potential for dose escalation on tumours while sparing normal tissue.
- Market adoption: >80 Unity MR-Linac orders to date; Varian MR-Linac entering market but less mature; clinically proven advantage narrowing. Adoption limited by cost (Unity costs ~3–4x standard linac) and technical complexity (magnet engineering, workflow integration).
- Addressable market: Estimated 500–1,000 MRI-linac units globally by 2030 (vs 10,000+ standard linac market); niche but higher-margin segment.
- Elekta advantage: Clear technology leadership; clinical publication advantage; installed base creating software lock-in (MOSAIQ, Monaco).
- Risk: If MRI-linac adoption slows (cost barriers, lack of reimbursement clarity), revenue upside deflates. Also, Varian's larger installed base enables faster technology adoption if MRI-linac becomes clinical standard.

Software / SaaS disruption (structural positive for Elekta):
- Trend: Shift from capital equipment (linac hardware) to software-centric revenue models (SaaS, recurring licensing, cloud-based analytics) is underway globally in medical devices.
- Elekta position: MOSAIQ leads KLAS rankings; Elekta ONE platform consolidates software ecosystem; recurring revenue currently ~48% of software sales, target >50% by FY26/27.
- Margin benefit: Software-as-a-service typically 60–70% gross margin vs 40–50% hardware; switching costs higher (workflow integration); revenue more predictable.
- Risk: Generic cloud platforms (AWS, Microsoft Azure) could commoditize oncology software; open-source treatment planning (e.g., TPS-like algorithms in academic repositories) could reduce Elekta's software pricing power.

Regulatory / reimbursement disruption (significant near-term):
- Medicare payment cuts : 3% reduction in advanced RT CPT codes effective Jan 2026; potential for further cuts pending Congressional action (MPFS review). Risk of 5–10% additional reductions if advanced linac procedures classified as lower-complexity procedures.
- NICE / EMA approval timelines: Regulatory pathway for advanced linac technologies (MRI-linac, adaptive RT) is clear but lengthy (2–3 years); not a disruptive threat but delays UK/EU adoption.
- EU procurement reform: Movement toward centralized EU procurement (vs member-state fragmentation) could benefit large vendors (Siemens/Varian) at the expense of niche players (Elekta). Risk moderate but worth monitoring.

Supply chain disruption (low risk, current mitigated):
- Component availability: RF components, magnets, accelerator tubes from concentrated supplier base; COVID-era shortages largely resolved; near-term supply risk low.
- Geopolitical: US-China tensions could disrupt Elekta's Sinopharm JV (China manufacturing) or Elekta China sales (US component restrictions). Current risk low but non-zero.

Assessment: Disruption threats are multiple and real: (1) proton therapy structural shift away from linac (3.3 bps CAGR advantage; long-term risk to Elekta's core business), (2) AI/ML in treatment planning (near-term opportunity for software differentiation if Elekta executes; risk if Varian moves faster), (3) MRI-linac adoption curve steeper than expected (opportunity for Elekta's Unity; risk if adoption plateaus), (4) Medicare reimbursement cuts (near-term demand headwind). Software-as-a-service transition is Elekta's primary hedge against hardware margin compression and proton/AI disruption.

Change rating: ORANGE — Proton therapy structural growth (13.3% CAGR) is longer-term threat to linac business model; AI/ML is near-term opportunity if Elekta executes faster than Varian; Medicare payment cuts are immediate near-term headwind. MRI-linac adoption is key leading indicator of Elekta's ability to defend share against disruption.

10. Value Chain — Political and Regulatory

Healthcare funding trends (macro context):
- Western Europe / EMEA: Government healthcare budgets facing pressure from ageing populations and chronic disease costs; radiotherapy typically receives stable funding (cost-effective vs surgery), but capex cycles are elongating (5–7 years vs 3–5 years historically).
- USA: Medicare/Medicaid represent ~45% of US healthcare spending; recent trend toward value-based care (outcome-based reimbursement) rather than volume-based.
