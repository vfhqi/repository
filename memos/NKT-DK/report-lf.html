EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 11 findings

Claes Westerlind represents a credible operator-track CEO with pristine technical credentials , holding an MSc in Mechanical Engineering from Chalmers University and accumulating over 15 years of uninterrupted focus on high- voltage DC power cables across ABB and NKT .

His elevation to CEO in May 2023 represents a clean internal succession, rewarding direct P&L ownership of NKT's most critical high-voltage manufacturing operations .

Since appointment, Westerlind has delivered rapid revenue expansion reaching €2.49bn in 2024 with 26% organic growth and expanded operational EBITDA margins from 9.9% to 13.8% as previous capacity investments yielded substantial volume leverage .

The business achieved record €344m operational EBITDA in 2024 and Q1 2026 posted the highest quarterly order intake in company history at €4.2bn , validating execution capability during an unprecedented industry boom.

3 5 , this stellar financial delivery masks severe credibility fractures that materially threaten the investment thesis . A €300m Karlskrona high-voltage expansion capex overrun critically undermined the CEO's conservative reputation, with DNB Carnegie explicitly flagging this 40% cost blowout as the "first scratch" in management's highly conservative reputation .

Active Central European antitrust probes in Slovakia and the Czech Republic proposing fines, combined with ongoing legacy cartel litigation alongside Nexans, indicate persistent compliance fractures . A massive 142% year-over-year spike in internal whistleblower complaints in 2025, alongside surging temporary employee turnover to 75%, signals severe operational strain during the rapid capacity scale-up . These execution failures and governance weaknesses represent the core bear case against an otherwise technically proficient operator.

1.
CEO Quality and Operator Credentials: Westerlind follows a pure operator track with deep technical roots, offering a stark contrast to previous generalist leadership at NKT

He successfully executed the "Charging Forward" strategy transition, completed the NKT Photonics divestment to achieve pure-play status, and acquired Portuguese manufacturer SolidAl for €192m at an attractive 7.0x EV/EBITDA multiple with full synergy realization confirmed by Q4 2025 .

Management demonstrated consistent execution on the Karlskrona high-voltage factory expansion milestones and secured an unprecedented €13.5bn transmission order backlog by Q1 2026, providing immense long-term revenue visibility versus peers .

His communication style balances professional transparency with authentic personal reflection, though he explicitly acknowledges constraints by telling analysts he will be "a little bit vague" regarding certain execution details .

Prior ABB tenure from 2006 to 2017 provides deep domain expertise, but the absence of standalone divisional P&L ownership means capital-allocation discipline remains untested, and earlier success may merely reflect riding ABB's broader corporate turnaround .

2.
Capital Allocation Discipline and Shareholder Returns: The CEO maintains a deeply reinvestment-heavy posture prioritizing high-voltage capacity expansions, leading to negative €175m free cash flow in Q2 2025 despite record

EBITDA generation and no dividends paid since 2016 . Share buyback execution remains minimal, with only 31,000 treasury shares repurchased in 2024 down from 150,000 in 2023, as capital is diverted to 2027 capacity expansions .

Despite operational delivery, Prysmian and Nexans outperformed NKT by approximately 70 and 25 points respectively over the last 1.5 years on a total shareholder return basis , indicating the market penalizes the zero-return capital allocation approach.

While relentless high-ROIC reinvestment into capacity expansion exists, zero opportunistic buybacks during stock weakness limits direct shareholder value creation . This allocation philosophy may prove prescient if capacity utilization materializes as projected, but it currently sacrifices near-term returns for uncertain long-term market share gains. 25

3.
Board Independence and Governance Structure: Exactly five of nine total board members are classified as independent, meeting basic Danish standards but lacking a heavy independent majority, while Chair Jens Due Olsen is non-independent due to his 20-year tenure since 2006

The crucial Audit Committee is chaired by independent director Anne Vedel and supported solely by other independent members, though the Nomination Committee includes the non-independent Chair despite being chaired by independent director Karla Lindahl .

Board equity ownership is highly skewed with Chair Olsen holding 51,891 shares representing 85% of total board equity, while four independent AGM-elected directors hold zero shares, signaling weak financial alignment versus peers .

The stock maintains 100% free float with no controlling shareholder, major holders include BlackRock at 5.26% and ATP at 5.5%, and no activist investors currently hold board seats or are executing public campaigns .

The lack of independent equity skin-in-the-game and a 20-year entrenched Chair create an environment ripe for management complacency if the current high-voltage super-cycle falters . and Perverse Incentives : Total 2025 CEO remuneration reached €2.21m with variable pay constituting a noticeable majority, reflecting performance-driven upper-end bonus payouts competitively benchmarked against Nasdaq Copenhagen C25 and European cable manufacturing peers .

The 2025 bonus heavily weighted financial metrics using Group EBITDA at 45% and Group Revenue at 31.5%, with the CEO achieving 86.9% total bonus payout driven by 100% achievement on both EBITDA and Revenue targets .

The 2025 Performance Share Program is weighted equally between 2027 operational EBITDA at 50% and Total Shareholder Return at 50%, but critically measures Absolute TSR rather than Relative TSR against peers .

Using Absolute TSR means executives could receive maximum payouts during a broad market rally even if NKT underperforms Prysmian or Nexans, creating a perverse incentive that rewards macro tailwinds without demanding genuine peer-relative outperformance .

Executive Management held 17,519 shares by year-end 2025 valued at approximately €1.9m, representing less than one year of the CEO's 2025 compensation and trailing founder-led industrial peers on skin-in-the-game metrics .

The 100% free float structure lacks major founder control theoretically exposing NKT to hostile bids if execution falters, while a competitor poaching the highly successful relatively young CEO during this hyper-growth cycle would critically derail the capacity expansion despite the COO buffer .

A massive 142% year-over-year spike in internal whistleblower complaints in 2025 alongside surging temporary turnover signals severe operational strain during rapid capacity scale-up, while four independent AGM- elected board members holding absolutely zero equity represents a stark lack of financial skin-in-the-game versus standard European large-cap industrial governance norms .

Management's much-praised conservative guidance may simply be sandbagging to protect absolute TSR-linked bonuses, while severe cost overruns are buried under industry super-cycle tailwinds threatening credibility when market conditions normalize .

Net Management Quality Verdict: Claes Westerlind demonstrates genuine operator credentials with deep technical expertise and has delivered exceptional top-line growth and margin expansion during an unprecedented industry boom

However, the Karlskrona cost overrun, active antitrust probes across multiple jurisdictions, explosive growth in internal grievances, weak independent board alignment, perverse absolute TSR incentives, and absence of meaningful insider conviction buying collectively indicate a medium plausibility operator thesis with elevated execution and governance risk .

The CEO's proven commercial acumen and industry tailwinds support continued near-term performance, but structural governance weaknesses and compliance fractures threaten long-term investment durability if cyclical conditions deteriorate.

The combination of zero shareholder capital returns despite record profitability and management's reluctance to deploy personal capital suggests peak-cycle caution among insiders despite bullish public positioning.
