Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Attendo's completed Finnish turnaround and emerging Scandinavian margin recovery position the company for highly predictable, double-digit earnings growth driven by structural aging demographics and proactive share buybacks.
1.
Structured own operations: Attendo manages residential care for older people, support for disabled individuals, and individual and family care. The company generates 92% of its sales from own operations and only 8% from public outsourcing contracts. This own operations model allows Attendo to control facility quality and retain pricing power
2.
Compelling investment case: The core case rests on defensive, regulated revenue streams capturing the structural aging of the Nordic population. Projections suggest that adjusted EPS can reach above SEK 9.00 by 2028 through margin expansion and share repurchases
3.
Critical swing factor: The single most important swing factor for earnings is the rate of Scandinavian margin recovery, which historically lagged behind Finland's sharp turnaround. If Scandinavia raises its margins back to the historical 7-8% level, it will unlock massive EPS upside
4.
Accelerating recent performance: In Q1 2026, lease-adjusted EBITA jumped 40% Y/Y to SEK 326m, beating broker estimates by 18%. Margins in Finland reached 9.4%, while Scandinavia improved for a second consecutive quarter to 4.7%
5.
Rebound multi-year trajectory: Attendo has rebuilt its profitability since the pandemic, lifting adjusted EPS from less than SEK 1.00 in 2022 to SEK 6.03 in 2025. This recovery was driven by successful price hikes in Finland to offset strict labor laws
6.
Strained public finances: The greatest threat to the investment case is the potential for funding constraints in Swedish and Finnish municipalities. This risk transmits directly through undercompensated wage inflation and patient referral delays
7.
Strong positive verdict: A detailed investigation is highly warranted because Attendo offers defensive growth at an attractive 11x 2026e EV/EBITAaL multiple. Furthermore, its free cash flow yield of 7-9% is being fully utilized for value-accretive share buybacks

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Attendo's specialized Nordic social care model has successfully pivoted toward high-margin own-account operations, insulating its revenue from municipal outsourcing price wars and securing strong local market shares.

Segments and Contract Models

1.
Balanced geographic exposure: Attendo generated SEK 18,991m in total sales in 2025, with Finland representing SEK 11,100m and Scandinavia representing SEK 7,891m. This geographic split anchors Attendo's exposure, with Finland acting as its largest market, compared to Ambea where Sweden represents about 65% of sales
2.
In-house dominance: Own-account operations, where Attendo leases and controls the premises, represent 92% of group net sales, leaving outsourcing at just 8%. This represents a deliberate strategic shift away from low-margin public outsourcing contracts over the last five years
3.
Service offering mix: Care for older people constitutes about two-thirds of group sales. The remaining revenue is earned from specialized disabled care and individual and family care
4.
Nursing home focus: Nursing homes represent 60% of older people care sales. Attendo offers private apartments with dementia or somatic care needs
5.
Home care services: Home care represents about 10% of older people care sales. Attendo provides comprehensive home health, cleaning, and meals

Value Chain and Competitive Advantage

6.
Regulated public payors: Attendo's primary customers are Finnish welfare regions and Swedish municipalities, which fully fund care placement. There is no major customer concentration risk, as no single customer exceeds 10% of total group revenue
7.
Scale advantages: As the largest care operator in the Nordics, Attendo utilizes its procurement power and digital care systems to lower costs. This scale is an advantage over smaller, fragmented competitors who lack centralized resources
8.
High switching barriers: The competitive moat is reinforced by extremely high customer switching costs, as residents in nursing homes rarely relocate once settled. This creates stable, recurring revenue streams based on multi-year framework agreements
9.
Standardized care model: The Attendo Way represents the company's proprietary operating model. It standardizes digital tools and staff scheduling, helping ensure quality care

Market Structure and Demographics

10.
Consolidating market structure: The Nordic care market is growing at a mid-single-digit rate, driven by a rapidly aging population. In Sweden, the supply of new nursing homes is highly consolidated, with only Attendo and Ambea actively building new capacity
11.
Fragmented Finnish landscape: Unlike the consolidated Swedish market, the Finnish private social care market remains fragmented. This fragmentation provides Attendo with a long runway of value-accretive bolt-on acquisitions across its core segments
12.
Other side of the story: Squeezed municipal budgets: Local authorities are increasingly facing severe budget deficits and are actively squeezing home care reimbursement rates. This has forced Attendo to exit unprofitable contracts, incurring significant termination costs and reducing overall volume

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Attendo's multi-year financial trajectory is defined by a powerful earnings inflection, with adjusted EPS and cash conversion surging past peers as the Finnish operations complete a historic turnaround.

Profitability and Margin Trends

1.
Accelerating margin recovery: Attendo has rebuilt its lease-adjusted EBITA (EBITAaL) margin from 4.4% in 2023 to 5.4% in 2024, and reached 6.8% in 2025. This margin recovery is driven by a massive turnaround in Finland, where pricing successfully adjusted to offset new staffing requirements
2.
High-quality cash conversion: Free cash flow reached SEK 1,179m in 2025, demonstrating a pure service model with very low capital expenditure requirements. This robust cash generation allows for capital redistribution without increasing absolute net debt
3.
Return on capital profile: Attendo's adjusted return on equity (adj. ROE) improved to 20.7% YTD in April 2026, up from 17.2% in April 2021. This stands above sector averages and highlights strong operational leverage

Balance Sheet and Capital Allocation

4.
Rapid deleveraging trajectory: The company's lease-adjusted net debt to EBITDA ratio fell to 1.1x in 2025 from 1.7x in late 2024. This leverage sits well below the maximum target of 2.5x, giving Attendo substantial financial flexibility
5.
Substantial buyback program: Board has initiated a SEK 275m share buyback program running from May 2026 to August 2026. DNB Carnegie estimates that Attendo could theoretically repurchase up to SEK 4.5bn through 2028 while maintaining its leverage targets
6.
Dividend reinstatement focus: Attendo distributes 30% of adjusted earnings per share, declaring a dividend of SEK 1.80 per share in 2025 compared to SEK 1.20 in 2024

Recent Results and Guidance

7.
Significant Q1 beat: In Q1 2026, lease-adjusted EBITA grew 40% Y/Y to SEK 326m, beating broker consensus estimates by 18%. This performance was driven by an improved margin in Finland reaching 9.4%
8.
Conservative target setting: Management has a history of conservative target-setting. They surpassed their 2026 adjusted EPS target of SEK 5.50 early, delivering SEK 6.03 in 2025, and have established a new target of above SEK 9.00 by 2028
9.
Other side of the story: Delayed Scandinavian turnaround: While Finland has performed exceptionally, Scandinavian margin improvements have been delayed multiple times. Unprofitable home care contracts and losses in Denmark have historically diluted consolidated results

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Attendo strengthens significantly, driven by high-margin Scandinavian operational leverage, a strong demographic tailwind, and a highly accretive capital distribution strategy.

Leverage from Scandinavia Margin Expansion

1.
High Scandinavian leverage: Unlocking the margin potential in Attendo Scandinavia represents the most powerful near-term earnings driver. The Scandinavian margin improved to 4.7% in Q1 2026 from 3.4% in Q1 2025. Projections suggest that returning margins to historical levels of 7-8% would provide massive earnings upside
2.
Plausible contract rationalization: This margin expansion carries high plausibility because Attendo is aggressively exiting low-margin outsourcing and unsustainable home care contracts. By focusing on higher-margin owned residential homes, Attendo is structurally improving its earnings mix
3.
Operational margin failure: This driver could fail if Scandinavia experiences persistent labor shortages, forcing the company to use expensive substitute nurses that erode the margin benefit

Demographic Capacity Additions

4.
Massive demographic tailwind: The aging population drives structural volume growth. The number of people over 80 is projected to grow by ~50% in Scandinavia from 2020 to 2030, creating a need for over 400 new nursing homes in Sweden by 2032
5.
Resumed construction pipeline: Plausibility of capacity growth is supported by Attendo having 931 beds under construction in Q1 2026. The company plans to open 400-600 new beds annually over the next few years to capture this demand
6.
Delay in referral: Capacity additions could fail if municipalities delay referral of patients to private facilities to protect their own public budgets

Accretive Capital Allocation

7.
Compelling EPS magnification: Share buybacks act as a highly predictable magnifier of forward EPS. The company is actively executing a SEK 275m buyback, which has already reduced outstanding shares to 142.78m in June 2026
8.
Independent bull case: These three drivers are largely independent. Even if new home construction delays occur, Scandinavian margin recovery and the share buyback program can independently drive adjusted EPS past SEK 9.00 by 2028
9.
Other side of the story: Wage inflation risks: A sudden, uncompensated wage spike in Finland or Sweden would destroy margin expansion. Because framework agreements are indexed to historical inflation, there is a lag before Attendo can pass cost increases through to public payors

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Attendo shares look highly attractive and fundamentally undervalued on the available evidence, trading at a steep discount to historical averages and peer multiples despite superior forward earnings growth.
1.
Attractive forward multiples: Attendo trades at an appealing 11x lease-adjusted EV/EBITA (EV/EBITAaL) multiple for 2026e and 10x for 2027e. This represents a discount to peer Ambea, which trades at 10.4x 2026e EV/EBITA and 9.5x 2027e EV/EBITA
2.
Superior FCF yield: The stock offers a robust free cash flow yield of 7-9% before buybacks. This cash flow is being fully utilized for share buybacks, compounding the equity value
3.
Steep historical discount: Attendo trades far below its historical multiples. At the time of its IPO in 2015-2017, the Swedish care sector traded at 12-15x EV/EBITAaL, reflecting its highly defensive growth qualities
4.
Generous analyst targets: The sell-side median target price is SEK 125.0. This implies substantial upside compared to the current share price of SEK 104.1 as of May 7, 2026
5.
Excessive market skepticism: The market is currently pricing in historical earnings volatility and political risks. However, with a highly stable regulatory framework, this valuation discount is unjustified
6.
Other side of the story: Political multiple compression: Valuation multiples could compress permanently if political noise regarding profits in welfare intensified ahead of the Swedish election in late 2026

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. While regulatory risks and staff shortages present credible threats, the bear case is mitigated by a highly disciplined industry pricing environment and immense demographic demand pressure.

Staffing and Wage Inflation

1.
Shortage of qualified personnel: The most critical operational risk is a shortage of qualified nurses and caregivers. If Attendo cannot recruit employees, it must rely on expensive staffing agencies, which dilutes EBITA
2.
Cost-price mismatch: High cost inflation can create a temporary cost-price gap. Because price adjustments are calculated on a lag, sharp spikes in food, energy, or labor costs hurt short-term margins

Regulatory and Political Risk

3.
Regulatory staffing requirements: The business is exposed to legislative changes in staffing density. For example, the changing staffing requirements in Finland from 2019-2022 severely strained profitability
4.
Municipal budget strain: Swedish municipalities are facing budget deficits, forcing them to squeeze pricing terms. This strain represents a structural risk to patient placement rates and pricing

Operational Failure Modes

5.
False friend growth: Rapid growth in home care or public outsourcing can mask underlying margin deterioration. This occurred in Scandinavia where low-price contracts had to be exited, reducing revenue but improving profitability
6.
Critical warning metrics: Investors should closely monitor group occupancy rates (currently at 88% in Q1 2026) and employee sick leave levels. A decline in occupancy or a spike in sick leave represents an early warning signal of margin compression
7.
Other side of the story: Indispensable private sector: The bear case is overstated because public-private collaboration is not optional. Municipalities cannot afford to fund the massive capacity additions required by aging demographics alone, forcing them to rely on efficient private providers like Attendo

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 4 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership remains highly stable under CEO Martin Tivéus, with key change concentrated on new business area leadership in Scandinavia to accelerate the margin recovery.
1.
Stable executive leadership: CEO Martin Tivéus has led Attendo since 2018, providing a highly stable foundation. Under his tenure, the company has completed its massive Finnish turnaround and exited non-core segments
2.
Divisional Scandinavian transition: Malin Fredgardh Huber was appointed as Business Area Director for Attendo Scandinavia in January 2025, succeeding Patrik Högberg. Her extensive background as a registered nurse and 23-year career at Attendo signals a sharp focus on quality and operational care to drive margins
3.
Aligned incentive structures: The 2026 Annual General Meeting approved new Employee Stock Option and Performance Share Programs. These programs align executive remuneration with strict, long-term financial and sustainability-related quality targets
