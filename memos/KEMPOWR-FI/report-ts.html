Section 3 – What the company does 20 findings

Section summary
  1. Kempower provides premium, highly differentiated DC fast-charging systems that utilize dynamic power allocation to optimize efficiency and site layout for global operators.

Product Portfolio and Technology

1.
Premium modular dispensers: Kempower designs and manufactures highly reliable DC charging solutions, including satellites delivering up to 400 kW and Mega Satellites capable of megawatt-level charging for heavy commercial fleets
2.
Dynamic power allocation: The patented power-sharing technology dynamically distributes electricity across up to 12 satellites based on vehicle demand, boosting average system utilization by 30 percent compared to static charging stations
3.
Space saving layouts: By separating the central power cabinets from the user satellites, the company's distributed architecture delivers up to 50 percent savings in real estate requirements at busy public forecourts
4.
Proprietary cloud platform: The ChargEye SaaS charging management system offers comprehensive cloud-based monitoring, predictive diagnostics, and remote maintenance tools that ensure high network availability and uptime
5.
Elite performance rankings: Multiple independent industry analyses have recognized Kempower as the top-ranked DC fast-charging provider globally based on critical metrics like charging success rate and user experience
6.
Patented charging controls: The company protects its technological lead through extensive patented IP covering dynamic power algorithms, embedded software, and cable support mechanisms

Revenue Model and Economics

7.
Transactional hardware model: Equipment deliveries represent 95 percent of group sales, exposing the business to the lumpy capex decisions of charging operators and public transit authorities
8.
Expanding lifecycle services: Aftermarket services and SaaS subscriptions contribute 5 percent of sales but are growing fast to build a resilient, high-margin recurring income stream as the installed base expands
9.
Asset light assembly: Production is concentrated in highly flexible, co-located assembly facilities in Finland and North Carolina, maintaining low capital expenditure requirements of only 4 percent of sales, which should preserve cash during volatile market cycles
10.
Resilient localized sourcing: Assembly processes rely on a robust network of European and American component suppliers, ensuring tariff-proof manufacturing compliance under strict local procurement rules
11.
High customer engagement: Ongoing training and proactive support help Kempower secure long-standing client relationships, yielding an impressive Net Promoter Score of 47 in annual customer satisfaction surveys
12.
Valuable lifecycle solutions: The strategy focuses on transitioning from pure equipment sales to full lifecycle solutions, capturing long-term recurring revenues from charger maintenance and digital upgrades

Customer Concentration and Market Structure

13.
Moderate customer concentration: The top 25 customers contribute 50 percent of group revenues, introducing transaction risks if major charge point operators pause or delay their network expansions
14.
Large public operator base: Public charging stations for passenger cars represent 75 percent of the global installed base, serving major European and American charge point operators
15.
Fast growing fleet base: Fleet and commercial vehicle depots represent 25 percent of the installed base, driving large-volume orders from logistics providers and municipal bus operators
16.
Explosive market demand: The global DC fast-charging addressable market is projected to expand from €4.5 billion to over €10 billion by 2030, supported by strict CO2 emissions regulations
17.
Crowded competitive field: Kempower faces stiff competition from major electrical engineering conglomerates such as ABB and Siemens, alongside smaller price-aggressive assemblers in regional markets
18.
Formidable primary competitor: Private European competitor Alpitronic poses a severe threat, having already captured a market share estimated to be three times larger than Kempower's outside the Nordics
19.
Other side of the story: Commoditization of hardware could strip the business of its premium pricing power if competitors successfully replicate dynamic load balancing at lower unit costs

Global Expansion Vector

2.

Continental European expansion: Revenue from Europe outside the Nordics grew by 87 percent in Q1 2026, offsetting maturity in the Nordic passenger car market
5.
APAC market entry: Rapid scaling in Australia and Southeast Asia has generated positive order intake, with regional orders up 129 percent in Q1 2026

7.
Highly plausible truck transition: The commercial vehicle driver carries high plausibility because heavy-duty trucks require high-power DC chargers, making AC alternatives obsolete
8.
Truck fleet lag risk: The heavy-duty driver could fail if commercial truck fleet operators delay purchases due to high vehicle pricing
9.
Strategic fleet contracts: Success in securing key logistics accounts like DSV and ASKO proves the company's competitive advantage in fleet depot electrification

Expanding public truck network: Strategic partnerships with fuel retailers like Circle K and DCB Energy support early public truck charging corridor deployment

Lifecycle Services and Software

11.

12.

13.
Software commoditization risk: The high-margin software driver could fail if fleet operators adopt open-source platforms or independent third-party charging software
14.
Rapidly expanding installed base: The global installed charger base is projected to grow over 2.5 times by 2030, driving highly predictable service revenues
15.
Product cost reduction execution: The company's unit cost savings program is successfully offsetting market price erosion, protecting core gross margins
16.
Independent investment drivers: The growth drivers operate independently, meaning transit authority bus contracts can succeed even if long-haul truck electrification is delayed
17.
Other side of the story: A persistent lag in EV adoption would severely derail the entire investment case by forcing charge operators to freeze investment plans

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The bear case centers on intensifying price erosion and a cyclical slowdown in passenger EV adoption, which could permanently depress Kempower's gross margin.

Market and Pricing Pressures

1.
Intensifying market price erosion: Pricing pressure in core European markets continues to drag down average selling prices, posing a direct threat to gross margins
2.
Sluggish passenger EV sales: A slowdown in passenger electric vehicle registrations across Europe and North America directly reduces charge operator profitability and capex budgets
3.
Sustained customer destocking cycle: Excess customer inventories accumulated during component shortages took several quarters to clear, and might continue to impact small local operators
4.
Unproven charge operator profits: High interest rates and volatile energy prices have severely hurt charge point operators, threatening the sustainability of their business models
5.
No short interest pressure: There is currently no published active short-seller report on Kempower, but the stock remains highly volatile on order news

Operational and Technical Vulnerabilities

6.
Deceptive order intake surges: Sizable transit authority contract wins can act as a false friend by masking an ongoing, structural slowdown in core retail CPO spending
7.
Availability of critical components: Structural shortages of key raw materials or semiconductors could halt factory production and cap potential top-line expansion
8.
Fierce competitor resources: Large electrical engineering conglomerates possess deep pockets to wage aggressive pricing wars, putting Kempower's market share at risk
9.
Critical key employee reliance: Losing key R&D engineers or regional sales managers could severely stall product innovation and North American sales execution
10.
Other side of the story: Unstoppable structural demand for charging infrastructure means any near-term capex pauses are temporary, as transport decarbonization is impossible without massive DC fast-charging networks
