Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The company operates a dominant, vertically integrated model supplying critical, consumable refractory solutions to steel and industrial clients with massive pricing power and scale.

Business Segments and Revenue Mix

1.
essential industrial protection: Refractories are high-performance materials designed to withstand extreme thermal, chemical, and physical stresses exceeding 1,200°C, making them completely indispensable for the steel, cement, non-ferrous metals, and glass sectors
2.
steel segment dominance: The Steel segment is the primary engine of profitability, accounting for 70.00% of revenue in FY2025 and serving approximately 1,500 steel plants globally with high-temperature lining and flow control materials
3.
stable industrial revenue: The Industrial segment comprises the remaining 30.00% of revenue, supplying capital-intensive linings to cement kilns (where it holds a 30.00% global market share), lime, glass, and chemical plants
4.
diversified product forms: Revenue is generated from three primary categories: basic and non-basic refractory bricks (63.00% of product revenue, ASP ~€1,500.00/t), monolithics and mixes (25.00% of product revenue, ASP ~€900.00/t), and high-value flow control functional systems (12.00% of product revenue, ASP ~€2,000.00/t)
5.
contractual solution model: Approximately 27.00% of group revenue is generated through advanced performance-based solutions contracts where the customer pays a fixed price per ton of steel produced

Competitive Advantage and Value Chain

6.
unmatched scale barrier: With a ~15.00% share of the advanced refractories market ex-China, RHI Magnesita has unmatched pricing power and procurement scale over a long tail of over 2,000 small regional players
7.
secured vertical integration: RHI Magnesita owns the highest degree of vertical integration among its global peers, sourcing raw materials (magnesite and dolomite) from 12 proprietary mining and deep processing plants
8.
high customer switching costs: Refractories represent only a tiny fraction of a steel mill's operating costs, but any structural failure causes catastrophic plant damage, forcing customers to rely on trusted global suppliers
9.
deep technical service barrier: Competitive advantage is reinforced by unmatched technical marketing capabilities, including a global team of 270 research scientists and over 340 technical engineers stationed at customer sites globally
10.
diversified customer concentration: Customer concentration is healthy and highly diversified, with the largest ten customers accounting for approximately 33.00% of total group sales and the single largest customer accounting for ~9.00%

Industry Structure

11.
consolidating market dynamics: The global refractory sector remains highly fragmented, creating a continuous M&A opportunity where leading players consolidate small regional and product specialists to gain share
12.
secular consumption headwind: Refractory volume consumption per ton of steel is slowly declining globally due to steelmaking technological progress, requiring RHIM to capture value through premium solution offerings
13.
Other side of the story: The backward integration model can become a massive margin drag during raw material oversupply cycles, as high fixed-cost mines operate with low asset utilization when Chinese magnesia dumping drives prices down

5.

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Key risks are driven by high cyclicality, raw material price volatility, and heavy M&A execution burdens that could disrupt the de-leveraging trajectory.

Macroeconomic and Operational Headwinds

1.
extreme downstream steel cyclicality: Refractory demand depends entirely on global steelmaker utilization, meaning any deeper industrial recession would lead to severe volume contractions and deferred performance contracts
2.
damaging raw material volatility: Sharp drops in global magnesia and magnesite pricing would squeeze the company's high-cost mining assets to near-zero margins, completely removing the competitive benefit of its backward integration
3.
costly m&a integration strain: An active consolidation strategy creates substantial operational integration risks, where any failure to realize Resco synergies would leave leverage dangerously elevated
4.
disruptive chinese export dumping: Excessive Chinese domestic steel overcapacity drives cheap export dumping into mature markets, forcing regional steel plants to idle operations and compress refractory demand
5.
damaging currency exchange fluctuations: High exposure to highly volatile emerging market currencies (such as Brazilian Real and Indian Rupee) introduces substantial reporting headwinds outside management's control

Early-Warning Metrics

6.
critical monitoring indicators: The single most critical early-warning signal to monitor is the monthly WorldSteel global steel production data and the ex-factory pricing of Chinese fused magnesia
7.
Other side of the story: The risk of a severe structural volume collapse is highly overstated because high-temperature industrial processes consume refractories continuously, meaning physical demand can only be delayed for a few quarters before risking terminal plant failure
