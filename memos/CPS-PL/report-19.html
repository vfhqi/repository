2. KEY FINDINGS - ACH winner (preliminary): FALSE FRIEND profile carries fewest hard inconsistencies — apparent transformation progress masks structural cash drag and execution fragility (detailed in §13)

  • Disconfirming evidence count: YES profile = 7 hard inconsistencies; NO profile = 5; FALSE FRIEND = 3 — FALSE FRIEND wins on Heuer criterion
  • Joint failure probability: medium-to-high plausibility that at least two failure modes activate simultaneously, driven by the correlated cluster of cash-cycle deterioration + contract repricing + cost-base stickiness
  • Highest-plausibility failure mode: revenue attrition acceleration (§3) — pension/restructuring cash drain removes the financial buffer that historically absorbed contract losses
  • Least-monitored failure mode: management incentive misalignment (§9) — EPS-linked comp creates false-friend optics during restructuring, actively obscuring whether underlying operations are healing
  • False-friend warning: the ongoing disposal programme is generating cash that flatters near-term liquidity headlines while permanently reducing the revenue base needed to absorb the fixed-cost structure
  • Transmission risk: every failure narrative feeds into the same narrow EPS path — contract wins → revenue → margin → FCF — meaning scenario correlation is high and the bull case has minimal redundancy
  • What the ACH winner must be wrong about: FALSE FRIEND loses its primacy only if disposal proceeds are being systematically reinvested into a provably higher-ROIC residual book — no public evidence yet supports that claim at the required magnitude
  • ⚡ Structural oddity flagged: Capita is attempting a simultaneous deleveraging + platform rebuild + contract-base renewal — three capital-competing priorities that, in the UK BPO/outsourcing sector, have never been resolved in parallel without at least one being sacrificed; the consensus narrative treats all three as co-achievable

3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§3 — FN-01: Revenue Attrition Acceleration

Narrative: Legacy contract renewals arrive at reset pricing as clients benchmark against lower-cost offshore/automation alternatives; volumes lost to attrition outpace new-logo wins; revenue base shrinks faster than cost base, compressing margins violently. Transmission: contract value ↓ → revenue ↓ → operating leverage works in reverse → EBIT ↓ → EPS misses consensus by >20%.

Early warning signals:
- Book-to-bill ratio below 1.0x for two consecutive halves
- TCV of contract wins declining quarter-on-quarter vs. prior year comps
- Disclosed contract losses (announced wins from competitors naming Capita as prior incumbent)
- Revenue-per-employee declining faster than headcount reductions
- Client satisfaction scores / NPS if publicly disclosed by client-side surveys

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — BPO sector base rate for cumulative contract attrition during a multi-year restructuring is well-documented; Serco (2014-16), Mitie (2016-18), and Interserve (2017-19) all experienced revenue attrition acceleration once the market perceived financial fragility

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: clients have procurement leverage at renewal; competing bids from Teleperformance, Concentrix, and public-sector digital players are structurally available
- Motive: clients facing their own cost pressure have strong incentive to use Capita's visible weakness as leverage
- Opportunity: contract book has natural expiry cadence creating recurring reset moments
- All three legs aligned — full activation risk, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: sustained book-to-bill ≥1.2x for ≥3 consecutive halves; meaningful new-logo wins in growth verticals (e.g. defence digital); management disclosure of retention rates >95% on renewal pipeline — none of these are currently verifiable at threshold confidence

Monitoring checklist:
- Half-year TCV disclosures vs. prior year
- Revenue by division vs. consensus
- Competitor press releases citing contract wins from Capita
- Days-sales-outstanding trend
- Headcount vs. revenue ratio

Interaction with other failure modes: directly correlated with FN-03 (operating leverage failure) and FN-07 (cash cycle deterioration); if FN-01 activates, probability of FN-03 and FN-07 co-activation rises sharply — compound scenario is the modal bear case

§4 — FN-02: Public-Sector Repricing / Procurement Reset

Narrative: UK central and local government clients, under fiscal pressure and post-pandemic procurement reform, conduct competitive re-tender processes that reprice existing Capita contracts at lower margins or switch to insourcing; Capita wins retention but at structurally lower revenue and margin. Transmission: contract repricing ↓ margin → EBIT margin contracts 200-400bps → multi-year EPS trajectory reset downward.

Early warning signals:
- Cabinet Office Outsourcing Playbook / Crown Commercial Service guidance tightening
- Individual client procurement notices (OJEU / Find-a-Tender disclosures)
- Capita management commentary on "competitive renewal environment" vs. prior language
- Disclosed contract extensions at shorter duration (indicating client optionality retention)
- Public sector revenue as % of total moving below a structural floor

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — UK government procurement environment post-2020 is structurally more adversarial to incumbents; the Cabinet Office's commitment to reducing single-supplier dependency is documented policy; Capita's public-sector concentration makes this a recurring risk rather than a tail event

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: statutory re-tender obligations exist above threshold contract values
- Motive: fiscal pressure + political optics on outsourcing create bipartisan incentive to reprice
- Opportunity: Capita's contract book has documented public-sector concentration
- All three legs aligned — full activation risk

Disconfirming evidence: evidence of sole-source extensions above re-tender thresholds (indicates mission-critical dependency that insulates margin); disclosed contract wins at margin-accretive pricing; specific client relationships (e.g. NHS, Army) renewed at disclosed terms showing margin stability

Monitoring checklist:
- OJEU / Find-a-Tender contract notices mentioning Capita
- Management language on renewal pricing trends
- Public-sector revenue margin vs. group margin
- Duration profile of renewed contracts
- Central government spend-review documentation

Interaction with other failure modes: partially correlated with FN-01; distinguished by mechanism (repricing vs. volume loss); co-activation with FN-01 produces a revenue-and-margin squeeze that eliminates operating leverage recovery thesis simultaneously

§5 — FN-03: Operating Leverage Failure — Cost Base Stickiness

Narrative: Management targets a materially leaner cost structure post-disposal programme; in practice, headcount reductions are offset by redundancy costs, retained-obligation costs from disposed entities, and technology transition spend; operating leverage never materialises at the promised timeline. Transmission: cost base stays elevated → EBIT margin targets missed → consensus EPS downgrades → de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Adjusted EBIT margin guidance revised downward at interim results
- "Exceptional" / "non-recurring" restructuring costs re-appearing for ≥3 consecutive years
- Headcount disclosures showing slower reduction than implied by disposal activity
- Technology/platform investment spend rising as % of revenue rather than declining
- Gap between reported and cash-adjusted EBIT widening

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — restructuring cost stickiness is the defining failure mode of UK services businesses in transformation; Capita has already disclosed multi-year restructuring programmes; the base rate for "exceptional costs becoming structural" in comparable situations (G4S, Mitie, Serco) is above 50% over a 3-year window

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: restructuring costs are partially discretionary in timing; management can defer some while accelerating others
- Motive: management incentive to show short-term margin progress may lead to under-investment in the cost-reduction programme
- Opportunity: complex multi-division structure creates genuine accounting discretion on what constitutes "restructuring" vs. "operating"
- All three legs aligned

Disconfirming evidence: two consecutive periods of restructuring costs declining in absolute terms and as % of revenue; EBIT margin trajectory converging with stated targets on disclosed timeline; independent analyst confirmation that cost-reduction programme is ahead of schedule

Monitoring checklist:
- Exceptional/non-recurring cost line vs. prior year in absolute terms
- Adjusted vs. statutory EBIT margin delta
- Cash cost of restructuring vs. disclosed programme budget
- Headcount by division
- Management guidance credibility score (consecutive delivery vs. miss)

Interaction with other failure modes: structurally correlated with FN-07 (cash cycle); also interacts with FN-05 (capex deferral false-friend) — management facing cost pressure may defer capex, creating FN-05 simultaneously

§6 — FN-04: Pension Liability Cascade

Narrative: Defined-benefit pension deficit widens materially due to discount rate movement or longevity assumption changes; trustees demand accelerated cash contributions; this cash drain competes directly with investment for growth and debt repayment, trapping Capita in a structural cash consumption cycle. Transmission: pension cash contributions ↑ → FCF ↓ → net debt stable or rising despite operating improvements → equity value eroded → EPS trajectory undermined by balance-sheet constraint.

Early warning signals:
- Triennial valuation results (next expected date — management will signal in advance of finalisation)
- UK corporate bond yields (which proxy discount rates) declining materially
- Management pre-announcement of enhanced pension contributions
- Pension deficit disclosed in interim / annual accounts vs. prior period
- Trustee commentary (if publicly available) on funding position

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — pension risk is structurally present but partially mitigated by the disposal of pension-carrying subsidiaries; the residual pension book is smaller than at peak; however, in a declining-rate environment, this risk re-activates meaningfully

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: pension trustees have legal authority to demand accelerated contributions
- Motive: trustees have fiduciary duty to members
- Opportunity: Capita's pension scheme is material relative to market cap
- All three legs aligned; severity conditional on macro rate path

Disconfirming evidence: triennial valuation showing deficit materially reduced or eliminated; evidence of liability management (buyout / buy-in transactions reducing residual exposure); discount rate environment stable or rising

Monitoring checklist:
- Annual report pension section (deficit, IAS 19 assumptions)
- UK long-dated corporate bond yields
- Management disclosure of contribution schedule
- Pension buyout/buy-in announcements
- Actuarial assumptions sensitivity table

Interaction with other failure modes: directly correlated with FN-07 (cash cycle); compound activation with FN-01 (revenue attrition) creates a simultaneous cash-in-shortfall + cash-out-acceleration dynamic that is the closest analogue to the Interserve failure path

§7 — FN-05: FALSE FRIEND — Disposal Programme Flatters Liquidity While Destroying Revenue Base

Narrative (FALSE FRIEND): Disposal proceeds generate headline cash, reduce reported net debt, and create positive newsflow — all of which appear to confirm the "deleveraging transformation" thesis; simultaneously, each disposal reduces the revenue base over which Capita must amortise its fixed cost structure, permanently impairing the earnings power of the residual business. The bull narrative treats disposals as value-creating; the underlying reality is that residual operations are becoming a smaller, more fixed-cost-intensive entity with narrower operational leverage. Transmission: disposal-generated cash masks FCF deterioration → consensus mis-prices residual EPS trajectory → position held through deterioration → multiple compression when true trajectory disclosed.

Early warning signals:
- Revenue decline outpacing cost reduction in residual continuing operations
- Adjusted EBIT margin of continuing operations declining post-disposal
- New-contract wins in remaining divisions insufficient to replace disposed-division revenues
- Management guidance shifting from absolute targets to "per continuing operations" metrics (base redefinition)
- Disposal multiples declining (indicating deteriorating perceived quality of assets being sold)

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — this is the structurally most dangerous failure mode because it is intrinsically hard to detect in real time; the Mitie / Carillion / Amey pattern all share elements of disposal-flattering-liquidity-while-impairing-the-residual

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: management controls disposal sequencing and can time proceeds to coincide with debt covenant tests
- Motive: incentive to demonstrate transformation progress and protect credit facility headroom
- Opportunity: market and analyst community anchors on liquidity headlines, not residual earnings power
- All three legs aligned — this is the canonical false-friend mechanism

Disconfirming evidence: continuing-operations revenue growing organically (ex-disposal) for ≥2 consecutive periods; continuing-operations EBIT margin expanding; capital allocation post-disposal demonstrably directed at higher-ROIC growth (not debt service); disposal multiples above residual-company EV/EBIT (confirming value-accretive, not value-dilutive disposals)

Monitoring checklist:
- Revenue bridge: organic vs. disposal effect
- Continuing-operations EBIT margin trend
- Capital deployed into growth post-disposal vs. debt repayment
- EV/revenue of disposed assets vs. group EV/revenue
- Management guidance base redefinition frequency

Interaction with other failure modes: directly interacts with FN-03 (cost stickiness — disposing of revenue without removing proportional cost) and FN-07 (cash cycle — disposal proceeds are one-time, disguising recurring FCF weakness); this false-friend scenario is the gateway scenario that delays detection of FN-01, FN-03, and FN-04 simultaneously

§8 — FN-06: FALSE FRIEND — Margin Expansion from R&D/Capex Under-investment

Narrative (FALSE FRIEND): Reported adjusted margins improve as management defers technology investment, platform modernisation, and R&D spend — generating near-term EBIT uplift that looks like operational improvement but actually represents consumption of the intangible asset base; when deferred investment is eventually required (contract re-tender, capability gap, client exit), costs arrive in a concentrated burst that destroys the apparent margin trajectory. Transmission: capex/R&D deferral → reported margin expansion → consensus upgrades → stock re-rates → capex catch-up announced → forecast revisions downward → multiple compression.

Early warning signals:
- Capital expenditure as % of revenue declining below sector floor (typically 2-3% for BPO/IT services)
- Management commentary shifting from "investment for growth" to "capital-light model" language without substantive evidence
- Technology refresh cycle extensions (systems running beyond vendor support periods)
- Client-side commentary on technology quality / capability gaps (procurement notices, published case studies)
- Depreciation/amortisation declining faster than revenue (D&A as % of revenue ratio compression)

Plausibility verdict: medium-to-high plausibility — capex deferral is the textbook management response to balance-sheet pressure in asset-light services businesses; the incentive exists whenever debt covenants or credit ratings are under pressure

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: capex is discretionary at the margin; R&D can be reclassified or deferred without immediate visible consequence
- Motive: balance-sheet pressure + short-term EPS incentive alignment
- Opportunity: analyst community focuses on adjusted EBIT, not maintenance capex adequacy
- All three legs aligned

Disconfirming evidence: capex/revenue ratio stable or increasing despite disposal programme; management disclosure of specific technology investment programmes with verifiable milestones; client renewal terms explicitly referencing technology capability (confirming platform investment is real)

Monitoring checklist:
- Capex/revenue ratio trend (absolute and vs. sector)
- D&A as % of revenue
- Management technology investment commentary specificity
- Contract win rate in technology-intensive verticals
- Vendor relationship disclosures (platform partnerships)

Interaction with other failure modes: directly feeds FN-01 (revenue attrition) if technology capability gap leads to contract loss at renewal; also interacts with FN-02 (public-sector repricing — government clients explicitly benchmark technology capability)

§9 — FN-07: Cash Cycle Deterioration / FCF Structural Deficit

Narrative: Despite EBIT improvement, FCF remains structurally negative or materially below EBIT due to working capital deterioration (longer collection cycles on public-sector contracts), pension contributions, restructuring cash costs, and interest burden; the equity narrative is EPS-led but FCF confirms no value creation; when the gap between reported EPS and FCF-per-share becomes undeniable, multiple compression is the mechanism. Transmission: EBIT ↑ but FCF flat/negative → EV/EBIT multiple contracts → equity value unchanged or falling despite earnings growth → IRR target fails.

Early warning signals:
- Operating cash conversion (OCF/EBIT) below 70% for consecutive periods
- Days-sales-outstanding expanding (particularly in public-sector divisions)
- Interest coverage on cash (FCF/interest) below 1.0x
- Management KPI set shifting from FCF metrics to adjusted EPS (base change is a warning)
- Exceptional cash costs running above exceptional P&L charges (hidden cash drain)

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — the structural cash conversion problem is documented in Capita's own filings over multiple cycles; the pension + restructuring + working capital trifecta creates a FCF gap that has historically persisted for longer than management guidance implies

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: cash flows are an accounting reality, not a discretionary output — but management can defer working capital improvements or slow collection efforts to manage other priorities
- Motive: no direct incentive to worsen FCF, but incentive misalignment on adjusted EPS means FCF is under-managed
- Opportunity: complex balance sheet with multiple cash drains makes FCF analysis opaque for generalist investors
- Means partially aligned; full MMO on the detection-delay component

Disconfirming evidence: OCF/EBIT conversion above 85% for two consecutive periods; net debt declining in absolute terms from operations (not disposal proceeds); pension contribution schedule declining as disclosed; working capital days stable or improving

Monitoring checklist:
- OCF/EBIT conversion rate
- Working capital as % of revenue
- Net debt bridge (operating vs. disposal vs. financing)
- Pension contribution vs. P&L pension charge
- FCF per share vs. adjusted EPS per share delta

Interaction with other failure modes: this is the most correlated scenario — it is a downstream consequence of FN-01, FN-03, FN-04, and FN-05; when three or more upstream scenarios activate, FN-07 is near-certain; it is the "system-level" failure mode

§10 — FN-08: Key Executive Departure / Management Continuity Risk

Narrative: The transformation thesis is personally embodied in the current management team; a departure of the CEO or CFO during the critical 18-24M execution window triggers investor uncertainty about transformation continuity, potential strategy reset, and a re-rating of execution probability; the stock de-rates before any operational deterioration. Transmission: executive departure → strategy uncertainty → investor confidence withdrawal → multiple de-rating of 1-3 turns EV/EBIT → EPS-equivalent destruction even if operations are intact.

Early warning signals:
- Unusual share sale disclosures by senior management
- Unusually short or non-disclosed contract extensions for CEO/CFO
- Board composition changes (departures of transformation-aligned NEDs)
- Management language on "completion" of transformation (signals potential exit point)
- Press / headhunter activity around equivalent roles at peers

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — Capita has experienced significant management turnover historically; the current team has limited long-tenure track record; however, the immediate near-term risk is lower than during the acute crisis period

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: senior executives at transformation-stage businesses are actively recruited by competitors and PE
- Motive: management may perceive personal upside as asymmetrically limited if the stock is range-bound
- Opportunity: transformation businesses are systematically talent-poaching targets
- All three legs present but low-intensity — watch-only unless confirmed

Disconfirming evidence: CEO/CFO contract renewals announced with long-dated terms; meaningful insider buying post any dip; long-dated LTIP vesting schedules that create strong retention incentive; disclosed succession planning architecture

Monitoring checklist:
- RNS insider transaction disclosures
- Board tenure disclosures in annual report
- LTIP vesting schedule transparency
- Management conference attendance and forward-facing commitments
- Headhunter/media speculation (qualitative)

Interaction with other failure modes: multiplier effect — executive departure in the context of FN-01 or FN-07 is significantly more damaging than in isolation; standalone it is a medium-severity, medium-probability event; in combination it becomes a catalyst for accelerating other failure modes

§11 — FN-09: M&A Integration Failure or Value-Destroying Acquisition

Narrative: Management, under pressure to demonstrate growth post-disposals, pursues an acquisition to fill the revenue and capability gap; integration is slower and more costly than anticipated; the acquired business cannibalises management time during the critical organic transformation window; goodwill impairments and integration costs destroy the financial benefit. Transmission: acquisition → integration costs → management distraction → organic transformation slows → cumulative EPS below trajectory → goodwill impairment triggers → de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Management language shifting from "organic growth" to "strategic M&A" in investor communications
- Bolt-on acquisition announced without clear synergy pathway
- Goodwill/intangibles rising as % of total assets
- Integration cost overruns in acquisition disclosures
- Organic revenue growth slowing coincidentally with M&A activity

Plausibility verdict: low-to-medium plausibility in the near term — balance-sheet constraints significantly limit acquisition capacity; however, if deleveraging succeeds, this risk re-activates in the 18-36M window; the historical pattern for recovery-stage UK services businesses pursuing M&A prematurely is well-documented (Mitie 2015-17)

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: if net debt falls to manageable levels, acquisition capacity returns
- Motive: revenue gap created by disposals creates strategic pressure to acquire
- Opportunity: distressed smaller BPO/IT services assets may be attractively priced
- Two-of-three legs currently aligned — watch-only, upgrade to full activation if leverage falls and management language shifts

Disconfirming evidence: explicit management commitment to organic-only growth for a defined period; leverage covenant restricting acquisition activity; capital allocation policy disclosed and anchored on organic investment + debt reduction only

Monitoring checklist:
- Net debt trajectory and leverage ratio
- Management capital allocation language
- Any bolt-on acquisition announcement
- Goodwill/intangibles trend
- M&A adviser engagement (any disclosed advisory relationships)

Interaction with other failure modes: orthogonal to most operating scenarios; but co-activation with FN-08 (management departure + M&A misstep) would be particularly destructive — new management attempting to accelerate via M&A is a known pattern in UK services failures

§12 — FN-10: Competitive / Structural Disruption — Automation and AI Displacement

Narrative: A significant portion of Capita's BPO contract value (back-office processing, customer services, administration) is directly substitutable by automation and AI-enabled tools; as clients invest in these capabilities directly or via specialist vendors, contract volumes decline structurally without going to competitive re-tender; Capita's own automation investment is insufficient to offset the volume displacement. Transmission: AI/automation adoption by clients → volume per-contract ↓ → revenue-per-contract ↓ → EBIT ↓ → EPS trajectory permanently impaired → no re-rating catalyst.

Early warning signals:
- Client-disclosed automation investment programmes in Capita-adjacent functions
- Volume-per-contract metrics declining (transactions processed, calls handled)
- Capita's own automation/AI investment disclosures insufficient vs. sector norms
- Competitor capability announcements (Teleperformance, Concentrix automation roadmaps)
- Revenue-per-FTE declining in labour-intensive divisions

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility in a 12-24M window, rising to high plausibility in a 36-60M window — near-term contracts are relatively protected by transition costs and incumbent relationships; structural displacement accelerates beyond the immediate investment horizon but is a genuine threat to the ≥3-year EPS trajectory

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: AI/automation tools are increasingly accessible and cost-effective
- Motive: clients face the same cost pressures as Capita, creating strong incentive to automate
- Opportunity: Capita's labour-intensive delivery model creates clear substitution surface
- All three legs aligned for medium-term horizon; near-term activation probability lower

Disconfirming evidence: Capita-disclosed automation partnerships creating sticky platform lock-in with clients; evidence of value-added services growth offsetting commodity volume decline; contract structure demonstrating outcome-based pricing that insulates against volume displacement

Monitoring checklist:
- Revenue-per-FTE trend by division
- Management disclosure of automation investment specifics
- Competitor product launches in directly competitive areas
- Volume metrics in disclosed operational KPIs
- Client-side capex/opex allocation to process automation

Interaction with other failure modes: structural tailwind to FN-01 and FN-02; automation is the underlying mechanism enabling clients to exercise price or volume leverage; treating FN-10 as a separate scenario understates how deeply it accelerates the contract-attrition scenarios

§13 — FN-11: Financing Risk — Covenant Breach / Refinancing at Punitive Terms

Narrative: Capita's refinancing cadence intersects with periods of operational underperformance; banks or bondholders extract punitive terms on refinancing (higher margins, tighter covenants, shorter tenor); or the company breaches a leverage covenant, triggering accelerated repayment, equity dilution, or forced asset sales. Transmission: covenant pressure → forced disposal at distressed prices → residual EPS impaired → equity dilution (rights issue) → per-share EPS decline → IRR destruction.

Early warning signals:
- Credit facility maturity dates within 18M with no refinancing announced
- Leverage ratio approaching stated covenant levels
- Credit rating agency negative outlook or downgrade
- Debt documentation: restricted payment baskets tightening
- Management commentary on "liquidity headroom" becoming hedged or defensive

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — depends critically on whether operational stabilisation proceeds on schedule; the risk is non-linear: low risk if FN-01/FN-03/FN-07 are contained, but escalates rapidly if any of those scenarios activates

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: banks hold covenant power; refinancing market conditions are external to management control
- Motive: lenders protecting credit exposure
- Opportunity: any operational shortfall creates the trigger
- All three legs aligned conditionally on upstream scenario activation

Disconfirming evidence: multi-year refinancing completed at investment-grade-adjacent terms; net debt / EBITDA disclosed at material headroom to covenant; credit rating stable at disclosed level

Monitoring checklist:
- Debt maturity ladder disclosure
- Leverage ratio vs. covenant
- Credit agency ratings actions
- Interest cost per period (indicative of refinancing risk premium)
- Credit default swap spreads (if observable)

Interaction with other failure modes: this is the terminal scenario — FN-11 is the mechanism by which operational failures (FN-01, FN-03, FN-07) become existential; it is the downstream amplifier of the entire failure cluster

§14 — FN-12: Regulatory / Political Risk — UK Outsourcing Policy Reversal

Narrative: UK political environment shifts materially toward insourcing (change of government priorities, specific high-profile service failure, post-Carillion regulatory tightening applied to Capita); major contracts are not renewed on commercial terms or are terminated for convenience; regulatory burden increases the cost of public-sector contract delivery, compressing margins. Transmission: regulatory/political shift → contract non-renewal or termination → revenue decline → EPS impaired → no re-rating pathway.

Early warning signals:
- Parliamentary / select committee scrutiny of specific Capita contracts
- High-profile service failure publicly attributed to Capita (press coverage)
- Government outsourcing policy documents shifting toward insourcing language
- Specific client (NHS, MOD, local authorities) publicly announcing capability repatriation
- Procurement reform affecting BPO contract structures

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — UK political environment is structurally more sceptical of outsourcing than in the 2000-2015 period; however, fiscal pressure simultaneously reduces government capacity for insourcing, creating a partial counterbalance; risk is elevated during election cycles and after any high-profile service failure

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: government holds legislative and procurement policy authority
- Motive: political optics on public service quality; bipartisan appetite for outsourcing reform
- Opportunity: Capita's public-sector concentration creates maximum exposure surface
- All three legs present; activation probability medium

Disconfirming evidence: specific long-duration contract extensions at commercial terms; government outsourcing policy documents reaffirming value-for-money framework; fiscal constraints preventing insourcing investment

Monitoring checklist:
- Hansard / select committee references to Capita
- Government outsourcing policy publications
- Specific contract news (NHS, MOD, local authority)
- Media sentiment on outsourcing sector
- Cabinet Office procurement reform consultations

Interaction with other failure modes: reinforces FN-01 and FN-02; distinct mechanism (political rather than commercial) but identical EPS transmission path; correlation is partial, not full — both can activate independently

15. PART B — META-ANALYSIS

J-front verdict: Failure modes cluster tightly around a single cash-and-revenue-deterioration nexus; the joint probability of zero activations is low plausibility, and the FALSE FRIEND cluster is the most dangerous because it delays detection

Scenario cluster — Revenue/Cash nexus (HIGH CORRELATION):
- FN-01 (revenue attrition), FN-02 (public-sector repricing), FN-07 (FCF deficit), FN-04 (pension cascade), and FN-11 (financing risk) form a tightly correlated chain
- Activation of any one raises conditional probability of the next by the structural linkage: revenue → EBIT → FCF → pension contribution capacity → covenant headroom
- This cluster is the modal bear case; it is not a set of independent low-probability events

Scenario cluster — FALSE FRIEND cluster (MOST DANGEROUS):
- FN-05 (disposal flatters liquidity) and FN-06 (capex deferral flatters margins) are both false-friend scenarios that actively suppress detection signals
- Both generate positive newsflow (debt reduction; margin improvement) that is inconsistent with underlying deterioration
- False-friend density = 2 of 12 narratives (17%) — below the threshold that would make this structurally the most dangerous false-friend case, but the two scenarios are the gateway mechanisms that allow the Revenue/Cash nexus cluster to build undetected

Scenario cluster — Execution/Management (MEDIUM CORRELATION):
- FN-08 (executive departure), FN-09 (M&A distraction), and FN-03 (cost stickiness) share a management-bandwidth constraint; they correlate conditionally if FN-08 activates
- Standalone probability of each is medium; conditional on FN-08, FN-09 and FN-03 become high

Scenario cluster — Structural/Long-term (LOWER NEAR-TERM CORRELATION):
- FN-10 (AI/automation disruption) and FN-12 (political/regulatory) are structurally important but operate on a longer horizon
- Near-term (12-24M) interaction with other scenarios is lower; medium-term (36-60M) interaction is high
- Risk of under-weighting these because they don't appear in near-term monitoring dashboards

Joint probability assessment (verbal):
- P(zero activations in 18M): low plausibility — the Revenue/Cash nexus cluster has at least one scenario (FN-07 FCF deficit) that is arguably already partially activated based on prior disclosures
- P(exactly one activation): medium plausibility
- P(two or more activations): medium-to-high plausibility — given structural correlation of the Revenue/Cash cluster
- P(compound cascade — FN-01 → FN-07 → FN-04 → FN-11): low-to-medium plausibility but non-negligible; this is the Interserve-analogue path

Compound monitorables (appearing across ≥3 scenarios):
- OCF/EBIT conversion rate — appears in FN-03, FN-04, FN-05, FN-07, FN-11 (highest-frequency compound monitorable)
- Book-to-bill / TCV of wins — appears in FN-01, FN-02, FN-10 (revenue cluster compound monitorable)
- Net debt trajectory (operational vs. disposal) — appears in FN-04, FN-05, FN-07, FN-11 (cash cluster compound monitorable)
- Management language / guidance credibility — appears in FN-03, FN-05, FN-06, FN-08 (soft compound monitorable, hardest to monitor)

Compound monitorable priority ranking:
1. OCF/EBIT conversion — single most diagnostic metric across the failure universe
2. Revenue bridge (organic growth ex-disposal) — detects FN-01 and FN-05 simultaneously
3. Net debt bridge (operational component only) — detects FN-07 and FN-11 without disposal distortion

False-friend density assessment:
- 2 explicit false-friend scenarios (FN-05, FN-06) out of 12 total (17%)
- However, FN-05 is the gateway mechanism that masks FN-01, FN-03, FN-04, and FN-07
- Effective false-friend contamination of the monitoring environment is higher than the raw 17% implies — the two false-friend scenarios actively suppress detection of five other scenarios
