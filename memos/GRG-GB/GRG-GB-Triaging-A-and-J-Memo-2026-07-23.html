1.
Business model. Greggs is a vertically integrated food-on-the-go retailer operating 2,759 locations across the UK, comprising 2,137 company-managed shops and 602 franchise units, generating GBP 2,151m in FY25 revenue
2.
Vertical integration. The company manufactures its own products across 15 production and logistics hubs, eliminating middleman margin leakage and delivering industry-leading gross margins of 61.5%
3.
Revenue mix. Retail company-managed shops contribute GBP 1,897m (88% of group revenue); business-to-business franchise and wholesale channels contribute GBP 254m (12%)
4.
Market position. Greggs holds approximately 9% of the pre-4pm UK food-to-go market (valued at GBP 24bn), approximately 10% of breakfast sales, and is the number one brand for out-of-home breakfast visits in the UK
5.
Value positioning. Products are priced 40% below Pret A Manger, 42% below Boots, and 18% below Marks and Spencer, though supermarket baskets remain 12% cheaper
6.
Growth channels. The company is expanding through net new shop openings, approximately 120 per year, a Tesco wholesale partnership, and digital and evening daypart extension
6.1
Channel detail. Tesco scaling from 800 larger stores to 1,900 Express stores; app scan rate at 26.7% of transactions; evening daypart at 9.4% and delivery at 6.8% of managed shop sales
7.
Financial character. This is a domestic UK single-country operator with zero direct foreign-exchange exposure and a net cash balance sheet
7.1
Balance sheet detail. GBP 46m net cash at FY25, though deteriorating, alongside a capital-intensive supply chain investment cycle that peaked in FY25

Investment style. Quality compounder under structural pressure: the business delivered a 12.4% three-year revenue compound annual growth rate, but margins and returns are contracting
8.1
Contraction detail. ROCE fell from 20.3% to 16.0% in a single year under wage inflation, volume deleverage, and peak supply chain investment
