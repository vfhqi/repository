EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Tele2 AB presents a compelling investment narrative anchored in planned, orderly leadership succession and comprehensive organizational renewal that has preserved strategic continuity while accelerating digital transformation under the operational influence of major shareholder Freya Investissement and the iliad Group. The transition from CEO Jean Marc Harion to Nicholas Högberg on July 1, 2026 represents a smooth handover to an experienced successor with deep Swedish market knowledge, having served as CEO of Tre Sweden for nine years and as Deputy CEO following 18 months on the Board. While Harion's unexpected departure for personal reasons was initially viewed negatively by markets given his impressive 70% share price appreciation since November 2024, the orderly transition plan including his advisory role through year-end and potential Board retention mitigates execution risk.

strategic landscape has been fundamentally reshaped since February 2024 when Freya Investissement, the joint investment vehicle of Xavier Niel's NJJ Holding and iliad Group, acquired Kinnevik's 20% economic stake and 27% voting interest, subsequently increasing its economic holding to 22% of capital while maintaining 29% of votes through skillful Class A and B share swaps. This hands-on ownership has driven a radical cost-conscious

cultural rejuvenation, successfully executing an ambitious 15% workforce reduction targeting 600 to 700 positions and ultimately eliminating approximately 650 positions by year-end 2025, fundamentally accelerating organizational simplification and operational efficiency. The immediate financial impact has been profound, with headcount cuts driving best-in-class 11.40% underlying EBITDAaL growth in FY2025 and group underlying EBITDAaL margins expanding 340 basis points to 39.40% on total sales of SEK 29.89 billion.
