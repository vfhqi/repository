EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Materiality of Change

The board and C-suite underwent substantial renewal with departing CEO Henrik Larsson Lyon replaced by Rikard Fröberg in March 2025, bringing extensive US and international M&A experience from Ansell, while Magnus Nicolin became Chairman in May 2024 accompanied by three new directors to enhance international industry expertise. Second-level management restructured significantly with the Fiber Solutions division consolidating into four regions, prompting the departure of EMEA Head Christian Priess in February 2026 and streamlining the Global Executive Team from eight to seven members under centralized European leadership. Management explicitly targets Data Center and Harsh Environment to generate 50% of group sales by 2028 versus 37% recently, representing a fundamental rebalancing of the portfolio from legacy telecom exposure. External demand forces are undergoing structural transformation with global data center capacity projected to expand 2.7x from 82GW to 219GW by 2030, AI clusters requiring 16x more fiber than traditional cloud racks, and defense applications now consuming roughly 15% of global optical fiber demand. Supply chain constraints intensified dramatically with spot prices for standard single-mode optical fiber surging 370% to 560% from recent lows, global preform manufacturing capacity structurally constrained, and US BABA compliance rules pushing domestic fiber lead times to more than a full year.
