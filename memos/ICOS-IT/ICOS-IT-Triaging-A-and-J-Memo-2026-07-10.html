B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What Intercos does. Intercos is a B2B cosmetics contract manufacturer (an "ODM", original design manufacturer) that designs and produces make-up, skincare, and hair-and-body products on behalf of prestige and mass-market beauty brands, rather than selling under its own brand
2.
How it makes money. Revenue comes from turnkey manufacturing contracts: Intercos owns the formulations (proprietary "formula intellectual property" covers roughly 80% of group sales) and charges brand customers for design, development and production, capturing margin on both innovation and manufacturing
3.
Primary market and geography. The group operates 16 manufacturing plants globally, with the largest revenue exposure split across EMEA, the Americas and Asia; China is a structurally important growth market via local-brand outsourcing
4.
Segment mix. Three reporting segments: Make-up (the largest and highest-margin, EBITDA margin 16.30% in FY25), Skincare (smaller, currently contracting, capacity-constrained in the US), and Hair & Body (lower-margin fragrance contract packaging being deliberately de-prioritised)
5.
Financial character. FY25 net sales were EUR 1,047m, flat at constant exchange rates, with adjusted EBITDA of EUR 156m at a 14.90% margin, up 140 basis points year-on-year
6.
