EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 8 findings

ENAV SpA is led by Pasqualino Monti , a state-aligned CEO with deep public infrastructure and maritime transport expertise concentrated within the Italian public sector domain, whose background is primarily that of a statealigned public administrator introducing persistent political succession risks, officially appointed in April 2023 and facing planned replacement in mid-2026 .

The core business remains highly exposed to regulatory rate resets despite achieving record operational delivery and launching ambitious non-regulated initiatives, with regulatory tariff adjustments compressing core margins even as management met or outperformed upgraded FY25 guidance across revenues, EBITDA, and net income .

Monti's prior record demonstrates a consistent pattern of state-mandated public-sector infrastructure turnarounds with high decision velocity and force, though financial metrics such as revenue, margins, and TSR are unavailable because his prior roles were at non-listed public port authorities, and his capacity to deliver commercial public-market EPS growth remains untested .

His leadership style exhibits outlier standards and high operational agency with direct, results-oriented communication, exceptional decision-making speed, and extreme prudence in guidance posture, though his cautious approach and delay in target setting may mask a lack of structural visibility during volatile tariff periods .

This governance vulnerability is compounded by the complete absence of open-market insider buying across ENAV's board, with eight of nine directors holding exactly zero shares, absent director equity throughout 2025, and the total lack of equity skin-inthe-game among non-executive directors limiting direct financial alignment with minority public shareholders, suggesting insiders view ENAV's regulated assets as fully valued .
