1. Stage Identification and History

Chart [5Y]. Chart [10Y].

Vodafone's chart over the last decade is a Minervini case study — and not, until very recently, a flattering one. The stock topped near £235 in late 2017. From there it entered a multi-year Stage 4 decline that ground lower through 2018, 2019, the COVID dislocation in 2020 (which produced the secondary low band), the brief 2021 reflation rally that failed below £140, and finally the 2022-23 capitulation phase that drove the share to its eventual 52W and multi-year low at £67.53. From peak to trough, that is a ~71% drawdown over roughly six years. The decline was punctuated by failed Stage 1 attempts — false bases that broke down and re-entered Stage 4 — every time the equity story tried to reassert itself without a fundamental catalyst.

The 2023-2024 period saw VOD trace out the bottom of what, with the benefit of hindsight, was a multi-year saucer base. The base depth measured from the 2017 peak is ~71%, which is large but not unusual for a Stage 4 stock that has burned out — Minervini's framework explicitly accepts deep bases provided the stock has stopped making lower lows and is showing accumulation. The defining shift came in mid-2024. Three fundamental catalysts crystallised in close succession: the Spain disposal to Zegona; the Italy disposal to Swisscom; and Margherita Della Valle's strategic reset positioning Vodafone as a Germany/UK/Africa-focused operator with a meaningful capital return programme. The share price responded by breaking out of the multi-year base in the £75-80 area, completing the Stage 1 base-to-Stage 2 advance transition.

From the mid-2024 breakout through 24-Apr-26, the stock has executed a clean, orderly Stage 2 advance. The trough-to-current move from £67.53 to £116.30 is approximately +72%. The advance has been characterised by:

  • A series of orderly pullbacks and retests of rising moving averages (12 retests of the 50D, 10 of the 100D, 7 of the 150D — see Section 7).
  • Healthy MA stack maintenance throughout.
  • A monthly MA200 that has risen for twelve consecutive months. This is critical. The MA200 is the slow-moving institutional anchor; twelve straight months of upside on the monthly chart removes any ambiguity about regime.
  • RS percentile climbing into the 80s, with the stock outperforming sector, industry, and market on every measurement window from 3M out to 12M.

Where is the stock now? Stage 2, full Capital qualification on the MM99 11-test filter, ~3.84% from the 52W high, ~72% from the 52W low. The stage label "Capital" — the highest qualification tier — means this is investable on the technical screen. The Probing Bet filter also flags Capital stage. Basing Plateau filter does not flag, because VOD is advancing rather than basing. VCP placeholder pending phase 2 implementation, but stage_2_uptrend = true.

This is not a stock at the start of a Stage 2 advance. It is a stock roughly 18-22 months into Stage 2, with much of the easy money already made from the 2024 breakout. The job here is to identify whether the technicals support a continuation entry (pullback to 50D, as discussed in Section 8) or whether the stock is approaching exhaustion. The data supports continuation, with caveats around the ~19.9% extension above MA200 and the mild near-term distribution.

3. Base Formation Analysis

Chart [10Y]. Chart [5Y].

The base from which VOD broke out in mid-2024 is unusually deep and unusually long. Both attributes warrant comment.

Base depth. From the 2017 peak (~£235) to the 2023 low (£67.53), the share lost ~71% of its value. Minervini bases more typically run 20-35% depth for stocks that have been advancing in Stage 2 and are forming a fresh consolidation before another leg up. Bases of 50%+ are less common and tend to be associated with stocks that have completed a full Stage 4 decline — burnout patterns rather than continuation patterns. VOD belongs to the latter category. This is a "Stage 4 to Stage 1 to Stage 2" transition, not a "Stage 2 pause-and-resume" pattern. The setup logic is different in two important respects:

  • The breakout from such a deep base tends to attract a different cohort of buyers — distressed-asset investors, value rotators, and (in VOD's case) catalyst-driven event funds anchored on the disposal programme. These are stickier holders than momentum chasers and provide more durable price support.
  • The advance from such a base tends to be longer in duration. Stage 2 advances from typical 20-35% bases often run 12-18 months. Stage 2 advances from deep, long bases can run 3+ years before exhausting. VOD is roughly 22 months into its Stage 2 advance. There is plausibly more left in this move.

Base shape. The base is best described as a long saucer, with a secondary low cluster around the 2020 COVID period and the primary low printed in late 2023. The shape is gentle — without sharp V-spikes off the lows — which is generally favourable. Whether one calls it a saucer-with-handle (with the late-2023 low forming the handle) or a multi-year cup-and-handle is a matter of preferred labelling. The substantive point is that the stock spent the last 18-24 months of the base accumulating in a tight £67-£80 range with rising volume on accumulation days — institutional fingerprints.

Pivot point. The breakout pivot in mid-2024 was the £75-80 area where the stock cleared the upper boundary of the 2023 consolidation. From that pivot to the current £116.30 is ~+45-50%. By Minervini standards, the post-pivot move has been measured rather than parabolic, which is consistent with the deep-base pattern (slower, steadier advance off institutional accumulation rather than retail momentum chase).

Implication for entry. A new buyer is not buying the breakout from the multi-year base. That entry window closed in mid-2024. The relevant question is: does the current setup support a continuation entry? See Section 8.
