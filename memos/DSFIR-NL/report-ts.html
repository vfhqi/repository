Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 13 findings

Section summary
  1. DSM-Firmenich represents a high-quality specialty chemicals play that has completed its core portfolio transformation through the divestment of its animal nutrition unit to focus on high-margin consumer markets.
1.
Specialty ingredients business: The group manufactures and sells high-value specialty ingredients, including flavors, fragrances, cultures, and nutritional molecules, serving global consumer brands
2.
Turnaround potential: The investment case rests on the business accelerating its organic growth and expanding margins as it completes the separation of the volatile animal nutrition segment
3.
Valuation discount: This transition is expected to trigger a significant multiple re-rating towards peer levels, unlocking substantial shareholder returns through dividends and share repurchases
4.
Divestment execution: The single most critical swing factor is the successful execution and separation of the Animal Nutrition and Health segment, which removes severe vitamin price volatility
5.
Improved profitability: During fiscal year 2025, the continuing operations generated €9,034 million in revenue, representing a solid 3% organic sales growth over the prior year
6.
Historical volatility: Over the multi-year period, legacy DSM suffered from severe EBITDA contractions, dropping from €2,275 million in pro forma 2022 to €1,777 million in 2023 due to the global vitamin crash
7.
Tariff headwinds: High raw material inflation and logistical disruptions in the Middle East present risks of temporary cost squeezes and consumer downtrading
8.
Compelling outlook: A closer look is highly warranted because the stock trades at a deep multiple discount to pure-play ingredients peers despite superior core growth potential
9.
Synergy capture: The delivery of targeted cost and revenue synergies remains on track, with the full €175 million cost-synergy run-rate completed by year-end 2025
10.
Cash flow recovery: Continuing operations generated €950 million in adjusted free cash flow in 2025, driven by improved working capital management and lower capital expenditures
11.
Capital allocation shift: Management has initiated a €540 million share buyback program, demonstrating a disciplined approach to returning capital to shareholders
12.
Core margin leverage: Post-separation core EBITDA margins are expected to expand by over 100 basis points, driven by self-help measures and structural mix improvements

Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The business model is built on innovative specialty ingredients and co-creation capabilities across three core consumer-facing segments that exhibit high customer stickiness and strong entry barriers.

Core Operating Segments and Products

1.
Segmental revenue mix: The continuing operations are organized into three core consumer-focused business units, comprising Perfumery and Beauty, Taste, Texture and Health, and Health, Nutrition and Care
2.
Perfumery and Beauty: This division creates premium fragrances, cosmetic ingredients, and specialty aroma molecules, generating €3,760 million in net sales during fiscal year 2025
3.
Taste, Texture and Health: This business unit partners with the food and beverage industry to supply flavorings, enzymes, cultures, and texturizers, delivering €3,146 million in 2025 revenue
4.
Health, Nutrition and Care: This segment provides critical human nutrients, dietary supplements, biomedical solutions, and early life nutrition ingredients, accounting for €2,102 million in sales
5.
Premium fine fragrances: Perfumery and Beauty's fine fragrance business designs prestige and lifestyle scent formulations for high-end luxury brands, exhibiting high-single-digit organic growth

Value Chain Economics and Customer Dynamics

6.
High-value position: The company occupies a highly profitable, research-intensive position in the ingredient value chain, developing proprietary intellectual property that is hard for competitors to replicate
7.
Fragrant product integration: Scent and flavor ingredients are deeply integrated into customers' final recipes, making up a negligible portion of total product cost but driving consumer purchase preference
8.
Diverse customer base: The customer base is highly diversified across global and regional consumer packaged goods brands, with no single customer representing more than 10% of total group sales
9.
High switching costs: Customer relationships are highly sticky due to significant switching costs, as any alteration in ingredient formulation can alter product taste or scent profiles
10.
Consolidated industry structure: The global ingredients industry is highly consolidated, dominated by a few key players, including Givaudan, Symrise, and International Flavors and Fragrances

Portfolio Transformation and Strategy

11.
Active business pruning: Under its strategic portfolio tuning program, management has actively divested non-core, commoditized assets like marine lipids and yeast extracts, which represented over €600 million in low-margin sales
12.
High-margin focus: Strategic focus is on high-growth, high-margin areas like early life nutrition, biomedical, and specialty flavors while deprioritizing volatile chemicals
13.
Other side of the story: Around 20% of the remaining core portfolio consists of non-differentiated ingredients that face severe pricing pressure from lower-cost Chinese producers

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The group is emerging from a severe multi-year downturn driven by a historic collapse in global vitamin prices, with continuing operations now displaying improving margins and cash conversion.

Growth and Margin Trajectory

1.
Revenue trend: Continuing operations revenue reached €9,034 million in 2025, down slightly by 0.2% on a reported basis from €9,054 million in 2024, but up 3% organically
2.
EBITDA margin expansion: Continuing adjusted EBITDA margins expanded from 19.3% in 2024 to 19.6% in 2025, driven by post-merger cost synergies and business mix optimization
3.
Synergy delivery: The company successfully completed its cost-synergy program by the end of 2025, achieving the full run-rate target of €175 million in annual savings
4.
Historical earnings drop: On a group level, pro forma adjusted EBITDA plummeted by 22% from €2,275 million in pro forma 2022 to €1,777 million in 2023 as global vitamin prices collapsed
5.
Divisional EBITDA growth: Taste, Texture and Health adjusted EBITDA increased by 4% to €648 million in 2025 from €626 million in 2024, reflecting structural margin improvement

Cash Conversion and Capital Structure

6.
Free cash flow generation: Continuing adjusted gross operating free cash flow reached €950 million in 2025, representing a solid cash conversion rate of 10.5% over net sales
7.
Solid balance sheet: Net debt sequentially increased to €3.3 billion by the end of 2025, representing a manageable leverage ratio of 1.4x net debt to adjusted EBITDA
8.
Shareholder cash returns: The group launched a €540 million share buyback program in early 2026, supported by initial cash proceeds from its asset divestments
9.
Most recent earnings: During the first quarter of 2026, the continuing operations generated €2,276 million in sales, representing a strong 4% organic growth beat against consensus expectations of 2%
10.
Leverage target headroom: Management maintains a comfortable medium-term leverage range of 1.5x to 2.5x, leaving significant debt headroom for capital returns
11.
Guidance conservatism: Management has a history of setting conservative financial targets, as shown in 2025 when they comfortably met their guidance of at least €2.4 billion in adjusted group EBITDA
12.
Other side of the story: Large-scale corporate integration has led to temporary operational disruptions and customer service issues, resulting in Givaudan capturing market share in fine fragrances

Section 5 – The possible investment case 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward earnings trajectory is supported by high-margin product innovations and corporate separation of the volatile animal nutrition segment, creating a high-probability case for multiple rerating.

Key Structural Growth Drivers

1.
Separation of animal nutrition: The divestment of the Animal Nutrition and Health segment to CVC for €2.2 billion removes commodity volatility and elevates group margins
2.
Separation proceeds: Total proceeds from the transaction include €1.2 billion in initial cash, a 20% retained stake, and an earn-out potential of up to €500 million
3.
High-margin nutrition innovation: Rapid commercialization of Human Milk Oligosaccharides in early life nutrition is expected to drive a €100 million sales step-up
4.
Methane-reducing Bovaer expansion: The innovative methane-reducing feed additive Bovaer is projected to scale up from €40 million in 2025 sales to over €200 million by 2028

Synergies and Margin Expansion Levers

5.
Cross-selling synergies: Ongoing commercial synergies from blending legacy DSM ingredients with legacy Firmenich flavors are tracking toward a €500 million revenue target by 2027
6.
Capital expenditure normalization: Capex is projected to normalize from 6.5% of sales in 2026 to around 5% of sales in 2027, substantially boosting free cash flow conversion
7.
Capital allocation optionality: Deleveraging allows scope for the company to buy back up to 8% of its total market capitalization by the end of 2028
8.
Early Life Nutrition gains: Temporary market share gains of 1-2% in early life nutrition are supported by customer concerns over competitor contamination issues

Medium-Term Financial Ambitions

9.
Accelerating organic sales: Management has guided to medium-term organic sales growth of 5% to 7% starting from fiscal year 2028 onwards
10.
EBITDA margin expansion: Adjusted EBITDA margins are projected to reach 22% to 23% in the medium term, driven by synergy execution and operating leverage
11.
Free cash flow targets: The adjusted gross operating free cash flow conversion target was upgraded to at least 14% of sales, reflecting high cash-generation potential
12.
Other side of the story: Top-line revenue synergies may fail to convert as quickly as expected, as historical precedents in the sector show only a 25% average conversion rate

Section 6 – Valuation 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The stock trades at a significant and unjustified valuation discount to its historical averages and pure-play ingredients peers, presenting a compelling entry point for multiple expansion.
1.
Multiple discount: DSM-Firmenich trades at an estimated forward multiple of 10.4x EV/EBITDA, representing a deep 26% discount to pure-play F&F peers trading at 14x
2.
Adjusted P/E discount: Morgan Stanley estimates that the core price-to-earnings multiple sits at approximately 17x when adjusted for non-cash amortization items, representing a discount to peers
3.
Amortization restatement: Management restated its reporting to add back pre-merger acquisition purchase price allocation amortization of €420 million annually, reducing the optical price-to-earnings multiple by three turns
4.
Peer dividend yield: The stock offers a dividend yield of 4.5% in 2027 according to ING, which represents a highly attractive cash return
5.
Price target upside: The sell-side target price of €84 per share from ING implies substantial upside potential from the current trading level
6.
Peer multiple comparison: Swiss peer Givaudan trades at a massive 60% EV/EBITDA premium to DSM-Firmenich, highlighting the massive valuation unlock potential post-separation
7.
Other side of the story: Multiple expansion may be delayed for several quarters as investors demand prolonged operational execution before valuing the company in line with Givaudan

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The primary risks to the investment thesis stem from macroeconomic consumer slowdowns and cost inflation, although the core business shows strong resilience to cyclical volatility.

Macroeconomic and Market Risks

1.
Consumer downtrading: Prolonged macroeconomic weakness may drive end-consumers to trade down to private-label products, which reduces the pricing power of global CPG brands and impacts ingredient orders
2.
Input cost inflation: Renewed geopolitical conflicts in the Middle East present risks of petrochemical raw material inflation, which compresses margins due to pricing lags
3.
Antitrust overhang: An ongoing industry-wide antitrust investigation launched in March 2023 against major flavor and fragrance players remains unresolved and represents a tail-risk
4.
Vitamin price deterioration: Although the animal nutrition business is being divested, the retained 20% stake leaves the group exposed to potential further earnings drag from low vitamin prices

Operational and Technical Risks

5.
Execution delays: Potential delays in finalizing the complex carve-out and regulatory approvals for the Animal Nutrition and Health divestment would keep the volatile segment on the balance sheet
6.
False friend signal: A strong volume rebound driven by short-term customer restocking, like the 8% organic sales growth in Perfumery and Beauty in first quarter 2026, can falsely signal permanent structural demand acceleration
7.
Early-warning signal: The key early-warning signal to monitor is the quarterly organic sales growth gap relative to best-in-class peer Givaudan, which has averaged 530 basis points historically
8.
Other side of the story: Approximately 55% of DSM-Firmenich's core product portfolio is highly inelastic, making overall demand highly resilient during recessions

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership transitions are actively driving a positive cultural pivot from integration-focused complexity to execution-led operational focus.
1.
Sole CEO appointment: In late 2023, the board simplified the governance structure by transitioning from a co-CEO structure to appointing Dimitri de Vreeze as sole Chief Executive Officer
2.
New CFO appointment: Ralf Schmeitz was appointed as Chief Financial Officer in September 2023, bringing extensive internal financial control and portfolio restructuring experience
3.
Division head change: In January 2026, Maurizio Clementi succeeded Patrick Niels as head of the Taste, Texture and Health business unit to drive execution-led growth
4.
HR leadership transition: Nikeisha van Sleeuwen was appointed as Chief Human Resources Officer in early 2026, succeeding Mieke Van de Capelle after the completion of the merger integration
5.
Insider alignment indicators: Shareholders approved the maximum executive compensation packages for 2027 at the Annual General Meeting, aligning leadership incentives with core earnings execution

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. DSM-Firmenich warrants immediate and deep fundamental research because the company is at an attractive inflection point with low valuation and high self-help margin potential.
1.
Strongest bull argument: The strongest argument is the structural margin uplift and volatility reduction resulting from the animal nutrition carve-out, coupled with high-value product innovation
2.
Other side of the story: Severe pricing pressure on the 20% commoditized portion of the portfolio could cap medium-term organic growth below management's targets
