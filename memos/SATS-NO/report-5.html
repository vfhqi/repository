EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

FY2025 was a genuine breakthrough year for SATS. The operating leverage thesis that management has articulated since the CMD preview became numerically visible: 9% revenue growth produced 18% EBITDA growth, 24% EBIT growth, 45% net profit growth, and 25% FCF growth. The margin of compounding — where each revenue pound/krone drops through to profit at a higher rate — is now firmly established in the financial history.

The catalyst calendar into early 2026 is active. Q1-2026 results (30 April 2026 — three days from the date of this memo) will be the first live test of the 2026 guidance framework. Management pre-guided that higher price increases may reduce Q1-2026 net member growth vs Q1-2025. This is actually a positive quality signal: it means the company is prioritising ARPM accretion over volume, which is the correct strategic choice given the operating leverage economics.

The balance sheet is the cleanest it has been since listing. Net debt at 1.1x EBITDA, undrawn revolving credit of NOK 891m, FCF running above NOK 500m per annum — SATS has the financial resources to pursue the CMD strategy (8–12 new clubs per year, remodelling programme, digital investment) without stretching capital allocation.

The first dividend payment is structurally important. It signals management's conviction in sustained FCF. The 61% payout ratio and simultaneous share buyback programme provide both income and mechanical per-share earnings support. Combined shareholder returns (dividend + buyback) represent a yield materially above the Norwegian 10-year government bond rate.

The Q1-2026 preview is the key near-term uncertainty. If member count holds at or above 750,000 (stable vs Q4-25 end) and ARPM continues growing above 4% year-on-year, the FY2026 earnings trajectory will be confirmed positive. If member count falls below 730,000 due to price-related churn, sentiment will turn cautious despite the revenue arithmetic being broadly neutral.

SECTION 3: COMPANY DESCRIPTION

SATS AS was founded in 1995 and is headquartered in Oslo, Norway. It listed on the Oslo Stock Exchange (Oslo Bors) and trades under the ticker SATS. The company operates fitness clubs and wellness facilities under the SATS brand (Norway, Sweden, Denmark), the ELIXIA brand (Finland), the Fresh Fitness brand (budget tier, Norway), and SATSYoga.

Geographic footprint: Approximately 250 clubs across four Nordic countries. Norway is the largest market (44% of Q4-25 revenue, approximately 345,000 members). Sweden is second (35%, approximately 256,000 members). Denmark and Finland together represent approximately 21%.

Capital structure: Shares outstanding approximately 326 million (implied). Net debt NOK 967m (before IFRS 16 lease liabilities). Revolving credit facility NOK 2,500m (NOK 891m undrawn). Financial covenants: Net Debt/EBITDA before IFRS 16 must not exceed 3.5x (well inside at 1.1x).

L3Y performance summary:
- FY2023: Revenue growth positive, EBITDA recovering post-COVID
- FY2024: Revenue growing, EBITDA before IFRS 16 up approximately 20%, dividend not yet paid
- FY2025: Revenue +9%, EBITDA +18%, net profit +45%, FCF +25%, first dividend paid

The three-year arc is a classical post-distress recovery: leverage declining, margins expanding, cash generation accelerating. The company is now past the recovery phase and into the compounding phase.

SECTION 6: MARGIN PROFILE

EBITDA margin before IFRS 16: NOK 871m / NOK 5,509m = 15.8%. This is the metric management uses and the CMD target is set on.

EBITDA including IFRS 16: Materially higher, as lease costs are capitalised rather than expensed. The reported (IFRS 16 inclusive) EBITDA would include the depreciation of right-of-use assets and interest on lease liabilities as separate line items, inflating the EBITDA figure but not representing true cash earnings from operations.

Operating leverage evidence: Revenue grew 9% in FY2025. EBITDA grew 18%. The incremental EBITDA margin on additional revenue is approximately 30–35% — significantly above the blended margin, indicating the fixed cost base is being well-controlled.

L3Y EBITDA margin trend:
- FY2023: approximately 12–13% EBITDA before IFRS 16 margin (recovery phase)
- FY2024: approximately 14–15%
- FY2025: 15.8%

The margin trajectory is consistently upward. CMD target of NOK 1.1bn on a revenue base of approximately NOK 6,200m (assumed) implies 17.7% margin — achievable given the trajectory.

Cost structure: Primary costs are club leases (treated as IFRS 16 right-of-use assets), staff costs, utilities/energy, and maintenance capex. The CMD maintenance capex guidance of 5% of revenues = approximately NOK 275m, consistent with historical capex patterns. New club capex (8–12 clubs/year) is additive.

Rating: GREEN-YELLOW — strong trend, CMD margin target requires continued discipline.

New clubs: 8–12 per year, quality over quantity. In a market with 30%+ penetration, new club economics are more demanding than in lower-penetration markets like continental Europe. SATS is disciplined about location selection.

SECTION 10: SCEPTICAL / BEAR CASE

CMD credibility: The NOK 1.1bn target has no formal year attached. This structural ambiguity allows management to claim "on track" for multiple years without defining what "on time" means. The risk is that EBITDA plateaus in the NOK 950–1,000m range due to competitive pressure or cost inflation, and the CMD target becomes a perpetually receding horizon.

Treningshelse: 93 Norwegian locations, 115,000+ members, stated market leadership ambition. In Norway's mature market, SATS's new member acquisition will increasingly be from Treningshelse's catchment, and vice versa. Competitive pricing pressure at the sub-NOK 400/month tier could slowly migrate members price-sensitive enough to churn SATS at the margin.

NOK weakness: SATS reports in NOK. If NOK weakens significantly against EUR (possible in a world oil demand decline scenario), the EUR/GBP-equivalent value of SATS's earnings declines. This is more relevant for international investors than for domestic Norwegian holders.

Price increase risk in 2026: Management pre-guided that 2026 price increases may dampen Q1-2026 member growth. If the churn response to higher prices is larger than anticipated, net member count could fall. A 2% decline in the member base (to approximately 740,000) combined with 5% ARPM growth would produce flat-to-slightly-negative revenue — not catastrophic but a negative earnings surprise.

IFRS 16 complexity: The distinction between reported and before-IFRS-16 metrics is genuinely confusing for less sophisticated investors. If IFRS 16 lease liabilities (not included in the 1.1x leverage ratio) were included, total leverage would be significantly higher. Analysts and investors who miss this can misread the company's true financial obligations.

Full downside scenario: Norwegian consumer weakens (Sweden weakness spills over), Treningshelse intensifies Norwegian pricing competition, 2026 price increases cause material churn, ARPM growth slows to 2%, revenue growth falls to 3–4%, EBITDA growth flat, CMD target delayed to FY2029+. In this scenario, the stock re-rates from a growth story to a mature consumer defensive. Share price risk: 20–30% downside from current levels.

Rating: YELLOW — bear risks are specific and manageable but not trivial.
