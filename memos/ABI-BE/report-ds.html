Section 1 – The move(s): what the data shows 4 findings

  1. The stock exhibited a pronounced V-shaped trajectory, absorbing macro-driven selling pressure in early spring before exploding upward on fundamental earnings delivery in May
1.
Initial weakness – macro drift: From early March through mid-April, the share price drifted lower by approximately 10 percent, falling from the mid-60s to near 60 euros as the market priced in perceived risks of global supply chain disruptions
2.
Macro correlation – sector drag: This downward drift was highly correlated with the broader European consumer staples sell-off as geopolitical anxiety regarding energy and commodity prices gripped global equity markets, suggesting that institutional sellers were unwilling to capitulate below a 16x forward earnings multiple despite peak macro anxiety
3.
Other side of the story: While the May rally appears structurally sound, the preceding April sell-off highlights the stock's acute vulnerability to external geopolitical shocks, meaning any sudden escalation in energy or commodity markets could swiftly reverse these gains

Regional pessimism – United States drag: Consensus heavily penalized the North American segment, expecting mid-single-digit volume declines as the market assumed the company's core domestic portfolio had permanently lost retail shelf space

Regional pessimism – China macro: Analysts also modeled severe contractions in the Asia-Pacific region, anticipating that Chinese consumer weakness and a structural shift away from on-premise dining would cripple premium beer sales

Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The company delivered a flawless fundamental beat across every major operating metric, dismantling the bearish consensus and proving the pricing power of its global portfolio

Earnings delivery

1.
Volume growth – surprise expansion: Against a published consensus of a 0.5 percent decline, organic volumes actually grew 0.8 percent, representing a massive 130 basis point deviation from the street's baseline expectation
2.
Revenue delivery – top-line surge: The company reported organic revenue growth of 5.8 percent versus the 3.0 percent consensus, driven by an exceptional 4.5 percent increase in revenue per hectoliter
3.
EBITDA outperformance – margin defense: Organic EBITDA grew 5.3 percent, effectively doubling the consensus expectation of 2.6 percent, proving that disciplined cost management could offset targeted increases in sales and marketing investments
4.
EPS trajectory – structural beat: Underlying earnings per share printed at 0.97 dollars, crushing the consensus estimate of 0.89 dollars and delivering a 20.8 percent year-over-year reported increase

Geographic performance

5.
Middle Americas – record throughput: The region shattered expectations with 4.8 percent organic volume growth versus the 2.1 percent consensus, as Mexico and Colombia delivered record-high first-quarter volumes
6.
South America – resilient pricing: While volumes were slightly negative at 0.3 percent, the segment delivered a stunning 9.0 percent organic revenue growth, totally defying the buy-side whisper that Brazilian inflation would compress margins
7.
EMEA delivery – holiday tailwind: The Europe, Middle East, and Africa segment posted 1.3 percent organic volume growth against a flat consensus, actively aided by the favorable timing of the Easter holiday trading period
8.
North America – mixed reality: United States shipments to wholesalers fell 3.2 percent, missing some optimistic models, but sales to retailers actually grew 0.3 percent, marking a critical stabilization that surprised even bullish sell-side models
9.
Asia-Pacific – truly weak spot: Asia-Pacific was the only truly weak spot with organic revenue declining 1.3 percent, though this actually beat the deeply pessimistic consensus expectation of a 4.0 percent contraction

Management commentary and capital allocation

10.
Cost hedge confirmation – bear thesis destroyed: Management explicitly confirmed that the 2026 cost base is almost fully hedged, instantly evaporating the primary bearish thesis that had depressed the stock in April
11.
Forward guidance – aggressively reiterated: Despite the volatile global backdrop, the board reiterated full-year guidance of 4 to 8 percent EBITDA growth, sending a clear signal of supreme confidence to a jittery market
12.
Capital allocation – shareholder returns: The company confirmed its commitment to systematic deleveraging and ongoing share repurchases, reinforcing the thesis that free cash flow conversion will remain exceptionally robust through 2027
13.
Digital transformation – platform scaling: Management highlighted that the proprietary BEES digital marketplace grew gross merchandise value by 55 percent to 1.1 billion dollars, proving that the company is successfully monetizing its downstream distribution ecosystem
14.
Other side of the story: While the first-quarter delivery was exceptional, bears argue that the outperformance was artificially inflated by the early timing of Easter, meaning the second quarter will face a severe negative volume reversion as this calendar benefit unwinds

Section 7 – Synthesis: most likely cause, counter-hypothesis, residual, decision 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The stock's violent upward re-rating was unequivocally caused by a massive earnings beat that destroyed the prevailing bearish consensus regarding emerging market weakness and commodity-driven margin compression
1.
Leading cause – fundamental superiority: The absolute primary driver of the move was the first-quarter delivery, where organic EBITDA expansion of 5.3 percent fundamentally broke the consensus thesis that pricing power had evaporated (High plausibility)
2.
Secondary cause – macro relief: The explicit management confirmation regarding 2026 cost hedging acted as a vital secondary catalyst, instantly neutralizing the Middle East commodity panic that had artificially depressed the shares in April (High plausibility)
3.
Tertiary cause – sector rotation: Global portfolio managers actively rotated capital out of struggling spirits companies and vulnerable European brewers into AB InBev as the superior emerging market play (Medium plausibility)
4.
Counter-hypothesis – calendar illusion: The strongest counter-argument to the bullish thesis is that the entire first-quarter volume beat was an accounting illusion created by the early Easter holiday, meaning the positive momentum will mechanically reverse in the second quarter to place the company in the second quarter
5.
Unexplained residual – valuation cap: Despite the flawless operational delivery and subsequent share price spike, the stock completely failed to close the structural valuation discount to European consumer staples, incorrectly valuing it as an internal IT tool rather than a highly lucrative, standalone B2B marketplace capable of generating pure-profit third-party merchandising revenue
6.
Investment decision – verify depletions: Investors should maintain their long positions to capture the compounding yield, but must immediately commission targeted channel checks on United States summer depletion rates to confirm that the first-quarter stabilization was not merely a temporary promotional mirage
7.
Other side of the story: If the United States consumer refuses to trade up to the company's premium innovations during the critical summer selling season, the reliance on Latin American pricing power will be stretched to its absolute limit, jeopardizing the 3-year predictable earnings trajectory that underpins the entire investment thesis
