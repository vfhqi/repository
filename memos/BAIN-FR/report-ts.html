Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM represents a highly robust sovereign asset play that combines inflation-protected premium rental yields with volatile luxury tourism and gaming in Monaco.
1.
Core operating model: SBM operates in three highly complementary sectors, casino gaming, luxury hotel accommodations and catering, and premium retail and residential real estate rentals. It owns the physical land and buildings of its entire resort, representing a unique asset-heavy luxury business
2.
Asymmetric investment case: SBM offers a unique combination of contractually indexed retail rental growth and ultra-luxury tourism demand, backed by an irreplaceable land bank in Monte-Carlo. This asset-heavy model delivers highly defensive yields while retaining significant operational leverage during high-roller tourist seasons
3.
Primary swing factor: The single most important swing factor is gaming luck variance combined with compliance-driven volumes, which caused gaming revenue to drop 3% in FY 24/25 before rebounding 20% in FY 25/26
4.
Robust revenue expansion: SBM has delivered solid top-line growth, with consolidated revenue rising 12% to €861.6 million in FY 25/26, following a 9% increase to €768.0 million in FY 24/25
5.
Positive operating performance: Group operating income grew 16% to reach €86.6 million in FY 25/26, up from €74.5 million in FY 24/25 and €73.6 million in FY 23/24
6.
Critical compliance risk: Strict anti-money laundering and regulatory compliance rules represent a major friction point that can suppress VIP gaming volumes and increase structural administrative costs
7.
Favourable first-look verdict: A closer look is highly warranted because SBM's prime rental indexation and high-end catering projects provide a highly predictable baseline that supports the forward earnings trajectory

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM operates an irreplaceable sovereign real estate and casino monopoly in Monaco, serving ultra-wealthy customers across premium retail, gaming, and hospitality.

Core Operating Divisions and Asset Portfolio

1.
Premium luxury offerings: SBM operates iconic real estate, gaming venues, and hospitality assets that define Monaco's global identity. Its properties include the legendary Casino de Monte-Carlo and Casino Café de Paris
2.
World-class hospitality footprint: The hotel segment comprises world-renowned hotels like the Hôtel de Paris and Hôtel Hermitage, which feature ten Michelin stars in total. SBM also runs high-end restaurants such as Amazónico, Cedric Grolet, Marlow, and Jondal at La Vigie
3.
High-yielding rental portfolio: SBM's rental division leases premium retail boutique spaces and luxury residential units at One Monte-Carlo and the Café de Paris complex. This division achieves occupancy rates close to 100% and generates highly predictable cash flows
4.
Strategic group investments: SBM holds strategic stakes in Banijay Group NV, a major listed entertainment company. It previously maintained a structured relationship with Betclic, which was re-organized to optimize capital return

Competitive Moat and Sovereign Concessions

5.
Exclusive sovereign monopoly: SBM holds an exclusive casino gaming concession in the Principality of Monaco, protecting it from local competition. This sovereign partnership ensures that SBM owns and controls the most premium land bank in Monte-Carlo
6.
Irreplaceable physical assets: The company's real estate assets are situated in the immediate vicinity of the Place du Casino, creating an absolute barrier to entry. These prime locations cannot be replicated by any competitor in the European luxury market
7.
High customer switching costs: VIP gaming clients benefit from highly customized luxury services, exclusive private suites, and ultra-private clubs built during the winter. These bespoke offerings foster immense customer loyalty and high switching costs among global jet-setters

High-End Customer Base and Concentration

8.
UHNW wealth concentration: SBM's client base is highly concentrated within the ultra-high-net-worth individual demographic. This concentration makes the hotel and gaming revenues sensitive to global wealth creation rather than mass consumer trends
9.
Global geographic footprint: While localized in Monaco, SBM's customers are globally diversified, pulling luxury tourists from the Middle East, Americas, and Asia. The strategic alliance with Galaxy Entertainment Group helps SBM capture premium Asian high-roller traffic

Industry Dynamics and Listed Peer Landscape

10.
Consolidated luxury destination market: SBM operates in a highly consolidated luxury destination market. It does not compete with mass-market operators, but shares luxury hospitality dynamics with peers like Accor, Melia, and InterContinental Hotels Group
11.
Unique asset-heavy model: Unlike major European peers that have transitioned to asset-light management models, SBM maintains full ownership of its real estate and hotel assets. This model aligns SBM more closely with premium asset-rich hotel REITs like Pandox
12.
Other side of the story: Asset concentration in a single geographical square kilometer leaves SBM highly vulnerable to local infrastructure disruptions, extreme weather, or regional security incidents. Any disruption on the French Riviera would immediately stop SBM's cash generation, unlike geographically diversified peers

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 11 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM's financial results reflect strong top-line momentum and rising operating income across rentals and hotels, although gaming luck introduces year-to-year net income volatility.
[ A data table from this section could not be extracted cleanly from the source and is omitted; the figures are carried in the surrounding findings. ]

Operating Income

Multi-Year Revenue and Earnings Expansion

1.
Sustained top-line growth: SBM has demonstrated consistent top-line growth, with revenue expanding by 12% to €861.6 million in FY 25/26 from €768.0 million in FY 24/25. This follows a 9% increase in FY 24/25 from the €704.0 million recorded in FY 23/24
2.
Improving operating income: Operating income reached €86.6 million in FY 25/26, representing a 16% increase compared to €74.5 million in FY 24/25 and €73.6 million in FY 23/24. This trend shows a highly positive operational leverage as revenue scales up
3.
Strong net profitability: Consolidated net income reached a record €112.9 million in FY 25/26, up from €110.1 million in FY 24/25 and €103.9 million in FY 23/24. These returns represent a robust net profit margin profile over several years

Segment Profitability and Margin Structure

4.
Excellent rental performance: Rental operating profit saw strong growth of €7.1 million in FY 25/26, following a €11.4 million operating income increase in FY 24/25. This was driven by gradual lease take-up at the refurbished Café de Paris complex and contractual rent indexations
5.
Hotel segment operating leverage: Hotel operating profit grew by €3.3 million in FY 25/26 and €7.3 million in FY 24/25. This expansion was driven by a rise in average room rates at Hôtel Hermitage and Monte-Carlo Bay Hotel & Resort
6.
Gaming operating profit rebound: Gaming operating profit grew by €13.8 million in FY 25/26, fully recovering from a €14.7 million decline in FY 24/25 when the house played unluckier. This shows the inherent volatility of SBM's gaming revenues despite steady underlying volumes

Balance Sheet Strength and Return on Capital

7.
Massive asset-backing: SBM's balance sheet is incredibly strong, with group shareholder's equity rising to €1,678 million as of March 31, 2026, from €1,643 million as of March 31, 2025. This substantial equity base represents deep asset-backing for long-term investors
8.
Declining financial income: Financial income decreased to €26.3 million in FY 25/26 from €35.6 million in FY 24/25. This fall was driven by lower eurozone interest rates, which reduced returns on SBM's short-term financial investments

Guidance Track Record and Transparency

9.
Conservative qualitative guidance: Management provides qualitative trend guidance rather than quantitative forecasts due to the gaming segment's random nature. For instance, in June 2026, management stated that the first two months of FY 26/27 were in line with FY 25/26 trends but refrained from full-year forecasting
10.
Other side of the story: SBM's massive balance sheet is highly capital-intensive, and its lack of leverage optimization keeps its return on equity (ROE) relatively low at approximately 6.73% based on FY 25/26 net income of €112.9 million and equity of €1,678 million. This represents a lower return profile compared to asset-light hotel peers covered by Morgan Stanley, such as InterContinental Hotels Group which operates at significantly higher ROE

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM's investment case is strengthening based on predictable premium rental growth and luxury room-rate inflation, which offset gaming volatility.

Multi-Year Real Estate Lease Indexation

1.
Highly predictable rental growth: SBM's rental property portfolio represents a high-plausibility driver of forward earnings. Rental revenues reached €156.5 million in FY 25/26, up 4% from €149.9 million in FY 24/25, which itself grew 11% from €135.4 million in FY 23/24
2.
Lease indexation and low vacancy: This growth is driven by contractual lease indexation and near-zero vacancy rates in prime areas like One Monte-Carlo and the refurbished Café de Paris complex. The mechanism of failure would be a severe eurozone economic crash that forces retail tenant defaults

Ultra-Luxury Hospitality and F&B Yields

3.
Premium accommodation price power: SBM's hotel revenue is a high-plausibility driver, rising 11% to €443.1 million in FY 25/26 from €399.9 million in FY 24/25 and €345.1 million in FY 23/24. This expansion is supported by strong growth in average accommodation rates across premium hotels
4.
Renovation-driven rate hikes: Average rates have increased partly thanks to newly refurbished rooms at Hôtel Hermitage and Monte-Carlo Bay Hotel & Resort. The mechanism of failure would be a decline in global jet-setter travel or high hospitality labor inflation
5.
F&B and Michelin-starred culinary draw: SBM's catering revenues rose 11% in FY 25/26, driven by high-profile openings like Amazónico Monte-Carlo, Marlow, and Cedric Grolet boutique. With ten Michelin stars, SBM's world-class culinary collection acts as a major customer acquisition tool

Strategic Financial and Group Investments

6.
Valuable media and sports-betting stakes: SBM holds strategic stakes in Banijay Group NV, a major listed entertainment company. It previously maintained a structured relationship with Betclic, which was re-organized to optimize capital return
7.
Independent driver execution: These drivers operate independently, meaning the rental division provides strong downside support even if casino gaming volumes suffer from unluckier outcomes
8.
Other side of the story: SBM's forward growth targets are highly reliant on high-roller volumes, making its forward EPS trajectory vulnerable to Chinese capital flight controls or stricter international AML scrutiny

Section 6 – Valuation 6 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM shares are highly illiquid and trade at a deep discount to real estate NAV, making them cheap on assets but demanding relative to traditional hotel earnings multiples.
1.
Asset-rich premium valuation: SBM's market value is heavily backed by its net asset value, with group shareholder's equity reaching €1,678 million as of March 31, 2026. This provides immense balance sheet safety compared to typical highly leveraged leisure operators
2.
Significant peer multiples premium: European luxury hotel peers trade at demanding levels, with InterContinental Hotels Group trading at 28.5x 2026e P/E and Accor at 19.2x 2026e P/E. These peer multiples highlight the high premium investors pay for established hospitality brands
3.
Gambling sector comparables: Global gambling peers covered by Morgan Stanley trade at a weighted average 11.9x 2027e P/E, whereas SBM's associate Banijay Group NV trades at a lower 7.0x 2027e P/E. SBM's indirect exposure to these digital sectors offers diversified upside potential
4.
Deeply discounted public float: The market appears to be pricing in a persistent "conglomerate and liquidity discount" due to SBM's extremely low free float and sovereign state control. This prevents short-term arbitrage or active activist pressure to unlock the underlying real estate value
5.
Other side of the story: If eurozone interest rates continue to fall, SBM's financial income, which reached €26.3 million in FY 25/26 and €35.6 million in FY 24/25, will continue to contract, compressing earnings. This compression would make the shares look increasingly expensive on a trailing price-to-earnings basis

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. SBM faces risks from gaming luck volatility, regulatory AML frictions, and interest rate compression, though cushioned by prime real estate and sovereign backing.

Key Operational and Regulatory Vulnerabilities

1.
High gaming luck sensitivity: Casino revenue is inherently volatile due to the random nature of gaming outcomes. In FY 24/25, the house played unluckier, resulting in a 3% decline in gaming revenue and a €14.7 million operating income reduction
2.
Strict regulatory friction: The strict application of financial compliance and anti-money laundering rules in Monaco represents a persistent headwind. This compliance burden can suppress gaming volumes and increase structural administrative costs

Financial and Structural Risks

3.
Eurozone interest rate sensitivity: SBM's financial results are highly sensitive to eurozone interest rates. A drop in interest rates directly reduced SBM's financial profit by €9.3 million in FY 25/26 to €26.3 million
4.
No published short-seller reports: There are no published short-seller reports or material short interest on SBM due to the stock's extreme illiquidity and state ownership. This lack of market float protects the stock from aggressive downside attacks
5.
Early-warning monitoring signal: The single most critical metric to monitor is the quarterly gaming volume trends alongside local regulatory policy announcements. A material slowdown in volumes would signal a deterioration in SBM's premium appeal
6.
Other side of the story: SBM's risks are significantly overstated because its premier retail leases are contractually linked to inflation indexation, securing highly predictable income. This inflation protection guarantees a solid financial baseline regardless of gaming volatility

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 4 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership remains stable under CEO Stéphane Valeri's strategic push into international and property development, despite a key commercial vacancy.
1.
Stable leadership and reorganization: SBM is led by Chairman and CEO Stéphane Valeri, who has successfully reorganized the group's governance to focus on property development and international expansion
2.
Key commercial executive vacancy: Julien Munoz, SBM's Chief Commercial Officer and Executive Committee Member, left the company to join The Hongkong and Shanghai Hotels in late September 2025. At SBM, Munoz led commercial strategy, digital/CRM transformation, and revenue optimization, creating a critical vacancy in the executive team
3.
No material insider transactions: No material insider buying or selling by directors has been reported in recent filings, reflecting stable but inactive insider capital activity

Strongest bull argument: SBM offers an irreplaceable, asset-heavy luxury monopoly with contractually indexed rental revenues that protect the downside, combined with significant operational leverage during high-roller tourist seasons

Other side of the story: SBM's extreme illiquidity, combined with sovereign state control, prevents institutional investors from actively engaging to unlock real estate value, leaving capital trapped in a low-ROE structure
