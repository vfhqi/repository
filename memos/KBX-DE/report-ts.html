Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Knorr-Bremse presents a highly predictable earnings inflection story supported by defensive aftermarket cash flows and aggressive cost restructuring.
1.
Dominant market leader: Designs and manufactures safety-critical braking, entrance, and HVAC systems for both global rail and commercial vehicle markets
2.
Self-help margin expansion: Driving earnings acceleration through its BOOST 2026 cost-cutting program and cyclical recovery of truck volumes
3.
Earnings swing factor: Profitability hinges on timing of cyclical recovery in North American truck production rates
4.
Strong margin expansion: Q1 2026 operating margin expanded 140 bps YoY to 13.5%
5.
Chinese market share risk: Increased competition from local Chinese manufacturers poses a long-term risk to high-margin aftermarket streams
6.
Deep work warranted: Highly attractive setup given the dual narrative of defensive rail longevity and cyclical truck operating leverage

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The company's defensive business model features high market shares in highly regulated niches, generating resilient annuity-like aftermarket streams.
1.
Rail segment products: RVS supplies safety-critical systems including braking, entrance, sanitary, HVAC, and wayside signaling systems
2.
Commercial vehicle products: CVS supplies pneumatic braking systems, electronic brake control, steering systems, and fuel-efficiency solutions
3.
Revenue mix split: Rail contributing 55% and Commercial Vehicles contributing 45% of total group revenues in FY25
4.
Aftermarket dominance: Nearly half of total revenues (46.0% in FY25) generated from highly predictable, annuity-like aftermarket streams
5.
Divisional aftermarket exposure: Aftermarket sales account for 56% of Rail division sales and 34% of CVS sales
6.
Highly localized supply: Over 80% of manufacturing, R&D, and procurement conducted locally in major regions – insulates from tariff volatility
7.
High barrier regulation: Extremely high regulatory and safety standards require product homologation processes typically taking 4–8 years
8.
Installed base moat: Products running on more than every second train worldwide
9.
Global duopoly structure: Rail market operates as a highly consolidated duopoly – Knorr-Bremse holds dominant ~50% market share alongside Wabtec
10.
Consolidated buyer power: Commercial vehicle buyer concentration is high – consolidated truck OEMs actively implement dual-source or multi-source procurement strategies
11.
Profit chain positioning: Tier-one supplier position allows co-development of safety-critical vehicle architectures early in design cycles
12.
Other side of the story: Consolidated rail transit buyers such as Alstom have strong bargaining power, threatening component supplier pricing flexibility

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is exceptionally strong, backed by structural cost reductions and cyclical margin leverage as CV markets trough.
1.
Cost structure transformation: BOOST efficiency program has lowered group breakeven by 400 bps since early 2023
2.
Structural portfolio rotation: Divestments of low-margin businesses (Kiepe, Sheppard) alongside ongoing HVAC sale will drive 120 bps margin expansion
3.
Rail division recovery: RVS margins achieved medium-term 16.5% target a year early in FY25; on path to exceed 19% by FY29
4.
High-margin greenfield signaling: Wayside signaling expansion via duagon and Alstom acquisitions, targeting double-digit growth with 18%–20% margin profiles
5.
CV segment inflection: North American truck orders began inflecting in late 2025 – positioning CVS for high-margin operating leverage of 20%–25%
6.
German infrastructure support: Reaching bull case requires German infrastructure spending to materialize, which could add 1.0% p.a. to revenue
7.
Independent driver performance: Key growth drivers are largely independent – CVS cyclical rebound can fire even if public rail spending lags
8.
Operational efficiency gains: Headcount reduction of ~2,400 FTE positions since 2023 has significantly improved regional cost-flexibility
9.
Other side of the story: Supplier margin gains could be fully offset if multi-year fixed rail contracts fail to recover inflation

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is moderate with potential headwinds from localized Chinese competition and short-term CV cyclical volatility.
1.
Customer concentration exposure: High buyer power as major truck OEMs implement dual-sourcing strategies, threatening CVS OE margins
2.
Chinese localization pressure: Local Chinese competitors such as CRRC expanding and threatening high-margin Chinese profit pool
3.
Inflation-related margin squeeze: Multi-year fixed price contracts in RVS restrict rapid pricing adjustments – exposes division to unexpected wage inflation
4.
Block family overhang: Thiele family trust controls 58.99% of shares – potential secondary placement risk
5.
False friend indicator: High short-term book-to-bill ratios can mask underlying project execution delays or order postponements
6.
Critical early warning: Monitoring regional truck production rates, especially in North America, provides earliest signal of CVS cyclical bottoming
7.
Other side of the story: Escalator clauses protect 85% of multi-year contracts, shielding Knorr-Bremse from devastating inflation hits
