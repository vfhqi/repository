§1 General overview
Business Model Post-Primary Products Divestment
Tate & Lyle has completed a multi-year strategic transformation, shedding its legacy bulk commodity roots to
become a pure-play specialty food and beverage ingredients provider. The company historically relied on highly
cyclical commodity products such as high-fructose corn syrup, but it successfully divested its remaining stake in the US-based Primient joint venture to KPS Capital Partners. This divestiture allowed the company to
completely exit the volatile primary products sector. Following this exit, the company acquired CP Kelco for
$1.8 billion in November 2024. 1 2 3 4 • Morgan Stanley This acquisition fundamentally shifted the company's operational profile toward higher-margin solutions. The
integration of CP Kelco elevated the company to a leading position in the global mouthfeel segment, which now
accounts for 56% of total group revenue. 5 6 • Deutsche Bank Research
The combined product portfolio is structured around three core platforms: mouthfeel, sweetening, and fortification. Within this structure, mouthfeel represents 66% of the pro forma sales base, sweetening accounts
for 27%, and fortification makes up the remaining 7%. Geographically, the newly integrated business has
adopted a regional reporting framework. The Americas region constitutes the majority at 51% of total sales,
followed by Europe, the Middle East, and Africa at 31%, and the Asia Pacific region at 18%. 7 8 9 10 • Rothschild & Co Redburn
The company's commercial strategy relies heavily on business-to-business customer relationships with major
food and beverage manufacturers. Large global consumer packaged goods companies such as Nestlé,
Danone, Kraft Heinz, and Unilever represent an estimated 20% to 30% of the total sales base. 11

M&A Strategy and the Ingredion Takeover Proposal
The forward outlook for Tate & Lyle has been fundamentally altered by a live, conditional takeover proposal from US-based competitor Ingredion. Ingredion has submitted a cash offer of 595 pence per share, alongside 20
pence per share in associated dividend payouts, valuing the total proposal at 615 pence per share. Under the
regulations of the UK Takeover Code, Ingredion faces a deadline of June 11, 2026, to formalize a firm intention to
make an offer. 117 118 119 • Morgan Stanley
Morgan Stanley notes that this proposal represents a 59% premium to the undisturbed share price of 375 pence and implies a forward EBITDA multiple of approximately 8.0x for the 2026/2027 fiscal year. The same broker
highlights that because Ingredion and Tate & Lyle represent the top two global players in the sweetener and
texturizer markets, a finalized combination would likely face significant antitrust scrutiny, particularly in North
America. 120 121 • Morgan Stanley
Deutsche Bank Research views the 615 pence offer as a fair price for a strategically aligned asset, noting that it
implies a valuation of 15.1x price-to-earnings (P/E) and 8.4x EV/EBITDA for calendar year 2026. This broker
emphasizes that the offer brings Tate & Lyle's valuation much closer to its specialty ingredient peer group, closing
a persistent and historically unwarranted discount. Furthermore, the broker points out that organic growth
has recently been lackluster, meaning that without this M&A catalyst, the stock had very limited scope for a near-
term valuation re-rating. 122 123 124 • Deutsche Bank Research
