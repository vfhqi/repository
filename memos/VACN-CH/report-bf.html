EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Market Position and Competitive Dominance

VAT Group commands an uncontested 70-77% global market share in semiconductor vacuum valves, reaching nearly 100% at the leading edge below 2nm nodes. The company sustained a 95% specification win rate in next-generation tool architectures, securing long-term technological leadership over fragmented Tier 2 and 3 competitors. KITZ Corporation's ¥9.4 billion acquisition of V-TEX establishes a consolidated global numbertwo player with 9% market share , creating credible second-sourcing pressure on VAT's quasi-monopolistic margins. VAT's annual R&D expenditure exceeds the total sales of its closest competitor, sustaining formidable technical barriers to entry across mechatronic valve applications.

Financial Profile and Capital Efficiency

VAT delivered exceptional 35.2% ROIC in 2025 , sustaining returns far above the estimated 10% cost of capital and outperforming semiconductor capital equipment peers. Adjusted EBITDA reached CHF 321.6 million at a 30.0% margin , landing at the lower bound of the 30-37% target corridor due to unfavorable currency translations. Gross margin contracted to 63.5% in 2025 from 66.4% in 2024, compressed by inventory normalization and persistent Swiss franc appreciation against the US dollar. Free cash flow reached an all-time high of CHF

230.4 million , representing 71.6% EBITDA conversion and enabling a disciplined 93% payout ratio to shareholders. Over 60% of operating costs are denominated in Swiss francs, leaving EBITDA margins intensely vulnerable to persistent currency headwinds during industry downturns. 4

Customer Concentration and Value Chain Position

The top-3 customers represented 43% of 2025 revenue , with Applied Materials, Lam Research, and a third OEM contributing 16%, 14%, and 13% individually. Mechatronic specification wins lock VAT's customized valves into OEM tools, imposing skyrocketing requalification costs and creating formidable switching barriers lasting five to fifteen years. VAT secured a record 150 specification wins in 2025 , with approximately 50% concentrated in advanced leading-edge semiconductor applications targeting sub-2nm nodes. Critical vacuum subsystems maintain superior 63.5% gross margins and 30.0% EBITDA margins , structurally outperforming upstream substrate suppliers and midstream WFE assembly peers.

Industry Structure and Long-Term Growth Drivers

The core semiconductor vacuum valve market is projected to reach CHF 1.3-1.4 billion by 2029 , expanding at a 12% CAGR from 2024 through 2029. The Gate-All-Around (GAA) architecture transition drives a 25-30% increase in vacuum-intensive process steps , skewing capital expenditure toward ALD and etch applications. VAT successfully launched its first ALD gas inlet valve in 2024 , targeting adjacencies to reach 15% of group sales by 2027 and 20% by 2029 . Extreme geographic concentration exposes VAT, with Asia generating 72% of sales and direct China sales representing 32% , heightening vulnerability to US trade restrictions.

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution

CEO Urs Gantner brings 20 years of vacuum technology experience, targeting 2x WFE outgrowth over the 20252029 strategic roadmap through mechatronic systems expansion. The Board remains committed to returning up to 100% of free cash flow to shareholders, raising the FY25 dividend 12% to CHF 7.00 per share . Entrants face immense capital barriers to replicate VAT's precision manufacturing footprints and deep materials science expertise built over decades in particle-free valve engineering. Apparent mechatronic moats are latecycle rather than structural if major OEM customers dual-source from consolidated Tier 2 players like KITZ-VTEX to compress subsystem pricing .
