3. BODY SECTIONS

§1 — General (Description, Financials, Peer Comparison)

Net signal: Vimian is a Swedish-listed specialty animal health holding company with EUR ~320-340m revenue (FY25E), operating at modest EBIT margins (~12-15% adjusted) and elevated leverage (~4-5x ND/EBITDA) — financial profile is clearly below specialty pharma peers at this stage of development [#J]

Description: Vimian Group AB is a specialty animal health company built via acquisitions, focused on products for companion animals, equine, aquaculture, and exotic/zoo species [#I]
- Sub: Founded 2016 by EQT-backed management team; listed on Nasdaq First North (Stockholm) in 2021; describes itself as "a collection of entrepreneurial specialty animal health businesses"
- Sub: Four reportable segments: Companion Animal , Specialty Animal (SA — equine/exotic), Aquaculture, and Diagnostics — CA is the largest (~45-50% of revenue)

Strategic objective: Dual-engine growth model — organic revenue growth (target 10%+ medium-term) combined with bolt-on M&A in specialty animal health niches [#I]
- Sub: Capital allocation priority is M&A at 5-8x EBITDA in niche markets with limited generic competition; secondary priority is internal R&D for line extensions
- Sub: Medium-term financial targets (per 2023 CMD): >10% organic growth, >30% adjusted EBITDA margin, ND/EBITDA <3x — all three targets remain outstanding as of FY24

Organisation: Decentralised holding structure — each acquired business retains separate management team, brand, and commercial organisation; Vimian Group functions as capital allocator and strategic overseer [#A]
- Sub: This "federation of specialists" model echoes Lifco/Addtech (Swedish serial-acquirer archetype) but in animal health rather than industrial niches
- Sub: Risk: decentralisation limits cross-selling and operational leverage realisation — a structural constraint on margin expansion per [AS-Carnegie] 2024 initiation

Listing: Nasdaq First North Growth Market (not the main Nasdaq Stockholm exchange) — lower disclosure requirements, less liquidity, and limited institutional index eligibility vs main-market peers [#A]
- Sub: SEK share price ~13-16 (May 2026); market cap ~SEK 4.8-5.5bn (~EUR 430-500m); this is at the low end of Richard's preferred EUR 5-50bn range
- Sub: Average daily value traded ~SEK 3-5m/day — thin liquidity vs main-market mid-caps; meaningful position-building would require patience
