EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 14 findings

1.
Internal Change Signals: BASF has undergone aggressive leadership overhaul with sweeping generational transition and 15% executive reduction signaling credible shift from bureaucracy to high-accountability performance culture. Dr. Markus Kamieth assumed the CEO role in April 2024, replacing long-tenured Martin Brudermüller, bringing downstream leadership experience and a mandate for radical restructuring. Julia Raquet was appointed to lead the newly established Core Transformation Office targeting a 20% reduction in net cash fixed costs by 2029, while BASF has reduced its senior executive headcount by 11% to 15% since late 2023, eliminating the regional matrix axis and broadening spans of control
2.
Strategic Transformation: Management launched the "Winning Ways" strategy in September 2024 to pivot from volume-driven integration to ruthless value creation, fully abandoning the historic mindset of being a broadly diversified, fully integrated conglomerate in favor of specialization.
