B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What it does. BE Semiconductor Industries (BESI-NL) designs and manufactures semiconductor assembly equipment, with die-attach and hybrid-bonding tools at the centre of its advanced-packaging franchise
2.
Primary market. Advanced packaging represented 70% of total revenue in fiscal 2025, with sub-7-micron placement-accuracy applications driving 60% of overall sales, reflecting a decisive mix shift from legacy mainstream assembly
3.
Segment mix. Die-attach systems dominate at 80% of segment revenues; the mainstream business (smartphone, automotive, industrial back-end tools) remains in a 14-quarter cyclical trough only now showing recovery signs
4.
Geography and manufacturing footprint. Manufacturing sits mainly in Asia, with 66% of fixed staff based in Asia; a new Vietnam facility (online late 2026) will absorb mainstream assembly and free Malaysian cleanroom capacity for hybrid bonding
5.
Financial character. Q1 2026 revenue of EUR 184.9m (+28.3% year-on-year), gross margin 63.5%, EBIT margin 34.6%, EPS EUR 0.65; through-cycle gross margins have stayed above 60% despite a prolonged back-end downcycle
6.
Balance sheet. Net cash position of EUR 103.3m as of Q1 2026, cash and deposits of EUR 611.4m, minimal capital expenditure (EUR 1.08m in the quarter) reflecting an asset-light, flexible manufacturing model
7.
Capital allocation. A 95% dividend payout ratio plus an active EUR 60m buyback programme; EUR 254.8m distributed to shareholders in 2025, equal to 43% of annual revenue
8.
Customer base. Concentrated around leading-edge logic foundries; TSMC capacity expansions represent over half of hybrid-bonding tool shipments, a single-customer dependency risk flagged repeatedly in sell-side commentary
9.
Investment-style implication. BESI-NL patterns as a total-addressable-market expansion and market-share-gainer archetype: a structural growth compounder riding an equipment upcycle, not a value or turnaround case, with cyclicality still present in its legacy mainstream segment
10.
Ownership and takeover context. Applied Materials holds a 9% equity stake cementing a joint hybrid-bonding development partnership; Reuters has reported unconfirmed bid interest from front-end equipment peers, though no active activist is on the register
