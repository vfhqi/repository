§4 — Synthesis (~280 words | judgement section)

Most likely explanation — the Q1 earnings print undershot the market's stabilisation thesis. Telia has spent 2024-25 restructuring: exiting the Baltics (completed), resizing the cost base, and restoring dividend credibility. The buy-side consensus entering Q1 2026 appears to have priced in a sequential improvement in Swedish and Finnish EBITDA margins and at minimum a reaffirmation of full-year guidance. The print delivered service revenue growth that was either flat or marginally negative in Sweden on a reported basis, and management commentary on the full-year was notably hedged on the Swedish B2C mobile pricing environment. The gap between what the market had modelled (stabilisation, mild re-rating) and what was delivered (continued pressure, muted guidance) is the mechanism for the move, not the absolute numbers.

Counter-hypothesis. The strongest alternative is that the move is macro/rate-driven rather than earnings-specific: if Nordic long rates ticked higher in late April (as the ECB rate-cut path was repriced after firmer Eurozone inflation data), Telia's dividend-proxy status would have amplified any sector weakness. The counter-evidence is that the timing of the sharpest leg down aligns with the earnings date, not with a macro inflection point. If WebSearch confirms the ECB repricing happened earlier (March-April) and the rate move was already absorbed, the macro hypothesis weakens further.

What remains unexplained. A residual 1-2% of the move could reflect passive-flow or positioning unwind — Telia is a large-cap index constituent (OMX, STOXX Europe 600 Telecoms) and can attract forced selling on a weak earnings print as systematic funds de-risk. This could not be confirmed from desk research.

Decision prompt. Run Q5 (earnings delivery vs consensus) before any other work. The key check: Swedish mobile ARPU vs consensus and the guidance bridge for FY2026 EBITDA.
