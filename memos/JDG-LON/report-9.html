  • Dominant risk from L3Y data: Organic growth volatility is higher than the acquisition-smoothed headline suggests; FY2024's -2% to -4% organic trough confirms JDG is exposed to lumpy capital equipment demand cycles at universities and research institutions [#J]

  • Sub: Amortisation of acquired intangibles has grown from ~£4.5m pa (FY2022) to ~£7-8m pa (FY2025e) as acquisition pace continues — directionally expected, not a concern per se

  • Sub: The absolute scale of cumulative intangibles on the balance sheet (~£90-100m by FY2025e) means any goodwill impairment could create material reported-vs-adjusted EPS divergence

  • Scale driver: Limited at group level due to decentralised structure; individual subsidiaries benefit from scale within their niches but do not share procurement, manufacturing, or distribution [#J]

  • Sustainability: Structural drivers (mix, pricing) are more durable than cyclical drivers; margin sustainability is high in a stable volume environment; key vulnerability is organic volume trough (as seen FY2024) where semi-fixed costs bite [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — margin sustainability: The bearish case on margins is straightforward: if research funding (the key demand driver for JDG's instruments) faces sustained compression — from UK/EU government austerity or US grant reductions — organic volume could remain below FY2023 peak for 2-3 years; in that scenario, acquisitions at 6-8x EBIT entry multiples would be EPS-dilutive before synergies, and group margins would drift toward 22-23% [#J]
