§1 — Executive Summary

  • Net verdict: Multi-variable thesis with one fulcrum (MEO revenue ramp) and several necessary conditions (cost, leverage, GEO floor); not a high-conviction single-driver setup [#J]
  • Driver list: Six identified drivers stack-ranked by estimated 24M share price impact: (1) MEO O3b mPOWER revenue ramp, (2) GEO video revenue floor/stabilisation, (3) Capital structure / leverage normalisation, (4) Cost transformation and EBITDA margin recovery, (5) Government & defence vertical growth, (6) Intelsat integration (post-close, optionality) [#A]
  • Top driver: O3b mPOWER commercial contract wins and revenue ramp — estimated to account for ~60-70% of SES's revenue growth guidance through 2027; no other driver approaches this magnitude [#J]
  • Single fulcrum: MEO ramp is load-bearing; all other drivers are either enabling conditions or secondary contributors [#J]

§2 — Key Driver #1: MEO O3b mPOWER Revenue Ramp

  • Net verdict: Most important driver by far; technology risk now largely retired but commercial sales execution is slower than originally guided — medium plausibility on 24M timeline [#J]

  • Driver definition: SES's O3b mPOWER constellation (11 satellites launched by 2024) delivers high-throughput, low-latency connectivity primarily for enterprise, cruise/maritime, and government customers; revenue ramp as fleet achieves full utilisation is the growth engine [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: MEO revenue is high-margin incremental satellite capacity; each contract win drops through at ~60-65% EBITDA margin once constellation opex is covered; revenue ramp drives both top-line growth and margin expansion simultaneously [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Market re-rates SES from "declining GEO satellite" to "growth connectivity platform" once MEO revenue constitutes a meaningfully large share of total — estimated inflection around MEO >25-30% of total revenue [#J]

  • Company guide: Management guided for MEO revenue to reach ~€500-600m by 2027 (from ~€200-250m in 2024); implies ~25-30% CAGR on MEO segment [#A]

  • Guidance issued at 2022-era CMD implied 2025 MEO revenues around €350-400m — as of H1 2025 public disclosures, tracking below that level
  • Management has revised MEO ramp timeline twice since original 2022 guidance, citing longer customer procurement cycles
  • Management commentary: CEO Adel Al-Saleh has framed mPOWER as "commercially validated" post-2024 launches; key reference customers include SoftBank, Liberty Satellite, US Government [#A]
  • Track record: Mixed; SES has a history of over-optimistic constellation commercialisation timelines (original O3b took 2-3 years longer to ramp than 2013-era guidance) [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Current guidance reads optimistic given L12M execution; management tendency to guide ambitious and revise [#A]

  • SS consensus: Most covering analysts model MEO revenue CAGR of ~20-25% through 2027, below management's 25-30% guide; Berenberg and Deutsche Bank most constructive, JPMorgan most cautious on timing [#A]

  • Berenberg (public note, 2025): mPOWER "inflection approaching" with 2026 as the year of meaningful commercial acceleration
  • JPMorgan (public note, 2025): flagged "elongated sales cycles" and trimmed 2026E MEO revenue by ~€50m
  • Oddo BHF: broadly neutral, views MEO ramp as "priced in at current levels"
  • Consensus changes L12M: Estimates trimmed approximately 10-15% on 2026E MEO revenue over L12M; no broker has upgraded MEO revenue forecasts over this period [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Full delivery of MEO guidance (€500-600m by 2027) would add approximately €0.10-0.14 to EPS vs current consensus, implying ~20-30% EPS uplift from this driver alone [#A]

  • Recent delivery: H1 2025 MEO revenues approximately €210-230m (annualised ~€420-460m); on the lower end of management's trajectory but not catastrophically off [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No direct MEO peer available (no cohort data); Eutelsat's LEO (OneWeb) ramp provides loose analogy — OneWeb commercial ramp also slower than original guidance, suggesting sector-wide pattern of over-optimism on new constellation commercialisation [#A]

  • Trend: Sequentially improving quarter-on-quarter in 2025; cruise/maritime vertical winning contracts, but enterprise and government procurement slower [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Demand for low-latency satellite broadband is structural and accelerating; underserved connectivity markets (aviation, maritime, remote enterprise) remain large [#A]

  • Macro headwind: SpaceX Starlink and OneWeb competitive pressure intensifying; price per Mbps declining across LEO/MEO market; SES's MEO advantages (latency, power) are real but narrowing competitively [#A]
  • Competitive dynamics: Starlink Business is expanding aggressively into maritime and aviation verticals where SES's mPOWER targets; Amazon Kuiper entering from 2026; competitive intensity rising not falling [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium; ~30-40% of 2026E MEO revenue already contracted (management claim, unverified independently); remaining ~60% dependent on new contract wins [#A]

  • Plausibility assessment: Medium plausibility — constellation is deployed and technically validated; key risk is sales cycle length and Starlink competitive pressure compressing addressable price points [#J]

  • Key assumptions: (a) No major Starlink maritime/enterprise price war in 2025-26; (b) SES closes ≥3-4 large enterprise anchor contracts in H2 2025; (c) cruise/maritime contracts signed in 2024-25 ramp to full utilisation [#A]

  • Magnitude if delivered: ~20-30% EPS uplift + multiple re-rating from ~5-6× EV/EBITDA toward 7-8× [#A]

  • Bear case: Starlink Business achieves price leadership in maritime/aviation by 2026, forcing SES to compete on price and MEO revenue tops out at €350-400m rather than €500-600m; combined with GEO decline, total revenue flat to down [#J]

  • Miss scenario: MEO 2027 revenues land at €380m vs €550m guide; combined with GEO erosion, SES total revenue flat vs guided +8-10% CAGR; EBITDA misses by ~€80-100m; consensus downgrades cycle begins [#A]
  • Miss magnitude: ~25-35% share price downside in miss scenario; multiple compresses from re-rating hope to distress discount [#A]
  • Red flags: Two prior timeline revisions; elongated enterprise sales cycles noted in multiple 2025 broker notes; no material increase in contracted backlog disclosed in H1 2025 [#A]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — constellation deployed, but commercial ramp slower than guided twice already; Starlink competitive risk is real and rising

§3 — Key Driver #2: GEO Video Revenue Floor / Stabilisation

  • Net verdict: Structural decline is irreversible; the question is pace — medium plausibility on "orderly decline" scenario but acceleration risk is underappreciated by consensus [#J]

  • Driver definition: SES's legacy GEO (geostationary) business generates ~60% of current total revenue; video broadcasting (DTH, cable contribution) is the largest GEO segment and is structurally declining as cord-cutting accelerates globally [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Each €100m of GEO video revenue lost at ~55-60% EBITDA margin represents ~€55-60m EBITDA erosion; at ~5-6× EV/EBITDA, that is ~€275-360m of enterprise value destruction [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: If GEO decline accelerates beyond management's "low-single-digit % annual" guided erosion, total revenue misses consensus and MEO growth cannot compensate in the 24M window [#J]

  • Company guide: Management guides GEO video decline at "low-to-mid single digit %" annually; network services (GEO data) expected to be more resilient [#A]

  • 2024 annual report: GEO video revenues declined ~6-7% YoY — already at the upper end of guidance range
  • Management frames GEO as "cash flow engine funding MEO investment," not a growth asset
  • Management commentary: Consistent messaging that GEO is "managed decline"; no guidance for stabilisation — management explicitly accepts secular erosion [#A]
  • Track record: GEO video has declined every year since 2018; management has generally guided slightly optimistically (actual decline exceeds guided range 3 of last 5 years) [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Guidance consistently optimistic vs actuals on GEO decline rate [#A]

  • SS consensus: All covering brokers model GEO video declining 5-8% annually through 2027; debate is whether decline is 5% or 8%, not whether it declines [#A]

  • Morgan Stanley (public note, 2025): flagged risk of "non-linear" GEO decline acceleration if major European DTH platforms shift to OTT faster than expected
  • Deutsche Bank: models GEO video at ~€600m by 2027 vs ~€750m today — ~6.5% CAGR decline
  • Estimated revenue/EPS impact: 1% acceleration in annual GEO decline rate vs guidance = ~€15-20m additional annual revenue loss = ~€9-12m EBITDA = ~€0.01-0.02 EPS per year [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: GEO revenue estimates stable-to-slightly-worse over L12M; no material upgrades [#A]

  • Recent delivery: GEO video declined ~7% in 2024 vs ~5% guided; network services (GEO data) relatively stable [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No peer data available; Eutelsat's legacy GEO is also declining but company has pivoted more aggressively to LEO; SES's GEO base is larger and more video-concentrated [#A]

  • Trend: Decline rate modestly accelerating; 2022: ~5%, 2023: ~6%, 2024: ~7% — suggests upper-bound of guidance is becoming the baseline [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: None structural; short-term support from major sporting events (Olympics 2024) temporarily boosted transponder demand [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Cord-cutting accelerating in Europe and US; streaming migration pulling major content off satellite distribution; 5G terrestrial fill-in further reducing satellite's addressable video distribution market [#A]
  • Competitive dynamics: OTT platforms (Netflix, Disney+, local streaming) reducing DTH broadcasters' satellite transponder demand; linear TV advertising declining 5-8% annually in key European markets [#A]

  • Visibility: Relatively high for next 12M (contracted transponder leases provide visibility); beyond 12M, renewal risk increases as long-term contracts expire [#A]

  • Plausibility — orderly decline scenario: Medium plausibility — short-term contracts provide some visibility, but L3Y trend shows decline rate at upper end or exceeding guidance [#J]

  • Key assumptions: No major DTH platform (Sky UK, Canal+, Astra-based broadcasters) terminates transponder contracts early; decline stays at 6-7% not 10%+ [#A]
  • Bear case: One or two major DTH customers accelerate OTT migration in 2025-26, triggering non-linear GEO revenue step-down; revenue declines 12-15% in a single year, invalidating SES's "bridge" funding model [#J]
  • Miss magnitude: A 10% GEO decline year vs 6% guided = ~€40-50m incremental revenue miss; multiplied by 5-6× EV/EBITDA = ~€150-200m EV destruction [#A]
  • Red flags: Actual 2024 decline at upper end of guidance range; no major multi-year GEO video contract renewals disclosed in L6M [#A]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — contracted visibility for next 12M is real, but trend acceleration means guided decline range is increasingly optimistic

§4 — Key Driver #3: Capital Structure / Leverage Normalisation

  • Net verdict: Leverage at ~4.5× EBITDA creates genuine refinancing and equity-dilution risk; this driver is a necessary condition for re-rating, not an upside catalyst [#J]

  • Driver definition: SES carried net debt of approximately €3.5-4.0bn as of 2025, with EBITDA of ~€850-900m, implying net leverage ~4.2-4.5×; key maturities fall in 2026-2028 [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: High leverage constrains free cash flow available to equity (interest costs ~€150-170m/year); deleveraging via FCF conversion or asset monetisation reduces interest burden and improves EPS quality [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: De-levering toward <3.5× would remove a structural discount from the multiple; current 5-6× EV/EBITDA trades at a discount to unlevered peers partly on leverage concern [#J]

  • Company guide: Management targets net leverage of ~3.0-3.5× by 2027; implies ~€400-600m net debt reduction over 2025-2027 [#A]

  • FCF generation guided at ~€250-350m/year for 2025-2027 after capex
  • Management has indicated no equity raise is planned; relies on organic FCF + possible asset disposals
  • Management commentary: CFO has reiterated "comfortable with leverage trajectory" in 2025 results calls; no specific asset disposal programme announced [#A]
  • Track record: SES has historically maintained leverage above its own target range; previous deleveraging cycles were slower than guided [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Guidance optimistic; FCF generation in 2024 came in below guidance due to working capital and capex overruns [#A]

  • SS consensus: Most brokers note leverage as a constraint but not an immediate crisis; Morgan Stanley flagged 2026-2027 refinancing as the key watch item [#A]

  • JPMorgan (2025): "balance sheet management is a watch item; equity story cannot decouple from leverage resolution"
  • Berenberg: more constructive, argues MEO FCF inflection provides deleveraging path by 2027
  • Estimated EPS impact: Successful deleveraging to 3.0× by 2027 = ~€60-80m annual interest saving = ~€0.08-0.10 EPS uplift; plus multiple re-rating removing leverage discount [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: No material change in leverage outlook consensus; FCF estimates trimmed slightly [#A]

  • Recent delivery: 2024 net debt slightly above 2023 level (flat to modestly up); deleveraging path not yet demonstrating progress [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: European satellite operators Eutelsat and Intelsat (pre-restructuring) both carried elevated leverage; sector norm is ~3.0-4.0× for mature operators; SES at ~4.5× is above peer median (estimated) [#A]

  • Trend: No improvement in L2Y; leverage trajectory flat to slightly deteriorating while MEO capex cycle peaks [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Declining rate environment (ECB cuts in 2024-2025) reduces refinancing risk somewhat; credit markets remain open for investment-grade-adjacent issuers [#A]

  • Macro headwind: If MEO ramp disappoints, EBITDA grows less than modelled, leverage stays elevated or deteriorates; credit markets could tighten for high-yield satellite operators in a risk-off environment [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium; 2025-2026 maturities manageable; 2027+ maturities require MEO EBITDA delivery to refinance without equity dilution [#A]

  • Plausibility — deleveraging to target: Medium plausibility — dependent on MEO EBITDA ramp AND FCF discipline; either variable alone is uncertain [#J]

  • Key assumptions: (a) No equity raise required; (b) MEO EBITDA reaches €350-400m by 2027 to fund debt service; (c) No major GEO revenue shock requiring incremental capex [#A]

  • Bear case: MEO ramp disappointment + GEO acceleration = EBITDA below €800m by 2026; leverage drifts above 5×; credit markets demand equity injection or forced asset sale [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Equity raise at depressed share price (dilutive); or asset disposal at below-market value; both scenarios destroy equity value [#A]
  • Miss magnitude: Forced equity raise at 20-30% discount = 20-30% direct dilution; plus re-rating compression from loss of management credibility [#A]
  • Red flags: 2024 FCF came in below guidance; no asset disposal pipeline announced; no specific refinancing milestones disclosed [#A]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — deleveraging path exists but is dependent on both MEO delivery AND FCF discipline that has not yet been demonstrated

  • Driver definition: SES announced a significant cost restructuring in 2023-2024, targeting ~€100-150m in annualised cost savings by 2026; combination of headcount reduction (~15-20% of workforce), network consolidation, and procurement savings [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Direct EBITDA improvement; €100m cost saving at current tax/interest structure = ~€0.08-0.12 EPS uplift; partially offsets GEO revenue erosion [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Margin recovery narrative supports multiple maintenance; without it, declining revenue + flat margin = earnings deterioration and multiple compression [#J]

  • Company guide: Management targets EBITDA margin recovery toward 50%+ by 2026-27 from ~44-46% range in 2023-2024 [#A]

  • H1 2025 EBITDA margin: ~47% — trajectory in the right direction
  • Headcount reduction broadly on schedule per management commentary
  • Management commentary: CEO has cited cost programme as "on track" in every quarterly update in 2025; specific savings milestones met per management claims [#A]
  • Track record: Positive — SES has historically executed well on cost reduction programmes; 2019-2021 restructuring delivered broadly as guided [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Guidance here is more credible than on revenue; cost is more within management's control [#A]

  • SS consensus: Broadly aligned; most brokers model EBITDA margin recovery to 48-51% by 2027 [#A]

  • Deutsche Bank: "cost programme the most visible earnings driver in 2025-26; gives earnings floor even if MEO disappoints"
  • Berenberg: models €120m in cost savings fully delivered by H1 2026
  • Estimated EPS impact: Full €100-150m cost saving = ~€0.08-0.14 EPS uplift (~15-25% EPS increase from this driver alone) [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No peer data available; European industrial/satellite sector restructurings typically deliver 70-80% of targeted savings within original timeline [#A]

  • Trend: Positive and improving; consistent execution across L4 quarters [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Satellite operator cost base has some fixed infrastructure element that limits ultimate margin expansion; €150m is probably close to the ceiling [#A]

  • Visibility: High for 2025-2026; restructuring charges are one-time, savings are recurring [#A]

  • Plausibility — cost delivery: Medium-high plausibility — cost is within management control, track record positive, H1 2025 on-plan [#J]

  • Magnitude if delivered: ~€0.08-0.14 EPS uplift; EBITDA margin at 50%+ removes a key bear talking point [#A]

  • Miss scenario: MEO operational costs exceed plan (ground infrastructure, customer support at scale); restructuring charges exceed one-time estimates; net savings €60-70m vs €120-150m guided [#A]
  • Miss magnitude: ~€50m EBITDA shortfall vs consensus; modest share price impact (~5-10%) unless combined with revenue miss [#A]
  • Red flags: None visible at present; execution appears credible [#A]

Plausibility verdict: medium-high plausibility — most controllable driver in the set; track record positive; H1 2025 execution on plan

  • Management commentary: CEO has emphasised "complementary orbit positions" and government customer overlap as key strategic rationale [#A]
