Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Röko's business model relies on a highly profitable, decentralised collection of 33 niche operating units, though its high exposure to consumer discretionary sectors differentiates it from peer compounders.

Segment and Revenue Breakdown

1.
Robust segment diversification: The business operates through 33 business units divided into two primary reporting segments, B2B and B2C, ensuring that no single portfolio company represents more than 9% of total group sales
2.
Highly profitable B2B: The B2B segment is the largest driver, accounting for MSEK 4,237 of net sales in FY 2025 and generating an adjusted EBITA of MSEK 920, representing a 21.71% segment margin
3.
High-margin consumer B2C: The B2C segment contributed MSEK 2,214 of net sales in FY 2025, yielding an adjusted EBITA of MSEK 474 and a highly profitable EBITA margin of 21.41%
4.
Highly capital-efficient mix: Revenues are primarily product-focused, with products accounting for MSEK 5,871 of total FY 2025 sales compared to only MSEK 581 for services
5.
Vast international footprint: The group has a strong international profile with 67% of revenues generated outside the Nordics, mainly led by the United Kingdom at 32% of sales, making it more global than the peer median of 41%

Competitive Positioning and Industry Dynamics

6.
Excellent capital efficiency: Röko achieves durable competitive advantage by acquiring asset-light niche market leaders with EBITA margins over 15% and capital expenditure to sales ratios below 3%
7.
Empowered local management: The company avoids forcing subsidiaries into a "large-company mold", leaving operational and strategic decision-making to local management teams who often retain minority equity stakes
8.
Immense target runway: The European SME market offers a massive runway of approximately 25,000 high-quality target companies in Italy, Germany, and the UK that meet Röko's strict financial criteria
9.
Disciplined entry multiples: Röko has successfully maintained its entry multiples, paying an average of 7x to 8x EBITA for its 33 acquisitions, which is lower than valuation multiples paid by larger, more mature peers
10.
Heavy cyclical exposure: Unlike peers like Addtech or Lifco that avoid consumer cyclicality, Röko has a hefty 33% exposure to consumer discretionary niches like ski instructor training and graduation products
11.
Other side of the story: Decentralized governance risks cultural misalignment or insufficient operational oversight across 33 distinct subsidiaries, which could lead to reporting errors or reputational damage

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens based on M&A expansion and a large European target runway, though success requires a recovery in highly cyclical consumer discretionary segments.

Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
Highly plausible M&A: The primary earnings driver is accelerated M&A deployment, touching both net sales and EBITA lines, which is highly plausible given a deep target pipeline of 25,000 high-quality SMEs across Europe
2.
Challenged M&A execution: This key driver could fail if intensified competition for targets inflates acquisition multiples, making it difficult for Röko to grow earnings without sacrificing strict return thresholds
3.
Highly reliable pricing: A second major driver is strategic pricing, which directly supports the operating margin line through targeted increases and prioritizing high-margin product categories
4.
High playbook plausibility: The plausibility of strategic pricing is high, as this was the core operational playbook successfully deployed by co-founder Fredrik Karlsson during his tenure at premium peer Lifco
5.
Moderately plausible recovery: A third driver is the recovery of cyclical consumer discretionary segments, touching roughly 33.00% of group revenues, which is moderately plausible as interest rates and inflation stabilize

Structuring the Compounding Algorithm

6.
Challenged cyclical demand: The consumer discretionary recovery driver would fail if sustained macroeconomic weakness or high tariffs prolong the downturn in retail and consumer confidence, dragging down B2C margins
7.
Highly resilient independence: The forward earnings drivers are largely independent – the strategic pricing and European B2B M&A engines can continue to deliver mid-teens earnings compounding even if consumer discretionary demand remains weak
8.
Demanding bull hurdles: For the full bull case to succeed, Röko must simultaneously re-accelerate its M&A deal velocity from its historical average of 5 to 6 acquisitions per year toward the proven compounder average of 12 deals
9.
Other side of the story: Valuation compression will bite if the company cannot maintain its pricing power or scale M&A, forcing the high starting multiple of 20x EV/EBITA to undergo a painful de-rating toward small-cap industrial averages

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderately high due to cyclical end-market exposures and integration complexities, though diversified business units provide some structural resilience.

Portfolio and Execution Vulnerabilities

1.
Severe cyclical vulnerabilities: A major risk stems from portfolio exposure to cyclical sectors like automotive, construction, and consumer discretionary, where a macroeconomic slowdown would directly depress subsidiary revenues and compress operating margins
2.
Challenging operational integration: Poor due diligence or bad alignment during integration could result in overpaying for assets or acquiring hidden liabilities, leading to costly restructurings that drag down group EPS
3.
Challenged scaling capacity: As the group grows, it must execute more transactions to maintain growth; failing to scale M&A resources would slow down deal flow and de-rate the multiple

Macroeconomic and Governance Risks

4.
High key-person dependency: The investment case is highly dependent on key individuals, and the departure of CEO Johan Bladh or founder Fredrik Karlsson would disrupt deal flow and damage investor confidence
5.
Severe translation headwinds: Röko's international sales in GBP, USD, EUR, NOK, and DKK expose it to severe currency fluctuations, which can negatively impact translated SEK revenues and profitability
6.
Misleading volume signals: A sudden rise in reported net sales from newly consolidated acquisitions can mask underlying organic volume declines in the existing portfolio, hiding structural deterioration
7.
Critical operational monitor: The single most critical metric to monitor is the quarterly organic sales growth rate in local currency, which provides immediate warning of portfolio deterioration before it is obscured by M&A
8.
Other side of the story: Extreme diversification across 33 independent business units ensures that no single company represents more than 9% of group sales, insulating the overall group from localized shocks
