§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Reverse-engineered bear: SOBI's bull case requires Alhemo to sustain ~40%+ volume growth AND Doptelet to continue geographic expansion AND no major pipeline failure — all three simultaneously [#J]

  • Any one failure creates a meaningful miss vs consensus; two failures simultaneously could compound disproportionately given operating leverage running in reverse

  • Single largest risk: Alhemo penetration plateauing at ~4,000-5,000 global patients (Hemlibra's non-inhibitor market growth analogy plateaued at ~24-30 months post-launch) would cause FY25-26 consensus to be 10-15% too high [#J]

  • This risk is company-specific and not sector-wide — Roche's Hemlibra (the dominant competitor) would not be similarly affected

  • Early warning — Alhemo: Quarterly patient add data in earnings calls — if new patient starts decelerate from ~400-500/quarter to <200/quarter, this is the most important early warning signal for the thesis [#A]

  • Alhemo is currently ~24 months into commercial launch, historically the plateau-risk window for first-in-class haemophilia agents

  • Early warning — Kineret: Any acceleration in biosimilar Kineret filing activity (US FDA ANDA or EMA pathway) should be tracked; Kineret is an older product with limited IP protection and represents ~SEK 1.5-2.0bn of annual revenues [#A]

  • Early warning — margins: If SOBI's H1 2025 gross margin declines >100bp vs H1 2024 without an explicit explanation, this would suggest either pricing pressure or mix deterioration masking the headline growth rate [#A]

  • Breaking points: Consensus EPS for FY26 implies ~SEK 18-20/share (adjusted); if Alhemo plateaus and Kineret erodes, realistic FY26 EPS could be ~SEK 13-15/share — a ~25-30% miss vs consensus [#J]

  • Peer-relative downside: SOBI's premium relative valuation vs peers (estimated 18-22x adjusted P/E vs peer median ~15-18x) means any fundamental miss would compress multiples AND earnings simultaneously — double downside vs a lower-rated peer experiencing the same headwind [#J]

  • Structural bear: SOBI's business model of licensed/partnered products means the company does not own the manufacturing IP for its largest assets; if Novo Nordisk or another partner renegotiates haemophilia terms, margin and revenue risk is disproportionately front-loaded onto SOBI [#A]

  • Consensus complacency: Sell-side appears to be extrapolating the FY22-24 beat-and-raise pattern into FY25-26 without adequately stress-testing the Alhemo plateau risk; this creates asymmetric downside if the pattern breaks [#J]
