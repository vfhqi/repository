Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Rightmove operates a virtuous double-sided network effect that makes its platform indispensable for UK property professionals despite low customer satisfaction

Core Portal Segments and Monetisation Mechanics

1.
Dominant core portal: Operates the primary UK portal for residential resale, rentals, and commercial property listings in the UK
2.
Diverse partner base: Sells advertising subscriptions to residential estate agents, lettings agents, commercial brokers, and new homes developers
3.
High subscription mix: Earns the vast majority of its revenue from recurring monthly subscription fees paid by property professionals
4.
Premium packages growth: Promotes advanced subscription tiers like Optimiser Edge to help agents win local seller mandates
5.
Auxiliary digital services: Monetises consumer traffic through tenant referencing fees, commercial property listings, and digital mortgage integrations

Competitive Moat and Value Chain Position

6.
Indispensable marketing channel: Holds a profit-favoured position in the value chain, capturing 50% of estate agency marketing budgets on average
7.
Low partner concentration: Retains over 19,000 core members, ensuring that its largest single partner represents less than 3% of revenues
8.
Massive consumer share: Controls over 80% of the total share of consumer time spent on UK property platforms
9.
Strong brand equity: Benefits from 96% home-mover brand awareness, driving over 85% of traffic direct and organic
10.
Defensive database moat: Curates a highly defensible dataset of 28m property addresses that general AI search models cannot scrape
11.
Other side of the story: Persistent annual pricing hikes have created severe customer resentment, leaving agents eager to adopt lower-cost alternatives if competitors build consumer traffic

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case rests on core pricing resilience and the expansion of high-growth digital adjacencies

Core Portal Pricing and Product Upgrades

1.
Consistent ARPA expansion: Drives the core residential revenue line through consistent ARPA expansion and local branding product additions
2.
High pricing plausibility: Plausibility is high as 2025 total ARPA grew 6% to £1,621, with 50% of agents buying discretionary products
3.
Core pricing failure: This driver would fail if competitive platforms build enough consumer traffic to force a pricing freeze
4.
New homes premiumisation: Accelerates developer ARPA through adoption of the new top-tier Ascend package

New Business Scaling and Capital Efficiency

5.
Strategic adjacencies scaling: Touches other revenue lines by expanding into commercial search, digital rentals, and mortgage origination
6.
Mixed SGA momentum: Plausibility is medium as SGA revenue grew 25% in 2025, but mortgage monetization remains early-stage
7.
Independent driver structure: The investment case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously; core ARPA alone can sustain double-digit EPS growth
8.
Other side of the story: Strategic growth initiatives in mortgages could face prolonged monetization delays as the NatWest transition disrupts broker integration

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by litigation and competitive spend, although the core consumer franchise remains highly defensive

Litigation and Competitor Spend Pressures

1.
Massive litigation overhang: Faces a £1bn collective class-action lawsuit alleging anti-competitive pricing practices and excessive agent fees
2.
Low branch support: Lawsuit participation is limited to 250 estate agencies, representing only 1.5% of Rightmove's branch base
3.
Financial damage mechanism: An adverse class-action ruling represents the single worst failure mode, potentially forcing massive cash payouts
4.
Intense competitor undercutting: CoStar-backed OnTheMarket is using deep pockets to aggressively undercut fees and offer agents introductory discounts
5.
Escalating marketing spend: Rightmove could face margin compression if it must increase marketing budgets to defend its traffic leadership

Economic Cycles and Technology Risks

6.
Cyclical transaction exposure: Slow UK property transactions could trigger independent estate agent branch closures, leading to membership churn
7.
False friend metric: Net membership stability can mask underlying branch downgrades to cheaper packages, impacting average revenue per advertiser
8.
Crucial warning signal: Investors must monitored average revenue per advertiser growth deceleration as the primary sign of pricing fatigue
9.
Other side of the story: Pricing power remains highly secure because UK estate agents win nine times more vendor mandates on Rightmove than on competitor sites
