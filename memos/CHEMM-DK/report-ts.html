Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. ChemoMetec dominates the cell therapy counting niche via a patented closed-system cassette design that generates highly predictable recurring revenue.

Business Segments and Product Portfolio

1.
Specialized automated cell counting: ChemoMetec develops, manufactures, and markets automated cell counters and associated consumables, primarily targeting the high-growth Life science research, Cell and gene therapy, and Bioprocessing market
2.
Dominant product lines: The core offering consists of the legacy NucleoCounter instruments (NC-200, NC-202, NC-203) and the new automated XcytoMatic platform (XM30, XM40, XM50)
3.
Innovative consumables model: The business operates a highly lucrative "razor and blade" model where instrument placements drive recurring sales of patented, preloaded disposable cassettes
4.
Steady after-market revenue: Consumables and services accounted for 45.00% and 23.00% of total revenue respectively in the first half of FY 2025/26, providing highly predictable, recurring cash flows
5.
Historical client concentration: Historically, early growth was heavily driven by exclusive supply agreements in cell-based immunotherapy, where Tier 1 clients accounted for 70.00% of the core segment's revenue

Competitive Moat and Market Structure

6.
Exceptional regulatory moat: ChemoMetec's competitive advantage is secured by extremely high customer switching costs, as any change to an approved drug's manufacturing protocol requires running multi-year parallel studies
7.
Premium pricing power: Private biopharma clients prioritize measurement accuracy and 21 CFR Part 11 compliance over price, enabling ChemoMetec to charge premium prices and earn 95.00% gross margins
8.
Robust patent protection: The proprietary closed-system cassette design is protected by patents, preventing competitors from replicating the ease-of-use and calibration advantages
9.
Deeply entrenched position: The company's instruments are integrated into 90.00% of all approved CAR-T therapies, creating a highly stable recurring consumables stream
10.
Fragmented geographical scale: Unlike global life sciences tools giants such as Thermo Fisher and Danaher, ChemoMetec is a niche player with a relatively small headcount of 172 employees, limiting its distribution scale
11.
Other side of the story: If the cell therapy market shifts from autologous to allogeneic manufacturing, instrument demand could collapse because a single machine can process therapies for hundreds of patients

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case is strengthened by structural product upgrades and bioprocessing partnerships, although execution depends on customer validation timelines.

Core Investment Catalysts

1.
Discontinuation upgrade driver: Near-term growth is supported by the planned NC-200 discontinuation of up to 5,000 legacy units, which is highly likely to trigger a highly lucrative instrument replacement cycle
2.
Automation tailwinds: Long-term earnings growth is highly plausible as CGT manufacturers automate workflows to reduce labor costs, driving the adoption of automated XcytoMatic instruments
3.
Strategic bioprocessing expansion: The agreement with Roche is highly plausible and provides a direct growth path, allowing ChemoMetec to penetrate Roche's 2,000 to 3,000 installed base
4.
Imposing margin leverage: Selling XcytoMatic instruments at an estimated 20 times manufacturing cost compared to 10 times for legacy counters provides massive incremental operating leverage

Execution Risks and Milestones

5.
High multi-product hurdles: The bull case requires the successful roll-out of the XM50 microplate instrument and the XM Octopus software platform, adding substantial product execution complexity
6.
Slower software monetization: Delays in customer validation and software adoption represent the primary threat to margin expansion, as qualification cycles typically take 6 to 18 months
7.
Interdependent clinical growth: Any clinical setbacks or reimbursement constraints in the CAR-T space will cap customer therapy volumes and consumables demand
8.
Other side of the story: If next-generation cell manufacturing systems integrate proprietary automated cell counters, they will bypass stand-alone equipment entirely, breaking ChemoMetec's business model
