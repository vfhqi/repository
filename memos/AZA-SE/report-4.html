[W] Avanza Bank (AZA-SE) — Earnings & Fundamental Trends [C]

Query #4-C | Triaging Stage | 19-Apr-26

Key Findings

  • Avanza delivered record FY2025 results: Revenue SEK 4,495m (+15% YoY), operating profit SEK 3,078m, net profit SEK 2,631m (+17% YoY), EPS SEK 16.57 (+16%). Return on equity hit 40%, exceeding the 35% target.
  • Revenue diversification is a strength: Brokerage, NII, fund commissions, and FX income all contributed positively in FY2025. NII held broadly stable despite 100bps of Riksbank rate cuts, offset by volume growth in deposits and lending.
  • Customer momentum remains solid but decelerating: Net additions of 171k in FY2025 (vs ~125k in 2023, ~97k in 2024 annualised run-rate through mid-year). Total customers reached 2.24m. Savings capital +13% to SEK 1,079bn. Net inflow SEK 54bn.
  • Nordnet is the closest peer and slightly larger: 2.35m customers, SEK 1,183bn savings capital. Nordnet's FY2025 adjusted operating income SEK 5,389m (+6%), adjusted operating profit SEK 3,748m (+5%), EPS SEK 11.83 (+9%). Nordnet's NII under more pressure (guided ~SEK 2.3bn, declining trajectory from rate cuts).
  • flatexDEGIRO is the growth outlier: Revenue EUR 560m (+17%), net income EUR 160m (+44%), customer base 3.5m (+13%), assets under custody EUR 95.5bn (+34%). Cost/income ratio improved to ~45%. Transaction-driven model with minimal NII exposure.
  • Hargreaves Lansdown delisted Mar-2025 (CVC/Nordic Capital takeover). Last public FY2024 data: revenue GBP 765m (+4%), AUA GBP 155bn (+16%), ~1.88m clients. Operating margin ~48%. FY2025 partial data: AUA rose to GBP 173bn, NNB +43% to GBP 6bn. Now private — limited comparability going forward.
  • Avanza has best-in-class operating margins: Operating margin ~68% in FY2025 (vs Nordnet ~70% Q4 but declining, flatexDEGIRO ~29% net margin, HL ~48%). Cost/savings capital ratio improved to 14.1bps from 14.5bps.
  • EPS momentum is strongest at flatexDEGIRO (+44%) and Avanza (+16%). Nordnet's EPS growth of +9% lagged due to NII headwind. Consensus expects Avanza EPS ~SEK 17.58 for FY2026, implying +6% growth — deceleration from FY2025.
  • Key risk for Avanza: NII sensitivity. NII represented ~41% of FY2024 income. Rate cuts have eroded this, though volume growth has partially offset. Further Riksbank cuts would compress NII further. This is a shared risk with Nordnet.
  • Avanza's cost trajectory is the main watch item. FY2025 opex +10.4% (guided 11%). Management targets sub-8% annual cost growth through 2030 (with 2024 base), trending to 5% by end of period. Cloud migration and Private Banking investment driving near-term cost inflation.

- Q4 2025 operating income: SEK 1,139m (+7% YoY). This was the weakest quarterly growth rate in FY2025, reflecting tougher comps and lower "other income."
- Revenue growth driven by: higher net brokerage income, net currency-related income, fund commissions, and stable NII despite rate cuts. All-time high annual revenues.

- Growth lagged Avanza in FY2025 due to greater NII headwind. Commission income growth (~SEK 3.1bn record) partially offset NII decline.

Verdict: flatexDEGIRO leads on revenue growth rate. Avanza is a clear second with +15%. Nordnet and HL lag at +6% and +4% respectively. Avanza's growth is more balanced across revenue lines; flatexDEGIRO's is heavily transaction-driven.

2. Net Interest Income Trends

Avanza
- NII represented ~41% of total income in FY2024 — the single largest revenue line.
- FY2025: NII increased modestly despite 100bps of Riksbank policy rate cuts during 2025. Volume growth in both deposits and lending offset the rate headwind.
- NII trajectory: peaked in FY2023/early FY2024 when Swedish rates were at their highest. Now in gradual decline on a per-unit basis, but absolute levels held by volume growth.
- Mortgage book is directly linked to the Riksbank policy rate. Deposits with the Riksbank likewise tied to the policy rate.
- Sensitivity: further Riksbank cuts would compress NII. Management has guided for NII to remain a headwind in 2026 if rates decline further.

Nordnet
- NII is a similarly large component (~43% of FY2024 income, estimated).
- FY2025 NII guided at ~SEK 2.3bn (JPMorgan noted this was ~2% above consensus).
- Q4 2025 NII: SEK 561m, down 12% YoY — the sharpest quarterly decline, reflecting the full impact of lower Nordic policy rates.
- NII headwind is greater for Nordnet than Avanza in absolute terms, as Nordnet has more rate-sensitive deposits and less offsetting volume growth.
- Multi-country exposure (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland) means NII sensitivity is blended across four central bank rate paths.

Hargreaves Lansdown
- NII/"revenue on cash" was a major tailwind in FY2023-FY2024 as BoE rates peaked at 5.25%.
- H1 FY2024 cash revenue: GBP 132.8m (annualised ~GBP 266m), representing ~35% of revenue.
- Average client cash balances >GBP 15bn provide a large pool for NII generation.
- BoE rate cuts (began Aug-2024) now creating NII headwind. HL historically passed less interest through to clients than Nordic peers, creating a wider NII margin but also reputational/competitive risk.

flatexDEGIRO
- NII is a minor revenue component. FY2025 interest income ~EUR 42m/quarter (Q3), largely stable YoY as higher deposits and margin loans offset lower rates.
- Minimal rate sensitivity relative to peers. The business model is overwhelmingly transaction-driven.
- This is a structural advantage in a falling rate environment.

Verdict: NII is the key vulnerability for both Avanza and Nordnet. Avanza has managed the transition better (volume growth offsetting rate cuts). flatexDEGIRO has a structural advantage with minimal NII dependence. HL's NII margin is wide but declining.

3. Fee & Commission Income

Avanza
- Net brokerage income: increased significantly in FY2025, the primary driver of the +7% Q4 operating income growth. Higher trading activity and market volatility correlated with customer activity.
- Net fund commissions: all-time high for both a single quarter and the full year in FY2025. Fund margin decreased to 24.4bps average (24.0bps at quarter-end), but volume growth more than offset margin compression.
- Net currency-related income: significant contributor to income growth in both FY2024 and FY2025. Driven by customer trading in non-SEK securities.
- Other income: declined in Q4 2025, partly due to wind-down of external savings account distribution income.
- Revenue mix is well-diversified: brokerage, NII, fund commissions, and FX income all material contributors.

Nordnet
- Net commission income: SEK 3.1bn in FY2025 — record level. Driven by increased share trading and growth in fund capital.
- Commission income growth was the primary offset to NII decline. Share trading activity picked up in H2 2025 as market volatility increased.
- Fund AUM growth supports recurring fund fee income.

Hargreaves Lansdown
- Platform fees (0.35% on first GBP 250k, scaled down thereafter): primary revenue stream. Ongoing/recurring revenue = 81% of total.
- Share dealing fees: GBP 11.95 per online trade. Material contribution from active client base.
- Fund revenue: GBP 128.9m in FY2024. Cash revenue: GBP 127.9m.
- Revenue model is more AUA-linked and less transaction-dependent than Nordic peers.

flatexDEGIRO
- Commission income: EUR 369m in FY2025 (+31% YoY) — the dominant revenue line.
- Average commission per transaction: EUR 4.90 (rising, contrary to industry trend of commission compression).
- Revenue model is heavily transaction-dependent. 75m settled transactions in FY2025 (+19%).
- Payment for order flow historically significant but being phased out under EU regulation. flatexDEGIRO has been transitioning to explicit commission model.

Verdict: Avanza has the most balanced fee/commission mix. flatexDEGIRO is most transaction-dependent. HL is most AUA-dependent. Nordnet sits between Avanza and flatexDEGIRO.

5. Margin & Profitability Trends

Avanza
- FY2025 operating margin: ~68% (operating profit SEK 3,078m / revenue SEK 4,495m)
- FY2024 operating margin: ~67% (operating profit SEK 2,621m / revenue ~SEK 3,905m)
- Operating margin has been stable-to-improving. The cost/savings capital ratio — Avanza's preferred efficiency metric — declined from 14.5bps to 14.1bps in FY2025.
- Return on equity: 40% in FY2025, exceeding the 35% target. This is an exceptional level for a platform business.
- Net profit margin: ~53% (based on gross margin 91%, operating margin ~62% per stockanalysis.com data, which may use different revenue definitions).
- Q4 2025 operating margin compressed slightly due to +20% cost growth in the quarter (cloud migration, Private Banking investment, marketing).

Nordnet
- FY2025 adjusted operating margin: ~70% (SEK 3,748m / SEK 5,389m)
- Q4 2025 operating margin: 69% (vs 70% prior year) — slight decline.
- Nordnet's margins are marginally higher than Avanza's, reflecting its larger scale and more mature cost base.
- Adjusted operating expenses (ex-Germany): SEK 1,573m (+8.1% YoY). Including Germany investment, cost growth was higher.
- Germany launch represents an investment phase that will temporarily depress margins.

Hargreaves Lansdown
- FY2024 operating margin: ~48% (underlying PBT GBP 456m / revenue GBP 765m = 60% pre-tax margin; reported operating margin ~48% including all costs).
- Margins lower than Nordic peers due to: higher regulatory costs , more labour-intensive service model (phone-based advice), lower platform fee rates.
- FY2025 (post-acquisition): underlying PBT GBP 544m (+19%). Margin expansion likely driven by cost discipline under new PE ownership.
- Long-term margin trajectory: declining from mid-50s% historically to high-40s% as platform fees were cut and investment in digital capabilities increased.

flatexDEGIRO
- FY2025 adjusted EBITDA margin: 47.8% (EUR 267.7m / EUR 560m)
- FY2025 net income margin: ~29% (EUR 160m / EUR 560m)
- H1 2025 cost/income ratio: 45% (vs 48% H1/2024 and 70% H1/2023) — dramatic improvement.
- Margins have been on a strong upward trajectory as the company emerges from a period of heavy investment, regulatory remediation, and integration.
- EBITDA margin expansion from ~38% in FY2023 to ~48% in FY2025 represents one of the best operating leverage stories in European fintech.

Verdict: Avanza and Nordnet are clear margin leaders (68-70% operating margins). This reflects the structural efficiency of Scandinavian digital-first platforms with high automation and low cost-to-serve. flatexDEGIRO's margin trajectory is the most impressive on a rate-of-change basis. HL lags structurally.

9. Key Differentiators & Relative Position

Where Avanza stands out:

  1. Revenue diversification. Four meaningful revenue streams (brokerage, NII, fund commissions, FX). This is more balanced than flatexDEGIRO (transaction-heavy) or HL (AUA-heavy). In FY2025, all four lines grew — a rare achievement given rate headwinds.

  2. Operating margin leadership. ~68% operating margin is best-in-class globally for investment platforms. Only Nordnet is comparable. This reflects high automation, low cost-to-serve, and a structurally lean cost base (Sweden market only).

  3. Market share momentum in Sweden. 7.9% stock of savings market, but ~22-33% of net inflow. This persistently elevated flow share suggests structural market share gains will continue for years. Avanza is the clear winner in Swedish retail savings.

  4. Customer satisfaction moat. 16 consecutive years as Sweden's most satisfied savers. NPS of 41. eNPS of 57 (employee satisfaction). This is a genuine competitive advantage that compounds over time.

  5. Return on equity (40%). Exceptional capital efficiency. Above the 35% target. This is a function of the asset-light platform model, high margins, and disciplined capital allocation.

Where Avanza is weaker:

  1. Single-market concentration. Sweden only. No multi-country diversification (unlike Nordnet's four markets or flatexDEGIRO's 16 countries). If Sweden's savings market slows, Avanza has no geographic hedge.

  2. NII sensitivity. ~41% of income from NII (FY2024). Riksbank rate cuts are a meaningful headwind. Volume growth has offset this so far but may not fully compensate if rates fall further.

  3. Cost trajectory. FY2025 opex +10.4%, with FY2026 guided at +9%. Revenue growth must stay above this to maintain operating leverage. Cloud migration, Private Banking, and occupational pensions are investment phases that will weigh on near-term margins.

  4. Smaller than Nordnet. On savings capital (SEK 1,079bn vs 1,183bn), customers (2.24m vs 2.35m), and total revenue (SEK 4,495m vs 5,389m). Nordnet's multi-market presence gives it a larger TAM.

  5. EPS deceleration expected. Consensus +6% for FY2026 vs +16% in FY2025. If this deceleration materialises, the valuation multiple could compress.

Relative positioning summary:
- Avanza is the highest-quality platform in the peer group: best margins, best customer satisfaction, strongest market share momentum in its home market.
- The main debate is whether the premium quality justifies a premium multiple given: (a) NII headwinds, (b) cost investment phase, (c) EPS deceleration, (d) single-market risk.
- flatexDEGIRO offers the best growth/value combination if you believe the operating leverage story continues. Nordnet is the "balanced" option with multi-market diversification. HL is a take-private situation with limited visibility.
