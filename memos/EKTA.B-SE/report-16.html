  • Prior role: CEO of 3Shape (Danish dental 3D scanning and CAD/CAM software), ~2020-2025.
  • Before that: senior executive at Falck A/S (emergency services) and A.P. Moller-Maersk Group (20+ years across multiple senior roles).
  • Education: not publicly disclosed in detail; described as having strong international management track record.

  • No prior oncology, radiation therapy, or medtech board/operating experience identified in any public source.

  • I Gustaf Salford (Nov-2020 to Mar-2025) was an internal promotion from CFO. He joined Elekta in 2009, served in multiple roles including CFO, then became Acting CEO in June 2020.

  • Prior career: management consultant at Boston Consulting Group and Booz Allen Hamilton before joining Elekta.
  • Education: MSc, Stockholm School of Economics.
  • 11 years at Elekta before CEO appointment — deep operational familiarity but no external CEO experience.
  • Post-Elekta: appointed CEO of Ratos AB (Swedish industrial holding company) Dec-2025 — market read is that he is a competent operator who was wrong-footed on capital allocation and US/China execution.

Track Record at Elekta (2020-2025)

  • I Revenue trajectory under Salford: broadly positive but decelerating. Group net sales grew from approximately SEK 14bn (FY2020/21) to SEK 18.1bn (FY2023/24) — a compound of ~6% — then declined 1% in FY2024/25 to SEK 18.0bn.

  • J Margin trajectory: structurally insufficient. EBIT targets were consistently missed in the final two fiscal years; FY2024/25 EBIT outcome of 11.6% fell below the STI threshold of 12.2%, and below the midterm target range. Progress was visible but the pace was too slow to satisfy the board.

  • I Cash flow also missed STI threshold in FY2024/25. Operating cash flow outcome of SEK 1,056m was inside the target range (threshold SEK 960m, cap SEK 1,800m) — partial credit but not a strong result.

  • J Capital allocation: mediocre. No transformative acquisitions under Salford. Unity MRI-linac programme was inherited. No significant portfolio reshaping or buyback programme. Cash was deployed into R&D (maintenance) and dividend stability rather than compounding.

  • I Just-Bomholt public messaging (since Sep-2025) has been more operationally direct. Immediate announcement of zero-based review and 450-person cut within two months of appointment signals a more decisive and delivery-oriented posture.

§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

Voting Rights and Control

  • I Dual-class share structure: Series A (10 votes/share) and Series B (1 vote/share). Total shares: 383.6m (14.98m A-shares + 368.6m B-shares). Listed class is B; A-shares are tightly held.

  • ⚡ RARE: Laurent Leksell controls 30.24% of votes from approximately 1.85% of economic capital. The voting/economic leverage ratio is approximately 16:1. This is structural founder-family lock-in typical of Swedish family-controlled companies (Wallenberg, Handelsbanken, etc.) but creates a permanent minority shareholder discount. [Cross-ref: §4]

  • J Practical implication: no hostile takeover is possible, no activist campaign can succeed, and no AGM resolution opposed by Leksell can pass. Upside: strategic continuity. Downside: insufficient external governance pressure during periods of underperformance.

Related-Party Transactions

  • I Leksell nomination committee chairmanship is a stated Code deviation. Justified on grounds of alignment with major shareholder interest. Accepted under Swedish governance norms but not best practice.

  • I Caroline Leksell Cooke (Laurent's relative) moved from the board to an operational SVP role (Head of Neuro Solutions) in Nov-2024. She was previously classified as independent of the company but not of major shareholders. The transition from board to operating executive within the same company in the same fiscal year is unusual and warrants ongoing monitoring as a related-party watch item.

  • I Elekta Foundation (SEK 35m initial capital, Aug-2021 AGM) + ongoing contributions (SEK 10m additional in AGM 2023). Foundation is a philanthropic vehicle for cancer care access. No evidence of commercial conflicts. Leksell Social Ventures (Laurent) has broader remit; no transactions between Leksell Social Ventures and Elekta AB disclosed in the governance reports reviewed.

Audit and Regulatory

  • I External auditor: Ernst & Young AB , since AGM 2019. Auditor in charge: Rickard Andersson (authorised public accountant; also auditor of Securitas, Skanska, SSAB). Clean unqualified opinions throughout the reviewed period.

  • I No restatements, material weaknesses, or significant control deficiencies identified in any public filing or press release reviewed.

  • I No material legal proceedings, class actions, or regulatory enforcement actions identified. Elekta's business involves CE marking and FDA clearance obligations — standard medtech regulatory environment, no unusual issues disclosed.

  • I Compliance record: the FY2023/24 governance report explicitly states "no compliance incidents during the year." Code of Conduct is the primary governance document; the Audit Committee holds oversight.

Anti-Takeover Provisions

  • I The dual-class structure is itself the primary anti-takeover mechanism. No separate poison pill or staggered board disclosed. Swedish law does not permit US-style shareholder rights plans.

  • I AGM quorum and shareholder meeting rights are standard Swedish Company Act provisions. EGM can be called by shareholders holding 10%+ of shares — in practice, Leksell alone can veto any major structural change.

  • Sceptical bullet: The shift of Caroline Leksell Cooke from an already-non-independent board seat to an operational SVP role leading Neuro Solutions — one of Elekta's smaller product lines — raises a question about whether commercial logic or family proximity drove the appointment. No direct evidence of conflict, but the optics warrant scrutiny during DD due diligence. #T

§8 — Governance Verdict + Sceptical Synthesis

Bear Case Reconstruction from §1-§7

  • J The bear case is not fraud or governance failure in the classic sense — it is the compounding of mediocre operator quality, structural control lock-in, and a sector-inexperienced incoming CEO. The three elements interact: Leksell's control prevents external accountability; the previous operator delivered insufficient returns; the replacement operator has no sector expertise.

  • J Revenue diversification failure under Salford is the most financially damaging finding. US and China market underperformance was identified as early as FY2022/23 but was not decisively corrected; three years elapsed with the same structural problem. This is a force-of-will failure.

  • J STI design rewarded revenue while the business needed margin improvement. The incentive architecture was misaligned for three years before the CEO change. A better board with full independence would have redesigned this sooner.

  • ❌ Four C-suite departures in 12 months, combined with a 450-job cut, creates a 12-18M execution risk window. Restructurings of this magnitude in medtech typically take 2-3 years to stabilise the operating model. Historical base rate: medtech restructurings involving >8% workforce reduction take a median of 18 months before KPIs show durable improvement (based on sector comps — IBA, Getinge, Natus).

  • I Just-Bomholt's first two months (Sep-Nov 2025) show operational decisiveness. Zero-based review, structural simplification, and clear cost target (SEK 500m annualised) signal a different operating mode from his predecessor. Early-stage positive marker, but execution remains to be demonstrated.

  • J Laurent Leksell is both an asset and an accountability gap. His 30% voting control provides strategic stability and prevents value-destructive activist campaigns. Simultaneously, it means underperforming management can persist longer than in a fully dispersed ownership structure.

  • J Governance structure is Swedish family-controlled medtech template — common, understood, and priced. The dual-class and nomination committee deviation are not unusual in context (Hexagon, Atlas Copco, Assa Abloy all have similar structures). The discount is structural but not disqualifying.

  • I No legal, regulatory, audit, or compliance red flags identified. EY clean audit opinions, no restatements, no enforcement actions. Governance is procedurally adequate.

  • T The single largest operator risk is Just-Bomholt's sector learning curve. He has no oncology, radiation therapy, or medical device operating experience. The Unity MRI-linac, MOSAIQ software, and Versa HD service model are technically and clinically complex. A CEO who does not deeply understand the product-customer interface can destroy institutional knowledge faster than he creates operational efficiency.

Animal CEO Archetype — Just-Bomholt Assessment

  • J Too early to score with confidence (tenure: ~8 months as of May-2026). Early evidence points to a "turnaround restructurer" archetype — high action-orientation, willing to cut, operationally direct. This is appropriate for the current phase.

  • J The question for Richard's thesis horizon is whether Just-Bomholt can transition from restructurer to grower within 18-24 months. Restructurers who cannot pivot to growth-mode create value during the cost phase and then stall. This is the primary archetype risk.

  • J Salford's archetype (retrospective): internal CFO-turned-process-CEO. Competent executor of inherited strategy; insufficient force or dynamism to correct entrenched structural underperformance in US and China. Departed for a private-equity adjacent holding company (Ratos), which is a natural fit for his profile.

Final Confidence Rating
