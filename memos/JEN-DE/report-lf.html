EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The incoming CEO, Dr. Dominic Dorfner, is expected to assume the role no later than October 1, 2026, creating a seven-month leadership gap during critical semiconductor recovery cycles. CFO Dr. Prisca Havranek-Kosicek has served since April 1, 2023, and the Supervisory Board prematurely extended her contract in March 2025 by five years through February 2031, securing essential leadership stability during the transitional phase. Dr. Ralf Kuschnereit manages the high-margin semiconductor and biophotonics business units, and the Supervisory Board aligned key management contracts by extending Kuschnereit's appointment to December 31, 2030. These synchronized contract extensions successfully mitigated immediate key-person vacancy risks, though the temporary leadership void introduces strategic drift risks around portfolio divestment velocity and non-core asset sales, particularly the stalled Prodomax automotive automation disposal.

Governance architecture at Jenoptik is exceptionally robust. All six shareholder representatives are classified as fully independent by the board, and each share carries exactly one vote, meaning there are no dual-class structures or special voting privileges, protecting minority shareholders from disproportionate voting dominance. The board features specialized committees including Nomination, Personnel, Audit & ESG, and Strategy & Investment, with a newly established Transformation Committee under Elke Eckstein assisting in accelerating strategic adaptations. However, no mandatory share ownership guidelines exist for Jenoptik's Supervisory Board members, creating a material alignment deficit relative to best-practice governance standards. The Compliance Management System was audited in late 2025 and successfully certified under ISO 37301, and no confirmed cases of corruption or bribery occurred in 2025.

Organizational culture indicators reflect successful operational verticalization but face mounting geopolitical recruitment risks. Jenoptik achieved a 73.00% global benchmark for employee satisfaction in its annual anonymous workforce survey, and the employee-related turnover rate declined to 4.50% in 2025 compared to 4.80% in the prior fiscal year. The legacy matrix organization was dissolved in January 2025 to transition toward four vertical Strategic Business Units, establishing direct accountability and aiming to accelerate decision velocity. However, the rise of the far-right AfD in Thuringia severely threatens Jenoptik's ability to attract highly skilled international specialists, with multiple prospective employees refusing job offers because of Thuringia's hostile political climate. This geopolitical talent bottleneck represents a structural operational risk to the newly built Dresden and Jena production campuses during the next semiconductor upcycle.
