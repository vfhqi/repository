HILTON FOOD GROUP (HFG.L) — SELL-SIDE SENTIMENT ANALYSIS

Equity Research Memo | April 2026

Prepared for: Fundamental equity portfolio manager | 12–24 month holding horizon
Subject: Recent shifts in analyst sentiment, earnings expectations, price targets, and narrative framing around HFG following FY2024 results (April 2025), H1 2025 results (September 2025), Q3 2025 trading update (October 2025), and FY2025 results (March 2026).
Tone: Data-driven assessment. The consensus story is fragmenting. Core business resilience is masking real structural headwinds in seafood, regulatory friction, and 2026 profit guidance erosion.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — 10 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Sentiment has turned markedly cautious since late 2024. Analyst consensus shifted from "Buy with 765p average PT" (April 2025) to mixed "Buy/Hold" with significant PT cuts. Berenberg slashed target from 1,090p to 790p; RBC cut from 750p to 540p; Peel Hunt downgraded to Hold from Buy (670p). Average 12-month PT now ~665p (April 2026), down ~13% from April 2025.

2. EPS delivery has disappointed. FY2024 adjusted EPS of 61.0p missed consensus by 7.2%. FY2025 adjusted EPS of 56.0p fell 7.4% YoY. FY2026 guidance (£60m–£65m PBT) implies mid-teens PBT decline vs 2025's £73.2m, pricing in continued deterioration.

3. Profit guidance reset is the key inflection. Management cut 2026 PBT guidance by ~15% midpoint (to £62.5m) in November 2025 (Q3 update). This is the third major reset since early 2025 and signals loss of near-term momentum. Analysts now assume difficult profit progression in 2026.

4. Seafood is a terminal problem, not cyclical. Seachill moved into loss-making; Foppen smoked salmon facing FDA regulatory restrictions (Greek facility non-operational until 2026), necessitating costly Netherlands relocation; UK whitefish demand crushed by raw material inflation. Management is now refocusing on core red meat, effectively admitting seafood expansion was strategic error.

5. Margins are under structural pressure. Operating margin collapsed to 2.3% (FY2025) from 2.6% (FY2024). Constant-currency operating profit fell 4.4%. Margin pressure reflects raw material inflation pass-through difficulty, seafood losses, and regulatory remediation costs. No path to 2024 margin levels visible in 2026 guidance.

  1. ROCE dipped below management targets. ROCE 20.1% (FY2025, down from 21.7% in FY2024) is now threading the 20% hurdle. Expected to fall further in 2026 due to elevated CapEx (Poland £30m investment), restructuring, and lower profits. This signals capital efficiency is deteriorating despite automation.

7. Growth catalysts (Saudi Arabia, Canada) are 18+ months away. Saudi JV (NADEC partnership) commences H2 2026; Canada facility online early 2027 with profit contribution from 2027 onwards. These are too distant to offset 2026 headwinds; market is pricing in near-zero value until first earnings accretion evident.

8. Customer concentration risk is structural. Tesco and Ahold Delhaize groups comprise majority of revenue. Tesco expanded Hilton's red meat mandate to 100% (June 2024), and Ahold Delhaize deepened collaboration. This is a double-edged sword: contract security but zero pricing power and exposure to retailer margin compression.

  1. The "resilient core" framing is becoming hollow. Analysts acknowledge red meat and fresh prepared foods are defensively solid (volume +0.2% in inflationary 2025, margins intact), but this is increasingly irrelevant given seafood drag, capex intensity, and regulatory overhang. Core profitability is not sufficient to cover group PBT shortfall.

10. Valuation reset is incomplete. Stock trades at ~12x FY2026E EPS (consensus ~5.5p), down from ~17x FY2025E two quarters ago. Downside risk persists if 2026 PBT misses guidance again, or if regulatory/seafood headwinds extend into 2027. Upside thesis hinges on successful Canada/Saudi ramp and 2027 profit recovery—a 2-year thesis at minimum.

### FY2026 Guidance — The Critical Miss in Progress

- Management Guidance Cut (November 2025 Q3 Update): In October 2025, management issued a profit warning, cutting FY2025 PBT guidance (not 2026, but the announcement reset 2026 expectations downward). More importantly, management stated 2026 adjusted PBT guidance of £60m–£65m, implying a ~15% decline from FY2025's £73.2m.

This is an abrupt reset. Just six months prior (May 2025 trading update), management had guided towards £75m–£80m for 2026. The November cut suggests either (a) Q2/Q3 2025 trading was materially worse than expected, or (b) management visibility on Foppen/Seachill deterioration only crystallized late in the year.

- Consensus Collapse on 2026: By March 2026, sell-side consensus for FY2026 adjusted PBT was £62m–£65m (midpoint ~£63.5m), implying:
- -13% PBT decline vs. FY2025 baseline
- EPS of ~47p–49p (vs. 56.0p in FY2025), a -13% decline
- Operating margin assumption of ~2.1% (further compression)
- ROCE falling to ~19.5% as capex (Poland £30m) and restructuring absorb cash

- Three Guidance Resets in 12 Months: This pattern is damaging:
- April 2025: Consensus £75m–£77m for FY2026
- May 2025: Management guidance ~£75m–£80m for 2026
- October 2025: Management cuts to £60m–£65m for 2026
- March 2026: Consensus settles at £62m–£65m for 2026

Each reset materially increases earnings uncertainty and weakens near-term investor confidence. Analysts have now lost visibility and are increasingly publishing "wide range" estimates (±5–10% from consensus).

Downside Risks:
1. Foppen FDA clearance slips into Q1 2027 instead of "late 2026," further delaying profit recovery. Impact: -£2m–£5m profit impact in 2026, pushing PBT to £58m–£60m.

3. Saudi JV reaches profitable run-rate by late 2026 instead of 2027. Impact: +£2m–£4m PBT contribution in 2027.

#### September 2025 — H1 2025 Interim Results

Tone: Cautiously Optimistic, With Caveats

Tone: Defensive, Credibility Damaged

Subtext: Management is defending large capex during a downswing. This is risky; if 2026 profit misses further, capex will come under pressure and Poland project could slip.

## SECTION 4: RISKS AND CONCERNS (STRUCTURAL vs. CYCLICAL)

Revenue & Demand Risks

Structural Concern: Customer Concentration & Pricing Power

HFG supplies 50%+ revenue to Tesco and Ahold Delhaize groups. Both are under margin pressure themselves (UK grocery market deflation, European retailer consolidation). Risk: Retailers demand price cuts to offset their own margin compression. Indicators:

- September 2025: Ahold Delhaize deepened collaboration with Hilton but tied it to "efficiency and cost reduction targets." Analyst interpretation: Delhaize is cost-cutting and will squeeze suppliers.

Impact on FY2026–27: If retailers demand 3–5% price cuts, Hilton's 2.1% operating margin becomes loss-making on red meat (0.5–1.5% negative). This would force asset disposals or restructuring.

Demand Softness in Seafood:

Seachill volume fell 2.6% in FY2025 (seafood, vegetarian, vegan combined). UK whitefish inflation has crushed demand (consumers trading down to frozen/imported fish). Management expects volumes to remain soft through 2026.

Impact on FY2026: Seachill losses could persist at £1m–£2m even with cost reduction, requiring further restructuring/closure by 2027.

Competition & Market Structure

Structural Risk: Shift Towards Retailer In-House Processing & Scale Advantage

HFG, despite "leading" UK position, has limited scale (market cap ~£700m, revenue ~£2,100m). Risk: Retailers may insource processing or consolidate with larger suppliers.

Impact on FY2027+: Loss of major contract = -£100m–£200m revenue, forcing portfolio restructuring.

### Regulatory & Political Risks

Critical Risk: Foppen FDA Clearance Slippage & Permanent Loss of US Market
