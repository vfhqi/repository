JC Decaux SA & Out-of-Home Advertising Industry

Business Model & Sector Primer

Author: [C] - Claude Research
Date: 13-Apr-26
Scope: Industry economics, profit drivers, and share price catalysts
Focus Company: JC Decaux SA (DEC, Euronext Paris)
Reference Peers: Ströer, Clear Channel Outdoor, Lamar Advertising, oOh!media, Global (Outdoor)

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Out-of-Home advertising industry is undergoing a structural bifurcation. Traditional static formats (billboards, street furniture, transit displays) face low single-digit organic revenue growth and mature margin profiles. Digital Out-of-Home , powered by programmatic auction systems and supply-side platforms (SSPs), is expanding 20%+ annually but from a smaller base, now representing ~40% of major operators' revenue.

JC Decaux—the world's largest OOH player (€3.96bn revenue, 2025)—achieved flat reported growth in 2025 (0.8%) following three strong years, signaling a material slowdown. This shift is critical: growth deceleration often precedes analyst downgrades and multiple compression, regardless of margin strength.

Share prices are moved by four tiers of drivers:

1. Fulcrum Drivers (Top 1/3): Digital penetration % & programmatic adoption rates; concession renewals/renegotiations in major geographies; overall advertising spend cyclicality (macro sensitivity); FX movements in GBP/USD/AUD exposure.

2. Key Drivers (Middle 1/3): DOOH segment organic growth rates; geographic diversification (China recovery, France/UK dual exposure); operating leverage & margin expansion; leverage & interest rate environment for refinancing.

  1. Tertiary Drivers (Bottom 1/3): Individual contract wins (airports, metro systems); share buyback pace; dividend policy; non-core asset sales.

4. False Friends: Management commentary on "addressability" and "audience data"; transit passenger numbers recovering to pre-COVID; street furniture CPM growth in static format.

This primer dissects each driver by timeframe (short/mid/long-term), macro backdrop, and persistence. It flags the gap between what companies discuss and what actually moves valuations.

1. DIGITAL DOOH PENETRATION & PROGRAMMATIC ADOPTION [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: Persistent uptrend | Duration: 2-5 years | Conviction: High
Color: GREEN

The Metric That Matters

Digital revenue now represents 39-42% of JC Decaux total revenue (2024-25). Programmatic specifically—revenue booked through VIOOH SSP and DSP auction systems—hit €180.5m in 2025, growing 19.2% organically, but remains only 10.9% of total digital revenue. This gap is material.

Programmatic adoption across JC Decaux's installed base grew from ~6% of digital revenue (2022) to 10-11% (2025)—meaningful, but not yet the majority. The next fulcrum is reaching 20-25% of digital revenue by 2027-28. When it does, three things compound:

1. Margin expansion: Programmatic auctions eliminate manual sales overhead; SSP take-rates are thin but COGS-light. Incremental DOOH at 20%+ margin vs. 12-15% for static formats.

  1. Velocity & flexibility: Advertisers gain instant, real-time CPM feedback. Budgets flow to screens that win auctions, not to pre-negotiated commitments. Inventory turns faster; sell-through rates climb.

  2. Valuation re-rating: Investors view "programmatic DOOH" as a software/platform play (tech multiples, 20-25x EBIT) rather than a utility operator (12-14x EBIT). JC Decaux's peer group would widen from Ströer/CCO to include The Trade Desk, Criteo, Vistar Media.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- Programmatic revenue line acceleration, especially DSP take (% of total DOOH booked via third-party DSPs vs. direct).
- VIOOH platform metrics: # of DSP integrations, geographic rollout, API uptime/technical credibility.
- Advertiser commentary on ease-of-use & repeat purchase rates.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Programmatic as % of digital hitting guidance (e.g., JC Decaux targeting 12-15% of digital by 2026).
- Net new DOOH screen installations & retrofit schedules (required to capture upside).
- Competitive parity: are Ströer, CCO, Lamar installing SSP/DSP stacks at similar pace? If JC Decaux lags, it signals execution risk.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Programmatic mix → total EBITDA margin. Model: 10% of revenue at 35% EBIT margin (programmatic) vs. 35% of revenue at 28% EBIT margin (static). Each 10pp shift in mix drives ~100bp of group EBIT margin lift.
- Replacement of "direct sales" headcount with engineering/platform teams (one-time restructuring charge, followed by sustained FCF accretion).
- Customer concentration risk: will programmatic commoditize screen pricing, attracting price-sensitive SME advertisers vs. premium brands? Or will layering of audience data (foot traffic, demographics, weather, timing) sustain premium CPMs?

False Friend

Management commentary on "rich audience data" and "addressability" is discussed frequently but rarely moves analysts' EBITDA estimates. Why? Because:

  1. JC Decaux lacks first-party login data (unlike Google/Facebook/Trade Desk). Audience inference is inferred from location patterns, demographics, and contextual signals, not identity.
  2. Privacy regulation (GDPR, DMA) constrains deterministic targeting; probabilistic modeling is legally grey.
  3. Advertiser feedback (per conference calls) focuses on CPM/scale, not audience precision. A premium CPM lift from audience targeting is 5-10%, not transformative.

Marker: If an earnings call features 3+ minutes on "audience addressability" but programmatic growth guidance doesn't change, it's noise.

Macro Backdrops

- Rising ad spend (bull): Programmatic adoption accelerates; budgets shift from static to dynamic formats faster. JC Decaux could guide +15%+ programmatic growth.
- Falling ad spend (recession): Lower total budget = sharper mix shift to lowest-cost-per-impression formats. Programmatic wins, but on a smaller base. Net EBIT impact: ⇒ margin accretion, ⇒ revenue growth.
- Interest rate swings: Do not directly affect programmatic adoption, but refinancing cost determines capex appetite for new DOOH screens. Rising rates → deferred screen installations → lower long-term digital mix.

2. CONCESSION RENEWALS & GEOGRAPHIC PRICING POWER [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: Event-driven, lumpy | Duration: 1-5 years | Conviction: High
Color: ORANGE (France & UK renewals due 2025-2027; renegotiation terms TBD)

Why It Matters

JC Decaux's core moat is exclusive, long-dated concession agreements with city governments, metro operators, and airport authorities. Street furniture, the company's largest segment (€2.0bn, 51% of revenue), lives entirely on these contracts. Transport (€533m, 13%) is similar.

A concession renewal is not a sales win—it's a renegotiation of economics. When a city's contract expires, the operator bids to renew. Outcomes:

1. Renewal at same terms: Static revenue, margin unchanged.
2. Renewal with price uplift: City captures part of digital uplift via higher rev-share or minimum guarantee. Operator margin compresses.
3. Renewal loss: Competitor wins; revenue gone for 10+ years.
4. Renewal deferral: Operator operates under temporary extension; uncertainty suppresses valuation multiples.

### Historical Pattern & Current Exposure

JC Decaux operates across 80+ countries, ~3,600 cities. ~15-20% of concessions renew each year, with $100-200m of revenue under renegotiation at any time. Material renewals in recent/upcoming windows:

  • France: €800m+ of street furniture & transport revenue. Major metro/city contracts due 2025-27. France has been extracting price concessions; Paris metro renegotiation (2023-24) resulted in higher rev-share demands. Precedent suggests 2-4% annual rev-share uplift per new contract.
  • UK: ~€600m of revenue (transport + street furniture). London TfL contract expires ~2026-2027. Political pressure to "maximize public benefit" is high. Precedent: TfL renegotiations historically result in 3-5% uplift in JC Decaux costs.
  • US (Clear Channel Outdoor): Dallas, Houston, other major cities renewing 2024-2026 on compressed multiples due to CCO's divested international footprint & lower profitability.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- Contract win/loss announcements, especially in Top 5 geographies (France, UK, Germany, China, Australia).
- Management tone on "pricing power" and "customer stickiness" in Q&A. Any sign of surprise price concessions → multiple compression (5-10pp EV/EBIT multiple haircut observed historically).
- Execution of signed renewals (on-time, on-budget service obligations).

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Aggregate organic growth in street furniture segment. If renewals are priced below organic growth trajectory, it signals negotiating weakness. E.g., if organic growth is 3% but renewal terms imply 0-1% uplift, analysts revise medium-term EBITDA guidance down.
- Competitive pressure evidence: does a CCO win displace JC Decaux, or does JC Decaux's global scale insulate it? Historical answer: JC Decaux rarely loses major contracts to small regional players; vulnerability is to other large platforms.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Total addressable TAM by geography. If cities systematically reduce concession values due to digital cannibalization or budget pressures, long-term terminal growth rate falls, affecting DCF.
- Optionality: can JC Decaux offset concession headwind by upgrading infrastructure, shifting to DOOH, or bundling adjacent services (cleaning, WiFi, 5G)? Historical success: Paris bundles public WiFi + ads; generates goodwill + allows price maintenance.

Macro Backdrops

- Economic boom (tight budget conditions in cities): Cities feel revenue pressure; demand higher rev-shares from JC Decaux. Negative for renewals.
- Recession (tight municipal budgets): Cities prioritize basic services; defer renegotiations; may accept lower bids from smaller operators to cut costs. Mixed: volume risk, but less renegotiation pressure.
- Rising debt/austerity (EU context, post-COVID): French/Spanish municipalities under fiscal stress; using concession fees as revenue source. JC Decaux faces headwind.

False Friend

"City beautification" and "public amenities" commentary. JC Decaux frequently highlights investment in bus shelters, seating, WiFi, and digital art as public good. Politicians love this narrative. It does not affect contract pricing. Cities care about net cash inflow; amenities are cost center. Mark these discussions as PR, not material to EBITDA.

3. ADVERTISING SPEND CYCLICALITY & MACRO SENSITIVITY [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: High, procyclical | Duration: 2-8 quarters | Conviction: Very High
Color: RED (2025 slowdown evident; 2026 cyclical tailwind offset by caution)

Why It Dominates

OOH advertising is pro-cyclical: harder hit by recessions than GDP, first budget cut in downturns, fastest recovery in booms. JC Decaux's organic growth correlates ~0.8-0.9 with global advertising spend indices.

Evidence from recent cycles:

| Period | Global Ad Spend | JC Decaux Organic Growth | Note |
|--------|------------------|--------------------------|------|
| 2021 | +7.9% | +6.2% | Post-COVID recovery |
| 2022 | +2.0% | +4.8% | Olympics/World Cup boost |
| 2023 | +3.2% | +9.1% | Tech advertising rebound |
| 2024 | +4.5% | +9.7% | Strong momentum sustained |
| 2025 | +2.5% (est.) | +1.8% | Slowdown, deceleration clear |

2025 was the inflection. Organic growth halved from 2024 (9.7%) to 2025 (1.8%), despite DOOH remaining strong at +10% organically. This signals weakness in static formats (street furniture, billboards), which comprise 55-60% of revenue.

Current Macro Environment

As of April 2026:
- Global recession risk elevated but not imminent. Consumer spending "resilient" but savings rates compressed.
- Advertising industry analysts project +9.5-9.8% ad spend growth in 2026, driven by Olympics (Feb), World Cup (2026), and political elections in 40+ countries. This is cyclical; excluding these events, growth is +7-7.5%.
- By 2027, if recession hits, expect -5% to -8% total advertising spend, with OOH down -10% to -15%.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters): Real-time Ad spend indices. Most OOH operators report monthly/quarterly revenue by region; analysts triangulate against:
- Google/Meta ad revenue (proxy for overall digital ad market health).
- S&P 500 earnings revisions (corporate ad budgets follow profitability).
- 10-year Gilt yields & credit spreads (investor risk appetite → brand spend).

Signal: If Google reports -2% YoY ad growth in a quarter, JC Decaux's next update will likely disappoint by 100-200bp of organic growth.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Analyst consensus revisions. Once macro weakness is evident (2-3 quarters of misses), sell-side typically revises EBITDA guidance down 5-15%. This cascades to FCF revisions, affecting valuation.
- Management guidance changes. JC Decaux has historically given full-year guidance; early cuts are rare but signal lack of visibility. Each guidance cut → 5-8pp multiple compression.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Mix shift. In downturns, advertisers cut premium placements first (airport, transit) and preserve street furniture (lower CPM, hyperlocal reach). This hurts high-margin segments, compressing group EBIT margins 100-200bp.
- Competitive behavior. If recession hits, smaller OOH operators (regional, leveraged) may become distressed sellers or file for protection. Consolidation opportunity for JC Decaux; or price war if Ströer/CCO aggressively defend share.

Macro Backdrop Mapping

| Scenario | 2026-27 Organic Growth | EBIT Margin Impact | Share Price Implication |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Boom (ad spend +8%+) | +7% to +10% | Stable/+50bp | +15-20% over 18m |
| Soft growth (+3-5%) | +2% to +4% | -50bp | Flat to +5% |
| Recession (-5% ad spend) | -3% to -5% | -150bp | -20-30% trough |
| Recovery (+6%) | +5% to +7% | +100bp | +20-25% |

Key: Share price is forward-looking; misses are already priced in. The inflection happens when guidance improves from trough, not when absolute growth turns positive.

False Friend

"Passenger traffic recovery to pre-COVID levels." This metric is discussed extensively on transport advertising calls. E.g., "airport footfall +8% YoY." However:

  1. Footfall growth alone does NOT drive OOH revenue. CPM and fill rate (% of inventory sold) matter equally.
    2.     In a weak ad market, passenger growth is offset by CPM compression. Net revenue impact: flat or negative.
  2. Airlines, travel brands (primary airport advertisers) cut budgets faster than footfall drops; they're acyclical.

Signal: If transport segment grows <4% despite footfall +6%, it's a CPM compression problem, not a volume problem. This signals weakness, not strength.

4. CURRENCY MOVEMENTS: GBP, USD, AUD EXPOSURE [FULCRUM DRIVER]

Classification: Fulcrum Driver | Volatility: High, exogenous | Duration: Persistent (quarterly) | Conviction: High
Color: YELLOW (GBP weakness beneficial in 2025; USD/AUD volatility increasing)

### Revenue Exposure by Currency

JC Decaux reports in EUR but earns across:

| Geography | % of Revenue | Primary Currency | Exposure |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|----------|
| France | ~35% | EUR | Neutral (base) |
| UK | ~12-15% | GBP | Material (most important for FX impact) |
| Germany & Rest of Europe | ~18% | EUR/Mixed | Moderate |
| Asia-Pacific | ~12% | AUD, CNY, SGD | Moderate-high (AUD most volatile) |
| North America | ~8% | USD | Moderate |
| Rest of World | ~10-15% | Mixed | Moderate-high |

The critical exposures are GBP and AUD. Why?

  • GBP: UK operations are second only to France; volatility is high post-Brexit. GBP/EUR trading 0.82-0.89; each 2pp move = ~€8-15m EBITDA swing on €600m UK revenue base.
  • AUD: Australia (oOh!media peer) and NZ are high-growth markets for JC Decaux. AUD/EUR trading 0.60-0.65; each 1pp move = ~€5-8m swing.
  • USD: Smaller absolute exposure; but US is growing segment. Each 2pp USD/EUR move = ~€3-5m swing.

Historical Impact

- 2015-2020: GBP weakness (-15%) after Brexit was a net positive for JC Decaux (pound revenues translated to lower EUR amount, but organic growth in UK offset FX headwind). Analysts largely ignored FX; it was "noise."
- 2021-2022: AUD strength (+8-10% vs. EUR) provided tailwind to EBITDA in Australia; visible in earnings surprises.
- 2023-2025: EUR strength vs GBP/AUD has been headwind. GBP/EUR weakness created ~€20-30m net headwind to reported EBITDA in 2024-25.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1 quarter):
- FX volatility creates timing distortions in reported (vs. organic) growth. Analysts parse this carefully, but it creates noise.
- Surprise moves (>3pp in a month) force guidance updates. E.g., if GBP weakness accelerates (Brexit uncertainty, political shock), JC Decaux may pre-announce lower reported revenue.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Hedging policy changes. JC Decaux has historically used FX forwards to hedge 50-70% of forward cash flows in GBP/AUD. If hedging is raised to 80%+ due to volatility, it signals management caution → multiple compression.
- Analysts begin to adjust FX assumptions in DCF models. If GBP continues weakness, terminal value is marked down. This is subtle but affects valuations.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Organic mix shift. If AUD weakness persists, Australia profitability declines; incentive to sell/divest Asia. Opposite of growth narrative.
- Refinancing cost. JC Decaux's debt is EUR-denominated but earns FX in GBP/AUD. If weakness persists, debt service in real terms rises; leverage ratios deteriorate.

Macro Backdrops

- UK recession / GBP weakness: Negative for JC Decaux. Expect 200-300bp organic growth headwind in UK operations plus FX translation loss. Aggregate impact: -3% EBITDA reported growth, even if organic is stable.
- AUD strength / RBA rate hikes: Positive. Each 50bp rate hike in Australia typically drives AUD +2-3% vs. EUR over next quarter.
- USD weakness / Fed rate cuts: Modest negative (smaller exposure), but impacts sentiment toward multinational operators.

Hedging Mechanics

JC Decaux uses layered hedging:
1. Operational hedges: Match revenue currency to spend currency (e.g., UK operations spend GBP, earn GBP).
2. Financial hedges: FX forwards on known cash flows (dividends, debt service).
3. No hedge on growth: Future organic growth in AUD is unhedged, exposing it to FX volatility.

Analyst signal: If JC Decaux increases financial hedging beyond 70%, it signals management expects FX headwinds; multiple compression likely.

5. DOOH ORGANIC GROWTH RATE DECELERATION [KEY DRIVER]

Classification: Key Driver | Volatility: Moderate (persistent within range 5-20%) | Duration: 2-4 quarters | Conviction: High
Color: ORANGE (growth decelerating; 2025 +10% organic vs. 2024 +22%)

The Inflection Point

DOOH was JC Decaux's hero in 2021-2024:

| Year | DOOH Organic Growth | As % of Total Revenue |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 2021 | +19% | 25% |
| 2022 | +17% | 28% |
| 2023 | +18% | 33% |
| 2024 | +22% | 39% |
| 2025 | +10% | 42% |

2025 marks a hard deceleration: 10% vs. 22% prior year. This is not cyclical noise; it reflects market maturation:

  1. Installed base saturation in major geographies (France, UK, Germany, Australia).
  2. Baseline DOOH revenue growing in absolute terms (now €1.66bn), making incremental growth harder to achieve at previous rates.
    3.     Macro softness disproportionately hits DOOH, which skews toward premium categories (airports, transit). Static formats more resilient (hyperlocal, lower CPM).

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- Sequential DOOH organic growth rates. Analysts track this obsessively; a deceleration of 200bp in a single quarter triggers immediate revisions.
- New screen installation targets. JC Decaux provides annual capex guidance; if screen installation count declines YoY, it signals lower conviction on DOOH durability.
- Programmatic mix within DOOH. If programmatic growth slows (vs. direct DOOH sales), it suggests SSP take-rate or adoption hitting wall.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Peer comparison. Is the DOOH slowdown industry-wide, or JC Decaux-specific? If Ströer/CCO sustain 15%+ DOOH growth, JC Decaux has execution problem.
- Margin profile of incremental DOOH. Early DOOH screens are high-margin (retrofits, high-traffic). Later cohorts may be lower-margin (interior locations, lower CPM). Analysts adjust unit economics.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Terminal DOOH growth rate. As a mature platform, DOOH should stabilize at 5-8% organic (in line with advertising spend growth), not 15-20%. Analysts will model this; it lowers long-term EBITDA trajectory.

Macro Backdrops

- Ad spend boom: DOOH growth accelerates; CPM inflation drives incremental margin. Offset slightly by screen installation capex.
- Ad spend slowdown: DOOH growth decelerates; but holds up better than static (mix shift tailwind).
- Tech slowdown (digital ad market weakness): DOOH could underperform static as advertisers cut programmatic budgets first.

6. GEOGRAPHIC DIVERSIFICATION & CHINA RECOVERY [KEY DRIVER]

Classification: Key Driver | Volatility: Regional, event-driven | Duration: 2-6 quarters | Conviction: Moderate (China recovery timeline uncertain) | Color: YELLOW

### Regional Performance Divergence

2024-25 revealed stark regional divergence:

| Region | 2024 Organic | 2025 Organic | Trend |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| France | +8% (est.) | +0-2% | Softening |
| UK | +12% | +3-5% | Sharp deceleration |
| Germany & Europe | +9% | +2-4% | Weakening |
| Asia-Pacific | +6% | -1% to 0% | China weight |
| North America | +8% (est.) | +4-6% | Stable |

China is the wild card. JC Decaux reports "Asia-Pacific" as single segment; China is ~60-70% of this. Pre-COVID, China was 5-6% of group revenue; it's now ~8-10% but suppressed:

- 2020-2021: COVID shutdowns killed China transport advertising (airports, metros shut for months).
- 2022-2023: Slow recovery, travel restrictions.
- 2024: Domestic travel recovering but weak (property crisis, consumer caution).
- 2025: China transport advertising "meaningfully below pre-COVID."
- 2026E: Management guidance suggests "positive impact from China" in H1 2026.

The upside is material. If China transport reverts to pre-COVID levels (€50-70m annual revenue base, +8-10% growth), that's +€4-7m EBITDA uplift. Small % of group (0.5-0.8%), but high-margin.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- China activity data: airport passenger numbers, metro ridership. JC Decaux management provides quarterly Asia-Pacific metrics. Any acceleration vs. guidance → upside surprise potential.
- Currency flows: RMB weakness supports China AD spend; any RMB appreciation headwind.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Organic growth rate acceleration in Asia-Pacific. If China stabilizes at +5%+, JC Decaux beats mid-term guides.
- Mix impact: Is China recovery in high-margin transport, or low-margin street furniture? If transport-heavy, margin accretion.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- China TAM and competitive moat. JC Decaux has strong Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou presence. Risk: Chinese competitors (China Outdoor Media Corp) gain market share; international expansion by JC Decaux constrained by China's FDI rules.

False Friend

"Travel in China recovering to 80% of pre-COVID by 2025" was widely cited by management in 2024-25 calls. True at a macro level, but does not guarantee ad spend recovery. Reasons:

  1. Chinese advertisers shifted budgets heavily to Alibaba, Tencent (digital, targeted). OOH low in mindshare.
  2. Corporate travel (ads for business services, luxury) remains depressed; this is OOH's strength.
  3. Government is tightening advertising budgets (regulations).

Result: Even with travel recovery, China advertising growth lags travel recovery. This surprised analysts in 2024-25.

Marker: If management says "China travel +20% YoY" but China organic segment revenue is flat/down, it's a CPM or sell-through headwind, not a volume problem.

7. LEVERAGE, REFINANCING COSTS & INTEREST RATE ENVIRONMENT [KEY DRIVER]

Classification: Key Driver | Volatility: Moderate (refinancing event-driven) | Duration: 2-8 quarters | Conviction: High
Color: YELLOW (2025-2026 benign; 2027 maturity wall risk)

### Debt Profile & Refinancing Schedule

JC Decaux 2025 debt metrics:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Net Debt | ~€1.8bn (down 22% YoY) |
| Debt/EBITDA | ~2.2x (2025 run-rate EBITDA ~€830m) |
| FCF/Net Debt | ~19% (2025 FCF of €342.9m) |
| Next Bond Maturity | 2028 |
| Undrawn RCF | €825m (expires 2030) |
| Fixed Rate Debt | ~60-70% of total |
| Avg. Coupon | ~3.0-3.5% |

Maturity schedule: No material debt maturities until 2028 (€500m bond). 2026-27 are clear for refinancing risk. But:

  1. If JC Decaux's EBITDA declines due to macro weakness, leverage ratios deteriorate. Debt/EBITDA of 2.5-2.7x is still investment-grade, but covenant breach risk emerges if EBITDA falls >10%.
  2. 2028 bond maturity at ~€500m will require refinancing or pay-down. If rates are 4.5%+ by then (vs. 3.0-3.5% coupons today), refinancing cost rises materially.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- FCF generation. JC Decaux's main lever to reduce leverage. If FCF misses (due to working capital swings, capex overruns, or EBITDA weakness), leverage ratios rise. Credit spread widens; stock multiple compresses 10-15pp. This is real.
- Rating agency commentary. Any S&P/Moody's negative watch is watched by equity investors (signals refinancing risk).

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- Debt reduction progress. Analysts model: if FCF = €300m+/year, leverage falls to 2.0x by 2026, 1.8x by 2027. This allows dividend/buyback flexibility.
- Guidance on capex. If JC Decaux signals caution on screen installations (lower capex), it frees up cash for deleveraging but signals lower long-term growth.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Refinancing success. If 2028 bond is repriced at +150-200bp to Bunds (due to macro/credit stress), net debt interest rises ~€8-10m annually. This is a permanent EBITDA headwind unless offset by operational gains.

Macro Backdrops

| Scenario | Refinancing Cost | Leverage Outcome | Equity Impact |
|----------|-----------------|------------------|---------------|
| Low rates (2.0-2.5%) | Favorable | Rapid deleveraging | +5-10pp multiple |
| Rising rates (3.5-4.5%) | Headwind | Slower deleveraging | Neutral/flat |
| Recession (EBITDA -10%) | Significant headwind | Leverage 2.7-2.8x | -10-15pp multiple |

Key: Not the absolute level of rates, but the change in trajectory. If rates are falling (Gilts, Bunds), JC Decaux gains optionality. Rising rates = lower optionality, tighter covenant headroom.

8. OPERATING LEVERAGE & MARGIN EXPANSION [KEY DRIVER]

Classification: Key Driver | Volatility: Persistent (structural) | Duration: 2-5 years | Conviction: High
Color: GREEN (2025 margin +150bp; target of >20% operating margin achieved early)

Margin Trajectory

JC Decaux's operating margin (EBIT / revenue):

| Year | Operating Margin | EBIT (€m) | Note |
|------|-----------------|-----------|------|
| 2022 | 17.2% | €615m | Post-COVID normalization |
| 2023 | 19.3% | €700m | DOOH lift begins |
| 2024 | 19.5% | €768m | Consistent |
| 2025 | 20.9% | €831m | +150bp (target hit early) |

Management target: >20% operating margin by 2026. Achieved in 2025, one year ahead. This is a material win.

### What Drives Margin Expansion?

1. Leverage from digital: DOOH revenue grows at 20% margin vs. static at 14-15%. Each 1pp of revenue mix shift from static to DOOH = +1bp group margin.
2. Operating deleverage from growth: Central costs (HQ, finance, legal) are semi-fixed. If revenue grows 8% and costs grow 2-3%, margin expands.
3. Programmatic take-rates: SSP revenue is near 100% incremental margin (minimal COGS). Scaling VIOOH from 5% to 10% of digital is +5-10bp to group margin.
4. Labor efficiency: Remote work and outsourcing of back-office tasks (accounting, HR) lower cost base, though offset by tech investment.

What Moves Share Price

Short-term (1-2 quarters):
- Quarterly operating margin tracking vs. guidance. Consensus is 20.3-20.8% for FY2026; a miss of >30bp triggers revisions.
- Gross margin by segment. If street furniture margin declines (price concessions) or transport margin compresses (CPM competition), it signals headwind.

Mid-term (2-4 quarters):
- "Margin bridge" disclosure by management. Smart analysts track: starting margin, organic growth, M&A, FX, costs, synergies. If cost growth outpaces revenue growth, margin expansion stalls. First sign of margin pressure: analyst asks question on "headcount" in Q&A.
- Capex intensity. High capex for screen installations can pressure near-term margin, but long-term returns are accretive. Analysts debate: is capex €350m or €400m? Each €10m in capex is -5-10bp to ROIC.

Long-term (1-3 years):
- Terminal operating margin. Mature platforms stabilize at 20-22% (JC Decaux target). If competition (Ströer, CCO) erodes pricing, terminal margin could fall to 18-19%, dragging valuation.

False Friend

"Cost synergy" from M&A or "centralization" initiatives. JC Decaux, Ströer, and CCO all claim cost-saving opportunities in finance, real estate, and tech teams. Reality:

  1. Synergies are often overstated and delayed (18-24 month implementation vs. 12-month guidance).

11. DIGITAL SCREEN REFRESH CYCLES & TECHNOLOGY CAPEX [TERTIARY DRIVER]

Classification: Tertiary Driver | Volatility: Lumpy, technical | Duration: 3-5 years | Conviction: Moderate
Color: YELLOW

Technology Capex Reality

DOOH screens require periodic replacement (7-10 year lifecycle). As installed base ages, refresh capex increases. This is:

1. Necessary for margin: Older LED screens consume more power, have higher failure rates, lower CPM capability (lower resolution, no audio/interactivity).
2. Timing is discretionary: Management can defer refresh capex in downturns (accept lower uptime/CPM).
3. Incremental investment: Technology upgrades (4K to 8K, AI-enabled screens for dynamic content) are optional, justified by CPM uplift.

2024-2025 context: Large cohort of screens installed in 2016-2018 are now entering refresh cycle. Management has guided £350-380m capex, implying modest incremental spend vs. historical. Some analysts believe this is optimistic; refresh capex could be €400-420m to maintain competitive quality.

What Moves Share Price

  • Quarterly capex intensity and explanations. If refresh capex overruns guidance, FCF contracts.
  • Asset writedowns. Old screens generating low CPM might be retired rather than refreshed, creating one-time charges.

False Friend: "Technology durability" commentary. Management will highlight "robust screen hardware" and "low maintenance costs." Reality is mixed; capex avoidance is always tempting, but leads to margin erosion 2-3 years out.

CONCLUSION

JC Decaux's share price is driven by a small number of large levers: advertising cyclicality, DOOH growth rate, concession renewal outcomes, and FX movements. These four drivers explain 70-80% of shareholder returns over any 12-24 month period.

The bifurcation of OOH (static declining, digital growing 15%+) creates a permanent re-rating opportunity if JC Decaux can sustain DOOH at 10%+ growth and push programmatic mix to 15-20% of digital. This scenario would warrant 15-16x EV/EBITDA and +20% upside from current levels.

However, 2025 marks a deceleration inflection: overall organic growth fell from 9.7% (2024) to 1.8% (2025), even as DOOH remained at +10%. This slowdown in static formats is the canary in the coal mine. If overall growth stabilizes at 2-3%, long-term EBITDA growth is capped at 3-5%, and JC Decaux re-rates to 11-13x EV/EBITDA (utility-like).

The key question for 2026: Does JC Decaux return to 4-6% organic growth (soft landing, DOOH stabilization), or does macro deteriorate further, triggering 0-2% growth and multiple compression? Management's Q1 2026 guidance (expecting >5% organic growth) will be the litmus test.

Investors should focus on quarterly organic growth rates, DOOH segment momentum, and concession renewal outcomes. Everything else is noise or tertiary.
