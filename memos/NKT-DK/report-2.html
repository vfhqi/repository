1. Company Description

  • #I NKT designs, manufactures, installs, and services power cables up to 525 kV HVDC submarine cables. Full value chain: extrusion, testing, NKT Victoria cable-laying vessel, offshore/onshore installation, lifecycle maintenance. ~5,500 employees. Karlskrona (Sweden), Cologne (Germany), Esposende (Portugal). [C]
  • #I Three business lines (renamed 1 Jan 2026): Transmission (HV, 19.1% EBITDA margin 2025); Distribution (MV, 10.5% margin); Grid Solutions & Accessories (HVAC onshore + accessories/maintenance, ~13% margin est.). [C+AS]
  • #I Revenue model: Transmission on long-term fixed-price contracts with metal pass-through; Distribution on shorter-cycle spot pricing; GSA on SLA-based recurring revenue. Back-to-back copper hedging. [C+AS]
  • #I 2025 group revenue EUR 2,722m SMP. Market cap ~DKK 42bn (~EUR 5.6bn). Net cash EUR 963m. [C]
  • #I Asset-light JV with Walsin Lihwa (Taiwan) — first offshore cable factory in Taiwan. Paid technical consultancy with no balance sheet risk. [AS]

Bottom Line

NKT is experiencing fundamental positive change on nearly every dimension. The transformation from conglomerate to pure-play is complete. The demand backdrop is structural, policy-mandated, and supported by a pricing inflection embedded in the EUR 10.2bn backlog. The competitive moat is wide. The financial trajectory points toward a near-tripling of EBITDA by 2030.

Key ESA questions: (1) Independently verify Karlskrona production timeline for 2027. (2) Normalised post-ramp margin profile — is 24% group EBITDA margin achievable? (3) EV/backlog comparison to Prysmian and Nexans. (4) Bear case if European grid investment disappoints 30% or Asian capacity arrives faster. (5) Does 2026 transitional year create a durable entry window?

Recommendation: Progress to ESA. Binary risk is Karlskrona execution.
