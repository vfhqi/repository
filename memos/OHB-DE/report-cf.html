Plausible problems to monitor 3 findings

1.
Volatile cash conversion relative to peers: OHB's project-driven cash flow profile significantly lags the more stable cash generation characteristics of larger defense primes , as evidenced by the deeply negative Q1 2026 free cash flow of -€119.3m, representing a further deterioration from -€106.5m in Q1 2025. Institutional project milestone payments create massive intra-year cash swings that compound working capital management challenges
2.
Elevated employee turnover pressuring retention: Staff turnover reached 11.9% in 2025, prompting management to focus heavily on retention and employer branding initiatives, though peer context remains limited for definitive comparison . This elevated churn threatens institutional memory retention during the critical scaling phase, particularly as the company aggressively expanded its workforce to 3,974 employees with a 13% year-over-year increase in newly hired engineers
3.
Supply chain bottlenecks constraining sector execution: Global supply chain disruptions continue causing project delays and cost overruns across the European aerospace sector, while lower-tier suppliers struggle with financial resources and capacity constraints . Propellant and PCB shortages specifically constrain satellite hardware production across the continent , threatening OHB's ability to execute its record €3.4bn order backlog on schedule. Persistent negative free cash flow tracking metric weakness: The company's inability to generate positive quarterly free cash flow during peak operational scaling phases requires diligent monitoring against structural working capital traps , as the cash burn is structurally driven by massive upfront working capital requirements for new constellation contracts. Competitive consolidation risk from mega-merger formation: The Airbus, Thales, and Leonardo merger into a €6.5bn revenue entity through "Project Bromo" dramatically alters the European oligopoly landscape , though OHB could paradoxically benefit as institutional customers actively seek to preserve supplier diversity . This dynamic creates uncertainty around OHB's ability to compete for future mega-constellation tenders against significantly larger, better-capitalized competitors
