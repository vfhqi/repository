Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Instalco presents a compelling operational turnaround story led by its founder to restore margins to 8.0% amid a recovering Nordic technical installation market.
1.
Highly decentralized model: Instalco operates as a highly decentralized roll-up of over 150 local technical installation units across Sweden, Norway, and Finland. These units maintain their local brands, management teams, and client relationships while leveraging the central group's purchasing power, financial administration, and best-practice sharing networks
2.
Compelling investment thesis: The core investment thesis rests on the business returning to profitable organic growth and accretive acquisitions as founder Per Sjöstrand executes the 'Instalco 2.0' efficiency program. This plan aims to leverage structural drivers such as building electrification and energy-efficiency renovations to restore EBITA margins to 8.0%
3.
Critical margin swing: The single most important swing factor for earnings is the successful recovery of operating margins in the Swedish segment, which historically generates the group's highest margins but recently faced regional cost pressures
4.
Softening historical performance: Financial results over the past few years reflect cyclical construction headwinds, with 2025 net sales declining by 0.7% to SEK 13,598 million and EBITA margin falling to 5.9% compared to 8.4% in 2021
5.
Rebounding backlog momentum: A positive trend shift is underway as Q1 2026 organic sales grew by 4.9% and the order backlog surged by 15.0% to SEK 10,375 million, which indicates a strong book-to-bill ratio and supports a return to organic growth in 2026
6.
Elevated financial leverage: The primary near-term risk is the company's net debt to EBITDA ratio of 2.8x at the end of 2025, which remains above its 2.5x financial target. This leverage has constrained cash flow deployment and limited the near-term acquisition pace
7.
Compelling investment verdict: A closer look is highly warranted because the stock trades at near-trough multiples of 12.7x 2026e EV/EBITA, leaving substantial upside as the founder's margin turnaround gains traction. This is an extraordinary opportunity to acquire a high-quality cash-generative business at cyclical low points

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Instalco provides low-risk, highly specialized technical installation services through a decentralized model that maintains entrepreneurship and drives strong local customer relationships.

Segment and Discipline Breakdown

1.
Highly diversified segments: Net sales are well-distributed across three country-based operating segments, with Sweden contributing 71% of net sales during the 2025 fiscal year, Norway representing 16%, and Finland at 13%
2.
Balanced discipline mix: The company provides system design, project planning, installation, and service across multiple disciplines, led by electrical installations at 33% of 2025 sales, heating & plumbing at 28%, industrial solutions at 19%, ventilation at 15%, and technical consulting at 5%
3.
Resilient service revenues: Resilient service, maintenance, and ongoing repair assignments represent a growing and highly stable source of income, accounting for 37% of 2025 net sales, while renovations represent 33% and new construction projects make up 30%
4.
Strategic consulting integration: Technical consulting services under the Intec brand represent 5% of net sales and provide early-stage project planning that acts as a valuable funnel to secure multidisciplinary installation contracts

Business Model and Economics

5.
Trough-mitigated project selection: To minimize execution risk, the subsidiaries focus almost exclusively on mid-sized projects between SEK 1 million and SEK 75 million, avoiding high-risk, low-margin mega-projects
6.
Lean corporate overhead: A streamlined central organization of fewer than 30 employees handles accounting, acquisitions, and communication, ensuring that over 99% of the workforce is deployed productively at the local subsidiary level
7.
Contract-driven payment terms: Approximately 57% of sales are generated through cost-plus contracts and other flexible forms of remuneration, which protects margins from material inflation, while fixed-price projects represent 43% of sales
8.
Fragmented market consolidated: The Nordic technical installation market is valued at SEK 214 billion, where Instalco maintains a 6.4% market share in a highly fragmented competitive landscape characterized by tens of thousands of small operators

Customers and Competitive Advantage

9.
Low customer concentration: The company maintains a diversified customer base of approximately 2,000 clients, with construction companies accounting for 48% of sales, while the five largest customers represent only 14% of 2025 revenue
10.
Durable switching barriers: High local customer switching costs are driven by deep long-term relationships, short lead times, and the unique ability of local subsidiaries to execute joint multidisciplinary projects through sister units
11.
Favourable purchase power: A central purchasing function negotiates group-wide supplier agreements, providing small acquired units with substantial scale-related volume discounts that they could not access independently
12.
Other side of the story: A prolonged residential construction downturn can drive larger, capital-heavy contractors like Bravida or Assemblin Caverion to bid aggressively on smaller mid-market projects, intensifying local price competition and eroding margins.

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Recent financial performance reflects cyclical margin compression from the Nordic construction downturn, but Q1 2026 marks a clear inflection point in sales, margins, and order backlog.

Growth and Margin Performance

1.
Decelerating organic growth: Net sales growth declined from 36.0% in 2022 (net sales of SEK 12,063 million) to -0.7% in 2025 (net sales of SEK 13,598 million), reflecting severe cyclical real estate headwinds. Organic growth went from 7.9% in 2022 to -0.3% in 2025
2.
Compressed operating margins: EBITDA margin contracted from 10.3% in 2021 (EBITDA of SEK 920 million) to 8.9% in 2025 (EBITDA of SEK 1,209 million), while EBITA margin dropped from 8.4% to 5.9%. This was driven by inflation and capacity underutilization in southern Sweden
3.
Fading per-share earnings: Earnings per share declined from SEK 2.10 in 2021 and SEK 2.31 in 2023 to SEK 1.28 in 2025, driven by the combination of margin pressure and rising net financial interest expenses of SEK 145 million

Cash Flow and Capital Returns

4.
Robust cash conversion: Robust cash flow from operating activities reached SEK 1,010 million in 2025, up from SEK 946 million in 2024, representing an outstanding 108.1% cash conversion rate in 2025
5.
Fading capital returns: Return on capital employed fell from a peak of 18.2% in 2021 to 9.6% in 2025, though this still compares favorably with the broader Nordic small-cap construction services index
6.
Elevated debt leverage: Net debt to EBITDA rose sequentially from 1.8x in 2021 to 2.8x in 2025 (representing net interest-bearing debt of SEK 3,444 million), driven by sequential earn-out settlements of SEK 160 million

Recent Performance and Outlook

7.
Favourable Q1 acceleration: Q1 2026 results demonstrated a strong inflection point, with revenue growing 4.4% to SEK 3,438 million and organic sales growth rebounded to 4.9% organically. EBITA rose to SEK 201 million, with margins expanding to 5.8%
8.
Deleveraging balance sheet: Sequential deleveraging was visible in Q1 2026, as net debt to EBITDA fell from 2.8x at the end of 2025 to 2.6x, driven by strong operational cash inflows
9.
Transparent guidance culture: Management's guidance history has been honest and conservative, as demonstrated in 2025 when they deliberately cut dividends to SEK 0.50 per share to accelerate balance sheet deleveraging
10.
Other side of the story: While cash flow remains strong, any unexpected rise in labor costs or raw material prices could trap Swedish segment EBITA margins near the 5.0% level seen in Q1 2026, preventing a recovery to the historical 8.0% peak.

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is exceptionally strong, driven by the founder's return to execute a margin turnaround alongside robust structural demand for energy-efficient technical installations.

Operational Turnaround Drivers

1.
Favourable founder-led turnaround: The return of founder Per Sjöstrand as CEO is the primary catalyst to execute the highly anticipated efficiency program called Instalco 2.0. This program is designed to restore group EBITA margins back to the long-term target of 8.0% from the depressed level of 5.9% reported in 2025
2.
Clear country-level accountability: The implementation of a new country-based organization as of January 2026 establishes Sweden, Norway, and Finland as distinct divisions to eliminate unprofitable project deviations and optimize local cost structures
3.
Strong organic pipeline: Greenfield operations like Inmatiq building automation and Intec technical consulting provide high-margin growth avenues that expand the group's multidisciplinary capability as the Nordic construction sector stabilizes

Structural and Market Drivers

4.
Robust energy-efficiency demand: European climate regulations, such as the Energy Performance of Buildings Directive, create permanent, regulatory-driven demand for energy-saving plumbing, lighting, and HVAC installations
5.
Resilient public volumes: Strong structural demand from public sector investments in schools, hospitals, correctional facilities, and defense installations provides highly visible, stable volumes that cushion private-sector cyclical headwinds
6.
Re-accelerating M&A flywheel: Deleveraging net debt to EBITDA to 2.6x in Q1 2026 unlocks the financial flexibility to resume accretive, small-scale acquisitions, which historically drove the company's 30.0% EPS compound growth

Geographic Expansion and Outlook

7.
Strategic European entry: The structured option-based acquisition of Germany's Fabri AG provides a highly capital-efficient platform to scale the decentralized roll-up model outside the core Nordic market
8.
Compound driver probability: While individual structural drivers are independent, the overall exceptional investment opportunity relies on the compound probability of a domestic real estate recovery and the successful integration of Germany's Fabri in the second half of 2026
9.
Other side of the story: If the implementation of 'Instalco 2.0' central controls results in a loss of entrepreneurial commitment among local subsidiary managers, it could lead to higher employee turnover and lower project execution quality.

Section 6 – Valuation 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Instalco shares trade at highly attractive, trough-like multiples that represent a substantial discount to historic averages and European peers, presenting significant re-rating potential.
1.
Undemanding forward multiples: Instalco trades at an attractive 12.7x 2026e EV/EBITA and 10.5x 2027e EV/EBITA, representing a substantial discount to its eight-year historical average multiple of 14.0x to 15.0x EV/EBITA
2.
Deep peer discount: The company trades at a 30% to 35% discount to European installation peers like Bilfinger, Burkhalter, and SPIE on a 2026e EV/EBITA basis, despite historical valuation premiums of 20%
3.
Strong cash yield: DNB Carnegie estimates that the stock trades at an attractive 8.0% lease-adjusted free-cash-flow yield, which highlights that the market is severely underpricing its cash-generative power
4.
Underpriced margin turnaround: The market appears to be pricing in a permanently depressed EBITA margin near 6.0%, failing to recognize that the founder's turnaround plan to achieve 8.0% margins implies a 15% upside to consensus expectations
5.
Other side of the story: Vinci Energies noted at its Capital Markets Day that European pure-play installation deals since 2018 averaged 11.0x EV/EBITDA, suggesting that if Nordic growth remains permanently low, a multiple of 10.0x EV/EBITDA is the absolute ceiling.

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk profile is dominated by project miscalculations and cyclical building downturns, but the decentralized model and high service exposure provide a robust buffer.

Structural and Operational Risks

1.
Severe project miscalculations: Fixed-price tender risk represents a major exposure, as these contracts account for 43% of sales and can result in severe margin erosion if labor or raw material costs escalate unexpectedly
2.
Damaging customer bankruptcies: Exposure to developer defaults represents a major operational risk, as highlighted by a large Swedish customer bankruptcy in 2025 that forced a substantial accounts receivable impairment
3.
Weak organic construction momentum: Sustained weakness in domestic building permits directly impacts new production sales, which account for 30% of group revenue, leading to intense regional price competition

Integration and Control Risks

4.
Erosion of entrepreneurship: A major false friend is the implementation of strict central control mechanisms under 'Instalco 2.0' which could lead to a loss of entrepreneurial commitment among subsidiary managing directors and dilute performance
5.
Key early-warning signpost: Investors must closely monitor the quarterly segment margin of Instalco Sweden, as any failure to improve sequentially in late 2026 would signal structural turnaround failure
6.
Other side of the story: Fears of high construction cyclicality are strongly overstated because approximately 70% of group sales are tied to non-discretionary maintenance, renovation, and service agreements that property owners cannot easily defer.

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. The return of founder Per Sjöstrand as permanent CEO has restored stability and introduced an aggressive, margin-focused culture to drive the next phase of profitable growth.
1.
Permanent leadership restoration: Following the departure of Robin Boheman in July 2025, founder Per Sjöstrand stepped in as acting CEO and was officially appointed permanent President and CEO on March 19, 2026
2.
Favourable executive restructuring: Sjöstrand immediately introduced country-specific management units for Sweden, Norway, and Finland, establishing dedicated Country Managers and COO roles to enhance operational execution
3.
Strong insider alignment: To align executive incentives with the margin-raising objectives, the board authorized a new long-term incentive program comprising 1,950,000 warrants for senior management and key subsidiary directors
4.
Stable corporate governance: Johnny Alvarsson succeeded Sjöstrand as Chairman of the Board, maintaining strong governance continuity while Sjöstrand focuses fully on the operational rollout of the 'Instalco 2.0' improvement plan

  1. Instalco warrants immediate, deep-dive research because the combination of a founder-led turnaround, depressed valuation, and structural green transition drivers presents an exceptional IRR opportunity.
1.
Outstanding turnaround case: The strongest argument for investing is the predictable earnings trajectory supported by a robust order backlog of SEK 10,375 million and the founder's capability to restore EBITA margins to 8.0%
2.
Other side of the story: The strongest argument against the case is that persistent wage inflation and labor shortages in Sweden could permanently cap group EBITA margins at 6.0% to 6.5%, preventing multiple expansion.
3.
Immediate action next: Investors must systematically verify the Swedish segment's quarterly margin development to confirm that the 'Instalco 2.0' cost-saving initiatives are successfully offsetting regional pricing pressures
4.
Strategic M&A evaluation: Conduct an immediate, deep-dive research audit of Germany's Fabri AG to determine the profitability and scale viability of Instalco's multi-phase, option-structured continental European expansion strategy
