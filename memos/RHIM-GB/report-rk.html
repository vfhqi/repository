EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

This report examines RHI Magnesita (RHIM.GB) across three analytical lenses – bear-case credibility, pre-mortem failure analysis, and foreign exchange exposure – benchmarked against key peers Vesuvius (VSVS) and Morgan Advanced Materials (MGAM). The synthesised verdict is one of medium-plausibility bear risk, elevated false-friend density, and a net FX headwind of €35 million over the forward N12-24M horizon that materially compounds structural operational pressures.

On bear-case credibility, the overall verdict is medium plausibility. RHI Magnesita has no history of published short-seller attacks from firms such as ShadowFall or Hindenburg, and highly depressed valuation multiples of 6.3x P/E protect the company from traditional valuation-based short theses. Nevertheless, the factual evidence underpinning the bear case is credible and coherent across several dimensions. RHI Magnesita's year-end leverage of 2.9x is a verifiable, factual risk that restricts near-term cash-accretive M&A activity, management's reliance on factoring €254 million of accounts receivable represents a factual balance sheet cash-management reality, and the €98 million gap between GAAP and Adjusted EBITA is a matter of factual public disclosure. Operating only 27 of 52 tunnel kilns in mid-2025 provides indisputable factual evidence of severe volume contraction. Relative to peers, RHI Magnesita's leverage of 2.9x is substantially worse than Vesuvius, which has historically maintained leverage around 1.1x and while Morgan Advanced Materials uses £37.2 million in supplier financing, RHI Magnesita's factoring is significantly larger at €254 million. Where the bear case weakens is in its more speculative claims: allegations of structural market-share decay in India and the assumption that Resco integration synergies will permanently fail to materialise remain opinion-based and lack comprehensive empirical validation.
