§2 — Key Driver: Organic Volume Recovery (D1)

  • Driver definition: Azelis returns to positive organic volume growth (LFL, excluding M&A and FX) in H2 2025, building to 4-6% organic growth by 2026 — consistent with pre-destocking growth rates [#A]

  • Magnitude required: Organic volumes declined approximately 7-10% cumulatively over 2023-24 (management commentary and public disclosures suggest overall revenue decline was price-mix + volume, with volumes the primary drag); recovery to flat-to-positive by H2 2025 and +4-6% by full-year 2026 is the implied case trajectory [#A]

  • Baseline: As of FY2024 results (published early 2025), Azelis reported organic growth returning modestly positive in H2 2024 vs deeply negative H1 2024, suggesting trough was approximately Q2-Q3 2024 [#A]

  • Target state: Full-year 2025 organic growth in the +3-4% range; 2026 at +5-6%, consistent with specialty chemicals distributor peer growth normalisation after destocking cycles [#A]

  • Required trajectory: Approximate quarterly step-up from ~0% LFL (Q3-Q4 2024) to +2-3% (Q1-Q2 2025) to +4-6% (H2 2025) — requiring acceleration but not an exceptional ramp by sector standards [#A]

Bull case:

  • Bull — historical: Azelis delivered 8-12% organic growth in 2021-22 (post-COVID restocking supercycle); the business has demonstrated volume elasticity in recovery environments [#A]

  • Bull — recent: H2 2024 organic growth reportedly turned mildly positive; order books showed improvement across EMEA personal care and food segments per management commentary at FY2024 results [#A]

  • Bull — sector tailwind: Specialty chemicals destocking cycles historically last 4-6 quarters; the 2022-23 destocking was severe but not unprecedented; peer distributors (IMCD, Brenntag — publicly named) showed volume recovery beginning H2 2024 [#A]

  • Bull — end-market mix: ~60% of Azelis revenue is in life-sciences and food/personal care — structurally less cyclical than industrials; these segments typically recover faster from inventory normalisation than industrial chemicals [#A]

  • Bull — geographic diversification: Americas and Asia-Pacific are earlier in destocking cycle resolution than EMEA; multi-region exposure smooths recovery trajectory [#A]

  • Bull — mgmt: Management guided for "gradual volume recovery through 2025" at FY2024 results, with explicit commentary on order patterns improving in Q4 2024; confidence language was cautiously optimistic vs 2023's more defensive tone [#A]

  • Bear — historical drag: In the 2015-16 specialty chemical downturn, similar distributors took 6-8 quarters to return to pre-downturn volume levels; 2022-24 destocking was deeper and broader, suggesting the upper end of historical recovery timelines may apply [#A]

  • Bear — SS skeptic: Some public broker commentary (Berenberg noted in press coverage) flagged that Azelis's EMEA volume recovery was lagging IMCD's in H1 2024, suggesting possible customer mix or end-market mix disadvantage in the near term [#A]

Forward trajectory:

  • Acceleration needed: Moderate — recent trend (mild positive in H2 2024) needs to be sustained and modestly accelerated; not a V-shaped ramp assumption [#J]
