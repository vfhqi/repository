EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Corporate Structure and Operating Model

Bayer AG operates as a global life science conglomerate divided into three operational divisions–Crop Science (€21.62bn in FY2025 sales, representing 47.44% of Group revenues), Pharmaceuticals (€18.13bn in sales, accounting for 39.78% of Group revenues), and Consumer Health (€5.80bn in sales, or 12.73% of total Group sales)–managed under a single strategic holding structure . The market applies a persistent 10% to 15% conglomerate discount to Bayer's sum-of-the-parts valuation because the hybrid life sciences model exhibits no logical operational synergies between crop protection and healthcare . Under CEO Bill Anderson's Dynamic Shared Ownership (DSO) operating model, Bayer has cut management positions by roughly two-thirds, reducing management layers by half and targeting €2.0bn in sustainable annual organizational savings by the end of 2026 . On July 1, 2026, Bayer consolidated its volatile U.S. glyphosate operations into a distinct, wholly owned subsidiary named Ruveon LLC based in St. Louis, Missouri, solely responsible for U.S. glyphosate pricing and goto-market strategies, appointing Alfonso Alba Ordóñez, a 30-year agricultural executive, as Chief Executive Officer . 1
