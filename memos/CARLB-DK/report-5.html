2. COMPANY DESCRIPTION AND CONTEXT

Overall rating: GREEN — Carlsberg is a high-quality franchise facing a cyclical headwind, not a structural impairment

  • Carlsberg Group: world's third-largest brewer by volume, headquartered in Copenhagen. Core brands: Carlsberg, Tuborg, 1664 Blanc, Somersby, Birra Moretti (licensed), and since January 2025 Britvic's UK/Ireland portfolio (Robinsons, J2O, Fruit Shoot, Tango, R. White's). Soft drinks now ~30% of total company volume. #I

  • Britvic acquisition (completed January 2025, ~GBP 3.3bn) transformed Carlsberg into a beverages company with genuine UK/Ireland soft drinks scale. Leverage increased to 3.4x NIBD/EBITDA at completion, now 3.28x end-FY 2025. #I

  • Financial scale: FY 2025 reported revenue DKK 89.1bn (+18.8% YoY, majority Britvic consolidation). Organic operating profit growth 5% in FY 2025, at the top of 3–5% guidance range. FY 2025 MPM operating profit: DKK 13,996m. EBITDA: DKK 18,784m. #I

  • Britvic's consolidation structurally diluted headline gross margin by 60bps to 45.2% (Per [AS]/INTRM) due to the inherently lower-margin profile of licensed soft drinks — despite underlying gross profit per hl continuing to improve driven by supply chain efficiencies and price/mix in the core beer portfolio. #A

  • L3Y revenue and profit trajectory:

2022 70.3 ~11.5 16.3%
2023 73.6 ~11.1 15.1%

  • The 2022 peak margin at 16.3% was eroded by input cost inflation, volume weakness (post-Russia exit) and portfolio restructuring. The 2025 recovery to 15.7% was Britvic-assisted. Underlying beer business margins remain below the 2022 peak. #A

  • Key strategic context — three pillars under CEO JAA: (1) Grow premiums and alcohol-free; (2) Expand soft drinks through composite selling; (3) Deliver Britvic synergies and deleverage. India IPO is a capital liberation lever nested under pillar 3. #I

  • Peer context: Carlsberg trades at ~15x forward P/E vs Heineken ~17-18x. Discount reflects leverage and lower Asia profit quality. #I

  • 2027 partial hedging already underway: Per [AS]/Transcript Carlsberg "has begun layering in hedges for 2027 across critical exposures including barley, sugar, energy and aluminium, taking an opportunistic approach to lock in rates during market dips."   • 2027 residual risk: Management conceded explicitly that if current elevated spot prices for energy and oil-linked commodities persist into 2027, Carlsberg will face a "mathematical cost increase" once existing favourable hedges expire (Per [AS]/Transcript).
