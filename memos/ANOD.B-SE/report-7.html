The share price over a 24-month horizon will be determined primarily by whether the company can translate a cyclical recovery in PLM/CAD software demand into sustained organic growth while simultaneously executing margin improvement — against a softening public-sector budget backdrop.

§2 — Key Driver #1: Design Management Organic Growth Recovery

  • J-front verdict: Design Management is the dominant EPS fulcrum — approximately 55-60% of the 24M upside case — but organic re-acceleration from the H2-2025 deceleration requires macro tailwinds that are currently uncertain [#J]

Definition:
- Driver definition: Design Management (Addnode's largest segment by revenue) supplies PLM, CAD, and BIM software to engineering, construction, and infrastructure customers primarily in the Nordics and DACH; organic revenue growth in this segment drives group EPS disproportionately [#A]
- Mechanism to EPS: Higher organic revenue at this segment's above-group-average EBIT margins (historically ~12-15% segment EBIT vs group ~9-11%) creates operating leverage that flows through to EPS at ~1.5-2× the revenue growth rate [#A]
- Mechanism to SP: Organic growth re-acceleration signals that the SaaS transition is not disrupting volume — validating the investment thesis and warranting multiple expansion from ~14× to ~16-18× EBIT [#J]

Company guidance and management view:
- Company guide: Management has not provided explicit organic growth targets for 2026; the company characterises Design Management trajectory as "resilient demand" with selective softness in project-dependent verticals [#A]
- Management commentary: Q4-2025 earnings commentary cited delayed project sign-offs in AECO (architecture, engineering, construction) verticals while affirming software subscription renewal strength — a split narrative that sell-side has accepted without adequate challenge [#J]
- Track record: ANOD.B-SE historically delivered organic growth of 6-10% in Design Management over 2020-2024; H2-2025 showed deceleration to an estimated 3-5% range based on segment disclosure [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: Management's "resilience" framing appears optimistic relative to disclosed renewal commentary — project-dependent revenue is not immaterial to this segment and delayed decisions compress near-term growth [#J]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: No proprietary SS database accessible under [C] source; public commentary (press summaries, company-quoted analyst consensus) implies 2026 group revenue growth consensus of approximately 7-10%, with Design Management as the assumed primary contributor [#A]
- Consensus changes: Sell-side estimates appear to have been modestly trimmed in Q1-2026 relative to Q3-2025 peaks — consistent with the broader Nordic IT services derating seen in the sector [#A]
- Estimated EPS impact: A 1-percentage-point improvement in Design Management organic growth rate (holding margins flat) translates to approximately 2-3% group EPS uplift at current revenue mix — based on segment revenue representing ~40-45% of group total [#A]

Recent performance and baseline:
- Recent delivery: Design Management organic growth decelerated in H2-2025 — estimated 3-5% vs 8-10% in H1-2024 — driven by delayed AECO project decisions and early-stage SaaS migration friction [#A]
- L3Y baseline: 2022-2024 average organic growth in Design Management approximately 7-8% annually; the H2-2025 deceleration is the first sustained moderation since 2020 [#A]
- Peer benchmark: No cohort peer context available (D-BATCH-25 nil); Nordic IT services sector broadly showed 4-6% organic growth deceleration in H2-2025 per public reporting aggregates — ANOD.B-SE's deceleration is consistent with, not worse than, sector trend [#A]
- Early warning signals: Delayed project sign-offs in AECO are a leading indicator of further softness — construction and infrastructure procurement cycles lag monetary policy by 12-18 months [#J]

Macro and end-market context:
- Macro tailwind: European infrastructure digitisation (BIM mandates, digital twin adoption) provides structural demand for PLM/CAD software — a genuine multi-year tailwind supporting the long-term case [#J]
- Macro headwind: Nordic/EU public-sector budget austerity in 2025-2026 is compressing capital project timelines — directly reducing near-term demand for project-based Design Management services [#J]
- Competitive dynamics: Autodesk, Nemetschek, and Trimble are global PLM/CAD competitors; ANOD.B-SE's position as a value-added reseller and integrator (not a pure ISV) means it benefits from vendor software transitions but faces margin pressure when vendors push direct-to-customer [#A]
- Visibility: Moderate — subscription renewals are visible 3-6 months ahead; project-dependent revenues are not, and this is the volatile component in the current environment [#A]

Plausibility assessment:
- Key assumptions: Re-acceleration to 6-8% organic growth requires: (1) AECO project decisions to resume in H2-2026; (2) SaaS transition renewal rates to hold above 90%; (3) no further public-sector budget cuts in key Nordic markets [#A]
- Magnitude if delivered: Re-acceleration to 8% organic growth + operating leverage = approximately 10-15% group EPS uplift in 2027E vs 2026E consensus [#A]
- What would derail it: Extended delay in infrastructure project sanctioning (most likely via continued Nordic fiscal tightening) or Autodesk/Trimble direct-channel push that displaces ANOD.B-SE as preferred integrator [#J]

Bear case:
- Bear case: Design Management organic growth stabilises at 3-4% in 2026 — below the implied consensus of 7-10% — as AECO project delays persist through H1-2026 and SaaS migration creates transient revenue headwinds [#J]
- Miss scenario: 4-5% organic growth miss vs consensus 7-10% → group revenue misses by ~2-3% → operating deleverage compresses EBIT margin by 50-100bps → EPS ~8-12% below consensus [#A]
- Miss magnitude: A sustained miss on this driver alone is sufficient to move the stock -15% to -25% given current multiple pricing in a recovery trajectory [#J]
- Red flags: Delayed AECO decisions flagged in Q4-2025 call; no explicit guidance on recovery timeline — management cannot credibly commit to H2-2026 re-acceleration without visible order intake improvement [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: Design Management's growth narrative rests on structural BIM/digital twin tailwinds — but ANOD.B-SE's revenue mix still contains a material project-services component that is cyclical, not structural; the market may be pricing the structural story while the actual earnings near-term are driven by the cyclical component [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — re-acceleration is plausible but requires AECO project cycle resumption that is not yet visible in leading indicators

§3 — Key Driver #2: EBIT Margin Expansion via SaaS Mix-Shift

  • J-front verdict: SaaS mix-shift is the second load-bearing driver — structurally credible and in management's control at the margin level — but speed of expansion is constrained by transition friction and cost structure stickiness [#J]

Definition:
- Driver definition: As ANOD.B-SE migrates customers from perpetual licence/services to subscription SaaS, recurring revenue share rises, revenue visibility improves, and EBIT margins expand due to lower cost-to-serve on renewal cohorts vs new project delivery [#A]
- Mechanism to EPS: Higher recurring revenue share reduces EBIT volatility; at full SaaS run-rate, incremental revenue margins on renewals are significantly above the blended segment EBIT margin — driving EPS compounding [#A]
- Mechanism to SP: SaaS mix improvement drives multiple re-rating as the market assigns a higher EV/revenue multiple to recurring revenue — potentially lifting EBIT multiple from ~14× to ~16-18× [#J]

Company guidance and management view:
- Company guide: Management targets increasing recurring revenue share over the medium term — no explicit percentage target publicly disclosed; qualitative commentary references "accelerating subscription transition" across Design and Process Management [#A]
- Track record: Recurring revenue share has increased from an estimated 55-60% (2020-2021) to an estimated 65-70% range (2024-2025) based on segment commentary — directionally consistent but not transformatively fast [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: Management framing is constructive but the pace is slower than Nordic software peers that have completed SaaS migrations; ANOD.B-SE still carries meaningful services/project revenue that will not migrate to SaaS [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Public commentary implies sell-side models approximately 30-50bps of annual EBIT margin improvement from mix-shift — consistent with the company's historical delivery rate [#A]
- Estimated EPS impact: Each 100bps of EBIT margin improvement at group level translates to approximately 5-8% EPS uplift at current tax and financing structure — based on group EBIT being ~70-75% of PBT [#A]
- Consensus changes: No evidence of material consensus upgrade on margin trajectory in L12M; stable/modest improvement priced in [#A]

Recent performance and baseline:
- Recent delivery: Group EBIT margin has been in the 9-11% corridor since 2021 — below the 12-15% levels that full SaaS compounder peers achieve — suggesting either structural cost headwinds or services revenue drag [#A]
- L3Y baseline: Margin improvement has been approximately 20-30bps per year on average — linear but not accelerating [#A]
- Peer benchmark: Nordic software/IT services peers with comparable SaaS transition profiles (e.g., Fortnox, Visma-type entities, B2B software resellers) operate at 13-18% EBIT margins at mature SaaS mix; ANOD.B-SE's 9-11% corridor indicates meaningful runway but also structural cost drag [#A]

Macro and end-market context:
- Structural driver: SaaS transition is vendor-driven (Autodesk, Nemetschek, PTC transitioning to cloud/subscription) — ANOD.B-SE is carried by vendor transition, which is a durable multi-year tailwind [#J]
- Headwind: Services revenue (which has lower margins) does not migrate to SaaS — it remains as a structural drag on blended margins; ANOD.B-SE cannot eliminate services entirely without losing customer relationship depth [#A]
- Visibility: High on the recurring revenue cohort — ARR growth and renewal rates are visible and in management's control; the wild card is new project services revenue which is cyclical [#A]

Plausibility assessment:
- Key assumptions: (1) Vendor-driven SaaS transition continues at current pace; (2) ANOD.B-SE retains preferred-partner status with Autodesk/PTC/Nemetschek; (3) no step-change increase in personnel costs disrupts improvement trajectory [#A]
- Magnitude if delivered: If EBIT margins reach 12% from current ~10% over 24 months, EPS would expand approximately 15-20% on flat revenues — a material compounding effect [#A]
- Base case trajectory: 20-40bps per year is the base rate; 100bps per year would require acceleration above recent trend [#A]

Bear case:
- Bear case: Margin expansion stalls at ~10-10.5% if services revenue declines faster than SaaS grows (creating a revenue-mix headwind), or if personnel cost inflation in Nordic markets (historically tight labour) resumes [#J]
- Miss scenario: Flat EBIT margin at 10% vs 11-12% consensus expectation → ~8-12% EPS miss on the margin line alone [#A]
- Red flags: ANOD.B-SE has been in the 9-11% corridor for 4+ consecutive years — the absence of a breakout above 11% despite ongoing SaaS migration is a warning signal that structural cost drag may be more persistent than framed [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: ❌ The SaaS transition narrative has been management's primary margin story for 3+ years without a breakout above the 11% EBIT ceiling — this is either a structural constraint or a management execution problem, and the memo should not assume the next 24 months will differ without a visible catalyst for acceleration [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — margin direction is correct and structurally supported, but acceleration above the historical 20-40bps/year rate requires a catalyst not yet visible

§4 — Key Driver #3: M&A Cadence and Integration Quality

  • J-front verdict: M&A is a consistent capital-deployment mechanism for ANOD.B-SE but integration quality opacity and acquisition multiple inflation reduce IRR predictability — medium confidence driver with wide outcome distribution [#J]

Definition:
- Driver definition: ANOD.B-SE operates a programmatic M&A strategy, acquiring niche software and IT services businesses (typically sub-€30m EV) in its core verticals; acquisitions add revenue, and if integrated effectively, drive EPS accretion [#A]
- Mechanism to EPS: Acquired businesses contributing EBIT at above cost-of-acquisition multiples create EPS accretion; additionally, acquisition of software businesses with recurring revenue improves group ARR profile and supports the SaaS mix-shift driver [#J]
- Mechanism to SP: Consistent deal flow signals execution competence and TAM expansion optionality — Nordic small-cap acquirers with credible M&A programmes typically trade at a 10-20% valuation premium to organic-only peers [#A]

Company guidance and management view:
- Company guide: Management explicitly commits to ongoing M&A as a pillar of capital allocation — no explicit deal-count or volume target publicly stated [#A]
- Track record: ANOD.B-SE has completed 20+ acquisitions over 2015-2025 — approximately 1-3 per year — with disclosed acquisition prices suggesting EV/EBIT multiples in the 6-12× range on smaller targets [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: M&A track record is directionally positive but ANOD.B-SE has not disclosed integration KPIs or post-acquisition organic growth rates for acquired entities — integration quality is opaque and may be overstated in sell-side models [#J]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side models typically include a "bolt-on M&A contribution" of 3-6% per annum to group revenue — this is incremental to organic growth and represents approximately 2-4% annual EPS contribution [#A]
- Estimated EPS impact: If ANOD.B-SE completes 2× acquisitions per year at 8× EBIT with 5% EPS accretion each, annual M&A EPS contribution is approximately 8-10% — material but dependent on deal pipeline and financing cost [#A]

Recent performance and baseline:
- Recent delivery: ANOD.B-SE completed multiple acquisitions in 2023-2025 — public announcements confirm continued activity; aggregate acquired revenue in 2024 estimated at SEK 200-400m based on announced deal sizes [#A]
- L3Y baseline: M&A-driven revenue growth has averaged approximately 3-5% annually on top of organic growth — consistent with programmatic acquirer archetype [#A]
- Peer benchmark: Nordic programmatic acquirers (e.g., Lagercrantz, Indutrade as sector reference points) typically achieve 5-8% inorganic revenue growth annually with EBIT accretion; ANOD.B-SE's 3-5% is below this peer range [#A]

Macro and end-market context:
- Structural tailwind: European IT services sector fragmentation in PLM/CAD verticals provides ongoing acquisition pipeline — addressable universe of sub-scale niche software businesses in DACH and Nordics remains large [#A]
- Headwind: Rising acquisition multiples in Nordic software M&A over 2021-2024 compressed IRR on deals closed at cycle peak; deals closed in 2025-2026 at lower multiples (if the market has corrected) may show better IRR [#J]
- Financing cost: ANOD.B-SE uses a mix of cash and debt for M&A; rising interest rates (2022-2024) increased cost of acquisition financing, compressing accretion; current rate cycle (declining) is supportive of renewed M&A IRR [#J]

Plausibility assessment:
- Key assumptions: (1) Continued deal flow from ANOD.B-SE's existing vendor ecosystem and DACH/Nordic network; (2) integration of acquired businesses maintaining their pre-acquisition EBIT margins; (3) financing cost declining in line with current rate expectations [#A]
- Magnitude if delivered: M&A contributing 4-5% EPS annually for 2 years adds approximately 8-10% cumulative EPS to the 24M case — a meaningful but non-dominant driver [#A]

Bear case:
- Bear case: M&A pipeline dries up due to seller price expectations remaining elevated, or acquired businesses underperform post-integration due to key-person risk in small software companies [#J]
- Miss scenario: Zero meaningful M&A in 2026 → group revenue misses inorganic contribution assumption of ~3-5% → roughly 2-4% EPS miss on this driver alone [#A]
- Red flags: ANOD.B-SE does not disclose post-acquisition performance — the absence of disclosed integration KPIs is a governance concern that prevents external assessment of M&A quality [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The M&A track record is real but unaudited — without post-acquisition disclosure, sell-side models that assume 5-8% annual M&A EPS contribution are implicitly trusting management's deal selection without any independent verification mechanism [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — deal flow is historically consistent but integration opacity and acquisition-multiple normalisation uncertainty introduce wide outcome range
