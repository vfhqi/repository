§1 — Business Overview and Model

Core Platform Architecture

Boku operates a leading global payments platform connecting enterprise merchants with consumers through Local Payment Methods (LPMs). Through a single integration, Boku provides access to a network of 200+ payment methods across 60+ countries. The company acts as an agent between merchants and local payment rails, facilitating seamless transactions for billions of consumer accounts without relying on traditional credit card networks. [ARS, 17 Apr; Press Release, 17 Dec 25]

Identity division divested (2022): The company previously operated an Identity and Authentications division (acquired Danal Inc. in 2019 for ~$25m to build out identity and compliance services). The business failed to scale adequately and generated consistent losses over its three-year lifespan. Management divested the division to Twilio in early 2022 for ~$33 million to fully concentrate resources on the highly profitable core payments platform.

Product Divisions and the Shift to LPMs

Direct Carrier Billing : Legacy and foundational business — consumers charge digital purchases directly to their mobile phone bills. 170+ mobile network operator connections worldwide. Highly resilient: +9% revenue growth in FY2025. Accounted for ~55% of group revenues in FY2025 (down from 65% in FY2024). [ARS, 17 Mar]

Digital Wallets and Account-to-Account (A2A): Primary future engine. Revenue +67% in FY2025 to $43.5m — now 34% of revenue. Capitalizes on structural shift: LPMs projected to capture ~60% of global e-commerce transaction value by 2028. Deutsche Bank: LPMs overtook physical cards and cash in last decade, rising from 34% of global e-commerce value (2014) to 66% (2024). [ARS, 17 Apr; ARS, 17 Mar; Deutsche Bank Research, 15 Jul 25]

Bundling: Transforms payment channels into subscriber acquisition engines — integrates merchant subscription offerings into carrier/wallet plans as promotional add-ons. Revenue +71% YoY in FY2025 to $14.9m (11% of revenues). Now reported as a standalone revenue item due to increasing scale. [Transcript, 16 Oct 25; ARS, 17 Mar]

Revenue Sources and Take Rate Dynamics

Two models:
1. Settlement model: Boku assumes responsibility for collecting funds from local issuers and settling with merchants — higher percentage fee due to operational complexity
2. Transaction model: Boku provides technical connectivity only; merchant handles direct settlement

Blended take rate: 82bps in FY2025 (up from 80bps in FY2024) — positive mix-shift as LPM checkouts carry settlement component that structurally drives take rates upward.

Jefferies: LPM checkouts cover full payments value chain including cross-border FX conversion — supporting continuous take rate expansion. [Jefferies Research]

Deutsche Bank: A2A take rates typically 10-20bps — compression risk in medium term, but more than offset by massive TPV expansion and value-added services (cross-border currency conversion). [Deutsche Bank Research]

§4 — Industry Structure and Competitive Positioning

LPM Structural Shift

Global structural shift away from credit cards toward LPMs: LPMs rose from 34% share of global e-commerce (2014) to 66% (2024); projected ~60% of e-commerce transaction value by 2028. Boku positioned as central convening network abstracting complexity of 100s of localized payment rails. [Deutsche Bank Research, 15 Jul 25]

Competitive Moat

Jefferies: deep, customized API integrations create prohibitive switching costs — Boku designs bespoke APIs embedded in complex backend systems of major tech and entertainment companies. [Jefferies Research, 18 May]

Additional moat layers:
- Carrier network effects: single integration point gives subscription providers access to growing global ecosystem of mobile operators and wallet providers
- Mobile identity lock-in: phone number as automatic account identifier + payment credential → seamless "tap-once-to-register" → lower CAC + higher merchant LTV

Peer Comparison

Bango (BGO-GB): Primary global DCB competitor — Google Play's largest DCB partner, sole DCB provider for NTT DOCOMO and Amazon Japan. Strategic pivot underway toward Digital Vending Machine platform, concentrating on subscription bundling and partner marketing. [Expert Call, 11 Mar]

PPRO: Indirect B2B model — aggregator for PSPs (Stripe, PayPal). Does NOT own direct merchant relationships (key differentiation vs Boku). [Expert Call, 11 Mar]

Apple Pay / Google Pay: Dual dynamic — competition for consumer checkout preference in developed markets AND partnership as mega-tech wallets act as merchants on Boku's network in emerging markets where carrier billing serves unbanked populations. [Deutsche Bank Research, 6 Apr]

PayNearMe: US-focused; cash, ACH, cards, digital wallets for regulated industries (consumer lending, tolling, iGaming). Different vertical. [Deutsche Bank Research, 15 Jul 25]

Paidy (Japan): BNPL specialist acquired by PayPal for ~$2.7bn (2021). Significant regional alternative to carrier billing in Japan BNPL. [Jefferies Research, 10 Sep 25]
