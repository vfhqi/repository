Business model and segmental positioning

1.
Diversified but vulnerable portfolio: Alfen's three business segments exhibit divergent profitability profiles, with EV Charging generating premium 43.4% adjusted gross margins in FY25, Smart Grid Solutions delivering stable 22.4% margins, and Energy Storage facing project-dependent margin volatility
2.
Concentrated domestic geographic exposure: The company derives 92% of FY25 corporate revenues from its domestic Dutch market, with international revenue contribution declining from 42% in FY23 to 37% in FY25, creating severe single-country political and regulatory risk
3.
Lumpy project-based revenue model: The Energy Storage segment operates as a highly volatile, project-based business reliant on large-scale utility deals, while the EV Charging business functions as a short-cycle flow business with highly volatile order intake and zero backlog
4.
Severe macro-structural constraints: Labor shortages and permitting delays at Dutch grid operators structurally restrict the deployment of secondary substations, while grid congestion prevents private customers from obtaining larger or new grid connections, representing a binding physical bottleneck independent of Alfen's execution

5.

6.

Collapsing capital returns: Alfen delivered a ROIC of just 0.6% in FY25 down from 9.3% in FY24, and return on equity fell to 1.2% from 13.6%, comparing unfavorably to peer Arcadis's peak ROIC of 11.2%, positioning returns in the bottom decile of European industrial peers
8.
Resilient working capital discipline: Net working capital fell to €77.0m in FY25 from €92.0m in FY24, with inventories reduced by 20% to €81.7m, preventing liquidity distress and enabling positive free cash flow generation of €16m despite profit pressures
