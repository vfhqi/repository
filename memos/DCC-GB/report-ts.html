Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. DCC operates a defensive, cash-generative distribution model in fragmented off-grid energy markets, utilizing high tank-ownership rates to secure long-term, predictable customer relationships.

Segment breakdown and operations

1.
Core energy solutions: DCC Energy operates through Solutions (representing 76.00% of operating profit) and Mobility (24.00% of operating profit), focusing on off-grid fuels, LPG distribution, and fleet services
2.
Traditional product distribution: Within Solutions, Energy Products distributes liquid gas, fuels, biofuels, and renewable power to 1.50 million direct customers, serving intense, off-grid heating and industrial processes
3.
High-growth services area: Energy Services provides engineering-led decentralized solar PV installation, heat pumps, and energy management mainly to commercial and industrial customers
4.
Transport mobility network: The Mobility business operates approximately 1,200.00 retail service stations and fleet card operations across Europe, managing fuel sales and growing non-fuel convenience services
5.
Discontinued non-core segments: DCC has completed the sale of its Healthcare division for £1.05bn in September 2025 and its Technology Info Tech business in November 2025, leaving only specialist Pro Tech for sale in late 2026

Value chain and competitive advantages

6.
Strong value-chain position: DCC sits between major energy refiners (such as Equinor and ExxonMobil) and millions of fragmented downstream users, capturing stable, margin-based profits without taking upstream commodity exposure
7.
Durable competitive moat: DCC's primary moat is its ownership of downstream asset infrastructure, specifically owning the majority of liquid gas tanks installed on customer sites
8.
High switching costs: Due to tank ownership and physical integration into customer facilities, commercial and domestic customers exhibit average lifetimes of 10.00 to 20.00 years with highly recurring demand
9.
Highly consolidated local shares: DCC holds number one or number two positions in most of its local geographic markets, allowing it to leverage routing density and purchase scale against smaller competitors
10.
Consolidation runway potential: The total addressable off-grid liquid gas market is highly fragmented, with DCC holding just a 5.00% market share, providing a deep runway for bolt-on M&A. It has completed c.400.00 transactions historically
11.
Other side of the story: Structural volume headwinds from the energy transition could bite as traditional oil and heating products face long-term decay, which would permanently shrink DCC's legacy gross profit pool if biofuel and solar PV offsets fail to scale

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Standalone DCC faces mild-to-moderate risks from weather volatility and energy transition pace, but its strong balance sheet and robust fuel-margins act as powerful shields.

Operational and transition risks

1.
Unseasonal weather sensitivity: Milder winter temperatures represent a key risk, directly reducing volumes of heating oils and LPG sold, which previously impacted Solutions profits
2.
Transition capital destruction: Softening investment in the energy transition caused high-margin Energy Services profits to collapse 67.5% in FY26 to £15.70m, highlighting risks in green capex deployment
3.
Asset-collateral damage risk: Increased competition for green energy assets could inflate M&A multiples, causing standalone DCC to overpay for solar and heat pump installations and dilute group ROCE
4.
Translational FX volatility: Translating foreign denominated earnings creates constant EPS volatility, as 70% of group profits are generated outside of Sterling, primarily in Euros and USD
5.
False friend signal: Excellent cash flows in FY26 were boosted by £50.00m of temporary working capital benefits from high commodity prices, a positive signal that will reverse as prices normalize
6.
Early warning monitoring indicator: Investors must monitor quarterly volume growth in off-grid LPG, as persistent volume decay would indicate a structural breakdown in legacy energy assets
7.
Other side of the story: The risks are highly overstated because DCC operates on a unit-margin basis, allowing it to automatically pass through any wholesale commodity inflation directly to end-consumers
