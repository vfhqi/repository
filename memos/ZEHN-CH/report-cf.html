EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 15 findings

Materiality of Strategic Transformation

1.
Structural platform shift: Zehnder has completed a fundamental business transformation from a traditional radiator manufacturer to a ventilation-led indoor climate solutions provider, with high-margin ventilation solutions now generating 66% of group sales and 96% of EBIT in FY25
2.
Material capital deployment: Management committed EUR 180 million to ventilation-related M&A over the last five years, with Group Executive Committee compensation rising 26.5% to CHF 5.1 million in 2025, driven by fixed pay increases of 19.8% and long-term incentive expansion of 25.7%
3.
Secured family governance: The Zehnder family controls 51.6% of voting rights through Graneco AG under a shareholders' agreement binding until at least 31 December 2032, eliminating activist vulnerability and ensuring long-term strategic continuity through a planned generational transition on the Board of Directors
4.
Depressed return profile: Reported group ROIC collapsed to 1.7% in FY25 from a historical peak of 24.9%, reflecting the ongoing drag from legacy radiator underutilization and the capital-intensive nature of the ventilation transformation despite recent ROCE recovery to 22.1%

Top 5-8 Key Drivers Ranked by Impact and Plausibility

Driver 1 – UK Building Regulation Compliance (HIGH impact, HIGH plausibility): Legislative changes including Awaab's Law mandate social housing ventilation replacement to combat indoor mold and dampness across strict legally defined timeframes, providing +150 basis points estimated EPS CAGR with high plausibility due to active enforcement and structural insulation against weak private new-build markets.

Driver 2 – Siber Integration and European Spillover (HIGH impact, HIGH plausibility): The July 2024 acquisition of Spain-based Siber for EUR 86 million enabled geographic expansion into mid-priced apartment block ventilation solutions, rebranded as "Zehnder EVO" and successfully launched in Italy, the Baltics, and Belgium during H1 2025, delivering +200 basis points estimated EPS CAGR with integration tracking ahead of initial business plans.

Driver 3 – Radiator Capacity Restructuring (HIGH impact, MEDIUM plausibility): Deep structural consolidation including the complete closure of the Swiss Gränichen flat tube production facility in April 2025 and transition to single-shift operations at the German Lahr plant aims to lower the radiator break-even point to approximately CHF 270 million, providing +180 basis points estimated EPS CAGR, though plausibility is constrained by persistent demand erosion and down-trading to lower-priced models.

Driver 4 – North American Sales Expansion (MEDIUM impact, MEDIUM plausibility): State-by-state footprint expansion targeting high-growth premium residential markets in Washington, North California, and Texas through distribution partnerships has increased North American ventilation sales to 15.2% of segment sales, providing +100 basis points estimated EPS CAGR, though execution faces headwinds from cautious Class 1 buyer spending and gentle US housing market slowdown.

Driver 5 – Industrial Air Services and Clean Air Solutions Expansion (MEDIUM impact, HIGH plausibility): Expansion of the high-margin industrial filtration subscription service (Clean Air Solutions) across new offices in the Czech Republic, Spain, and Hungary provides stable, non-cyclical recurring revenues now representing approximately 10% of total ventilation sales with double-digit operating margins, delivering +80 basis points estimated EPS CAGR with limited required capital expenditure and steady customer acquisition momentum.

Most Material Risks and plausible problem to monitor

Risk 1 – Extreme profit reliance on ventilation: The company's 96% EBIT concentration in ventilation versus 66% of sales creates severe exposure to regulatory risks if UK building code mandates like Awaab's Law are delayed, watered down by regional governments, or face installation capacity constraints, potentially stranding high fixed ventilation capacity and risking capital impairment if the radiator segment requires further restructuring cash drain.

Risk 3 – Continuing radiator sales erosion: Legacy EMEA radiator sales declined 8% to EUR 216.4 million with segment EBIT margin of only 0.9%, and analysts note the path to double-digit group margins remains hampered by persistent radiator drag, with potential for further asset write-downs and restructuring charges that could unravel the ventilation segment's operating leverage if volume declines accelerate faster than cost-cutting measures.
