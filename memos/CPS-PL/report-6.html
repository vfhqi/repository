  • Consensus gross margin: Not widely disclosed on a standalone basis; implied from EBITDA and EBIT data: gross margin ~45-50% for the group (services-heavy, so content cost is the primary CoR driver) [#A]

  • Consensus EBIT margin: FY25 ~12-14%; FY26 ~14-16% (recovery assumed); FY27 ~15-17% [#A]

  • Consensus net margin: FY25 ~4-6% (high interest burden); FY26 ~5-8%; net margin is thin due to PLN ~1.5-2.0bn annual net-interest expense on the leveraged capital structure [#J]

  • Consensus EBITDA margin: FY25 ~27-28% (trimmed vs prior ~29-30%); FY26 ~28-32% (wide analyst range); FY27 ~30-33%; bull analysts assume margin recovery to pre-acquisition levels, which has not yet been demonstrated [#A]

  • Sub: Wide FY26 EBITDA margin range (28-32%) is the most concerning indicator in the entire consensus landscape — four percentage points of EBITDA margin on ~PLN 15bn revenue = ~PLN 600m EBITDA uncertainty, which is enormous relative to PLN 3.9-4.0bn EBITDA base

  • Margin trend assumed: Expanding (mildly) in FY26-27 across most analysts; premised on (a) content cost moderating after sports rights renewals, (b) energy cost pass-through catching up, (c) Polkomtel network synergies continuing to flow through [#J]

  • Productivity assumption: Modest; most analysts assume headcount flat to -1%; no analyst models a transformational cost reduction program [#I]

  • Pricing assumption: Inflation-linked price increases in mobile (+3-5%); limited in Pay-TV; energy margin recovery assumed but not quantified with high precision by most analysts [#A]

  • Mix assumption: Shift toward higher-margin mobile and energy, away from lower-margin Pay-TV hardware; this is directionally plausible but the quantum is debated [#J]

  • Operating leverage: Limited; fixed costs as % of revenue declining only slowly; 5G network investment actually increases fixed-cost base in FY25-26; operating leverage doesn't materialise meaningfully until FY27+ in most models [#A]

  • Fixed cost: Network opex (5G) and content (sports rights) are the two largest fixed cost vectors; both are inflation-exposed and both have surprised to the upside in L12M [#J]

  • One-off vs structural: FY24-25 margin pressure is partially one-off (sports rights super-cycle, energy cost spike); but the structural component — Pay-TV mix shift toward OTT with lower near-term ARPU — is persistent [#A]

  • Margin downgrade: [AS-Trigon DM] has made the most explicit margin downgrade in L12M (FY25 EBITDA margin cut from 30% to 27%); [AS-mBank Securities] followed with a smaller cut [#I]

  • Margin upgrade: [AS-Jefferies] maintained their FY26 margin outlook at ~32-33% — the only analyst not to trim; this is the primary source of PT outlier status [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The consensus margin recovery thesis (FY26: ~28-32% EBITDA) assumes content cost normalisation and 5G opex plateau; neither assumption has been validated by management guidance or industry data; the Polish sports rights market (particularly football) has historically exceeded renewal-cost expectations; if FY26 content costs come in 5-10% above model, EBITDA margin recovery is deferred to FY27-28, invalidating the near-term re-rating thesis for all but Jefferies' longer-horizon model [#J]

§7 — Narrative + Market Story

  • J-front verdict: The consensus narrative is transitioning from "convergence value creation" to "deleveraging patience required" — a less exciting but more honest framing, and one that suggests bulls are adjusting timeline expectations rather than thesis direction [#J]

  • Bull case: Core bull narrative: CPS-PL is a three-legged stool (mobile/telecoms, Pay-TV/OTT, energy) where the sum-of-parts is worth materially more than the current market cap; Polkomtel synergies (cost savings + cross-sell) plus Polsat Plus renewable growth provide non-linear upside; the stock is unloved because of leverage, but leverage is declining and the FCF engine can fund deleveraging while maintaining modest dividends [#J]

  • Sub: Secondary bull element: Polish consumer is relatively resilient; ARPU growth in bundled offers has held up better than feared given inflation

  • Sub: Tertiary bull element: convergence bundling (mobile + TV + broadband + energy under one contract) reduces churn and improves LTV; no pure-play Polish competitor offers the full bundle

  • Bear case: Hold-rated analysts' bear narrative: the balance sheet is the ceiling; at 3.5-3.8x net debt/EBITDA, any EBITDA miss triggers covenant anxiety and potentially dividend suspension; Pay-TV structural decline is not yet offset by OTT at scale; energy business is capital-intensive with regulatory risk; free cash flow after capex and interest is thin (~PLN 500-800m annually), limiting both deleveraging pace and shareholder return capacity [#J]

  • Sub: Deeper bear (not an active analyst view, but implied by market pricing): the convergence premium never materialises because Polish consumers don't value bundling the way Western European consumers do

  • Bullish theme 1: Polkomtel synergies — [AS-mBank Securities], [AS-Erste Group Research], [AS-Wood & Company] all assign value to ongoing Polkomtel integration; headcount reduction + network sharing savings expected to deliver PLN 200-350m additional EBITDA by FY26-27 [#J]

  • Bullish theme 2: Energy services growth — [AS-Jefferies] and [AS-Santander Bank Polska Brokerage] highlight Polsat Plus as an undervalued asset; PV/wind buildout + retail energy margin recovery as a multi-year theme [#A]

  • Bullish theme 3: Leverage reduction trajectory — even the cautious analysts acknowledge that if EBITDA stays at PLN 3.9-4.0bn and FCF is ~PLN 600-800m, net debt should decline to ~3x by FY27, potentially unlocking dividend restoration and multiple re-rating [#J]

  • Sceptical theme 1: Pay-TV structural decline — all analysts acknowledge cord-cutting; the debate is pace and offset; no analyst has a confident quantitative model for when Polsat Box GO reaches critical mass [#J]

  • Sceptical theme 2: 5G capex drag — Polish 5G spectrum obligations require network rollout capex that most analysts model at PLN 1.5-2.0bn over FY25-27; this competes with deleveraging for FCF [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 3: Governance and minority protections — not widely discussed by analysts but Polsat Group's controlling shareholder (Zygmunt Solorz) has a history of related-party transactions and corporate restructuring moves that have not always been minority-friendly; this is a non-zero risk [#J]

  • Narrative shift L12M: 12 months ago, the dominant theme was "convergence value creation on track"; today the dominant theme is "we need to see leverage come down before the stock re-rates"; analysts have lowered expectations without formally downgrading [#J]

  • Tone shift: Tone in analyst notes has shifted from "imminent catalyst" language to "patient accumulation" language; this is not bearish but it is a notable shift in conviction intensity [#A]

  • Near-term focus: Q2 26 EBITDA print; management FY26 guidance revision (if any at half-year); 5G rollout capex updates [#I]

  • Medium-term focus: Net debt/EBITDA trajectory to 3.0x target; Polsat Box GO subscriber growth; energy segment EBITDA inflection [#J]
