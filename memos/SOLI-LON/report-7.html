  • Track record: Pre-acquisition SOLI-LON operated at 8-10% EBIT margins consistently in FY19-21; the margin compression is acquisition-related rather than structural business deterioration [#A]

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: Bearish synthesis: SOLI-LON is a multi-variable story where drivers 1-3 are correlated to the same underlying cycle; if defence programme timing disappoints, it likely hits Systems margin AND Power recovery simultaneously [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers #1 (Defence demand) and #2 (Margin normalisation) are not independent — margin recovery is partly volume-driven; if defence revenues disappoint, overhead absorption stays poor and margins stay depressed; both drivers miss together [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers #1 (Defence demand) and #3 (Power cycle) are also correlated — Power's defence-facing customers are the same ecosystem as Systems division's customers; a defence programme delay hits both simultaneously [#A]

  • Correlation across drivers: Drivers 1, 2, and 3 are positively correlated to the same UK/NATO defence procurement cycle — they are not 3 independent bets; they are closer to 1.5 independent bets in a downside scenario [#J]

  • Cumulative plausibility: If each of drivers 1-3 is independently 65% likely, and they are 60% correlated, the joint delivery probability is materially below 65³ (27%) but also materially above; rough estimate ~35-45% probability of all three delivering within 24M timeline [#A]

  • Sub: This framing suggests the base case is partial delivery (2 of 3 drivers), not full delivery; partial delivery implies modest EPS recovery, not transformative re-rating [#J]

  • Setup archetype: "Cyclical recovery + acquisition integration normalisation" — a recognisable UK small-cap industrial setup; these setups typically take 2-3 years longer than management guides, and deliver IRR of 15-25% over 3-5Y holding periods, not 25%+ in 24M [#I]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The bear case is: (a) UK defence procurement continues to prioritise major platform programmes over sub-system electronics spend in FY26-27; (b) Power destocking is genuinely structural (customer base shifting to lower-specification power systems) rather than cyclical; (c) integration overhead is permanent not temporary; compounding these three = EBIT margins stuck at 5-6%, revenues flat, FCF generation weak, stock re-rates further toward asset value rather than earnings multiple [#J]

  • Single largest case-level risk: UK MoD budget reallocation toward platforms + programme delays — removes the macro tailwind, exposes the integration execution risk in isolation, and delays the earnings recovery by 2+ years; this is the scenario that most damages IRR potential [#J]

  • Stacked downside: If drivers #1, #3 both disappoint simultaneously: ~£15-20m combined revenue miss vs consensus, EBIT margins stay at 5-6%, EPS stays at 20-25p, P/E re-rates from current ~14× depressed to ~10× distressed small-cap = share price 200-250p vs current ~320-360p; ~30-35% downside scenario [#A]

  • Sub: Stacked downside is asymmetric with the upside — upside from full delivery is 80-100% in 24M; downside from correlated driver failure is 30-35%; risk/reward is wide but not skewed enough for low-conviction entry [#J]

  • Driver independence test: Only driver #5 (Components organic growth) and driver #6 (FCF conversion) are genuinely independent of the defence-cycle correlation; but these are the smallest-impact drivers; the thesis's load-bearing drivers are correlated [#A]

  • Fulcrum candidate: The true fulcrum is whether management can demonstrate H1 FY26 orders-vs-revenue inflection — this single data point would confirm or deny drivers 1 and 3 simultaneously and signal margin trajectory [#J]

  • Small-cap structural risk: SOLI-LON at £60-70m market cap is below most institutional mandates; limited liquidity constrains the investor base; multiple expansion potential is partly a function of ESG/thematic re-rating from specialist institutions, not generalist institutional interest [#I]
