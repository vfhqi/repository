EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Hammerson has completed a highly material five-year turnaround characterized by the transition to an experienced real estate CEO, active board refreshment, and strong colleague engagement , positioning the group to pivot from defensive restructuring to accretive net investment. The pivotal divestment of Value Retail completed a £1.5bn multi-year capital recycling programme that transformed the group's balance sheet, while capital has been aggressively redeployed into buying out flagship joint venture partners to establish full operational and cash flow control. The net signal on materiality of change is decisively positive , with the company having transitioned from a distressed asset disposer to a stabilized operator of prime UK and Irish retail destinations, though execution risks remain elevated given the concentrated exposure to physical retail and the simultaneous departure of both the CEO and CFO within an eight-month window.

Net signal on materiality of change

Activist pressure from Lighthouse Properties, which built a stake reaching 22.8% by December 2022 and tabled AGM resolutions expressing lack of confidence in the board, has substantially subsided following strategic share disposals that reduced its holding to 20.9%, allowing the board to stabilise governance and focus on net investment . The exit from Value Retail for £595m cash proceeds and the generation of £1.5bn total disposal proceeds over four years systematically reshaped the portfolio and marked the end of restructuring . The magnitude of structural change is profound : Hammerson has evolved from a multi-geography, multi-format portfolio operator with material outlet village exposure into a concentrated, wholly-owned prime destination retail specialist focused on flagship UK and Irish shopping centers.
