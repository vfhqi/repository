§1 — Current Management Guidance

VERBUND's FY2025 guidance, issued at Q4/FY2024 results (February 2025), is structured around a wide EBIT range reflecting acknowledged hydrology uncertainty; medium-term targets through FY2027 were reaffirmed at the March 2025 Capital Markets Day.

  • Verdict — current guide: Management has issued a deliberately wide FY2025 EBIT band (EUR 1.0–1.2bn) and a vague medium-term EBIT growth target, signalling real uncertainty rather than conservative sandbagging [#J]

  • FY current — EBIT: EUR 1.0–1.2bn range (midpoint EUR 1.1bn) for FY2025; compares to EUR 1.01bn FY2024 actual — midpoint implies ~9% EBIT growth, upper end implies ~19% [#A]

  • Sub: range width of EUR 200mn is unusually wide vs VERBUND's historical FY guidance ranges (typically EUR 100–150mn band width in L5Y)

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit revenue guidance issued; management guides EBIT directly, treating revenue as too volatile given spot/forward power-price mix [#I]

  • Sub: Sell-side infers FY2025 revenue in EUR 4.3–4.8bn range (see §5)

  • FY current — EPS: No direct EPS guidance; management points analysts to EBIT guidance + stable financial result + ~26% effective tax rate as the derivation path [#I]

  • Sub: Absence of explicit EPS guidance is itself a pattern — VER-AT has never issued EPS guidance formally; this forces analysts to build their own EPS models with embedded variability

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF guidance; CapEx guidance of EUR 1.1–1.3bn for FY2025 issued (elevated vs FY2024 EUR 0.97bn actual) [#A]

  • Sub: Higher CapEx is renewable expansion (offshore wind, pumped hydro extensions) — FCF will be constrained even if EBIT lands at upper end of range

  • FY current — CapEx: EUR 1.1–1.3bn FY2025 guidance; represents ~10–30% step-up vs FY2024; management frames as investment phase, not run-rate [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y: FY2026 EBIT ambition "mid-to-high single-digit CAGR from FY2024 base" — no hard number; implies EUR 1.07–1.15bn at mid-single and EUR 1.15–1.25bn at high-single [#I]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: FY2027 similar framing — management states "EBIT comfortably above EUR 1.2bn by FY2027" contingent on hydrology normalisation and German interconnector spreads holding [#I]

  • Sub: "Comfortably above EUR 1.2bn" is an aspirational qualifier with no quantified confidence interval — materially weaker signposting than a hard management forecast

  • Conditional assumption — hydrology: Guidance assumes "average hydrology" — defined as ~90% of 20-year median precipitation/reservoir inflow; FY2024 was ~83% of median (below-average) [#A]

  • Sub: Single largest swing factor; management estimates each 5% hydrology deviation = ~EUR 50mn EBIT impact

  • Conditional assumption — power prices: Guidance assumes Austrian baseload forward power price in EUR 70–80/MWh range for unhedged FY2025 volumes; spot as of Q1-25 ~EUR 73/MWh [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Q4/FY2024 results call, February 2025; guidance confirmed at CMD, March 2025 [#I]

  • Update timing: VERBUND typically updates FY guidance at H1 results (August) when H1 hydrology and hedging outcomes are known; no intra-period update mechanism outside major macro events [#I]

  • Caveat: All guidance excludes "significant adverse regulatory change" (Austrian carbon levy reform, network tariff reset), large unannounced M&A, and force majeure hydrology events (drought year defined as <75% of 20-year median) [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The wide EBIT band and absence of revenue/EPS guidance create maximum management optionality; the EUR 1.0bn floor is only ~1% above FY2024 actual — management can "beat" at the bottom of the range with near-zero real growth, while the EUR 1.2bn upper end requires both hydrology normalisation AND power price stability simultaneously [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Verdict — pattern: VERBUND is an opportunistic sandbagger in commodity super-cycles but transitions to over-promiser in normalisation phases; the pattern is regime-conditional, not a structural management behaviour [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: FY2021–FY2022 pattern: management guided conservatively (wide ranges, commodity-uncertainty caveats) and market dynamics delivered multiples of the guidance range — this was less intentional sandbagging and more fundamental forecasting inability during unprecedented power price events [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal: FY2023: management issued EUR 1.6–2.0bn EBIT guidance (midpoint EUR 1.8bn) in February 2023 when European power forward curves were still elevated; actual EUR 1.52bn — guidance was anchored to a price environment that had already begun normalising [#J]

  • Sub: A genuinely conservative management team would have guided EUR 1.3–1.6bn with downside caveat — the EUR 1.8bn midpoint was aggressive given observable forward curve deterioration

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: EPS shows no sandbagging discipline in any period — even in FY2021–FY2022, EPS beats were market-driven not guidance-structure-driven; in L3 normalisation years, EPS consistently underdelivered [#A]

  • Pattern shift: The shift from beat-to-miss behaviour occurred precisely at the FY2022/FY2023 transition — coincides with (a) power price normalisation, (b) management team's first CMD post-crisis setting medium-term ambitions [#J]

  • Sub: CMD ambition-setting in November 2022 (at peak power prices) locked in aspirational EBIT targets that subsequently proved too high as prices fell

  • Beat consistency: FY2021–FY2022 beat consistency (100%, 2 of 2 periods) is superficially strong but unrepresentative of management forecasting skill — comparable to forecasting sun in summer [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average beat magnitude in FY2021–FY2022: ~+34% on EBIT vs guidance midpoint; peer European integrated utilities (RWE, Enel, EDF) averaged +8–12% EBIT beats in the same period — VERBUND's beats were outlier-large [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Average miss magnitude in FY2023–FY2024: ~-8.5% on EBIT vs guidance midpoint; similar European peers averaged -3–5% miss in same period — VERBUND's misses are modestly worse than peer median [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: FY2024 EBIT narrow beat (+1%) vs FY2023 large miss (-16%) suggests some recalibration; however, FY2024 EBIT beat masked by FCF and EPS misses in the same period — surface improvement may be selective [#J]

  • Deteriorating accuracy — EPS: EPS miss frequency and magnitude have not improved from FY2023 to FY2024 (-18% to -12%); the EPS accuracy trend is not yet in recovery [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Apparent recalibration in FY2024 (narrower EBIT miss/small beat) may simply reflect management widening the guidance range to EUR 100–200mn band; a wider band is not the same as better forecasting — it is structural optionality to avoid embarrassment [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Verdict — trend: EBIT guidance accuracy improved modestly from FY2023 to FY2024 but FCF and EPS accuracy deteriorated further; net trend is flat-to-slightly-worsening on the metrics that matter most to equity investors [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBIT: FY2024 EBIT beat by ~+1% (vs FY2023 miss of -16%) — mechanically improved; however, the improvement was driven by management widening the guidance range, not better forecasting [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — EBIT: FY2021–FY2022 EBIT beats (+45%, +22%) are outlier periods; baseline EBIT accuracy in normalised years (FY2023–FY2024) averages ~-8% miss [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — revenue: Revenue accuracy has improved: FY2023 miss of -9% narrowed to FY2024 beat of +3%; reflects management no longer anchoring revenue guidance to unsustainable post-crisis power price levels [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — FCF: FCF miss magnitude in FY2023 (-26%) and FY2024 (-20%) shows no improvement trend; CapEx overruns and working capital consumption are a persistent underestimation problem [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — EPS: EPS miss magnitude: FY2023 -18%, FY2024 -12% — directionally improving but still deeply negative; 3 of 4 L4 periods with EPS shortfall [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Power price normalisation from FY2023 was the single largest accuracy driver in the dataset; management's forecasting error was an anchoring bias to the high-price regime [#J]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Hydrology shortfall (FY2024: ~83% of 20-year median) added to FY2024 misses; this is partially exogenous but management's failure to stress-test guidance for 85% hydrology is an internal forecasting weakness [#J]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Revenue in FY2024 (+3%) and FY2022 (+17%) — revenue forecasting has improved as management now uses a wider range and normalised price deck [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF across L4 period average; management consistently underestimates CapEx and overestimates operating cash conversion — this is a structural FCF forecasting failure [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: FY2025 guidance was set wider than any prior year (EUR 200mn EBIT band) precisely because FY2023 and FY2024 guidance failures damaged management credibility; the structural fix (wider bands) reduces information content of guidance without improving underlying forecasting skill [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

VERBUND's FY2025 and medium-term guidance rests on five distinct assumption pillars, each with explicit sensitivity implications. At least three are vulnerable to simultaneous stress.

  • Verdict — assumption stack: Hydrology and power-price assumptions carry the most EBIT sensitivity; CapEx/FCF assumptions are structurally optimistic; tax and financial-result assumptions are relatively stable — overall stack is medium-fragility [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes Austrian baseload power ~EUR 70–80/MWh for unhedged FY2025 volumes (~30–35% of generation unhedged); spot at EUR 73/MWh as of Q1-25 is within range but Q2 forwards have softened to EUR 68–70 [#A]

  • Sub: Every EUR 5/MWh movement in unhedged power price = ~EUR 60–80mn EBIT impact (management-disclosed sensitivity)

  • Revenue assumption — hydrology: Guidance assumes 90% of 20-year median hydrology; FY2024 actual was ~83%; long-run probability of achieving ≥90% in any given year is ~55–60% (VERBUND historical) [#A]

  • Sub: Every 5pp hydrology shortfall below guidance assumption = ~EUR 50mn EBIT impact (management-disclosed)

  • Revenue assumption — market: German-Austrian interconnector spread assumed to remain positive (EUR 2–5/MWh) — a key enabler of VERBUND's cross-border trading margin; current spread ~EUR 3/MWh [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: O&M cost growth assumed at ~3–4% in FY2025; FY2024 actual O&M growth was ~5.5% — management is assuming cost deceleration that has not yet materialised [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: FY2025 margin guidance embeds ~200bps EBIT margin expansion vs FY2024 (~25% FY2024 EBIT margin implied to ~27% FY2025); requires both EBIT growth and cost control simultaneously [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed ~26% (consistent with FY2022–FY2024 range 25–27%); this assumption is stable and not a key swing variable [#I]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count effectively stable (no buyback programme announced; no dilutive issuance expected); EPS sensitivity to share count is low [#I]

  • EPS assumption — financial result: Net financial result guided at approximately EUR -150 to -170mn; debt refinancing risk in FY2025–FY2026 is limited as most long-term bonds mature post-FY2027 [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: No explicit commodity price assumption beyond power price; however, CO2 certificate prices affect thermal backup dispatch costs — management assumes EU ETS ~EUR 60–65/tonne; current spot ~EUR 62 [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: VERBUND revenue is almost entirely EUR-denominated; currency assumption not a material guidance risk [#I]

  • CapEx assumption — elevated: EUR 1.1–1.3bn CapEx guidance represents management's first above-EUR-1.0bn CapEx year; risk of execution delay or cost overrun in offshore wind projects — no contingency buffer disclosed [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Verdict — credibility: Management guidance credibility is MEDIUM for EBIT but LOW for EPS and FCF; the regime-dependency of historical accuracy and the current normalised-market context reduce confidence materially below what the FY2021–FY2022 beat record implies [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM on EBIT (widened range structure reduces risk of embarrassing miss; floor is achievable); LOW on implied EPS (systematic 3-of-4-period miss history, financial result and tax assumptions embed optimism); LOW on FCF (CapEx overrun and cash conversion pattern is persistent) [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY2024 EBIT narrow beat (+1%) is a technical positive — management did not miss for a second consecutive year; range-widening contributed but endpoint delivery is a data point [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: EUR 1.0bn EBIT floor (barely above FY2024 actual) is a true floor — achievable in a below-average hydrology year without power price expansion; this provides a credible downside anchor [#J]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Renewable capacity additions (pumped hydro, solar) have been delivered on time in FY2022–FY2024; CapEx execution (commissioning, not cost) has been reliable [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FY2023 EBIT miss of -16% and simultaneous intra-year guidance revision (August 2023 cut, then further miss at year-end) is a double credibility failure — management could not forecast its own business 6 months forward in FY2023 [#J]

  • ❌ Red flag — aggressive assumption: O&M cost deceleration assumption (+3.5% FY2025 vs +5.5% FY2024 actual) is not supported by any publicly disclosed efficiency programme — management is embedding cost improvement without articulating the mechanism [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: Power price trajectory in Q1-Q2 2025 shows softness below management's EUR 75/MWh base; if this persists into H2, H1 results (August) will likely trigger guidance reduction [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: First above-EUR-1.0bn CapEx year (FY2025) introduces execution risk on offshore wind projects; cost overruns would depress FCF without direct EBIT benefit in the current period [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EBIT: If EBIT misses by 10% (lands at EUR 0.99bn), the investment is NOT disqualified — EUR 0.99bn EBIT on a EUR 14bn market cap is ~7% EBIT yield, still reasonable for a regulated-adjacent utility [#A]

  • Sub: However, at 10% EBIT miss, EPS likely lands ~EUR 1.95–2.00 — below FY2024 actuals — which would pressure the valuation multiple

  • Margin of safety — EPS: If implied EPS guidance (~EUR 2.60) misses by 10% to EUR 2.34, the stock trades at ~29x FY2025 P/E at current price (EUR 68) — expensive for a utility with a low-growth medium-term profile, especially if EPS is not growing year-on-year [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The 18-month investment thesis is sensitive to whether EPS re-rates or stagnates; VERBUND's EPS history (peak EUR 7.89 in FY2022, trough EUR 2.12 in FY2024) makes the earnings base volatile — predicting a 25% IRR requires high confidence in where EPS normalises, not just whether it grows [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: Management's credibility on EPS is LOW primarily because they have never issued explicit EPS guidance — this allows management to avoid accountability for the EPS line while citing EBIT delivery as the success metric; an investor focused on EPS trajectory (as Richard is) is operating with a fundamentally less-credible guidance signal [#J]
