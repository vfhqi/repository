§1 — General

What Goodwin Is

  • BUSINESS DESCRIPTION: Goodwin plc is a 143-year-old family-controlled UK engineering conglomerate operating two divisions — Mechanical Engineering and Refractory Engineering. Headquartered in Hanley, Stoke-on-Trent; listed on London Stock Exchange (AIM from inception; FTSE 250 constituent from 2024). #I
  • Ticker: GDWN.L. 10p ordinary shares. ~7.63 million shares in issue. #I

  • Founded 1883 as R. Goodwin & Sons (iron foundry). Limited company since 1935. Listed since 1958. #I

  • MARKET CAP / EV: Market cap ~£1.5bn pre-crash; ~£900m-£1.1bn post-March-2026 sell-off. At peak (Sep-25 post-Northrop announcement) shares approached 28,500p; post-March-2026 correction to ~12,160p. EV data not formally published; net debt at 30-Apr-25 was £13.6m (gearing 9.9%), making EV approximately equal to market cap at current pricing. #I

  • Share count is very small (7.63m); this creates illiquidity and volatility amplification. Peer IMI plc has ~260m shares; SPX (private since KKR buyout 2022) not comparable. #A

  • STRATEGY: Management philosophy is explicit — target niches with high barriers to entry, pursue superior gross margin per unit rather than raw revenue growth, invest organically in human and physical capital. This is stated consistently across 10+ years of annual reports. #I

  • The company does not chase turnover; it explicitly prioritises gross margin percentage and absolute contribution per product. #A
  • This explains why revenue grew modestly (~3% CAGR FY16-22) while margins and earnings compounded faster. #A

§2 — Company Details

Revenue by Division

  • MECHANICAL ENGINEERING (Mech. Eng.): ~82% of group revenue in FY24 (£156.9m of £191m). This division is growing faster and carries structurally higher margins due to nuclear/defence mix shift. #I
  • FY24 segment pre-tax profit: £18.9m (+55% YoY). FY23: £12.2m. #I

  • Includes: Goodwin Steel Castings (foundry), Goodwin International (nuclear waste fabrication), Noreva GmbH (axial valves, Germany), Easat Radar Systems (defence/civil radar), and submersible slurry pumps (mining). #I

  • REFRACTORY ENGINEERING (Ref. Eng.): ~18% of group revenue in FY24 (~£34m). Stable, highly profitable (21% pre-tax margin) with recent acceleration. #I

  • FY24 segment pre-tax profit: £13.5m (+5% YoY vs £12.8m). FY23: Growing 37% YoY following 40% prior year. #I
  • Includes: Goodwin Refractory Services (investment casting plasters/powders/waxes for jewellery/aerospace/automotive), Hoben International (cristobalite, ground silica), minerals processing (perlite, exfoliated vermiculite). #I
  • Refractory division is NOT primarily a steel furnace lining business — that is a common misread. Its primary markets are jewellery casting consumables and mineral processing, not Vesuvius-style bulk steelmaking refractories. #A

Revenue by End-Market

  • END-MARKET MIX (Mech. Eng.): Oil/gas/LNG valves, nuclear castings/fabrication, defence/naval castings, aerospace castings, mining pumps, radar/surveillance. Precise % split not reported; the company does not break this out formally. #I

  • Oil/gas/LNG: Material — Noreva GmbH axial valve sales described as "buoyant" driven by large US and Qatar LNG projects (H1 FY26). Iran war created dispatch delays, not cancellations, on Gulf LNG contracts. #I

  • Defence/naval: Rolls-Royce UK submarine approval (scope: high alloy steel and nickel alloy castings); NAVSEA US nuclear submarine approval (HY80 castings); Northrop Grumman MoU (4 defence programmes, $16m initial, $200m+ potential). #I

  • END-MARKET MIX (Ref. Eng.): Jewellery casting consumables (~60% est.), minerals processing (cristobalite/perlite/vermiculite for insulation/fire protection/horticulture), AVD fire extinguishing agents (patented for lithium-ion battery fires), niche aerospace/automotive casting plasters. #I

  • Jewellery casting is the dominant Refractory market — global fine jewellery production (~25m kg gold per year) drives steady consumable demand for casting powders, waxes, rubbers. #A
  • AVD lithium-ion battery fire extinguishing agent is a new high-potential niche — sole solution for Class D fires involving lithium batteries; emerging market as EV and energy storage growth accelerates. Duvelco (polyimide) is adjacent and complementary. #A

Safety-Critical Products and Switching Costs

  • ⚡ RARE: Nuclear valve casting and fabrication carries the hardest regulatory switching cost in UK engineering. A customer switching nuclear valve or casting supplier must re-qualify the new supplier through the nuclear safety case process — a multi-year, multi-million process with zero tolerance for failure. #J
  • Goodwin Steel Castings: Rolls-Royce approval (UK nuclear submarines, scope-limited to high alloy steel/nickel alloy castings + NDT/radiographic testing + heat treatment). NAVSEA US approval (HY80 castings for US nuclear submarines). ASME NCA 3800 accreditation history. ISO9001 from 1984 (first steel foundry globally). #I
  • Goodwin International: Primary manufacturer of Self-Shielded Boxes (SSBs) and 63 Element Racks for Sellafield — via Westinghouse supply contract. Loss of Sellafield retender (>£45m, Mar-26) shows this is not entirely secure, though full narrative unclear. #A
  • Switching cost framing: The barriers are not merely financial — they are temporal (5-10 year qualification timelines typical in nuclear), regulatory (national nuclear safety case), and technical (metallurgical expertise built over decades). #A
  • Vs aerospace castings (Senior plc, Precision Castparts): aerospace qualification is also multi-year but less onerous than nuclear; Goodwin's nuclear work represents the harder end of the spectrum. #A

Family Ownership and Governance

  • FAMILY CONTROL: Goodwin family controls the business through the 5th generation. Company founded 1883; family majority ownership continuously held. Post-2019 succession: Timothy Goodwin (Executive Chairman), Matthew Goodwin (MD, Mechanical Engineering), Simon Goodwin (MD, Refractory Engineering). #I
  • This is not passive ownership; family members actively manage divisions. Long-term orientation is evident: the company's stated philosophy (pursue quality not volume, reinvest in skills and equipment, avoid dilutive M&A) is consistent with family capital at risk. #A

  • Minority shareholders benefit from family alignment but are subject to governance risks: limited independent board presence; AIM-era disclosure minimalism persists even post-FTSE 250 entry. No independent analyst with deep access is confirmed. #A

  • CAPITAL ALLOCATION RECORD: Highly conservative and value-creating over the cycle. Minimal debt accumulation (net debt <£14m at FY25, rapidly declining); no dilutive equity issuances identified; organic capex in high-quality niches; one major acquisition of Noreva GmbH (2007) which was accretive and complementary. Duvelco (polyimide polymer) is a long-horizon bet, now inflecting to profit. #A

  • Dividend policy historically capped at 38% of post-tax profit + D&A — disciplined, not capital-return-maximising. The special 532p dividend (Oct-25) was exceptional recognition of accumulated cash generation. The potential reversion to pre-special policy is rational given geopolitical backdrop. #A

L12M and N3Y Internal Change

  • L12M: FTSE 250 entry (2024), Northrop Grumman MoU (Sep-25), special dividend (Oct-25), profit doubling (H1 FY26), then March-26 Sellafield tender loss + Iran geopolitical shock. Rapid oscillation from "breakout moment" to "geopolitical exposure surfaced." #A
  • Easat Radar completed restructuring — from loss-making drag to ~£1m profit (FY25). This is a positive inflection. #I

  • Duvelco: heavy capex phase complete (UK resin plant + German processing facility expansion). Transitioning from cost centre to revenue generator. #I

  • N3Y DIRECTION: Nuclear and defence backlog visibility is multi-year. Order book £365m (Oct-25 trading update), £357m (Sep-25, up 24% from Apr-25 in 4 months), £330m (Dec-25 H1 report). These are real, contracted workloads not pipeline estimates. #I

  • Rolls-Royce SMR programme (£600m Great British Energy contract signed Apr-26, Wylfa site, Wales): Valves, pumps, heat exchangers confirmed as supply chain categories. Goodwin is a natural supplier — but formal SMR contract award not yet announced. #A
  • Northrop Grumman $200m+ potential orders: Subject to US submarine programme funding release timelines. Initial $16m order live. #I

§3 — Industry Context

Specialist Alloy Castings Market

  • CASTINGS MARKET: Specialist alloy castings for nuclear, defence and aerospace is a genuinely small-volume, high-value global market with severe qualification barriers. Total addressable market is not large in commodity terms — but margin premium over commodity castings is extreme (GM 40%+ vs ~15-20% for standard industrial castings). #A
  • UK nuclear casting capacity is extremely limited. Goodwin is credibly described by independent commentary as holding "practically nonexistent" capacity competition in certain nuclear-specific alloy casting segments. This is a strong claim that cannot be fully verified from public sources but is consistent with the Rolls-Royce/NAVSEA approval data. #A

  • The claim that "it takes decades to build the pedigree required to pour steel for a nuclear submarine reactor casing" (sourced from Scribd deep-dive analysis, corroborated by Sweet Stocks Substack) is directionally correct based on qualification framework evidence. #A

  • REFRACTORY COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE: Two distinct sub-markets — Goodwin serves investment casting consumables, not bulk steelmaking refractories. This matters for competitive benchmarking. #A

  • Bulk steelmaking refractories (Vesuvius, RHI Magnesita): £1-2bn revenue businesses serving blast furnaces, EAF linings, tundish slide gates. Goodwin Refractory Services does NOT compete here. #I
  • Investment casting powders/plasters (jewellery, aerospace, automotive): A niche global market; Goodwin Refractory Services is one of a small number of global manufacturers. Sister companies in China, Thailand, India give geographic coverage. Castaldo silicone rubber acquisition (2020) broadened portfolio. #I
  • Correct peer for Refractory segment: Not Vesuvius but smaller, private specialty casting consumable suppliers. No direct listed peer. #A

Nuclear Revival Tailwind

  • NUCLEAR NEW BUILD: UK SMR programme is now contracted and real. Rolls-Royce SMR selected as preferred technology (Jun-25); £2.6bn allocated in Spending Review; firm contract signed Apr-26 for Wylfa site. GBE-N target: 70% British supply chain content. Valves, pumps, heat exchangers are explicitly listed supply chain categories. #I
  • This is a meaningful multi-decade tailwind for Goodwin. However, the nuclear new build revenue contribution will not materialise at scale until late-2020s/early-2030s at the earliest, given design and licensing timelines. #A

  • European nuclear life extension (France, Belgium, others) and new build (Finland Hinkley-comparable projects) provide additional nuclear valve and casting demand. #I

  • NUCLEAR DECOMMISSIONING (NDA/Sellafield): Near-term, contracted, but tender-competitive. Sellafield SSB and 63-can rack contracts are live and delivering; however, the Mar-26 retender loss (>£45m bid, awarded to LTi Metaltech) demonstrates that even established nuclear contractors face competitive retenders. The loss was described as unexpected given Goodwin's strong incumbent delivery record. #A

  • This is a key risk signal: regulatory moat confers qualification right to bid, not guaranteed award. Commercial and pricing competition exists even within the qualified supplier set. #A

Green Steel — EAF Transition

  • GREEN STEEL / EAF TRANSITION: Largely irrelevant to Goodwin's actual refractory business. The company does not make bulk furnace lining refractories. Its Refractory segment serves jewellery casting and minerals processing — EAF transition does not materially impact this. #A
  • Indirect minor positive: EAF steelmakers still need precision alloy castings (Mech. Eng. segment) and could represent incremental demand if Goodwin chooses to pursue steel sector casting customers. Not a current focus area. #A
  • For Vesuvius/RHI Magnesita, EAF transition is a strategic challenge (different refractory chemistry needed); Goodwin is insulated from this entirely. This is a differentiated positive vs refractory peers. #A

Oil/Gas Capex Cycle

  • OIL/GAS LNG VALVE MARKET: Structural tailwind from global LNG capacity build (US export expansion, Qatar North Field expansion) partially offset by cyclical/geopolitical vulnerability. Noreva GmbH axial valves have 20+ years of LNG project history; this is a proven product in the sector. #I
  • Iran war (Mar-26): Strait of Hormuz closure forced QatarEnergy force majeure; Gulf LNG valve dispatch delays confirmed by Goodwin. Brent spiked to $120+. Longer-term, a resolution to the conflict (or rerouting of LNG) could restore order flow. #I
  • US LNG mega-projects (Sabine Pass, Corpus Christi, Golden Pass expansions) are proceeding; these are not affected by Middle East disruption. #I
  • ❌ The oil/gas cycle dependency is a persistent structural vulnerability: approximately the third time in a decade that Goodwin's oil/gas revenue stream has experienced a headwind (2015-16 capex collapse; 2020 covid; 2026 Iran). #A

Defence Spending Uplift

  • DEFENCE: Genuine secular tailwind, not just a cycle. NATO 2% GDP spending commitments are hardening into real procurement. UK defence budget increases are multi-year. US submarine programme (SSN, Virginia class continuation) represents decades of demand. Northrop Grumman MoU is evidence of US procurement appetite for UK foundry capacity. #I
  • The logic is compelling: high-specification submarine and nuclear casting capacity is a strategic national asset; the US and UK have limited qualified suppliers; Goodwin has a 40-year head start in qualification. #A
  • Order book visibility on defence (multi-year) provides structural buffer to cyclical softness in oil/gas or decommissioning. #A

§4 — BB#2 Foundation Quality

§4.1 Operator: Family Management Quality

  • OPERATOR: POSITIVE VERDICT: Family management is demonstrably long-termist and quality-focused. The 40-year consistency of strategic positioning (niche, high-barrier, high-margin) across four to five family generations is strong evidence of cultural DNA. The 2019 succession (from Richard/John Goodwin to Timothy/Matthew/Simon Goodwin) proceeded smoothly without strategic discontinuity. #J
  • Timothy Goodwin (Chair, from 2019): ACA-qualified accountant; joined the business ~8 years pre-appointment; 4 years as director before taking Chair. This is a finance-disciplined succession, not purely patrimonial. #I
  • Capital allocation over L10Y: debt-free bias, no dilutive equity, organic capex in defensible niches, one accretive acquisition (Noreva), long-horizon R&D bets (Duvelco polyimide, AVD fire agent). Record is excellent. #A

  • Weakness: Family control without strong independent board oversight is a governance risk. There is no visible mechanism for holding family management accountable if strategy or execution deteriorates. AIM/FTSE-250 small-cap governance norms allow this. #A

  • TRANSPARENCY: BELOW PEERS. Goodwin does not provide formal end-market revenue splits, formal aftermarket %, formal ROIC disclosure, or detailed segment capex data. Communications are characteristically understated — the company explicitly stated it "does not care if competitors read the annual updates" (rare boast in Oct-25, quickly followed by bad news in Mar-26). #A

  • Annual reports are filed on time; financial statements are audited and clean. But the qualitative disclosure depth is less than IMI (which has detailed investor presentations, capital markets days, formal segment margin targets). #A
  • Vs SPX (private), Vs IMI: IMI has formal segment reporting, IR function, consensus analyst coverage. Goodwin has no confirmed full-time IR, no formal analyst consensus, no capital markets days. #A

§4.2 Advantaged Business + SRCA

MOAT Assessment

  • ⚡ RARE: MOAT — Nuclear/defence casting certification is a verified hard moat. Rolls-Royce submarine approval (scope-limited, years to qualify), NAVSEA US nuclear submarine approval (HY80 castings), ISO9001 since 1984 (first globally in steel casting). These are documented, not merely claimed. #J
  • The temporal barrier is the deepest part: it is not feasible for a new entrant to replicate 40 years of nuclear quality history in a commercially relevant timeframe. Existing nuclear operators cannot casually qualify new suppliers — the safety case requirement prevents it. #A
  • Noreva GmbH: 20+ years LNG valve history; established qualification pedigree with major energy companies. Switching cost lower than nuclear but still substantial (3-5 year qualification typical for critical valve service). #A
  • Goodwin Refractory Services: 60+ years in investment casting plasters; patented silica-hazard-free plaster (world first); global sister company network. Switching cost is lower (product switching easier) but brand loyalty and formulation IP provide margin protection. #A

GENERATE: Earnings and Returns

  • GENERATE: EPS trajectory is exceptional — 96p (FY19) to 327p (FY25), 22.9% 5-year average growth rate per Simply Wall St. FY26E ~654p+ implies another ~100% step-up. ROE currently 38.9%; ROCE 22.4% (these are current-year calculations on a still-leveraged base). #I
  • Vs IMI 2024: IMI operating margin ~16%, ROCE ~20-25% (estimated from public data). Goodwin FY25/26 ROCE is approaching or exceeding IMI levels from a lower starting point. #A
  • Vs Vesuvius: Vesuvius ROCE in 2024 ~12-14% on refractory business. Goodwin Refractory segment at 21% pre-tax margin is materially ahead per unit of capital. #A

  • RONIC (Return on New Invested Capital): Not formally disclosed. But capex in nuclear/defence applications is yielding extreme returns — FY25 gross margin jump to 42% on moderate incremental capex suggests very high RONIC on these specific investments. #A

  • FCF CONVERSION: Strong and improving. £67m operating cash (FY25) on £35.5m pre-tax profit. Net debt extinguished from £55m (FY23) to £14m (FY25) while paying growing dividends — organic cash generation is the funding mechanism. #I

  • FCF will accelerate further as Duvelco capex phase ends. Management guided to lower future capex levels in H1 FY26. #A

§4.3 Value Chain: Nuclear Supply Chain Positioning

  • VALUE CHAIN: Goodwin occupies a critical Tier 1-2 position in UK and US nuclear supply chains. It is not a simple parts supplier — it is a qualified source for safety-classified components (nuclear safety class castings, non-destructive examination, radiographic testing). #J
  • UK nuclear submarine programme (Rolls-Royce PWR3 reactor for Dreadnought class): Goodwin Steel Castings is a qualified casting source. Submarine reactors are 30+ year operational assets; component supply is a multi-decade relationship once established. #A
  • Sellafield NDA decommissioning: Goodwin International (via Westinghouse contract) manufactures SSBs and 63-element racks — large, safety-critical stainless steel fabrications for nuclear waste interim storage. The Mar-26 tender loss to LTi Metaltech indicates this specific revenue stream is re-tendereable; the qualification remains but the contract is not locked indefinitely. #A

  • Northrop Grumman : Four defence programmes, exclusivity for one critical component (~25-30% of MoU value). This is the most strategically significant recent development — US nuclear submarine casting capacity is constrained and Goodwin's approvals are a genuine strategic asset from a US national security perspective. #A

  • CUSTOMERS: Rolls-Royce (submarines), Westinghouse/Sellafield , Northrop Grumman, UK MoD, NAVSEA (implied), QatarEnergy (LNG valves), various Gulf/US LNG EPC contractors. Customer names are partially disclosed through RNS announcements rather than systematic disclosure. #I

§4.4 Industry Structure

  • INDUSTRY STRUCTURE: Goodwin operates in genuinely niche sub-markets with limited formal competitive intelligence available. In alloy castings for nuclear/defence, Goodwin is one of a handful of globally qualified UK suppliers — possibly the most capable single-foundry source in the UK for the specific qualification combination it holds. #J
  • Competitive alternatives: For nuclear castings — other UK-approved suppliers include specialist defence foundries (e.g. those in the Sheffield steel cluster), but these typically serve different programmes or alloy specifications. For LNG axial valves — Noreva is a global top-3 specialist; competitors include Neles (Metso), Cameron (Schlumberger), but Noreva's 20+ year track record in LNG is a genuine differentiator. #A
  • For refractory investment casting powders — the market is global but fragmented; Goodwin is one of a small number with the R&D capability to develop patented formulations and serve aerospace/medical quality tiers. #A
  • ❌ Independent competitive analysis is difficult: no formal industry reports on nuclear casting market structure are publicly available; competitive dynamics must be inferred from contract awards and qualification data. This is a research limitation. #A

§4.5 Long-Term Growth Vectors

  • LT GROWTH VECTOR 1 — Nuclear new build. UK SMR (Rolls-Royce, Wylfa, contracted Apr-26); UK Hinkley Point C (EDF, ongoing); European life extension programmes; potential further sites (Sizewell C). Goodwin is pre-positioned with the qualification pedigree to be a natural supplier. Revenue impact: medium-term (2028-2035). #A

  • LT GROWTH VECTOR 2 — Defence/naval escalation. NATO 2% commitments; UK/US submarine programmes multi-decade; Northrop Grumman MoU represents a foothold in the US defence casting market with $200m+ potential. Growing at 24% workload over 4 months (Apr-Sep 25) — organic acceleration visible. #A

  • LT GROWTH VECTOR 3 — Duvelco (Ducoya polyimide). Patented new-to-world material; applications in aerospace jet engines, spacecraft, semiconductor manufacturing.
