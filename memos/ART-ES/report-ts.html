Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk profile is highly manageable, as primary cost and logistics pressures are mitigated by robust structural and contractual protections.

2.
Contractual price pass-through: Cost inflation risks are heavily mitigated by pricing pass-through clauses in master contracts across the utility customer base
3.
Supply chain logistics bottlenecks: Delays in global freight shipping or packaging material shortages could temporarily delay product deliveries and impact quarterly revenue recognition
4.
Foreign exchange volatility: Exposure to international currencies, particularly the US Dollar and Mexican Peso, creates translational and transactional risks to reported earnings
5.
No short seller presence: The company has no published short-seller reports or elevated short interest, indicating high corporate credibility and reporting transparency
6.
Backlog as a false friend: A rising backlog could act as a false friend if driven by manufacturing inefficiencies rather than strong organic orders
7.
Early-warning indicators: Investors should closely monitor raw material cost trends as an early-warning signal for prospective gross margin health
8.
Other side of the story: These operational risks are overstated because Arteche's decentralized factory footprint and strong customer relationships allow rapid manufacturing and pricing adjustments
