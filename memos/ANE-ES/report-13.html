2. KEY FINDINGS - Net verdict: ANE-ES management guidance is structurally over-optimistic on EBITDA and EPS timelines while revenue volume targets carry more credibility; overall credibility is MEDIUM-LOW [#J]

  • Miss pattern: Three of four assessed periods produced EBITDA misses versus initial guidance, driven by interest cost underestimation and curtailment drag; miss frequency exceeds European renewables peer median of ~1.5 misses per 4 periods [#J]

  • Assumption fragility: Interest-rate sensitivity is the single most dangerous embedded assumption; ANE-ES carries ~€5.5bn gross debt (FY24), and a 100bps rise in refinancing cost translates to ~€55m EBIT erosion, representing ~15% of FY24 EBIT [#A]

  • EPS trajectory: EPS guidance for FY24-FY26 period was reset materially downward at FY23 results (Mar 2024); the prior medium-term EPS CAGR target of ~20% was effectively abandoned in favour of a "return to growth from FY25" framing [#J]

  • Sandbagging vs over-promising: Management is a consistent over-promiser on margin/EPS and an honest tracker on MW-installed targets; the asymmetry matters because the investment thesis rests on margin recovery, which is the least credible guidance component [#J]

  • Sell-side consensus: Consensus FY25 EBITDA sits ~4% below management's own guidance midpoint, an unusual configuration suggesting market-level skepticism; consensus EPS estimates have been revised DOWN six consecutive quarters [#J]

  • Tracking trigger: H1 2025 interest coverage ratio and curtailment rates in Spain/Australia are the two highest-frequency early-warning metrics; either deteriorating meaningfully should trigger immediate thesis re-evaluation [#A]

  • Credibility verdict: MEDIUM-LOW — management's guidance framework is not fabricated or misleading, but embedded optimism on financing costs and operational ramp rates means guidance should be haircut 10-15% on EBITDA before building any valuation model [#J]

  • Peer gap: Unlike Orsted or Iberdrola, ANE-ES has not demonstrated a repeatable sandbagging pattern; Iberdrola beats EBITDA guidance in ~80% of periods vs ANE-ES's ~25% beat rate in the L4 assessment window [#A]

§1 — Current Management Guidance

Management has publicly committed to a quantified FY25/FY26 recovery, but the guidance rests on three assumptions that are simultaneously under stress.

  • Net verdict: Current guidance signals EBITDA recovery to €1.0-1.1bn by FY26 and positive net income re-establishment by FY25; both targets carry moderate execution risk given the L4 miss pattern [#J]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management guides FY25 EBITDA of approximately €850-900m, implying ~15% YoY recovery from FY24's €764m; sourced from FY24 results call (Mar 2025) [#A]

  • Sub: FY24 EBITDA of €764m was itself ~8% below the prior-year guidance midpoint of ~€830m — the baseline is already depressed

  • FY current — revenue: Management guides FY25 revenue of approximately €2.2-2.4bn, broadly consistent with sell-side consensus; volume targets (MW in operation) drive this line and have historically been more accurate [#A]

  • FY current — EBIT: No explicit standalone EBIT guidance issued at FY24 results; implied from EBITDA guide minus guided D&A of ~€480m, producing an implied EBIT of approximately €370-420m [#I]

  • FY current — EPS: No specific FY25 EPS guidance given at FY24 results call; management referenced "return to positive net profit" but declined to quantify, citing interest cost uncertainty — a red flag given EPS was the primary misfire metric in FY23/FY24 [#J]

  • FY current — FCF: Management guided "positive operational FCF in FY25 before growth CapEx"; no numerical midpoint provided; operationally this implies FCF of approximately €200-300m pre-growth CapEx based on implied EBITDA minus interest and tax [#I]

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided FY25 growth CapEx of approximately €1.4-1.6bn, down from FY24's ~€2.0bn as the MW installation pace moderates; maintenance CapEx separately guided ~€150m [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY26): Management targets EBITDA of €1.0-1.1bn and "clear positive net income"; no explicit EPS number; MW target of ~13GW gross operational capacity [#A]

  • Sub: The FY26 13GW target implies ~1.5GW net additions in FY25-FY26 combined, a rate lower than FY23's installation pace — suggests management is deliberately moderating ambition after over-reaching

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY27): No explicit FY27 targets provided at the most recent investor communication; prior strategic plan (2025-2030 plan issued 2022) targeted 20GW by 2030 but this plan is under internal review following the FY23/FY24 reset [#J]

  • Source — earnings call: Primary source is FY24 results call (Mar 2025, CEO Jose Entrecanales and CFO); supplemented by Q3 2024 IMS (Nov 2024) [#A]

  • Source — investor day: No standalone investor day held since the 2022 Capital Markets Day; absence of a refreshed CMD is itself a credibility signal — management has deferred committing to a new multi-year framework [#J]

  • Sub: Peers Orsted and RWE held CMDs in FY24; Iberdrola held its CMD in Nov 2024 — ANE-ES absence is conspicuous

  • Update timing: Q1 2025 trading update expected May/Jun 2025 — first hard data point testing FY25 guidance; watch for any softening of language on curtailment or interest cost [#A]

  • Caveat: All guidance explicitly conditioned on "no material change in Spanish regulatory framework" and "normal wind/solar resource conditions" — both conditions have recently been stressed [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: Interest cost assumption for FY25 guidance appears to embed approximately Euribor + 150bps average on floating book; if refinancing rates remain elevated, this assumption is too optimistic by ~50-80bps [#I]

  • Sceptical read: Management's deliberate avoidance of a hard EPS number for FY25 — after missing EPS guidance by >30% in FY23 — masks the most sensitive line in the model; the EBITDA-to-EPS bridge contains three compressible assumptions (interest, D&A rate, minority interests) each of which management has previously guided inaccurately [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • Net verdict: ANE-ES is a consistent over-promiser on financial return metrics (EBITDA, EPS, revenue); there is no credible sandbagging pattern [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal: Three of four periods produced EBITDA below initial guidance; directional bias is negative (management systematically issues guidance above what it delivers); peer comparison: Iberdrola beats EBITDA guidance in ~80% of periods vs ANE-ES's ~25% [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal: Physical delivery metrics (MW installed, CapEx) show no over-promising pattern; these lines are set conservatively and consistently delivered — the organisation's project management function is credible even when its financial planning is not [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: The divergence between physical delivery (credible) and financial delivery (not credible) is the most analytically important pattern; it suggests the execution team guides accurately but the finance/IR function embeds unrealised optionality into financial targets [#J]

  • Sub: Specifically, management consistently assumes energy prices, curtailment levels, and financing costs that prove too optimistic — operational execution is then "right" but financial translation is wrong

  • Miss consistency: The EBITDA miss is not random — it has occurred in three consecutive periods (FY22 on an underlying basis, FY23, FY24); the consecutive run suggests a structural rather than idiosyncratic gap in guidance methodology [#J]

  • Magnitude — miss: Average financial guidance miss is approximately -8% on EBITDA across L4; FY23's -21% miss represents a 2.5-sigma event relative to the L4 average; miss magnitude is widening in recent periods, not narrowing [#A]

  • Pattern shift: There is no observable improvement in guidance accuracy post-FY23; the FY24 guidance process appears not to have been fundamentally reformed; FY24's -8% miss suggests the same systematic overoptimism persists [#J]

  • Specific example — interest cost: In FY23, management guided interest cost of ~€220m; actual came in at ~€290m (a +32% overshoot); in FY24 the guidance-to-actual gap on interest narrowed to +~15%; improvement is present but insufficient [#A]

  • Sub: Interest cost is now ANE-ES's single largest financial guidance error driver, having overtaken energy price optimism as the primary miss mechanism

  • Specific example — curtailment: FY23 Australian curtailment of ~850GWh was not anticipated in initial guidance; FY24 guidance included a curtailment buffer of ~400GWh but actual came in at ~600GWh — guidance now includes the wrong-sized buffer [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The apparent narrowing of the miss from -21% (FY23) to -8% (FY24) could be read as improvement, but the mechanism is base-effect (FY23 was a catastrophic year), not structural reform of guidance methodology; the same interest/curtailment optimism persists [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Net verdict: Guidance accuracy is improving in absolute terms from the FY23 nadir but has not structurally reformed; the miss trajectory is narrowing but remains biased [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY24 EBITDA miss of -8% is materially better than FY23's -21% but still below the European renewables peer benchmark of ±5% as the acceptable guidance range [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY21-FY22 period showed modest EBITDA accuracy (±4%); the FY23 breakdown represents a step-change deterioration that has not been fully recovered [#J]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — structural driver: Business complexity increased materially after IPO (Jun 2021) as ANE-ES accelerated CapEx from ~€1.0bn/yr to ~€2.0bn/yr; higher installation rates multiply curtailment and grid-connection risks, both of which amplify guidance error [#A]

  • Sub: This is a structural, not cyclical, deterioration driver — the complexity of a rapidly growing 11GW+ fleet is inherently harder to forecast than a steady-state 7GW fleet

  • Volatility driver — macro: European energy price volatility (2022 spike, 2023 collapse) and ECB rate cycle (2022-2024) each contributed approximately 30-35% of total L4 guidance error; these are partially macro-driven but management's failure to scenario-plan guidance is an execution failure [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: ~35% of L4 guidance error attributable to execution factors: curtailment higher than modelled, grid connection delays, O&M cost creep; execution-driven errors are more concerning than macro-driven errors because they are within management's control [#I]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx guidance has been most accurate across L4 (average error <3%); management's project cost estimating is robust even when financial return translation is not [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: EPS has the widest guidance error of any tracked metric; FY23 saw a directional reversal (guided profit, delivered loss); EPS aggregates all upstream errors (EBITDA miss + D&A underestimation + interest underestimation) making it the most leveraged miss [#J]

  • Accuracy improving — caveat: Management has added explicit interest cost and curtailment disclosure in FY24 guidance language; this process improvement is positive but has not yet translated into a clean EBITDA delivery [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The apparent accuracy improvement from FY23 to FY24 may be partially cyclical — FY23 was so anomalous that any normal year looks better in comparison; the real test is FY25/FY26 when the business enters a new capital recycling phase with new financial complexity [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The guidance is analytically dissectible into three dominant assumption clusters — energy price realisation, interest cost, and curtailment — each of which has independently caused previous misses.

  • Net verdict: The FY25/FY26 guidance stack embeds three simultaneously optimistic assumptions on energy prices, refinancing costs, and curtailment; joint stress of even two of three would invalidate the EBITDA recovery thesis [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management's FY25 revenue guide appears to embed Spanish merchant power price of approximately €65-70/MWh for unhedged volumes; forward curve at time of Q13 research implies ~€55-60/MWh — a ~10% pricing optimism gap [#I]

  • Sub: ANE-ES hedges approximately 60-65% of output; unhedged tail carries meaningful price risk in a normalising energy market

  • Revenue assumption — customer / PPA: PPA book covers ~35% of total output with weighted average price of ~€55/MWh with 3-5 year remaining tenors; this portion of revenue guidance is credible and relatively stable [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Management assumes Iberian energy market remains structurally deficit in H2 2025; if hydro conditions normalise or new French interconnector capacity comes online, spot prices could fall faster than guidance assumes [#I]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Management guides O&M cost of approximately €25-28/MWh; industry benchmark for an 11GW+ fleet of this vintage is €27-32/MWh — guidance is at the optimistic end of the range [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Expert: Renewables Operations Specialist] flagged in a Mar 2025 call that ANE-ES's Spanish wind fleet is aging into a higher-maintenance phase, implying O&M cost creep of ~3-5% annually for 3-4 years

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage assumptions appear reasonable; management correctly identifies that incremental MW additions carry ~70% EBITDA conversion once fixed cost base is absorbed [#A]

  • EPS assumption — interest: The most fragile single assumption is the ~Euribor+150bps average blended cost embedded in FY25 guidance; with ~€2bn of project debt maturing in FY25-FY27, refinancing at current market rates (Euribor+200-220bps for BBB-rated project debt) adds ~€100-150m to interest cost relative to guidance [#A]

  • Sub: If Euribor stays at 2.5% and spread widens to +200bps, blended cost is ~4.5% vs embedded ~3.5%, representing ~€150m of EPS risk on a €5.5bn gross debt book

  • EPS assumption — share count: No dilution assumption required; share count is stable; the parent company ACCIONA owns ~72% and no convertible instruments are outstanding [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided at 22-25%; historically has come in at 18-22% due to Spanish accelerated depreciation credits; guidance is slightly conservative on tax — a modest positive offset [#I]

  • EPS assumption — minority interests: Minority interest deduction has been systematically underestimated in L2 periods; FY24 actual minority interest charge was ~€35m vs guidance-implied ~€20m; small in absolute terms but material at the EPS level given thin margins [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: ~85% of revenues are euro-denominated; no material FX risk in the guidance [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Steel and turbine costs have moderated; management assumes flat-to-down supply chain costs in FY25; this is broadly consistent with market data but assumes no re-escalation from trade tariff effects [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP/regulatory: Spanish regulatory framework assumed stable; Iberian politics carry non-trivial risk of retroactive renewable subsidies changes — happened in 2013 and the market assigns residual tail risk [#J]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Net verdict: Overall guidance credibility is MEDIUM-LOW; physical delivery credentials are genuine but financial guidance has been structurally over-optimistic, and the assumption stack still carries unreformed over-optimism [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-LOW — the qualitative framing is reasonable, the execution of the build programme is credible, but the financial translation of operational performance into EBITDA/EPS has a demonstrated systematic over-optimism problem [#J]

  • Red flag — recent miss: EBITDA missed initial guidance in 3 of 4 assessed periods; FY23 miss (-21%) is in the top quartile of European renewables peers for single-year guidance error magnitude; this is the primary credibility damage [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: Interest cost embedding of ~Euribor+150bps is 50-70bps below current project debt market pricing; management has not fully reset this assumption despite two consecutive misses [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: Iberian energy price forward curve sits ~10% below management's assumed revenue price; while hedges partially offset this, the unhedged tail is a meaningful risk given ~35-40% of volumes are merchant [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: Australian curtailment is a structurally recurring item; the grid investment lag in Queensland and New South Wales means curtailment events are likely to persist for 18-24 months even in a base case [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Expert: Australian Grid Infrastructure Specialist] cited in an Apr 2025 call that ANE-ES's Queensland assets face curtailment of 10-15% of potential generation through at least FY26 under current AEMO grid plans

  • Green flag — recent beat: CapEx delivery and MW installations are consistently accurate; the organisational capability to build and commission is not in question [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Tax rate guidance is slightly conservative (guided 22-25%, history suggests 18-22% realisation); a modest but genuine sandbag on the one line where management is structurally careful [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Operational availability of installed fleet consistently above 95%; asset quality is high and not a guidance risk [#A]

  • Margin of safety — low: At current valuation, the investment thesis is substantially predicated on achieving FY26 EBITDA of €1.0-1.1bn; a -10% guidance miss on EBITDA combined with the interest cost sensitivity would push net income back to near breakeven, significantly reducing terminal value [#J]

  • Sub: IRR sensitivity: a permanent EBITDA base 10% below management's FY26 target reduces IRR from the implied ~18-22% to approximately 10-13% — below Richard's 25%+ threshold

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment case is highly sensitive to the financial guidance being broadly achieved; there is not sufficient valuation discount at current prices to absorb a repeat of FY23-style guidance misses [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The credibility verdict of MEDIUM-LOW could deteriorate to LOW if Q1 2025 trading update (expected May/Jun 2025) shows any EBITDA guidance softening; the FY23 pattern of maintaining guidance through Q1-Q2 then cutting in Q3 is a specific recurrence risk [#J]
