Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. CVS Group operates a highly defensive integrated veterinary model where recurring subscription revenues and scale advantages in medicine procurement insulate it from consumer discretionary cycles.

Segments and Revenue Models

1.
Dominant clinical core: The group's Veterinary Practices division generated £616.10m in FY2025, contributing 88.90% of total group revenues through a nationwide network of primary care and referral hospitals
2.
Integrated laboratory services: The Laboratories segment generated £31.40m or 4.50% of group revenue in FY2025, providing essential diagnostic tests and in-practice desktop analyzers
3.
Digital e-commerce segment: Animed Direct, the online retail business, generated £45.90m or 6.60% of FY2025 revenue by selling prescription and non-prescription pet medicines directly to consumers
4.
Defensive subscription base: Recurring revenues are heavily driven by the Healthy Pet Club preventative care program, with 519,000 active members as of June 30, 2025, providing a stable cash stream of c.£80-90m annually
5.
Geographic revenue diversification: Australia generated £52.10m of revenue in FY2025 and rose to represent 10.00% of group revenue in H1 2026. This rapid international expansion provides a hedge against UK-specific headwinds

Value Chain and Competitive Advantage

6.
Favored surgeon positioning: Veterinary clinics occupy an exceptionally favorable position in the pet healthcare value chain because the diagnosing veterinary surgeon acts as the key decision-maker for treatments, creating extremely high customer stickiness
7.
Scale-driven economic moat: CVS Group leverages its unparalleled procurement scale to secure deep volume discounts on veterinary pharmaceuticals, a barrier that independent practices cannot replicate
8.
Consolidated market structure: The UK companion animal veterinary sector is c.60.00% consolidated, with CVS holding a c.9.00% national market share of practice sites
9.
Fragmented Australian runway: The Australian companion animal market represents a highly attractive, highly fragmented £2.60bn sector with a low corporate consolidation level of c.15.00%
10.
Other side of the story: Increased regulatory price transparency could mandate clearer pricing lists and itemized bills, which might encourage more customers to seek prescriptions online, eroding the high margins historically generated on in-practice medicine sales
