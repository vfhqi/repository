§1 — Current Management Guidance

CPS-PL's formal guidance is characterised by EBITDA-anchored range targets with limited granularity on EPS and FCF; the company has moved toward narrower bands over recent periods but retains meaningful caveats on spectrum costs and M&A.

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management has guided for FY2025 consolidated EBITDA of approximately PLN 3.85-3.95bn, broadly flat-to-modest growth vs FY2024 actuals of c.PLN 3.82bn; implies c.1-3% YoY growth [#A]

  • Sub-note: Guidance range is deliberately wide at 100bps, consistent with prior-year framing

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit revenue growth percentage target published for FY2025; management references "stable-to-modest growth" in bundles and B2B, consistent with historical framing that avoids pinning a top-line number [#A]

  • Sub-note: This absence of formal revenue guidance is a recurring pattern — see §3 for credibility implications

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided at PLN 1.6-1.8bn for FY2025, consistent with FY2024 CapEx of c.PLN 1.7bn; management has consistently framed fibre densification and 5G spectrum as multi-year spend commitments [#A]

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF target provided; management references "improving FCF generation" as the network investment cycle matures; this is non-quantified and carries historical miss risk — see §2 [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No formal EPS guidance; EPS is implicitly derivable from EBITDA minus guided D&A, interest (c.PLN 0.9-1.0bn annually at current rates), and tax; consensus-implied EPS c.PLN 0.85-0.95/share for FY2025 [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: Management's most recent multi-year framing (investor day language, FY2024 results call) targets EBITDA growth of low-to-mid single digits CAGR through 2027, anchored by B2B growth, broadband subscriber expansion, and cost synergy completion from Polkomtel integration [#A]

  • Sub-note: No specific N3Y EPS CAGR or FCF target has been quantified in public guidance

  • Medium-term — N2Y: Revenue implied to reach c.PLN 13.5-14.0bn by FY2026 vs FY2024 c.PLN 13.0bn, equating to low-single-digit CAGR; this is management's implicit assumption set, not a formal stated target [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Primary guidance vehicle is the quarterly results call; written guidance supplement issued at FY close and H1; no standalone investor day guidance published in the L18 months window [#A]

  • Update timing: Last formal guidance update at FY2024 results (March 2025); Q1 2025 call (May 2025) confirmed guidance without revision — a mild green flag given sector headwinds [#A]

  • Caveat: Management explicitly caveats guidance on: (1) spectrum auction timing and cost, (2) no material M&A, (3) macro stability in Poland (inflation, consumer spending), and (4) regulatory outcomes on wholesale broadband pricing [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The conspicuous absence of formal revenue and FCF guidance numbers — while presenting EBITDA ranges as the headline metric — may mask an aggressive embedded revenue-growth assumption that is self-consistent only if ARPU stabilisation and subscriber growth both materialise simultaneously; neither has historically delivered reliably [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

CPS-PL management is not a consistent sandbagger; the pattern is aspirational revenue framing offset by cost-side management delivering EBITDA proximity — an honest-tracker-with-topline-bias characterisation.

  • Pattern verdict: Mixed pattern — EBITDA honest-tracker, revenue/EPS consistent over-promiser; no evidence of systematic sandbagging on any metric across L4 [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — revenue: Revenue guidance set 2-4% above eventual delivery in every period; this is not randomness but structural: management consistently embeds ARPU recovery and subscriber growth assumptions that do not materialise at guided speed [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EPS: EPS guidance implicitly assumes interest cost stability and benign FX that has repeatedly proven wrong; management's guidance construction appears to anchor on a "normal-conditions" capital structure that the company's actual leverage profile renders aspirational [#J]

  • Honest tracker — EBITDA: EBITDA delivery within ±3% of guided range in 3 of 4 periods is consistent with a management team that has genuine operational visibility on the cost side and communicates that metric credibly [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal: No discernible sandbagging pattern on any metric; the company does not consistently beat guidance; absence of upward surprise is a material distinction from higher-credibility peers in European telecoms (e.g. Deutsche Telekom, Iliad, where sandbagging on EBITDA is documented) [#I]

  • Beat consistency: 0 of 4 periods showed revenue beat; 0 of 4 showed EPS beat; 0 of 4 showed FCF beat; EBITDA the only metric with near-delivery record [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Revenue average -2.7%; EPS average -15.1%; FCF average -19.6%; magnitudes are not trivial and are directionally consistent across periods [#A]

  • Pattern shift: CapEx guidance has improved (FY2024 near-perfect); if management is applying similar discipline to revenue framing in FY2025, some improvement is possible — but no confirmed structural shift yet [#A]

  • Improving accuracy — partial: FY2023 EPS miss (-8.5%) and FY2024 EPS miss (-8.2%) are smaller than FY2022 (-30%); improvement likely reflects rate stabilisation rather than better guidance discipline [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The cost-management skill that has preserved EBITDA proximity may be approaching limits as labour costs in Poland (real wage growth c.8-10% YoY in 2023-24) and energy costs structurally increase; if revenue over-promise continues AND cost buffers compress, EBITDA guidance loses its historical credibility anchor [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

Guidance accuracy has modestly improved on operational metrics (EBITDA, CapEx) but remains structurally weak on revenue and FCF; improvement is rate-environment-driven, not discipline-driven.

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: FY2023-24 EBITDA delivery within ±1% of mid-point — better than FY2021-22 where cost surprises from Polkomtel integration created larger variance [#A]

  • Recent accuracy — EPS: EPS miss narrowed from -30% (FY2022) to -8% (FY2023-24); improvement is primarily explained by interest rate stabilisation post-2023 peak rather than improved guidance construction [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FCF: FCF miss has NOT improved; FY2024 miss (-17.3%) is broadly similar to FY2021 (-15.3%) and FY2023 (-18.0%); structural CapEx overspend and working capital timing are persistent, not episodic [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — CapEx: CapEx guidance has clearly tightened; FY2024 <1% overspend vs FY2021 8%; suggests management's network rollout modelling has matured — green flag for FCF forecast credibility going forward [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — revenue: Revenue misses have not narrowed; FY2024 -3.7% was the largest of the L4 periods; deterioration driven by accelerating cord-cutting in legacy pay-TV, which management's guidance assumptions have consistently underweighted [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: FY2022 EPS volatility was predominantly macro (PLN/EUR FX + Euribor rate shock); not an execution failure — but management's capital structure choice (high EUR-denominated floating-rate debt) is itself a governance decision that creates this vulnerability [#J]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Revenue misses are execution-related (subscriber acquisition, ARPU management, bundle uptake) — not external shocks; this is a meaningful distinction for credibility assessment [#J]

  • Metric — best accuracy: EBITDA; consistently closest to guided range across all 4 periods [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF (structural -19.6% average miss); EPS second worst (-15.1% average miss) [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The apparent stabilisation of EPS miss magnitude in FY2023-24 is contingent on interest rates not rising again; if NBP maintains higher-for-longer or if EUR/PLN weakens, the FY2022-level EPS miss could recur without any change in management behaviour [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The guidance assumption stack has two load-bearing assumptions that are individually fragile and correlated: ARPU stabilisation and interest cost containment; simultaneous failure would compress both revenue and EPS by 15-25%.

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Management assumes subscriber net adds of c.+100-150k broadband per annum (FY2025-27) and pay-TV churn contained below 12% annualised; FY2024 actual broadband net adds c.+90k — slightly below assumption pace [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: ARPU recovery assumption embeds c.2-3% bundle price increase annually in B2C; achievable in inflationary environment but contested as Polish consumer real-income pressure eases and streaming alternatives intensify [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance explicitly excludes M&A; organic-only assumption is appropriate framing but limits upside optionality if consolidation accelerates in Polish market [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: B2B growth assumed at c.5-7% YoY; anchored in IT services and cloud convergence; this is the fastest-growing segment and arguably the most credible assumption given structural demand [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Guidance embeds personnel cost growth c.5-7% (below current Polish real wage inflation of c.8-10%); labour cost assumption appears optimistic given CPS-PL's unionised workforce and public-sector wage benchmarking pressures [#J]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operational leverage assumption requires revenue growth to materialise; if revenue grows 1% instead of 3%, EBITDA margin compresses c.60-80bps assuming fixed-cost base [#I]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Pricing assumption depends on CPS-PL maintaining bundle premium vs MVNOs and OTT; Netflix/Disney+ penetration in Poland now c.35-40% of broadband HHs — growing competitive pressure on pay-TV ARPU [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Management assumes interest cost c.PLN 0.9-1.0bn annually for FY2025-26; this requires no material refinancing risk and PLN/EUR stability; EUR/PLN at c.4.25 vs debt predominantly EUR-denominated [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed c.19-20% (Polish CIT); no material change assumed; straightforward and credible assumption [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count stable at c.857m shares; no buyback programme active; no dilution risk from convertibles; assumption is credible [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: PLN/EUR assumed stable; c.15% of EBITDA is directly affected by EUR/PLN via EUR-denominated programming costs and debt service; 5% PLN depreciation = c.PLN 50-75m EBITDA headwind [#I]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Guidance implicitly assumes WIBOR/EURIBOR at current levels; CPS-PL carries c.PLN 8-9bn floating-rate debt exposure; 100bps rate increase = c.PLN 85-90m additional annual interest cost, c.10% EPS headwind [#I]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — EBITDA guidance is credible based on L4 delivery; revenue, FCF, and EPS guidance carry material downside risk from persistent structural miss patterns.

  • Green flag — EBITDA execution: 3 of 4 L4 periods within EBITDA guidance range; cost management discipline is genuine and documented [#A]

  • Green flag — CapEx accuracy improving: FY2024 CapEx <1% overspend signals improving operational transparency on the network investment cycle [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative EBITDA framing: Management's stated EBITDA guidance range (PLN 3.85-3.95bn) is wide enough to accommodate modest revenue miss via cost offset; this is structurally conservative on the metric they can control [#J]

  • Red flag — revenue over-promise: ❌ Revenue missed in 4 of 4 L4 periods; structural, not episodic; management has not demonstrated it can guide revenue credibly [#A]

  • Red flag — FCF persistent miss: ❌ FCF missed in 4 of 4 L4 periods, average -19.6%; the metric most relevant to the thesis (cash generative business → deleveraging → re-rating) is the most consistently under-delivered [#A]

  • Red flag — leverage amplification: ❌ At c.4x EBITDA net leverage, any EBITDA miss is amplified at the EPS level by fixed interest costs; the credibility of EPS guidance is structurally limited by this leverage position [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: Labour cost guidance c.5-7% in a 8-10% real-wage-growth environment; if Poland's labour market remains tight, this assumption fails and EBITDA guidance requires material cost offsetting elsewhere [#J]

  • Margin of safety — FCF: If FCF guidance is off by 10% downside (i.e., FCF c.PLN 0.82bn vs implied c.PLN 0.91bn), the deleveraging narrative stalls and debt/EBITDA ratio remains above 3.5x — potentially affecting refinancing optionality and market re-rating thesis [#I]

  • Margin of safety — EPS: If EPS misses by 10% (i.e., EPS c.PLN 0.79-0.83 vs consensus c.PLN 0.88-0.92), the stock is trading at a P/E that embeds a growth premium not currently being earned; multiple compression risk is meaningful [#I]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis (deleveraging re-rating + FCF yield expansion) is highly sensitive to FCF delivery; FCF is the weakest guidance metric historically; this is the primary credibility tension for the thesis [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The "MEDIUM credibility" verdict is contingent on EBITDA holding; if revenue deterioration accelerates (cord-cutting + ARPU pressure) beyond the c.2-3pp management assumption, cost levers will be insufficient to defend EBITDA, and the credibility verdict collapses to LOW in a single reporting period [#J]

§9 — Forward Tracking Metrics + Sceptical Synthesis

The most credible bear case on CPS-PL guidance is not an EBITDA failure but a sustained FCF miss driven by CapEx persistence + revenue soft landing simultaneously eroding the deleveraging thesis that underpins the re-rating case.
