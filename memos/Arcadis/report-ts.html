Section 3 – What the company does 17 findings

Section summary
  1. Arcadis operates a resilient, diversified business model positioned in high-value segments of the engineering value chain with substantial switching costs and key client relationships
  2. The business is strategically shifting resources away from lower-margin traditional environmental services towards high-growth markets like data center design and grid modernization

Global Business Areas and Segments

1.
Resilience division overview: The Resilience division comprises 38% of net revenues and focuses on water optimization, climate adaptation, environmental restoration, and high-growth energy transition projects, representing the company's highest-margin segment compared to the group average
2.
Places segment focus: Representing 35% of revenues, Places designs advanced industrial facilities, social infrastructure, and smart buildings, serving technology, life sciences, and manufacturing clients, which remains stable compared to legacy real estate
3.
Mobility infrastructure solutions: Accounting for 27% of revenues, Mobility develops intelligent rail, connected highways, and maritime port systems for public and regulated transport authorities, representing stable public sector work compared to cyclical private markets
4.
Integrated digital offering: The Intelligence division, representing 3% of revenues, was integrated in 2026 into Mobility and other divisions to embed AI tools, moving away from the standalone segment reporting of 2025
5.
Rapidly growing subsegments: Inside Resilience, energy transition services grew by more than 29% in FY24 compared to flat growth in traditional environmental services, highlighting the rapid shift toward clean energy consulting

Value Chain and Revenue Model

6.
Consulting fee structure: Arcadis operates primarily as a professional services firm where approximately 60% of projects are structured as lump-sum contracts and 40% as time-and-materials, representing a higher share of fixed pricing compared to historical periods
7.
Favorable cash-generative position: Within the project lifecycle, Arcadis sits at the high-value design and feasibility phases, avoiding the high-risk physical execution phase that typically depresses construction contractors' margins
8.
Enduring client relationships: The revenue model is highly recurring with 95% of revenues in 2025 originating from clients that the company had already served in 2024, demonstrating strong client retention compared to the industry average
9.
Fragmented customer base: Customer concentration is exceptionally low as the largest single project represents less than 5% of net revenues and no single customer exceeds 10%, providing superior diversification compared to peer firms with concentrated public contracts
10.
Key client program: Arcadis services approximately 270 key accounts under a systematic management program, with these key clients contributing a substantial 65% of net revenues, up from 60% in prior years

Competitive Advantages and Market Structure

11.
Secular growth trends: The global engineering consulting market is valued at over $600bn annually and is highly fragmented with the top 20 global design firms controlling under 12% of the market
12.
High switching costs: Arcadis establishes durable competitive advantages through long-term regulatory compliance advisory, creating high switching costs that lead to repeat business rates above the sector median
13.
Substantial barriers to entry: The company's global scale of 34,136 professionals and global delivery model create significant scale barriers that smaller local engineering firms cannot match
14.
High-value credentialing barrier: Arcadis holds the number one European global design firm ranking, serving as a vital credential to win complex public tenders over smaller peers
15.
Other side of the story: A critical vulnerability is that revenue is entirely dependent on billable hours, meaning that any failure in employee utilization or project pricing immediately impairs margins relative to software-driven peers

Multi-Year Financial Performance

Earnings Quality and Balance Sheet

Section 5 – The possible investment case 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is rapidly strengthening due to secular tailwinds in energy transition, water adaptation, and digital twin services driving a highly predictable forward EPS trajectory
  2. Global market demand remains robust and structurally insulated from macro headwinds, underpinned by multi-year environmental regulations and public infrastructure spending programs

Key Structural Growth Drivers

1.
Rapidly expanding energy transition: Arcadis is capturing significant market share in electricity grid modernization and industrial decarbonization, which generated over €170M in FY24 revenues compared to legacy environmental consulting
2.
Substantial water optimization demand: Water and climate adaptation projects are experiencing strong tailwinds, with US municipal water and UK AMP8 infrastructure cycles driving positive backlog growth compared to legacy construction sectors
3.
High-value digital twin offerings: The company's Enterprise Decision Analytics platform and partnership with Nvidia Omniverse enable high-margin, recurring software and advisory revenues compared to low-margin transactional work
4.
Booming data center sector: The acquisition of German design leader KUA Group for €70M positions Arcadis to capture massive AI-driven data center infrastructure investments compared to weaker residential markets

Plausibility and Failure Modes of Drivers

5.
High transition market plausibility: The energy transition driver is highly plausible with expected annual sector growth of 20%+ compared to low-single-digit GDP growth, but would fail if grid operators slow capital deployment
6.
Credible water optimization path: The water optimization driver is highly plausible given rising regulatory requirements for lead pipe replacements, but would fail if public funding faces political gridlock compared to prior cycles
7.
Solid data center design outlook: The data center driver has high near-term visibility compared to weak office construction, but is vulnerable to delays if electrical grid constraints restrict power access
8.
Moderate digital growth conversion: The digital software driver has medium plausibility as clients slowly adopt new tools, but would fail if proprietary platforms lack scaling capability compared to peer offerings

Compound versus Independent Probabilities

9.
Independent driver pathways: The bull case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously, as water regulation, energy transition, and data center growth operate on independent funding cycles compared to correlated cyclical markets
10.
Highly predictable EPS outcome: If even two drivers fire together, the resulting operating leverage through GECs will accelerate EPS toward targeted mid-double digit growth compared to legacy low-margin performance
11.
Other side of the story: Slower-than-expected public procurement cycles and regulatory delays on the back of policy uncertainty in the US could stall contract awards, severely delaying EPS progression compared to stable peer execution

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Key risks to the earnings trajectory are manageable but require active oversight, particularly around professional talent retention and project delivery execution
  2. The business has implemented robust commercial and pricing controls to mitigate labor inflation and prevent unprofitable project wins that historically impaired profitability

Talent and Labor Cost Inflation

1.
Severe professional talent war: Arcadis operates in a highly competitive market where voluntary employee turnover of >10% poses a constant constraint to organic growth and margins compared to less labor-intensive industries
2.
Substantial cost structure pressure: Personnel expenses represent a massive 77% to 78% of net revenues, exposing Arcadis to significant profitability erosion if wage inflation cannot be passed to clients compared to peer firms with lower labor intensity
3.
Increasing GEC mitigation reliance: While the company is expanding its Global Excellence Centers to low-cost regions to reduce overhead, this transition cannot entirely solve local senior engineer shortages compared to historical local staffing models

Project Execution and Contractual Risks

4.
Extremely bespoke project portfolio: The company manages approximately 40,000 highly bespoke projects annually, providing very limited visibility and creating high project execution and contract loss risks compared to standardized service firms
5.
Significant fixed-price exposure: A major portion of project work is completed under fixed-price or lump-sum contracts, meaning project overruns or incorrect pricing flow directly to margin contraction compared to pure-play hourly consulting
6.
Persistent restructuring disruptions: Continuous cost-out and restructuring programs, including the reduction of another 250 roles in Q1 2026, could trigger operational disruption and lower morale compared to stable operational structures

Public Funding and Political Roadblocks

7.
High public sector exposure: With approximately 50% of revenues dependent on government contracts, Arcadis is highly vulnerable to prolonged procurement reviews and political spending delays compared to pure-play commercial peers
8.
Other side of the story: These material risks are substantially overstated because Arcadis' multi-year backlog and diversified international footprint provide strong resilience and insulation from any single-market downturn compared to localized competitors
