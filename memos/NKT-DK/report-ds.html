Section 2 – Consensus expectations immediately before the move 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Before the surge, the buy-side viewed 2026 as a transition year with flat EBITDA guidance and paused Transmission growth before capacity additions in 2027.
1.
Consensus EBITDA - modest expectation baseline: Sell-side consensus going into Q1 modeled 2026 operational EBITDA at EUR 399m, representing flat growth against the FY 2025 actual of EUR 390m
2.
Guidance comparison - alignment with cautious management: NKT guided to 2026 revenue of EUR 2.63-2.78bn and EBITDA of EUR 360-410m, which the market expected would limit near-term earnings acceleration
3.
Quarterly expectations - back-half weighted distribution: Consensus expected a slow start in Q1 with operational EBITDA of EUR 84m, reflecting a seasonal ramp-down of major legacy contracts
4.
Transmission segment - project transition pause: The market expected Transmission to contract organically by -1.5% in Q1, capturing the planned ramp-down of the high-margin Champlain Hudson project
5.
Backlog expectations - stable execution baseline: Order backlog was expected to remain stable at around the Q4 2025 level of EUR 10.2bn, with little expectation of major short-term additions
6.
Long-term expectations - conservative EBITDA targets: Published consensus modeled 2030 EBITDA at EUR 910m, barely exceeding the company's own baseline ambition of "more than EUR 900m"
7.
Differentiated research - early pricing power indicators: Lars Topholm of DNB Carnegie held a highly bullish outlier view, arguing that doubled revenue per cable kilometer was not yet modeled by consensus
8.
Other side of the story: Sceptics had anticipated that heavy investment ramp-up costs and rising plastic material costs would compress Q1 EBITDA margins below the 13.5% consensus expectation

Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 17 findings

Section summary
  1. NKT delivered a massive 15% operational EBITDA beat in Q1 2026 alongside a record EUR 13.5 billion backlog, signaling structural pricing power.

Earnings beat and segment delivery

1.
Operational EBITDA beat - margin mix outperformance: Operational EBITDA printed at EUR 97m, beating consensus of EUR 84m by 15%, representing the highest first-quarter result in company history
2.
Margin expansion - mix and execution surprise: Operational EBITDA margin reached 15.9% against consensus of 13.5%, driven by superior project execution and favorable project mix
3.
Grid Solutions beat - offshore repair tailwinds: Grid Solutions & Accessories EBITDA beat consensus by 24% to reach EUR 19m, fueled by high-margin offshore wind power cable repair works
4.
Distribution outperformance - medium-voltage grid demand: Distribution revenue beat consensus by 5% to reach EUR 212m, driven by robust demand in power distribution grids and data centers
5.
Revenue miss - lumpy turnkey scope reduction: Standard revenue missed consensus by 3% at EUR 610m, with organic growth down -4% due to Champlain Hudson project ramp-downs
6.
Non-allocated costs - operational hedging windfall: Unallocated costs saw a EUR 13m positive swing to EUR 6m, driven by foreign exchange hedge eliminations

Backlog explosion and market visibility

7.
Order backlog - sequential backlog surge: High-voltage order backlog jumped to EUR 13.5bn from EUR 10.2bn in Q4 2025, completely defying expectations of a stable transition quarter
8.
Order intake - historic booking volume: NKT secured over EUR 4.2bn in new firm orders during Q1, driven by the massive Eastern Green Link 3 and SSEN Scottish projects
9.
Project pricing doubling - structural margin expansion: Lars Topholm of DNB Carnegie reported average high-voltage contract value doubled from EUR 0.61m/km in 2019-2021 to EUR 1.21m/km post-2022
10.
Revenue capacity - long-term consensus mismatch: This pricing trend implies 2030 Solutions revenue capacity of EUR 3.5-4.2bn, dwarfing consensus expectations of EUR 2.7bn
11.
EBITDA upside - long-term consensus upgrade: DNB Carnegie's proprietary analysis indicates up to 20-40% upside to the 2030e consensus EBITDA of EUR 910m

Capacity expansion and execution progress

12.
Karlskrona expansion - machinery installation on track: High-voltage capacity additions in Karlskrona are progressing on schedule, with extrusion towers constructed and machinery installation underway
13.
Vessel construction - fleet upgrade timeline: NKT Eleonora cable-laying vessel construction is progressing, with the hull launched into water in April 2026, securing H1 2027 operations
14.
Free cash flow - capital investment drain: Free cash flow fell to negative EUR 92m, representing a heavy but scheduled capex outflow as capital employed rose to expand capacity
15.
Capital structure - hybrid bond refinancing: NKT successfully refinanced its green hybrid bond, securing EUR 149m in proceeds to maintain its conservative leverage targets
16.
Other side of the story: Management maintained 2026 guidance despite the Q1 beat, warning that the project ramp-down in Transmission will continue to weigh on organic growth through Q3 2026

Section 7 – Synthesis: most likely cause, counter-hypothesis, residual, decision 10 findings

Section summary
  1. NKT's re-rating was primarily caused by the EUR 4.2 billion backlog expansion, validating a structural upward shift in long-term pricing power.
1.
Primary driver - backlog step-change: The single most-likely cause of NKT's move was the sequential high-voltage backlog jump to EUR 13.5bn, proving unmatched long-term earnings predictability
2.
Plausibility ranking - pricing power dominance: We rank the doubled revenue capacity per kilometer as a highly plausible structural driver, while the Q1 earnings beat is medium-plausibility
3.
Peer re-rating plausibility - data center tailwinds: Sector-wide data center demand re-rating is a highly plausible secondary driver, as evidenced by Morgan Stanley's upgrades on Prysmian
4.
Low-plausibility candidate - Q1 EBITDA beat: We mark the Q1 operational EBITDA beat as low-plausibility, as the beat was heavily driven by lumpy, non-recurring offshore repair works
5.
Strongest counter-hypothesis - post-2028 overcapacity: The core threat to the thesis is that industry-wide high-voltage capacity expansions could create a supply-demand imbalance after 2028, compressing margins
6.
Unexplained residual - cleanup factor: Approximately 5% of NKT's share appreciation remains unexplained by specific backlog wins, likely representing broader clean-tech index fund inflows
7.
Outlier call-out - ignored cash generation potential: The market is ignoring that NKT is projected to generate EUR 2.7bn in net cash by 2030, creating massive potential for share buybacks
8.
Decision prompt - next research priority: Investors should immediately verify the average pricing-per-kilometer in upcoming offshore wind tenders to confirm the durability of the margin thesis
9.
Other side of the story: If global interest rates remain high, cash-constrained utilities could postpone major offshore grid interconnector upgrades, exposing NKT's backlog to cancellation risks
