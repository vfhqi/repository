BLUF

  • J Change forces verdict: strongly positive, multi-vector, structural. Prysmian sits at the intersection of grid modernisation, electrification, and digitalisation — three policy-backed, multi-decade tailwinds that are simultaneously accelerating. The Encore Wire acquisition ($4.2bn, July 2024) fundamentally altered geographic exposure, margin profile, and competitive moat. FY25 EBITDA hit a record €2.40bn at 14.2% margin. Transmission hit its 2028 EBITDA target three years early.

  • J The Transmission backlog is the single most important fact. €16-17bn backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility to 2028+. HV manufacturing capacity is entirely sold out through 2028-2029. Morgan Stanley calculates average pricing on new HV orders has effectively doubled over three years, from ~€0.80m/km in 2024 to a projected ~€1.45m/km by 2027. [AS: Morgan Stanley] This is a structurally capacity-constrained seller's market, not a cyclical upcycle.

  • J Encore Wire is the swing factor for medium-term margin trajectory. Post-acquisition, Prysmian controls ~25% of the US cable market. Vertical integration (domestic copper rod milling in Texas, 90% domestically sourced copper) creates a tariff shield and structural cost moat. US Section 232 tariffs on imported copper/aluminium cables are net strongly positive — Jefferies estimates importers would need to raise prices ~35% to offset proposed copper tariffs. [AS: Jefferies]

  • J Bear case is valuation, not fundamentals. At ~10-11x EV/EBITDA the stock is not cheap for an industrial. Morgan Stanley notes PRY trades at 15.2x EV/EBITA for 2026 estimates — a 13% discount to broader Capital Goods Electrical peers. [AS: Morgan Stanley] The question is not whether change is occurring — it is — but whether execution risk (three simultaneous acquisitions, two vessel builds, four factory expansions, €3.1bn debt) is adequately priced.

  • J Leadership transition is smooth and results-oriented. Battaini (CEO since April 2024) delivered record EBITDA, record profit, and significant deleveraging in his first full year. An expert call noted he is "even greater operational rigor" than his highly successful predecessor. [AS: Expert Call] FY26 guidance (€2.625-2.775bn EBITDA, €1.30-1.40bn FCF) implies continued step-up.

1. Company Description

Prysmian has transformed from a European cable manufacturer into a vertically integrated global infrastructure platform with dominant North American presence.

  • I Prysmian S.p.A. is the world's largest cable manufacturer by revenue. Headquartered in Milan. Listed on Borsa Italiana (PRY.MI). Market cap ~€25-27bn (April 2026). Over 33,000 employees, 107 manufacturing plants, ~30 R&D centres across 50+ countries. [AS: ARS 2025]

  • I Four business segments since January 2024: Transmission (submarine/underground HVDC/HVAC), Power Grid (MV/HV distribution), Electrification (industrial, construction, LV cables), Digital Solutions (optical fibre, telecom, connectivity).

  • I FY25 revenues: €19.65bn. Adjusted EBITDA: €2.40bn (14.2% margin). Net profit: €1.27bn. Organic revenue growth: +5.4%.

  • I Geographic pivot: North American revenues grew from €4.86bn in 2023 to €7.81bn in 2025. [AS: Canalyst Models] EMEA remains largest at €9.38bn.

  • I Transmission: FY25 revenues €3.26bn (+28.7% organic), EBITDA €582m, 18.3% margin. Hit 2028 EBITDA target in 2025 — three years early. Q4-25 margin crossed 20% threshold (20.9%). [AS: ARS 2025]

  • I Power Grid: FY25 revenues €3.81bn (+7.6% organic), EBITDA €480m, 14.4% margin.

  • I Electrification: Largest segment at €10.96bn FY25 revenue. I&C margins improving to 13.5% Q4-25. Specialties margins compressed -80bps YoY to 11.1%.

  • I Digital Solutions: €1.62bn FY25 revenue. EBITDA almost doubled in Q4-25 to €75m (18.3% margin). Returned to 8.4% organic growth Q4-25.

  • I Acquisitions: Encore Wire (July 2024, $4.2bn), Channell Commercial Corporation (June 2025, $950m+), Warren & Brown Technologies (late 2024, €36m), ACSM (February 2026, €169m), Xtera (2025, JV). [AS: M&A Screener]

  • I Fleet: 6 cable-laying vessels rising to 8 by 2028. €350m for two new vessels.

  • I Net debt: €3.10bn at FY25-end (down from €4.30bn). Leverage 1.3x. FY25 FCF: €1.17bn. Dividend: €0.90/share (+12.5% YoY).
