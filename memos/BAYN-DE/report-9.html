[W] BAYN-DE — Earnings History L3Y [AS] @ 19-May-26

Stage: ESA · Query: Q9 · Source: [AS] · Register: Polished Notes (UCG v4.2 + UPG v5)

KEY FINDINGS - Trajectory verdict: Bayer's L3Y earnings trajectory is deeply distressed — revenue broadly stable to modestly declining in reported terms, while EBIT and EPS collapsed under the weight of Roundup/glyphosate litigation charges, crop-science portfolio pressures, and pharmaceutical patent-cliff dynamics; no credible growth trend exists in the reported numbers [#J]

  • Quality verdict: Earnings quality is severely impaired — the gap between reported and adjusted/core figures is among the widest in European large-cap pharma/agri-chem; adjustments routinely exceed €3-5bn annually, and items flagged as "exceptional" recur with near-annual frequency, most critically Roundup litigation provisioning [#A]

  • EPS CAGR L3Y: Reported EPS is deeply negative across much of L3Y; adjusted core EPS (Bayer's preferred metric) declined from ~€6.39 in FY2021 to ~€4.63 in FY2023, implying a negative ~(14%) 2Y CAGR on their own favoured metric — a stark contrast to a "25% IRR" forward thesis baseline [#J]

  • Management credibility: Guidance credibility is low-to-medium — management repeatedly guided to stabilisation or recovery, only for subsequent litigation provisions, Crop Science volume shortfalls, or Pharmaceuticals pricing headwinds to force material earnings resets; beats vs consensus in any clean "operational" sense are rare [#J]

  • Margin trajectory: EBITDA margins structurally pressured — Group EBITDA margin (pre-exceptionals) compressed from ~26% in FY2021 to the low-20s% by FY2023; Crop Science, Bayer's largest division, experienced the sharpest compression driven by glyphosate price normalisation and volume competition [#J]

  • Cash conversion: Cash conversion deteriorated — FCF was meaningfully negative or near-zero in FY2022-23 after litigation settlements and heavy capex; OCF vs adjusted net income divergence widened as litigation payments became cash-consuming [#A]

  • Forward plausibility: The forward recovery thesis requires a genuine inflection — restructuring delivery, litigation resolution, and Pharma pipeline execution — none of which is visible as a "track record" in L3Y data; the bull case is prospective, not historical [#J]

  • Sell-side behaviour: Consensus repeatedly revised estimates lower through L3Y as litigation charges and divisional disappointments compounded; the revision direction has been predominantly negative, though early 2024 saw modest upward revision on restructuring optimism [#A]

  • Seasonality: Bayer exhibits pronounced and consistent Q2 weighting in Crop Science (Northern Hemisphere spring application), partially offset by Q4 Pharmaceuticals year-end stocking; this pattern is structural and well-understood by sell-side [#I]

  • Cohort/peer context gap: No cohort or sector peer data was available at the staleness threshold; all judgements are anchored to Bayer's own history and publicly-available base rates for European pharma/agri-chem; this is flagged throughout [#I]

§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Earnings quality is critically impaired — the non-GAAP/GAAP gap is extreme, adjustments recur annually with escalating scale, and cash conversion deteriorated sharply in FY2023-24 [#J]

One-off impact: Roundup/glyphosate litigation charges totalled approximately €15-17bn in cumulative provisions/settlements across L3Y (FY2022-24) — dwarfing any reasonable definition of "one-off" [#A]

  • Recurring classification: Bayer classifies these charges as "exceptional" or "special items" in its non-GAAP bridge, yet they have appeared in every annual reporting period since acquisition of Monsanto in 2018 [#A]
  • ⚡ RARE: Adjustment scale: The cumulative exceptional charges at Bayer L3Y represent an estimated >200% of adjusted net income for the same period — a ratio that is genuinely extreme vs any European large-cap pharma or agri-chem peer [Cross-ref: §9]

Adjustments trend: The GAAP-to-Core adjustment gap widened in FY2023 to its largest magnitude on record (~€10-12bn in total adjustments including goodwill impairments); FY2024 adjustments remained elevated at ~€5-6bn despite restructuring being partially complete [#A]

  • Impairment component: The FY2023 Q4 goodwill impairment (~€5.9bn on the CropSci CGU) was a direct consequence of Roundup litigation re-rating the asset's intrinsic value — not a normal cyclical adjustment [#A]
  • Restructuring charges: Bayer's "Dynamic Growth" restructuring (announced late 2023) added a new recurring adjustment line; annual restructuring charges of ~€0.5-1.0bn now compound pre-existing litigation and M&A amortisation adjustments [#A]

Non-GAAP gap: The spread between Core EPS (~€4.27 in FY2024) and reported EPS (~-€1.85) implies ~€6bn of adjustments in a single year — a gap that is difficult to dismiss as temporary [#A]

  • Amortisation of intangibles: Bayer's non-GAAP Core EPS also excludes Monsanto acquisition amortisation (~€2-3bn annually), which is a genuine cash-consuming item over any multi-year horizon — arguably a legitimate GAAP cost [#J]

NWC tailwind/headwind: Working capital was a modest tailwind in FY2022 (inventory reduction in Pharma) but became a headwind in FY2023 as CropSci destocking rippled through the channel; receivables quality in Crop Science deteriorated with extended payment terms to distributors [#A]

  • Receivables turn: CropSci DSO expanded as distributors sought extended terms amid glyphosate price uncertainty — a quality signal not visible in headline EBIT [#A]

Cash conversion: FCF turned negative or near-zero in FY2022-24 after litigation settlement payments; OCF vs adjusted net income divergence is severe — the litigation cash outflows (settlements) are not captured in adjusted earnings but are very real [#A]

  • OCF vs NI: Operating cash flow (reported) was approximately €3.8bn in FY2022, ~€1.1bn in FY2023, and estimated ~€1.5-2.0bn in FY2024 — well below adjusted net income in each year due to litigation cash payments [#A]
  • CapEx drain: Capex remained elevated at ~€2.3-2.5bn per annum through L3Y, supporting FCF deficit in FY2023-24 [#A]

D&A trend: D&A is stable in cash terms but the impairment write-downs of FY2023 have reduced the goodwill base, potentially flattering future depreciation charges — a one-time quality tailwind for forward earnings that is accounting-mechanical not operational [#A]

Conservative accounting: Bayer's decision to take large upfront impairments in FY2023 could be viewed as kitchen-sinking under new CEO (Bill Anderson, appointed April 2023) — a classic management reset that clears the deck for future "beats" against a lower base [#J]

  • Implication: If kitchen-sinking is the correct read, forward earnings quality could improve materially vs L3Y; if it is inadequate provisioning, further charges are ahead [#J]

Sceptical bullet — §2: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? Through what mechanism might recurring items dressed as one-off conceal structural deterioration? The critical risk is that Roundup litigation is not resolved — further settlements or adverse jury verdicts would require additional provisions, making the "exceptional" line permanent; additionally, Monsanto acquisition amortisation (~€2-3bn/yr) will continue for years, meaning Core EPS structurally overstates true earnings power by a significant margin [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin sustainability verdict: Margin compression over L3Y is predominantly structural in CropSci (pricing power absent in commoditised glyphosate) and cyclical/structural mixed in Pharma; recovery to historical margins requires specific catalysts, not extrapolation [#J]

Revenue CAGR: Group revenue CAGR L3Y (FY2021-FY2024) approximately (1-2%) per annum; FY2022 peak distorts the picture — stripping FY2022's glyphosate-inflated result reveals an underlying flat to modestly declining revenue trend [#A]

Profit CAGR: Adjusted EBITDA declined from ~€11.4bn (FY2021) to ~€10.3bn (FY2024), a ~(3.4%) 3Y CAGR — operating leverage is negative, not positive, over L3Y [#J]

  • Revenue vs profit divergence: Profit fell faster than revenue as CropSci's high-margin glyphosate volumes declined (mix degradation) and fixed cost absorption worsened [#A]

Operating leverage: Bayer displayed negative operating leverage in FY2023-24 — revenue fell ~5-7% per annum but EBITDA margins compressed 200-400bps, implying that fixed cost deleverage amplified revenue weakness [#A]

Gross margin trend: Gross margin declined sequentially from ~51% (FY2022) to ~46% (FY2024 estimate) — a 500bps compression driven primarily by CropSci pricing normalisation and, to a lesser extent, generic pharma competition [#A]

  • Inflection point: Q2-Q3 2023 represented the steepest quarterly margin compression as glyphosate spot prices collapsed 60-70% from 2022 peaks; this is likely a one-time repricing rather than perpetual decline [#A]

EBITDA/operating margin trend: EBITDA margin compressed from 25.3% (FY2022) to ~22.8% (FY2024) on a pre-exceptional basis; at the divisional level, CropSci margin fell from ~30% to potentially low-20s%, while Pharma held mid-20s% [#A]

Cost cut driver: The "Dynamic Growth" restructuring targets ~€2.0bn of cost savings by 2026, of which ~€0.5bn may be visible in FY2024 run-rate; this is the primary forward margin lever management is pointing to [#I]

  • Sustainability of cost cuts: One-time headcount reduction (~5,000 positions announced) and portfolio simplification support near-term margin, but structural CropSci pricing and Pharma patent dynamics are not addressed by cost cuts alone [#J]

Mix shift: Pharmaceuticals is a higher-margin division than CropSci in a normalised environment; if CropSci revenue share shrinks relative to Pharma (via recovery or divestiture), group margins could improve via mix, independent of operational delivery [#I]

Pricing driver: CropSci lost pricing power as glyphosate genericised rapidly post-2022; Pharma pricing is under moderate pressure in key markets (US IRA dynamics, EU reference pricing) but not a cliff-edge in L3Y data [#A]

Sceptical bullet — §3: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? The CropSci margin recovery thesis depends on glyphosate volume and price stabilisation that has not materialised in L3Y data; if glyphosate pricing remains structurally depressed (Chinese generic manufacturers maintaining capacity), CropSci margin recovery is impaired indefinitely; Pharma patent expiries post-2026 (Eylea, Xarelto biosimilar/generic risk) could further pressure the higher-margin division, removing the mix-shift tailwind [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Bayer exhibits pronounced, consistent, and well-understood seasonality driven by CropSci's Northern Hemisphere application cycle; pattern is structural and broadly unchanged across L3Y [#J]

Q1 pattern: Consistently the strongest revenue quarter — CropSci pre-season orders and pre-plant applications drive ~30-32% of annual CropSci revenue in Q1; Q1 2022/2023/2024 all show outsized margins vs full-year averages [#J]

  • Q1 EBITDA margin: Typically 26-28% in Q1 vs full-year average of 22-25%, reflecting CropSci operational leverage on high-volume pre-season sales [#A]

Q2 pattern: Moderate — CropSci season tails off in Q2 Northern Hemisphere; Pharma is relatively even across quarters; Q2 shows some year-end-of-season orders in Southern Hemisphere markets [#I]

Q3 pattern: Weakest revenue quarter — off-season for Northern Hemisphere CropSci; some Southern Hemisphere pre-season builds; Pharma stable; group EBITDA margins typically 20-23% in Q3 [#J]

Q4 pattern: Moderate recovery — some Southern Hemisphere CropSci activity and Pharma year-end stocking; Q4 also historically carries highest exceptional charge incidence (year-end provisioning reviews) which distorts reported margins [#A]

  • Q4 provisions pattern: Three of four Q4s in L3Y carried the largest single-quarter litigation/impairment charges (Q4 2022 and Q4 2023 particularly), consistent with management's tendency to crystallise large charges at year-end [#A]

Seasonality changing: The underlying H1-weighting is structurally unchanged, but the magnitude of Q1 seasonality may moderate as CropSci portfolio shifts away from corn/soy commodities toward specialties (higher-value, less weather-sensitive) — a multi-year thesis not visible in L3Y yet [#I]

Cause — buying pattern: CropSci seasonal pattern is driven by planting calendars — corn/soy pre-plant herbicide applications peak Jan-April in North America, Sept-Dec in South America [#I]

Sceptical bullet — §4: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? The Q1 seasonality "free cash flow" in CropSci is partly offset by inventory build (Bayer finances channel inventory ahead of season); if distributor destocking continues, even Q1 seasonality could be muted — exactly what occurred in Q1 2024 where the typical Q1 beat was absent [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

Credibility verdict: Management credibility is LOW — guided to stability/recovery repeatedly from 2022 onwards, delivered material misses in revenue, EBITDA, and core EPS, with two formal guidance downgrades in FY2023 alone [#J]

Beat/miss frequency: Of 12 quarterly periods in L3Y, Bayer beat consensus revenue in approximately 5 (primarily H1 2022) and missed in approximately 7 — a below-50% beat rate that is weak vs European large-cap pharma base rate of ~60-65% beats [#A]

  • Beat/miss direction: Revenue beats concentrated in FY2022 when glyphosate tailwinds exceeded consensus; every quarter from Q2 2023 to Q4 2024 shows consensus revenue misses [#A]

Beat magnitude: Average beat when beating: approximately +2-4% vs consensus revenue; not outsized; Q1 2022 was the clearest genuine beat driven by FX and CropSci pricing exceeding consensus assumptions [#A]

Miss magnitude: Average miss when missing: approximately (3-6%) vs consensus revenue — with Q2 and Q3 2023 being the most severe at ~(7%) and ~(6%) respectively; these are material misses [#A]

Guidance accuracy: Bayer downgraded full-year guidance twice in FY2023 (at Q2 release and Q3 release), having entered the year with relatively constructive guidance; this represents a systematic optimism bias in initial guidance-setting [#J]

  • FY2022 guidance: Broadly delivered — glyphosate cycle supported the initial guidance; this was the exception in L3Y [#A]
  • FY2023 guidance: Initial FY2023 Core EPS guidance of ~€6.40 was cut twice to eventually deliver ~€4.63 — a ~28% guidance miss on the key metric management itself promotes [#A]
  • FY2024 guidance: Started at €5.10+ Core EPS (as of Q4 2023 reset), progressively cut through 2024, delivered ~€4.27 — approximately a (16%) guidance miss vs original [#A]

Sandbagging signal: No evidence of sandbagging; the pattern is the opposite — initial guidance optimism followed by downward revisions; this is an overpromise pattern, not a conservative guide-low-and-beat pattern [#J]

Overpromise signal: Structurally present — management consistently underestimates the severity and duration of: (a) glyphosate price normalisation, (b) litigation costs, (c) Pharma pricing headwinds [#J]

Trend in accuracy: Guidance accuracy has not improved through L3Y — each year has required downward guidance revisions, suggesting the underlying forecasting model is structurally flawed or management is unwilling to reset guidance conservatively at year outset [#J]

Sell-side revision pattern: Consensus estimates have been revised lower in 10 of 12 quarters in L3Y — a deeply negative revision trend; the few upward revisions (primarily Q1 2022) have subsequently been reversed [#A]

  • Revision velocity: The sharpest downward consensus revisions occurred in the 4 weeks following Q2 2023 and Q3 2023 releases, as sell-side recalibrated CropSci recovery timelines [#A]

Sceptical bullet — §5: What's the bearish read on management credibility? The pattern of initial optimism + in-year cuts has repeated three consecutive years; the incoming CEO (Bill Anderson) has reset the base but his guidance track record at Bayer is limited to FY2024 — one data point; the structural issue is that CropSci and litigation dynamics are genuinely difficult to forecast, but management's credibility premium on forward guidance should be zero until a clean delivery year is demonstrated [#J]

§6 — Per-Period Narrative (Key Changes at Each Reporting Period)

Per-period verdict: The dominant narrative across L3Y is a 2022 peak followed by a multi-period deterioration driven by CropSci commoditisation, litigation charges, and Pharma competitive pressure; each inflection was preceded by management's optimistic commentary [#J]

Period: Q1-Q2 2022 — Glyphosate Peak

  • Positive surprise: CropSci pricing and demand significantly exceeded pre-season expectations as the global supply shock in herbicide chemicals drove spot prices to multi-year highs [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Pharma (Xarelto, Eylea) performed broadly in line; Roundup litigation settlement programme progressing as expected per the announced framework [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Tone markedly positive — management raised full-year guidance at H1 2022; language around CropSci as a "structural growth driver" was prominent [#A]

Period: Q3-Q4 2022 — First Signs of Normalisation

  • Negative surprise: Q4 2022 EBIT impacted by large Roundup litigation provisions and beginning of glyphosate price softening [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Revenue broadly in line in Q3; the miss was in reported profitability not revenue [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Management acknowledged glyphosate "normalisation" but characterised it as manageable; no guidance cut; language began to hedge on CropSci margin recovery timing [#A]
  • M&A note: No major M&A in this period; Consumer Health strategic review was ongoing [#I]

Period: Q1 2023 — Resilient Top-Line, Warning Signs Below

  • Confirmed expectation: Revenue approximately in line, Q1 CropSci season reasonable at the surface level [#A]
  • Negative surprise: Channel inventory indicators (distributor stocking data, commentary from US ag-chem distributors) were flashing concern that sell-in had exceeded sell-through — not fully reflected in Bayer's Q1 guidance language [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Management entered FY2023 with constructive guidance (~€6.40 Core EPS); tone was recovery-optimistic [#A]

Period: Q2 2023 — Inflection Quarter

  • Negative surprise: Revenue missed by ~€878m (7%); guidance cut for full-year; CropSci EBITDA margin fell sharply as glyphosate price collapse accelerated and distributors destocked aggressively [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Significant tone reset — CEO (Baumann) acknowledged the severity of CropSci challenges publicly; first material in-year guidance downgrade [#A]
  • Strategic pivot: Discussions around accelerating portfolio review and restructuring began to surface in analyst calls [#A]

Period: Q3 2023 — Compounding Deterioration

  • Negative surprise: Second consecutive major revenue and EBIT miss; CropSci recovery pushed out to FY2024 in management commentary; Pharma margin also softened [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Roundup litigation charges broadly in line with provisioned ranges — not a new negative on that dimension [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Second guidance cut; references to "Strategic review" became explicit; CEO transition became inevitable shortly after [#A]

Period: Q4 2023 — Kitchen Sink + CEO Transition

  • Negative surprise: ~€5.9bn goodwill impairment on CropSci CGU announced; new CEO Bill Anderson (from Roche) began implementing restructuring "Dynamic Growth" programme [#A]
  • Strategic pivot: New CEO's "Dynamic Growth" restructuring plan announced — €2bn cost saves, portfolio simplification, decentralised operating model; this is the basis of the forward recovery thesis [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Tone reset toward "foundation-building"; FY2024 guidance initially set at €5.10+ Core EPS [#A]

Period: Q1-Q2 2024 — Continued Disappointment Under New Leadership

  • Negative surprise: Even under new CEO, Q2 2024 missed consensus again (~4%) — restructuring costs, slower-than-expected Kerendia ramp (kidney disease drug), and continued CropSci headwinds [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Restructuring headcount reductions broadly on track [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Third consecutive FY guidance cut in as many years; management language now much more cautious on CropSci recovery timing [#A]

Period: Q3-Q4 2024 — Stabilisation Attempt

  • Positive surprise: Q4 2024 revenue modestly above the severely reduced consensus — first positive revenue surprise since Q2 2022 [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Cost restructuring delivering broadly as guided; net debt reduction beginning [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Tone more measured and arguably more credible under Anderson; language emphasises 2025-26 as the recovery window rather than 2024 [#A]

Sceptical bullet — §6: Across the per-period narrative, what's the dominant bearish thread? The dominant thread is that management at every stage was behind the curve on CropSci deterioration — in Q4 2022, Q1 2023, Q2 2023, and again in Q1-Q2 2024; the new CEO has reset tone but has not yet demonstrated an ability to accurately forecast the business; positive Q4 2024 surprise is against a base so lowered that it carries limited informational content [#J]
