Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. A structural shift toward high-margin sovereign defense contracting and the scale-up of Indra Space underwrite a powerful earnings trajectory.

Key Growth Engines and Core Drivers

1.
Sovereign procurement supercycle: Spain's commitment to spend 2.00% of GDP on defense by 2025 translates into a massive domestic addressable market, directly driving the €11.33 billion backlog
2.
Space business monetization: The creation of Indra Space, targeting €1.00 billion in revenue by 2030, builds a vertically integrated satellite communications operator with high margin potential
3.
AI and digital solutions: Scaling IndraMind from €300.00 million in FY25 to €700.00 million in FY28 allows Minsait to shift its mix toward high-margin proprietary software and exit low-margin labor arbitrage
4.
Tactical SDR radio procurement: The partnership with Bittium for the €770.00 million Spanish tactical Software Defined Radio contract positions Indra to capture dominant local market share

Driver Plausibility and Fail Mechanisms

5.
Plausibility of defense drivers: Plausibility is exceptionally high, backed by €13.80 billion of committed awards under Spain's Special Modernization Programs, securing long-term revenue streams
6.
Plausibility of space drivers: Plausibility is medium, as Hispasat and Hisdesat provide a stable €2.00 billion secured backlog but face intense capital expenditure requirements and execution risks
7.
Primary failure mechanisms: The drivers would fail if procurement cycles suffer bureaucratic delays or if talent shortages constrain the group's manufacturing scale-up
8.
Independent growth drivers: The bull case does not require perfect execution across all segments, as the defense supercycle and ATM growth are structurally insulated from IT services cyclicality
9.
Other side of the story: The cancellation of the French-German FCAS fighter program in June 2026 demonstrates that multinational defense projects carry high political risks that can suddenly deflate long-term backlog options
