Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Market share gains in radio network hardware support Ericsson's core business, although its aggressive expansion into enterprise communication software remains heavily loss-making

Core Product Segments and Revenue Mix

1.
Dominant Networks division: Business Area Networks comprises 67.00% of Q1 2026 sales, delivering highly advanced radio access network hardware, mobile base stations, and massive MIMO antenna systems
2.
Transitioning Software division: Business Area Cloud Software and Services accounts for 24.00% of sales, providing core network software, business support systems, and managed network operations
3.
Loss-making Enterprise unit: Business Area Enterprise represents 8.00% of sales, focusing on private 5G networks, wireless WAN solutions, and the developer-focused Vonage communications platform
4.
Lucrative licensing stream: Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are highly profitable, leveraging over 60,000 granted patents to generate a steady, high-margin annual licensing run-rate near SEK 13.00B
5.
Contractual revenue mix: Revenue is predominantly earned through multi-year hardware supply agreements, software licensing contracts, and long-term recurring managed service agreements
6.
Fixed Wireless growth: Strong rollout of Fixed Wireless Access in India and the US drives incremental radio coverage and mid-band network core sales

Competitive Position and Industry Structure

7.
Consolidated industry oligopoly: Ericsson operates in a highly consolidated global market, maintaining a dominant 37.00% share of the RAN market outside of mainland China
8.
Substantial switching barriers: Competitive advantage is secured by high switching costs, as proprietary radio software interfaces historically locked operators into single-vendor configurations
9.
Extreme customer concentration: Customer concentration is incredibly high, with the ten largest telecom operators representing 46.00% of net sales in FY2025
10.
Anchor operator dependence: Reliance on key customers has intensified, with the largest customer, AT&T, growing from 8.00% of net sales in FY2023 to 14.00% in FY2025
11.
Eroding value chain: The company sits in a profit-favored position for physical RAN hardware, but faces long-term risks as cloud providers encroach on core network computing
12.
Varying market demand: While mature 5G markets in North America plateau, demand is expanding in South East Asia, India, and North East Asia
13.
Growing data consumption: Long-term growth is supported by expansion of mobile data subscriptions, projected to reach 6.30 billion global 5G connections by 2030
14.
Other side of the story: Rapid open architecture disaggregation could allow operators to mix-and-match third-party software with cheap, commoditized white-box radio units, destroying Ericsson's consolidated gross margin structure
