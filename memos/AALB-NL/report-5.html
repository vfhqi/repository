§1 — Executive Summary (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

FY2024 results confirmed a cyclical trough in Aalberts's building-exposed segment with margin compression materialising as expected, leaving the thesis contingent on a 2025-2026 construction cycle recovery that management could not date with confidence.

  • J-front verdict: FY2024 was an in-line-to-slight-miss against already-reset expectations; the earnings cycle neither broke the thesis nor provided a recovery catalyst [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Revenue of ~€3.15bn came in broadly at consensus (consensus ~€3.10-3.18bn range per public data); EBITA of ~€460m at ~14.6% margin was at the lower end of the expected range [#A]

  • Sub: No material positive surprise on any headline metric; result best described as "confirmation of weakness, not acceleration of recovery"

  • Sub: EPS (adjusted) approximately €2.85-2.95, down ~12-15% vs FY2023 — in line with the downward revision path established through H2-2024

  • Key surprise: Management's refusal to reinstate a forward EBITA margin target was the single most negative signal — prior Aalberts communications style included directional margin goals [#J]

  • Sub: Market had tentatively expected a floor confirmation at ~14.5% and a 2025 target of ~15.0-15.5%; neither was offered

  • Sub: This vagueness is consistent with management genuinely lacking visibility, not tactical sandbagging — a meaningful distinction for thesis confidence

  • Magnitude: The EBITA margin miss vs informal ~15% expectation was ~40bps — modest in isolation but compounding on a 240bps two-year compression already priced in [#A]

  • Forward guide change: FY2025 guidance language: "moderate organic growth" + "margin improvement" — no numbers; prior guidance style (e.g. FY2022 CMD targets) was more quantified [#A]

  • Sub: "Moderate organic growth" is likely low-single-digit % — consistent with a mild construction recovery but not a V-shaped bounce

  • Consensus revision: Post-FY2024 results, sell-side nudged FY2025 EBITA estimates down ~3-5% and revenue estimates marginally (per public consensus trackers); no significant FY2026 revision [#A]

  • Analyst tone: Broadly neutral-to-cautious — no ratings upgraded; one or two brokers (Kepler Cheuvreux, ING) trimmed price targets modestly; Berenberg maintained constructive stance citing trough valuation [#A]

  • Investment implication: Results confirm Aalberts is in a mid-cycle building construction downturn with APS providing partial offset; balance sheet is not stressed and the franchise is intact, but the recovery timing remains the thesis's critical unknown [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The apparent in-line delivery may mask a deteriorating forward outlook — management's shift to vague guidance language could signal that even modest H2-2025 recovery assumptions embedded in consensus are at risk of downgrade if European residential construction remains depressed through mid-2025

  • Gross margin: Not separately disclosed (Aalberts reports EBITA as primary margin metric); estimated gross margin declined in BIS on volume deleverage, stable/slightly contracted in APS [#A]

  • Sub: Aalberts's vertically integrated manufacturing model means gross margin is sensitive to volume throughput — semi-fixed manufacturing costs spread over lower volume = margin dilution

  • EBIT margin — trend:

  • Margin direction: Two consecutive years of ~120bps compression — a persistent, not one-off, deterioration; total compression from peak is ~240bps [#A] ❌

  • Decomposition: BIS volume deleverage primary driver (~200bps); SG&A absorption (~40bps); partially offset by APS mix benefit (~20-30bps offset) [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Negative operating leverage in BIS — revenue down 4-5% but EBITA margins down more proportionally, confirming fixed cost base is not being flexed quickly enough [#A]

  • Sub: Aalberts has historically guided to positive operating leverage on the way up; the asymmetry (faster margin compression on the way down) is a structural feature of speciality manufacturing

  • D&A trend: D&A elevated by accumulated M&A amortisation; PPA charges ~€60-80m/year persistent — amortisation of Aalberts IPS and prior acquisitions; not declining materially [#I]

  • Sub: EBIT (reported, post-PPA) significantly below EBITA (adjusted) — reported EBIT margin estimated ~10-11%, EBITA margin ~14.6%; the gap is M&A history

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A as % of revenue likely edged higher in FY2024 on revenue decline — cost actions taken but structural cost base is not rapidly variable [#A]

  • One-off costs: Modest restructuring charges in FY2024 (estimated <€20m) — headcount actions in BIS to rightsize for lower volume; not a large one-off item [#A]

  • Sub: Aalberts does not aggressively restructure in downturns — management philosophy is to retain skilled trades workforce and absorb near-term margin pain; a double-edged quality

  • Adjusted vs reported: EBITA-to-EBIT gap of ~400bps is structurally persistent and growing with M&A history — investors anchoring on reported EBIT will systematically underestimate operational profitability [#I]

  • Sub: Non-GAAP adjustments are not shrinking — Aalberts's serial M&A strategy means PPA amortisation is a permanent feature, not a fading one-off

  • One-off benefit: No material gains on disposal or one-off benefits in FY2024 — EBITA margin compression is clean, not obscured by benefits [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The margin floor assumption embedded in consensus (~14.5% for FY2024 exit, recovering to ~15.5% by FY2025) requires both volume recovery AND operating leverage to materialise simultaneously — if construction volumes recover only partially (say +1-2% instead of +4%), EBITA margins may trough at ~14.0-14.3%, implying further EPS downside not yet in consensus
