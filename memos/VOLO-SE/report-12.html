  • Switching cost — qualification: In technical services sub-segments, customer qualification of a new provider involves site visits, protocol testing, and contract re-negotiation — a 3-6 month cycle consistent with Nordic B2B services norms [#A]

  • Switching cost — integration: Trade distribution subsidiaries face lower integration lock-in — customers can substitute trade suppliers within a catalogue cycle; switching cost is primarily search cost and relationship disruption rather than technical [#J]

  • Pricing pass-through: Management has communicated ability to pass through input cost increases across most subsidiaries; reported EBITA margin expansion in 2021-2023 period during commodity inflation cycle is partial evidence of pass-through [#J]

  • Sub: Base rate for Nordic niche industrials passing through cost inflation: ~60-75% of input cost increases passed within 2 quarters [#A]

  • Customer pushback: No disclosed instances of material customer pushback or price renegotiation; absence of negative commentary in earnings calls is consistent with niche pricing power but also reflects self-reported management narrative [#A]

  • Renewal pricing: Redeye commentary suggests subsidiaries maintain pricing at renewal, with modest annual escalators in service agreements — consistent with moderate pricing power [#A]

  • Competitive bidding: In trade distribution, competitive RFP bidding is structurally present but mitigated by relationship-based purchasing; professional craft customers tend to be relationship-sticky rather than RFP-driven [#J]

  • Concession signal: No disclosed concession patterns; management language on pricing has been consistently constructive — appropriate scepticism warranted given self-reported nature [#J]

  • Volume loss signal: No disclosed volume losses attributed to pricing; Services division revenue growth has been positive in recent periods, inconsistent with significant price-driven volume loss [#J]

  • RFP pressure — Trade: Construction-sector customers face margin pressure themselves, creating secondary pressure on trade-supply pricing during market slowdowns — a cyclical risk rather than structural [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on switching costs and pricing leverage? In trade distribution, switching costs are low enough that a sustained housing/construction downturn could force Volati subsidiaries into price concessions to retain volumes; the apparent pricing-power narrative relies heavily on benign input cost conditions post-2023 — if input costs re-accelerate (e.g., steel, timber), a repricing test may reveal less pass-through ability than the 2021-2023 cycle demonstrated; customer stickiness in niche tools may also reflect lack of alternatives rather than genuine preference, which dissolves if a better-capitalised entrant targets the segment [#J]
