EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 12 findings

Irish Continental Group operates as Ireland's leading maritime transport and port terminal infrastructure provider, serving the critical trade corridors linking Ireland, the United Kingdom, and Continental Europe. The company's business model rests on two structurally advantaged divisions: the Ferries segment, which operates modern Rollon/Roll-off passenger and freight vessels under the Irish Ferries brand, and the Container & Terminal division, which combines short-sea container shipping through Eucon with ownership and operation of Dublin Ferryport Terminals and Belfast Container Terminal. ICG's dominant market position is reinforced by generational port concessions, including a 150-year Dublin Port lease and a Belfast concession extended to 2032, which create formidable barriers to competitive entry . The group completed a strategic milestone in 2025 by transitioning to 100% ownership of its ferry fleet, eliminating volatile charter cost exposure and enhancing operational control. This fleet consolidation, combined with disciplined pricing and effective fuel and carbon cost pass-through mechanisms, positions ICG to generate superior through-cycle returns despite exposure to volatile bunker markets and escalating environmental regulations .

The strength of ICG's operator quality and competitive foundation is evidenced by multiple structural advantages and management execution capabilities. Chief Executive Officer Eamonn Rothwell has led the company for 34 years since 1992 and holds a substantial 21.6% beneficial ownership stake, ensuring extraordinary alignment with shareholders . The group delivered an exceptional 15.11% total return compound annual growth rate from flotation through 2025 and maintained post-tax ROACE of 18.9% in FY2025, significantly outperforming regional European transport peers . Returns are supported by high-quality asset bases, with the Container & Terminal division achieving an attractive 25.6% ROACE and the Ferries segment maintaining 17.5% ROACE in FY2025 . ICG's terminal operations control 46% of all Ireland LoLo container movements and operate nine high-efficiency electric gantry cranes at Dublin Ferryport Terminals, creating substantial scale-driven cost advantages . The company demonstrates robust pricing power, successfully passing through 100% of rising EU ETS carbon costs via transparent surcharge mechanisms and recovering volatile bunker expenses through monthly lagged fuel adjustment factors embedded in freight contracts . 9 positioning for long-term growth is supported by structural trade re-routing dynamics and disciplined competitive capacity management. Post-Brexit customs friction has created durable demand for direct maritime

links bypassing the UK landbridge, with ICG successfully launching a two-ship daily service on the Dublin- Cherbourg route to provide Irish exporters with reliable access to Continental European markets . Competitive dynamics on the critical Dover Straits corridor have improved through capacity rationalization, with total ferry capacity decreasing from 13 to 11 vessels and ICG establishing a space charter alliance with P&O Ferries aimed at optimizing sailing frequency and improving segment margins . Barriers to entry remain exceptionally high, with control over core port berths creating immense structural protection anchored by Dublin Ferryport Terminal's 150-year lease and Belfast Container Terminal's concession extended to 2032 . Rising environmental regulations are being successfully navigated, with transparent carbon surcharge mechanisms enabling complete ETS cost recovery and contributing to a 13.9% group revenue increase in early 2026 .

, Irish Continental Group exhibits exceptional operator quality and advantaged competitive positioning anchored by generational port infrastructure concessions, disciplined capital allocation, and a fully owned modern fleet. The company's ability to generate superior returns relative to peers stems from integrated control of highbarrier terminal assets and effective cost pass-through mechanisms that protect margins from volatile input prices and regulatory inflation. Long-term growth opportunities are supported by structural post-Brexit trade rerouting and capacity rationalization on key corridors, though execution risks remain tied to unhedged commodity exposure, port infrastructure disruptions, and competitive intensity on the Dover Straits. The foundation quality assessment concludes with high confidence, tempered by persistent vigilance requirements around fuel cost volatility, governance alignment on executive compensation, and the macroeconomic sensitivity of discretionary passenger demand to pricing increases necessitated by environmental compliance burdens.
