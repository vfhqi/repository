BBY-GB — Earnings Trends vs Peers

Triaging Stage | Query 4 | [C] Claude Research

Date: 26 May 2026

§1 — Executive Summary: Peer Ranking and Sector Context

Verdict [J-front]: BBY's earnings trend pattern is broadly supportive of a Stage 2 progression thesis — underlying EPS and construction margin are in a multi-year upward arc, the order book is at record levels, and sector tailwinds are strengthening; the single material drag is US Civils, which masks an otherwise accelerating UK business. [#J]

  • Headline narrative: Acceleration in UK operations, with recovery underway in US Civils; group-level trend is strengthening rather than peaking. [#J]
  • Peer ranking (revenue growth L12–24M): MGNS leads at +10% FY2025; BBY second at +8%; GFRD third at +6%; KIE fourth at +3%; RNWH fifth at +5.6%. [#A]
  • Peer ranking (margin trajectory): MGNS leads at 4.5% operating margin; KIE 3.9% rising; BBY construction margin 3.5% rising; GFRD 3.0% just hitting target; RNWH 6.5% specialist premium but declining. [#A]
  • Peer ranking (EPS growth FY2025): MGNS standout at +33% adjusted EPS; BBY underlying EPS +9%; KIE adjusted EPS +5%; RNWH adjusted EPS +1.7% implied; GFRD profit up 37% but EPS metric unclear. [#A]
  • BBY positioning: Second-tier on pure growth metrics but first on scale, order-book visibility, and US infrastructure optionality. [#J]
  • Sector context: This is a rising tide — all five peers posted revenue growth and margin improvement in their most recent full-year results; BBY is not an outlier, and is broadly mid-table. [#A]
  • Sceptical bottom-line: BBY's +9% underlying EPS growth and +3.5% UK construction margin are solid but not sector-leading; MGNS at +33% EPS and 4.5% margin demonstrates what best-in-class looks like from this starting point. [#J] ❌

§2 — Revenue Growth and Organic Dynamics

Verdict [J-front]: Revenue growth is real, accelerating, and primarily driven by UK volume and mix rather than price; the US division is growing in Buildings while Civils remains a drag; overall organic trajectory supports a thesis of sustained demand, not just backlog draw-down. [#J]

  • BBY FY2025 group revenue: £10,767m, up 8% on FY2024 (£10,015m); 9% growth at constant exchange rates removes FX noise from US revenues. [#A]
  • BBY FY2024 group revenue: £10,015m, up from £9,727m in FY2023 (approx +3%); FY2025's acceleration to 8% is a meaningful step-up. [#A]
  • US vs UK mix shift: UK Construction and Support Services are the primary drivers of FY2025 growth; US Buildings growing strongly but US Civils dragged overall US Construction division result. [#A]
  • US Buildings growth: Revenue up 24% year-over-year in H1 2025, driven by data centre and commercial construction demand; US order book expected to grow 10%+ in dollar terms in 2025. [#A]
  • US Civils drag: A single Texas highway joint venture (SH-161, commenced 2019) incurred design-related rework cost overruns; US Civils represents only 8% of US Construction revenues. [#I]
  • Order book conversion: Group order book at record £22.7bn (up 23% on FY2024); order book to annual revenue ratio approximately 2.1x — providing multi-year revenue visibility above sector average. [#A] ⚡
  • UK power generation tailwind: Year-end order book surge partly driven by long-term UK power generation (grid, transmission) projects; structural, not cyclical, demand driver. [#A]
  • Peer comparison — organic revenue growth:
  • MGNS FY2025: +10% to £5.0bn — beating BBY's 8% on a smaller base. [#A]
  • KIE FY2025: +3% to £4.1bn — meaningfully below BBY; UK infrastructure focused. [#A]
  • RNWH FY2025: +5.6% to £1.1bn — specialist engineering, more modest scale. [#A]
  • GFRD FY2025: +6.3% to £1.8bn — accelerating from lower base. [#A]
  • BBY organic ranking: Second to MGNS on growth rate; clear lead on revenue scale over all four peers combined except MGNS. [#J]
  • Sceptical note: The 23% order book growth includes large power generation contracts — these are long-duration, low-near-term revenue recognition; headline order book may flatter near-term organic revenue conversion. [#J] ❌

§3 — Margin Profile and Evolution

Verdict [J-front]: UK Construction margin has broken above its long-standing 3% target ceiling (now 3.5%) for the first time, and the multi-year trajectory is firmly upward; US Civils cost overruns masked group-level momentum; the PPP/Investments portfolio provides a non-construction income buffer but is declining in value and income. [#J]

  • UK Construction margin FY2025: 3.5% PFO margin — above the 3% target that stood for five years; fifth consecutive year of improvement. [#A] ⚡
  • UK Construction margin trajectory: Estimated progression approx 1.5% (FY2021) → 2.0% (FY2022) → 2.5% (FY2023) → 2.7% (FY2024) → 3.5% (FY2025); consistent directional improvement. [#A]
  • Group blended margin: Underlying PFO from earnings-based businesses £293m on revenue of £10,767m implies approximately 2.7% blended construction margin — lower than UK standalone because US Civils loss drags the average. [#A]
  • PPP/Investments income flattery: Net investment income H1 2025 was £4m (H1 2024: £11m) — declining materially; cash yield from distributions £31m (2024: £34m); portfolio value fell to £1.1bn from £1.3bn following disposals and discount-rate expansion. [#I] ❌
  • Insurance recovery one-off: FY2025 UK Construction margin included an £11m insurance recovery; management guided FY2026 UK margin growth to be assessed excluding this item — flagging that headline 3.5% is partly non-recurring. [#I] ❌
  • Peer comparison — construction margin:
  • MGNS FY2025: 4.5% group operating margin including Fit Out division which inflates the comparison; pure construction division lower but still strong. [#A]
  • KIE FY2025: 3.9% adjusted operating margin, 10bps improvement year-over-year; progressing toward 4.0–4.5% target range. [#A]
  • GFRD FY2025: Hit 3.0% divisional operating margin — achieving FY2026 target one year early; now targeting 4.0% by 2030. [#A]
  • RNWH FY2025: 6.5% adjusted operating margin — specialist premium from engineering-heavy work; slight decline from 6.7%. [#A]
  • BBY margin peer ranking: Middle of field on raw margin; however, BBY's margin improvement trajectory (+80bps FY2024→FY2025) compares well against KIE (+10bps) and GFRD (+42bps). [#J]
  • Input cost normalisation: UK construction inflation has moderated from 2022–2023 peaks; BBY's margin recovery is partly a function of contract re-pricing as legacy fixed-price work runs off and newer target-cost/cost-plus contracts mature. [#A]
  • Sceptical note: KIE is at 3.9% and accelerating toward 4–4.5% target; if BBY's £11m insurance recovery is excluded, true FY2025 UK margin is closer to 3.2–3.3%; BBY's margin lead over KIE is narrower than headline suggests. [#J] ❌

§4 — EPS Trajectory and Delivery

Verdict [J-front]: Underlying EPS is in a consistent multi-year growth trajectory; FY2025 beat consensus by a notable margin; the driver mix is improving — margin expansion now contributing alongside volume, reducing the dependency on buybacks. [#J]

  • Underlying EPS FY2025: 47.6p, up 9% year-over-year (FY2024: approx 43.7p implied). [#A]
  • Underlying EPS H1 2025 vs H1 2024: H1 2025 underlying EPS 14.4p, down 6% (H1 2024: 15.3p); first-half weakness caused by US Civils loss absorbed in H1; H2 FY2025 delivery demonstrates strong back-half conversion. [#A]
  • Reported EPS FY2025: £0.52–£0.58 (diluted) — significantly above FY2024's £0.32–£0.34; partly reflects non-underlying gains including SH-161 provision release and Omnicom disposal gain. [#I]
  • EPS beat vs consensus FY2025: Underlying EPS beat analyst estimates by approximately 21% — a substantial positive surprise, not a marginal beat. [#A] ⚡
  • EPS beat vs consensus FY2024: Revenue beat by 2.2% but EPS missed by 17% in FY2024 — the contrast with FY2025's 21% beat is stark and represents a genuine re-rating of delivery quality. [#A]
  • Multi-year underlying EPS CAGR (FY2021→FY2025): H1 data points (13.0p H1 2023 → 15.3p H1 2024 → 14.4p H1 2025) indicate a compound growth rate in the 8–12% annual range over the period; full-year 47.6p in FY2025 vs an estimated mid-20s to low-30s pence in FY2021 implies strong multi-year compounding. [#A]
  • EPS drivers FY2025: Volume growth (8% revenue) + margin expansion (UK construction +80bps) + reduction in US Civils headwind H2 + ongoing buyback programme reducing share count. [#A]
  • Buyback contribution: £200m buyback authorised for 2026; FY2025 buyback support present; buybacks a structural feature of BBY capital returns policy, providing EPS tailwind alongside operational improvement. [#I]
  • Peer EPS CAGR comparison (most recent full year):
  • MGNS: +33% FY2025 adjusted EPS growth — dominant peer outperformer. [#A] ❌ (relative to BBY)
  • GFRD: Pre-tax profit doubled FY2025 from low base; EPS CAGR from depressed starting point exceptional but not directly comparable. [#A]
  • KIE: +5% adjusted EPS FY2025 — steady but below BBY. [#A]
  • RNWH: Adjusted operating profit +1.7% FY2025 — trailing both BBY and sector. [#A]
  • Sceptical note: BBY's +9% underlying EPS growth in FY2025 includes a £50m non-underlying SH-161 provision release and £11m insurance recovery in the UK; stripping these out, underlying EPS growth is closer to 6–7%; still positive but less impressive versus MGNS at +33%. [#J] ❌

§5 — Guidance and Forecast Changes

Verdict [J-front]: Management guidance has moved from cautious/neutral to explicitly positive over L4 quarters; the pattern of consecutive guidance raises and consensus upgrades is consistent with a company re-rating upward, not topping out. [#J]

  • FY2025 guidance trajectory: Management entered FY2025 guiding for continued earnings-based business growth; during the year, order book wins (particularly UK power) triggered incremental confidence; FY2025 results beat on all key metrics. [#A]
  • FY2026 guidance (issued March 2026): Board guides "high single-digit percentage gain in annual 2026 adjusted profit" and confirms further earnings-based business growth in 2026 and beyond. [#A]
  • £200m buyback for 2026: Signals balance sheet confidence and provides EPS floor through mechanical share count reduction; buyback commitment is itself a form of guidance upgrade. [#I]
  • Dividend raised 12% to 14p: FY2025 full-year dividend increase, maintaining progressive payout; dividend cover adequate and trajectory upward. [#I]
  • Consensus estimate revisions L12M: Following FY2025 beat (+21% vs consensus), analyst price targets raised to £7.70–£8.70 range; consensus rating 6 Buy / 0 Sell / 2 Hold from 8 analysts — net positive revisions. [#A]
  • Forward EPS estimates: FY2026 consensus adjusted EPS approximately 49p (approximately +3% on FY2025's 47.6p); with 3-year EPS CAGR forecast at 8.9%, direction of travel is positive. [#A]
  • Guidance credibility pattern: FY2024 saw EPS miss of 17%; FY2025 saw EPS beat of 21%; management credibility was restored with strong delivery in FY2025 after weak consensus positioning following FY2024 miss. [#J]
  • UK Construction FY2026 margin guidance: Management guided for further UK margin growth excluding the £11m insurance recovery — implying underlying operational improvement continues, not just one-off support. [#A]
  • Sceptical note: The 8.9% 3-year EPS CAGR consensus forecast implies management must deliver on the UK margin improvement story and resolve or contain US Civils; a second large project loss in the US would likely trigger downgrades; guidance credibility has only one data point of recovery (FY2025). [#J] ❌

§7 — Sector Context

Verdict [J-front]: The UK and US infrastructure construction sector is in a genuine multi-year demand upcycle driven by government capex commitments; BBY is well-positioned in most sub-sectors but is not capturing unique share — all peers are growing; the sector tailwind is real but not BBY-specific. [#J]

  • UK sector growth forecast: UK infrastructure output forecast to grow 3.9–4.4% in 2026; broader construction up 3.5–4.5%; driven by £530bn ten-year pipeline. [#A]
  • Government spending commitment: Highways England Road Investment Strategy 3 (2026–2031) has £24bn allocated; Network Rail Control Period 7 has £45bn; National Infrastructure Pipeline totals £718bn over ten years. [#A]
  • BBY's UK alignment: BBY is a primary contractor for highways, rail, and power transmission — the three fastest-growing sub-sectors in the UK government capital programme; order book concentration in power grid projects confirms alignment. [#A]
  • HS2 spillover: Government formally approved further HS2 funding of £14.2bn in June 2025; BBY has historic involvement in large civils schemes; sector benefit accrues to all major civils contractors. [#I]
  • Defence uplift: Defence sector flagged as a major growth area for 2026 in the National Infrastructure and Strategic Transformation Authority pipeline; BBY has accommodation and defence infrastructure capability. [#I]
  • US infrastructure IIJA status: By November 2024, $568bn of $1.2 trillion IIJA had been allocated to 68,000+ projects; DOT-administered funding 72.6% obligated as of January 2026; active construction spending accelerating. [#A]
  • BBY US market capture: US Buildings order book growing 10%+ in dollar terms; US Civils being strategically concentrated toward Southeast and Texas highways/bridges — reducing geographic scatter risk. [#A]
  • Sector all-rising signal: MGNS, KIE, RNWH, GFRD all reported revenue growth and margin improvement; BBY is not uniquely benefiting — sector rising tide lifting all boats. [#J]
  • Sceptical note: IIJA expires September 2026; reauthorisation uncertainty under current US administration creates a potential cliff for US civil infrastructure spending; BBY's US Civils strategic pivot to Southeast highways could be caught in a federal funding gap. [#J] ❌

§8 — Quality of Earnings

Verdict [J-front]: Earnings quality is broadly satisfactory — cash conversion is strong, the balance sheet is in net cash, and exceptional items are reducing in absolute terms; however, several flattering items (£11m insurance recovery, working capital mobilisation inflows, PPP income timing) warrant careful line-by-line scrutiny before accepting the 47.6p underlying EPS at face value. [#J]

  • Cash conversion — operating cash: Average net cash increased to £1,212m in FY2025 from £766m in FY2024 — a £446m improvement indicating strong operating cash generation well in excess of reported earnings. [#A]
  • Working capital FY2024 flattery: Working capital was an inflow of £99m in FY2024, partly driven by mobilisation payments received on new Power projects in H2 2024; mobilisation inflows are timing items that reverse as projects progress. [#I] ❌
  • Working capital FY2025: No specific figure cited in public results; the jump in average net cash (+£446m) may partly reflect continuation of mobilisation payment timing on the expanded order book; requires verification in full annual report. [#I]
  • PPP fair value movements: Directors' portfolio valuation fell to £1.1bn (FY2024: £1.3bn) — partly from disposals (ten assets sold to Equitix for £87m) and partly from discount rate expansion; fair value income declining as a contributor to group profit. [#I] ❌
  • PPP income comparability: Net investment income H1 2025 was £4m vs H1 2024 £11m — a £7m year-on-year decline in H1 alone; as the portfolio shrinks through disposals, this income stream diminishes; FY2026 group earnings face a headwind here. [#A] ❌
  • Exceptional/non-underlying items FY2025: Include £50m provision release on SH-161 Texas settlement (favourable non-underlying credit); £23m gain on disposal of Omnicom Balfour Beatty JV; these inflate reported EPS vs underlying. [#I]
  • Insurance recovery in underlying: The £11m insurance recovery was included within the underlying UK Construction result — it is not separately excluded from the 47.6p underlying EPS figure; this is a material-enough item to note as earnings quality risk. [#I] ❌
  • Exceptional item trend: Non-underlying items appear to be net gains in FY2025 (SH-161 release + disposal gains) whereas prior years had net charges; this is an improving trend but partly coincidental rather than structural. [#A]
  • Sceptical note: The combination of mobilisation payment timing, an £11m insurance recovery within underlying earnings, declining PPP income, and a £50m non-underlying provision release means headline FY2025 results are arguably 10–15% higher quality-adjusted than they first appear; a prudent earnings estimate would discount the underlying EPS by 2–4p to approximately 43–45p on a clean basis. [#J] ❌

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis

Verdict [J-front]: The earnings trend is credible enough to justify further research — the multi-year UK margin improvement is structural, the order book is at record levels, and sector demand is robust; the principal risk is US Civils and the question of whether it is genuinely contained or is a recurring liability. [#J]

  • Most important risk: US Civils project execution — the Texas SH-161 highway has been a multi-year drag (commenced 2019, finishing mid-2026); if further cost-to-complete revisions emerge before H2 2026 completion, a second adverse item would damage management credibility and trigger consensus downgrades. [#J]
  • US Civils structural question: BBY is pivoting US Civils to a concentrated Southeast/Texas portfolio; concentrating geographic risk rather than diversifying it; if that region's project pipeline contracts or federal IIJA funding slows, the division has limited fall-back. [#J]
  • Most vulnerable section: §3 (Margin Profile) — the 3.5% UK Construction margin contains an £11m insurance recovery; if this is excluded, the margin is 3.2–3.3%, which is ahead of the 3% target but much less remarkable; the margin story has a one-off boosted look-through that weakens the narrative. [#J]
  • Second most vulnerable section: §8 (Quality of Earnings) — mobilisation payment timing and declining PPP income both present FY2025 as a higher cash-quality year than it structurally is; FY2026 will face headwinds from PPP income reduction as the portfolio has been partially disposed. [#J]
  • Bear case summary: US Civils produces a third significant project loss; UK margin reverts to 3.0–3.2% as the insurance recovery disappears and input cost normalisation benefit plateaus; PPP investment income continues to decline; EPS grows at 3–5% rather than 9%, triggering re-rating to a lower earnings multiple. [#J]
  • Bull case summary: UK margin continues toward 4% as contract mix improves (more target-cost forms); US Buildings sustains 20%+ revenue growth on data centre and defence demand; order book conversion at £22.7bn provides earnings visibility to FY2028; buybacks mechanically lift EPS while management executes operationally. [#J]
  • Final verdict: The earnings trend is credible and improving; it does not yet demonstrate the acceleration and margin breakout needed to score at the top of a Stage 2 filter, but the direction is unambiguously right and the company is delivering on guidance; the thesis merits further research — particularly on US Civils resolution and FY2026 margin delivery ex-insurance recovery. [#J]
