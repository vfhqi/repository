Section 3 – What the company does 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Deep engineering integration and proprietary technology allow Solid State to secure premium, highly sticky, custom-designed products with extensive barriers to entry.

Divisional Structure and Products

1.
Three operational divisions: Transitioning from two divisions, the group will report across Systems, Power, and Components segments from FY26 onwards
2.
Custom Systems segment: The Systems division designs and manufactures industrial computing, advanced imaging platforms, wideband antennas, and secure military radio systems
3.
Ruggedised Power segment: The Power division specialises in custom-engineered battery packs and portable energy storage systems where size, weight, and reliability are critical
4.
Niche Components segment: The Components division offers own-brand and franchised electronic, electro-mechanical, and optoelectronic displays alongside specialized sourcing support
5.
High-margin own brands: Own-brand component lines like Durakool and Gateway ferrites command 10.00% higher profit margins than standard franchise distribution
6.
Differentiated optoelectronic manufacturing: The world-class optoelectronic facility in Weymouth is increasingly recognized by UK defense primes and medical equipment companies

Customer Stickiness and Value Chain

7.
Outsourced design integration: Solid State embeds its engineers directly with customer teams at the architectural stage, creating massive, long-term switching costs
8.
Sticky commercial partnerships: Highly trusted client relationships underpin repeat business, with some customer accounts spanning over 30 to 40 years
9.
Tier-1 blue-chip base: Long-standing relationships with top-tier global contractors like Siemens Healthcare, BAE Systems, and Ultra Maritime provide stable recurring revenue streams
10.
Valuable cross-selling synergies: Initial component design-ins frequently lead to full systems-level contracts, amplifying organic growth across separate business units
11.
Iceberg revenue model: Beyond initial hardware sales, large programmes generate multi-year, lucrative through-life support revenues including training, upgrades, and obsolescence management

Industry Structure and Accreditations

12.
Formidable entry barriers: High regulatory hurdles and specialized industry accreditations, such as ISO13485 for medical, restrict low-cost competitor entry
13.
Sovereign security advantage: Deep-rooted integration with NATO primes positions the company perfectly to capture escalating public defense spending
14.
Fragmented market landscape: The specialist electronics sector remains highly fragmented, allowing Solid State to consolidate niche players profitably
15.
Acquisitive growth history: Successful execution of 14 acquisitions over two decades has broadened the technology portfolio without diluting equity returns
16.
Strategic US foothold: The $2.00m acquisition of Q-Par Antennas provides approved supplier status with US defense primes, accelerating geographical expansion
17.
Other side of the story: Heavy reliance on governmental defence primes leaves Solid State exposed to unpredictable legislative reviews and procurement pauses, as demonstrated by the UK's Strategic Defence Review
