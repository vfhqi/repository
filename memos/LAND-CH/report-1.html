[W][C] Q1 Business Description — Landis+Gyr Group AG (LAND-CH)

Stage: IG | Source: Claude [C] | Date: 2026-05-15 | Query: Q1 — Business Description Cohort: Energy Transition Equipment Recovery (ALFEN-NL / S92-DE / KEMPOWR-FI / LAND-CH)
Cohort CQs addressed: CQ-C1 (structural vs cyclical de-rating), CQ-C3 (competitive position), CQ-C4 (services/recurring %)

VERDICT [J]

  • De-rating is substantially structural, not purely cyclical: smart metering market in the Americas is commoditising at the hardware layer; EMEA was a low-margin drag; execution failures (UK delays, inventory writedowns) accelerated the repricing but were symptoms, not cause.
  • LAND-CH is now a fundamentally different entity post-EMEA divestment (completed April 2026): the remaining business — Americas + APAC — carries a 14.4% Adj. EBITDA margin (FY2025 continuing ops), versus group-level ~10% pre-divestment; this transformation is underappreciated by most LAND-CH analysis written before Oct 2024.
  • The key bull case is structural demand floor plus services mix shift: Americas backlog of USD 3.9bn at ~2.7x annual revenue of continuing ops provides multi-year revenue visibility; 35% software/services in committed backlog signals mix shift toward recurring revenue streams.
  • The key bear case is valuation re-rating not yet achieved: despite Itron trading at ~15x EV/EBITDA on Nasdaq, LAND-CH trades at ~7.5x on SIX Swiss Exchange; a US relisting is targeted but execution risk is real; activist Spectrum is pushing urgently.
  • CQ-C4 verdict: services/recurring is real but still minority. 35% of backlog is software/services; current revenue mix skews hardware-heavy. The transition is directional, not yet structural in P&L terms.
  • CQ-C1 verdict: de-rating is ~60% structural (commoditisation, EMEA value destruction) and ~40% execution (margin misses, CEO transition). The strategic pivot resolves the execution element; it does not resolve the hardware commoditisation question.
  • CQ-C3 verdict: competitive position in Americas appears solid (35-40% market share duopoly with Itron); Chinese competition has not yet penetrated US/Europe in meaningful volume. EMEA was the competitively challenged segment — now divested.

§1 — GENERAL

Business Description

  • Landis+Gyr Group AG is a Swiss-headquartered, globally operating smart metering and grid-edge technology company.
  • Listed on SIX Swiss Exchange (ticker: LAND); ISIN CH0371153492; functional currency USD (reports in USD since FY2020).
  • Pre-EMEA divestment: three geographic segments — Americas, EMEA, Asia-Pacific.
  • Post-EMEA divestment (April 2026): two segments — Americas, Asia-Pacific; EMEA sold to AURELIUS for USD 215m enterprise value (Sep 2025 agreement, closed April 8, 2026).
  • HQ: Zug, Switzerland. Workforce: ~6,900 pre-divestment; ~4,200 post-divestment (Americas + APAC).
  • Core products: advanced metering infrastructure hardware (electricity meters, communication modules), AMI software and network management (GridStream platform), grid-edge intelligence software, managed services, data analytics.
  • Customers: regulated electric utilities and grid operators; demand is substantially non-discretionary (regulatory mandate-driven capex).

Revenue Model

  • Hardware (majority of current P&L): AMI electricity meters, gas and water meters, communication modules, head-end systems.
  • Hardware is project-based but sits inside long-duration framework contracts, limiting spot-market exposure.
  • Software & Services (growing): GridStream network management, data analytics, managed services (hosted head-end, field service), grid-edge intelligence software.
  • 35% of committed backlog (USD 4.6bn as of FY2024) is software/services — up from minimal disclosure in prior years; exact current revenue split is not formally disclosed in segment reporting.
  • Framework contracts: multi-year agreements (typically 3-10 years) with regulated utilities covering meter supply schedules, communications networks, and software services.
  • Committed backlog represents firm orders placed against framework contracts, providing high revenue visibility.
  • FY2024 committed backlog of USD 4.6bn = ~2.7x FY2024 net revenue; this is the standout valuation support.

Capital Allocation

  • Dividend: progressive dividend policy anchored to FY2019 CHF 2.00/share base; maintained through earnings trough.
  • Buyback: USD 175m share buyback approved (up to 10% of shares) using EMEA divestment net proceeds; initiated 2025/26.

  • CDPQ: no current meaningful stake identified — CDPQ stake appears to have been reduced/exited pre-2024. Kirkbi (Lego family) was a major shareholder until 2024 when it sold 5% to Spectrum Entrepreneurial Ownership .
  • SEO / Spectrum: ~5% activist stake; Fabian Rauch (SEO co-founder) on board since Aug 2024. SEO is explicitly pushing for US relisting and Americas-focused strategy — broadly aligned with management's Oct 2024 announcement.

Revenue Model — Hardware vs Software/Services

  • Hardware (majority): single largest revenue element; electricity meters, gas/water/thermal meters (in EMEA), communications modules (RF and PLC), head-end systems.
  • Hardware margins under structural pressure from Asian competition (primarily in Asia-Pacific) and increasing scale requirements in Americas.
  • Software/Services (growing mix):
  • GridStream Connect: wireless AMI network management software (deployed with Itron Silver Spring Networks protocol — note: Landis+Gyr and Itron use competing network protocols in US).
  • GridStream Services: managed services layer — hosted network operations, field service, data analytics.
  • Grid-edge intelligence: distribution grid visibility software, fault detection, EV load management, demand response.
  • 35% of FY2024 committed backlog (USD 1.6bn equiv.) is software/services — this is forward-looking; spot revenue mix likely 20-25% currently (not formally disclosed). ⚡ The directionality of mix shift toward software/services, if sustained, transforms the margin profile materially.
  • Contracted backlog mechanics: framework contracts lock in minimum volume commitments over multi-year periods; backlog is committed (firm orders), not aspirational pipeline. Book-to-bill of 1.5x in FY2024 confirms very strong commercial momentum heading into FY2025.

Contract Backlog Detail — FY2024

  • Total committed backlog: USD 4.6bn (+22.9% YoY); record high.
  • Americas backlog: USD 3,762m (+26.2% YoY); book-to-bill 1.8x — exceptional.
  • EMEA backlog: USD 697m (stable); book-to-bill 1.0x.
  • Asia-Pacific backlog: USD 172m (+92% YoY); book-to-bill 1.6x.
  • Order intake FY2024: USD 2,614m (+33.6% CC); book-to-bill 1.5x group — strongest H2 order intake since 2017 IPO.
  • Services/software in backlog: ~35% (~USD 1.6bn) — confirmed by FY2024 results presentation.
  • Assessment: backlog depth provides multi-year revenue underpin; at ~2.7x annual revenue, LAND-CH has better forward visibility than most industrial peers (typical industrial backlog: 0.5-1.2x revenue). This is the single strongest bull-case metric.

UK Smart Metering — DCC Programme

  • Programme scale: 37m+ smart meters installed across Great Britain by end-2024; Landis+Gyr has supplied 21m+ smart meters to GB energy suppliers — approximately 57% of installed base.
  • Current status (2025): 70% of GB meters now smart (40m units as of Sep 2025); 64% operating in smart mode. DCC central network operational.
  • LAND-CH UK position: extended 3-year partnership with Centrica Smart Meter Assets signed April 2025 — one of largest UK energy suppliers.
  • Prior execution issue: SMETS1/SMETS2 transition and DCC enrolment delays were cited 2019-2022 as execution overhang for LAND-CH; the DCC is now operational and SMETS2 rollout is proceeding; LAND-CH's UK book is normalising.
  • EMEA divestment impact: UK business was part of EMEA segment, now divested to AURELIUS. UK is no longer a direct LAND-CH P&L risk post-April 2026. However, the UK execution history contributed to the persistent credibility discount still embedded in the share price.

CEO Transition and Management

  • Werner Lieberherr (CEO 2020-2024): departed November 2024 (reached retirement age). Legacy: oversaw COVID supply disruption, post-COVID demand catch-up, but also sustained margin underdelivery vs Itron, UK execution failures, and the market's loss of confidence in guidance reliability.
  • Peter Mainz (CEO from November 2024): former President & CEO of Sensus (US smart metering — Xylem subsidiary, direct LAND-CH competitor). Board member since 2018. Industry-native appointment — strongest possible signal of strategic direction change.
  • Mandate: Americas focus, EMEA divestment, US relisting, margin improvement.
  • Key actions within 12 months: EMEA strategic review announced Oct 2024, EMEA sale to AURELIUS agreed Sep 2025, USD 175m buyback authorised, US listing preparation initiated, raised margin guidance from 10.5-12.0% to 13.0-14.5% (FY2025 continuing ops).
  • Assessment: execution on announced strategy has been rapid and decisive. Mainz's Sensus background means he knows the Americas AMI market with unusual granularity. CEO transition risk appears substantially mitigated by credentials and pace of action. However, it is too early to assess operational execution vs guidance.

Recent Delivery — Execution History 2022-2025

  • FY2022: revenue recovery post-COVID (+16% YoY); semiconductor/component shortages created delivery delays and cost overruns. ❌
  • FY2023: USD 2.0bn revenue (+14.3%), highest in company history; USD 185m Americas EBITDA — but partly pent-up demand; sustainable base was lower.
  • FY2024: -10.5% revenue decline; USD 20m inventory write-off (Americas product portfolio transition to next-gen platform); adj. EBITDA margin 9.9% vs guidance of ~11%. ❌ Guidance miss was the most damaging credibility event in management's track record under Lieberherr.
  • FY2025 (continuing ops): revenue USD 1,166m (+4.2% Americas-driven); adj. EBITDA margin 14.4% — first year showing material margin improvement; 90bp above prior year; 450bp above prior group structure. First meaningful evidence of margin trajectory under Mainz.

CQ-C3: Competitive Position — Has It Changed?

  • Americas duopoly intact: Landis+Gyr and Itron each hold approximately 35-40% of North American AMI market share. Evidence: CNBC / NBC reporting of ~35-40% market share per player; confirmed by industry analyst reports (MarketsandMarkets, ResearchandMarkets).
  • EMEA: was the structurally challenged segment — Sagemcom consolidating European share, Shenzhen Wasion dominant in Asia; Landis+Gyr's competitive position in EMEA was eroding at the margin, contributing to the low-margin profile. Now divested — risk excised.
  • Asia-Pacific: Wasion strong in China; Landis+Gyr targets regulated utility markets in Australia, Japan (included in Americas segment for reporting). Strong FY2024 order intake in Australia (+92% backlog).
  • Key contract wins cited (FY2024): not individually disclosed in press releases but management referenced wins in North America and Japan driving the 1.8x Americas book-to-bill.
  • Assessment: Americas position is not deteriorating. The divestment of EMEA is explicitly strategic derisking of the more competitive geography. Net competitive position for the remaining business is stronger post-divestment.

  • Disclosed metric: 35% of USD 4.6bn committed backlog (FY2024) = ~USD 1.6bn of software/services in backlog.
  • Current P&L reality: no formal revenue split of hardware vs software/services; estimated ~20-25% of current annual revenue is services/recurring based on backlog composition and GridStream platform commentary.

The 35% backlog figure is the strongest piece of quantitative evidence available. Revenue recognition on multi-year services contracts will appear over time — visibility today is backlog not actuals. ❌ This is a surveillance item.

§3 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT

Smart Metering Market Structure

  • Global smart metering market: estimated ~USD 25-30bn annually (2024), growing to USD 46bn by 2030 (MarketsandMarkets); CAGR ~8-10%.
  • Key verticals: electricity (dominant), gas, water/thermal; LAND-CH primarily electricity AMI.
  • Market segments: hardware (meters, comms modules), software (MDMS, network management), services (managed services, analytics). Hardware is largest by revenue; software/services fastest growing and highest margin.
  • Leading players globally: Landis+Gyr (Switzerland), Itron (US, Nasdaq: ITRI), Sagemcom (France, private), Shenzhen Wasion (China, dominant in Asia), Osaki Electric/EDMI (Singapore/Japan), Honeywell (Elster brand, primarily gas), Diehl Metering (Germany, gas/water).

EU Smart Metering — Regulatory Mandate

  • EU Electricity Directive 2019/944: obligates member states to achieve 80% smart meter penetration by 2024 (subject to cost-effectiveness assessment); from 2025, rollouts are mandatory with binding deadlines for 100% coverage by 2030.
  • Current status (2024): approximately half of EU27 member states have completed national rollouts; the other half including Germany, Czech Republic, Bulgaria, Cyprus have very low penetration. Germany in particular is a large pending market — smart meter obligation for households with >6,000 kWh consumption entered force in 2023.
  • Forward rollout pipeline: ~90m additional smart electricity meters forecast across Europe 2025-2029 (ResearchandMarkets); installed base to reach 285m by 2029 (80% penetration). CEE/Southeast Europe accounts for 52% of expected 2029 EU shipments (up from 28% in 2023) — large unserved markets.
  • Assessment for LAND-CH (pre-divestment): EU mandate was a genuine multi-year demand underpin for EMEA revenue. Post-divestment, LAND-CH (Americas+APAC) does not directly benefit from the EU rollout pipeline — AURELIUS (new EMEA owner) captures this. This is a significant change from the classic LAND-CH bull narrative.
  • ⚡ RARE: The regulatory mandate in Europe constitutes a government-enforced demand floor largely ignored by market analysis focused on execution/margin issues. Post-divestment, this floor benefit accrues to AURELIUS not LAND-CH.

US AMI Market — IRA and Grid Modernisation

  • US regulatory drivers: Inflation Reduction Act (IRA, 2022) allocates multi-billion USD to grid modernisation; DOE grid resilience grants; FERC Order 881 (transmission planning) driving distribution system investment.
  • AMI penetration: US already has ~80% AMI penetration for electricity (most large IOUs deployed); second-wave spending is on communications network upgrades (e.g., cellular-to-RF migration), grid-edge sensors, software services layer.
  • Implications: Americas backlog growth of 26% and 1.8x book-to-bill in FY2024 reflects this second-wave spending cycle. LAND-CH is well-positioned in North America for the comms network upgrade cycle and software services build-out. ⚡ RARE: US second-wave AMI spend (network upgrades + services) is often conflated with the original hardware rollout by buy-side; it is structurally different — more software/services intensive, stickier, and less exposed to Asian hardware competition.
  • IRA risk: tariff dynamics created ~USD 30m revenue shift from FY2024 to FY2025 (management disclosure); tariff uncertainty a persistent risk for LAND-CH's US supply chain configuration.

Competition — Chinese Threat Assessment

  • Shenzhen Wasion Group: dominant in China and increasing in Asian developing markets; captured majority of Asia-Pacific smart metering volumes (excl. Japan/Australia) on price. No significant penetration in Europe or North America yet.
  • Regulatory/certification barriers: smart meters in Europe are regulated products; "Measurement Instruments Directive" certification required; cybersecurity requirements (GDPR, NIS2) and utility IT system integration requirements create ~12-24 month certification process for new entrants. Chinese vendors face additional geopolitical scrutiny on critical infrastructure supply chains (Huawei precedent widely cited in utility procurement discussions).
  • Assessment (CQ-C1 — structural or cyclical?): Chinese competition in Americas/Europe is a medium-term structural risk but has NOT meaningfully penetrated these markets as of 2025. The de-rating of LAND-CH is not primarily driven by Chinese competition in its core markets. The de-rating is better attributed to: (a) EMEA low-margin profile now resolved via divestment; (b) Americas hardware margin compression from own product transition; (c) SIX-vs-Nasdaq listing discount. Monitoring watch: Chinese vendor attempted entry to European tenders is the canary-in-the-coal-mine indicator.

Market Concentration — Americas

  • LAND-CH + Itron = ~70-80% of North American AMI market.
  • Sensus (Xylem subsidiary) holds ~10-15%; small residual to Honeywell Elster, Siemens.
  • Sagemcom focused on Europe (France primarily); not a meaningful Americas competitor.
  • True duopoly is maintained by: (a) deep utility IT system integration (billing systems, SCADA, outage management — replacing a meter vendor means re-integrating the entire network software stack); (b) multi-year framework contract lock-in; (c) US regulatory certification requirements; (d) incumbency preference in critical infrastructure.
  • Itron's 2018 acquisition of Silver Spring Networks (IoT network platform) and LAND-CH's GridStream platform represent the key strategic moats — utilities have invested heavily in one network protocol/platform and have very high switching costs.

§4 — BB#2 FOUNDATION QUALITY

§4.1 — Operator Assessment

CEO — Peter Mainz (from November 2024)
- Former CEO of Sensus (US smart metering company, acquired by Xylem for USD 1.7bn in 2016). Mainz ran Sensus as a direct LAND-CH competitor.
- Board member of LAND-CH since 2018 — deep institutional knowledge of the business before taking CEO role.
- First 12 months in role: EMEA divestment agreed, USD 175m buyback authorised, US relisting preparation advanced, margin guidance raised materially. Pace of execution is high relative to predecessor.
- Mandate is clear and consistent: concentrate on Americas, exit EMEA, relist in US, expand services mix.
- Risk: Americas-only focus leaves the company more cyclically concentrated in one regulatory environment; if North American grid capex slows, there is no EMEA/APAC offset (APAC too small). Itron has similar Americas concentration.
- Assessment: Mainz appointment is the single most significant positive development in LAND-CH's recent history. Credentials are directly relevant. Execution in year one has been decisive. Guidance credibility improving but not yet fully restored — FY2025 continuing ops at 14.4% margin is first concrete evidence.

Board — Governance
- Fabian Rauch (SEO/Spectrum) on board since Aug 2024 — activist discipline now embedded at board level.
- Mainz retains delegate board role — unusual CEO+delegate structure; acceptable given company's Swiss governance framework.
- No concerns about entrenched board; activist presence provides accountability mechanism.

Capital Allocation Discipline
- USD 175m buyback represents ~12-15% of market cap at current prices — meaningful shareholder return.
- Dividend maintained through earnings trough — management commitment to shareholder returns.
- No large-scale M&A in recent history; capital allocation is focused and disciplined.

§4.2 — Advantaged Business - Deep utility IT integration: smart metering is not a standalone hardware purchase; AMI deployment integrates with utility billing systems (SAP IS-U, Oracle CC&B), outage management systems , and distribution management systems . Ripping out LAND-CH or Itron involves re-integrating the entire back-office stack — estimated 5+ year programme for a large IOU. ⚡ RARE: this switching cost is not a hardware switching cost — it is a software/systems integration switching cost, far stickier and poorly appreciated by investors focused on meter hardware prices.

  • GridStream platform network lock-in: LAND-CH's RF mesh network (GridStream) and Itron's competing protocols operate on proprietary standards. Once deployed, a utility's 10-15 year communications lifecycle is locked to a single vendor platform. The platform is the moat, not the meter.
  • Framework contract duration: 5-10 year master supply agreements with renewal cycles; utilities cannot easily switch mid-contract. The USD 3.9bn backlog represents ~3.3x FY2025 Americas annual revenue — contractual revenue certainty over 3+ years.
  • Certification and approval cycles: smart meters are regulated measurement instruments; country-level metrology certification (MID in Europe, ANSI in US), utility-specific type testing, cybersecurity clearance. A new entrant faces 12-24 months of qualification work before a single meter can ship. This is a meaningful competitive deterrent.
  • Scale economies: meter manufacturing benefits from volume; LAND-CH's manufacturing scale (Shenzhen and Americas facilities) limits cost disadvantage vs Chinese competitors in regulated markets where quality/certification barriers protect margin.
  • Normalised EBIT margin target: FY2025 Adj. EBITDA margin of 14.4% (continuing ops) is an improvement but not yet at Itron's ~16% Adj. EBITDA equivalent; medium-term target appears to be 15-17% as services mix increases. ❌ Margin convergence with Itron is the core financial recovery thesis — not yet achieved.

§4.3 — Value Chain: Customer Quality

  • Customers: regulated electric utilities and grid operators (IOUs, co-ops, munis in US; DSOs in Europe/APAC).
  • Regulated utility capex is approved by state/national regulators through rate case processes; AMI investment earns regulated return in rate base.
  • Non-discretionary driver: regulatory mandates (EU 2025-2030, US IRA grid provisions) make AMI spend mandatory, not optional.
  • AMI is not subject to utility financial cycles in the same way as discretionary T&D capex — it is a regulatory compliance item, not a preference item.
  • ACH-C3 relevance (grid infrastructure capex slip): YES, relevant. If a government delays or waters down AMI mandate enforcement, spending can slip in time but not disappear permanently. Example: UK SMETS2 delays pushed revenue recognition from FY2019-21 into FY2022-24 — revenue was deferred, not lost. The timing risk is real; the structural risk is low.
  • Customer concentration: LAND-CH does not disclose specific customer concentration; framework agreements mean single customers can represent significant annual volumes. Centrica (UK, now AURELIUS customer) extended 3-year deal; top 10 North American IOUs are likely >50% of Americas revenue. Concentration risk exists but is mitigated by the regulated nature of each counterparty (utilities do not go bankrupt).

§4.4 — Industry Structure

  • Americas: effectively a duopoly (LAND-CH + Itron ~70-80% share); concentration maintained by switching costs and framework contract lock-in.
  • EMEA (now divested): more fragmented — LAND-CH, Itron, Sagemcom (strong in France), Diehl (gas/water), and emerging Chinese pressure in lower-income markets. Sagemcom winning French utility contracts; Landis+Gyr had lost marginal share at the tender margin. This is now AURELIUS's problem.
  • Asia-Pacific: LAND-CH targets Japan (Americas segment), Australia, Southeast Asia. Chinese vendors dominate China and much of developing Asia; LAND-CH is non-China APAC focused.
  • Structural outlook: duopoly structure in Americas is durable over a 5-year horizon given platform switching costs. The medium-term risk is if cellular-native meter companies (e.g., Itron Riva network using cellular) reduce the platform moat — a technology evolution risk rather than a competitive entry risk. Itron and LAND-CH both face this same risk simultaneously.

§4.5 — Long-Term Growth

  • Americas: second-wave AMI — communications upgrades (5-7 year cycle post initial deployment), grid-edge software services, EV load management, demand response, VPP integration. US grid investment is structurally elevated for the foreseeable future driven by data centre load growth, electrification, and grid reliability requirements.
  • Asia-Pacific (continuing): Australia and Japan are expanding AMI programmes. Backlog +92% in FY2024 suggests meaningful acceleration. Small base (~USD 164m FY2024 revenue) limits near-term group impact.
  • Grid-edge intelligence: distribution grid visibility software (DERMS, DRMS, VPP integration) is a genuine incremental TAM as utilities manage increasing distributed energy resources. This TAM is likely USD 500m-1bn within LAND-CH's addressable universe over 5-7 years — real but early stage.
  • Services mix shift: if software/services reaches 40-50% of revenue from ~20-25% currently, EBITDA margin could converge toward 18-20% over 5-7 years — structurally re-rating the stock toward a software/services multiple. This is the long-duration bull case. It is directionally credible but not yet supported by reported P&L data. ❌ Surveillance item.

§5 — FUTURE

Sell-Side Coverage and Consensus

  • Coverage: LAND-CH is covered by a limited sell-side panel (SIX-listed, Swiss institutional focus plus some global coverage). Approximately 5-8 analysts active; key houses include UBS, Vontobel, Berenberg, Baader Helvea, Bank of America (cross-coverage from US industrials/energy).
  • Consensus (as of May 2026 search data): ratings broadly "Hold" with buy/hold mix; consensus 12-month price target range ~CHF 50-80, average ~CHF 57-73 (range reflects divergent pre/post-EMEA divestment assumptions).
  • EPS revision trajectory: FY2024 was a material earnings miss (margin miss vs guidance); FY2025 continuing ops guidance subsequently raised (13.0-14.5% Adj. EBITDA). Estimates for FY2025/26 will need significant revision upward for continuing ops once EMEA is cleanly stripped out of models — this creates a potential positive surprise for analysts who have not yet updated for the transformation.
  • US relisting potential: a Nasdaq listing would expand the institutional investor base dramatically (US tech, US energy, ETF inclusion); LAND-CH currently absent from most US technology or clean energy ETFs. This is an underappreciated potential re-rating catalyst.

Recovery Thesis — What Needs to Improve

Three conditions for re-rating:

  1. Margin recovery confirmed: FY2025 adj. EBITDA margin at 14.4% (continuing ops) is first data point. FY2026 needs to show further progress toward 15-17% (Itron peer range). Timeline: 1-2 years of evidence needed.

  2. US relisting executed: management targets US listing by end-2026. If achieved, valuation re-rating from ~7.5x to ~12-15x EV/EBITDA (Itron comparator) would be significant. The listing itself does not create economic value, but removing the SIX-vs-Nasdaq liquidity/investor discount is material. Risk: US tariff/macro uncertainty may cause delay.

  3. Services mix shift visible in P&L: if management discloses formal software/services revenue disclosure (percentage or absolute) showing growth to 30%+ of revenue, this changes the multiple conversation from industrial hardware to software/services. Timeline: 3-5 years.

What does NOT need to improve:
- EMEA: already removed.
- UK execution: already legacy under EMEA (now AURELIUS).
- CEO: transition complete with credible appointment.
- Backlog: already record; demand visibility is not the problem.

Share Price Context

  • All-time high: CHF ~91 (September 2024 — briefly); more sustained peak ~CHF 80-85 in 2021.
  • Current (March 2026): ~CHF 50.
  • De-rating from peak: ~40% from the 2021 highs.
