EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

External value-chain forces provide structural tailwinds, with over 55% of Novonesis sales positively correlating with crude oil prices as biosolutions become more cost-competitive than petrochemicals, while biological pure-play businesses exhibit lower relative exposure to energy and petroleum-derived raw materials than traditional chemical ingredients peers. However, complex regulatory frameworks across 45 countries can cause delays in marketing new biosolutions, and enzymes are classified as substances of concern under the EU Chemical Strategy for Sustainability due to potential respiratory risks. FMCG customer inventories remain slightly above mid-cycle averages suggesting limited near-term restocking tailwinds, while agricultural and animal health sales are highly susceptible to timing shifts which temporarily depressed Q4 2025 growth.

The net investment case rests on Novonesis executing a rare biological pure-play growth story supported by structural demand tailwinds, unmatched R&D capabilities, and demonstrated delivery credibility, while investors must balance this against elevated capital requirements and execution risks inherent in the post-merger integration phase.
