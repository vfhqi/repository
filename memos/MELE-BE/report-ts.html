Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible investment case for Melexis is strengthening as the automotive sector transitions from severe destocking to a restocking phase, bolstered by long-term content expansion in lighting, ADAS, and robotics

KEY RECOVERY AND CONTENT GROWTH DRIVERS

1.
Cyclical auto restocking: The first growth driver is the anticipated shift from destocking to restocking in the automotive supply chain during late FY2026 and FY2027, which is highly plausible as customer inventories have fallen to low levels
2.
Thermal management tailwinds: The second driver is the rapid content growth in electric and hybrid vehicle thermal management, which touches high-value pressure and current sensor lines, and is highly credible given robust hybrid demand
3.
Comfort premiumization expansion: The third driver is the expansion of ambient and animated interior cabin lighting, which increases the use of smart LED drivers and is backed by design wins with Chinese and European carmakers
4.
Steer-by-wire ADAS penetration: The fourth driver is the penetration of steer-by-wire and brake-by-wire safety systems, which utilize custom Triaxis magnetic position sensors, and carries high plausibility due to increasing driving autonomy

STRATEGIC LOCALIZATION AND LONG-TERM ADJACENCIES

5.
Localized Chinese foundries: The fifth driver is the local China-for-China foundry sourcing strategy using local fabs like HH Grace and local packaging partners, which is highly plausible to reduce lead times and counter domestic pricing pressure
6.
Robotics optionality expansion: The sixth driver is the long-term expansion into humanoid robotics using Tactaxis tactile sensors and motor drivers, which is highly promising but carrying low near-term earnings predictability because mass commercialization is not expected until post-2027
7.
Compound success requirement: A successful bull case requires several structural drivers to fire in parallel, particularly gross margin normalization and Chinese volume recovery, which raises the compound probability of execution risks
8.
Other side of the story: The rapid shift to pure battery electric vehicles in Western markets could act as a structural drag because Melexis' market share and total content value per car in electric platforms is estimated to be lower than in hybrid or internal combustion drivetrains

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Melexis faces significant, high-credibility operational risks from its extreme supplier concentration on X-FAB and intensifying pricing competition from Chinese domestic semiconductor vendors

CORE OPERATIONAL AND SUPPLY CHAIN RISKS

1.
Single point failure: Wafer supply is highly concentrated with X-FAB fabricating approximately 43.00% of Melexis' wafers in FY2025, which exposes Melexis to severe manufacturing or financial disruptions
2.
Chinese domestic competition: Local Chinese analog vendors like Novosense are aggressively qualifying pin-compatible sensors and drivers, which threatens to commoditize low-complexity parts and erode historical gross margins
3.
Automotive cycle exposure: Near-term growth is heavily exposed to global car production volumes, meaning that prolonged macro-economic weakness or high interest rates will delay the highly anticipated restocking cycle
4.
False friend signal: Expanding design wins are a misleading performance indicator because converting these wins into meaningful revenue takes several years and does not guarantee near-term volume recovery
5.
Inventory load risk: Melexis is intentionally maintaining elevated inventory levels of around €300.00m, which supports manufacturing plant utilization but carries high obsolescence risks if the automotive recovery stalls
6.
Early warning indicator: The key early-warning signal to monitor is the quarterly book-to-bill ratio of X-FAB, which serves as a highly reliable leading indicator for Melexis' downstream wafer demand
7.
Other side of the story: The bear concerns are overstated because Melexis' transition to advanced 0.11mu silicon-on-insulator technology and 12-inch wafers in China significantly lowers its manufacturing cost base
