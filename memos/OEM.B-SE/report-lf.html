EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

CEO Quality and Leadership Credibility Jörgen Zahlin has delivered world-class operational performance over his 24-year tenure, with the company improving its return on invested capital from a range of 10% to 22% during 2000–2019 to a peak of 28% to 29% in 2023–2024 .

Over the 10-year period ending in 2025, OEM delivered a total shareholder return of approximately 800%, translating to a 25% CAGR, which stands up remarkably well against the 26% average CAGR of best-in-class compounder peers Addtech, Indutrade, Lagercrantz, and Lifco .

Zahlin demonstrates a strong owner-mindset by holding equity worth approximately 6x his annual base salary , substantially exceeding standard alignment benchmarks.

Zahlin consistently under-promises and over-delivers by setting multi-year cycle targets instead of precise quarterly guidance, with the group delivering an EBITA margin of 15.0% in 2025, comfortably exceeding the 12% cycle floor .

However, acquisitions contributed only approximately 3% annually to sales growth between 2015 and 2024, representing a highly conservative execution playbook that is significantly lower than the approximately 10% average growth added by acquisitive serial-acquirer peers .

2 Governance Robustness and Structural Concerns EQT acquired 73.7% of the total voting rights in 2025, taking over control from historical family owners, with EQT's voting power of 73.7% versus capital of 26.3% representing a highly asymmetric control ratio of 2.8x .

Six of the seven current directors were first elected in 2025 during the EQT transition, with average board tenure dropping to 1.43 years compared to the legacy board's multi-decade involvement .

Partners Matthias Wittkowski and Albert Gustafsson are not independent of EQT, and the Audit Committee lacks complete independence from major shareholders due to Wittkowski's seat .

Six of seven board members own zero direct Class B shares in the company, with the 86% of directors without direct stock ownership representing a major deviation from peer compounders like Addtech . No material weaknesses or financial restatements were identified in the 2025 annual audit reports, and zero environmental or material commercial disputes are currently pending against any Group companies .

Insider Transaction Signals and Alignment Patterns The four largest shareholder families sold approximately 75% of their collective equity stakes during early 2025, divesting about 26% of the company's total capital to EQT Partners at SEK 110 per share, with the family block reducing its collective voting power from approximately 77% down to 19% after the sale .

Selling families retained roughly 25% of their historical holdings to remain aligned with the long-term compounder business, though this transaction represented the largest insider divestment in the company's 43-year history since its 1983 public listing .

Managing Director Jörgen Zahlin holds 183,645 Class B shares directly, representing a massive skin-in-the-game commitment, with Zahlin's personal equity position worth approximately 6x his annual base salary .

Approximately 60 senior managers subscribed to 95,000 call options in the new 2025-2028 program, with the newly issued 2025 options carrying an exercise price of SEK 166.70 per share, and option holders receiving a gross salary bonus equal to the option premium if they remain employed for 24 months .

The massive founding family exit to EQT at a substantial premium raises questions about whether insiders viewed long-term value creation as fully realized at current valuations. 10 Expert Network Alignment and Operational Reality Expert network feedback reveals substantial disconnects between management narrative and operational reality across multiple dimensions.

Managing Director Jörgen Zahlin's planned retirement at age 62 in 2026 introduces critical leadership succession uncertainty, with Henrik Forsberg Schoultz hired as Deputy CEO in 2026 to ease the complex leadership transition .

Schoultz brings McKinsey and CellMark corporate background, creating a potential cultural mismatch risk with OEM's deeply-rooted, decentralized entrepreneurial culture.

Private equity firm EQT assumed ownership in March 2025, initiating an ambitious long-term strategy to accelerate the historical pace of acquisitions, with EQT and the new board broadening the M&A focus from traditional technical trading companies to include product-owning niches to capture higher complementary margins .

This transition occurs against a backdrop where the legacy team has minimal large-scale M&A integration experience, OEM operates without a separate internal audit function , and remuneration guidelines fail to disclose any explicit clawback provisions to protect against aggressive, value-destructive acquisitions.
