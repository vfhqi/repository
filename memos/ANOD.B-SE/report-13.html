§1 — Current Management Guidance

Addnode does not issue point-estimate EPS or revenue guidance in the Nordic reporting tradition; what exists is a combination of rolling qualitative direction-setters from quarterly reports and occasional medium-term framing from the CEO/CFO. The analytical challenge is characterising what has actually been "promised."

  • Verdict — §1: Guidance is qualitative-directional, not hard-numerical — lower accountability bar than Nordic software peers; current posture is cautiously optimistic on organic growth and margin expansion [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit FY revenue target issued; CEO (Staffan Hanstorp) has guided "continued organic growth in line with market + selective M&A bolt-ons" — consistent with ~5–8% organic revenue growth framing from Q4-2024 earnings call [#I]

  • FY current — EBIT: Not guided explicitly; medium-term EBITA margin framing is "towards 14–15% group EBITA margin over the medium term" — EBITA currently tracking ~12.5–13% (FY24A); gap of ~150–250bps is the live credibility test [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No EPS guidance issued — Addnode does not guide on EPS; EPS forecasts are entirely sell-side derived; this is a meaningful red flag for an investor whose thesis requires 20%+ EPS CAGR visibility [#I]

  • FY current — FCF: Not guided; management references "strong cash conversion" as a stated objective (FCF/EBITDA >80%) but no annual target is committed [#I]

  • Medium-term — N2Y/N3Y: The operative medium-term target articulated in 2024 annual report: organic growth "above market" (implied ~5–8% given Nordic software market growth), EBITA margin "14–15%", and capital allocation "primarily M&A-led compounding" [#I]

  • Source — written: Primary guidance source is the Q4-2024 results release (Feb 2025) and FY2024 Annual Report; no standalone investor day presentation issued in the 2022–2025 window [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Q4-2024 call (Feb-25) and Q3-2024 call (Oct-24) provided the most substantive colour; CFO confirmed "margin improvement trajectory remains intact despite Q3 Design Management softness" [#I]

  • Update timing: Guidance updated quarterly via interim reports; no formal annual guidance reset event — incremental quarterly colour is the update mechanism [#I]

  • Caveat — M&A: All medium-term targets explicitly stated as "organically plus M&A as opportunities arise" — M&A timing is unpredictable and can inflate reported revenue while temporarily compressing margins, creating false reads against targets [#I]

  • Caveat — segment mix: Design Management (~40–45% revenue) is exposed to public-sector IT budgets and PLM licence cycle timing; management has flagged this as "inherently lumpy" — sets a lower credibility bar for that segment [#I]

  • Conditional assumption — SaaS transition: Medium-term margin expansion is partly conditional on Design Management customers completing licence-to-SaaS migration, which management guided would be "substantially complete by 2026" — now tracking behind initial schedule [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §1: The absence of hard EPS targets, and the "medium-term" framing for EBITA margin goals without a defined end year, allows management to perpetually defer accountability; apparent quantification (14–15% EBITA margin) masks an unanchored timeline — the most aggressive embedded assumption is that margin improvement happens on a 2–3Y horizon without specifying which fiscal year [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • Verdict — §3: Split personality — revenue guidance is honest-to-conservative (credible); margin/EPS guidance is structurally over-optimistic (problematic for EPS-trajectory thesis) [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Revenue guidance meets the sandbagging definition on a narrow reading — consistently delivered at or above implied range; Carnegie FY24 preview noted "management has an established pattern of conservative organic growth framing, then beating modestly" [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBITA: ❌ EBITA margin guidance is persistently optimistic; management frames a "margin improvement trajectory" every period and then delivers below the implied starting-range floor — this is a structural over-promise pattern, not a one-off [#A]

  • Honest tracker — revenue organic: On organic revenue specifically (stripping acquisition contributions), Addnode tracks reasonably well to its "above market" framing; the Nordic PLM/CAD/PDM software market grew ~4–6% organically FY22–24, and Addnode organic growth was ~5–7% — honest tracking [#A]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 4 of 4 L4 periods delivered revenue at or above implied range — consistent; Vitec Software achieves comparable revenue-beat consistency (3-4 of 4 periods per Carnegie L3Y data) [#A]

  • Miss consistency — EBITA: ❌ 4 of 4 L4 periods missed EBITA vs initial framing — bottom-quartile behaviour vs Nordic software peer group where median is 2 of 4 EBITA misses per Carnegie peer-sector note [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No clear improvement trend in EBITA delivery — the miss magnitude has ranged -5% to -10% without systematic narrowing over L4 periods; if anything, Q1-2025 showed renewed margin pressure [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat (revenue): Average revenue beat +0.5–1.5% — modest, not aggressive sandbagging; closer to honest tracking with a small conservative tilt [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss (EBITA): Average EBITA miss -5–10% — material; in the context of a 12–13% delivered EBITA margin, a 5–10% miss on the EBITA line equates to 60–130bps margin miss per period [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — FCF: FCF has an independent miss pattern partly separate from EBITA — driven by working capital dynamics and acquisition timing; Redeye flagged FCF conversion "below stated objective of >80% EBITDA for second consecutive year" in FY24 note [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §3: The "conservative revenue, aggressive margin" pattern is dangerous precisely because it appears credible on the headline metric (revenue) while the economically critical metric (margin) consistently disappoints; there is no evidence the margin sandbagging cushion exists — each new period's margin "target" appears to be management's genuine expectation, not a conservative anchor [#J]

  • Margin assumption — SaaS transition: ❌ Assumes Design Management SaaS migration "substantially complete" by FY26 unlocks ~100–150bps margin improvement (higher SaaS gross margin vs perpetual licence); this transition is running ~12–18M behind initial schedule per Q4-2024 call [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Shares outstanding have been broadly stable (minor buyback programme); no dilution risk from large equity issuance in current M&A strategy [#I]

  • Macro assumption — currency: ~30–35% of revenue is in EUR/USD (export-facing manufacturing customers); SEK weakness in FY23 was a modest tailwind; current assumption is broadly neutral FX [#I]

  • Macro assumption — Nordic IT spending: Assumes Nordic public-sector IT spending stabilises / modestly recovers in FY25–26 after FY23–24 softness — a debatable assumption given Swedish fiscal consolidation backdrop [#A]
