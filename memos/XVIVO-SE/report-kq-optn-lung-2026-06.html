KQ Research [C] — XVIVO-SE — US Lung Transplant Volume & Machine Perfusion Trends

Stage: DD / Revenue Monitoring
Source: [C] Public domain — OPTN/SRTR annual data reports, HRSA press releases, peer-reviewed literature
Date: 2026-06-22 (amended same day — post-primary-verification pass)
KQ: What is the trajectory of US lung transplant volumes (total and machine-perfusion sub-segment) over the last 12 months and on rolling 4W/8W/12W/QTD horizons, and what does this imply for Xvivo's near-term revenue?
Word target: ≥3,000w
Role: RESEARCHER [C] — no grades; APM grades.

KEY FINDINGS - Record volumes, structural inflection: 2023 US lung transplants hit 3,080 — first year to exceed the pre-COVID peak (2,759 in 2019) — driven by the March 2023 Continuous Distribution policy change, which lifted the transplant rate +16% within 12 months. 2024 extended the record to 3,404 (+10.5% YoY). The policy-driven step-change, not a cyclical recovery, is the correct causal read.

  • EVLP penetration still low but accelerating: Ex vivo lung perfusion reached 6.5% of US lung transplants in 2022 (148 cases, n=2,743) and was used by 61% of all US transplant centres. The absolute EVLP volume ceiling rises mechanically as total transplant volume grows — from a 2,743 base, 6.5% = 148 EVLP cases; from a 3,404 base at the same rate, 6.5% = 221 EVLP cases. That structural lever is the primary commercial driver for Xvivo's XPS system.

  • DCD growth is the dominant EVLP tailwind: DCD lung donors rose from 3.7% (2017) to 7.4% (2022) of total. DCD lungs are disproportionately evaluated via EVLP (marginal organ quality requires functional assessment). As DCD donor volumes accelerate — driven by the anoxia/overdose epidemic now accounting for the majority of deceased donors — EVLP penetration rate should rise independently of total transplant volume growth.

  • Absolute 2024 EVLP volume estimate (derived): Applying the 2022 penetration rate (6.5%) to 2024 total volume (3,404) yields ~221 EVLP cases. Even a conservative 7.5% penetration (modest uplift from DCD growth) yields ~255 cases — a ~70% increase in absolute EVLP volume vs the 2018 baseline (101 cases at 4.8%).

  • Near-term weekly/monthly granularity is not publicly accessible: The OPTN weekly metrics dashboard is a JavaScript-rendered Tableau tool; no open API or downloadable file exists. Rolling 4W/8W/12W/QTD trend data cannot be independently validated from public sources. This is a Critical Data Gap for the monitoring mandate and must be addressed via Xvivo quarterly reporting or investor day disclosures.

  • CD policy durability is the key risk: The 2023 surge is partly a one-time expansion in eligible recipients (sicker patients now waitlisted); if recipient pool quality deteriorates (higher primary graft dysfunction, worse 1-year survival), clinical practice may moderate. Early data does not show this — but it is the non-consensus downside the consensus mapping ignores.

§1 — KQ Definition + Mission Command Brief

J-front verdict: The US lung transplant volume trajectory is strongly positive and structurally accelerating; the KQ is partially resolvable from annual public data but not at the weekly granularity originally specified.

  • KQ stated: What is the trajectory of US lung transplant volumes (total and machine-perfusion sub-segment) over the last 12 months, on rolling 4W/8W/12W/QTD basis, and what does this imply for Xvivo revenue?

  • Higher intent: Xvivo derives the majority of its revenue from EVLP (XPS system + STEEN solution) and Perfadex Plus (cold static preservation). EVLP carries ~10× the ASP of static preservation. A rising US lung transplant volume directly grows the static addressable market; rising EVLP penetration grows the more valuable machine-perfusion sub-market. The investor cares about: (a) total procedure volume trend (TAM expansion), (b) EVLP penetration rate trend (ASP mix shift), and (c) any near-term inflection signals that would accelerate or decelerate both.

  • Adjacent KQs:

  • What is Xvivo's EVLP market share vs OCS Lung (TransMedics) in the US? [separate KQ]
  • Does the CD policy durably increase recipient pool quality or does it admit marginal candidates at higher failure rates? [separate KQ]

  • What is the timeline and penetration trajectory for EVLP in Europe? [separate KQ]

  • Stage context: DD/ongoing revenue monitoring. Xvivo is commercial in the US for EVLP (XPS FDA-cleared); CD policy is the pivotal policy event that created the current positive backdrop. Research sits on the critical path for revenue model calibration.

  • Sceptical bullet: If the CD policy surge is a one-time bolus of previously-ineligible patients clearing the backlog, volume growth could normalise or plateau in 2025-2026 without implying any structural change to the long-run EVLP opportunity.

§2 — Total US Lung Transplant Volume: Historical Series and Structural Trend

J-front verdict: US lung transplant volumes have compounded at ~4-5% annually since 2012, with a CD-policy-driven step-change in 2023 that appears durable based on 2024 data.

  • 12-year CAGR 2012-2024: Approximately 5.5% per annum. Pre-CD policy (2012-2022): ~4.6% CAGR. Post-CD policy run rate (2023-2024): ~10.4% annualised — though the 2023 figure contains a one-time bolus effect.

  • CD policy step-change mechanics: The Continuous Distribution lung allocation policy was implemented March 9, 2023. Within 12 months, the transplant rate rose from 281 to 327 transplants per 100 patient-years — a 16.4% increase in the rate. Waiting list mortality fell 29%. The policy replaced the Lung Allocation Score with a continuous score ranking candidates across a broader set of attributes, improving organ-to-recipient matching and reducing geographic discontinuities.

  • Volume in 2024 confirms durability: The 2024 total (3,404) represents a further +10.5% on the already-elevated 2023 base — ruling out a simple "bolus clears, volume reverts" outcome. If anything, the 2024 growth rate accelerating above the pre-CD trend suggests the policy created genuine structural expansion, not just a timing pull-forward.

  • Bilateral transplant dominance: 80.0% of 2022 lung transplants were bilateral (vs. single lung), a share that has risen over time. Bilateral transplants require larger STEEN solution volume and longer EVLP run-times — beneficial for Xvivo's per-procedure economics.

  • Recipient age skew to 65+: The 65+ cohort grew 63.5% since 2011 (from 36.9% of waitlist). Older, sicker recipients have lower-quality donors — another structural tailwind for EVLP (marginal donors require functional assessment).

  • Centre expansion: Transplant centres performing ≥1 lung transplant annually have expanded steadily. More centres = broader commercial coverage for Xvivo's field team.

  • Non-consensus read (Concept A): The 2016 LAS-era peak at 2,345 was followed by only modest growth for three years. There is a historical precedent for policy-driven surges stalling when the "matched" population is exhausted. The CD policy may have a larger structural effect than LAS (because it is more comprehensive), but analysts pricing in 10%+ annual growth beyond 2025 are extrapolating from only two data points.

  • Sceptical bullet: The 2024 data shows the growth rate continued at 10.5%, but the base effect is increasingly unfavourable. A return to the pre-CD 4-5% CAGR from 2025 onward would still be strongly positive for Xvivo but would imply a significant deceleration from consensus growth models.

Confidence: High — anchor: OPTN/SRTR Annual Data Reports are the primary registry source; figures are internally consistent across multiple publications.

§3 — Machine Perfusion Sub-Segment: Penetration Rate and Absolute Volume

J-front verdict: EVLP penetration is rising but remains at low single-digit percentages of total US lung transplants; the absolute volume effect from total transplant growth is mathematically more significant in the near term than penetration rate expansion.

Note: Year-by-year EVLP data for 2019-2021 not reported in public OPTN/SRTR publications; the AATS/Mayo Clinic analysis (UNOS database, Feb 2018-Dec 2022) provides the most complete annual series available. A 2024 JHLTONLINE paper (OPTN/UNOS data Jan 2013–Jun 2023, n=23,807, EVLP n=813) reports a national EVLP penetration of approximately 5.8% across the study period. A separate 2024 JTCVS paper (UNOS data through June 2023) reports 5.7% for the 2018–June 2023 window — consistent with the 6.5% 2022 annual rate, given the aggregate includes earlier lower-rate years. Both papers confirm the upward trend from 2018 to 2023 with national penetration approaching but not exceeding 7% at any single recent year.

  • Absolute volume mechanics: The EVLP TAM in procedures is total lung transplants × penetration rate. At 3,404 total transplants (2024) and a conservative 6.5% rate, ~221 EVLP procedures are implied — compared with 101 in 2018. Even with zero penetration rate growth, the total transplant volume surge since 2018 (+33%) has driven ~119% growth in implied EVLP case volume.

  • Centre penetration is the more actionable metric: The jump from 24 centres (34% of all) to 43 centres (61%) from 2018 to 2022 shows adoption is broad-based and accelerating. At 61% centre penetration there is still headroom — 39% of centres are not yet using EVLP at all, and the highest-volume centres running EVLP likely run it at rates well above the national 6.5% average.

  • Two competing EVLP systems: In the US, clinically available EVLP platforms are Xvivo's XPS (STEEN solution) and TransMedics OCS Lung. TransMedics's overall OCS market share across all three organs (heart, lung, liver) was 13.8% (2023) → 20.9% (2024), equating to 3,715 total US OCS cases in 2024 (+58% vs 2,347 in 2023) per TransMedics FY2024 earnings (February 27, 2025). This is blended across all organs — no organ-specific breakdown is published. OCS Lung competes directly in the machine-perfusion space, though Xvivo is described in the literature as the established market leader in lung EVLP. No public US Xvivo-specific market share percentage has been published.

  • EVLP case economics for Xvivo: Xvivo ASP for EVLP (XPS system + STEEN solution consumables) is approximately 10× static Perfadex Plus ASP. If 2024 EVLP volume is ~221 cases vs 2018's 101 cases, and each case generates 10× the revenue of a static case, the EVLP sub-segment alone has grown approximately 120% in revenue terms on volume alone — before any ASP inflation.

  • DCD lungs and EVLP selection bias: DCD donors represented 7.4% of lung donors by 2022, up from 3.7% in 2017. DCD lungs are more likely to be assessed via EVLP because the organ quality is more variable (warm ischaemia during circulatory death creates uncertainty about function). As DCD volumes grow — particularly as the "abdominal DCD" practice extends to thoracic organs — EVLP evaluation rates should rise above the 6.5% national average. NRP (normothermic regional perfusion, an in-situ alternative) is used in 9.3% of DCD lung cases but NRP does not preclude post-procurement EVLP; the two are not mutually exclusive.

  • Non-consensus read (Concept A): The 6.5% US penetration is substantially below European adoption rates at leading centres (Toronto General, Hannover, Vienna all report EVLP in 20-40% of their transplants). The US is not a laggard — it is in early-middle adoption. There is a credible path to 10-15% US penetration over 3-5 years if centre training and EVLP infrastructure are funded — this would mean 340-510 EVLP cases/year at 3,400 total volume.

  • Sceptical bullet: EVLP centre adoption (61%) is not the same as EVLP case penetration (6.5%); many centres adopt EVLP for marginal-donor cases only and may never reach double-digit utilisation rates. The bottleneck is not centre adoption but the clinical comfort to use EVLP on a higher fraction of all donor lungs — which requires RCT-level evidence showing equivalent or better outcomes vs direct transplant from standard-quality donors.

Confidence: Medium — anchor: EVLP penetration data confirmed at 2018 and 2022 from a peer-reviewed UNOS analysis; 2023-2024 estimates are derived and unverified.

§4 — Donor Dynamics: DCD Acceleration and Quality Trends

J-front verdict: The structural shift toward DCD donors and anoxia/overdose deaths is the most durable tailwind for both total lung volume and EVLP adoption; it is largely independent of policy and likely to persist for 5-10 years.

  • DCD lung trend:
  • 2017: 3.7% of lung donors were DCD
  • 2022: 7.4% of lung donors were DCD — doubling in five years
  • 2024: DCD donors overall reached 7,280 (largest single-year increase in history; +23.5% vs 2023)

  • The 7,280 total DCD donor figure across all organs implies material ongoing growth in DCD lung availability

  • Anoxia (overdose) as cause of death: By 2022, anoxia had become the most common cause of death for lung donors, accounting for the largest and fastest-growing share of lung-eligible deceased donors. Anoxia donors tend to be younger and healthier lungs than CVA/stroke donors (the historical majority), partially offsetting quality concerns from DCD warm ischaemia. This is a net positive for lung transplant volumes.

  • Nonuse rate rising (bearish offset): The lung nonuse rate rose from 4.2% (2011) to 9.0% (2022) — a 112% increase. Lungs from older donors (55+) have the highest nonuse rate (14.0%). This partially offsets the DCD volume growth. EVLP directly addresses the nonuse problem — "rescue" of marginal or borderline lungs is one of the core clinical arguments for EVLP, and centres with EVLP capability have structurally lower discard rates.

  • Standard vs. increased-risk donors: Interestingly, OPTN data shows standard-risk donor lungs had a higher nonuse rate (9.5%) than PHS increased-risk donors (6.9%). This counterintuitive finding may reflect organ allocation logistics rather than clinical quality decisions — centres more aggressively accept PHS-risk organs when the recipient-donor matching is otherwise ideal.

  • NRP for DCD lungs: NRP is an in-situ technique (performed before organ recovery) used in 9.3% of DCD lung transplant cases in the US (2020-2024 OPTN STAR data, Williams et al. 2025). NRP and ex-situ EVLP can be complementary — NRP to mitigate ischaemia at procurement, EVLP to evaluate and recondition post-procurement. No published data yet shows that NRP reduces subsequent EVLP rates.

  • Non-consensus read (Concept A): The nonuse rate increase (4.2% → 9.0% since 2011) is an under-discussed problem that EVLP directly monetises. If EVLP reduces the 14% discard rate for 55+ year donors to even 8%, the incremental EVLP cases from "rescued" organs at growing centres would be additive to the volume models above. This is the "hidden" market — EVLP as a discard-prevention tool rather than purely an organ-assessment tool.

  • Sceptical bullet: DCD lung availability in the US is materially lower than DCD heart (7.4% of lung donors vs 17.9% of heart donors by 2024). This gap reflects clinical reluctance — lungs are more sensitive to warm ischaemia than hearts. If clinical practice does not evolve to embrace DCD lungs at higher rates, the DCD tailwind for EVLP may be less powerful than the DCD tailwind for NRP in hearts.

Confidence: High — anchor: DCD percentage data drawn directly from SRTR ADRs; nonuse rate confirmed by the 2022 Lung ADR abstract.

§5 — Competitive Landscape: EVLP Market Dynamics

J-front verdict: Xvivo holds the established market-leader position in US lung EVLP with XPS/STEEN; TransMedics OCS Lung is the primary competitor but no public market share split exists.

  • Xvivo XPS (eXtra Vivo Lung Perfusion) System: US FDA PMA-approved EVLP platform (PMA P180014, granted April 26, 2019 — not a 510(k) clearance; PMA requires clinical evidence of safety and effectiveness, a higher standard). Prior to full PMA, XPS held an HDE (Humanitarian Device Exemption, 2014) with volume restrictions. STEEN solution (the perfusate) is a proprietary, single-use consumable that generates recurring revenue per procedure. Perfadex Plus holds approximately 90% market share in traditional cold static lung preservation globally — a separate, high-volume, lower-ASP revenue stream (the 90% figure is Xvivo's own disclosure of global share; a US-specific figure is not independently published).

  • TransMedics OCS Lung: Part of TransMedics's three-organ OCS portfolio (heart, lung, liver). TransMedics's overall OCS market share across all three organs (heart, lung, liver) was 13.8% (2023) → 20.9% (2024), equating to 3,715 total US OCS cases in 2024 (+58% vs 2,347 in 2023) per TransMedics FY2024 earnings (February 27, 2025). No organ-specific breakdown is published. The company targets 10,000 OCS cases/year across all organs in the US by 2028.

  • Competitive differentiation: OCS Lung uses a normothermic (warm, ventilated) perfusion approach during transport — combining transport and evaluation in one device. Xvivo XPS evaluates the lung at the recipient centre (static cold transport followed by EVLP at destination). The clinical debates around "which is better for which donor category" are unresolved in the RCT literature and create differentiated clinical niches rather than winner-take-all dynamics.

  • Implication for Xvivo: Xvivo's per-procedure economics are protected by the XPS system lock-in (centres buy the XPS platform, then purchase STEEN solution per case). TransMedics competition is primarily at the centre acquisition level, not the repeat-revenue consumable level, making established Xvivo centres relatively sticky.

  • Centre adoption dynamics: 43 of 70 US lung transplant centres (61%) had adopted EVLP by 2022 — but this includes XPS users AND OCS Lung users and any other approach. Xvivo's specific centre count is not public. The observation that the highest-volume regions (Texas, Midwest) have the highest EVLP rates suggests commercial penetration is positively correlated with centre volume — Xvivo benefits disproportionately from volume growth at existing high-adopter centres.

  • Non-consensus read (Concept A): TransMedics's liver OCS has shown strong traction and the company's logistics/perfusion aviation infrastructure (OCS transplant flights) could eventually bundle lung OCS as a service rather than purely a device. This logistics moat, if it extends to lung, would be a competitive threat Xvivo cannot replicate with a static EVLP system.

  • Sceptical bullet: The absence of any public US market share data for Xvivo EVLP specifically means the bull case (Xvivo as clear EVLP market leader) rests on analyst commentary and company disclosure, not independent registry data. Market share could be materially lower than assumed if TransMedics OCS Lung has penetrated faster at new centres post-FDA clearance.

Confidence: Medium-Low — anchor: competitive data relies on secondary sources; no independent market share split confirmed.

§6 — Near-Term Rolling Trend Data: Availability, Proxies, and Limitations

J-front verdict: Weekly/monthly US lung transplant data is not publicly accessible; the best available proxies are OPTN's annual press releases and Xvivo's quarterly earnings commentary.

  • OPTN weekly dashboard: The OPTN maintains a real-time interactive dashboard at hrsa.gov/optn (Tableau-based) showing monthly and weekly transplant volumes. This data is not accessible via static web fetch — it requires a live JavaScript session. No downloadable CSV or API endpoint exists in the public domain. This is the primary barrier to fulfilling the "rolling 4W/8W/12W/QTD" mandate from public data.

  • Best available rolling proxies from public data:

  • Annual totals (OPTN/SRTR ADR): Published approximately 12-18 months after the reference year. 2024 ADR published in early 2026.
  • Preliminary press releases : Published in January following the reference year, within ~3 weeks of year-end. These give annual totals but no monthly granularity.

  • CD policy monitoring report: OPTN published a lung transplant CD policy monitoring report (data through April 25, 2025) showing quarterly-level data on waiting list additions and transplant rates. This confirms the post-2023 trend is sustained through at least Q1 2025.

  • QTD Q2 2026 estimate (derived): If 2024 ran at 3,404/year = ~851 per quarter, Q2 2026 (assuming ~+5% YoY growth on a stabilising trajectory) would imply ~893 lung transplants in Q2 — but this is a modelled estimate, not verified from public data.

  • Rolling 4W/8W/12W: At 3,404/year, implied run rate = ~65 transplants per week. A 4W rolling window = ~260 transplants; 8W = ~520; 12W = ~780. These are volume estimates, not case-by-case data. EVLP within those windows: at 6.5% penetration, ~17 EVLP cases per 4W window nationally.

  • The OPTN metrics dashboard as the solution: The only path to real rolling weekly trend data is the OPTN interactive dashboard. It should be accessed via a Chrome session (Claude in Chrome tool), navigating to hrsa.gov/optn and selecting the lung transplant metrics view. Given the JavaScript rendering barrier encountered in this research session, this is flagged as a recurring operational friction requiring a workaround (e.g. dedicated Chrome-based data pull in the next session).

  • Xvivo Q1 2026 earnings — the best available near-term signal: Xvivo's Q1 2026 results (April 28, 2026) provide the most current available proxy for US EVLP run rates: global EVLP disposables +56% YoY; thoracic segment net sales SEK 160m, organic growth +27% (+19% ex-CAP heart trial revenue); OPO hub model — 10 procedures in Q1, second OPO onboarded Q2, 4–5 further OPOs in pipeline; Flowhawk monitoring software live at six of the ten largest US transplant centres. Xvivo does not disclose US revenue or US case counts as a standalone line item. These figures are directional and global-level, not US-specific rolling metrics.

  • Non-consensus read (Concept A): ⚡ The CD policy monitoring data (through April 2025) shows the transplant rate held at the elevated post-policy level, but the absolute transplant volume in Q4 2024 and Q1 2025 is not independently published. If seasonal effects (winter allocation, holiday disruption) are larger than typical, short-run rolling windows could show apparent deceleration that is purely seasonal. Any 4W or 8W signal from Xvivo earnings would need to be benchmarked against OPTN seasonal norms.

  • Sceptical bullet: The inability to access rolling weekly OPTN data from public sources is a structural limitation, not a temporary friction. It means the "weekly trend monitoring" mandate cannot be fulfilled from this public-source [C] research stream; it requires either OPTN dashboard access or proprietary data from Xvivo.

Confidence: Low — anchor: rolling granular data is confirmed unavailable from public sources; proxies only.

§7 — US Lung Transplant Survival Outcomes: Clinical Context for Volume Durability

J-front verdict: Lung transplant survival has improved modestly over the study period; the CD policy has not impaired outcomes in early data, supporting volume durability.

  • 1-year post-transplant survival (2021 cohort): 87.8% (adult). 5-year survival (2017 cohort): 59.6% (adult). Lung survival is the worst of any major transplant organ (kidney ~95%/yr; liver ~88%; heart ~91%) — reflecting the uniquely challenging immunobiology of lung transplantation.

  • Survival has improved vs. the 2012 era: The 5-year survival rate for the 2017 cohort (59.6%) compares favourably to historical benchmarks, reflecting improvements in immunosuppression and post-transplant management. This improving trajectory supports long-run demand for transplantation.

  • CD policy and recipient quality: The CD policy expanded the eligible recipient pool to include sicker candidates (higher acuity) who previously did not receive transplants. This creates a theoretical risk of worse post-CD outcomes. Early OPTN monitoring data (through April 2025) does not show deterioration — but the follow-up window is short (max 24 months for 2023 transplants).

  • EVLP outcomes vs. standard cold storage: Peer-reviewed data (including the DEVELOP-UK RCT and multiple single-centre series from Toronto, Vienna, and Hannover) confirms EVLP lungs have equivalent or better outcomes vs. standard cold storage, with particular benefit in marginal-donor lungs. EVLP's outcome data underpins continued centre adoption. (Note: the NIHP European RCT referenced in some prior research relates to Xvivo's heart perfusion system, not lung EVLP — the lung-specific RCT data is from DEVELOP-UK and single-centre series.)

  • Primary graft dysfunction risk: PGD is the most common early complication post-transplant and is the primary clinical risk from accepting lower-quality organs. EVLP reduces PGD incidence by reconditioning marginal organs before implantation. If the CD policy drives higher rates of extended-criteria donor acceptance, EVLP becomes more, not less, relevant.

  • Non-consensus read: ⚡ A single high-powered RCT showing EVLP equivalent — not superior — outcomes in standard-quality donor lungs (rather than only marginal donors) would be a commercial inflection point: it would justify EVLP use in the 93.5% of lungs currently transplanted without machine perfusion. No such trial is currently published or registered with confirmed US enrolment.

  • Sceptical bullet: If PGD rates rise in the post-CD era (sicker recipients, more marginal donors) and the field attributes this to poor donor quality rather than recipient fragility, clinical practice could pull back from the expanded CD recipient pool — capping volume growth at 2023-2024 levels rather than extrapolating to 2025+.

Confidence: Medium — anchor: survival data from SRTR 2022 ADR confirmed; CD policy outcome data preliminary only.

SECOND + THIRD-ORDER IMPLICATIONS

J-front verdict: The non-consensus implications of the CD policy and DCD expansion are potentially more valuable than the headline volume numbers — particularly the EVLP-as-discard-prevention thesis and the organs-per-donor efficiency dynamic.

  • Second-order — Marginal donor pool expansion fuels EVLP faster than headline growth suggests: As CD policy admits sicker recipients, allocating committees face pressure to expand the donor pool by accepting more marginal organs. EVLP is the enabling technology for this expansion. The CD policy is not just a volume driver — it is an implicit EVLP demand driver one step removed.

  • Second-order — Bilateral transplant dominance (80%) amplifies Xvivo per-procedure revenue: EVLP for bilateral transplant (most common procedure type) requires a longer run, more STEEN solution, and higher technician costs than single-lung.   • ❌ TransMedics competitive pressure at greenfield centres: The 39% of centres without any EVLP are the growth frontier. TransMedics's bundle offering (OCS Heart + Lung + Liver) may win commercial priority at centres choosing their first machine perfusion platform.

AGGREGATE OUTLIERS (⚡)

  • ⚡ Decentralised EVLP at OPO level (flagged above in §5): If OPO-level EVLP normalises in the US, the addressable market expands from ~70 transplant centres to ~58 OPOs — multiplicative, not additive. This is the non-consensus upside scenario for Xvivo's total addressable US market.
