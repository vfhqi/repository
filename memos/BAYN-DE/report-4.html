  • Crop Science gross margin has deteriorated significantly — estimated ~40-42% in FY2024 vs ~48% in FY2022

  • Bear case: structural glyphosate price erosion means Crop Science never returns to pre-2022 margins; group ceiling is ~25%

  • Productivity: R&D productivity is a critical margin lever that is currently a drag — Bayer spends approximately 14-15% of sales on R&D, above the ~12% pharma/ag peer median [#A]

  • Post asundexian Phase 3 failure (Jan-2024), R&D productivity credibility suffered

  • Management committed to R&D rationalisation but pipeline optionality is needed for the equity story

  • Sceptical bullet: What is the inversion case on margin direction? Through what mechanism might the margin expansion narrative prove one-off? The cost programme savings (~€500-600m) are real but one-time in nature — once captured, the next margin driver must come from revenue volume recovery. If Crop Science volumes disappoint in FY2025 (weather, commodity pricing, competitive dynamics), the cost savings will be absorbed by deleverage and margin will stay flat or worsen, not expand [#J]

§8 — Quality of Earnings & Accounting

  • Verdict: Earnings quality is materially below peer median — the gap between adjusted and GAAP earnings is exceptionally large, FCF conversion is poor, and non-cash adjustments include items (litigation, impairments) that are recurring in effect if not in name [#J]

  • Non-GAAP gap: The FY2024 gap between adjusted EPS (~€4.96) and GAAP net result (~€-4.30 per share) is approximately €9-10bn in absolute terms [#A]

  • This gap is composed of: litigation charges (~€3-4bn), impairments (Crop Science goodwill ~€2-3bn), amortisation of acquisition intangibles (~€1.4bn), restructuring (~€0.5bn)

  • The impairment and litigation items, while classified as "one-offs," have recurred in each of the last 3-4 fiscal years — they are effectively recurring charges that adjusted EPS permanently excludes

  • One-offs: Litigation charges have appeared in every fiscal year since FY2020 — by definition, a charge that recurs annually is not a one-off [#J]

  • Peers: Novartis, Sanofi, and AstraZeneca carry meaningful adjustments but none with this frequency and scale of recurring litigation reserve builds

  • ⚡ RARE: This makes Bayer's adjusted EPS a structurally optimistic measure — the "true" normalised EPS is likely €1-2/share below the reported adjusted figure on an economic basis

  • Cash conversion: FCF conversion vs adjusted earnings is approximately 10-30% in FY2024 — vs a typical peer norm of 60-80% [#A]

  • FY2024 FCF approximately €0.5-1.5bn vs adjusted net income ~€4.9bn

  • Primary FCF drags: litigation settlement cash payments (~€1-2bn annually), restructuring cash cost (~€0.5bn), elevated capex for Crop Science normalisation

  • OCF vs EPS: Operating cash flow is substantially below adjusted EPS on a per-share basis — the divergence has widened over L3Y as litigation cash outflows have grown [#A]

  • This is the single most important earnings quality red flag — adjusted EPS flatters economic earnings power

  • NWC days: Working capital dynamics are mixed — Pharmaceuticals NWC is broadly in line with peers (~70-80 days net); Crop Science NWC is elevated due to seed and chemical inventory build timing [#A]

  • Crop Science by nature carries high seasonal NWC (inventory built Q4/Q1, sold Q1/Q2) — this is structural and not a quality concern per se

  • However, elevated channel inventory in 2023-24 meant Crop Science NWC did not release on schedule — a drag on FCF

  • Receivables days: No unusual receivables aging signals from public filings — Pharma DSO approximately 60-70 days, consistent with prior periods and peer norms [#A]
