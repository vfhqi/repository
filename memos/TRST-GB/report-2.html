Trustpilot Change Forces: IG Memo Ask Question Key Findings (BLUF)

Trustpilot is experiencing a meaningful structural inflection as it successfully pivots to enterprise customers and monetizes its proprietary data moat in an AI-driven search ecosystem, driving rapid margin expansion. The company delivered 20% constant currency revenue growth in FY25 to $261m, massively outperforming Yelp's sluggish ~5% guidance and Tripadvisor's outright declines. Adjusted EBITDA margins expanded a massive 420bps year-over-year to 15.6% in FY25, demonstrating severe operating leverage that starkly contrasts with Yelp, which expects ~300bps of margin compression in FY26 due to heavy product investments.

ARS • 17 Mar

Jefferies Research • 8 May

Enterprise adoption is surging, with customers paying over $20,000 annually growing 35% year-over-year in 2025, deeply contrasting with Yelp, which suffered a 5% decline in paying advertising locations during late 2025. Management dramatically upgraded medium-term margin targets to 25% by 2028 and 30% by 2030, completely separating Trustpilot from consumer-internet base rates and repositioning it as a high-margin B2B SaaS platform. The platform ranked as the 5th most cited domain globally on ChatGPT in early 2026, with AI search click-throughs surging 1,490% year-over-year, driving high-intent consumer traffic.
