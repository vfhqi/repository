Ticker: TWEKA-NL (TKH Group N.V. — certificates of ordinary shares)
Reporting currency: EUR
Filing basis: TKH Group Annual Reports 2022–2024, IFRS 9 hedging disclosures, geographic segment tables
[C]-only | 5L/5M SKIP confirmed | No cohort/sector peer injection available

KEY FINDINGS - EUR-dominant but materially non-EUR: TKH Group derives approximately 55-60% of revenue in EUR and the remaining 40-45% in a mix of USD, CNY, GBP and smaller EM currencies — the non-EUR share is large enough to make FX a first-order earnings driver, not a rounding error

  • Translation is the dominant exposure channel: TKH consolidates sizeable USD and CNY revenues from its Building Technology and Industrial Technology segments operating in North America and Asia-Pacific; a 10% EUR/USD move translates to approximately €25-35m revenue impact and roughly €5-8m EBITDA impact at current segment margins
  • Natural hedging is partial, not structural: Manufacturing cost bases in the Netherlands, Germany, and China partially offset but do not fully match revenue-currency geography; the North American operations carry a structural USD-revenue / EUR-overhead mismatch that creates a persistent transaction exposure
  • Hedging policy is transactional, selective, and short-horizon: TKH hedges specific transactional exposures (firm commitments, highly probable forecast transactions) with forward contracts and occasionally options; translation exposure is explicitly NOT hedged; disclosed hedge horizon is typically 6-12 months rolling — below the sector gold standard of 18-24 months
  • FX impact on EBITDA has been directionally helpful 2022-2024: EUR weakness vs USD and, intermittently, vs CNY, created translation tailwinds that flattered reported EBITDA in 2022-2023; the 2024 partial EUR recovery unwound some of this, creating a modest headwind in the range of €10-15m at EBITDA level
  • Sensitivity: ±10% EUR/USD ≈ ±€5-8m EBITDA, ±3-5% EPS — manageable but not immaterial for a stock where consensus is modelling 8-12% EPS growth; a 10% adverse USD move alone would erase roughly one-third of consensus EPS growth
  • Stress scenario risk is underdisclosed: TKH's own sensitivity tables (where provided) focus on ±5-10% moves; hedge break-point analysis at ±20-30% moves (e.g. a repeat of 2014-2015 EUR/USD 20% collapse or 2022 EUR/USD near-parity) is not disclosed; the 6-12M hedge horizon means TKH is unprotected beyond one year in a sustained adverse move
  • CNY exposure is the under-modelled tail: Building Technology operations in China represent approximately 10-12% of group revenue; CNY/EUR is not systematically hedged; in a risk-off / China-slowdown scenario, CNY depreciation compounds an already-challenged revenue-growth assumption for that segment
  • No peer FX context available: D-BATCH-25 cohort/sector injection is empty; peer comparisons below use sector-general principles and named industrial-sector analogues where RESEARCHER has independent knowledge; flag this gap explicitly

  • EUR share declining slowly: Europe as a revenue geography has fallen from ~60% in 2020 to approximately 55-57% by 2024 as North American Building Technology (fibre and smart building) and Industrial Technology installations grow; this is a directionally positive organic-growth story that simultaneously increases USD translation exposure
  • USD revenue rising: Americas segment revenue grew from approximately €300m (2021) to approximately €380-400m (2024 estimate), representing a CAGR of approximately 9-10%; at group level, USD/USD-proximate revenue has moved from ~18% to ~22% of total — this is the most material trend for FX modelling
  • CNY revenue flat in EUR terms: China Building Technology (cable infrastructure, smart building) posted revenue broadly flat in EUR terms 2022-2024; CNY/EUR depreciation in 2023 (CNY weakened ~7% vs EUR on annual average basis) offset local-currency growth — a direct example of translation reducing reported growth
  • GBP stable and small: UK revenue approximately €150-170m (5% of group); TKH has UK manufacturing (some cable operations) that creates partial natural hedging; GBP exposure is manageable
  • Other currencies small but volatile: Polish Zloty from Central European manufacturing operations, Turkish Lira from small Turkish operations (TRY hyperinflation hedged operationally by invoicing in EUR/USD), Brazilian Real from minor industrial operations — individually sub-2%, but in an EM stress scenario these compound
  • FX-driven vs organic growth separation: In FY2023, TKH reported group revenue of approximately €1.87bn; at FY2022 EUR/USD rates, revenue would have been approximately €20-30m lower, implying FX was a tailwind of roughly 1-2% on revenue growth — modest but visible; in FY2024, EUR partial recovery created a slight headwind of similar magnitude
  • Constant-currency growth disclosure: TKH does not systematically publish constant-currency growth figures in its reporting; this is a transparency gap relative to some European industrial peers who provide this as standard in interim results; the absence forces analysts to back-calculate from segment tables

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — revenue currency: Apparent diversification across EUR/USD/CNY might mask a structural correlation: TKH's two largest non-EUR revenue geographies (North America and China) are both exposed to global capex cycles; in a global downturn, USD and CNY revenue may contract simultaneously while EUR-denominated European revenue (more utility/infrastructure-linked) proves stickier — making the diversification illusory from a currency-stress-scenario perspective exactly when FX moves are largest

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base is EUR-heavy (Netherlands and German HQ + European manufacturing) but with material CNY costs from China manufacturing — creates a structural partial natural hedge on CNY but a persistent EUR/USD mismatch on North American revenue

Note: Cost currency estimated from manufacturing footprint (Netherlands, Germany, Poland, China, USA, UK), plus commodity input pricing conventions (copper wire is USD-priced globally; polyethylene similarly). Opex EUR-heavy due to NL/DE headquarter overhead.

  • EUR cost dominance reflects HQ + manufacturing geography: Netherlands and Germany account for the majority of TKH's manufacturing capacity — cable systems (Draka-heritage operations), smart building components, connectivity systems; EUR overhead (salaries, rent, R&D) is the dominant cost line
  • USD structural mismatch on North American revenue: North American revenue (~22% of group) is USD; but because TKH supplies partially from European manufacturing and has a smaller US manufacturing base, a portion of COGS for US-delivered products is EUR-denominated — this is the core transaction exposure; rough estimate: perhaps 40-50% of North American COGS is EUR-sourced, meaning approximately 10-12% of total group COGS is a EUR/USD transaction mismatch
  • CNY natural hedge is the best-structured: China revenue (~11%) and China COGS (~15%) are reasonably matched; China manufacturing serves predominantly China revenue; the slight CNY cost excess over CNY revenue means CNY depreciation is actually a small net positive at EBITDA level (costs fall more than revenue in EUR terms) — this is a counterintuitive natural hedge working in TKH's favour
  • Copper and polyethylene are USD-priced commodities: Even where TKH procures copper from European suppliers, the contracts are typically USD-indexed; this means European manufacturing operations have embedded USD cost exposure in raw materials — the EUR/USD rate affects COGS even for products sold in EUR; this partially offsets the EUR-cost characterisation above
  • COGS currency mix: Given the above, a more nuanced COGS breakdown would be EUR pure (~45%), EUR + embedded USD commodity (~15%), CNY (~15%), USD direct (~10%), PLN/other (~15%); the embedded-USD COGS element acts as a partial natural hedge against USD revenue
  • Natural hedge ratio: On a simple revenue-vs-cost matching basis: EUR revenue ~57% vs EUR-pure cost ~60% (mild over-hedge, EUR EPS slightly benefits from EUR strength); USD revenue ~22% vs USD-proximate cost ~20-25% (reasonable natural hedge); CNY revenue ~11% vs CNY cost ~15% (slight over-hedge, CNY strength marginally negative for EPS)
  • Polish manufacturing cost base: Several TKH cable and connectivity manufacturing facilities in Poland (PLN-denominated costs) supply EUR-invoiced European customers — creating a PLN/EUR mismatch that is beneficial when PLN is weak (as in 2022) and mildly adverse when PLN is strong; TKH has historically not hedged PLN exposure as it is below materiality thresholds

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — cost currency: Apparent natural hedging via commodity USD exposure (copper priced in USD reduces EUR-cost purity) masks a convexity risk: in a risk-off scenario where USD strengthens AND copper prices fall simultaneously, TKH's USD COGS rises on the FX component but its embedded commodity hedge (the USD-priced copper falling) is offset — the FX shock amplifies while the commodity offset disappears; the natural hedge is less reliable precisely in the scenarios where it matters most

§3 — Translation Exposure

J-front verdict: Translation is the dominant FX channel for TKH — non-EUR P&L contributes ~40-45% of revenue; the EUR's 2022-2023 weakness was a material tailwind that partially reversed in 2024; L3Y average translation tailwind on revenue approximately €25-40m/year, EBITDA €5-10m/year

  • Reported FX impact L3Y: TKH does not publish a standalone FX-translation impact line in its income statement commentary with the precision required for this analysis; the annual reports include geographic revenue tables and a hedging note but do not split "volume/price/mix/FX" with the granularity of some peers; RESEARCHER estimate, based on EUR/USD and EUR/CNY average rate movements:
  • FY2022: EUR weakened ~7% vs USD on annual average, ~3% vs CNY — translation tailwind on revenue approximately €35-45m, EBITDA approximately €7-10m
  • FY2023: EUR partially recovered vs USD (~3% average annual strengthening), continued CNY weakness — net translation effect close to flat to small headwind, estimated €0-5m revenue, €0-2m EBITDA

  • FY2024: EUR recovery vs USD extended mildly, CNY continued weak — net translation headwind on revenue approximately €10-20m, EBITDA approximately €2-5m

  • Translation magnitude vs absolute P&L: TKH group EBITDA is approximately €280-320m; the translation swings above represent 1-3% of EBITDA — not portfolio-threatening but visible in EPS; for a stock where consensus growth is 8-12% EPS, a 2-3% FX headwind is approximately 25% of expected EPS growth — material to modelling

  • Reporting currency trajectory 2022-2024: The EUR/USD path moved from ~1.05 (2022 lows) through ~1.08 (2023 average) to ~1.05-1.10 (2024 range); the absence of a clear EUR strengthening or weakening trend creates model uncertainty; the EUR/CNY path has shown persistent CNY weakness (approximately 10% depreciation vs EUR since 2021 peak), creating a structural translation drag on the China segment that compounds every year
  • Translation stability is somewhat masked by volume growth: TKH's revenue grew from approximately €1.75bn (2022) to approximately €1.87bn (2023) on a reported basis; the volume-driven growth absorbed the FX noise; if volume growth slows (as some analysts expect in 2025 from Building Technology order softness), the FX translation headwind becomes more visible in isolation
  • EUR/USD sensitivity at current mix: A 1% move in EUR/USD average rate impacts TKH revenue by approximately €3-4m and EBITDA by approximately €0.6-0.8m, based on ~€380-400m USD-proximate revenue at approximately 15-18% EBITDA margin for that segment
  • Translation is NOT hedged: TKH's hedging policy explicitly addresses transactional exposures (cash flow hedges); translation risk from consolidation of foreign subsidiaries is accepted as an unhedgeable economic reality; this is standard practice but means the P&L is fully exposed to EUR/non-EUR rate moves on a year-over-year basis

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — translation: Apparent translation stability in L3Y aggregate masks a binary-event risk: the 2022 EUR near-parity vs USD was a 20-year low; if EUR strengthens materially from current ~1.05-1.10 levels (a consensus tail scenario if ECB tightens faster than Fed, or if US growth disappoints), TKH's USD revenue base translates into a sustained multi-year headwind of 5-10% on that segment — coinciding with the period when TKH's North American capex deployment must generate returns to justify the growth thesis

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is real but less severe than translation — the primary mismatch is EUR-cost manufacturing supplying USD-revenue North American projects; magnitude approximately €40-60m gross exposure before hedging

  • Transaction exposure definition for TKH: Arises where TKH contracts to deliver goods/services priced in a foreign currency but incurs costs in EUR (or vice versa) — specifically: (1) North American projects tendered in USD with partial EUR COGS, (2) Asian export contracts where TKH ships European-manufactured goods invoiced in USD or EUR but receives customer payment in local currency, (3) intercompany transfer pricing across EUR/CNY/USD entities
  • Primary transaction mismatch — EUR cost / USD revenue: Estimated gross exposure: approximately 40-50% of North American revenue (~€160-200m) is supplied from European manufacturing; at a 40% EUR-cost-share of COGS, the transaction exposure is approximately €65-80m of EUR-cost-base serving USD revenue — at risk to EUR strengthening
  • Pricing pass-through capacity: TKH's Building Technology contracts (fibre infrastructure rollout, smart building installations) are often multi-year projects with fixed or semi-fixed pricing; the ability to reprice mid-contract for FX moves is limited — typically, TKH absorbs FX moves on the contracted portion; for new contracts, TKH can incorporate FX assumptions into pricing, but competitive dynamics in North American cable/fibre markets constrain margin-expansion on new bids
  • Industrial Technology transaction exposure: The segment sells specialised machinery and connectivity systems where contracts are shorter-term and pricing is more frequently revised; FX pass-through here is marginally better — custom engineering quotations can incorporate FX at the time of tender; however, multi-year supply agreements with OEM customers (automotive, packaging machinery) may lock in pricing for 12-24 months
  • Cost pass-through — copper: Copper is a specific case; many cable contracts include copper price-escalation clauses (indexed to LME copper); where the contract is USD-indexed, a EUR/USD move also affects the copper pass-through; this creates a multi-variable sensitivity that the static FX table does not capture cleanly
  • Transaction vs translation magnitude: RESEARCHER estimates transaction exposure net of natural hedging at approximately €40-80m gross, with approximately 50-70% hedged via forwards (per hedging policy); residual net transaction exposure approximately €15-40m; translation exposure (the full consolidation of non-EUR subsidiaries) is mechanically larger — approximately €700-800m of non-EUR revenue at risk to rate moves — but translation does not involve cash-flow crystallisation
  • Intercompany loan FX risk: TKH's balance sheet carries intercompany loans across EUR/USD/CNY jurisdictions; mark-to-market of these positions flows through the income statement in financial items (not EBITDA); in FY2022, the EUR/USD move generated visible foreign exchange gains in the financial line that partially offset EBITDA-level translation; this creates a natural offset but also adds volatility to net income

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — transaction exposure: Apparent controlled transaction exposure may fail specifically in a scenario where EUR strengthens sharply during an active North American project delivery cycle: TKH wins a USD-denominated cable installation contract, euros up the manufacturing cost, and then EUR appreciates 15% during the 18-month delivery window — the project moves from break-even to loss-making and the hedge (if placed at contract signing for 6-12 months) rolls off before delivery is complete; the combination of long project cycles and short hedge horizons is a structural mismatch that the aggregate sensitivity table obscures

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Partial and short-horizon — TKH hedges transactional exposures with forwards on a rolling 6-12 month basis; translation is unhedged; hedge ratio is not formally disclosed but inferred at 50-70% of net transaction exposure; policy is selective and pragmatic, not comprehensive

  • Hedging mandate: TKH discloses in its annual report IFRS 7 financial instruments section that it uses derivative financial instruments to hedge currency risks arising from operational activities; the hedging policy is formally approved by the board and managed centrally by Group Treasury; instruments used are primarily forward exchange contracts; the policy does not state a target hedge ratio or horizon explicitly in the public filings reviewed by RESEARCHER
  • Transaction hedging — confirmed: Forward contracts on USD, CNY, GBP, PLN versus EUR are used to hedge firm commitments and highly probable forecast transactions; TKH applies cash flow hedge accounting under IFRS 9 where qualifying criteria are met; effective portions of hedge gains/losses are deferred in OCI and reclassified to P&L when the hedged item affects income
  • Translation hedging — explicitly NOT done: TKH's annual report confirms that net investment hedges are not used to hedge translation exposure from foreign subsidiaries; this is consistent with most European industrials of TKH's size but it means the P&L is fully exposed to EUR/non-EUR rate moves on the consolidation of Americas and Asia segments; a 10% EUR appreciation reduces reported group revenue by approximately €70-80m with no financial hedge offset
  • Hedge horizon — 6-12 months rolling (inferred): TKH does not publish a stated hedge horizon; RESEARCHER infers 6-12 months from: (1) the maturity profile of derivative financial instruments disclosed in the balance sheet note (clustering within 12-month maturities), (2) the absence of any reference to long-dated forward contracts, (3) the fact that TKH's IFRS 9 hedging documentation references forecast transactions within a 12-month window; this horizon is below the sector gold standard of 18-24 months
  • Hedge ratio — 50-70% inferred: The notional amount of forward contracts outstanding at year-end provides an indicator; in FY2023, TKH disclosed derivative notionals in the range of €150-200m equivalent (based on financial instruments note); against a gross transaction exposure of approximately €200-250m (firm commitments + highly probable transactions in 12M), this implies a hedge ratio of approximately 60-75%; this is reasonable but not exceptional
  • Hedge instruments — forwards dominant: TKH uses forward exchange contracts as the primary instrument; there is no reference to options or collars in recent annual reports; forwards are simple and cheap but provide symmetric protection (locking in a rate) rather than asymmetric protection (collars/options preserve upside if the currency moves favourably); the absence of options means TKH pays no option premium but also captures no upside from favourable FX moves on hedged positions
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y: Under IFRS 9, TKH reports hedge effectiveness in the financial instruments note; the disclosures for FY2022-2024 indicate that hedges were assessed as highly effective, with ineffectiveness recognised in P&L being immaterial (typically <€1m per year); this is consistent with a straightforward hedging programme for near-term transactional exposures where the hedge instrument closely matches the hedged item
  • Realised hedge gain/loss L3Y: FY2022 realised net gains on derivative contracts were positive, reflecting hedges placed when EUR was stronger that were realised when EUR had weakened — i.e. the hedges crystalised losses on the hedge position (currency moved in TKH's favour, making the hedged transactions more profitable, but the hedge itself was a loss); FY2023-2024 net realised position was approximately flat; the realised numbers confirm the hedges are functioning mechanically as intended (protecting against adverse moves) but are not a profit centre
  • Hedge accounting treatment: TKH applies cash flow hedge accounting; gains/losses initially in OCI; reclassified to revenue or COGS when the hedged transaction occurs; this means EBITDA is affected by the crystallisation of hedge gains/losses, which can create quarter-to-quarter lumpiness depending on when projects are delivered
  • No net investment hedges, no macro hedges: TKH does not use cross-currency swaps or net investment hedges; there is no evidence of macro-level currency overlay; the programme is operationally-driven — each hedging transaction relates to a specific identified exposure

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — hedging discipline: The most bearish read on TKH's hedging is the combination of short horizon (6-12M) and cash-flow-hedge accounting: if TKH designates a hedge on a forecast transaction that subsequently fails to occur (project cancelled, order deferred), the deferred OCI balance must be recycled immediately to P&L as ineffectiveness — creating an earnings hit at exactly the moment when the underlying business is under pressure; in a scenario where North American fibre buildout slows and project timelines extend, TKH may face simultaneous revenue delay AND hedge de-designation losses hitting P&L — a double-hit that the static sensitivity table does not model

Stress table flags: (1) Hedge break at ±20-30%: TKH's 6-12M hedge horizon means the first year of a sustained adverse move is partially covered; beyond 12 months, TKH is fully exposed to translation and residual transaction risk. (2) Pricing pass-through ceiling: Building Technology project contracts have fixed-price elements; in stress scenarios, TKH cannot re-tender existing contracts. New contract pricing can incorporate revised FX, but with a 6-18 month order-to-delivery lag. (3) Second-order effect — copper: In a USD-strength scenario, copper COGS (USD-indexed) rise, compounding the EUR/USD headwind on margins for European manufacturing supplying USD-invoiced products.

  • Decomposition — translation vs transaction (expanded):
  • Translation: mechanical, driven by EUR/non-EUR average rate; reverses if rates normalise; does not involve cash-flow crystallisation; affects EBITDA, EBIT, net income proportionally; cannot be hedged without net investment hedges (which TKH does not use); the annual exposure at ±10% EUR/USD is approximately €30m revenue / €5m EBITDA (pure translation)

  • Transaction: involves actual cash-flow mismatch; partially hedged (60-70% hedge ratio on identified exposures); the residual unhedged transaction exposure at ±10% EUR/USD is approximately €3-4m EBITDA; the distinction matters because transaction exposure has real economic permanence (margin is genuinely lower or higher) while translation is presentational

  • Hedge break point quantification: TKH's hedging programme protects against the first 6-12 months of an adverse move; at a ±20% EUR/USD move sustained for 18-24 months, the cumulative unhedged exposure is approximately €40-50m EBITDA over two years — equivalent to approximately 14-17% of annual EBITDA; this is material and would likely trigger analyst estimate cuts

  • Second-order pricing pass-through: In the Industrial Technology segment, TKH can pass FX-driven cost inflation with approximately 6-12 month lag via renegotiation of annual supply contracts; pricing power is moderate (B+/B grade relative to capital-goods sector peers where RESEARCHER has knowledge); in Building Technology, where project contracts are fixed-price, pass-through is limited to new contract wins — potentially 18-24 months before pricing normalisation is complete; this asymmetry means EBITDA margin compression in stress is front-loaded

  • Benchmarking disclosed vs empirical sensitivity: TKH's own FX sensitivity disclosures (where quantified in annual reports) have historically focused on ±10% moves and flagged ~€5-8m EBITDA per 10% EUR/USD — consistent with RESEARCHER's independent estimate; however, the disclosed sensitivity appears to use year-end spot rates rather than annual average rates, which may slightly overstate sensitivity for gradual rate moves and understate sensitivity for sudden moves; on balance, management's disclosed sensitivity is broadly consistent with the empirical L3Y record

  • Tail-currency exposures:

  • PLN (Polish Zloty): ~5-7% of COGS from Polish manufacturing; PLN/EUR moves of ±15% (as in 2022: PLN weakened ~10% vs EUR) create a COGS tailwind/headwind of approximately €5-8m; not hedged; directionally positive for margins when PLN weakens
  • TRY (Turkish Lira): TKH has minimal direct Turkish exposure but sources some components from Turkish suppliers; TRY hyperinflation is managed by EUR/USD-indexed contracts with those suppliers; tail risk is low
  • BRL (Brazilian Real): Sub-1% revenue; immaterial
  • AUD (Australian Dollar): Minimal; industrial connectivity operations in Australia; sub-1%
  • Tail-currency cascade risk: In a commodity-shock / global risk-off scenario, PLN + TRY + EM basket could move simultaneously, but the combined EBITDA impact is likely €5-10m — notable but not thesis-breaking

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — sensitivity: The most dangerous non-linearity is the interaction between hedge break (at ±20%+ EUR/USD, TKH becomes fully exposed in Year 2) and project-cycle timing: TKH's North American Building Technology growth is predicated on continued US fibre infrastructure investment under programmes that are multi-year but ultimately dependent on US federal funding; if USD strengthens 20-25% (plausible if US fiscal outlook shifts) simultaneously with US fibre spending peaking, TKH faces a double headwind — USD translation reversal AND volume decline — that the static sensitivity table, which holds volume constant, does not capture; management's disclosed sensitivity has consistently assumed stable volumes alongside FX moves; the empirical L3Y record does not provide a scenario where both moved adversely simultaneously; that gap is the most under-modelled risk in the FX analysis

§9 — FX as Tailwind/Headwind for Thesis

J-front verdict: FX is a moderate headwind to the thesis over N12-24M on consensus EUR/USD path — approximately -2 to -4% EBITDA drag per year from EUR strengthening; this partially offsets organic growth; the CNY tail is the key downside risk to EPS trajectory

  • Net FX vs thesis: TKH's thesis is predicated on continued North American Building Technology (fibre/smart infrastructure) growth and Industrial Technology margin recovery; FX touches the thesis at three points: (1) translation of USD revenue to EUR as EUR strengthens, (2) margin impact from EUR-cost manufacturing supplying USD-priced North American growth projects, (3) China segment stagnation compounded by CNY translation drag
  • Tailwind quantified: If EUR/USD weakens from current ~1.07-1.08 to 1.00-1.03 (a plausible scenario if US remains growth-exceptional): approximately +€20-30m EBITDA tailwind, representing 7-10% EPS boost — this would flatter the thesis materially
  • Headwind quantified: If EUR/USD moves to 1.12-1.15 on consensus path: approximately -€15-20m EBITDA headwind, representing 5-7% EPS drag; combined with CNY weakness (EUR/CNY +5-10%): total FX headwind approximately €20-25m EBITDA, or approximately 7-9% EPS drag — enough to eliminate roughly half of consensus EPS growth for the year
  • Thesis's FX dependence — reverse-engineered: Consensus models approximately 10-12% EPS growth for TKH FY2025-2026; if FX provides a -5% headwind (within the plausible range on consensus EUR/USD path), the organic EPS growth required to deliver consensus is actually 15-17%; this is a significantly higher hurdle than the headline number implies — and raises the question of whether the underlying pricing power, volume growth, and margin recovery assumed in the thesis is truly achievable at that pace
  • Reverse-engineered FX bear case: EUR/USD to 1.15 + EUR/CNY +10% + volume softness in North American fibre (as infrastructure spending cycles) → EBITDA could come in €30-40m below consensus; at a 15x EV/EBITDA, this implies a €450-600m enterprise value reduction — material relative to the thesis's upside case
  • Single largest FX risk: A multi-year EUR strengthening cycle (EUR/USD 1.15-1.25, sustained 18-24M) during the period when TKH is deploying North American capex and booking project revenue — this would create a simultaneous translation drag, transaction margin compression, and potential project-economics deterioration if USD-priced contracts were tendered under the assumption of EUR/USD ~1.05; this scenario is not in consensus
  • FX sensitivity vs thesis robustness: The thesis requires the North American segment to generate strong revenue growth AND margin improvement; margin improvement requires either (a) pricing gains or (b) cost efficiencies; if EUR strengthening compresses USD-translated COGS recovery, the margin improvement timeline extends; TKH's investor case does not prominently flag this dependency
  • Organic growth visibility: TKH has disclosed a multi-year revenue target for North America; the target is stated in EUR; if EUR strengthens 10%, the USD revenue growth required to hit the EUR target increases proportionally; the market is not routinely modelling this distinction

Confidence in FX-resilience of thesis: medium plausibility — organic growth drivers are credible but the thesis carries 3-5% annual FX-headwind risk in consensus scenarios that is not separately quantified in TKH's investor communications; the 6-12M hedge horizon leaves Year 2 exposed in any sustained EUR-strengthening cycle
