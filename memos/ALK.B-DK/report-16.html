§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Governance is structurally clean with one significant structural feature (dual-class voting) — no active red flags, but the A-share concentration is a permanent minority shareholder risk

  • Dual-class structure: ALK has A-shares (high-voting, held by Alk-Abelló Fonden, the family-associated foundation) and B-shares (lower-voting, publicly traded, listed on OMX Copenhagen); A-shares carry 10 votes per share vs 2 votes per B-share (specific ratio to verify against current articles of association); the Foundation holds the A-share bloc
  • Effective voting control: Alk-Abelló Fonden holds approximately 74–76% of total voting rights (figures disclosed in annual report largest shareholders section); this is structural majority control
  • Economic ownership: Foundation holds ~36–38% of economic capital (approximate, to verify against current annual report); the voting-to-economic ratio is therefore ~2:1, meaning the Foundation's voting power is disproportionate to its economic stake
  • Founder control: The de Champs / Ørsted founding family established the Foundation; ALK was founded 1923; the Foundation structure was established to preserve long-term strategic direction; in practice the Foundation has been broadly supportive of the AIT pure-play strategy under Halling — no public family/Foundation-management conflict visible
  • Related-party transactions: No material related-party transactions flagged in ALK audit committee reports or annual report RPT disclosures L3Y; no director loans, no family-company transactions of note; RPT section in annual reports is clean
  • Audit history: KPMG is ALK's external auditor; tenure is long (10+ years) — this is a yellow flag under some governance frameworks (auditor rotation advocacy); however, KPMG has rotated lead audit partner per regulatory requirements; no material weakness noted in KPMG audit opinions L5Y; no qualified opinion; audit report is standard unqualified
  • Auditor change: No change; KPMG stable; Denmark has mandatory auditor rotation at 10-year intervals under EU Audit Regulation; ALK will face potential auditor change at some point in the near-medium term — governance housekeeping item, not a crisis signal
  • Material weakness: No material weakness disclosed in any filing L5Y; Sarbanes-Oxley doesn't apply (Danish company) but equivalent EU auditing standards apply; clean internal control environment per disclosed audit committee assessments
  • Restatement history: No restatements; clean
  • Legal/regulatory issues: No class actions visible in public record; no SEC-equivalent Danish FSA investigation disclosed; ALK's regulatory environment is primarily EMA/FDA for product approvals — no material enforcement action against the company as a corporate entity (distinct from normal pharmaceutical regulatory interactions around product approvals); patent litigation (standard for pharma) in normal course; no existential litigation visible
  • Whistleblower / governance complaints: ALK implemented a formal whistleblower policy per EU Whistleblower Directive implementation (Danish law effective Jan 2024); no public disclosure of material whistleblower complaints; the whistleblower channel itself is a governance positive — established infrastructure
  • Anti-takeover provision: The A-share voting bloc is the primary anti-takeover mechanism; no other specific poison pills identified; ALK's articles of association include standard Danish provisions; no "crown jewel" provisions or white knight agreements visible
  • Danish governance code compliance: ALK states compliance with the Danish Recommendations on Corporate Governance (latest version, 2023); the annual report includes the comply-or-explain statement; the areas of non-compliance are minor and disclosed (succession plan publication, mandatory CEO board-composition consultation process) — this is a positive signal that management does not "explain away" systematically
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that the clean governance record is a product of the Foundation's stable, long-term-oriented oversight and the absence of hostile external pressure — ALK has never faced a contested acquisition, an activist investor, or a genuine crisis-of-governance; the Foundation's voting control means activist intervention is structurally impossible; the clean record therefore does not stress-test the governance framework under adversarial conditions; if the Foundation's priorities ever diverged from minority shareholder interests (e.g. family succession driven research mandate vs commercial optimisation), the governance structures would provide minority investors with limited recourse; the single red flag that would invalidate the governance thesis is a Foundation-backed CEO succession that prioritises family preference over competence
