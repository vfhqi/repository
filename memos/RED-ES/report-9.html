  • Sub: Cumulative L3Y Hispasat impairments ~€87m; adjusted EPS excludes these, but reported EPS absorbs them below the EBIT line

  • Sub: ❌ Frequency of impairment: three successive FY periods with same-source impairment is textbook "recurring dressed as exceptional" — investors should add these back to adjusted figures with caution rather than full exclusion

  • Non-GAAP gap: Narrow and stable — reported vs adjusted EPS gap is €0.02-0.03 per share per annum, consistent with impairment magnitude; no large non-GAAP bridge items such as share-based comp add-backs, M&A cost exclusions, or restructuring charges inflating adjusted figures [#A]

  • Adjustments trend: Declining in absolute terms — Hispasat impairments falling each year (€38m → €27m → €22m); if trend continues, adjusted/reported convergence improves FY2025-26 [#A]

  • Cash conversion: Strong — OCF/Net Income ~90-95% across L3Y — REE's utility model generates highly predictable operating cash flow; D&A is the primary reconciliation item between EBITDA and OCF [#A]

  • Sub: FY2024 OCF ~€950m vs Net Income ~€655m (implied); OCF/NI ratio ~145% when D&A add-back included — consistent with asset-heavy regulated utility base rate

  • NWC tailwind/headwind: NWC is a structural non-issue — transmission billing is on regulated settlement schedules with CNMC; debtor days are structurally short and stable (~30-35 days) given quasi-sovereign counterparties [#A]

  • Sub: No evidence of NWC build being used to flatter near-term earnings; receivable turns stable L3Y

  • Conservative accounting: RAB depreciation schedules are conservatively long (40+ year grid asset lives) — this is standard regulated utility accounting and provides underlying earnings quality confidence [#A]

  • OCF vs NI: Structurally positive OCF/NI ratio throughout L3Y; no working capital or accruals distortion detected [#J]

  • Pension / liability quality: Spanish utility sector pension obligations are modest vs Northern European peers; RED-ES has funded obligations with no material actuarial surprises in L3Y reporting [#A]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: Structurally stable with modest drift upward — EBITDA margin in 54-56% band is durable for a regulated transmission utility; no evidence of cost-cutting-driven artificial inflation. [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: ~4.0% L3Y (FY21→FY24); entirely tariff/RAB-driven; no volume or mix acceleration component [#A]

  • EBITDA CAGR: ~5.5% L3Y — outpacing revenue by ~150bps on an annualised basis, reflecting operating leverage in REE network ops (staff and maintenance costs growing below tariff revenue) [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Present but modest — REE is a regulated network; cost base is largely fixed (network maintenance, staff, D&A); revenue increments from tariff uplifts drop through at high margin; estimated incremental EBITDA margin on tariff revenue >80% [#J]

  • Gross margin: Not a clean disclosure given utility revenue = regulated pass-through; EBITDA margin is the operative metric; stable ~54-56% EBITDA margin is the relevant measure [#A]

  • EBITDA margin trajectory: H1 2022 (55.1%) → FY2022 (54.3%) → H1 2023 (55.7%) → FY2023 (55.0%) → H1 2024 (56.1%) → FY2024 (55.7%) — clear gentle upward drift of ~140bps over L3Y [#A]

  • EBIT margin trajectory: 33.5% (FY22) → 34.4% (FY23) → 34.8% (FY24) — also drifting upward despite D&A growth; slower than EBITDA margin expansion (~130bps vs ~140bps) but directionally consistent [#A]

  • Margin expansion driver: Primarily the FY2023 tariff reset (structural) + network cost efficiency (structural); partially offset by Hispasat EBITDA drag (cyclical/structural negative) and rising D&A (mechanical) [#J]

  • Sub: Hispasat EBITDA margin is materially lower (~25-30%) than REE (~60%+); if Hispasat revenue share grows, group EBITDA margin would dilute — but Hispasat share has been stable/declining

  • Mix shift: Mildly positive mix shift — REE (higher margin) growing faster than Hispasat (lower margin) in recent periods; not a dramatic effect but directionally supportive [#A]

  • Sustainability: High confidence for 2-3Y forward given regulatory tariff period locked; next CNMC reset is the swing factor; no evidence of margin sustainability being artificially manufactured [#J]

  • Cost cut driver: Cost efficiency is genuine but narrow — OpEx/revenue ratio has improved marginally over L3Y (staff productivity, digitalisation); not the primary driver [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on margin sustainability: if the next CNMC regulatory period (late 2026+) resets allowed returns downward (possible if interest rates moderate and CNMC applies lower WACC), EBITDA margin could compress 100-200bps without any operational deterioration; this would be a regulatory mechanism misread as structural margin strength

  • Seasonality direction: Stable and predictable — H2 tariff settlement adjustments (annual settlement of transmission system charges by CNMC) create slight H2 revenue recognition skew; this is structural and expected [#J]

  • Seasonality changing: Not materially changing over L3Y; H2 skew consistent year-on-year [#A]

  • Cause — production: Spanish electricity demand has modest seasonal variation (higher winter heating, summer cooling); grid transmission volumes track this but are largely pass-through for revenue purposes [#A]

  • Q-level pattern: Not directly observable given semi-annual reporting; management commentary (H1 calls) consistently notes "no unusual seasonality"; full-year guidance is typically reiterated at H1 [#J]

  • EPS seasonality: H1 EPS consistently slightly below H2 given minor settlement and finance cost timing; H1/FY EPS ratio ~48-49% across L3Y — stable [#A]

  • CapEx timing: H2 CapEx recognition slightly higher (construction milestones in network expansion tend to complete H2) — impacts D&A recognition timing marginally [#A]

§8 — Forward Plausibility

Forward plausibility verdict: L3Y track record supports mid-single-digit EPS growth through FY2027; a 25% IRR thesis requires either multiple re-rating or EPS acceleration well beyond what the track record implies — neither is well-evidenced. [#J]

  • Track-record support: L3Y EPS CAGR ~4.5%; FY2024-2027 consensus EPS CAGR implied ~5-6% (per AS sell-side estimates); forward is a modest acceleration vs L3Y, plausibly supported by CapEx programme maturing into RAB and continued tariff visibility [#A]

  • Required CAGR for 25% IRR: At current valuation (~15x forward EPS, ~5% dividend yield), a 25% IRR over 18M-3Y requires either: (a) EPS growing 10%+ pa, (b) P/E multiple expanding from ~15x to ~20x+, or (c) a combination; L3Y provides no evidence of either occurring at the required magnitude [#J]

  • Acceleration required: Any forward acceleration beyond ~5-6% EPS requires a non-base-case event: Hispasat disposal at premium, step-change in RAB allowed return, or large capital recycling; none of these are evident in L3Y data [#A]

  • Margin runway: EBITDA margin has expanded ~140bps over L3Y; at ~55.7% FY2024, the realistic ceiling for a regulated transmission utility is ~57-58% (global regulated network median); ~100-130bps of additional margin runway plausible but not transformational [#A]

  • Quality runway: Cash conversion is already strong (>85% OCF/EBITDA); NWC discipline is structurally embedded; no quality deterioration risk from reaching a ceiling — but quality can't improve much further to boost EPS [#A]

  • Inflection plausibility: The FY2023 tariff reset was the most plausible recent inflection; the next plausible positive inflection is the FY2027+ regulatory period reset; medium confidence that this is positive given current higher-for-longer rate environment (higher risk-free rate → higher allowed WACC) [#J]

  • Dividend yield contribution: RED-ES offers ~5-6% dividend yield at current price; if the IRR thesis includes yield, the required capital gain component drops to ~18-19% over 3Y — still demanding for a regulated utility [#A]

  • Forward EPS implied: FY2025E ~€1.09, FY2026E ~€1.15, FY2027E ~€1.22 (AS consensus range) — 5.5% CAGR consistent with L3Y but not materially ahead [#A]

  • Deceleration scenario: If CNMC 2026 reset is flat or modestly negative (allowed return unchanged or compressed), FY2026-27 EPS growth could slow to 2-3%; this would likely prevent multiple expansion and risk a modest P/E de-rating [#J]
