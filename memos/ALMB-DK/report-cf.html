EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Alm. Brand has emerged as a pure-play Danish P&C insurance franchise following its successful divestment of banking, life operations, and Marine & Energy assets, culminating in the DKK 618 million Codan integration that was completed in 2025. The company now operates a simplified two-segment model targeting Personal Lines via bancassurance partnerships covering 45% of the Danish retail banking market and Commercial Lines through direct and broker channels, although the latter faces deliberate portfolio pruning. CEO Andreas Ruben Madsen assumed leadership on March 1, 2026 , transitioning from CFO to lead a consolidated Executive Board structure that signals centralized cost control and execution discipline. Board governance was simultaneously strengthened through the April 2025 appointment of Chairman Jais Valeur , former Group CEO of Danish Crown, ensuring independent oversight aligned with Nordic corporate standards. The strategic transformation signals a strong net-positive directional shift , pivoting from a diversified financial conglomerate to a streamlined underwriting business targeting an 82% combined ratio by 2028 , which would narrow the profitability gap to peers Tryg's 81% and Gjensidige's 82% long-term targets. The most recent Q1 2026 financial delivery demonstrated robust underlying momentum, with the insurance service result reaching DKK 496 million , up 47% year-on-year , and a combined ratio of 83.1% that beat

conservative guidance by 1.9 percentage points . Personal Lines premiums expanded 6.4% driven by bancassurance indexation and pricing power, while Commercial Lines contracted 1.8% due to intentional exits from unprofitable standalone workers' compensation accounts representing a DKK 50 million growth drag . However, the Q2 2026 outlook was severely disrupted by a retroactive Danish Supreme Court ruling that lowered the workers' compensation earnings loss threshold from 15% to 5% , triggering a DKK 700 million pre-tax reserve provision and forcing management to trim the planned DKK 1.5 billion share buyback by DKK 500 million . Despite this judicial headwind, the underlying claims ratio improved 1.7 percentage points year-on-year , validating the Codan synergy thesis and positioning the company to deliver on its 2028 strategic targets of DKK 2,350 million insurance service result , 16% expense ratio , and 40% return on own funds . Forward guidance credibility is exceptionally strong, as management exhibits a proven sandbagger archetype , consistently establishing conservative targets that are systematically upgraded as underwriting results exceed expectations. Across the last four fiscal periods , the company beat pre-tax profit guidance in three consecutive years , with FY25 earnings of DKK 2.12 billion exceeding the upgraded range by DKK 140 million . The only material historical miss occurred in FY23 , driven entirely by exogenous weather claims totaling DKK 128 million , reinforcing that execution competence remains high when insulated from macro volatility. Management's disciplined forecasting posture creates consistent space for positive surprises , as evidenced by repetitive quarterly guidance upgrades throughout FY25 and a 5.1 percentage point combined ratio beat in Q1 2026 . Earnings sensitivity is highly geared to underwriting margins, with each 50 basis point combined ratio shift translating to a 3.7% EPS impact , while interest rate and equity market exposures remain moderate at DKK 136 million and DKK 110 million respectively per standardized shock.
