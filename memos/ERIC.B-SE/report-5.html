§1 Executive Summary

Ericsson delivered Q1 2026 on 17 April 2026. The quarter was characterised by strong underlying organic momentum (+6% group-wide, all three segments contributing for the first time in several quarters) obscured by three simultaneous headwinds: a SEK 7.8bn FX drag, SEK 3.8bn in elevated restructuring charges, and rising semiconductor input costs flagged explicitly by the CEO. Reported revenue fell 10% YoY to SEK 49.3bn; adj. EBITA fell 20% YoY to SEK 5.6bn; adj. EBITA margin contracted to 11.3% from 12.6%.

  • Verdict: Mechanically mixed quarter — organic growth intact, but margin regression after 9 consecutive expansions is a signal investors cannot ignore; the question is whether it is a one-quarter noise event (FX + restructuring timing) or the start of a new structural pressure regime. #J
  • CQ-1 signal: Networks (core RAN) dominates at 67% of reported revenue (SEK 32.9bn); CSS 24% (SEK 11.8bn); Enterprise small but growing — Ericsson is overwhelmingly a RAN business with a software turnaround as the secondary story. #A
  • Investment implication: The positive FCF story and buyback announcement are genuine positives; the adj. EBITA miss and semiconductor cost pressure are genuine negatives; the bear case is that the margin recovery thesis is more fragile than the 9-quarter run suggested. #J
  • Sceptic note: Q1 tends to be seasonally light in telecom equipment; full-year guidance was not reissued in specificity; management's "broadly similar seasonality" language for Networks guidance is minimal forward commitment. #J

§9 Industry + Sector Context + Sceptical Synthesis

  • Judgement: Verdict: RAN equipment market is structurally subdued with no catalyst for material acceleration before 6G around 2029–2030; Ericsson's near-term fortunes depend on North America capex recovery and semiconductor cost resolution, both of which are uncertain. #J
  • RAN market consensus (Dell'Oro): Global RAN market expected flat in 2026 after flat 2025; top 5 vendors hold 96% share; concentration is high; share shifts between Ericsson and Nokia are the primary competitive dynamic in the observable market. #A
  • North America capex cycle: AT&T and Verizon consolidation (T-Mobile acquisition completed) compressed investment cadence in Q1 2026; comparison base in Q1 2025 was inflated by tariff-front-loaded procurement; Q2 2026 onwards the base effect normalises; underlying North American 5G deployment is mid-cycle. #A
  • AI as headwind, not just tailwind: The Ericsson case is unusual — AI is creating semiconductor supply competition that inflates input costs for RAN equipment manufacturers; this is not a standard sector tailwind; it adds a structural cost pressure not present in Nokia's more software-defined architecture. #J
  • Nokia vs Ericsson divergence: Nokia Q1 2026 comparable profit +93% YoY; reported revenue +3%; Nokia's architecture is more software/IP-networks weighted, less hardware-intensive; Nokia did not face the same semiconductor input cost pressure in Q1; the divergence may persist if AI chip demand remains elevated. #A
  • ❌ 6G timeline risk: ABI Research projects mobile network spending to fall 29% from 2026–2031 before 6G investment begins; if this scenario materialises, the structural case for RAN equipment is negatively framed for 5 years; this is the most significant sector-level bear case. #J
  • Ericsson market share stability: Dell'Oro confirms Ericsson maintained share in 1Q25–3Q25 with modest improvement; the Omdia #1 ranking provides some competitive credibility but is a lagging indicator; Nokia's explicit share gains in 2025 are the more concerning real-time signal. #A
  • Iran/geopolitical context: Richard's current risk-off posture targets non-cyclical recurring revenues; Ericsson's RAN business is capex-cycle dependent, not recurring — low fit with current market positioning framework. #J
  • Sceptical synthesis: Three interlocking structural risks exist simultaneously — flat RAN market through 2026 (volume ceiling), semiconductor cost inflation (margin ceiling), and North America concentration (revenue volatility). Each individually is manageable; the combination limits the bull case re-rating potential meaningfully. The FCF story and buyback are genuine counterweights but may not be sufficient to drive re-rating absent a margin recovery catalyst. Ericsson sits in a "show me" position entering H2 2026 — the Q2 2026 print will be the decisive signal. #J
