3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

SCENARIO 1 — Leverage trap: refinancing cost overwhelms equity IRR

Narrative: ZEG acquired Vodafone Spain using a heavily levered structure; the equity thesis depends on EBITDA growing faster than debt service costs over the hold period. If Spanish base rates remain elevated or ZEG's credit profile deteriorates (churn, revenue miss), refinancing of the near-to-medium-term debt maturity wall will occur at materially higher coupons. Transmission: higher interest expense → reduced free cash flow → EPS/equity value destruction at a non-linear rate due to the operating leverage in a capital-intensive telco asset.

Early warning signals:
- Spread widening on ZEG / Vodafone Spain HY bonds vs. Spanish telecom reference cohort
- Net debt / EBITDA ratio moving above initial underwriting assumptions; watch quarterly
- Management language shift from "deleveraging trajectory" to "managing the capital structure"
- Credit rating agency (Moody's / S&P) outlook change from stable to negative
- EBITDA miss in any single quarter exceeding 3–4% vs. consensus — triggers covenant headroom concerns

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — HY-levered telecom carve-outs have a well-documented base rate of refinancing stress when the underlying asset's EBITDA recovery stalls; comparable structures (e.g. MásMóvil pre-consolidation, Iliad Italy) show this path is not tail-risk

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: existing debt structure with near-term maturities; rate environment not fully normalised
- Motive: debt holders will demand par refinancing or covenant enforcement regardless of equity holder preference
- Opportunity: any quarter where EBITDA misses materially vs. underwriting creates the trigger
- Verdict: all three legs aligned — FULL scenario, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: if ZEG has pre-hedged majority of floating-rate exposure at sub-4% and has no material debt maturity within 36M, this scenario is significantly defanged; evidence of strong EBITDA overperformance vs. underwriting (>5% above) also partially disconfirms

Monitoring checklist:
- Quarterly net debt / EBITDA vs. initial underwriting model
- Bond spread vs. Telefónica Spain / MásMóvil HY curve
- Cash interest coverage ratio trend (trailing 4Q)
- Covenant headroom disclosure in interim/annual reports
- Free cash flow conversion rate (EBITDA → FCF)

Interaction with other failure modes: directly correlated with Scenarios 2 (ARPU/revenue miss), 4 (margin compression), and 8 (capex deferral catch-up); if any two co-occur, leverage trap probability jumps substantially — compound cluster

Sceptical close: disconfirming evidence that would invalidate this hypothesis: ZEG refinances 2025–2027 maturities successfully at ≤5.5% effective cost AND net debt/EBITDA declines consecutively for 3+ quarters — neither has been conclusively demonstrated

SCENARIO 2 — Revenue attrition: ARPU erosion outpaces subscriber recovery

Narrative: Spanish telecom is structurally competitive (Telefónica, Orange, MásMóvil/Bouygues, low-cost MVNOs); ZEG's Vodafone Spain inherited a declining subscriber base and must restore ARPU through convergence offers. If pricing competition intensifies — particularly via MásMóvil following its Bouygues integration synergies — ARPU decline accelerates and subscriber recovery stalls simultaneously. Transmission: revenue line misses → EBITDA misses → cash flow shortfall → leverage deteriorates.

Early warning signals:
- Sequential mobile ARPU decline ≥ 2 consecutive quarters
- Subscriber net-add numbers turning negative after any period of stabilisation
- Competitor promotional pricing moves below ZEG/Vodafone Spain price floor
- B2B contract churn above 5% annualised
- Revenue per convergent customer declining even as convergence penetration rises

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — ARPU erosion has been the defining feature of Spanish telecom for 8+ years; structural pricing competition from a now-integrated MásMóvil/Bouygues entity creates direct near-term pressure; no moat disruption is needed, simply continuation of existing trends

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Spanish telecom market structure; MásMóvil now has scale and cost structure to price aggressively
- Motive: MásMóvil integration synergies create margin headroom to fund promotional pricing
- Opportunity: ZEG's subscriber base is already weakened; promotional windows during integration are common
- Verdict: all three legs aligned — FULL scenario

Disconfirming evidence: if Vodafone Spain demonstrates 2+ consecutive quarters of positive ARPU growth AND positive mobile subscriber net-adds simultaneously, this materially weakens the scenario; structural consolidation of the Spanish market (4-to-3) reducing competitive intensity is partial disconfirmation

Monitoring checklist:
- Monthly/quarterly Vodafone Spain mobile ARPU (absolute + yoy change)
- Net subscriber adds (mobile + fixed)
- Competitor promotional calendar (Orange, MásMóvil)
- Convergence penetration rate (% of base on multi-play)
- B2B revenue growth rate vs. consumer

Interaction: correlated with Scenarios 1 (leverage), 3 (B2B narrative break), 4 (margin compression); revenue miss is the most common triggering event that cascades across the cluster

Sceptical close: disconfirming evidence: ARPU inflects positively and holds for 2+ quarters while the wider Spanish market shows no broad price recovery — this would isolate recovery to execution rather than macro tailwind; not yet demonstrated

Transmission: B2B miss → total revenue miss → multiple compression as the market reprices from "recovery with quality" to "another declining telco."

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:

- Motive: rivals (Telefónica, Orange) have stronger B2B brand equity in Spain
- Opportunity: ZEG early in transformation; B2B customer inertia not yet established under new ownership
- Verdict: all three legs present — FULL scenario, though motive is weaker than Scenarios 1–2

Disconfirming evidence: B2B revenue growing consistently above 5% yoy with named contract wins and expanding customer count would invalidate; successful displacement of Telefónica in any large public-sector contract is strong disconfirmation

Monitoring checklist:

SCENARIO 4 — Margin compression from cost inflation re-acceleration

Narrative: ZEG's EBITDA margin recovery thesis depends on holding operational cost reductions (labour restructuring, vendor renegotiations, energy costs) while growing revenue. If any of these cost lines re-accelerate — Spanish labour market tightening, energy cost reversal, or vendor renegotiations failing at renewal — the cost base rises faster than revenue, compressing margins back toward or below acquisition levels. Transmission: cost base expansion → EBITDA margin contraction → EPS misses → multiple de-rating.

Early warning signals:

- Energy cost per unit of network traffic increasing
- Management guidance language shifting from "cost savings locked in" to "inflationary headwinds"

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — structural cost programmes in telecom frequently underdeliver on timeline; Spanish labour law (EREs) adds friction; energy costs have been volatile; not a high-probability standalone but materially elevated as a correlated risk

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Spanish labour law rigidity; energy commodity markets
- Motive: cost inflation is market-structural, not company-specific
- Opportunity: ZEG early post-acquisition; restructuring savings are front-loaded, risks are back-loaded
- Verdict: two-of-three strong (means + opportunity); motive is exogenous rather than behavioural — classify as WATCH scenario unless energy/labour data deteriorates

Disconfirming evidence: EBITDA margin expanding yoy for 3 consecutive quarters post-acquisition cost programme completion, with explicit disclosure of locked-in savings vs. variable cost base — this would partially disconfirm

Monitoring checklist:

Interaction: directly correlated with Scenarios 1 (leverage) and 8 (capex deferral); cost inflation that erodes EBITDA simultaneously worsens leverage ratios and may force capex deferrals that create further structural risk

Sceptical close: disconfirming evidence: cost savings exceed initial programme targets AND management provides forward cost guidance with explicit floor — neither currently visible in public disclosures

SCENARIO 5 (FALSE FRIEND) — EBITDA "recovery" is deferred capex, not structural improvement

Transmission: apparent EBITDA recovery → capex catch-up cycle → FCF collapse → EPS miss → re-rating reversal at the worst possible moment (investor base fully committed).

What makes this a FALSE FRIEND: EBITDA improving while capex intensity falls looks like operational success; it is actually balance-sheet borrowing from the future; the distinction is not visible in headline metrics until the catch-up is unavoidable

Early warning signals:

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — capex deferral as a lever to improve near-term EBITDA is endemic in levered telecom carve-outs; it is the most common false-friend in the telecom space; comparable precedents: multiple European MNOs post-PE or activist acquisition showing this exact pattern with 18–36M lag before network quality impact

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: capex is discretionary in the short-to-medium term for existing network; can be deferred without immediate customer-visible impact
- Motive: ZEG management incentivised on near-term EBITDA and deleveraging metrics; capex deferral directly serves both
- Opportunity: post-acquisition integration period provides cover for capex reduction framed as "integration efficiency"
- Verdict: all three legs strongly aligned — this is the HIGHEST-PRIORITY false friend in the case

Disconfirming evidence: capex / revenue ratio maintained at or above 17% consistently; network quality rankings stable or improving vs. peers; Opensignal Spain data showing Vodafone Spain speed position not deteriorating — this specific combination disconfirms

Interaction: this scenario is the MOST DANGEROUS because it correlates positively with the YES profile in the short term (EBITDA improving) while actually loading future risk; directly triggers Scenarios 1 (leverage) and 6 (network quality churn) in sequence; false-friend density amplifier for the entire case

Sceptical close: disconfirming evidence that invalidates: ZEG publishes capex intensity data explicitly and maintains ≥17% capex/revenue for 6+ consecutive quarters while network rankings hold — this transparency and metric sustainability has not been demonstrated
