EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Davide Campari-Milano N.V. presents a moderately compelling investment case anchored by superior organic growth trajectory versus global spirits peers, structural margin expansion initiatives , and rapid deleveraging progress , offset by persistent external headwinds including US tariff pressures, competitive pricing wars in tequila and cognac, and volatile seasonal execution. The company delivered an 8.4% organic sales CAGR from 2019 to 2025, significantly outperforming Diageo's 5.0% and Pernod Ricard's 3.0%, and maintained 2.4% organic growth in FY25 during a severe spirits downturn . The core strategic transition to a Houses of Brands model and disciplined portfolio streamlining position the group to concentrate resources on fewer, higher-return assets, though execution volatility remains elevated following frequent leadership changes and operational disruptions.

Strategic and Governance Signal

The net governance read is stabilizing positive following the abrupt resignation of former CEO Matteo Fantacchiotti after only five months in September 2024, succeeded by Simon Hunt in December 2024 following a comprehensive assessment process, with shareholders approving his appointment as executive director in January 2025 until 2028 . The CFO transition finalized with Francesco Mele appointed as group CFO effective November 2025, replacing long-tenured Paolo Marchesini who transitioned to vice chairman after 28 years and received a severance payment of 33.8 million euros . This orderly succession contrasts sharply with the prior executive churn, though the massive severance liabilities present near-term cash flow drags.

Strategically, Campari adopted the transformational Houses of Brands operating model effective January 2025, segmenting the portfolio into Aperitifs, Whiskey and Rum, Agave, and Cognac and Champagne, with each brand house maintaining end-to-end accountability for global performance, innovation, and marketing resource allocation . Management executed a focused asset streamlining strategy, divesting Cinzano for 100 million euros in mid-2025 and selling Averna and Zedda Piras brands in December 2025 for total proceeds over 210 million euros . The aggressive portfolio pruning removes lower-profitability tail brands and accelerates deleveraging, while CEO Simon Hunt's strategic vision emphasizes sharper focus on fewer, bigger bets, with the ambition to become the fastest-growing top 10 spirits company globally and to win the first, shared drink, every day, everywhere . 4 - Earnings Calls Governance control remains tightly concentrated with Lagfin, the Garavoglia family vehicle, holding 51.0% of share capital and 82.3% of voting rights , and no active activist presence exists in the AlphaSense library for Campari , eliminating near-term activist catalysts but reducing external accountability pressure.
