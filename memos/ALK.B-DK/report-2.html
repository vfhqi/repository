  • Market structure: ALK holds the dominant SLIT-tablet position in Europe and the first-mover position in the U.S. FDA-approved SLIT-tablet market (ODACTRA for HDM, GRASTEK for grass pollen); no direct U.S. SLIT-tablet competitor has FDA approval at the same scale [#A]

  • New competitor: DBV Technologies' Viaskin peanut epicutaneous immunotherapy patches are not direct competition but signal the innovation trajectory in immunotherapy; biologic adjacency (see §8) is the more relevant competitive threat [#A]

  • Intensity: Competitive intensity within SLIT-tablets is moderate; the larger competitive challenge is the SCIT vs SLIT substitution dynamic, where SCIT (injections) still dominates U.S. volume and SCIT manufacturers (e.g. Greer Labs, ALK's own legacy business) have structural resistance to cannibalising to SLIT [#A]

  • BtE: Barriers to entry for new SLIT-tablet competitors are high: (1) 3+ year clinical trial requirement per allergen/indication; (2) standardised allergen extract manufacturing complexity; (3) established allergist relationships [#A]

  • Competitor health: Stallergenes-Greer is private; its financial health is not publicly disclosed but it has not made major U.S. commercial investments at ALK's scale, suggesting it is not an imminent U.S. market entrant [#A]

  • Substitute — biologics: Dupixent (dupilumab, Sanofi/Regeneron) treats allergic disease broadly without disease-modification; if payers prefer it to long-course AIT on an outcomes basis, this is a structural competitive threat [#J]

  • ❌ The biologics substitution risk is potentially the most under-priced competitive risk in ALK's investment case — dupilumab's label is expanding and its payer acceptance is growing faster than the consensus AIT model assumes

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish competitive read is that AIT's "disease modification" claim (the argument that AIT changes immune response rather than just treating symptoms) is not yet consistently reimbursed at a premium vs biologics by U.S. managed care; the mechanism — if Dupixent achieves parity or preferred formulary status vs SLIT-tablets for moderate allergic rhinitis, ALK's U.S. revenue model faces structural pricing compression rather than the current volume-growth story [#J]

§8 — Value Chain — Disruption

J-front verdict: Biological disruption from monoclonal antibodies is a medium-term threat, not an immediate crisis; but the pace of biologic label expansion is faster than ALK's investor communications acknowledge [#J]

  • Technology disruption: Monoclonal antibodies (anti-IgE: omalizumab/Xolair; anti-IL-4/13: dupilumab/Dupixent; TSLP: tezepelumab/Tezspire) address allergic disease through mechanisms that compete with AIT for the same patient population [#A]

  • Market framing: biologics and AIT are currently positioned as complementary (biologics for severe; AIT for moderate); this framing is maintained by both pharma and allergist societies but is not immutable

  • Disruption pace: Biologic label expansions are running at approximately 1-2 per year across the anti-IgE/anti-IL-4 class; each expansion brings a new indication into potential competition with AIT [#A]

  • Adoption curve: Biologic adoption in allergic rhinitis specifically is still below 5% of the moderate-severe population; AIT adoption is similarly low; the two are growing in parallel rather than one cannibalising the other currently [#I]

  • Incumbent response: ALK has not developed a biologic; it is a pure-play AIT company; its response to the biologic threat is to differentiate on disease modification and the head-to-head clinical evidence programmes comparing AIT vs biologics [#A]

  • This is a legitimate differentiation strategy but requires clinical evidence accumulation that takes 3-5 years

  • New entrant — innovative: ASIT Biotech is developing next-generation peptide-based immunotherapy with potential for shorter treatment courses (months vs years); this is a longer-term disruptive threat to ALK's current 3-year treatment model [#A]

  • Disruption cost: If the treatment duration for effective AIT is reduced from 3 years to 6-12 months by a new entrant, ALK's revenue model (which depends on long-course patient commitment) would be structurally impacted [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish disruption read is that ALK is investing in incremental innovation (new allergen types, geographic expansion) rather than in the step-change innovation that could protect it from biologic displacement; the mechanism — a well-resourced biologic company (Sanofi, AZ, GSK) achieving first-in-class evidence for cost-effective biologic AIT combination therapy could reframe the entire treatment paradigm faster than ALK's clinical programme timeline allows [#J]
