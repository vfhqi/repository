Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Vaisala holds unrivalled technological leadership in high-end weather and industrial measurement markets, serving highly diversified public and private customer bases. The business model is transition-resilient, with expanding high-margin software subscriptions under its newly isolated Xweather segment.

Core Products and Segment Structure

1.
Realigned segment model: As of January 1, 2026, Vaisala reports financial results across Industrial Measurements, Weather, Energy, and Environment, and the newly carved-out Xweather segment
2.
Premium industrial focus: Industrial Measurements sells advanced thin-film and optical sensors measuring humidity, dew point, and carbon dioxide in demanding factory cleanrooms and power grids
3.
Critical weather systems: The Weather, Energy, and Environment division sells weather radars, lightning detection networks, and automatic sounding systems to national meteorological institutes and airports
4.
Advanced digital meteorology: Vaisala Xweather leverages advanced artificial intelligence and machine learning storm prediction models to provide premium environmental monitoring, real-time alerts, and climate analytics
5.
Diversified contract mix: Vaisala's revenue is earned through transactional hardware sales, multi-year project contracts, recurring calibration and maintenance services, and high-margin software subscriptions
6.
Accelerating recurring revenue: In Q1 2026, Xweather's annual recurring revenue reached EUR 58.2m, growing 12% in constant currencies to represent an increasing share of highly predictable group earnings

Value Chain and Market Moat

7.
Favoured upstream position: Vaisala occupies a highly profitable upstream position by designing proprietary sensor chips in its own wafer cleanrooms, capturing premium value across industrial supply chains
8.
Fragmented customer profile: The company serves a highly fragmented base of public and private sector clients across 150 countries, virtually eliminating customer concentration risks
9.
Formidable R&D barriers: Vaisala invests a massive 11.4% of net sales, or EUR 68.3m in 2025, into R&D to maintain product durability and protect its dominant technological moats in extreme environments
10.
Massive cleanroom switching: Continuous monitoring systems are embedded into pharmaceutical cleanrooms, where switching costs are massive due to strict regulatory compliance and validation requirements
11.
Oligopolistic market structure: Vaisala is the undisputed global leader in weather observations, facing only fragmented regional competition, while its industrial segment competes against high-end peers like Renishaw and Spectris
12.
Other side of the story: In the weather segment, Vaisala is highly exposed to government budgets which can cause unpredictable project postponements and lower structural growth relative to industrial cleanroom markets

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Vaisala exhibits a strong financial trajectory marked by record-high revenues of EUR 596.9m in 2025 and consistent cash conversion of 1.1x. While reported profits were flat in 2025 due to tariff and currency headwinds, Q1 2026 signals a powerful margin recovery led by industrial sales.

Historical Performance and Profitability

1.
Steady revenue expansion: Vaisala's group revenue grew 6% reported and 7% in constant currencies to reach EUR 596.9m in 2025, up from EUR 564.6m in 2024 and EUR 540.4m in 2023
2.
Robust gross margins: The group gross margin remained robust at 55.2% in 2025, down slightly from 56.3% in 2024 and 55.8% in 2023, due to tariffs and currency headwinds
3.
Expanding operating margins: Vaisala's EBIT margin was 14.3% in 2025, compared to 14.7% in 2024 and 12.3% in 2023, reflecting operational leverage and tight cost control
4.
Solid historical earnings: Earnings per share was EUR 1.65 in 2025, compared to EUR 1.76 in 2024 and EUR 1.35 in 2023, showcasing significant earnings expansion since 2023
5.
Outstanding cash conversion: Vaisala maintained outstanding cash conversion of 1.1x in 2025, up from 1.0x in 2024, demonstrating its highly asset-light business model
6.
Strong capital returns: Vaisala's return on capital employed was 19.9% in 2025, and Carnegie expects this to grow to 22.4% in 2026e and 25.2% in 2027e

Balance Sheet and Guidance History

7.
Impeccable net cash: Vaisala's balance sheet is in a net-cash position as of Q1 2026, with net debt of EUR -3.7m compared to net debt of EUR 14.3m at year-end 2025
8.
Ultra-low balance leverage: The company's net debt to EBITDA was 0.1x in 2025, and Carnegie expects this leverage to decline further to -0.1x in 2026e and -0.2x in 2027e
9.
Positive earnings surprise: Vaisala reported solid Q1 2026 results with EBITA of EUR 20.7m, beating consensus by 4% and keeping the full-year 2026 EBITA guidance of EUR 95-110m intact
10.
Conservative guidance history: Management's history reads as conservative, for instance, in July 2024, they narrowed 2024 EBIT guidance to EUR 68-78m, ultimately delivering EUR 82.9m
11.
Organic sales dominance: Sales growth is highly organic, with recent software acquisitions like Speedwell and WeatherDesk in late 2024 boosting Xweather sales by 50% reported but 11% organically
12.
Other side of the story: Margins can be compressed by temporary cost items, such as the EUR 4.9m of restructuring costs in 2025, which can dilute short-term earnings predictability and depress annual GAAP results

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens significantly as structural demand in data centers and digital weather SaaS shifts the group mix toward high-margin recurring revenues. Delivering a 25%+ IRR depends on the joint execution of industrial demand recovery and rapid recurring Xweather SaaS expansion.

Primary Forward Growth Drivers

1.
Strong industrial recovery: The recovery in factory cleanrooms and battery manufacturing directly drives the high-margin Industrial Measurements revenue line, with a high plausibility of sustained double-digit organic growth
2.
Delayed capacity return: The industrial driver would fail if global macroeconomic stagnation or US tariffs cause a major slowdown in electronics and semiconductor capital expenditures
3.
Accelerating software subscriptions: Xweather's subscription sales directly touch the group gross margin line with a massive 79.6% segment margin, carrying a high plausibility of growing organically over 10% annually
4.
Moderate software churn: The subscription expansion would fail if corporate customers or API developers reduce data usage during recessions or opt for lower-cost municipal alternatives
5.
Selective weather investments: Rationalising Weather, Energy, and Environment R&D and focusing on service attachment rates direct positive operating leverage to the group EBITA line, holding a medium plausibility
6.
Protracted public contracts: The weather margins driver would fail if public meteorological agencies experience significant budget cuts or push out critical weather radar upgrade contracts

Compound Probabilities and Risks

7.
Favourable independent triggers: For the bull case to deliver a 25%+ IRR, the industrial and Xweather drivers must both fire, representing a medium compound probability of execution
8.
Required operational agility: For the bull case to succeed, global industrial activity must remain active and Vaisala's high-margin products must successfully offset any tariff-related import duties through proactive pricing
9.
Other side of the story: If trade policy changes escalate, increased tariffs and a weaker US Dollar would severely dent group operating profit, completely breaking the margin expansion trajectory

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderately high due to trade policy and currency headwinds, though the core industrial business remains highly resilient. The bear case is highly incredible given Vaisala's historical ability to fully pass through tariff costs via price increases.

Macroeconomic and Policy Headwinds

1.
Severe tariff compression: Higher US tariffs directly inflate product costs, threatening to compress gross margins if Vaisala cannot raise prices to pass costs to US customers
2.
Harmful exchange headwinds: A rapid depreciation of the US Dollar and Chinese Yuan against the Euro directly reduces reported sales and operating results, as 37% of sales are in the Americas
3.
Unpredictable budget delays: Potential budget cuts at the US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration could postpone large weather projects, directly reducing the Weather segment's forward revenue visibility

Operational Risks and Mitigants

4.
Concentrated Cleanroom risk: A prolonged fire or hazard at Vaisala's centralized sensor cleanroom in Vantaa, Finland, represents the single most damaging failure mode, immediately stopping deliveries and decimating group EPS
5.
Deceptive backlog signals: A rising weather order backlog is a false friend that can mask deteriorating underlying demand in the far more profitable Industrial Measurements division
6.
Favourable short metrics: No published short-seller reports or high short interest are present on the stock, while the US Dollar spot rate remains the most critical early-warning signal to monitor
7.
Other side of the story: The bear risks are significantly overstated because Vaisala has successfully fully mitigated US tariffs through price increases and maintained a rock-solid 15.1% EBITA margin in Q1 2026
