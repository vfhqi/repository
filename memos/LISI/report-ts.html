Section 3 - What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI has simplified its business to focus on high-barrier aerospace and automotive fastening solutions where it commands leading global positions.

SEGMENT OPERATIONS AND REVENUE STRUCTURE

1.
AEROSPACE FASTENERS ENGINE: LISI Aerospace Fasteners represents 70% of segment sales, producing safety-critical bolts, nuts, collars, and latches for aircraft structures
2.
AEROSPACE STRUCTURAL PARTS: The Structural Components business makes up 30% of segment sales, specializing in leading edges and complex forged assemblies
3.
AUTOMOTIVE CLIPPED SOLUTIONS: Clipped Solutions represent 38% of automotive sales, manufacturing plastic and multi-material assembly components that reduce vehicle weight
4.
AUTOMOTIVE THREADED FASTENERS: Threaded Fasteners contribute 33% of automotive revenue, supplying standard and specialized metal screws and bolts
5.
AUTOMOTIVE SAFETY COMPONENTS: Safety Mechanical Components account for 29% of automotive sales, producing critical braking system parts and parking brakes
6.
CONTRACTUAL PRICE INDEXING: Most revenues are contractual and governed by long-term agreements, incorporating price-revision formulae to pass through raw material inflation

VALUE CHAIN DYNAMICS AND COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGES

7.
HIGH QUALIFICATION BARRIERS: Strict aerospace certifications like EN9100 create high switching costs, as qualifying a new supplier can take years
8.
STRATEGIC CUSTOMER INTIMACY: LISI maintains direct engineering partnerships with major original equipment manufacturers, co-developing bespoke fastening systems
9.
CONCENTRATED OLIGOPOLY STRUCTURE: LISI is one of only five large players in the global aerospace fastener market with an estimated 20% global market share
10.
PROPRIETARY ELECTROMOBILITY IP: The group possesses proprietary patents like the LISI-mu MACH assembly process, enabling localized components metallization
11.
POWERFUL BARRIERS TO ENTRY: A massive capital-intensive footprint and specialized metallurgy knowledge protect LISI from low-cost competitors
12.
DENSE CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION: LISI's consolidated customer base is dominated by Airbus, Boeing, and major global automotive manufacturers, creating structural volume dependence
13.
LEGACY DESIGN SCRUTINY: A former manufacturing engineer argues that aerospace fasteners are legacy products with minimal design innovation, making standalone margin expansion heavily volume-dependent
14.
Other side of the story: Heavy dependence on Airbus and Boeing exposes LISI to severe financial setbacks if these original equipment manufacturers halt production because of regulatory or technical problems

Section 5 - The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for LISI strengthens considerably as structural aerospace demand drives highly predictable margin expansion and cash generation.

AEROSPACE GROWTH ENGINE AND MARKET SHARE GAINS

1.
AIRBUS NARROWBODY RAMPUP: Airbus narrowbody assembly rates are increasing, with LISI shipping A320 products at a cadence of 64 monthly units in Q1 2026
2.
COMPETITOR SUPPLY DISRUPTIONS: A major fire at competitor SPS Jenkintown disrupted the aerospace supply chain, positioning LISI to capture highly profitable market share
3.
POWERFUL RETROACTIVE PRICING: LISI completed several long-term aerospace contract renewals, baking in post-inflation price hikes that will support multi-year margin expansion
4.
ROBUST GENERAL DEFENSE: Military and helicopter segments, representing 10% to 15% of aerospace sales, are growing strongly due to global restocking programs
5.
UNPRECEDENTED BOOKTOBILL CAPACITY: LISI's aerospace book-to-bill ratio remains well above 1, providing extensive multi-year revenue visibility for the division

AUTOMOTIVE RESTRUCTURING AND ELECTROMOBILITY MIX

6.
DRASTIC COST RECONSTITUTION: LISI Automotive is executing a major reorganization, closing manufacturing operations at its Puiseux-Pontoise factory in France
7.
HIGHER VALUE PORTFOLIO: The automotive product mix is shifting toward high-value battery environment components, sealing screws, and electric braking systems
8.
CRITICAL GEOGRAPHIC REBALANCING: LISI acquired Polysemble Hungary to expand its low-cost European manufacturing footprint, supporting lightweight assembly components
9.
INDEPENDENT DRIVER PROBABILITY: The investment case does not require all drivers to fire together because aerospace volume expansion operates independently of automotive restructuring
10.
Other side of the story: Supply chain bottlenecks and separate engine issues at original equipment manufacturers could delay aircraft assemblies, capping LISI's near-term volume leverage
