EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Yara International operates as the world's second-largest ammonia producer and number-one global manufacturer of nitrates and NPKs, commanding an extensive proprietary logistics network of 18 terminals and leveraging a structural cost advantage of roughly $50 to $80 per tonne versus competing unintegrated projects . The company has executed decisively on operational restructuring, delivering approximately $230 million in run-rate fixed-cost reductions by late 2025 while aggressively curtailing total capital expenditure to $0.95 billion . However, despite management targeting an additional $200 million in EBITDA improvement by 2027, European assets remain structurally disadvantaged by high regional gas costs compared to the US and Middle East , exposing the business model to severe margin compression during geopolitical energy shocks. 1

Business Model Mechanics and Competitive Positioning Yara's revenue model operates primarily through spot transactions to distributors, exposing the top line to cyclical commodity pricing volatility while high global prices increasingly prompt farmers to delay purchases until application is immediately required . Approximately 60% of overall production comprises premium products, which structurally command higher margins than commodity urea , yet the reliance on ammonia imports to shield European margins could compress the premium nitrate spreads the model requires if geopolitical tensions ease

and commodity prices revert . Yara can shift its European facilities between using regional natural gas and importing ammonia, currently sourcing exactly 50% of the 3 million tonnes consumed for European nitrate production from external sources , providing meaningful operational flexibility but leaving the company perpetually exposed to upstream supplier leverage.

Financial Profile and Returns Trajectory Top-line results have fluctuated significantly, peaking at $24.0 billion in 2022 before normalizing to $15.7 billion in 2025, while EBITDA excluding special items reached $2.8 billion in 2025, up 37% year-over-year . Return on invested capital climbed to 10.7% in 2025, surpassing the through-cycle target of 10%, with Africa & Asia and the Americas segments delivering strong ROICs of 16.4% and 15.6% respectively , though the European segment generated a subpar 6.5% ROIC in 2025, highlighting the structural disadvantage of local gas costs . Yara trades at approximately 5.2x EV/EBITDA, a 30% discount to its peer group average, while CF Industries achieves significantly higher EBITDA margins generating roughly double Yara's profitability due to structurally lower North American gas costs, and OCI is projected to maintain a return on invested capital approximately twice as high as Yara's over the next three years . Cash from operations increased by $608 million in 2025, demonstrating the inherent cash-generation capability of the model, while the board proposed a NOK 22 per share dividend for 2025, exceeding consensus estimates of NOK 18 .

Industry Structure and Value Chain Economics The global nitrogen industry is experiencing structural tightening, with urea demand projected to grow at a 3% CAGR through 2030 while supply additions track at only 1.8% CAGR, pushing the supply-to-demand balance below 100% . Natural gas represents between 60% and 80% of total ammonia cash production costs, functioning simultaneously as hydrogen feedstock and primary energy source , creating severe margin vulnerability for European operators. The Middle East controls approximately 35% to 40% of globally traded urea, and the 2026 Strait of Hormuz conflict effectively trapped one-third of the world's traded urea , demonstrating acute geopolitical supply chain risk. At least ten major fertilizer plants have permanently closed across Europe since 2022, with global market share consolidating toward low-cost MENA and North American producers through competitive elimination . The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism phases into its definitive charging period in 2026, with CBAM estimated to transform Europe's €30/tonne carbon disadvantage into a €20/tonne competitive advantage over imports , providing meaningful regulatory protection for Yara's European asset base.

Value Chain Position and Margin Distribution Yara captures value primarily in Stage 2 base synthesis with 15-30% gross margins and 10-20% EBIT margins, and Stage 3 upgrading with 20-35% gross margins and 15-25% EBIT margins , while raw material extraction captures the highest gross margins at 40-60%, illustrating the company's structural disadvantage in lacking captive upstream gas assets. Yara successfully secures a premium of roughly $200 per tonne for its NPK compounds

compared to standard commodity fertilizer blends , yet lacks significant captive raw material extraction assets, relying heavily on third-party commodity supply where natural gas accounts for 70-90% of variable production costs . The company currently imports approximately 1.5 million tonnes of ammonia annually to continuously feed its European fertilizer upgrading facilities and is actively negotiating to acquire US-based low-emission ammonia production from Air Products to further diversify its upstream energy exposure . Yara's proprietary logistics infrastructure provides a durable cost advantage estimated at $50 to $80 per tonne over competing unintegrated operators, operating a dedicated proprietary fleet of 14 vessels and 18 specialized terminals while reaching end customers through a vast network exceeding 10,800 Yara-branded retail outlets .

Customer Dynamics and Pricing Leverage Yara serves over 140 markets through 10,800 retail outlets, ensuring no material credit or revenue concentration risk and minimal exposure to single-buyer churn , though European and American product sales occur primarily on a spot basis via standard purchase orders, operating without long-term take-or-pay volume guarantees from its core agricultural customer base . The critical ratio of urea prices to cereal prices remains historically poor, directly threatening baseline application demand, with high global gas costs decoupling fertilizer prices from soft crop values and up to 80% of specific regional crop farmers reporting an absolute inability to afford full-season agronomic nutrient requirements . When nitrogen prices spike, farmers frequently defer application purchases until immediately necessary, disrupting traditional forward-order visibility, with high working capital costs compelling distributors and farmers to hold zero buffer stock . In high-value specialty crops, Yara's established brand and proven biostimulants command strong loyalty and sustained premium pricing over unknown generics, with high-value orchard and vineyard operators relying exclusively on trusted brands as the risk of crop failure prohibits trialing unproven discount alternatives , while for standard NPK blends, growers easily switch to cheaper generic alternatives during severe margin squeezes, with premium NPK compound volumes the first products removed from the grower's input budget .

Supplier Concentration and Channel Structure Yara acts as a pure price taker in European gas markets, leaving it fully exposed to TTF spot market surges, with approximately 70% of ammonia imports deriving from Yara's captive overseas production and the remaining 30% procured from external suppliers . The Strait of Hormuz represents the single most critical structural chokepoint for the global fertilizer trade, with blockages trapping approximately one-third of the world's traded urea and one-fifth of global LNG volumes , demonstrating severe geopolitical vulnerability. Yara's proprietary YaraConnect mobile application actively integrates 14,880 SME retailers, providing unprecedented last-mile demand visibility and enabling superior inventory coordination against generic competitors , creating meaningful downstream channel lock-in. To de-risk its European supply chain, Yara is advancing a massive low-carbon ammonia partnership in the US, with the proposed Louisiana facility securing 2.8mtpa of blue ammonia under a

strict 25-year offtake agreement , materially reducing long-term feedstock concentration risk.

Competitive Landscape and Market Share Dynamics CF Industries dominates the cost-curve via North American natural gas, capturing structural margins double that of Yara's European base, with Yara occupying the highest-cost quartile globally and operating at a $50-100 per tonne disadvantage to Chinese and MENA producers , yet the company retains top-tier status in specialty nitrates, consistently extracting a $200 per tonne premium over commodity blends . The company has recently lost market share in Europe to cheaper imported urea from carbon-tax-evading producers, while in Brazil Yara's market position is severely challenged by aggressive pricing from EuroChem and high domestic logistical costs , though Yara is gaining share in the Indian market, achieving rapid reputation growth through superior premium product quality . European peer OCI is actively divesting its nitrogen assets, signalling a structural capitulation to high regional production costs, while Western operators are prioritising reliability and decarbonisation over greenfield expansion, limiting supply growth to below 1.8% CAGR . Upstream energy majors increasingly target the ammonia value chain, with Air Products developing massive US-based blue ammonia capacity, prompting Yara to acquire 2.8mtpa of low-carbon ammonia from Air Products to mitigate its lack of upstream gas integration . Cross-Value-Chain Signals and Power Dynamics TTF gas prices surged 47% year-over-year, cementing absolute pricing power for upstream energy providers over European operators, with forward curves projecting elevated TTF pricing persisting through 2027 , directly contradicting management's narrative of temporary energy shocks. The urea-to-crop ratio hit 1.83x, signaling historic downstream pushback against current nitrogen fertilizer pricing, with over 80% of US rice, cotton, and peanut farmers reporting an absolute inability to afford full-season nutrient requirements , demonstrating severe demand destruction risk. Farmers are actively rotating acreage away from nitrogen-heavy corn toward soybeans to escape crippling fertilizer input costs, with desperate growers structurally accepting lower crop yields rather than paying spot-market premiums for bulk nitrogen , threatening baseline volume assumptions. At least ten major European fertilizer plants have permanently closed, signaling capitulation to high regional production costs, with peers like BASF and CF Industries exiting Europe mechanically shifting residual market share toward Yara's surviving assets . Retailer destocking continuously forces Yara to absorb massive inventory holding costs during seasonal application lulls, with elevated receivable balances increasing structural exposure to farmer default during periods of severe agricultural margin compression , revealing deteriorating working capital power dynamics. 44 45 47

Long-Term Disruption Forces and Growth Adjacencies The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism phases in from 2026, structurally protecting European producers from high-carbon imports, with importers facing full carbon levies that effectively flip Yara's historical €30/t carbon disadvantage into a €20/t structural advantage by 2026 , creating meaningful regulatory tailwinds. Yara holds a surplus of approximately 6 million EU ETS quotas, creating a hidden €500 million buffer against tightening regulations , providing significant latent value. The maritime sector's shift to zero-carbon fuels creates a massive new TAM, with ammonia demand in shipping projected to reach 180mt by 2050, while co-firing ammonia in coal-heavy Asian power grids provides a structurally expanding demand vector completely independent of agricultural cycles . Advanced negotiations with Air Products target a 2.8mtpa blue ammonia facility in Louisiana, securing cheap US gas, with analysts estimating this $2 billion investment cleanly clears a 10% ROIC hurdle at current €80/t European carbon prices , unlocking material value creation potential. However, a full repeal or sustained suspension of CBAM would strand billions in blue ammonia capital, with cheap grey imports flooding Europe and fatally undermining Yara's low-carbon premium justification , representing the single largest structural risk to the investment thesis. Aggregate Assessment and Key Vulnerabilities Yara International combines world-class downstream logistics infrastructure and demonstrated operational execution with a structurally disadvantaged European cost position that exposes returns to severe cyclical volatility. The business generates strong mid-cycle free cash flow and maintains disciplined capital allocation , yet operates without the mechanical gravitational pull of deeply advantaged upstream peers who command captive low-cost gas assets. Management's $350 million EBITDA improvement program appears credible and materially de-risked by CBAM implementation , though execution relies heavily on sustained agricultural demand resilience and successful commissioning of the US blue ammonia partnership. The single largest risk remains a geopolitical resolution in the Middle East triggering a collapse in European gas prices and simultaneously eliminating CBAM's protective barrier, which would instantaneously compress premium nitrate margins and render the blue ammonia capital allocation uneconomic, destroying the fundamental investment case before the 2027 earnings inflection materializes.
