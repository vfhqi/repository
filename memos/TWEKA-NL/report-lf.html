EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The governance architecture is dominated by a severely restrictive STAK trust structure with a 1% exchangeability rule that insulates management from activist and takeover discipline , creating an asymmetric control-to-ownership differential that contrasts sharply with pure-play peers maintaining standard one-share, one-vote frameworks. Insider transactions reflect balanced activity under share schemes , where net selling to cover taxes aligns with peer diversification programs, though reliance on programmatic sales may obscure capital preservation motives if insiders anticipate further margin pressures. Governance Structure and Board Independence Assessment

The Supervisory Board consists of five members , significantly smaller than peer averages, though all five are fully independent per Dutch Corporate Governance Code criteria. No Supervisory Board member owns any shares or depositary receipts, meaning directors have no direct financial alignment with minority shareholders. This contrasts starkly with peers like Belden and Amphenol, where directors must hold shares worth 5x their cash retainer to ensure ownership alignment. TKH's ordinary shares are held in trust by Stichting Administratiekantoor TKH Group , which issues depositary receipts and can exercise voting rights at its own discretion during hostile scenarios. Depositary receipts are exchangeable for ordinary shares only up to 1.00% of total issued capital , and the Continuity Foundation can acquire preference shares up to 50.00% of outstanding capital . This defensive mechanism contrasts with peers like Prysmian, Nexans, Belden, and Amphenol, which maintain standard one-share, one-vote structures , creating an asymmetric control-to-ownership differential.
