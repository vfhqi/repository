EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Net Signal on Change

Top 5-8 Drivers of Forward EPS

Driver 1 – Molson Coors US Distribution Rollout: transferring US distribution to Molson Coors unlocks access to 400 independent distributors and 500,000 accounts, with management guiding to double-digit US volume acceleration, though Redburn warns consensus US sales growth expectations of 18% remain overly ambitious given broader spirits market weakness, creating a medium plausibility but high-impact driver with estimated +15-20% EPS contribution if successfully executed.

Driver 2 – US Production Onshoring: eliminating transatlantic freight and import tariffs through local production provides a mechanically locked-in +400-500bps margin expansion, with glass and aluminium costs fully hedged for 2026 and largely covered for 2027, establishing high plausibility and high confidence that structural profitability improvement will materialize by 2027 regardless of top-line execution.

Driver 4 – Non-Tonic Portfolio Expansion: non-tonic products now represent 45% of total group revenues, with premium adult soft drinks and cocktail mixers achieving an 11% CAGR in the UK, delivering an estimated +3-5% revenue contribution with high plausibility given established baseline momentum, though offset by structural UK tonic volume declines.

Driver 6 – UK EPR Levy (Downside Risk): a £2.8m provision related to the UK Extended Producer Responsibility levy introduces a potential £3m structural annual cost headwind pending legal challenges, representing a -6% EBITDA drag with medium plausibility dependent on unpredictable court rulings.
