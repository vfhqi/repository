Industry Structure and Competitive Positioning

The global tabletop games market demonstrates exceptional structural resilience , valued at approximately EUR 13 billion in 2024 with a projected steady 4-5% CAGR through 2028 , while exhibiting a solid 3% CAGR during the 2006-2011 financial crisis period, demonstrating strong resilience during economic downturns despite board game sales declining in 2022 due to post-pandemic normalization . The sector remains highly fragmented upstream with between 150 and 500 active publishers in key regional markets, though larger corporate entities are actively executing bolt-on M&A to capture market share and creative studios , with the four largest global operators controlling nearly 40% of the total tabletop market creating significant pricing leverage, and Asmodee holding the second position with approximately 12% market share following Hasbro's 19% leadership . 13

Geographic concentration patterns reveal European dominance with North American exposure , as Western Europe represents 72% of total fiscal year 2025/2026 revenues, with France contributing 20%, Germany 15%, the United Kingdom 13%, and other European territories 24% , while the United States contributes 17% of sales presenting a complex tariff and inventory environment, with North American sales burdened by high retail inventory levels though US retail sell-in began normalizing in Q4 2025/2026 driven by healthier commercial and

marketing initiatives . The competitive landscape exhibits asymmetric market power by geography , with Asmodee holding dominant market-leading positions across core Western European tabletop markets including number one in France and the UK, and number two in Germany , though ranking as the number three player in the United States representing 17% of sales, where Hasbro and Mattel maintain stronger direct retail footprints in the highly consolidated US mass-market channel .

Critical Structural Vulnerabilities and Uncontracted Dependencies

The single largest fundamental risk centers on massive uncontracted partner exposure , as five key product lines including Pokémon, Magic: The Gathering, Ticket to Ride, Exploding Kittens, and Dobble account for 51% of net sales, with the Pokémon Trading Card Game representing an estimated 20% of total company revenues, yet despite a 20-year history as Pokémon's European distributor Asmodee operates without a long-term contract for this critical franchise . This creates extreme single-product risk , where Magic: The Gathering representing approximately 15% of net sales is distributed via annual contracts with volume allocations, while other major partner-published TCGs such as Bandai Namco's One Piece are distributed under pluri-annual contracts , contrasting sharply with Asmodee fully owning the IP for its 25% revenue-contributing studio games ensuring permanent monetization rights . 27 28 dynamics present elevated maritime freight and tariff exposure , with Asmodee highly exposed to maritime transport cost inflation and fuel price volatility across its global distribution network, with group shipping

Customer Dynamics and Working Capital Power Signals

Customer concentration remains well-diversified across fragmented retail channels , with Asmodee distributing its catalog across three distinct commercial routes including independent hobby stores, specialty/mass retail, and dedicated e-commerce platforms, with the top 10 customer accounts representing approximately 25% of net sales and the largest single customer account contributing approximately 10% , while maintaining direct commercial access to over 7,000 independent hobby shops globally creating a massive logistical barrier, with revenue globally diversified as France contributes 20%, the US 17%, Germany 15%, and the UK 13% as of March 2025 . Customer switching costs exhibit bifurcated structural lock-in , as high-end lifestyle and strategy board games such as Catan and Ticket to Ride command deep consumer lock-in through active localized player communities, reinforced by supporting tournaments including over 200,000 organized play events for Star Wars: Unlimited since launch , though casual party games exhibit lower customer switching costs driven by impulse buying, price, and novelty, while in a weak consumer spending environment Asmodee is seeing some consumers shift toward lower-priced products .

Management Quality and Reactivated M&A Engine

The operator profile reflects experienced leadership executing strategic spin-off and refinancing , with Thomas Koegler appointed CEO on August 27, 2024 in preparation for the Nasdaq Stockholm listing, bringing 11 years of experience in the gaming sector having joined Asmodee in 2015, and holding a Master's degree in Entrepreneurship from HEC Paris , while Chief Financial Officer Andrea Gasparini joined Asmodee in 2024 bringing five years of game industry experience, having previously held finance positions within Lagardère Group and Mediobanca Corporate Investment Banking . The strategic rationale centers on operational freedom and resumed acquisition activity , as the Nasdaq Stockholm listing in February 2025 aimed to provide operational freedom and restart its M&A engine, facilitated by a EUR 400 million capital contribution from former parent Embracer .
