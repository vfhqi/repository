EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 64 findings

Section summary
  1. Slawomir Krupa is an insider-track wholesale operator with a 27-year tenure, representing structural strategic continuity but raising legacy risk relative to external turnaround peers
  2. Krupa has delivered exceptional capital efficiency and cost reduction, outperforming peers on capital returns while restructuring retail and leasing operations

Slawomir Krupa is a 27-year internal veteran who delivered exceptional value creation with annualized total shareholder return reaching +237% between 2023 and 2025 , outperforming peer Credit Agricole's Q1 2026 net income growth of 1.8% versus Societe Generale's 5.5% expansion pace .

Group net income surged 43% to €6.00bn in 2025 while the cost-to-income ratio improved dramatically to 63.6% from 69.0% , beating the sub-65% target .

Krupa upgraded the 2026 Return on Tangible Equity target to above 10% from the previous 9%-10% range, with Deutsche Bank forecasting further improvement to 12.0% by 2028 , supported by €887m in realized gross savings between 2024 and Q1 2026 .

However, the insider-track appointment of a 27-year veteran risks entrenched thinking, as his career is deeply bound to legacy wholesale structures he must restructure , and his achievements remain deeply wholesale-centric, leaving his ability to manage complex retail and leasing divisions unproven .

CEO Quality Verdict

Krupa exhibits owner-mindset discipline, initiating deep headquarters reorganization in early 2024 with 900 job cuts and delayering General Management to only two executive officers to simplify operations .

He refuses to chase unprofitable volume growth at expense of return thresholds, prioritizing strict 13.0% CET1 capital buffers and rational capital deployment .

Retail execution shows mixed signals with physical branch footprint reduced to 1,450 in 2025 from 1,700 in 2023 , yet BoursoBank customer base expanded 22% year-on-year to 8.8m clients with record 575,000 client onboarding in Q4 2025 .

The Ayvens mobility integration delivered €360m in pre-tax synergies in 2025 with cost-to-income ratio of 56.1% , beating its 57%-59% target range . Capital discipline proved exceptional with CET1 ratio reaching 13.5% at Q1 2026 end , superior to BNP Paribas's 12.8% , enabling €4.68bn total distributions (+169% year-on-year) including two separate €1.00bn extraordinary buybacks

. Track record at prior divisions shows force, with Krupa restructuring Global Markets in 2021 to reduce illiquid structured products warehousing, executing €450m gross cost reduction within GBIS, and overseeing SG Americas turnaround from 2016 .

Yet performance attribution remains contested as much record 2021-2022 CIB performance was driven by elevated rate and FX volatility with rising policy rates and steepening curves acting as primary macro tailwinds .

Governance Robustness Verdict

Board independence stands at outstanding 91.6% (11 of 12 members excluding employee representatives) , outperforming BNP Paribas at 85% and Santander at 66.67%

. Chairman succession executed smoothly with William Connelly assuming role in May 2026 after meticulous preparation using independent consultant, while Dame Clara Furse joined bringing exceptional UK regulatory experience .

Committee construction shows strength with 80% independent Audit and Compensation Committees, Risk Committee chaired by Clara Furse, and Nomination Committee containing 100% independent directors

. Executive compensation features stretching performance conditions with financial criteria equally weighted across ROTE, operating ratio, and CET1 thresholds, achieving 125% financial target rate and 92.1% CSR rate in 2025, while LTI vests over five years split between relative TSR, ROTE, and ESG targets .

ESG integration mandates 80% reduction in oil and gas exposure by 2030 and requires contributing €500bn to sustainable finance , reaching €165bn in 2025

. However, independent directors show very low equity holdings with several owning only 600 shares , limiting true alignment as directors face negligible wealth exposure, contrasting Santander which requires executive directors hold shares worth twice net salary

. Legal disputes remain elevated with €1.26bn litigation provisions in December 2025, French Financial Prosecutor raids at La Défense offices in March 2023 and June 2025, and $157.00m Swiss Ponzi settlement finalized March 2025 .

Insider Transaction Signal

Officer transactions display defensive posture with zero open-market sales, multiple small-scale director purchases rarely exceeding €60,000 , and Board Chairman Lorenzo Bini Smaghi deploying only €99,754.38 across two transactions signaling weak incremental buying conviction . CEO Slawomir Krupa recorded zero personal open-market purchases during last 12 months, relying entirely on existing 120,000 shares mandated from May 2023 appointment .

Peer comparison reveals superior alignment with Santander requiring executive directors hold shares worth twice gross salary with Chair Ana Botín holding 33.85 million shares representing 99.2x gross salary , while BNP Paribas CEO executed €242,919 personal acquisitions in 2025 .

Employee participation provided broader internal capital vote with 7,531,065 new shares subscribed on July 24, 2025 under Global Employee Share Ownership Plan .

Expert Network Alignment

Former UK private bank subsidiary Lead revealed centralized ownership imposes costly, highly rigid internal services and IT infrastructure mandates actively damaging operating efficiency and morale during mergers .

CEO Krupa admitted Crédit du Nord "Project Vision" merger assumptions completely failed to foresee massive inflation explosion, severely eroding net-net financial benefits .

Analysts challenged management on why massive Crédit du Nord merger barely visible on core French retail cost line which continued ticking upward, with integration strategy relying on forcing all clients onto single centralized IT system cited as primary operational disruption source .

Single Largest Credibility and Management Risk

Culture indicators reveal structural inertia with elevated employee departure rate of 13.3% in 2025 deteriorating from 12.4% in 2024 , substantially higher than domestic French peers with BNP Paribas reporting stable 8.5% turnover in 2025 down from 9.6% .

Restructuring friction intensified with 1,800 position cuts announced January 2026 within French retail and headquarters by end-2027, triggering CGT union slamming plan as "fait accompli" and criticizing internal redeployment measures as "deficient" for displaced workers .

Legacy culture remains heavily bureaucratic and hierarchical with Morgan Stanley pointing out Societe Generale generates lowest revenue per FTE in European coverage, lacking relentless operational agility of highperformance archetypes .

Key-person risk stands acute because Krupa assumed direct supervision of French Retail, Private Banking, and Insurance in late 2024 making his departure trigger massive operational disruption, while replacing newly appointed external CFO Leopoldo Alvear who drives €1.7bn cost-cutting program represents severe strategic recruitment risk .

Executive committee shows unusually long tenures with several members exceeding 25 years risking entrenched outdated retail models, while Ayvens suffered elevated 17.1% turnover rate in 2024 following LeasePlan merger integration friction .

The convergence of elevated voluntary attrition, intense union resistance, acute key-person concentration under a 27-year internal veteran, and structurally weak culture archetypes relative to domestic peers represents the single largest management and credibility risk to sustained operational execution and return improvement.
