  • Q4 pattern: Industry norm for debt purchasers is Q4 collection strength driven by debtors resolving obligations ahead of year-end; Hoist's H2 outperformance is consistent with this sector-wide dynamic [#I]

  • Q1 pattern: Not directly observable from semi-annual reporting; H1 weakness vs H2 implies Q1 is typically the softest quarter [#I]

  • Seasonality direction: Seasonality not materially changing over L3Y; consistent H2 skew throughout; no evidence of structural shift toward more even distribution [#A]

  • Cause — collection intensity: Primary driver is debtor payment behaviour clustering in Q4 (tax refund season in some European markets, employer bonuses, consumer finance consolidation) rather than company-specific production or buying patterns [#I]

  • Cause — M&A timing: Portfolio acquisition activity tends to cluster in H2 as banks and originators finalise NPL disposals before year-end balance sheet targets; this can boost H2 revenue through initial recognition effects [#A]

  • Seasonality changing: No evidence of meaningful change in the H1/H2 split over L3Y; FY22 was distorted by impairment timing but underlying collections were still H2-skewed [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: Bearish seasonal read — if regulatory pressure on year-end collection practices intensifies (EU consumer credit directive tightening), the H2 collection boost could moderate; apparent H2 seasonality could be a regulatory-window effect misread as structural [#J]
