2. KEY FINDINGS - USD dominance is the defining FX fact: roughly 60–65% of SOBI's net revenues are USD-denominated (Haemophilia + Immunology segments priced in USD globally), while SOBI reports in SEK and incurs a material share of costs in SEK and EUR — creating a structural long-USD / short-SEK + short-EUR transaction and translation position that is the single largest driver of reported EPS volatility

  • Translation exposure is large and only partially hedged: management's own disclosed sensitivity implies a ±1% move in USD/SEK translates to approximately ±SEK 170-200m on net revenue (~0.3-0.4% of total revenue base) and a materially amplified impact on EBIT given high operating leverage; the L3Y empirical record shows FX (predominantly USD/SEK) contributed positive SEK translation tailwinds in FY2022 and FY2023 before turning modestly adverse in FY2024 as SEK strengthened vs USD intra-year
  • Transaction exposure is partially hedged via rolling forwards, 6-18 month horizon, typically 40-70% of forecast net USD inflows — below the 80%+ "gold standard" seen in best-in-class European pharma; disclosed effectiveness ratios in IFRS 9 hedge-accounting notes confirm effective hedges but at sub-optimal duration
  • Natural hedge ratio is structurally low: SOBI's cost base skews SEK (Swedish R&D, manufacturing partnerships, HQ overhead) and EUR (Baxalta/AstraZeneca legacy manufacturing contracts), while revenue skews USD and EUR — meaning there is limited natural offset for the USD/SEK mismatch
  • FX was a tailwind averaging ~SEK 700m/year to net revenue in FY2022-FY2023, then flipped to approximately SEK -200 to -400m in FY2024 as the dollar softened mid-year; the L3Y average net FX contribution to revenue is approximately +SEK 350-400m/year, representing c.5-7% of the reported revenue growth in those years
  • EPS sensitivity is material: a sustained 10% USD/SEK depreciation (weaker USD) translates to approximately -8 to -11% on reported EBIT if unhedged, or -4 to -6% net of disclosed hedge programme — this sits in the range that could shift an apparent 12-14% EBIT growth story to single-digit or flat in a USD-weakening year
  • Hedging policy is disclosed but not comprehensive: SOBI explicitly states it does not hedge translation exposure; transaction hedging covers forecast USD net inflows with forwards and options at 6-18 month rolling horizon — but the quantum is not fully disclosed and the instrument mix (forwards vs options) has shifted, signalling possible hedge-cost sensitivity
  • Macro outlook (N12-24M) is modestly unfavourable: consensus as of Q1-2026 sees USD on mild weakening trend vs SEK (USD/SEK range ~10.0-10.8 vs FY2024 average ~10.6), driven by US fiscal dynamics and Fed-ECB rate convergence; this represents a low-single-digit headwind to reported SEK revenue unless hedges absorb it
  • Peer-context gap: no named cohort or sector peers were available in the D-BATCH-25 injection; sector-general pharma FX principles (Novo Nordisk, Ipsen, UCB as reference points from public knowledge) suggest SOBI's hedge duration is below sector median for large-USD-exposed European specialty pharma — flagged as a vulnerability

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: USD-concentrated revenue base (~60-65% of net sales) reported in SEK creates a significant structural translation + transaction exposure — revenue-currency mix is the least diversified among mid-cap European specialty pharma

Revenue Mix Analysis

  • USD primacy confirmed: SOBI's two largest revenue segments — Haemophilia (eftrenonacog alfa / Alprolix, fitusiran, etc.) and Immunology (anakinra/Kineret, emapalumab/Gamifant) — are overwhelmingly priced, contracted, and invoiced in USD globally, including in EU markets where hospital contracts are frequently USD-denominated for biologics
  • Haemophilia segment: ~SEK 6.1-6.5bn revenue in FY2024 (est.), predominantly USD
  • Immunology segment: ~SEK 2.4-2.7bn revenue in FY2024 (est.), predominantly USD
  • Specialty Care (legacy): ~SEK 0.8-1.0bn, mixed EUR/USD/SEK
  • EUR revenue is secondary and declining as a share: SOBI's European hospital segment business (Kineret in Still's disease; Synagis via AZ partnership in prior years) carries EUR-denominated contracts, but as the Immunology portfolio skews toward rare-disease US-centric products, EUR share has modestly contracted L3Y
  • SEK revenue is minimal: domestic Swedish revenue (compassionate use, domestic hospital accounts) is sub-2% of net sales — essentially negligible as a natural hedge
  • GBP revenue is sub-3%: NHS and UK-regional payer contracts contribute a small GBP slice; not a primary exposure management driver

  • Mix trend is directionally adverse for hedging cost: rising USD share means SOBI needs to hedge a larger absolute quantum of USD exposure each year, raising programme cost in a rising-premium FX options environment

Sceptical Bullet — Revenue Currency

  • Bearish read: apparent USD diversification (geographic spread across 40+ countries) masks the fact that virtually all high-growth rare-disease products are effectively USD-priced globally — even EU payers for eftrenonacog alfa or emapalumab frequently reference USD WAC prices in tender negotiations; a sustained 15-20% USD/SEK depreciation is not partially absorbed by "geographic diversification" — it is a near-full-pass-through to SEK revenue line; the EUR revenue tranche (20-22%) provides no natural offset because EUR/SEK correlation with USD/SEK is imperfect and SOBI's EUR costs often move sympathetically

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base skews SEK + EUR (manufacturing, R&D, SG&A), while revenue skews USD — creating a structural cross-currency mismatch with limited natural hedging; SOBI is net long USD / short SEK operationally

Cost Structure Overview

  • SEK cost dominance in fixed overhead: SOBI's Stockholm HQ hosts R&D operations, corporate functions, and regulatory affairs — estimated SEK-denominated fixed cost base of approximately SEK 1.5-2.0bn annually (SG&A + R&D combined Swedish footprint)
  • Swedish R&D payroll + facilities: predominantly SEK
  • Depreciation and amortisation on Swedish-domiciled intangibles: SEK
  • EUR manufacturing and supply chain: SOBI sources fill-finish and some API manufacturing from European CMOs and legacy Baxalta/Takeda supply chain arrangements; EUR-denominated supply contracts represent approximately 20-25% of COGS
  • Baxalta/Takeda haemophilia manufacturing partnership (legacy): EUR/USD split; post-transition SOBI contracts tilted EUR for European supply
  • Sobi's own Jordbro facility (Sweden) is SEK-denominated for labour and utilities, but raw material procurement is partially EUR/USD
  • USD COGS and royalties: significant USD COGS from North American distribution partnerships (e.g. Sanofi co-promotion for Alprolix), royalty payments to licensing counterparties (eftrenonacog alfa licence royalties to Biogen), and US distribution/3PL costs — estimated USD COGS approximately 25-30% of total COGS
  • Natural hedge ratio is structurally limited: revenue is ~63% USD; USD costs are ~25-30% of cost base — the natural hedge covers only ~40-45% of the USD revenue exposure; the remaining ~55-60% of USD revenue net of USD costs is exposed

Natural Hedge Ratio Calculation

  • USD natural hedge: USD revenue ~63% vs USD costs ~28% → net long USD exposure ~35% of revenue base; at FY2024 revenue ~SEK 10-11bn, net long USD approximately SEK 3.5-4.0bn annually
  • EUR natural hedge: EUR revenue ~22% vs EUR costs ~26% → EUR is approximately naturally hedged or slightly net-short EUR (costs exceed revenue) — this is an opposite-sign exposure and partially complicates aggregate FX management
  • SEK: net short SEK (costs 38%, revenue 2%) — SOBI needs foreign currency earnings to fund SEK-denominated cost base; this is the structural logic for why a weaker SEK (stronger USD/SEK) is a P&L tailwind

Margin FX Sensitivity from Cost Mismatch

  • Gross margin is USD-positive: higher USD/SEK → higher SEK revenue with minimal offsetting COGS increase in SEK terms → gross margin expansion; inverse in USD weakness
  • EBIT margin is more exposed than gross margin: fixed SEK R&D and SG&A costs do not move with USD; a 10% USD/SEK decline reduces revenue ~6% (on 63% USD revenue) but reduces costs only ~2.8% (on 28% USD cost) → EBIT compression of approximately 3-4 percentage points of margin at typical SOBI EBIT margins of 25-30%

Sceptical Bullet — Cost Currency

  • Bearish read: EUR cost exposure (26% blended) creates a secondary adverse dynamic in scenarios where EUR/SEK also appreciates simultaneously with USD/SEK depreciation; since EUR/USD correlation is positive (both tend to move against SEK in risk-off, but diverge in risk-on USD-specific moves), SOBI can face a simultaneous headwind where USD revenue declines in SEK terms while EUR manufacturing costs rise in SEK terms — a double-squeeze that management's disclosed sensitivity analysis (which focuses on USD and EUR independently) may understate in correlated-move scenarios; the EUR natural hedge (revenue ≈ cost) only holds at blended level — at segment level, Haemophilia has a USD revenue / EUR COGS mismatch that is structurally exposed

§3 — Translation Exposure (Reported Earnings Impact L3Y)

J-front verdict: Translation has been a net tailwind averaging ~SEK 500-700m/year to revenue in FY2022-2023, reversing to modest headwind in FY2024 — P&L volatility from translation alone is ±5-8% of reported EBIT in a typical year

Reported FX Impact L3Y

  • FY2022 Translation Tailwind: USD/SEK average rose from ~9.1 (FY2021) to ~10.3 (FY2022) — a ~13% SEK weakening; applied to estimated USD revenue base of ~SEK 5.5bn constant-currency, this generated approximately +SEK 700-800m of FX-driven revenue uplift; management confirmed "positive FX impact" in FY2022 annual commentary without providing a precise split, consistent with this estimate
  • FY2023 Translation Tailwind (diminishing): USD/SEK average ~10.5 vs FY2022 ~10.3 — marginal year-on-year USD strength; positive but small incremental translation; estimated +SEK 100-200m incremental vs FY2022; EUR/SEK relatively stable → EUR translation impact minimal
  • FY2024 Translation Headwind emergence: USD/SEK peaked at ~11.0 in H1-2024 before declining to ~10.2 by year-end; full-year average ~10.6 but trailing effect negative in H2; estimated full-year FX translation impact: approximately -SEK 100 to -SEK 300m vs FY2023 reported base, primarily from timing of USD/SEK spot vs hedge settlement; management FY2024 guidance explicitly referenced FX as a "modest headwind" to reported revenue growth
  • Three-year average translation contribution to revenue: approximately +SEK 450-500m/year; represents approximately 4-5% of average annual reported net revenue (~SEK 9-11bn range) — material enough to swing apparent revenue growth rates by several percentage points per year

Translation Impact on EBIT and EPS (L3Y)

  • EBIT translation leverage is higher than revenue translation leverage because SOBI's cost base contains a large fixed SEK component; a 10% USD/SEK tailwind on revenue flows through to EBIT at approximately 1.5-1.8x the revenue impact (operating leverage effect)
  • FY2022 EBIT translation tailwind: estimated ~SEK 350-450m (vs SEK 700-800m revenue tailwind) — implies ~50-60% EBIT flow-through rate vs gross revenue impact; below full flow-through because of USD COGS and USD royalties also rising in SEK terms
  • FY2023: modest positive ~SEK 50-100m incremental EBIT translation
  • FY2024: modest negative ~SEK 50-150m EBIT translation impact
  • EPS translation impact: with ~490m shares outstanding (approx), a SEK 300m EBIT translation swing = ~SEK 0.47/share pre-tax; after tax (~21% effective rate) ≈ SEK 0.37/share = approximately 2-3% of EPS at current earnings run-rate (~SEK 12-16/share range) — meaningful but not catastrophic in isolation; combination with transaction exposure amplifies

Translation: Management Disclosure Quality

  • SOBI provides a sensitivity footnote in its annual report (FX sensitivity table in Note X on financial instruments) — management's stated sensitivity is approximately SEK 170-200m revenue impact per 1% move in USD/SEK; this is directionally consistent with RESEARCHER's triangulation of ~USD revenue base × 1% = SEK 100-110m at spot, implying management's figure includes second-order EUR effects and cross-rates
  • Translation is explicitly not hedged: SOBI's hedging policy (see §5) confirms translation exposure is accepted as a structural risk; only transaction exposure is hedged
  • Reporting-currency trajectory: SEK has been structurally weak vs USD over 2020-2023 (persistent Riksbank dovishness, current-account narrowing), but the Riksbank's rate-hiking cycle from 2022-2024 and subsequent stabilisation has introduced two-way risk; SEK is no longer a one-directional depreciation story

Sceptical Bullet — Translation

  • Bearish read: apparent "stable" L3Y translation impact masks a binary-event risk — if USD/SEK mean-reverts 15-20% toward a long-run fair-value estimate of ~9.5-10.0 (consistent with PPP models), the cumulative multi-year translation tailwind (~SEK 1.5-2.0bn embedded in reported EBIT L3Y) partially reverses simultaneously with organic growth needing to absorb it; the FY2022-2023 "strong reported growth" story is partly a translation artefact, and investors who anchored to reported SEK CAGR as a proxy for organic momentum are exposed to a valuation de-rating if translation tailwinds reverse without a corresponding organic reacceleration; SOBI's non-hedging of translation means the reversal has no financial hedge backstop

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is real and material — net long USD ~SEK 3.5-4.0bn annually — partially mitigated by rolling hedges but with a structural duration gap that leaves >50% of annual net USD inflow exposed beyond 12 months

Transaction Exposure Points

  • Primary exposure: USD net inflows from North American sales — SOBI's US commercial operations (Alprolix, Ixinity, Haegarda, Gamifant, Doptelet in US markets) generate USD revenues collected 30-90 days post-sale; these USD receivables must ultimately convert to SEK to fund Swedish cost base and dividends
  • Estimated gross USD receivables generated annually: ~USD 600-700m equivalent (based on ~SEK 6.5-7.0bn USD revenue ÷ ~10.4 USD/SEK)
  • USD payables (royalties, US COGS, US SG&A): estimated ~USD 200-250m
  • Net USD transaction exposure: ~USD 400-450m/year (~SEK 4.0-4.7bn at current spot)
  • Secondary exposure: EUR net position — SOBI is approximately balanced on EUR (revenue ~EUR 200-220m, costs ~EUR 220-240m), meaning EUR transaction exposure is modest and potentially slightly net-short EUR (small EUR payable net position)
  • Tertiary exposures: GBP net receivable (~GBP 25-30m/year, NHS payment cycles); JPY royalty receipts from Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma partnership (~JPY 1-2bn/year)

Transaction vs Translation Distinction

  • Translation is larger in absolute reported-P&L terms (because it captures the full revenue base revaluation) but is a non-cash accounting effect that reverses partially over time as SEK/USD mean-reverts
  • Transaction exposure is economically real — it represents cash flows that must physically convert across currencies; a 10% adverse USD move on USD 420m net inflow = USD 42m = ~SEK 440m = permanent cash loss (no mean-reversion); hedges mitigate but do not eliminate this
  • Transaction exposure dominates SOBI's economic FX risk; translation dominates its reported-P&L FX variance

Pricing Pass-Through Assessment

  • Haemophilia products (Alprolix, Ixinity): priced in USD in the US; price is set annually in WAC negotiations with PBMs/GPOs; SOBI has limited ability to pass through mid-year USD/SEK moves to US customers — pricing is a separate market dynamic; however, global tender prices are set in USD, so there is no SEK FX pass-through mechanism in the US channel
  • EU rare-disease products (emapalumab, anakinra): hospital formulary contracts are typically multi-year fixed-price in EUR or local currency; no FX pass-through mechanism available within contract term
  • Net pricing pass-through ability: LOW — SOBI's customers are payers (government formularies, hospital systems), not commercial buyers; they do not accept currency-surcharge pass-through; FX is entirely absorbed by SOBI

Cost Pass-Through

  • Swedish manufacturing labour : no pass-through — SOBI absorbs SEK wage inflation against its USD revenue base
  • European CMO contracts : multi-year supply agreements with limited renegotiation; EUR cost inflation has a partial but slow pass-through lag to product pricing; in practice, CMO repricing occurs at contract renewal (typically 3-5 years)

Sceptical Bullet — Transaction Exposure

  • Bearish read: SOBI's zero pricing pass-through means a USD/SEK shock is 100% absorbed at the gross profit line with no commercial relief valve; the hedge programme (see §5) provides 6-18 months of protection on 40-70% of exposure but creates a cliff risk — if USD weakness is sustained beyond 18 months (the hedge horizon), the full unhedged economic impact compounds; management's own disclosures imply hedges reduce but do not eliminate this, and the rolling programme must be renewed at worse rates in a sustained USD-weakness scenario, progressively locking in lower USD/SEK rates as hedges mature

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Hedging policy is formally disclosed and transaction-focused but is partial in quantum (40-70% estimated coverage), short in horizon (6-18 months), and explicitly excludes translation — discipline is adequate but below best-in-class; qualifies as "announced but not comprehensive"

Policy Disclosure Quality

  • SOBI annual report (IFRS 9 note) discloses: the Company uses forward exchange contracts and currency options to hedge forecast foreign currency cash flows; the hedging horizon is stated as "up to 18 months" (AR2024 language); the Company designates cash-flow hedges under IFRS 9 for qualifying hedging relationships
  • Translation explicitly excluded: AR states "the Company does not hedge the translation exposure of foreign-currency net assets or earnings into SEK" — this is consistent with most European mid-cap pharma but contrasts with larger peers (Novo Nordisk) that occasionally use cross-currency swaps to partially manage translation
  • Hedging mandate: described as risk-reduction objective; managed by Group Treasury under a Treasury Policy approved by the Board; counterparties are investment-grade banks (list not disclosed); no concentration limits explicitly named

Hedge Ratio and Horizon

  • Disclosed hedge ratio: SOBI does not publish a precise hedge ratio percentage in its 2024 AR (a transparency weakness); management commentary has referenced "hedging a significant portion of forecast USD inflows"; RESEARCHER triangulates from disclosed net fair value of hedging instruments (approximately SEK 200-400m fair value range on derivative book L3Y) vs estimated annual USD net exposure (SEK 4.0-4.7bn) — implies approximately 40-60% of annual net USD exposure is hedged at any point
  • This is below the 70-80% coverage that SOBI's own risk profile (100% absorbed FX, zero pass-through) would warrant
  • Hedge horizon: stated "up to 18 months" but practical rolling ladder likely concentrates in 3-12 month forwards (most liquid); 12-18 month coverage is typically thinner and more option-based
  • Instrument mix: forwards (primary) + currency options/collars (secondary); mix has shifted in FY2024 AR disclosures toward slightly more options vs forwards, suggesting Treasury is paying for asymmetric protection — possibly pro-cyclical response to higher FX volatility in 2023-2024

Hedge Effectiveness L3Y (IFRS 9 Reported)

  • FY2022: SOBI reported a hedge effectiveness gain (OCI — other comprehensive income) on cash-flow hedges; favourable as USD/SEK rose through the year while hedges locked in lower USD/SEK rates (hedges were set at lower rates, so gains materialised on settlement at higher spot)
  • Approximate OCI gain from hedging instruments: ~SEK 150-250m (AR2022 disclosed, OCI hedge reserve movement)
  • FY2023: modest OCI position — hedges approximately at market as USD/SEK was relatively stable year-on-year; effectiveness ratios near 100% per IFRS 9 disclosure (no material ineffectiveness recognised in P&L)
  • FY2024: as USD/SEK softened in H2-2024, hedges at higher rates provided protection; approximately SEK 100-200m gain on hedge settlements recycled from OCI to P&L through the year — partially offsetting spot translation headwind; full-year hedge settlement gain estimated SEK 100-180m
  • L3Y hedge effectiveness: consistently reported as "highly effective" per IFRS 9 (>80% test met); ineffectiveness recognised in P&L was immaterial in all three years

Hedge Cost / Income Recognition

  • Hedge cost (time value of options): SOBI's shift toward options/collars in FY2023-2024 increases hedge cost; option premium expensed through OCI "cost of hedging reserve" under IFRS 9; not individually quantified but estimated at SEK 20-50m/year based on option notional and market premiums
  • Realised hedge gain/loss: in FY2022 (strong USD), hedges were set at lower USD/SEK rates, so settlements were at SOBI's disadvantage vs spot (hedges cost economic upside); in FY2024 (softening USD), hedges protected downside — the programme is functioning as insurance, not speculation

Instrument + Counterparty Risk

  • Forward contracts: vanilla FX forwards with named Swedish and international banks; standard ISDA agreements; counterparty risk is diversified across 4-6 banks (inferred from treasury policy disclosures)
  • Options/collars: purchased collar structures (buy put on USD, sell call on USD at higher strike) — gives downside protection while capping upside; rational for a company with zero pass-through pricing
  • Counterparty concentration risk: not explicitly disclosed; risk of regional-bank counterparty failure in extreme stress scenarios is residual and typical for this profile

Sceptical Bullet — Hedging Policy

  • Bearish read: "highly effective" IFRS 9 hedges are a compliance test, not a risk-management test — the 80% effectiveness threshold means up to 20% of notional can be ineffective without triggering P&L recognition; more importantly, the quantum coverage gap (40-60% vs 80% needed) and the 18-month duration cap mean a sustained 24-36 month USD weakness (e.g. secular USD bear market driven by twin-deficit dynamics) would progressively erode SOBI's protection as hedges roll off at lower rates; the shift toward options in FY2024 may be a pro-cyclical response to rising FX volatility rather than a structural improvement in discipline — if true, SOBI is buying expensive protection after the fact, compressing margins through hedge premium without improving long-run coverage

§6 — Quantified Sensitivity Analysis

J-front verdict: A 10% sustained USD/SEK depreciation reduces reported EBIT by approximately 6-9% net of hedges — large enough to move a 15% EBIT growth guide into mid-single-digit territory; sensitivity is the primary FX risk in the thesis and requires explicit modelling

  • Transaction: economically real cash impact; affects only the NET long USD position (SEK 4.5bn); partially hedged (~50%); does not revert without operational response
  • The split matters for valuation: investors should discount translation impacts by a mean-reversion probability; they should discount transaction impacts at full present-value cost since they are permanent cash losses; SOBI's combined sensitivity table without this decomposition (as management presents it) overstates the "permanent" earnings impact but also understates it for sustained moves
