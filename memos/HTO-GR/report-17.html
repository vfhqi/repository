2. KEY FINDINGS (BLUF)

  • Dominant verdict: OTE Group is a structurally EUR-centric business — approximately 85-90% of consolidated revenue is denominated or settled in EUR (Greece + Deutsche Telekom-linked contracts), meaning headline FX exposure is low relative to European telco peers with active EM operations; the residual risk is RON/EUR translation and modest USD transaction exposure on equipment procurement
  • RON is the live variable: Romania (Telekom Romania Communications, TRC, plus the Mobile subsidiary until disposal) contributed roughly 15-18% of consolidated revenue at peak; post the 2023 disposal of Telekom Romania Mobile, Romanian revenue is reduced but OTE still consolidates the fixed-line operation TRC — RON/EUR is the single largest FX driver
  • Translation dominates; transaction is minor: OTE does not operate in USD-revenue markets — USD appears primarily on the cost side (network equipment, roaming settlement, some debt service); this is a classic translation-risk profile, not a transaction-risk profile
  • Hedging is minimal and largely undisclosed: OTE's annual report hedging disclosures are thin — the company acknowledges currency risk, discloses net monetary position by currency, but does not publish a formal hedge ratio, hedge duration, or instrument-level detail that would let an analyst reconstruct a hedge ladder; this is a transparency deficiency
  • Sensitivity is manageable at base case: A ±10% RON/EUR move translates to approximately ±1.5-2.5% EBITDA impact at the consolidated level; a ±10% USD/EUR move on procurement costs is approximately ±0.3-0.8% EBITDA — neither is thesis-breaking in isolation
  • Tailwind scenario (EUR strength vs USD): OTE benefits modestly from EUR/USD strength because equipment procurement costs fall in EUR terms; this is a structural, low-magnitude tailwind when EUR is strong
  • Headwind scenario (RON weakens vs EUR): Romanian operations translate at a worse rate; this is the single largest FX tail risk given Romania's current account dynamics and historical RON volatility
  • Peer comparison gap: No D-BATCH-25 cohort data available; §7 uses named European-telecom analogues (Deutsche Telekom, Telekom Austria, BICS/Proximus) for structural comparison only — treat as directional, not quantitatively rigorous
  • ⚡ Structural oddity: OTE's parent Deutsche Telekom (DTAG) consolidates OTE — DTAG may centrally hedge EUR/RON and EUR/USD at group level, which would mean OTE's standalone hedging disclosure understates the effective economic hedge; this is confirmed nowhere in OTE's own filings and creates an interpretive gap

3. BODY SECTIONS

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: OTE's revenue base is heavily EUR-concentrated (~85-88% in 2023 post-Romania Mobile disposal); RON exposure is residual but still the dominant FX variable; USD revenue is negligible

  • Reporting currency: EUR; Greece segment revenue is entirely in EUR; Romanian fixed-line revenue is in RON, translated to EUR at average rate
  • EUR revenue %: estimated 85-88% of 2023 consolidated revenue (Greece domestic ~€3.0bn, plus EUR-denominated wholesale/roaming)
  • Greece contributed approximately €3.05bn revenue in FY2023 at reported rates
  • Greece is structurally 100% EUR — tariffs, wholesale interconnect, B2B contracts all in EUR
  • No USD, GBP, or CHF revenue line in the Greek segment
  • RON revenue %: estimated 12-15% of consolidated revenue post-TRC retention
  • Telekom Romania Communications (fixed broadband, TV, B2B in Romania) contributed approximately €400-450m at EUR-translated rates in FY2023
  • RON/EUR rate averaged approximately 4.96 in 2023; this is the mechanical translation rate applied to TRC's RON-denominated revenue
  • Historical RON/EUR range L5Y: 4.75-5.05 — relatively stable but not pegged; see §8 for macro outlook
  • USD revenue %: approximately 0-1% — wholesale roaming settlements with non-EUR counterparties, some international carrier revenue; immaterial as a standalone driver
  • GBP / other: negligible; OTE has no meaningful UK or EM operations beyond Romania

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base is also EUR-dominated but with a structurally important USD component in network equipment procurement; natural hedge ratio is high but imperfect — the USD cost exposure is unmatched by USD revenue

  • COGS currency mix:
  • Labour costs: ~EUR 100% — Greek and Romanian employees paid in EUR and RON respectively; Greek labour (majority of workforce) entirely in EUR
  • Network interconnect / roaming: primarily EUR (European counterparties) with modest USD settlement for non-European roaming
  • Content costs (COSMOTE TV): primarily EUR under domestic licensing deals; some USD exposure via Hollywood studio content licensing — estimated 3-5% of total content opex
  • Equipment / capex: this is the primary USD-cost node — Ericsson, Nokia, Cisco, and Huawei supply contracts are typically USD-denominated or USD-indexed even where invoiced in EUR; RESEARCHER estimates 20-30% of annual capex (~€400-500m total capex) has USD exposure, implying €80-150m USD-denominated or USD-indexed capex per annum
  • Opex currency mix:
  • Core Greek opex (SGA, labour, facilities): ~95% EUR
  • Romania opex: RON-denominated (wages, local suppliers) — natural match to RON revenue
  • IT/software: partial USD exposure via US-vendor SaaS/licensing (Microsoft, AWS, etc.) — estimated sub-5% of total opex
  • Debt service: OTE carries some USD-denominated bonds (historically); current debt profile is predominantly EUR-denominated following refinancing — see §4
  • Capex currency mix:
  • Fibre rollout in Greece: EUR contractors, EUR-denominated construction; capex is EUR-heavy
  • Equipment procurement: USD-indexed as above — single largest FX cost mismatch

§3 — Translation Exposure

J-front verdict: Translation impact has been small and relatively stable L3Y given RON/EUR stability; the operative risk is a scenario where RON weakens sharply, which has not been stress-tested publicly by management

  • Reported FX impact L1Y (FY2023): OTE's 2023 Annual Report notes FX movements as immaterial to the consolidated income statement; RESEARCHER estimates translation impact on revenue was approximately -€5 to +€5m (< 0.2% of consolidated revenue €3.5bn) given RON/EUR stability
  • Reported FX impact L3Y average: Averaging 2021-2023, the FX translation drag/tailwind on reported revenue is estimated at less than ±0.5% per annum — this is genuinely low by European telecom standards, reflecting the structural EUR-dominance
  • Translation mechanism:
  • Romanian revenue (RON) × average RON/EUR rate = EUR-translated Romanian revenue
  • Romanian assets (net monetary position in RON) × closing rate = balance sheet translation reserve movement
  • P&L hit = (average rate movement) × Romanian EBIT/EBITDA (~€80-120m); balance sheet hit = (closing rate movement) × net RON-denominated assets
  • Translation tailwind (EUR weakening vs RON): OTE reports higher EUR-equivalent Romanian revenue — a modest uplift; not a structural driver
  • Translation headwind (EUR strengthening vs RON / RON weakening): Romanian revenue shrinks in EUR terms; historically the EU accession anchor and NBR's inflation-management have kept RON relatively stable but the risk is asymmetric (RON can drop faster than it can appreciate)
  • Balance sheet translation reserve: OTE's translation reserve (equity line) moves with RON/EUR; given net Romanian assets ~€500-700m, a 5% RON depreciation would reduce equity by ~€25-35m — meaningful at balance sheet level but not material to operating earnings
  • Historical FX translation P&L L3Y: RESEARCHER triangulates from Romanian segment revenue in EUR vs approximate Romania mobile subscribers × ARPU in RON:
  • FY2021: translation impact ~-€10m (RON slightly weak)
  • FY2022: translation impact ~+€5m (RON marginally stronger)
  • FY2023: translation impact ~-€3m (RON near-flat, slight weakening H2)
  • Average annual translation P&L impact: approximately -€3m to +€5m — below 0.5% of EBITDA

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on translation is that apparent stability reflects a historically-compressed RON/EUR range (4.75-5.05 over L5Y) that itself reflects artificially-suppressed volatility from NBR intervention; the binary-event risk is Romania joining the ERM-II/euro convergence path under elevated inflation and political instability — a stress scenario where NBR abandons the implicit peg corridor and RON re-rates 10-20% is not in the management sensitivity table, and given net RON assets of ~€500-700m, a 20% move would create a ~€100-140m equity hit and a ~€15-25m annual EBITDA translation drag; this would not be visible in L3Y empirical data because it hasn't happened

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is limited and primarily on the cost side (USD equipment procurement, some USD content licensing); there is no material USD-revenue stream to create a bilateral transaction exposure

  • Transaction exposure points:
  • USD equipment procurement: Ericsson/Nokia/Cisco/Huawei contracts for network infrastructure — USD-indexed pricing creates a EUR/USD transaction mismatch on capex; annual exposed capex estimated €80-150m
  • USD content licensing (COSMOTE TV): Hollywood studio deals typically USD-denominated; annual exposed content opex estimated €15-30m
  • USD SaaS/cloud: Microsoft, AWS, similar — annual exposed opex estimated €15-25m
  • RON/EUR cash repatriation: TRC dividends / intercompany loan repayments from Romania to OTE (EUR parent) — transaction exposure on actual cash movement
  • USD debt service (historical): Legacy USD bonds; current profile is EUR-dominant post-refinancing; residual USD debt service estimated sub-€30m annually
  • Total estimated USD transaction exposure: ~€130-230m annually (capex dominant, opex secondary), against zero USD revenue — pure one-directional exposure
  • Magnitude vs translation: Transaction (USD) exposure is directionally similar in magnitude to translation (RON) exposure in a moderate-stress scenario; in a sharp USD-strength / RON-weakness combo, both hit simultaneously
  • Pricing pass-through on equipment: OTE cannot pass through EUR/USD moves to Greek telecom subscribers — regulatory price floors (EETT), market competition, and social pricing norms prevent equipment-cost pass-through in B2C tariffs; B2B contracts may have indexation provisions but RESEARCHER has no evidence of systematic FX-indexation in OTE's enterprise contracts
  • Pricing pass-through on content: COSMOTE TV subscription pricing is EUR-denominated and market-competitive; USD content cost rises cannot be rapidly passed through without subscriber churn risk; pass-through lag estimated 18-24 months at best (contract renewal cycles)
  • Cost pass-through ability — verdict: Low-to-moderate; transaction-cost FX moves absorb into margin rather than pass through to customer, particularly in the near term
  • Cash repatriation from Romania: When TRC pays dividends to OTE, the conversion of RON cash to EUR creates a transaction event; OTE's 2023 Annual Report notes intercompany balances between Romania and parent but does not quantify the FX impact of repatriation — an information gap

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on transaction exposure is that the static analysis understates the mid-shock impact because pricing pass-through takes 18-24 months while EUR/USD can move sharply within a 3-6 month window; a scenario where EUR depreciates 15-20% vs USD (e.g. rate-differential shock, US fiscal premium repricing) would hit OTE's equipment and content costs immediately, compress EBITDA margin 2-3pp in the capex-absorption period, and not reverse through pricing power for 1.5-2 years; the incremental margin compression from the transaction exposure, while individually small, would coincide with a macro environment that also pressures Greek consumer spending — double compression that the static sensitivity table misses

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: OTE's hedging policy disclosure is thin — the company acknowledges currency risk, discloses net monetary position by currency in IFRS 7 notes, but provides no disclosed hedge ratio, no formal hedge duration, and no instrument-level detail in public filings; effective hedging discipline is difficult to assess from external vantage

  • Hedging mandate: No formal published hedge mandate in OTE's Annual Report; the IFRS 7 qualitative disclosure states "the Group is exposed to foreign exchange risk arising from various currency exposures, primarily with respect to the US dollar and Romanian leu" and that "the Group uses derivative financial instruments to hedge certain foreign currency risks" — no further specifics on ratio or duration
  • Translation hedging: OTE does not appear to formally hedge translation exposure (RON/EUR balance sheet translation) — standard practice for European telecoms with EM subsidiaries; balance sheet translation reserve absorbs RON/EUR movements without derivative protection
  • Transaction hedging:
  • Evidence of derivative usage exists: OTE's financial statements show derivative financial instrument line items (typically €0-30m notional across recent years), suggesting forward contracts or interest-rate swaps are in use, but scale is limited
  • Currency-specific derivative positions are not broken out explicitly in the notes reviewed; RESEARCHER cannot reconstruct a hedge ladder
  • The limited derivative notional (if RESEARCHER's reading is correct) implies transaction hedging is partial and short-duration, not comprehensive
  • Hedge horizon: Not disclosed; RESEARCHER inference — likely 3-6 months rolling based on typical practice for similarly-sized European telcos with limited treasury function
  • Hedge ratio: Not disclosed; RESEARCHER inference — possibly 50-70% of near-term USD transaction exposure hedged, based on scale of derivative notionals relative to estimated USD exposure; this is explicitly uncertain
  • Hedge instruments: RESEARCHER identifies forward FX contracts as the likely instrument (consistent with IFRS 9 fair-value hierarchy disclosures showing Level 2 derivative assets/liabilities); no evidence of options, collars, or cross-currency swaps for FX specifically
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y: No hedge-effectiveness ratio published; IFRS 9 requires effectiveness documentation for cash-flow hedge accounting; OTE applies hedge accounting on selected instruments (evident from OCI line movements) but the scope is not explicitly stated for FX vs interest-rate
  • Hedge cost / income recognition:
  • Time-value of options (if used) would flow through P&L; forwards at market create no upfront cost
  • OTE's derivative fair-value movements are disclosed in aggregate but not decomposed by instrument type or currency
  • RESEARCHER cannot compute hedge cost as % of protected exposure — information gap
  • Deutsche Telekom group-level hedging: DTAG (62% shareholder, management control) operates a centralised Treasury; it is plausible (though not confirmed) that DTAG hedges EUR/RON and EUR/USD at group level in a way that partially covers OTE's exposures; if true, OTE's standalone derivative book understates the effective economic hedge; equally, if true, OTE's subsidiary-level P&L still bears the translation/transaction hits because DTAG's group hedge sits above OTE's consolidated P&L — the EPS impact for OTE shareholders is unaffected by DTAG group hedging

Cumulative IFRS 7 note review (FY2021-2023): - Disclosed net foreign-currency monetary position: - USD net position: typically small negative (USD liability > USD asset), consistent with equipment payables and residual debt - RON net position: positive (RON assets > RON liabilities), consistent with Romanian net operating assets - Other currencies: immaterial - Management's own sensitivity table (where disclosed): OTE's 2023 Annual Report provides a standard IFRS 7 sensitivity — a 10% appreciation of EUR vs USD and vs RON would have the following approximate impacts on pre-tax profit: - EUR/USD +10%: approximately +€8-12m pre-tax profit improvement (lower USD costs in EUR terms) - EUR/RON +10%: approximately -€10-15m pre-tax profit impact (lower RON-translated earnings) - These numbers are RESEARCHER's approximations from the IFRS 7 note; confirm against actual 2023 AR page references

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on hedging discipline is that OTE's sparse disclosure is not a sign of good hedging — it is a sign of limited hedging infrastructure relative to the complexity of the exposures; a company genuinely well-hedged with a formal programme publishes its hedge ratio, duration, and instrument mix (Vodafone, Deutsche Telekom, and Proximus all do this to varying degrees); OTE's disclosure style is closer to a company that hedges reactively and short-duration, which means in a sustained multi-quarter FX shock the effective protection is low; the inference of DTAG group hedging is speculative and cannot be relied upon

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on sensitivity is that the non-linearity emerges specifically in the +20-30% RON depreciation tail where (a) OTE's short-duration hedges provide no protection, (b) Romanian EBITDA collapses in EUR terms, (c) OTE may face impairment pressure on the Romanian net asset base (€500-700m), and (d) management's IFRS 7 sensitivity table, which is calibrated at ±10%, gives no indication of this cliff; the management-disclosed sensitivity has NOT been publicly benchmarked against a >15% RON-depreciation scenario, which is historically plausible (RON depreciated >15% vs EUR in 2009-2010, though that predates the current NBS/NBR stability framework); the tail-risk is not addressed in OTE's public investor communications and represents the single largest unquantified FX risk in the thesis

§7 — Peer FX Exposure Comparison

J-front verdict: No D-BATCH-25 cohort/sector peer data available; RESEARCHER uses named European-telecom analogues for structural benchmarking only — OTE's EUR-concentration is comparably high among EU-peripheral telecoms, but hedging transparency is below best practice

Sector-general FX-hedging principles applied to OTE:

  • Hedge duration (Duration > Ratio principle): OTE's inferred 3-6 month hedge horizon is significantly below the 12-18 month gold standard exhibited by Vodafone (USD/GBP, ZAR hedging), Deutsche Telekom (USD equipment, EUR/USD group level), and Proximus (USD equipment) — OTE scores D on hedge duration relative to European large-cap telecom peers
  • Counter-cyclical vs pro-cyclical: No evidence OTE has increased hedge ratios during periods of cheap FX volatility (when hedges are cheap, e.g. low VIX periods in 2021-2022); the absence of a published hedging programme prevents assessing cyclicality — assigned to "unknown / likely pro-cyclical by default"
  • Instrument mix discipline: Forward contracts only (inferred) vs forward + collar combinations at more sophisticated peers — OTE lacks asymmetric protection (i.e. collars that cap upside but limit downside); instrument discipline scores D vs peer best practice
  • Natural hedging quality: OTE scores well here — 85-88% EUR revenue matched by ~88-90% EUR cost is structurally clean; the Romania segment has good internal natural hedging (RON-on-RON); this is OTE's single FX strength; natural hedge scores B relative to peers
  • Transparency ("boring hedger" test): OTE fails the boring-hedger test — it does not publish quarterly hedge summaries, hedge ratio, horizon, or realised effectiveness ratio; disclosure is IFRS 7 minimum; scores D on transparency
  • Operating-side vs financial-side hedge: OTE's geographic/currency matching (natural hedge) does the structural heavy lifting; the financial hedge (derivative book) is supplementary and apparently small — this is the correct hierarchy but relies on maintaining EUR-concentration, which is exposed to Romania-mix reversal risk

§9 — FX as Tailwind/Headwind for Thesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: FX is net neutral-to-slight-tailwind in the base case for OTE's EPS trajectory thesis; it becomes a moderate headwind in the stress case (RON -15%, USD +15%) but is not thesis-breaking at ±10% moves; the bigger risk is the hedging opacity masking an unquantified exposure

  • Net FX vs thesis:
  • Base case (RON stable, EUR/USD at 1.08-1.12): FX impact on EBITDA is approximately ±€5-10m annually — immaterial to the thesis
  • OTE's EPS trajectory thesis rests on: (a) Greek fibre monetisation, (b) cost discipline, (c) capital returns; none of these are materially FX-driven in the base case
  • FX is a second-order variable, not a first-order thesis driver — this is fundamentally different from a stock with 30-40% USD revenue where FX can make or break the EPS guide
  • Tailwind quantified: EUR/USD appreciation of 5-10% over N12-24M = +€10-20m EBITDA tailwind through lower USD procurement costs; this is a mild additive to earnings but below the 1% materiality threshold
  • Headwind quantified: RON depreciation of 10-15% = -€15-25m EBITDA headwind; EUR/USD depreciation of 10-15% = -€20-30m EBITDA headwind; combined moderate stress = -€35-55m EBITDA (2.6-4.1% of EBITDA) — meaningful but not thesis-destroying
  • Thesis robustness vs FX:
  • OTE's EBITDA consensus estimate range for 2024-2026 is approximately €1.3-1.5bn; even a -€50m FX headwind keeps EBITDA in the 3.4-3.8% correction range — the thesis has sufficient buffer if the organic growth thesis (Greek fibre + cost discipline) holds
  • EPS impact at severe stress (combined -€70-90m EBITDA): approximately -€0.05-0.07 per share (7-10% EPS dilution) — this would compress the return profile materially but not invalidate a long thesis if bought at sufficient margin of safety
  • Reverse-engineer FX bear case:
  • Bear case FX scenario: RON -20% + EUR/USD to parity (EUR/USD 1.00) occurring simultaneously in a risk-off EM-stress year
  • Impact: -€115-160m EBITDA combined; EPS dilution ~15-22%
  • Probability assessment: low (5-10%) but non-negligible given Romanian fiscal overhang + US fiscal risk
  • At this scenario, OTE's EPS falls from ~€0.67-0.70 to ~€0.52-0.58 — dividend sustainability remains but IRR thesis is compromised
  • Single largest FX risk: RON depreciation > 15% sustained, which breaks the hedge (undisclosed hedge horizon exhausted), triggers Romanian net-asset impairment review, and removes Romanian EBITDA contribution — all simultaneously
  • FX-driven EPS growth illusion check:
  • FY2022 reported vs FY2021: no meaningful FX flattery — EUR share rising, RON stable
  • FY2023 reported vs FY2022: Romanian Mobile disposal changes basis; FX impact on continuing operations is sub-1%
  • Conclusion: OTE's reported EPS growth is not materially FX-flattered — unlike some UK exporters or European industrials with USD-revenue tailwinds, OTE's growth is organic (Greece) or disposal-driven (Romania Mobile off); this is reassuring — the organic growth story is not a disguised FX carry

Confidence in FX-resilience of thesis: medium-high plausibility — OTE's EUR-concentration and natural hedging provide structural protection; the thesis is not FX-dependent, but the Romanian tail and hedging opacity introduce a non-trivial floor risk that is currently unquantifiable from public disclosures
