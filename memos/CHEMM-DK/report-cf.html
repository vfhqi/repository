EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Chemometec has experienced frequent leadership turnover that has settled into a young executive team under CEO Martin Helbo Behrens and CFO Kim Nicolajsen, while major shareholders BlackRock and SEB Investment Management support the strategic pivot toward automated solutions with 14.21% and 7.20% holdings respectively and no active hostile activist presence identified . The company is transitioning from lowpriced manual counters to fully automated high-throughput systems targeting well-established pharma companies, with the XM40 carousel-based counter priced at USD 100,000 and XcytoMatic instrument sales growing organically by 60% year-on-year to DKK 30.8 million in H1 2025/26 . However, customer validation processes for automated workflows are lengthy, typically requiring 6 to 18 months , creating near-term execution uncertainty despite the structural long-term opportunity .

The competitive landscape is structurally shifting as Roche has announced the end-of-life for its market-leading Cedex HiRes cell counter by early 2029 without offering any successor product, leaving its active installed base of 2,000 to 3,000 units open , and Chemometec signed an LOI with Roche to capture this base . Industry adoption of real-time inline monitoring sensors is expanding at a high-single-digit rate, though offline sampling remains dominant because inline sensors still face calibration and regulatory cleanroom challenges .

Biopharma customers operate under draconian regulatory compliance requirements including FDA 21 CFR Part 11, and changing an established standard operating procedure is extremely difficult for bioprocess manufacturers , creating both switching cost protection for incumbents and barriers to new platform adoption.
