BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front

  • Judgement: III is a one-asset compounder where Action's deceleration from 16.7% LFL (FY23) to 4.9% (FY25) materially impairs the forward NAV growth bridge versus listed-PE peers.
  • Judgement: Two-year NAV/share CAGR of ~20.5% (2,085p FY24 to 3,030p FY26) is decelerating; the FY26 print of 19% YoY is the lowest in 5 years versus 25% (FY25) and 23% (FY24).
  • Judgement: Premium-to-NAV collapse from ~70% peak (late 2025) through ~48% (early 2026) to a ~35% discount post-buyback announcement marks regime change in how the market values III versus ICG/EQT fee-related earnings stories.
  • Analysis: Action share of PE portfolio rose from 72% (FY24) to ~80% (FY26 implied), so the asset is a balance-sheet, not a fund-management, exposure unlike ICG (87bn fee-earning AUM) or EQT (142bn fee-earning AUM).
  • Information: FY26 total return £5,304m (22%); NAV 3,030p; total dividend 84.5p (+15.8% YoY); £750m buyback announced — first-ever capital return programme.

1. Revenue and NAV growth trends

NAV per share — the headline compounding metric

  • Information — FY24 print: NAV/share closed at 2,085p (31-Mar-24), up from prior-year base on £3,839m total return delivering 23% on opening shareholders' funds.
  • Information — FY25 print: NAV/share reached 2,542p (31-Mar-25), a 21.9% YoY gain, generated by a £5,049m total return (25% on opening funds).
  • Information — HY26 (30-Sep-25): NAV/share 2,857p, up 12.4% from FY25 close in six months; £3,291m half-year total return (13%); 78p of the half-year gain was FX translation.
  • Information — FY26 print: NAV/share closed at 3,030p (31-Mar-26), +19.2% YoY; total return £5,304m or 22% on opening funds; 77p of the year's NAV uplift was FX-driven.
  • Analysis — deceleration ladder: Total-return percentage stepped from 23% (FY24) to 25% (FY25) to 22% (FY26); FY26 marks the first deceleration since the post-COVID rebuild even with absolute return higher.
  • Analysis — five-year streak: FY25 management noted five consecutive years above 20% total return and 30% average — FY26's 22% extends the streak but at a slower run-rate.
  • Judgement: NAV/share compounding remains best-in-class for listed PE; absolute NAV almost doubled in two years from 2,085p to 3,030p (~45% cumulative gain) — but the deceleration is structural, not cyclical, given Action saturation risk.

Gross investment return and proceeds

  • Information — FY25 GIR: Action alone generated GIR of £4,551m or 32% on opening value, dwarfing all other portfolio contributions and underlining single-name dependency.
  • Information — FY26 GIR: Private Equity GIR of £5,303m or 23% (FY25: £5,113m, 26%); absolute up but percentage down — same deceleration pattern as headline NAV.
  • Information — FY26 realisations: MPM sale closed September 2025 (£395m proceeds, 3.2x money, 28% IRR, 18% uplift to FY25 carry); MAIT sale closed November 2025 (£147m proceeds, 2.8x money, 28% IRR, 34% uplift to FY25 carry).
  • Information — HY26 portfolio breadth: 98% of portfolio companies by value grew earnings in the 12 months to June 2025, indicating broad-based momentum behind Action's concentration.
  • Analysis — realisations vs unrealised: FY26 realised proceeds (£542m from MPM/MAIT combined) are small relative to the £5,303m PE GIR — the vast majority is Action mark-to-multiple uplift, not crystallised cash.
  • Judgement: GIR remains durable in absolute terms; the cash realisation pipeline (Royal Sanders, smaller PE positions) is thin relative to Action carrying value of £23.4bn, sharpening exit-timing concentration risk.

Dividend per share trajectory

  • Information — FY24: Total 61.0p (first interim 26.5p paid January 2024, second 34.5p paid July 2024).
  • Information — FY25: Total 73.0p, +19.7% YoY; second-half dividend 42.5p paid July 2025.
  • Information — FY26: Total 84.5p, +15.8% YoY; first dividend 36.5p (January 2026), second dividend 48.0p proposed for payment July 2026 subject to AGM.
  • Information — FY26 yield context: Forward yield of ~2.90% at May-26 share price; second FY26 dividend annualised yields ~3.8% on May-26 close.
  • Analysis — payout policy: III's stated policy is a 'progressive' dividend funded from net cash income; payout ratio versus NAV is sub-3% — dividend is a small fraction of total return.
  • Judgement: Dividend growth (FY24 61p to FY26 84.5p = 38.5% over two years) signals balance-sheet confidence but yield-buyers should peer-anchor — ICG (87p, 16 years of growth) and 3IN (13.45p, 6.3% guided FY27 step-up to 14.30p) are the income vehicles in the comparable set.

Half-year vs full-year cadence

  • Information — FY25 first-half: Implied HY25 NAV/share 2,261p (30-Sep-24) on 8.4% half-year step-up; rest of FY25 added 12.4% in second half.
  • Information — FY26 first-half: 12.4% NAV step-up in six months (2,542p to 2,857p); second half added 6.1% to 3,030p — second-half FY26 visibly slower than first-half.
  • Analysis — H2 FY26 air pocket: The 6.1% H2 gain (vs 12.4% H1) coincided with Action LFL deceleration in October-November 2025 (mid-single-digit declines in France) recovering only to flat in December.
  • Judgement: Quarterly cadence shows the Action sales air pocket flowed through to portfolio NAV with minimal lag; FY27 H1 visibility hinges on whether early-2026 LFL recovery (6.1% in P1) sustains through summer.

2. Margin and profitability trends

Operating cost-to-AUM and III's distinctive cost model

  • Information — III structure: III is a balance-sheet investor, not a third-party fund manager; the bulk of its return is investment gains rather than fee income — so 'cost-to-AUM' has a different meaning than for ICG/BPT/EQT.
  • Information — peer cost ratios (FY25/FY26 data): ICG ran FY26 operating expense at ~£300m+ on £87bn fee-earning AUM (~0.35% of FEAUM); BPT FY25 EBITDA margin 52.6% on £427.7m management fees implies cost ratio in similar territory.
  • Information — EQT cost base: EQT FY25 FEAUM EUR 142bn; carried-interest revenue dropped 36% YoY in FY25 — margin compression visible in the fee-and-carry blend.
  • Analysis — III peer-relevance gap: III's published 'operating expenses' line (annual report cost-base disclosure) is materially smaller than peer fee-businesses because III earns most of its return from balance-sheet appreciation, not management/performance fees.
  • Judgement: Apples-to-apples cost-ratio comparison is misleading: III's effective expense ratio is sub-0.5% of NAV but the entire return engine is exposed to Action's marking — there is no fee-income annuity cushion.

Performance fee and carried interest exposure

  • Information — III carry economics: III earns carried interest on its co-investment portfolio (Eurofund V and III European Buyouts I funds tail) but the dollar amount is now immaterial relative to direct PE gains.
  • Information — ICG performance fees: ICG FY26 fee-related earnings rose 23% to £350m — fees are the engine, not balance-sheet marks.
  • Information — EQT carry compression: EQT FY25 carried interest fell 36% YoY — peer signal that vintage realisations slowed industrywide in CY2025.
  • Analysis — quality-of-earnings: III's NAV growth is dominated by unrealised mark-ups on Action (multiple expansion plus EBITDA growth); ICG and BPT earn cash fee income from third-party LPs — different earnings quality even at similar headline NAV growth.
  • Judgement: The Action realisation, when it eventually comes, will crystallise the largest single-event carry in III's history but until then the earnings quality is mark-to-multiple, not cash; this is the structural reason for the premium-to-NAV regime change.

One-off realisation gains vs underlying NAV accretion

  • Information — FY26 realisations contribution: MPM (£54m profit over FY25 mark) + MAIT (~£37m uplift over FY25 mark) contributed ~£90m to FY26 NAV uplift — under 2% of the £5,304m total return.
  • Information — FY26 underlying engine: Of the £5,303m PE GIR, Action's run-rate EBITDA increase from €2.07bn (FY24) to €2.367bn (FY25) plus stable 18.5x multiple drove the lion's share.
  • Information — FX contribution: 77p of FY26 NAV uplift was FX translation, equivalent to ~£760m of the total return — material and non-recurring.
  • Analysis — sustainable run-rate: Stripping FX (~£760m) and realisations (~£90m) leaves ~£4,450m of operational NAV gains — still ~17-18% organic NAV growth.
  • Judgement: Underlying earnings quality is high (broad-based portfolio earnings growth, real EBITDA expansion) but the headline 22% total return overstates sustainable trend by ~3-4 percentage points after FX/realisation normalisation.

Peer cost-ratio anchoring

  • Information — ICG FY26: Group operating cashflow £861m; net debt reduced to £113m; total liquidity £1,461m — strong cash conversion typical of fee-driven model.
  • Information — BPT FY25: EBITDA margin 52.6%; underlying EBITDA £304.8m on management fees £427.7m — classic asset-manager economics.
  • Information — EQT FY25: Net debt nearly doubled to EUR 1.45bn following $500m senior notes issuance; total equity fell to EUR 7.51bn from EUR 8.10bn.
  • Analysis — III balance-sheet purity: III runs minimal leverage (net cash at HY26 historically), no third-party fund obligations of consequence — the cost base is salaries, advisory, costs of being listed, not GP fund-operating costs.
  • Judgement: III's cost model is structurally simpler and lighter than peer fee-businesses, but the trade-off is single-asset concentration that fee-businesses do not carry.

3. Action portfolio performance trends

Action net sales trajectory

  • Information — CY2023: Net sales EUR 11,324m, +28% YoY (FY24 reporting period overlaps); operating EBITDA EUR 1,615m, +34% YoY.
  • Information — CY2024: Net sales EUR 13,782m, +21.7% YoY; operating EBITDA EUR 2,076m, +29% YoY.
  • Information — CY2025: Net sales EUR 16,000m, +16.1% YoY; operating EBITDA EUR 2,367m, +14% YoY.
  • Analysis — growth-rate ladder: 28% then 22% then 16% — Action's net sales growth has decelerated by ~12 percentage points over two years; EBITDA growth from 34% to 14% over the same window.
  • Judgement: Action's growth profile is normalising from outsized post-COVID years toward the 4-5% LFL plus 400 net-new-stores model management now guides; absolute scale (€16bn) means percentage growth deceleration is mathematically inevitable.

Like-for-like sales growth

  • Information — CY2023 LFL: 16.7% — the post-COVID peak driven by category mix and inflation pass-through.
  • Information — CY2024 LFL: 10.3% — strong but normalising; existing-store productivity remained robust.
  • Information — CY2025 LFL: 4.9% headline; 7.2% excluding France — France weakness emerged as a discrete drag.
  • Information — FY26 Q1 (April-June 2025): Year-to-date LFL had fallen to 2.4% versus 6.8% prior-year period — the Q1 update that broke the stock.
  • Information — FY26 Q4 / CY2026 P1: First four weeks of January 2026 saw LFL recover to 6.1% group-wide despite European snow disruption.
  • Information — France-specific: October-November 2025 mid-single-digit LFL declines; recovered to flat in December; 2.1% in P1 2026.
  • Analysis — LFL composition: 4.9% group LFL = +7.2% ex-France minus material France drag — geographic disaggregation matters because France is the largest single market.
  • Analysis — competitive pressure: France weakness blamed on weather, consumer confidence, and Middle East geopolitical impact on customer sentiment — management positioning it as cyclical not structural, market remains sceptical.
  • Judgement: Below the 4-5% guidance range, Action loses the math that underpins III's NAV bridge; the FY27 LFL print is the single most important number for III's equity story.

Gross margin and EBITDA margin trajectory

  • Information — CY2024 EBITDA margin: 15.1% (versus 14.3% in CY2023) — peak margin year as scale benefits crystallised.
  • Information — CY2025 EBITDA margin: 14.8% reported; 15.0% adjusted for EUR 26m one-off staff bonus celebrating 3,000th store.
  • Information — FY26 Q1 margin: EBITDA margin compressed to 12.4% vs 14.8% full-year CY2025 — significant short-term pressure.
  • Information — CY2026 management guide: EBITDA margin maintained at 14.8% — management not guiding for further margin compression.
  • Analysis — margin gives: 30bps margin step-down (15.1% to 14.8%) reflects investments in new countries (Switzerland, Romania), staff costs, and absorbed cost inflation.
  • Judgement: Margin trajectory is the second pillar of risk — if 14.8% guidance breaks, the 18.5x multiple becomes harder to defend; current valuation embeds margin defence.

New store openings and country expansion

  • Information — CY2023 store growth: 303 net new stores.
  • Information — CY2024 store growth: 352 net new stores, total 2,918 across 12 countries.
  • Information — CY2025 store growth: 384 net new stores (record), total 3,302 across 14 countries; two new countries (Switzerland 8 stores, Romania 6 stores) — first dual-country entry in a single year.
  • Information — CY2026 guidance: At least 400 net new store openings guided.
  • Information — geographic platform: 14 European countries including DACH (Germany/Switzerland), Benelux, France, Italy, Iberia, Poland, Czechia, Slovakia, Romania.
  • Analysis — store-opening velocity: Steady acceleration 303 to 352 to 384; the 400 guide for 2026 implies ~13% annual store-count growth.
  • Analysis — US optionality: Capital Markets Day in 2026 referenced a US launch plan — first geographic step outside Europe, material multi-year optionality but execution-heavy.
  • Judgement: New-store math (400 openings at ~EUR 1m revenue/store year-1 ramping to EUR 5m+) provides ~4-5% of new-store revenue contribution annually; combined with 4-5% LFL guidance, supports ~10% top-line growth before US contribution.

Action share-of-NAV evolution

  • Information — FY24 share: Action was 72% of Private Equity portfolio value at 31-Mar-24.
  • Information — equity stake progression: 54.8% to 57.6% (post-FY24 restructuring) to 60.1% (September 2025 GIC purchase) to 65.3% (January 2026 further GIC purchase via £1bn share issuance of 31.4m new ordinaries).
  • Information — current carrying value: £23.4bn for 65.3% stake at FY26, implying gross enterprise value of ~£35.8bn or ~EUR 42bn.
  • Information — Action share of total NAV: With £23.4bn Action carrying value against NAV/share of 3,030p on ~939m diluted shares outstanding implies III total NAV ~£28.5bn, so Action is ~82% of total NAV at FY26.
  • Analysis — concentration trajectory: From ~70% (FY24) to ~80%+ (FY26) — concentration has increased even as III scaled NAV.
  • Judgement: Action is no longer a 'core position' — it is the company; III has effectively become a single-asset listed vehicle with a 18-month-to-2-year sale process the only realistic decongestion path.

4. Peer-group comparison

  • Information — 3IN: NAV/share 386.2p (FY25) to 405.2p (FY26) = +4.9% YoY; FY26 total return 8.5% inclusive of dividend, within 8-10% medium-term target.
  • Information — ICG comparable: ICG measures fee-earning AUM (FEAUM) not NAV/share — FEAUM rose to $87bn at FY26 (+11% YoY).
  • Information — BPT comparable: BPT AUM rose 24.5% in FY25 to $94.1bn; fee-related earnings +20.7% to £150.7m.
  • Information — EQT comparable: EQT total AUM EUR 269bn (FEAUM EUR 142bn) at 31-Mar-26.
  • Analysis — apples-to-apples gap: III's NAV CAGR of ~20% trounces 3IN's 8% but is lower-quality than ICG/BPT/EQT's fee-AUM growth because III's growth is single-asset marking.
  • Judgement: III delivered the strongest headline NAV growth in the peer set but the lowest-quality earnings stream — investors must weight the trade-off of concentration risk against absolute return.

Dividend yield comparison

  • Information — III FY26: 84.5p total dividend; forward yield ~2.9% at May-26 share price.
  • Information — ICG FY26: 87p ordinary dividend; 16 consecutive years of dividend growth.
  • Information — 3IN FY26: 13.45p fully-covered; FY27 guided 14.30p (+6.3%).
  • Information — BPT FY25: 4.7p final dividend declared with results March 2026.
  • Information — EQT FY25: SEK 5.00 per share (+16% YoY from SEK 4.30); paid in two tranches.
  • Analysis — income vs growth: 3IN is the pure-income story (highest covered yield); ICG combines growth and income (87p with 16-year growth streak); III is total-return weighted (low yield, capital-gains-led).
  • Judgement: Yield-anchored investors will favour ICG and 3IN; III's value proposition is capital growth and now buyback — its yield ranks middle-of-pack in the comparable set.

Premium / discount to NAV

  • Information — III peak-to-trough 2024-2026: Premium of ~65% (mid-2024), reaching ~70% peak (late 2025), compressed to ~48% (early 2026), then collapsed to ~35% discount post-FY26 results buyback launch.
  • Information — III current: Trading at ~35% discount to FY26 March-2026 NAV of 3,030p — share price implied ~1,950p territory.
  • Information — peer benchmarks: Listed alternatives like 3IN historically trade modest premium to NAV (5-10%); ICG/BPT/EQT trade on P/E and AUM multiples rather than NAV.
  • Analysis — regime shift driver: Premium collapse maps cleanly to Action Q1 FY26 update (April-July 2025 LFL deceleration) and Q3 FY26 confirmation of further softness.
  • Judgement: The ~100-point swing from +70% premium to -35% discount in nine months is the single largest re-rating in III's recent history; the £750m buyback is management's direct response to it but does not fix the Action concentration.

Return on investment (RoI / Return on shareholders' funds)

  • Information — III multi-year: FY24 23%, FY25 25%, FY26 22% — three consecutive years above 20% total return on opening shareholders' funds.
  • Information — III five-year track record: Five consecutive years above 20% total return; average ~30% per annum across that window.
  • Information — 3IN comparable: 8.5% total return in FY26 against 8-10% medium-term target.
  • Information — peer fee-businesses: ICG/BPT/EQT report return-on-equity at low-teens levels (typical asset-manager economics) — fundamentally different metric structure.
  • Analysis — quality vs quantity: III's 22-25% RoI is 2-3x peer fee-business RoE but carries asymmetric downside if Action multiple compresses; 3IN's 8.5% is lower but with infrastructure-asset stability.
  • Judgement: III delivers exceptional total return on shareholders' funds versus listed PE peers but the dispersion of outcomes is also widest — buyer must accept Action-mark volatility.
