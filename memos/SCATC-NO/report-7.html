  • Mechanism to SP: High PPA coverage ratio reduces earnings volatility, supporting a higher multiple; loss of a key PPA or forced renegotiation is an extreme negative event [#J]

  • Company guide: Management consistently reports ~95%+ of revenues contracted; weighted average PPA life ~18-20 years across portfolio; strong DSCR ratios on operating projects [#A]

  • SS consensus: This driver is broadly consensual — sell-side gives full credit for contracted EBITDA; risk is modelled as event risk (one-off PPA stress) rather than structural [#J]

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The bearish synthesis is compelling — Scatec's six drivers are heavily correlated via a single macro-EM-stress pathway; a risk-off EM event simultaneously impairs all six, not just one [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers 1, 2, 4, and 6 are not independent — EM stress cascades: EGP devaluation (driver 6) → Egypt EBITDA falls (driver 3) → asset recycling valuations compress (driver 2) → refinancing gets harder → COD timelines slip as capex commitments are reviewed (driver 4) → pipeline conversion slows (driver 1) [#J]

  • Single-driver risk: If any one of the top three drivers (backlog conversion, deleveraging, Egypt) materially disappoints, the others are likely to be simultaneously impaired — these are not 6 independent coin flips [#A]

  • Correlation across drivers: High positive correlation in the downside scenario — the EM macro stress pathway creates a correlated downside that conventional bull/bear analysis misses if drivers are assessed in isolation [#A]

  • Cumulative plausibility: Treating each driver as ~60% probability of delivery (generous given track record), joint probability of 5/6 delivering on 24M horizon = ~60%^5 ≈ 7-8% — crude but illustrative of multi-variable compounding risk [#A]

  • Driver dependency — upside: Correlation also works in the upside — Egypt stabilisation (driver 3) → FX translates better (driver 6) → operating cash flows improve → recycling valuations improve (driver 2) → corporate leverage falls → multiple re-rates; the virtuous cycle is real but requires the stabilisation anchor [#J]
