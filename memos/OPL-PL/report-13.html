§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net verdict: Management is an honest-tracker on revenue but a systematic mild over-promiser on EBITDA; not a sandbagger; no evidence of deliberate conservatism in margin guidance [#J]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue guidance is set at a level management almost always delivers; spread between guidance and actual is ≤0.3% in all 4 periods — consistent with a management team that has good visibility on contracted and semi-contracted revenue streams [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBITDA: EBITDA guidance has been set above eventual outturn in 3 of 4 years; management consistently assumes cost savings and efficiency gains that do not fully materialise on schedule [#A]

  • Sub: This is a classic Tier 2 European telco pattern — revenue predictable, cost transformation harder than guided; peer reference: Telefonica Deutschland exhibited similar pattern 2018-2021 [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: FCF has missed the guidance midpoint in 3 of 4 years; cumulative FCF shortfall vs initial guidance over FY2022-FY2024 is approximately PLN 147m — material relative to annual DPS capacity [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal: None identified; there is no period in L4 where management beat by >2% on EBITDA, suggesting no deliberate cushion-building [#I]

  • Mixed pattern — CapEx: CapEx guidance is executed with near-precision; if anything, CapEx outturn is slightly below top of range, which modestly supports FCF — but this offset is insufficient to compensate EBITDA shortfalls [#A]

  • Pattern shift: FY2024 EBITDA AL outturn (PLN 2,810m) landed at the low end of the guidance range (PLN 2,800-2,880m), representing a narrowing of the miss versus FY2022's -3.6%; trend is improving but not yet converged [#J]

  • Improving accuracy: EBITDA variance has tightened from -3.6% (FY2022) to -1.4% (FY2023) to -1.1% (FY2024); directionally constructive but still consistently on the miss side [#A]

  • Sub: Three consecutive misses, even if narrowing, are a pattern — not noise; a sandbagger would have beaten at least once in a normalising macro environment [#J]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 4 of 4 years; this is the single genuine positive pattern in the guidance track record [#A]

  • Sceptical take — §3: The improving EBITDA accuracy trend may be cyclical: FY2022-FY2023 macro headwinds (energy) are subsiding, making the miss-narrowing look structural when it may simply reflect cost tailwinds reverting; if Polish wage inflation re-accelerates (as seen in 2024 minimum wage rounds), the pattern could deteriorate again rather than convert to beats [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The FY2025-FY2027 EBITDA and FCF bridge rests on five interconnected assumptions; sensitivity analysis below shows margin compression from any single adverse move.

  • Net verdict: The assumption stack is moderately aggressive on energy costs and ARPU trajectory; the most fragile single assumption is labour/energy cost containment in 2025-2026; simultaneous failure of two assumptions collapses the N3Y EBITDA case [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Fibre net adds of ~200-250k/year in FY2025-FY2026 implied; requires sustained +12-15% fibre customer growth vs base; FY2024 actual adds ~210k suggest this is achievable but requires consistent execution [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: ARPU blended flat-to-+2% guided; fibre ARPU premium vs copper is PLN ~10-15/month but realisation has lagged guided pace in FY2023-FY2024 due to promotional pricing at launch [AS-Trigon] [#A]

  • Sub: If ARPU uplift is +1% vs guided +2%, revenue impact ~PLN 60-80m annually — within the guidance range but narrows the beat corridor [#I]

  • Revenue assumption — B2B: B2B digital services (cloud, cybersecurity, ICT) guided to grow 8-10% in FY2025; FY2024 actual was ~6% — 4pp gap between guided and delivered growth rates is a watch item [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — wholesale: Fixed wholesale and roaming assumed stable to modest decline; UKE (Polish regulator) wholesale access pricing review is an unmodelled downside risk explicitly excluded from guidance assumptions [#I]

  • Margin assumption — cost / energy: Energy costs assumed flat in real terms 2025-2026; Polish industrial energy prices rose ~8% in 2024; management's hedge book (detail undisclosed) provides protection through mid-2025 but guidance through FY2026 assumes normalisation [AS-Erste] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — labour: Polish minimum wage rose 19% in 2024 and 5-6% in 2025; management assumes cost of employee savings (automation, insourcing) offset ~50-60% of wage pressure; this assumption has never been verified against delivered cost savings data [#I]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: ~PLN 200m EBITDA improvement guided FY2024-FY2027 is ~50% operating leverage (revenue growth dropping through) and ~50% cost efficiency; if revenue growth is only 2% (vs guided 3-4%), leverage contribution halves [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Management guides effective tax rate ~19% (Polish CIT baseline 19%); no change expected; this is a low-risk assumption given stable Polish corporate tax law [#I]

  • EPS assumption — share count: ~1.31bn shares outstanding; no buyback guided; dilution risk low; share count is a stable, reliable assumption [#I]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt / EBITDA guided ~1.7-1.9x in FY2025 declining to ~1.5x by FY2027; interest cost sensitivity moderate at current PLN rates; if PLN rates stay elevated, interest expense erodes EPS by ~PLN 0.02-0.03/share vs base case [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: PLN/EUR stability assumed; OPL's roaming and network equipment CapEx has modest EUR exposure; not a material risk driver [#I]
