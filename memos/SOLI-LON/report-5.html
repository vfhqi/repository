§1 — Executive Summary (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

SOLI-LON's FY2025 results (year to 29 March 2025) delivered the sharpest earnings reset in the company's recent history, driven by a prolonged destocking cycle in electronic components distribution that proved significantly deeper than H1 guidance implied.

  • Verdict: FY2025 was a material earnings trough — revenue -18% and adjusted PBT -~50% YoY; the thesis hinges on whether FY2026 delivers the recovery management signalled [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Full-year revenue and profit missed the trajectory implied by pre-H1 management commentary (broadly flat growth expected); H1 FY2025 warning note in November 2024 began the re-basing, but full-year numbers confirmed the scale of the shortfall [#A]

  • Revenue ~£185m vs ~£226m FY2024 prior year; delta approximately -£41m

  • Adjusted PBT ~£9-10m vs ~£19m FY2024; approximately -50% decline

  • Key surprise: Components destocking duration — management and sell-side entered FY2025 expecting H1 weakness to clear; H2 did not recover as expected, making the full-year miss larger than the November profit warning implied [#J]

  • Components revenue estimated to have fallen 25-30% YoY within the division

  • Systems revenue held flat-to-slightly-positive, partially offsetting

  • Magnitude: A ~50% PBT decline is at the severe end of distribution for destocking corrections in UK electronic components peers (peer comparables: Diploma PLC and Volex experienced 5-15% PBT dips in comparable destocking episodes 2022-23) [#A]

  • Forward guide change: No quantified FY2026 guidance provided at full-year results; management cited improving order trends and order book rebuild but declined to provide explicit revenue or PBT numbers, a notable step-back in guidance practice vs prior years [#A]

  • Consensus revision: Sell-side FY2026 EPS estimates cut 20-30% post November 2024 profit warning; full-year FY2025 results broadly confirmed re-based numbers, triggering only incremental further cuts rather than a second large revision [#A]

  • Analyst tone: Cautiously constructive — Zeus Capital and Cavendish maintained positive ratings but flagged that FY2026 recovery evidence is needed before conviction improves; tone less enthusiastic than 12 months prior [#A]

  • Cavendish: maintained Add, similar price target compression

  • Shore Capital: moved to Hold pending recovery evidence (per public domain note references)

  • Investment implication: The trough is visible but the recovery timeline is not confirmed — FY2026 PBT recovery to £15-17m would represent +60-70% growth from trough, achievable if Components normalises; but guidance opacity increases uncertainty [#J]

  • Sceptical counter: The apparent "trough" could extend if electronic component inventory cycles prove longer than typical 12-18M (as occurred in 2019-2020 for broader distribution sector); management's optimistic order-book commentary has been wrong before in this cycle; through what mechanism — sustained customer inventory drawdown or structural share loss — could FY2026 also disappoint?

§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

  • Reported growth: -18% reported YoY in FY2025 versus +13% in FY2024 and +11% in FY2023; a three-year growth CAGR of approximately +8% has been fully unwound in a single year [#A]

  • Organic growth: Organic decline broadly tracks reported decline (~-18%) given absence of major M&A in FY2025; no FX distortion (GBP-denominated business) [#A]

  • Segment trajectory — Components: Accelerating deceleration — Q1 FY2025 showed modest decline, Q2-Q3 deepened, Q4 showed early stabilisation per H2 management commentary [#A]

  • Electronic component distribution: customer destocking cycle driven by OEMs drawing down 2021-22 safety stock

  • Pricing: spot market component prices fell 15-25% for passive components (industry data: IHS Markit/S&P Global components index); SOLI pricing power negligible in spot distribution

  • Segment trajectory — Systems: Stable-to-modest growth — defence/aerospace programme revenue relatively insulated from inventory cycle; custom power systems and ruggedised communications held [#A]

  • UK defence spending backdrop: MoD procurement uplift (2.5% GDP target) provides medium-term tailwind

  • Long lead-time programme work creates 12-18M revenue visibility

  • Geographic mix: Predominantly UK — SOLI revenue >85% UK-sourced; limited geographic diversification means UK industrial demand conditions are the primary driver; UK manufacturing PMI was <50 for most of H1 FY2025, consistent with SOLI's destocking experience [#A]

  • Price contribution: Negative price contribution in Components — electronic component prices normalised sharply post-2022 supply crisis; SOLI passed lower input costs through but with compressed margins, suggesting volume also fell [#A]

  • Volume contribution: Volume decline in Components estimated at -15 to -20%; price decline estimated -5 to -10%; combined effect produced the -25 to -30% segment revenue fall [#A]

  • Pricing power: Limited in distribution — Components distribution is inherently low-pricing-power; SOLI is a value-added distributor but cannot fully resist spot market pricing; Systems has better pricing power via long-term contracts [#A]

  • Market share signals: Ambiguous — revenue decline consistent with sector-wide destocking but SOLI's decline (-18% group) appears steeper than some UK peers; unclear whether share is being lost or whether SOLI's customer mix is more exposed to the destocking dynamic [#A]

  • No contract loss announcements in public domain

  • End-market demand: Mixed across SOLI's end-markets — industrial and commercial electronics customers (Components) suffered inventory overhang; defence customers (Systems) remained active [#A]

  • UK industrial output contracted in 2024; recovery tentative in late 2024/early 2025

  • Forward revenue guidance: No quantified FY2026 guidance — management referenced improving order book and early H2 FY2025 stabilisation in Components; sell-side extrapolated ~10-15% revenue recovery for FY2026 based on management commentary [#A]

  • Sell-side revision: FY2026 revenue estimates cut ~10-15% post profit warning; post full-year results, limited incremental revision as re-basing had occurred [#A]

  • Sceptical counter: Volume recovery in Components is not confirmed — management's "improving order book" commentary refers to the Systems backlog (inherently strong); it is unclear whether Components order intake is genuinely recovering or merely stabilising at a lower run-rate; if Components settles at £100-110m annualised rather than recovering to £130-140m, FY2026 group revenue would be ~£170-180m, not the sell-side's implied £200-210m recovery scenario

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

  • Verdict: Margins contracted sharply in FY2025 — operating deleverage on falling Components revenue; Systems partially cushions; recovery requires volume, not cost-cutting [#J]

  • Gross margin: Gross margin ~22-24% in FY2025 vs ~23-25% FY2024 and ~24-26% FY2023; approximately -200bps three-year compression [#A]

  • Components gross margin fell more sharply (~18-20% vs ~21-23% prior year) as spot pricing compressed

  • Systems gross margin relatively stable (~28-32%) due to contract pricing and higher technical value-add

  • Margin direction: Contracting — FY2021-FY2024 margin expansion story has reversed; gross margin now below FY2022 levels on a Components-drag basis [#A]

  • EBIT margin: Adjusted EBIT margin fell from ~9% (FY2024) to ~5-6% (FY2025) — a 300-400bps decline; this compares to Diploma PLC maintaining EBIT margins of ~19-21% across the same period, illustrating the structural margin gap between value-added distribution and commodity-adjacent distribution [#A]

  • EBITDA margin: EBITDA margin somewhat more stable due to D&A absorption (~£8-10m annual D&A base from acquired assets); EBITDA approximately £17-20m, margin ~9-11% [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Negative operating leverage confirmed — revenue fell ~18% but adjusted EBIT fell ~50%; implies high fixed-cost proportion in the business; fixed costs (headcount, property, distribution infrastructure) not cut materially during trough [#A]

  • Incremental margin on revenue decline: approximately 25-30p of EBIT lost per £1 of revenue lost — high sensitivity

  • Decomposition — price contribution: Negative price contribution in Components eroded gross margin; pricing pressure partly offset by lower input (product) costs but spread compression evident [#A]

  • Decomposition — mix contribution: Positive mix from Systems growth — as Systems maintained revenue and Components fell, Systems became a larger share of group; high-margin Systems partially offset gross margin compression [#A]

  • Productivity: No material restructuring undertaken in FY2025 — management did not cut headcount aggressively, preserving capability for the expected recovery; this was a deliberate choice but created earnings drag [#A]

  • D&A trend: D&A elevated and rising due to cumulative M&A; acquired intangible amortisation ~£4-6m per year; if SOLI continues bolt-on acquisitions this line will grow, widening the adjusted/reported gap further [#A]

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A as % of revenue increased as revenue fell but costs held; selling costs and support functions not scaled down proportionately [#A]

  • One-off costs: No material restructuring charges flagged in FY2025; minor integration costs from recent acquisitions remain in the adjusted/reported bridge [#A]

  • Adjusted vs reported: Material non-GAAP adjustment (~£4-6m amortisation) on a ~£9-10m adjusted PBT base means reported PBT could be as low as £3-6m — almost 40-60% of "adjusted profit" is an add-back; this ratio is deteriorating as earnings trough and M&A base grows [#A]

  • Sceptical counter: If Components volume does not recover, the fixed-cost base means margins will not recover proportionately — the business has not restructured for a permanently smaller Components division; a scenario where Components settles at £100-110m (vs £140-150m peak) would imply EBIT margin structurally impaired at 4-6% group level, not the 8-10% sell-side FY2026 scenario implies; hidden integration cost drag from acquisitions also yet to fully unwind
