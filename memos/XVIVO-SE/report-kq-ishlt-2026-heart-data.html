2. Heart Transplant Data Presented

2a. XVIVO NIHP2019 European RCT -- 12-Month Results (Published 16 April 2026)

This is the headline dataset. Published in the European Heart Journal (doi: 10.1093/eurheartj/ehag264) just 3 days before ISHLT 2026. Timing is deliberate -- maximum visibility.

Trial design:
- Randomised, controlled, open-label, multicentre
- 204 patients enrolled across 15 institutions in 8 European countries
- Enrollment period: November 2020 to May 2023
- Intervention: XVIVO Heart Assist Transport using hypothermic oxygenated perfusion at 8 degrees C
- Control: standard static cold storage (ice)

12-month results:
- Survival: 92% XVIVO group vs 86% control -- 6 percentage point absolute improvement
- Severe complications: 33% XVIVO vs 47% control -- 38% relative risk reduction
- Severe PGD (primary graft dysfunction): 76% relative risk reduction in the XVIVO group
- Mechanism: Cold, oxygenated perfusion maintains the heart in a low-metabolic resting state; the graft is reoxygenated in a controlled fashion while cellular metabolism is suppressed

Assessment: These are strong data. A 6pp survival advantage at 12 months in a randomised trial is clinically meaningful. The 76% PGD reduction is particularly compelling because severe PGD is the primary cause of early mortality post-heart transplant. The open-label design is a limitation, but the hard endpoints (survival, PGD) are relatively objective. Publication in the European Heart Journal (top-5 cardiology journal by impact factor) adds credibility.

2b. XVIVO PRESERVE Trial -- US Single-Arm (Preliminary 1-Year Data Expected at ISHLT)

2d. DCD Heart Transplant Trends

DCD heart transplantation is one of the fastest-growing segments in transplant medicine.

Key data points (from ISHLT registry and recent publications):
- In 2023, 613 DCD hearts were transplanted globally, representing 13.5% of all heart transplants
- DCD accounts for ~8.8% of heart transplants in North America and ~19% in Europe
- Based on ISHLT survival analysis (1,500 DCD recipients vs 15,000 DBD recipients), 1-year survival is equivalent at ~92% for both groups
- Yield modelling suggests 700-1,200 additional DCD hearts could be utilised annually, representing a 15-27% expansion of total heart transplant volume

XVIVO's DCD position: The FDA granted XVIVO approval to include DCD hearts in the PRESERVE IDE clinical trial. This is strategically important because DCD hearts require machine perfusion for assessment and resuscitation -- cold storage is not viable for DCD hearts. This gives perfusion technologies (XVIVO, TransMedics) a structural advantage over passive storage (Paragonix) in the DCD segment.

  2. PRESERVE (US single-arm): 141 ECD patients, preliminary 1-year data at ISHLT 2026. Supports FDA PMA.

Technology Description

XVIVO Heart Assist Transport uses non-ischemic heart preservation via hypothermic oxygenated perfusion :
- Continuously circulates oxygenated, nutrient-rich perfusion solution through coronary arteries
- Maintains heart at 8 degrees C in a controlled, low-metabolic resting state
- Combines advantages of hypothermia, cardioplegia, and oxygenated perfusion
- The heart is reoxygenated in a controlled fashion while cellular metabolism is reduced

This is fundamentally different from TransMedics OCS, which perfuses the heart at normothermic (37 degrees C) temperatures with warm blood, maintaining the heart in a beating state.
