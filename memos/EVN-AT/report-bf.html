EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

EVN AG has strategically repositioned itself as a lower-risk integrated utility by consolidating around regulated networks and divesting volatile international wastewater projects, anchoring approximately 85% of EBITDA in stable activities concentrated in Lower Austria while maintaining exposure to politically complex Bulgarian and North Macedonian markets . The company's updated Strategy 2030 commits to deploying approximately €1 billion annually through 2030, with roughly 80% allocated to domestic Lower Austrian infrastructure to expand the regulatory asset base under new executive leadership . However, this high dependence on network regulatory returns creates vulnerability to political tariff caps and regulatory WACC reductions , while uncoordinated national pricing interventions and extended windfall taxes could permanently suppress investment returns .

Value Chain Position and Margin Concentration

EVN occupies a vertically integrated position across generation, distribution, and retail supply, with distribution networks capturing 12-18% EBIT margins through stable monopoly returns while retail supply margins remain

compressed at 3-5% . The company owns 980 MW of renewable and 622 MW of thermal capacity alongside 142,423 km of electricity grids, but maintains zero presence in high-voltage transmission networks controlled entirely by Verbund . The Austrian utility market exhibits highly concentrated structures with generation assets capturing volatile merchant profits historically representing 75% of peer Verbund's EBITDA, though TSO EBITDA contribution is forecast to reach 22% by 2030 . Distribution networks represent the profit-favored stage for EVN, providing highly stable regulated returns while generation margins face 95% windfall taxes above €90/MWh . 20

Customer Dynamics and Retention Barriers

Supplier Risks and Procurement Dynamics

EVN stabilizes wholesale energy exposure through disciplined rolling 12-18 month hedges and structural procurement relationships with joint venture EnergieAllianz, yet severe European-wide grid equipment shortages threaten network upgrade timelines as lead times for critical high-voltage substations stretch up to three years. The company maintains tight customer collection terms of 14 days providing robust cash inflows, but working capital deteriorated from rising receivables at EVN Wärme alongside a 34.49% contraction in outstanding trade payables . Strategic supplier tier-classification protects operations from direct ESG breaches but leaves the company exposed to systemic upstream risks, while bilateral OTC trading platforms streamline wholesale procurement.

Competitive Landscape and Vertical Integration Threats

Competitive pressure intensifies as municipal giant Wien Energie targets 1,800 MW of renewable capacity by 2030 and peers aggressively expand charging infrastructure networks, while industrial consumers increasingly bypass traditional retail intermediaries by securing direct long-term power purchase agreements with generators like Verbund and deploying captive self-generation. Major petrochemical group OMV secured 49% of its Schwechat refinery power from bilateral renewable PPAs, representing structural erosion of utility retail margins. Peer R&D investments pivot toward grid-stabilizing battery technologies and digital middleware platforms to integrate volatile decentralized generation sources, with Verbund building 92 MW of German battery capacity and investing in SPiNE energy management systems targeting 28 million smart gateways.

Operator Quality and Foundation Strength

EVN benefits from exceptional governance stability with CEO Stefan Szyszkowitz extended until January 2031 and constitutional 51.0% majority provincial ownership preventing hostile takeovers, complemented by Vienna's 28.4% minority stake . The updated Strategy 2030 pivots capital away from volatile international projects toward Lower Austria through a massive €900m annual investment program, with asset divestment to STRABAG for €100m eliminating highly volatile construction risks . The company operates natural gas and electricity grid monopolies spanning 142,423 km with high regulatory stability providing nominal pre-tax returns, earning efficiency premiums up to 5.30% that place returns near the peer top end . Strategic targets call for sustainable group ROCE exceeding 6.0% through 2030, supported by Moody's A1 and Scope A+ credit ratings with stable outlooks , though state majority control and restricted free float could allow regional politicians to enforce unprofitable price caps or demand excessive capital distributions. 31

Structural Disruption and Long-Term Growth

Evolving national grid plans secure a massive multi-decade growth pipeline as electrification drives EVN's €1bn average annual capex program with four-fifths concentrated in Lower Austria, protected by high capital barriers blocking new entrants . However, accelerating rooftop solar and battery storage deployment directly erodes traditional retail supply volumes, with domestic household electricity sales declining 8.9% in the first half-year as prosumers meet their own needs . The company is pivoting defensively by targeting 300 MW of battery storage capacity by 2030 with 200 MW co-located at existing Lower Austrian plants to capture arbitrage spreads and balance grid volatility . EVN's 50.03% stake in RAG-Beteiligungs-AG positions it at the forefront of green hydrogen storage through the Underground Sun Storage project controlling approximately 6.3bn m³ of capacity in depleted gas reservoirs , while strong electrification tailwinds drive Austrian electricity demand projections to 93 TWh by 2030 and 125 TWh by 2040 representing 76% expansion . Favorable grid regulation provides fixed legacy returns of 4.16% on assets commissioned through 2022 and elevated returns of 6.33% in 2024 rising to 6.11% in 2026 on new investments , though persistent regulatory delays in transposing European energy packages could stall green hydrogen and storage infrastructure returns indefinitely .
