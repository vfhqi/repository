KEY FINDINGS - Delivery verdict: PPC-GR's FY2024 results confirmed a genuine operational inflection — EBITDA reached ~€1.85bn, net profit ~€370m, both broadly in line with upgraded full-year guidance issued mid-2024; the trajectory is structurally up from the loss-making 2021-22 trough [#J]

  • Earnings turnaround: Net earnings swung from a €(1.3)bn net loss in FY2022 to +€370m in FY2024, a recovery scale that is unusual for a Southern European regulated utility over a 24-month window; driven by energy retail margin normalisation, RES capacity additions, and cost discipline [#J]

  • Guidance track record: Management guided to ~€1.8-1.9bn EBITDA for FY2024 at the start of the year; delivery at ~€1.85bn represents an in-line-to-slight-beat pattern, consistent with a credible guidance regime that replaced the erratic period of 2021-22 [#A]

  • RES growth engine: Renewables capacity additions (targeting 5.5GW by end-2025) are the primary organic growth driver; installed RES base roughly doubled from ~2GW (2021) to ~4GW+ (2024), providing both volume and green-premium revenue tailwind [#J]

  • Leverage elevated: Net debt reached ~€4.0-4.5bn at FY2024, implying net debt/EBITDA of ~2.2-2.5x — above typical European regulated-utility comfort zones of 3.0x+ but high for a company simultaneously funding a >€3bn capex programme [#A]

  • Regulatory risk live: The Greek government retains a ~34% stake; tariff and subsidy framework for the transition period remains a material variable; any adverse regulatory reset would compress merchant margins and undermine the 18M-3Y EPS path [#J]

  • Sell-side consensus: Coverage is concentrated among Greek and regional brokers plus a handful of European utility desks; post-FY2024 consensus points to ~€1.95-2.05bn EBITDA for FY2025, implying modest ~5-10% growth — a conservative-to-achievable trajectory given capex ramp [#A]

  • Investment implication: The thesis rests on continued RES delivery + retail margin durability + leverage normalisation; FY2024 results neither invalidated nor decisively confirmed the full bull case — they represent a credible stepping stone, not a proof-of-concept completion [#J]

§1 — Executive Summary (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

J-front verdict: FY2024 delivered EBITDA and net profit broadly in line with raised guidance, confirming operational recovery but not yet resolving leverage or regulatory uncertainties central to the thesis [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Revenue ~€7.0-7.5bn for FY2024, EBITDA ~€1.85bn, net profit ~€370m; all three metrics came in at or modestly above the guidance range management issued after H1 2024 results [#A]
  • Sub: Revenue growth was driven by RES volume additions and continued retail tariff normalisation rather than spot-price windfalls, making quality relatively higher than 2022's energy-crisis beat

  • Sub: Net profit beat was modest in absolute terms but highly significant relative to the FY2022 loss base — trajectory direction is unambiguously positive

  • Key surprise: The margin resilience in the retail electricity supply segment was the key positive surprise; fears of margin compression from competitive intensity in the Greek retail market did not fully materialise in FY2024 [#A]

  • Sub: Competitive market share loss (from ~70% in 2020 to ~55-60% by 2024) was slower than bear-case feared, preserving retail EBIT contribution

  • Sub: This was partly offset by higher-than-expected D&A from accelerated RES asset commissioning

  • Magnitude: EBITDA delivery of ~€1.85bn represents approximately +30% growth vs FY2022's depressed ~€1.4bn trough; normalised (ex-energy-crisis distortions) EBITDA growth is a more modest but still solid ~15-18% CAGR [#J]

  • Forward guide change: Management guided FY2025 EBITDA to ~€2.0bn+ at the results presentation, representing ~8-10% growth vs FY2024 actuals; this was broadly in line with sell-side consensus and did not surprise the market materially [#A]

  • Consensus revision: Post-FY2024, consensus FY2025 EPS estimates moved modestly upward (~3-5% revision), reflecting the slight beat on net profit; this is a muted revision magnitude compared to growth-phase European utilities that typically see 8-15% post-beat revisions [#A]

  • Analyst tone: Covering brokers (National Bank Securities, Alpha Finance, Piraeus Securities, Eurobank Equities, plus select European desks) were constructively positive but tempered; the main hesitation centred on capex execution risk for the 2025 RES pipeline and Greek macro sensitivity [#A]

  • Sub: No material rating upgrades or downgrades post-FY2024; price target adjustments were incremental (+5-10%)

  • Sub: Absence of a pan-European broker upgrade cycle limits share price momentum potential in the near term

  • Investment implication: FY2024 strengthens the thesis that the 2021-22 trough was cyclical not structural; the 18M-3Y EPS trajectory to ~€500-600m net profit (FY2026E) is achievable but depends on three parallel execution variables — RES commissioning, retail margin defence, and leverage reduction [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: Apparent operational strength in FY2024 could prove partially transient if Greek retail electricity margins compress further under competitive pressure in 2025-26; the bear mechanism is market share erosion accelerating as competitors (Mytilineos Energy, HELLENiQ Energy retail) gain scale, compressing PPC's retail EBIT contribution faster than RES earnings replace it [#J]

§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

J-front verdict: Revenue growth is real but moderating from crisis-era levels; RES volume additions are the primary organic growth engine; retail volume share loss is a structural headwind partially offset by capacity growth [#J]

  • Reported growth: FY2024 revenue ~€7.2bn, +6% vs FY2023; this compares to +15-25% reported growth in FY2022 (energy crisis tailwind) and ~flat to slight decline in FY2023 as spot prices normalised; the current trajectory is more sustainable but less impressive [#A]

  • Organic growth: Stripping out energy price normalisation effects, underlying organic growth is driven by RES capacity additions (~0.5-0.8GW net new per year) and network tariff escalation (~3-5% regulated revenue escalator); organic volume growth is approximately 5-8% annually at the generation level [#J]

  • Sub: Organic growth is concentrated in the generation/RES segment; retail supply organic growth is flat to slightly negative (volume share loss offset by tariff increases)

  • Sub: Network segment organic growth is ~3-4% in line with regulatory asset base escalation — predictable but limited

  • Segment trajectory: RES/generation segment accelerating (capacity additions driving volume), infrastructure stable, retail supply gently decelerating (share loss vs competitors) [#A]

  • Sub: RES segment growth is the thesis-critical variable — if commissioning delays materialise, organic growth compresses materially

  • Sub: Retail deceleration is manageable at current pace (~1-2pp share loss per year) but would become problematic at 3-5pp annual loss

  • Price contribution: In FY2024, approximately 60-65% of revenue growth was volume/capacity driven, 35-40% was price/tariff driven (estimated from segment disclosures); this is a healthier mix than 2022's price-dominated growth [#J]

  • Pricing power: In regulated networks, pricing power is formula-driven (WACC-based regulatory regime); in retail, pricing power is constrained by competition; in RES generation, pricing is partly merchant (spot exposure) and partly contracted (PPAs) [#A]

  • Sub: PPA coverage for RES output was ~50-60% as of end-2024, providing partial revenue certainty; management targeting 65-70% PPA coverage by end-2025

  • Sub: Merchant exposure creates earnings volatility — if Greek/European spot electricity prices decline materially, RES merchant revenue underperforms

  • Market share: Retail electricity market share declining from ~70% (2020) to estimated ~55-58% (FY2024); this is structurally significant — PPC is losing ~3-5pp per year in a market that is genuinely competitive ❌ [#J]

  • Sub: Share loss is partially structural (liberalisation) and partially competitive (rival pricing aggression)

  • Sub: The bear case is that share loss accelerates as the transition period subsidies phase out, exposing PPC to unsubsidised retail competition for the first time

  • End-market demand: Greek electricity consumption has been broadly stable to slightly growing (~1-2% per annum); electrification trends (heat pumps, EVs) provide a modest medium-term volume tailwind; this is below the 2-4% demand growth rates in faster-growing European markets (Iberia, CEE) [#A]

  • Forward revenue guidance: Management targets ~€7.5-8.0bn revenue for FY2025, implying ~5-10% growth; driven primarily by new RES capacity (targeting commissioning of ~0.8-1.0GW in 2025) and continued network tariff escalation [#J]

  • Sell-side revision: Post-FY2024, consensus FY2025 revenue estimates revised modestly upward (~2-4%); sell-side is broadly aligned with management guidance range; no significant bull/bear divergence in revenue forecasts [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Reported revenue growth of 6% in FY2024 masks the structural tension between RES volume additions (growing) and retail supply (declining share); the bear mechanism is that RES additions fail to offset retail revenue erosion at the pace management assumes — Greek merchant electricity prices have been declining from 2022 highs and any further normalisation compresses RES merchant revenue faster than contracted PPA volumes can compensate; forward guidance achievability is 70-75% probability in base case, lower under a merchant price bear scenario [#J]

  • Margin direction: EBITDA margin expanding from ~14.5% (FY2021) to ~25.7% (FY2024), a significant structural improvement; normalisation of energy procurement costs post-crisis is the primary driver, not operational magic [#J]
  • Sub: The FY2022 margin appears high in EBITDA terms but masks massive net losses from hedging; FY2024 is the first "clean" high-margin year

  • Sub: Target structural EBITDA margin is ~26-28% per management strategy plan, implying ~1-2pp of further expansion potential

  • Gross margin drivers: Primary drivers of margin improvement: (1) elimination of loss-making below-market hedge positions (unwound by end-2023); (2) RES capacity additions with near-zero marginal cost; (3) retail cost rationalisation (workforce reduction, branch network optimisation) [#A]

  • Sub: Input cost sensitivity has reduced — PPC's natural gas procurement exposure is now primarily pass-through in regulated tariffs, limiting direct margin risk

  • Sub: Labour cost reduction (headcount ~down ~15% from ~12,000 to ~10,000+ over 2021-24) delivered structural OpEx savings

  • EBIT margin: ~13% in FY2024 vs ~12% FY2023; below EBITDA margin due to elevated D&A (~€900m per year) from growing asset base; D&A is expected to increase further as the €3bn+ capex programme commissions more assets [#A]

  • Sub: D&A/Revenue ratio ~12-13%, high relative to a mature utility; this will remain elevated for 3-5 years during peak capex phase

  • Sub: EBIT margin expansion will lag EBITDA margin expansion due to this D&A headwind — net margin expansion is the higher-quality signal

  • Operating leverage: Revenue grew ~6% in FY2024; EBITDA grew ~12%; positive operating leverage confirmed — fixed cost base is scaling below revenue, validating the recovery thesis [#J]

  • D&A trend: D&A growing ~15% YoY in FY2024; this is a structural headwind that will persist through the capex investment phase (2025-2027); EBITDA-to-FCF conversion is constrained accordingly [#A] ❌

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A and other OpEx as % of revenue declining from ~15% (FY2022) to ~12% (FY2024); workforce rationalisation and procurement savings are real and measurable [#J]

  • One-off costs: No material restructuring charges in FY2024; prior years (2021-23) carried €100-150m of restructuring/special charges; the absence of restructuring drag in FY2024 is a mild tailwind to reported net profit quality [#A]

  • Adjusted vs reported: Gap is minimal; non-GAAP adjustments are primarily pension/employee benefit provisions; the gap (~€50-80m) has been stable and is not expanding — positive quality signal vs European utility peers that sometimes run large non-GAAP adjustments [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The margin expansion story from FY2022-24 is substantially a "normalisation from distortion" story, not a "structural competitive moat" story; the bear mechanism is that once energy procurement cost normalisation is fully absorbed (which it largely is by FY2024), incremental margin expansion must come from genuine operational improvement and RES growth — and the FY2024 data point is the first year where this must stand on its own; if RES merchant margins compress (lower Greek/EU spot prices), the FY2025-26 margin expansion story slows materially with limited alternative drivers [#J]

§5 — Cash Flow + Capital Allocation

J-front verdict: Operating cash flow is strong and growing, but high capex absorption limits FCF; leverage is elevated and declining only slowly; no capital return programme in place [#J]

  • Operating cash flow: FY2024 operating cash flow estimated ~€1.5-1.6bn, up from ~€1.2-1.3bn FY2023; cash conversion (OCF/EBITDA) of approximately ~82-85% — reasonable for a capital-intensive utility, consistent with European regulated utility peer ranges of 75-90% [#A]

  • NWC trend: Working capital is broadly neutral to mildly favourable in FY2024; receivables management improved (Greek retail customers paying more reliably post-energy-crisis); payables stable; no material NWC distortion to cash conversion [#A]

  • Sub: Regulatory receivables (state subsidy settlements) are historically a NWC drag for PPC; FY2024 saw partial settlement, marginally improving cash position

  • Sub: Bad debt provisions in retail electricity supply have normalised post-crisis — a positive quality signal

  • Capex: FY2024 capex ~€1.6bn, representing ~22% of revenue; this is elevated relative to European utility peers at steady-state (~10-15% of revenue) but appropriate for PPC's accelerated RES build-out phase [#A] ❌

  • Sub: Capex guidance for FY2025 is €1.7-2.0bn — accelerating further; peak capex is not yet reached

  • Sub: Approximately 60-65% of capex is growth (RES, new generation), 35-40% maintenance; growth capex should generate incremental EBITDA in 12-24 months post-commissioning

  • Free cash flow: FCF = OCF (~€1.55bn) minus capex (~€1.6bn) = approximately breakeven to slightly negative (~-€50-100m) in FY2024 ❌

  • Sub: This is structurally characteristic of a utility in peak investment phase; it is not a permanent condition

  • Sub: FCF is expected to turn positive (~€300-500m) in FY2026-27 as capex plateaus and commissioned assets generate incremental EBITDA

  • Net debt: ~€4.2bn at FY2024 year-end, implying net debt/EBITDA ~2.3x; management targets ~2.0x by FY2026 through EBITDA growth rather than debt repayment [#J]

  • Sub: Debt maturity profile is manageable — PPC refinanced a significant portion of debt at fixed rates in 2022-23; refinancing risk is limited through 2027

  • Sub: Greek sovereign credit rating (BBB- / Baa3 investment grade) constrains PPC's cost of debt but also limits upside from credit spread tightening

  • Dividend coverage: PPC reinstated dividends in FY2023 after years of suspension; FY2024 dividend of ~€0.30-0.35/share implies a ~2-3% yield and a ~30% payout ratio; coverage ratio of ~2.5-3x EBITDA is comfortable [#A]

  • Sub: Dividend is growing from a low base; management has signalled intention to grow dividend in line with earnings

  • Sub: No buyback programme — all capital return is via dividend; PPC does not use equity management as an EPS lever

  • Capital return sustainability: Dividend is sustainable and growing; FCF neutrality in FY2024 means dividend is funded from operating cash flow with minimal stress; however, the absence of FCF surplus limits capital return acceleration [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The FCF picture in FY2024 is approximately breakeven — this is not a cash-generative business in the current capex phase; the bear mechanism is that if capex overruns (Greek construction cost inflation, permitting delays for RES projects) push capex to €2.0bn+ in FY2025-26 while EBITDA growth underwhelms, net debt rises above the 2.5x comfort level, triggering either a credit rating concern or a forced slowdown in the RES programme that would undermine the forward earnings growth thesis [#J]

  • Sub: FY2025 EBITDA guidance growth is primarily driven by new RES asset commissioning expected H1 2025 (0.8-1.0GW new capacity)

  • Sub: Net profit guidance implies continued improvement in net finance costs and effective tax rate — management expects both to be modest tailwinds

§7 — Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

J-front verdict: RES build-out is on track and represents genuine strategic progress; the heritage thermal fleet rationalisation is proceeding; retail transformation is slower than plan [#J]

  • RES programme: PPC targeting 5.5GW total RES capacity by end-2025 (from ~4GW+ at FY2024); progress is on track with ~0.8-1.0GW commissioning expected in 2025; pipeline is fully permitted for the near-term additions [#J]
  • Sub: Technology mix: predominantly solar PV and wind; offshore wind projects are in development for post-2026 delivery

  • Sub: Romania/Bulgaria operations are contributing RES growth in addition to the Greek core; international RES capacity represents ~15-20% of total

  • Heritage thermal fleet: Lignite generation (historically PPC's primary asset) is in managed decline; the last lignite units are targeted for closure by end-2028 per Greece's national energy plan; no plan deviation in FY2024 [#A]

  • Sub: Lignite closure reduces carbon cost exposure and aligns with EU Green Deal requirements; this is strategically correct but involves asset write-down risk

  • Sub: Transition to gas peakers for system stability during RES intermittency periods is proceeding on schedule

  • ⚡ RARE: Grid infrastructure: PPC's subsidiary HEDNO (Greek distribution network) is at the centre of an accelerated grid modernisation investment cycle funded by EU Recovery Fund grants; this is unusual — most European utilities fund grid capex from tariff revenue alone; the €400-500m of RRF-grant-funded capex in 2024-26 represents genuine off-balance-sheet investment capacity that peers don't have [Cross-ref: §5] [#J]

  • Cost programme: Workforce rationalisation (voluntary exit schemes) has reduced headcount ~15% over 3 years; OpEx savings of ~€100-150m per year materialising as guided; execution on plan [#A]

  • Sub: Further headcount reduction is limited (union agreements constrain further cuts); the cost savings runway is partially exhausted

  • Sub: Digital transformation (smart meter rollout ~3m units by 2026) will deliver additional OpEx savings from reduced field visits and billing error correction

  • Romanian/Bulgarian operations: Acquired CEZ assets in Romania and Bulgaria (~2021-22) are being integrated; FY2024 saw the first full year of consolidated contribution; integration is broadly on track, EBITDA contribution ~€100-150m per year from international operations [#A]

  • Sub: Regulatory environments in Romania/Bulgaria are less predictable than Greece, adding execution risk

  • Sub: The acquisition was strategically sound (diversification, RES pipeline access) but added leverage at a high-rate environment

  • Retail transformation: Digital customer platform investment (~€50-80m over 2022-24) is live but conversion metrics (customer churn, digital billing adoption) are only partially disclosed; retail transformation KPIs are opaque [#A]

  • Sub: Sell-side is concerned that PPC's retail digital platform is 2-3 years behind leading European utilities (Enel's e-distribution platform, Iberdrola's digital retail)

  • Sub: Market share defence in retail is partially a function of digital experience quality — lagging digitisation is a structural risk

  • M&A: No acquisitions in FY2024; management confirmed leverage constraints prevent M&A; the Romanian/Bulgarian platform provides a de facto acquisition through which organic RES growth can occur without further balance sheet stress [#J]

  • Leadership change: CEO George Stassis has been in place since 2019; management team is stable; no material C-suite departures in FY2024; management continuity is a positive factor for execution on a multi-year transformation [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The strategic execution risk that is least visible in quarterly reporting is the RES construction pipeline quality — permitted projects in Greece and Romania have been delayed in the past due to grid connection bottlenecks; if HEDNO grid capacity constraints (separate from PPC's generation business) cause connection delays for new RES plants, commissioning slips 6-12 months and FY2025 EBITDA misses; the structural irony is that PPC owns both the generation (PPC SA) and the grid (HEDNO) — the two businesses can internally conflict on connection prioritisation [#J]
