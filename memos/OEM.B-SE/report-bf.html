Section 1 – Business Model + Organisation 21 findings

Section summary
  1. strong: robust, diversified distribution model with structural tailwinds, high ROIC, and an aggressive M&A pivot under EQT ownership.
1.
Description – core business: OEM functions as a technical trading intermediary, connecting specialised component manufacturers with industrial end-users across Europe
2.
The group distributes an extensive catalogue of more than 60,000 products to a highly fragmented base of 30,000 customers. The business partners with over 400 leading manufacturers, essentially functioning as their outsourced local sales and marketing arm. The portfolio spans electrical components, flow technology, motors, transmissions, brakes, bearing solutions, and appliance components. Revenue mix – distribution-heavy: The product mix is heavily tilted toward third-party components rather than in-house manufacturing, distinguishing it from pure industrial producers
3.
Standard trading and distribution products constitute the vast majority, generating approximately 70-80% of total group sales. Modified products and bespoke semi-assemblies account for an additional 10-15% of revenues, adding sticky value-add services. Proprietary, OEM-owned products represent the remaining 10-15% of the sales mix, a segment targeted for future expansion. Geography – Nordic-centric: Operations span 15 to 16 countries globally, but revenue remains heavily concentrated within the domestic Nordic footprint
4.
independent, locally managed subsidiaries across its geographic footprint
5.
Newly acquired specialist firms retain their legacy names and brands to preserve local market equity and customer trust. Despite decentralised sales teams, subsidiaries share back-office IT platforms and central warehousing to extract operational synergies. End market – industrials: Demand is intrinsically tied to industrial production cycles, though the specific verticals served are exceptionally diverse
6.
General industrial manufacturing comprises the largest customer segment, accounting for approximately 41% of group sales. Building installation contractors and electronics manufacturers represent roughly 11% and 9% of revenues, respectively. Other key end-markets include mining, energy, healthcare, automotive, food processing, and data centre construction. Cyclicality – moderate-to-late: The industrial exposure creates inherent cyclicality, though diverse end- markets dampen extreme volatility compared to single-sector peers
7.
Operations are highly sensitive to European industrial production volumes, fluctuating exchange rates, tariffs, and interest rate environments. DNB Carnegie notes OEM's organic growth consistently outpaces nominal industrial production by approximately 1.5% annually. Customer behaviour – highly fragmented: No single customer dictates terms to the group, resulting in an exceptionally balanced revenue profile
8.
The single largest customer accounts for merely 4% to 5% of total group sales. The top ten customers combined constitute only 22% of group revenues, severely limiting catastrophic churn risks. Customers regularly consolidate their purchasing with OEM to simplify procurement, treating OEM as a critical supply chain partner. Supplier dependency – well-diversified: Supplier concentration is remarkably low, mitigating supply chain disruption risks and preventing supplier monopolisation
9.
Value proposition – market access: OEM provides suppliers with instant, scalable access to deeply fragmented European industrial markets
10.
Suppliers gain a ready-made local sales force without the prohibitive capital expense of establishing direct distribution networks. OEM effectively functions as the outsourced, extended marketing arm for component manufacturers seeking entry into the Nordics. Scale economy – logistics-driven: Scale advantages manifest primarily in warehousing, inventory management, and distribution efficiency
11.
The group operates 15 proprietary logistics units, enabling high delivery precision and rigid cost control across regions. Redeye research identifies this as a "scale economies shared" model that structurally lowers logistics costs for both suppliers and customers. Operating leverage – moderate: Margin expansion has historically tracked revenue scale, though the distribution model requires variable headcount investments
12.
The Swedish region enjoys the highest structural margins due to its dominant market share and absolute regional scale. Structural driver – automation: Secular macro tailwinds support long-term volume growth consistently above baseline European GDP
13.

Structural driver – nearshoring: The reversal of globalisation and the relocation of manufacturing supply chains directly benefits OEM's geographic footprint
14.
Predictability – moderately high: While short order books limit visibility, recurring and sticky customer purchasing patterns ensure revenue stability
15.
Order backlogs typically hover around 18% of trailing twelve-month turnover, providing short-term downside protection. Deep integration into customer product development cycles ensures high retention and predictable, recurring component reorders. Internal change – ownership shift: EQT acquired a majority principal stake in OEM International in March 2025, fundamentally altering corporate governance
16.
The new board possesses a clear mandate to accelerate growth and aggressively scale the pace of corporate acquisitions. Strategic objective – M&A acceleration: The corporate strategy has decisively shifted from organic-first cash- piling to an acquisition-heavy compounder model

The group now aims to acquire product-owning companies and technical traders to build a second formidable growth engine. Leadership change – CEO retirement: A significant, potentially disruptive management transition is imminent for the highly centralised leadership structure

Recent delivery – strong execution: Financial performance has remained resilient despite undeniably weak macroeconomic conditions across Europe
19.
M&A delivery – rapidly ramping: The newly established EQT acquisition mandate is already yielding tangible, high-volume deal flow
20.
OEM completed six acquisitions in early 2026, significantly up from previous years where M&A was sparse. Recent targets include Vierpool, Multicomponent Norden, Motion29, Cre8 System, and Weingrill, expanding both geography and product depth. Sceptical counter-read – cultural erosion: What is the bearish counter-read on OEM's business model?
