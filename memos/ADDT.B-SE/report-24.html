§4 — Synthesis (~350 words | judgement)

Most likely explanation — two overlapping causes. The 6-week period almost certainly contains two distinct events: a sector-level selloff in early April driven by US tariff escalation, and a company-specific reaction to Addtech's Q4 FY2026 earnings report (Addtech's fiscal year ends 31-March, so Q4 FY26 results were due late April or early May 2026).

The early-April global risk-off hit every Nordic industrial compounder. Addtech's exposure to automation, power, and industrial distribution makes it highly cyclical-adjacent, and the market re-priced the organic growth runway on tariff-driven demand uncertainty. This was not Addtech-specific — it was a broad multiple-compression event across the peer group.

The sharper question is whether the results print, when it came, delivered a second leg down or a partial recovery. Addtech has historically compounded earnings through small bolt-on acquisitions and robust organic growth (mid-single to high-single digit). The consensus expectation coming into Q4 FY26 would have been for continued organic growth, EBITA margin resilience above 14%, and a constructive M&A pipeline update. If Addtech delivered any of these below the published consensus — or if organic growth turned negative, or if the company flagged a cautious start to FY2027 — the stock would have re-priced again in May on company-specific news.

Counter-hypothesis. The leading explanation is macro-then-results. The strongest alternative is that the April move was entirely macro, the results were broadly in line, and the May partial recovery reflects the market re-engaging with Addtech's compounding narrative. If that is the case, the stock has simply moved with the sector, and there is no company-specific derating to worry about. The one piece of evidence that would flip the call: if Addtech's Q4 FY26 organic growth came in above the peer-group average and EBITA margins held, the entire move is sector/market, not company-specific.

What is still unexplained. The exact magnitude of each leg is not confirmed from data files (see freshness caveat below). Any broker action — downgrade from a major Nordic house — could have added 2-3% to the move.

Decision prompt. This is worth more work. The highest-value next step is running Q5 (earnings delivery analysis) on the Q4 FY26 print against the sell-side consensus, specifically checking organic growth rate vs expectation and the FY2027 guidance wording. If organic growth has decelerated below 5% against a consensus of 7-8%, that is a meaningful thesis-level development for a compounder.
