§4 — TRANSACTION EXPOSURE

J Transaction exposure is a real but secondary risk for Elekta: the primary mechanism is translation. Transaction exposure arises mainly from USD-invoiced contracts outside the US, and from GBP/EUR manufacturing costs incurred against multi-currency revenues.

  • I Elekta sells capital equipment (linacs, MRI-linacs) on long contract cycles — typically 12-36 months from order to revenue recognition. This creates transaction exposure when a contract is priced in one currency and costs incurred in another.

  • A Key transaction exposure pairs:

  • USD revenues vs. GBP/SEK costs: US/dollar-area orders are priced in USD but manufactured in Crawley (GBP). A USD weakening between contract signature and delivery erodes margin in SEK terms.
  • EUR revenues vs. SEK/GBP costs: European orders (EUR) partially match EUR cost base (Netherlands manufacturing), creating partial natural hedge.
  • CNY revenues vs. CNY costs: China local operations largely match — natural hedge for China.
  • USD revenues from Middle East / EM markets vs. SEK/GBP costs: Pure transaction exposure with no natural hedge.

  • I Natural hedges exist but are incomplete:

  • EUR: Revenue ~27% of group, cost ~18-22% of cost base → partial natural hedge, net EUR long (receive more EUR than pay).
  • CNY: Revenue ~15%, Beijing manufacturing costs ~5-7% → partial natural hedge, but a net long CNY position remains.
  • GBP: Revenue ~3%, cost ~18-22% → structurally short GBP (pay significantly more GBP than receive). This is Elekta's sharpest transaction mismatch.
  • SEK: Revenue ~1%, cost ~25-30% → structurally short SEK (pays most costs in SEK, earns almost no SEK revenue). This is the core structural issue — Elekta is a SEK-reporting company with a SEK cost base but almost no SEK revenue.

  • A Residual transaction exposure after natural hedging is dominated by:

    1. GBP (long GBP costs, short GBP revenue) — manufacturing mismatch
    2. USD (long USD revenues outside US from EM markets, short USD costs) — partially positive
    3. SEK (long SEK costs, almost no SEK revenue) — this is the core structural FX issue

  • I The long multi-year contract horizon (order to delivery 12-36 months) means transaction exposure is difficult to hedge completely at contract initiation — Elekta must either price in FX risk or absorb margin variability.

  • I Management commentary (Q3 FY2026) explicitly cited "dollar depreciation versus our main cost currencies in euro and pound" as the mechanism — confirming the GBP and EUR cost base dynamic.

  • Sceptic: Exact transaction hedge ratios and notional amounts are not disclosed. The degree to which pricing decisions incorporate forward FX is unclear — medical equipment contracts in public tenders may have limited FX repricing flexibility.
