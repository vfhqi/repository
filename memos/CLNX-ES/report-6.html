§6 — Margin + Profitability Outlook

  • Consensus signal: EBITDA margin expansion broadly expected — from ~62–64% currently toward ~65–68% by FY27; operating leverage story intact [#J]

  • Consensus EBITDA margin — FY25: ~62–64%; steady vs FY24 [#A]

  • Consensus EBITDA margin — FY26: ~64–66%; ~100–200bps expansion assumed [#A]

  • Consensus net margin: Negative to near-zero on IFRS basis (large D&A, interest expense); analytically irrelevant; margin analysis should focus on EBITDA and recurring FCF margin [#J]

  • Margin trend assumed: Gradual expansion — consensus assumes fixed cost absorption as tenancy ratios rise; ground lease costs (largest expense) broadly fixed per tower; incremental tenant adds are high-margin [#A]

  • Operating leverage: The core bull thesis — each incremental MNO tenancy on an existing tower adds ~€15–20k revenue with minimal incremental cost; consensus models this lifting EBITDA margin ~30–50bps per 0.1x tenancy ratio improvement [#J]

  • Fixed cost declining as % of revenue: Ground leases ~30–35% of revenue; declining as % on revenue growth; energy costs manageable post-2022 spike; [AS-Societe Generale] and [AS-Morgan Stanley] both model this explicitly [#A]

  • Productivity assumption: No major cost-reduction programme assumed in consensus; margin expansion is operating-leverage-driven not cost-cut-driven [#A]

  • One-off vs structural: Margin improvement modelled as structural (leverage-driven) not one-off; risk is if tenancy gains disappoint, operating leverage does not materialise [#J]

  • Margin downgrade L12M: Modest — ~50–100bps EBITDA margin cuts in FY25 estimates over L12M; driven by energy cost re-assessment and slower tenancy uptick; not severe [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Ground lease renegotiations represent a margin risk underweighted by consensus — in markets where landowners have pricing power (UK rural, French TDF legacy sites), lease re-pricing on renewal can compress margins by 100–200bps at site level; [AS-Berenberg] flags this; most bull-case models assume flat or declining ground lease costs [#J]

  • Bull case: Tower infrastructure is a regulated-utility-like asset; long-term anchor leases with CPI escalators; 5G densification creates secular demand tailwind; CLNX trades at a discount to global peers because leverage is elevated, not because the asset is impaired; as net debt/EBITDA falls from ~6.5x toward ~5.0x by FY27, equity re-rates from 12x to 14–15x EV/EBITDA [#J]

  • Sub: Most explicit bear articulation: [AS-Berenberg], [AS-Redburn Atlantic] — both argue the discount is appropriate not an opportunity [#A]

  • Bullish theme (1): Deleveraging visibility — Q1/Q2 2025 results confirming FCF trajectory; asset disposals (Cellnex Ireland sold; Cellnex Denmark/Sweden portfolio rationalisation) accelerating deleveraging; analysts increasingly see 5.0x leverage by FY27 as achievable [#J]
