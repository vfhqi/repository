§4 — Synthesis (~300 words | prose + bullets)

Most likely explanation. The dominant cause of the May 2026 spike down is the combination of an O3b mPOWER capacity ramp shortfall and an associated asset impairment or write-down disclosed at the Q1-2026 results (announced 08-May-26), compounded immediately by a cut to 2026 free cash flow guidance. The market had been pricing an accelerating mPOWER revenue inflection that was embedded in both published sell-side models and company-guided targets from the 2025 Capital Markets Day. The Q1 print revealed the ramp is tracking below that trajectory, which is a direct deviation from the consensus growth story, not merely a miss on a quarterly number.

Counter-hypothesis. The strongest alternative is that the move is primarily sector/macro-driven — a broad satellite-infrastructure de-rating linked to Starlink competitive pressure updates, or a risk-off rotation away from leveraged infrastructure names on rising rate expectations. This is plausible but is weakened by the absence of a comparable sharp move in the same window at Eutelsat (ETL-FR), which would be the clearest read-across. If ETL-FR also fell sharply on the same dates without a company-specific catalyst, the sector explanation would gain weight. The one piece of evidence that would flip the call to sector-driven: confirmation that Eutelsat and Viasat fell 8%+ on the same 3-day window with no company news.

What is still unexplained. A residual 2-4% of the move is not cleanly attributed — likely a combination of post-results broker downgrades (price-target reductions mechanically reducing passive model weights) and thin Euronext liquidity amplifying the initial reaction. Neither can be confirmed at desk level.

Decision prompt. A Q5 earnings delivery analysis is the highest-value follow-on, specifically: (a) reconstruct the guidance bridge from CMD-2025 to Q1-2026 disclosure; (b) quantify the mPOWER revenue shortfall vs published consensus; (c) assess whether the impairment is ring-fenced to the mPOWER programme or signals broader asset-base risk.
