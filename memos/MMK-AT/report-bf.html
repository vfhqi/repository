EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 73 findings

Mayr-Melnhof Karton occupies a vertically integrated leadership position in European consumer packaging, spanning upstream cartonboard production through downstream specialized folding carton conversion. The Group operates six mills with production of 3.1 million tonnes annually and 53 packaging plants strategically positioned near major consumer hubs. The 2024 structural split of MM Packaging successfully isolated the higher-margin pharma business from standard food segments, improving operational transparency. Geographic revenue remains concentrated with 78% in Europe and 11% in the Americas , while secular plastic substitution trends drive solid volume growth despite short-term retail destocking pressures. Substantial scale economies in Board & Paper are supported by high capacity utilization, though operating leverage remains exposed to raw material volatility and persistent European overcapacity.

Section 3 – Industry Structure + Concentration + Value Chain Economics 30 findings

Section summary
  1. European cartonboard and packaging markets face structural oversupply with operating rates depressed in the low 80s, requiring major capacity closures to balance the market
  2. Market concentration is highly fragmented in recycled containerboard and consumer folding cartons, but highly consolidated in specialized virgin-fiber and niche sack kraft segments
  3. Competitors show divergent attitudes with larger players pushing price-over-volume while graphic paper conversions threaten to trigger further local oversupply
  4. Tightening environmental regulations and plastic substitution trends provide long-term support, though near-term margins are severely squeezed by high wood and carbon costs

European Packaging Market Scale and Growth Trajectory

1.
Muted consumption scale: Western European recycled containerboard demand reached approximately 20 million tonnes in FY2025, failing to reclaim peak historical levels
2.
Depressed operating rates: European containerboard and consumer folding carton mills are operating at depressed rates in the low 80s, compared to North American operating rates near 95%
3.
Oversupply closure requirement: The European recycled containerboard industry remains structurally oversupplied and requires at least 2 million tonnes of capacity closures to lift utilization rates back to the 90% balanced benchmark
4.
Grade volume divergence: While Western European publication and magazine paper demand declined by 9% in FY2025, packaging grades saw modest volume growth but suffered from severe margin compression
5.
Long-term volume opportunity: Sustainable food and beverage fiber-based packaging solutions are projected to expand global annual demand by 12 million tonnes over the next five years

Supportive niche dynamics: The specialized European sack kraft paper market of 1.7 million tonnes maintained resilient operating rates of 87% in FY2025, avoiding the extreme overcapacity seen in containerboard

High entry barriers: Strong structural moats defend virgin packaging and sack paper stages, requiring more than 95% of producers to be vertically integrated into pulp for cost survival
