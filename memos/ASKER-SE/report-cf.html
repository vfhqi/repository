EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Activist presence is notably absent, with no active activist campaigns recorded in the coverage universe. Interogo Long-Term Equity's 40.21% holding represents a stable principal shareholder aligned with long-term value creation, transitioning from sister company Nalka Invest to hold mature, listed European compounders. Board integration tightened through Karl Petersson's December 2025 appointment and the issuance of 291,970 call options to incoming Chair Mikael Stöhr with a five-year term and SEK 94.90 strike price representing a 120% premium, carrying no dilution risk for minority shareholders since Interogo issued them from its own holding.

Strategic M&A execution remains disciplined, with the company completing 14 margin-accretive acquisitions during 2025 including Van Heek Medical adding SEK 350m in annual sales, German Healthcare/MPF securing SEK 300m, and RMS Medical Devices contributing SEK 170m. However, management mutually discontinued the SEK 200m Danish Kirstine Hardam transaction in June 2026 after regulatory approval delays, signaling that highly protected markets may present consolidation hurdles even as capital and focus are redirected to other attractive European targets.

External value-chain forces remain broadly constructive, with structural demographic shifts providing highly predictable underlying demand that is insulated from broader macroeconomic cycles as the European cohort aged 74+ is projected to rise 9 percentage points between 2023 and 2027. Proprietary digital health solutions like automated wound care software reduce hospital treatments by 67% and costs by 80%, building switching barriers for institutional buyers. Tightening MDR, IVDR, and SBTi regulatory standards requiring a 42% absolute reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions advantage scaled operators over smaller regional competitors.

Near-term operational and macro headwinds include the transition to a new regional distribution center in Sweden that will temporarily inflate double manning costs and inventory holdings during late 2026, with full P&L benefits not expected until H2 2027. Currency translation volatility imposed a negative 4% sales impact in Q1 2026 from weaker EUR and USD against SEK, while floating interest exposure on variable-rate bank loans means a 1 percentage point interest increase reduces financial items by SEK 45.8m, though these pressures are cushioned by non-cyclical end-markets.
