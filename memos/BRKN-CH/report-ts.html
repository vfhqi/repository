Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Burkhalter is a predictable Swiss market leader capitalizing on long-term energy transition mandates and a highly successful regional acquisition flywheel.
1.
Plain business description: Swiss ancillary construction trade – mechanical, electrical, and HVACP installations and maintenance. Highly diversified local residential and commercial contracts
2.
Compelling investment case: Potential rests on ability to cross-sell technical services across unified network, amplified by self-funded regional M&A rollover
3.
Key earnings swing factor: Execution velocity of heating replacements and solar integrations driven by Swiss Energy Strategy 2050 federal decarbonization mandates
4.
Consistent financial outperformance: FY2025 EPS +7.23% YoY to CHF 5.78; 2024 EPS CHF 5.39
5.
Transformational integration: Poenina Holding AG (2022 merger) created leading full-service building technology provider with cross-selling synergies
6.
Major operational risk: Severe and persistent shortage of technical skilled labor in Switzerland limits ability to accept new high-margin renovation contracts
7.
Positive first-look verdict: Deeper review warranted – strong domestic moat, defensive pricing power, net cash balance sheet supporting high dividend yields

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Burkhalter dominates Swiss building services via decentralized, multi-trade model driving strong localized pricing power and high customer retention.

Core Service Offerings

1.
Comprehensive technical capabilities: Heating, ventilation, air conditioning, refrigeration, plumbing, and electrical installations. Transformed from pure-play electrical firm via 2022 Poenina merger – now delivers fully integrated building technology from single source
2.
Revenue model: Contract execution on percentage-of-completion method; balanced mix of small-scale local service projects and complex multi-trade renovations; no material customer concentration
3.
Regional footprint: 169 locations in Switzerland and Liechtenstein; serves general contractors, local SMEs, public institutions, private property owners
4.
Favourable value chain: Ancillary building trade is structurally profit-favoured; 90% materials sourced from domestic Swiss wholesalers; mitigates supply disruption
5.
Swiss Projects department: Centralized unit coordinates complex multi-location corporate client installations – accelerates execution and improves delivery quality

Moats, Fragmentation, Competitor Dynamics

6.
Durable moats: Significant customer switching costs and high technical complexity – clients face high coordination friction with multiple sub-contractors; Burkhalter's integrated model cements local moat
7.
Scale procurement advantages: >100,000 distinct components annually; strategic framework agreements with ~100 top-selling Swiss wholesalers covering 80% of materials
8.
Highly fragmented industry: ~3,500 local competitors in Swiss building technology – provides multi-decade M&A consolidation runway; shields from nationwide price competition
9.
No new-build exposure: Zero exposure to depressed residential new-build markets (vs Nordic peer Bravida); >40% of Swiss energy consumption tied to buildings driving massive renovation pipeline
10.
Apprenticeship program: Burkhalter Education brand; >1,000 apprentices employed across group – vital internal talent pipeline to combat labor shortages
11.
Other side: Elevated tail-risk of poor localized project execution and cost overruns across 83 highly autonomous group companies

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Investment case compelling and highly visible, driven by legal transition mandates and successful domestic acquisition flywheel.
1.
Swiss Energy Strategy 2050: Most powerful long-term driver – targets massive reduction in national energy consumption, accelerating replacement of fossil boilers with heat pumps; directly benefits HVACP segment
2.
Residential solar and EV charging: Rapidly growing demand for domestic PV systems and EV charging stations; Burkhalter uniquely positioned to cross-sell complex interdisciplinary systems
3.
Renovation tax window: Swiss voters approved second home tax reforms (Eigenmietwert) on Sep 28, 2025 – homeowners accelerating renovations ahead of 2028 tax deduction phase-out
4.
Proven M&A flywheel: 43 acquisitions since 2008 IPO with reliable integration schedules; integrates acquired firms within months
5.
Non-interdependent drivers: Growth drivers highly independent – public sector building programs continue even if residential tax reforms slow
6.
Other side: Over-expansion into low-margin infrastructure engineering via acquisitions like anplaq ag (water/pipeline infrastructure) could dilute group EBIT margins

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Risk profile moderate but highly credible, driven by Swiss technical skilled labor constraints and climate-enforced construction site delays.
1.
Chronic labor shortage: Severe shortage of qualified technical electricians and HVAC specialists – competitors bid up salaries; restricts capacity for large renovation projects
2.
Weather stoppages: Swiss cantons enforce construction bans during extreme summer heatwaves (2025: Ticino, Valais, Geneva stoppages) – unpredictable scheduling friction
3.
Raw material exposure: Volatile copper, steel, plastic prices; 90% from domestic wholesalers under price-offsetting contracts; strategic inventory buffers maintained
4.
False friend margin signals: Single half-year margin expansion can mask delivery delays or seasonal timing issues – monitor multi-year trailing trends
5.
Early-warning indicators: 6-month forward order book and apprentice headcount – decline signals construction slowdown or worsening labor bottleneck
6.
Other side: Significant regional pricing power to pass through wage and material inflation; physical building technology installations inherently non-disruptable by global digital forces
