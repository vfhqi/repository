Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Daimler Truck presents a strong cyclical recovery play with massive self-help margin opportunities in Europe, but near-term earnings are heavily constrained by US tariff complications on Mexican imports.
1.
Investment thesis framework: The core thesis centers on a substantial re-rating and earnings acceleration over the next 18 months. This trajectory is supported by a significant US fleet replacement cycle and structural cost-down programs in Europe, which will allow the company to expand margins and narrow the valuation gap with its peers
2.
Key earnings swing-factor: The single most critical driver of forward earnings is the effective tariff rate on Mexican vehicle exports to the United States. Because Mexico historically accounts for 50% to 60% of Daimler's North American truck volumes, the implementation of Section 232 tariffs has presented a severe cost headwind
3.
Order momentum inflection: A massive Q1 2026 order intake surge of 50% year over year to 114,043 units indicates a rapid cyclical recovery. This order acceleration was driven primarily by an 86% order increase in North America, highlighting strong fleet replacement demand
4.
Primary downside risk: The most severe near-term risk is the permanent margin impairment from 25% Section 232 tariffs on Mexican production if mitigation negotiations with the US government fail. This failure mode would prevent Daimler from achieving its mid-term North American profitability targets
5.
First-look investment verdict: A deeper look is highly warranted because the shares trade at an unjustified 25% discount to peer Volvo, offering a compelling self-help margin recovery story. This discount does not reflect the significant structural improvements from the divestment of low-margin Asian operations

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The business model couples dominant market-share leadership in the highly profitable North American market with a restructuring-led European business and a recurring services profit pool.
1.
North American business: Trucks North America is the jewel of the group, selling heavy-duty and vocational trucks under the Freightliner and Western Star brands. The segment is highly profitable and captured a dominant 41% heavy-duty market share in 2025, supported by the launch of the Fifth Generation Freightliner Cascadia
2.
European Mercedes-Benz brand: Mercedes-Benz Trucks serves European and South American markets with a comprehensive lineup of diesel and alternative-powertrain trucks. The segment has historically lagged behind Scania and Volvo in profitability and is currently undergoing a massive structural restructuring through its Cost Down Europe program
3.
Deconsolidated Trucks Asia: The group completed the active deconsolidation of its former Trucks Asia segment on April 1, 2026, merging Mitsubishi Fuso with Toyota's Hino Motors to form the newly listed ARCHION venture. This strategic transaction removes a structurally lower-margin business from Daimler's consolidated reporting and unlocks technology synergies in commercial fuel cells
4.
Daimler Buses segment: Daimler Buses is the undisputed market leader in European coaches and zero-emission city buses, achieving a record double-digit adjusted return on sales of 10% in 2025. The segment operates a dedicated subsidiary, Daimler Buses Solutions GmbH, to design and establish EV and hydrogen charging infrastructure for fleet operators
5.
Financial Services division: Daimler Truck Financial Services supports vehicle sales across 21 markets with customized leasing, financing, insurance, and charging infrastructure packages. The division's contract volume reached €31.2bn in Q1 2026, though rising credit loss allowances from macroeconomic uncertainties have pressured near-term profitability
6.
Global platform commonality: Daimler's core competitive moat is driven by maximum commonality of platforms, engines, and software-defined vehicle architectures across its global brands. This strategy allows the company to spread massive technology investments over high production volumes, out-scaling smaller competitors
7.
Zero-emission transition leadership: Daimler commands a 35% battery-electric medium and heavy-duty truck market share in Europe, positioning the Mercedes-Benz brand as the clear frontrunner in clean commercial transport. The long-haul Mercedes-Benz eActros 600 has received strong customer traction, helping secure early fleet electrification commitments
8.
Vocational market expansion: Growing the vocational Western Star brand in North America represents a key incremental profit opportunity. The vocational segment features higher ASPs and more robust aftermarket sales, allowing Daimler to capture share from Paccar in a less volatile market segment
9.
Highly consolidated industry: The heavy-duty commercial vehicle market is structurally highly consolidated, with the top four global OEMs historically controlling over 60% of global volumes. This structure supports disciplined pricing and provides significant barriers to entry for new competitors
10.
Other side of the story: Slower customer adoption of electric trucks due to inadequate highway charging infrastructure forces Daimler to maintain expensive parallel development programs for diesel, battery, and hydrogen powertrains. This regulatory mismatch risks diluting capital returns if sales volumes remain low

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is highly compelling on a 18-month horizon as significant cyclical replacement demand in the US pairs with structural European cost-down programs.
1.
US replacement cycle: Strong US order momentum, which grew 86% YoY to 59,195 units in Q1 2026, provides high visibility for sequential and second-half volume recovery. This recovery is supported by capacity exiting the market and rising carrier spot freight rates
2.
Cost Down Europe savings: The execution of the Cost Down Europe program targets over €1bn in structural, annual EBIT savings by 2030. The program is on track, with €250m in cumulative savings targeted by the end of 2026 through headcount reductions and relocation of Atego cabin production
3.
ARCHION structural benefits: The deconsolidation of Trucks Asia into the newly listed ARCHION joint venture removes a structurally lower-margin segment from Daimler's financial statements. The transaction is expected to generate a total cash inflow between €1.5bn and €2.0bn from partner Toyota, supporting balance-sheet liquidity
4.
Global defense scaling: Daimler plans to scale its global defense business, targeting €1.0bn in defense-related revenue by 2028. Growth is backed by a massive framework agreement with the French Armed Forces for 7,000 trucks on the Zetros platform and 1,500 logistics trucks for Canada
5.
High North American plausibility: Improving carrier freight rates and the need to replace aging fleets support a high probability of Class 8 volume recovery. However, the driver would fail if US economic growth stalls, forcing fleets to delay replacement cycles and run vehicles longer
6.
Medium European turnaround plausibility: Headcount reduction agreements are finalized, but success depends on managing labor friction and the complex operational ramp-up of the Czech plant. Failure to optimize logistics networks would prevent Mercedes-Benz from narrowing its margin gap with peers
7.
High portfolio cleanup plausibility: The ARCHION transaction closed successfully on April 1, 2026, removing lower-margin commercial activities from the group's consolidation. Failure to realize procurement synergies with Toyota would limit the long-term technological benefit of the fuel cell partnership
8.
Independent driver execution: These earnings drivers are highly independent, meaning the US cyclical recovery and ARCHION cash inflow can deliver the targeted 25%+ IRR even if European cost-cutting measures experience delays
9.
Other side of the story: Aggressive discount pricing offered to megafleets in the US to gain market share could neutralize any volume-driven margin expansion as competitive intensity rises

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by temporary geopolitical and tariff headwinds in North America, but these are increasingly mitigated by proactive supply chain management.
1.
Mexican tariff exposure: Since November 1, 2025, exports from Mexico have been subject to 25% Section 232 tariffs. Because Mexico accounts for ~57% of DTNA's production, this creates direct cost headwinds, presenting a low three-digit million euro headwind in Q1 2026
2.
No reshoring mitigation: Q1 2026 data shows Freightliner is not reshoring production from Mexico to US plants due to significant cost advantages of Mexican operations, increasing tariff risk. Shifted production would carry significant labor and manufacturing inefficiencies
3.
Geopolitical supply chain risks: Escalating tensions in the Middle East present material risks of supply chain disruption and energy cost inflation. These risks are not currently factored into management's guidance and could increase manufacturing cost bases if left unmitigated
4.
Supplier credit risk: Financial bottlenecks at highly strained suppliers could trigger sudden production interruptions, delaying vehicle delivery and inflating cost of sales. This risk is addressed through proactive supplier monitoring and stockpiling critical components
5.
Delayed electric commercial vehicle transition: Slower-than-expected electric truck transition in the US forced a €200m non-cash impairment in Q1 2026 from the Amplify cell joint venture. This slower transition delays the cost-reduction benefits from manufacturing scale
6.
Operational early warning signal: Slower order velocity and rising dealer inventory levels in late summer represent the most critical operational indicators of cyclical cooling in North America. Investors should monitor these metrics to assess whether the US cycle has peaked
7.
Other side of the story: Concerns regarding US tariffs are highly overstated because Daimler is actively negotiating for preferential tariff treatment through increased US content, which could eliminate most of the tariff cost in H2 2026

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Daimler Truck's leadership is undergoing a profound performance-driven generational transition that aligns incentives directly with margin expansion goals.
1.
Profitability-focused CEO appointment: Karin Rådström, ex-Scania executive, assumed the CEO role, bringing Scania's industry-benchmark profitability mindset to accelerate the Mercedes-Benz Trucks turnaround. This change signals a shift from volume maximization to high-margin, value-based sales execution
2.
Leadership team contract extensions: The Supervisory Board extended the mandates of CFO Eva Scherer (until 2032), Achim Puchert (until 2031), and John O'Leary (until 2028), ensuring deep leadership continuity. These extensions provide the executive team with the long-term horizon required to execute structural cost transformations
3.
New human resources chief: Yvonne Bettkober, ex-Audi Chief Transformation Officer, joins the board on October 1, 2026, bringing extensive performance management experience to oversee the Germany headcount cuts. This appointment will strengthen execution discipline for the Cost Down Europe program
4.
Independent governance upgrade: Kurt Sievers was appointed as the Lead Independent Director in May 2026, creating an additional independent point of contact for institutional investors. This role strengthens corporate governance and reinforces independent supervisory board oversight

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Daimler Truck warrants a strong positive investment decision as deep restructuring and a US cyclical upswing point to clear EPS outperformance.
1.
Strongest bull argument: The combination of a massive 50% Q1 order book surge and €1bn in European cost-down savings creates a highly visible runway for earnings growth and margin expansion
2.
Other side of the story: Slower US economic growth or extended 25% Section 232 tariffs on Mexican imports without content mitigation could permanently depress North American margins and cap EPS growth
3.
First verification action: Verify the progress of US government negotiations regarding Mexico tariff credits and US content rules during the Q2 2026 earnings call. This represents the most immediate catalyst for earnings revisions
4.
Second verification action: Monitor month-by-month Mexico export data from INEGI to confirm that Freightliner production is sustaining its strong sequential volume trajectory. This tracking will provide early confirmation of second-half volume recovery
