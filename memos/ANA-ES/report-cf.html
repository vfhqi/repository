EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Acciona SA confronts a period of strategic transformation marked by structured but narrow executive succession, the expiration of a critical family stability pact introducing governance vulnerability, and rising voluntary turnover rates that signal cultural strain beneath stable engagement scores . Leadership continuity remains anchored by long-tenured CEO José Manuel Entrecanales Domecq who has served since April 1997, while key executive transitions including the appointment of Arantza Ezpeleta Puras as CAER CEO at the June 2025 AGM and the departure of Andrés Pan de Soraluce effective September 1, 2025 reshape the operational leadership bench . The looming expiration of the Entrecanales family stability pact on July 14, 2026 removes a critical anti-takeover shield and introduces meaningful strategic uncertainty . Strategic priorities have pivoted decisively from volume-driven capacity expansion toward value-focused selective growth and aggressive balance sheet deleveraging, with management systematically executing a €3.2 billion divestment program that generated €900 million in capital gains while moderating annual installation targets to 1.0 to 1.5 GW to preserve investmentgrade credit ratings. 1

Longduration concession dividend inflows projected at €60 billion over contract lifetimes present high plausibility anchored to multi-decade concession structures .
