EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Foundations Altri's core operations center on three industrial mills with combined capacity exceeding 1.1 million tonnes , including the dominant 850,000-tonne Celbi BEKP facility and specialized 115,000-tonne Caima dissolving plant . The company is executing a strategic mix shift by converting its Biotek mill to full dissolving pulp production by end-2026, establishing total dissolving capacity of 185,000 tonnes annually. Dissolving pulp historically commanded a 47% average price premium over standard hardwood pulp from 2021 to 2025. Altri Florestal

manages 102,595 hectares of certified Portuguese forests yielding 625,058 cubic meters of timber harvest annually, though this supplies only 15% to 20% of industrial wood requirements. Co-generation assets ensure mills produce sufficient electricity to meet their own operational needs, achieving fossil fuel elimination at key facilities. 5 6

and Value Chain Position The global hardwood pulp industry is consolidating toward low-cost Latin American giants, with value structurally shifting from industrial mills to forest landowners whose real assets appreciate faster than standard inflation. Global leader Suzano commands a massive 29% share of the global hardwood market while the five main European BEKP producers control 10 million tonnes of total capacity, with Navigator leading at 16% market share, Ence at 12% , and Altri at 11% . Greenfield pulp mills require staggering capital investments exceeding $1.8 billion for standard-scale facilities, effectively blocking speculative new entrants. Altri operates primarily in commodity hardwood pulp and specialty dissolving pulp stages across three highly efficient Portuguese mills, though unlike major regional peers, it remains a pure-play producer exposed to open-

market pricing volatility that amplifies EBITDA declines during deep commodity downturns. Regional competitor Navigator has high integration into premium woodfree papers and doubled downstream UK tissue revenues to £0.5 billion through the Accrol acquisition. Pure-play European pulp producers face intense pricing pressure from low-priced Asian paper imports, while escalating European timber prices directly squeeze margins of nonintegrated regional mills during peak forestry demand.
