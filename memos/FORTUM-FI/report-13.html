§1 — Current Management Guidance

Fortum's current guidance for FY25 is expressed primarily through comparable operating profit ranges and divisional EBIT corridors, supplemented by a capital markets day framework issued in November 2023 that anchors medium-term targets through FY27.

  • J-front verdict: Management has issued qualified, corridor-style guidance for FY25 — credible on EBIT direction but highly conditional on power prices; medium-term targets rest on an optimistic macro stack [#J]

  • FY current — EBIT: Group comparable EBIT guided to EUR 1.0-1.4bn for FY25 (midpoint EUR 1.2bn), conditional on Nordic system price averaging EUR 45-55/MWh across the year [#A]

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit group revenue guidance issued; management directs analysts to divisional COP rather than top-line, consistent with utility-sector norm but limiting comparability to peer guidance frameworks [#I]

  • Sub: Vattenfall and Statkraft (unlisted comparables) similarly de-emphasise top-line guidance; E.ON and RWE (listed peers) provide explicit revenue corridors — Fortum's approach is sector-middle [#A]

  • FY current — FCF: Management guided "positive free cash flow" for FY25 without a numerical floor; FCF guidance deliberately vague following the Uniper-related volatility in FY22-FY23 [#A]

  • Sub: Absence of a numerical FCF floor is a credibility gap — peer RWE provides FCF guidance to within ±€200m; Fortum's open-ended language limits accountability [#J]

  • FY current — CapEx: Total group CapEx guided to EUR 1.2-1.5bn for FY25, with ~60% allocated to generation maintenance and ~40% to growth (mainly wind/solar in Nordics) [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance; management references dividend capacity and balance sheet metrics rather than EPS trajectory — atypical vs E.ON/RWE which provide adj. EPS guidance ranges [#I]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY26): CMD framework targets group COP of EUR 1.3-1.6bn, with nuclear fleet running at 85%+ load factor as enabling assumption [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY27): CMD targets EUR 1.5-1.8bn COP; implies ~7-9% annual EBIT CAGR from FY24 actual; contingent on capacity market improvements and hedging roll-forward gains [#A]

  • Sub: CAGR of 7-9% compares to RWE's guided 8-10% adj. EBIT CAGR and E.ON's ~5%; Fortum sits in-line with upper-mid-tier European utility guidance ambition [#A]

  • Source — investor day: CMD November 2023 is primary anchor; updated at Q4 2024 results (February 2025) with no change to corridor midpoints [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1 2025 call (April 2025) reaffirmed FY25 COP range; management noted "no reason to revise" but flagged Q1 power prices slightly below full-year assumption [#A]

  • Update timing: Next guidance update expected at H1 2025 results (July 2025); management has historically narrowed corridor at H1 [#A]

  • Caveat — power price: All COP and EBIT guidance explicitly conditional on Nordic power price assumption; a EUR 5/MWh deviation from EUR 50/MWh midpoint changes group EBIT by ~EUR 80-100m [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — nuclear: Full-year guidance assumes 85% nuclear availability across Finnish and Swedish fleet; below 80% would require downward revision [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that management's COP corridor is wide enough to be un-falsifiable until H2 — a EUR 400m EBIT range (EUR 1.0-1.4bn) covers most realistic scenarios; the apparent quantification masks that the midpoint rests on power prices ~10% above current Q3-Q4 forward prices, embedding upside optionality that is not yet priced in the floor [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: Fortum is a conditional sandbagger — conservative on operating cost and EBIT corridor width, but the conservatism is a function of power price optionality, not management discipline [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — COP: Midpoint of COP guidance has been beaten in 3 of 4 periods (ex-Uniper) by an average of ~10%; corridor lower-bound has never been breached in FY2021, FY2023, FY2024 [#A]

  • Sub: Consistent 10% beat on EBIT midpoints is a moderate sandbagging signal; RWE and E.ON beat guidance midpoints with comparable frequency (~65-75%) — Fortum not unusual [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — nuclear: Management has set 85% nuclear load factor as the guidance assumption across all four periods; actual has averaged 83%; this is a persistent aspirational over-promise on a key earnings driver [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — capital deployment: CapEx and capital-return timelines have consistently slipped vs initial guidance; share buyback programmes have been announced and delayed in 2 of last 3 years [#A]

  • Honest tracker — CapEx (recent): FY2023-FY2024 CapEx landed within or near the guided range, suggesting improved capital-planning discipline post-Uniper; but the baseline period (2022) was severely disruptive [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — FCF: FCF guidance posture has shifted from "open floor" (FY2021-FY2022) to "numerical corridor" (FY2024); this represents a positive evolution in guidance discipline, though the corridor remains wide (EUR 300m range) [#J]

  • Beat consistency: COP beats are concentrated in strong Nordic power price environments; when power prices normalise toward EUR 35-40/MWh (2023 H1 trough), COP guidance accuracy deteriorates — the sandbagging signal is price-conditional, not structural [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average COP beat magnitude of ~10% (EUR 100-150m) is meaningful in absolute terms but modest relative to peer-level sandbagging at Vattenfall (~15-20% per third-party COP estimates) [#I]

  • Pattern shift: Management appears to have learned from FY2022 — guidance language in FY2023-FY2025 is more explicitly conditional and caveated than 2020-2022 vintage; this represents a constructive regime change in guidance framing [#J]

  • ⚡ RARE: Guidance language shift: Post-FY2022, management explicitly introduced power-price sensitivity tables into results presentations — a practice only ~3-4 of 15 European integrated utilities routinely provide; this transparency is a genuine positive differentiator vs peers [Cross-ref: §6 + §7]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the apparent sandbagging cushion is entirely a function of realised power prices exceeding the embedded assumption — in a secular power price deflation scenario (e.g. accelerated renewables buildout suppressing Nordic baseload), the cushion evaporates and management may have no genuine conservative buffer remaining [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • J-front verdict: Guidance accuracy is improving on a normalised basis — FY2023 and FY2024 show tighter corridor adherence and more explicit conditionality; but the improvement is partly a function of a simpler post-Uniper group structure [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY2024 COP landed within the guided corridor (EUR 1.0-1.3bn actual EUR 1.25bn); FY2024 CapEx also in-range — the cleanest L4P result for management [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY2022 was the worst accuracy year across all metrics; FY2021 was the best; both were heavily influenced by exogenous Uniper dynamics rather than core-operations execution [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — structural driver: Post-Uniper deconsolidation (completed late 2023), group structure is significantly simpler — generation + India/APAC solar, with cleaner cash flows; simpler structure mechanically improves guidance accuracy [#J]

  • Sub: Peer comparison: E.ON post-Innogy integration (2021+) similarly showed 2-year accuracy improvement following structural simplification — Fortum's improving accuracy trajectory follows the same pattern [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — process driver: Management introduced quarterly power price sensitivity disclosures (Q4 2023 onward), allowing investors to self-adjust guidance for price movements — effectively shifting accuracy burden to the market [#I]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Nordic power prices remain the dominant accuracy driver; FY2025 accuracy will be determined predominantly by H2 weather patterns and hydrology, not management execution [#J]

  • Volatility driver — nuclear: Nuclear availability variance (~±3-5pp annually) adds EUR 50-100m annual EBIT noise; this is structural, not improvable by management guidance discipline [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx guidance accuracy has improved most measurably (FY2022: −36% miss; FY2023-2024: in-range); reflects investment programme rationalisation and reduced Uniper-era emergency spending [#J]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF remains the weakest-guided metric — bi-directional swings of >EUR 1bn in FY2021-FY2022; FCF accuracy is improving but still below the standard of investable predictability for an 18M-3Y EPS CAGR thesis [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that the improvement is a simplicity dividend — Fortum has traded complexity (Uniper) for geographic concentration (Nordic power + India); if Nordic power markets reprice downward or Indian solar execution stumbles, the accuracy improvement reverses immediately and management loses the structural improvement narrative [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front verdict: The guidance assumption stack is fragile at the macro layer — two assumptions (Nordic power price + nuclear availability) jointly explain >70% of EBIT variance; both are outside management's control [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Management assumes Nordic system price of EUR 45-55/MWh for FY25; as of May 2025, Q3-Q4 forward prices average approximately EUR 40-44/MWh — guidance midpoint sits ~10% above forward curve [#A]

  • Sub: EUR 5/MWh adverse move = EUR 80-100m EBIT impact per management's own sensitivity disclosure [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Hedging programme covers approximately 60-70% of FY25 generation volume at EUR 48-52/MWh lock-in — hedged book provides partial protection but ~30-40% of volume remains spot-exposed [#A]

  • Margin assumption — nuclear: 85% load factor embedded in all forward guidance; as established in §2, actual average L4P is 83%; management has never achieved 85% across a full fiscal year in the last 4 periods [#J]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Management guidance implies no share count reduction in FY25 guidance period; SS models 2-4% share count reduction via buybacks at N2Y-N3Y, driving the EPS premium identified in §5 [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed at approximately 21-23%; Finnish windfall tax provisions create upside/downside uncertainty of ~EUR 30-50m annually [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Fuel cost (mainly uranium/enrichment for nuclear) assumed flat in real terms; uranium spot has risen ~15% YTD 2025 — partially hedged through long-term contracts but creates 2H2026+ cost pressure [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: EUR/INR assumed stable for Indian solar segment; INR has depreciated ~3% YTD — immaterial to group (~5% of EBIT) but signals emerging market execution risk [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Net debt of approximately EUR 4.5bn; floating-rate exposure ~25%; 100bps rate increase = ~EUR 11m interest cost uplift — manageable but not disclosed in management sensitivity tables [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • J-front verdict: Overall guidance credibility is MEDIUM — operational execution on core COP is credible; macro-assumption layer and capital-return timeline are not [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — management has delivered within or above COP corridor in 3 of 4 periods (ex-Uniper distortion); however, the assumptions underpinning the corridor contain embedded optimism on power prices and nuclear availability that has not been consistently achieved [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY2024 COP at EUR 1.25bn vs EUR 1.0-1.3bn guided — clean beat with no extraordinary items; post-Uniper execution is credible [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption width: Guidance corridor of EUR 400m (EUR 1.0-1.4bn) is deliberately wide; management explicitly declines to narrow until H1 results — conservative in form if not in midpoint [#J]

  • Green flag — sensitivity disclosure: Post-FY2022, management provides explicit EUR-per-MWh EBIT sensitivity tables; one of the most transparent macro-sensitivity disclosures in the European utility sector [#A]

  • Red flag — nuclear assumption: 85% load factor guidance has never been achieved in L4 periods; management has not revised this assumption downward despite persistent miss — represents aspirational rather than empirical calibration [#J]

  • Red flag — power price vs forward curve: Current guidance midpoint assumes EUR 50/MWh vs EUR 40-44/MWh forward curve for H2 2025; guidance is effectively betting on power price recovery that the market is not pricing [#J]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FY2022 COP miss was catastrophic in magnitude even on a post-hoc "continuing ops" basis; while Uniper was the proximate cause, management's failure to update or withdraw guidance intra-year as the Uniper situation deteriorated is a credibility mark against communication quality [#J]

  • Margin of safety: If FY25 EBIT lands at EUR 1.0bn (floor of guided corridor — a ~17% miss vs midpoint), DCF-based equity value falls by approximately 15-20% at a 7x EBIT multiple; the investment case is not trivially robust to a floor-landing scenario at current market price [#J]

  • Sub: A 10% adverse miss (EUR 1.1bn vs EUR 1.2bn) reduces equity value by ~8-10% — manageable at a discount [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity — EPS CAGR: Management's CMD 7-9% COP CAGR assumes power prices recover to EUR 50-55/MWh by FY27; if prices average EUR 42-45/MWh (flat from current forwards), COP CAGR compresses to ~3-5% — below the 12-20% EPS growth threshold for Richard's thesis [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on credibility is that management's MEDIUM verdict is fragile to a single assumption — Nordic power prices; if the forward curve is correct and spot prices don't recover, the "credible" EBIT track record evaporates because all three COP beats occurred in above-average power price years, leaving zero evidence of operational sandbagging in a soft price environment [#J]
