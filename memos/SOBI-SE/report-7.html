§2 — Key Driver #1: Alhemo/Fitusiran Commercial Ramp

  • Mechanism to EPS: Revenue ramp directly expands absolute EBIT given orphan-drug pricing (list price ~€400-500k/patient/year estimated); each 100-patient incremental addition approximates ~SEK 400-500m gross revenue; high gross margin (~80%+ for biologic orphan drugs) flows through to EBIT [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Visible revenue trajectory + label expansion optionality = multiple expansion from ~18x to ~22-24x forward EBIT; peer orphan-drug compounders (Recordati, Ipsen) trade at 18-22x when ramp is confirmed [#A]

  • Company guide: Management guided "significant growth" for Alhemo in 2025; no explicit patient or revenue number disclosed publicly; medium-term ambition described as blockbuster potential (>SEK 10bn peak sales across all rights globally) [#A]

  • Management commentary: CEO Guido Oelkers (Q4 2024 earnings call, public) noted Alhemo uptake "exceeding internal expectations" in both EU and US; described physician engagement as strong; noted formulary access improving quarter-on-quarter [#A]

  • Track record: Kineret launch trajectory (prior SOBI flagship) demonstrates SOBI's capability to execute rare-disease launches in EU — Kineret grew from <SEK 1bn to ~SEK 2.5bn over 5 years; Alhemo starting from similar base [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Management guidance is deliberately vague on patient numbers — typical for rare-disease launches where per-patient revenue is high and patient count is commercially sensitive; likely conservative on total patient count, optimistic on reimbursement timeline [#J]

  • SS consensus: Public sell-side estimates (Berenberg, Jefferies, Carnegie — all cited public research 2024-2025) cluster around SEK 1.5-2.5bn Alhemo revenue by 2026, with Berenberg at the upper end; no public estimate below SEK 1.2bn [#I]

  • Consensus changes L12M: Mild upward revision trend — H2 2024 launch updates prompted +5-10% Alhemo revenue estimate upgrades across visible public notes [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Delivering SEK 2.5bn Alhemo revenue vs SEK 1.5bn base (consensus low) implies ~SEK 1.5-2.0 EPS difference on a SEK 13-15 2026E EPS base — ~10-15% EPS swing purely from Alhemo trajectory [#A]

  • Recent delivery: Q4 2024 results showed Alhemo growing >50% YoY from a low base; Q1 2025 commentary (public) maintained above-consensus trajectory; no guidance reduction [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: Alhemo EU approval April 2023; US approval August 2023; still in early commercial ramp — L3Y baseline is launch period, not steady state [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Fitusiran competes with emicizumab (Hemlibra, Roche) in inhibitor patients; Hemlibra achieved $1bn+ global sales within 3 years of launch; Alhemo's TAM is initially smaller (inhibitors only) but mechanism differentiation (works in both A and B) is real [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Orphan-drug pricing resilience; EU rare-disease framework protective; US payer pushback on high-cost biologics is a headwind but inhibitor patients represent a high-cost population where switching incentives are strong [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium-high — physician panels and patient registry data give SOBI reasonable forward visibility on patient pipeline; inhibitor population is identifiable and limited (~30,000 patients globally) [#A]

  • Bear case: Inhibitor label proves too narrow; gene therapy adoption faster than expected; Hemlibra launches label extension into inhibitors more aggressively; Alhemo reimbursement delays in France/Italy/Spain (historically slow payer markets) [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Alhemo achieves SEK 1.2bn by 2026 (vs SEK 2.0bn consensus mid); EPS impact ~−8%; multiple compression to ~16x if growth narrative questioned; ~−20% SP downside from driver-specific miss [#A]

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — early launch data is ahead of expectations; risk is label width, not execution quality, over the 24M horizon

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Conservative guidance likely — SOBI has historically beaten haemostasis guidance; switching from standard factor to extended half-life is an ongoing tailwind not fully priced [#J]

  • Visibility: High — patient registries, long prophylaxis contracts, HTC (haemophilia treatment centre) relationships provide >12 months revenue visibility [#A]

  • Bear case: Emicizumab conversion rate accelerates; new gene therapy uptake draws patients who would otherwise start on Elocta; payer-driven rebating in EU erodes net pricing [#J]

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

Cross-cutting bear: The haemophilia competitive landscape is the single systemic risk — if gene therapy + competing subcutaneous agents inflect simultaneously, SOBI loses the Alhemo ramp (Driver 1), faces base haemostasis pressure (Driver 2), and margin expansion (Driver 4) stalls; three drivers fail in concert [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers 1 and 4 are positively correlated — Alhemo revenue scale is the engine of operating leverage; if Alhemo underdelivers, margin expansion is delayed not cancelled [#A]

  • Driver dependency: Driver 3 (immunology) is structurally independent of Driver 1-2 (haemostasis); Synagis/Kineret/Doptelet dynamics are uncorrelated to haemophilia competitive dynamics — this independence is a real diversification benefit [#A]

  • Correlation across drivers: Drivers 1, 2, and 5 (reimbursement) are positively correlated — a haemophilia pricing/access reversal (e.g., AMNOG rebate worsening) hits all three simultaneously; the portfolio is less diversified than it appears [#J]

  • Cumulative plausibility: Seven independent drivers each at 65-70% plausibility → joint probability all seven deliver ~0.70^7 ≈ 8%; practically, 3-4 of 7 delivering is the realistic base case; question is which 3-4 [#A]

  • Multi-driver risk: The thesis requires at minimum Drivers 1 + 2 + 4 to all deliver partial upside for consensus 2026 EPS to clear; no single-driver knockout [#J]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: Bear case = Alhemo grows to SEK 1.2bn by 2026 (vs SEK 2.0-2.5bn bull), Synagis declines 40% (Beyfortus), Italy reimbursement delayed to mid-2026; combined EBIT miss ~SEK 600-800m; EBIT margin stalls at 26-27%; SP flat-to-down 15-20% vs current [#J]
