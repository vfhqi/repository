EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Buzzi SpA operates under entrenched family control that has structurally consolidated through double-voting rights while pursuing an aggressive portfolio reshaping strategy focused exclusively on cement-only acquisitions in Brazil and the United Arab Emirates. The company demonstrates elite operating margins that consistently outperform diversified European peers, yet this profitability advantage is increasingly threatened by mounting carbon compliance capital burdens and structural exposure to regional construction cycle volatility across fragmented geographies.

Materiality of CHANGE

The full consolidation of Nacional Cimentos Participações in Brazil during October 2024 represents the most material strategic transformation, adding 7.20 million tons of cement capacity and fundamentally altering Buzzi's international production footprint. The simultaneous entrance into the Abu Dhabi market through majority ownership of Gulf Cement Company, reaching 65.70% by December 2025, further diversifies geographic revenue streams. The strategic exit from Ukrainian operations generated approximately €90 million to €100 million in cash proceeds, marking a decisive withdrawal from geopolitical risk exposure. However, governance reforms implementing double-voting rights have concentrated voting power to 204.50 million votes out of 294.87 million total votes as of June 2026, effectively insulating family control from external activist pressure. Net signal :

Material strategic repositioning executed through high-conviction M&A and governance entrenchment, creating structural barriers to capital allocation accountability while expanding emerging market exposure.
