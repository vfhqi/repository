§1 Analytical Bullets

  • L3Y revenue CAGR: +3.1% CAGR (FY2022–FY2024) on a reported basis — modest headline figure reflecting the flat FY2023 interruption after 18% growth in FY2022 [#J]
  • Sub: organic strip estimated ~0–2% CAGR excluding M&A contribution; inorganic contribution of ~3–5% per year is the growth engine, not organic demand alone

  • Sub: FY2022 represented the post-COVID construction/infrastructure spending surge peak; FY2023 flat revenue was a high-base correction, not demand destruction

  • L3Y EPS CAGR: Adjusted EPS SEK 6.09 (FY2022) → SEK 6.40 (FY2024) — cumulative +5% over two years from the FY2022 peak; measuring from FY2021 base (~SEK 4.80) gives a cleaner ~+10% CAGR [#J]

  • Sub: FY2022 was an exceptional year; using it as the L3Y anchor flatters deceleration; base-corrected view from FY2021 is the more honest trajectory

  • Trajectory: Classic "peak-trough-recovery" arc — FY2022 peak, FY2023 trough, FY2024 recovery; investor read depends critically on whether one treats FY2022 as the new normal or the cycle high [#J]

  • Acceleration: FY2024 exit rate is encouraging — Q4 2024 EBIT margin 10.4% is back above FY2022 levels; sequential quarterly EBIT improvement through FY2024 is the strongest positive signal in the dataset [#A]

  • Deceleration: FY2023 was a genuine earnings recession — FY2023 EBIT -9% YoY, EPS adjusted -11% YoY vs FY2022; in the context of a software/IT-services peer group typically growing EBIT 10–15% per year, this was a meaningful underperformance period [#J]

  • Inflection point — Q3 2023: Lowest EBIT margin in L3Y at 6.8% — triggered by DM license softness, restructuring accrual, and FX headwinds; this was the cycle trough and management commentary explicitly flagged DM recovery as the key watch item [#J]

  • Inflection point — Q4 2023: Recovery inflected here — DM backlog commentary turned positive; Q4 2023 EBIT margin 9.4% recovered from Q3's 6.8% trough; set the trajectory for FY2024's sustained recovery [#A]

  • M&A distortion flag: Serial acquirer pattern — Addnode completed 3–4 bolt-on acquisitions in L3Y (including CISAG, Komsa, smaller PLM targets); each adds 1–3% to reported revenue growth in acquisition year; organic-only trajectory is structurally harder to read [#A]

  • Sub: Management does not consistently provide organic-vs-acquired revenue split in quarterly interim reports; analyst reconstructions vary by ±1–2% organic growth assumption

  • Sceptical note — trajectory read: What might invalidate this trajectory read? The FY2024 recovery might be a second-order bounce from a structurally weakening DM licensing market as Autodesk's SaaS transition compresses reseller margins; if DM EBIT margins normalise lower, the recovery shape flatters the forward implied growth rate [#J]

§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Net clean — cash conversion is strong and consistent; the reported/adjusted gap is structural (acquired intangible amortisation) not manufactured; no evidence of aggressive NWC management or recurring items dressed as one-off [#J]

  • Recurring vs one-off: One-off items are genuinely small — M&A transaction costs ~SEK 6–12m per year, restructuring ~SEK 12m in Q3 2023 (one occurrence in L3Y); these are minor relative to SEK 500–600m annual EBIT [#A]

  • Sub: No evidence of asset disposal gains, pension income, or non-recurring fair-value uplifts inflating reported earnings in L3Y

  • Non-GAAP gap: Adjustments are dominated by amortisation of acquired intangibles — ~SEK 200–240m per year (SEK 0.45–0.65/share per quarter); this is structural given the M&A model and grows as the acquisition portfolio expands [#A]

  • Sub: Gap between reported EBIT and "EBITA" (company's preferred metric) is consistent and explainable; management does not adjust for items that should be expensed

  • Sub: Amortisation trending upward (~SEK 195m FY2022 → ~SEK 230m FY2024) as acquired asset base grows — this is expected but worth monitoring as it grows faster than revenue

  • Adjustments trend: No evidence of adjustment creep — the company's definition of EBITA has not changed in L3Y; recurring items are not being reclassified as one-off to inflate adjusted metrics [#A]

  • NWC tailwind: Working capital broadly disciplined — Addnode is a software reseller and professional services business with relatively low inventory; trade receivables / revenue ratio stable at ~22–24% across L3Y [#A]

  • Sub: No aggressive DSO compression visible that would suggest pull-forward of cash from future periods

  • NWC headwind: Q3 2023 showed modest NWC build (~SEK 45m above seasonal norm) coinciding with DM softness — likely timing of customer payments; reversed in Q4 2023 [#A]

  • Cash conversion: OCF/EBIT consistently 90–100% across L3Y — FY2022 ~94%, FY2023 ~91%, FY2024 ~96%; above median for European IT-services sector (peer base-rate ~80–85%) [#A]

  • Sub: No deterioration trend; strong cash conversion is a structural feature of the software reseller/SaaS distribution model

  • OCF vs NI: OCF consistently exceeds net income — D&A adds back meaningfully; NWC not consuming cash; Addnode's cash earnings are real [#A]

  • D&A trend: Depreciation rising moderately — driven by IFRS 16 lease additions and incremental CapEx on internal systems; not aggressive accounting, but worth watching as a minor margin headwind [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx/revenue ratio stable at ~1.5–2.0% across L3Y; not a capital-intensive business

  • Receivables turn: Stable at ~50–55 days receivable outstanding across L3Y; no deterioration suggesting customer credit issues or aggressive revenue recognition [#A]

  • Aggressive accounting: No signals detected — revenue recognition is straightforward (software licence, maintenance, professional services); no evidence of channel stuffing or premature licence recognition [#A]

  • ❌ Amortisation growing faster than revenue — SEK 195m → SEK 230m FY2022→FY2024 (+18%) vs revenue +6.5%; widens the reported/adjusted gap incrementally, reducing reported EPS transparency over time [Cross-ref: §3]

  • Sceptical note — quality: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? The most credible concern is that Addnode's M&A model structurally requires the recurring amortisation charge to grow, meaning reported P&L increasingly diverges from adjusted; if the acquisition pace slows and goodwill impairment charges eventually flow through, reported earnings could be materially worse than EBITA-adjusted earnings suggest [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: Mixed sustainability signal — FY2022 peak margins appear partially cyclical (DM volume leverage); FY2024 recovery to near-peak suggests structural cost discipline is genuine, but DM-cycle dependency means margin is not fully derisked [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: FY2022–FY2024 +3.1% CAGR — below European IT-services sector median of ~8–10% for comparable periods; reflects the DM interruption in FY2023 [#J]

  • Sub: FY2021→FY2024 CAGR is a cleaner ~11–12%, more representative of Addnode's underlying combined organic + acquisition model

  • Profit CAGR: EBIT CAGR FY2022→FY2024 +3.8% (SEK 559m → SEK 602m); minimal operating leverage on this short window given DM headwinds [#J]

  • Sub: FY2021→FY2024 EBIT CAGR approximately +14–15%, substantially ahead of revenue — this is the correct longer-arc view

  • Operating leverage: Visible on multi-year arc, absent on L3Y peak-to-recovery arc — structural operating leverage exists (PM and PLM divisions scale well on incremental revenue) but DM cycle masks it on the 3Y window [#A]

  • Gross margin: Stable to slightly expanding — FY2022 36.2%, FY2023 35.5% (DM pressure), FY2024 36.6% (recovery + mix); 36.5% exit rate is the highest in L3Y [#A]

  • Sub: Gross margin improvement in FY2024 driven by higher-margin PLM professional services growing faster than DM hardware/licensing pass-through

  • EBITDA margin: FY2022 12.7% → FY2023 11.8% → FY2024 12.8% — V-shaped recovery; FY2024 exit slightly above FY2022 peak on an absolute margin basis [#A]

  • EBIT margin: FY2022 9.0% → FY2023 8.1% → FY2024 9.1% — similar V-shape but tighter amplitude; FY2024 9.1% is the highest L3Y full-year EBIT margin, marginally above FY2022 [#A]

  • Cost cut driver: Modest restructuring in Q3 2023 (SEK 12m) but not a cost-cutting story; margin recovery in FY2024 driven by volume recovery, not structural cost reduction [#A]

  • Mix shift: PLM growing faster than DM — PLM professional services carry higher gross margins (~42–45%) vs DM software reselling (~32–34%); mix tailwind is a genuine structural margin lever as PLM gains share [#A]

  • Sub: PM (Process Management) also growing, contributing stable ~9% EBIT margins; less mix-positive than PLM but adds revenue quality

  • Scale driver: Fixed cost leverage visible at group level — central costs (~SEK 100–110m/year) are broadly stable; incremental division revenue flows through at higher conversion rates [#A]

  • Sustainability assessment: Margin expansion is partially structural (PLM mix, scale) and partially cyclical (DM volume recovery); the structural component is durable; the DM component is exposed to the next Autodesk purchasing cycle [#J]

  • Sceptical note — margin: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? Autodesk's accelerating SaaS/subscription transition could structurally compress DM reseller economics over 3–5 years — if DM margins decline from ~10% to ~7%, group EBIT margin could compress 150–200bps structurally, overwhelming the PLM mix benefit [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Pronounced and stable — Q4 is consistently the strongest quarter, Q3 the weakest; pattern held across all L3Y years and appears structural to Addnode's software licence/maintenance renewal cadence [#J]

  • Q4 pattern: Consistently the highest-revenue and highest-EBIT quarter — Q4 EBIT margins: 10.2% (2022), 9.4% (2023), 10.4% (2024); Q4 revenue ~27–29% of full-year in each of the three years [#A]

  • Sub: Q4 strength driven by software licence renewals, year-end budget flush in customer procurement, and DM annual maintenance contract cycling

  • Q3 pattern: Consistently the weakest quarter — Q3 revenue ~20–21% of full-year; EBIT margins 7.7% (2022), 6.8% (2023), 8.0% (2024); Nordic summer holiday effect reduces professional services hours [#A]

  • Sub: Q3 2023's 6.8% EBIT margin was below the Q3 norm even allowing for seasonality, confirming that DM softness was a genuine incremental headwind

  • Q1 pattern: Moderate — ramping from Q4 renewals — Q1 is the second-weakest quarter; revenue ~22–24% of full-year; EBIT margins ~7.7–8.5% [#A]

  • Q2 pattern: Second strongest — Q2 revenues ~27% of full-year; EBIT margins ~9.0–9.2% in normal years; summer pre-ordering by construction clients lifts DM volumes [#A]

  • Strongest quarter: Q4 in every L3Y year without exception — margin premium vs full-year average ~120–140bps; consistent with software-sector Q4 licence renewal patterns (European IT-services sector base-rate: ~60% of companies report Q4 as peak quarter) [#A]

  • Seasonality direction: Stable and predictable — the Q4-peak/Q3-trough pattern did not change in L3Y; this provides forecast confidence in quarterly phasing even when annual trajectory is uncertain [#A]

  • Cause — buying pattern: Primary driver is software licence/maintenance renewal clustering in Q4 — Autodesk ELA and maintenance contract renewals, PLM enterprise deals, and public-sector year-end spend all concentrate in Q4 [#A]

  • Seasonality changing: No evidence of change — inter-quarter distribution of revenue and EBIT has been stable; no sign that SaaS transition is smoothing the pattern yet (would reduce Q4 clustering over time) [#A]

  • Sub: SaaS model transition in Autodesk ecosystem could gradually smooth seasonality over 3–5 years — watch Q4/Q3 ratio trending toward 1.2x from current 1.4x as a signal

  • Sceptical note — seasonality: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? The Q4 strength is heavily dependent on licence renewal concentration; if Autodesk SaaS migration converts lumpy renewals to monthly subscriptions, the Q4 revenue spike flattens, creating a Q4 consensus-miss risk even if annual totals are unchanged [#J]
