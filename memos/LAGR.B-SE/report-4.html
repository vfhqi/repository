  • Reverse-engineered bear — acquisition economics: The model requires consistent acquisition at multiples below the group's own P/E; as Nordic serial acquirer capital increases and competition for small/mid-cap industrial targets intensifies, the average acquisition multiple has likely risen 2-4 turns vs FY18-21 [#A]

  • Sub: No public data confirms the exact drift, but anecdotal commentary and transaction comparables suggest the "cheap acquisition" era for Nordic industrials may be in its final innings

  • Single largest risk: Acquisition pipeline quality deterioration — if Lagercrantz is forced to deploy capital at higher multiples to maintain EPS growth pace, the accretion arithmetic unravels and the forward EPS CAGR becomes uninvestable at current P/E [#J]

  • Sub: This risk is invisible in trailing earnings data (which reflect historical acquisition prices) and would only manifest in FY26+ EPS when recently acquired higher-priced businesses contribute to the reported figures

  • Early warning — organic growth: Organic growth near zero in FY24-25 reduces the organic earnings buffer that normally provides quality confirmation of the M&A accretion; if organic remains near-zero into FY26, the market may begin discounting the organic engine entirely [#A]

  • Early warning — margin plateau: If EBITA margins plateau at ~17-18% rather than continuing to expand, the dual engine of margin expansion + acquisition growth that has driven 20-25% EPS CAGR begins to single-engine — and revenue growth alone at low-single-digit organic is insufficient to maintain the pace [#A]

  • Early warning — NWC deterioration: Any signs of receivables aging or inventory build at acquired subsidiaries would be an early-stage signal of integration underperformance and possible goodwill impairment risk [#A]

  • Breaking points — capital markets: If credit spreads widen materially (e.g. Riksbank policy tightening or European credit market dislocation), the cost of acquisition financing rises and the accretion threshold increases — deals that were 3-4% EPS accretive at 5% debt cost become breakeven at 7-8% [#J]

  • Peer-relative downside — Diploma comparison: Diploma is running a similar model with arguably stronger recent momentum on both organic growth and acquisition pipeline — if Diploma is re-rated upward relative to Lagercrantz, capital rotation risk could compress LAGR.B-SE's relative multiple [#J]

  • Cross-cutting synthesis: All four per-section sceptical bullets converge on the same mechanism: the model is acquisition-dependent, and the acquisition engine is vulnerable to (a) multiple creep, (b) credit cost, and (c) an increasing absolute base requiring ever-larger deals to move the needle — none of these risks are in the current-year earnings data [#J]

  • M&A visibility gap: Unlike organic businesses where forward revenue is visible through order books, Lagercrantz's M&A pipeline is entirely opaque to investors — the 55-65% EPS contribution from M&A is unforecastable in any conventional sense [#A]
