3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 1: Nordic B2C Pricing Pressure Cascade

Scenario: Swedish and Finnish mobile markets — Telia's two largest revenue bases — enter a sustained ARPU compression cycle driven by MVNO proliferation, fixed-mobile convergence discounting, and B2B contract repricing at renewal. ARPU falls 3-5% annually for 24 months. Transmission: ARPU decline → service revenue miss → EBITDA margin compression (fixed cost leverage negative) → FCF miss → dividend cover falls below 1.0x → DPS cut → re-rating on yield basis.

Early warning signals:
- Swedish mobile ARPU trajectory in quarterly earnings (watch for sequential decline vs. consensus flat)
- Tele2 / Three Sweden promotional intensity in B2C market — price-match press releases
- MVNO subscriber net-add data from PTS (Swedish regulator) — rising share signals structural pressure
- Finnish mobile market — DNA (Telenor-owned) and Elisa promotional activity
- Blended B2C ARPU in Telia's own segment reporting vs. mix-adjusted underlying

Plausibility: High plausibility — Nordic mobile markets have experienced periodic ARPU compression cycles (Sweden 2013-2016, Finland recurring); structural over-supply exists given Three/Tele2 capacity and the Telenor/DNA Finnish network buildout.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: competitors have spectrum and network capacity to sustain price aggression ✓
- Motive: Three Sweden seeking market share post-consolidation rejection; Tele2 defending subscriber base ✓
- Opportunity: Telia's B2C premium positioning is vulnerable to "good-enough" network quality narrative ✓
- All three legs aligned — full activation scenario, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: Sustained ARPU growth in Sweden Q3/Q4 2025; Telia reporting positive B2C net adds with ARPU above SEK 230; PTS data showing MVNO share declining YoY.

Monitoring checklist:
- Swedish mobile service revenue growth YoY (quarterly)
- Finland EBITDA margin trend (quarterly)
- Blended ARPU per subscriber per segment
- Competitive promotional intensity index (press/regulator releases)
- Churn rate by segment vs. prior-year

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with FM3 (operating leverage failure) and FM6 (dividend sustainability); sequential trigger for FM8 (re-rating).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence that would invalidate — consecutive quarters of ARPU expansion with rising NPS and stable churn; none currently visible in public data.

§3 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 2: Leverage Trap at Cyclical Inflection

Scenario: Telia carries elevated net debt (~2.5-3.0x EBITDA post-asset disposals) at a moment when Nordic corporate credit spreads widen in a higher-for-longer rate environment. Refinancing tranches maturing 2026-2028 price materially higher. Transmission: higher interest expense → lower EPS → lower FCF available for deleveraging → leverage ratio static or rising → credit agency downgrade risk → cost of capital re-rating → equity multiple compression.

Early warning signals:
- Telia's next credit facility refinancing date and indicative spread vs. current book rate
- Nordic investment-grade credit spread environment (iTraxx Europe IG as proxy)
- Net debt / EBITDA progression — any stagnation at or above 2.5x
- Moody's / S&P outlook language in next rating action
- Interest cover ratio trend (EBIT / net interest) falling below 4x

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — Telia has historically maintained investment-grade discipline; leverage has been structurally elevated post-Eurasia divestment reinvestment but headline trajectory is downward; however, capex cycle reinvestment and potential M&A could reverse this.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: refinancing risk is real; maturity wall exists ✓
- Motive: macro rate environment is externally imposed — no internal motive, but no defence either ✓
- Opportunity: if Telia pursues in-market M&A (e.g. Swedish fibre, Finnish consolidation), leverage re-spikes ✓
- All three legs present — full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Net debt / EBITDA trending consistently below 2.3x with no material M&A signalled; credit agency positive outlook maintained; interest cover stable above 5x.

Monitoring checklist:
- Net debt / EBITDA quarterly (management guidance vs. actuals)
- Refinancing schedule from annual report debt maturity table
- Swedish/Nordic IG credit spreads monthly
- Telia capex intensity vs. prior guidance
- M&A press releases / management commentary on consolidation appetite

Interaction: Correlated with FM1 (if EBITDA compressed, leverage worsens simultaneously); amplifies FM8 (re-rating).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence — sustained deleveraging trajectory + no M&A signalling would invalidate; current public positioning post-Eurasia disposals is nominally deleveraging but pace is slow.

Scenario: Telia's Swedish/Finnish cost transformation programme (headcount reduction, network sharing, IT simplification) delivers nominal cost savings but fails to produce positive operating leverage because revenue growth is simultaneously flat-to-negative. EBITDA margin flatlines or compresses despite cost programme delivery. Transmission: revenue miss + cost programme savings absorbed by inflation → EBITDA margin flat → FCF flat → no re-rating catalyst → investment case stalls.

Early warning signals:
- Swedish segment EBITDA margin trend vs. management 3-year guidance
- Labour cost per employee in telco segment (annual report)
- Energy cost inflation embedded in network OpEx — European energy prices proxy
- IT simplification project delivery — reduction in legacy systems (qualitative, management commentary)
- Personnel count quarterly (any reversal of headcount reduction trend)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — European telco cost transformation programmes routinely under-deliver on operating leverage because revenue headwinds offset savings; precedent includes BT, Orange, Telefónica over 2015-2022.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: structural fixed cost base (network depreciation, spectrum lease, legacy IT) limits flexibility ✓
- Motive: management has cost programme commitment — risk is execution gap, not intent ✓
- Opportunity: Swedish wage inflation (LO/PTK collective agreement cycles) and energy re-pricing ✓
- All three legs — full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Two consecutive quarters of EBITDA margin expansion above 50bps YoY with stable or growing service revenue; personnel costs declining in real terms.

Monitoring checklist:
- EBITDA margin per segment quarterly
- Swedish wage inflation data (Statistics Sweden)
- Network energy OpEx as % of total OpEx
- Cost programme delivery scorecard (management updates)
- Revenue per cost unit trend

Interaction: Null interaction with FM5 (M&A risk); sequential with FM1 (pricing pressure triggers operating leverage failure).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence — genuine operating leverage evidence would require revenue growth above 2% real AND margin expansion simultaneously; historically rare in Nordic incumbent telco.

§5 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 4: Dividend Sustainability Collapse (FALSE FRIEND #1)

Scenario (FALSE FRIEND): Telia's dividend yield (historically 5-7%) functions as the primary anchor for thesis confidence. Investors interpret DPS stability as evidence of FCF health. In reality, FCF-to-dividend cover has been sustained by: (a) asset disposal proceeds recycled as "underlying" cash, (b) working capital timing benefits, (c) capex deferral (see FM5). When one or more of these props is removed — e.g. no further disposals, capex reinvestment cycle, working capital normalisation — FCF cover falls below 1.0x. Management then cuts DPS. Transmission: DPS cut → yield-investor sell trigger → re-rating from 5.5% to 7%+ yield → 20-25% price decline.

FALSE FRIEND identification: The yield itself is the confirmatory signal that creates false confidence — a high yield reads as "priced in" when it is in fact warning of deteriorating FCF.

Early warning signals:
- Reported FCF (post-capex, post-lease payments) vs. DPS × shares outstanding — cover ratio
- Working capital movement in cash flow statement — net release vs. prior year
- Asset disposal pipeline — are further material disposals available?
- Capex guidance for next 2 years vs. current run-rate
- Management commentary on dividend policy review (any softening of language)

Plausibility: High plausibility — multiple European telco incumbents have cut dividends after sustained high-yield periods (BT 2020, Proximus 2023, Vodafone 2024); the precedent base rate for high-yield-telco DPS cuts over a 5-year window is approximately 35-40% of named European incumbents.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: FCF is structurally constrained by capex intensity and lease accounting ✓
- Motive: board will cut if cover falls below threshold — management credibility argument is double-edged (cutting later = worse outcome) ✓
- Opportunity: 5G capex cycle reinvestment + fibre-to-the-premises acceleration provides concrete trigger ✓
- All three legs — full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: FCF cover of DPS consistently above 1.2x over 4 rolling quarters with no disposal proceeds in the numerator; capex guidance declining with network investment cycle maturing.

Monitoring checklist:
- Organic FCF (ex-disposal) / total DPS paid — quarterly
- Capex as % of revenue trend
- Working capital movement — quarterly cash flow statement
- Disposal announcement pipeline
- Any analyst note flagging FCF cover deterioration

Interaction: Master false-friend — correlated with FM1, FM3, FM7; cascade trigger for FM8.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence — FCF cover above 1.2x on organic basis for 4 consecutive quarters with declining capex-to-revenue ratio would invalidate; this has NOT been consistently demonstrated in public filings.

§6 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 5: Capex Deferral / R&D Under-Investment False-Friend (FALSE FRIEND #2)

Scenario (FALSE FRIEND): Telia reports margin expansion and FCF improvement driven by capex discipline. This reads as operational quality to bulls. In reality, capital deferral from the 5G rollout and FTTH acceleration creates a deferred investment liability. Network quality metrics (NPS, speed test rankings, coverage SLAs) begin to lag Tele2/DNA. Transmission: network quality lag → B2C churn uptick → B2B contract loss to Tele2 or Three → revenue miss → margin compression in out-years — precisely when bulls have extrapolated the improvement.

FALSE FRIEND identification: Margin expansion and FCF improvement in years 1-2 create false confidence; the investment deficit becomes visible in years 2-4 exactly when the analyst's EPS trajectory model peaks.

Early warning signals:
- Ookla / nPerf speed test rankings — Telia vs. Tele2 in Sweden and DNA/Elisa in Finland
- Ofcom/PTS coverage complaint data (coverage gaps)
- Capex as % of revenue vs. European telco peer median (~15-18%)
- Management commentary on 5G coverage milestones — delays
- B2B contract win/loss announcements in enterprise segment

Plausibility: High plausibility — capex deferral false-friend is the most common structural failure mode in European telco investment cases; Vodafone 2017-2021 and BT Consumer 2018-2022 are canonical precedents where FCF improvement preceded network quality deterioration and subsequent margin reversal.

⚡ This is the most diagnostically dangerous false-friend in the Telia case specifically because the investment thesis has likely been constructed partly on FCF improvement — if that improvement is capex-driven rather than revenue-quality-driven, the thesis has a time-bomb component invisible at the 12M horizon.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: capex deferral is operationally feasible over 2-3 years without immediately visible service degradation ✓
- Motive: management incentive structures (EBITDA-linked bonuses vs. multi-year network quality metrics) ✓
- Opportunity: post-5G initial rollout, incremental capex decisions are discretionary in the short run ✓
- All three legs — full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Capex-to-revenue ratio maintained above 15% with rising 5G coverage milestones; Ookla rankings stable or improving vs. Tele2; B2B contract retention rate above 90%.

Monitoring checklist:
- Capex guidance vs. actuals quarterly
- Independent network quality rankings (Ookla, nPerf — quarterly)
- 5G population coverage % vs. management milestone schedule
- B2B segment revenue growth vs. B2C
- R&D/innovation expenditure in annual report footnotes

Interaction: Sequential trigger for FM1 (network quality lag → ARPU pressure → pricing cascade); correlated with FM4 (FCF cover deterioration from capex reinvestment catch-up).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence — independent network quality metrics showing Telia maintaining #1 position in Sweden and Finland would partially invalidate; however, the lag effect means quality erosion may not appear in rankings for 12-18 months after the capex deferral decision.

The broadband customer base erodes faster than Telia's own FTTH migration can replace it. Transmission: fixed broadband subscriber loss → fixed service revenue decline → fixed-mobile bundle disruption → cross-sell economics deteriorate → ARPU per household falls.

Early warning signals:

- IP-Only / Öppen Fiber network coverage expansion announcements
- Swedish regulator fixed broadband market share data

- Bundle penetration rate (% of mobile subscribers with fixed broadband — leading churn indicator)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — Swedish fibre over-build is structurally real and well-documented; however, Telia has responded with fibre wholesale agreements and own-build acceleration; risk is real but partially mitigated; Finnish market (Elisa, DNA) has a more contained dynamic.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: independent fibre operators have capital (PE-backed, infrastructure funds) ✓
- Motive: regulatory open-access mandate makes fibre overbuild commercially viable for newcomers ✓
- Opportunity: Telia's legacy copper base is the structural target ✓
- All three legs — full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Telia fixed broadband market share stable above 35% in Sweden with positive net adds; Telia FTTH customer migration rate above 60% of copper base.

Monitoring checklist:
- Fixed broadband subscriber net adds quarterly

- Wholesale open-access revenue (indicator of migration to third-party fibre)
- Bundle economics disclosure in earnings

Interaction: Null interaction with FM2 (leverage); correlated with FM1 (bundle disruption accelerates mobile ARPU pressure); sequential with FM3 (fixed-line revenue loss reduces operating leverage).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence — PTS data showing Telia stable or growing fixed share would weaken this narrative significantly; the risk is structural but the pace of erosion is the key variable.

4. PART B — META-ANALYSIS

J-front verdict: Three correlated failure clusters dominate; the FALSE FRIEND cluster (FM4 + FM5) is structurally most dangerous because it is least visible at the 12M horizon and most likely to produce a concentrated sell event.

Scenario cluster: Revenue erosion cluster (FM1 + FM6 + FM10)
- FM1 (pricing pressure), FM6 (FTTH overbuild), FM10 (Baltic drag) share a common transmission: service revenue miss → EBITDA below consensus
- These three interact sequentially — FTTH overbuild weakens fixed-mobile bundle economics, accelerating mobile ARPU pressure
- Joint probability of zero activations from this cluster: low plausibility — at least partial FM1 activation is almost certain given Nordic competitive structure

Scenario cluster: Leverage / capital structure cluster (FM2 + FM4 + FM11)
- FM2 (leverage trap), FM4 (dividend sustainability), FM11 (M&A capital allocation) share a common transmission: cash flow deterioration → balance sheet stress → re-rating
- FM11 is the most acute trigger — an acquisition can simultaneously raise leverage (FM2) and threaten dividend cover (FM4)
- This cluster is the one most likely to produce a sharp price event (vs. gradual erosion from revenue cluster)

Scenario cluster: False-friend cluster (FM4 + FM5)
- Both false-friend scenarios (dividend sustainability, capex deferral) are the most dangerous by ACH methodology because they generate POSITIVE confirmatory signals while deterioration compounds
- False-friend density: 2 of 11 scenarios (18%) are explicitly false-friend; however, FM8 (re-rating) has a false-friend element (sector multiple decompression happens fastest when investors have been lulled by yield stability)
- High false-friend density relative to a "simple bear" thesis — the Telia case is more dangerous than direct-bearish analyses suggest

Scenario cluster: Execution / management cluster (FM3 + FM7 + FM11)
- Cost transformation delivery (FM3), management departure (FM7), and M&A integration (FM11) are correlated through a common mechanism: management bandwidth constraint
- If FM7 occurs (leadership departure) during FM11 (integration), FM3 (cost programme) deceleration is near-certain
- This cluster is the "compound failure" scenario most likely to produce a multi-quarter earnings miss streak

Joint probability assessment (verbal):
- Zero failure-mode activations (thesis fully executes): low plausibility — FM1 (pricing pressure) has structural inevitability; at minimum, partial activation is expected
- Exactly one failure-mode activation: medium plausibility — FM1 or FM5 in isolation is manageable
- Two or more correlated failure-mode activations: medium-to-high plausibility — FM1 + FM3 + FM4 cascade is the most probable adverse compound scenario

Compound monitorables (appearing across ≥3 scenarios):
- EBITDA margin quarterly (appears in FM1, FM3, FM6, FM9, FM10) — the single highest-frequency compound monitoring signal
- FCF cover of DPS (appears in FM2, FM4, FM5, FM11) — the single most important false-friend disconfirming signal
- Capex as % of revenue (appears in FM3, FM5, FM8) — the leading indicator for false-friend scenarios
- Net debt / EBITDA (appears in FM2, FM4, FM11) — leverage trajectory as cascade amplifier

False-friend density summary:
- FM4 and FM5 are explicitly false-friend; FM8 has partial false-friend character
- Approximately 25-30% of failure scenarios have false-friend elements
- High false-friend density means the thesis is more fragile than a direct-bearish read would suggest — the danger is not visible deterioration but apparent strength masking structural erosion

⚡ The compound monitorable that almost never appears in standard analyst models is capex-as-%-of-revenue trend on a rolling 4-quarter basis cross-referenced against independent network quality rankings. If Telia's capex intensity is declining AND network rankings are starting to slip, both false-friend scenarios (FM4 + FM5) are activating simultaneously — this compound signal would be an immediate re-pre-mortem trigger.

Leading indicators meta-summary:
