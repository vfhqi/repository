§1 — KQ Definition + Mission Command Brief + Competing Hypotheses

J-front verdict bullet: H-A (structurally durable organic growth, competitively entrenched) accumulates fewest hard inconsistencies; H-B (cyclically inflated, mean-reverting) fails M/M/O causal completeness test at current evidence level [NO PEER ANCHOR]

KQ stated verbatim:
- KQ: "Is the target company's organic revenue growth trajectory structurally durable over the next 3 years, or is it cyclically inflated and mean-reverting?"

Higher intent — why does this KQ matter:
- Decision at stake: the entire IRR underwrite at DD stage is sensitive to whether mid-to-high-teens organic revenue growth is real and compounding or a mean-reverting cyclical artifact; a misjudgement here invalidates the EPS trajectory model
- Concentrated long-only context: a 12-24 month hold on a 5-15 position book means there is no diversification buffer; if growth decelerates sharply 18 months in, the position is a material drag with limited recovery path inside the hold window
- Singular downstream use: APM uses this ACH verdict to grade the "Revenue Growth Quality" required attribute within the Growth pillar; a low-plausibility verdict on H-A kills or parks the case regardless of valuation attractiveness

Competing hypotheses (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive, falsifiable):

  • H-A — Structurally durable, competitively entrenched: organic revenue growth is driven by expanding wallet-share in a genuinely underpenetrated TAM, reinforced by high switching costs and rising NRR; growth rate is stable-to-accelerating regardless of macro cycle; falsified if NRR contracts below 100% or organic growth decelerates >500bps ex-FX in a non-recessionary environment
  • H-B — Cyclically inflated, mean-reverting: observed growth rate is elevated by a combination of post-COVID demand catch-up, easy prior-year comparables, and/or a broader macro tailwind specific to the sector; growth rate reverts to mid-single-digit structural baseline within 2-4 quarters as comparables tighten; falsified if growth remains above 10% organic ex-FX through a full comparable-normalisation cycle (4+ quarters)
  • H-C — Composite durable + cyclical (mixed): the core product/segment exhibits structural durability (H-A characteristics) while an expansion segment or geography is cyclically elevated (H-B characteristics); aggregate reported organic growth conflates the two; falsified if either the core segment shows NRR contraction OR the expansion segment demonstrates full-cycle sustainability above its pre-cycle trend
  • H-D — Null / narrative inflation: reported growth metrics are definitionally inflated (organic ex-FX definition excludes meaningful revenue items, or M&A contribution is mis-classified); underlying "true organic" growth is materially lower than reported; falsified if independent revenue-audit or channel-check confirms the organic definition is conservative

Stage context:
- DD stage: Q22 is the analytic forcing function — the analyst cannot exit DD without a verbal verdict on this KQ; the output directly populates the APM's pillar grading
- Ideal pairing note: this Q22 runs without a prior Q21 on the same KQ; the landscape-mapping work is therefore compressed into §2 of this memo — the analyst is accepting higher hypothesis-generation risk

Sceptical bullet — runner-up hypothesis:
- H-C is the runner-up, not H-B, because the evidence structure consistently shows segment-level heterogeneity that aggregate reporting obscures; H-C loses to H-A only because the available evidence does not yet cleanly separate core-segment NRR from expansion-segment NRR — that data gap favours H-A by default, which is itself a form of analytical risk
