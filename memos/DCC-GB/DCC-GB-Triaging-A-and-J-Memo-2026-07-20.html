B.1 Business Description 12 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Downstream energy distributor mid-transformation. DCC plc sells and distributes liquid petroleum gas, other energy products, and mobility fuel across Europe and North America, having exited Healthcare and now exiting Technology to become a pure-play multi-energy group
2.
Two-division structure post-simplification. Operations now split into Solutions (Energy Products, 68% of group profit, plus Energy Services) and Mobility (roughly 1,200 retail forecourts and commercial refuelling sites)
3.
Large revenue base, thin reported margin. Continuing revenue of GBP 15,440m in FY2026 (down 2.9%) carries an EBIT margin of only 4.11% (calculated), typical of a low-margin, high-volume distribution model
4.
Adjusted operating profit growing faster than revenue. Continuing adjusted operating profit rose 3.6% to GBP 634.0m despite the revenue decline, reflecting unit-margin expansion, operating profit per litre up 15.1% to 3.8 pence, rather than volume growth
5.
Negative working-capital, cash-generative model. Group working capital days improved to 0.4 days sales, down from 5.7 days, driving free cash flow conversion of 108%, a structurally advantaged cash profile for a distribution business
6.
Low balance-sheet leverage. Net debt to EBITDA of 0.9x, well inside the 1.0x-2.0x internal target range, leaves capacity for further bolt-on mergers and acquisitions alongside the GBP 700m FY2026 capital return
7.
Geography. Roughly 70% of group profit is generated outside the UK, with meaningful exposure to continental Europe and North America; a 10% foreign-exchange move affects group pre-tax profit by GBP 28.6m
8.
Unbroken 32-year dividend record. The final dividend rose 5.0% to 147.22 pence, underscoring a cash-compounder self-image that management is protecting through the transition
9.
Investment style implication. The case reads as a self-help cost and portfolio programme plus capital-redeployment compounder, GARP-adjacent, event-driven on the pending Nexora technology divestment
10.
Sector classification. Viewforth taxonomy places DCC in Energy, downstream distribution; the cohort (HLD08, Irish and UK diversified distribution conglomerates) is currently a cold-start with zero peer A&J memos on disk
11.
Divisional profit split detail. Energy Products, Energy Services and Mobility deliver very different trajectories, a three-way split that matters for how the group's overall change-forces read is weighted
11.1
Energy Products: GBP 404.1m operating profit in FY2026, up 11.1%, the largest single profit pool
11.2
Energy Services: GBP 15.7m operating profit, down 67.5%, the segment dragging on the group read
11.3
Mobility: GBP 134.4m operating profit, up 8.6%, the second-strongest performer after Energy Products
12.
Capital allocation track record. DCC has completed approximately 400 acquisitions historically, typically at 6-8x EV/EBITDA for European bolt-ons, evidencing a long-run, repeatable capital-deployment discipline that underpins the compounder self-image
