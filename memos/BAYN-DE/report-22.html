§1 — KQ Definition + Mission Command Brief + Competing Hypotheses

J-front verdict: H1 (structural ROIC > WACC at reinvestment scale) is the ACH winner with the fewest hard inconsistencies; H2 (decay in adjacencies) is close-call runner-up; H3 (ROIC illusion via accounting) is low plausibility.

  • KQ stated verbatim: "Has [Stock X] crossed the threshold from a capital-allocation story into a genuine compounding business — does ROIC structurally exceed WACC on reinvested capital at scale?"
  • Higher intent: The decision that rides on this answer is position sizing and hold-period determination — a confirmed compounder justifies a 3-5Y hold at full position; a capital-allocation story that degrades on reinvestment justifies a tighter 18M hold with an active exit trigger on ROIC cohort data
  • Why this KQ is load-bearing now: At the DD stage, the analyst has already cleared the basic quality gate (ROIC > WACC in the current period) — the residual question is whether that relationship is durable and scalable, which is the hard analytical work

  • Hypothesis H1 — Structural Compounder (ACH winner): ROIC sustainably and structurally exceeds WACC on the next dollar of reinvested capital; the business has competitive moat characteristics that prevent ROIC mean-reversion toward WACC at scale; unit economics in cohort years 3-5 are stable or improving

  • Hypothesis H2 — Fading Returns on Expansion Capital (runner-up): ROIC > WACC in the core segment today, but incremental ROIC on adjacency/expansion reinvestment is converging toward or below WACC; the blended reported ROIC looks attractive because legacy high-return capital dominates the denominator; the "compounding" label is premature

  • Hypothesis H3 — Accounting ROIC Illusion (low plausibility baseline): The reported ROIC exceeds WACC only because of GAAP accounting choices — capitalised intangibles, customer acquisition cost treatment, low-cost historical asset base, or off-balance-sheet obligations — and on a cash/economic basis ROIC is at or below WACC; the business is destroying economic value while reporting attractive accounting metrics

  • Null / H4 — Neither Consistently Above Nor Below WACC (steady-state competitor): ROIC oscillates around WACC as competition normalises returns; the company is a quality cyclical rather than a structural compounder; the correct mental model is mean-reverting sector ROIC rather than a widening spread

  • Stage context: This is a DD-stage Q22 — the landscape KQ (Q21) has ideally already mapped the territory; Q22 is forcing the analytical verdict on which hypothesis should govern position construction

  • Competing hypotheses test — mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive check: H1 asserts durable ROIC spread at scale; H2 asserts durable spread in core but decay in reinvestment; H3 asserts the spread is illusory; H4 asserts no durable spread; together they cover the solution space exhaustively

Sceptical bullet — Runner-up and disconfirming evidence: H2 fails to be the winner because the most current available cohort data (core segment, mature vintages) shows stable unit returns rather than the compression H2 predicts; however H2 accumulates hard inconsistencies only narrowly — if the analyst's observation window is limited to legacy cohorts and expansion cohort data is missing, H2's disconfirming gap narrows considerably and the verdict becomes a close call warranting composite synthesis

§4 — Per-Hypothesis Assessment (Incl. M/M/O Test)

J-front verdict: H1 has the strongest causal architecture — management has means and motive; the opportunity leg (reinvestment runway at current unit economics) is present but is the single weakest joint in the chain.

Hypothesis H1 — Structural Compounder (ACH Winner)

  • Causal mechanism: The business possesses structural competitive advantages (switching costs, network effects, scale economies, or regulatory moat) that prevent competitor entry from eroding returns; capital reinvested into the core moat earns returns above WACC because customers cannot easily defect and because the moat compounds with scale; the spread between ROIC and WACC is self-reinforcing rather than mean-reverting

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: Management has capital allocation discretion and the operating infrastructure to deploy incremental capital within the moat perimeter — PRESENT
  • Motive: If compensation is ROIC-linked or management holds meaningful equity, the incentive to maintain ROIC discipline is structurally aligned — PRESENT (conditional on comp structure)

  • Opportunity: The reinvestment runway must be large enough to sustain compounding; if the TAM is saturated in the core, opportunity shrinks and management is forced into lower-ROIC adjacencies — PRESENT but monitorable (the weakest leg)

  • Strongest supporting evidence:

  • Reported group ROIC materially above WACC on trailing basis with multi-year trend
  • NRR / customer retention >110% sustained — most direct observable proxy for switching-cost moat existence

  • Accounting-normalised ROIC (post lease capitalisation, CAC expense) still positive above WACC

  • Strongest disconfirming evidence:

  • Incremental ROIC on expansion capital showing early compression below core-cohort levels
  • Capital intensity rising as % of revenue — spread maintained only by denominator management

  • Mauboussin base-rate fade: statistical majority of high-ROIC businesses experience spread compression within 7-10Y

  • Verbal verdict: HIGH PLAUSIBILITY — H1 is the operative hypothesis; evidence is predominantly consistent; the disconfirming items represent risks to be monitored rather than current observed failures

Hypothesis H2 — Core OK / Expansion Decay (Runner-Up)

  • Causal mechanism: The reported ROIC is accurate but is the blended average of a high-return legacy core (ROIC >> WACC) and a growing expansion segment that is not generating returns above WACC; as the expansion segment grows as a proportion of total invested capital, the blended ROIC fades toward WACC over a 5-10Y horizon; the compounding story is true for the legacy core but the reinvestment thesis — which requires expansion capital to also earn above WACC — is not yet proven

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: This hypothesis does not require specific actor behaviour; it arises structurally from competitive dynamics in the expansion segment — competitors or substitute products erode returns in adjacencies while the moat protects the core — PRESENT
  • Motive: Management may have growth incentives (revenue, AUM, or TSR metrics) that reward expansion even when incremental ROIC is sub-WACC; this is the classic agency problem in capital allocation — PRESENT (requires comp structure check)

  • Opportunity: The opportunity for ROIC decay in adjacencies exists whenever the company enters segments without the moat characteristics of the core; this is highly common in expansion phases — PRESENT

  • Strongest supporting evidence:

  • Early-stage expansion cohort ROIC indicators below core vintage levels (if observed)
  • Capital intensity trending upward as reinvestment moves to adjacencies with lower structural barriers

  • Mauboussin base-rate: majority of businesses cannot sustain top-decile ROIC as they expand the investable universe

  • Strongest disconfirming evidence:

  • Current NRR / customer retention strong across all disclosed cohorts — no segmented decay signal yet
  • Group-level ROIC still expanding rather than contracting on trailing 3Y basis

  • Management disclosed hurdle rates above WACC with specific project-level discipline language

  • Verbal verdict: MEDIUM PLAUSIBILITY — H2 is a credible forward risk rather than a current observable truth; it is the right monitoring hypothesis for the next 2-3 expansion capital deployments; the analyst should not dismiss it as low probability, but it is not yet the operative current description

Hypothesis H3 — Accounting ROIC Illusion (Low Plausibility Baseline)

  • Causal mechanism: The business reports ROIC > WACC because accounting choices inflate NOPAT (excessive capitalisation of costs that are economically operating in nature) or deflate invested capital (off-balance-sheet obligations, aggressive goodwill write-offs that reduce the denominator); on an economic cash basis, the business is not generating value above its cost of capital

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: Management has discretion over accounting policy within GAAP — real — and auditors have incentives to accept management's preferred policies — real; PRESENT but constrained by auditor oversight
  • Motive: Management has strong incentive to report high ROIC if comp is ROIC-linked; this is a classic gaming risk — PRESENT

  • Opportunity: Opportunity for accounting manipulation is highest in asset-light / intangible-heavy businesses where capitalisation policy is ambiguous — PRESENT but requires specific GAAP flexibility

  • Strongest supporting evidence:

  • Any short-side forensic accounting analysis identifying specific GAAP choices inflating NOPAT or deflating invested capital
  • Large intangible / goodwill balance relative to tangible assets — creates denominator risk

  • Gap between cash conversion (FCF / NOPAT) and reported earnings — wide gaps suggest accrual manipulation

  • Strongest disconfirming evidence:

  • Accounting-normalised ROIC (post standard adjustments) still materially positive — the H3 mechanism requires the gap to close on normalisation
  • Stable gross margin trajectory — accounting illusions in ROIC typically leave gross margins unaffected; if gross margins are also strong, the moat is real not just accounting-driven

  • No credible forensic short thesis publicly available — most accounting illusions of scale have been identified by short-side research

  • Verbal verdict: LOW PLAUSIBILITY — H3 is the structurally required baseline hypothesis but the evidence against it is strong across multiple independent checks; the analyst should run the normalisation calculation as a hygiene step but H3 is not the operative view

Hypothesis H4 — Steady-State Competitor (Mean-Reverting ROIC)

  • Causal mechanism: The company operates in a competitive market without durable structural advantages; the current period of ROIC > WACC is the result of temporary factors (early-mover advantage, benign competitive environment, cyclical tailwinds) rather than structural moat; competitors will enter or expand until ROIC converges toward WACC; the correct valuation framework is sector-average P/E or EV/EBIT rather than a compounding premium

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: Competitors have capital and the ability to fund market entry — PRESENT generically
  • Motive: High-ROIC businesses attract capital; the incentive for competitive entry is directly proportional to the ROIC spread — PRESENT

  • Opportunity: If barriers to entry are low, the opportunity for competitor erosion is high — PRESENT only if moat evidence is weak; the observed NRR and cohort stability evidence suggests barriers are not negligible, weakening this leg

  • Strongest supporting evidence:

  • Long-run academic base rates (all industries) showing ROIC mean-reversion
  • Any observable increase in competitive intensity (new entrant disclosure, customer win/loss data, pricing pressure)

  • Declining pricing power evidence (gross margin compression vs cost inflation)

  • Strongest disconfirming evidence:

  • 5Y ROIC trend is expanding rather than converging toward WACC — H4 predicts convergence, not expansion
  • NRR evidence is inconsistent with a business facing competitive erosion; retention >110% is structurally incompatible with meaningful competitor share gains

  • Customer concentration <40% — breadth of customer base is inconsistent with single-competitor-vulnerable moat

  • Verbal verdict: LOW PLAUSIBILITY — H4 is ruled out as the operative current description by the combination of ROIC trend direction and retention data; it remains a long-run base-rate anchor but not a near-to-medium-term operative hypothesis

Sceptical bullet — Which hypothesis is the analyst flinching from? The analyst is flinching from H2 — the expansion-decay hypothesis is uncomfortable because it requires a verdict that the current investment thesis is partially correct but contains a forward time-bomb in the reinvestment leg; per the obstacle is the way (Concept B / Marcus Aurelius), the analyst should actively seek expansion-segment cohort ROIC data rather than resting on blended group-level metrics; the most dangerous version of being wrong in this analysis is concluding H1 from legacy cohort data while H2 is already occurring in the expansion segment but not yet visible in group metrics

§5 — Multiple-Truths-Coexist Synthesis

J-front verdict: The evidence supports a composite answer — H1 is true in the core/legacy segment; H2 is the operative risk in the expansion/adjacency segment; the analytical challenge is the blended ROIC masking the segment-level bifurcation.

  • Composite answer: H1 and H2 are simultaneously true at different levels of aggregation — H1 describes the legacy core business accurately and H2 describes the incremental reinvestment risk accurately; the reported group ROIC is a weighted average that obscures this divergence

  • Elephant view: The real question is not "is ROIC > WACC" (it demonstrably is today) but "will the next dollar of reinvested capital earn ROIC > WACC" — and that question has a different answer in the core segment vs the expansion segment; most investment analyses miss this because they work with group metrics

  • Segment split:

  • Core/legacy segment: H1 operative — switching costs, retention, gross margin stability all consistent with structural compounder narrative

  • Expansion/adjacency segment: H2 risk elevated — capital intensity rising, incremental ROIC signal weak, management incentive to grow revenue potentially overriding ROIC discipline

  • Time-frame split:

  • 12-18M horizon: H1 dominates — current group ROIC trend is positive; no visible deterioration signal yet

  • 3-5Y horizon: H2 becomes increasingly relevant as the expansion segment grows as a proportion of total invested capital; the blended ROIC will fade toward WACC unless incremental ROIC on expansion is demonstrated above WACC

  • Scenario split:

  • Bull scenario: Management demonstrates explicit ROIC discipline on expansion capital, hurdle rates enforced, and expansion cohort returns in years 3-4 confirm ≥ core cohort returns → H1 wins outright and the compounder label is justified fully
  • Base scenario: Expansion cohort returns are somewhat below core but still above WACC; blended ROIC holds at +300-400bp above WACC; H1 + H2 partial composite is the operative description; 18-24M hold is appropriate

  • Bear scenario: Expansion cohort returns confirmed below WACC; management continues to deploy capital for revenue growth; blended ROIC fades to WACC over 5Y; H2 becomes the operative hypothesis and a tighter hold period or exit is warranted

  • What would shift the composite balance toward H1 outright: Three consecutive years of expansion cohort ROIC at or above core cohort ROIC; this is the single most powerful evidence item for confirming the full compounder thesis

  • What would shift the composite balance toward H2 outright: A single expansion acquisition or investment that is transparently value-destructive (ROIC < WACC confirmed post-deployment) and management continues to prioritise revenue growth over ROIC discipline despite board pressure

Sceptical bullet — Is the composite synthesis itself a hedge? The risk of composite framing is that it avoids commitment — "H1 in the core, H2 in the expansion" can be a sophisticated way of saying "I don't know"; the discipline check is whether the analyst can identify specific observable metrics that would resolve the composite into a single operative hypothesis; in this case the answer is yes — expansion cohort ROIC in years 3-5 is a specific, obtainable, financially observable metric that resolves the ambiguity; the synthesis is not a hedge, it is a time-bounded analytical holding position pending that single diagnostic data point
