FY2024 Revenue Delivery

Analysis: COMET guided FY2024 revenue at CHF 370-400m (issued with FY2023 results, March 2024). The company delivered CHF 390.3m — approximately at the midpoint, slightly toward the upper end. On constant currency, the performance was modestly better given CHF appreciation through 2024. The PCT division was the primary driver, with WFE end-market recovery from the H1 2024 trough contributing. IXS performed in line with guidance. The FY2024 delivery was solid: no major misses, guidance proved accurate, and management signalled confidence going into 2025. #A

FY2025 Revenue: The Miss

COMET guided FY2025 revenue at CHF 420-440m (issued with FY2024 results, March 2025). Actual FY2025 revenue: CHF 406.3m. This is a CHF 19-34m undershoot versus the guidance range, or approximately 6.5% below the CHF 430m midpoint. #I

Dissecting the miss:

Analysis: The CHF appreciation was the largest single factor. COMET generates approximately 60-65% of revenue in USD (PCT is predominantly USD-denominated; WFE customers are US and Asian semiconductor OEMs). The CHF/USD rate moved from approximately 0.89 to 0.93 (CHF strengthening) through 2025, creating a structural revenue headwind of approximately CHF 15-18m on translation alone. #A

Analysis: IXS underperformed. The industrial X-ray segment was expected to stabilise following FY2024 restructuring actions. Instead, IXS revenues declined further in 2025 — the automotive and industrial end-markets in Europe remained weak, and the restructuring benefits were slower to materialise than management projected. IXS contributed approximately CHF 8-10m of the revenue shortfall relative to guidance. #A

PCT recovered broadly as expected. The PCT division (plasma generators, power systems for WFE) grew approximately 7.3% in reported CHF, or approximately 13-15% in constant currency. This was broadly in line with guidance for PCT specifically. The WFE cycle recovery from H1 2024 trough was real but not as strong as the most optimistic scenarios assumed. #A

Section 3 — Cash Flow and Balance Sheet

FCF collapsed to CHF 8.5m — driven by three identifiable and mostly temporary factors: inventory build (cycle confidence), Penang capex peak (one-time), and weak operating profit. The balance sheet is stretched but manageable. The dividend cut is arithmetically justified.

Free Cash Flow

FY2025 FCF: CHF 8.5m (vs CHF 41.4m in FY2024). #I

The FCF compression has three drivers:

  1. Inventory build: Working capital increased by approximately CHF 22m in FY2025. COMET built inventory ahead of the expected WFE cycle recovery — this is standard semiconductor capital equipment behaviour. The inventory build is a leading indicator of management confidence in FY2026 revenue, but it consumes cash in FY2025. #A

  2. Capex elevated: CHF 52m capex in FY2025 (vs CHF 47m in FY2024). Penang manufacturing site is the primary driver — COMET is constructing a new production facility in Malaysia to serve Asian WFE customers and reduce CHF-denominated cost exposure. Penang capex is expected to peak in FY2025-FY2026 and then decline sharply. Peak Penang capex is a one-time capital intensity event, not an ongoing feature of the business model. #A

  3. Weak operating profit: The EBIT near-zero level reduces the operating cash generation base despite EBITDA being positive (non-cash charges in EBIT do add back as depreciation/amortisation). #A

Analysis: Balance sheet: net debt of approximately CHF 85m at end-FY2025, up from CHF 48m at end-FY2024. Net debt/EBITDA approximately 2.1x — elevated but not dangerous given the asset base and expected FCF recovery. The company maintains a CHF 200m credit facility with significant undrawn capacity. #A

Dividend

Proposed FY2025 dividend: CHF 1.00/share (subject to AGM approval in May 2026). Prior year: CHF 3.00/share. #I

Analysis: The 67% dividend cut is a significant optics event. However, the arithmetic is straightforward: CHF 8.5m FCF minus CHF 5-6m in mandatory debt service leaves limited room for a full dividend without borrowing. Management preserved financial flexibility. The cut signals that management does not expect a rapid normalisation of FCF — they are planning for a prolonged recovery period. This is conservative and arguably appropriate given cycle uncertainty. #A

The cut also signals that the CMD dividend commitment (CHF 3.00+ per share maintained) has been walked back. This is a minor negative for management credibility, but the capital allocation logic is sound. #A
