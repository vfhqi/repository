Annual capital expenditure reached SEK 1,089 million in 2025 representing 5.8% of revenue, compared to SEK 1,190 million in 2024 and SEK 815 million in 2023, with group operational maintenance capex remaining stable at approximately SEK 400 million per year .

Industry Structure and Market Positioning Advanced stainless steel represents a specialized niche of 2 to 4 million tons per year globally, a fraction of the 50 million tons global stainless steel volume . Western producers utilize base-plus-surcharge pricing to insulate margins from raw material volatility, with surcharges dynamically adjusting based on previous-month average metal exchange prices calculated from LME prices for nickel, molybdenum, and chromium .

Value Chain Integration and Margin Profile Alleima's fully integrated metallurgy model secures superior value-accrual at advanced finishing stages while maintaining absolute upstream feedstock control, with high-pressure extrusion and thermal processing stages acting as significant capital-intensive barriers . Input material consists of 81% recycled steel in metallurgy operations . Customer Concentration and Switching Barriers Alleima maintains deep, hard-to-replicate customer relationships secured by multi-year capital-project backlogs, though revenue exposure remains moderately concentrated across major global key accounts, with Oil and Gas representing 24.00% of total group revenues in FY2025 and Nuclear applications contributing 16.00% of FY2025 revenues . Lengthy, complex customer and regulatory qualifications prevent easy supplier switching, with production facilities meeting strict international ASME and RCC-M nuclear-grade requirements and umbilical tubing SAF 2507 complying with stringent NACE MR0175, ISO 15156, and DNV approvals . Metal cost volatility is structurally transferred to customers via surcharge pricing clauses, with surcharge mechanisms covering approximately 40.00% of total group sales though carrying a typical 3-to-6 month time lag between purchase and revenue recognition .

Carpenter Technology is expanding its Specialty Alloys segment, delivering a record 35.60% SAO operating margin in F3Q26 driven by ongoing productivity gains, product mix optimization, and pricing actions while investing to add brownfield primary and secondary melt capacity . Aperam is shifting focus toward high-value Alloys and Specialties to hedge against carbon steel cyclicality, acquiring USAP in January 2025 to secure a powerful position in the US aerospace market with expected annual synergies of EUR 27.00 million by year three, and acquiring Magnetec in April 2026 to expand downstream into high-value magnetic solutions .

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution CEO Göran Björkman has improved Alleima's financial foundation since 2017, establishing lower volatility and resilient through-cycle margins, while industry entry barriers remain formidably defended by capital-intensive mills requiring approaching USD 2.30 billion for moderate-capacity facilities and multi-year qualifications, with technical product qualifications normally requiring one to two years of close collaboration with customer engineers . Production requires specialized metallurgy knowledge utilizing more than 900 active alloy recipes, with direct control of the entire value chain remaining a strict prerequisite for developing advanced new materials .

Long-Term Growth Vectors and Structural Tailwinds Kanthal capitalizes on industrial decarbonization converting fossil furnaces to high-temperature electric solutions, with the global industrial furnace market representing a massive SEK 120 billion annual opportunity and Kanthal expanding its Perth and Concord facilities, increasing silicon carbide element capacity by 40% .
