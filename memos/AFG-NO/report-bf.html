EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 28 findings

AF Gruppen operates as a project-based contracting and industrial group primarily focused on Norway and Sweden, delivering resilient revenue growth through a decentralized business model. However, profitability remains highly dependent on execution risk across uneven segments, with the company historically achieving operating margins of 6.30% compared to only 3.20% for its Nordic peer group. The group's exceptional capital returns are driven by negative working capital contracting and specialized recycling operations, though persistent project execution failures in the Offshore segment and Swedish contract disputes create material risks to through-cycle performance.

Business Model and Geographic Positioning

1.
Geographic concentration and revenue dominance: Norway generated 76% of total revenue in fiscal year 2024, providing a stable domestic foundation but limiting geographic diversification versus pan-Nordic competitors like Skanska and Peab
2.
Contract-driven revenue mechanics: Revenues are overwhelmingly generated through construction contracts, representing NOK 30,427 million of the NOK 31,992 million total, with revenue recognized over time using degree of completion methodology
3.
Asset-light operational structure: General contracting and civil engineering execution are asset-light, relying on extensive subcontractor networks to maintain flexible cost structures with capital expenditure representing under 1.00% of sales
4.
Decentralized execution autonomy: Decision-making is placed close to production, giving project managers autonomy to adapt to local markets, though this model contributed to severe cost overruns in the Offshore segment

Segment Performance and Portfolio Quality

5.
Civil Engineering segment leadership: Civil Engineering is the largest segment by EBIT, delivering NOK 723 million in operating profit with NOK 11,190 million in revenue during 2025, driven by large-scale public infrastructure projects
6.
Betonmast margin inflection achieved: Betonmast reached an EBIT margin of 5.3% in 2025, finally exceeding the group's margin requirement after years of restructuring efforts
