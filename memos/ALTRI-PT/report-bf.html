EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 46 findings

Section summary
  1. Altri's vertically integrated bio-industrial pulp model leverages competitive Iberian wood sourcing to transition from volatile paper pulp markets into sustainable textiles.

Altri operates a vertically integrated bio-industrial pulp model leveraging competitive Iberian wood sourcing to transition from volatile paper pulp markets into sustainable textiles, but this strategic pivot confronts severe near-term headwinds and structural market threats that require careful examination.

1.
Model Assessment: Altri's tri-mill operational footprint comprises Celbi (850,000 tonnes BEKP capacity), Caima (115,000 tonnes dissolving wood pulp), and Biotek (185,000 tonnes), targeting combined capacity of 1.15 million tonnes by 2027. The business model remains highly exposed to global commodity cycles, with Europe absorbing 61% of total pulp volumes and Asia representing 19% of sales in early 2026. Management is executing a full conversion of Biotek to dissolving wood pulp by end-2026, representing a deliberate strategic shift toward higher-margin, less cyclical textile applications. However, Altri secures only 15% to 20% of its wood supply from managed forest assets and must purchase 80% to 85% from independent Iberian smallholders, creating structural vulnerability to upstream raw material inflation that vertically integrated global peers do not face
2.
Competitive Position: The top five hardwood pulp producers control approximately 56% of global capacity, with Suzano maintaining undisputed leadership at a 29% share, while Altri controls just 2% of global BHKP capacity. The company occupies a precarious middle position–too small to compete on absolute scale with Latin American giants, yet positioned as a pure-play cellulosic fiber producer without downstream paper or tissue integration, exposing it directly to volatile global pulp market pricing. This structural disadvantage manifests acutely in European markets, where realized prices are heavily compressed by commercial discounts that reached 52% to 53% in 2026, widening significantly from historical levels of 41% to 45% in 2024. The competitive threat intensifies as Suzano maintains production 450,000 to 500,000 tonnes below capacity through 2026, removing approximately 1% of total global hardwood pulp demand, while South American greenfield mill additions, including Arauco's 3.5 million tonne Sucuriú project starting late 2027 and Suzano's 2.55 million tonne Cerrado mill added in 2024, present severe oversupply risks. Against these macro headwinds, Altri's specialized dissolving pulp strategy offers partial insulation, as dissolving wood pulp averages a historical 41% net price premium over Bleached Hardwood Kraft Pulp and customers face deep lock-in due to multi-month product qualification cycles, with long-term demand structurally supported by textile regulations favoring wood-based fibers over synthetic alternatives

The investment case ultimately rests on whether Altri's specialized dissolving pulp positioning and sustainable textile pivot can offset the structural disadvantages of limited scale, low wood self-sufficiency, and punitive European discount dynamics in a market increasingly dominated by low-cost South American capacity additions and Chinese backward integration.
