Executive Summary

Hexatronic Group's financial performance over the past three years highlights a significant transition from the peak of the fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) expansion to a period characterized by market softness and subsequent strategic diversification into Data Center and Harsh Environment end-markets. Historically, the company delivered exceptional financial performance, boasting a five-year compound annual sales growth rate of 33% and an EBITA growth rate of 43% leading up to the market shift. However, this period of record sales was abruptly followed by a steep downturn that began in the second half of 2023, with the company facing severe deceleration in its core Fiber Solutions business throughout 2024 and into early 2025, driven by weakened FTTH demand, industry-wide overcapacity, and intense price pressure across European and North American markets.

The critical inflection point occurred in Q2 2025, when the company missed EBITA expectations by approximately 26% due to severe weakness and intense price pressure in the Fiber Solutions segment. This miss forced a comprehensive strategic reset. Management initiated a comprehensive performance improvement program aimed at consolidating the European manufacturing footprint and adjusting capacity in the United States, with these decisive actions combined with conservative forward guidance allowing the company to begin outperforming consensus estimates in the second half of the year.

The diversification strategy has proven remarkably successful. The Data Center segment maintained strong momentum with 39% organic growth in Q3 2025, culminating in a massive 62% organic growth rate in Q4 2025. Similarly, the Harsh Environment segment delivered consecutive quarters of 15% organic growth in Q3 and Q4 2025. By the end of 2025, the Data Center and Harsh Environment segments combined to generate 60% of the group's adjusted EBITA.

Earnings Quality Rating: Yellow

The earnings quality assessment reveals a stark dichotomy between volatile reported profits and exceptionally strong underlying cash generation. On the positive side, Hexatronic demonstrates formidable cash conversion efficiency, with operating cash flow reaching SEK 349 million in Q4 2025, equating to an exceptional cash conversion rate of 235%. Working capital management has been exemplary, with Q4 2025 working capital changes contributing a positive SEK 201 million to cash flow, including a SEK 144 million positive effect from inventory reduction and a SEK 94 million benefit from efficient accounts receivable collection.

However, significant concerns exist. During Q3 2025 alone, the company recognized SEK 202 million in non-recurring items related to severance, facility consolidation, transition costs, and write-downs of tangible assets and inventory. Q4 2025 added SEK 96 million in non-recurring costs. The Q4 2025 reported EBITA margin fell to 2.0%, while the underlying adjusted EBITA margin stood at 7.2%. Gross margin profile has experienced significant compression from historical highs above 43% down to 37.5% by the end of 2025.

Forward Guidance Credibility Rating: Yellow-Green

Following the Q2 2025 forecasting failure, management has demonstrably improved its guidance framework. The company adopted a markedly more cautious guidance framework reflecting a highly realistic view of present conditions and abandoning previous assumptions that the FTTH softness was merely a temporary, cyclical issue. By setting highly achievable targets, the company subsequently delivered an 8.6% revenue beat and a 10.8% EBITA beat in Q4 2025.

The structured rollout of cost-reduction programs with a short cash payback period of approximately 1.1 years lends strong credibility to management's cost-reduction guidance. However, achieving aggressive Data Center sales targets is heavily dependent on inorganic growth, requiring three to six acquisitions per year, which introduces substantial execution risk and capital allocation concerns related to cash flow financing capability.

Major Red Flags include the closure of the Weterings duct facility in the Netherlands, acquired for EUR 5.6 million in 2021, marking a clear example of capital destruction from a poorly timed acquisition. Additionally, operating cash flow conversion has averaged below 80% over the past five years, indicating potential discrepancies between reported earnings and actual cash generation.

The second quarter of 2025 served as a notable negative inflection point, where the company missed EBITA expectations by approximately 26% due to unexpected weakness and severe price pressure in the Fiber Solutions segment. Earnings Trajectory

Hexatronic's earnings trajectory over the past three years reflects a profound structural transition, moving from a hyper-growth phase driven by fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) rollouts to a strategic pivot toward Data Center and Harsh Environment end-markets.

The FTTH Boom and Inflection PointHistorically, Hexatronic delivered exceptional financial performance, boasting a five-year compound annual sales growth rate of 33% and an EBITA growth rate of 43% leading up to the market shift. During the peak years of 2022 and early 2023, a strong macro environment and widespread fiber infrastructure investments provided exceptional top-line expansion. However, this period of record sales was abruptly followed by a steep downturn that began in the second half of 2023. Throughout 2024 and into early 2025, the company faced a severe deceleration in its core Fiber Solutions business. This contraction was driven by weakened FTTH demand, industry-wide overcapacity, and intense price pressure across European and North American markets.

The Mid-2025 Inflection PointThe middle of 2025 served as the critical inflection point for Hexatronic's earnings trajectory. In the second half of 2025, Fiber Solutions experienced significant organic sales declines of 23% in North America and 12% in Europe. This forced management to initiate a comprehensive performance improvement program and structurally reposition the firm. By the third quarter of 2025, the overarching trajectory began to shift positively as the company recorded a consolidated organic sales growth of 2%. While Fiber Solutions still declined by 7% organically in Q3 2025, this was entirely offset by surging demand in the company's diversification segments.

Growth Acceleration in Niche MarketsThe standout driver of Hexatronic's recent revenue acceleration has been the Data Center business area. After growing organically by 41% in the first quarter of 2025, the segment maintained strong momentum with 39% organic growth in the third quarter. Redeye analysts highlight that this momentum culminated in a massive 62% organic growth rate for the Data Center segment in Q4 2025, marking a clear acceleration from the roughly 40% run rate observed in previous quarters.

Similarly, the Harsh Environment segment established a robust growth trajectory, delivering consecutive quarters of 15% organic growth in Q3 and Q4 2025.

Redeye research views this trajectory as a successful transition from a commoditized FTTH play into a diversified niche fiber powerhouse, noting that these high-margin segments are already fueling group-level recovery.

EBITDA and EPS DynamicsWhile top-line growth has stabilized and returned to positive territory—reaching 10% organic growth in Q4 2025—the profit trajectory reflects the ongoing cost of this transition. The company's adjusted EBITA margin compressed from historical peaks of over 15% down to 7.7% in Q3 2025 and 7.2% in Q4 2025. This margin compression is primarily attributable to low capacity utilization and adverse pricing dynamics within the Fiber Solutions factories. Furthermore, the EPS trajectory has been severely impacted by substantial non-recurring restructuring costs, leading to a reported net loss and an EPS of SEK -0.59 in Q3 2025 and SEK -0.27 in Q4 2025. Despite these bottom-line pressures, the underlying operational trajectory shows that Hexatronic has successfully replaced its fading FTTH revenue with highly profitable Data Center and Harsh Environment sales, fundamentally altering its future earnings profile.

Quality of Earnings

Hexatronic's earnings quality over the past three years reveals a stark dichotomy between volatile reported profits—which have been heavily burdened by restructuring charges and non-recurring items—and exceptionally strong underlying cash generation. The company has navigated a transition period where non-recurring costs masked the true operational cash flow capabilities of the business.

Recurring vs. One-Off Items and Adjustments Trend

The gap between Hexatronic's reported and adjusted earnings widened significantly during the second half of 2025. Facing severe market headwinds and overcapacity in the Fiber Solutions segment, management launched a comprehensive performance improvement program in the third quarter of 2025. During Q3 2025 alone, the company recognized SEK 202 million in non-recurring items related to severance, facility consolidation, transition costs, and the write-down of tangible assets and inventory.

In the fourth quarter of 2025, Hexatronic expanded this program to address its North American footprint, specifically targeting its duct manufacturing facility in South Carolina. This expansion resulted in an additional SEK 67 million in non-recurring costs for the quarter. Consequently, total non-recurring items in Q4 2025 amounted to SEK 96 million. It is important to note from an earnings quality perspective that a significant portion of these charges were non-cash items, including approximately SEK 58 million of the Clinton restructuring costs. Due to these adjustments, the Q4 2025 reported EBITA margin fell to 2.0%, while the underlying adjusted EBITA margin stood at a much healthier 7.2%.

Working Capital Management

Hexatronic's working capital dynamics underwent a distinct shift over the L3Y period, transitioning from inventory accumulation to aggressive and successful destocking. Earlier in 2025, the company faced working capital pressure as accounts receivable increased by SEK 204 million in the first quarter, driven by higher sales and a customer mix that demanded slightly longer payment terms. Concurrently, inventory levels swelled ahead of the summer months.

However, management initiated strict working capital controls that yielded substantial benefits in the latter half of the year. By the third quarter of 2025, active efforts reduced inventory by SEK 68 million, primarily within the Fiber Solutions area. This optimization accelerated in Q4 2025, where working capital changes contributed a positive SEK 201 million to cash flow. This included a SEK 144 million positive effect from inventory reduction and a SEK 94 million benefit from highly efficient accounts receivable collection.

Redeye research notes that Hexatronic has demonstrated efficient inventory and accounts receivable management, maintaining both metrics well below 20% and outperforming their respective five-year averages.

Cash Conversion Efficiency

The ultimate testament to Hexatronic's earnings quality is its formidable cash conversion rate, which has consistently provided the financial flexibility needed to weather top-line softness. Operating cash flow for the full year 2024 was robust at SEK 921 million. This strength carried through the 2025 transition period, with the company achieving a 117% cash conversion rate in Q3 2025 on operating cash flows of SEK 154 million.

The cash generation profile peaked in the fourth quarter of 2025, where operating cash flow reached SEK 349 million. This performance equated to an exceptional cash conversion rate of 235%. This superior cash generation allowed Hexatronic to self-fund strategic acquisitions—such as Communication Zone—while simultaneously reducing interest-bearing net debt to SEK 1.58 billion and maintaining a comfortable leverage ratio of 1.9x.

Redeye analysts emphasize that Hexatronic's strong cash flow from operations has effectively removed any market concerns regarding the potential need for dilutive share issues to fund operations.

D&A Trends and Capacity Investments

Another crucial component of Hexatronic's earnings quality relates to depreciation and amortization (D&A) trends following an era of heavy capital expenditure. During 2022 and 2023, the company invested heavily in capacity expansion, most notably constructing a new duct manufacturing facility in Ogden, Utah. As these facilities came online concurrently with a market downturn, the fixed costs and increased D&A created a structural drag on reported margins. For example, depreciation as a percentage of sales increased by 0.6 percentage points year-over-year in Q2 2025, reaching 4.8%.

However, this elevated depreciation schedule is paired with structurally lower future capital requirements. Management has confirmed that after completing the Utah investment program, Hexatronic can grow for several years without extensive investments in Fiber Solutions. Going forward, baseline capital expenditures are projected to drop to just 3% to 4% of total sales, with only 1% to 2% required for strict maintenance. This trend was already highly visible in late 2025, where Q4 total CapEx fell to merely SEK 27 million, representing just 1.5% of sales. Ultimately, the combination of elevated non-cash depreciation and suppressed capital expenditure requirements structurally enhances Hexatronic's forward free cash flow generation.

Revenue vs Profit Growth

The relationship between Hexatronic's revenue growth and profitability has undergone a dramatic decoupling over the past three years. While the company historically delivered profit growth that exceeded top-line expansion through operational leverage and positive margin trajectory, the recent market cycle has exposed severe operational leverage sensitivities within its legacy manufacturing operations. However, a rapid mix-shift toward asset-light, high-margin segments is fundamentally restructuring the group's margin sustainability.

Gross Margin Contraction and Utilization Pressures

Hexatronic's gross margin profile has experienced significant compression, dropping from historical highs above 43% down to 37.5% by the end of 2025. This deterioration is almost entirely attributable to the Fiber Solutions business, which has suffered from a toxic combination of severe price pressure on duct products and dramatically lower capacity utilization. The company invested heavily in factory capacity during the 2022-2023 boom, meaning the subsequent volume declines have resulted in poor fixed-cost coverage and margin dilution. Furthermore, this gross margin squeeze was exacerbated by unfavorable exchange rate dynamics as higher-margin North American FTTH sales decelerated.

Seasonal Patterns

Hexatronic's consolidated financial performance is subject to distinct seasonal variations, primarily driven by weather conditions and calendar effects. Historically, the second and third quarters are the strongest, while the first and fourth quarters exhibit lower activity levels. During the hyper-growth phase of 2021 to mid-2023, extreme market demand temporarily masked these seasonal effects, leading to unusually strong Q4 and Q1 results. However, as market conditions have normalized, the traditional seasonal patterns have reasserted themselves.

Redeye analysts confirm that the company's seasonal pattern, characterized by a soft Q4 and Q1 and a strong Q2 and Q3, has returned and significantly dictates short-term margin fluctuations.

Fiber Solutions: Weather and Calendar ConstraintsThe Fiber Solutions segment, which accounts for the majority of group sales, is highly sensitive to weather conditions. Operations in key markets like North America and Europe experience slowdowns in Q4 and Q1 because frozen ground and harsh weather conditions impede civil engineering and trenching work required for fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) rollouts. Additionally, the fourth quarter is negatively impacted by the holiday season in December, which results in fewer working days and customers actively trying to reduce their year-end inventory levels. Because Hexatronic's manufacturing facilities have high fixed costs, these lower seasonal volumes inevitably result in reduced capacity utilization, which depresses EBITA margins during the winter quarters. Furthermore, this seasonality dictates working capital rhythms, as the company typically builds inventory during the first half of the year to manage the higher sales volumes expected in Q3 and Q4.

Data Center: Inverted SeasonalityThe Data Center business area displays an inverted seasonal profile, typically operating with higher activity during the first half of the year. Management notes that the first quarter is historically the strongest for this segment. Conversely, the second half of the year, and particularly the fourth quarter, is seasonally softer. The primary driver for Data Center's Q4 softness is the reduced number of working days in December, which limits the time available for service-heavy data center installations.

Redeye research highlights that the Data Center segment's fourth quarter is traditionally softer, with margins likely to cool during the second half of the year compared to the extra high profitability often observed during the first half.

Harsh Environment: Project-Based VolatilityIn contrast to the other divisions, the Harsh Environment segment is generally unaffected by traditional weather or calendar-based seasonal patterns. Rather, this segment is highly project-driven, meaning that its revenue and profit fluctuations are dictated by the timing of large deliveries. For example, significant submarine cable shipments or major defense orders can create lumpiness, causing individual quarters to spike or drop independently of the broader group's seasonality. Consequently, management advises evaluating Harsh Environment's performance on a long-term, year-over-year basis rather than relying on sequential quarterly trends.

Beat/Miss Pattern

Hexatronic's historical beat and miss pattern over the analyzed reporting periods reveals a distinct tale of two differing forecasting environments. As the company transitioned away from the peak of the fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) boom, its ability to accurately guide the market underwent severe stress testing before stabilizing.

Looking at the six most recent quarters where consensus estimates were tracked, the company exhibited a mixed track record. For revenue, Hexatronic delivered positive surprises in approximately 33% of these periods (calculated). The average absolute revenue surprise magnitude was approximately 4.05% (calculated). On the profitability side, adjusted EBITA met or exceeded market expectations in 50% of the tracked periods (calculated). The average absolute EBITA surprise magnitude was significantly more volatile at approximately 8.97% (calculated).

The second quarter of 2025 stands out as the most severe forecasting failure during this window. During that period, the company missed EBITA expectations by a staggering 25.9% due to an unexpected, sharp contraction in Fiber Solutions profitability. This severe miss forced a complete reset of both internal forecasting and external market expectations.

Redeye research emphasizes that management was genuinely surprised by this weak development, noting that there were no prior signs of the deteriorating to that extent. The analysts observe that following this negative surprise, the company adopted a markedly more cautious guidance framework. This updated communication strategy reflects a highly realistic view of present conditions and abandons previous assumptions that the FTTH softness was merely a temporary, cyclical issue.

Consequently, management's historical tendency to inadvertently over-promise during the transition out of the fiber boom has recently morphed into a conservative approach bordering on sandbagging. By setting highly achievable targets and maintaining a cautiously optimistic tone regarding market rebounds, Hexatronic successfully lowered the bar for the second half of 2025. This strategy yielded immediate results, as the company subsequently delivered an 8.6% revenue beat and a 10.8% EBITA beat in the fourth quarter.

Redeye analysts highlight that these recent consecutive beats were heavily fueled by the Data Center division dramatically outperforming conservative expectations. The broker points out that Q4 2025 delivered a massive 36% revenue beat specifically within the Data Center division, demonstrating that management's newer, cautious guidance style allows for significant positive operational surprises. Ultimately, while Hexatronic struggled to guide accurately through the initial FTTH downturn, their recent pattern indicates a deliberate pivot toward under-promising and over-delivering.

Key Changes at Each Reporting Period

An analysis of Hexatronic's last six reporting periods (Q3 2024 through Q4 2025) reveals a volatile narrative characterized by false dawns, severe market corrections, and an ultimately successful strategic pivot. The periods document a profound shift in management tone from cautious optimism regarding legacy fiber markets to aggressive restructuring and a full embrace of Data Center growth.

Q3 2024: The Calm Before the Storm

During the third quarter of 2024, Hexatronic exhibited substantially improved profitability with adjusted EBITA margins reaching 11.8%. Net sales grew by 2%, and management expressed cautious optimism that the market would stabilize in 2025. Positive surprises in the quarter included the Harsh Environment segment growing by 47% and the Data Center segment growing by 8% year-over-year. However, the market was negatively surprised by unexpectedly weak demand and intense price pressure in the European Fiber Solutions business. Consequently, management's guidance for softer Q4 and Q1 activity tempered near-term expectations.

Q4 2024: A False Dawn in Fiber

The fourth quarter of 2024 initially appeared to validate management's stabilization thesis, as Fiber Solutions returned to a 2% growth rate after prior declines. Adjusted EBITA margins held steady at 10.7%, and operating cash flow was exceptionally strong. To better highlight the diversification strategy, management introduced a new segment reporting structure for 2025, dividing the company into Fiber Solutions, Harsh Environment, and Data Center. The market was positively surprised by a remarkable 150% cash conversion rate during the quarter. Conversely, Harsh Environment revenues fell by 20% due to tough prior-year comparisons, dampening overall top-line momentum. Furthermore, the board proposed no dividend, which disappointed some investors.

Sell-Side Estimate Revisions

The past three years of sell-side estimate revisions for Hexatronic highlight a dramatic shift in analyst sentiment, moving from severe downgrades during the FTTH market collapse to cautious upward revisions driven by the recent Data Center boom.

Late 2023 to Early 2024: The Initial Downgrade Cycle

In late 2023, analysts were forced to rapidly recalibrate their models as the FTTH market abruptly deteriorated. Following management's short-term guidance downgrade in November 2023, Redeye slashed its 2024 sales and EBITA forecasts by 13% and 21%, respectively. This pessimistic trend briefly reversed following the Q4 2023 report, where strong cash flow and stabilization signs prompted analysts to raise 2024 and 2025 EBITA forecasts by approximately 8%.

Mid-2024 to Early 2025: False Dawns and Choppy Adjustments

Through mid-2024, analysts struggled with poor visibility regarding the exact timing of an FTTH market rebound. Following the Q2 2024 report, Redeye temporarily raised its EBITA forecasts by 7% to 16% on hopes that the US market would firmly recover in late 2024. However, this optimism faded by the first quarter of 2025, as continued macroeconomic uncertainty and severe currency headwinds forced brokers to cut 2025 and 2026 EBITA estimates by 9% to 11%.

Mid-2025: The Q2 Capitulation

The most drastic downgrade cycle occurred in response to Hexatronic's surprisingly weak second-quarter 2025 preliminary figures. Analysts were caught off guard by the severity of the Fiber Solutions contraction, leading to a massive capitulation in forward estimates. Redeye aggressively cut its long-term sales forecasts by up to 28% and slashed its 2025 through 2027 EBITA estimates by 16% to 18%. Consequently, Redeye's Base Case valuation was severely downgraded from SEK 50 to SEK 24 per share, reflecting a permanent structural reset of FTTH market expectations.

Late 2025 to Early 2026: The Data Center Upgrade Cycle

The narrative and estimate trajectory shifted positively in the second half of 2025 as Hexatronic's strategic diversification took hold. Following the Q3 2025 report, Redeye analysts confidently raised their 2026 and 2027 EBITA forecasts by 3% to 6%, driven entirely by unexpected momentum in the Data Center segment. This upward momentum continued into late 2025, with management's acquisition of a US data center services firm prompting another 4% to 5% increase in out-year EBITA and EPS forecasts. After the strong Q4 2025 results confirmed an acceleration in Data Center organic growth, Redeye implemented further upward revisions, raising group sales and EBITA forecasts for 2026 through 2028 by an additional 2% to 6%. By March 2026, the acquisition of JOWO in the Harsh Environment segment fueled another 2% to 3% increase in EBITA and EPS estimates. Ultimately, the timeline demonstrates that while legacy fiber estimates have been permanently rebased lower, analysts are now actively upgrading the group's earnings potential based on its successfully diversified niche segments.
