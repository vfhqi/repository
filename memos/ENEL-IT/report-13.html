§1 — Current Management Guidance

Enel's current forward guidance is anchored on the November 2024 Capital Markets Day Strategic Plan covering FY2024–2026, supplemented by Q1-2025 and FY2024 results releases. The plan has not been formally revised since CMD-Nov-24.

  • Net verdict: Management has provided comprehensive quantitative targets through FY26 — EBITDA, ordinary net income, FFO, net debt/EBITDA, and DPS — with the FY25 intermediate milestone on-track per Q1-2025 datapoints [#J]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management guides FY25 EBITDA of ~€21.5–22.0bn (mid-point ~€21.7bn) as an intermediate milestone toward FY26 target of €22.8bn; represents ~4–5% organic growth on FY24 reported €21.0bn [#A]

  • FY current — ordinary net income: FY25 guidance ~€6.8–7.0bn; FY26 target €7.1bn; implies ~8% cumulative 2024–2026 CAGR from FY23 base of ~€6.5bn [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: Management targets ordinary EPS of €0.53–0.55 for FY25, rising to ~€0.56–0.58 by FY26; based on ~19.4bn diluted share count (broadly stable) [#A]

  • FY current — FCF / FFO: FFO guided ~€14–15bn for FY25 (cumulative 2024–2026 FFO ~€43bn); this underpins the leverage deleverage path and dividend coverage [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: Total gross CapEx guidance ~€35bn over FY24–26 (cumulative), roughly €11–12bn annually; weighted toward regulated networks (~50%) and renewables (~35%) [#A]

  • FY current — net debt: Net debt target ≤€55bn at FY25, declining to ≤€52bn by FY26; net debt/EBITDA target ≤2.4× by FY26 [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY26): EBITDA €22.8bn, ordinary net income €7.1bn, DPS growth ~7% p.a. (minimum), net debt/EBITDA ≤2.4× [#A]

  • Medium-term — renewables CapEx: 15GW of new renewables capacity additions by FY26 as the physical delivery metric underpinning green EBITDA growth; FY24 delivery ~4.8GW was below the linear pace needed [#A]

  • Source — investor day: All major targets from CMD November 2024; no material written guidance revisions since [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1-2025 call (May 2025) confirmed "on-track" for FY25 EBITDA milestone with Q1 EBITDA ~€5.2bn (+6% y/y organic), broadly consistent with full-year pacing [#A]

  • Update timing: Next formal guidance update expected FY25 H1 results (August 2025); CMD cycle typically every 2 years (next CMD expected November 2026) [#I]

  • Caveat — currency: All targets stated at budget FX rates; Latam FX is not explicitly hedged beyond 12 months — plan assumes broadly stable BRL/EUR and CLP/EUR [#A]

  • Caveat — regulatory: Italian network tariff period runs through FY27; no adverse regulatory reset assumed within plan horizon [#I]

  • Conditional assumption: CapEx target assumes Italian permitting acceleration for renewables (grid connection procedures) materialises as per government commitments — a conditional assumption with structural risk of slippage [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that FY26 EBITDA of €22.8bn requires ~4.5% organic growth annually against a backdrop where Italian and Spanish retail margins are normalising post-energy-crisis; the "growth" in guidance may be more regulatory indexation (Italy RAB step-up) than genuine operational expansion — the absolute number looks achievable but the quality of earnings growth matters for re-rating [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net verdict: Enel's pattern is conditional sandbagger on income metrics (EBITDA/EPS) and honest tracker to mild over-promiser on balance-sheet metrics (net debt/CapEx); the duality is persistent across L4 periods and prior CMD cycles [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: In FY21 and FY24, EBITDA met or beat; in FY23 it narrowly missed by 2.3%; in FY22 it missed materially but in extraordinary circumstances; the base pattern outside crisis years is consistent underpromise [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal — EPS: EPS ordinario has a slight positive bias in normal years (+2–6% beats vs guidance); management's stated preference for "conservative and achievable" guidance is consistent with Bernstein and Mediobanca commentary calling Enel a "mild sandbagger" on earnings [AS-Bernstein] [AS-Mediobanca] [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — net debt: Net debt targets have been missed in 2 of 4 periods; the miss pattern is consistent — CapEx is deployed or working capital events absorb liquidity faster than modelled [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — CapEx deployment pace: Renewables CapEx deployment has consistently lagged annual targets by 5–15% due to permitting delays; this is chronic not acute [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — FCF: FCF/FFO is the most volatile metric; it was the key FY22 miss driver but has recovered cleanly in FY23–24; pattern is inconsistent, reflecting commodity-driven working-capital volatility more than execution quality [#A]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA ex-crisis: 3 of 3 non-crisis years beat or met EBITDA guidance; this is a strong base-rate signal for the current plan [#A]

  • Pattern shift: FY24 delivered the first clean across-the-board (EBITDA, earnings, net debt) guidance hit in 3 years; this may indicate management has recalibrated guidance conservatism post-FY22 crisis [AS-UBS] [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: EBITDA beats in normal years average +1.5–3%; EPS beats average +2.5–4% — modest, not reckless [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: The FY22 EBITDA miss (−12.3%) is the only material miss in 6+ years of CMD cycles; all other deviations are within ±5% [#A]

  • Improving accuracy — post-FY22: Management restructured guidance framing post-FY22 — introduced explicit scenario ranges for Latam FX sensitivity and separated "ordinary" from "reported" metrics more clearly; this structural improvement in guidance hygiene is credibility-positive [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the "sandbagger" label on earnings may be running out of cushion — Italian network RAB growth (the structural income tailwind) is already embedded in consensus, and the normalisation of retail energy margins post-crisis removes a recurring source of upside surprise; the guidance cushion may be thinner than the L4P record implies [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Net verdict: Guidance accuracy has demonstrably improved from the FY21–22 disruption cycle toward FY23–24 normalisation; the trend is structurally positive but depends on commodity-market stability persisting [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — FY24: Clean delivery across EBITDA, earnings, and net debt for the first time since FY20; represents the strongest single-year guidance hit in the current CMD cycle [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FY21–22: FY21 was a net beat on income (miss on net debt); FY22 was a broad miss driven by energy-crisis exogenous shock — the worst accuracy point in at least a 6-year window [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — structural reason: Post-FY22, Enel simplified its business portfolio (disposal of Enel Americas stake reduction, Endesa simplification, Russia/Romania exits) reducing idiosyncratic EM risk; fewer moving parts structurally improves guidance tractability [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — guidance hygiene: Introduction of explicit FX sensitivity ranges (€/BRL scenario) and "ordinario vs reported" metric discipline since CMD-Nov-22 reduces the noise between guided and actual numbers [AS-Citi] [#I]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Ordinary EPS is the metric with best guidance accuracy — ±3% average in normal years; the regulated earnings floor provides a natural dampener on forecast error [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Net debt guidance has the worst accuracy track record — average miss of +€1.5bn (vs target) across L4P; driven by CapEx timing and working-capital unpredictability [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Energy-commodity spike (FY22) was the dominant macro driver of guidance miss; Enel's earnings remain exposed to commodity basis risk through Spanish/Italian retail even in regulated mix [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Renewables permitting delays (Italy, Spain) are the primary execution driver of CapEx slippage; this is a structural Italian bureaucratic issue, not unique to Enel [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: Latam subsidiaries (Chile, Colombia, Peru, Brazil) add FX translation complexity that is genuinely hard to guide precisely at 2-year horizons; management's FX sensitivity disclosures help but don't eliminate this risk [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend improvement is that the FY23–24 recovery coincides with normalising energy markets (commodity tailwind unwinding = lower distortion) and Italian network RAB step-up providing a mechanical income tailwind; accuracy improvement may be cyclically-assisted rather than reflecting a structural upgrade in management forecasting capability [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Net verdict: The current strategic plan rests on 4–5 assumptions that are individually plausible but jointly fragile: Italian network RAB growth, Latam FX stability, renewables permitting acceleration, input-cost containment, and energy retail margin normalisation [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — network (Italy): Assumes Italian RAB (Regulated Asset Base) grows ~4–5% p.a. through FY26 under ARERA's regulatory framework; historically reliable but subject to regulatory reset risk at FY27 review [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — renewables: Plan assumes 4–5GW new renewables additions p.a. through FY26; FY24 delivered ~4.8GW — below the linear pace required; assumes Italian/Spanish permitting acceleration, which has structural risk [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — Latam: Assumes BRL stable at ~5.5–6.0/EUR and CLP at ~1,100–1,150/EUR over 2024–26; both currencies have historically volatile purchasing periods; no multi-year hedges disclosed [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — retail pricing: Assumes Italian and Spanish retail energy margin normalises at €15–20/MWh (above pre-crisis levels) — plausible but dependent on wholesale electricity price stability [AS-Mediobanca] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Assumes O&M cost inflation of ~2.5–3% p.a. (broadly tracking Italian/Spanish CPI); no major supply-chain disruption assumed; grid investment costs tracked to regulatory allowed returns [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Plan models operating leverage from network capacity additions and renewables commissioning; leverage benefit is €0.3–0.5bn incremental EBITDA per GW commissioned — delivery-dependent [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Assumes effective group tax rate of ~24–26%; Italian windfall tax (expired FY22) assumed not reinstated; any reintroduction would reduce ordinary EPS by ~€0.02–0.03/share [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Assumes ~19.4bn diluted shares (no buyback; no new equity issuance assumed within plan); dividend reinvestment plan (scrip) adds ~0.5% dilution annually — already embedded [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Assumes gross debt broadly stable at €60–62bn (net debt declining as FFO generated exceeds CapEx); average cost of debt ~3.5–4.0% assumed — current hedging profile provides visibility through ~FY26 [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Latam FX is the single most sensitive macro assumption; management discloses ~€400–500m EBITDA sensitivity to a 10% Latam basket depreciation [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Plan uses fixed/hedged debt costs for ~70% of gross debt through FY26; floating rate exposure is manageable; ECB rate trajectory benign for Enel's refinancing profile [AS-UBS] [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Assumes normalised electricity forward curve (Spain/Italy); no assumption of another energy-crisis spike; this is the correct base case but contains tail risk [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Net verdict: Guidance credibility is MEDIUM-HIGH on income metrics (EBITDA/EPS) with a 75% L4P hit rate outside crisis years, and MEDIUM on balance-sheet metrics (net debt/leverage) where structural overruns have been chronic [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH — management has earned reasonable credibility on ordinary earnings delivery, undermined moderately by balance-sheet guidance misses and one material EBITDA miss (FY22); higher credibility than most Italian large-cap industrials, in-line with European regulated utility median [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY24 clean delivery across all metrics is the strongest recent green flag; the first multi-metric clean hit since FY20 [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Post-FY22, management widened guidance ranges and introduced explicit scenario framing; this is structurally more conservative than the pre-FY22 approach [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Italian network business (Enel Distribuzione, highest-earnings-quality segment) has never missed EBITDA guidance in any of the L4 periods — the regulated core is genuinely credible [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Green flag — macro tailwind: Italian network RAB step-up under current ARERA regulatory period provides a mechanical 4–5% annual earnings uplift through FY26 — reducing reliance on execution to hit targets [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: Net debt has missed guidance in 2 of 4 periods; balance-sheet credibility remains impaired relative to income-statement credibility [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: The FY26 terminal-year renewables commissioning assumption (5GW+ in FY26 alone) is the most stretched in the plan, implying significant acceleration from FY24's ~4.8GW — which itself was below-plan pace [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: Latam FX and energy retail margin assumptions are the two macro risks most capable of triggering a guidance miss; the FY22 miss was partly driven by exactly this combination [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EBITDA: If guidance is off by 10% on EBITDA (down to ~€19.4bn in FY25), dividend coverage is still intact (FFO/net debt >18%, DPS still covered 1.4–1.5×) — investment thesis survives a 10% EBITDA miss at the plan level [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EPS: A 10% EPS downside (to ~€0.47–0.48 for FY25) still supports dividend of €0.43 (FY25 guided) with coverage of ~1.1× — thin but not break-even; the leverage-vs-dividend trade-off becomes the key constraint [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis is more sensitive to net debt trajectory than EBITDA; if net debt remains above €57bn by FY25 (vs ≤€55bn target), rating agencies could flag negative watch, compressing the equity multiple [AS-Morgan Stanley] [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on credibility is that the MEDIUM-HIGH verdict is backward-looking — it reflects 3 years post-FY22 restructuring during which commodity markets have been broadly supportive; apparent credibility could prove brittle if energy markets spike again; the single assumption failure that would invalidate the credibility verdict is a sustained BRL depreciation to 7.5+/EUR combined with Italian retail margin compression below €10/MWh — a plausible scenario in a risk-off EM environment [#J]
