Combined ratio underperformance: Generali's undiscounted combined ratio of 92.60% in fiscal year 2025 lags AXA's 90.60% by 200 basis points , indicating weaker underlying technical profitability in the P&C segment despite management's emphasis on disciplined underwriting. This margin shortfall is particularly concerning given Generali's retail-centric positioning and the theoretical pricing power advantages that should accrue to dominant market share in Italian personal lines. The gap suggests either higher structural expense ratios or less effective claims steering relative to global composite peers
3.
Distributor margin pressure in fragmented markets: Independent broker networks in fragmented jurisdictions like Spain and Belgium command high commission pricing power, while falling yields and price transparency in motor lines risk compressing overall channel margins . This dynamic creates a structural profitability headwind that directly contradicts the group's narrative around proprietary distribution advantages. 5.
Sovereign concentration overhang: The group remains exposed to Italian government debt linked directly to its domestic liabilities , creating a correlated tail risk between asset performance and liability cash flows that peers with more geographically diversified investment portfolios avoid. While management emphasizes that Italian sovereign exposure sits under 25% of general account assets , the absolute concentration remains elevated relative to the pan-European diversification achieved by AXA or Allianz. Any future Italian sovereign stress scenario would simultaneously compress asset values and trigger elevated policyholder lapses, amplifying capital volatility through a negative correlation channel that diversified peers structurally insulate against
