§1 — Current Management Guidance

ELEC-FR is a majority-owned EDF subsidiary operating French electricity distribution and supply in the Bas-Rhin and Moselle regions. Its investor communications are materially sparser than standalone European utility peers; formal guidance is largely absent beyond dividend and directional revenue comments.

  • Net verdict: Current formal guidance is minimal in scope — dividend stability + directional revenue growth implied; no explicit EPS, EBITDA, or medium-term margin targets are publicly committed [#J]

  • FY current — dividend: DPS commitment is the clearest guidance signal — management has consistently flagged intention to maintain/grow DPS in line with earnings; 2024A DPS was €7.50, an increase from €6.00 in FY2022, implying ~25% growth L2Y [#A]

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit revenue guidance range is published; directional commentary in H1 2024 results release indicated "continued activity growth in network services and distribution" with no quantified target [#A]

  • FY current — EBIT: No EBIT target range issued; EBIT trajectory is effectively implied by the combination of TURPE-driven network revenue + supply margin performance, neither of which is formally committed [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No EPS guidance issued — this is atypical for European utility peers; Elia Group (Belgium), RED Eléctrica (Spain), and Terna (Italy) all issue explicit EPS or net income targets with ±ranges [#A]

  • FY current — FCF / CapEx: CapEx directionals are referenced in terms of network investment programme alignment with TURPE regulatory periods; no absolute CapEx figure is formally guided; FY2023 CapEx was approximately €80-90m, in line with recent-year run-rates [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y/N3Y: No medium-term numerical targets are publicly articulated; investor-facing materials do not contain 3Y revenue CAGR, margin expansion path, or EPS CAGR — a gap versus European regulated utility norm [#A]

  • Source — written: Guidance-equivalent language appears in annual results press releases and the Document de Référence ; no formal investor day with quantified targets has been identified in the L4Y period [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: ELEC-FR does not conduct public earnings calls in the conventional sell-side format; investor interaction is limited given ~97.7% EDF ownership leaving minimal free float; this structurally suppresses the guidance-setting mechanism [#A]

  • Update timing: Results releases (H1 in September, FY in April/May) are the primary update cadence; interim trading updates do not appear to be a feature of ELEC-FR's investor communications [#A]

  • Caveat — EDF dependency: All forward views must carry an implicit caveat: EDF group strategy decisions (transfer pricing, group-level restructuring, ARENH/nuclear policy) can reset ELEC-FR's cost structure in ways management cannot pre-disclose [#J]

  • Conditional assumption — tariff: Any revenue growth assumption is conditioned on French government and CRE not implementing adverse tariff changes; the 2022-2023 energy crisis demonstrated how rapidly the regulatory framework can be modified [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that its very sparseness is itself a credibility problem — management cannot be held accountable to targets it does not set; apparent "delivery" on unquantified directional commentary is unfalsifiable, masking the possibility that embedded regulatory and EDF-dependency assumptions are quietly deteriorating [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Revenue assumption — tariff/regulatory: TURPE network tariff decisions drive ~60-65% of ELEC-FR revenue; TURPE 7 (2025+) parameters are the single largest revenue assumption; a 5% adverse TURPE outcome would compress revenue by ~€55-65m [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — supply market: Supply/retail electricity margin assumes normalised French wholesale power prices and stable regulated tariff levels; 2022-2023 demonstrated how rapidly these can destabilise (~20-30% revenue swings) [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — volume: Electricity distribution volume growth is assumed broadly stable (0-2% pa); structural demand trends (EV penetration positive, industrial demand uncertain) create modest but observable uncertainty [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Operating cost growth assumed broadly at French CPI (~2-3% pa); wage inflation and ERDF (Enedis/EDF-related) service costs are the primary uncertainty [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage is modest in a regulated network business; cost structure is predominantly fixed, so revenue growth falls through at higher-than-normal incremental margin [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: French corporate tax rate (25% headline) applied; no unusual deferred tax positions flagged; ELEC-FR's tax line is relatively predictable [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count is stable; with ~97.7% EDF ownership, buybacks and dilution are not material variables [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: French wholesale electricity price is the dominant commodity assumption; ELEC-FR's supply margin is exposed to basis risk between regulated retail tariffs and wholesale procurement costs [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Balance sheet gearing is moderate; interest cost sensitivity is not a primary risk at current debt levels [#A]
