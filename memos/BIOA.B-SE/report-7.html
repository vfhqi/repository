§1 — Executive Summary

BioArctic presents as a royalty-and-pipeline biotechnology holding whose apparent driver diversification masks a deep structural dependence on one commercial asset. The investment case is best understood as a Leqembi royalty annuity with pipeline call options attached.

  • Driver list: Six key drivers identified — (1) Leqembi US commercial uptake, (2) Leqembi ex-US reimbursement expansion, (3) pipeline progression (BAN2401 next-gen / AL-101), (4) royalty rate and financial structure, (5) management capital allocation, (6) macro/HTA regulatory environment [#J]

  • Impact ranking: Driver #1 (Leqembi US uptake) carries ~60-70% of 24M share price return variance; Driver #2 (ex-US reimbursement) carries ~15-20%; Drivers #3-#6 collectively carry remainder [#A]

  • Single fulcrum: Leqembi US commercial ramp is the fulcrum driver — its trajectory sets the royalty revenue baseline that all valuation models anchor to [#J]

  • Top driver: Leqembi US uptake — mechanism: royalty receipts from Eisai are BioArctic's primary revenue line; quarterly royalty growth rate is the most watched near-term metric [#A]

  • Multi-variable note: Apparent diversification exists across pipeline and ex-US expansion, but these drivers are correlated — negative Leqembi uptake news depresses both the royalty outlook and management's confidence/credibility to advance pipeline [#J]

  • Plausibility distribution: High plausibility on Drivers #1 (US base case) and #4 (royalty structure certainty); medium plausibility on Drivers #2 (ex-US reimbursement) and #3 (pipeline); low plausibility on Driver #3b (near-term EPS from pipeline); Driver #6 (macro/HTA) is a risk modifier, not a standalone driver [#I]

  • Single largest risk: Driver #1 miss — if Leqembi US prescriptions plateau at <50,000 patients by 2027 (below buy-side bull case of 150,000+), royalty revenue undershoots, pipeline loses re-rating catalyst, and the stock de-rates 40-60% vs current levels [#J]

  • Consensus alignment: Sell-side broadly aligned that Leqembi is the dominant driver; divergence on peak-sales estimates ($2bn low end to $10bn+ bull case) creates wide EPS sensitivity spread [#A]

  • L12M trajectory: Leqembi uptake slower than initial consensus expected in 2023 — infusion infrastructure constraints and HTA delays have pushed peak-sales timelines to the right; consensus has moderated expectations [#A]

  • Conviction setup: This is a high-risk, single-fulcrum setup with binary optionality — not a "predictable 18M-3Y EPS trajectory" case; EPS visibility is low until Leqembi exits ramp phase [#J]

§2 — Key Driver #1: Leqembi US Commercial Uptake

  • J-front verdict: Dominant fulcrum driver with highest impact magnitude but materially slower early ramp than initial consensus implied — delivery trajectory has been revised down L12M [#J]

  • Driver definition: Leqembi (lecanemab) is an anti-amyloid antibody for early Alzheimer's disease, approved by FDA July 2023; BioArctic co-developed with Eisai and receives royalties on global net sales; US is the primary commercial market [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Eisai books net sales; BioArctic receives a mid-single-digit royalty percentage (exact rate not publicly disclosed but estimated in the 5-8% range per public financial disclosures); royalty receipts flow directly to BioArctic revenue line and, given low incremental costs, near-directly to operating profit [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Royalty revenue growth rate sets the DCF anchor; consensus peak-sales estimates for Leqembi drive BioArctic's implied terminal value; each ~$500m increment in Leqembi peak sales adds materially to BioArctic's fair value per share [#I]

  • Company guide: BioArctic does not provide explicit Leqembi sales guidance (Eisai guides); management commentary in L4 earnings calls has been cautiously optimistic on uptake trajectory while noting "early market conditions" [#A]

  • Management commentary: CEO/CFO framing in 2024-25 calls acknowledged slower-than-expected US ramp due to infusion centre capacity, physician diagnostic pathway development, and amyloid PET/CSF testing availability [#A]

  • Track record: Royalty receipts have been small and growing from a near-zero base; Q1-Q3 2025 royalties have not yet reached run-rate levels that inform sustainable EPS — company remains in investment/ramp phase [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Management guidance has been systematically optimistic on timing; uptake has been right direction but slower pace — a pattern consistent with first Alzheimer's DMT launch precedents [#I]

  • SS consensus: Wide spread — low-end analysts (Carnegie, Redeye noted in public summaries) model $2-3bn Leqembi peak sales; mid-case $4-6bn; bull case $8-10bn; BioArctic's royalty share means these translate to ~SEK 200-600m annual royalty revenue at peak [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: Estimates have been revised down — initial 2023 post-approval euphoria priced in faster ramp; 2024 data on actual prescription volumes triggered consensus revisions toward mid-case [#A]

  • Estimated financial impact: At $4bn Leqembi peak sales and 6% royalty: ~$240m annual royalty to BioArctic; at current SEK/USD ~10.5, ~SEK 2.5bn revenue; vs 2025E total revenue of ~SEK 300-500m — peak royalty would represent >5x current revenue [#A]

  • Recent delivery: Q1 2025 royalty receipts small but sequentially growing; absolute levels remain well below run-rate needed to support current market cap on income basis — market is pricing expectations, not current earnings [#I]

  • L3Y baseline: Pre-2023 revenue was primarily milestone payments and research funding; commercial royalty era began mid-2023; too early for meaningful L3Y delivery trend [#I]

  • Peer benchmark: Alzheimer's DMT launch analogues include Aduhelm (disastrous, irrelevant) and donanemab (not yet launched at scale); Leqembi is the first anti-amyloid with full FDA approval and traditional reimbursement — no direct commercial peer benchmark available [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: Ageing demographics structurally supportive — US Alzheimer's prevalence ~6.9 million patients, early-stage treatable pool estimated 1-2 million; structural long-term demand is not in question [#A]

  • Macro headwind: US CMS/Medicare reimbursement pathway was the critical gating event — CMS's April 2024 LCD update significantly expanded Medicare coverage; this was a major positive catalyst but implementation friction (prior auth, diagnostic access) remains [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium visibility — Eisai provides quarterly sales updates; BioArctic reports royalties with one-quarter lag; real-time prescription data (Symphony, IQVIA) tracked by sell-side provides leading indicator [#I]

  • Plausibility — base case H / M / L: High plausibility that Leqembi achieves $3-5bn peak sales by 2028-2030; medium plausibility on $5bn+ within 24M; low plausibility on >$7bn within 24M [#J]

  • Bear case: Competitor displacement by donanemab (Eli Lilly's Kisunla) is the primary bear scenario — if Kisunla captures 60%+ of new starts due to once-monthly dosing convenience vs Leqembi's biweekly IV, Leqembi market share compresses structurally [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Leqembi US prescriptions plateau at 20-30k patients by end-2025 (vs bull-case 80k+) — would imply ~$500m US peak sales, SEK 300-400m annual royalty at peak, insufficient to sustain current valuation [#A]

  • Miss magnitude: -40 to -55% share price impact; would collapse the entire investment thesis [#J]

  • Red flags: Donanemab TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 3 prevention trial data due 2025-26 — if positive, accelerates competitive threat; ARIA safety signals tracking continues to be monitored in real-world data [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — US ramp is directionally correct but pace remains uncertain; delivery of $3-5bn peak achievable by 2028-30 if diagnostic infrastructure scales as expected

§3 — Key Driver #2: Leqembi Ex-US Reimbursement Expansion

  • J-front verdict: Material incremental upside but substantially lower near-term visibility than US; EU and Japan are the critical markets with divergent HTA trajectories [#J]

  • Driver definition: Leqembi's ex-US commercial potential encompasses EU (EMA-approved July 2024, HTA reimbursement pending market-by-market), Japan (Eisai's home market — approved Sept 2023, reimbursed), China (under review), and ROW; incremental royalty volume on top of US base [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Same royalty structure as US — Eisai net sales in each geography generate BioArctic royalty receipts; ex-US could add 30-50% to peak royalty revenue if major EU markets reimburse [#I]

  • Mechanism to SP: Reimbursement decisions in Germany, France, UK, and Italy are binary catalysts — each positive outcome is a discrete share price event; negative HTA outcomes are negative catalysts [#J]

  • Company guide: Management has highlighted Japan as "early proof of concept" for ex-US market; EU reimbursement framed as a 2025-2027 process with country-by-country variation [#A]

  • Management commentary: Cautiously constructive on EU but explicitly not guiding to ex-US revenue timelines — CEO commentary in 2024 annual results call noted EU HTA complexity without committing to specific country reimbursement dates [#A]

  • Track record: Japan launched late 2023; early Japan royalties modest given pricing constraints but confirm commercial model works; EU access timeline remains uncertain as of Q1 2026 [#A]

  • SS consensus: Most sell-side models include EU contribution beginning 2026-2028 with high uncertainty; Japan modelled at modest volumes given price concessions; China optionality largely excluded from base case [#I]

  • Estimated revenue impact: EU G5 markets at full reimbursement could add $1-2bn to global Leqembi peak sales; BioArctic royalty increment ~$60-120m annually; SEK 600m-1.2bn at peak — material but 5-8 year horizon [#I]

  • Recent delivery: EMA approval confirmed July 2024; Germany's IQWiG/G-BA process initiated; UK NICE appraisal ongoing; France has not initiated HTA; country-by-country access will be patchwork through 2026-27 [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Comparison to other amyloid therapies (donanemab EU EMA submission pending as of early 2025) suggests EU HTA bodies are applying high clinical-benefit vs cost thresholds for Alzheimer's DMTs; precedent from aducanumab (EMA rejection) is unhelpful but distinct given Leqembi's cleaner data package [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: EU Alzheimer's prevalence ~8 million, ageing population structurally supportive; EU healthcare budgets under pressure but political will for Alzheimer's access is high given societal burden [#A]

  • Macro headwind: EU HTA reform (HTAR 2025) and joint clinical assessment process creates additional complexity for launches across EU; pricing pressure in EU substantially higher than US; infusion infrastructure deficit in EU greater than US [#A]

  • Visibility: Low near-term visibility — reimbursement decisions are binary, country-specific, and opaque prior to outcome; 2-3 year horizon before material revenue [#J]

  • Bear case: EMA approval does not translate to reimbursement — Germany, France, and UK NICE all decline or impose highly restricted indications with low volumes; EU contributes <$300m to peak Leqembi sales; BioArctic EU royalty contribution negligible within 24M investment horizon [#J]

  • Miss scenario: UK NICE negative decision (H1 2026 expected) acts as a signal for other EU markets; French ATU approval not forthcoming; European contribution pushed to 2028+ [#I]

  • Miss magnitude: -10-15% share price impact if EU broadly disappoints; not thesis-destroying alone but negative in combination with US softness [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — EU reimbursement likely eventual but timing uncertain and beyond 24M investment horizon for most markets; Japan adds marginal near-term royalties

§4 — Key Driver #3: Pipeline Progression (BAN2401 Next-Gen / AL-101 / Other Assets)

  • J-front verdict: Pipeline provides real but long-dated optionality; no pipeline asset will drive EPS within 24M; value is DCF terminal optionality, not near-term earnings [#J]

  • Driver definition: BioArctic's proprietary pipeline beyond lecanemab includes: (a) next-generation anti-amyloid antibody programmes building on BAN2401 platform, (b) AL-101 (alpha-synuclein antibody for Parkinson's disease / Lewy body dementia), and (c) other undisclosed preclinical assets; these represent the post-Leqembi growth narrative [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Pipeline assets generate EPS impact only upon (1) partnering/licensing deal (upfront milestone cash), (2) Phase 2/3 clinical readout triggering existing deal milestone, or (3) eventual commercial royalties; none likely within 24M [#I]

  • Mechanism to SP: Pipeline assets are valued as risk-adjusted NPV additions; positive Phase 1/2 data, partnership announcements, or IND filings each represent discrete share price catalysts; AL-101 is the most watched near-term pipeline catalyst [#J]

  • Company guide: Management has guided to Phase 1/2 data on AL-101 in Parkinson's disease by 2026; next-generation amyloid programmes described as "early-stage" with no timeline commitments [#A]

  • Management commentary: CEO framing in 2025 CMD positioned pipeline as "platform validation" rather than near-term revenue — appropriate expectation-setting but confirms pipeline is a 3-5 year value driver [#A]

  • Track record: BioArctic's track record on pipeline execution is underpinned by Leqembi itself — the company successfully developed and partnered a first-in-class asset; this is genuine evidence of scientific capability, but Leqembi took ~20 years from concept to approval [#I]

  • SS consensus: Pipeline modelled with significant risk discount in most sell-side DCFs; AL-101 risk-adjusted value typically $200-500m in bull-case models; most bear-case models exclude pipeline entirely [#I]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Near-zero EPS contribution within 24M; a partnership deal on AL-101 could generate SEK 200-500m upfront milestone — one-time, not recurring [#I]

  • Recent delivery: AL-101 Phase 1 data readout expected 2025-2026; no recent pipeline milestone events in L6M at time of this report; no licensing deals announced in L12M [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Anti-synuclein antibody programmes (Roche/Prothena prasinezumab, Biogen BIIB054) have not demonstrated robust clinical efficacy in Parkinson's to date — BioArctic's AL-101 faces a scientifically challenging indication [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: Parkinson's disease prevalence is ~1 million US patients with large unmet need; no disease-modifying therapy approved — regulatory appetite for breakthrough designation high [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Anti-synuclein antibody class has a disappointing track record; Phase 2 failures from prasinezumab (Roche/Prothena) and BIIB054 set a cautionary precedent; BioArctic must differentiate AL-101's mechanism [#J]

  • Visibility: Low visibility — clinical data outcomes are binary and uncertain; Phase 1/2 readout within 24M is the key event but outcome range is wide [#J]

  • Bear case: AL-101 Phase 1/2 data non-compelling; next-gen amyloid programmes fail to attract partners; pipeline contributes zero incremental value; BioArctic is solely a Leqembi royalty vehicle with single-asset risk [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Phase 1/2 data shows safety signals or biomarker non-engagement; no partnership interest from Big Pharma; sell-side trims DCF pipeline credit; -5-10% incremental share price impact [#I]

  • Miss magnitude: Modest share price impact in isolation; but confirmation of single-asset status increases multiple discount [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Low plausibility for near-term EPS contribution; medium plausibility for share price catalyst from AL-101 data readout within 24M; long-dated option, not near-term fulcrum

§5 — Key Driver #4: Royalty Rate and Financial Structure Certainty

  • J-front verdict: Highest certainty driver in the deck — royalty structure is contractually established; risk here is mismodelling the rate, not renegotiation [#J]

  • Driver definition: BioArctic's royalty entitlement from Eisai on global Leqembi net sales is governed by the 2005 research collaboration and subsequent partnership agreements; rate structure is not publicly disclosed at precise level but confirmed as "tiered single-digit" percentages [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Royalty rate directly determines BioArctic's revenue per dollar of Leqembi sales; a 1 percentage point uncertainty in rate estimate translates to ~$40-50m variance in peak royalty revenue — meaningful at current market cap [#I]

  • Company guide: BioArctic has not publicly disclosed the exact royalty rate; management has confirmed tiered structure with rates varying by geography and sales tier; this creates modelling uncertainty even at known sales levels [#A]

  • Management commentary: CFO has indicated royalty revenue is the "primary economic benefit" from the Eisai relationship; no guidance on rate change risk or renegotiation — structure appears stable [#A]

  • Track record: Royalty receipts in 2023-2025 are consistent with sell-side modelling assumptions, providing indirect validation that market assumptions on rate are approximately correct [#I]

  • SS consensus: Consensus models 5-8% blended royalty rate; individual analyst models vary; Carnegie and Redeye (primary Swedish sell-side coverage) explicitly model tiered structure [#A]

  • Estimated financial impact: Royalty rate confirmation (e.g., above-consensus 8%+ tiered) would be a positive catalyst; below-consensus (3-4% blended) would be a significant negative — uncertainty premium likely ~5-10% of current share price [#I]

  • Recent delivery: No renegotiation events in L12M; quarterly royalty receipts tracking modelling assumptions — evidence of rate stability [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Royalty structures for neurological disease partnerships (comparison to Royalty Pharma portfolio assets, FLJ Group structures) suggest mid-single-digit rates for foundational IP partnerships are standard; BioArctic's rate appears market-consistent [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: No macro sensitivity — contractual structure is independent of rates/FX beyond translation effects; SEK/USD FX is the primary financial risk [#A]

  • Macro headwind: SEK/USD FX — BioArctic reports in SEK; royalties earned in USD; meaningful currency exposure at peak royalty levels; 1% SEK appreciation vs USD reduces peak royalty SEK value by ~$20-25m [#I]

  • Visibility: High visibility on structure — contract is established; primary risk is modelling uncertainty on exact tiers, not existential structure risk [#J]

  • Bear case: Rate discovery (if/when BioArctic discloses full rate structure) reveals below-consensus blended rate; or Eisai seeks to renegotiate milestone/royalty terms in exchange for increased development collaboration on next-gen programmes [#I]

  • Miss scenario: Rate disclosure event (e.g., in a prospectus or detailed investor day) shows 4-5% blended vs consensus 6-7%; EPS modelling compression of ~15-20% at peak [#A]

  • Miss magnitude: Limited — primary driver of this section is certainty, not upside; rate risk is a modelling downside, not an existential threat [#I]

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — royalty structure is contractually stable; primary risk is rate estimation uncertainty, not structural change; certainty anchor for the financial model

§6 — Key Driver #5: Management Capital Allocation

  • J-front verdict: Neutral-to-watch driver: BioArctic is transitioning from R&D cost centre to royalty-receiving entity; capital allocation choices in 2025-2027 will define whether excess cash is reinvested productively or diluted [#J]

  • Driver definition: As Leqembi royalties grow, BioArctic will accumulate cash; capital allocation choices (R&D reinvestment in pipeline, M&A for new assets, buybacks/dividends, or cash accumulation) will determine long-term EPS per share trajectory and multiple [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Productive R&D reinvestment maintains pipeline optionality; unproductive R&D spend erodes EPS; M&A at premium valuations destroys value; buybacks in a growth biotech are unusual but possible [#I]

  • Company guide: Management has not articulated a formal capital allocation framework; R&D investment framed as "continued platform development"; no buyback programme announced [#A]

  • Management commentary: Management has maintained R&D-first philosophy consistent with founder-led biotech culture; no shareholder return mechanism beyond theoretical future dividend; capital discipline record is thin given limited historical royalty cash [#A]

  • Track record: BioArctic has operated with lean cost structure historically; as royalty cash builds, the discipline test emerges for the first time in 2025-2027 — no direct track record on this specific challenge [#I]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side models assume R&D spend grows with royalty cash; no analyst models buybacks; M&A not explicitly modelled by any major broker in public coverage [#I]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Buyback on 10% of shares at current market cap would add ~10% EPS per share; R&D overspend on failed pipeline would subtract $50-100m over 3 years — modest relative to royalty trajectory [#I]

  • Recent delivery: 2024-2025 cost base has grown modestly with organisational build-out; no evidence of capital misallocation yet; cash conservation discipline maintained [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Swedish biotech peers (Hansa Biopharma, Calliditas, Oncopeptides historically) have mixed capital allocation records; royalty-to-growth reinvestment efficiency is a known value-destruction risk for single-asset biotechs receiving unexpected windfalls [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: Low Swedish interest rates (historically) made cash accumulation less efficient vs deployment; rising rate environment in 2022-2024 marginally improved return on idle cash [#I]

  • Macro headwind: SEK weakness increases the real cost of USD-denominated R&D partnerships; licence acquisition costs are USD-denominated [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium visibility — management is known but untested on capital allocation at scale; board composition and governance quality is the lead indicator [#I]

  • Bear case: Management overinvests in pipeline programmes that fail; or pursues a dilutive acquisition to demonstrate growth beyond Leqembi; share count increases while royalty revenue growth doesn't compensate [#J]

  • Miss scenario: SEK 500m+ R&D spend on AL-101 programme that produces Phase 2 failure; EPS -10 to -15% vs model for 2-3 years [#I]

  • Miss magnitude: Modest standalone but confidence-damaging for long-term holder [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — capital allocation decision points are emerging but undetermined; founder-culture discipline is positive signal; scale-up risk is real but manageable within 24M

§7 — Key Driver #6: Macro and HTA Regulatory Environment

  • J-front verdict: Dominant risk modifier across all drivers — HTA/reimbursement decisions are exogenous, binary, and can reset the entire commercial trajectory; not a stand-alone upside driver but a constraint on every other driver [#J]

  • Driver definition: The regulatory and reimbursement environment for amyloid-targeting therapies determines the accessible patient population, reimbursement price, and competitive intensity; includes US CMS/Medicare/Medicaid policy, EU HTAR, Japan MHLW, NICE [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Unfavourable reimbursement decisions reduce Leqembi's addressable market and/or price; favourable decisions expand it; this feeds directly into royalty revenue and BioArctic EPS [#J]

  • Company guide: BioArctic cannot guide on regulatory outcomes; management highlights CMS April 2024 LCD expansion as the pivotal positive event — acknowledges further policy risk exists [#A]

  • Management commentary: CEO has characterised US reimbursement environment as "much improved" post-April 2024 CMS decision; EU described as "complex but progressing" [#A]

  • Track record: CMS April 2024 expansion of Medicare coverage to Leqembi was the single most important commercial catalyst in BioArctic's history; delivery of this regulatory milestone was positive and confirmed management's framework [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side broadly agrees US reimbursement risk is "largely resolved" post-CMS 2024; residual risk around CMS administrative process and prior authorisation implementation; EU risk remains high [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: A hypothetical US CMS reversal or re-restriction would be catastrophic (-40%+ to royalty assumptions); EU non-reimbursement across G5 markets reduces peak sales by $1-2bn (-$60-120m peak royalty) [#I]

  • Recent delivery: April 2024 CMS LCD expansion was delivered on time and as expected; operationalisation through 2025 has been slower than expected but direction confirmed [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: NICE precedent on amyloid therapies remains cautionary — UK has not approved any amyloid immunotherapy for reimbursement as of early 2026; France's ATU process for lecanemab is slow [#I]

  • Macro tailwind: US political environment in 2025 broadly supportive of Alzheimer's drug access; bipartisan support for Alzheimer's research and access; DOGE/CMS cost-cutting focus is a counter-risk [#A]

  • Macro headwind: IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) drug pricing provisions — if Leqembi is subject to CMS price negotiation after 9-year milestone, ceiling price could reduce net sales and thus royalty by 20-40% on impacted volumes; timeline: 2031+ but creates uncertainty in terminal value modelling [#J]

  • Visibility: Low visibility on EU decisions; medium on US implementation path; binary outcomes on NICE, German G-BA, French HAS each individually meaningful [#J]

  • Bear case: IRA price negotiation accelerated or scope expanded; CMS implements additional prior-authorisation barriers to Leqembi access; EU G5 all decline reimbursement in 2025-2027; effective addressable market shrinks 40% below bull case [#J]

  • Miss scenario: UK NICE rejections creates negative signal for Germany/France HTA processes; EU contribution to Leqembi sales pushed to 2029+; IRA ceiling price set at 80% of US list price by 2031 [#I]

  • Miss magnitude: EU disappointment: -10-15% to BioArctic share price; IRA acceleration: -20-30% to terminal value; combination: significant multiple compression [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility as a net-positive environment (post-CMS 2024); high plausibility as a downside risk modifier; US is resolved near-term, EU is the live risk, IRA is a long-dated terminal value risk

§8 — [Supplementary Driver Note: Competitive Dynamics / Donanemab Displacement Risk]

Note: This section addresses a driver not initially listed in §1 that warrants dedicated attention due to its potential to be the actual dominant fulcrum. Per ⚡ RARE tagging protocol, this competitive dynamics driver is flagged as the deliberately-weird signal — evidence strength is thin but consequence magnitude is severe.

  • J-front verdict: ⚡ RARE: Donanemab (Kisunla/Eli Lilly) competitive displacement is potentially the true fulcrum driver for Leqembi's long-term trajectory — and it is underweighted in most BioArctic sell-side models [#J]

  • Driver definition: Kisunla (donanemab), FDA-approved July 2024 with once-monthly IV dosing vs Leqembi's biweekly IV, offers a meaningful convenience advantage; head-to-head market share dynamics will determine Leqembi's share of the anti-amyloid market [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: If Kisunla captures 60-70% of new starts (vs current ~40-50% estimated market share), Leqembi's peak-sales trajectory is structurally capped; every sell-side Leqembi peak-sales estimate presupposes a market-share assumption [#I]

  • Recent delivery: Post-Kisunla launch data (late 2024) suggests competitive market; neurologist surveys indicate dosing convenience is a real prescribing factor; Leqembi retains advantages in safety profile (lower ARIA rates) and stronger amyloid clearance data [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side broadly models 40-60% market share for Leqembi; limited sensitivity analysis on sub-40% scenario; this is a gap in consensus modelling [#I]

  • Bear case: Leqembi market share falls below 35% by 2027; Lilly captures majority of new starts via once-monthly convenience; Leqembi peak US sales falls to $1.5-2bn vs $4-6bn consensus; BioArctic royalty peak ~$90-120m vs $240-360m consensus — fundamental thesis impairment [#J]

  • Miss magnitude: -40 to -60% BioArctic share price under bear-case competitive displacement [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: Leqembi's once-monthly subcutaneous formulation is in development (Eisai Phase 3 AHEAD 3-45 subcut arm) — if approved, convenience gap narrows substantially and competitive dynamics reset; timeline 2026-2027 [#A]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility for material competitive displacement within 24M; the subcut formulation is the mitigation; this driver deserves higher weighting than current sell-side consensus assigns

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

  • J-front verdict: Strongest bearish case: Leqembi US market share plateaus at 35-40% to donanemab, EU reimbursement stalls, and management over-invests in pipeline — a correlated triple miss that compresses BioArctic's share price 50-60% from current levels [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers #1 (US uptake) and #2 (ex-US expansion) are directly correlated — the same commercial and regulatory headwinds that slow US ramp also complicate EU case-making with HTA bodies [#A]

  • Single-driver risk: The thesis is functionally a single-driver setup dressed as a multi-driver thesis; if Driver #1 (Leqembi US) disappoints, Drivers #2, #3, and #5 all simultaneously weaken [#J]

  • Correlation across drivers: Drivers #1-3 are highly correlated — a negative Leqembi commercial event destroys: royalty revenue, pipeline partnership attractiveness, management credibility, and valuation multiple simultaneously [#J]
