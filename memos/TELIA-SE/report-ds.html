Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Telia's Q1 report delivered a clear EBITDA beat and a massive cash flow surprise, while its peer Telenor shocked the market with a guidance cut.

Q1 2026 Earnings Delivery

1.
Revenues - slight underperformance: Reported group revenues of SEK 19.97bn missed published consensus by 0.9%, while core service revenues of SEK 17.14bn came in virtually in-line with consensus expectations
2.
Adjusted EBITDA - clear beat: Adjusted EBITDA printed at SEK 7,942m, beating consensus of SEK 7,832m by 1.4%, representing a strong step up driven by cost control and opex reductions in Sweden
3.
Free cash flow - massive positive deviation: Reported FCF reached SEK 1.92bn, beating consensus of SEK 1.02bn by 88.3%, driven by a positive working capital contribution that deviated from expectations of negative reversals
4.
EBITDA growth - accelerating trajectory: Like-for-like EBITDA growth rose to 4.0% YoY, beating Telia's full-year guidance of around 3% and the prior quarter's print of 3.7%
5.
Sweden segment - core outperformance: Swedish EBITDA grew by 6.4%, beating consensus by 2.6% due to lower personnel expenses and lower TV content costs, offsetting weaknesses in Finland and Estonia

Guidance Reiteration and Operational Trends

6.
Guidance - reiterated as expected: Telia reiterated its full-year 2026 guidance of around 2% service revenue growth and 3% EBITDA growth, aligning with sell-side expectations
7.
Finland - stabilization print: Finland mobile trends showed less bad EBITDA growth of +1.3% compared to a -6.2% decline in Q4 2025, beating expectations of continued aggressive campaign drag
8.
Norway - wholesale loss drag: Norway's EBITDA declined by 2.9% due to the loss of ICE wholesale revenues, though this was less severe than the 9.3% drop in Q4 2025
9.
TV and Media - strong growth: Swedish fixed service revenue grew +4.2% YoY driven by +15% TV growth, confirming that the decision to exit owning TV content and focus on distribution is bearing fruit

Strategic and Corporate Developments

10.

12.

2.
Secondary cause - Telenor peer rotation: A secondary catalyst was peer rotation, as investors fled Telenor's disappointing results and guidance cut to seek 'safe haven' stability in Telia and Tele2
3.
Cause ranking - plausibility read: The cost-reduction turnaround is a high-plausibility driver, the Telenor-rotation is a medium-plausibility catalyst, and the sovereign AI partnership with Brookfield is a low-plausibility driver due to its long-term, non-cash-generative nature in the near term
4.
Strongest counter-hypothesis - margin erosion: The main threat to the bullish thesis is that Telia's cost actions fail to offset price competition, particularly in Swedish B2B or Finnish mobile, leading to margin erosion

Hypothesis confirmation - monitor churn: Investors can confirm or kill the turnaround thesis by monitoring Swedish mobile net adds and Finnish ARPU trends in the upcoming Q2 report
6.
Unexplained residual - minor gap: The named operational causes account for roughly 80% of the share-price move, with the remaining 20% residual likely driven by general European defensive equity inflows
7.
Explicit outlier callout - TV and Media value: An outlier factor the consensus may be ignoring is the deep value in Telia's TV and Media division, where the 15% revenue growth in TV could justify a higher sum-of-the-parts multiple than currently modeled

Closing decision prompt - next steps: The reader should commission a deep-dive report on Telia's TV and Media segment to verify if the 15% TV growth and content cost savings can be sustained into 2027

Other side of the story: If the general market re-rates European telcos down as interest rates remain elevated, Telia's high premium valuation over its peers will make it the first to suffer a multiple contraction regardless of its stable predictable cash flow coverage
