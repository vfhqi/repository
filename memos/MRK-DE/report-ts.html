Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The three-pillar model is highly defensive and insulated because it combines sticky bioprocessing consumables and early-stage semiconductor integration with high-margin rare disease medicines

Three-pillar global conglomerate model

1.
Diversified operational footprint: Operations are distributed across three highly distinct divisions, mitigating localized end-market downturns and structural regulatory shifts The model provides unique exposure to structural healthcare, biopharma tools, and tech hardware markets
2.
Unique legal structure: The Kommanditgesellschaft auf Aktien structure combines limited partners with personally liable general partners, preserving defensive family control This legal architecture encourages long-term capital allocation over short-term earnings pressure
3.
Balanced revenue mix: Healthcare and Life Science each represented over forty percent of group sales in fiscal year 2025, providing excellent sector diversification This balance ensures that cyclical downturns in electronics are buffered by steady pharmaceutical demand
4.
Resilient base portfolio: The conglomerate structure delivers highly recurring cash flows, with the majority of sales insulated from classic pharmaceutical patent expiries Only about thirty percent of the Healthcare division's sales are exposed to immediate competitive threats

Life science bioprocessing leadership

5.
Strong market position: Merck holds the second-largest global market share in bioprocessing at nineteen percent, trailing market leader Danaher's thirty-six percent This scale establishes Merck as a primary beneficiary of global biologic pipeline expansion
6.
Purification technology leadership: Differentiated capabilities in chromatography resins and filtration position the company as a preferred supplier for complex biologics purification These downstream bioprocessing technologies are highly complex and represent the most capacity-constrained manufacturing stages
7.
High consumable exposure: The Life Science segment utilizes a consumables-heavy product mix, offering predictable demand patterns that scale directly with biologic volumes Consumables represent over ninety percent of the Process Solutions portfolio, shielding the business from capital equipment cycles
8.
Decentralized manufacturing resilience: Ongoing strategic investments in Blarney, Ireland, and South Korea enhance regional supply chains, supporting long-term onshoring requirements These manufacturing expansions ensure that Merck can provide localized supply security amid changing geopolitical landscapes

Specialty healthcare and rare diseases

9.
Targeted oncology commercialization: The pharmaceutical segment focuses on maximizing established blockbusters Erbitux and Bavencio while scaling its newly added Rare Diseases franchise This commercial approach optimizes cash generation to fund targeted research and development
10.
Accretive portfolio scaling: The acquisition of SpringWorks adds Ogsiveo and Gomekli, immediately accelerating mid-term healthcare revenue growth without clinical-stage risk This transaction strengthens Merck's presence in the lucrative U.S. specialty market
11.
Insulated fertility franchise: Gonal-f and Pergoveris represent high-margin fertility treatments that provide a stable, highly cash-generative commercial foundation The franchise benefits from expanding patient access and demographic shifts toward delayed childbearing

Pure play semiconductor solutions

12.
Leading electronics positioning: Electronics operates as a pure-play supplier following the divestment of Surface Solutions, focusing primarily on high-growth semiconductor materials The business segment accounts for seventeen percent of group sales, driven by advanced nodes
13.
Advanced nodes integration: Thin-film depositions and chemical formulations are integrated early in customer roadmaps, locking in highly recurring multi-year revenue This early design-in strategy ensures high switching costs as advanced packing and 3D integration intensify
14.
Other side of the story: Complex structural cyclicality: The integration of cyclical chemicals and specialty pharmaceuticals can unsettle sector-focused investors, sustaining a persistent valuation discount Chemicals investors remain cautious of pharmaceutical pipelines, while healthcare investors are unnerved by semiconductor capacity cycles

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is exceptionally robust due to conclusions of bioprocessing destocking and structural AI-led semiconductor materials volume expansion

Driver one - Bioprocessing consumables normalization

1.
Strong bioprocessing rebound: Process Solutions recorded sixteen point two percent organic growth in first-quarter 2026, confirming biopharmaceutical inventory destocking has concluded This double-digit expansion indicates that major customers are returning to normalized procurement patterns
2.
Attractive consumable pull-through: High consumables attachment guarantees predictable recurring revenue as customer clinical and commercial biologic production volumes expand Over seventy percent of bioprocessing market revenues are highly recurring

Driver two - Advanced semiconductor materials demand

3.
Accelerating wafer intensity: Heterogeneous integration and 3D densification demand advanced materials, allowing Merck to outgrow underlying wafer volume trends Materials intensity rises by two hundred to four hundred basis points as customers migrate to sub-seven nanometer nodes
4.
Surging AI demand: Demand for advanced logic and memory chips translates directly to increased volumes of high-value thin films and metallization precursors Ongoing AI adoption is likely to expand the global semiconductor market to one trillion dollars by 2030

Driver three - Rare disease commercial scaling

5.
De-risked healthcare scaling: The SpringWorks integration adds approved, high-margin rare tumor therapies Ogsiveo and Gomekli, immediately accelerating mid-term healthcare sales The deal delivers programmatic expansion into high-value indications without clinical failure risks
6.
Promising Phase 3 asset: TLR7/8 antagonist enpatoran represents a significant oral therapy in Phase 3 development for active skin manifestations of lupus Phase 3 ELOWEN-1 and ELOWEN-2 trials initialized dosing in April 2026, bolstering pipeline visibility
7.
Regulatory fast-tracking advantage: Pergoveris is being filed under the US Food and Drug Administration priority review program, accelerating private payer market entry The CNPV program grants expedited communications, reducing overall commercialization timelines in the US
8.
Other side of the story: Sluggish lab spending: Muted academic and government budgets continue to drag discovery solutions performance, potentially slowing broader life science acceleration Research funding remains highly fragmented, limiting the immediate growth prospects of early-stage laboratory consumables

1.
SOTP valuation breakdown: Deutsche Bank maintains a Hold rating with a target of one hundred twenty-five euros, applying a seventeen times multiple to Life Science The SOTP approach applies a six times multiple to Healthcare and fourteen times to Electronics on 2027 estimates
2.
Fair value perspective: Jefferies maintains a Hold rating with a target of one hundred twenty-nine euros, viewing the current multiple as fairly rewarding growth drivers The target price implies a ten point six times 2027 estimated EV/EBITDA multiple
3.
Conglomerate multiple discount: Bernstein applies a fifteen percent conglomerate discount, deriving a one hundred twenty-one euro price target based on narrow pharmaceutical scale The valuation assumes Healthcare trades at a fifty percent discount to European peers, while Life Science and Electronics trade at parity
4.
Attractive relative value: Redburn maintains a Buy rating with a target of one hundred seventy-nine euros, arguing that bioprocessing market recovery represents a compelling entry The target price reflects the underappreciated long-term earnings durability of the downstream portfolio
5.
Other side of the story: Multiple compression risk: Slower-than-expected recovery in laboratory capital expenditure and delayed semiconductor facility timelines could trigger multi-year multiple compression Until bioprocessing and advanced logic materials recover fully, the market will remain unwilling to value Merck on an undiscounted Sum-of-the-Parts basis

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is moderate and manageable because the severe generic erosion of Mavenclad is well understood, though clinical pipeline execution remains a critical potential point of failure

Operational and generic erosion risks

1.
Immediate US patent erosion: Mavenclad generic competition in the United States starting May 2026 creates an immediate headwind for neurology and immunology revenues The revised guidance incorporates complete generic erosion in the U.S. from May onward
2.
Clinical trial vulnerability: The total failure of clinical-stage candidates like enpatoran would eliminate crucial mid-term revenue pipelines, leaving the division dependent on established brands Previous pipeline failures have unsettled chemicals investors, maintaining the conglomerate valuation overhang
3.
Lab expenditure constraints: Prolonged research funding cuts in the United States and China represent a direct threat to discovery solutions sales The US government shutdown in late 2025 has already impacted early academic ordering patterns

Commercialization and regulatory headwinds

4.
Intense competitive pricing: Low-cost competitors from Asia are aggressively pricing commodity products like buffer bags, putting downward pressure on basic bioprocessing margins Incumbents must continually innovate or leverage complex downstream chromatography portfolios to protect margins
5.
Capacity execution delays: Delayed construction of Western fabrication facilities could constrain the near-term volume growth of advanced semiconductor materials EMD Electronics remains exposed to project timing and customer capital expenditure delivery
6.
Other side of the story: Resilient base portfolios: Highly stable cardiovascular, metabolism, and fertility franchises deliver massive, recurring cash flows that insulate the group from R&D volatility Over seventy percent of Healthcare sales are entirely decoupled from classic patent-expiry dynamics
