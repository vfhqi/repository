§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Verdict: The strongest bearish case for ALK is a combination of US growth deceleration and margin plateau arriving simultaneously with a premium multiple, creating a triple-compression event [#J]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The bull thesis requires sustained ~20-25% US SLIT tablet growth for 3+ more years; the bear case does not require a product failure — only a slower adoption curve than modelled [#A]

  • Mechanism: US payer formulary management cycles typically hit new specialty products at years 3-5 of launch; ALK's tablets are now in this window

  • If payer rebate requirements increase, revenue realisation per prescription falls without volume offsetting; EBIT margin expansion stalls

  • Single largest risk: US payer/reimbursement risk is the single largest risk; not a binary risk (product failure or withdrawal) but a gradual margin squeeze that makes the long-range margin target (20-25%) harder to reach on the timeline implied by consensus [#J]

  • Mechanism: increased mandatory rebates compress net revenue per unit; US revenue growth decelerates to 15-20%; consolidated organic growth falls below 15%

  • Multiple contraction follows as the growth rate ceases to justify a premium multiple

  • Early warning signals: The key early warning metrics to monitor are: (1) US net revenue per prescription trends; (2) managed care formulary status updates; (3) gross margin trajectory in the US segment [#A]

  • If gross margin in North America begins to compress before EBIT margin closes the gap to the long-run target, the bear case is materialising

  • No current evidence of these early warnings, but they are the right things to watch

  • Breaking points: Three sequential events that would break the thesis: [#J]

  • First: US revenue growth decelerates below 20% for two consecutive halves
  • Second: EBIT margin expansion stalls below 18% (below the FY2025 guidance target)
  • Third: Management lowers full-year guidance for the first time, triggering a de-rating of the "conservative guidance" premium embedded in the valuation

  • Any single event is manageable; all three in sequence within 12-18 months would fundamentally change the risk-reward

  • Peer-relative downside: Even in a downside scenario, ALK is unlikely to underperform specialty pharma dramatically — the structural tailwind (AIT market growth, SLIT tablet adoption) provides a floor; the risk is a relative re-rating from premium to sector-median rather than absolute impairment [#J]
