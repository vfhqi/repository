Financial Performance & Cost Efficiency
Revenue and Profitability Evolution

ENAV SpA's financial performance during the transition into the new regulatory period (RP4) demonstrated robust underlying operational growth, despite the expected accounting impacts of the tariff mechanisms. For the full year 2025, consolidated total revenues amounted to 1,024.7 million euros, representing a slight decrease of 1.2% compared to the 1,036.7 million euros recorded in 2024. This top-line contraction was entirely driven by a negative balance component of 190.4 million euros, a typical regulatory effect in the first year of a new reference period. However, revenues from core operations were exceptionally strong, reaching 1,173.1 million euros, which marks an 11.2% increase over 2024. Furthermore, revenues from the non-regulated market grew by 5.8% year-over-year to 52.1 million euros, fully aligning with the targets set out in the 2025-2029 Strategic Plan.

Profitability margins normalized in 2025 as the company navigated the regulatory reset and managed rising operational volumes. Consolidated EBITDA stood at 252.7 million euros, an 18.7% decline from the record 310.9 million euros achieved in 2024. This resulted in an EBITDA margin of 24.7%, down from 30.0% in the previous year. The company's EBIT for the year amounted to 140.7 million euros, representing a 24.9% decrease compared to 2024. Ultimately, ENAV closed the 2025 financial year with a consolidated net profit of 93.1 million euros, down 25.9% from the 125.7 million euros reported in 2024.

Cost Structure and Efficiency Measures

ENAV's total operating costs in 2025 amounted to 772.0 million euros, reflecting a controlled increase of 6.4% compared to the 725.8 million euros recorded in 2024. Management successfully contained this cost growth below the projected 9% increase embedded in the industrial plan by activating stringent efficiency measures in response to late-year traffic dynamics.

Personnel costs, which form the largest component of ENAV's expense base, reached 632.6 million euros in 2025, a 6.8% increase over the previous year. This rise was primarily driven by the fixed component of remuneration, which incorporated a 2% increase in minimum contractual salaries and inflation differentials for the 2023-2025 period. The variable component of remuneration also expanded due to trade union agreements linked to the higher volume of air traffic handled. Operationally, the Group's workforce increased by 91 actual employees to reach a total headcount of 4,467 at year-end, reflecting targeted recruitment in operational, technical, and IT roles.

Other operating expenses increased by 7.6% to 174.2 million euros. This 12.3 million euro absolute increase was mainly attributed to higher maintenance costs for sales orders, increased Eurocontrol contributions, and higher personnel-related costs such as travel and meal vouchers. To optimize efficiencies, these upward pressures were partially offset by a reduction in costs for the purchase of spare parts, as the company strategically utilized existing inventory for air navigation systems rather than making new purchases.

Cash Flow Generation and Debt Deleveraging

ENAV demonstrated exceptional cash flow generation capabilities in 2025, significantly improving its liquidity profile. Cash flow generated from operating activities totaled 340.6 million euros, reflecting a positive change of 62.7 million euros compared to 2024. Free cash flow for the year reached 263.6 million euros, marking a 64.5 million euro improvement over the prior year's figure. This robust cash conversion was fueled by higher core business cash inflows and 11.8 million euros in receipts from projects funded under the National Recovery and Resilience Plan.

The accelerated cash generation facilitated a rapid deleveraging of the company's balance sheet. Net financial debt dropped sharply to 137.5 million euros as of December 31, 2025, which represents a massive improvement of 120.8 million euros compared to the 258.3 million euros reported at the end of 2024. This net debt reduction was achieved alongside strategic capital expenditures and the distribution of 146.2 million euros in dividends. Heading into 2026, the Group maintained a highly flexible financial position, supported by 447.7 million euros in cash and cash equivalents and 203 million euros in available unused short-term credit lines.

Traffic Trends & Operational Metrics
Service Unit Growth and Volume Execution

In 2025, ENAV managed a record 2.4 million flights, representing a 4.3% increase compared to the previous year. This robust operational volume translated into 12.4 million en-route service units (SUs), marking a 5.9% year-over-year growth trajectory. This en-route expansion allowed Italy to organically outperform major European peers, including Spain, the UK, Germany, and France. Terminal traffic also demonstrated positive momentum, growing by 3.4% to reach a total of 1.13 million service units for the year.

Traffic Mix: International and Overflight Outperformance

The underlying top-line operational performance was overwhelmingly driven by international and overflight traffic, which collectively accounted for 85% of total service units. Within the en-route segment, highly profitable overflight traffic grew by 7.8%, while international commercial traffic increased by 6.9%. Conversely, domestic en-route traffic remained pressured, recording a slight year-over-year decline of 2.1%.

Regulatory Environment & Tariff Mechanisms
The RP4 Performance Plan and Regulatory Stability

ENAV operates under a highly protective, pan-European regulatory framework that provides significant visibility into long-term revenues and cost recovery. The 2025 financial year marked the beginning of the fourth regulatory reference period (RP4), which covers the five-year stretch from 2025 through 2029. The European Commission officially formalized the Italian Performance Plan for RP4 in May 2025 through Implementing Decision 2025/1058. This approval confirmed that ENAV's operational and economic planning is fully compliant with the Union-wide performance targets.

The RP4 framework preserves the core stabilizing mechanisms of the previous regulatory era while imposing an annual efficiency improvement target of 1.2% on determined unit costs for en-route services. The system guarantees full recovery of capital expenditures, depreciation, and the cost of debt, calculating the allowed cost of capital based on the Regulatory Asset Base multiplied by the Weighted Average Cost of Capital . Consequently, the formal approval of the RP4 plan ensures absolute stability for the tariffs applied to airlines and secures ENAV's financial trajectory through the end of the decade.

Structural Revision of Terminal Charging Zones

One of the most profound structural changes introduced in the RP4 regulatory period is the consolidation and alignment of the terminal charging zones. Historically, ENAV operated with three distinct terminal zones, but from January 2025, these were streamlined into two zones that are both entirely governed by the European performance and unit rate scheme.

The previous Zone 1 and Zone 2 were merged to create a new, unified Charging Zone 1, which encompasses high-traffic hubs including Rome Fiumicino, Milan Malpensa, Milan Linate, Venice Tessera, and Bergamo Orio al Serio. Meanwhile, the former Zone 3, which consists of 40 medium and low-traffic regional airports, was reclassified as the new Charging Zone 2.

Crucially, under the previous regulatory regime, the legacy Zone 3 operated on a national regulatory scheme based strictly on a cost-recovery logic, meaning ENAV carried no traffic risk but also had no performance incentives for these airports. With the integration of the new Charging Zone 2 into the EU performance scheme, all Italian commercial airports now operate under a unified regulatory logic. While this introduces traffic and cost efficiency risk to regional airport operations, it also aligns terminal revenue generation with the company's broader operational performance incentives.

Tariff Mechanics and Traffic Risk Sharing

Tariffs for both en-route and terminal services are determined ex-ante by calculating the ratio between the planned chargeable costs for the regulatory period and the planned traffic volumes, which are measured in Service Units (SUs). To protect the company from extreme volume volatility, the European framework utilizes a traffic risk-sharing mechanism that acts as a revenue stabilizer.

At the end of each financial year, deviations between the forecasted traffic and actual traffic generate a balance that is either owed to ENAV (in the case of a shortfall) or returned to the airlines (in the case of an overperformance). The risk-sharing bands dictate exactly how these deviations are managed between the provider and the airspace users.

The Balance N-2 Reversal Mechanism

The regulatory balances generated in any given year (Year N) due to deviations in traffic, inflation, or service quality are not immediately cash-settled; rather, they are integrated into the tariff calculation two years later (Year N+2). This "N-2 reversal" is a core feature of ENAV's financial reporting, as the cash inflow or outflow impacts the current year's liquidity, but the economic effect is neutralized in the income statement by accounting for the N-2 balance with the opposite sign to avoid double counting.

During the 2025 financial year, the balance adjustment component had a significant negative accounting impact on reported top-line revenues, totaling roughly 190.4 million euros. This heavy negative adjustment was predominantly driven by a 207.7 million euro N-2 reversal. The vast majority of this reversal relates to the continued recovery of the massive revenue shortfall experienced during the 2020-2021 COVID-19 combined period. The European Commission allowed ENAV to recover this pandemic-era balance in equal installments over five years for the en-route and Zone 1 networks, and over seven years for Zone 2.

Impact of Inflation Resets and Eurocontrol Costs

Beyond traffic volumes, the regulatory framework neutralizes inflation risk by guaranteeing the recovery of the delta between the planned inflation rate and the actual inflation rate published by Eurostat. Because the start of RP4 reset the baseline assumptions for the regulatory mechanisms, the YoY impact of inflation balances on 2025 EBITDA was stark.

In 2024, the final year of RP3, ENAV benefited from a massive positive inflation balance of 63.6 million euros because actual inflation vastly exceeded the legacy forecasts established years earlier. However, with the RP4 reset in 2025, the new inflation assumptions were perfectly aligned with the current macroeconomic reality. Consequently, the 2025 actual inflation rate was slightly lower than the new RP4 plan, resulting in a negative inflation balance of approximately 5.0 million euros.

Similarly, the company reconciles determined costs associated with third parties, such as Eurocontrol. During 2025, ENAV recognized a 3.3 million euro refund related to the 2024 Eurocontrol cost delta, which was determined based on the final closed accounts for that agency.

Overall Impact of the Regulatory Reset

The transition into the RP4 regulatory period fundamentally reshaped the optical presentation of ENAV's 2025 profitability. Management had clearly telegraphed that the first year of any new regulatory period is structurally weaker than the final year of the preceding period due to the comprehensive reset of performance baselines.

In total, the regulatory reset of inflation balances, the shift of Terminal Zone 3 away from guaranteed cost recovery, and the normalization of the cost of capital generated an estimated 80 to 85 million euro mechanical headwind to 2025 EBITDA. Therefore, the reported decline in 2025 EBITDA to 252.7 million euros does not reflect deteriorating business fundamentals, but rather the anticipated and highly visible execution of the European regulatory reset mechanism.

Non-Regulated Business & Technological Capex
Non-Regulated Segment Performance and Subsidiary Dynamics

ENAV has structurally improved its risk-return profile by accelerating its expansion into the non-regulated market, leveraging its proprietary know-how to diversify revenue streams away from purely regulated air traffic management. During the 2025 financial year, revenues from the non-regulated segment reached 52.1 million euros, representing a 5.8% year-over-year increase. This performance aligns perfectly with the Group's ambitious 2025-2029 Strategic Plan, which targets doubling non-regulated revenues to over 100 million euros by the end of the decade. The revenue mix is well-diversified, heavily supported by the sale of software licenses and services (24.9 million euros), technical and engineering services (14.5 million euros), and aeronautical consulting (7.4 million euros).

The execution of this commercial strategy is driven by ENAV's specialized subsidiaries, which actively monetize the Group's operational expertise across 89 countries. Techno Sky, the subsidiary responsible for maintenance and technical services, secured approximately 51.5 million euros in new orders during 2025. IDS AirNav, which focuses on Aeronautical Information Management software solutions, signed new contracts worth roughly 26 million euros, including significant system upgrades for international air navigation service providers in Bulgaria, Portugal, and Austria. Furthermore, ENAV is aggressively positioning itself in the rapidly growing unmanned aerial vehicle ecosystem through its 60%-owned subsidiary, D-Flight. In 2025, D-Flight solidified its strategic vision by securing dual certification from the Italian aviation authority as both a Common Information Service Provider and a U-space Service Provider . To further strengthen its drone market presence, ENAV also signed a binding term sheet for the acquisition of an 85% stake in the AIView Group.

International Commercial Contracts

The Group's technological leadership was validated in 2025 by several high-profile international and defense contract wins. Most notably, ENAV, through Techno Sky, was awarded a contract to supply an advanced Remote Digital and Virtual Tower system at Senai International Airport in Johor Bahru, Malaysia. This 5.1 million euro contract utilizes ENAV's proprietary "Full Digital Tower" model and represents a crucial strategic milestone in exporting Italian digital ATM innovation to the high-growth Asian market. The four-year project kicked off in November 2025, with the vast majority of cash inflows expected across 2026 and 2027.

Domestically, ENAV capitalized on increased defense spending by securing a major contract with Teledife for the modernization of air traffic control radar systems at six Italian Air Force bases. This joint venture agreement, worth 39.7 million euros for the ENAV Group, includes comprehensive maintenance management services extending until 2032. The Group also expanded its international consulting and engineering footprint, securing contracts for airspace redesign in Saudi Arabia and Brunei, alongside the supply of ATM and communication systems for the Benghazi Area Control Center in Libya.

Strategic Capital Expenditures and Digital Modernization

To preserve its operational excellence and accommodate future traffic growth, ENAV has committed to a massive infrastructure modernization cycle. Under the 2025-2029 Business Plan, the company has earmarked 568 million euros for strategic capital expenditures, representing a 15% increase compared to the previous regulatory period. These investments are heavily weighted toward proprietary technology, with 50% of the CapEx allocated to Navigation & ATM systems and 27% to civil infrastructure upgrades. In 2025 alone, Group CapEx amounted to 113.1 million euros, funding the immediate rollout of the new operating model.

The cornerstone of ENAV's technological transformation is the continued rollout of the 4-Flight automation platform and the Coflight system, which are slated for full adoption in Rome by 2028 and in Milan by 2030. This software overhaul is running in parallel with physical airspace reorganization, primarily the consolidation of four Area Control Centers (ACCs) into two flexible hubs located in Milan and Rome. ENAV is also transferring radar approach management from local airports directly into these centralized ACCs, with completion expected by 2027.

Perhaps the most visible infrastructure upgrade is the aggressive transition toward remote digital towers. Following successful localized digitization, ENAV achieved a major milestone in 2025 with the operational launch of the Brindisi Remote Tower Control Center , which activated its first remote tower module. The company plans to fully digitalize 26 traditional towers into two massive RTCCs, aiming to remotely manage 16 towers by 2029 and achieve full integration by 2033. To support the heavy data flows required for these remote operations, ENAV is simultaneously deploying the new ENET-2 high-speed operational network. Ultimately, these deep technological investments are designed to unlock profound structural efficiencies, with management projecting 21 million euros in cumulative savings over the plan period and up to 47 million euros in annual savings once the new operating model reaches maturity.

DFS Deutsche Flugsicherung: Technological Resilience and Drone Integration

Germany's DFS Deutsche Flugsicherung, which manages approximately 3 million flights in peak years and up to 10,000 flights daily, is aggressively pursuing deep technological modernization to secure its heavily congested airspace. A cornerstone of this strategy is the rollout of PHOENIX nG, a next-generation, cloud-native platform developed in partnership with Frequentis. Designed initially as a high-capacity backup system for the Munich Area Control Center, PHOENIX nG marks the first operational deployment of the new service delivery model defined by the European ATM Master Plan 2025. This transition toward service-oriented architectures ensures interoperability and positions DFS to scale its flight data processing more efficiently than legacy systems allow.

Simultaneously, DFS is rethinking its physical infrastructure and surveillance capabilities. The company is advancing a new ASMR ground radar system at Frankfurt Airport, featuring a 65-meter antenna support structure and an integrated facility managed via advanced 3D building information modeling. In a forward-looking move to reduce maintenance on its network of approximately 30 large rotating primary radars, DFS has partnered with HENSOLDT to test Twinvis passive radar technology for civil aviation. These studies, utilizing non-rotating antennas and advanced software, have successfully generated stable tracks for fast-maneuvering aircraft, indicating that passive radar could soon become a resource-efficient alternative within the German ATM infrastructure.

DFS is also heavily involved in the drone ecosystem, driven by an urgent need to counter hybrid threats after experiencing over 190 disruptive drone sightings near German airports by late 2025. In response, DFS is collaborating with commercial hubs like Munich Airport to test advanced drone detection technologies. Concurrently, DFS has issued tenders to procure specialized multicopter-based measurement drones, which will be utilized to conduct airborne calibrations of air traffic control navigation systems (such as ILS and DME), reflecting a shift toward automated infrastructure maintenance.

NATS Holdings: Airspace Design and Commercial Expansion

Great Britain's NATS Holdings shares ENAV's dual focus on modernizing domestic airspace while actively commercializing its proprietary technology on a global scale. Domestically, the UK government has appointed NATS to lead a sweeping national airspace design service, an initiative essential for handling capacity constraints. To drive structural efficiencies at critical hubs like Heathrow, NATS recently implemented Pairwise Separation, a sophisticated arrival spacing tool developed with Leidos. Furthermore, NATS continues to leverage its early-adopter advantage with Aireon's Space-Based ADS-B service. This real-time satellite surveillance system has vastly improved predictability across the North Atlantic, yielding an estimated £19 million in annual fuel burn savings and reducing CO2 emissions by roughly 45,000 tonnes annually compared to legacy position-reporting methods.

Globally, NATS is recognized as the most mature European ANSP in terms of exporting third-party aeronautical consultancy services, holding a competitive edge over DFS and ENAV in the unregulated market. Seeking to capitalize on high-growth geographies, NATS recently signed a strategic alliance with Bayanat Engineering to deliver advanced air traffic management solutions, including digital tower technologies, across the Middle East and Africa.

Competitive Positioning in the Commercial Market

While ENAV's core regulated performance currently outpaces its peers in traffic growth and delay minimization, the international commercial landscape remains highly competitive. Industry experts note that ANSPs like NATS, DFS, and ENAV share a structural advantage over private aviation consultants because their baseline operational costs are heavily subsidized by regulated tariffs, allowing them to price competitive international contracts based primarily on incremental costs. NATS historically leads this unregulated consultancy segment, followed by Germany's DFS, with ENAV currently positioned as the third major player. However, ENAV's recent commercial success—particularly in exporting remote digital tower platforms to Asia and restructuring airspace in the Middle East—demonstrates that the Italian ANSP is rapidly closing the gap with its Northern European peers in the race to monetize ATM intellectual property.
