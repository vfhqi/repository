§3 — Translation Exposure

  • FY2024: EUR/SEK stabilised at 11.5-11.8 range; tailwind moderating rather than accelerating — flatter translation contribution

  • Translation hit to EBITDA: Because costs are heavily SEK-denominated while EUR revenue translates at a stronger EUR/SEK, translation has also lifted EBITDA margins modestly in reported SEK terms — a flattering effect that is not organic

Sceptical bullet — §3: The bearish read on translation is that the SEK's structural weakness (2022-2024) has created a multi-year earnings tailwind that is systematically invisible in constant-currency disclosure and may have inflated the perceived "quality" of Roko's earnings growth; the binary event risk is a Riksbank rate normalisation cycle that strengthens SEK faster than consensus expects (e.g. if Riksbank hikes ahead of ECB expectations in 2026-2027), creating a simultaneous translation reversal AND potential demand softening in rate-sensitive Nordic construction/industrial sectors — a correlated shock, not independent risks

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is secondary to translation for Roko but concentrated in USD-denominated input costs; the cash-flow mismatch is structurally asymmetric — USD cost inflation not passable at short lag in most industrial/service contracts

  • Transaction exposure — primary points:
  • USD technology procurement (SaaS licences, cloud infrastructure, components) — cost in USD, revenue in SEK/EUR; unhedged portion creates cash-flow margin squeeze when USD/SEK rises
  • Any USD-priced commodity inputs (steel, aluminium, plastics if manufacturing-adjacent) — same asymmetry

  • International project contracts denominated in EUR (if bid/pricing cycle is >6 months ahead of execution): EUR revenue locked at time of bidding vs SEK/NOK delivery cost — classic contract-currency mismatch

  • Cash-flow currency mismatch: The most visible cash-flow mismatch arises when project bids are priced in EUR (for cross-Nordic/EU contracts) with delivery costs predominantly in SEK; if EUR/SEK weakens between bid and delivery, the contracted EUR revenue converts to fewer SEK — a realised transaction loss, not merely a translation rounding

  • Magnitude vs translation exposure: Translation is likely ~3-5x the scale of transaction exposure in a normal FX environment; however, transaction exposure can exceed translation in a single large-contract scenario — lumpy risk rather than smooth

  • Pricing pass-through — assessment:

  • Swedish industrials/services firms typically operate on 12-36M contract cycles; within-contract currency pass-through is limited to explicit FX-escalation clauses
  • Roko's ability to pass through FX-driven cost increases depends on market position and contract structure; without evidence of explicit FX-escalation clauses in customer contracts, assumption is limited within-contract pass-through

  • At contract renewal, Roko can reprice — so pass-through is available with 12-36M lag, not instantly

  • Margin economic impact: A sustained 10% USD/SEK appreciation (USD strengthens) with 10-15% of COGS in USD would create ~1.0-1.5pp EBITDA margin headwind that cannot be offset within existing contract cycles; a 20% USD/SEK move doubles this to ~2-3pp [EST]

  • Cost pass-through: Labour cost is by far the largest cost; this has no transaction-FX component — a significant structural insulator on the cost side

Sceptical bullet — §4: The bearish read on transaction exposure is that apparent "limited pass-through risk" is predicated on short-duration USD cost exposure (technology licences renew annually); if Roko enters any multi-year capital investment cycle with USD-priced equipment commitments (expansion capex, new manufacturing line), the transaction exposure could spike from routine to material in a single year — and the commitment is typically made before the FX environment deteriorates; investors rarely see this before it flows through capex cash conversion

  • SEK outlook:

  • Consensus N12-24M: modest SEK appreciation toward 10.8-11.2 EUR/SEK range [EST from publicly available FX consensus surveys, mid-2025 vintage]; the central case is a 3-5% SEK strengthening vs EUR — a modest translation headwind reversal

  • Consensus FX view (summary): EUR/SEK mild appreciation (SEK strengthening, headwind for Roko); USD/SEK modest decline (mild cost benefit); NOK/SEK broadly stable; DKK/SEK tracks EUR/SEK

  • Rate differential: ECB-Riksbank rate differential was the primary EUR/SEK driver 2022-2024; as Riksbank cuts move toward ECB levels (or below), the differential narrows — supporting SEK recovery; this is a multi-quarter process, not overnight

  • SEK crash extension: if Swedish housing market re-deteriorates or Riksbank overtightens recession, SEK could weaken further — a continued tailwind but likely associated with Roko earnings headwinds from demand side (not a clean positive)

Sceptical bullet — §8: The bearish FX-outlook read is that consensus systematically underestimates SEK recovery speed because Riksbank rate-cut cycles in small open economies tend to overshoot on the downside before reversing sharply; if Riksbank signals a policy pivot earlier than expected (e.g. H2 2026 inflation rebound), SEK could strengthen 8-12% vs EUR within 12 months vs current consensus of 3-5% — this would double the translation headwind and simultaneously hit the domestic Swedish demand backdrop if the rate reversal is driven by sticky inflation; the scenario where both revenue and FX move adversely is the tail Roko's hedging programme is most poorly equipped to absorb

§9 — FX as Tailwind/Headwind for Thesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: FX has been a 2-4% annual EPS tailwind for L3Y driven by EUR/SEK translation; this tailwind is now maturing and the N12-24M base case is a neutral-to-mild headwind rotation — thesis EPS growth needs to be stress-tested with FX stripped out
