SECTION 2 — QUANTIFIED IMPACT BY DRIVER (12-24m OUTLOOK)

2.1 Driver D1 — Action operating EBITDA growth

  • Latest data point: 2025 EBITDA €2,367m, +14% YoY; LFL sales 4.9%; net sales €16.0bn (+16%).
  • Guidance for 2026: LFL 4-5%, ≥400 net new stores, EBITDA margin held at 14.8%.
  • Early-2026 reality: P1 LFL 6.1%, but P1-P3 cumulative LFL 3.6% — softer than guide.
  • Run-rate EBITDA used in valuation: €2.63bn at 18.5x multiple (net of liquidity discount).
  • NAV contribution to total: Action stake = ~80% of NAV; every 10% Action EBITDA move → ~6-8% NAV move (assuming multiple held).
  • Sensitivity ranges: +10% EBITDA → +~7% NAV; -10% EBITDA → -~7% NAV; +1x multiple expansion → +~4-5% NAV.
  • Combined sensitivity: Bear case (-10% EBITDA + -2x multiple) ≈ -15% to -17% NAV impact.
  • Bull case: (+10% EBITDA + +1x multiple) → +12-13% NAV impact.
  • Share-price correlation: High (>0.7 directional) but premium/discount dynamic now decouples spot price from underlying NAV.

2.2 Driver D2 — Action exit timing/price OR hold-and-compound

  • Strategy stated: Long-term hold; 3i increased stake from 57.9% to 65.4% in FY26 using £827m cash + £1,739m non-cash consideration.
  • No IPO planned 2026: Management language explicit on compounding intent.
  • Why this matters: Removes near-term realised-gain trigger but locks in NAV mark-to-multiple optionality.
  • NAV contribution: Stake increase added value through purchase from co-investors below carrying multiple — accretive transaction signal.
  • Sensitivity: A hypothetical exit at 22x (above current 18.5x) on €2.63bn EBITDA → ~£5-6bn uplift = ~17-20% NAV gain (illustrative).
  • Conversely: Forced sale at 15x → ~£4-5bn impairment = ~14-17% NAV hit.
  • Correlation to share price: Modest near-term (no exit catalyst); high long-term once exit path crystallises.
  • Peer anchor: EQT-SE shows large-cap PE exits achievable at scale (BPEA IX $15.6bn fundraise demonstrates LP appetite).

2.3 Driver D3 — Broader PE portfolio NAV growth (ex-Action)

  • Correlation to share price: Low-to-moderate; dwarfed by Action narrative.

  • Peer anchor: Capital returns active sector-wide; ICG-GB and BPT-GB also raising distributions. III-GB stands out for the size of buyback vs market cap (~£15-18bn).
