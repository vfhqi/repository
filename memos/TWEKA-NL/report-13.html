§1 — Current Management Guidance

TKH's current forward guidance spans both a FY2024 near-term frame and a medium-term (2025-2026) strategic target set. The medium-term targets were refreshed at the November 2023 Capital Markets Day and remain the primary reference for the investment thesis.

  • FY current — revenue: Management guides for mid-single-digit organic revenue growth in FY2024 at group level, with Smart Connectivity as the primary growth driver; no specific € revenue target published [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • FY current — EBIT: Group EBIT margin guided at 13.5-14.5% for FY2024, representing modest expansion from FY2023 actual (approximately 12.8-13.0% reported); expansion assumed to come from Smart Manufacturing recovery [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • Sub: The 50-100bps margin expansion step embedded in FY2024 guidance requires Smart Manufacturing to recover from H2 2023 lows — a conditional assumption that has been deferred twice previously
  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance published; implied EPS growth of 8-12% if EBIT and margin guidance is met, assuming stable tax rate (~25%) and broadly stable share count [#I]
  • FY current — FCF: Management references "strong cash generation" and targets net debt/EBITDA below 1.0x through FY2025; no explicit FCF € guidance [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • FY current — CapEx: Guided at approximately €120-140m for FY2024 (broadly stable vs FY2023 ~€125m); consistent with ongoing capacity expansion in Smart Connectivity cable manufacturing [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • Medium-term — N2Y/N3Y targets (CMD Nov-23): Group EBIT margin target of 14-16% by FY2026; Smart Connectivity targeting 15-18% EBIT margin; Smart Manufacturing targeting 12-15% EBIT margin [AS-company CMD materials] [#A]
  • Medium-term — revenue: Smart Connectivity guided for double-digit revenue CAGR through FY2026, underpinned by offshore wind cable, data-centre interconnect, and energy-grid investment; Smart Manufacturing guided for mid-single-digit CAGR [AS-CMD Nov-23] [#A]
  • Sub: Smart Connectivity CAGR guidance implies roughly €200-250m incremental revenue above FY2023 base — a large but arguably defensible number given cable market order backlog dynamics
  • Source — investor day: Primary guidance source is the November 2023 CMD; supplemented by H1 2024 interim results call (August 2024) and FY2023 full-year results call (February 2024) [#A]
  • Update timing: Management typically updates group EBIT margin guidance at H1 results (August) and confirms at FY results (February); no Q1 trading update with guidance revision expected [#A]
  • Caveat — organic only: All revenue growth guidance is stated on an organic basis, explicitly excluding bolt-on M&A; TKH has a history of small acquisitions (~€30-80m/year) that supplement but are not in guidance [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • Conditional assumption — Smart Mfg recovery: Management explicitly conditions 14-16% group EBIT margin on Smart Manufacturing recovering towards its 12-15% sub-segment target; the timing of this recovery is left vague ("second half of 2024 onwards") [AS-FY2023 call] [#A]
  • Sceptical read: The quantification of the 14-16% group margin target is precise enough to appear credible but conceals a substantial embedded assumption — Smart Manufacturing margin recovery of ~150-250bps — that management has guided toward without delivery for 18+ months; apparent precision may mask aggressive embedded timing [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

TKH exhibits a mixed pattern — modest revenue sandbagging coexists with a consistent margin over-promise tendency, particularly in Smart Manufacturing; this is NOT a clean sandbagger profile.

  • Mixed pattern: Revenue guidance is modestly conservative (positive bias +1-3% historically); EBIT margin guidance is consistently optimistic (negative bias -30-60bps average) — two different guidance behaviours co-exist [#J]
  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Smart Connectivity revenue has beaten guidance in 3 of the last 4 periods, driven by offshore wind cable project timing variability (large discrete orders can beat or miss a period) [#A]
  • Sub: The "beat" in Smart Connectivity revenue may be structural positive skew from lumpy cable project awards rather than deliberate conservatism
  • Over-promiser signal — Smart Manufacturing margin: Smart Manufacturing EBIT margin has missed guidance in 3 of 4 periods; management consistently underestimates the sensitivity of this segment's margins to volume deleverage [#J]
  • Over-promiser signal — group margin: Group EBIT margin guidance has been at or above subsequent actuals in 3 of 4 reviewed periods; management appears to guide to the optimistic scenario rather than the central case [#A]
  • Pattern shift: FY2021 was the clearest "beat" period (revenue and margin guidance met or exceeded); FY2022 and FY2023 show deteriorating delivery vs guidance — the pattern is worsening, not improving [#J]
  • Beat consistency — revenue only: Revenue-only beats occur 3 of 4 periods; this is the sandbagger signal; however margin beats occur only 1-2 of 4 periods; overall beat consistency is therefore below the European industrial peer norm [#A]
  • Magnitude — miss (margin): Margin misses average 30-60bps at group level; Smart Manufacturing segment misses have been 100-200bps in recent periods — economically material for a business with ~€500-600m of revenue in that segment [#A]
  • Management language signal: Post-FY2022 earnings call language shifted toward qualifying Smart Manufacturing guidance with "subject to demand environment normalisation" — a verbal hedge that was not present in FY2021 calls; management is signalling its own uncertainty [AS-FY2022 earnings call transcript] [#A]
  • Structural vs cyclical pattern question: The FY2023 miss in Smart Manufacturing is plausibly cyclical (inventory destocking, auto production cuts); if structural, the margin target is aspirational not achievable in medium-term [#I]
  • Sceptical read: Management's apparent revenue sandbagging may be running out of cushion — Smart Connectivity growth guidance (double-digit CAGR through 2026) is now aggressive in absolute terms; if offshore wind project delays materialise (documented in 2024 European wind industry), the revenue beat buffer disappears and the miss pattern accelerates [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

Guidance accuracy has deteriorated over the L4-period window; FY2021 was the high-accuracy period; FY2022-2023 show a consistent trend of guidance optimism exceeding delivery, concentrated in margin metrics.

  • Accuracy deteriorating: FY2021 guidance accuracy (revenue and margin both met/exceeded) contrasts with FY2022 (mild miss on margin) and FY2023 (moderate-to-significant miss on margin) — a clear deteriorating arc [#J]
  • Recent accuracy: FY2023 is the worst guidance accuracy year in the L4 window; group EBIT margin missed by ~50-100bps; Smart Manufacturing missed by ~150-200bps; revenue guidance was also slightly missed (H2 2023 organic revenue flat/slightly negative) [#A]
  • Historical accuracy: FY2021 was the strongest; buoyed by post-Covid industrial recovery and energy cable super-cycle tailwinds that made conservative guidance easy to beat [#A]
  • Volatility driver — macro: Smart Manufacturing accuracy is materially driven by macro conditions in automotive and factory automation end-markets; when these normalise (2022-2023 destocking), accuracy deteriorates [#A]
  • Sub: Approximately 40-50% of guidance variance in Smart Manufacturing can be attributed to demand-side macro volatility rather than management execution errors
  • Volatility driver — execution: Smart Connectivity accuracy is mainly driven by project award timing and offshore wind supply-chain variability; less management-controllable but more visible (backlog) [#A]
  • Metric — best accuracy: Revenue (organic growth %) has the best accuracy record across L4 periods; management understands its revenue building-blocks better than its cost/margin trajectory [#A]
  • Metric — worst accuracy: EBIT margin (particularly Smart Manufacturing sub-segment) has the worst accuracy record; this is the single metric where guidance optimism is most persistent and most consequential for EPS [#J]
  • Guidance revision pattern: Two mid-year downward guidance revisions in L2Y (H1 2022 upward, H1 2023 downward) suggest management has limited H1 visibility into H2 margin trajectory; not unusual for capital-goods companies but relevant to monitoring cadence [#A]
  • Sceptical read: The FY2021 "accuracy peak" coincided with a cyclical recovery tailwind that inflated apparent guidance credibility; the subsequent deterioration (FY2022-2023) is therefore not a temporary blip but a mean-reversion to management's underlying, less accurate forecast track record; if macro conditions remain mixed in 2024-2025, accuracy is unlikely to recover to FY2021 levels [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The medium-term guidance rests on three key assumptions of varying credibility; the Smart Manufacturing margin recovery assumption is the most fragile; the Smart Connectivity revenue growth assumption is the most defensible.

  • Revenue assumption — market (Smart Connectivity): Management assumes offshore wind cable installation activity grows structurally through 2026, underpinned by EU and UK government offshore wind capacity targets; this assumption is defensible and well-supported by backlog disclosures [AS-company filings, CMD materials] [#A]
  • Sub: TKH's Smart Connectivity revenue guidance is fundamentally a backlog-conversion assumption; as of FY2023, order backlog was reportedly multi-year in offshore cable, reducing revenue forecast risk
  • Revenue assumption — customer (Smart Manufacturing): Management assumes industrial automation end-markets (automotive, machine-builders) recover in H2 2024 to underpin Smart Manufacturing revenue CAGR; this is a macro-dependent assumption with limited internal controllability [#A]
  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management embeds modest price increases (~1-3% annually) in Smart Connectivity revenue guidance; pricing power in specialty cable is defensible given oligopolistic market structure (Nexans, Prysmian, TKH compete on delivery capacity more than price) [#A]
  • Margin assumption — leverage (Smart Manufacturing): The 12-15% EBIT margin CMD target for Smart Manufacturing assumes volume recovery sufficient to absorb fixed-cost base; operating leverage is high (estimated contribution margins ~35-40%), making margin trajectory highly sensitive to volume [#J]
  • Sub: A 5% revenue miss in Smart Manufacturing (€25-30m) translates to approximately 100-150bps margin compression at the operating leverage rate implied by segment disclosures
  • Margin assumption — cost (input costs): Management assumes broadly stable raw material costs (copper, specialty polymers) through 2025-2026; copper price volatility is a wildcard — TKH typically passes through copper costs with a lag, creating short-term margin drag in a rising-copper environment [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • Margin assumption — pricing power (Smart Connectivity): Offshore cable contracts are typically long-term, fixed at award; margin expansion in Smart Connectivity requires new contracts to be awarded at higher margins than legacy book — an assumption exposed to competitive pressure from Nexans and Prysmian capacity expansion [#I]
  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed stable at ~24-26%; no significant change in Dutch corporate tax regime expected; low sensitivity [#A]
  • EPS assumption — share count: No buyback programme guided; share count effectively stable; dilution risk minimal [#A]
  • Macro assumption — currency: ~25-30% of TKH revenues are non-EUR (USD, GBP-linked offshore wind contracts); EUR appreciation scenario creates ~1-2% headwind per 5% EUR/USD move; not explicitly hedged in guidance [#I]
  • Macro assumption — GDP: Smart Manufacturing is materially exposed to European industrial production; management's guidance embeds a "mild recovery" scenario for 2024-2025 European manufacturing PMI; this assumption is contested given current PMI levels [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

Overall guidance credibility: MEDIUM — revenue guidance is moderately credible; margin guidance carries persistent optimism bias; combined the guidance is reachable but requires Smart Manufacturing to execute a recovery management has promised and not yet delivered.

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — management is neither a chronic over-promiser nor a disciplined sandbagger; credibility is segment-dependent, with Smart Connectivity guidance more credible than Smart Manufacturing guidance [#J]
  • Green flag — Smart Connectivity track record: Revenue guidance in Smart Connectivity has been met or beaten in 3 of 4 periods; backlog visibility provides genuine forecast anchoring; offshore wind structural demand is real [#A]
  • Green flag — balance sheet guidance: Leverage and capital structure guidance (net debt / EBITDA targets) has been consistently accurate across L4 periods; management's financial discipline and balance sheet management are credible [#A]
  • Green flag — dividend consistency: TKH has maintained a consistent dividend growth policy (paid and increased dividend through FY2023 despite earnings miss); demonstrates financial commitment and management confidence in FCF trajectory [AS-company filings] [#A]
  • Red flag — Smart Manufacturing repeat miss: Three periods of missing Smart Manufacturing EBIT margin guidance is not a one-off; it is a pattern; management appears to structurally underestimate cycle sensitivity in this segment [#J]
  • Red flag — mid-year guidance revision: Two mid-year guidance revisions in FY2022-2023 (one up, one down) indicate limited H1-to-H2 visibility; management is issuing full-year guidance without sufficient forward visibility in Smart Manufacturing [#A]
  • Red flag — aggressive medium-term targets: The 14-16% group EBIT margin CMD target implies TKH achieving a margin level it has not sustained for more than 1-2 quarters in the L4 period review; aspirational rather than central-case [#J]
  • Margin of safety — low: If guidance is off by 10% on EBIT (within the observed historical range), the investment case requires the current valuation to embed enough margin of safety to absorb a ~9-12% EPS miss; at current consensus multiples (~14-17x P/E), the margin of safety is moderate but not generous [#I]
  • Margin of safety — revenue: Revenue-side guidance has a positive bias historically; if Smart Connectivity delivers above guidance again, partial offset to margin misses is possible — providing a natural hedging mechanism within the guidance package [#A]
  • Thesis sensitivity: The 25%+ IRR thesis is achievable at management's central guidance case; it becomes challenging if Smart Manufacturing margin recovery is delayed by 12-18 months, as this pushes the EPS CAGR below the required threshold in the investment horizon [#J]
  • Sceptical read: Apparent credibility in Smart Connectivity may prove brittle if offshore wind project delays (documented in H1 2024 European wind market reports) push 2025-2026 backlog conversion later; and any credibility built on balance sheet discipline does not compensate for earnings quality; the single assumption failure that would invalidate the MEDIUM credibility verdict is a 12-month delay in both Smart Manufacturing recovery AND Smart Connectivity project conversion — a joint probability of ~15-20% based on recent macro and sector data [#J]
