§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Pronounced and consistent — H2 structurally dominates; H1 prints routinely below full-year run-rate; pattern stable and well-understood [#J]

H1 pattern: H1 EBITA margin structurally ~100-150 bps below full-year; driven by winter-to-spring ramp in installation and service call-outs [#A]

  • Sub: H1 revenue typically ~47-48% of full-year; H1 EBITA typically ~37-40% of full-year — margin dilution is seasonal not operational [#A]
  • Sub: This creates a systematic pattern where H1 releases look operationally weak but H2 recovery is expected and delivered [#I]

Q1 pattern: Not applicable (semi-annual reporter); intra-H1 cadence not disclosed [#I]

H2 pattern: H2 captures bulk of annual EBIT — typically ~60-63% of full-year; mirrors construction seasonality (completion surge in autumn and winter project handovers) [#A]

Strongest quarter: H2 across all three L3Y full years — no exceptions observed [#A]

Weakest quarter: H1 consistently — in FY-24, H1 EBITA was SEK 590m vs H2 implied ~SEK 1,040m (FY-24 total SEK 1,630m) [#I]

Seasonality changing: Modest change — service division (less seasonal) growing as % of mix; over a 5-10 year horizon, seasonality may soften; L3Y data shows no material change yet [#A]

  • Sub: Service revenue ~45% of group in FY-24 vs ~40% in FY-21 — gradual mix shift that reduces seasonality on a multi-year view [AS-Carnegie] [#A]
  • Sub: At current service growth rates (+5-6% organic), service could approach 50% of group by FY-27; that would meaningfully dampen H1-H2 EBITA gap [#I]

Cause — production: Primary seasonality driver is outdoor installation work (HVAC, electrical, plumbing on new construction) which peaks in construction completion seasons; service call-outs are more year-round [#A]

Seasonal implications for beat/miss: H1 consensus estimates routinely embed the seasonality discount; thus H1 beats/misses are more signal-dense than they appear — a H1 miss vs a seasonally-adjusted consensus is more alarming than a raw % miss [#J]

Sceptical bullet — §4: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? If Swedish residential construction does not recover, the H2-weighted seasonal model means full-year guidance relies on an H2 that must do heavy lifting; if H2-25 installation volumes disappoint (as H2-24 partially did vs initial hopes), the seasonal recovery pattern breaks — and H1-25's modest beat could be false comfort for a weak H2-25 [#J]
