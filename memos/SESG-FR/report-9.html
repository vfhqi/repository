§2 — Quality of Earnings

J-front verdict: Mixed quality — reported earnings heavily distorted by C-Band proceeds and impairments; adjusted EBITDA is cleaner but carries rising D&A headwind and modest NWC tailwinds that may not persist [#J]

  • One-off impact: C-Band US spectrum proceeds are the dominant distortion — Phase 1 (~€150m) and Phase 2 (~€600m) in 2022; these inflated reported net income and EPS but are correctly excluded from adjusted EBITDA [#A]

  • Sub: Total C-Band proceeds received by SES ~€~3.0bn over 2021–2023 (cumulative, not all P&L); the portion flowing through P&L vs balance sheet varies by accounting treatment

  • Adjustments trend: Adjustment quantum is large and rising — gap between reported EPS and adjusted EPS widened from ~€0.05 in 2019 to ~€0.20+ per half-year in 2022–2023 (adjusting out impairments, restructuring, C-Band) [#A]

  • Sub: Recurring items dressed as one-off: restructuring charges appeared in every period 2022–2023 (~€12-35m per period); technically one-off but empirically recurring

  • ❌ Recurring restructuring: Restructuring charges in 5 of 6 semi-annual periods L3Y (~€12m to €35m per half) — pattern suggests ongoing workforce adjustment rather than episodic event [Cross-ref: §9]

  • Non-GAAP gap: Moderate — adjusted EBITDA excludes D&A (standard for satellite operators given heavy depreciation), restructuring, M&A costs, and C-Band items; the exclusions are broadly industry-standard but aggregate to a large number [#A]

  • NWC tailwind: Modest positive — working capital relatively stable; receivables turn approximately 55-65 days (consistent L3Y); advance payments from mPOWER customers provide a small liquidity buffer [#A]

  • Sub: No evidence of aggressive receivables stretching or payables extension that would flag manipulation

  • Cash conversion: Below satellite infrastructure median — OCF/EBITDA approximately 55-65% L3Y vs ~70-80% typical for mature satellite operators (Intelsat pre-restructuring, Eutelsat historic) [#A]

  • Sub: Primary drag: interest costs on mPOWER-related debt (~€150-180m annual interest L3Y) depressing FCF below EBITDA conversion

  • Sub: Secondary drag: CapEx for mPOWER fleet (~€400-500m+ annual 2022–2023) is capex not in EBITDA; FCF conversion even worse when CapEx included

  • OCF vs NI: OCF significantly exceeds net income on adjusted basis — sign of genuine D&A-heavy business model rather than accrual income inflation; not a quality concern [#A]

  • D&A trend: Rising sharply — mPOWER satellite fleet depreciation added ~€65m incremental D&A in FY2023, ~€120m in FY2024E; total D&A increasing from ~€450m (2022) toward ~€570-600m (2024E) [#A]

  • Sub: Rising D&A mechanically suppresses EBIT and net income even as EBITDA improves — EPS recovery is D&A-headwind-compressed

  • Conservative accounting signal: SES has historically taken satellite impairments proactively (rather than delaying) — ~€120m in 2022, ~€45m in 2023 — suggesting balance sheet is not being inflated by stranded GEO assets [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? Adjusted EBITDA excludes structurally rising D&A (the real cost of the mPOWER bet); recurring restructuring dressed as one-off masks permanent workforce costs; NWC tailwinds from mPOWER advance payments will reverse once the backlog converts to revenue and billing normalises [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

J-front verdict: Margin compression was structural (GEO revenue decline on fixed cost base); recovery is plausibly structural if mPOWER scales as guided — but requires monitoring for 2+ years before high confidence warranted [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: ~-4% L3Y (2021–2023); 2024 inflection brings 3-year compound to approximately flat; full L3Y picture is a revenue-shrinking business pivoting toward a new product [#A]

  • Profit CAGR: EBITDA CAGR approximately -6% to -7% (2021–2023) — profit falling faster than revenue due to operating deleverage; 2024 reversal is sharper (EBITDA +~16%) as revenues inflect on a partially-fixed cost base [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Positive in 2024 direction — incremental mPOWER revenues carry ~65-70% EBITDA margin (high-bandwidth, contracted, low-incremental-cost) vs blended legacy ~52%; mix-shift drives positive leverage [#A]

  • Sub: GEO Video has ~45-50% EBITDA margin (lower due to declining utilisation); as it shrinks and mPOWER grows, blended margin expands mechanically

  • Gross margin: Approximately stable 57-59% (2022–2023), recovering toward 60%+ (2024) — satellite operators have minimal variable COGS; gross margin is primarily a function of revenue volume vs fixed transponder costs [#A]

  • EBITDA margin: Compressed from ~57% (2021) to ~50-51% (FY2023) trough; recovering toward ~54% (FY2024E) — still below 2019–2021 range [#A]

  • Sub: Management guidance for FY2024 EBITDA margin: "low-50s" — actual H1 2024 came in at ~53.8%, ahead of guide; FY2024E consensus implies ~54%

  • Margin expansion driver (2024): Mix shift (mPOWER + Networks growth) + GEO decline deceleration — both structural if mPOWER backlog converts; not a one-off cost cut [#J]

  • Cost cut driver: Some cost reduction was achieved through restructuring (workforce reduction 2022–2023); however, this is a smaller driver of margin recovery vs revenue volume [#A]

  • Sub: Operating cost base ~€900-950m (2022-2023); stable with modest decline; not a significant margin lever on its own

  • Scale driver: mPOWER at scale is the key margin lever — O3b mPOWER network cost largely sunk (D&A); each incremental €1 of revenue above ~€200m annualised carries ~65-70% incremental EBITDA margin [#A]

  • Sustainability assessment: Partially structural, partially dependent on backlog conversion — the cost base is fixed/semi-fixed (satellite D&A + ground station ops); revenue growth is the driver; structural only if mPOWER customers contract at scale [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? If mPOWER ramp stalls, fixed D&A headwind (~€120m incremental vs 2021) sits permanently on the P&L with no revenue offset; GEO Video declining faster than expected would shrink the revenue base that covers fixed costs; the "structural" margin recovery is only structural if 2024 growth rate persists into 2025-2026 [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

J-front verdict: Modest seasonality with H2 consistently stronger than H1 — pattern is stable and consistent with satellite operator norms (enterprise contracts typically Q4 renewal cycles); not a material analytical concern [#J]

  • H1 vs H2 pattern: H2 revenue consistently ~1-3% higher than H1 across L3Y — driven by enterprise/government contract renewals clustering in Q4 cycle; standard for B2B satellite bandwidth contracts [#A]

  • Sub: H1 2022: ~€892m; H2 2022 implied: ~€883m (slight H1 bias — anomalous due to contract timing); H1 2023: ~€855m; H2 2023: ~€848m; H1 2024: ~€910m; H2 2024E: ~€950m (H2 stronger resumes)

  • Strongest period: H2 in most years — government/defence customers concentrate renewals and new contracts in H2; Video broadcast contracts also tend to renew at calendar year-end [#A]

  • Weakest period: H1 — modestly so; difference is 2-5% of semi-annual revenue rather than a dramatic seasonal swing [#A]

  • Seasonality direction: Stable and modest — no evidence of seasonality intensifying or diminishing over L3Y; the pattern is structural to the business model [#J]

  • Cause — contract renewal cycles: Primary driver is B2B contract renewal timing; satellite capacity contracts typically multi-year with renewals clustering in Q4; not weather or production driven [#A]

  • mPOWER seasonality emerging: Uncertain — mPOWER serves mobility (maritime/aviation) and enterprise segments which may carry different seasonal patterns than legacy Video; insufficient history to establish pattern [#I]

  • Seasonality changing: Possible modest shift as mPOWER (maritime = some weather/volume seasonality) grows as proportion of revenue — worth monitoring but not yet material [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? If H2 2024 misses the stronger-H2 pattern (i.e., comes in weaker than H1 2024), this would signal mPOWER ramp is stalling rather than accelerating; the H2 2023 revenue miss vs consensus was partly attributed to mPOWER slippage and temporarily broke the H2-stronger pattern [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

J-front verdict: Management is a reasonable-but-imperfect tracker — conservative on GEO decline (slight sandbagging on downside containment), but materially overoptimistic on mPOWER ramp timing in 2022–2023; 2024 guidance appears recalibrated [#J]

  • Beat/miss frequency (L3Y, 6 periods): Revenue: 2 beats (H1 2024, H2 2024E), 4 misses (all 2022–2023) — miss-dominated pre-inflection [#A]
  • Sub: EBITDA: 2 beats, 3 small misses, 1 in-line — closer to consensus tracking; management better at protecting margins than hitting revenue

  • Sub: EPS adjusted: 1 beat (H1 2024), 5 misses/in-line — EPS harder to forecast given D&A volatility and interest charges

  • Beat magnitude (2024): H1 2024 revenue beat ~+€20m vs consensus (+2.2%) — modest but directionally significant given prior miss streak; EBITDA beat ~+€15m (+3.2%) [#A]

  • Miss magnitude (2022–2023): Revenue misses ranged €13-27m per half (-1.4% to -2.5%) — small in percentage terms but consistent in direction; cumulative signal of systematic consensus overoptimism on GEO recovery / mPOWER ramp [#A]

  • Sandbagging signal: Partial — management guidance on GEO Video decline rate has been conservative (actual declines came in at the better end of guidance range in H2 2022 and FY2023) [#A]

  • Sub: However, mPOWER revenue guidance was overoptimistic: guided ~€40m H2 2023, delivered ~€15m — a significant undershoot on the growth driver

  • Overpromise signal: Present on mPOWER ramp — management deferred H2 2023 mPOWER targets citing customer onboarding delays; deferred again to H1 2024; credibility cost is real [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Berenberg] noted in Jan 2024 note: "mPOWER ramp guidance has been reset twice in 18 months; we now apply a 2-quarter lag to management's stated ramp timelines"

  • Sub: [AS-JPMorgan] applied similar 15-20% haircut to FY2024 mPOWER guidance when initiating coverage in Q4 2023

  • Guidance accuracy trend: Improving in 2024 — FY2024 guidance set in Feb 2024 proved conservative; H1 2024 beat at both revenue and EBITDA; signals management recalibrated after 2023 miss; credibility partially restored [#A]

  • Direction of surprise: Revenue and mPOWER-specific surprises dominate; margin surprises smaller (EBITDA margin outcomes closer to guide than revenue) — management has better cost visibility than revenue [#A]

  • Sell-side revision pattern: Consistent downward revisions to FY2023 estimates through 2023 (consensus revenue moved from ~€1,800m to ~€1,730m over the year); 2024 consensus has been stable-to-rising since H1 2024 beat [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Deutsche Bank] revised FY2024 EBITDA +4% post H1 2024 results; [AS-Citi] revised revenue +2% same period

  • Honest-tracker assessment: Honest on legacy , optimistic on growth (mPOWER) — a common pattern for companies pivoting to a new product; more a structural behaviour than a credibility crisis [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on management credibility? mPOWER ramp delays were not one-off slippage — they reflected genuine uncertainty about customer readiness and technology deployment; if H2 2024 and FY2025 mPOWER targets are deferred again, the sandbagging-recalibration story collapses and management enters a credibility deficit that depresses multiple; consistent beats on GEO decline may be sandbagging running out of cushion as Video approaches a smaller denominator [#J]
