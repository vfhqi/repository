KEY FINDINGS (BLUF)

  • Net signal: LTMC earnings trend is exceptional for its peer group — revenue 3-year CAGR ~17%, EBITDA margin expanding 28pp since 2021, ANP +45% in FY2025 — driven by structural online mix-shift and M&A-aided scale build. The absolute level of delivery is peer-best among European gaming operators with a comparable retail-to-online transition narrative. [#J]
  • EBITDA margin trajectory best-in-class: 11% (FY2021) → 38% (FY2025) → 39.1% (Q1 2026) — LTM's margin is now aligned with Flutter (~39%), having compressed the gap from ~28pp to ~1pp in four years. Peer Entain (20%) and Evoke (23%) remain far behind, demonstrating LTM's operational execution. [#A]
  • Revenue growth decelerating naturally: +23% (FY2024, M&A-assisted) → +10-12% (FY2025, post-SKS365 consolidation) → guided +6-9% (FY2026). The step-down is structural and expected: the M&A acquisition phase is complete and organic growth is the primary driver going forward. [#A]
  • ANP outgrowing EBITDA: ANP grew +45% (FY2025) vs EBITDA +21% — driven by falling interest expense as leverage declines (3.7x → 2.4x). This leverage-to-EPS transmission dynamic will continue through FY2026-FY2027 as net debt falls further. [#J]
  • EPS upgrade momentum: 14 net EPS upgrades in L12M vs flat/slightly negative EBITDA revisions — the market is recognising the P&L leverage below EBITDA that most gaming models don't have. Consensus FY2026 EPS: €2.26 (~18x P/E at current price — not demanding for a 20%+ EPS grower). [#A]
  • Guidance beat pattern: Four consecutive guidance beats/raises since IPO (May 2023). Buffer discipline is evident — management consistently guides conservatively. [#J]

  • Organic growth quality: The FY2023 +2% was notably weak — reflects COVID recovery normalisation (FY2022 was boosted). The core organic pace is better framed as: FY2022 + FY2024-2025 average = ~12-14% pa, with FY2023 as a pause year. [#A]

Sceptical bullet (§1): Revenue growth at +6-9% guided for FY2026 is the lowest growth rate since FY2023's +2% pause year. The deceleration is natural post-M&A, but a scenario where the Italian online gaming market also decelerates to 10% (from 15%+ in 2025) would put LTM at risk of missing even the low end of guidance. The +6% scenario implies LTM is not gaining market share; +9% implies slight gains — the gap between the two represents ~€60m revenue and ~€24m EBITDA. ❌ [#A]

§2 — EBITDA TREND + MARGIN EXPANSION

Net signal: EBITDA expansion is the single most powerful financial signal for LTM over the last 4 years — from €153m (FY2021) to €856m (FY2025) = 41% pa CAGR; margin from 11% to 38%. The mechanics are well-understood (online mix) and the trajectory is continuing (39.1% in Q1 2026). [#J]

  • Margin at convergence with Flutter: LTM EBITDA margin 38% (FY2025) vs Flutter ~39% — LTM has essentially closed the margin gap with the world's best-in-class online gaming operator. Entain (~20%) and Evoke (~23%) remain far behind. [#J]
  • Margin driver — online mix: Online EBITDA margin estimated 50-55% (company does not publish per-segment EBITDA explicitly but implied from industry benchmarks). Blended margin rises as online grows toward 50% of revenue. [#A]
  • Margin driver — operational leverage: Fixed cost base (compliance, IT infrastructure, management overhead) is being spread over a growing revenue base. Customer acquisition costs per incremental euro of GGR declining as brand scale grows. [#A]
  • FY2021-FY2022 EBITDA spike (+136%): Primarily an accounting consolidation event — the Lottomatica B2C (IGT) acquisition in mid-2021 added a high-EBITDA business that was not in the 2021 full-year base. Not an organic doubling. [#A]
  • Guidance consistency: FY2024 EBITDA guidance €700-730m → outturn €739m (+1.2% beat). FY2025 EBITDA guidance originally €840-870m (Mar 2024) → raised to €856m outturn. FY2026 guidance €940-980m. [#I]

Sceptical bullet (§2): The EBITDA margin expansion story will eventually reach a structural ceiling. At 100% online revenue mix, the EBITDA margin ceiling would be approximately 50-55% — but LTM will never reach 100% online (Gaming Franchise and Sports Franchise have long-term licence obligations). At a sustainable equilibrium of ~50% online/50% retail, blended EBITDA margin likely plateaus at 41-44%. The "margin expansion" narrative has perhaps 3-5 percentage points of structural expansion remaining vs 28pp already captured. Future EPS growth must come increasingly from revenue growth and leverage reduction, not margin expansion. [#A]

Net signal: ANP is the fastest-growing P&L line — +45% in FY2025, +12% in Q1 2026 annualised — driven by triple leverage: (1) EBITDA growth, (2) interest expense decline as net debt falls, (3) improving tax efficiency. This leverage-to-EPS dynamic has 2-3 more years of run. [#J]

Interest saved per €100m net debt reduction at ~4% average cost = ~€4m pa pre-tax. Full path from 3.7x to 2.0x (€500-600m net debt reduction) = €20-24m incremental pre-tax EPS contribution. [#A]
  • EPS CAGR implied by consensus: €0.99 (FY2024) → €2.26 (FY2026E consensus) over 2 years = ~51% EPS CAGR. This is a very strong growth rate embedded in consensus — but the base is depressed by historical leverage costs. [#A]
  • P/E context: At current price €25.56 and FY2026E EPS €2.26, P/E = ~11.3x. At FY2027E (assuming further ~20% EPS growth = €2.70), P/E = ~9.5x. These multiples are materially below Flutter (25-30x forward) and below European regulated gaming median (~15x forward). [#A]
  • EPS upgrades L12M: +14 net upgrades — the strongest EPS revision profile identified in the gaming sector cohort at this depth. [#J]

Sceptical bullet (§3): The consensus FY2026 EPS of €2.26 implies 14x P/E on a ~€7.3bn EV company. This sounds cheap, but consider: (1) the €2.26 EPS is substantially leveraged on financial deleveraging (not all earnings quality); (2) the "true" earnings power excluding leverage benefit is approximately €1.60-1.80 (back-calculating at normalised leverage cost) — at which point P/E is ~14-16x, more fair value. The valuation discount to Flutter may be fully justified by single-market Italy risk and legacy retail exposure. ❌ [#A]

§4 — FREE CASH FLOW + CAPITAL RETURNS TREND

Net signal: FCF generation is growing strongly and is now being returned to shareholders rather than absorbed by leverage paydown — this marks a transition from a PE-deleveraging story to a capital-returns story. [#J]

  • Operating CF: FY2024 operating CF €556.8m (75% EBITDA conversion); Q1 2026 operating CF €196m (83% EBITDA conversion). Improving trajectory. [#A]
  • Capital return inflection: FY2024 dividend: €0.30/share (~€75m). FY2025 proposed: €0.44/share (~€111m = +47%). Buyback: 12.5% of shares authorised (€700m equivalent at current price, 18 months). [#A]
  • Dividend yield: At €25.56 and €0.44 dividend: 1.7% yield. Not a high-yield story but growing fast. [#I]
  • FCF yield (buyback + dividend): At current price and assuming €111m dividend + €350-400m annual buyback pace: ~3.5-4.5% total return yield. [#A]
