SECTION 1 — Headline Numbers and Consensus Comparison

  • Judgement: FY26 total return of £5,304m, or 22% on opening shareholders' funds — the standout headline metric and a clear beat on 3i's own 15% through-cycle target.
  • Analysis: This 22% return-on-equity comfortably exceeded the 15% target for the year ending March 2026 — 700bp of excess return.
  • Analysis: Total return printed slightly below the FY25 run-rate but within the historical 18-25% band Action-driven years have delivered since 2018.
  • Analysis: H-o-H read — H1 FY26 total return was £3,291m (13% on opening shareholders' funds) reported November 2025 — so H2 FY26 contributed roughly £2,013m or ~7% incremental.
  • Analysis: The implication — H2 was meaningfully softer than H1 in absolute terms, consistent with the in-period Action LFL deceleration.
  • Information: Total return is composed of Private Equity GIR £5,303m plus Infrastructure GIR £106m, less Group costs and net financial items.
  • Information: No formal sell-side consensus on total return is published by 3i (consensus is on NAV/share, dividend, and Action EBITDA).

1.2 NAV per share

  • Judgement: NAV per share of 3,030p at 31 March 2026 is the primary headline — up 19% on the 2,542p print at 31 March 2025.
  • Judgement: Quality of the NAV uplift is mixed — 77p (~3% of NAV) came from FX translation, not operational uplift; "clean" operating NAV growth was ~16%.
  • Analysis: Peer anchor — ICG-GB and EQT-SE report different metrics (FUM and fee-earning AUM) but 3IN-GB delivered 8.5% NAV total return — 3i's 19% remains the highest in the listed UK PE/infra peer set.
  • Analysis: Beat/miss — sell-side consensus had clustered around 2,960-2,980p NAV/share entering the print; 3,030p is a ~1-2% headline beat.
  • Analysis: The beat narrative was fully offset on print day by the Action in-period trading slowdown disclosure.
  • Information: NAV/share rose from 2,542p (31 March 2025) to 3,030p (31 March 2026) — absolute add of 488p including dividend deduction.
  • Information: Portfolio value reached £29.7bn at 31 March 2026, including long-term hold portfolio companies.

1.3 Dividend per share

  • Judgement: Total FY26 dividend of 84.5p — up 15% YoY — is the unambiguous "good news" line on the print and the income story analysts had been seeking.
  • Analysis: 84.5p split — interim 36.5p paid January 2026 plus a proposed second dividend of 48.0p payable July 2026 subject to AGM approval.
  • Analysis: Cumulative dividends since 2012 restructuring now reach ~£5.4bn — Borrows used this framing on the call to anchor the income credibility story.
  • Analysis: Peer anchor — ICG-GB historically pays a more progressive dividend versus NAV-anchored 3i; 3i's payout ratio remains modest at ~3% of NAV/share.
  • Analysis: Beat/miss — consensus had pencilled in ~82p total dividend; the 84.5p print is a modest beat and aligned with the 15% growth signal management has been guiding.
  • Information: Dividend policy remains "minimum equal to prior year" with 3i opting to grow rather than flat-line.
  • Information: The 48.0p second dividend confirms 3i is comfortable distributing despite the Action LFL slowdown.

1.4 EPS and earnings basis

  • Judgement: 3i is reported on an investment-company basis — total return per share, not GAAP EPS, is the substantive earnings line; EPS as conventionally framed is not the primary metric.
  • Analysis: Total return per share implied from £5,304m total return on ~970m shares is approximately 547p — versus 488p of headline NAV/share addition (after dividend).
  • Analysis: This is the highest annual total-return-per-share number 3i has delivered in absolute terms since the post-COVID Action revaluation cycle.
  • Information: 3i does not break out a "consensus EPS" line in the same way an operating company would.
  • Information: Sell-side modelling typically anchors on NAV/share growth, fee income, and Action EBITDA — not GAAP EPS.

1.5 Buyback announcement

  • Analysis: £750m on a ~£23bn-£25bn market cap pre-print is ~3% of shares outstanding — meaningful but not transformative.
