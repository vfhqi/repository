Section summary
  1. Forvia's strategic transition to a high-margin dual-cluster model and the divestiture of the dilutive Interiors business establishes a far healthier operational core

Forvia is executing a comprehensive strategic transition to a high-margin dual-cluster model through the divestiture of its dilutive Interiors business, targeting pro forma sales between €21bn and €22bn by 2028 under the IGNITE roadmap, which assumes global vehicle production remains flat at 94.9 million units and includes over

€1bn in planned value cluster disposals. The group maintains moderate customer concentration with Volkswagen representing 18.8% of 2025 total sales and Stellantis accounting for 10.5%, creating structural exposure to legacy European OEM volume declines. Forvia occupies a structurally squeezed Tier-1 systems integration stage with depressed mid-cycle margins averaging just 5.6% pro forma, though management targets group operating margins of at least 7.0% by 2028 through structural cost measures and favorable product mix shifts.

company commands a leading 30% global market share in seating mechanisms and ranks as the third- largest complete seat manufacturer globally, representing 70% of divisional sales. Forvia Electronics holds a leading 10% global market share in body control and radar modules, targeting a robust 8.9% sales CAGR to reach €3.8bn-€4.2bn by 2028.

Clean Mobility maintains powertrain-agnostic global leadership in exhaust and aftertreatment systems, with increasingly strict emission standards sustaining high-margin exhaust content for hybrids and range-extenders. Lifecycle Solutions delivered a highly resilient 9.7% operating margin in 2025, providing stable cash flows as parts replacement demand remains decoupled from new vehicle sales. CEO Martin Fischer assumed office on March 1, 2025, bringing deep automotive background from driving profitable division growth as a board member of ZF Group between 2019 and 2024, offering a fresh, unattached perspective to old Faurecia and HELLA assets.

The company delivered €400m in annualized HELLA cost synergies by the end of 2025, with the EU-FORWARD program expected to yield €500m in annualized cost savings by 2028.

Management aims to reduce the net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio to 1.5x by end-2026, supported by the Interiors sale which is expected to reduce Forvia's gross debt by more than €1.4bn and decrease annual run rate financing costs by €50m to €70m. global tier-1 automotive supplier industry faces depressed growth and structural overcapacity, with governments supporting loss-making domestic automakers keeping industrial capacity bloated and margins compressed, while May producer price inflation jumped with unprocessed raw materials rising 22.00% year-on- year, squeezing near-term margins for auto component manufacturers.

Forvia occupies a structurally squeezed Tier-1 systems integration stage exposed to intense downstream OEM price pressure, as tier-1 suppliers face unfavorable bargaining dynamics because automakers hold extreme purchasing leverage in price negotiations.

The balance of power across the automotive value chain remains firmly skewed downstream toward massive OEMs, with standard customer supply agreements generally mandating annual pricing step- downs over the life of vehicle production platforms, shifting productivity gains directly to the OEM, while raw material indexation clauses successfully protect suppliers from steel volatility but other cost centers like energy and logistics require prolonged customer-by-customer negotiations that often result in margin squeeze.

Forvia utilizes contractual indexing to pass raw material cost volatility directly to OEMs, successfully insulating the company from upward pressure in steel and chemical raw materials, while the group has successfully hedged more than 70% of its electricity requirements for 2026 and contractual pass-through agreements successfully mitigated over 84% of raw material inflation in 2023.

The Group does not use financial derivatives to hedge raw material purchases, leaving it structurally dependent on OEM indexation agreements.

Forvia's consolidated Days Sales Outstanding stood at 52 days in 2025, heavily weighed down by Asia where receivable collection averages a high 85 days, while the company's average Days of Purchases Outstanding reached 114 days in 2025, remaining virtually flat against the 115 days recorded in 2024. topic-specific reports 14 's return on invested capital stood at a modest 4.80% in 2025, though consensus estimates project group ROIC will improve to 6.20% in 2026 and 7.90% in 2027 following portfolio rationalization and margin expansion initiatives.

Custom software for ADAS radar and zonal modules locks vehicle manufacturers into Forvia's hardware, while complete vehicle seating systems cannot be easily switched due to complex cabin mechatronic safety certifications, defended by a robust portfolio of over 13,000 global patents.

However, accelerating OEM in-sourcing and direct semiconductor sourcing could bypass tier-1 suppliers entirely, structurally shrinking the outsourced component TAM.

principal limitations of this analysis include reliance on management guidance that assumes no further deterioration in European vehicle production, uncertainty regarding the pace of OEM software insourcing which could materially reduce systems integration margin pools, and execution risk on the Apollo Interiors transaction which remains subject to regulatory approvals and could face delays beyond late-2026 expectations.

Future research should focus on tracking the realization of EU-FORWARD cost savings against announced job reductions, monitoring the competitive response of Chinese domestic suppliers as they expand globally with lower-cost platforms, and assessing the financial health of Forvia's top-five customers to quantify downside volume exposure risks.
