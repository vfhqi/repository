Corporate Structure and History
Spirax Group is a global industrial technology leader headquartered in Cheltenham, United Kingdom. [1] With
a corporate history spanning over 130 years, the company has established a consistent track record of adapting
to macroeconomic and environmental shifts. [2] The group operates more than 30 strategically located
manufacturing plants worldwide and employs a workforce of approximately 10,000 colleagues. [3] Spirax Group
maintains a direct presence in nearly 70 countries, enabling the firm to serve an extensive base of over 100,000
global customers. [4] 1 3 • ARS 2 4 • ARS

model. [5] Approximately one in three colleagues is employed in a sales or service role, translating to a global
force of roughly 2,900 direct sales and application engineers. [6] This direct engagement allows the company to
focus heavily on consultative solution-selling rather than commoditized equipment distribution. [7] Furthermore,

Watson-Marlow Fluid Technology Solutions (WMFTS)
Watson-Marlow Fluid Technology Solutions engineers vital fluid handling technologies, specializing in peristaltic
pumps and associated fluid path components. The core technological advantage of a peristaltic pump is that
the pumped product remains entirely contained within a tube, ensuring the fluid never touches the pump's moving
mechanical parts. This mechanism provides industry-leading sterility, accuracy, and safety, making it ideal for
shear-sensitive, abrasive, or highly corrosive materials. [33] 31 33 • ARS 32 • Company Publication
The WMFTS division generates approximately 50% of its revenue from the Pharmaceutical and Biotechnology
(Biopharm) sector, supporting the complex upstream and downstream production of vaccines, monoclonal
antibodies, and cell therapies. The remainder of the segment's revenue comes from Process Industries, which
includes critical applications in water and wastewater treatment, mining, and food and beverage. [34]

cleanliness for clients while driving long-term profitability for the firm.
