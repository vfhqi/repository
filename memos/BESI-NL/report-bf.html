EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Investment Judgement

Technology Leadership and Competitive Positioning

BESI captures 67% global market share in hybrid bonding , establishing the benchmark tool standard that competitors struggle to replicate. BESI collaborates with Applied Materials to deliver the Kinex integrated die-towafer hybrid bonding platform, where Applied provides wafer processing and metrology expertise while BESI delivers sub-micron placement accuracy bonders, creating a front-end standard that establishes a moat against stand-alone back-end competitors . BESI has achieved an installed base of over 150 cumulative hybrid bonding systems by 2026, with the technology adopted by 20 customers spanning leading foundry, logic, and memory makers . Memory makers transition to hybrid bonding because it reduces HBM stack temperatures by 20% versus traditional TCB , providing a compelling thermal efficiency advantage that supports premium pricing. Systems command a 10% to 15% price premium relative to ASMPT due to superior alignment speeds , demonstrating tangible performance-based differentiation . Traditional thermocompression bonding remains a fiercely contested oligopoly where Hanmi commands 90% market share in TCB tools supplied to SK Hynix for high-bandwidth memory , while ASMPT holds a dominant position in logic chip-to-substrate TCB tools and expects the TCB addressable market to expand from $760 million to $1.6 billion by 2028 .

Financial Profile and Peer Benchmarking

Value Chain Position and Margin Distribution

BESI concentrates exclusively on high-value advanced die attach systems, capturing the absolute highest-margin value chain stage and securing durable gross margins of 63% to 65% . Contract assemblers realize gross margins near 14% to 26% due to high material costs , while high-precision tool providers command gross margins above 60% due to intense technological barriers , confirming asymmetric profit pool distribution favoring upstream equipment specialists. BESI commands superior pricing power over downstream foundry and OSAT customers due to sub-micron accuracy differentiation, and once a customer integrates BESI's hybrid bonders into cleanrooms, switching vendors becomes extremely unlikely . BESI's high-precision hybrid bonding systems cost between $1.5 million and $2 million per tool, with higher unit costs justified by UPH improvements that

reduce the total number of tools a customer must purchase . BESI executes only final assembly and testing while outsourcing substantially all base units and modules to subcontractors, with 80 suppliers accounting for approximately 80% of total purchasing volume , creating a highly flexible but concentrated supply base . 21 23

Industry Structure and Competitive Dynamics

The semiconductor packaging and assembly market represents a highly cyclical capital equipment segment , with back-end process investments projected to reach US$24.9bn in 2026 and US$29.3bn in 2027. Advanced packaging capital equipment spending is expected to expand by more than 50% in fiscal 2026 alone, substantially outpacing overall wafer fab equipment growth rates . The back-end assembly market exhibits deep concentration with BESI holding 11% overall share, ASMPT at 9%, Kulicke & Soffa at 8%, and Samsung-owned SEMES at 5%. Competitor ASMPT is actively developing its second-generation hybrid bonding platform to address nextgeneration packaging, though adoption remains slow and is still undergoing active evaluation phases . Frontend semicap giants are aggressively expanding into advanced packaging, posing major M&A and entry threats, with takeover rumors circulating around BESI citing Lam Research, ASML, and Applied Materials as potential bidders . The industry-wide transition to hybrid bonding faces near-term delays of up to two years as JEDEC height standards are relaxed, allowing memory makers to prolong existing TCB asset utilization .

Structural Moats and Defensive Positioning

Once BESI bonders are qualified inside customer cleanrooms, switching to rival platforms is highly costprohibitive, with next-generation hybrid bonding tools requiring up to 12 months of active testing and process

Disruption Threats and Adoption Delays

JEDEC members are discussing raising HBM stack height limits to 825-900 microns for 20-high configurations, with historical standards relaxations from 720 to 775 microns successfully materializing and prolonging legacy back-end tool utility . Easing thickness limits allows memory makers to prolong existing thermocompression bonding tools and delay hybrid bonding, as entrenched memory producers seek to maximize the lifespan of their substantial capital investments in TCB tooling . High-volume hybrid bonding faces steep yield challenges, with initial 12-high stacking yields estimated at 20% to 30%, and stacking up to 20 thin DRAM layers causes geometric yield degradation during high-volume manufacturing . Despite relaxed standards, critical thermal and speed requirements will force high-end HBM4E designs to adopt hybrid bonding, as bumpless hybrid configurations reduce HBM stack thermal resistance by more than 20 percent . High upfront capital requirements and complex tool engineering prevent smaller startups from entering the sub-micron assembly market , establishing durable barriers to new competition . Samsung relies on its subsidiary SEMES for in-house advanced packaging tools, reducing external equipment capital costs, though Samsung actively dual-sources hybrid bonders from BESI and SEMES to ensure cleanroom supply , demonstrating vertical integration risks tempered by dual-sourcing preferences .
