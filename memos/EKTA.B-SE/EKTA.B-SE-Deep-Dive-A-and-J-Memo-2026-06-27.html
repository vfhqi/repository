B EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)
Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.

Bottom Line Up Front — third pass, robust depth

Investment verdict: CONDITIONAL YELLOW-GREEN. Three conditions, all due 28 May 2026.

The DD has produced a picture of a company in genuine operational recovery — new CEO with a measurable restructuring track record, a cost programme that is >80% complete and landing as designed, improving order momentum in China and (nascent) in the US post-Evo FDA clearance. Against this, the competitive landscape has deteriorated more sharply than the prior C-only memo estimated: a new Varian linac platform confirmed for September–October 2026 narrows Evo's window materially; UIH has achieved CE marking for its RT systems in early 2026 and is now a live European competitor in the current procurement cycle, not a 3–5 year risk; and the ARPA-H FLASH threat timeline has been revised from 7 years to 4–5 years. The stock trades at EV/EBITDA ~7.1x and EV/EBITA 11.5–13.5x — materially cheaper than the prior memo assumed — which partially compensates for the deteriorated competitive outlook. But cheap does not clear the DD gate on its own.

The three gate conditions remain unchanged and are binary: 1. No further order book write-down (Q4 FY26 report, due ~28 May) 2. Adjusted EBIT margin ≥13.5% for FY26 full year 3. Double-digit US order growth confirmed post-Evo FDA clearance (Jan 2026)

If all three clear: upgrade to YELLOW-GREEN, progress to DD P2 (financial model + valuation). If any one fails: park in [-] and reassess at Q1 FY2026/27.

Five BLUF judgements:

1. The operational recovery is real and measurable but front-loaded with execution risk. The SEK 500M annualised savings programme (~290bps on the revenue base) is substantially banked. Q3 FY26 adjusted EBIT margin was ~12%, trending toward the Q4 target. The CEO's 3Shape track record (EBITDA 8% → 35%+) is the most credible evidence that margin targets are achievable. The risk is not the cost programme — it is whether volume recovery in the US and China arrives before the new Varian platform (Sept–Oct 2026) resets customer purchase decisions.

2. Competitive headwinds are more acute than the prior C-only memo reflected. Three simultaneous deteriorations: Varian new platform launch confirmed for September–October 2026 (Jefferies, DNB Carnegie); UIH CE marking achieved early 2026 — they can now bid on European RT tenders in the current cycle; ARPA-H FLASH timeline is 4–5 years to clinical milestone, not 7 years. Collectively these compress Evo's window from ~18 months to ~5 months before the next competitive intervention.

3. The stock is genuinely cheap. EV/EBITDA ~7.1x and EV/EBITA 11.5–13.5x (Deutsche Bank, DNB Carnegie FY27 estimates) represent a material discount to sector norms. This is not priced for recovery — it is priced for continued disappointment. If the Q4 gate conditions clear and CMD (17 June) delivers a credible margin bridge, the re-rating opportunity is real. Bull case: DNB Carnegie SEK70 (+20% from current). Bear case: Deutsche Bank SEK49 (-15%).

4. Watson verdict is CONDITIONAL, not unconditional, for disciplined reasons. The pre-mortem assigns 65% failure probability to the full bull thesis. The three simultaneous compound failure vectors — CEO learning curve, Siemens/Varian scale, MOSAIQ displacement — are all active and have not been falsified. The Q4 gate is not set high; it is set at the minimum level needed to trust the narrative. If the company can't deliver this minimum, the thesis is broken before it started.

5. CMD 17 June 2026 is the upgrade gate for all active sell-side coverage. DNB Carnegie (BUY), Jefferies (Hold), and Deutsche Bank (Hold) are all explicitly waiting for CMD guidance. Jefferies writes "CMD In Sight" as their entire framing. This means the stock has a binary catalyst structure in the next 5 weeks: Q4 report (28 May) then CMD (17 June). Both must deliver or the thesis breaks.
