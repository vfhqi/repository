EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 29 findings

Elementis plc has executed a transformational strategic pivot from a multi-segment industrial chemicals conglomerate into a pure-play specialty additives business focused exclusively on high-margin hectorite clay derivatives serving coatings and personal care end-markets. The portfolio transition involved divesting its commoditized Chromium segment in January 2023, capital-intensive Talc operations in May 2025, and non-core pharmaceutical manufacturing to Associated British Foods in June 2026, while simultaneously executing the bolton acquisition of sustainable skincare modifier specialist Alchemy Ingredients in November 2025. This fundamental reshaping occurred against a backdrop of persistent activist shareholder pressure and followed the installation of an experienced internal CEO in Luc van Ravenstein, who launched the ambitious Elevate Elementis strategy targeting industry-leading 23%-plus operating margins and greater than 30% returns on capital employed.

simplified business model , having shed low-return, capital-intensive assets to concentrate resources on its proprietary 60-plus year high-grade hectorite mine in California, which serves as the foundation for value expansion from commoditized clay into blended bio-actives commanding pricing premiums exceeding five times historical levels.
