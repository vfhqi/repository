§3 — Industry Context (Peer Comparison, Structure, Macro + Non-Macro Change)

Net signal: Swiss energy infrastructure operates in a concentrated, quasi-monopolistic regulated environment with benign competition; the Services market is fragmented but consolidating — BKW benefits from both structural positions, but macro headwinds (construction slowdown) and Services M&A multiple inflation are live risks [#J]

Market structure: Swiss electricity distribution is a regulated monopoly market — DSOs hold geographic concessions; no new entrant competition [#I]
- Sub-bullet: ~600+ Swiss DSOs nationally, but cantonal consolidation has reduced meaningful competitors to ~20 large entities (EKZ, EKS, EnB, Romande, BKW, etc.)
- Sub-bullet: BKW serves ~400,000 end customers in its concession area (Canton Bern + parts of neighbouring cantons) — a captive base

Concentration: Swiss DSO market highly concentrated at regional level — effectively monopolistic within each concession zone; at national level, top 5 DSOs account for ~50-60% of total grid assets [#A]
- Sub-bullet: BKW is among the top 3 Swiss DSOs by regulated asset base — strong relative position within the regulatory framework

Competitive intensity: Networks — zero competition (concession monopoly); Energy generation — moderate (Swiss hydro pool, pumped storage, cross-border trading); Services — moderate-to-high fragmentation [#A]
- Sub-bullet: Services market in Switzerland/DACH is highly fragmented — thousands of electrical/mechanical contractors; top-5 share probably <20% nationally
- Sub-bullet: BKW is consolidating this fragmented Services market — but it is not yet a dominant force nationally in any single services sub-discipline

New entrant: No meaningful new entrant risk in Networks (regulatory barrier); Energy generation — some new solar/wind developers, but BKW's hydro fleet is non-replicable; Services — constant flow of new sub-scale entrants from tradesperson/contractor sector [#A]
- Sub-bullet: Digital energy management (smart grid software, energy-as-a-service) is the area of greatest new entrant/disintermediation risk — tech players could bypass traditional contractors

Disruption: Digitalisation of building systems (BMS, energy management software, IoT) creates both opportunity (BKW has acquired digital energy management capabilities) and risk (software players displacing hardware contractors) [#A]
- Sub-bullet: Distributed solar and battery storage could reduce grid-dependent customer dependency over 10-20 years — a long-cycle risk to Networks, not a 3Y threat

BtE (barriers to entry): Networks — absolute regulatory barrier (concession required); Energy generation — hydro assets are irreplaceable physical assets; Services — licensing/certification required but not prohibitive for a well-capitalised entrant [#A]
- Sub-bullet: Services: barriers are local relationships, multi-trade capability, and scale rather than formal entry barriers — low BtE by BB#2 standards

Macro shift — L12M and N36M: Swiss construction activity moderated in 2023-24 — residential construction slowdown weighs on building technical services demand [#A]
- Sub-bullet: Commercial energy efficiency retrofit demand accelerated post-Swiss energy law reform (2024 Energiegesetz) — positive for BKW's energy retrofit/grid connection services
- Sub-bullet: European power prices normalised 2023-24 from 2022 spike — Energy segment EBIT structurally lower post-normalisation; not a temporary headwind but a return to mean

- Sub-bullet: Lower rates also support Services M&A multiples — EV/EBITDA multiples for European technical services businesses rose 8-12x in 2021-22, partly compressed in 2023; recovery possible if rates stay low

- Sub-bullet: Swiss Energiegesetz accelerates renewable deployment obligations — positive for Networks investment program and BKW's grid services

Political shift: Swiss cantonal ownership structure insulates BKW from privatisation pressure; federal energy policy broadly supportive of infrastructure investment [#I]
- Sub-bullet: Risk: Swiss direct democracy (referenda) can constrain infrastructure siting — transmission line permitting delays are a persistent execution risk

Thematic trend: Energy transition spending in Switzerland structurally elevated over next 10-15 years — transmission/distribution reinforcement, EV charging infrastructure, heat pump installation — all addressable by BKW across Networks and Services [#A]
- Sub-bullet: BKW has positioned Services to capture energy transition capex from commercial customers — solar EPC, heat pump installation, EV charging infrastructure
