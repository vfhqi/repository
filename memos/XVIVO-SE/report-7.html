Driver Assessment

1. Heart Transplant Market Entry

Current Status: XVIVO is transitioning from a lung-focused company to a multi-organ platform. Heart is the centrepiece of this transformation.

European pathway: The NIHP2019 European multicenter trial results were published in the European Heart Journal in April 2026. Data is compelling: XVIVO Heart Assist Transport showed a 38% reduction in severe complications (33% vs 47% control) and superior 12-month survival (92% vs 86%). The XVIVO Heart Assist Transport device and Perfusion Set have received CE marking. However, the heart perfusion solution and supplement face a 6-12 month CE delay because the notified body has referred to the EMA and another EU competent authority for a scientific opinion before issuing the certificate. This is a procedural delay, not a safety signal, but it pushes European commercial launch into H2 2026 at earliest, more likely early 2027.

US pathway: The PRESERVE trial enrolled 141 patients across leading US heart transplant centres, completing enrolment five months ahead of schedule -- a strong signal of clinical demand. One-year follow-up data collection is ongoing, with preliminary results planned for ISHLT Toronto 2026. The FDA-approved Continued Access Protocol study -- with the first US patient enrolled in July 2025 at 26 transplant centres -- allows continued clinical use while PMA data is compiled. PMA submission is targeted for H1 2026. However, an FDA expert panel meeting is expected, adding at least 180 days to the review timeline. Realistic US commercial launch: 2027, possibly H2 2027.

TAM: Global heart transplants total approximately 6,000-6,500 per year, with the US representing roughly 4,500. Current standard of care is static cold storage (ice). TransMedics OCS Heart is the only FDA-approved warm perfusion alternative. XVIVO's hypothermic approach is differentiated -- portable, no need for continuous warm perfusion, potentially simpler logistics. If XVIVO captures even 20-30% of hearts in key markets, this could add SEK 300-500m+ in annual revenue at maturity, roughly doubling the current revenue base.

DCD hearts expansion: The FDA granted XVIVO approval to include DCD (donation after circulatory death) hearts in its IDE clinical trial. This is strategically important because DCD hearts are the fastest-growing donor pool (25% to 35% of all donations in recent years), and hypothermic perfusion may be particularly well-suited to preserving these more fragile organs. This expands the addressable market beyond standard brain-dead donors.

Direction of Travel: Improving. Clinical data is strong and getting stronger. Regulatory pathway is proceeding, albeit with delays. Commercial infrastructure is being built (2026 is the investment year for a 2027 launch).

Impact Magnitude: HIGH. This is potentially transformational -- it could double the addressable market and fundamentally change the company's growth trajectory and valuation multiple.

Probability-Weighted Assessment: 70% probability of US approval by end-2027. 80% probability of European launch by H1 2027. The clinical data is strong enough that outright rejection is unlikely; the question is timing. Probability-weighted revenue impact: SEK 150-250m run-rate by 2029 (conservative), SEK 400-600m at maturity (optimistic).

2. US Regulatory Tailwinds (CMS OPO Rules)

Current Status: CMS issued proposed rule CMS-3409-P on 28 January 2026, fundamentally changing how OPOs are evaluated. The critical shift: OPOs are now measured on actual transplantation rates (organs that reach a recipient), not just procurement rates. This creates a direct economic incentive for OPOs to maximise the usability of every recovered organ.

Previously, an OPO could procure an organ, have it discarded, and still count it positively. Under the new framework, only transplanted organs count. This changes behaviour: OPOs now need technologies that can assess and recondition marginal organs to make them transplantable.

EVLP (ex vivo lung perfusion) is precisely such a technology. It allows assessment of marginal donor lungs outside the body, potentially converting "no-go" organs into transplantable ones. XVIVO's XPS system is the market-leading EVLP platform. The rule also expands the eligible donor pool by including DCD donors and extended criteria donors more aggressively.

The data supports the trend: non-usable/non-transplantable kidneys recovered have more than doubled in four years, and DCD donors have risen from 25% to 35% of all donations. OPOs are increasingly desperate to find programmes that will accept and transplant these marginal organs.

Direction of Travel: Improving. The regulatory direction is clear -- more accountability, more organ utilisation, more technology adoption. This is a multi-year structural tailwind, not a one-off event. The final rule implementation in 2026 will generate widespread disruption to the US organ donation system, and XVIVO is on the right side of that disruption.

Impact Magnitude: MEDIUM-HIGH. This does not create immediate revenue spikes, but it shifts the demand curve structurally upward for XVIVO's core products. It also creates a favourable environment for the heart and kidney launches.

Probability-Weighted Assessment: 85% probability that the final rule is implemented substantially as proposed (it has broad political support). The impact on XVIVO is indirect but persistent -- expect a 3-5 percentage point uplift in underlying EVLP demand growth over the next 3 years.

Key Monitoring Signals:
- CMS final rule publication and implementation timeline
- OPO performance data releases (quarterly)
- EVLP utilisation rates at major US transplant centres
- Any legal challenges to the rule (AOPO has already filed suit)
- Changes in organ discard rates

3. TMDX Competitive Vacuum

Current Status: The "TMDX in trouble" narrative is more nuanced than it appears. TransMedics grew revenue 37% to $605.5m in 2025, completed 5,139 US OCS cases (up 38% YoY), and generated $152m in positive cash flow. This is not a company in distress.

However, TransMedics faces genuine headwinds. In January 2025, Scorpion Capital published a short report alleging fraudulent billing schemes, off-label device misuse, and kickbacks. Multiple law firms launched securities fraud investigations. The company conducted an independent review (Kirkland & Ellis + forensic accountants) and reported no evidence of misconduct, but the investigations are ongoing (DOJ, IRS, state AGs looking at Medicare fraud linked to organ acquisition costs). The stock has been volatile.

Additionally, TransMedics' vertically integrated model (owning 22 aircraft, acquiring Summit Aviation) creates operational complexity and cost exposure that XVIVO avoids entirely. There are reports of aviation safety concerns and pilot retaliation issues.

The competitive dynamic is more subtle than "TMDX weakness = XVIVO gain." The two companies use fundamentally different technology approaches: TransMedics uses warm perfusion (keeping the organ functioning at body temperature during transport), while XVIVO uses hypothermic perfusion (cooling the organ and using machine perfusion to maintain viability). These are complementary more than directly competitive in many clinical settings. However, in heart transplantation specifically, they will compete directly -- TMDX with OCS Heart (warm) vs. XVIVO Heart Assist Transport (hypothermic).

Where XVIVO could gain: if TMDX's legal/regulatory issues slow its commercial momentum, create uncertainty among transplant centres, or distract management, XVIVO's cleaner corporate story and simpler business model could attract centres that want an alternative. The CAP study at 26 US centres is already building clinical relationships.

Direction of Travel: Mildly improving for XVIVO's relative position. TMDX's absolute position remains strong, but the reputational overhang from investigations creates opportunity at the margin.

Impact Magnitude: MEDIUM. TMDX is not collapsing. The competitive benefit to XVIVO is incremental, not transformational. In lungs, XVIVO and TMDX serve somewhat different clinical needs (cold vs warm). In hearts, the competition will be direct but XVIVO needs FDA approval first (2027+).

Probability-Weighted Assessment: 40% probability that TMDX investigations result in material commercial disruption (fines, consent decrees, or changed clinical behaviour at major centres). 60% probability this is resolved without material impact on TMDX's market position. Net effect on XVIVO: modest positive optionality.
