EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Électricité de Strasbourg is undergoing a material governance and strategic transformation, with tightening EDF alignment through board refreshes and the separation of Chairman and CEO roles, while simultaneously pivoting toward high-stakes geothermal lithium extraction alongside its traditional regional utility operations, though this strategic shift drastically alters the risk profile from predictable regulated returns to speculative mining exposure.

Employee turnover improved structurally to 8.35% in 2025 from 9.94% previously, yet board independence fails AFEP-MEDEF thresholds with only two independent directors, effectively neutralizing minority shareholder influence under overwhelming EDF control. CEO Marc Kugler provides strong strategic continuity since 2015, now supported by Deputy CEO Anne-Véronique Fauré in an orderly executive burden-sharing arrangement established in September 2024.

The strategic transformation centers on the 2027 Carbone & Climat programme targeting deep regional decarbonisation, anchored by the transformative Alsace Geothermie Lithium joint venture with Eramet that aims to produce 15,000 tonnes of lithium carbonate annually by 2030, supplying 10-15% of French automotive battery requirements and establishing material European sovereignty leverage. European Union designation as a strategic project in March 2025 unlocked accelerated administrative pathways, though final investment decision is prudently deferred until late 2027, mitigating near-term capital intensity risks during pre-feasibility studies.

1.
The fundamental investment tension is stark: the pivot from predictable regional utility to speculative mining proxy could heavily compress near-term return on capital if wholesale energy margins compress further or lithium extraction proves commercially unviable at scale

French electricity demand remains structurally suppressed by industrial weakness, with transmission operator RTE cutting 2035 consumption projections over 5% while warning supply heavily outpaces demand growth, though the company's weather-adjusted distributed volumes held essentially flat at 6,911 GWh in 2024 versus 6,923 GWh in 2023, reflecting persistent post-crisis consumption sobriety. Data center expansion and AI workloads provide a long-term growth floor, projected to add up to 20 TWh of incremental French baseload demand by 2030.

Accelerating renewable penetration is severely disrupting grid operations, driving record 513 hours of negative spot power prices in 2025 up from 352 hours in 2024, forcing EDF to curtail over 30 TWh of nuclear output through damaging modulation cycles that accelerate turbine wear and maintenance costs. The regulatory framework is undergoing generational overhaul, with ARENH's fixed €42/MWh access formally ending December 31, 2025, replaced by the Universal Nuclear Payment capturing 50% of EDF revenues above €78/MWh starting January 2026, fundamentally reshaping alternative supplier economics.

Domestic power supply is heavily oversupplied following normalization of EDF's nuclear fleet to 372 TWh generation in 2025, forcing structural export dependence with French electricity exports hitting a record 92.1 TWh. Pricing cycles exhibit extreme intraday volatility with baseload forward prices crashing 60% to near €48/MWh for 2026 delivery, returning the French wholesale market to a structurally oversupplied, low-price environment following the full unwinding of the energy crisis peak.

The macroeconomic backdrop remains fragile with French public deficit projected to widen to 5.4% of GDP by 2027, heavily restricting state capacity to subsidize future energy transitions, while elevated ECB interest rates continue suppressing European industrial activity and keeping aggregate power demand 6% below pre-crisis levels.

Strategic execution milestones include successfully installing 500,000 Linky meters achieving 80% park penetration to enable smart grid transition, formalizing mission-driven status embedding societal goals directly into corporate mandate, and consolidating renewable production to 114 GWh thermal and 91.8 GWh electrical. However, a material negative surprise emerged with the ECOGI geothermal plant at Rittershoffen halted following a seismic event in December 2025, though AGELI lithium project studies continue on track preserving the core diversification thesis.

Published sensitivities focus entirely on post-employment benefit liabilities rather than operational metrics, with pension discount rates using a 3.90% base for 2025 where a 0.25% increase reduces N+1 charges by 6% while a 0.25% drop mechanically increases charges by 7%, and crucially a 0.25% increase in salary growth assumptions drives a 12% spike in N+1 pension charges.

The aggressive dividend hikes mask a structurally rising net debt burden that increased to €140.9 million by end-2025 from €96.1 million in 2024, creating risk that if wholesale energy margins compress further, the combination of high network capex and elevated payouts could force an abrupt deleveraging cycle.
