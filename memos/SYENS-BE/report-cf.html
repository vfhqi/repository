Bottom-Line Investment Verdict

Syensqo represents a high-conviction specialty materials transformation play undergoing a complete strategic and governance overhaul following its December 2023 demerger from Solvay, with the critical catalyst being the May 2026 strategic review of the €2.0 billion Performance & Care segment aimed at creating a pure-play advanced materials company focused on aerospace, defense, electronics, and advanced mobility . The leadership transition to CEO Mike Radossich in January 2026, following Dr. Ilham Kadri's departure after credibility challenges and guidance downgrades, signals a pivotal shift toward operational accountability and disciplined capital allocation . The institutional landscape shifted materially in late May 2026 when BlackRock crossed the 3.06% direct voting rights threshold, valued at approximately €196 million, introducing heightened capital allocation scrutiny that could drive a specialty re-rating of 15% to 40% via multiple expansion . The core investment thesis centers on portfolio high-grading that could expand group EBITDA margins by approximately 260 basis points through the exit of lower-margin Aroma Performance and Performance & Care businesses , while the company navigates a painful cyclical trough in semiconductor polymers and consumer electronics

that has compressed margins from peak pandemic levels.

Materiality of Strategic and Operational Change

The complete overhaul of senior leadership represents a direct reaction to recent share price turmoil and guidance downgrades, with board Chair Heike van de Kerkhof appointed in March 2026 to replace Rosemary Thorne, following five board departures and significant executive turnover in under 12 months . The strategic review launched on May 21, 2026 aims to transform Syensqo into a pure-play specialty materials company focused on high-growth areas including aerospace, defense, electronics, and advanced mobility, with the Performance & Care segment generating €2.0 billion in sales and €358 million in underlying EBITDA in 2025 . Management has already demonstrated execution capability by completing the sale of its Oil & Gas business to SNF Group in January 2026 at an enterprise value of €135 million (approximately 7x EV/EBITDA), immediately elevating group EBITDA margins by ~100 basis points . The Board recalibrated short-term incentive metrics for 2026 to center on net sales, EBIT, and operating cash flow to strengthen financial discipline, with CEO Radossich's compensation package set lower than his predecessor's and aligned with the median of the target peer group . The cumulative strategic, governance, and operational changes represent a material inflection point in the company's post-demerger evolution, though execution risks remain elevated given the complexity of carving out a €2.0 billion segment while managing cyclical headwinds.
