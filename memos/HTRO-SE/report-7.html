Key Drivers Assessment — Hexatronic Group AB (HTRO SS)
Key Takeaways

A 25%+ IRR thesis over 24 months is plausible but aggressive (Yellow/Orange rating), requiring the macroeconomic alignment of hyperscaler AI spending, US federal broadband subsidies, and a flawless string of synergistic acquisitions to reach a mid-50s SEK share price.

European FTTH markets remain severely challenged, with sales plummeting 31% year-over-year in Q1 2026 due to structural overcapacity and a strategic shift from network expansion to subscriber connections, drastically reducing bulk microduct demand.

North America marks a critical inflection as the US Fiber Solutions market returned to positive organic growth in Q1 2026 with broad-based momentum across the product suite, earning a Green rating as the most reliable near-term driver.

Margin recovery is on track (Green rating) with adjusted EBITA margin improving to 8.6% in Q1 2026 after five consecutive quarters of decline, supported by SEK 120 million in annualized savings from completed restructuring programs.

Data Center hyper-growth requires reaching the SEK 3 billion revenue target by 2028 while sustaining 15% EBITA margins through the aggressive execution of three to six service-oriented acquisitions per year at historical multiples of 5x to 7x EBITDA.

Management execution under new CEO Rikard Fröberg earns a Yellow rating due to his lack of prior telecom experience, though early signs are promising with decisive restructuring action and a disciplined M&A-heavy strategy requiring a multi-year track record.

Balance sheet strength remains robust (Green rating) with 2.2x EBITDA leverage, ample SEK 1.7 billion liquidity, and recent acquisitions (JOWO, Communication Zone) executed at attractive 4x to 6x EBITDA multiples without over-leveraging the group.

Overall Assessment and 25%+ IRR Thesis Evaluation

A 25%+ Internal Rate of Return over a 24-month horizon requires a cumulative share price appreciation of approximately 56%. With Hexatronic's stock trading in the SEK 32 to SEK 38 range during the Q1 2026 period, achieving this return profile necessitates a target price between SEK 50 and SEK 59 by mid-2028. The plausibility of this thesis depends entirely on Hexatronic executing a flawless strategic pivot from a cyclical Fibre-to-the-Home supplier to a diversified digital infrastructure powerhouse.

Redeye's published Bull Case explicitly models a target valuation of SEK 58 per share, perfectly aligning with the upper bound required for a 25%+ IRR. To justify this valuation, the broker assumes an optimistic scenario featuring a terminal EBIT margin of 13%, a 2025-2031e sales CAGR of 8%, and the successful mitigation of long-term FTTH market saturation. The broker notes that in this upside scenario, Hexatronic must increase its non-FTTH revenues rapidly enough to offset the inevitable downturn in legacy fibre markets by 2030.

Driver 1: Fibre Demand Recovery in Core Markets
Overview of the Primary Fulcrum Driver

The stabilization and subsequent recovery of fibre demand across Hexatronic's core markets—Sweden, the UK, and the US—serves as the single most determinative factor for the company's near-term share price performance. The legacy Fiber Solutions segment remains the largest revenue contributor for the group, meaning that factory utilization rates and overall gross margins are highly sensitive to end-market deployment volumes. Following a protracted period of inventory digestion and macroeconomic headwinds, the market is currently exhibiting a stark regional divergence between a persistently weak European landscape and a stabilizing North American sector.

European FTTH Challenges and Duct Overcapacity

The European Fibre-to-the-Home market continues to face severe structural and macroeconomic headwinds that are stifling demand. Sales in Europe plummeted by 31% year-over-year in the first quarter of 2026, exacerbated by an unusually cold winter that severely delayed installation groundwork. Management expects these challenging conditions to persist throughout the remainder of 2026 without any material near-term improvement.

A primary drag on European volumes is the strategic shift among network operators from building "homes passed" to connecting individual subscribers to existing infrastructure. This transition drastically reduces the bulk demand for microducts, which historically served as a high-volume product category for Hexatronic. Consequently, the industry is grappling with acute overcapacity. During the pandemic-era broadband boom, Hexatronic and its competitors aggressively expanded manufacturing footprints, resulting in a supply glut that has severely compressed pricing power across the duct and conduit markets. Higher financing costs and elevated inflation have further suppressed the appetite for capital-intensive rollout projects in key growth markets like Germany and the UK.

North American Market Dynamics and Seasonality

Conversely, the North American market is beginning to validate expectations of a gradual structural recovery. The United States market officially returned to positive organic growth in the first quarter of 2026, demonstrating a broad-based uptick across both new private-equity-backed tier-2 networks and existing legacy clients. This positive momentum spans the entire product suite, encompassing both duct and specialized fibre systems.

While the US market is accelerating, the broader North American segment's reported numbers have been temporarily dragged down by ongoing weakness in Canada. Canadian sales have remained depressed due to sluggish project build-outs and aggressive inventory reduction efforts by major domestic telecom customers.

Furthermore, evaluating the trajectory of this recovery requires accounting for the segment's pronounced seasonality. Hexatronic's fiber sales are historically skewed toward the summer months (Q2 and Q3), while the first and fourth quarters face naturally lower demand due to harsh weather conditions hindering underground deployments. The anticipated influx of US federal subsidies via the Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment program is expected to further catalyze this seasonal upswing in the latter half of 2026.

Drivers 2 & 4: North America Growth Trajectory and Order Intake Trends
Driver 2: North America Growth Trajectory

North America represents a massive growth potential for Hexatronic, accounting for roughly 37% of the group's total revenues. The region achieved a critical inflection point in the first quarter of 2026 as the United States Fiber Solutions market officially returned to positive organic growth. The growth momentum in the region is broad-based, fueled by an uptick in both fiber cable systems and duct and conduit deployments.

Redeye analysts highlight that this structural rebound in the US Fiber Solutions market is a clear positive step that supports the long-term investment case. The broker notes that US duct peers have also reported strong sales growth, corroborating Hexatronic's statements regarding a stabilizing and improving underlying market environment.

A major component of Hexatronic's US growth strategy is its conduit business operated through Blue Diamond Industries. To expand its addressable market and capture demand in the western United States, the company recently completed capacity investments and opened a new duct factory in Ogden, Utah. Strategic customer wins are also materializing to support this expanded footprint, highlighted by a three-year agreement with Novos Fiber for complete FTTH systems that is expected to generate approximately SEK 400 million in sales.

The $42.5 billion Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment program serves as a significant long-term catalyst for rural fiber buildouts across the country. Management expects the BEAD program to become a more meaningful driver of volumes in the US market starting in the second half of 2026. However, the company emphasizes that BEAD is primarily a secondary growth driver, given that approximately 80% of US fiber network builds are privately funded by telecom operators and private equity firms.

Redeye reports that early adopter states have allocated the vast majority of their BEAD funds to physical fiber, aligning with the thesis that fiber remains the preferred infrastructure technology despite competitive threats from satellite broadband.

Driver Evaluation Matrix:

Probability of Delivery: High.

Failure Causes: Delays in BEAD funding deployment, regulatory shifts favoring wireless technologies, or a severe macroeconomic downturn restricting private equity capital for alternative network providers.

Share Price Impact Magnitude: High, given the US market's size and strategic importance to the group's overall growth and margin targets.

Rating: Green.

Driver 4: Order Intake Trends and Revenue Visibility

Order intake dynamics provide crucial visibility into Hexatronic's near-term revenue trajectory and factory utilization rates. During the pandemic-era supply chain disruptions, the company's order book artificially swelled to over five months of sales as customers placed advance orders to secure inventory. As global supply chains normalized, the order book underwent a substantial digestion phase and has now stabilized at approximately 2 to 2.5 months of sales. Management views this 2 to 3 month window as a healthy, normalized level for the business that reflects standard purchasing behaviors.

In the first quarter of 2026, Hexatronic reported that overall customer activity remains at a high level with no signs of slowing down. Furthermore, the demand for submarine cables remains robust, providing a rather full order book for the remainder of 2026 with bulk deliveries scheduled for the third quarter. This strength in specific niches, combined with the US market recovery, indicates that the order book is actively rebuilding in strategic growth areas.

Redeye analysts point out that despite elevated industry-wide inventory levels in the past, ordering behaviors across the sector are now showing positive indicators of normalization. The broker expects this stabilized backlog to support gradual revenue recovery throughout the remainder of 2026.

Driver Evaluation Matrix:

Probability of Delivery: High.

Failure Causes: A renewed freeze in customer capital expenditures, elevated interest rates stalling project financing, or an aggressive return to inventory destocking among major telecom clients could compress the order book.

Share Price Impact Magnitude: Moderate, as a stable order book is largely priced in, but a severe contraction would heavily penalize the stock through lowered factory utilization.

Rating: Green.

Drivers 3 & 7: Margin Recovery and Management Execution
Driver 3: Margin Recovery Trajectory and Restructuring Execution

A central pillar of the investment thesis for Hexatronic relies on expanding the consolidated adjusted EBITA margin from its current depressed levels back toward double-digit historical peaks. In the first quarter of 2026, the company reported an adjusted EBITA margin of 8.6%, marking a crucial sequential improvement after five consecutive quarters of margin decline. This inflection point demonstrates that the group's strategic shift toward higher-margin segments, combined with aggressive cost-cutting measures, is beginning to yield tangible operational leverage.

The primary drag on consolidated profitability has been the severe volume drop and structural overcapacity within the Fiber Solutions segment. To combat these headwinds and recalibrate the fixed cost base, management launched a comprehensive, multi-step performance improvement program starting in the third quarter of 2025. The initial phase targeted the European footprint, focusing on the closure of the Weterings plastics factory in the Netherlands and the subsequent consolidation of those manufacturing volumes into modernized facilities in Sweden and Austria. This European consolidation, alongside broader organizational rightsizing, affected approximately 120 employees.

As market challenges persisted in late 2025, management expanded the scope of the restructuring program to include the North American operations. Specifically, the company executed targeted capacity adjustments to downsize its duct and conduit factory in Clinton, South Carolina, deliberately reducing scale to better match the subdued demand environment and improve indirect cost coverage. This expansion triggered an additional SEK 67 million in non-recurring restructuring charges, of which roughly SEK 58 million were non-cash asset write-downs.

Collectively, the combined European and North American restructuring programs incurred total non-recurring costs of approximately SEK 326 million. However, the initiatives were successfully finalized ahead of schedule in early 2026 and are projected to generate SEK 120 million in annualized EBITA savings. Management reported that the cash payback period for these comprehensive efficiency measures is highly attractive at approximately 1.1 years.

Redeye analysts highlight that the cost-saving programs delivered earnings impacts slightly earlier than modeled, which successfully insulated the first quarter 2026 Fiber Solutions margin at 6.2% despite continued European volume weakness. The broker forecasts that group adjusted EBITA margins will continue a steady upward trajectory, projecting a margin of 9.6% for full-year 2026, 10.7% for 2027, and 11.7% by 2028. Furthermore, Redeye notes that this margin expansion will be increasingly fueled by the Data Center segment, which consistently generates margins above the group's 15% cross-cycle target.

Driver 7: Management Execution and Leadership Credibility

Hexatronic is currently navigating a pivotal leadership transition following the departure of its long-tenured CEO Henrik Larsson Lyon, who spent a decade transforming the company into a global fiber optics player. On March 1, 2025, Rikard Fröberg officially assumed the role of President and CEO, tasked with executing a critical strategic pivot away from commodity legacy markets and toward high-growth niches. Fröberg brings extensive commercial and operational experience from his previous tenure as Chief Product and Marketing Officer at Ansell Ltd, a global protective equipment manufacturer where he managed a USD 800 million global industrial business unit.

Supporting the CEO is CFO Pernilla Lindén, who joined the group in 2021 after serving as CFO at Handicare Group. Together, the executive team oversees a highly decentralized organizational structure that grants significant autonomy to local subsidiaries. This decentralized model is a core tenet of Hexatronic's operating philosophy, promoting rapid local decision-making and accountability while insulating the broader group from isolated regional disruptions. Furthermore, management and the board maintain a strong alignment with minority shareholders, holding approximately 13% of the company's outstanding shares.

Redeye views the new CEO's strategic direction positively, noting that Fröberg has demonstrated a strong long-term vision by successfully challenging conventional practices and accelerating the pivot toward Data Center and Harsh Environment targets. The broker commends the management team's disciplined capital allocation, highlighting a consistent history of value-creating M&A and a focus on maintaining a strong balance sheet rather than prioritizing aggressive dividend payouts. However, Redeye assigns a slight "execution uncertainty" risk to the leadership profile, acknowledging that Fröberg's lack of prior Hexatronic and direct telecommunications industry experience creates a learning curve. Despite this, the broker concludes that the highly decentralized nature of the operating entities significantly mitigates the operational risks typically associated with a top-level executive transition.

Drivers 5 & 6: Competitive Dynamics and Capital Allocation
Driver 5: Competitive Dynamics and Market Positioning

Hexatronic operates in a highly fragmented global landscape, facing competition from multinational giants, regional specialists, and low-cost manufacturers. While the company historically competed directly against major wire and cable conglomerates like Prysmian and Nexans, research did not surface explicit recent benchmarking data or market share analyses directly comparing Hexatronic's current performance against Prysmian, Nexans, or Chinese suppliers for the 2025-2026 period.

Despite this specific data gap, Hexatronic's strategy explicitly avoids competing strictly on price in highly commoditized microduct and raw fiber markets. Instead, the company differentiates itself by offering complete, end-to-end system solutions that integrate passive fiber infrastructure with targeted training, installation, and field support. By positioning itself as a system integrator rather than a pure component supplier, Hexatronic aims to lower the Total Cost of Ownership for network builders, effectively creating higher switching costs for its customers.

Furthermore, Hexatronic's localized manufacturing strategy acts as a structural moat against low-cost Chinese suppliers and volatile global supply chains. The company produces the majority of its products in the regions where they are sold, significantly mitigating geopolitical and tariff-related risks. Management notes that direct exposure to US import tariffs is exceptionally low, affecting less than 5% of total sales. To further insulate the North American business from competitive threats and trade barriers, Hexatronic is accelerating the establishment of local fiber optic cable manufacturing in the United States.

Driver Evaluation Matrix: Competitive Dynamics

Probability of Delivery: Moderate to High. The localized manufacturing strategy is fully operational and effectively shields the company from broad import tariffs that might otherwise advantage Chinese or Eastern European suppliers.

Failure Causes: If major telecom operators prioritize lowest upfront capital expenditure over long-term TCO, Hexatronic's premium full-system solutions could lose market share to cheaper, standalone commodity products from scale players like Prysmian or Nexans.

Share Price Impact Magnitude: Moderate. While Hexatronic's FTTH market share remains important, the company's deliberate pivot toward Data Center and Harsh Environment niches inherently reduces its exposure to the fiercest competition in legacy fiber commodity markets.

Rating: Yellow. The lack of transparent, real-time market share data against primary legacy competitors creates some uncertainty, though the localized business model provides substantial downside protection.

Driver 6: Capital Allocation, Balance Sheet Strength, and M&A Execution

Hexatronic's aggressive strategic pivot is entirely dependent on its ability to execute highly accretive Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) without destabilizing its balance sheet. To support its goal of acquiring three to six service-oriented companies annually, management has prioritized financial flexibility and targeted capital deployment over direct shareholder returns. Reflecting this aggressive growth mandate, the Board of Directors proposed to suspend the dividend entirely for the 2025 financial year, redirecting all generated cash flow into the M&A pipeline and debt management.

At the close of the first quarter of 2026, Hexatronic reported an interest-bearing net debt of SEK 1,672 million (excluding IFRS 16 lease liabilities). This resulted in an adjusted leverage ratio of 2.2x EBITDA, up slightly from 1.9x at year-end 2025. The sequential increase in leverage was primarily driven by foreign exchange fluctuations and a weaker trailing twelve-month EBITDA base stemming from the European Fiber Solutions downturn. Management expects leverage to temporarily tick up further in the second quarter of 2026 due to scheduled earnout payments from previous acquisitions, before resuming a downward operational trajectory. The company maintains ample liquidity, holding SEK 1.725 billion in available funds, which is heavily supported by a recently refinanced SEK 3.5 billion syndicated bank facility that runs until 2028 with extension options to 2030.
