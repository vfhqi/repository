Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Statutory monopoly, not a commercial franchise. Enav is Italy's sole civil air navigation service provider under national law "without any time limit" — every flight through or landing in Italian airspace must use Enav's infrastructure, with zero legal alternative.
2.
Regulated-utility financial character at scale. FY2025 consolidated revenue EUR 1,024.7m (76% en-route / 24% terminal split), EBITDA EUR 252.7m (24.7% margin), net income EUR 93.1m, net debt/EBITDA 0.5x — a conservative, income-generative balance sheet.
3.
Geography is narrow and structurally insulated. Revenue is 96.3% Italy-domestic by billing currency and jurisdiction, but over 80% of en-route traffic is international overflight, so the revenue base is geographically concentrated in regulatory terms yet demand-diversified across European carriers.
4.
Investment style is a regulated-monopoly income compounder, not a cyclical or event-driven case. The RP4 (2025-2029) five-year performance plan locks tariffs, capex (EUR 568m) and a rising dividend (EUR 0.29 to EUR 0.32/share) — the case is a bond-like equity with optionality, not a re-rating story.
5.
Vertical integration is unusually deep for the sector. Captive Techno Sky (maintenance) and IDS AirNav (software, 100+ clients/70 countries) put Enav in three of the four air-traffic value-chain stages, missing only hardware manufacturing (Thales/Indra/Leonardo territory).
6.
Non-regulated optionality is real but small. Non-regulated revenue reached EUR 52.1m in 2025 (+5.8%), targeted to double to EUR 106m by 2029 via drone/UTM (AIView Group acquisition), consulting and technology exports — currently ~5% of group revenue.
7.
Ownership is majority-state, single-listed-ANSP structure. The Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF) holds 53.28% and appoints six of nine board directors; Enav is the only listed air navigation service provider globally, a governance and float-liquidity outlier.
8.
