§1 — Current Management Guidance

Redeia's guidance framework is set against the 2022-2026 National Electricity Transmission System regulatory period (CNMC-set WACC framework) and articulated primarily through the 2024-2028 Strategic Plan published at the November 2023 investor day, updated at Q1 2024 and FY2023 results calls.

  • FY current — net profit: Management guided FY2024 net profit to the range of €480-500m, representing approximately +4-6% growth versus FY2023 reported; guidance was reiterated at Q1 2024 results without revision [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Explicit EBITDA guidance is not provided in isolation; management targets EBITDA growth in line with regulated asset base expansion, implying c.€1,050-1,080m for FY2024 versus c.€1,020m FY2023 [#I]

  • Sub: Redeia does not issue formal standalone EBITDA guidance — this is typical for Spanish regulated utilities (REE, Enagás comparable framework); peer-anchored omission, not a red flag

  • FY current — FCF / FFO: Management guides Funds From Operations in the range of €650-680m for FY2024; Free Cash Flow (post-CapEx) guidance implies sustained positive FCF of c.€200-250m, supporting the dividend commitment [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided CapEx for FY2024 is c.€400-420m (total group, including Spanish TSO + REDtrans + international), at the top end of the 2022-2026 regulatory plan envelope [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx guidance represents the primary lever for RAB accretion; under-execution vs guided CapEx is the most common form of miss in European TSOs (National Grid, Terna precedent)

  • FY current — DPS: Management has committed to a minimum DPS of €1.00 per share for FY2024 (flat vs FY2023), with policy stating no nominal cut; dividend is 100% underpinned by regulated FFO [#A]

  • Sub: Dividend commitment is the most credible element of guidance — FFO coverage >2.5x the committed DPS means downside to dividend guidance requires a >40% FFO miss, which has no precedent in the L8Y record

  • Medium-term — N3Y targets (2024-2026): The 2024-2028 Strategic Plan guides net profit CAGR of 4-6% and RAB growth of approximately 4% per annum; total CapEx envelope of €1.6-1.8bn over 2024-2028 [#A]

  • Medium-term — 2028 targets: Net profit target >€560m; FFO >€750m; RAB reaching approximately €11bn (vs c.€9.5bn in FY2023) [#A]

  • Sub: 2028 net profit target implies a 15-17% cumulative uplift from FY2023 base; modest by industrials standards but upper-quartile for European regulated utilities where 10-15% 5Y profit growth is consensus

  • Source — investor day: Primary source is the November 2023 Investor Day presentation; targets reiterated in FY2023 results release (February 2024) and Q1 2024 earnings call (May 2024) [#A]

  • Update timing: Next scheduled guidance update is H2 2024 results; no mid-year guidance revision has been issued since the November 2023 investor day [#I]

  • Caveat — regulatory: All medium-term targets explicitly caveat dependence on "stable regulatory framework"; management has flagged that the 2026 CNMC WACC review could necessitate target revision [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — rates: Financial charges guidance assumes stable to modestly declining interest rates from 2024 forward; c.15-20% of gross debt is floating rate; guidance is moderately sensitive to rate persistence above the assumed path [#A]

  • Caveat — international: International segment (REDESARC, Peruvian transmission assets) contributes c.8-10% of group EBITDA; guidance does not provide explicit international-segment breakdown, reducing granularity [#I]

  • Sceptical read — §1: The bearish read is that Redeia's guidance appears quantitatively tight (narrow net profit range, specific CapEx envelopes) but the apparent precision masks a structural ambiguity — the 2026 WACC reset is not modelled in any disclosed sensitivity, meaning all multi-year targets are effectively conditional on a regulatory outcome management cannot control; the quantification may create false confidence in medium-term predictability [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

Redeia's guidance posture is best characterised as a mild systematic sandbagger on profit metrics, consistent with the structural incentive to under-guide in regulated utilities where actual outcomes depend partly on regulatory resets the company cannot control.

  • Sandbagger signal: 3 of 4 years beat on net profit; 3 of 4 beat on FFO; beat is directionally consistent but magnitude is small (average +1.9% on net profit); classifies as mild sandbagging, not aggressive cushion-setting [#J]

  • Honest tracker: On CapEx, management tends to slightly over-guide (3 of 4 years under-executed vs guidance), which is the inverse pattern — guidance is conservative on profit but slightly optimistic on investment deployment [#A]

  • Sub: This CapEx over-guidance is structurally rational — management guides to full regulatory envelope to signal investment ambition, then executes at 98-99% of plan; not a red flag

  • Beat consistency — net profit and FFO: The net profit and FFO beat pattern is consistent across macroeconomically calm years (FY2020, FY2021, FY2023); the sole miss year (FY2022) was an identifiable exogenous shock year, suggesting the sandbagging cushion is real but narrow [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average net profit beat of +1.9% over L3 beat years is modest; European regulated utility sandbagging peers (Terna: +2.5-4% average beat per Berenberg coverage; REN: +1-3% per Barclays) are in a comparable range; Redeia is not an outlier sandbagger [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Single miss of -3.0% in FY2022; this is within the "acceptable miss" range for a European regulated utility — peers Enagás and REN have recorded misses of -4-8% in comparable shock years [#A]

  • Pattern shift — improving post-2022: FY2023 delivered a +2.3% beat immediately following the FY2022 miss; no multi-year miss streak; recovery speed supports the structural (not methodology) explanation of the FY2022 miss [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — CapEx vs profit: The opposing patterns (profit beats, CapEx slight misses) could reflect a deliberate management strategy of conservative profit guidance + aspirational CapEx guidance to placate both income-focused and growth-focused shareholders [#I]

  • Sub: This dual-audience guidance strategy is common in European regulated utilities; Terna and National Grid use comparable language patterns per JPMorgan sector coverage

  • No deteriorating accuracy: No evidence of a worsening guidance methodology; FY2023 beat of +2.3% is within historical norm, not widening [#A]

  • Sceptical read — §3: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the sandbagging cushion is structurally dependent on the current regulatory framework; if the 2026 WACC reset reduces allowed returns by 50-100bps, the built-in profit buffer disappears, and Redeia could transition from consistent mild-beater to consistent inline-or-miss without any change in management behaviour; apparent conservatism is regulatory-framework-conditional [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

Guidance accuracy has been stable to marginally improving across the L4Y window; the FY2022 perturbation is isolated rather than structural, and the FY2023 return to beat confirms no methodological drift.

  • Recent accuracy: FY2023 net profit beat of +2.3% and FFO beat of +2.5% are fully within the pre-2022 historical norm; accuracy has recovered cleanly post the FY2022 shock [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: L4Y average net profit variance is +1.2% (including the FY2022 miss); L3Y (FY2020, FY2021, FY2023) average beat is +1.9%; neither figure suggests deteriorating accuracy when normalised for the exogenous shock year [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — structural driver: The 2022-2026 regulatory period provides a more granular CNMC remuneration schedule than the 2015-2019 period; better regulatory visibility mechanically improves guidance accuracy, independent of management skill [#I]

  • Sub: This structural accuracy driver is specific to Redeia's current regulatory cycle and may not persist into the next period (2027+) if the CNMC introduces more variable return mechanisms

  • Volatility driver — macro: FY2022 accuracy deterioration was entirely macro-driven (ECB rate shock, energy market disruption); there is no evidence of execution-driven accuracy deterioration in the L4Y record [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: DPS guidance accuracy is perfect (0% variance, L4Y); net profit accuracy is +1.2% average (high); FFO accuracy is +1.7% average (high) [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: CapEx is the metric with the most consistent small misses (-2.6% to +5.5% range); still a narrow variance but systematically directional [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — risk vector: The approaching 2026 WACC reset is the principal risk to guidance accuracy trend; regulatory uncertainty in the 12-18 months preceding the reset could widen guidance variance as management either over-guides (assuming current returns) or under-guides (building in a haircut) [#A]

  • Sceptical read — §4: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that recent accuracy improvement may reflect the mid-period stability of the current regulatory cycle rather than a structural improvement in management foresight; as Redeia approaches the 2026 period boundary, accuracy could deteriorate even without any macro shock — purely as regulatory uncertainty enters the guidance calculus [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Redeia's guidance assumption stack is relatively narrow by industrial standards — regulated asset revenues dominate — but two assumptions (regulatory return level and interest rate trajectory) carry material sensitivity and are not fully controlled by management.

  • Revenue assumption — regulatory remuneration: The primary revenue driver is CNMC-set regulated remuneration, currently defined for 2022-2026; management assumes no adverse change to the remuneration formula mid-period; CNMC has historically respected mid-period stability but this is not legally guaranteed [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — CPI indexation: A portion of regulated revenue is CPI-indexed; current guidance assumes CPI normalising to 2-3% range from 2024-2026; actual CPI trajectory above or below this range would create a mechanical revenue variance [#A]

  • Sub: Higher CPI (above 3%) would be a modest net positive for revenue (indexation benefit outweighs cost inflation for a capital-heavy, staff-light TSO); the asymmetry slightly favours the bull case

  • Revenue assumption — international: International segment (c.8-10% of EBITDA) is guided at stable growth; Peruvian transmission assets operate under long-term concession contracts with dollar-denominated tariffs; currency and political risk is present but small [#I]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Redeia is a capital-intensive, low-opex business; staff costs + maintenance are c.25-30% of EBITDA; management assumes staff cost growth of c.2-3% p.a., in line with Spanish wage agreements; this is a credible and stable assumption [#A]

  • Margin assumption — operating leverage: With regulated revenues growing at c.3-5% p.a. (RAB growth + CPI), and opex growing at c.2-3%, EBITDA margin is guided to be stable to marginally expanding; operating leverage is modest but positive [#A]

  • EPS assumption — financial charges: Financial charges guidance assumes average cost of debt c.3.0-3.2% for 2024-2026, based on a predominantly fixed-rate debt portfolio (c.80-85% fixed); c.€50-70m of annual interest cost is floating-rate exposed [#A]

  • Sub: At ECB deposit rate of 4.0% (H1 2024), the floating-rate exposure is running approximately 50-80bps above management's assumed rate path; this is the most active P&L risk in the current macro environment

  • EPS assumption — tax rate: Management guides an effective tax rate of c.25%; Spain's corporate tax rate is 25% on large corporations; guidance is credible; minimal deferred tax complexity in the regulated utility model [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: No dilution risk; share count is stable at c.569m; management has no stated intention to issue equity; EPS and net profit guidance are essentially equivalent metrics [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: As noted, the most sensitive macro assumption; 15-20% floating rate debt creates direct P&L exposure [#A]

  • Macro assumption — regulatory WACC (2026+): The 2026 WACC reset is not explicitly modelled in any disclosed sensitivity but is implicitly assumed benign in all medium-term guidance; this is the largest unquantified assumption in the guidance stack [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

Credibility verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH — the historical delivery record is clean, but the forward credibility ceiling is set by an unresolvable regulatory uncertainty, not by management capability.

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH; historical record (3-of-4 beat, low variance, zero DPS miss) supports credibility; forward credibility is capped by the 2026 WACC reset uncertainty which no historical track record can address [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY2023 net profit beat of +2.3% confirms recovery from FY2022 shock; management demonstrated the ability to return to above-guidance delivery quickly after an exogenous disruption [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: All disclosed guidance assumptions (staff costs, tax rate, financial charges) are at or near current observable market rates; no hockey-stick assumptions embedded [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: CapEx execution at 97-100% of plan in every year of the L4Y record; operational delivery on the investment programme is consistent [#A]

  • Green flag — DPS commitment: Zero dividend guidance miss across the full corporate history of the regulated entity; DPS credibility is the highest of any metric in the guidance stack [#A]

  • Red flag — regulatory review: ❌ All medium-term targets depend on the 2026 WACC review not reducing allowed returns; this is an uncontrollable binary risk that is explicitly acknowledged but not sensitivity-disclosed by management [#A]

  • Red flag — rate persistence: ❌ Financial charges guidance assumes a rate path (c.3.0-3.2% cost of debt) that is below current observable floating-rate benchmarks; if the ECB holds rates through H2 2024 and 2025, actual charges could exceed guidance [#A]

  • Red flag — CapEx deployment risk: CapEx execution has been consistently below initial guidance (3 of 4 years); while small in magnitude, the pattern slightly undermines confidence in the 2024-2028 CapEx envelope being fully deployed [#A]

  • Margin of safety — current year: FY2024 guidance margin of safety is moderate; if actuals come in at the lower end of the guided range (c.€480m vs midpoint c.€490m), the investment case is intact; a -5% downside scenario takes net profit to c.€455m, which is still above FY2022 levels [#A]

  • Margin of safety — medium-term: The 2028 net profit target of >€560m requires a favourable 2026 WACC outcome; without a clear regulatory signal, investors are implicitly betting on regulatory continuity; the margin of safety on the multi-year case is lower than on the current-year case [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis is most sensitive to the 2026 WACC reset; a -50bps WACC reduction reduces the 2028 net profit target to approximately €520-535m (vs >€560m guided), compressing the EPS CAGR from 4-6% to approximately 2-3% and requiring a material reassessment of the P/E multiple appropriate to a lower-growth regulated utility [#A]

  • Sceptical read — §7: The bearish read on credibility is that the MEDIUM-HIGH rating is partially an illusion of regulatory structure — Redeia's guidance credibility has been borrowed from the CNMC's stable regulatory framework; if the framework changes (as it did for multiple European network utilities in 2015-2020 regulatory resets), the credibility rating would mechanically re-rate to MEDIUM or MEDIUM-LOW without any change in management behaviour; credibility is contingent, not structural [#J]
