§2 — COMPANY DETAILS (Revenue model, Business model, Delivery, Internal change) — [C] Layer

Net signal: Three-segment business with online at its core — online growing at 27-31% CAGR (FY2024: +50%, FY2025: +27% for H1), now primary revenue driver, and best-in-class Italian market position. Sports Franchise and Gaming Franchise are contractual, lower-growth cash cow segments. [#J]

  • Revenue source: Three segments. Online (iGaming + online sports betting) is the growth engine. Sports Franchise is managed-decline retail (betting shops). Gaming Franchise (AWP/VLT machines) is concession-based regulated. FY2025 revenue split approximately: Online ~45%, Sports Franchise ~25%, Gaming Franchise ~30% (estimate from H1 2025 data). [#I]
  • Revenue model — Online: Player-funded accounts; GGR basis; Italian ADM-licensed. iGaming (casino, slots, poker, bingo) growing mid-to-high teens market pace + market share gains. Online sports betting growing 20%+ pa in Italy. Recurring, digitally acquired customer base. [#I]
  • Revenue model — Sports Franchise: Retail betting shops (terrestrial franchise network); fixed-odds sports betting, virtual sports. Secular headwinds from online cannibalisation but LTM managing through ongoing in-shop digital migration (tablet-based betting terminals, casino-style machines in-shop). [#I]
  • Revenue model — Gaming Franchise: State-mandated AWP (Amusement With Prize) and VLT machine networks in bars, restaurants, arcades. Concession-based; legally licensed and regulated. Contractual revenue floor. Secular pressure from online substitution but lower rate than betting retail. [#I]
  • Geography: Italy-only. ⚡ This is a concentrated single-market operator, unlike Flutter or Entain — the entire thesis is Italy-specific. ADM is the only relevant regulatory authority. Single-market concentration = both strength (dominant scale) and vulnerability (no geographic diversification). [#A]
  • Structural trend — online mix shift: Italian online gaming market grew to €5.1bn GGR in 2025 (from ~€4.3bn in 2024), projected €5.5bn+ by 2026. Online is growing faster than retail gaming in all subsegments. LTM's exposure to this trend is its primary structural tailwind. [#A]
  • Competitive advantage — brand and scale: "Lottomatica" brand has near-universal recognition in Italy (40+ years as the country's lottery operator); brand = lowest-cost customer acquisition channel in Italy. Scale advantage: €44.7bn in bets processed = >3x next comparable Italian-focused competitor. [#J]
  • Recent delivery: FY2024 revenue +23% (M&A-assisted SKS365 close Apr 2024), organic estimate +10-12%. FY2025 revenue +12% (full SKS365 year, more organic). ANP growth: +45% in FY2025. Consistent guidance beat pattern: FY2024 EBITDA guidance €700-730m, outturn €739m (+1.2% above top). [#A]
  • Internal change — L12M: CEO also became Chairman (Jul 2025) — dual role; governance question at ESA. Apollo fully exited (2025). €700m+ buyback authorised (12.5% of shares, 18 months). FY2025 dividend €0.44/share (proposed), up from €0.30 in FY2024 (+47%). [#I]
  • M&A track record: Three acquisitions under Apollo stewardship: IGT Italian B2C (2021, €1.1bn), Betflag (2022, ~€310m), SKS365/Sportbet (2024, €640m). Each acquisition added online scale. CEO screened 57 targets, completed 3 — ≤5% completion rate. [#A]

Sceptical bullet (§2): The bearish read on LTM's revenue model is single-market concentration — Italy is LTM's entire business. If Italian regulatory policy tightens materially (advertising further restricted, tax rates raised, AWP/VLT concessions restructured), LTM has no geographic offset. The 2018 Italian gambling advertising ban (Decreto Dignità) already compressed the sector; a repeat tightening is not inconceivable. Through what mechanism might moat be brittle? Sky Sports-style data rights deals or Flutter's migration of acquired brands onto a superior tech platform (post-Snaitech acquisition) could accelerate share loss in online specifically. [#A]

§3 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT — [C] Layer

Net signal: Italian gaming market is undergoing structural consolidation — the Nov 2025 ADM overhaul halved the number of licensed operators, reinforcing scale players like LTM and Flutter. Market growth trajectory is intact (+15% online gaming pa); competition is intensifying but LTM is gaining share. [#J]

  • Market structure: Italian regulated gaming total GGR ~€20bn+ (land-based + online); online is ~€5.1bn (2025). Post-Nov 2025 overhaul: 52 licensed domains, 46 operators. Pre-overhaul: 407 licensed sites. Drastic consolidation in one regulatory step. [#I]
  • Concentration — top players: LTM #1 online (31.3% market share, Q4 2025); Flutter Italy (Sisal + Snaitech + Betfair + PokerStars + Tombola) #2 (~26.5% combined). Top 2 players hold >57% of online market. Effective duopoly in practice. [#A]
  • Competitive intensity: Rising. Flutter's April 2025 Snaitech acquisition + platform migration to SEA creates a technically stronger #2. Betsson, Entain (Eurobet), Evoke (888) are in top 10 but well behind the top 2. New entrants structurally inhibited by ADM licensing requirements (€7m fee, 9-year term, compliance requirements). [#A]
  • Regulatory shift — ADM Nov 2025 overhaul: Largest restructuring of Italian online gambling in 20 years. Effects: (a) 407→52 licensed sites concentrates market to established players; (b) €7m per-license fee and compliance requirements filter out weaker operators; (c) new Sports Betting Protocol 5.0 (March 2026) requires technology upgrades — favours scale. [#A]
  • Regulatory risk — advertising: Italy's Decreto Dignità (2018) already bans gambling advertising in many contexts. ADM extended advertising restrictions in 2025 review process. Risk of further tightening is material — LTM has large marketing spend that could be constrained. [#A] ❌
  • Market growth — online gaming: €3.2bn Italian online casino spending (2025) +15.8% vs 2024. Sports betting €1.7bn. ADM/MEF project total online GGR exceeds €5.5bn by 2026. Long-term secular growth driven by mobile penetration and demographic shift (younger Italian cohort — digital native). [#I]
  • Value chain — supplier side: Technology vendors (odds compilers, gaming software, RNG providers) have meaningful power in the online segment; LTM has partially internalised some technology via Betflag/SKS365 product stacks. Land-based AWP machine capex is managed through machine manufacturers (Aristocrat, IGT equipment). [#A]
  • Thematic trend — responsible gambling: Italy is tightening responsible gambling requirements (self-exclusion systems, stakes limits debate). This creates near-term compliance cost but is a moat against smaller operators who can't absorb the overhead. [#A]

Sceptical bullet (§3): The bearish cross-cutting read on Italian market structure: Flutter's combination of Sisal + Snaitech + Betfair + PokerStars is a genuinely powerful platform aggregation. If Flutter completes platform migration successfully onto SEA and wins the next ADM concession round in 2034 with upgraded tech, the Italy duopoly could tip from "LTM first, Flutter a distant second" to more of a genuine 50/50 competition. The mechanism: superior product, retention, and data analytics on Flutter's global platform vs LTM's Italian-specific stack. This is a 3-5 year risk, not an immediate one, but worth flagging. [#A]

§4.2 — Advantaged Business + SRCA (BB#2 TC2)

  • Customer moat: "Lottomatica" brand = Italy's most recognised gambling brand (40+ years of lottery operation). Customer stickiness from account funding, loyalty points, multi-product (sports + casino + scratch). Hard to quantify NPS publicly; churn rate not published. [#A]
  • Regulatory moat: ADM concession holder. Post-Nov 2025 overhaul, new licenses require €7m fee, 9-year term, stringent compliance — near-prohibitive for new entrants. Existing 9-year license for LTM has 8+ years remaining. This is a hard regulatory moat. [#J]
  • Scale moat: Processing €44.7bn in bets = infrastructure, data, and risk management scale advantages that accrue naturally. Largest Italian book provides best-odds capability in sports betting. At 5 companies total in the "Gambling B2C Lottery-led" sector, LTM commands by far the largest Italian operation. [#J]
  • TAM: Italian online gaming projected €5.5bn+ GGR by 2026. Land-based regulated gaming adds another ~€14-15bn. Total TAM ~€20bn+ — LTM captures ~11-12% of total Italian regulated GGR. Meaningful room for online share capture. [#A]
  • GENERATE (RA6):
  • Revenue growth: +10-12% organic pa (FY2025 equivalent) — above Italian market growth (+8% GGR), implying share gain. iGaming midto-high teens growth; sports betting +20%+ in 2025. [#A]
  • EBITDA margin: 38% (FY2025), expanding toward 40%+. Online EBITDA margin estimated 50-55%, Gaming Franchise ~20%, Sports Franchise ~15-25% — blended margin rises with online mix. [#A]

  • ROCE: LTM ~10-12% FY2025 estimated (improving from ~7% at IPO). Below Flutter (~20%) but with a clearer path to improvement as leverage falls. [#A]
