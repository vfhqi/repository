EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 24 findings

Cirsa Enterprises SAU operates a resilient omnichannel gaming platform combining 450+ casinos across eight countries with fast-growing online operations , generating €2,339.20 million revenues in FY25 and consolidated EBITDA margins near 32% . The group leverages unique vertical integration through its Unidesa manufacturing unit, which supplies 81% of internal Spanish slot machines and commands 50-55% B2B market share , providing proprietary game access and real-time market intelligence unavailable to competitors like Lottomatica or Codere. The Spanish slots route segment holds market share four to eight times larger than its nearest rival, secured through strict ﬁve-year exclusivity contracts with 17,000 independent bars and achieving an exceptional 90% renewal rate . Geographic diversiﬁcation across ten regulated jurisdictions ensures 91% of EBITDA originates from investment-grade sovereign nations , while massive retail fragmentation across 50 million annual casino visitors and negligible customer concentration insulate cash ﬂows from single-point failures.

However, Blackstone retains a 78.4% post-IPO stake following the July 2025 listing at €15.00 per share , which generated €373 million net proceeds to drive net leverage from 3.80x to 2.70x . Structural margin pressure persists in the Italian slots division , which operates on thin 7-8% EBITDA margins due to punitive gaming taxes exceeding 70% of GGR , while the online segment faces regulatory headwinds with margins compressed to 14.9% in Q1 2026 following Peru's progressive turnover taxes creating a 5.4 percentage point drag . The aggressive M&A strategy , including the €152.50 million Apuesta Total acquisition , has triggered arbitration disputes over 2024 EBITDA calculations , raising integration execution risk. Escalating regulatory tax pressures across key markets–including UK's 40% online iGaming tax , Colombia's 19% VAT on deposits , and Peru's turnover tax regime –threaten medium-term margin sustainability and require continuous capital reallocation to offset legislative erosion.
