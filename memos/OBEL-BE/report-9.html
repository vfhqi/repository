§2 — Quality of Earnings

OBEL-BE's earnings quality analysis is materially shaped by two structural features: (1) the TOBI tower transaction (completed 2021-2022), which created a large and ongoing D&A/lease charge that depresses reported EBIT and EPS; and (2) energy cost volatility (FY2022-FY2023), which was a real cash headwind that inflated the appearance of margin recovery when it normalised.

  • Quality verdict: Mixed — EBITDA quality is reasonable; reported EPS quality is poor due to structural D&A distortion; cash conversion has weakened relative to pre-TOBI baseline [#J]

  • One-off impact — TOBI charges: TOBI-related lease costs (~€15-20m annually) are recurring in economic substance but treated as transitional by management; these are not one-offs and should not be excluded from economic earnings [#A]

  • Sub: The TOBI transaction effectively sold and leased back tower infrastructure; the lease cost is a permanent operating charge, not a transitional item [#I]

  • One-off impact — restructuring: FY2022 and FY2023 each carried ~€8-15m restructuring charges — above the European telco base-rate of €3-7m for similarly-sized operators; if these recur in FY2025, they represent a quality drag [#A]

  • Adjustments trend: The gap between reported EBIT and adjusted EBITDA has widened L3Y — from ~€245m in FY2022 to ~€251m in FY2024; D&A is absorbing an increasing share of operating income [#A]

  • Sub: D&A as % of revenue estimated at ~20-21% in FY2024 vs ~18-19% in FY2021 pre-5G; this is a structural shift, not cyclical [#I]

  • Non-GAAP gap: Adjusted EBITDA is the operative metric; management and sell-side consistently guide/model on EBITDA, rendering reported EPS near-meaningless as a valuation anchor for L3Y — this is a genuine quality concern for an investor seeking EPS-trajectory clarity [#A]

  • NWC tailwind/headwind: Working capital has been broadly neutral L3Y — receivables growth has tracked revenue; no unusual payables stretching detected; DSO approximately stable at ~45-50 days [#A]

  • Sub: Some evidence of handset financing receivables growth in FY2024 as B2C upgrade cycle recovered — bears monitoring as a potential future NWC headwind [#I]

  • Cash conversion — OCF vs EBITDA: OCF/EBITDA approximately 70-75% L3Y average — below the European telco median of ~80-85% for comparable operators; the gap is driven by elevated CapEx cash outflows (5G deployment) flowing through working capital and lease payments [#A]

  • CapEx intensity: CapEx/revenue running ~18-20% in FY2022-FY2023 during peak 5G deployment, moderating toward ~16-17% in FY2024; cash FCF has been constrained but is improving as 5G build winds down [#A]

  • Sub: Post-5G CapEx normalisation is a key forward quality driver — if CapEx/revenue drops to ~13-15%, FCF/EBITDA conversion improves materially [#I]

  • D&A trend: D&A is rising in absolute terms L3Y — from ~€245m FY2022 toward ~€251m FY2024 — reflecting 5G network capitalisation and TOBI lease amortisation; this is a structural earnings quality depressant [#A]

  • Conservative vs aggressive accounting: No evidence of aggressive revenue recognition, unusual capitalisation of costs, or aggressive pension assumptions; OBEL-BE accounting is broadly conservative relative to sector base-rate [#A]

  • Receivables turn: Stable at approximately 45-50 days DSO — no deterioration signal; B2B receivables growth tracked revenue growth proportionally [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? TOBI lease charges are economically recurring but management continues to frame them as transitional; if a future analyst unpacks the EBITDA-to-FCF bridge, the "recovery story" in EBITDA looks considerably less compelling in FCF terms; D&A creep also means EBIT margins are structurally lower than EBITDA margins imply [#J]
