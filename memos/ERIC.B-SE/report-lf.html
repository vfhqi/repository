The succession execution represents a wellprepared and orderly transition contrasting sharply with the crisis-driven appointment in 2017, though promoting a 29-year insider carries risks of execution inertia during rapid technological shifts. achieved through aggressive restructuring . However, Ericsson's total shareholder return of 51.0% significantly lagged peer Nokia's 184.0% over the past year , raising questions about the sustainability of margin expansion without organic growth momentum. 1

CEO Quality and Operator Archetype Match

The functional progression of incoming CEO Narvinger ensures critical alignment of technical and commercial competencies needed for next-generation AI networks, having progressed through research, product development, product management, and customer sales over his 29-year internal career .

Narvinger successfully led a critical operational turnaround of the Cloud Software and Services division, demonstrating concrete operator capacity to restore segment profitability , though his complete internal career path may entrench legacy thinking during strategic inflections.

The CEO transition from a finance-focused generalist to a technical operator marks a sharp strategic shift from financial engineering to AI software networks, positioning leadership for the industrialization of AI-native network architecture. 5

Capital Allocation Discipline and M&A Track Record

Ericsson acquired Vonage in July 2022 for an enterprise value of $6.2bn at peak market valuations, subsequently taking a SEK 11.4bn impairment in Q2 2024 followed by another SEK 15.3bn in late 2024, with DNB Carnegie applying zero valuation to the entire Enterprise unit because Vonage remains unproven .

This valuedestructive acquisition severely damaged investor confidence and represents the most fragile dimension of Ekholm's operational tenure, with former telecom strategy executives criticizing the CPaaS expansion as a completely unjustified vertical software play. Conversely, financial discipline delivered a strong net cash position of SEK 61.2bn by year-end 2025, enabling the board to propose an increased ordinary dividend of SEK 3.00 per share and initiate the first-ever share buyback program of up to SEK 15bn in April 2026 .

Net cash increased rapidly from SEK 13.1bn in Q1 2024 to SEK 68.1bn in Q1 2026, with Jefferies Research noting cost optimizations in a flat RAN market consistently generate strong cash flows , demonstrating effective operational deleveraging despite strategic missteps.

Leadership Style, Strategic Congruence, and Execution Standards

Ekholm maintained a plain-spoken, exceptionally execution-congruent strategy focused on core technology leadership, consistently maintaining a conservative under-promise and over-deliver forecasting methodology that enabled Networks gross margin to beat or match the upper outlook range in most quarters.

However, management faced severe criticism for evasive positioning regarding Vonage regulatory challenges, with the CEO demonstrating fast restructuring execution velocity by rapidly removing thousands of positions globally and divesting iconectiv for SEK 9.9bn.

Earnings calls show growing margin defensive maneuvers as management navigates a flat RAN environment, with the CEO confirming the RAN market is highly likely to remain flat in 2026 while warning that rising memory chip costs represent a near-term gross margin risk that management dismissed as immaterial .

Strategic congruence remains fragile, as despite heavy strategic promotion of enterprise 5G and network APIs through the Aduna joint venture, the Enterprise division remains deeply unprofitable and lossmaking with extremely low institutional investor expectations.

Governance Structure and Board Independence

Ericsson's board features strong technical and industrial expertise across 11 shareholder-elected members, with the Nomination Committee confirming at least seven are independent at 63.64%, though this lags peer Nokia's

100% independent board . Non-independent directors are directly linked to Investor AB and Industrivärden which hold outsized voting stakes, with these two largest institutional shareholders

controlling approximately 38% of the company's voting rights despite owning only 12.50% of capital .

The board achieved a 45% refreshment rate by electing five new directors within three years, including Marachel Knight and Investor AB CEO Christian Cederholm in 2025, while activist Cevian Capital maintains board representation through Jonas Synnergren to enforce disciplined operational restructuring.

Ericsson's corporate governance remains compromised by a dual-class share structure where Class A shares carry one vote while Class B shares carry only one tenth of a vote, heavily weakening the influence of public shareholders who hold 94.11% of share capital but reduced votes .

Board and executive open-market alignment is exceptionally thin, with collective insider holdings representing only 0.05% of float, though the CEO easily fulfills his mandate of maintaining at least 200% of annual base salary in shares. 17

Executive Compensation and Pay-for-Performance Alignment

Ericsson's pay structure maintains a high proportion of performance-based variable pay with the CEO's short-term variable target at 65% of base salary and long-term variable target at 150%, pivoting heavily toward variable equity components at maximum performance to minimize fixed operating cost burdens. Performance conditions utilize rigorous relative TSR and economic profit targets, with long-term performance criteria weighting Group EBITA at 45%, absolute TSR at 25%, and relative TSR at 20%, requiring 6% to 14% annual absolute TSR growth to trigger vesting .

Governance structures enforce rigorous skin-in-the-game mandates with multi-year holding periods, requiring the CEO to maintain shareholding equivalent to at least 200% of annual fixed salary and other Executive Team members to hold at least 75% of base salary. Historical payouts demonstrate strong sensitivity to financial cycles, with CEO total compensation growing from SEK 71.62m in 2024 to SEK 102.65m in 2025 as performance rebounded, though in 2023 the CEO received zero short-term variable payout due to missed annual business plan targets .

The reliance on Group EBITA for 45% of long-term incentives could incentivize aggressive costcutting over long-term technological investment, creating tension between short-term margin protection and sustained innovation capacity. 29

CFO Lars Sandström brings external medtech and Scania financial discipline that contrasts with Nokia's industryheavy CFO profile, joining in April 2024 from Getinge with a strong external corporate perspective and senior financial roles at Scania and Swedish Orphan Biovitrum .

Ericsson's stringent capex limits protect profitability, with capex at SEK 2.6bn in 2025 representing only 1.1% of sales versus Nokia's higher 3% footprint, as management historically maintains group capex within a highly restrictive 1% to 2% of annual sales while peer Nokia accelerates 2026 capex to EUR 0.9-1.0bn for Infinera indium phosphide expansion . The finance team strengthened balance sheet resilience with record net cash, updating alternative performance measures in 2025 using five-quarter rolling averages to increase reporting transparency, while free cash flow before M&A reached

SEK 26.8bn achieving an 11% cash-to-sales conversion rate . Orderly executive successions ensure deep leadership continuity, with Per Narvinger stepping up as CEO on October 1, 2026 while Ekholm remains as executive advisor until June 2027, David Hammarwall succeeding Narvinger as Networks chief, and Charlotte Levert promoted internally to Chief People Officer .

The CFO's focus on cost-cutting may mask longterm R&D deficits as competitors invest more heavily in AI-RAN capabilities.

Insider Transactions and Ownership Alignment

Ericsson's senior management maintains negligible direct equity ownership, with collective insider ownership for Board and Executive Team at just 3,126,672 Class B shares representing only 0.05% of total voting rights, mirroring peer Nokia where Board and Group Leadership hold only 0.06% of outstanding shares .

Outgoing CEO Börje Ekholm holds 854,288 Class B shares and 1,009,000 ADSs easily fulfilling his mandate of maintaining at least 200% of annual base salary in shares, though only four other Executive Team members have fully met their shareholding guideline targets, contrasting with Nokia CEO Justin Hotard who maintains a massive 597% of base salary in shares .

Recent transaction dynamics remain benign, with Ericsson announcing routine plans to sell Class B shares to cover tax liabilities for LTV 2023 participants, while peer Nokia saw substantial insider buying activity with CEO Hotard acquiring 84,404 shares at €9.15 on April 28, 2026 under its co-investment long-term incentive framework. The reliance on automatic share-based grants to satisfy holding rules may fail to build genuine entrepreneurial ownership culture, as elevated peer buying at Nokia demonstrates stronger openmarket conviction in long-term value creation. 39

Expert Network Insights and Operational Realities

Expert network sentiment reveals unmatched radio and Massive MIMO technical superiority, with Ericsson's performance remaining unrivaled among Tier 1 operators during live network trials while competitors like Fujitsu struggle to match legacy radio performance, and exclusion of Huawei from North America leaves Ericsson as the default premium radio supplier .

However, experts expose severe Cloud RAN integration deficits, noting Ericsson retains a legacy mindset attempting to force proprietary end-to-end stacks while software partners report a decidedly closed corporate culture less open to collaborative flexibility with chip giants like Nvidia, struggling to deploy core applications on third-party cloud stacks with customers reporting two-year delays .

The $14 billion AT&T contract saved Ericsson's financial positioning, but customer executives reveal the installation is not actually Open RAN and simply swaps Nokia hardware for proprietary equipment, introducing

long-term risks by giving operators greater supplier bargaining power . Ericsson is half a step ahead in automation because of its legitimate managed services platform leveraging operational data from managing 3 billion lines, though former unit heads describe service operations as historically bloated and inefficient, typically throwing more people at problems rather than fixing underlying process errors .

The transition to open architectures might accelerate structural market-share erosion if low-cost rivals successfully mature their virtualized portfolios.

Employee Culture and Organizational Morale

External employee satisfaction indicates severe retention and morale pressures with elevated annual turnover of 12.0% in 2024, down slightly from 13.0% in 2023, while females comprised only 25% of the total workforce in 2021 landing Ericsson in the bottom regional sector tertile with female representation among senior executives at only 21% in 2023 .

Despite closed collaborative habits, Ericsson behaves far more aggressively than Nokia in customer relationship management, trying exceptionally hard to cater directly to specific operator needs, though the organization was highly cautious and conservative in responding to early Open RAN disruption before successfully shaping a single-vendor deal with AT&T using stellar customer management .

Third-Party Customer and Competitor Dynamics

Direct customer endorsements validate Ericsson's radio performance superiority, with Informa's survey confirming Ericsson and Huawei dominate global contract shares and are highly regarded for technology, while Spanish MasOrange is removing ZTE completely by 2027 and increasing Ericsson's radio share from 40% to 60%.

However, competitors leverage AI-native data center partnerships to challenge long-term market dominance, with Ericsson's proprietary silicon strategy lacking the datacenter growth upside of Nokia's Nvidia partnership while Ericsson remains publicly skeptical of Nokia's GPU-dependent RAN approach citing extreme energy consumption and vendor lock-in.

Nokia's Nvidia-backed AI-RAN trials could create an insurmountable spectral efficiency advantage, leaving Ericsson's custom ASIC architecture isolated as 6G approaches.

Single Most Important Credibility Risk

The single most important credibility risk facing Ericsson is the structural governance compromise created by its dual-class share structure concentrating voting control, combined with lingering regulatory and anti-terrorism litigation risks that pose material compliance overhangs .

Extreme Swedish voting control through dual-class shares where Investor AB and Industrivärden control approximately 38% of voting rights but only 12.50% of capital may entrench these holding companies and prevent independent outside intervention, severely weakening the influence of public shareholders who hold 94.11% of capital but reduced votes.

Shaky investor confidence persists due to historical corruption breaches with ongoing anti-terrorism litigation regarding Iraq, while the 2022 Vonage acquisition remains subject to a CFIUS National Security Agreement imposing strict data access restrictions that adversely affect operations.

The termination of the DOJ monitorship in June 2024 may lead to compliance complacency, exposing the company to devastating recidivist penalties, with repairing ethics trust remaining the single most important governance challenge facing incoming leadership to restore market confidence.

This credibility risk is compounded by management's hollow Open RAN claims where customer executives reveal the AT&T contract retains proprietary hardware lock-in despite public marketing of open architecture, creating a severe narrative versus reality disconnect that threatens institutional investor trust.

Ericsson's management shows strong core operational execution but severely broken capital-allocation discipline, with outgoing CEO Ekholm demonstrating a strong match for operational force through 15,600 headcount cuts and strong match for commercial agency by securing the SEK 147bn AT&T contract, alongside partial match for strategic dynamism in shifting the core radio portfolio to proprietary custom silicon.

However, Ekholm is a weak match for capital-allocation discipline due to the value-destructive $6.2bn Vonage acquisition forcing massive asset impairments, while exhibiting partial match for communication clarity being direct on cost-cutting but tightlipped on opex details, and delivering strong outlier standards in radio engineering while struggling to replicate performance metrics in enterprise software.

The most vulnerable dimension is capital allocation severely damaged by the disastrous peak-valuation purchase of Vonage that forced SEK 11.4bn write-down in Q2 2024, with the enterprise unit continuing to be heavily loss-making and failing to generate promised synergies.

Major narrative versus reality disconnects persist, as while management promotes the AT&T contract as an Open RAN victory customer executives reveal it retains proprietary hardware lock-in, and contrary to open-architecture marketing software partners report Ericsson has a highly closed collaboration culture with proprietary silicon strategy restricting ability to capture high-growth cloud and AI data center demand.

Confidence in management quality and operator-thesis execution capability is assessed at medium plausibility , with the long-term strategic trajectory highly dependent on Per Narvinger's impending transition to revitalize technical execution and accelerate software monetization beyond conservative projections while managing the structural headwinds of flat carrier spending and intensifying AI-RAN competition.
