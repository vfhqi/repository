EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Alerion Clean Power operates as an independent power producer managing 44 special purpose vehicles across Italy, Romania, Bulgaria, and Spain, with gross operational capacity reaching 958.2 MW by mid-2025 . The company ranks as the fourth-largest onshore wind operator in Italy , concentrating primarily on wind generation supplemented by emerging solar photovoltaic capacity. Alerion generates revenue through direct merchant electricity sales, regulatory feed-in premiums paying EUR 55.3 per MWh in 2025 (up 31% year-on-year), and stable power purchase agreements , with approximately 84% of EBITDA margins protected through commodity swap hedging .

5

However, the foundation exhibits material governance vulnerabilities. The Gostner family maintains absolute control through a 64.78% direct equity stake via Fri-El Green Power and a 23.60% stake through FGPA, commanding 93.87% of total voting power . CEO Josef Gostner faces an active criminal investigation by the Trento Public Prosecutor's Office concerning alleged abuse or illicit communication of inside information . Alerion's heavy reliance on related-party Fri-El Service for operations and maintenance exposes minority shareholders to potential conflicts of interest , while related-party revenues reached EUR 45.11 million in 2025, primarily from power sales to Fri-El Trading under 12-year contracts through December 2035 . 8

The business model faces structural execution risks. Wind turbine procurement exhibits extreme supplier concentration, with Vestas supplying 70% of operational capacity, Gamesa 15%, and Repower 7% , while Alerion relies entirely on Phono Solar as the single-source supplier for all photovoltaic plants, exposing solar projects to component failures or delivery delays . European renewable sources contributed approximately 45% of total electricity generation in 2025, but rapid solar buildout materially increases zero and negative pricing events, with Spain's average captured solar prices plunging to approximately EUR 37 per MWh, capturing just 58% of baseload pricing . Wind SPVs sell merchant electricity through 12-month bilateral contracts with power trading companies containing no automatic renewal provisions, creating severe renewal risk and potential payment defaults .

Strategic positioning exposes the company to margin compression vectors. Alerion operates as a pure-play generator lacking the downstream retail networks of integrated European utilities , while smaller scale versus larger peers like ERG SpA exposes the firm to unfavorable equipment procurement terms compared with diversified utilities . Uncontracted renewable generators face severe margin pressure as daytime power prices trend toward zero, with independent power producers bearing high grid connection and resource volatility risks . Alerion faces competition from significantly larger operators, exposing the company to weaker equipment procurement terms , compounded by entire dependence on transmission network operators to deliver generated power to merchant markets, where network congestion or infrastructure malfunctions can limit or suspend power distribution capacity .

Regulatory dynamics present asymmetric policy intervention risks. The European Commission approved a EUR 23 billion Italian renewable support scheme in June 2026, targeting 37.15 GW of new capacity over 20 years through 20-year two-way Contracts for Difference stabilizing revenues at a fixed strike price . The 2026 Italian "Decreto Bollette" introduces a two percentage point IRAP tax increase for energy generators in 2026 and 2027, estimated to cost peer ERG SpA EUR 5 million in higher tax advances . Spain enacted Royal Decree 997/2025 strengthening mainland grid voltage stability after blackouts, with urgent voltage control mandates limiting

renewable market participation and triggering uncompensated solar curtailments up to 90% in regions like Extremadura .

Long-term structural evolution favors established platforms while intensifying margin capture competition. Extreme permitting complexity and capital intensity prevent smaller developers from entering the Italian market, with onshore wind project permitting frequently exceeding two years . Rapid solar PV capacity growth depresses daytime capture prices to extreme lows, with major European electricity markets recording 600 to 800 hours of negative wholesale power prices, forcing battery storage to transition from optional technology to mandatory requirement . European power demand is entering a structural growth phase, rising 0.6% year-onyear to 2,770 TWh in 2025, driven by transportation, heat electrification, and rapid digital infrastructure expansion, with Spanish data centers projected to require up to 2.5 GW of power capacity by 2030 .

  1. Extreme regulatory permitting delays and capital intensity form highly protective entry barriers for established independent power producers.
  2. Sustained midday power price cannibalisation forces developers to accelerate co-located battery energy storage system integration to preserve project returns.
  3. Robust electrification and energy security mandates secure long-term utility-scale volume demand despite volatile wholesale merchant prices.
  4. Full Alignment with EU Green Taxonomy provides Alerion with superior access to dedicated green bond financing pools.

Barriers to entry and exit dynamics

1.
Challenging entry barriers: extreme permitting complexity and capital intensity prevent smaller developers from entering the Italian market
2.
Prolonged development timelines: Italian onshore wind project permitting frequently exceeds two years, halting rapid competitor market entry
3.
Land use restrictions: Italy's bans on ground-mounted solar installations on agricultural land severely limit suitable development locations
4.
High capital requirements: developing a utility-scale wind pipeline requires massive upfront capital before securing connection rights
5.
Restrictive grid connection: grid connection bottlenecks act as a severe physical barrier, locking out non- permitted portfolios
6.
Severe exit barriers: long-term land leases with 22-year average residual durations prevent easy operational exits
7.
Inflexible grid contracts: developers are locked into long-term grid injection agreements that could carry substantial uncompensated curtailment risks

Technological disruption and storage co-location

8.
Severe pricing cannibalisation: rapid solar PV capacity growth depresses daytime capture prices to extreme lows across Southern Europe
9.
High negative hours: major European electricity markets recorded 600 to 800 hours of negative wholesale power prices
10.
Urgent storage mandate: battery storage is transitioning from an optional technology to a mandatory requirement to mitigate curtailments
11.
Falling battery costs: global benchmark costs for 4-hour systems declined 25% to 30% year-on-year in 2025
12.
Strong BESS growth: European operational grid-scale battery capacity surpassed 10 GW in 2025, driven by grid flexibility needs
13.
Enhanced project economics: co-locating battery storage with Spanish solar assets increases project internal rate of returns by 100 basis points
14.
Future hydrogen optionality: green hydrogen production through electrolysis could play a major role in absorbing excess renewable generation long term

Long-term volume and pricing demand

15.
Growing power demand: European power demand is entering a structural growth phase, rising 0.6% year-on- year to 2,770 TWh in 2025
16.
Core structural drivers: transportation, heat electrification, and rapid digital infrastructure expansion underpin long-term power consumption growth
17.
Accelerating digital load: Spanish data centers are projected to require up to 2.5 GW of power capacity by 2030
18.
Favorable price scenario: gas turbine power generation continues to set marginal electricity prices during non- renewable peak evening hours
19.
Protected revenue streams: the majority of Alerion's revenues are de-risked through regulated tariffs and fixed-price contracts
20.
Symmetrical contract safety: new Italian Contracts for Difference schemes provide 20-year revenue stabilization, mitigating long-term merchant volatility

Green taxonomy and ESG and paradigm fit

21.
Clear taxonomy alignment: Alerion's wind and solar operations contribute directly to "Climate mitigation" under the European Union taxonomy
22.
Superior financing access: taxonomy compliance enables Alerion to successfully issue structured Green Bonds to fund capital-intensive projects
23.
Structured sustainability goals: executive long-term incentive plans link 10% of performance targets to measurable corporate ESG achievements
24.
Rigorous environmental audits: Alerion's assets maintain UNI EN ISO 45001:2023 and ISO 14001:2015 certifications for ecological management
25.
Positive regime fit: Alerion benefits from the EU Green Deal's binding mandate of a 42.5% renewable share by 2030
26.
Other side of the story: transatlantic equity markets occasionally discount independent developers as pure- play merchant risk assets during pricing downturns
