EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model and Strategic Positioning

Trelleborg has successfully pivoted towards high-margin engineered polymer solutions by systematically divesting cyclical, commodity-like operations such as tires and printing blankets, fundamentally transforming its competitive position. The company operates through three core divisions: Trelleborg Sealing Solutions accounting for 48% of sales and maintaining a global top three position in industrial sealing, Trelleborg Industrial Solutions representing 43% of sales and specializing in critical hose systems and marine docking infrastructure, and Trelleborg Medical Solutions contributing 9% of sales with silicon precision focus . The decentralized operating structure consisting of 20 business units and 40 product areas places decision-making authority close to customers , driving operational agility and supporting a high-margin pricing model. Geographic diversification is balanced across Europe at 43%, Americas at 33%, and Asia/ROW at 24%, with local-for-local manufacturing mitigating tariff exposures . The massive SEK 22,000 M divestment of Trelleborg Wheel Systems to Yokohama Rubber, finalized in May 2023 for EUR 2,100 M, unlocked structural margin expansion and funded strategic reinvestment .

Value Chain Economics and Competitive Positioning

Trelleborg operates primarily in the component manufacturing stage, which generates typical gross margins of 33% and EBIT margins of 16.5%, while selectively expanding into aftermarket distribution with exceptional margins of 45% gross and 22% EBIT . The company bypasses volatile upstream raw material extraction and custom compounding stages, procuring pre-compounded materials from specialists like Hexpol . Raw material inputs representing 48% of production costs are primarily fossil-derived, though Trelleborg successfully passes raw material price movements directly through to customers . Downstream integration through acquisitions like CRC Distribution expands high-margin aftermarket access, where short sealing lifecycles of approximately 200 hours generate recurring revenue streams valued at 3-4x the original equipment sale . Trelleborg competes against diversified giants including Parker-Hannifin generating $19.90 billion in sales, SaintGobain across construction and industrial solutions, DuPont in medical and semiconductor niches, and direct specialists like Hutchinson and Semperit , maintaining competitive discipline through value-based pricing rather than margin-dilutive market share wars.

Customer Dynamics and Switching Economics

Trelleborg maintains a highly diversified customer base across a large number of customer groups, preventing material concentration risks , with diversified industrials representing 59% of total sales and the sealing division generating 50% of revenues from diversified industrial applications . Customer switching costs are exceptionally high due to the business-critical role of specialized components, with industrial customers prioritizing chemical consistency over low prices and avoiding supplier changes due to high re-qualification costs . The vast majority of sales are single-sourced, as Trelleborg's precision products typically represent a very small fraction of customer total manufacturing costs . Trelleborg demonstrates superior pricing power across business cycles, with proactive pricing adjustments in early 2022 fully offsetting material, logistics, and energy cost increases while defending and expanding gross profit margins . Decentralized regional manufacturing allows local pricing hikes to fully compensate for unavoidable tariffs, with customer awareness of global trade tensions making price increases readily accepted with minimal volume pushback .

Operator Quality and Foundation Strength

President Peter Nilsson has engineered a multi-year strategic pivot away from low-margin commodity markets, systematically divesting volatile segments including printing blankets and offshore oil assets, with the massive tire divestment for EUR 2.10 billion permanently raising the group's financial baseline . Capital allocation focuses on integrating high-quality companies that expand geographic and technological reach, including Minnesota Rubber & Plastics to strengthen North American presence and Baron Group representing the largest medical technology transaction to date . The group returns substantial capital to shareholders through continuous buyback execution at approximately SEK 500 M per quarter, with the 2026 AGM approving cancellation of over 6.40 million repurchased shares and a proposed dividend increase to SEK 8.00 per share . Specialized elastomeric solutions like the Stefa HiSpin EV40 radial shaft seal designed for electric vehicles operating up to 40 meters per second, and the proprietary Seal-Glide treatment reducing semiconductor stiction by up to 85%, create exceptional product-level moats . The rolling 12-month cash conversion ratio reached an elite 95% in early 2026, supported by efficient working capital control and flexible capital expenditures , though goodwill of SEK 23,026 M representing 42% of total assets temporarily drags ROCE to 12.1%, while ROCE excluding goodwill reached a highly impressive 24.6% . 23

Long-Term Growth Vectors and Structural Evolution

Entry barriers remain formidable due to extreme technical qualification requirements taking several years to secure and co-development of bespoke sealing profiles preventing simple product substitution . The Polymers for Tomorrow program targets sustainable material alternatives to replace fossil feedstocks, with bio-based and recycled materials reaching 17% of products in 2025 and a long-term target of 25% by 2030 . High-growth adjacencies include commercial aerospace projected to grow above 5% annually with the Magee Plastics acquisition expanding thermoplastic cabin offerings, semiconductor manufacturing with Seal-Glide treatment reducing stiction by up to 85%, the medical division targeting SEK 5,000 M in sales, and green hydrogen with the specialized H2 Pro product family for high-pressure applications . Trelleborg's transition from a cyclical short-cycle stock to a high-margin niche specialist fits quality-oriented equity regimes, with active portfolio management and higher profitability leading to material valuation re-rating over time .
