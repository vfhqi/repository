EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Strategic Transformation and Leadership Change

The unexpected CEO transition in June 2026, with Olivier Biebuyck replacing Yves Kerstens ahead of his 2028 mandate , represents a major structural shift in leadership agility at a critical juncture as the company executes concurrent portfolio exits , footprint consolidations , and specialty business integrations . The absence of active

activist presence provides management with operational flexibility , though the heavy C-suite turnover across strategy, digital, and HR functions during 2025-2026 introduces execution risks during a period requiring seamless integration of the €40 million BEXCO synthetic ropes acquisition and the €60 million Bridgestone tire cord plant deal . The Latin American commodity wire divestments slashed regional sales exposure from 18% to approximately 4% , eliminating €57 million in cumulative translation losses while generating €20 million in disposal gains , though the €162 million in one-off restructuring charges during 2025 signals the painful cost of repositioning the industrial footprint through 13 plant closures since 2019 . The temporary halt of green hydrogen production at Wetteren , Belgium, triggering a €55 million impairment , underscores the material gap between ambitious clean-tech targets and real-world regulatory delays in European and US hydrogen subsidy implementation, forcing a postponement of the >10% mid-term EBITu margin ambition .
