Change log v2 → v3:

The HTRO investment thesis has been substantially stress-tested by a comprehensive dual-source ([C] + [AS]) research programme covering 15 topics. The core thesis — DC portfolio transformation, Fröberg execution, Fibre Solutions floor — is intact and Stage 1 confirmed by Q1 2026 results. Three material new developments complicate the picture:

Positive: Hexatronic acquired Superior Fiber & Data Services (May 2026), funded by a SEK 600m rights issue at 8.2% discount. This acquisition targets the Enterprise DC market — a direct strategic response to the Corning hyperscaler ceiling. Management is moving faster than expected and is explicitly de-risking the DC concentration from hyperscalers.

Negative (governance): The LTIP 2026 proposal — which was linked to the 2028 financial targets (SEK 8.7–11.7bn revenue, 10.5–12.5% EBITA margin) — was withdrawn at the May 12, 2026 AGM. This materially weakens the management alignment case. Without a long-term equity incentive tied to 2028 targets, the incentive for Fröberg to remain through the full transformation and to execute precisely against those targets is less explicit.

Negative (competitive): Corning has secured not just Meta + Nvidia, but also two additional hyperscaler LTAs of similar scale to the Meta deal — potentially locking 3–4 of the top 5 US hyperscaler relationships. Corning also has a Poland manufacturing facility serving European customers. The geographic buffer for European DC suppliers is relationship-based, not logistics-based. Prysmian is simultaneously securing its own hyperscaler LTAs (€5bn+ announced). HTRO's effective DC addressable market is the regional/Enterprise DC tier.

Net assessment: The thesis holds, the position rationale is intact at 3%, the IC is B- with P4=D+ and P6=D+. The LTIP withdrawal is the most actionable new negative and warrants a direct follow-up question to management.
