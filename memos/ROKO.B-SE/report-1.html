Company Overview & Business Model
The 'Perpetual Owner' Philosophy

Röko AB was founded in 2019 with a business model centered on acquiring and developing small and medium-sized European niche companies. The overarching strategy is built upon the concept of "perpetual ownership" of consistently profitable businesses. Unlike traditional private equity firms that operate with a defined exit horizon, Röko's perpetual investment perspective guarantees that the long-term future of its subsidiaries is secured. This infinite horizon often acts as a decisive factor for founders and family owners when choosing a reliable buyer for their life's work.

To ensure continuous alignment of interests, the company typically acquires a majority stake while enabling the original founders and local management teams to retain a minority share. This co-ownership model guarantees that both the parent company and the subsidiary's leadership share the same financial incentives and operational goals.

Decentralized Governance and Entrepreneurial Culture

Röko operates a highly decentralized governance structure guided by three core values: continuous development, simplicity, and empowerment. The firm explicitly avoids forcing its newly acquired subsidiaries into a rigid, large-company mold. Instead, subsidiaries retain their original brand identity, unique culture, and operational independence.

Local management teams are fully empowered to make swift, day-to-day operational decisions close to their customers and employees. Centralized management from Röko is intentionally minimal, designed primarily to ensure internal control, track key performance indicators, and provide strategic expertise only when requested. This autonomous structure fosters a strong entrepreneurial spirit, which the company views as a critical element in motivating and retaining key personnel post-acquisition.

Overview of Röko AB's operational model and value chain responsibilities

Segment Division: B2B and B2C

Although Röko operates as a sector-agnostic serial acquirer, its portfolio is structurally divided into two primary reporting segments: Business-to-Business (B2B) and Business-to-Consumer (B2C). Grouping the portfolio in this manner enables closer collaboration and knowledge exchange between subsidiaries facing similar end-market demands and customer behaviors.

The B2B segment constitutes the larger portion of the group, comprising 20 business units by the end of 2025. These B2B companies are typically niche manufacturers and distributors characterized by longer sales cycles, larger transaction volumes, and complex purchasing processes. Conversely, the B2C segment consists of 10 business units that directly target private consumers. These consumer-facing entities feature shorter sales cycles and place a heavier emphasis on brand marketing and customer experience.

Leadership Track Record and Transition

Röko's rapid expansion is deeply rooted in the proven historical success of its founders, Fredrik Karlsson and Tomas Billing. Prior to establishing Röko, Karlsson served as the CEO of the renowned Swedish compounder Lifco for over two decades, during which Lifco's value increased roughly 80-fold. Billing simultaneously served as CEO of the investment company Nordstjernan, growing its net asset value from SEK 3 billion to SEK 30 billion. Drawing on these successful compounder models, Karlsson and Billing successfully listed Röko's Class B shares on Nasdaq Stockholm in March 2025.

In March 2026, Röko announced a significant leadership transition, appointing Johan Bladh as the new CEO effective April 22, 2026. Bladh was the founders' first hire in 2019, previously serving as Deputy CEO and CFO, and has been instrumental in executing nearly all of the company's transactions. Following the transition, Karlsson will step down as CEO but remain operationally active as Deputy CEO and Chairman of the Investment Committee to continue driving the firm's acquisition strategy alongside the new leadership.

M&A Strategy & Track Record
Strict Investment Criteria and ESG Screening

Röko AB employs a highly disciplined, programmatic approach to M&A, targeting small to medium-sized European niche companies. The firm evaluates hundreds of potential candidates annually, filtering them through a stringent set of core financial and operational prerequisites. Primarily, a target must demonstrate a ten-year track record of continuous profit growth and generate an adjusted EBITA margin exceeding 15%. Additionally, Röko seeks asset-light businesses with capital expenditures below 5% of net sales, strong control over working capital, and an EBITA typically ranging between EUR 2 million and EUR 10 million. To mitigate customer concentration risks, the target's largest single customer must account for less than 12% of total net sales.

Beyond financial metrics, environmental, social, and governance factors serve as strict prerequisites rather than mere formalities during the due diligence process. The company explicitly excludes investments in heavy polluters, fossil fuel-dependent operations, controversial weapons, and the tobacco industry. A detailed climate-risk analysis is performed on each candidate to ensure that physical and transitional climate risks are low and that the business model is resilient enough for perpetual ownership.

The 85% Ownership Model and Aligned Incentives

A defining characteristic of Röko’s M&A framework is its unique ownership structure, which is designed to preserve the entrepreneurial drive of its subsidiaries. Instead of acquiring 100% of a company upfront, Röko typically acquires a majority stake while requiring the original founders and local management team to retain a significant minority interest. On average, Röko initially secures around 75% of a subsidiary's shares, with a long-term strategic target of reaching 85% ownership over time.

This co-ownership model effectively aligns the financial incentives of the parent group with the local management teams executing the daily operations. To secure the infinite investment horizon, the outstanding minority shares are governed by mandatory put and call options. These contractual agreements give Röko the ultimate right to acquire all remaining shares in the future, while providing founders with a structured liquidity mechanism based on the subsequent performance of the business.

Historical Acquisition Pace and Valuation Discipline

Since its inception in 2019, Röko has rapidly established a proven track record, executing 33 acquisitions consisting of 28 stand-alone platforms and five bolt-on additions. The company has maintained a consistent acquisition pace of approximately five to six deals per year. The average acquired entity generates around SEK 153 million in annual sales and SEK 30 million in EBITA.

Geographical Expansion and Sector Agnosticism

While Röko is sector-agnostic, its M&A activities are structurally categorized into Business-to-Business (B2B) and Business-to-Consumer (B2C) segments. Historically, approximately 70% of the company's acquisitions have been directed toward the B2B segment, as these niche manufacturing and distribution businesses frequently align best with Röko's stringent investment criteria.

Geographically, Röko has pursued a highly international expansion strategy, differentiating itself from many Nordic-centric peers. By 2024, approximately 67% of the group's sales were generated outside of the Nordic region. The United Kingdom has emerged as Röko's largest market, accounting for roughly 34% of group sales, supported by nine platform acquisitions including the recent additions of ABP Group and Godiva Bearings.

Recent transaction history underscores the firm's accelerating pan-European footprint across multiple new jurisdictions. In Italy, Röko established its presence with the acquisitions of ITIB Machinery and the laser manufacturer Lambda S.p.A. The company also penetrated the German market with the acquisition of ATEMAG and its subsequent add-on, OPPOLD. Further diversification into Western Europe includes the additions of conveyor lubrication specialist CHP in Belgium, Pureoptics in France, and cleaning products manufacturer Baymax in the Netherlands.

Financial Performance & Capital Structure
Revenue Growth and Profitability

The programmatic M&A framework discussed previously has directly translated into robust top-line expansion and resilient margins. For the full year 2025, Röko reported net sales of SEK 6,452 million, representing a 4% increase from SEK 6,182 million in 2024. This growth was driven by a combination of new platform acquisitions and organic development, though it was partially offset by a negative 4% impact from currency exchange fluctuations. When adjusting for these currency effects, organic net sales grew by 2% in local currency.

Profitability has consistently trended upward, reflecting the successful integration of asset-light, highly profitable niche businesses. In 2025, the Group's Adjusted EBITA increased by 9% year-over-year to SEK 1,339 million. In local currency terms, organic Adjusted EBITA demonstrated a healthy growth rate of 4%. Consequently, the overall Adjusted EBITA margin expanded by a full percentage point to reach 21% for the year.

Capital Efficiency and Cash Generation

Capital efficiency metrics highlight the transitional nature of Röko's aggressive foundational growth phase. The Return on Capital Employed stood at 14.8% for 2025, marking a slight increase from 14.4% in the previous year. While this headline ROCE appears somewhat lower than that of mature Swedish compounders, it is heavily diluted by the massive goodwill and intangible assets added during the company's rapid recent acquisition spree. When excluding intangible assets arising from acquisitions, the underlying ROCE is exceptionally strong, reaching 186% in 2025.

Operating cash flow remained highly robust, generating SEK 1,073 million in 2025. This excellent cash conversion is a direct result of the subsidiaries operating with minimal capital expenditure requirements, which typically average below 1.5% of sales. This internal cash generation serves as the primary funding mechanism for the group's ongoing M&A pipeline.

Leverage Profile and Financial Targets

Röko maintains a disciplined capital allocation framework guided by strict internal financial targets. The overarching leverage objective is to maintain financial net debt below 3.0x Adjusted EBITDA over the long term, though the board permits temporary elevations following major strategic acquisitions.

By the end of 2025, total financial net debt stood at a comfortable 2.0x LTM Adjusted EBITDA, a reduction from 2.1x in 2024. It is important to note that Röko conservatively calculates this metric by including the substantial liabilities associated with the put and call options for non-controlling minority stakes. When isolating purely interest-bearing net debt, the leverage ratio is exceptionally low at just 0.3x Adjusted EBITDA. This conservative leverage profile is paired with a shareholder distribution policy that targets a modest dividend payout of 0% to 20% of net profit, ensuring the vast majority of capital is retained and reinvested for compounding.

Broker Perspectives on Valuation and Future Growth

Sell-side analysis highlights the structural advantages of Röko's current financial profile and capital allocation track record. According to research from DNB Carnegie, Röko generates a Return on Incremental Capital Employed (ROICE) of approximately 14%, underscoring strong value creation on newly deployed capital. Analysts forecast that the company's excellent historical cash conversion and disciplined acquisition multiples will allow it to sustain EBITA growth exceeding 15% annually without the need to increase its net debt to EBITDA ratio.

Looking at the longer term, the broker assesses that Röko is fundamentally well-positioned to double its earnings over the next five years. From a valuation perspective, DNB Carnegie values the stock using a peer group discount methodology, placing a fair value target price around SEK 1,865, which equates to an estimated 2026 EV/EBITA multiple of roughly 20x. The broker justifies this premium valuation by pointing to the extensive M&A runway remaining in the fragmented European market, which is expected to fuel double-digit acquired EBITA growth through at least 2031.

ESG, Sustainability & Operational Risks
ESG Integration in Due Diligence

Röko AB views sustainable business practices as a fundamental prerequisite for achieving long-term, resilient profit growth. The company's investment philosophy is inherently linked to ESG principles, meaning that sustainability is treated as a mandatory qualifying criterion rather than a mere formality during the acquisition process. Consequently, the firm's strict investment guide explicitly excludes investments in heavy polluters and businesses dependent on fossil fuels. The exclusion policy also strictly prohibits the acquisition of companies involved in the production of controversial weapons, tobacco, and gambling.

During the evaluation of potential acquisition targets, Röko conducts a formalized and comprehensive ESG due diligence process. This deep-dive analysis evaluates a target's working conditions, biodiversity impact, environmental footprint, and gender equality metrics. A dedicated climate-risk analysis is also performed to assess both physical and transitional climate risks, ensuring that the business model is viable for a perpetual ownership horizon. If a candidate presents significant transition risks, even under a low-emission scenario, the investment opportunity is decisively rejected.

Governance and Decentralization Challenges

While Röko's highly decentralized governance model empowers local management and fosters entrepreneurial spirit, it introduces distinct challenges regarding corporate culture and compliance oversight. Because the parent company operates with a minimal centralized workforce, the primary environmental and social impacts occur exclusively at the subsidiary level. Management must constantly strike a delicate balance between granting operational autonomy and ensuring that all portfolio companies align with the Group's overarching ethical values.

Mismanagement, cultural misalignment, or compliance failures within an individual subsidiary can adversely impact the entire Group's reputation and financial performance. To mitigate these operational risks, Röko enforces an ethical framework anchored by a unified Code of Conduct and a formalized whistleblowing policy. The internal control system heavily relies on the expectation that subsidiary CEOs will proactively report any deviations from applicable laws or ethical guidelines to their respective Röko representatives.

Sustainability Targets and Reporting Standards

Historically, Röko has not adopted Group-wide sustainability targets, such as specific greenhouse gas emissions reductions or quantified social objectives. Management believes that dictating rigid, top-down sustainability mandates contradicts the decentralized governance model, preferring instead that autonomous subsidiaries make the business decisions necessary for continuous environmental improvement.

Despite the lack of centralized targets, the company has heavily invested in structural reporting enhancements to comply with impending European regulations. During 2024 and 2025, Röko executed a double materiality assessment to thoroughly identify the financial impacts of sustainability risks and its operations' effects on the environment. The firm aims to achieve full compliance with the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive by 2025, aligning its data collection and internal controls with the European Sustainability Reporting Standards . Furthermore, the Group's strategic philosophy is deliberately aligned with several United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, specifically targeting economic productivity (SDG 8.2), decent job creation (SDG 8.3), and inclusive industrialization (SDG 9.2).

Operational Risks and Supply Chain Scrutiny

Given its diverse portfolio across B2B and B2C segments, Röko is exposed to an array of operational and macroeconomic risks. A large portion of the Group's revenue stems from inherently cyclical industries, such as automotive, construction, and consumer discretionary sectors, making the portfolio vulnerable to economic downturns and swings in demand.

To manage social and environmental risks outside its direct operations, Röko places significant emphasis on supply chain scrutiny. The company conducts a "cradle to grave" analysis of its value chains, identifying potential vulnerabilities in procurement, manufacturing, and distribution. Through the Group's Code of Conduct and local supplier assessments, subsidiaries are strictly prohibited from conducting business with suppliers that fail to uphold fundamental human rights, respect environmental regulations, or comply with applicable international laws.
