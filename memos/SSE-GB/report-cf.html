EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 2 findings

SSE plc emerges as a top-tier European utility growth story anchored by an unprecedented £33bn capital programme through 2030, with 80% allocated to regulated transmission networks that will deliver a sector-leading 17% EPS CAGR from FY26 to FY29, outperforming mainland peers while simultaneously navigating unprecedented executive turnover and severe supply chain bottlenecks that threaten execution timelines. The investment thesis rests on regulated network RAB growth and flexible thermal capacity step-ups driving over 80% of forward EPS expansion, though this concentration exposes the group to compounding execution risks across grid consenting, turbine commissioning, and equipment supply chains.

Leadership transitions unfolded orderly as Martin Pibworth succeeded Alistair Phillips-Davies in July 2025, bringing insider continuity after 27 years with the organization, while the Group Executive Committee expanded aggressively with fresh hires across IT, Procurement, Legal, Sustainability, and Business Development. However, employee sustainable engagement collapsed from 86% to 78% amid corporate efficiency reviews targeting £200m of annual recurring savings, signaling cultural friction during the most capital-intensive scaling period in company history. The simultaneous departure of the CEO, CFO, General Counsel, and Chief Sustainability Officer over a compressed 24-month window raises material questions about institutional knowledge retention during a period demanding flawless execution of an £11bn annual capital programme.
