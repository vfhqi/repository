  • Production capacity is being expanded aggressively at the Virginia facility (Phase II adding 200,000 sq ft; completion Q2 2026) and in Texas.   • Management's ambition is explicit and large: Forsström has stated the company is closer to $10bn revenue potential than $5bn. 2024 revenue was SEK 15.45bn (~$1.5bn). That is a 6-7x ambition from current scale. It should be stress-tested, not taken at face value.

COMPANY DESCRIPTION

Munters Group AB (MTRS-SE) is a Swedish industrial technology company founded in 1955, headquartered in Kista outside Stockholm. It designs, manufactures, and services energy-efficient climate control systems — primarily air handling, precision cooling, dehumidification, humidity management, and digital control systems. It listed on Nasdaq Stockholm in 2017.

The company operates across three business areas:

AirTech — the original and historically largest division — provides humidity and temperature management solutions across industrial end-markets: battery/EV manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, semiconductor fabrication, food processing, cultural heritage preservation, and general manufacturing. AirTech products include desiccant dehumidifiers (Munters' original technology), air handling units, and evaporative cooling systems. Revenue model is primarily equipment sales with a growing aftermarket/service component. Revenue approximately SEK 5-6bn (estimated L12M).

**Data Center Technologies * — Munters' fastest-growing division, carved out as a standalone business area in 2022. Produces precision air handling systems (CRAHs), cooling distribution units (CDUs), indirect evaporative cooling units, and now chillers (via Geoclima acquisition). Customers are hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, Google-tier), co-location providers, and enterprise data centre operators, primarily in North America but growing in EMEA. Revenue model is large project-based equipment orders with long lead times. Revenue approximately SEK 4-5bn (estimated L12M, growing fast).

FoodTech — repositioned aggressively over 2024-2025. Previously operated in two segments: Equipment (ventilation for poultry, pig, and layer houses — divested May 2025) and Digital Solutions (software, sensors, controllers for farm climate management). FoodTech is now entirely a digital/SaaS business following the disposal of physical manufacturing. Revenue model is recurring SaaS subscriptions plus hardware controller sales. FoodTech SaaS ARR grew 46% in Q4 2024 and >50% in Americas. Total FoodTech revenue is small relative to AirTech/DCT but growth rate is high and margin profile is improving.

Over L3Y (2022-2024): group revenue grew from approximately SEK 11bn to SEK 15.45bn. EBITA margin expanded from ~12% to 15.1%. Over N3Y (2025-2027), management's stated ambition is continued double-digit annual growth driven primarily by DCT, with AirTech recovering as battery markets normalise and FoodTech growing as a pure digital business.

CHANGE IN STRATEGY AND STRUCTURE

Divisional Structure: Munters operates three business areas: AirTech, DCT, FoodTech. DCT was only established as a standalone division in 2022 — it previously sat within AirTech. This structural change is significant: it gave DCT dedicated leadership, P&L accountability, and investor visibility.

AI/Data Centre Strategic Priority: DCT has become the explicit primary growth engine. Capital investment, M&A, and capacity expansion are all concentrated here. The Geoclima acquisition (October 2024) extended Munters' DCT offering from air handling and evaporative cooling into chillers, completing a full data centre cooling stack: CRAH + CDU + IEC + chiller. The Virginia factory Phase II expansion adds 200,000 sq ft dedicated to smaller DCT units with completion Q2 2026. Together with the Texas facility, Munters will have ~700,000 sq ft of US DCT manufacturing capacity.

FoodTech Pivot: The divestiture of FoodTech Equipment (May 2025, MEUR 97.5) is a fundamental portfolio rationalisation. Munters retained the Digital Solutions business and divested ~400 employees and five physical manufacturing sites. The stated rationale is to focus FoodTech on recurring digital revenue with higher margins and lower capital intensity.

AirTech — Battery Exposure: AirTech serves battery/EV, semiconductor, pharma, and general industry. The battery sub-segment was a material growth driver in 2022-2023 as EV investment surged. It became a major drag in late 2024 and through 2025. Munters took restructuring actions in AirTech to reduce fixed cost exposure. The long-term thesis on battery (EV electrification growth at 10-15% CAGR to 2030) is intact; the timing of recovery is uncertain.

CMD 2024 Targets: Net sales growth >14% per year average over a business cycle; OWC 13-10% of sales; EBITA margin 13-16%.

Rating: Green. Strategy is clear, differentiated, and consistent. Portfolio simplification (FoodTech Equipment divestiture) reduces complexity. DCT investment is credible and backed by order evidence. The key risk is execution complexity during simultaneous capacity ramp, CEO transition, and tariff disruption.

VALUE CHAIN — DEMAND

DCT / Data Centre: The demand pulse is structurally elevated and driven by AI infrastructure investment. The hyperscaler capex cycle is the primary driver. Microsoft, Meta, Alphabet, Amazon, and Oracle have each committed to multi-year data centre capex programmes totalling hundreds of billions of dollars. Munters' order win rate has been exceptional: a $215m CRAH order from a single US hyperscaler; a BSEK 2.1 order batch in Q4 2025; an $82m Geoclima chiller order (H1 2025). The DCT backlog now extends into 2027-2028, providing multi-year revenue visibility.

AI-specific demand drivers: AI training clusters run at power densities of 30-100kW per rack versus 5-15kW for conventional compute. This requires precision cooling solutions at unprecedented power density. The Q1 2025 report noted DCT "one of its highest-ever first-quarter order intake, driven by small and mid-sized orders" — suggesting demand is broadening beyond the very largest hyperscalers.

Creative probing: Management narrative consistently emphasises hyperscaler relationships and large-order announcements. What is less visible: the project margin profile on large custom CRAH orders. Custom design at high volumes should generate operating leverage, but Munters has acknowledged product mix and transition costs are affecting DCT margins. The question is whether large hyperscaler orders carry above or below DCT average margins. The evidence (tariff impact flagged on Italian-sourced products, dual-site Amesbury costs) suggests DCT margins were actually compressed in 2025 despite volume surge. This is the opposite of what a high-quality earnings ramp looks like.

AirTech — Battery: Battery/EV was a high-growth sub-segment in 2022-2023 when European and US gigafactory investment peaked. Demand collapsed in late 2024 as EV adoption growth slowed, battery manufacturers paused capacity expansion, and competitive price pressure reduced investment appetite. Munters says the long-term growth rate of EVs at 10-15% CAGR to 2030 remains intact. The structural thesis is plausible. The timing of recovery is not visible. Management has described the outlook as "weak throughout 2025" and into 2026.

FoodTech Digital: Demand for agricultural digital solutions is driven by farmer productivity economics, regulatory pressure on animal welfare and emissions, and the biosecurity imperative. Churn rates are described as low. ARR growth of 46-50% in Q4 2024 and Q1 2025 is impressive but from a small base.

Rating: Green for DCT; Orange for AirTech; Yellow for FoodTech Digital.

VALUE CHAIN — COMPETITION

DCT Competitors:

Vertiv Holdings (VRT, NYSE): Revenue ~$8bn. Competes in thermal management and power distribution. Has more liquid cooling exposure — acquired CoolTera and developed direct liquid cooling solutions — giving it an AI density positioning advantage.

Stulz (private, German): Precision cooling for data centres. Primarily single-aisle and row-based cooling units. Strong in European co-location market. Does not have the CRAH/IEC/chiller breadth Munters has assembled via Geoclima.

Schneider Electric (SU, Paris): Competes in data centre infrastructure including cooling. Scale and hyperscaler relationships are significant, but cooling business is embedded within a larger power management offering.

Munters' Differentiation: Energy efficiency (PUE/WUE metrics). Indirect evaporative cooling uses ambient air with minimal water consumption. Custom CRAH design for high-density AI racks. Full stack: CRAH + CDU + chiller (post-Geoclima). Regional production in US reduces lead times and tariff exposure. The ZutaCore partnership extends Munters into a hybrid air+liquid architecture.

Barriers to Entry in Hyperscale DCT: High. Hyperscalers qualify vendors rigorously; Munters' hyperscaler-qualified status is a meaningful moat. Custom design and testing to hyperscaler specifications requires engineering capability and manufacturing discipline at scale.

Rating: Yellow. Competitive intensity is elevated but Munters holds genuine differentiation. The risk is Vertiv's liquid cooling investment positioning it better for ultra-high density AI cooling.

VALUE CHAIN — DISRUPTION

Liquid Cooling vs Air Cooling: This is the central technology disruption question for Munters' DCT thesis. Air cooling is effective up to approximately 20-40kW per rack. AI GPU clusters running at 50-100kW+ per rack are at or beyond the effective limit of air cooling. Direct liquid cooling , immersion cooling, and two-phase liquid cooling are gaining adoption at the highest density deployments.

Munters' response is two-pronged. First, via ZutaCore — a September 2024 partnership to integrate ZutaCore's HyperCool with Munters' SyCool refrigerant-based thermosiphon loop. The combined system claims waterless operation, PUE of 1.05-1.07, and 50% space reduction. Second, Geoclima's chiller technology provides fluid-side cooling for liquid cooling deployments at facility level.

The honest assessment is that liquid cooling will take share at the highest AI density deployments. The majority of data centre racks globally remain at conventional densities where air cooling is superior on TCO. Munters' core CRAH business remains relevant for the bulk of data centre builds. The risk is that hyperscaler AI clusters — Munters' highest-growth order segment — are exactly the deployments most likely to transition to liquid cooling fastest.

Rating: Orange. Liquid cooling displacement risk is real and accelerating with AI density. Munters has responded with ZutaCore and Geoclima but has not yet demonstrated at scale that it can win on liquid cooling.

VALUE CHAIN — POLITICAL AND REGULATORY

EU Energy Efficiency Directive : Mandates that data centres with rated power of ≥500kW must publicly report energy performance data annually from May 2024. Impact on Munters: net positive. Regulatory pressure on PUE/WUE incentivises investment in energy-efficient cooling. Munters' IEC and SyCool solutions are specifically positioned on energy efficiency.

US Trade Tariffs: A material near-term headwind. Munters manufactures Geoclima chiller equipment in Italy and ships to US hyperscaler customers. The Trump tariff regime imposes duties on European-manufactured HVAC and industrial equipment imported into the US. Munters flagged tariff costs explicitly in Q4 2025 results. The Virginia factory Phase II expansion is in part a strategic response — shifting chiller production to the US to eliminate tariff exposure.

Data Centre Regulation in Europe: The EU has discussed caps or moratoria on new data centre construction in some markets (Dublin, Amsterdam) due to power grid constraints.

Rating: Yellow. EU energy efficiency regulation is a structural tailwind. US tariffs are a near-term headwind that Munters is actively mitigating through domestic capacity expansion.

VALUE CHAIN — SUPPLY SIDE

Component Supply for DCT: Key material inputs include aluminium, copper, steel, and electronic components. Lead times for custom CRAHs historically ran 26-52 weeks. The post-COVID component shortages (2021-2022) extended lead times significantly. By 2024-2025, the component supply environment had normalised for most inputs.

Geoclima Chillers: Italian manufacturing with a production site also in Thailand. Chillers require compressors (Copeland, Danfoss), refrigerant (transitioning away from high-GWP HFCs under EU F-Gas regulation), heat exchangers, and controls. F-Gas regulation requires refrigerant transitions in Geoclima's product line.

Virginia Capacity Ramp: The Amesbury (Massachusetts) to new Virginia facility transition was a near-term supply disruption. Munters explicitly cited "dual site operations" in Amesbury as a margin drag in Q1 2025. Virginia Phase II adds 200,000 sq ft and a chiller test laboratory.

Rating: Yellow. Supply chain is broadly normalised. The primary constraint is Munters' own production capacity ramp, not external component availability.

REVENUE AND DEMAND CYCLE

Munters' revenue cyclicality is bifurcated by division:

DCT is structural, not cyclical — for now. Hyperscaler data centre capex is driven by multi-year cloud and AI infrastructure roadmaps. The backlog extending to 2027-2028 provides unusual visibility. The risk is not the normal demand cycle — it is a hyperscaler pause triggered by macro deterioration, margin pressure, or AI monetisation disappointment.

AirTech is cyclical. Battery/EV investment tracks the underlying EV adoption curve and manufacturer economics, both of which are cycle-sensitive. Semiconductor capex is notoriously cyclical. Pharma is more defensive.

FoodTech (digital) is counter-cyclical to industrial. Agricultural climate management software is a recurring subscription. Farmers' incentive to optimise productivity and biosecurity does not track the economic cycle in the same way as industrial capex.

Current cycle position: DCT is at an extraordinary peak of structural demand (AI capex cycle, 2024-2027). AirTech battery is at cyclical trough (2024-2026). General industry is mid-cycle.

Rating: Yellow. The demand cycle picture is more complex than the market's simple "Munters = AI cooling play" narrative suggests. AirTech's recovery timeline matters materially for group earnings quality.

VALUE CHAIN — MACROECONOMIC

Interest Rates: Sweden (Riksbank) cut rates in 2024-2025 as inflation normalised. Lower Swedish/European rates reduce financing costs on SEK-denominated debt. Interest rate environment is broadly neutral to mildly supportive.

Currency — SEK/EUR/USD: Munters reports in SEK. DCT revenue is predominantly USD. A weaker SEK is positive for reported results when USD and EUR revenues are translated back. In Q3 2025, currency-adjusted growth was 26% — implying currency was a significant tailwind on a reported basis.

Global Data Centre Investment Outlook: Total global data centre capex is forecast to grow at double-digit rates annually through 2030. The US is the single largest market. Munters' current order portfolio is US-concentrated.

EV/Battery Macro: Global EV sales projected at 10-15% CAGR to 2030. European gigafactory investment has slowed materially in 2024-2025 as OEM EV strategies were revised. The medium-term thesis is intact; the near-term is soft.

Rating: Yellow. The macro environment is broadly supportive for Munters' growth markets. The key macro risk is a US recession causing hyperscaler capex retrenchment — low probability in 2025-2026 base case but the primary tail risk.
