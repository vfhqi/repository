Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Munters is a globally dominant niche climate provider that commands structurally high margins in mission-critical industries while shifting from simple hardware sales to highly integrated digital and service ecosystems.

Segment Offerings and Revenue Mix

1.
Dominant dehumidification core: The AirTech segment, which contributed 49% of Q1 2026 sales, designs precise moisture-control systems utilizing specialized desiccant rotors for demanding industrial cleanrooms. Dehumidification rotors are sold directly to pharmaceutical, battery, and food processing customers where precise moisture levels are mission-critical
2.
Advanced cooling powerhouse: The Data Center Technologies division, contributing 39% of sales, provides customized CRAH, CDU, and liquid cooling architectures for high-performance computing facilities. Its flagship SyCool indirect evaporative cooling platform provides highly energy-efficient heat rejection without water consumption
3.
Digital agricultural pioneer: The FoodTech segment, representing 12% of sales, has transformed from a commodity equipment manufacturer into a digital sensor, controller, and software provider. Its newly launched Speria brand offers a fully integrated digital food system that monitors livestock, greenhouses, and feed software
4.
Lucrative aftermarket expansion: Highly recurring service and replacement components represent 26% of group sales, moving closer to management's long-term corporate target of exceeding one-third. Services boast significantly higher margins and provide a critical buffer during cyclical downturns

Economics and Competitive Advantages

5.
Favorable pricing power: Munters operates in a highly profitable niche where climate infrastructure accounts for 10% to 25% of data center capital expenditures but dictates operational efficiency. Because power consumption is a critical bottleneck, operators gladly pay a premium for Munters' high-efficiency thermal units
6.
Significant customer concentration: Hyperscalers and tier-one colocation providers like Equinix control the data center segment, leading to naturally lumpy, high-value contract awards. To manage this concentration, hyperscalers intentionally diversify their procurement across multiple vendors, including Munters and Vertiv
7.
Insurmountable switching costs: Industrial lithium battery plants require absolute humidity control with low dew points to prevent chemical explosions, creating massive customer switching barriers. Once integrated, replacing a custom Munters dehumidifier is operationally disruptive and extremely expensive
8.
Proprietary technology moats: Munters' deep application knowledge and patented technologies, including its SyCool indirect evaporative cooling, are regarded as robust, premium offerings. Munters is a premium brand, meaning it does not compete on price but on proprietary desiccant dehumidification rotors and patented indirect evaporative cooling
9.
Consolidated competitor landscape: The high-density cooling market is heavily consolidated, with major competitors like Vertiv and Schneider Electric controlling 70% to 80% of U.S. market share. Munters remains a specialized, high-growth alternative that is capturing share
10.
Targeted software roll-ups: Bolt-on acquisitions like the UK-based AI livestock monitoring provider Optifarm have driven software annual recurring revenues to approximately SEK 300 million with healthy margins. - Earnings Calls Targeted software roll-ups (cont.): This digital business features high gross margins exceeding 70% and remains highly insulated from macro cycles

Disruptive transition risks: Evolving server chip architectures are accelerating direct liquid cooling, which could render Munters' core air-based evaporative cooling patents obsolete and commoditize its product offering. Disruptive transition risks (cont.): This shift would force Munters to compete directly in the standard chiller market, where margins are substantially lower

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is moderately elevated but manageable as temporary US import tariffs and battery market weakness compress near-term margins, while structural data center demand remains robust.

Operational Bottlenecks and Tariffs

1.
Severe tariff penalties: Import tariffs on European-manufactured chillers imported to the US slashed data center operating margins by 4 percentage points in recent quarters. This drag will persist until domestic production in Virginia is fully operational
2.
Vulnerable project revenues: In Q1 2026, Munters suffered a USD 28 million project cancellation in AirTech, proving that lumpy capital project revenues are vulnerable to macroeconomic downturns. This cancellation highlights the ongoing cyclicality of industrial battery end-markets
3.
Critical reference architectures: Vertiv and Schneider Electric maintain massive advantages by co-designing reference architectures directly with chipmakers like NVIDIA. Munters' lack of such status could exclude it from standard hyperscale designs
4.
109 Inflationary margin squeeze: A massive backlog can be a "false friend" because fixed-price contracts expose Munters to unexpected raw material inflation (steel, copper, and aluminum). If inflation spikes, the profitability of the pre-announced backlog will contract

Metrics and Early Warnings

5.
Essential early-warning metric: Investors must closely monitor the sequential book-to-bill ratio in the DCT segment to verify that order momentum is outpacing backlog monetization. A book-to-bill below 1x would indicate a rapid normalization of the data center cycle
6.
112 Overstated policy threats: Fears regarding US trade protectionism are heavily overstated because Munters produces approximately 90% of US sales domestically, providing an exceptional tariff shield. This localized footprint allows Munters to benefit as US customers prioritize supply chain reliability
