EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Materiality of Change

The five-year capital envelope expanded from €43 billion to €48 billion, with €40 billion allocated to regulated electricity distribution networks, representing the most material strategic shift in E.ON's operating profile since the 2019 Innogy integration. The OVO Energy acquisition adds 4 million UK accounts, creating a 9.6 million customer retail base with projected €90 million net income contribution by 2030, fundamentally altering E.ON's competitive position in the consolidating UK retail market. Management transitioned to timing-adjusted reporting from FY26, normalizing FY25 adjusted EBITDA from €9,849 million to €9,419 million and net income from €3,022 million to €2,744 million, introducing material comparability breaks that obscure historical trend analysis. The orderly CFO transition to Nadia Jakobi on June 1, 2024, ensures strategic financial continuity while weakened German redispatch proposal drafts raised the capacity-constrained designation hurdle from 3 percent to 5 percent, reducing potential opex penalties for network operators. Maturing low-coupon bonds require refinancing at current yields of 3.4 percent (5-year) and 3.9 percent (10-year), driving interest expenses upward and creating persistent headwinds to net income growth trajectories.
