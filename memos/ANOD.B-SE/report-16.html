§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Clean track record on observable metrics; dual-class share structure and concentrated ownership are the material structural governance risks; no red-flag events on record

Dual-class structure:
- Addnode Group has a dual-class share structure: Class A shares (10 votes per share) and Class B shares (1 vote per share); ANOD.B is the widely-traded B-class; ANOD.A is thin-float, high-vote
- Class A shares are held by founding-era investors and major shareholders; this structure concentrates voting control with a small group of long-term shareholders
- Söderberg family / major shareholder vehicles control a disproportionate share of voting rights relative to economic ownership; exact voting concentration: not precisely confirmed but major shareholders collectively likely control 40-60%+ of votes vs lower economic stake — consistent with Swedish dual-class norms
- ⚡ marker candidate: dual-class structure is a systemic governance feature of Swedish listed companies (AB Ericsson, Investor AB, Volvo — all dual-class); Addnode's structure is not aberrant vs Swedish norms but is a structural constraint on minority shareholder agency that international investors should weight explicitly

Founder control:
- Founding-era influence persists via Chair tenure, voting structure, and nomination committee composition; management has professionalised but is not fully independent of the founding shareholder base
- This is a governance feature, not a governance failure — but it does limit activist or external-pressure corrective mechanisms if management performance deteriorates

Voting rights:
- B-share investors have limited blocking power on resolutions; major shareholder coalition required for any major governance challenge to be successful
- AGM resolutions: no contested AGM votes visible in L5Y; no activist shareholder engagement publicly documented

Related-party transactions:
- Annual report disclosures: no unusual related-party transactions beyond standard director remuneration and arm's-length management fees within the group structure
- No transactions with Söderberg family interests beyond board-level compensation visible in public disclosures
- No related-party M&A (acquisitions from directors or major shareholders) visible in L5Y — positive signal

Audit history:
- Auditor: PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) has been the group auditor for multiple years; Big 4 presence, consistent — no auditor change concern
- No audit qualification or emphasis-of-matter paragraph on going concern or material uncertainty in L5Y annual reports
- No material weakness in internal controls disclosed
- No restatement of financial statements in L5Y

Legal/regulatory issues:
- No SEC investigations (Addnode is not SEC-registered; listed only in Sweden)
- No Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority (Finansinspektionen) enforcement actions visible in public record
- No class action litigation visible (Swedish legal system does not have US-style class action securities litigation; different risk profile)
- No material legal proceedings beyond ordinary commercial disputes disclosed in annual reports

Whistleblower / governance complaint signals:
- No whistleblower events or public governance complaints visible
- Addnode has a Code of Conduct and whistleblower policy (disclosed in annual reports); no violations or cases disclosed

Anti-takeover provisions:
- Dual-class structure is itself the primary anti-takeover mechanism; in Sweden, this is a structural feature not an added provision
- No poison pill or shareholder rights plan equivalent visible (not standard in Swedish corporate law)
- Swedish takeover rules (the Takeover Act and Nasdaq Stockholm Takeover Rules) apply; no special exemptions noted

Sceptical bullet — bearish read on governance:
- Risk: The clean governance track record is partly a function of the controlling shareholder alignment — when major shareholders and management are aligned (as they have been through a compounding period), governance problems rarely surface; the structural red flag is that if Addnode's performance deteriorates materially, the dual-class structure + nomination committee shareholder control would make it very difficult for minority B-share investors to force management change, strategic review, or value-maximisation sale; the governance structure is not designed to protect minority shareholders from a value-erosion scenario where the controlling shareholder group prefers continuity over shareholder returns
