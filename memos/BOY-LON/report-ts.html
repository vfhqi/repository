Section 3 – What the company does 20 findings

Section summary
  1. Bodycote commands immense pricing power through its highly localized network and safety-critical technical accreditations.
  2. The business model embeds the company deeply into customer workflows, creating exceptional barriers to entry and long-term customer lock-in.

Core Technologies and Segment Operations

1.
Differentiated segment structure: Operations are split into Specialist Technologies representing c.29.00% of revenue and Precision Heat Treatment representing c.63.00% of revenue
2.
Differentiated technologies mix: Specialist Technologies comprise highly differentiated, high-margin processes like Hot Isostatic Pressing, Speciality Stainless Steel Processes, and Surface Technology
3.
Precision heat treatment: Core thermal services involve controlled heating and cooling of metal components to achieve contractually specified mechanical and chemical properties
4.
Organic footprint upgrades: Management is commissioning a new automotive facility in Mexico and expanding S3P capacity into high-growth Asian markets
5.
Product lifecycle benefits: Thermal treatments extend product lifespan by up to 55.00%, offering substantial customer cost savings over the component lifecycle
6.
Outsourced emissions reduction: Bodycote's aggregated furnace operations deliver up to 60.00% lower emissions compared to customers running in-house thermal processing

Value Chain Moat and Switching Costs

7.
Stringent accreditation barriers: Aerospace and defense components require Nadcap certifications, which act as a massive barrier to entry for smaller competitors
8.
Dominant network scale: Bodycote operates c.50 Nadcap certified sites, which is more than double its nearest competitor in the United States
9.
High switching costs: Changing thermal processing suppliers requires customers to undergo complete component reaccreditation, incurring severe regulatory delays and costs
10.
Elite customer partnerships: The company holds preferred supplier status with major aerospace primes including Airbus, Boeing, Rolls-Royce, and GE Aerospace
11.
Spectrum acquisition advantage: The January 2026 acquisition of Spectrum Thermal Processing adds key Nadcap-accredited precision capacity in the dense Northeast US corridor
12.
Localized service dependency: Thermal processing is highly localized due to transportation logistics, meaning Bodycote's local plant density prevents competitive market share erosion

Customer Landscape and Market Consolidation

13.
Fragmented addressable market: The global thermal processing market is valued at £30.00bn, with c.80.00% currently processed in-house by OEMs
14.
Low customer concentration: No single customer contributes more than 10.00% of group revenue, protecting earnings from isolated customer losses
15.
Long term agreements: Significant portions of the high-margin aerospace and defense business are covered by long-term agreements, stabilizing forward revenues
16.
Aggressive market consolidation: Growth is driven primarily by bolt-on acquisitions of independent local shops, as building new facilities remains economically unviable
17.
In-house outsourcing trend: Customers are increasingly closing down their expensive, energy-intensive in-house ovens and outsourcing work to Bodycote's efficient network
18.
Other side of the story: Large industrial and automotive OEMs can choose to in-source their thermal processing during deep volume downturns to absorb fixed costs
