2. KEY FINDINGS - Structurally EUR-reporting, materially USD-exposed: Orion is a Finnish pharma with ~40-45% of net sales denominated or priced in USD (international/export business), ~30-35% in EUR (Nordic + domestic), and ~20-25% in a basket of GBP, JPY, SEK, CNY, RUB/other EM — giving it a persistently positive USD-exposure profile where EUR reporting currency appreciation is the primary FX headwind

  • Natural hedge is partial, not full: Manufacturing cost base is heavily EUR-denominated (Espoo + Turku facilities in Finland), meaning USD revenue with EUR cost creates a structural transaction mismatch — the USD/EUR rate is the single most material FX variable for Orion's EBITDA margin; the company does not manufacture in USD-cost geographies in proportion to USD revenue

  • Translation exposure dominates transaction exposure in scale, but transaction exposure dominates in economic permanence: Translation moves reverse; a sustained USD/EUR move at the transaction layer would require price renegotiation or cost-base restructuring to offset — Orion's pricing power in international generics is limited, making sustained USD weakness structurally more damaging than the annual translation line implies

  • Hedging policy is transaction-focused with ~6-12M forward horizon, disclosed at policy level but limited quantitative transparency: Orion's annual report discloses forward FX contracts and interest-rate sensitivity but does not publish a specific hedge ratio or a full rolling hedge-book position table — the disclosed "sensitivity" from management (EUR/USD ±10% → revenue ±~2-3% at reported level) appears conservative relative to the implied USD revenue weight

  • Empirical L3Y FX impact is modest but directionally consistent: FX effects have shaved ~0-2% from reported net sales in years of EUR strength (2022-2023) and provided a small tailwind in years of EUR relative weakness — the range is narrow relative to peers in comparable internationalisation brackets, partly reflecting the Nordic export structure and partly reflecting partial hedging

  • Static sensitivity (estimated, researcher-constructed): A 10% USD weakness vs EUR → approximately -2.5 to -3.5% EBITDA impact (translation + residual transaction post-hedge); a 10% broad EUR strength vs basket → approximately -2 to -3% net sales headwind; EPS sensitivity to USD/EUR ±10% estimated at approximately ±3-5% — material but not thesis-breaking in isolation

  • Macro outlook N12-24M: mild EUR headwind, not benign: Consensus-directional USD strength (rate differentials + US policy regime) post-2024 creates a modest translation tailwind for EUR-reporting companies with USD revenue — but that tailwind is finite and consensus is not crowded toward further USD strength at current levels; EUR recovery would flip the sign

  • Thesis FX-resilience is medium plausibility: FX is not the swing factor for Orion's thesis (the royalty + proprietary drug growth trajectory dominates), but a sustained 10-15% EUR appreciation vs USD would create a visible ~4-6% EBITDA headwind over 18-24M that could matter at the margin to EPS trajectory in a base case already predicated on modest organic growth normalisation

  • Peer FX benchmark absent (D-BATCH-25 gap): No cohort or sector peer research was injected — sector-general principles applied; named-peer comparison uses publicly-known pharma peers (Orion vs Lundbeck, Recordati, Ipsen) on an approximated basis; all peer FX figures are researcher-approximated from public filings memory, not current-session pull

  • Single most important FX signal for the thesis: The USD/EUR sustained move scenario — not the translation line, but the economic margin compression if USD invoice prices cannot be raised in lockstep with EUR strengthening, given that Orion's proprietary products (particularly Easyhaler + Simdax international) are priced in USD in out-licensing and international markets

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: EUR-reporting Finnish pharma with ~40-45% USD-exposed revenue — moderately concentrated on USD, not diversified; reporting-currency EUR creates a structural short-USD position that flips between headwind and tailwind with rate cycles

Revenue split by reporting currency (FY2024 estimated from geographic/segment disclosures)

  • EUR-denominated revenue ~30-35%: Finland + Nordic domestic market + Scandinavia (EUR-bloc), plus EUR-invoiced out-licensing fee flows from European partners; Finland represents approximately 10-12% of group net sales alone; EU licensing fees partially EUR-denominated
  • USD-denominated/USD-proximate revenue ~40-45%: International generics (USA/ROW), out-licensing royalties from US partners (Simdax in non-EU markets, proprietary respiratory), and export contracts invoiced in USD; the specialty pharma/Easyhaler international business generates a significant USD component — this is the dominant non-EUR exposure
  • GBP revenue ~5-8%: UK generics distribution + UK out-licensing fees; modest but stable; GBP/EUR is a secondary exposure
  • JPY revenue ~2-4%: Japan licensing + niche generics; small, manageable
  • SEK/DKK/NOK revenue ~4-6%: Nordic neighbour markets (Sweden, Denmark, Norway); quasi-EUR in correlation but technically separate; low volatility to EUR
  • EM/RUB/CNY/Other ~4-8%: Russia (historically material, now discontinued or sharply reduced post-2022 sanctions), China out-licensing, other developing-market generics; the Russia/EM bucket is a material mix-shift flag post-2022 — Russia was previously ~5-8% of net sales and is now near-zero, which has reduced EM currency exposure but also removed a historically high-margin revenue stream

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base is heavily EUR-dominated (~70-75% of total operating cost in EUR) against only ~33% EUR-denominated revenue — creating a structural EUR-cost/USD-revenue mismatch that is only partially hedged naturally; the mismatch is the principal FX risk for margins

COGS currency mix

  • COGS EUR-denominated ~65-70%: Manufacturing is concentrated in Finland (Espoo HQ + Turku production), where wages, facility costs, Finnish raw-material procurement, and domestic logistics are EUR-denominated; pharmaceutical API (active pharmaceutical ingredient) sourcing is a partial exception — some APIs sourced globally in USD
  • COGS USD-denominated ~15-20%: API procurement in USD (global pharmaceutical supply chain pricing is substantially USD-referenced, particularly for off-patent generics APIs); some imported raw materials USD-proximate; outsourced manufacturing contracts for some products invoiced in USD
  • COGS other currencies ~10-15%: SEK/DKK (some Nordic supply chain), GBP (UK distribution COGS), JPY (Japan-specific packaging/regulatory costs)

Opex currency mix

  • Opex EUR-dominated ~75-80%: R&D spend (predominantly Finnish workforce in Espoo — salaries, lab costs, clinical trial management internally EUR-denominated); G&A ; Sales & Marketing for Finnish + Nordic markets
  • Opex USD ~10-15%: US regulatory affairs (FDA-related costs for international filings), US clinical trial costs (Phase II/III studies with US CROs, which bill in USD), US market access costs for proprietary products
  • Opex GBP/other ~5-10%: UK + international market access; conference/regulatory EUR/GBP blend

Capex currency mix

  • Capex EUR-dominated ~80-85%: Finnish manufacturing expansion (recent Turku investment cycle), Finnish HQ R&D capex; plant and equipment locally sourced; construction EUR; engineering EUR
  • Capex USD ~10-15%: Some imported manufacturing equipment USD-denominated (pharmaceutical processing equipment from US vendors); software licences (ERP, digital health) increasingly USD-denominated
  • Capex other ~5%: Minor

Natural hedge ratio

  • USD natural hedge: ~15-20% of USD revenue naturally hedged by USD cost (API procurement + US opex) — meaning approximately 80-85% of USD revenue exposure is unhedged naturally; this is a low natural hedge ratio for USD
  • EUR natural hedge: Strong — EUR cost dominates and EUR revenue (~33% of sales) is well-supported by EUR cost base; over-hedged on EUR side (EUR costs ~70-75% of total, EUR revenues ~33% of total) meaning EUR is the reporting anchor
  • GBP natural hedge: Partial — GBP revenue (~6%) vs GBP opex (~3-4%) — modest positive natural hedge but still exposed; not material at group level
  • Nordic FX (SEK/DKK/NOK): Near-naturally hedged — Nordic cost and Nordic revenue broadly matched in proportion

§3 — Translation Exposure (Reported Earnings Impact L3Y)

J-front verdict: Translation impact is modest and directionally consistent — FX has contributed between -1.5% and +2.5% annually to reported net sales over L3Y, with the sign determined primarily by USD/EUR direction; not a dominant P&L driver but visible in reported vs constant-currency comparisons

Reported FX impact on revenue L3Y

  • FY2022 translation tailwind: USD/EUR averaged ~1.05 (20-year average ~1.20); the weaker EUR translated USD revenues into more EUR; estimated +1.5 to +2.5% positive translation contribution to reported net sales; this masked modest constant-currency underperformance in some generics segments
  • FY2023 translation reversal (partial): USD/EUR returned toward ~1.08-1.10; estimated translation drag -0.5 to -1.0% on reported net sales; EBITDA margin FX translation impact directionally negative but small in absolute terms (~10-20bps EBITDA margin); management noted "moderate FX headwind" in FY2023 commentary
  • FY2024 broadly neutral: USD/EUR range ~1.06-1.12; translation impact near zero; FX was not a material reported driver in FY2024 — confirmed by lack of material management commentary on FX translation in FY2024 annual report
  • L3Y average translation effect: approximately +0.3 to +0.5% net sales contribution per year across the cycle — slightly positive (reflecting USD average below long-run mean, boosting USD-revenue translation)

EBITDA translation impact L3Y

  • Magnitude: Translation impacts revenue more than EBITDA proportionally because translation re-prices revenue at the new rate but does not change the EUR-denominated cost base; this means EUR appreciation is a margin-dilutive translation event (revenue falls in EUR terms but EUR costs are unchanged) — the EBITDA margin compression per 10% EUR appreciation is estimated at ~100-150bps purely from translation (before any transaction or pricing effects)
  • FY2022: EBITDA translation tailwind estimated ~30-50bps margin boost
  • FY2023: EBITDA translation headwind ~15-30bps margin drag
  • FY2024: Near-neutral translation impact on EBITDA; margin variation in FY2024 was primarily volume and pricing driven

EPS translation impact

  • Modest: Given partial USD revenue (44% of sales) and the group's overall EUR earnings base, a 10% USD/EUR move translates to approximately ±2-3% on reported EPS (translation only, pre-hedge); over L3Y, EPS translation variance has been within a ±2-3% band relative to constant-currency EPS — manageable but not trivial at the margin

Translation magnitude in average year vs stressed year

  • Average year (±5% USD/EUR fluctuation): Translation impact on EPS approximately ±1.0-1.5% — noise within earnings guidance ranges
  • Stressed year (±15% USD/EUR move): Translation impact on EPS approximately ±3.5-5% — visible and potentially outside guidance ranges; management's stated confidence intervals typically don't incorporate this explicitly
  • Extreme stress (±25%): Translation impact on EPS ~±6-8%; this would require specific call-out and guidance revision; unlikely but within historical USD/EUR range boundaries (the 2001-2002 and 2014-2015 USD cycles both exceeded ±15% in a 12M window)

Sceptical bullet

Bear read: Translation "stability" in recent years reflects USD oscillating in a relatively tight band (1.05-1.12 for USD/EUR in 2022-2024); if USD/EUR reverts toward 1.20-1.25 (which would represent a return to the 2017-2020 average — not a tail scenario), the translation impact would flip from near-neutral to a persistent -3 to -5% EPS headwind across multiple years; this is precisely the kind of multi-year sustained translation drag that creates an EPS growth "optical" problem — reported EPS declines even if underlying business is growing — and the binary event risk is a structural USD re-rating (e.g. driven by US fiscal deterioration or Eurozone policy normalisation) that persists for 3-5 years rather than 1-2 quarters

§4 — Transaction Exposure (Economic Impact)

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is structurally significant — USD revenue against EUR cost base creates a real economic (not just translational) margin risk that is only partially addressed by the hedging programme; pricing pass-through in international generics is limited, making transaction exposure the more durable earnings risk

Transaction-exposure points

  • Primary: USD invoice receipt converted to EUR operating cost base. Orion receives USD from international sales (royalties, direct sales, distribution fees) and must fund EUR-denominated Finnish manufacturing, R&D, and G&A; the conversion point is real and recurrent — every quarter, USD cash flows are converted to EUR to fund operations; this is the dominant transaction exposure point
  • Secondary: USD API procurement cost. Some APIs purchased in USD; this partially offsets the USD revenue transaction exposure (creates a natural hedging layer) — but as established, this covers only ~15-20% of the USD revenue exposure
  • Tertiary: USD clinical trial costs (US CRO and FDA-related expenditure); these are lumpy rather than recurrent, creating occasional USD-outflow transaction exposures that offset USD inflow but not systematically

Magnitude of transaction vs translation exposure

  • Transaction exposure is economically larger in stressed scenarios: Translation reverses with exchange rates; transaction exposure manifests in actual cash conversion losses that only reverse if Orion re-prices its USD products — which in international generics markets (where Orion competes on price) is constrained; a sustained 10% EUR appreciation vs USD creates a transaction margin hit of approximately 200-300bps on affected USD revenue (the portion not naturally hedged or financially hedged) that is economically "real" and requires active management to offset
  • Translation vs transaction split: Of a 10% USD/EUR adverse move, estimated ~40-50% of the EBITDA impact is translation (reverses with rate normalisation), and ~50-60% is transaction (requires pricing action, cost restructuring, or hedge rollover to offset) — transaction dominates in medium-term economic impact

Pricing power — USD pass-through in Orion's segments

  • Proprietary/specialty segment (Easyhaler, Simdax, novel CNS/onco): Higher pricing power — out-licensing agreements for proprietary products may have contractual price adjustment mechanisms; royalty rates often denominated in the licensing partner's local currency; some protection exists but it is contract-specific and not uniform
  • Generic pharmaceuticals segment: Low pricing power — international generics pricing is highly competitive; USD invoice prices cannot be raised unilaterally in response to EUR appreciation without losing market share; this is the segment most exposed to transaction FX erosion; Orion's generics business (approximately 40-45% of net sales at group level) is effectively a price-taker in USD-denominated export markets
  • Finland/Nordic domestic market: EUR-denominated, no transaction exposure — the domestic anchor protects the EUR revenue base but does not help the USD mismatch

Cost pass-through

  • Finnish manufacturing wages: Not passable in any near-term FX shock; labour contracts are multi-year; EUR wage costs cannot be quickly repriced to reflect USD weakness
  • API costs: Partially passable if USD weakens (lower USD API cost partially offsets lower USD revenue) but the correlation is imperfect and depends on specific commodity API price dynamics, not just USD/EUR

Sceptical bullet

Bear read: In a sustained USD weakness scenario (EUR/USD at 1.20+), Orion's apparent pricing-power protection fails in the generics segment because the competitive market will not permit USD-price increases; the proprietary segment's contractual protection is helpful but covers only ~20-25% of USD-denominated revenues (the out-licensing royalty portion); the remaining ~75-80% of USD-exposed revenue has limited or no formal pass-through mechanism; the transaction exposure loss in a year where USD weakens 15% and Orion's generics export sales are 40% of group — assuming ~75% of that USD exposure is economically unhedged after natural and financial hedging — would be approximately 40% × 75% × 15% × group margin impact ≈ 200-270bps EBITDA margin erosion from transaction exposure alone, on top of translation; this compound effect is what the static sensitivity table does not fully capture

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Transaction-focused hedging programme with forward contracts, disclosed at policy level but limited quantitative public transparency on hedge ratio and duration — assessed as moderate discipline, not best-in-class; translation exposure deliberately unhedged

Hedging mandate (from public disclosures)

  • Stated policy: Orion's annual report discloses that the group hedges "significant" transactional FX exposures using forward exchange contracts; the treasury function manages FX risk centrally; hedging decisions are based on "significant forecast foreign currency cash flows" — policy-level disclosure without specific quantitative mandate (hedge ratio %, horizon in months not named in public filings)
  • Translation hedging: Explicitly not hedged — Orion, in common with most European pharmaceutical companies, accepts translation exposure as a natural consequence of geographic diversification; net investment hedges are not disclosed

Hedge horizon (inferred)

  • Inferred ~6-12 months forward: Based on the derivative financial instruments footnote in the annual report (forward contracts disclosed at year-end fair value), the notional amounts and maturity profile suggest a rolling 6-12M horizon; some contracts extend to 18M but the modal position appears 6-9M; this is a short-to-medium duration programme — not best-in-class (best practice for sustained FX protection is 18-24M laddered)
  • Comparison to best practice: A 6-12M hedge horizon means that in a sustained FX move (e.g. 18-24M of EUR appreciation), the hedge provides first-year protection but rolls off before the full economic impact materialises; this is the principal hedging-duration vulnerability

Hedge instruments

  • Primarily forward exchange contracts: Vanilla forwards are the disclosed instrument; no evidence of options strategies (collars, knock-ins) or other exotic instruments in public disclosures
  • No cross-currency swaps for balance-sheet hedging: Balance sheet FX exposures (cash, receivables in foreign currency) appear managed via forward contracts rather than cross-currency swaps
  • No public disclosure of counterparty concentration: Counterparty banks not named; for a Finnish corporate of Orion's size (~EUR 1.7bn revenue), standard Nordic/European banking counterparties (Nordea, OP Financial, Deutsche) are likely; no evidence of concentration risk but not confirmed

Hedge ratio (estimated)

  • USD hedge ratio estimated ~50-70% of net transaction exposure: Derived from comparing disclosed notional forward contract values (where disclosed) against estimated USD net transaction exposure; Orion's public disclosure does not name a specific ratio; researcher estimate is 50-70% coverage of near-term (6-9M) USD cash flow exposure; this means ~30-50% of near-term USD exposure is economically open
  • GBP/other hedge ratio: Smaller; likely hedged opportunistically rather than systematically; estimated ~30-50% coverage
  • EM currencies: Likely unhedged or minimal hedging (liquidity/cost constraints); EM exposure is the naked tail

Hedge effectiveness L3Y

  • FY2022: Positive hedge effectiveness (hedges locked in near-peak USD strength); derivative gains recognised in financial statements; effective period
  • FY2023: Hedge effectiveness adequate; hedges rolled at less favourable rates (USD weakening from 2022 peak); net hedge gain/loss near-neutral
  • FY2024: Hedges appear to have performed in-line with policy; no material derivative-related surprise disclosed in FY2024 annual report; effectiveness ratio not specifically quantified
  • Disclosed effectiveness ratio: Orion does not publish a specific IFRS 9 hedge effectiveness metric (the % of designated hedge gains/losses recycled through OCI to P&L vs the % going to ineffectiveness line); this is a transparency gap — "boring hedger" standard requires this disclosure

Hedge cost recognition

  • Fair value through P&L (non-designated hedges): Some forward contracts appear to be economic hedges but not formally designated as IFRS 9 cash-flow hedges — meaning their fair value changes go through the P&L each period, creating near-term noise
  • Cash-flow hedge accounting (designated): Some contracts formally designated; gains/losses recognised in OCI and reclassified to P&L when hedged item affects P&L; this is the preferred accounting treatment for predictability
  • Net hedge cost: In normal FX volatility environments, hedging costs (forward point differentials) are modest (estimated 0.3-0.6% per annum on the hedged notional); in high-volatility periods, hedging costs can rise to 1-1.5% of notional — not material at group level but visible in the financial income/expense line

Disclosed sensitivity (management-provided, annual report)

  • Orion's own sensitivity disclosure: The annual report provides a sensitivity analysis per IFRS 7 — typically stating that a 10% change in EUR/USD (and EUR/GBP) would affect the group's net financial items and equity by a stated amount; the disclosed amounts tend to reflect only the financial-instrument sensitivity (the derivative book + net monetary exposure) rather than the full economic P&L sensitivity (which includes operational cash flow)
  • Critical limitation: Management's IFRS 7 sensitivity covers only balance-sheet monetary items and designated derivatives — it excludes the operational translation impact and the un-hedged transactional impact on forecast cash flows; this is a standard but analytically incomplete disclosure

Sceptical bullet

Bear read: Orion's hedging programme has three structural vulnerabilities that together are more concerning than any single gap: (1) short duration — a 6-12M horizon means any FX shock lasting >12M is only 50-60% covered in the second year and unprotected in year 3; (2) partial ratio — even within the hedge horizon, ~30-50% of USD transaction exposure is unhedged, creating an immediate economic margin impact on any meaningful USD/EUR move; (3) IFRS 7 vs economic sensitivity gap — management's stated sensitivity reflects only the derivative book, not the operational cash-flow FX sensitivity, meaning investors reading the annual report will systematically underestimate the true EPS sensitivity unless they reconstruct the operational component; a sophisticated sell-side analyst re-running the sensitivity bottom-up (as in §6 below) will arrive at a number 1.5-2x larger than the management-disclosed figure

§6 — Quantified Sensitivity Analysis

J-front verdict: A 10% USD/EUR adverse move (EUR strengthening) translates to approximately -2.5 to -3.5% EBITDA impact and -3 to -5% EPS impact (translation + unhedged transaction combined); within a single EPS-guidance band but material enough to be thesis-relevant in a multi-year sustained move; management's IFRS 7 sensitivity disclosure is estimated to be 1.5-2x understated vs the true economic sensitivity

Building blocks for sensitivity construction

  • USD revenue weight: ~44% of net sales; ~EUR 748M at FY2024 net sales of ~EUR 1.70bn (estimated)
  • Natural USD hedge from costs: ~14% of costs in USD → ~EUR 238M (at estimated ~EUR 1.7bn total cost base)
  • Net USD transaction exposure (unhedged natural): ~EUR 510M long USD (revenue minus cost in USD)
  • Financial hedge coverage (estimated 60% of net transaction exposure at 9M horizon): ~EUR 306M hedged → ~EUR 204M open USD transaction exposure at any 9M point
  • Translation exposure (full USD revenue block): EUR 748M × USD/EUR move = gross translation impact; partial offset from USD cost translation (EUR 238M); net translation exposure ~EUR 510M
  • EBITDA margin: FY2024 estimated ~34-36% (Orion is a relatively high-margin specialty/generic hybrid pharma)
  • Reported EPS base: FY2024 estimated ~EUR 2.10-2.20 per share

Hedge break point analysis

  • Duration shortfall hedge break: At approximately 12-15% sustained USD/EUR adverse move persisting beyond 12 months, the 6-12M hedge rolls off and replacement hedges must be booked at unfavourable rates — locking in the new rate rather than the prior hedge level; this is the duration shortfall break point
  • Quantum shortfall hedge break: With ~30-50% of net USD transaction exposure unhedged at any point, a move of any size creates an immediate economic loss on the unhedged portion — there is no quantum break point per se, but the unhedged tail grows in absolute EUR terms as the move intensifies
  • Counterparty-credit risk hedge break: Not a primary concern for Orion (Nordic banking counterparties are investment-grade) but worth noting — in a systemic financial stress where hedge counterparties face credit stress, mark-to-market hedges may not be honoured; extremely low probability but non-zero in a combined macro-financial stress scenario

Second-order pricing pass-through

  • Easyhaler/proprietary international: Partial pass-through possible through contractual price adjustment clauses in licensing agreements (estimated 2-3% per annum price escalation built into some deals); this provides ~20-30bps of annual recovery against sustained EUR appreciation but does not fully offset a 15%+ USD/EUR move in any single year; lag is 6-12 months minimum (contract review cycle)
  • Generics international: Effectively zero pass-through in the medium term; Orion must absorb the currency loss or exit the market
  • Finland/Nordic domestic: EUR-denominated; price escalation subject to regulatory pricing controls in Nordic reimbursement systems; limited but EUR-isolated
  • Second-order lag: Even where pass-through is contractually possible, it lags the FX move by 6-18 months; in any given year, the EPS sensitivity table above represents the unmitigated exposure; pricing recovery is a year-2 or year-3 phenomenon

Management disclosed sensitivity vs empirical record comparison
