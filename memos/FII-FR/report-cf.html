EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

LISI SA has strengthened operational leadership through Benoît Heubert's appointment as Chief Operating Officer to drive manufacturing performance across both industrial divisions, while governance remains highly stable with family control dominating 58.33% of board seats alongside employee-level transition initiatives. The company implemented a strategic refocusing on core businesses by divesting its medical operations in late 2025, selling 100% of its Medical division to private equity firm SK Capital in a transaction that officially closed on October 31, 2025, generating net cash proceeds of €270.4 million. The aerospace division demonstrates exceptionally strong demand momentum, with civil aerospace market conditions highly favorable across all major commercial platforms, driving LISI Aerospace sales to grow organically by 17.6% in Q1 2026 and achieving 18.3% organic sales growth for the full year 2025. 2 The group delivered robust financial performance with sales reaching €1,747.9 million in 2025 representing 11.5% organic growth, consolidated operating margin reaching 9.2% in 2025 representing a 310 basis point expansion, net financial debt dropping to €233.7 million from €488.5 million, and net leverage ratio falling sharply to 0.91x EBITDA in 2025 from 2.45x. Sell-side analysts have demonstrated substantial confidence in the company's trajectory, with Deutsche Bank highlighting LISI as a top upward reviser in European Aerospace and Defense since

FY27 EPS revisions of 42%. Management operates as an honest-tracker archetype, utilizing highly cautious qualitative statements and consistently outperforming these baselines, having achieved EBIT improvement objectives for four consecutive years from FY2022 to FY2025 and successfully met stated goals for positive cash generation in both divisions. However, significant headwinds persist in the automotive segment, with global automotive production contracting by -1.4% in the first quarter of 2026, LISI Automotive sales declining by -3.5% reported to €143.0 million in Q1 2026, and continued volume pressure across multiple reporting periods. Critical supply chain risks include Pratt & Whitney's engine delivery constraints negatively impacting Airbus's short-term delivery ramp-up, while Airbus production gliders could quickly convert LISI Aerospace's component deliveries into massive inventory builds. Foreign exchange volatility represents a persistent drag on reported performance, with the weakening of the average US dollar rate generating a -€24.0 million revenue headwind in Q1 2026, the aerospace sector featuring structural transaction exposure due to pricing primarily in US dollars, and LISI recording -€57.4 million in foreign exchange losses for fiscal year 2025. 6

The forward investment thesis hinges on five high-impact drivers with varying degrees of execution risk and external dependency, requiring close monitoring of operational tracking metrics to validate the honest-tracker management archetype and sustain the strong peer-relative earnings momentum demonstrated across recent reporting periods.
