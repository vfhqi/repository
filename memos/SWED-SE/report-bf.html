EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Banking Model and Geographic Footprint

Swedbank operates as a universal bank serving over seven million private customers, with Sweden representing 71% of total group income and the Baltic region contributing 25%. The revenue model concentrates heavily on net interest income derived from lending, with private mortgages comprising 57% of total loans. Fee-based income streams are anchored by Swedbank Robur, the largest fund manager in Sweden and the Baltics managing SEK 2.6 trillion, contributing approximately SEK 2 billion quarterly. Swedbank maintains exceptionally high rate sensitivity because of its massive share of low-interest current deposits, creating structural exposure to central bank policy shifts. Baltic Banking operations secure leading market shares in retail lending and deposits across Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania, providing a high-growth complement to the mature Swedish market. 1

Peer-Relative Financial Profile and Profitability

Industry Structure and Value Chain Position

Sweden's financial landscape is highly oligopolistic, with Swedbank, Svenska Handelsbanken, and SEB collectively controlling 51.2% of all Swedish lending in 2025. Baltic banking sectors exhibit significantly steeper concentration, with the four largest banks accounting for nearly 90% of newly issued residential mortgage lending in Latvia. Lending margins on Swedish residential mortgages have reached historic lows because of intense competition, with standalone mortgage products described as unprofitable by peers. Capital-light business lines offset margin pressure, with Swedbank Robur holding a dominant 21% fund market share in Sweden and Baltic domestic payments increasing 8% year-on-year. Swedbank manages upstream integration by routing Swedish residential mortgages through Swedbank Mortgage AB, funding approximately 59% of group loans via AAA-rated covered bonds backed by SEK 1,115.5 billion of assets. Downstream, the bank captures exceptional pricing power on its unhedged deposit funding base, successfully maintaining a 0% interest rate on substantial transaction and Easy-Savers accounts.

Competitive Advantages and Strategic Moat

Swedbank leads the Swedish retail deposit market with an 18% private deposit share and commands a dominant 45% private deposit market share in Estonia. A unique co-dependent relationship with 55 independent Swedish savings banks provides unmatched geographic distribution, with partners sharing IT development costs and administrative systems. Latent pricing leverage stems from a massive transaction deposit base paying 0% interest, with every 50 basis point rate increase translating to a SEK 2.9 billion net interest income benefit. Accelerated digital migration reduces physical branch costs, with more than 99% of total customer interactions now managed via mobile apps and internet banking. Operational AI deployment enables automated call summarization, helping call centers answer over 80% of calls within three minutes compared to historic 20+ minute wait times. Regulatory capital barriers protect the franchise, with Swedbank maintaining a 17.5% CET1 ratio providing a large credit cushion against macroeconomic uncertainties.

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution

President Jens Henriksson has led the group since 2019, shifting focus from post-crisis cleanup to a customerfirst growth model with deep public and private sector expertise. On March 1, 2026, the group reorganized to shift business development closer to customers, transferring product responsibilities from central units directly into individual business segments. In January 2026, the US Department of Justice closed its AML investigation without enforcement, and the US Securities and Exchange Commission concluded its disclosure probe in September 2025 without action.
