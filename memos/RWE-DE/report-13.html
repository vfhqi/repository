§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: RWE is best characterised as an honest tracker with cyclical over-optimism: guidance is set at realistic midpoints in normal conditions but consistently fails to adequately stress-test power price and project timing downside, producing lumpy misses in adverse years [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: Absent — RWE does not systematically guide below achievable levels; the EUR 0.6-1.0bn guidance range is wide but centred at management's genuine central expectation, not a low anchor [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal: Partial — in favourable macro conditions (FY2021), guidance proves conservative by accident (commodity tailwind); in adverse conditions (FY2023), guidance proves aggressive — management does not adequately discount downside scenarios when setting range midpoints [#A]

  • Honest tracker: Closer to this label for EBIT and EBITDA; guidance midpoints are reasonable central estimates but lack the conservatism cushion characteristic of true sandbagging utilities (e.g. National Grid historically guides 5-8% below outturn; RWE guides 0-3% below in benign years) [#A]

  • Beat consistency: In commodity-normalised years (FY2021, FY2024), RWE delivers within or slightly above EBIT guidance — this is a positive signal for normal-conditions performance; the problem is adverse-condition resilience [#A]

  • Miss consistency: ❌ FCF misses in 3 of 4 years — this is the most consistent miss pattern in the dataset; driven by systematic CapEx overshoot and limited FCF guidance anchor [#A]

  • Pattern shift: FY2024 CapEx delivery in-range for the first time in 3 years — potentially signals improved project cost discipline post-inflation peak; too early to call structural [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Normal-conditions beats average ~5-10% above EBIT guidance midpoint (FY2021, FY2024 upper half if achieved) [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Adverse-condition misses can be severe: FY2023 EBIT -11 to -18%, EPS -28 to -36% — asymmetric downside that is disproportionate to upside beats in benign years [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: FY2024 represents an accuracy improvement vs FY2023 — EBIT landed in-range vs prior year's below-range delivery; the question is whether this is structural or simply reflected the absence of an exogenous shock [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the pattern is that apparent honesty-tracking reflects high macro sensitivity rather than calibrated guidance discipline; RWE can only achieve guidance accuracy when power prices are stable — a condition that is not guaranteed and is arguably deteriorating in the current low-price German power environment
