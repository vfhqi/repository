Section 3 – What the company does 21 findings

Section summary
  1. Orange Polska occupies a dominant infrastructure position in the Polish telecom market, leveraging its extensive fibre network to drive profitable retail and wholesale convergence.
  2. The company utilizes strong competitive advantages in high-speed connectivity to expand retail average revenues and build durable customer relationships.

Core Products and Segment Split

1.
Integrated consumer offerings: The company offers convergent B2C bundles combining high-speed fibre broadband, television, and mobile post-paid voice services under a single household account
2.
Broadband technology transition: Orange Polska has successfully transitioned from a legacy copper-based operator into a high-speed fibre provider with 1.70 million fibre customers
3.
Valuable television assets: IPTV has become the leading television technology, replacing legacy satellite TV and driving convergent package uptake
4.
Corporate market solutions: The business segment delivers complex integration, cloud, software engineering, and cybersecurity services to large enterprises and small businesses
5.
Broad wholesale engine: Wholesale operations have emerged as a massive commercial success, monetizing core infrastructure assets through sales to competing Polish operators
6.
Low customer concentration: No individual retail customer or corporate account represents a material portion of revenues, maintaining a diversified revenue base

Revenue Generation and Mix

7.
Predictable revenue streams: Subscription-based retail services generate highly recurring, contractual revenues that insulate the business from short-term transactional volatility
8.
More-for-more upsell: Value-based pricing encourages customers to migrate to higher-speed fibre plans, raising average monthly revenues per offer
9.
Long-term wholesale contracts: Fibre backhaul contracts signed in the second half of 2025 secure predictable, multi-year wholesale revenues
10.
Equipment sales contribution: Smartphone and hardware sales provide transactional revenue that supports retail customer acquisition and value upselling
11.
ICT contract pipelines: Large enterprise B2B revenue is project-based, relying on integration contract pipelines and software license resales
12.
Dwindling legacy lines: Traditional PSTN voice and legacy copper broadband continue to decline structurally, though high-margin fibre growth more than offsets this headwind

Industry Structure and Positioning

13.
Highly fragmented market: The Polish telecommunications market remains highly fragmented, elevating the value of Orange's large infrastructure assets
14.
Fierce infrastructure competition: Orange Polska faces key infrastructure competition in mobile and fixed segments from Cyfrowy Polsat, T-Mobile, and Play
15.
Unmatched network reach: Orange Polska holds a major competitive advantage through its fibre network which reaches nearly 10.00 million connectable households
16.
High switching barriers: Fixed-mobile convergence creates significant switching costs, reducing retail churn and securing a stable customer base
17.
Valuable spectrum portfolio: The company owns critical national mobile licenses across the 700 MHz to 3600 MHz bands to support its expanding 5G network
18.
Co-controlled infrastructure partnerships: The co-controlled joint venture Światłowód Inwestycje actively commercializes open fibre networks, sharing capital expenditure burdens with APG
19.
Other side of the story: A major risk is that competitors bypass Orange's wholesale infrastructure by co-investing in alternative open networks, which would dilute wholesale revenues and erode wholesale pricing power

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is well-managed and bounded by low financial leverage, though competitive pricing pressure and capital expenditure inflation require ongoing monitoring.

Operating Inflation Risks

1.
Wage and labor inflation: Heavy wage inflation in Poland forces the company into recurring headcount optimization programs that trigger near-term restructuring provisions
2.
Capital expenditure pressure: Severe inflation in construction or telecom equipment prices could push annual economic capex above the targeted PLN 1.80 billion ceiling
3.
Energy price volatility: Energy price exposure is a persistent threat to margins, though the company hedges up to 86% of its energy consumption through long-term power purchase agreements
4.
Derivative hedging sensitivity: Wind farm derivative contracts used to hedge energy risk carry non-observable valuation sensitivities that fluctuate based on long-term electricity price forecasts
5.
Retail pricing wars: Aggressive, irrational promotions from mobile-only or fixed operators could force Orange to lower its core consumer tariffs
6.
High B2B price sensitivity: B2B mobile and ICT markets grow slower than consumer segments and are subject to intense, margin-diluting competitor pricing

Market and Competitor Threats

7.
No active short reports: There are no published active short-seller campaigns or elevated short interest reported on Orange Polska shares
8.
Regulatory frequency obligations: Regulatory delay in spectrum allocation, such as the C-band or 700 MHz auctions, historically increased uncertainty and delayed rollout
9.
Wholesale customer migration: A shift of wholesale volumes by major operators to competing networks represents a major risk to infrastructure margins
10.
Joint venture financing obligations: Collateral commitments for the Światłowód Inwestycje joint venture include share pledges of up to PLN 6.60 billion to secure long-term loan agreements
11.
Underlying competitive resilience: The company has managed competitor pricing attacks effectively, maintaining steady retail subscriber additions through a multi-brand strategy
12.
Early warning indicator: The key early-warning signal to monitor is B2C churn, which would indicate deteriorating market sentiment
13.
Other side of the story: A severe, prolonged economic downturn in Poland would reduce corporate ICT spending and force consumer downgrades, fracturing the premium value-driven average revenue per user trajectory
