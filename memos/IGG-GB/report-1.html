Company Overview and Strategic Vision
Corporate History and Mission
IG Group Holdings plc is a global financial technology company that operates at the rare intersection of retail trading, technology, and capital markets. Established in 1974 as the world's first spread betting firm, the
company has evolved over five decades into a global leader in online trading and investments. The
organisation's core purpose is to power the pursuit of financial freedom for the ambitious. Reflecting its
sustained growth and market capitalisation, IG Group is scheduled to enter the prestigious FTSE 100 index in
March 2026. 1 4 • IRPRESS 2 • Company Publication 3 • ARS
Business Model and Operating Structure
The company's business model is highly cash-generative and relies on sophisticated market-making and
brokerage services. In its core over-the-counter derivatives business, IG differentiates itself from
competitors by heavily internalising market risk. The platform acts as the direct counterparty to customer
trades, centralising global dealing activity and offsetting opposing positions to determine a net real-time
exposure. External hedging is only deployed passively when residual market risk breaches strict Board-
approved tolerance limits. 5 • Company Publication 6 7 8 • ARS
To foster agility and customer-centricity, IG implemented a decentralised organisational structure in early 2024. This transformation divided the business into five geographically aligned divisions, giving regional leaders clear
ownership of their respective profit and loss statements. This structure ensures that strategic decisions are
tailored to local customer needs and can be implemented at a rapid pace. Management notes that this
decentralised approach has materially accelerated product delivery velocity and sharpened the company's
commercial focus across all regions. 9 11 • ARS 10 • ARS
Target Addressable Market The company is positioning itself to capture a larger share of a massive Target Addressable Market that
exceeds £130 billion across its existing product categories. This TAM expansion is underpinned by long-term
structural tailwinds, including a generational wealth transfer, pension freedom, and the convergence of trading
and gaming-adjacent experiences. 12 13 • Investor Presentation
In the OTC derivatives space, IG estimates the global TAM at over £8 billion, where it currently holds a penetration
rate of less than 9%. The addressable markets for stock trading and investments (£62 billion), cryptocurrency

less than 1% of the revenue pool in each of these three rapidly expanding segments. 14 15
