B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What Auto1 does. Auto1 Group SE operates Europe's largest vertically integrated digital used-car transaction platform, sourcing vehicles directly from private consumers (Merchant B2B wholesale) and reselling both wholesale to dealers and retail to consumers under the Autohero brand
2.
Primary markets. The business splits into two segments: Merchant (B2B wholesale auctions to 54,371 partner dealers) and Retail/Autohero (B2C online used-car sales with integrated logistics, reconditioning and captive financing)
3.
Geography and scale. Auto1 sources from private sellers via 725 physical drop-off branches (759 by Q1 2026) across 9 European countries and wholesales to dealers across 33 European nations, transacting 842,271 units in FY25 for a 3.1% European market share of a EUR 600-700bn, 27.5 million-transaction pool
4.
Financial scale. FY25 total revenue surged 30.3% year-on-year to EUR 8.17bn (from EUR 6.27bn in FY24); Q1 2026 revenue reached an all-time record EUR 2.44bn, +25.4% YoY, beating consensus by 4-6%
5.
Profitability trajectory. FY25 Adjusted EBITDA expanded 80.8% year-on-year to EUR 197.5m (2.4% margin, up 70bps YoY); Q1 2026 Adjusted EBITDA reached EUR 59.8m, the best post-IPO quarterly print
6.
Balance sheet. Zero corporate debt, EUR 652m cash, with inventory and receivables funded via non-recourse asset-backed securitisation (ABS) facilities scaled up to EUR 1.6bn; non-recourse ABS liabilities reached EUR 1.71bn in Q1 2026
7.
Capital intensity. Capex is guided at just 0.25% of revenue (EUR 2.8m in Q1 2026, down 59.8% YoY), materially lower than Inchcape's sub-1% capex guidance, reflecting an asset-light-adjacent sourcing model despite the physical branch network
8.
Moat mechanism. Approximately 90% of inventory is sourced direct from private consumers via the branch network, an infrastructure Deutsche Bank calls an "unreplicable sourcing moat"; Morgan Stanley estimates up to EUR 500m in working-capital investment would be needed to replicate at just 1% market share
9.
Leadership context. Founder-CEO Christian Bertermann was reappointed for a five-year term to December 2030 at the June 2026 AGM; new CFO Christian Wallentin (ex-Hoist Finance, Permira, Goldman Sachs) started January 2026, succeeding a ten-year incumbent who executed the 2021 IPO
