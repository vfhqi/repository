§5A — Historical Bear Events [+C]

Judgement: AutoStore's listed history (Oct 2021 to May 2026) includes three major bear catalyst events: the 2022-2024 demand trough, the Ocado patent loss and settlement, and the Q1 2025 revenue air-pocket; the IPO itself was launched at structurally bear-friendly conditions (thin float, PE overhang, high valuation). [#J]

  • IPO (October 2021) — the original bull-to-bear pivot: AutoStore IPO'd at NOK 31/share, implying a market cap of ~NOK 110bn (~$12.4bn) — the largest Nordic tech IPO ever; institutional allocation was 17.4% float with 82%+ remaining with PE; lockup-free float was insufficient for meaningful price discovery. THL sold 429.7m shares in the IPO, reducing its stake but retaining ~36%; the IPO proceeds went to selling shareholders, not the company — a classic PE exit vehicle structure. Within months, rising interest rates and post-pandemic e-commerce deceleration created a dual headwind; the stock entered a multi-year decline from ~NOK 31 to a trough around NOK 4-5 (~85% peak-to-trough destruction). [#I]

  • Ocado patent dispute (2020-2023): AutoStore initiated the dispute in October 2020, claiming Ocado infringed six of its patents — framed as AutoStore defending its IP position. March 2023: UK High Court Judge Hacon ruled two AutoStore patents invalid and not infringed by Ocado (AutoStore had already withdrawn or seen four other patents invalidated). Bears argued this ruling exposed the weakness of AutoStore's IP position — it had initiated the dispute from a position of claimed strength and lost decisively on the merits. July 2023: Settlement announced — AutoStore to pay Ocado GBP 200m ($257m) in 24 monthly instalments. Market reaction: Ocado +12%, AutoStore -7.7%. Bear reading: AutoStore paid GBP 200m to end a dispute it had started, implying its offensive IP position was weaker than the IPO prospectus suggested; the cross-licence gives Ocado access to pre-2020 patents. [#A]

  • 2022-2024 demand trough: Following the post-pandemic boom (revenue CAGR ~80% in 2021-2022), warehouse CapEx decisions decelerated sharply as interest rates rose. Q2 2024: Revenue -12% YoY; Order Intake -23% QoQ; stock -15% post-print. Full-year 2024 revenue: consensus cut by ~$40m post-Q2 miss. Multiple quarters of downward revisions eroded institutional confidence in management's visibility claims. [#A]

  • Q1 2025 revenue air-pocket: Revenue of $85.9m (-38% YoY); management blamed AaaS model adoption; Deutsche Bank cut price target 50% to NOK 5.70. This was the peak of AaaS-opacity bear concern — the market could not determine whether the miss was model-transition (recoverable) or demand destruction (structural). [#A]

  • Finanstilsynet review (April 2025): Norwegian financial regulator's formal criticism of Order Intake/Backlog disclosure practices — published publicly and amplified in Norwegian financial press (Finansavisen headline: "Finanstilsynet kritiserer AutoStore"). First Finanstilsynet enforcement action on AutoStore since its IPO. [#A]

  • B1 robot write-down (Q2 2025): $8.5m inventory charge reducing gross margin to 68.8% from underlying 75.2%; product line termination reinforced execution-risk narrative. Positive framing: strategic focus on higher-margin R5 Pro. [#I]
