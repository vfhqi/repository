Investor AB

Company Overview & Mandate: Investor AB, founded by the Wallenberg family in 1916, is a Swedish industrial holding company that aims to create value for people and society by building strong and sustainable businesses. It operates as an engaged, long-term owner with a business model centered on significant board participation and driving value-creating initiatives. Its overarching strategic pillars focus on "Performance, Portfolio, and People" to ensure sustainable profitable growth. The firm's ultimate financial target is generating an attractive total return, targeting a long-term return requirement equal to the risk-free interest rate plus an equity risk premium of 8-9 percent annually.

Portfolio Structure: The company manages its portfolio through three distinct business areas: Listed Companies, Patricia Industries, and Investments in EQT. Listed Companies represent approximately 70 percent of total assets and include major multinational holdings such as ABB, AstraZeneca, Atlas Copco, Ericsson, and SEB. Patricia Industries, comprising roughly 20 percent of assets, develops wholly-owned private companies in the Nordics and North America, such as Mölnlycke, Permobil, and Advanced Instruments. The remaining 10 percent of the portfolio is allocated to Investments in EQT, which includes both ownership in the EQT AB management company and minority investments in underlying EQT funds.

The core business concept revolves around investing in sustainable companies that possess proprietary products, operate in niches supported by global megatrends, and demonstrate excellent prospects for internationalization. As an active owner, the firm employs a highly decentralized decision-making structure where subsidiaries operate with significant autonomy, supported by business-centric boards and a lean central headquarters of only 16 employees. This long-term commitment allows management to navigate economic cycles smoothly while heavily investing in product development, digitalization, and sustainability.

Industrivarden

Company Overview & Mandate: Founded in 1944 by Handelsbanken, Industrivarden is a leading listed holding company that works long-term to develop and create shareholder value. Its core strategy is to exercise active ownership by taking sizable equity stakes in a concentrated selection of listed Nordic companies with proven business models and clear development potential. The firm actively participates in corporate governance through board and nomination committee representation, advocating for decentralized leadership, operational flexibility, and financial strength. The overarching objective is to generate an attractive long-term return at a balanced risk profile by fostering sustainable business practices within its portfolio.

Degree of Change Rating

Rating: GreenExplanation: Investor AB, Latour, and Industrivarden operate as legacy Swedish investment holding companies with established, highly predictable business models that have remained fundamentally unchanged for decades. While their underlying portfolio companies are adapting to technological, macroeconomic, and geopolitical shifts, the holding companies themselves maintain extremely stable operational mandates: buy and hold structural assets, manage passively-but-actively via board seats, maintain strict and conservative leverage policies, and rely on dividend generation for capital allocation. The degree of internal corporate change at the parent holding company level is exceedingly low, indicating minimal disruption to their core investment frameworks. To warrant a higher rating (Yellow or Orange), these firms would need to announce drastic pivots in their target asset classes, a departure from their long-term dividend models, or highly irregular organizational restructurings, none of which are currently present or plausible.
