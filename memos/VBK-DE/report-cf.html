EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 4 findings

Verbio's leadership remains deeply entrenched with founder Claus Sauter retaining absolute control, but recent workforce reductions and the emergency US deployment of the Deputy CEO signal severe operational stress .

Management is investing €80-100 million into a new ethenolysis plant at the Bitterfeld site, with production targeted to start in the first half of FY 2026/27 , representing a bold diversification into green chemicals for surfactants, plastics, and lubricants.

Leadership outlined an estimated mid-to-long-term annual EBITDA earnings power of €700-800 million, though achieving this target relies on completing an attractive opportunity pipeline requiring over €1 billion in capex .

No active activist presence exists in the shareholder base, while the Sauter family voting pool binds over 70% of total voting capital, ensuring the founders retain absolute control over long- term strategic direction .

tightening via RED III and recovering GHG quota prices structurally improve Verbio's operating environment, with Germany retroactively eliminating double-counting for advanced biofuels starting January 1, 2026, restoring the cost advantage of conventional biofuels and immediately improving margins for first- generation bioethanol and biodiesel .

The elimination of double-counting forces fuel distributors to purchase real physical biofuel volumes, with management expecting the required volume of physical biomass molecules for compliance to double between 2024 and 2027 .

Germany's RED III implementation tightens the national GHG reduction quota from 12% in 2026 to 17.5% by 2027, with the legislative trajectory mandating a massive 65% greenhouse gas reduction target by 2040 . Seasonal demand for GHG quotas surged ahead of the June 2025 compliance deadline, with German GHG quota prices structurally recovering to recently exceed €410 per tonne of CO2-equivalent .

European market is normalising following severe distortions caused by fraudulent, mislabelled advanced biodiesel imports from Asia, as stricter RED III controls and mandatory on-site inspections for non-EU producers are actively eliminating these structural false incentives .

Verbio holds a unique competitive advantage with proprietary technology allowing simultaneous co-production of biomethane and bioethanol, while competitors primarily produce animal feed as a byproduct with a comparatively higher CO2 footprint .

North American demand visibility is improving, supported by record US Renewable Volume Obligations for 2026-2027, while Canada's Renewable Volume Obligation decision offers upside for winter production and higher effective blending rates .

Structural recovery is underway with free cash flow inflecting positive and margins rebounding, though elevated near-term leverage reflects lingering transition-year strains, as top-line is expected to grow approximately 8% in FY26E to €1.74 billion, rebounding from flat FY25 delivery, while FY26E EBITDA guidance was upgraded to €160- 180 million, indicating margin recovery to roughly 10% versus the FY25 trough of 0.9% .

Operating cash flows suffered heavily in FY25 due to approximately €100 million structurally tied up in GHG quota inventory, with inventory normalisation artificially delayed until 2027 due to politically suspended quota carry-forward rules .

Free cash flow turned positive in Q2 and Q3 FY26, reaching €33 million and enabling rapid sequential debt reduction .

Net financial debt declined sequentially to €126.8 million in Q3 FY26 from a recent peak of €203.8 million in Q1, while management maintains a hard net debt limit of €190 million, explicitly targeting a net debt-to- EBITDA ratio strictly below 1.0x . L3Y earnings display extreme volatility, with a severe FY25 margin collapse now aggressively inflecting upward driven by RED III implementation, as EBITDA margins have expanded sequentially from 3.5% in Q1 25/26 to 13.5% in Q3 25/26, while the preceding FY24/25 period saw brutal margin compression with Group EBITDA collapsing to €14.2 million from €121.6 million the prior year .

Earnings quality in FY25 was heavily impaired by a €66 million non-cash write-down on the Iowa straw biomethane plant .

Management has consistently framed the depressed FY24/25 results as a cyclical trough year distorted by fraudulent Asian imports .

The third fiscal quarter consistently acts as the high watermark for GHG quota monetisation ahead of the June compliance deadline , creating predictable seasonal patterns in earnings delivery. 14 Strong Q3 earnings beat driven by rebounding GHG quota prices and biomethane records significantly strengthens the near-term cash-flow recovery thesis, with Q3 2025/26 EBITDA of €60.2 million massively exceeding the €39.8 million consensus estimate, reported EPS of €0.35 marginally surpassing the €0.33 broker consensus expectation, while Group sales of €447.1 million missed the €538.0 million consensus estimate by 16.9% .

EBITDA doubled sequentially from the €30.1 million reported in the prior quarter, with the Bioethanol/Biomethane segment driving the beat with an unexpected €34.2 million EBITDA contribution, while Group EBITDA margins expanded to 13.5%, a sharp recovery from previously compressed levels .

Management significantly upgraded full-year FY25/26 EBITDA guidance to €160-180 million on May 27, with the upgraded guidance including an €18 million reversal of previous GHG quota inventory write-downs .
