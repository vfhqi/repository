Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 18 findings

Section summary
  1. ASML beat Q1 expectations but failed to satisfy the market’s aggressive Q2 outlook, as a shifting services mix depressed gross margins while capacity constraints and geopolitical bill proposals limited multiple expansion.

Q1 Results and Segment Delivery

1.
Sales performance – modest beat: ASML reported actual Q1 net sales of €8.77 billion, representing a 1% beat against the sell-side consensus of €8.69 billion
2.
Gross margin – high-end print: Actual Q1 gross margin reached 53%, beating consensus of 52.20% by 80 basis points, driven by a highly profitable product mix in the services segment
3.
Segment sales – split distribution: Net system sales came in at €6.28 billion, almost equally divided between memory at 51% (driven by DRAM) and logic at 49%
4.
Installed base – robust results: Q1 Installed Base Management sales reached €2.49 billion, beating consensus of €2.39 billion by 4% due to a strong contribution from high-margin service and field upgrades
5.
EUV shipments – volume expansion: The company sold and shipped 16 EUV units in Q1, up from 14 units in Q4 2025, confirming solid execution on advanced lithography tools
6.
Earnings per share – consensus beat: Diluted EPS came in at €7.15, beating consensus of €6.70 by 7% on superior operating leverage and cost control

Guidance Softness and Mid-Term Controversy

7.
Q2 sales guide – disappointing miss: ASML guided Q2 sales to €8.40-9.00 billion (midpoint €8.70 billion), missing consensus of €8.90 billion by 2.2%, which sparked concerns about a back-half loaded year
8.
Q2 margin guide – profit pressure: ASML conservatively guided Q2 gross margin to 51% to 52%, falling below consensus of 52.30% because of shifting mix from high-margin upgrades to lower-margin maintenance fees
9.
FY26 sales guide – positive raise: ASML raised and narrowed its FY26 sales guidance to €36-40 billion (midpoint €38.00 billion), beating consensus of €37.70 billion on strong non-EUV immersion demand
10.
FY26 margin guide – flat baseline: Despite the top-line raise, the FY26 gross margin guide was held at 51% to 53%, which failed to offer the upward revision the market expected given the higher revenue guide
11.
EUV capacity cap – post-print concern: Management guided to 2027 EUV capacity of "at least 80 units", which disappointed buy-side expectations of a 90-unit floor and raised concerns about cleanroom bottlenecks

China mix decline – guidance confirmation: Q1 China system sales plummeted 56% sequentially to €1.19 billion (19% of total), confirming prior guidance of a structural decline from 33% in FY25

Section 7 – Synthesis: most likely cause, counter-hypothesis, residual, decision 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The primary cause of ASML’s share price decline was cyclical multiple compression as peak order momentum normalized, leaving long-only investors with a critical next decision to verify Eindhoven cleanroom construction timelines.
1.
Leading cause – valuation de-rating: We assess the primary cause of the share price move is multiple compression from peak historical valuations as Q4 2025's blowout order book normalized, consistent with the industry's historical trading cycles
2.
Zeiss bottleneck – secondary cause: The second ranked cause is lens manufacturing constraints at Zeiss which forced DUV immersion shipments below 2025 levels, creating a mix headwind that depressed the gross margin guide
3.
MATCH Act threat – secondary cause: The third ranked cause is the proposed MATCH Act bill which introduced a highly restrictive regulatory overhang on ASML's profitable Chinese service revenues
4.
Plausibility read – MATCH Act passage: We assign a low plausibility to the standalone passage of the MATCH Act into law in 2026, viewing it primarily as a negotiating lever ahead of US/China talks
5.
Plausibility read – Zeiss constraint resolution: We assign a high plausibility to Zeiss normalising its optics box ramp in the second half of 2026, which would lift ASML's high-margin non-China DUV volumes
6.
Strongest counter-hypothesis – structural re-rating: The strongest counter-hypothesis is that ASML is undergoing a structural re-rating to historical premium valuations of 60% to 100% over peers, driven by a multi-year AI-led DRAM upcycle
7.
Unexplained residual – market sentiment: Approximately 15% of the recent volatility is unexplained by company-specific causes, representing broader macro profit-taking and technical sector-wide rotation out of semiconductor equipment
8.
Outlier call-out – 3800F platform shift: A critical non-obvious driver is the transition to the NXE:3800F platform, which prevents in-field upgrades and will force customers to purchase expensive new systems, boosting ASPs
9.
Actionable investor decision – BIC timeline: Long-only investors should immediately verify the progress of the Brainport Industries Campus construction starting in Q3 2026, as this cleanroom expansion is the gating factor to unlocking 2027 EPS of €45+
10.
Other side of the story: – MATCH Act disruption: If the MATCH Act is unexpectedly passed or enacted via executive order, ASML's recurring service gross profit could drop by up to 10%, destroying the €45+ EPS bull case and rendering all current sell-side targets obsolete
