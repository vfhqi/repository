§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Revenue assumption — customer/defence: Guidance assumes continued UK Ministry of Defence and NATO-allied procurement; UK defence spending budgeted to grow to ~2.5% of GDP by 2030 (UK government 2025 defence review) — a structural tailwind [#A]

  • Sub: concentration risk: Defence segment represents ~50-60% of revenue; single-customer or platform concentration within that segment is not fully disclosed — a credibility opacity [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management implies modest price increases passed through to customers, consistent with cost-plus defence contracting norms; defence contract pricing is relatively protected vs. pure commercial markets [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Medium-term revenue guidance implicitly or explicitly includes potential future acquisitions; stripping out M&A optionality, organic revenue growth is likely 4-8% — lower than implied aggregate growth rate of 8-12% [#A]

  • Margin assumption — integration cost: The single most load-bearing margin assumption is that Custom Power and earlier acquisition integration costs fall away in FY2025/26-FY2026/27; management has guided to this integration completion timeline for two consecutive years without delivery [#A]

  • Sub: repeated miss: This is the assumption that has been missed in each of L3 periods — it represents the primary credibility vulnerability [#A]

  • Margin assumption — operating leverage: Guidance implies operating leverage on fixed costs as revenue scales; valid in principle but the fixed cost base has grown with each acquisition, resetting the leverage calculus [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Gross margin stability assumed; defence segment gross margins are relatively sticky (cost-plus) but commercial/components segment faces competitive pricing pressure [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Standard UK corporation tax rate (25%) applied; no unusual tax shield or deferred tax asset driving EPS; tax assumption is conservative and low-risk [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count approximately stable (~5.1m diluted); no large dilutive equity issuance guided; low risk unless M&A is equity-funded [#A]

  • Sub: M&A equity risk: SOLI-LON has historically used equity alongside debt for acquisitions; a new deal could add 5-10% dilution — not guided but a latent EPS risk [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: SOLI-LON has meaningful USD exposure (Custom Power US operations); GBP strengthening represents a translation headwind not explicitly sensitivity-tested in public guidance [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity/component: Electronic component prices have normalised post-2022 disruption; no major commodity input risk for an electronics distributor/manufacturer [#A]
