§4 — Synthesis [~320 words | judgement-first]

Most likely cause of the crash: Regulatory quasi-receivership compounding a pre-existing accounting scandal

The -55% weekly crash in late March 2026 was directly triggered by the Bank of Italy's appointment of two Temporary Supporting Commissioners on 27-28 March, invoking Article 75-bis of Italy's Consolidated Banking Law. This was not a routine supervisory action: it is the most severe measure short of full administration, interpreted by markets as signalling that the Bank of Italy had lost confidence in the board's ability to remediate independently. The crash was the third consecutive market shock in six weeks, amplifying a stock already down ~35-40% from January highs.

Causal chain: The root cause is a multi-year accounting irregularity in the factoring business — specifically the recognition of late-payment interest income and classification of overdue public-sector receivables. The Bank of Italy identified these issues in a 2024 on-site inspection. BFF restated 2024 accounts, disclosing EUR54m misbooked between 2019 and 2023. The inspection also identified that up to EUR1.3bn in exposures warranted reclassification as non-performing (NPLs rose from EUR1.81bn to EUR2.99bn by Q1 2026). When Milan prosecutors disclosed their false-accounting probe on 17 February, the stock hit a record low — establishing the pattern that each new disclosure reset the floor.

Counter-hypothesis (low plausibility): A liquidity or funding crisis triggered the crash. This is not well-supported: deposits remained stable, CET1 stood at 11.0% in Q1 2026, and the bank's Q1 2026 reported net profit actually rose 40% YoY. No run on deposits was reported.

Unexplained residual: The specific timing of the 26 Mar - 2 Apr crash window is precisely coincident with the 27 March commissioner appointment; however, it is unclear whether pre-announcement leakage contributed to the move in the days before the official announcement. The Banca d'Italia announcement was dated 27 March; the stock began declining sharply from 26 March, suggesting possible market intelligence ahead of the announcement.

Decision prompt: BFF is a distressed-but-functioning bank undergoing supervised remediation. The key unresolved question is whether the EUR1.2bn NPL securitisation closes without capital dilution — management target Q3 2026. The 16-Jun-2026 AGM (virtual) to approve 2025 results is the next near-term catalyst.
