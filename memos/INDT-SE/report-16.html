§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Net signal on governance health is clean — single share class, no related-party concerns identified, long-tenured auditor with no changes, no legal/regulatory issues; structurally sound

Dual-class structure:
- None: Indutrade operates a single share class with equal voting rights — no A/B share split, no dual-class mechanism, no founder super-voting rights
- This is a material positive; many Swedish industrial groups (especially family-controlled) use A/B share structures; Indutrade's single-class structure means the ~27% Latour stake reflects economic + voting ownership in direct proportion

Founder control:
- Not applicable in the classical sense — Indutrade was founded by Handelsbankens Industri (a Swedish institutional vehicle) and floated; Latour (Douglass family) built its stake through market purchases over time
- The Latour stake (~27%) is the closest analogue to founder control — but Latour is itself a publicly listed investment company with its own governance obligations, creating a governance feedback loop that constrains abuse

Voting rights:
- Single class; each share = one vote; AGM resolutions pass by simple majority unless supermajority required by law
- No poison pill, no staggered board under Swedish law framework; AGM is the primary governance mechanism with full shareholder participation rights
- Swedish Code mandates that key remuneration policy resolutions are binding (not just advisory) at AGM — stronger shareholder rights than UK/US advisory votes

Related-party transactions:
- No material related-party transactions identified in AR 2022-2024 disclosures
- Latour relationship: Indutrade discloses Latour as a major shareholder; no commercial transactions between Latour and Indutrade identified that would create a conflict of interest; Carl Douglas (Latour representative on Indutrade board) recuses from any item where a Latour interest exists — disclosed in AR governance section
- The Nomination Committee is the one structural area where Latour's influence is concentrated; this is transparent and subject to minority shareholder scrutiny at AGM

Audit history:
- Auditor: KPMG (long-standing; 15+ year relationship estimated)
- No auditor changes in the public record — continuity is a double-edged signal: familiarity breeds quality + efficiency, but very long auditor relationships risk independence erosion
- No material weaknesses disclosed in KPMG audit opinions L5Y
- No restatements in the public record

Legal / regulatory issues:
- No class action lawsuits identified
- No AMF (Swedish Financial Supervisory Authority) investigations or sanctions in public record
- No GDPR enforcement actions of note
- No antitrust/competition investigations identified
- Indutrade's distributed business model (200+ subsidiaries, small average deal size) structurally minimises systemic legal risk — no single subsidiary is large enough to pose group-level legal exposure

Whistleblower / governance complaint signals:
- No public whistleblower disclosures identified
- Indutrade operates a confidential whistleblower channel per Swedish law requirements; no public disclosures of material complaints
- Glassdoor review themes do not surface governance complaints or ethics-related negative commentary

Anti-takeover provisions:
- None identified beyond standard Swedish law protections (pre-emption rights, mandatory offer threshold at 30% under LMA rules)
- No poison pill, no supermajority requirement for a change-of-control vote

⚠️ Sceptical bullet — bearish read on governance:
- Risk: The single most credible governance fragility is the Latour concentration combined with the Swedish Nomination Committee model — in normal operation this is constructive, but the scenario where Latour is acquired or its investment philosophy changes (e.g. activist pressure on Latour itself to crystallise its Indutrade stake) could simultaneously disrupt Indutrade's board composition, its Nomination Committee, and its strategic stability; a 27% block trade to a less-aligned buyer would represent a governance earthquake that the remaining governance structure cannot fully absorb; this is not a present risk, but it is the single structural governance fragility worth tracking
