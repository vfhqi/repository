Three bear pillars are independently credible: (1) North America revenue faces a prolonged air pocket as carrier capex pull-forward unwinds; (2) Vonage destroyed ~SEK 43bn of goodwill across two impairment cycles and the CSS turnaround is one quarter deep; (3) EPS CAGR of ~5% 2025–2028E implies the recovery is already priced at current multiples. The bear case is weakened, not defeated, by Q3-25 CSS margin and the IPR renewal outcome. [#J]

  • NA revenue air pocket confirmed Q1-26: Sales declined mid-single-digit YoY vs +20% Q1-25; management guided full-year trend similar — 41% revenue exposure to a falling knife. [#A]
  • Vonage impairment total ~SEK 43bn: SEK 32bn write-down Oct-23 + SEK 11.4bn Q2-24 + additional Q4-24 charge = two-thirds of $6.2bn purchase price destroyed within 30 months. No comparable capital allocation failure at Nokia in same period. [#A]
  • TAM structurally flat: Global RAN market -11% in 2023, -10–20% in 2024; Dell'Oro projects 0% CAGR over next five years. Bears argue Ericsson's EPS growth requires share gains in a deflationary TAM. [#A]

  • CONTEXT — Bear case has three nested layers: (1) macro/cycle headwinds (NA capex, flat TAM), (2) company-specific failures (Vonage, CSS history), (3) competitive erosion (Samsung, Huawei RAN share). [#I]

Absence of a dedicated short seller report does not defuse concerns — the arguments are mainstream. [#A]
  • OBSERVATION — Q1-26 miss was large: Reported sales SEK 49.3bn vs ~SEK 55bn consensus; EPS $0.0285 vs $0.1152 consensus = -75% miss. Adjusted EBITA fell 20% YoY, margin 11.3% vs 12.6% prior year. [#A] ❌
  • OBSERVATION — Bernstein bear thesis 2025: Avoid until management provides FY25 clarity; concern about lack of visibility beyond single-quarter guidance; RAN market downgrades from Dell'Oro cited as structural not cyclical. [#A]
  • JUDGEMENT — Bear camp has momentum in 2026: Stock has de-rated on Q1-26 miss; management guided full-year NA headwind similar to Q1-26 — this is not a "one quarter blip" narrative. [#J]
  • SCEPTICAL CHECK: The bear case requires NA to remain depressed into 2027; AT&T's $14bn multi-year deal provides contractual floor. The question is whether discretionary top-up spending resumes, not whether base spending stops. [#A]

§2 — Structural Business Model Concerns

J-front verdict: Three structural concerns are live: (1) RAN-hardware dependency in a flat TAM; (2) Vonage/CPaaS exposure as an albatross; (3) IPR revenue concentration in a handful of licensees. None are imminently fatal; all constrain the upside case. [#J]

  • STRUCTURE — Revenue mix too hardware-heavy: Networks = ~55% of revenue; CSS = ~27%; IPR = ~12%. RAN is cyclical, capital-intensive, margin-volatile. Software share too low vs Nokia's optical/software mix. [#A]
  • STRUCTURE — TAM = flat at best: Global RAN $40bn in 2023 (after -11% decline); 2024 further -10–20%. Dell'Oro 5-year CAGR projection = 0%. Comparison: Nokia has optical and enterprise software exposure in growing sub-markets. [#A] ❌
  • OBSERVATION — 5G cycle incomplete but not restarting: Key markets (US, W.Europe) completed primary 5G rollout; 5G-Advanced not a near-term capex trigger; 6G TAM not before 2030+. Network operators in many countries paused after initial 5G rollouts failed to improve ARPU or profitability. [#A]
  • STRUCTURE — IPR revenue is concentrated binary risk: SEK ~11bn (2023) from small number of OEM contracts. Huawei renewed Aug-23 — terms undisclosed, duration undisclosed beyond "long-term." Apple litigation resolved. Key question: what happens at next renewal cycle if 6G licensing rates compress? [#A] Uncertain
  • OBSERVATION — Vonage capital allocation destruction: $6.2bn acquisition Nov-21. SEK 32bn impairment Oct-23 (first). SEK 11.4bn impairment Q2-24 (second). Additional Q4-24 charge. Total written down = ~two-thirds of purchase price within 30 months. CPaaS market commoditised; BSFI enterprise growth never materialised at scale. [#A] ❌
  • OBSERVATION — CSS still below peer benchmarks: 12.5% EBITA in Q3-25 is first-ever positive quarter trend; Nokia's networks software margins are 15%+; Amdocs (pure-play CSP software) trades 18%+ EBITDA. CSS at 12.5% is early-recovery, not sector-leading. [#A]
  • JUDGEMENT — Structural bear argument: Ericsson's core business is in a flat TAM with no near-term catalyst; its diversification attempt (Vonage) was a disaster; its software leg has one quarter of sustained profitability. The bull case requires all three legs to work simultaneously — high coordination risk. [#J]
  • SCEPTICAL CHECK: The Vonage impairments are non-cash and now largely behind the company. The question is cash deployment going forward — management has indicated no large M&A appetite; capex is modest. The damage is sunk, not recurring. [#A] Bull rebuttal

§3 — Accounting Quality + Earnings Quality Concerns

J-front verdict: Accounting quality is a medium-grade concern, not a fraud thesis. The Vonage impairments were real and well-signalled; no evidence of revenue manipulation. Key risk is restructuring charges obfuscating underlying margins and the consistency of "adjusted" EBITA with reported financials. [#J]

  • OBSERVATION — Heavy use of "adjusted" reporting: Ericsson routinely excludes non-cash impairments, restructuring charges, and one-offs to present adjusted EBITA. Q1-26: adjusted EBITA margin 11.3% vs headline impacted by restructuring charges. Adjustments have been consistently additive for 3+ years. [#A]
  • OBSERVATION — Vonage impairment sequence raises governance questions: How did Ericsson's board approve a $6.2bn acquisition with insufficient due diligence on CPaaS TAM? First impairment followed just 12 months after close. Second within 30 months. No executive accountability disclosed publicly. [#A] ❌
  • OBSERVATION — Currency volatility distorts headline reporting: Q1-26 reported sales -10% YoY but organic +6%. SEK 7.8bn negative FX impact in one quarter. Analysts struggle to model true underlying trend when FX is this volatile. [#A] Uncertain
  • STRUCTURE — Restructuring charges are recurring, not one-off: Ericsson announced job cuts in Sweden in 2026 following Q1 miss; previous restructuring rounds 2022–24 also required charges. "Underlying" margin trajectory is materially better-looking than headline with adjustments. [#A]
  • OBSERVATION — CSS profitability is new and untested at scale: Q3-25 CSS EBITA 12.5% = first-ever positive momentum quarter. Margin is below steady-state software peer benchmarks. Bears argue this is an early-recovery margin that could revert if revenue growth disappoints in H1-26. [#A]
  • JUDGEMENT — Earnings quality not fraudulent but structurally complex: The adjustment policy inflates optics; the Vonage write-downs were non-cash but signal poor capital allocation controls; the CSS turnaround is real but thin. Bears are right to hold adjusted EBITA to a higher standard of proof. [#J]
  • SCEPTICAL CHECK: Ericsson is a large-cap Swedish company with Big Four audit, SEC ADR obligations, and DOJ compliance monitoring. The bar for accounting manipulation is very high. The concern is presentation quality, not integrity. [#A] Bull rebuttal
