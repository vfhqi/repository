§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

  • Cross-cutting verdict: The strongest bearish case is not a single-driver failure but a simultaneous confidence loss across drivers 1 and 2 — M&A drought + organic miss — which would break both the EPS growth narrative and the premium multiple simultaneously [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Drivers 1 (M&A) and 2 (organic recovery) are partly correlated — both depend on end-market confidence (customers must be growing to become acquisition targets, and to order instruments); a macro shock hits both simultaneously [#J]

  • Driver dependency: Driver 3 (margin conversion) is partially dependent on Driver 2 (organic volume growth providing operating leverage); margin recovery in a volume-flat environment requires cost cuts alone, which are harder and one-time [#A]

  • Driver dependency: Driver 4 (re-rating) is almost entirely dependent on drivers 1, 2, and 3 delivering; it is not an independent driver and should not be treated as additive probability [#J]
