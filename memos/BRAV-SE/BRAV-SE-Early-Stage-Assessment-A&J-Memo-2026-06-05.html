Section B — Executive Summary

BLUF

Genuine recovery story, but not a confirmed one yet. Bravida enters this ESA having delivered its first positive organic quarter (+1%, Q1 2026) in eight periods. The Danish turnaround is in the numbers. The Swedish cost savings are flowing. But the FALSE FRIEND ACH — M&A masking organic decay — remains the leading hypothesis with the fewest disconfirming items. The ESA's job is to test whether the business quality underneath justifies the recovery bet.

The business quality is real, though imperfect. The operator is a margin-obsessed, cycle-tested internal successor who has compounded the business across 160 acquisitions with a 99% average cash conversion since IPO. The Bravida Way operating model is a genuine institutional asset. The competitive position — #1 or #2 in Sweden, Norway, Denmark — is durable. Assemblin-Caverion is a stronger competitor than Bravida manages in its public commentary. But the multi-technical sector structure, with 25,000 operators and only 18% top-5 share, remains a multi-decade consolidation runway.

The case simplicity is holding, but Finland is the outstanding question. Sweden has zero organic growth but clear self-help levers. Denmark is fixed. Norway is weak but manageable. Finland has -14% organic in 2025, a 1.3% EBITA margin, and a #5 market position. If Finland is structural, not cyclical, the case perimeter is less clean than the Triaging thesis assumed.

Valuation leaves thin near-term upside vs consensus. The consensus target of SEK 114.8 is only 4% above the current price. This is a significant constraint: the stock is not obviously cheap at current levels on a 12-month view. The medium-term case (17-18x on recovery, M&A re-acceleration, receivables resolution) provides the real upside. If the market is already pricing Q1 2026's organic inflection, the entry point is not as attractive as the 9.5x EV/EBITDA headline suggests.

The receivables binary is the most important near-term event. A Norwegian court ruling was expected in June 2026 on the largest disputed receivable. Q2 2026 results (July 13) will contain the outcome. This is the highest-priority catalyst between ESA and Deep Dive. Bull case: SEK 400-550m recovered, ~13-18% EPS uplift in collection year, governance discount partial reversal. Bear case: full write-down SEK 600-800m, ~33-40% EPS hit in write-down year, stock to SEK 80-90 range.

Verdict: progress to Deep Dive, but with calibrated conviction. The business quality clears the ESA test — not at A level but at solid B-. The change forces are credible. The case outputs are achievable under the base scenario. The value-chain dynamics are broadly supportive. The operator is cycle-tested. The industry structure provides a structural growth runway. But the False Friend profile is live, Finland is unresolved, and the consensus target gap is thin. This is a "progress to DD with a specific research programme" recommendation, not an "obvious buy confirmed."

Bravida's electrical and HVAC competencies are directly in the critical path for EPBD compliance work across Sweden, Norway, Denmark, and Finland.   • Electrification (EVs, heat pumps, grid) represents a structural demand surge that is already visible in current order books: The transition from gas to heat pumps for residential and commercial heating, combined with the grid capacity upgrades required for EV charging and renewable energy integration, creates an installation demand surge in Bravida's core competency. Management identified this as a primary driver of data-centre and infrastructure project flow. The BF report confirms order intake grew 5% year-on-year in Q1 2026 and the backlog rose 7% to SEK 16.7bn, with management explicitly attributing this to structural infra verticals
  • Data-centre construction in Northern Europe is generating a disproportionate project pipeline for Bravida's scale and competency profile: Northern Europe is the preferred location for hyperscaler and enterprise data-centre expansion (climate, energy costs, political stability). These facilities require precision electrical, cooling, and HVAC systems that cannot be delivered by sub-scale local operators. Bravida's scale, multi-technical capability, and established presence in Sweden and Norway make it one of a handful of qualified operators for complex data-centre commissions
  • Defence spending increases across all Nordic markets (NATO obligations) are creating a growing public infrastructure pipeline independent of residential cycles: Post-2022 defence spending commitments have materially increased construction of military and dual-use facilities across Bravida's four markets.
