Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Auto1 exhibits rapid organic revenue growth and accelerating profitability as it digitalizes a highly fragmented European automotive market.
1.
Large-scale business model: Auto1 generates transaction revenues by purchasing used cars directly from private consumers and wholesaling them to professional dealers or selling them retail to consumers
2.
Compelling investment case: Sourcing scale through its physical drop-off network combined with proprietary real-time algorithmic pricing allows Auto1 to generate a highly predictable cash-generative trading margin This digital transaction platform leverages operational scale to compound adjusted EBITDA margins toward 5-9% long term
3.
Crucial swing factor: The ultimate driver of the forward earnings trajectory is the stabilization of average retail gross profit per unit at the target level

5.
Major breakdown risk: Sudden used car retail price shocks and inventory devaluations pose the primary risk of margin compression and earnings disruption
6.
Positive investment verdict: A deeper evaluation is highly warranted because Auto1 is structurally profitable, maintains an efficient balance sheet, and trades at a steep discount to international peers

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Auto1 occupies a profit-favoured position in the used car value chain by utilizing a dual wholesale and retail digital transactional platform.

Segment Distribution and Monetization Mechanics

1.
Dual marketplace segments: The business is organized into the Merchant segment (wholesale dealer auctions) and the Retail segment (direct-to-consumer online sales under the Autohero brand)
2.
Diversified revenue streams: Revenue is generated through direct vehicle sales, dealer auction fees, vehicle transportation and logistics, and integrated financing products
3.
Profit-favoured market positioning: Direct-from-consumer sourcing bypasses intermediate wholesale physical auctions, capturing a superior gross margin spread compared to traditional dealers
4.
Extreme customer fragmentation: Auto1 serves 54,371 partner dealers, eliminating customer concentration risk and providing highly diversified demand liquidity across Europe
5.
Comprehensive logistics integration: Auto1 operates Europe's largest used car logistics network, conducting 1.5 million transports across 170 logistics compounds to ensure reliable fulfillment
6.
Captive finance monetization: The retail segment monetizes customer financing directly, boosting unit economics and matching classic dealer profit profiles

Competitive Advantage Moat and Industry Dynamics

7.
Proprietary algorithmic pricing: Real-time pricing engines are trained on over a decade of transaction data, enabling accurate, low-risk quotes
8.
Replicability barriers: Competitors cannot easily replicate the platform because they lack the physical drop-off footprint and localized transactional history
9.
Substantial TAM backdrop: The European used car market is valued at €600B and is highly fragmented, with online penetration in its infancy
10.
Formidable regional scale: Auto1 has captured approximately 3% market share, making it the clear market leader with an unstoppable, self-reinforcing network flywheel
11.
Sourcing network dominance: Operating over 725 sourcing branches allows Auto1 to secure high-quality consumer inventory that traditional dealer groups cannot access
12.
Other side of the story: Skeptics argue that direct-to-consumer online vehicle sales face structural adoption barriers as European consumers continue to heavily prioritize physical inspections and test drives

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Sustained retail reconditioning scale and integrated financing should drive a predictable 18-month EPS acceleration path.

Key Structural Forward Drivers

1.
Driver 1: Scaling retail in-house reconditioning capacity. Moving Autohero's refurbishment to 12 centralized physical hubs reduces preparation costs by several hundred Euros per vehicle, structurally improving retail gross margin efficiency High plausibility demonstrated by Autohero's gross profit margin expanding as capacity reached 248k cars per year
2.
Driver 2: Integrated captive consumer financing rollout. Offering in-house car loans drives retail attachment rates and adds up to €250 in incremental retail gross profit High plausibility shown by competitive loan offerings in Germany, Austria, and Spain
3.
Driver 3: Captive B2B dealer financing. Merchant Financing has expanded to 8 markets, improving dealer purchasing power and boosting transaction volumes High plausibility shown by a 74% year-on-year increase in financed merchant sales
4.
Driver 4: Algorithmic pricing precision gains. Over a decade of transaction data improves AI model pricing, driving steady gross profits High plausibility proven by Merchant GPU increasing €62 year-on-year
5.
Driver 5: Sizable market consolidation. Capturing share in highly fragmented European wholesale car markets drives massive transactional volume High plausibility shown by active merchant accounts growing 23% year-on-year
6.
Driver 6: Rapidly maturing cross-border flows. Shifting inventory from high-residual source markets to high-demand retail markets expands trade spreads High plausibility demonstrated by cross-border sales accelerating to 60% of total volumes

Bull Case Requirements and Probability

7.
Bull case requirements: The forward EPS trajectory relies on steady wholesale volume growth on flat group GPUs of €1,200 The key structural drivers are largely independent, meaning the bull case is not exposed to single-point failure
8.
Decoupled growth dynamics: Merchant B2B growth and Autohero B2C retail growth operate independently, lowering overall compound probability risks
9.
Other side of the story: The biggest risk is that credit performance deteriorates in the consumer financing book, which would trigger impairment of Auto1's risk-retention tranche

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Macroeconomic shocks and inventory price volatility constitute the primary downside threats to the forward EPS path.

Key Structural Vulnerabilities and Monitoring Metrics

1.
Sizable inventory valuation risk: Holding automotive inventory exposes Auto1 to pricing write-downs This was seen in Q4 2022 when aggressive Tesla price cuts depressed Merchant GPU
2.
Growing credit risk exposure: Rapidly expanding merchant and consumer loan books increases default risk during a credit cycle downturn
3.
Linear physical capacity bottlenecks: Scaling operations requires physical infrastructure, reconditioning hubs, and transport personnel, limiting rapid exponential growth
4.
High false friend signal: Rising unit volumes that occur alongside dilutive GPU or rising logistics costs could mask structural margin decay
5.
Primary early warning signal: Investors should monitor the monthly Auto1 Group Price Index for used car wholesale pricing softening
6.
Chinese OEM new imports: Low-cost Chinese vehicles entering the European market could deflate used car residual values
7.
Other side of the story: The risk of pricing disruption is highly mitigated because 90% of vehicles are priced dynamically using real-time machine learning models This allows Auto1 to lower buying prices and protect trading margins during downturns
