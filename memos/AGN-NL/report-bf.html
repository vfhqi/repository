EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Aegon has executed a strategic pivot to the United States where its Transamerica franchise contributes approximately 60% to 70% of group underlying earnings and manages €261 billion globally through its asset management division , while simultaneously completing a landmark sale of its capital-intensive UK operations to

Standard Life for £2.0 billion and preparing for a US re-domiciliation that will cost approximately €350 million in one-time implementation expenses . The full-year IFRS operating result rose by 15% year-on-year to EUR 1.7 billion in FY25, while operating capital generation reached EUR 1.287 billion , surpassing the group's original EUR 1.2 billion target , demonstrating excellent execution discipline despite remaining highly sensitive to equity and credit market volatility which could impair capital generation and restrict future buybacks if the US RBC drops below the 350% threshold .

Core Business Model and Strategic Transformation

Aegon operates as an international financial services holding company focused on life insurance, pensions, and asset management, with its primary operating unit Transamerica serving millions of middle-market customers across the United States . The group derives revenues from insurance premiums, investment spreads, and feebased commissions, actively shifting its portfolio mix away from spread-based products toward capital-light, feebased retirement solutions organized into individual life insurance, retirement plan services, and mutual fund offerings .

The workplace platform remains a key driver of recurring fee income supported by regular employer plan contributions, while World Financial Group represents a proprietary channel of over 95,000 licensed agents driving life and annuity sales . The asset management division specializes in alternative fixed income and private debt managing €261 billion globally, while the move to relocate corporate headquarters from the Netherlands to Delaware directly aligns the parent's corporate structure with its dominant, high-growth US operating footprint .

The UK platform sale represents a premium valuation multiple of 14.2x the 2025 operating result after tax

and is expected to be accretive to group free cash flow per share forecasts, with net cash proceeds deployed toward senior debt deleveraging and accelerating share buyback initiatives . 5

Financial Performance and Peer Benchmarking

Aegon delivered robust capital generation with operating capital generation before holding funding and expenses reaching EUR 1.287 billion in FY25, outperforming the original target of EUR 1.2 billion , though Morgan Stanley notes underlying US OCG was 6% below consensus after removing EUR 78 million of one-offs .

The group's net result increased to EUR 980 million in FY25 from a net loss in prior years, aided by higher operating results and lower non-operating fair value losses .

Gross financial leverage remained stable at EUR 4.9 billion at year-end FY25, systematically reduced from EUR 5.1 billion reported in the first half of 2024, while the estimated group solvency ratio stood at 184% at year-end 2025

. Transamerica maintained a strong US Risk-Based Capital ratio of 424% at the end of FY25, comfortably above the company's internal operating level of 400% .

Aegon delivered an impressive return on regulatory capital of 22.60% during FY25, reflecting robust earnings on in-force and capital distributions received from its remaining a.s.r. stake, while free cash flow reached EUR 829 million representing strong conversion from OCG .

Dutch composite peer a.s.r. reported an operating return on equity of 14.10% in FY25, exceeding its mid-term target of greater than 12.00%, while generating organic capital creation of EUR 1.315 billion representing a 10.20% increase .

Aegon returned nearly EUR 1.0 billion of capital to shareholders in the second half of FY25, proposed a final dividend of EUR 0.21 per share totaling EUR 0.40 for FY25 representing an 11% increase, executed EUR 400 million of share buybacks reducing share count by 5%, and maintains a valuable 24.00% equity stake in the leading Dutch insurer a.s.r. which Morgan Stanley notes offers an attractive free cash flow yield of approximately 11% .

Customer Profile and Platform Dynamics

Transamerica focuses on serving middle-market and mass-affluent families serving over 10 million individual customers, with total US Retirement Plans assets under administration reaching USD 253.7 billion at the end of 2H25 including mid-sized retirement plans that grew assets to USD 63.0 billion up 17% year-on-year in 2H25.

Retirement plan net outflows improved to USD 3.2 billion in 2H25 versus outflows of USD 15.9 billion in 2H24 reflecting a major reduction in low-margin large plan discontinuances, while mid-sized retirement plan net deposits benefited from a large pooled plan takeover deposit.

Aegon UK served 3.8 million customers with GBP 246 billion in assets under administration in FY25 with total UK platform AuA growing to GBP 128.3 billion split between Workplace Platform assets that reached GBP 73.7 billion up 17% year-on-year and Adviser Platform that grew by only 6% year-on-year to GBP 54.6 billion.

Adviser platform net outflows reached GBP 1.97 billion in 2H25 up from GBP 1.77 billion in 2H24, though the planned sale of Aegon UK to Standard Life will transfer £160bn of platform AUA shifting 1.8 million retail platform customers to Standard Life to create a scaled provider.

Adviser platform customers are historically sticky with retention rates for leaders regularly exceeding 95% in typical conditions , with Jefferies noting that customer inertia, brand, and the high cost of using financial advisors prevent a rapid race to the bottom on price.

Structural Evolution and Long-Term Growth Vectors

Aegon is strategically positioned to capture secular retirement growth though faces near-term headwind of EUR 350 million in transitional accounting and regulatory relocation costs, while strict regulatory capital regimes and capital-intensive solvency frameworks prevent subscale challengers from entering the core retirement space and modernizing technology infrastructure creates a heavy operational cost burden for smaller players .

Group supervision transferred from the Dutch Central Bank to the Bermuda Monetary Authority on September 30, 2023 with Aegon's Bermuda solvency ratio calculation broadly aligned with Solvency II rules during the transition period ending in 2027, after which the group will fully adopt the Bermuda solvency aggregation approach with no anticipated capital impact .

The Perpetual Cumulative Subordinated Bonds lost Restricted Tier 1 capital eligibility on January 1, 2026 reducing the pro-forma group solvency ratio by 7 percentage points, while legal seat relocation to the US requires transition to US GAAP reporting beginning with full-year 2027 results with implementation of US GAAP and headquarters winding-down costing an estimated EUR 350 million .

Aegon focuses long-term growth on alternative fixed-income assets and higher-margin private credit with rapidly aging populations globally driving structural demand for private retirement savings while growth is targeted in thirdparty asset management to generate valuable fee-based income, with margin improvements at Aegon Asset Management lifting the operating margin from 3.50% to 16.00% in recent years and strategic assets targeting operating result growth of around 5% annually through 2027

. However, core US earnings remain highly correlated with equity market returns and interest rate volatility with rapidly rising interest rates triggering liquidity strain due to mandatory collateral posting under derivative contracts, while elevated rates can increase policy loans and contract surrenders forcing uneconomic asset sales and sustained low interest rates compress investment spreads due to minimum contract guarantees . 53
