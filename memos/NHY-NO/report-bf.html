Upstream to downstream structural value-chain mapping

1.
Complete value-chain architecture: The global aluminium value chain spans five highly capital-intensive, interdependent stages from bauxite extraction to downstream fabrication
2.
Bauxite extraction mechanics: Mining operations strip lateritic or karstic ores, which typically contain 45% to 50% alumina content
3.
Alumina refining process: Bauxite is processed using the chemical Bayer process, requiring 2 to 3 tonnes of bauxite and substantial caustic soda to yield 1 tonne of alumina
4.
Power generation feedstock: Smelting is exceptionally energy-intensive, requiring approximately 14 MWh of electricity per tonne of finished primary metal, making energy the dominant variable cost
5.
Primary smelting electrolysis: Smelters use Hall-Héroult electrolysis to reduce alumina into liquid metal, which is cast into standardized ingots or high-value extrusion billets
6.
Secondary recycling and remelting: Recycling remelts post-industrial and post-consumer scrap, consuming 95% less energy than primary electrolysis
7.
Downstream extrusion fabrication: Extruders push heated billets through steel dies to fabricate custom, high-margin structural profiles for automotive, construction, and industrial clients
