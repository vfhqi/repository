§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Assumption credibility verdict: Mixed credibility — revenue growth assumptions are plausible given backlog size; margin expansion assumptions are partially credible but embed RES pricing optimism; EPS CAGR assumption is the weakest and most tenuously supported [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — backlog: €4.5bn+ construction and infrastructure backlog (end-2024) at ~1.5–2x annual revenue run-rate; historically management has converted backlog at ~25–30% annually; guided pace implies no degradation in conversion rate [#A]

  • Sub: Key risk is that Greek RRF-linked contracts in backlog face disbursement delays as EU scrutiny of Greek project management increases post-2026

  • Revenue assumption — renewables: ~2.0–2.5GW operational capacity guides ~€180–220m annual RES revenue contribution in FY2025; based on P50 capacity factor assumptions (~28% wind, ~22% solar) [#J]

  • Sub: P50 is the median expectation; actual capacity factors have deviated ±5–8% from P50 in recent periods; a 5% capacity factor miss = ~€10–12m EBITDA impact

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Construction contracts priced with partial inflation escalation clauses; guided margins embed ~3–4% residual input cost inflation, within current consensus macro [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Guided 17–19% EBITDA margin assumes construction wage inflation of ~4–5% YoY (Greece labour market tightening) and energy input cost stability; both assumptions are broadly in line with current forecasts but could deteriorate [#J]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Guided margin expansion to 19–21% by FY2026 requires renewables EBITDA margin contribution of ~40–45% (typical for Greek onshore wind/solar); this is achievable but assumes DAPEEP regulatory pricing mechanism remains in current form [#A]

  • EPS assumption — D&A: D&A escalation risk — each €100m of new renewables assets added generates ~€5–7m incremental annual D&A; at guided capex pace, D&A headwind of €20–35m by FY2027 will suppress EPS even if EBITDA grows in line [#J]

  • EPS assumption — interest: Net debt expected to rise from ~€900m (FY2024E) to ~€1.1–1.2bn (FY2026E) at guided capex pace; interest expense growth of €15–25m by FY2026 is a direct EPS drag not prominently flagged in management guidance [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided ~22–24% (Greek corporate tax); no structural change expected but deferred tax position from renewables depreciation introduces volatility [#I]

  • EPS assumption — minority interest: GEK Terna's consolidated EBITDA includes significant minority interests (via TERNA Energy and infrastructure JVs); minority interest deduction from net profit is guided conservatively at ~15–20% of net income but historically varied 18–28% [#J]

  • Sub: ❌ Minority interest variability is an underappreciated EPS drag — if it rises from guided 18% to 25%, EPS misses by ~10–12%

  • Macro assumption — GDP: Guidance implicitly assumes Greek GDP growth of ~2–2.5% (2025–2027 consensus); Greek macro has been outperforming EU average but risks of fiscal tightening in 2026 are real [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Fully EUR-denominated business; no FX risk in core operations; minor currency exposure from international renewables projects [#I]

  • Macro assumption — RES policy: DAPEEP mechanism underpins ~60% of renewables revenue; regulatory continuity assumed; any reform reducing support prices = immediate margin compression [#J]
