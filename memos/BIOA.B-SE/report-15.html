§7 — Narrative vs Reality Check + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The strongest narrative-vs-reality disconnect is between management's royalty-ramp confidence and the structural commercial constraints that are visible in partner and competitor commentary but underweighted in BioArctic's own investor communications

Narrative claim — "gradual ramp is the expected pattern":
- Reality signal: Structurally accurate; diagnostic bottleneck is real and well-documented; management gets credit for honest framing here
- Disconnect identified: None at a directional level, but the pace of "gradual" has consistently come in at the slower end of the range; each quarter's "in line with expectations" from Eisai represents a new data point confirming the slow-ramp reality

Narrative claim — "market large enough for multiple agents":
- Reality signal: True in total addressable market terms (~6-7m US Alzheimer's patients); but the early-stage, amyloid-confirmed, low-ARIA-risk patient sub-pool that is actually eligible for near-term treatment is substantially smaller (estimated 400-600k actionable patients in Y1-3)
- Disconnect identified: BioArctic's TAM framing implicitly references the larger number; the actionable market is meaningfully smaller and is now being competed for by two agents; this is not a lie but it is optimistic framing

Narrative claim — "Leqembi's safety differentiation is a competitive advantage":
- Reality signal: True at the class-level versus the failed predecessors; but within the live anti-amyloid class (Leqembi vs Kisunla), safety differentiation is contested — Kisunla's ARIA rate is comparable in many patient subgroups
- Disconnect identified: Management's competitive-safety framing is becoming less defensible within the live competitive set and management has not updated the language accordingly — a detectable lag

Insider-vs-narrative gap:
- Verdict: No significant gap; insider buying pattern is consistent with management's patient-capital, long-cycle royalty narrative; if management were privately expecting a material negative event, the buying pattern seen in L18M would be anomalous and potentially problematic; the consistency between narrative and insider behaviour reduces (but does not eliminate) the false-friend risk

Expert-vs-narrative gap:
- Verdict: Modest gap on ARIA monitoring burden and Kisunla dosing preference; material gap is not detectable from available expert proxies; the diagnostic infrastructure bottleneck is where experts and management converge most clearly

Customer-vs-narrative gap:
- Verdict: Eisai's own measured language ("in line with trajectory") is slightly more cautious than BioArctic's investor presentations imply; Biogen's de-emphasis of Leqembi is a mild negative not fully reflected in BioArctic communications

False-friend pattern:
- Low evidence of classic false-friend: Classic false-friend is management buy-signal language + insider selling; the opposite is present here — cautious public language + insider buying; this is a more credible configuration than the false-friend archetype
- Sub: The risk is not false-friend but rather genuine-but-slow: management is honest about constraints, insiders believe in the long game, but the timeline to material royalty cash is longer than consensus models and execution risk is concentrated in a single commercial partnership

Disconnect identified — single largest credibility risk:
- Kisunla competitive displacement + Eisai commercial resource allocation: The scenario where Kisunla gains dominant early-patient share AND Eisai re-prioritises commercial resources is the single scenario that breaks the royalty narrative; management does not address this scenario directly; insider behaviour provides some comfort that internal view is not catastrophist, but it does not cover a slow-bleed scenario

Reverse-engineered bear:
- Bear case from §1-§6: Leqembi enters a multi-year ramp that is perpetually "gradual" because: (1) Kisunla takes early-patient share on dosing convenience, (2) diagnostic bottleneck resolves more slowly than modelled, (3) Biogen under-invests in field sales as their commercial attention moves to other pipeline assets, (4) BioArctic's secondary pipeline (BAN0805) delivers inconclusive Phase 2 data, removing the pipeline option value that currently supports the multiple; the royalty stream is real but small, and the company re-rates from royalty-growth to royalty-flat
- Sub: None of these are inevitable; the bull case (Eisai invests heavily, Leqembi gains class-level pull from diagnostic expansion, subcutaneous formulation launches and resets the dosing comparison) is live and reasonably probable; but the bear case is coherent and is not fully refuted by management narrative

Single largest credibility risk:
- Competitive displacement by Kisunla is underweighted in BioArctic's investor communications relative to the evidence in competitor and partner commentary; management's implicit assumption that the market is pre-competitive is more consistent with 2023 framing than 2025 competitive reality

Confidence in management narrative: Medium plausibility — the core royalty model and ramp direction are credible, but competitive positioning language is modestly behind the evolving evidence and the single-partnership dependency risk is structurally understated
