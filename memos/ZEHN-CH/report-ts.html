Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Zehnder holds a dominant market leadership position across key European residential ventilation markets, supported by high customer switching costs.

Business Segments and Product Portfolio

1.
Core ventilation segment: Zehnder's ventilation segment comprises residential heat recovery ventilation units, air distribution units, clean air solutions, and heat exchangers
2.
Legacy radiators segment: Within the radiators segment, Zehnder offers a wide range of decorative bathroom and multi-column radiators
3.
Growing service component: Although primarily a transactional hardware business, Zehnder has steadily expanded its recurring services and aftermarket business
4.
Superior economic margins: The economics of the ventilation segment are far superior to radiators, with ventilation operating on an assembly-only model that requires minimal capital depth
5.
Favoured value chain position: Zehnder sits favorably in the HVAC value chain as a high-value system provider, designing and assembling complete indoor climate systems rather than manufactured sub-components

Competitive Moats and Industry Structure

6.
Technical switching moats: Zehnder benefits from strong switching costs in its centralized ventilation systems since the tubing must be cast directly into concrete floors during construction
7.
Massive geographic concentration: The company's geographical footprint is heavily concentrated in Europe, which generated 81.50% of group sales in FY2025
8.
High industry fragmentation: The residential ventilation market is highly fragmented, with numerous local players but consolidated around tier-1 leaders
9.
Fragmented customer base: Zehnder's customer base consists of thousands of independent HVAC installers and wholesale distributors, leading to zero customer concentration risks

European Divisional Restructuring of June 2026

10.
Landmark organizational pivot: On June 19, 2026, management announced a complete restructuring of its European operations starting October 2026
11.
Elimination of compromises: Prior to this restructure, shared commercial and sales structures forced compromises across the different market realities of ventilation and radiators
12.
Staged rollout timeline: The separation will be implemented in three distinct phases starting with functional splits on October 1, 2026, and legal entity separation on January 1, 2027
13.
Other side of the story: Legacy radiator operations remain a structural drag, as overcapacity and commoditization in the decorative radiator market restrict margin improvement despite severe capacity consolidation and factory closures

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by extreme cyclical residential dependency and raw material cost inflation.

Core Risk Transmission Mechanisms

1.
High residential exposure: Zehnder maintains an 85.00% exposure to residential applications across the group, which is the highest among its peers, exposing it to cyclical downswings
2.
Severe installer constraints: The physical capacity to install centralized systems is constrained by severe shortages of skilled HVAC installers across Western Europe
3.
Supply chain vulnerabilities: Zehnder is highly dependent on external suppliers for key sub-components, such as blowers and electronic controls used in ventilation assembly
4.
Volatile input costs: The group is highly sensitive to commodity price increases, particularly steel for radiators and specialty plastics for air distribution ducts

Early-Warning Monitoring Metrics

5.
Material False Friend signal: Positive sales growth in the radiator segment can act as a false friend if driven by lower-priced decorative models that dilute group profitability
6.
Critical early-warning indicator: The single most reliable early-warning signal for Zehnder's EPS trajectory is the quarterly residential building permits in Germany and France
7.
Lagging building completions: Residential building completions typically lag structural permits by three to four quarters, delaying the physical installation of ventilation hardware
8.
Other side of the story: The structural growth in ventilation systems is partially insulated from new-build cycles due to a rapidly expanding, non-discretionary aftermarket filter-replacement and lifecycle service business
