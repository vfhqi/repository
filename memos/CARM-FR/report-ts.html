Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila offers a defensive, highly predictable cash flow stream underpinned by its strategic adjacency to Carrefour hypermarkets, yielding stable rental growth and an attractive high single-digit dividend yield.
1.
Favourable retail property model: Carmila owns and operates 250 shopping centres adjacent to Carrefour hypermarkets in France, Spain, and Italy, deriving its revenue from long-term retail leases. This spatial co-location represents a massive competitive advantage for Carmila's retail tenant galleries
2.
Solid investment thesis: The core investment case lies in a high starting dividend yield of 8.4% coupled with stable organic rental growth from indexation. Predictable cost of debt locked in at 3.0% protects the recurring earnings profile
3.
Favourable indexation swing factor: The rate of consumer price indexation applied to lease agreements is the single most critical driver of forward organic net rental income growth. This metric determines the baseline organic cash flow trajectory
4.
Steady historical outperformance: Recurring earnings per share expanded by 8.7% to €1.81 in 2025, compared to €1.67 in 2024, demonstrating robust operating cash flow generation. This growth reflects the successful integration of Galimmo
5.
Slowing French retailer sales: Retailer sales in French assets flatlined at 0.0% in 2025, exposing the portfolio to domestic discretionary spending pressures. France accounts for the vast majority of Carmila's GAV, making this trend important
6.
Technical share overhang: Carrefour's equity stake of 29.9% remains a potential market overhang following its 7% placement in June 2025. This structural relationship presents a regular block sale risk
7.
Compelling asymmetric value: A closer look is highly warranted as the stock's 38% discount to Net Tangible Assets provides substantial valuation support. Aligned operational momentum suggests a defensive entry point

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila's defensive grocery-anchored portfolio across France and Spain delivers highly visible rental income, though its structural dependence on the Carrefour ecosystem introduces unique tenant concentration dynamics.

Asset Portfolio and Segment Breakdown

1.
Dominant physical footprint: Carmila's physical assets comprise 250 shopping centres and retail parks located across France, Spain, and Italy, valued at a combined €6.7bn. The portfolio is characterized by medium-sized assets with an average valuation of approximately €30m per property
2.
Focused geographic concentration: France represents the core market with 75% of Gross Asset Value, while Spain accounts for 20% and Italy represents the remaining 5% of the portfolio. French assets contribute the dominant portion of gross rental income
3.
Defensive grocery anchorage: Almost all assets are co-located with Carrefour hypermarkets, which act as dominant anchor tenants that generate frequent customer footfall. This grocery-anchored model provides strong defensive characteristics during economic downturns
4.
Improving merchandising mix: Management has actively shifted the tenant mix away from apparel, reducing clothing and accessories from 34% of rental income in 2019 to 28% in 2024. This strategy focuses on expanding less cyclical retail categories
5.
Expanding defensive exposure: Health, beauty, dining, and fitness sectors have been expanded, with food and restaurants now accounting for over 13% of total rental revenues. Healthcare and local service formats continue to attract robust tenant demand

Revenue Model and Competitive Moat

6.
Stable lease structure: Revenues are earned through long-term commercial leases, with minimum guaranteed rents representing 97.8% of annualized rents in 2025. This provides a highly predictable revenue baseline with low variable exposure
7.
Favourable rental indexation: Lease contracts are indexed to national inflation rates, providing a predictable top-line growth trajectory and a natural inflation hedge. The company historically outperforms indexation by active asset management
8.
High lease collection: The rental collection rate was 97.8% in 2025, which underscores the high affordability of Carmila's retail gallery locations. Sustainable tenant effort ratios prevent widespread lease defaults
9.
Favourable network synergies: National and international brands scale efficiently by rolling out across multiple Carmila centres simultaneously. Retailers such as Rituals and Jack & Jones use the platform to accelerate European expansion
10.
High catchment concentration: Catchment areas are highly secure, with one in three residents in France and Spain living within a 20-minute drive of a centre. This proximity generates recurring local visits
11.
Competitive industry positioning: Carmila is the third largest retail property company in Continental Europe, positioned behind major institutional peers Unibail-Rodamco-Westfield and Klépierre. This scale supports efficient access to capital markets
12.
Other side of the story: Technical tenant concentration risk exists because store consolidation or hypermarket closures by Carrefour potentially reducing shadow footfall in adjacent shopping galleries. This would ultimately impair adjacent retail gallery values

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila's strong balance sheet and stable cost of debt have successfully driven recurring earnings growth, outperforming conservative management guidance despite recent asset revaluation headwinds in secondary regions.

Financial Trajectory and Return Profile

1.
Sustained revenue expansion: Gross rental income grew to €434.4m in 2025 from €404.1m in 2024, representing 7.5% growth. This top-line expansion was driven by both indexation and scope changes
2.
Expanding operating profitability: EBITDA reached €344.5m in 2025, which represents a year-on-year increase of 9.8% compared to €313.8m in 2024. This cash flow generation supports robust ongoing distributions
3.
Improved margin efficiency: The EBITDA margin expanded by 160 bps to 79.3% in 2025 from 77.7% in 2024, driven by overhead cost discipline. AI-driven operating efficiencies have supported lower administration costs
4.
Accelerating earnings outperformance: Recurring earnings per share increased by 8.7% to €1.81 in 2025, up from €1.67 in 2024. The 2025 FFO trajectory shows strong underlying operational momentum
5.
High cash conversion: Operating cash flow after debt and taxes expanded to €207.0m in 2025 from €202.4m in 2024, ensuring strong dividend coverage. High rent collection supports rapid cash translation

Balance Sheet Quality and Guidance Track Record

6.
Stable leverage ratios: Average Net Debt to EBITDA remained comfortable at 7.4x in both 2024 and 2025, supporting a BBB rating from S&P. Leverage is well below covenant thresholds
7.
Favourable EPRA LTV: Loan-to-value was 38.8% in 2025 (including transfer taxes), providing headroom below the 40.0% target. The group maintains strong financial flexibility
8.
Highly conservative guidance: Management's guidance history reads as highly conservative, evidenced by the 2025 EPS result of €1.81 beating initial guidance of €1.75. This reflects a track record of setting conservative management guidance
9.
Successful M&A execution: The integration of Galimmo was completed ahead of schedule, providing €5m in run-rate synergies. Galimmo has immediately contributed to EPS accretion
10.
Predictable debt coverage: The interest coverage ratio was solid at 4.6x in 2025, up from 4.5x in 2024. Extensive derivative hedges protect interest costs
11.
Other side of the story: Revaluation headwinds in Italy resulted in a 1.3% like-for-like asset value decline in 2025, limiting overall Net Tangible Assets expansion. This decline was primarily driven by asset restructuring on the ground

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila's forward earnings case strengthens under its high-margin innovation initiatives and disciplined capital rotation, but remains highly dependent on successfully managing refinancing headwinds after 2026.

Strategic Earnings Growth Pillars

1.
Predictable indexation capture: Long-term leases are linked to consumer price indexes, with indexation capturing 2.4% in 2025. This supports consistent rental base expansion
2.
Favourable innovation growth: Non-traditional services, including specialty leasing and retail media, contributed €27m to 2025 earnings, with high EBITDA margins. These high-margin, low-capex cash flows represent Carmila's fastest-growing segment
3.
Favourable retail media upside: Strategic retail media partnership with Carrefour and JCDecaux is estimated to add 1% to 2% to EBITDA growth. This leverages existing physical footfall data
4.
Disciplined asset rotation: Management continues to execute capital recycling, disposing of €69m of assets in 2025 at book value to fund new pipelines. This recycling maintains LTV discipline
5.
Accretive acquisition spreads: Carmila targets €100m of annual acquisitions at spreads of 100 to 150 bps above cap rates. This supports direct earnings accretion

Portfolio Enhancements and Refinancing Protection

6.
Clear development pipeline: Mixed-use development projects, in partnership with Carrefour, enhance asset density starting in 2027. Urban planning regulations restrict greenfield supply, enhancing existing asset values
7.
High-yielding agile capex: Redeployment of capital into agile restructuring projects targets a high 10% yield on cost. This creates immediate operating value
8.
Robust hedging protection: Swap instruments cover €585m of debt, protecting cash flows from floating rate volatility. The average cost of debt was stable at 3.0% in 2025
9.
Aligned execution requirements: The bull case depends on compound execution, where organic rental indexation must remain positive to offset rising interest costs. Investors must watch this rate spread closely
10.
Other side of the story: Rapid upward shifts in interest rates could compress acquisition yields if marginal financing costs exceed the targeted returns. This would impair capital allocation plans

Section 6 – Valuation 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila shares look historically cheap relative to prime retail peers, trading at a steep 38% discount to Net Tangible Assets and offering a highly supportive cash dividend yield.
1.
Steep discount to net assets: Carmila trades at a substantial 38% discount to its reported EPRA Net Tangible Assets of €26.52 per share. This provides strong valuation downside protection
2.
Favourable peer valuation support: The 38% discount is significantly wider than retail peer Klépierre, which trades at a 15% discount. This illustrates Carmila's relative undervaluation
3.
Highly attractive dividend yield: The shares yield 8.4% on a price of €16.80, providing a significant cash yield premium to peers. Proposed 2025 dividend was increased to €1.36
4.
Low FFO multiple: Carmila trades at a 2026E P/FFO multiple of 9.2x, presenting a highly defensive valuation entry point. This represents an attractive cash multiple relative to historical averages
5.
Supportive median target upside: Sell-side median price target of €20.00 represents a 14% upside from a close of €17.58. This indicates positive consensus re-rating potential
6.
Pessimistic market assumptions: The current share valuation implies that listed markets are pricing in an extensive downside scenario. This creates a highly asymmetric entry opportunity
7.
Other side of the story: A persistent valuation discount is likely if Carrefour's long-term intention to remain the reference shareholder is questioned by the market. This technical factor remains key

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is mitigated by grocery-anchored asset resilience, but structural retail headwinds and tenant-specific lease defaults present persistent cash flow challenges in soft macro environments.

Operational and Tenant Default Risks

1.
Slowing French organic sales: Tenant retail sales in France flatlined at 0.0% in 2025, lagging peer Mercialys' performance of 2.6%. French operations represent the core revenue engine
2.
Rising rent-to-sales ratios: Tenant occupancy cost ratios rose by 10 bps to 10.9% in 2025, indicating potential lease affordability pressures. France and Spain both registered 10.8% occupancy cost ratios
3.
Technical share overhang: Carrefour's reduction of its stake to 29.9% creates ongoing market overhang risks regarding future block sales. This structural alignment keeps technical pressures high
4.
Geographically concentrated exposure: Concentrated exposure with 75% of assets in France ties earnings performance directly to French macroeconomic metrics. This creates a structural geographic risk profile
5.
Refinancing cost step-ups: Legacy bonds with a low 1.625% coupon maturing in 2027 must be refinanced at significantly higher rates. Hedges partially mitigate the financial impact

Structural Disruptions and Early Warnings

6.
Threat of online commerce: The secular expansion of online shopping represents a structural threat that could permanently impair retail asset valuations. This could eventually lead to lower physical occupancy levels
7.
High-impact failure mode: A sudden wave of tenant insolvencies within mid-market fashion represents the single most damaging failure mode. This would quickly spike retail vacancies
8.
Critical warning signals: Financial vacancy rate trends represent the most important leading indicator of overall portfolio asset health. Financial occupancy reached 96.5% at end-2025
9.
Absent short interest: Research indicates there are no major short-seller campaigns or high short interest levels outstanding on Carmila shares
10.
Other side of the story: Bear arguments are likely overstated given the high resilience of grocery anchor footfall, meaning downside risk is highly asymmetric. This anchors the long-term value of the assets

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila's leadership team remains highly stable and execution-focused under CEO Marie Cheval, with minimal executive turnover and aligned long-term equity compensation structures.
1.
Stable executive leadership: CEO Marie Cheval has led the company since November 2020 ensuring high consistency in strategic plan execution. Pierre-Yves Thirion continues as CFO, with no planned financial management transitions
2.
Board structural changes: Sébastien Cayla was co-opted to the Board of Directors in October 2025, replacing Claire Noël du Payrat. Cayla is a permanent representative of Carrefour, reflecting the shareholder's active engagement
3.
Aligned executive compensation: Corporate officer remuneration consists of performance-linked free share allocations subject to strict equity holding limits. The CEO is required to hold 50% of vested free shares to align with equity interests
4.
Strict market abuse controls: Corporate officers operate under a strict Stock Market Ethics Code defining closed trading windows prior to releases. No family ties exist between directors and executive management

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Carmila warrants deeper investment due diligence as its high earnings visibility, massive asset discount, and strong dividend yield create an asymmetric positive risk-reward profile.
1.
Highly predictable organic growth: The strongest argument for investing is the predictable recurring EPS trajectory driven by inflation indexation. This cash flow is extremely resilient through macro cycles
2.
Other side of the story: The strongest argument against investing is the structural cap on long-term rental growth if online commerce continues to compress physical retail margins. This structural threat could block long-term valuation expansion
3.
Targeted tenant due diligence: Investigate lease maturity profiles and occupancy cost ratios of tenants within the recently integrated Galimmo portfolio. This verifies whether Galimmo's collection parameters can be aligned with Carmila
4.
Quantitative hedge verification: Verify the maturity schedules of Carmila's interest rate swap agreements to model post-2027 interest expenses. This enables a precise calculation of cash flow sensitivities under high-rate scenarios
