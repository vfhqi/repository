Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Comet holds uniquely dominant market positions in front-end plasma control components and is expanding into high-margin semiconductor inspection.

FRONT-END RF POWER AND PLASMA CONTROL

1.
Product portfolio at front-end: The Plasma Control Technologies division develops and manufactures vacuum capacitors, matching networks, and radio frequency generators that enable precise plasma control in critical front-end semiconductor fabrication steps, such as dry etching and thin-film deposition
2.
Transactional revenue model: Revenue is primarily transactional and driven by capital equipment cycles, as major semiconductor toolmakers purchase Comet's subsystems to integrate into complete wafer fabrication equipment
3.
Strategic value chain position: Comet sits in a highly profit-favoured position as the undisputed global leader in vacuum capacitors, capturing dominant market shares and maintaining high barriers to entry based on over 75 years of specialized engineering experience
4.
Front-end customer concentration: The customer base is highly concentrated, with primary toolmakers such as Lam Research and Applied Materials accounting for the vast majority of Plasma Control Technologies' segment sales
5.
Durable competitive advantages: The company's competitive advantage is secured by deep switching costs, where custom impedance matching networks are co-designed with major OEMs, making substitution technically risky and commercially disruptive
6.
Shifting industry dynamics: The front-end industry is structured as an oligopoly, growing in process complexity as advanced nodes transition to gate-all-around architectures, which structurally elevates the demand for high-precision, multi-level RF pulsing subsystems

BACK-END X-RAY INSPECTION AND METROLOGY

7.
Back-end product offerings: The X-Ray Systems and Industrial X-Ray Modules divisions design non-destructive 3D inspection systems, computed tomography platforms, and compact X-ray sources deployed by foundries, integrated device manufacturers, and outsourced assembly and test providers
8.
Transition to semiconductor inspection: While historically exposed to general industrial, automotive, and aerospace markets, Comet is pivoting its back-end X-ray business toward high-margin semiconductor advanced packaging metrology, specifically through the commercialization of its CA20 system
9.
Joint development with TSMC: The CA20 system was non-exclusively co-developed with Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, positioning Comet directly at the center of advanced packaging platforms like Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate
10.
Back-end customer diversity: Unlike the highly concentrated front-end division, the back-end X-ray business serves hundreds of projects across diverse automotive, defense, and electronics clients globally, providing a partial cyclical buffer during deep semiconductor downturns
11.
Advanced packaging competitive barrier: Competitive barriers are rising in back-end packaging metrology as the cost of failure grows exponentially with complex multi-chiplet integrations, prompting a structural shift from optical surface checks to non-destructive 3D X-ray inspection
12.
Restructuring legacy industrial activities: Comet is actively restructuring its unprofitable legacy industrial X-ray systems to phase out low-margin, non-semiconductor lines and focus resources on semiconductor advanced packaging metrology
13.
Other side of the story: Customers frequently express dissatisfaction with their high dependency on Comet's vacuum capacitors, motivating competitors to fund and qualify alternative Chinese suppliers, which could gradually erode Comet's pricing power over a multi-year horizon

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case rapidly strengthens as mainstream memory capital spend rebounds and next-generation product lines scale commercially.
1.
Cyclical memory upswing: The first primary driver is the highly anticipated recovery in NAND flash and DRAM equipment spending, which touches the Plasma Control Technologies division's high-margin matching network and vacuum capacitor lines. Plausibility of this driver is high, as utilization rates at global foundries have normalized and average selling prices for enterprise solid-state drives are rising strongly. The failure mechanism would be a sudden demand freeze in consumer electronics, which would halt OEM restocking
2.
Strategic TAM expansion: The second driver is the commercialization of the Synertia RF power generator platform, which expands Comet's addressable market and targets a 10.00% market share in the larger generator segment by 2028. Plausibility is medium; while technically advanced with fast pulsing and integrated machine learning, it must displace entrenched, highly trusted incumbents. The failure mechanism would be extended qualification delays or customer reluctance to break 30-year relationships with market leaders
3.
Metrology market entry: The third driver is the CA20 3D X-ray packaging inspection system, designed to capture material share in the nascent advanced packaging inspection market, which is expected to reach CHF 50.00m in sales for Comet by 2028. Plausibility is high because the system has been co-developed with TSMC, and the number of active engagements with foundries and outsourced assembly partners has surged from 2 in 2023 to over 25 by late 2025. The failure mechanism would be slower-than-expected commercial adoption of panel-level packaging technologies
4.
Operational cost reductions: The fourth driver is the operational launch of the Penang, Malaysia facility in the second half of 2026, which is designed to drastically lower direct labor and utility costs. Plausibility is high; labor costs in Malaysia are estimated to be five times lower than in Switzerland, providing an immediate margin benefit and natural hedging against Swiss franc appreciation. The failure mechanism would be severe execution delays or supply chain bottlenecks during the initial factory ramp-up
5.
Broadening addressable market: The cumulative impact of these forward-integration initiatives is a doubling of the serviceable addressable market from USD 1.70bn to USD 3.50bn, representing a 105.00% growth profile
6.
Independent growth drivers: Crucially, the investment case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously; the core memory-led WFE cyclical rebound can deliver the required earnings inflection independently of the pace of Synertia or CA20 market share gains
7.
Other side of the story: The Synertia generator might fail to gain significant traction if major OEMs, such as Lam Research, continue to favor eVerest and NavX platforms from rival Advanced Energy, limiting Comet's volume opportunities to minor, low-volume metal deposition tools

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by customer concentration and execution risks, though underlying memory demand provides a highly resilient buffer.
1.
Severe customer concentration: The single most critical structural risk is the extreme concentration of the customer base, where the top three semiconductor OEMs, Applied Materials, Lam Research, and Tokyo Electron, account for 40.00% to 50.00% of Comet's group revenues. Any customer-specific design shift or inventory management directly damages earnings
2.
High memory exposure: Comet carries over fifty percent exposure to the volatile memory market, specifically 3D NAND, where layers are proportional to RF power demand, exposing the firm to rapid spending halts by memory manufacturers
3.
Geopolitical trade barriers: Geopolitical trade tensions and export controls pose a significant risk, particularly with approximately 20.00% to 25.00% of group sales derived from legacy X-ray and PCT product lines in China
4.
Regional competitive threats: Increased competition in legacy X-ray and PCT from domestic Chinese sub-suppliers, who are benefiting from localized sourcing initiatives, presents a medium-term threat to market share
5.
Malaysia execution risks: The massive production expansion in Malaysia carries operational execution risks, where manufacturing delays or capacity scale-up issues could limit Comet's capability to capture market share
6.
Robust early warning indicator: The key early-warning signal to monitor is Lam Research's quarterly NAND equipment sales, which have historically shown a direct, leading correlation to Comet's Plasma Control Technologies segment revenues
7.
Other side of the story: These geopolitical and trade risks are highly overstated because Comet's Swiss neutrality protects its market access, while its US-based competitors are restricted from supplying Chinese domestic OEMs, allowing Comet to capture outsized market share
