EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Investment Thesis and Structural Position

The UK wealth platform industry presents multi-decade structural tailwinds , with an estimated £2.4 trillion of UK savings held off-platform out of a total addressable market of £6.2 trillion , driven by demographic aging, pension responsibility shifts from institutional to individual provision, and ongoing regulatory complexity that favors scaled digital platforms. The eight largest UK platforms collectively administer nearly 60% of total on-platform assets , creating a consolidated oligopoly where sub-scale competitors face insurmountable barriers to organic growth. AJ Bell occupies the highly lucrative platform administration stage of the value chain, capturing

40%+ EBIT margins while leveraging upstream vertical integration via AJ Bell Investments to recapture asset management economics on £9.8 billion in managed portfolio service assets.

Financial Performance and Peer Benchmarking

Capital allocation discipline remains exemplary , combining a progressive 5.00p interim dividend up 11% year-onyear with a repeatable £15.00 million share buyback following an existing £50.00 million allocation . Customer acquisition economics validate the operational model's sustainability, with D2C customer acquisition costs of £314 versus an average relationship duration of nine years generating an estimated customer lifetime value of £1,373 . Platform operating leverage continues to compound, as automation allowed the group to decrease operational headcount by 2% despite a 22% platform customer increase , validating management's thesis that scale benefits mechanically widen competitive moats without requiring heroic execution.

Competitive Advantages and Structural Moats

AJ Bell's competitive positioning rests on four durable structural advantages that compound over time. First, platform customer retention of 94.6% in H1 2026 translates to average client relationship longevity of 11 years for advised customers, 9 years for D2C clients, with management estimating overall duration spans over 17 years , driven by substantial transfer friction including tax wrapper restrictions and penalties such as a 25% government charge on Lifetime ISA transfers . Second, scale economies create self-reinforcing pricing power , where the platform proactively shares cost benefits through reduced online dealing fees to £5.00 per trade and lowered MPS charges to 0.12% in October 2026 , mechanically attracting inbound transfers from higher-cost legacy platforms including the 45% of Hargreaves Lansdown customer transfers following that competitor's recent fee announcements.

Third, technological infrastructure presents insurmountable barriers for new entrants, as AJ Bell outsources its critical back-office custody engine to FNZ under a 10-year contract extended in September 2025 while maintaining proprietary front-end interfaces that decouple customer experience from outsourced systems. Fourth, regulatory complexity under FCA Consumer Duty rules creates natural consolidation gravity , favoring operators with transparent low-cost pricing frameworks that align with fair value expectations while sub-scale platforms face margin compression from compliance investments. The confluence of these moats positions AJ Bell to capture disproportionate market share as industry concentration intensifies and weaker competitors exit through M&A or voluntary wind-downs.

Operator Quality and Capital Allocation Excellence

CEO Michael Summersgill demonstrates exceptional operational discipline , having served as CFO since 2011 before assuming the CEO role in October 2022 following a carefully planned succession . Management prioritizes organic development over dilutive acquisitions, successfully doubling platform scale over five years to surpass £100 billion in assets while attracting £7.5 billion in net inflows during FY25 . Strategic focus remains lasertargeted on core platform expansion, evidenced by the disposal of the Platinum SIPP and SSAS non-platform business to simplify corporate structure and redeployment of capital into higher-return customer acquisition and technology investments.
