§2 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 1: Industrial-Cycle Demand Collapse / Hydraulics Volume Cliff

  • Early warning signals:
  • European manufacturing PMI sustained below 48 for 3+ consecutive months
  • Interpump quarterly organic revenue growth (disclosed segment level) turns negative ex-M&A
  • Parker Hannifin / Bosch Rexroth order intake data (proxy — leads Interpump by 1-2 quarters)
  • Customer capex guidance cuts from major OEM end-markets (agriculture, construction, industrial)

  • Distributor inventory build-up signals in Interpump's quarterly trade-working-capital disclosures

  • Plausibility: High plausibility — European manufacturing has been in contraction since mid-2023; hydraulics demand is highly GDP/capex-elastic; sector peers have already reported 10-20% volume declines in prior cycle; base rate of hydraulics cycle troughs per decade = 2-3 events

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: macro contraction is underway (European PMI sub-50 for 18+ months to early 2025)
  • Motive: distributors have every incentive to de-stock before inventory becomes a cash drag
  • Opportunity: Interpump has limited revenue visibility (typically <3 months order backlog in hydraulics)

  • Verdict: All three legs present — full activation

  • Disconfirming evidence: New product cycle launches at major OEM customers that require Interpump-specific hydraulic content; backlog commentary suggesting order book has stabilised; infrastructure stimulus programs creating non-cyclical demand floor

  • Monitoring checklist:

  • Book-to-bill proxy from Parker / Danfoss order data
  • Trade working capital days (inventory DSI at Interpump)
  • European PMI monthly readings

  • Interpump H1 guidance commentary re: order visibility

  • Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with FM3 (margin compression via operating deleverage), FM6 (M&A pause — cash conservation), and FM9 (false-friend earnings beat via cost-cutting masking revenue weakness)

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence would require: order backlog stabilisation commentary + PMI recovery above 50 + OEM capex guidance upgrades — currently NONE of the three are present

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

  • Means: Interpump distribution channel is structurally two-tier in several geographies
  • Motive: distributors protect their own working capital; rational short-term incentive

  • Verdict: All three legs present

  • Peer margin compression (Parker, Eaton, Danfoss) confirming industry-wide fixed cost burden

  • Plausibility: High plausibility — operating leverage is a structural feature of Interpump's manufacturing model; gross margin sensitivity to volume is high (estimated 55-65% incremental margins); prior cycles show 400-600bps EBIT compression during troughs

  • Means/Motive/Opportunity:

  • Means: structurally high fixed cost base in manufacturing
  • Motive: N/A (not an agent-driven failure — structural)
  • Opportunity: volume decline is the trigger

  • Verdict: Two-of-three (Motive N/A — structural mechanism) — activate as full watch given structural nature

  • Monitoring checklist:

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence requires either a variable cost structure Interpump has NOT explicitly described OR margin resilience during a genuine volume trough — the latter has not been tested since 2020 (COVID) which was short-duration

  • Plausibility: High plausibility — FALSE FRIEND scenarios have the highest base rate of undetected risk precisely because they are invisible in the near-term data; industrial sector earnings-beat-followed-by-sharp-miss episodes following cycle peaks are extremely common; Parker Hannifin, Eaton, SMC have all exhibited this pattern; base rate = very high (majority of industrial cycle peaks feature this mechanism)
