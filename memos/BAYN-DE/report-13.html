§1 — Current Management Guidance

Management issued FY2025 and medium-term guidance at the Q4 2024 results release (5 March 2025) and reconfirmed at the Annual Report publication. The guidance set is quantified but heavily conditioned on Crop Science stabilisation and litigation cost containment.

  • Net verdict: Guidance is quantified but structurally optimistic — FY2025 midpoints imply material H2 weighting and continued Crop Science recovery that the L4-period track record does not support [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Management guided FY2025 group sales of €46–49bn, implying ~flat to +3% growth vs FY2024 actual of ~€46.6bn; currency-neutral growth ~2–4% [#A]

  • Sub: Pharmaceuticals targeted at €18.5–19.5bn; Crop Science €22–23bn; Consumer Health €5.5–6bn

  • FY current — EBITDA: FY2025 EBITDA (pre-exceptionals, pre-amortisation — "EBITDA before special items") guided at €10.0–10.5bn, implying margin recovery to ~21–22% from FY2024 ~20% [#A]

  • Sub: Margin recovery embeds ~€400–600m Crop Science gross margin improvement as primary driver

  • FY current — EPS: Core EPS guided at €5.00–5.50 for FY2025; this is "core" (pre-amortisation, pre-impairments, pre-litigation specials) and thus materially higher than reported EPS [#A]

  • Sub: Reported EPS has been negative in FY2023 (impairments) and near zero in FY2024; guidance is on a "core" definition that strips out the most visible pain

  • FY current — FCF: FCF guidance ~€1.5–2.0bn for FY2025; management uses "free cash flow" pre-litigation settlement payments; post-settlement FCF materially lower and not explicitly guided [#A]

  • Sub: Distinction between pre- and post-settlement FCF is a significant caveat; settlement cash outflows have run €1–2bn/year in recent periods

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guided at ~€2.5–2.8bn, roughly flat vs FY2024 actual; Pharma pipeline investment maintained, Crop Science capacity held [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y: No formal 2026 point guidance; management indicates "mid-single-digit revenue CAGR" and "EBITDA margin recovery toward 22–24% by 2026" as directional ambition [#A]

  • Sub: N2Y revenue implies ~€48–51bn; N2Y EBITDA implies ~€10.5–12bn — the upper bound is a significant stretch vs FY2024 base

  • Medium-term — N3Y: 2027 strategic targets (from November 2023 Capital Markets Day, partially revised): revenue €50–53bn, EBITDA margin 24–26%, Core EPS €6–7 [#A]

  • Sub: These CMD targets were set before the full scale of the Crop Science pricing collapse became visible; market treats them as aspirational not anchor

  • Source — investor day: CMD November 2023 is the primary medium-term framework; FY2025 specific guidance from Q4 2024 results call (March 2025) [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: CEO Bill Anderson (from August 2023) has re-anchored guidance language toward "dynamic allocation" and "efficiency first" rather than growth-led targets — a tone shift vs prior CEO Baumann-era guidance [#A]

  • Update timing: Bayer typically updates FY guidance at Q1 results (May), Q2 results (August), and Q3 results (November); Q1 2025 update is the next credibility checkpoint [#A]

  • Caveat: Guidance explicitly excludes "material Roundup/PCB litigation settlements above provisioned amounts" and assumes "no significant FX headwinds beyond current spot" [#A]

  • Sub: These are the two largest exogenous risk factors; excluding them from guidance framing is aggressive

  • Conditional assumption: Crop Science H2 2025 volume recovery is the single most load-bearing conditional — management acknowledges H1 will be soft but assumes pricing stabilises and volumes recover in H2 [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that management's FY2025 guidance is structurally back-end loaded — H1 weakness is flagged as known but "recovery in H2" is the assumed healing mechanism; the mechanism for that recovery (pricing stabilisation in Brazil/North America, generic glyphosate floor) is the same assumption that was embedded in the 2023 and 2024 guidance sets, both of which proved wrong [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net verdict: Bayer management is a structural over-promiser — the pattern across FY2022–FY2024 is consistent initial optimism followed by intra-year downgrades, not low guidance followed by delivery upside [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal: Initial guidance consistently set at the midpoint or above of what is ultimately achievable; the "bridge" from guidance to outcome runs consistently downward, not upward [#J]

  • Sub: Three consecutive years of EBITDA misses vs initial guidance is statistical evidence of a systematic upward bias, not random noise

  • Sandbagger signal: Absent — no period in L4Y shows a pattern of conservative guidance followed by material beat; the only "beat" period (FY2022 revenue) was commodity-price driven and coincided with an EBITDA miss [#A]

  • Beat consistency: Revenue: 50%; EBITDA: 25%; EPS: 25%; FCF: 0% — no metric shows sandbagging-consistent beat rates (which would require ≥70% beat frequency) [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: EBITDA misses when they occur average ~-7% vs initial guidance; EPS misses average ~-12%; these are material relative to a 5-15 position concentrated portfolio where EPS trajectory is the thesis pillar [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: No EBITDA or EPS beats of material size in L4Y; the FY2021 EBITDA beat of +1.8% is within rounding margin and does not constitute evidence of sandbagging [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: Not mixed — directionally consistent over-promise; the only question is whether the over-promise is intentional (management incentive alignment with optimistic targets) or a genuine inability to forecast the business [#J]

  • Pattern shift: Under new CEO Bill Anderson (from August 2023), guidance language has become more cautious in tone ("we are focused on delivery not promise") but the numerical record since August 2023 (FY2023 full miss, FY2024 cut + miss) does not yet confirm behavioural change [#A]

  • Sub: It is too early (two reporting periods under Anderson) to confirm whether guidance credibility is structurally improving

  • Improving accuracy: No — FY2024 guidance was cut intra-year and still came in near the lower bound of the revised range; this is improvement in the sense of "smaller miss vs revised guidance" but not vs initial guidance [#A]

  • Incentive structure note: Management compensation is linked to "core EPS" and "EBITDA before special items" — these are the same metrics where guidance consistently misses; the incentive to guide optimistically on these specific metrics is structurally present [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the over-promise dynamic has two compounding mechanisms: (1) management genuinely cannot forecast Crop Science because glyphosate pricing is exogenous; (2) management is incentivised to set aspirational targets on core EPS/EBITDA because their comp references those metrics; running out of cushion looks likely since the litigation reserve is already €4.5bn and any incremental settlement above that is a direct miss trigger [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Net verdict: Guidance accuracy is deteriorating, not recovering — FY2022–FY2024 shows worsening misses on EBITDA and EPS vs the FY2021 baseline, and FY2024 only "met" guidance because it was cut twice first [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY2024 EBITDA came in ~€10.0bn vs initial guidance midpoint ~€10.85bn (pre-cut) — a ~-8% miss vs initial; "meeting revised guidance" is not evidence of accuracy improvement [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY2021 EBITDA +1.8% beat represents the high-water mark; FY2022 -5.8%, FY2023 -14%, FY2024 ~-8% vs initial — the trend line is negative even acknowledging FY2023 as the cyclical low [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating: Three factors are driving deterioration: (1) Crop Science structural repricing (glyphosate generic competition) introduced a new source of earnings volatility management did not model accurately; (2) litigation provisioning methodology was repeatedly insufficient; (3) Pharma pipeline transition (Xarelto patent erosion vs. Nubeqa/Kerendia ramp) creates genuine timing uncertainty [#J]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Glyphosate pricing is set by commodity markets and Chinese generic supply — genuinely exogenous; management's inability to forecast this is partly structural, not purely a management quality failure [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Margin guidance misses in Crop Science (FY2022 input cost inflation, FY2023 pricing collapse) reflect both macro and internal cost control failure — the split is approximately 60/40 macro vs execution in analyst commentary [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: Three-division conglomerate (Pharmaceuticals, Crop Science, Consumer Health) with divergent cyclicality makes aggregate EBITDA guidance intrinsically less reliable than a single-business issuer; same problem exists at peers like BASF [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Revenue guidance has been most accurate (macro tailwinds/headwinds visible earlier); EBITDA margin guidance is the least accurate metric due to input cost and pricing mix uncertainty [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF guidance is structurally the worst — 0-for-4 in L4Y; management's FCF definition excludes litigation outflows, creating a systematic gap between guided FCF and cash the company actually generates and retains [#A]

  • Trend outlook: Even under Anderson's more cautious guidance posture, the underlying business complexity and litigation tail mean that accuracy improvement requires structural resolution of Crop Science pricing and litigation, not just communication discipline [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that FY2024's apparent "improvement" (meeting revised guidance) is a low-quality signal — the bar was lowered twice before year-end; genuine accuracy would require meeting the guidance set in February/March at year-end without intra-year cuts; Bayer has not achieved that in any period since FY2021 [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Net verdict: The 2025 guidance rests on three fragile assumptions — Crop Science H2 recovery, contained litigation cash outflows, and Pharma new product ramp — each individually risky; a two-of-three simultaneous failure is a plausible scenario [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Crop Science assumes glyphosate and herbicide pricing stabilises and volumes recover in H2 2025 in Brazil and North America — this is the most fragile single revenue assumption given generic supply overhang and Brazil channel destocking [#A]

  • Sub: Brazilian soy farmer economics (soymeal/soyoil prices) are the proximate driver of herbicide demand; current soy price levels are below breakeven for many Brazilian producers

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Pharmaceuticals assumes Nubeqa (darolutamide) reaches €2.5–3.0bn in peak-year revenue trajectory; FY2024 Nubeqa run-rate ~€1.8bn — bridge requires ~40% growth [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-JPMorgan] flags that Nubeqa competitive set is intensifying with J&J's Erleada holding market share in mCRPC

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Consumer Health assumes mid-single-digit organic growth driven by Claritin, Aleve, and Bepanthen — relatively low-risk assumption vs peer category growth; this is the most credible component of the guidance [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Crop Science margin recovery assumes ~€400–600m of cost-out from Anderson "Dynamic Shared Ownership" restructuring; only ~€150m of confirmed savings have been achieved and validated against budget [#A]

  • Sub: ❌ The gap between announced restructuring savings and confirmed/validated savings is ~€300m — a material assumption shortfall embedded in the EBITDA bridge

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Pharmaceuticals Xarelto erosion in Europe and Japan was guided as "manageable, €200–300m EBITDA headwind" in FY2025; actual erosion pace has consistently exceeded initial estimates since 2022 [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Core EPS guidance uses an assumed tax rate of ~23–25%; actual effective tax rates have been volatile (FY2023 impacted by non-deductible goodwill write-downs); guided tax rate is plausible but sensitive [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count ~982m shares; no buyback guided; dilution from employee equity plans ~0.3–0.5% annually; not a major sensitivity [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Guidance assumes ~current FX rates (USD/EUR ~1.08–1.10); 10% USD appreciation vs EUR would add ~€300–400m to reported revenue but ~€100–150m to EBITDA (partial offset from USD-denominated input costs in Crop Science) [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Glyphosate pricing assumption not explicitly stated; embedded in Crop Science margin recovery; analyst estimates suggest guided margin recovery requires glyphosate pricing to stabilise at ~$450–500/tonne (vs recent spot ~$380–420) [#A]

  • Sub: ❌ The glyphosate pricing assumption appears to be above current spot by ~10–15% — this is a live guidance risk as of Q1 2025

  • Litigation assumption: Management has provisioned ~€4.5bn for outstanding Roundup/PCB/Dichlorprop litigation; guidance assumes no material outflow above this amount in FY2025; expert estimates (external legal sources) suggest possible exposure in the €5–8bn range [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Net verdict: Overall guidance credibility: LOW — the combination of systematic over-promise, four consecutive FCF misses, two EBITDA misses >5%, and SS consensus below management midpoints constitutes a low-credibility guidance regime [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: LOW — defined as: current guidance midpoints should be treated as aspirational upper bounds, not achievable base cases; the probability-weighted outcome is below the management midpoint for all major metrics [#J]

  • Red flag — recent miss: ❌ Three consecutive EBITDA misses vs initial guidance (FY2022, FY2023, FY2024); four consecutive FCF misses; FY2023 Core EPS miss of -35% — together, the most damaging red flag cluster [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: ❌ Crop Science H2 recovery embedded in FY2025 guidance is the same "H2 recovery" narrative embedded in FY2024 and FY2023 guidance — both of which failed to materialise as expected [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: ❌ Glyphosate pricing assumption is above current spot; Brazilian farming demand is deteriorating; FX sensitivity is underweighted in guidance [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: ❌ Restructuring savings (Anderson's DSO programme) have been announced but not yet fully validated against actual delivery; historical restructuring programmes at Bayer (post-Monsanto) came in under-target on timeline [#A]

  • Green flag — recent beat: No genuine EBITDA or EPS beat vs initial guidance in FY2022–FY2024; FY2021 marginal beat was in a more benign cycle [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Consumer Health guidance is the one segment where guidance has been consistently met or marginally exceeded — low-risk assumption set for that unit [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: CEO Anderson's cost-discipline messaging and the pace of announced restructuring (global headcount reduction ~5,000+) are positive directional signals — but signals, not delivery [#A]

  • Margin of safety — low: Investment thesis is highly sensitive to guidance delivery — at current valuation (EV/EBITDA ~7x FY2024), a 10% EBITDA miss would not be fatal to valuation; but a 10% EBITDA miss combined with a downward EPS trajectory revision would challenge the "cheap on normalised earnings" thesis [#J]

  • Sub: The 25%+ IRR thesis requires not just that Bayer is cheap but that earnings normalise — and normalisation requires management guidance credibility to recover

  • Thesis sensitivity: If Crop Science EBITDA is permanently impaired at €2.5–3.0bn (vs pre-impairment ~€4–4.5bn), the group EBITDA normalisation case collapses; this is the single largest binary thesis risk [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on credibility is that LOW credibility is currently priced into the market (BAYN trades at deep discount to European pharma and ag peers), but the risk is not that credibility is merely low — it is that credibility continues to deteriorate as Anderson's restructuring savings are revealed to be insufficient, requiring a further strategic reset (potential Crop Science divestiture, Consumer Health spin) that creates multi-year uncertainty and prevents any earnings normalisation within the 18M–3Y investment horizon [#J]
