SECTION A — BUSINESS QUALITYF

A1 — What Hexatronic Actually Is

Three businesses, very different competitive positions. Q1 2026 confirmed the portfolio rotation: DC = 46% of group adjusted EBITA for the first time.

Fibre Solutions (~58% Q1 2026 revenue, ~32% EBITA): The legacy business in structural transition. Q1 2026: SEK 982m, organic -11% (European FTTH phase transition from "homes passed" to "subscriber connections"), EBITA margin 6.2% (above 6% floor, PIP ahead of schedule). US Fibre Solutions positive organic despite BEAD headwinds — private-sector DC-adjacent demand is the offset. BEAD quantified at 63% fibre share (not ~100% under original rules) — fibre TAM within BEAD ~37% smaller than original assumptions, construction mostly 2027+. Chinese manufacturers (Hengtong) establishing EU manufacturing within 18–36 months — the logistics buffer is being removed. Floor at 6–7% probably structural rather than transitional. RedEye flags "inconsistent historical returns on capital" — ROIC has not been reliably above cost of capital through prior downturns.

Harsh Environment (~17% Q1 2026 revenue, ~22% EBITA): Q1 2026: SEK 283m, organic +9%, EBITA 8.5% (below 11–14% late-2025 range, JOWO dilution timing). JOWO closed April 1 at 4.7x EBITDA — rational pricing. Post-JOWO, HE approaching SEK 1.5bn annualised pro forma. Defence capex tailwind (NATO 2%+ GDP) is structural and multi-year. SRCA is real — qualification barriers, certification relationships, bespoke design IP. On track toward SEK 2.0bn 2028 target.

Data Centre (~26% Q1 2026 revenue, 46% EBITA): Q1 2026: SEK 434m, organic +20%, EBITA 16.8% (above 15% target). DC is now the largest profit contributor — Stage 1 thesis confirmed. Two new Q2 2026 developments: (1) Superior Fiber & Data Services acquisition — Enterprise DC focus, explicitly reducing hyperscaler dependency; (2) SEK 600m rights issue at 8.2% discount to fund M&A pipeline. DC addressable market recalibrated post-Corning: regional/Enterprise DC tier, not US hyperscaler top tier. Prysmian Digital Solutions at 19.5% EBITDA margin confirms the European regional DC market is large and profitable. DC "boom-and-bust" risk acknowledged by RedEye — service model (shorter cycles, managed services) is the structural defence but not yet proven at scale.

A2 — Competitive Position

Fibre Solutions: Narrow moat, structurally challenged. Chinese EU manufacturing entry (18–36 months) removes logistics buffer. Floor at 6% confirmed near-term; susceptible to further erosion. Assessment unchanged.

Harsh Environment: Strong moat. JOWO adds German defence connector capability. On track. Assessment unchanged.

Data Centre — significantly revised post-AS research:

Corning's competitive position is more severe than [C] research indicated:

  • Meta $6bn + Nvidia $3.2bn + two additional hyperscaler LTAs of similar scale = Corning likely anchors 3–4 of the top 5 US hyperscaler DC connectivity relationships
  • Corning has a Poland factory serving EU customers — geographic buffer is NOT a complete protection
  • Prysmian is simultaneously securing its own hyperscaler LTAs (€5bn+ announced)
  • Corning estimated >50% market share at several major US hyperscalers already

HTRO's achievable DC market is explicitly the regional/Enterprise DC tier. The Superior Fiber & Data Services acquisition confirms management has made this strategic choice explicitly. This is the right strategic response. The Enterprise DC market is large, growing, and profitable — but it is a smaller total addressable market than "all DC connectivity."

Revised DC moat assessment: Early-stage moat in the correct sub-markets. The Corning/Prysmian hyperscaler ceiling is now structural. HTRO's DC growth ceiling has been lowered, but the floor has been raised (Enterprise DC economics are confirmed attractive, managed services reduce cyclicality).

A3 — Management Quality [FINAL — GOVERNANCE CONCERN ADDED]

Fröberg execution: Positive signals maintained. PIP ahead of schedule. JOWO at 4.7x EBITDA (rational). Communication Zone and Superior Fiber & Data Services acquired in 2026 — DC M&A engine running. Q1 2026 call commentary confident and precise. Leadership style: visionary pivot (RedEye), transparent communication, lean decentralised organisation.

Governance concern — LTIP 2026 WITHDRAWN:

  • The proposed LTIP 2026, which included 2028 targets (SEK 8.7–11.7bn revenue, 10.5–12.5% EBITA margin), was withdrawn at the May 12, 2026 AGM
  • The reasons for withdrawal are not disclosed in available sources
  • Three possible interpretations: shareholder opposition (negative), board preemptive withdrawal (neutral-negative), replacement with alternative structure (neutral)
  • If there is no replacement equity incentive linking Fröberg's personal wealth to the 2028 targets, the alignment case is materially weaker than assumed
  • This is the single most important unanswered management question

Additional governance concern (RedEye): No controlling shareholder with >20% voting stake, limited board member personal shareholdings. Reduces the concentrated long-term stewardship associated with European small-cap outperformance.

Management assessment revised: Overall management quality remains B, but the LTIP withdrawal introduces a governance tail risk not previously in the model. The alignment confidence is now B- rather than B at the management layer specifically.
