Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Bucher Industries holds world-leading market shares in specialized niches of agricultural, municipal, and glass manufacturing machinery, backed by resilient aftermarket spare parts revenue.

Divisional Business ActivitiesBucher Industries operates five distinct divisions, each acting as a market leader within its specific technological niche. Kuhn Group manufactures specialized farm implements for tillage, crop protection, and livestock bedding, while Bucher Municipal produces street sweepers and winter clearing vehicles. Bucher Hydraulics designs electrohydraulic systems, and Bucher Emhart Glass manufactures machines for glass container production. Bucher Specials rounds out the portfolio with winemaking and fruit juice equipment. Deutsche Bank Research notes that these five business segments collectively cover a highly diversified array of industrial equipment applications, reducing exposure to any single customer group.

1.
Specialized agricultural machinery: Kuhn Group manufactures specialised agricultural machinery for tillage, seeding, fertilisation, crop protection, and hay and forage harvesting, accounting for 36.00% of 2025 group sales
2.
Leading municipal vehicles: Bucher Municipal engineers municipal vehicles for street cleaning, snow clearing, sewer cleaning, and refuse collection, comprising compact sweepers and representing 21.00% of 2025 sales
3.
High-performance hydraulic systems: Bucher Hydraulics manufactures custom electrohydraulic systems, pumps, valves, and compact power units for agricultural and construction equipment, contributing 21.00% of 2025 sales
4.
Dominant glass forming: Bucher Emhart Glass provides advanced glass forming and inspection machinery, spare parts, and consulting services for the global glass container industry, representing 13.00% of 2025 group sales
5.
Niche process technologies: Bucher Specials encompasses winemaking grape presses, fruit juice extraction systems, Swiss agricultural machinery distribution, and automation controls, contributing 11.00% of 2025 sales

Revenue Models and Customer DynamicsThe company's revenue model is split between capital equipment sales and highly profitable aftermarket services. Product sales are transactional and cyclical, driven by agricultural incomes and glass manufacturer capex budgets. However, aftermarket spare parts and maintenance services provide a massive recurring profit pool, with "MyKUHN" and "Bucher Connect" digital platforms locking in customers. The customer base is extremely fragmented, particularly in agriculture, where independent dealers act as distributors, mitigating the threat of customer concentration. This decentralized structure ensures that decisions are made close to the customer, supporting high market share.

6.
Transactional machinery sales: The majority of revenue is transactional, generated through the sale of high-performance capital equipment to agricultural dealers, municipalities, and global glass container manufacturers
7.
Resilient aftermarket spare-parts: A vital buffer against cyclicality is the highly profitable spare parts and maintenance services business, which remains relatively stable during downturns in new machine orders
8.
Fragmented customer base: Customers are highly diversified, ranging from thousands of independent agricultural dealers in Kuhn Group to various local government municipalities and global packaging corporations

Competitive Moat and Industry StructureBucher sits in a profit-favoured position in its value chains, holding the top market position in several global niches. Competitive advantages are protected by proprietary engineering IP, dense service networks, and high switching costs. In glass forming, Emhart Glass dominates as the undisputed world leader with major technological moats in automation. The industry structure is consolidated in glass and hydraulics, while agriculture remains highly consolidated among key global machinery groups.

9.
Technological product moats: Competitive advantages are driven by proprietary engineering technology and high switching costs, particularly in automated glass container equipment and precision agricultural systems
10.
Consolidated market structure: The industry structure is consolidated in glass forming where Bucher Emhart Glass operates as the clear world market leader in glass forming machines
11.
Cyclical end markets: End markets are structurally supported by global population growth, urbanization, and sustainable food demands, though they remain highly cyclical over three-to-five year periods
12.
Other side of the story: High reliance on capital expenditure budgets makes new machinery demand extremely vulnerable to interest rate hikes and farming subsidy cuts, forcing deep production adjustments during cyclical troughs

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

European Agricultural Demand ReboundThe forward earnings trajectory is heavily dependent on Kuhn Group, which accounts for more than one-third of group net sales. Demand in Europe is showing clear signs of recovery as farmers' willingness to invest improves, driven by higher crop yields and solid livestock prices. Kuhn's European order intake rose significantly by 19.90% adjusted for currency effects in 2025, suggesting that dealer destocking is nearing completion. This volume inflection directly feeds through to high-operating leverage, propelling segment EBIT margins back toward the mid-term target of 12.00%.

1.
Forward agricultural recovery: A key driver is Kuhn Group's demand rebound, directly impacting net sales and operating profit margins as European farmers' willingness to invest improves
2.
Medium driver plausibility: Plausibility is medium, backed by Kuhn's order intake growing 19.90% adjusted for currency effects in 2025, though US trade policy and low crop prices could delay a broader global recovery
3.
Rebound failure mechanism: The driver would fail if grain and oilseed prices continue to decline, discouraging farmers from capital purchases and extending the dealer destocking cycle

Capital Allocation and EPS AccretionThe second core driver is the completed 4.00% share buyback program, which directly enhances the EPS trajectory. Shareholders approved the capital reduction at the April 2026 AGM, reducing the outstanding shares from 10.25 million to 9.84 million. This structural reduction guarantees a 4.00% mechanical boost to EPS, even under flat net profit conditions. Additionally, the massive net cash of CHF 498.20 million allows for continued consistent dividend payments and opportunistic bolt-on M&A.

4.
EPS accretion driver: Capital reduction via the completed 4.00% share buyback program directly supports EPS growth, reducing the share count from 10,250,000 to 9,840,000 registered shares
5.
High buyback plausibility: Plausibility is high as shareholders approved the cancellation of 410,000 treasury shares at the April 2026 annual general meeting, making EPS accretion certain

Aftermarket and Municipal StabilityDefensive characteristics protect Bucher during cyclical troughs, primarily through Bucher Municipal and high-margin spare parts. Bucher Municipal achieved stable sales in 2025 and improved its operating profit margin to 9.40%, showing that municipal demand is relatively insulated from agricultural cycles. Aftermarket spare parts across Kuhn and Emhart Glass remain highly defensive, providing steady gross profit margins. This prevents material EPS collapses when capital equipment orders are postponed, ensuring a more predictable forward trajectory.

High aftermarket plausibility: Plausibility is high, evidenced by sweepers and sewer vehicles showing steady backlogs and aftermarket spare parts remaining highly defensive during prior downturns

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The bear case is moderately credible due to persistent overcapacities in global glass container manufacturing and potential margin pressure from US import trade tariffs.

Key Structural and Macro RisksBucher is exposed to several macro and structural risks that directly impact its forward earnings trajectory. A primary structural concern is the continued overcapacity in global glass container manufacturing, which has caused glass makers to severely restrict new capital purchases, causing Emhart Glass sales to decline by 19.20% in 2025. On the macro side, US import trade tariffs present a major threat to Kuhn Group's North American business, raising raw material purchase costs and hampering dealer pre-orders. While no active short-seller campaigns or high short interest exist, these structural headwinds require close cost-cutting discipline.

1.
Published short reports: Research did not surface any published short-seller reports or unusually high short interest in Bucher Industries shares
2.
Glass capacity risks: Continued overcapacity at glass manufacturers directly hurts Bucher Emhart Glass, threatening to depress division sales which fell 19.20% in 2025
3.
US import tariffs: Rising purchase prices from US import tariffs directly squeeze Kuhn Group margins, requiring further capacity adjustments and factory job cuts in North America

Failure Modes and Warning SignalsThe single worst failure mode is a synchronized cyclical downturn in both agricultural machinery and automated packaging equipment. Such a downturn would overwhelm local cost-saving programs, restrict cash generation, and depress margins below the mid-term 10.00% group target. A key "false friend" is a temporary elongation of the order book caused by logistics bottlenecks, which can mimic strong demand while underlying order volumes contract. To monitor this, investors must track monthly European commodity grain prices as a leading indicator of farm equipment demand.

4.
Severe failure mode: The single failure mode that would most damage the case is a synchronized agriculture and packaging downturn, restricting cash
5.
False friend signals: A potential "false friend" is a temporary rise in order book length due to logistics delays, which might mask underlying customer demand weakness
6.
Key warning indicators: The single early-warning signal worth monitoring is monthly European grain and commodity prices, which directly dictate the willingness of farmers to buy machinery
7.
Other side of the story: The risks are likely overstated because Bucher's high aftermarket spare-parts sales share provides an immediate profit buffer, preventing major cash margin erosion
