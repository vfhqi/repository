Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Trelleborg represents a highly transformed polymer pure-play with record-high EBITA margins of 18.3% and a clear path to 20.0% over the next three years, driven by aerospace and medical solutions.
1.
Structural evolution: The company operates as a decentralized polymer specialist, generating revenue from critical components with high switching costs where product value is high but relative cost is low
2.
Investment thesis: We believe Trelleborg is a notably stronger franchise today than in its history due to the divestment of Wheel Systems, which has reduced earnings volatility and unlocked capital for share buybacks
3.
Predictable expansion: The most critical swing factor for forward earnings is the acceleration of organic growth in the core fluid power hydraulics segment and the integration of Minnesota Rubber & Plastics
4.
Financial performance: Trelleborg delivered solid execution in 2025 with record EBITA of SEK 6.29B, representing an 18.3% margin, up from 18.0% in 2024
5.
Core vulnerability: The primary risk to the investment case is a prolonged global cyclical downturn that depresses short-cycle industrial demand across its diversified industrial markets
6.
Initial verdict: A closer look is strongly warranted because of Trelleborg's record profitability, solid 95% cash conversion, and net debt/EBITDA of 1.0x, providing massive capacity for capital return

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Trelleborg's high-margin decentralized model focuses on engineered polymer components across three business areas, establishing strong niche leadership and deep customer integration with high switching costs.

Segment and Product Breakdown

1.
Segment structure: Trelleborg operates three business areas since April 2024: Sealing Solutions (48% of sales), Industrial Solutions (43%), and the newly established Medical Solutions (9%)
2.
Product offering: Sealing Solutions provides safety-critical precision seals, bearings, and custom polymer components for demanding environments, including aerospace and industrial applications
3.
Industrial solutions: The Industrial Solutions area delivers polymer-based critical solutions, including protective boots, marine fenders, docking equipment, and sustainable water infrastructure
4.
Medical specialized: Medical Solutions, formed in 2024, specializes in engineered polymer solutions for medical devices, medtech, and disposable biopharma products

Revenue Model and Customer Dynamics

5.
Value-added pricing: Trelleborg operates a "high value by performance, low cost by total spend" pricing model, enabling strong pricing power since its solutions are critical to customer end-product performance
6.
Low concentration: The customer base is highly diversified across industries, with 59% of sales in diversified industrials, 18% in automotive niches, 12% in aerospace, and 11% in medical sectors in 2025
7.
Local presence: The company utilizes a "local-for-local" production and sales model, minimizing the impact of international trade barriers and global tariffs

Competitive Advantage and Industry Structure

8.
High barriers: High competitive moats are sustained by high switching costs, proprietary material formulations with over 2,000 active compounds, and deep co-engineering with OEMs
9.
Consolidation leader: The polymer industry is highly fragmented, allowing Trelleborg to act as a consolidator by acquiring high-margin niche players like Baron Group and Gomet
10.
Other side of the story: A potential risk is that competitors in low-cost regions could replicate standard polymer profiles, dragging down pricing power in less-specialized industrial application niches

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case materially strengthens as Trelleborg deploys its massive balance sheet capacity into high-margin acquisitions while expanding organic exposure to high-growth aerospace and medical segments.

High-Growth Aerospace and Medical Integration

1.
Segment growth: Trelleborg is shifting its sales mix toward high-growth niches including aerospace and medtech, targeting a sales growth rate above 8% per year over the cycle
2.
Aerospace demand: Sales to the aerospace industry are demonstrating robust global growth, driven by a substantial order book and high-performance material demand
3.
Medtech expansion: Medical Solutions is expanding capacity via its newly certified Costa Rica facility, capitalizing on strong medtech demand in Europe and North America

M&A Execution and Synergy Capture

4.
Synergy realization: Earnings growth will be driven by the extraction of cost and revenue synergies from the Minnesota Rubber & Plastics and Baron Group acquisitions
5.
Capital deployment: A strong balance sheet allows for continuous bolt-on acquisitions and ongoing share buybacks at a run rate of SEK 500M per quarter
6.
Compound probability: The bull case requires successful integration of multiple bolt-on acquisitions alongside structural margin improvement to the 20% group target
7.
Other side of the story: The core investment case would fail if the integration of Minnesota Rubber fails to yield the targeted synergies, leaving the Sealing Solutions forecasts overly optimistic

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Trelleborg's shares look fairly valued at approximately 13.5x to 15.0x EV/EBITA, reflecting the structural margin improvements but capping further near-term re-rating potential.
1.
Trading multiples: Trelleborg trades at an adjusted EV/EBITA of approximately 14.5x for 2026e, representing a premium of about 10% to the overall Nordic capital goods sector
2.
Peer premium: The stock trades at a premium of approximately 13% relative to its Swedish capital goods peer group, which reflects its structurally superior profit margins
3.
Target price: Sell-side analysts at DNB Carnegie maintain a Hold rating with a target price of SEK 390.00, valuing the firm at approximately 12.5x to 13.0x 2027e EV/EBITA
4.
Peer comparison: Industrial composite competitors like Michelin trade at a significant discount at 8.7x EV/EBIT, while Trelleborg commands a superior multiple of over 14.0x due to its high-margin non-tire assets
5.
Market pricing: The current share price of SEK 394.80 suggests that the market has fully priced in the structural portfolio improvements and robust cash generation, leaving limited near-term upside
6.
Other side of the story: If Trelleborg successfully accelerates organic growth above general industrial production trends while achieving its 20% margin target, a material re-rating opportunity remains possible

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderately cyclical given Trelleborg's short-cycle industrial exposure, but its decentralized operational structure and strong balance sheet provide robust downside protection.

Cyclical End-Market Vulnerability

1.
Business cycle: As a short-cycle company with over 50% exposure to general diversified industry, Trelleborg faces earnings risk from a sudden slowdown in global industrial production
2.
Integration risks: Failure to extract the anticipated synergies from the large Minnesota Rubber & Plastics acquisition could lead to lower-than-expected margins in Sealing Solutions
3.
False friend: Sequential order book expansion can act as a false friend if project delivery delays in Industrial Solutions persist, masking underlying revenue weakness

Currency and Regulatory Risks

4.
Currency exposure: The company has significant foreign exchange translation risk since the vast majority of its sales are generated outside of Sweden
5.
Transition costs: Ongoing regulatory developments, such as EU carbon taxes and chemical restrictions, could increase operational costs for Eurocentric production sites
6.
Short-seller interest: Research does not surface any published short-seller reports or unusually high short interest in Trelleborg's shares
7.
Other side of the story: The risk of global trade barriers is highly overstated because Trelleborg's "local-for-local" business model ensures that products are manufactured and sold within the same region

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Trelleborg's core leadership remains highly stable with the re-election of long-standing CEO Peter Nilsson and the strategic appointment of Marina Bill to the board in 2026.
1.
Leadership stability: Long-standing CEO Peter Nilsson and CFO Fredrik Nilsson continue to lead the group with stable execution, having overseen the entire portfolio transformation since 2005
2.
Board evolution: At the April 2026 Annual General Meeting, Marina Bill, Global Head of Sales and Commercial Operation at ABB Robotics, was elected as a new board member, replacing Monica Gimre
3.
Incentive alignment: A new performance share plan (PSP 2026/2028) was approved at the 2026 AGM to align executive compensation with long-term earnings per share targets
4.
Capital adaptation: The board authorized continued Series B share buybacks of up to SEK 2.00B through April 2027 to adjust the group's capital structure

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. We recommend a mixed first-look verdict with a tilt toward deeper work on specific aerospace and medical divisions, given Trelleborg's high-margin profile but fair current valuation.
1.
Strongest argument for: Trelleborg represents a structurally superior business with record 18.4% EBITA margins, excellent cash conversion, and a strong balance sheet capable of supporting significant capital return
2.
Other side of the story: The strongest argument against is that the shares already trade at a premium to Nordic capital goods peers, fully pricing in the portfolio transformation and leaving a demanding valuation margin
3.
Next step action: Investors should verify the progress of the Minnesota Rubber integration and confirm whether the targeted synergies are being realized in the Sealing Solutions division
4.
Operational verification: Investors must monitor the recovery of delayed large-scale marine and LNG project shipments in the Industrial Solutions division during the second half of the year
