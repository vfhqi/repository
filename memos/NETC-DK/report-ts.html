Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Netcompany represents a highly resilient, platform-led growth engine that is taking market share in the European IT services market.
1.
Differentiated business model: Generates high-margin revenue by delivering complex public and private sector systems through an efficient fixed-fee model that incentivizes on-time and on-budget delivery
2.
Compelling investment thesis: Uniquely positioned to drive a multi-year EPS expansion by replicating its highly profitable Danish platform suite across larger European markets while capturing substantial integration cost synergies from its recent Nordic banking merger
3.
Critical margin driver: Successfully capturing planned cost synergies within Netcompany Banking Services is the single most critical swing factor for management to achieve forward profitability targets
4.
Outstanding historical growth: Revenue CAGR of 19.4% between 2015 and 2025, significantly outgrowing traditional Nordic peers
5.
Severe execution hazard: Central threat is a potential inability to successfully export the company's unique Danish delivery model into larger, relationship-driven European public sector environments
6.
Highly promising verdict: A closer look is highly warranted because Netcompany's front-footed shift to agentic AI and proprietary IP creates a highly predictable EPS trajectory that isolates it from standard offshore IT consulting disruption

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The company leverages proprietary software platforms and a fixed-price delivery model to command a highly competitive, profit-favoured position.
1.
Dominant Danish core: Denmark remains the foundational market, delivering high-margin private and public sector system integration projects that establish a strong reference library for international expansion
2.
Highly scalable GovTech: The South East Europe and EU Institutions segment leverages acquired Intrasoft capabilities to digitize complex tax, customs, and border systems across Europe
3.
Accelerating UK traction: Netcompany UK has emerged as a high-growth segment – secured Transit Customs Solution contract with the public sector; grew constant-currency revenue by 51.4% in Q1 2026
4.
Targeted Netherlands growth: The Netherlands segment operates as a highly specialized player focused entirely on public sector digital projects
5.
Proprietary software suite: Pre-built platforms including PULSE for real-time data orchestration and AMPLIO for regulated case management accelerate development times
6.
Front-running AI deployment: All developers must use the proprietary Easley AI assistant; newly launched Feniks AI platform optimizes IT development times by up to 45% without compromising compliance or data sovereignty
7.
Regulated banking platform: Netcompany Banking Services provides a core multi-currency infrastructure powering operations for over 50 commercial and regional banks across the Nordics
8.
Efficient delivery architecture: Overhead-light structure where IT professionals scope, price, and directly manage projects – structural sales costs below peer averages
9.
Powerful switching costs: Builds society-critical core infrastructure (central registries, case management portals) that customers face extreme operational risks in considering replacing
10.
Resilient contract pricing: High concentration of fixed-price contracts protects from technology-driven pricing deflation
11.
Intense regional competition: Scandinavian banking IT landscape is highly consolidated – Nordic incumbent Tietoevry acts as primary rival to Netcompany Banking Services
12.
Other side of the story: The rigid fixed-fee pricing model exposes Netcompany to severe margin compression and cost overruns if project complexities or delivery delays arise

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens due to a highly predictable EPS trajectory driven by cost synergies, international expansion, and structural AI productivity gains.
1.
International platform export: Geographical replication of the Danish platform model, exporting pre-built solutions into high-value public sectors across Europe. Medium plausibility – UK achieved 54.3% constant-currency revenue growth in Q1 2026, but localized relationships remain hard to build
2.
Defense vertical scaling: Rollout of the VERÁ AI-enabled platform, targeting the high-growth European defense and hybrid threat resilience market
3.
Structural AI productivity gains: Proprietary Easley AI assistant significantly lowering development costs. Highly credible – sudden upward revision in 2026 group margin guidance following direct internal agentic coding improvements
4.
AI efficiency failure mode: Would fail if aggressive competitive bidding in IT services causes AI-driven savings to be passed entirely to customers
5.
Defense sales cycles: Medium plausibility – Netcompany has secured its first undisclosed contract, but defense procurement cycles with European ministries are notoriously long
6.
Independent catalyst convergence: Bull case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously – cost-synergy path and organic GovTech expansion operate as highly independent commercial engines
7.
Other side of the story: Client relationship concentration in the Danish public sector could severely impair medium-term EPS trajectory if budget constraints lead to delayed or reduced sovereign digitalisation spending

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The bear case remains a credible threat due to talent attrition and AI-driven pricing pressure, though mitigated by strong public sector backlog visibility.
1.
Core delivery risk: Single most critical failure mode is the potential failure of the Danish fixed-fee delivery model in more competitive international markets like the United Kingdom
2.
Severe talent constraints: High employee burnout and escalating churn rates among experienced Danish managers threaten consistency of project delivery as operations rapidly scale
3.
Fixed-fee cost overruns: Large, complex projects expose Netcompany to severe financial losses if delayed, as previously occurred during the slow ramp-up of the UK's DALAS framework
4.
AI contract pricing deflation: Generative AI tools could commoditize basic software programming, driving significant contract pricing erosion
5.
Geopolitical budget delays: Macroeconomic uncertainty and geopolitical turmoil across Europe could cause sudden public sector budget cuts and delays in tendering activity
6.
False friend headcount indicators: Steady headcount growth can mask operational inefficiency when staff are reallocated to non-billing business development during project delays
7.
Primary monitoring signal: Investors must closely monitor quarterly movement in the non-Danish public sector order backlog as a vital early-warning signal for international scaling success
8.
Other side of the story: Heavy exposure to highly regulated, sovereign public contracts provides strong protection against tech-driven budget disruption
