Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Aperam's highly integrated business model combines low-cost upstream scrap collection with downstream distribution, protecting margins and capturing short-cycle price leverage.

Segment Operations and Revenue Mix

1.
Diversified segment structure: The company reports operations across four key segments, which are Stainless & Electrical Steel, Services & Solutions, Alloys & Specialties, and Recycling & Renewables These segments come together under the "One Aperam" banner to create a unique closed-loop platform built for resilience
2.
High-volume stainless core: The Stainless & Electrical Steel segment produces a full range of stainless steel products in Europe and South America, maintaining a flat steel capacity of 2.5 million tonnes European facilities include meltshops in Genk and Châtelet, Belgium, which produce high-quality flat products
3.
Captive distribution frontline: The Services & Solutions division distributes Aperam products directly to end-users, operating 14 steel service centers and 4 transformation facilities to provide value-added processing This segment serves as the strategic frontline of industrial mills, providing real-time market intelligence
4.
High-growth specialty niches: The Alloys & Specialties segment designs and manufactures advanced specialty alloys at its meltshop in Imphy, France, targeting high-margin industrial applications Specialty alloys are designed for cryogenics, aerospace, and energy, priced on a per-kilogram basis
5.
Upstream recycling backbone: The Recycling & Renewables segment, anchored by the ELG acquisition, sources and processes stainless steel and superalloy scrap from over 4,000 global suppliers This division links industrial scrap collectors with meltshops, ensuring raw material security

Value Chain and Competitive Advantage

6.
Integrated circular loop: Aperam's cross-segment collaboration represents a unique closed-loop system where customer scrap is recovered via Services & Solutions to feed upstream meltshops, reducing virgin raw material dependency This cross-segment circular value chain significantly enhances the quality and stability of earnings
7.
Durable cost advantage: Sourcing more than 80% of its raw material from recycled scrap in Europe allows the company to operate a highly efficient electric arc furnace footprint that avoids high carbon tax exposure This electric arc furnace setup is structurally cheaper than blast furnace operations
8.
Unchallenged regional moat: In South America, Aperam operates as the sole integrated producer of flat stainless steel in Brazil, insulated from global scrap volatility by BioEnergia's captive forestry charcoal assets This captive biomass supply acts as both a carbon sink and a highly competitive local energy source
9.
Differentiated price leverage: By operating its own Services & Solutions division, Aperam maintains a short order book of about 2.5 months, allowing it to rapidly capture spot price upswings compared to peers with fixed long-term contracts This direct-to-customer distribution channel promotes excellent capital efficiency
10.
Aerospace mix upgrade: The strategic acquisition of US-based Universal Stainless embeds the company into the high-margin US aerospace value chain, creating complementary alloy product offerings with minimal integration costs Universal's strong footprint enables immediate entry into high-value specialty alloy segments

Customer Base and Industry Structure

11.
Well-diversified customer base: The client base is highly diversified across catering and appliances, transport, building and construction, automotive, and energy, mitigating individual sector cyclicality No single customer represents a concentration risk, with sales spread across both developed and emerging markets
12.
Consolidated peer landscape: The European market is highly consolidated with four major players, Acerinox, Outokumpu, Arvedi Acciai Speciali Terni, and Aperam, competing alongside rising Asian import volumes In Brazil, Aperam enjoys a near-monopoly on local flat stainless production
13.
Other side of the story: Intensifying global overcapacity, led by massive Chinese and Indonesian production expansions from Tsingshan and TISCO-BaoWu, can bypass European trade safeguards and flood South American and regional markets, depressing global base prices

Section 5 – The possible investment case 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case strengthens dramatically due to structural self-help gains and policy-driven price protections that should double EBITDA by 2028.

Structural Self-Help (Leadership Journey Phase 6)

1.
Targeted cost reductions: Driver 1 targets a €150m cumulative EBITDA uplift from 2026 to 2028 through Phase 6 of the Leadership Journey, focusing on automation, scale, and digital transformation This self-help program is designed to secure structural growth and ensure the company remains competitive
2.
Rapid initial execution: Plausibility is high, supported by a strong initial print of €18m in savings delivered in Q1 2026 alone, which represents 12% of the three-year target This rapid cost execution highlights the effectiveness of self-help measures
3.
Potential failure mode: This driver would fail if operational execution bottlenecks or rising raw material input inflation in Europe and Brazil erode targeted efficiency gains High maintenance costs could also slow down targeted productivity improvements

Trade Policy Tailwinds (CBAM and Safeguards)

4.
Regulatory market protection: Driver 2 leverages CBAM (effective January 2026) and tightened safeguard quotas (effective July 2026) to shift European market share from cheap imports back to domestic mills These trade-defence tailwinds are expected to progressively tighten supply
5.
Sizable capacity utilization uplift: Plausibility is high as broker analyses project a 7-10% capacity utilization uplift for European mills, which could translate to roughly €50m in volume-driven earnings growth Tighter trade measures are highly likely to support a powerful pricing rebound
6.
Potential failure mode: This driver would fail if circumvention of trade barriers by Asian producers or delays in CBAM enforcement leave import penetration high Increased low-cost imports would prevent domestic capacity utilization from expanding

High-Value Alloy Diversification (USAP Integration)

7.
Aerospace mix enhancement: Driver 3 leverages the acquisition of Universal Stainless to enter the high-margin US aerospace market, targeting $30m (€27m) in annual synergies within five years The transaction is highly complementary, requiring minimal integration costs
8.
Earnings diversification buffer: Plausibility is high because the combined alloys platform is set to generate €180m in annual EBITDA, significantly reducing overall cyclicality and European exposure This high-value alloy footprint provides a structurally more resilient earnings profile
9.
Potential failure mode: This driver would fail if extended aerospace customer destocking or Boeing production delays prevent the realization of synergy targets
10.
Potential failure mode (cont.): If management has overpaid for this M&A, it would also drag down forward returns

Compound Growth Probability

11.
Independent case drivers: To achieve the mid-cycle EBITDA inflection to €700-800m+ by 2028, the forward drivers do not need to fire simultaneously given they are structurally independent, providing a high compound probability of success
12.
Independent case drivers (cont.): These independent levers provide a powerful, defensive investment setup
13.
Other side of the story: Widespread customer substitution towards cheaper, non-compliant carbon steel or alternative materials could undermine the low-carbon product premiums that Aperam counts on to offset its higher operational costs

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is well mitigated by geographic diversity and captive raw materials, making the bear case of a structural market failure look highly improbable.

Operational and Market Risks

1.
Extreme demand disruption: The single most damaging failure mode is a severe recession in Europe and Brazil, which would suppress base volumes and prevent price recovery A demand pullback across both regions constitutes a major risk
2.
Energy cost volatility: While rising gas and power prices pose a headwind, Aperam is insulated by French nuclear pricing and captive Brazilian charcoal, limiting exposure to high-single-digit millions per quarter This geographic and product diversification protects the group from European energy crises
3.
Unhedged currency exposure: Fluctuations in the Euro against the US Dollar and Brazilian Real can cause material transaction impacts, though mitigated by active hedging policies FX volatility remains a risk to the quarterly numbers
4.
Flawed margin signals: A positive spot price increase can be a "false friend" if it is entirely cost-led by rising raw materials and scrap, failing to deliver actual margin expansion Early-2026 price increases were primarily cost-led rather than demand-driven
5.
Monitoring order backlogs: The single most critical early-warning signal is a sudden decline in European service center order backlogs, indicating that distributors have ceased restocking Distributors currently maintain very low stock levels in Europe
6.
Other side of the story: Regulatory trade protections provide a powerful, inelastic demand floor that should shield domestic volume and utilization rates even if broader industrial demand remains sluggish
