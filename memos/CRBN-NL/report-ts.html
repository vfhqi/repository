Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is strengthened by structural margin expansion and high-margin product growth under the BRIGHT 2030 plan.

Driver 1: Sugar Input Cost Tailwinds and Hedging

1.
Favourable raw materials: Lower sugar prices represent a massive gross margin tailwind, as sugar represents 28.00% of raw material costs (34.00% including corn dextrose), directly expanding the gross margins of both core segments
2.
Extensive price hedging: Plausibility is high because Corbion has aggressively locked in these multi-year lows, hedging 100.00% of its sugar exposure for fiscal year 2026 and 100.00% for fiscal year 2027 to avoid
3.
geopolitical price disruption. This driver could fail if global sugar prices spike after 2027 and Corbion lacks the pricing power to pass costs through

Driver 2: New Circular Thai Lactic Acid Plant Savings

4.
Revolutionary cost leadership: The state-of-the-art circular lactic acid plant in Rayong, Thailand, utilizes chemical-recycling technology that reduces cradle-to-gate greenhouse gas emissions by >30.00% while driving down unit manufacturing costs
5.
Gradual margin step-up: Plausibility is high as the plant is ramping up on schedule, running at an above- 70.00% capacity rhythm in late 2025 and expected to deliver full structural savings in 2026. This driver would fail if technical bottlenecks or equipment downtime prevent the plant from running at optimal operating leverage

Driver 3: High-Margin Algae Omega-3 Portfolio Expansion

6.
Accelerating nutrition sales: Corbion is expanding its algae-derived omega-3 business beyond aquaculture aquafeed into higher-value pet food and human nutrition, targeting a revenue increase from €157.00m in 2024 to €275.00m by 2030
7.
Favourable pricing disconnect: Plausibility is medium to high, supported by rising wild-catch fish oil prices and new long-term customer contracts negotiated at $4,000.00 to $5,000.00 equivalent per ton to decouple from fish oil volatility. This driver would fail if wild fish oil catches surge, collapsing market prices and forcing Corbion to cut its premium pricing

Driver 4: Biomedical Polymers Aesthetics Acceleration

8.
Striving premium niche: The Biomedical Polymers business targets aesthetics, drug delivery, and orthopedics, aiming to reach €125.00m in sales over the next five years, capturing margins well above the group average of ~30.00%
9.
Strategic joint ventures: Plausibility is medium, supported by drug delivery pipeline conversion through the MedinCell joint venture and record volumes in aesthetics, though it could fail if clinical trials stall or regulatory approvals are delayed

Investment Case Synthesis

10.
Independent growth drivers: Crucially, these drivers do not depend on each other to fire together (low compound risk) since sugar hedging, Thai plant cost savings, and biomedical demand are structurally independent, meaning the investment case can succeed even if individual drivers underperform
11.
- Analyst/Investor Other side of the story: Severe competitive pressure in functional food ingredients from restructured market giants like IFF, who recently recruited Corbion's former President of FI&S, could lead to aggressive pricing wars that erode the expected margin gains from cost-saving programs
