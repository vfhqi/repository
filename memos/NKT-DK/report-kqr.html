Section 2 – Market structure and supply-demand balance (2026-2028) 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Structural undersupply in the high-voltage cable market will persist through 2028 despite ongoing capacity additions, securing robust pricing power and margin expansion for established European manufacturers before supply potentially balances by 2030.

Aggressive aggregate demand drivers

1.
Unprecedented offshore acceleration: The European offshore wind sector is re-entering a structural growth phase, with awarded auction results forecast to exceed 25GW in 2026 and 30GW in 2027, driven by a robust project pipeline of over 50GW
2.
Surging interconnector requirements: Cross-border transmission capacity needs are scaling rapidly, with the European Association of Transmission System Operators projecting that required capacity in regions like Austria will jump from 10.05GW in 2025 to 16.85GW by 2030, necessitating massive high-voltage direct current cable deployments
3.
Data center megatrend: The exponential growth of artificial intelligence and data center infrastructure is directly straining the grid, forcing data centers to seek connections to higher voltage networks and competing directly with traditional sectors for high-voltage power equipment
4.
US grid modernization: The North American market is experiencing a massive replacement cycle for aging power grids alongside new data center loads, further amplifying the global high-voltage cable deficit as domestic US supply remains constrained
5.
Structural market expansion: High-voltage power cables remain one of the most critical bottlenecks in the global green transition, ensuring that European demand represents a multi-decade structural requirement rather than a short-term cyclical boom
6.
Edge capacity constraints: The actual pace of grid expansion required to meet these targets–such as building 8,000 kilometers of high-voltage transmission lines annually in the US–appears far beyond current global construction and cable manufacturing rates

Sustained capacity deficits and pricing power

7.
Prolonged lead times: The global supply shortage is so severe that deliveries for new first-quarter 2026 orders in North America and Norway are already being scheduled for 2031 and 2032, confirming an exceptionally tight market
8.
Oligopolistic barrier resilience: The high-voltage market is highly consolidated with immense barriers to entry, leaving only a handful of non-Chinese global players–including Prysmian, Nexans, NKT, and LS Cable–capable of meeting the stringent technical and installation requirements of Western utilities
9.
Pricing momentum visibility: The value of high-voltage cable contracts has surged, with proprietary analysis indicating that average revenue per kilometer of cable length has doubled between the 2019-2020 period and the 2024-2026 period
10.
Margin expansion certainty: Driven by the prolonged supply shortfall and ongoing technological advancements in 525kV systems, the upward pricing trend is projected to persist, securing structurally higher profit margins for the limited supplier base through the end of the decade
11.
Delayed market equilibrium: While aggregate cable supply and demand is currently characterized by a severe deficit, industry capacity expansions are projected to bring the market closer to balance only as the decade approaches 2030
12.
Edge installation bottlenecks: Even as cable manufacturing capacity scales up, the market faces secondary constraints in third-party installation vessels and skilled labor, meaning physical cable supply is only one facet of the broader execution bottleneck
13.
Other side of the story: While the current order backlog implies deep undersupply, aggressive parallel capacity expansions by Nexans, Prysmian, NKT, and emerging Asian players like Taihan and LS Cable could trigger an unexpected oversupply glut by 2028 if permitting delays, labor shortages, or political shifts cause utility demand to stall

Prysmian's accelerated deployment

1.
Rapid operationalization: Analysts at Morgan Stanley note that Prysmian has already successfully expanded its strategic plant in Pikkala, Finland, which features a new 185-meter vertical continuous vulcanization tower capable of producing one kilometer of 525kV HVDC submarine cable per day
2.
Vessel fleet dominance: Demonstrating strong installation execution, Prysmian recently inaugurated the Monna Lisa, its latest state-of-the-art cable-laying vessel, and expects to operate a fleet of eight vessels by 2028 to maintain unquestioned installation supremacy over European peers
3.
Broad European upgrades: Beyond Pikkala, Prysmian is executing targeted upgrades across its European footprint, utilizing funding from the European Investment Bank to enhance high-voltage onshore production lines at facilities in Gron, France, and Arco Felice, Italy
4.
Strategic US pivot: Following slower-than-anticipated offshore wind development in the United States, Prysmian officially cancelled its planned high-voltage subsea factory in Brayton Point, Massachusetts, reflecting a cautious pivot away from unproven regional demand
5.
Technology qualification: According to company disclosures, Prysmian successfully qualified 525kV submarine cable systems operating at elevated temperatures up to 90 degrees Celsius, enabling up to 2.5GW of power transmission per link and cementing a formidable technological moat

Nexans' strategic regional utilization

Comparative ramp-up against NKT

Section 4 – NKT order backlog and pricing dynamics 17 findings

Unprecedented backlog: NKT achieved an industry-leading high-voltage order backlog of 13.5 billion euros by the end of the first quarter of 2026, marking a massive sequential jump from 10.2 billion euros at year-end 2025
2.
Transformational mega-awards: The exceptional backlog growth was driven by the conversion of booking commitments with SSEN Transmission and the firm award of the Eastern Green Link 3 project, a 525kV HVDC link valued at over 2.2 billion euros
3.
Customer concentration: NKT's order book remains heavily insulated from broader macroeconomic volatility, with more than 95 percent of the current backlog contracted directly to large, well-capitalized European Transmission System Operators
4.
Prolonged execution phasing: The company is currently executing on lower-margin orders awarded between 2020 and 2022, meaning the financial benefits of the massive 2023-2026 order intake will be deferred and spread across the latter half of the decade

Guaranteed visibility: Jefferies researchers emphasize that NKT's current high-voltage backlog provides close to full visibility on earnings straight through to 2030, covering more than seven times the company's trailing twelve-month revenues

Section 5 – NKT's technological moat and asset base 16 findings

Section summary
  1. NKT's highly concentrated asset footprint and pioneering high-voltage technology establish a formidable structural moat, securing a sustainable margin premium through operational synergies and execution reliability that transcends basic supply-demand imbalances.

Unrivalled asset concentration and Karlskrona synergies

1.
Concentrated manufacturing supremacy: Unlike global peers who operate fragmented production networks, NKT has strategically centralized its entire high-voltage subsea cable manufacturing, along with its research and development facilities, at a single mega-site in Karlskrona, Sweden
2.
Unmatched logistical advantages: Analysts at Jefferies emphasize that this asset concentration provides profound cost and logistical synergies compared to Prysmian, which splits its high-voltage capacity across four different sites in Finland, Italy, Germany, and Norway
3.
Massive extrusion scale: The Karlskrona facility is currently undergoing a massive expansion that includes a third 200-meter extrusion tower, recently named NKT Crown, which joins the existing Lighthouse and Anchor towers to create the world's largest high-voltage offshore cable production site
4.
Projected margin leadership: Driven by the unparalleled efficiencies of the Karlskrona site, NKT is forecast to deliver the highest high-voltage margins in the sector by 2028, potentially reaching an industry-leading 21 percent EBITDA margin compared to approximately 20 percent for Prysmian and 17.5 percent for Nexans
5.
Strategic mainland integration: Complementing the subsea focus in Sweden, NKT operates a heavily upgraded high-voltage onshore facility in Cologne, Germany, perfectly positioning the company to capture lucrative land-corridor contracts like SuedLink and SuedOstLink without disrupting its offshore manufacturing flow

Next-generation installation fleet and execution capabilities

6.
Dominant vessel payload: NKT is significantly widening its installation moat with the construction of the NKT Eleonora, a state-of-the-art cable-laying vessel scheduled for 2027 that features three turntables and a massive 23,000-tonne cable-laying capacity, enabling the deployment of heavier, longer-length continuous power cables
7.
Advanced subsea trenching: To complement its surface vessels and mitigate the rising geopolitical risk of underwater sabotage, NKT is investing in the world's most powerful subsea trencher, delivering 3,600 horsepower to securely bury critical infrastructure up to 5.5 meters below the seabed
8.
Upgraded legacy assets: NKT's existing installation vessel, the NKT Victoria, has been retrofitted with a new Azimuth retractable thruster for enhanced station keeping and an integrated jet plough, cementing its status as a highly reliable asset capable of simultaneous laying and burial operations
9.
Pioneering sustainable execution: Strengthening its ESG value proposition for green-focused European utilities, NKT Victoria is now certified to run on hydrotreated vegetable oil, while the upcoming Eleonora will operate on e-methanol, offering a distinct competitive advantage in carbon-conscious public tender processes

Pioneering 525kV HVDC and accessory technologies

10.
First-mover technology advantage: Having launched its 525kV XLPE DC technology for commercial use as early as 2014, NKT possesses an extensive track record and unmatched qualification history that creates extreme barriers to entry for new Asian competitors attempting to penetrate the top-tier European transmission market
11.
High-margin accessory dominance: Beyond raw cable manufacturing, NKT maintains a highly profitable Service and Accessories division, recently bolstered by a major expansion of its testing facilities in Alingsås, Sweden, allowing for the rigorous qualification of power cable accessories up to 640kV
12.
Disruptive dry-type terminations: Addressing industry safety and environmental concerns, NKT is actively replacing conventional oil-filled infrastructure by launching the KFEV 245 dry-type outdoor termination, which eliminates oil-leak risks and simplifies complex field installations
13.
Standardized switchgear innovation: The company has also introduced the KSEV 145 S5 dry plug-in termination utilizing the CIGRE TB 784 standard interface, a move that breaks the proprietary lock-in of traditional gas-insulated switchgear manufacturers and captures lucrative new market segments
14.
Comprehensive end-to-end moat: By vertically integrating the production of the 525kV cable, the specialized high-voltage accessories, and the physical installation vessels, NKT creates an end-to-end turnkey offering that drastically lowers execution risk for transmission system operators, justifying a persistent price premium
15.
Other side of the story: While the Karlskrona site offers immense theoretical synergies, its absolute concentration creates a severe single-point-of-failure risk, meaning any localized labor dispute, prolonged equipment breakdown, or logistical bottleneck at the Swedish facility could instantly cripple NKT's entire high-voltage offshore delivery pipeline

Section 6 – Confidence classification 2 findings

Section summary
  1. The aggregate evidence base demonstrates high conviction that NKT retains a sustainable pricing advantage through 2028, though execution risk and the theoretical timing of competitive capacity additions introduce meaningful variability around margin trajectory beyond that horizon.

Competitor capacity assessment reveals moderate confidence in the relative competitive positioning through 2028. Prysmian's Pikkala expansion with its 185-meter vertical tower producing one kilometer of 525kV cable daily and Nexans' finalized 560 million euro investment in Halden are tangible completed assets, providing high visibility on near-term supply additions. However, the critical timing uncertainty centers on whether NKT's massive 2 billion euro Karlskrona expansion, suffering 300 million euros in cost overruns but targeting 2027 commissioning, will meet its aggressive schedule or experience further delays that compress the window of differentiated advantage.

The technological moat assessment achieves high confidence in NKT's structural advantages but identifies execution concentration risk as a material offset. The strategic centralization of all high-voltage subsea manufacturing at the single Karlskrona mega-site, contrasted against Prysmian's fragmented four-site network, delivers quantifiable logistical and cost synergies that justify projected industry-leading 21 percent EBITDA margins by 2028. The installation fleet expansion, including the NKT Eleonora's massive 23,000-tonne cable-laying capacity scheduled for 2027, creates tangible barriers to entry that transcend pure manufacturing scale. However, this asset concentration simultaneously creates a severe single-point-of-failure vulnerability, where any localized disruption at Karlskrona could instantly cripple the entire offshore delivery pipeline.

Cross-thematic implications carry moderate to low confidence due to their inherently speculative nature, yet the directional signals warrant serious consideration. The exponential growth of artificial intelligence and hyperscaler data center loads, including rapid adoption of 800V high-voltage DC architectures, represents a non-consensus demand vector that could structurally compound grid constraints beyond current utility planning assumptions. The geopolitical dimension, particularly potential Chinese supplier lockouts through carbon border mechanisms and escalating trade friction, introduces binary upside scenarios where European oligopoly pricing power could tighten dramatically, though the probability and timing remain highly uncertain.

The installation vessel bottleneck emerges as a critical under-discussed constraint that warrants elevation in the confidence framework. Secondary constraints in third-party installation vessels and skilled labor mean physical cable supply is only one facet of the broader execution bottleneck, suggesting that even if Asian competitors successfully scale manufacturing capacity, their inability to access compliant installation fleets will strand theoretical capacity additions. This dimension provides structural protection to incumbent European players with captive vessel assets, materially increasing confidence in sustained pricing power.
