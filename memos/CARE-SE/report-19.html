3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — Failure Narrative 1: Acquisition-Multiple Creep and ROIC Compression

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's inorganic growth model depends on acquiring niche industrial service businesses at disciplined entry multiples (historically sub-8x EBIT). As private-equity competition for Nordic SME acquisitions intensifies and target supply compresses, management stretches entry multiples to sustain deal flow — paying 10-13x for assets that previously cleared at 7-8x. The transmission mechanism: higher entry multiples reduce ROIC on deployed capital below the cost of capital within 24 months, impairing goodwill, triggering impairment charges, and resetting EPS trajectory downward by 15-25% versus bull-case projections.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Average acquisition EV/EBIT multiple disclosed per deal trending above 9x in two consecutive years
- Disclosed: Goodwill as percentage of total assets exceeding 65% and accelerating
- Disclosed: Post-acquisition EBIT margin of acquired entities running 200-300bps below standalone CARE-SE margin
- Inferred: Acquisition announcement frequency accelerating while average deal size declines (fishing in shallower pools)
- Inferred: Increase in earn-out structures as % of total consideration — signals vendor leverage and price uncertainty

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — directly precedented across Nordic serial acquirers (Addtech 2007-2009, Indutrade 2015-2016 periods showed multiple creep under PE competition); base rate of at least one acquisition-quality deterioration episode per 5-year cycle across comparable acquirers is approximately 60-70%

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Available — CARE-SE has balance sheet capacity and access to acquisition pipeline
- Motive: Present — management and market both reward deal count; organic growth shortfall creates pressure to deploy
- Opportunity: Present — Nordic SME acquisition market is competitive but not shut; seller expectations have risen with PE multiples
- MMO verdict: All three legs aligned — full activation risk; NOT downgraded to watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: What would invalidate this hypothesis? Evidence that average disclosed entry multiples have held stable or compressed over the past 8 quarters; post-acquisition ROIC on vintage-3Y cohort tracking at or above group WACC; acquisition pipeline breadth metrics (deal rejections per completion) showing maintained selectivity

Monitoring checklist:
- Goodwill additions per acquisition vs stated EBIT at acquisition — trend
- Rolling ROIC on acquisition cohorts (3Y post-close)
- EV/EBIT disclosed per deal vs prior 5-year average
- Leverage ratio trajectory (net debt/EBITDA trend over 4 rolling quarters)
- Impairment charges as % of goodwill stock — any uptick

Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with Narrative 3 (organic growth stall — creates pressure to acquire more) and Narrative 7 (working capital deterioration — acquired businesses bring legacy WC structures); sequential activation risk is high

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence required here — stable disclosed multiples, cohort-level ROIC data — is typically NOT available in public disclosures at sufficient granularity; absence of evidence is not evidence of absence, and this failure mode is precisely the least visible until goodwill impairment forces disclosure

§3 — Failure Narrative 2: Organic Revenue Growth Stall

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's reported revenue growth conflates organic and inorganic components. If underlying organic growth decelerates to 0-2% (from a reported headline of 6-10%), the growth narrative breaks without any visible accounting event. The transmission mechanism: organic stall signals end-market demand softness or competitive share loss → sell-side revises growth assumptions → multiple de-rates 3-5 turns → EPS trajectory misses by magnitude that exceeds any margin improvement offset.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Like-for-like or organic growth disclosure (where provided) decelerating two consecutive quarters
- Disclosed: Revenue per employee declining year-on-year — signals utilisation deterioration
- Inferred: Acquisition-adjusted revenue growth narrowing to within 100bps of headline growth (organic component near-zero)
- Inferred: Order intake or backlog metrics (if disclosed) declining vs prior-year
- Inferred: Customer renewal rates / volume metrics in any disclosed segment stagnating

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — organic growth stalls in Nordic industrial services businesses are common at the late-cycle stage; sector base rate suggests 40-50% of companies reporting 8%+ headline growth are running sub-3% organic during acquisition-heavy phases

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — end-market cyclicality and share loss both accessible failure mechanisms
- Motive: N/A (this is a structural risk, not an intentional management decision)
- Opportunity: Present — Nordic construction/industrial activity softening in 2025-2026 macro environment
- MMO verdict: Means and Opportunity fully present; Motive inapplicable (structural) — full activation risk maintained; structural failure modes do not require motive

Disconfirming evidence: Organic growth disclosed explicitly and holding at 4%+ for three consecutive quarters; end-market volume indicators (Nordic PMI, construction starts) recovering; customer concentration metrics showing no top-3 customer share loss

Monitoring checklist:
- Organic vs reported growth gap — trend direction
- Revenue per head — annual trend
- Segment-level volume disclosures where available
- Nordic PMI and construction activity index (leading 1-2 quarters)
- Customer tenure disclosures or renewal language in annual reports

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 1 (organic stall triggers acquisition pressure) and Narrative 5 (pricing pressure — organic stall often co-occurs with pricing concession to retain volumes); null interaction with Narrative 8 (key executive departure — independent axis)

Sceptical bullet: CARE-SE's disclosure granularity on organic vs inorganic growth is the key variable — if management does not split these cleanly, the analyst cannot distinguish a genuine growth business from an acquisition roll-up masquerading as an organic compounder; the bull case IS the organic compounder narrative

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's niche industrial service clients are themselves consolidating — larger procurement functions, group purchasing agreements, and cross-supplier benchmarking compress CARE-SE's pricing power over 18-36 months. The transmission mechanism: gross margin compression of 150-250bps → operating leverage reverses → EBIT margin falls 100-200bps below consensus → EPS 10-18% below bull case.

Early warning signals:

- Disclosed: Revenue growth from existing customers (same-store) lagging CPI — real revenue per client declining
- Inferred: Contract duration shortening — clients negotiating annual rather than multi-year terms
- Inferred: New customer win rate data (if disclosed) declining — pipeline quality
- Inferred: Competitor pricing announcements or tenders won at below-market rates

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — niche industrial services typically exhibit stronger pricing power than volume-commodity services; but customer consolidation in Nordic industrials is a documented trend (2018-2024 wave); base rate of meaningful pricing pressure in niche services over any 3-year window is approximately 30-40%

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — customer procurement leverage increasing
- Motive: Present — customers face own margin pressure, incentivised to consolidate suppliers
- Opportunity: Present — Nordic corporate consolidation trend ongoing
- MMO verdict: All three legs aligned; full activation risk

Disconfirming evidence: Gross margin stable or expanding in most recent two reporting periods; evidence of price increases passed through (ASP data or management commentary); customer concentration metrics showing no single customer above 10% of revenue

Monitoring checklist:
- Gross margin trend — quarterly where available, annual minimum
- Revenue per existing customer — year-on-year
- Contract term length disclosures
- CARE-SE pricing commentary in investor presentations
- Customer concentration (top-5 as % of revenue) — annual report

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 2 (organic stall) and Narrative 5 (cost inflation — margin gets squeezed from both sides simultaneously); this two-way squeeze is the classic industrial services margin trap

Sceptical bullet: Pricing power is the most cited competitive moat in niche industrial services bull cases — yet it is also the hardest to verify externally before it erodes; the analyst must obtain primary customer-side pricing evidence, not rely solely on management commentary

§5 — Failure Narrative 4: Cost Inflation — Labour and Input Cost Squeeze

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's service delivery is labour-intensive. Nordic wage inflation running at 4-6% annually and a tight Scandinavian skilled-labour market compress gross margin if pricing increases lag by even one to two quarters. The transmission mechanism: 4% wage inflation on a 50%+ labour cost base → 200bps gross margin headwind annually → operating leverage reverses → EBIT misses by 10-15% in a sustained inflationary episode without offsetting price increases.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Employee cost growth outpacing revenue growth for two consecutive periods
- Disclosed: Headcount growth exceeding revenue growth (efficiency declining)
- Inferred: Nordic wage settlement data (sector-specific collective bargaining) pointing to above-CPI increases
- Inferred: Management commentary shifting from "pricing fully offsetting cost" to "partial offset" language
- Inferred: Gross margin declining concurrent with revenue growth — the classic inflation squeeze signature

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — Nordic labour market is structurally tight; wage inflation in skilled industrial services has run 4-7% in 2022-2025; any business with 50%+ labour cost base in Scandinavia faces this risk structurally; base rate of a meaningful labour-cost squeeze episode within any 3-year holding period is approximately 55-65%

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — labour cost structure exposed
- Motive: N/A (structural macro)
- Opportunity: Present — macro environment supportive of continued wage pressure
- MMO verdict: Structural failure mode; Means and Opportunity fully present

Disconfirming evidence: Gross margin holding flat or improving despite documented wage growth; evidence of automation investment reducing labour intensity; pricing mechanisms (indexation clauses) structurally linked to wage cost inflation in contracts

Monitoring checklist:
- Employee cost per head vs prior year — annual minimum
- Gross margin vs revenue growth correlation — quarterly
- Nordic wage settlement announcements — sector-specific
- Capex per employee trend (automation investment proxy)
- Management commentary on contract indexation mechanisms

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Narrative 3 (pricing power — if pricing can't pass through cost, both fail simultaneously); partially correlated with Narrative 6 (operating leverage failure — cost inflation accelerates deleveraging)

Sceptical bullet: Contract indexation clauses would substantially mitigate this risk — the analyst must verify whether such clauses exist and what percentage of revenue they cover; absent this, the labour cost risk is underweighted in most industrial services bull cases

§6 — Failure Narrative 5 (FALSE FRIEND): Margin Expansion from Capex Deferral and R&D Under-investment

Specific failure narrative [FALSE FRIEND]: Reported EBIT margins expand 50-100bps over 12-18 months, appearing to confirm the thesis of operational leverage and execution quality. In reality, margin expansion is driven by deferred maintenance capex, reduced discretionary investment, and R&D under-spend — creating a short-term EPS tailwind that reverses sharply when deferred investment must be made (equipment failure, platform obsolescence, talent atrophy). The transmission mechanism: investment deferral → near-term margin beat → analyst confidence peaks → catch-up investment required → EPS step-down of 15-25% in the investment year → thesis breaks at precisely the moment conviction is highest.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Capex as % of revenue declining year-on-year while margins improve — suspicious divergence
- Disclosed: Depreciation growing faster than capex additions (underdepreciation risk flipping to catch-up)
- Inferred: Maintenance capex language disappearing from management commentary
- Inferred: Employee training/development cost as % of revenue declining
- Inferred: Margin expansion concentrated in years with no major restructuring or investment cycle

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — capex deferral as a false-friend mechanism is documented across Nordic industrials (Haldex 2014-2016, Nibe early 2020s periods show similar patterns); base rate of at least one capex-deferral margin inflation episode per 5-year cycle in asset-light Nordic services is approximately 25-35%

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — management controls capex and investment timing
- Motive: Present — EPS-linked compensation and analyst expectations create incentive to smooth investment
- Opportunity: Present — service businesses have more discretion over investment timing than capital-intensive manufacturers
- MMO verdict: All three legs aligned — false-friend risk is FULLY activated; this is the most dangerous scenario type

Disconfirming evidence: Capex per employee stable or growing concurrently with margin expansion; maintenance capex explicitly disclosed and holding at historical norms; R&D/innovation spend (if disclosed) not declining as % of revenue

Monitoring checklist:
- Capex as % of revenue — 5-year trend vs margin trend
- Depreciation vs capex additions ratio — trend
- Free cash flow conversion — if FCF conversion exceeds 100% of net income for 2+ years, investigate source
- Management capex guidance vs actuals — is capex consistently coming in below guidance?
- Employee-related investment disclosures

Interaction with other failure modes: This scenario creates maximum danger when combined with Narrative 9 (earnings beat from unsustainable factors — both false friends amplify each other); also interacts with Narrative 8 (executive departure — new management inherits the deferred-investment problem and must take the pain)

Sceptical bullet: Margin expansion is the single most consensus-positive signal in an industrial compounder thesis — yet it is also the single easiest metric to manufacture via investment deferral; the analyst must decompose margin expansion into genuine efficiency gains vs investment reduction before assigning it as confirming evidence

§7 — Failure Narrative 6: Operating Leverage Failure — Fixed Cost Creep

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's bull case embeds significant positive operating leverage — revenue growth should drop through to EBIT at 40-50% incremental margin. If fixed cost infrastructure (central overhead, IT systems, compliance functions) expands ahead of revenue — a common pattern in acquisition-driven businesses that must integrate multiple organisational structures — incremental margins underperform and the leverage story inverts. The transmission mechanism: fixed cost creep → SGA as % of revenue expands 100-200bps → EBIT margin misses by 80-150bps → EPS 8-15% below consensus.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: SGA as % of revenue increasing in two consecutive reporting periods
- Disclosed: Headcount in central/corporate functions growing faster than field operations
- Disclosed: IT and systems investment disclosures increasing without corresponding revenue benefit
- Inferred: Integration cost disclosures increasing as % of revenue
- Inferred: Management commentary shifting from "scalable platform" to "investment in infrastructure" language

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — fixed cost creep is a classic acquisition roll-up failure mode; base rate of SGA expansion during periods of high acquisition activity is approximately 40-50% across Nordic acquirers

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — management controls cost structure
- Motive: Partial — management may not intend cost creep, but integration complexity drives it organically
- Opportunity: Present — high acquisition pace creates integration demand
- MMO verdict: Two of three legs clearly present; Motive is partial — downgrade to watch risk; NOT full activation

Disconfirming evidence: SGA as % of revenue declining or flat despite high acquisition activity; incremental EBIT margin on organic revenue demonstrably above 35%; management disclosure of scalable cost model with evidence (revenue per employee growing)

Monitoring checklist:
- SGA % of revenue — quarterly trend
- Revenue per employee — annual trend (falling = bad)
- Corporate headcount vs field headcount ratio
- Integration cost line — trend as % of revenue
- EBIT bridge disclosures separating volume/price from cost

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 1 (acquisition multiple creep — both worsen together when deal pace accelerates); correlated with Narrative 4 (labour cost inflation compounds the fixed cost creep); null interaction with competitive disruption narratives

Sceptical bullet: Many acquirers cite "platform scalability" as a moat — the empirical test is whether SGA grows slower than revenue over a 3-5 year period; any acquirer where SGA/revenue has expanded during an acquisition-heavy period is NOT demonstrating a scalable platform, regardless of management claims

§8 — Failure Narrative 7: Working Capital Deterioration and Cash Conversion Collapse

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's earnings quality depends on high cash conversion. Acquired businesses may carry structurally worse working capital profiles — longer debtor days, higher inventory requirements — that deteriorate group cash conversion over time. The transmission mechanism: working capital absorption → reported earnings exceed free cash flow → FCF conversion falls below 70% of net income → refinancing risk increases → equity story shifts from compounder to leveraged roll-up → multiple de-rates 2-4 turns.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: FCF conversion (FCF/net income) declining below 80% for two consecutive years
- Disclosed: Debtor days increasing year-on-year post-acquisition
- Disclosed: Net debt/EBITDA trending above 2.5x without corresponding revenue accretion
- Inferred: Accounts receivable growing faster than revenue
- Inferred: Management emphasising EBITDA rather than FCF per share in investor communication — language shift signals

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — working capital deterioration in acquisition roll-ups is a well-documented mechanism; base rate of FCF conversion falling below 75% in a high-acquisition-activity year is approximately 30-40% for Nordic acquirers

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — acquired business WC profiles are often worse than disclosed
- Motive: N/A (structural risk from acquisition mix)
- Opportunity: Present — high acquisition pace increases WC exposure
- MMO verdict: Structural failure mode; Means and Opportunity present

Disconfirming evidence: FCF conversion consistently above 85% including post-acquisition periods; debtor days stable or declining over 4-year trend; CARE-SE's acquired businesses disclosed as having WC profiles within 5% of group average

Monitoring checklist:
- FCF conversion ratio — annual
- Debtor days — annual trend
- Net debt/EBITDA — quarterly
- Accounts receivable % of revenue — trend
- Management language on FCF vs EBITDA emphasis ratio

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 1 (multiple creep — poor acquisitions bring bad WC); interacts with Narrative 11 (leverage/covenant risk — WC deterioration accelerates covenant risk)

Sceptical bullet: FCF conversion is the most reliable earnings quality indicator — if CARE-SE's reported earnings persistently exceed FCF over multiple years, the bull case is built on accrual accounting, not cash generation; this single metric should be the first line of defence in ongoing monitoring

§9 — Failure Narrative 8: Key Executive Departure — CEO or CFO

Specific failure narrative: Serial acquisition businesses are often highly dependent on a small number of individuals with deal-sourcing networks, capital allocation discipline, and cultural authority. Departure of the CEO or CFO — through retirement, health, competitor poaching, or conflict — disrupts the acquisition pipeline, introduces uncertainty about strategic direction, and triggers a re-rating. The transmission mechanism: leadership departure → deal pace drops → organic growth narrative under scrutiny → sell-side revises multiple by 2-4 turns → 15-25% TSR hit within 6 months of announcement.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Age of key executives and proximity to typical retirement horizon (CEO 60+ at appointment, 10+ years tenure)
- Inferred: Executive compensation structure changes — shift away from long-term incentives may signal shorter-term horizon
- Inferred: Unusual insider selling by executives in a period of apparent strong performance
- Inferred: Board composition changes — new independent directors appointed in areas relevant to succession
- Inferred: Unusual or unexplained absences from investor events

Plausibility verdict: Low plausibility in the 18-month window — but elevates to medium plausibility over a 3-year horizon; base rate of CEO departure at a Nordic mid-cap over any 3-year holding period is approximately 20-30%; CFO departure base rate is higher (30-40%)

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present — executives are human; retirement, health, and competitive poaching all possible
- Motive: Present — if compensation structures change or strategic disagreements emerge
- Opportunity: Partial — succession planning quality unknown from external observation
- MMO verdict: Two of three legs clearly present (Opportunity partial) — watch risk; monitoring priority

Disconfirming evidence: Executive tenure well within typical cycle; long-term incentive awards recently refreshed with multi-year vesting; public commentary from executives indicating continued strategic commitment; disclosed succession planning framework

Monitoring checklist:
- Executive age and tenure disclosure — annual report
- Insider transaction filings — any unusual selling
- LTIP refresh cycles — any gap in awards
- Board composition changes — new directors
- Management attendance at investor days and capital markets events

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 5 (capex deferral — new management inherits deferred investment problem); correlated with Narrative 1 (acquisition multiple creep — new deal team may lack legacy discipline); sequential rather than simultaneous activation

Sceptical bullet: Leadership dependency risk is often dismissed in bull cases because the current team is performing well — yet this is precisely when departure risk is highest (compensation expectations have been met, strategic vision arguably achieved, succession window opens); the analyst should explicitly assess whether the business model is leader-dependent or system-dependent

§12 — Failure Narrative 11: Leverage and Covenant Breach Risk

Specific failure narrative: CARE-SE's acquisition-funded growth model relies on debt capacity. In a combination of acquisition pace acceleration and organic growth stall, net debt/EBITDA could breach bank covenants (typically 3.0-3.5x for Nordic mid-cap industrials), triggering a forced equity raise or asset disposal at an inopportune time. The transmission mechanism: covenant breach → equity raise at discount → EPS dilution 15-20% → acquisition pace halted → growth narrative breaks → 5-8 turn multiple de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed: Net debt/EBITDA trending above 2.5x with no deleverage signal
- Disclosed: Free cash flow declining while acquisition pace maintained
- Disclosed: Refinancing events approaching within 18 months
- Inferred: Management declining to provide leverage guidance or softening prior guidance
- Inferred: Bond/credit spread widening on any public debt instruments

Plausibility verdict: Low plausibility in isolation — most Nordic industrial acquirers operate conservatively within covenant headroom; but medium plausibility as a second-order failure mode triggered by Narrative 2 + Narrative 1 co-activation

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Present if M/M/O from Narratives 1 and 2 activate
- Motive: N/A (structural — lenders impose covenants)
- Opportunity: Conditional on leverage creeping above 2.5x
- MMO verdict: Conditional failure mode — activates only if upstream narratives activate first; watch risk only

Disconfirming evidence: Net debt/EBITDA below 2.0x with deleverage trend; RCF headroom >50% utilised; covenant waiver language not appearing in lender disclosures; positive FCF even in a scenario of organic revenue stall

Monitoring checklist:
- Net debt/EBITDA — quarterly
- Covenant headroom disclosures — annual minimum
- RCF utilisation
- FCF generation vs acquisition spend
- Credit rating or banking relationship disclosures

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the catastrophic downstream failure that other narratives can cascade into; highest priority as a second-order risk amplifier

Sceptical bullet: Leverage risk is often treated as a binary (covenant breach = crisis) but the warning signs are visible 2-3 quarters before breach — the analyst must track the trend, not wait for the event

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

- Motive: Present — new segment narrative creates re-rating opportunity and management profile
