Competitive landscape and market share dynamics

The global tobacco industry is highly concentrated and dominated by four transnational giants and state monopolies, including Philip Morris International, British American Tobacco, Japan Tobacco Inc., and Imperial Brands, with transnational majors collectively controlling over 30% of global tobacco volumes excluding China. Karelia achieved a consolidated 25.3% market share in Bulgaria in the first half of 2024, representing a notable expansion from 23.42% recorded in the prior-year period, while subsidiary cigarette sales volumes expanded by 11.2% in Bulgaria during fiscal year 2025. Karelia's domestic cigarette market share increased to 19.23% in the first half of 2024 from a previous 18.73% consolidated market share, while Greek cigarette sales volumes rose by 4.6% in 2025 driven by core brand demand. Alternative nicotine delivery products, including heated tobacco and e-vapor, threaten traditional combustible volumes, with peers focusing heavily on next-generation products as British American Tobacco prioritizes smokeless categories and the UK nicotine pouch market growing by 55% year-on-year to reach approximately £230 million. Karelia prioritizes investments in traditional manufacturing assets to meet current combustible demand, ordering new production lines worth EUR 5 million in 2024, with factory capital expenditures reaching EUR 6.9 million in 2025 to increase production flexibility.
