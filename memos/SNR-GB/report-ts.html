Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Senior plc operates a high-barrier engineering model focused on thermal and fluid management, enjoying deep sole-source positions on major aircraft and heavy vehicle platforms.

Segment and Product Portfolio

1.
Favourable divisional split: Senior operates via two primary divisions, with Aerospace contributing 58.00% and Flexonics contributing 42.00% of FY25 continuing revenues. This represents a permanent structural shift following the disposal of its legacy structures operations
2.
40 Advanced aerospace capabilities: The Aerospace division specialises in bleed air duct systems, high-pressure ducting, metal bellows, and fluid control fittings. These systems are designed to withstand extreme thermal stresses of up to 700 degrees Celsius
3.
Engineered industrial offerings: The Flexonics division focuses on thermal management for land vehicles, diesel emission controls, and industrial expansion joints. Key land vehicle applications include exhaust gas recycling, battery cooling, and flexible exhaust connectors
4.
45 Expanding adjacent markets: Sales to adjacent high-growth sectors, such as advanced semiconductor equipment and medical tech, reached 10.00% of continuing sales in FY25. Senior supplies specialized edge-welded bellows to major semi OEMs

Revenue Model and Value Chain

5.
Contractual revenue model: Revenue is primarily driven by long-term contract pricing aligned with major OEM production schedules. The business model is straightforward with no exposure to long-term contract accounting risk
6.
Favourable value-chain position: Senior sits as a critical Tier 2 or Tier 1 supplier, producing sole-source engineered components. Its high value-add design role guarantees a highly defensible and profit- favoured position
7.
Global manufacturing footprint: Manufacturing is diversified across 19 operating businesses in 10 countries, including facilities in Mexico, India, and France. This local-for-local manufacturing strategy helps mitigate geopolitical supply disruptions and tariff impacts

Customers and Competitive Advantage

8.
Elevated customer concentration: The customer base is concentrated among major global OEMs, with Airbus representing 9.00% and Caterpillar at 4.00% of FY25 revenue. Aerospace sales are split commercial at 32.00%, military at 16.00%, and regional at 33.00%
9.
57 High switching costs: Competitive advantage is secured through deep engineering IP and lengthy customer qualification cycles. Qualification typically takes 18 to 24 months, establishing formidable barriers to entry
10.
Proprietary technology advantage: Patents and specialized fabrication techniques for edge-welded metal bellows protect the company's product pricing. These bellows perform mission-critical roles in digital infrastructure, space, and vacuum environments

Industry Structure and Peer Context

11.
Consolidating industry landscape: The aerospace component industry is highly consolidated, demanding high capital and technical certification. Senior competes with a select group of specialized global manufacturers
12.
Differentiated peer positioning: Unlike diversified capital goods peers, Senior's business combines high- barrier aerospace standard parts with heavy vehicle components. This structure exposes it to both commercial aerospace and cyclical land vehicle end markets
13.
66 Aerospace sector exposure: Senior represents one of the few pure-play vehicles for aerospace recovery among UK industrials, alongside heat-treatment specialist Bodycote. Unlike Bodycote, Senior's sales are almost entirely original equipment manufacturer contracts
14.
Other side of the story: The core risk to the business model is customer insourcing or supply chain disintermediation as OEMs seek to control costs. If major airframe manufacturers select standardized standard-compliant parts over Senior's custom designs, the firm's margins would deteriorate

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Senior's risk profile remains exposed to cyclical heavy truck volumes and aerospace manufacturing strikes, though balance sheet strength mitigates operational volatility.

Supply Chain and Strike Disruption

1.
Vulnerable platform delivery: Production schedule volatility represents a major threat to aerospace revenue predictability. Disruptions at Boeing directly translate to delayed standard parts deliveries for Senior
2.
Severe strike impacts: Labor disputes at major customers represent a direct threat to earnings. A strike at Boeing in late 2024 severely impacted quarterly schedules, forcing temporary furloughs and cost-cutting actions
3.
Fragile supply logistics: Component shortages and localized supplier constraints can delay fluid conveyance assembly. Senior has selectively in-sourced critical capabilities to defend against supply chain blockages

Cyclical Land Vehicle Exposure

4.
Depressed heavy truck demand: Cyclical land vehicle markets expose the Flexonics division to demand volatility. Softening North American heavy truck production represents a significant headwind for flexible exhaust volumes
5.
Commoditized pricing pressure: Highly competitive upstream oil and gas sectors present high competitive pricing risks. Senior has proactively reduced focus on commoditised products to protect operating margins

Financial and Contractual Risks

6.
Severe FX translation exposure: Reporting in sterling exposes the group to substantial translation headwinds since two-thirds of earnings are US-dollar denominated. A strong sterling represents a material headwind to reported revenue
7.
Significant tariff headwinds: Macroeconomic trade policy shifts, such as import tariffs, present moderate margin headwinds. However, the localized structure of manufacturing reduces direct export exposure
8.
151 Key warning indicator: The single most critical forward metric to monitor is the monthly commercial aircraft delivery rate published by Airbus. This indicator provides early warnings of platform delivery disruptions
9.
Other side of the story: Sceptics argue that risks are overblown because Senior's sole-source contracts on advanced engines provide immense contract protection. Engine components cannot be easily replaced without undergoing extensive and expensive certification processes
