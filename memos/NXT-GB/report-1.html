1. What the Business Does

NEXT plc is the UK's largest clothing and home retailer by sales, operating through six reporting segments: Retail Stores, Online , Online (International), NEXT Finance, Total Platform, and Other Business Activities.

Retail Stores operates approximately 500 stores in the UK and Ireland and roughly 200 franchise stores across 37 countries. Store profitability has come under pressure from rising wage costs — Retail Stores profit fell to £193m in FY26, down -5.0% YoY, with margin compressing to 10.2%.

**Online * is the core revenue engine. Online sales have exceeded retail sales at NEXT for six consecutive years. In FY25, UK online sales exceeded £3.2bn. 80% of orders dispatched within 24 hours, cut-off as late as 10pm.

Online (International) ships to over 70 countries. International full-price sales grew +22% in FY26.

NEXT Finance is the proprietary consumer credit business. NEXT offers an open credit account at 29.9% APR. Finance income provides a high-quality, recurring earnings stream above the blended NEXT group margin.

Total Platform is NEXT's white-label e-commerce services business. Current clients: Victoria's Secret, Laura Ashley, Reiss, Childsplay Clothing, and Gap. Total Platform profit (including related interest) was £76.6m in FY25 vs £42.8m in FY24, growth of +79% YoY.

3. Competitive Positioning

NEXT is the UK's largest clothing retailer by revenue. Brand recognition at 94%. Key moats: logistics infrastructure (next-day for 80%+ orders, 10pm cut-offs), proprietary data (25+ years of customer transaction data), consumer credit product (29.9% APR revolving credit), brand strength (94% aided awareness).

4. Own-Label and Margin (CQ4)

Own-brand is structurally higher margin than third-party brands. The overseas business is more NEXT-branded and growing faster — creating a geographic mix tailwind. Third-party brands represent 19% of UK online sales; margin improving +1.4% as NEXT culls uneconomical lines.

5. Management and Capital Allocation (CQ5)

Simon Wolfson: CEO since 2001. Guides conservatively and upgrades through the year — sustained for over a decade. FY26: £286.5m ordinary dividends, £131.4m buybacks, balance via B Share Scheme. ROIC 20.2%. M&A bolt-on and strategic (Reiss stake to 72%). A-grade capital allocator.

6. Consumer Cycle Resilience (CQ2)

In 2022-23, NEXT still grew profit through the sharpest UK consumer downturn in a decade. Pricing at quality/value (not luxury, not ultra-cheap) positions it to capture trade-down. Consumer credit book adds cyclicality risk but NEXT has managed this through multiple cycles.

7. Fulcrum Driver (CQ3)

Total Platform is the fulcrum driver. Trajectory: £42.8m (FY24) → £76.6m (FY25) → tracking toward £150m+ over 3-5 years. Trackable via segment disclosure at each half-year and full-year results.

8. Key Risks

NLW + NI: £25m+ hit to Retail Stores. Middle East disruption: £27m additional international costs. International execution risk on ZEOS. Retail estate drag.
