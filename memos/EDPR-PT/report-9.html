§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Mixed — EBITDA generation is fundamentally clean (contracted/regulated revenues dominate), but recurring use of asset rotation gains as P&L contributors and D&A acceleration from the commissioning wave both warrant scrutiny [#J]

Recurring vs one-off:
- Asset rotation pattern: EDPR books gains from selling minority stakes or fully-developed assets into infrastructure funds; this has generated €150-300m/yr of P&L contribution in each of FY2022-FY2024 — treated as "recurring" in company adjusted metrics but is deal-flow dependent [#A]
- FY2022: ~€280m rotation gains; FY2023: ~€180m; FY2024: ~€200m — declining trajectory
- Peer base rate: Ørsted historically generated similar divestiture income; Iberdrola runs a comparable asset-light rotation model — so sector-normal, but not zero-risk [#I]
- US IRA tax credits: FY2024 saw first meaningful IRA Production Tax Credit monetisation (~€120-150m); structurally growing as US wind/solar capacity scales, but dependent on US policy continuity [#A]
- One-off restructuring: No material restructuring charges in L3Y window; relatively clean on this dimension [#I]

Adjustments trend:
- Non-GAAP gap: Adjusted EPS consistently 20-40% above reported EPS in FY2023-2024 due to removal of D&A step-up impact and certain minority interest adjustments; the gap has widened year-on-year, suggesting increasing reliance on adjusted metrics [#A]
- D&A trajectory: D&A rose from ~€950m (FY2022) to ~€1,100m (FY2024) as new GWs were commissioned and capitalised; this is economically real cost (physical asset depreciation), not a paper adjustment [#A]
- D&A as % of EBITDA: ~42% (FY2022) to ~48% (FY2024) — creeping upward; compresses EBIT conversion [#A]

Working capital trends:
- NWC signal: Renewables developers have relatively thin working capital cycles (electricity sold on power curves, receivables are short-dated); EDPR's NWC swings are modest vs industrials peers [#I]
- NWC headwind: ❌ Receivables grew in absolute terms as revenue scaled and some PPA structures elongated collection timelines; no material deterioration confirmed but directional risk [#A]
- Payables turn: Capex-related payables are the larger swing factor; EDPR runs elevated construction payables during heavy build phases, which normalise post-commissioning — creates P&L-OCF timing differences [#A]

Cash conversion:
- OCF vs NI: OCF is robust (~€1.8-2.2bn range L3Y) vs reported net income (~€350-600m range); this high OCF/NI ratio reflects D&A addback (economically real) — a renewable developer should show this pattern [#I]
- ❌ Free cash flow: FCF is structurally deeply negative (~-€1.5 to -€2.5bn/yr after growth capex); EDPR is a capital-intensive growth vehicle, not a cash returner — normal for sector but constrains equity return optionality [#A]
- Peer base rate: Ørsted and Iberdrola Renovables both showed similar FCF-negative profiles during peak growth phases; EDPR's capex intensity (~3-4% of installed base/yr maintenance + growth) is sector-consistent [#I]

D&A / amortisation trends:
- D&A acceleration: The commissioning wave of FY2022-2024 (+3-4 GW/yr new capacity) has mechanically driven D&A higher; this will continue as 2025-2026 pipeline comes online [#J]
- Conservative vs aggressive: D&A policy is relatively conservative (25-30yr asset lives for wind/solar — industry standard); no evidence of life extension accounting adjustments [#I]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? Asset rotation gains are "recurring one-offs" that inflate EBITDA by €150-300m/yr; if the M&A market for renewables assets tightens (rising rates → lower infrastructure fund appetite), this contribution disappears; simultaneously, IRA monetisation is policy-reversible; together these represent ~15-20% of reported EBITDA that is higher-risk than the contracted generation base [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: EBITDA margin structurally stable at ~55% but EBIT and EPS margins are compressing from D&A acceleration — the margin story is sector-normal but not expanding; no operating leverage in L3Y window [#J]

Revenue CAGR: ~11-12% L3Y (FY2021 base to FY2024); driven by capacity additions (GW growth ~12-15%/yr) partially offset by power price headwind post-FY2022 [#A]

Profit CAGR:
- EBITDA CAGR: ~0-2% L3Y (FY2022-FY2024) — revenue growth not translating to profit growth due to price normalisation offsetting volume gains [#A]
- EBIT CAGR: ~-5% L3Y (FY2022-FY2024) due to D&A step-up [#A]
- EPS CAGR: ~-15% L3Y (FY2022-FY2024) on reported basis; adjusted basis ~-8-10% [#A]

Operating leverage:
- Absent in L3Y: Revenue +12% CAGR with EBITDA flat — negative operating leverage on the profit line [#J]
- Structural explanation: Electricity price is the dominant variable cost offset; as prices fell post-2022, the incremental GW capacity generated incremental revenue but at lower capture prices, approximately offsetting fixed cost scale benefits [#A]
- Scale benefits visible: Opex per MW has declined modestly (~3-5%/yr) as fleet scale builds; this is partially offsetting the price headwind but insufficient to expand margins [#I]

Gross margin:
- Gross margin trend: Broadly stable at ~59-61% across L3Y; electricity generation has structurally high gross margins (fuel cost near-zero for wind/solar) [#I]
- No compression at gross level: Cost of revenue (grid fees, O&M, land rents) growing modestly but in line with capacity additions [#A]

EBITDA margin:
- 55% EBITDA margin: Consistent and high by any sector comparison; peer base rate for European wind-dominant utility is 50-60% EBITDA margin [#I]
- Flat but resilient: Margin stability despite price headwind reflects disciplined opex management and growing contracted/hedged revenue share [#J]

EBIT / operating margin:
- Compression from 32% (FY2022) to 27% (FY2024): 5pp EBIT margin compression in 2 years is material; entirely driven by D&A step-up [#A]
- Not a quality concern per se — it's the mechanical result of a high-capex growth strategy — but it directly impairs EPS [#J]

Sustainability assessment:
- Structural elements of 55% EBITDA margin: Contracted/PPAs cover ~60-70% of generation at fixed prices; long-life assets; low variable cost — durable [#J]
- Cyclical risk: ~30-40% of generation is merchant/spot-exposed; power price recovery would expand margins; further normalisation would compress them [#A]
- D&A headwind structural for 3-5 more years: As the commissioning pipeline delivers, D&A will continue rising; EBIT margin recovery requires either power price uplift or pipeline slowdown [#J]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? If European power prices continue normalising toward €60-80/MWh (vs €100-150/MWh in FY2022), EDPR's merchant generation book will face another reset; simultaneously, rising interest rates on refinancing cycles could add €50-100m/yr of finance cost, further squeezing below-the-line margins that are already compressing [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Modest and predictable H2 weighting; wind resource seasonality (stronger autumn/winter in Iberia and Northern Europe) drives modest H2 EBITDA premium; not a significant analytical variable for an annual-view investor [#J]

H1 vs H2 pattern:
- H2 heavier: EDPR's H2 EBITDA has consistently exceeded H1 by approximately €50-150m across the L3Y window; FY2022 H2/H1 split ~53/47; FY2023 ~51/49; FY2024 estimated ~52/48 [#A]
- Cause — wind resource: Iberian and Northwestern European wind resource is historically stronger in Q3-Q4 (autumn/winter), consistent with EDPR's geographic mix (Spain, Portugal, UK, Poland dominant) [#I]
- Solar offset: EDPR's growing solar portfolio (~25-30% of capacity) has H1 generation bias (summer irradiance), partially offsetting wind seasonality — the net seasonal skew is moderating as solar share grows [#J]

Q1 pattern:
- Weakest quarter operationally in years with detailed quarterly operational data; typically weakest in terms of generation and revenue [#I]
- Not extreme: Q1 weakness is typically 10-15% below Q3 generation — manageable and consistent [#I]

Seasonality changing:
- Moderating trend: As US and Brazilian capacity (year-round solar-heavy markets) grows as a share of EDPR's portfolio, the European wind seasonality effect is diluting; directionally positive for earnings smoothness [#J]
- Commissioning timing: EDPR typically commissions large tranches of capacity in H2 (construction season completion), which amplifies H2 revenue recognition in commissioning-heavy years [#A]

Seasonality direction:
- H2 weighting confirmed in FY2022, FY2023, FY2024 — consistent with wind resource patterns and commissioning timing [#I]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? EDPR's H2 skew creates a forecasting risk where H1 results consistently appear weak, inviting sell-side estimate resets mid-year; if H2 commissioning targets slip (as occurred partially in FY2023-2024), the H2 recovery thesis fails and full-year misses accumulate; the apparent consistency of H2 recovery could mask a one-way ratchet that is weakening [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

Credibility verdict: EBITDA line is a modest sandbagger (consistent small beats); net profit/EPS is a consistent miss due to below-the-line cost surprises; management's credibility on operational delivery is reasonable but financial guidance has been systematically optimistic on the EPS line [#J]

Beat/miss frequency:
- EBITDA beat rate: ~5 of 6 formal reporting periods (H1+FY) showed EBITDA at or above consensus; average beat magnitude +1-3% [#A]
- EPS miss rate: ~4 of 6 formal reporting periods missed consensus EPS; average miss magnitude -10 to -25% of consensus estimate [#A]
- Revenue miss rate: ~3 of 6 periods missed consensus revenue, primarily in FY2023-FY2024 as power prices came in below consensus assumptions [#A]

Beat magnitude:
- EBITDA beat: +€20-75m range above consensus in beat periods — modest but consistent; suggests opex discipline and/or conservative EBITDA guidance [#A]
- EPS miss: -€0.05 to -€0.15/share vs consensus in miss periods — material in the context of €0.20-0.46 reported EPS range [#A]

Direction of surprise:
- Consistent pattern: EBITDA beats + EPS misses = below-the-line leakage (D&A, interest, minority interest) coming in worse than modelled [#J]
- D&A surprise: D&A has exceeded consensus estimates in each of FY2023 and FY2024 as commissioning pace accelerated and useful-life assessments were updated for some offshore assets [#A]
- Finance cost surprise: Net finance costs rose above consensus in FY2023 and FY2024 as floating-rate debt repriced and new project finance was drawn at higher rates [#A]

Sandbagging signal:
- EBITDA line: Modest sandbagging signal — EDPR management consistently guides to mid-point EBITDA slightly below what operational trajectory implies; beats are small and relatively consistent [#J]
- EPS line: Opposite of sandbagging — management has consistently implied EPS recovery that has not materialised; the FY2023 and FY2024 annual guidance implied EPS recovery to €0.35-0.45 range that was not delivered [#A]

Guidance accuracy:
- FY2022: EBITDA guidance met/exceeded; EPS guidance approximately met (high-price environment) [#I]
- FY2023: EBITDA guidance ~met; EPS guidance missed by ~25-30% (D&A + interest underestimated) [#A]
- FY2024: EBITDA guidance ~met (-1%); EPS guidance missed by ~20-25% (same structural drivers) [#A]
- Trend in accuracy: Deteriorating on EPS; stable on EBITDA — the gap between management's implied EPS trajectory and delivery has widened in each of the last two FY cycles [#J]

Sell-side revision pattern:
- Pre-release: Sell-side has been systematically reducing EPS estimates in the months preceding FY results for FY2023 and FY2024 — evidence that the market is partially aware of below-the-line pressure but consistently underestimates magnitude [#A]
- Post-release: Post-results revisions in FY2023 and FY2024 were net negative for EPS consensus; EBITDA consensus broadly maintained [#A]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on management credibility? The consistent EPS sandbagging on the downside (missing guidance) while EBITDA beats suggests management either does not have adequate visibility into below-the-line costs (concerning for financial discipline) or is deliberately guiding EPS optimistically to maintain equity story momentum (concerning for trust); if FY2025 delivers a third consecutive EPS miss, the valuation re-rating risk materialises [#J]
