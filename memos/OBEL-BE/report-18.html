§2 — Track Record at Orange Belgium

J-front verdict: Track record at Orange Belgium since March 2021 is characterised by strategic boldness on VOO acquisition but modest financial delivery in an intensely competitive market; jury is still out on execution

Tenure at Orange Belgium:
- March 2021 to present — approximately 4+ years
- Relatively short tenure for a full-cycle assessment; the VOO integration (completed 2023) means the most important execution test is still mid-stream

Revenue at appointment vs current:
- Orange Belgium revenue at appointment (FY2020/early 2021): approximately €1.35–1.40bn
- Revenue has been broadly stable to modestly growing — the Belgian mobile market has faced sustained pricing pressure from Proximus (dominant incumbent) and Telenet/Base competition; pure mobile revenue growth is structurally challenged
- The VOO acquisition added incremental revenue from the cable footprint (Wallonia and Brussels residential broadband/TV), pushing consolidated revenues toward the €1.5–1.6bn range in more recent periods
- Revenue trajectory: flat-to-modest organic growth; M&A-inflated top-line more recently

Margin at appointment vs current:
- EBITDA margin at appointment: approximately 30–32% EBITDAaL margin (after leases, standard European telco reporting convention)
- Margin has been maintained rather than expanded organically — cost discipline has offset competitive revenue pressure; no margin expansion story to point to on pure operational grounds
- Post-VOO, margin dynamics are complicated by integration costs and the step-up in D&A from the cable asset base; underlying margin trajectory is broadly stable
- Margin trajectory: defended rather than expanded; not a margin-expansion CEO story

EPS trajectory:
- EPS has been broadly flat during Pichon's tenure on an underlying basis; the VOO acquisition has introduced D&A drag and integration costs that suppress reported EPS near-term
- The investment thesis depends on EPS expansion as convergence ARPU lifts and integration costs roll off — this is a forward-looking story, not a delivered-EPS story

Strategic shifts executed:
- VOO residential cable acquisition (announced 2021, cleared by Belgian competition authority 2022, operational integration 2023): the defining strategic act — Orange Belgium acquired the residential cable assets of VOO (covering Wallonia + Brussels) for approximately €200m (subject to various adjustments); transforms Orange Belgium from mobile-challenger to converged fixed-mobile operator
- Rationale: convergence is the dominant value-creation thesis in European telco; Orange Belgium was structurally disadvantaged as a mobile-only player; acquiring cable assets provides the fixed-line anchor for bundle selling, ARPU uplift, and churn reduction
- Outcome so far: integration is operational; commercial launch of convergence bundles has proceeded; early convergence penetration data has been positive in Wallonia but the commercial ramp is still in progress
- 5G roll-out: Belgium was a late market for 5G spectrum relative to peers (regulatory and political delays); Pichon has managed the 5G deployment programme, including network sharing arrangements
- Cost efficiency programme: Orange Belgium has maintained cost discipline through structural cost reduction initiatives (network modernisation, operational efficiency); not a dramatic cost-cutting story but steady execution

M&A:
- VOO residential is the only material M&A transaction under Pichon's tenure
- Pre-Pichon, Orange Belgium had a mobile-only footprint supplemented by some enterprise fixed services; the VOO deal is a genuine strategic transformation
- Integration outcome: too early for a clean ROIC verdict — the cable footprint requires continued capex for modernisation (including fibre upgrade plans) and the convergence commercial ramp takes 3-5 years to generate full ROIC; the acquisition price of ~€200m appears reasonable relative to the cable subscriber base, but convergence execution is the swing variable

Capital allocation history:
- Orange Belgium has maintained a consistent ordinary dividend throughout Pichon's tenure, consistent with Group parent income requirements (Orange SA depends on dividends from subsidiaries)
- No share buybacks of note — the VOO acquisition absorbed available capital; leverage modestly elevated post-acquisition but manageable given strong FCF generation
- Capital allocation priority sequence reads as: (1) dividend maintenance (Group requirement), (2) convergence capex / VOO integration, (3) 5G network investment
- Owner-allocator signal is absent: no evidence of aggressive buybacks, special dividends, or unconventional capital return; allocation is orderly and Group-consistent
