Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The business operates a highly integrated, resilient multimetal network, though structural returns remain constrained by complex feedstock sourcing.

Sourcing models and segment operations

1.
Dual segment reporting: Sourcing documents confirm that Aurubis' organizational and segment reporting framework is divided into two primary operating segments, Custom Smelting & Products, which processes primary concentrates, and Multimetal Recycling, which focuses on secondary scrap copper
2.
Primary smelting assets: The Custom Smelting & Products segment operates primary smelting plants in Hamburg, Germany and Pirdop, Bulgaria, converting raw concentrates into refined copper cathodes and precious metals
3.
Circular recycling loop: Sourcing documents show the Multimetal Recycling segment manages specialized secondary plants in Germany, Belgium, and Spain, recovering critical metals from electronic waste and industrial residues
4.
Transactional copper sourcing: Sourcing models show procurement relies on a circular model where Aurubis takes back production scrap from downstream copper fabricators and returns high-purity copper cathodes, securing long-term partner relationships
5.
Sizable chemical by-products: Smelting primary concentrates produces massive volumes of sulfur dioxide, which Aurubis processes into 2.2 million tonnes of sulfuric acid annually, acting as a highly profitable chemical revenue stream

Value chain and competitive positioning

6.
Highly diversified customers: Procurement analysis shows that the company's customer base is highly fragmented, with no single commercial partner accounting for more than 10% of total group revenues
7.
Strong competitive barriers: Sourcing advantages arise from proprietary metallurgical capabilities to process complex, low-grade scrap feedstocks, creating a significant barrier to entry against less advanced recyclers
8.
Fragmented global competition: Primary smelting operations compete with low-cost smelters in China and Japan, while secondary recycling operations face local scrap collection networks and specialized metal processors
9.
Favourable sourcing target: Recycling margins are structurally higher than primary smelting, prompting Aurubis to target 50% recycled content in copper cathode production by 2030 to expand margins
10.
Protective domestic tariffs: Sourced expert interviews suggest the North American scrap market is shielded by 50% tariffs on semi-finished imports, incentivizing regional manufacturers to purchase refined cathodes directly from domestic US producers
11.
Tight raw feedstock: Metal purchasing experts indicate that high-grade electronic waste like printed circuit boards remains structurally supply-constrained, which limits the physical capacity utilization of global pyrometallurgical smelters
12.
Other side of the story: The primary smelting segment's profitability could structurally collapse if intensifying global mine supply deficits cause concentrate treatment charges to settle below the smelter's cash break-even of $60 per tonne

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Multi-year performance shows a highly volatile operating margin profile where massive capital expansion has temporarily diluted returns on capital.

Financial performance and margins

1.
Volatile operating income: Sourced results indicate that operating EBT fell 14% to €355 million in FY 2024/25 from €413 million in FY 2023/24, reflecting severe copper concentrate raw material bottlenecks
2.
Diluted capital returns: Operating ROCE fell to 8.8% in FY 2024/25 from 11.5% in FY 2023/24, reflecting the capital-intensive strategic capex drag of growth assets still undergoing construction
3.
Constrained free cash: Heavy growth capital expenditure of €757 million in FY 2024/25 resulted in a negative free cash flow of €-116 million, delaying the company's expected cash inflection
4.
Strong recent recovery: The most recent H1 2025/26 results beat expectations with revenues rising 23% to €11,320 million, supported by elevated precious metal volumes and recovering scrap charges

Earnings quality and guidance tracking

5.
Sizable inventory impacts: The company's earnings quality is impacted by massive differences between IFRS and operating results, requiring a €-410 million operating EBT adjustment in FY 2024/25 to strip out non-cash metal price fluctuations
6.
Elevated metal exposure: Sourced analysis shows that unhedged metal gains from copper, gold, and silver have risen to represent 40% of group gross profit, which exposes earnings to commodity price volatility
7.
Conservative guidance history: Management has a clear history of conservative guidance tracking, as they raised full-year operating EBT guidance twice in H1 2025/26 to reach €425 million to €525 million
8.
Startup margin headwind: Earnings are temporarily diluted by €50 million in annual startup losses associated with the phased commissioning of strategic recycling assets
9.
Continuous returns drag: Operating ROCE contracted further to 8.1% in H1 2025/26, reflecting the unproductive capital drag of strategic assets that have not yet contributed positive cash flows
10.
Other side of the story: The forecast free cash flow recovery could fail if sustained working capital expansion from rising metal prices consumes all operational cash, keeping the business cash-negative despite profitable metal processing

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case remains highly transitionary and mixed, as massive US recycling growth is countered by persistent primary smelting headwinds.

Operational drivers of expansion

1.
Greenfield Richmond asset: Sourcing documents confirm that the primary driver of forward EPS growth is the greenfield Richmond recycling plant in Georgia, USA, which is designed to process 180,000 tonnes of complex scrap annually
2.
Slower commissioning curve: Richmond Module 1 commenced gradual commissioning in September 2025 under a 12-month schedule, while Module 2 is planned to begin commissioning in summer 2026
3.
Sizable EBITDA targets: Richmond is guided to deliver €170 million in annual EBITDA by FY 2028/29, which represents more than 65% of Aurubis' total €260 million growth-related EBITDA target
4.
Optimistic unit margins: Sourced models indicate that Richmond's implied unit EBITDA margin is roughly 3.5x higher than the five-year average of Aurubis' European recycling operations
5.
Structural margin cushions: Higher US unit margins are supported by lower domestic energy costs (one-third of German levels) and wider procurement discounts on local copper scrap feedstock

Market and cyclical tailwinds

6.
Severe sulfuric tightness: Global sulfuric acid prices have spiked due to Middle East logistics disruptions, acting as an important tailwind for Aurubis, which produces 2.2 million tonnes of acid annually
7.
Sizable EBT sensitivity: Sourced analysis shows that a 10% move in sulfuric acid prices impacts group operating EBT by €47 million, though the benefit is lagged since 85% of acid volumes are contracted
8.
Cyclical scrap recovery: European scrap refining charges have recovered from 2025 troughs, supported by higher copper prices and tighter export regulations designed to retain scrap within Europe
9.
Compound execution risk: For the bullish consensus target of over €900 million EBITDA by FY 2028/29 to materialize, all strategic growth drivers must fire in perfect synchronization, with no further commissioning delays
10.
Other side of the story: The flagship Richmond facility's earnings could permanently disappoint if unexpected technical smelting bottlenecks prevent Aurubis from processing complex, low-grade electronic scrap feedstock at scale

Prudent target prices: Sourced analysis indicates that sell-side target prices of €181.00 per share imply downside to the current stock level of over €195.00, suggesting that earnings tailwinds are fully priced

Optimistic bull scenarios: For the bull-case share price target of €238.00 to materialize, the market must assume a sustained 8.0x EV/EBITDA multiple alongside zero hedging of free metal exposure from FY 2026/27
6.
Other side of the story: The stock could face a severe structural de-rating down to €67.00 per share if commodity prices reverse, scrap refining charges contract, and Richmond fails to achieve positive EBITDA contributions

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side` 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is highly elevated due to lingering compliance loopholes from past fraud and costly project execution delays.

Sourcing, fraud, and control failures

1.
Sizable metals theft: The company was impacted by a massive €169 million pretax inventory shortfall in FY 2022/23, driven by organized criminal theft and inflated billing of scrap metals
2.
Costly control upgrades: Remediation required "middle double-digit millions" in security expenses since September 2023, alongside an extra €300m compliance investment at the Hamburg plant
3.
Unresolved compliance gaps: Control analysts warn that ongoing criminal investigations mean that unresolved internal process loopholes and residual compliance risks could still exist in the procurement network
4.
Protracted Richmond returns: Execution delays at the flagship Richmond recycling plant have already pushed back the first positive EBITDA contributions, which were initially expected in the current fiscal year
5.
Costly startup bottlenecks: Richmond faced technical challenges in Q2 2025/26, generating a "low double-digit million earnings impact" and eliminating previous break-even targets
6.
Deceptive premium signals: A dangerous "false friend" is the record copper cathode premium of €128 per tonne, which could temporarily mask structural volume declines or persistent project execution delays
7.
Other side of the story: Risks of prolonged capex slippages could be overblown if new management's capital allocation discipline successfully restricts future annual baseline capital expenditure to €300m - €400m

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Executive board leadership is a pivotal driver of strategic change following a complete operational overhaul post the fraud scandal.
1.
Complete executive turnover: Sourcing documents confirm that the supervisory board replaced almost the entire executive board, including the former CEO, CFO, and COO, in response to the €169 million fraud scandal
2.
Refocused management mandate: CEO Toralf Haag, CFO Steffen Hoffmann, COO MMR Inge Hofkens, and COO CSP Tim Kurth were brought in with a clear mandate to make processes waterproof
3.
Disciplined capital pivot: Sourcing reports state that the new management team has officially abandoned plans for a capital-intensive battery recycling facility, opting to focus on smart-capex and process debottlenecking
4.
Formalized risk integration: Leadership has integrated "Risk Awareness" as one of seven core topics in its "Power for Performance" campaign, conducting group-wide training and aligning with best-practice internal control systems

Section summary
  1. Aurubis does not warrant deeper work for a concentrated strategy because the stock already trades at peak cyclical valuation levels.

Strongest cyclical catalyst: Sourcing documents suggest that the most compelling positive driver is the sharp cyclical recovery in European scrap refining charges combined with persistent, long-term global sulfuric acid supply tightness
2.
Other side of the story: Sourcing analysis indicates that the most critical bear counter-read is that the execution timeline for Richmond's EBITDA uplift is highly protracted, meaning that near-term ROCE of 7% to 8% will continue to lag the company's cost of capital
3.
Immediate analytical action: Sourced analysis advises reviewing upcoming Richmond operational updates during the summer of 2026 to verify whether Phase 2 commissioning has commenced without further technical delays
4.
Secondary capital monitor: Financial documents suggest investigating strategic capital decisions regarding further US downstream expansion, which are scheduled to be finalized this summer, to assess potential capital expenditure slippage
