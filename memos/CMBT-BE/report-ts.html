Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. CMB.TECH dominates the maritime value chain by integrating five core shipping segments with proprietary dual-fuel engine technology.

Fleet Composition and Operational Segments

1.
Highly diversified segments: The Marine division owns and operates five distinct brands: Bocimar (dry bulk), Euronav (tankers), Bochem (chemicals), Delphis (containers), and Windcat (offshore wind services)
2.
Stellar dry bulk footprint: Bocimar is the largest division, operating Newcastlemax and Capesize bulkers on spot voyages to monetize elevated bulk freight rates
3.
Streamlined tanker fleet: Euronav has been downsized by selling older vessels at market peaks, retaining 10.00 VLCCs, 17.00 Suezmaxes, and 2.00 Floating Storage Units
4.
Contracted container niche: Delphis owns medium-sized container vessels that are fully fixed on long-term time charters to secure highly stable, multi-year revenues
5.
Resilient chemical tankers: Bochem operates modern stainless steel chemical carriers, with 70.00% of the fleet fixed on stable multi-year contracts
6.
High-margin offshore wind: Windcat operates 53.00 Crew Transfer Vessels and several Commissioning Service Operation Vessels under lucrative short-to-medium term time charters

Cleantech Innovation and Competitive Moat

7.
Pioneering dual-fuel technology: CMB.TECH develops dual-fuel hydrogen-diesel and ammonia-diesel combustion engines to ensure long-term regulatory compliance without operational risk
8.
Proprietary engine joint-ventures: Joint ventures like BeHydro design and manufacture next-generation hydrogen combustion engines, creating unmatched intellectual property barriers
9.
Strategic WinGD partnership: The company co-develops WinGD's X-DF-A ammonia engines, which achieved identical thermal efficiency to diesel during Swiss testing with only 5.00% pilot fuel consumption
10.
Integrated value chain: Unlike pure-play shipowners, CMB.TECH develops H2 Infra to secure green fuel molecule supply and H2 Industry to manufacture land-based hydrogen applications
11.
Formidable competitive moat: The combined green fleet and proprietary technology create massive switching costs and a first-mover advantage under strict environmental laws
12.
Favorable industry consolidation: Tight shipyard capacity and rising compliance costs favor consolidated giants like CMB.TECH over fragmented, cash-constrained small shipowners
13.
Other side of the story: Delayed environmental mandates or slow infrastructure rollouts could leave the company with redundant green technology and uncompetitive asset values if conventional fuels remain cheaper
