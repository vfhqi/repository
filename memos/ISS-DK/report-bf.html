EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 16 findings

ISS A/S operates a highly resilient, asset-light self-delivery model across integrated facility services, employing 325,000 placemakers globally to deliver cleaning, technical, food, and workplace services to over 40,000 corporate and public customers. The business demonstrates robust recurring revenue characteristics with 84% of sales derived from committed multi-year contracts, though structural exposure to office-based hybrid work models and persistent single-service margin pressures pose long-term profitability risks.

Business Model and Revenue Structure

1.
Core service dominance: Cleaning remains the foundational legacy business accounting for 42% of total Group revenue in fiscal year 2025, where management maintains clear ambition to remain the undisputed global leader. Integrated Facility Services (IFS) represents 52% of sales while single service accounts for 48%, with IFS providing clients coordinated multi-service solutions managed through a single operational contact point
2.
Balanced vertical exposure: Office-based customers form the largest segment at 41% of Group revenue, followed by production-based clients at 21% and healthcare at 15%, creating diversified demand across knowledge-based sectors, regulated life sciences, and public healthcare institutions
3.
Revenue predictability and pricing power: Portfolio contracts of 3-5 years generate 84% of revenue as committed recurring streams, while built-in indexation clauses cover approximately 80% of contracts to pass through labor inflation, though some customers demand reduced contract scope rather than absorbing full price hikes under tough conditions
4.
concentration and performance variance: Northern Europe is the largest region generating 38% of total Group revenue, followed by Central and Southern Europe at 35%, while Americas contributes only 9% of sales but suffers from prolonged operational and margin underperformance requiring active turnaround investment

Financial Profile and Peer Benchmarking

5.
Revenue scale and margin recovery: ISS reported total revenue of DKK 84.7 billion for fiscal year 2025 with projected growth to DKK 90.1 billion in FY26, while adjusted EBITDA margin reached 6.8% in FY25 compared to 6.1% in FY23, demonstrating steady operational recovery under the OneISS strategy
6.
margin gap versus peers: Lower margin profiles support a record 43% NTM P/E discount to higher-margin pure-play caterers, with ISS operating profit margin reaching 5.0% in FY25 compared to peers Compass and Sodexo maintaining superior EBIT margins at 7.8% and 4.7% respectively
7.
deleveraging and cash generation: Gearing improved to 2.3x in FY25 firmly within the 2.0x to 2.5x target range, while cash conversion beat guidance reaching 64% in FY25, and return on invested capital reached 11.0% aligned with the median of ex-ISS peers
8.
Return profile outlier: Compass Group acts as a clear returns outlier, achieving superior ROIC of 18.0% through North American scale and food procurement advantages that ISS cannot readily replicate in its soft servicesdominated portfolio

Industry Structure and Competitive Landscape

9.
Value chain positioning and margin distribution: ISS operates at the integrated stage of the value chain bypassing sub-contractor margin leaks, with single service delivery representing a low-barrier stage yielding thin operating margins of 2-4% while the Integrated Facility Services stage consolidates multiple lines to capture healthier operating margins of 6-8%
10.
scale and differentiation dynamics: Compass Group and Sodexo operate as dominant global peers generating revenues two to three times larger than ISS, with Compass leveraging massive catering scale to drive superior EBITA margins of 7.5% while focusing strictly on food services and utilizing support services only as a tactical layer to defend contracts
11.
Evolving competitive threats: Sodexo's new leadership is transitioning from margin protection to aggressive revenue bidding threatening pricing discipline, while corporate real estate brokers like CBRE and JLL rely heavily on subcontractors but are acquiring technical capabilities, and technology integration creates substantial barriers to entry for smaller regional competitors through proprietary AI systems

Customer and Supplier Dynamics

12.
High retention with fragmented renewal exposure: Customer base is diversified across more than 40,000 clients with group customer retention maintained at 94% on an LTM basis in Q1 2026, while built-in indexation clauses cover approximately 80% of contracts to successfully pass through labor cost inflation protecting absolute operating margins
13.

14.

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution
