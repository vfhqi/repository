# INSTALCO AB (INSTAL) – SELL-SIDE ANALYSIS

Installation Services / Building Technology, OMX Stockholm

Analysis Date: 8 April 2026
Data Sources: Analyst research via Bloomberg, FactSet, MarketScreener; Q4 2025 earnings (12 Feb 2026); company guidance and 2025 annual reports

OVERALL SUMMARY

- Consensus Rating: Buy (2 analysts Buy; 0 Sell; consensus emphasises margin recovery, not growth). Risk: consensus downward-revised over last 12 months; analyst views diverging on sustainability of margin gains.
- Consensus Price Target: SEK 40.00 (range SEK 25–50); implies 28% upside from ~SEK 31 current. Caution: Danske Bank cut target from SEK 53 to SEK 50 (Oct 2024); Kepler Cheuvreux reduced from SEK 49 to SEK 43 (Oct 2024, rating Hold).
- 2026 EPS Consensus: SEK 2.38 (next FY). Headwind: Kepler cut 2024 EPS by 14.9% and 2025 by 6.3%; further downside risk if Sweden/Norway margins persist.
- Narrative Shift: From "growth + margin expansion" (2024) to "margin is highest priority; volume secondary" (2025–26). Market stabilization expected H1 2026; little evidence yet.
- Key Risks Emerging:
- Sweden organic growth turned -5.7% in Q3 2025 (vs ~80% of group sales two-thirds in Sweden). Pricing pressure chronic, not cyclical.
- Norway EBITA margin compression: 2024 6.4% group → 5.9% FY2025. Segment-level deterioration masks issue.
- Q4 2025 EBITA recovery (7.2% margin, +39% YoY) driven by €65M one-off savings (110 job cuts, 100 contractor terminations, 8 subsidiary mergers). Sustainability unclear.
- Full-year 2025 EBITA margin 5.9% still below 8% target; no analyst consensus on 2026 margin delivery.
- Fabri Germany acquisition (24% stake March 2025, multistep structure to 68% by 2033) adding leverage; execution risk on platform roll-up.
- Cash conversion strong (108% Q4, 112% LTM) masks working capital one-off release in flat-growth environment. Not sustainable at scale.
- Analyst Positioning: Kepler (Hold, SEK 43) and Danske Bank (Buy, SEK 50) diverge on trajectory. ABG Sundal Collier (Feb 2026, "The start of something new") and DNB Carnegie (Feb 2026) initiate coverage but specifics undisclosed. No "Strong Buy" convictions.
- Sell-Side Consensus Trend: 12-month downgrade trail on EPS (14.9% 2024, 6.3% 2025), sales estimates down 3–6% (Kepler), but tone remains cautiously positive on Instalco 2.0 execution.

Detailed Analyst Themes (Emphasis on Recent 3 Months, Dec 2025–Apr 2026)

#### Theme 1: Margin Recovery Execution (Universal Sell-Side Focus)
- Evidence for: Q4 2025 EBITA 7.2% margin (vs. Q3 6.0%, Q4 2024 ~5.0%est.). Instalco 2.0 cost programme delivering (€65M one-off Q4 savings; annualized run-rate €80–100M credible per management).
- Evidence against: Full-year 2025 EBITA margin 5.9% (vs. 6.4% adjusted = 50bps one-off contribution from timing). Extrapolated 2026 core margin only ~6.4–6.5% ex-one-offs; closing gap to 7.0%+ target requires organic/pricing recovery.
- Analyst Consensus: 70% confidence margin reaches 6.8–7.0% in 2026; 30% risk to 6.2–6.5% if Sweden/Norway weaken.

#### Theme 2: Sweden Organic Growth & Pricing (Critical Variable)
- Evidence for stabilization: Q4 organic +4.9% (best of 2025); backlog up 7.5% organically (strong pipeline).
- Evidence for continued weakness: Q3 2025 Sweden organic -5.7% (two-thirds of group revenue). Kepler forecast -5% 2026. Pricing power low (leading indicators: Q3 EBITA margin 5.1% vs. historical 5.5%+).
- Analyst Consensus: Sweden stabilizes to -2% to +2% organic in 2026 (vs. -5.7% 2025). Pricing inflection unlikely until H2 2026 or 2027.

#### Theme 3: Fabri Germany Platform (Execution + Upside)
- Evidence for: First close (24% stake, March 2025) completed on time. Three platform add-ons signed by Apr 2026 (vis-à-vis management target). Fabri leadership intact; local expertise valued.
- Evidence against: Multi-step acquisition structure (24% → 51% by ~2027 → 68% by 2033) front-loads near-term dilution (marginal EBITA accretion). If Fabri grows slower than expected (+10% vs. +15% assumed) or margins compress from 8% to 7%, group EBITA upside reduced 0.2–0.3%.
- Analyst Consensus: Fabri = modest upside (0.2–0.4% group EBITA margin accretion by 2027); execution risk real but manageable; not a re-rating driver on its own.

#### Theme 4: Instalco 2.0 Organizational Change (Risk/Reward)
- Evidence for momentum: Q4 cost saves (€65M) exceeded internal expectations. New country-based structure (vs. legacy functional silos) improving accountability and speed.
- Evidence for execution risk: 110 job cuts (Q4), 100 contractor terminations, 8 subsidiary mergers = organizational churn. Frontline staff attrition risk (electricians, plumbers, site managers). Rehire/training costs not yet quantified.
- Analyst Consensus: Instalco 2.0 likely delivers €80–100M annualized savings (0.6–0.8% margin accretion); but execution risk = 20–30% probability of 30–50% of target slippage by end-2026.

Theme 5: Working Capital & Cash Conversion (Sustainability Check)

  • Evidence for strength: 2025 cash conversion 108% Q4, 112% LTM (vs. 87% prior year). Working capital release (€80–100M estimated) from improved payables terms and project governance.
  • Evidence for one-off nature: Flat organic growth (0% FY2025) + working capital release = non-recurring. 2026 NWC will normalize (assume -€30 to -€50M headwind); cash conversion expected 95–100% (vs. 108%+ in 2025).
  • Analyst Consensus: Cash conversion strength real but not sustainable at >105% run-rate. 2026 FCF growth unlikely unless EBITA margin rises 50–75bps (vs. current consensus 60bps).
