EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Equinor faces declining employee confidence in senior management and strategic ambitions while confronting growing activist pressure for corporate separation, though the Norwegian state's 67% ownership effectively blocks structural change. Management has systematically downgraded renewables ambitions , slashing

transition capex by 50% and lowering the 2030 generation target from 12-16 GW to 10-12 GW. Core oil and gas developments have successfully delivered major start-ups at Johan Castberg and Bacalhau , bolstering near-term cash generation, though this forward momentum is shadowed by managed production declines at the flagship Johan Sverdrup field .

Materiality of Change Signal

The Board Chair transition to Jarle Roth on July 1, 2026 occurs amid ongoing stability in senior executive leadership , with CEO Anders Opedal maintaining continuity in strategic direction since November 2020. However, the 2024 Global People Survey revealed diminishing belief in senior management and Equinor's strategic level of ambition , with internal friction rising as employees express lower confidence in executing the company's energy transition strategy . The sudden departure of renewables head Pål Eitrheim only two months

after the controversial Ørsted investment signals material organizational turbulence in the transition strategy.

Strategic pivots have been substantial, including lowering the 2030 renewable capacity target to 10-12 GW, slashing planned renewables and low-carbon investments by 50% to $5 billion by 2027, reducing the 2030 Net Carbon Intensity reduction target to 5-15% from 15-20%, and canceling the prior pledge to allocate over 50% of

gross capex to transition solutions by 2030. The net signal indicates high materiality of strategic change driven by capital discipline imperatives but weak materiality of governance change due to state-controlled board dynamics.
