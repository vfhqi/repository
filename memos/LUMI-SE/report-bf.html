EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 17 findings

Business model and strategic positioning

1.
Lundin Mining successfully transformed into a high-growth Latin American copper pure-play after divesting European zinc and US nickel assets, concentrating 85% of revenue exposure on copper and positioning the company to become a top-ten global copper producer targeting over 500,000 tonnes annual output. The flagship Candelaria Copper Mining Complex in Atacama, Chile represents the historical production core, while Caserones, Chapada, and the gigantic Vicuña joint venture with BHP provide decades of tier-one base metal growth. The company is advancing the high-altitude Vicuña joint venture with BHP to unlock multi-generational base metal volumes, modeled as a top-five global copper, gold, and silver mine
2.
The company operates under a contractual sales structure with multi-year agreements to strategic Asian and European smelters, though customer concentration spiked severely in Q1 2026 with the largest customer representing 51% of total sales, creating elevated counterparty risk. Rising market prices unlocked $268.80 million in positive provisional adjustments in FY2025, demonstrating significant revenue leverage to commodity price movements. Strategic leadership under CEO Jack Lundin since December 2023 has executed aggressive portfolio streamlining and launched company-wide Full Potential operational excellence programs delivering measurable unit cost savings and throughput gains

Financial profile and peer benchmarking

3.
Lundin Mining delivered record FY2025 revenue of $4.05 billion and adjusted EBITDA of $1.92 billion, representing a 46% EBITDA margin that significantly outperforms Freeport-McMoRan's 35% and Teck Resources' estimated 38%. Revenue grew at a calculated 3-year CAGR of 10.04% from FY2022 to FY2025, exceeding Freeport-McMoRan's 4.40% and Vale's estimated 2.00%, driven by copper price strength and strategic M&A. Return on capital employed reached 14.70% in FY2025, outperforming several large diversified miners, while adjusted return on equity stood at 12.50%, tracking in line with mid-cycle peer averages
4.
The company achieved a net cash position of $249.40 million by March 2026 after fully repaying its $1.15 billion term loan, transitioning to zero financial leverage while upsizing its revolving credit facility to $4.50 billion to fund Vicuña construction. Capital expenditure reached $669 million for FY2025, with sustaining capital at $477.80 million and expansionary spending of $191.20 million focused on Saúva and Vicuña development, reflecting a capital intensity of 16.51% that remains competitive versus Teck Resources' elevated 25%. Group C1 cash costs averaged $1.87 per pound in FY2025, positioning Lundin favorably against First Quantum's elevated $2.51 per pound
5.
However, headline free cash flow in FY2025 was significantly impacted by a massive $414.00 million working capital build-up, illustrating severe liquidity strain from shipment delays and concentrate inventory accumulation risks

Lundin Mining operates exclusively at the upstream mining and milling stage, capturing the highest value-chain margin accrual of 40-60% gross margin and 25-45% EBIT margin, while avoiding downstream smelting and refining stages that generate only 5-15% gross margin and 2-8% EBIT margin. The company maintains high vertical integration at Candelaria through wholly-owned desalination plants and the Punta Padrones port, while strategically outsourcing Vicuña's Stage 3 offsite infrastructure to conserve upfront capital but accepting permanent opex exposure to third-party tariff inflation
