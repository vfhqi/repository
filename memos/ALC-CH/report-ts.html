Section 3 – What the company does 17 findings

Section summary
  1. Alcon dominates the highly sticky ophthalmic market through an unparalleled integrated ecosystem of surgical consoles, high-volume consumables, daily contact lenses, and high-margin ocular therapeutics

Segment operations and offerings

1.
Dominant surgical portfolio: The Surgical franchise provides a complete one-stop shop for ophthalmic surgeons, spanning high-volume equipment, consumables, and implantables
2.
Large consumables scale: Consumables represent Alcon's largest surgical division, generating $3,028.00 million in sales from single-use surgical cassettes and hand-held instruments
3.
Premium lens solutions: Advanced technology intraocular lenses represent the fastest-growing part of the implantable lens market, which patients pay for primarily out-of-pocket
4.
Contact lens offerings: The contact lens business, generating $2,770.00 million, is led by daily disposable and weekly contact lens portfolios like TOTAL30 and Precision7
5.
Ocular health expansion: Ocular health includes dry eye drops, ocular allergy drops, and glaucoma pharmaceuticals, contributing $1,798.00 million in reported sales

Value chain and competitive moats

6.
High switching costs: Proprietary surgical cassettes lock centers into long-term consumables purchase contracts once Alcon hardware is installed in the operating room
7.
Strong vertical integration: Alcon leverages its dominant equipment installed base to secure long-term contractual commitments for its intraocular lens portfolios
8.
Resident relationship moat: Alcon actively donates equipment and provides early educational training to residents, securing long-term brand preference
9.
Ocular pharmaceutical expansion: The company's massive $990.00 million R&D budget in 2025 represents an unmatched investment in pipeline innovation
10.
Clinical brand equity: Practitioners highly respect Alcon's long-standing specialist track record, noting its clinical precision and reliability

Market dynamics and structures

11.
Sticky market dynamics: The global ophthalmic surgery and vision care markets exhibit highly defensive and consolidated characteristics
12.
Demographic demand tailwinds: Aging populations and screen-time-driven myopia structurally support mid-single-digit global eye care demand growth
13.
Defensive out of pocket: Patients exhibit high resilience in out-of-pocket eye care spending, rarely delaying recommended cataract surgery
14.
Formidable global leadership: Alcon holds the uncontested number one market position across phacoemulsification consoles, consumables, and presbyopia-correcting lenses
15.
Aggressive competitor portfolios: Competitors like Bausch + Lomb and Johnson and Johnson are rapidly expanding their rival premium and toric lens portfolios
16.
Other side of the story: Ophthalmic surgeons note that rival platforms such as Bausch + Lomb's enVista are priced up to $100.00 cheaper than Alcon's premium Clareon, which could incentivize price-sensitive private clinics to switch
