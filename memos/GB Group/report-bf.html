GB Group plc is a global digital identity and location verification platform operator that processes over 65 billion transactions annually through two highly complementary core offerings: Identity (61% of revenue) and Location (31% of revenue). The company serves more than 20,000 customers globally across financial services, ecommerce, gaming, and technology verticals. Management launched the flagship GBG Go unified orchestration platform in April 2025, which has surpassed adoption expectations with over 100 customer contracts secured and more than 225 qualified prospects in the pipeline.

topic-specific reports The company operates within a $50 billion global digital identity and fraud prevention market that is expanding rapidly due to AI-driven fraud threats and tightening regulatory mandates. Synthetic identity fraud losses are projected to exceed $23 billion by 2030 , while deepfake-related fraud losses are expected to reach $40 billion by 2027 . GB Group occupies the high-margin platform orchestration stage of the value chain, sitting between upstream credit bureau data suppliers and downstream enterprise customers. The platform orchestrates verification workflows by integrating over 200 global suppliers to deliver real-time identity checks embedded directly inside critical customer onboarding systems.

The competitive landscape exhibits a dual structure of intense fragmentation among specialized point-solution providers and high concentration among credit bureau oligopolists like Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. Value chain dynamics operate under a complex "coopetition" framework where credit bureaus act as critical data suppliers while simultaneously competing with their own downstream fraud prevention suites. GB Group's multi-source triangulation model delivers superior match rates over single credit bureaus, with former executives noting 10% to 15% higher verification accuracy . the leadership of CEO Dev Dhiman , who joined from Experian, the company has executed a focused global turnaround of the historically underperforming Americas Identity division. The segment returned to growth in Q4 FY26 following strengthened leadership and disciplined customer segmentation. Operational improvements compressed customer time-to-revenue by over 50% , accelerating commercial conversion cycles. Management is transitioning from fragmented regional brands to a unified global functional operating model to reduce operational complexity and improve cross-selling efficiency. 13

From a foundation quality perspective, GB Group benefits from substantial structural customer moats driven by deep API and SDK integrations that create high switching costs. Unified platform orchestration via GBG Go embeds modules directly inside critical business onboarding workflows, creating substantial structural barriers to switching competitors. Proprietary data networks spanning over 200 external data suppliers and 470 datasets across 200 countries protect against direct competitive duplication by local point-solution providers. Entry is restricted by the extreme data intensity and data licensing requirements needed to achieve high verification match rates, requiring massive capital investments to establish comprehensive global registries.

, the business faces persistent structural risks from upstream credit bureau verticalization threats. Operating as a pure-play data intelligence aggregator exposes GB Group to structural upstream pricing power, and upstream credit bureaus could aggressively verticalize by restricting core registries or undercutting on bundled software pricing, rendering pure-play platform models structurally unviable. Equifax represents the largest individual data provider in GBG's cost structure, historically accounting for 12% of total data costs . Financial performance faces risk if a major supplier stops providing data, representing a persistent operational threat to software delivery.
