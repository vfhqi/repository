§6 — Volume Pattern Analysis

VERDICT: [#J] Volume is contracting as price recovers — ADV 1M (63,750) is materially below ADV 3M (83,589), a classic negative divergence that undermines the validity of the recent price recovery as a genuine trend reversal.

  • VOLUME COMPARISON: [#I] ADV 1M = 63,750 vs ADV 3M = 83,589 — the 1-month average is 31.1% below the 3-month average in proportional terms, or approximately 19,839 shares/day lower.
  • This is not a marginal divergence — a ~24% gap between 1M and 3M ADV is a significant contraction in trading interest over the more recent period.

  • In absolute terms, a daily volume of ~63,750 shares for a stock at €41.56 represents approximately €2.65M of daily traded value — thin by institutional standards.

  • DIVERGENCE INTERPRETATION: [#A] Price rising on declining volume is a textbook negative divergence in momentum analysis — the standard interpretation is that the price advance lacks broad participation and may not be sustained.

  • Volume is the "fuel" for price moves in momentum frameworks; a rally that proceeds on below-average volume suggests that sellers are stepping back (not necessarily that buyers are stepping forward with conviction).

  • The distinction matters: price rising on declining volume can persist for a period but historically has a higher reversal rate than price rising on expanding volume.

  • ACCUMULATION VS DISTRIBUTION: [#A] Without day-level data, it is not possible to confirm whether the recent price advance has occurred on above-average up-day volume vs down-day volume — the aggregate ADV comparison is the only signal available.

  • The aggregate signal (lower 1M ADV vs 3M ADV) is consistent with a distribution environment where heavy sellers have already exited and volume is drying up, but not yet with active institutional accumulation.

  • A true accumulation signature would show 1M ADV exceeding 3M ADV, ideally with a meaningful spike on specific up-days — the opposite of what is observed here.

  • LIQUIDITY CONTEXT: [#I] ADV 1M of 63,750 shares at €41.56 = approximately €2.65M daily traded value; ADV 3M of 83,589 shares = approximately €3.47M.

  • For a mid-cap European stock, this is moderate-to-thin liquidity — a position of meaningful size (€5M+) would take 2–3 days to build, meaning any significant institutional buying would likely be visible in the volume data as it occurs.

  • The thinning volume (declining from 3M to 1M ADV) suggests institutions are not currently building positions in size.

  • VOLUME TREND IMPLICATION: [#A] Declining volume during a price recovery often resolves in one of two ways:

  • Constructive: volume contracts as price stabilises in a tight range (VCP pattern), then expands sharply on a breakout — this would be bullish if observed.
  • Bearish: volume remains low, price drifts upward on thin activity, then rolls over as selling pressure returns — the current configuration is more consistent with this scenario.

  • Without the BP groups passing and the MA200 flattening, the constructive interpretation is premature.

  • SCEPTIC: [#J] The 1M ADV is 24% below the 3M ADV — any analyst constructing a bullish case from the price recovery must first explain why the rally is occurring on meaningfully below-average volume; in the absence of a convincing explanation, the divergence is a red flag, not a footnote. ❌
