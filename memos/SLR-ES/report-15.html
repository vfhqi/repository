Disconnect identified — merchant exposure opacity: Management does not disclose the current PPA-vs-merchant revenue split or the sensitivity of EBITDA to a 10-20% correction in Spanish wholesale power prices; given that Spanish merchant prices have been structurally elevated and may correct, this omission is material [§1 topic avoidance + §5 Endesa commentary]

Expert-vs-narrative gap: Sector experts' 2-4 year grid-queue resolution estimate vs management's implied 12-18M deferral resolution is a structural gap, not a rounding error; at the midpoint of the expert range, projects in queue today would commission 2-3 years later than management's narrative implies, with material impact on the 2025-27 EPS trajectory

Customer-vs-narrative gap: Absence of construction-level EPC mentions in contractor earnings calls despite management's implied advanced-development activity is a modest but consistent data point that active construction is smaller in scale than the pipeline narrative suggests [§5]

Single largest credibility risk: The pace-of-COD-conversion assumption is the single largest credibility risk — if grid-queue resolution in Spain takes 2-4 years vs management's implied 12-18M, the EPS inflection story is effectively deferred by 1-2 years; at a growth valuation, this is not a small modelling error; it could account for 30-50% of the equity's current multiple
