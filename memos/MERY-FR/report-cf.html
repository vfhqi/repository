EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Mercialys has executed a decisive strategic repositioning , completing the pivot to its "Shop*Park" convenience- focused model while aggressively restarting external growth through highly accretive acquisitions at yields exceeding 8%, offsetting the structural headwind from fading inflation-linked indexation that historically drove the majority of organic rental growth. The portfolio now concentrates 96% of its €3.04 billion value across 34 dominant regional retail parks targeting high-footfall suburban catchments, fundamentally altering the risk-return profile from the legacy convenience-center structure. The materiality of change at Mercialys is substantial but introduces fragility: management successfully neutralized the existential Casino Group tenant crisis while simultaneously pivoting to debt-funded acquisition-led growth, yet this strategic shift occurs precisely as French inflation indexation turns negative and leverage approaches rating-agency thresholds, creating a narrow execution window where M&A deployment must outpace rising debt costs to sustain forward earnings momentum. The Casino exposure collapse from 20.5% to 1.7% of rental income represents the single largest portfolio transformation in recent history, eliminating systemic supplier risk while simultaneously forcing aggressive capital reallocation toward replacement anchors.

Materiality of Leadership and Governance Change

The consolidation of executive power under CEO Vincent Ravat following Deputy CEO Elizabeth Blaise's December 2025 resignation marks a meaningful governance shift, partially mitigated by the newly empowered six-member Executive Committee that distributes strategic responsibilities and prevents total operational bottlenecking. The Board's independent director ratio strengthened to 78% , well above regulatory requirements, though female representation declined marginally to 44% following a second director resignation, mechanically reducing board size from ten to nine members. The simultaneous exits of two experienced female leaders removes valuable institutional memory at a critical juncture when the acquisition-led growth strategy demands flawless execution across unfamiliar markets. Cultural health metrics signal strengthening internal stability : employee turnover plummeted from a concerning 24.1% peak to 12.0% in 2025, while the company maintained Great Place to Work certification with 74% employee satisfaction and achieved a near-perfect 96/100 gender equality index score, providing a resilient organizational foundation for the strategic pivot ahead.
