Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 10 findings

Section summary
  1. BioArctic represents an exceptionally high-growth biopharmaceutical play offering highly scalable royalty revenues from Leqembi alongside validation of its proprietary BrainTransporter platform.
  2. While commercialization is in its early stages and faces competition, the company's strong net cash position of SEK 2,034.6 million provides substantial financial flexibility.
1.
Highly scalable: BioArctic generates revenue primarily through a highly scalable royalty structure of 9% on global sales of Leqembi, which is a disease-modifying treatment for early Alzheimer's disease
2.
Inflecting growth: The investment thesis is centered on the rapid commercial inflection of Leqembi as it gains approvals globally, alongside the substantial long-term optionality of out-licensing its proprietary BrainTransporter platform
3.
Critical catalyst: The single most important swing factor for forward earnings is the inflection of the subcutaneous formulation, which simplifies administration from bi-weekly clinic infusions to weekly at-home injections
4.
Record profitability: BioArctic achieved record profitability in full-year 2025 with an operating profit of SEK 1,258.8 million, heavily driven by milestone payments from global licensing partners
5.
Expanding sales: Global Q1 2026 sales of Leqembi reached JPY 26.2 billion, generating SEK 161 million in royalties for BioArctic, which is a 68% increase year-on-year
6.
Clinical bottleneck: The investment case faces material risk from safety concerns regarding Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities, which require frequent magnetic resonance imaging scans and restrict patient eligibility
7.
Reimbursement headwinds: European uptake faces hurdles after Sweden's advisory body NT-rådet recommended against Leqembi in April 2026, citing a lack of cost-effectiveness even at a zero drug price
8.
Positive verdict: A closer look is highly warranted as the upcoming launch of the subcutaneous starting dose in late 2026 could trigger a significant sales acceleration and reverse competitive dynamics

Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. BioArctic operates as a highly scalable biopharmaceutical innovator that captures high-margin royalties from its out-licensed lead drug Leqembi.
  2. The business model relies on strategic partnerships with global giants like Eisai, Bristol Myers Squibb, and Novartis to finance late-stage development.
  3. While commercialization faces regional reimbursement hurdles, the company's proprietary BrainTransporter platform unlocks massive pipeline optionality.

Lecanemab Commercial Operations

1.
Slowing cognitive decline: Lecanemab, marketed globally as Leqembi, is the first approved disease-modifying antibody shown to slow cognitive and functional decline in early Alzheimer's disease by removing toxic amyloid-beta aggregates
2.
Authoritative licensing deal: BioArctic out-licensed lecanemab to Eisai in 2007, under which Eisai manages and funds all global clinical development, regulatory filings, and commercialization activities
3.
Scalable royalty stream: BioArctic is entitled to a highly scalable 9% royalty structure on global sales of Leqembi, excluding the Nordic region, which provides a high-margin recurring revenue stream
4.
Collaborative Nordic structure: In the Nordic region, BioArctic and Eisai co-promote Leqembi on a 50/50 profit-and-loss split, with BioArctic managing the customer-facing commercial activities
5.
Predictable milestone terms: The Eisai partnership has a total milestone structure of EUR 222 million, with EUR 34 million in potential milestones remaining as of May 2026
6.
Partner execution risk: Since Eisai is the sole global commercializer of Leqembi, BioArctic's commercial royalty revenues are highly concentrated and dependent on Eisai's execution capabilities

Platform Technologies and Pipeline

7.
Overcoming brain barrier: BioArctic's proprietary BrainTransporter technology utilizes transferrin receptor-mediated transport to actively ferry biological drugs across the blood-brain barrier, raising brain exposure by up to 70 times in preclinical models
8.
Valuable BMS deal: In December 2024, BioArctic signed a global exclusive license with Bristol Myers Squibb for its PyroGlu-Abeta program, including BAN2803 which utilizes the BrainTransporter platform, receiving USD 100 million upfront
9.
Long-term BMS milestones: Under the Bristol Myers Squibb agreement, BioArctic is eligible to receive up to USD 1.25 billion in milestone payments alongside low double-digit royalties on future sales
10.
Innovative Novartis deal: In August 2025, BioArctic partnered with Novartis on an option and license agreement to combine the BrainTransporter technology with a Novartis neurodegeneration antibody, receiving USD 30 million upfront
11.
Substantial Novartis milestones: If Novartis exercises its option, it will assume full development responsibility, making BioArctic eligible for up to USD 772 million in milestones plus tiered mid-single digit royalties
12.
Clinical pipeline progress: The company's pipeline includes exidavnemab (ABBV-0805), a selective alpha-synuclein antibody in a Phase 2a clinical study for Parkinson's disease and Multiple System Atrophy
13.
Other side of the story: Slower global commercialization by Eisai or safety setbacks with the subcutaneous formulation could severely delay the expected inflection of the royalty stream, exposing BioArctic to high partner-dependence risk

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 15 findings

Section summary
  1. BioArctic enjoys extraordinary profitability and a debt-free balance sheet with over SEK 2 billion in cash to fund its expanding pipeline.
  2. The financial trajectory is highly lumpy, driven by unpredictable upfront milestone payments that obscure the underlying growth of recurring royalties.
  3. While recent operating margins reached 62.97% in 2025, management's ability to maintain high margins depends on the royalty ramp outpacing operational costs.

Financial Trajectory and Profitability

1.
Uneven revenue profile: BioArctic's revenues are highly uneven, as shown by the drop from SEK 1,289.6 million in Q1 2025 to SEK 437.6 million in Q1 2026 due to the timing of major partner upfronts
2.
Growing royalty income: Underlying recurring royalties from Leqembi increased by 68% year-on-year to SEK 160.8 million in Q1 2026, compared to SEK 96.0 million in Q1 2025
3.
Milestone windfall gains: Financial results in Q1 2026 were strengthened by a EUR 20 million (SEK 218.8 million) sales milestone from Eisai as Leqembi annual sales crossed EUR 500 million
4.
Rising operating expenses: Operational expenses increased to SEK 681.1 million in 2025, up from SEK 458.9 million in 2024, as the company expanded its R&D activities
5.
Substantial R&D spend: Research and development costs grew to SEK 376.9 million in 2025, compared to SEK 311.1 million in 2024, to advance its proprietary clinical assets
6.
Dividend policy milestone: Reflecting a highly profitable 2025, the board proposed an initial dividend of SEK 2.00 per share, a rare achievement for a clinical-stage biotechnology company

Balance Sheet and Partner Guidance

7.
Debt-free balance sheet: BioArctic maintains an exceptionally strong, highly liquid and debt-free liquidity profile, with SEK 2,034.6 million in cash and short-term investments as of March 31, 2026
8.
Receivables cash impact: Cash flow from operating activities was negative SEK 166.0 million in Q1 2026, compared to positive SEK 11.8 million in Q1 2025, reflecting timing differences in receivables
9.
Conservative partner forecast: Eisai's FY2026 guidance for Leqembi sales of JPY 143.5 billion was considered conservative by analysts, suggesting slower near-term royalty growth of SEK 880 million
10.
Disappointing target simulation: Eisai's FY2028 simulation of JPY 300 billion in Leqembi sales was below consensus of JPY 303 billion, raising concerns about peak sales potential
11.
Margin squeeze risk: Eisai's guided increase in its companywide COGS ratio to 30% in FY2028 suggests that Leqembi, particularly the subcutaneous formulation, carries high manufacturing costs that could dilute margins
12.
Other side of the story: If Eisai fails to achieve its simulated JPY 300 billion in Leqembi sales by FY2028, BioArctic's forward royalty stream will severely miss the market's high growth expectations

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for BioArctic strengthens with the upcoming launch of the subcutaneous starting dose of Leqembi.
  2. This launch will bypass hospital infusion capacity constraints and expand patient access directly to the home.

Core Commercial Catalysts

1.
Subcutaneous formulation launch: The primary earnings driver is the FDA approval of Leqembi's subcutaneous initiation formulation, which has a PDUFA date of August 24, 2026
2.
Approving label timeline: Plausibility is high as the FDA's three-month delay was due to a request for additional analysis of existing data rather than safety or chemical concerns
3.
Convenient switch option: Switch dynamics are highly favorable as weekly subcutaneous injections at home take only 15 seconds compared to bi-weekly, 1.5-hour clinic intravenous infusions
4.
Growing biomarker adoption: Growth is driven by the rapid adoption of blood-based biomarker tests, which simplify diagnosis and triage, increasing US positron emission tomography scan volume by 75% in 2025
5.
Easing diagnostic bottlenecks: Plausibility is high as Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services now allow blood-based biomarkers for confirmation, significantly reducing the diagnostic bottleneck

Pipeline and Long-Term Value

6.
Expanding European sales: Earnings will benefit from the commercial roll-out in Germany and Austria, which started in late 2025 and is expanding across 350 registered centers
7.
Prolonged price discussions: Plausibility is medium because pricing and national reimbursement negotiations are complex and can take substantial time, limiting rapid volume growth in the near term
8.
Long-term licensing optionality: Longer-term upside is driven by the out-licensing of the BrainTransporter platform, with potential new partnerships validating the platform's high-concentration brain delivery
9.
Presymptomatic trial data: The long-term bull case requires success in the Phase 3 AHEAD 3/45 study, which could expand the addressable patient pool by treating presymptomatic patients
10.
Other side of the story: If the FDA rejects the subcutaneous initiation formulation or if safety concerns regarding ARIA intensify, the commercial trajectory will stall and the investment case will break

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by significant safety and reimbursement hurdles that could limit patient eligible pool and delay cash generation.
  2. While clinical data remains robust, commercial competition and partner execution risks pose material headwinds to the forward EPS trajectory.

Clinical and Safety Challenges

1.
Brain swelling side effects: The most material safety risk is Amyloid-Related Imaging Abnormalities, which caused brain swelling in 12.6% of patients in clinical trials
2.
Intense patient monitoring: Patient logistics are highly complex, requiring an initial MRI scan and four follow-up scans over seven months to monitor for potential brain swelling
3.
Lilly market share gains: Eli Lilly's Kisunla has achieved over 50% share of total US prescriptions, benefiting from a convenient monthly intravenous dosing regimen
4.
Superior competitor profiles: Roche is developing trontinemab, a brain-shuttle anti-amyloid therapy showing rapid amyloid plaque clearance and significantly lower ARIA rates of under 5%

Commercial and Competition Risks

5.
No shorting pressures: There is currently no notable published short-seller report or unusually high short interest recorded for BioArctic's shares in the market
6.
Misleading out-of-pocket growth: A potential "false friend" is rising patient interest in private markets, which may mask severe reimbursement blocks in publicly funded healthcare systems
7.
Monitoring prescriptions closely: Investors must monitor quarterly US Leqembi prescription growth as a critical early-warning signal for potential market share erosion to Lilly's Kisunla
8.
Other side of the story: The risk of competitive erosion is highly overstated because Leqembi's permanent treatment design and high adherence of 80% at 18 months ensure a far more stable and recurring revenue stream than Kisunla's finite therapy
