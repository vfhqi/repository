EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

BASF's strategic separation of core and standalone businesses simplifies portfolio steering and unlocks considerable trapped value, though the company faces severe structural headwinds from elevated European energy costs and persistent global commodity chemical overcapacity. The group's return on capital employed languished at 4.5% in 2023 and recovered modestly to 5.8% in 2025 , sitting materially below peer medians and the cost of capital. Despite these cyclical pressures, the completion of the €7.7 billion Coatings divestment to Carlyle and the launch of the CoreShift program targeting up to 20% net cash fixed-cost savings by 2029 demonstrate management's decisive commitment

to operational transformation under CEO Markus Kamieth. successful commissioning of the Zhanjiang Verbund site in early 2026 positions BASF to capture Asia's rapid demand expansion with low-carbon, cost-competitive production

, while severe German overcapacity and energy penalties continue to constrain upstream profitability at legacy European sites . Deep vertical integration allows BASF to capture a 10-fold margin multiplier along downstream specialty value chains , providing structural competitive moats

through technical switching barriers and formulation know-how. However, global commodity chemical sector ROCE plummeted from 8.0% in 2019 to 4.0% in 2024 , reflecting industry-wide capacity discipline failure and persistent margin compression. Value-chain positioning remains strategically advantageous in downstream specialties: upstream feedstocks capture a 6.0% EBIT margin, basic chemicals average 2.5%, intermediates reach 7.5%, and specialty formulations peak at 18.0% , with the 10-fold margin multiplier maximizing capital efficiency.

Around 80% of core business assets are deeply integrated into the Verbund value chain, with over 280 km of pipelines at Zhanjiang eliminating internal freight penalties .

However, competitors operating in the "squeezed middle" lack true specialty models and suffer severe margin pressure, with non-integrated sites facing structural uncompetitiveness .

15 - Earnings Calls Customer-side dynamics provide moderate lock-in : long-term agreements utilize variable prices that fluctuate based on raw material costs and sales volumes , while products used in personal care and nutrition require extensive regulatory approvals that can take up to three years to finalize, creating substantial switching barriers

. BASF achieved a 42% customer response rate in its 2024 Net Promoter loyalty surveys, indicating solid customer engagement . On the supply side, management secured long-term pipeline and LNG contracts, including a 10-year agreement with Equinor delivering up to 23 terawatt-hours annually, mitigating absolute supply risks despite persistent price volatility. Long-term structural forces present mixed disruption threats : formidable capital entry requirements, including the €8.7 billion Zhanjiang investment with multi-decade paybacks, establish immense barriers for potential competitors .

Yet decarbonizing primary building blocks through green hydrogen requires immense capital, with a 54-megawatt electrolyzer yielding only 8,000 metric tons annually while demanding massive renewable electricity supply , risking prolonged cash-flow dilution

before achieving commercial parity. Advanced battery binders and cathode materials provide high-value specialty runways aligned with electromobility transitions , though internal strategic modeling indicates the battery materials business has been triple-digit loss-making at the EBITDA level during peak market oversupply .

foundation quality is moderate , supported by defensible Verbund integration that reduces transport costs and captures margin across the entire value chain while downstream custom formulations secure sticky multiyear customer relationships .

However, trailing twelve-month ROIC fell as low as 1% to 2% in 2024, sitting significantly below the cost of capital, while European natural gas prices averaged 3.30 times US benchmarks , indicating severe structural erosion . The mechanical recovery potential from Zhanjiang's positioning at the very bottom of the local Asian cost curve and ramping volumes driving cost absorption from 2026 provides structured earnings inflection , though execution remains contingent on sustained regional demand and disciplined capacity additions by competitors.
