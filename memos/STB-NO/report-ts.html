Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. Storebrand has successfully transformed from a legacy guaranteed pension provider into a high-margin, capital-light savings and insurance powerhouse across Norway and Sweden.

Core Segments and Offerings

1.
High growth savings segment: The Savings business segment includes long-term pension savings products with zero interest rate guarantees, comprising Defined Contribution pensions in Norway and Sweden
2.
Digital wealth platforms: Within the Savings segment, Storebrand runs retail banking services and the digital savings app Kron, which was ranked number one in Norwegian customer satisfaction
3.
Scalable asset management: Storebrand Asset Management provides active, index, and alternative fund mandates, acting as a gateway to the Nordic region for international and local institutional investors
4.
Broad insurance segment: The Insurance segment manages non-life, retail P&C, group life, and employee-related risk insurance products in the Norwegian and Swedish retail and corporate markets
5.
Closed guaranteed books: Legacy Guaranteed Pension encompasses closed defined benefit schemes, individual capital, and paid-up policies that are closed to new business and in long-term run-off
6.
Corporate and retail split: In the first quarter of 2026, retail portfolio insurance premiums grew to NOK 6,580.00 million, while corporate portfolio insurance premiums reached NOK 4,565.00 million

Revenue Model and Value Chain

7.
AuM based fees: Savings revenues are primarily derived from management fees calculated as a fixed percentage of total assets under management, creating highly predictable and scalable fee-based revenues
8.
Insurance underwriting premiums: Insurance revenues are driven by earned premiums, with underwriting profitability measured by the combined ratio of claims expenses and operating costs to premiums
9.
Profit sharing mechanics: Guaranteed pensions generate income via administrative fees and net profit-sharing with customers when investment returns exceed the guaranteed rate in Norway and Sweden
10.
Interest rate pricing: Occupational pension agreements and guaranteed products require employers to pay a premium for the interest rate guarantee, which must be priced in advance based on equity risk
11.
Low customer concentration: Value chain dynamics are supported by a highly diversified client base of 2.60 million individual pension and retail banking customers across Norway and Sweden
12.
Profit favoured distribution: Storebrand occupies a highly profitable, integrated position in the Nordic savings value chain by acting as both the direct pension manufacturer and the retail distributor

Competitive Moats and Industry Structure

13.
High switching barriers: Corporate occupational pension programs create powerful switching costs for clients, as moving integrated employer-sponsored pension schemes requires significant operational and payroll adjustments
14.
Scaled asset management: Storebrand Asset Management is the fourth-largest asset manager in the Nordic region, managing NOK 1,543.00 billion of total assets as of Q1 2026
15.
Retail P&C consolidation: The Norwegian P&C insurance market is highly consolidated, allowing major players to pass through aggressive price hikes to offset claims inflation
16.
Challenger positioning: In Sweden, Storebrand SPP acts as a nimble challenger within the non-unionised corporate pensions segment, utilizing digital conversion and sustainability-focused funds
17.
Strong local brand: The Storebrand brand enjoys extreme regional recognition, which has been leveraged to build a growing bank mortgage and retail insurance challenger position
18.
Other side of the story: Fee margin compression represents a structural threat in the defined contribution pension market, where fee margins have been in a steady decline

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Downside risks are well mitigated by massive customer buffers and robust capital ratios, protecting the dividend stream even during periods of extreme financial market volatility.

Primary Market and Biometric Risks

1.
Market downturn fee compression: A severe equity market crash represents a major risk, as fee income is directly linked to the market value of assets under management
2.
Longevity reserve strain: Increases in average life expectancy could force Storebrand to strengthen reserves for its guaranteed paid-up policies, reducing free cash flow
3.
Disability claim inflation: Elevated disability claims in corporate markets, particularly group life, can temporarily damage insurance underwriting results
4.
Regulatory Solvency II risk: Changes to Solvency II standard model calculations could reduce the solvency margin, potentially restricting extraordinary capital distributions
5.
Absence of short campaigns: There is currently no active short-seller campaign or abnormally high short interest on Storebrand shares in the market
6.
False friend signal: High reported accounting profits in the Guaranteed segment can be a false friend if they are driven by short-term mark-to-market bond gains rather than sustainable fee growth
7.
Primary early warning metric: The Solvency II ratio and insurance combined ratio serve as the single most critical early-warning metrics to monitor

Sceptical Counter Analysis

8.
Sizable customer buffer protection: Scepticism regarding downside risk is justified as Storebrand has built robust customer buffers of NOK 34.10 billion, or 8.20% of guaranteed reserves in Norway
9.
Strategic interest rate hedge: Customer portfolios are highly hedged, with expected investment returns currently standing 180.00 basis points above the average guaranteed interest rate
10.
Other side of the story: Systemic risk remains high for life insurers, as failure to meet capital regulatory thresholds arising from potential shortfalls on both the asset and liability side would be highly costly
