EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Deutsche Telekom operates as a structurally advantaged global telecommunications provider commanding exceptional transatlantic scale, robust free cash flow generation, and peer-leading profitability driven overwhelmingly by T-Mobile US operations, though this geographic concentration creates material single-market dependency risks.

The foundation quality is exceptional, combining tier-one operator execution with structurally widening scale advantages that drive peer-leading capital allocation and mechanical earnings compounding.

The United States segment contributes approximately sixty-six percent of total net revenue for the group, creating a unique transatlantic profile that simultaneously provides structural growth momentum and concentrated execution risk.

The revenue model is highly advantaged, with service revenues accounting for roughly eighty-three percent of total group revenue in the most recent fiscal year, ensuring high cash flow visibility through subscription-based voice, data access, and internet television offerings.

Recent delivery demonstrates robust fundamental momentum, with group net revenue growing four-point-seven percent organically in the first quarter of 2026 to reach twenty-nine-point-nine billion euros, while service revenues expanded four-point-six percent organically and adjusted EBITDA AL outpaced revenue by expanding seven-point-five percent organically during the same period.

Strong operational execution led management to upgrade full-year financial targets, with the company now projecting adjusted EBITDA AL of approximately forty-seven-point-five billion euros and free cash flow AL expected to exceed nineteen-point-eight billion euros for 2026, primarily reflecting upgraded targets from the T- Mobile US subsidiary.
