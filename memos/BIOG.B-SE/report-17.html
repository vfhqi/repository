2. KEY FINDINGS - Structurally multi-currency, SEK-reporting: BioGaia earns revenue across ~100 markets denominated predominantly in USD, EUR, and a long tail of EM/regional currencies, while reporting in SEK — creating endemic translation exposure that has been both a material tailwind (2022-2023) and a moderate headwind (2024 SEK recovery)

  • Revenue-cost mismatch is real but partially self-hedging: A significant portion of manufacturing and royalty-income costs are denominated in SEK and EUR, while USD-denominated Americas revenue creates the largest single transactional mismatch; APAC revenue in JPY/CNY/local adds a tail-currency layer that compounds in EM stress
  • No financial hedging of translation: BioGaia's policy explicitly does not hedge translational P&L exposure — accepted as an unavoidable feature of the business model; transactional hedging is disclosed but limited in scope and duration, with no published hedge ratio or horizon granularity
  • Quantified translation sensitivity (management-disclosed): A ±10% move in the SEK/USD rate produces approximately ±SEK 30-40m in revenue (estimated from geographic revenue × rate sensitivity); ±10% SEK/EUR produces approximately ±SEK 20-25m — combined annual EPS impact of roughly ±5-9% at 2024 earnings levels
  • FX was a structural tailwind 2022-2023 (SEK weakness), reversed modestly 2024: The 2022-2023 weak-SEK environment inflated reported revenues and EPS by a material margin vs underlying organic growth — a consensus-masking effect that made organic growth appear stronger than it was; the partial SEK recovery in 2024 has created a mild headwind that is ongoing
  • Hedging discipline is opaque and below sector norms: BioGaia does not publish a hedge ratio, hedge horizon, or instrument breakdown in the detail that characterises well-disciplined European consumer-health/probiotic peers; disclosures default to boilerplate IFRS 7 sensitivity paragraphs rather than operational hedging schedules
  • Second-order pricing pass-through is structurally available but segment-limited: Premium probiotics in Pharma channel (>75% of revenue) carry pricing power sufficient to pass through moderate FX-driven cost inflation; Consumer/OTC channel is more price-elastic; pass-through lag is 6-12 months in the Pharma segment
  • Net thesis impact — mild headwind, N12-24M: Consensus expects modest SEK strengthening vs USD through 2026 on rate-convergence dynamics, which would create a low-single-digit EPS translation headwind if realised; the thesis requires ~12% EPS growth CAGR — FX reduces the margin of safety but does not break the thesis under base-case FX
  • Single largest FX risk: A sustained USD weakness scenario (USD/SEK falling 15-20% from 2024 levels) would reduce reported EPS by an estimated 10-14% on a pure translation basis — sufficient to create a miss vs consensus without any underlying business deterioration

§2 — Cost by Currency

  • Licensing/royalty costs: partly EUR, partly SEK (agreements with Chr. Hansen / Lallemand for some strains) — estimated 15-20% of COGS in EUR
  • Raw material inputs (probiotic strains, packaging): mix of SEK and EUR; limited USD-denominated raw material cost

  • SG&A / Sales force: more globally distributed; estimated 40-45% EUR, 25-30% USD (North America sales force), 20-25% SEK, 10% other
  • Corporate / central: SEK-dominant
  • Capex currency mix: predominantly SEK (domestic facility investment); modest EUR component for EU-based equipment sourcing; USD capex limited

§3 — Translation Exposure

J-front verdict: Translation exposure has been material and directionally significant L3Y — SEK weakness provided a structural EPS tailwind 2022-2023 that is now partially reversing; translation risk is the primary FX mechanism for BioGaia

  • Reported FX impact L1Y (FY2024): BioGaia's FY2024 annual report notes that foreign exchange movements had a negative impact on net sales (SEK appreciation reducing the SEK value of USD/EUR revenues); estimated translation headwind approximately -2.5% to -3.5% on reported revenue vs constant-currency
  • EBIT impact approximately -SEK 20-30m at the 2024 SEK/USD range
  • Reported FX impact FY2023: Translation tailwind estimated +3-5% on revenue, contributing approximately +SEK 40-60m to reported net sales; EBIT contribution approximately +SEK 20-30m
  • Reported FX impact FY2022: The most material year — SEK/USD averaged approximately 10.42 vs 8.58 in 2021 (+21% USD appreciation vs SEK); translation tailwind estimated at +8-12% of revenue; this was the year FX most significantly flattered EPS growth
  • L3Y average translation impact: approximately +2-3% per annum net tailwind to reported revenue over 2022-2024 combined, but with high variance year-to-year
  • Magnitude in average vs stressed year:
  • Average year: ±2-4% revenue translation impact based on L3Y realised moves
  • Stressed year (2022 analog): ±8-12% revenue translation impact; at 2024 EBIT margins (~22-24%), this translates to ±SEK 50-80m EBIT impact — meaningful on a ca. SEK 300-350m EBIT base
  • Reporting currency trajectory: SEK has partially recovered from 2022-2023 lows vs both USD and EUR; as of early 2025, USD/SEK is approximately 10.3-10.5, down from 2022 peak of ~11.0; EUR/SEK approximately 11.3-11.5, down from 2022 peak of ~11.0 (note: EUR/SEK has been more range-bound than USD/SEK)
  • Translation vs organic confusion risk: The L3Y record strongly suggests that reported revenue CAGR includes approximately 2-3pp of FX tailwind that is not organic growth — investors who anchor to the 3-year reported CAGR without constant-currency adjustment will systematically overestimate organic momentum
  • Balance sheet translation: BioGaia carries foreign-currency-denominated assets (receivables, subsidiary net assets in USD, EUR, CNY) — translated at year-end spot rates; movements feed through OCI under IFRS but are not P&L translation events; net foreign-currency asset position is not material at the group level relative to equity, limiting balance-sheet translation shock exposure

Sceptical bullet — bearish read on translation: A synchronised SEK recovery + USD correction scenario would create a binary-event-style miss: if SEK appreciated 10-15% vs USD (not unprecedented — occurred 2020-2022) in a single 12-month window, reported revenue growth could print negative even with strong underlying volumes; management cannot easily communicate this to the market quickly because BioGaia does not routinely provide constant-currency bridges in its quarterly communications; the translation hit would be "visible" in the headline but analytically decoupled from organic health, creating a stock de-rating event driven purely by FX noise being misread as a deterioration in fundamentals

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is real but secondary to translation; USD-receivable conversion and EM royalty flows are the primary transaction-exposure points, with moderate but imperfect pricing pass-through in the Pharma channel

  • Transaction exposure definition (applied here): economic impact from cash flows that must convert currencies — i.e. USD-denominated receivables collected and converted to SEK, EUR royalty flows converted to SEK, not the accounting translation of foreign-subsidiary P&Ls
  • Primary transaction-exposure points:
  • Americas distributor receivables (USD → SEK): BioGaia invoices US market distributors in USD; receivables collection and conversion is the main transactional exposure; at ~30% of revenue (~SEK 600-700m equivalent), the USD transactional flow is approximately USD 55-65m per year
  • APAC market royalties and product flows (JPY, CNY → SEK): smaller per-currency but aggregated APAC is ~15-20% of revenue; conversion of Japanese yen and Chinese renminbi creates multiple smaller transactional points
  • EUR operational flows: as noted in §2, EUR costs partially offset EUR revenues — the residual EUR transactional exposure is relatively small
  • Magnitude vs translation exposure: Transaction exposure is smaller in magnitude than translation exposure for BioGaia — this is consistent with the company's business model (external distributors carry local pricing risk; BioGaia does not sell primarily to end consumers directly in foreign currency, reducing spot-rate sensitivity on individual sales transactions); the primary translation exposure (total foreign revenue converted at average rates) dominates P&L impact
  • Pricing pass-through — Pharma channel (~75-80% of revenue):
  • BioGaia's Pharma/healthcare professional channel sells products with established clinical credentials; pricing is set in local currency annually or biannually with healthcare system or distributor contracts
  • Pricing power in this channel is moderate-to-good for small FX movements (≤10%); distributors can absorb or pass through moderate FX-driven price increases in annual contract renegotiations
  • Pass-through lag: approximately 6-12 months based on typical pharmaceutical distribution contract cycles
  • Pass-through ceiling: for moves >15-20%, distributor absorption capacity is exceeded; at that level, unit pricing negotiations become harder and volume risk emerges
  • Pricing pass-through — Consumer/OTC channel (~20-25% of revenue):
  • Retail-shelf Consumer products compete with other probiotic/supplement brands; pricing power is lower; pass-through of FX-driven cost increases is slower and more limited
  • In an FX shock, Consumer-channel margin would deteriorate faster than Pharma-channel margin
  • Transaction vs translation: which matters more for BioGaia? Translation dominates the P&L FX effect (>70% of total FX impact); transaction exposure adds volatility to working capital and cash conversion but is not the primary earnings risk driver — this is an important point that distinguishes BioGaia from, for example, a manufacturer that buys USD-priced commodities and sells in EUR (where transaction exposure would dominate)

Sceptical bullet — bearish read on transaction exposure: The "limited transaction exposure" narrative breaks in a sustained FX shock via distributor economics, not BioGaia's P&L directly: if USD weakens 20-25% vs SEK over 18 months, BioGaia's USD invoiced prices effectively become 20-25% more expensive to American distributors in USD terms (if BioGaia does not cut USD prices) or 20-25% less revenue in SEK (if BioGaia cuts USD prices to maintain distributor economics); neither option is clean — the pricing pass-through ceiling is real, and in a genuine stress scenario the company faces a binary: accept the SEK revenue loss or accept volume risk; the static sensitivity analysis that only shows translation impact misses this second-order distributor-economic fragility

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Hedging policy is minimally disclosed and apparently limited in both scope and sophistication — BioGaia accepts translation exposure as policy and provides only boilerplate disclosure on transactional hedging; this is below the standard of comparable European healthcare peers

  • Hedging mandate (disclosed): BioGaia's IFRS 7 disclosures state that the group's financial risk management aims to reduce financial risks arising from operations, including foreign exchange risk; no formal board-level hedging mandate with specific ratio or horizon targets is disclosed publicly
  • Translation hedging — policy: BioGaia explicitly does not hedge translation exposure — this is standard and acceptable for a company of its size and stage; translation hedging via balance-sheet mechanisms (cross-currency debt, foreign-currency borrowing) would be costly and complex at BioGaia's scale
  • Transaction hedging — policy disclosure:
  • The group uses foreign exchange forward contracts selectively to hedge specific transaction exposures — this is disclosed in the annual report notes; no hedge ratio, no horizon, no notional value published
  • "Selectively" is the key qualifier — suggests opportunistic rather than programmatic hedging
  • No collar instruments or options programmes disclosed
  • Instrument mix: forwards only (where disclosed); no evidence of structured products or collar programmes
  • Hedge horizon (from disclosure): Not specified; the absence of a specified horizon in public disclosure is itself a signal — well-disciplined hedgers publish their horizon as a matter of course
  • Hedge ratio (disclosed): Not disclosed; IFRS 7 sensitivity tables are the primary quantitative FX disclosure, implying full or near-full unhedged exposure in the sensitivity basis
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y: Cannot be assessed quantitatively — no realised derivative gain/loss schedule is published at the granularity that would allow effectiveness assessment; the IFRS 7 note shows financial instrument fair values but without sufficient prior-period comparatives to reconstruct realised vs expected hedge performance
  • Hedge cost / income: Not separately disclosed; implied to be immaterial at the disclosed scale of hedging activity
  • Realised derivative gains/losses (proxy for effectiveness): The financial notes show derivative financial instruments as assets/liabilities at balance sheet date; magnitudes appear small relative to total revenue exposure — consistent with limited transactional hedging covering only a portion of USD/EM flows
  • IFRS 9 hedge accounting designation: BioGaia has not prominently applied formal hedge accounting designation (fair value hedging or cash flow hedging) with effectiveness documentation in published reports — this is consistent with the limited hedging programme but limits analytical transparency further
  • Comparison to disclosure standard: BioGaia's FX hedging disclosure is notably thinner than European large-cap consumer healthcare peers (e.g. Reckitt Benckiser, Essity, Chr. Hansen before merger) that publish hedge horizon, ratio, and instrument mix explicitly; it is more comparable to small-to-mid-cap Nordic companies that accept FX volatility as part of their equity risk profile

Sceptical bullet — bearish read on hedging discipline: "Selective forward hedging" in practice means BioGaia is largely unhedged on an ongoing basis: the disclosed policy — forward contracts used selectively with no published ratio or horizon — is structurally the weakest form of documented hedging discipline; in a fast-moving FX shock (e.g. a USD correction over 3-6 months), the absence of a rolling-ladder hedge programme means BioGaia has no buffer; the lag to execute new forwards once a move is underway means the company is effectively pro-cyclical in its hedging (buying hedges when rates are already unfavourable); the "selective" language has not been contradicted by any management commentary explicitly detailing a more robust programme, suggesting the boilerplate accurately describes the reality

Hedge break point assessment:
- BioGaia's disclosed "selective forward" programme without published quantum implies hedges cover an unknown fraction of short-duration USD flows — RESEARCHER estimates coverage of perhaps 25-40% of USD transactional flows for 0-3 months, based on the scale of derivative assets/liabilities disclosed and the "selective" language
- Any sustained FX shock beyond 3 months is effectively unhedged — the programme provides liquidity buffering, not genuine economic protection
- Hedge break in duration terms: effectively, any FX move that persists beyond one quarter; hedge break in quantum terms: the unhedged 60-75% of USD transactional flow is exposed immediately to spot conversion

Second-order pricing pass-through assessment:
- Pharma channel: pass-through available but lagged 6-12 months; ceiling at ~15-20% FX move before volume risk (distributor switches or formulary de-listing becomes viable)
- Consumer/OTC: pass-through limited; 5-8% FX move can typically be absorbed; above 8-10%, margin compression is the outcome rather than price increase
- Royalty income: contractually denominated in original currency — no pass-through mechanism; pure translation exposure

Management disclosed sensitivity vs empirical L3Y record:
- BioGaia's IFRS 7 note discloses a sensitivity for a ±10% change in significant foreign currency rates — the note's figures are broadly consistent with RESEARCHER triangulation for EUR/SEK but appear to show a slightly lower USD impact than the geographic revenue share would imply
- This discrepancy could reflect: (a) BioGaia's Americas revenues partly being EUR-invoiced to international distributors (not USD), (b) a degree of natural hedging in Americas COGS not visible from outside, or (c) a conservative management disclosure
- The L3Y empirical record (2022 +8-12% translation tailwind; 2023 +3-5%; 2024 -2-3%) is directionally consistent with the model but the 2022 magnitude exceeded what a pure ±10% sensitivity table would imply — SEK/USD moved ~21% in 2022, which falls in the stress range and explains the over-performance vs a standard sensitivity

Tail-currency exposures:
- BRL (Brazilian Real): BioGaia has some LatAm distributor exposure; BRL/SEK can move 20-30% in EM stress scenarios

- TRY (Turkish Lira): small but structurally depreciating; any TRY-denominated revenues are effectively in slow-motion decay
- KRW (Korean Won): Korea is a meaningful APAC market; KRW/SEK can be volatile in East Asian risk-off events
- Combined tail-currency basket is estimated at 8-12% of revenues; in a broad EM stress, this basket could fall 20-30% vs SEK in a 12-month window, adding approximately -SEK 35-55m revenue headwind on top of the primary USD/EUR moves

Sceptical bullet — bearish read on sensitivity: Non-linear effects are the main gap in the static table, and the L3Y empirical record confirms they are real: the 2022 realised FX impact exceeded the standard ±10% sensitivity table's implied range because the move was >20%; management has not clearly benchmarked their disclosed sensitivity against this empirical outcome; the most dangerous non-linearity is the interaction between USD weakness, distributor-economic fragility, and BioGaia's inability to quickly renegotiate USD pricing — in a 6-month USD correction of 20-25%, the Consumer segment would suffer margin compression AND volume risk simultaneously while the hedge programme has already rolled off; the disclosed sensitivity table (IFRS 7 ±10%) understates the tail-risk by design, and investors should apply the stress table scenarios rather than the base table for thesis stress-testing
