§1 — Brief Recap + Key Components Summary

One line: EssilorLuxottica is the world's dominant integrated eyewear company, combining lens manufacturing (Essilor) with frames (Luxottica) and retail (LensCrafters, Sunglass Hut, GrandVision), now extending into AI-connected wearables (Ray-Ban Meta), medical devices (Nuance Audio) and myopia management.

All active/plausible change forces, L12–18M or N3Y:

Internal — initiated or accelerating:
- Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses: 7M units FY2025, revenue tripling, 20M unit production target mooted
- Oakley Meta smart glasses launch (H2 2025) — second product line in AI wearables
- Meta Ray-Ban Display glasses ($799) — new premium tier product
- Nuance Audio hearing-aid glasses: FDA approved Q1 2025, US launch April 2025, EU H1 2025 — new medical device category
- Stellest 2.0 myopia management launch (Nov 2025) — upgraded efficacy vs gen 1
- Essilor Stellest Smartglasses debut (CIIE Nov 2025) — myopia + wearables intersection
- GrandVision synergy capture: run-rate approaching completion by end-2026
- LensCrafters Adaptive Progressive lens launch (Aug 2024) — proprietary premium lens
- Supreme brand integration (acquisition closed Oct 2024, $1.5bn) — new non-optical revenue stream
- New production facilities in Mexico and Thailand (tariff/supply chain diversification)

External — changing environment:
- US tariff regime (Apr 2025): 145% on China goods, 36% Thailand, 20% EU — material COGS headwind
- Meta/Apple/Google competitive intensification in AI glasses category
- Warby Parker gaining ECP-channel share in North America (+7.2% market share, up from 6.8%)
- Fielmann aggressive European pan-regional expansion (15 countries, €2.3bn revenue, growing 15% FY2024)

§2 — Change in Leadership

[J] Milleri is passing the Animal CEO test on innovation but has yet to prove he can manage a portfolio of bets simultaneously without margin compression.

  • Tenure and background: Francesco Milleri has been sole CEO since May 2021 (previously co-CEO from 2018 merger close alongside Hubert Sagnières, who stepped down); he was the original Luxottica-side candidate, backed by Del Vecchio.
  • Del Vecchio succession: Leonardo Del Vecchio died June 2022. His eight heirs hold ~32.3% of EL through Delfin S.à r.l. (Dec 2024 figure). The ownership structure is stable; no reported activist pressure or family fragmentation to date.
  • Milleri's mandate: Publicly framed as transforming EL from "optical company" to "technology-meets-health" platform. Smart glasses, medical devices, AI diagnostics — he has repositioned the strategic narrative materially from the Del Vecchio era.
  • Paul du Saillant: Deputy CEO, previously CEO of Essilor standalone. Key operational anchor, particularly for lenses and professional solutions. CFO is Stefano Grassi (appointed 2021). Governance is stable.
  • Animal CEO test (Gold WL model): Milleri scores positively on vision, boldness of bet (Meta partnership, Nuance Audio, Supreme), and external authority (FashionNetwork profile, CES 2024 presence). Counter: the Meta partnership was inherited from Del Vecchio's era — Milleri is executing and extending it, not originating it. Del Vecchio's deal-making instinct (Oakley 2007, Sunglass Hut 2001, Ray-Ban 1999) is not yet matched.
  • ❌ Sceptic: Milleri's record on margin discipline is uncertain. FY2025 margin contracted despite record revenue. Three large concurrent investments (AI glasses, myopia management, Supreme) create capital allocation opacity.
  • Succession risk: Milleri is 59 (born 1965). No disclosed succession plan. Board renewal post-Del Vecchio death has not been tested under pressure.

§3 — Change in Strategy / Structure

[J] The strategy has shifted from "integrate and synergise GrandVision" (2021–23) to "build platform adjacencies" (2024+). Two of the three new bets (AI glasses, myopia) are capital-light extensions of existing manufacturing/distribution. One (Supreme) is not.

GrandVision integration:
- Acquisition closed Aug 2021 for ~€7.2bn. HQ closed H2 2023. Integration of assortment, lenses, and frames substantially complete.
- Revenue synergy (cross-selling Essilor lenses into GrandVision stores) running in 2024; management flagged synergy contribution still material in 2024.
- Cost synergies from HQ elimination, procurement consolidation: benefits tracking into 2025–26 but tail is narrowing.
- GrandVision ~7,000 stores across Europe (Netherlands, Germany, Belgium, Benelux) — now integral to EMEA professional solutions channel.

Online / DTC strategy:
- Varilux and Essilor brands available online, but EL's core model remains ECP (Eye Care Professional) channel — not disintermediating opticians.
- LensCrafters.com and proprietary e-commerce for Sunglass Hut are the consumer-facing DTC surfaces.
- Supreme acquisition adds a DTC-native digital brand for the first time — strategic learning vehicle for EL's luxury brands.
- ❌ EL has not launched a direct lens subscription product. Warby Parker's DTC subscription growth and 1-800 Contacts' share gains in contacts suggest EL is under-investing in direct online optical specifically.

Myopia management strategic bet:
- Essilor Stellest (own IP, launched 2021 China, expanding globally) — children's myopia-slowing lens. Stellest 2.0 launched Nov 2025 with upgraded efficacy data.
- MiYOSMART (licensed from Hoya) — another myopia management product in EL's portfolio.
- Myopia management revenue growing +22% in FY2025. Stellest China +50% Q4 2024.
- Internal target cited by analysts: up to €2bn run-rate by 2027 — significant vs a €28.5bn revenue base (~7%), but plausible given trajectory.

Ray-Ban Meta / AI glasses strategy:
- Partnership with Meta initiated pre-2019 (Del Vecchio era). Milleri has accelerated: Oakley Meta launched H2 2025; Meta Ray-Ban Display ($799) debuted late 2025.
- Production capacity targeting 20 million units — from 7 million FY2025.
- EL's role: hardware design, manufacturing, Ray-Ban/Oakley brand ownership. Meta's role: AI software, distribution platform.
- ❌ Key strategic risk: EL does not own the AI; if Meta's AI ecosystem weakens or Apple/Google capture AI glasses mindshare, EL's value-add (brand + manufacturing) is commoditised.

Supreme acquisition ($1.5bn, Oct 2024):
- First-ever non-eyewear acquisition. Streetwear brand with ~$600M revenue (pre-acquisition VF estimates), primarily US.
- Rationale: Gen Z reach, DTC DNA, brand energy to inject into EL's owned optical brands (Ray-Ban, Oakley collaborations).
- ❌ Sceptic: Supreme under VF Corporation was a declining asset — revenue peaked circa 2020. EL is paying 2.5x revenue for a weakened brand. Integration track record in non-optical is zero.

§4 — Change in Financial Profile

[J] FY2025 is a genuine step-change year — 11.2% constant-FX revenue growth vs 6% in FY2024 vs ~5% structural baseline. But the margin trajectory is moving in the wrong direction, with 70bps compression in FY2025 despite the revenue beat. Tariffs + investment cycle are the proximate causes, but they create a medium-term EPS question.

Revenue trend:
- FY2023: ~€24.5bn (+8% cc)
- FY2024: €26.5bn (+6.0% reported, ~7.5% cc)
- FY2025: €28.5bn (+11.2% cc) — first double-digit year in company history
- Q4 2025: +18.4% cc — acceleration driven by AI glasses ramp and North America +24%

Gross margin / EBIT margin:
- Adjusted EBIT margin: 16.5% (FY2023) → 16.7% (FY2024) → 16.0% (FY2025)
- Long-term target: 19–20% by 2026 (reiterated in guidance). The gap to target has widened in FY2025, not narrowed.
- FY2025 margin drivers: favourable premium mix (Varilux, myopia lenses) offset by tariff cost headwinds (COGS +8.8% H1 2025), Supreme integration costs, AI glasses mix (lower-margin hardware vs lens business).

FCF:
- FY2023: ~€3.6bn
- FY2024: ~€3.6bn (roughly flat)
- FY2025: €2.796bn — reported as "record" but this appears to reflect reclassification; needs verification against prior methodology
- FCF conversion is solid (>10% of revenue) underpinning capital returns.

Net debt:
- End FY2024: €10.97bn net debt (inc. lease liabilities)
- End FY2025: €10.85bn — marginal deleveraging despite Supreme acquisition ($1.5bn) and buybacks (~€850M in 2024–25)
- Net debt ex-lease: ~€7.3–7.8bn — 2.5–2.8x EBITDA. GrandVision leverage is substantially absorbed.

ROIC:
- Reported ROIC ~5% (Dec 2024 peak per Alpha Spread) — Goodwill-burdened from €30bn+ of acquisition premiums. Structurally low and unlikely to recover to WACC on a reported basis without significant goodwill write-down or multi-year earnings step-change.
- Underlying tangible ROIC significantly higher — optical manufacturing is a high-ROIC business pre-M&A.

EPS upgrade cycle:
- Adjusted Group net profit FY2024: €3.1bn (+9.4% cc). EPS upgrade trajectory intact at mid-to-high single digits on organic basis; AI glasses acceleration is driving consensus upgrades (Morgan Stanley €280→€320, JPMorgan €267→€300 in L12M).

§5 — Change in Shareholder Returns

[J] Capital returns are normalising post-GrandVision. EL has moved from pure M&A-and-integrate mode to a dividend-grow + modest-buyback framework. The buyback is too small to be a primary return driver but signals management confidence.

  • Dividend: Board recommending €4.00/share for FY2025 (ex-date May 5 2026, pay June 3 2026). Scrip option available. Progressive dividend policy in place — DPS has risen from €1.03 (2018) to €4.00 (2025); ~7-year CAGR ~20%.
  • Buyback: €400M programme launched July 2024; €450M programme in 2025. €469M executed H1 2025 vs €0 H1 2024. ~1.9M shares repurchased by end-2025, representing ~0.4% of share capital at current prices. Scale is token relative to €60bn+ market cap (~0.7% yield).
  • Capital allocation priority sequence: (1) organic investment (capacity, R&D, myopia), (2) M&A (Supreme closed, pipeline unclear), (3) dividend, (4) buybacks. Buybacks are residual.
  • Net debt trajectory: Marginally declining despite Supreme outlay. GrandVision leverage cycle is ending — the next 24 months should see either accelerating deleveraging or a redeployment into M&A.
  • ❌ Sceptic: With ROIC ~5% and net debt ~€11bn, the case for aggressive buybacks is not obvious. Management's preference for bolt-on acquisitions (Supreme; potential medical technology) risks recycling capital into ROIC-dilutive deals.

§6 — Change in Demand / Revenue Dynamics

[J] Three demand forces are in structural acceleration — AI glasses adoption, myopia management, and premium lens mix. A fourth (GrandVision traffic recovery) is a normalisation trade now largely complete. The bear case on demand is that AI glasses is a consumer electronics cycle, not a structural optical upgrade cycle.

Premium lens mix (Varilux / progressives):
- Varilux XR series (AI-integrated progressive) launched 2023; the lens uses eye-tracking data to customise optics.
- LensCrafters Adaptive Progressive (Aug 2024) — proprietary premium lens using Eye-Ruler 2+ measurement system and Shamir AI data. 9/10 satisfaction rate.
- Premium lens mix is driving average transaction value growth at LensCrafters — partially offsetting weaker traffic trends vs Warby Parker's ECP share gains.
- Global progressive lens penetration remains low (~30–35% of prescription lens wearers in developed markets; much lower in EM) — long-run mix-shift runway intact.

Myopia management:
- Global myopia: 2.6bn myopes (2020) → 3.4bn projected 2030. Asia leading (52% prevalence projected 2030).
- Stellest China grew +50% Q4 2024. FY2025 myopia management portfolio +22%.
- EL's multi-product platform (Stellest, Stellest 2.0, MiYOSMART licensed from Hoya, Nikon DOT, myopia smart glasses) gives category breadth.
- ❌ Revenue contribution remains a small % of €28.5bn group revenue — despite strong growth, myopia management is likely sub-€500M today. The €2bn 2027 target requires a 4–5x in 3 years; plausible directionally but requires significant China scale-up.

AI glasses demand:
- 7 million units FY2025 (Ray-Ban + Oakley Meta). Revenue tripled H1 2025. Production targeting 20M units.
- Estimated EL revenue contribution: ~€800M–€1.5bn (based on ~€200–300/unit ASP split).
- Meta Glasses drove "more than a third" of EL's FY2025 incremental growth per upload VR sourcing.
- Demand is supply-constrained: Meta Ray-Ban Display launch paused in Jan 2026 due to inventory limits.

GrandVision / retail traffic:
- GrandVision stores (7,000+ across Europe) benefited from improved price-mix and Essilor lens integration in 2024. Comparable sales growth returned to positive in 2023.
- Warby Parker gained LensCrafters' ECP share in North America in 2024: LensCrafters customers shifted 3.4% more spend to Warby Parker YoY. ATV improvement is partially offsetting volume loss.

Online optical competitive pressure (quantified):
- Warby Parker: $771M revenue FY2024 (+15.2%), ~7.2% US market share (up from 6.8%). Active customers 2.51M.
- Zenni Optical: private, estimated >$500M revenue. DTC lens market growing.
- 1-800 Contacts: gaining share in contact lens refills.
- ❌ EL's response to online optical disruption is not structural — it is premium-up (higher ASPs to offset unit share loss), not platform-based. If Warby Parker's EBITDA margins normalise (9.5% adjusted in 2024), a profitability inflection could trigger capital market interest in the category.

§7 — Change in Competitive Dynamics

[J] EL's competitive position in lenses and premium frames is intact; the threat is at the retail/ECP channel layer. The AI glasses space is entering a competitive phase that could compress EL's margin even as volume scales.

Online optical:
- Warby Parker: 7.2% US share, ~$771M revenue. Gaining at LensCrafters' expense. EBITDA now positive (9.5% margin). Expanding stores (600-store target) — shifting from pure DTC to hybrid.
- Zenni: Private, volume-oriented, <$50 glasses. Structurally different from EL's premium positioning — the real risk is market segmentation (premium → EL; value → Zenni) eroding the mid-tier where LensCrafters competes.
- Amazon Optical: nascent but distribution scale is a structural threat to prescription lens retail.
- 1-800 Contacts: contacts DTC; does not directly compete with EL's lens business but competes for wallet at ECP level.

Fielmann (European retail):
- €2.3bn revenue FY2024 (+15%). Operating in 15 countries. Target: 50% revenues outside Germany by 2030.
- Expanding in Spain (+10%), Poland, Czech Republic (double-digit), UK (nascent).
- GrandVision and Fielmann will increasingly compete for same European mid-market ECP customer. Fielmann's value-positioned model (free frames with lens purchase in Germany) is a structural low-price competitor.
- ❌ Fielmann's expansion is underappreciated risk for GrandVision's European store estate. If GrandVision traffic comes under pressure from Fielmann in Spain/Italy, synergy assumptions get revised down.

AI glasses competitive entry:
- Apple: Rumoured smart glasses in development. Apple's distribution/ecosystem creates potential for rapid category displacement.
- Google: Return to AR glasses (Project Iris); prototype-stage but strategic intent is established.
- Snap: Spectacles with AR display — niche, creator-oriented.
- Meta-EL partnership tension (Bloomberg Feb-26): Meta wants lower price points; EL wants premium positioning. Pricing conflict could slow demand or shrink ASPs.
- EL + Meta patent lawsuit (Jan 2026, Bloomberg) — third-party patent challenge. Ongoing legal risk.

Lens/frame competitive dynamics:
- Safilo: Perennially weak; losing licensed brands (Hugo Boss, Moschino). Not a structural threat to EL.
- LVMH/Kering eyewear: In-house production of luxury frames (LVMH acquired Thelios stake; Kering Eyewear operating independently). Premium licensed frame business at risk as luxury houses internalise eyewear margins.
- Alcon : No overlap in frames; contact lenses competitive with EL's contact lens business (Crizal etc.) — but EL is not primarily in contacts.
- Zeiss (private): Direct competitor in premium ophthalmic lenses (Zeiss individual lenses vs Varilux). Zeiss gaining independent ECPs who want to dual-source.

§8 — Change in Macro Environment

[J] The macro backdrop is bifurcated: tailwinds from ageing demographics, myopia, and EM premiumisation are structural; headwinds from US tariffs and consumer discretionary pressure in the premium mid-tier are cyclical. Net, EL's demand outlook is resilient but not recession-proof.

Consumer spending sensitivity:
- Prescription lenses are semi-essential (optical correction is a health need, not discretionary). Premium frames and sun are more discretionary.
- The luxury slowdown in 2024 (LVMH, Kering revenue declines) did not materially hit EL — premium lens mix continued to improve. Frames are more exposed.
- LensCrafters US traffic: facing pressure from Warby Parker share gains; average ticket growth partially offsets. Not a structural collapse.

Ageing demographics:
- Presbyopia (need for progressives) onset at ~40. Baby Boomer cohort in prime progressive lens cycle in Europe and US. Structural tailwind for Varilux for 10–15 years.
- Over-65 population in EU growing at ~2% per annum through 2030. Optical need does not plateau until very late life.

Myopia acceleration:
- Prevalence accelerating post-pandemic: screen time + reduced outdoor time driving earlier onset in children.
- Asia Pacific is epicentre: 52% prevalence in Asia projected by 2030 (vs 40% global).
- China government policy actively supporting myopia prevention — aligns with EL's Stellest positioning as a state-endorsed health product.

FX:
- EL reports in €; meaningful USD (North America ~35% revenue), CNY (China ~12%), BRL (Brazil) exposure.
- FY2025 constant-FX growth 11.2% vs reported growth lower — currency was a headwind. USD weakness (post-Trump tariff volatility) is a headwind.
- Tariffs (China 145%, Thailand 36%, EU 20%) are a COGS headwind. EL quantified: COGS +8.8% H1 2025 attributable primarily to tariffs. Company response: price increases (selective) + supply chain diversification (Mexico, Thailand ex-tariff expansion).

EM penetration:
- China: Long-term growth market. 30 years presence. Myopia + rising disposable income + premium frame penetration. Government myopia policy is a structural accelerator.
- India: Sub-scale penetration but growing. Not material to near-term numbers.
- Brazil: Currency-volatile but significant market. EL has substantial Brazilian operations.

§9 — Change in Regulatory / Political Environment

[J] The tariff environment is the most immediate regulatory change force. Healthcare regulation (FDA device approval for Nuance Audio) is an enabler, not a headwind. China optical regulation is a long-term risk to monitor but not acute.

US tariff regime (2025):
- April 2025 reciprocal tariff announcement: China 145%, Thailand 36%, EU/Italy ~20% on luxury goods.
- EL's manufacturing footprint: Italy (premium frames and lenses), China (volume frames, some lenses), Thailand (expanding), Mexico (new facility).
- Response: selective price increases (mid-single digit % passed to consumer per management commentary), supply chain diversification. Mexico and Thailand facilities being expanded.
- ❌ Risk: If tariffs persist at elevated levels and EL cannot fully pass through, gross margin erosion will be structural. Italian/French manufacturing cannot be moved (craft skills); the Italy 20% tariff on luxury exports is particularly problematic for high-ASP frames.

FDA / CE marking (Nuance Audio):
- EL received FDA OTC medical device approval for Nuance Audio Glasses (April 2025). CE marking under EU Medical Devices Regulation also secured.
- This is regulatory progress enabling EL to enter the hearing aid market — a meaningful new revenue vertical with higher margins than standard frames.
- US launch April 2025 at $1,100/unit. European launch H1 2025.

China optical regulation:
- China has enacted myopia prevention policies mandating schools reduce screen time and outdoor activities. Beneficial to Stellest/myopia management demand.
- No adverse regulatory threat to EL's China optical or myopia business identified.

EU digital health regulation:
- AI-enabled smart glasses (Ray-Ban Meta with health monitoring features) may attract EU AI Act scrutiny. No immediate regulatory headwind identified but one to monitor as AI glasses gain health application capability.

US-EU trade (luxury goods):
- European luxury goods tariff risk is real but partially mitigated by EL's diversified manufacturing. Key watch point: if Italy-origin premium frames face sustained 20% US import duty, margin on Luxottica's core product is under pressure.

§10 — Technology / Disruption Changes

[J] Ray-Ban Meta is the single most material technology disruption EL has internalized — it converts a pure optical hardware company into a wearable tech platform company. The question is margin trajectory and partner dependency, not demand trajectory.

Ray-Ban Meta AI glasses:
- Gen 1 (2023): 1M units. Gen 2 (2024): ~2M cumulative. FY2025: 7M units. Production target: 20M+ by end-2026.
- Revenue tripled in H1 2025. Revenue contribution: ~€1.2–1.5bn estimated FY2025 (based on tripling from ~€365M cited for FY2024).
- New SKUs: Oakley Meta (launched H2 2025), Meta Ray-Ban Display ($799, display-integrated).
- Margin profile: Hardware (glasses + embedded electronics) likely thinner than pure optical lens business. EL recognises full product revenue; royalties/IP from Meta split not disclosed.
- Strategic fit: Excellent — leverages EL's manufacturing scale, Ray-Ban brand, and ECP distribution. Does not require EL to build AI.
- ❌ Strategic risk: Meta controls the AI stack. If Meta's AI offering is commoditised (OpenAI partnering with another frame maker; Apple Glasses launch), EL's uniqueness is manufacturing + brand — commoditisable.

Pricing tension (Bloomberg Feb-26):
- Meta wants lower price points to drive volume and ecosystem; EL wants premium pricing to protect Ray-Ban brand equity.
- "Repeated and at times pointed internal debates." No outright standoff. Partnership intact as of Feb-26.
- Patent lawsuit (Jan 2026): third-party filing naming both Meta and EL. Monitoring status.

Nuance Audio hearing glasses:
- FDA-approved OTC hearing device integrated with prescription frames. $1,100 price point.
- Launched US April 2025, EU H1 2025. First product to enter a new healthcare vertical.
- Structural optionality: 1.5bn people globally have hearing loss; ~30% also have visual impairment (co-morbidity argument for combined device).
- Near-term revenue: Immaterial to group. Long-term strategic value: establishes EL as a health-device platform company.

Photochromic / lens innovation:
- Transitions Next Generation lenses: continuous improvement programme. Transitions is ~75% owned by EL (through Essilor heritage).
- Varilux XR: AI-personalised progressive. Next wave of personalised lens is the growth vector within professional solutions.

AR/VR longer-term:
- Meta's display glasses ($799 with single-lens display) is a bridge to full AR. If full AR glasses become mainstream (5–7 year horizon), EL's manufacturing and optical expertise is essential for any AR OEM.
- ❌ Long-range risk: if AR glasses displace conventional corrective eyewear (digital correction vs lens correction), EL's core lens business faces structural displacement. Timeline is very long-dated.

AI-powered prescription:
- EL investing in AI optometry tools (remote refraction, vision testing). Enables prescriptions without in-person ECP visit — could support DTC channel growth, or could cannibalise ECP channel relationships.

§11 — R&D and Pipeline

[J] EL's R&D investment is material but disclosed inconsistently. The pipeline is differentiated in myopia management and hearing (Nuance). The smart glasses R&D is primarily Meta's. EL's proprietary R&D is lens and device-focused.

R&D spend:
- EL does not prominently disclose a standalone R&D % revenue. Analyst estimates suggest ~2–3% of revenue (~€550–800M) at current scale. Essilor heritage had historically disclosed R&D spend; post-merger, disclosure is consolidated.
- Peers: Alcon ~7% R&D/revenue; BLCO ~12%. EL is at the lower end, reflecting its manufacturing/brand mix vs pure MedTech positioning.

Myopia pipeline:
- Stellest 2.0: New optical design with stronger axial elongation inhibition signal (Nov 2025). Clinical evidence: 6-year cumulative data; 8-year MiYOSMART study (60% retention at 8 years).
- Essilor Stellest Smartglasses: myopia management function embedded in AI glasses form factor. Concept demonstrated at CIIE Nov 2025.
- Pre-myopia solution: EL announced an intervention-before-onset product at CIIE — very early stage.
- Nikon DOT: Licensed/partnership product ramping "swiftly" per H1 2025 commentary. Adds portfolio breadth.
- Acuvue Abiliti (J&J partnership): EL has a distribution/co-marketing arrangement; J&J owns the IP. Orthokeratology contact lens for myopia — different modality.

Hearing / medical:
- Nuance Audio: FDA-approved OTC hearing aid integrated with glasses. Pipeline: potential upgrade to prescription hearing classification (higher market access vs OTC).
- Broader medical ambition (Milleri rhetoric): AI diagnostics, telehealth optometry. No concrete product pipeline beyond Nuance Audio disclosed.

Transitions / photochromic:
- EL is the majority owner of Transitions Optical. Innovation pipeline: Transitions Vantage (variable polarisation), Transitions XTRActive. Incremental, not disruptive.

AI / diagnostics:
- Partnership with Essilor Instruments for AI-powered measurement (Eye-Ruler 2+). LensCrafters using AI data to personalise lenses. Platform optionality for clinical deployment.
- ❌ Sceptic: EL's AI investments are primarily manufacturing/personalisation AI, not healthcare AI. The medical AI rhetoric from Milleri is aspirational; no revenue-generating medical AI product pipeline is visible.

§13 — Historical Track Record

[J] The Essilor-Luxottica merger is the most important test of management credibility — and it was delivered. GrandVision is on track.   • Margin compression in FY2025 despite record revenue — adjusted EBIT fell from 16.7% to 16.0% YoY; if tariffs persist and AI glasses margin is dilutive, the 19–20% 2026 target is unreachable. This would be a negative catalyst.
