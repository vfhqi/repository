Financial Performance: FY2025 Results & Q1 2026 Update
FY2025 Results Deep Dive: P&L and Profitability

Banca Generali closed FY2025 with a record consolidated net profit of €445.8 million, marking a 3.4% increase year-over-year. This headline performance was largely driven by a solid expansion in recurring net profit, which grew by 6.8% to reach an all-time high of €362.5 million. Net banking income increased by 2.0% to €1,000.3 million, supported by an 8.6% rise in gross recurring fees to €1,130.9 million. Net operating expenses for the year stood at €361.2 million, up 22.9% from the prior year, primarily reflecting a 21.9% increase in staff expenses. Despite these cost increases, the bank maintained an efficient operating profile with an adjusted cost/income ratio landing at 28%.

Net Inflows Clarification and AUM Growth

While initial expectations and queries suggested an €8.4 billion net inflow figure for FY2025, official company disclosures confirm that total net inflows actually amounted to €6.84 billion. This figure successfully exceeded management's initial target of >€6.0 billion, representing a 2.9% increase over 2024 despite headwinds from Mediobanca's exchange tender offer earlier in the year.

The €6.84 billion inflow was driven heavily by Assets under Investment , which contributed €4.0 billion (58% of the total). Within AUI, managed solutions saw strong demand for financial wrappers (€1.8 billion since year-start) and in-house funds (€1.0 billion). Other assets contributed €2.8 billion, fueled primarily by demand for assets under administration (€2.1 billion) and deposit acceleration (€0.7 billion) linked to maturing bonds.

Banca Generali's acquisition of Intermonte Partners SIM S.p.A. represents a significant strategic pivot aimed at internalizing market-recognized capabilities that would be difficult to replicate organically. The primary industrial rationale is to bring trading, derivatives, and investment banking skills in-house to enhance the value proposition for entrepreneurs and small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs). A central component of this initiative is a pilot program focused on distributing Banca Generali's selected banking and investment products through the insurance agents of Generali Italia and Alleanza. Additionally, the bank is launching a targeted pilot program to explore cross-selling and up-selling synergies by deploying joint teams of insurance agents and financial advisors for high-end clients.

Partnership Execution: Withheld Metrics and Compliance Complexities

Despite the strategic importance of the Generali and Alleanza partnership, specific success metrics, full rollout timelines, and revenue contribution modeling are currently unavailable. However, due to the pending voluntary exchange offer from Mediobanca targeting Banca Generali shares, the bank has suspended the announcement of its new strategic plan and will not communicate numerical targets regarding the Alleanza partnership until the takeover situation is resolved.

The partnership also introduces notable compliance and governance complexities due to its nature as a related-party transaction. Consequently, the agreement entails a potential risk of conflicts of interest regarding the fairness and substantial correctness of the underlying terms. To mitigate these channel conflicts and compliance risks, the Control and Risk Committee relied on assessments from an independent expert, ultimately concluding that the risks are no different from those typically inherent in standard related-party transactions.

The implementation of the new CRR3 regulatory framework at the start of 2025 created a negative impact of approximately 3.8 percentage points.
