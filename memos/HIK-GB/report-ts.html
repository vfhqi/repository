Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Hikma is undergoing an operational and leadership transition as it resets injectable margins, presenting a highly cash-generative entry point at c.8x P/E.
1.
Diversified pharma manufacturer: Operating globally, Hikma generates revenue by producing generic, specialty sterile injectable, and branded medicines across three key business segments: Injectables, Branded, and Hikma Rx
2.
Asymmetric investment thesis: A patient investor could capture significant upside as Hikma resets injectable margins in 2026 and ramps up higher-value contract manufacturing and proprietary ready-to-use platforms by 2027
3.
Critical earnings driver: The single most important swing factor for forward earnings over the next 18 months is the successful commercialization of Tyzavan and other Zagreb-developed ready-to-use injectable formulations
4.
Consistent historic performance: Multi-year growth has been consistent, with 2025 core revenue growing 6.00% to $3.35 billion, though core operating margin declined slightly to 22.10% due to injectable headwinds
5.
Material margin pressure: Earnings predictability is threatened by unabated price erosion on US small-molecule generics and intensifying biosimilar competition, which historically compressed injectable margins from c.35.00% down to 31.00% in 2025
6.
Favourable strategic verdict: A deeper look is strongly warranted because the current valuation of c.8x P/E excessively discounts the resilient, high-margin MENA Branded business and is supported by a $250.00 million share buyback
7.
Decisive structural refocus: The strategic decision to wind down the non-core 503B compounding business allows management to focus capital entirely on high-margin sterile injectables

Section 3 – What the company does 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Hikma maintains a market-leading position in MENA branded pharmaceuticals and US sterile injectables, though the latter faces temporary competitive headwind resets.

Segment Economics and Product Portfolios

1.
Premium sterile injectables: Producing and supplying generic injectable and specialty sterile medicines to hospitals globally, the segment is Hikma's historic profit driver
2.
Leading regional branded: Selling branded generics and in-licensed patented products across 17 Middle East and North Africa countries, this segment represents c.25.40% of group sales
3.
Differentiated oral solids: Operating under the Hikma Rx banner, this US-based generic business focuses on complex respiratory, nasals, and contract manufacturing
4.
Contractual revenue streams: Revenues are highly contractual, derived from institutional hospital tenders in MENA and group purchasing organisation agreements in the United States
5.
Profit-favoured value chain: Sitting as a vertically integrated manufacturer with 29 plants, Hikma avoids intermediary dependency and captures high manufacturing margins

Barriers to Entry and Industry Structure

6.
Extensive switching barriers: High switching costs are driven by sterile manufacturing complexity and regulatory approvals, making Hikma the seventh largest supplier of generic medicines in the US
7.
Fragmented retail customers: US retail sales are concentrated with three wholesalers representing 34.00% of 2025 revenue, while MENA customers are highly fragmented across public and private channels
8.
Intense generic competition: Global generic markets are crowded and subject to price deflation, but consolidated sterile injectable sectors favor scaled players like Hikma and Sandoz
9.
Other side of the story: Sceptical analysts warn that the US generic market is structurally commoditizing, with rapid price drops of 60.00% to 70.00% on newly launched generics like liraglutide demonstrating that high margins cannot be sustained

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case is strengthened by asymmetric rerating potential as injectable margins bottom and ready-to-use small molecules scale up.

US CMO Expansion and Device In-Licensing

1.
Differentiated CMO pipeline: Preparing the Columbus, Ohio facility for a significant contract starting in 2026 and scaling in 2027 will transition the US oral generic business to profitable growth
2.
High plausibility support: The Columbus CMO opportunity has high plausibility because domestic US manufacturing is highly favored by global pharma clients looking for secure supply chains
3.
Device co-development driver: Signing an exclusive licensing agreement for device technology accelerates the development of the generic Ellipta inhalation program to access premium respiratory markets
4.
Device failure mechanism: The generic Ellipta program's success is dependent on clinical device-drug bioequivalence, where any formulation mismatch or regulatory delay would halt the US launch

Zagreb RTU Scaling and Branded Chronic Shift

5.
High-margin RTU platforms: Launching Tyzavan and developing 15 ready-to-use formulations will drive high-margin hospital volumes, serving as the core forward growth driver
6.
Zagreb R&D plausibility: The commercialization of Zagreb ready-to-use formulations has high plausibility because room-temperature stable RTU bags resolve immediate hospital pharmacy operational bottlenecks
7.
Zagreb failure mechanism: Intensified pricing pressure from competitors launching secondary ready-to-use formulations could erode the expected premium margin
8.
Branded chronic disease shift: Accelerating oncology and chronic treatments in Saudi Arabia and Algeria positions the high-margin Branded segment for sustainable double-digit CER growth
9.
Chronic shift failure: Local currency devaluations across volatile North African markets could completely erase the translation of regional sales growth into US dollars
10.
Independent multi-engine probability: The bull case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously, as the MENA chronic portfolio operates independently of US generic inhalation and sterile CMO timelines
11.
Other side of the story: Sceptical analysts note that if US generic pricing pressure worsens back to double-digits, the cash flow from Hikma Rx will dry up and limit R&D reinvestment into the complex sterile pipeline

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by US pricing erosion and MENA geopolitics, but balanced by Hikma's localized supply chains.

Pricing and Wholesaler Risks

1.
Aggressive competitor pricing: Severe US pricing erosion on high-margin small molecules like testosterone or a rapid 60-70% price drop in liraglutide immediately compresses injectables profitability
2.
Severe distributor concentration: Extreme concentration is present in the US where three major wholesalers account for 34.00% of 2025 revenue, creating significant credit and contract risk

Geopolitical and Regulatory Headwinds

3.
Middle East instability: Ongoing conflict in the Levant and North Africa poses shipping, energy, and insurance cost inflation, which could compress the Branded operating margin
4.
Unfavourable tariff dynamics: Imposition of aggressive US tariffs on drug imports would hurt European-manufactured injectables, though domestic Ohio plants provide partial insulation
5.
Partner performance dependency: Extreme reliance on external licensing partners like Celltrion for biosimilars makes Hikma's pipeline growth highly dependent on third-party manufacturing quality
6.
Critical warning signal: The key early-warning metric to monitor is the monthly market share and volume adoption of Tyzavan in the US room-temperature vancomycin market
7.
Other side of the story: Sceptical analysts overstate Middle East risks, as local governments are ordering up to six months of stockpiled medicine, driving substantial volume growth for Branded

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership is actively transforming the company with the return of CEO Said Darwazah and a regional decentralization strategy.
1.
CEO leadership transition: In December 2025, CEO Riad Mishlawi stood down, and Said Darwazah stepped back in as CEO for a designated two-year period to restore strategic stability
2.
Regional decentralization structure: Management has implemented a decentralization of management into regional structures, creating Deputy CEO roles for MENA (Mazen Darwazah) and North America/Europe (Khalid Nabilsi)
3.
Expanded commercial responsibility: R&D head Hafrun Fridriksdottir took on the expanded role of President, US, taking full responsibility for US injectables sales in addition to oral generics
4.
Corporate governance realignment: Said Darwazah resigned as Executive Chairman to focus entirely on the CEO role, with Victoria Hull appointed as independent Chair of the Board
