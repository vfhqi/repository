KEY FINDINGS (BLUF)

  • Structural shrinkage underway: LBTYK consolidated revenue has contracted meaningfully since the Sunrise spin-off (Nov-24), now reflecting a sub-$5bn HoldCo wrapper over equity-accounted JVs; the reported top-line is not comparable L2Y without stripping Sunrise — all growth optics are distorted [#J]
  • JV EBITDA under coordinated pressure: VodafoneZiggo EBITDA fell ~10.5% in FY-25 to €1.7bn (from €1.9bn in FY-24); Telenet EBITDAaL declined low-to-mid single digits in FY-25; VMO2 guided -3 to -5% in 2026 — all three JVs in negative trajectory simultaneously [#J]
  • Q1-26 inflection signal — real but early: Consolidated Q1-26 revenue of $1,274.6m (+8.8% YoY) and adj EBITDA of $366.5m (+12.9%) represent the strongest quarter in several periods; driven by commercial broadband momentum at Telenet and VZ, but not yet visible in full-year trajectories [#J]
  • ARPU deceleration is structural, not cyclical: VodafoneZiggo consumer ARPU growth slowed from +4.2% in Q1-24 to +1.0% in Q4-25; Telenet ARPU turned negative (-0.7% Q4-25); pricing power is eroding under FTTH overbuild pressure across both core markets [#J]
  • HoldCo FCF is negative and structurally so: Adjusted FCF was -$319m in Q1-26 (vs -$141m Q1-25); FY-25 HoldCo FCF negative; $2.2bn corporate cash is the sole buffer; buybacks funded by asset recycling, not operating cash generation [#J]
  • Leverage is high and rising at opco level: VodafoneZiggo ND/EBITDA at 4.5-5.0x; Telenet 3.5-4.5x — both materially above the EU telco median of ~2.8x; limited deleveraging capacity given negative FCF [#J]
  • Ziggo consolidation reshapes the story from H2-27: Liberty Global to acquire Vodafone's 50% VZ stake (close July-26); Ziggo Group spin-off targeted H2-27; this is a structural transformation event, not an earnings fix — current period P&L remains pressured [#J]
  • Peer comparison unflattering: Proximus delivered domestic EBITDA growth of ~2.8% in FY-24 and expects ~2% group EBITDA growth in FY-25; Swisscom EBITDAaL rose 22.6% in FY-25 (including Vodafone Italia consolidation); LBTYK's JVs are all declining in EBITDA [#J]

§1 Executive Summary

LBTYK sits at a peculiar junction: a HoldCo undergoing deliberate structural simplification (Sunrise out, Ziggo consolidation in) while its three JV operating companies face simultaneous EBITDA headwinds from overbuild, football-rights gaps, and capex peaks.

  • Verdict: The L2Y earnings trend at every OpCo is negative on EBITDA; top-line pressure is partially cushioned by mobile and B2B growth but not enough to offset fixed-network subscriber and ARPU erosion [#J]
  • Portfolio lens: LBTYK does not consolidate VMO2 or VodafoneZiggo — both are equity-accounted JVs; consolidated P&L covers Telenet (100%) + VMO2 Ireland + corporate; the real earnings story is in the JV financials [#I]
  • Q1-26 data point: Consolidated revenue $1,274.6m (+8.8% YoY); adj EBITDA $366.5m (+12.9%); these are the cleanest like-for-like comparisons available post-Sunrise spin [#I]
  • FY-25 HoldCo baseline: Liberty Telecom (consolidated) revenue ~$3.7bn, EBITDA ~$1.5bn; VMO2 JV revenue ~$13.3bn, EBITDA ~$4.7bn (100% basis) [#I]
  • Earnings trajectory: VZ EBITDA -10.5% in FY-25; Telenet EBITDAaL down low-to-mid SD in FY-25; VMO2 guiding -3 to -5% EBITDA for FY-26 — no OpCo in EBITDA growth [#J]
  • Commercial inflection note: Q1-26 showed best broadband net adds in three years at Telenet; VZ broadband losses improving for fourth consecutive quarter — operationally better, financially still negative [#A]
  • Buyback vs FCF mismatch: ~$700m returned in 2024 (~10% of shares); funded by Sunrise proceeds and asset sales, not operating FCF — capital return is unsustainable at current cash generation rates [#J]
  • Sceptical note: Q1-26 revenue beat optics partly reflect FX (USD/EUR) and the inclusion of post-Sunrise perimeter; underlying rebased growth is more modest and management has a track record of rebaselined comparisons masking organic decline [#J]

  • Q1-25 vs Q1-26 clean comp: Q1-25 consolidated revenue $1,171.2m; Q1-26 $1,274.6m (+8.8%); this is the most comparable period-on-period figure available post-Sunrise [#I]
  • Telenet revenue trajectory: FY-24 revenue ~€2,851m (broadly flat vs FY-23 €2,855m); Q4-25 revenue €723.8m (-1.3% YoY); revenue stability masks deteriorating EBITDA mix as football-rights costs fell away but subscriber pressure mounts [#A]
  • VodafoneZiggo revenue trajectory: FY-24 revenue €4.1bn (+1.5% YoY); FY-25 revenue €4.0bn (-2.4% YoY); Q4-25 revenue ~€1,020m (-2.3% YoY) — accelerating decline [#I]
  • VMO2 revenue trajectory (100% basis): FY-24 revenue £10.68bn (-2.1% YoY); FY-25 guided revenue edged +0.2% to £7.7bn (9-month comparison excluding Daisy); fixed consumer revenue -1.1%; B2B fixed +22% (Daisy consolidation distortion) [#I]
  • Mobile ARPU pressure (VZ): Consumer mobile postpaid ARPU Q4-24 €19 (-2.3% YoY); consumer broadband lost 115,000 subscribers in FY-24 to 3.2m — KPN and Odido/T-Mobile capturing share via FTTH [#A]

  • B2B as partial offset: VMO2 B2B fixed +22% (Daisy-inflated); Telenet B2B revenue growth tracking mid-SD organically; VZ B2B growing — but not large enough base to offset residential erosion [#A]
  • Revenue rebasing opacity: Management's consistent use of "rebased" and "excluding Daisy Transaction" adjustments creates difficulty in assessing true organic momentum; multiple bases apply simultaneously across OpCos [#J]
  • FX headwind/tailwind: LBTYK reports in USD; most operational revenues in EUR/GBP; USD strength in 2024-25 created reported headwinds on translation; USD weakness in Q1-26 partially inflated the reported +8.8% [#A]
  • Netherlands FTTH structural threat: KPN and Odido/T-Mobile together passing >70% of NL broadband addressable market with FTTH; VZ HFC network modernisation (XGS-PON) underway but reaching 20,000 connections by YE-25 — far behind the overbuild pace [#A]
  • Sceptical note: VZ revenue decline of ~2.4% in FY-25 is likely to persist or worsen in FY-26 as FTTH competitive intensity peaks; the "improving commercial momentum" narrative in Q1-26 has not yet translated into revenue growth, only slower decline [#J]

  • EU telco sector reference: EU converged telco median adj EBITDA margin ~30-35%; LBTYK JVs historically operated at premium margins (cable economics); that premium is eroding [#I]
  • Telenet EBITDA evolution: Q4-25 adj EBITDA €316.2m (-8.8% YoY); FY-24 adj EBITDAaL €471.6m (-24.2% rebased) — the football-rights gap (exclusivity loss in Jupiler Pro League from 2023 cycle) created a structural step-down [#I]
  • Football rights quantification: Telenet's loss of exclusive Belgian Pro League rights from the 2023-24 cycle removed an estimated €50-70m annual EBITDA contribution; Play Sports retains UEFA CL (non-exclusive), Premier League and Bundesliga rights — lower absolute value [#A]
  • Telenet 2026 guidance: Low single-digit EBITDA growth expected in FY-26 — the first positive guidance in three years; base effect of football rights gap now cycling through [#J]
  • VodafoneZiggo margin: FY-24 EBITDA ~€1.9bn on €4.1bn revenue = ~46% adj EBITDA margin; FY-25 EBITDA €1.7bn on €4.0bn revenue = ~42.5% — approximately 350bps margin compression in one year ❌ [#J]
  • VMO2 margin: FY-24 adj EBITDA £3.95bn on £10.68bn revenue = 36.9% margin (-3.7% EBITDA decline); well below EU cable premium (historically 45-50%) but broadly in line with converged telco median [#A]
  • Capex intensity vs margin: VMO2 capex ~£2.0-2.2bn (FY-25 guided), ~18-21% of revenue — at EU median; VZ capex/revenue ~21% (FY-24 €850m on €4.1bn); Telenet capex/revenue ~38% in Q4-25 (elevated, Wyre-related build) — well above EU norm [#I]
  • EBITDAaL vs adj EBITDA gap: Lease-adjusted metrics are lower by €80-120m annually at Telenet level; as network build accelerates, lease capitalisation and amortisation weigh further on EBITDAaL [#A]
  • Proximus margin comparison: Proximus domestic EBITDA margin ~35-37% (FY-24, converged telecom); EBITDA growth +2.8% in FY-24; Proximus delivering EBITDA growth while LBTYK JVs decline — direct peer unfavorable comparison ❌ [#J]
  • Swisscom margin (Switzerland only): Swiss operations EBITDA margin ~40%+ historically; stable in FY-25 (transition year with Italy consolidation); Swisscom Switzerland generating positive FCF — unlike LBTYK JVs [#A]
  • Cost efficiency partial offset: VMO2 achieved £540m synergy target 18 months ahead of schedule; Telenet driving digital efficiency savings; but these are one-time or slowing savings that cannot offset structural revenue headwinds indefinitely [#A]
  • Quality-of-margin risk: Reported EBITDA at VZ and Telenet includes non-recurring cost savings and transitional items; sustainable EBITDA run-rates post-footprint reset likely lower than reported figures suggest [#J]
  • Sceptical note: The pattern across all three JVs is margin compression driven by structural forces (overbuild, rights costs, capex peaks) that management frames as "transitional" — but the transition has now lasted 3+ years at VZ and Telenet; Wisdom Library flywheel-vs-treadmill model applies: are these cost saves a genuine flywheel or a treadmill that requires perpetual reinvestment? §4 EPS Trajectory

  • Verdict: LBTYK does not generate positive reported EPS at HoldCo level on a consistent basis; the company is in a phase of HoldCo FCF burn, funded by asset disposals; EPS at the JV operating company level is also under pressure [#J]
  • EPS disclosure note: LBTYK does not formally report GAAP EPS in the conventional sense; the company focuses on adj FCF and adj EBITDA as primary metrics; Q1-26 EPS not disclosed per company filings (GuruFocus notes this) [#I]
  • HoldCo adjusted FCF trajectory: Q1-26 adj FCF -$319.3m (vs -$141.2m Q1-25); FY-25 HoldCo adj FCF negative; FY-24 also negative; the direction of travel on adjusted FCF is deteriorating, not improving ❌ [#J]
  • Capex peak timing: Wyre (VZ's NL fibre vehicle) capex peak expected in 2025-26; Telenet capital intensity expected to reduce from 2026 onward per guidance; VMO2 capex £2.0-2.2bn; aggregate peak capex across JVs is suppressing FCF [#I]
  • JV-level FCF (VZ): VZ adj FCF guidance ~€200m (FY-26); on an EBITDA base of €1.7bn this represents ~12% FCF conversion — weak by EU telco standards [#A]
  • VMO2 FCF: FY-24 FCF £494.5m; £850m distributed to shareholders (including debt-funded distributions); FCF generation adequate to cover JV-level distributions but not HoldCo dividends + buybacks [#A]
  • Buyback arithmetic: ~$700m repurchased in FY-24 (~10% of shares outstanding); cash sourced from Sunrise spin proceeds ($3bn+ cash) and asset recycling; this is financial engineering of the capital structure, not organic FCF-backed shareholder returns [#J]
  • Corporate cash buffer: $2.2bn corporate cash at EOY-25; provides ~2-3 years of buyback runway at current pace even with zero HoldCo FCF; creates a floor on buyback optionality [#A]
  • EPS-equivalent proxy (adj FCF/share): Deteriorating sharply in FY-25 and Q1-26; trajectory is unfavorable even adjusting for the Sunrise spin-off perimeter change [#J]
  • VZ acquisition accounting impact: Liberty Global acquiring Vodafone's 50% VZ stake for €1bn cash + 10% Ziggo Group equity; transaction closes July-26; post-close VZ is fully consolidated — this will mechanically inflate reported EBITDA and revenue but also dramatically increase reported debt [#A]
  • Sceptical note: The buyback-while-FCF-negative pattern is a classic incumbent-dilemma signal (Wisdom Library: Silver) — management returning capital to maintain share price support while underlying cash generation deteriorates; this is not indefinitely sustainable [#J]

§7 Sector / Macro Context

  • Verdict: The EU telco sector macro is modestly supportive (stable pricing environment, regulatory relief on spectrum costs, FMC as defensive bundle) but structural FTTH overbuilding in NL and Belgium creates sector-specific headwinds that disproportionately affect LBTYK vs Proximus/Swisscom [#J]
  • FTTH overbuild acceleration: Netherlands has the most intense FTTH competitive environment in Europe; KPN + Odido/T-Mobile + regional players passing majority of addressable market; VZ's HFC network faces structural substitution risk that is not a 5-10 year threat — it is a 2-3 year subscriber retention problem [#J]
  • Belgian market dynamics: Orange Belgium + SFR Belux competing on mobile; Proximus fibre build accelerating in Flanders (Telenet's stronghold); Base/Telenet FMC is a defensive response, not a market-share offensive [#A]
  • EU regulatory environment: Digital Markets Act + National Regulatory Authority rulings generally supportive of infrastructure competition; no regulatory backstop preventing further overbuild of Telenet/VZ footprints [#I]
  • Interest rate context: European rates declining from 2024 highs; positive for refinancing JV debt at lower coupon; VMO2/VZ/Telenet carry floating-rate portions; some relief at the margin — insufficient to offset EBITDA declines [#A]
  • Energy cost tailwind: European energy prices moderated in 2025; network operating costs partially deflating; some margin protection from energy normalisation but this is cyclical, not structural improvement [#A]
  • FMC bundle as macro hedge: FMC convergence (mobile + fixed bundle) reduces churn structurally vs single-play customers; BASE FMC growth at Telenet is a genuine defensive moat vs mobile-only competitors [#A]
  • Capex super-cycle context: EU telco sector is mid-cycle on FTTH + 5G capex; Telenet guiding capex intensity reduction from 2026; VZ Wyre capex peak in 2025-26; the macro trajectory points toward improving FCF from 2027 onward as capex normalises — but this requires sustained EBITDA to materialise FCF [#A]
  • Sceptical note: The "post-capex FCF inflection" thesis for LBTYK is contingent on EBITDA stabilising while capex falls — but if FTTH overbuild continues to erode EBITDA, the FCF recovery is delayed or cancelled; the bull and bear cases are highly path-dependent [#J]

§8 Quality of Earnings

  • Verdict: Earnings quality at LBTYK is below the EU telco cohort average: heavy use of rebasing and adjustments, negative HoldCo FCF, aggressive buyback-while-burning-cash behaviour, and structural complexity from equity-accounted JVs reduce confidence in reported metrics [#J]
  • Adjusted vs reported gap: LBTYK consistently reports "adj EBITDA" stripping restructuring, integration, SBC, and one-off costs; the cumulative gap between adj EBITDA and GAAP EBITDA has been wide and persistent — a yellow flag on underlying profitability [#A]
  • JV equity accounting opacity: VMO2 and VodafoneZiggo are equity-accounted (50% JVs pre-Ziggo consolidation); LBTYK's P&L reflects a share of net income/loss, not EBITDA — true operational performance is only visible in JV-level filings, not LBTYK consolidated [#I]
  • Rebasing proliferation: At any given reporting period, LBTYK provides 3-4 adjusted growth comparators (reported, rebased, excl-Sunrise, excl-Daisy, excl-football-rights) — legitimate in isolation, problematic in aggregate; it becomes structurally difficult to hold management to an unadjusted organic growth accountability ❌ [#J]
  • Asset recycling as earnings proxy: Sunrise spin-off proceeds, Slovakia disposal proceeds, Wyre-related asset transfers are all used to fund buybacks; this is not earnings quality — it is controlled liquidation of the asset base to return cash to shareholders while operating cash flows are insufficient [#J]
  • FCF conversion rate: VZ adj FCF ~€200m on €1.7bn EBITDA = ~12% conversion; Telenet FCF negative in Q4-25 (capex ~38% of revenue); VMO2 ~£494.5m on £3.95bn EBITDA = ~12.5%; EU telco median FCF conversion ~20-25% — LBTYK OpCos are running at half the sector conversion rate ❌ [#J]
  • PREDICTABILITY score (Wisdom Library: Gold): Predictability-obsession model flags: LBTYK has no consistent, unadjusted earnings metric that has been stable or improving over L2Y; every headline metric requires adjustor familiarity; a generalist investor cannot assess this business from headline figures [#J]
  • Proximus quality comparison: Proximus provides GAAP revenue growth, unadjusted EBITDA, and adj FCF with clear comparators; reports are structurally simpler; quality of earnings is higher even if absolute EBITDA is lower [#A]
  • Sceptical note: The incumbent-dilemma (Wisdom Library: Silver) is evident in LBTYK's reporting: the company optimises for short-term capital return metrics (buyback, NAV framing) while the underlying operational cash generation deteriorates; this pattern historically precedes forced deleveraging or strategic repositioning [#J]

§9 Sceptical Synthesis

  • Bear case thesis: LBTYK is structuring itself as a "sum-of-parts" HoldCo to extract value via Ziggo Group spin-off; the bull case requires VZ EBITDA to stabilise and grow post-capex peak; the bear case is that FTTH overbuild in NL continues to erode VZ EBITDA faster than capex normalises, the Ziggo Group spin-off arrives into a structurally weaker earnings base, and leverage at 4.5-5.0x becomes distressed in a scenario where EBITDA declines persist to 2028 [#J]
  • ARPU deceleration not bottomed: VZ ARPU +1.0% in Q4-25 from +4.2% in Q1-24; Telenet ARPU negative Q4-25; neither market shows evidence of ARPU flooring — further deterioration plausible as FTTH alternatives offer aggressive introductory pricing [#J]
  • Leverage trap: VZ ND/EBITDA 4.5-5.0x; if EBITDA falls another 10% in FY-26 (per mid-to-high SD guidance), VZ ND/EBITDA approaches 5.0-5.5x — refinancing risk and covenant headroom become primary investor concerns, not EBITDA growth [#J]
  • Ziggo consolidation timing risk: VZ 100% consolidation (July-26 close) will double-down on a market currently losing broadband subscribers and ARPU; Liberty Global is deepening its commitment to a structurally challenged NL cable asset at the moment competitive pressure is peaking [#J]
  • Football rights base effect at Telenet: The Jupiler Pro League rights loss (2023) created the EBITDA step-down that Telenet is now cycling through; FY-26 guidance of low SD EBITDA growth is valid as a base effect — but it does not represent genuine operational improvement [#A]
  • VMO2 2026 -3 to -5% EBITDA guidance: This is the first meaningful EBITDA decline year for VMO2 after the Daisy-boosted 2025; underlying consumer fixed revenue is -1.1%; 5G/fibre capex continues at £2bn+ pace; VMO2's 2026 trajectory mirrors the VZ 2024-25 trajectory — a pattern that at VZ then deteriorated further [#J]
  • HoldCo cash burn rate: $2.2bn corporate cash at EOY-25; adj FCF -$319m in Q1-26 annualises to ~-$1.3bn; even assuming improvement in H2-26, HoldCo cash runway is ~18-24 months at current burn before buybacks must stop [#J]
  • Q1-26 inflection — one quarter is not a trend: Broadband net adds improving at Telenet and VZ is genuinely positive; but single-quarter broadband adds improvements have appeared before (Q4-24 at Telenet) and faded; requires 3-4 quarters of sustained evidence to be investable as an inflection thesis [#J]
  • Reporting complexity is a warning signal: A business that requires 4+ adjusted growth metrics, equity-accounted JVs, asset recycling narratives, and constant perimeter rebasing to present an investable case is structurally opaque; opacity correlates with downside surprise risk [#J]
  • Red Queen risk (Wisdom Library: Silver): LBTYK's HFC networks require continuous capex just to maintain addressable market position vs FTTH overbuilders; the network investment is not generating subscriber growth, merely slowing subscriber loss; this is the Red Queen dynamic — running to stand still [#J]
  • Absolute bear scenario: VZ EBITDA declines to €1.4-1.5bn by FY-27 (vs €1.7bn FY-25); VZ ND/EBITDA rises to 5.5-6.0x; Ziggo Group spin-off is delayed or restructured; HoldCo cash consumed by buybacks and FCF burn; LBTYK forced to reduce or halt buyback programme and issue equity to stabilise balance sheet [#J]

Confidence in thesis: MEDIUM — earnings trajectory is demonstrably negative across JVs but Q1-26 broadband inflection and Telenet base-effect recovery introduce genuine uncertainty about the slope of decline
