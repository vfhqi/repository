Mixed quality signal; cash conversion is respectable but adjusted EBITDA exclusions have become a pattern rather than genuinely episodic.

  • One-off impact — FY-24: Reported EBIT of NOK 17.8bn vs adjusted EBIT of approximately NOK 19.8bn; gap of approximately NOK 2.0bn; restructuring declining but impairments persisted (-NOK 820m Q4-24) [#A]

  • Recurring vs one-off: The Extruded Solutions restructuring costs were first flagged as "temporary" in H2-2022; they have appeared every subsequent year; this is the primary quality concern [#A]

  • NWC tailwind: Working capital was a tailwind in 2023 (inventory destocking released approximately NOK 3-4bn cash as aluminium prices fell and physical volumes slowed); reversed partially in 2024 as volumes recovered [#A]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonal patterns are pronounced and consistent across all three L3Y years; Q1 structurally weakest, Q3 structurally strongest.

  • Seasonality verdict: Pronounced and stable; Q1 weakest (power costs, production efficiency), Q3 strongest (realisations + efficient production), with Q4 weather-sensitive; pattern consistent across FY-23, FY-24, and FY-25 [#J]
  • Q1 pattern: Consistently lowest EBITDA margin of each year (11-12% adjusted); Nordic winter power consumption peaks, production efficiency lower, aluminium realisations often lag spot LME due to quarterly pricing lags [#A]
  • Q2 pattern: Recovery quarter — realisations improve, power costs moderate, European construction demand picks up seasonally; margins typically step up 200-300bps vs Q1 [#A]
  • Q3 pattern: Strongest quarter each year in L3Y; Q3-23 (14.7%), Q3-24 (15.9%), Q3-25 (16.4%) each the highest margin of respective years; favourable power positioning, strong LME realisation timing [#A]
  • Q4 pattern: Moderately weak; European demand softness, year-end inventory management at customers, power costs beginning to rise; typically second-weakest quarter [#A]
  • Seasonality direction: Pattern has been remarkably stable; Q3 > Q2 > Q4 > Q1 holds in each of FY-23, FY-24, and FY-25 YTD — ⚡ near-perfect seasonal ordering across all three years [#A]
  • Cause — production: Aluminium smelting and hydropower generation are correlated with Norwegian hydrological cycles; summer/autumn favourable, winter adverse [#A]
  • Cause — buying pattern: European construction and automotive sectors show Q2-Q3 buy-in patterns that feed downstream into Hydro's extrusions volumes [#A]
  • Seasonality changing: Q3 margin peak has been rising each year (14.7% → 15.9% → 16.4%) — reflects the cyclical recovery more than seasonal intensification; the pattern is not changing structurally [#A]
  • Sceptical read — seasonality: The stable seasonal pattern could mask a weakening underlying business; if Extruded Solutions continues to underperform, the Q3 peak might appear structurally strong while Q1/Q4 troughs deepen — seasonality can obscure a widening intra-year dispersion that signals deteriorating base business [#J]
