EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Structural Organization and Holding Discount

Compagnie de l'Odet owns 71.60% of Bolloré SE through a cascading cross-shareholding loop involving Sofibol, Financière V, and Omnium Bolloré, with Omnium owned 50.04% by Bolloré Participations SE . Unlisted stakes are valued using a Protective Put Chaffe model imposing a 7.22% liquidity discount, ensuring minority shareholders have virtually zero influence on core capital allocation and locking up billions in non-voting capital layers . The group trades at a persistent conglomerate discount exceeding typical European investment

peers, with comparable EXOR trading at a 54.50% discount to spot NAV of €29.50 billion . Analysts view recent intermediate holding simplifications as a precursor to eventual de-listing of Bolloré SE, potentially funded by exceptional asset sale proceeds of €10 billion to buy out Odet minorities .

Governance and Foundation Quality

Compagnie de l'Odet is backed by a highly stable, multigenerational family shareholding base, with the Board voting to keep Chairman and CEO roles combined under Vincent Bolloré who was officially reappointed in June 2025 . Bolloré SE and Financière de l'Odet entered a judicial public interest agreement in 2021 with Financière paying a €12 million public interest fine and Bolloré SE submitting compliance programs costing up to €4 million, though the settlement equates neither to admission of guilt nor conviction . The Paris Court of Appeal ruled in April 2025 that Vincent Bolloré and Bolloré SE controlled Vivendi prompting a mandatory AMF buyout order, but the French Supreme Court overturned the ruling in November 2025 declaring the AMF instruction null and void, with remand hearing scheduled for May 22, 2026 . Expert signals highlight a persistent lack of group-wide operational synergies despite consolidated ownership, with former executives noting talented employees experienced severe burnout under Vivendi's central management .

Long-Term Disruption and Structural Evolution

UMG's landmark agreement with Udio launches a licensed AI music platform in 2026, with new streaming contracts explicitly excluding unlicensed AI content from the royalty pool . Havas plans to invest €400 million by 2028 to scale its Converged.AI operating system, rolling out the AVA portal to deliver secure, centralized access to prominent LLMs . Bolloré Energy has marketed B100 biodiesel under the brand Koolza100 to commercial carriers since 2021, with 100% renewable synthetic fuel Izipure reducing carbon emissions by up to 90% and Calorza bio-fuel oil incorporating 30% French-grown vegetable oil . In 2025, Bolloré SE completed buyout offers for Compagnie du Cambodge and Financière Moncey, with mandatory squeeze-outs targeting Artois to eliminate minority stakes within the unlisted loop .
