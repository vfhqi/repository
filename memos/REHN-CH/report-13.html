§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Overall signal: REHN-CH management is a qualified sandbagger — consistent directional conservatism but the conservatism is largely structural (regulatory/weather-driven) rather than deliberate management caution [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: Directional language ("stable", "low single-digit growth", "normalisation") consistently sets a floor below actual delivery in benign years; this is partly by design given the regulated-utility mandate to avoid equity-analyst overhype [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — absent: No documented instance of management setting a numeric target subsequently missed by >10% outside the FY22 exogenous shock; over-promising pattern not established in L4 periods [#A]

  • Beat consistency: 3 of 4 full-year periods produced EBIT delivery above the directional midpoint; beat consistency at 75% is above the Swiss utility sector median of approximately 60% based on BKW/Alpiq/Romande peer set [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Beat magnitudes cluster in the +3–8% EBIT range; this is a "soft sandbag" pattern rather than aggressive under-guidance followed by large beats [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Single material miss (FY22 EBIT -10%) was driven by energy procurement costs, a macro event; management's guidance in that year was defensible given available information at guidance date [#A]

  • Pattern shift — none observed: No identifiable structural shift in guidance posture across L4 periods; management language and conservatism level have been consistent [#I]

  • ⚡ RARE — guidance language stability: Management has used near-identical directional language ("stable to improving profitability") across all 4 annual results cycles without numerical upgrade despite consistent beats — this level of linguistic conservatism is unusual in a rising-earnings environment and may signal deliberate sandbagging discipline rather than uncertainty [Cross-ref: §2 + §7] [#J]

  • Honest tracker — partial: On CapEx, management is an honest tracker; CapEx guidance has been reliable within ±5% in every L4 period, suggesting operational delivery credibility is stronger than earnings delivery credibility [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: If the soft-sandbag pattern is primarily weather-contingent, it will invert in a bad hydro year (Switzerland experienced drought stress in 2022 and intermittently in 2023); management's apparent conservatism may be cyclically-comfortable rather than structurally disciplined [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Overall signal: The guidance assumption stack is dominated by two variables management cannot fully control — hydrology/weather and Swiss wholesale electricity pricing — making the assumption base moderately fragile in adverse scenarios [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Guidance assumes mid-single-digit revenue growth driven primarily by tariff indexation (~60% of revenue regulated/quasi-regulated); this is the most stable assumption [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Spot electricity price assumption embedded in guidance is implicitly CHF 90–110/MWh for FY25; consensus forward curve is ~CHF 95–105/MWh — broadly aligned [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Customer base growth assumed flat-to-low (+0.5–1% volume); Switzerland has limited population growth dynamics; this assumption is conservative and unlikely to miss downward [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Network cost pass-through (Swissgrid fee component) is the primary margin swing variable; management assumes Swissgrid costs remain stable; a ≥10% Swissgrid fee increase would reduce EBIT margin by approximately 50–80bps [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage from renewable additions is modest; each 10 MW of new solar/hydro capacity adds approximately CHF 0.8–1.2m EBIT at current Swiss electricity prices — gradual not step-change [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Regulated tariff component (~60% of revenue) provides pricing floor but limits upside; management guidance does not embed any above-formula tariff uplift [#I]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided implicitly stable at 16–18% (Swiss cantonal norm); this assumption is low-risk given stable Swiss corporate tax environment post-OECD Pillar 2 phase-in [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count effectively stable (no buyback programme, no material dilution); EPS and net profit move in tandem [#I]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: No significant commodity exposure beyond electricity price; management assumes no material deviation from current forward curve [#A]

  • Macro assumption — hydrology: The single most important macro assumption — "normal" hydrology (approximately 100% of long-run average precipitation/snowmelt); a 20% below-average hydro year reduces run-of-river generation by approximately 15–20%, reducing EBIT by approximately CHF 4–7m (6–10%) [#A]
