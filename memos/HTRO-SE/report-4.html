Revenue Growth Trajectory and Margin Evolution
Absolute Revenue and Organic Growth Trends

Hexatronic experienced a dramatic boom-and-bust cycle in its top-line trajectory between FY2022 and FY2024, followed by early signs of stabilization in the current trading period. In FY2022, the company achieved record sales of SEK 6,574 million, representing an 88% year-over-year increase, with organic growth driving 53% of this expansion. This hyper-growth phase continued into early FY2023, propelling full-year 2023 net sales to SEK 8,150 million, a 24% increase compared to the prior year. However, the market environment shifted abruptly in the second half of 2023, leading to a challenging FY2024 where total net sales declined by 7% to SEK 7,581 million, corresponding to a 13% organic contraction.

The downward organic trajectory began to moderate in late FY2025, culminating in a full-year 2025 net sales figure of SEK 7,519 million, a minor 1% decline from FY2024. Geographical Performance

Hexatronic has intentionally diversified its geographic footprint, reducing its reliance on its native Swedish market. In FY2024, Europe (excluding Sweden) represented 45% of total sales, North America accounted for 38%, the APAC region and Rest of World contributed 9%, and Sweden made up just 8%.

The North American market has historically been a critical growth engine, particularly driven by US duct and FTTH system sales. While North American sales faced a temporary setback in late 2024 and 2025 due to inventory destocking and a slowdown in Canadian FTTH build-outs, the US market specifically returned to positive organic growth in Q1 2026.

Conversely, the European market has remained structurally challenged due to higher interest rates, customer overstocking, and a general transition from "homes passed" to connecting new subscribers. In Q1 2026, European sales declined by 31% organically, further exacerbated by an unusually cold winter that delayed deployment activities. Meanwhile, the APAC region has demonstrated robust momentum, delivering 28% year-over-year growth in Q1 2026 driven by strong project activity across primary markets like Australia.

Business Area Shift and Product Categories

To better align with its diversification strategy, Hexatronic transitioned its reporting structure into three distinct business areas: Fiber Solutions, Harsh Environment, and Data Center.

Fiber Solutions, encompassing traditional FTTH systems, microducts, and submarine cables, remains the largest segment, generating SEK 5,441 million or roughly 72% of total group sales in FY2024. However, this segment has borne the brunt of recent market headwinds, suffering an 11% organic decline in Q1 2026 due to persistent weakness in European FTTH deployments and intense pricing pressure.

To offset the volatility of the FTTH cycle, management has aggressively expanded into Data Center and Harsh Environment applications. The Data Center segment has rapidly scaled through strategic acquisitions, generating SEK 972 million in FY2024. Benefiting from massive global investments in AI, cloud computing, and hyperscale infrastructure, Data Center delivered an exceptional 20% organic growth rate in Q1 2026, achieving SEK 434 million in quarterly sales.

The Harsh Environment segment, specializing in dynamic subsea cables and connectivity for defense and offshore energy, contributed SEK 1,178 million in FY2024. This segment sustained solid momentum with 9% organic growth in Q1 2026, reaching SEK 283 million in sales, despite minor timing delays related to US government defense orders. Together, Data Center and Harsh Environment now account for a structurally larger portion of the group's revenue profile.

Margin Evolution and Key Drivers

Hexatronic's profitability profile has experienced significant compression since the peak of the fiber boom, though recent restructuring efforts have initiated a margin recovery. The group's EBITA margin hit a cycle high of 16.6% in FY2022, comfortably exceeding management's targets due to exceptional factory utilization and a highly favorable product mix. Margins remained robust at 15.1% in FY2023 before contracting sharply to 10.6% in FY2024. This margin compression was primarily driven by severe price pressure in the Fiber Solutions segment, reduced overhead coverage from lower factory utilization, and unexpected carve-out challenges following the Rochester Cable acquisition.

In response to the deteriorating FTTH environment, Hexatronic launched a comprehensive performance improvement program in late 2024 and 2025, aimed at rightsizing its European and North American manufacturing footprints. This restructuring program was finalized in Q1 2026, successfully delivering SEK 110 million in annualized cost savings slightly ahead of schedule.

As a direct result of these cost interventions and a favorable mix shift, the group's adjusted EBITA margin improved sequentially to 8.6% in Q1 2026, marking the first sequential margin expansion after five consecutive quarters of decline. The strategic pivot has fundamentally altered the group's profit composition; Data Center and Harsh Environment now collectively generate over 60% of the group's adjusted EBITA. The Data Center segment has become the company's most profitable division, achieving a 16.8% adjusted EBITA margin in Q1 2026 and serving as the largest absolute profit contributor.

Conversely, the Harsh Environment margin was temporarily muted at 8.5% in Q1 2026 due to an unfavorable product mix and delayed high-margin defense orders. Looking forward, management is actively managing new cost pressures stemming from rising raw material inputs, particularly resin and fiber. While these input cost increases did not materially impact Q1 2026, the company is implementing price hikes to fully offset them, though a temporary lag may pressure margins slightly in Q3 2026.

Redeye analysts note that Hexatronic's sequential margin improvement in Q1 2026 is highly encouraging given that the first quarter is historically the weakest due to seasonal weather patterns.

Redeye projects that the sustained recovery in the higher-margin US duct market, combined with the full realization of the cost savings program, should facilitate further margin expansion throughout 2026.

Redeye views the strategic expansion of the Data Center segment into new verticals, such as enterprise campuses, as a logical step to diversify away from pure hyperscaler reliance, even if it requires short-term margin investments.

Earnings Quality, Cash Conversion, and Capital Efficiency
Earnings Per Share Trajectory and Quality of Earnings

Hexatronic's reported earnings per share (EPS) have exhibited significant volatility over the past three fiscal years, reflecting the boom-and-bust nature of the telecommunications infrastructure cycle. During the peak of the market expansion, EPS after dilution surged to SEK 4.17 in FY2023. As end-market demand softened and inventory destocking took hold, FY2024 EPS after dilution contracted sharply to SEK 1.69. The earnings trough materialized in FY2025, where the group reported a full-year net loss and an EPS after dilution of SEK -0.07. The company successfully returned to positive territory in the most recent trading period, delivering an EPS after dilution of SEK 0.45 in Q1 2026.

Despite the recent dip in reported EPS, the underlying quality of Hexatronic's earnings remains highly resilient. The statutory net income in late 2024 and 2025 was heavily suppressed by substantial non-cash items and aggressive restructuring charges. For example, the comprehensive performance improvement program initiated in late 2025 incurred total costs of approximately SEK 230 million. A significant portion of these charges consisted of non-cash write-downs of intangible assets and inventory, obscuring the true cash-generating power of the underlying business.

Redeye analysts assign Hexatronic a high rating for efficient inventory and accounts receivable management, noting that both ratios sit comfortably below 20% and are outperforming their respective five-year historical averages. Furthermore, Redeye highlights the company's strong gross profit margins exceeding 40%, which indicates structural pricing power and supports high-quality, sustainable profit generation over the long term.

Working Capital Management and NWC Trends

Hexatronic has demonstrated a highly disciplined approach to working capital management, particularly during the recent revenue downturn. The group actively targets a net working capital (NWC) to sales ratio between 12% and 14%. Throughout 2024 and 2025, management aggressively optimized the balance sheet by running down elevated inventory buffers and streamlining accounts receivable collections.

In Q4 2025, strict working capital management yielded a massive positive cash flow effect of SEK 200 million. This included a SEK 144 million release from inventory reduction initiatives and SEK 94 million driven by highly efficient accounts receivable collection, which was only partially offset by a decrease in accounts payable. By Q1 2026, working capital dynamics shifted to a negative effect of SEK 83 million. This temporary reversal was primarily a function of a very strong end to the quarter, which naturally inflated accounts receivable balances heading into the subsequent reporting period.

Free Cash Flow Generation and Cash Conversion

Hexatronic's ability to consistently convert operating profits into free cash flow represents a fundamental strength of its business model. Cash flow from operating activities remained exceptionally robust throughout the cycle, amounting to SEK 670 million in FY2022, SEK 944 million in FY2023, and SEK 921 million in FY2024.

The group's cash conversion metrics have been highly elevated recently due to the aggressive release of working capital. In the fourth quarter of 2024, Hexatronic delivered a cash conversion ratio of 150%. This metric surged even higher in Q4 2025, reaching an extraordinary 235% as the company generated SEK 349 million in operating cash flow during a single quarter. In Q1 2026, operating cash flow moderated to SEK 29 million, reflecting a normalized cash conversion rate of 26%. Management noted that this lower first-quarter conversion is entirely consistent with the group's standard seasonal working capital patterns.

Capital Expenditures and Profitability Impact

The group's free cash flow generation is further supported by a structurally lower capital expenditure burden following a period of intensive capacity expansion. In FY2022 and FY2023, Hexatronic executed heavy investment programs, culminating in the completion of a major new duct manufacturing facility in Ogden, Utah. With these foundational investments complete, management explicitly stated that the company can absorb several years of future growth without requiring extensive new capacity additions in the Fiber Solutions segment.

Looking forward, normalized capital expenditures are guided to represent approximately 3% to 4% of total net sales annually. Within this envelope, pure maintenance CapEx is expected to account for a mere 1% to 2% of sales. The immediate realization of this asset-light transition was evident in Q1 2026, where total CapEx amounted to just SEK 22 million, equivalent to an immaterial 1.3% of net sales.

Leverage Profile and Financial Health

Hexatronic maintains a solid balance sheet, actively managing its leverage to ensure sufficient financial flexibility for its mergers and acquisitions strategy. The group is governed by bank covenants tied to an adjusted leverage ratio, defined as net debt (excluding IFRS 16 lease liabilities) relative to rolling 12-month adjusted EBITDA.

At the conclusion of FY2024, the adjusted leverage ratio stood comfortably at 1.9x. Despite facing severe earnings pressure throughout FY2025, the exceptional cash conversion allowed the company to hold the adjusted leverage ratio steady at 1.9x by year-end 2025. As of Q1 2026, absolute net debt amounted to SEK 1.67 billion. The adjusted leverage ratio drifted upward sequentially to 2.2x, driven primarily by negative foreign exchange translation effects and a mechanically weaker trailing 12-month EBITDA denominator.

Management anticipates a minor, temporary increase in leverage during Q2 2026 of approximately 0.2x due to scheduled earn-out payments related to historical acquisitions. Following this disbursement, the company expects robust operational cash flows to drive organic deleveraging throughout the remainder of the year. Liquidity remains exceptionally strong, with the group holding SEK 603 million in cash equivalents and SEK 1.1 billion in unutilized backup facilities at the end of Q1 2026, providing total available liquidity of over SEK 1.7 billion.

Order Intake, Recent Results, and Market Expectations
Order Intake, Backlog, and Revenue Visibility

Hexatronic's order book stood at approximately 2.5 months of net sales at the end of the first quarter of 2026. Management views this 2.5-month level as a normalized order backlog for the business, following the artificially elevated levels seen during the pandemic supply chain disruptions. The group's overall order intake remained sequentially stable, matching the levels seen at the close of the prior quarter.

Within the Fiber Solutions segment, visibility is highly bifurcated between traditional telecom products and specialized infrastructure. The submarine cable division currently enjoys exceptional forward revenue visibility, with the order book largely full for the remainder of 2026. Management has noted that the submarine order pipeline is already filling up rapidly for 2027, making production capacity the primary bottleneck rather than underlying demand. Shipments for these submarine projects are expected to be lumpy, with major deliveries scheduled to peak during the third quarter of 2026. Conversely, visibility in the European Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) market remains challenged by soft demand and a slow deployment pace.

Recent Earnings Results: Q3 2025 to Q1 2026

Over the last three quarters, Hexatronic's financial results have demonstrated a clear divergence between its struggling legacy fiber business and its booming diversification segments. In the third quarter of 2025, the group reported net sales of SEK 1.88 billion, representing an organic growth rate of 2%. This performance was heavily supported by the Data Center segment, which surged by 39% organically. The positive momentum accelerated in the fourth quarter of 2025, with total net sales reaching SEK 1.85 billion and organic growth climbing to 10%. During this period, the Data Center segment achieved an extraordinary 62% organic growth rate, while the core Fiber Solutions business continued to contract.

In the most recent trading period, Q1 2026, group net sales amounted to SEK 1.70 billion. This represented a 10% year-over-year decline, heavily influenced by negative foreign exchange translation, while underlying organic growth contracted by a modest 2%. Despite the top-line contraction, the company delivered an adjusted EBITA of SEK 146 million, achieving an 8.6% margin. Management highlighted that this margin stabilization was driven by the successful early completion of the Fiber Solutions restructuring program, which delivered necessary cost savings ahead of schedule.

Management commentary paints a mixed but gradually improving picture for the near-term trading environment. The European FTTH market remains structurally challenged, with customers focused on connecting existing subscribers rather than passing new homes. However, executives confirmed that the vital North American market has begun validating the anticipated recovery, with the US specifically returning to positive organic growth in early 2026. For the Data Center business, the company expects robust market strength to persist, fueled by global hyperscale and cloud infrastructure investments. Harsh Environment is guided to maintain solid activity levels across the defense and energy sectors, though management warned of a temporary margin suppression in Q2 2026 before normalizing in the third quarter.

Investment Thesis and Overall Earnings Trend Rating
Transitioning to a Niche Fiber Powerhouse

Hexatronic's current earnings trajectory broadly supports an evolving investment thesis centered on transitioning from a pure Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH) play into a diversified niche fiber optics powerhouse. The core investment case hinges on a strategic pivot toward higher-growth, higher-margin segments, primarily driven by targeted acquisitions. Management has set an ambitious target for the Data Center and Harsh Environment segments to collectively constitute 50% of total group sales by 2028. Following a period of aggressive restructuring, the legacy Fiber Solutions segment is expected to stabilize and generate the solid cash flows required to fund this ongoing strategic pivot.

Major Tailwinds and Headwinds

The Data Center segment serves as the primary tailwind validating the new investment thesis, having already demonstrated its ability to fuel group-wide growth. Built through a series of successful acquisitions, this division benefits significantly from strong underlying market fundamentals related to hyperscale cloud and AI infrastructure build-outs. The Harsh Environment segment shares a similar structural growth narrative, benefiting from elevated demand in the defense and offshore energy sectors, albeit on a smaller absolute scale.

Conversely, the European FTTH market remains the company's most significant structural headwind. Demand in core European geographies is depressed by macroeconomic pressures, a shift away from new "homes passed" deployments, and lingering industry overcapacity. However, management's willingness to implement severe structural changes within Fiber Solutions demonstrates a disciplined approach to prioritizing the segments with the strongest forward prospects.

Key Challenges and Risks

Despite the promising strategic shift, several challenges could undermine the long-term thesis. While the Data Center market is currently experiencing exceptional structural growth, it is heavily reliant on capital expenditure cycles and may eventually exhibit the same "boom-and-bust" characteristics that recently severely impacted the FTTH market. Furthermore, Hexatronic's ability to achieve its 2028 revenue and margin targets is highly dependent on the successful execution of its M&A strategy. If the legacy Fiber Solutions division fails to generate sufficient free cash flow, the group may struggle to fund the acquisitions necessary to complete its diversification pivot.
