SECTION 3: REVENUE GROWTH

SATS Revenue Profile

Revenue growth decomposition: Approximately 3% from member count growth (22,000 net adds to 755,000) and approximately 5–6% from ARPM growth. Q4-25 ARPM reached NOK 630, up 5% blended. Country-level: Norway NOK 607 (+3%), Sweden NOK 651 (+8%), Denmark +9% on total revenue, Finland +9-10% estimated.

The pattern is positive for earnings quality: revenue growth is biased toward pricing/ARPM rather than pure volume. Pricing-driven growth is more sustainable and carries higher incremental margins than volume-driven growth (new clubs, new members at marginal acquisition cost).

CMD guidance: The mid-term ARPM trajectory is not explicitly quantified, but management's NOK 1.1bn EBITDA target implies sustained revenue growth of 8–10% p.a. with operating leverage.

Rating: GREEN — strong ARPM trajectory, pricing power demonstrated, revenue growth quality high.

Comparative Revenue Analysis

GYM FY2025: GBP 244.9m, up 8%. Average membership +4% to 945,000, ARPMM +4% to GBP 21.60. Balanced revenue growth with roughly equal contribution from volume and price. The ARPMM at GBP 21.60 represents significant pricing improvement over the historical sub-GBP 20 range.

Rating for peer comparison: SATS pricing power is highest. BFIT volume growth is highest. GYM balance is respectable. In a market environment that values quality earnings over volume expansion, SATS's revenue profile is most favourable.

SECTION 4: MARGIN PROFILE

SATS EBITDA Margin

FY2025 EBITDA (before IFRS 16): NOK 871m on NOK 5,509m revenue = 15.8% EBITDA margin. Up from approximately 14.5% in FY2024 (estimated). Q4-25 EBITDA before IFRS 16: NOK 224m on NOK 1,428m = 15.7%.

The operating leverage dynamic is clear. Revenue grew 9%, EBITDA grew 18%, EBIT grew 24–34% (varying by quarter). Fixed costs (club leases, staff) are largely unresponsive to revenue changes above inflation. As revenue grows above the cost inflation rate, incremental revenue drops disproportionately to the EBITDA line.

The CMD NOK 1.1bn EBITDA target on a revenue base of approximately NOK 6,200–6,500m (assuming 9% revenue CAGR to the CMD target year) implies a margin of approximately 17–18%. This is achievable given the demonstrated operating leverage, but requires the cost base to grow more slowly than revenue. Energy, staffing, and lease costs are the primary watch points.

Rating: GREEN — EBITDA margin expanding, operating leverage demonstrated, CMD target implies achievable further margin expansion.

SECTION 8: SECTOR CONTEXT

The Scandinavian gym market is structurally distinct from the UK or continental European market. Norway's 30%+ gym penetration rate is the highest in Europe — the market is already mature in terms of population participation. This means SATS's growth must come from ARPM compounding and share gain, not from a greenfield market expansion. The upside: Norway's gym habit is deeply embedded, making churn structurally lower than in less health-conscious markets. Members who train at NOK 500–700/month are habitual exercisers, not casual joiners.

Norway's macro backdrop provides an additional buffer. Oil revenues (NOK 656bn in 2025 government net cash flow), the sovereign wealth fund (NOK 21,300bn), and above-trend consumer spending growth (2.5% p.a. through 2028) insulate the Norwegian consumer from the austerity visible in the UK and southern Europe. This is SATS's most underappreciated structural advantage.

Post-COVID normalisation: All three operators benefited from pent-up demand returning to gyms in 2022–2023. By 2025, this normalisation effect is largely complete. What remains is structural demand, not cyclical recovery. SATS's visit growth of +4–6% per annum on top of member growth confirms the thesis.

Rating: GREEN — best sector context of the trio, structurally advantaged macro position.

SECTION 9: QUALITY OF EARNINGS

Cash conversion: FCF NOK 506m on EBITDA NOK 871m = 58% cash conversion. This is consistent with the CMD high cash conversion target of 55%. The gap is explained primarily by maintenance capex and working capital movements.

NWC: Gym operators typically carry negative working capital (members pay in advance, club costs are paid with a lag). SATS's NWC dynamics should be structurally cash-generative in an environment of rising membership and ARPM.

IFRS 16 impact: IFRS 16 capitalises lease liabilities onto the balance sheet, creating right-of-use assets and corresponding lease liabilities. Reported EBITDA (including IFRS 16) is materially higher than EBITDA before IFRS 16. For SATS, the company explicitly manages the business on a before-IFRS-16 basis: the NOK 1.1bn target is before IFRS 16, leverage is measured before IFRS 16, and covenant compliance is measured before IFRS 16. This is the right approach for a gym operator where leases are the primary fixed cost.

Net debt: NOK 967m (before IFRS 16 lease liabilities). Leverage 1.1x — conservative. The undrawn revolving credit facility of NOK 891m provides significant liquidity headroom.

Rating: GREEN — high FCF conversion, conservative leverage, strong liquidity, transparent IFRS 16 treatment.

SECTION 10: SCEPTICAL SUMMARY

The bear case for SATS's earnings trajectory has three main elements.

First, CMD credibility requires sustained execution. NOK 1.1bn from NOK 871m is a 26% uplift. Each year of delay is a year the investment case remains a "promise vs delivery" story rather than a "delivery confirmed" story. If FY2026 EBITDA comes in below NOK 950m (less than 9% growth), investors will begin discounting the CMD target.

Second, Sweden's recovery has been impressive, but the Swedish economy is more exposed to European cyclicality than Norway. A Swedish consumer downturn (possible given higher Swedish interest rates and more limited fiscal buffer than Norway) would compress Sweden EBITDA — approximately 20% of group.

Third, Treningshelse is a medium-term competitive threat in Norway. If aggressive pricing by the challenger forces SATS to reduce price increases or invest more heavily in Norwegian club quality to defend members, the ARPM growth trajectory and capex discipline could both come under pressure simultaneously.

Currency: SATS reports in NOK. For international investors, NOK/EUR or NOK/GBP movements are a secondary risk. NOK has tended to correlate with oil prices — a sharp decline in oil would weaken NOK and reduce the EUR-equivalent value of SATS earnings for European investors.

Overall bear case rating: YELLOW — risks are real but not imminent. The underlying business quality and macro backdrop provide substantial cushion.
