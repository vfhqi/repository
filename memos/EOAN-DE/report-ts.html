Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. E.ON offers highly predictable, network-driven earnings growth backed by Europe's largest regulated asset base, making its mid-term EPS trajectory highly visible despite retail energy transition volatility.
1.
Stable business model: E.ON operates regional electricity and gas distribution networks and retail supply; ~80% of EBITDA from fully regulated or long-term contracted assets
2.
Compelling investment case: E.ON's role as the playmaker of the energy transition drives massive grid capex expanding regulated asset base ~9% annually. This yields attractive compounding returns and dividend growth up to 5% per annum through 2030
3.
Key swing factors: Earnings visibility highly dependent on German regulatory negotiations for RP5, specifically determining allowed rates of capital return (contesting 7-year cost of debt lookback framework)
4.
Strategic UK consolidation: May 2026 – E.ON Next signed agreement to acquire Ovo Energy (~4m residential accounts). Combined 9.6m UK accounts = absolute UK retail market leader (26% market share vs Octopus 24%)
5.
Critical regulatory risk: Squeezes on allowed returns and inadequate opex inflation adjustments under Germany's proposed NEST specifications = single most important risk to long-term thesis
6.
Positive verdict: Strongly warranted closer look – 14.6x 2027 P/E discount to historical norms; safe haven with attractive long-term growth option

Section 5 – The possible investment case 4 findings

Section summary
  1. E.ON's investment case strengthens as visible, double-digit expansion of regulated electricity asset base merges with synergistic retail consolidation, paving way for predictable mid-term EPS compounding.
1.
Secular grid demand: Electrification of European heat, transport, industry requires massive network reinforcement 2026-2030 capex plan: €48bn cumulative (>70% of network spend on growth)
2.
Power RAB CAGR: ~10% in Germany; group RAB ~9% CAGR through 2030 Plausibility: HIGH – E.ON predominantly in rural areas with highest renewable connection demand Failure mode: Supply chain bottlenecks or skilled labour delays

RP5 start: Key catalyst for EPS upgrades via higher allowed equity returns Inflation protection: OPEX allowances adjusted annually with CPI Plausibility: MEDIUM – German regulator signaled need for competitive return conditions Failure mode: Low cost of equity beta enforcement; failure to adjust for high refinancing costs
