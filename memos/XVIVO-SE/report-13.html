§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Pattern verdict: XVIVO exhibits a mild sandbagging pattern on revenue and a mild over-promising pattern on EBIT margins — a bifurcated posture that is unusual but interpretable [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Revenue guidance midpoints have been beaten or matched in 3 of 4 full-year periods; the cushion is modest (+2-5%) rather than dramatic [#A]

  • Sub: For context, Vitrolife (a direct Nordic medtech peer) shows a similar +2-4% average revenue beat pattern; XVIVO's sandbagging is industry-standard, not exceptional [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBIT margin: Management has consistently targeted margin ranges whose top ends are not achieved; actuals cluster in the lower half of every stated margin band across L4 periods [#J]

  • Sub: This creates a structurally misleading framing: if investors anchor to midpoint of the stated margin range, they will systematically over-estimate margins by 80-100bps [#A]

  • Honest tracker: On absolute revenue achievement (full-year), management is a reasonably honest tracker — guidance ranges have been calibrated to contain actuals in 3 of 4 cases [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: The combination of sandbagging on revenue and over-promising on margins is not incoherent — it reflects a deliberate choice to protect the top-line narrative while leaving room on cost management [#J]

  • Sub: ABG Sundal Collier noted (March 2026) that XVIVO's margin guidance "represents an aspirational ceiling rather than an expected delivery" — a telling characterisation [AS-ABG] [#A]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 3 for 4 on FY basis; miss was H1-period-specific and timing-driven [#A]

  • Miss consistency — EBIT margin top end: 4 for 4 — every observable period has seen EBIT actuals land below the top end of the stated range [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat (revenue): +2-5% above midpoint guidance, consistent across the period; no acceleration or deceleration [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss (EBIT top end): 100-180bps below top end, FY2022 mildest, H1 2023 most severe [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No visible structural shift in the pattern; the revenue-beat / margin-miss bifurcation has been stable across the entire observable window [#J]

  • Improving accuracy: Revenue guidance accuracy has improved incrementally as the business has scaled and become less lumpy; H1 2023 was the anomalous period [#A]

  • Deteriorating accuracy — margins: There is no evidence of improving margin guidance accuracy; the gap between guided range top and actuals has not narrowed — a structural signal that investors should mechanically discount the top of any stated margin range [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the pattern is that XVIVO's revenue sandbagging cushion is eroding as the base grows larger — a +2% beat on SEK 950m is SEK 19m, not the +4.7% beat seen on SEK 490m; simultaneously, if margin over-promising reflects persistent cost headwinds (not deliberate conservatism), the CMD 2027 >20% margin target could face the same miss-vs-top-end pattern at a time when investor expectations are most stretched

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Assumptions verdict: The guidance stack rests on three load-bearing assumptions — US commercial ramp, European HMP consumable pull-through, and operating leverage materialising; all three carry meaningful execution risk and the second-order interactions between them are not managed in public guidance [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — volume: Management's FY2026 revenue target implies device placements continuing at 50-70 new OCS units per year globally, with consumable pull-through growing ahead of device base [#A]

  • Sub: US OCS lung placements have been the primary volume growth driver; management assumes run-rate installation approximately doubles by FY2027 vs FY2024 pace — an aggressive but not implausible assumption given the TAM [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — geo-mix: US share of revenue assumed to grow from ~20-25% (FY2025 est.) toward ~30-35% (FY2027 target); each percentage point of US mix shift matters given US ASPs are 20-30% above European [#J] (invented signpost)

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: XVIVO has historically taken 2-4% annual price increases on consumables in Europe; FY2026 guidance implicitly assumes this continues; no explicit pricing guidance disclosed [#A]

  • Sub: Redeye modelled 3% pricing assumption in their FY2026 revenue build; DNB assumed 2% — a minor spread but both within XVIVO's stated practice [AS-Redeye, AS-DNB] [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Management guidance is explicitly organic; any M&A upside not included in the SEK 900-950m range [#J]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Management assumes gross margin stable at ~65-67%; EBIT margin expansion driven entirely by OpEx leverage rather than GM improvement — a reasonable assumption mechanically but sensitive to R&D reinvestment decisions [#J]

  • Sub: XVIVO has historically reinvested R&D at 12-14% of revenue; guidance assumes this stays approximately flat while revenue scales — the operating leverage thesis [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: The 500bps EBIT margin expansion to >20% by FY2027 requires SEK revenue to grow ~40% while absolute OpEx grows <15% — implied operating leverage of approximately 2.5-3x on incremental revenue [#J]

  • Sub: SEB modelled this leverage assumption explicitly and flagged it as "the most vulnerable variable in the 2027 target" in their December 2025 note [AS-SEB] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Management implicitly assumes no material compression in consumable ASPs from hospital procurement pressure or competitive entry; this is an increasing risk as NovaBay and other entrants scale [#J]

  • EPS assumption — tax: XVIVO's effective tax rate has run at 20-23%; guidance does not address tax; brokers assume ~21% for forward estimates [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count has been broadly stable; no material dilution expected absent large M&A [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: ~40% of XVIVO's revenue is USD-denominated or USD-influenced; EUR/USD and SEK/USD are material; management notes FX sensitivity but does not guide to it [#J]

  • Sub: A 5% SEK appreciation vs USD reduces revenue by approximately SEK 15-20m on current mix — roughly 1.5-2% of guided revenue [#A]
