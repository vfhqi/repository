EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 8 findings

Operational Structure and Strategic Position

1.
DSV operates a non-asset-based divisional structure across Air & Sea (62% of revenue), Road, and Solutions divisions, maximizing capital flexibility while shielding the balance sheet from capital-intensive physical assets. The combined group employs approximately 160,000 people globally following the transformative DB Schenker acquisition completed in May 2025 for €14.3 billion , establishing DSV as the world's largest logistics provider with a 6-7% global market share . DSV's standardization on single divisional IT systems–migrating 85% of air and sea volumes to the proprietary TANGO platform while rolling out the STAR transport management system groupwide–eliminates operating complexity and provides a robust foundation for AI deployment. The asset-light model controls cargo but outsources physical transportation to third-party providers, preserving high cash flow conversion and enabling rapid capacity adjustments during negative volume cycles. Revenue generation relies primarily on freight forwarding fees, warehousing contracts, and value-added logistics services , with pricing power derived from bundling complex forwarding with customs clearance and consolidation services across a globally dense network spanning more than 90 countries

Financial Profile and Peer Benchmarking

2.
DSV delivered group revenues of DKK 247,331 million in fiscal year 2025 with an adjusted EBIT margin of 6.10% and EBITDA margin of 9.60% , positioning the company as a market-leading performer on margins and returns compared to asset-heavy peer groups. Peer comparison reveals DSV's conversion ratio stands approximately 20 percentage points above closest peer Kuehne + Nagel, while the Swiss rival delivered only a 4.5% adjusted EBIT margin. The company maintains structurally superior capital efficiency through its asset-light strategy, converting 65-70% of EBIT into free cash flow compared to asset-heavy competitors, although net interestbearing debt ended 2025 at DKK 86,600 million with adjusted gearing at 2.8x net debt to EBITDA . Post-tax return on capital employed of 7.5% in 2025 trails peer Kuehne + Nagel's 12.8% but is projected to reach 16.1% by 2028 as Schenker synergies materialize. Management suspended share buyback programs in 2025 to prioritize post-acquisition balance sheet deleveraging, maintaining a strict target leverage ratio of less than 2.0x net debt to EBITDA while distributing a modest DKK 7.00 per share dividend . Working capital intensity remains exceptionally lean at 2.70% of sales in 2025 , materially outperforming Kuehne + Nagel's 5.2% intensity driven by freight rate increases

Industry Structure and Value Chain Economics

3.
The global freight forwarding market represents a USD 284.80 billion opportunity by 2030, projected to expand at a 4.80% compound annual growth rate, driven by ongoing transitions from captive first-party logistics to thirdparty providers and growing supply chain complexity. The market remains highly fragmented with over 100,000 forwarders globally, yet the top 20 global players control only 30-40% of total volumes, leaving substantial consolidation runway for scaled technology-enabled operators. Profit pools concentrate within asset-light freight forwarding and value-add contract logistics stages, which capture EBIT margins of 4-8% and 4-10% respectively on low capital intensity of approximately 1%, while physical transport asset owners face heavy capital intensity of 8-15% with lower through-cycle returns. Asset-light forwarding models translate 65-70% of operating profit directly into free cash flow, maintaining stable returns during economic downturns by quickly flexing variable labor costs, while top-tier forwarders leverage scalable digital platforms to target group conversion ratios exceeding 45%. Competitive pricing attitudes remain aggressive during cyclical downturns , with contracted ocean freight rates dropping approximately 40% year-over-year amid persistent excess vessel capacity, yet highly integrated 3PL warehousing contracts successfully pass through 90% of labor and fuel cost inflation via structured indexation clauses

Value Chain Position and Customer Dynamics

DSV maintains a highly consolidated model across forwarding and contract logistics, delivering superior margins and asset efficiency versus peers, with its Air & Sea conversion ratio targeted to reach 55%+ by 2030, significantly outperforming Kuehne + Nagel's 30-40% range. The company's balanced vertical exposure is led by industrial (30% of gross profit) and consumer (25%) segments, cushioning the group against sector-specific cyclical downturns, while growing technology exposure has risen to 15% of gross profit post-Schenker integration. Deep technological integration on CargoWise creates exceptionally high customer switching costs, with 7-10 year trade data retention requirements legally locking shippers into CargoWise databases and requiring expensive parallel systems for migration, minimizing structural attrition risks. Competitive Landscape and Integration Threats

6.
The pro-forma DSV-Schenker merger establishes clear global market leadership, with the acquisition adding 86% to legacy airfreight volumes and 67% to ocean volumes, positioning DSV as the undisputed leader while creating tight three-way competition at the top with DHL and Kuehne + Nagel. DSV has consistently captured volume market share from main competitor Kuehne + Nagel, outpacing the market and Swiss peer with 8% year-over-year ocean and air growth while maintaining its profitability lead via superior conversion margins.
