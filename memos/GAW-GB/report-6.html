Financial Performance & Core Business Growth

Games Workshop Group has demonstrated a step change in profitability and cash generation through improved product innovation, geographic expansion, and a relentless focus on operational costs. The core non-licensing business has consistently delivered a return on capital employed (RoCE) well above 100%, culminating in a 191% RoCE in the 2025 financial year. Management's strategy of expanding its global footprint has been highly successful, with international sales accounting for approximately 79% of core revenue by FY25. The company possesses strong pricing power, implementing low to mid-single-digit price increases on new products that can be expanded further during periods of input inflation.

Triennial Sales Cycles and Intellectual Property

The core business revenue trajectory is heavily influenced by the triennial release cycle of new editions for its two primary intellectual properties, Warhammer 40,000 (40K) and Warhammer Age of Sigmar (AoS). Historically, this clear three-year pattern begins with the launch of a new 40K edition, followed by an AoS edition in the subsequent year, and concluding with a third "off" year before the cycle resets. Because 40K is the larger property by revenue, its launch years typically drive relatively higher rates of core revenue growth, greater absolute gross profit, and stronger operating margins due to volume-driven operational leverage.

Edison Investment Research • 2 Mar

Conversely, the launch years for AoS generally exhibit a lower gross percentage margin compared to 40K release windows, though the expanding scale of the business continues to drive absolute profit higher. The third year of the cycle has historically seen a slower rate of growth and lower gross profit than the preceding two years. However, the continuous release of ancillary products across all properties during the intervals between major edition launches has proven highly effective at keeping hobbyists continuously engaged. This engagement strategy resulted in better-than-expected revenue growth during recent "off" periods, bucking the historical trend of slower growth.

Margin Progression, Operational Gearing, and Inventory Management

The core business has historically demonstrated high operational gearing, with an average of approximately 40% of incremental revenue dropping through to operating profit between FY17 and FY25. Core operating margins have consistently settled in the mid-30s since FY22, peaking at nearly 40% in recent half-year results as tight cost control provided additional leverage against rising sales volumes.

Edison Investment Research • 2 Mar

Gross margin progression is subject to several variable factors, with inventory provisions representing one of the most critical annual swing components. The scale of the inventory provision is directly dependent on the sell-through rates of individual product lines relative to management's expectations. For instance, during the launch of the Age of Sigmar edition in FY22, the core gross margin declined by 560 basis points compared to the prior year, largely driven by an elevated inventory provision and increased post-pandemic freight costs. Conversely, robust sell-through rates on new products have historically resulted in lower provisions and favorable gross margin recoveries in subsequent periods.

Licensing Strategy & Media Expansion
Licensing Financials and Margin Impact

Games Workshop's intellectual property licensing division has evolved into a highly profitable growth engine that significantly impacts the group's overall financial profile. The financial characteristics of this division are exceptional, featuring a 100% gross margin due to the lack of associated manufacturing or physical distribution costs. With limited incremental operating costs required to develop and maintain partner relationships, the licensing segment consistently achieves operating margins in the high 80s or low 90s, reaching 93% in the first half of the 2025 financial year.

Historically, management was cautious about exploiting its IP in outside media to protect the integrity of its universes. Over the past two decades, the greatest commercial success has materialized in the video game sector across PC, console, and mobile platforms.

The Amazon.com Film and Television Agreement

The most significant recent development in Games Workshop's media expansion strategy is its landmark partnership with Amazon. Following an agreement in principle announced in December 2022, management confirmed in December 2024 that a final, exclusive agreement had been reached with Amazon to adapt the Warhammer 40K universe into films and television series. The agreement also includes associated merchandising rights, with an option for Amazon to secure exclusive rights to the Warhammer Fantasy universe (Age of Sigmar and The Old World) following the initial 40K productions.

Edison Investment Research • 2 Mar

The primary strategic objective of the Amazon partnership is to introduce the 40K intellectual property to a mainstream global audience, which management hopes will serve as a recruitment funnel for the core tabletop hobby. The live-action endeavor is currently in development alongside Amazon MGM Studios, Vertigo, and actor Henry Cavill. Because the content production cycle for major film and television series is inherently long, management cautions that it will likely be "several years" before these major projects are released and generate meaningful financial returns.

Warhammer+ and Direct-to-Consumer Digital Media

Beyond third-party licensing, Games Workshop has successfully launched its own proprietary digital media ecosystem to monetize its highly engaged fanbase. In August 2021, the company launched Warhammer+, an online subscription service offering exclusive animated shows, deep-dive gaming and miniature painting tutorials, and access to premium features. Subscribers are also rewarded with free, exclusive physical miniatures, tightly linking the digital media offering back to the core physical hobby.

Edison Investment Research • 2 Mar

The direct-to-consumer service has demonstrated rapid and consistent growth since its inception. Priced at £5.99 per month or £49.99 annually, Warhammer+ expanded its subscriber base from 105k at the end of FY22 to 176k by the end of FY24. This momentum continued through the recent financial year, with total active subscribers climbing to 232k by the close of FY25. Peer Comparables and Premium Justification

Because Games Workshop lacks direct, publicly traded competitors of a similar scale, analysts contextualize its valuation by comparing it to two distinct peer groups: multinational toy/special interest companies (such as Hasbro, Mattel, and Tomy) and global intellectual property/content creators.

Cash Generation and Dividend Sustainability

The foundation of sell-side confidence in Games Workshop lies in its robust cash generation and unparalleled Return on Capital Employed (RoCE), which reached 191% in FY25. Edison Investment Research notes that the company's vertically integrated model and total control over its IP allow it to convert incremental revenue into cash at an exceptionally high rate.

Edison Investment Research • 2 Mar

The company's dividend policy is highly sustainable because it does not rely on a fixed earnings payout ratio; instead, it distributes "truly surplus cash" to shareholders. Edison Investment Research details that this distribution only occurs after the company has funded near-term operating requirements, capital expenditures, and a strict cash buffer. To accommodate the expanding scale of operations, management recently increased this required cash buffer from £80m to £85m. Because visibility on surplus cash improves as the year progresses, the company typically announces multiple distributions, with dividends having grown at a 33% compound annual growth rate between FY16 and FY25.
