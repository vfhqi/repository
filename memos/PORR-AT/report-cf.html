EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

This comprehensive analysis reveals PORR AG as undergoing material strategic transformation from volume- chasing to margin expansion, with management successfully pivoting to the "Green and Lean" programme while maintaining stable leadership continuity and securing multi-year EU-funded infrastructure tailwinds . The materiality of change registers as high , driven by structural margin expansion targets of 3.5-4.0% EBIT by 2030 versus the current 3.1% baseline , complemented by strategic geographic refocus on seven European home markets while actively winding down non-core Middle East operations .

The top six key drivers underpinning forward EPS trajectory demonstrate strong fundamental momentum . Polish and CEE civil engineering funding emerges as the primary growth engine, directly fueling top-line volume growth through massive EU-backed infrastructure pipelines . Contract indexation and proactive cost control mechanisms structurally protect the targeted margin expansion from unpredictable raw material inflation shocks . The EUR 500bn German infrastructure stimulus represents critical medium-term upside optionality rather than

immediate revenue necessity . Higher-margin vertical expansion into data centers and healthcare facilities addresses group margin mix accretion . Structural working capital reduction drives mechanical EPS uplift through balance sheet optimization independent of market conditions . The exceptional infrastructure order backlog exceeding EUR 10bn generates a massive 1.5x book-to-bill ratio with civil engineering dominating 57.8% of the portfolio .

Recent delivery demonstrates exceptional operational resilience across multiple dimensions. Q1 2026 EBIT grew 13.1% year-on-year to EUR 14.3m despite seasonally weak production windows , while order intake surged 14.7% year-on-year to EUR 1.76bn, severely defying broader European construction sector stagnation . FY25 EBIT of EUR 196.7m cleanly surpassed consensus estimates, representing a 5.1% positive earnings surprise that reinforces management's reliable delivery reputation . Free cash flow reached EUR 232.9m, materially outperforming expectations due to rigorous late-cycle receivables collection generating a EUR 50m working capital inflow . Forward credibility registers as highly robust based on management's established honest-tracker archetype. PORR structurally sets achievable financial targets and routinely delivers results at or slightly above guidance boundaries, with FY25 EBIT landing at EUR 196.7m versus initial EUR 180-190m guidance . The EUR 10bn order backlog provides visibility far exceeding one full year of production output, structurally buffering near-term macro shocks . Management secured 90% of gas requirements through 2030 via forward hedging, though the 4.0% margin target mathematically requires massive fixed-cost absorption from continuous Polish civil engineering volume expansion . Sell-side positioning reflects bullish narrative consensus with significant concentration risk. The rating mix shows 100% Buy recommendations with price targets clustering between EUR 42-46, implying 19-33% upside potential. The dominant thesis hinges on predictable, margin-accretive growth from EU-funded civil engineering pipelines, with analysts unanimously highlighting the credibility of PORR's 2030 EBIT margin targets. However, no hold-rated or sell-rated analysts were surfaced in the available library, precluding direct contrarian credibility assessment. Fundamental peer positioning establishes PORR as a clear second-quartile performer with significant upside runway. PORR screens in the second quartile for fundamental momentum, offering superior 10% EBIT CAGR but trailing Hochtief's absolute margin levels . Current 3.1% margins severely lag Polish peer Budimex's highly profitable 8.5% margin delivery, confirming substantial improvement scope . PORR generates over 58% of revenue from civil engineering, perfectly insulating the company from European housing market weakness while benefiting from Polish infrastructure exposure alongside Budimex .

Activist presence remains entirely absent . No active activist presence exists, deterred by the 47.4% controlling syndicate stake held by management and aligned shareholders . The two most material risks threaten the core investment thesis through funding dependency and operational leverage. EU funding delays represent the primary systemic risk, where German political gridlock delaying the EUR

500bn infrastructure fund while Polish EU cohesion funds suffer bureaucratic friction would collapse the vaunted civil engineering tailwind . Operational leverage vulnerability emerges as the secondary risk, where structural wage inflation outpacing indexed contract caps would permanently compress margins rather than expand them , while simultaneous political delays would compress top-line volumes, making the 4.0% 2030 margin target mathematically impossible without double-digit volume growth to absorb the rigid fixed-cost base.
