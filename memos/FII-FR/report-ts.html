Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI represents a compelling turnaround and structural growth story driven by aerospace ramp-ups and a newly deleveraged balance sheet, making deeper work highly warranted.
1.
Specialized industrial focus: LISI specializes in manufacturing high-strength metal fasteners and critical structural components, generating 68% of its revenue from Aerospace and 32% from Automotive after selling LISI Medical in late 2025 to SK Capital for €270.40 million
2.
Critical narrowbody rate swing-factor: The single most important swing factor for earnings is the narrowbody ramp-up rate of commercial airframers, particularly Airbus, which is lifting its monthly A320 production cadence upstream
3.
Sustained cash generation: LISI has generated positive operating Free Cash Flow for many years, reaching €56.10 million in 2025 compared to €19.00 million in 2022
4.
Deeply deleveraged balance sheet: The group's net debt fell dramatically to €233.70 million in 2025 compared to €488.50 million in 2024, lowering leverage to a comfortable 0.90x EBITDA
5.
Geopolitical and supply chain risks: The most critical risk is supply chain disruption or rising trade tariffs, which could impose a gross tariff cost of €10 million to €12 million across divisions
6.
Highly favourable research verdict: An immediate deep dive is warranted because the sell-side has revised LISI's FY2027e EPS upward by 42%, reflecting superior earnings momentum at an undemanding valuation

Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI occupies a profit-favoured position within high-barrier manufacturing niches, supported by high switching costs, dual-certified operations, and consolidated customer networks.
  2. The group's refocused aerospace portfolio provides exceptionally high visibility through long-term agreements, although LISI Automotive remains exposed to cyclical passenger car weaknesses.

Segment and Product Taxonomy

1.
Specialized aerospace components: LISI Aerospace (68% of sales) is divided into Fasteners (70% of segment sales) and Structural Components (30% of segment sales), providing critical high-strength assembly solutions
2.
Upstream value positioning: LISI Aerospace's fasteners are typically used upstream of aircraft final assembly lines, ensuring structural integrity in extreme operating environments
3.
Highly engineered automotive parts: LISI Automotive (32% of sales) is divided into Threaded Fasteners (33% of segment), Clipped Solutions (38%), and Safety Mechanical Components (29%), focusing on high-value braking systems
4.
Refocused corporate portfolio: The group completed the strategic divestout of LISI Medical in October 2025 to SK Capital for €270.40 million, which previously generated €185.30 million in 2024
5.
Deep technical co-designing: LISI acts as a co-designer and solutions provider to its customers, incorporating advanced metalworking, cold heading, hot forming, additive manufacturing, and automated machining
6.
Advanced automation integration: The group is deploying disruptive artificial intelligence solutions for fully autonomous machining to support operational efficiency

Value Chain Position and Moat Analysis

7.
High entry barriers: Durable competitive advantages stem from rigorous aerospace qualifications (EN9100, NADCAP), high tooling investments, and dual-certified factories that make customer switching highly expensive
8.
Long-term backlog visibility: Revenues are primarily earned through multi-year long-term agreements (LTAs) awarded through competitive bidding, which lock in market share but limit immediate pricing changes
9.
Manageable customer concentration: Customer concentration is moderate, with only three clients representing more than 5% of group sales historically, and the largest customers being blue-chip global OEMs
10.
Favourable aerospace market structure: LISI operates in a highly consolidated $4 billion A&D fasteners market, where five large players control the market and LISI maintains a strong 20% market share
11.
Competitive market share position: LISI Aerospace sits as the third largest player in global aerospace fasteners, following Howmet with 29% and Precision Castparts with 26% market shares
12.
Below-peer margin profile: LISI Aerospace's operating margin of 8% is lower than Howmet Fastening Systems' 23% and TriMas Aerospace's 13%, partly due to its 30% structural component mix
13.
Other side of the story: LISI faces continuous commercial pressure from automotive and aerospace contractors to lower unit selling prices on existing programs, which could erode margin stability if LISI fails to continuously lower its manufacturing break-even point

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 12 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI's financial profile showcases rapidly accelerating operating margins and superior ROCE, bolstered by record aerospace demand and a massive debt-reduction transaction.
  2. The group's leverage has dropped to a historic low of 0.90x EBITDA, delivering unmatched balance-sheet strength and substantial liquidity headroom beneath authorized bank covenants.

Growth and Profitability Analysis

1.
Expanding operating profitability: Current operating profit (EBIT) surged 63.20% year-on-year to €161.01 million in 2025 from €98.71 million in 2024, driving the EBIT margin up to 9.20%
2.
Superb cash flow performance: Free Cash Flow reached €56.10 million in 2025, showing solid recovery from €19.00 million in 2022 and €22.20 million in 2023, reflecting disciplined working capital
3.
Superior capital returns: Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) before tax improved significantly to 12.20% in 2025 compared to 7.70% in 2024, reflecting increased industrial asset utilization
4.
Rising dividend track record: LISI proposed a dividend of €0.46 per share for 2025, up from €0.39 in 2024 and €0.31 in 2023

Debt, Leverage, and Current Trading

5.
Historic deleveraging milestone: Net financial debt decreased to €233.70 million in 2025 from €488.50 million in 2024, driven by cash inflows from the LISI Medical divestout
6.
Pristine balance sheet: Leverage fell to an ultra-low 0.90x EBITDA in 2025 from 2.30x in 2024, providing significant headroom beneath LISI's bank leverage covenant of 3.50x
7.
Impressive recent trading: LISI's Q1 2026 sales reached a record €325.10 million in Aerospace, up 17.60% organically, supported by Airbus shipping single-aisle jets at 64 per month
8.
Cyclical automotive weakness: LISI Automotive saw Q1 2026 sales contract 3.50% to €143.00 million, reflecting global client production drops estimated at -1.40%
9.
Strategic asset reallocation: Deinvesting LISI Medical removed a division that accumulated €149.90 million in revenue over 10 months in 2025, which was fully offset by Aerospace's +€189 million expansion
10.
Other side of the story: Working capital could face severe pressure and cash conversion might decline if LISI is forced to build costly strategic inventories, as occurred in H1 2024 when inventory levels peaked at 103 days of sales

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is powerfully strengthening as LISI transitions into a focused, highly profitable aerospace supplier with exceptional operating leverage and capital flexibility.
  2. Key forward drivers include accelerating narrowbody rates and lag-mitigated price catch-ups, which are independent and highly plausible over the next 24-36 months.

Core Value and Earnings Drivers

1.
Aerospace volume leverage: Increasing single-aisle rates at Airbus and Boeing directly expand LISI Aerospace revenues (68% of sales), driving rapid fixed-cost absorption and industrial productivity
2.
Highly focused corporate strategy: De-consolidating LISI Medical for €270.40 million removed a slower-growing division, leaving LISI with €254.80 million less net debt to fund high-return aerospace expansions
3.
Advanced manufacturing investment: High CAPEX of €63.80 million in LISI Aerospace in 2025 was targeted at capacity increases in the United States and structural components in France, securing future growth

Plausibility and Bull Case Mechanics

4.
High-probability volume outlook: The volume driver is highly plausible given the extensive aircraft backlogs (Airbus holds over 7,000 orders), providing strong demand visibility over 24-36 months
5.
Moderate-probability automotive recovery: The automotive driver has lower predictability, as global passenger car sales remain highly volatile, requiring ongoing cost restructuring
6.
High independent probability: Importantly, the bull case does not require all segments to fire; the aerospace division's expansion is sufficient on its own to drive a 25%+ IRR
7.
Other side of the story: The investment case would break if a prolonged technical grounding or safety crisis at a major airframer forces rate cuts, leading to severe plant underutilization across LISI's fixed-cost-heavy manufacturing base

Section 6 – Valuation 5 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI's valuation is unusually cheap and undemanding on both absolute and relative bases, creating a rare entry point for a sector-leading earnings trajectory.
1.
Substantial sector discount: LISI trades on an EV/EBIT multiple of approximately 14.50x or lower for FY2027e, standing at a significant discount to the European Aerospace & Defense sector median EV/EBIT of 16.30x
2.
Deep discount to global peers: The stock is valued at a deep discount to its global peers, such as Howmet Aerospace which trades at a forward EV/EBIT multiple well above 21.00x
3.
Unjustified market skepticism: Current multiples suggest the market is mispricing LISI as a struggling automotive stock, failing to recognize its structural transition into a high-margin aerospace pure-play
4.
Other side of the story: LISI's valuation could experience structural multi-year discount if its 32% automotive exposure keeps the group categorized as a slow-growing industrial conglomerate rather than a pure-play aerospace player

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is centered around customer-specific assembly disruptions and currency fluctuations, which are heavily mitigated by robust contract indexation and pricing power.
  2. The absence of short-selling pressure and stable family control provide a highly secure capital structure that protects LISI from aggressive market volatility.

Operational and Geopolitical Risks

1.
Customer rate volatility: LISI Aerospace is directly exposed to sudden production rate halts or strikes at Airbus and Boeing, which can disrupt the manufacturing cadence and delay sales
2.
Material foreign currency headwind: Because LISI reports in Euros but generates substantial sales in USD, a weakening dollar creates severe transaction headwinds, such as a -€31.90 million cash translation hit in H1 2025
3.
Global supply chain bottlenecks: Geopolitical conflicts can disrupt raw material logistics in Europe, requiring LISI to qualify alternative suppliers or carry higher safety stocks
4.
Absence of short-selling pressure: No active short-seller reports or significant short interest are present, indicating stable long-term institutional ownership
5.
Key early-warning metric: The single early-warning indicator to watch is the monthly narrowbody delivery rates of Airbus and Boeing and LISI's quarterly inventory turnover days
6.
Other side of the story: The risks of raw material inflation and tariffs are highly overstated because LISI's long-term contracts feature robust indexation clauses, allowing the group to largely pass through inflation costs directly to its customers

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 4 findings

Section summary
  1. Executive leadership has become a significant catalyst for change following the appointment of a highly capable Chief Operating Officer to drive industrial execution.
1.
Operational leadership reinforcement: In March 2026, LISI announced the creation of a General Operations Department, appointing Benoît Heubert as Chief Operating Officer to lead manufacturing, procurement, transformation, organization, and CSR across both divisions
2.
Impressive operational track record: Benoît Heubert brings 28 years of industrial strategy experience from Michelin Group and previously managed Euromaster, a €3 billion revenue mobility company, as CEO in 2025
3.
Alignment with core strategy: This new appointment will closely support long-standing CEO Emmanuel Viellard in executing the group's long-term strategic plan and improving factory efficiency

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 4 findings

Section summary
  1. LISI warrants an immediate positive investment decision to initiate deeper due diligence, driven by its superb earnings momentum, structural deleveraging, and attractive valuation.
1.
Other side of the story: The strongest argument against LISI is its persistent 32% revenue exposure to the structurally weak and highly cyclical automotive market, which could depress the consolidated group's multiple
2.
Focused primary check action: Verify the precise inflation indexation lags and pricing pass-through capabilities within LISI's newly renewed long-term contracts with Airbus and Boeing to confirm margin resilience
3.
Secondary operational tracking action: Track the monthly physical deliveries of Airbus A320 products to confirm that Airbus's cadence of 64 per month in Q1 2026 translates directly into sustained sales growth for LISI Aerospace
