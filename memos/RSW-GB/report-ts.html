Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Renishaw represents a high-quality cyclical recovery story experiencing an unprecedented surge in semiconductor and aerospace demand.
1.
Primary business model: Renishaw makes money by selling premium metrology probes, software, and position encoders directly to machine tool builders and advanced manufacturing end-users. It operates a highly integrated in-house manufacturing model, allowing it to capture high gross margins before research expenses
2.
Possible investment case: A cyclical turnaround in semiconductor capital expenditure combined with a structural defence surge is driving rapid revenue growth. When overlaid with a massive £20m labour cost-reduction program, this top-line acceleration is set to unleash substantial operating leverage
3.
Key swing factor: The critical driver of the forward EPS trajectory is the pace of semiconductor equipment capital cycles, particularly for position encoders in APAC. This market is historically volatile and highly geared to consumer electronics retooling
4.
Strong recent execution: Record third-quarter revenue of £206.0m rose 14% actual and constant currency, driven by position measurement sales. This performance represents a material acceleration from a flat prior year
5.
Promising margin recovery: Management upgraded its full-year fiscal year 2026 adjusted profit before tax guidance to £145m-165m, representing a 22% operating margin progression target. This represents a substantial improvement over the flat 15.7% margin achieved in fiscal year 2025
6.
Critical downside risk: Escalating trade tensions and potential tariffs on the 20% US revenue base could disrupt exports. Since most manufacturing is based in the UK, any global supply chain disruption or rising shipping costs represents a clear headwind
7.
First-look verdict: A deeper investigation is highly warranted because the earnings trajectory is inflecting strongly due to structural tailwinds. At the same time, the stock trades at a premium that requires detailed valuation underwriting against peers

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Renishaw operates a virtually monopolistic position in machine tool probing and high-end position encoders.

Segment Dynamics and Revenue Streams

1.
Segment revenue breakdown: Effective from July 2025, the business is split into Industrial Metrology (~60% of sales), Position Measurement (~30%), and Specialised Technologies (~10%). This reporting structure provides unprecedented transparency into distinct end-market drivers
2.
Industrial Metrology products: This segment comprises physical probes for machine tools, five-axis coordinate measuring machines, and metrology software platforms. These systems act as the eyes and ears of CNC machinery to measure precision tolerances
3.
Position Measurement encoders: This division sells highly sensitive open optical, magnetic, and enclosed encoders to motion control and semiconductor equipment builders. It represents the highest-margin segment in the entire group
4.
Specialised Technologies division: This segment houses metal additive manufacturing machines, spectroscopy systems, and remaining neurosurgical surgical robots. Additive systems are particularly geared toward medical titanium implants and aerospace customers
5.
Transactional sales model: Most revenue is transactional, driven by customer capital expenditure budgets and system sales. Aftermarket services, including repairs, software licences, and training, contribute a small but growing recurring revenue stream

Moats, Value Chain, and Market Structure

6.
Unrivalled switching costs: Renishaw's machine tool probes represent the absolute industry standard. Once designed into a high-volume CNC automotive or aerospace line, switching to a competitor would be highly disruptive
7.
Powerful scale barrier: High barriers to entry are maintained through robust technology, long-standing customer trust, and a global support network. It would take new entrants over ten years to replicate these precision capabilities at the high end
8.
Favourable value chain: The company occupies a profit-favoured position as a premium technology supplier to capital equipment builders. It achieves gross margins of nearly 70% before R&D, showing strong pricing power
9.
High geographic concentration: APAC remains the dominant region, accounting for £337.7m of FY2025 revenue, followed by EMEA at £207.8m and the Americas at £167.5m. Within APAC, China is a critical growth market but faces rising low-cost competition
10.
Other side of the story: Rising low-cost Chinese competition in the metrology and encoder space is beginning to capture market share in lower-end applications. While the high-end gap remains significant, Chinese competitors are sponsoring university research to close this gap within five to ten years

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Renishaw's financial profile is characterized by high operational gearing, exceptionally strong cash generation, and zero debt.

Multi-Year Trends and Profitability

1.
Revenue growth trajectory: Renishaw achieved a record revenue of £713.0m in FY2025, representing 3.1% growth over FY2024. Multi-year performance was strongest in FY2022, when revenue grew by 19.0% as markets recovered. For fiscal year 2026, Deutsche Bank estimates top-line acceleration to £798.0m, representing 12.0% annual growth
2.
Adjusted operating margin progression: The adjusted operating profit margin was flat at 15.7% in FY2025, representing a material contraction from the high-water mark of 24.1% recorded in FY2022, primarily due to adverse currency movements and labor cost inflation. Deutsche Bank expects the operating margin to recover to 19.7% by FY2028
3.
Adjusted EPS and cash flow: Adjusted EPS rose from 133.2p in FY2024 to 137.8p in FY2025, remaining below the record 185.5p in FY2022. Cash and deposit balances expanded to £273.6m in FY2025, estimated to reach £279.0m by end of June 2026
4.
Recent quarterly beat: Performance in Q3 FY2026 was exceptionally strong, prompting management to upgrade full-year guidance. First-half constant currency growth was 11.5%, accelerating to 13.5% for the nine-month period

Cash Flows, Balance Sheet, and Guidance Quality

5.
Cash generation profile: Adjusted cash conversion from operating activities reached 91% in FY2025, comfortably exceeding the company's 70% target. First-half cash conversion was lower at 68% due to temporary working capital build-ups
6.
Pristine balance sheet: The group carries no bank debt, leaving it with net current assets of £468.5m and £240.9m cash at H1 FY2026. This cash cushion provides strong capital protection during macro downturns
7.
Conservative guidance history: Management's historical guidance reads as conservative. In three previous instances since fiscal year 2014 where management upgraded H1 guidance, the final full-year outturn achieved or exceeded the upper bound
8.
Other side of the story: Working capital remains highly volatile, with inventory representing nearly 23% of sales in H1 FY26. This high inventory load acts as a drag on return on capital and cash flow during demand slowdowns

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens dramatically due to a dual-engine recovery in global semiconductor demand and aerospace capital expenditure.

Key Revenue and Margin Drivers

1.
Semiconductor capex turnaround: Position Measurement revenue growth accelerated to 21.6% constant currency in 9M FY2026. This driver is highly plausible given strong order bookings from global wafer-fab equipment makers. It could fail if geopolitical tensions or consumer demand slowdowns halt fabrication investments
2.
Aerospace and defence surge: Industrial Metrology and Specialised Technologies are experiencing robust demand from aerospace and defence customers. This driver is highly plausible, as additive manufacturing sales are accelerating in Europe and the Americas. It would fail if fiscal spending caps restrict government military procurement
3.
Annualized cost savings: Management implemented a £20m labour cost-reduction program alongside a £3m drug delivery exit. This driver is highly plausible and provides a direct 300 basis point operating margin benefit. It could fail if national wage hikes or UK tax increases completely absorb these savings

Structural Operating Leverage

4.
Aggressive operating leverage: Renishaw's business possesses high operational gearing of approximately 37% in positive growth years. Driven by the completed expansion at the Miskin facility, higher throughput immediately expands EBIT margins
5.
Independent driver probability: The investment drivers are mostly independent, meaning the case does not require all of them to fire simultaneously. The semiconductor turnaround and internal cost restructuring operate on separate dynamics, reducing compound failure risk
6.
Other side of the story: High operational gearing cuts both ways, meaning any unexpected volume slowdown would result in a sharp contraction in operating margin. This downside gearing is exacerbated by the company's historical reluctance to reduce headcount during cyclical downturns

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Renishaw's valuation looks historically demanding following a substantial year-to-date share price re-rating.
1.
Current valuation multiples: Renishaw trades on a revised fiscal year 2026 P/E multiple of 28.2x, reducing to 26.0x when excluding net cash. This represents a material premium compared to the wider UK Capital Goods sector median of 18.2x
2.
Forward sales multiple: The shares trade on a forward EV/Sales multiple of 4.1x, which sits above the historical 3.0x long-term valuation average
3.
Premium relative to peers: The company historically trades at a five-year average premium of 41% to its international peer group. This premium is justified by its industry-standard position in metrology probes, but leaves little margin for error
4.
Bullish analyst target: The price target from Deutsche Bank is set at 4,960p, reflecting strong confidence in the cycle based on historic valuation averages
5.
Bearish analyst target: By contrast, Jefferies sets a price target of 3,450p. This caution stems from concerns over top-line recovery and multiple expansion sustainability
6.
Other side of the story: Sector consolidations and recent takeover activity in UK Capital Goods suggest that the valuation could receive structural support. Spectris was acquired by KKR at a forward EV/EBITA multiple of 18.9x, indicating strong private equity appetite

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is credible but balanced by highly defensive balance sheet cash.

Market and Operational Risks

1.
Volatile operational gearing: The single most dangerous failure mode is a sudden drop in customer capex, which historically collapses margins due to high operational gearing. This risk is elevated by the company's fixed cost base and predominantly UK manufacturing footprint
2.
Currency transaction exposure: The company has high transaction exposure to the US Dollar and Euro. A strengthening Sterling creates a massive profit headwind, illustrated by a £13.2m adverse foreign exchange impact in H1 FY26
3.
Shareholder illiquidity: The ultimate controlling party is Deltam Holdings with a 50.25% stake, which combined with John Deer's holding represents nearly 53%. This concentration limits public float and leaves long-term strategic plans uncertain
4.
Pricing pass-through issues: While the company passed through 1.4% of price increases to offset US tariffs, further tariff escalation could damage competitiveness. This is highly critical given that the company lacks a local US manufacturing presence

Early Warnings and Mitigants

5.
False friend caution: High forward hedging contract gains can create a false sense of security. For example, H1 results included £8.6m of forward contract gains, which can mask underlying margin deterioration from core segment operational issues
6.
Primary warning signal: The most reliable early-warning signal is the monthly Japanese Machine Tool orders, which historically correlate closely with Renishaw's share price. A decline in these orders indicates a cooling in global precision manufacturing
7.
Other side of the story: The risks are significantly overstated because Renishaw possesses massive component inventory representing 23% of sales. This inventory allows it to immediately capture market share when the capital investment cycle inflects upward

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership is experiencing a generational and operational shift that introduces fresh financial discipline.
1.
Executive leadership transition: John Shipsey was appointed CFO and Executive Director effective April 2026, succeeding long-standing Group Finance Director Allen Roberts. Shipsey brings extensive listed experience from Dyson and Smiths Group to strengthen corporate governance
2.
Permanent Board chair: Sir David Grant was appointed permanent Chair for a period of up to two years, following his role as Interim Chair since Sir David McMurtry stepped down from Executive Chairman in July 2024
3.
Generational family commitment: Camille Deer, granddaughter of co-founder John Deer, joined the board as a Non-executive Director in September 2025. This appointment, alongside the creation of Deltam Holdings, signals the founding families' long-term commitment to the group
4.
Shift in compensation: Executive bonus criteria have evolved, moving from 100% EPS-driven to incorporating strategic measures including operational efficiency. This shift aligns leadership incentives with long-term margin and ESG targets

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. A closer look is highly warranted because the operating leverage opportunity is substantial.
1.
Strongest bull argument: The key positive is the rapid recovery in the high-margin Position Measurement segment combined with the £20m structural cost-out program. This dual effect is highly likely to drive operating margins back toward the company's 20% target
2.
Other side of the story: The key negative is the highly demanding valuation multiple, which leaves no margin for error if the semiconductor capital cycle slows. Additionally, the company lacks a US manufacturing base, making it vulnerable to trade policies and US tariff changes
3.
First check next step: Validate the progression of the £20m labour cost-savings program to ensure the run-rate benefits are fully captured in the H2 results
4.
Second check next step: Monitor monthly Japanese Machine Tool orders and global semiconductor wafer-fab capital expenditure guidance to track the early-cycle inflection
