B.1 Business Description 11 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Pure-play cable manufacturer: NKT A/S designs, manufactures, and installs low-, medium-, and high-voltage power cables, operating exclusively in European and Nordic markets following the 2024 divestment of NKT Photonics
2.
Three business lines: Transmission (extra-high-voltage subsea and underground interconnectors — the profit engine), Distribution (medium-voltage grids, low-voltage building wires), and Grid Solutions & Accessories (AC onshore projects, offshore repair, accessories)
3.
Revenue scale: EUR 2,722m standard metal revenue in FY25, up from EUR 1,263m in 2021 — a 21% CAGR driven overwhelmingly by organic high-voltage execution
4.
Backlog dominance: EUR 13.5bn high-voltage order backlog as of Q1 2026; 75–80% of group EBITDA derives from high-voltage vs 20–25% for Prysmian and Nexans — NKT is the purest play
5.
Financial character: EBITDA margin 14.3% in FY25 (expanding toward mid-20s by 2030 on management targets); net cash EUR 963m (-2.5x leverage); FCF deeply negative during the EUR 2bn peak capex cycle; ROCE 24% (headline) / 8.4% ROIC (underlying — negative working capital flatters the headline)
6.
Investment style: Classic capacity-expansion compounder setup; earnings visibility locked through a USD 13.5bn backlog; FCF inflection point post-2027 when capex normalises to ~4% of revenue
7.
Geographic focus: Europe-only; insulates against Chinese competitive pressure that plagues global cable markets
8.
Operational split (FY25 approx): Transmission segment: ~55% revenue, ~75–80% EBITDA; Distribution segment: ~30% revenue, ~15–20% EBITDA; Grid Solutions & Accessories: ~15% revenue, ~5% EBITDA; the gross margin skew toward Transmission is the primary profit-quality differentiator vs Nexans and Prysmian
9.
Balance sheet character: Net cash EUR 963m / leverage -2.5x as of FY25; NWC is structurally negative at -EUR 1,524m (-35.8% of revenue) due to prepayments on long-cycle HV projects; this is a working capital advantage that reverses if order intake slows materially; headline leverage flatters underlying financial gearing
10.
Peer comparison: Among the three grid-hardening cohort members, NKT is mid-size (revenue EUR 2.7bn vs Prysmian EUR 21bn and Nexans EUR 7bn standard metal) but commands the highest HV EBITDA margin concentration; NKT is the purest single-stock expression of the HV cable investment thesis in the cohort
11.
CEO: Claes Westerlind (CEO since May 2023; internal succession from EVP Karlskrona); 15+ years high-voltage domain expertise; CFO transition April 2026 (Michael Yong replacing Line Fandrup)
