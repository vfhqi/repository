1.
Industry structure – highly favourable: Three-player Swiss oligopoly commands an HHI over 4,000, delivering sector-leading pricing power and margin accrual despite flat baseline revenue growth versus GDP
2.

Revenue stability masks an underlying volume-to-value transition, where operators rely on upselling premium features rather than capturing new household formation. Growth trajectory – lagging GDP: Swiss telco revenue growth remains structurally flat to slightly negative, materially underperforming projected national GDP growth of 1.2% to 1.5% for 2026-2027
4.
The macroeconomic environment features subdued economic expansion and historically low inflation, which provides little cyclical tailwind for telco pricing. As connectivity reaches absolute saturation, revenue expansion relies entirely on price increases or B2B IT service adjacencies rather than underlying volume growth.
