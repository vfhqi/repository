Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. XVIVO's highly attractive razor-and-blade business model enjoys high barriers to entry and global lung dominance, though its future profitability depends on successfully defending proprietary fluid margins and expanding US abdominal market share .

Core Product Segments and Revenue Model

1.
Leading lung foundation: The Thoracic business segment, generating SEK 509 million in 2025, comprises the company's leading lung evaluation products (XPS and STEEN Solution) and its heart preservation technologies
2.
High-growth abdominal portfolio: The Abdominal segment, representing SEK 225 million in 2025, focuses on the Liver Assist and Kidney Assist systems, which are currently expanding rapidly in Europe
3.
Service network integration: The Services segment, generating SEK 78 million in 2025, offers US-based organ recovery and digital workflows through the acquired FlowHawk communication platform
4.
Lucrative razor-and-blade: Revenue is heavily anchored in single-use consumables, which represent the razor-and-blade model where machines are placed to lock in highly recurring disposable sets
5.
High-margin recurring profile: The business model is highly predictable as consumables carry exceptional gross margins of 85% in Thoracic, compared to 61% in Abdominal

Customer Concentration and Moat Mechanics

6.
Single customer risk: The company maintains some concentration risk, with its largest single customer in the Thoracic segment representing SEK 132 million, or 16% of 2025 group revenues
7.
Multi-layered competitive barriers: XVIVO's competitive moat is defended by extensive clinical trial datasets, deep customer relationships with academic centers, and proprietary ex vivo fluids
8.
Unique fluid chemistry: XVIVO is the only medical technology company that develops its own proprietary ex vivo perfusion and preservation fluids, which are critical to cellular survival
9.
Unrivalled lung share: For traditional static preservation, XVIVO's Perfadex Plus is the undisputed global standard, controlling an estimated 90% market share
10.
Rising clinic adoption: In Europe, the Liver Assist device is entering the mainstream, achieving a dominant position with approximately 25% penetration in core markets

Industry Structure and Peer Comparison

11.
Favorable demand trends: The addressable market is growing steadily, driven by aging demographics, clinical guidelines favoring ex vivo perfusion, and US political pressure on OPOs to increase organ utilization
12.
Cold versus warm: Unlike TransMedics' highly complex and expensive normothermic (warm) transport system, XVIVO's heart system uses a cost-effective, logistically simpler hypothermic (cold) oxygenated perfusion method
13.
Named listed competitors: XVIVO competes primarily with TransMedics Group Inc (PMA-approved heart/lung/liver), Getinge's Paragonix (cold static transport), and Terumo's OrganOx (European liver perfusion)

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. XVIVO's forward investment case strengthens significantly due to several near-term clinical and regulatory catalysts in the United States and Europe that will accelerate consumables revenue .

Major Earnings Drivers and Clinical Catalysts

1.
Heart preservation launch: The imminent European CE mark approval and subsequent late 2026 FDA PMA filing for the XVIVO Heart Assist Transport represents the primary forward driver
2.
Outstanding clinical data: In clinical trials, the cold oxygenated heart perfusion method demonstrated a 38% reduction in severe post-transplant complications, making wide clinical adoption highly plausible
3.
Potential trial delays: This driver would fail if regulatory and commercial catalysts are delayed further, or if clinical safety signals emerge under the ongoing US Continued Access Protocol (CAP)
4.
Liver and kidney ramp: Launching the US liver DeLIVER trial and accelerating Kidney Assist Transport (KAT) sales represents the second major earnings driver
5.
Solid market demand: Expanding the US abdominal portfolio is highly plausible due to strong clinical validation, although the kidney market faces an entrenched competitor
6.
Hospital cost resistance: This driver would fail if US transplant clinics reject the abdominal devices due to high cost compared to simple cold storage
7.
Logistics model scaling: Establishing ex vivo lung perfusion (EVLP) hubs at Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs) represents a strategic driver to bypass clinical capacity constraints
8.
Proven commercial model: The OPO model is highly plausible, as evidenced by XVIVO's first US partnership with Perfusion Solution Inc, which has already driven a sequential recovery in EVLP disposable sales
9.
Surgical staffing churn: The OPO service model would fail if the company cannot hire and retain surgical recovery teams, leading to service capacity constraints

Investment Synthesis and Catalyst Paths

10.
Multi-path growth options: The forward earnings drivers are largely independent, meaning that delays in US heart PMA filings will not halt the commercial momentum of US kidney and European liver sales
11.
Flawless execution target: For the full bull case of SEK 4.0 billion in sales by 2028 to materialize, all drivers must execute flawlessly, making the bull case a compound probability

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward risk profile is moderate but highly concentrated around US regulatory approval timelines and the operational complexity of scaling US service networks .

Operational and Competitive Moats

1.
PMA approval delays: The most critical failure mode is any significant delay in US US FDA approvals for the heart technology or the liver Assist device, which would flatten the forward earnings trajectory
2.
Logistics network failure: Failing to build or maintain a highly responsive 24/7 organ transportation and perfusion service network in the US could limit customer uptake of its heart and lung systems
3.
Entrenched kidney leader: In the US kidney market, Organ Recovery Systems maintains a massive 95% market share, presenting a significant competitive barrier for XVIVO's Kidney Assist Transport
4.
Competitor product pipeline: TransMedics unveiled its own hypothermic preservation system (CHOPS) in April 2026, directly targeting XVIVO's core cold-perfusion clinical trial control arms
5.
Short selling activity: No published short-seller reports or unusually high short interest levels are currently present for XVIVO, unlike its US peer TransMedics which has faced short campaigns
6.
Compassionate use sales: While Compassionate Use sales in Australia represent 40% of all donor hearts, this is a misleading indicator of near-term commercial revenue as these procedures are not yet fully reimbursed
7.
Key metric monitoring: Investors should closely monitor sequential monthly US lung transplantation volumes and the timing of the CE mark heart approval as the primary early-warning indicators of commercial traction
8.
Other side of the story: Inelastic structural demand: Despite regulatory and competitive risks, the extreme inelasticity of organ demand–with the transplant list 10 times larger than available supply–underpins longterm volume growth
