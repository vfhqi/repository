Section summary
  1. Grupa Kety operates a highly integrated aluminium processing and flexible packaging model, which is structurally optimized under its new Strategy 2025-2029.

Business model and operational foundation

Grupa Kety operates a highly integrated aluminium processing and flexible packaging model, structurally optimized under its new Strategy 2025-2029, through four distinct business segments that offer diversified products to multiple industrial end markets . The Architectural Systems Segment designs and distributes aluminium façade, window, and door systems under the flagship Aluprof brand, acting as a leading European supplier of advanced, energy-efficient architectural solutions . The Extruded Products Segment manufactures precise aluminium profiles and custom components used across construction, automotive, and transport industries, operating 15 modern presses representing roughly 125,000 tons of annual production capacity across four complexes in Kety, Tychy, Slovenska Bistrica, and Borodianka in Ukraine . The Sun-shading Systems Segment focuses on roller blinds, shutters, pergolas, and sunbreakers under the Aluprof brand, with all essential production assets located in the Opole region of Poland . The Flexible Packaging Segment operates under the Alupol Packaging brand, producing printed laminates and BOPP films, holding an estimated 20% market share in the Polish flexible packaging market and representing the largest Polish manufacturer of BOPP films with 60,000 tons of annual capacity .

The group balances its revenue streams almost equally between domestic sales and international export markets, with Poland remaining the largest single country market, accounting for 51% of consolidated sales in 2025, while international sales spanning over 60 export countries generate 49% of total group revenues . The European

Union represents 39% of total sales, driven by Germany, Czechia, Italy, and the Netherlands, while sales outside the EU, representing 10% of revenues, are led by the United Kingdom and Ukraine . The group services over 6,000 active customers globally, mitigating high individual client concentration risks, with products distributed through specialized sales offices and distribution centers across ten European nations .

The group leverages its massive domestic purchasing scale to secure raw materials at competitive rates, with the capital-intensive nature of extrusion and film production creating significant profitability swings based on capacity utilization, while the in-house foundry in Kety recycles over 35,000 tons of scrap annually, lowering raw metal costs . The Management Board adopted Strategy 2025-2029 in April 2025 to scale European operations, uniting EPS, ASS, and SSS under the unified ALUPROF brand for aluminium processing, with the newly-separated Sun-shading Systems Segment formally incorporating SELT sp. z o.o. via merger with Aluprof S.A. on 8 December 2025 .

Financial profile and capital returns

Kety S.A. delivered an impressive separate parent ROE of 49.50% in 2025 and a separate ROA of 25.70% in 2025, while consolidated return on equity reached a strong 28.40% in 2025, remaining highly resilient at 28.50% in 2023 and 28.90% in 2024 . The exceptional capital returns reflect downstream architectural systems integration and operational leverage from vertical profile supply, positioning the group materially above fragmented European extruder peers who lack integrated downstream brands.

Value chain positioning and competitive moats

Grupa Kety is a highly integrated downstream leader, bypassing raw smelting to focus on extrusion and systems, with strategic focus on becoming a leading European player in branded downstream architectural systems under Aluprof, while the Extruded Products Segment operates as the internal manufacturing engine across four regional complexes . The Extruded Products Segment supplies approximately 85% of profiles used by Architectural Systems, representing roughly 23% of total extrusion output from EPS, while EPS also supplies approximately 7%

Aluprof commands a leading 46% share of Poland's architectural systems market, with architectural solutions spanning windows, doors, façades, and sun protection systems, and Aluprof has won the Market Leader in Joinery title 15 times consecutively . The Extruded Products Segment supplies approximately 85% of profiles internally to Aluprof, with this high integration shielding Aluprof from upstream profile procurement bottlenecks, as inter-segment transfers absorb roughly 23% of total extrusion output . The Kęty foundry recycles over 35,000 to 40,000 tons of scrap annually, with the share of scrap in soft-alloy billets cast in Kęty reaching a high 75%, enabling manufactured profiles to achieve a low carbon footprint of 3.3 kg CO2e/kg .

topic-specific reports The European transition to low-carbon aluminum is accelerating, driving growing demand for billets with high recycled content, with manufactured profiles achieving a low carbon footprint of 3.3 kg CO2e/kg compared to the European average of 9.0 kg, while Kety accelerated its emissions reduction goals, targeting a 25% reduction in carbon footprint intensity . R&D centers drive durable competitive advantage through complex engineering, with the Glassprof division designing fire-rated glass for commercial projects, while EPS engineers customized components for premium luxury automotive platforms, and qualified automotive components secure long-term contracts spanning thousands of tons .

Industry structure and competitive landscape

The integrated model captures superior value-chain economics through internal profile transfers, low-emission scrap recycling, downstream brand pull, and diversified end-market exposure across construction, automotive, and packaging applications. Structural regulatory tailwinds from European decarbonisation mandates and circular

economy requirements reinforce competitive advantages and support long-term margin expansion despite nearterm residential construction headwinds across Western Europe.
