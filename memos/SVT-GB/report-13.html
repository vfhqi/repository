  • Verdict — current guidance: Quantitatively anchored by the regulatory framework but AMP8 complexity introduces wider-than-AMP7 delivery ranges; management's stated targets are credible in base case but carry execution tail risk [#J]

  • FY current — RORE target: Management targets RORE of 8-9% across AMP8 vs Ofwat's base WACC of c.6.6% (CPIH-real); implies c.130-230bps of outperformance [#A]

  • Sub: outperformance route is Totex efficiency + ODI rewards; management guided to "broadly neutral to modestly positive" ODI balance in Year 1

  • FY current — revenue: Regulated revenue for FY25/26 guided to increase c.26-28% YoY, largely reflecting AMP8 allowed revenue step-up under the new Price Review [#A]

  • Sub: this is a mechanistic Ofwat-determined number, not a management discretionary target; actual delivery risk is low on revenue line

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management guided to EBITDA of approximately £820-840m for FY25/26 (May-25 results call); represents c.20-22% step-up from FY24/25 [#A]

  • FY current — EPS / DPS: No explicit EPS guidance issued; DPS policy confirmed as RPI+4% growth through AMP8 (minimum £1.2246/share in FY25/26 vs £1.1370 in FY24/25) [#A]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • Verdict — guidance pattern: Structural sandbagger on EBITDA and DPS; not aggressive over-promiser on any metric; EBIT guidance is implicitly optimistic but mechanistically explained rather than management-credibility-damaging [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: 4-for-4 EBITDA beats across L4 periods; average beat magnitude of +2%; this is consistent with a management team that sets EBITDA guidance at the midpoint of the regulatory allowed range and then delivers opex savings [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal — DPS: DPS policy is stated as "RPI+4% minimum"; actual delivery has consistently exceeded the floor by c.2.5-3% per annum; this is a deliberate communication strategy — set the floor, beat it, preserve optionality [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — absent: No instance in L4 periods where management guided to a number and then missed by a material margin on EBITDA; the closest was FY22/23 but management explicitly flagged the drought impact in advance [#A]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue delivery is essentially a regulatory pass-through; management does not "guide" to a discretionary revenue target; in-line delivery on this metric is mechanistically expected [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EBIT: Consistent EBIT misses are not a guidance credibility problem per se but do mask true earnings power; investors anchoring on EBITDA beats miss the D&A drag that compounds through the income statement [#J]

  • Pattern shift — AMP8: AMP8 introduces a structural pattern shift risk; the sandbagging cushion in AMP7 was partially financed by underspending on ODI commitments; AMP8 penalties for missing ODI targets are materially stiffer, reducing the opex flexibility that funded prior EBITDA beats [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat consistency: +2% EBITDA beat per period over 4 periods; in peer context (UK regulated utilities), United Utilities averaged +1.5% EBITDA beat in the same window, Pennon +0.8%; SVT's sandbagging discipline is modest but above sector average [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: Guidance accuracy on CapEx has marginally improved from FY21/22 (underspend) to FY22/23–FY24/25 (small, consistent overspend); management appears to have recalibrated supply chain cost assumptions post-pandemic [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the sandbagging pattern is that AMP8's ODI structure removes the primary lever management used to engineer EBITDA beats in AMP7 — historical sandbagging may not be structural to the business model but rather a feature of the old regulatory contract [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Management's AMP8 financial guidance rests on a layered assumption stack; each layer carries distinct fragility.

  • Verdict — assumption credibility: Revenue and DPS assumptions are mechanistically credible; Totex efficiency, ODI balance, and financing cost assumptions carry material execution and market risk [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Allowed revenue is Ofwat-determined and inflation-linked ; management is not assuming above-regulatory revenue growth; this is a low-fragility assumption [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Household connections and volumetric assumptions underpin c.3-5% of allowed revenue variability; SVT guides to population growth consistent with East Midlands development plans [#A]

  • Margin assumption — Totex efficiency: Management targets 5-8% Totex efficiency vs Ofwat's baseline; this is the primary RORE outperformance lever; the £13.9bn programme is 80% larger in nominal terms than AMP7, creating execution risk at scale not previously demonstrated [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Power costs assumed at c.£90-100m per annum (FY25/26) with c.70% hedged through FY26/27; unhedged tail creates EBITDA sensitivity [#A]

  • Sub: FY22/23 precedent: power cost overshoot added c.£30m to cost base above original budget

  • Margin assumption — ODI balance: Management guidance assumes broadly neutral ODI balance in FY25/26; the downside ODI penalty range is ±£200m NPV per Ofwat's own assessment; a £50m annual ODI penalty would reduce EBITDA by c.6-7% [#A]

  • EPS assumption — D&A: D&A set to step up c.£60-80m per annum from FY26/27 as AMP8 assets commissioned; this is not a management assumption but a mechanical consequence of CapEx delivery timing [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed at c.25% (UK corporation tax); no windfall tax exposure currently; management has not flagged any deferred tax reversal risk [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt/RCV guided to 60-65%; gearing maintained via a mix of RCF drawdowns and infrastructure-linked bond issuance; SVT has c.£3bn of bond maturities across AMP8 to refinance [#A]

  • Sub: refinancing risk: if credit spreads widen materially, refinancing at higher rates compresses interest cover

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: SVT's debt is c.55% index-linked; remaining c.45% fixed-rate exposure at maturity creates refinancing risk if long-term rates remain elevated vs AMP7 vintage [#A]

  • Macro assumption — inflation: RORE target depends on CPIH indexation of the RCV; if CPIH falls rapidly (below 2%), real revenue growth slows while nominal costs may remain elevated [#A]
