EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Campari's management quality assessment reveals a company navigating through profound leadership turbulence while delivering best-in-class organic revenue growth versus peers . The net verdict on CEO quality positions Simon Hunt as a highly disciplined owner-mindset operator with a proven track record of profitable premium brand-building at William Grant & Sons, where he delivered 12.7% organic sales CAGR and 21.8% operating profit CAGR between 2015 and 2019, expanding margins by 620 basis points .

governance robustness verdict reveals critical structural weaknesses that fundamentally constrain management autonomy and minority shareholder protections. Lagfin S.C.A. holds 82.30% of voting rights despite owning only 51.00% of total issued shares , creating a hyper-concentrated family control structure enabled by a three-tier Special Voting Share system that ultimately allows long-term holders to exchange certain shares for Special Ordinary Shares carrying twenty votes each, acting as a permanent anti-takeover defense .

This voting architecture is compounded by non-independent director Eugenio Barcellona chairing the Remuneration and Appointment Committee , representing an explicit deviation from Dutch governance best practices , and by the board's complete absence of formal succession plans for executive directors and lack of formal selfassessments of director performance .

5 transaction signals present a troubling absence of organic conviction buying by executive leadership. Campari executives executed zero open-market share purchases over the last 12 months , relying exclusively on equity grants that are heavily overshadowed by the family's voting control.

CEO Simon Hunt was assigned 105,803 PSUs and 211,607 RSUs on April 16, 2025, while Fabio Di Fede received 58,035 PSUs and 116,071 RSUs , but these awards lack the personal financial commitment signaled by open-market purchases. The controlling Garavoglia family maintained its dominant 50.98% equity interest and 82.33% of total voting rights as of December 31, 2025, executing no open-market sales , signaling structural stability but providing no incremental conviction data.

This stands in stark contrast to peers like Pernod Ricard's affiliate Société Paul Ricard, which executed €3.05 million and €1.95 million share acquisitions in late 2024 . network alignment confirms a bifurcated operating reality characterized by exceptional aperitif pricing power offset by severe US distributor destocking and cognac integration challenges.
