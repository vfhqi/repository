§2 Revenue Delivery Pattern

Nokia's revenue delivery has been less consistent than its profit delivery over L3Y, primarily due to large-scale volatility in global telecom capital expenditures.

Two major revenue misses exceeding 5% of consensus during the L3Y period: Q3 2023 (reported €4.98B vs €5.66B consensus, -12%) and Q4 2023 (€5.71B vs €6.12B consensus, -7%). [AS-Jefferies 19 Oct 23; AS-DNB_Carnegie 25 Jan 24]

Segment reliability — tale of two halves: Network Infrastructure (Optical and IP Networks) emerged as the most reliable growth engine toward end of L3Y, benefiting from AI data center demand. In Q4 2024, IP Networks grew 24% and Fixed Networks 16% YoY. Mobile Networks was least reliable, plagued by India normalization and loss of AT&T contract in late 2023. [Transcript-Earnings Calls 17 Oct 24; Press Release 30 Jan 25]

Revenue guidance accuracy: management was forced to lower full-year 2023 guidance mid-year due to weakening operator spending. By late 2025 and early 2026, guidance accuracy improved as NI order book solidified with book-to-bill consistently above 1. [Transcript-Earnings Calls 17 Oct 24]

"Nokia: Tonic therapy — Return to strong NI growth after the loss of the 5G contract at AT&T is welcome. Q4 sales came in 4% ahead, led by a strong performance in IP Networks (+24% yoy) within Network Infrastructure , even if flattered by weak comps." [AS-Deutsche_Bank 31 Jan 25 — Robert Sanders]

§3 Margin and Profit Delivery Pattern

Nokia has demonstrated a strong track record of beating EBIT and profit estimates even when revenue disappointed, largely due to "one-time" catch-up payments in Nokia Technologies and aggressive cost management.

Nokia quarterly operating profit and margin trends Q1-2025 through Q1-2026. [Nokia Q1 2026 Presentation 23 Apr]

EBIT consistently outperformed gross margin expectations in latter half of L3Y. In Q4 2025, comparable gross margin reached 48.1%, well ahead of historical averages. In Q4 2023, Nokia beat EBIT consensus by 11% (€846M vs €765M) despite 7% revenue miss — driven by better-than-expected margins in both NI and MN. [INTRM 29 Jan; AS-DNB_Carnegie 25 Jan 24]

"Conclusion: Nokia's Q4-25 sales were in-line, but gross margin at 48% was well ahead of both our forecast (46%) and consensus (45%)." [DNB Carnegie Q4-25 review]

Profit Drivers:
- Mix and Pricing: margin beats frequently driven by favorable product/regional mix, shift toward higher-margin software-enriched Network Infrastructure. [AS-Jefferies 26 Mar]
- Cost Savings: major cost-cutting program initiated Oct 2023, targeting €800M–€1.2B in gross savings by 2026. By Q3 2024 already achieved €500M in run-rate savings. [Transcript-Earnings Calls 17 Oct 24]
- Nokia Technologies: provided massive margin cushion, contributing 36% of total group operating profit in late 2023 with operating margins of nearly 75%. [Investor Presentation 1 Dec 23]

"While we are not counting on any upside in Mobile Networks, any success in the company's AI-RAN strategy in partnership with Nvidia is likely to further boost earnings and margins, despite some pressure on hardware revenues." [AS-Jefferies 3 Mar — Janardan Menon — AI-RAN gains momentum]

Restructuring Charges: largely flagged in advance as part of 2023 efficiency program. Q4 2025 saw significant impact from restructuring totalling €299M. Management guided for further restructuring cash outflows of €450M in 2026. [INTRM 29 Jan; Transcript-Earnings Calls 29 Jan]

§4 Guidance Pattern and Management Credibility

Nokia's management credibility faced a reset in 2023–2024 but has recovered as revised targets were met or exceeded.

Mid-Year Cuts: Management cut 2023 net sales guidance in July 2023 from €24.6B–€26.2B to €23.2B–€24.6B. In mid-2025, again adjusted guidance due to currency and venture fund reporting, revising comparable operating profit to €1.7B–€2.2B from original €1.6B–€2.1B. [AS-Jefferies 19 Oct 23; Press Release 23 Oct 25]

Guidance Upgrades (2026): Management reiterated full-year EBIT guidance of €2.0B–€2.5B but noted tracking "above the mid-point" following solid Q1 performance. [AS-DNB_Carnegie 23 Apr]

Comparison vs Ericsson: Nokia viewed by sell-side as having more resilient outlook than Ericsson due to greater diversification across NI segments (IP, Optical). While Ericsson has struggled with M&A failures and lack of clear growth drivers, Nokia's AI data center exposure through NI is seen as superior long-term play. [AS-Deutsche_Bank 6 Mar; 1 Feb 23]

"We expect the stock to re-rate to the high twenties on the back of a consistent growth outlook in Network Infrastructure, driven by the rising adoption of Nokia's Optical and IP Networking portfolio in AI data centre infrastructure deployments." [Long View Nokia Investment Thesis]

§5 Beat/Miss Distribution

Over 12 periods Q1-2023 to Q1-2026:

Revenue: Nokia beat consensus in only 4 of 12 periods (~33%), with 8 misses or in-line results. Average magnitude of revenue misses during 2023 downturn: approximately 7–12%.

EPS: Nokia beat or met EPS consensus in 9 of 12 periods (~75%).

Trend: Delivery quality markedly improving. Company ended 2025 and started 2026 with consistent EBIT and gross margin beats, signaling transition from cost-cutting defensive posture to growth-oriented phase driven by AI infrastructure. [INTRM 29 Jan]

§6 Working Capital and Cash Conversion

FCF vs Guidance: In 2023, FCF volatile — bottomed at negative €0.4B in Q3 2023 before recovering to €1.7B in Q4 2023. By 2025, FCF conversion reached 72% of comparable operating profit, well within guided 50–80% range. [AS-DNB_Carnegie 25 Jan 24; Earnings Presentation 25 Jan 24; Transcript-Earnings Calls 29 Jan]

Working Capital Dynamics: Headwinds in mid-2023 due to timing of large projects and inventory building; eased from Q4 2023. By Q4 2025, maintained robust net cash position of €3.4B. [INTRM 29 Jan; Press Release 19 Oct 23]

Cash Conversion Quality: Nokia targeting 65–75% EBIT-to-FCF conversion for 2028 long-term targets, up from TTM base of 61% in late 2025. [Investor Presentation 19 Nov 25]

§7 Sell-Side Reaction Pattern

Post-Q3 2023 (major miss): significant negative sell-side reaction; consensus revenue estimates cut materially; Jefferies maintained BUY but flagged risks to Mobile Networks recovery timeline. [AS-Jefferies 19 Oct 23]
