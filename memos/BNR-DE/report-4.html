  • IMCD EBITDA margin ~14-16% in FY-24 — a structurally higher level reflecting pure specialty positioning

  • But full-year EBITDA declined ~8-12% in FY-24 vs FY-23 — the full year masks the H2 stabilisation

  • Price contribution: Price was a net ~-5 to -7% EBITDA headwind in FY-24 as the spread compression from post-supercycle price normalisation fed through [#A]

  • The price headwind is now largely annualised; FY-25 price should be roughly neutral

  • This is the most important mechanical driver of margin stabilisation in FY-25

  • Volume contribution: Volume recovery added ~+1-2% positive EBITDA contribution in H2-24; the operating leverage on incremental volumes is moderate given the relatively fixed cost base [#A]

  • Operating leverage ratio at Brenntag is estimated ~3-4x (i.e. ~3-4% EBITDA growth per 1% volume growth)

  • At IMCD, this leverage is estimated higher (~5-6x) given smaller fixed cost base relative to GP

  • Input costs: Logistics and warehouse costs moderated in FY-24 after the 2022 cost inflation spike; energy costs in European warehousing fell meaningfully [#A]

  • Net positive contribution to margins of ~0.5-1.0% in FY-24 — a tailwind that is now largely exhausted

  • The full €400m target by 2026 remains ambitious; roughly €150-200m is estimated delivered by end of FY-24

  • IMCD runs a leaner structure and has no equivalent restructuring programme — its margins are structurally higher, not programme-dependent

  • Operating leverage: The revenue-to-EBITDA conversion at current volume levels is insufficient to drive significant margin expansion; meaningful expansion requires both volume recovery AND Project Brenntag delivery simultaneously [#J]

  • The two levers are partially correlated (volume up = easier to hit cost targets) but not guaranteed to coincide

  • Sell-side outlook: Consensus expects EBITDA to recover ~10-15% in FY-25 and a further ~8-12% in FY-26, driven by volume recovery + Project Brenntag cost savings combined [#A]

  • This consensus view is contingent on both levers materialising — a compounding assumption

  • Any miss on either lever would likely result in double downside to consensus

  • Cost programmes: Project Brenntag includes a headcount reduction of ~1,900 FTEs (announced 2023), digitisation of order management, and SKU rationalisation [#I]

  • Progress updates in FY-24 results showed ~€120-140m incremental EBITDA from the programme to date

  • Structural not one-off: the savings are expected to compound as volume returns

  • Sceptical bullet: The margin recovery story is almost entirely programme-dependent and volume-recovery-dependent; if either stalls, consensus EBITDA growth of ~20-25% cumulatively in FY-25-26 is unachievable — and the market may not be pricing sufficient execution risk at current multiples [#J]
