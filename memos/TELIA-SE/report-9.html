§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Mixed — EBITDA cash conversion is solid but restructuring charges are quasi-recurring, disposal gains inflated FY22 reported earnings, and the dividend/EPS gap is a structural quality concern [#J]

  • One-off impact: Restructuring charges ~SEK 0.58-0.90bn/year L3Y — management classifies as "one-off transformation costs" but they have recurred every year for at least 5 consecutive years; the declining trend (SEK 0.9bn → 0.58bn) is directionally positive [#A]
  • Cumulative L3Y restructuring charges: ~SEK 2.2bn; if treated as recurring, adjusted EBITDA and adjusted EPS are overstated relative to true economic earnings [#A]

  • Nordic telecom peer base-rate for genuinely one-off restructuring: charges should disappear within 2-3 years of initial programme; Telia is in year 5+ of the same programme — credibility of "one-off" label is low [#I]

  • Adjustments trend: Declining absolute size — total adjustments (restructuring + disposal impacts + impairments) moved from ~SEK 1.2bn (FY22) to ~SEK 0.7bn (FY24); directionally positive but not zero [#A]

  • Non-GAAP gap (adjusted EPS vs IFRS EPS): SEK ~0.02-0.12/share L3Y depending on period; narrower than FY20-21 peak adjustments [#A]

  • Cash conversion: Adequate — OCF/adjusted EBITDA approximately 50-55% L3Y (implying ~45-50% of EBITDA consumed by CapEx and working capital before reaching FCF); typical for a European incumbent telecom in mid-5G investment cycle [#A]

  • ❌ OCF-to-net-income conversion is weaker: ~SEK 8-9bn OCF vs ~SEK 4-5bn net income , implying D&A >> net income — Telia is a heavy D&A depreciator; EPS is partly a D&A allocation story [#A]

  • NWC tailwind: Modest positive — working capital has been a small tailwind FY22-FY24 (~SEK 0.2-0.4bn/year improvement); receivables turning slightly faster as enterprise mix shifts [#I]

  • Risk: if B2B revenue growth slows, receivables improvement reverses; payables extension appears to be near maximum for the sector [#I]

  • Recurring vs one-off: ❌ At least 3 categories of "one-off" recur annually — (1) restructuring/transformation charges, (2) M&A-related costs (even in years with no M&A), (3) "other items" in below-EBITDA; cumulative ~SEK 3.0bn over L3Y excluded from adjusted EPS [#A]

  • If half of these are "economic recurring," adjusted EPS would be ~SEK 0.10-0.15/share lower per year than management-reported — material relative to SEK 1.34 FY24 adjusted EPS [#A]

  • D&A trend: Rising — D&A increasing approximately SEK 0.3-0.4bn/year as 5G network investment depreciates; EBIT margin improvement is less impressive than EBITDA margin improvement because D&A eats the benefit [#A]

  • EBIT margin L3Y: ~20.0% (FY22) → 21.7% (FY24) vs EBITDA margin: 41.0% → 43.5%; the gap is D&A — rising D&A is a lagging indicator of past CapEx, not a new risk [#I]

  • Disposal gains in reported earnings: FY22 reported net income inflated by ~SEK 0.5bn net disposal gains (Fintur stake, Central Asian remnants); not in adjusted EPS but visible in reported EPS of SEK 1.50 vs adjusted SEK 1.52 (coincidentally similar) [#A]

  • FY23 disposal activity minimal; FY24 near-zero — reported/adjusted convergence improving [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — quality: The bearish read on earnings quality: Telia's "adjusted" EBITDA (their preferred metric) strips out SEK 2.2bn of cumulative L3Y costs. If those costs are economic (which 5+ years of recurrence suggests), true earnings power is materially below reported. The cash conversion looks solid only because CapEx is the constraint — FCF is actually thin, and interest coverage ratios have tightened as rates rose. A leverage-sensitive earnings story with quasi-recurring add-backs is a quality yellow flag [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: Structural but approaching plateau — cost-transformation-driven EBITDA margin expansion is real but the programme is maturing and volume leverage is absent [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: -2.6% L3Y (FY22→FY24) within continuing Nordic operations; service revenue (ex-hardware/equipment) decline slightly less severe at approximately -1.5% CAGR [#A]

  • Revenue decline driven by: (1) Swedish Consumer ARPU erosion under price competition (~-2% to -3% ARPU/year), (2) mix shift away from low-margin hardware bundles (revenue-reducing but margin-accretive), (3) Norway competitive pressure post-Telenor price cuts [#A]

  • Profit CAGR: EBITDA +0.5% CAGR L3Y vs revenue -2.6% — 3.1pp of operating leverage generated entirely by cost management, not volume [#A]

  • EBIT CAGR approximately +1.4% L3Y (FY22 SEK 5.20bn → FY24 SEK 5.35bn) — rising D&A partially offsets EBITDA improvement [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Negative revenue leverage, positive cost leverage — the company is demonstrating cost discipline in a revenue-declining environment; this is respectable but it is not the same as genuine operating leverage from volume growth [#I]

  • European incumbent telecoms benchmark: Telefonica, Orange, BT — all have similar margin-expansion-via-cost programmes; Telia's ~250bps L3Y expansion is in-line with peer range (200-350bps) but from a structurally lower revenue base [#I]

  • Gross margin: Not separately disclosed in Telia's reporting format; EBITDA is the first profit sub-total. The implied "gross margin before network/labour costs" is not independently auditable from public disclosures [#I]

  • EBITDA margin trajectory: Consistently improving — H1/H2 split shows both halves improving; H1 seasonally higher margin (leaner OpEx quarter) [#A]

  • FY22: 41.0% → FY23: 41.8% → FY24: 43.5% — acceleration of margin expansion in FY24 is the strongest single positive data point [#A]

  • Q1 2025 interim suggests continuation (~44%) — if sustained through FY25, would mark a further ~50bps improvement [#I]

  • Cost cut driver: Primary driver is headcount and network rationalisation — FTE reductions ~15-20% since FY20 transformation launch; Sweden network sharing agreements (with Telenor) reducing duplication [#A]

  • Diminishing returns signal: SEK 0.9bn restructuring in FY22 vs SEK 0.58bn in FY24 — the cost programme is in mature execution; the "easy" savings are taken [#I]

  • Mix shift: Margin-accretive hardware de-emphasis — Telia has systematically reduced low-margin handset/device revenues; service revenue mix is now ~88-90% of total (vs ~82-85% in FY21), which mechanically lifts EBITDA margin [#A]

  • Sustainability: Limited runway from FY25 — the two structural margin drivers (headcount reduction, hardware de-emphasis) are both largely exhausted; further margin expansion would require either (a) ARPU recovery in Sweden, (b) B2B/enterprise pricing improvement, or (c) network cost deflation via tech cycle [#I]

  • Management's FY25 guidance implies ~44% EBITDA margin (flat-to-small improvement) — consistent with this plateau thesis [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet — margin sustainability: The bearish read: Telia's margin expansion story is largely a one-time restructuring benefit and a mix-shift mechanical effect. With revenue declining, fixed cost leverage is actually negative (declining volumes across a fixed-cost network base). If Swedish B2C ARPU continues to erode at -2% to -3%/year, the cost programme cannot fully offset, and EBITDA margins will plateau or contract in FY26+. The "operating leverage" narrative is backwards — there is no volume leverage [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Modest and stable — Telia shows a consistent H1 > H2 EBITDA margin pattern; H2 revenue nominally higher but margin weaker due to seasonal OpEx loading [#J]

  • H1 pattern: Consistently higher EBITDA margin — H1 margins ~41-44% vs H2 ~40-43% across L3Y; driven by lower OpEx seasonality in H1 (fewer holiday/marketing campaigns, lower energy costs in Nordic spring/summer) [#A]

  • H2 pattern: Revenue nominally higher, margin lower — consumer device upgrade cycles (autumn), enterprise year-end IT spending, and Q4 marketing spend all load into H2; net effect is higher reported revenue but lower margin [#A]

  • H2 is also the period where restructuring provisions are most commonly charged (year-end true-up) — inflating H2 adjustments vs H1 [#A]

  • Q1 pattern (interim): Lowest absolute revenue, highest EBITDA margin — consistent with Nordic telecom seasonality (lean quarter for consumer upgrades; institutional spending light) [#A]

  • Seasonality direction: Stable over L3Y — H1/H2 split has not materially changed; no evidence of seasonality shifting [#A]

  • Cause — buying pattern: Swedish/Nordic consumer upgrade cycles drive H2 revenue but compress margin; B2B enterprise IT spending Q4-heavy [#I]

  • Energy cost seasonality (Nordic cold winters) creates H2 network OpEx uplift of approximately SEK 0.1-0.2bn; modest but directionally consistent [#I]

  • Seasonality changing: No material shift detected L3Y; mix-shift away from hardware de-emphasises the H2 upgrade cycle effect, very slightly moderating H1/H2 margin differential [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet — seasonality: The bearish read: H2 2024 showed a SEK 0.56 reported EPS vs H1 2024 SEK 0.72 — a wider H1/H2 gap than prior years. If H2 deterioration is becoming structurally worse (interest costs, D&A increases loading disproportionately into H2), what looks like seasonality may be a deteriorating underlying trend masked by a stable H1 [#J]
