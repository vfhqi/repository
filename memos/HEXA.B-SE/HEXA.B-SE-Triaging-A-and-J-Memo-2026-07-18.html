Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Group structure. Hexagon AB is a Swedish-listed industrial technology group organised into three core divisions post-restructuring, Manufacturing Intelligence, Geosystems and Autonomous Solutions, plus an emerging Robotics unit
2.
What it does. The company supplies precision metrology, geospatial surveying, positioning and industrial software that let customers measure, model and automate physical assets and processes
3.
Recent portfolio simplification. Hexagon divested its Design & Engineering software business to Cadence for a EUR 2.70bn cash-and-stock deal, completed February 2026, and separated its Octave software unit via an April 2026 spin-off
4.
Recent portfolio addition. Hexagon agreed to acquire Waygate Technologies from Baker Hughes for a USD 1.45bn cash deal, extending Manufacturing Intelligence into internal non-destructive testing and the maintenance, repair and operations market
5.
Financial character. Continuing-operations revenue of EUR 963.8m in Q1 2026, an adjusted EBIT1 margin of 26.1%, and net debt/EBITDA at 0.6x post-Octave, rising to 1.5x pro forma post-Waygate, well inside the 2.5x ceiling
6.
Margin target. Management's 2026-2030 targets are 4-6% organic growth and a 24-26% EBITAC margin, up from 22% in 2025, alongside 90-100% cash conversion
7.
Segment mix. Software and recurring services now represent 44% of pro forma sales, with 28% of the group recurring, a structural shift from the pre-spin-off hardware-heavy mix
8.
Geography and end markets. Exposure spans Chinese infrastructure construction (weak), Western European automotive (weak), aerospace and defence (strong) and global electronics manufacturing (strong)
9.
New optionality. The AEON humanoid robotics platform, developed in-house with NVIDIA, Microsoft and Maxon partnerships, is piloting with BMW and Schaeffler but management-guided material revenue is 5-7 years away
10.
Investment-style implication. The case reads as a self-help cost programme layered on a cyclical product recovery, closer to an event-driven turnaround than a steady compounder, per the source report's own archetype classification
