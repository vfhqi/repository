§1 — Current Management Guidance

OTE's current guidance stack spans FY25 (most recent full-year guidance, issued with FY24 results in February 2025) and a medium-term framework anchored to the FY24 Investor Day (November 2024). The guidance is quantitative on EBITDA-AL and CapEx; EPS guidance is implicit via dividend policy, not explicit.

  • J-front verdict: Management has issued quantitative FY25 EBITDA-AL and CapEx guidance; explicit EPS guidance is absent — dividend guidance proxies it; medium-term margin expansion targets are aspirational rather than hard-committed. [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: No explicit FY25 revenue growth target provided; management guided "low-single-digit organic group revenue growth" in the FY24 results call; Greece segment guided ~1-2% growth, Romania ~flat-to-slightly-positive. [#A]

  • Sub: Romania guidance was described as "stabilisation" — a qualitative rather than quantitative commitment

  • FY current — EBITDA: FY25 EBITDA-AL target €1.27-1.30bn (group, after leases), representing ~2-4% YoY growth from FY24 reported €1.25bn; midpoint €1.285bn is the operative assumption. [#A]

  • Sub: Guidance provided at FY24 results presentation, February 2025; no subsequent update as of Q1 2025 results

  • FY current — EBIT: No explicit EBIT guidance issued; EBIT is derivable from EBITDA-AL minus D&A; management does not guide D&A explicitly, creating a layer of opacity. [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance; management guides dividend per share (€0.72/share for FY25, subject to AGM approval) and implicit EPS trajectory is inferrable; sell-side consensus implies FY25 EPS ~€0.85-0.90. [#A]

  • Sub: Dividend guidance at €0.72 represents payout ratio ~80-85% of consensus EPS — a high payout that constrains reinvestment optionality

  • FY current — FCF: FY25 FCF guidance of "at least €450m" (organic FCF before spectrum payments and dividends); this is the most explicitly guided metric and historically the most reliable. [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: FY25 CapEx guidance ~€580-600m, essentially flat vs FY24 actuals; management flagged fibre rollout completion reduces sustaining CapEx pressure from FY26 onward. [#A]

  • Sub: Fibre subscriber pass completion in Greece ~85% by end FY25 per guidance — completion pace is a key monitoring item

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY26): FY26 EBITDA-AL target €1.33-1.38bn implied by Investor Day framework; no discrete FY26 guidance has been formally committed; the range is derived from the N3Y trajectory. [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY27): EBITDA-AL margin target of ~38-39% by FY27 (from ~36.5% FY24 base); EBITDA-AL absolute ~€1.40-1.45bn at these margins; implies ~4-5% annual EBITDA growth CAGR over 3Y. [#A]

  • Sub: This CAGR is consistent with the historical delivery record — FY21-FY24 EBITDA-AL CAGR was ~4-5%

  • Medium-term targets: Management has committed to dividend per share growth of "at least" €0.05/year through FY27, implying DPS of €0.77 (FY26) and €0.82 (FY27); this anchors the implicit EPS floor. [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Investor Day, November 2024; formal presentation slides and Q&A; reconfirmed at FY24 results, February 2025 [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1 2025 results call (May 2025) — no guidance revision, management described trading as "in line with expectations" [#A]

  • Update timing: No intra-year guidance updates typical; next formal update expected at H1 2025 results (August 2025) [#A]

  • Caveat: Management guidance explicitly excludes "material spectrum auction costs beyond FY25 budgeted amounts," "unforeseen regulatory changes in Romania," and "any M&A larger than bolt-on" [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: Greek retail market pricing stability assumed; Romania market price war assumed to "normalise" in H2 2025 — a conditional assumption that has been invoked in prior years and not fully materialised on schedule [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that explicit EPS guidance is conspicuously absent, allowing management to anchor investor attention on the more controllable EBITDA-AL metric while EPS — which reflects minority interests, DTA movement, and one-offs — remains deliberately vague; the mechanism is selective quantification that flatters the most controllable line. §3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: OTE management is a moderate sandbagger on EBITDA-AL and FCF; guidance is consistently set at or slightly below internal budgets, producing small but consistent beats; EPS is too noisy to classify. [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — FCF: FCF guidance uses "at least" floor language in all L4 periods; this is deliberate asymmetric framing; "at least" commits to a floor, not a ceiling, and all 4 actuals exceeded the floor by 7-10% [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal — CapEx: CapEx guidance is always stated as a range and actuals land at or below the bottom of the range in 3 of 4 periods; this directly supports FCF beats [#A]

  • Sub: Management's publicly stated reasoning is "capital discipline and efficiency gains" — a consistent narrative

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA-AL: EBITDA-AL guidance midpoints are consistently ~0.5-1.5% below internal analyst-implied targets; the range is set conservatively relative to the budget [#A]

  • Sub: This is consistent with German parent Deutsche Telekom's corporate governance approach — DT tends to instil conservative guidance culture across subsidiaries

  • Over-promiser signal: No systematic over-promising pattern detected on operational metrics; the sole exceptions are qualitative medium-term narratives (Romania stabilisation, margin expansion) that have taken longer to materialise than initial investor day timelines implied [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: EPS does not fit the sandbagger pattern; with 2 beats and 2 misses, EPS is better characterised as "honest uncertainty" — management does not explicitly guide EPS and the implicit trajectory is subject to below-EBITDA noise [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No clear pattern shift in guidance philosophy over L4 periods; the FCF/EBITDA sandbagging posture has been consistent; there is mild evidence that revenue guidance is becoming slightly more precise (qualitative language gradually being replaced with tighter implied ranges) [#A]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA: 3 of 4 beats is a 75% beat rate; for European mid-cap telecoms, peer reference is 60-65% beat rate — OTE is in the top quartile of EBITDA guidance accuracy [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: EBITDA beats are small (+0.1-1.2%); FCF beats are more material (+7-10%); the asymmetry reflects that FCF guidance is more deliberately conservative [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: EBITDA miss (FY2022) was -0.6% — minor; EPS misses were -5-7% but driven by one-offs not operational underperformance [#A]

  • ⚡ RARE: DT subsidiary governance: OTE's consistent conservative guidance posture on operational cash metrics (FCF "at least" every period, CapEx underspend every period) resembles Deutsche Telekom's own group-level guidance approach; this cultural transmission from parent to subsidiary is an unusual positive credibility anchor — rare among Southern European telecoms where parent influence on guidance quality is typically absent or negative. [Cross-ref: §7] [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on sandbagging is that the FCF buffer has been predominantly generated by CapEx underspend, not demand-side or margin outperformance; as fibre rollout completes and OTE enters a more normalised CapEx phase, the CapEx underspend mechanism diminishes — future FCF beats may require actual EBITDA outperformance, which has been thinner. [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • J-front verdict: Guidance accuracy on core operational metrics (EBITDA-AL, FCF) has been stable to modestly improving over L4 periods; EPS accuracy remains volatile and unimproved; the operational accuracy trend is a positive signal but the EPS noise is a structural rather than cyclical issue. [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: FY2024 EBITDA-AL variance was +0.3% (tightest in L4 periods); FY2021 was +1.2%; accuracy has been improving as Romania's contribution normalises and Greek cost base becomes more predictable [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — EBITDA: FY2022 was the worst EBITDA year (marginal miss of -0.6%); this was an idiosyncratic Romanian event, not a structural deterioration in Greek business forecasting [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — FCF: FCF guidance discipline has been consistently applied; no deterioration across L4; management's "at least" floor guidance is structurally conservative regardless of year [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — EPS: EPS predictability has not improved; FY2021 beat was DTA-driven (non-recurring), FY2023 miss was minority cost-driven; neither represents management improving its below-EBITDA forecasting [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Greek macro (inflation, consumer sentiment) contributed to mild revenue guidance imprecision in FY2022-23; Greece's GDP growth turned positive in 2023-24, improving the macro tailwind [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Romanian operations are the primary execution volatility source; Romania represents ~17% of group revenue but has disproportionately driven FY-level guidance misses; execution risk there is persistent [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: OTE's structure (Greek retail + Greek wholesale + Romania + international wholesale) is moderately complex; intra-group transfer pricing and wholesale pricing changes occasionally create below-EBITDA surprises [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: FCF guidance accuracy is the best in the set — all 4 periods beat guidance floor; CapEx accuracy is second-best [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: EPS accuracy is worst; the lack of explicit EPS guidance compounds this — management doesn't commit to a number, so "accuracy" is a derived measure with embedded imprecision [#A]

  • Accuracy trend signal: On the metrics management explicitly guides (EBITDA-AL, FCF, CapEx), accuracy has been stable-to-improving over L4; this is a genuine positive signal for the forward period [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend improvement is that the operational improvement in guidance accuracy coincides with a period of Greek macro recovery (GDP +2-3%) and declining CapEx intensity; if Greek macro reverses or Romania deteriorates further, the accuracy track record would likely weaken — the "improvement" may be cyclical, not structural management capability. §6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front verdict: The EBITDA-AL guidance rests on two credible assumptions (Greek cost control, CapEx discipline) and one fragile assumption (Romania stabilisation); EPS trajectory additionally rests on DTA recapture timing — the assumption least within management's control. [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Greek mobile and broadband subscriber bases are largely stable; management assumes net additions of ~30-50k broadband subs per year on fibre convergence; churn rates in Greece are low (~14-15% mobile annual) and not assumed to deteriorate [#A]

  • Sub: Greek broadband market penetration ~70% of households — limited greenfield subscriber growth; ARPU uplift from fibre is the primary Greek revenue driver

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes ~1.5-2% ARPU growth in Greece (inflation pass-through) and flat ARPU in Romania; Greek pricing assumption is credible given limited competition; Romanian flat ARPU assumption is more fragile given pricing war dynamics [#A]

  • Sub: Romania has 4 major MNOs competing aggressively; flat ARPU in that context requires market stabilisation that has not yet durably occurred

  • Revenue assumption — Romania: Romanian revenue "stabilisation" assumed from H2 2025 onward; this assumption has been embedded in OTE guidance since FY2022 and has repeatedly proven over-optimistic by 1-2 quarters; it is the most fragile single revenue assumption [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Greek operating cost base assumed to grow at ~1% annually (well below CPI); management has demonstrated this in practice: Greek OPEX grew ~0.8% in FY2024 vs CPI of ~2.5%; the structural driver is workforce reduction and digitisation [#A]

  • Sub: OTE headcount in Greece has declined ~3-4% annually for 4 consecutive years — this is a real, operational cost lever, not an accounting construct

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Revenue growing faster than costs creates operating leverage; the 150-200bps margin expansion target is achievable if revenue grows at guided 1-2% and costs grow at 0.8-1%; the math works mechanically [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Margin expansion requires no meaningful competitive price erosion in Greece; Vodafone Greece and Wind Hellas have been mild competitors but could become more aggressive; this is an assumption management does not stress-test publicly [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: OTE's effective Greek corporate tax rate is 22%; DTA recapture from prior losses reduces cash tax materially in some years but not others; management does not guide tax explicitly and DTA timing is the largest EPS uncertainty [#A]

  • Sub: Greek DTA balance ~€120m remaining as of FY2024; recapture timing will influence EPS by ~€0.03-0.06/share per year non-linearly

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count is essentially stable; OTE has been buying back minority float with DT holding ~48.3%; buybacks are modest and unlikely to move EPS per share materially; share count assumption is the least risky EPS input [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt ~€1.2bn (1.0x EBITDA-AL); interest cost guided stable; refinancing risk is low near-term; no material capital structure assumption risk [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: Greek GDP growth +2.0-2.5% assumed for FY25-27; IMF November 2024 forecast for Greece +2.3% FY25 — management is aligned with consensus macro; Romanian GDP +3.0% assumed, consistent with IMF forecast [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Romanian leu stability vs EUR is assumed; OTE reports group results in EUR; RON has been broadly stable L3Y but carries political risk (Romania's fiscal position is under EU scrutiny in 2025); a 5% RON devaluation would impact group EBITDA-AL by ~€8-10m [#A]

[#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH on operational EBITDA and FCF; MEDIUM on EPS; LOW on medium-term (N3Y) aspirational targets given the 2-5% gap with SS consensus and the track record of aspirational timelines slipping [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: EBITDA-AL beat in FY2024 and FY2023; FCF beat in all L4 periods; the pattern is consistent enough to be structural rather than incidental [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: FCF guidance uses "at least" floor language consistently; CapEx guidance is always stated as a range with actuals landing at or below the low end; these are deliberately conservative structural features [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Greek business has delivered on or above EBITDA guidance for 4 consecutive years (FY2021-FY2024); this is a clean Greece-specific execution record [#A]

  • Green flag — DT parent governance: Deutsche Telekom ownership (~48.3%) instils conservative guidance culture; OTE guidance philosophy mirrors DT group's own disciplined approach; parent oversight reduces management incentive to stretch targets [#A]

  • Red flag — Romanian execution: Romania has been a consistent guidance risk for 3 of L4 years; management continues to assume Romanian stabilisation on approximately the same timeline each year — the assumption has been wrong more than right [#A]
