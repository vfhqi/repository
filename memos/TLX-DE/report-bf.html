Core Business + Multi-Brand Architecture

1.
Decentralised composite holding: Talanx AG is a listed financial and management holding company that coordinates a highly diversified global insurance and financial services group. Operating functions are structured into six reportable segments: Corporate & Specialty, Retail International, Retail Germany, Property/Casualty Reinsurance, Life/Health Reinsurance, and Group Operations. Operational units maintain a high level of entrepreneurial freedom to make pricing and underwriting decisions independently. 36 37
2.
Multi-brand architecture: The group manages distinct brands tailored to specific capital market, corporate, and retail target audiences. The corporate parent Talanx brand is oriented toward capital markets, while operational divisions deploy customer-facing B2B and B2C brands. Global corporate lines are written under the HDI brand, while reinsurance is transacted via Hannover Re and E+S Rück SE. Domestic German retail operations leverage specialized bancassurance brands including neue leben and LifeStyle Protection. International retail markets deploy powerful local brands including Warta and TU Europa in Poland, and Yelum (formerly Liberty Seguros) in Brazil. 39
3.
Balanced division of earnings: Talanx has successfully established a structural balance where group earnings are split approximately 50:50 between Primary Insurance and Reinsurance. The reinsurance business group consists of a 50.2% majority stake in listed global tier-1 reinsurer Hannover Re SE. In FY2025, reinsurance contributed EUR 1.319B (approx. 50% of group net income after minorities), while primary insurance segments collectively contributed EUR 1.335B (approx. 50%). - Earnings Calls Strategic parent role: Talanx AG operates as the central strategic manager, capital allocator, and internal group reinsurer. In 2019, Talanx obtained a primary reinsurance license from the German Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin) to pool primary group requirements. Pooling primary insurance cessions at the holding level allows Talanx to exploit diversification effects and optimize capital efficiency. Group operations also encompass Ampega Asset Management GmbH, which oversees the group's EUR 100B+ investment portfolio
4.
Ultimate mutual ownership: Talanx AG's majority shareholder is HDI Haftpflichtverband der Deutschen Industrie V.a.G., a mutual insurance association with over 120 years of history. HDI V.a.G. holds 76.74% of Talanx AG's shares, with the remaining 23.26% held in free float. This mutual structure provides Talanx with long-term stability and structural protection, shielding the group from short-term market pressures. 55
