§2 — COMPANY DETAILS

J-front verdict: Avanza's revenue model is best understood as a high-fixed-cost, scalable platform where NII and fund commissions provide recurring base revenue and brokerage/FX commissions are the high-beta, customer-activity-driven layer. ISK/IPS wrappers provide structural stickiness that is meaningfully higher than raw transfer cost economics suggest. FY2025 revenue architecture is now confirmed from primary company sources.

2.1 Full Revenue Breakdown

For full-year 2025, total operating income reached SEK 4,495m. [AS·company] (Avanza Investor Presentation, 07-Apr-26 and 06-May-26):

  • Net interest income : 35% / SEK 1,577m — primary revenue engine, driven by the Riksbank policy rate spread on deposit and lending book.
  • Net brokerage: 27% / SEK 1,201m — commission income from equity, ETF, and derivatives trades; now the second largest pillar.
  • Net fund commissions: 18% / SEK 818m — distribution kickbacks from ~1,300 third-party funds plus management fees on 23 proprietary Avanza Fonder funds.
  • Currency/FX: 15% / SEK 661m — FX spread on foreign securities trading; growing as Swedish retail investors increase allocation to global equities.
  • Other: 5% / SEK 238m — Avanza Markets, stock lending, corporate finance, distribution compensation.

[C]: The revenue mix evolution is positive — brokerage and FX are growing faster than NII in absolute terms, progressively reducing NIM concentration. However, both brokerage and FX are partially bull-market-dependent, creating the scenario where all revenue lines could face simultaneous pressure in a rate-cut + bear-market environment.

2.2 Net Interest Margin Mechanics

Avanza's NII is driven by a large, low-cost customer deposit base reinvested into treasury assets and internally financed lending. [AS·company] (Q1 2026 Interim Report): as of early 2026, customer deposits totalled approximately SEK 113.8bn; loan book SEK 31bn (Private Banking mortgages + margin lending); surplus liquidity ~SEK 94bn invested in covered bonds, municipal bonds, and at the Riksbank, carrying average interest rate duration of up to three months.

In Q1 2026, Avanza paid an average annualised interest rate of 0.76% to customers on deposits, while earning an average of 2.80% on its internally financed lending portfolio — a spread of ~204bps. [AS·Broker] DNB Carnegie notes NII relies heavily on internal deposit volumes and expanding spreads on the treasury portfolio.

Rate sensitivity (quantified from primary disclosure):
- A 100bps decrease in policy rate → SEK -450m impact on full-year NII (holding volumes constant). [AS·company] (Prospectus, 22-Apr-26)
- A 100bps increase in policy rate → up to SEK +1,170m impact on full-year NII. [AS·company] (Q3 2024 Earnings Call / INTRM)
- Per 25bps cut: approximately SEK -112.5m annualised (calculated).
- As market rates approach zero, an increasing portion of Avanza's deposit volumes hits a zero-bound floor — the bank will not pass negative rates to retail depositors, exacerbating downside NII sensitivity. [AS·company] (Q3 2024 Earnings Call)

[C]: The asymmetry in the rate sensitivity is striking — upside from hikes (up to SEK 1,170m) is ~2.6x the stated downside from cuts (SEK 450m). This reflects the zero-bound floor problem: Avanza cannot recoup NII below zero-rate by passing negative rates. Management demonstrated partial mitigation in 2024–2025 via: (1) deposit volume growth, (2) margin loan growth, (3) slower rate pass-through to customers than policy rate decline. [AS·Broker] DNB Carnegie (07-Feb-25) notes Avanza managed the easing cycle with "better-than-expected pricing power, effectively shielding profitability by aggressively passing policy rate cuts directly onto the interest rates it offers on customer savings accounts." The sustainability of this lag strategy is finite.

2.3 Commission Structure

Avanza monetises trading activity through a tiered, volume-based brokerage structure. [AS·company] (Annual Report 2025): "Start" tier — 0% commission on Stockholm Stock Exchange trades for accounts with <SEK 50,000 savings capital. Beyond this, variable fees: 0.25% (Mini class, SEK 1 minimum) down to 0.055% for higher-tier accounts (SEK 59 minimum). Fund trades are entirely commission-free on the front end.

[AS·Broker] Deutsche Bank Research (06-Aug-25, Swissquote initiation): Swissquote, Avanza, and Nordnet share a strategic alignment focused on mass-affluent investors, distinguishing them from flatexDEGIRO which caters to cost-sensitive high-frequency clientele.

[AS·Broker] Deutsche Bank Research (European online brokers, 17-Oct-22): The EU's ban on Payment For Order Flow under MiFID II prevents international zero-commission entrants from fully replicating US monetisation mechanics in European markets, structurally defending Avanza's transparent commission model. PFOF ban strictly enforced from summer 2026. [C]: This regulatory protection is a genuine structural advantage — it differentiates the European neobroker model from US disruption patterns. Avanza does not monetise order flow routing and is structurally unaffected by the PFOF ban; the ban exclusively penalises zero-commission entrants like eToro and Robinhood EU.

2.4 The ISK Account Moat

The Investeringssparkonto is a cornerstone of the Swedish retail investing landscape and a critical component of Avanza's structural moat. [AS·company] (INTRM multiple): since its 2012 introduction, the ISK has become the most popular tax wrapper for securities in Sweden, replacing per-transaction capital gains tax with a low standardised annual yield tax calculated on the total account balance. Savings within ISK wrappers have grown at ~20% CAGR over recent years.

Switching friction mechanics:
- Transferring assets between institutions within an ISK framework involves complex administrative processes. Technically, assets within an ISK can be transferred between institutions, but liquidating and moving cash manually risks triggering double taxation events under the ISK's standardised return tax calculation. [AS·company] (Interactive Brokers ISK press release, 30-Sep-25; cited in AS).
- Occupational pension and traditional life insurance segments face even higher switching friction — complex, administratively demanding processes and manual signature requirements continue to lock customers into legacy agreements. [AS·company] (Q1 2026 Interim Report).
- Incumbent banks leverage their balance sheets to create additional friction through product bundling: conditioning competitive mortgage rates on customers maintaining primary investment/pension accounts at the bank. [AS·company] (Q2 2025 Earnings Call).

Policy tailwind — ISK tax-exempt ceiling doubled:
From January 1, 2026, the Swedish government increased the ISK tax-exempt ceiling from SEK 150,000 to SEK 300,000. [AS·Broker] DNB Carnegie views this as a significant structural tailwind, lowering barriers to entry and making the ISK optimal for even low-risk products like fixed-income funds. The expansion is expected to drive new account generation and increases in average deposit sizes.

[C]: The ISK/IPS moat operates on two levels: (1) financial friction from transfer complexity and double-taxation risk; (2) behavioural friction from Avanza's integrated UX — customers who use Avanza Global (0.08% TER), follow Placera, and trade on the Avanza app face a genuine UX downgrade if they switch. The behavioural dimension is a more durable form of lock-in than raw transfer cost economics.

2.5 Fund Platform Economics

Avanza operates an open-architecture fund platform distributing over 1,300 external funds alongside approximately 23 proprietary funds managed by Avanza Fonder. [AS·company] (Annual Report 2025): by late 2025, internal funds accounted for 38% of total fund capital. Fund margin yields approximately 23.3–24.9 basis points per SEK of fund capital, though facing slight downward pressure as investors increasingly allocate to low-cost index funds (surpassed 52% of total fund assets in Q1 2026).

Revenue from fund distribution comes from distribution kickbacks (revenue-sharing agreements) from external asset managers and management fees on internal funds.

Regulatory risk — MiFID II / RIS inducements: While a blanket EU-wide ban on inducements was avoided in the December 2025 RIS political agreement, the Swedish FSA is actively investigating national rules on commissions. [AS·company] (Q4 2025 Earnings Call / Q1 2026 Interim Report): management acknowledges that a national ban on fund kickbacks "would severely disrupt Avanza's external fund distribution and Avanza Markets economics, forcing the company to radically adapt its business model, likely by introducing direct platform or custody fees." This is a material tail risk affecting ~18% of revenue. [#A]

[C]: Avanza's response to potential kickback bans would likely mirror the Nordnet and Swissquote playbook — introducing explicit custody fees or platform subscription tiers. The business model is adaptable, but a transition year would create revenue uncertainty.

2.6 Flywheel vs. Treadmill Assessment

[AS·company] (Annual Report 2024): empirical evidence points strongly to a flywheel dynamic. Because ~60% of operating costs are fixed (personnel and proprietary IT infrastructure), incremental asset growth drops aggressively to the bottom line. Management has driven cost-to-savings-capital ratio from 14.5bps (2024) to 14.1bps (2025). [AS·Broker] DNB Carnegie (Avanza Buy note, 04-Jul-24) explicitly identifies this as a "virtuous circle" — cost-efficient and highly scalable platform enables Avanza to absorb targeted price reductions, driving structural growth and further cementing dominance.

[C]: Flywheel classification confirmed — not a treadmill. Evidence: ROE has expanded (not compressed) as scale has grown; operating margin has risen from ~47% (2016) to ~70% (2025); customer satisfaction has held #1 for 16 consecutive years despite the customer base quadrupling since 2010. The flywheel compresses over time: at 8.3% market share, incremental gains become harder. The private banking, pension, and Denmark expansion are the next flywheel-expansion levers.

2.7 Revenue Source — FX/Currency (15% of FY2025 income)

Income from foreign securities trading FX spread. Grows with customer activity in international stocks (US equities, ETFs). [C]: FX income is high-margin (spread product) and has been growing as Swedish retail investors increasingly trade global equities — in FY2025 FX reached 15% of total revenue vs. ~12% in 2024, reflecting the ongoing internationalisation of Swedish retail investment portfolios.

2.8 Customer Retention and Churn

[AS·company] (Q1 2026 Interim Report): empirical customer churn rate of 0.9% on rolling 12-month basis as of Q1 2026, improving from 1.3% at end-2025. [C]: Avanza does not historically publicise churn — this primary-source disclosure is an important data point. The 0.9% annualised churn rate is exceptionally low for a financial platform. Cross-referencing against 2.31m customers adding 171k/year net = implied gross churn consistent with <5% and potentially sub-1% at the portfolio level. NPS of 41 vs. banking industry average of 3 — [AS·company] (Q4 2025 Earnings Call) — is a direct retention driver.

2.9 Scale Economy

[C]: Avanza operates a fixed-cost digital platform — incremental customers cost essentially nothing beyond KYC/regulatory overhead. Cost-to-AuC ratio was 14.1bps in 2025 (down from 14.5bps in 2024) — best-in-cohort efficiency metric. Each SEK 100bn of new AuC adds limited cost but generates meaningful fund commission and NII revenue. With ~697 employees (Q1 2026) serving 2.31m customers, headcount per customer is approximately 1 per 3,300 customers — extraordinary leverage. [AS·company] (Investor Presentation): "roll out design changes in minutes and maintain a high platform availability rate of 99.9%."

§3 — INDUSTRY CONTEXT

J-front verdict: Swedish retail savings is a structurally growing market (ISK adoption, pension reform tailwinds, demographic wealth transfer) with a highly concentrated online segment dominated by Avanza and Nordnet. Incumbent bank digital push is real but has repeatedly failed to dislodge the specialists. Rate cycle is the primary near-term macro variable. Regulatory risk is low-moderate — PFOF ban helps Avanza; RIS/kickback reform is the tail risk.

3.1 Swedish Savings Market Structure

The total Swedish savings market is estimated at approximately SEK 13,000bn. [AS·Broker] Deutsche Bank Research (Nordic Banks, 02-Sep-24): Swedbank and Svenska Handelsbanken each hold approximately 20% of retail market share, largely through legacy mortgage lending franchises. There is a continued structural migration of customers from traditional institutions to digital platforms.

Avanza has established an 8.3% structural market share of total Swedish savings capital, with a 11.3-11.4% share of net structural inflows during 2025. [AS·company] (Q1 2026 Interim Report; Investor Presentation). [C]: Avanza (8.3%) and Nordnet (~7-8%) together hold ~16% of Swedish savings capital — the highest combined neo-broker market share of any European savings market. The combined duopoly share has been gaining ~80-100bps per year.

[AS·company] (Expert Call, Former Head of Analytics at Avanza, 07-Sep-25): "Avanza dominates domestic customer acquisition, capturing roughly nine out of every ten new digital broker accounts opened in Sweden."

3.2 Competitive Dynamics — Avanza vs. Nordnet

[AS·Broker] DNB Carnegie (14-Jul-25): Nordnet acts as Avanza's closest peer and primary neobroker rival, offering a highly similar product suite but operating across a broader pan-Nordic footprint (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland).

Despite Nordnet's regional scale, Avanza leads on:
- Swedish customer satisfaction — 16 consecutive SKI #1 awards
- NNA share capture — 11.4% of Swedish net flows vs. 8.3% stock
- Domestic brand trust — ranked alongside Volvo and IKEA in independent consumer surveys [AS·company] (Q2 2025 Earnings Presentation)

[C]: Nordnet's structural advantage is geographic diversification — four Nordic markets with different rate cycles provides NIM hedging that Avanza lacks. However, Avanza's Swedish market depth extracts more value per customer in its home market. DNB Carnegie (Avanza Buy, 22-Apr-26: "Crossing the Øresund") explicitly prefers Avanza over Nordnet for this reason. The Denmark expansion directly attacks Nordnet's geographic advantage and begins the process of replicating Nordnet's multi-market hedge.

[Analytical disagreement] [C] view: Nordnet added 256k new customers in 2024 vs. Avanza's 97k, indicating aggressive marketing investment and faster headline growth. [AS] view per [AS·company] (DNB Carnegie, 13-Apr-26): over the trailing 12 months into Q1 2026, Avanza attracted 164,000 new clients (+8% YoY). The disagreement centres on the measurement period — [C] used FY2024 data; [AS] uses TTM to Q1 2026. Both are consistent with Nordnet's structurally faster gross customer growth rate due to pan-Nordic TAM.

3.3 New Entrant Risk

[C]: Genuine new entrant risk is low for a full-scale competitor. Barriers include: banking licence (Avanza is a licensed bank, not just a broker), SFSA (Finansinspektionen) regulatory approval, Swedish language/cultural requirements, and ISK/IPS technical integration complexity.

[AS·company] (Press Releases): eToro recently expanded coverage to Nasdaq Nordic equities; Interactive Brokers launched a localised ISK product (Sep-2025). [AS·company] (Expert Call, VP at SAVR, 24-Dec-24): "international players generally struggle to gain meaningful traction in Sweden simply by competing on price. Winning market share against Avanza requires overcoming immense local trust barriers and matching a highly localized product ecosystem that foreign entrants lack."
