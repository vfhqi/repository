  • Founder-led culture is a structural asset: Marc Bürki (co-founder 1999, CEO since) has driven a product-first, software-engineer-led culture through three crypto cycles; skin-in-the-game as founding shareholder; consistent track record of exceeding medium-term profit guidance. [#J]

  • ⚡ RARE: Revenue-per-account outlier. CHF ~1,020 revenue per account (2024, 650k accounts, CHF 664m revenue) versus Avanza ~SEK 4,500 (~CHF 390) and flatexDEGIRO ~€150 (~CHF 145). §1 — GENERAL

Swissquote is Switzerland's dominant online brokerage bank, holding a full FINMA banking licence since 2000, offering a uniquely diversified platform spanning equity trading, crypto custody and exchange, eForex, robo-advisory (LEON/Invest Easy), ePrivate banking, and multi-currency savings — a revenue model materially richer than any direct peer in the neobroker cohort.

  • Verdict: Best-in-cohort revenue diversification and highest revenue-per-account; pre-tax margin (~52%) is best-in-cohort; but earnings quality is mixed — ~52% transactional, rate-sensitive, and crypto-cycle-exposed. [#J]

  • Business identity: Swiss-licensed bank and online brokerage platform founded 1996 (Marc Bürki and Paolo Buzzi); listed on SIX Swiss Exchange (SQN) since 2000; headquartered Gland, Vaud; 1,217 employees at end-2024; full FINMA banking licence held since 2000. [#I]

  • Peer headcount: Avanza ~600 employees, Nordnet ~1,000, flatexDEGIRO ~500 — Swissquote's larger headcount reflects banking operations, compliance, and treasury overhead absent in pure broker models.

  • Scale: CHF 76.3bn client assets (end-2024); 650,089 core SQN accounts; plus 399,201 Yuh accounts (post Jul-25 100% acquisition); 3m+ tradeable products across 60 exchanges; 52 crypto assets on SQX exchange. [#I]

  • Peer context: Avanza SEK 930bn (~CHF 80bn) at end-2024; Nordnet SEK 920bn (~CHF 79bn); flatexDEGIRO €60bn (~CHF 58bn). Swissquote broadly on par with Swedish neobrokers for AuC but with one-third the client count, indicating far higher assets-per-account (~CHF 117k vs ~CHF 30k for Avanza).

§2 — COMPANY DETAILS

  • Verdict: Revenue model is genuinely more diversified than any cohort peer, with five distinct income lines and an institutional business (eForex, Malta custody) unavailable to peers; but only NIM and robo-advisory fees are structurally recurring — the rest (eForex, crypto, trading commissions) are transactional and cycle-dependent. [#J]

  • Revenue model architecture — five pillars:

Pillar 1 — Net Interest Income (NIM, ~32% of 2024 revenue):
- NIM source: Spread between client cash deposit rates paid and reinvestment in Swiss money market and bonds. Swiss banking licence enables Swissquote to act as direct banker, not intermediary, capturing full NIM. [#I]
- Rate trajectory: SNB policy rate: -0.75% pre-2022 → +1.75% peak (2023) → 0.25% current (post Mar-25 cut). NII jumped from CHF 16m (2021) → CHF 74m (2022) → CHF 213m (2023), then +5.2% to ~CHF 224m in 2024 despite 125bps of cuts — protected by AuC/deposit volume growth and multi-currency mix. [#A]
- Multi-currency hedge: Client cash held in CHF, EUR, USD, GBP — USD rates still 4%+ in 2024 buffered CHF rate decline. Structurally different from Avanza (SEK-only NIM) and Nordnet (SEK-dominated). [#A]
- Rate cut sensitivity (100bps CHF): Rough estimate — every 100bps CHF cut reduces CHF-leg NIM by ~CHF 30-40m; USD/EUR legs partially offset. Net impact maybe -CHF 15-25m, or ~4% of pre-tax profit — manageable but not trivial. [#A]
- SNB at near-zero: March 2025 cut to 0.25%; further cuts to 0% are possible given Swiss deflationary tendencies. CHF NIM effectively floored. ❌ [#J]
- Bear scenario: SNB returns to -0.50% (CHF safe-haven appreciation in geopolitical shock). CHF-leg NIM drops to zero or negative; USD/EUR legs only partially offset. Net NIM headwind ~CHF 20-30m. ❌ [#A]
- Bull scenario: USD Federal Reserve rate cycle inflects higher; USD deposit leg NIM expands. Swissquote uniquely positioned among European retail brokers to benefit from higher-for-longer USD. [#J]

Pillar 2 — Net Fee and Commission Income (~27% of 2024 revenue, CHF 178m):
- Driver: Equity, ETF, and fund transaction commissions across 60 exchanges; quarterly custody fees on CHF 76bn AuC; fund retrocessions on LEON/Invest Easy. [#I]
- Growth: +24.7% in 2024, driven by growing investor appetite for foreign securities and larger AuC base (custody fees scale linearly with assets). [#A]
- Recurring element: Custody fees (quarterly, AuC-linked) are semi-recurring; transaction commissions are flow-dependent. Estimated 40-50% of this pillar is genuinely recurring. [#A]

Pillar 3 — Net eForex Income (~14% of 2024 revenue, CHF 95m):
- Model: Swissquote runs one of Europe's largest bank-to-bank eForex desks — institutional white-label FX service for regional banks, broker-dealers, and wealth managers. Over 90 bank partners. [#I]
- Dynamics: Revenue highly sensitive to FX volatility. 2024: -6.4% decline as FX volatility was subdued. 2022 was a strong year (Ukraine/SNB shock). ❌ [#A]
- Competitive strength: Swissquote is a top-10 European eForex provider by volume; this franchise is unique in the cohort — no peer has an institutional FX desk of this scale. [#J]
- Recurring quality: Essentially transactional/flow. Revenue can halve in low-volatility years vs high-volatility years. [#A]

Pillar 4 — Net Crypto Assets Income (~13% of 2024 revenue, CHF 85.5m):
- Components: Crypto trading commissions, crypto custody fees, SQX exchange volumes (launched Oct-22), crypto staking revenues (4 assets). [#I]
- Cycle history: CHF 102m (2021 boom) → CHF 28m (2022 crypto winter) → CHF 19m (2023 low volatility) → CHF 85.5m (2024, +353%, Bitcoin >$100k). Extreme volatility — 5x range between trough and peak. ❌ [#A]
- ⚡ RARE: Only FINMA-regulated full banking institution with a proprietary crypto exchange (SQX). Regulatory moat — Swiss banking + FINMA crypto custody — is structurally hard to replicate; EU peers face a multi-year regulatory gap. [#J]
- SQX exchange: Launched Oct-22; institutional-grade Swiss-domiciled exchange; 52 crypto assets; 4 staking options; Crypto Finance AG (Deutsche Börse subsidiary) partnership for institutional liquidity. [#I]

Pillar 5 — Net Trading Income (ex-eForex, ex-crypto, ~14% of 2024 revenue):
- Driver: FX trading gains on client flows, treasury activity, structured products. [#I]
- Growth: +48.7% in 2024, driven by stronger foreign-currency trading activity. Lumpy and not independently disclosed in granular detail. [#A]

  • Current state: All five revenue pillars operational and scaled; question is which grows from here and which mean-reverts. [#J]

  • Institutional client segment — underappreciated pillar:

  • Beyond retail, Swissquote serves 90+ institutional bank partners (eForex), fund managers (Malta depositary/custody), and high-value expat/UHNW clients (ePrivate Banking, Dubai DIFC). [#I]
  • Institutional revenues are stickier than retail — longer contractual relationships, higher switching costs, technology embedding. Estimated 20-30% of group revenue. [#J]

  • This is structurally differentiated from Avanza, Nordnet, and flatexDEGIRO which are 95%+ retail-derived. [#A]

  • LEON is Swissquote's algorithmic robo-advisor; Invest Easy is the simplified version (savings/ETF strategies). Fee-based recurring revenue (management fee on AuM). [#I]
  • Robo/savings accounts +14.9% in 2023. AuM not disclosed separately but estimated mid-single CHF billion. [#A]

  • Background: Yuh was 50/50 JV with PostFinance; neobank targeting younger Swiss retail segment (Twint-linked, mobile-first, lower fees). 399,201 accounts at end-2025. [#I]
  • Strategic rationale: Expands TAM downward to mass-market Swiss; PostFinance's 2m+ customers as potential funnel; two-brand Swiss market coverage. [#J]
  • Economics: Revenue per Yuh account estimated CHF 30-50 (vs CHF 1,020 for SQN core). Structurally dilutive to group margin near-term; long-term cross-sell optionality if Yuh clients accumulate wealth and graduate to SQN platform. [#A]
  • Risk: PostFinance referral channel may be disrupted post-divestiture if commercial agreement not maintained. ❌ [#J]

  • Data gap: Management has not disclosed Yuh standalone financials — a KQ for subsequent research stages. [#A]

  • Swiss crypto regulatory landscape — unique enabler:

  • Switzerland enacted the DLT Act (2021) recognising crypto as a distinct asset class with clear legal ownership. FINMA provides explicit licensing for crypto custody — 3-4 years ahead of EU regulatory clarity. [#I]
  • This enabled Swissquote to build SQX exchange and obtain FINMA crypto custody approval before EU peers could act. Institutional clients who chose Swissquote for FINMA-regulated crypto custody face multi-year switching friction. [#J]
  • EU MiCA (live Jan-25) begins aligning EU with Swiss standard — but EU institutions seeking Swiss legal certainty and FINMA oversight still prefer Swissquote's Swiss domicile. This advantage narrows over time but is durable for 3-5 years. [#A]

  • Crypto Finance AG (Deutsche Börse subsidiary) partnership: adds Deutsche Börse's institutional distribution network to Swissquote's custody infrastructure — significant for European institutional deal flow. [#J]

  • Recent 3-year delivery (2022-2024):

  • 2023: Revenue +30% to CHF 531m; NIM tripled to CHF 213m; client assets record CHF 58bn; NNA CHF 7.4bn. [#A]
  • 2024: Revenue +25% to CHF 664m; crypto boom (+353% crypto revenue); NNA CHF 8.3bn record; pre-tax profit CHF 346m (+35%). [#A]

  • Pattern: Every year in the 3-year window delivered record results — but driven by at least one external tailwind each year. [#J]

  • Non-transaction revenue share: Management guides "48% non-transaction-based revenues" in 2024 — NIM plus recurring fees. Better than flatexDEGIRO (~30%) but below Avanza (~60% including NIM). [#A]

  • 2026 investment cycle: Swissquote flagged heavy investment in technology, data engineering, and AI for 2026 — driving 2026 pre-tax profit guidance below 2025 actual (CHF 385m guidance vs CHF 420m achieved). Management frames it as front-loaded spend with payback in H2-26 and 2027. [#J]

  • Sceptic bullet: With CHF rates at near-zero and crypto cycle potentially maturing, the two biggest growth drivers of 2023-24 (NIM surge + crypto boom) face mean-reversion. The 2028 CHF 500m profit target requires ~8-9% compound profit growth from 2025's CHF 420m base — achievable but depends on new growth vectors (Yuh monetisation, AI, international) delivering before rate tailwinds fully unwind. ❌ [#J]

  • Swiss wealth context — structural tailwind:

  • Switzerland has the highest financial assets per capita globally (~CHF 250k per adult). Swissquote's ~650k accounts represent ~8% of Swiss adult population — large penetration but with 90%+ of retail savings still at traditional banks. TAM penetration remains low. [#A]
