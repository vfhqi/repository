SECTION 3: COMPANY DESCRIPTION AND CONTEXT

Basic-Fit is Europe's largest low-cost gym operator by clubs and members. The company was founded in 1999 in the Netherlands, listed on Euronext Amsterdam in 2016, and has grown from ~200 clubs at IPO to over 2,100 clubs by Q1 2026.

Business model: the company operates owned gyms (predominantly) and, since the Clever Fit acquisition, franchise gyms. Revenue is primarily subscription-based (monthly memberships at €19.99–€34.99), with ancillary revenue from vending, app subscriptions, and personal training. The membership model provides exceptional revenue visibility — churn is the primary risk, but BFIT's low price point creates strong retention.

Scale: Q1 2026 shows 6.0m members across 2,184 clubs in 12 countries (Netherlands, France, Belgium, Spain, Germany, Luxembourg, Austria + Clever Fit's footprint in 6 additional countries including Switzerland and Central/Eastern Europe).

L3Y performance summary:
- FY2023: Revenue ~€1,050m, positive FCF emerging
- FY2024: Revenue €1,215m (+16%), underlying EBITDA €313m, net leverage 2.6x
- FY2025: Revenue €1,420m (+17%), underlying EBITDA €348m (+11%), FCF positive (~€26m), net leverage ~3.0x (post-Clever Fit)

Share price L3Y: the stock declined from ~€40+ in 2022 to a low of €10.52 (all-time low 2020) and recovered to a recent range of €19.47–€34.76 over the past 12 months. The 52-week high of €34.76 was set in a period of strong earnings momentum.

Strategic initiatives underway: Clever Fit integration, France 24/7 rollout, franchise model expansion under BFIT brand, club density optimisation in Netherlands/Belgium (mature markets), growth acceleration in Spain, Germany, France.

SECTION 5: REVENUE AND ORGANIC GROWTH

FY2025 total revenue of €1,420m represents +17% growth. The key question is the organic/inorganic split.

Organic drivers:
- 85 net new owned clubs added in FY2025 (vs ~100 target — slightly short, but principally timing-driven).
- ARPM grew from €24.24 to €24.91 (+2.8%).
- Members grew from ~4.0m (Q4 2024) to ~4.9m (Q4 2025) for owned clubs — approximately +22.5% in the membership base.
- Volume (members) grew more than ARPM — the growth is primarily volume-driven.

Inorganic driver:
- Clever Fit contributed ~6 weeks of consolidated revenue in FY2025 (November–December 2025). The contribution was modest in the FY2025 number but will be material in FY2026.

ARPM trajectory analysis: The €24.91 ARPM reflects a mix of Basic (€19.99–24.99) and Premium (€29.99–34.99) memberships. Premium adoption above 50% of new joiners, per industry commentary, is the single most important pricing lever. Continued mix-shift to Premium adds €5–10/member/month on the incremental joiner.

Member per club density: Q1 2026 average of 2,981 members/club (+8% YoY from 2,764). This is the purest operating leverage metric — more revenue over the same fixed cost base. The trajectory (if sustained) adds ~€30m/year in EBITDA per 100 basis points of density improvement.

Forward guidance credibility on revenue: The €1.64–1.69bn guide implies Clever Fit franchise revenue contributes ~€80–100m in FY2026. Given the January-March actual of €396m (which included ~€20–25m in franchise revenue), the full-year trajectory is coherent.

Sceptical view: Organic revenue growth for owned clubs could slow from 17% to 10–12% in 2026 as the base grows larger. The top-line growth rate for FY2026 is heavily influenced by the Clever Fit consolidation. Ex-Clever Fit, the organic story is solid but not spectacular.

Rate: **GREEN * — volume-driven growth with ARPM tailwind is the right combination.

SECTION 9: STRATEGIC EXECUTION

Clever Fit integration: Completed November 2025. First reported months show profitability improvement. Management is exploring BFIT-branded franchise rollout using the Clever Fit infrastructure. Integration risk: IT systems, brand standards, franchisee relationship management, and cross-selling capabilities are all in early stages.

France 24/7: Regulatory approval received Q1 2026. Implementation begins May 2026 with 50 clubs. Full rollout of 200 qualifying clubs to follow. The €10m annualised saving is additive to EBITDA. Further regulatory amendments are targeted but not confirmed. This is the highest-quality execution catalyst in the 2026 story — it depends on management logistics, not business development.

Club rollout pace: Net 50 owned clubs in 2026 (vs 85 net owned + 39 Clever Fit in 2025). The reduced pace reflects a deliberate capital efficiency focus — management is prioritising FCF over top-line growth. This is a positive strategic signal.

Spain expansion: Spain remains a high-growth market for BFIT (230 clubs at year-end 2025). The company is second behind Vivagym in Spain. Planet Fitness entered Spain in 2024 with one club — too early to represent a competitive threat. Spain club economics are attractive (lower rents, lower labour costs than France).

Germany: Post-Clever Fit, BFIT leads Germany with ~450 clubs. Germany is the largest opportunity in the footprint given population density and current penetration levels. However, Germany club economics have historically been tighter than France or Belgium due to competitive intensity.

Sceptical view: executing simultaneously on Clever Fit integration, France regulatory rollout, Spain expansion, and convertible bond management is a substantial management bandwidth demand. Any one of these workstreams encountering friction creates a queue of priorities that could delay the others.

Rate: YELLOW-GREEN (Y/G) — strong pipeline of execution catalysts, but complexity is high.

SECTION 10: SCEPTICAL / BEARISH SUMMARY

Full bear case on FY2025 results and 2026 guidance:

The reported earnings quality is poor. Underlying net profit of €54.3m versus reported of €14.3m — a €40m gap representing ~3% of revenue. At this scale, every non-cash adjustment is material. The convertible bond amortisation catch-up alone was €10.8m in H1 2025. These are not "clean" earnings — they are deeply adjusted.

The leverage position is uncomfortable. Post-Clever Fit, net debt excluding leases is ~€1.2bn, implying net debt/EBITDA of ~3.4x on FY2025 EBITDA. The June 2026 bond put crystallises €303.7m in potential demand — even with €290m in committed facilities, this leaves the balance sheet under significant stress if EBITDA delivery is weak. The bank facilities at commercial lending rates will carry a meaningfully higher interest rate than the convertible bonds they replace, compressing net profit further.

The FCF of €26m in FY2025 is positive but underwhelming given the scale of the business. After subtracting the Clever Fit acquisition cash payment, underlying FCF was approximately €180–200m — that is a more honest representation. The reported FCF of €26m is acquisition-distorted, not reflective of run-rate.

Top 3 risks:
1. Convertible bond put exercise at scale + elevated refinancing costs — could add €15–25m/year in incremental finance costs, eliminating reported profit
2. Clever Fit franchise revenue miss — if franchisees churn or underperform, the €80–100m revenue assumption for 2026 does not hold
3. France energy cost spike or consumer confidence deterioration — would simultaneously pressure ARPM and new joiner acquisition in the single largest market

Early warning signals: (1) H1 2026 EBITDA below €185m; (2) Group memberships below 5.5m at H1 2026 trading update; (3) France 24/7 implementation delayed beyond Q3 2026; (4) Any public guidance cut

Rate downside risk: **YELLOW * — meaningful downside exists but is not the base case.
