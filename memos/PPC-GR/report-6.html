  • Consensus gross margin: Not separately disclosed in most models (utility segment accounting); EBITDA margin is the primary metric [#A]
  • Consensus EBIT margin: FY25E ~18–20%; FY26E ~20–22%; FY27E ~22–25% — expansion driven by RES mix and HEDNO RAB growth [#A]
  • Consensus EBITDA margin: FY25E ~25–26%; FY26E ~27–28%; FY27E ~28–30% — consensus assumption of ~400bp expansion over 3 years [#J]
  • Consensus net margin: FY25E ~8–9%; FY26E ~10–12% — meaningful improvement but still modest vs European integrated utility peers at 10–15% [#I]
  • Margin trend assumed: Expanding across all active buy-rated analysts; Mediobanca (Hold) models more modest margin expansion, assuming higher provisioning and financing costs [#J]
  • Productivity assumption: Cost reduction of €150–200mn cumulative by FY27 flagged by management; analysts broadly credit 70–80% of this in models [#A]
  • Pricing assumption: RES capacity captures higher-than-market pricing via long-term PPAs — analysts assume ~70–80% of new RES capacity under PPAs or CfD structures [#I]
  • Mix assumption: Key margin driver — RES carries higher EBITDA margins (~50–60%) vs supply/retail (~3–5%); as RES share of EBITDA grows, blended margin improves structurally [#J]
  • Operating leverage: 3 brokers (DB, JPM, GS) explicitly model positive operating leverage from fixed cost base against growing RES revenue; implied incremental EBITDA margins on RES additions of 55–65% [#A]
  • Fixed cost: Analysts assume fixed cost base grows modestly (+2–3% p.a.) as RES operations are largely automated; heritage thermal fixed costs declining as plants retire [#I]
  • One-off vs structural: Majority of modelled margin improvement characterised as structural (mix shift + operating leverage); Mediobanca flags ~€80mn of one-off labour costs related to lignite phase-out that others may be under-provisioning [#J]
  • Margin downgrade: ❌ No active broker has downgraded margin assumptions in L12M — all revisions have been upgrades or flat; this uniformity warrants scepticism given the execution dependencies [#J]
  • Sceptical close: The bearish read on margin is that the RES margin expansion story assumes grid connection and permitting on a timeline that Greek regulatory history does not support; a 12-month delay in 500MW of RES additions would reduce FY27 EBITDA by ~€80–100mn (5–6% miss vs consensus) — not catastrophic but enough to reset the narrative [#J]

  • Bull case: The dominant bull narrative across 8 buy-rated analysts is that PPC is a unique EUR mid-cap entry point into the Greek energy transition: the company has first-mover advantages in Greek renewables licensing, a regulated distribution asset (HEDNO) worth €5–6bn in isolation, and a Romanian platform providing geographic diversification — all available at a 25% discount to European utility peers on EV/EBITDA [#J]
    • Sub: Secondary bull narrative: Greek state (still ~34% shareholder) has strong incentive to support the company's credit rating and investment plan, providing an implicit sovereign backstop

  • Bear case: The dominant bear narrative from Hold analysts (Mediobanca, SocGen) is that the stock's 65% re-rating since 2023 has already priced in successful execution; at current multiples, the margin of safety is thin and any permitting or macro shock would cause a sharp de-rating [#J]
    • Sub: SocGen's specific concern: Greek electricity price normalisation is not priced into any buy-side or sell-side model
  • Bullish theme: RES pipeline delivery — 3.5GW target by 2027 represents a 3x capacity increase; if achieved, EPS compounding of 15–20% p.a. is genuinely visible [#J]
