EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Business Operations and Strategic Perimeter

Manufacturing Intelligence specializes in stationary and portable metrology, computer-aided manufacturing software, and industrial quality control serving aerospace, automotive, and electronics with coordinate measuring machines and laser trackers. Infrastructure & Geospatial, formerly Geosystems, leads in precision surveying and reality capture, reorganized in 2025 into six vertically integrated divisions to improve local execution. Autonomous Solutions provides high-performance positioning, operational intelligence, and vehicle autonomy technologies across six divisions including Mining, Agriculture, Aerospace & Defence, and Positioning brands NovAtel and Septentrio. The Robotics Division launched early 2025 focuses on the AEON humanoid robot (165cm height, 34 degrees of freedom), with BMW Leipzig moving to production deployment by end-2026 and Schaeffler committing to deploy at least 1,000 units by 2032.

Industry Structure and Market Dynamics

The addressable market for precision measurement, positioning, and industrial autonomy is projected to grow to approximately €38bn by 2030, with standalone Hexagon operating in a €6bn serviceable addressable market growing at an annualized rate of 8-10%. The global high-precision positioning market is highly consolidated with top-5 players commanding more than 80% market share; in Italian surveying, Leica holds a dominant 40% to 50% share while Trimble and Topcon each control approximately 20%. Low-cost Chinese brands like CHC and Stonex are expanding rapidly in Europe via aggressive pricing, while premium peers Trimble and Topcon concede entry-level share to focus on high-margin machine control and scanning. Regulatory tailwinds include the EU CAP simplification package supporting agricultural digitalization with up to €1.6bn in annual savings, Galileo satellite enablement for autonomous tractors, a projected 10% annual increase in critical mineral mining investment, and the European Union Deforestation Regulation boosting demand for Geospatial tools.

Supply Chain and Distribution Channels

Hexagon maintains an extremely fragmented supplier footprint where the largest single supplier accounted for just 1% of total net sales in 2025, with no single supplier considered decisive for financial or operational performance. Standalone Hexagon remains vulnerable to rising trade barriers, with approximately 9% of group revenues subject to potential US tariff headwinds, primarily involving approximately €400m of Geosystems products shipped from Switzerland. Downstream operations use a hybrid model combining direct sales forces and a network of more than 1,500 downstream resellers globally, with indirect sales representing between 10% and 30% of sales depending on industry verticals. Standalone Hexagon utilizes a consultative direct sales model for premium metrology installations, positioning as a sole provider of frames, probes, and software while enabling high-margin bundling of software maintenance and service contracts.

Competitive Landscape and Integration Threats

Manufacturing Intelligence maintains #1 global market share of 27% in coordinate measuring machines, with portable metrology market share double that of its nearest competitor. Hexagon competes primarily against Zeiss in high-end metrology, while facing a premium geospatial triopoly with Trimble and Topcon across surveying and machine control segments. Septentrio's high-integrity, anti-jamming GNSS chipsets create a hard-to-replicate military technology moat securing key defense design wins. John Deere and Caterpillar vertically integrate autonomy software completely bypassing third-party technology providers, while Chinese entrants are rapidly closing the general-purpose hardware gap according to former Stellantis leadership. Peer M&A activity includes AMETEK's integration of Faro Technologies and Sandvik's consolidation of digital mining software through Deswik and Universal Field Robots acquisitions.

Cross-Value-Chain Power Dynamics

Partner signals confirm robust premium demand, with BMW Leipzig factory trials translating into full AEON production deployments and Schaeffler expanding its partnership to deploy at least 1,000 humanoid robots by 2032. Hexagon Core operates with 36 working capital days, significantly outperforming Sandvik's 85 days, while software-centric peers like Autodesk and Trimble achieve negative working capital days through up-front subscription billing models. Cash conversion was seasonally strong at 121% in Q4 2025, with standalone Hexagon targeting 90% to 100% cash conversion of cumulative EBITAC on a sustained basis. Cross-valuechain signals validate management's narrative on software transition momentum despite cautious buyer behavior in European automotive and construction markets.

Long-Term Structural Advantages and Disruption Forces

The industry requires massive initial capital and specialized sensor technology to achieve micron-level accuracy, with combining high-precision optical hardware and proprietary software creating a formidable competitive moat reinforced by deeply interwoven service layers acting as complex spaghetti preventing system swaps. Hexagon's industrial humanoid robot AEON brings together precision measurement and AI-based motion control, with R&D investments doubling to €50m in 2026 as BMW Leipzig production deployments progress and Schaeffler expands collaboration to deploy at least 1,000 units by 2032. Acquiring Waygate Technologies for $1.45bn establishes Hexagon as a leading global provider of non-destructive testing, with CT platforms completing the measurement chain from external metrology to internal part inspection, though Waygate's 10% EBIT margin initially dilutes MI segment margins from 23.5% to 20.3%. Severe workforce shortages across manufacturing and construction accelerate secular demand for industrial autonomy, EU CAP policies support smart farming systems utilizing high-precision GNSS positioning, and sovereign defense requirements protect Hexagon's high-integrity positioning from foreign market entrants.
