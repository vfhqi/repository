EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Francis Deprez brings a highly credentialed consultant-track pedigree from McKinsey and Deutsche Telekom, aligning strategically with D'Ieteren's capital allocation mandate rather than traditional industrial operations . Under his leadership, the group delivered exceptional long-term financial compounding with a 22.0% annualized total shareholder return over ten years and record adjusted profit before tax reaching €921m in 2024 . However, the late-2024 family reorganization involved a massive €4.0bn extraordinary dividend that pushed Belron's leverage to 5.5x EBITDA and increased group-level debt by €1.0bn, though deleveraging has since become the primary focus . His prior track record reflects a highly strategic, consultant-driven profile with deep advisory expertise but limited direct hands-on industrial P&L ownership before Detecon . Deprez exhibits an active owner-mindset with structured communication, moderate risk appetite, and disciplined execution focus, continually questioning whether D'Ieteren remains the natural owner of its assets .

2

Sell-Side Sentiment and Investor Perception

Sell-side sentiment exhibits a strong unanimous consensus buy bias across the active coverage pool, with all three major brokers maintaining Buy or Outperform recommendations . Analysts upgraded D'Ieteren from Hold to Buy in January 2026, raising target prices from €168 to €235, while Bernstein initiated with Outperform at €230 in April 2026 . Bullish arguments focus on collapsing the persistent holding discount to NAV through a potential Belron IPO, with Jefferies calculating that shares provide access to Belron at an attractive 60% discount . Bearish concerns highlight that the 2024 family transaction structurally damaged corporate governance perception and drove Belron's leverage to an elevated 5.4x EBITDA . Management maintains a highly cautious and conservative guidance posture, with strict silence on market rumors regarding the Belron IPO .

CFO and Senior Leadership Quality

CFO Edouard Janssen succeeded Arnaud Laviolette in September 2023 and prioritized aggressive debt reduction following the group's €4.0bn family-led capital reorganization . The executive committee comprises a lean team of three senior leaders, though Chief Investment Officer Nicolas Saillez departed at the end of 2025 . Core assets Belron and D'Ieteren Automotive maintain experienced leadership with long tenures, though TVH experienced significant leadership friction after laying off CEO Dominiek Valcke, while Moleskine appointed new co-CEOs in February 2026 . The holding launched the Voyager development program in 2025 to cultivate senior leadership talent and directly assists subsidiaries with succession planning and executive-level recruitment .

Board Composition and Governance Structure

The Board comprises 12 non-executive members with seven out of twelve directors independent, representing 58.33% of total board seats and exceeding Belgian minimum legal requirements . The late-2024 shareholder reorganization triggered a significant board refresh, with three directors resigning and new independent and family representatives co-opted and ratified . Chairman Nicolas D'Ieteren represents the seventh generation with ultimate voting control of 53.82% via Nayarit, though the company deviates from Belgian governance codes by granting no shares to directors . D'Ieteren operates with a family-controlled dual-class voting structure, with Nayarit holding absolute voting control of 53.84% despite owning only 49.54% of share capital through disproportionate participating shares . The group employs active governance scaffolding including a dedicated Risk, Compliance, and Whistleblowing Officer, and recorded zero confirmed incidents of corruption or bribery in 2025 . 19

Executive Compensation and Alignment

D'Ieteren provides strong variable alignment via TSR-linked incentive structures, with CEO Deprez receiving €1.83m total remuneration in 2023 including €577.5k annual bonus and €350k Cash LTI payout . Cash LTI requires minimum shareholder return and annualized group TSR to exceed the MSCI Gross World Index CAGR, with board conducting qualitative assessments of ESG targets . Stock options align executive compensation with long-term shareholder value, with CEO Deprez receiving 50k options vesting in the fourth year and exercisable until the tenth year . D'Ieteren requires no minimum shareholdings for Executive Committee members, deviating from Corporate Governance Provision 7.9, though CFO Janssen bought €800k shares in late 2025 showing material personal commitment .

Culture and Key-Person Risk

D'Ieteren maintains a highly engaged, family-oriented workforce with engagement scores exceeding 70% across all businesses, while Belron registered a world-class 87.1% employee engagement score in 2025 . Consolidated group employee turnover fell to 16.0% in 2025 from 18.0% in 2024, though corporate head office turnover increased to 12.0% from 7.0% reflecting administrative restructuring . The corporate head office relies on a highly lean team of 27 members representing bottom-decile overhead, though the departure of CIO Nicolas Saillez concentrates strategic oversight on the CEO . D'Ieteren mitigates corporate risk through decentralized standalone operational management at portfolio companies, though TVH and Moleskine experienced significant C-suite churn creating dual-leadership execution risks . The group launched the Voyager Development Programme in 2025 training 20 senior leaders and actively assists portfolio companies with C-level recruitment and management long-term incentive plans .

Management Credibility and Strategic Execution

Expert network feedback reveals high operational execution credibility, with former TVH Directors praising exceptional global distribution capabilities and unmatched inventory breadth . Industry experts commended D'Ieteren's humane management approach and disciplined capital allocation model, systematically redeploying steady cash flows from automotive distribution into growth assets . Management maintains a highly conservative and disciplined communication posture using structural reorganizations to streamline operations, while systematically avoiding commenting on press rumors concerning Belron IPO timing . The consultant- track advisory archetype prioritizes high-level portfolio transaction design over direct hands-on operational excellence at subsidiary levels, while the decentralized delegation model may prevent rapid holding-level interventions if multiple subsidiary turnarounds fail simultaneously.
