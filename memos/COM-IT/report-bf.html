EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 24 findings

Business Model and Strategic Positioning

1.
Dual-segment resilience model: Comer Industries operates a resilient dual-segment powertrain model combining agricultural (59% of revenues) and industrial (41%) divisions, recently enhanced by the high-end Comtesco hydraulic acquisition to structurally mitigate agricultural cyclicality
2.
System-level integration advantage: The company has evolved from a standalone component supplier to a global systems partner developing advanced transmission systems that integrate mechanical gearboxes with e-comer electric motors and Comtesco hydraulic components to maximize system value
3.
Transformational M&A scale expansion: The 2021 Walterscheid acquisition transformed pro-forma revenues from 800 million euros to over 1.2 billion euros, while the January 2026 Comtesco transaction (70% stake for approximately 82.2 million euros) adds high-pressure hydraulic technologies and unlocks the Japanese construction machinery market
4.
High-margin aftermarket leverage: Downstream capabilities are anchored by Walterscheid Power Drive Group, which runs an extensive parts distribution network supplying specialized spare parts to more than 130 distribution partners across the globe

Financial Profile and Returns Generation

5.
Superior margin resilience: Comer sustained its consolidated EBITDA margin at approximately 17.0% in FY2025 despite cyclical end-market destocking pressure, materially outperforming industrial peers like Dana (8.1% to 8.7% margins)
6.
Best-in-class leverage discipline: Comer reduced its adjusted net debt leverage to a conservative 0.5x EBITDA in FY2023 from 0.8x in FY2022, representing materially superior positioning versus JOST SE's 2.27x leverage ratio
7.
Strong cash conversion power: Comer generated 71.3 million euros of free cash flow in FY2025, representing a cash conversion rate of 49.3% of EBITDA, down from 53.3% in FY2024 due to temporary inventory build pressures
8.
High-teen return profile: Post-tax ROIC remains in the high-teen range, outperforming Nabtesco's sub-6% returns, driven by permanent procurement synergies and favorable working capital dynamics (DSO averaging 20 to 30 days below DPO)

Industry Structure and Competitive Moats

9.
Consolidating oligopoly structure: The off-highway competitive landscape has consolidated rapidly, reducing fragmentation and improving structural pricing discipline, with Comer Industries establishing itself as the leading global player in off-highway power transmission mechatronics
10.
Downstream margin accrual bias: Value chain analysis reveals thin raw input margins (15.0% gross margin), moderate machining margins (7.0% EBIT margin), and high-value downstream stages with premium hydraulics commanding 16.0% EBIT margins and aftermarket distribution delivering estimated 22.0% EBIT margins
11.
High technical switching costs: Drivetrain components are deeply integrated into machine designs, creating high technical and operational switching costs, while suppliers involve OEMs early in development projects through co-design to solidify long-term supply partnerships
12.
Concentrated customer base risk: Drivetrain suppliers sell into highly concentrated customer bases, with John Deere holding a dominant 57% share of US agriculture equipment, CNH Industrial at 20%, and Caterpillar commanding 23% of North American construction equipment

Value Chain Power Dynamics and Cross-Chain Signals
