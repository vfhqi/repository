EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Materiality of Internal and External Change

Dassault Aviation maintains an exceptionally stable, long-tenured executive team that prioritises internal promotion but continues to face criticism for minimal financial market communication. Both Chairman and CEO Éric Trappier and COO Loïk Segalen have served in their respective roles since 2013, with executive terms set to expire at the 2027 General Meeting. Minimal communication from C-suite management exists with the public market, creating a persistent information gap for external investors. Externally, resilient business aviation departures up 4% YTD through May 2026 support steady demand for high-end corporate travel, while global business aviation flights increased 4% YTD through May 2026, outpacing commercial airline passenger growth. However, supply chain issues directly delayed Falcon deliveries below the group's annual target, as Tier-3 suppliers struggle with raw materials and timely deliveries. France and Germany officially cancelled the joint FCAS sixth-generation fighter jet program, representing a material strategic setback for future defense collaboration. The net internal change is minimal and stability-focused, while external forces exhibit high materiality driven by supply disruptions and defense budget expansion.
