Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Comer Industries is a resilient global leader in mechatronics, navigating cyclical agricultural downturns while expanding into high-margin hydraulics through strategic acquisitions.
1.
Dual-segment revenue model: The business operates in the agricultural and industrial sectors, earning revenues by supplying specialized mechatronic gearboxes and powertrains directly to global vehicle manufacturers under long-term serial production contracts
2.
Strategic investment case: The potential investment case rests on the transformational acquisition of Comtesco from Nabtesco, which expands Comer into the premium hydraulic market and sets up significant margin expansion
3.
Cyclical demand recovery: A concurrent driver of the investment thesis is the expected cyclical recovery of global agricultural machinery markets, which would trigger rapid organic operating leverage
4.
Principal earnings driver: The single most important swing factor for earnings over the next 18 months is the integration speed of Comtesco and the extraction of cost synergies
5.
Resilient financial performance: Comer proved its operational strength by maintaining its EBITDA margin at 16.2% in FY2025 compared to 16.7% in FY2024, despite a 5.2% sales drop to 893.7 million euros
6.
Outstanding cash flow: High cash generation was sustained with free cash flow reaching 71.3 million euros in FY2025, which allowed net leverage to drop to a negligible 0.2x
7.
Key downside threat: The most critical risk is a prolonged global agricultural recession, which caused a 10.5% segment contraction in FY2025 and could delay core demand bottoming
8.
Highly positive verdict: A deeper look is highly warranted because Comer represents a premium mechatronics business trading at an attractive valuation with highly predictable earnings drivers

Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. Comer Industries commands a dominant global niche in power transmission systems, leveraging deep OEM co-development and scale to maintain a high-value mechatronics position.

Segment-Level Operations and Product Portfolios

1.
Agricultural division size: The agricultural sector is the company's largest division, representing 449.99 million euros or 50.4% of consolidated sales in FY2025, which declined from 502.6 million euros or 53.3% of sales in FY2024
2.
Specialized agricultural gearboxes: This agricultural division manufactures speed increasers, speed reducers, bevel gearboxes, PTO driveshafts, axles, and wheel drives designed for tractors and combine harvesters, providing critical mechanical power transmission components to global OEMs
3.
Industrial division size: The industrial sector represents Comer's second core division, contributing 443.69 million euros or 49.6% of consolidated revenues in FY2025 compared to 439.82 million euros or 46.7% in FY2024
4.
Specialized industrial equipment: The industrial division produces modular planetary gearboxes, rigid and steerable axles, and mechatronic systems for municipal, mining, forestry, and material handling applications
5.
Renewable energy solutions: Comer's industrial division also designs and delivers specialized gearboxes and transmissions for the wind and renewable energy sectors, including biogas machine augers
6.
Electric vehicle motors: Through its mechatronic expansion, Comer designs and manufactures electric motors, transaxles, and gearmotors for stationary industrial applications and electric mobility logistics, supporting advanced mechatronics transition

Value Chain Positioning and Customer Dynamics

7.
Mid-chain value position: Comer occupies a high-value, central position in the heavy machinery value chain, acting as an integrated Tier-1 partner that connects raw component suppliers to downstream global vehicle manufacturers
8.
Long-term customer relationships: The revenue model relies on long-lasting relationships with global OEMs, who integrate Comer's customized mechatronic assemblies directly into their serial production lines, providing high back-log visibility
9.
Customization and co-development: Over 15% of Comer's product line is developed in co-design with OEMs, while approximately 70% is fully customized to customer-specific machinery platforms, creating high integration
10.
Global delivery model: Comer operates local production sites across major markets, achieving short delivery times of 16 to 20 weeks to support downstream supply chain resilience
11.
Broad geographic sales: Revenues are diversified across EMEA at 55.4%, APAC at 20.4%, North America at 19.3%, and Latin America at 4.8% in FY2025, which protects from localized recessions
12.
Limited direct end-user exposure: Comer does not sell directly to end-user farmers or construction operators, instead supplying parts that determine the final competitiveness of OEM machinery in the market

Competitive Moats and Industry Consolidation

13.
Scale and cost advantage: Comer's massive global manufacturing scale, operating 11 plants worldwide, enables cost-optimized production and high purchasing power for raw materials
14.
Deep engineering moat: High switching barriers are sustained by deep systems integration, co-development processes, and an active intellectual property base of approximately 75 patents
15.
High quality benchmark: Low complain rates of ~300 parts per million and an average product lifetime of 10 years ensure a strong brand reputation among global OEMs
16.
Active market consolidator: Strategic transactions like the 2021 Walterscheid acquisition and 2023 e-comer acquisition have consolidated Comer's market share in both agriculture and industrial sectors
17.
Oligopolistic industry structure: The heavy driveline mechatronics sector is structured as a concentrated oligopoly, creating a protective barrier against new market entrants
18.
Other side of the story: Customer concentration remains a major structural concern, as global OEMs can easily dictate pricing and delay capital plans during agricultural downcycles, threatening margins

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Comer Industries has delivered a resilient financial profile, sustaining high operating margins and reducing net leverage despite cyclically weak agricultural revenues.

Multi-Year Revenue and Earnings Profile

1.
Consolidated sales performance: Sales revenues stood at 893.7 million euros in FY2025, down by 5.2% compared with 942.4 million euros in FY2024
2.
Organic contraction factors: The sales contraction in FY2025 was mainly driven by a 10.5% drop in the agricultural sector, following a weak demand trend from farmers
3.
Multi-year sales comparison: The FY2025 turnover of 893.7 million euros represents a cyclical decrease from the peak revenues of 1,223.9 million euros reported in FY2023
4.
EBITDA margin stability: EBITDA came in at 144.7 million euros in FY2025, representing an EBITDA margin of 16.2%, down only slightly from 16.7% in FY2024
5.
EBIT margin performance: EBIT stood at 90.2 million euros in FY2025, with an EBIT margin of 10.1% of consolidated revenues compared to 10.5% in FY2024
6.
Net income development: Net profit reached 61.3 million euros in FY2025, equal to 6.9% of consolidated revenues, down from 67.2 million euros in FY2024

Cash Generation, Capital Intensity, and Return Metrics

7.
Consistent cash generation: Comer generated strong free cash flow of 71.3 million euros in FY2025, down from adjusted free cash flow of 84.0 million euros in FY2024
8.
Solid cash conversion: The group recorded a cash conversion rate of 49.3% in FY2025, which reflects high operating cash generation and controlled net investments of 40.4 million euros
9.
Returns on invested capital: The company's ROI stood at 14.1% in the rolling 12 months ending June 30, 2025, compared to 16.5% in the comparative prior period
10.
Return on equity: Adjusted ROE stood at 11.2% in the rolling 12 months ending June 30, 2025, down from 15.2% in the prior period
11.
Adjusted net profit: Adjusted net profit, excluding acquisition-related amortization, stood at 76.6 million euros in FY2025 compared to 82.5 million euros in FY2024

Balance Sheet Strength and Guidance Accuracy

12.
Active net debt reduction: Comer reduced its net financial debt to 28.9 million euros at the end of FY2025, down by 29.5 million euros from 58.4 million euros in FY2024
13.
Outstanding leverage profile: Net leverage ratio fell to an exceptionally safe level of 0.2x in FY2025 compared to 0.4x at the end of FY2024
14.
Strong equity cushion: Total shareholder's equity increased to 561.7 million euros as of December 31, 2025, up from 544.2 million euros in FY2024
15.
Proactive treasury management: Comer renegotiated its committed credit lines, securing a single committed 100 million euro medium-long term line maturing in December 2028 on improved terms
16.
Reliable guidance pattern: Management has established a history of conservative and honest guidance, successfully meeting its margin preservation forecasts during both the FY2024 and FY2025 downturns
17.
Other side of the story: Organic growth remains cyclically volatile, as shown by the separate parent company's revenues dropping by 31.9% in FY2024 to 287 million euros, highlighting severe operational leverage

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Comer strengthens significantly due to the high-margin Comtesco acquisition and an imminent cyclical agricultural recovery.

Forward Drivers of the Earnings Trajectory

1.
Comtesco consolidation catalyst: The primary driver is the acquisition of 70% of Comtesco from Nabtesco for approximately 82.2 million euros, expanding Comer into high-end hydraulics from January 1, 2026
2.
Transformational scale addition: Comtesco contributes approximately 272 million euros of pro-forma sales, creating a combined entity with pro-forma revenues over 1.2 billion euros and EBITDA of 179 million euros
3.
Acquisition plausibility check: The plausibility of this driver is high as the transaction has closed and initial Q1 2026 results confirm positive integration trends
4.
Agricultural demand recovery: European agricultural segment revenues, which fell 10.5% in FY2025, showed a strong demand inflection of +1.6% in the second half of FY2025
5.
Positive business barometer: CEMA business climate indices turned positive in late 2025, with sales expectations pointing to an imminent cyclical recovery for agricultural machinery OEMs
6.
Agricultural recovery plausibility: The plausibility is medium-high, supported by rising farmer sentiment, though it could fail if soft commodity prices remain depressed and delay OEM orders

Cost Synergies, Financial Independence, and Bull Case Targets

7.
Operational integration synergies: Comer continues to harvest cost-saving and procurement synergies from its Walterscheid integration, which should support margins during the volume recovery
8.
Asian market expansion: Comtesco's consolidation provides Comer with advanced hydraulic manufacturing plants in China and Thailand, facilitating high-growth market penetration
9.
Massive organic firepower: With leverage at 0.2x and high cash generation, Comer possesses over 400 million euros of acquisition firepower to continue its roll-up strategy
10.
Asymmetric catalyst independence: The core catalysts (Comtesco accretion and agricultural recovery) are largely independent, meaning the investment case can succeed even if only one driver fires
11.
Other side of the story: Comtesco could suffer severe margin dilution if competitive pressures in the Chinese construction market persist, dragging down the consolidated EBITDA margins

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Comer Industries trades at an attractive valuation discount on forward EV/EBITDA compared to selected industrial peers despite its high-margin mechatronics and acoustics profiles.
1.
Low forward multiple: Comer currently trades at a highly attractive 2026E EV/EBITDA of 6.4x, down from 7.9x on 2025E, representing a significant discount to its historical valuation peaks
2.
Favorable peer discount: Comer's forward multiple of 6.4x sits at a discount compared to comparable peer SKF, which trades at 6.9x EV/EBITDA for 2026E
3.
High transaction benchmark premium: Private market transactions, such as Eaton's Mobility Group divestiture at 8.3x 2026E EV/EBITDA, show that Comer's public multiple is significantly undervalued
4.
Pricing of cyclical recession: The current valuation multiples suggest that the public market is pricing in a prolonged agricultural downturn, completely ignoring Comtesco's consolidation
5.
Strong price performance: Despite estimates pressure across the sector, Comer's shares rose 40% in the recent period since late 2025, showing strong relative strength
6.
Other side of the story: Multiple expansion could remain blocked if the market continues to penalize the Italian small-cap sector with a structural liquidity discount, trapping Comer at low multiples

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderately concentrated around agricultural cycle duration and Asian integration friction, though mitigated by proactive debt management and hedging.

Core Macroeconomic and Currency Headwinds

1.
Prolonged agricultural recession: The primary operating risk is an extended downturn in the global agricultural sector, where weak demand has already caused a 10.5% sales drop in FY2025
2.
Global trade barriers: Bilateral tariff hikes and trade policy uncertainty, particularly between the US and trading partners, represent a threat to Comer's international supply chain
3.
Material input inflation: Sharp fluctuations in raw material prices (steel, copper, aluminum, cast iron) can squeeze margins, although mitigated by trade indexing clauses with major OEMs
4.
Severe currency exposure: The Euro's appreciation against the USD and CNY poses a severe headwind, having already reduced FY2025 revenues by 16.3 million euros
5.
No short-seller presence: Regulatory and market searches confirm that there is currently no active public short-seller report or exceptionally high short interest in Comer shares

Strategic Integration and Early Warning Metrics

6.
Comtesco integration friction: The single failure mode that would most damage the investment case is severe integration friction at Comtesco, which would delay crucial synergy realization
7.
High Asian market volatility: The newly acquired Comtesco division is highly exposed to the volatile Chinese construction market, which has deteriorated since 2022 due to local competition
8.
False friend revenue growth: High consolidated revenue growth in Q1 2026 could act as a false friend, masking a deeper and continued organic decline in core European divisions
9.
Core early warning indicator: The organic quarterly order intake in the EMEA agricultural division represents the single most critical early-warning metric to monitor for cycle bottoming
10.
Other side of the story: The investment risks are significantly overstated because Comer's highly flexible variable cost structure and local manufacturing plants allow rapid adaptation to demand downcycles

Robust executive LTIP: Shareholders approved the "Long Term Incentive Plan 2024-2026" for executive directors, ensuring that management is financially incentivized to generate sustainable long-term value
4.
Minimal treasury sales: Insider and treasury transactions remain quiet, with the parent company holding a negligible 53,638 treasury shares representing only 0.19% of the share capital
