Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Rosebank operates as a highly differentiated turnaround vehicle replicating Melrose's high-returning "Buy, Improve, Sell" playbook across underoptimised industrial assets.

Portfolio division overview

1.
Multi-platform holding structure: The group operates three distinct industrial platforms – Electrical Components International, MW Components, and CPM – following the completed dual-acquisitions in Q2 2026
2.
Electrical Components International: This division manufactures critical electrical distribution systems, control box assemblies, and wire harnesses for consumer appliance, HVAC, and industrial markets
3.
MW Components business: This division provides highly engineered fasteners, precision springs, and specialized metal products for critical applications, including the expanding aerospace and defence sector
4.
CPM platform business: This division is a global supplier of specialized industrial process equipment and aftermarket parts used extensively across animal feed, oilseed, food, and bio-energy markets
5.
Strategic segment focus: ECI operates via two core divisions – Electrification and Industrial (high-growth) and Appliance and HVAC (stable consumer applications)

Revenue models and value chain

6.
Dual segment revenue: ECI earns revenue across its two segments, with Electrification and Industrial contributing $195.00 million and Appliance and HVAC contributing $250.00 million of 2025 revenue
7.
Highly resilient aftermarket: CPM exhibits highly predictable economics because its aftermarket parts and services business generates approximately 50.00% of total sales
8.
Favorable value chain: The group occupies a profit-favoured, high-barrier position in the industrial value chain by supplying custom-engineered, low-cost components that are vital to customer end-products
9.
High switching costs: ECI benefits from significant customer switching barriers due to its high wallet share, deep engineering integration, and the high cost of redesigning certified wire harnesses

Industry structure and competitive advantage

10.
Massive scale footprint: The group leverages a global footprint of 26 manufacturing sites for ECI alone, allowing regional optimization and agile supply chain responses for complex multinational programs
11.
Niche industry structure: The precision components and process equipment markets are highly fragmented, offering Rosebank a rich pipeline of bolt-on M&A opportunities to consolidate market share
12.
Differentiated corporate vehicle: Unlike standard listed engineers, Rosebank is uniquely positioned as an active steward that optimizes operations rather than pursuing volume growth for its own sake
13.
Proven operational levers: Competitive advantage is sustained through Rosebank's active management style – footprint rationalisation, contract renegotiation, and overhead reduction
14.
Long-term holding flexibility: Rosebank acts as a responsible steward of its assets, investing heavily in them as if it will own them forever, though divestment typically occurs in 3-5 years
15.
Other side of the story: The transactional model creates extreme earnings volatility because the group is highly dependent on periodic, large-scale divestments to crystallize value and return cash to shareholders

3.
Restructuring failure mechanism: The driver would fail if local labor shortages, plant closure resistance, or operational friction during factory transfers lead to prolonged customer shipping delays

4.

7.

9.
De-leveraging failure mechanism: The driver would fail if high integration cash outlays or unexpected working capital swings consume the free cash flow targeted for debt reduction

Key driver: institutional-grade platform scaling

Section summary

Operational and macroeconomic risks

1.
Operational execution risk: The single most critical risk is a failure to deliver the targeted 6.00-7.00 percentage point operating margin expansion across newly acquired MW Components and CPM
2.
Macroeconomic downturn risk: A prolonged recession in US residential construction would severely depress volumes at ECI's Appliance and HVAC division, which is heavily construction-exposed and represents 56.00% of sales
3.
Integration friction risk: The ambitious plan to consolidate ECI's manufacturing sites by over 25.00% risks causing temporary supply chain disruptions and losing key customer approvals during factory transitions

Financial and structural risks

4.
Severe failure mode: The single failure mode that would most damage the investment case is a prolonged delay in restructuring ECI, which would trap group capital and block future acquisitions
5.
False friend signal: An early increase in statutory revenues could act as a false friend, masking underlying volume declines and customer attrition with temporary inflationary pricing actions
6.
Early-warning monitor: The key leading indicator is ECI's adjusted operating margin, which rose to 15.60% in FY25; any quarterly deterioration would signal restructuring cost overruns
7.
Geopolitical supply disruption: Escalating international trade tensions or sudden tariff changes represent a material threat to ECI's global supply chain network, which spans Mexico, China, and Morocco
8.
Dilution from capital raises: Future large-scale acquisitions would require massive public equity raises, which could dilute existing shareholders if executed at an unfavorable share price
9.
Other side of the story: Concerns over leverage are overstated because Rosebank rapidly reduced ECI's leverage to 2.40x annualised adjusted EBITDA, creating the financial headroom needed to execute restructuring
