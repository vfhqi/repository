Organic tenancy guidance has been systematically exceeded, with management upgrading 2023 targets twice (ultimately delivering 2,433 vs. initial 1,600-2,100) and repeating the pattern in 2024 (delivering 2,538 vs. initial 1,600-2,100), validating strong underlying customer demand trends.

Historical Guidance Tracking: Met/Miss Analysis

Over the past strategic cycles, Helios Towers has established a consistent track record of meeting or exceeding its financial and operational guidance. Management's forecasting methodology typically relies on conservative initial targets at the beginning of the fiscal year, which are frequently followed by upward revisions and tightening as operational visibility improves. This "beat and raise" pattern has been evident across key performance indicators, particularly during the post-2022 period when the company shifted from inorganic expansion to organic lease-up and portfolio integration.

This momentum carried into 2023, where initial guidance of 1,600 to 2,100 additions was upgraded in the first half to a floor of 1,900, and upgraded again in the third quarter to a range of 2,200 to 2,400. A similar trajectory occurred in 2024; management initially guided for 1,600 to 2,100 additions, tightened this upward to greater than 2,400 by the third quarter, and closed the year with a record 2,538 additions. For 2025, the initial target of 2,000 to 2,500 additions was comfortably met, bringing the group's tenancy ratio to 2.2x a full year ahead of the 2026 strategic timeline.

Navigating Macro Sensitivities: Fuel, FX, and Market Disruptions

Helios Towers' management has meticulously engineered a business model designed to insulate earnings from severe macroeconomic turbulence. Over the past decade, the company has faced a barrage of external shocks, including the COVID-19 pandemic, acute oil price volatility, high inflation, and aggressive interest rate hikes. Despite these headwinds, management's claims regarding business model resilience have been heavily validated by an uninterrupted ten-year track record of double-digit Adjusted EBITDA growth.

FX Fluctuations and Inflation Protections

The foundation of the company's macroeconomic defense is its immense exposure to hard currency. Management has strategically concentrated operations in markets that are either dollarised or pegged to the Euro, such as the Democratic Republic of Congo, Senegal, Congo Brazzaville, and Oman. By the end of 2025, 71% of the group's Adjusted EBITDA was generated in hard currency, effectively neutralizing significant local foreign exchange risk.

Furthermore, management has insulated the platform against local inflation and currency devaluation through strict contract mechanisms. Long-term tenant contracts feature embedded annual Consumer Price Index escalators. These CPI escalators systematically reprice revenue upwards, providing a mechanical offset to the depreciation of local currencies and protecting absolute dollar earnings.

Power Volatility and Fuel Pass-Throughs

Navigating grid unreliability and fuel price spikes is a critical operational risk in African markets. To address this, the company enforces annual or quarterly power price escalators across its contracts, passing the fluctuating cost of diesel directly to mobile network operators. Management's regular disclosures continuously verify that when power prices spike, revenue escalators trigger to match the increased operating expenditures, resulting in a flat, fully protected impact on the bottom-line EBITDA.

Beyond contractual protections, management has delivered highly credible physical resilience. During 2025, the company achieved a record 99.99% power uptime across its network, ensuring consistent connectivity for customers despite grid availability averaging just 18 hours per day in some regions. To further mitigate future fuel exposure, management is executing "Project 100," a targeted $100 million capital program designed to replace diesel generators with grid connections, solar power, and advanced battery solutions by the end of the decade.

Weathering Interest Rates and Global Crises

Management has also guided investors smoothly through capital market disruptions. To combat the global surge in interest rates, the company actively manages its debt profile through a prudent mix of fixed-rate borrowings and interest rate swaps. This treasury discipline, combined with the company's aggressive organic deleveraging trajectory, has dramatically reduced the equity's sensitivity to central bank rate movements.
