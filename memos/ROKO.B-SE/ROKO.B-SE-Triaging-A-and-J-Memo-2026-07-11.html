B.1 Business Description 11 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Serial-acquirer platform. Roko AB is a Swedish, Stockholm-listed diversified industrial acquirer that buys majority stakes, typically 75%, in small, asset-light, niche-leading businesses across Europe
2.
Decentralised model. Each subsidiary retains its founder-manager as a minority shareholder and runs with high autonomy, mirroring the governance template of Lifco, Indutrade and Addtech
3.
Acquisition criteria. The group targets asset-light niche leaders generating EUR 2m-10m EBITA with capex below 5% of sales, financed largely from internal cash flow
4.
Financial scale. Revenue is approximately SEK 6,450m on a last-twelve-months basis, with an adjusted EBITA margin of roughly 20%, up from 18.5% in 2023
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt to EBITDA is reported by the company at 2.0x versus a broker-modelled 2.6x for the same period, an unresolved discrepancy (see F.2)
6.
Capital allocation. The group initiated a maiden dividend in 2025 at a 10% payout ratio, a first-time capital-return signal alongside continued M&A primacy
7.
Segment mix. Portfolio companies span B2B and B2C niches in automotive, construction and general industrials, with roughly 80% of revenue exposed to cyclical end-markets
8.
Geography. Historically Nordic-concentrated, now diversifying: roughly 55% of the group's 33 historical acquisitions sit outside the Nordics
9.
Leadership. New chief executive Johan Bladh, previously deputy chief executive and chief financial officer, took over from founder Fredrik Karlsson on 22 April 2026
10.
Founder role. Karlsson remains deputy chief executive and chairman of the investment committee, focused exclusively on M&A execution rather than day-to-day management
11.
Investment style. This is an event-driven, M&A-compounding case: the thesis rests on continued deployment of cash into acquisitions at roughly 7x operating profit before amortisation, not on organic growth, which has recently been weak
