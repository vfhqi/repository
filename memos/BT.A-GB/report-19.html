3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 1: Fibre Monetisation Lag (Revenue Risk)

Category: Revenue risk — growth narrative break

Narrative: BT deploys FTTP to ≥25m premises by FY26 on schedule but average revenue per user on full-fibre products fails to exceed legacy FTTC ARPU at the portfolio level; the volume-fills-the-funnel story breaks because pricing power is capped by Openreach's regulated wholesale access obligations and retail competitive intensity from VMO2, Sky, and alt-nets

  • Transmission mechanism: Higher capex commitment (c.£5bn/yr peak) + flat ARPU = operating leverage reversal; EBITDA growth stalls post-FY27 when cost savings are exhausted, FCF misses consensus by 20-30%, and dividend sustainability is questioned

Early warning signals:
- Openreach fibre wholesale take-up rate falls below 35% at 12-month post-build; monthly disclosures show line loss to alt-nets in build footprint
- BT Consumer blended ARPU (fibre vs. legacy copper) fails to show sequential quarter-on-quarter uplift through FY26
- VMO2 / Sky retail pricing campaigns hold or undercut BT's full-fibre pricing tier
- Ofcom consultation outcomes lean toward mandating structural separation pricing floors below BT's business case assumption
- Openreach external CP take-up fails to compensate for internal BT Consumer share loss

Plausibility: High plausibility — multiple UK telco restructurings (TalkTalk, Plusnet price wars, Sky broadband margin compression 2018-22) demonstrate that ARPU uplift from network upgrade is not automatic; Openreach's regulated access framework explicitly caps wholesale price evolution

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: VMO2 has national network + pricing war history; alt-nets have low-cost structures and VC funding still active
- Motive: Market share defence in fibre transition period
- Opportunity: BT's transition period (FY26-28) is the window of maximum vulnerability — capex peak, subscriber churn, legacy copper switch-off noise
- All three legs present — ACTIVE failure mode, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: Sequential ARPU uplift of ≥3% per full-fibre customer vs. legacy copper baseline sustained for 4+ quarters; Openreach external CP volume growing >20% y/y on fibre; BT Consumer market share of fibre connections holding flat or improving against VMO2 in overlap footprint

Monitoring checklist:
- Openreach fibre active connections vs. premises passed (monthly)
- BT Consumer ARPU per product type (quarterly)
- Ofcom Openreach compliance and pricing review timeline
- VMO2 / Sky broadband net adds (quarterly)
- Alt-net build coverage overlap % with Openreach (semi-annual)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Narrative 3 (pricing ceiling) and Narrative 5 (margin compression via operating leverage); if Narrative 1 fires, probability of Narrative 5 rises materially

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence would be sustained ARPU uplift per connection AND external CP take-up growth — the hypothesis is invalidated only if BT demonstrates both volume AND price improvement simultaneously, not one in isolation

§3 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 2: Consumer Pricing Ceiling (Revenue Risk)

Category: Revenue risk — pricing pressure

Narrative: UK consumer broadband and mobile pricing reaches a structural ceiling as cost-of-living pressure persists into FY27; BT Group's CPI+3.9% annual price escalator — already politically and regulatorily scrutinised — faces either Ofcom intervention or mass lapse events when consumers downgrade or churn to SIM-only / MVNO / budget ISP alternatives

  • Transmission mechanism: CPI-linked price escalator removal or restriction → consumer revenue growth falls from c.4% to c.1-2% → EBITDA growth assumptions broken → consensus upgrades go into reverse; management guidance issued at c.4% consumer revenue growth is the first casualty

Early warning signals:
- Ofcom opens formal review of CPI-linked mobile/broadband contract terms (already flagged in 2024 consultation)
- BT Consumer churn rate rises >1.3% per month on broadband (vs. c.1.1% baseline)
- Competitor pricing anchors (Sky, VMO2) hold or reduce retail prices in new customer promotions
- Government or CMA intervention signals on escalator clauses in consumer contracts
- BT Consumer broadband subscriber base shows net decline for 2+ consecutive quarters

Plausibility: High plausibility — Ofcom's CPI+3.9% review has real regulatory traction; Virgin Media O2 and Sky have already committed to more transparent pricing; parliamentary pressure is documented; base rate for regulatory intervention on pricing clauses in UK utilities/telecoms is materially above 30% per 5-year cycle

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Ofcom has statutory authority; government has consumer protection mandate
- Motive: Political salience of household bills in cost-of-living environment
- Opportunity: BT's pricing review is due in the Ofcom forward workplan; timing aligns with FY27 guidance period
- All three legs present — ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: Ofcom formally closes the pricing review without restriction; BT Consumer churn rate declines sequentially; ARPU data shows consumers voluntarily upgrading vs. downgrading

Monitoring checklist:
- Ofcom pricing review timeline and consultation outcomes (quarterly)
- BT Consumer churn rate by product (quarterly)
- Competitor retail pricing promotions (monthly)
- CMA consumer contracts review (annual)
- BT Consumer ARPU vs. prior year (quarterly)

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 1 (ARPU ceiling) and Narrative 5 (margin pressure); independent of Enterprise revenue risk; if Narrative 2 fires it is a direct catalyst for analyst downgrade consensus shift

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence requires Ofcom to formally and definitively close the pricing review — without that, the threat persists as a monitoring item irrespective of BT's operational performance

§4 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 3: Enterprise / Government Revenue Erosion (Revenue Risk)

Category: Revenue risk — customer concentration and legacy migration

Narrative: BT Enterprise and Global (EE corporate + BT Business + BT Global Services rump) continues to lose revenue at a rate exceeding the market's modelled stabilisation; legacy voice, WAN, and managed services contracts roll off faster than cloud/security/SD-WAN replacements, and BT fails to retain margin on the replacement mix because hyperscalers (AWS, Microsoft) commoditise the connectivity layer

  • Transmission mechanism: Enterprise revenue declines at -4 to -6% p.a. vs. consensus expectation of stabilisation at -2%; group revenue growth targets missed; fixed-cost base over-scales for the shrinking enterprise footprint; EBITDA misses compound over 3 years

Early warning signals:
- BT Enterprise quarterly revenue decline accelerates beyond -4% y/y for 2+ quarters
- Key government/public sector contract losses (NHS, MoD framework) disclosed
- SD-WAN / managed security product revenue growth fails to offset legacy WAN declines in Enterprise segment disclosure
- Microsoft / AWS public cloud networking displacement accelerates in BT's largest accounts
- BT Enterprise headcount reduction signals over-running the plan (cost-out outpacing revenue stabilisation)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — BT Enterprise has been in structural decline for a decade and management has repeatedly guided to "approaching stabilisation" without delivering; however, the rump is now sufficiently small that a genuine floor is plausible; the failure mode is well-understood by the market and partially priced

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Hyperscalers have superior product, pricing, and ecosystem integration
- Motive: BT's enterprise base represents a large captive pool for displacement
- Opportunity: Legacy contract renewal cycles peak in FY26-28 (5-7yr contract vintage from 2019-21 signing)
- All three legs present — ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: BT Enterprise quarterly revenue decline slows to -1% or better for 2+ consecutive quarters AND a large public sector contract renewal (≥£100m TCV) is confirmed publicly

Monitoring checklist:
- BT Enterprise revenue by product line (quarterly)
- Contract renewal TCV announcements
- Hyperscaler direct-connectivity product launches in UK market (semi-annual)
- BT Enterprise operating margin vs. prior year (quarterly)
- Headcount and cost trajectory vs. revenue trajectory in Enterprise segment

Interaction with other failure modes: Partially correlated with Narrative 9 (management credibility — repeated guidance misses in Enterprise); low correlation with fibre narratives (separate revenue line); could amplify Narrative 10 (M&A — management may seek acquisition to fill Enterprise gap)

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence requires BT to demonstrate both revenue stabilisation AND margin preservation in Enterprise simultaneously — historical pattern shows cost-out precedes revenue stabilisation, so margin can look good while revenue continues to fall, making this a potential FALSE FRIEND indicator

§5 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 4: Capex Overrun / Fibre Build Cost Inflation (Margin/Profitability Risk)

Category: Profitability risk — cost inflation, capex discipline

Narrative: BT's fibre build cost per premise passed exceeds the £250-300 baseline assumption due to labour market tightness in telecoms engineering, materials cost inflation (ducting, cable), planning permission delays in dense urban areas, and the escalating complexity of multi-dwelling unit connections; the "cost savings fund the build" narrative breaks

  • Transmission mechanism: Capex per premise overrun of 10-15% → total peak capex rises from c.£5bn to c.£5.5-5.8bn → FCF profile delayed by 1-2 years → dividend covered but growth suspended; re-rating compression because the FCF inflection narrative is the primary multiple driver

Early warning signals:
- Openreach build rate falls below 750k premises/quarter (current run rate c.800k) for 2+ consecutive quarters without cost-per-premise improvement
- BT discloses upward revision to total FTTP build cost guidance (any upward revision from current c.£12bn total programme cost)
- UK construction labour cost indices show >5% annual increase in civils and telecom infrastructure labour
- Planning objection rates / wayleave disputes disclosed as materially above baseline in quarterly operational updates
- MDU penetration rates disclosed as below 30% at 12-month post-pass in pilot areas

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — BT has so far managed capex broadly in line; however, the final 20-30% of the build (hardest premises, MDU, rural) is systematically more expensive than the first 70%; peer builds internationally (Openfibre Italy, Inexio Germany, Cityfibre UK) all experienced cost step-ups in Phase 2

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Labour and materials inflation are exogenous and real
- Motive: N/A (structural cost pressure, not adversarial motive)
- Opportunity: The hardest portion of the build is ahead; current good execution does not guarantee Phase 2 delivery
- Two-of-three (no adversarial motive) — classify as WATCH, not immediate ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: BT explicitly confirms cost-per-premise holding at ≤£300 for the trailing 12-month build tranche; Openreach MDU pass rate disclosed as on-plan; build rate holds ≥750k/quarter in quarters 3-4 of each financial year (historically slower quarters)

Monitoring checklist:
- Openreach quarterly build rate vs. plan (quarterly)
- BT capex guidance updates vs. full-year target (each results announcement)
- UK BCIS telecoms civils cost index (quarterly)
- Planning permission / wayleave disputes in Openreach operational updates (semi-annual)
- Openreach cost-per-premise disclosed or inferred from segment disclosure (annual)

Interaction with other failure modes: Highly correlated with Narrative 6 (operating leverage failure) and Narrative 1 (fibre monetisation); compound failure of Narratives 1 + 4 produces the most dangerous outcome (revenue below plan, cost above plan simultaneously)

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence requires BT to explicitly disclose cost-per-premise — BT does NOT currently provide this figure, meaning this failure mode is practically unmonitorable in real time; the absence of disclosure is itself a yellow flag

§6 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 5: Operating Leverage Failure / Cost Programme Exhaustion (Margin/Profitability Risk)

Category: Profitability risk — operating leverage failure

Narrative: BT's £3bn gross cost savings programme (FY22-FY25 vintage, extended into FY26) exhausts its identified pipeline; incremental savings in FY27-28 prove structurally smaller because the easy cuts (offshoring, procurement, property rationalisation) are done; headcount reduction through voluntary redundancy becomes involuntary and costly; operating leverage fails to materialise as revenue growth slows

  • Transmission mechanism: Cost savings run rate falls from c.£800m/yr to c.£200-300m/yr post-FY26 → EBITDA growth depends entirely on revenue growth → if revenue flat, EBITDA flat → consensus assumes continued cost-driven EBITDA expansion into FY28; mis-set expectations produce re-rating

Early warning signals:
- BT discloses deceleration in gross cost savings run rate (any quarter below £600m annualised run rate)
- Employee restructuring charge falls (signals pipeline is exhausted, not improving)
- BT resets cost savings guidance downward at any CMD/results
- EBITDA margins fail to expand in FY27 despite revenue growth (cost offset failed)
- SG&A or network opex line shows unexpected inflation relative to guidance

Plausibility: High plausibility — cost programme exhaustion is the canonical failure mode in UK telecoms post-restructuring (BT, Vodafone, TalkTalk all experienced this); the savings pipeline has a natural expiry; BT has already taken £2.5bn+ of the £3bn target; the incremental opportunity narrows sharply

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Fixed-cost structures, union agreements, and operational constraints limit further savings
- Motive: N/A (structural exhaustion)
- Opportunity: FY27 is the most likely point of exhaustion given programme vintage
- Two-of-three — WATCH status per MMO; however, high plausibility elevates monitoring priority

Disconfirming evidence: BT announces a new cost savings programme of ≥£1bn with identified pipeline at a CMD; OR EBITDA margins expand sequentially in FY27 driven by revenue ARPU (not cost savings) — demonstrating organic operating leverage rather than programme-driven leverage

Monitoring checklist:
- BT gross cost savings disclosure per half-year results
- SG&A / network opex as % of revenue (quarterly)
- EBITDA margin bridge (cost vs. revenue contribution) at half-year results
- Restructuring charge trajectory (declining = pipeline exhausted)
- FTE count vs. prior year (semi-annual)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Narrative 4 (capex overrun); partially correlated with Narrative 2 (pricing ceiling — if revenue is capped, cost programme is the only remaining EBITDA driver); this is the single most dangerous correlated cluster with FALSE FRIEND properties

Sceptical bullet: BT's EBITDA beats in FY25-26 are almost entirely cost-programme-driven; an analyst reading "EBITDA beat" as evidence of durable margin expansion may be observing the FALSE FRIEND — the beat is real but its source is temporary

§7 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 6: Pension Liability Re-Emergence (Profitability / Balance Sheet Risk)

Category: Balance sheet risk — pension / interest rate sensitivity

Narrative: BT's c.£47bn defined benefit pension scheme is reported on an IAS 19 basis with a surplus under current discount rate assumptions; a 150-200bps decline in corporate bond yields (e.g., in a UK recession / BoE cutting cycle) would convert the IAS 19 surplus to a deficit, triggering a triennial valuation renegotiation with the trustee and potentially a material cash contribution schedule that competes directly with the dividend and capex plan

  • Transmission mechanism: Yield decline → IAS 19 deficit → trustee negotiation → cash contributions of £200-400m/yr reintroduced → FCF available for dividend and buyback falls materially → dividend sustainability narrative pressured

Early warning signals:
- UK 10-year gilt yield falls below 3.5% and sustains for 2+ quarters
- BTPS trustee publicly signals concern about funding adequacy at any point
- BT discloses a change in actuarial assumptions at annual report
- UK life expectancy tables updated upward (increases liability duration)
- BT's IAS 19 surplus falls below £1bn (vs. current c.£3-4bn surplus)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — pension risk is real but rate-path-dependent; the current surplus is genuine under current rates; however, BoE cutting cycle is underway and the risk is not symmetric; UK DB pension risk is well-precedented (BT itself paid £2bn/yr contributions pre-2021)

⚡ OUTLIER FLAG: The pension risk receives almost zero sell-side monitoring airtime in the current bull case — virtually no Q4-Q14 ESA memo commentary addresses this; in a rate-cutting environment the IAS 19 surplus is more fragile than consensus assumes, and the trustee has explicit authority to demand higher contributions independent of BT's management preferences

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Actuarial framework, trustee powers, and gilt yield trajectory all exist
- Motive: Trustee has legal duty to act in member interests; no adversarial motive required
- Opportunity: Triennial valuation due FY27; rate-cutting cycle creates adverse timing
- All three legs present — ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: BT confirms at FY27 triennial valuation that no additional cash contributions are required; BoE rate path stabilises above 4% through FY28; BTPS IAS 19 surplus expands or holds

Monitoring checklist:
- UK 10-year gilt yield (continuous)
- BT annual report IAS 19 assumptions and surplus/deficit position
- BTPS trustee public communications (annual)
- Triennial valuation date and outcome (FY27 due)
- BT pension contributions in cash flow statement vs. prior year

Interaction with other failure modes: Low direct correlation with revenue narratives; high correlation with Narrative 8 (FCF and dividend — pension contributions directly reduce FCF available for distribution); compound failure of Narrative 5 + 6 (cost programme exhaustion + pension cash call) is the highest-severity scenario

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence for this failure mode is entirely rate-path-dependent — BT cannot operationally disconfirm the pension risk; the only disconfirming evidence is an external macro variable (gilt yields staying high), which the analyst does not control

§8 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 7: Altnet Competition / Structural Overbuild (Competitive / Structural Risk)

Category: Competitive risk — new entrant / structural overbuild

Narrative: UK alt-net builders (CityFibre, Netomnia, Nexfibre, Gigaclear, Hyperoptic, and the Virgin Media O2 upgrade) collectively overbuild Openreach's fibre footprint in Tier 2/3 urban areas more aggressively than BT's planning assumes; take-up rates for Openreach fibre fall below the 30-35% 3-year assumption in overbuild areas; alt-net-induced price competition structurally lowers industry ARPU

  • Transmission mechanism: Overbuild in 30-40% of Openreach's addressable fibre market → Openreach take-up rate in those areas falls to 20-25% → wholesale revenue per passed premise below business case → Openreach regulated return on capital squeezed → BT Group EBITDA misses by £200-400m cumulatively FY27-29

Early warning signals:
- Alt-net coverage reaches >10m premises with aggressive retail pricing
- Ofcom overbuild metrics show >40% of Openreach fibre footprint subject to direct infrastructure competition
- Openreach external CP wins below 40% of line growth vs. alt-net CPs
- BT Consumer market share in alt-net overlap areas declines >2ppt in 2 consecutive half-years
- Alt-net capital raising continues at scale (post-2025 VC funding indicators)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — alt-net build pace has slowed due to funding pressures (2023-25 VC/PE retrenchment); however, VMO2's network upgrade programme is structural and well-funded; the overbuild risk in urban areas is real; CityFibre has secured long-term anchor tenants (Vodafone, TalkTalk) reducing default risk

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Alt-nets have capital (if funding holds), infrastructure, and regulatory support
- Motive: Market share acquisition in a winner-takes-most fibre transition
- Opportunity: Transition period FY26-28 when Openreach is building but not yet dominant in each area
- All three legs present — ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: Alt-net build rate falls materially below 1m premises/yr; major alt-net funding withdrawals occur (VC/PE exits forcing consolidation); Ofcom data shows Openreach fibre take-up in overbuild areas matching non-overbuild areas within 5ppt

Monitoring checklist:
- Ofcom Connected Nations report (annual/semi-annual) — overbuild data
- Alt-net premises passed and take-up rates (quarterly operator disclosures)
- Alt-net capital raise announcements (continuous)
- BT Consumer market share in fibre (quarterly)
- Wholesale pricing pressure signals from Openreach CPs

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 1 (fibre monetisation) and Narrative 2 (pricing ceiling); partially correlated with Narrative 9 (management credibility — management has downplayed alt-net risk repeatedly)

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence relies on alt-net funding withdrawals — which are partially exogenous (PE/VC environment) and not fully monitorable from public data; the failure mode may materialise slowly and only become visible in Ofcom's annual Connected Nations report, by which time thesis damage is done

§9 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 8: FCF / Dividend Sustainability Failure (FALSE FRIEND — PRIMARY)

Category: FALSE FRIEND — earnings beat from unsustainable FCF structure

Narrative: BT's FCF appears to improve sequentially through FY26-27, and dividend payments look covered on reported metrics; however, the FCF is inflated by working capital timing, capex accrual management, and pension contribution holidays that are not sustainable; when normalised, the dividend cover is below 1.0x and the payout requires either debt increase or dividend cut; the thesis appears confirmed when it is actually deteriorating

  • Transmission mechanism: Reported FCF beats → no dividend cut announced → investor confidence builds → price appreciates → sudden normalised FCF disclosure or analyst-triggered FCF bridge reveals structural shortfall → sharp de-rating at the moment of maximum crowding

Early warning signals:
- Working capital as % of revenue deteriorates (payables extension, receivables shortening)
- Capex cash spend in cash flow statement consistently below P&L depreciation (deferred maintenance signal)
- Pension contributions in cash flow statement fall to zero (contribution holiday — unsustainable)
- "Adjusted FCF" to "reported FCF" reconciliation gap widens year-on-year
- Net debt / EBITDA fails to decline despite positive reported FCF (debt not actually falling)

Plausibility: High plausibility — FCF bridge management is endemic in UK telecoms; BT has historically used pension contribution scheduling, lease accounting adjustments, and capex phasing to manage reported FCF; base rate for FCF-to-dividend sustainability issues in investment-grade UK infrastructure-adjacent companies is non-trivial (Carillion, Thames Water, Centrica all exhibited this pattern)

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Accounting flexibility in FCF definition is real and BT uses an "adjusted FCF" metric
- Motive: Dividend commitment is a primary anchor for BT's retail/institutional shareholder base; management has strong incentive to maintain
- Opportunity: Capex peak period provides maximum flexibility to phase timing; pension holiday is operationally possible for 1-2 years
- All three legs present — ACTIVE, HIGH DANGER (FALSE FRIEND)

Disconfirming evidence: BT provides a detailed FCF bridge from operating cash to dividend capacity, explicitly excluding working capital timing items; net debt / EBITDA falls sequentially for 4+ consecutive quarters; dividend is raised, not merely maintained (raising dividend is harder to fake than maintaining)

Monitoring checklist:
- FCF definition in results presentations — track any changes year-on-year
- Cash capex vs. P&L depreciation ratio (quarterly)
- Working capital movements in cash flow statement (quarterly)
- Pension cash contributions in cash flow (annual)
- Net debt absolute level vs. EBITDA (quarterly)

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the AGGREGATOR — Narrative 8 is the financial transmission point for Narratives 1, 4, 5, and 6 all appearing in FCF; if any one of those narratives partially fires, Narrative 8 is the mechanism by which it becomes a dividend risk; HIGH CORRELATION with all revenue and margin narratives

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence must include net debt actually declining — not "adjusted net debt" or "pro-forma net debt"; BT's net debt absolute level is the single hardest metric to manipulate and therefore the most reliable disconfirming data point for this failure mode

§10 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 9: Management Credibility / Guidance Track Record (Execution Risk — FALSE FRIEND)

Category: FALSE FRIEND — management credibility appears restored when guidance miss risk is structurally embedded

Narrative: CEO Allison Kirkby has delivered early-tenure credibility signals (cost programme acceleration, CMD2023 framework, Openreach fibre milestones) that appear to confirm management quality; however, the guidance framework inherited legacy optimism from prior management (Jansen-era EBITDA trajectories) and the FY28 targets assume execution across fibre ARPU, Enterprise stabilisation, and cost exhaustion simultaneously — a triple-execution dependency that no UK telco has achieved concurrently

  • Transmission mechanism: One of three execution pillars misses → EBITDA guidance withdrawn or rebased → market re-rates from "recovering telco" multiple to "distressed telco" multiple → 20-30% de-rating irrespective of fundamental value

Early warning signals:
- FY27 EBITDA guidance issued at a CMD that is below sell-side consensus (first public guidance cut)
- Enterprise revenue decline accelerates beyond -4% y/y despite management stabilisation narrative
- Management turnover: CFO or Openreach CEO departure before FY27 programme completion
- Capital Markets Day postponed or cancelled (a historically reliable signal of guidance confidence loss)
- BT issues a profit warning outside the normal results calendar

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — Kirkby has genuine operational credibility vs. predecessors; however, the FY28 targets require multi-year simultaneous execution; the base rate for UK large-cap telco management delivering on 5-year CMD targets is below 50% (BT 2016 CMD targets, Vodafone 2020 CMD targets both missed materially)

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: External headwinds (narratives 1, 2, 3, 7) could prevent delivery irrespective of management quality
- Motive: Management has incentive to maintain guidance as long as possible (option value, bonus structure)
- Opportunity: FY26-27 is the guidance-test window
- All three legs present — ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: BT raises FY28 EBITDA target above CMD2023 guidance at a subsequent CMD; Enterprise quarterly revenue shows positive growth for 2+ quarters; no senior management departures in FY26-27

Monitoring checklist:
- EBITDA guidance trajectory vs. CMD2023 baseline (each results announcement)
- BT Enterprise quarterly revenue growth rate
- Senior management announcements (continuous)
- CMD scheduling (annual)
- Market consensus EBITDA estimate revision direction (monthly)

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the CONFIDENCE AMPLIFIER — if any of Narratives 1-7 fires, Narrative 9 amplifies the de-rating because management credibility is the secondary multiple driver; correlated with ALL revenue and margin narratives

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence for this narrative is a raised CMD target — which management will not issue unless they are highly confident; the absence of a target raise is neutral, not confirmatory; the failure mode is most dangerous precisely because it appears neutral until it isn't

§11 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 10: M&A / Capital Allocation Failure (Execution Risk)

Category: Execution risk — M&A integration / capital misallocation

Narrative: BT deploys balance sheet capacity (post-fibre-peak FCF, or debt capacity) in an M&A transaction — either defensive (acquiring an alt-net to pre-empt overbuild) or expansive (acquiring a content or enterprise services asset) — that destroys value via overpayment, integration complexity, or distraction from the fibre programme; the history of BT M&A (EE acquisition 2016, Global Services legacy) provides the base rate

  • Transmission mechanism: Debt-funded acquisition → leverage increases → FCF available for dividend compressed → multiple compression as "strategic risk" premium applied → thesis de-railed even if underlying fibre economics are on track

Early warning signals:
- BT board-level statements about "portfolio optionality" or "strategic flexibility" increase in frequency
- Press / analyst community reports BT evaluating alt-net acquisitions (Cityfibre, Nexfibre)
- BT investor relations flagging "disciplined M&A" as a capital allocation priority
- Leverage guidance changes to accommodate "strategic optionality" headroom
- Activist pressure to monetise Openreach stake triggers defensive M&A response

Plausibility: Low plausibility in the near term (FY26-27) — BT is in capex peak and has limited balance sheet capacity; however, post-capex-peak (FY28+), management will face pressure to deploy FCF; the historical base rate for UK large-cap management deploying post-restructuring FCF in value-destructive M&A is high

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: Post-peak FCF and investment-grade credit rating provide capacity
- Motive: Management desire for growth narrative when organic growth plateaus
- Opportunity: Post-FY27 capex normalisation creates the timing window
- Two-of-three (opportunity is forward-dated, not current) — WATCH status, not immediate ACTIVE

Disconfirming evidence: BT explicitly commits to a capital return framework (buyback programme or special dividend) post-capex-peak, removing the ambiguity about capital deployment; OR BT sells a non-core asset (remaining international businesses) demonstrating disposal rather than acquisition discipline

Monitoring checklist:
- BT strategic statements on capital allocation at each CMD
- Press speculation on M&A (continuous)
- Leverage trajectory post-FY27 capex peak
- Activist investor position changes (semi-annual)
- Analyst community M&A scenario modelling prevalence

Interaction with other failure modes: Independent of revenue narratives; correlated with Narrative 9 (management credibility) and Narrative 6 (pension — if pension cash calls arrive simultaneously with M&A ambition, balance sheet stress escalates)

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence for this narrative is a concrete capital return commitment — the absence of which keeps the risk on the monitoring list; management's track record on M&A discipline is the prior on which to anchor, not their stated intentions

§11b — FAILURE NARRATIVE 11: Technology / Infrastructure Disruption (Competitive / Structural Risk)

Category: Competitive risk — technology disruption
