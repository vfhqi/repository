EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model and Organizational Structure

Grupo Catalana Occidente operates through three primary segments: Occident (traditional multi-line insurance), Atradius (global credit insurance), and Mémora (funeral services) , creating resilient revenue diversification across traditional insurance, global credit cover, and funeral services to buffer macroeconomic cyclicality . Occident generated 55.6% of total turnover , Atradius contributed 40.0% , and Mémora represented 4.4% in FY2025 , while Spain represented 65.8% of GCO's total revenues , exposing the group to concentrated domestic economic conditions. Corporate simplification consolidated traditional underwriting subsidiaries by integrating Seguros Catalana Occidente, Plus Ultra, Seguros Bilbao, and NorteHispana Seguros into a unified Occident platform by January 2025 , achieving headcount reductions of 20% via a voluntary exit plan and generating substantial operating synergies. Controlling shareholder INOC launched a voluntary takeover bid in March 2025, achieving 94.27% acceptance and leading to GCO's delisting in January 2026 , fundamentally altering the group's capital structure and governance flexibility. 1 2 3

Financial Performance and Peer Benchmarking

GCO achieved €6,260 million in consolidated turnover during FY2025, representing 4.70% year-on-year growth , though Atradius experienced premium growth deceleration to 1.10% in H1-2025 amid sluggish global trade volumes. Occident delivered a combined ratio of 90.30% for FY2025, consistently outperforming the Spanish multi-line sector average of 92.20% , while Atradius sustained superior credit underwriting profitability with a combined ratio of 76.40% , highly competitive versus peer Coface's 73.10% . GCO's portfolio yield increased to 3.06% in September 2025 , improving steadily from 3.03% in FY2022 due to rising European central bank interest rates , directly benefiting financial margins. The €387.50 million Mémora acquisition in February 2023 was financed by a €400 million loan , initially penalizing GCO's Solvency II ratio by 20 percentage points due to goodwill adjustments , though the solvency ratio recovered to 231.40% at year-end FY2025 , demonstrating robust capital regeneration capacity. Consolidated profits grew 7.80% to €742.40 million in FY2025, led by Atradius ordinary profits of €412.90 million and Occident at €336.60 million , while GCO's estimated return on equity (ROE) of 11.30% aligned with credit peer Coface's 11.40% but remained below domestic competitor Mapfre Iberia's 13.60% . 6

Industry Structure and Competitive Position

Three global insurers account for around 60.00% of the global credit insurance market , with Atradius holding the second position globally with a 19.00% market share as of 2024 , creating a highly disciplined oligopolistic structure with significant barriers to entry. GCO holds a 5.80% market share in the Spanish non-life insurance segment , remaining smaller than domestic leader Mapfre's 11.40% , yet GCO ranks fourth in Spain's multi- risk segment with 9.10% share and commands 43.50% of Spain's credit insurance market through Crédito y Caución , securing absolute domestic leadership in trade credit. The global trade credit insurance market is protected by immense structural barriers , requiring entrants to build massive, expensive global debtor databases and maintain vast international networks of specialized credit analysts , while the triopoly maintains high underwriting and pricing discipline , targeting combined ratios below 80.00% to secure underwriting profitability . The upcoming Solvency II review, applicable in January 2027, will reduce quantitative capital requirements , resulting in capital releases that may provide additional strategic flexibility across the European insurance sector. 12 13

Customer Dynamics and Switching Costs

Occident sustained excellent customer loyalty , registering a stable retention rate of 87.80% in 2025 , while Atradius achieved an outstanding 93.70% customer retention rate in Spain and 94.30% retention globally , reflecting deep client relationships and high service quality. GCO mitigates risk by requiring trade credit policyholders to insure their entire debtor portfolio , protecting Atradius from clients only seeking insurance for highly distressed buyers , effectively eliminating adverse selection. The Atradius Flow add-on integrates directly into client Microsoft Dynamics ERP platforms , creating high operational barriers to switching insurers through seamless automation of credit administration , while the proprietary Atrium portal was rebuilt with advanced features to streamline real-time credit limit requests, and Atradius Agora allows international policyholders to submit and track debt recovery cases across 16 countries , embedding GCO deeply into client workflows. 22

Supply Chain and Distribution Dependencies

Occident allocates 98% of total supply expenditures to local Spanish suppliers , Atradius allocates 82% to local suppliers globally, and Mémora allocates 99% to local suppliers , minimizing execution delays but concentrating operational risk on regional cost inflation. The Symphony database acts as the single repository containing the vast majority of credit limits, with credit analysts using this infrastructure to track default risks across global counterparties, sectors, and nations, representing both a critical operational dependency and a formidable competitive moat. Occident operates with more than 14,000 mediators across Spain and maintained 1,366 physical offices at year-end 2023 to anchor local agent activities, creating high geographic barriers to entry for digital-only competitors while requiring ongoing investment in physical infrastructure.
