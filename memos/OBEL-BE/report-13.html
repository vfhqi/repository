§1 — Current Management Guidance

Orange Belgium's current guidance stack is anchored to the FY25 fiscal year and the multi-year "Fit for Future" strategic plan running to FY26; guidance was most recently reaffirmed at the FY24 full-year results release (February 2025).

  • Verdict — current guidance: Management has reaffirmed FY25 revenue growth and EBITDA-AL corridor targets; medium-term FY26 plan remains formally in place but glide-path execution is behind [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: FY25 revenue guidance is low-to-mid single-digit % growth on an organic basis (~€1.55–1.60bn corridor, per February 2025 results release); no hard numeric ceiling published [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: Revenue guidance implicitly excludes material new M&A beyond VOO, which was already consolidated from mid-2023 onward [#I]

  • FY current — EBITDA-AL: FY25 EBITDA-AL guided to ~€540–560m corridor, representing ~33–34% margin; management language is "in line with or above FY24 level" [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: FY24 EBITDA-AL printed €527m (post-restated for IFRS 16); so "in line with or above" sets the floor at ~€527m with no stated ceiling [#I]

  • FY current — EBIT: No standalone EBIT guidance published; implied from D&A guidance (~€420–430m) applied to EBITDA-AL corridor [#I]

  • FY current — FCF: FY25 FCF (equity FCF after leases) guided to ~€50–80m positive; very wide corridor reflects VOO integration cost residuals and spectrum payment timing [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: Peer comparison: Proximus guides FCF to a similar absolute corridor at ~€300m scale (far larger base); OBEL's FCF corridor as % of revenue (~3–5%) is at the low end of small-cap European telcos [#I]

  • FY current — CapEx: FY25 CapEx (ex-spectrum, ex-lease) guided to ~€220–240m, declining ~5–10% vs FY24 (~€250m); management cites post-VOO network rationalisation as driver [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: CapEx/revenue ratio guided ~14–16%, consistent with Belgian telco sector average post-5G peak; KPN equivalent ~14–15% [#I]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY26): "Fit for Future" plan targets €580m+ EBITDA-AL implying ~35%+ margin; management has not formally withdrawn this target despite FY23–24 glide-path slippage [#A]

  • Medium-term targets: Three pillars cited: (1) B2B revenue growth to €400m+ by FY26; (2) convergent customer penetration >40% of base; (3) net debt/EBITDA declining toward 2.0x by FY26 [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: All three medium-term pillars remain formally "on track" per management language; no individual target has been explicitly cut [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: FY24 results call (February 2025); H1 2024 results call (August 2024); Capital Markets Day references (2023) [#I]

  • Source — written: FY24 annual report, press release guidance table; Fit for Future plan deck (2022) [#I]

  • Update timing: Last formal update February 2025 (FY24 results); next update H1 2025 results (~August 2025) [#I]

  • Caveat: All guidance explicitly excludes "material spectrum or regulatory events"; VOO one-time integration costs are partially excluded from some line items [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: EBITDA-AL targets assume Belgian household broadband ARPU stabilisation; any further price undercutting from Telenet or Proximus bundles would pressure the corridor [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §1: Bearish read: "in line with or above FY24" as the EBITDA-AL floor is a classic escape-hatch formulation; it sets no growth expectation and allows any outcome above stagnation to be claimed as guidance delivery; the medium-term €580m FY26 target implies a €50m EBITDA step-up in a single year (FY25→FY26) for which no operational mechanism has been publicly modelled [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Verdict — guidance pattern: Dual-register pattern; management is a modest sandbagging on revenue (credible) and a consistent over-promiser on EBITDA-AL and FCF (structurally concerning) [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Revenue guidance has been set below actual delivery in all four reviewable periods; beats are small (+1.2% mean) and consistent; this resembles deliberate conservatism rather than forecasting error [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: [AS-Degroof Petercam] notes management "systematically guides revenue corridors on the lower end of achievable range," consistent with parent Orange SA group guidance culture [#I]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBITDA: EBITDA-AL guidance misses in 3 of 4 periods; management consistently targets margin corridors implying cost efficiencies that arrive late or below forecast magnitude [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: The over-optimism appears most pronounced on the cost-efficiency timeline: savings guided for H2 have repeatedly shifted to the following year [#I]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: FCF guidance is set to an aspirational level; the wide corridor (e.g., €50–80m for FY25) provides 30m EUR of slack but has still been missed in 3 of 4 years when using corridor midpoints [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EBIT: EBIT accuracy is mixed; FY21–22 in-line, FY23 severe miss (-19%), FY24 miss (-11%); the large FY23 miss was EBITDA-plus-D&A driven, not a standalone phenomenon [#A]

  • Pattern shift — post-VOO: Pre-VOO (FY21–22) the pattern was broadly honest-tracker: small revenue beats, marginal EBITDA misses; post-VOO (FY23–24) the pattern deteriorated to persistent over-promising across profitability metrics [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: EBITDA-AL mean miss magnitude (excluding FY21) is ~2.9%; at the sector level, Berenberg's 2024 European telco survey shows median EBITDA guidance miss of 1.1%; OBEL is approximately 2.6x the sector median miss rate [#I]

  • Magnitude — beat: Revenue beat magnitude (+1.2% mean) is in-line with sector median revenue beat of +1.0–1.5%; no outlier [#I]

  • ⚡ RARE — dual-register discipline: OBEL's consistent revenue-beat + EBITDA-miss pairing across all four review periods is a statistically unusual pattern; the majority of European telcos that beat revenue also beat EBITDA (positive operating leverage is the typical mechanism); OBEL's consistent decoupling suggests a structural cost-base problem, not random forecasting error; this is a qualitative "things that make me go hmmmm" signal [Cross-ref: §2 + §6] [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §3: Bearish read: the revenue sandbagging provides false comfort; if management's revenue conservatism is offsetting their EBITDA over-optimism in terms of headline narrative, investors may be anchoring on the beat-on-revenue signal and missing the more important miss-on-margin signal; the revenue beat has zero operating leverage flow-through, implying below-average incremental margins on upside revenue [#J]
