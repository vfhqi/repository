Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The company operates a resilient, highly diversified omnichannel model with dominant retail market shares in Spain and Latin America providing a defensive base to fund high-growth online gaming.

Segment and Product Breakdown

1.
Dominant Casino division: Physical casinos and gaming halls represent the largest business unit, contributing approximately 53% of total group EBITDA as of late 2025
2.
Recurring Slots Spain: Street slot routes in bars and cafes across Spain account for approximately 28% of group EBITDA, generating highly stable and recurring daily revenue streams
3.
Rapid Online growth: Digital casino and sports betting activities are the fastest-growing division, representing 16% of group EBITDA and 23% of net revenues by late 2025
4.
Regulated Slots Italy: This unit operates slot machines in central and northern Italy under regulatory concessions, contributing a smaller 5% of group EBITDA
5.
Vertical manufacturing integration: Vertical integration in Spain allows the B2B segment to supply 81% of new slot machines internally, lowering capital expenditure and accelerating machine rotation

Geographic and Value Chain Positioning

6.
Geographic revenue balance: Revenues are geographically balanced, with Spain contributing 41% of net revenues, Italy contributing 25%, and Latin American markets generating over 30%
7.
Core Latin markets: Within Latin America, the group is a market leader in Panama, Colombia, Peru, Costa Rica, and the Dominican Republic, catering to highly recurring local leisure demand
8.
Intermediary value position: Cirsa operates as an integrated retail and digital operator, holding the proprietary licenses and entering into long-term revenue-sharing or lease contracts with local venues
9.
Focus local players: Unlike tourism-dependent destination casinos, 94% of Cirsa's players are local, frequent visitors living within a few kilometers of the physical properties

Competitive Moats and Industry Structure Moats

10.
Substantial scale advantages: Being significantly larger than regional mom-and-pop competitors allows Cirsa to spread cash-collection and rapid-response repair overheads across 40,602 slot machines
11.
Formidable regulatory moats: Operational licenses act as a formidable competitive moat, as Spain requires separate approvals from 17 autonomous regions, and Latin American concessions are tightly capped
12.
Seamless omnichannel wallet: A proprietary single-wallet system in Spain and Latin America enables seamless funds transfer between retail and online channels, driving superior player retention
13.
Highly stable concessions: Most operating licenses are renewed automatically at negligible costs through administrative processes, protecting long-term market access
14.
Other side of the story: Sceptics argue that rising digital gaming penetration will trigger a material profit dilution because the online business unit operates at a significantly lower EBITDA margin compared to land-based divisions

Section 5 – The possible investment case 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible investment case strengthens significantly due to high visibility on massive post- refinancing interest savings and a high-ROI casino renovation program driving organic growth.

Driver 1 – Post-IPO Interest Savings

1.
Guaranteed financial savings: Post-IPO bond refinancings are locked in, delivering €62 million in annualized interest savings starting in FY26
2.
High driver visibility: Plausibility is high, as Q1 2026 financial expenses already dropped 34.0% year-on-year to €34.6 million
3.
M&A leverage threat: The driver would only fail to boost EPS if management undertook highly expensive, debt- fueled acquisitions

Driver 2 – Goldmine Casino Renovation Program

4.
Casino space expansions: The "Goldmine" strategy implements space extensions and facility renovations across 20 selected high-performing physical properties
5.
Accelerated project delivery: Plausibility is high, with 14 of the 20 planned projects successfully completed before August 2026
6.
Macroeconomic demand risks: Goldmine fails if macro headwinds in Latin America trigger severe local consumer discretionary spending declines

Driver 3 – Spanish Slot Machine Replacement

7.
Record Spain margins: The rollout of market-leading slot products like Manhattan Magic and Premium increases average revenue per machine
8.
Proven operational scaling: Plausibility is high, with Slots Spain achieving 13.1% revenue growth in Q1 2026 on a stable machine footprint
9.
Regulatory tax risks: Driver fails if regional governments in Spain implement abrupt increases in fixed annual taxes per machine

Driver 4 – Online Scaling and Peru Taxes

10.
Rapid online growth: Online Gaming continues to scale rapidly, with turnovers expanding 22.4% in Q1 2026 and customer unique count up 7%
11.
Severe online margin drag: Plausibility is medium, as excellent top-line expansion is offset by progressive gaming taxes in Peru
12.
Peru tax impact: The progressive online tax in Peru fully implemented in late 2025 created a 539 basis point margin headwind in Q1 2026

Driver Synergy and Long-Term Targets

13.
Independent earnings driver: Interest savings are independent and locked in regardless of operational expansion speed
14.
Other side of the story: To hit the bull-case EPS targets, the group must successfully mature early-stage online operations in Colombia, Mexico, and Panama to add a projected 150 to 200 basis points to online margins

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Cirsa shares look unusually cheap and highly demanding of attention because they trade at a bottom-of-the-sector multiple that heavily discounts its consistent earnings growth profile.
1.
Bottom-end EBITDA multiple: The stock trades at 5.4x FY26E EV/EBITDA, representing the absolute bottom end of the European gaming sector
2.
Deep historical discount: The current multiple is a steep discount to the 7.0x EV/EBITDA paid by Blackstone in 2018, despite EBITDA doubling
3.
Compelling peer discount: Shares trade at 4.5x FY27E EV/EBITDA, representing a significant 30% discount to direct peer Lottomatica which trades at roughly 7.1x EV/EBITDA
4.
Uncovered stock value: The price-to-earnings multiple of 7.4x FY26E falls to a highly attractive 6.7x on FY27E estimates
5.
Compelling target price: The sell-side median price target of €19.30 to €20.00 implies compelling upside of 47% to 53% from the current trading price of €13.08
6.
Other side of the story: Sceptics argue the valuation discount is structurally justified by the persistent risk of share flowback as Blackstone seeks to gradually sell down its massive 72% stake

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderately high but manageable as progressive regulatory tax changes and Latin American exchange rate fluctuations represent the primary sources of potential earnings volatility.

Regulatory and Tax Moat Threats

1.
Severe gaming tax risk: Abrupt regulatory tax changes represent the primary threat to profitability, as seen in Peru where progressive online taxes slashed Q1 2026 online margins by 5.39%
2.
Concession Bullet Payment: Italian Slot concessions are subject to periodic renewals, which could trigger a material future bullet payment estimated at €35 million to €40 million

Geographic and Macroeconomic Vulnerabilities

3.
Currency translation volatility: With Latin America generating 41% of group EBITDA, translation risks to the Euro are material, particularly from a weaker US Dollar or Colombian Peso
4.
Consumer spend impact: Rising inflation or political instability in core LatAm markets could severely impact regional consumer discretionary leisure budgets

Operational Overhang and Technicals

5.
Selling overhang risk: Blackstone's reduction of its stake from 78.4% to 72% in April 2026 creates a persistent selling overhang, capping near-term share price performance
6.
Deceptive online metrics: Robust top-line online turnover growth of 22% is a false friend, as it masks severe EBITDA contraction due to tax hikes and lower gross margins
7.
Critical warning indicator: Investors must closely monitor the monthly Latin American foreign exchange movements, particularly the Colombian Peso and Mexican Peso against the Euro, as a leading indicator
8.
Other side of the story: Bulls argue that Cirsa's extensive geographic footprint across 11 countries acts as a powerful shock absorber, ensuring that regulatory or tax shocks in any single market cannot break the group's consolidated earnings trajectory

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Management is transitioning toward improved corporate governance standards following its successful IPO while the stable leadership team remains focused on operational execution and capital discipline.
1.
Separation of powers: The board split the Chairman and CEO roles in April 2022, appointing long-time executive Antonio Hostench as CEO to manage operations, while Joaquim Agut became Executive Chairman focusing on governance
2.
Deep industry tenure: The core leadership team boasts outstanding stability, with the Executive Chairman and CEO bringing an average of 18 years of gaming industry experience
3.
Sponsor share reduction: Blackstone began reducing its majority shareholding on April 1, 2026, placing 4.2% of the company's capital at €12.75 per share, which expanded the free float
4.
Governance targets integration: Governance standards have been enhanced through a new 2025-2027 Remuneration Policy, which links 20% of management's variable compensation to ESG and responsible gaming targets
