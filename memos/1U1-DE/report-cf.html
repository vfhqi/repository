EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Activist Presence shows no active activist investors holding positions above 3% ownership or engaging in public campaigns, with the shareholder structure dominated by parent United Internet AG's 86.46% stake following a voluntary partial buyout offer at €18.50 in May 2025. The passive minority shareholding of 4.51% held by value investor Norman Rentrop represents the only notable outside institutional position, yet this investor has not pursued board representation or public demands for strategic changes. The concentrated ownership structure effectively precludes traditional activist intervention, as United Internet's voting control enables unilateral strategic decisions including the Versatel acquisition and segment reorganization without requiring minority shareholder approval. This ownership concentration creates a stable shareholder base that supports long-term capital-intensive network investments, yet simultaneously limits minority float liquidity and reduces market discipline on management execution, as parent company alignment may prioritize group-level synergies over standalone 1&1 shareholder value maximization in scenarios where network rollout economics deteriorate.

1.
Investment Verdict: 1&1 AG represents a high-risk, capital-intensive transformation with structurally depressed standalone profitability and extreme valuation optionality contingent on strategic M&A rescue scenarios or favorable regulatory spectrum relief, positioning the equity as a leveraged call option on corporate consolidation rather than a defensible operational investment case
