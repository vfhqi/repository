B.1 Business Description 14 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What TKH does. TKH Group is a Dutch technology conglomerate designing and manufacturing machine-vision systems, automated tyre-building machinery, and subsea and onshore power cables and fibre-optic connectivity, listed on Euronext Amsterdam under the legacy ticker TWEKA (from former name Twentsche Kabel Holding)
2.
Three-segment structure. The group currently reports Smart Vision (machine vision and imaging, 29.5% of FY2025 turnover), Smart Manufacturing (tyre-building machinery and robotics, 29.5%), and Smart Connectivity/Electrification (subsea and onshore cables, fibre, 41.0%)
3.
The core catalyst. Management has committed to a dual-track separation of the Electrification division, valued at approximately EUR 0.8bn (over 50% of group market capitalisation), targeted to complete in 4Q26 or 1Q27
4.
Post-separation shape. The remaining "new TKH" becomes an asset-light Automation core (Smart Vision plus Smart Manufacturing, approximately 60% of historical turnover) targeting a 17-19% EBITA margin by FY2028, though organic growth ambition was simultaneously cut to 5-7% per annum from over 7%
5.
Financial scale. FY2025 group revenue was EUR 1,761.2m, adjusted EBITA margin 10.8% (down from 11.9% in FY2024), with market capitalisation of approximately EUR 1.9-2.0bn at the EUR 46.76 live price
6.
Segment margin dispersion is extreme. Smart Manufacturing (tyre-building machinery) runs at approximately 19-20% EBITA margin with over 70% global market share in outsourced tyre-building equipment, while Electrification's EBITA margin collapsed to an all-time low of 2.3% in FY2025, down from 4.9% in FY2024
7.
Balance sheet. Net debt/EBITDA improved to 1.9x at year-end FY2025 from 2.0x in FY2024, comfortably inside the 2.0x covenant ceiling; net bank debt fell to EUR 461.4m from EUR 496.0m
8.
Cash conversion is a genuine weakness. Free cash flow recovered to EUR 74.9m in FY2025 from negative EUR 73.3m in FY2023, but cash conversion of 32.1% in FY2025 lags Prysmian's own >50% target, and working capital remains elevated at 17.0% of turnover against a 12-15% target band
9.
Capital allocation. A EUR 1.35 per share dividend was proposed for FY2025 (60.5% of normalised profit, below the Bloomberg consensus EUR 1.50), and the AGM authorised a buyback of up to 10% of issued capital, gated on net debt staying below 2.0x EBITDA and funded from separation proceeds
10.
Governance structure is a live constraint. A STAK (Stichting Administratiekantoor) trust structure caps depositary-receipt exchangeability at 1.0% of issued capital and retains a Continuity Foundation call option of up to 50% (100% if the 1% limit is removed), materially insulating management from hostile takeover or activist-forced action
11.
Ownership context. Largest shareholder HAL Trust doubled its stake to 10.2% in early 2026 from 5.17% a year prior, an activist-adjacent pressure point pushing for simplification and capital-allocation focus, though HAL holds no board seats
12.
Recent portfolio moves. TKH divested the Dewetron business in October 2025 for a EUR 35.8m one-off net profit gain (38% of reported net profit), part of an approximately EUR 458m non-core divestment programme since 2019, with a further EUR 250m of non-core revenue targeted
13.
Investment style read. This is best characterised as a conglomerate break-up-plus-self-help margin recovery case with a materially damaged credibility base, not a clean quality compounder and not a pure cyclical trough turn 1. Compound bull case: the thesis requires the Electrification separation, the Eemshaven subsea ramp-up, and machine-vision demand recovery to inflect roughly simultaneously, a genuinely multi-variable setup
14.
Leadership context. Chief executive Alexander van der Lof has led the group since 2001 (41-year internal tenure, joined 1985), with his contract recently amended to continue past the normal 2026 retirement age through to the 2028 AGM
