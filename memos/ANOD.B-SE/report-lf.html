EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

CEO Background and Operator Quality Assessment

Johan Andersson joined Addnode Group in 2006, establishing a remarkable 18-year tenure with the firm, progressing through Group Management Team membership in 2007, CFO responsibilities from 2013 to 2017 while managing business development and acquisitions, and ascending to President and CEO in a highly structured, low-risk internal succession. The leadership handoff was announced in late 2016 to ensure a seamless operational handover, with Andersson officially assuming the CEO role on May 4, 2017, as outgoing CEO Staffan Hanstorp became Chairman maintaining strong strategic involvement and organizational memory, coinciding with Addnode achieving a 51% recurring revenue share and expanding its credit facility to SEK 750M, priming Andersson's early M&A pipeline. Andersson's career history matches Addnode's strategy of delivering more than 50 acquisitions since 2003, supporting valuation arbitrage and add-on software cross-selling that demonstrates strong capital-allocation competency, though management and board members collectively hold under 10% of total shares, presenting lower insider stakes than typical founder-led holdings of elite European serial compounders, partially offset by founder Hanstorp's major stake via Aretro Capital Group.

Johan Andersson lacks any prior external CEO or divisional-head operational roles due to his long tenure at Addnode Group, spending 11 years within the organization in financial, IR, and business development roles before his 2017 CEO appointment, demonstrating continuity over external cross-industry operational experience, though the lack of external operational leadership roles may mean Andersson is highly dependent on inherited structural processes and Staffan Hanstorp's continuing oversight. His prior track record demonstrates strong alignment with Addnode's acquisitions strategy rather than external operational turnarounds, with group net sales expanding from SEK 1,444m to SEK 2,195m during his CFO tenure from 2013 to 2016, co-managing the onboarding of key acquisitions like EssVision and Stamford while active software users grew from 400,000 to 600,000, though standalone external divisional operating track records are absent from the public record due to his internal promotion path.

Governance Structure and Insider Alignment Risks

The board maintains 71% independence and deep domain expertise with seven non-executive directors, though five out of seven board members are fully independent of the company and its major shareholders, with Staffan Hanstorp and Jonas Gejer classified as non-independent insider directors, while Hanstorp served as CEO from 2007 to 2017 presenting long-term governance-capture risks, and Jonas Gejer's Audit Committee presence introduces an insider voice into audit controls representing the most significant committee governance risk, with Hanstorp chairing the Remuneration Committee potentially reducing objective pay-setting. Founder-Chairman Staffan Hanstorp co-controls Aretro Capital holding a 16.42% voting stake, with Hanstorp and Gejer jointly holding 16.42% of votes above peer-median holding, though independent directors collectively hold under 1% of total share capital, below the peer median for Swedish compounders which might limit active owner oversight, while the heavy concentration of voting power in Aretro Capital Group AB leaves independent shareholders with limited actual governance influence.

Class A shares carry ten votes each whereas Class B shares carry only one vote each, with Chairman Staffan Hanstorp and Jonas Gejer jointly controlling 16.42% of total group voting rights via Aretro Capital Group holding only 4.20% of total outstanding share capital creating a substantial control-to-ownership wedge, with Addnode's 3.9x voting-to-ownership control ratio wider than typical Nordic software peers lacking high-vote share classes, while specific corporate debt agreements contain clauses allowing discontinuation if control changes after a public takeover bid. Chairman Staffan Hanstorp invoiced SEK 2.63m in 2025 for M&A and strategic consulting via his private firm, continuing this arrangement with an additional SEK 0.70m in Q1 2026, while Board member Jonas Gejer invoiced SEK 0.64m in 2025 for post-employment business development activities, representing materially higher related-party transactions than the zero reported by standard software peers, with Gejer serving on the Audit Committee while simultaneously invoicing the company for private consulting services.
