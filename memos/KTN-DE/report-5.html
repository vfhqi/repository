§4 Margin Profile + Quality

Judgement: Gross margin trajectory is genuine and structurally driven by S+S mix-shift; EBITDA margin is improving but remains 700–1,000bps behind Curtiss-Wright and 350–400bps behind Advantech; the gap is a valuation discount driver. #J

  • CQ-4 (margin trajectory):
  • FY2022 gross margin: ~35–36% (pre-S+S scale)
  • FY2023 gross margin: ~38.0%
  • FY2024 gross margin: 41.2% (+320bps)
  • FY2025 gross margin guide: ~43% (+180bps)

  • This is +650–700bps of gross margin expansion over 3 years — the most credible structural signal in the dataset. #I

  • EBITDA margin progression:

  • FY2023: ~10.3%
  • FY2024: 11.4%
  • FY2025 adjusted (ex one-off): ~12.0–12.5%

  • FY2026 guidance: adjusted EBITDA ~EUR 225m on EUR ~1.77bn revenue = ~12.7% #I

  • CQ-5 / peer gap — MISSION CRITICAL:

  • Curtiss-Wright FY2024 adjusted operating margin: 17.5% (Defence Electronics segment: 21.3%)
  • Advantech FY2024 operating margin: 15.1%
  • Mercury Systems FY2025 adjusted EBITDA margin: recovering toward ~20% target post-restructuring

  • Kontron FY2024 EBITDA margin: 11.4% — ❌ 600–1,000bps below comparable peers. #J

  • Margin gap structural causes: Kontron carries (1) a low-margin EMS service business being pruned, (2) a GreenTec division running at negative EBIT, (3) IT services wind-down tail, and (4) higher D&A from acquisition goodwill; the S+S EBITDA margin of ~46% is peer-competitive at segment level. #A

  • S+S segment margin mechanics: S+S revenue ~EUR 330–340m in FY2024; EBITDA ~EUR 86–90m implied (46% of EUR 191.8m total); segment EBITDA margin ~26%; this is 2x the group average and 3x the IT Services margin. #I
  • GreenTec negative EBIT: solar division revenue declined from EUR 193m to EUR 151m in FY2025; EBIT was approximately -EUR 8m; with VW call-off deferrals and solar market weakness, this division is a ~EUR 15–25m drag on group EBIT before restructuring savings flow. #A
  • Margin convergence thesis: if GreenTec restructuring delivers EUR 30m annualised savings from mid-2026, and S+S continues to grow to 30%+ of revenue, the group EBITDA margin could reach 15–17% by 2028; this is still below Curtiss-Wright's current level, underscoring the peer gap. #J
  • ❌ EBIT vs EBITDA gap: Kontron reports primarily on EBITDA; EBIT is not separately highlighted in press releases; at acquisition-heavy companies, D&A from goodwill amortisation is material; EBIT margins are likely 400–600bps below EBITDA margins, meaning EBIT margins in the 6–9% range vs Curtiss-Wright EBIT at ~17% — a substantial quality gap. #A
  • Sceptical margin read: the gross margin improvement of +650bps over 3 years is real, but it is partly structural (S+S mix) and partly cyclical (defence orders at premium pricing); a defence spending deceleration or S+S growth stall would pressure both gross margin and EBITDA concurrently. #J

§7 Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

  • CQ-1 (carrier telecom vs defence vs industrial vs automotive) — segment positioning:
  • Defence/Aerospace: EUR ~500–550m revenue (est.); growing ~20%+ organically; highest margin profile within hardware segments; ITAR-free is a durable competitive moat vs US peers. #A
  • Industrial IoT: EUR ~450–500m revenue (est.); stable; Advantech is the dominant peer and directly competes here; Kontron's edge computing and KontronOS proprietary OS differentiate on software depth. #A
  • Transportation/FRMCS: EUR ~250–300m revenue (est.); fastest-growing; FRMCS is a generational technology replacement cycle across European rail; Kontron holds several framework agreements with DB (Deutsche Bahn) and other national operators. #A
  • GreenTec: EUR ~150–170m revenue (est.); loss-making; restructuring in progress. #I

  • Automotive: EUR ~80–100m revenue (est.); ramp expected 2026; NAD 5G connectivity modules for OEMs. #A

  • CQ-3 (S+S as recurring revenue engine): S+S book-to-bill 1.47x in FY2025; organic growth +15.5%; this segment contains licensed IoT platforms (KontronOS, SMARC modules, Q7 modules), managed services, and software subscriptions; the recurring mix within S+S is not separately disclosed. #A

  • ⚡ RARE: ITAR-free defence positioning: Kontron is explicitly marketed as "Leading ITAR-free Technology Supplier" for defence; this addresses a structural gap in the European defence supply chain — the EUR 165m Dec-2024 classified order is a proof point; EU defence spending uplift (post-Ukraine rearmament) creates a multi-year demand tailwind unique to non-ITAR European suppliers. #J
  • COM business divestiture (strategic rationale): Computer-on-Modules was a commoditising hardware segment; divestiture retains a commercial cooperation for S+S distribution through the buyer's channels; this is a net quality upgrade — lower revenue, higher margin mix, and a distribution partnership. #A

  • KontronOS proprietary platform: CEO commentary emphasises KontronOS as a differentiation layer enabling software subscription revenue; comparable positioning to Advantech's WISE-PaaS but at earlier commercial maturity; cross-sell opportunity to existing hardware customer base. #A
  • VPX defence product line: high-performance VPX computing units for mobile/stationary surveillance are the product behind the EUR 165m order; VPX is a VITA standards-based rugged architecture used across NATO defence applications; Kontron competes with Mercury Systems and Curtiss-Wright here but with European supply chain advantage. #A
  • Sceptical strategic read: the transformation narrative (hardware to software-led solutions) is well-communicated but not yet quantitatively decisive — S+S at 20% of revenue and 46% of EBITDA is still a minority of the top line; the company needs S+S to reach 35–40% of revenue before the margin profile fundamentally changes. #J
