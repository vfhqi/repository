§1 — Executive Summary

The thesis requires gold price to hold/appreciate, Ernest Henry to ramp on schedule, and Mungari to commission cleanly. Each is plausible independently; the joint probability narrows.

  • Net signal: Multi-variable thesis — 3-4 concurrent deliveries required for 25%+ IRR; not a classic single-fulcrum setup where one driver overwhelmingly dominates [#J]

  • Driver list: Seven key drivers identified — gold price realisation, Ernest Henry ramp, Mungari mill expansion, AISC/cost discipline, balance sheet deleveraging + capital return, AUD/USD currency sensitivity, and exploration/reserve replenishment [#J]

  • Impact ranking: Gold price is the dominant driver by EPS sensitivity; Ernest Henry is the highest-optionality driver; Mungari is the key execution risk; balance sheet deleveraging is the catalyst for multiple re-rating [#A]

§2 — Key Driver #1: Gold Price Realisation (AUD/oz)

  • Net signal: Gold price is the overwhelming EPS lever — EVN's earnings are highly geared to AUD gold; this driver is large in magnitude but entirely macro-dependent and consensus-visible [#J]

  • Driver definition: AUD gold price (USD gold × AUD/USD inverse) directly determines EVN's revenue per ounce; with ~730,000-760,000 oz annual production guidance, each A$100/oz move is approximately A$73-76m revenue and A$50-60m EBITDA impact [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Revenue sensitivity flows almost entirely to EBITDA given largely fixed operating cost base; tax-effected EPS impact of A$100/oz gold ≈ A$35-42m net profit, or approximately 5-7% of consensus FY26E net profit at mid-point [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Gold producer P/NAV re-rating is gold-price-driven; higher sustained gold price increases NAV directly and compresses perceived execution risk, driving PE and EV/EBITDA multiples higher [#A]

  • Company guide: EVN does not guide on gold price (appropriately); FY26 production guidance ~730,000-760,000 oz at AISC A$1,400-1,500/oz implies budgeted gold price implicitly around A$4,500-4,800/oz for plan assumptions [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: At current spot AUD gold (~A$5,000+/oz as of early 2026), EVN is operating materially above budget gold, creating strong earnings upside vs consensus at mid-cycle pricing [#A]

  • SS consensus: Broad sell-side consensus uses ~A$4,500-4,800/oz AUD gold for FY26-27 base case; spot at A$5,000+ implies consensus estimates are conservative by approximately 5-10% on gold price alone [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: Estimates revised upward through 2024-25 as USD gold moved from ~US$1,900 to ~US$2,600-2,800/oz; consensus has not yet fully reflected sustained USD gold above US$2,500 in outer-year models [#A]

  • Recent performance: EVN FY25 H1 revenues materially ahead of prior period, driven by AUD gold averaging above A$4,700/oz; FCF generation strong and ahead of consensus [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: AUD gold has appreciated from ~A$2,800-3,000/oz (CY22) to A$5,000+ (CY26), a ~65% compound lift — the single largest driver of EVN's re-rating over the period [#A]

  • Trend L12M: Accelerating — USD gold price has moved to all-time highs driven by central bank buying, de-dollarisation thesis, and geopolitical uncertainty; AUD/USD has not strengthened commensurately, amplifying AUD gold benefit [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Structural tailwinds genuine — central bank gold accumulation (EM central banks buying ~1,000t/year), US fiscal deficit concerns, and de-dollarisation thesis provide structural floor; cyclical drivers (rate cut expectations) provide additional near-term support [#A]

  • Macro headwind: USD strength, risk-on rotation into equities, and a resolution of geopolitical tensions could compress USD gold; AUD strengthening would further dilute [#A]

  • Visibility: Low — gold price is the most macro-dependent, uncontrollable driver; EVN has no ability to influence the gold price and limited ability to hedge (current policy is unhedged or minimal hedge book) [#A]

  • Plausibility assessment: At current AUD gold levels, this driver is already delivering; the question is whether it is sustained at A$4,800-5,000/oz or mean-reverts

  • If sustained: FY26-27E EPS could be 15-25% above consensus base case models using A$4,500/oz assumptions

  • If mean-reversion to A$3,500-4,000/oz: EBITDA compression of A$370-500m; balance sheet deleveraging thesis breaks; capital return optionality evaporates

  • Bear case: Gold price mean-reversion is the single largest external risk to the entire EVN thesis; a sustained fall to US$2,000/oz and AUD strengthening to 0.72 would put AUD gold at ~A$2,800/oz — effectively a 45% revenue haircut per ounce [#J]

  • Miss magnitude: A$3,500/oz AUD gold scenario = EVN EBITDA approximately A$1.5bn vs ~A$2.8bn at A$5,000/oz — near-halving of cash generation [#A]

  • Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — current AUD gold levels are highly supportive; sustained delivery depends on macroeconomic and geopolitical conditions outside management control; mean-reversion risk is real but structurally diminished vs prior cycles by central bank demand floor

§3 — Key Driver #2: Ernest Henry Underground Expansion (Copper-Gold Ramp)

  • Net signal: Ernest Henry is EVN's highest-optionality driver — underground expansion unlocks copper-gold production growth and structural AISC reduction via by-product credits; execution on track as of H1 FY25 [#J]

  • Driver definition: Ernest Henry Mine in Queensland is a copper-gold operation where EVN completed acquisition of full ownership in 2022; underground expansion targets lifting copper production from ~40kt to ~50kt CuEq, with copper by-product credits fundamentally altering EVN's cost structure [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Higher copper production at EHM reduces AISC across the group (copper credit offsets gold production costs); FY27E copper production uplift of ~10kt CuEq at ~US$4/lb copper ≈ A$90-100m incremental revenue contribution [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: EHM copper exposure provides diversification to a tier-1 structural demand driver (energy transition); market has historically undervalued this asset given EVN's gold-miner label [#A]

  • Company guide: EVN has guided to EHM underground development proceeding on schedule; FY26 production ~40-43kt CuEq from EHM; ramp to ~50kt CuEq targeted FY27-28 [#A]

  • Management commentary: Management explicitly positioned EHM as the key differentiator vs pure-play gold peers; copper credits described as a structural AISC advantage [#A]

  • Track record: Mixed — EHM has performed broadly in line with acquisition case (FY23-25); the underground expansion is the incremental risk, not the base operation [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side broadly credits EHM copper upside in NAV models but diverges on timing of ramp and achievable copper recovery rates underground; bullish analysts (Macquarie, Citi cited in public summaries) see ~A$0.40-0.60/share NAV contribution from EHM expansion alone [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: Upgraded moderately as copper price has held above US$4/lb and EHM production has tracked to plan; no material consensus downgrades on EHM specifically [#A]

  • Recent performance: EHM delivered approximately 41kt CuEq in FY25 (at guidance midpoint); underground development progressing; ore reserve update expected H2 CY26 [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: EVN acquired 100% EHM in Dec 2022; base production tracking to acquisition case; copper price has been broadly supportive at US$3.80-4.50/lb [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No cohort peer context available (D-BATCH-25 gap — flagged); within Australian gold sector, EVN's copper by-product credit (AISC benefit ~A$150-200/oz equivalent at current copper prices) is a structural differentiator vs Newcrest (now Newmont), Northern Star [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Copper structural demand is genuine — energy transition (EVs, grid, renewables) drives medium-term copper deficit scenarios from multiple forecasters (Wood Mackenzie, BloombergNEF); copper price consensus ~US$4-4.50/lb through 2027 [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Chinese demand slowdown (property sector) could pressure copper near-term; LME copper has shown volatility around US$3.80-4.50/lb range [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium — EHM underground development is engineering-driven (largely controllable) but copper grade reconciliation underground carries geotechnical uncertainty [#A]

  • Plausibility assessment: Base EHM production is low-risk; the incremental ramp from 40kt to 50kt CuEq is the swing

  • Key assumption: underground ore body grades reconcile to resource model (±10% grade variance is normal; ±20% would materially impair the case)
  • If delivered: AISC group reduction of A$50-80/oz equivalent; meaningful EPS uplift vs base case

  • If missed: copper credit reduction; AISC stays elevated; EVN loses the structural AISC advantage vs peers

  • Bear case: Underground resource grade disappointment is the primary risk; EVN has disclosed limited stope-level data historically, making independent grade verification difficult [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Copper production stays at ~40kt CuEq (no ramp); copper price falls to US$3.50/lb; combined impact = A$80-100m EBITDA miss vs bull case; AISC benefit fails to materialise

  • Red flags: None visible in public data as of early 2026; EHM production tracking plan

  • Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — base case EHM production is high-confidence; incremental ramp to 50kt is the swing; copper price assumptions are broadly supported by consensus but not guaranteed; underground grade reconciliation risk is real

§4 — Key Driver #3: Mungari Mill Expansion Commissioning

  • Net signal: Mungari is the key execution risk — a A$320m mill expansion (throughput from 3.2Mtpa to 4.2Mtpa) must commission cleanly in H2 CY26 for FY27 production/cost guidance to be credible [#J]

  • Driver definition: Mungari Gold Operations is EVN's largest Australian gold asset by reserve; the mill expansion increases throughput by ~31%, enabling gold production uplift from ~230,000 oz/year to ~290,000-310,000 oz/year at lower unit costs [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Throughput increase at similar or lower unit cost (fixed cost leverage) reduces AISC per ounce; at 300,000 oz Mungari and AISC improvement of A$100-150/oz, EBITDA uplift ~A$30-45m annually [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Clean commissioning removes the execution overhang; market currently discounts some execution risk premium in EVN's multiple relative to pure-play peers with simpler asset bases [#J]

  • Company guide: Commissioning targeted H2 CY26 (calendar year); capex guidance A$320m total (largely expensed by FY25 year-end); FY27 production uplift assumed in management's 5-year guidance [#A]

  • Management commentary: Management emphasised Mungari as the "production growth engine" for FY27-28; commissioning timeline described as on track at H1 FY25 results [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Optimism bias likely — mineral processing plant commissioning at this scale (A$300m+) has an industry-wide track record of 3-6 month delays; EVN's own Cowal expansion (CY19-20) experienced commissioning delays [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side broadly models Mungari expansion delivering FY27 production uplift; a minority of analysts (flagged in public research summaries without specific broker naming available in [C] mode) apply a 1-2 quarter delay assumption [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Mungari on-time commissioning vs 6-month delay ≈ ~A$25-35m EBITDA difference in FY27 alone; full ramp difference over FY27-28 could be ~A$60-80m EBITDA cumulative [#A]

  • Recent performance: Capital drawdown at Mungari tracking budget; procurement complete; civil/structural works substantially done as of H1 FY25; commissioning risk window is mechanical/electrical (Q3-Q4 CY26) [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: Mungari base production has been broadly stable at ~225,000-240,000 oz/year since EVN consolidation (Ramelius Mining swap CY23); no operational disasters but throughput below nameplate capacity periodically [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No cohort peer context available; Australian gold sector mill commissioning track record (Northern Star's Thunderbox, Regis Resources' Tropicana expansion) suggests 3-6 month delays are the median outcome, not the exception [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Western Australian gold mining operations benefit from AUD gold pricing directly; no specific macro tailwind to the commissioning event itself [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Western Australian labour market remains tight (mining boom); skilled trades availability for commissioning activities is a genuine constraint [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium-low — mechanical commissioning is largely within EVN's operational control but depends on contractor performance and equipment availability [#A]

  • Plausibility assessment: Base case (commissioning H2 CY26, 6-month delay tolerance) is medium plausibility

  • Key assumption: no major equipment failure or design flaw surfaces during wet commissioning; contractor performance meets schedule
  • If delivered on time: execution overhang lifts; FY27 production guidance credible; AISC reduction visible in H1 FY27 results

  • If delayed by 6+ months: FY27 guidance requires downgrade; market re-prices execution risk; capital return timeline pushed out

  • Bear case: Commissioning delay of 6-12 months is the base bear scenario given industry base rates; a more severe scenario (design flaw requiring mill re-work) could cost A$50-100m additional capex and 12-18 month delay [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Mill commissioning slips to H1 CY27; FY27 Mungari production guidance cut by ~20,000-30,000 oz; AISC stays elevated at Mungari through FY27; capital return timeline delayed by ~12 months

  • Red flags: No specific red flags visible in public data; EVN management tone on Mungari has been consistently positive but commissioning milestone still ahead — investor verification not yet possible [#A]

  • Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — commissioning tracker is on schedule at last reporting date; industry base rates suggest delays are probable rather than exceptional; the question is magnitude of delay, not binary delivery

§5 — Key Driver #4: AISC Trajectory and Cost Discipline

  • Net signal: AISC trajectory is under pressure — group AISC has drifted higher over L18M (labour, energy, consumables inflation); Mungari expansion and EHM copper credits are the planned offsets; delivery uncertain [#J]

  • Driver definition: All-In Sustaining Cost per ounce determines EVN's margin per oz; group AISC is the blended cost across Cowal , Mungari , Ernest Henry , Red Lake (Canada), and Cosmos (WA, care and maintenance) [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: AISC directly determines operating margin; a A$100/oz AISC reduction on ~750,000 oz = ~A$75m EBITDA uplift before tax; or conversely, cost inflation of A$100/oz = A$75m EBITDA compression [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Gold producers re-rate on margin expansion as well as gold price; AISC reduction at mid-cycle gold price is the purest operational value creation signal [#J]

  • Company guide: FY26 group AISC guidance A$1,400-1,500/oz; management targets sub-A$1,400/oz group AISC by FY28 post Mungari commissioning and EHM ramp [#A]

  • Track record: Guidance often at high end — in FY24, actual AISC came in at the upper end of guidance range (approximately A$1,480/oz vs A$1,380-1,480/oz guided); Red Lake (Canada) has been a persistent cost drag [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Optimism bias detectable — management's sub-A$1,400/oz FY28 target assumes both Mungari commissioning and EHM ramp deliver; if either misses, the cost target likely misses [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side consensus broadly aligned with management FY26 AISC guidance range; more divergence on FY27-28 trajectory with Macquarie and Citi cited as more bullish on EHM copper credit contribution than Morgan Stanley [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: AISC estimates edged higher through H1 CY25 as labour inflation persisted; partial offset from copper credit improvement at EHM [#A]

  • Recent performance: FY25 H1 AISC approximately A$1,420-1,450/oz (estimated from public commentary); Red Lake continues to be the highest-cost asset (AISC A$2,000+/oz equivalent); Cosmos on care and maintenance removes immediate drag [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: Group AISC has moved from ~A$1,250/oz (FY22) to ~A$1,450/oz (FY25E) — an approximate 16% cost inflation over 3 years, faster than revenue growth at mid-cycle gold pricing [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No cohort peer context available; Australian gold sector AISC inflation of 15-20% over FY22-25 is broadly consistent with EVN's experience; Northern Star AISC ~A$1,650-1,700/oz; Newmont (Aus operations) ~A$1,400-1,600/oz — EVN broadly competitive but not standout [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Energy cost stabilisation (diesel prices lower than CY22 peaks) provides modest relief; AUD weakness increases AUD-denominated cost of imported equipment but gold revenue benefit outweighs [#A]

  • Macro headwind: WA/QLD labour market remains tight; mining services contractor pricing elevated; consumables (grinding media, reagents, explosives) remain elevated [#A]

  • Visibility: Medium — costs are largely within management's influence (contractor negotiations, mining method, fleet efficiency) but labour market conditions are exogenous [#A]

  • Plausibility assessment: Group AISC staying within A$1,400-1,500/oz in FY26 is high plausibility; the medium-term reduction to sub-A$1,400/oz is the swing

  • Key assumption: Red Lake performance improves (or is divested); Mungari delivers throughput; EHM copper credits sustained

  • Red Lake: The Canadian asset has been a persistent drag; at AISC ~A$2,000+/oz, it dilutes group metrics; management has not signalled divestiture but the market would likely re-rate EVN positively on a Red Lake exit

  • Bear case: Red Lake cost escalation and further industry-wide inflation could push group AISC above A$1,600/oz — the level at which FCF generation at mid-cycle gold prices becomes constrained [#J]

  • Miss scenario: WA labour costs +10% in FY26; Red Lake AISC stays above A$2,000/oz; Mungari commissioning delay means no throughput leverage; group AISC edges toward A$1,550-1,600/oz
  • Red flags: Red Lake has underdelivered on its standalone improvement programme for 2 consecutive years; EVN management's "improvement trajectory" commentary has not yet been validated by the numbers [#A]

❌ Red Lake drag — Red Lake AISC persistently above A$2,000/oz for 2 consecutive years despite management improvement programme; no peer benchmark of comparable Canadian asset available in [C] mode; structural drag on group metrics [Cross-ref: §5]

  • Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — FY26 AISC guidance range is achievable at current input cost trajectory; sub-A$1,400/oz medium-term target is execution-dependent on Mungari and EHM delivering simultaneously; Red Lake remains the structural wildcard

§6 — Key Driver #5: Balance Sheet Deleveraging and Capital Return

  • Net signal: Balance sheet deleveraging is the most visible and high-plausibility driver — FCF trajectory at current AUD gold prices structurally supports net debt reduction from peak ~A$1.2bn (FY24) to target ~A$400m (FY26E) [#J]

  • Driver definition: EVN's net debt peaked post-Mungari capex and EHM acquisition; rapid FCF generation at high gold prices enables deleveraging; reaching target leverage unlocks capital return (buybacks/dividends) and multiple expansion [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Debt reduction lowers interest expense (~A$60-70m annual saving on A$800m debt reduction at ~7-8% blended rate); buybacks directly enhance EPS per share [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Gold producers re-rate when leverage normalises; EV/EBITDA multiple expands as balance sheet risk premium unwinds; buyback announcement is a near-term catalyst [#J]

  • Company guide: Management targets net debt <A$400m by end FY26 (June 2026); FY25 FCF generation approximately A$700-800m at current gold prices (estimated from production guidance × margin); capex drawdown falling as Mungari nears completion [#A]

  • Management commentary: Capital allocation framework: debt reduction priority 1, sustaining capex priority 2, growth capex / returns priority 3; threshold for capital return = net debt <A$400m per public statements [#A]

  • Track record: Credible — EVN has demonstrated rapid deleveraging post-acquisition historically (post-Cowal, post-EHM); the mechanism is well understood [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side broadly models net debt ~A$350-450m by FY26E; consensus dividend yield stepping up from ~1.5% (FY25) toward 2.5-3.0% (FY27) on normalised balance sheet [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Buyback at current FCF: ~A$200-300m capital return in FY27 = approximately 2-3% EPS accretion annually; modest but real [#A]

  • Consensus changes L12M: Capital return expectations have stepped up materially as gold price uplift has compressed the deleveraging timeline [#A]

  • Recent performance: FY25 H1 FCF strong; net debt reduction on track; no covenant concerns at current leverage ratios (net debt/EBITDA ~0.4-0.5x at current gold prices) [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: Net debt was ~A$1.6bn at peak (post-EHM acquisition FY23); reduction to ~A$700-800m by FY25E demonstrates consistent FCF generation [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: No cohort peer data; Australian gold sector broadly de-levering at similar pace driven by high gold prices; EVN's leverage trajectory (peak ~1.5x net debt/EBITDA to target <0.5x in 3 years) is strong but not unique [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Gold price at current levels dramatically accelerates deleveraging; every A$200/oz upside in AUD gold = approximately A$150m additional annual FCF [#A]

  • Macro headwind: If gold price reverts, FCF generation compresses and capital return timeline extends; Mungari capex overshoot would delay net debt reduction [#A]

  • Visibility: High — this driver is the most visible in the near term; capex programme largely complete, FCF generation calculable from guidance × gold price × cost structure [#J]

  • Plausibility assessment: Net debt target <A$400m by FY26E is high plausibility at current gold prices

  • Key assumption: AUD gold ≥A$4,500/oz (already at A$5,000+); Mungari capex not materially overrun; no M&A
  • If delivered: capital return announcement likely H2 CY26; multiple expansion of 1-2x EV/EBITDA turns plausible as balance sheet risk premium unwinds

  • M&A risk: ❌ EVN has a history of capital allocation via acquisitions (Cowal, EHM, Mungari consolidation, Red Lake) — a return of the acquisition instinct would interrupt capital return and potentially reset leverage [Cross-ref: §1 sceptical]

  • Bear case: Acquisitive capital redeployment is the bear case for this driver; if management announces a material acquisition (A$500m+), the deleveraging narrative breaks and the capital return story disappears [#J]

  • Miss scenario: EVN announces acquisition of a Canadian or WA asset using FCF and new equity; net debt stays elevated; buyback/dividend uplift pushed to FY28+; multiple remains discount to peers

  • Red flags: EVN CEO Jake Klein has historically been acquisitive; management commentary on M&A in public forums has been notably non-committal on "we won't acquire" [#A]

  • Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — at current gold prices, FCF trajectory mechanically delivers net debt target by FY26; primary risk is management's acquisition instinct overriding capital return framework; not a macro risk, a governance/capital allocation risk

§7 — Key Driver #6: AUD/USD Currency Sensitivity

  • Net signal: Currency is a structural amplifier of the gold thesis, not a standalone driver — AUD/USD movement in either direction creates material EPS volatility; current weak-AUD environment is an underappreciated tailwind [#J]

  • Driver definition: EVN reports in AUD; gold is priced in USD; costs are predominantly AUD-denominated (WA/QLD operations) with partial USD costs at Red Lake; a weaker AUD amplifies USD gold price in AUD terms [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: A 1 US cent depreciation in AUD/USD (~1% move at 0.65 level) adds approximately A$35-45m to annual revenue on ~750,000 oz production; cost base partially offsets via USD-denominated consumables/energy [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: AUD gold price (the combined signal of USD gold and AUD/USD) is the true revenue driver; investors in AUD-reporting miners should monitor AUD gold, not USD gold alone [#J]

  • Company guide: EVN does not provide FX guidance explicitly; unhedged or minimal hedge position means full AUD/USD translation exposure [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Management guidance implicitly uses spot-ish FX; no structural sandbagging on FX assumptions discernible from public data [#A]

  • SS consensus: Sell-side models typically use spot AUD/USD as base case with sensitivity tables; AUD/USD 0.62-0.68 range covers most analyst scenario bands [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: AUD/USD move from 0.65 to 0.70 (AUD appreciation of ~8%) = approximately A$160-200m revenue reduction annually at ~750,000 oz, or ~A$0.07-0.10/share EPS impact [#A]

  • Recent performance: AUD/USD has traded in 0.62-0.67 range through most of CY25; AUD gold has benefited significantly vs USD gold terms [#A]

  • L3Y baseline: AUD/USD has depreciated from ~0.73 (CY22) to ~0.63-0.65 (CY25-26), a ~11% move that has contributed approximately A$300-400/oz to the AUD gold uplift over the period [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: AUD weakness is structurally linked to China growth concerns, commodity cycle risk-off, and USD strength; all three have been present over L2Y [#A]

  • Macro headwind: AUD re-rating risk if China stimulus succeeds (lifting bulk commodity prices and AUD), or if USD weakens materially on US fiscal concerns (paradoxically, USD weakness could lift AUD while also lifting USD gold — net effect uncertain) [#A]

  • Visibility: Very low — FX forecasting is notoriously unreliable; treating AUD/USD as a standalone positive driver over 24M is speculative [#A]
