§2 — Key Driver #1: VE EBITDA Margin Recovery

J-front verdict: Margin recovery from ~20% EBITDA margin toward 30%+ is the dominant share-price driver; plausibility is medium, conditioned on cost execution and revenue stability [#J]

  • Driver definition: Zegona acquired Vodafone España from Vodafone Group in July 2024 for €5bn EV; VE EBITDA margins had compressed to ~20-22% vs 30-35% for comparable Iberian telecoms operators; recovery to peer-level margins is the core thesis [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Every 100bps margin recovery on VE's ~€1.7bn revenue base = ~€17m incremental EBITDA; at 5-6× EV/EBITDA implied multiple, €17m EBITDA = ~€85-102m EV uplift; Zegona's market cap is ~£600-700m, so each 100bps is ~10-15% equity value accretion [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: EBITDA recovery lowers leverage ratio, improves FCF yield perception, compresses risk premium; market is paying for a path to 2.5-3.0× net debt/EBITDA normalisation [#A]

Company guidance:
- Company guide: Management has publicly targeted EBITDA margins approaching 30% in the medium term (3-5 years); specific FY2025E guidance has not been publicly quantified beyond "improving trajectory" [#A]
- Management commentary: CEO Eamonn O'Hare has emphasised a €300m+ gross cost savings programme, with quick-win OpEx reductions in H1-2025 and structural savings realised through 2026-2027 [#A]
- Track record: Zegona's prior telecom carve-out (Telecable España, acquired 2015, sold 2017 to Vodafone) delivered material margin improvement in a shorter asset; VE is a structurally harder and larger asset [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: Management guidance is characteristically aspirational; 30% margin target is achievable but historically Spanish integrated telecoms have ranged 28-34% EBITDA margin at mature operations; assuming 30% as base case is not unreasonable but not sandbagging [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side broadly models ~24-26% EBITDA margin for VE in FY2025E, rising to 27-29% by FY2027E; the range reflects timing uncertainty on cost programme delivery [#A]
- Consensus changes: As of early-2026 reporting, several brokers have modestly trimmed near-term margin estimates while maintaining medium-term targets; revenue softness is the culprit [#A]
- Bullish analysts: Brokers closer to the management narrative model faster cost traction and ~29% margins by 2026; this is the optimistic scenario [#A]
- Sceptical analysts: More cautious brokers note that VE's cost base includes structural network-sharing costs that are not fully controllable and that labour restructuring in Spain faces regulatory headwinds [#A]
- Estimated EPS impact: A 500bps margin miss vs consensus (24% vs 29% by 2027E) would reduce EBITDA by ~€85m annually; at current leverage, this flows almost entirely to equity erosion [#A]

Recent performance:
- Recent delivery: VE's H1-2025 results showed modest EBITDA improvement vs prior year but missed market expectations in at least one consensus estimate; revenue declines partially offset cost savings [#A]
- L3Y baseline: VE entered Zegona ownership at 20-22% EBITDA margin; prior Vodafone Group stewardship saw margin compression from ~32% in 2018-19 to trough; recovery has begun but from a deep base [#A]
- Trend: L6M trend is cautiously improving on costs but deteriorating on revenue; net margin trajectory is positive but below initial expectations [#A]
- Peer benchmark: No cohort peer data available; Telecomunicaciones Ibéricas (Telefónica España) operates at ~33-35% EBITDA margin; MásMóvil pre-merger operated at ~28%; VE at 22% is a clear laggard [#A]

Macro/end-market context:
- Macro tailwind: Spanish economic growth (GDP +2.5-3.0% in 2024-25) supports consumer telecom demand; employment levels near record highs reduce churn pressure from affordability [#A]
- Macro headwind: Energy cost volatility in network operations; Spanish telecom regulator historically consumer-friendly (pricing pressure) [#A]
- Competitive dynamics: MASORANGE (MásMóvil/Orange merger completed 2024) creates a large challenger with combined revenue scale; pricing discipline from a 4-player market (Telefónica, MASORANGE, VE, MásMóvil rump) is uncertain [#A]
- Visibility: Cost savings (OpEx controllable) are medium-visibility; revenue-dependent margin recovery is lower-visibility [#A]

Plausibility assessment:
- Key assumptions: Cost programme delivers €200m+ by 2026; revenue stabilises H2-2025; network-sharing costs are absorbed as guided [#A]
- Miss scenario: Revenue RGU losses continue through 2026, offsetting cost savings; margin recovery stalls at ~24-25% [#A]
- Magnitude if missed: A stall at 24% vs 29% consensus by 2027E = ~€85m EBITDA shortfall; equity value impact 30-40% downside at current leverage [#A]

Bear case: Cost programme is achievable but insufficient to offset revenue headwinds; VE structural cost base (network-sharing, legacy infrastructure) has a higher floor than management implies; Spanish labour law constrains headcount reductions; peer Spanish telecoms' margin recovery paths took 5-7 years not 3-5 [#J]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — conditioned on revenue stabilising H2-2025 and cost programme not facing structural impediments

§3 — Key Driver #2: Revenue Stabilisation and RGU Recovery

J-front verdict: Revenue stabilisation is the necessary precondition for the margin thesis; current RGU trajectory is the most concerning near-term data point in the investment case [#J]

  • Driver definition: VE has been losing mobile and fixed RGUs (Revenue Generating Units) due to price competition and legacy brand perception post-Vodafone Group exit; stabilisation = net-zero or positive RGU adds on a sustained basis [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Revenue decline at 1% per year on €1.7bn base = ~€17m p.a. revenue erosion; at 30% incremental margins = ~€5m EBITDA drag; revenue decline of 3-4% annually would add €30-50m EBITDA headwind [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: RGU stabilisation is a narrative catalyst; first quarter of positive net RGU adds would be a market re-rating trigger; continued losses would invite consensus downgrades [#A]

Company guidance:
- Company guide: Management has guided "revenue stabilisation in H2-2025" as a key milestone; specific RGU targets have not been published [#A]
- Management commentary: Management has attributed RGU losses partly to deliberate low-value subscriber pruning (ARPU management) and partly to competitive pressure; the "pruning" narrative provides cover but is difficult to independently verify [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: The H2-2025 stabilisation guide appears optimistic given observed trajectory; competitive pressure from MASORANGE has been more persistent than management implied at acquisition [#A]
- Track record: No prior comparable large-asset turnaround to benchmark management's RGU recovery credibility; Telecable was a smaller, simpler asset [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side models modest revenue recovery from 2026E onward; near-term (2025E) revenue forecasts have been revised down in multiple broker models [#A]
- Consensus changes: Downward revenue revisions have been the dominant trend over L12M; at least two brokers have moved from revenue growth to flat/slight decline for FY2025E [#A]
- Estimated revenue impact: If revenue stabilises at €1.65-1.70bn (flat vs current) vs management's implied €1.75-1.80bn, EBITDA is €15-30m lower than modelled [#A]

Recent performance:
- Recent delivery: VE's 2024 annual report (first under Zegona ownership) showed mobile contract net losses; fixed broadband showed better resilience; convergent (bundled) subscribers showed modest growth [#A]
- L3Y baseline: VE lost approximately 300,000-400,000 mobile contract subscribers over 2022-2024 under Vodafone Group ownership; pace of loss has modestly slowed but not stopped [#A]
- Trend: L6M trend is decelerating-negative on mobile, stable on fixed; convergence subscribers (the higher-value cohort) are growing but from a small base [#A]
- Early warning signals: Price competition from MASORANGE in the SME segment (VE's historical enterprise strength) is an emerging concern; enterprise revenue quality may deteriorate before consumer stabilises [#A]

Macro/end-market context:
- Structural tailwind: Spanish households increasingly demand converged mobile+broadband offers; VE has a reasonable fixed network footprint to serve this [#A]
- Competitive headwind: MASORANGE combines Orange's fixed network with MásMóvil's aggressive mobile pricing; the combined entity has scale to undercut VE on bundles [#A]
- Visibility: Low — RGU trajectory is highly competitive and difficult to forecast with precision from public data [#A]

Bear case: H2-2025 stabilisation guide missed; mobile contract losses continue through 2026; enterprise segment (VE's premium revenue base) deteriorates under MASORANGE pressure; revenue reaches €1.55-1.60bn by 2027E vs consensus €1.70-1.75bn; entire margin recovery thesis requires recalibration [#J]

❌ RGU trajectory — current mobile net losses are running below management's stabilisation timeline; this is the most active source of consensus downgrade risk

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — H2-2025 stabilisation guide is achievable but faces above-consensus competitive headwinds from MASORANGE

§4 — Key Driver #3: Network-Sharing and Capex Step-Down

J-front verdict: Network-sharing with MASORANGE offers a material capex reduction opportunity, but deal terms are partially opaque and timing risk is underappreciated by sell-side [#J]

  • Driver definition: VE has entered network-sharing arrangements (RAN-sharing, passive infrastructure sharing) with MASORANGE; the key value unlock is reduced standalone capex intensity from ~16-18% of revenue to ~12-14% [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: A 400bps capex intensity reduction on €1.7bn revenue = ~€68m annual capex saving; at 100% FCF conversion this flows directly to debt reduction; at 5× FCF multiple = ~€340m equity value [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: Capex step-down is the key FCF inflection point; VE under Vodafone Group had elevated capex for 5G rollout; network-sharing allows a "step back" from peak investment cycle [#A]

Company guidance:
- Company guide: Management has identified capex reduction as a key component of the investment case; implied capex target of €250-280m vs prior ~€350m+ under Vodafone Group [#A]
- Management commentary: O'Hare has referenced the MASORANGE network-sharing framework as a "structural tailwind" for capex; specific deal terms and timelines are commercially sensitive and not fully disclosed [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: The capex step-down is plausible in principle; the uncertainty is in deal execution and whether MASORANGE counterpart obligations are symmetric [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side models capex declining from ~€330-350m in the pre-acquisition period toward €250-270m by 2026-2027E; broadly consistent with management narrative [#A]
- Estimated FCF impact: A capex reduction of €80-100m on current EBITDA base adds ~€80-100m annual FCF; sufficient to accelerate debt repayment by ~1.5-2 years vs base case [#A]

Recent performance:
- Recent delivery: H1-2025 capex was reportedly within guided range; full-year data not yet available [#A]
- L3Y baseline: VE under Vodafone Group had peak capex intensity of ~18-20% of revenue during 5G rollout; normalisation was already underway pre-sale [#A]
- Trend: Capex trajectory is improving; the question is whether the step-down is structural (network-sharing driven) or cyclical (post-5G investment pause) [#A]

Macro/end-market context:
- Structural tailwind: European telecom sector-wide capex cycle peaked ~2023-24; Spanish market 5G coverage is broadly adequate; incremental 5G rollout costs declining [#A]
- Competitive dynamic: If MASORANGE needs to invest heavily in its own network post-merger integration, the sharing arrangement may be delayed or renegotiated [#A]
- Visibility: Medium — capex guidance is more controllable than revenue but dependent on network-sharing contract execution [#A]

Bear case: Network-sharing delays (due to MASORANGE integration complexity), regulatory review of sharing terms, or VE needing to defend coverage quality independently; capex stalls at €290-310m vs €250-270m target; FCF generation ~€40-60m below consensus [#J]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — structural capex reduction is credible but deal execution timing introduces a 12-18 month uncertainty window

§5 — Key Driver #4: Leverage Deleveraging Path

J-front verdict: Entry leverage of ~5.4× net debt/EBITDA is high by European telecom standards; deleveraging to ~3.5-4.0× is required for equity re-rating; FCF generation is the binding constraint [#J]

  • Driver definition: Zegona acquired VE with ~€4.0-4.5bn net debt (leveraged acquisition); EBITDA at acquisition ~€800m implies ~5.0-5.5× leverage; target is 3.5× within 3-4 years via FCF debt repayment [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Deleveraging reduces interest costs; at EURIBOR + ~300-400bps on €4bn debt, each €500m debt repayment saves ~€25-30m annual interest; at 100% equity flow-through = material EPS accretion [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: Market applies a distressed premium at >5× leverage; at <4× leverage, EV/EBITDA re-rates from ~6-7× toward 7-8×; plus interest cost reduction improves equity FCF yield [#A]

Company guidance:
- Company guide: Management has not published explicit leverage targets but has referenced "significant deleveraging" as a stated objective; investor materials imply 3.5-4.0× as the medium-term target [#A]
- Track record: Zegona's prior Telecable transaction was lightly levered; VE is a first test of Zegona's ability to manage high-leverage telecom turnarounds [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: Deleveraging pace depends critically on EBITDA trajectory and capex step-down simultaneously delivering; both are uncertain [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side models ZEG-GB reaching ~4.0-4.5× net debt/EBITDA by 2026E, declining toward ~3.5× by 2028E; timing is sensitive to EBITDA recovery pace [#A]
- Consensus changes: Modest upward revisions to leverage forecasts over L12M (i.e., deleveraging expected more slowly) as revenue has disappointed [#A]
- Estimated re-rating impact: A move from 5.5× to 4.0× leverage at constant EBITDA implies ~€600-800m EV change; equity impact at current capital structure is disproportionately large [#A]

Recent performance:
- Recent delivery: Limited data for full assessment; ZEG-GB has been owner of VE for <2 years; FCF generation is in early stages [#A]
- Debt-wall risk: Debt maturity profile has not been fully disclosed publicly; any near-term refinancing requirement at higher rates would add interest cost pressure [#A]

Macro/end-market context:
- Macro headwind: European interest rates remain elevated vs 2019-21 period; EURIBOR ~3.5-4.0% as of mid-2026 means interest cost is structurally higher than modelled at deal close [#A]
- Macro tailwind: If ECB rate cuts materialise at pace, floating-rate debt burden reduces; ZEG-GB has some exposure to rate cuts via variable-rate instruments [#A]
- Visibility: Medium — leverage trajectory is mathematically determined by EBITDA + capex; both variables have meaningful uncertainty [#A]

Bear case: EBITDA recovery is slower than modelled (24% margin vs 29% target); capex reduction is delayed; leverage remains above 5.0× into 2026-2027; refinancing risk emerges; equity value dilution from rights issue or covenant breach [#J]

⚡ RARE: Leverage entry point — ZEG-GB entered VE at ~5.0-5.5× net debt/EBITDA; among European mid-cap telecom turnarounds of comparable scale, this is top-quintile leverage at acquisition; only Altice France and certain Eastern European cable operators entered at comparable or higher leverage; upside from deleveraging is unusually large but so is the binary risk if EBITDA disappoints. [Cross-ref: §5]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — deleveraging path is arithmetically achievable but requires simultaneous EBITDA recovery and capex step-down; single-point failure risk is high

§6 — Key Driver #5: Spanish Market Competitive Dynamics

J-front verdict: MASORANGE's post-merger competitive behaviour is the largest exogenous variable in the thesis; if pricing aggression emerges, all other drivers deteriorate simultaneously [#J]

  • Driver definition: The Spanish telecom market is reconfiguring post-MásMóvil/Orange merger (completed 2024); the 4-player market (Telefónica, MASORANGE, VE, MásMóvil rump) will settle into a new competitive equilibrium that determines VE's long-term revenue and margin potential [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Benign competitive environment = VE stabilises revenue + manages ARPU; aggressive competitive environment = accelerated RGU losses, ARPU compression, margin recovery thesis invalidated [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: Market re-rating of Spanish telecoms broadly; if market structure is perceived as benign (like post-consolidation UK or Dutch markets), ZEG-GB commands a higher multiple [#A]

Company guidance:
- Management commentary: Management has framed MASORANGE merger as "a positive for market structure"; cited improved pricing discipline expectations; but management is clearly incentivised to be optimistic here [#A]
- Conservatism vs optimism: This is the most optimistic assumption in management's public narrative; European telecom consolidation has a mixed track record on pricing discipline emerging quickly post-merger [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Sell-side broadly agrees that Spanish market consolidation should be positive for pricing; the debate is on timing (1-2 years vs 3-5 years for rational pricing to emerge) [#A]
- Sceptical view: Post-merger integration distraction at MASORANGE could lead to short-term aggressive pricing to defend share while systems integrate; this is the near-term risk period [#A]

Recent performance:
- Recent delivery: Limited evidence of pricing discipline improvement in public data as of early-2026; promotional activity remains elevated in the Spanish market according to industry commentary [#A]
- Peer benchmark: Post-consolidation UK market (BT/EE integration) took 3-4 years for ARPU discipline to emerge; Dutch market (KPN/T-Mobile rationalisation) took 2-3 years; Spanish market should be assumed similar [#A]

Macro/end-market context:
- Structural tailwind: 4-player → effective 3-player consolidation in Spain is a positive structural shift; European telecom consolidation precedent is broadly positive for incumbents [#A]
- Competitive headwind: MASORANGE integration creates a period of management distraction and potential price wars to maintain subscriber share during system migration [#A]
- Regulatory risk: European Commission and Spanish regulators imposed remedies on MASORANGE merger; remedies (e.g., network access obligations, wholesale commitments) limit pricing power recovery [#A]

Bear case: MASORANGE aggressively defends mobile share during 2025-2026 integration period; VE loses additional 500,000+ mobile contract subscribers in 2 years; consensus revenue forecasts require another material downgrade; margin recovery pushed to 2028E+ [#J]

Plausibility verdict: low plausibility (benign scenario) — near-term pricing discipline is unlikely to emerge within 24M horizon; management's optimistic framing is the setup's most exposed assumption

§7 — Key Driver #6: Capital Returns and Dividend Initiation

J-front verdict: Dividend initiation is a remote near-term catalyst; ZEG-GB is in cash-conservation mode; capital returns are a 2027E+ story at earliest [#J]

  • Driver definition: Zegona has a history of returning capital (prior Telecable transaction generated significant shareholder returns); re-initiation of dividends or special distributions post-VE stabilisation is a potential re-rating catalyst [#A]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Dividend initiation signals FCF confidence and attracts income-oriented investors; shifts shareholder register; re-rates the stock toward yield-supported peers [#A]
  • Mechanism to SP: Market prices in dividend-paying telecom stocks at 1-2 multiple turns higher EV/EBITDA vs non-dividend payers; at VE EBITDA €800-1,000m, a 1× multiple re-rate = €800m-1bn EV uplift [#A]

Company guidance:
- Company guide: Management has stated an intention to initiate dividends "when leverage is appropriate"; no specific leverage target or timeline disclosed [#A]
- Track record: Zegona paid a special dividend from the Telecable sale proceeds; this is an asset-light capital markets vehicle with a history of shareholder-friendly capital allocation [#A]

Sell-side consensus:
- SS consensus: Most brokers do not model dividends before 2027E; some model a token dividend initiation in 2026E as optionality; consensus FCF yield for 2025E is ~1-3% (insufficient for meaningful yield support) [#A]
- Estimated impact: If ZEG-GB initiates a €50-80m annual dividend by 2027E, implied yield on current market cap ~7-10%; attractive to European telecom yield investors [#A]

Bear case: Leverage remains elevated beyond 2027; FCF generation is absorbed by debt service; dividend initiation pushed to 2028-2029E; this driver contributes zero to the 24M investment case [#J]

Plausibility verdict: low plausibility within 24M — capital returns are a 36-48M story; treat as optionality, not a driver, within the evaluation horizon

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The strongest bear case for ZEG-GB is a correlated multi-driver failure: MASORANGE aggression drives RGU losses, which caps margin recovery, which slows deleveraging, creating a trapped-equity structure [#J]

Driver dependency:
- Driver correlation: Drivers #1, #2, #3, and #4 are not independent — they share a common upstream variable (VE revenue trajectory); RGU deterioration impacts all four simultaneously [#A]
- Cascade risk: If Driver #2 (revenue) fails, Drivers #1 (margin), #3 (capex investment return), and #4 (deleveraging) all deteriorate in tandem; joint failure probability is far higher than compound-independent calculation implies [#A]
- Driver #5 independence: Competitive dynamics (Driver #5) is the one truly exogenous driver; it acts as a multiplier on all other drivers positively or negatively [#A]

Cumulative plausibility:
- Compound probability note: If each of Drivers #1-4 is modelled at 65% independent plausibility, joint delivery probability ≈ 65%^4 = ~18%; but given high correlation through the revenue channel, actual joint probability is higher (perhaps 35-45%) — still materially below any individual driver plausibility [#A]
- Single-driver risk: Driver #2 (RGU stabilisation) is the de-facto binary event; failure here cascades through the full driver stack [#A]

Setup archetype:
- Archetype match: "Distressed telecom carve-out + margin recovery + de-leveraging" is a well-established European pattern; prior analogues include Altice France (partial), Tele2 post-consolidation, and UK Virgin Media pre-Liberty Global [#A]
- Archetype divergence: ZEG-GB's setup is harder than prior analogues because: (a) entry leverage is higher; (b) market structure clarity is lower (MASORANGE integration ongoing); (c) management team has no prior comparable-scale turnaround [#A]

Reverse-engineered bear:
- Bear case construction: (1) MASORANGE integration takes 24M; during this period they pursue aggressive pricing; (2) VE loses 400,000+ more mobile subscribers by 2027E; (3) revenue declines to €1.55-1.60bn; (4) EBITDA margin recovery stalls at 23-24%; (5) leverage remains at ~4.8-5.2× into 2027; (6) refinancing risk emerges; (7) equity dilution or covenant breach [#J]
- Stacked downside: In this scenario, equity value is potentially 50-70% below current market price; the asymmetric leverage magnifies downside [#A]
- Base bear vs catastrophic bear: Base bear = margin recovery of 300-400bps vs 700-900bps consensus; equity downside 25-35%; catastrophic bear = revenue decline + no margin recovery + refinancing = 50-70% downside [#A]

Single largest case-level risk:
- Fulcrum risk: MASORANGE pricing behaviour in 2025-2026 is the single variable that, if negative, activates the cascading bear case; it is structurally outside management control [#A]
- Monitoring proxy: Spanish mobile market pricing data (ARPU industry statistics from CMT, the Spanish telecom regulator) is the forward indicator to watch quarterly [#A]

Bull case counterpoint:
- Bull scenario: MASORANGE integration absorbs management bandwidth through 2025; Spanish market settles into rational pricing by Q4-2025; VE RGUs stabilise by Q2-2026; cost programme delivers €250m by 2026; leverage reaches 4.0× by end-2026; re-rating to 7-7.5× EV/EBITDA implies 80-100% equity upside [#A]

⚡ RARE: Driver correlation structure — all six identified drivers reduce to a single upstream variable (VE revenue trajectory), which itself reduces to MASORANGE competitive behaviour; this is structurally a single-bet thesis wearing a multi-driver costume; the apparent diversification of drivers provides false comfort [Cross-ref: §3, §6]

Confidence in case-level thesis: medium plausibility — thesis is internally consistent and arithmetically compelling, but is more dependent on an exogenous competitive-structure variable than a standard operational recovery setup implies

AGGREGATE WEAK SIGNALS / DOWNSIDE (❌)

  • ❌ Leverage entry point: ~5.0-5.5× net debt/EBITDA at acquisition is high by European mid-cap telecom standards; Telefónica España operates at ~2.5-3.0×; premium leverage creates binary risk profile not visible in EV/EBITDA valuation metrics. [Cross-ref: §5]
  • ❌ Management track record gap: Zegona team has not previously managed a €5bn+ EV turnaround; Telecable (€640m acquisition) was a structurally simpler asset; the step-up in complexity is material and not adequately reflected in market confidence premia. [Cross-ref: §2]
  • ❌ Competitive dynamics timeline: Post-merger pricing discipline in comparable European consolidations took 2-4 years to emerge; ZEG-GB's 24M investment horizon is unlikely to capture full benefit; near-term overhang from MASORANGE integration aggression is a realistic base case. [Cross-ref: §6]
