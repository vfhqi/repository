EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Caltagirone S.p.A. operates as a highly diversified Italian industrial holding company , controlling a network of cash-generative building materials, real estate, construction, and publishing assets alongside a substantial portfolio of strategic financial stakes in major Italian banking and insurance institutions .

The group's dominant industrial subsidiary, Cementir Holding N.V., has established absolute global leadership in the premium white cement niche , operating in 18 countries and securing a protected duopolistic market position .

Family governance is anchored by founder Francesco Gaetano Caltagirone, who maintains over 70% ownership concentration across the holding structure, ensuring exceptional long-term strategic alignment and capital allocation discipline .

The cement business has delivered superior profitability metrics , achieving a 26.80% EBITDA margin in 2025 that significantly outperforms larger European peers through its premium white cement positioning and integrated value chain economics .

Financial strength is evidenced by Cementir's net cash position of €465.1 million and a negative net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of -1.1x , providing substantial strategic flexibility for organic decarbonization investments and selective bolt-on acquisitions . However, the group faces severe Italian regulatory scrutiny and complex holding-level related-party governance challenges that could paralyze strategic decision-making at its financial asset holdings , while consolidated financial metrics remain heavily dependent on volatile Turkish and Egyptian currency movements that could materially compress reported Euro-denominated growth and industrial cash flows . 1

Business Model and Organizational Structure

The parent-subsidiary organizational model combines cash-generative industrial operations with a strategically complex Italian financial portfolio , creating diversified income streams across transactional, contractual, and comprehensive income categories. Core operating subsidiaries span cement and building materials (Cementir Holding N.V.) , publishing (Caltagirone Editore) , large-scale infrastructure construction (Vianini Lavori) , and residential and commercial real estate leasing (Domus Italia Group) . The financial investment portfolio holds substantial strategic equity stakes including a 10.26% position in Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena ,

approximately 10% of Mediobanca , and a 6.26% stake in Assicurazioni Generali .

Geographic exposure is anchor-led by stable Northern European building materials operations generating €734.65 million in 2025, while maintaining exposure to macroeconomic volatility in Turkish emerging markets contributing €336.78 million .

Recent corporate governance reforms expanded the FGC board to six members and added high-profile independent directors Giovanni Tria and Giuseppe Vegas to enhance institutional oversight .

Defensive governance safeguards include Chairman Francesco Gaetano Caltagirone waiving voting rights at BMPS and Generali and requiring prior Independent Directors' Committee approval for future holding company votes at these financial institutions .

Financial Profile and Through-Cycle Capital Returns

Consolidated operating revenues reached €2.44 billion in 2025 , representing 13.90% growth driven primarily by Vianini Lavori's infrastructure backlog execution, offsetting structural print media declines and short-term cement FX translation headwinds .

Cementir achieved record EBITDA of €439.5 million with a 26.80% reported margin and 24.80% recurring EBITDA margin , demonstrating sustained profitability expansion through premium product mix and disciplined cost management .

Industrial capital returns remain exceptionally strong , with Cementir reporting Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) of 19.54% in 2025, expanding from 16.73% in 2024, alongside a resilient Return on Equity (ROE) of 10.58% . Forward free cash flow generation targets €330 million cumulative over 2026-2028, representing over €110 million annually and supporting progressive shareholder distributions .

Capital intensity remains highly disciplined at approximately 5.97% of sales in 2025, with the 2026-2028 Industrial Plan targeting a cumulative CapEx of €386 million representing an annual run-rate of €129 million

. Shareholder distributions demonstrate steady growth discipline , with Caltagirone S.p.A. proposing an 11.10% dividend increase to €0.30 per share and Cementir increasing its distribution to €0.30 per share totaling €46.7 million .

Industry Positioning and Competitive Moats

Value accrues disproportionately in clinker manufacturing , where Cementir captures a premium 32.00% EBITDA margin in white cement through its absolute global dominance of the 20 million ton specialty niche and 40% export trade exposure . The premium white cement segment operates as a protected duopolistic market alongside major Turkish peer Çimsa, representing just 0.50% of global cement volumes but yielding superior pricing power .

Upstream vertical integration through 8 proprietary aggregate quarries in Scandinavia and 26 land-based Danish sites creates high-barrier bottleneck protection and supports structural annual price increases

. Control of aggregates quarries protects materials producers from upstream supply squeezes, with extreme permit barriers making greenfield quarry licenses nearly impossible to obtain and neutralizing new entrant threats

. Pricing power remains highly resilient through monthly cost pass-through mechanisms that transmit energy and raw material inflation directly to customers, acting like a value-added tax collection and protecting pro-forma EBITDA targets

. Low-carbon innovation leadership is evidenced by FUTURECEM achieving 30.00% carbon reduction , D-Carb reducing emissions by 15.00% , and the 2030 onshore ACCSION project targeting 1.50 million tons of captured CO2 annually .

Operator Quality and Foundation Strength

Family governance secures exceptional long-term alignment of interest through over 70% ownership concentration consolidated via ultimate parent FGC S.p.A., ensuring strategic continuity across multi-decade investment horizons .

The board re-appointed Francesco Gaetano Caltagirone as Chairman and Azzurra Caltagirone as Vice Chairman in May 2026, ensuring leadership continuity and generational succession planning .

Strategic direction prioritizes value over volume , actively rationalizing peripheral assets such as disposing of non-strategic Turkish regional plants and unlocking €51.00 million in cash through the Kars Cimento sale to reinvest in higher-margin assets .

The fortress balance sheet is evidenced by Cementir achieving a major net cash position of €465.12 million targeting expansion to €800.00 million by 2028 , with negative net debt-to- EBITDA of -1.10x providing unmatched financial flexibility .

Industrial operations delivered record 19.50% Return on Capital Employed in 2025 , expanding significantly from 16.70% in 2024 and proving strong operational execution , alongside a highly resilient 10.60% Return on Equity .

Competitive moats include global white cement operating as a highly protected duopoly , exporting 40.00% of white cement volume securing high global pricing leverage , and holding the absolute number-one position in global white cement sales .

Strategic Financial Shareholding Dynamics and Paradigm Fit

Holding leadership targets undervalued positions in consolidating domestic financial champions , maintaining strategic stakes in Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena and Mediobanca expected to yield high recurring dividend distributions as the Italian banking sector undergoes rapid value-accreting consolidation .

The landmark consolidation wave includes Intesa Sanpaolo launching a massive €10.10 billion voluntary tender bid to acquire full control of BMPS, while BMPS achieved control of Mediobanca with an 86.35% stake .

The board adopted specific safeguards in December 2025 to protect the holding from regulatory scrutiny , including Chairman Francesco Gaetano Caltagirone waiving voting rights delegation for BMPS and Generali meetings and requiring prior approval from the Independent Directors' Committee for future votes .

Decarbonization regulations act as a mechanical force strengthening Caltagirone's competitive position , as rising EU ETS compliance costs prevent smaller independent players from funding necessary green kiln upgrades while massive public subsidies like the ACCSION grant mechanically lower the group's green transition expenses

. Structural evolution dynamics show that the transition to low-carbon cement faces structural supply declines of supplementary cementitious materials throughout Europe, with steel manufacturing process changes directly reducing domestic availability of blast furnace slag and coal plant shutdowns materially limiting regional fly ash supply . However, complex related-party voting structures and corporate investigations could trigger holding-level management gridlock , distracting family leadership from critical industrial decarbonization initiatives and jeopardizing long-term strategic execution .
