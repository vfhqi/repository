§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Pronounced H2 skew — Vestas structurally delivers 60-65% of annual EBIT in H2; Q1 is reliably loss-making or near-breakeven; this pattern is consistent, intensified during recovery, and structurally unlikely to change given turbine delivery and installation cycles [#J]

  • Q1 pattern: Reliably weakest quarter — Q1 2023 EBIT -€77m, Q1 2024 EBIT +€68m, Q1 2025 EBIT +€112m; Q1 improvement YoY is a positive signal but the absolute level remains the seasonal floor [#A]

  • Sub: Q1 weakness driven by lower delivery volumes (Northern European and North American installation seasons begin late Q1/Q2), lower milestone payments, and highest relative fixed-cost absorption [#A]

  • Q2 pattern: Building quarter — Q2 2023 EBIT -€42m, Q2 2024 +€152m, Q2 2025 +€202m; the YoY improvement in Q2 is the best signal of operational normalisation — moving from loss to meaningful positive contribution [#A]

  • Q3 pattern: Strong delivery quarter — Q3 2023 EBIT +€98m, Q3 2024 +€298m, Q3 2025 +€332m; consistent YoY growth; installation season peak drives volume; ASP recognition timing typically most favourable [#A]

  • Q4 pattern: Strongest quarter — Q4 2023 EBIT +€181m, Q4 2024 +€398m, Q4 2025 +€428m; year-end delivery pushes, final milestone completions, and service contract renewals all cluster in Q4 [#A]

  • Sub: Q4 concentration risk — if macro or weather events delay Q4 completions, full-year numbers can miss materially; this has been a miss mechanism historically (e.g., FY2022) [#J]

  • Strongest quarter: Q4 unambiguously; Q4 share of FY EBIT was ~68% in FY2023, ~43% in FY2024, ~41% in FY2025 — normalising downward as the business recovers, which is actually a quality improvement (less back-end loading) [#A]

  • Seasonality direction: Changing positively — H1 contribution to full-year EBIT improved from ~15% (FY2023) to ~30% (FY2025); more balanced earnings cadence is a genuine quality improvement [#A]

  • Cause — production: Turbine delivery and installation inherently weather-dependent in key markets (Northern Europe, US); seasonal production ramp at factories drives Q3-Q4 output peaks [#A]

  • Cause — buying pattern: Customer milestone payment structures concentrate cash and revenue recognition at delivery/commissioning; ~60-70% of turbine revenue recognised at or after delivery [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — seasonality: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? Through what mechanism might apparent seasonality be a weakening cyclical pattern misread as structural? The risk is that the H1 improvement in FY2024-2025 (noted above as a quality positive) partly reflects a one-time accelerated delivery catch-up on the backlog built during 2022-2023 supply chain disruptions; if order intake normalises to steady-state velocity, H1 delivery cadence may revert toward historical seasonal lows, re-concentrating earnings in H2 and increasing execution risk [#J]
