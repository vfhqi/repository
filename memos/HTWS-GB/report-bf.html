Industry Dynamics and Competitive Positioning

Smartphone penetration is projected to rise from approximately 50% in 2023 to over 80% by 2030, with mobile data consumption across Africa projected to grow fourfold between 2024 and 2030. Mobile network operators are increasingly outsourcing tower ownership, with independent tower companies owning approximately 52% of African towers in 2025, up from 39.9% in 2019. Helios Towers holds the number-one independent market position in seven of its nine operational markets across Africa and the Middle East, owning approximately 68% of all marketable towers in its footprint when excluding those retained by mobile network operators. The company faces materially less independent competition in its post-IPO expansion territories of Senegal, Madagascar, and Malawi, with zero other independent tower companies in Senegal except for Al Karama Towers which has only 450 sites, no independent tower competitors in Madagascar's 679 towers, and sole independent provider status in Malawi managing a dominant 61% share of all marketable sites.

Long-Term Growth and Disruption Resilience

Building new sites requires high capital outlays of $100,000 to $150,000 per macro tower, with new entrants unable to easily match the extensive regional footprint built over fifteen years, as duplicating a country's main tower grid is prohibitively expensive and practically impossible. Low Earth orbit constellations are structurally complementary to terrestrial macro tower networks, with satellite capacity remaining orders of magnitude lower due to massive antenna distances, while satellites cannot efficiently support urban network densification where most tower growth occurs. Mobile data traffic across Africa is projected to expand fourfold by 2030, with wireless networks carrying 75% of African data compared to only 15% in Europe, while the region will add 142 million unique mobile subscribers, driving massive network capacity requirements. The company represents a highly defensive, cash-generative asset backed by secure multi-year order books, trading at 7.4x 2028 EBITDAaL representing a 35% discount to developed-market peers, while the projected 10% EBITDA CAGR significantly outperforms European peer averages of 5%.
