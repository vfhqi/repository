§5 — MULTIPLE-TRUTHS-COEXIST SYNTHESIS

J-front verdict bullet: Whether the answer is composite depends on the ACH matrix disconfirming-evidence counts; the scaffold below shows the synthesis architecture for the close-call scenario; single-dominant-hypothesis scenario is noted as the skip condition

Composite answer architecture (applicable when H_A and H_B are within 1-2 disconfirming-evidence counts):

  • Elephant view: The underlying composite truth that contains both partial views — {ELEPHANT_STATEMENT} — e.g. "The company has genuine structural advantages in {SEGMENT_A} but is losing competitive position in {SEGMENT_B}; the KQ answer is yes-and-no, and the investment case depends on segment-mix trajectory"
  • Time-frame split: H_A may hold over a 12-18M window (near-term earnings visibility) while H_B holds over a 36-60M window (competitive erosion arriving later); the investor with an 18-24M holding period may correctly hold both views simultaneously
  • Segment split: The attribute may be durable in the core business but absent in adjacent segments the company is expanding into; growth initiatives that appear accretive in the bull case may be dilutive in a competitive normalisation scenario
  • Scenario split: H_A holds under the base-case macro (soft landing, stable pricing environment); H_B holds under the bear-case macro (demand destruction, pricing pressure, rising cost of capital); neither is the "truth" — the truth is a probability-weighted composite
  • What would shift the composite balance: {COMPOSITE_SHIFT_TRIGGER} — the single most important catalyst that would decisively tip the composite toward H_A or H_B; this should appear in §6 monitoring triggers

Skip condition (single-dominant hypothesis):
- If H_A or H_B dominates by ≥4 disconfirming-evidence items: §5 is brief — note that the evidence distribution is decisive, composite synthesis is not warranted, and proceed directly to §6 verdict

Sceptical bullet — is the composite synthesis itself a hedge:
- The most dangerous §5 outcome is a synthesis that sounds analytically sophisticated but is functionally equivalent to "we don't know" — the test is: does the composite answer provide decision-useful guidance? If the investor's position-sizing decision is unchanged whether H_A or H_B holds under the stated conditions, the composite is a hedge masquerading as a synthesis; it must be sharpened into a conditional verdict ("size at X if condition Y holds; reduce to Z if condition W holds")
