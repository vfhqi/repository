EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 5 findings

Givaudan SA is executing an unprecedented leadership transition from an unusually long internal operator track under Gilles Andrier to an external consumer-packaged-goods leader in Christian Stammkoetter, a shift designed to drive its ambitious 2030 strategy though carrying medium plausibility of success given potential dilution of the company's technical flavor and fragrance culture.

Andrier delivered exceptional long-term volume-led outperformance during his 21-year tenure, expanding revenues from CHF 2,778 million in 2005 to CHF 7,472 million in 2025 while achieving 6.8% like-for-like organic growth during 2021-2025 against a 4-5% target and maintaining a best-in-class 24.2% comparable EBITDA margin in 2025.

Stammkoetter established a strong execution track record leading Danone's Asia, Middle East & Africa division which delivered €4.44 billion in net sales with 5.6% like-for-like sales growth in FY2025, outperforming the company-wide average of 4.5% and supported by healthy volume/mix gains of 2.1%.

Sell-side sentiment on the CEO transition remains divided, with Rothschild & Co Redburn noting the external appointment surprised the market and will likely present additional uncertainty for investors, though Jefferies argues strategic risk is mitigated because Andrier is transitioning into the Board Chairman role with limited risk around the CEO succession.

1.
CEO Quality Verdict: Legacy Excellence Meets External Execution Risk Gilles Andrier's career trajectory is characterized by more than 30 years of continuous service within Givaudan, beginning as Fragrance Division Controller in 1993 and culminating in an exceptional 21-year tenure as Chief Executive Officer from 2005 through March 2026. His capital allocation discipline prioritized organic business reinvestment followed by progressive dividends and selective bolt-on M&A, spending CHF 4.7 billion to acquire 22 companies between 2015 and 2025 while generating cumulative free cash flow surpassing CHF 13.9 billion since public listing in 2000, with FCF margin averaging 12.5%
2.
Governance Robustness: Elite Independence Undermined by Chairman Conflict Givaudan establishes exemplary 50% gender diversity and 87.5% board independence with seven out of eight board members independent, a ratio that exceeds peer DSM-Firmenich's 73% independent board

However, outgoing CEO Gilles Andrier was elected Board Chairman at the 2026 Annual General Meeting, a transition that violates standard corporate governance separation of roles guidelines, though the board created a Lead Independent Director role filled by Sophie Gasperment to provide independent oversight and balance Andrier's legacy executive influence.

Executive compensation establishes strong pay-for-performance alignment through clear financial and ESG targets, with former CEO Andrier receiving CHF 5.97m total compensation in 2025, though Givaudan approved up to CHF 4.10m in prospective replacement awards for incoming CEO Christian Stammkoetter in 2026.

The short-term incentive is weighted 50% on sales growth and 50% on EBITDA margin paid in cash, reaching 111% of target payout for the CEO in 2025, while long-term awards feature a three-year vesting period weighted 20% on non-financial ESG metrics and 80% on financial outcomes focusing on relative sales growth and cumulative free cash flow.

Givaudan maintains a clean one-share-one-vote structure with zero related-party transactions, each registered share carrying exactly one vote with no statutory voting rights restrictions, no shareholder agreements with significant shareholders, and no outstanding loans or credit facilities from the company to Executive Committee members.

3.
Insider Transaction Signals: Thin Skin-in-the-Game Despite Policy Alignment Givaudan exhibits stable insider ownership with Executive Committee members required to hold shares equivalent to at least two times annual base salary, though outgoing CEO Gilles Andrier held 4,369 registered shares while CFO Stewart Harris held a minor position of only 205 registered shares at year-end 2025, with total Executive Committee holdings of 6,965 registered shares representing a decrease from 8,400 shares in the prior year

The company enforces strict blackout periods preventing insiders from dealing in shares before financial releases, starting 10 trading days before annual and half-year reporting periods and 5 trading days prior to quarterly sales releases.

Total board and executive shareholdings compare unfavorably to peer Symrise where holdings amounted to less than 1% in 2025, though Symrise executive Walter Ribeiro executed an open-market purchase of EUR 49,995.72 in August 2025.

The sequential decline in aggregate executive shareholdings raises alignment concerns and exposes investors to risk during the incoming leadership transition.

4.
Expert Network Alignment: Validating Creative Edge While Exposing Organizational Inertia Expert networks validate Givaudan's elite creative edge and sticky customer relationships, with a Nestlé Head of Corporate Innovation praising unmatched co-creation, service, and flexibility while a Campbell's Research Scientist confirms Givaudan's joint development and troubleshooting are real and highly valued

However, a former Kerry Group Strategy Director asserts all major flavor houses pitch identical co-creation branding, and the Groupe Rocher Procurement Head believes co-creation is over-marketed though Givaudan's client attentiveness is superior.

A Colgate-Palmolive R&D Director confirms Givaudan has a distinct creative edge in AI-assisted fragrance formulation with proprietary AI platforms CARTO and ATOM accelerating formulation cycles versus rival houses, while a former Givaudan Manager states innovation processes are identical across houses but Givaudan delivers faster and the Nestlé Corporate Innovation Head praises Givaudan's MiniVAS system for real-time aroma smelling.

In contrast, a former Givaudan Global Marketing Head complains R&D pipeline is too slow and conservative, noting high internal pride prevents effective market adaptation and integration of acquisitions.

The IKKARI CEO reveals Givaudan successfully stole market share from Firmenich post-DSM merger, though a former Key Account Manager warns large supplier pricing power has significantly decreased via CPG e-auctions and expects agile, medium-sized companies to win market share going forward.

A Colgate-Palmolive R&D Director expresses cautious concern over Christian Stammkoetter's lack of chemical experience though notes integration challenges from large competitor mergers are a far greater risk, while a Givaudan Technical Director fears the incoming CEO might alter Givaudan's highly-prized ethical culture.

5.
Single Largest Credibility Risk: Multi-Jurisdictional Cartel Investigation Threatens Premium Valuation Givaudan is fully cooperating with global authorities in an ongoing industry-wide antitrust investigation launched in March 2023, with Swiss, European, UK, and US authorities continuing to investigate price-fixing and market allocation, while the UK CMA continues its active probe into Givaudan, dsm-firmenich, and IFF, and putative civil class actions are proceeding through discovery in New Jersey federal court after dismissal motions failed alongside Canadian class actions alleging material violations of the Competition Act

A prolonged, multi-year global cartel investigation could result in massive financial fines, destroying Givaudan's highly-prized premium valuation.

This unresolved legal overhang represents the most severe threat to management credibility and shareholder value, dwarfing operational execution risks associated with the CEO transition and potentially triggering material balance sheet impairment if regulatory authorities conclude coordinated anti-competitive behavior across the flavor and fragrance sector.
