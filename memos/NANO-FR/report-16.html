§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Governance is thin-but-clean by French small-cap standards — no material red flags, no structural dual-class weaponisation, no related-party concerns identified; net signal mixed (adequate, not robust)

  • Voting rights structure:
  • Nanobiotix is incorporated as a French Société Anonyme with standard one-share-one-vote structure
  • French corporate law permits "loyalty shares" (actions à droit de vote double) under the Florange Law (2014) — shares held continuously for 2+ years automatically receive double voting rights unless the company opts out; Nanobiotix has not opted out of the Florange default (requires cross-check of statuts/articles)
  • If Florange double-vote applies, long-held shares (including Levy's founder shares) carry 2x voting rights, concentrating effective control without appearing as dual-class on the face of the cap table — this is a governance subtlety investors miss; it partially explains why minority protection at Nanobiotix is weaker than the nominal cap table suggests

  • IRT holds equity interests from the strategic transaction; whether IRT shares qualify for Florange double-vote depends on holding period and structure

  • Founder control: Levy's economic interest (~3–7%) combined with potential Florange double-vote gives him voting influence disproportionate to his economic stake; this is not "weaponised" control (unlike dual-class tech companies) but is a structural concentration that limits shareholder activism capacity

  • Anti-takeover provisions: No poison pill or shareholder rights plan in the French SA structure; French law provides other defences (board referral to shareholders on hostile approaches); standard for French listed small-cap; no unusual anti-takeover provisions identified
