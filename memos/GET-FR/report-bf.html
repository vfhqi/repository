Section summary

Substantial corridor scale: the Short Straits corridor represents the primary artery for trade and passenger transit between Great Britain and continental Europe, carrying over 9 million passenger (PAX) ferry travelers annually in addition to tunnel volumes

3.
Resilient shuttle performance: in contrast to the declining passenger ferry trend, Getlink's Eurotunnel passenger shuttles maintained a highly stable volume footprint, registering a minor negative 0.3% CAGR over the same 2003 to 2024 timeframe
4.
Strategic market value: despite long-term volume pressures, the corridor remains highly lucrative, with high- speed city-center rail and premium shuttle services commandingly capturing the highest-yield customer segments

Market Concentration and Short Straits Duopoly Dynamics

5.
Structural duopoly control: the cross-Channel market operates as a tight structural duopoly between the Eurotunnel fixed link and a consolidated maritime ferry sector
6.
High barrier concentration: the massive capital expenditure required to replicate undersea infrastructure protects Getlink's ~55% passenger vehicle market share, creating an effective barrier to new physical infrastructure entrants
