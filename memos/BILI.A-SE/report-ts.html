Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Dominant Nordic scale and circular service integration create high switching costs and robust barriers to entry

Core Business Activities and Segments

1.
Comprehensive automotive offering: Bilia acts as a full-service provider throughout the car's entire life cycle, integrating vehicle acquisition, financing, insurance, aftermarket parts, maintenance, and vehicle dismantling
2.
Stable service business: The Service segment encompasses workshop repairs, spare parts, tyre hotels, glass replacement, and dismantling, providing stable demand that is relatively unaffected by economic fluctuations
3.
Diverse brand portfolio: Bilia sells and services 18 leading car brands, including premium names like Volvo, BMW, Porsche, Lexus, and Mercedes-Benz, alongside new electric vehicle manufacturers like Polestar and XPENG
4.
Fragmented customer base: Customer concentration is low as services are sold directly to thousands of private retail buyers and corporate fleet accounts across northern Europe
5.
Circular revenue stream: Bilia's circular model includes five car dismantling facilities in Sweden and Norway, which recycle parts to supply workshops with low-cost, sustainable spare parts
6.
Underdeveloped fuel segment: The minor Fuel segment comprises retail fuel stations and automated car washes in Sweden, contributing SEK 778m or just 1.92% of group revenues in 2025

Competitive Moats and Industry Structure

7.
High switching costs: Bilia binds customers through integrated service subscriptions and tyre hotels, securing repeated workshop visits and making alternative service providers less attractive
8.
Significant scale barriers: Bilia's scale of 185 facilities across Sweden, Norway, and western Europe creates a massive purchasing and brand advantage over smaller, independent repair workshops
9.
Direct manufacturer franchises: Bilia is positioned favourably in the value chain through long-term authorized dealer and service relationships with global automotive manufacturers, preventing direct aftermarket bypass
10.
Consolidated industry structure: The Nordic automotive retail market is highly consolidated and dominated by large groups, while the independent workshop aftermarket remains highly fragmented and ripe for acquisition
11.
Circular economic moat: Bilia is the single largest vehicle recycler in its regions, providing a unique competitive advantage in used spare parts sourcing that independent peers cannot replicate
12.
Other side of the story: Original equipment manufacturers are actively testing direct-to-consumer agency sales, which threatens Bilia's traditional showroom margins and could weaken dealer franchise values over time

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by cyclical macroeconomic headwinds and potential brand access restrictions from manufacturers

Operational and OEM Transition Risks

1.
Agency model disruption: Original equipment manufacturers transitioning to direct agency models present a threat that would compress Bilia's showroom gross margins and restrict customer data access
2.
Currency translation friction: A persistently weak SEK or NOK increases vehicle import costs for manufacturers, forcing up retail car prices and hitting dealership sales volumes
3.
Residual value exposures: Rapid depreciation of electric vehicles returning from private leases could trigger material write-downs on vehicle inventory, hurting car segment margins
4.
Short seller activity: Research shows no published short-seller reports or unusually high short interest exists for Bilia, indicating stable institutional investor confidence

Macroeconomic and Early Warning Signals

5.
False friend indicator: Growing used car deliveries can act as a false friend by masking severe volume declines and collapsing margins in the more profitable new car segment
6.
Critical early-warning signal: Investors must closely monitor monthly Nordic passenger car registrations, as any extended decline represents the first warning of prolonged service and sales contraction
7.
Other side of the story: The risks are significantly overstated because Bilia's diversified brand portfolio and authorized status insulate its service workshops from OEM agency shifts, as manufacturers still rely on Bilia's local physical infrastructure
