B Investment case — Executive summary (BLUF, Bottom Line Up Front) 32 findings
Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.
Judgement summary
  • Overall verdict. C EQT AB combines genuinely large, verifiable change forces (leadership succession complete, the transformative Coller Capital acquisition, flagship fund activation ahead) with a currently negative earnings-momentum signal and a business that is not simple.
  • Key risk. Sell-side earnings momentum is graded D (data-pinned) on broad-based negative EPS/EBITDA/sales revisions and a Q1 2026 print that missed consensus on fee-paying AUM growth, gross inflows (-53.6% vs consensus) and signed exits (-39.4% vs consensus).
  • Key support. The change-forces case is unusually concrete for a Triaging-stage memo: a completed CEO and Chair succession, a $3.2bn scaled M&A step-up (Coller Capital, closing Q3 2026), a forward P/E at the 14th percentile of its own 10-year range, and 62% Overweight/Buy sell-side positioning against a SEK 375 mean target (~18% upside from SEK 318.30).
  • Recommendation. Progress to Early-Stage Assessment, medium conviction, the case earns its place in the funnel on change-forces strength, but the negative revisions trend and the 40+-strategy business complexity are load-bearing enough that ESA must resolve whether Q1 softness is timing noise or genuine deceleration before conviction can firm up.
B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Global alternative asset manager at mega-platform scale. EQT AB is a Stockholm-listed, Sweden-founded private-markets manager running EUR 313bn in pro forma assets under management across 40-plus strategies, spanning Private Capital, Real Assets, Real Estate and (post-close) Secondaries
2.
Fee-for-service economics, not principal risk-taking. Revenue is split between contractual management fees (70-75% of total revenue through the cycle, charged at a blended 1.40% on committed then invested capital) and carried interest (20% of fund profits above a 6-8% hurdle, of which EQT AB Group keeps 35% and investment professionals keep 65%)
3.
Locked-up, non-redeemable capital base. The flagship closed-end funds carry 10-to-12-year lock-ups with no unilateral LP redemption right; the report states no LP has defaulted through the Global Financial Crisis or any subsequent cycle, which is the structural source of EQT's fee-revenue predictability
4.
Financial character: high margin, capital-light, high return on equity. FY25 adjusted total revenue was EUR 2,732m (+16% year on year), adjusted EBITDA margin reached 60.1% (versus a c.50% average for listed European asset managers), and reported ROE was 40.96%
5.
Segment mix is diversifying via the pending Coller Capital acquisition. The USD 3.2bn all-share deal (7.0% dilution, closing expected mid-to-late Q3 2026) adds Secondaries and Solutions as a fourth business line, bringing EUR 28-30bn of initial fee-paying AUM and a target to double that AUM inside four years
6.
Ownership is highly concentrated. EQT partners together with Investor AB (a 14.0% stake) control approximately 75% of shares, leaving a free float of only 25-28%, a structural defence against activist campaigns, and no active activist presence has been identified in the source material
7.
Leadership just completed a generational handover. Per Franzen became CEO in May 2025 and Jean Eric Salata took the chairmanship in May 2026, succeeding founder Conni Jonsson after nearly two decades of founder-led leadership
8.
Investment-style read: a structural compounder with a cyclical carry overlay. The contractual fee base behaves like a quality compounder (predictable, non-cyclical, high margin); the carried-interest and realization component is genuinely cyclical and currently depressed, with 78% of flagship fund EQT IX's value still unrealized
9.
Geography and client base skew institutional but are diversifying toward retail. More than 1,400 active global clients, pension funds at 40% of pro forma commitments and sovereign wealth funds up from 13% (2019) to 22% (2025) of commitments; private wealth was 26% of capital raised in 2024-25 via a growing evergreen product suite
10.
Technology exposure inside the portfolio is a genuine idiosyncratic risk, not just a sector-level one. EQT's own Private Equity book carries 30% technology exposure against a 7% peer average, per Rothschild & Co Redburn, making the manager unusually geared to listed and private software valuation swings even though its own vertical software assets are still growing EBITDAC 20-30%
