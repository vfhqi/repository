Section 3 – What the company does 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Structural Tier-1 automotive technology exposure is offset by intense software-defined vehicle competition from technology giants and low-cost Chinese suppliers.

Segment Breakdown

1.
Risky autonomous: Segment ACM develops ADAS components like radar and camera sensors, and focuses on an uncertain pay-per-mile commercial truck partnership with Aurora
2.
Complex architecture: Segment ANS designs high-performance computers and electronic control units that manage the background electronic architecture of software-defined vehicles
3.
Stable safety: Segment SAM is the historical cash cow, manufacturing electronic brake systems, airbag controls, and advanced wheel speed sensors
4.
Vulnerable cockpit: Segment UX manufactures large displays and head-up displays for driver cockpit interaction, though it faces severe pricing pressure
5.
Lagging cockpit: Segment UX had been slated for divestment before the spin-off, but those plans were shelved during listing preparation

Revenue Model and Chain Position

6.
Volatile transactional: Core revenue is transactional and driven by vehicle production volumes, while ACM has pioneered a uniquely risky hardware-as-a-service model
7.
Lagging pricing: Tier-1 position exposes Aumovio to intense cost inflation from semiconductor suppliers and strong pricing pressure from consolidated automotive OEMs
8.
High concentration: Customer base is highly concentrated with the three largest OEMs accounting for €4.19bn, €1.75bn, and €1.64bn of FY25 group sales
9.
Favourable localization: Regional production proximity is maintained via manufacturing across dominant automotive markets, namely Europe, the United States, and China

Moat Analysis and Competitive Dynamics

10.
Eroding moats: Competitive advantages rely on proprietary mechatronic IP like the MK C2 brake system, but moats are constantly eroded by rapid technology shifts
11.
High switching: Switching costs exist via deep OEM platform integration, but these are diluted as OEMs shift software architectures to in-house development models
12.
Intensifying competition: Global competition is intensifying from tech giants like LG in displays, and low-cost Chinese suppliers in ADAS hardware
13.
Vulnerable structural: Long-term competitive moats are threatened as software-defined vehicle architectures shift profits away from Tier-1 hardware suppliers toward advanced silicon chipmakers

Industry Structure and Peer Context

14.
Muted volume: Worldwide light vehicle production growth is slowing to flat levels, with European and North American production projected to contract sequentially
15.
Consolidated landscape: Industry consolidated as seen with the recent takeovers of UK peers GKN Automotive and GKN Powder Metallurgy by Dauch Corporation
16.
Depressed demand: Europe is experiencing weak medium-to-heavy commercial vehicle assembly volumes, placing further cyclical strain on Aumovio's commercial segments
17.
Other side of the story: Decoupling of software and hardware means tech companies and semiconductor giants are capturing the high-value Tier-1 profit pool, permanently crushing Aumovio's pricing power

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens on a highly visible self-help restructuring pathway and deep innovation partnerships, despite persistent cyclical vehicle production headwinds.

Restructuring Self-Help Program

1.
Aggressive cost-cutting: The primary driver of the forward earnings trajectory is the Cost Down initiative, which targets reducing R&D to sales below 10.00% by 2027 to match European peers
2.
Strategic footprint: Rightsizing measures target reducing production sites from 55.00 to under 45.00, raising factory utilisation and standardising global manufacturing
3.
Severe execution: The cost-down program touches the gross margin and R&D lines, but risks failing if severe headcount reductions impair long-term R&D capabilities
4.
Streamlined staff: Headcount reductions are targeting up to 4,000.00 positions globally, representing a rapid workforce rightsizing to match lower vehicle volumes

Autonomous Trucking Option

5.
Risky Aurora: The hardware-as-a-service contract with Aurora provides highly non-linear option value, charging on a per-mile usage basis rather than upfront
6.
High plausibility: Commercial plausibility is medium-high, as Aurora expects driver-as-a-service launch in 2027, backed by major truck OEM integration programs
7.
Risky option: The trucking driver fee would fail to drive high margin if unit usage rates fall short of the 250,000 annual miles required per truck
8.
Significant volume: Hitting group revenue goals requires Aurora to put approximately 13,646.00 autonomous trucks on the road by 2030, which is highly ambitious

Software Defined Vehicles Opportunity

9.
Rapid platform: The transition toward software-defined vehicle architectures allows Aumovio to expand cockpit value-per-vehicle via cross-domain high-performance computers
10.
Innovative technology: Product momentum is backed by deep technological agreements, such as manufacturing Aurora's third-generation commercial hardware kit and alliances with Tesa
11.
Uncertain alliance: The cockpit and connectivity strategy would fail if automotive OEMs increasingly insource software development directly from semiconductor chipmakers
12.
Limited risk: Real software-defined vehicle platform awards are scheduled from 2026 onward, meaning limited immediate disruption risk from AI in early concept phases
13.
Strict compound: For the ultimate bull case to fire, Aumovio requires both successful cost restructuring and high vehicle production volumes, as these drivers act independently
14.
Other side of the story: Slashing R&D and capex to achieve short-term margins will ultimately starve Aumovio of the innovation budget required to win future platform RFQs
