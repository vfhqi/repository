Section 3 – Industry Structure + Concentration + Value Chain Economics 88 findings

Section summary
  1. The global online gambling sector benefits from structural expansion, though near-term profitability is heavily challenged by evolving tax frameworks.
  2. Unprecedented taxation increases in core mature markets force operators to deploy aggressive promotional cuts and operational mitigation strategies.
  3. Consolidation is rapidly accelerating toward scaled duopolies in premium jurisdictions, isolating sub-scale operators who lack local infrastructure.
  4. Supplier power remains highly concentrated upstream as official data vendors utilize exclusive rights to capture a sizeable portion of operator revenues.
  5. Flutter is vertically integrated across proprietary platforms to control core trading, reducing expensive dependencies on third-party software providers.
  6. Upstream sports data feeds remain highly concentrated under a vendor duopoly, which exerts substantial pricing leverage over direct B2C operators.
  7. Downstream distribution remains constrained by mobile operating systems, leaving operators vulnerable to arbitrary app store policy changes and commission taxes.
  8. Core online gambling margins accrue at the proprietary pricing layer via parlay product penetration, shielding Flutter from retail hold volatility.
