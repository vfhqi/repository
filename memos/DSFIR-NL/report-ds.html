Section 1 – The move(s): what the data shows 9 findings

Section summary
  1. DSM-Firmenich shares experienced pronounced volatility, dropping after the February earnings print before recovering sharply post-Q1 on the Swiss listing announcement.
1.
Price drop - FY25 results: The share price fell to EUR 63.24 on February 12, 2026 from EUR 66.82 on February 10, following a small EBITDA miss in continuing operations and deferred FY26 guidance
2.
Support levels - share buyback: The share price established a structural support floor around EUR 60.50 in late March 2026, supported by the execution of the newly launched EUR 500 million capital reduction buyback
3.
Share recovery - Q1 beat: Following the May 6, 2026 Q1 update, the stock surged 4.3% in absolute terms, outperforming the Eurostoxx Chemicals index which rose 3.4%
4.
Peer comparison - Givaudan premium: Over the six-month window, DSM-Firmenich traded at a persistent 33% EV/EBITDA discount to Swiss peer Givaudan, which historical averages place at 23%
5.
Valuation floor - multiple compression: By April 2026, the stock's NTM EV/EBITDA multiple had compressed to 10x from its 16x peak in 2024, signaling that transformational risks were fully priced in
6.
Market outperformance - listing debut: The shares successfully debuted on the SIX Swiss Exchange on May 21, 2026, triggering immediate passive buying as the stock was slated for SPI index inclusion
7.
Performance comparison - sector laggard: Givaudan's 29.20% absolute decline over 12 months underperformed the SPI Swiss Performance Index's 7.20% gain
8.
Other side of the story: The stock's recovery may be capped by the persistence of low vitamin pricing, which continues to depress the valuation of the discontinued ANH division until final deal closure

Investor decision - capital commitment: Long-only investors should buy DSM-Firmenich to capture the multi-stage multiple expansion driven by the Swiss index inclusion and the completion of the ANH carve-out
7.
Other side of the story: If management fails to achieve its self-help margin improvements or if vitamin prices collapse further, the stock's re-rating momentum will break
