§2 DRIVER 1 — Margin Recovery to 8% EBITA Target (Instalco 2.0)

J-front verdict [#J]: Early evidence is genuine — Q4 2025 adjusted EBITA margin 7.1%, Q1 2026 5.8% — but the 8% target requires sustained execution across 150+ subsidiaries in an uneven market; structural proof is not yet in hand.

  • Starting point [#I]: FY2024 adjusted EBITA margin 6.4%; FY2025 reported EBITA margin 5.9%; adjusted 6.4% — 140-160bps below the 8% long-term ambition.
  • Q4 2025 adjusted EBITA margin: 7.1% (note: [AS] Q4 2025 adj. margin = 7.1%, [C] cited 7.2% — minor source discrepancy; [AS] cites INTRM 12 Feb as source).
  • Q4 2025 EBITA growth: +39% YoY — most significant recovery signal to date.

  • Q1 2026: 5.8% EBITA margin vs 3.7% Q1 2025 — driven by "operational discipline" per management. [#I]

  • Programme mechanics [#A]: Instalco 2.0 (launched autumn 2025) targets margin via three levers.

  • Lever 1: Stricter project selection — reject low-margin contracts, accept volume loss.
  • Lever 2: Accountability cascade — COO roles inserted under each country CEO. Named: Patrik Persson as COO for Sweden (announced 26 Aug 2025). [#I]

  • Lever 3: Earlier deviation detection — group-level reporting flags subsidiary underperformance at 4-week intervals rather than quarterly.

  • [AS] Structural context: Sjöstrand committed to remain CEO "for as long as it takes to achieve the stated 8% EBITA target" (FY2025 Annual Report / ARS 12 Feb). Restructuring includes phasing out the "Extended management team" by 1 Jan 2026, forcing a streamlined reporting structure directly to Group Management. [#I]

  • [AS·DNB Carnegie] SS consensus gap: DNB Carnegie models 2026e EBITA margin 7.0%, 2027e 7.6% — leaving a 40-240bps gap to the 8% target. Sell-side is explicitly not modelling a full recovery, pricing in structural underperformance. [#A]

  • DNB Carnegie (29 Apr): Q1 2026 confirms "operational traction, fewer major negative project deviations."

  • Path to 8% — three simultaneous conditions [#A]:

  • Condition A: Service/maintenance mix rising (36% Q1 2026, growing 6% YoY; higher margins than project work).
  • Condition B: Sweden segment exits project-mix drag (residential new-build weakness was primary compressor).

  • Condition C: No new large write-downs (Northvolt precedent live; remaining exposure ~SEK 60m per DNB Carnegie).

  • Peer comparison [#A]: SPIE 7.6% EBITA FY2025 (targets 8% by 2028); ACG 7.9% adjusted FY2025. Instalco 5.8% Q1 2026 — below both but outperforming Bravida (4.6% Q1 2026). [#A]

  • [AS·DNB Carnegie] Bear case quantified: "Stagnant organic growth of 0% in 2026 could limit EBITA margins to 6.5%, driving a valuation de-rating to 9x EV/EBITA." [#A]

  • [AS·expert] Cultural friction risk: CEO of ARE Sverige AB (Expert Call, 17 Feb 2025): "Instalco's core subsidiary managers are fiercely independent entrepreneurs accustomed to high autonomy" — Instalco 2.0 risks "permanently stripping autonomy from local entrepreneurs who built the business." DNB Carnegie (8 Apr): explicitly warns that "if management enforces overly strict initiatives, subsidiary managing directors could easily lose their commitment." [#A] ❌

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Seven consecutive quarters of EBITA miss built the credibility deficit; one good quarter (Q4 2025) does not repair it. The 8% target was set pre-downcycle; it may require market conditions that do not return for 2-3 years.

Plausibility verdict: Medium — Q4 2025 + Q1 2026 trajectory is real; SS consensus gap signals informed scepticism; full 8% by 2027e is a stretch unless Sweden recovers AND no further write-downs AND cultural friction is managed.

§3 DRIVER 2 — Sweden Organic Recovery

  • Scale of the problem [#I]: Swedish segment adjusted EBITA missed consensus by 10% in Q3 2025, -110bps margin compression YoY.
  • Group organic growth FY2024: -6.5% — driven overwhelmingly by Swedish new-build collapse.

  • Q4 2025 organic growth: +4.9% — first positive organic quarter since the downcycle.

  • Housing market context [#A]: Swedish housing starts forecast ~35,100 in 2026 vs ~28,300 in 2025 — approximately 24% recovery YoY. Key policy driver: Swedish mortgage rules eased from April 2026 (minimum deposit 15%→10%). Riksbank policy rate at 2.0%; forecast 1.75% by end-2026. [#A]

  • [AS] Order backlog signal [#I]: Instalco order backlog grew 15% YoY to SEK 10.38bn in Q1 2026 — most reliable forward indicator. Organic backlog growth 14.2%. Order backlog/FTE ratio highest level since Q4 2023 (DNB Carnegie, 29 Apr).

  • Backlog growth across all operating countries; strongest in Norway (>40% organically). [#I]

  • Service sales in Q1 2026: growing 6% YoY, now 36% of group revenues. [#I]

  • [AS·Deutsche Bank] Market confirmation: Ben Wild (Deutsche Bank, 17 Apr): "fundamentals across Nordic construction markets are gradually improving, though overall consumer confidence remains stubbornly low." [#A]

  • [AS·expert] Skanska procurement view: Nordic Procurement Manager at Skanska (Expert Call, 19 Sep 2024): "restoration market is currently at a cyclical high, actively offsetting the low volume in new-build construction; infrastructure investments continue steadily." [#A]

  • [AS·Jefferies] Pricing discipline: Jefferies Research (27 Apr): "temporary volume softness in parts of the Nordic region is effectively offset by stable pricing and disciplined competition sector-wide." [#A]

  • [AS·peer] Bravida read-across: Instalco backlog +15% YoY significantly outpaced Bravida's 7% order backlog increase in Q1 2026. Instalco ahead of Bravida in this metric; historically Bravida's heavier service mix means it turns earlier in the cycle. [#A]

  • Mix shift as partial insulation [#A]: Sweden's residential new-build is the volatile element; service/maintenance (~36% mix) is sticky and growing. Infrastructure, data centres, and energy projects are growing order sources. [#A]

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Sweden has disappointed forecasters consistently since 2022. A 24% housing start recovery in 2026 is plausible but rate-sensitive — any delay in Riksbank cuts or policy reversal on mortgage rules pushes recovery into 2027+. Norwegian-led backlog recovery "heavily relies on long-duration public contracts, which carry fixed-price risks that could devastate future profitability if regional wage inflation unexpectedly accelerates" (per [AS] sceptical synthesis). ❌

Plausibility verdict: Medium — directional recovery confirmed by order backlog and peer read-across; magnitude and timing depend on Swedish housing market following through on 2026 forecast.

§4 DRIVER 3 — Deleveraging (2.6x to <2.5x)

J-front verdict [#J]: The leverage overhang is the single most important structural constraint — it freezes M&A, sustains the peer discount, and forces capital discipline that limits strategic optionality; reduction to <2.5x is achievable by YE2026 but requires sustained cash generation without accidents.

  • Current position [#I]: Net debt/EBITDA 2.6x at Q1 2026 (down from 2.8x YE2025, 3.3x peak Q3 2025).
  • Cash conversion: 108% FY2025 — exceptional; well above the 100% target. Q1 2026 cash conversion: 100% (vs Bravida 77% same period — Instalco outperforming on this metric). [#I]

  • Dividend cut to SEK 0.50/share — capital discipline signalled. [#I]

  • [AS·DNB Carnegie] Forecast trajectory: DNB Carnegie (29 Apr): net debt/EBITDA forecast to decline rapidly to 2.09x by Q4 2026 if margin trajectory holds and no major working capital shock. Broker explicitly notes "underlying fiscal health is substantially better than current market perception suggests." [#A]

  • [AS] Earn-out liability caveat: DNB Carnegie (22 Aug 2024): management's preferred leverage calculation "conspicuously excludes SEK 193m in historical earn-out liabilities, presenting an artificially sanitised view of true financial obligations." [#A] ❌

  • Deleveraging mechanics [#A]: Three pathways to <2.5x.

  • Pathway 1: EBITDA denominator grows (margin recovery + organic volume).
  • Pathway 2: Net debt numerator falls (strong cash conversion + M&A pause + minimal capex).

  • Pathway 3: Equity issuance (used selectively for Defensio/Fabri — dilutive but debt-neutral).

  • [AS] Interest rate tailwind: Easing Riksbank rates on floating-rate debt "provide mechanical tailwinds accelerating the organic deleveraging timeline" (DNB Carnegie, 28 Mar 2025). [#A]

  • Market re-rating trigger [#A]: Peers trade at EV/EBITA 12-15x; Instalco at 30-35% discount. If leverage falls below 2.5x, the discount rationale weakens materially — re-rating back toward 14.0x historical average could deliver substantial valuation uplift. [#A]

  • [AS·peer] Leverage comparison:

  • Instalco 2.6x — still elevated vs peers.
  • Bravida 1.1x — lowest in peer group, far ahead.
  • SPIE 1.3x — European benchmark.

  • ACG: elevated (above 5.0x — Contify data, 4 Jul 2025). [#A]

  • [AS·DNB Carnegie] Covenant comfort: Northvolt write-down excluded from bank covenant calculations, "providing comfortable headroom against technical default." [#A]

  • Risk items that could arrest deleveraging [#A]:

  • Remaining Northvolt exposure: ~SEK 60m (DNB Carnegie, 20 Dec 2024). [#I]
  • Serneke provisions: SEK 58m in standard going-concern bad debt provisions throughout 2024. [#I]
  • Fabri consolidation (Q1-Q2 2026) adds debt to balance sheet — scale unclear. [#A]

  • Project write-downs (Northvolt-style): would push net debt back up and/or compress EBITDA. [#A]

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: 2.6x→2.5x seems close but three prior quarters showed leverage could spike on working capital timing; 108% cash conversion in FY2025 may be a peak year (exceptional payment timing) rather than a structural new normal. Earn-out liabilities (SEK 193m) understated in management's preferred metric. ❌

Plausibility verdict: High — the maths work if margin holds; cash conversion track record strong; main risk is unexpected one-off hit; DNB Carnegie 2.09x by Q4 2026 is achievable target.

§9 DRIVER SYNTHESIS + SCEPTICAL SYNTHESIS

9.1 Driver Interaction Map

  • Drivers 1, 2, 3 are sequentially coupled and mutually reinforcing:
  • Sweden organic recovery (#2) drives EBITA volume → margin recovery (#1) easier at higher revenue base → EBITDA denominator grows → deleveraging (#3) accelerates.

  • If any one stalls, all others are impaired: Sweden stagnation = margin target pushed right = leverage stays elevated = M&A frozen = re-rating postponed.

  • Drivers 4 and 5 are enabling conditions, not independent drivers:

  • Nordic market recovery (#4) is the external tailwind that makes #1 and #2 achievable.

  • Founder CEO (#5) is the execution credibility mechanism — he does not create the tailwind but determines whether Instalco captures it better than peers.

  • Drivers 6 and 7 are long-duration options, not 24-month contributors.

9.2 The Fulcrum Question

  • Core question [#J]: Is the 8% EBITA margin achievable by 2027e, or is 7.0-7.5% the structural ceiling under current conditions?
  • If 8%: stock is materially undervalued — consensus is wrong; 15% EPS upside per DNB Carnegie.

  • If 7.0-7.5%: stock is roughly fairly priced — discount to peers is justified.

  • Watson's first-pass view [#J]: 8% is achievable but unlikely before 2027e; 7.0-7.3% is the realistic 2026e landing zone. Stock is priced around fair value (not screaming cheap) with asymmetric upside if Sweden beats or Sjöstrand outperforms.
