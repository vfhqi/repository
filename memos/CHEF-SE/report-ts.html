Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Cheffelo operates a highly optimized, demand-driven meal kit duopoly model in Scandinavia, capturing strong profitability through a capital-light fulfillment chain, extreme product personalization, and localized supplier networks

Core Brands and Value Proposition

1.
Market-leading local brands: Cheffelo operates under three primary consumer-facing brands including Linas Matkasse in Sweden, Godtlevert and Adams Matkasse in Norway, and RetNemt in Denmark
2.
Regional segment distribution: Norway represents Cheffelo's largest country segment at 52.00% of Q1 2026 revenue, followed by Sweden at 37.00% and Denmark at 11.00%
3.
Tactical brand consolidation: The Adams Matkasse brand previously targeted premium cooking experiences in Norway but was consolidated under Godtlevert in Q1 2026 to optimize marketing spend
4.
Time-saving consumer proposition: The core service solves the daily meal planning and grocery shopping problem for modern Scandinavian families by delivering nutritionally calculated recipes
5.
Price-inelastic customer behavior: Jefferies Research notes that consumer demand remains inelastic after meal kit operators increase prices, indicating strong customer willingness to pay for convenience

Operational Sourcing and Logistics

6.
Zero inventory procurement: The business operates an on-demand ingredient purchasing model, which holds near-zero perishables inventory and minimizes operational food waste
7.
Locally tailored menu planning: Local chefs and dietitians plan weekly menus that leverage Scandinavian sourcing and match localized taste preferences
8.
Automated pick-to-light production: Cheffelo owns three specialized fulfillment centers across Sweden, Norway, and Denmark that utilize pick-to-light packing technologies to custom produce every box
9.
Asset-light delivery network: Outbound distribution is fully outsourced to third-party partners like Gordon Delivery, ensuring logistics cost efficiency and delivery density benefits
10.
Zero customer concentration: The customer base consists entirely of tens of thousands of individual households, completely mitigating customer concentration risk

Competitive Moat and Industry Structure

11.
Unrivaled menu choice: The group offers up to 285.00 unique recipes weekly across its brands, representing the largest selection in the Nordic meal kit market
12.
Advanced tech-enabled personalization: Cheffelo utilizes in-house software and AI recommendation engines to pre-select recipes based on customer historical ratings and purchase behavior
13.
Consolidated market duopoly: The Nordic flexible meal kit space has consolidated into a duopoly where HelloFresh holds an estimated 54.00% share and Cheffelo holds a 21.00% share
14.
Competitor capital flight: Numerous smaller competitors have filed for bankruptcy or exited the market, including Simple Feast, Marley Spoon, Kokkens, and City Gross
15.
Other side of the story: The meal kit business is structurally exposed to high customer churn as consumer cohorts can be difficult to retain without continuous promotional discounting

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is moderate but highly dependent on competitor behavior, consumer spending patterns, raw material inflation, and the successful execution of the new single-brand strategy in Norway

Competitive and Macro Risks

1.
Competitive pricing threats: A significant reduction in capital costs could allow competitors to resume aggressive, discount-led customer acquisition tactics, eroding Cheffelo's marketing return on investment
2.
Consumer demand elasticity: Weakening consumer sentiment in Sweden and Norway represents the most material risk to subscription volumes, though order volumes currently remain highly resilient
3.
Brand migration friction: Consolidating Adams Matkasse under Godtlevert carries the risk of losing premium customers who do not wish to transition to the main brand

Sourcing and Operational Vulnerabilities

4.
Raw material inflation: Input food price increases typically lag Cheffelo's price adjustments by 1-2 quarters, which can compress gross margins in high-inflation periods
5.
Related-party transaction exposure: Cheffelo conducted advertising space transactions worth MSEK 1.30 in Q1 2026 with Smood AS, an agency associated with Chairman Petter von Hedenberg
6.
High early cohort churn: Aggressive customer acquisition campaigns can serve as a "false friend" by temporarily inflating active customers while masking unsustainably high early-stage subscriber churn
7.
Critical tracking metric: The single early-warning signal to monitor is the ratio of deliveries to active customers, which indicates underlying customer retention and order frequency trends
8.
Other side of the story: The risks of inflation and competition are largely mitigated because Cheffelo has proven its pricing power and competitors are focusing strictly on their own profitability
