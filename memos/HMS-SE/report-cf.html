EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

HMS Networks presents a highly compelling investment case anchored on structural transformation through its ambitious 2030 Strategy targeting doubled revenue to SEK 7.5 billion with half from mergers and acquisitions, while delivering record Q1 2026 EBITA margins of 27.2% despite macro uncertainties including geopolitical uncertainties and tariff risks that could freeze customer capital expenditure . The net signal on materiality of change is decisively positive, driven by three concurrent inflections: the organizational transition to three customer-centric divisions on January 1, 2025, replacing the prior matrix structure to deliver full divisional accountability ; the strategic acquisition of Molex's industrial communications business completed January 2, 2026 for USD 7 million ; and the December 2024 launch of the Ewon Edge & Cloud platform targeting annual recurring revenue expansion to 10% of net sales by 2030 . These structural changes are materializing against a backdrop of normalized customer inventories as destocking cycles complete and backlog-to-sales ratios return to pre-Covid levels of around 0.2x from 0.65x peaks , positioning HMS Networks to capture accelerating industrial automation demand.
