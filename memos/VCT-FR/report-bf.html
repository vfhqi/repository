Section summary
  1. European carbon regulations have fundamentally shifted cement from a high fixed cost to a high variable cost industry, reinforcing regional pricing power

Regional oligopolies and logistics barriers

1.
Limited travel radius: Cement is a highly local commodity, structurally constrained to an inland shipping radius of ~150km due to high heavy transport costs
2.
Aggregates constraints: Aggregates are even more localized, constrained to a travel distance of only 30- 40km from quarries to batching plants
3.
Protected inland markets: Localized market dynamics create highly profitable regional oligopolies protected from cheap seaborne imports by steep trucking costs
4.
Inland plateau premium: Inland demand centers, such as Mexico's central plateau, effectively block imported cement due to the high cost of uphill trucking
5.
Stiff permitting barriers: Acquiring environmental permits for new cement plants remains extremely difficult, taking 5-7 years in mature developed markets
6.
Capped regional supply: These strict regulatory and environmental barriers structurally cap new capacity additions, safeguarding the market share of incumbent players
