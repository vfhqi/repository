EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Materiality of Change

AXA secured CEO Thomas Buberl's four-year reappointment through 2030 , while Guillaume Borie consolidated finance, strategy, underwriting, risk, and technology under a single executive mandate, signaling heightened operational centralization but introducing transition risks across second-level management rotations . The €5.4 billion divestment of AXA IM to BNP Paribas fundamentally simplified AXA's capital model, reducing financial risk exposure to approximately 15% of earnings and funding a massive €3.8 billion anti-dilutive share buyback that neutralized near-term EPS dilution. AXA executed targeted Italian bolt-on acquisitions of Nobis (€423 million) and Prima (€0.5 billion) to double its Italian motor franchise and capture direct distribution scale advantages . Internal cultural fragmentation persists, with AXA operating as a group of federated, independent entities where legacy IT systems and regional silos slow cross-border strategic execution .
