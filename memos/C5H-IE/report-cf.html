EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

The investment case is underpinned by severe structural undersupply in the Irish residential market, with exceptionally strong first-time buyer demand supported by favorable demographics and robust personal savings. Critical state incentives through Help to Buy and First Home Schemes cover over 80% of Cairn's private sales , while the Croí Cónaithe Cities scheme provides up to €120,000 per apartment to bridge viability gaps. This demand visibility is reflected in Cairn's expanded closed and forward order book of 3,850 homes valued at €1.5

Cairn demonstrates leadership in sustainable construction disruption , becoming the first Irish developer to deliver over 3,000 homes commenced to rigorous Passivhaus standards . The company utilizes standardized product assembly through bathroom pods and pre-fabricated steel balconies to reduce site waste and accelerate delivery timelines. This modern methods of construction (MMC) capability positions Cairn to mitigate severe skilled labor shortages while reducing future regulatory compliance costs, contrasting with peer approaches where Glenveagh targets 70% pre-manufactured value through off-site timber frames and next-generation foundations.

The regulatory and policy environment provides unmatched tailwinds for medium-density residential development. The government's "Delivering Homes, Building Communities" plan targets building 300,000 new homes by 2030 , while local authorities mandate medium-density development on 88% of zoned land , effectively forcing developers to construct apartments. The state has allocated €36.0 billion in capital funding through the National Development Plan review, and the Planning & Development Act 2024 extends consents while reducing judicial review delays . This comprehensive policy framework creates a structurally advantaged operating environment for scaled apartment developers like Cairn.

Supply-chain dynamics present manageable near-term headwinds with build cost inflation escalating to circa 2.5% year-to-date in 2026 due to geopolitical volatility. However, Cairn has proactively hedged this risk by securing over 75% procurement for 2026 and 50% for 2027 , protecting core margin profiles. The company's standardized repeatable designs from its "Library of Homes" enhance workforce productivity and mitigate labor constraints, though concentration risk remains elevated as Cairn's top 20 subcontractors manage 60% of all procurement . Critical utility connection delays and capacity constraints from state providers represent an increasing risk to completion schedules that warrants close monitoring.

The macroeconomic backdrop remains highly supportive with loosening monetary policy as the ECB deposit rate fell from 3.00% to 2.00% over 2025 , lowering average Irish mortgage borrowing rates to 3.50%. Ireland's economy demonstrates resilience with Modified Domestic Demand projected to grow by circa 2.1%, while impressive demographic expansion drove population growth of 1.5% to 5.46 million in 2025 . Most critically, Ireland's massive €10.3 billion government surplus in 2025 provides significant fiscal headroom to sustain national housing programs. Bank of Ireland reports that transaction volume expansion is driven entirely by a 30% surge in new-build properties , validating the structural demand thesis.

Additional material EPS drivers include state funding and subventions where Cairn has delivered over 6,000 social and affordable homes to state partners since 2022, while Help to Buy and First Home shared equity schemes support over 80% of private home buyer transactions . The Croí Cónaithe scheme provides up to €120,000 per unit to make urban developments affordable, backed by the Irish government's massive €36.0 billion capital funding allocation through its National Development Plan and the €10.3 billion budget surplus in 2025 guaranteeing long-term program continuity. Build cost management efficiencies represent the fourth driver, where despite rising inflationary headwinds pushing build cost inflation up to circa 2.5% year-to-date in 2026 , Cairn's proactive hedging strategy secured over 75% procurement for 2026 active construction sites. The company's scaled platform advantages held FY25 inflation to circa 1%, far below the 2% industry average , while building over 3,000 homes to Passivhaus standards reduces future regulatory compliance costs, though supplier concentration risk remains elevated as the top 20 subcontractors manage 60% of procurement .
