EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Internal Change Forces and Leadership Transition

The strategic transition to CEO Olof Persson maintains strategic continuity while the refreshed Senior Leadership Team structures operations for the Tata transaction, with Mr. Persson succeeding Gerrit Marx as Chief Executive Officer effective July 1, 2024, bringing prior experience as Volvo Group CEO from 2011 to 2015 and service as an independent Iveco director since 2022. Deutsche Bank Research highlighted that Mr. Persson was deeply involved in developing the 2024-2028 strategic business plan and was the architect of Volvo's major restructuring, leading to benchmark industrial truck margins today. Mr. Domenico Nucera was appointed Chief Quality & Operations Officer on January 21, 2025, with the newly created role centralizing all quality, manufacturing, and supply chain departments directly under the CEO. The board comprises 9 directors with 2 executive and 7 non-executive directors as of late 2025, with Exor N.V. holding 42.5% of the total voting power of the company as of March 31, 2026. The sudden departure of legacy CEO Gerrit Marx combined with high executive turnover could delay the execution of the €1 billion efficiency program.

External Change Forces and Value-Chain Dynamics

European truck demand is inflecting positively with industry volumes rising while regulatory pressures intensify through stricter Euro 7 timelines and Golden Power oversight mechanisms. The European truck market increased 9% year-on-year in Q1 2026, outpacing South American registrations which declined 17%. The European heavy truck market remains highly consolidated with five incumbents dominating approximately 98% of registrations as Tata CV integration looms to reshape competitive dynamics. Electric commercial vehicle disruption accelerates through expanded partnerships with Stellantis and Microvast despite battery residual volatility challenges. Stricter emissions standards for Euro 7 start November 2026 for light vehicle approvals while heavy vehicles face May 2028 deadlines, requiring substantial investments in product design and R&D potentially compressing cash flows, and the Italian government maintains special veto and prescription powers over strategic transport and defence assets. Engine supply continuity is preserved post-defence spin-off as IDV transitions to an external FPT Powertrain customer, with Iveco utilizing its "TIGRAN" risk tool to perform weekly financial risk assessments on 949 direct suppliers. The commercial vehicle cycle enters a normalization phase with replacement demand and pre-buying supporting order books, while macroeconomic pressures from elevated interest rates are countered by surging European defense budgets and structural EU funding programs.
