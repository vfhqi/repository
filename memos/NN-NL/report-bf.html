EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Operations and Business Model NN Group operates a highly cash-generative insurance franchise serving 18 million customers across Europe and Japan, organized into five core reporting segments: Netherlands Life, Netherlands Non-life, Insurance Europe, Japan Life, and NN Bank . The company commands a formidable 40% market share in Dutch group pensions and 23% in individual life , anchoring its operations in a highly concentrated domestic market . Insurance Europe operates a capital-light model focused on small-ticket protection products, holding top-three life insurance positions across the vast majority of its eight active European markets . The business exhibits secular pension tailwinds from the ongoing Dutch DB-to-DC transition, though sales distribution remains heavily broker-intermediated, limiting direct customer feedback loops and slowing agile product innovation . 1 - Earnings Calls Financial Profile and Peer Positioning

Industry Structure and Concentration NN benefits from a highly concentrated domestic market where the top-three Dutch non-life players now control over 66% of market share , up dramatically from just 46% in 2015 . Western Europe P&C premiums reached EUR 529bn in 2025 , growing at a solid 5.3% year-on-year pace , though geopolitical fragmentation and persistent inflation are structurally increasing claims severity across the broader European insurance landscape . The broader European life insurance sector remains highly fragmented, creating a rich pipeline for established consolidators like NN to leverage massive domestic scale and absorb closed-book portfolios with cost efficiencies that smaller mid-tier rivals lack . Value Chain Position and Margin Economics NN Group occupies the highly profitable risk-bearing and underwriting stage, with downstream integration generating 36% of sales via proprietary tied agents while relying heavily on global reinsurers like Swiss Re and Munich Re to absorb upstream longevity risks . Underwriting capital-light protection products yields double-digit margins , significantly outperforming the 2-3% margins seen in unit-linked and traditional savings products, perfectly aligning with NN's strategic European pivot . The company exhibits strong pricing power in Dutch Non-life, successfully pushing through 5-7% rate increases in motor to offset inflation, with peers like ASR Nederland similarly prioritising value over volume to support a rational pricing environment . However, reinsurers dictate terms in a hard market and independent brokers control significant Dutch market share, with margin pools slowly shifting toward distributors despite carriers retaining dominance through underwriting capacity control . Customer Dynamics and Competitive Standing NN services approximately 19 million retail and SME customers , effectively eliminating single-client dependency, with top-1, top-3, and top-5 customer concentration metrics approaching zero . Corporate pension contracts exhibit exceptional switching costs due to complex HR data integration and multi-year durations, with over 50% of corporate customers facing DB renewals successfully migrating to NN's DC platforms . The company achieved eight of ten international markets scoring at or above market average for relational Net Promoter Score, and consistently ranks number one for broker satisfaction in the highly intermediated Dutch life and pension markets . Competitive pressures remain intense, with specialist Athora aggressively capturing Dutch pension buyout share by securing exclusive agreements for roughly 60% of upcoming transition volume , and ASR Nederland executing bolt-on acquisitions to close the P&C market-share gap . Operator Quality and Foundation Strength

Disruption Forces and Long-Term Structural Positioning NN is structurally advantaged by severe Solvency II capital requirements that prevent sub-scale new entrants from underwriting core life risks, with its 220% solvency ratio providing a massive capital moat, while widening technology barriers from heavy AI investments create efficiency moats that smaller regional peers cannot replicate. The Dutch DB-to-DC transition creates a massive secular growth vector, with NN targeting a EUR 10bn buyout pipeline , while Europe's aging population drives reliable long-term demand for retirement decumulation and capital-light health protection products. Central and Eastern European economies demonstrate robust GDP+ growth , driving rapid insurance penetration and double-digit VNB expansion, positioning NN's top-tier regional franchises for sustained compounding, while the company targets a cost-to-income ratio below 55% by 2028 through automated straight-through processing. However, independent Dutch broker networks are rapidly consolidating and shifting distribution pricing power downstream, autonomous driving will eventually structurally shrink the long-term motor premium pool despite NN smartly limiting motor exposure to roughly 25% of Non-life , and private-equity-backed entrants actively target the Dutch pension buyout market with lower return-hurdle pricing.
