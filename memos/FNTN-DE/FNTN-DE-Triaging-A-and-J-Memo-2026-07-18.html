B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Freenet AG is a German asset-light telecommunications service provider, reselling mobile capacity across all three national network operators (Deutsche Telekom, Vodafone, Telefonica) rather than owning its own network infrastructure
2.
Two reporting segments from 2026. Management split out the "Consumer & Small Business" and "Enterprises & Networks" mobile units and, for the first time, will report waipu.tv IPTV as a standalone segment from financial year 2026 onward
3.
Revenue scale and mix. Group revenue reached EUR 761.9m in the first quarter of 2026 alone, up 26.1% year-on-year, though the entire increase was inorganic, driven by the first-time consolidation of the EUR 230m mobilezone Deutschland acquisition
4.
Margin profile. Group adjusted EBITDA margin stood at approximately 21.0% at the end of financial year 2025, but the mix masks a split story
4.1
Mobile compression. Mobile segment margins compressed from 34.3% to 26.7% in a single quarter, the sharpest negative data point in the group blend
4.2
IPTV expansion. The IPTV segment's margin structure is expanding on software-based operating leverage, the offsetting positive trend inside the same group number
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt to adjusted EBITDA sat at a conservative 1.2x at the end of 2025, comfortably inside management's self-imposed 3.0x ceiling, and the company repaid its EUR 100m revolving credit facility in early 2026
6.
Cash generation and payout. The business converts more than 50% of EBITDA to free cash flow
6.1
Dividend floor. Management supports a minimum dividend of EUR 2.00 per share through the 2026-2028 transition window
6.2
Policy framing. Management frames this floor as a hard commitment under total shareholder return, not a discretionary target
7.
Segment growth engine. Waipu.tv, the IPTV product, grew its subscriber base at a 37% five-year compound annual growth rate
7.1
Recent delivery. The segment delivered 49% year-on-year segment EBITDA growth in the first quarter of 2026, evidencing the growth rate is converting into earnings, not just subscribers
7.2
Forward target for 2028. Management targets at least EUR 120m of segment adjusted EBITDA by 2028, the clearest forward marker for this Element
8.
Segment drag. The mobile segment carries a quantified, contractual EUR 50m annual EBITDA headwind from an uncompetitive legacy wholesale contract with a single network operator
8.1
ARPU erosion adds a second drag. Postpaid average revenue per user (ARPU) fell 5% year-on-year to EUR 16.60, a headwind independent of the MNO contract dispute
9.
Geography and market structure. Freenet operates exclusively in the German domestic market, a saturated four-player mobile structure since 1&1's launch as an independent network operator, giving zero direct foreign exchange exposure
10.
Investment-style implication. The case reads as a "cash-cow core funding a high-growth disruptor" archetype, a defensive, high-payout mobile reseller business funding and increasingly reporting a scaling, higher-margin IPTV growth asset, with a new external chief executive driving the transition
