§1 General overview
Business Model and the Multi-Oil Ingredient House Strategy
AAK AB operates as a specialized midstream and downstream player in the global agricultural and food value
chain. Positioned fundamentally as a "Multi-oil Ingredient House," the company sources a broad spectrum of
raw materials to create functional, value-adding plant-based oil and fat ingredients for the fast-moving consumer
goods industry. Unlike vertically integrated agribusinesses that focus heavily on commodity crushing
and upstream plantations, AAK focuses primarily on the value-adding steps of refining and specialized
formulation. 1 2 • ARS 3 • Earnings Presentation
The company's multi-oil strategy allows it to blend various feedstocks to achieve specific functional properties,
mitigating reliance on any single crop. Its raw material mix is highly diversified, consisting of palm oil (51%),
rapeseed and canola oil (12%), palm kernel oil (12%), shea kernels (5%), coconut oil (6%), soybean oil (3%),
sunflower oil (4%), and various other specialty oils (7%). By combining deep expertise in specialty oils and fats
with an agile supply chain, AAK formulates solutions that offer improved end-product quality, extended shelf life,
and superior sustainability credentials compared to animal fats or fossil-based ingredients. 4 5

§3 Industry context
Market Size and Demand/Supply Dynamics
The global specialty food ingredients sector is a dynamic and rapidly expanding market, driven by shifting consumer preferences toward healthier, innovative, and sustainable food products. In 2024, global demand for
specialty food ingredients was estimated at approximately USD 85 billion. The overall market is projected to
grow at a compound annual growth rate of 6.8%, reaching an estimated USD 118 billion by 2029. In
terms of volume, global consumption stood at roughly 55,518 kilotons in 2024 and is forecast to reach 73,598
kilotons by 2029. 74 75 76 77 • PROSP
Within the broader landscape, the total global market for edible oils and fats encompasses approximately 200
million tons and is growing at an underlying rate of about 3% annually. Specialty oils and fats currently
account for only about 5% of this total edible oils and fats volume. However, this niche specialty segment is structurally positioned to grow at a structurally higher rate than the broader commodity market. This growth
divergence is fueled by an ongoing transition away from highly commoditized bulk products toward value-added
formulations that offer specialized functional properties, improved sensory profiles, and enhanced nutritional
value. 78 79 80 • ARS 81 • ARS
Supply dynamics in the specialty ingredients space remain tight, constrained by the highly technical nature of
product formulation and the stringent quality approvals required by multinational food manufacturers. This
creates significant barriers to entry, resulting in an oligopolistic market structure at the premium end of the value
chain. 82 83 • ARS
Structural Trends: Premiumization, Health, and Sustainability
The specialty food ingredients market is undergoing a fundamental transformation shaped by several intersecting
structural megatrends. First, there is a pronounced consumer shift toward health, wellness, and "clean label" formulations. Food
manufacturers are actively reformulating their portfolios to remove artificial additives, reduce sugar and sodium, and incorporate natural, plant-based ingredients. This creates a structural headwind for synthetic ingredients
but provides a long runway for growth for natural and biotech-based specialty solutions. 84 • ARS 85
86 • Deutsche Bank Research
Second, the rising adoption of GLP-1 weight-loss medications is accelerating the demand for nutrient-dense, high-
protein, and functional foods. Consumers utilizing these therapies are seeking optimized nutritional intake, driving
RESULT FOR QUERY
food companies to prioritize portion-controlled formats rich in protein and fiber to combat potential muscle loss. 87 • Deutsche Bank Research
Third, sustainability and responsible sourcing have evolved from niche consumer preferences into mandatory regulatory requirements, fundamentally altering supply chain economics. 88 • ARS
Regulatory Catalyst: The EU Deforestation Regulation The European Union Deforestation Regulation represents the most significant regulatory shift in the global
agricultural commodities market in recent years.
The EUDR mandates that companies trading in specific commodities—including palm oil, cocoa, coffee, soy, and
rubber—must prove that their products do not originate from land deforested or degraded after December 31,
2020. To comply, companies are required to implement rigorous due diligence processes, tracing supply chains back to the exact geographic plot of origin before goods can clear European customs. 89
90 • Morgan Stanley
Following industry pushback regarding supply chain readiness, the European Council adopted a mandate to postpone the implementation of the EUDR. The application date has been delayed by 12 months, shifting the
compliance deadline to December 30, 2026 for large and medium operators, and to June 30, 2027 for micro and
small enterprises. 91 92 • Morgan Stanley
Despite this delay, the regulation imposes substantial structural costs on the industry. Compliance necessitates
the physical segregation of certified sustainable materials from non-certified materials throughout the entire
supply chain—a highly labor-intensive process requiring separate storage, processing, and documentation protocols. These stringent traceability mandates are expected to create supply shortages for compliant raw
materials, subsequently driving up compliance costs and raw material premiums. 93 • Jefferies Research
94 • ARS
