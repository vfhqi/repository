KEY FINDINGS - Thesis signal: TIT-IT's FY2024 results represent a genuine operational inflection — domestic service revenue stabilised, EBITDA AL margins expanded, and net debt trajectory improved post-NetCo disposal, but leverage remains elevated and free cash flow visibility for FY2025–26 is limited [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Domestic service revenue and EBITDA AL broadly in line with upgraded FY2024 guidance; EPS less meaningful given ongoing asset-disposal and restructuring complexity; cash generation the key positive surprise [#A]

  • Structural shift: Disposal of the fixed network infrastructure (NetCo/FiberCop, sold to KKR-led consortium) completed November 2024, fundamentally repositioning TIT-IT as a pure-play services operator; this is a once-in-a-decade balance sheet reset, not a routine earnings cycle [#J]

  • Leverage: Net financial debt (post-NetCo disposal) reduced materially to approximately €8bn from prior ~€25bn; debt/EBITDA AL now tracking toward 2.5–3.0x range, still above investment-grade target but a step-change improvement versus L3Y [#A]

  • ❌ Revenue trajectory: Domestic service revenue decline arrested but not yet inflecting to growth; Consumer segment ARPUs under pressure from competitive intensity with Iliad and WindTre; MVNO substitution and fixed-to-mobile migration ongoing headwinds [#J]

  • Brazil (TIM Brasil): TIM Brasil delivered robust organic growth (mid-single-digit service revenue), providing a meaningful earnings diversification offset to weak domestic Italy trends; retaining TIM Brasil stake is now a key pillar of the equity story [#A]

  • Guidance credibility: Management issued FY2025 targets at the February 2025 Capital Markets Day covering domestic EBITDA AL and equity free cash flow; guidance is directionally credible post-NetCo but relies on Italy consumer stabilisation that has not yet been demonstrated [#J]

  • ❌ Sell-side consensus: Coverage broadly Hold-to-cautious-Buy; price targets clustered €0.24–0.36 range (ordinary shares); sell-side broadly sceptical on Italy consumer ARPU recovery timeline and execution risk on the "new TIT" cost restructuring [#A]

  • Investment implication: The NetCo disposal changes the risk/return profile fundamentally — lower leverage, lower capex intensity, but also structurally lower EBITDA; 25% IRR within 18–24M requires Italy consumer stabilisation AND TIM Brasil multiple re-rating AND further debt reduction — three simultaneous execution items [#J]

  • Sceptical flag: Post-NetCo TIT-IT is a smaller, simpler, less levered business — but the residual Italy ServCo faces structurally declining fixed-line volumes, a fiercely competitive mobile market, and a legacy cost base that restructuring alone may not fix within the thesis horizon [#J]

§1 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

TIT-IT's FY2024 cycle was dominated not by quarterly operational metrics but by the transformative closure of the NetCo disposal — a reset that changes every forward model assumption.

  • J-front verdict: FY2024 delivered in line with management's own raised guidance on domestic EBITDA AL and cash; the real signal is post-NetCo repositioning, which is structurally positive but execution-dependent [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Domestic service revenue approximately flat year-on-year in H2 2024 (vs prior decline trend), meeting management's "stabilisation" objective; EBITDA AL margin expanded modestly on cost actions; reported net income distorted by NetCo disposal accounting [#A]

  • Sub: Consensus had been revised lower through 2024 post multiple guidance cuts in FY2022–23, meaning the FY2024 "meet" came against a low bar

  • Sub: EPS comparability is essentially nil given disposal proceeds, one-off restructuring, and D&A step-changes

  • Key surprise: Equity free cash flow for FY2024 came in ahead of management's own guidance range; this was the single most important positive data point for equity holders [#A]

  • Sub: NetCo proceeds deployed to debt reduction faster than some analysts modelled

  • Sub: Working capital timing was a partial contributor — sustainability is a question for FY2025

  • Forward guide change: At the February 2025 CMD, management issued FY2025 guidance: domestic EBITDA AL growth in low-single-digit percentage range; EFCF guidance of approximately €0.5–0.6bn for FY2025 (exact figure varies by source) [#A]

  • Sub: This is the first multi-year guidance cycle post-NetCo; prior guidance was complicated by disposal timing

  • Sub: FY2026 targets were indicated directionally (EFCF >€1bn) but not formally guided

  • Consensus revision: Post-FY2024 results and CMD, sell-side broadly maintained estimates rather than making large upgrades; scepticism on Italy consumer recovery and cost base stickiness dampened upside revision momentum [#A]

  • Sub: Some brokers (Mediobanca, Equita) nudged EBITDA AL and EFCF modestly higher; others (JPMorgan, BNP Paribas) held flat or trimmed on Italy mobile concerns

  • Analyst tone: Mixed-to-cautious; the structural positive (debt reset) is acknowledged, but execution risk on the leaner ServCo model limits enthusiasm; no meaningful upgrade wave post-CMD [#A]

  • Sub: Mediobanca and Equita Sim skew most constructive; JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs most sceptical on Italy consumer

  • Investment implication: This earnings cycle confirms TIT-IT has navigated the existential leverage crisis; it does NOT confirm the operational recovery needed for 25% IRR within 18–24M [#J]

  • Sub: The thesis now pivots on three sequential items: Italy ServCo cost savings materialising, domestic ARPU stabilisation, and EFCF compound growth toward the >€1bn FY2026 target

  • Sub: All three must track for the equity re-rating; missing any one compresses the IRR substantially

  • Sceptical bullet: The "beat" on EFCF in FY2024 may reflect working capital timing and one-time disposal-related cash items rather than recurring operational cash generation; if FY2025 EFCF disappoints the ~€0.5bn guidance, the thesis de-risks materially and the re-rating thesis stalls

  • Sub: This creates valuation opacity that may suppress multiple re-rating even as cash metrics improve

  • Sub: Restructuring charges in FY2024 were lower than peak FY2022–23, suggesting the heavy-lifting phase has passed

  • Sub: Ongoing solidarity contract costs and management of ~20,000+ headcount reduction programme mean adjusted/reported gap will persist into FY2025

  • Sceptical bullet: The EFCF beat may be substantially explained by the timing of capex reductions and one-time working capital inflows post-NetCo; if FY2025's access agreement fees with FiberCop (the new network owner) prove higher than modelled, "capex-lite" economics will reverse into access-cost headwinds, suppressing the EFCF trajectory the bull case depends on

§3 — REVENUE + ORGANIC GROWTH ANALYSIS

Revenue trajectory is the weakest element of TIT-IT's FY2024 story — stabilisation achieved, but not yet a growth platform.

  • J-front verdict: Domestic revenue is stabilising after three years of decline but organic growth is not yet positive; TIM Brasil provides the only genuine organic growth contribution in the group [#J]

  • Reported growth: Group revenue declined on a reported basis in FY2024 YoY, primarily reflecting the NetCo perimeter removal (infrastructure revenues transferred to FiberCop); on a like-for-like service revenue basis, domestic Italy was approximately flat [#A]

  • Sub: The NetCo disposal complicates every YoY comparison; reported revenue is not a clean growth signal for at least 2 transition years

  • Sub: Analysts are rightly focused on domestic service revenue (ex-infrastructure) as the operational indicator

  • Organic growth by segment:

  • Sub: Domestic Consumer mobile: ARPU slight decline YoY, offset partially by SIM volume stabilisation; net organic flat to marginally negative
  • Sub: Domestic Enterprise/B2B: modest growth in managed services and cloud connectivity products, partially offsetting legacy voice/data declines; Enterprise the most constructive domestic segment
  • Sub: Domestic Wholesale (ex-NetCo): significantly smaller post-disposal; residual wholesale revenue from FiberCop access agreement now recognised as a cost-offset arrangement

  • Sub: TIM Brasil: approximately +5–6% organic service revenue growth in local currency; mobile data and 5G monetisation driving [#A]

  • Price vs volume decomposition: Domestic mobile market — price competition remains intense with Iliad, WindTre, and Fastweb Mobile; TIT has attempted selective ARPU management via premium bundling but volume risk persists [#A]

  • Sub: Fixed broadband (Consumer): market transitioning to FTTX; TIT-IT selling access over FiberCop's network at regulated or commercial rates — the ARPU capture per broadband line is structurally lower post-NetCo than when TIT owned the infrastructure

  • Sub: This is a structural pricing headwind the bull case must overcome: TIT captures service margin but not infrastructure margin on fixed broadband going forward

  • Pricing power evidence: Limited in Consumer mobile; moderate in Enterprise managed services where switching costs are higher and contract tenures longer [#A]

  • Sub: Iliad Italy's ongoing market share gains (approaching 11–12% of mobile subscribers by end-2024) impose a structural price ceiling on TIT's Consumer mobile ARPUs

  • Sub: Peer Vodafone Italy similarly reported flat-to-declining mobile ARPUs in same period, confirming this is market-structural rather than company-specific

  • ❌ Market share: TIT-IT has been a net loser of mobile subscriber market share since Iliad's 2018 Italy entry; the pace of share loss has slowed but the trajectory remains negative in Consumer mobile [#J]

  • Sub: Enterprise segment shows more stability; TIT retains dominant share in large corporate and public sector connectivity

  • Sub: No evidence of material market share recovery in any domestic segment in FY2024

  • End-market demand: Italy's GDP growth was marginal in 2024 (~0.5–0.7% estimate); consumer spending remains constrained; enterprise IT/connectivity spending modest but stable [#A]

  • Sub: Macro tailwinds are limited; Italy is not a high-growth telecom market by any reasonable framework

  • Forward revenue guidance: Management guided FY2025 domestic service revenue growth at low-single-digit positive — the first positive growth guidance in approximately 4 years [#A]

  • Sub: This is the most watched metric; if Q1 2025 data shows continued flat-to-negative, the guidance credibility gap reopens

  • Sub: Low-single-digit growth on a declining base requires approximately €100–150m of incremental domestic service revenue — achievable but not certain

  • Sell-side revisions: Limited upward revision to revenue estimates post-CMD; most brokers model flat-to-low-single-digit domestic service revenue growth for FY2025, broadly aligned with management guidance [#A]

  • Sub: The absence of large positive revisions reflects continued scepticism on Italy consumer recovery

  • Sceptical bullet: Forward guidance of positive domestic service revenue growth in FY2025 is TIT-IT management's most bullish statement in years; the mechanism for delivery (ARPU recovery + volume stabilisation amid Iliad competition) has not yet been demonstrated empirically, and three prior years of declining delivery suggest this forward guidance carries material downside risk

§4 — MARGIN PROFILE + QUALITY

Margin narrative is the most constructive element of FY2024 — but the quality of the expansion requires scrutiny.

  • J-front verdict: EBITDA AL margin expanded modestly in FY2024 on the domestic ServCo perimeter; expansion is real but driven by cost reduction rather than revenue leverage, raising sustainability questions [#J]

  • EBITDA AL margin trend (domestic):

  • Sub: FY2022: approximately 38–39% (under pressure from energy costs and competitive revenue decline)
  • Sub: FY2023: approximately 39–40% (partial recovery as energy costs normalised)
  • Sub: FY2024: approximately 40–41% (marginal expansion; cost savings offsetting flat revenue)

  • Sub: Direction is positive but the absolute level remains below best-in-class European telco peers (Deutsche Telekom domestic: ~44%; Proximus: ~42%)

  • Gross margin drivers: TIT-IT does not report gross margin in the standard sense; EBITDA AL is the operative profitability metric; within EBITDA AL, the expansion reflects: (i) energy cost tailwind normalising, (ii) headcount reduction via solidarity contracts, (iii) network cost efficiencies from outsourcing to FiberCop [#A]

  • Sub: None of these three drivers represents a permanent structural improvement — energy costs can reverse; headcount savings are finite; FiberCop access costs could rise in future contract renegotiations

  • EBIT/EBITDA margin: EBIT margin is heavily distorted by elevated D&A; post-NetCo, D&A falls as infrastructure assets are no longer on TIT's balance sheet — this will be a genuine margin tailwind in FY2025 forward [#A]

  • Sub: D&A reduction post-NetCo is one of the clearest and most predictable margin levers available

  • Sub: The magnitude of D&A step-down depends on final accounting treatment of the FiberCop service agreement — if capitalised as an IFRS16-type liability, the benefit is partially offset

  • Operating leverage: Near-flat revenue with modestly expanding EBITDA AL implies minimal operating leverage; growth-driven margin expansion is not yet visible [#A]

  • Sub: Positive operating leverage requires domestic service revenue inflection to positive; not yet achieved

  • D&A trend: Step-down expected in FY2025 as fixed-network assets are removed from TIT's balance sheet post-NetCo disposal; this is a mechanical tailwind to reported margins and EBIT, not an operational improvement [#A]

  • OpEx ratio: Controllable OpEx (labour, commercial costs) broadly flat YoY on the ServCo perimeter; the "new TIT" cost programme targets further reduction through FY2025–26 [#A]

  • Sub: Labour costs remain the largest single controllable OpEx item; solidarity contracts provide a temporary mechanism but are not permanent workforce reduction

  • Sub: Management guided for headcount-related savings contributing approximately €200–300m to EBITDA improvement over FY2025–26

  • One-off costs: FY2024 restructuring charges included accelerated depreciation, solidarity contract costs, and legal provisions; total one-offs remained elevated at approximately €400–600m (estimated, management guidance range) [#A]

  • Sub: The pace of one-off charges is declining YoY — this is a genuine quality improvement signal

  • Sub: A non-trivial portion of FY2025 "cost savings" reflects the run-off of these one-offs, not incremental operational efficiency

  • Adjusted vs reported margins: Non-GAAP adjustments remain material but are declining in absolute size; the shrinking gap is the quality signal that matters [#A]

  • Sub: Peer benchmark: BT Group's adjusted/reported EBITDA gap was approximately 8–10% in FY2024; TIT-IT's gap has been larger historically but is converging

  • Sceptical bullet: Margin expansion in FY2024 is driven by cost headwinds (energy) reversing and one-time restructuring charges declining — neither constitutes a structurally improved competitive position; if Italy mobile competition intensifies further (via a potential Iliad/MVNO price war), revenue pressure could overwhelm cost savings and EBITDA AL margins could contract back toward 38–39% in FY2026

§5 — CASH FLOW + CAPITAL ALLOCATION

Post-NetCo, TIT-IT's cash profile is the most materially changed aspect of the business — and the most important for the equity thesis.

  • J-front verdict: Cash generation improved materially in FY2024 post-disposal; the "capex-lite" ServCo model is structurally supportive of EFCF growth, but access agreement costs and residual debt service limit near-term distribution capacity [#J]

  • Operating cash flow: Domestic OCF improved YoY in FY2024 primarily due to lower capex (infrastructure investment transferred to FiberCop) and EBITDA AL stability; the OCF/EBITDA AL ratio improved [#A]

  • Sub: The OCF improvement is real but partly reflects one-time working capital benefits from disposal proceeds and timing of supplier payments

  • Sub: FCF after leases is the most relevant metric; approximately €0.5bn in FY2024 exceeded guidance

  • Cash conversion: EFCF/EBITDA AL conversion improved to approximately 13–14% in FY2024; still below European telco median (BT Group: ~18%; Deutsche Telekom: ~22%) but improving [#A] ❌

  • Sub: The structural EFCF ceiling for TIT Italy is limited by access agreement costs to FiberCop, interest expense on residual ~€8bn net debt, and ongoing restructuring cash costs

  • Sub: Getting to BT-equivalent cash conversion requires completion of restructuring and interest cost reduction — a 2–3 year trajectory

  • NWC trend: Working capital was a modest source of cash in FY2024; the sustainability of this NWC benefit is limited; FY2025 likely to revert to broadly neutral NWC contribution [#A]

  • Capex: Capital expenditure declined sharply post-NetCo; domestic capex/revenue ratio expected to fall toward 10–12% of service revenue (from ~17–20% when TIT owned network) [#A]

  • Sub: The capex-lite model is the central bull case structural change; lower capex directly translates to EFCF improvement over L3Y

  • Sub: Risk: if FiberCop access agreement costs are indexed to inflation or capacity usage, the off-balance-sheet "capex" proxy could increase over time

  • Net debt: Net financial debt approximately €8bn post-disposal vs approximately €25bn pre-disposal; debt/EBITDA AL approximately 2.0–2.5x on a post-NetCo EBITDA basis (exact ratio depends on EBITDA perimeter used) [#A]

  • Sub: This is a dramatic deleveraging — arguably the most significant balance sheet event in TIT-IT's history

  • Sub: However, absolute interest costs remain meaningful; approximately €400–500m per annum in cash interest on residual debt

  • Leverage ratio: Management target is net debt/EBITDA AL below 2.5x; current position broadly at or slightly below target [#A]

  • Sub: Peer context: Vodafone Group targets ~2.5x; BT Group operates at ~2.0–2.5x; TIT-IT post-NetCo is now within the range of investment-grade-leaning European telcos (though still rated sub-investment-grade by major agencies)

  • Sub: Achieving IG-equivalent ratings would be a significant re-rating catalyst — not near-term but visible on a 2–3 year horizon

  • Capital returns: No ordinary dividend reinstated for FY2024; management has indicated dividend reinstatement is a FY2026+ consideration conditional on EFCF trajectory [#A]

  • Sub: Absence of dividend limits the investor base (income funds excluded) and suppresses price recovery vs peers who have reinstated dividends

  • Sub: Share buybacks: none initiated; balance sheet repair is management's sole capital allocation priority

  • Sceptical bullet: EFCF of ~€0.5bn in FY2024 looks healthy against the pre-disposal business, but TIT-IT's residual debt (€8bn) consumes approximately 80–90% of FY2024 EFCF in annual interest alone — the business is generating barely enough cash to service debt after restructuring costs, leaving no meaningful capacity for dividends or buybacks until FY2026 at the earliest

§6 — MANAGEMENT GUIDANCE + FORWARD OUTLOOK

The February 2025 CMD was the most strategically important management communication since the NetCo decision; guidance credibility now rests on Italy ServCo execution.

  • J-front verdict: FY2025 guidance is directionally constructive (first positive revenue growth in years, EFCF growth) but rests on Italy consumer stabilisation assumptions that lack empirical support in current market data [#J]

  • Forward guide — FY2025: Management guided at CMD (February 2025):

  • Sub: Domestic service revenue growth: low-single-digit positive (approximately +1–3%)
  • Sub: Domestic EBITDA AL growth: mid-single-digit positive (approximately +3–6%)
  • Sub: Equity free cash flow: approximately €0.5–0.6bn (some sources indicate upper end of this range)

  • Sub: Net debt/EBITDA AL: stable to slightly improving from ~2.5x [#A]

  • Guidance change vs prior: FY2025 guidance represents a more constructive frame than FY2024 annual guidance (which was cautious throughout); the CMD is management's most optimistic formal communication in approximately 4 years [#A]

  • Sub: The positive revenue growth guidance is the most significant change — prior years had guided for "stabilisation" at best

  • Sub: Credibility risk: management has guided positively and subsequently missed in FY2022 and FY2023; the track record weakens this guidance's credibility

  • FY2026 directional targets: Management indicated EFCF >€1bn and domestic EBITDA AL continued growth; these are medium-term targets, not formal guidance [#A]

  • Sub: The €1bn+ EFCF target requires roughly doubling FY2024 EFCF in approximately 24M — ambitious but mechanically achievable if restructuring costs decline and revenue grows

  • Sub: The doubling pathway: EBITDA AL growth ~+€200–300m + D&A step-down benefit + restructuring one-off run-off ~+€200m + interest cost reduction as debt amortises — the maths are traceable but every assumption must hold

  • Management tone: Noticeably more optimistic than FY2022–23 CMD communications; CEO Pietro Labriola emphasised "new TIT" as a services company with lower risk and higher cash conversion; tone is confident but may reflect pressure to establish a new narrative post-disposal [#J]

  • Sub: Sell-side noted cautious optimism; no analyst called out overt sandbagging; some flagged that the tone shift itself should be calibrated against the track record

  • Key driver assumptions: The FY2025–26 guidance rests on:

  • Sub: Italy mobile market stabilising (no further pricing war from Iliad)
  • Sub: Enterprise/B2B managed services growth compensating Consumer weakness
  • Sub: Cost restructuring delivering approximately €500m cumulative savings over FY2024–26

  • Sub: FiberCop access agreement costs remaining within contractual parameters

  • Sell-side consensus vs guidance: Most brokers broadly aligned with management's FY2025 EBITDA AL guidance but more cautious on revenue growth and EFCF timing [#A]

  • Sub: Mediobanca and Equita modelled EFCF closer to €0.5–0.55bn (in line); JPMorgan and Citi modelled closer to €0.4bn (below guidance)

  • Sub: Divergence on EFCF reflects different assumptions on working capital, restructuring cash costs, and FiberCop access fees

  • Sell-side estimate revisions post-CMD: Modest upward revision to EBITDA AL for FY2025 (average ~+2–3%); EFCF estimates broadly unchanged to slightly higher; revenue estimates barely moved [#A]

  • Sub: The absence of large upward revisions reflects the credibility discount from prior misses

  • Sub: L12M pre-CMD, consensus estimates had been revised down approximately 5–8% on EBITDA AL — the CMD revision partly offsets but doesn't reverse this

  • L1M/L3M/L12M momentum: Consensus going into FY2024 results had negative momentum (estimates drifting down through 2024); post-results the momentum is approximately neutral; no clear positive estimate revision trend has yet established [#A]

  • Forward milestones:

  • Sub: Q1 2025 results (expected May 2025) — first post-NetCo quarterly read; will set tone for FY2025 delivery
  • Sub: Italy regulatory environment: AGCOM broadband price review could impact wholesale access pricing, affecting TIT's economics on FiberCop network

  • Sub: TIM Brasil strategic review: any decision on stake sale/IPO of TIM Brasil would be a significant portfolio event

  • Sceptical bullet: Management's transition to optimism at the CMD could reflect genuine operational improvement OR could reflect institutional pressure to establish a positive narrative post-transformative disposal; with a 3-year track record of guidance misses, the inversion case is that FY2025 guidance for positive domestic revenue growth is overconfident — if Italy Consumer mobile deteriorates further (Iliad pricing pressure, MVNO substitution), the revenue guidance misses and EFCF guidance falls below €0.5bn

§7 — STRATEGIC EXECUTION + OPERATIONAL PROGRESS

The NetCo disposal was the defining strategic event; "new TIT" execution is now what matters for the next 24M.

  • J-front verdict: The NetCo disposal executed on plan; "new TIT" ServCo strategy is coherent but implementation is early-stage with limited operational proof points yet visible [#J]

  • NetCo disposal: The sale of TIT's fixed infrastructure (FiberCop stake and related assets) to a KKR-led consortium completed November 2024; proceeds used primarily for debt reduction [#A]

  • Sub: Transaction value approximately €18.8bn enterprise value; a landmark for European telecom asset separation

  • Sub: The disposal is strategically irreversible — TIT is now a pure-play services company with no option to re-integrate infrastructure

  • FiberCop access agreement: TIT-IT entered a long-term wholesale access agreement with FiberCop for network usage; the terms and economics of this agreement are critical to the "capex-lite" model [#A]

  • Sub: The agreement has 10–15 year terms with regulated and commercial pricing components

  • Sub: Key risk: if FiberCop needs to invest in network maintenance/upgrade beyond plan, costs could be passed through to TIT via access fees — partially insulated by contract terms but not eliminated

  • Cost programme: The "new TIT" cost reduction programme targets approximately €500m in cumulative savings by end-FY2026; approximately €150–200m realised through FY2024 [#A]

  • Sub: Savings are primarily from headcount reduction (solidarity contracts, voluntary exits targeting ~4,000–5,000 roles in Italy by end-2026), vendor renegotiations, and IT platform simplification

  • Sub: Progress on-track per management; Q1 2025 will provide first update on actual run-rate

  • Enterprise/B2B push: TIT-IT is repositioning its Italy commercial strategy toward Enterprise managed services, cloud connectivity (TI Sparkle), and government digital infrastructure [#A]

  • Sub: This is a credible strategic direction — Enterprise has better ARPUs, longer contracts, and lower churn than Consumer

  • Sub: However, the Enterprise managed services market in Italy is competitive (AWS, Microsoft, Accenture, BT Italy); TIT-IT has brand recognition but limited cloud-native capability

  • TIM Brasil: Continues to execute well; FY2024 performance solid; management has not signalled any near-term intention to divest the Brazil stake [#A]

  • Sub: TIM Brasil represents approximately 30–35% of group EBITDA AL; its retention is a key pillar of group cash generation

  • Sub: Any strategic decision on TIM Brasil (IPO, partial sale, full disposal) would be a material equity event; this optionality is not fully reflected in the current share price

  • Digital/transformation: TIT-IT is investing in cloud, AI-assisted network management, and digital customer interfaces; progress is incremental and commercially early-stage [#A]

  • Sub: No material revenue contribution from digital transformation initiatives in FY2024

  • Sub: These initiatives are table-stakes for a modern telco, not a differentiated growth driver

  • Leadership: CEO Pietro Labriola remains in post; management team broadly stable post-disposal — a positive signal given prior years of instability [#A]

  • Sub: Board governance has improved; state investor CDP (Cassa Depositi e Prestiti) remains a key stakeholder (~10% stake) and provides implicit political stability

  • Sub: Government relations normalised post-NetCo (state had been an obstacle to some prior disposal plans); new regulatory clarity is a modest positive

  • Sceptical bullet: The "new TIT" strategic plan is coherent on paper but operationally untested — TIT-IT has never operated as a pure-play services company; the organisational transformation required (skills, culture, commercial model shift from infrastructure to services) is material and execution risk is high; comparable telco infrastructure separations (Openreach/BT, EE UK model) took 5–7 years to fully normalise
