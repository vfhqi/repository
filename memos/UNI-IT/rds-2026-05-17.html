### 4.1 Italian Insurance and Motor Premium Cycle Context

The Italian insurance market has been in a multi-year growth cycle — P&C premiums grew roughly 8% in both 2024 and the first part of 2025, with motor insurance the primary volume driver. The 2026 Budget Law introduced a step-change in Insurance Premium Tax on driver injury and road assistance policies (2.5% to 12.5%), creating some near-term pricing noise but not materially denting Unipol's underwriting discipline, as evidenced by the 90% combined ratio in Q1. Motor insurance pricing in Italy has been structurally firmer since 2022-23 when claims inflation accelerated; Unipol as the market leader (largest insurer by premiums) has benefited disproportionately from the pricing cycle. The Q1 non-life pre-tax result of EUR 365m beat the EUR 333m consensus by approximately 10%, a meaningful surprise in the context of an already-elevated solvency ratio. The life segment also contributed, with profitability improvements across both traditional and unit-linked products. On M&A, Unipol has been an active consolidator in Italian financials — its stake in Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena (MPS) became more prominent as MPS itself advanced a strategic bid for Mediobanca, potentially reshaping Italian financial sector ownership structures. The MPS dividend of EUR 0.86 per share ex-dated May 18 added a related catalyst for Italian financial sector stocks broadly in this period.
