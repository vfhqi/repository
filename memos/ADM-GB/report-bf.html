EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model and Organizational Structure

Admiral operates a capital-light motor insurance business generating £4.2 billion UK Motor turnover from 5.8 million customers , delivering £1.02 billion pre-tax profits in 2025. UK Home, Pet, and Travel generated £756 million turnover , with Household exceeding 2 million customers and achieving 60% profit growth . European operations delivered 1.9 million customers and £674 million turnover , with L'olivier France growing turnover by 23% while ConTe Italy shrank its base by 15% to restore profitability. Advanced pricing leverages over 120 machine learning models in production, enabling a 12 percentage point loss ratio advantage versus UK competitors. The MultiCover ecosystem locks in 1.6 million multi-product policyholders , reduces acquisition costs , and delivers 2% to 3% points better retention than UK market averages. Admiral Money administers £1.46 billion in gross loans , with pre-tax profits doubling to £25.8 million after transferring £426 million off- balance sheet to materially boost through-cycle ROE . 1 2 3

Financial Profile and Capital Efficiency

Admiral delivered an average Return on Equity of 53% over the last decade, outpacing peers Aviva, Tryg, Intact, and Direct Line on ROIC metrics. The group achieved £5.90 billion turnover and 16.8% EBITDA margin in FY25, with 43% ROIC versus Tryg's 40.5% and Direct Line's 10% . Solvency II ratio registered 193% in FY25 , down from 203% in FY24 , supporting a new capital framework introducing share buybacks . Admiral retains only 22% of UK Motor underwriting risk , with Munich Re and Great Lakes underwriting 40% of the UK Car business via proportional quota shares. The group returned £3.2 billion of capital since 2020 , historically distributing over 90% of post-tax profits as combined normal and special dividends.

Industry Structure and Competitive Positioning

The UK non-life market registers a Herfindahl-Hirschman Index of only 209 points , the most competitive insurance landscape globally , versus Germany's 502 , Spain's 651 , and France's 681 . Admiral, Aviva, and Hastings jointly hold approximately 50% combined market share in UK personal motor following Aviva's acquisition of Direct Line Group. Price Comparison Websites drive 90% of new UK business sales , with Comparethemarket and GoCompare commanding nearly 40% website traffic share each . Motor premiums rose for six consecutive months , increasing 5.7% year-on-year in May 2026, with Admiral, Hastings, and Aviva implementing premium pricing increases . 9

Value Chain Position and Margin Distribution

Admiral dominates upstream pricing by embedding over 120 machine learning models , delivering a 12 percentage point loss ratio edge over market peers. Price comparison websites command 90% of UK new business , capturing EBITDA margins of 40% to 50% , with Comparethemarket and GoCompare controlling nearly 40% traffic share each . Aggregators command 73% of UK motor retail versus only 14% aggregator penetration in Italy, requiring Admiral's multichannel distribution pivot in Europe. The threat of automotive OEMs embedding insurance has temporarily receded , with embedded products achieving only highly marginal market penetration historically.

Customer Quality and Retention Mechanics

Approximately 20% of unique UK policyholders , representing 1.6 million customers (up from 1.5 million in mid- 2024), hold multi-product contracts, with over half of Motor policyholders being homeowners. Existing motor customer cross-selling generates one-third of all Home sales , with multi-product policyholders showing a 5-point retention improvement versus single-risk motorists. UK Motor achieved Net Promoter Score exceeding 55 and Trustpilot score of 4.9 , while Household registered relational NPS above 50 . Lower competitor pricing triggers 46% of polled customers to consider switching, with elevated sensitivity during inflationary periods as aggregators rank primarily by premium cost .

Group CEO Milena Mondini de Focatiis delivers strong strategic execution since 2021, with CFO succession to Rachel Lewis preserving cultural continuity via internal promotion. Admiral secured 2nd position on Great Place to Work UK super large rankings, remaining the only UK firm with 25 consecutive years of recognition and 83% positive employee sentiment .
