Business Overview and Product Portfolio
Segment Structure and Strategic Diversification

Hexatronic Group operates as a global provider of fiber optic infrastructure, organized into three distinct reportable segments: Fiber Solutions, Harsh Environment, and Data Center. This decentralized structure aligns the company's product development and capital allocation directly with its diverse end-customer applications. By transitioning from a pure-play Fiber-to-the-Home provider into a diversified connectivity group, the company addresses the global demand for secure and future-proof digital infrastructure.

Fiber Solutions: Core Infrastructure and Technology

Fiber Solutions represents the foundational core of Hexatronic's operations, supplying passive fiber networks for FTTH deployments, 5G infrastructure, transport networks, and submarine systems. The comprehensive product portfolio includes microducts, distribution hubs, joint closures, fiber cables, and specialized installation instruments. The company emphasizes total cost of ownership reductions for contractors and network owners by focusing on products that drastically decrease installation times.

Blown Fiber and Micro CablesHexatronic's signature air-blown fiber technology is engineered to enable rapid, low-friction deployment into underground microducts. The Stingray blown-fiber drop cable features an ultra-slim 1 to 1.6 millimeter profile carrying 2 to 24 fibers, designed to be blown into 2 to 6 millimeter microducts. The Stingray sheath utilizes a zero-shrinkage design to protect fragile splice points from temperature fluctuations and environmental stress. Hexatronic also markets the Viper Ease micro cable, which incorporates dry inner tubes and tangle-free binder yarns to reduce an installer's cable preparation time by 50 to 70 percent.

Transport Networks and InterconnectsTo capture growth in national backbones and long-haul connections, Hexatronic launched the Viper Core system in March 2026. Viper Core is a high-capacity micro cabling system supporting up to 864 fibers with an ultra-low optical attenuation of 0.18 dB/km. When paired with Hexatronic's transport-enhanced microducts, the smooth inner surfaces allow air-blown cable installations to exceed distances of 3,000 meters without intermediate splicing. This technology permits network operators to scale capacity incrementally through multi-way ducts rather than over-investing in upfront physical infrastructure.

Submarine Cable SystemsHexatronic manufactures regional submarine cables using Fiber In Metallic Tube technology. These underwater systems provide scalable capacity for national network owners and island connectivity. The unrepeatered FIMT architecture enables continuous cable runs of up to 400 kilometers without the need for expensive intermediate signal amplification.

Sustainable and Hybrid Ducting SolutionsHexatronic has integrated circular economy principles into its ducting portfolio through the Microduct ECO line. Manufactured using 51 percent recycled polymer materials, these ducts deliver a 33 percent reduction in carbon footprint compared to traditional fossil-based alternatives. Additionally, the company offers the InOne Powered Fiber solution, a hybrid cable combining power and fiber to serve 5G small cells, edge networks, and fixed wireless backhaul. By consolidating utilities into a single micro-trench, InOne eliminates the need for separate, emissions-heavy excavation processes.

Harsh Environment: Specialized Connectivity

The Harsh Environment division develops highly robust dynamic cables and connectivity solutions capable of withstanding extreme physical stress. This segment's products are deeply embedded in critical infrastructure across the offshore energy, defense, marine technology, and industrial sectors. The portfolio includes advanced fiber optic sensing systems deployed to monitor industrial processes and energy facilities in real time. To expand its footprint in the European defense market, Hexatronic acquired German manufacturer JOWO Systemtechnik AG in April 2026, adding complementary connector technologies and direct defense exposure.

Data Center: Enterprise and Hyperscale Solutions

The Data Center segment provides end-to-end physical infrastructure and integration services for hyperscalers, colocation providers, and large enterprises. The product suite includes high-density fiber optic assemblies, Local Area Network cables, and containment architectures designed to optimize passive thermal management and reduce energy consumption. The cloud segment is a primary growth engine, historically accounting for approximately 40 percent of Hexatronic's Data Center sales. In addition to hardware, Hexatronic functions as a specialist service provider offering network design, ICT installation, and project management.

Key End Markets and Value-Added Services

Hexatronic's primary end customer base spans telecom operators, municipal networks, utility companies, distributors, and dedicated data center operators. Because modern fiber deployment relies on precision excavation, duct laying, and high-quality fusion splicing, Hexatronic bundles value-added services alongside its hardware. The company offers comprehensive field support and certified training programs to ensure contractors master air-blown installation techniques and precision splicing. This direct involvement in the field allows Hexatronic to secure long-term relationships, lower deployment risks for municipalities, and capture real-time feedback for future product innovation.

Business Model and Geographic Footprint
Revenue Generation and Contract Dynamics

Hexatronic generates the vast majority of its revenue through the sale of physical infrastructure goods, supplemented by a steadily expanding services component. In the first quarter of 2026, physical goods accounted for SEK 1,424 million of total revenue, while integration and installation services contributed SEK 274 million. The company operates a highly capital-intensive manufacturing model that requires continuous reinvestment to sustain growth. Consequently, the business model features a distinctly low share of recurring revenue.

Instead of subscription-like revenues, Hexatronic's sales are heavily project-based, which inherently leads to quarter-over-quarter fluctuations in both geographic and consolidated group revenues. This lumpiness is particularly pronounced in the Harsh Environment and submarine cable divisions. Harsh Environment revenues are highly dependent on large-scale infrastructure contracts within the offshore energy and global defense sectors. Similarly, submarine cable orders often result in significant revenue spikes in specific quarters based purely on the timing of manufacturing completion and physical shipment. To counterbalance this volatility and capture more value across the infrastructure lifecycle, Hexatronic is actively prioritizing the expansion of its service offerings, particularly project management and installation services within the Data Center segment.

Pricing Strategy and Cost Pass-Through

In terms of pricing power, Hexatronic operates in a relatively fragmented competitive landscape with somewhat commodity-like passive infrastructure products, which can limit sustainable, long-term pricing leverage. The company has recently navigated substantial price pressure and industry overcapacity, particularly in the European and North American microduct markets.

Despite these structural limitations, management employs a proactive pricing strategy to manage macroeconomic volatility and raw material inflation. Rising input costs for polymer resins and optical fibers—driven by geopolitical tensions impacting oil prices and surging hyperscaler demand tightening global fiber supply—are systematically passed through to customers. Because the company must honor existing contracts and pre-placed orders, there is often a timing lag that temporarily suppresses margins during periods of rapid cost escalation.

Redeye analysts note that the total full-cycle impact of increasing raw material prices is ultimately expected to be neutral to absolute gross profit, as management maintains a strong track record of successfully transferring these costs to end customers.

Geographic Footprint and Market Maturity

Hexatronic's global footprint is heavily concentrated in Northern Europe and North America, though the company deliberately diversifies its exposure across over 100 sales countries to mitigate localized economic downturns. The company addresses geographic markets based on their structural growth potential, local attitudes toward high-quality infrastructure, and overall market maturity. In the first quarter of 2026, Europe accounted for 50 percent of total sales, followed by North America at 38 percent and the Asia-Pacific region at 12 percent.

Europe: Mature Infrastructure and Strategic Transition

Europe represents Hexatronic's largest but most mature geographical market, historically anchoring the company's Fiber Solutions segment. Recently, the European market has proven highly challenging due to weak demand for Fiber-to-the-Home equipment, exacerbated by an unusually cold winter that delayed civil excavation and project execution.

Redeye analysts highlight that European sales within the Fiber Solutions segment plummeted 31 percent year-over-year in early 2026.

This structural slowdown in Europe reflects a market transition from aggressive physical infrastructure build-outs—creating new "homes passed"—toward a focus on connecting individual subscribers to existing networks, which inherently requires significantly lower material volumes. Despite these FTTH headwinds, Hexatronic expects underlying demand from European defense and energy sectors to remain robust, supporting the Harsh Environment division's regional sales.

North America: The Primary Growth Engine

North America, particularly the United States, serves as Hexatronic's most critical growth market. While the broader North American region experienced a reported sales decline of 18 percent in early 2026 due to lingering weakness in Canada, the US market itself successfully returned to positive organic growth.

The US market improvement is broad-based, fueled by an accelerated rollout pace from existing clients and the successful acquisition of new, private equity-backed tier 2 and tier 3 network operators.

Furthermore, North America is the epicenter of Hexatronic's Data Center expansion strategy. The hyperscaler and cloud markets in the US continue to report ambitious capital expenditure plans, driving exceptional organic growth for Hexatronic's tailored data center products and integration services. US market expansion is also structurally supported by massive government infrastructure initiatives, such as the BEAD broadband funding program, which incentivizes domestic manufacturing and deployment.

Asia-Pacific : Targeted Project Expansion

The APAC region, primarily comprising Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea, provides a smaller but highly dynamic growth avenue for the group.

Redeye analysts report that APAC delivered a standout 28 percent year-over-year sales increase in Q1 2026, driven by sustained strength across all of the company's primary geographic sub-markets in the region.

Growth in this territory is typically fueled by large-scale project deliveries and the cultivation of long-term partnerships. A prime example of this strategy is the recent seven-year supply contract signed with New Zealand's leading broadband operator, Chorus, which secures a stable revenue pipeline in an otherwise project-heavy region.

Customer Base and Competitive Positioning
Customer Base and Relationship Dynamics

Hexatronic has cultivated a highly diversified customer base that spans multiple end markets and geographies, significantly reducing its reliance on any single sector. The company's primary buyers within the Fiber Solutions segment include traditional telecom operators, municipal network builders, utility companies, and specialized installation contractors. In the rapidly expanding Data Center business area, the customer profile is heavily weighted toward hyperscalers, colocation operators, and large corporate enterprises. The cloud and hyperscale segment represents the primary growth engine in this division, accounting for approximately 40 percent of Data Center sales. The remaining 60 percent of Data Center revenue is distributed across a highly stable, diverse mix of enterprise data centers and adjacent information and communication technology markets. Meanwhile, the Harsh Environment segment serves specialized, high-requirement buyers in the global defense, offshore energy, marine technology, and aerospace sectors.

Customer concentration has historically been a point of focus for Hexatronic, but management has successfully executed a diversification strategy. While the top ten customers accounted for approximately 50 percent of total group revenue in recent years, successful onboarding of new clients has steadily driven this concentration down. Recent company disclosures indicate that the top ten clients now account for a much more balanced 30 percent of total revenue. This deliberate broadening of the customer base provides Hexatronic with a resilient foundation to weather localized project delays or regional macroeconomic headwinds.

Hexatronic's relationship dynamics are characterized by high stickiness and significant switching costs, engineered through a "complete system solution" approach. Rather than selling commoditized, standalone products, the company provides an integrated ecosystem encompassing microducts, blown fiber cables, distribution hubs, and measurement instruments designed specifically for seamless compatibility. This end-to-end architecture creates substantial vendor lock-in, as contractors trained on Hexatronic's proprietary blown-fiber systems face productivity losses and re-training expenses if they switch to alternative equipment providers. Furthermore, the company successfully secures long-term visibility through extended supply agreements, highlighted by the recent seven-year partnership contract signed with New Zealand's leading broadband operator, Chorus.

Competitive Positioning and Differentiation

Hexatronic differentiates itself in a highly competitive infrastructure market through a relentless focus on minimizing the Total Cost of Ownership for network owners and installation contractors. Because labor costs typically account for up to 80 percent of total network deployment expenses, Hexatronic engineers its products to drastically reduce physical installation time. A prime example is the Viper Ease micro cable, which features structural innovations that cut an installer's cable preparation time by 50 to 70 percent. This labor-saving value proposition is highly attractive to tier-2 and tier-3 network operators, as well as private equity-backed builders who require rapid deployment schedules to generate return on investment.

The company's market share and geographic positioning vary significantly depending on the region's structural maturity. In the Nordic countries, Australia, and New Zealand, Hexatronic commands a leading market position as the dominant system provider. In Europe, the company maintains sizeable and highly competitive market shares in key growth territories such as the United Kingdom and Germany. In the massive North American market, Hexatronic currently holds a strong position within the conduit and duct sub-segment, but maintains a relatively low market share in the broader fiber optic system category. This lower penetration in US fiber systems is viewed strategically as a major market share capture opportunity, particularly as tier-2 and tier-3 operators expand their footprint.

Redeye equity research notes that Hexatronic has consistently gained market share due to its high-quality, easily deployed system solutions, particularly appealing to small and mid-sized customers who possess limited in-house technical capabilities.

Industry consultants point out that Hexatronic operates as a highly formidable niche player in the US market, excelling specifically in high-density underground microduct solutions for new-build neighborhoods.

Competitors and Structural Barriers to Entry

The competitive landscape for passive fiber infrastructure is relatively fragmented, featuring a mix of global telecom conglomerates and specialized regional manufacturers. In the North American market, Hexatronic competes against industry heavyweights such as Corning, which maintains a dominant grip on premium fiber cable supply for Tier-1 service providers. Clearfield is another primary competitor in the US, offering a broad line of fiber management and connectivity products targeted at regional broadband service providers. Within the specific US microduct and conduit segment, Hexatronic faces direct competition from Dura-line, a subsidiary of Orbia. Globally, large-scale manufacturers like Prysmian also act as formidable alternative suppliers, particularly when top-tier vendors face supply constraints.

Despite the presence of well-capitalized competitors, Hexatronic benefits from multiple structural barriers to entry. The company's strategic commitment to localized manufacturing serves as a massive competitive moat in an era of increasing geopolitical fragmentation. By operating production facilities locally in the US, Europe, and APAC, Hexatronic shields its cost base from international trade tariffs, protectionist policies, and volatile global freight rates. This localized footprint also qualifies the company for lucrative government infrastructure subsidies, such as the BEAD program in the US, which heavily incentivizes domestic manufacturing.

Furthermore, the manufacturing of fiber infrastructure is inherently capital-intensive, requiring continuous and significant reinvestment to sustain capacity growth, which naturally deters new market entrants. In the Harsh Environment division, the barriers to entry are exceptionally high due to the mission-critical nature of the products. Competing in the defense and subsea energy sectors requires decades of technical experience, rigorous military-grade certifications, and a deeply entrenched foundation of customer trust that new entrants cannot easily replicate.

Financial Profile and Performance
Revenue Scale and Growth Trajectory

Hexatronic Group has demonstrated significant revenue scale, though its growth trajectory over the past three years reflects a transition period for its core end markets. During the 2023 fiscal year, the company achieved total net sales of SEK 8,150 million, representing a robust 24 percent annual growth rate fueled by broad-based fiber deployment. However, as the European and North American Fiber-to-the-Home markets experienced a severe cyclical slowdown due to high inventory levels and macroeconomic headwinds, revenue contracted in 2024. For the 2024 fiscal year, net sales declined by 7 percent to SEK 7,581 million, with an underlying organic growth contraction of 13 percent.

By 2025, Hexatronic successfully stabilized its top line through strategic diversification into high-growth connectivity segments. The company reported total net sales of SEK 7,519 million for 2025, which represented a modest 1 percent reported decline but crucially masked a positive underlying organic growth rate of 3 percent. This return to organic growth in 2025 was entirely driven by the Data Center segment, which surged 45 percent, and the Harsh Environment segment, which grew 5 percent, effectively offsetting an 11 percent decline in the legacy Fiber Solutions division.

Profitability and EBITDA Margins

Hexatronic evaluates its core operational profitability using adjusted EBITA and adjusted EBITDA margins, which strip out non-recurring items and the amortization of acquisition-related intangible assets. For the full year 2025, the group reported an adjusted EBITDA margin of 12.5 percent. When looking at the adjusted EBITA, the group delivered a margin of 8.4 percent, corresponding to an absolute adjusted EBITA of SEK 632 million. This consolidated margin profile blends highly divergent performances across the company's three distinct business areas.

The Data Center business is currently the group's most profitable division, achieving an exceptional adjusted EBITA margin of 17.9 percent for the full year 2025. The Harsh Environment segment also delivered strong double-digit profitability, recording an 11.2 percent adjusted EBITA margin. Conversely, the legacy Fiber Solutions segment heavily diluted the group average, posting a significantly lower adjusted EBITA margin of 6.4 percent due to weak volume leverage and lower capacity utilization at its manufacturing facilities. To combat this margin degradation in Fiber Solutions, management executed a comprehensive performance improvement program throughout late 2025, incurring SEK 298 million in non-recurring restructuring charges to right-size European operations and adjust capacity in the United States.

By the first quarter of 2026, the initial benefits of these cost-saving measures began to materialize, helping the consolidated adjusted EBITA margin sequentially improve to 8.6 percent.

Redeye equity research highlights that the slight year-over-year margin compression seen in the Data Center segment during early 2026 (down to 16.8 percent) is highly reasonable and strategically sound.

Redeye analysts note that Hexatronic is deliberately investing its high Data Center margins into organic growth initiatives, specifically expanding its service capabilities into adjacent enterprise segments like university campuses and hospitals to diversify away from pure hyperscaler concentration.

Capital Expenditure Intensity

Historically, Hexatronic operates with a relatively low capital expenditure intensity, relying on flexible manufacturing footprints and an increasingly service-oriented revenue mix. Following a period of elevated capacity expansion, capital expenditures (defined as investments in tangible and intangible assets) decreased dramatically from SEK 325 million in 2024 to just SEK 98 million in 2025. This 2025 capex figure represents a very light capital intensity of approximately 1.3 percent of total net sales.

The SEK 98 million in 2025 capital expenditures was primarily allocated toward maintenance and targeted efficiency improvements rather than major footprint expansions. The Harsh Environment segment received the largest share of this investment at SEK 47 million, largely dedicated to production upgrades at the Rochester Cable facility. Fiber Solutions accounted for SEK 40 million, while the highly scalable, service-heavy Data Center segment required only SEK 11 million in capex to support its 45 percent revenue growth.

Cash Generation and Balance Sheet Health

Despite the cyclical headwinds in its legacy fiber business, Hexatronic maintains a highly resilient cash generation profile. For the full year 2025, cash flow from operating activities before changes in working capital remained strong at SEK 599 million. After accounting for a minor negative working capital change of SEK 14 million, total operating cash flow for 2025 stood at SEK 584 million. The company has placed a major strategic focus on optimizing its working capital, successfully reducing absolute inventory levels from SEK 1,442 million in 2024 to SEK 1,202 million by the end of 2025.

This rigorous focus on cash generation peaked in the fourth quarter of 2025, where the company delivered a massive operating cash flow of SEK 349 million, representing an exceptional cash conversion rate of 235 percent. Cash conversion naturally fluctuates due to the project-based lumpiness of the business; for instance, conversion normalized to 26 percent in the first quarter of 2026 due to a temporary build-up in accounts receivable following a strong end-of-quarter shipping schedule.

From a balance sheet perspective, Hexatronic is well-capitalized to execute its ongoing M&A agenda. The company closed the 2025 fiscal year with an adjusted leverage ratio of 1.9x pro forma EBITDA (excluding IFRS 16 lease liabilities), comfortably within management's target range. Total interest-bearing net debt decreased from SEK 1,880 million in 2024 to SEK 1,582 million at the end of 2025. By the end of Q1 2026, adjusted leverage ticked up slightly to 2.2x due to unfavorable foreign exchange revaluations and an earnout payment, but overall liquidity remained exceptional with SEK 603 million in cash and SEK 1,122 million in unutilized backup facilities.
