EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (500 words)

IG Group Holdings presents a superficially strong narrative—record revenues (£1.12bn FY25), record customer acquisition, diversification into Freetrade and crypto, management confidence in 2026 growth—but harbours five material risks that collectively could reprice the stock by 25-40% if materialized.

Headline risks:

1. Goodwill Impairment (tastytrade £497m, Freetrade £160m valuation, Independent Reserve acquisition Jan 2026). If tastytrade customer growth stalls post-Sosnoff (Sept 2025 departure), if Freetrade ARPU remains near-zero (~£0-5 per customer annually), or if crypto volumes in Asia-Pacific fail to scale, write-downs are inevitable. IG's crypto holdings are marked-to-Kraken's latest round (now $20bn, up 33% in eight weeks); if Kraken's IPO prices below current marks or volumes decline, a £50m+ write-down is plausible.

2. Revenue Volatility masquerading as growth. FY25 net trading revenue grew 10% to £1,005m, but this masks a structurally fragile customer base. Active customer count rose only 3-5% while revenue per customer was volatile. The Q1 FY26 revenue decline (-4%, despite 3% customer growth) suggests trading velocity is falling—a signal that IG's customer acquisition is generating low-value clients. If market volatility reverts to the VIX-12-14 environment of H2 2024, revenue declines to £950m+ (near 2023 levels) become likely.

3. Interest Income Cliff. Net interest income fell 16% YoY (£142m → £119m) as benchmark rates declined. Management expects another 15%+ decline to ~£100m. This is a structural headwind lasting 3-5 years. Net interest income has shrunk from 14% to 11% of total revenue. Management repositioning toward NTR and spreads masks the reality: if rate-sensitive revenue is declining and client volatility is normalizing, the company faces a dual headwind to margins (NTR down + NII down simultaneously).

4. Execution risk across three acquisitions in 12 months. Freetrade (£160m, April 2025), Independent Reserve ($178m/£140m, Jan 2026), plus organic integration of US options (tastytrade) post-Sosnoff. Employee morale has already deteriorated sharply (Net Promoter Score -0.3 vs industry +29). Three brands = three compliance regimes, three tech stacks, three marketing budgets. The "standalone brand" strategy is code for delayed integration and hidden costs.

5. Valuation appears full at 13-14x consensus FY26 EPS (119p). Analyst consensus price target is 1,468p (0.7% downside from 1,478p). If bears are right and FY26 EPS falls to 90-95p (a 15-20% miss), the stock reprices to 1,100-1,200p, a 25-35% decline. UBS has downgraded IG on "growth concerns ahead of FY26."

Overall Bear Case Credibility: ORANGE. The risks are real, well-documented, and material. The probability of all five occurring simultaneously is low. But any three would materially impair returns.

ACCOUNTING & GOVERNANCE RED FLAGS

Goodwill Impairment Risk

tastytrade (£497.2m goodwill, no accumulated impairment):
- Acquired June 2021 for ~$1bn
- Founder Tom Sosnoff departed September 2025, citing "new ventures" (Lossdog AI platform)
- IG management states Sosnoff remains a shareholder and consultant, but his operational departure removes the brand's founder-centric appeal
- Bear Thesis: tastytrade's growth was built on Sosnoff's personal brand, cult following, and content (daily show, Twitter/X presence). Without founder-driven differentiation, tastytrade risks commoditization in US options space.
- Impairment Trigger: If tastytrade NTR growth decelerates to <5% YoY, goodwill testing will be required. Conservative case: £100-150m write-down by FY27.

Freetrade (£160m acquisition price, April 2025):
- 720k customers as of end-2024; AUA £2.5bn → £3.3bn by Nov 2025 (+36% YoY)
- Red Flag: ARPU is not disclosed. Given Freetrade's zero-commission model and limited monetization (interest on cash balances, optional premium tier), ARPU is likely £0-10 per customer annually.
- 720k customers at £5 ARPU = £3.6m annual revenue. Amortizing £160m acquisition over 5 years = £32m cost of capital. Unless ARPU scales to >£15-20, Freetrade produces negative ROI.
- Bear Thesis: IG overpaid for customer headcount in a low-ARPU business. Cross-sell success from Freetrade cohorts (typically lower-risk, passive investors) to IG's higher-risk products (CFDs, leverage) is structurally challenging.

Independent Reserve ($178m/£140m, completed Jan 2026):
- Australian crypto exchange; 30% retained ownership by existing team
- IG's crypto holdings are marked-to-Kraken valuation ($20bn). A 20% decline in Kraken valuation = £40-50m loss.
- Impairment Magnitude: tastytrade £497m + Freetrade £160m + Independent Reserve £140m + Kraken position ~£100m = ~£900m of goodwill and intangibles exposed to impairment if execution falters.

COUNTER-ARGUMENTS TO BULL CASE

### Bull Thesis #1: "Multi-brand platform diversification reduces risk"
Bear Rebuttal: Three brands = three regulatory regimes, three compliance budgets, three tech stacks, three customer acquisition funnels. Operational complexity increases, not decreases. Decentralized operating model has already lowered staff morale to NPS -0.3 and triggered workforce reductions.

### Bull Thesis #2: "Freetrade adds 457k net new customers + £3.3bn AUA"
Bear Rebuttal: Customer headcount growth ≠ revenue growth if ARPU is near-zero. Freetrade's zero-commission model = estimated ARPU of £0-5 annually. At £5 ARPU: £3.6m annual revenue on a £160m acquisition = negative ROI unless cross-sell works at scale. Cross-sell success is unproven; Freetrade customers may have been attracted precisely because they avoided CFDs.

### Bull Thesis #3: "tastytrade US options growth continues"
Bear Rebuttal: Sosnoff departed Sept 2025. tastytrade's growth was driven by founder's personal brand and content empire. Without him, differentiation erodes vs. Schwab/TD Ameritrade/Webull. If active customers flatten, goodwill write-down triggers in FY27.

### Bull Thesis #4: "47% EBITDA margins are structural"
Bear Rebuttal: Dual margin headwind scenario: (1) NII declining 15%+ (£142m → ~£100m over 24 months) = -£42m revenue drag; (2) If volatility normalizes and client trading volumes decline 10%, NTR declines ~£100m. Combined revenue impact -£140m (12% of total). If costs held flat, EBITDA margin compresses from 47% to ~40%.

### Bull Thesis #5: "Volatility tailwind supports NTR"
Bear Rebuttal: Q1 FY26 already showed -4% revenue decline despite +3% customer growth — early signal trading velocity is falling. Long-term VIX mean is 15-18; H2 2024 was VIX 12-14 for 18+ months. If VIX reverts, NTR deflates to FY23 levels. Historical precedent: 2016 FCA leverage restrictions drove 20-30% revenue declines.

HISTORICAL PATTERN

2016: FCA Binary Options Ban — IG voluntarily withdrew binary options. Share price fell 38% but recovered within 18 months. Lesson: Regulatory changes are periodic; IG has proven adaptability but short-term SP volatility is real.

2017-2018: ESMA Leverage Ban — IG exited certain markets, accepted lower leverage ratios. Revenue impact ~15% in affected product lines. No formal short attack; managed through operationally.

PFOF Ban (June 2026, EU-wide): IG has been PFOF-free since UK ban (2012), so direct impact is minimal. EU-wide ban affects competitors more; potential for selective market share gain.

No formal short-seller campaigns in L10Y history. Suggests IG has avoided the fraud/accounting red flags that trigger activist short campaigns.
