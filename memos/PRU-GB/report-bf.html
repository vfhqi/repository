EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

BUSINESS MODEL AND OPERATIONAL PLATFORM

Prudential provides life and health insurance plus asset management across Greater China, ASEAN, India, and Africa with corporate headquarters strategically located in Hong Kong and exclusively focused on high-growth emerging markets following structural demergers. Hong Kong represents the largest segment delivering $1,221 million in new business profit during 2025, while CITIC Prudential Life operates as a 50-50 joint venture covering 102 cities representing approximately 80 per cent of Chinese GDP. The ASEAN segment is anchored by Singapore generating $938 million in annual premium equivalent sales in 2025 and Malaysia conventional and Takaful businesses combined making it the largest life provider in the country. Operating via ICICI Prudential Life, the business maintains a leading private-sector top-5 market position in India with a 22 per cent shareholding and progressing standalone health regulatory approvals. Eastspring Asset Management spans 10 markets managing $277.7 billion of total funds under management including $174.5 billion of funds on behalf of Prudential's life entities.

Prudential deploys a balanced multi-channel model with agencies representing 55 per cent of first-half new business profit and bancassurance new business profit increasing by 27 per cent in 2025. The group maintains approximately 57,000 average monthly active agents possessing the second-largest Million Dollar Round Table force globally with MDRT qualifiers over seven times more productive than non-qualifiers. The business is the number one independent insurer in Asia bancassurance with 180 bank partners including 11 strategic partners, a 15-year strategic bancassurance agreement with Standard Chartered covering 11 key markets, and key relationships with UOB and CITIC Bank. The GenAI platform PruAction was launched in Singapore to provide real-time performance insights.

FINANCIAL BENCHMARKING AND PEER PERFORMANCE

The Group GWS shareholder solvency cover ratio stood at 262 per cent at end-2025 with shareholder surplus above the Group Prescribed Capital Requirement reaching $17.1 billion, supporting an S&P Global Ratings upgrade of core entities to AA in 2025. Moody's basis leverage was 13 per cent at end-2025 unchanged year-on-year, remaining well within the 5 per cent to 20 per cent range for AA-rated firms with Morgan Stanley estimating Net Debt at $1,556 million. The shareholder investment portfolio is highly defensive with 94 per cent rated investment grade and sixty-three per cent of the non-government shareholder portfolio rated A- and above.

VALUE CHAIN POSITIONING AND MARGIN DISTRIBUTION

Prudential focuses entirely on underwriting and asset management in Asia with operating segments writing high-quality regular premium protection policies and premium value captured through agency and bancassurance channels. Proprietary agents represent the second-largest Million Dollar Round Table force globally while Eastspring manages $174.5 billion of funds directly for Prudential's life insurance entities. Prudential is the only pan-Asian insurer with integrated stand-alone asset management. Expert interviews reveal internal infighting over mandate allocations with Eastspring and management debates placing life funds with external managers like BlackRock.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE AND MARKET POSITIONING

Asia remains the largest global life insurance market driven by demographic expansion with Asia life premiums rising 9.9 per cent in 2025 outstripping historical averages while emerging Asia ex-Japan and China premiums are projected to grow 6.8 per cent annually. The health protection gap across Prudential's footprint is estimated at $300 billion in premium terms. Singapore boasts a high private life insurance penetration rate of 7.4 per cent of GDP while life penetration sits at 3.7 per cent in Malaysia, 3.4 per cent in Thailand, 0.9 per cent in Indonesia, and India and China exhibit penetration rates of 2.7 per cent and 2.4 per cent respectively. Prudential holds top-3 positions in major ASEAN markets including Singapore, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Prudential maintains low customer concentration as its retail model focuses on millions of individual policyholders serving more than 17 million customers across 20 markets in Asia and Africa. The group's customer retention rate reached 88 per cent at the end of fiscal year 2025 representing a steady increase from 87 per cent in 2024 and 86 per cent in 2023 with Prudential aiming to reach customer retention rates of 90 to 95 per cent by 2027. The Singapore business ranked No. 1 Insurer in The Straits Times Best Customer Service survey for three consecutive years. Prudential manages morbidity risks through aggressive and regular repricing of medical portfolios with Jefferies indicating the group has shifted from multi-year cycles to pricing every year to manage inflation.

FOUNDATION QUALITY AND OPERATOR DISCIPLINE

Chief Executive Officer Anil Wadhwani assumed leadership in February 2023 bringing over 30 years of global financial services experience, previously serving as President and CEO of Manulife Asia, with background including a 25-year tenure at Citi across Asia Pacific, EMEA, and the United States. Executive compensation metrics are highly aligned with the group's transformation agenda with the CEO's annual objectives weighting operational transformation at 35 per cent and strategy at 25 per cent, and the board judging the CEO achieved 95 per cent of his personal and strategic priorities in 2025.

Prudential has executed a massive $400 million investment program to modernise regional technology platforms with the intensive capability investment cycle scheduled to reach completion by end-2026 and system enhancements successfully delivering over 99.5 per cent platform availability. The group delivered consistent double-digit growth in new business profit during every quarter of 2025 with operating and experience variances improving significantly from negative $113 million in 2024 to negative $45 million in 2025. Dual-channel distribution creates a highly defensive platform with over 180 bank partners and 11 strategic alliances while monthly active agents stood at 57,000 in 2025 providing a powerful direct-to-consumer advisory network.

STRUCTURAL RISKS AND plausible problem to monitor

Prudential's aggressive agency professionalisation strategy relies heavily on double-digit productivity gains to offset persistent active agent count declines, leaving the distribution network exposed if PruVenture recruitment fails to scale. Monthly average active agents fell to 57,000 in 2025 down from 65,000 in the prior year period with the decline concentrated across emerging ASEAN markets including Malaysia and Indonesia. A 15-year strategic bancassurance agreement with Standard Chartered covers 11 Asian markets expiring in 2029 while Standard Chartered's India distribution arrangement faces migration risks under open architecture framework and may redirect new business flows toward Bharti Life once Prudential secures a 75 per cent controlling stake. The undiscounted free surplus release profile has become more back-end weighted due to recent product rebalancing.
