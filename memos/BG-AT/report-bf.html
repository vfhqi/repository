EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Retail deposit gathering anchors a highly sticky, digital-first funding model with customer deposits supporting circa 65.5% of total group assets , maintaining a low blended deposit beta of circa 35% and an average account balance of approximately €12,000 indicating structurally lower pricing sensitivity than typical corporate depositors . BAWAG retains exceptional structural pricing power by leveraging a 35% deposit beta that

declined sequentially from 37% in Q4 2025 , maintaining customer spreads by systematically avoiding high-yield savings deposit bidding wars . A €24 billion long-dated deposit hedge covering approximately 50% of deposits with a four-year average duration provides robust structural protection against rapid central bank rate cuts, though net interest income remains sensitive with €25 million exposure per 25 basis point rate change .

Management alignment remains exceptionally strong, with the Management Board holding a substantial 4.7% equity stake and the senior leadership team collectively owning over 5.0% of BAWAG , with approximately 74% of Management Board shareholding stemming from voluntary personal investments made in the open market . BAWAG's digital operating platform systematically lowers transaction cost-to-serve with each sequential year, successfully decoupling all Transitional Service Agreements for recent acquisitions within twelve months while the Knab integration completed in late 2025 drives significant cost take-outs . Strategic utilization of Significant Risk Transfer transactions optimizes capital requirements and releases regulatory capital, with Morgan Stanley estimating BAWAG as a top-tier European bank in regulatory capital relief via SRT programs , immediately redeploying released capital into high-yielding consumer credit portfolios or earnings-accretive acquisitions . topic-specific reports The de-risked commercial real estate portfolio totals €5.7 billion with 61% residential exposures , aggressively managing office exposure down to 10% of real estate assets and reducing highly distressed US office assets to just €0.2 billion , while limiting real estate financing to senior secured structures with average loan-to-values below 50% . However, the Austrian bank levy increased regulatory charges by €38.9 million in 2025 , representing a material headwind to profitability . Rising digital competition challenges traditional retail deposit yields in core German-speaking regions, with JP Morgan entering Germany offering a competitive 4% savings rate , though Germany's massive retail deposit pool remains highly rational and resilient to digital newcomer pricing pressure .

transformative Permanent TSB acquisition expands the total customer base by approximately 1.3 million clients and increases group total assets by a massive 40% , with Deutsche Bank analysts expecting the transaction to add over €250 million in net profit by 2028 through cross-selling high-margin brokerage, personal consumer credit, and insurance products . Critical risks include highly concentrated geographic focus on DACH and retail lending leaving the business model vulnerable to regional macroeconomic downturns, while any integration bottlenecks in German and Irish portfolios could delay synergy realization and compress targeted returns . Unanticipated integration delays or severe credit deterioration within newly acquired Irish and

German mortgage portfolios could degrade BAWAG's premium RoTCE and stall capital returns, though the bank's defensive balance sheet, robust replication portfolio, and proven M&A integration playbook position it favorably against these structural headwinds.
