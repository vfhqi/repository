EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Net Verdict on CEO Quality

Michael O'Leary represents an extreme sector outlier, serving as CEO since 1994 with 32 years of continuous operational oversight at Ryanair . His early financial grounding as a trained accountant and tax consultant established a rigorous foundation for obsessive cost reduction and capital-intensive aviation focus . O'Leary's 4% ownership stake aligns him directly with equity holders unlike salaried peer executives at competing airlines . He exhibits extraordinary speed in capital allocation, executing large counter-cyclical aircraft orders during major macroeconomic downturns . O'Leary demands extreme outlier standards of cost control that are beaten into

every airline employee . Bernstein Research highlights his defensive low-cost model and net cash balance sheet as structurally superior .

Operator Archetype and Strategic Posture

Delivery Record and Shareholder Returns

Ryanair achieved a 40% increase in pre-exceptional PAT to €2.26BN in FY26, surpassing prior FY25 profit of €1.61BN. Passenger traffic grew 4% to 208.4M in FY26, cementing its status as Europe's largest short-haul carrier. Operating profit jumped 52% to €2.37BN, reflecting O'Leary's relentless cost control relative to peers. Capitalized ROIC reached 18.9% in the latest fiscal year, far outshining the sector-average of high single-digits. Total shareholder return reached over 150% over the past three years, placing Ryanair in the top European quartile. Ryanair completed 80% of its €750M share buyback, reaching €600M in total spend by May 2026. The airline repaid its last €1.2BN bond in May 2026, leaving the group completely debt-free.

Governance Robustness and Board Structure

The board consists of 12 active directors with one executive and 11 independent non-executive directors . Average non-executive director tenure is only four years, significantly below the typical nine-year limit of UK Corporate Governance Code . Board changes are scheduled far in advance to minimize disruptions, with Chairman and SID extended through 2029-2030 . Directors as a group held 4% of outstanding ordinary shares, materially higher than European aviation peers . O'Leary receives a basic salary of €1.20m with annual bonus capped at 50% and zero pension benefits . EU regulations require majority EU ownership to protect operating licenses, with non-EU shares treated as Restricted Shares with suspended voting rights .

CFO and Senior Leadership Team Quality

Group CFO Neil Sorahan was appointed in October 2019, serving Ryanair in senior finance roles since 2003 . Sorahan maintained €3.6BN gross cash in FY26 and oversaw repayment of the final €1.2BN bond, leaving the group debt-free . Sorahan locked in 80% of FY27 fuel at $67/bbl, protecting margins against Middle East spot spikes and widening the cost gap versus peers . The group operates with only three layers of management, with C-suite averaging 20 years of airline experience . Ryanair prioritizes internal promotions with a formal

Graduate Programme training future leaders across subsidiaries . 12

Insider Transaction Signals and Capital Allocation

Michael O'Leary holds a 4.1% personal equity stake comprising 44,096,725 ordinary shares, considerably exceeding standard UK/European guidelines of 200% salary . The board negotiated a contract extension to April 2032 featuring 10 million share options requiring Group PAT exceeding €2.2 billion to vest . Ryanair executed a €536.1 million equity repurchase and cancelled 20.5 million repurchased shares in fiscal 2026, directly boosting per-share metrics . Company Secretary Juliusz Komorek exercised 3,000 options at €11.12 and sold 3,000 shares at €27.165 in November 2025 . Wizz Air CEO József Váradi maintains a 1.5% stake of 1,504,472 shares, meeting his 400% salary guideline .

Expert Network Alignment on Execution

Single Largest Credibility Risk

The extreme length of O'Leary's four-decade institutional grip risks creating absolute key-person dependency and an unresolvable corporate succession vacuum . O'Leary reached 64 years of age in May 2025, with the board extending his contract from July 2028 to April 2032 . SEC filings explicitly warn that losing any senior executive without a replacement could materially damage results . The structural division of airline CEOs remains largely cosmetic as O'Leary's highly centralized authority risks leaving no successor with genuine Group-level strategic autonomy . The AGCM levied an unprecedented €256.00m antitrust fine in December 2025, though management is confident the fine will be overturned on appeal . Extreme cost-cutting and aggressive communication may permanently damage key partnerships, causing supplier gridlock or severe labor strikes .
