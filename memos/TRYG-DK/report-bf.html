EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Business Model and Strategic Positioning

Tryg operates as the largest non-life insurer in Scandinavia, leveraging a dominant retail focus across Denmark, Sweden, and Norway to generate highly predictable, annuity-like premium streams from 6 million customers. The company handles 2.1 million claims annually and maintains a low-risk, underwriting-centric earnings model where technical results historically contribute around 90% of total pre-tax profits. This business model architecture creates exceptional insulation from volatile investment income fluctuations while delivering superior capital returns through disciplined portfolio management and scale-driven cost advantages.

The unique TryghedsGruppen member bonus scheme represents an unmatched competitive moat in the Danish home market. TryghedsGruppen, which owns 49.8% of outstanding shares, utilizes its dividend cash flow to pay a 5% to 8% premium rebate to Danish policyholders, significantly lifting customer retention by 11 percentage points for policyholders aware of the scheme. This circular loyalty flywheel creates durable pricing power and switching cost barriers that cannot be replicated by pure commercial competitors.

Financial Performance and Peer-Leading Return Profile

Tryg delivers exceptional industry-leading return on own funds of approximately 40%, substantially outperforming peers through world-class underwriting discipline and structural cost advantages. The company maintains superior return on own funds of 40.3% in 2025, comfortably exceeding its 35-40% target and standing substantially above Gjensidige's 34.2% and Sampo's 26.0% return profiles. This premium return generation stems primarily from high-quality technical underwriting results rather than volatile investment income, providing exceptional earnings stability across market cycles.

Tryg has improved its underlying loss ratio year-on-year in almost every quarter since 2017, recording an average annual improvement of 33 to 57 basis points and delivering a highly competitive 80.3% combined ratio in 2025, with the 'United Towards 27' strategy targeting a sustainable group combined ratio of around 81% by 2027. The company's market-leading gross expense ratio of 13.4% in 2025, subsequently compressed to 12.5% in Q1 2026, demonstrates significant structural cost advantages over peers like Gjensidige at 12.7% and Alm Brand at 16.8%.

Capital allocation remains highly disciplined, with Tryg reporting a robust Solvency II ratio of 192% in Q1 2026, supporting a nominally stable to increasing ordinary dividend of DKK 8.20 per share for 2025 representing an 82% payout, supplemented by a DKK 1bn extraordinary share buyback program with total distributions of DKK 17bn to 18bn targeted over the 2025-2027 strategic period.

Industry Structure and Competitive Dynamics

The highly concentrated Nordic non-life oligopoly creates a structurally disciplined pricing environment, with cumulative top-4 player market share reaching 79% in Sweden, 71% in Norway, 64% in Denmark, and 90% in Finland. This concentrated market structure supports stable market shares and rational competitor behavior, as Nordic insurers strongly prioritize underwriting profitability over aggressive market share volume growth.

Formidable entry barriers protect incumbent margins, with new entrants facing substantial underwriting losses as over half of expenses tie to customer acquisition, while Nordic insurers maintain a direct-to-consumer model and have avoided distributing products via disruptive price comparison websites, protecting insurers from paying high customer acquisition fees that drag down UK motor margins by 10 percentage points. This structural defense mechanism insulates the Nordic oligopoly from price-walking dynamics and aggregator disintermediation that have permanently compressed margins in other European markets.

Competitors in the Nordic region strongly prioritize underwriting profitability over aggressive volume growth, and when one insurer raises premiums to combat claims inflation, competitors tend to follow suit to protect their own margins, as demonstrated by insurers rapidly implementing double-digit motor price increases in Norway to successfully offset elevated electric vehicle claims inflation.

Value Chain Economics and Power Dynamics

Tryg captures exceptional technical margins by owning both the direct distribution and underwriting stages, with bypassing aggregators protecting Tryg from paying high customer acquisition fees that drag down UK motor margins, while technical results historically contribute around 90% of total pre-tax profits. The primary profit pool in Scandinavian non-life insurance resides in high-quality technical underwriting results, with Nordic insurers consistently generating combined ratios between 81% and 90%, protected by exceptionally low operating expense ratios compared to European peers.

Tryg possesses strong pricing power, allowing it to pass claims inflation through to satisfied customers by implementing double-digit motor price increases in Norway to successfully offset elevated electric vehicle claims inflation, although premiums are usually only repriced annually, leaving Tryg temporarily exposed to sudden claims inflation spikes, while Nordic competitors tend to follow pricing hikes to protect margins rather than competing on volume.

Tryg manages a massive annual purchasing budget across auto repair shops, carpenters, and private hospitals to resolve claims, systematically channeling claims to its preferred network of claims suppliers to secure volume discounts, with small local suppliers gaining a steady, highly predictable flow of work in exchange for accepting standardised payment terms. This circular procurement model captures significant scale benefits in repairs and property restoration while maintaining strict ethical oversight across the supply chain.

Customer Dynamics and Retention Excellence

Tryg's retail-heavy customer base delivers highly predictable, annuity-like premiums anchored by the unique TryghedsGruppen bonus, with Private retail lines constituting 68% of insurance revenue serving over 6 million customers across Scandinavia, with no single private retail customer dominating the portfolio reducing top-1 and top-10 concentration to negligible levels, while Commercial segment represents 32% of group revenue.

Group customer retention rates hover around 90% across core Private and SME segments, with retention in Private Denmark, representing 30% of group revenue, successfully stabilizing after prolonged inflation-linked adjustments, while Swedish Trygg-Hansa is gaining momentum through 12 new motor partnerships adding over SEK 130m in sales. The TryghedsGruppen member bonus, which distributed DKK 1,050m in 2025 and will escalate to a 7% payout for 2026, creates an unmatched retention moat that lifts customer loyalty by double-digit percentage points.

Product-driven switching costs are anchored by products like pregnancy and child insurance which span decades, with Private customers who purchase 7 or more products remaining with Tryg for an average of 15.8 years, while pricing adjustments were mostly accepted by retail customers proving resilient brand loyalty, and customer retention held stable in Norway despite necessary mid-to-high teens premium increases to fight motor inflation.

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution

Tryg's operator quality is anchored by the execution of the United Towards 27 strategy targeting growing the insurance service result by DKK 1,000m from 2024 to 2027, relying on DKK 500m from scale, DKK 300m from technical excellence, and DKK 200m from commercial initiatives, with management targeting a group combined ratio of around 81% by 2027 and best-in-class return on own funds of 35% to 40%.

CEO Johan Kirstein Brammer leads strategy execution with extensive cross-border non-life integration expertise, while CFO Allan Kragh Thaysen has maintained commercially-focused cost discipline since 2018, the Supervisory Board ensures risk management remains firmly integrated across three lines of defence, Executive Board members must hold shares equal to 150% of their base salary within five years, and performance-based pay incorporates return on own funds targets alongside customer satisfaction metrics.

The €550mn TCS infrastructure partnership represents a transformative simplification initiative, consolidating legacy IT platforms and reducing IT development processes from 10 models down to 1, enabling an approximate 33% reduction in internal IT headcount by 2026 while freeing resources to develop next-generation customer-facing digital applications. Tryg's new data-driven underwriting platform improves combined ratios by 2% to 3% when applied, with adoption rising from 30% in 2024 to 45% in mid-2025, achieving full adoption in Norway, while a newly implemented AI model automates liability allocation in 85% of Danish two-car collision claims.

Disruption Risk and Long-Term Structural Resilience

Formidable entry barriers characterized by massive scale, direct distribution networks, and strong local brands make the Nordic non-life market highly insulated against new entrants, with heavy start-up customer acquisition costs and strict Solvency II requirements preventing greenfield competitors from scaling, while bypassing price comparison sites protects incumbents from price-walking dynamics, and Tryg's market-leading low expense ratio of 12.5% creates an impenetrable cost barrier for sub-scale players.

The Danish Supreme Court workers' compensation decision retroactively lowers the disability threshold from 15% to 5%, requiring Tryg to recognize a DKK 1.2bn pre-tax one-off charge in Q2 2026, although capital management actions offset the reserve charge restricting the net solvency hit to only 4 percentage points, leaving Tryg's long-term capital repatriation outlook intact. This manageable one-off regulatory shock demonstrates the resilience of Tryg's capital base and management's proactive risk mitigation capabilities.

Non-life premium growth historically develops in line with nominal GDP hovering in the low-to-mid single digits, with Tryg's PregLife pregnancy insurance achieving a 20% market share in Denmark and Norway serving as a low-cost lead generator for the highly profitable child insurance portfolio, while Tryg Denmark launched a simpler agricultural product in February 2026 to expand its SME market share, with management targeting organic growth of 4% to 7% over the strategy period without diluting underwriting quality.

Investment Paradigm Fit and Quality Characteristics

The primary structural risks confronting the investment thesis center on persistent claims inflation outpacing annual premium adjustments or regulatory prohibition of automated premium indexation practices, either of which could compress underwriting margins and force dilutive capital actions. Persistent premium rate hikes to combat claims inflation could push customer churn past the historical 10% threshold, diluting Tryg's competitive home-market brand advantage. However, the company's dominant market positions, structural cost advantages, unique TryghedsGruppen retention flywheel, and demonstrated ability to navigate regulatory challenges provide substantial resilience against these downside scenarios.
