Key Findings

  1. THEON is the fastest-growing listed European defence name. Revenue CAGR FY22-FY25 of ~46% dwarfs every peer in the comparison set. Rheinmetall is the closest large-cap at ~29% FY25 growth, but off a far larger base. THEON grew 53% in FY23, 61% in FY24, and 26% in FY25.

  2. Order intake inflected to a different magnitude in FY25. EUR 1,314m order intake vs EUR 466m in FY24 (+182%). Book-to-bill of ~3.0x on FY25 revenue. Backlog of EUR 1.4bn entering FY26 provides ~2.5x coverage of FY26 revenue guidance. Soft backlog extends visibility to 2029 for the first time.

  3. Margins are best-in-class and stable-to-expanding. Adjusted EBIT margin ran 26.0% in FY23, 25.0% in FY24, and 26.2% in FY25. This is materially above Hensoldt (18.4%), Rheinmetall (18.5%), Leonardo (9.0%), BAE (10.8%), and Elbit (~9.3% EBITDA). Only Safran's recurring operating margin (16.6%) is in the same conversation, and THEON still leads by ~10pp.

  4. FY26 guidance implies continued strong momentum. Revenue guidance of EUR 570-600m (+30% YoY, of which 20%+ organic, remainder from Kappa Optronics consolidation). Mid-20s% EBIT margin maintained. This is the strongest organic growth guidance in the peer set.

  5. Consensus is supportive with limited coverage. 7 analysts covering, 6 Buy / 1 Hold. Average target ~EUR 37-40. Forward P/E ~25x (FY26e). Recent EPS surprise of +48% suggests consensus has been too conservative.

  6. Valuation is elevated but supported by growth. EV/EBITDA ~21-25x depending on source and date. P/E ~25-31x. This is a premium to Leonardo (13.6x EV/EBITDA) and BAE (16-19x), in line with Safran (~21x), and cheaper than Hensoldt (~25x) and Rheinmetall (~31x) -- both of which grow slower than THEON.

  7. Balance sheet has been de-risked. EUR 150m rights issue (Dec-25) plus EUR 300m revolving credit facility (Oct-25). Pro-forma net debt/EBITDA ~1.8x, well below the <2.5x target. Working capital absorption declining. No red flags.

  8. The key risk is execution at scale. THEON is transitioning from a EUR 143m revenue business (FY22) to a EUR 570-600m business (FY26e) in four years. That is a 4x scaling. Margin resilience through this ramp is the critical test. So far, no margin slippage.

Several observations stand out:

Synthesis and Judgement

THEON International presents an unusual combination: the fastest organic revenue growth in European defence, the highest operating margins, the highest book-to-bill ratio, and a valuation that is competitive with or cheaper than peers on a growth-adjusted basis.

Bull case: THEON is a structural winner from NATO night-vision modernisation. The EUR 1.3bn order intake in FY25 is not a one-off -- it reflects Germany and other NATO members committing to multi-year night-vision programmes. Margins hold because the product is high-IP, niche, and sticky. At 25x forward P/E with 25-30% growth, the stock is mispriced. Fair value: EUR 40-45.

Bear case: Growth decelerates sharply as the OCCAR order is digested. Margin compression occurs as the company scales and integrates Kappa Optronics. The share dilution from rights issues and placements means EPS growth lags revenue growth. Limited liquidity (small-cap) creates volatility risk. Fair value: EUR 25-28.

Base case: Revenue and margins track guidance. Consensus upgrades 10-15% over the next 6 months as the Annual Report provides full detail. Multiple re-rates modestly higher as the market recognises THEON's PEG advantage. Fair value: EUR 36-40.

Key items to watch from the Annual Report (20-Apr-26) and webcast (21-Apr-26):
1. Free cash flow -- the missing piece. Does FCF conversion support the growth trajectory?
2. Net income and EPS detail -- confirms or challenges the P/E-based valuation
3. Kappa Optronics integration timeline and expected margin impact
4. FY26 order intake outlook -- can book-to-bill remain above 1.5x?
5. Gross margin disclosure -- would help assess the durability of EBIT margins
6. Working capital dynamics -- the "normalisation" needs to be quantified
7. Geographic revenue split -- how concentrated is the Germany/OCCAR dependency?
