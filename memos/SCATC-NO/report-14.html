§3 — Key Driver: EBITDA-to-FCF Conversion / Cash Generation Quality

Verdict: Medium plausibility — EBITDA growth has not consistently translated to proportional corporate FCF; cash conversion quality is the most underappreciated risk in the driver set [#J]

Driver definition:
- Driver definition: Scatec's EBITDA must convert to distributable cash at corporate level, net of project-level debt service, minority distributions, and maintenance capex, at a rate sufficient to fund growth equity and service corporate debt [#A]
- Magnitude required: A corporate-level FCF yield of ~5-7% on market cap (~NOK 20-25bn) is required to demonstrate self-funding growth; currently FCF yield is believed to be ~2-3% or below based on public disclosures [#A]
- Baseline: Scatec reported EBITDA of ~$450-500m in 2023-24 (proportional) but corporate-level recurring FCF has been materially lower due to project-level debt service absorbing ~60-70% of project EBITDA [#A]
- Target state: Corporate-level recurring FCF improving to ~$150-200m per year equivalent (from ~$80-100m implied) within 18-24 months is the investment-case requirement [#J]
- Required trajectory: FCF improvement of ~$60-100m over 18-24 months requires combination of (a) completed project refinancing at lower rates, (b) commissioning of higher-EBITDA-margin projects, (c) working capital normalisation [#A]

Bull case:
- Bull — historical: Scatec's project-level EBITDA margins have been structurally high (~55-65%) once projects are operating; the pipeline of projects transitioning from construction to operation in 2025-26 should mechanically lift recurring FCF [#A]
- Bull — recent: Interest rate expectations in core markets (Brazil, South Africa) have begun declining from 2023-24 peaks, which should ease refinancing costs on floating-rate project debt [#A]
- Bull — mgmt: Management has guided to "improved cash generation profile" from 2025 onward as project commissioning pipeline converts to operating assets; specific FCF targets not publicly disclosed [#A]
- Bull — initiatives: Scatec's shift toward larger projects (300+ MW) vs smaller legacy projects improves per-MW operating leverage and reduces fixed overhead per MW [#A]

Bear case:
- ❌ Bear — historical drag: Scatec has consistently guided to improving FCF conversion for 3-4 consecutive years without delivering at the guided trajectory; this is a pattern of optimistic guidance, not a single miss [#A]
- ❌ Bear — structural: Project finance structures in frontier markets routinely include cash sweeps, reserve account requirements, and DSRA top-ups that trap cash at project level for 3-5 years post-commissioning [#A]
- Bear — recent deterioration: Rising interest rates in 2022-24 materially increased project-level debt service costs on floating-rate portions; even if rates decline, the lag between rate moves and refinancing completion is 12-24 months [#J]
- Bear — execution: Working capital has been a persistent absorber of cash, particularly EPC receivables and guarantee deposits in markets with weak commercial legal frameworks [#A]
- ❌ Bear — structural: Minority partners (development finance institutions, local equity) in project companies often have priority distribution rights that further subordinate Scatec's proportional cash claim [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:
- Acceleration needed: FCF conversion must improve from ~20-25% of proportional EBITDA to ~30-35%; improvement of ~10pp in FCF conversion ratio over 18-24M is achievable but not certain [#J]
- Key dependency: Interest rate trajectory in Brazil (SELIC at ~10.5% post-peak cycle) and South Africa (repo rate ~8.25%) determines refinancing cost trajectory; no FCF improvement without rate normalisation [#A]
- Downside scenario: If FCF conversion remains stuck at current level, Scatec's corporate liquidity tightens further, forcing either dilutive equity issuance or asset sales at distressed pricing [#J]
- Probability — verbal: Medium plausibility — mechanical improvement from commissioning pipeline is real, but structural cash trapping at project level is a persistent countervailing force [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:
- Consensus alignment: DNB and ABG model material FCF improvement from 2025E onward, embedded in their price targets; conservative bear case (Pareto) discounts FCF improvement by ~18 months [#A]
- Estimate revision: FCF per-share estimates for 2025E have been revised down; consensus FCF is ~10-15% below management-guided trajectories as of Q1 2025 [#A]
- Evidence gap: Scatec does not publish a corporate-level FCF bridge that separates project-trapped cash from distributable cash; investors are forced to model this from incomplete disclosure [#A]

Evidence gaps:
- Evidence gap: Project-level cash waterfall disclosures are incomplete; DSRA balances and mandatory reserve account top-ups are not publicly quantified [#A]
- Evidence gap: Minority partner distribution priority terms are not disclosed in standardised format across the project portfolio [#A]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — FCF improvement from commissioning pipeline is mechanically real but offset by structural cash trapping; requires rate normalisation + refinancing execution to hit thesis levels. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: VALIDATES
