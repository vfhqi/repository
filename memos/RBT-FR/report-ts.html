Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Robertet is the undisputed global king of natural ingredients, leveraging a vertically integrated "seed-to-scent" model that creates immense customer stickiness and high barriers to entry.

Segment mix and revenue streams

1.
Balanced segment contribution: Revenue is divided among four divisions, with Fragrances representing 37.00% of FY2025 sales, Flavors 35.00%, Raw Materials 25.00%, and Health & Beauty 3.00%
2.
Raw Materials core: The Raw Materials division serves as the historical heart of the business, sourcing 1,600 natural products to support both external B2B sales and internal formulation
3.
Fragrance division focus: The Fragrances division specializes in creating olfactory signatures for fine fragrances, personal care, cosmetics, and household products
4.
Flavors division focus: The Flavors division develops taste formulations primarily for beverages, dairy, confectionery, and pharmaceutical applications, utilizing 70.00% to 80.00% natural inputs
5.
Transactional B2B model: Revenue is primarily transactional and formulation-specific, generated by selling customized ingredients that customers incorporate into finished consumer goods

Value chain and competitive advantage

6.
Integrated sourcing model: Robertet's "seed-to-scent" model controls the agricultural sourcing, extraction, and final blending, providing full traceability
7.
High switching costs: Custom formulations create high switching costs because fragrance and flavor profiles are integral to brand identity and difficult to substitute
8.
Sourcing network moat: The company manages 55 certified supply chains and sources botanical ingredients from over 60 countries, establishing a supply security moat
9.
Proprietary extraction technology: Scale-backed expertise in advanced extraction techniques, such as supercritical carbon dioxide and fractioning, ensures superior olfactory quality
10.
Storytelling brand advantage: Market-leading backward integration enables luxury consumer brands to leverage natural ingredient origins in their marketing narratives

Customer profile and industry dynamics

11.

12.
Boutique player appeal: Proximity to local and regional brands, which account for 56.00% of sales, provides faster growth than slow-growing global multinationals
13.
Moderate industry concentration: The global flavor and fragrance industry is moderately concentrated with an Herfindahl-Hirschman Index of 1,617, leaving room for further consolidation
14.
Niche market expansion: Robertet ranks seventh globally in the overall sector but remains the undisputed leader in natural raw materials niche
15.
Other side of the story: Intense competition from consolidating large-cap players, who are aggressively expanding their natural portfolios, threatens Robertet's market share if sourcing costs escalate out of hand
