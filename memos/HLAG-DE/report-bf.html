Customer and Supplier Power Dynamics

Hapag-Lloyd serves 27,400 customers globally with the fifty largest accounting for less than 50% of volume , while long-term contract share increased to 55% providing capacity guarantees, though average freight rates declined 9.5% year-on-year to USD 1,330 per TEU in Q1 2026. The company achieved a record Net Promoter Score of 69 in FY2025 , supported by 90% Gemini schedule reliability and over 90% of the 2 million TEU container fleet equipped with GPS tracking , creating operational switching barriers through WiseTech CargoWise integration and HERE Tracking AI-driven inland ETA predictions . Marine fuel costs represent 14% of the cost base with no active fuel hedges , though Marine Fuel Recovery formulas pass through bunker inflation with a 4 to 6-week lag , while EU ETS compliance costs surged to EUR 50.8 million in Q1 2026 and War Risk Surcharges reached USD 1,500 per TEU for standard containers .

Operator Quality and Structural Threats

CEO Rolf Habben Jansen has directed operations since July 2014 with contract extension through March 2029 , executing Strategy 2030 targeting 20% unit cost reduction through the Ventus program delivering over USD 1 billion in savings by end-2026 , while conservative guidance consistently settles near upper forecast limits and past CSAV and UASC integrations achieved synergies ahead of schedule . The company faces formidable decarbonization costs with 24 LNG dual-fuel vessels ordered for USD 4 billion and five methanol retrofit conversions costing USD 120 million , secured by a 15-year green methanol contract for 250,000 tonnes annually , though IMO postponed final Net-Zero Framework votes to October 2026 creating regulatory uncertainty. Critical structural headwinds include massive global fleet oversupply at 35.5% orderbook-to-fleet ratio threatening rates below cash break-even of approximately USD 1,100 per TEU , rising protectionism with

proposed US Section 301 tariffs disrupting trans-pacific lanes, ongoing Red Sea rerouting forcing Cape of Good Hope voyages inflating vessel opex, and net working capital days of 39.5 exceeding ONE's 31.4 days restricting cash conversion during downcycles.
