Purpose is attribution-scoring, not narrative. The seven outputs exist so the APM can score every Live and Short List stock A–F against each active thematic. The research only has value if attributes are matchable — specific enough that a stock either has the characteristic or it does not.

Dual-source synthesis rule. Query 23 requires both a [C] sub-agent (broad public analysis, macro framing, historical parallels) and an [AS] sub-agent (proprietary broker research, earnings call commentary, European margin sensitivity data). "Contractual price escalators indexed to CPI, renegotiated annually, flowing directly to gross margin at ~60–70% conversion to EBIT" is an attribute.

Scoring thresholds used by the APM:
- 3+ beneficiary attributes matched → thematic tailwind → A–B rating → influences sizing up, entry timing, pipeline prioritisation
- 3+ at-risk attributes matched → thematic headwind → E–F rating → influences sizing down, exit urgency, avoidance

European grounding rule. All company examples must be European-listed, £5–50bn market cap sweet spot. Global context for framing; European for action. US examples only where no European equivalent exists, flagged explicitly.

(50–100 words)

Required content:

- Time horizon: 6-month / 1-year / multi-year — commit to one

Quality standard: If this section does not name a specific earnings channel (revenue, gross margin, EBIT, working capital, cost of debt), it has failed.

2. BENEFICIARY SUMMARY

(30–60 words)

Required content: One elevator pitch. Name the type of company that benefits. State the mechanism in one sentence. State the magnitude of potential EPS uplift or re-rating where estimable.

Quality standard: A PM reading this in 10 seconds should know which sector/sub-sector to screen first.

5. BENEFICIARY PROBABLE SETUPS

(200–400 words)

Required content:

Entry profile
- Valuation at entry: what multiple, what discount/premium to sector, why this level creates asymmetry
- Momentum condition: where in the earnings revision cycle does the thematic typically first appear in numbers
- Earnings trajectory: what does the next 2–3 quarter EPS path look like for an ideal entry

Catalysts that crystallise the thesis
- List 3–5 specific events (earnings beat + guidance raise, regulatory approval, contract announcement, commodity price move, macro data release) that convert thematic potential to realised return

Risks even for beneficiaries

- Valuation stretch: what multiple signals crowding
- Execution risk: where do beneficiaries historically disappoint despite having the right attributes

7. AT-RISK PROBABLE SETUPS

(200–400 words)

Required content:

Typical at-risk stock profile at time of exposure
- Earnings vulnerability: what consensus assumption gets challenged first
- Valuation assumption embedded in current price that the thematic invalidates
- Balance sheet stress: leverage levels, refinancing timelines, covenant proximity

Triggers that crystallise the at-risk thesis
- List 3–5 specific events (earnings miss, guidance cut, margin compression disclosure, regulatory adverse ruling, commodity price threshold breach)
- Timeline for each trigger

Temporary vs structural distinction — the critical test
- Temporary at-risk: earnings hit is quantifiable, management has a credible response, business model survives
- Structural at-risk: the thematic permanently impairs the earnings base — substitution, stranded assets, regulatory closure
- Flag which European industries are most likely to face structural (not temporary) impairment under this thematic

Setup mapping (where natural):

- Multiple compression
- Liquidity trap
