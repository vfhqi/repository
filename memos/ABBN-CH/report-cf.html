EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

ABB has undergone a profound transformation under the decentralized ABB Way operating model , marked by the August 2024 CEO transition from Björn Rosengren to Morten Wierod , a 25-year ABB veteran who previously drove margin turnarounds in Motion and Electrification . The leadership continuity extends through the planned February 2026 CFO succession , with Christian Nilsson replacing Timo Ihamuotila after delivering an 850bps EBITA margin increase in Electrification. While this internal promotion ensures strategic alignment with the ABB Way's continuous improvement mandate, the simultaneous turnover of CEO, CFO, and multiple divisional presidents introduces compounding execution risk at a moment when the company is aggressively pushing accountability down from 19 divisions to approximately 75 business lines .

The strategic architecture has evolved dramatically, with management deepening the ABB Way to the business- line level and pursuing aggressive M&A ambitions targeting $500m-$1bn deals to add 1-2% of revenues annually through acquisitions . The landmark $5.375 billion divestiture of the Robotics division to SoftBank , closing in H2 2026, represents the culmination of a multi-year portfolio pruning strategy that removed assets

lacking meaningful synergies with the core electrification and automation businesses. This transaction, combined with leverage near 0.5x ND/EBITDA , provides ABB with roughly $18 billion in M&A capacity and fuels an expanded $2.0 billion share buyback programme launched in February 2026. The strategic shift toward larger acquisitions, however, marks a material departure from ABB's historically conservative bolt-on approach ,

External demand dynamics are creating a bifurcated industrial landscape , with hyper-growth in AI data centers and grid modernization driving triple-digit organic order growth in Electrification while traditional short-cycle industrial markets remain muted . The rapid shift to 800V DC architectures and medium-voltage 34.5kV systems positions ABB at the epicenter of a structural data center super-cycle , with the segment now representing 12% of group revenues and expected to grow above 10% annually . Simultaneously, utility and grid equipment spending is forecast to grow at 6% into 2026 , decoupling from broader GDP trends. ABB's local-for- local manufacturing strategy , producing 75-80% of US goods domestically , effectively insulates the company from US tariff impacts limited to the tens of millions. However, extended transformer lead times of 12 to 36 months and copper and silver inflation currently outpacing pricing create near-term margin pressure , particularly in Electrification where pricing lags input costs. The financial profile transformation stands as ABB's defining achievement, with operational EBITA margins expanding from 11% in 2019 to 19.1% in Q1 2026 , a 1,000bps expansion that closes the historical profitability gap with Schneider and Legrand . The Electrification division delivered an exceptional 24.0% margin in Q1 2026 , with management guiding toward a best-in-class 22-26% through-cycle range . US Electrification operations still offer nearly 200bps of upside potential as margins catch up to European levels. Capital efficiency metrics screen best-in-class , with Q1 2026 ROCE hitting 27.2% and operational ROCE running at approximately 50% excluding goodwill and PPA, materially exceeding the company's historical mid-teens baseline . Free cash flow conversion remains robust between 12-14% of revenues, enabling the company to deploy capital aggressively while maintaining balance sheet strength. The premium to peers is stark, with ABB trading at 21x 2027 EV/EBITA versus Siemens at 15.7x and Schneider at 16.9x , reflecting the market's confidence in sustained execution.

ABB's historical earnings trajectory over the last three years demonstrates a clear honest-tracker forecasting archetype , with consistent beats on revenue, orders, and operational EBITA across reporting periods. The company delivered Q1 2026 revenues of $8.7bn beating consensus by 4% and operational EBITA beating by 5% , while orders reached $11.3bn representing a 16% beat driven by 24% comparable organic growth . The Electrification division's book-to-bill ratio surged to 1.44x on 44% organic order growth , demonstrating unprecedented demand strength. Management successfully executed a flawless beat-and-raise pattern , upgrading FY26 comparable revenue growth guidance to high-single to low-double-digit from the prior 6-9% range, and raising full-year operational EBITA margin guidance to a slight increase . The track record includes occasional one-offs, such as a $377 million Q1 2026 real estate gain and persistent E-mobility losses of $47 million , but underlying business performance remains structurally sound with 70bps year-on-year margin expansion excluding non-recurring items. ABB's peer-relative earnings momentum ranks in the top tier alongside Schneider and Eaton , with the company delivering 11% organic growth in Q1 versus the 5% European capital goods sector median . The 23.5% operational EBITA margin materially surpasses Siemens Smart Infrastructure at 18.6% and positions ABB as a

clear margin leader among diversified industrial peers. However, this operational outperformance is fully reflected in valuation , with ABB commanding a demanding 35-40% premium to the sector average at 21x 2027 EV/EBITA . Schneider Electric matched ABB's 11.2% organic growth and maintains a more balanced 16.9x multiple , while Eaton's 13% organic growth and 30% year-on-year backlog increase in electrical drove its forward P/E to a peak 28.5x . ABB's revenue growth momentum and margin trajectory position the company favorably , but the premium valuation leaves limited room for further multiple expansion absent sustained fundamental delivery exceeding already elevated expectations.
