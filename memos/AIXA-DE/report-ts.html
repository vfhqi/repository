Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. AIXTRON dominates the global compound semiconductor deposition market with a near-monopoly in GaN and InP MOCVD tools, protected by substantial IP and switching costs.

Core Product Segments and Revenue Model

1.
Specialized product suite: AIXTRON's G10 product family consists of three specialized platforms, the G10-SiC for silicon carbide power devices, the G10-GaN for gallium nitride power electronics, and the G10-AsP for arsenide and phosphide optoelectronic applications
2.
Accretive aftersales model: Deposition equipment sales generated €444.60m or 80.00% of FY2025 revenues, while spares and services (aftersales) contributed €112.00m or 20.00%, representing a stable, high-margin, and highly recurring revenue stream
3.
Shifting application mix: The optoelectronics and communications segment has emerged as the largest near-term demand driver, accounting for 52.00% of equipment sales in Q1 2026, followed by LEDs and Micro LEDs at 31.00%, and GaN/SiC power at 17.00%
4.
Fragmented customer landscape: AIXTRON's customers are divided into vertically integrated device manufacturers (IDMs), independent foundry partners, and leading-edge university research labs that promote compound semiconductor material science
5.
Concentrated geographic distribution: Compound semiconductor manufacturing is heavily concentrated in Asian hubs, resulting in more than 64.00% of equipment shipments being delivered to customers in Asia during Q1 2026, while Europe represented 27.00% and the Americas represented 9.00%
6.
Superior value position: By operating as a specialized sole-source hardware supplier at the very bottleneck of the supply chain, AIXTRON commands superior gross margins of 40.00% in FY2025 despite low factory capacity utilization

Technology Barriers and Competitive Advantage

7.
Dominant market share: AIXTRON maintains an undisputed global market leadership, commanding a 77.00% market share in the MOCVD (GaN/LED/Opto) equipment market as of 2024, compared to Veeco's 8.00% and AMEC's 4.00%
8.
Challenging silicon carbide: In the highly competitive SiC market, AIXTRON is a leading challenger to incumbents, holding a 27.00% market share compared to ASM International's 32.00% and Tokyo Electron's 16.00% as of 2024
9.
Proprietary technical moat: The company's competitive advantage is anchored by its proprietary Close Coupled Showerhead (CCS) and planetary reactor technologies, protected by a deep library of registered trademarks and intensive university R&D partnerships
10.
High customer stickiness: Switching costs are massive for chip manufacturers, as recipes developed on AIXTRON tools cannot be easily transferred to other platforms without risking months of yield degradation and re-qualification
11.
Compelling cost advantages: AIXTRON's planetary batch tools provide a double-digit cost-of-ownership advantage to customers compared to single-wafer alternatives, driven by superior throughput and chemical utilization
12.
Latent competitive threat: Large silicon semiconductor equipment giants like Applied Materials possess the financial resources to develop competing GaN and SiC deposition tools, representing a potential long-term threat that could erode AIXTRON's niche competitive position

Section 5 – The possible investment case 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is highly compelling, driven by a multi-year AI optical capex cycle and GaN power adoption that will drive massive revenue growth and margin expansion.

Secular Growth Drivers and Plausibility

1.
Explosive optical demand: AI data center scale-out is driving a massive transition from copper to fiber optics, triggering exponential demand for InP-based edge-emitting lasers
2.
High optical plausibility: This driver is highly plausible and backed by actual multi-tool orders from Lumentum and Coherent following NVIDIA's multi-billion-dollar supply chain investments
3.
Potential optical digestion: The primary failure mechanism is any near-term digestion period or capacity over-ordering by transceiver manufacturers, which would cause tool shipments to soften in late 2027
4.
Accelerating power efficiency: AI data centers are transitioning to next-generation power supply units (PSUs) utilizing GaN to achieve up to 5.00% end-to-end power efficiency improvements
5.
Strong insourcing trend: This driver is highly plausible, reinforced by ROHM Semiconductor's strategic decision to bring GaN epitaxy in-house on AIXTRON's G10-GaN platform
6.
Delayed power inflection: The mechanism of failure is a slower-than-expected adoption of GaN power electronics, which has already pushed major customer volume inflections out into late 2027

Strategic Capacity Expansion and Bull Case

7.
Intact automotive transition: Despite the cyclical downturn in electric vehicle demand, the long-term shift from 400.00V to 800.00V battery systems using SiC inverters remains intact
8.
Muted near-term recovery: This driver has medium near-term plausibility, with Western fab utilization currently stuck at 50.00%, meaning volume orders may not inflect until H2 2026 or 2027
9.
Strategic capacity expansion: AIXTRON is investing €40.00m to build a new greenfield production facility in Penang, Malaysia, which will increase manufacturing capacity by 20.00% to support up to €1.20bn in revenues by 2027
10.
Compelling bull path: For the strategic Penang capacity target of €1.20bn in revenues and 46.00% gross margins to work, the optical and GaN growth drivers do not need to fire simultaneously, as the optical upcycle alone is sufficient to support near-term targets
11.
Non-datacenter GaN opportunities: Low-voltage applications like household appliances are expected to remain the dominant long-term driver of GaN, though adoption remains slow
12.
Transition year expectations: Deutsche Bank notes that while order momentum is highly encouraging, management is consciously keeping expectations in check as FY2026 remains a transition year on the P&L
13.
Underabsorbed fixed costs: If the optoelectronics capex cycle turns out to be short-lived, like the face ID VCSEL wave in 2017, AIXTRON could face severe overcapacity and underabsorbed fixed costs on its new Malaysian and Italian sites

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by geopolitical tensions and customer concentration, but the company's strong technological lead and financial flexibility mitigate potential downsides.

Geopolitical and Concentration Risks

1.
Severe export exposure: AIXTRON is highly exposed to US/China trade tensions, as any tightening of export control restrictions could block tool shipments to China, which has historically been a major source of demand
2.
Lumpy purchase decisions: Decisive purchasing decisions in the semiconductor equipment industry are highly lumpy and concentrated, exposing AIXTRON to severe revenue volatility if a key tier-1 customer delays capacity expansion
3.
Negligible short interest: Research did not surface any published short-seller reports or unusually high short interest on AIXTRON, indicating strong institutional confidence in its business model
4.
Disruptive technical failure: The single failure mode that would most damage the investment case is a technological shift where major customers transition to 300.00mm GaN wafers and prefer competitor Veeco's single-wafer platform over AIXTRON's platform

Technical and Operational Risks

5.
Misleading backlog signals: A rapidly growing order backlog could act as a false friend, masking underlying customer push-outs and delayed delivery acceptances that keep the P&L depressed
6.
Critical utilization metrics: The single early-warning metric to monitor is the progression of industry-wide factory capacity utilization rates, where a drop below 60.00% would signal prolonged inventory digestion
7.
Unreplicated process advantages: Bear concerns regarding local Chinese competition are overstated, as AIXTRON's sophisticated multi-wafer process uniformity cannot be easily replicated by Chinese entrants like AMEC
8.
Other side of the story: Favorable cost-of-ownership dynamics allow AIXTRON to protect its dominant market share, as its batch tool chemistry achieves superior yields and lower cost-per-wafer compared to competitors
