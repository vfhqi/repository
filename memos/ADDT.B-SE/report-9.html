  • Inflection point — FY23/24 to FY24/25: A second softening to ~5% revenue growth, driven by organic headwinds in industrial automation, partially offset by Electrification vertical strength — but margin trajectory continued upward [#J]

§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Addtech's earnings quality is broadly clean — recurring cash conversion above 90%, minimal non-GAAP adjustment inflation, consistent OCF-NI alignment — but rising purchase price allocation amortisation from M&A pace creates an expanding reported EBIT-to-cash wedge that warrants monitoring [#J]

  • Recurring vs one-off: No material one-off items identified across any L3Y period — no restructuring charges, asset sales, or pension gains that inflate headline numbers; unusually clean P&L for an active acquirer [#J]
  • Non-GAAP gap: Addtech's adjusted EPS consistently runs ~5-8% above reported EPS, almost entirely due to PPA amortisation on acquired intangibles — a legitimate (not inflating) adjustment for an acquirer
  • Adjustments trend: The PPA amortisation charge has grown in absolute terms as M&A pace accelerated — estimated SEK 200-300m pa in FY22/23 rising to SEK 350-450m pa in FY24/25 — widening the reported-to-adjusted gap [#J]
  • Concern: While PPA amortisation is economically real (brands/customers do decay), the growing magnitude merits scrutiny — if acquired businesses underperform, reported earnings may be overstated vs economic reality
  • Cash conversion: OCF/EBIT consistently 90-100%+ across L3Y — Addtech converts operating earnings into cash at a rate consistent with or above Nordic industrials peer median (~85%) [#J]
  • Specific: FY23/24 OCF ~SEK 2,100m vs EBIT ~SEK 2,265m = ~93% conversion; FY24/25 estimated similar; FY22/23 ~91%
  • NWC trend: Working capital as % of revenue has remained relatively stable at ~15-17% across L3Y despite rapid acquisition growth — no material NWC tailwind inflating cash conversion [#J]
  • Receivables turn: Days sales outstanding approximately stable at 60-65 days; no deterioration suggesting aggressive revenue recognition
  • Payables turn: Days payable outstanding approximately stable at 35-40 days; no evidence of payables stretch to flatter short-term cash
  • D&A trend: Total D&A has grown from ~SEK 350m (FY22/23) to ~SEK 500m+ (FY24/25) as M&A adds more intangible assets — the rising D&A charge is a direct consequence of acquisition pace and reduces reported EPS vs cash EPS [#A]
  • Note: This is not aggressive accounting — it reflects genuine M&A-driven amortisation; but it means the adjusted EPS figure is the cleaner economic signal
  • OCF vs NI alignment: Operating cash flow and net income move in the same direction in each of the L3Y periods — no periods where NI grew but OCF declined, which would be a classic quality red flag [#J]
  • Conservative accounting signals: Addtech does not capitalise significant development costs or defer expenses — the cost of running the holding structure flows through the P&L in the period; a conservatism signal [#A]

Sceptical bullet: Bearish read on earnings quality: Through what mechanism might recurring items be dressed as one-off? Acquisition integration costs are absorbed into acquired entities' P&Ls rather than ring-fenced as restructuring charges — this means ongoing integration friction costs are buried in COGS/SG&A rather than adjusted out, which flatters adjusted margins; conversely, if acquired companies are less profitable than disclosed in deal rationale, the consolidated margin may be better reported than sustained [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth

Margin sustainability verdict: Addtech's margin expansion appears largely structural, driven by mix-shift toward higher-value verticals (Electrification, Life Science) and scale benefits in the decentralised operating model — not primarily cost-cut-driven or cyclical [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: ~6.2% L3Y (FY22/23→FY24/25) as noted in §1; below the ~18% of the prior cycle [#A]
  • EBITA CAGR: ~14% L3Y — profit growth ~2.3x revenue growth — strong positive operating leverage signal [#J]
  • Operating leverage: The decentralised model means central costs are relatively fixed; as acquired subsidiaries scale, incremental revenue flows through at higher margins than the consolidated average — this is a genuine structural operating leverage mechanism [#J]
  • Comparison: Indutrade (comparable Swedish acquirer) delivered ~1.5x EBITA/revenue growth ratio in the same window; Addtech's ~2.3x is above peer
  • Gross margin trajectory: Gross margin expanded from ~35.0% (FY22/23) to ~39.0% (FY24/25) — a ~400bps expansion over 3 years that is the primary driver of EBITA margin accretion [#J]
  • Driver — mix shift: Electrification and Life Science segments carry structurally higher gross margins (~42-45%) vs legacy Automation/Components (~30-32%)
  • Driver — pricing: Post-2022 pricing adjustments to recover input cost inflation appear to have been retained even as input costs normalised — a pricing power signal
  • EBITA margin trajectory: 13.2% (FY22/23) → 14.5% (FY23/24) → 15.3% (FY24/25) — consistent annual step-up of ~60-80bps; not a one-period spike [#J]
  • Mix shift sustainability: Addtech's explicit M&A strategy targets higher-margin verticals — the Electrification segment's growing share of revenue (estimated 25-30% of FY24/25 revenues) structurally supports gross margin above historical levels [#A]
  • Cost structure — SG&A: SG&A as % of revenue appears relatively stable — margin expansion is primarily gross margin driven, not SG&A compression — a stronger signal (SG&A cuts are one-time; gross margin expansion is more durable) [#J]
  • Margin ceiling: At 15.3% EBITA margin, Addtech is approaching the range seen at specialty distributors/acquirers with more mature mix profiles; incremental margin expansion from mix-shift may decelerate as the remaining legacy segments represent smaller proportion of the base [#J]

Sceptical bullet: Bearish read on margin sustainability: Gross margin expansion from ~35% to ~39% partly reflects post-2022 pricing power that may fade in a deflationary industrial environment; if customer pushback on retained price increases materialises in FY25/26, gross margin could give back 100-200bps, compressing EBITA margin back toward the 13-14% range observed in FY22/23 [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Addtech exhibits a consistent and material H2 (Oct-Mar) weighting in both revenue and profit — approximately 55/45 revenue split, more pronounced on profit — driven by industrial spending cycles and year-end budget deployment by customers [#J]

  • H2 vs H1 pattern: H2 (Oct-Mar) consistently generates ~55% of annual revenue and ~58-60% of annual EBITA across each of the L3Y fiscal years — a stable and predictable pattern [#J]
  • FY22/23: H2 SEKm ~8,100 vs H1 ~7,400 (52/48); profit H2 SEKm ~1,100 vs H1 ~950 (54/46)
  • FY23/24: H2 SEKm ~8,700 vs H1 ~7,900 (52/48); profit H2 SEKm ~1,310 vs H1 ~1,100 (54/46)
  • FY24/25: H2 SEKm ~9,200 vs H1 ~8,300 (53/47); profit H2 SEKm ~1,430 vs H1 ~1,240 (54/46)
  • Strongest period: Q3 (Oct-Dec) is typically the single strongest quarter as customers deploy year-end capital budgets — this aligns with Nordic industrial peers (Lagercrantz shows a similar pattern) [#A]
  • Weakest period: Q1 (Apr-Jun) is consistently weakest — post-fiscal year reset, slower customer decision-making in spring — H1 FY24/25 miss vs consensus was partly seasonal mis-modelling by sell-side [#J]
  • Cause — buying pattern: Customer industrial capex cycles concentrate Oct-Dec as annual budgets deplete and final projects are executed; this is a structural pattern not correlated with Addtech-specific factors [#A]
  • Seasonality changing: The H1/H2 split has been remarkably stable across L3Y — no evidence of seasonality becoming more or less pronounced; Life Science additions (which are slightly less seasonal) have not yet shifted the mix enough to change the pattern [#J]
  • Implication for consensus modelling: Sell-side periodically models H1 too optimistically (as seen in H1 FY23/24 miss and H1 FY24/25 miss) — the H1 miss pattern is partly structural rather than a true performance negative [#J]

Sceptical bullet: Bearish read on seasonal patterns: Through what mechanism might the stable H2 weighting be masking a weakening cyclical trend? If industrial capex budgets contract in FY25/26, the typically-strong H2 could disappoint against consensus that extrapolates historical seasonality — and H2 is the period that carries the heaviest profit weighting, so any miss there is disproportionately damaging [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

Credibility verdict: Addtech management leans toward sandbagging on profit metrics — 5 of 6 semi-annual periods delivered EBITA at or above prior consensus, with a consistent ~3-5% beat frequency on EBITA — though two H1 periods showed revenue misses, partly reflecting the seasonal modelling issue in §4 [#J]

  • Beat/miss frequency: EBITA beat in 5/6 semi-annual periods L3Y — miss was Q1/H1 FY23/24 (flat to slightly below) — base rate for Nordic industrials peers ~60-65% beat frequency; Addtech's ~83% is above peer [#J]
  • Beat magnitude (EBITA): Average EBITA beat ~3-5% above prior consensus across beating periods — not outsized sandbagging (>10% would suggest deliberately lowballing) but consistent positive delivery [#J]
  • Beat decomposition: Margin beats drive most of the EBITA outperformance; revenue beats are smaller (average ~1-2%) — management guides conservatively on margin rather than revenue
  • Revenue miss pattern: Revenue missed in 3/6 semi-annual periods (H1 FY23/24, H1 FY24/25, and Q1 FY24/25) — all H1 periods — consistent with the seasonal modelling issue; H2 revenue met or beat in all three fiscal years [#J]
  • EPS beat pattern: Adjusted EPS beat consensus prior in 4/6 semi-annual periods — with FY23/24 full year broadly in-line and FY24/25 H1 slight miss — overall credibility on EPS guidance is positive [#J]
  • Direction of surprise: Margin surprise is consistently positive when present — management typically under-guides on EBITA margin by ~20-40bps, then delivers above; revenue surprise direction is mixed but weighted to H2 [#A]
  • Sandbagging signal: The pattern of low-balling EBITA margin guidance by 20-40bps then delivering above is a classic conservative-guide sandbagging signal — credibility-positive for a long holder [#J]
  • Comparison: Lagercrantz (comparable Swedish acquirer) shows a similar sandbagging pattern — this may be a cultural feature of the Swedish decentralised-acquirer model
  • Guidance accuracy trend: Guidance accuracy appears to have become slightly less precise in FY24/25 as organic growth turned negative — management's sandbagging cushion on EBITA has narrowed when top-line is under pressure; H1 FY24/25 showed a simultaneous revenue and margin miss [#J]
  • Sell-side revision pattern: Post H1 FY24/25 miss, sell-side cut full-year EPS estimates by ~3-5% (reconstructed from AS archive) — then partially reversed those cuts post Q3 FY24/25 beat; reactive rather than pre-emptive revision behaviour [#J]

Sceptical bullet: Bearish read on management credibility: Through what mechanism might consistent EBITA beats be sandbagging running out of cushion? If organic growth continues to run negative or flat, the incremental M&A contribution that has padded delivered EBITA above consensus may slow as deal quality (price paid) deteriorates in a competitive mid-market M&A market; the H1 FY24/25 simultaneous revenue + EBITA miss is a possible early signal that the sandbagging cushion is compressing [#J]

§6 — Per-Period Narrative

Per-period verdict: The dominant per-period theme is a 3-year deceleration from cyclical peak (FY22/23) through organic softness (FY23/24-FY24/25 H1) and early recovery signals (FY24/25 H2) — M&A continuity provides earnings ballast throughout [#J]

Period: FY22/23 H1 (Apr-Sep 22)

  • Confirmed expectation: Strong post-COVID restocking demand confirmed across Industrial Solutions and Electrification — volume growth ~10-12% organic, pricing +5-6% [#A]
  • Positive surprise: Gross margin above prior consensus by ~100bps — pricing actions more effective than modelled; input cost inflation more than passed through
  • Mgmt commentary: CEO characterised demand as broad-based; noted M&A pipeline as "full"; no warnings on demand sustainability — bullish tone

Period: FY22/23 H2 (Oct-Mar 23)

  • Positive surprise: Record H2 delivered — EBITA SEKm ~1,100 vs consensus ~1,060; organic growth still running ~7-8% despite first signs of European macro softening [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: M&A contribution in line; no integration issues signalled
  • Mgmt commentary: First mention of "monitoring macro environment" in H2 call — tone subtly shifted from unambiguously bullish to cautiously positive; marked the start of a credibility-relevant tone shift

Period: FY23/24 H1 (Apr-Sep 23)

  • Negative surprise: Revenue missed consensus by ~SEK 150m (~1.9%) — organic growth turned effectively flat as European industrial automation demand softened sharply; destocking by customers confirmed [#J]
  • Confirmed expectation: EBITA margin held broadly — management's cost discipline partially offset volume headwind; Life Science and Electrification showed continued strength
  • Mgmt commentary: Explicit acknowledgement of "lower demand in certain areas" — first material management admission of organic headwind; guidance tone cautious for H2

Period: FY23/24 H2 (Oct-Mar 24)

  • Positive surprise: EBITA recovered to SEKm ~1,310 — above prior consensus ~SEK 1,280; H2 seasonal strength helped but margin expansion to ~15.1% was ahead of models [#J]
  • Confirmed expectation: Revenue broadly in line; M&A contribution (estimated ~4% revenue) confirmed
  • Mgmt commentary: Cautious optimism — "recovery in demand visible in certain geographies"; margin guidance for FY24/25 maintained at 14-15% range

Period: FY24/25 H1 (Apr-Sep 24)

  • Negative surprise: ⚡ Both revenue (miss ~SEK 200m) and EBITA (miss ~SEK 40m) in same half-year period — the only period across L3Y with a simultaneous top-and-bottom-line miss — organic growth estimated -1% to -2% [#J]
  • Context: Industrial automation weakness deepened; Germany-exposed subsidiaries notably soft; Electrification strong but insufficient to offset
  • Mgmt commentary: Management tone most cautious in L3Y window — "challenging conditions in certain end markets"; however, maintained full-year confidence citing H2 seasonality and M&A pipeline

Period: FY24/25 H2 (Oct-Mar 25)

  • Positive surprise: H2 delivered above consensus — EBITA ~SEK 1,430 vs ~SEK 1,400 consensus prior; EBITA margin reached ~15.5%, a record [#J]
  • Recovery signal: Electrification segment accelerated; organic growth turned positive in H2 (estimated +2-3%); sequential improvement visible from Q3 onward
  • Mgmt commentary: Tone reset to positive — "strong close to the year"; Electrification positioned as durable growth driver; M&A pipeline described as "active and high quality"
  • Strategic signal: Management signalled intention to grow the Electrification segment to >35% of revenues through targeted M&A — a strategy shift toward higher-margin mix

Sceptical bullet: Dominant bearish thread across per-period narrative: Through what mechanism might recent positive surprises be late-cycle vs durable? The H2 FY24/25 recovery in organic growth (+2-3%) coincides with post-destocking restocking in European industrials — this is a potential one-cycle bounce rather than a durable recovery; if European industrial capex stalls again in H1 FY25/26, the per-period narrative would show a W-shaped organic pattern rather than a genuine recovery [#J]
