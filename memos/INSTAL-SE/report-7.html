§2 Driver 1: Margin Recovery to 8% EBITA Target (Instalco 2.0)

J-front verdict [#J]: Early evidence is genuine — Q4 2025 EBITA margin 7.2%, Q1 2026 5.8% — but the 8% target requires sustained execution across 150+ subsidiaries in an uneven market; structural proof is not yet in hand.

  • Starting point [#I]: FY2024 adjusted EBITA margin 6.4%; FY2025 reported EBITA margin 5.9%; adjusted 6.4% — 140–160bps below the 8% long-term ambition.
  • Q4 2025 adjusted EBITA margin: 7.2% — first quarter above 7% since the downcycle began.

  • Q4 2025 EBITA growth: +39% YoY — most significant recovery signal to date.

  • Programme mechanics [#A]: Instalco 2.0 (launched autumn 2025) targets margin via three levers.

  • Lever 1: Stricter project selection — reject low-margin contracts, accept volume loss.
  • Lever 2: Accountability cascade — COO roles inserted under each country CEO (Sweden, Norway, Finland) to supervise operational execution.

  • Lever 3: Earlier deviation detection — group-level reporting now flags subsidiary underperformance at 4-week intervals rather than quarterly.

  • Structural context [#A]: The 150+ subsidiary model previously left margin governance to local managers; the 2023–2024 downturn exposed this as insufficient.

  • Subsidiaries with poor project selection autonomy drove the majority of margin compression.

  • Instalco 2.0 inserts group oversight without dismantling subsidiary autonomy — delicate balance.

  • SS consensus gap [#I]: DNB Carnegie models 2026e EBITA margin 7.0%, 2027e 7.6% — leaving a 40–240bps gap to the 8% target. Sell-side is explicitly not modelling a full recovery, pricing in structural underperformance.

  • Kepler Cheuvreux: target SEK 43, reiterated Hold — lowered from SEK 49.

  • Analyst consensus target: SEK 40–44 range; stock at SEK 38–39 (8-May-26).

  • Path to 8% [#A]: Three conditions required simultaneously.

  • Condition A: Service/maintenance mix continues rising (36% in Q1 2026, growing 6% YoY; higher margins than project work).
  • Condition B: Sweden segment exits project-mix drag (residential new-build weakness was the primary compressor).

  • Condition C: No new large write-downs or loss-making project completions (Northvolt precedent is live).

  • Comparable [#A]: Bravida (closest peer) delivered 5.9% EBITA margin on LTM basis at Q1 2026, operating in same markets. Instalco's 8% target is premium to Bravida's run-rate — historically achievable but requires margin-positive mix.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Seven consecutive quarters of EBITA miss built the credibility deficit; one good quarter (Q4 2025) does not repair it. The 8% target was set pre-downcycle; it may require market conditions that do not return for 2–3 years.

Plausibility verdict: Medium — Q4 2025 + Q1 2026 trajectory is real; SS consensus gap signals informed scepticism; full 8% by 2027e is a stretch unless Sweden recovers.

§3 Driver 2: Sweden Organic Recovery

J-front verdict [#J]: Sweden is the single highest-impact driver — ~65% of group revenues, worst margin performer in 2023–2025, most exposed to residential new-build cycle; a genuine recovery is beginning but remains fragile and rate-dependent.

  • Scale of the problem [#I]: Swedish segment adjusted EBITA missed consensus by 10% in Q3 2025, with -110bps margin compression YoY.
  • Group organic growth FY2024: -6.5% — driven overwhelmingly by Swedish new-build collapse.

  • Q4 2025 organic growth: +4.9% — first positive organic quarter since the downcycle.

  • Housing market context [#A]: Swedish housing starts collapsed 2023–2024; Boverket forecasts ~35,100 starts in 2026, up from ~28,300 in 2025 — roughly 24% recovery YoY.

  • Key policy driver: Swedish government easing mortgage rules from April 2026 (minimum deposit 15%→10%, reduced amortisation requirements).
  • Riksbank policy rate at 2.0% (Sept 2025); forecast 1.75% by end-2026 — supporting affordability.

  • Mortgage rates ~2.8–3.1% currently; forecast 2.6–2.8% by 2026.

  • Order backlog signal [#I]: Instalco order backlog grew 15% YoY to SEK 10.38bn in Q1 2026 — most reliable forward indicator of organic revenue trajectory.

  • Backlog growth was described as across all operating countries.

  • Service sales in Q1 2026: growing 6% YoY, now 36% of group revenues.

  • Mix shift as partial insulation [#A]: Sweden's residential new-build is the volatile element; service/maintenance (~36% mix) is sticky and growing.

  • Infrastructure, data centres, and energy projects are growing order sources — partially offsetting residential weakness.

  • Renovation/retrofit (driven by EU energy performance directives) is structurally supportive in 2026–2028.

  • Peer read-across [#A]: Bravida Q1 2026 organic growth returned after a two-year decline — order intake +16% YoY.

  • Bravida noted northern Sweden as a standout strength area (overlaps with Instalco's geographic footprint).

  • Peer confirmation that Nordic installation market is turning — Bravida is ahead of Instalco in the cycle.

  • Risk: new-build dependency not fully resolved [#A]: Instalco historically skewed toward new-build (vs Bravida's heavier service mix). If housing starts recovery stalls, Swedish segment organic growth stalls with it.

  • Euroconstruct (Fall 2025): Swedish construction expected to grow modestly; residential recovery "tentative" — uncertainty flagged explicitly.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Sweden has disappointed forecasters consistently since 2022. A 24% housing start recovery in 2026 is plausible but rate-sensitive — any delay in Riksbank cuts or policy reversal on mortgage rules pushes the recovery into 2027+.

Plausibility verdict: Medium — directional recovery confirmed by order backlog and peer read-across; magnitude and timing depend on Swedish housing market following through on 2026 forecast.

§4 Driver 3: Deleveraging — Path from 2.8x to <2.5x

J-front verdict [#J]: The leverage overhang is the single most important structural constraint — it freezes M&A, sustains the peer discount, and forces capital discipline that limits strategic optionality; reduction to <2.5x is achievable by YE2026 but requires sustained cash generation without accidents.

  • Current position [#I]: Net debt/EBITDA 2.8x at YE2025; peaked at 3.3x at Q3 2025.
  • Interest-bearing debt (including leases): SEK 3,791m at YE2025 vs SEK 3,989m at H1 2025 — SEK 198m reduction in H2 2025.
  • Cash conversion: 108% for FY2025 — exceptional; well above the 100% target.

  • Dividend cut to SEK 0.50/share — capital discipline signalled.

  • Deleveraging mechanics [#A]: Three pathways to <2.5x.

  • Pathway 1: EBITDA denominator grows (margin recovery + organic volume = faster denominator expansion).
  • Pathway 2: Net debt numerator falls (strong cash conversion + M&A pause + minimal capex = cash accumulates).

  • Pathway 3: Equity issuance (used selectively for Defensio Invest and Fabri minority stake — dilutive but debt-neutral).

  • Timeline estimate [#A]: At current EBITDA trajectory and 108% cash conversion, reduction of ~0.3x per annum is achievable.

  • YE2026 exit leverage: ~2.4–2.5x if margin trajectory holds and no major working capital shock.

  • This is the trigger point for M&A re-activation (see Driver 6).

  • Risk items that could arrest deleveraging [#A]:

  • Any large project write-down (Northvolt-style) would push net debt back up and/or compress EBITDA.
  • Working capital swings in H1 (seasonally weak) can temporarily spike leverage.

  • Fabri consolidation (expected Q1–Q2 2026 when majority stake closes) adds debt to the balance sheet — scale unclear, but Fabri revenue ~EUR 70m; likely modest.

  • Market re-rating trigger [#A]: Peers (SPIE, Bravida, Bilfinger) trade at EV/EBITA 12–15x; Instalco at 30–35% discount.

  • If leverage falls below 2.5x, the discount rationale weakens materially — re-rating could be fast and significant.

  • SS models are not pricing in the re-rating: Kepler target SEK 43 implies modest upside from SEK 38–39.

  • CEO emphasis [#A]: Per Sjöstrand's mantra is "Margin, margin, margin" — explicitly framing margin improvement as the primary deleveraging mechanism. This is correct sequencing.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: 2.8x→2.5x seems close but three prior quarters showed leverage could spike on working capital timing; 108% cash conversion in FY2025 may be a peak year (exceptional payment timing) rather than a structural new normal.

Plausibility verdict: Medium-High — the maths work if margin holds; cash conversion track record is strong; the main risk is an unexpected one-off hit rather than a structural deleveraging failure.

§5 Driver 4: Nordic Market Recovery (Cyclical Tailwind)

J-front verdict [#J]: The Nordic installation market is turning — Sweden housing starts +24% forecast 2026, Finland +4% after -8% in 2024, Norway leading the CAGR rankings; Instalco's exposure to the full Nordic cycle gives it optionality peers with single-country focus lack.

  • Sweden (largest market, ~65% of group) [#I]: Housing starts forecast 35,100 in 2026 vs 28,300 in 2025.
  • Infrastructure stimulus hardwiring: rail, energy, defence facilities — growing pipeline.
  • Euroconstruct CAGR for Scandinavia construction: 4.89% through 2031.

  • Swedish construction investment: SEK 645bn (2024) → SEK 686bn (2026e).

  • Finland (~15% of group) [#I]: Construction sector +4% expected in 2025 after -8% in 2024.

  • Defence spending uplift post-NATO accession — infrastructure spend increasing.

  • Energy transition (wind, grid) providing additional demand.

  • Norway (~20% of group) [#I]: Quickest CAGR among Nordics — 6.00% forecast 2026–2031.

  • Instalco's order backlog growth specifically noted Norway as a strong contributor in 2025.

  • Oil & gas services and offshore energy support ongoing demand.

  • Sector mix tailwind [#A]: Growth tilting away from speculative housing toward infrastructure and energy.

  • Favours Instalco's electrical/HVAC/industrial mix.

  • Renovation and energy-performance upgrades (EU taxonomy compliance) structurally supported 2026–2030.

  • Data centre upswing [#A]: Nordic data centre construction market growing rapidly — hyperscaler investment in Finland (Microsoft, Google) and Sweden (Amazon) driving electrical and HVAC demand.

  • Instalco positioned in electrical and technical services — direct beneficiary of data centre fit-out demand.

  • Lag vs Bravida [#A]: Bravida's organic growth turned positive in Q1 2026 (order intake +16% YoY); Instalco's own organic growth +4.9% in Q1 2026.

  • Bravida historically runs 6–12 months ahead of Instalco in cycle timing (larger service mix means earlier demand signal).

  • Instalco's improving backlog (+15% YoY) suggests it is now catching up.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Nordic market recovery is real but construction forecasts have been wrong by 12–18 months consistently since 2022; macro risks (Iran conflict, tariff uncertainty, rate stickiness) could delay housing recovery further.

Plausibility verdict: Medium-High — multiple independent data sources confirm Nordic recovery; Q1 2026 backlog growth corroborates; risk is pace rather than direction.

§6 Driver 5: Founder CEO Effect (Per Sjöstrand)

J-front verdict [#J]: Sjöstrand's return is the highest-conviction qualitative catalyst in the thesis — he invented the decentralised model, built it from 0 to 100 acquisitions, delivered 700%+ share price appreciation 2017–2021, and returned specifically because the model needed correcting, not replacing.

  • Track record [#I]: Founded Instalco in 2014 with the decentralised acquisition model.
  • CEO 2014–2021: oversaw 100+ acquisitions, IPO in May 2017, share price appreciation of ~700% to mid-2021.
  • Moved to Chairman 2021; returned as CEO August 2025 after Robin Boheman's exit.

  • Nearly 40 years of construction/installation industry experience (Skanska, Midroc, NEA Group).

  • Why the return matters [#A]: Sjöstrand did not return as caretaker — he restructured the organisation within weeks of arriving.

  • Country-based structure (Sweden/Norway/Finland) with COOs: implemented September 2025, one month after Sjöstrand took over.

  • This pace of structural change signals high conviction and operator-level understanding of where the rot was.

  • Model creator advantage [#A]: The decentralised model is Sjöstrand's intellectual property — he understands its failure modes better than any outside CEO could.

  • The 2023–2024 margin erosion reflected over-extension of the model without adequate accountability mechanisms.

  • Instalco 2.0 is his diagnosis and prescription — not a consultant's.

  • Alignment [#I]: Per Sjöstrand is a significant shareholder (founding-era position).

  • Skin in the game: he bears personal financial cost if the recovery fails — this is not a hired manager.

  • Risk: can lightning strike twice? [#A]: The 2014–2021 run occurred in an ultra-low-rate, high-M&A-multiple environment.

  • Current conditions (2.8x leverage, frozen M&A, market headwinds) are structurally harder.

  • The organic model (Instalco 2.0 margin focus) is different from the acquisition-led 2014–2021 playbook.

  • Succession risk [#A]: Sjöstrand is not a permanent fixture — at some point transition back to professional management will be required.

  • Market may price in a "founder discount unwind" if/when he leaves again.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Founder returns are romanticised; the structural playbook that worked 2014–2021 (cheap acquisitions, rising multiples, easy credit) does not exist in 2025–2026; execution of Instalco 2.0 is a harder and different challenge than the original build.

Plausibility verdict: Medium-High — track record is real; structural speed of reorganisation is reassuring; the harder sceptical question is whether 2014 conditions can be replicated, not whether Sjöstrand is capable.

§7 Driver 6: M&A Re-activation

J-front verdict [#J]: M&A is frozen and should remain frozen until leverage <2.5x — this is the correct decision, but it removes the primary historical EPS growth engine (80%+ of growth 2014–2021) from the near-term model.

  • Historical dependence [#I]: M&A drove the majority of Instalco's revenue CAGR in 2014–2021.
  • Model: acquire high-quality local installation businesses at 4–7x EBITDA; apply Instalco's network support; extract operational synergies.

  • Bravida: 166 acquisitions in 12 years (~12.5bn SEK acquired sales) — same M&A-heavy model as Instalco.

  • Current status [#I]: M&A paused — balance sheet repair explicitly prioritised.

  • Exception: Fabri (Germany) — minority stake acquired with Instalco equity, not cash; completes step-up to majority by Q1–Q2 2027.
  • Defensio Invest (TSM brand) — treasury shares used, no cash impact.

  • Both transactions were structured to avoid cash drain — signals discipline, not paralysis.

  • Germany platform (Fabri) as optionality [#A]: Fabri now has 20 subsidiaries; EUR 70m revenue; annual acquisitions continuing.

  • German installation market: ~5x size of Nordic market — structural TAM expansion.
  • Majority stake expected Q1–Q2 2027; consolidation into group accounts at that point.

  • Germany is a long-duration option, not a near-term EPS driver — risk is distraction and capital misallocation during the balance sheet repair phase.

  • Re-activation trigger [#A]: If leverage drops to ~2.3–2.4x by H2 2026, M&A could resume in H1 2027 at modest scale — bolt-on acquisitions only, not transformational deals.

  • Small Nordic bolt-ons at 4–6x EBITDA would be immediately margin-accretive given Instalco's SG&A leverage.

  • Nordic installation market remains fragmented — deal pipeline has not disappeared during the pause.

  • Structural argument for patience [#A]: Acquisition multiples in Nordic installation services have likely compressed with the credit cycle — Instalco could potentially acquire at lower multiples in 2026–2027 than in 2021–2022.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: The M&A engine may not restart cleanly — Sjöstrand's model depends on finding quality local operators willing to sell; the best targets may have been acquired by Bravida or Assemblin Caverion during Instalco's pause.

Plausibility verdict: Low-Medium — M&A is structurally sound as a long-term driver, but 24-month EPS contribution is close to zero given the leverage constraint; the Germany distraction is a risk factor.

§8 Driver 7: Mation/Inmatiq — Building Automation (Risk or Optionality?)

J-front verdict [#J]: Inmatiq (building automation) is a cash drag at the wrong moment — breakeven delayed by ~12 months from original plan, absorbing management bandwidth during the balance sheet repair phase; the strategic rationale is sound but the timing is poor.

  • What it is [#I]: Inmatiq is Instalco's building automation business unit — launched via start-up model (not acquisition) in 2024.
  • Targets building automation solutions for real estate and industrial facilities.

  • Built organically, mirroring how Instalco built Intec (technical consulting).

  • Strategic rationale [#A]: Building automation is the highest-margin segment in technical installation services.

  • Energy performance upgrade demand (EU taxonomy, BREEAM standards) creates structural tailwind through 2030.
  • Instalco's existing customer relationships in electrical/HVAC provide natural cross-sell entry.

  • If successful, Inmatiq could eventually command 10–12% EBITA margins vs group's 6–8% — material mix uplift.

  • Current status [#A]: Start-up phase — pre-profitability; breakeven delayed approximately 12 months from original plan.

  • No separate segment disclosure — limited visibility on cash burn rate.

  • Management bandwidth risk: launching a new business unit while repairing a balance sheet and restructuring the group is operationally complex.

  • The timing problem [#A]: Inmatiq is a mid-cycle investment being launched at a trough — appropriate for building the capability, but the cash drag hits at exactly the moment when Instalco needs every SEK of free cash flow for deleveraging.

  • Optionality framing [#A]: If building automation is valued at sector multiples (15–20x EBITDA) once profitable, even a modest SEK 50–100m EBITDA contribution could add SEK 750m–2bn to enterprise value.

  • This is embedded, unlisted optionality — not in sell-side models, not in current market price.

  • Sceptical challenge [#J]: Start-up businesses within decentralised conglomerates historically struggle — the Instalco model is not designed for incubating new ventures; Inmatiq's delayed breakeven is an early confirmation of this structural tension.

Plausibility verdict: Low — near-term EPS drag; strategic value is real but 3–5 year horizon; recommend monitoring quarterly disclosure rather than embedding in base case.

§9 Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

9.1 Driver Interaction Map

  • Drivers 1, 2, 3 are sequentially coupled and mutually reinforcing:
  • Sweden organic recovery (#2) drives EBITA volume → margin recovery (#1) easier to achieve at higher revenue base → EBITDA denominator grows → deleveraging (#3) accelerates.

  • If any one of the three stalls, the others are impaired: Sweden stagnation = margin target pushed right = leverage stays elevated = M&A frozen = re-rating postponed.

  • Drivers 4 and 5 are enabling conditions, not independent drivers:

  • Nordic market recovery (#4) is the external tailwind that makes #1 and #2 achievable — without it, margin recovery requires purely internal execution in a flat market (harder).

  • Founder CEO (#5) is the execution credibility mechanism — he does not create the tailwind but he determines whether Instalco captures it better than peers.

  • Drivers 6 and 7 are long-duration options, not 24-month contributors:

  • M&A (#6) and Inmatiq (#7) are relevant for a 36–60 month investment horizon; including them in a 24-month EPS model overstates their contribution.

9.2 The Fulcrum Question

  • Core question [#J]: Is the 8% EBITA margin achievable by 2027e, or is 7.0–7.5% the structural ceiling under current conditions?
  • If 8%: stock is materially undervalued at current price — consensus is wrong.

  • If 7.0–7.5%: stock is roughly fairly priced — the discount to peers is justified.

  • Watson's first-pass view [#J]: 8% is achievable but unlikely before 2027e; 7.0–7.3% is the realistic 2026e landing zone, implying the stock is priced around fair value (not screaming cheap) with asymmetric upside if Sweden beats or Sjöstrand outperforms.

9.3 Sceptical Synthesis (Bear Case)

  • The bear case that the whole driver set misses [#J]: The entire thesis assumes the Nordic installation market normalises. If it does not — if Swedish housing stagnates, if data centre demand delays, if EU energy mandates are watered down — then Instalco's internal improvements (Instalco 2.0, COO structure, accountability cascade) generate margin uplift of 50–80bps not 140–160bps, leaving the stock stuck at SEK 35–40 on peer-relative discount multiples with no re-rating catalyst.

  • Northvolt write-down as governance signal [#A]: SEK 64m Northvolt exposure was not flagged until bankruptcy — implies the prior decentralised model allowed significant single-counterparty risk to accumulate undetected at subsidiary level.

  • How many other "Northvolts" exist across 150+ subsidiaries? The answer is not visible from group-level disclosure.

  • Instalco's stated policy: single customer max <4% of sales. Northvolt exposure SEK 64m vs group revenue ~SEK 13–14bn = ~0.4–0.5% — technically compliant, but still a material one-off.

  • Decentralised model structural risk [#A]: 150 subsidiaries = 150 P&Ls, 150 project portfolios, 150 sets of counterparty relationships.

  • The COO insertion (Instalco 2.0) helps but does not eliminate the monitoring gap.

  • A recurrence of margin compression through poor project selection at subsidiary level is the structural bear case.

  • Concentration in founder [#A]: If Per Sjöstrand departs again (health, frustration, another board role), the credibility of the recovery narrative collapses rapidly — key-person risk is elevated.
