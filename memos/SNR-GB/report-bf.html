EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 67 findings

Senior plc's CEO David Squires represents a highly specialized operator-track leader with deep aerospace procurement and operational expertise spanning thirty years of continuous manufacturing focus, though his long defense sector tenure and the group's heavy reliance on cyclical civil aerospace build rates introduce strategic agility concerns . The company delivered exceptional absolute share performance of 39.7% over twelve months, significantly outperforming the FTSE All Share's 17.7% return , while completing the transformational sale of the lower-margin Aerostructures division for £200m at a strong 13.1x EBITDA multiple on New Year's Eve 2025, positioning Senior as a pure-play thermal and fluid conveyance specialist . Sell-side confidence has strengthened materially, with Jefferies upgrading its price target to 230p in November 2025 and Deutsche Bank raising its target from 215p to 255p in January 2026 , though consensus optimism may be overly dependent on civil aerospace recovery, leaving valuation vulnerable to further Boeing production disruptions . The board maintains robust independent oversight with a 78% independence ratio, outperforming FTSE small-cap engineering peers , while CEO total remuneration reached £2.10m in 2025 with the annual bonus achieving 93.30% of maximum opportunity, driven by strong EPS and cash flow outperformance . Expert network testimony reveals persistent divergences from management's polished narrative, particularly regarding siloed divisional structures and slow footprint optimization , and insider transactions display strong director alignment with Chairman Ian King executing a conviction purchase of 75,000 shares in November 2025 while CEO Squires holds 401% of salary, comfortably exceeding the mandatory 200% guideline . The single most critical credibility risk centers on Boeing's unilateral right to redirect production volumes elsewhere if performance falters, exposing Senior to sudden work transfers despite customer validation through major contract awards .
