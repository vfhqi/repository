Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Besi is entering a major cyclical upcycle backed by structural AI and hybrid bonding growth.
1.
Technology focus: Besi designs advanced packaging systems, earning high-margin revenue from selling precision die attach tools and providing after-sales spares to global logic and memory chipmakers
2.
Core thesis: The investment case rests on Besi's near-monopoly in die-to-wafer hybrid bonding, which should fuel a massive operating margin expansion to 45-55% as leading chipmakers transition to 3D stacked chiplet architectures
3.
Swing factor: The most critical swing factor for forward earnings is the adoption timeline of hybrid bonding by leading memory manufacturers for HBM stacking
4.

6.

7.
First-look verdict: A closer look is highly warranted as Besi offers a highly predictable operating margin model of 45-55% and clear exposure to structural AI growth

Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Besi dominates the high-precision advanced packaging market through its technically superior hybrid bonding systems.

Core Products and Revenue Segmentation

1.
Die attach focus: Die attach product group is Besi's primary business line, ~80% of total revenue in FY25; packaging molding at 17%; plating at 3%
2.
Advanced packaging mix: ~70% of Besi's systems revenue from advanced packaging applications, with 60% featuring placement accuracy of less than 7 microns
3.
Sub-micron accuracy: Hybrid bonding (100nm to 25nm accuracy) and thermo-compression bonding address the highest value-added chiplet, memory, and logic applications
4.
Molding and plating: Mature legacy lines provide standardized, highly profitable cash flows, though subject to heavy cyclicality tied to broad electronics unit demand
5.
Services and spares: ~15-16% of group revenues; growing, stable, less-cyclical revenue stream

Value Chain and Competitive Advantage

6.
Value chain positioning: Advanced packaging is the critical gating item to bypass traditional physical transistor scaling limitations
7.
High customer concentration: Top customers include TSMC, Intel, Samsung, ASE, and Amkor; Chinese customers contributed 35% of FY25 revenues
8.
Tedious qualification cycles: Extremely high switching costs protect Besi's position – long qualification cycles; customers cannot easily risk yield excursions on expensive multi-chip assemblies
9.
Applied Materials moat: Five-year partnership with Applied Materials creates a unique competitive moat via integrated, cleanroom-ready front-end cluster tools
10.
Sub-micron IP barrier: Technical barriers to entry in sub-micron bonding are exceptionally high

Market Structure and Peer Comparison

11.
Hybrid bonding monopoly: In die-to-wafer hybrid bonding, Besi faces virtually no commercial competition today – near-monopoly for logic applications
12.
Flip-chip market share: Dominant 60-70% market share in high-accuracy flip-chip bonding
13.
TCB competitive field: Unlike hybrid bonding, TCB market is highly crowded with ASMPT, Shibaura, and Hanmi
14.
Back-end outperformance: Backend packaging equipment projected to structurally outperform frontend equipment as industry pivots from lithography scaling to heterogeneous integration
15.
Other side: Chinese equipment suppliers rapidly advancing; local Asian competitors could eventually pressure Besi's market share

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Besi's investment case is powered by rapid hybrid bonding adoption in logic and memory and a cyclical back-end recovery.

Advanced Packaging and Hybrid Bonding

1.
Logic adoption driver: Rapid hybrid bonding adoption in high-performance logic; high plausibility as AMD, Broadcom, and Apple integrate the tech
2.
Memory adoption driver: HBM adoption – medium plausibility; memory players actively qualifying hybrid bonded HBM4e 16-high stacks
3.
Memory failure mechanism: Hybrid bonding memory driver fails if memory manufacturers successfully extend less expensive thermo-compression bonding methods or face permanent yield limitations below 50% in high-volume production

Silicon Photonics and Mainstream Recovery

4.
Silicon photonics driver: Co-packaged optics / silicon photonics – high plausibility as transceivers transition to passive alignment
5.
Optics failure mechanism: Fails if transceivers remain on legacy active optical alignment systems
6.
Mainstream recovery driver: Cyclical recovery in mainstream assembly markets – high plausibility as PC and smartphone markets rebound
7.
Mainstream failure mechanism: Fails if broad consumer electronics demand remains sluggish, forcing subcontractors to cancel bulk flip-chip orders

Compound Growth and Execution

8.
Bull case requirements: For the bull case of €8.00 EPS to work, Besi does not need all drivers to fire simultaneously – logic and mainstream recovery are highly independent of HBM memory timing
9.
Independent driver proof: Logic hybrid bonding adoption already accelerating; TSMC's SoIC capacity expanding from 14,000 wpm in 2026 to 28,000 in 2027
10.
Other side: If transition to chiplet designs slows due to high advanced packaging costs, Besi's massive sub-micron R&D investments could fail to generate anticipated cash flows

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Besi faces credible risks surrounding the timing of advanced packaging adoption and intense geopolitical tensions.
1.
HBM adoption delay: Delayed transition from TCB to hybrid bonding in the memory space would stifle high-volume sub-micron tool shipments
2.
Yield failure mode: Major failure mode is low manufacturing yields – single packaging defect can render expensive completed multi-chip processor assemblies entirely obsolete
3.
High customer concentration: High concentration exposes Besi to massive capex fluctuations – pullback in spending by TSMC or Intel directly impacts order books
4.
China export risk: Chinese customers 35% of FY25 revenue – tightening US or European export restrictions on advanced packaging tools would severely hurt EPS
5.
False friend signal: Rising mainstream revenues can mask structural loss of market share or slow traction in the high-value sub-micron segment
6.
Best monitoring signal: Investors should monitor quarterly order bookings as the single best early-warning signal – look specifically for consecutive sequential declines in advanced packaging demand
7.
Other side: Risks likely overstated because severe thermal limitations in traditional packaging will ultimately force all leading-edge players to adopt hybrid bonding
