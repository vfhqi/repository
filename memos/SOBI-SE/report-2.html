§10 — Value Chain: Supply Side

J-front verdict: Supply-side is benign — SOBI's outsourced manufacturing model provides flexibility, and no critical single-source supplier dependency has been identified; Alhemo manufacturing scale-up proceeding without public issues [#J]

  • Supplier economics: Alhemo (fitusiran) is manufactured via contract manufacturing organisations (CMOs); SOBI does not own the manufacturing asset; creates supply concentration risk but avoids capex intensity [#A]

  • Concentration sub-bullet: Identity of Alhemo CMOs is not publicly disclosed; if CMO concentration is high (common for siRNA biologic manufacturing), a supply disruption would have no rapid mitigation path [#A]

  • Relationship: Alnylam (originator) retains a supply/royalty relationship on fitusiran; SOBI pays royalties and depends on the Alnylam-originating manufacturing know-how chain [#I]

  • Supply risk — Synagis: AstraZeneca retains manufacturing and supply responsibilities for palivizumab (Synagis) under the existing license; SOBI is downstream of AZ's supply decisions as Synagis declines — AZ incentive to maintain supply is weakening [#A]

  • Backward integration sub-bullet: SOBI has no ability or likely incentive to backward-integrate Synagis manufacturing as the product declines; managed exit is the rational path [#J]

  • Supplier health: Alnylam is well-capitalised and growing; no near-term financial distress risk that would impair Alhemo supply chain [#I]

  • Vertical integration: SOBI does own some biologics manufacturing capacity in Stockholm for legacy haematology products; this provides some supply security for older portfolio items [#I]

Sceptical bullet: The CMO model for Alhemo is the single most opaque supply risk — siRNA manufacturing requires specialised lipid nanoparticle technology, and the number of qualified CMOs globally is small; a force-majeure event at the primary Alhemo CMO would create a supply disruption that SOBI could not solve quickly, with patient safety and revenue consequences [#J]

  • Peer anchor sub-bullet: Rare disease biopharma (Alexion/AZ, Ultragenyx, Recordati) consistently demonstrates top-quartile revenue predictability vs broad healthcare — SOBI fits this profile [#A]

  • Synagis seasonality: Synagis revenues are concentrated in H2 (RSV season, Oct–March); this creates a recurring H2-weighted revenue pattern that can look like cyclicality but is structural seasonality [#I]

  • Cycle stage sub-bullet: As Synagis declines (Beyfortus market share gain), the H2 revenue weighting will diminish; SOBI's revenue profile becomes more smoothly quarterly distributed — a quality-of-earnings improvement [#J]

  • Alhemo launch curve: Alhemo is in the launch-ramp phase (commercial since 2023 broadly); patient addition curves for rare disease biologics typically plateau 3–5 years post-launch at ~15–25% of addressable population penetration [#A]

  • Demand pulse caution sub-bullet: The current Alhemo patient-addition velocity may partially reflect pent-up demand from pre-launch clinical trial patients converting; underlying sustainable new-patient rate may be lower [#J]

  • Through-cycle profile: During COVID (2020–2021), SOBI haemophilia revenues declined ~3–5% (reduced HTC visits, delayed dosing decisions) before recovering; trough behaviour was mild — supports low-cyclicality characterisation [#A]

  • Inventory cycle: No meaningful inventory cycle in rare disease biologics; specialty distributors hold minimal inventory; demand signal is close to real-time patient need [#I]

  • Capex cycle: SOBI's capex cycle is not a demand driver; the company is not a manufacturing-intensive business with equipment replacement cycles affecting revenue [#I]

Sceptical bullet: The low-cyclicality narrative is robust, but SOBI's revenue is NOT as predictable as fully-established rare disease franchises (e.g., PNH/Soliris in its prime) because Alhemo penetration is still launch-variable and Synagis is a declining wildcard; investors who over-weight the "predictable rare disease compounder" framing may miss the execution volatility embedded in the current financials [#J]
