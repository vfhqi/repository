§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Governance structure is mixed — German SE form is compliant with DCGK requirements, no restatements or auditor changes detected, but founder-control concentration and related-party transaction exposure are structural red flags that cannot be fully resolved from public disclosures

Voting rights structure:
- Single-class shares — DMP-DE does not have a dual-class share structure per se; however, Beier's 76% direct economic and voting ownership via Themis GmbH achieves near-identical founder-control to a dual-class structure without the structural label
- Anti-takeover provisions: Beier's voting bloc is the de facto anti-takeover provision; no poison pill or golden share disclosed; unsolicited bids are structurally impossible without Beier's consent
- SE articles: DMP-DE is structured as a Societas Europaea ; the SE form gives flexibility in corporate governance design; this was chosen at IPO and gives management/supervisory board structure somewhat more flexibility than German AG

Related-party transactions:
- Themis GmbH / Beier family holding: The relationship between DMP-DE and Themis GmbH (Beier's holding vehicle) is the primary related-party risk vector; any IP, real estate, or service relationships between DMP-DE and Themis require disclosure; public filings note related-party transactions in the Notes to accounts — the nature and magnitude warrant specific review
- Manufacturing facility ownership: Some DMP-DE production assets have historically been on complex ownership structures; whether any facilities are owned by Beier-controlled entities and leased to DMP-DE (creating an implicit related-party rental income) should be specifically verified against Notes to Accounts L3Y
- Materiality: No related-party transaction has been publicly flagged as a governance scandal or minority shareholder complaint in press record — but this absence of flagging is not the same as absence of the structure

Audit history:
- Auditor: KPMG AG Wirtschaftsprüfungsgesellschaft audits DMP-DE (confirmed from annual report disclosures) — a Big-4 firm; no auditor change detected since IPO
- Material weaknesses: No material weakness disclosures found in public filings; German HGB/IFRS reporting framework; audit opinions are unqualified (clean) in available annual reports
- Restatement history: No restatements detected; accounts appear consistently presented L3Y

Legal / regulatory issues:
- No class action lawsuits detected — DMP-DE is not subject to any US-style securities litigation; German litigation landscape is structurally less litigious
- No BaFin enforcement actions detected in public record; no German market abuse probes visible
- Competition/antitrust: Licensed-drug niche has lower antitrust exposure than original-drug blockbuster businesses; no antitrust actions detected
- COVID contract regulatory review: BioNTech fill/finish contract was a regulatory success; no subsequent inquiry into procurement process detected in public record
- Product liability: German pharma market; standard product liability framework applies; no material product liability actions disclosed in annual report Notes

Whistleblower / governance complaint signals:
- No whistleblower events detected in public record; Glassdoor reviews (see §7) do not prominently flag ethics concerns — mild positive signal
- German whistleblower law (HinSchG 2023): Required internal reporting channels now legally mandated for companies >50 employees; DMP-DE's compliance with this is assumed but not specifically disclosed in public governance materials

Anti-takeover provisions:

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on governance is that the single red flag that would invalidate the operator-quality thesis is a minority-unfriendly take-private or strategic restructuring — Beier controls the vote, chairs (via nominees) the oversight board, and faces no meaningful shareholder activist risk due to the absence of any blocking minority; the cleanliness of the public audit record (no restatements, no enforcement) is consistent with both genuine governance quality AND a well-managed information environment under a dominant controlling shareholder; distinguishing the two from external [C]-only sources is not fully possible

§8 — Governance Verdict + Sceptical Synthesis
