Driver Assessment

1. Rate Environment & NII

The single most consequential variable for Avanza's earnings trajectory.

NII contributed approximately 41% of Avanza's 2024 operating income, making it the dominant revenue line. The Riksbank cut the policy rate by 100bps through 2025 (from 2.75% to 1.75%), and despite this, full-year NII remained broadly stable thanks to deposit and lending volume growth offsetting margin compression.

Current state: The Riksbank held at 1.75% through March 2026 (fourth consecutive hold). Management stated in the Q4 2025 earnings call that they believe NII is "near the bottom" assuming no further cuts. The average deposit rate fell to 0.76% (down 4bps QoQ in Q4 2025), while the share of deposits on interest-bearing accounts rose to 58% (from 54%). Internally financed lending rates averaged 3.40% in Q1 2025, down from higher levels. Deposits stood at SEK 76.9bn (Q1 2025).

Rate path consensus: The Riksbank's own forward guidance points to rates holding at 1.75% through 2026, with slow increases beginning in 2027. Nordea forecasts three hikes during 2027. Handelsbanken expects the first hike in Q1 2027. DNB Carnegie sees a possible first hike as early as November 2026, reaching 2.50% by late 2027.

Mechanism: Avanza earns a spread between the rate it pays depositors and the rate it earns on placing those funds (at the Riksbank or in lending). As rates fell, the spread compressed. If rates stabilise at 1.75%, the NII floor is established. If rates rise from 2027, NII re-expands -- and with a larger deposit base (~SEK 77-90bn+), the NII gearing is significant. Each 25bps rate increase on the deposit base generates an estimated SEK 50-60m in incremental annual NII (rough calculation on ~SEK 80bn deposits, assuming partial pass-through).

Scenario analysis:
- Bull (rates to 2.50% by late 2027): NII re-expands materially. +15-20% NII uplift vs 2025 baseline.
- Base (rates flat at 1.75% through 2027): NII grows modestly via volume (deposit/lending growth). +3-5% NII.
- Bear (Riksbank cuts to 1.25%): NII compresses again, partially offset by volumes. -10-15% NII. Low probability given current inflation trajectory.

  • Direction: Tailwind (rate floor established, potential for hikes from 2027)
  • Magnitude: High
  • Confidence: Medium-High (Riksbank guidance is clear; execution risk is on deposit volumes and pass-through timing)
  • Leading indicators: Riksbank policy rate decisions (8x/year), Swedish CPI prints, deposit volume monthly statistics (published by Avanza), interest-bearing deposit share

2. Equity Market Activity

The highest-volatility driver -- directly controls brokerage and currency income.

Brokerage income contributed ~23% and currency-related income ~12% of 2024 operating income (combined ~35%). These are directly tied to trading volumes, which in turn depend on equity market levels, volatility, retail participation, and macro sentiment.

Q1 2025 was a standout: operating income rose 21% YoY to SEK 1,152m, driven by record trading activity. This was the best quarterly result in Avanza's history. However, Q2 2025 saw a sequential decline as trading-related income normalised, though NII picked up the slack.

Structural vs cyclical: The ISK tax-free threshold doubling to SEK 300,000 in 2026 is a structural positive -- it incentivises more Swedes to invest via ISK accounts and could stimulate trading activity. However, there is a secular shift toward passive/index investing that pressures brokerage revenue per trade over time.

Avanza Markets: The internal market-making operation (Avanza Markets) contributed SEK 64m in Q1 2025 (vs SEK 42m prior year), adding a proprietary income stream linked to trading volumes.

  • Direction: Neutral-to-Tailwind (ISK reform positive, but cyclicality high)
  • Magnitude: High
  • Confidence: Low (inherently unpredictable; depends on macro and sentiment)
  • Leading indicators: OMX Stockholm 30 daily volumes, Avanza monthly trading statistics, VIX/VSTOXX, retail participation surveys, ISK account openings

3. Customer Acquisition

The growth engine, still running but approaching cruising altitude.

Avanza added 171,000 net new customers in 2025, bringing the total to over 2.2 million. Q1 2025 alone added 62,000+ -- the strongest quarter since the pandemic. Net inflows were SEK 54bn for the full year. Market share of Swedish savings rose to 8.3% (from 7.8%).

Penetration analysis: Sweden has ~10 million inhabitants and ~4 million ISK holders. Avanza's 2.2m customers represent ~55% of ISK holders (though not all are unique ISK holders). The question is whether growth can sustain 150,000-200,000 net additions annually, or whether it decelerates toward 100,000-120,000 as penetration deepens.

Competitive position: Avanza has been voted Sweden's most-satisfied savings provider for 16 consecutive years. Customer churn is low at 1.7%. The brand strength in Sweden is exceptional.

Demographics: Younger cohorts (Gen Z, Millennials) are digital-native and increasingly drawn to platform-based savings. Avanza is well-positioned for this structural shift.

  • Direction: Tailwind (still growing, but decelerating)
  • Magnitude: Medium
  • Confidence: High (well-established trend with high visibility)
  • Leading indicators: Monthly customer statistics (Avanza publishes), net inflow figures, market share of savings, new ISK account openings (Swedish Tax Agency data)

4. Occupational Pensions Expansion

The most important long-term strategic bet -- sticky, recurring, counter-cyclical.

Avanza Pension aims to become the market leader in non-collectively agreed occupational pensions by 2030. Currently fourth in market. The 9% cost guidance for 2026 is partly driven by investment in this segment.

Why it matters: Occupational pension contributions are paid by employers regardless of market conditions. This creates a revenue stream that is fundamentally different from brokerage or trading income -- it is recurring, contractual, and grows with wages and employment. For a business whose other revenue lines are highly market-sensitive, this is a critical diversification play.

Market size: Sweden's occupational pension system (Pillar 2) is substantial. Total occupational pension assets are in the trillions of SEK. The non-collectively agreed segment is smaller but growing as more SMEs and self-employed individuals seek flexible pension solutions.

Recent initiatives: Partnership with Lifeplan (2025) for digital pension advice tools. Investment in corporate segment capabilities. These are the building blocks for scaling.

Revenue contribution: Still relatively small as a percentage of total income, but growing. The key metric is premium inflow growth, which management has not fully disclosed but is tracking positively.

  • Direction: Tailwind (strategic priority with clear runway)
  • Magnitude: Medium (N18M), potentially High (N3Y+)
  • Confidence: Medium (execution risk is real; competitive response from incumbents uncertain)
  • Leading indicators: Quarterly premium inflow data, number of corporate clients, pension AUM, competitive moves from AMF/Skandia/SPP

5. Lending Book Growth

A meaningful NII contributor, growing from a small base.

Loans to the public stood at SEK 25.9bn as of Q1 2025. Internally financed lending volumes grew 20% YoY, though the average lending rate fell to 3.40% as the policy rate declined. The lending book is conservatively managed: average mortgage LTV of 39%, margin lending LTV of 28%.

Components: Mortgages and securities-backed lending. Avanza's mortgage product competes on price with traditional banks, leveraging the platform's low cost base. Securities lending is a natural extension of the brokerage business.

NII contribution: Income from lending declined 7% in Q1 2025 due to lower rates, but volume growth partially offset this. As rates stabilise and potentially rise, lending margins should recover.

Risk profile: Low. Conservative LTV ratios, full collateralisation, and a customer base that is generally higher-income/financially sophisticated. Credit losses have historically been negligible.

  • Direction: Tailwind (volume growth, rate stabilisation)
  • Magnitude: Low-to-Medium
  • Confidence: High (predictable volume trajectory, conservative risk)
  • Leading indicators: Quarterly lending volumes, average lending rates, LTV ratios, Swedish housing market data, credit loss provisions

6. Operating Leverage

The platform's scalability is Avanza's structural advantage, currently in investment phase.

Avanza's 2025 operating margin was 68%. The cost-to-savings capital ratio declined to 14.1bps from 14.5bps. The platform serves 2.2m customers with ~60% of costs being personnel-related, demonstrating that marginal customers generate high incremental profit.

Investment cycle: 2026 cost growth guidance of 9% is elevated versus the long-term target of 5% by 2030. The primary drivers are cloud migration (a one-off transition), Private Banking expansion, and occupational pensions build-out. Average annual cost growth target until 2030 is 8%.

Scalability mechanism: Each new customer adds AUM, which drives fund commission income (ad valorem) and deposit volume . Trading income is variable. The fixed cost base (technology, compliance, office) does not scale linearly with customers. The result is expanding margins as AUM and customer count grow -- provided revenue growth exceeds the 8-9% cost growth trajectory.

Key risk: If revenue growth disappoints (e.g., equity markets decline, NII compressed further), the 9% cost growth creates a margin squeeze. Management would likely pull back discretionary spend, but the cloud migration is a committed cost.

  • Direction: Tailwind (structurally, but temporarily muted by investment cycle)
  • Magnitude: Medium
  • Confidence: Medium-High (platform economics proven, but 2026 is an investment year)
  • Leading indicators: Cost-to-savings capital ratio (quarterly), operating margin, cost growth vs guidance, headcount trends

7. Competitive Dynamics

Duopoly structure in Sweden provides durable pricing power.

Avanza and Nordnet dominate Swedish online investing. Together they hold accounts for millions of Swedish investors. The duopoly-like structure limits aggressive pricing wars, with competition focused on product features, user experience, and mobile app quality rather than price.

Nordnet: Operates across four Nordic markets (Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Norway). Pareto research notes that Avanza's Sweden-only focus is advantageous because Swedish households are expected to see relatively stronger disposable income growth than other Nordic countries. Nordnet's diversification is a strategic difference, not necessarily an advantage in Sweden specifically.

Traditional banks: SEB, Swedbank, Handelsbanken, and Nordea have digital savings offerings, but none have matched Avanza's user experience or pricing. The gap has not closed despite years of investment.

Neobroker threat: eToro, Robinhood, and other global neobrokers have minimal presence in Sweden. Regulatory barriers (Swedish language requirements, ISK account complexity, local compliance) create natural entry barriers. No credible entrant has emerged.

Pricing pressure: Limited. Avanza's commission-free equity trading (for Swedish stocks on certain account types) is already price-leading. The competitive threat is more about product innovation than price.

  • Direction: Neutral (stable competitive position)
  • Magnitude: Low
  • Confidence: High (structural factors are well-established)
  • Leading indicators: Nordnet Sweden market share, new entrant announcements, pricing changes from traditional banks, app store rankings

8. Regulatory Risk

Currently benign, with one clear tailwind (ISK reform) and manageable compliance burdens.

ISK tax reform (tailwind): The doubling of the ISK tax-free threshold to SEK 300,000 in 2026 is a direct positive for Avanza. It incentivises saving and investing through ISK accounts, which are Avanza's core product. This is the most significant regulatory development.

T+1 settlement (Dec 2026): Pre-settlement process changes to support T+1 are scheduled for 7 December 2026. This requires technology investment but is manageable for a platform of Avanza's sophistication.

Consumer credit reform: New stricter requirements for consumer credit providers will increase compliance costs for some players, but Avanza's lending is primarily secured (mortgages, margin lending) and the reform's impact on the core business is limited.

MiFID evolution: No imminent MiFID III changes that would fundamentally threaten Avanza's business model. Payment-for-order-flow restrictions are a potential risk for some brokers, but Avanza's revenue model is not heavily dependent on PFOF.

ESG/climate regulation: SFSA requiring climate scenario stress tests from 2026. Manageable compliance burden.

Capital requirements: No signals of material increases that would constrain Avanza's capital return capacity.

  • Direction: Neutral-to-Tailwind (ISK reform is positive; other changes manageable)
  • Magnitude: Low
  • Confidence: Medium (regulatory risk by nature carries uncertainty)
  • Leading indicators: Swedish government proposals, SFSA announcements, EU regulatory pipeline (MiFID review), ISK account statistics post-reform

9. Platform Ecosystem Deepening

Cross-sell and product expansion is the long runway for revenue per customer growth.

Avanza's platform spans savings, trading, lending (mortgages + margin), insurance, occupational pensions, and Corporate Finance. The strategic playbook is to deepen the relationship with each customer by cross-selling additional products.

Key initiatives:
- Private Banking: Investment in higher-touch service for wealthier clients. This captures a higher share of wallet from the ~1.17m Swedes with ISK balances above SEK 300,000.
- Corporate Finance: Growing advisory capability, contributing to "other income." Q1 2025 saw higher Corporate Finance revenue.
- Insurance: Life and pension insurance products within the Avanza Pension wrapper.
- Avanza Markets: Proprietary market-making, growing from SEK 42m to SEK 64m QoQ (Q1 2025). A high-margin, scalable income stream.

Revenue per customer: As AUM per customer grows and product penetration deepens, revenue per customer should increase even if net new customer growth slows.

  • Direction: Tailwind
  • Magnitude: Medium
  • Confidence: Medium (execution-dependent; cross-sell success varies)
  • Leading indicators: Products per customer, AUM per customer, Private Banking client numbers, Avanza Markets contribution, Corporate Finance deal count

10. Swedish Equity Market Structural Growth

Long-term secular tailwinds remain firmly in place.

Sweden has one of the highest retail equity participation rates globally. Approximately 40% of the population holds ISK accounts. The pension system's structure -- with individual choice over Pillar 2 (occupational) and Pillar 3 (private) allocations among 600+ funds -- embeds equity market participation into the national savings culture.

Financial literacy: Swedish financial literacy levels are among the highest globally. Comprehensive public pension information campaigns and "learning by doing" culture have created a population comfortable with equity investing.

ISK as structural driver: The ISK account was introduced in 2012 and has been transformative. With total ISK wealth estimated at SEK 1,665bn (2023) and ~4m account holders, this is a deeply embedded structural feature of Swedish savings. The 2026 tax-free doubling reinforces this trend.

Demographics: Sweden's population is growing (immigration-driven) and urbanising. Younger cohorts are more digitally engaged and more likely to use platform-based savings solutions.

Macro backdrop: Swedish GDP growth is recovering, with the IMF and EC projecting moderate growth. Retail sales have rebounded (April 2025: +5.3% YoY). Consumer confidence is improving as rate cuts feed through to mortgage costs.

  • Direction: Tailwind
  • Magnitude: Medium (steady, not explosive)
  • Confidence: High (structural, long-duration trend)
  • Leading indicators: ISK account openings (Tax Agency), Swedish savings rate, population growth, OMX market capitalisation growth, fund inflow statistics (Swedish Investment Fund Association)

Fulcrum Driver Analysis

THE FULCRUM DRIVER: Rate Environment & NII (Driver #1)

Why this is the fulcrum:

  1. Scale of impact. NII is ~41% of operating income. No other single revenue line comes close. A 10% swing in NII moves group operating income by ~4%, which at a 68% operating margin translates directly to a ~6% swing in operating profit. The gearing is substantial.

  2. Binary clarity. The Riksbank's rate path is relatively transparent and observable. Unlike equity market activity (unpredictable) or occupational pension growth (slow-burn, execution-dependent), the rate path provides a clear, trackable signal that directly flows through to earnings.

  3. Asymmetric positioning. Avanza enters the N18M-3Y period at the bottom of the NII cycle. Management has stated NII is "near the bottom." The 100bps cut cycle is fully reflected in Q1 2026 results. From here, the base case is flat (stable NII via volume growth) and the upside case is material (rate hikes from 2027 re-expanding spreads on a growing deposit base). The downside case (further cuts) is low-probability given Swedish inflation and the Riksbank's hawkish lean.

  4. Compounding with volume. The deposit base is growing (SEK 76.9bn Q1 2025, likely approaching SEK 85-90bn+ by end-2026 given customer and inflow momentum). Rate hikes applied to a larger deposit base create a double compounding effect on NII.

  5. Market mispricing potential. The market may be anchoring on the depressed NII run-rate from 2025. If the consensus rate path materialises (hikes from Q1 2027), the NII re-rating could surprise to the upside and drive a multiple re-rating of the stock.

Scenario framework for the fulcrum driver:

What to watch (trigger points):
- Immediate: Riksbank April/June 2026 decisions and updated rate path projections
- Key data: Swedish CPI (monthly), labour market data, GDP growth
- Company-specific: Quarterly NII disclosure, deposit volumes (monthly statistics), interest-bearing deposit share, average deposit rate
- Signal for bull case activation: First Riksbank hike signal in monetary policy report (likely September or November 2026 if early)
- Signal for bear case activation: Swedish CPI falling below 1.5%, Riksbank language shifting to "may need to ease further"

Interaction with other drivers: NII is not independent. Higher rates generally correlate with stronger equity markets (risk-on) and higher trading activity (Driver #2). A rising rate environment therefore creates a positive feedback loop across multiple revenue lines. Conversely, rate cuts often accompany risk-off periods that also depress brokerage revenue. This correlation amplifies the fulcrum effect.
