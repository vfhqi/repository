Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. ZIGUP occupies a profit-favoured position as the UK and Spain market leader in integrated commercial vehicle rental.

Operating divisions and vehicle assets

1.
Segmented fleet operations: ZIGUP operates two main segments, which are commercial vehicle rental (Northgate UK&I and Spain) and accident claims services (Redde and FMG)
2.
Substantial asset base: The company owns and leases a diversified fleet of over 135,000.00 vehicles, supporting over 1.00 million managed vehicles across its network
3.
In-house service support: Operations are supported by over 180.00 physical branches and a proprietary workshop network, providing rapid servicing and repairs
4.
Specialist fleet expansion: ZIGUP has broadened its rental mix through acquisitions like Blakedale for highway traffic management and FridgeXpress for temperature-controlled vehicles
5.
Used vehicle defleeting: The company defleets and recycles its fleet assets through Van Monster, which is the largest used commercial vehicle reseller in the UK

Revenue mechanics and contract structures

6.
Diversified revenue mix: ZIGUP earns revenue through contract hire, transactional flexible rental, used vehicle sales, and insurance referral fees
7.
Contractual hire visibility: Minimum-term rental contracts provide multi-year visibility, allowing clients to provision light commercial vehicles for up to several years
8.
Flexible hire dynamics: Flex rental allows corporate clients to upsize or downsize their fleets seasonally without incurring vehicle defleeting or termination penalties
9.
Accident management commissions: The Claims and Services division earns high-margin fee income from managing third-party not-at-fault vehicle recovery, repairs, and legal support
10.
Lucrative ancillary services: ZIGUP captures high-margin incremental revenue from customer add-ons, including telematics software, fuel cards, and EV fleet consulting
11.
Profit-favoured value position: ZIGUP sits at a highly advantageous point in the automotive value chain, acting as an outsourced partner for major insurance companies

Competitive advantage and industry dynamics

12.
Durable purchasing scale: ZIGUP is one of the largest single buyers of light commercial vehicles in Europe, securing preferential buying terms directly with manufacturers
13.
High switching costs: Corporate clients face high switching costs due to ZIGUP's integrated fleet management software, telemetry, and in-house workshop support
14.
Strong brand reputation: ZIGUP maintains an industry-leading Net Promoter Score of over 50.00% in Spain and over 65.00% in its UK Auxillis division
15.
Consolidating market structure: The UK accident management industry is consolidating, with insurers shifting toward large, integrated providers to reduce administrative friction
16.
Immature Spanish market: The Spanish light commercial vehicle rental market is highly immature, with rental penetration at only 5.00% versus 12.00% in the UK
17.
In-sourced workshop edge: ZIGUP's in-house repair capabilities allow it to internalize repair margins and manage vehicle downtime more efficiently than unlisted competitors
18.
Other side of the story: A primary bear argument is that unlisted independent hire competitors often operate with much higher leverage and lower cost of capital, allowing them to price rental contracts more aggressively and erode ZIGUP's market share

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk landscape is well-mitigated by asset backing, though cyclical economic demand and used car prices require monitoring.

Used vehicle residual value normalization

1.
Normalizing disposal profits: Used light commercial vehicle residual values are declining from post-pandemic highs, which directly pressures ZIGUP's disposal profit line
2.
Material earnings drag: Disposal profits fell by 15.20% in FY25, acting as a primary headwind to headline earnings growth
3.
Extended asset usage: To manage used market declines, ZIGUP can extend its vehicle holding periods beyond the standard 4.50 years, deferring disposals
4.
Higher maintenance costs: Extending fleet age requires increased servicing and repair expenditures, which partially offsets the deferred capex benefits

Accident management duration contraction

5.
Contraction in hire duration: Improved automotive supply chains have reduced vehicle repair cycle times, leading to shorter replacement vehicle hire periods
6.
Claims division headwind: Shorter rental durations in Claims and Services directly reduce total billing days and segment profit margins
7.
Stable baseline durations: Management has indicated that replacement hire lengths have stabilized since late 2024, mitigating the near-term headwind
8.
Customer retention risk: The loss of any major insurance referral partner or fleet customer could materially impact FMG's accident volumes

Post-merger operational integration friction

9.
Incomplete system integration: Legacy IT platforms and siloed operational teams have persisted since the 2020 merger, limiting potential cross-selling synergies
10.
Complexity in technology: Implementation of large-scale technology upgrades carries near-term execution risk, potentially disrupting call-centre or billing infrastructure
11.
False friend signal: Higher average weekly rental rates can mask underlying volume weakness or customer attrition if sales incentives focus on rates
12.
Key early warning: Analysts should closely monitor average vehicle hire durations and monthly fleet utilization rates to identify early margin deterioration
13.
Other side of the story: Bear concerns are overstated because ZIGUP's liquid vehicle assets are highly realizable, providing a solid hard-asset floor that covers its entire valuation
