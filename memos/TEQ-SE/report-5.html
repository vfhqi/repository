  • Sub: Compression drivers: (a) D&A on accumulated acquisition intangibles, (b) HQ overhead scaling, (c) organic volume diluting fixed-cost absorption

  • Sub: Redeye models a gradual EBIT margin recovery to ~12% by FY2026 — contingent on organic recovery AND M&A integration maturity

  • Margin direction: Stable to marginally improving QoQ in Q1 2025 vs Q4 2024, but YoY still compressing vs Q1 2024; no inflection evidence yet [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Negative to neutral in the current organic growth environment; at 2–5% organic, Teqnion is not generating meaningful positive operating leverage; the model requires approximately 5–8% organic to absorb fixed HQ cost growth [#J]

  • Sub: This is a critical threshold: sub-5% organic means every percentage point of HQ cost growth eats into margin

  • Sub: At 10%+ organic (2022 experience), operating leverage was clearly positive and margins expanded to peak 13–14%

  • Sub: The PPA amortisation component is "non-cash" in an economic sense but very real in IFRS reported earnings

  • Sub: Teqnion does not disclose adjusted EBIT pre-PPA, making apples-to-apples comparison with internally-managed estimates difficult

  • One-off costs: No material one-off costs reported in Q1 2025; Teqnion's model does not typically involve restructuring charges (subsidiaries run autonomously; no integration overhead costs of the kind seen in strategic acquirers) [#A]

  • Sub: Earn-out obligations on prior acquisitions represent a form of deferred cost — not trivial in aggregate

  • Adjusted vs reported: Gap is meaningful but not exploited — Teqnion does not produce adjusted non-GAAP metrics; the reported EBIT includes acquisition amortisation; an investor-adjusted EBIT margin removing PPA amortisation would be approximately 150–250bp higher than reported [#A]

  • One-off benefit: No material one-off benefits identified in Q1 2025; margins reflect ongoing operational run-rate [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The "D&A explains the margin compression" narrative is partially true but potentially over-relied upon; if underlying subsidiary-level EBITDA margins are also compressing (not just being D&A-diluted), the quality of the earnings stream is deteriorating in ways that PPA amortisation cannot fully explain — Teqnion's opacity on subsidiary-level margins makes this unverifiable [#J]
