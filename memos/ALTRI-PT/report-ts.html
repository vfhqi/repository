Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Altri operates a low-cost, pure-play pulp business in Portugal, enjoying scale and location advantages in Europe, but faces intense structural competition from lower-cost Latin American producers.

Products and Operating Segments

1.
Large industrial capacity: Altri operates three main bio-industrial mills in Portugal with a total annual capacity of over 1.00 million tonnes, producing Bleached Eucalyptus Kraft Pulp and Dissolving Wood Pulp
2.
Specialized pulp assets: Celbi focuses on Bleached Eucalyptus Kraft Pulp with an annual capacity of 800.00 thousand tonnes. Caima produces 115.00 thousand tonnes of Dissolving Wood Pulp, and Biotek is being converted to Dissolving Wood Pulp
3.
Tissue-dominant sales mix: Eucalyptus pulp produced by Altri is primarily used to manufacture tissue paper, representing 48.00% of sales volume in 2025, with printing and writing paper taking 22.00%, and textiles at 10.00%
4.
Growing textile penetration: Dissolving wood pulp sales are expanding and expected to increase further as Biotek completes its conversion, aiming to capture growing demand in the sustainable textile specialties market

Revenue Model and Customer Profiles

5.
Highly cyclical pricing: Altri generates its revenues entirely from transactional pulp shipments, which are highly cyclical and do not benefit from recurring or long-term contract pricing mechanisms
6.
Europe-centered geographic mix: Geographically, Europe represents Altri's largest market at 59.00% of sales volume in 2025, followed by the Middle East and North Africa at 29.00%, and Asia at 12.00%
7.
Segmented regional demand: Altri's bleached kraft pulp is predominantly sold to European paper and tissue manufacturers, whereas its dissolving wood pulp is mostly exported to Asian viscose and lyocell producers

Value Chain and Resource Sourcing

8.
Low raw material self-sufficiency: Altri manages a forestry estate of 92.80 thousand hectares in Portugal, composed of 80.00% eucalyptus, but has a low wood self-sufficiency rate of only 21.00%
9.
Strategic forestry integration: Altri completed the acquisition of Altri Forestal and Altri Forestal Logistics in 2025, establishing a leading forestry sourcing and logistics platform in Galicia to secure wood raw materials
10.
Ambitious sustainability targets: To align with strict European environmental regulations, Altri is targeting to source 80.00% of its wood from third-party certified suppliers by 2030, compared to 57.00% in 2018

Competitive Moat and Peer Comparison

11.
Leading cost efficiency: Altri is recognized as Europe's lowest cash-cost hardwood pulp producer, utilizing advanced cogeneration and biomass energy assets to operate with high efficiency and lower net carbon emissions
12.
Severe structural disadvantage: Despite European efficiency, Altri faces substantial competitive disadvantages against Latin American producers like Suzano, which benefits from an unreplicable 6.50-year wood growth cycle versus Portugal's 10.00-12.00 years
13.
Defensive regional proximity: Altri relies on its proximity to European customers as its primary competitive shield, as shipping costs and delivery times insulate it from cheaper Brazilian imports
14.
Other side of the story: European pulp mills operate on a structurally uncompetitive cost curve compared to Brazilian giants like Suzano, whose cash costs are under BRL800.00 per tonne, meaning Altri is highly vulnerable to prolonged global oversupply and margin compression if Latin American capacity floods European markets

1.
Constructive pricing momentum: In early 2026, European hardwood pulp prices experienced five consecutive price increases between January and May, which will flow into Altri's revenue line with a standard 2.00-month lag
2.
Resilient Asian demand: Hardwood pulp demand has rebounded strongly out of China, with Chinese buyers absorbing U.S. tariff pressures and stabilizing global market dynamics
3.
Fragile price sustainability: Morgan Stanley reports that hardwood shipments remain weak in Asia, meaning recent price gains could prove difficult to sustain if China's demand fails to catch up

4.

5.

6.
High-margin product launch: The green furfural and acetic acid plant at Caima is set to finish in 1H26, generating €6.00-€7.00 million in revenues at an 80.00% EBITDA margin and >15.00% unlevered IRR

Long-Term Greenfield Expansion

7.
Ambitious greenfield expansion: Project Gama in Galicia is designed to produce 250.00 thousand tonnes of dissolving pulp and 60.00 thousand tonnes of lyocell per year, yielding an expected project IRR of over 10.00%

9.
