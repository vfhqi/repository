§6 — Counter-Arguments to Bull Case

J-front: The strongest bear rebuttal targets the twin pillars of the bull case — Kineret durability and fitusiran peak sales — and argues both are materially overestimated; the bear case is coherent and well-evidenced on these dimensions. [#J]

  • Bull thesis #1: Kineret is a durable, defensible revenue base with multiple indication expansion opportunities:
  • Bear rebuttal #1: Kineret's core defensibility argument is patent/biosimilar protection, but its orphan drug exclusivity in key markets has been expiring or has expired; biosimilar anakinra entrants are in development in Europe
  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: Sobi Kineret biosimilar threats have been discussed in regulatory submissions; EU orphan drug exclusivity has market-specific expiry dates; the COVID MIS-C volume tailwind was one-time and non-recurring

  • Track record evidence: Kineret revenue growth decelerated materially post-2022 as MIS-C volumes normalised; this was predictable and predicted by bear-leaning analysts

  • Bull thesis #2: Fitusiran is a best-in-class haemophilia A/B therapy that will capture meaningful market share:

  • Bear rebuttal #2: Hemlibra (emicizumab) has already captured the majority of haemophilia A prophylaxis prescriptions; switching from an established, well-tolerated Roche product to fitusiran requires a compelling differentiation narrative that is not clearly established
  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: fitusiran's Phase 3 data (ATLAS programme) showed non-inferior but not clearly superior bleed reduction vs existing standard of care; thrombotic event signals in early Phase 3 created prescriber hesitancy

  • Competitive evidence: Hemlibra 2023 sales ~$3.5bn globally; fitusiran bears model peak sales of €300–500m vs bull case of €700m–€1bn+ — the range of outcomes is extraordinarily wide

  • Bull thesis #3: SOBI's rare disease commercial infrastructure creates a durable competitive moat:

  • Bear rebuttal #3: SOBI's commercial infrastructure in rare disease is real but undersized for the haemophilia franchise battle; Roche, Novo Nordisk, and Pfizer/BioMarin have fundamentally larger haemophilia commercial organisations
  • Evidence supporting rebuttal: SOBI's total global headcount (~4,000) vs Roche haemophilia franchise team alone (~1,000+ dedicated globally); resource asymmetry creates structural market share ceiling

  • Macro evidence: specialty pharma commercial moat arguments typically hold in sub-500 patient populations; haemophilia A is a 200,000+ patient disease globally, squarely in big-pharma competitive territory

§8 — Quality of Bear Evidence (rate the bear case strength)

J-front: The bear case on SOBI is predominantly fact-based on competitive dynamics and partially fact-based on accounting quality concerns; it is NOT a fraud-allegation bear case; overall quality of bear evidence is medium — well-reasoned on competition, thinner on valuation and governance. [#J]

  • Fact-based allegation — Kineret maturation: Substantiated — Kineret revenue growth deceleration post-COVID-era MIS-C is documented in company filings and consensus estimates; this is fact, not opinion [#A]

  • Filings evidence: SOBI annual reports show Kineret volume/revenue trend inflection post-2022; the COVID tailwind was always non-recurring and was disclosed as such

  • Fact-based allegation — fitusiran competitive position: Partially substantiated — Hemlibra's market leadership in haemophilia A is documented fact; fitusiran's differentiation relative to Hemlibra is a contested analytical question, not settled fact [#A]

  • Filings evidence: Roche's Hemlibra revenue disclosures confirm dominant market position; fitusiran Phase 3 ATLAS data is public

  • Bear inference: "fitusiran cannot displace Hemlibra" is opinion extrapolated from fact; the degree of penetration is genuinely uncertain

  • Fact-based allegation — non-GAAP gap is large: Substantiated — SOBI's intangibles amortisation exclusion from adjusted EBITDA is confirmed in financial statements; size of gap is documentable [#A]

  • Pattern evidence: gap has grown as acquisition pace has increased; this is structural, not manipulated

  • Opinion-based allegation — M&A overpayment: Partially substantiated at best — the Biogen haemophilia franchise acquisition price was widely described as full; subsequent commercial underperformance vs deal-model is documented but causation (overpayment vs execution) is contested [#A]

  • Opinion-based allegation — management quality: Speculation — assertions that SOBI management is not strong enough to execute the haemophilia market share gain are opinion; track record on Kineret franchise management has actually been solid [#A]

  • Speculation — gene therapy displacement timeline: Partially speculative — gene therapy for haemophilia A/B has been anticipated for a decade and timelines have repeatedly slipped; BioMarin (Roctavian) launched but uptake has been disappointing; bears using gene therapy as a near-term SOBI bear catalyst may be premature [#A]

  • Bear coherence: gene therapy displacement is a valid 5–10 year structural risk but a poor 18-month bear catalyst; credibility declines as near-term trigger

  • Refuted allegation — fitusiran safety is fatally compromised: Refuted by subsequent events — early Phase 3 thrombotic events triggered a clinical hold that was lifted after dose adjustment; Phase 3 ATLAS programme completed with an acceptable safety profile and regulatory approval was achieved [#A]
