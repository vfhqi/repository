§7 — Narrative + Market Story

  • Verdict: The dominant bull narrative is coherent but structurally dependent on US uncertainty resolution; the bear narrative is tighter and more falsifiable — unusual for a Buy-majority consensus [#J]

  • The 2023–2024 impairments were a US-specific aberration that cleaned up the balance sheet and reset expectations

  • Leverage (net debt/EBITDA approaching 4–5x at trough) creates balance sheet fragility if project delays extend
  • The European pipeline is real but pricing in construction cost headwinds that are structural, not cyclical

  • Dividend sustainability is a live question if FY25 FCF disappoints [#J]

  • Medium-term focus: FY27–FY28 EBITDA recovery trajectory, Hornsea 3 COD, and hydrogen/Power-to-X optionality (currently zero-valued by most bears, partially valued by bulls) [#A]
