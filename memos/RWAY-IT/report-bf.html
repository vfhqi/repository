EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Investment Thesis and Value Driver Transmission

Rai Way possesses highly predictable, inflation-indexed broadcasting revenues from a monopolistic national tower network while actively diversifying into regional digital infrastructure and edge data centers, occupying a highly insulated, high-barrier broadcasting core while positioning itself to capture Rome-Milan edge data center and solar-hedged digital infrastructure expansion. The company displays exceptional operator quality under CEO Roberto Cecatto , combining 100% dividend payouts with rigid, inflation-indexed structural moats protecting cash generation, leveraging its unique network of over 2,300 telecommunication towers that is practically impossible for competitors to replicate.

However, the business exhibits extreme customer concentration with parent company RAI contributing 84.00% of revenues under an exclusive Service Contract, though this is mitigated by structural inflation-indexed protection extending to 2028 with potential tacit renewals through 2035.

The financial profile reveals best-in-class EBITDA margins of 67.8% and exceptionally low leverage of 0.7x Net Debt/EBITDA , positioning Rai Way as a massive outlier compared to peer Cellnex at 6.3x and providing vast capital flexibility for inorganic expansion.

The company occupies a highly defensible position with strategic rural and suburban tower locations that are virtually impossible to replicate under modern zoning and environmental restrictions, creating durable physical moats and long-term contractual lock-in through June 30, 2028, with structural extensions to 2035.

Broadcasting Business Stability and Structural Protection

Italy represents a global structural anomaly where the complete absence of cable television makes linear terrestrial Digital Terrestrial Television (DTT) exceptionally resilient against OTT streaming platform encroachment.

The core broadcasting business generated €249.5 million in 2025, representing 88.2% of total core revenues , with the turnkey transmission services contract for parent RAI contributing €237.4 million or 84.0% of consolidated turnover , underpinned by long-term anchor contract protection through 2028.

Revenue indexation provides full CPI pass-through with almost all customer contracts 100% linked to CPI with no annual caps, with management estimating that every 1.00% increase in inflation translates to roughly €2.00 million of incremental EBITDA, providing a structural hedge against operating cost inflation.

The Service Contract features a tacit renewal clause for subsequent seven-year periods extending to June 30, 2035 unless terminated, with RAI's right of withdrawal before June 30, 2028, subject to the payment of substantial penalties, though no penalties apply for withdrawal after the 2028 renewal date.

High technical switching costs secure hosting volumes as relocating active radio and TV broadcasting equipment to a competing grid is highly complex and logistically constrained, with traditional revenue streams underpinned by the Service Contract running through June 30, 2028 with structural extensions to 2035 .

Digital Infrastructure Diversification and Execution Risks

Digital infrastructure revenues reached €33.3 million in 2025, up 4.1% year-on-year , with underlying third-party revenues growing 4.4% in 2025 and accelerating to 10.0% in the fourth quarter , serving third-party mobile operators, fixed wireless access providers, and private radio broadcasters.

Commercialization of the first 5 operational edge data centers generated €0.30 million in Q1 2026 compared to €0.10 million in Q1 2025, with direct commercial outreach engaging over 200 prospects , resulting in 30 submitted offers in 2025 representing a €5.80 million potential revenue backlog, of which €0.50 million has converted into signed contracts.

The Italian data center market is at a clear inflection point, growing from €7.50 billion in 2025 to €13.50 billion by 2030 at an expected compound annual growth rate of 11.00% to 12.00% , with total Italian IT load capacity projected to expand from 1.08 GW in 2025 to over 4.00 GW by 2030 , marking one of the fastest growth rates in continental Europe.

Rai Way obtained crucial municipal approvals in early 2026 to construct a modular 35.2 MW Tier IV hyperscale data center in Pomezia , positioned to capture Rome's public and enterprise cloud workloads. However, bureaucratic bottlenecks in Italian administrative processes have delayed critical hyperscale and solar project capex by one year, pushing the full EBITDA contribution of solar generation back to 2028, while enterprise edge computing demand remains nascent with corporate B2B clients prioritizing slow private cloud migration and simple proximity colocation over advanced low-latency applications.

Financial Performance and Capital Allocation Excellence

Rai Way achieved €282.8 million in 2025 core revenues with organic core growth reaching 4.4% year-on-year , while the operator achieved Adjusted EBITDA of €191.8 million , a 3.3% increase year-on-year, with Adjusted EBITDA margin expanding by 60 basis points to 67.8% compared to 67.2% in 2024. Total capital expenditures reached €52.1 million in 2025, representing 18.4% of core revenues , divided between €23.4 million for maintenance and €28.7 million for development , reflecting phased deployment of diversification projects.

Recurring free cash flow to equity was stable at approximately €118 million in 2025 , with cash conversion reaching 96.2% in 2024 , up from 94.0% in 2023, demonstrating outstanding capital efficiency.

Management maintains a consistent 100.00% dividend payout policy , proposing €88.60 million in total distributions for 2025 , with the proposed 33.00 cents per share dividend translating to an attractive 5.70% coupon yield , while fully selffunding active diversification capex.

Rai Way retains a highly conservative balance sheet, reporting extremely low net debt of €136.50 million with leverage of 0.70x Net Debt/EBITDA , massively lower than peer Cellnex at 6.30x Net Debt/EBITDA , retaining comfortable safety margins well below the restrictive 3.00x limit. 16

Competitive Landscape and Market Structure Dynamics

The long-standing consolidation discussions between Rai Way and EI Towers officially ended on July 1, 2026 , with the non-binding Memorandum of Understanding expiring without the parties finding a mutually agreed-upon basis for negotiations, though share prices declined only 5% to 6% on the news, reflecting moderate prior market expectations.

Italy remains characterized by a unique double national broadcasting tower infrastructure represented by the two operators, with EI Towers operating approximately 2,400 sites with €285 million in FY2023 revenues , with both groups running highly overlapping national networks that cover over 90% of the Italian population.

Rai Way faces intense competition from established global colocation providers and domestic cloud operators, with Equinix operating four high-performance data centers across Milan and Genoa, while Brookfield-backed Data4 is expanding rapidly with plans for a new 100MW facility in Vittuone, potentially controlling over 350MW of data center capacity in Italy upon completing Vittuone by 2027.

Major OTT platforms are bypassing commercial CDNs by deploying proprietary, zero-cost edge caching appliances, with Netflix having embedded over 18,000 Open Connect Appliances directly into local ISP networks globally, with these proprietary appliances caching video content locally overnight, eliminating the need for paid commercial transit .

Operator Quality and Foundation Strength Assessment

CEO Roberto Cecatto provides strong executive leadership, focusing on the execution of the 2024-2027 Industrial Plan, having successfully revamped the corporate structure in 2024 into three specialized, market-oriented business divisions, with the management team including CFO Adalberto Pellegrino and Chief Corporate Development Officer Giancarlo Benucci.

The business has delivered ten consecutive years of uninterrupted growth across all key financial metrics, with EBITDA increasing 60.00% over nine years post-IPO , demonstrating superior commercial execution and performance remaining highly resilient during downturns, outperforming cyclical sectors during periods of macroeconomic volatility.

The 2024-2027 Industrial Plan prioritizes growth initiatives, earmarking over half of development capex for diversification, with management targeting minimum internal rates of return of 10.00% on development capital investments, while new services to RAI generate roughly €0.20 million in recurring revenue per €1.00 million invested .

The unique network of over 2,300 telecommunication towers occupying strategic locations that cover suburban and rural areas is practically impossible for competitors to replicate, with Rai Way serving as the sole operator of the transmission networks carrying national public broadcaster RAI's signals, legally required to guarantee 99.00% population coverage for RAI's primary television multiplex.

However, extreme single-customer concentration and the parentsubsidiary governance structure expose the business foundation to severe institutional renegotiation risks upon the 2028 renewal, with the single largest risk identified as customer concentration and potential renegotiation of the RAI Service Contract , with early warning signals including any institutional changes in RAI's public service concession or legislative moves regarding national broadcasting frequency ownership.

Cross-Value-Chain Power Dynamics and Pricing Pressure

TIM Enterprise signals solid demand with over 65.00% of 2026 revenues already secured under multi-year contracts, though Inwit confirms conflictual behaviors from TIM and Swisscom , cutting its medium-term financial outlook significantly, with Swisscom alleging tower costs are above market levels and terminating its primary Inwit agreements.

Energy Cost Mitigation and Sustainability Initiatives

The company is deploying a large-scale solar panel project across its extensive land portfolio, with the 2024-2027 Industrial Plan allocating approximately €32.00 million to €35.00 million in development capex for solar initiatives, targeting a run-rate capacity of 40.00 MW to generate approximately 75.00 GWh/year , with 12.50 MW fully authorized across 4 key sites as of March 2026, expected to generate approximately €4.00 million in annual EBITDA at full run-rate in 2028.

Rai Way achieved Carbon Neutrality for its Scope 1 and market-based Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions in 2025 , extending this ESG baseline across all five active edge data centers, while deploying a €32.00 million, 40.00 MW solar panel project to mitigate volatile national electricity prices and secure €4.00 million in run-rate EBITDA by 2028.

Electricity price volatility remains a key risk to plant operating costs, exacerbated by geopolitical tensions, with lower raw energy prices of €106.00/MWh in 1H2024 offset by rising non-incentive tariffs, resulting in a total average energy tariff of €201.00/MWh versus €188.00/MWh in 1H2023 .

Long-Term Disruption Vectors and Structural Evolution

Expert insights indicate that Open RAN (O-RAN) and Virtual RAN (vRAN) technologies are currently in the early stages of commercial deployment globally , presenting no material threat to traditional tower hosting in the near to medium term, with high system integration costs and complexity delaying widespread MNO adoption, though former telecom executives suggest that O-RAN's shift toward software-defined networks will ultimately drive tower demand by allowing dynamic cell configuration and AI-driven network automation.

Major global TowerCos and mobile operators view Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite direct-to-device (D2D) services as a complementary coverage layer for remote, rural zones rather than a structural threat to terrestrial grids in densely populated suburban markets, with terrestrial 4G and 5G networks continuing to deliver significantly higher capacity, lower latency, and superior cost efficiency for urban and suburban consumer workloads relative to space-based constellations.

However, long permitting delays for grid reinforcement and a potential slowdown in enterprise cloud migrations could leave newly built edge and hyperscale data center assets underutilized, depressing return on invested capital.
