4.1 Sector and macro context

Solaria is a pure-play Spanish solar IPP (independent power producer) selling power under long-term PPAs and at merchant (spot) rates. Two structural forces converged sharply in 2026. First, Spanish solar capture prices fell to EUR 18/MWh as 397 hours of negative wholesale prices were recorded in Q1 2026 (vs 48 in Q1 2025) -- the canonical cannibalisation dynamic as Spain's solar base roughly doubled in 2024-25. S&P Global Ratings has flagged merchant-exposed IPPs as specifically at risk relative to integrated utilities. Second, ECB rate trajectory: rates held at 2.00% on 30 April 2026 but markets priced three 2026 hikes following Iran-related oil price volatility; each 25 bps hike reduces solar investment by approximately 5.3% per ECB-cited academic work. Against these headwinds, Solaria's data-centre pivot partially insulates the revenue profile: the EUR 300 million raise (29 April, 6.7x oversubscribed, priced at EUR 24) funds the "Powered Land" platform of renewable-powered hyperscaler sites, and the Merlin Properties deal (213 MW solar + 600 MWh BESS for Madrid data centre, 40-year PPA, signed March 2026) provides long-duration contracted revenue immune to spot cannibalisation.
