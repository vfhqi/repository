§8 — Value chain: Disruption

  • J-front verdict: Disruption risk is real but medium-term (3-5 year horizon), not immediate; Boku's LPM pivot is itself a hedge against DCB structural disruption [#J]
  • Aduna telco API disruption: Ericsson Aduna exposes billing triggers directly to developers; if adopted at scale, reduces need for DCB aggregators; 12-operator coverage launched 2025; adoption curve likely slow given developer/merchant integration inertia [#A]
  • Open Banking / A2A substitution: Open Banking-enabled A2A payments (e.g., UK Pay.UK, EU SEPA instant) reduce friction for bank-account-pull payments; Boku is adapting by incorporating A2A into its LPM network rather than resisting [#A]
  • Card scheme re-entry: Neal has directly invoked "replacing a 50-year payments infrastructure" — positioning Boku as long-term card-scheme challenger in EPI (European Payments Initiative) context; this is aspirational, not yet operational [#I]
  • App store payment disruption: Epic Games vs Apple/Google legal battles globally could force sideloading and alternative in-app payment routes; Boku historically benefits from app-store DCB but alternative in-app payments could cannibalise [#A]
  • Biometric/FIDO2 authentication displacement: In identity verification (sector Boku exited), SIM-based auth is being challenged by biometric/FIDO2 standards; Boku has no current exposure, but this shows the speed of disruption in adjacencies [#I]
  • Wallet consolidation risk: Digital wallet market may consolidate around 3-5 super-wallets per region (WeChat Pay, GrabPay, MoMo, Paytm); if consolidation happens, Boku's 200+ wallet catalogue becomes a smaller network [#A]
  • Sceptical note: DCB as a category is mature in developed markets; disruption risk is asymmetric — wallet/A2A upside if Boku executes, but DCB decline could accelerate if Aduna gains traction faster than consensus expects [#J]

§11 — Revenue/demand cycle

  • J-front verdict: Boku's revenue cycle is structurally counter-cyclical in EM (unbanked user DCB) and mildly cyclical in DM (digital subscription churn); net exposure is modest vs a pure DM subscription play [#J]
  • DCB cyclicality: DCB demand is tied to digital content (gaming, streaming, apps); gaming is more discretionary than streaming; in recessions, gaming MAU tends to rise (stay-at-home effect) but spending per user can compress [#A]
  • Streaming subscription resilience: Netflix, Spotify, Disney+ are Tier-1 Boku merchants; streaming subscription cancellation rates rise in cost-of-living squeezes (UK/EU 2022-2024 precedent); Boku revenue falls if subscribers cancel [#A]
  • EM insulation: 57% of DCB transactions from unbanked users; these users have no card alternative — DCB is the only digital payment method; recession sensitivity structurally lower for EM unbanked segment [#I]
  • Wallet cycle: Digital wallet adoption is a secular trend — GSMA projects 57% of global population using mobile wallets by 2025; secular adoption is not recession-sensitive in the medium term [#I]
  • Bundling subscription cycle: Telecom bundling (connectivity + subscription) insulates subscribers from churn — bundled subscribers cancel less than standalone subscribers; Boku's bundling segment benefits from this stickiness [#A]
  • Seasonality: H2 typically stronger than H1 for DCB (gaming seasonality, holiday content spend); FY2025 H2 implied revenue ($65.5m) vs H1 ($63.3m) confirms modest H2 weighting [#A]
  • Contract renewal cycle: Enterprise merchant contracts (Netflix, Spotify, Microsoft) likely have multi-year terms; renewal risk is concentrated; no disclosed contract expiry information but loss of a single Tier-1 merchant would be a material revenue event [#A]
  • Identity contract replacement: The Identity division generated recurring verification revenue before the 2022 Twilio sale; Boku replaced that recurring revenue stream with LPM expansion — the bundling segment (recurring subscription revenue) is the structural equivalent [#A]
  • Sceptical note: If US consumer spending slows materially (Morgan Stanley projects deceleration through 2026), Boku's gaming/streaming DCB volume could disappoint in H1 2026; EM growth may not fully offset DM softness [#J]
