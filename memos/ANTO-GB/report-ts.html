Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Tier-one asset quality and district scale support high operating margins and a durable competitive advantage in copper concentrates.

Core mining operations

1.
Flagship asset Pelambres: Los Pelambres produced 295.3 kt of copper in 2025 at an exceptionally low net cash cost of zero point eighty-two dollars per pound
2.
Low-cost Centinela concentrates: Centinela produced 240.4 kt of copper in 2025, achieving an outstanding net cash cost of zero point seventy-five dollars per pound
3.
High-cost cathode operations: Antucoya and Zaldívar are high-cost heap-leaching operations that produced 81.2 kt and 36.7 kt respectively in 2025
4.
Significant by-product contribution: In 2025, the group produced 211.3 koz of gold and 15.8 kt of molybdenum, generating $1,683.7 million in by-product revenues

Revenue model and value chain

5.
Toll-treatment revenue model: Concentrates are shipped to global smelters, where net revenues are deducted by treatment charges based on global refining terms
6.
By-product cost offsets: Molybdenum and gold revenues are treated as cash cost credits, which reduced 2025 cash costs by one point nineteen dollars per pound
7.
High customer concentration: Concentrates are sold under long-term contracts to a concentrated pool of global smelters, primarily located in East Asia
8.
Downstream value positioning: Antofagasta sits in a favorable upstream position, capturing high mining rents, while smelters suffer from tight refining charges

Moat and industry structure

9.
Extreme district scale: The group controls 20.7 Bt of mineral resources, providing a massive, highly secure multi-decade reserve runway for long-term growth
10.
Infrastructure competitive advantage: Owned ports, pipelines, and desalinated water systems create immense structural barriers to entry and lower logistics costs
11.
Consolidated industry structure: The copper mining industry is highly consolidated, dominated by giant producers like Freeport-McMoRan, Southern Copper, BHP, and Rio Tinto
12.
Other side of the story: Rising hauling distances and grade depletion at older pits threaten to structurally lift base cash costs before by-product offsets
