B.1 Business Description 9 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Pan-European and African converged telecoms incumbent. Orange serves 340 million customers across 26 countries, combining mobile, fixed-broadband and enterprise IT/cybersecurity services under a single converged brand
2.
France remains the single largest market but Spain has just been transformed. France generates approximately 43% of group sales, while the EUR 4.25bn MasOrange transaction makes Spain the group's second-largest European market
3.
Africa and the Middle East is the structural growth engine. The region contributes roughly 20% of consolidated revenue and has delivered eleven consecutive quarters of double-digit growth, led by mobile data and Orange Money financial services
4.
Financial character: mid-single-digit cash-flow growth on a conservative balance sheet. Group revenue reached EUR 40.4bn in 2025 (+0.9% comparable), EBITDAaL grew 3.8% comparably to EUR 12.5bn at a 31.0% margin
4.1
Detail: net financial debt was EUR 22.5bn (1.8x EBITDAaL) at end-2025, temporarily rising toward 2.4x to fund MasOrange before a 2.0x 2028 target
5.
Vertically integrated infrastructure owner, unusual within the cohort. Orange retains full ownership of its European tower assets via Totem (approximately 27,000 masts across France and Spain), unlike Vodafone or Telefonica, which divested passive infrastructure
6.
Segment mix spans consumer, enterprise and financial services. Orange Cyberdefense generated EUR 1.3bn in 2025 revenue against a EUR 2.0bn by 2030 management target, while Orange Business (legacy B2B) continues to shrink structurally
7.
Corporate-action-rich near-term perimeter. The binding MasOrange transaction sits alongside optional upside from a EUR 20.35bn Orange-Bouygues-Iliad consortium bid for Altice France, creating a rare density of concurrent M&A catalysts for an incumbent of this scale
8.
Leadership fully refreshed but with real churn friction. Chief executive Christel Heydemann has solidified control since 2022, and a new Chairman took office in May 2026
8.1
Detail: Against this, 60% top-300 manager rotation is a RARE outlier versus the sector's 15-25% norm
