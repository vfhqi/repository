EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

1.
Investment Verdict: Draegerwerk represents a medium-conviction self-help turnaround story anchored on credible profitability-first portfolio restructuring and high-margin defense scaling, but sustained mid-term margin expansion above historical ceilings remains constrained by persistent medical segment competitive deficits, unresolved US regulatory overhang, and structural hospital purchasing consolidation favoring larger integrated peers

Strategic and Operational Change Signal

The company has executed a decisive strategic pivot from volume-led growth to profitability-first portfolio optimization over the past twenty-four months. Management shifted corporate focus from pure sales growth to profitability-first, adjusting or discontinuing underperforming non-strategic activities and enforcing reinforced pricing policies . This transformation manifests through discontinuing underperforming activities like Dräger MSI flue gas analysis and selling non-core Dutch fire alarm business in 2024 , generating €32 million in one-off EBIT from asset sales . Organizational restructuring includes splitting Europe, separating Germany, and merging Middle East/Africa with Europe to form new EMEA and APAC regions , alongside closing eight out of

seventeen office locations by 2026 to generate €1 million in annual rental savings . The forward innovation agenda prioritizes shifting capital allocation from transaction-oriented sales to interoperable connected systems based on the international SDC standard , exemplified by launching the Silent Care Package in late 2025, the first interoperable system reducing ICU noise pollution . However, skeptical perspectives highlight that Dräger lacks competitive strength in patient monitoring solutions relative to comprehensive larger suppliers , creating material execution risk around the clinical technology roadmap .

Activist Presence Assessment

No active activist presence exists in the AS library for Draegerwerk . The absence of visible activist engagement reflects the company's concentrated family governance structure , where Stefan Dräger restructured his holdings on December 15, 2025, holding the general partner directly instead of through a company . This ownership architecture creates intense concentration of family power that prevents independent board oversight, as Stefan Dräger directly controls the general partner . Executive alignment incentives remain modest, with Executive Board members holding only 0.578% of preferred shares and 1.548% of common shares as of late 2025 . The insulation from external activist pressure removes a potential catalyst for accelerated strategic portfolio rationalization or aggressive margin expansion targets beyond management's self-imposed roadmap. 29 30
