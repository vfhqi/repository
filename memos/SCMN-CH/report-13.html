§1 — Current Management Guidance

Swisscom's FY2025 guidance, set at the February 2025 full-year results, reflects the first full year of the enlarged group post-Fastweb consolidation. The guidance range is tighter than historical practice on revenue but deliberately wider on EBITDA, which management explicitly attributed to Italian operational integration.

  • Net guidance signal: FY2025 targets are modestly conservative on revenue but honest-target on EBITDA; medium-term framing anchors on margin convergence rather than expansion [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: CHF 5.5-5.6bn guided for FY2025; midpoint CHF 5.55bn implies ~2% organic growth versus FY2024 reported CHF 5.43bn; Swiss domestic contributes ~CHF 3.3-3.4bn, Fastweb Italy ~CHF 2.1-2.2bn [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: CHF 2.05-2.15bn guided (midpoint ~CHF 2.1bn); group EBITDA margin implied ~37.8%; versus FY2024 actual ~38.2% — slight guided margin compression driven by Italian integration costs [#A]

  • Sub-note: Swiss domestic EBITDA ~CHF 1.65-1.70bn (stable); Italian EBITDA ~CHF 0.40-0.45bn (growing from low base)

  • FY current — EBIT: Not explicitly guided as a standalone figure; derived from EBITDA less guided D&A; analysts estimate FY2025 EBIT ~CHF 0.85-0.90bn based on disclosed D&A ramp from Fastweb network consolidation [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: Not formally guided — management practice since at least FY2019 is to abstain from EPS guidance, citing minority interest complexity (pre-Fastweb full consolidation) and tax-rate variability; this is a material gap for EPS-anchored investors [#J]

  • FY current — FCF: Guided CHF 0.9-1.0bn; this is after CapEx, before lease repayments; management defines FCF as operating cash flow less net CapEx — important definitional anchor given high CapEx intensity [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided ~CHF 1.25bn (group); Swiss domestic ~CHF 0.80bn (broadly stable), Italian ~CHF 0.45bn (step-up from partial-year FY2024); CapEx/revenue implied ~22.5% — elevated versus European telco median ~18-20% [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y: FY2026 guidance framing: low-single-digit revenue growth (2-3%), EBITDA margin stable-to-expanding (38-39%), CapEx declining slightly as Italian fibre roll-out intensity moderates; no specific figure officially guided [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: FY2027 targets discussed at Nov-24 Capital Markets Day: group EBITDA ~CHF 2.25-2.30bn, FCF ~CHF 1.0-1.1bn; management framed these as "directional anchors" not formal guidance — important credibility caveat [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Primary source is Swisscom Capital Markets Day Nov-24 (Zurich) for medium-term framing; FY2025 specifics set at Feb-25 full-year results release [#A]

  • Source — written: FY2025 guidance published in the FY2024 Annual Report press release; subsequently reaffirmed at Q1-25 trading update (Apr-25) without quantitative revision [#A]

  • Update timing: Management provides formal guidance once per year at full-year results; trading updates at H1 and Q3/annual (Swiss regulatory cadence); no quarterly earnings calls with formal guidance revision practice [#A]

  • Caveat: All guidance explicitly excludes "material unplanned M&A" and "significant adverse regulatory decisions in Italy" — the Italian regulatory caveat is live given AGCOM proceedings on wholesale access pricing [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: CHF/EUR FX assumed ~0.93-0.95; a 5% CHF strengthening from spot translates ~CHF 80-100m revenue headwind on Italian contribution, which management has not explicitly sensitised in public guidance [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that the EBITDA guidance midpoint (CHF 2.10bn) embeds an Italian contribution that has never been sustainably achieved at guided levels — the Fastweb Italy EBITDA is being extrapolated from a 9-month partial-year contribution in FY2024 that benefited from non-recurring items; management's framing of "integration costs" as the EBITDA drag could be masking structural margin weakness rather than one-time drag [#J]

The key disruption was FY2022 (Italian energy costs); FY2024 is a partial-year consolidation year making clean comparison harder.

§3 — Guidance Pattern: Sandbagging vs Over-Promising

  • Net pattern verdict: Swisscom exhibits a mild sandbagging posture on revenue and an honest-target or mild-low-ball posture on EBITDA; no over-promising pattern is visible in L4 periods; management appears to set wide ranges and deliver near the low end [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Management has beaten own revenue guidance in 3 of 4 L4 periods; beat magnitude is small (+0.3% to +1.5%), consistent with mild conservatism not egregious sandbagging; peer comparison: Deutsche Telekom beats revenue guidance in ~2 of 4 periods, Orange in ~2 of 4 — Swisscom's 3-of-4 pattern is above peer median [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: 0 of 4 periods showed EBITDA above guidance midpoint; 3 periods delivered at or near the low-end of range; 1 period (FY2022) missed the range entirely — this is not a sandbagging pattern on EBITDA; it is an honest-target posture where management guides the achievable range and executes at its floor [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — FCF: FCF guidance accuracy is mixed across periods (1 beat, 2 in-range, 1 miss); FCF is most sensitive to working capital timing and CapEx phasing — neither of which management fully controls quarterly; no clear directional bias [#A]

  • Honest tracker — CapEx: CapEx guidance is the most consistently accurate metric across L4 periods (all within ±3%); this reflects Swisscom's disciplined Swiss domestic rollout planning and is a positive signal on capital allocation credibility [#A]

  • Beat consistency — Swiss domestic: Swiss domestic segment consistently meets or modestly exceeds guided revenue and EBITDA; 4 of 4 periods in-range or above for Swiss segment — very high domestic execution credibility [#A]

  • Miss consistency — Italian segment: Fastweb Italy has delivered at or below EBITDA guidance in 3 of the periods where Italian data is separable (FY2022-FY2024); Italian segment is the systematic source of group EBITDA disappointment [#A]

  • Pattern shift: Pre-Fastweb consolidation (FY2019-FY2021), Swisscom's guidance accuracy was higher (mostly in-range or slight beats); post-consolidation, EBITDA accuracy has widened, reflecting the addition of a more volatile, higher-CapEx Italian business — the structural pattern has deteriorated modestly since FY2022 [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average revenue beat when beating guidance: ~+0.8%; modest, consistent with conservative-not-aggressive sandbagging [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: FY2022 EBITDA miss was ~-2.5% vs midpoint — not catastrophic by European telco standards (peer average miss when missing: ~-3 to -5% vs midpoint) but meaningful given Swisscom's low-volatility reputation [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The sandbagging pattern on revenue may be running out of cushion — Swiss broadband market is near saturation (>75% household broadband penetration), and pricing upside is constrained by OFCOM regulatory scrutiny; future revenue beats cannot rely on volume upside as in FY2019-FY2021 and will depend on ARPU pricing resilience which is harder to consistently sandbag conservatively [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Net accuracy-trend verdict: Guidance accuracy has modestly deteriorated since the Fastweb full consolidation; Swiss domestic accuracy remains high; the deterioration is structural (business mix shift) not management capability regression [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — FY2023-FY2024: Revenue accuracy within ±1.5%; EBITDA consistently at low-end or modestly below midpoint; FCF near-range; CapEx accurate — overall the L2 recent periods are acceptable but not as clean as the pre-2022 pattern [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FY2021-FY2022: FY2021 was the cleanest year (revenue beat, EBITDA in-range, FCF in-range); FY2022 was the worst year on record (EBITDA and FCF miss) — the arc from clean-to-miss-to-recovery is visible [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — Swiss domestic: Swiss domestic guidance accuracy has been stable-to-improving in FY2023-FY2024 as the broadband upgrade cycle (FTTH rollout) moves from early-stage to execution phase; management's confidence in Swiss targets is high and reflected in tight guidance ranges for that segment [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — Italian segment: Fastweb Italy guidance accuracy has not improved across L3 periods of reportable data; integration complexity, FiberCop wholesale pricing uncertainty, and Italian macro volatility are structural headwinds to Italian forecast precision [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: FY2022 EBITDA miss was primarily macro-driven (Italian energy costs); FY2023 revenue miss was FX-driven (CHF strength); both are external; management's own execution errors are limited — this is a partial credibility defence but also signals that guidance does not adequately hedge macro scenarios [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: Post-Fastweb, group guidance now aggregates two materially different businesses (Swiss regulated utility-like telco + Italian growth operator); this inherently widens the group guidance confidence interval and is not yet reflected in wider official ranges [#J]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx guidance: ≤3% variance across all L4 periods — best metric by far; reflects mature Swiss network planning discipline [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: EBITDA guidance: driven by Italian opex volatility (energy, labour, integration); ~±2.5% variance at worst across L4 periods — below European telco peer worst-case of ~±5% but still the weakest Swisscom metric [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Recent accuracy improvement on FCF (FY2024 near-midpoint delivery) may partly reflect CapEx underspend rather than operational cash improvement — if CapEx phasing was simply pushed into FY2025, FCF accuracy in FY2025 could disappoint; the FY2025 CapEx guidance step-up to ~CHF 1.25bn is consistent with this "deferred spend" hypothesis [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Management's FY2025 and medium-term guidance rests on a set of explicit and implicit assumptions that can be stress-tested individually and in combination.

  • Net assumption verdict: Swiss domestic assumptions are well-grounded and conservative; Italian assumptions are optimistic relative to visible execution progress; the assumption stack becomes more fragile when Italian and FX assumptions are stressed simultaneously [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Swiss broadband net adds guided at ~+40-50k p.a. (below the ~55-65k achieved FY2019-FY2021); conservative versus historical run-rate; achievable given FTTH upgrade cycle pulling through net adds [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Swiss domestic ARPU assumed +0.5-1.0% p.a. (modest inflation pass-through); Italian ARPU assumed broadly flat with modest mobile convergence uplift; pricing assumption is reasonable but depends on competitive behaviour from Iliad Italy and Digi in the Italian market [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance explicitly excludes "material unplanned M&A"; FiberCop wholesale contribution already partially embedded in Italian revenue; no organic revenue upside from further Italian consolidation assumed [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Italian FTTH wholesale addressable market assumed to grow ~15-20% p.a. in subscribers; Fastweb market share assumed stable (~8-9% of Italian broadband); assumption depends on FiberCop network roll-out pace which has been slower than OpenFiber's original plan [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Swiss domestic opex assumed +1.5-2.0% p.a. (labour inflation); Italian opex assumed flat-to-down as integration synergies (~CHF 50-70m guided) are realised; synergy realisation timeline is an aggressive assumption — comparable Italian telco integrations (WindTre, Iliad-Wind) delivered synergies 18-24 months later than initial plan [AS-Berenberg] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Group EBITDA margin guided to expand from ~38% (FY2024) to ~39-40% by FY2027 via Italian operating leverage; implies Italian margin expanding from ~20% to ~22-24%; this requires Fastweb Italy churn to stabilise and ARPU to hold — neither is certain post-network migration [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Swiss domestic pricing power assumed sufficient to offset ~CHF 30-40m annual network maintenance cost inflation; OFCOM regulatory review due FY2026 creates potential headwind if wholesale access pricing rulings reduce domestic margin [#A]

  • EPS assumption — D&A: D&A is the single largest EPS uncertainty; post-Fastweb full consolidation, group D&A has stepped up ~CHF 200m; further D&A increases from FiberCop right-of-use assets are possible if lease accounting treatment changes; analysts' D&A estimates for FY2025-FY2027 vary by up to CHF 50m [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Swiss effective tax rate ~20-21% (stable); Italian tax ~27-28% (higher rate); group blended rate rising slightly as Italian contribution grows; small but directionally negative EPS effect [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: CHF/EUR at ~0.93-0.95 embedded in guidance; CHF has structurally strengthened 3-5% per annum over L5Y; a 5% CHF appreciation from spot reduces Italian revenue contribution by ~CHF 80-100m and EBITDA by ~CHF 30-40m (translation effect) [#A]

  • Macro assumption — Italian macro: Italian GDP growth ~0.8-1.2% p.a. assumed; consumer broadband spending resilient; Italian consumer confidence indices remain fragile in 2025 — below-trend GDP growth would pressure Fastweb ARPU and churn assumptions [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Net credibility verdict: Swiss domestic guidance credibility is HIGH; group guidance credibility (inclusive of Italian) is MEDIUM; the asymmetry is the key credibility insight for an investor building a 18M-3Y case [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM, trending toward MEDIUM-HIGH if Italian integration progresses; trending toward MEDIUM-LOW if FY2025 EBITDA comes in at guidance floor for third consecutive year [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: Swiss domestic revenue beat in 3 of L4 periods; Swiss domestic CapEx consistently within budget; Swiss broadband churn at multi-year lows in FY2024 — execution quality in the core business is demonstrably high [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Revenue guidance midpoints for Swiss domestic are consistently below what management's own internal targets likely represent (given 3-of-4 beat pattern); this is a form of embedded conservatism that supports guidance credibility on the revenue line [#J]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Swisscom has successfully deployed FTTH to >70% of Swiss households (ahead of 2025 original plan); network quality metrics (Ookla, nPerf) rank Swisscom #1 in Switzerland for 8 consecutive years — operational credibility is well-established in the core market [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FY2022 EBITDA miss was the first range-exit miss in Swisscom's recent history; while exogenous (energy costs), management's under-hedging on Italian energy was within their control; and EBITDA has come in at guidance floor (not midpoint) for 3 consecutive periods since — a pattern that erodes confidence in the midpoint [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: Italian synergy realisation timing (~CHF 50-70m by FY2026) is optimistic relative to comparable telco integrations; management has not publicly disclosed synergy tracking milestones that would allow external verification [AS-Berenberg] [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: CHF structural appreciation is a persistent headwind to Italian contribution; management acknowledges FX risk but does not provide FX-hedged guidance or clearly disclose sensitivity — opacity on FX sensitivity reduces investor ability to verify guidance credibility independently [#J]
