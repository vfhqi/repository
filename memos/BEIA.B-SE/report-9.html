§2 — Quality of Earnings

  • Quality verdict: Earnings quality is broadly clean for BEIA.B — adjustments are modest and transparent, cash conversion is consistently strong, and there is no pattern of recurring "one-off" charges inflating adjusted EPS [#J]

  • Recurring vs one-off: Total disclosed one-off items across L3Y are small: ~SEK 55m of restructuring charges (Habia, FY 2023–H1 2024); no asset disposal gains, no pension gains, no material M&A accounting benefits distorting headline EPS [#A]

  • Sub: SEK 55m vs L3Y cumulative EBIT of ~SEK 3,815m implies one-offs represent <1.5% of aggregate profit — negligible distortion
  • Adjustments trend: The Non-GAAP / reported EPS gap is narrow and stable across L3Y — largest gap is ~SEK 0.10 (FY 2023), reflecting the Habia restructuring charge only [#A]
  • Sub: No trend toward widening adjustments; no evidence of management "adjusting away" deterioration
  • Cash conversion: OCF vs EBIT — Beijer Alma historically converts ~85–90% of EBIT to operating cash flow; L3Y average estimated at ~87%, above the ~80% base-rate for Nordic industrial peers (no cohort data provided; base-rate from general Nordic industrials experience) [#A]
  • Sub: FY 2023 cash conversion dipped slightly as working capital built during the destocking period, but recovered in H1 2024
  • NWC tailwind/headwind: H1 2023 saw a NWC headwind as receivables extended (customers slowing payments during destocking) and inventory held up; this reversed through H2 2023 / H1 2024 as destocking completed [#A]
  • Sub: Net NWC movement over L3Y is roughly neutral — no artificial earnings flattery via payables extension
  • Receivables turn: Stable at approximately 50–55 days throughout L3Y; no deterioration suggesting channel stuffing or aggressive revenue recognition [#A]
  • D&A trend: D&A rising modestly (~SEK 135m in FY 2022 to ~SEK 155m in FY 2024) reflecting organic CapEx; not a sign of aggressive accounting — EBITDA-to-EBIT bridge is clean [#A]
  • OCF vs NI: OCF consistently exceeds reported net income across L3Y, with D&A and stable NWC the main positive reconciling items; no material divergences flagging accrual inflation [#A]
  • Non-GAAP gap discipline: Beijer Alma does not prominently market an "adjusted EPS" that systematically excludes ordinary business costs; the adjustments that do appear are clearly labelled restructuring items with defined endpoints [#A]

  • Conservative accounting signal: Lesjöfors carries significant goodwill (~SEK 1.8–2.0bn) and intangibles from serial spring-company acquisitions; impairment tests run annually; no impairments taken in L3Y, suggesting either genuine value preservation or a potential future risk if automotive end-markets structurally weaken [#I]

  • ❌ NWC headwind (H1 2023): Working capital consumed ~SEK 80–100m of cash in H1 2023 vs typical NWC release in H1; while temporary, it flagged demand weakness before P&L reflected the full extent [Cross-ref: §1]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

  • Margin verdict: Margin compression is real but partly cyclical — the FY 2022 peak margin (~14.4% EBIT) was itself above-normal, and the current ~12.4% level likely represents a sustainable mid-cycle floor, not a structural deterioration trend [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: L3Y (FY 2021–FY 2024) revenue CAGR ~+5.2%; broadly in line with Nordic industrial revenue growth norms of 4–7% across cycles (base-rate; no cohort peer provided) [#A]

  • Profit CAGR: L3Y (FY 2021–FY 2024) EBIT CAGR approximately flat to slightly negative; profit CAGR has underperformed revenue CAGR, implying negative operating leverage over the full L3Y window [#A]
  • Sub: This is the inverse of the typical industrial thesis where growth should produce leverage; driven by FY 2022 peak being the base problem
  • Operating leverage: In FY 2022 (expansion phase), EBIT grew ~25%+ on ~22% revenue growth — positive leverage present in up-cycle; in FY 2023 (contraction phase), EBIT fell ~24% on ~8% revenue decline — negative leverage in down-cycle, confirming semi-fixed cost structure [#A]
  • Gross margin: Stable in a narrow band — approximately 27.0–28.0% throughout L3Y; no structural gross margin deterioration; commodity input pass-through appears effective at Lesjöfors; Habia Cable margin dilution partially offset [#A]
  • Sub: Gross margin resilience suggests pricing power at the product level; EBIT compression is primarily an OpEx/fixed-cost issue
  • EBITDA margin: Compressed from ~16.0% (FY 2022) to ~13.8–13.9% (FY 2023/2024); ~215bps contraction; approximately half from Habia Cable de-leverage, half from Lesjöfors volume softness [#A]
  • Cost cut driver: The Habia Cable restructuring (FY 2023–H1 2024) was explicitly cost-reduction focused — targeted SEK 40–50m of annualised cost savings; H1 2024 margin uptick (+20bps EBIT margin vs H1 2023) reflects early benefit [#A]
  • Mix shift: Habia Cable, the lower-margin segment, grew its revenue share in 2022 via defence/energy demand, then contracted disproportionately; if cable re-accelerates, mix will be a slight margin drag relative to springs-only recovery [#A]
  • Scale driver: Lesjöfors has a credible scale story — >50 acquisitions since IPO; plant density in Europe creates distribution and manufacturing scale advantages; but this has not translated to margin expansion in L3Y given automotive softness [#A]

  • Sustainability assessment: The 12–13% EBIT margin range appears sustainable as a mid-cycle floor; the 14–15% range is achievable in a favourable demand environment; 16%+ (FY 2022) required exceptional conditions unlikely to repeat near-term [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? If automotive end-markets structurally weaken under EV transition (EV spring content per vehicle is lower than ICE), Lesjöfors faces a structural volume headwind rather than a cyclical one; simultaneously if defence cable demand softens post-conflict, Habia Cable loses its recovery catalyst; the combination could keep margins structurally at 11–12%, below current consensus assumptions [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

  • Seasonality verdict: Modest but consistent H2-weighting in both revenue and EBIT; pattern is structural (H2 benefiting from customer budget cycles and full construction season), not changing materially over L3Y [#J]

  • H1 pattern: H1 consistently represents ~48–49% of full-year revenue and ~46–47% of full-year EBIT; H1 EBIT margin structurally ~50–80bps below H2 margin across L3Y [#A]

  • Sub: H1 2022 (SEK 4,285m revenue) vs FY 2022 (SEK 8,630m) = 49.7% H1 share — consistent with pattern
  • H2 pattern: H2 benefits from: (a) spring-company customers fulfilling annual volumes before year-end; (b) infrastructure/construction activity peaking in Nordic summer into autumn; (c) cable customers deploying capex budgets H2 [#A]
  • Strongest quarter: H2 in aggregate is strongest — but BEIA.B doesn't report Q3/Q4 separately so intra-H2 cadence is opaque [#I]
  • Weakest quarter: H1 is structurally weaker, particularly Q1 (Nordic winter = low construction and automotive production seasonality) [#A]
  • Seasonality direction: Pattern has been stable throughout L3Y; the H2/H1 split has not widened or narrowed materially; no evidence of seasonality changing [#A]
  • Cause — M&A timing: Lesjöfors acquisitions are typically completed H1 (when valuations are being negotiated post prior-year results); this adds a slight acquisition-timing H2 skew to organic growth comparisons [#I]

  • Analytical implication: H1 period results consistently read as softer than full-year trajectory would imply; risk of over-interpreting H1 weakness as a new trend, as has arguably happened with H1 2023 market reaction [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? If automotive production shifts further toward EV (which has lower seasonality given OEM production smoothing), Lesjöfors's H2 volume boost from automotive restocking could structurally diminish; this would erode the H2 revenue kicker and make earnings more linearly weak rather than back-half-loaded-strong [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on management credibility? §6 — Per-Period Narrative

  • Per-period verdict: The dominant narrative across L3Y is a boom-bust-recovery arc; FY 2022 exceptional, FY 2023 materially negative, FY 2024 early-stage recovery — the per-period story is macro-driven more than company-specific [#J]

H1 2022 — Strong beat; demand boom fully underway

  • Positive surprise: Revenue +22.9% YoY, EPS beat consensus ~+6%; Lesjöfors order books at record levels; Habia Cable benefiting from fibre rollout and early defence cable demand [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: No change to M&A acquisition pace; Lesjöfors completed 2 bolt-on acquisitions in H1 2022; management described capacity as "stretched" — a positive signal [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Gross margin stability at ~28% confirmed analyst expectations of pricing power amid steel cost inflation [#A]

FY 2022 — Record year; seeds of normalisation visible

  • Positive surprise: Full-year revenue beat (~+2.7%); record EPS SEK 10.88; highest delivered EPS in company history to this point [#A]
  • Negative surprise (embedded): Management's year-end commentary flagged "inventory build at customers" and "some ordering ahead of need" — early destocking signal that sell-side underweighted [#A]
  • Strategic pivot: Habia Cable restructuring plan first mentioned; management acknowledged telecom capex was plateauing [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Shift from "exceptional demand" to "normalisation expected" — a materially more cautious register than H1 2022 [#A]

H1 2023 — Miss; destocking hits harder than guided

  • Negative surprise: Revenue -7.1% YoY, EPS missed consensus by -6.9%; sharpest negative deviation in L3Y window; both Lesjöfors and Habia Cable declined simultaneously [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation (negative): The destocking that management had flagged at FY 2022 materialised; what surprised was the depth and simultaneity across both segments [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: First explicit reference to Habia Cable restructuring timeline and cost; tone shifted to "managing through the cycle" from "executing the growth strategy" [#A]
  • Sub: [AS-Carnegie] noted the H1 2023 call was the most defensively-toned in recent history; management guided to "sequential improvement" without quantifying it
  • M&A: Lesjöfors acquisition cadence slowed to 1 bolt-on in H1 2023 vs 2–3 per half-year in 2021–2022 [#A]

FY 2023 — Modest beat on adjusted basis; restructuring executing

  • Positive surprise (modest): Full-year EPS beat ~+3.4% on adjusted basis despite revenue decline of 8.2%; cost discipline at Lesjöfors partially offset volume de-leverage [#A]
  • Negative surprise: Habia Cable restructuring charge (~SEK 35m) reduced reported EPS vs adjusted; investors focused on adjusted but noted the cash cost was real [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: Management explicitly guided to "recovery expected in H2 2024" for Habia Cable driven by defence and energy cable demand; this was a more specific forward signal than typical [#A]
  • Customer win / tailwind: Defence cable demand (primarily Scandinavian defence procurement and NATO-adjacent) cited as emerging tailwind for Habia — first time defence was foregrounded in earnings commentary [#A]

H1 2024 — Slight beat; recovery embryonic

  • Positive surprise: EPS beat consensus +4.9% on adjusted basis; revenue essentially flat YoY (+1.8%) but ahead of low buy-side expectations [#A]
  • Sub: [AS-Handelsbanken] characterised H1 2024 as "confirmation of trough, not yet recovery start" in their post-results note
  • Confirmed expectation: Habia Cable restructuring largely complete; cost savings beginning to show in margins (+20bps EBIT margin YoY) [#A]
  • Negative surprise (embedded): Lesjöfors automotive volumes remained soft; no material recovery in traditional spring demand despite macro stabilisation [#A]
  • Tone shift: Management upgraded from "managing through the cycle" to "cautious optimism"; defence cable demand quantified as double-digit growth in that sub-segment [#A]

FY 2024 — Stabilisation confirmed; recovery visible but gradual

  • Positive surprise: Modest consensus beat (~+4.1% EPS); revenue +4.2% YoY — first positive growth period since H1 2022 [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Defence cable and energy infrastructure cable driving Habia Cable revenue recovery; Lesjöfors seeing gradual automotive volume return [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: "Cautious optimism" maintained; specific mention of multi-year European defence investment programmes as structural tailwind for Habia Cable [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-ABG Sundal Collier] upgraded to Buy post-FY 2024, citing the defence cable cycle as underappreciated by consensus

  • Sceptical bullet: Across the per-period narrative, the dominant bearish thread is that every positive development in H2 2023 / FY 2024 relies on defence/energy cable demand at Habia — a single exogenous macro driver; the Lesjöfors recovery narrative (automotive normalisation) remains unconfirmed; if both catalysts slip simultaneously, the FY 2025 recovery thesis has no internal fallback [#J]

§8 — Forward Plausibility

  • Forward plausibility verdict: The L3Y track record provides partial support for a recovery thesis but does NOT support 25%+ IRR from current levels without multiple re-rating — organic EPS growth of 15–20% CAGR from FY 2024 is required and is at the upper end of what L3Y patterns imply [#J]

  • Track-record support: L3Y EPS CAGR is negative (FY 2021 to FY 2024); the forward thesis requires a positive inflection; this inflection is plausible but not evidenced in the historical delivery record [#A]

  • Forward EPS implied: AS consensus implies FY 2025E EPS ~SEK 9.00–9.50, FY 2026E ~SEK 10.50–11.00; this implies ~20–25% EPS growth FY 2024–FY 2026E (2Y CAGR ~10–12%) [#A]
  • Sub: At a P/E of ~18–20x (BEIA.B's typical range), 10–12% EPS CAGR would generate ~10–12% earnings-driven return before multiple change — well short of 25% IRR without re-rating
  • Required CAGR: To achieve 25% IRR from current levels (assuming flat multiple), EPS CAGR of ~20%+ over 3 years is required; consensus implies ~10–12%; the gap between required and consensus is significant [#J]
