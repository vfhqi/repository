Preamble: Key Data Anchors

FY2025 results announced 24 February 2026. Revenue £1,699.4m (+4.4% reported, +6.6% constant currency). Adjusted PBT £276.2m (+8.4% vs FY2024 £260.0m), landing at the upper end of guidance range. Adjusted operating profit £295.3m (+5.6% vs FY2024 £279.8m), slightly ahead of sell-side consensus of ~£292m. Adjusted EPS 146.20p vs FY2024 142.60p (+2.5%). Volume the primary growth driver: +9.6% organic volume growth. Management formally ended the "transition period" and introduced a 2028 financial framework. Q1 2026 trading: group sales £431m, +1% at constant currency, described as in line with guidance.

§1 — FY2025 Headline Results vs Expectations

  • J: FY2025 was a modest beat on profit; revenue in line; the result ended a three-year earnings deterioration cycle. [#J]
  • Revenue £1,699.4m: +4.4% reported, +6.6% constant currency; broadly in line with sell-side expectations at the time. [#I]
  • Adjusted PBT £276.2m vs guided range: management had guided for PBT toward the upper end of market expectations; the outturn delivered exactly that, described as "in line with guidance" in CEO statement (Croda Annual Report 2025). [#A]
  • Adjusted operating profit £295.3m vs consensus ~£292m: a modest £3m beat (~1%); not a blowout but directionally positive (HL Research, 24 Feb 2026). [#A]
  • ⚡ Volume recovery +9.6% organic: the single largest positive surprise in FY2025; pure volume-driven recovery after three years of destocking-induced collapse. [#J]
  • Adjusted EPS 146.20p vs FY2024 142.60p: only +2.5% growth despite PBT +8.4%; reflects higher interest charges on the debt stack and ongoing dilution from the cost base; still well below peak EPS of ~230p+ in 2022. [#A]
  • EBITDA £397m (+5% vs FY2024); EBIT margin 17.4% vs 17.2% in FY2024: marginal improvement, not yet meaningful margin recovery. [#I]

Prior profit warning history (2022–2024):
- 2022: peak year, adjusted PBT ~£496m; revenue £2.09bn; this was the post-COVID mRNA/pandemic-preparedness windfall peak. [#I]
- June 2023 (Warning 1): guidance cut to £370–400m adjusted PBT vs £496m; shares fell ~14% on the day; driver was accelerating customer destocking across Consumer Care and Crop Protection (Sharecast, Jun 2023). [#I]
- October 2023 (Warning 2): guidance cut again to £300–320m; Crop Protection destocking accelerated faster than anticipated; shares crashed to multi-year lows (MarketScreener, Oct 2023). [#I]
- FY2023 outturn: adjusted PBT £236.3m — a 52% decline from 2022 peak; the trough. [#I]
- FY2024 outturn: adjusted PBT £260.0m (+10% from trough); EPS 142.6p; described as another "transition year" by CEO; Life Sciences still pressured by absence of COVID lipid revenues. [#I]
- ❌ The cumulative scale of the decline — PBT more than halving from peak to trough — meant that FY2025's £276m, while genuinely improving, remains 44% below the 2022 peak; recovery is real but still partial. [#J]

§2 — Divisional Performance

  • J: Both Consumer Care and Life Sciences recovered in FY2025, but the quality of recovery differs materially — Consumer Care is more structural; Life Sciences is partly one-cycle (Crop Protection) with Pharma still fragile. [#J]

Consumer Care:
- Sales +8% at constant currency in FY2025; adjusted operating margins improved; profit grew. [#I]
- Key growth driver: Fragrances and Flavours (F&F) +15% at constant currency, outperforming peers; F&F is the Iberchem/Parfex acquisition now fully integrated and scaling. [#I]
- Beauty Actives and Beauty Care: growth supported by improved consumer sentiment in North America H2 2025; Solus Biotech ceramides (acquired 2023) +36% at constant currency as globalisation of sales proceeds (CEO Statement, Croda 2025 Annual Report). [#A]
- Local and regional customer sales +9% in Consumer Care at constant currency; L&R mix now 82% of Consumer Care (vs 73% in 2019); this is structurally positive for pricing power and margin. [#A]
- Top-5 Beauty customers: grew with 4 of 5 MNCs in FY2025; NPS Consumer Care improved to +42 (2024: +31) — the highest jump across divisions. [#I]
- ❌ Beauty Care underlying still faces patchy MNC demand; Home Care recovery more modest; the F&F surge somewhat masks slower recovery in the core emollients/actives business. [#A]

Life Sciences:
- Sales +5.5% to £532.2m (+7.7% at constant currency); growth skewed H2 as Pharma had its strongest quarter in Q4 2025. [#I]
- Crop Protection: +14% at constant currency in FY2025, driven by restocking from larger crop science customers following an extended destocking cycle; this mirrors the Consumer Care pattern but lagged by ~12 months. [#A]
- ⚡ Pharma: delivered strongest quarter in Q4 2025 driven by higher excipient sales for small-molecule and biologic applications; the excipients franchise (core pharma ingredients ~70% of Pharma sales) is recovering organically. [#J]
- Drug Delivery / Lipid Systems (mRNA): Avanti lipid sales for drug research grew double-digit % CAGR over three years at constant currency; however, large-scale manufacturing projects for mRNA took longer to materialise than anticipated, resulting in a decision to put the Lamar, USA lipid facility on standby — a £60.5m non-cash exceptional charge. [#I]
- ❌ The Lamar standby decision is a significant signal: the mRNA volume build-out that underpinned peak 2022 earnings has not returned at the scale anticipated; Croda is now rightsizing its lipid manufacturing footprint (CEO Statement, Croda 2025 Annual Report). [#J]
- Vaccine adjuvants: US regulatory environment (likely FDA/BARDA uncertainty post-COVID) created ongoing uncertainty for customers, holding back adjuvant sales in 2025. [#I]

Industrial Specialties:
- Largely divested: PTIC (Performance Technologies and Industrial Chemicals) sold June 2022; residual Industrial Specialties segment has become negligible in the group mix (89% of 2025 sales now in consumer, pharma, agriculture vs 73% in 2019). [#I]

§3 — Margin Delivery

  • J: Margin improvement in FY2025 was real but minimal — 17.4% vs 17.2% in FY2024; the volume recovery has not yet flowed into meaningful operating leverage. [#J]
  • Group adjusted EBIT margin FY2025: 17.4% (operating profit £295.3m / revenue £1,699.4m); confirms £295m / £1,699m = 17.37%, rounded to 17.4% as reported. [#I]
  • FY2024 margin was approximately 17.2% (£279.8m / £1,628m reported); FY2025 improvement is ~20bps — well short of the trajectory required to reach >20% by 2028. [#A]
  • Route to >20% margin: management targets ~£100m annualised transformation efficiency benefits by FY2028 plus operational leverage from volume recovery; in 2025 transformation benefits were described as "slightly ahead of expectations" — a modest positive signal. [#A]
  • Positive margin drivers: improved asset utilisation at shared manufacturing sites and £28m in gross benefits from the transformation programme in FY2025. - Negative margin variance: price/mix was a 3% headwind in FY2025 as the company selectively reduced prices in Beauty Care and Crop Protection to regain volume share. (Accounts, 11 May)
  • H2 2025 operating margin: 17.6% — roughly in line with consensus (~10bps above). (Deutsche Bank Research, 24 Feb)
  • CapEx reduced: from £138m in FY2024 to £108m in FY2025, below prior guidance of ~£135m; CapEx now targeted at ~6% of sales going forward, down from the elevated investment cycle of 2020–25. [#I]
  • ❌ The gap between 17.4% actual and >20% target requires ~270bps improvement over three years; this requires both meaningful volume growth AND structural cost extraction to materialise simultaneously — a binary outcome risk. [#J]
  • Fixed cost base: management explicitly acknowledged that "operating cost margins" have been adversely impacted by a higher cost base, while "product and gross margins remain relatively stable" — confirming the margin compression is in the SG&A/overhead layer, not the product itself. [#A]
  • Free cash flow: £161.6m post-exceptionals (£162m rounded in market commentary), representing 9.5% of sales vs 2028 target >12%; H2 improvement driven by lower working capital and CapEx (CEO Statement, Croda 2025 Annual Report). [#I]

§7 — Was FY2025 an Inflection or Still in Trough?

  • J: FY2025 is a genuine inflection point — the first year of meaningful PBT recovery — but it is an early-stage inflection, not confirmation that the recovery is self-sustaining or that management's 2028 targets will be met. [#J]

Evidence for genuine inflection:
- PBT +8.4% YoY (£276m vs £260m); first meaningful recovery after two years of sequential decline from 2022 peak. [#A]
- Volume +9.6% organic in FY2025: this is the most important leading indicator; volume recovery drives fixed-cost absorption and operational leverage, which is the mechanism for margin improvement. [#A]
- Management confidence to introduce a specific 3-year financial framework (3–6% CAGR, >20% EBIT margin, >12% FCF) at FY2025 results: management would not have made these commitments without internal visibility into the order book and transformation pipeline. [#J]
- Both Consumer Care and Life Sciences grew sales and margins in FY2025: not a one-division story; breadth of recovery is encouraging. [#A]
- Transformation programme ahead of plan in first year; £100m annualised savings programme credibly underway. [#A]
- Sell-side sentiment: the multi-year downgrade cycle is widely considered concluded; estimate revisions have stabilised, with Morgan Stanley's April 2026 update showing only a minor -0.20% adjustment to outer-year EPS estimates. (Rothschild & Co Redburn, 26 Feb; Morgan Stanley, 22 Apr)

Key caveats and risks:
- ❌ EPS +2.5% only: despite PBT +8.4%, EPS barely moved; the gap reflects higher net finance costs on the debt stack and the ongoing drag of diluted share count from prior-year activity; if debt costs remain elevated, EPS recovery will lag PBT recovery. [#J]
- ❌ Margin still far from peak: 17.4% vs Croda's 2019 peak of ~24%+ and the >20% 2028 target; the gap requires ~270bps improvement over 3 years — demanding in the context of uncertain Life Sciences volume. [#J]
- ❌ Life Sciences mRNA/lipid still depressed at scale: Lamar facility on standby, large-batch pharma lipid projects delayed; this was the highest-margin segment at peak; its absence suppresses group margins materially. [#J]
- Recovery partly stimulus-dependent: Crop Protection +14% in FY2025 was driven by restocking from large crop science customers — already showing reversal in Q1 2026 (-8% CC); volume recovery in this segment may not sustain at FY2025 rates. [#A]
- ❌ The mRNA/pandemic-preparedness premium that drove 2022 peak earnings is structurally reduced; the 2028 framework targets are achievable but imply a steady-state business earning at a structurally lower level than the pandemic windfall years. [#J]
- Life Sciences missed Q1 2026 consensus expectations of +3.3% growth; delivered -3.0% CC instead — recovery pace slower than hoped. (Morgan Stanley, 22 Apr)

Verdict on recovery trajectory:
- FY2023 = trough (adjusted PBT £236m); FY2024 = first year of recovery (£260m, +10%); FY2025 = second year of recovery (£276m, +6%); FY2026 consensus = £321m operating profit, with PBT likely ~£298–305m — still below 2022 peak levels. [#A]
- The recovery is real, gradual, and proceeding largely as management guided; it is not V-shaped; it is a multi-year normalisation with structural self-help (transformation) layered on top of volume cycle recovery. [#J]
- Is the recovery self-sustaining? Partially: Consumer Care has structural tailwinds (F&F, L&R customer growth, Asia expansion); Life Sciences Pharma has genuine long-term demand for excipients and drug delivery; Crop Protection is more cyclical. The self-sustaining elements are present but they are not yet the dominant earnings driver. [#J]
- Sell-side consensus: U-shaped recovery, trough in 2023–2024, with 2025 marking stabilisation and 2026–2028 as gradual recovery phase. No broker yet models a V-shaped return to peak margins. (Rothschild & Co Redburn, 26 Feb)
