§7 — Narrative + Market Story

  • Bull case: [AS-JP Morgan], [AS-Jefferies], [AS-Morgan Stanley] share the dominant bull narrative: PR24 FD has locked in a clearly investable regulatory framework through 2030; SVT's AMP8 RAB will compound at ~8-10% pa (capex deployment + CPIH); AEPS will grow ~9-11% pa; the dividend is well-covered and growing; the stock should trade at a premium EV/RAB to sector peers given historical ODI outperformance track record; the entry point (post-FD relief rally fully digested) offers a visible compounding return with limited downside [#A]
  • Bear case: [AS-Société Générale] and [AS-Deutsche Bank] share a shorter bear/hold narrative: the stock is expensive on EV/RAB (~1.25-1.30x vs estimated fair value ~1.05-1.10x); AMP8 ODI commitments are materially more ambitious than AMP7 — SVT's historical outperformance was achieved under less stringent benchmarks; capex delivery risk on £14bn programme is real; balance sheet leverage (~60%+ Net Debt/RAB) limits financial flexibility; and the sector re-rating above 1.0x RAB is predicated on a low-rate environment that may not persist [#A]
  • Bullish theme 1 — regulatory visibility: PR24 FD accepted by SVT without appeal (unlike United Utilities which appealed to CMA); management acceptance signals confidence in deliverability and removes a 12-18M overhang [#A]
  • Bullish theme 2 — RAB compounding: AMP8 capex of ~£14.1bn (2025-2030) is the largest capital programme in SVT's history; every £1 of capex deployed grows RAB and hence regulated revenue entitlement [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 1 — ODI risk: AMP8 performance commitments include ~80 ODI metrics; SVT faces potential £3.4bn gross penalty exposure if it misses worst-case; while historically SVT has been a net ODI earner, the AMP8 bar is structurally higher [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 3 — political/regulatory risk: A future Ofwat PR29 reset could be tighter if public/political sentiment against water companies remains hostile (post-Thames Water distress); minor flag from [AS-HSBC] and [AS-Berenberg] [#A]
  • Narrative shift L12M: Significant positive shift post-Dec-24 FD; pre-FD, ~30% of notes were cautious on regulatory uncertainty; post-FD, that caution has largely been retired [#A]
  • Near-term focus: Most analysts focused on: (1) FY25 full-year results (May-25 announcement) confirming ODI outturn, (2) early AMP8 capex ramp data [#A]
  • Medium-term focus: AMP8 capex delivery and ODI performance trajectory (2025-2030) [#A]
  • Original insight: [AS-RBC Capital Markets] has the most original research — built an ODI scorecard model tracking SVT's relative performance vs Ofwat benchmarks on a quarterly basis; this is the most differentiated analytical framework in the pool [#A]
  • Management echo: Majority of Buy-rated notes closely echo management's AMP8 business plan narrative; limited independent stress-testing of capex delivery assumptions in most buy notes [#A]
  • Narrative credibility: Bull narrative credibility is HIGH on revenue/dividend (regulatory formula); MEDIUM on ODI outperformance (historically justified but AMP8 bar higher); LOW on capex delivery certainty (limited precedent for this scale) [#J]
  • Sceptical bullet — bull narrative hidden risk: The bull narrative implicitly assumes SVT can maintain AMP7 ODI-outperformance standards under AMP8's stricter metrics, AND deliver a doubled capital programme, AND maintain cost efficiency — any one of these three assumptions failing simultaneously would cause a significant narrative reset and consensus downgrade; most Buy notes do not stress-test all three jointly [#J]
