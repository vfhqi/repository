  • Sub: Acquisition multiples for niche European software have inflated significantly since 2018-2022; if Addnode is paying 8-12x EBIT for targets (vs prior 5-7x), returns on incremental capital deployed have deteriorated

  • Sub: Track record of value creation via M&A is partially circular: rising share price enables equity issuance for deals, which drives EPS accretion, which drives share price — until it doesn't

  • Bull thesis #2: "Recurring revenue base provides earnings visibility and downside protection" [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #2: Recurring revenue in B2B software is not unconditionally sticky; customer churn accelerates during industrial downturns as clients renegotiate or cancel maintenance contracts
  • Sub: Addnode's exposure to infrastructure, construction, and industrial design means its "recurring" revenues are partially correlated with European capex cycles

  • Sub: Maintenance contract renewals in legacy CAD/PLM software face substitution risk as cloud-native competitors (Autodesk, Bentley, Dassault) offer bundled SaaS alternatives that disintermediate resellers

  • Bull thesis #3: "Management has a proven playbook and long track record of execution" [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #3: Management longevity is a double-edged sword; the same leadership team that succeeded in a low-interest-rate, high-multiple environment has not been tested in a structurally different capital cost environment
  • Sub: At higher interest rates, acquisition financing is more expensive and target valuations are stickier (sellers anchored to prior multiples); the playbook that worked 2010-2021 is harder to replicate 2023-2026+

  • Sub: CEO Johan Andersson and the senior team have limited observable track record of managing through a genuine industrial recession while maintaining acquisition pace

  • Bull thesis #4: "Addnode is transitioning its portfolio toward SaaS/cloud, improving revenue quality and reducing churn" [#J]

  • Bear rebuttal #4: Cloud migration narratives in legacy software resellers are frequently overstated; much of what Addnode calls "subscription" is legacy maintenance repackaged, not genuine SaaS with net dollar retention >100%
  • Sub: The company does not disclose NRR (Net Dollar Retention) or gross retention by segment — without this, the "improving revenue quality" thesis is unverifiable

  • Sub: Genuine cloud-native competitors are taking share in Addnode's core CAD/PLM reseller niche; Autodesk's shift to subscription has reduced the value-add of third-party resellers

  • Bull thesis #5: "Valuation is reasonable given compounding EBITA growth trajectory" [#J]
