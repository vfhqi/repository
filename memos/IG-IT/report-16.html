§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Clean on observable hard red flags; structurally mixed due to CDP Reti quasi-control and Italian state-adjacent ownership dynamics; no restatements, no enforcement actions, no class actions.

Voting rights structure:
- No dual-class shares: Italgas has a single class of ordinary shares; one-share-one-vote
- No founder control: post-IPO from Snam (2016); state-adjacent ownership via CDP Reti rather than founder
- Golden share / special rights: CDP Reti's 42.1% stake creates de facto blocking control on most strategic decisions without requiring a formal golden share; this is economic blocking power, not a structural governance anomaly, but it is functionally equivalent for a minority investor
- Anti-takeover: no formal poison pill; however, CDP Reti stake effectively prevents a hostile takeover; Italgas is not practically acquisible

Shareholder agreement:
- CDP Reti + Snam Shareholders' Agreement: formally governs board nomination rights (Voto di Lista slates), dividend policy consultation, and M&A thresholds above a specified size; this agreement is disclosed in Italian annual reports; it is the primary governance document that constrains Italgas's autonomous strategic decision-making
- Minority investors' risk: any strategic decision (major M&A, capital structure change, dividend policy revision) that conflicts with CDP Reti's interests is, in practice, blocked; this creates alignment between Italgas strategy and CDP / Italian state infrastructure priorities — positive when those are aligned with minority ROI, negative when they diverge (e.g., forced green investment at sub-commercial returns)

Related-party transactions:
- Snam: Italgas uses Snam's transmission network for gas transport; tariff governed by ARERA-regulated rates; disclosed in annual report related-party note; not commercially concerning — regulated-price transactions with a quasi-public counterparty
- CDP Group: banking / financing relationships with CDP's subsidiary BancoCDP for project financing; disclosed; arms-length by Italian standards
- No evidence of non-arms-length related-party transactions that would benefit insiders at shareholder expense

Audit history:
- Auditor: EY Italy — unchanged since IPO (2016); 9+ years tenure at Italgas; long but not unusual for Italian large-cap context (KPMG/PwC/Deloitte/EY all have long tenures at Italian utility majors)
- Audit opinion: clean unqualified opinions L5Y; no emphasis-of-matter paragraphs on going-concern or critical estimation uncertainty beyond standard regulatory-tariff timing items
- No material weaknesses disclosed in internal control reports L5Y
- No restatements L5Y

Legal / regulatory:
- No CONSOB enforcement actions against Italgas or senior management L5Y
- No class actions in Italian or international courts
- Regulatory proceedings: standard ARERA tariff appeals (which all Italian utilities engage in as routine regulatory management); no evidence of adverse regulatory sanction for market abuse or disclosure failure
- Environmental: no material environmental litigation; gas distribution generates limited direct environmental liability compared to upstream E&P

Whistleblower / governance complaints:
- No publicly disclosed whistleblower cases L5Y; whistleblower hotline is disclosed in annual report (mandatory under Italian law D.Lgs 24/2023 from 2023)
- Absence of disclosure is not absence of issues — but no evidence of suppressed governance complaints from press or Glassdoor
