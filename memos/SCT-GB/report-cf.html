EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Strategic pivot toward complex AI consulting and data enablement materially expands the addressable market while introducing execution risk around internal IT transformation and margin dilution. The Oakland acquisition transforms Softcat's capability to engage earlier in AI and data consulting journeys, shifting the business model from pure infrastructure reselling toward higher-margin advisory partnerships. The company currently captures only 5% of the highly fragmented UK market and serves 20% of its target customer base with 20-25% average wallet share, supporting management's assertion of substantial structural runway. Yet traditional hardware resale economics are structurally thinning as cloud standardization compresses transactional margins, forcing an urgent pivot that carries meaningful integration and execution risk.

Orderly C-suite succession maintains strategic continuity, though drifting employee engagement metrics and unconventional governance structure introduce latent cultural and oversight risks. Graham Charlton assumed the CEO role in August 2023, completing a well-telegraphed, multi-year internal succession plan, while Graeme Watt transitioned directly to Non-Executive Chairman, a governance friction point offset by deep institutional continuity. The employee net promoter score drifted lower from 63 in late 2022 to 55 in 2024, stabilising at 56 in 2025, and staff attrition ticked up to approximately 16% in early 2024 from historical sub-15% levels, signaling minor cultural friction post-transition that warrants monitoring despite remaining above broader market norms.
