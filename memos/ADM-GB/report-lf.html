EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Admiral Group plc presents a rare combination of elite CEO operational pedigree and world-class governance alignment , anchored by Milena Mondini de Focatiis's 14-year internal track record scaling international subsidiaries and delivering record profit before tax of £958 million in 2025 .

This performance represents a 16% year-on-year increase and sustains a 9% compound annual growth rate over the past decade, achieved while maintaining exceptional underwriting discipline through volatile pricing cycles .

The executive compensation framework enforces stringent 400% of salary shareholding guidelines with both the CEO and CFO comfortably exceeding these thresholds, establishing world-class alignment through long-term equity-linked performance metrics spanning EPS growth, relative TSR, and ROE targets .

The board composition demonstrates exemplary structural independence at 83%, significantly outperforming European peers, with zero proprietary or nominee directors and strict nine-year tenure caps minimizing entrenchment risks .

1 6 The CEO operator quality assessment delivers an unambiguously positive verdict based on deep functional expertise and demonstrated pricing agility. Mondini de Focatiis joined Admiral in 2007, founded the Italian ConTe subsidiary in 2008, and served as its CEO for over a decade, establishing direct P&L accountability and operational leadership credentials .

ConTe achieved breakthrough profitability in 2012 during its fourth full operational year and delivered six consecutive profitable years through 2019, validating her capacity to execute complex greenfield launches and sustain technical underwriting discipline . Sell-side analysts characterize Admiral as the UK equivalent of US direct-insurance powerhouse Progressive, praising management's capability to react faster than peers to claims trends while systematically out-underwriting the market by a 20-percentage- point combined ratio margin .

However, her consulting-centric background at Accenture and Bain carries typical generalist-track risks if unaccompanied by continued long operational P&L exposure , and sell- side skepticism persists regarding Admiral's attempts to diversify, with analysts arguing that international operations have historically failed to contribute meaningfully to earnings despite reaching 1.6 million policies by 2020 .

8 Executive team stability and succession planning register as institutional strengths with minimal near-term disruption risks. Geraint Jones will retire as Group CFO on July 1, 2026, transitioning to a part-time consultant role, while Rachel Lewis–currently UK Insurance CFO with two decades of internal experience, actuary credentials, and successful IFRS17 implementation leadership–will assume the Group CFO position .

Admiral utilizes a robust internal promotion philosophy to preserve its legendary entrepreneurial corporate culture, with the Future Leaders MBA program celebrating its 20th anniversary and supporting more than 60 corporate participants, including Group CEO Mondini de Focatiis and Admiral Seguros CEO Sarah Harris . The corporate culture exhibits world- class health indicators, achieving Great Place to Work Legendary Status in the UK following 25 consecutive years on the Best Workplaces list, ranking among Fortune's Top 25 World's Best Workplaces globally, and securing 15th place on Europe's Best Workplaces list in 2025 .

Key-person risk assessment reveals moderate and well-mitigated exposure through structured contingency frameworks and deep operational bench strength. The CFO retirement transition of Geraint Jones in July 2026 ensures structured cultural continuity after 24 years of service, with internal successor Rachel Lewis already serving as UK Insurance CFO .

Expert network participants validate Admiral's core competitive advantage stemming from its cultural focus on technical pricing and collaborative data projects, with proprietary pricing strategy leveraging deep internal analytics to outperform competitors on risk selection and actively seeking to understand why they outperform the market while conducting extensive process analysis to maintain a 20-point combined operating ratio advantage . The IT excellence center at Admiral India achieved an outstanding 91% employee retention rate in 2025, demonstrating exceptional offshore operational stability .

Nevertheless, the pure internal promotion model risks entrenching legacy underwriting biases if teams fail to respond to external tech disruptions , and expert testimony reveals that replicating motor underwriting advantages in the UK home market faces substantial competitive structural hurdles, with price comparison and risk-selection advantages far less pronounced in household insurance, with management utilizing the home insurance division primarily to create customer stickiness and enhance multi-product retention .

Core regulatory and credibility concerns center on historical operational friction points with measured near-term financial impact .

A £50 million provision was recognized to remediate historical UK motor total loss valuation processes, as internal systems failed to react quickly enough to the extreme used car price volatility between 2019 and 2024 .

Ongoing FCA reviews target UK ancillary sales, travel insurance claims, and broad Consumer Duty compliance , and Admiral faces a capped €24 million indemnity exposure from a historical Spanish VAT intermediary service audit, though the company won its Spanish court appeal in December 2025 for two key periods under audit .

Proactive governance scaffolding includes a rigorous three lines of defense model managed by the Group Risk Committee, annual mandatory training covering whistleblowing and anti- bribery for all employees, and solvency models stressed against severe downside macroeconomic scenarios to protect the group's capital surplus .

The group maintains a clean single-class voting structure with ordinary shares totaling 306,304,676 shares in issue and no shares carrying special control rights, ensuring absolute one- share, one-vote democratic equity .

The definitive management quality verdict classifies Admiral as a high-conviction, operator-led execution story with peer-superior technical underwriting capabilities, though strategic diversification credibility and non-motor replication execution remain under active surveillance. Management executed highly lucrative capital allocation, completing a £146.4 million carrying value back-book loan sale generating a net profit of £9.8 million, while implementing a forward-flow arrangement of up to £300 million annually to scale off-balance sheet growth, successfully forward-flowing £279.5 million of newly originated loans and generating £11.2 million in fee income .

Strategic M&A activity included the integration of More Than's home and pet renewal rights for £82.5 million, an agreement to acquire digital fleet specialist Flock for £80 million, and the finalization of the unprofitable US motor subsidiary Elephant sale in late 2025 .

Effective HY26, the board introduced share buybacks to return surplus capital dynamically, marking a historic transition from special dividends .

The execution confidence level registers at high plausibility with measured downside scenarios , as Admiral's structural cost advantages and underwriting pricing agility remain intact despite narrowing competitive moats and regulatory friction costs.

The Animal-CEO archetype assessment scores Mondini de Focatiis as demonstrating exceptional Force through operational underwriting discipline, superior Agency via rapid pricing velocity, proven Dynamism through strategic portfolio pruning, disciplined Capital-allocation via structured M&A selectivity, transparent Communication clarity across earnings cycles, and sustained Outlier technical standards maintaining a 20-point combined ratio advantage over UK motor peers . 26
