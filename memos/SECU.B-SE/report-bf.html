Supplier dynamics and distribution channels

Securitas does not manufacture proprietary electronic hardware, relying entirely on global original equipment manufacturers for security components while maintaining broad general agreements with leading suppliers of cameras and sensors to ensure technology neutrality . Disruption forces and long-term paradigm fit

The security industry faces substantial long-term volume threats from AI-powered monitoring, with the rapid advancement of computer vision enabling automated cameras to perform surveillance tasks once requiring human presence . Premium vertical expansion in data center contracts delivers exceptional 30% annual compound growth , while the global security risk management sector represents a fast-growing USD 21 billion addressable market expanding at over 10% annually . Inelastic security budgets and rising global geopolitical instability shield the business model from macroeconomic downturns, with contractual price-indexation clauses allowing prompt recovery of direct labor inflation through rate adjustments . The core structural risk is that rapid AI-powered video surveillance adoption could permanently cannibalize core guarding volumes , while failing to accelerate technology sales would compress consolidated margins and leave Securitas structurally exposed to low-margin physical guarding inflation. 38
