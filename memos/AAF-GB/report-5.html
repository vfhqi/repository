The Airtel Money IPO deferral is the main negative surprise. [#J]

  • Delivery snapshot (AS-backbone): Revenue $6,415m (+29.5% reported / +24.0% CC); EBITDA $3,162m (+37.2% reported / +30.4% CC); EBITDA margin 49.3% (+150bps); PAT $813m (+147%); EPS 18.6 cents (from 8.2 cents — +127.7% before exceptionals, or from 6.0 cents basic prior year). [AS·DB]: Q4 EBITDA margin 50.3% (all-time high). [#I]

  • Investment implication: FY2026 demonstrates AAF is structurally through the FX trough — naira stabilisation, tariff realisation, and data/mobile money mix shift are working simultaneously. [C] analytical framing: The investment question for triaging is whether this is a one-off FX/tariff reset or a durable ARPU-led compounding story. [AS·Jefferies]: The Africa operations beat Q3 and Q4 EBITDA estimates, prompting upward revision to Bharti Airtel consolidated forecasts. [#J]

  • Key risk: Airtel Money IPO deferred to H2 2026. [AS·DB]: "A notable headwind to near-term sentiment and a material catalyst pushback." Without IPO proceeds, the valuation uplift from fintech separation is deferred. [C]: Geopolitical risk-off (Iran, EM sentiment) makes timing uncertain. [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The 147% profit surge is partly a low base effect (FY2025 PAT $328m after two years of FX-driven losses). [AS·ARS]: Top-line result was a -1.1% miss on revenue vs Zedcrest's $6,490m; EBITDA also slightly missed ($3,162m vs $3,203m consensus).   • J-front verdict: 24% CC growth is industry-leading for a pan-African telco of AAF's scale — but the base effect from FY2025 naira devaluation materially flatters the year-on-year comparison; the post-tariff normalisation trajectory (H1 FY2027) is the critical test. [#J]

  • Group revenues: $6,415m (+29.5% reported / +24.0% CC). Reported-to-CC gap of 5.5pp reflects naira appreciation (NGN/USD: 1,529 weighted avg Q4 FY25 → 1,386 Q4 FY26). [#I]

  • Nigeria — the inflection engine: [AS·ARS]: Nigeria +47.5% CC revenue in FY2026 following January 2025 tariff adjustment (~50% uplift). Nigeria EBITDA margin record ~56%. [C]: Nigeria ARPU +36.7% CC; customer base +9.4%. Revenue ~$697m (+49% reported). ⚡ [#I]

  • Data overtakes voice: [AS·ARS]: Data revenues surged +35.2% CC to eclipse voice as the largest revenue component for the first time; fueled by +14.8% increase in data customers and smartphone penetration reaching 49.5%. [C]: Data usage per customer 8.9GB/month (+27% YoY). [#I]

  • Airtel Money — strong scale, decelerating vs M-Pesa comp: [AS·ARS]: Mobile money revenues +28.4% CC. [C]: 54.1m customers (+21.3% YoY); transacting customers +74% YoY ⚡; TPV >$215bn/yr (+49% reported); agent network 660,000+. At $623m revenue, Airtel Money is ~40% of M-Pesa's absolute scale but growing faster (+28% CC vs M-Pesa +13.4%). [#I]

  • Nigeria: ~$697m revenue; +49% reported / +47.4% CC; margin ~56% (record) ⚡
  • East Africa: ~$498m; +17.6% reported / +16.9% CC; M-Pesa competition in Kenya structural overhang

  • Francophone Africa: ~$749m; +17.1% CC; margin 39.5% ❌ — 990bps below group average

  • Subscriber metrics: Total customers 183.5m (+10.5%); data customers 84.2m (+14.8%); smartphone penetration 49.5%. [AS·ARS]: MyAirtel app transacting customers surged +74%. [#I]

  • Peer anchor: MTN Group Nigeria revenue ~+55% in FY2025 (tariff-driven — same dynamic). AAF's 24% CC growth broadly in line with MTN Nigeria's tariff-reset trajectory. Safaricom group service revenue +~5% in FY2026 — far lower, reflecting Kenya market maturity. [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The 24% CC growth is unsustainable — the tariff-reset cycle is a one-time repricing event.
