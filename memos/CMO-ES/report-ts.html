Section 3 – What the company does 21 findings

Section summary
  1. The company operates a highly integrated business model across six construction-focused product lines, securing local quarry access to maintain high profit barriers. Extensive geographic diversification balances cyclical market risks across Europe, Latin America, and Asia to deliver remarkably stable consolidated EBITDA margins.

Product segments and revenue structure

1.
Stable transaction model: Molins generates revenue from transactional sales of cement and concrete, complemented by long-term precast construction solutions and landscape elements
2.
Dominant cement segment: Portland and specialty cements remain the primary earnings engine, contributing 58% of consolidated sales in 2025
3.
Growing precast solutions: Precast concrete solutions rose to 20% of sales in 2025, driven by strategic acquisitions that expand structural building capabilities
4.
Specialized specialty offerings: Under the Calucem brand, Molins holds a leading global position in calcium aluminate cement, supplying advanced industrial chemicals worldwide
5.
Integrated circular economy: The company incorporates waste management and eco-materials into its business model, replacing expensive fossil fuels to reduce carbon exposure
6.
Growth in precast: Precast solutions represented €281.82 million of sales in 2025, showing strong momentum compared to €220.90 million in 2024
7.
Underperforming building solutions: Construction solutions sales rose to €105.88 million in 2025, up from €86.97 million in 2024, demonstrating steady downstream diversification

Geographic market diversification

8.
Massive Mexican contribution: Joint operations in Mexico represent the largest single geographical market, generating €942 million in sales and €428 million in EBITDA during 2025
9.
Strong Spanish base: The domestic Spanish market remains highly profitable, contributing €530 million in sales and €101 million in EBITDA in 2025
10.
Resilient South American footprint: South American operations span Argentina, Uruguay, Colombia, and Bolivia, together contributing over €440 million in sales in 2025
11.
Strategic Asian presence: The company operates in Bangladesh and India, generating €213 million in sales and €70 million in EBITDA in 2025
12.
Underperforming North African assets: Operations in Tunisia and Turkey face structural headwinds, with Tunisian sales standing at €108 million in 2025

Value chain positioning and competition

13.
Local monopoly advantages: High transport costs and localized quarry assets create natural geographic monopolies around cement plants, protecting Molins from international import competition
14.
Vertical margin capture: By vertically integrating quarries, ready-mix concrete, and precast plants, Molins secures raw materials and captures downstream margins
15.
High entry barriers: Building a modern cement plant requires enormous capital, with historical investments in Colombia exceeding €335 million, deterring new entrants
16.
Fragmented customer profile: The company sells to local contractors, public entities, and retail distributors, avoiding customer concentration and preserving high pricing power
17.
Expanding technical barriers: Regulatory carbon compliance and specialized product certifications, such as Susterra, create high technical barriers for smaller, unintegrated competitors
18.
High logistical barriers: A competitive advantage is secure logistics, as trucking cement over long distances is highly unprofitable compared to localized rail transport
19.
Strong environmental regulation: Tightening carbon regulations in the European Union create a high compliance barrier that favors large, established players like Molins
20.
Other side of the story: Rapid tightening of European carbon tax policies exposes European operations to high compliance costs if alternative fuel rates fall short

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Continuous margin expansion and high cash conversion have enabled the group to build a robust net cash position of €94 million by 2025. Strong operational pricing power and cost efficiency programs successfully mitigated severe currency translation devaluations across South America and Mexico.

Revenue and EBITDA trajectory

1.
Growing multi-year revenue: Proportional sales expanded from €1,268 million in 2022 to €1,349 million in 2023, and stabilized at €1,368 million in 2025
2.
Expanding operating margins: Proportional EBITDA margins increased from 21.8% in 2022 to 25.0% in 2023, and held flat at 26.1% in both 2024 and 2025
3.
Growing net earnings: Consolidated net profit reached an all-time high of €185 million in 2025, yielding an EPS of €2.80 compared to €2.78 in 2024
4.
Effective pricing offsets: Top-line growth has been driven by proactive price-cost management and efficiency programs that fully neutralized currency devaluations
5.
Steady quarterly performance: In Q4 2025, EBITDA grew by 14% year-on-year, demonstrating accelerating operating momentum toward the end of the year

Cash generation and leverage profile

6.
Outstanding cash conversion: Molins recorded free cash flow of €176 million in 2025, reflecting a strong cash conversion rate of 50%
7.
Solid balance sheet: The group improved its net debt from €158 million in H1 2022 to net cash of €91 million in 2024 and €94 million in 2025
8.
Structured debt maturities: The balance sheet is highly secure, with 53% of the outstanding debt facilities maturing after 2028, reducing refinance risk
9.
Sustaining capital efficiency: Maintenance capex remained stable at €67 million in 2025 compared to €68 million in 2024, focusing on operational descarbonization
10.
Conservative guidance history: Management's guidance is conservative, as shown in 2024 when they predicted slight sales declines but delivered flat sales of €1,365 million

Accounting consolidation differences

11.
Equity method exclusions: Under IFRS, key high-margin markets like Mexico and Bangladesh are equity-accounted, meaning statutory sales of €1,027.65 million exclude their direct top-lines
12.
Proportional management view: Proportional consolidation is the primary steering metric because it incorporates Molins' 33% share of Mexico and 50% share of Bangladesh
13.
Other side of the story: Earnings quality is reduced by persistent non-cash hyperinflation and FX adjustments in Argentina, which reduced H1 2025 consolidated net profit by 9%

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The strategic investment case strengthens based on M&A integration and cost efficiency, targeting a highly predictable 18-month forward EPS trajectory. Substantial growth drivers operate independently to deliver a high-probability path toward over 25% annualized IRR potential.

Expansion via strategic M&A

1.
Massive capacity expansion: The pending acquisition of Secil expands Molins' global annual cement capacity by 10 million tons, strengthening its presence across Europe and South America
2.
High transaction certainty: Plausibility of the Secil driver is high because the agreement is signed and slated to close in Q1 2026, though anti-trust delays represent a failure mechanism
3.
Downstream precast expansion: Recent precast acquisitions in Portugal, Southeastern Europe, and Spain directly drive higher-margin structural revenues and capture industrialized construction growth
4.
Precast growth feasibility: Plausibility of the precast driver is medium, backed by a strong order book, but faces failure if European housing markets contract severely

Structural cost and decarbonization drivers

5.
Low-cost alternative fuels: Raising fossil fuel substitution from 25% to a targeted 40% by 2030 directly reduces energy operating expenses and improves margins
6.
Alternative fuel feasibility: Plausibility is medium, as shown by the 25% level achieved in 2025, but the mechanism would fail if local municipal waste supplies are disrupted
7.
Digital kiln optimization: Deploying real-time artificial intelligence tools to optimize cement kilns and grinding processes directly enhances thermal efficiency and lowers unit costs

Portfolio synergies and correlation

8.
Independent driver correlation: These strategic drivers operate independently, meaning that delays in alternative fuel substitution will not prevent M&A-driven capacity expansion from boosting EPS
9.
Bull case requirements: To unlock the 25%+ IRR bull case, the group must execute the Secil integration seamlessly while maintaining Spain's stable domestic pricing environment
10.
Other side of the story: Integrating a large asset like Secil exposes Molins to high execution risk, and a failure to capture synergies would materially dilute consolidated EBITDA margins

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The shares appear highly attractive on a relative basis, trading at a discount given the company's strong net cash and solid earnings growth.
1.
Historical valuation ranges: Historical broker research from Deutsche Bank Research indicates Molins has historically traded at P/E multiples of 9.3x to 17.4x and EV/EBITDA of 5.9x to 10.2x
2.
Steady market re-rating: The company's total market capitalization has rerated upward from €1,300 million in 2023 to €1.7 billion in 2024, and reached €2.2 billion in 2025
3.
Implied valuation discount: Trading at c. €2.2 billion market cap with €356 million proportional EBITDA, the implied multiple is low considering the €94 million net cash cushion
4.
Market pricing assumptions: The current share price appears to be pricing in stable European construction demand while discounting Latin American assets due to foreign exchange volatility
5.
Attractive earnings yield: With net profit reaching €185 million and EPS at €2.80 in 2025, the valuation is backed by strong underlying earnings yield
6.
Other side of the story: The stock's structural discount is highly likely to persist because the extremely low 6% public free float creates severe illiquidity barriers for institutional buyers

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Key risks are dominated by emerging market currency translations and extreme public share illiquidity, which could disrupt the reported EPS growth path.

Currency and operational risks

1.
Foreign exchange translation: Translating volatile emerging market currencies like the Mexican and Argentinian pesos into Euros represents the most material risk, directly depressing reported EPS
2.
Operational energy intensity: Cement kilns are highly electricity-intensive, and any sudden spike in European power prices would directly compress consolidated margins
3.
Structural market overcapacity: In South American markets like Argentina, industry overcapacity of 18 million tons against dispatches of 15 million tons restricts volume growth
4.
European regulatory costs: Tightening environmental carbon policies in Europe represent a major risk, as failing to reduce clinker ratios will escalate compliance expenses

Market liquidity and failure modes

5.
Inactive short selling: No active short-seller campaigns or high short interest exist, which is primarily a function of the tightly held family ownership
6.
Critical integration failure: A failure to successfully integrate Secil represents the ultimate failure mode, leading to capital destruction and margin dilution
7.
False friend indicator: High like-for-like sales growth can act as a false friend by masking severe currency devaluations that depress Euro-reported results
8.
Primary monitoring signal: Investors must closely track monthly domestic cement dispatch volumes in Spain and Argentina to detect early cyclical demand inflections
9.
Other side of the story: The risk profile is overstated because Molins' outstanding balance sheet and €94 million net cash fully insulate the company from high interest rates

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Senior leadership is executing a highly structured, planned succession plan, transferring executive power smoothly while preserving deep corporate knowledge.
1.
Planned CEO succession: In June 2024, Marcos Cela Rey became CEO, replacing Julio Rodríguez after a planned eighteen-month transition process
2.
Former CEO becomes Chairman: In June 2025, former CEO Julio Rodríguez succeeded Juan Molins Amat as Chairman of the Board of Directors, ensuring strategic continuity
3.
Refreshing board independence: Independent director Andrea Christenson resigned, leading to the appointment of Paloma Alonso Casillas, a veteran chemicals executive, in February 2026
4.
Inactive insider trading: No large-scale insider transaction signals have been recorded, reflecting stable family commitment to their 90% ownership stake

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. A deeper look is warranted but mixed because although the company exhibits outstanding cash generation, its severe illiquidity limits institutional accessibility.
1.
Strongest investment thesis: The strongest reason to buy is Molins' combination of exceptional pricing power, solid EBITDA margins of 26.1%, and €94 million net cash
2.
Other side of the story: The most serious counter-view is that the tiny 6% free float represents extreme structural illiquidity, effectively preventing concentrated institutional funds from establishing or exiting positions
3.
Urgent execution check: Investors must immediately verify the final anti-trust clearances and execution timeline of the Portuguese Secil transaction in Q1 2026
4.
Structural cost verification: Request a deep-dive operational audit of Calucem's production facilities in Croatia and Turkey to verify structural energy supply risks and margin vulnerability
