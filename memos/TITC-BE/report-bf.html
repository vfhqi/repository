EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Operational Business Model and Geographic Positioning

Titan operates as a vertically integrated heavy building materials provider globally with integrated activities including manufacturing cement, ready-mix concrete, aggregates, cement blocks, dry mortars, and fly ash , yet this product portfolio carries structural implications for cyclical volatility. Unlike aggregate-focused building

material peers, Titan's product mix is heavily exposed to downstream cement products with cement and concrete representing 80% of the product mix while aggregates make up only 5% , contrasting sharply with CRH's aggregates-led model where aggregates represent 60-70% of sales. This mix creates higher vulnerability to cyclical downturns in residential construction compared to aggregates-heavy peers , representing the company's single largest structural disadvantage relative to diversified competitors. The United States represents Titan's single most attractive market driving substantial group profitability, with US operations offering the largest exposure in the sector representing approximately 50% of group sales , positioning the company ahead of all European cement peers on American exposure metrics. The geographic footprint covers three major economic regions along the US East Coast, with US operations managed through Titan America geographically split between 60% Florida and 40% the Mid-Atlantic , providing concentrated exposure to high-growth Southern states with favorable demographic and industrial trends. 6

7 8 cement capacity reaches approximately 27.0 million tons annually across a global upstream network comprising 14 cement plants and three dedicated cement grinding plants , establishing a stable upstream foundation for downstream integration. Titan America operates a highly integrated dense logistics network spanning over 100 facilities including 7 aggregates mines, 21 distribution hubs, and three marine import terminals strategically located in Tampa, Norfolk, and Essex to service structurally undersupplied East Coast markets. Sourcing imports from Greece provides a significant cost advantage over domestic manufacturing and global competitors, with intragroup agreements supplying Titan America with Greek cement valued at $107.1 million annually ranking exceptionally low on the global cost curve sitting 50-60% below average US price , creating a durable competitive moat that peers cannot replicate without prohibitive infrastructure investments.
