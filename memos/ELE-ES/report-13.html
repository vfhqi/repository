§1 — Current Management Guidance

The following section maps Endesa's current formal guidance commitments as stated in the FY2024 results release (February 2025), the accompanying Capital Markets Day framework (updated November 2024), and Q1 2025 earnings commentary. Guidance is primarily framed at group level, with Spanish regulated and liberalised segments separately disclosed.

  • J-front verdict: Management's current guidance is numerically specific at the net income level but relies on non-trivial hydrology normalisation and regulatory-return assumptions that introduce meaningful scenario dispersion around the stated midpoints [#J]

  • FY2025 — net income: Guided EUR 1,700-1,800m net income for FY2025, midpoint EUR 1,750m; represents approximately +6% on FY2024 reported EUR 1,651m; sourced February 2025 results call and written guidance note [#A]

  • FY2025 — EBITDA: No formal EBITDA range given for FY2025 at company level; management guides to "broadly stable to slightly growing" EBITDA; SS interpolates EUR 4,150-4,300m range; analyst consensus centres near EUR 4,220m [AS-Barclays, AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

  • FY2025 — EPS: Implied EPS of approximately EUR 1.60-1.70 per share on current share count (1,058m shares); management does not explicitly guide EPS in published documents; derives from net income guidance [#A]

  • Sub: Endesa share count stable L3Y — no material buyback programme — making net-income-to-EPS translation clean [#I]

  • FY2025 — FCF: Management targets "positive net ordinary result after CapEx and dividends"; no explicit FCF midpoint in EUR provided; JPMorgan models approximately EUR 950m FCF (pre-dividend) for FY2025, below guidance-implied level [AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

  • FY2025 — CapEx: EUR 2,000-2,100m CapEx guided for FY2025; above FY2024 actual spend of approximately EUR 1,850m; management has guided this step-up every year since FY2021 and consistently underdelivered [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y (FY2026): Management's 2024 CMD targets EUR 1,850-2,000m net income for FY2026 (midpoint EUR 1,925m); CAGR of +5 to +7% from FY2024 base; no EBITDA midpoint guided explicitly [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (FY2027): CMD framework targets EUR 2,000m+ net income for FY2027; CapEx envelope of EUR 8,500-9,000m total for 2025-27 plan period; renewable capacity additions of approximately 3.5 GW net by FY2027 [#A]

  • Medium-term targets — dividend: Dividend policy confirmed at minimum EUR 1.70/share for FY2025, growing pari passu with net income; payout of approximately 100% of net ordinary income — characteristically high payout for Iberian regulated utilities [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Source — investor day: November 2024 CMD updated the three-year financial envelope; supersedes prior May 2024 interim guidance; key change was a modest reduction in FY2025 EBITDA expectation and an upward revision to renewable CapEx [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1 2025 call (May 2025) maintained FY2025 net income guidance with management citing on-track hydrology for Q1 and recovering Spanish retail margins; no formal upgrade to top of range despite Q1 beat [#I]

  • Update timing: Guidance reviewed and updated at each half-year results (February, July) and confirmed or revised at Q1/Q3 trading updates; CMD occurs roughly every 18-24 months [#I]

  • Caveat: All guidance explicitly conditioned on "normalised hydrological conditions" — a hedging clause that management has invoked on at least two occasions in the L4 period to explain variance [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: Regulatory WACC for Spanish distribution RAB (Rate of Return on Regulatory Asset Base) assumed stable at current approved levels through FY2025; CNMC review currently underway creates upside/downside scenario that management does not quantify in public guidance [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that the EUR 1,750m net income midpoint for FY2025 is achievable only if hydrology tracks long-run average AND the CNMC does not impose adverse revisions to grid tariffs — two independent regulatory/weather variables that have historically co-moved adversely in stress years; the "normalised hydrology" caveat provides a get-out without quantifying the miss scenario [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • J-front verdict: Endesa exhibits a mild sandbagging posture on net income — setting initial guidance at the bottom of the credible range — but this is weakening as EBITDA variance deteriorates and CapEx delivery worsens, suggesting the pattern may not be intentional conservatism but rather genuine execution uncertainty [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — net income: Consistent net income beats at +3 to +8% imply management sets guidance at a modest discount to its central internal estimate; this is common in regulated European utilities (Barclays notes approximately 60-65% of Iberian utilities guide to a "floor not midpoint" in CMD presentations) [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — CapEx: ❌ Inverse pattern on CapEx — management consistently over-promises deployment and underdelivers; this is not sandbagging but appears to be aspirational CapEx guidance that does not survive project permitting, supply chain, and labour constraints [#J]

  • Mixed pattern — EBITDA: EBITDA shows a 3-of-4 modest beat pattern (avg +4%), but FY2024 produced the first meaningful miss; the pattern is not clean enough to classify as systematic sandbagging — rather, EBITDA accuracy is a function of hydrology luck [#J]

  • Beat consistency: 4/4 net income beats; 3/4 EBITDA beats; this level of consistency is above the SS sector median for Iberian utilities where 2-3 out of 4 net income beats is typical [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Net income beat average +6.1% across L4; EBITDA beat average (3 beats only) +4.3%; both above peer medians, suggesting genuine conservatism, not one-off luck [#A]

  • Pattern shift — FY2024: The FY2024 EBITDA miss is a notable regime shift signal; prior to FY2024, all core EBITDA beats were broad-based; the FY2024 shortfall came despite management not lowering guidance at H1 — a failure to update guidance in the face of deteriorating hydrology that Deutsche Bank flagged as a credibility concern [AS-Deutsche Bank] [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: No clear improving trend; net income accuracy has remained stable but EBITDA accuracy has deteriorated at the margin; the pattern is broadly stationary with a late-period deterioration signal [#J]

  • Specific example — FY2021: Strong hydrology year; net income beat +7%; management guided conservatively citing COVID demand uncertainty; the beat was partly luck (better hydrology) and partly conservatism on retail volumes [#A]

  • Specific example — FY2024: Dry year with below-average hydrology; management maintained EBITDA guidance through H1 2024 despite visible hydrological deficit, ultimately missing by EUR 129m; suggests guidance updates are lagged, not proactive [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that apparent sandbagging on net income is eroding — FY2024 saw the narrowest net income beat (+3.2%) coinciding with an EBITDA miss, suggesting underlying earnings quality is under pressure and management is relying on below-the-EBITDA-line items (lower net debt costs, tax timing) to rescue net income "beats" even when operational delivery disappoints [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • J-front verdict: Net income guidance accuracy is stable but slowly narrowing (beat buffer compressing); EBITDA accuracy has deteriorated meaningfully in FY2024 — the trend is directionally negative though not yet alarming [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — FY2024: Net income beat narrowed to +3.2% (vs +6-8% in prior years); EBITDA missed by -3.1% for the first time; CapEx underspend persisted at -7.6%; overall accuracy trend is weakening [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FY2021-23: FY2021-23 showed above-peer accuracy on net income (+7%, +7.6%, +6.4%) and consistent EBITDA beat (+5%, +3.8%, +4.1%); this was the strongest accuracy window in the observable period [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — EBITDA: EBITDA accuracy moved from a consistent +4-5% beat corridor (FY2021-23) to a -3.1% miss in FY2024; the spread between best and worst EBITDA variance (FY2021 +5% vs FY2024 -3%) is approximately 800bps, implying high sensitivity to single-variable shocks [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Hydrology is the dominant variance driver for EBITDA; the 2024 drought year (Spain and Portugal among the driest in a decade per AEMET data) cost approximately EUR 150-200m versus a normalised year; this is a macro variable management cannot control but also cannot forecast reliably [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: CapEx underspend is an execution-driven trend; supply chain constraints (grid equipment lead times, transformer scarcity) explain a portion, but the persistence across all four periods suggests systematic optimism in the CapEx budget rather than episodic delays [AS-Morgan Stanley] [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Net income is best-tracked metric historically; management appears to have more direct control over net income via financing cost management, tax optimisation, and exceptional item timing than over EBITDA [#J]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF is worst-tracked metric due to CapEx timing distortions; EBITDA in hydrology-adverse years is second-worst; investors should weight net income as the credibility anchor, not EBITDA or FCF [#J]

  • Accuracy improving — partial signal: Q1 2025 tracking data (management confirmed FY2025 guidance on May call) suggests hydrology is normal in Q1-Q2 2025 — if that continues, FY2025 accuracy could revert to the pre-FY2024 pattern; but this is weather-dependent, not structural [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that the narrowing net income beat in FY2024 (+3.2% vs +6-8% prior) combined with an EBITDA miss suggests the natural cushion from conservative guidance is being used up — and in a year where both hydrology and power prices normalise simultaneously (FY2025 base case), there may be insufficient buffer to deliver even a modest beat [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front verdict: The guidance assumption stack has two genuinely fragile pillars — hydrological normalisation (uncontrollable, weather-driven) and regulatory WACC stability (CNMC review in progress) — both of which have meaningful downside scenarios that management does not quantify in public disclosures [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: FY2025-27 guidance embeds Spanish electricity demand growth of approximately +1 to +2% per annum; below IEA and REE long-term forecasts of +2 to +3% CAGR; conservative on this dimension; downside scenario is flat demand if industrial activity stalls [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Liberalised generation revenues guided on assumption of EUR 55-65/MWh Spanish pool price for FY2025; current forward curve at approximately EUR 60-65/MWh as of April 2025; within guided range but with downside if new renewables capacity additions suppress spot prices faster than expected [AS-Goldman Sachs] [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Regulated distribution revenue assumes RAB growth of approximately +3 to +4% per annum from new grid connections and network investment; this is a relatively mechanical assumption dependent on CNMC-approved formulaic returns; low discretionary risk [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Guidance embeds operating cost inflation of approximately +2 to +3% per annum; above current CPI trajectory in Spain (approximately +2.2% YoY as of Q1 2025 per INE) but reasonable given grid maintenance and labour costs in the energy sector; not aggressive [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage is limited in a regulated utility; EBITDA margin sensitivity to revenue upside is lower than in non-regulated peers; this is a margin-stability positive [#J]

  • Macro assumption — hydrology: ❌ Guidance explicitly assumes "normalised hydrological conditions" for FY2025-27; management does not provide a scenario analysis for 1-in-5 or 1-in-10 dry-year outcomes; EBITDA sensitivity to a -20% hydrology deficit is approximately EUR 150-200m per annum based on FY2024 outturn; 4-5% of EBITDA [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Gas cost assumption embedded in supply margin guidance is approximately EUR 35-40/MWh for TTF 2025; current forward curve broadly aligned; no major stress scenario here for FY2025 but FY2026-27 embeds normalisation below FY2022-23 peaks that is already largely delivered [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Guidance assumes normalised effective tax rate of approximately 24-26%; FY2024 effective rate was approximately 22.8% (benefiting from timing credits); if tax rate normalises upward, a 200bp adverse move reduces EPS by approximately 2-3% versus guidance [AS-Morgan Stanley] [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt approximately EUR 9.5bn; guidance assumes average cost of debt of approximately 3.2-3.5% through FY2027; fixed-rate hedging on approximately 70-75% of debt stack limits refinancing risk; interest cost sensitivity modest at current rates [AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

  • Regulatory assumption — WACC: ❌ Most fragile single assumption: CNMC is undertaking a regulatory period review; current post-tax WACC for Spanish distribution at approximately 5.6%; a -25bp adverse outcome reduces EBITDA by EUR 40-55m per year — approximately 1% of EBITDA; material over a 3-year plan period; management has not disclosed sensitivity in public guidance [AS-Deutsche Bank, AS-Santander] [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • J-front verdict: Overall guidance credibility is MEDIUM — the net income beat record is genuine and the sandbagging posture on that metric is credible, but the FY2024 EBITDA miss, persistent CapEx delivery failure, and unquantified regulatory/hydrology assumptions constrain the verdict from HIGH [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — supported by L4 net income beat record but undermined by EBITDA guidance quality deterioration, CapEx guidance systematic failure, and the absence of scenario quantification for the two most fragile assumptions (hydrology + WACC) [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: 4/4 net income beats across L4 with above-peer average magnitude (+6.1% vs peer median +2-3%); consistent directional signal is a genuine credibility positive [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption (demand): Demand growth embedded in guidance (+1 to +2%) is below REE and IEA long-run forecasts (+2 to +3%); management appears to guide demand conservatively, providing upside optionality if Spanish electrification pace accelerates [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record (regulated): Regulated distribution business has delivered consistent RAB-linked revenue in line with or above formula returns in every year of the L4 window; execution in the regulated segment is high-credibility [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FY2024 EBITDA miss (-3.1%) with management not flagging risk in H1 guidance update is a credibility demerit; Santander highlighted this as inconsistent with management's track record of proactive disclosure [AS-Santander] [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption (CapEx): ❌ Management sets CapEx guidance it has never delivered; 0-for-4 on CapEx; this is a systematic pattern suggesting the CapEx plan is aspirational, not credible; it artificially inflates FCF guidance-vs-actual comparison [#J]

  • Red flag — macro risk (hydrology): Management does not quantify downside scenario for below-average hydrology; the caveat in guidance allows for a miss without explicit acknowledgement of a previously-guided range; this is a disclosure quality issue as well as a macro risk [#J]

  • Red flag — execution risk (renewable CapEx ramp): 3.5 GW renewable target by FY2027 requires material acceleration from current delivery pace; H1 2024 renewable capacity additions were approximately 40% below CMD pacing target; JPMorgan flags this as the most likely execution miss in the medium-term plan [AS-JPMorgan] [#A]
