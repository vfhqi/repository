  • Consensus gross margin: Not the primary analytical metric for utilities; brokers focus on EBITDA margin; reported EBITDA margin FY2024 ~21–22%; consensus FY2026E ~23–24% — implies ~200bps expansion [#A]

  • Sub: 200bps EBITDA margin expansion over 2 years is achievable for a regulated utility in network expansion mode — Hera achieved ~150bps over 2022–2024; Iren's target is slightly more ambitious [#A]

  • Consensus EBIT margin: FY2024 reported ~9–10%; consensus FY2026E ~11–12% — expansion dependent on D&A stabilisation relative to EBITDA growth [#A]

  • Consensus net margin: FY2024 ~3.5–4%; consensus FY2026E ~4.5–5.5% — the leverage in this expansion is significant: a 100bps move in net margin on ~€6bn revenues is ~€60m in net income, or ~20% EPS change [#A]

  • Margin trend assumed: Expanding in all broker models; the only debate is pace — [AS-Kepler Cheuvreux] models the fastest expansion (200bps EBITDA by FY2026); [AS-Equita SIM] models the slowest (50–70bps, essentially flat) [#J]

  • Productivity assumption: [AS-Banca Akros] and [AS-Mediobanca] model labour cost efficiency gains from digital metering rollout (AMM2 programme in Italy) — ~€20–30m EBITDA benefit by 2026; this is a real catalyst but timing risk exists [#I]
