EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 74 findings

Zalando SE represents a structurally transitioning European fashion platform navigating a critical inflection from traditional wholesale retail toward a capital-light, multi-tenant ecosystem model encompassing both consumer-facing marketplaces and business-to-business infrastructure services. The strategic pivot centers on accelerating B2C platform expansion while simultaneously scaling B2B logistics-as-a-service and enterprise software offerings through ZEOS and SCAYLE. However, this ambitious transformation unfolds against persistently challenging macro conditions, including sluggish European GDP growth of just 1.30% and deeply depressed consumer confidence at -13.1 points, while facing intensifying competitive pressure from rapidly scaling Asian entrants like Temu and Shein capturing 2.00% market share each, and recommerce platform Vinted trebling share from 3.00% to 9.00%.

Critical plausible problem to monitor cluster around structural margin ceiling risks, as B2C gross margins compressed to 42.30% in 2025 from 43.50% in 2024 due to lower-margin ABOUT YOU integration, while B2B growth remains heavily driven by lower-margin, low-quality fulfillment services rather than high-margin software. The cross-cutting bearish failure mechanism involves a compounding macro-competitive shock where European consumers permanently shift discretionary spend away from clothing into essential categories while lower-income household budgets remain squeezed by post-pandemic inflation, simultaneously triggering promotional discounting spirals that erode targeted synergy benefits and enabling agentic AI commerce to disintermediate platform traffic entirely.
