§2 — Failure Narrative 1: BoNT Aesthetic Volume Deflation (Revenue Risk — Pricing / Volume)

Specific failure narrative:
The US aesthetics injectable market undergoes a consumer-led volume correction as discretionary spending compresses under sustained higher-for-longer interest rates and GLP-1 weight-loss adoption displaces facial aesthetics budgets in the 25-44 demographic. Galderma's Dysport volumes (already in a structurally disadvantaged position vs. Allergan's Botox with ~20-25% US market share) fall faster than market — revenue miss of 5-8% against consensus with operating leverage turning negative at the EBITDA line.

Transmission mechanism: Consumer spend compression → reduced treatment frequency → clinic throughput decline → Dysport unit pricing pressure as clinics renegotiate contracts → revenue shortfall amplified by fixed promotional spend → EBITDA miss → EPS trajectory break

Early warning signals:
- US clinic survey data showing reduced appointment booking rates in the 25-44 cohort (track Allergan/AbbVie commentary on Botox US volume in quarterly calls as leading indicator for Dysport)
- GLP-1 prescription data (Ozempic / Mounjaro) relative to facial injectable procedure volumes — any inflection where weight-management spend displaces aesthetics spend
- Galderma net revenue per treatment area in the US Injectable Aesthetics segment — if price/mix is deteriorating rather than volume alone
- Consumer confidence indices (University of Michigan, Conference Board) correlated against injectable procedure deferral in prior cycles (2008-09, 2020 precedent)
- Channel checks: dermatologist and plastic surgeon practice revenue commentary in industry publications

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — the GLP-1 displacement thesis has not yet produced measurable volume declines in aesthetics per AbbVie's 2024 commentary, but the consumer cohort correlation is real and the precedent from 2009 (aesthetics volume -15% to -20% YoY) is sector-confirmed

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: macro compression + GLP-1 spend substitution — both present ✓
- Motive: consumer reallocation of discretionary spend — structurally motivated ✓
- Opportunity: Dysport's lower brand equity vs. Botox = first-cut candidate when clinics tighten formularies ✓
- All three legs present — full failure mode, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: AbbVie commentary that overall BoNT market grew in high-single-digits through late 2024 into early 2025 (if confirmed) would invalidate the volume-deflation narrative; Galderma's own management commentary on Dysport market-share stability would be partially disconfirming but not fully (market can grow while Dysport loses share)

Monitoring checklist:
- Dysport US unit volume QoQ (from Galderma quarterly disclosures)
- AbbVie US Botox Cosmetic segment growth as proxy market indicator
- GLP-1 script volume trend (IQVIA data)
- Galderma US Injectable Aesthetics revenue vs. Skincare revenue divergence (if injectables underperform skincare, signals the mix shift is real)
- Clinic-level promotional intensity — rising rebate offers = volume stress

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Narrative 2 (pricing pressure) and Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure); null-interaction with Narrative 9 (pipeline/biologics)

Sceptical close: The disconfirming evidence that would invalidate this hypothesis — Galderma publicly disclosing consistent US Dysport volume growth in the mid-to-high single digit range — has not been made structurally trackable because the company does not segment unit volume from value in its primary disclosures ❌

§6 — Failure Narrative 5: Operating Leverage Inversion at EBITDA — FALSE FRIEND #1 (Margin Risk)

Specific failure narrative [FALSE FRIEND]:
Galderma's EBITDA margin expands in H1 2026 reporting — appearing to confirm the thesis — but the expansion is driven by: (a) post-IPO marketing normalisation (promotional spend rationalised after the 2023 IPO build), (b) deferral of R&D spend into H2, and (c) one-time SG&A leverage from headcount freeze. When H2 revenue growth disappoints (driven by any of Narratives 1-4), the deferred costs re-enter the P&L while revenue misses, producing a "margin cliff" that hits EPS sharply. The thesis appeared confirmed through Q2 reporting and breaks sharply at Q3.

Transmission mechanism: Promotional spend deferral → H1 EBITDA beat → analyst upgrades → higher share price → investor overconfidence → H2 revenue miss + cost normalisation → EBITDA undershoot → multiple compression from elevated base → EPS trajectory break in a single quarter with maximum share price impact (because position was built on the "confirmation" signal)

Early warning signals:
- H1 vs. H2 EBITDA seasonality pattern (2024 actuals as baseline — if H1 margin is unusually high vs. H2, this is a flag)
- R&D spend as a % of revenue — any dip below 3-year average signals deferral
- SG&A headcount disclosures — hiring freeze followed by accelerated hiring is the tell
- Cash conversion cycle — if working capital absorbs the promotional spend deferral, it shows in receivables
- Management guidance language on H2 cost phasing — listen for hedging language

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — post-IPO cost normalisation is a documented phenomenon across healthcare-adjacent consumer companies (precedent: GALP, Neogen, Grifols post-IPO cost rationalisation waves), and Galderma's IPO was March 2024, putting the normalisation window squarely in 2025-2026

⚡ Outlier signal: The FALSE FRIEND structure here is particularly dangerous because Galderma's investor base was assembled in the IPO context — meaning consensus models were built on the post-IPO "clean" cost base, not the fully-loaded normalised cost base. The margin expansion could be amplified by a structural modelling artefact, not just operational deferral.

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: management discretion over R&D and promotional phasing — confirmed ✓
- Motive: post-IPO pressure to demonstrate margin improvement to new institutional holders ✓
- Opportunity: H1 revenue seasonality in aesthetics (spring treatment season) enables front-loading without immediate visibility cost ✓
- All three legs present — this is the highest-MMO false-friend scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Full-year R&D spend as % of revenue maintaining consistent with 3-year pre-IPO average; H2 EBITDA margin sustaining within 50bps of H1; management providing explicit quarterly cost phasing disclosure would substantially invalidate

Monitoring checklist:
- H1 vs. H2 EBITDA margin split (2024 and 2025 actuals)
- R&D % of revenue by half
- SG&A absolute spend QoQ (not % — look for absolute reduction followed by rebound)
- Management commentary on "investment" vs. "efficiency" framing for cost actions
- Free cash flow conversion vs. EBITDA (deferred promotional spend shows up as working capital not FCF)

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the scenario most likely to AMPLIFY all other failure modes — because it delays detection by a full half-year, meaning the position is held through the deterioration with the investor believing confirmation signals

Sceptical close: The specific data that would invalidate this — granular quarterly cost phasing across R&D and marketing — is not provided in Galderma's standard quarterly disclosure format, making this false-friend scenario structurally under-monitored ❌

§7 — Failure Narrative 6: Input Cost Inflation in Biologics Manufacturing (Margin Risk — Cost Inflation)

Specific failure narrative:
Galderma's biologics manufacturing cost base — critical for both the BoNT complex (Dysport) and the pipeline injectable-biologic candidates — faces sustained inflation in: fermentation feedstock costs, sterile fill-finish capacity pricing (tightened globally post-COVID biosecurity capex decisions), and qualified workforce compensation in Swiss/European manufacturing sites. This compresses gross margin by 150-250bps, flowing directly to EBITDA and EPS — a cost-push margin miss that is structurally harder to recover than a revenue miss.

Transmission mechanism: Input cost inflation → gross margin compression → EBITDA margin miss → EPS below trajectory → re-rating of the margin-expansion narrative → multiple compression alongside earnings miss → double-impact on IRR

Early warning signals:
- European energy costs (TTF gas benchmark) — sterile manufacturing is energy-intensive
- Swiss CPI and pharmaceutical wage inflation data - Third-party CMO pricing commentary (e.g., Samsung Biologics, Lonza earnings calls)
- Galderma gross margin % vs. consensus — any widening underperformance gap
- CHF/EUR cross — Swiss-manufactured product costs in EUR-reporting context

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — European energy prices moderated in 2024-25 from 2022 peaks, but Swiss wage inflation for specialised biologics manufacturing staff remains elevated; CMO pricing has not fully normalised per Lonza and Samsung Biologics 2024 disclosures

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: structural input cost pressures (energy, wages, CMO capacity) — confirmed ✓
- Motive: N/A (cost inflation is exogenous — no "motive" required) — modified test: Is the company unable to pass through costs? Yes, in a price-sensitive consumer market ✓
- Opportunity: Long-term supply contracts may buffer short-term inflation (opportunity to delay onset but not prevent) ✓
- Two-of-three legs (cost means + passthrough limitation) — downgrade to WATCH ONLY in isolation, but amplifier when combined with Narrative 5

Disconfirming evidence: Galderma reporting stable-to-improving gross margin % for three consecutive quarters with disclosed commentary on supply chain hedging would substantially invalidate this narrative

Monitoring checklist:
- Gross margin % QoQ
- Management commentary on COGS phasing and supply chain hedging
- TTF gas and European energy benchmarks
- Lonza / Samsung Biologics CMO pricing commentary per quarter
- CHF/EUR cross vs. guidance sensitivity disclosure

Interaction with other failure modes: Additive with Narrative 5 (operating leverage inversion — cost inflation amplifies the deferred-cost normalisation impact); additive with Narrative 7 (currency — CHF cost base + non-CHF revenue = double exposure)

Sceptical close: Management has incentive to frame input cost pressures as temporary and hedged — the disconfirming data (sustained gross margin improvement) is the same metric management controls through presentation, creating an interpretation risk

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — CHF has historically appreciated in risk-off environments; but Galderma's hedging programme (detailed in 2024 annual report) provides 12-18M buffer, suggesting the exposure is a 2026-2027 risk rather than immediate

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:

Interaction with other failure modes: Silent amplifier to Narrative 1 (BoNT volume decline) and Narrative 3 (China revenue miss) — a currency miss compounds a volume miss without being detectable until the quarterly report; correlated with Narrative 6 (CHF cost base)

Sceptical close: The false-friend element here is specifically that the company legitimately reports constant-currency growth accurately — it is NOT misleading management; but the investor anchored to constant-currency misses the shareholder-level EPS degradation
