EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model Resilience and Revenue Mechanics

The revenue model exhibits high independence from general economic cycles because products are medically prescribed and insurance-funded regardless of macro interest rates or GDP fluctuations . North America

represents extreme geographic concentration at 76% of FY 2025 net sales , creating substantial exposure to US healthcare policy decisions. Only 2% of annual diagnoses receive communication aids despite a 50 million global population unable to communicate verbally , establishing massive long-term penetration runway. Approximately 120 internal employees or 15% of total workforce operate as dedicated Funding Experts navigating complex multi-stage insurance documentation , representing a structural moat that generic technology entrants cannot economically replicate. Semi-fixed reimbursement rates limit short-term pricing flexibility, though Medicare adjusted rates upward by 9% in 2023 and 2.6% in 2024 .

Competitive Position and Market Structure

Foundation Quality and Operational Moats

Flagship software TD Snap creates impenetrable clinical lock-in through proprietary Picture Communication Symbols that cause extreme cognitive friction when users attempt platform migration . Extensive speechlanguage pathologist training creates formidable diagnostic referral barriers, with brand reputation representing the global clinical gold standard . The proprietary network of over 675 payer contracts combined with a 120- person funding specialist team blocks smaller non-integrated software entrants from competing . Consolidated gross margins sustain 67-70% levels with operating margins expanding 2.5 percentage points to 9.8% in Q1 2026 , demonstrating clear operating leverage as restructuring investments complete. The massive underpenetrated TAM of USD 4.7 billion and 98% unserved patient pipeline naturally consolidate toward Dynavox sales teams through brand dominance .
