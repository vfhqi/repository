EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Unipol SpA has executed material corporate transformation through the late-2024 absorption of UnipolSai, streamlining decision-making processes under CEO Matteo Laterza while expanding board composition from fifteen to nineteen members to secure deep insurance expertise, though the governance structure remains under regulatory scrutiny following the 2023 IVASS inspection and ongoing antitrust litigation . The company's strategic ambition centers on the 2025-2027 plan targeting cumulative consolidated net profits of €3.8bn with excess organic capital generation of €1bn , positioning Unipol to capitalize on structural Italian insurance market dynamics while navigating execution risks inherent in complex bancassurance integrations. The strategic architecture features Unipol's 19.9% shareholding in BPER Banca with a proposed acquisition of 635 BMPS branches for €3.0bn to €3.5bn, potentially achieving de facto control over BPER with a 30% stake and generating approximately €800m in synergies . This consolidation leverages BPER's completed merger of Banca Popolare di Sondrio, creating a combined banking platform serving approximately six million clients through commercial networks with confirmed integration synergies of €290m by fiscal year 2027 . However, BPER CEO Gianni Franco Papa acknowledged that extracting €290 million of synergies by 2027 represents a very

difficult exercise, with the bancassurance integration presenting severe execution risks that could impair strategic plan targets .

The five to eight primary earnings drivers exhibit differentiated plausibility profiles across financial, operational, and strategic dimensions. First, Unipol aims for €10.6bn Non-Life premiums by 2027 growing at a 4.9% CAGR with Health premiums projected to expand at a 7.7% CAGR to €1.4bn, while stated targets aim to lower the Non-Life combined ratio to 92.0% by 2027 . Direct insurance income expanded to €17,361m in FY25, up 11.10% from €15,621m in FY24, with specialized healthcare subsidiary UniSalute delivering 15.40% direct premium growth to €1,183.8m , establishing structural health growth through UniSalute as a high-plausibility driver anchored by aging population demographics and private welfare demand. Second, bancassurance channel penetration represents a high-plausibility driver as pre-tax profits from banking associates BPER and BPSO reached €604m in 9M25, with consolidated net profit in 9M25 boosted by a €150m net gain from exchange tender offers . Third, digital cost efficiencies through the Unica platform and UniSalute 2.0 centralization constitute a mediumplausibility driver constrained by legacy systems complexity and regulatory compliance costs. Fourth, strategic asset returns delivered a 4.3% running yield in Q1 2026, a 17 basis points year-on-year increase , representing a high-plausibility driver supported by eurozone rate normalization, though Italian Govies represent 30.3% of strategic asset allocation with a 100 bps widening in the Italian Government spread cutting Solvency II by eight percentage points . Fifth, underwriting discipline targeting a 92.0% combined ratio remains a mediumplausibility driver heavily dependent on weather volatility , as claims provisions in 2023 were adversely impacted by severe atmospheric flooding in the Emilia Romagna region with third-quarter Combined Ratio escalating to 98.60% due to catastrophic July hailstorms in Northern Italy .

Recent delivery trends demonstrate strong momentum as direct insurance premiums grew by 7.1% year-on-year to €4,809m in Q1 2026 with Life premiums delivering double-digit expansion of 11.2% to €2,338m, while the Non- Life combined ratio improved by 1.1 percentage points to 90.0% . Standalone Insurance Group Solvency II ratio surged to 295%, up sixteen percentage points from fiscal year 2025, while the post-combination consolidated Solvency II ratio increased to 248% from 230% at year-end 2025 . Historical guidance credibility reflects an honest-tracker to sandbagger posture archetype, as Unipol delivered €2,957m cumulative consolidated net profit during 2022-2024, exceeding the €2,300m target by 29%, with cumulative dividends distributed reaching €1,283m, beating the €958m strategic plan target by 34%, and cumulative insurance net profit reaching €2,319m, representing a 10% beat against the guided €2,104m .
