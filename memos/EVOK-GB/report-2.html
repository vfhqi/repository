Evoke plc is a leading global betting and gaming company operating renowned brands such as William Hill, 888,
and Mr Green. In May 2024, the group officially rebranded from 888 Holdings to Evoke plc. This rebranding
was a pivotal step to unite the legacy 888 and William Hill businesses under a cohesive corporate identity and a
shared "One Company" culture. CEO Per Widerström, who took the helm in October 2023, noted that the legacy
businesses had not been performing to their full potential despite possessing strong proprietary technology and
global brand recognition. Consequently, the newly assembled executive team implemented a comprehensive
Value Creation Plan aimed at executing a fundamental reset of the business model. 1 • PROXY 2 3

company's capabilities. A central pillar of this turnaround is the transition to a streamlined "Glocal" operating

Revenue Growth and Segment Performance
Evoke plc has demonstrated a consistent return to top-line stability, recording a 3% revenue increase to £1,754.5
million in the 2024 fiscal year. This positive momentum accelerated throughout 2025, culminating in five
consecutive quarters of year-over-year revenue expansion by the third quarter. During the first half of 2025, total revenue reached £888 million, representing a 4% increase on a constant currency basis. The International
segment acted as the primary growth engine, delivering a 13% revenue surge fueled by double-digit gains in core markets like Italy, Spain, Romania, and Denmark. Conversely, UK and Ireland (UK&I) Online revenue experienced a slight 1% contraction during the same period. This domestic decline was largely intentional,
reflecting a strategic shift away from unprofitable promotional activities toward higher-value customer retention. The group ultimately finished the 2025 fiscal year strongly, with fourth-quarter revenues reaching £464 million

Profitability and Adjusted EBITDA Margins
The strategic pivot from chasing low-margin volume to prioritizing profitable growth has yielded a significant step- change in Evoke's earnings profile. During the first half of 2025, adjusted EBITDA surged by 44% year-over-year
to £166 million. This margin expansion was driven by a combination of higher gross margins, structural cost
optimization, and significantly improved returns on marketing spend. The effectiveness of this disciplined
approach was particularly evident in the UK&I Online division, which posted a 37% increase in EBITDA despite its
flat revenue trajectory. Operating leverage has also improved as the company scales the use of artificial

year, management expects adjusted EBITDA to land between £355 million and £360 million. This performance

Efficient capital structuring and debt reduction remain critical priorities for the executive team as they seek to
accelerate shareholder equity returns. Evoke's gross borrowings stood at £1,834.8 million midway through 2025, comprising a mix of sterling, euro, and US dollar-denominated facilities. M&A Developments and Strategic Review
Following the severe external financial headwinds introduced by the UK Autumn Budget, Evoke's board formally
initiated a comprehensive strategic review in December 2025 to explore all available alternatives for maximizing
shareholder value. The company appointed Morgan Stanley and Rothschild & Co as joint financial advisers to
guide the ongoing evaluation process. The board indicated that this review encompasses a wide range of
potential outcomes, including the outright sale of the entire group or the divestiture of specific assets and
business units. 63 64 • CIRC 65 • IRPRESS
