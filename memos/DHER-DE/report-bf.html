EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Delivery Hero is executing a high-stakes transition from loss-making geographic sprawl into a profit-focused Everyday App leveraging high-margin AdTech and Quick Commerce, but this strategic pivot occurs under severe activist pressure following the announced March 2027 exit of co-founder Niklas Östberg. The leadership succession was accelerated immediately after activist Aspex Management built a roughly 14% stake and publicly criticized legacy execution errors. The platform occupies defensible local network positions in Korea and MENA generating over 90% of GMV from number-one markets, yet these moats remain fragile against capital-rich disruptors like Meituan's Keeta and Coupang Eats deploying aggressive subsidy campaigns. The sector has entered a global consolidation paradigm, with Uber accumulating a 36.8% economic ownership stake and multiple takeover scenarios under active evaluation.

Industry Dynamics and Competitive Intensity

Meituan's Keeta launched in Saudi Arabia with a capital commitment exceeding SAR 1B and captured approximately one-third of the Riyadh food delivery market within its first month. Keeta offered aggressive pricing making it at least 20% cheaper than Talabat, forcing HungerStation to fast-track its HPlus subscription which reached 10M subscribers defensively. Coupang Eats is utilizing unlimited free delivery bundled into its WOW membership to disrupt Woowa's Baemin dominance in South Korea, triggering the defensive launch of Baemin Club covering nearly 50% of order volumes.

Customer Economics and Loyalty Dynamics

Multi-vertical platform customers generated 55% of platform GMV during Q1 2026 and spend five times more on average than customers ordering prepared meals exclusively. When meal delivery customers place their first Quick Commerce order, monthly food frequency increases from 4.6 to 5.9 orders, with Quick Commerce GMV growing 30% like-for-like YoY comprising 18% of the group mix. The talabat pro program drives a 22% uplift in order frequency after customer adoption, with high-value subscriber retention sustained because partner brands fund the vast majority of benefits. Customer cohorts exhibit highly predictable patterns with both order frequency and basket sizes expanding over time, with reorder rates consistently accounting for more than 95% of total platform transactions. Platform pricing strategies prioritize targeted incentives for high-value customers over dilutive broad discounting, with management deliberately avoiding deep vouchers for promo-driven users classified as essentially useless for long-term retention.
