§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: NKT-DK's earnings quality is broadly clean with one structural caveat — project revenue recognition creates periodic lumpiness, but OCF/NI conversion has tracked well and the adjustments trend is not deteriorating [#J]

Recurring vs one-off: Adjusted vs reported EPS gap is narrow and stable — in FY24, the reported/adjusted gap is less than DKK 1 per share; NKT does not have a pattern of large recurring "one-offs" [AS-Berenberg] [#A]

  • Sub: FY22 settlement income (~DKK 130m) was the largest single non-recurring item in L3Y; it has not recurred, consistent with management's one-off framing [#A]
  • Sub: Restructuring charges have been sub-DKK 80m in each period — modest for a company at this revenue scale [#I]

Adjustments trend: Adjustments are shrinking as a proportion of reported earnings — FY22 adjusted EPS was materially higher than reported (settlement gain netted out); FY24 delta is negligible; the direction is positive [#J]

Non-GAAP gap: NKT does not publish aggressive non-GAAP metrics; the company reports EBIT and NIBD (net interest-bearing debt) as primary measures, which are relatively clean by industrial standards [AS-Carnegie] [#A]

NWC tailwind / headwind: NWC has been a modest headwind in growth phases — as revenues have grown rapidly, trade receivables and work-in-progress on projects have grown; this is mechanically expected but bears watching if revenue slows while NWC does not unwind [#A]

  • Sub: NKT receives advance payments on some large projects (reducing NWC exposure), but the balance shifts period-to-period depending on milestone timing [#I]
  • Sub: Inventory days have risen modestly with capacity expansion but remain within normal industrial ranges [AS-SEB] [#I]

Cash conversion: OCF/EBITDA conversion has generally tracked 70-85% across L3Y — slightly below 100% due to working capital consumption during growth, but not structurally deficient [#J]

  • Sub: FY24 OCF was reported at approximately DKK 2.8-3.0bn vs EBITDA of DKK 4.2bn — roughly 67-71% conversion; the gap is largely NWC and growth CapEx, not earnings manipulation ❌ [#A]
  • Sub: ❌ The 67-71% FY24 OCF/EBITDA ratio, while directionally normal for a growth capex phase, is below the 80-90% range typical of mature industrial compounders and warrants monitoring as capacity investment peaks [#I]

D&A trend: D&A rising in absolute terms (consistent with accelerating CapEx for capacity expansion — Cologne 2, Cologne fibre upgrades), but as a % of revenue D&A has been broadly stable at ~3.5-4% [#A]

  • Sub: No evidence of aggressive D&A extension or capitalisation of costs that should be expensed [AS-Jefferies] [#A]

Receivables turn: Receivables days have edged up from ~55 days (FY22) to ~65 days (FY24) as project portfolio scales; this is within normal bounds but accelerating revenue growth masks the underlying build [#A]

OCF vs NI: OCF consistently exceeds net income across L3Y — D&A add-back more than compensates for NWC drag in most periods; the ratio is positive and not deteriorating [#J]

Conservative accounting: NKT appears to use percentage-of-completion accounting for projects on a conservative basis — margin recognition lags physical completion in some contracts, suggesting earnings may slightly understate economic reality in rapid-growth periods [AS-ABG Sundal Collier] [#A]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? The mechanism is project-based revenue recognition: if NKT has been front-loading margin recognition on contracts where physical risk remains back-end-loaded (marine cable installation, grid operator acceptance), a cluster of project completions could trigger margin write-backs not visible in L3Y data [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: NKT-DK's margin expansion looks structurally driven rather than cyclical — mix shift to high-value Solutions projects plus manufacturing utilisation leverage are durable mechanisms; the pace of expansion will likely moderate but the level should hold [#J]

Revenue CAGR: FY22-FY24 revenue CAGR = ~22%; consensus implies ~10-12% forward CAGR as base effects normalise [#J]

Profit CAGR: FY22-FY24 EBIT CAGR = ~104% (>2x revenue CAGR) — the operating leverage is exceptional by any industrial standard [#J]

  • Sub: EBITDA CAGR FY22-FY24 = ~78%; still dramatically above revenue CAGR, confirming leverage is pervasive not just at EBIT line [#A]

Operating leverage: ~2x operating leverage ratio (EBIT growth / revenue growth) across L3Y — each 1% revenue increase generated ~2% EBIT increase; this is a function of high fixed-cost base at Cables + project mix improving at Solutions [#J]

Gross margin: Expanded from ~16% (FY22) to ~26% (FY24) — 10pp in two years; driven by Solutions project mix enrichment and Cables segment pricing [#J]

  • Sub: Solutions EBIT margins approaching mid-to-high teens on major offshore wind/interconnector contracts — structurally richer than prior maintenance/service work [AS-Berenberg] [#A]
  • Sub: Cables segment (Nordkabel + retail cables) margins less dramatic but benefiting from copper cable demand and volume throughput [#I]

EBITDA margin trajectory: ~9% (FY22) → ~12% (FY23) → ~19% (FY24) → ~20% (H1-25e) — a clean staircase with no counter-direction periods [#J]

Cost cut driver: Margin expansion is NOT primarily cost-cut driven; headcount has grown with capacity; SG&A has risen in absolute terms; the driver is gross margin enrichment and volume leverage [AS-Kepler Cheuvreux] [#J]

Mix shift: Solutions as % of group EBIT has risen from ~40% (FY22) to ~65%+ (FY24) — this structural mix shift is the single most important margin driver and is sustainable as long as offshore wind buildout continues [#A]

Scale driver: Cables manufacturing utilisation has risen toward capacity; fixed-cost absorption per metre of cable produced has improved materially; Cologne factory at or near nameplate [AS-Citi] [#A]

Sustainability assessment: Mid-teen EBITDA margins appear sustainable through FY26-27 given backlog visibility; the risk is margin mean-reversion post-supercycle when competitive dynamics normalise; current 19-20% may represent a peak rather than a floor [#J]

Margin runway: Incremental margin expansion from current levels is harder to achieve — gross margin at 26% is approaching what mixed industrials with comparable project exposure typically sustain; further expansion requires either pricing power maintenance or further mix enrichment [#I]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? The mechanism is competitive entry: as high cable margins attract new entrants (Prysmian, Nexans, and potential Asian entrants), pricing power erodes and Solutions project margins compress from current elevated levels back toward historical 8-10% EBIT range — a 5-7pp EBIT margin compression from FY24 levels would roughly halve EBIT [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: NKT-DK exhibits pronounced and well-established H2 weighting, with H2 revenues and margins typically materially exceeding H1 due to project delivery schedules; pattern is consistent but not rigidly predictable [#J]

H1 pattern: H1 is structurally the weaker half — project milestones, sea-lay campaigns, and commissioning are disproportionately back-half weighted; winter weather constraints on cable-laying suppress H1 execution [#J]

  • Sub: H1 2024 EBIT (~DKK 1.22bn) vs FY24 EBIT (~DKK 3.41bn) implies H2 2024 EBIT of ~DKK 2.19bn — roughly 64% of full-year in H2 [#A]
  • Sub: H1 2023 EBIT (~DKK 530m) vs FY23 EBIT (~DKK 1.35bn) implies H2 2023 EBIT of ~DKK 820m — 61% of full-year in H2; pattern consistent [#A]

H2 pattern: H2 is consistently the delivery half; cable-laying vessels operate in summer/autumn weather windows; project milestones cluster in Q3-Q4; management guidance implicitly (and explicitly) signals H2 loading [#J]

Seasonality direction: Seasonality may be intensifying — as Solutions project mix rises, and projects are inherently lumpier than cable sales, the H1/H2 split may widen further; FY24 split at 64% H2 is wider than FY22 implied split [#A]

Cause — production: Marine installation seasonality is the dominant cause — cable-lay vessels are constrained by North Sea weather windows (April-October primary window); this is structural and will not change [#J]

Cause — buying pattern: Grid operator and utility budget cycles also contribute — H2 capex approvals at utilities and TSOs tend to trigger milestone payments and project acceptances [#I]

Q1/Q3 trading updates: Q1 updates (calendar-year) consistently signal H1 will be back-half loaded within H1 — even within the half, Q2 > Q1 [AS-ABG Sundal Collier] [#I]

Strongest quarter: Q4 consistently the strongest quarter for deliveries, invoicing, and profitability; margin in Q4 is typically highest due to project completion recognition [#J]

Weakest quarter: Q1 consistently the weakest — winter weather, low marine activity, project ramp-up phase [#J]

Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? The mechanism is project delay compounding: if a key project slips from Q4 to Q1 of the following year (as happened in Q3-23 with Bornholm), the seasonal pattern becomes an earnings void — H2 weighting means a single project delay can miss consensus by 10-15% and the natural recovery comes only after six months of negative narrative [#J]

Beat magnitude: Average EBIT beat vs consensus-prior = ~DKK 100-150m per period (~8-13%); beat magnitude has RISEN over time (FY24 beat ~DKK 310m vs FY22 beat ~DKK 120m) — consistent with expanding earnings power outrunning analyst model updates [#J]

  • Sub: EPS beat pattern mirrors EBIT beat — every period delivered adjusted EPS above consensus-prior, with H1-24 and FY24 showing the largest beats [AS-Citi] [#A]

Sandbagging signal: Guidance pattern is structurally conservative — NKT typically guides EBIT ranges where the midpoint proves to be the floor, not the midpoint; FY24 EBIT guidance was DKK 3.0-3.5bn upgraded to 3.0-3.8bn, vs actual DKK 3.41bn — delivered at top end of raised guidance [#J]

Sell-side revision pattern: Consensus has been in continuous upward revision mode since H2 2022 — analysts consistently ratchet up FY+1 and FY+2 estimates after each NKT reporting period; no significant downward revision cycle in L3Y except the brief Q3-23 Bornholm delay [AS-SEB, AS-Jefferies] [#J]
