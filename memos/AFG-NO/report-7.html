KEY FINDINGS (BLUF)

  • Cohort misfit is the primary analytical problem. AFG is a Norwegian EPC contractor (civil engineering ~40%, building/construction ~25%, offshore/energy ~15%, Sweden ~15%) — not a technical installation company. The business model is project-bid, fixed-price execution, no installed base. Bravida runs ~50% service/maintenance revenue with 7.5% EBITA; AFG runs ~10-15% recurring revenue with 3.5-5% EBT. These are structurally different businesses. The cohort comparison is valid for sizing but not for benchmarking quality or applying the same FD framework. #J

  • No company-specific fulcrum driver has been identified. The Norwegian infrastructure cycle (NTP 2025-2036) is the dominant earnings engine, but it is a sector-beta tailwind shared with Veidekke, NCC, Skanska, and Strabag. The Claxton Engineering acquisition (April 2026, GBP 63.5m) is the closest thing to a company-specific catalyst — but it deepens fixed-price offshore exposure immediately after a loss. There is no proprietary recurring revenue base, no re-rating story separable from the Norwegian construction cycle, and no evidence of franchise value above the sector. #J

  • Margin recovery is real but arithmetically fragile. EBT margin went from 2.3% (2023 trough) to 3.5% (2024) to 5.2% (2025). Civil Engineering is the engine at 7-9% EBIT margins; the rest of the group — Building, Offshore, Sweden — delivers thin or negative margins. Strip Civil out and the remaining AFG portfolio resembles NCC in quality: below 3% EBIT, volatile, bid-dependent. The group average is a blend of a good business dragged by three mediocre ones. #A

  • Offshore is a structural drag, not a one-off. Three consecutive Q4 loss provisions (Q4 2023, Q4 2024, Q4 2025) meet the Cockroaches Never Alone threshold. Claxton doubles down on the same contract structure (fixed-price lump-sum offshore decommissioning) that generated the losses. Management language post-Q4 2025 ("all campaigns complete") is precise enough to deflect but not definitive enough to close the risk. #J

  • Norwegian infrastructure cycle is the central driver, and it is durable — but it is sector-level, not company-specific. NTP 2025-2036 commits USD 40bn+ to rail and civil infrastructure over 12 years; AFG is a qualified beneficiary. But so is Veidekke, which runs similar margins on similar contract structures with similar public-sector clients. The cycle is real; the AFG-specific edge within it is undemonstrated. #A

  • Dividend sustainability looks solid in the base case. NOK 6.50 proposed dividend (FY2025, +30% YoY) is covered ~1.5x by earnings (EPS ~NOK 9.99) and ~4.7x by operating cash flow (NOK 3.04bn). The FCF profile is the strongest quality feature of the business. But the payout ratio of ~65% is high for a contractor with significant fixed-price execution risk, and any material Offshore provision or Building reversal reduces cover fast. #A

  • Prior verdict of PARK stands. The combination of cohort misfit, no demonstrable company-specific FD, recurring Offshore losses, and structural margin ceiling (~5% EBT group at best, vs 7-8% EBITA for genuine technical installation compounder) means AFG does not clear the quality bar for the Nordic Technical Installation cohort. It may be investable within the Norwegian Construction cohort — but that requires a different analytical frame. #J

  • Reclassification to European Construction cohort is the correct disposition. AFG already has a prior research file under the European Construction cohort (VEI-NO, STR-AT, SCYR-ES). It belongs there. The CQs in that cohort — order book quality, margin trajectory, cyclical vs defensive mix — are designed for AFG's business model. The Nordic Technical Installation CQs (service/maintenance mix, M&A engine, Assemblin-Caverion impact) mostly do not apply. #J

§1 DRIVER OVERVIEW

Ranked by importance to the investment thesis (highest first):

  1. Norwegian infrastructure cycle — primary earnings driver. Civil Engineering at 7-9% EBIT is the group's quality asset and is directly fed by NTP 2025-2036. Weight in group earnings: ~60% of EBIT at current margins, ~40% of revenue. #I
  2. Margin recovery path — the trajectory from 2.3% trough (2023) to 5.2% (2025) is the valuation re-rating thesis. The question is whether 5-6% is a ceiling or a floor for the group. #I
  3. Energy/Offshore segment resolution — three consecutive Q4 losses. Claxton doubles the bet. This is the most binary risk item: if fixed, it adds ~1-2% to group EBIT margin; if it recurs, it continues to cap the group at below-7% margin ambition. #I
  4. Dividend sustainability — the 3.6% yield at NOK 178 is the primary attraction for income-oriented holders. Its durability is a direct function of FCF quality, which is currently strong but depends on items 1-3 above. #I
  5. M&A consolidation play — is AFG a strategic consolidator? At NOK 32bn revenue, it is Norway's second-largest contractor behind Veidekke. Acquisition of Claxton is an outlier in the company's history of disciplined NOK 100-500m bolt-on deals. #I
  6. Technical & Industry segment growth — can the Energy & Environment / Offshore cluster grow to become a genuine technical installation peer? Currently ~8-10% of revenue, ~5% EBIT margin. Too small and too volatile to be the FD. #I
  7. Assemblin-Caverion merger impact — minimal direct impact. AFG competes in civil infrastructure and general contracting, not the HVAC/electrical installation market where Assemblin-Caverion dominates. This CQ primarily belongs to BRAV/INSTAL/FASAD. #I

Cross-cutting verdict: The driver map confirms there is no company-specific FD. Drivers 1-4 are all either sector-beta (Norwegian construction cycle), financial metrics (margin recovery, dividend), or risk items (Offshore). Driver 5 is modest at current deal scale. Drivers 6-7 are immaterial to the thesis. This is a well-run contractor in a good cycle — not a compounder or a change-force story. #J

§2 DRIVER 1: NORWEGIAN INFRASTRUCTURE CYCLE

Definition: Norwegian government infrastructure spending under NTP 2025-2036, with a near-doubling of rail maintenance budgets and USD 40bn+ committed to roads/tunnels/bridges. AFG's Civil Engineering segment (~40% of revenue by backlog weighting) is the direct beneficiary.

Evidence:

  • NTP 2025-2036 budget: approximately NOK 1.2 trillion total committed, with rail maintenance nearly doubled to NOK 6.6bn for 2025 alone. This is government-backed visibility over a 12-year horizon. #I
  • E39 highway project (NOK 33bn+ total authorised) and numerous tunnel and bridge projects are in active procurement. AFG has secured civil backlog of NOK 17,966m (end-2025), approximately 40% of group backlog. #I
  • Civil Engineering revenue grew ~30% YoY in Q4 2024 and ~16% in Q1 2025 — the acceleration is not seasonal noise. #I
  • Civil EBIT margin delivered: ~9.5% Q4 2025, ~7% full-year 2025 — materially above the 3-5% typical of European general contractors. #I
  • Judgement: The structural reason for this outperformance is AFG's position in alliance/framework contracts with Norwegian public infrastructure clients (Statens vegvesen, Bane NOR, Statnett), which provide partial cost reimbursement and reduce fixed-price execution risk versus a pure lump-sum contractor. This is a genuine competitive moat within Civil — but it is shared with Veidekke, which runs a near-identical model. #J
  • Norwegian construction output: contracted -1.8% (2023) and -3.9% (2024) in real terms; recovering to +2% estimated (2025). The residential and commercial sectors are weak; public infrastructure is the counterweight. #I
  • Norges Bank rate trajectory: benchmark cut from 4.5% peak toward 3.75-4.0% in 2025, supporting marginal improvement in private investment appetite. Not yet at levels that would unlock residential pipeline materially. #I

Transmission mechanism: Norwegian government approves NTP appropriations → Statens vegvesen / Bane NOR tender large contracts → AFG bids via alliance or competitive tender → contract awards feed Civil Engineering backlog (NOK 17.9bn at end-2025) → multiyear revenue execution at 7-9% EBIT margins → group earnings.

Bear case:
- The NTP is a headline commitment, not ring-fenced appropriations. Political cycle risk: a new government (Norwegian elections due 2025) could reprioritise or slow the infrastructure budget. #J
- The bidding pool has deepened: Strabag, Skanska, NCC, and international majors are increasingly active in Norwegian public tenders as the programme scale attracts pan-European competition. Margin pressure on new tenders is not a near-term certainty but is directionally negative. #A
- Mean reversion: the 9.5% Q4 2025 Civil margin is above the full-year ~7% norm and reflects favourable project completion timing. As new work at competitive tender margins enters the backlog, the segment margin could normalise to 6-7%, compressing group EBIT by ~50-80 bps. #A

Sceptical bullets:
- Norway's infrastructure cycle benefits all large Nordic contractors (VEI, NCC, Skanska), not AFG specifically. The argument that AFG is a "beneficiary" of NTP is equivalent to saying any Norwegian contractor is. There is no proprietary position. #A
- ❌ Civil at 9% margins is a high-watermark, not a structural new level. The full-year average is 7% — and even that may moderate as new NTP projects are tendered at current competitive spreads. #A

Plausibility verdict: The Norwegian infrastructure cycle is a genuine, durable, multi-year tailwind for AFG Civil Engineering. Its durability is high (government policy, 12-year horizon). But it is sector-wide, not company-specific, and the margin sustainability at 7-9% is a question rather than a certainty. REAL and DURABLE, but NOT DIFFERENTIATING. This driver alone does not generate above-peer returns.

§3 DRIVER 2: MARGIN RECOVERY PATH

Definition: AFG's group EBT margin trough in 2023 (2.3%) and subsequent recovery to 3.5% (2024) and 5.2% (2025). Can the company reach and sustain the stated 7%+ EBIT target from the 2025-2028 strategy?

Evidence:

  • The recovery arc: EBT margin 4.5% (2022) → 2.3% (2023 trough) → 3.5% (2024) → 5.2% (2025). This is a 290 bps improvement over two years — material for a contractor. #I
  • Judgement: The recovery is real. However, it is driven almost entirely by two factors: (1) Civil Engineering delivering 7-9% EBIT margins sustained by public sector demand, and (2) Building/Betonmast recovering from the Betonmast Boligbygg residential write-down trough. Neither is a step-change in business quality; both are cyclical recoveries from crisis-level lows. #J
  • Building Q4 2025: EBIT margin ~6%, 48% above consensus — a genuine beat. The driver is project mix shift toward renovation and public commercial work (AF Byggfornyelse, AF Bygg Oslo delivering "very good" results). #I
  • Sceptical: Q4 2025 Building order intake was -37% YoY (NOK 2,579m vs NOK 4,070m) — the pipeline filling the 2026 workbook is thinner than the 2025 workbook. Sustaining 5-6% Building margins requires the public/commercial mix to hold and residential recovery to accelerate. Neither is certain. #A
  • Input cost inflation: Norwegian construction cost inflation peaked 2022-2023; moderating through 2025. Fixed-price contracts signed in the inflationary period are completing; new work at current inflation rates reduces provision risk. This is a tailwind for Building margin recovery. #I
  • Peer margin benchmark:
  • Strabag: 6.1% EBIT (2024) — best-in-class pan-European contractor
  • Skanska Construction: ~4.5%
  • Veidekke: ~3.9-4.1% (2024 pre-tax)
  • AFG: ~3.8% EBIT (2024), 5.2% EBT (2025)
  • NCC: ~3.0% operating margin
  • Bravida: ~7.5% EBITA (includes service mix premium)
  • AFG's 2025 EBT margin of 5.2% is above Nordic contractor peers but below Strabag and well below Bravida. #I

Transmission mechanism: Input cost moderation → fewer provisions on fixed-price projects → Building margin recovery → Norway public sector mix supports Civil → group blended margin expands → EPS growth → re-rating.

Bear case:
- The 7% EBIT target (2025-2028 strategy) requires all four problem areas to perform simultaneously: Building at 5%+, Offshore at breakeven or better, Sweden at 4%+, Civil at 7%+. Historically rare. The base case achieves 5-6% not 7%. #J
- ❌ The structural margin ceiling for a general contractor without recurring service revenue is ~5-6% in normalised conditions. Bravida's 7%+ is achieved through ~50% service/maintenance mix, purchasing scale, and decentralised empowerment — none of which AFG replicates. The 7% target is aspirational, not evidenced by the business model. #J
- Further Offshore provisions in H1 2026 (as Claxton integrates and legacy campaigns close out) could drag the group back to 4.5-5.0% EBT margin for 2026 — flat to 2025, not the 12% EPS growth consensus implies. #A

Sceptical bullets:
- Building's Q4 2025 6% margin is exceptional and driven by project completion clustering. The full-year norm is likely 3-4%. #A
- Norway residential market: 21 homes sold in Q4 2024 (AFG's share: 8). This is not a recovery — it is near-zero. Building workload is sustained by public commercial/retrofit work, which is inherently less certain than funded residential pipelines. #I
- ❌ The group margin trajectory from 2.3% to 5.2% looks impressive, but base effects are flattering. In 2023 the business suffered simultaneous shocks (Betonmast write-downs, Sweden losses, Offshore provisions). A recovery to 5% from that base is expected, not exceptional. #A

Plausibility verdict: The margin recovery to 5.2% in 2025 is real and attributable to specific, identifiable drivers. The path to 7%+ requires structural improvements (Offshore resolution, Building mix shift, Sweden stability) not currently demonstrated. The recovery thesis is PLAUSIBLE TO 5-6% but the 7% target is ASPIRATIONAL, NOT EVIDENCED. This is a mid-table Norwegian contractor, not a margin-expansion compounder.

§4 DRIVER 3: ENERGY/OFFSHORE SEGMENT RESOLUTION

Definition: AF Offshore Decom (offshore platform removal) has generated project losses in Q4 2023, Q4 2024, and Q4 2025. The April 2026 Claxton Engineering acquisition (GBP 63.5m) is billed as a strategic upgrade of offshore capability. Is this being fixed — or is the risk growing?

Evidence:

  • Three consecutive Q4 offshore loss events:
  • Q4 2023: material project provision, AF Offshore Decom. #I
  • Q4 2024: further Offshore Decom project loss. #I
  • Q4 2025: ~NOK 100m loss from one fixed-price decommissioning campaign. Management stated "all planned campaigns are complete." This caused the 9% EBIT miss vs Kepler Cheuvreux consensus. #I
  • Judgement: Three consecutive Q4 provisions on the same type of contract (fixed-price lump-sum offshore removal) meet the Cockroaches Never Alone threshold. This is a pattern, not a one-off. The Q4 2025 management language is precisely worded to close the specific campaign but does not preclude cost tail risk on completed projects (warranty claims, final measurement disputes) or margin erosion on new work being tendered at cycle-competitive prices. #J
  • Claxton Engineering Services (acquired April 2026, GBP 63.5m EV):
  • ~GBP 82m revenues, ~14% operating margins, ~400 employees. #I
  • Proprietary technology: SWAT (subsea wellhead removal), SABRE (flexible riser handling), MWR (modular wellhead removal). 30+ years in North Sea. #I
  • Acquisition rationale: add technology-led capability and proprietary tools to differentiate from generic fixed-price decom competitors, reducing execution risk on future campaigns. #I
  • ⚡ Claxton adds ~NOK 1.0bn of revenues and ~NOK 115-130m EBIT on a full-year basis if margins hold. This is approximately 7-8% of current group EBIT — meaningful but not transformative. #A
  • Bear: the acquisition doubles AFG's offshore execution risk precisely after disclosing a loss from the same type of work. If the root cause of the Q4 2025 loss was operational execution rather than purely contract structuring (which is what the proprietary technology thesis assumes), Claxton does not fix it. #J
  • AF Offshore Decom order intake Q4 2025: NOK 783m vs NOK 309m in Q4 2024 (+153% YoY) — paradoxically, the segment is winning new work at the same time as it is losing money on existing work. #I
  • Analysis: This illustrates the structural challenge: decom market is growing (North Sea tail is large, Claxton's addressable market is multi-decade), but execution quality on fixed-price campaigns has not kept pace with the growth in wins. Winning more of the same contract structure does not reduce risk; it concentrates it. #A
  • Offshore backlog at end-2025: NOK 1,965m — small (4% of group) but growing. #I

Transmission mechanism: Claxton technology integration → proprietary tools reduce execution variance on future campaigns → fewer provisions → Offshore segment moves from loss-making to 10-14% EBIT → group margin expands by ~1-2% → re-rating.

Bear case:
- The Worser, Odder, Longer, Further model: offshore decommissioning campaigns are technically complex, weather-dependent, and logistically unpredictable. Cost overrun risk is asymmetric — scope expansions and delays are more common than scope reductions or schedule gains. The Q4 2025 loss of NOK 100m on a single campaign is not implausible as an understatement of total risk on ongoing and pending campaigns. #J
- ❌ If Claxton integration fails to deliver (culture clash, technology integration complexity, talent retention risk in Aberdeen), AFG has paid GBP 63.5m to acquire both the technology AND the liability of ~GBP 82m additional fixed-price offshore work. The leverage to the bear case is higher than the leverage to the bull case. #J
- Energy & Environment (the healthier component): AF Decom (demolition, environmental), AF Energi (industrial electrification), AF Elkraft (electrical services). Running ~5% EBIT, growing ~8% pa. This is the higher-quality component of the energy cluster — but it is not Technical Installation in the Bravida/Instalco sense. No service contract annuity; no multi-year framework maintenance book. #A

Sceptical bullets:
- AFG management has now disclosed Offshore losses in three of the last three Q4 results. The pattern has not been interrupted; it has continued despite management characterising each prior event as "project-specific." #J
- The language after each offshore loss has been similar: "specific project," "campaigns nearing completion," "risk now contained." The track record of these assurances has not been good. #J
- ❌ Claxton at 14% operating margins is attractive — but those margins were earned by a private business with full operational control and a concentrated management team. Integration into a NOK 32bn Norwegian contractor introduces overhead allocation, procurement integration, and reporting complexity that may compress margins at Claxton even before the first combined campaign executes. #A

Plausibility verdict: The Claxton-driven Offshore resolution thesis is strategically coherent but operationally unproven. The proprietary technology is a genuine differentiator if integrated successfully. The probability of clean integration given three prior loss events and simultaneous Building and Sweden execution demands is below 50% in the near term. This driver is HIGH RISK, MEDIUM REWARD — not the basis for a concentrated position thesis at Triaging stage.

§5 DRIVER 4: M&A CONSOLIDATION PLAY

Definition: Is AFG a meaningful consolidator in Norwegian construction? Can it acquire businesses at scale to drive EPS growth above the organic rate?

Evidence:

  • AFG's acquisition history is dominated by small-to-mid bolt-ons (NOK 50-500m deal size) — Betonmast being the largest exception (transformative at purchase, then problematic through residential trough). #I
  • Recent transactions: AF Elkraft (electrical services, 2025), majority stake in Broderne Myhre (well drilling, Dec 2025), Claxton (GBP 63.5m, April 2026). These are all strategic builds in Energy/Offshore — not the construction contracting core. #I
  • ⚡ Claxton at GBP 63.5m is the largest single acquisition in AFG's recent history. At 5-6x EBIT implied multiple (NOK 800m / ~NOK 115-130m EBIT), the price is disciplined for a technology-led niche business. But it is an outlier — AFG is not primarily an M&A roll-up. #A
  • AFG at NOK 32bn revenue is Norway's second-largest contractor (behind Veidekke at ~NOK 40bn). At this scale, acquisitions of meaningful size (>1% revenue) require targets of NOK 300m+. The Norwegian contractor universe is dominated by Veidekke, AFG, and a long tail of regional SMEs. Meaningful bolt-on acquisition of peers without paying strategic premiums is structurally limited. #A
  • Peer comparison: Bravida executes 10-15 bolt-on acquisitions per year at SEK 50-300m each, compounding its service network and purchasing scale. This is the flywheel model — acquiring small installation businesses, integrating them into the service platform, expanding geographic reach. AFG does not have this model. #A
  • Norway's construction industry is not highly fragmented at the scale where AFG would typically acquire — regional SMEs are too small to move the needle on a NOK 32bn group; mid-size targets (NOK 1-5bn revenue) are rare and contested. #A

Transmission mechanism: AFG acquires bolt-on services businesses → adds revenue and margin → EPS accretion at low integration cost → repeat → compounding effect. This is the Bravida playbook — but it requires a recurring-revenue platform to acquire into.

Bear case:
- Without a recurring service infrastructure to bolt onto, AFG M&A adds project capacity, not service annuities. Each acquired project business needs new work to sustain revenues. This is a treadmill, not a flywheel. #J
- ❌ The Betonmast acquisition is the cautionary case: transformative scale, followed by residential downturn, followed by repeated write-downs. Large construction M&A in Norway concentrates project risk, it does not reduce it. #A
- Claxton's international focus (Aberdeen, North Sea) creates integration complexity that AFG's prior deal-making has not needed to manage. #I

Sceptical bullets:
- AFG is a construction contractor that occasionally acquires businesses. It is not a programmatic M&A compounder. The distinction matters for valuation and return forecasting. #J
- The Technical & Industry acquisition thesis applies only to Energy/Offshore bolt-ons — a market too small and too risky to anchor the investment case. #A

Plausibility verdict: M&A consolidation is NOT a meaningful driver for AFG at current scale. The market is insufficiently fragmented, the targets in Civil are strategic rather than bolt-on, and the company lacks the recurring service platform that makes the Bravida compounding model work. Claxton is a one-off strategic move, not the first step in a disciplined acquisition programme. WEAK DRIVER — does not support the investment case.

§6 DRIVER 5: DIVIDEND SUSTAINABILITY

Definition: AFG proposes NOK 6.50/share for FY2025 (+30% YoY from NOK 5.00). At ~NOK 178 share price, this is a 3.6% yield. Is it sustainable?

Evidence:

  • FY2025 EPS ~NOK 9.99. Proposed dividend NOK 6.50. Implied payout ratio: ~65%. Above the stated 50%+ policy minimum, but within historical range. #I
  • Operating cash flow FY2025: NOK 3.04bn vs EBT NOK 1.65bn — OCF/EBT ratio of ~184%. Negative working capital (NOK -3.4bn at end-2024) is a structural feature of the contractor model. #I
  • FCF cover of dividend: NOK 6.50 x ~120m shares = ~NOK 780m total dividend. Against NOK 3.04bn OCF, this is ~3.9x covered by operating cash flow. Against capex of ~NOK 500-600m (estimated), FCF of ~NOK 2.4-2.5bn implies ~3x FCF cover. This is strong. #A
  • Net interest-bearing receivables NOK 1.27bn at end-2025 — net cash position. After the Claxton acquisition (~NOK 800m), pro-forma net cash narrows but does not become net debt. #I
  • Dividend per share history: NOK 3.50 (2022) → NOK 2.50 (2023, reflecting trough earnings) → NOK 5.00 (2024) → NOK 6.50 (proposed 2025). The 2023 cut was modest and quickly reversed. #I

Transmission mechanism: Strong OCF from negative working capital contractor dynamics → excess cash after capex → high dividend payout → income investor base → yield support for share price.

Bear case:
- A NOK 200-300m Offshore provision in H1 2026 (consistent with the three-year Q4 pattern, now including Claxton integration costs) would reduce FY2026 EBT toward NOK 1.4-1.5bn. At 65% payout, the dividend could be maintained but cover would tighten to ~1.3x earnings. #A
- ❌ If EBT falls to NOK 1.0bn in a downside scenario (Civil margin compression + Offshore + Sweden), the 65% payout yields NOK 6.50/share only if the board chooses to maintain the absolute DPS rather than the payout ratio. Risk of cut is real but not base case. #J
- Claxton acquisition reduces net cash buffer; additional bolt-ons in Energy/Offshore would further reduce headroom. #I

Sceptical bullets:
- A 3.6% yield on a cyclical Norwegian contractor running mid-single-digit margins is reasonable income but not a structural moat. Nordic construction peers (Skanska, NCC) also offer 3-4% yields at similar valuation multiples. #A
- The 30% dividend increase signals board confidence, which is positive — but boards of cyclical contractors have a track record of overconfidence at margins near cycle peaks. #J

Plausibility verdict: Dividend sustainability is SOLID in the base case. FCF cover is strong (~3-4x), net cash position is robust after Claxton, and the contractor's negative working capital structure provides structural cash generation. The risk is a 2026 earnings deterioration driven by Offshore provisions and/or Building reversal — which would not cut the dividend but would reduce cover to a level that makes income investors nervous. REAL AND COVERED, but cyclically sensitive.

§7 DRIVER 6: ASSEMBLIN-CAVERION IMPACT (CQ-C6)

Definition: Does the Assemblin-Caverion merger (SEK ~43bn combined entity, creating a Nordic #1 multi-technical installer) affect AFG's competitive position?

Evidence:

  • Assemblin-Caverion operates in HVAC, electrical, fire/safety, and plumbing installation and maintenance across the Nordics. AFG Civil Engineering and Construction businesses do not compete directly with multi-technical installers. #I
  • AFG's Energy & Environment segment (AF Decom, AF Energi, AF Elkraft) operates in demolition, industrial electrification, and offshore services — partially adjacent to the building services market, but primarily industrial and infrastructure rather than commercial building installation. #I
  • The overlap in HVAC/electrical building services between AFG and Assemblin-Caverion is minimal. AFG's construction business subcontracts technical installation to third parties (including potentially Bravida, Assemblin) on Building projects — the merger may affect subcontractor pricing for AFG, but not materially. #A

Transmission mechanism: None identified that is material to AFG's investment thesis.

Bear case:
- ❌ Assemblin-Caverion's combined purchasing scale and talent pool could intensify competition for qualified tradespeople in Norway/Sweden, increasing labour costs for AFG's building operations. This is a second-order effect and quantitatively immaterial at <0.5% EBIT impact. #A
- If AFG's Technical & Industry segment aspires to compete in multi-technical building services (long-term), Assemblin-Caverion's scale makes that market entry harder and more expensive. #A

Plausibility verdict: MINIMAL IMPACT. The Assemblin-Caverion merger is primarily relevant for BRAV, INSTAL, and FASAD in this cohort. For AFG as a general contractor, the indirect labour cost effect is second-order and the direct competitive overlap is negligible. This CQ does not affect the AFG investment case.

§8 DRIVER 7: TECHNICAL INSTALLATION DIVISION — CAN IT BECOME A GENUINE PEER?

Definition: Can AFG's Technical & Industry cluster (Energy & Environment + Offshore, collectively ~15-20% of group revenue) develop into a genuine peer of Bravida or Instalco by revenue quality, margin, and recurring revenue characteristics?

Evidence:

  • Energy & Environment FY2025: ~NOK 3.0-3.2bn revenues estimated (based on ~10% of group), ~5% EBIT margin, 8% YoY revenue growth. AF Decom (demolition/industrial), AF Energi (electrification, renewables), AF Elkraft (electrical services, acquired 2025). #I
  • Service/recurring character: AF Decom's environmental centres provide some repeat-work visibility. AF Energi's industrial services have framework elements. But total recurring share is ~10-15% of this cluster — not the 50% that Bravida achieves. #A
  • Offshore: ~NOK 2.0-2.5bn revenues (Aeron marine services + AF Offshore Decom). Aeron is profitable and recurring; Decom is episodically loss-making. Claxton adds ~NOK 1.0bn at 14% margins. #I
  • Combined Technical & Industry cluster (post-Claxton): ~NOK 6.0-6.5bn revenues, blended margin likely 5-7% if Offshore provisions cease. As a standalone entity, this would be an interesting technical services business. Within a NOK 32bn contractor group, it is ~20% of revenue and perpetually subordinate to Civil and Building capital allocation decisions. #A
  • Judgement: The cluster is growing and improving. But the structural gap to Bravida or Instalco is not closable within a general contractor's organisational model. The reasons:
  • No branch-network model generating recurring maintenance and service annuities from installed systems.
  • No purchasing scale advantage from volume buying of standard installation components (Bravida's 25-30% purchasing discount model).
  • No decentralised entrepreneurial branch P&L culture (Instalco model of retained founders).
  • Technical installation at Bravida is the core business with 50 years of specialisation. AFG's equivalent is a bolt-on cluster assembled via acquisitions within a Civil/Construction-centric group. #J

Transmission mechanism: AF Elkraft + Claxton + AF Energi + AF Decom → expanded technical services revenue → gradually improving margin mix → group weighted toward technical services over time → potential eventual re-rating toward installation/services multiples (12-14x EV/EBIT vs contractor 8-10x).

Bear case:
- ❌ This re-rating narrative would require AFG to spin out or materially demerge the technical cluster — which management has not signalled and which would be strategically inconsistent with the current acquisition pattern. #J
- Even at optimistic trajectory, the Technical cluster reaches ~25-30% of group revenue by 2028 — insufficient to shift the overall valuation multiple from contractor to installer. #A

Sceptical bullets:
- The comparison to BRAV/INSTAL is structurally invalid. Building an installation business via bolt-ons within a general contractor is not the same as operating a purpose-built decentralised installation platform. #J
- ❌ Three years of Energy/Offshore growth have not changed the fact that this cluster is still defined by its losses (Offshore Decom) rather than its gains (AF Energi). #J
