Section 1 – Leadership and Governance Change 14 findings

Section summary
  1. sweeping structural and personnel overhauls are driving a shift from a bloated central matrix to a leaner, country-led model, though historical cultural inertia poses execution risks.

Executive and board transition

1.
New CEO: Patrik Hofbauer took over in February 2024 after Allison Kirkby's departure, bringing a renewed focus on ROCE and aggressive cost rationalisation
2.
New CFO: Eric Hageman assumed the role in September 2023, steering a stringent capital-allocation framework and significantly reducing vendor-financing obligations
3.
Stable Board: Lars-Johan Jarnheimer remains Chair, though the elected board size proposed for the 2026 AGM contracted to six members following a recent withdrawal

Operating model and second-level restructuring

4.
Reorganised structure: A new country-led operating model launched in December 2024, designed to shift accountability locally and cut central resources by half
5.
Sweeping 2nd-level changes: Leadership refreshed across the footprint in 2025, including new heads for Finland, the Baltics, and Norway
6.
New tech leadership: Alexandra Fürst was appointed Chief Technology and Information Officer effective March 2025 to spearhead the refocused Technology unit's scalable support

Culture, turnover and organisational health

7.
Aggressive headcount reduction: The 2024 change programme targeted 3,000 position cuts, representing a near 25% reduction in total workforce and consultant numbers
8.
Continued structural rightsizing: Management announced an additional net reduction of 450 positions in early 2025 to pursue further efficiencies under the new model
9.
Elevated turnover: Employee turnover spiked to 19% in 2024 as a direct consequence of the massive restructuring and redundancy initiatives
10.
Resilient engagement: Despite heavy layoffs, the 2025 employee engagement score reached 8.0 out of 10, placing Telia in the region's top quartile
11.
Strengthening culture: The newly launched "Telia Way" roadmap aims to improve collaboration, targeting a 50/50 gender balance for senior leaders by 2028
12.
Persisting bureaucratic inertia: An expert notes that Telia historically behaves like a slow government entity, warning that cultural transformation requires substantial time despite new management
13.
Other side of the story: The massive 25% headcount reduction may have severed vital operational muscle; failing to navigate this capability drain could fracture the targeted commercial turnaround

Section 3 – External Change: Value-Chain Forces (Demand, Competition, Disruption, Regulation, Supply, Cycle, Macro) 43 findings

Section summary
  1. consumer demand is trading down to value brands, offset by structurally robust enterprise appetite for mission-critical security services
  2. Nordic competitive intensity is stabilising, aided by Telia's value-brand consolidation in Sweden and strategic network sharing in Norway
  3. cloud-native 5G standalone architecture is unlocking enterprise automation, while legacy TV disruption has been successfully excised
  4. tightening EU cybersecurity mandates increase compliance burdens, while legacy asymmetric roaming and termination rates continue to compress
  5. aggressive energy hedging and AI- driven cooling are insulating the cost base against volatile Nordic electricity markets
  6. the capex cycle is structurally peaking, transitioning from heavy 5G and fibre buildouts toward lower-intensity network densification
  7. sticky Nordic unemployment and elevated interest rates maintain pressure on consumer wallets, though geopolitical fears boost security spending

Plateauing sector volume: European mobile service revenue growth has stagnated to near flat after a period of low-single-digit expansion

Resilient premium segments: despite macro pressures, Telia's Swedish consumer base shows healthy intake across premium TV and broadband bundles

Strategic infrastructure alliance: the Norwegian RAN sharing joint venture with Ice will significantly improve network coverage parity against Telenor

25.
Strengthening technological partnerships: deepening ties with Ericsson and Nokia accelerate 5G SA capabilities while reducing standalone R&D burdens

Cycle

26.
Peak capex behaviour: the capital intensity cycle is breaking downwards as initial 5G and national fibre rollouts transition into cheaper densification phases
27.
Improving predictability: divesting the ad-funded TV & Media unit to Schibsted materially dampens group cyclicality by removing volatile advertising revenues

Lumpy enterprise cycle: B2B integration projects and equipment sales in Finland exhibit high quarterly volatility based on public sector procurement timing

Macro

Adverse macro shift: elevated interest rates and living costs across the Nordics continue to depress disposable incomes and constrain B2B IT budgets

Stable FX dynamics: recent currency stabilization provides clearer visibility into Baltic and Norwegian translated earnings trajectories versus 2023 volatility
