§1 — Current Management Guidance

Sandoz's current guidance framework spans a near-term FY2025 annual outlook and a medium-term 2027 strategic framework set at the November 2024 Capital Markets Day; both layers were last updated at Q1 2025 results (April 2025).

  • FY current — revenue: Management guides to mid-to-high single-digit revenue growth in constant currency for FY2025, explicitly ~6-8% cc growth; as-reported USD/EUR sensitivity creates ±1% reported variance at current rates [#A]

  • Sub: FY2024 delivered 10.4% cc growth (above initial 7-9% guidance), so FY2025 guide is a deliberate step-down reflecting biosimilars comparator effects

  • FY current — EBITDA: Core EBITDA margin guided to ~22-23% for FY2025, expanding from 21.4% reported in FY2024; management explicitly frames this as a ~80-100bps year-on-year expansion step [#A]

  • Sub: FY2024 margin of 21.4% was itself above the initial ~20-21% guided range at spin-off — baseline is therefore already ahead of inception guidance

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS per share guidance provided for FY2025 at group level; management directs investors to core EBITDA × depreciation run-rate derivation; implied core EPS ~CHF 1.85-2.05 per consensus derivation [#I]

  • Sub: Absence of explicit EPS guidance is notable vs European generics peers (Teva, Viatris provide explicit adj. EPS ranges); reduces one verification anchor for Q13 purposes

  • FY current — FCF: Free cash flow guidance is qualitative only: "meaningful improvement in cash conversion in 2025 vs 2024"; no numeric FCF range provided; management targets cash conversion ratio >75% of core EBITDA over the medium term [#A]

  • Sub: Qualitative FCF language is a credibility flag — FCF was the one material miss in FY2023; management's reluctance to quantify may reflect continued caution

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guided at ~5-6% of net sales for FY2025, broadly consistent with ~5.4% delivered in FY2024; no material step-up expected [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (2027): Revenue: mid-single-digit cc CAGR 2024-2027 (implying ~5-6% p.a.); EBITDA margin: aspirational 24-26% by 2027; EPS CAGR: double-digit (not quantified precisely — "high teens to 20%" per Capital Markets Day language) [#A]

  • Sub: 24-26% EBITDA margin would represent ~250-450bps expansion from FY2024 baseline; European generics peer Teva has guided to similar trajectory but from a lower base

  • Medium-term targets: Biosimilars revenues targeted at USD 2bn+ by 2027 (vs ~USD 1.3bn in FY2024); this is the single largest driver of margin expansion thesis [#A]

  • Sub: USD 2bn+ biosimilars implies ~50%+ growth in 3 years; Hyrimoz US ramp + Tyruko (natalizumab) + pipeline launches are the delivery mechanism

  • Source — investor day: Capital Markets Day (Holzkirchen, November 2024) set the 2027 framework; subsequently reaffirmed at Q4 2024 results (February 2025) and Q1 2025 results (April 2025) [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1 2025 call confirmed FY2025 guidance unchanged; no intra-year revision yet [#A]

  • Update timing: Next update at H1 2025 results (expected late July/early August 2025); management has signalled willingness to narrow FY2025 guidance range at H1 if trajectory is clear [#A]

  • Caveat: All revenue and margin guidance is on a constant currency basis; with CHF strength vs EUR/USD, as-reported results could miss guidance ranges despite operational delivery [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: EPS growth cadence assumes no material debt refinancing penalty; Sandoz carries ~USD 3.5bn net debt from the spin; interest cost trajectory matters for reported net income [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that FY2025's "step-down" growth rate vs FY2024's exceptional delivery already embeds a softer volume assumption, and the EBITDA margin guidance of 22-23% leaves almost no room for US pricing to deteriorate beyond the modelled 3-4% annual erosion; if US generics pricing accelerates to 6%+, both revenue AND margin guidance would be simultaneously under pressure with no offsetting lever management has identified

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • Overall pattern verdict: Sandoz management exhibits a modest sandbagging pattern on revenue and EBITDA; guidance is set at the lower edge of what management's own internal models imply, consistent with a new-listing management team keen to build a beat track record [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: 3 of 4 periods delivered above the guided midpoint on revenue; average overshoot ~+1.5-2.5pp; this is directionally consistent with sandbagging but modest in magnitude vs typical European consumer staples sandbagging (which averages +3-5pp over guidance midpoint) [#A]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA margin delivered at top-of-range or above in 3 of 4 periods; average overshoot ~+50-80bps vs midpoint; again, modest not aggressive cushioning [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: FCF is the only metric where management has consistently over-promised; 3 of 4 periods missed FCF/conversion guidance; this is an over-promiser pattern on cash, even if operational metrics beat [#J]

  • Sub: The over-promise on FCF is structurally explainable (post-spin costs, restructuring cash) but the pattern is now 3/4 — management's FCF credibility is specifically impaired

  • Honest tracker — CapEx: CapEx guidance has been met within narrow bands (±0.2pp of net sales) across all 4 periods; this is a clean honest-tracker pattern and consistent with management controlling a discretionary spending line [#A]

  • Beat consistency: Revenue/EBITDA beat rate ~75%; no period with a revenue or EBITDA miss (only meets or beats); this is structurally positive for near-term guidance credibility [#J]

  • Miss consistency: FCF miss rate 75% (3/4 periods); the one "meet" was H1 2023 where FCF was not formally guided with a numeric target [#J]

  • Pattern shift: No material shift detected across the 4 periods; the revenue/EBITDA beat magnitude actually narrowed slightly in FY2024 vs FY2023, suggesting the initial post-spin sandbagging cushion is being recalibrated tighter as management builds confidence in its own model [#I]

  • Magnitude — beat: Typical revenue beat = +100-250bps vs guided midpoint; typical EBITDA beat = +40-100bps; both below European pharmaceutical peer average beat magnitudes (Roche/Novartis average ~+200-300bps on revenue) but consistent with generics sector norms [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Typical FCF miss = -200 to -800bps vs stated conversion target; worst miss (FY2023) = -800bps; concentrated in a single metric [#A]

  • Improving accuracy — operating: Operating metric accuracy has marginally improved: FY2024 EBITDA beat was tighter (nearer the guidance range) than FY2023, suggesting management's model is maturing [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the sandbagging pattern is that it may be running out of cushion — FY2025 guidance of 6-8% cc growth vs FY2024's actual 10.4% represents a significant guided step-down, and if management has correctly modelled the biosimilars comparator effect and US generics pricing, there is less hidden upside embedded than in FY2023/2024; the sandbagging cushion may be a post-spin one-time feature, not a structural management style

  • Sub: The FY2024 beat was the second consecutive annual beat on operating metrics, strengthening the pattern

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: FY2025 guidance (6-8% cc growth) represents a deliberate step-down from FY2024's 10.4% delivery; management is not extrapolating the strong year forward, which is a conservative posture [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Supply chain stability post-spin has been better than feared; no major quality events or product recalls since independence; manufacturing execution has been clean [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FCF conversion guidance has been missed 3 of 4 periods; management's response (move to qualitative language) does not resolve the underlying cash conversion underperformance [Cross-ref: §2 + §3] [#J]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: Biosimilars USD 2bn+ 2027 target embeds ~50% revenue growth from a USD 1.3bn base; this requires Hyrimoz US share expansion + successful European biosimilar launches + Tyruko ramp — execution dependencies are high [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: US generics pricing environment is exogenous; management has no control over this; the assumed -3-4% net erosion rate has been violated historically (2018-2019) and could be again under Medicare negotiation dynamics [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: Biosimilars pipeline execution (Tyruko US approval, pipeline biosimilars Phase III completion) requires regulatory and commercial delivery on multiple parallel tracks simultaneously [#I]
