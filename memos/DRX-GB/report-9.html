  • Operating leverage: Inverted during the normalisation phase — Drax's cost base is predominantly variable (biomass fuel ~55-65% of generation costs) with a meaningful fixed layer (capex depreciation, corporate overhead ~£200m+); the variable cost base means margins track price, not volume [#A]

  • Sub: This inverts the typical "positive operating leverage" story; for Drax, the leverage is to power prices, not to output scale [#A]

  • Mix shift: Revenue mix has shifted modestly toward contracted generation (ROC-backed and CfD-supported output) vs merchant; this provides a partial floor under EBITDA at lower power prices [#A]

  • Sub: Approximately 60-70% of Drax's generation is now under some form of long-term contract or subsidy; the merchant exposure has reduced but remains material [#A]

  • Sustainability assessment: Margin sustainability is low in the current configuration; the path to margin re-expansion requires either (a) power price recovery toward £100-120/MWh GB baseload, or (b) BECCS CfD at a strike price that supports ~£900m+ EBITDA — neither is guaranteed within the 18-36M investment horizon [#J]

  • Scale driver: Absent — Drax's generation capacity has not grown materially L3Y; the forward BECCS scale-up is capex-intensive and uncertain in regulatory timing [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — margin sustainability: What's the bearish read? Biomass fuel costs (wood pellet procurement, shipping) remain structurally elevated vs pre-2021 levels; if power prices stay at £70-90/MWh while biomass costs stay at current levels, EBITDA margins compress further toward 15% — not a stable investment base [#J]
