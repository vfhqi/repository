Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. VAT Group dominates the highly profitable specialized niche of semiconductor vacuum valves with an unmatched 70.00% to 75.00% market share.
  2. The business model secures highly defensive recurring revenue through its massive serviceable installed base of 1.70 million valves globally.

Core Product Portfolio and Business Segments

1.
Valves segment division: The Valves segment comprises two business units, Semiconductors, which represents approximately 70.00% of sales, and Advanced Industrials, serving scientific research, nuclear fusion, and coating markets
2.
Core semiconductor valves: VAT's core vacuum valve business makes up over 50.00% of revenue and focuses on three highly differentiated categories, control valves, transfer valves, and isolation valves
3.
Global Service segment: The high-margin Global Service segment provides spare parts, retrofits, and repairs, representing 18.00% to 20.00% of group sales and generating three to five times the revenue of the original valve sale over its lifetime
4.
Adjacent expansion products: VAT is leveraging its precision engineering skills to expand into adjacent fields like advanced modules, motion components, and gas delivery systems, aiming for adjacencies to reach 20.00% of sales by 2029

Value Chain Positioning and Defensible Competitive Moats

5.
Favored value chain position: Sitting as a critical subsystem supplier to tier-one toolmakers like Applied Materials, Lam Research, and ASML, VAT occupies a profit-favored position in the semiconductor supply chain
6.
Customer concentration risk: Revenues are highly concentrated, with the top three original equipment manufacturer customers accounting for 16.00%, 14.00%, and 13.00% of total group revenues in 2025
7.
High switching costs: High switching costs protect VAT, as changing a valve supplier during a certified tool's life is rare and triggers long requalifications and skyrocketing costs for the equipment maker
8.
Severe barriers to entry: The vacuum valve market has steep barriers to entry due to decades of specialized materials expertise, precision machining, and strict supplier approval protocols
9.
Massive patent moat: VAT holds a superior intellectual property moat, owning more vacuum valve technology patents than all of its main competitors combined
10.
Consolidated industry structure: The industry is consolidated into a single-source or "balance and alliance" stage, with VAT holding over 75.00% share in leading-edge valves, leaving peers like Comet or Inficon far behind
11.
Other side of the story: Large OEM customers like Applied Materials or ASML may put pressure on VAT's exceptionally high market share by qualifying secondary sources to mitigate single-source supply chain risks

Section 5 – The possible investment case 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for VAT Group strengthens structurally due to node transitions and massive market-share gains in adjacent fields.
  2. Expanding share of wallet per semiconductor tool delivers accelerated multi-year growth that outpaces the broader capital equipment market.

Forward Earnings Driver 1: Advanced Node Transition (GAA and 2nm)

1.
Node transition driver: The transition to Gate-All-Around transistor architectures at the 2nm design node significantly increases vacuum intensity across lithography, deposition, and etching steps. This directly impacts the core Semiconductors revenue line
2.
High plausibility: Plausibility of this driver is high, as key global foundries are ramping high-volume GAA manufacturing for 2026. The failure mechanism would be delays in foundry cleanroom completions or slower consumer adoption of 2nm chips

Forward Earnings Driver 2: HBM and Advanced DRAM

3.
Memory capacity expansion: The massive growth of artificial intelligence applications is driving unprecedented demand for high-performance High-Bandwidth Memory DRAM capacity, which requires complex multi-layer architectures. This raises the core control and isolation valve sales
4.
Medium plausibility: Plausibility is medium, as meaningful greenfield memory investments are only expected to accelerate in late 2026. The failure mechanism would be a sudden collapse in artificial intelligence demand leading to capital expenditure cuts

Forward Earnings Driver 3: Expanding Share of Wallet in Adjacencies

5.
System integration push: VAT is scaling non-valve adjacent products, advanced modules, motion components, and gas delivery systems, to raise its average share of wallet per semiconductor tool. This touches the adjacencies revenue line
6.
Medium plausibility: Plausibility is medium, as adjacent product specification wins grew by 20.00% of the total in H1 2025, but VAT faces strong incumbents. The failure mechanism would be the inability to displace established subsystem competitors like MKS Instruments

Forward Earnings Driver 4: Aftermarket Services and Retrofits

7.
Premium service growth: VAT is actively mapping its massive serviceable installed base of 1.70 million valves to expand direct-to-chipmaker spare parts, upgrades, and retrofits. This directly boosts the high-margin Global Service revenue
8.
High plausibility: Plausibility is high, as high fab utilization rates over 90.00% automatically trigger recurring maintenance and replacement cycles. The failure mechanism would be chipmakers choosing cheap non-genuine Asian components

Synthesis of the Bull Case Scenario

9.
Compounded vs independent drivers: For the absolute bull case to materialize, these drivers do not all need to fire simultaneously. However, a simultaneous upswing in advanced logic (GAA) and memory (HBM) would create a compounding growth vector
10.
Peer outperformance expectation: Driven by these technological inflections, VAT's earnings analysis suggests it is at an inflection point and will now start to outgrow its peers
11.
Other side of the story: A severe slowdown in NAND memory investments could neutralize the positive logic growth, capsizing the double-digit revenue growth expectations of the consensus

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is skewed by extreme cyclicality and heavy direct and indirect revenue exposure to mainland China.

Semiconductor Market Cyclicality and Demand Shocks

1.
Severe cyclical exposure: VAT's revenues are highly correlated with the highly volatile semiconductor capital investment cycle, meaning any sudden industry slowdown immediately hits profits
2.
Short seller or short interest: There is currently no active published short-seller report or unusually elevated short interest flagged in public market disclosures
3.
Single catastrophic failure mode: The single failure mode that would destroy the investment case is a technological shift away from vacuum-intensive manufacturing processes, which would wipe out valve demand

China Geopolitics and Trade Barriers

4.
China dependency risk: China represents 32.00% of VAT's direct sales, and when combined with indirect sales through non-Chinese equipment makers, total China exposure reaches 50.00% of revenue
5.
Domestic Chinese competition: Heavy Chinese government investments in domestic self-sufficiency could lead to Chinese domestic suppliers replicating VAT's technology within five to ten years
6.
Strict export regulations: Any expansion of United States or European export restrictions on advanced tools to Chinese chipmakers would block VAT's high-end sales

False Friends and Early Warnings

7.
False friend caution: An early surge in lagging-edge DUV lithography or non-vacuum tool sales in China represents a "false friend" that masks an underlying slowdown in advanced vacuum-related spending
8.
Key warning signal: The most critical early-warning metric to monitor is the month-on-month trend in global DRAM and NAND memory spot prices
9.
Other side of the story: The risks of Chinese competition are overstated because Western equipment makers will refuse to use Chinese valves due to severe intellectual property theft concerns
