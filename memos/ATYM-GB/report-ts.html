Section 3 – What the company does 20 findings

Section summary
  1. Atalaya operates a low-risk mining operation utilizing central processing hub infrastructure in Spain, providing a durable cost advantage over remote projects with high capital intensity.

Core Asset Portfolio

1.
Flagship operational core: Proyecto Riotinto serves as the primary cash engine for Atalaya, processing a record 16.60 Mt of ore in the financial year 2025
2.
Synergistic processing hub: The modern 15.00 Mtpa processing plant acts as a central hub, receiving ore from nearby regional projects to maximize capital efficiency
3.
Favourable near-mine stripping: San Dionisio open pit stripping continued during Q1 2026, aiming to blend higher-grade material with Cerro Colorado ore
4.
Promising underground development: Proyecto Masa Valverde represents a high-grade stockwork deposit within trucking distance of Riotinto, currently undergoing infill drilling
5.
Transformational brownfield asset: Proyecto Touro in Galicia provides significant long-term scale potential, progressing steadily through its final Galician permitting phase
6.
Aggressive resource definition: Exploration and other project expenses for FY2026 are guided at €5.00m to €7.00m, focusing on San Antonio and Riotinto East
7.
Opportunistic option acquisition: Earn-in agreements signed in late 2024 with Mineral Prospektering i Sverige AB grant exploration rights in Sweden
8.
High-grade adjacent deposit: San Antonio polymetallic deposit, located 1.00 km from Cerro Colorado, boasts an inferred resource of 11.80 Mt at 1.32% copper
9.
Long-term exploration option: Proyecto Ossa Morena in southwest Spain represents a promising base metal play, with drilling starting at Guijarro-Chaparral
10.
Strategic exploration permits: Riotinto East includes Peñas Blancas and Cerro Negro investigation permits covering 10,016.00 hectares adjacent to Riotinto

Value Chain and Market Structure

Unhedged market exposure: Atalaya operates without currency or commodity price hedging agreements, exposing revenues directly to global copper market volatility

Favoured low-capital position: Spain provides superb infrastructure including access to roads, water, power, and nearby ports, eliminating expensive infrastructure costs

Stable mining jurisdiction: Southern Spain's Iberian Pyrite Belt is a historically significant mining region offering strong legal backing and supportive authorities
16.
Competitive differentiation: A durable competitive advantage through low-capital hub operations is driven by utilizing fully constructed, modern processing facilities

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Regulatory and permitting challenges represent the primary risk factors, with any development delay directly impacting the projected forward EPS growth trajectory.

Regulatory and Operational Challenges

1.
Permitting dependency risk: Delays in securing final permits for Touro or San Dionisio stripping could push production timelines past the 18.00-month investor window
2.
Grade dilution risk: Lower head grades at Cerro Colorado, which dropped to 0.33% in Q4 2025, could damage profitability if high-grade blending is delayed
3.
Input cost inflation: Volatility in diesel and explosives prices, sparked by Middle East conflicts, poses a $0.15/lb to $0.20/lb risk to cash costs
4.
Underlined execution risk: permitting delay is the single most critical failure mode because it blocks access to the high-grade regional deposits
5.
Surface rights delays: The need for forced expropriation of surface rights at Masa Valverde could delay initial portal and ramp development
6.
Extreme weather impact: High rainfall in Q1 2026 caused minor production shortfalls, proving that operational predictability remains subject to seasonal weather anomalies
7.
Evolving EU regulations: Evolving environmental and sustainability rules in Spain could lead to increased compliance and site restoration liabilities

Technological and Market Exposures

8.
Unproven technology risk: E-LIX electrochemical extraction technology is a major source of uncertainty, leading to a €24.10m asset impairment
9.
Balance sheet risk of partners: Lain Technologies has a highly stressed balance sheet, creating operational bottlenecks for the E-LIX industrial plant
10.
Foreign exchange exposure: Revenues are USD-denominated while costs are EUR-denominated, exposing profit margins to adverse EUR/USD currency movements
11.
Tailings tax provision: Financials were impacted by a €4.40m provision in 2025 for a potential land tax reassessment, showing local regulatory friction
12.
Early warning signal: Investors should monitor monthly copper head grades at Riotinto, as persistent grades below 0.35% will harm processing margins
13.
Other side of the story: Abundant local water and electricity infrastructure in Spain shields Atalaya from the costly infrastructure investments typical of remote operations
