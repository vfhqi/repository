Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Landis+Gyr represents a highly predictable, grid technology business following its strategic divestment of EMEA and focus on North America.
1.
Core business model: Generates revenue by selling advanced grid-edge sensing hardware and layering multi-year communications and cloud-based software services onto this installed base
2.
Predictable investment case: Divestment of low-margin European operations creates a streamlined vehicle with structurally elevated EBITDA margins of 14.4% and a robust backlog of $3.9bn
3.
Single swing factor: Long-term earnings predictability relies on the successful deployment and billing ramp-up of next-generation Revelo smart meters with major US utilities
4.
Structurally expanding margins: Increased software contribution (~25% of revenues) supports management's mid-term target of group gross margins exceeding 35%
5.
Material execution risk: Planned transition between two large utility contracts will cause temporary top-line contraction in FY2026 before growth resumes in FY2027
6.
Catalyst-driven US listing: Management expects listing on a US exchange by mid-2026 will simplify the investment story and close the valuation gap with US peers
7.
US board refreshment: Proposed Scott Reese, former CEO of GE Vernova's Electrification Software, as a new board member to support the US transition
8.
Duopoly market structure: Itron and Landis+Gyr maintain an 80% combined market share in the US electric metering industry

Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. The group possesses a highly defensive business model anchored by a $3.9bn backlog and dominant market shares in North America and APAC.
1.
Scale-providing Connected Platforms: Delivers connected smart devices, communications hardware, and deployment services – 76% of FY2025 segment revenues
2.
High-margin Grid Intelligence: Layers software, analytics, and managed services onto the installed base – 24% of FY2025 segment revenue
3.
Structurally elevated margins: Software and software-enabled services ~25% of net revenues in FY2025, carrying significantly higher margins than legacy hardware
4.
Long-duration backlog visibility: Contracts are long-cycle – typically 3–5 years for hardware shipping and up to 15 years for recurring software
5.
Regulated deployment cycles: Sales cycles for complex transactions typically span two years, creating high entry barriers and ensuring a highly predictable pipeline
6.
Segment financial targets: Mid-single-digit revenue CAGR for Connected Platforms and 10%+ revenue CAGR for Grid Intelligence through FY2028
7.
High segment profitability: Expected segment gross margins ~30% for Connected Platforms and ~50% for Grid Intelligence through FY2028
8.
Massive deployed base: Over 127 million intelligent connected devices deployed globally – substantial base for software upselling
9.
Annual recurring revenue: Grid Intelligence segment concluded FY2025 with $207m in annual recurring revenue
10.
US electric duopoly: Itron and Landis+Gyr control a combined 80% market share in North American smart metering
11.
High switching barriers: Incumbency is highly secure – switching vendors is complex, expensive, and risk-averse utilities prefer established partnerships
12.
Patent-backed protection: Robust IP portfolio containing 1,484 active patents and 1,180 pending patents as of FY2025
13.
Contractual customer concentration: Top two customers represent a combined 28.8% of net revenues in FY2025
14.
Asset-light manufacturing model: Production relies on automated assembly and partnerships with contract manufacturers in Mexico
15.
Defensive customer base: Over 2,000 utilities in ~40 countries – primarily risk-averse investor-owned utilities, cooperatives, and municipalities
16.
Highly consolidated market share: Leading market positions of 40%–50% across core markets including the US, Japan, and Australia
17.
Next-gen edge computing: Revelo platform provides high-resolution data at the grid edge, enabling real-time grid orchestration
18.
Other side of the story: Revenue remains lumpy as utility procurement is tied to unpredictable regulatory rate cases and multi-year deployment approvals

Section 5 – The possible investment case 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Landis+Gyr is highly compelling due to unprecedented grid investments and massive backlog visibility.
1.
Generational utility spending: Large US utilities expected to invest $1.4 trillion over the next five years to upgrade grids and accommodate AI data center demand
2.
Unprecedented electrification demand: Total electricity demand growth projected at 2.8% annually through 2035
3.
Backlog-backed predictability: The contracted backlog of $3.9bn covers multiple years of forward revenues with low risk of cancellation
4.
Accelerating software monetization: Software and services represent 43% of the existing backlog and carry ~50% gross margins
5.
Asset-light returns expansion: Low capital requirements enable streamlined business to target ROIC exceeding 30% through FY2028
6.
Gas modernization cycle: Gas utilities adopting ultrasonic gas technologies like Surent to automate leak detection and improve safety
7.
Flexible network platform: Gridstream Connect platform enables multi-commodity utilities to utilize a single shared communications network
8.
High probability of success: Compound target is highly plausible because its drivers are largely independent and supported by regulator-approved multi-year utility budgets
9.
Software attach rate mechanism: Forward margin targets rely on increasing software attach rates on the growing installed base of Revelo platforms
10.
M&A capital deployment: Strategic flexibility supported by estimated doubling of available capital to $600m by FY2028 for targeted software acquisitions
11.
Steady deployment backing: A $0.7bn contract already in the backlog is fully ramped and will run through FY2028, contributing steady 5% annual growth
12.
Australian grid contract: Major grid-edge contract award in Australia expected to contribute ~2% annual growth at full scale deployment
13.
Other side of the story: Bull case will fail if tight supplies of critical electrical components or regulatory rate-case delays postpone utility deployments

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The valuation of Landis+Gyr is highly attractive because its shares sell at a material discount to historical multiples and close peer Itron.
1.
Historically cheap multiples: Group shares trade at CHF 53.00, equivalent to enterprise value of approximately $2.0bn – significant discount to historical levels
2.
Discount to main peer: Trades at material valuation discount to Itron, which historically traded on premium EV/EBITDA multiple of 11.4x–18.6x
3.
Substantial valuation support: Board proposes increased progressive dividend of CHF 1.20 per share from statutory capital reserves
4.
Pricing in listing catalyst: Current price does not fully reflect planned US exchange listing by mid-2026, which is expected to support a significant re-rating
5.
Progressive buyback program: Active $175m share buyback program launched in October 2025
6.
Other side of the story: Valuation discount will persist if Swiss-listed market sentiment remains depressed by limited disclosure during the geographic transition to the US listing

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Key operational and transition risks are highly manageable given the company's strong contracted backlog and diversified manufacturing bases.
1.
High customer concentration: Top two clients represent 28.8% of net sales in FY2025 – loss of a single major customer would severely impact revenues
2.
Component supply bottlenecks: Production depends on contract manufacturers in Mexico – vulnerable to logistics bottlenecks and passive component supply risks
3.
Failure mode of technology: Single most damaging failure mode is a product quality defect or battery failure similar to historical incidents that caused competitor market share loss
4.
False friend metric: Rising short-term order backlog could mask deterioration if underlying product mix shifts back toward low-margin legacy hardware
5.
Early-warning signal: Investors must closely monitor pace of regulatory rate-case approvals for major US utilities – delays directly impact timing of meter rollouts
6.
Cybersecurity risk exposure: Increased connectivity of edge infrastructure exposes utilities to cyber threats
7.
Other side of the story: Contracts allow passing on supply chain costs, and manufacturing in Mexico mitigates impact of direct US-China tariffs

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership changes represent a powerful catalyst for strategic focus as a US-centric management team executes the post-divestment plan.
1.
Strategic executive rotation: Peter Mainz appointed CEO in November 2024, stepping down from Board role to focus fully on the group's operational transformation
2.
New financial oversight: Davinder Athwal appointed EVP and CFO effective April 1, 2025, bringing extensive international and US finance experience
3.
Targeted board refreshment: Proposed election of Scott Reese, former CEO of GE Vernova's Electrification Software, to strengthen software-driven capabilities
4.
Share ownership commitment: Executives must build up shareholding targets equivalent to 300% of base salary for CEO and 200% for other members by FY2029
5.
Board chair succession: Audrey Zibelman took over as board chair from long-standing chair Andreas Umbach in June 2025
6.
Structural alignment adjustments: Management recalled head of EMEA from executive management in April 2025 to avoid conflicts during the regional sale

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. A deeper investigation is strongly recommended because Landis+Gyr is a high-quality, pure-play US grid technology compounder trading at a deep discount.
1.
Strongest case for: Massive $3.9bn backlog, 80% US duopoly market share, and predictable 25%+ EPS growth trajectory through FY2028 provides a highly predictable IRR
2.
Other side of the story: Investment case would break if planned US exchange listing is delayed, keeping shares trapped in a Swiss market that lacks comparable peers

First next step: Verify contract implementation timeline and quarterly billing milestones for the critical Revelo smart meter rollout with National Grid in the US
