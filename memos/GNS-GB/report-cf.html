EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Core Investment Thesis and Bottom-Line Verdict

Internal and External Change Forces

External demand forces remain resilient, with PIC capturing 18% global porcine genetics share and 50% US market dominance, supported by highly defensive royalty models representing 97% of revenues. The competitive landscape is highly fragmented, with Genus competing primarily against private regional cooperatives lacking global scale, though accelerating market consolidation and competitor alliances like JSR partnering with Topigs Norsvin intensify rivalry. Disruptive forces include CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing removing the CD163 virus binding site to eliminate $1.2bn annual US PRRS costs, while Sexing Technologies litigation settlements in FY24 remove patent overhangs. Regulatory tailwinds accelerate with landmark US FDA approval in April 2025, Canadian approval in January 2026, and favorable determinations in Colombia, Brazil, and Argentina, though commercialization requires outstanding approvals in Mexico and Japan for export market access. Supply security relies on stringent biosecurity protocols at nucleus farms, with catastrophic outbreak risks representing the primary threat to genetic dissemination, partially mitigated by geographic diversity and the 370,000 square-foot Canadian nucleus farm housing 2,000 elite females. Agricultural cycle forces turn favorable as post-African Swine Fever farm professionalization drives Chinese consolidation toward large-scale producers, while lower global corn and soybean meal prices improve producer margins in 1H FY26. Macroeconomic headwinds persist through £7.9m Sterling translation drag against Mexican Peso and Brazilian Real in FY25, though rising global GDP per capita escalates animal protein consumption supporting elite genetics volumes.

Top 5-8 Forward EPS Drivers

Deutsche Bank's valuation framework applies a DCF model for core operations combined with a 50% risk-adjusted weighting for the PRRSv-resistant pig program, valuing the North American PRP pipeline at 600p per share on a 100% basis (risk-discounted to 300p), using a 9.25% cost of equity and 3% perpetuity growth rate. The compound probability of delivering all five drivers concurrently is estimated at approximately 18.3%, with PIC royalty compounding and ABS VAP initiatives carrying high independent plausibility above 80%, while North American PRP commercialization remains the primary gatekeeper with outstanding export regulatory approvals. If the PRP pipeline experiences delays, outer-year EPS estimates would compress by approximately 11.5% in broker models, collapsing the embedded option valuation and leaving Genus with high stranded R&D costs.
