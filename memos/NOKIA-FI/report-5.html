§1 Executive Summary

Nokia's Q1 2026 earnings print (23 April 2026) represents the clearest evidence yet that the Infinera acquisition has structurally reoriented the company's growth profile away from cyclical RAN toward AI-era optical infrastructure.

  • Delivery snapshot: Net sales EUR 4.50bn (+4% cc); comparable OP EUR 281m (+54% YoY); comparable OM 6.2% (+200bps); FCF EUR 629m (+40% YoY). #A
  • EPS beat: Reported comparable EPS EUR 0.05, beating ~EUR 0.045 consensus by 31%; stock +7.3% in Helsinki on day of release, touching 16-year high. #A
  • CEO transition context: Justin Hotard replaced Pekka Lundmark as President and CEO; Q1 2026 was Hotard's first full-quarter earnings call; tone explicitly shifted from operational restructuring narrative to AI network growth framing. #A
  • Investment implication: The print confirms Nokia is executing the Infinera integration while growing AI/cloud revenues at hyperscaler-level rates; the bull case (optical infrastructure re-rating) has entered the mainstream analyst consensus. #J
  • Residual caution: Group-level margins remain depressed (comparable OM 6.2% vs Ericsson's reported 11.3% adj. EBITA); the strong Q1 partially benefits from Mobile settlement one-off; full-year guidance range is wide (EUR 2.0-2.5bn OP implies 25% variance). #J
  • Sceptical check: Q1 seasonality typically benefits FCF (working capital release); the EUR 629m Q1 FCF should not be annualised without applying management's 55-75% conversion guidance against mid-point OP; implied full-year FCF EUR 1.24-1.69bn, lower than 2024's EUR 2.0bn if volume skews are unfavourable. #J

§7 Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

Verdict: Infinera integration tracking on plan; AI/cloud repositioning has materially progressed from concept to revenue; segment restructuring completed; operational execution meaningfully better than feared 18 months ago.

  • Infinera integration (closed Feb 2025): EUR 200m+ net comparable OP synergy target by 2027; approximately one-third from cost of sales (supply chain, portfolio rationalisation), two-thirds from opex (engineering consolidation); Q1 2026 gross margin expansion reflects early synergy capture. #A
  • Optical market position: Nokia claims #1 optical market share globally in Q4 2025 (Dell'Oro data); now deployed in 9 of 10 hyperscaler networks for IP and/or optical; OFC Conference March 2026 — Nokia launched coherent optical pluggable transceivers, transponder modules, and amplifiers for datacenter interconnect. #A
  • Segment restructuring: Nokia reorganised from 4 business groups to 2 reporting segments (Network Infrastructure; Mobile Infrastructure including Technology Standards) effective Q1 2026; simplification intended to sharpen capital allocation discipline. #A
  • Cost reduction programme: Approximately EUR 300-400m in annualised cost savings delivered over 2023-2025 restructuring; Nokia reduced headcount by approximately 14,000 employees from peak (2022-2024 programme); programme largely complete per management. #A
  • China strategy: Full ownership of Nokia Shanghai-Bell enables more flexible China operations management; Huawei dominance in Chinese domestic RAN means Nokia's China exposure is predominantly NI/optical. #A
  • CQ-5 (Competitive position vs. Huawei/Samsung): In RAN: Nokia ranks 3rd globally; Nokia gained RAN share outside North America in 2025 vs 2024 (Dell'Oro); in optical: Nokia is now global #1 — a position Huawei does not dominate and Samsung does not occupy at scale. #J
  • Private networks / enterprise: Mission Critical Enterprise and Defence +19% in Q1 2026; Nokia's CBRS/4.9G/DECT portfolio targets industrial, defence, and public safety private networks; sub-scale today (~5-7% group revenues) but growing at 2-3x the rate of RAN. #A
  • ❌ AI-RAN trials not revenue: Nokia's headline differentiator narrative for Mobile is AI-RAN; but 10 committed trial customers generating zero revenue is an early-stage commercial signal, not a financial catalyst; first commercial deployments likely 2027 at earliest based on stated timelines. #J
  • Sceptical check: Nokia's new CEO framing as "AI infrastructure company" resonates with the optical/IP story; it fits less cleanly with the EUR 2.5bn Mobile Infrastructure segment which has a structurally different growth and margin profile; investor consensus may be over-attributing optical's multiple premium to the whole group. #J
