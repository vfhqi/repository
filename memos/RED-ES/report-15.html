§7 — Narrative vs Reality Check + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: The strongest narrative-reality disconnect is management's confidence register on post-2025 regulatory certainty co-existing with an open RETA review, a CAPEX execution track record of modest slippage, and an allowed ROE that peers characterise as "adequate but not generous" rather than compelling

Narrative claim: Management consistently presents the post-2025 regulatory framework as effectively resolved — "constructive dialogue with CNMC" + "framework will reflect investment needs" + precise 2030 RAB targets presented without scenario ranges

Reality signal: (1) RETA regulatory review is formally open; (2) CNMC has not published binding post-2025 allowed ROE parameters; (3) former regulatory-affairs expert flagged that Spanish regulatory decisions historically lag published timelines by 6-18 months; (4) CMD refused to disclose the ROE scenario range underpinning the 2030 RAB target

Disconnect identified: Management projects ~certainty on a variable (post-2025 allowed ROE) that remains formally unresolved — the confidence register is approximately 12-18 months ahead of the information that would justify it

Narrative claim: CAPEX delivery described as "on track" with "phasing" explaining modest undershots

Reality signal: FY24 CAPEX ~5% below guidance; Pyrenean interconnector delayed by ~6-12 months vs original H2 2025 target; former employee characterises CAPEX slippage as systematic; National Grid (peer) cites labour/materials cost inflation as a persistent delivery challenge

Disconnect identified: The "on track / phasing" framing is a consistent retroactive smoothing pattern — individual instances are defensible, but the pattern across multiple periods and multiple projects suggests structural execution pace below management's declarative confidence level

False-friend pattern: On insider transactions — management projects high public confidence but CEO has zero open-market purchases in the L12M or visible prior periods; the confidence-vs-skin-in-game gap is mild but present; non-executive director purchases (€156k aggregate) are at governance-optics scale rather than conviction scale

Insider-vs-narrative gap: Management narrative implies high visibility on regulatory outcome; insider transaction pattern implies low personal conviction to back that narrative with capital — not a red flag in isolation (Spanish state-influenced utility CEO is a different profile from founder-CEO), but it weakens the "management believes their own narrative" signal

Expert-vs-narrative gap: Expert commentary (thin but directionally consistent) suggests: (a) CAPEX execution is slower than management's register implies; (b) regulatory timeline may slip; (c) labour/skills constraints are real but unacknowledged — on all three dimensions, expert signal is mildly bearish relative to management narrative

Single largest credibility risk: The 2026-2030 CAPEX envelope (~€1.0-1.2bn/year) and the ~€16bn 2030 RAB target both depend on an allowed ROE that CNMC has not yet confirmed for the post-2025 period. If the allowed ROE is cut (or indexation terms revised), both targets become simultaneously wrong — the RAB target is overstated (less capital deployed if returns are inadequate), and the earnings trajectory is overstated (lower allowed returns on existing and new RAB). This is a single-point-of-failure regulatory risk that management's confidence register systematically underweights.

Reverse-engineered bear: The bear case is not that the grid investment thesis is wrong — it is structurally correct. The bear case is that the valuation already prices regulatory certainty that does not yet formally exist, CAPEX delivery will continue to phase-slip (meaning RAB growth is back-end loaded and discounted more heavily), and the allowed ROE on new investment is "adequate but not generous" in a structurally higher interest rate environment — creating a persistent cost-of-equity overhang that prevents the re-rating the bull case requires.

Confidence in management narrative: Medium plausibility — the directional narrative (grid investment need is real, Spain is structurally underpowered, electrification demand is building) is well-supported; the execution-pace and regulatory-certainty-timing dimensions of the narrative are optimistic relative to observable evidence.
