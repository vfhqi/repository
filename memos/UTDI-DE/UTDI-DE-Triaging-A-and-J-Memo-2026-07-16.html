B Executive Summary 30 findings
Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.
Judgement summary
  • Narrow progress case, not a clean pass. C United Internet clears the Triaging bar on change-force intensity and a well-evidenced sum-of-the-parts discount, but carries the weakest technical picture and the worst case-simplicity grade of any DACH or sector peer reviewed.
  • Key risk. A four-layer holding structure (1&1 at 86.46%, IONOS at 63.80%, wholly-owned Mail & Media, plus a stranded legal stub) stacks holdco-level complexity on top of 1&1's already-binary network build
    • Why it compounds. This multiplies the number of independent things that must go right
  • Key support. Group capex is tapering hard (EUR 775m to EUR 731m to EUR 600–650m guided) into a free cash flow inflection (EUR 47m to EUR 321m)
    • The discount behind it. The combined market value of the listed 1&1 and IONOS stakes structurally exceeds United Internet's own market capitalisation, a real, sell-side-corroborated discount, not a Watson invention
  • Recommendation. Progress to Early-Stage Assessment, low-to-medium conviction. Richard's own March-2026 note on this exact structure concluded direct 1&1 exposure was cleaner than the holdco; ESA's job is to test whether anything has changed that conclusion.
1.
What would raise conviction: a dated, committed mechanism to close the sum-of-the-parts discount (spin-off, further squeeze-out, or a market re-rating) would raise conviction materially
1.1
What would argue for parking instead. A second consecutive year of guidance disappointment, or an unfavourable spectrum-sharing ruling that locks in permanently higher Vodafone wholesale costs, would argue for parking in favour of the cleaner, already-progressing 1&1 case

Thesis Summary -- The one-paragraph investment thesis: what is changing, what the expected outcome is, and what the entry conditions are.
Judgement summary
  • Overall verdict. C United Internet passes the Triaging bar on change-force intensity and a real, sell-side-corroborated sum-of-the-parts discount
    • Where it falls short. On every other axis reviewed it is the weakest name in its own four-stock cohort and among the weaker names in the wider telecoms sector set

  • The complexity is real and worse than every peer reviewed. United Internet is the only name in this comparison set that is a holding company over another already-complex, already-binary telecom case (1&1)
    • The extra layers on top. Plus a second, separately listed compounder (IONOS) plus a legally stranded minority stub (Kublai/Tele Columbus)
  • Governance signals warrant explicit tracking at ESA. Related-party extraction identified at the 1&1 level (Dommermuth-family property leases, BVB Dortmund sponsorship, a cashless intra-group debt transfer) sits under United Internet's direction as controlling shareholder
    • Why it is a parent-level issue too. This makes it as much a United Internet governance question as a 1&1 one
  • Recommendation: progress to Early-Stage Assessment, low-to-medium conviction, with the ESA scope explicitly built to test whether the sum-of-the-parts discount is closing (worth progressing) or structurally permanent (worth Richard's prior "1&1 direct is cleaner" conclusion standing unchanged).
