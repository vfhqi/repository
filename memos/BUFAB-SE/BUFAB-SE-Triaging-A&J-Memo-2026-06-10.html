Section B — Executive Summary

BLUF

  • Structural margin compounder mid-cycle: Bufab has executed a decisive strategic refocus over 2023-2025, divesting capital-intensive manufacturing subsidiaries (Lann, Hallborn, CSG US), raising its EBITA margin target from 12% to 14%, and then already exceeding that target, reaching a record 15.3% in Q1 2026. This is not a cyclical blip: eleven consecutive quarters of gross margin expansion (28.3% to 32.9%) demonstrate structural pricing discipline that persisted through a severe industrial destocking downturn. The investment case is real, credibly evidenced, and not yet widely reflected in the valuation multiple.
  • The M&A flywheel is the fulcrum driver and its key risk: DNB Carnegie models 8% acquired sales growth annually through 2029. With a 15%+ EPS CAGR needing the M&A engine to keep running, the thesis is constructive when the pipeline flows and credit is available, but fragile when target multiples inflate (private equity competition is rising) or integration slips. The FALSE FRIEND scenario flagged in the Risks report is the correct frame: M&A revenue can mechanically mask organic decay until a sudden earnings miss exposes the gap.
  • Cyclical and thematic positioning is bifurcated: Exposure to energy, defence, digital infrastructure, and agriculture provides structural non-cyclical demand. Exposure to automotive, construction, and furniture remains weak and rate-sensitive. The T1 bear-market headwind is real for the cyclical portion but substantially mitigated by the margin-resilience track record: EBITA held through the 2024 downturn on pricing power alone, and the VMI-embedded customer model provides stickiness unavailable to pure commodity distributors.
  • Relative to cohort peer OEM International, Bufab is the higher-leverage, higher-cyclicality, lower-ROIC variant but at a material valuation discount: OEM at 15.3% EBITA margin with ROCE of 30%+ and net cash is a genuinely superior business on capital efficiency metrics. Bufab at 15.3% EBITA but 2.4x net debt/EBITDA and 13.9% ROCE is a structurally inferior though still highly capable compounder. The cohort comparison is thin with only one peer report on disk (OEM BF only; Teqnion and Beijer Ref have no reports), so grades carry wider confidence intervals than normal.
  • Progress verdict: borderline B-range case with clear near-term catalysts: The combination of an organic recovery trajectory (2.2% in Q1 2026, improving from -10.6% trough), continued M&A pipeline execution (VITAL and novia both accretive at or above 14%), and an undervalued multiple versus peers (21x EV/EBITA vs 27-29x for Swedish compounder peers) creates a compelling GARP setup. The governance and leverage weaknesses prevent a strong-B rating but do not disqualify Progress.

Section C.1 — Pillar I: Required Change Forces

E1 — Required Input Change Forces (×2 double-weight)
RA1 — Change Forces Present: External Tailwinds
CQ1 — Strong External Change Forces / Tailwinds?
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Vendor consolidation structural mandate: Industrial OEMs are actively reducing fragmented supplier bases, driven by total cost of ownership logic rather than piece-price optimisation. C-parts represent only 20% of direct material costs but consume roughly 80% of indirect supply chain administration time, making consolidation to a single-source partner economically compelling and structural rather than cyclical. DNB Carnegie explicitly identifies this outsourcing shift as the primary multi-year organic share-gain engine for scaled intermediaries like Bufab, and an expert confirmed that industrial customers increasingly prioritise process reliability and automation over pure piece-price, with vendor consolidation mandates accelerating during market uncertainty.
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Near-shoring and supply chain regionalisation: Geopolitical instability, US-China tariff escalation, and Red Sea freight disruptions have accelerated European manufacturers' preference for resilient, locally-managed supply chains over pure cost-minimisation. Bufab's 75% European revenue concentration and extensive pan-European warehouse network position it directly in the path of this trend. Management noted in the CMD that global trade barriers and logistics constraints accelerate Bufab's ability to capture market share from sub-scale or Asian-reliant competitors, and Q1 2026 results confirmed the Red Sea disruption drove customers toward Bufab's reliable European distribution network.
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Regulatory compliance as structural barrier to entry: The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism arriving from 2026 creates a compliance burden that falls disproportionately on smaller non-compliant distributors. Approximately 50% of Bufab's purchases originate from non-EU countries — creating CBAM cost exposure — but simultaneously requiring the sophisticated Scope 3 tracking infrastructure that squeezes out sub-scale rivals who cannot absorb the compliance investment. CSRD sustainability reporting obligations similarly favour scaled operators with dedicated ESG infrastructure across Bufab's 8,000 global suppliers, creating a regulatory moat that is structural and widening.
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Defence, energy, and digital infrastructure demand diversification: Three non-cyclical verticals provide growing demand independent of European manufacturing PMI trajectories. Defence facility upgrades across NATO members are politically mandated with multi-year spending commitments. Energy sector C-parts demand is driven by grid expansion and renewables buildout. Data-centre construction linked to the AI computing capacity shortage creates specialist fastener and C-parts demand at complexity levels that favour scaled operators with technical service capability. Management named all three verticals explicitly in Q1 2026 commentary as offsets to construction and automotive weakness, with a major Babcock (defence contractor) framework agreement confirmed as a new win.
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Industrial destocking completion and volume recovery underway: DNB Carnegie assessed that inventory reductions across the industrial value chain were concluding by late 2025. Q1 2026 organic growth of +2.2% against consensus expectation of +1.0% and against peer Bossard's European organic decline of -3.0% provides the first material empirical evidence the volume trough has passed. The book-to-bill ratio reached 1.03x in Q1 2026, indicating net order intake expansion. Calibrated against Nordic sector context (Bravida: +1% organic in Q1 2026 vs consensus -0.5%, order intake +5%), the Nordic industrial early-recovery reading is consistent across comparable industrial acquirers in the region.
  • E1 RA1 CQ1 — Fragmented acquisition target supply from aging founder demographics: The global C-parts distribution market remains extremely fragmented with thousands of family-owned local operators approaching succession without natural internal successors. Jefferies Research confirms that procurement advisors increasingly recommend vendor consolidation to fewer large distributors, reducing institutional resistance to M&A and making Bufab's value proposition easier to communicate to target companies. Capital requirement squeeze and succession challenges make smaller operators "entirely natural sellers," providing a highly reliable and growing pipeline for serial consolidators.
CQ2 — Strong Internal Change Forces?
  • E1 RA1 CQ2 — Manufacturing divestment programme complete: The strategic refocus from capital-intensive manufacturing to pure-play distribution was announced at the December 2023 Capital Markets Day and executed fully through 2024-2025: Bufab Lann AB and Hallborn Metall AB divested July 2024; US manufacturing unit (CSG) divested mid-2025. These units historically dragged group margins at 5-10% EBITA while consuming approximately 40% of total group capex despite representing only 5% of net sales. Their removal mechanically lifted the margin baseline and released capital intensity, with capex requirements structurally reduced post-divestment and the group freed to allocate capital exclusively toward margin-accretive distribution acquisitions.
  • E1 RA1 CQ2 — Margin target raised and already exceeded ahead of schedule: At the December 2023 CMD management raised the EBITA margin target from 12% to 14% by 2026. Q1 2026 delivered 15.3% — 130bps above the raised target — roughly 18 months ahead of the stated deadline. The 14% target was not achieved by volume leverage: it was achieved through eleven consecutive quarters of gross margin expansion from 28.3% to 32.9%, demonstrating structural pricing discipline. A 1 percentage-point resale price change impacts operating profit by SEK 80m (approximately 15% of net income), making the pricing model the single highest-leverage internal dial and confirming that the margin improvement is mechanically durable.
  • E1 RA1 CQ2 — Accretive M&A execution: VITAL and novia Group: Two transformative acquisitions in 2024-2025 demonstrate the M&A engine functioning at its best. VITAL (Italy, November 2024, EUR 48m sales, margins exceeding the 14% target) expanded geographic reach and margin quality simultaneously. novia Group (Germany, October 2025, EUR 50m sales, 19%+ margins generated in Europe West segment) added downstream assembly capabilities and elevated the structural margin profile through deliberate value-chain ascension. Both acquired at or above group margin, immediately accretive, and integrated under the pull model that preserves acquired entrepreneurs. DNB Carnegie forecasts 8% acquired sales growth annually through 2029 based on demonstrated pipeline quality and historical acquisition pace.
  • E1 RA1 CQ2 — Five-region organisational restructuring with full P&L accountability: The transition to a five-region structure in 2024 pushed full P&L accountability to local sister companies with three-year plans mandating stability before profitability before growth, installing governance architecture required to sustain margin discipline at scale. Local managing directors retain entrepreneurial freedom but are directly accountable to measurable financial targets. Employee NPS climbed from 11 in 2023 to 17 in 2025; staff attrition declined from 15.4% in 2023 to 14% in 2025, with cultural indicators pointing in a consistently positive direction and reinforcing that the operational model is working as designed.
  • E1 RA1 CQ2 — CFO succession resolved with returning incumbent: The CFO seat instability (three transitions in 24 months: Pär Ihrskog resigned October 2025 after two years; Helena Häger served as interim) was resolved with the appointment of Marcus Söderberg, who returns to Bufab in March 2026 in the exact role he previously held until 2023. A CFO returning with full institutional knowledge of the financial architecture, decentralised control model, and M&A integration processes is the least disruptive possible succession outcome, materially reducing the near-term execution risk of the transition and restoring continuity in the function most critical to sustaining the serial acquisition model.
RA2 — Robust Base Present: CQ1-CQ5
  • E1 RA2 CQ1 — Revenue base durability: VMI integration creates structural switching costs: The core commercial model transitions customer relationships from transactional spot trading to deeply embedded vendor-managed inventory partnerships. Bufab's weight-sensor bins are physically installed on customer factory floors, C-parts are tracked across approximately 175,000 active SKUs, and Scope 3 carbon emissions are monitored across the Asian supply chain at a level customers cannot replicate internally. An expert confirmed customers save roughly 20% of indirect costs through Bufab, making the economic case for switching extremely difficult to justify. Replacing Bufab requires the customer to rebuild compliance infrastructure across hundreds of individual manufacturer contracts simultaneously — a task that exceeds the procurement capacity of typical industrial OEMs.
  • E1 RA2 CQ2 — Earnings quality: track record of consistent guidance beats driven by internal factors: Four consecutive quarterly EBITA beats (Q2 2025: +1%, Q3 2025: +8%, Q4 2025: +6%, Q1 2026: +9%) with all beats attributable to gross margin expansion through pricing discipline and purchasing consolidation rather than one-off accounting changes or benign macro tailwinds. DNB Carnegie designates this the "honest-tracker archetype": management provides cautious demand guidance while consistently outperforming on margins through internal execution. Delivery misses in 2024 (Q2 and Q4) were attributable to genuine macro deterioration (industrial destocking, single-customer obsolescence charge) rather than management credibility failures, and the team diagnosed and adjusted without lowering the structural margin ambition.
  • E1 RA2 CQ3 — Financial base: leverage within target range and delevering simultaneously with M&A: Net debt to adjusted EBITDA of 2.4x at Q1 2026, within the stated 2.0-3.0x policy range and improving from 2.5x year-on-year. DNB Carnegie models organic deleveraging to 1.8x by late 2026 absent further acquisitions. The group funded VITAL and novia simultaneously with deleveraging progress, demonstrating the cash generation capacity of the distribution model (operating cash conversion hit 93% in Q4 2025 and 146% for full year 2023). Working capital as a share of sales declined to 34.4% by Q3 2025 from prior elevated levels, releasing balance sheet capacity for continued M&A without equity issuance.
  • E1 RA2 CQ4 — Market position: leadership at less than 5% global share confirms consolidation runway: Despite 50+ acquisitions over its history, Bufab controls less than 5% of a highly fragmented global C-parts market. This figure alone defines the scale of the remaining consolidation runway. Within Europe, Bufab holds stronger positioning (75% revenue concentration) than direct peer Bossard (55% European), providing defensive depth in the core market and demonstrating that geographic concentration has not yet created a growth ceiling. The 14% ten-year sales CAGR and 17% EPS CAGR from 2014 to 2024 are delivered from a starting market share position that still leaves decades of acquisition runway remaining.
  • E1 RA2 CQ5 — Culture and leadership: Solutionist identity evidenced in measurable indicators: The "Solutionist" identity emphasising entrepreneurial problem-solving and decentralised accountability is evidenced in measurable indicators rather than management self-description alone. Employee NPS rising from 11 to 17 over two years, attrition falling from 15.4% to 14%, and the consistent retention of acquired CEOs through the pull integration model all point to a culture that is operationally real. CEO Lundén's personal equity investment of approximately SEK 8.6m (exercised 26,000 call options in late 2025 to acquire 130,000 shares at SEK 66.40 each) and mandatory personal investment in the 2025/2028 LTI programme provide credible skin-in-the-game alignment between executive incentives and shareholder outcomes.

Section C.2 — Pillar II: Core Analytical Framework

E5 — Thematic Fit (×2 double-weight)
RA1 — T1: Bear Market / Top of Bull Market
  • E5 RA1 T1 — C-parts demand is partially recurring but materially cyclical: Over half of Bufab's revenue is tied to industrial production, which is highly correlated with the global manufacturing cycle. The primary cyclical end-market exposures include automotive (weak, rate-sensitive), construction (weak, rate-sensitive), furniture and interior design (weak), and general industrial machinery (moderate). These are the same verticals that drove the -10.6% organic growth trough in Q1 2024 and the reported revenue decline of -7.4% in full-year 2024. In a sustained bear market where industrial production contracts for 12-18 months, Bufab's organic growth reverts negative and the operating leverage that has been additive on the way up reverses. This is a genuine and material T1 headwind, not dismissible by reference to pricing power alone.
  • E5 RA1 T1 — Embedded VMI service model provides partial defensive buffer: The shift from transactional C-parts sales to deeply embedded vendor-managed inventory partnerships means that even in downturns, a meaningful portion of Bufab's volume is quasi-contracted through service arrangements. VMI customers cannot easily reduce their Bufab spend below a minimum level without disrupting their own production lines, since Bufab bins are physically integrated into manufacturing workflows. This provides a structural buffer compared to pure-spot commodity distributors: it reduces the elasticity of volume to cycle rather than eliminating cyclical sensitivity, meaning Bufab will underperform in a bear market but with a shallower drawdown than the headline industrial exposure would suggest.
  • E5 RA1 T1 — Pricing power demonstrated through the trough confirms structural rather than cyclical margin: The eleven consecutive quarters of gross margin expansion from 28.3% to 32.9% occurred through the worst industrial destocking cycle since 2016. This empirically demonstrates genuine pricing power — not just in bull markets when volume leverage provides cover, but in deflationary-volume environments where weaker business models face pricing pressure from customer pushback. The value-based pricing model, anchored on total cost of ownership rather than piece-price, gives Bufab structural insulation from the price deflation that hits commodity distributors in downturns and from customer attempts to trade down to local cheaper alternatives.
  • E5 RA1 T1 — M&A engine can continue in mild downturns but freezes in deep bear scenarios: The serial acquisition model provides an alternative growth engine independent of organic volume, but requires access to credit at reasonable rates, willing sellers at non-inflated multiples, and management bandwidth to execute. In a severe bear market characterised by rising credit costs and private equity defending portfolio valuations, acquisition multiples for quality C-parts distributors could inflate while Bufab's own leverage constraints tighten. DNB Carnegie explicitly identifies prolonged high interest rates and German industrial weakness as the primary scenario that could simultaneously freeze the M&A engine and trigger a multiple contraction, with a bear-case scenario valuation of SEK 75 per share (approximately 38% below recent trading levels).
  • E5 RA1 T1 — Sector mix includes material non-cyclical offsets: Energy, defence, digital infrastructure, and medical technology collectively represent a growing and non-trivial share of Bufab's customer base. Management's Q1 2026 commentary specifically named energy, agriculture, digital infrastructure, and defence as the strong demand pockets offsetting construction and automotive weakness. These verticals are driven by policy, security, and technology investment rather than economic confidence. The trend toward these verticals is structural: defence spending commitments are multi-year, data-centre construction pipelines are fully funded, and energy transition capex is regulatory-mandated, all providing genuine T1 resilience that is absent in a pure-play industrial cyclical.
  • E5 RA1 T1 — T1 verdict: The bear market thematic is a net headwind for Bufab's cyclical end-market exposure but is substantially mitigated by pricing power evidence, the VMI embedded model, and the non-cyclical vertical mix. Bufab is not a defensive stock in a bear market but will defend margins better than the cyclical revenue exposure would naively suggest. T1 grade: C+ (above neutral, not strongly positive).
RA2 — T2: AI Disruption
  • E5 RA2 T2 — Digital disintermediation is the structural risk but complexity provides genuine structural protection: The theoretical AI-disruption scenario is that large OEMs use AI-driven procurement platforms to directly aggregate hundreds of Asian manufacturers, eliminating the distributor layer entirely. This risk is structurally real: Amazon Business and digital freight marketplaces are already applying machine learning to supplier matching in standardised components. However, the C-parts distribution model's core defensibility lies in the complexity of managing 175,000 SKUs across thousands of suppliers with compliance requirements, quality audits, just-in-time delivery, and Scope 3 tracking — a complexity that AI procurement tools have not yet meaningfully addressed and that is actively increasing due to regulatory requirements (CSRD, CBAM) rather than decreasing.
  • E5 RA2 T2 — VMI sensor integration creates AI-resistant switching costs: Bufab's proprietary weight-sensor bins installed on customer factory floors represent a physical embedded digital infrastructure layer that cannot be replicated or replaced by a software procurement tool. The data generated from these sensors — consumption patterns, reorder frequencies, production batch intelligence — is proprietary to Bufab and creates information asymmetry in the distributor's favour. Critically, AI-enhanced automated warehousing and procurement tools are being actively deployed by Bufab itself (AI-driven pricing and shared master data systems that lower customer administration costs), meaning the technology is being used defensively to deepen lock-in rather than threatening the business model from outside.
  • E5 RA2 T2 — AI data-centre demand creates a direct positive demand tailwind: The AI computing boom is generating documented capacity shortages in data centres across Northern Europe. These facilities require specialist fasteners, electrical C-parts, and HVAC components at complexity and volume levels that reward full-service operators over local generalists. Management explicitly named digital infrastructure as a strong demand pocket in Q1 2026, and this demand vector is growing structurally as AI deployment accelerates. The data-centre tailwind is the clearest example of AI being a net positive rather than a net negative for Bufab's business model.
  • E5 RA2 T2 — 3D printing as long-run substitute for low-volume engineered fasteners is a slow-developing risk: A former Bufab director flagged additive manufacturing (3D printing) as a credible long-term challenge for traditional C-parts businesses, specifically enabling customers to produce specialised low-volume engineered fasteners on-demand and eliminating distributor inventory for those items. This risk is real but slow-moving: 3D printing for industrial fasteners at production quality and cost parity with conventional manufacturing remains years away for the vast majority of Bufab's 175,000 SKUs, and the substitution threat is highly selective (low-volume, high-complexity engineered parts) rather than systemic. It is a risk to monitor at ESA, not a Triaging disqualifier.
  • E5 RA2 T2 — T2 verdict: AI is net positive for Bufab at the Triaging stage. The direct positive (data-centre demand) is immediate and growing. The defensive use of digital tools deepens customer stickiness. The disintermediation risk is structurally mitigated by physical VMI embedding and C-parts management complexity. T2 grade: B (positively aligned).
RA3 — T3: Iran War / Oil Price
  • E5 RA3 T3 — Freight cost exposure from Middle East disruption is real and already materialised: The Red Sea conflict has already manifested as a real operational impact on Bufab. Management confirmed in Q1 2026 commentary that geopolitical developments in the Middle East increased freight costs and exacerbated customer caution, with air freight cost inflation flagged and the group forced to permanently elevate safety inventory buffers. However, management also demonstrated full pricing pass-through capability: US tariff costs from Chinese imports were passed directly to customers, fully protecting operating margins at the American Bolt and Screw subsidiary which sources approximately 38% from China, establishing the pass-through as structural rather than opportunistic.
  • E5 RA3 T3 — Oil price spike compresses industrial output in customer base through two channels: Higher energy costs reduce industrial operating margins for Bufab's manufacturing customers, which historically translates into deferred capital investment and component procurement delays. The logistics channel (freight inflation directly hitting Bufab's cost base) is manageable given demonstrated pass-through capability. The demand compression channel (customers reducing production activity and component spend in response to energy cost inflation) is a genuine risk given the greater than 50% cyclical revenue exposure. These two channels compound in a sustained oil-price-escalation scenario and interact multiplicatively with the working capital drag already present in the base case.
  • E5 RA3 T3 — Near-shoring tailwind from geopolitical escalation partially offsets the headline headwind: Paradoxically, a sustained Iran war or broader Middle East escalation raises oil prices and freight costs but also accelerates the near-shoring trend that benefits Bufab's European distribution hubs. European manufacturers have strong incentives to reduce Asian supply chain dependence when geopolitical risk premiums make Asian sourcing less economically attractive. Bufab's 550 core suppliers provide an alternative sourcing ecosystem that becomes relatively more valuable as Asian supply chain reliability deteriorates and freight time predictability collapses.
  • E5 RA3 T3 — FX vulnerability is the primary T3 transmission risk due to zero derivative hedging: Bufab generated SEK 1,290m in USD sales in 2025 against materially larger USD procurement costs, creating a structural negative USD exposure. The Risks report stress-tests a +30% USD shock erasing SEK 282m in EBITA — over 26% of 2025 operating profit — equating to approximately SEK 1.14 per share EPS impact. This structural mismatch is compounded by Bufab maintaining zero financial derivative transaction hedges, contrasting unfavourably with Addtech (12-month rolling forwards) and Indutrade (up to 31-month forwards). Oil price spikes in a war scenario often correlate with USD strengthening against SEK, making the T3 scenario interact multiplicatively with the FX exposure rather than independently.
  • E5 RA3 T3 — T3 verdict: The Iran/oil thematic is a net headwind. The direct freight cost and FX exposure are real, material, and not hedged. The demand compression channel through industrial customers is significant given greater than 50% cyclical revenue exposure. The near-shoring offset is genuine but slow-acting, operating over months and years rather than quarters. T3 grade: C (neutral to slight negative).
RA4 — Paradigm Fit
  • E5 RA4 — ESG paradigm alignment is structural: Bufab's business model is structurally aligned with the current corporate ESG mandate. T1 bear-market risk is partially mitigated by pricing power evidence and VMI embedded model. T3 oil/Iran is a manageable headwind with demonstrated pass-through capability. The ESG and supply-chain-resilience paradigm alignment is structural and actively referenced by customers as a reason to choose Bufab over cheaper local alternatives.

Dimension 4 — Valuation provides a margin of safety: Trading at 21x EV/EBITA with a 15.3% margin (versus peers at 27-29x with comparable or lower margins) provides a valuation buffer that is not dependent on exceptional growth delivery — the base case merely requires peer re-rating, not earnings outperformance. The 29% discount to direct peer Bossard (with inferior margins) is the most striking specific valuation anomaly and is hard to justify on fundamentals.

Dimension 5 — Setup archetypes are clear and Viewforth-aligned: Primary setup (serial acquirer compounder) has a decade of empirical validation. Secondary setup (quality at discount/multiple re-rating) provides downside protection. Both setups are appropriate for Viewforth Stage 2 analytical focus.

E.2 — Risk Factors Requiring ESA Priority Focus

Three specific risk factors are insufficiently resolved at Triaging and require priority investigation at ESA:

Risk 1 — FALSE FRIEND scenario: M&A revenue masking organic decay. The Risks report explicitly identifies this as the modal risk outcome (Analysis of Competing Hypotheses winner). The diagnostic test requires separating acquired revenue from organic at the regional and subsidiary level to confirm that underlying organic volumes are genuinely recovering rather than being cosmetically masked by purchase accounting. The Asia-Pacific -14% organic decline in Q1 2026 is a specific data point requiring scrutiny: is this customer-loss-driven (structural problem) or cyclical softness (recoverable)? Management characterised it as large customer losses — that characterisation needs independent verification at ESA.

Risk 2 — CFO instability and financial control quality. Three CFO transitions in 24 months in a highly decentralised operating model raises questions about the financial control environment's robustness. The earn-out revaluation charges (SEK 48m statutory profit impact in 2024), routine exclusion of warehouse restructuring costs from adjusted EBITA, and low Q1 2026 operating cash conversion (58% versus 80-100% peer norm) collectively warrant deep-dive scrutiny at ESA to confirm adjusted metrics accurately reflect underlying cash generation quality rather than obscuring structural cost issues.

Risk 3 — FX structural mismatch and zero hedging policy. With 91% of invoicing in foreign currencies, 93% of costs in foreign currencies, a structural negative USD position, and zero financial derivative hedges, Bufab has material unmanaged currency risk. A +30% USD stress scenario erases 26% of EBITA per the Risks report quantification. ESA requires a full FX scenario analysis and an explicit assessment of whether the zero-hedging policy is a deliberate natural-hedging strategy (in which case the natural hedge quality needs to be evidenced) or a cost-management decision that is creating uncompensated earnings volatility that shareholders are not pricing.
