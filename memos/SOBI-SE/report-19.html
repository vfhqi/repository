§2 — Failure Narrative 1: Alhemo Volume Ramp Disappointment (Haematology Revenue Miss)

Specific failure narrative: Alhemo (fitusiran), Sobi's subcutaneous haemophilia prophylaxis asset, fails to penetrate the inhibitor and non-inhibitor haemophilia A/B market at the rate embedded in consensus models. The transmission mechanism: slow physician adoption → below-consensus Alhemo revenues → haematology segment misses → EPS miss on a segment that carries the EPS growth narrative for 2025-2027. Fitusiran's thrombotic event black-box warning creates a structural adoption ceiling among cautious haematologists, particularly in paediatric settings.

Early warning signals:
- Bearish: Monthly ex-US new patient start data from named-patient programmes below internal Sobi guidance cadence
- Bearish: US market share data (Symphony Health / IQVIA) showing Alhemo new-to-brand prescriptions flat or declining QoQ
- Bearish: Key opinion leader survey data (if available via expert network) indicating persistent physician hesitation on thrombotic risk
- Bearish: European HTA decisions (NICE, HAS, G-BA) imposing severe patient-selection restrictions that narrow addressable population below model assumptions
- Bearish: Competing subcutaneous haemophilia B asset labelling improvement (Fitusiran vs. gene therapy timeline)

Plausibility: High plausibility — haematology prophylaxis launches in rare disease consistently underperform in years 1-3 when competing against established factor replacement and emicizumab (Hemlibra); black-box warnings on mechanism-based thrombosis have materially limited similar assets (concizumab precedent)

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Thrombotic signal creates prescriber hesitation — structural ceiling exists
- Motive: Haematologists have established Hemlibra pathways for haemophilia A; inertia is motive for non-switching
- Opportunity: Every competitive label update or gene therapy advance creates a renewed opportunity for physicians to delay Alhemo adoption
- Verdict: All three legs aligned — full MMO qualification — not a "watch only"

Disconfirming evidence: Alhemo achieving ≥15% inhibitor patient share in EU5 by end-2026 would be inconsistent with this failure narrative; absence of reported serious thrombotic events post-launch would partially disconfirm physician hesitation thesis

Monitoring checklist:

- Alhemo new patient start commentary in quarterly earnings calls
- FDA label update or REMS modification announcements
- Haemophilia A/B gene therapy approval timelines (BioMarin, uniQure)
- Physician switching surveys from relevant haematology congresses (ASH, EHA)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Failure Narrative 2 (Alprolix royalty cliff) — if Alhemo underperforms AND Alprolix royalties decline, haematology EPS exposure is compound; also interacts with Narrative 9 (gene therapy structural disruption)

Sceptical close: Disconfirming evidence would need Alhemo to simultaneously show: (a) clean post-market safety record, (b) robust payer coverage in 5+ major EU markets, and (c) physician willingness to switch stable Hemlibra patients — none of these is yet visible in public domain
