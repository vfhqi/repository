EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

ACS Group operates under consolidated leadership with CEO Juan Santamaría Cases concurrently serving as Chief Executive Officer of Hochtief AG and Executive Chairman of CIMIC, while Executive Chairman Florentino Pérez holds a 14.5% stake as the largest individual shareholder . This centralized governance model streamlines capital allocation but introduces key-man dependency risk across the entire conglomerate structure. The group has executed major strategic simplification through the 100% CIMIC acquisition, Flatiron-Dragados merger creating the second-largest US civil builder, and increasing direct Hochtief ownership to 77.8% by December 2025, with no active activist presence recorded . Management rhetoric reflects high strategic confidence, with Florentino Pérez confirming the group surpassed its strategic targets more than a year ahead of schedule and projecting 20% to 25% net profit growth by end of 2026 .

The external value-chain landscape presents both structural tailwinds and execution bottlenecks that materially shape forward earnings visibility. Infrastructure and digital backlog growth is accelerating globally, propelled by a 20% per annum data center capex surge , while subsidiary Hochtief reported FY25 sales of €38,236m, up 14.8% year-on-year, driven by Turner and advanced technologies . However, historical operating cash flow remains highly volatile year-to-date driven by typical project payment milestones, with Q1 2026 reflecting seasonal working

capital outflow of negative €1.5bn . Competition remains intense in North American managed lanes bidding, although CIMIC's data center growth offsets transport roll-offs, with operational PBT reaching €116m representing a 4.8% increase . Regulatory support through unprecedented public funding programs provides multi-year revenue visibility, yet severe specialized craft labor shortages and equipment lead times stretching up to 40 months represent the primary execution constraints .

The forward earnings trajectory hinges on six prioritized operational drivers ranked by estimated impact and plausibility. First , Turner Data Center EPC delivered outstanding profitability with sales growing 12% reported and 25% FX-adjusted to €6.54bn in Q1 2026, expanding EBITDA margin by 72 bps to 3.9% on high-tech mix, while new orders surged 48% FX-adjusted to €10.3bn . Second , greenfield US managed lanes concessions offer long- duration recurring toll revenues, although bid success remains subject to competitive pressure from international consortia. Third , the BlackRock GIP co-development platform combines ACS construction expertise with external equity funding to develop 1.7 GW of data center capacity , capturing infrastructure-like valuations rather than contracting margins. Fourth , the Flatiron-Dragados merger targets $30m to $40m in annual synergies through procurement and centralized functions , optimizing scale while mitigating execution risks through collaborative contracting. Fifth , energy transition platforms including SMR nuclear partnerships establish structural multi- decade capex pipelines, though regulatory approvals create lengthy development cycles. Sixth , Australian defense infrastructure expansion targeting €10bn backlog by 2030 insulates revenue streams from traditional non-residential real estate volatility.

Recent earnings delivery demonstrates robust operational execution against conservative guidance thresholds. Q1 2026 delivered sales of €12.34bn up 12.5% FX-adjusted but 0.5% below consensus due to project timing, while EBITDA reached €772m up 15.9% FX-adjusted and beating consensus by 2.3%, with ordinary net profit reaching €239m up 25.0% reported and 0.9% ahead of consensus . Group net profit of €950m in FY25 exceeded revised guidance of €820m to €855m, while Hochtief's FY25 profit of €789m exceeded initial guidance of €680m to €730m , confirming management's consistent pattern of conservative sandbagging followed by upward revisions. Broker analysis indicates ACS leadership has been consistently conservative in its guidance policies, with management upgrading FY25 net profit growth targets mid-year from 9-17% to 20-25% on Turner strength, and Turner's Q1 operational results exceeding its full-year guidance range of 25% to 30% PBT growth . 11

Peer-relative fundamental momentum positions ACS in the top quartile of its European infrastructure peer group on forward earnings growth despite structurally lower operating margins versus concession-heavy competitors. ACS Group sits in the top quartile on forward earnings growth driven by Turner Construction's specialized advanced technology backlog, with revenue growing from €50,377m in FY25 to €57,205m in FY26E representing a 13.55% top-line expansion that leads peers . Despite top-line outperformance, ACS Group's operating margin profile remains low-tier at 4.20% compared to Vinci's best-in-class 13.10% operating margin, highlighting the structural dilution of pure contracting versus toll concessions . Bernstein raised its underlying earnings estimates for subsidiary Hochtief by 5.60% on average over the FY26-28E horizon following record Q1 data center bookings , while Ferrovial's core Canadian asset the 407 ETR increased average revenue per trip by 12.40% year-on-year in Q1 2026, confirming the high inelasticity of its unregulated toll structure . Vinci maintains superior structural cash generation targeting €6bn in FCF for FY26, whereas ACS ended Q1 2026 with net debt of €1,472m up from €400m

net cash in 2023, though LTM Free Cash Flow reached €2,020m .

Sell-side commentary remains highly polarized between structural growth bulls and concession-focused value skeptics. Rating mix stands at 35% Overweight, 50% Equal-weight, and 15% Underweight, with target prices exhibiting high dispersion ranging from €53.10 to €156.00 and Bloomberg consensus at €114.00 per share . Deutsche Bank initiates coverage with Hold and €86.70 target arguing structural growth is fully priced in, while Jefferies historical Hold thesis focuses on re-investment risks across concessions, and Deutsche Bank highlights that Construction remains a low-margin business of only circa 2% on net profits . The group raised €1.75bn to €1.8bn in fresh funds in May 2026 through equity swaps and rights, contradicting earlier executive comments that operating cash flow fully covered investments , creating short-term balance sheet questions and dilutive EPS cuts of approximately 8.3% by Morgan Stanley .

Core downside risks cluster around four transmission mechanisms that could rapidly compress forward valuation multiples. First , extreme concentration on US hyperscaler data center spending leaves the thesis vulnerable to any cloud capex deceleration, which would immediately expose underlying low-margin contracting cash flows. Second , severe electrical grid connection bottlenecks stretching up to ten years in European hubs could simultaneously halt Turner's backlog execution and derail the BlackRock GIP JV commercialization timeline. Third , highly centralized leadership under Juan Santamaría Cases creates key-man risk across ACS, Hochtief, and CIMIC simultaneously, with no clear succession depth disclosed. Fourth , cash conversion remains highly sensitive to working capital swings with extreme volatility suggesting operating cash remains dependent on complex subsidiary dividend upstreams , amplified by substantial first-quarter cash outflows at CIMIC due to project ramp-downs .

Final investment verdict: ACS Group presents a high-conviction cyclical growth case underpinned by unprecedented data center infrastructure demand and conservative management guidance that establishes a favorable delivery track record. The company occupies a leading fundamental momentum position within its peer group, capturing exponential advanced technology order books that materially outpace traditional civil works volumes. However, the investment case carries elevated execution dependency on sustained hyperscaler capex, grid infrastructure availability, and subsidiary cash upstream efficiency, while trading at elevated forward multiples that fully embed structural growth expectations. The recent €1.8bn equity raise introduces near-term share dilution headwinds and raises questions regarding balance sheet optionality versus stated operating cash sufficiency. Investors should monitor Turner's quarterly data center revenue execution against the $17bn to $19bn FY26 target, net working capital cadence at CIMIC, and GIP JV commercialization milestones as critical validation points for the forward earnings trajectory
