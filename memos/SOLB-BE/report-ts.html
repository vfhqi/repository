Section 2 – Thematic Synthesis 9 findings

Thematic Diagnosis and Core Investment Debate

1.
Core investment debate: The fundamental question for Solvay investors is whether the company's post-demerger streamlining and €300 million cost-saving program can offset persistent structural headwinds from Chinese soda ash overcapacity and European energy cost disadvantages
2.
Competing structural and cyclical forces: On one side, the structural thesis rests on the natural soda ash cost advantage of the Wyoming Green River expansion, the global #1 position in hydrogen peroxide serving high-value semiconductor markets, and a growing electronic-grade peroxides business
3.
Overarching macro risk: On the other side, the bear case centers on Chinese synthetic soda ash overcapacity permanently depressing seaborne spot prices, compounding European energy cost disadvantages, and preventing margin recovery in the Basic Chemicals segment

Five Defining Themes

4.
, hosting the high-margin soda ash, bicarbonate, and hydrogen peroxide business lines
5.
Unmatched soda ash scale: Solvay is the global leader in bicarbonate and the global #2 in soda ash, serving major end markets including flat glass, container glass, and detergents
6.
Leading peroxide positions: The peroxides business line (22% of group sales) is the global #1 in hydrogen peroxide, serving paper bleaching and high-value semiconductor markets
7.
Specialty performance platforms: Performance Chemicals represents 38% of group sales, hosting highly dispersible silica, regional bio-sourced solvents, and specialized rare earth applications
8.
Premium silica leadership: The Silica business (12% of sales) is the global leader in highly dispersible silica, primarily providing tire manufacturers with fuel efficiency-enabling compounds
9.
Rare earth recycling optionality: Solvay is uniquely positioned to capitalize on the rare earth recycling opportunity through its La Rochelle facility, which can recover and separate rare earth elements from permanent magnets used in wind turbines and electric vehicle motors

Section 3 – Business Quality 12 findings

Revenue and Margin Architecture

1.
Revenue trajectory: FY25 group revenue of €4,262 million represents a -5.0% decline from €4,482 million in FY24, driven primarily by volume weakness in Basic Chemicals from the scheduled maintenance of the Dombasle soda ash unit and lower peroxide volumes due to geopolitical disruptions in the Middle East
2.
Segment margin divergence: Basic Chemicals EBITDA margin contracted from 21.1% in FY24 to 20.1% in FY25, driven by seeaborne soda ash spot price erosion

Roadshow Presentation

3.
Resilient performance chemicals margins: Performance Chemicals margins remained highly resilient, reporting 18.9% in 2023 and ending FY25 at 18.8% due to favorable product mix in Special Chem
4.
Steeper net income decline: Underlying net profit fell from €445 million in 2024 to €306 million in 2025, resulting in pro-forma EPS falling from approximately €4.20 to €2.89

Cash Conversion and Capital Efficiency

5.
Defensive cash conversion: Solvay achieved exceptional cash generation, delivering €350 million free cash flow in FY25 through disciplined capital expenditures of €292 million
6.
Strong balance sheet defense: Management successfully protected its investment grade rating, maintaining net debt/EBITDA at 2.0x versus a 2.5x covenant threshold, with €869 million of committed undrawn credit facilities providing liquidity headroom

Competitive Position and Moat Assessment

7.
Global hydrogen peroxide leadership: Solvay's global #1 position in hydrogen peroxide and its proprietary anthraquinone oxidation process provide a significant manufacturing cost advantage and technical barrier to new entrants
8.
Electronic-grade differentiation: Solvay has doubled its Zhenjiang electronic-grade hydrogen peroxide capacity and extended customer agreements with major Asian semiconductor fabs, establishing a high-margin, high-purity platform within the broader commodity peroxides business
9.
Natural soda ash cost moat: The Green River site in Wyoming benefits from direct access to the world's largest natural trona ore deposit, providing a structural energy cost advantage of approximately 30-40% versus European synthetic production
10.
Premium purity pricing power: Solvay commands significant pricing premiums for low-iron soda ash, with expert network sources confirming customer premiums of up to $60 per container for glass manufacturers requiring optical-grade purity
11.
Semi-captive customer relationships: Solvay maintains long-term supply agreements, often with take-or-pay structures, with major glass producers and tire manufacturers, providing revenue visibility in its core Basic Chemicals segment
12.
Rare earth separation expertise: Solvay's La Rochelle plant is one of only two European facilities capable of separating mixed rare earth oxides, giving it a structurally irreplaceable role in the emerging European permanent magnet supply chain

Section 5 – Valuation 8 findings

Absolute Valuation Anchors

1.
Current multiple: Solvay trades at approximately 5.5-6.0x EV/EBITDA on consensus 2026 estimates, representing a 25-35% discount to the 7.5-8.5x range commanded by comparable European specialty chemical peers with stronger earnings growth profiles
2.
Dividend yield signal: The prospective dividend yield of over 9% on the current share price signals extreme skepticism about dividend sustainability, with Deutsche Bank and Jefferies both forecasting that 2026 free cash flow will fail to cover the €254 million dividend

Bull, Base and Bear Scenarios

3.
Bull case (12-month €40 target): Deutsche Bank's bull case assumes full Green River ramp, cost-saving delivery, and rare earth expansion approval, implying ~55% upside
4.
Base case (€30.00 target): Morgan Stanley maintains an Overweight rating with a €30 price target, implying a 16% upside, based on near-term earnings trough recognition and Green River volume ramp
5.
Bear case (€20.50 target): Bernstein maintains an Underperform with a €20.50 target, implying 21% downside, arguing that structurally impaired European assets will overwhelm Green River upside

Variant Perception and Upside Optionality

6.
Significant EBITDA option: Deutsche Bank argues that a successful rare earths expansion could add up to €100 million in EBITDA, representing unique upside optionality
7.
Independent growth drivers: The bull case drivers are largely independent, meaning the cost-saving and US natural capacity programs can succeed even if the rare earths opportunity fails

Symmetrical Scepticism and Bear Views

8.
Other side of the story: The expansion benefits could be entirely offset if global overcapacity in soda ash persists, with seaborne pricing remaining at depressed levels and undermining Green River's export margins

Section 6 – Risks 4 findings

Top Risks and Mitigants

1.
Chinese soda ash overcapacity (Critical): Continued capacity additions from Chinese synthetic producers could permanently suppress global seaborne soda ash prices, destroying the Green River expansion's margin assumptions
2.
European energy cost exposure (High): Solvay's Dombasle, Torrelavega, and other European synthetic soda ash plants remain structurally exposed to gas price volatility, with European production carrying a 30-40% energy cost disadvantage versus Green River
3.
Jubail geopolitical disruption (High): The Middle East conflict has disrupted Solvay's Jubail joint venture peroxides plant since March 2026, directly impacting 4-5% of group revenue with uncertain timing of resumption
4.
Dividend sustainability risk (High): Deutsche Bank and Jefferies both forecast 2026 free cash flow of approximately €200 million will fail to cover the €254 million dividend commitment, creating unsustainable leverage build
