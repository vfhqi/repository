EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

easyJet operates as a distinctive hybrid point-to-point short-haul carrier across Western Europe, generating revenue through passenger ticket sales, ancillary products including baggage and seat allocation, and a rapidly expanding capital-light easyJet holidays tour operator business that contributed approximately 31% of group headline profit in full-year 2025 . The carrier leverages a network concentrated at slot-constrained Level 3 primary airports including London Gatwick, Geneva, and Amsterdam, differentiating itself from ultra-low-cost carriers that target secondary airport locations . This strategic positioning enables the group to command pricing premiums by attracting higher socio-economic passenger segments, though it imposes structurally higher landing charges and operating costs compared to pure ultra-low-cost competitors.
