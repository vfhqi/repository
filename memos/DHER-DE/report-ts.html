Section 3 – What the company does 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Delivery Hero holds unmatched leading positions in approximately 90% of its markets, driving highly profitable regional scale.
1.
Asia platform dominance: Asia remains Delivery Hero's largest geographic segment, contributing 42% of Group Gross Merchandise Value in FY25, primarily through market leadership in South Korea High-margin MENA engine: The MENA segment contributes 30% of GMV and represents the primary driver of Group profitability through highly lucrative local brands European localized presence: Europe accounts for 20% of Group GMV, with its commercial footprint heavily centered on Glovo's operations in Spain and Italy Americas growth exposure: The Americas segment contributes 8% of GMV, showing healthy localized growth and delivering €100 million in Adjusted EBITDA in FY25 Localized platform brands: Platforms operate locally under prominent brand names, including Woowa in South Korea, talabat and HungerStation in MENA, and Glovo in Europe Active market exits: Delivery Hero took the disciplined decision to close subscale foodpanda operations in Thailand in May 2025 to reallocate capital to higher-return regions
2.
Direct principal model: The Integrated Verticals segment operates 811 local Dmarts, acting as principal for delivering groceries within 20 to 30 minutes Turnaround vertical EBITDA: Integrated Verticals achieved a major milestone in FY25 by delivering positive Adjusted EBITDA of €2.90 million, up from negative €98.70 million in FY24 Mutually reinforcing cohorts: Multi-vertical grocery customers spend five times more than food-only users, significantly enhancing customer lifetime value and order density Expanding store network: The micro-fulfillment network expanded to 811 stores across 46 countries in FY25, driving improved logistics capacity and local speed Favourable grocery TAM: On-demand grocery in MENA presents over USD 150 billion TAM with low single-digit penetration, supporting rapid micro-fulfillment scaling
3.
Multi-layered monetisation: Delivery Hero generates revenue from restaurant commission fees, consumer delivery and service fees, online payment processing, and advertising retail media Contractual commission base: Commission fees are based on contractually specified percentages of order values, varying by country, delivery model, and merchant size High-margin AdTech engine: Retail media advertising reaches 3% of GMV, providing a lucrative high-margin monetization engine with pure profit drop-through Sticky subscription pro: Loyalty subscription programs, such as talabat pro, reached 43% of Group GMV, locking in recurring order volumes and boosting frequency Mutually reinforcing frequency: Subscriptions lower customer order barriers while significantly boosting user retention and preventing cross-shopping across competitor applications
4.
Powerful network effects: The competitive moat is anchored in a three-sided network connecting millions of consumers, freelance riders, and local business partners Exceptional merchant lock-in: Holding number-one positions in 90% of markets provides immense merchant pricing power, as local restaurants must list to access digital demand Centralized platform integration: Consolidating all regional brands onto a unified technology platform has unlocked central development synergies and enabled automated AI order dispatch Consolidating industry structure: The global food delivery landscape has consolidated into regional oligopolies, with DoorDash acquiring Deliveroo and Prosus acquiring Just Eat Takeaway Deliveroo asset acquisition: DHER acquired selected assets from Deliveroo in Hong Kong in March 2025 to consolidate vendor footprint and market share Other side of the story: Stand-alone platforms face structural disadvantages against super-apps like Grab that bundle payments and mobility services, requiring DHER to maintain higher promotional subsidies

Section summary
  1. Stretched balance sheet leverage and complex European labor classifications present critical earnings threats.

European gig reclassification: Stricter platform work directives threaten to mandate full rider employment, inflating logistics labor costs across Europe Severe Italian investigations: Italy's competition probe into Glovo's algorithms introduces significant localized operational cost and reputational risks Antitrust regulatory barriers: European Commission and local antitrust rules could block geographic asset exits, leaving DHER trapped in subscale, cash-burning markets
2.
Massive Spanish contingencies: Spain rider reclassifications expose DHER to up to €1.30 billion in off-balance-sheet contingent liabilities Elevated debt leverage: Stretched leverage of 2.20x Net Debt/EBITDA makes the company highly vulnerable to interest rate refinancing shocks Subsidiary reinvestment starvation: Extracting cash from subsidiary balance sheets to repay DHER debt limits local marketing spend, risking regional share losses
3.
Deceptive GMV indicators: A key false-friend is accelerating European GMV, which masks margin deterioration from Spain's mandatory rider laws South Korean share-loss: Rapid market share gains by Coupang WOW threaten Woowa's core profitability in South Korea Uncontrolled hyperinflationary exposure: High exposure to hyperinflationary economies in Argentina and Türkiye introduces persistent reporting volatility and operating margin compression Other side of the story: Cost inflation is manageable as platforms have proven highly capable of passing rider costs to consumers via higher service fees
