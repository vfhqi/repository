Section 3 – What the company does 25 findings

Section summary
  1. Nolato operates a highly advanced polymer development model serving resilient medical and high-tech industrial niches with customized, high-volume production setups.

Business segments and capabilities

1.
Advanced medical solutions: The Medical Solutions business area manufactures complex medtech devices, pharmaceutical packaging, and automated drug delivery systems
2.
Technical industrial solutions: The Engineered Solutions business area develops specialized polymer products, subsystem assemblies, and heat-management products
3.
Integrated materials technology: Within Engineered Solutions, the Materials segment specializes in custom electromagnetic shielding and thermal solutions
4.
Shared operational synergies: Both divisions share a centralized technology platform, enabling significant infrastructure and knowledge transfer
5.
High-volume box-building: Medical Solutions utilizes advanced automated cleanroom technologies to assemble fully integrated drug delivery devices
6.
Automotive industrial footprint: Engineered Solutions maintains a cyclical automotive segment, representing roughly 23% of the division's total sales
7.
Pharmaceutical packaging solutions: Medical Solutions also designs and manufactures advanced packaging configurations for pharmaceutical and dietary supplement products
8.
In vitro diagnostics: Diagnostic components and PCR test systems represent a substantial market area, accounting for 18% of Medical Solutions sales
9.
Pharma packaging size: Specialized pharmaceutical packaging products account for roughly 11% of Medical Solutions' total business area sales
10.
Cardiology device products: Advanced catheter balloons and components for cardiac anchors represent 7% of the company's Medical segment sales
11.
Continence care applications: Continence care and urology products contribute 12% of the company's Medical Solutions segment sales

Value chain and competitive advantages

12.
Durable switching costs: High customer switching costs arise from the complex, multi-year validation procedures required for medical cleanroom manufacturing
13.
Deep co-development integration: Technical Design Centres ensure deep product integration from early concept design to high-volume automated production phases
14.
Strategic material positioning: Nolato sits favorably in the value chain between basic material suppliers and global product owners
15.
Durable proprietary scale: Massive global manufacturing capacity across three continents provides a competitive shield that smaller competitors cannot easily match
16.
Robust IP shielding: The company utilizes unique polymer and silicone formulation expertise to build custom shielding for durable customer switching costs
17.
Advanced cleanroom capabilities: Highly automated, specialized cleanroom facilities in Europe and North America prevent smaller competitors from entering medtech manufacturing
18.
Localized production footprint: Proximity to global pharmaceutical assembly plants represents a key driver of customer trust and supply-chain stability

Market and industry dynamics

19.
Growing medical outsourcing: The global medical outsourcing market grows at 3% to 5% annually, driven by aging demographics
20.
Fragmented industrial market: The industrial molding sector remains highly fragmented, allowing Nolato to expand its addressable market
21.
Consolidated medical supplier: The medtech segment features consolidated, high-barrier dynamics where Nolato competes with major players like Gerresheimer
22.
Improving customer diversification: Customer concentration has decreased significantly following the strategic wind-down of historically volatile vaporiser heating product contracts
23.
Competitive peer insulation: Peer West Pharmaceutical Services maintains a dominant 70% share in rubber markets, showing high medtech entry barriers
24.
Other side of the story: Intensive pharmaceutical customer price negotiations and dual-sourcing strategies constantly challenge contract manufacturer margin expansion potential

Transformational medtech win: A major SEK 700 million obesity contract in Hungary represents the primary catalyst for Medical Solutions' forward organic growth

Pen injector mechanics: Success relies on Novo Nordisk scaling its regional production, with pen injector volumes expected to be highly margin-accretive

Long term EBITA contribution: The Hungary obesity contract is projected to contribute 7% to 8% of group EBITA at full capacity by 2028-29
6.
Significant regional investments: Nolato has invested over SEK 600 million in factory expansion in Hungary to support this specific contract

7.
Surging shielding demand: Rapidly growing AI data centre investments drive substantial demand for Nolato's specialized electromagnetic shielding and thermal products
8.
Highly positive momentum: The Materials segment recorded outstanding 15% organic growth in Q1 2026, benefiting from satellite and defence demand

Driver failure mode: Shielding growth depends on continuous data centre capex, which could decelerate if tech giants optimize server designs

Low starting base: While data centre exposure is currently small, it represents a substantial driver of Engineered Solutions' growth momentum

Additional high tech areas: Advanced thermal products are also scaling across space satellites, defense applications, and electronic vehicle architectures

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk landscape is moderate and heavily scrutinized, with short-term petrochemical price lags representing the most immediate threat to quarterly operating margins.

Short-term margin pressures

1.
Petrochemical inflation risks: Nolato faces rising plastic raw material costs, which are projected to increase by 5% to 10% in mid-2026
2.
Precious metal headwind: Elevated silver prices, a key input for electromagnetic shielding materials, created margin pressure in Q4 2025 and early 2026
3.
Pass-through timing lag: It takes roughly one quarter to pass material costs to customers, resulting in petrochemical cost pass-through lags of SEK 30 million
4.
Project start-up dilution: Start-up costs for new medtech projects in the US temporarily diluted Medical Solutions' margins by 0.5 percentage points in Q4 2025
5.
Elevated input cost base: Nolato's petrochemical-linked material cost base is estimated at SEK 1.6 billion to 1.7 billion annually
6.
Pricing adjustment lags: Contracts generally restrict immediate price increases, forcing Nolato to absorb petrochemical inflation for at least one quarter

Long-term cyclical and customer risks

7.
Cyclical automotive exposure: Engineered Solutions remains exposed to cyclical industrial production, with automotive clients accounting for 23% of segment sales
8.
Customer strategic shifts: Loss of major contracts remains a risk, as seen historically when British American Tobacco shifted its vaporizer strategy
9.
False friend signals: Rapid growth in Engineered Solutions sales can mask margin dilution if driven by low-margin industrial automotive tooling
10.
Utilization monitoring trigger: Investors must monitor quarterly Medical Solutions cleanroom capacity utilization as the key early-warning signal for earnings
11.
Other side of the story: Bear arguments are overstated because the resilient Medical Solutions segment now represents 62% of total sales, neutralizing industrial cyclicality
