Tate & Lyle plc (TATE-GB) is currently navigating a period of profound structural change, having officially
"completed" its multi-year pivot into a pure-play specialty food and beverage solutions provider just as it faces a
conditional £2.7 billion takeover bid from US rival Ingredion . 1 • ARS 2 • Deutsche Bank Research
While the internal transformation—marked by the total divestment of the Primient commodity business and the
integration of CP Kelco —has created a higher-margin, asset-lighter profile, the external environment has soured. Management has been forced to downgrade FY26 and FY27 expectations due to "disappointing" volume
performance in North America and intense pricing competition in Europe and APAC, leading to a significant sell- side de-rating and skepticism regarding the credibility of the company's medium-term growth algorithm.
3 • ARS 4 • Morgan Stanley 5 • Morgan Stanley
The shares currently trade on 9.2x PE and 6.0x EV/EBITDA for CY 26 which represents a ~40% discount to
peers. Whilst we regard this as undeserved given the quality of the business, we appreciate that the market will remain sceptical on its ability to generate growth.
