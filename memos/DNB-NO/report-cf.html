EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

DNB Bank maintains long-tenured CEO stability under Kjerstin R. Braathen since September 2019, though a CFO transition and 500-position workforce reduction raise operational integration and internal morale risks. The bank is executing a strategic pivot toward simplification and digitalisation anchored by the transformative NOK 13.7 billion Carnegie acquisition, positioning capital-light fee income to offset mounting net interest margin pressures. No active activist presence exists in the shareholder register, with the Norwegian Government maintaining its dominant 34.6% anchor stake. Net interest margins peaked at 1.94% in 4Q24 before contracting by 7 basis points sequentially to 1.74% in 1Q26, driven by intensifying retail mortgage competition and repricing pressures. Commission and fee income surged 18% year-on-year in 1Q26 to NOK 4,129 million, successfully cushioning the bottom line against cyclical interest rate headwinds through wealth management and Carnegie integration synergies.
