3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — Failure Narrative 1: European Stainless Demand Structural Break

Narrative: European automotive and industrial end-markets — ORNBV's core demand base — undergo secular contraction rather than cyclical trough, driven by German industrial de-industrialisation, EV platform shifts reducing stainless content per vehicle, and Italian/Spanish white-goods weakness. Transmission: volume throughput falls below breakeven utilisation at Tornio/Avesta, triggering fixed-cost absorption failure and EBITDA collapse disproportionate to volume decline.

Early warning signals:
- European Stainless Steel Association monthly shipment data trending below prior-cycle lows on a 6M rolling basis
- German IFO manufacturing subindex sustained below 42 for ≥3 consecutive months
- ORNBV service-centre customer order lead-times compressing to spot-only (no forward-book visibility)
- Automotive OEM stainless content per platform guidance declining in public EV programme disclosures
- ORNBV capacity utilisation guidance falling below 75% at Tornio complex

Plausibility: High plausibility — precedent from 2015-2016 and 2022-2023 shows European stainless demand can fall 15-20% in 12 months; secular-versus-cyclical distinction is historically very difficult to make in real time, and the current European industrial investment gap makes the secular case stronger than prior cycles

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: structural demand shift is underway (EV transition, reshoring slow, Chinese competition) ✓
- Motive: not an actor-driven failure, but structural incentive for European manufacturers to substitute stainless with composites/coatings exists ✓
- Opportunity: European market is ORNBV's dominant revenue source, no diversification buffer ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED — full activation threshold met

Disconfirming evidence: Demand break would be invalidated by (a) European industrial output recovering above pre-2022 levels on a sustained basis, (b) ORNBV backlog data showing multi-quarter order cover, (c) stainless content-per-vehicle metrics holding flat in public EV programme specifications

Monitoring checklist:
- Monthly ESSA apparent consumption data vs. 5Y average
- ORNBV quarterly shipment volume (tonnes) vs. consensus
- European capacity utilisation (Tornio/Avesta) disclosed in quarterly reports
- German/Italian PMI manufacturing sub-index
- ORNBV forward-order-book commentary in results calls

Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with Failure Narrative 4 (Chinese import pressure) and Failure Narrative 6 (operating leverage failure); if demand break and Chinese competition co-activate, compound failure probability is significantly above sum of individual probabilities

Disconfirming evidence test: ORNBV disclosed 2024 shipment volumes showed partial sequential recovery — this partially contradicts the secular-break hypothesis, but does not falsify it given the recovery base was distress-level 2023 volumes

§3 — Failure Narrative 2: Nickel/Alloy Surcharge Mechanics False Friend (FALSE FRIEND #1)

Narrative: ORNBV's reported EBITDA improvement reflects the lag-structure of alloy surcharge pass-through mechanisms rather than genuine conversion margin recovery. When Ni prices fall, ORNBV's surcharge formulas reset with a 1-2 quarter lag, temporarily inflating reported margins before reverting. Transmission: analyst models using current-quarter EBITDA as forward-run-rate are miscalibrated; when the lag resolves, EBITDA falls sharply even with volumes flat.

Early warning signals:
- LME nickel spot falling while ORNBV reported margins temporarily hold or expand
- Alloy surcharge per-tonne disclosure widening vs. underlying Ni cost trajectory
- Sell-side consensus revisions lagging Ni spot moves by ≥1 quarter
- ORNBV management commentary emphasising "surcharge recovery" rather than "base conversion margin improvement"
- Peer (Aperam) reporting weaker margins on identical Ni price dynamics, diverging from ORNBV

Plausibility: High plausibility — this is a structurally well-documented mechanic in stainless steel that has triggered earnings surprises in both directions multiple times across European producers since 2010; the 2021-2022 Ni spike and subsequent collapse was a live case study in surcharge-mechanics mispricing

MMO test:
- Means: surcharge lag mechanics are contractually embedded in ORNBV customer contracts ✓
- Motive: not actor-driven; structural mechanic ✓
- Opportunity: Ni price has been volatile through 2023-2025; surcharge reset windows are predictably exploitable for mispricing ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED

Disconfirming evidence: Failure hypothesis invalidated by (a) ORNBV explicitly disclosing "alloy-adjusted" conversion margins improving in parallel with reported margins, (b) base EBITDA/tonne (ex-surcharge) trending upward on a multi-quarter basis, (c) Aperam and other peers showing similar margin trajectory on same Ni dynamics (confirming sector, not lag-mechanics)

Monitoring checklist:
- ORNBV EBITDA/tonne vs. LME Ni 2-quarter lag
- Alloy surcharge disclosure per tonne in quarterly reports
- Conversion margin (base EBITDA ex-surcharge) as separately disclosed
- Peer (Aperam) EBITDA/tonne on same-quarter basis for calibration
- Ni futures curve — contango/backwardation as leading indicator of surcharge mechanics

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Failure Narrative 8 (FALSE FRIEND #2 — margin expansion from capex deferral); both produce identical surface-level signals (margin improvement) with different root causes; compound false-friend risk if both active simultaneously

Disconfirming evidence test: ORNBV has in some periods disclosed alloy-adjusted metrics — if those metrics show independent improvement, the false-friend hypothesis weakens materially; the analyst must locate and verify this specific disclosure

§4 — Failure Narrative 3: Chinese Stainless Overcapacity Export Surge

Narrative: Chinese stainless producers (TISCO, Beihai Chengde, Delong) accelerate export of hot-rolled coil and slabs into European/global markets at sub-cost pricing, structurally depressing transaction prices in ORNBV's key 300-series and 400-series grades. Transmission: ORNBV loses pricing power, transaction prices fall below cost inflation, and the company is forced into negative-contribution volume decisions or market share retreat.

Early warning signals:
- Chinese stainless export volumes (customs data) rising >15% YoY sustained for ≥2 quarters
- European spot transaction prices for 304/316 cold-rolled coil falling despite stable input costs
- ORNBV transaction price per tonne (net of surcharge) declining quarter-on-quarter
- EU anti-dumping investigation filings or European Commission safeguard extension proceedings
- Indonesian/Indian RNSS production capacity coming online ahead of schedule (Chinese-financed)

Plausibility: High plausibility — Chinese stainless exports have surged in 2023-2025 and are a documented structural feature of the global stainless market; EU anti-dumping measures provide partial but imperfect protection; precedent from flat carbon steel (2015-2016 surge) is directly applicable

MMO test:
- Means: Chinese producers have excess capacity and low domestic demand ✓
- Motive: export at below-domestic-cost pricing to maintain utilisation and employment ✓
- Opportunity: EU safeguard measures have gaps; ORNBV's 300-series grades are most exposed ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED

Disconfirming evidence: Failure invalidated by (a) EU imposing meaningful new anti-dumping duties on Chinese stainless in ≥2 major product categories within the investment horizon, (b) Chinese domestic demand recovery absorbing excess production, (c) ORNBV transaction price per tonne (ex-surcharge) holding flat or rising despite Chinese export data

Monitoring checklist:
- Chinese customs stainless export monthly volumes
- EU 304 cold-rolled coil transaction price (Metal Bulletin / Fastmarkets)
- EU trade defence investigation pipeline (European Commission Official Journal)
- ORNBV realised transaction price per tonne disclosed quarterly
- Indonesian RNSS capacity ramp announcements

Interaction with other failure modes: Tightly correlated with Failure Narrative 1 (European demand structural break); demand falling while Chinese supply rises is the most dangerous compound failure; also interacts with Failure Narrative 5 (pricing pressure) as secondary amplifier

Disconfirming evidence test: ORNBV's 2024 commentary referenced "stabilised European pricing" — this partially contradicts but does not falsify the narrative; the question is whether stabilisation is structural or a temporary plateau before another leg down

§5 — Failure Narrative 4: Energy Cost Re-acceleration (European)

Narrative: European natural gas and electricity prices re-accelerate due to Russian supply disruption, extreme weather, or LNG market tightness, reversing the 2023-2024 energy cost tailwind that partially drove ORNBV margin recovery. Transmission: Tornio (Finland) is an electricity-intensive EAF operation; energy represents a material portion of conversion cost; a 2x energy price shock (precedented in 2022) would compress EBITDA/tonne by €30-60 at current volumes.

Early warning signals:

- Nordic electricity spot (Nordpool) rising above €80/MWh sustained for ≥4 weeks
- ORNBV energy hedging position disclosed as less than 6M forward cover
- European LNG storage drawdown rate exceeding seasonal norms by >20%
- ORNBV management flagging energy cost as a headwind in guidance commentary

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — European energy prices remain structurally elevated versus pre-2021 levels, but a full 2022-magnitude re-shock requires a specific catalyst; partial re-acceleration (€10-20/MWh above current levels) is more probable and still materially impacts ORNBV

MMO test:
- Means: European energy market remains vulnerable to supply shocks ✓
- Motive: not actor-driven ✓
- Opportunity: ORNBV Tornio plant has limited short-term energy sourcing flexibility; Finnish hydro exposure partially buffers but doesn't eliminate risk ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED (but Opportunity leg is medium-strength given partial hedging)

Disconfirming evidence: Failure invalidated by (a) ORNBV disclosing multi-year fixed-price energy contracts covering >70% of Tornio consumption, (b) Nordic electricity wholesale prices declining on expanded renewable capacity additions, (c) ORNBV's energy cost per tonne remaining flat quarter-on-quarter despite spot market moves

Monitoring checklist:
- Nordpool monthly average electricity price (Finnish bidding zone)
- TTF front-month gas futures
- ORNBV energy cost disclosure (€/tonne) in quarterly results
- ORNBV hedging position disclosure in annual report

Interaction with other failure modes: Partially independent from demand-side failures; however, energy cost inflation + demand weakness is a classic stainless-sector double squeeze (precedented 2022-2023 for ORNBV itself); interacts with Failure Narrative 6 (operating leverage) as cost amplifier

Disconfirming evidence test: ORNBV has invested in energy efficiency and has some hydro-linked procurement in Finland — the analyst must verify current hedge book coverage before concluding energy risk is live

§6 — Failure Narrative 5: Conversion Margin Structural Compression (Operating Leverage Failure)

Narrative: ORNBV's multi-year restructuring (closure of Krefeld carbon steel operations, rationalisation of European footprint) was premised on achieving €100-150/tonne EBITDA through-cycle conversion margin. If structural demand falls and pricing competition intensifies simultaneously, conversion margin settles at €40-60/tonne — insufficient to service the capital base and fund the ferrochrome/Tornio investment programme. Transmission: EBITDA falls below €400m at trough, free cash flow turns negative, dividend cut and/or balance sheet stress becomes the investment narrative.

Early warning signals:
- EBITDA/tonne falling below €80 on a trailing 12M basis
- Analyst consensus EBITDA estimates declining for ≥3 consecutive quarterly revision cycles
- ORNBV guidance for full-year EBITDA below €300m
- Working capital absorption rising disproportionate to revenue (inventory build at lower prices)
- Free cash flow turning negative for ≥2 consecutive quarters

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — ORNBV has demonstrated through-cycle margin improvement versus the 2015-2019 baseline, but the restructuring benefits are largely realised; the next leg of margin improvement requires either volume recovery or further cost reduction, both of which are uncertain

MMO test:
- Means: fixed-cost base is still substantial post-restructuring ✓
- Motive: not actor-driven; structural economics ✓
- Opportunity: utilisation leverage means below-threshold volumes directly hit EBITDA ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED

Disconfirming evidence: Failure invalidated by (a) ORNBV achieving EBITDA/tonne sustainably above €100 across a full cycle including a trough quarter, (b) conversion cost per tonne showing absolute decline year-on-year (inflation-adjusted), (c) management providing explicit through-cycle floor guidance with credible basis

Monitoring checklist:
- ORNBV EBITDA/tonne (conversion margin) per quarterly disclosure
- Fixed cost base (SG&A + depreciation as % of revenue)
- Capacity utilisation rate at Tornio and Avesta
- Free cash flow generation vs. capex
- Net debt trajectory vs. EBITDA leverage ratio

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the aggregator failure mode — operating leverage failure is the output of demand break (FN1), pricing compression (FN3), and energy cost (FN4) combining; it is the EPS mechanism through which upstream failures transmit to the P&L

Disconfirming evidence test: ORNBV's 2021-2022 supercycle demonstrated that conversion margins can reach €200+/tonne — but mean reversion from those levels is itself a form of this failure narrative activating at lower intensity

§7 — Failure Narrative 6: Capex Deferral False Friend (FALSE FRIEND #2)

Narrative: ORNBV manages near-term FCF and reported EBITDA margins through systematic under-investment in Tornio and Avesta maintenance/upgrade capex, producing strong near-term cash conversion that appears to confirm operational leverage improvement. The real transmission mechanism: deferred maintenance creates a future capex catch-up obligation that will suppress FCF for 3-5 years, while the decision also allows competitor reinvestment to erode ORNBV's relative cost position.

Early warning signals:
- Annual capex/depreciation ratio falling below 0.8x for ≥2 consecutive years
- ORNBV management guiding capex "in line with or below depreciation" as a conscious policy
- Maintenance capex as % of gross assets declining on a 3Y rolling basis
- Unplanned downtime incidents at Tornio or Avesta increasing (disclosed in safety/operations data)
- Competitor (Aperam, Acerinox) capex per tonne rising while ORNBV declines

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — ORNBV has been in a period of capital discipline following the balance sheet restructuring; this is a known sector pattern (ArclorMittal used similar mechanics in 2013-2016); the question is whether current capex levels are disciplined-optimal or structurally-deficient

MMO test:
- Means: management has discretion over capex timing within a range ✓
- Motive: short-term incentive compensation tied to FCF/EBITDA metrics ✓
- Opportunity: ageing plant base at Tornio and Avesta creates the window for deferral ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED

Disconfirming evidence: Failure invalidated by (a) ORNBV providing multi-year capex programme with credible maintenance vs. growth disaggregation, (b) capex/depreciation ratio consistently above 1.0x, (c) no deterioration in plant reliability metrics (uptime, yield rates), (d) independent technical review confirming asset condition

Monitoring checklist:
- Annual capex disclosed vs. D&A (ratio)
- Maintenance vs. growth capex split (if disclosed)
- Unplanned downtime incidents (sustainability/operations report)
- Gross PP&E age profile (notes to accounts)
- Competitor capex intensity benchmarking (Aperam/Acerinox annual reports)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Failure Narrative 2 (surcharge false friend); both produce near-term margin improvement signals; compound false-friend risk if both are simultaneously active; also links to Failure Narrative 9 (balance sheet/debt stress) as deferred capex eventually creates a capital call

Disconfirming evidence test: if ORNBV's 2024-2025 capex run-rate is above 1.0x depreciation AND they have publicly committed to the Tornio debottlenecking programme with milestone disclosure, the capex-deferral false-friend hypothesis is materially weakened

§9 — Failure Narrative 8: CBAM / Green Premium Transition Failure

Narrative: The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism , phasing in from 2026, creates a complex compliance burden and competitive asymmetry for ORNBV. If ORNBV cannot capture a "green premium" from European customers willing to pay for low-carbon stainless, while simultaneously bearing higher CBAM-related compliance costs, the net competitive effect is negative. Transmission: ORNBV's cost base rises, no offsetting price premium materialises, and CBAM transition becomes a multi-year margin headwind of €10-30/tonne rather than a competitive shield.

Early warning signals:
- Customer survey/industry data showing unwillingness to pay green premium for stainless
- European Commission CBAM implementation scope narrowing or delayed, reducing competitive protection
- ORNBV carbon intensity per tonne not improving on a trajectory to remain competitive post-2030
- Peer (Aperam) or competitors achieving lower carbon intensity per tonne at lower cost
- ORNBV CBAM compliance cost disclosure in annual accounts rising without offsetting revenue premium

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — CBAM is a real and novel risk; the green-premium hypothesis is unproven in stainless (it has more evidence in flat carbon steel where ESG procurement policies are more advanced); the CBAM creates genuine opportunity for ORNBV IF customers pay the premium, and genuine risk if they do not

MMO test:
- Means: CBAM regulatory framework is being implemented ✓
- Motive: ORNBV's competitive position vs. non-EU peers affected by CBAM design ✓
- Opportunity: green-premium capture window is currently open and unproven ✓
- MMO: ALL THREE LEGS ALIGNED

Disconfirming evidence: Failure invalidated by (a) documented customer willingness to pay studies showing ≥€20/tonne green premium for certified low-carbon stainless, (b) ORNBV achieving Science-Based Target verified reductions ahead of CBAM phase-in, (c) EU CBAM scope applied broadly enough to create genuine import cost barrier

Monitoring checklist:
- EU CBAM implementation progress and product scope updates (European Commission)
- ORNBV Scope 1/2 carbon intensity per tonne (sustainability report)
- Customer procurement policy disclosures for stainless ESG requirements
- Aperam green steel pricing premium data (public disclosures)
- ORNBV explicit green premium realisation disclosed in segment reporting

Interaction with other failure modes: ⚡ CBAM is the least-monitored failure mode in sell-side coverage of ORNBV — it operates on a 2026+ horizon that consensus models do not explicitly capture, creating a monitoring gap where slow-moving adverse dynamics could compound undetected until phase-in is close

Disconfirming evidence test: if ORNBV has signed binding long-term supply agreements with explicit green-premium pricing with European OEM customers, the CBAM-failure hypothesis is significantly weakened — no such contracts have been publicly disclosed as of Q1 2026
