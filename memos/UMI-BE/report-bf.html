EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Umicore operates as a diversified advanced materials and recycling group across four business segments, positioned at the intersection of global electrification and circular economy megatrends. The company's unique business model combines high-margin precious metals recycling, mature automotive catalyst manufacturing, and capital-intensive battery cathode materials production. While the legacy foundation businesses of Catalysis and Recycling generate exceptional cash flows and returns, the Battery Materials segment faces severe structural headwinds from Chinese overcapacity and slower-than-expected European electric vehicle adoption. Under new CEO Bart Sap's CORE 2028 strategy, management has successfully pivoted from aggressive growth to capital discipline, unlocking significant working capital and stabilizing leverage. However, the group's long-term value creation remains heavily dependent on Western regionalization policies and the ability to restore acceptable returns from its battery materials investments.

Business Model and Organizational Architecture

Umicore's circular business model excels in foundation cash extraction, operating through four distinct Business Groups: Catalysis, Energy & Surface Technologies, Recycling, and Specialty Materials . The Catalysis segment focuses on automotive emission control, with light-duty vehicle catalysts representing 86% of business unit revenues , positioning the company as the global market leader in gasoline catalyst technologies. The Recycling segment anchors around the Hoboken, Belgium refinery, recovering 17 precious and specialty metals

from complex industrial waste streams , creating a formidable pyrometallurgical processing moat. Battery Materials Solutions integrates cathode active materials production with battery recycling, targeting nickel- manganese-cobalt (NMC) chemistries for premium electric vehicles . The revenue model isolates manufacturing margins from commodity volatility through comprehensive metal pass-through contracts, though the Autocatalyst business unit remains directly exposed to platinum group metals fluctuations . Long-term eight-to-ten year battery supply agreements carry enforced take-or-pay clauses, supporting EBITDA despite depressed cathode sales volumes , though this structure creates commercial tension with major automotive customers. 1 2 3 4 5

Financial Performance and Peer Benchmarking

Umicore's revenues excluding metals grew 3% to €3.56 billion in 2025, but demonstrated a negative 2.87% CAGR from 2021 to 2025 , underperforming against the recovery trajectory anticipated under the previous RISE 2030 strategy. Adjusted EBITDA improved 11% to €847 million in 2025, driven by €100 million in efficiency savings, expanding margins by 200 basis points to 24.0% . Return on capital employed improved to 15.7% in 2025 from 12.3% in 2024 , though this improvement was heavily flattered by a massive €1.6 billion asset impairment in Battery Materials during 2024 . Peer benchmarking reveals stark divergences: Johnson Matthey reported sales excluding precious metals of £2.56 billion for FY25/26, representing a 7% decline and a negative 12.87% three-year CAGR , while BASF maintained vastly larger revenues of €61.4 billion despite sustained volume destocking . Umicore carries exceptionally high inventory days of 141 days compared to Johnson Matthey's 62 days, reflecting refining bottlenecks, yet both companies achieve closely matched ROCE at 15.70% and 15.50% respectively . Capital expenditure intensity fell to 8.70% in 2025, down from 16.04% in 2024, following the postponement of the Canadian cathode plant . Leverage improved to 1.60x adjusted EBITDA at year-end 2025, well below the anticipated peak of 2.50x . 7

Industry Dynamics and Value Chain Positioning

The automotive catalyst sector is dominated by a tight triopoly of Umicore, BASF, and Johnson Matthey, with Umicore maintaining the number-one market position in light-duty gasoline catalysts . Battery cathode materials are plagued by persistent Asian overcapacity, with lithium iron phosphate (LFP) chemistries now supplying almost half of the global electric vehicle market and more than 98% of global LFP production concentrated in China . South Korean cathode manufacturers experienced severe profit compression throughout 2025, with LG Chem's advanced materials cathode business remaining loss-making due to low utilization . Umicore operates as a specialized materials and recycling actor across refining, precursor/cathode manufacturing, and precious metals recycling stages, bypassing capital-intensive raw mining . Customer Concentration and Contract Structures

The company's business segments have no single external customer accounting for 10% or more of total turnover, providing structural diversification that protects consolidated revenues from isolated client defaults . However, within the Battery Materials division, customer exposure is heavily concentrated in a few key automotive platforms, with primary European volumes tied to ACC, PowerCo, and Envision AESC . Battery Materials operates under eight-to-ten year supply agreements designed for mutual risk-sharing, protected by minimum volume commitments averaging 85% of total contracted capacity . Volume shortfalls triggered by slow EV ramp-ups are offset by take-or-pay compensations, with a significant portion of Battery Materials earnings improvement in 2025 stemming from take-or-pay penalty fees . Automotive Catalysts contracts exhibit high customer stickiness driven by expensive recalibration costs and joint R&D programs where Umicore and OEMs share downstream product cost savings . Supply contracts utilize standard metal pass-through formulas to transfer raw material price volatility directly to customers, isolating manufacturing margins but causing massive fluctuations in gross revenues .

Disruption Vectors and Paradigm Alignment

Low-cost lithium iron phosphate chemistries have rapidly captured almost half of the global passenger electric vehicle market, dominating mass-market vehicles and eroding long-term demand growth for nickel-rich NMC cathodes . Sodium-ion batteries represent a low-cost, lithium-free alternative with CATL scheduling commercial mass production in late 2026, though current chemistries exhibit approximately 30% lower energy density than standard LFP cells . Hydrometallurgical recycling is gaining popularity by facilitating high- purity recovery of all metals including lithium, accounting for 70% of global recycling capacity compared to 30% for pyrometallurgy . Umicore is developing High-Lithium Manganese-rich (HLM) cathode chemistries designed to match LFP costs while delivering higher energy density and helping Western automakers reduce reliance on Chinese LFP technology chains . Western regulatory frameworks reward sustainable, ethically sourced

localized materials under Critical Raw Materials rules, with proposed EU local content mandates shielding domestic manufacturers from imports . The company's strategic positioning aligns well with regionalization trends, though execution risk remains elevated given the magnitude of capital deployed and the structural oversupply dynamics in battery materials.

Battery Chemistry Disruptions and Substitution Threats

7.
Disruption force – LFP: Low-cost lithium iron phosphate chemistries have rapidly captured almost half of the global passenger electric vehicle market
8.
Mass-market dominance: LFP dominates mass-market electric vehicles and grid energy storage systems due to thermal safety and low cost

Nickel demand erosion: Rapid LFP adoption continues to erode the long-term demand growth thesis for nickel-rich NMC cathodes

Innovative entrant – sodium-ion: Sodium-ion batteries represent a low-cost, lithium-free alternative targeting entry-level mobility and stationary storage applications

Commercial scale timeline: CATL has resolved manufacturing hurdles and scheduled commercial mass production of sodium-ion batteries in late 2026
12.
Performance density gap: Current sodium-ion chemistries exhibit approximately 30% lower energy density than standard LFP cells
13.
Substitute – solid-state: Solid-state batteries represent a long-term technology shift offering superior safety and higher energy densities
14.
Industrialization hurdles: Mass production of solid-state platforms faces high manufacturing costs and remains several years away
