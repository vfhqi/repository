Key Bull vs. Bear Arguments

Bull Case: Operational Resilience and Cash Returns

  • Margin stability: Networks gross margins ~50% despite regional mix shifts (India rising, US falling) — proves structural resilience. [DNB Carnegie • 20 Apr]
  • Shareholder remuneration: FCF SEK 5.90bn Q1 2026 supporting 10% share buyback program. [Jefferies • 26 Jan, 17 Apr]
  • Market share gains: Ericsson taking share outside the US in flat RAN market; Nokia appears more willing to walk away from low-margin business. [Deutsche Bank • 21 Apr]

Bear Case: Macro Headwinds and Growth Gaps

  • Component cost inflation: DRAM and semiconductor pricing rising sharply — H2 2026 margins headwind that Ericsson may struggle to fully offset. [Jefferies • 20 Apr]
  • Meagre growth: EPS CAGR only ~5% 2025-2028 per Deutsche Bank — limited profit and cash flow growth potential. [Deutsche Bank • 21 Apr]
  • Enterprise uncertainty: CPaaS market low margins; network APIs slow takeoff; Enterprise division remains loss-making. [Jefferies • 20 Apr] [Expert Call • 21 Oct 25]
