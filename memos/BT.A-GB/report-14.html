§2 — Key Driver: Openreach FTTP Build & Monetisation

J-front verdict: Openreach build execution is on track at headline level; the genuine fulcrum variable is take-up rate inflecting above 40%, which is not yet evidenced and is the critical swing factor for EBITDA contribution [#J]

  • Driver definition: FTTP premises passed growth + take-up rate (activated / passed) → Openreach wholesale revenue per line uplift → Group EBITDA contribution from fibre [#A]
  • Magnitude required: Take-up rate rising from current c.35-38% toward 40-45% by FY27; implied incremental Openreach EBITDA contribution c.£300-500m on consensus estimates [#A]
  • Baseline: FY25 reported — c.16.4m FTTP premises passed; take-up rate c.36% (disclosed in BT FY25 results, April 2025); Openreach EBITDA broadly flat YoY at c.£3.4bn [#A]
  • Target state: c.22-25m premises passed by FY27 (BT guidance corridor); take-up 40-45%; Openreach EBITDA approaching £4.0bn+ [#A]

Bull — historical: Openreach's FTTC (copper-to-fibre-to-the-cabinet) rollout 2010-18 demonstrated the infrastructure can be built on time and cost once government/regulatory alignment achieved; unit capex per-premise passed for FTTP has fallen from c.£400 in 2020 to c.£280-300 by FY24 per management disclosures [#A]
- Sub: Unit capex decline of ~25-30% over four years is a credible precedent for continued learning-curve benefits, supporting bull case on capex efficiency [#A]

  • Bull — recent: FTTP premises-passed run-rate exceeded 10m exiting FY22, reached 16m+ by FY25 — on trajectory with guidance; quarterly build cadence of c.400-600k premises/quarter maintained consistently [#A]
  • Bull — mgmt: CEO Allison Kirkby (since Jan 2024) reinforced the build commitment in FY25 results call; no guidance reduction to FY27 target [#A]
  • Bull — competitive: BT/Openreach remains the only national-scale UK FTTP operator; altnets collectively have c.12-14m footprint but fragmented, often urban-heavy; Sky and VMO2 wholesale from Openreach, aligning their economics with BT's [#A]

  • Bull — regulatory: Ofcom's 2021-26 regulatory framework provides Openreach with a wholesale pricing corridor that supports return on FTTP capital — Ofcom Wholesale Fixed Telecoms Market Review (WFTMR) locked in inflation-linked price uplift mechanisms [#A]

  • Bear — historical drag: BT's original FTTC roll-out overshot capex budget in early years; FTTP is materially more capital-intensive per premise than FTTC, and take-up ramped more slowly than planned (BT originally guided 45%+ take-up by FY25; actual is c.36%) [#A]

  • ❌ Bear — take-up shortfall: Take-up of 36% vs 45%+ original guidance represents a c.9ppt miss — at 16m premises passed, that is c.1.4m fewer active FTTP lines than original case required, material to EBITDA contribution estimate [#J] [Cross-ref: §4 FCF]
  • Bear — altnet pressure: CityFibre (backed by Mubadala/Antin) is targeting 8m+ premises by 2025-26; Nexfibre (Liberty Global/Telefonica JV) targeting 5m; combined they overlap meaningfully with BT's target geography in mid-density suburbs [#A]
  • Bear — pricing pressure: Ofcom's WFTMR includes a "glide path" that may cap Openreach FTTP pricing below BT's internal case assumptions in 2026+ regulatory review; new review cycle begins 2026 [#A]

  • Bear — execution: FY26 build rate may slow as easiest-to-pass areas are completed; remaining c.6-9m premises to FY27 target skew toward harder rural geography [#A]

  • Quarterly trajectory: Take-up has been improving c.1-2ppt per half-year; to reach 40% by FY27 requires c.2-3ppt per year — requiring modest acceleration from current pace [#A]

  • Acceleration needed: From c.36% to 40% in 24M = c.4ppt; at current pace c.4ppt per 24M — on-the-line, with downside if altnet competition intensifies [#A]
  • Key dependency: FTTP take-up is partly driven by BT Consumer's own migration campaigns; if Consumer gross adds stall, Openreach wholesale volumes suffer [#A]

  • Downside scenario: If take-up plateaus at 38% rather than inflecting to 43%, Openreach EBITDA misses by c.£150-200m vs bull case — meaningful given group FCF sensitivity [#A]

  • Consensus alignment: Sell-side (public sources — Berenberg, Citi, Jefferies, Barclays — citing published notes) broadly assumes take-up reaches 40-42% by FY27; there is a minority view (noted in public press coverage of Jefferies commentary) that 38% is the realistic near-term ceiling given altnet competition [#A]

  • Estimate revision: No recent upward revision to Openreach EBITDA assumptions visible in public consensus data; modest downward drift from peak FY23 forecasts [#A]

  • Evidence gap: No public disclosure of take-up rate by geography (urban vs rural vs altnet-overlap areas); this would be the single most valuable data point for refining plausibility [#A]
