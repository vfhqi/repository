Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Geopolitical exposure in the Middle East and a short proved reserve life constitute the most significant risks to Shell's forward earnings predictability.The key risks to Shell'

Geopolitical and Asset Risks

1.
Severe Middle East exposure: Shell has c.20% of its total global capacity exposed to the Middle East, with 10% passing through the blocked Strait of Hormuz
2.
Major GTL outages: Projectile strikes on the Pearl GTL complex in Qatar have forced a major shut-in, with Train 2 requiring a year and $500 million to repair
3.
High commodity sensitivity: A $1 per barrel change in the Brent oil price impacts annual cash flows by $390 million, making Shell highly vulnerable to price declines
4.
Short proved reserve life: Shell's proved reserve life of 7.8 years is a key investor concern, representing a lack of long-term organic portfolio depth

Structural and Litigation Risks

5.
Shifting distribution mix: The rebalancing of distributions toward dividends and away from buybacks reduces total cash returned to shareholders by $390 million quarterly
6.
Climate litigation overhang: Although Shell won its appeal against the 45% carbon reduction court order in November 2024, climate litigation remains an active regulatory threat
7.
False friend margin signal: Rising refining margins look highly positive but can mask low utilization rates and high feedstock costs in the chemicals segment
8.
Primary early warning signal: Investors should monitor the quarterly volume of LNG liquefaction as a key early-warning signal for structural portfolio health
9.
Other side of the story: Shell's extensive global logistics and trading network can fully mitigate localized outages by routing alternative cargoes to capture high-margin European spot gas demand
