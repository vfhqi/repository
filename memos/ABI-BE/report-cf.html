EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The comprehensive analysis of Anheuser-Busch InBev reveals a company navigating a material strategic inflection characterized by orderly internal leadership transitions that maintain strategic continuity while expanding the EBITDA margin trajectory through structural premiumization and digital ecosystem monetization .

The financial profile demonstrates superior cash conversion enabling aggressive shareholder returns, with net debt to EBITDA falling to 2.87x in 2025 alongside a massive $6 billion share buyback program

and a 15% dividend increase . The historical earnings trajectory reveals a clear structural decoupling where revenue expansion completely separated from volume performance, as sustainable premiumization and price-mix growth of 4.5% in 1Q26 more than offset persistent volume contractions ranging from -0.8% to -3.7% across recent quarters .

1 4 exhibits a highly reliable sandbagger to honest-tracker archetype, consistently guiding to a conservative 4-8% EBITDA range while routinely delivering near the top end, evidenced by the 1Q26 EBITDA beat of approximately 2.7% above consensus and the $0.97 EPS print decisively outperforming the $0.89 consensus estimate .

The sell-side positioning remains overwhelmingly bullish with 92% Overweight ratings and 8% Equal-weight, European share price targets clustering tightly between €66.20 and €88.00, while US ADR targets ranging from $82.00 to $103.00 imply substantial upside potential from current trading levels . The fundamental peer-relative position establishes AB InBev as the clear momentum leader among the big six global brewers, driven by megabrands that grew 8.2% in 1Q26 and premium brands delivering low-twenties volume growth, while Bernstein models premiumization yielding +3.2% organic price-mix growth for 2026 .

The China off-trade distribution pivot represents the bottom-ranked key driver with low plausibility, as the legacy network struggles to capture at-home consumption while Chinese volumes declined 11.4% in 3Q25, severely underperforming the broader market due to negative channel mix . The deleveraging trajectory faces persistent headwinds from emerging-market currency depreciation, as a 13% euro appreciation inflated 40% of the debt stack while constant transactional currency depreciation structurally masked underlying mid-single-digit cash flow growth throughout the last three years .
