**Disclosed policy — group level:** Volati's annual report financial risk section states the group is exposed to currency risk through its international operations and that subsidiaries are expected to manage their own transactional FX risks; the group Treasury function sets policy guidelines but specific ratios and horizons are not publicly disclosed - **Translation hedging — explicitly not done:** Consistent with most European industrials of comparable size, Volati does not hedge translation exposure; the rationale is standard — translation hedging of equity is expensive and reverses over time; this is a deliberate policy choice, not a gap; the consequence is that the SEK/non-SEK translation effect flows through reported results unmitigated - **Transaction hedging — subsidiary-level, forward contracts:** The annual report notes the use of forward exchange contracts at subsidiary level to hedge committed or highly probable transaction cash flows; the specific businesses most likely to use these are Tornum (project-based export) and any subsidiary with significant cross-border supply chains - **Hedge horizon — not disclosed:** Volati does not publish a hedge horizon; RESEARCHER infers a typical horizon of 3-12 months for transaction hedging based on the nature of Tornum's project cycles and standard Nordic industrial practice; this is short relative to sector best practice (18-24 months for European industrials with material FX exposure) - **Hedge ratio — not disclosed:** Group hedge ratio against transactional exposure is not quantified; RESEARCHER estimates 30-50% of identified transaction exposure based on subsidiary-level implementation and the absence of a centralized treasury mandate; this estimate carries significant uncertainty - **Hedge instruments — forwards only (inferred):** The AR24 notes refer to forward exchange contracts; there is no mention of options, collars, or other derivative instruments; a forwards-only approach is cost-efficient but limits upside optionality in a favourable FX scenario (i.e. if SEK weakens after a forward is placed to sell EUR, Volati misses the upside) - **Hedge effectiveness L3Y:** Volati's IFRS notes (AR22-AR24) disclose realised and unrealised gains/losses on derivative instruments; from available disclosures: - FY2022: Net derivative gain — modest positive (SEK depreciation environment made EUR-sell forwards profitable for those covering EUR costs; those covering EUR revenues less relevant in a depreciating SEK) - FY2023: Net derivative position — small and within normal bounds - FY2024: Net derivative position — not material in disclosed notes - **Assessment:** The L3Y realised derivative P&L is small relative to the scale of non-SEK revenues, consistent with a partial/moderate hedge programme; there is no evidence of large hedge gains or losses that would indicate either aggressive hedging or significant hedge misalignment - **Hedge cost — not separately disclosed:** Forward contract costs (bid-ask spreads + rate differential from interest rate parity) are embedded in the effective exchange rate achieved; given modest hedge ratios, total annual hedge cost is likely sub-SEK 10-15m — immaterial at group level - **Treasury centralisation — limited:** Volati operates a decentralised conglomerate model; there is no evidence of a Group Treasury with sophisticated centralised hedging capability comparable to, say, a Sandvik or Atlas Copco; the CFO function likely coordinates policy but execution is subsidiary-level; this decentralisation is consistent with Volati's operating model but means hedging is less optimised than a centralised treasury - **Disclosure against "boring hedger" standard:** Applying the sector-general principle that "the boring hedger wins" (publishes quarterly hedge-position summaries, names horizon, reports realised effectiveness ratios), Volati scores poorly: no quarterly hedge summary, no named horizon, no realised effectiveness ratio disclosure; the FX risk note is qualitative-dominant with limited quantitative backing - **Counter-cyclical vs pro-cyclical assessment:** Cannot be determined from public disclosures; RESEARCHER notes that the absence of a centralised treasury mandate makes counter-cyclical hedging (buying protection when FX is cheap) less likely than reactive/pro-cyclical hedging (increasing cover when FX begins to move adversely) - **Peer hedging philosophy comparison (no D-BATCH-25 peers available; named-sector analogues used):** Comparable European diversified industrials with similar revenue-mix profiles (e.g. Indutrade, a Swedish diversified industrial comparable) disclose more on hedging; Indutrade's annual report includes geographic revenue breakdown by currency with clearer identification of hedged vs unhedged exposure; Lagercrantz Group (another Swedish serial acquirer comparable) similarly identifies its most significant currency pairs; Volati's disclosure lags both on transparency **Sceptical bullet:** The bearish read on hedging discipline is that "subsidiary-level hedging with group guidelines" is a description that can mean anything from a sophisticated, well-executed programme to a series of ad hoc forward contracts placed when subsidiaries feel exposed; the absence of any disclosed hedge ratio, duration, or effectiveness metric means Richard cannot verify that the programme is functioning as intended; in a stress scenario (e.g. rapid SEK appreciation), the fragility of a decentralised hedging programme — where different subsidiaries may have different hedge ratios, different counterparty banks, and different horizons — would become apparent precisely when centralised coordination is most needed --- ## §6 — Quantified Sensitivity Analysis **J-front verdict:** A 10% SEK appreciation against the full non-SEK basket reduces Volati's reported EBITDA by an estimated SEK 60-80m (~8-10% of group EBITDA) via translation and a further SEK 15-25m via unhedged transaction exposure at Tornum — total ~SEK 75-105m impact, or ~0.35-0.50 SEK/share EPS impact; this is material at current earnings levels ### Static Sensitivity Table — Translation + Transaction Combined **Assumptions:** -
