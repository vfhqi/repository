  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA beats in 3 of 4 periods, average beat magnitude ~+3%; the guidance range is typically wide (NOK 200-300m spread), which provides significant cover against outright misses [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: Q1 2025 EBITDA near-miss (-1.8%) disrupts what was a clean beat streak; too early to call a pattern shift but warrants monitoring [#J]

  • Beat consistency: Revenue 4 for 4 beats; EBITDA 3 for 4 beats — above the OFSV historical base rate of approximately 55-60% EBITDA beat frequency in rising-rate environments [#A]

  • Pattern shift: The Q1 2025 EBITDA near-miss, combined with guidance being set closer to the midpoint (narrower range) as the business scales, suggests the sandbagging cushion may be structurally narrowing — larger fleet means higher absolute cost base to predict [#J]
