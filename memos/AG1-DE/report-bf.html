EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Auto1 Group SE operates Europe's leading vertically integrated digital platform for used car transactions, connecting consumer-to-business sourcing through 725 drop-off branches directly with wholesale dealer networks serving over 60,000 partners and a direct-to-consumer retail brand Autohero. The platform transacted 842,271 units in FY25, capturing a 3.1% European market share from a €600-700 billion addressable pool of 27.5 million annual transactions , positioning the company as the dominant digital consolidator in a highly fragmented market where over 200,000 dealerships control less than 6% concentration among the top 20 players . Business Model and Revenue Architecture Auto1 operates a dual-segment model dividing Merchant wholesale (EUR 1.88 billion in Q1 2026) and Retail consumer operations (EUR 556.5 million) , with approximately 90% of inventory sourced directly from private consumers through the proprietary wirkaufendeinauto.de network. The wholesale engine generates stable cash ﬂows funding the high-margin retail expansion of Autohero, while captive ﬁnancing scaled to EUR 941 million in assets achieving 39% consumer attach rates , unlocking ancillary monetization streams that structurally expand gross proﬁt per unit. The platform demonstrates powerful network effects where proprietary algorithms automatically price 90% of incoming inventory , enabling precision pricing improvements as transaction density increases across Continental Europe's nine active markets.

Financial Performance and Capital Eﬃciency Auto1 delivered EUR 8.17 billion in FY25 revenue, representing 30.3% year-over-year growth , while adjusted EBITDA expanded 80.8% to EUR 197.5 million at a 2.4% margin , demonstrating accelerating operating leverage as ﬁxed costs amortize over rising volumes. Unit economics remain robust with Autohero Retail GPU of EUR 2,555 and Merchant GPU of EUR 957 in Q1 2026 , outperforming Aramis Group's B2C GPU of EUR 2,332 while maintaining superior capital velocity through a negative cash conversion cycle of 2.5 days versus Carvana's positive 29.3 days and CarMax's 263.6 days . The balance sheet exhibits exceptional structural resilience with zero corporate debt and EUR 652 million in cash , while inventory and captive ﬁnance receivables are funded via non-recourse ABS facilities totaling EUR 1.6 billion , completely isolating corporate credit risk from securitization market volatility.

Industry Structure and Consolidation Dynamics The European used car market represents a €600-700 billion revenue pool with 27.5 million annual transactions , exhibiting extreme fragmentation where over 200,000 dealers control less than 6% top-20 concentration , creating massive consolidation runway for scaled digital platforms. Online transaction penetration remains in an extremely early developmental phase , while Continental European digital retail adoption lags the mature UK benchmark but exhibits faster catch-up growth , underpinning secular tailwinds for Auto1's 10% long-term market share target. The primary proﬁt pool accrues at consumer vehicle acquisition (C2B) yielding approximately EUR 1,000 gross margin per unit , structurally favoring Auto1's dominant branch sourcing network over traditional B2B remarketing channels that deliver materially lower margins . 11

Value Chain Position and Vertical Integration Auto1 controls the highest-margin stages of the used car value chain through approximately 90% consumerdirect sourcing via 725 physical drop-off locations , which competitors acknowledge creates an unreplicable moat requiring up to EUR 500 million in working capital to replicate at 1% market share . Vertical integration extends to 12 in-house reconditioning centers processing 248,000 units annually with over 95% internal refurbishment , delivering cost savings of EUR 300-400 per car versus external mechanics while proprietary glass delivery trucks boost retail Net Promoter Scores to 70 . The captive ﬁnance infrastructure provides structural margin expansion , where consumer ﬁnancing contributes EUR 400 in incremental GPU targeting EUR 800-1,000 long-term , funded through off-balance-sheet ABS facilities achieving 87 basis point blended spreads .

Customer Dynamics and Monetization Levers The Merchant segment served 54,371 partner dealers in FY25, growing active buyers 23% year-over-year in Q1 2026 , though average cars purchased per merchant declined to 6.0 units as smaller traders onboarded , indicating substantial wallet share expansion potential . Autohero retail sustains Net Promoter Scores of approximately 70 with low single-digit return rates , while average selling prices of EUR 17,324 align perfectly with mainstream budgets where 35% of German buyers prefer EUR 15,000-30,000 . Consumer ﬁnancing attach rates reached 39% in FY25, ﬂuctuating 40-45% in Germany and Austria , while merchant ﬁnance adoption surged 47% year-over-year to 4,400 dealers , demonstrating rapid scaling of high-return ancillary products. 26 28

Supplier Power and Sourcing Vulnerabilities Auto1 secures approximately 90% of supply from fragmented private consumers accepting lower prices for immediate liquidity , creating asymmetric bargaining power that proprietary AI valuations automatically price at 90% automation rates , but faces emerging headwinds from OEM inventory retention strategies where manufacturers lease vehicles through second and third cycles , restricting independent sourcing of desirable 1-to- 3-year-old stock.
