Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Galderma presents a highly attractive, pure-play dermatology growth story with robust top-line momentum, clear margin expansion, and a rapidly deleveraging balance sheet.
1.
Resilient dermatology model: The business leverages an integrated platform spanning injectable aesthetics, dermatological skincare, and therapeutic dermatology to target a global dermatology market valued at approximately $87B in 2023. This synergistic structure allows the company to cross-sell products across medical and consumer-driven channels while maintaining high brand loyalty
2.
Compelling investment case: The company provides unique pure-play exposure to high-growth skin health markets, enabling a projected revenue CAGR of 17% and an EPS CAGR exceeding 30% from FY2025 to FY2028. This double-digit expansion is forecast to be more than double that of most large-cap pharmaceutical and consumer peers
3.
Outstanding recent results: Galderma surpassed the $5B threshold for the first time in FY2025, delivering record net sales of $5,207M representing an attractive 17.70% constant currency growth rate. This top-line momentum reflects broad-based growth across all major geographies and product categories
4.
Severe competitor headwinds: The single largest threat to the forward case is intense commercial and pipeline competition from established immunological giants in the systemic biologics space. Players like AbbVie and Sanofi command massive resources and possess deeply entrenched standard-of-the-art products in atopic dermatitis
5.
Positive first-look verdict: Deeper research is strongly warranted as the company successfully deleverages below 2.0x net debt to EBITDA and continues to outperform its self-care and medical aesthetics peers. The combination of premium pricing power, a highly diversified portfolio, and significant clinical launch momentum creates an exceptional risk-reward profile
6.
Valuation debate: "Galderma currently trades at 30x 2026 EV/EBITDA; adjusting for Nemluvio, Galderma still trades at 27x EV/EBITDA, a large premium over global self-care peers and skincare peers. Valuation remains a key point of debate with investors, with bulls arguing that valuation remains reasonable in the context of growth (14% sales growth / 23% EBITDA growth 2026-2029 including Nemluvio), and more cautious investors noting that a large share of the growth and margin expansion originate from a pharma asset and that this should therefore be reflected in the multiple." – Morgan Stanley, 27 Apr

Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. Galderma operates a highly differentiated, consumer-and-clinical platform that commands market-leading positions and superior pricing power across skin health channels.
1.
Leading aesthetics portfolio: The Injectable Aesthetics segment represented 49% of sales in FY2025 and comprises a premier neuromodulator portfolio alongside premium dermal fillers and biostimulators. This segment forms the largest revenue contributor for Galderma, serving as a highly effective commercial engine across approximately 90 countries. Dysport, Alluzience, and the newly launched Relfydess comprise a highly unique triple-threat offering in neuromodulation
2.
Innovative neuromodulator technologies: Relfydess, QM-1114, is the first and only ready-to-use liquid botulinum toxin A developed using PEARL Technology, demonstrating a highly competitive one-day onset and six-month duration of action. This pre-reconstituted liquid format significantly reduces clinical reconstitution errors and streamlines med spa operations
3.
Regenerative biostimulator leadership: Sculptra is the world's first proven poly-L-lactic acid regenerative biostimulator, designed to address skin quality and facial volume loss by stimulating natural collagen production. This therapeutic profile has positioned Sculptra as a primary treatment for GLP-1 weight loss-induced facial volume deflation
4.
Trusted consumer skincare: The Dermatological Skincare segment contributes 28% of sales, anchored by the Cetaphil brand for sensitive skin and Alastin, a premium physician-dispensed clinical line. Cetaphil remains the number-one dermatological recommended brand in major growth markets like India, driving massive consumer volume
5.
High-growth prescription biologics: The Therapeutic Dermatology segment, accounting for 23% of sales, features acne and rosacea treatments alongside Nemluvio, a novel biologic targeting interleukin-31 receptor alpha. Nemluvio is a first-in-class monthly injection designed specifically to treat prurigo nodularis and atopic dermatitis
6.
Revenue mechanics and industry value chain:
7.
Insulated private-pay economics: A substantial portion of Galderma's portfolio comprises private-pay consumer and aesthetic products, shielding the company from government reimbursement risks and insurance coverage disputes. This cash-pay dynamic allows the company to maintain highly stable pricing and avoid typical pharmaceutical price erosion
8.
Direct professional access: The company sits in a profit-favored position in the dermatology value chain, bypasses broad primary care networks, and sells directly to a highly concentrated channel of specialized dermatologists. This specialty-channel density reinforces long-term physician relationships and maximizes commercial sales force efficiency
9.
Material customer concentration: The US market is Galderma's only country contributing over 10% of revenue, and the company relies on a single US wholesale distributor for 22% of its total 2025 net sales
10.
Strong professional loyalty: The company secures durable competitive advantages through its Galderma Aesthetic Injector Network, which builds high physician switching costs through advanced clinical education. This institutional training model locks in practitioners and creates durable repeating sales cycles for premium fillers
11.
Industry structure and competitor landscape:
12.
Consolidated upstream structure: The medical aesthetics industry is highly concentrated, with Galderma and AbbVie acting as the dominant players, creating significant barriers to entry for smaller competitors
13.
Expanding biostimulator demand: The biostimulator market is experiencing rapid expansion, with average monthly clinic volumes projected to grow at an 11% CAGR from 2024 to 2030, heavily favoring Sculptra
14.
Intensifying price sensitivity: Aesthetics med spas are facing price competition and commoditization, as younger, cost-conscious patients drive demand for entry-level procedures and shop around for discounts
15.
Other side of the story: Rapid penetration of low-cost Korean and Japanese imports in the dermal filler and skin booster categories could disrupt established pricing structures and erode Galderma's premium market share

Section 5 – The possible investment case 5 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible investment case is highly compelling as the rapid roll-out of high-margin biological and liquid toxin platforms triggers an outstanding earnings inflection.
1.
Clinical competitive failure risk: The Nemluvio driver would fail if Eli Lilly's Ebglyss or entrenched standard-of-care Dupixent successfully block formulary access, or if insurance coverage proves prohibitive
2.
Regulatory failure mechanism: The Relfydess driver would fail if US FDA resubmission is delayed beyond H2 2026, or if clinicians refuse to switch from dominant Botox accounts
3.
Bull case dynamics: The investment case is robust because key drivers are independent and do not require simultaneous clinical or commercial success to deliver the bull case
4.
Other side of the story: A prolonged economic slowdown in discretionary consumer spending could cause patients to delay aesthetic treatments, dragging down Injectable Aesthetics growth despite strong clinical launches

Section 6 – Valuation 3 findings

Section summary
  1. Valuation multiples appear highly demanding on near-term metrics but remain justifiable when framed against the company's superior, low-risk earnings compound.
1.
Premium trading multiple: Galderma trades at approximately 30x 2026 EV/EBITDA (or 27x adjusting for Nemluvio), representing a steep valuation premium over traditional European large-cap pharma and skincare peers
2.
Defensible premium valuation: The valuation premium is justified by a Core EPS CAGR exceeding 30% from FY2025 to FY2028, more than double its peers

Section 7 – Key risks 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Competitive and trade risks are structurally manageable but warrant active tracking as next-generation assets and geopolitical trade policies evolve.
1.
Immunology competitive intensity: Nemluvio operates in highly crowded atopic dermatitis space facing potential disruption from Pfizer and Apogee developing long-acting IL-13 antibodies with quarterly or bi-annual dosing potential
2.
Aesthetics pipeline disputes: Ipsen's next-generation toxin corabotase (LANTIC Phase 2 data) represents a possible long-term competitive headwind to Relfydess, amid ongoing commercial partnership disputes
3.
Manageable tariff risk: Guidance factors in a 15% US tariff on imported Restylane and Sculptra, while Dysport (produced in UK) and Cetaphil (Canada) remain completely exempt. Relfydess manufacturing transfer to US site will fully mitigate long-term tariff exposure
4.
Clinical failure modes: The most damaging failure mode is a major clinical or regulatory setback for Nemluvio's ongoing development in chronic pruritus or systemic sclerosis
5.
Early-warning indicators: Critical tracking metric is paid new patient starts (NBRx) for Nemluvio in atopic dermatitis monthly. Competitive disruption risk in AD is significantly overstated because newly released Phase II assets are several years away from commercial market
