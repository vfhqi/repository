Section 3 – What the company does 19 findings

Section summary
  1. Operating through a highly decentralized structure, AQ Group secures a highly sticky position in industrial value chains by delivering custom component engineering to market-leading blue-chip customers.

Segment and Product Breakdown

1.

2.
Component product depth: Inside Component, the company offers sheet metal processing, plastic injection molding, precision stamping, custom wiring systems, and high-complexity inductive components like dry-type transformers
3.
System segment execution: The System segment covers Electric Cabinets and System Products, generating SEK 315m of external sales in Q1 2026, which represents about 13.36% of group sales
4.
System product integration: The System segment specializes in assembling complete machines, power grids, and automation solutions, acting as an outsourced integrator for demanding industrial applications
5.
Inductive component growth: Inductive components have surged from 6.00% of sales in 2018 to accounting for over one-fifth of group revenue as of 2024, expanding further with the completed mdexx acquisition in 2025
6.
Custom transformer design: AQ's Trafotek division in Finland maintains several dedicated design offices where doctors and advanced engineers customize high-complexity magnetic and medium-voltage transformer solutions

Position in the Value Chain and Customer Dynamics

7.
High engineering integration: AQ sits in a profit-favored position early in the product life cycle, helping blue-chip OEMs with design, prototyping, and optimal industrialization of complex capital goods
8.
No proprietary intellectual property: AQ Group owns virtually no patents of its own, relying entirely on manufacturing custom products according to customer specifications, which eliminates product obsolescence risk
9.
Diverse customer base: While the ten largest customers account for just over half of net sales, AQ's overall concentration is well managed across multiple niches like commercial vehicles, power, and medtech
10.
Blue-chip customer profile: Key relationships are maintained with global industrial market leaders, including ABB, Alstom, Hitachi, Scania, Epiroc, and Volvo, creating highly sticky, multi-decade partnerships
11.
Early-stage R&D involvement: R&D engagement starts during the prototype design phase, enabling AQ to embed its engineering teams directly into the customer's product development cycle
12.
Low end-use market risk: The company operates as a pure-play component manufacturer, meaning it acts as an outsourced production unit and is fully shielded from direct consumer-market volatility

Industry Structure and Competitive Advantage

13.
Durable switching cost advantage: Competitive advantage is built on high switching costs and exceptional delivery reliability, as custom wiring harnesses and inductive designs cannot easily be moved to rival factories without costly qualification delays
14.
Local-for-local manufacturing: The company's geographic dispersion across 17 countries allows it to meet OEM demands for regionalized, nearshored supply chains, bypassing costly intermediaries like logistics firms
15.
Consolidating market structure: The European contract manufacturing market is highly fragmented but consolidating, with AQ Group positioned as one of the larger players alongside Nordic peers Scanfil, Inission, and Note
16.
Barriers to market entry: High capital requirements and stringent certification audits from blue-chip industrial customers prevent low-cost, uncertified competitors from entering the high-demand segment
17.
Decentralized company culture: The company's unique culture centers around rapid local decision-making, where factory managers are given full autonomy to run their units as independent entrepreneurs
18.
Other side of the story: A major weakness is that industrial OEMs hold immense bargaining power, routinely demanding shared productivity gains that prevent AQ from expanding its underlying gross operating margins over time
