DEFINITION **What the thematic IS:** Persistent structural inflation in European services describes the sustained elevation of wage costs across service-sector industries — retail, hospitality, healthcare, logistics, outsourcing, facilities management, and professional services — driven by minimum wage legislation, tight labour markets, and multi-year collective bargaining settlements. Unlike goods inflation (which has largely mean-reverted), services inflation in the Eurozone and UK has remained sticky at 4-5% through 2024-2025, and forward wage agreements suggest it persists into 2026-2027. **Primary transmission mechanism:** Labour as a percentage of revenue (labour intensity) determines exposure. For every 1% of wage cost growth above revenue growth, EBIT margin compresses by approximately the labour cost ratio multiplied by the gap. A company with 50% labour costs and 5% wage growth but only 3% revenue growth faces ~100bps of annual EBIT margin headwind. This compounds across multi-year contract cycles. **Time horizon:** Multi-year thematic, with peak EPS divergence expected 2025-2027 as pre-inflation contracts roll off. Not a 6-month trade. **Confidence in persistence:** **High.** EU minimum wage directive enforcement, UK National Living Wage statutory trajectory, and locked-in collective bargaining settlements make rapid normalisation structurally implausible before 2028. --- ## 2. AT-RISK PROBABLE SETUPS ### Typical At-Risk Stock Profile The prototypical at-risk stock has: **flat or growing consensus EPS estimates that embed management guidance on cost mitigation** without modelling the full roll-through of multi-year wage settlements; a **forward P/E that looks "cheap" on stated earnings** (8-12x) but embeds inflated EBIT margins that are structurally unsustainable; and **a dividend yield that appears attractive** until the payout ratio is stress-tested against normalised margins. The dangerous variant is the company where management has communicated a "cost efficiency programme" that offsets 50-60% of the wage headwind in the model — but the programme's deliverability is uncertain or back-end loaded. In these cases, the stock is priced for successful execution; any slippage triggers an outsized de-rating. ### Trigger Events (Crystallisation) - **H1 results showing EBIT margin below consensus by 50bps+** — the market interprets this as temporary; the researcher knows it is structural; the second miss 6 months later triggers the full re-rating - **Contract renewal at flat or below-inflation terms** — often disclosed quietly in notes or appendices; signals customer pricing leverage is dominant - **CEO or CFO resignation or "strategic review" announcement** — at-risk companies under margin pressure often face management turnover; this is both signal and amplifier - **Debt covenant waiver or amendment request** — the most direct signal that leverage + earnings compression has reached the danger zone; equity de-rating is abrupt - **Public sector funding settlement below inflation** — UK Spending Review or NHS tariff updates that embed sub-inflation uplifts lock in the negative wedge for 2-3 years; these are scheduled and predictable triggers ### Temporary vs Structural At-Risk **Temporarily at-risk** companies share three features: they have a clear contract repricing event within 12-18 months that will restore margin; they have the competitive position to execute that repricing (pricing power, switching costs); and their balance sheet can absorb the 1-2 year margin trough without covenant breach or dividend cut. These are not shorts — they are potential entries after the temporary trough is priced. **Structurally at-risk** companies lack all three: repricing is not available (government-funded), competitive position is weak (commoditised), and balance sheet is stressed. The earnings deterioration is multi-year and non-recoverable without a fundamental model change. These are active avoidance or short candidates. **The key diagnostic question:** Can the company raise the price it charges to the customer above the rate wages are rising? If yes, temporarily at-risk or not at-risk. If no, structurally at-risk. ### Setup Mapping At-risk stocks map to the **earnings deterioration** setup: a series of consensus EPS cuts as wage costs roll through; each cut brings the stock to "look cheap" on new consensus before the next cut arrives. The multiple compression is secondary — the primary mechanism is EPS decline. The most dangerous setup is the **value trap**: a low P/E that reflects a structurally impaired earnings base, defended dividend, and management that is one restructuring announcement away from confirming the structural thesis. **Historical parallel:** The 2014-2016 period in UK outsourcing (Capita, Mitie, G4S, Interserve) is the closest analog. All four entered the period appearing cheap on stated earnings. All experienced sequential earnings cuts over 2-4 years as public-sector contract pricing failed to absorb cost inflation and productivity assumptions proved optimistic. Interserve was ultimately liquidated. The lesson: once the structural pricing gap is established, the earnings deterioration is not linear — it accelerates as management loses credibility and customers begin re-tendering. --- ## SUPPLEMENTARY: ATTRIBUTE MATCHABILITY GUIDE FOR APM SCORING To score a stock A-F against this thematic, the APM should answer five binary questions: **Beneficiary scoring:** 1.
