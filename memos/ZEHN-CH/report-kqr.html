EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 22 findings

Strategic transformation and portfolio mix

1.
Structural portfolio pivot: Zehnder has systematically transformed from a legacy radiator manufacturer into a high-efficiency indoor ventilation platform, with the ventilation segment now generating approximately 66% of group sales (EUR 501.7 million in FY25) and management positioning the firm as an integrated indoor climate solutions provider. This ventilation focus delivers superior profitability versus local peers, with Zehnder's ventilation EBIT margin reaching 12.50% in FY25 compared to Systemair's 9.40%, Lindab's approximately 9.00%, and Munters' EBITDA margin of 15.70% (though Munters benefits from data center exposure)
2.
Extreme segment margin divergence: The ventilation platform accounts for roughly 96% of group EBIT despite representing only two-thirds of sales, while the mature radiator division contributed just EUR 2.4 million in adjusted EBIT during FY25, highlighting a severe profitability imbalance across the portfolio. The Gränichen plant closure–responding to radiator volume declines exceeding 30% since pre-pandemic periods and triggering EUR 35.9 million in FY24 restructuring charges–has lowered the radiator segment's break-even point and restored segment EBIT margin to 0.90% in FY25 from negative 8.20% in FY24
3.
Strategic M&A execution: The EUR 86 million acquisition of Spanish ventilation leader Siber in July 2024 provides mid-price ventilation solutions tailored for Southern European multi-family apartments and supported reported ventilation sales growth by 5% in FY25, expanding Zehnder's addressable market beyond premium single-family homes. Concurrent portfolio pruning included the divestment of the loss-making European Climate Ceiling Solutions division in mid-2024, removing EUR 23 million in low-margin commercial office sales through a cash-neutral transaction that triggered a EUR 10 million one-off loss
4.
Revenue predictability expansion: Service and maintenance contracts are expanding to reduce cyclical new-build housing dependency, with after-sales service representing approximately 15% of ventilation segment sales in FY24 targeting over 1 million European installed units, while the industrial Clean Air Solutions division operates a product-as-a-service subscription model delivering a 99% recurring revenue rate across more than 3,000 active service contracts

Financial recovery and peer-benchmarked returns

5.
Unstable top-line recovery: Group revenues fell from EUR 812.5 million in FY22 to EUR 705.8 million in FY24 before recovering to EUR 760.7 million in FY25, driven by 7.80% growth (7% organic plus Siber integration), though the long-term 2017-2022 CAGR of 6.90% was disrupted by restructuring and market cycles. Segment performance diverged sharply, with ventilation sales growing 18% to EUR 501.7 million (15% organically, supported by strict UK building standards) while radiator sales fell 8% to EUR 259 million, suffering from weak European renovation activity and radiator volumes declining approximately 30% versus pre-pandemic baselines
6.
Margin inflection and operating leverage: Group adjusted EBIT margin rebounded to 8.60% in FY25, up from 7.10% in FY24 and 8.30% in FY23, with adjusted EBIT rising 30.20% year-over-year to EUR 65.2 million, benefiting from structural radiator capacity cuts after margins bottomed at 2.00% in FY24 (excluding restructuring charges). Reported EBIT recovered to EUR 63.4 million in FY25 from a depressed EUR 14.1 million in FY24, when EUR 35.9 million in restructuring provisions (primarily from closing Swiss radiator assembly) severely impacted profitability, with residual EUR 1.8 million charges in FY25 from selective German and Chinese radiator adjustments
7.
Exceptional capital return rebound: Return on capital employed surged to 22.10% in FY25 from 15.40% in FY24, driven by capital employed dropping to EUR 234.7 million through radiator plant divestments, substantially outperforming the historical ROCE baseline of 22.60% in FY21, 18.90% in FY22, and 17.90% in FY23. This ROCE performance positions Zehnder favorably against local capital goods peers, with Systemair delivering an adjusted operating margin of 9.40% and Lindab achieving approximately 9.00% EBIT margin, while Munters' 15.70% EBITDA margin reflects its advantaged data center thermal management exposure
8.
Balance sheet restoration: Zehnder returned to a net cash position of EUR 32.8 million at the end of FY25 after slipping into EUR 11.8 million net debt in FY24 due to Siber acquisition cash outflows, recovering from stronger historical net cash positions of EUR 70.8 million in FY23, EUR 45.4 million in FY22, and EUR 123.2 million in FY21. Cash flow from operating activities rose to EUR 80 million in FY25 from EUR 60.6 million in FY24, with operating cash conversion reaching 91.60% of EBITDA (up from 78.70%) and unlevered free cash flow rebounding to EUR 64 million from negative EUR 57 million in FY24
