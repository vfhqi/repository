EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 9 findings

Material Internal and External Changes

Galderma executed an orderly CFO transition in May 2026 as Luigi La Corte succeeded Thomas Dittrich, bringing 30 years of financial leadership experience including a proven M&A track record at Recordati. The transition featured a one-month overlap period to ensure continuity, while Dittrich had successfully guided Galderma through its March 2024 IPO and subsequent refinancing before joining Amgen as EVP and CFO. Board composition was substantially refreshed at the April 2026 AGM as EQT representatives departed following the consortium's full divestment, replaced by Harry Kirsch (CFO of Novartis since 2013) and two L'Oréal representatives.

The full EQT-led consortium exit via a record-breaking CHF 4.89 billion block trade in March 2026 transitioned Galderma to an 80% free float structure, representing the largest fully monetized capital gain from a single fund in private equity history. L'Oréal doubled its strategic equity stake to 20% through coordinated off-market block trades in February 2026, securing board representation through Delphine Viguier-Hovasse and Samuel du Retail to deepen scientific partnerships focused on co-developing advanced skin-diagnostic tools.

Externally, global non-invasive medical aesthetics expanded from $36 billion in 2025 toward a projected $64 billion by 2033, with significant underserved demand emerging among 1.2 billion perimenopausal women globally by 2030 and incremental facial volume restoration demand from rising GLP-1 weight-loss drug adoption. Competitive intensity remains elevated but manageable, as AbbVie received a Complete Response Letter delaying its TrenibotE toxin launch while Ipsen's next-generation long-acting neurotoxin Corabotase entered Phase III trials with Phase II LANTIC composite response rates of 66% at Week 4, below Relfydess' historical 83%.

Manufacturing and regulatory positioning improved substantially, with over $650 million committed to US manufacturing investments through 2030, successful Relfydess production capacity expansion in Uppsala Sweden, and strategic tariff mitigation through USMCA-compliant Canadian production that exempts Cetaphil and mature therapeutics while Dysport remains completely exempt due to UK-based manufacturing, with a 180-day tariff exemption buffering immediate trade policy risks.
