§1 — General Description, Financials, Peer Comparison

Saipem is an Italian EPCI (Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Installation) contractor serving the global oil and gas industry with a primary focus on complex offshore projects. Founded in 1956 as an ENI subsidiary and listed on Borsa Italiana, it operates across offshore engineering and construction, onshore engineering and construction, and offshore drilling — executing some of the largest subsea pipeline and platform installation programmes in the world.

§2 — Company Details: Revenue Model, Segments, Delivery, Internal Change

  • J-front verdict: Saipem's three-segment structure concentrates 65% of backlog in Asset Based Services (offshore EPCI), with a high-conviction repositioning underway: exiting low-margin lump-sum onshore EPC, growing offshore wind and NOC-driven deepwater, and completing divestiture of Onshore Drilling. Contract mix is improving but execution risk on fixed-price offshore contracts remains the primary earnings volatility driver. [#J]

Revenue Segments (restructured 2024)

  • Asset Based Services (ABS): Offshore Engineering and Construction (deepwater pipelines, subsea structures, platform installation) plus Offshore Wind installation; backlog €22.5bn (66% of total); the company's highest-margin and fastest-growing segment. [#I]

  • Flagship projects: Bonga North (Nigeria, SNEPCO), GranMorgu (Suriname, TotalEnergies, $1.9bn), Qatar North Field Compression ($4bn, QatarEnergy LNG), Marjan field offshore Saudi Arabia ($2bn, Aramco) — signed Sep-Dec 2024. [#I]

  • Energy Carriers: Onshore Engineering and Construction (pipelines, gas infrastructure), Sustainable Infrastructure and Robotics / Industrialized Solutions; backlog €10.3bn (30% of total). [#I]

  • Qatar NFS compression offshore platform work sits here alongside onshore pipeline and processing plant EPC — segment is lower-margin and carries legacy lump-sum exposure. [#A]

  • Offshore Drilling: Standalone segment created 2024; backlog €1.3bn (4% of total); 7 semi-submersibles and drillships operating in deepwater; contract durations typically 1-3 years day-rate. [#I]

  • Onshore Drilling (DRON) transfer completed 30-Jun-2024: divested, recorded only €13m revenue and small operating loss in 2024. [#I]

Contract Types and Risk Profile

  • Lump-sum vs cost-plus: Offshore EPCI contracts are predominantly lump-sum or fixed-price — margin upside is capped but downside unlimited on execution failures (the root cause of 2021-2022 crisis); management's "value over volume" doctrine post-2022 involves refusing low-margin tenders and adding contractual protections. [#A]
  • Onshore EPC historically dominated by lump-sum EPC; management has explicitly stated intent to shift Energy Carriers segment toward reimbursable / cost-plus to reduce risk. [#I]
  • Offshore Drilling: pure day-rate contracts; more predictable earnings but exposed to utilisation and day-rate cycles. [#I]
