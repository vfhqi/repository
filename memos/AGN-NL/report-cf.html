Materiality of Change Aegon is executing a transformational corporate headquarters relocation to the United States, securing executive continuity through CEO Lard Friese's mandate extension to 2030 while eliminating legacy dual-class share voting to adopt standard US public market practices . The company will relocate its legal seat to Delaware by early 2028 and rename itself Transamerica Inc. , representing a material structural pivot from its Dutch heritage to a US-centric operating model . The GBP 2.0 billion divestment of Aegon UK to Standard Life at 14.2x post-tax operating profit completes the strategic simplification of the group's footprint, redirecting capital toward higher-return US middle-market life insurance and retirement solutions. The reinsurance of 30% of Secondary Guarantee Universal Life face value accelerates balance sheet de-risking , reducing capital trapped in legacy closed books. The convergence of governance modernization, geographic realignment, and capital structure simplification constitutes a highly material change trajectory that fundamentally redefines Aegon's strategic positioning and peer comparability framework.
