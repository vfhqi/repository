Demographic Shifts: The Gen Z Catalyst

The Gym Group is experiencing substantial membership growth driven by changing demographic proﬁles,
particularly the rapid adoption of ﬁtness routines by Generation Z. [1] Gen Z now accounts for 44% of the
company's average membership base, despite representing only 19% of the broader UK population. [2] According
to the company's Gen Z Fitness Pulse Report, 73% of this cohort exercise at least twice a week, and 44% rank
ﬁtness as their ﬁrst or second discretionary spending priority. [3] This younger, image-conscious demographic
views gym membership as a form of social currency and an essential part of their lifestyle and identity, rather
than a discretionary expense. [4] Furthermore, broader consumer trends such as the "Fit not Thin" movement and
an increased focus on mental health are expanding the addressable market. [5] The rising adoption of GLP-1
weight loss medications is also contributing to gym demand, as over 2 million users in the UK seek to preserve
muscle mass through strength training. [6]

Utilities and Energy Hedging Strategies

Energy is a substantial site-level cost, exposing the company to volatility in wholesale utility markets. To manage

electricity and natural gas directly to cover its expected operational usage. [50] By taking physical delivery without
ﬁnancial settlement prior to maturity, the company utilizes the "own use" exemption under IFRS 9, recognizing
energy costs as consumed. [51]
