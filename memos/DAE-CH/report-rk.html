Section 3 – What the company does 17 findings

Section summary
  1. Daetwyler holds high-barrier, profit-favored positions in the global supply chains for injectable drug packaging and aluminum food capsules.

Segment capabilities and customer dynamics

1.
Injectable primary packaging: Division Healthcare manufactures plungers, stoppers, and caps for injectable drug containers. These components are system-critical to preserve the safety of drugs
2.
Industrial sealing applications: Division Industrial produces custom seals and composite parts for automotive and industrial markets. These customized components represent a low cost contribution to customer applications
3.
Innovative food packaging: The Food & Beverage unit supplies high-tech aluminum coffee capsules with paper or liquid silicone rubber seals. Regulatory shifts from plastic to aluminum accelerate capsule demand
4.
Pure-play strategic advantage: Healthcare's pure-play strategy ensures open access to all drug manufacturers and system suppliers. This positioning avoids conflicts of interest with integrated device competitors
5.
Low customer concentration: The company serves global innovation leaders and has low customer concentration risk. Products are integrated into millions of syringes and coffee capsules worldwide
6.
Regional sales diversification: Geographic revenue mix is highly balanced. Rest of Europe represents CHF 344.90M, Switzerland CHF 235.40M, and Americas CHF 291.10M in 2025

Competitive moats and value chain positioning

7.
High regulatory barriers: Stricter sterile manufacturing rules like EU-GMP Annex 1 raise regulatory barriers. This creates structural growth for high-value elastomer components
8.
Substantial switching costs: Customers face high switching costs due to lengthy learning curves and regulatory filings. No significant new competitor has successfully entered their core markets
9.
Scale-driven cost advantage: Global FirstLine plant network provides standardized high-value production across three regions. This setup guarantees identical standards of quality and safety globally
10.
Increasing high-value offering: High Value Offering projects now make up 70.00% of the Healthcare pipeline. This includes FirstLine, rapid transfer ports, and ready-to-use services
11.
Co-engineering integration: Early-stage collaboration integrates Daetwyler deep into customer design cycles. Co-engineered solutions secure long-term product lifecycles and high customer retention

Industry structures and consolidation trends

12.
Consolidating food market: The high-tech food sealing market is consolidating through strategic moves. Daetwyler acquired a majority stake in Capsul'in to expand scale
13.
Fragmented industrial markets: Industrial solutions serve highly fragmented end markets with customized seals. Failure to consolidate could limit bargaining power with raw material suppliers
14.
Growing medical niches: General Industry unit targets high-performance seals in high-margin niches like energy and aerospace. Performance and reliability are primary customer purchasing decisions
15.
Chinese automotive momentum: Localized production in China supports market share gains with domestic OEMs. This offsets softer demand for combustion and electric vehicles in Europe
16.
Bull rebuttal: Technical commoditization of low-end elastomer parts limits margins in standard applications. Industrial solutions struggle with high price competition in flat volume markets

Section 5 – The possible investment case 14 findings

Section summary

Core drivers of the earnings trajectory

1.
Driver one - Healthcare volume recovery: High operating leverage in Healthcare is a major earnings driver. The division can absorb CHF 180.00M in incremental revenues without building new plants. This driver has high plausibility as customer destocking has ended. Failure would occur if customer ordering patterns turn negative again
2.
Driver two - GLP-1 expansion: Ramp-up of serial components for leading GLP-1 obesity therapies provides a major volume boost. In late 2025, a customer validated a second plant for GLP-1 production. This driver is highly plausible given rising demand. Failure would occur if competitor market share gains emerge
3.
Driver three - Product mix shift: Shifting sales toward high-value offerings (HVO) improves group gross margins. HVO rose to 35.00% of Healthcare revenues in 2025. This driver is highly plausible given the pipeline composition. Failure would occur if standard components grow faster
4.
Driver four - ForwardNow program: Structural changes from the ForwardNow operating model aim to deliver cumulative savings. Target savings are CHF 52.00M over 2025-2027. This driver has medium-to-high plausibility as initiatives are already rolled out. Failure would occur if execution delays arise
5.
Driver five - Food & Beverage growth: Solid volume expansion in coffee capsules is driven by long-term supply agreements. Capacity expansion and the majority stake in Capsul'in support mid-term growth. This driver has high plausibility because of the regulatory shift to aluminum. Failure would occur if capsule consumption slows

Operational leverage and scaling potentials

6.
High capital fall-through: Scale effects in Healthcare should drive operating profit margins up significantly. Underutilization in previous years means incremental sales fall-through is exceptionally high
7.
Streamlined production network: Footprint optimization and SKU streamlining simplify industrial operations. Merging Mobility and Connectors business units should generate global cross-selling opportunities
8.
Localized supply strategy: Localized manufacturing footprints in three major regions minimize trade conflict disruptions. This setup allows rapid industrialization and standard quality compliance worldwide
9.
Strong commercial execution: Disciplined value pricing and project selectivity improve top-line quality. This commercial focus prevents standard lower-margin projects from diluting returns

Bull case criteria and compounding risks

10.
Biologics secular tailwinds: Increasing demand for large molecules and biologics fuels structural growth in injectable drug delivery. Demographics and self-administration trends support a positive growth trajectory
11.
Deleveraging EPS kicker: Debt reduction should directly lower net interest expenses. Net interest fell from CHF 4.70M in H1 2024 to CHF 4.00M in H1 2025. This provides a highly predictable boost to net profit
12.
Compounding success factor: The bull case relies on independent drivers that do not all need to fire simultaneously. Strong Healthcare results can offset cyclical industrial headwinds. This lowers the execution risk for investors
13.
Bull rebuttal: Oral formulation alternatives for GLP-1 drugs could severely disrupt the long-term injectable market. This threat would directly hurt demand for primary elastomer plungers and stoppers
