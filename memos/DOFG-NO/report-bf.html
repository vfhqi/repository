EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

DOF Group's high-specification fleet and integrated subsea engineering capabilities command a market-leading position, with robust contract coverage mitigating cyclical spot exposure, though the business model remains highly exposed to severe cyclical downturns and customer concentration . The company has successfully transitioned from a distressed restructuring case into a high-margin subsea services champion, delivering industry-leading EBITDA margins of 38.37% in 2025 and post-tax ROIC of 15.20% , substantially outperforming peer medians. However, this exceptional financial performance is structurally dependent on maintaining preferential access to Petrobras contracts in Brazil , where the group faces material concentration risks that could compress margins rapidly if state-backed spending priorities shift.

DOF Group is a leading provider of integrated subsea and marine services to the global offshore energy market, combining high-specification asset ownership with advanced project engineering, headquartered in Storebø, Austevoll, Norway, operating across six continents with a 45-year history of offshore delivery . The business model strategically positions the company between pure-play vessel charterers and high-risk turnkey EPCI contractors, balancing dayrate-based subsea contracts to avoid lump-sum execution liabilities while capturing premium margins through vertical integration. Operations are supported by 5,753 employees globally, with 3,800 dedicated offshore workers managing subsea installations and life-of-field operations, backed by a substantial proprietary inventory of 74 Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) and Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) .

This integrated capability allows DOF to earn 15% to 20% higher returns compared to pure bareboat chartering, creating a defensible competitive moat in high-complexity subsea projects. 2
