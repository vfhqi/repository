  • Disruption cost: Capital investment in energy management systems embedded in existing tower contracts; manageable but not immaterial

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish disruption read: the power-as-a-service model adds operational complexity that HTWS may not have at scale; if solar/hybrid maintenance costs prove higher than modelled, the initiative could compress rather than expand margins; and the LEO threat in rural areas is precisely where HTWS has its lowest-quality, lowest-tenancy towers — the disruption may hit the weakest part of the portfolio first [#J]
