Mechanism to SP: PPA repricing surprise (positive or negative) creates earnings revision waves; positive repricing tends to be underestimated because sell-side models typically assume flat renewal rates [#I]

Visibility: Low — PPA expiry schedule not publicly disclosed in detail; repricing timing is not management-guided, making this driver difficult to model from outside [#A]

Bear case: Wholesale power prices fall in 2025-27 (demand softening, new renewables capacity oversupply); PPAs renewing in this window lock in lower rates; existing contracted revenue book is fine but new merchant/repricing revenue disappoints [#A]

Miss scenario: European power prices compress toward €50-60/MWh as renewables build-out accelerates and demand growth disappoints; new PPAs signed at lower rates; Brazil hydrology event drives below-PLD spot prices for 6+ months [#A]

Warning signal: Voltalia's limited disclosure on PPA expiry schedule and repricing pipeline means analysts (and Watson) are modelling this driver with insufficient data; absence of disclosure is not a positive signal [#A]

Sceptical bullet: The power price tailwind of 2021-23 is widely acknowledged and likely priced into sell-side medium-term models; the incremental discovery value from PPA repricing for Richard is lower than it might appear because it's a consensus-visible driver [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — modest repricing tailwind in 2025-27 as new contracts capture post-2020 higher price environment; Brazil remains a downside asymmetry risk [#J]
