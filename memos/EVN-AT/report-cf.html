EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 10 findings

Net Investment Signal and Materiality of Change EVN AG presents a moderately positive investment case anchored by an updated Strategy 2030 that leverages energy transition opportunities through a stepped-up EUR 1 billion annual investment program running through the end of the decade.

The successful deconsolidation of the international project business WTE Wassertechnik for EUR 100 million in cash, completed on March 2, 2026, removed a volatile earnings drag and delivered a favorable EUR 206 million positive effect on net debt .

The unanimous re-appointment of CEO Stefan Szyszkowitz until 2031 locks in long-term strategic stability, while new CTO and CFO appointments bring technical and financial expertise to execute the capital-intensive growth plan. However, the massive annual capital expenditure program will push free operating cash flows into negative territory over the medium term, creating a structural tension between growth ambitions and near-term cash generation.

- Earnings Calls generation, large battery storage, and drinking water supplies.

The strategic pivot toward domestic regulated assets and away from volatile international project exposure represents a de-risking of the earnings profile , though it simultaneously increases the company's capital intensity and leverage trajectory. - Earnings Calls Drivers with Plausibility Verdicts Strategy 2030 targets a 2030 EBITDA range of EUR 1.1 billion to EUR 1.2 billion, with regulated networks contributing approximately 45 percent of EBITDA, the expanded Generation segment providing 15 to 20 percent, and the Energy trade and supply segment contributing roughly 10 percent of group EBITDA.

1.
The following drivers underpin this trajectory:
2.
Driver One: Regulated Grid Capex and RAB Expansion remains the highest-plausibility, highest-impact strategic pillar. EVN plans to invest up to EUR 470 million annually in Lower Austrian electricity networks, driving a 75 percent cumulative expansion in the Regulated Asset Base with stated regulatory WACC for new assets set at 6.24 percent pre-tax nominal

Regulatory authority E-Control approved calendar year 2026 household tariff increases of 6.9 percent for electricity, providing positive regulatory offsets that protect profitability. This driver carries high confidence and high plausibility , anchored by visible project pipelines and stable five-year regulatory frameworks. The primary dependency is regulatory approval continuity in Austria, which has historically been supportive.

3.
Driver Two: Onshore Wind and Photovoltaic Fleet Expansion carries medium-to-high plausibility but faces execution and external volatility risks . EVN targets onshore wind capacity growth to 770 MW by 2030 from 561 MW and photovoltaic capacity expansion to 300 MWp by 2030 from 133 MWp. However, below-average wind and water flows in Austria depressed total electricity generation by 9.9 percent during the first half of 2025/26. Fixed feed-in tariffs ranging from EUR 81 to EUR 95 per MWh protect wind returns, partially mitigating merchant price exposure

The key dependency is local community zoning approval , which has occasionally delayed projects in Lower Austria. Weather-related generation volatility represents a persistent external headwind that capacity additions can only partially offset.

4.
Driver Three: Battery Storage Fleet Deployment is a high-plausibility emerging driver with significant strategic optionality

Stated battery targets require scaling capacity to 300 MW by 2030 from 12 MW, with construction commenced on the 70 MW battery in Theiss and 16 MW battery in Dürnrohr.

Storage assets enable grid stability optimization and balance midday solar peaks, preventing severe localized bottlenecks.

The dependency on renewable co-location is manageable given EVN's existing wind and solar asset base.

Battery deployment faces supply chain risks related to lithium and critical minerals, but the overall plausibility remains high given the company's technical expertise and balance sheet capacity.

5.
Driver Four: Energy Retail Turnaround at EVN KG carries medium plausibility and medium confidence . Supply subsidiary EVN KG reported a positive EBITDA contribution of EUR 1.7 million in the first half, though retail recovery was slowed by a EUR 13 million provision for social tariffs

Stated retail strategies introduced a net 10 cents per kWh electricity tariff on April 1, 2026, targeting competitive positioning in a challenging market. The dependency on social tariff legislation creates regulatory risk, as Austrian authorities may impose further burden- sharing requirements on utilities to support vulnerable customers. Historical supply losses reached EUR 162 million in FY 2023/24, underscoring the segment's volatility.

6.
Driver Five: Verbund AG Dividend Contribution provides EUR 122.9 million in annual cash inflow but carries medium confidence due to exposure to external hydrological coefficients. EVN's 12.63 percent Verbund AG stake represents a market value of EUR 2.88 billion, with Verbund proposing a dividend of EUR 3.15 per share for FY25. Financial results in FY 2024/25 fell due to a lower Verbund dividend of EUR 2.80 per share. This nonoperating earnings stream is highly correlated with Austrian hydropower generation , creating a significant sensitivity to weather patterns. Verbund's own earnings volatility directly flows through to EVN's consolidated financials
7.
Driver Six: Southeast European Network Modernization presents modest EBITDA upside but is constrained by regulatory return limitations

Network distribution volumes in South East Europe increased by 2.6 percent to 8,560 GWh during the first half, reflecting growing household demand in Bulgaria and North Macedonia.

However, analysts apply a 5.0x EV/EBITDA valuation multiple to this segment due to heightened political volatility and regulatory frameworks that often set allowed returns below WACC.

The plausibility of meaningful margin expansion in this region remains low to medium .

Recent Delivery and Forward Credibility Assessment EVN consistently delivers results at the upper end of its original guidance, signaling conservative sandbagging, with stated FY 2024/25 net profit of EUR 436.7 million landing near the top of EUR 400 million to EUR 440 million guidance and stated group EBITDA of EUR 909 million rising 19 percent year-on-year.

Historical beat patterns classify management as a highly credible honest-tracker with a conservative bias.

Group revenue increased by 3.2 percent year on year during the first half of the 2025/26 financial year, stated EBITDA rose by 7.9 percent year on year driven by investment-led organic growth, and the H1 2025/26 group net result grew 24.7 percent year on year benefiting from discontinued operations.

EVN expects its FY 2025/26 group net result to fall within a solid corridor of EUR 430 million to EUR 480 million, with stated group EBITDA and net results projected to remain roughly at the prior year level, assuming a stable regulatory and energy policy environment across Austria and Southeastern Europe.

The recommended minimum dividend for FY 2025/26 is fixed at at least EUR 0.90 per share, with management aiming to raise the dividend to at least EUR 1.10 per share by FY 2029/30, calibrated to reach a medium-term payout ratio of approximately 40 percent by 2030.

The forward credibility posture is classified as conservative honest-tracker with a structural sandbagging bias .

Management has demonstrated a consistent ability to protect guidance ranges even in adverse external conditions, utilizing buffer margins built into initial forecasts.

This archetype provides high confidence in baseline target delivery but limits upside surprise potential absent extraordinary external tailwinds.

The primary credibility risk stems from sustained below-average hydrological flows , which could compress renewable generation EBITDA faster than network investments can offset.

Sell-Side Positioning and Analyst Coverage Dynamics The overall sell-side analyst pool size currently consists of five historically named individual analysts covering the stock, with active and historical coverage including analysts from Bernstein Research and Deutsche Bank Research, though Bernstein Research formally discontinued its coverage of EVN on December 12, 2025, due to resource reallocation.

Bernstein raised its price target marginally to EUR 40.90 from EUR 40.70 in January 2025, with the PT upgrade supported by raising 2026 EPS estimates by 1.2 percent (calculated) to EUR 2.46.

The combination of limited free float, provincial government control at 51 percent, and EnBW's 31 percent strategic stake creates structural impediments to active trading and analyst engagement.

The remaining coverage base is thin, potentially leading to consensus groupthink and reduced scrutiny of emerging risks. arguments among cautious analysts focus on volatile energy trading margins, massive capex requirements, and Southeast European regulatory limits .

Escalating capital expenditure limits near-term cash generation with core capex projected at EUR 815.4 million in 2026/27E, while regulatory risks in Bulgaria and North Macedonia drag on valuation, limiting the SSE segment to 5.0x EV/EBITDA.

Position and Relative Valuation EVN occupies a solid middle-tier position within the European utility peer group, with stated network growth of 3.2 percent year on year anchoring EVN's relative stability. Bernstein models EVN's adjusted P/E multiple at 11.3x for 2025E and 10.9x for 2026E, noting EVN's EV/EBITDA multiple of 7.5x for 2025E is highly supportive of an Outperform rating.

EVN's dividend yield of 3.9 percent is below the peer median of 4.9 percent for major European utilities.

trades at a meaningful valuation discount to integrated peers such as Iberdrola (10.9x EV/EBITDA) and E.ON (8.1x EV/EBITDA), reflecting the market's assessment of execution risk on the EUR 1 billion capex program, hydrological volatility, and Southeast European regulatory uncertainty .

The discount is partially justified by EVN's smaller scale, lower liquidity, and binary exposure to Austrian weather patterns .

However, the valuation also embeds limited credit for the strategic de-risking achieved through WTE divestment and the visible regulated RAB growth trajectory .

On a peer-relative momentum basis, EVN ranks in the upper-middle quartile , outperforming Verbund (which faces severe hydrological deficits) but trailing Fortum and Iberdrola on organic growth metrics.

The 51 percent provincial government control and 31 percent EnBW strategic stake create formidable barriers to activist entry, as the free float of 17 percent provides insufficient leverage for campaign viability.

This ownership configuration is a structural impediment to capital allocation discipline but also insulates management from short-term earnings pressure.

Material Risks and plausible problem to monitor

8.
Risk One: Sustained Free Cash Flow Deficits and Leverage Trajectory represents the most immediate financial risk . Scope Ratings projects net capex to increase from EUR 753 million in 2024/25 to EUR 930 million in 2025/26E and EUR 1,000 million in 2026/27E to fund renewable expansion, with FOCF expected to turn negative at EUR negative 29 million in 2025/26E from positive EUR 150 million in 2024/25, deteriorating further to EUR negative 131 million in 2026/27E

Scope Ratings models adjusted debt/EBITDA rising from 1.10x in 2024/25 to 1.60x in 2026/27E, with EBITDA interest coverage projected to slide to 17.00x in 2026/27E from greater than 20.00x, noting that rating downgrade pressure occurs if debt/EBITDA exceeds 1.75x on a sustained basis and a prolonged period of negative FOCF represents a key downgrade risk for the Scope rating.

The company's Arange credit ratings provide limited cushion before triggering negative rating actions, which would increase funding costs and constrain strategic flexibility. 21

9.
Risk Two: Chronic Hydrological and Wind Generation Deficits create persistent earnings volatility that capacity additions cannot fully offset

Colder temperatures increased household heating but industrial electricity grid volumes fell 1.0 percent in Lower Austria during HY1 2025/26, while water and wind generation coefficients in Austria fell substantially below long-term averages.

Below-average wind and water flows in Austria depressed total electricity generation by 9.9 percent.

The company's renewable generation EBITDA is structurally exposed to multi-year hydrological cycles that are beyond management control.

Climate change may be amplifying weather pattern extremes, increasing the frequency of below-average generation years.

This risk is partially hedged by fixed feed-in tariffs but cannot be fully eliminated.

10.
Risk Three: Accelerating Prosumer Solar Penetration and Structural Volume Decline threatens the long-term utilization of network assets

EVN's Lower Austrian network distribution volumes fell by 1.0 percent to 4,374 GWh, network gas distribution in Lower Austria contracted by 6.6 percent to 8,101 GWh, and steady prosumer PV feed-in continues to drive structural domestic electricity volume declines.

As more customers install behind-themeter solar and battery systems, grid purchase volumes will continue to erode even as EVN invests heavily in network capacity to handle bidirectional power flows .

The regulatory framework may not adjust tariffs quickly enough to maintain returns on underutilized assets, particularly if political pressure limits E-Control's ability to approve steep tariff increases.

This dynamic could result in stranded capital in oversized distribution infrastructure.

The synthesis of these factors produces a cautiously constructive investment thesis with execution risk concentrated in 2026-2028 as the company navigates negative free cash flow while simultaneously scaling renewable capacity.

The strategic pivot toward regulated domestic infrastructure is directionally correct, but the pace and magnitude of capital deployment relative to cash generation creates near-term balance sheet pressure.

Management's conservative guidance posture and consistent delivery track record provide confidence in baseline target achievement, but the structural erosion of sell-side coverage and limited free float liquidity reduce the probability of meaningful valuation re-rating absent a catalyst.

Investors must weigh the visible regulated RAB growth and de-risked portfolio against leverage creep, hydrological volatility, and prosumerdriven volume headwinds .
