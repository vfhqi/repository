EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Change Materiality

Croda executed sweeping board and C-suite refreshment bringing much-needed transformation expertise , while improving cultural metrics suggest the workforce is absorbing the rapid pace of change . The company successfully transitioned into a pure-play Consumer Care and Life Sciences entity following the £651m PTIC divestiture , positioning 82% of Consumer Care sales toward resilient local and regional customers. Adjusted operating margins collapsed from 24.8% in 2021 to 17.2% in 2024 due to severe negative operating leverage , but FY25 marked a definitive inflection with margins ticking up 20bps to 17.4% . Standard Industries accumulated a >5% equity stake in September 2025 , escalating pressure for further portfolio pruning and manufacturing footprint rationalization.

Top Key Drivers with Plausibility Verdicts

Structural cost and efficiency savings targeting £100m by 2028 carry HIGH plausibility , offering ~150-200bps margin expansion independent of volume recovery . Shared asset utilisation recovery from current 92% to historical norms carries HIGH plausibility but remains dependent on volume recovery , delivering an estimated

100-200bps margin uplift . Pricing agility and pass-through provide absolute profit protection with HIGH plausibility , leveraging Croda's sector-leading one-quarter inflation lag versus peers' two quarters. Pharma pipeline and lipid acceleration offer ~2-3% group organic sales growth with MEDIUM plausibility , constrained by inherently volatile clinical trial timelines. Local and regional customer penetration delivers ~1-2% group organic sales growth with HIGH plausibility , capitalizing on 82% Consumer Care sales exposure to agile L&R brands . Crop protection volume normalisation contributes ~1-2% group organic sales growth with MEDIUM plausibility , as underlying agricultural economics remain structurally uncertain. 5

£145m by 2% , driven by pharma strength and disciplined cost execution. Stringent working capital discipline drove H2 2025 free cash flow to £133.6m, a sharp sequential improvement . 11

Forward Credibility

Management's conservative 2028 margin and growth targets provide a highly achievable baseline to under- promise and over-deliver , earning a HIGH credibility verdict . The FY26-FY28 outlook models 3-6% organic sales growth, relying heavily on volume recovery rather than pricing leverage . Margin guidance explicitly relies on delivering £35m in gross savings during FY26 from the £100m transformation programme . Jefferies warns that prioritising volumes to restore plant utilisation will delay clarity on incremental earnings delivery until late 2026 , indicating a narrow margin of safety . The steady progression of the £100m cost-efficiency programme provides a highly visible, controllable internal lever .

Sell-Side Positioning

Consensus retains a bullish skew on Croda's volume recovery , with 50% of covering analysts rating the stock Overweight per Refinitiv . Morgan Stanley upgraded Croda to Overweight in March 2026, citing superior organic growth prospects and absolute profit protection . Jefferies downgraded the stock to Hold in November 2025, reflecting concerns that margins are materialising later than initially expected . Despite a robust forward outlook, Croda absorbed downward consensus organic sales revisions of ~13% over the last 12 months . Broker targets span from a low of 2,800p to a high of 4,360p , indicating wide dispersion in valuation perspectives. 16 17 18

Fundamental Peer Position

Croda screens highly on medium-term organic growth, ranking second-best-in-class with FY26 core constant- currency growth forecast at 4.8%, roughly 100bps ahead of the peer average . Croda currently trails Givaudan and Novonesis on margin trajectory, driven by severe historical underutilisation of its shared manufacturing sites . Croda is the sector's fastest to pass through inflation, acting with a one-quarter lag versus peers' two-quarter

delays . Croda targets a structural £50m reduction in net working capital by 2028 and is positioned for robust free cash flow progression , supporting potential special returns post-peak capex cycle.

Activist Presence

Standard Industries accumulated a >5% equity stake, publicly disclosed in September 2025 , bringing a proven operational activist record including Johnson Matthey's £1.8bn catalyst division sale . The activist is expected to push for divestment of the lower-margin Seed Enhancement business and rationalisation of Croda's 11 underutilised shared manufacturing sites . Market participants anticipate pressure to enforce deeper SG&A and headcount cuts beyond the existing £100m programme . Bear case: Croda's highly integrated shared-site manufacturing model severely complicates further divestments, limiting potential structural breakup upside .

Material Risks

Croda's heavy reliance on passing through raw-material inflation assumes inelastic customer demand; if global FMCG partners aggressively substitute with cheaper generic alternatives during prolonged macro downturn, both volume and pricing power would collapse simultaneously . Reliance on volume recovery across shared assets creates a stacked downside risk if both crop and consumer end-markets decelerate simultaneously. Croda's margin expansion thesis contains a fatal circular dependency: it assumes pricing power remains intact while simultaneously relying on deep price cuts to win back volumes needed to fill empty factories .

Investment-Case Verdict

Croda offers highly leveraged upside to cyclical volume normalization and structural cost discipline, but the 20% margin ceiling remains contingent on simultaneous execution across multiple dependent drivers with a narrow safety buffer.
