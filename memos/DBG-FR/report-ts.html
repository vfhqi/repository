Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The business model combines a highly resilient industrial scrap metal processing network with stable municipal service contracts and a substantial equity exposure to contract catering.

Segmental Operations and Product Portfolio

1.
Core recycling focus: Derichebourg is a European leader in collecting, sorting, and processing metal scrap, handling 4.78 million tons of ferrous and non-ferrous metals annually
2.
Highly automated facilities: The company owns and operates an extensive network of 272 recycling sites across Europe, featuring high value-added sorting lines for complex scrap materials
3.
Favorable metal mix: Non-ferrous metals generate EUR 1.01 billion in H1 2026 revenue, demonstrating a strategic tilt toward high-value products like copper granulates and aluminum
4.
Stable municipal contracts: The Public Sector Services division collects household waste under long-term public contracts, generating EUR 94.80 million in H1 2026 revenue with low cyclicality
5.
Strategic Elior holding: Derichebourg holds a 48.17% stake in Elior Group, which broadens the business profile into contract catering and multiservices

Competitive Advantage and Value Chain Position

6.
High barriers to entry: Replicating Derichebourg's industrial asset base is extremely difficult due to strict environmental permitting and heavy capital needs for advanced shears and shredders
7.
Crucial intermediate positioning: The company operates as a key intermediary, capturing protected processing spreads before selling refined raw materials to European steel mills and secondary aluminum smelters
8.
Fragmented industry structure: The European metal recycling market remains highly fragmented, allowing Derichebourg to act as a consolidation platform at cycle bottoms
9.
Independent governance protection: The relationship with Elior is governed by a strict agreement limiting Derichebourg's voting rights on strategic board resolutions, ensuring balanced and professional governance
10.
Other side of the story: Extreme commodity price sensitivity means that a sustained decline in global steel and aluminum demand would compress spreads and volume throughput, rendering high fixed overheads highly dilutive to earnings

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible investment case is highly compelling, driven by structural European green steel demand, the integration of Scholz Recycling, and Elior's ongoing turnaround.

Long-Term Structural Drivers

1.
Electric arc furnace transition: European steelmakers are replacing blast furnaces with electric arc furnaces, which boosts long-term demand for high-quality scrap metal
2.
Favorable carbon regulatory landscape: The implementation of the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) in January 2026 shields domestic scrap margins by penalizing high-carbon metal imports
3.
Tightening trade protections: The EU's new quota and tariff regulations on steel imports from July 2026 are expected to increase capacity utilization at European mills, driving robust scrap demand

M&A Synergy and Financial Deleveraging

4.
Highly accretive Scholz acquisition: The pending acquisition of Scholz Recycling adds over 100 centers, expanding operational scale and delivering meaningful medium-term EPS accretion
5.
Significant Elior synergy capture: Upgraded synergy targets of EUR 56.00 million between Derichebourg Multiservices and Elior will accelerate Elior's deleveraging toward a target of under 3.00x by end-FY26
6.
Independent investment drivers: The forward earnings trajectory is independent of any single driver, as either recycling volume recoveries or Elior re-ratings could single-handedly support a 25% plus IRR
7.
Other side of the story: The acquisition of Scholz Recycling remains subject to Chiho's right to settle with secured creditors, creating transaction termination risk before closing in late 2026

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture looks manageable and is well-mitigated by Derichebourg's prudent family-led financial policy, robust liquidity headroom, and structural trade protections.

Key Structural and Execution Risks

1.
Cyclical industrial downturns: A severe slowdown in European steel production would directly hurt scrap throughput and drag down group EBITDA margins
2.
Material energy cost inflation: Higher electricity and gas prices in Europe represent a persistent headwind for smelters and recycling facilities, though partially mitigated by French power cost savings
3.
Exposure to pricing disputes: Elior's H1 2026 results were impacted by a EUR 25.00 million pricing dispute in Italy, exposing Derichebourg to volatility from its non-controlled catering affiliate
4.
Potential transaction friction: The integration of Scholz Recycling could face regulatory or cultural delays, slowing the realization of expected logistical synergies
5.
Early-warning signals: Investors should closely monitor European steel mill capacity utilization rates and spot non-ferrous metal premiums as leading indicators of recycling profitability
6.
Other side of the story: Geopolitical supply constraints and trade policies, such as the CBAM, actually enhance the competitive position of local European recyclers by restricting extra-EU scrap leakage
