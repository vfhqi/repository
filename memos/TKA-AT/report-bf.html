EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 21 findings

Business Quality and Competitive Position

1.
Telekom Austria operates a dual-market business model balancing stagnant domestic Austrian operations with high-growth Central and Eastern European digital services, supported by centralized sourcing and logistics optimization through a Dubai hub that manages carrier-to-carrier connectivity trading between Europe and Africa
2.
The group maintains domestic market leadership with 37% Austrian market share and highly defensive B2B services supported by specialized managed cloud and security offerings, while achieving a EUR 1 billion net debt reduction through the strategic separation of passive tower infrastructure into EuroTeleSites AG
3.
Independent network quality tests reveal A1 has lost its domestic network quality leadership to Magenta Telekom, while the popularity of cheap 5G wireless Cubes limits customer willingness to pay premium prices for high-speed physical fiber broadband
4.
Management fears that aggressive low-value retail mobile promotions could eventually contaminate lucrative B2B corporate accounts, as procurement agents frequently optimize plans based on retail discount advertisements, driving heavy client migration between various low-value brands

Financial Profile and Capital Returns

5.
The group ended FY25 with lease-adjusted net debt compressing to an exceptional 0.0x multiple, backed by an S&P confirmed A- credit rating that ranks among the highest within the European telecom sector
6.
Operating ROIC decreased from 12.9% to 11.2% in FY24 due to tower asset base adjustments, though the group's 11.2% return still exceeds Orange's 8.0% return on invested capital
7.
A1 Belarus delivers solid local results despite severe geopolitical restrictions, with currency controls preventing repatriation of approximately 20% of group cash as of Q1 2026, while cancellation of the beCloud exclusive frequency contract shifted network costs from capital expenditures to operating expenses
8.
The stock trades at a massive, unwarranted discount of 3.8x EV/EBITDAaL versus the European peer average of 6.6x, while leading operators Swisscom and Deutsche Telekom command elevated multiples of 13.8x and 10.2x respectively

Market Structure and Competitive Intensity

9.
Resellers and MVNOs collectively represent roughly 18% of total mobile subscribers across more than 30 virtual operators crowding the domestic mobile core market, driving aggressive retail tariff optimization across the entire Austrian telecom market
10.
Central and Eastern European segments generate stronger growth dynamics than mature Austria, with favorable regional drivers including upselling, high-speed broadband demand, and ICT solutions, while A1 Bulgaria improved its fixed RGU market share from 18% to 27% in 2025
11.
The potential sale of United Group by its private equity owners introduces regional competitive risks, as a recapitalized or consolidated United Group could launch aggressive promotional campaigns in CEE markets

Operational Execution and Strategic Initiatives

14.

Governance and Capital Allocation

15.
Public capital market influence remains limited by the dominant two-owner partnership controlling the company, with public shareholders controlling only 11% of outstanding group equity as América Móvil and ÖBAG extended their joint syndicate pact for a ten-year term, leaving public investors with no practical capability to steer corporate capital allocation

17.
Performance-related pay elements form the predominant portion of executive board remuneration accounting for 55% to 65% of total target executive pay, with the 2025 annual bonus linking 33% of payouts to free cash flow generation and one-third to group service revenue growth, while previous long-term incentive tranches prioritized capital efficiency by weighting operating ROIC at 34%

Long-Term Structural Position

18.
Entering the regional telecom market requires exorbitant upfront capital deployments for physical networks with civil engineering deployment costs reaching approximately EUR 3,000 per household connected in Austria, while national spectrum auction licenses create insurmountable legal and financial barriers for prospective entrants, though the group secured a key 2.6 GHz spectrum extension in Austria for only EUR 11 million
