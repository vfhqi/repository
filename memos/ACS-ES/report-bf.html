ACS Group has successfully executed a transformational strategic pivot toward high-tech infrastructure and low- risk collaborative contracting, reducing organizational complexity while strengthening its exposure to global digitalization and electrification megatrends, with over 93% of consolidated revenues now derived from highly secure international jurisdictions anchored by a synergistic operating model that allows subsidiaries to share engineering software, talent pools, and procurement logistics. The group released €4.9bn in cash through the disposal of its high-risk Cobra IS industrial division to Vinci, strategically removing engineering, procurement, and construction liabilities from the balance sheet while reallocating freed capital into long-duration transport concessions and next-generation digital assets. The company achieved record sales approaching EUR 50bn in FY25 with 19.70% year-on-year growth, driven by a digital and technology backlog that grew 130% year-on-year on robust hyperscaler demand. 1

The global infrastructure market is experiencing a massive synchronized expansion driven by multi-trillion dollar transition and digital megatrend investments, with the European energy transition alone requiring over €25tn in capital by 2030, US grid investment growing at a 10% CAGR, and the addressable data center EPC contracting market projected to reach $600bn by 2030. Chairman Florentino Perez and CriteriaCaixa anchor the shareholder base with aligned long-term interests, while the company generated a 15% average annual return over 25 years leading industry peers and achieved an exceptional 33.50% average annual shareholder return over the last five years.
