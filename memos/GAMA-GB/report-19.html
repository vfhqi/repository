3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 1: Organic Revenue Growth Narrative Break (Revenue Risk)

Scenario: Physical miniatures volume growth stalls as the addressable hobbyist base plateaus in core UK/US/Europe markets; new customer cohorts are smaller, churn faster, and spend less per head than the legacy wargaming community; revenue growth narrative that justified premium multiple collapses.

Transmission mechanism: Flat unit volumes → negative operating leverage on Nottingham manufacturing overhead → EBIT margin compression 300-500bps → EPS misses consensus by 15-20% → re-rating from 30x+ PE toward 20x sector median → 35-40% total return destruction.

Early warning signals:
- Like-for-like sales from owned Warhammer retail stores turns negative for 2 consecutive quarters
- New Citadel Colour / beginner set volumes (disclosed in Trade/Direct splits) decelerate faster than core range
- Warhammer Community engagement metrics (forum active users, YouTube tutorial views) plateau or decline
- Third-party specialist retailer order books shrink — detectable via trade customer commentary in channel checks
- Average selling price growth decelerates to below CPI, suggesting pricing power exhaustion

Plausibility: High plausibility — physical hobby markets have well-documented participation ceilings; Games Workshop's UK core market is demographically mature; precedent base rate from legacy wargaming (Historical miniatures sector, ~2008-2018 period) shows category ceiling effects are real.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: demographic maturing of core wargaming cohort, digital entertainment substitution — ✓
- Motive: hobbyists redirect discretionary spend to video games, streaming — ✓
- Opportunity: macro consumer squeeze on discretionary spending — ✓
- All three legs present → full failure scenario, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: Strong growth in non-UK/non-US geographic markets (Asia-Pacific, Latin America) absorbing maturity of legacy markets; net new retail store openings showing positive 12-month payback; trade customer reorder rates stable or growing.

Monitoring checklist:
- H1/H2 segmental Trade vs. Direct revenue growth differential
- Retail store LFL disclosed in trading updates
- New product release sales velocity (launch-week sell-through commentary)
- Geographic revenue split — Rest of World growth rate
- Employee count at Nottingham manufacturing vs. revenue growth (productivity proxy)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with FM3 (operating leverage failure) and FM6 (pricing pressure); if volume stalls AND pricing fails simultaneously, magnitude doubles; null interaction with FM9 (M&A integration) since GAMA has no M&A track record.

Sceptical close: Disconfirming data would be sustained positive LFL retail store sales + Rest of World outperforming at 20%+; absent those two simultaneously, volume ceiling thesis cannot be dismissed.

Scenario: Games Workshop's Trade channel (independent and specialist retailers) constitutes a meaningful revenue block; structural shift toward Amazon and mass-market retail erodes specialist channel economics; independent hobby stores close at accelerating rate post-pandemic; Games Workshop's bilateral trade terms deteriorate as channel power shifts.

Transmission mechanism: Trade channel attrition → lower reorder frequency from remaining accounts → Games Workshop forced to discount or increase marketing spend to maintain revenue → gross margin erosion 150-250bps → modest EPS miss but sustained multiple compression from narrative degradation.

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed Trade channel revenue growth underperforms Direct channel for 3+ consecutive halves
- Independent hobby store closures visible via community forums and industry press accelerate
- Games Workshop increases trade discount or "support" provisions — visible in cost of sales trends
- Competing products (e.g., Mantic Games) gaining shelf space at surviving independents
- Amazon UK becoming primary discovery channel for new hobbyists (detectable via review velocity data)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — Games Workshop has successfully defended channel mix by investing in own retail (Direct channel), but Trade channel remains important for geographic coverage outside major cities; specialist retail sector globally under secular pressure from e-commerce.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means: e-commerce structural shift already ongoing — ✓
- Motive: channel partners prioritise higher-margin or faster-selling products — ✓
- Opportunity: hobby retail closures visible post-2020 — ✓
- All three legs present → full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Direct channel growing faster than Trade (which GAMA reports and which would confirm successful channel migration, not dependency failure); own-retail store count growing; digital direct-to-consumer (webstore) share increasing.

Monitoring checklist:
- Trade vs. Direct revenue split each half-year
- Net owned retail store openings/closures
- Webstore revenue as % of Direct segment
- Trade channel gross margin commentary (implicit from segmental disclosures)
- Competitor shelf-space capture at Waterstones, FLGS surveys

Interaction: Correlated with FM1 (volume ceiling) — if both channel and volume fail, compounding effect; partially offset if Direct channel migration is successful (which itself carries execution risk per FM8).

Sceptical close: GAMA's structural advantage is its own-retail network and webstore — if Direct share is growing to 50%+, Trade channel attrition becomes manageable; the disconfirming evidence is the Direct acceleration story, which must be sustained, not one-off.

§4 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 3: Pricing Power Exhaustion (Revenue + Margin Risk)

Scenario: Games Workshop has delivered above-inflation price increases on core miniatures (5-15% in recent years), partly offsetting any volume softness; the community absorbs price rises up to a threshold, beyond which hobby substitution and secondary-market arbitrage (eBay re-sales of older kits) accelerate; pricing power exhaustion triggers a volume/price double-squeeze.

Transmission mechanism: Price ceiling hit → volume elasticity above threshold → revenue miss + gross margin pressure (since overhead is fixed) → 20-25% EPS downside vs. consensus → re-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Community forums (Warhammer Community, Reddit/r/warhammer) sentiment shift — price complaints become dominant versus product excitement
- eBay/secondary market price premium for new kits collapses — indicates demand softer than supply
- New kit launch velocity metrics (units sold per SKU per week) decelerating relative to price
- Age of Sigmar / 40K tournament participation declining — active competitive community is core spend driver
- Citadel Paint standalone sales decelerate — paint is re-order habit; deceleration signals active hobbyist count falling

Plausibility: High plausibility — price elasticity in physical goods hobbies is well-documented; the Games Workshop community has shown historical price resistance (prior protest periods in 2012-2013); as price points escalate toward £40-50 per infantry box, the accessible-entry argument weakens materially.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means: continued above-inflation pricing already implemented — ✓
- Motive: management has historically used pricing as the primary growth lever — ✓
- Opportunity: cost-of-living pressures on discretionary spend — ✓
- All three legs present → full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Volume growth remaining positive despite price increases (i.e., demand is inelastic through current price levels); new product architecture (Combat Patrol boxes, Starter Sets) maintaining accessible price points for new entrants; secondary market price premiums sustained, indicating supply/demand balance positive.

Monitoring checklist:
- Unit volume data (estimated from revenue / ASP trends)
- Secondary market (eBay completed listings) pricing trends for recent releases
- Tournament attendance statistics (Warhammer World events, third-party TO data)
- Community sentiment proxy metrics
- Combat Patrol / starter set relative SKU count vs. full army kit count in new release cadence

Interaction: Directly interacts with FM1 (volume ceiling) — pricing failure + volume ceiling = worst-case revenue scenario; also interacts with FM5 (digital substitution) since price elasticity makes the digital alternative relatively more attractive.

Sceptical close: Disconfirming evidence requires simultaneous positive volume AND positive ASP trends — a clean "both-and" test; if volume is only flat-to-slightly-negative while ASP grows, the pricing-power-exhaustion thesis is building, not yet triggered.

§5 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 4: Manufacturing / Supply Chain Single-Source Risk (Margin + Execution Risk)

Scenario: Games Workshop manufactures substantially all plastic miniatures from a single Nottingham facility; fire, flood, industrial action, or critical equipment failure causes multi-month production outage; revenue gap of £50-80m, unrecoverable in the financial year, with knock-on effects to retail channel stocking.

Transmission mechanism: Production outage → revenue recognition gap → fixed cost leverage reverses → significant EBIT/EPS miss → market re-prices operational risk premium upward → 15-25% stock decline even if event is "one-off."

Early warning signals:
- Insurance coverage levels (disclosed in annual report risk section) declining in real terms
- Capital expenditure on manufacturing equipment declining relative to depreciation (under-investment signal)
- Any press/community reports of factory disruption, flooding events (Nottingham flood plain proximity)
- Management commentary on business continuity investments becoming more defensive in tone
- Long lead time for new kit releases extending unexpectedly — early signal of production scheduling stress

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — no peer precedent for a major GAMA factory event in public record, but single-site manufacturing concentration is a known structural vulnerability; comparable: Reckitt Benckiser Mead Johnson facility fire (2017) caused significant revenue gap; hobby sector no precedent but industrial sector base rate exists.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means: single-site concentration — ✓
- Motive: N/A (exogenous risk, not intentional) — N/A
- Opportunity: physical risks always present — ✓
- Two-of-three (exogenous, no motive) → WATCH ONLY per M/M/O doctrine

Disconfirming evidence: Games Workshop has meaningful finished goods inventory buffer (disclosed in balance sheet); supply from third-party resin/Forge World operation provides partial coverage; management explicitly investing in manufacturing resilience (capex disclosures).

Monitoring checklist:
- Finished goods inventory vs. historical norms (days cover)
- Capex on manufacturing equipment as % of revenue
- Insurance provisions in risk section of annual report
- Release cadence regularity — any slippage vs. announced schedule
- Forge World / resin production as % of total output

Interaction: Null interaction with competitive/digital failure modes; correlated with FM7 (cost inflation) since a recovery rebuild scenario would involve cost inflation; could interact with FM10 (management credibility) if a known risk was inadequately hedged.

Sceptical close: Disconfirming evidence is adequate finished-goods buffer + ongoing capex-for-resilience disclosures; absent explicit management commentary on business continuity investment, this risk remains structurally under-monitored.

⚡ Outlier flag: GAMA's Nottingham single-site concentration is a top-5% operational concentration risk for a £4bn+ consumer goods manufacturer; virtually no peer in the FTSE 250 consumer segment manufactures >90% of revenue from a single facility without explicit secondary-source disclosure.

§6 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 5: Digital / Competitive Substitution (Structural / Competitive Risk)

Scenario: Video games set in the Warhammer universe (40K: Space Marine, Darktide, Total War: Warhammer) onboard players to the IP but the conversion rate to physical miniature purchasing is structurally lower than the bull case assumes; a generation of "Warhammer fans" who never pick up a paintbrush accumulates; simultaneously, competing physical hobby systems (Infinity, Kings of War, One Page Rules) improve product quality and price competitiveness, capturing switchers.

Transmission mechanism: Lower conversion rate → new customer cohort smaller per IP engagement unit → growth narrative miss over 24-36 month horizon → downward EPS revision cycle → de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Video game player counts (Steam concurrent users, Metacritic review volume) growing while miniature sales growth decelerates — divergence signal
- One Page Rules / alternate system community growth metrics (subscriber counts, print-on-demand sales) accelerating
- Warhammer Community "New to Hobby" content consumption vs. "Advanced/Competitive" content — mix shift toward screen content
- Age of entry to physical hobby rising (older cohort average) — detectable via community surveys
- Warhammer+ subscriber count plateauing despite IP investment — if subscribers don't grow, IP engagement is not sticky

Plausibility: High plausibility — digital entertainment substitution is the defining structural risk in physical hobby for the next 5-10 years; there is no sector precedent where a physical IP-driven hobby successfully maintained physical volume growth while digital adaptation accelerated indefinitely; analogies: CCG physical cards vs. digital (Magic: The Gathering Arena cannibalised MTGO and partially physical); board games vs. Tabletop Simulator.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means: rich digital Warhammer ecosystem already built and expanding — ✓
- Motive: hobbyists rationally time-budget toward digital (faster, cheaper, instantly accessible) — ✓
- Opportunity: Warhammer 40K: Space Marine 2 (2024) drove significant IP awareness at scale — ✓
- All three legs → full scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Historical pattern shows video game releases have driven physical miniature sales spikes (co-release sell-through lifts); if the 12-month post-launch trailing physical sales remain elevated vs. pre-launch baseline, conversion is working; management KPI disclosures on new customer acquisition directly from digital channels.

Monitoring checklist:
- Physical miniatures revenue growth in quarters post-major video game release
- Warhammer+ subscriber trajectory
- Third-party digital game licensing deal count and royalty income growth rate vs. core physical growth rate
- New customer onboarding source tracking (if management discloses)
- Competing system community size proxies (BGG user data, Reddit subscriber counts)

Interaction: This is the highest-interaction failure mode — correlated with FM1 (volume ceiling), FM3 (pricing power), and is the structural driver of the FALSE FRIEND ACH profile (below).

Sceptical close: Disconfirming evidence requires demonstrable physical uplift tied to digital releases — not just royalty income growth; royalty growth alone is a FALSE FRIEND signal because it can grow while physical volumes plateau.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:

4. PART B — META-ANALYSIS

Net signal on scenario clustering: Revenue and margin failure modes are highly correlated (5 of 11 scenarios cascade through the same operating leverage mechanism); FALSE FRIEND density is elevated at 3 of 11 scenarios, suggesting the thesis is more dangerous than direct-bearish density implies.

Scenario cluster: Revenue deterioration cluster (FM1, FM2, FM3, FM10, FM11)
- These five scenarios share the same transmission mechanism endpoint: volume/revenue miss → operating leverage reversal → EPS below consensus
- They are not independent — if FM1 (volume ceiling) activates, FM3 (pricing exhaustion) and FM10 (macro impact) are amplified
- The correlation coefficient across this cluster is high; treating them as independent scenarios understates joint probability

Scenario cluster: FALSE FRIEND cluster (FM8, FM9, FM11)
- Entertainment deal + R&D under-investment + Warhammer+ all produce positive-looking near-term signals while embedding medium-term deterioration
