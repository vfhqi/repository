Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Colruyt's core Belgian retail engine remains highly profitable, but structural wage and competitive pressures require careful monitoring.
1.
Dominant market position: Colruyt Group maintains a strong market share of approximately 29.00% in Belgium
2.
Resilient value proposition: The group earns transactional revenues through its "Lowest Prices" discount supermarkets by consistently matching competitor promotions and adjusting up to 600.00 prices daily
3.
Compelling investment case: Profitability is set to inflect strongly over the next 18 months as divesting the structurally loss-making French integrated retail stores eliminates a severe EUR 20m operating margin drag
4.
Unfavourable wage indexation: The single most critical swing factor for group earnings is the automatic Belgian wage indexation system, which adds substantial fixed cost pressure
5.
Muted recent performance: Underlying operating profit for FY24/25 reached EUR 446.00m, a slight decrease of 5.00% compared to EUR 470.00m in the previous year
6.
Aggressive competitive threat: Aggressive regional expansion by Dutch grocery competitors Albert Heijn and Jumbo continues to threaten Colruyt's core customer base
7.
Favorable capital return: Balance sheet strength has significantly improved following major asset sales, enabling management to execute a new EUR 50.00m share buyback program
8.
Mixed first-look verdict: A closer operational look is warranted but highly conditional on whether management can successfully execute self-help productivity gains to offset automatic Belgian labor cost indexation

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The core discount supermarket model provides a highly defensive domestic profit pool, though its lack of international scale creates structural constraints.
1.
Dominant supermarket format: The flagship Colruyt Lowest Prices banner operates over 260.00 stores across Belgium and Luxembourg, acting as the primary transactional engine
2.
Broad multi-brand format: The group's broader retail portfolio includes Okay convenience stores, Bio-Planet organic supermarkets, Cru experience markets, and online pharmacy Newpharma
3.
Defensive transactional model: Retail revenues are highly transactional with cash collected directly at the point of sale, providing a favorable working capital profile
4.
Strong vertical integration: Colruyt maintains its own food processing, bread baking, wine bottling, and packaging departments under the Fine Food division
5.
Stable wholesale exposure: The Wholesale and Foodservice segment accounts for 12.80% of revenues, delivering food to professional customers via Solucious and supplying independent retailers under the Spar banner
6.
Robust price leadership: Primary competitive advantage is the strict lowest-price guarantee, protected by a dedicated secretariat recording competitor price points daily
7.
Superior operational efficiency: Simple store layouts, pallet-based shelf restocking, and unhedged energy exposures keep operating costs structurally lower than traditional peers
8.
Expanding e-commerce platform: E-commerce sales represent nearly 8.00% of retail revenues, driven by the Collect&Go online grocery platform which reaches 65.00% of Belgian households
9.
Consolidated market structure: The Belgian grocery market is highly consolidated with the top five players controlling 76.00% of the industry
10.
Fierce competitive pressure: Entrants from neighboring countries (Albert Heijn, Jumbo, and Intermarche) actively expand to capture Belgian market share
11.
Other side of the story: Albert Heijn has narrowed its price gap to just 7.00% more expensive than Colruyt, diluting Colruyt's absolute price advantage

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is mixed but strengthens as the exit of loss-making French operations structurally uplifts the group's margin floor.
1.
Substantial margin expansion: Strategic exit from France removes a major drag, which recorded an operating loss of over EUR 20m in the prior fiscal year
2.
Favorable profit inflection: This divestment is highly likely to improve group EBIT margins by approximately 40-50bp pro forma
3.
Strategic urban expansion: Acquired Match, Smatch, and Delitraiteur stores expand Colruyt's presence in high-density urban areas like Brussels and Wallonia
4.
Successful market share recovery: Opening integrated Okay stores on Sundays starting January 2026 allows Colruyt to claw back convenience market share
5.
Favorable capital return accretion: Share buybacks and the cancellation of 4m treasury shares in December 2025 will directly accrete forward EPS
6.
Other side of the story: The bull case would fail if automatic labor cost indexation, adding EUR 20m in fixed costs per half-year, consistently outpaces efficiency gains

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is credible and dominated by wage inflation, though the exit from non-core operations significantly reduces operational risks.
1.
Severe wage indexation risk: Automatic salary adjustments tied to the Belgian CPI directly increase staff costs, representing a significant headwind
2.
Unfavorable competitor advantage: Major competitors base their logistics operations outside Belgium, escaping the local automatic wage indexation system
3.
Fierce competitor expansion: The rapid expansion of Albert Heijn and Jumbo in Belgium poses a direct market share erosion threat
4.
Challenging non-core dilution: Non-core retail investments (clothing, gyms) dilute management focus and historically depressed returns on capital
5.
Critical early warning signal: The most critical early-warning metric to monitor is the monthly market share data published by Nielsen
6.
Other side of the story: Aurelius's potential takeover of Carrefour Belgium could trigger significant competitor store closures, benefiting Colruyt
