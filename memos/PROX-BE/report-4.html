§1 Executive Summary

Proximus is a classic incumbent-dilemma case: a dominant Belgian operator with strong domestic infrastructure, locked in a high-capex fibre transition, facing structural revenue pressure from legacy service erosion and intensifying competition, while an ambitious international expansion (Proximus Global/BICS/TeleSign/Route Mobile) has become a material drag on group earnings.

  • Verdict: Earnings trajectory over L2Y is flat-to-deteriorating at the Group level; domestic EBITDA recovery is real but modest, and the Global segment impairment in FY25 erases the domestic progress — this is a treadmill, not a flywheel [#J]
  • Revenue arc: Domestic total revenue ~EUR 4,821m in FY25 (-0.1% YoY), following +3.4% domestic growth in FY24; the FY24 uplift was largely price-indexation-driven, not structural volume recovery [#A]
  • EBITDA arc: Group EBITDA grew from EUR 1,727m (FY23) to ~EUR 1,869m (FY24, ~+8.2% reported) to EUR 1,883m (FY25, +0.8%); domestic EBITDA grew +2.8% in FY24 and +1.9% in FY25 [#A]
  • EPS arc: EPS EUR 1.11 (FY23) → EUR 1.39 (FY24) → materially lower in FY25 due to goodwill impairment and higher D&A; net income (group share) EUR 398m in FY25, -11% YoY [#A]
  • FCF arc: Adjusted FCF EUR 96m (Q4-23 alone), full-year FY24 adjusted FCF EUR 58m, organic FCF EUR 130m (FY25) — tight but improving as capex peaks [#A]
  • Capex trajectory: EUR 1,355m (FY24) → EUR 1,249m (FY25) → EUR 1.2-1.25bn guided FY26; peak capex phase now passing [#A]
  • Guidance delivery: Proximus met its FY25 guidance (as revised at Q3-25 results); organic FCF beat by EUR 30m; domestic EBITDA grew +1.9% vs guidance of "up to 2%" — technically delivered [#A]
  • Market reaction: Stock fell 15-16% on Q4-25 results day (27-Feb-26) on dividend cut and absence of specific FY26 guidance; stock down ~40% over 12 months, reflecting sustained confidence erosion [#J]
  • Sceptical view: The domestic recovery narrative depends on price-driven ARPU growth and cost efficiencies with no visible demand-side volume catalyst; fibre monetisation is slow; Global is a value destroyer; the stock is a yield trap that just cut its yield [#J]

§2 Revenue Growth Profile (L2Y)

  • Verdict: Domestic revenue grew +3.4% in FY24 (price-driven) and declined -0.1% in FY25; Group total revenue EUR 6.54bn in FY24 (vs EUR 6.05bn FY23, +8.1% reported, inflated by consolidation of Route Mobile into BICS/Proximus Global); structural domestic volume trends are flat-to-negative [#J]
  • Domestic residential revenue (FY24): Residential unit delivered +3-4% revenue growth in FY24, driven by January 2024 price increases and broadband ARPU uplift to EUR 48.8 (+4.1% YoY); internet and TV base grew ~2% YoY [#A]
  • Domestic B2B revenue (FY24): B2B full-year revenue +~2% in FY24; Q4-24 B2B revenue +2.1% YoY; B2B broadband ARPU +4.1%; but mobile services revenue -4.4% and fixed voice -7% in Q4-24 — legacy erosion ongoing [#A]
  • Mobile base growth (FY24): Mobile customer base +2.5% YoY to end-2024; mobile postpaid additions moderate; mobile services revenue benefiting from July 2023 and January 2024 price increases but volume trajectory is slow — vs KPN growing mobile ARPU and subscriber base simultaneously [#A]
  • Internet base (FY24): Internet base +2% YoY; fixed broadband ARPU EUR 48.8 at FY24 year-end vs EUR 43 in 2021-22; fibre upsell contributing positive mix shift as ~37% of population now passed by fibre [#A]
  • ARPU trajectory (CQ precommit #1): Broadband ARPU grew ~10% from 2021 to mid-2023 (price tiering + fibre upsell); EUR 48.8 at FY24 year-end; this is a structural trend anchored in price indexation (CPI-linked increases in Jan 2024 and Jul 2023) + fibre upsell — demand-pulse uplift is durable only while inflation passthrough holds and subscriber retention remains strong [#J]
  • Fixed voice erosion: Fixed voice revenue declining at -7% YoY in B2B, broadly consistent with industry structural decline; residential fixed voice similarly declining; this is a multi-year headwind that price increases only partially offset ❌ [#A]
  • Domestic revenue FY25: EUR 4,821m domestic revenue (-0.1% YoY); price increases in 2025 broadly offset subscriber and legacy volume erosion; no net organic growth [#A]
  • Proximus Global revenue (FY25): Proximus Global revenue declined -22.2% in Q4-25 YoY (-17.1% at constant currency); full-year FY25 group revenue total impacted by Global contraction offsetting domestic flat performance ❌ [#A]
  • Route Mobile consolidation effect: FY24 reported total group revenue EUR 6.54bn (vs EUR 6.05bn FY23, +8.1%) was partly inflated by Route Mobile consolidation into Proximus Global; organic domestic growth was +3.4% [#I]
  • ARPU structural vs demand-pulse: Price increases (Jul-23, Jan-24) and CPI-linked indexation drove the bulk of ARPU uplift — these are one-time step-changes, not continuous demand-driven ARPU growth; future ARPU growth depends on fibre upsell velocity and further price indexation capacity [#J]
  • Peer context — revenue growth: KPN grew revenue +2-3% organically in FY24/25; Swisscom Switzerland domestic revenue broadly stable; Telenet (now Wyre) revenue flat-to-slightly growing in Belgium — Proximus FY24 +3.4% domestic revenue growth appears above-peer but was primarily price-pull, not competitively differentiated [#A]
  • Alt-net competitive impact (CQ precommit #2): Wyre (Telenet cable) overbuild covers ~70% of Flanders (HFC/coax); Proximus fibre direct overbuild of Telenet territory ongoing; the proposed Proximus/Fiberklaar + Telenet/Wyre Flanders collaboration (signed Apr-26, subject to BCA approval) would reduce duplication but signals competitive pressure has peaked at infrastructure level; Wyre passes ~3.3m homes in Belgium; Proximus fibre now at 2.6m homes passed — effective dual-infrastructure in large parts of Flanders [#A]
  • Sceptical view: FY25 domestic revenue -0.1% proves FY24 was a price-uplift peak; underlying volume trends (fixed voice down, mobile services flat-to-down, broadband only +2% base growth) offer no acceleration path without a new price event [#J]

§3 Margin Profile & Evolution

  • Verdict: Domestic EBITDA margin recovering modestly on cost efficiency; Group EBITDA margin structurally below EU converged telco cohort at ~29% vs Swisscom ~43% and KPN ~45%; Global segment dragging group margin lower in FY25 [#J]
  • Domestic EBITDA (FY23): Domestic EBITDA declined -1.7% in FY23 vs FY22 — cost base growing faster than revenue; absolute domestic EBITDA ~EUR 1,530m based on reported Group data [#A]
  • Domestic EBITDA (FY24): Domestic EBITDA +2.8% in FY24, reaching ~EUR 1,572m; Q4-24 domestic EBITDA EUR 392m, broadly stable YoY; momentum benefiting from cost optimisation (Fit for the Future programme) and revenue growth [#A]
  • Domestic EBITDA (FY25): Domestic EBITDA +1.9% in FY25, Q4-25 domestic EBITDA EUR 401m (+2.3% YoY); like-for-like basis +3.1% YoY in Q4-25 as opex declined; cost efficiency programme delivering on domestic margins [#A]
  • Group EBITDA trajectory: Group EBITDA EUR 1,727m (FY23) → ~EUR 1,869m (FY24, +8.2% reported, Route Mobile consolidation effect) → EUR 1,883m (FY25, +0.8%); the FY24-25 Group EBITDA growth is almost entirely domestic with Global failing to contribute [#A]
  • Group EBITDA margin (absolute): Group EBITDA EUR 1,883m on total revenues of ~EUR 6.5bn implies a ~29% EBITDA margin; vs Swisscom Switzerland ~43%, KPN ~45%, EU incumbent median ~30-35%; Proximus is at the lower end of the EU incumbent range, partially explained by the lower-margin Proximus Global segment ❌ [#A]
  • Domestic EBITDA margin: Domestic-only EBITDA margin is higher; domestic EBITDA ~EUR 1,600m on domestic revenue ~EUR 4,821m = ~33% domestic EBITDA margin — closer to EU converged incumbent median but still trailing premium peers [#A]
  • Proximus Global EBITDA (FY25): Proximus Global EBITDA declined -9.3% in FY25 from EUR 501m to EUR 445m; Q4-25 Global EBITDA -19.4% YoY (-14.2% at constant currency); FY26 guidance EUR 100-130m for Global EBITDA — a further collapse from EUR 445m implies re-classification/restructuring, not ongoing operational entity at current scale ❌ [#J]
  • Capex/revenue ratio: Booked capex EUR 1,355m / total revenue ~EUR 6.5bn = ~21% capex/revenue in FY24; EUR 1,249m / ~EUR 6.5bn = ~19% in FY25; moving toward sector median ~18-22% range but at the upper end for this revenue base [#A]
  • Fit for the Future cost programme: Proximus has been executing a multi-year opex reduction programme; Q4-25 domestic EBITDA +3.1% like-for-like despite revenue decline confirms cost discipline is working [#A]
  • D&A pressure: Higher depreciation from accelerating fibre rollout is compressing net income even as EBITDA recovers; FY25 net income -11% YoY driven partly by higher D&A charges — EBITDA flatters true earnings power [#J]
  • Swisscom peer anchor: Swisscom EBITDA margin ~42-43% in FY24/25; Swisscom benefits from premium Swiss market pricing, lower competitive intensity, and no international drag; structural margin gap to Swisscom is ~13-14pp — Proximus cannot close this gap without fundamental cost base restructuring or market pricing power improvement [#A]
  • KPN peer anchor: KPN EBITDA AL margin ~45% in FY25; KPN benefits from Netherlands market structure (more rational), pure-play domestic focus, and disciplined operational execution — Proximus structural margin gap to KPN is ~16pp, reflecting cost inefficiencies and the Global segment drag [#A]
  • Sceptical view: Domestic EBITDA is recovering via cost cuts, not via revenue upside; the margin improvement ceiling is set by the fibre D&A uplift; Group margin will be further compressed in FY26 as Proximus Global EBITDA collapses from EUR 445m to EUR 100-130m [#J]

§4 EPS Trajectory

  • Verdict: EPS improved in FY24 to EUR 1.39 (beat consensus vs EUR 1.11 in FY23) but FY25 net income (group share) collapsed -11% YoY to EUR 398m, driven by goodwill impairment on Proximus Global assets and rising D&A; reported FCF improved but only with asset sale proceeds [#J]
  • FY23 EPS: EUR 1.11/share; results described as "in line with expectations"; domestic EBITDA -1.7%, Group EBITDA -1.6%; cost growth outpacing revenue that year [#I]
  • FY24 EPS: EUR 1.39/share, beating consensus; FY24 domestic EBITDA +2.8% and cost programme execution delivered upside; Simply Wall St reported "EPS beats expectations" for FY24 [#A]
  • FY25 net income: Group net income (group share) EUR 398m in FY25, -11% YoY; driven by goodwill impairment charges on Proximus Global assets (TeleSign/Route Mobile), higher depreciation from fibre rollout, and higher income taxes ❌ [#A]
  • EPS implied FY25: EUR 398m group share net income on ~330m shares implies EPS ~EUR 1.20/share, a ~14% decline from FY24 EUR 1.39 [#A]
  • Goodwill impairment signal: Goodwill impairment on Proximus Global is an early signal that the acquisition thesis (BICS + TeleSign + Route Mobile synergy) is not delivering as promised; management's FY26 Proximus Global EBITDA guidance of EUR 100-130m vs FY24 EUR ~501m pro-forma implies >75% EBITDA destruction at the Global level [#J]
  • D&A trajectory: D&A is rising as fibre network is depreciated; this is expected to peak post-rollout (~2028 per management guidance), but in the interim suppresses reported earnings relative to EBITDA [#A]
  • Adjusted FCF: Adjusted FCF EUR 58m (FY24 organic); organic FCF EUR 130m (FY25, beating guidance by EUR 30m, partly on lower capex); FCF generation is barely covering the reduced dividend at current capex levels — EUR 0.60/share on ~330m shares = ~EUR 198m total dividends vs EUR 130m organic FCF — hence the dividend cut [#J]
  • Dividend per share trajectory: EUR 1.20 (FY22) → EUR 1.20 (FY23) → EUR 0.60 (FY24) → EUR 0.60 (FY25) → EUR 0.30 (FY26e) → EUR 0.40 (FY27e) → EUR 0.50 (FY28e); total dividend reduction of 75% from FY22 peak to FY26 guided — a catastrophic outcome for income investors ❌ [#J]
  • FCF conversion quality: Reported FCF EUR 480m in FY25 included proceeds from asset sales; organic FCF EUR 130m; this gap (EUR 350m in non-organic FCF) is a low-quality cash generation signal [#A]
  • Hybrid bond issuance: EUR 700m hybrid bond issued Oct-24 at 4.75% coupon; hybrid bonds count as 50% equity / 50% debt under S&P methodology, helping leverage ratio optics — this is financial engineering to preserve leverage ratios, not organic deleveraging [#J]
  • Sceptical view: Reported EPS is being inflated by hybrid bond equity classification while organic FCF generation is too thin to sustain even the revised dividend; FY25 EPS decline and goodwill impairment signal the Global strategy has destroyed shareholder value; EPS recovery to EUR 1.39 would require both domestic growth acceleration and Global stabilisation — neither is visible [#J]

  • KPN EBITDA margin: KPN EBITDA AL margin ~45.2% in FY25 (up 70bps YoY); KPN grew revenue +2-3% organically and expanded margins simultaneously; KPN is executing the FMC convergence playbook more effectively, with fibre rollout well advanced in Netherlands, lower competitive intensity, and no international drag ❌ [#A]
  • Telenet/Liberty Belgian operations (LBTYK): Wyre (formerly Telenet) operates at ~34% EBITDA margin; operates the legacy HFC/cable plant in Belgium with ~3.3m homes passed; Wyre is the direct Belgian peer — Wyre's HFC network faces fibre overbuild from Proximus, creating mutual infrastructure investment pressure; the Flanders collaboration agreement (Apr-26) signals both parties recognise dual-build is destructive [#A]
  • EU telco sector median benchmarks: EU incumbent EBITDA margin ~30-35%; capex/revenue ~18-22%; ND/EBITDA ~2.5-3.0x; domestic revenue growth flat-to-low-single-digit; Proximus domestic margins ~33% are at the lower end of the range, capex/revenue is at the upper end at ~19-21%, and leverage at ~3.0x (end-2024) is at the top of comfort zone [#A]

  • Deutsche Telekom peer anchor: DT EBITDA margin ~38% domestically; DT is growing revenue organically through FMC convergence and price increases; DT's scale (EUR 90bn+ revenue) makes direct comparison imperfect but its margin expansion trajectory is what converged incumbents aspire to — Proximus is 9-10pp below DT domestically [#A]
  • FMC penetration comparison (CQ precommit #3): Proximus FMC (convergent) customer base growing; broadband + mobile convergent packages are the core retention tool; no specific churn data vs non-FMC disclosed but management cites FMC as key to subscriber retention; vs KPN which explicitly discloses FMC churn uplift benefit, Proximus disclosure is less precise [#A]
  • Fibre rollout pace comparison (CQ precommit #5): Proximus at 42% population coverage (2.6m homes, end-FY25); Openreach ~65%+; KPN Netherlands ~60%+; Proximus rollout pace is moderate; the Flanders collaboration with Telenet/Wyre would accelerate medium/low density rural rollout but regulatory approval timeline is uncertain [#A]
  • Balance sheet vs peers (CQ precommit #4): ND/EBITDA ~2.8x (end-FY25, S&P definition); Swisscom <2x; KPN ~2.5x; Telenet/Liberty historically 4-5x; Proximus leverage is moderate vs Liberty Global legacy but elevated vs Swisscom/KPN; hybrid bond issuance improves optics but not substance [#A]
  • Sceptical view: Proximus consistently ranks below KPN and Swisscom on every financial quality metric (margin, leverage, FCF conversion, EPS growth) and is broadly in line with the weaker end of EU incumbents; there is no cohort metric on which Proximus is a leader except perhaps its FMC convergence push, which KPN is also executing with greater precision [#J]

§7 Sector / Macro Context

  • Verdict: EU converged telecoms sector is navigating a fibre investment supercycle while benefiting from inflation passthrough pricing; Proximus is executing this playbook but starting from a lower margin base and with the added burden of a failing international strategy [#J]
  • Belgian market structure: Three-player mobile market (Proximus, Orange BE, Telenet/Base); duopoly fixed (Proximus + Telenet/Wyre in most areas); market structure is rational enough to support price increases but Proximus does not have the pricing authority of a true monopoly — Wyre/Telenet provides effective fixed infrastructure competition in Flanders [#A]
  • Fibre investment supercycle: European telcos are spending heavily on FTTH rollout through 2025-2030; Proximus EUR 1.2-1.35bn annual capex is consistent with this cycle; the Flanders cooperation deal with Wyre, if approved, would reduce Proximus rollout burden and accelerate coverage to ~75-80% of Belgium by 2028 target [#A]
  • Inflation passthrough pricing: Jan-24 and Jul-23 price increases leveraged Belgian CPI-indexation clauses in subscriber contracts; this is a sector-wide mechanism that boosted ARPU across Belgian telecoms in 2023-24; the passthrough has now been absorbed — future price increases require new indexation events or product upsell [#A]
  • EU regulatory environment: BIPT (Belgian telecom regulator) and European Commission are monitoring the Proximus-Fiberklaar-Telenet-Wyre Flanders cooperation agreement; regulatory approval is not guaranteed; if the deal is blocked, Proximus faces continued dual-build capex pressure and slower rural rollout [#A]
  • 5G spectrum context: Belgian 5G auctions; Proximus holds 5G spectrum across bands; 5G has not been a meaningful revenue driver yet in Belgium — consumer willingness to pay premium for 5G has not materialised at scale [#I]
  • Messaging / A2P revenue secular decline: BICS (now Proximus Global) messaging revenues face structural decline from over-the-top messaging substitution; A2P (application-to-person) messaging revenues are in secular decline — this is the structural headwind driving Proximus Global's revenue collapse and was visible from 2023 results onward [#A]
  • EU capital markets sentiment: EU telcos re-rated lower from 2022 as rising rates increased cost of capital; Proximus with high capex and elevated leverage has been disproportionately affected; dividend cuts in the sector (including Proximus) reflect the mismatch between FCF generation and legacy dividend commitments [#A]
  • Sceptical view: The macro tailwinds (inflation pricing, FMC bundling) have already been harvested in FY23-24; FY25-26 face a tougher environment with pricing normalisation, competitive entry (Orange Belgium mobile share gains), and no new demand catalyst — the sector tailwind is fading just as Proximus needs it most [#J]

  • EBITDA vs net income divergence: Group EBITDA +0.8% in FY25 but net income -11% — the divergence is explained by higher D&A (fibre rollout), goodwill impairment (Proximus Global), and higher taxes; the EBITDA growth headline overstates true earnings quality [#A]
  • FCF quality — asset sales: FY25 reported FCF EUR 480m includes proceeds from asset sales (EUR ~350m non-organic); organic FCF EUR 130m; reliance on asset sales to fund dividends and report positive FCF is a quality concern ❌ [#A]
  • Goodwill impairment — Proximus Global: Goodwill impairment on TeleSign/Route Mobile assets taken in FY25; scale not precisely disclosed but implied by the -11% net income decline; this is a capital allocation failure signal — Proximus overpaid for global digital communications assets at peak valuation [#J]
  • Hybrid bond accounting: EUR 700m hybrid bond (Oct-24) classified as 50% equity / 50% debt under S&P methodology; this artificially improves ND/EBITDA from reported ~3.1x to stated ~2.8x; investors should adjust leverage upward to ~3.1x on a pure debt basis [#A]
  • Revenue recognition — domestic: Domestic revenue is straightforward recurring subscription revenue; no complex revenue recognition concerns; ARPU growth is real and verifiable [#I]
  • Proximus Global revenue quality: Proximus Global revenue EUR 368m in Q4-25 (-22.2% YoY); BICS messaging revenue subject to pass-through pricing; Route Mobile A2P revenues are low-margin, high-volume with secular headwinds; TeleSign revenues dependent on digital authentication market — these are mixed-quality revenue streams [#A]
  • Cost efficiency programme credibility: Domestic opex reductions are driving EBITDA margin recovery; Q4-25 domestic EBITDA +3.1% like-for-like on flat revenue confirms cost cuts are real; Fit for the Future programme is delivering [#A]
  • FMC churn and ARPU sustainability: Convergent bundle penetration supports subscriber retention; broadband ARPU growth via fibre upsell is structural; the domestic earnings engine has genuine quality attributes in its converged fixed-mobile offering and price indexation capacity [#A]
  • Sceptical view: Strip out asset sale proceeds, add back hybrid bond debt adjustment, and adjust for Global EBITDA collapse — the underlying group FCF picture is one of a company spending EUR 1.25bn capex to generate EUR 130m organic FCF; this is a capital-intensive, low-return business model at group level that does not justify a premium valuation multiple [#J]

§9 Sceptical Synthesis

This section synthesises the cross-cutting bear case for Proximus at Triaging stage.

  • Revenue treadmill confirmed: FY24 revenue growth (+3.4% domestic) was a price-event anomaly; FY25 domestic revenue -0.1% is the structural baseline; no demand-side volume growth is visible in subscribers, ARPU uplift, or new service categories [#J]
  • Global is the value destroyer: Proximus spent ~EUR 3bn+ acquiring BICS controlling stake, Route Mobile, and TeleSign; FY26 Global EBITDA guided EUR 100-130m against what was originally a EUR 600m+ direct margin ambition; this is a ~EUR 400-500m permanent EBITDA shortfall vs plan [#J]
  • The flywheel-vs-treadmill test: The domestic business shows treadmill characteristics — opex cuts drive EBITDA recovery but cap out when the cost base is optimised; revenue growth requires price events that cannot be repeated annually; fibre monetisation (the flywheel hope) is progressing at 42% population coverage but the incremental ARPU uplift is modest and slower than expected [#J]
  • Dividend credibility destroyed: Two dividend cuts in 12 months (EUR 1.20 → EUR 0.60 → EUR 0.30) after years of "stable dividend" commitment is a severe governance signal; income investors have structurally exited or will exit on the next cut [#J]
  • FCF mismatch is structural: EUR 130m organic FCF in FY25 is insufficient to fund even EUR 0.30/share dividend (~EUR 99m at ~330m shares) while also reducing leverage from ~3.0x; capex must fall materially and/or EBITDA must grow substantially to create FCF headroom [#J]
  • Leverage at the high end of comfort: ND/EBITDA ~2.8-3.0x (adjusted) leaves limited tolerance for EBITDA underperformance; if Proximus Global continues to deteriorate, Group EBITDA could fall, pushing leverage above 3.0x and potentially triggering covenant scrutiny [#J]
