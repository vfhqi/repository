Brief company description(s)

Group Overview: D'Ieteren Group is a family-controlled investment firm based in Belgium that manages a portfolio of six market-leading businesses. The founding D'Ieteren and Perrier families retain a controlling interest, holding approximately 60% of the total shares.

Belron (Crown Jewel): The group's most significant asset is a 50.3% stake in Belron, the undisputed global leader in vehicle glass repair, replacement, and recalibration. Belron acts as the primary earnings engine for the conglomerate, contributing 52% of the group's proportionate operating profit and 27% of its sales in FY24.

D'Ieteren Automotive & PHE: D'Ieteren Automotive represents 44% of group sales and operates as the exclusive distributor for Volkswagen Group brands within Belgium. Parts Holding Europe (PHE), which is fully owned by the group, serves as a leading independent distributor of vehicle spare parts across Western Europe.

TVH Parts & Moleskine: The portfolio is diversified further by a 40% equity-accounted stake in TVH Parts, a global distributor of aftermarket components for material handling and agricultural equipment. Additionally, the group fully owns Moleskine, a premium lifestyle and notebook brand, alongside its internal real estate management arm, D'Ieteren Immo.

Historical Financial Performance: Over the past three years, the group has demonstrated a robust financial trajectory despite macroeconomic volatility. Adjusted profit before tax (group share) grew 28.1% to €970.8 million in FY23, followed by a 10% increase to €1.06 billion in FY24. In FY25, reported adjusted PBT declined 10% to €955.6 million, though this actually reflected a 3.8% underlying organic growth when excluding exceptional financing charges raised in late 2024.

Belron's Margin Expansion: Belron has delivered exceptional margin expansion over recent years, primarily driven by increasing windscreen complexity and the rising penetration of Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) recalibration. The segment's operating margin expanded dramatically from 5.4% in 2017 to 21.2% in FY24, further reaching 22.9% in FY25.

Near-Term Forward Outlook: Looking ahead to FY26, management has guided for low- to mid-single-digit growth in adjusted profit before tax at constant exchange rates. This group-level growth is expected to be led by Belron, which projects mid- to high-single-digit top-line expansion, offsetting anticipated weakness and normalizing margins in the Automotive and TVH segments.

Plausible 3-Year Trajectory (FY28 Ambitions): Plausible three-year forward performance is anchored by the group's stated FY28 strategic ambitions. Belron targets an adjusted operating margin exceeding 25% by 2028, driven by continued operational leverage and pricing power. Conversely, D'Ieteren Automotive expects sales to remain broadly flat over this period, with margins normalizing down to around 4% as the Belgian new car market cools.

Share Price Performance: D'Ieteren's stock has materially outperformed the broader market recently. As of March 2026, the shares were up 16% year-to-date, comparing favorably to a 3% gain for the EuroStoxx index. Over a trailing 3-month and 12-month period, the stock delivered returns of +23.0% and 3.6% respectively.

Sell-Side Narrative Shifts: The sell-side narrative has inflected highly positively over the last six months due to increasing visibility on a potential liquidity event for Belron. Analysts had previously maintained Hold ratings due to elevated holding discounts and high interest costs following the 2024 family shareholder consolidation. However, reports that Belron's private equity owners are exploring an IPO or exit in the second half of 2026 have catalyzed bullish sentiment.

Target Price & Rating Changes: Jefferies upgraded the stock from Hold to Buy in January 2026, raising its sum-of-the-parts price target to €215. This target was subsequently raised to €228 in February and €235 in March 2026, reflecting a higher assumed valuation for Belron at roughly €34 billion.

Peer Comparison & Valuation: In comparison to peers, Belron is benchmarked against US support service firms like Boyd Group and Driven Brands, which trade around 19.2x EV/EBIT. D'Ieteren Automotive is valued closer to 10x EV/EBIT, in line with traditional European automotive retail peers. As a conglomerate, D'Ieteren Group typically trades at a 30% to 40% holding discount relative to its estimated net asset value.

Company's focus at the most recent earnings

Underlying Group Profitability: Management emphasized that underlying adjusted profit before tax (Group share) grew 3.8% year-over-year at constant exchange rates when excluding additional financial charges. However, on a reported basis, this headline metric declined by 10.3% to €956 million due to a weakening US dollar and elevated interest expenses.

Cash Flow and Shareholder Returns: The company reported a strong free cash flow of €374 million, demonstrating the ongoing robustness of its diversified portfolio. Supported by this cash generation, the Board of Directors proposed a gross ordinary dividend of €2.00 per share for approval at the May general assembly.

Organizational Restructuring: D'Ieteren announced an evolution in its organizational setup by bringing the Executive Committee and Investment Directors together to form a new Operating Committee. Following the departure of Chief Investment Officer Nicolas Saillez, three Investment Directors were promoted to the senior leadership team to maintain continuity in value creation and origination.

Belron's Record Performance: Belron delivered an exceptional year with top-line growth exceeding 7% at constant currency and achieving a 23% adjusted EBIT margin. This strong operational execution fully absorbed the segment's additional financial charges, leading to a broadly stable adjusted profit before tax contribution.

Belron H2 Acceleration: Management highlighted a meaningful margin step-up in the second half of 2025, supported by an additional trading day and a strong focus on cost management. The North American segment finished the year with strong momentum, overcoming an 18-month period of claims avoidance to deliver full-year organic growth above 7%.

Belron Deleveraging and Refinancing: Belron generated approximately €430 million in free cash flow and paid out over €300 million in dividends to its shareholders. The segment successfully reduced its leverage to 4.5x and secured multiple loan margin reductions from lenders, including a 25 basis point cut on the US dollar tranche and a 50 basis point cut on the euro loan in early 2026.

D'Ieteren Automotive Resilience: Despite a 7.8% contraction in the Belgian net new car market and a 13% drop in its own deliveries, D'Ieteren Automotive posted a robust adjusted operating margin of 4.7%. This profitability was sustained by a highly favorable product mix, particularly driven by strong performance in battery-electric vehicles and a positive initial contribution from the scale-up pole.

PHE Compounding Growth: Parts Holding Europe (PHE) continued its consistent growth trajectory, posting a 6.3% top-line increase fueled by market share gains and strategic acquisitions. The segment maintained a solid adjusted operating margin of 9.1% and grew its adjusted profit before tax by nearly 10% year-over-year.

PHE M&A Expansion: PHE actively executed its buy-and-build strategy by completing acquisitions of Top Part in Ireland and AD Freco in Spain. Furthermore, the segment entered exclusive negotiations to acquire a 51% stake in two major Spanish distributors, Polaris and Regueira, which collectively generated €340 million in 2025 sales.

TVH Parts Headwinds: TVH faced a challenging environment characterized by soft activity levels in material handling and agriculture, resulting in flat organic sales growth. The segment's adjusted operating margin declined to 13.4%, and its adjusted profit before tax dropped 26% due to adverse mix effects, higher freight costs, and increased depreciation from prior investments.

TVH Leadership Transition: Management noted that the collaboration with TVH CEO Dominiek Valcke ended by mutual agreement in August 2025. Patrick Lecluyse has taken on the role of Executive Chairman while the search for a new permanent CEO remains ongoing.

Moleskine Struggles and Leadership Change: Moleskine reported a 2.2% organic sales decline, heavily impacted by cautious inventory management within its wholesale channel, particularly in the US. Following the departure of CEO Christophe Archaimbault, D'Ieteren appointed Thomas Boucar and Laure Browne as co-CEOs to lead the brand's next development phase.

Corporate Capital Allocation: At the corporate level, net financial debt was reduced significantly from €908 million to €542 million following dividend inflows from subsidiaries and the early repayment of a bridge loan. Reflecting confidence in the group's financial health, management reinstated a €100 million solidarity-based share buyback program at the end of 2025.

FY26 Group Guidance: Looking forward to 2026, D'Ieteren Group guided for a low- to mid-single-digit percentage increase in adjusted profit before tax at constant exchange rates. This outlook assumes stable foreign exchange rates and excludes any potential impacts from geopolitical tensions or additional US trade tariffs.

FY26 Segment Outlooks: For 2026, Belron is expected to deliver mid- to high-single-digit sales growth with continued margin improvement toward its 2028 ambitions. Conversely, D'Ieteren Automotive anticipates a negative top-line evolution and a material decline in its operating margin as the exceptional market conditions of recent years normalize.

Sell side's focus

Positive Shift in Sell-Side Sentiment and Ratings: Jefferies executed a major sentiment shift over the last 12 months, upgrading D'Ieteren Group from a "Hold" to a "Buy" rating in January 2026. This upgrade was accompanied by a series of aggressive price target hikes, with the analyst moving the target from €168 in mid-2025 to €235 by March 2026. The bullish narrative is heavily anchored on the rising sum-of-the-parts valuation driven by Belron's compounding profitability.

Mixed Reaction to FY25 Earnings and FY26 Guidance: Post-earnings commentary highlighted a bifurcated financial delivery, where FY25 results beat expectations but FY26 guidance disappointed the market. Jefferies noted that FY25 adjusted pre-tax profit of €955.6 million came in 3% ahead of consensus, largely due to better-than-expected automotive profitability. However, the forward guidance for low-to-mid-single-digit profit growth implied a FY26 pre-tax profit of roughly €1.0 billion, which sat 7% below the sell-side consensus of €1,077.2 million.

The Belron Liquidity Event (IPO) Catalyst: The primary strategic focus for the sell-side is the highly anticipated Belron liquidity event, which has acted as the core catalyst for the recent stock rerating. Jefferies heavily focused on reports that Belron's private equity owners—CD&R, Hellman & Friedman, Blackrock, and GIC, who collectively hold a 40% stake—are exploring an IPO in Amsterdam or New York for the second half of 2026. D'Ieteren Group's strategy is expected to focus on retaining its 50.3% majority stake during this transition.

Belron's Surging Enterprise Value: Analysts have consistently raised their implied valuation for Belron as market conditions normalize. Jefferies increased its DCF-based enterprise value for Belron to approximately €34 billion by March 2026, implying an EV/EBIT multiple of roughly 20.0x for FY26E. This valuation aligns closely with recent private equity transaction multiples and bridges the gap toward best-in-class support services peers, which can trade as high as 27x EV/EBIT.

Belron's 2H25 Growth Acceleration: A massive positive highlighted by analysts was the sharp acceleration in Belron's top-line and earnings momentum during the second half of FY25. Jefferies pointed out that operating profit growth surged to 20% in 2H25 from just 5% in 1H25, fueled by an 8% organic revenue growth rate. This reacceleration was primarily attributed to normalizing market conditions and reduced claims avoidance, particularly in the critical US market, which saw 11% growth.

Structural Margin Expansion via ADAS: Analysts remain highly focused on Belron's structural margin expansion, driven by the increasing complexity of modern windscreens. Jefferies noted that Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) recalibration penetration rose to 47.2% in FY25, driving significant pricing power and margin accretion. Consequently, Belron's operating margin reached 22.9% for the full year, with the second half printing at an impressive 24.4%, bringing the segment extremely close to its FY28 target of exceeding 25%.

D'Ieteren Automotive's Cyclical Normalization: Conversely, a major negative focus area is the impending cyclical downturn in the D'Ieteren Automotive segment. Jefferies models relatively stable, to slightly negative, sales for the period through FY28E as the Belgian new car market normalizes. Analysts expect the segment's exceptional adjusted EBIT margins, which sat at 5.1% in FY24, to contract materially toward 4% by FY28E as favorable product mix effects fade.

TVH Parts Facing End-Market Softness: The sell-side has persistently downgraded expectations for TVH Parts due to challenging macroeconomic conditions. Jefferies highlighted that soft activity levels in the material handling and agricultural end-markets caused a 15% drop in TVH's adjusted operating profit for FY25. Analysts expect top-line and margin pressure to persist into FY26, prompting severe estimate cuts of more than 20% for this specific division.

PHE's Consistent Compounding: Parts Holding Europe (PHE) is viewed as a steady, reliable contributor within the portfolio. Jefferies noted that PHE delivered a 4% increase in operating profit for FY25, supported by steady market share gains in the independent distribution of vehicle spare parts. The sell-side expects PHE to sustain mid-single-digit annual sales growth and maintain its operating margins around 9% through ongoing bolt-on M&A and operational efficiencies.

Moleskine's Ongoing Struggles: Moleskine remains a persistent drag on group performance, drawing negative commentary from the sell-side. Jefferies highlighted that Moleskine's operating profit collapsed by 50% in FY25. Analysts attribute this severe underperformance to weak inventory management and adverse conditions in the brand's wholesale channels, particularly in the Americas.

Revisions to Sell-Side Estimates: Post-earnings, the composition of sell-side estimates shifted dramatically, reflecting the bifurcated underlying performance of the group. Jefferies lowered its aggregate FY26 adjusted pre-tax profit forecasts slightly to account for the softer group guidance. Under the hood, this involved slashing the profit forecasts for Automotive and TVH by more than 20%, which was partially offset by a 4% upward revision to Belron's estimates following its strong 2H25 acceleration.

The Persistent Holding Discount: The conglomerate holding discount applied to D'Ieteren Group remains a central valuation debate. Jefferies applies a standard 30% holding discount to the stock's SOTP-based net asset value, which is in line with the historical ten-year average. However, analysts note that a successful IPO of Belron could provide direct market access to the asset, potentially narrowing this structural holding discount and unlocking significant shareholder value.

Deleveraging and Interest Costs: Following the massive family shareholder consolidation in late 2024, which was funded by a €4.2 billion Belron dividend, the sell-side carefully monitors the group's debt levels. Jefferies views the recent deleveraging positively, noting that Belron's net leverage ratio successfully decreased from 5.4x in FY24 to 4.5x EBITDA in FY25. Despite this improvement, elevated absolute interest charges remain a headwind to the group's reported pre-tax profit growth.

Change in leadership

Group Level Executive Reorganization: A significant restructuring of the corporate leadership team occurred at the end of 2025 following the departure of Chief Investment Officer Nicolas Saillez, who had been instrumental in shaping the group's investment strategy over the past decade. Rather than directly replacing the CIO role, CEO Francis Deprez opted to distribute these responsibilities among the existing internal Investment Directors. Charlotte Boucquéau, Timothy Muschs, and Stephan Cammaert were promoted to the senior leadership team, merging with the existing Executive Committee to form a newly established "Operating Committee." This six-member structure aims to empower the investment team, maintain continuity in active portfolio management, and streamline decision-making directly under the CEO.

Recent Group CFO Transition: The group recently finalized a critical transition in its financial leadership, with Edouard Janssen fully integrating into the role of Group Chief Financial Officer following the retirement of Arnaud Laviolette, who served the company for eight years. Janssen brings extensive international financial leadership experience from his previous tenure at Solvay and currently serves as a board member across several of D'Ieteren's key subsidiaries, including Belron, TVH, and D'Ieteren Immo. His appointment underscores the group's focus on enhanced cash generation, capital allocation discipline, and managing the elevated leverage profile following the 2024 shareholder consolidation.

Board of Directors Overhaul: The monumental family shareholding reorganization in late 2024, which concentrated control within the Nayarit branch (50.1%) and facilitated the exit of the SPDG branch, catalyzed a major reshuffle of the Board of Directors. Three legacy directors—Olivier Périer, Denis Pettiaux, and Michel Allé—resigned from their positions. In their place, the board appointed three new members: Thierry le Grelle as an independent director, alongside Olivier Chapelle and Charles-Antoine Leunen as non-executive directors. This restructuring means the board is now composed of a majority of independent directors (seven out of twelve), reinforcing a modernized governance framework tailored to the company's new simplified ownership structure.

TVH Parts Executive Turmoil: Leadership stability at TVH Parts has been severely disrupted over the last 12 months amid challenging end-market conditions in the industrial and agricultural sectors. In August 2025, long-serving CEO Dominiek Valcke departed the company by mutual agreement, prompting the immediate appointment of Patrick Lecluyse as Executive Chairman on an interim basis. The group is actively searching for a permanent replacement who can align with TVH's entrepreneurial DNA while managing sustainable growth and steering the company back toward its 16% margin targets. This CEO vacancy follows closely on the heels of the December 2024 appointment of Carine Van Landschoot as the new CFO, indicating a near-complete turnover of TVH's top executive tier.

Moleskine's Revolving CEO Door: Moleskine has experienced intense leadership instability, navigating through three different CEO configurations within a 14-month span. Following the retirement of Daniela Riccardi at the end of 2024, Chief Commercial Officer Christophe Archaimbault was promoted to CEO in January 2025. However, after struggling with severe wholesale inventory issues and a 50% collapse in operating profit, Archaimbault abruptly stepped down in February 2026. D'Ieteren Group responded by installing a dual-leadership model, appointing internal Investment Director Thomas Boucar and external consumer goods veteran Laure Browne (former CEO of VEJA) as co-CEOs to lead the brand's turnaround efforts.

PHE's Management Expansion: In contrast to the turmoil at TVH and Moleskine, Parts Holding Europe (PHE) has proactively upgraded its organizational structure from a position of strength to manage its aggressive pan-European M&A strategy. In 2024, PHE introduced several new key leadership roles, including a COO for International B2B, a COO for France B2B, a Head of Purchasing & Supply Chain, and a Head of AD Parts Intergroup for the Spanish market. These additions are specifically designed to decentralize operational bottlenecks, support the integration of newly acquired entities like Top Part (Ireland) and AD Freco (Spain), and scale management capabilities across broader geographies.

D'Ieteren Immo Leadership Transition: The group's real estate division, D'Ieteren Immo, also underwent a planned leadership succession in 2024. Steven Boel was appointed as the new Chief Executive Officer in August 2024, succeeding Paul Monville. Boel brings extensive asset management, development, and investment experience from prior roles at Ahold Delhaize, Carrefour, and Mileway, and is tasked with overseeing major sustainable real estate projects, including the completion of the 32,000 square meter Mobilis multi-tenant site.

Belron's Technician Workforce Management: At the operational level, Belron is heavily focused on the recruitment, retention, and safety of its vast technician workforce, which is critical to executing its highly profitable ADAS recalibration services. Management reports world-class engagement scores across its technicians, driven by significant investments in training, safety equipment, and improved job scheduling technologies. The company has also successfully reduced voluntary turnover and made concerted efforts to broaden its talent pool by actively recruiting more female technicians into the industry.

Safelite Cultural and Personnel Challenges: Despite Belron's positive corporate messaging, expert channels indicate underlying personnel friction within its critical North American subsidiary, Safelite. Following an 18-month period of soft market volumes and increased claims avoidance, Safelite reportedly executed major layoffs of tenured personnel in early to mid-2025 to protect margins. Industry competitors have highlighted these layoffs as a sign of internal cultural deterioration, aggressively hiring dismissed Safelite talent and capitalizing on perceived disruptions within the subsidiary's operational ranks.

Corporate Culture and Diversity Initiatives: D'Ieteren Group actively fosters an inclusive, family-oriented corporate culture that emphasizes long-term employee development and care. To ensure diverse perspectives reach the executive level, the group established a "Gen-Z board"—a cohort of approximately 20 young employees mandated to discuss diversity, inclusion, and workplace topics, providing direct feedback and suggestions to the CEO. Furthermore, the central corporate team utilizes "personal development plans" to facilitate internal mobility and continuously upskill its investment professionals.
