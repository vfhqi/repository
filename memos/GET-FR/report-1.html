Getlink SE: Natural Monopoly Infrastructure with Long-Term Concession Advantage Offset by Near-Term Freight Headwinds
Key Takeaways

Exceptional Concession Duration – Getlink operates the Channel Tunnel under an exclusive concession extending to 2086, vastly exceeding peers like Vinci whose French motorways expire 2035-2036, providing unparalleled long-term visibility for capital allocation and debt amortization.

Margin Resilience Despite Revenue Pressure – 2025 consolidated revenue declined 1% to €1,595 million due to ElecLink normalization, yet strict cost discipline drove EBITDA up 4% to €859 million with an impressive 50.4% margin, demonstrating powerful operating leverage.

Persistent Freight Volume Weakness – Truck shuttle volumes declined 5.3% in January 2026, 1.2% in February, and 0.9% in March, signaling sustained market share pressure from ferry operators amid sluggish UK and Eurozone macroeconomic conditions.

Massive ElecLink Regulatory Overhang – A €516 million provision has accumulated for mandatory profit-sharing on ElecLink's excess returns above a 13% IRR threshold, representing an extraordinary unresolved liability that creates significant opacity over future cash flows.

Aggressive 2030 Growth Targets Dependent on Unproven Rail Entrants – Management's €1 billion EBITDA target relies heavily on accommodating 10 million additional high-speed rail passengers, but new operators face severe barriers including rolling stock regulations and maintenance depot constraints.

Critical EES Implementation Risk – The biometric Entry/Exit System rollout (April-September 2026) introduces unprecedented border processing complexity; any digital infrastructure failures could create catastrophic queues and destroy the Tunnel's core speed advantage.

Capital Allocation Tension – The new dividend policy (€0.80 in 2026 rising to €1.00 by 2030) faces skepticism given guided annual capex of €170-220 million through 2032, potentially compressing free cash flow and limiting strategic flexibility.

SECTION 1 - GENERAL: Description and Minimum Criteria Check

Summary Judgment - Business Model and Infrastructure: Getlink SE operates a highly defensive, unique natural monopoly through its concession to manage the Channel Tunnel infrastructure until 2086. The company operates across three complementary segments: Eurotunnel (75% of 2025 revenue), Europorte (11%), and ElecLink (14%), providing essential low-carbon transport for passengers, freight, and electricity between the UK and continental Europe. The core Eurotunnel segment collects highly predictable tolls from high-speed passenger trains (Eurostar) and operates its own LeShuttle vehicle transport services. This asset commands a significant structural advantage over maritime competitors due to unparalleled speed, reliability, and lower carbon emissions.

Summary Judgment - Strategic and Financial Objectives: Management has outlined an aggressive growth and profitability trajectory, targeting €1 billion in consolidated EBITDA by 2030. This growth is expected to be fueled by a significant capacity expansion in the high-speed rail network, with preparations underway to accommodate an additional 10 million high-speed rail passengers over the next decade. The group's strategic priorities heavily emphasize operational excellence, stringent cost discipline programs, and targeted investments to renew key 30-year lifecycle assets. Additionally, the company is actively expanding its high-margin ancillary services, such as Getlink Customs Services, to capture new post-Brexit regulatory value.

Summary Judgment - Capital Allocation and Shareholder Returns: Getlink benefits from robust cash generation, which has enabled a significant reduction in net debt to €3.39 billion and a strong cash position of €1.49 billion at the end of 2025. The company's management has utilized this balance sheet strength to formally reset its shareholder return policy. The new framework establishes a baseline dividend of €0.80 per share in 2026, with a commitment to increase this distribution by €0.05 annually to reach €1.00 per share by 2030. This capital allocation strategy balances the need for ongoing infrastructure modernization with prioritized, highly visible cash returns to equity holders.

Summary Judgment - Peer Rating (B+): We rate Getlink a "B+" compared to European infrastructure peers such as Vinci, Ferrovial, Aena, and Fraport. Getlink's primary advantage is its exceptionally long concession duration (2086), which vastly exceeds the remaining life of Vinci's French motorways (expiring 2035-2036). However, it scores lower than Ferrovial's dynamic North American managed lanes due to structural volume weakness in the cross-Channel freight market. A higher rating could be achieved if Getlink successfully launches a second electricity interconnector (ElecLink 2) or if competitive capacity from short-strait ferries rationalizes, which is a highly plausible scenario given incoming maritime carbon taxes.

Eurotunnel Segment Operations: The Eurotunnel division forms the bedrock of the group's profitability and cash flow. It is split into two primary activities: the Shuttle Services, which transport passenger cars and heavy goods vehicles directly through the tunnel, and the Railway Network, which earns regulated toll revenues from third-party operators like Eurostar and rail freight companies. The infrastructure consists of two single-track running tunnels and a central service tunnel, spanning 50 kilometers beneath the English Channel. Growth in this segment is driven by dynamic yield management pricing strategies and an incentive scheme (ETICA) designed to encourage new cross-border rail routes.

ElecLink Segment Operations: ElecLink represents a unique, highly profitable diversification of Getlink's infrastructure, operating a 1 GW electricity interconnector entirely within the Channel Tunnel. Commissioned in 2022, it capitalizes on the structural energy price differentials between the UK and France. Revenues are generated primarily through the auctioning of capacity to market participants and the provision of ancillary services to transmission system operators. As an uncommon feature for typical infrastructure operators, Getlink is subject to a complex regulatory profit-sharing mechanism on ElecLink's excess returns, for which the company has accumulated a €516 million provision as of 2025.

Europorte Segment Operations: Europorte operates as France's leading private rail freight provider, generating €172 million in revenue in 2025. The segment manages the entire logistics chain, including national and international haulage, local services for secondary lines, and customized industrial junction management. The division leverages synergies with the core Eurotunnel business to promote cross-border rail flows to Belgium and Germany. Recent strategic acquisitions, such as the purchase of Electrofer SAS, have allowed Europorte to expand its presence in high-value infrastructure maintenance and track renewal activities.

Organisational Structure and Governance: Getlink SE acts as the parent holding company, with its shares listed on Euronext Paris. The corporate governance structure separates the roles of Chairman (Jacques Gounon) and Chief Executive Officer (Yann Leriche) to ensure balanced oversight and agile decision-making. The Executive Committee, guided by the CEO and Deputy CEO, oversees daily operations and the implementation of strategic initiatives across all three business segments. The Board of Directors comprises 15 members, including six independent directors and three staff representatives, ensuring alignment with the Afep/Medef corporate governance code.

Concession Details and Timeline: The foundation of Getlink's business is the Concession Agreement, originally signed in 1986 to award the design, financing, construction, and operation of the Fixed Link. Through successive legislative amendments in France and the United Kingdom, the concession duration was extended from its initial 55-year term to 99 years, securely expiring in 2086. This uniquely long timeframe provides the company with extraordinary visibility for long-term capital allocation and debt amortization. Under the agreement, the Concessionaires operate the transport system entirely at their own risk, without ongoing government subsidies or state financial guarantees.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - Weak Core Traffic: Despite management's optimistic long-term targets, short-term traffic volume trends for the core Shuttle business remain highly concerning. Truck volumes fell by 5.3% year-over-year in January 2026, 1.2% in February, and a further 0.9% in March. This sustained weakness indicates that the company is struggling to recapture market share lost to ferry operators amid a sluggish broader macroeconomic environment in the UK and Eurozone.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - ElecLink Earnings Peak: The outsized profitability generated by ElecLink over the last three years was largely driven by an exceptional, unrepeatable European energy crisis. As electricity spreads between the UK and France normalize closer to historical averages, the division's EBITDA contribution is widely expected to decline significantly. Furthermore, the ultimate cash impact of the €516 million regulatory profit-sharing provision remains a massive overhang, as the precise payout rules have not yet been formalized by the relevant authorities.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - Questionable High-Speed Rail Growth: Management's 2030 targets rely heavily on the assumption that competing high-speed rail operators (such as Virgin Trains and FS Group) will rapidly launch new services to rival Eurostar. Skeptics argue that significant barriers to entry, including stringent rolling stock regulations and tight maintenance depot capacity constraints, make these timelines highly unrealistic.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - Questionable High-Speed Rail Growth (cont.): If these new entrants fail to materialize, Getlink's projected revenue acceleration and utilization rate improvements will severely disappoint the market.

Minimum Criteria Check - Core Metrics: Getlink SE is publicly listed on Euronext Paris under the ticker GET.FR, with all financial reporting conducted in Euros. As of mid-2024, the company's current share price closed at approximately €17.29. Over recent months, the company has maintained an average market capitalization of roughly €13.6 billion and an enterprise value in the range of €9.4 billion. The stock demonstrates healthy trading liquidity, averaging over 600,000 shares traded daily.

SECTION 1 - GENERAL: Financials - Tabular, Industry Comparison, and Analysis

Summary Judgment - Profitability and Operating Leverage: Getlink SE operates a highly defensive, natural monopoly infrastructure asset characterized by extreme fixed-cost operating leverage. For the 2025 financial year, the company reported consolidated revenue of €1,595 million, a slight 1% year-over-year decline primarily caused by the normalization of European electricity markets impacting the ElecLink segment. Despite this top-line headwind, strict cost discipline allowed the group to achieve a robust consolidated EBITDA of €859 million, marking a 4% increase from the prior year and yielding an impressive 50.4% margin. This financial performance highlights the underlying strength of the core Eurotunnel and Europorte divisions, which grew revenues by 4% and 2% respectively against a sluggish macroeconomic backdrop.

Summary Judgment - Balance Sheet and Debt Structure: The company's balance sheet is underpinned by a uniquely long-dated and sophisticated debt structure designed to align with its 2086 concession expiration. As of December 2025, Getlink's net financial debt stood at €3,392 million, representing a massive €800 million reduction compared to pre-pandemic levels in 2019. The net debt to EBITDA ratio has consequently improved to 3.9x, providing the group with substantial headroom beneath strict credit rating downgrade thresholds. Furthermore, approximately 50% of the group's outstanding debt matures after the year 2040, offering extraordinary long-term visibility and completely shielding the company from near-term refinancing risks.

Summary Judgment - Capital Allocation Framework: Supported by historically elevated free cash flow generation, management has officially reset its shareholder return framework to prioritize highly visible, long-term dividend distributions. The group ended 2025 with an exceptionally strong cash and cash equivalent position of €1,498 million. Utilizing this liquidity, the Board of Directors proposed a base dividend of €0.80 per share for 2026, alongside a formal commitment to increase this payout by €0.05 annually to reach €1.00 per share by 2030. This capital allocation strategy effectively balances the ongoing capital expenditure requirements of the aging infrastructure with consistent cash returns to equity holders.

Segment Analysis and Uncommon Accounting Dynamics: Getlink's revenue mix is highly concentrated, with the core Eurotunnel segment generating €1,198 million, or roughly 75% of the total, in 2025. An exceptionally uncommon feature of Getlink's financials is the unique regulatory profit-sharing mechanism attached to the ElecLink interconnector. Because the interconnector generates returns well above its initial investment case due to widened cross-Channel energy spreads, Getlink is mandated by regulators to share 50% of all profits generated above a strict 13% internal rate of return threshold. Consequently, the company has accumulated a massive €516 million provision on its balance sheet for this future regulatory payout.

Debt Structure Evolution and Liability Management: Getlink's capital structure is governed by the Master Intra-Group Debt Agreement and features uniquely complex securitization vehicles, notably Channel Link Enterprises Finance Ltd. In early 2025, the company proactively issued €600 million in new Green Bonds maturing in 2030 at a 4.125% coupon. Management utilized the proceeds from this issuance, alongside existing cash reserves, to officially retire €850 million of legacy Green Bonds ahead of schedule. This agile liability management minimizes near-term refinancing risks and locks in an attractive, blended cost of debt in a newly normalized interest rate environment.

Capital Expenditures and Free Cash Flow Squeeze: The group's cash flow generation is fundamentally robust but is currently entering a constrained phase due to elevated capital intensity. Getlink recorded €374 million in free cash flow for 2025, a stark decrease from previous years directly tied to the normalization of the ElecLink segment's windfall profits. Looking ahead, the company faces a heavy "Life Cycle & Growth" capital expenditure cycle, largely dedicated to the Passenger Shuttle mid-life refurbishment program and critical ERTMS rail signaling upgrades. These bespoke infrastructure investments are essential to unlock a potential 20% increase in total tunnel capacity, though they will temporarily weigh on equity free cash flow conversion.

SECTION 2 - COMPANY DETAILS: Revenue Model, Business Model, and Recent Delivery

Summary Judgment - Business and Revenue Model Overview: Getlink operates a highly resilient, natural monopoly infrastructure asset that generates revenue across four distinct pillars: Eurotunnel Shuttle Services, the Railway Network, Europorte rail freight, and the ElecLink electricity interconnector. The core business model leverages the Channel Tunnel's unique geographic positioning to facilitate the movement of people, goods, and energy between the United Kingdom and continental Europe. While the Railway Network collects highly predictable, regulated tolls from third-party train operators, the proprietary Shuttle Services operate in a fiercely competitive cross-Channel market, relying on dynamic pricing and yield management to balance volume and price. Getlink supplements this transport core with ElecLink, which auctions highly predictable, forward-sold electricity transmission capacity, and Europorte, which provides domestic and cross-border rail logistics.

Summary Judgment - Economies of Scale and Operating Leverage: The financial architecture of Getlink is defined by extreme fixed-cost operating leverage and significant barriers to entry. The company's cost base is predominantly fixed, meaning that incremental traffic volumes on the Railway Network and Shuttle Services flow through to profitability with minimal marginal cost. This structural advantage allows Getlink to aggressively target high-margin ancillary revenues and optimize asset utilization without requiring proportional increases in operating expenditures. As the infrastructure is already built and operational, the marginal cost of accommodating an additional high-speed train or truck shuttle is negligible, driving exceptional EBITDA conversion rates during periods of volume recovery.

Summary Judgment - Recent Delivery and Post-Crisis Execution: Over the last three years, Getlink has delivered record financial results by skillfully navigating the post-Brexit trade environment, the post-Covid travel recovery, and the European energy crisis. The successful commissioning of the 1 GW ElecLink interconnector in May 2022 was perfectly timed to capture unprecedented spreads in the electricity market, driving massive cash flow generation. Simultaneously, management successfully pivoted the Shuttle business toward a "value over volume" yield management strategy, deliberately sacrificing lower-margin truck volumes in a competitive market to preserve pricing power and overall profitability.

Summary Judgment - Strategic Shifts and Future Initiatives: Internally, management has completely overhauled its long-term strategy, officially targeting €1 billion in consolidated EBITDA by 2030. This ambitious growth phase is underpinned by an anticipated explosion in High-Speed Rail traffic, with Getlink actively preparing the network to accommodate an additional 10 million passengers over the next decade as new operators enter the market. Furthermore, the company has rapidly scaled its new Getlink Customs Services division through strategic acquisitions like ChannelPorts and Customs 4 All, transforming post-Brexit border friction into a highly profitable, digitalized revenue stream.

Peer Rating (A-): We rate Getlink an "A-" for its business and revenue model compared to European transport infrastructure peers like Vinci, Ferrovial, and Aena. Getlink deserves a premium rating because its core asset is a completely irreplicable natural monopoly with a uniquely long concession life extending to 2086, far surpassing the duration of typical European toll road contracts. Furthermore, its diversification into electricity transmission (ElecLink) provides a distinct, high-margin counter-cyclical hedge that traditional airport or road operators lack. The rating is constrained from a perfect "A" only by the structural vulnerability of its Shuttle business to aggressive, low-cost maritime ferry competition on the Short Straits. An upgrade to "A" would require sustained, structural rationalization of ferry capacity or the successful deployment of ElecLink 2.

Revenue Model Dynamics: Getlink's revenue generation is highly diversified across its segments, with Shuttle Services contributing the largest share through dynamic, demand-based ticket pricing for passenger vehicles and freight trucks. The Railway Network provides highly predictable income through a regulated toll formula that applies variable charges based on passenger numbers and fixed annual fees for track access. ElecLink revenue is secured far in advance through the auctioning of long-term and short-term physical transmission rights, providing exceptional visibility into future cash flows. Europorte generates revenue through traditional rail freight traction contracts and highly specialized infrastructure management services at industrial sites.

Customer Decision Factors and Competitive Position: In the Short Straits market, Getlink competes directly against major ferry operators like P&O and DFDS, relying on distinct competitive advantages to justify premium pricing. Customers consistently choose the Tunnel for its unparalleled speed, high frequency of departures, and absolute immunity to adverse weather conditions that regularly disrupt maritime crossings. For commercial freight customers, the Tunnel offers critical fluidity and just-in-time supply chain reliability, which is further enhanced by Getlink's proprietary smart border technologies and newly acquired customs clearance services.

Secular Demand Trends: The company's long-term business model is deeply aligned with powerful European secular trends, most notably the urgent drive toward transport decarbonization. The Tunnel offers a massive environmental advantage, generating 14 times less carbon emissions for truck transport and 52 times less for passenger cars compared to traditional ferry crossings. Additionally, the growing intermittency of European renewable energy production necessitates robust cross-border power sharing, permanently elevating the strategic value and utilization of the ElecLink interconnector.

Uncommon Information - ETICA-Pax Incentive Scheme: An highly unusual feature of Getlink's business model is its proactive financial subsidization of competing train operators through the Eurotunnel Incentive for Capacity Additions (ETICA-Pax) program. To artificially stimulate High-Speed Rail market expansion and break Eurostar's historical monopoly, Getlink offers direct, one-off financial grants to new entrants to reduce the prohibitive initial launch costs of establishing new cross-Channel routes. This unique mechanism demonstrates management's willingness to sacrifice short-term margin to permanently widen its long-term addressable market.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - Structural Market Share Loss: A highly sceptical interpretation suggests that Getlink's "yield over volume" strategy is merely a convenient excuse to mask permanent market share erosion. Short-sellers argue that the aggressive, low-cost operational models deployed by ferry competitors like Irish Ferries have fundamentally reset the pricing floor in the Short Straits. Consequently, Getlink may never recapture its historical truck volumes, leaving its massive fixed-cost infrastructure structurally underutilized and highly vulnerable to any further macroeconomic deterioration in the UK.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - ElecLink Volatility and Regulatory Overhang: Bears highlight that the recent exceptional profitability of the business model was artificially inflated by unrepeatable anomalies in the European energy markets. As electricity price spreads between France and the UK rapidly normalize to historical averages, ElecLink's outsized EBITDA contribution is guaranteed to collapse, dragging down consolidated group margins. Furthermore, the unresolved regulatory profit-sharing mechanism hangs over the company as a massive, unquantified liability that could severely drain future equity free cash flows.

Bearish/Short-Seller Perspective - High-Speed Rail Execution Risks: Getlink's aggressive €1 billion EBITDA target relies entirely on the assumption that new, unproven High-Speed Rail entrants will successfully launch competing services by 2030. Short-sellers point out that the European rail sector is plagued by glacial regulatory approvals, severe rolling stock manufacturing bottlenecks, and a lack of adequate terminal capacity at key stations like London St. Pancras. If these new entrants fail to materialize, Getlink's projected revenue acceleration will completely stall.

Broker Perspective - Morgan Stanley: Morgan Stanley analysts maintain an Overweight rating but emphasize that the short-term earnings trajectory remains highly fluid due to intense price competition in the Truck and Car segments. The broker notes that Getlink's near-term profitability heavily depends on its ability to leverage its premium pricing power amid a soft UK macroeconomic environment. However, Morgan Stanley views the medium-to-long-term optionality of the High-Speed Rail business as a massive, underappreciated catalyst that could drive disproportionate growth in the highly lucrative Railways division.

Broker Perspective - Jefferies: Jefferies analysts maintain a Hold rating, expressing deep caution regarding the long-term traffic demand for the Tunnel in a slow-growth environment. The broker points out that while Getlink offers attractive inflation-linked cash flows, the company's escalating capital expenditure requirements will likely restrict free cash flow conversion until at least 2032. Jefferies argues that without a clear, near-term operational catalyst, the stock is likely to remain range-bound, heavily reliant on the downside protection provided by Eiffage's significant equity stake.

SECTION 3 - INDUSTRY CONTEXT: Industry Structure, Macro, and Regulatory Changes

Summary Judgment - Industry Structure and Competitive Dynamics: Getlink operates a highly unique infrastructure asset that enjoys a strict natural monopoly over the physical cross-Channel rail link, yet it simultaneously navigates an intensely competitive oligopoly against maritime ferry operators in the Short Straits. While the Railway Network faces no direct infrastructure substitutes, the proprietary Shuttle Services are locked in a persistent battle for market share against operators like DFDS, P&O Ferries, and Irish Ferries. This dynamic forces Getlink to rely on its structural advantages of speed, high frequency, and absolute weather reliability to command a premium pricing yield over cheaper maritime alternatives. Over the last 12 months, ferry overcapacity and aggressive pricing strategies have suppressed Getlink's truck volumes, necessitating a strict "value over volume" management approach to preserve operating margins.

Summary Judgment - Macroeconomic Environment: Getlink's top-line performance is heavily exposed to the macroeconomic health of the United Kingdom and the broader Eurozone, making it vulnerable to cyclical downturns in cross-border trade. Recently, freight volumes have been materially pressured by a sluggish UK economic environment, declining household purchasing power, and structural weakness in European automotive manufacturing. Conversely, the ElecLink interconnector's performance remains uniquely tethered to the volatility of UK-France electricity price spreads, which generated exceptional windfall profits during the 2022-2023 energy crisis but are now rapidly normalizing. Furthermore, the company's dual-currency structure exposes its consolidated euro-reported revenue to fluctuations in the GBP/EUR exchange rate.

Summary Judgment - Regulatory and Non-Macro Evolutions: The non-macroeconomic operating environment is currently undergoing a massive transformation driven by post-Brexit border management and stringent new environmental legislation. The imminent rollout of the European Entry/Exit System and the Electronic Travel Authorisation introduces unprecedented biometric and digital registration requirements for third-country nationals crossing the Channel. Concurrently, European environmental mandates, notably the inclusion of maritime shipping in the EU Emissions Trading System and the FuelEU Maritime regulation, are structurally increasing the operating costs of competing ferries. Getlink has strategically invested in these regulatory shifts, attempting to turn border friction and decarbonization mandates into competitive moats.

Summary Judgment - Concession and Barriers to Entry: The foundation of Getlink's industry context is its exceptionally long concession agreement, which grants the company exclusive operating rights until 2086. This timeline provides an extraordinary degree of visibility for long-term capital allocation, completely insulating the core asset from direct fixed-link replication due to the prohibitive tens of billions in capital required to construct a new tunnel. While barriers to entry in the competing ferry market have historically been lower due to the ability to flexibly charter or relocate vessels, these barriers are now rising rapidly as new entrants face severe port capacity constraints at Dover and escalating regulatory compliance costs.

Peer Rating : We rate Getlink a "B" regarding its industry positioning compared to European transport infrastructure peers like Vinci, Ferrovial, Aena, and Fraport. Getlink benefits from a significantly longer concession life than traditional French toll roads, which face expiries in the mid-2030s. However, Getlink is severely geographically constrained compared to globally diversified airport operators like Aena or Fraport, making it uniquely vulnerable to localized UK-EU bilateral trade shocks. To achieve an "A" rating, the Short Straits ferry market would need to undergo definitive capacity consolidation, thereby removing the structural pricing pressure that currently suppresses Getlink's Shuttle margin expansion.

Differences vs. Ferries (DFDS, Irish Continental): Getlink's business model diverges sharply from maritime operators because its infrastructure is entirely fixed, rendering it unable to relocate capacity to other geographic routes during localized demand slumps.

Differences vs. Ferries (DFDS, Irish Continental) (cont.): Ferry operators possess highly mobile assets, allowing them to shift vessels to the Mediterranean or the North Sea to optimize fleet utilization. However, Getlink's fixed nature provides a massive environmental and operational advantage, generating 14 times less CO2 for trucks compared to ferries and operating immune to the severe marine weather disruptions that routinely cancel ferry crossings.

Summary Judgment - Strategic Risks and Vulnerabilities: The primary risks to Getlink's strategic realization over the next three years center on regulatory implementation, competitive dynamics, and infrastructure taxation. The imminent rollout of the European Entry/Exit System introduces a massive operational risk, as any failure to smoothly process biometric data could create catastrophic border delays and destroy the Tunnel's core value proposition of speed. Concurrently, Getlink faces a sudden and severe tax headwind in the United Kingdom, where the rateable value for its business rates is slated to surge by approximately 200%. Furthermore, the structural threat of unregulated, low-cost ferry competition continues to cap the company's ability to drive meaningful volume recovery in the highly lucrative freight segment.

Summary Judgment - Peer Rating (B+): We rate Getlink a "B+" regarding its future financial predictability and risk profile compared to European transport infrastructure peers like Vinci, Ferrovial, Aena, and Fraport. To achieve an "A" rating, Getlink would need to successfully commercialize its excess High-Speed Rail capacity, thereby permanently diluting its reliance on the highly volatile, commoditized Short Straits truck freight market.
