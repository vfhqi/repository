§2 — REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT

Two frameworks evolving in Elia's favour simultaneously — Belgium's April 2026 draft provides near-term certainty; Germany's RP5 is the medium-term WACC variable that defines 50Hertz's trajectory.

Belgium — CREG 2028–2031 Draft Framework

On April 17, 2026, CREG published the draft determination for the 2028–2031 tariff period. The headline is constructive: maximum incentive ceiling raised to 3.75% vs 2.43% in the prior framework; regulatory certainty extended through 2031.

Key draft parameters:

  • Risk-free rate floor lowered to 1.0% (from 1.68%); beta unchanged at 0.69; equity risk premium unchanged at 3.5%; gearing 40% equity / 60% debt
  • Historical distinction between existing and new RAB removed — structurally positive for RAB growth model
  • Maximum total incentive ceiling: 3.75% (up from 2.43% theoretical limit)
  • CREG's own regulated ROE calculation: 8.37% after corporate tax in 2028, descending to 8.08% by 2031
  • Bernstein baseline (OLO 3.5%): 5.9% base + ~2.0% incentive (53% capture) + ~0.3% MOG incentives = ~8.2% total ROE
  • ING: CREG expected to maintain evolution of existing cost-plus model supplemented by incentive mechanisms

Current 2024–2027 tariff mechanics:

  • Approved November 2023; ~77% cumulative rise over 2020–2023 tariffs; Belgian capex plan €6.4bn vs €1.5bn in prior period
  • Tariff uplift is mechanically flowing through P&L as capex converts to RAB; no discretionary variable
  • Political watch point: Belgian tariffs among EU's highest; FEBELIEC (industrial federation) formally objected to 77% rise; regulated reset in 2028 could face political headwinds if industrial competitiveness remains a policy concern

Germany (50Hertz) — BNetzA RP5 Framework (from 2029)

BNetzA's RP5 reform is the single largest unresolved variable in the Elia investment thesis. The December 2025 draft establishes architecture but WACC — the number that matters — is not finalised until autumn 2026.

RP5 architecture:

  • Transition from five-year revenue-cap to dynamic annual cost-plus approach; OPEX adjusted annually (vs fixed for whole period)
  • Harmonisation of onshore and offshore frameworks under uniform WACC-based remuneration
  • WACC calculation uses arithmetic average return spread (vs prior geometric/arithmetic mix) — mechanically higher WACC value
  • Risk-free rate: trailing average of zero-coupon EUR bonds, 10–20 year residual maturity; gearing maintained 60% debt / 40% equity
  • December 2025 draft did NOT retain explicit equity ROE adder TSOs advocated for — disappointing to sector; BNetzA argues overall package should be assessed; adder could still improve through consultation. Final decision: autumn 2026.

Current 4th Regulatory Period (2024–2028):

  • 50Hertz Xind = 100%: no BNetzA efficiency deduction — at regulatory frontier; significant positive signal
  • Pre-2024 50Hertz assets earn 2.3% base equity rate; post-2024 assets earn floating rate — already materially improving
  • Floating-rate benefit real and visible: 50Hertz adj. NP +42.6% FY2025 to €439m; RAB +27.6% to €14.8bn
  • Jefferies forecast: consolidated ROE rising 7.9% (2023) → 8.9% (2030E); ETB stable 7.2–7.8%; 50Hertz above 9%
