2.
Second cause – dividend blowout: High plausibility. The declaration of a USD 0.67 dividend against a USD 0.50 consensus provided undeniable, hard cash validation of the balance sheet's extreme near-term strength
3.
Third cause – geopolitical capacity absorption: Medium plausibility. The Red Sea and Panama Canal disruptions structurally absorbed the dreaded orderbook, but this is a fragile, politically contingent tailwind rather than a permanent moat
4.
Fourth cause – strategic newbuilds: Low plausibility. While the USD 940 million Panamax order signals confidence, placing massive capital expenditure into a peak cycle usually dampens equity sentiment rather than driving it.
5.
Counter-hypothesis – the terminal trap: The strongest counter to the bullish thesis is that US export terminals are already at maximum capacity; if they cannot physically load more LPG, surging Asian demand will simply crush the arbitrage rather than boost shipping rates

Validation metric – FOB premiums: To confirm or kill the counter-hypothesis, investors must watch US FOB premiums; a sustained spike above USD 150/t will signal terminal exhaustion and immediately kill the bullish shipping thesis
7.
Unexplained residual – the orderbook paradox: The named causes entirely explain the near-term cash flow surge, but they do NOT account for why the market is suddenly willing to ignore a historic 30% orderbook delivery wave set for 2027
8.
OUTLIER CALL-OUT: The market is entirely ignoring the volatile accounting mechanics of the Product Services division, where massive unrealised mark-to-market paper gains are masking a physical realised trading loss of USD 10 million
9.
Decision prompt: The reader should immediately commission a deep-dive infrastructure report on US Gulf Coast LPG terminal expansion timelines to determine precisely when physical loading capacity will cap the current export boom
10.
Other side of the story: The entire 15% re-rating rests on the assumption that the Middle East conflict will indefinitely paralyse the Strait of Hormuz; if a sudden diplomatic resolution occurs, the artificial ton-mile stretch will instantly evaporate, leaving the stock completely exposed to the catastrophic 2027 orderbook.
