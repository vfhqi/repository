Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Gränges operates a resilient, customer-centric regional business model with leading positions across more than twenty highly specialized rolled aluminum product niches.

Niche product segments and regional footprint

1.
Balanced regional setup: Gränges Americas, Europe, and Asia manage regional operations and customer relations, serving local OEMs with customized, high-performance alloys
2.
High automotive exposure: The largest segment is automotive, representing 38 percent of sales volume in 2025, driven by lightweight vehicle production and electric vehicle thermal management
3.
Robust climate control: HVAC applications represent 15 percent of volume, with Gränges holding a leading supplier position for residential and commercial heat exchangers
4.
Stable specialty packaging: Packaging accounts for 16 percent of volume, where barrier properties and recyclability support long-term demand for food and pharmaceutical packaging
5.
Rapid battery expansion: Niche applications in batteries, including cooling plates and cell casings, represent 31% of volume under "Other Niches"
6.
Underutilized plant capacity: Total global production capacity stands at 800 ktonnes, but utilization is currently at 70-75%, presenting a large operating leverage opportunity

Competitive advantage and industry dynamics

7.
High customer integration: Product development is collaborative and customer-driven, establishing durable, long-term relationships spanning fifteen to forty years
8.
Formidable switching costs: Technical qualification barriers are high, as custom alloys must be certified for vehicle and building safety standards
9.
Pass-through pricing model: Aluminum costs, making up 60% of revenues, are fully passed through, protecting operating margins from commodity price volatility
10.
Strategic sustainability leadership: Advanced recycling capabilities and lower carbon intensity act as a commercial differentiator, as customers focus on green supply credentials
11.
Consolidating regional industry: The global rolled product market is highly complex and regionalized, with consolidation driven by trade barriers and environmental regulations
12.
Other side of the story: Cyclical end-markets expose Gränges to sudden demand drops, as a persistent weakness in residential housing construction would directly impair HVAC sales volumes

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Gränges strengthens significantly as it enters a harvesting phase with declining capital expenditure and accelerating electric vehicle battery foil demand.

Secular battery foil and electrification growth

1.
Surging battery foil demand: Battery cathode foil is a high-growth driver, with global demand projected to expand by over 25 percent annually
2.
Doubled European capacity: Gränges doubled its battery foil capacity in Finspång, Sweden, positioning it to capture regional demand from battery manufacturers
3.
Global supplier footprint: Production capabilities in Europe, Asia, and the Americas establish Gränges as a preferred supplier for global OEMs
4.
Formidable technical specs: Cathode foil requires precise rolling to 12-20 microns, a demanding process that acts as a strong competitive moat

Local US market dominance and green aluminum

5.
Supportive US tariffs: Section 232 tariffs protect domestic manufacturers, strengthening Gränges' pricing power and domestic market share in US HVAC and packaging
6.
Strategic US recycling footprint: Completed casting and recycling lines in Huntingdon, US, enable near-zero carbon aluminum solutions with high recycled content
7.
Leading sustainability credentials: Raising the 2030 decarbonization target to 3.0 tonnes CO2e per tonne creates a highly attractive green differentiator
8.
Independent forward drivers: Growth drivers are independent, meaning battery foil expansion can succeed even if residential HVAC remains temporarily weak
9.
Other side of the story: Slower electric vehicle adoption could disrupt the investment case, as lower-than-expected battery demand would reduce utilization rates at newly expanded facilities

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by volatile raw material scrap spreads and geopolitical tariff exposures, though mitigated by local production footprints.

Operational and raw material risks

1.
Volatile scrap spreads: Squeezes on aluminum scrap margins present a headwind, as rising scrap costs cannot always be passed instantly to customers
2.
Foreign transaction volatility: Material currency fluctuations expose results to transaction risk, with Q1 2026 experiencing SEK 85 million in negative currency headwinds
3.
Unexpected machine failures: Severe operational failures or fires can interrupt production, as seen in historical incidents at Newport
4.
Workplace safety hurdles: High workplace accident rates present liabilities, with recordable accidents rising to 57 in 2024 from 44, and a severity rate of 245

Geopolitical and demand risks

5.
US primary aluminum tariffs: Tariffs on primary imports drive up domestic metal premiums, increasing working capital by SEK 500 million in 2025
6.
Delayed Shandong integration: Delays in ramping up the newly acquired Shandong plant could result in lower utilization and a temporary drag on returns
7.
Other side of the story: Geopolitical tariffs act as a structural positive, as they protect domestic pricing and drive customers to shift supply to local Gränges plants
