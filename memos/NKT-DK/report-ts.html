Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. NKT offers highly predictable, multi-year earnings growth driven by a massive order backlog in high-voltage cables, though near-term results rely heavily on flawless execution of ongoing factory expansions.
1.
Pure-play cable manufacturer: The company designs, produces, and installs the physical high-voltage cables connecting offshore wind farms and national grids, alongside medium and low-voltage cables for terrestrial power distribution and building infrastructure
2.
Structural transition beneficiary: The investment case rests on the fundamental requirement to rapidly upgrade and expand European power grids to handle renewable energy penetration. NKT is leveraging a severe industry supply shortage to lock in premium pricing on long-term contracts that will fuel major margin expansion once new factory capacity comes online in 2027. 4
3.
Capacity execution swing factor: The most critical variable for future earnings is the timely, on-budget completion of the company's capital expenditure programme, specifically the massive extrusion tower and new cable-laying vessel in Karlskrona, Sweden
4.
Exceptional recent growth: Revenue measured at standard metal prices doubled from €1.26 billion in 2021 to €2.72 billion in 2025, while operational EBITDA margins expanded concurrently from 10.4% to 14.3% over the same period
5.
Unprecedented forward visibility: The high-voltage order backlog surged to a record €13.5 billion in the first quarter of 2026, providing guaranteed production volume that stretches well into the next decade
6.

7.
Firm initial verdict: Yes, the company warrants deeper investigation, because its unrivalled earnings visibility through 2030 and discounted growth-adjusted valuation present a compelling opportunity for investors willing to look past a transitional 2026

Section 3 – What the company does 16 findings

Section summary
  1. The business operates as a highly capital-intensive, specialised industrial manufacturer benefiting from severe barriers to entry and a structurally undersupplied market.

Core product lines and segment structure High-voltage turnkey provider: The Transmission segment,

1.
which historically accounted for roughly 75% of group earnings, delivers end-to-end solutions by designing, manufacturing, and installing massive subsea and underground cables used for international interconnectors and offshore wind farms
2.
Medium and low-voltage supplier: The Distribution segment produces the standard power cables required by local utility companies to upgrade terrestrial power grids, alongside conventional building wires utilised in residential and commercial construction
3.
High-margin lifecycle services: The Grid Solutions & Accessories division acts as a recurring revenue engine by providing vital maintenance, emergency offshore repair operations, and specialised cable joints and terminations that connect discrete cable segments
4.
Advanced technological capabilities: The business has successfully transitioned toward 525kV crosslinked polyethylene direct current technology, which has become the industry standard for long-distance power transmission because it suffers far lower power losses than alternating current alternatives
5.
Strategic facility footprint: Production is strategically concentrated in Northern and Central Europe near major offshore wind developments, utilising advanced facilities in Sweden and Germany that possess rare deep-water harbour access required for loading massive cable reels directly onto installation vessels

Revenue model and long-term backlog Contractual high-voltage model: Revenue in the Transmission

6.
business is secured through massive, multi-year contracts that are recognised over time based on the percentage-of-completion method, providing exceptional long-term revenue predictability compared to standard industrial peers
7.
Transactional distribution model: Conversely, the Distribution and Accessories segments operate on a much shorter cycle, with revenue recognised at a point in time upon physical delivery of standardised products, making these divisions more sensitive to immediate macroeconomic conditions
8.
Record-breaking order book: The company entered 2026 with an unprecedented €13.5 billion firm order backlog in high-voltage cables, effectively selling out its current production capacity through the end of the decade
9.
Blue-chip customer concentration: More than 95% of the high-voltage backlog is contracted with large, statebacked European Transmission System Operators such as National Grid and SSEN, resulting in virtually zero counterparty credit risk
10.
Raw material pass-through: To protect operating margins from volatile commodity markets, the company structurally eliminates copper and aluminium price risk by passing these costs directly to customers through contractual indexation mechanisms and back-to-back hedging

Industry structure and competitive moat Extreme barriers to entry: The high-voltage cable industry is

11.
protected by massive capital requirements, stringent technological qualifications, and customer demands for decades of proven installation track record, making it nearly impossible for new entrants to disrupt the market
12.
Consolidated oligopoly structure: The global market is dominated by a tight oligopoly comprising NKT, Prysmian, and Nexans, who collectively control the vast majority of Western capacity, ensuring rational pricing behaviour and limited competitive friction
13.
Geopolitical market protection: European grid operators increasingly factor national security and supply chain resilience into their procurement decisions, effectively locking out low-cost Asian competitors from the most lucrative European interconnector and offshore wind tenders
14.
Integrated installation advantage: The company owns and operates its own state-of-the-art cable-laying vessel, the NKT Victoria, which allows it to capture the full margin of turnkey projects while avoiding reliance on third-party marine contractors during peak offshore construction seasons
15.
Other side of the story: The Distribution segment lacks the oligopolistic protection of the high-voltage business, leaving it highly fragmented, fiercely competitive, and structurally vulnerable to pricing pressure whenever European residential construction markets slow down

2.
Premium to French peers, discount to Italian peers: NKT trades at a roughly 13% premium to Nexans on 2028 P/E multiples due to its superior concentration in high-margin transmission cables, but maintains a 19% discount to market-leader Prysmian

4.
Bullish sell-side consensus: Broker price targets range from DKK 1,070 at DNB Carnegie (using a conservative discounted cash flow model) up to DKK 1,305 at Jefferies (using a sum-ofthe-parts framework based on 2030 earnings), implying potential upside of up to 24% from current levels. 56

Market pricing assumptions: The current share price suggests the market is willing to price in a successful completion of the capacity expansions, but remains deeply sceptical about whether the company can actually achieve its ambitious 2030 margin targets
6.
