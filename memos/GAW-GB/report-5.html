1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Games Workshop Group plc delivered exceptional results in H1 FY2025 (six months to November 2024). Core revenue grew +17.3% to £316.1m, comfortably clearing management's "no less than" £310m guidance floor. Core operating profit grew +25.3% to £126.2m, implying core operating margin of 39.9%, up +350bp YoY and well above the company's own 35%+ target. Earnings per share grew +28.6% to 345.0p. Operating cash flow was £169.4m (+27.8% YoY), with FCF conversion above 90%.

The reported group result was flattered by licensing distortion. Licensing revenue fell -46.8% to £14.4m due to the Space Marine 2 base effect (H1 FY2024: £27.1m), pulling total group revenue growth to +11.7% and masking the strength of the core.

The quality of earnings is high. Gross margin expansion is driven by manufacturing scale and mix; operating leverage on a largely fixed cost base is intact. Management's royalty income guidance is deliberately conservative. The balance sheet is net cash. Capital allocation is disciplined: dividends are maintained, no dilutive M&A.

The forward catalyst stack is the most compelling since FY2019. WH40K 11th Edition (Armageddon, June 2026) represents the largest edition refresh in a decade. Amazon Prime series (production phase 2026, air 2028-2029) represents a step-change in mainstream brand awareness. Total War: WH40K (2027, Creative Assembly) extends the video game licensing cycle. Factory 4 investment provides manufacturing capacity for a volume uplift.

One risk deserves active monitoring: the gap between H1 FY2025 execution (exceptional) and FY2026 H1 (11th Edition preparation costs, no SM2-type licensing boost). The transition period could produce a soft print before the 11th Edition catalyst lands.

Overall rating: Green.

2. COMPANY DESCRIPTION (Context for Earnings Assessment)

Games Workshop Group plc (LSE: GAW) is the world's largest producer of tabletop miniature wargames. Headquartered in Nottingham, UK. Founded 1975; listed 1994. Market cap ~£3.3bn (Apr 2026).

Business segments:
- Core: Design, manufacture, and sale of miniature wargames (Warhammer 40,000, Age of Sigmar, The Horus Heresy, Warhammer Old World, Legions Imperialis, others). Revenue ~£316m (H1 FY2025). Sold through ~530 own retail stores, ~8,100 independent trade retailers, and direct (web/mail order).
- Licensing: Royalties from third-party exploitation of Warhammer IP in video games, books, animated series, merchandise. Revenue ~£14.4m (H1 FY2025). Structurally growing but lumpy due to project-timing.

Key financial characteristics:
- High-margin, capital-light manufacturing (Nottingham factory)
- Recurring revenue base from engaged hobbyist community
- Proprietary IP moat: >30 years of Warhammer universe worldbuilding
- No debt; dividend-paying; FCF conversion >90%
- International: ~60% non-UK revenue (Americas, Continental Europe, Asia-Pacific)

Trade is the structural growth engine. At +21.7% YoY and £189.7m (60% of core revenue), trade is the dominant and fastest-growing channel. Web/mail order is growing but not prioritised. +12.8% reflects direct customer relationships and exclusive product releases. GAW does not compete aggressively on digital commerce; it uses web/mail order to serve the most engaged hobbyists with limited-edition releases.

8. STRATEGIC EXECUTION ASSESSMENT

8.1 Factory 4 — Manufacturing Capacity

Status: Under construction. £35-40m total capex. Expected completion FY2027.
Purpose: Double miniature production capacity. Current factory (Lenton, Nottingham) is running at capacity utilisation that constrains unit volume growth during peak periods (new edition launches).
Significance: Without Factory 4, 11th Edition launch volume would be constrained by production. With Factory 4, GAW can meet demand spikes at launch. The investment is capacity insurance for the most significant edition launch in a decade.
Risk: Construction delays, cost overruns. GAW has managed its single-site manufacturing successfully for 30+ years; execution risk is low but non-zero.

8.2 Amazon Series — Media Licensing

Status: Pre-production. Henry Cavill attached (Warhammer Ambassador since Dec 2022). Amazon Studios confirmed series development June 2025. Creative guidelines/style bible published Dec 2024.
Revenue timing: No near-term revenue impact. Production phase 2026-2027; streaming 2028-2029 earliest.
Strategic significance: A successful Amazon series (Fallout analogue) would expand the Warhammer addressable market by 5-10x, driving trade channel sell-through for years. This is the most significant non-organic growth option since the company's listing.
Risk: Amazon cancellation, poor execution, community backlash (grimdark tone). Yellow-rated risk — real but manageable.

8.3 Warhammer+ Subscription Platform

Status: Live. ~100,000-150,000 subscribers (estimate; GAW does not disclose).
Revenue: Small in absolute terms (~£5-8m annualised est.) but growing. Primary value is community engagement and brand stickiness, not near-term P&L impact.
Content: Animated series, vault of legacy material, exclusive miniatures. Quality has improved materially since launch (V1 was criticised). Community sentiment neutral-to-positive.
Strategic trajectory: Not a Netflix competitor. A retention tool for the most engaged hobbyists. Medium-term potential as a licensing content library if Amazon or other media partners want curated Warhammer animated content.

10. SCEPTICAL / BEAR CASE

10.1 What Could Disappoint?

H1 FY2026 soft print risk: The period immediately before 11th Edition launch (H1 FY2026, June–Nov 2025) may see slower sell-through as retailers and hobbyists defer purchases in anticipation of the new edition. This is the classic "Osborne Effect" in miniature wargaming: knowledge of an upcoming edition can pull forward waiting behaviour. Sell-side models may not fully price this in.

11th Edition execution risk: The June 2026 launch is a single make-or-break event for FY2027 consensus. If the launch is delayed (tooling, Factory 4 completion), or if community reception is poor (change in game mechanics, perceived degradation of the hobby), the earnings trajectory could miss by 20-30%. The precedent of WH40K 9th Edition (mixed community reception in 2020) is a live risk.

Licensing lumpiness: Licensing revenue will recover in H2 FY2025 (more favourable base) but the trajectory is lumpy and not visible. Amazon series upside is years away. A period of below-expected licensing could compress consensus estimates.

Multiple compression: At 35x trailing PE, GAW is priced for perfection. Any miss on revenue growth, margin, or earnings delivery would compress the multiple. The entry point matters. An investor buying at 35x who misses on 11th Edition faces both earnings downgrade and multiple compression — a double-hit scenario.

3D printing structural risk (medium-term): Resin 3D printing technology is improving rapidly. The cost of a 3D printer capable of producing hobbyist-quality miniatures has fallen from ~£5,000 to ~£300-400. GAW's legal enforcement (IP lawsuits, DMCA takedowns) is slowing but not stopping the proliferation of "proxy" miniatures. This is a 5-10 year risk to the trade channel business model, not a near-term earnings risk — but it deserves monitoring.
