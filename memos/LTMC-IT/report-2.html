§1 Structural Changes in Italian Gambling Regulation

Net signal: The November 2025 ADM online overhaul is the most significant structural change in Italian regulated gaming in 20 years — a one-way door that concentrates the market to 46 operators and elevates LTM's regulatory moat permanently for a 9-year term to 2034. [#J]

1.1 Online Gaming Concessions — New 9-Year Framework

A new 9-year online licensing regime became effective on November 13, 2025. This is not an incremental tightening — it is a step-function redefinition of Italian online gambling market structure.

Sources: Morgan Stanley (20 May), Press Release (3 Mar), Rothschild & Co Redburn (17 Mar 25, 23 Jan)

  • Licence cost: €7m upfront fee per concession — a massive increase from the previous ~€200,000, which effectively filters out smaller tail operators with revenues below €50m. [Morgan Stanley • 14 May 25; Expert Call • 14 Apr 25]
  • Taxation: Fixed 28% GGR tax rate baked in. A new 3% NGR tax expected in 2026 adds a modest incremental burden, but overall framework provides multi-year visibility that analysts view as a material positive. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 17 Mar 25; Expert Call • 18 Jun 25]
  • Concentration impact: Domain count: 407 licensed domains → 52 concessions. Effective operator count: ~46. Customers from delisted operators migrating to licensed platforms — structural demand transfer that benefits scale players. [#A]
  • LTM's position: Secured 5 concessions through subsidiaries GBO Italy, Betflag, PWO, and Totosì. Maximum permitted market share concentration under the new framework. [ARS • 16 Apr; ARS • 18 Mar]

[AS·Rothschild & Co Redburn]: "As always, an increasing regulatory burden increases barriers to entry and reinforces the competitive position of large incumbent operators such as Lottomatica. So not all of the regulatory changes in Italy are necessarily a negative." — Rothschild & Co Redburn, 17 Mar 25

[AS·Morgan Stanley]: "The new draft framework instead introduces long-awaited nine-year tenders with substantial upfront capital commitments, formal concentration caps and much tighter technical obligations." — Morgan Stanley, 20 May

1.2 Retail Betting and Gaming Machine Renewals — Upcoming (2028)

Draft government frameworks for retail concessions signal a parallel move toward concentration, with material upfront capital commitments expected.

Source: Morgan Stanley (20 May)

  • Timeline: New retail concessions expected to take effect in 2028, with payments potentially staged over 2028-2029. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Betting concessions: Proposals suggest ~400 lots of 25 rights each, priced at approximately €1.40-1.45 million per lot. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Gaming machines (AWP/VLT): Auctioned in €25 million lots (4,000 AWPs + 920 VLTs each). Nationwide machine count reducing to approximately 200,000 AWPs and 46,000 VLTs to improve productivity. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Technical transition: AWP-R (remote-linked) machine migration required within 24 months of new regime; LTM technical testing completing summer 2026. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May; Transcript - Earnings Calls • 3 Mar]
  • Concentration caps: Maximum market share caps apply; LTM expected to bid for permitted maximum in both sports and gaming machine auctions. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Projected LTM spending: Analysts estimate LTM may spend approximately €600-690m to maintain leading market position under the new retail framework. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May; Expert Call • 8 Apr]

[AS·Morgan Stanley]: "New online licensing tender in 2025. The tender for online concessions is already established, with bids expected between 1st March and 30th May 2025, approximately one month expected for processing and a go-live date no later than 17 September." — Morgan Stanley, 14 May 25

1.3 Regulatory Moat — Analytical Assessment

  • Hard regulatory moat established: ADM 9-year concession granted November 2025 — LTM has 8+ years of licensed security. €7m fee + stringent compliance = near-prohibitive for new entrants. No comparable European gaming operator has simultaneously locked in this degree of regulatory protection. [#J]
  • Advertising restriction permanence: Italy's Decreto Dignità (2018 gambling advertising ban) remains in force; ADM reviewing whether to tighten further on streaming platforms and sports sponsorships. Further tightening raises customer acquisition costs across the sector but disproportionately impacts online-only operators without LTM's retail network. [#A]
  • AWP concession renewal risk (2027-2028): Next land-based concession cycle is a separate regulatory track. Government has historically used AWP licence renewals as revenue-raising mechanisms. Machine count reduction + potential GGR tax rate increase at renewal could impair Gaming Franchise EBITDA. [#A]

Sceptical bullet: Regulatory moat cuts both ways. The ADM framework that entrenches LTM's position in 2025-2034 will be renegotiated in 2034. Italian governments have passed surprise gambling restrictions before (Decreto Dignità 2018). A government seeking gambling tax revenue at the 2034 renewal could extract materially higher payments or restrict AWP machine counts, removing a portion of the regulated utility floor that currently underpins LTM's valuation floor. [#A]

§2 Online Gaming and Sports Betting Growth Drivers

Omnichannel customers generate 3.6x higher lifetime value than pure online — LTM's retail network is a structurally irreplaceable customer acquisition engine in Italy's advertising-restricted environment. 2.1 Market Share and Competitive Dynamics

  • LTM's online leadership: 32.2% online market share in Q1 2026 — highest on record. iGaming share 32.2% (+1.9pp YoY); iSports share 32.5% (+0.7pp YoY). Online bets +15% YoY to €8.49bn. [Deutsche Bank Research • 6 May; Jefferies Research • 6 May]
  • Duopoly formation: LTM and Flutter together hold ~60% of Italian online market. Top 2 concentration rising. Tail operators' collective iGaming share fell from 15.5% (pre-concession announcement) to 13% (Q1 2026). LTM captured >50% of share lost by tail operators. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 25 Sep 25; Deutsche Bank Research • 6 May]
  • Flutter competitive sharpening: Flutter's April 2025 Snaitech acquisition added approximately €2.3bn to their Italian asset base. SEA platform migration (completed end-April 2025) gives Flutter a globally competitive tech stack in Italy. Most credible competitive threat to LTM's omnichannel dominance. [#A]

2.2 Digital Migration and Omnichannel Advantage

  • Italian online penetration gap: ~35-36% currently vs 50%+ EU average. Structural convergence toward EU average represents a multi-year demand tailwind independent of market share gains. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 17 Apr]
  • Omnichannel premium: Omnichannel customers generate 3.6x higher lifetime value than pure online players, with lower churn and lower customer acquisition costs. LTM's 17,000+ physical PoS network is a structurally irreplaceable advantage in Italy's advertising-restricted environment. [Deutsche Bank Research • 1 Mar 24]
  • Growth projections: Revenue CAGR 10%, EBITDA CAGR 14% through 2029 per analyst consensus. Management expects online to reach approximately 48% of total revenue by 2027 (from ~45% currently). [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 17 Apr, 17 Mar 25, 14 May]

2.3 Advertising Ban as Competitive Moat

Italy's strict advertising ban (Decreto Dignità) is structurally advantageous for established brands with large retail networks.

[AS·Rothschild & Co Redburn]: "LeoVegas has done no better and continues to be a less than 1% market share operator in Italy — a market that increasingly favours scale operators amid the new online concession regime and where an omnichannel strategy is crucial to further participate in Italy's structural shift to higher online penetration." — Rothschild & Co Redburn, 19 Nov 25

Sceptical bullet: Flutter SEA platform migration risk: once Flutter has its full Italian portfolio on a unified global platform (post-Snaitech integration), cross-product promotions, AI-personalised recommendations, and superior liquidity in sports will be deployable at scale. LTM's tech stack is Italian-built (Betflag + LTM proprietary). A 2-3pp share loss to Flutter over 2 years (31% → 28-29%) would be material to online EBITDA growth assumptions. LTM's product development investment would need to accelerate to match Flutter's global R&D scale. [#A]

§3 Post-PE/IPO Transformation and Financial Strategy

  • Debt maturity profile: Over 50% of debt successfully refinanced in 2025/2026, extending maturities to 2030 and beyond. No material near-term debt wall. [Investor Presentation • 3 Mar; Deutsche Bank Research • 8 May 25]

3.2 Capital Allocation and Shareholder Returns

  • €1 billion total return target: Authorised up to €1bn in shareholder returns (dividends + buybacks) across 2026-2027. Buyback programme: €700m over 18 months, covering approximately 12.5% of share capital. [Earnings Presentation • 6 May; Rothschild & Co Redburn • 8 Apr]
  • Dividend trajectory: FY2024 dividend €0.30/share (~€75m) → FY2025 proposed €0.44/share (~€111m) = +47% YoY. Dividend policy: 30% of consolidated Adjusted Net Profit. [Earnings Presentation • 6 May]
  • Buyback timing: Management noted buyback execution is weighted toward 2027 (less than half in 2026). This reduces near-term accretion impact but preserves balance sheet flexibility. [Transcript - Earnings Calls • 6 May]
  • Return yield (at current levels): At €25.56/share, dividend yield ~1.7%; buyback run-rate yield 3-5% during active phases. Combined cash return yield approximately 5-7% — meaningful for a growth company at 8-9x EV/EBITDA.

3.3 PWO Integration and Synergy Completion

  • PWO (SKS365/Sportbet) synergies: €87m delivered — 34% higher than originally announced target. Full run-rate effects expected across 2026. Online share recovered: PWO brand reaching 6.3% Italian online share by Q4 2025. [Investor Presentation • 3 Mar; INTRM • 6 May]
  • Corporate restructuring: Structural simplification initiated early 2026 — rationalising the multi-brand portfolio and standardising operating cost structures across the merged entity. Serbian PWO branch closed March 2026 (€26m exceptional costs). [ARS • 18 Mar; Morgan Stanley • 6 May]

  • Governance risk: Combined role reduces independence of board oversight.
