Getlink SE: Navigating ElecLink's Structural Reset and Forward Earnings Risks
Key Takeaways

ElecLink's revenue collapse from €558m (2023) to €225m (2025) reflects a permanent structural baseline rather than a temporary trough, as normalized Franco-British electricity spreads settle above pre-crisis levels but well below the 2022-2023 energy super-cycle.

The 2025 EBITDA beat masks significant composition quality risks, with outperformance driven entirely by aggressive Eurotunnel cost-cutting rather than structural revenue growth, while ElecLink suffered margin degradation from regulatory profit-sharing charges.

LeShuttle Freight's 3% volume contraction in 2025 combines structural UK macro weakness with transient ferry competition, though rising environmental compliance costs (UK ETS, CO2 taxation, seafarer minimum wages) should eventually force competitor rationalization and benefit tunnel market share.

Virgin Trains' 2030 entry and Trenitalia's 2029 launch pose a sell-side blind spot as 6-7 million passengers of new capacity threaten Eurostar's pricing power, despite boosting Getlink's tunnel utilization and toll revenues through higher volumes.

ElecLink's €444m cumulative profit-sharing provision represents an unresolved structural margin drag, with final cash settlement terms and timing still under active negotiation with French and UK grid regulators, creating a major FCF overhang.

EES/ETIAS biometric border implementation could strip ~€25m from 2026 earnings through operational friction and delayed passenger processing, with phased rollout at Eurotunnel sites expected April-September 2026.

Getlink's progressive dividend target of €1.00/share by 2030 (from €0.80 in 2026) will absorb virtually all FCF as infrastructure capex ramps, with net debt tracking toward 3.6x EBITDA by 2030 and limited headroom for exceptional returns despite a €1.5bn cash balance.

Key Earnings Cycles

Pre-Pandemic Baseline (2018-2019): Prior to the COVID-19 disruption, Getlink operated at a stable run-rate, generating €560 million to €569 million in consolidated EBITDA. During this phase, Eurotunnel was the overwhelming driver of profitability, contributing nearly all of the Group's earnings while the Europorte rail freight business delivered marginal single-digit EBITDA.

Pandemic Trough (2020-2021): The outbreak of COVID-19 triggered severe mobility restrictions, causing a massive volume contraction that dropped 2020 consolidated EBITDA by €229 million down to €328 million. Performance bottomed out in 2021 at €297 million in EBITDA as border friction and lingering travel restrictions suppressed Eurostar and LeShuttle volumes.

ElecLink Super-Cycle (2022-2023): The successful commissioning of the ElecLink interconnector in May 2022 coincided with historic volatility in the Franco-British energy markets. This dynamic generated exceptional electricity price spreads, instantly transforming ElecLink into a major cash engine. Propelled by ElecLink's €368 million EBITDA contribution, the Group achieved a record consolidated EBITDA of €979 million in 2023.

Post-Crisis Normalization (2024-2025): As European energy markets stabilized, ElecLink's revenue contracted rapidly, falling 20% year-over-year in 2025 to €225 million. However, the Group's historical activities reached record highs, with Eurotunnel EBITDA climbing 5% to €667 million and Europorte EBITDA surging 6% to €34 million in 2025. This underlying core strength allowed the Group to comfortably beat its 2025 guidance with an aggregate EBITDA of €859 million.

FX Exposure and Structural Imbalances (GBP/EUR)

Getlink prepares its consolidated financial statements in euros, but its operations span the English Channel, creating a natural exposure to sterling. A little less than half of the Group's revenue is generated in sterling. Conversely, a substantially larger proportion of its operating and capital expenditures is denominated in euros.

This structural mismatch generates continuous currency imbalances depending on the direction of funding flows. To mitigate this structural cash flow risk, the company actively engages in currency hedging operations. Furthermore, the sterling/euro exchange rate impacts the translation of Getlink's UK-based debt tranches, leading to significant non-cash swings in reported balance sheet liabilities.

Morgan Stanley notes that a weakening British pound acts as a direct headwind to Getlink's near-term profitability. A 2% to 3% depreciation in the GBP/EUR exchange rate mechanically strips an estimated €10 million to €20 million from the Group's consolidated annual EBITDA.

(B) Transient vs. Structural Earnings Setbacks

Section Rating: Orange

Getlink’s recent earnings profile has been materially distorted by acute volatility in its ElecLink interconnector business and persistent volume weakness in the LeShuttle Freight division. Unpacking these developments reveals a mix of permanent structural resets and temporary operational or competitive dislocations.

ElecLink Volatility: The Return to a Structural Baseline

Getlink reported ElecLink revenue of €225 million in 2025, a steep decline from the historic peak of €558 million in 2023. This contraction was driven by a combination of a structural normalization in European electricity markets and transient operational outages.

The structural component reflects the expected tightening of electricity price differentials between France and Great Britain following the unprecedented energy crisis of 2022 and 2023. Conversely, the transient setbacks involved two major physical cable suspensions. Operations were halted from September 25, 2024, to February 5, 2025, due to a malfunction and weakness in the cable support structure outside the tunnel in France. A second preventive suspension occurred between May 19 and June 2, 2025, after a slight cable misalignment was detected in Great Britain. Getlink subsequently recorded €55 million in insurance compensation in 2025 to offset the operating losses stemming from the primary winter outage.

Eurotunnel Shuttle Freight: Prolonged Weakness with Structural Catalysts

LeShuttle Freight volumes have remained persistently weak, with truck traffic contracting by 3% in 2025 to 1,163,124 vehicles.

Structural Macro Headwinds: This sluggish performance is structurally tied to a subdued economic environment in the United Kingdom and prolonged weakness in the European automotive sector. Post-Brexit logistics chain reorganizations and weak British export manufacturing have permanently altered the baseline demand profile for cross-Channel freight.

Transient Competitive Pressures: Compounding these structural macro headwinds is intense, and potentially transient, price competition from ferry operators. Competitors currently operate in a Short Straits market with significant overcapacity and frequently deviate from the social models and minimum wage laws applicable in France and the UK. Despite these aggressive pricing pressures, Eurotunnel maintained its market leadership with a 35.4% share of the truck market in 2025.

Morgan Stanley anticipates that this competitive pressure will prove transient as environmental and social regulations force ferry operators to rationalize their pricing models. Ferry operators face rising compliance costs from the impending UK ETS scheme in 2026 and 2027, higher CO2 emissions taxation, and the strict enforcement of the Seafarers' Minimum Wages decree.

Jefferies highlights that UK reshoring trends and these new regulatory cost burdens for ferries should serve as structural catalysts, eventually driving competitor prices higher and positively benefiting LeShuttle Freight volumes over the medium term.

Getlink faces a complex forward risk environment shaped by near-term macroeconomic volatility, shifting competitive dynamics in the high-speed rail market, and ongoing integration efforts within its freight division. The company's resilience will be tested by energy shocks and evolving cross-Channel market conditions over the next 12 to 24 months.

Macroeconomic and Geopolitical Shocks (Iran Conflict)

Designation: Key Risk (5-10% EBITDA) | Consensus Consensus

The escalation of the Iran conflict in early 2026, which included disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, has reignited volatility in global energy markets.

Energy Cost Pass-Through: The resulting surge in European gas and oil prices poses a direct threat to operating expenses, reminiscent of the 2022 energy crisis. However, Europe's expanded renewable energy infrastructure provides a stronger cushion for wholesale electricity prices than in previous crises, potentially limiting the severity of the impact on Getlink's power costs.

High-Speed Rail Competition and Passenger Trajectory

Designation: Key Risk (5-10% EBITDA) | Sell-Side Blind Spot

Eurostar achieved a record 11.8 million passengers in 2025, representing a 5% year-over-year growth, driven heavily by the resumption of direct Amsterdam-London services. Management aims to facilitate an increase of 10 million cross-Channel high-speed rail passengers by the mid-2030s.

Virgin Trains Entry: The UK Office of Rail and Road (ORR) recently approved Virgin Trains' access to the Temple Mills International depot, a critical step for launching competing cross-Channel services by 2030. Virgin plans to operate up to 40 daily services with a capacity of approximately 6 to 7 million passengers annually.

Trenitalia/FS Group Threat: Concurrently, Italy's state railway (FS Group) partnered with Evolyn to target a 2029 launch on the Paris-London route, aiming for up to 10 trains.

Volume vs. Yield Risk: While increased competition will structurally boost tunnel utilization and Getlink's toll revenues via higher passenger volumes, the competitive capacity influx could disrupt Eurostar's pricing power and near-term market share.

Volume vs. Yield Risk (cont.): The market may be underestimating the execution risks and potential delays associated with rolling stock procurement and regulatory approvals for these new entrants.

Europorte delivered steady performance in 2025, achieving 2% revenue growth to €172 million and a 6% increase in EBITDA to €34 million.

Refinancing Risk and Interest Rate Outlook

Designation: Tertiary Risk (<5% EBITDA) | Consensus Consensus

Getlink maintains a highly defensive and long-duration capital structure, significantly shielding near-term earnings from interest rate volatility.

Maturity Profile: The company faces no major debt maturities before 2027. In April 2025, Getlink successfully issued €600 million in Green Bonds due in 2030 at a fixed rate of 4.125%, utilizing the proceeds and existing cash to proactively retire €850 million of 2025 maturities.

Designation: Key Risk (5-10% EBITDA) | Sell-Side Blind Spot

The market appears to be underestimating the structural margin drag associated with ElecLink's ongoing regulatory obligations and maintenance requirements.

Balance Sheet Solidity & Debt Refinancing

Getlink maintains a defensive debt architecture that heavily insulates the company from near-term interest rate shocks. Approximately 50% of the Group's debt matures post-2040. In April 2025, Getlink proactively eliminated its nearest maturity wall by issuing €600 million in Green Bonds due in April 2030 at a fixed annual coupon of 4.125%. The proceeds, combined with available cash, were utilized to execute an early repayment of €850 million in outstanding 2025 Green Bonds. Consequently, the Group ended 2025 with an exceptionally high cash position of €1.498 billion and a net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 3.9x.
