EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Strategic transformation and pure-play positioning Elementis has successfully transitioned into a

high-margin, pure-play specialty additives leader by executing major divestments and launching the Elevate Elementis strategy, developing distinctive solutions to improve product performance and enhance sustainability while owning and operating one of the largest commercial high-grade hectorite mines in the world. The multiyear portfolio reshaping eliminated commodity volatility through the divestment of the capital-intensive Chromium business in January 2023, the sale of the Talc business to IMI Fabi in May 2025 for $60.2 million, and the disposal of the pharmaceutical manufacturing business to Associated British Foods in June 2026 for €34.3 million, with the pharma transaction lifting group adjusted operating margins by 56 basis points. The highly concentrated pure-play model increases earnings volatility because the group is now entirely exposed to cyclical Coatings end markets and beauty demand, with no commodity buffers.

Business model and competitive advantages Elementis controls high-purity clay that controls

viscosity and stabilizes formulations for clean-label products, organized into two primary operating segments, Personal Care and Coatings. The rheology value chain escalates from raw hectorite clay at $15-20/kg to highvalue bio-actives over $100/kg, with high-purity hectorite clay providing superior swelling capacity and heat

stability compared to common bentonite clay, while hectorite's natural white color reduces the need for heavy pigment correction in premium skincare formulations. Elementis operates a fully integrated mine-to-market model by owning a high-grade hectorite mine in California that boasts more than 50 years of estimated hectorite reserves, guaranteeing favorable raw material cost security compared to non-integrated peers, with the California site representing the world's only commercial high-grade hectorite mine.
