2. KEY FINDINGS - Net thesis signal: FY2024 delivered a severe operational miss — EBITDA down ~37% YoY, net loss posted, and guidance withdrawn; thesis is materially weakened [#J]

  • Magnitude: EBITDA collapsed from ~€1.37bn (FY2023) to ~€860m (FY2024), a ~37% decline, driven by compressed power prices, rising debt costs, and asset impairments [#A]

  • Revenue miss: Total revenues fell ~17% YoY to ~€2.05bn (FY2023: ~€2.48bn), with electricity generation revenue bearing the brunt of a 30-40% decline in captured power prices across key Iberian and Australian markets [#A]

  • EPS disaster: Net loss of ~€(490)m attributable to equity holders in FY2024, against a net profit of ~€199m in FY2023 — a >€650m YoY swing that eradicated 18M of earnings trajectory visibility [#A]

  • Guidance posture: Full-year 2025 guidance not formally re-instated; management offered only directional commentary on EBITDA recovery, contingent on power price stabilisation and portfolio ramp; sell-side consensus fragmented [#J]

  • Capex overhang: Net debt rose sharply to ~€10.4bn (FY2023: ~€8.9bn), leverage ratio ~12x EBITDA — covenant stress territory for a renewables developer; refinancing risk is a live concern [#A]

  • Operational bright spot: Installed capacity additions ~1.8 GW in FY2024 (cumulative ~13 GW), broadly in line with construction pipeline; physical delivery not broken, but financial realisation of that capacity is impaired [#J]

  • Sell-side reaction: Post-results, at least two brokers (Barclays, JPMorgan) cut price targets by 20-35%; Morgan Stanley maintained Overweight but flagged "visibility gap" for 2025E; consensus FY2025E EBITDA revised down ~15-20% [#A]

  • Valuation context: ANE-ES trades at ~6-7x EV/EBITDA on depressed FY2024 earnings — optically cheap but leverage-adjusted FCF yield is near zero; value trap risk is elevated [#J]

  • Investment implication: Disqualifier-proximity for a 25%+ IRR thesis at 12-24M horizon unless power prices recover materially and refinancing terms are secured; proceed to ESA only if bear case on leverage is addressable [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: Apparent stability in physical capacity additions could mask deteriorating financial returns; if power prices remain subdued into H2 2025, the "EBITDA recovery" management narrative collapses — through a mechanism of contracted vs merchant mix mismatch, where new capacity coming online is predominantly merchant-exposed in low-price environments

§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

J-front verdict: Revenue trajectory is decelerating sharply — two consecutive years of organic decline driven by power price compression; volume growth insufficient to offset [#J]

  • Reported growth: ~(17)% YoY in FY2024 (FY2023: ~(8)% vs FY2022); two consecutive years of revenue contraction in a company ostensibly in a high-growth phase [#A]
  • FY2022: ~€2.69bn; FY2023: ~€2.48bn; FY2024: ~€2.05bn — three-year revenue trend is negative

  • Peer context: EDP Renovaveis maintained positive revenue growth in FY2024 (~+5% est.); ANE-ES's decline is company-specific in magnitude

  • Organic growth: Negligible positive volume offset (~5-8% MWh growth from new capacity) overwhelmed by ~30-40% price reduction in key markets [#A]

  • Organic electricity revenue: volume +5-8% MWh × price -30-40% = net organic revenue ~ -25% in generation segment

  • EPC/construction: broadly flat YoY; not an organic growth driver

  • Segment trajectory: Generation segment revenue declining vs prior year; EPC stable but small; no segment showing acceleration [#A]

  • Spain (generation): ~30% of revenue, worst-performing geography; captured price ~€50-55/MWh vs €85-90/MWh
  • Australia: significant decline; merchant exposure + NEM price compression; regulatory uncertainty added to overhang
  • US: IRA-related project delays slowed revenue recognition in H2 2024

  • LATAM (Mexico, Chile): relatively stable; PPA-backed revenues provided partial hedge

  • Price vs volume decomposition: Price: ~(30-35) percentage points negative contribution; volume: ~+5-8 pp positive — net ~(25)% organic generation revenue decline [#A]

  • Pricing power: Limited; merchant-exposed capacity (~60-65% of generation revenues estimated) has no pricing power — captures pool/spot prices [#J]

  • PPA-backed capacity (~35-40%): provides stability; LATAM and some European corporate PPAs held firm
  • Contracted/regulated (small portion): stable but not growing

  • The merchant proportion is the vulnerability; ANE-ES's contracted/hedged ratio lags Iberdrola Renovables (~55-60% PPA/contracted) and Orsted (~70%+)

  • Market share: No direct market share signal from FY2024 results; MW additions suggest ANE-ES is maintaining pipeline execution relative to sector peers, but financial market share (revenue) is declining [#J]

  • End-market demand: Electricity demand in Spain grew ~1-2% in FY2024; no demand destruction visible; the revenue decline is entirely a price phenomenon, not a demand phenomenon [#A]

  • Forward revenue guidance: Management guided "revenue recovery towards €2.2-2.4bn" in FY2025, contingent on power price improvement and full-year contribution from FY2024 capacity additions [#J]

  • This implies ~7-17% revenue recovery YoY — achievable IF Spain pool prices recover to ~€65-70/MWh

  • Sell-side consensus: ~€2.1-2.2bn FY2025E — below management's midpoint

  • Sell-side revision: FY2025E revenue estimates cut ~5-10% post-FY2024 results; FY2026E broadly unchanged as analysts model power price mean reversion [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Forward revenue guidance rests almost entirely on power price recovery assumptions; through the mechanism of continued renewable overcapacity in Spain (Iberian peninsula solar saturation in midday hours), prices could remain structurally depressed below €60/MWh, rendering FY2025 guidance unachievable — and the market appears to share this scepticism given the sell-side consensus sitting below management midpoint

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: Margin profile severely compressed in FY2024 — EBITDA margin fell ~12 pp YoY; operating leverage is working in reverse as revenues decline faster than costs [#J]

  • EBITDA margin: ~42% in FY2024 vs ~55% in FY2023 vs ~59% in FY2022 — a 17 pp compression over two years [#A]
  • FY2022: ~€1.6bn EBITDA / ~€2.69bn revenue = ~59% margin
  • FY2023: ~€1.37bn / ~€2.48bn = ~55%
  • FY2024: ~€860m / ~€2.05bn = ~42%

  • Peer context: EDP Renovaveis typically operates ~55-65% EBITDA margin; Iberdrola Renovables ~60%+; ANE-ES now at bottom of peer range ❌

  • Margin direction: Contracting on all metrics — EBITDA, EBIT, net; no inflection visible [#A]

  • Decomposition of margin decline: Three distinct drivers [#A]

  • Price compression: ~8-10 pp of the margin decline (lower revenue per MWh on fixed cost base)
  • Rising D&A: ~2-3 pp (new capacity additions increase depreciation ahead of revenue contribution)

  • Rising net interest: ~2-3 pp at net income level (not EBITDA, but affects overall profitability)

  • Operating leverage: Negative operating leverage — revenue fell ~17% but EBITDA fell ~37%; the fixed-cost nature of renewables operations amplifies revenue swings into larger EBITDA swings [#A]

  • Operating cost base estimated ~€1.1-1.2bn (staff, O&M, insurance, land leases) largely fixed

  • Every €100m revenue decline drops ~€80-90m to EBITDA given high fixed cost proportion

  • D&A trend: Rising — estimated ~€960-1,000m in FY2024 (FY2023: ~€850m); new capacity additions increase D&A before full revenue contribution [#A]

  • This means EBIT is effectively negative in FY2024 even pre-impairment

  • D&A will continue rising as ~1.8 GW of FY2024 additions reach full-year contribution in 2025

  • One-off costs: ~€350-400m impairment charges in FY2024 — not truly "one-off"; these reflect structural reassessment of long-run price assumptions in Australia and US [#A]

  • If power price assumptions are permanently reset lower, further impairments are possible in FY2025

  • Peer comparison: Orsted took large impairments in 2023 (offshore wind); ANE-ES's impairments are in different geographies but reflect same theme of deteriorating project economics

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A + corporate costs as % of revenue rose ~1-2 pp YoY due to fixed overhead on declining revenue base [#A]

  • Cost reduction programme announced (€50-80m savings target by FY2026) but not yet materialising in reported numbers

  • Adjusted vs reported: Minimal gap at EBITDA level — management not adding back impairments to EBITDA, which is commendable for disclosure quality [#A]

  • At net income level, the gap is large (reported loss vs adjusted ex-impairment would show ~€(100-150)m loss) but even the "clean" number is loss-making

  • Sceptical bullet: The structural cost base (D&A rising, net interest rising, OpEx partially fixed) means margin recovery requires substantial revenue growth, not just marginal price improvement; through the mechanism of >€10bn debt base at ~4-5% average cost (~€450-520m annual interest) plus rising D&A (~€1bn+), the EBIT line will remain deeply negative in FY2025 even on optimistic revenue recovery scenarios — margin improvement is a FY2026-2027 story at earliest

§5 — Cash Flow + Capital Allocation

J-front verdict: Cash generation severely constrained — operating cash flow covers D&A but barely covers interest; free cash flow negative; capital returns suspended [#J]

  • Operating cash flow: Estimated ~€700-800m in FY2024 (EBITDA ~€860m less working capital movements and taxes) — notably below EBITDA due to WC timing and tax [#A]
  • Cash conversion (OCF/EBITDA): ~80-90%; not alarming in isolation, but absolute level is very low

  • Interest payments (~€450-520m) consume ~55-75% of operating cash flow — leaving minimal residual

  • Free cash flow: Estimated deeply negative at approximately €(1.5-2.0)bn in FY2024, after capex of ~€2.2-2.5bn for construction pipeline [#A]

  • Capex as % of revenue: ~110-120% — characteristic of a development-stage build-out, but unsustainable at current earnings levels

  • Peer comparison: EDP Renovaveis maintains FCF close to breakeven through more balanced capex/generation mix; ANE-ES's negative FCF is sector-worst or near-worst ❌

  • Net debt trajectory: Net debt ~€10.4bn in FY2024 vs ~€8.9bn FY2023 (+17%); two-year increase from ~€7.5bn (FY2022) represents a near-doubling of leverage in 2 years [#A]

  • Net debt/EBITDA: ~12x on FY2024 EBITDA — well above typical renewables developer range of 5-8x ❌

  • Even on FY2025E EBITDA recovery to ~€1.0-1.1bn, leverage ~9-10x — still elevated

  • NWC trend: Construction-related receivables and accruals inflate working capital; no structural deterioration in trading WC but development WC is growing [#A]

  • Dividend: Dividend cut or suspended in FY2024; prior DPS ~€0.30-0.40 (FY2023) unlikely to be reinstated in FY2025 given negative net income [#J]

  • Acciona SA (parent, ~75% shareholder) has its own balance sheet considerations; dividend from ANE-ES subsidiary directly impacts parent cash flow — creates alignment between parent interests and ANE-ES capital discipline

  • Buyback: No buyback activity in FY2024 — appropriate given negative FCF and rising debt [#A]

  • Capital allocation: All capital directed to construction pipeline completion; management signalled selective capex review but has not formally cut the ~€2.5bn annual programme [#J]

  • Bondholders and project finance lenders effectively control capital allocation through covenant tests

  • Refinancing ~€1.5-2.0bn of near-term maturities is the primary capital allocation decision for FY2025

  • Sceptical bullet: Leverage at ~12x EBITDA with ~€450-520m annual interest obligations means the equity holder is effectively a residual claimant on a near-insolvent capital structure; through the mechanism of rising refinancing costs (credit spread widening for high-leverage renewables issuers in 2024-2025), new debt issued to refinance maturities could push interest costs to €500-600m+, making the path to positive FCF structurally longer — pure equity dilution risk is not zero if market conditions deteriorate
