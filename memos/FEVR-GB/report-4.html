§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Verdict: The strongest bear case is that FEVR-GB's recovery is narrowly input-cost-driven, the US market position is less defensible than management implies, and the stock is priced for a recovery that may prove slower and shallower than consensus [#J]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: If gross margin stalls at ~50% (mix headwind from US growth + input cost tailwind exhausted) and US revenue growth disappoints, FY2026 adj. EPS of ~48-55p (consensus) becomes ~38-42p — implying ~20-25% consensus downside from a single miss [#J]

  • Sub: At a P/E of ~30-35x (typical for recovering premium FMCG), a 20% EPS cut translates to a 20-30% stock price decline — material downside from the status quo

  • Single largest risk: The US market is the single largest risk — it is simultaneously the growth engine, the margin recovery driver, and the least-understood segment; no peer has comparable US exposure, so consensus forecasts for the US are structurally less reliable [#J]

  • Sub: FEVR-GB's US distribution model (partnership with Southern Glazer's and others) means management has limited real-time visibility on sell-through vs sell-in — a structural information asymmetry

  • Early warning signals: Working capital days elevated (§8); NWC days above peer median (§8); US revenue as % of total growing but at below-group-average margin (§2, §3); guidance conservatism that may reflect genuine uncertainty rather than deliberate sandbagging (§5) [#J]

  • Sub: None of these signals individually is alarming; in combination they form a pattern of a business operating at the edge of its operational visibility

  • Breaking points — margin: If US logistics costs re-inflect (ocean freight, glass), group gross margin could retrace 3-4pp in a single year — erasing the FY2024 recovery in one reporting cycle [#J]
