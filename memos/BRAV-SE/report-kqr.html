Section 3 – Sweden organizational restructuring and SEK 65m self-help run-rate 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Bravida's Swedish division is undergoing a major structural consolidation expected to yield annual cost savings of SEK 65m.
  2. Restructuring charges will peak in 2026 at up to SEK 100m, but Q1 2026 order intake grew by 16% as northern demand offsets southern weakness.

Operational restructuring and cost-saving dynamics

1.
Structural consolidation: Bravida merged its North, Central, and South divisions into a single unified Swedish entity on 1 January 2026 to capture scale advantages
2.
Leadership transition: Lars Tauber has been appointed to lead the unified Swedish business, following the retirement of former Division North head Thommy Lundmark
3.
Financial charge profiling: The total program cost is now estimated at SEK 90m to 100m, with SEK 20m recognized in Q4 2025 and SEK 20m in Q1 2026
4.
Phasing of charges: Management expects the remaining SEK 50m to 60m of restructuring charges to impact EBITA primarily during the second and third quarters of 2026
5.
Cost reduction efficiency: Deutsche Bank notes that adjusting for the SEK 20m restructuring charge in Q1 2026, Swedish cost discipline was exceptionally strong
6.
Broker margin projections: DNB Carnegie projects that these self-help measures will help stabilize underlying Swedish margins, which deteriorated throughout the second half of 2025

Regional demand trends and order book expansion

7.
Order acceleration: Swedish order intake increased by 16% in Q1 2026 to reach SEK 4,009m, compared to SEK 3,442m in Q1 2025
8.
Key contract wins: The Q1 2026 order book expansion was driven by a major SEK 200m contract with EcoDataCenter in Borlange for power and lighting installations
9.
Geographic demand divergence: Northern Sweden continues to show strong industrial activity, while southern Sweden experiences a gradual recovery with persistent pricing pressure
10.
Non-consensus backlog view: Deutsche Bank highlights that although Swedish order intake rose 16% in Q1 2026, the overall Swedish order backlog fell slightly to SEK 8,278m from SEK 8,326m YoY
11.
Under-the-radar branch actions: Management's strategic decision to close several underperforming branches in southern Sweden has temporarily reduced high-margin service sales to prioritize overall profitability
12.
Recovery trajectory risks: DNB Carnegie cautions that despite the positive order momentum, a lower number of full-time employees in Sweden could constrain organic revenue growth in the near term
13.
Other side of the story: While the restructuring aims to deliver SEK 65m in annual savings, the upward revision of total restructuring costs to SEK 100m and a 4% decline in full-time employees could choke off capacity just as Swedish construction demand begins to recover

Root causes of operational margin degradation

1.
Severe margin compression: The Finnish EBITA margin fell to a record low of 0.6% in the third quarter of 2025, representing a sharp decline from the 5.1% margin recorded in the prior year period
2.
Isolated branch write-downs: Profitability in the third quarter of 2025 was primarily damaged by project write-downs related to two specific contracts within a single underperforming branch
3.
Depressed installation demand: Total sales in Finland fell by 18% in the third quarter of 2025 as the installation business contracted, compounding the impact of low operational volume leverage
4.
Persistent margin drag: Although the fourth quarter of 2025 rebounded to a 3.4% EBITA margin, the full-year margin of 2.1% remained well below the historical level of 4.5% achieved in 2024
5.
Cautious broker assessment: Deutsche Bank notes that the Finnish market remains fundamentally weak with installation volumes under significant pressure, especially in industrial regions
6.
Weak backlog visibility: Carnegie highlights that the order book for the Finnish segment was exceptionally thin during the first half of 2025, which delayed any rapid top-line recovery

8.
Operational cost adjustment: The Finnish division is continuously resizing its workforce and G&A structure to adapt capacity to the weak demand environment
9.
Strategic service focus: Bravida has introduced new line renovation models in Helsinki to expand high-margin service revenue and provide a stabilizing base against volatile installation contracts
10.
Strategic bolt-on acquisitions: Growth was supported by the acquisition of TS Sahkotekniikka Oy on 1 July 2025, which added SEK 45m in annual sales and specialized electrical capabilities
11.
Strong backlog buildup: The division's order backlog grew by 57% year-on-year to reach SEK 1,619m in the first quarter of 2026, providing solid pipeline support for the late 2026 recovery timeline
12.
Gradual margin progression: DNB Carnegie expects the implementation of the standardized Swedish operating framework to drive a gradual, multi-year recovery toward the group's target levels
13.
Extended recovery timeline: Deutsche Bank projects that despite positive order dynamics, continuing high energy costs and local weakness will limit margin expansion during the first half of 2026
14.
Other side of the story: While management is confident in a swift margin rebound to 4.5% by utilizing a strong backlog, the low margin of 1.3% in the first quarter of 2026 proves that prolonged industrial weakness and the finalization of written-down projects continue to dilute Finnish profitability
