Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Clariant has transitioned to purely specialty chemicals but remains highly exposed to cyclical end-market pressures. [ ]

Segment Split and Value Chain Positioning

1.
Personal care dominance: The Care Chemicals business generates 54% of sales, serving highly attractive and low-cyclicality personal and home care applications [ ]
2.
Industrial care applications: Care Chemicals also serves mining, crop solutions, and aviation, utilizing a transactional model with formula-based raw material pricing [ ]
3.
Catalyst premium economics: The Catalysts segment provides proprietary process technologies to petrochemical refineries, achieving an excellent 20.1% EBITDA margin in FY25 [ ]
4.
Catalyst project cycle: Catalyst revenue is cyclical and project-driven, with refills dependent on customer operating rates and maintenance schedules [ ]
5.
Additives structural demand: Adsorbents & Additives manufactures bentonite-based cargo protection and halogen-free flame retardants for electric vehicles [ ]
6.
Additives financial profile: The Adsorbents & Additives unit generated sales of CHF 987m and a 16.0% EBITDA margin in FY25 [ ]
7.
Customer value-add: Sits in a profit-favoured positioning, providing customized formulations that yield up to 25% customer cost savings [ ]
8.
Low customer concentration: Customers are highly fragmented across diversified global markets, with no single account presenting a material concentration risk [ ]

Competitive Moat and Industry Structure

9.
Strong tech moat: Moat is secured by high switching costs, specialized technical services, and persistent R&D at 3% of sales [ ]
10.
Consolidated chemical structure: Competes in a consolidated global specialty chemicals sector against high-barrier peers like Croda, Evonik, and Ashland [ ]
11.
Localized production footprint: Operates a regional localized strategy, with 16 key manufacturing sites in Care Chemicals to optimize delivery speed [ ]
12.
Sustainable product advantage: Focuses on sustainable solutions, aiming to have over 80% of its portfolio rated sustainability-positive by 2030 [ ]
13.
Other side of the story: Competitive advantages could be eroded as low-cost Chinese capacity expansions and agile asset-light distributors like Mstack pressure legacy pricing [ ]

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is highly mixed as powerful self-help cost savings conflict with severe geopolitical and volume headwinds. [ ]

Cost Efficiency and Asset Integration Drivers

1.
Cost program risk: This self-help driver could fail if cash restructuring costs balloon beyond the planned CHF 75m total expense [ ]
2.
Lucas Meyer integration: High-margin active cosmetics formulations from Lucas Meyer are expected to drive meaningful Care Chemicals margin expansion [ ]
3.
Integration execution risk: Lucas Meyer's accretion could fail to materialize if overstocked personal care channels delay the volume recovery [ ; ]

Volume Recovery and Structural Market Drivers

4.
Green additives expansion: Strong demand for halogen-free flame retardants from Daya Bay and U.S. biofuel purification adsorbents represent promising multi-year top-line drivers [ ; ]
5.
Additives macro risk: This volume driver would fail if global automotive demand or construction activity suffers a protracted slump [ ]
6.
Post-conflict catalyst bounce: Reopening of local routes and operating rate normalization in the Middle East could quickly restore premium Catalyst refill volumes [ ]
7.
Catalyst volume risk: Refills could remain suppressed if Middle East tensions prolong customer plant shutdowns, extending force majeure situations through 2026 [ ]
8.
Compound probability constraints: To achieve a 25%+ IRR, all drivers must fire together, but compound success is highly unlikely due to independent operational risks Other side of the story: Persistent raw material, energy, and logistics inflation could trigger downstream demand destruction in H2 2026, erasing volume gains [ ]

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk landscape is highly concentrated in a multi-billion euro cartel litigation and geopolitical bottlenecks that directly threaten earnings stability. [ ; ]

Legal Overhang and Cartel Damage Claims

1.
Massive litigation exposure: Major buyers have filed civil claims totaling €9.3bn (~CHF 8.5bn) following the 2020 cartel ruling [ ]
2.
Key litigation claimants: Shell claims €1.0bn, BASF claims €1.4bn, TotalEnergies claims €625m, and LyondellBasell claims €1.6bn [ ]
3.
Other active claimants: Dow Europe claims €767m, OMV claims €1.0bn, BP claims €1.1bn, and ExxonMobil claims €860m [ ]
4.
Tail-end claimants: MOL Group claims €548m, while Braskem claims €402m, altogether bringing cumulative litigation to a critical level [ ]
5.
Zero balance sheet protection: Clariant has booked zero provisions, exposing the balance sheet to disastrous capital erosion if damages are awarded [ ]

Geopolitical and Operational Risks

6.
Strait of Hormuz exposure: Key chemical feedstocks pass through the Strait, exposing Clariant to severe supply and logistics disruptions [ ]
7.
Catalyst volume hit: Over 44 customer force majeure events delayed Catalyst refill orders in Q1 2026, causing a 50.8% drop in division EBITDA [ ; ]
8.
False pricing signal: Stable pricing (+0.4% in Catalysts) is a false friend, masking a severe 2.0% volume decline [ ]
9.
Key warning metric: Investors must closely monitor global customer force majeure declarations, currently at 88 in the industry [ ]
10.
Other side of the story: Risks are overstated if Clariant successfully defends all claims or secures a negligible consolidated settlement [ ]
