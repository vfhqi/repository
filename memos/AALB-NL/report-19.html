3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — Failure Narrative 1: Construction Cycle Volume Collapse

Narrative: European residential and commercial construction activity contracts sharply through 2026-27 under sustained high interest rates; AALB's Climate & Fluid Controls segment (HVAC, radiant heating, building services fittings) loses 15-20% volume; EBITA margin compresses 200-300bps as high-fixed-cost machining capacity is under-absorbed. Transmission mechanism: volume decline → under-absorption of fixed manufacturing overhead → EBITA margin contraction → EPS miss of 20-30% vs consensus.

Early warning signals:
- European residential building permits data (Eurostat) declining >15% YoY for two consecutive quarters
- AALB Climate & Fluid Controls organic growth turning negative in any reported quarter
- Management commentary shifting from "volume recovery in H2" to "market conditions remain challenging" language
- Competitor Watts Water Technologies or IMI plc issuing profit warnings on European HVAC end-markets
- AALB order intake/backlog disclosures (where available) showing deterioration ahead of revenue

Plausibility: High plausibility — European construction output contracted materially in 2023-24 and interest rate normalisation is only partial; residential construction in Germany, Netherlands, France remains 20-30% below 2021 peak by starts volume; peer precedent from 2008-09 cycle shows 25-35% segment revenue declines for comparable fitting suppliers

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — macro interest-rate environment provides direct mechanism for volume contraction
- Motive ✅ — AALB has no structural ability to prevent end-market volume declines
- Opportunity ✅ — construction cycle exposure is structural and ~35-40% of group revenue
- All three legs aligned — full failure scenario, not watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: Strong disconfirming would be: AALB Climate & Fluid Controls organic growth sustaining positive through mid-2026 despite weak permits data; successful geographic mix shift toward Middle East/North Africa infrastructure offsetting European construction weakness; or evidence that AALB's HVAC content per unit (heat-pump retrofit fittings) is growing faster than starts data implies.

Monitoring checklist:
- Eurostat monthly residential permits (Germany, NL, France, Benelux)
- AALB quarterly organic growth by segment — Climate & Fluid Controls specifically
- Gross margin and EBITA margin quarterly trend vs prior year
- Competitor earnings releases: IMI plc, Watts Water, Aalberts adjacency peer Oventrop
- European PMI Construction sub-index monthly

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Failure Narrative 3 (operating leverage failure) and Failure Narrative 7 (false-friend margin via pricing); compound risk cluster — if volume collapses and pricing simultaneously normalises, EBITA impact is multiplicative not additive.

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? Sustained positive Climate & Fluid Controls organic growth through Q3-Q4 2026 despite weak macro — that would indicate AALB has structurally gained share or shifted end-market mix away from new-build construction toward retrofit/replacement, which carries different volume cyclicality.

§3 — Failure Narrative 2: Semiconductor/Industrial Capital Equipment Downcycle

Narrative: AALB's Advanced Flow Solutions segment serves semiconductor fabs (wafer-cleaning, fluid management, ultra-pure water systems) — a segment the market is pricing for structural growth; a semiconductor capex digestion cycle in 2026-27 sees fab customers defer tool installations; AFS revenue growth decelerates from 20%+ to low-single-digit or flat, compressing the premium multiple the market assigns to this segment. Transmission mechanism: semi-capex deferral → AFS organic growth miss → market re-rates the entire AALB group away from quality-industrials toward cyclical-industrials; 25-35% P/E compression risk even with modest EPS miss.

Early warning signals:
- TSMC, Samsung, Intel fab tool installation schedules slipping (public capex guidance revisions)
- ASML order intake declining (leading indicator for AALB AFS by ~2-3 quarters)
- AALB AFS organic growth decelerating to <10% for two consecutive quarters
- Management changing language on AFS from "structural" to "timing-dependent"
- Competitive pricing pressure from Entegris, Parker Hannifin fluid systems division in semiconductor sub-segment

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — ASML order intake did weaken in 2024; semi capex digestion is a recurring 2-3Y cycle; however, long-term secular trend for advanced node capacity (3nm, 2nm fabs) provides structural demand floor; prior cycles (2015-16, 2019, 2022) saw 12-18M slowdowns followed by recovery; AALB AFS has limited historical track record as distinct segment, making base-rate anchoring harder

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — semiconductor capex cycles are empirically documented
- Motive ✅ — fab customers have discretion on tool installation timing
- Opportunity ✅ — AALB AFS is explicitly tied to fab-build cadence
- All three legs aligned — full failure scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Persistent positive ASML order intake through 2026; AALB AFS organic growth sustaining above 15% despite ASML moderation (would imply market share gains or content-per-fab-tool growth); or AFS customer diversification away from leading-edge to specialty/legacy fabs that carry different capex profiles.

Monitoring checklist:
- ASML quarterly order intake (leading indicator)
- TSMC/Samsung fab construction start announcements
- AALB AFS segment organic growth per quarter
- Industry capex trackers: SEMI, VLSI Research equipment spending forecasts
- AALB management guidance language on AFS at each earnings call

Interaction: Relatively isolated failure mode — AFS and Climate & Fluid Controls end-markets are largely uncorrelated; however, combined EPS hit from simultaneous construction + semi downcycle would be severe (~35-45% EPS downside); compound probability is low but tail risk is real.

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB AFS demonstrating book-to-bill >1.0 through a period of ASML intake softness — indicating content intensity per installed fab-tool is rising, which would decouple AALB revenue from headline semi-capex.

§4 — Failure Narrative 3: Operating Leverage Failure / Fixed-Cost Deleverage

Narrative: AALB's manufacturing model is moderately capital-intensive with significant fixed-cost machining infrastructure across 100+ manufacturing sites; if organic growth falls below ~3-4% (the approximate fixed-cost absorption threshold implied by reported incremental margins), EBITA margin compresses disproportionately; 1% organic growth shortfall → ~30-50bps EBITA margin compression based on prior cycle precedent. Transmission mechanism: revenue miss → fixed manufacturing overhead unabsorbed → EBITA margin contraction → EPS misses consensus by 15-25%.

Early warning signals:
- Reported incremental EBITA margin (organic EBITA change / organic revenue change) falling below 25% — below structural norm of 30-35% for AALB
- Gross margin trend diverging from revenue trend (cost base inflating while revenue stalls)
- Manufacturing headcount not declining in proportion to revenue decline (management reluctance to cut capacity)
- Competitor Spirax-Sarco or Georg Fischer issuing profit warnings on margin due to volume deleverage
- AALB guiding "efficiency programme" or "restructuring charges" — historically a lagging indicator of margin pressure already crystallised

Plausibility: High plausibility — operating leverage failure is the most documented failure mode for precision engineering/flow-control manufacturers; Georg Fischer 2022-23, IMI plc 2015-16, Spirax-Sarco 2023 all experienced 150-300bps margin compression in volume-flat/down environments; AALB is not structurally different; incremental margins are high on the upside and painful on the downside

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — fixed cost structure directly exposed to volume shortfall
- Motive ✅ — AALB management has not demonstrated willingness to aggressively cut manufacturing capacity mid-cycle
- Opportunity ✅ — volume risk from Narratives 1 and 2 is live
- All three legs aligned

Disconfirming evidence: AALB achieving positive volume-driven organic growth (not just price/mix) through 2026; evidence of lean manufacturing initiatives reducing fixed-cost per unit through cycle (AALB has discussed operational excellence programmes — verifiable via gross margin trend); or AALB increasing variable-labour proportion of cost base.

Monitoring checklist:
- Quarterly gross margin and EBITA margin by segment
- Incremental EBITA margin calculation (organic EBITA delta / organic revenue delta)
- Headcount data at annual reporting
- Restructuring charge disclosures (indicates management recognising volume problem)
- Working capital days trend (inventory build = unsold production = volume problem)

Interaction: High correlation with Narratives 1 and 2 (volume drivers); correlation with Narrative 8 (capex deferral false friend — management may cut capex rather than headcount, masking the problem temporarily).

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? Two consecutive quarters of AALB reporting volume-driven organic growth with flat-to-improving incremental margins — proof that the fixed-cost base is genuinely leveraging, not just cycling on price.

§5 — Failure Narrative 4: Pricing Cycle Reversal

Narrative: AALB achieved meaningful price/mix gains in 2021-23 as commodity (copper, brass, stainless) and energy cost inflation allowed industrial suppliers to push through price increases; if commodity costs normalise (copper -20% from peak, gas -60% from 2022 spike already observed), customers push back on pricing with force, and AALB's organic growth composite deteriorates as price contribution goes negative; the volume picture does not compensate. Transmission mechanism: pricing normalisation → negative price/mix contribution → organic revenue turns negative or flat → EPS misses consensus by 10-20%.

Early warning signals:
- Copper/brass spot prices declining >15% YoY for two consecutive quarters without corresponding COGS benefit
- AALB management commentary acknowledging "pricing headwinds" or "value pricing discipline maintained" (the latter is euphemism)
- Organic revenue turning below +2% with management attributing to "price normalisation" explicitly
- Competitor Watts Water or Rexnord (Zurn) disclosing negative price/mix in quarterly reports
- Customer (installer/distributor) order patterns showing destocking — return of product purchased at elevated prices

Plausibility: Medium-to-high plausibility — commodity-linked pricing cycles are empirically documented; AALB benefited from 2021-23 inflation; the reversal is structural in nature; however, AALB has a proportion of "value pricing" (based on engineering content, not commodity pass-through) which provides some buffer; peer Spirax-Sarco demonstrated pricing resilience through 2023-24 despite commodity correction, suggesting pure-content-value businesses are more protected

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — commodity deflation is ongoing
- Motive ✅ — customers (installers, OEMs) have purchasing departments specifically targeting price recovery
- Opportunity ✅ — contract renewal cycles allow pricing pressure to manifest with 6-12M lag
- All three legs aligned

Disconfirming evidence: AALB demonstrating stable or growing price/mix contribution through a commodity-soft environment — proof that engineering-content value pricing is genuinely cycle-independent; or AALB successfully offsetting price mix with volume growth so that organic composite remains positive.

Monitoring checklist:
- Commodity price indices: LME copper, brass rod spot
- AALB price/mix/volume split in management commentary (not always disclosed explicitly — watch for proxy language)
- Organic revenue quarterly trend
- Gross margin trend relative to commodity input cost trend
- Distributor inventory levels (channel checks)

Interaction: Correlated with Narrative 3 (operating leverage) — pricing reversal + volume weakness is the compound scenario with highest EPS downside; partially offsets Narrative 1 (lower commodity costs improve gross margin, mitigating EBITA margin compression from volume).

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB gross margins expanding through a period of falling copper prices — indicating that commodity pass-through was symmetric (price up AND costs down both benefiting AALB), and that the margin structure is genuinely improving not just cycling.

§6 — Failure Narrative 5: FALSE FRIEND — Margin Expansion from Capex/R&D Deferral ⚡

Narrative: AALB's reported EBITA margin improvement (~13% to ~14.5% over 2020-24) is partially attributable to capex and R&D investment deferral during the 2021-23 supply-chain disruption period, not structural efficiency gains; reduced maintenance capex preserves short-term cash flow and EBITA, but defers necessary plant refresh; R&D as % of revenue has not been separately disclosed with granularity, making this opaque; in 3-4 years, catch-up capex requirements and technology position deterioration become visible. Transmission mechanism: under-investment today → product obsolescence / plant inefficiency tomorrow → competitive position erodes → volume loss accelerates → EPS misses compoundingly in Year 3+.

Early warning signals (FALSE FRIEND — early signals are POSITIVE, not negative):
- Reported FCF conversion improving (this is the false friend — looks like quality, may be deferral)
- EBITA margin trending up while capex/depreciation ratio declines (capex < D&A = underinvestment signal)
- Product launch cadence slowing (harder to detect externally but manageable via patent filings, trade press)
- Competitor (Georg Fischer, Parker Hannifin fluid division) launching products in AALB's core fitting segments
- Management discussing "portfolio optimisation" and "asset-light" more frequently (often code for underinvestment rationalisation)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — capex deferral is a documented management lever for reported-metric management; AALB capex/revenue ratio has been approximately 3-4% historically; if this has drifted below 3%, it is a warning signal; the R&D opacity makes this difficult to verify from public filings; peer Georg Fischer experienced exactly this pattern pre-2019, followed by a major restructuring that damaged margins for two years

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — management has full discretion over capex timing within reasonable ranges
- Motive ✅ — margin improvement narrative supports higher stock price and management compensation
- Opportunity ✅ — capex deferral is invisible in the short term and only manifests with a 2-4Y lag
- All three legs aligned — FALSE FRIEND confirmed

Disconfirming evidence: AALB capex/revenue ratio sustaining at or above historical norm (~3.5-4%) through 2022-24; management providing granular R&D investment disclosure showing stable or increasing spend; independent product pipeline evidence (new fitting platforms, new semiconductor fluid management designs) demonstrating continued innovation cadence.

Monitoring checklist:
- Capex as % of revenue (annual — target >3.5%)
- Capex vs. depreciation ratio (target >1.0 — if below 1.0, underinvestment signal)
- Product launch announcements and patent filings (qualitative)
- R&D expense disclosure in annual report (often embedded in SG&A — extract separately)
- Competitor new product introductions in core fitting markets

Interaction: Amplifies Narratives 1, 3, and 7; most dangerous compound failure — if volume weakens AND capex deferral has degraded product position, AALB has less pricing power and less volume resilience simultaneously; the FALSE FRIEND nature means the EPS misses come when the company appears most healthy.

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB providing capex and maintenance-vs-growth capex split with R&D clearly disclosed, showing investment intensity at or above industry norm — combined with product-pipeline evidence of active innovation; absence of this disclosure itself is mildly disconfirming of the bull case.

§7 — Failure Narrative 6: M&A Integration Failure / Goodwill Impairment

Narrative: AALB has been an active acquirer (30+ bolt-on acquisitions over the past decade); the balance sheet carries significant goodwill (~€1.2-1.5bn approximate from public filings); if integration of recent acquisitions (particularly AFS-adjacent semiconductor fluid management businesses) underperforms, goodwill impairment charges could be material; separately, acquisition multiples paid at cycle peak (2021-22) may not be sustainable at current interest rates. Transmission mechanism: acquisition underperformance → goodwill impairment → EPS distortion + balance sheet stress → investor confidence erosion → de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- AALB disclosing "impairment testing sensitivity" or "headroom reduced" language in annual report
- Acquired revenue lines growing below management's stated acquisition IRR assumptions
- Return on invested capital declining despite EBITA margin stability (acquisition dilution)
- Leverage (net debt/EBITDA) trending toward or above 2.5x (covenant sensitivity zone)
- Management guidance reducing acquisition pace — may signal integration difficulty or balance sheet stress

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — AALB's acquisition track record is above average for the European industrial sector (minimal large-scale goodwill impairments historically); however, the 2021-22 acquisition cohort was completed at peak-cycle multiples; rising interest rates increase the cost of capital against which future cash flows are tested; Georg Fischer and GF Piping experienced acquisition integration issues in 2018-20 cycle

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — integration complexity is real; AALB runs a decentralised model which can mask underperformance
- Motive — ⚠️ — management has a strong incentive NOT to impair (bonus/compensation link); this is a motive to hide, not to fail; failure is structural not intentional
- Opportunity ✅ — goodwill on balance sheet is the structural opportunity for impairment
- Two-of-three legs clearly aligned — downgrade to watch-only unless leverage deteriorates

Disconfirming evidence: AALB ROIC trending stable or improving through 2025-26, indicating acquisitions are earning above cost of capital; leverage staying below 2.0x net debt/EBITDA; management providing post-acquisition revenue and margin disclosure showing above-hurdle performance.

Monitoring checklist:
- ROIC trend (annual)
- Net debt/EBITDA quarterly
- Goodwill as % of net assets (annual)
- Management commentary on acquisition pipeline discipline (multiples, integration status)
- Any disclosed "impairment test sensitivity" in notes to accounts

Interaction: Partially correlated with Narrative 2 (AFS semi-capex downcycle) — if AFS segment underperforms, semiconductor-fluid-management goodwill is at risk; relatively isolated from Narratives 1 and 3.

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB publishing post-acquisition return disclosure (even informal) showing acquired businesses growing at or above assumed rates, combined with ROIC stability at 12-14% through an earnings growth period.

§8 — Failure Narrative 7: FALSE FRIEND — Organic Growth Masking Volume Weakness via Price/Mix

Narrative: AALB's reported organic growth of mid-single-digit for 2022-24 has not been explicitly decomposed into price, volume, and mix in granular public reporting; if a significant portion of organic growth is price/mix (plausible given 2021-23 inflation cycle) and volume is flat-to-declining, the apparent health of the business is a false friend; when pricing normalises, the underlying volume stagnation is exposed and organic growth turns sharply negative. Transmission mechanism: price-driven organic growth → masking of volume weakness → pricing normalisation → organic revenue turns negative → consensus de-rates growth multiple.

Early warning signals (FALSE FRIEND — early signals are POSITIVE):
- Organic revenue consistently positive while volume indicators (building permits, PMI) are negative — the divergence IS the signal
- Management consistently attributing organic growth to "pricing discipline" and "value add" without volume commentary
- AALB market share data (where available from trade associations) flat or declining despite positive organic growth
- Gross margin declining while organic revenue grows (pricing not covering cost — pure pass-through)
- Distributor inventory data showing destocking despite positive AALB revenues

Plausibility: High plausibility — price/volume decomposition opacity is a documented false-friend pattern across European industrial companies; Legrand, Schneider Electric, and Hager all showed similar patterns in 2022-23; the lack of explicit volume disclosure in AALB reports makes this structurally verifiable only via channel checks or management direct questioning

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — pricing levers were available and used industry-wide
- Motive ✅ — volume weakness is structurally obscured by aggregate organic growth reporting
- Opportunity ✅ — reporting convention does not require decomposition of organic growth in the same granularity as IFRS revenue
- All three legs aligned — FALSE FRIEND confirmed

Disconfirming evidence: AALB management explicitly disclosing volume growth as a component of organic revenue, showing positive volume even in weak end-markets — or third-party market data (HVAC fittings market volumes from trade associations such as VDZ, ZVSHK) showing AALB gaining or holding market share during the organic growth period.

Monitoring checklist:
- Management Q&A transcripts at every earnings call — probe for volume/price split
- European HVAC and industrial fittings market volume data (trade associations)
- AALB revenue per unit proxies where available (ASP trends)
- Competitor volume disclosures (Watts, IMI) as proxy reference
- Customer/installer trade press for demand commentary

Interaction: This is the most important false-friend scenario because it interacts with ALL other scenarios — if underlying volume is already weak, every other failure mode has higher plausibility than consensus implies.

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB voluntarily decomposing organic growth into price, volume, and mix at a segment level for two consecutive years, showing genuine volume growth in both Climate & Fluid Controls and AFS — this would be strong disconfirmation of the false-friend narrative.

Means/Motive/Opportunity:

- One-of-three legs clearly aligned — watch only; does not rise to full failure scenario probability

Disconfirming evidence: Pelsma confirmed to extended contract post-2026; active investor day participation demonstrating engagement; visible CFO/COO succession bench depth; AFS customer contracts structured at business-unit rather than CEO relationship level.

Monitoring checklist:

§10 — Failure Narrative 9: Technology Disruption — Digital Flow Metering / Composite Fittings

Narrative: AALB's Climate & Fluid Controls segment is substantially built on precision-machined brass and copper fittings; two technology vectors threaten long-term moat erosion: (a) digital flow metering (smart water meters, IoT-embedded sensors) reducing the physical fitting count per installation; (b) composite/polymer fittings (PP-R, PE-X, CPVC) substituting copper/brass in residential applications where corrosion resistance and cost are the primary specs. Neither poses near-term (12-18M) EPS risk, but both compress the long-run growth expectation embedded in the multiple. Transmission mechanism: technology substitution → volume share loss in residential/light-commercial segment → 10-15Y revenue CAGR deteriorating from ~3-4% to ~1-2% → multiple compresses from 18-20x to 14-16x PE even with current-year EPS intact.

Early warning signals:
- EU Water Directive mandating smart metering penetration rates (Directive 2020/2184 — already in force)
- AALB Climate & Fluid Controls organic growth persistently below European construction market growth (implies share loss)
- Major fitting OEMs (Uponor, Rehau, Wavin) reporting accelerating polymer fitting volumes in new-build segment
- AALB not announcing product expansions into smart metering or composite fitting adjacencies
- Patent analysis showing AALB R&D focus remaining on machined-metal vs. digital/composite

Plausibility: Low plausibility over 18-36M (DD investment horizon); medium plausibility over 5-10Y; polymer substitution has been discussed for 15+ years but brass/copper has retained residential dominance due to fitting reliability, pressure rating, and installer tradition; digital metering affects meter segments not fluid control fittings directly; this is a long-dated structural concern not a near-term catalyst

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — technology alternatives exist and are improving
- Motive ✅ — cost reduction incentive for builders/installers is strong
- Opportunity ⚠️ — transition is slow; regulatory and building-code barriers protect incumbent fittings
- Two-of-three legs — watch only for DD horizon; material for 5Y+ thesis

Disconfirming evidence: AALB Climate & Fluid Controls gaining volume market share (vs. market) consistently; AALB launching composite or digital-adjacent products; polymer fitting penetration remaining flat in European residential applications per trade data.

Monitoring checklist:
- EU smart metering rollout data (annual)
- European fitting market material share data (trade associations)
- AALB product portfolio press releases and trade show appearances
- Competitor Uponor/Wavin composite volume growth rates
- AALB patent filings by technology type

Interaction: Isolated from near-term scenarios; long-dated amplifier of Narrative 4 (pricing cycle reversal — if technology substitution accelerates, pricing power deteriorates faster).

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB demonstrating metal fitting volume growth at or above market through 2025-26 in the residential segment, combined with product launches in digital metering adjacency — proof of proactive moat defence.

§11 — Failure Narrative 10: Geopolitical / Supply Chain Re-fragmentation

Narrative: AALB sources brass/copper and precision components across European and Asian supply chains; geopolitical re-fragmentation (US tariffs on European industrial goods, EU-China trade restrictions on raw materials, Middle East supply chain disruption) could create cost inflation spikes or supply interruption that management cannot fully pass through; unlike 2021-23 where cost inflation was industry-wide and passable, geopolitical disruption may be asymmetric and AALB-specific. Transmission mechanism: supply chain disruption → unhedged cost spikes → gross margin compression → EPS miss of 10-15%.

Early warning signals:
- New US tariffs on European industrial goods (under current US trade policy trajectory — plausible)
- LME copper or brass rod supply disruption announcements
- AALB announcing supply chain diversification investment (implies current concentration risk)
- European peer industrial companies (Rexnord, Georg Fischer) reporting supply chain cost spikes
- AALB gross margin diverging from commodity input cost trends unexpectedly

Plausibility: Medium plausibility — US tariff risk on European goods is elevated given 2025 US trade policy trajectory; AALB has moderate US revenue exposure (AFS semiconductor segment has US-based customer fabs); however, AALB's primary manufacturing is European with European cost base, limiting direct tariff impact on cost side; the demand-side (US customers of AFS) is more exposed than the supply-side

Means/Motive/Opportunity:
- Means ✅ — geopolitical mechanisms are active and documented
- Motive ✅ — US policy trajectory provides clear mechanism
- Opportunity ✅ — AALB AFS has US fab customer exposure
- All three legs aligned

Disconfirming evidence: US tariff exemptions for European industrial fluid management components; AALB confirming in investor communications that AFS customer contracts are structured with tariff pass-through clauses; or AALB demonstrating manufacturing localisation capability (US-based production for US customers).

Monitoring checklist:
- US trade policy announcements on European industrial goods tariff lists
- AALB geographic revenue split by customer location (annual)
- Gross margin sensitivity disclosures in annual report risk section
- Currency hedging policy (EUR/USD for AFS revenues)
- Industry association (VDMA, Euroalliages) commentary on trade policy impact

Interaction: Correlated with Narrative 2 (AFS semi-capex downcycle — if US tariffs simultaneously hit AFS customers' capex, the volume and cost risks compound); partially correlated with Narrative 4 (pricing cycle — if raw material costs spike geopolitically, pricing power may be tested).

Sceptical bullet: What would invalidate this hypothesis? AALB providing explicit tariff sensitivity analysis in its annual report showing <€20M annual EBITA exposure to plausible US tariff scenarios, combined with documented supply chain flexibility to absorb material disruption.

Transmission mechanism: activist entry → management distraction + forced capital return → under-investment in AFS growth → AFS thesis unravels → EPS trajectory misses.

Early warning signals:
