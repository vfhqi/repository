EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Digi leverages extreme vertical integration and low-cost execution to disrupt European telecom markets, driving robust user growth and structural margin expansion. The Romanian telecommunications operator has evolved from a regional cable provider into Western Europe's most aggressive challenger , systematically undermining incumbent pricing structures through proprietary infrastructure ownership and radical operational efficiency. Digi operates as a convergent European telecommunications provider, offering fixed broadband, mobile, Pay TV, and fixed-line telephony services across mature core markets like Romania and emerging expansion territories across Western Europe. Operations are anchored in Romania and Spain, with emerging presences in Portugal, Belgium, Italy, and the UK. In Q1 2026, services transferred over time accounted for €568.6m of the €582.6m total revenue, with mobile services representing the largest segment at 51% of overall client contracts. 1

Digi delivers peer-leading revenue and EBITDA growth driven by aggressive expansion, but aggressive network rollouts structurally constrain near-term free cash flow generation. Consolidated revenue reached €2.21bn in FY2025, up from €1.92bn in FY2024, demonstrating consistent double-digit expansion. The company achieved an outstanding fully organic revenue CAGR of ~20% over the last three years , while incumbent peers typically exhibit organic revenue growth in the flat to low single-digit range. - Analyst/Investor Capital expenditures represented an exceptionally high 35.9% of revenues in 2025 and 45.8% in 2024 , compared to traditional telco peers that typically operate with capex-to-sales ratios in the 12-18% range. Digi generates negligible or negative near-term free cash flow due to its strategy of reinvesting almost all operating cash flow into growth projects. Net leverage stood at 2.8x to 3.19x net debt/EBITDA in 2025, reflecting heavy borrowing to fund network rollouts. 4 Digi commands a structurally advantaged value-chain position through extreme upstream network integration, yielding superior margins despite heavily discounted retail pricing. The company internalises approximately 90% of its network deployment, operations, maintenance, and IT software development. Digi's FTTH deployment cost is €49 per home passed, an extreme structural outlier against incumbent peers who spend €80- €150 per home.

Spain, with MNO economics beginning in July 2025, converting variable data traffic costs into a fixed-cost structure. Building proprietary fibre allows Digi to bypass wholesale bitstream fees, capturing infrastructure-level returns with FTTH gross margin reaching an exceptional 53% to 55% in recent quarters.

Digi leverages elite Net Promoter Scores, transparent pricing, and convergent bundles to secure deep retention despite rejecting forced-contract lock-in mechanisms. Digi Spain reports a stellar Net Promoter Score of 61, dramatically outperforming incumbent Spanish competitors who consistently languish with substantially inferior scores ranging between 13 and 26. In the hyper-competitive Spanish market, an overwhelming 80% of mobile net additions over the last twelve months were highly sticky convergent mobile services.

Digi weaponises structural cost advantages to decimate incumbent pricing, driving immense share gains while facing negligible vertical integration threats from highly fragmented customers. The company commands an overwhelming 73.1% broadband market share and 76.0% pay-TV share in Romania, cementing its undisputed structural dominance. In Spain, Digi operates as the definitive growth engine, capturing roughly 80% of all Spanish broadband market net additions. Fearing catastrophic volume losses, Spanish incumbents are abandoning traditional pricing discipline to launch highly targeted, margin-dilutive win-back campaigns, with MasOrange launching an extreme €4.90 per month unlimited mobile data plan specifically targeting existing Digi customers. Severe margin pressure from Digi's value-tier expansion forces legacy operators into defensive in-market megamergers to preserve cash flows, with the massive Orange and MasMovil Spanish merger explicitly aiming to generate synergies to offset relentless value-tier price erosion. Cross-chain signals universally corroborate management claims of structural disruption, with working capital metrics revealing immense leverage over suppliers. Digi operates with a massive net current liability position of EUR 609.3m at year-end 2025, confirming management's claims of high operational efficiency and intense supplier bargaining power. The group utilizes extensive reverse factoring and vendor financing to stretch supplier payments drastically, with payment due dates aggressively extended to an average of 221 days after the invoice date. Digi pairs immense founder-led operational rigor with an extreme structural cost advantage, driving relentless mechanical market share capture across European telecommunications. Serghei Bulgac has served as Chief Executive Officer since 2015, while controlling shareholder Zoltán Teszári has directed all key strategic decisions since founding the business in 1992. This concentrated control enables aggressive, long-term infrastructure investments without bowing to short-term public market pressures for immediate cash extraction. Digi commands an immense, highly durable structural cost advantage through its fully internalised network deployment teams, with the €49 cost per home passed for Spanish fibre severely undercutting the €80-120 costs borne by incumbent urban deployers. The operator consistently delivers fully organic, double-digit top-line expansion, achieving a ~20% revenue CAGR over the last three years. Digi's immense market share gains are entirely mechanical, with the structural €49 deployment cost mechanically enabling a highly profitable €10 retail price, creating an irresistible gravitational pull for value-conscious consumers. 20
