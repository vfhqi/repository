## Section 1 — Stage Identification and History

IPO and the Peak (October–November 2021)

AutoStore Holdings listed on Oslo Børs on 20 October 2021 at an IPO price of NOK 31 per share. The IPO valued the company at approximately NOK 103.5 billion (c.$12.4 billion) — the largest Nordic IPO in 20 years and the largest Nordic technology IPO on record at the time. SoftBank was a major shareholder. The listing was backed by Thomas H. Lee Partners and capitalised on peak euphoria in post-COVID warehouse automation.

The stock immediately surged on debut, trading up approximately 11% intraday. It subsequently extended gains as the warehouse automation narrative was at maximum momentum. The all-time high was reached in late November 2021 at approximately NOK 46.24 — a 49% premium to the IPO price in under six weeks. [Chart: 5Y]

Stage 3 Distribution (Late 2021 — Mid 2022)

The ATH of 46.24 marked the beginning of a textbook Stage 3 topping process. As central banks signalled rate hikes and growth-tech multiples compressed globally, AutoStore's premium valuation — built on 2021's 78% revenue growth rate and peak-cycle warehouse capex expectations — became indefensible. Institutional distribution was underway throughout early 2022. The stock failed to retest the November high, established a series of lower highs, and broke below key MAs in sequence.

The fundamental narrative began deteriorating in parallel. Revenue growth decelerated sharply in 2022 and into 2023. By FY2023, the company reported only 11% revenue growth — a dramatic deceleration from the 78% pace two years prior. Customer capex freezes and order book weakness became recurring themes on earnings calls.

### Stage 4 Decline (Mid 2022 — Late 2024/Early 2025)

The Stage 4 decline was severe and sustained. From the ATH of c.46 NOK, the stock fell into the NOK 4–5 range — a drawdown of approximately 90% peak to trough. The dashboard data confirms the 52-week low of NOK 4.60, which was reached in approximately April/May 2025 per available data. For a stock that IPO'd at 31 and ripped to 46 within weeks, this represented one of the more dramatic destructions of value in the Norwegian equity market over the period.

The driver was straightforward: the market repriced a high-growth, high-multiple software-and-robotics hybrid at a fraction of peak valuations as growth rates normalised. There was no single catastrophic event — this was a slow, grinding re-rating over approximately three years.

### Stage 1 Base / Transition (2024–Late 2025)

The base formation phase — Stage 1 in the Minervini framework — appears to have developed during 2024 and into late 2025. During this phase the stock established a floor in the NOK 4–6 range, volume dried up, and the 200D MA began to flatten after years of decline. This is the "quiet period" before a Stage 2 advance — institutions accumulate, sellers exhaust, and price coils.

The dashboard data provides the key anchor: the 200D MA has been rising for 4 months as at the data date (2026-04-24). A rising 200D MA is the primary confirmation of Stage 2 entry. Four months of 200D ascent, combined with the 200D sitting at NOK 9.964 vs a current price of 11.79, suggests the Stage 2 breakout likely occurred in approximately Q4 2025 or very early Q1 2026.
