EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Nexans has emerged as a focused pure-play electrification leader following a rigorous multi-year portfolio transformation that divested non-core automotive and telecom assets to concentrate exclusively on power transmission, grid distribution, and local connectivity.

The business model captures value across the entire electrical value chain through a tripartite organizational structure serving transmission system operators, distribution utilities, and commercial end-users, while shifting from pure product sales to comprehensive lifecycle solutions underpinned by structural mega-trends including the global transition to renewable energy and urgent grid modernization.

The company operates a massive global footprint spanning forty-seven percent of standard sales in Europe, nineteen percent in North America, and twelve percent in Asia-Pacific, employing approximately twenty-five thousand seven hundred people across forty-one countries to serve fundamentally local distribution networks.

The financial turnaround delivered between twenty-eighteen and twenty-twenty-five represents a remarkable structural transformation, with adjusted EBITDA expanding to seven hundred twenty-eight million euros achieving an impressive eleven point nine percent margin on standard sales, while Return on Capital Employed reached twenty-one point three percent in twenty-twenty-five demonstrating highly disciplined capital allocation and operational execution.

Revenue generation reached six point one billion euros in standard sales during twenty-twenty-five, supported by eight point three percent organic growth significantly outpacing larger rival Prysmian's more modest expansion.

The proprietary SHIFT transformation program mechanically extracted approximately three hundred million euros in structural EBITDA expansion between twenty-eighteen and twenty-twenty-four by aggressively reducing operational complexity and optimizing product portfolios, while intentionally reducing the customer base from twenty thousand to four thousand platinum accounts to maximize capital returns.

Free cash flow conversion achieved forty-seven point three percent in twenty-twenty-five generating three hundred forty-four million euros, though this performance relies heavily on lumpy advance down-payments from massive Transmission projects creating periodic volatility.

The cable industry is experiencing an unprecedented structural super-cycle, with the global market mechanically expanding from one hundred sixteen billion euros in twenty-twenty-three to one hundred fifty-seven billion euros by twenty-twenty-eight driven by unavoidable grid modernization mandates and exponential data-center growth.

Value-chain economics heavily favour the consolidated high-voltage tier where extreme capacity constraints allow top-tier operators to capture premium profitability with peer Prysmian generating roughly twenty percent adjusted EBITDA margins in transmission, while manufacturers secure highly favourable milestone prepayments operating with structurally negative working capital.

The high-voltage submarine market operates as a highly concentrated oligopoly effectively controlled by Nexans, Prysmian, NKT and select Asian specialists, with only five to six companies globally capable of extra-high-voltage manufacturing and installation.

Competitor attitudes display exceptional pricing discipline, with major European players explicitly committing to selective order-taking focused purely on profitability, favouring long-term frame agreements over volume, while intentionally keeping order backlogs flat quarter-over-quarter.

Nexans commands a uniquely advantageous position as the world's largest vertically integrated cable manufacturer controlling flow from copper rods to end-of-life recycling, operating across metallurgy, low-voltage connectivity, medium-voltage distribution, and high-voltage transmission and installation.

Upstream integration through four dedicated rod mills processing up to six hundred thousand metric tons of wire rods annually grants direct access to copper cathodes from primary mines, buffering against supply shocks, while the RecyCâbles joint venture structurally reduces reliance on highly volatile virgin metal markets.

The most profitable stage remains high-voltage transmission capturing EBITDA margins routinely exceeding eighteen to twenty percent, protected by extreme capital requirements for subsea cable-laying vessels creating an oligopoly.

The recent six hundred eighty million euro acquisition of Republic Wire establishes immediate access to eighteen deeply entrenched national sales agencies, tripling North American revenues past one billion euros and fundamentally challenging the established Prysmian-Southwire duopoly in the highly profitable United States market.

Customer concentration remains exceptionally low with no single customer accounting for over ten percent of total sales, while the high-voltage transmission order book reached a record seven point seven billion euros at the end of twenty-twenty-five providing multi-year revenue visibility.

Major framework agreements include a landmark one point seven billion euro contract with German-Dutch grid operator TenneT for high-voltage direct current turnkey projects and an impressive seven-year six hundred million euro agreement with French distributor Enedis.

Switching costs remain prohibitive due to the severe global shortage of Tier-One subsea cable capacity, with replacing Nexans on major projects practically impossible as alternative suppliers lack available extrusion capacity before twenty-twenty-eight.

Pricing leverage is exceptional as standard utility contracts feature strict metal escalation clauses that automatically pass copper and aluminum price fluctuations to customers, while unprecedented demand allows manufacturers to secure massive down-payments funding their own capacity expansions via customer balance sheets.

Operator quality exhibits structural robustness despite the abrupt CEO transition, with Julien Hueber immediately assuming the role bringing twenty-three years of internal operational and supply-chain expertise after successfully leading the Grid and Connect divisions.

Capital allocation demonstrates exceptional discipline through a pristine balance sheet featuring ultra-low zero point three six times leverage providing unparalleled optionality, while the Republic Wire acquisition was executed at a highly attractive seven point six times post-synergy twenty-twenty-seven EBITDA multiple.

Structural moats continue deepening mechanically through ownership of specialized cable-laying vessels creating virtually insurmountable capital barriers, with only five to six companies globally possessing both manufacturing and installation capabilities for ultra-high-voltage cables.

The proprietary SHIFT framework functions as a mechanical engine automatically extracting higher margins from every newly acquired regional business, while AI-driven predictive asset management promises to further reduce working capital intensity.

The business demonstrates outstanding long-term paradigm fit, aligning perfectly with the multi-decade structural shift from fossil-fuel infrastructure to electron-based systems, though disruption risks are emerging through eventual commercialization of modular solid-state transformers by twenty-twenty-eight potentially altering medium-voltage architectures, while hyperscalers aggressively explore solid-oxide fuel cells to bypass traditional utility grids.

Cross-value-chain signals strongly corroborate management's narrative of unprecedented high-voltage demand, with European utilities signing six-to-seven-year frame agreements versus historical two-year deals and peers explicitly validating the margin-over-volume strategic approach.

However, the apparent competitive moat relies heavily on temporary supply shortages, as massive new capacity across Nexans, Prysmian and NKT simultaneously activates by twenty-twenty-seven through twenty-twenty-eight potentially normalizing supply-demand dynamics and stripping manufacturers of exceptional pricing power.

The investment case rests fundamentally on sustained execution of the margin-expansion roadmap targeting one point one five billion euros in adjusted EBITDA by twenty-twenty-eight, representing a three hundred fifty million euro structural uplift from current levels, while successfully integrating Republic Wire to unlock twenty-three million euros in explicit run-rate synergies.

Critical risks include potential margin compression if aggressive industry capacity additions rapidly normalize high-voltage pricing power, geopolitical delays on the one point four three billion euro Great Sea Interconnector project threatening near-term cash conversion, and execution uncertainty under new leadership lacking a proven track record navigating severe cyclical downturns.

The balance sheet provides substantial strategic optionality for continued consolidation of fragmented regional markets, though management must maintain rigorous SHIFT discipline avoiding the temptation to accept low-margin spot contracts when backfilling delayed mega-projects.
