§1 — KQ DEFINITION + MISSION COMMAND BRIEF + COMPETING HYPOTHESES

J-front verdict: Hypothesis B (Durable Compounder with Cyclical Surface Noise) has fewest hard inconsistencies; Hypothesis C (Structural Moat Erosion) is close-call runner-up, making composite-truth synthesis mandatory

  • KQ stated: Does this company possess a durable, competitively-insulated earnings compounding engine capable of sustaining 20%+ EPS CAGR over a 3-year horizon?
  • Higher intent: the answer determines position sizing, hold duration, and whether the 18M–3Y EPS trajectory is forecastable at the precision level a concentrated long-only portfolio requires; a wrong answer here is a sizing error, not just an analytical error
  • Decision stakes: concentrated 5-15 position portfolio means a full-position allocation in a structurally-broken compounder is a 500–800bps portfolio-level drag; the KQ is therefore asymmetric — over-caution costs alpha, under-caution risks permanent capital impairment
  • Stage: Deep Dive — landscape mapping (Q21 equivalent) is assumed to have been completed; this Q22 forces the verdict

Competing hypotheses (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive, falsifiable):

  • Hypothesis A — Secular Growth, No Cycle Discount: the company's earnings growth is driven by a secular demand shift that is fundamentally orthogonal to the macro cycle; any near-term EPS softness is noise, not signal; the 20%+ EPS CAGR is achievable through the cycle without structural adjustment
  • Falsifiable claim: if ROIC declines more than 200bps in a sector downcycle, or if volume growth decelerates below the secular demand growth rate, Hypothesis A is falsified
  • Hypothesis B — Durable Compounder with Cyclical Surface Noise: the company has a genuine structural moat (pricing power + customer captivity + cost position), but near-term EPS is suppressed by one or two identifiable cyclical/macro overlays; once those overlays normalise (12–24M horizon), the compounding engine resumes; 20%+ EPS CAGR is achievable on a through-cycle basis but not in every 12M window
  • Falsifiable claim: if the cyclical overlay lasts beyond 24M or proves to be structural (i.e., the company cannot recover margin once volumes recover), Hypothesis B collapses into Hypothesis C
  • Hypothesis C — Structural Moat Erosion Masked by Cycle: the apparent cyclical softness is partially or wholly structural — a competitor, technology shift, or customer behaviour change is permanently eroding the company's pricing power or volume position; the cycle is a convenient narrative cover for a slower-moving structural deterioration; 20%+ EPS CAGR is not achievable on a 3-year view
  • Falsifiable claim: if the company recovers to prior-cycle ROIC and margin levels on the next volume upturn, Hypothesis C is substantially falsified
  • Hypothesis D — Null / Neither (Adequate but Uninspiring): the company is neither a structural compounder nor structurally broken; it is a mid-single-digit EPS grower with occasional cycle amplification; the 20%+ EPS CAGR target is simply the wrong benchmark for this asset; appropriate for a passive-weight holding but not a concentrated position
  • Falsifiable claim: if either (a) ROIC sustainably exceeds WACC by >500bps or (b) incremental returns on reinvested capital clearly exceed hurdle rate, the null hypothesis drops out

Stage context note: at DD stage, Hypothesis D should already have been screened out by the IG/ESA process; its inclusion here is a QC discipline check — the analyst should be able to confirm dismissal with evidence, not just assumption

Sceptical bullet — runner-up: Hypothesis C is the runner-up; it did not win because the evidence on competitor-driven volume displacement is suggestive but not conclusive — pricing realisations in the most recent period held above the 3-year trend line, which is inconsistent with moat erosion; however, one or two additional quarters of below-trend pricing would materially shift the matrix; the analyst is NOT home-free on Hypothesis B
