EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

AXA stands as a leading global composite insurer serving 92 million clients across 52 countries , having successfully completed a pivotal decade-long business-mix transformation that reduced financial market risk exposure from 80% to 15% of the portfolio. The group now operates as a capital-efficient underwriting franchise generating Euro 115.5 billion in annual revenues through five core geographic divisions spanning France, Europe, AXA XL commercial lines, and emerging markets. Management has systematically pivoted toward capital-light products including unit-linked savings, protection, and health insurance, driving record net inflows of Euro 5.4 billion while deliberately allowing legacy general account savings outflows. This strategic repositioning has established AXA as a predominantly underwriting-focused operator with 57% of group earnings originating from property and casualty operations, complemented by 30% from life and savings businesses. Financial performance demonstrates robust multi-year profitability , with underlying return on equity reaching 16.00% in fiscal 2025, though this trails Allianz's 18.10% and significantly lags Zurich's industry-leading 26.90% . AXA's property and casualty operating margins stand 3 to 4 percentage points above peer averages , supported by a combined ratio of 90.6% that reflects superior technical underwriting discipline . The group maintains a highly competitive 75% total payout ratio framework matching Allianz's capital return posture, executing Euro 3.8 billion in anti-dilutive buybacks following the Euro 5.4 billion divestment of AXA Investment Managers. Solvency capitalization remains robust at 224% , though the loss of grandfathered debt created a 10 percentage point headwind that will be fully offset by an estimated 17 percentage point benefit from the Solvency II review taking

effect in January 2027. Revenue growth trajectories show AXA's 3.80% three-year CAGR lagging Allianz's 4.70% , Zurich's 5.60% , and Generali's 5.10% , signaling relative market share pressure in key European geographies where the group ranks second in French P&C but only fourth in German P&C . Value chain positioning reflects deliberate vertical integration choices , with AXA occupying primary underwriting stages while maintaining selective upstream reinsurance exposure through AXA XL Reinsurance and dominant downstream distribution control via 25,000 proprietary tied agents globally . The group's 15,000-agent French network delivers structurally high 96% retention rates in core European retail portfolios, creating formidable customer lock-in that protects against digital aggregator pricing pressure. Distribution economics strongly favor AXA's proprietary channel model, with brokers and agents capturing 30% to 40% EBITDA margins compared to primary underwriting's 5% to 10% operating margins , though the group's direct acquisitions including 51% of Italian digital leader Prima aim to recapture distribution value and accelerate capital-light growth. Upstream reinsurance positioning remains strategically managed , with management reducing AXA XL property catastrophe exposure by 21% to curb earnings volatility while benefiting from softening reinsurance treaty pricing that secured 20% lower costs on recent renewals. Competitive moats stem from multiple reinforcing advantages including massive scale serving 92 million clients , impenetrable regulatory capital barriers under Solvency II, proprietary claims data pools spanning decades, and exclusive agency distribution networks that limit customer disintermediation. Technology investments enable advanced AI integration that delivers a 1 percentage point reduction to property and casualty loss ratios through dynamic pricing, while scale economies allow AXA to match Allianz's technology spending capacity and maintain superior data availability for risk selection. Customer concentration metrics show highly diversified retail exposure with minimal pricing leverage, though commercial lines exhibit moderate concentration with the top 200 accounts representing 27% of AXA XL premiums. Switching costs remain structurally high for life insurance due to mortality benefits and tax incentives, while embedded insurance partnerships with corporates like Stellantis and preferred medical network steering reaching 80% of UK claims further enhance retail customer stickiness. Operator quality reflects high strategic discipline under CEO Thomas Buberl's leadership, successfully executing the 'Unlock the Future' plan that targets 6% to 8% annual underlying EPS growth and Euro 21 billion in cumulative cash remittances . Capital allocation demonstrates prudent governance , prioritizing disciplined bolt-on acquisitions with minimum 15% internal rate of return hurdles while avoiding transformational M&A risks and maintaining debt gearing within the 19% to 23% target range . Financial output generation remains excellent , with Euro 7.5 billion in organic cash remittances during 2025 and conservative technical reserving where prior- year releases represent less than 10% of P&C operating profit . Corporate culture metrics show 85% employee engagement and 39% women in leadership roles , though expert commentary warns that rigid foreign underwriting mandates have alienated certain corporate clients and high reliance on offshoring threatens localized service quality. Primary structural risks center on softening commercial property pricing with AXA XL renewal rates declining 4% in first-quarter 2026 , rising broker consolidation in Germany that pressures acquisition costs, and sustained climate volatility where unmodeled secondary perils could outpace pricing algorithms. The most significant near- term threat remains commercial property market softening at AXA XL, with early warning signals including

sequential combined ratio deterioration or negative reserve development in casualty lines facing persistent social inflation pressures.
