EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 5 findings

ZEAL Network operates as the leading private online lottery broker in Germany with a dominant 44% market share, executing an asset-light brokerage model that transfers stakes to state lottery operators without carrying direct jackpot payout risks. This positioning enables ROCE/ROIC consistently exceeding 20% and free cash flow conversion exceeding 20% of sales. The German online lottery market remains structurally underpenetrated at 31.2% digital penetration versus Norway's 60% and the UK's 45%, creating substantial secular migration runway while the digital duopoly structure concentrates 44% private market share with ZEAL versus 58% fragmented across 16 state lottery providers.

1.
Industry Structure and Competitive Moats: The German digital lottery market is structurally a duopoly with combined digital sales dominated by 16 state lottery providers via their shared portal 'lotto.de' at 58% market share and ZEAL at 41-44% market share, while the state-owned market share is fractured across 16 independent regional state lottery companies organized under the Deutscher Lotto- und Totoblock (DLTB) which suffer from localized digital offerings and lack a unified nationwide app. High entry barriers stem from regulatory licensing complexity as operating as an online lottery broker requires a commercial brokerage license under the strict German State Treaty on Games of Chance (GlüStV) with only 14 licensed private entities operating nationwide, while strict enforcement of the GlüStV has successfully prohibited unlicensed secondary lotteries from operating in Germany. Effective July 1, 2026, the Joint Gambling Authority (GGL) implemented a tiered stake limit increase for licensed German online slots, raising maximum stake per spin from €1.00 to €3.00 for adults and up to €5.00 for pre-qualified consumers, explicitly introduced to boost the competitiveness of the regulated onshore market against unlicensed offshore operators
2.
Customer Economics and Retention: The average monthly active lottery customer base reached 1,575 thousand in Q1 2026, representing a 5% year-on-year increase. Average monthly billings per active user reached €56.72 in Q1 2026, representing a 3% billing decline from €58.57 in Q1 2025. The consolidated lottery cost per lead reached €53.80 in Q1 2026, representing an 11% cost increase compared to €48.50 in Q1 2025. Approximately 35% of regular lottery players use a rolling subscription model, with rolling subscriptions accounting for approximately 60% of total lottery billings on the platform. ZEAL possesses strong pricing pass-through capabilities with negligible impact on customer retention, executing a strategic ticket fee price adjustment in mid-2024 that was successfully absorbed by rolling subscribers with minimal player churn and increased the 9M 2025 billing margin by 2.2 percentage points
3.
Value Chain Position and Supplier Dependencies: State lottery operators represent approximately 90% of total German lottery volume, with lottery supply running through 16 independent Landeslotteriegesellschaften organized under the Deutscher Lotto- und Totoblock, creating structural dependency on their product definitions. Direct operating expenses rose 19% in Q1 2026, driven primarily by a 24% rise in payment processing fees, with payment fees rising by €457 thousand due to disproportionate customer adoption of PayPal. Customer identification expenses rose 79% to €208 thousand in Q1 2026, while customer identification costs previously grew 44% (€418 thousand) in FY2024 due to intensive registration growth. However, unilateral state commission reductions or Google advertising policy blocks would immediately crush brokerage profitability
4.
Long-Term Growth and Disruption Resilience: The German commercial lottery brokerage market requires nationwide licenses under the strict State Treaty on Games of Chance with only 14 private commercial lottery brokers holding valid nationwide permits, reflecting high regulatory entry barriers, while assembling compliance, security, and financial infrastructure takes several years, creating high operational barriers to entry. European regulators are increasingly easing product restrictions to boost regulated market competitiveness against offshore platforms, with Germany raising online slot limits from €1.00 to €3.00-5.00 on July 1, 2026 to protect channelization, providing positive institutional support and enhancing regulated slots margins. The primary long-term volume driver is the structural shift of lottery demand from retail to digital, with Germany's online lottery penetration standing at only 31% far below mature markets like Norway's 60%, suggesting online lottery penetration can plausibly exceed 50% mid-term as consumers embrace digital convenience
5.
Aggregate Risks: Apparent platform leverage is highly contingent upon volatile jackpot volumes and regulatory broker licensing, with low jackpot periods compressing customer transaction sizes and significantly depressing monthly billing levels, while regulatory clampdowns or licensing issues in Germany would immediately compromise over 95% of core brokerage revenues. Customer engagement and margins remain highly vulnerable to jackpot statistical variations and marketing inflation, with prolonged periods without peak jackpots of €120 million depressing average billing levels per user, while the consolidated cost per lead rose rapidly by 32% during 2025 as media pricing expanded. State-owned operators hold the regulatory keys, meaning they can vertically integrate or compress commission rates to structurally impair commercial brokerage returns
