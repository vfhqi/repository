Section 3 – What the company does 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Genus occupies a highly favored, high-margin position at the absolute top of the global protein value chain, leveraging deep intellectual property and proprietary elite breeding herds.

Core Species Divisions and Product Portfolio

1.
Porcine genetics division: Genus PIC is the primary profit engine of the group, supplying elite parent gilts, boars, and semen to breed pigs with superior feed conversion and growth traits
2.
Bovine genetics division: Genus ABS sells high-quality beef and dairy bull semen and embryos to maximize milk and meat yields for progressive commercial farmers
3.
Advanced semen sexing: ABS produces and sells sexed cattle semen under its proprietary Sexcel brand, ensuring a heifer offspring 90% of the time on average
4.
Shared technology platform: Both divisions leverage a common R&D platform specializing in genome science, quantitative genomic selection, biosystems engineering, and gene-editing technologies
5.
Proprietary indices and metrics: Genus utilizes proprietary indices like TransitionRight and CBV Max to evaluate and price its most elite genetics based on commercial farm economic benefits

Business Model and Value Chain Dynamics

6.
High-margin royalty contracts: The PIC segment is structured around multi-year royalty contracts, where customers pay a recurring fee for the performance and value delivered by the genetics
7.
Insulated earnings stream: Because royalty revenues are independent of daily pig prices, this model creates a highly stable, highly predictable earnings stream for the porcine segment
8.
Transactional bovine sales: Conversely, the ABS division has historically operated on a transactional, per- straw basis, making its earnings more vulnerable to global dairy and beef herd cycles
9.
Strategic value position: Genus sits at the absolute beginning of the agricultural supply chain, with genetic inputs representing less than 2% of a farmer's total operating cost base
10.
Significant pricing power: Due to the clear economic advantages delivered by its elite genetics, Genus commands premium pricing relative to traditional, non-proprietary competitors
11.
Capital-light multiplier model: PIC does not own massive commercial production herds, instead distributing its genetics through over 450 subcontracted multiplication farms globally to maintain a capital-light footprint

Competitive Landscape and Switching Costs

12.
Monopolistic porcine positioning: Genus PIC is the global leader in pig genetics with an 18% market share, which is more than double its nearest competitor, establishing a monopolistic porcine positioning globally
13.
High customer switching costs: Customers face high switching costs as establishing a new genetic pyramid requires years of herd replacement cycles, discouraging sudden provider changes
14.
Proprietary gene-editing patents: Genus owns exclusive global patent rights to the CD163 gene edit for PRRS resistance, preventing competitors from commercializing identical disease-resistant solutions
15.
Deep research barriers: Regional cooperatives lack the capital and molecular biology scale to match Genus's team of over 100 PhD-qualified researchers and global supply network
16.
Ongoing industry consolidation: The animal genetics sector is steadily consolidating as commercial farmers increasingly demand advanced scientific technologies rather than traditional breeding
17.
side of the story: Competitors could replicate gene-editing research by targeting alternative gene receptors, which would disrupt Genus's patent monopoly and pressure industry-wide margins

Section 5 – The possible investment case 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case is highly compelling because of the compounding effects of the porcine royalty model, bovine margin self-help, and imminent North American gene-editing commercialization.

Porcine Royalty Compounding (Driver 1)

1.
Royalty expansion driver: Genus PIC's high-margin royalty revenue grew 5% in FY25, and is expected to compound at a high single-digit rate over the next three years
2.
Chinese royalty ramp: PIC China signed 25 new royalty customers over the last two years, creating a Chinese royalty runway that will deliver predictable cash flows as herd populations mature
3.
High driver plausibility: The plausibility of this driver is high because it relies on existing commercial contracts that take up to four years to reach mature financial run-rates
4.
Severe disease failure: This royalty expansion mechanism would fail if catastrophic disease outbreaks, such as African Swine Fever, force customers to liquidate herds and cancel contracts

Bovine Value Acceleration (Driver 2)

5.
Structural margin driver: The ABS Value Acceleration Programme completed its second phase in FY25, achieving £11.8m of annualized cost savings and supply chain efficiencies
6.
ABS cost-saving target: Phase three of the program is currently underway, targeting an additional £9m of annualized benefits to drive ABS margins to 10%
7.
High execution plausibility: The plausibility of this cost driver is high, as the savings are entirely within management's operational control and have already improved ABS margins by 190 basis points
8.
Competitor price failure: This margin expansion would fail if intense cooperative competition in the bovine genetics sector forces Genus to pass all savings to farmers

Gene-Editing Commercialization (Driver 3)

9.
Revolutionary product driver: The FDA's landmark approval of the PRRS-Resistant Pig in April 2025 paves the way for commercialization of a gene edit that solves a $1.2bn disease
10.
Canadian regulatory milestone: Canada's regulatory approval in January 2026 represents another critical milestone, bringing Genus closer to full North American launch
11.
Medium driver plausibility: The plausibility of immediate commercial earnings is medium, as Genus must still secure regulatory approvals in Japan and Mexico to protect the export supply chain
12.
Trade restriction failure: This gene-editing driver would fail if trade restrictions or delayed international approvals prevent processors from slaughtering and exporting gene-edited pigs
13.
Independent bull case: For the bull case to deliver a 25% IRR, the drivers do not need to fire simultaneously, as the core porcine and bovine self-help cases are independent of PRP commercialization
14.
Other side of the story: : Consumer backlash or retail bans on gene-edited animal proteins could completely destroy the commercial market for the PRRS-Resistant Pig, rendering decades of R&D investment worthless
