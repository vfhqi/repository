B.1 Business Description 9 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Vertically integrated cable manufacturer: Nexans S.A. is the world's largest vertically integrated cable manufacturer, covering the full chain from copper rod mills to end-of-life recycling via the RecyCâbles JV; operates across metallurgy, low-voltage connectivity, medium-voltage distribution, and high-voltage transmission and installation
2.
Three-segment structure: Electrification split into Transmission (HV subsea/land, 27% of sales), Grid (medium-voltage distribution, 22%), and Connect (low-voltage commercial/residential, 38%); Other/Metallurgy at 13% (declining, legacy)
3.
Revenue scale: EUR 6.1bn standard sales in FY25 (current sales EUR 7.81bn, including copper pass-through); 25,700 employees across 41 countries; 47% revenue in Europe, 19% North America, 12% APAC
4.
Financial character: EBITDA EUR 728m (11.9% margin); ROCE 21.3%; FCF EUR 344m (47.3% conversion — best in cohort); net debt EUR 266m / 0.36x (pre-Republic Wire; pro forma ~1.2x post-acquisition); leverage will rise materially from Republic Wire (EUR 680m) and La Triveneta Cavi (EUR 520m) closings
5.
Business model shift: Moving from product-only sales to comprehensive lifecycle solutions including digital monitoring and smart grid management; SHIFT programme extracted EUR 300m structural EBITDA expansion 2018–2024 through aggressive customer base reduction from 20,000 to 4,000 platinum accounts
6.
CEO transition: Christopher Guérin (7-year CEO) departed October 2025; Julien Hueber (23-year internal veteran, former PWR Grid & Connect Europe head) installed as CEO; CFO Juillard also departed; Vincent Piquet (from Renault Ampere) joined as external CFO from January 2026 — both new leaders are untested in a downcycle
7.
US expansion: Republic Wire acquisition (EUR 680m, 10.3x EV/EBITDA) establishes US Connect beachhead; triples North American revenue to >EUR 1bn; challenges Prysmian-Southwire duopoly in US low-voltage market
8.
SHIFT transformation legacy: The SHIFT programme reduced the customer base from 20,000 to 4,000 platinum accounts (2018–2024); extracted EUR 300m in structural EBITDA; this is the primary internal moat of the Nexans investment case and distinguishes it from the pure capacity-expansion story at NKT
9.
Peer context: Nexans is largest by revenue in the cohort (EUR 6.1bn vs NKT EUR 2.7bn vs PRY EUR 21bn); least HV/EHV-concentrated (~20–25% Transmission EBITDA vs NKT 75–80%); most diversified but least "pure" grid-hardening expression
