1. PUBLISHED SHORT-SELLER REPORTS

A comprehensive search across financial, regulatory, and news repositories yields no formal published short-seller reports on Elekta AB from established activist firms. No campaigns from Muddy Waters, Citron, Gotham City, Hindenburg, Viceroy, ShadowFall, QualityValue, J Capital, Quintessential Capital, or Spruce Point have been published against EKTA as of April 2026.

The absence of a flagship activist presentation is analytically highly significant. It indicates that the substantial short interest in Elekta is not predicated on allegations of outright corporate fraud, fabricated revenues, or hidden off-balance-sheet liabilities. Instead, the bearish positioning is fundamentally driven, reflecting widespread institutional skepticism regarding the company's operational execution, earnings quality, and competitive standing. #I

Why no dedicated short campaign?
- Short-sellers who publish reports typically target companies with alleged fraud, fabricated revenues, or egregious governance failures. Elekta's issues — order book quality, margin delivery, competitive position, execution credibility — are legitimate fundamental concerns but do not offer the narrative hook that generates a viral short report.
- The order book write-downs (SEK 7.1bn aggregate) are material but disclosed; a short-seller needs undisclosed problems to generate a publishable report.
- Elekta's accounting is complex (percentage-of-completion revenue recognition on long-cycle contracts) but consistently audited by a Big Four firm with no known fraud allegation.
- The company is Swedish-listed in Stockholm in a niche medtech sector — it does not attract the same attention as US-listed tech or Chinese reverse-merger targets.

DNB Carnegie notes that the core risk in the Elekta investment case is the company's protracted inability to restore its margins, which have struggled to return to pre-pandemic levels of around 14%. The broker highlights that Elekta's high-end product portfolio has been dated, making it difficult to offset cost inflation and potentially leading to price erosion. Furthermore, Elekta has been significantly outgrown by its larger competitor, Varian Medical Systems, which has increased sales by approximately 10% organically on average over the past three years compared to Elekta's 3%. #I

Jefferies similarly points to a mixed track record on execution, persistent market share losses, and external pressures such as tariffs and foreign exchange headwinds as the primary drivers of bearish sentiment. The firm highlights that Elekta's order growth has lagged its sales targets for several years, meaning that medium-term guidance relies heavily on backlog conversion — a dynamic that has invited institutional scrutiny over the true quality and deliverability of the backlog. #I

Jefferies reported that Elekta shares surged over 35% in a single week following the announcement of a SEK 500 million cost-saving plan and optimism surrounding FDA clearance for the new Evo linear accelerator — demonstrating how crowded and fragile the bear position had become. This violent short-covering rally was fundamentally ignited by a welcome shake-up from the new CEO, the announcement of the cost-saving plan, and rising optimism regarding imminent US FDA clearance for Evo. Prior to this squeeze, analysts had warned the heavy short positioning was a powder keg highly sensitive to any tangible operational improvements. #I

4. ORDER BOOK QUALITY AND BACKLOG WRITE-DOWNS

A central pillar of the institutional bear case is deep-seated scepticism regarding the integrity of Elekta's historical order book disclosures. Over the past twelve months, management cancelled a total of SEK 7.1 billion from its backlog, executed in two tranches: a SEK 4.9 billion cancellation announced following the FY2024/25 review, and an additional SEK 2.2 billion cancellation in Q2 FY2025/26.

Management has defended these write-downs as a proactive "clean-up" to improve predictability, rooted in a much firmer interpretation of order criteria: strictly enforced delivery timelines (cancelling orders extending beyond three years without delivery), verified end-customer site readiness, and formal down payments required. Management has emphasised that the adjustment will have zero impact on near-term cash flow or revenue, with no customer deposits to refund.

However, from a bearish accounting perspective, this historical backlog accumulation represents a severe red flag regarding the company's past commercial disclosures. The sudden elimination of SEK 7.1 billion validates long-standing institutional concerns that the historical backlog was artificially inflated with stale or non-accretive orders that lacked genuine commercial viability. This aggressive order-inclusion policy effectively created an optical illusion of robust demand for years, masking the true extent of Elekta's underlying commercial deterioration. #I

The accounting issue at the heart of the bear case is not straightforward revenue fraud. Revenue is only recognised upon customer acceptance, meaning that Elekta's revenue recognition itself was technically appropriate. The bearish argument is not that Elekta was prematurely recognising revenue on active builds, but rather that the forward-looking revenue pipeline was systematically misrepresented to the market through loose order-inclusion policies.

Revenue recognition complexity (C additive):
Elekta uses percentage-of-completion methodology on long-cycle hardware/software contracts. This requires management estimates of project completion, contract profitability, and collectability. If backlog quality was deteriorating, percentage-of-completion revenue may have been pulled forward before the underlying contract risk was recognised. PwC audits the accounts with no qualified opinion issued; however, the write-downs raise the question in bears' minds. #I

Governance read-through:
The two consecutive write-downs occurred in the first two quarters of the new CEO's tenure — a classic "kitchen-sinking" pattern. New management writes down legacy issues aggressively to set a low base from which to recover. The scale of the write-downs (SEK 7.1bn, ~39% of annual revenue) suggests either the prior management team had serious order quality issues that were not disclosed promptly, or the new CEO is being especially aggressive in cleaning house. Neither narrative is entirely clean. #I

The remaining order book of SEK 34.2 billion is characterised by management as high quality and highly executable, but there is no stated completion date for the order book review as of Q2 reporting — meaning further write-downs cannot be ruled out.

5. RECURRING RESTRUCTURING CHARGES AND NON-GAAP ADJUSTMENTS

Another persistent accounting concern is Elekta's reliance on "Items Affecting Comparability" to adjust its reported profitability. Management frequently utilises non-GAAP adjustments to strip out restructuring costs, allowing the company to report Adjusted EBIT margins that significantly outpace statutory figures.

In Q3 FY2025/26 alone, Elekta recorded a SEK 417 million restructuring charge linked to its "Must-Win Battle 1" initiative — transitioning to a regionally based P&L organisation and cutting approximately 450 jobs (roughly 10% of the global workforce) to generate SEK 500 million in annualised savings.

This is not an isolated event; it is part of a recurring historical pattern. In FY2022/23, Elekta launched a separate cost-reduction initiative targeting SEK 450 million in savings, carrying its own implementation costs of approximately SEK 100 million. The recurring nature of these programmes — each with substantial implementation costs — raises valid institutional concerns about whether Elekta's reported "adjusted" profitability is genuinely reflective of the company's underlying economic earnings power. #I

6. R&D CAPITALISATION AND EARNINGS QUALITY

Perhaps the most critical accounting distortion within Elekta's financials is its aggressive historical capitalisation of Research and Development expenses. Elekta typically spends around 12-13% of total sales on R&D. Historically, the company capitalised a massive portion of this expenditure — sometimes reaching up to 60% of total R&D (SEK 1.2-1.4 billion annually) — artificially supporting reported Adjusted EBIT margins by delaying the recognition of development costs. #I

Morgan Stanley has specifically warned that Elekta's high R&D capitalisation rates mean its earnings are optically inflated relative to industry peers. Varian Medical Systems historically expensed the vast majority of its R&D costs through the P&L — on a like-for-like accounting basis, the margin and valuation gap between Elekta and its primary competitor is substantially wider than reported figures suggest.

This accounting strategy is now unwinding, creating severe headwinds for reported profitability. As historical projects mature, the amortisation of previously capitalised R&D is accelerating rapidly. In FY2023/24 alone, Elekta recorded a massive SEK 1.06 billion non-cash impairment charge after abandoning its internally developed cloud-based software development in favour of an external supplier's platform. #I

Under pressure from the market, Elekta's new management team is executing a structural shift to align capitalisation with amortisation, driving the capitalisation rate down toward 40%. While this will eventually improve cash conversion and earnings quality, it ensures that near-term P&L margins will face continuous downward pressure as the accounting shield is removed.

7. UNITY MR-LINAC ADOPTION CONCERNS

Jefferies survey data shows approximately 76% of radiation oncologists don't view Unity as a viable purchase due to its $8-10 million price tag, specialised workflow requirements, and lack of reimbursement support. Beyond the initial capital cost, the Unity system requires highly expensive, specialised room modifications and suffers from lower daily patient throughput compared to traditional systems. #I

Competitor Varian's Ethos CT-based adaptive therapy system reached 100 orders substantially faster than Unity, proving that competitors are successfully capturing the adaptive radiotherapy market without the prohibitive costs and complexities of MRI integration.

Industry insiders argue that unless Elekta significantly reduces the price to compete directly with other high-end traditional linacs, the Unity will remain a niche product confined to a handful of well-funded university research hospitals. An independent clinical expert corroborates that reimbursement pathways for MR-guided radiotherapy remain largely undeveloped in most key markets. Unity was launched circa 2018; after 7+ years, the global installed base remains limited (estimated 100-150 systems), making it difficult to verify the mass-adoption ramp thesis. #I

8. SOFTWARE STRATEGY FAILURES

Beyond the Unity debate, a significant and underappreciated pillar of the institutional bear case centres on a fundamental failure of Elekta's oncology informatics and software strategy. Industry experts consistently argue that Elekta's proprietary, closed software ecosystem is a severe commercial liability that is systematically locking the company out of lucrative enterprise hospital accounts.

Varian, powered by the Siemens Healthineers ecosystem, has developed a deeply integrated, open-architecture oncology IT platform that enables seamless whole-hospital software deployment, effectively locking Elekta out of lucrative enterprise accounts. Jefferies points out the ultimate failure of Elekta's internal software strategy: the company recently booked a massive SEK 1.06 billion non-cash impairment on its R&D assets after abandoning its internally developed cloud software platform in favour of an external provider. #I

9. HISTORICAL EPISODES — BEAR CYCLE PATTERN

Elekta's equity has historically been subjected to entrenched bearish patterns, driven by a succession of operational and accounting crises.

FY2018/19 — IFRS 15 Revenue Recognition Scare
In 2018, Elekta's transition to IFRS 15 served as a major catalyst for bearish sentiment. The retroactive restatement artificially boosted the company's FY2017/18 EBITA margin by approximately 240 basis points to 21%. This accounting transition masked what turned out to be a rapidly deteriorating commercial environment. As the new revenue recognition standard began flowing through reported numbers, underlying order momentum was revealed to be far weaker than previously apparent. Morgan Stanley raised alarm about the divergence between reported results and underlying order momentum, fuelling concerns that Elekta's core CT linear accelerator business was quietly losing ground to competitors. Stock underperformed the medtech sector for approximately 12 months. #I

FY2020/21 — COVID-19 Disruption
The onset of COVID-19 triggered the next major cycle of institutional short selling. Global hospital lockdowns severely restricted site access, leading to a sharp, immediate decline in installation volumes and capital equipment orders. Order intake plunged by 10% in constant currency during Q4. Elekta's executive team was ultimately forced to entirely withdraw its mid-term financial guidance. Short interest rose to approximately 6-7% of float; stock declined approximately 25% from peak. However, recovery was strong — bulls who bought the COVID dip made 60%+ returns over 18 months, and FY2021/22 order intake recovered strongly. #I

FY2022/23 — Supply Chain and Cost Inflation Crisis
A third major bearish cycle emerged amid severe supply chain disruptions and aggressive global cost inflation. Jefferies documented a compression of roughly 200 basis points in a single quarter. Morgan Stanley documented a massive 14% miss on Adjusted EBIT versus consensus expectations in Q2 FY2022/23, and calculated that gross margins suffered a staggering cumulative compression of 450 basis points between FY2018/19 and FY2021/22. A staggering gap formed between Elekta's recorded order growth and its actual sales realization, leaving the company heavily reliant on converting an aging backlog of orders that were increasingly unprofitable due to unmitigated cost inflation. Short interest rose to 7-8%; stock underperformed for approximately 6 months before stabilising. #I

FY2025/26 — Order Book Write-Downs (Current Episode)
The current episode involves SEK 7.1bn written down in two quarters — the largest in absolute terms in recent history. Critically, unlike the FY2020/21 dislocation (externally driven by COVID), the current episode is internally driven (management-acknowledged order quality failure), which is harder to recover credibility from. Historical precedent from FY2020/21 suggests that if the cleanup is genuine, the stock could re-rate meaningfully over 18-24 months — but only if the turnaround delivers. #I

12. BULL CASE REBUTTALS AND BEAR COUNTER-REBUTTALS

Bull Thesis 1: New Management and Structural Cost Programme

The bull case argues that the appointment of a new CEO represents a genuine and decisive inflection point. The new management team has already demonstrated willingness to make bold, operationally painful decisions: massive backlog clean-up, "Must-Win Battles" framework, comprehensive cost-saving programme.

Bear counter: The cost programme was announced under prior management; restructuring charges have been recurring. At flat revenue and 11.6% margins, the path to 14-16% requires approximately SEK 430-860M of additional EBITA — implausible without revenue growth. After three years of restructuring charges, the gap has not closed materially. Just-Bomholt comes from a commercial/marketing background, not operational turnaround.

Bull Thesis 2: Order Book Write-Downs Are a One-Time Clean-Up

Bullish analysts argue that the cancellation of SEK 7.1 billion in orders represents a final one-time baseline reset by the new management team. Management has confirmed the remaining SEK 34.2 billion order book is of high quality and the clean-up will have no adverse impact on near-term revenue forecasts.

Bear counter: Two consecutive write-down quarters argues against "one-time." The Q2 write-down came three months after Q1, suggesting the underlying order book review is ongoing. There is no independent verification that the remaining backlog is clean, and no stated completion date for the review.

Bull Thesis 3: Unity MRI-Linac Holds a 5+ Year Competitive Lead

The bull case assumes Elekta's Unity MR-linac holds an insurmountable technological lead. A landmark NEJM study demonstrated that Unity delivered significantly better outcomes for prostate cancer treatment (erectile dysfunction at 18 months: 16% Unity vs 36% conventional). The global MOMENTUM study has accumulated data from roughly 6,000 patients.

Bear counter: After 7+ years, the global installed base remains limited (estimated 100-150 systems). Reimbursement pathways for MR-guided radiotherapy remain largely undeveloped in most key markets (US, Japan). Varian's Ethos system reached 100 orders faster at materially lower cost. Unity revenue contribution has not been separately disclosed, making it impossible to verify the ramp thesis.

Bull Thesis 4: China Recovery

Bullish analysts point to a robust recovery in Elekta's Chinese order pipeline, with book-to-bill ratio above 1.3x in Q2, signalling double-digit growth expectations.

Bear counter: China's VBP programme created temporary disruption, but geopolitical risk has escalated since 2024. US-origin component restrictions, potential Chinese localisation requirements, and hospital budget retrenchment all represent genuine headwinds. The "recovery" narrative has been in place for 18+ months without fully materialising at order intake level.

Bull Thesis 5: Software and Service Mix Shift

A key pillar of the long-term bull thesis is that a structural revenue shift toward high-margin software and recurring service contracts will permanently lift profitability. Elekta ONE software revenues demonstrated robust 10% growth in Q4 FY2023/24; software segment margins are approximately 80%.

Bear counter: Software revenue remains a minority of total revenue. Service contracts are often signed at competitive pricing to protect the hardware installed base. Elekta's historical R&D investments have now resulted in a SEK 1.06bn impairment, calling into question the durability of the software strategy pivot. DNB Carnegie warns that service margins have become significantly more volatile than historically observed.

In FY2022/23, the bears were partially right but the stock recovered.
