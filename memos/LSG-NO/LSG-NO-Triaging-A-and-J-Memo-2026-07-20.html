B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Fully integrated seafood group. Lerøy Seafood Group ASA is a Norwegian, fully vertically integrated seafood company dating to 1899, spanning fisheries, farming, processing, sales and global distribution across roughly 350,000-400,000 tonnes annually
2.
Three-segment structure. The group operates through Farming, Wild Catch and Value-Added Processing, Sales and Distribution (VAPS&D)
2.1
Farming sub-units: Lerøy Aurora (North), Lerøy Midt (Central) and Lerøy Sjøtroll (West), plus a 50% joint venture in Scottish Sea Farms
2.2
Wild Catch sub-units: Lerøy Havfisk's 10 trawlers holding ~9% of Norwegian cod quotas north of 62 degrees, and Lerøy Norway Seafoods' 10 processing plants
3.
Downstream is the revenue engine. VAPS&D distributes into 80 markets and 14 end markets, generating the large majority of group revenue and producing roughly 5 million seafood meals a day for customers worldwide
4.
Geographic mix. Europe accounts for approximately 52.3% of Q4 2025 revenue, Norway 16.9%, and Asia-Pacific 16.1%, supported by new branch offices in South Korea, Vietnam and Thailand
5.
Product mix. Whole salmon is the single largest category at 39.8% of FY2025 revenue, with processed salmon second at 26.1%, reflecting only a partial shift toward value-added, less-commoditised product
6.
Financial character. LTM revenue is approximately NOK 34,496m with an LTM operational EBIT margin of 6.70%, though the margin is highly volatile
6.1
Quarterly swing evidence: Quarterly operational EBIT swung from NOK 858m (Q1 2026) to just NOK 15m (Q3 2025) inside four quarters
7.
Balance sheet. Net interest-bearing debt was NOK 7,667m at Q1 2026, book equity ratio 50.0% against a 30.0% covenant minimum
7.1
Deleveraging trajectory: Net debt/EBITDA is guided down to 1.2x in 2026E from 2.4x in 2025, an investment-grade (BBB+) credit profile
8.
Ownership. Austevoll Seafood ASA holds a controlling 52.7% stake, leaving an effective free float of roughly 47.3%; there is no activist presence on the register
9.
Investment-style implication. This is a cyclical, biologically-exposed compounder attempt, not a steady compounder or a pure event-driven special situation
9.1
Closer to self-help turn: The case rests on closing a persistent cost gap against SalMar and Mowi while riding a salmon-price cycle, closer to a self-help / trough-on-trough turn than a quality compounder
10.
Dividend policy. The board proposed NOK 2.5 per share for FY2025, with forward yield guided by DNB Carnegie at approximately 6.3-6.7%, a meaningful income component alongside the re-rating thesis
