Kitron ASA: European Defense Rearmament Powering Structural Transformation in Nordic EMS Leader
Key Takeaways

Defense segment experiencing unprecedented growth , with revenues surging 147% year-over-year in Q4 2025 , driven by European rearmament and sovereign manufacturing imperatives, transforming Kitron from a cyclical industrial supplier into a structural defense compounder.

Record order backlog of EUR 709 million provides exceptional multi-year revenue visibility , with defense procurement contracts featuring strict cancellation penalties and offset requirements that guarantee manufacturing baseloads through at least 2028.

Operating margins expanded to 8.7% in 2025 , approaching the strategic 9% EBIT target , while achieving a world-class 39.3% return on operating capital (ROOC) through exceptional working capital management and customer prepayments in the defense sector.

Strategic acquisition of DeltaNordic fundamentally strengthens aerospace engineering capabilities and establishes localized Swedish manufacturing presence, funded conservatively through equity issuance rather than debt to preserve balance sheet flexibility.

Bifurcated business model creates portfolio tension , as booming defense revenues mask persistent weakness in legacy Connectivity and Industry segments that remain vulnerable to macroeconomic destocking and lack organic growth momentum.

Valuation premium appears stretched at 55% above historical averages , with the stock trading at defense contractor multiples despite maintaining a fundamentally low-margin contract manufacturing model, leaving zero margin for execution missteps.

Critical supply chain dependency on Chinese components for raw printed circuit boards exposes structural vulnerability, threatening the marketed "local-for-local" European manufacturing narrative if geopolitical tensions escalate into export blockades.
