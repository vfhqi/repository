CHF appreciation is the single most persistent structural headwind facing COMT. Approximately 60% of COMT's revenue is denominated in USD and EUR. Costs are predominantly in CHF (Swiss R&D, Swiss headquarters, Swiss manufacturing workforce). When the CHF strengthens, reported revenue in CHF terms falls, but the cost base does not adjust proportionally. #A

Numerically: CHF 457m reported on a +7.3% constant-currency basis implies constant-currency revenue of approximately CHF 510-520m. AEIS comparison: AEIS operates predominantly in USD. Its cost base and revenue base are both USD-denominated. It has no CHF structural risk. When WFE revenue grows in USD terms, AEIS captures it fully. When COMT's WFE revenue grows in USD/EUR terms, a significant portion is translated away into CHF strength. This systematic disadvantage means COMT must grow faster in volume/local currency terms than AEIS just to report the same CHF growth rate. #A

Penang manufacturing as FX hedge: the logic is sound. If COMT manufactures in Penang (Malaysian Ringgit and USD cost environment), those production costs are USD-adjacent rather than CHF. As Penang scales to full utilisation, a growing share of COMT's variable cost base will be in currencies that align with its revenue. But the transition is not instantaneous. #A

Section 232 tariffs (US imports, effective January 2026): COMT ships products from Switzerland and Penang to US-based WFE OEMs. Swiss-origin goods imported into the US may face tariff exposure. The impact is threefold: (1) direct tariff cost increase on Swiss-origin shipments to the US; (2) competitive disadvantage versus AEIS (US-domestic, no tariff exposure); (3) potential for OEM customers to qualify AEIS as an alternative supplier to avoid tariff complications. #A

The combined FX + tariff headwind creates a structural margin gap versus US-domiciled peers that is not easily closed by operational improvement alone. #J

Judgement: Currency/structural headwinds rating: Red. CHF appreciation is persistent, structural, and large (11-12% revenue impact in FY2025). Section 232 adds a new layer of uncertainty. Penang is the right strategic response but is a 2-3 year execution project, not a 2026 fix. #J
