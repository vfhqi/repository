1. Executive Summary

- AutoStore's two-year revenue decline (-7% in 2024, -10% in 2025) places it in the bottom quartile of industrial technology peers on trailing growth, but the order intake inflection in Q4 2025 is genuine.
- Sector context is essential: the 2024-2025 period represented a broad CapEx freeze across European warehouse automation. Kion Group revenue declined approximately 5-8% over the same window; Jungheinrich saw single-digit organic contraction in 2024.
- Kardex Group (KARN-SW) outperformed peers materially in this window, posting mid-single-digit organic growth in 2024, driven by its more diversified vertical exposure and lower ticket sizes — this is a relevant contrast to AutoStore's project-based lumpiness.
- The most significant trend reversal in the peer group over L12M is Kion's stabilisation: after a severe 2022-2023 supply chain and margin reset, Kion's industrial truck segment returned to positive organic growth in H2 2024 and order intake strengthened through 2025.
- The second significant trend reversal is Jungheinrich's margin recovery: after commodity cost inflation compressed EBIT margins in 2022-2023, Jungheinrich returned to >8% EBIT margins in 2024-2025 through pricing discipline and cost structure work.
- The third reversal is AutoStore itself: order intake went from deeply negative YoY in H1 2024 to +4.3% full-year 2025, with Q4 2025 at +35% — the fastest quarterly recovery in the company's public history.
- Sector-wide tailwinds include: labour cost inflation in Europe and North America continuing to justify automation ROI; e-commerce volume normalisation after 2022-2023 overhang; increasing EU regulatory burden on logistics operators favouring capital intensity.
- Sector-wide headwinds include: elevated interest rates compressing customer CapEx IRR thresholds (though rates are now easing); prolonged macro uncertainty in European manufacturing; geopolitical supply chain volatility affecting customer project timing.
- AutoStore's peer ranking on fundamental momentum: Kardex (1st), AutoStore (2nd, recovering), Jungheinrich (3rd), Kion (4th, recovering from deeper trough).
- The AaaS transition is not yet revenue-accretive on a reported basis but is margin-enhancing over time; the market is pricing a clean recovery in system sales alongside AaaS ramp — this simultaneity assumption deserves scrutiny.
- The Ocado patent settlement — $256m paid across 2023-2025 — is now behind AutoStore. This removes a significant cash drain and legal overhang. Post-settlement, free cash flow conversion should improve materially.
- Net debt position remains elevated following the settlement payments; management has guided for deleveraging through 2026 operational cash flow.
- No major M&A activity in 2025. The Berkshire Grey (CarouselAI) partnership is positioned as a product differentiator but has not yet contributed materially to order book or revenue.
- Pio (SMB product) is still subscale; revenue contribution not separately disclosed; represents option value rather than current earnings driver.

Overall Rating: YELLOW — Genuine inflection signal in orders, peer-best margins, but two-year revenue decline and guidance credibility gap prevent a Green rating. Monitor Q1/Q2 2026 order intake for confirmation.

2. Revenue Growth & Organic Dynamics

- AutoStore revenue declined from a peak of $646m in 2023 to $539m in 2025 — a cumulative 16.5% contraction over two years, unwinding approximately 60% of the pandemic-era acceleration from 2021-2022.
- The 2022 revenue of $583m represented +78% YoY growth — a clear pull-forward. The current trough should be read in that context: normalisation rather than structural market share loss.
- Kion Group total revenue declined approximately 4% organically in 2024 before partial stabilisation in 2025. Jungheinrich reported roughly -3% organic revenue in 2024. AutoStore's -7% and -10% are steeper, reflecting greater project-cycle concentration.
- Kardex Group posted approximately +5% organic revenue growth in 2024-2025, driven by its Megamat and Remstar product lines serving industrial/pharmaceutical customers. This is the benchmark AutoStore needs to converge towards.
- AutoStore's end-market breakdown: e-commerce/retail (~40%), food & grocery (~20%), pharma/healthcare (~15%), industrials/third-party logistics (~25%). The e-commerce weighting is a structural headwind in a post-pandemic demand normalisation; pharma and 3PL are more resilient.
- Geographic trajectory: North America has been the strongest region in recent order intake, particularly driven by pharma and consumer goods distribution. European order intake softened more in 2024 but showed recovery signs in late 2025. Asia-Pacific remains a small but growing contributor.
- Pricing power is demonstrated by the gross margin trajectory: revenue fell while gross margin expanded from 58% to 73.1%. This is only possible if price per unit held or increased while mix shifted toward higher-margin software/licensing components.
- Volume trends are harder to parse given AutoStore's project lumping, but the -10% revenue decline in 2025 against a +4.3% order intake growth implies unit volume trough occurred in H1-H2 2024 (booking-to-recognition lag of 12-18 months).
- SS revenue forecast revisions: consensus for 2026 has been progressively revised upward through Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 as the order intake inflection became clearer. The current 2026 consensus revenue estimate implies approximately 15-20% growth from the 2025 base of $539m, targeting approximately $620-640m.
- Forward guidance: management has not reinstated formal full-year guidance for 2026, citing continued macro uncertainty. This is a negative credibility signal. The market is operating on consensus projections without management anchor.
- The AaaS transition introduces a structural accounting distortion: system sales booked upfront; AaaS booked ratably over contract life (typically 3-5 years). As AaaS mix increases, reported revenue growth will systematically understate economic activity. Investors need to track bookings/ARR data, not just reported revenue.
- Sceptical interpretation: the Q4 2025 order intake surge of +35% may reflect project timing acceleration ahead of anticipated 2026 price increases or customer budget-year end effects rather than genuine sustainable demand acceleration. One strong quarter does not establish a trend. The prior "recovery" attempt in Q2 2024 also briefly showed positive order momentum before reverting.
- Volume market share: AutoStore has reportedly maintained ~30-35% global cubic ASRS market share. Exotec and post-settlement Ocado have been gaining at the mid-market. The Pio product is a response to Exotec's Skypod system but is still subscale.
- The backlog of $557m provides approximately 12-13 months of revenue cover at current run rates. This is reasonable but not exceptional; Kardex maintains a similar backlog-to-revenue ratio.
- Revenue guidance credibility: AutoStore issued full-year guidance in early 2025 and withdrew it mid-year. Any 2026 guidance reinstatement will be welcomed by the market but should be treated with appropriate scepticism given the track record.
- North America order momentum is a positive structural signal: the US warehouse automation market is earlier in its adoption curve than Western Europe, providing a longer runway for AutoStore's go-to-market investment in that region.

Revenue Rating: ORANGE — Two-year contraction is real, recovery signal emerging but not confirmed. AaaS accounting adds complexity. No guidance anchor. Watch Q1 2026 order intake.

3. Margin Profile & Evolution

- AutoStore's gross margin expansion from 58% (2022) to 73.1% (2024) is one of the most remarkable margin improvement stories in European industrial technology. The 2025 dip to 72.4% is negligible — less than 1 percentage point on a 15-point expansion.
- Peers by comparison: Kardex gross margin approximately 45-50%; Jungheinrich gross margin approximately 25-30% (manufacturing-heavy); Kion gross margin approximately 22-28% (diversified industrial). AutoStore's 72%+ gross margin profile is more analogous to software/IP businesses than traditional industrial peers.
- The gross margin expansion was driven by three factors: (1) increasing software/licence revenue as a proportion of total system revenue; (2) manufacturing cost efficiencies from Poland and Thailand facilities; (3) price discipline maintained even as volumes declined.
- EBITDA margin trajectory: peak of ~47% in 2023, declining to ~42% in 2025. The 5-percentage-point compression reflects: fixed cost deleverage as revenue fell; elevated R&D investment in AI integration (CarouselAI/Berkshire Grey); management reorganisation costs.
- At 42% EBITDA margin, AutoStore still commands the best margin profile in the peer group by a wide margin: Kion ~8-9%, Jungheinrich ~10-11%, Kardex ~18-22%. The comparison underlines AutoStore's IP-led model advantage.
- Operating leverage trajectory: as revenue recovers toward $620-640m in 2026, the fixed cost base (R&D, G&A, sales infrastructure) should provide meaningful positive operating leverage. Each $100m revenue recovery at a 70%+ gross margin drops approximately $50-60m incremental EBITDA.
- The key margin risk is not on gross margin but on R&D spending: AutoStore has committed to increased AI/software investment to defend its technology moat against Exotec and other entrants. If this investment accelerates, EBITDA margin recovery may be slower than consensus models.
- Depreciation and amortisation: the Ocado patent settlement created significant intangible amortisation charges that will now roll off. This is a positive EBIT (but not EBITDA) tailwind for 2026-2028.
- Peer margin trends: Kion is in active margin recovery mode — targeting EBIT margins of 5-6% for 2026 versus ~3-4% trough. Jungheinrich is more stable at 7-9% EBIT, reflecting its mature market position and strong aftermarket revenue. Neither represents a margin challenge to AutoStore.
- SS expectations for margin: consensus EBITDA margin projections for AutoStore are approximately 44-46% by 2027, implying 2-4 percentage points of recovery from the 2025 trough. This seems achievable if revenue recovers as projected but relies on R&D discipline.
- Management cost commentary: in Q4 2025 results, management highlighted ongoing investment in AI capabilities and Pio SMB product development. No explicit headcount reduction programme. The absence of a cost optimisation programme is worth noting — in a revenue trough environment, peers like Kion pursued active restructuring.
- The AaaS margin impact: AaaS contracts typically carry higher lifetime gross margins (software-like, 80%+) but recognise revenue ratably. As AaaS mix increases, reported gross margin should rise further over time. This is a structural positive that the current margin snapshot understates.
- Manufacturing geography: Poland + Thailand provides cost structure below Western European alternatives. No supply chain disruption flagged in 2024-2025 results. The Thailand facility provides an ASEAN/Asia-Pacific supply buffer.
- Sceptical interpretation: the 73%+ gross margin is structurally high, but its sustainability depends on AutoStore maintaining pricing power as competition intensifies from Exotec (well-funded, French, targeting the same market) and from Ocado's newly licensed system. If AutoStore is forced to offer price concessions to win back volume, gross margin compression back toward 65-68% is possible and would be a material consensus miss.
- Working capital dynamics: project-heavy revenue model creates lumpy working capital. System sale advance payments are a source of early cash collection; AaaS transition shifts this pattern, reducing upfront cash collection. Cash conversion cycle is likely to lengthen as AaaS scales.
- Service/maintenance revenue — not separately disclosed — is presumably growing as the installed base of deployed AutoStore systems grows. This creates an embedded recurring revenue stream that should provide margin floor support regardless of new system sales cyclicality.

Margin Rating: GREEN — Structurally best-in-class margins with durable IP foundation. AaaS mix shift is a long-term margin positive. Key risk is R&D investment acceleration. Minor 2025 EBITDA deleverage is expected; not concerning.

4. EPS Trajectory & Delivery

- EPS declined 51% in 2025 — the single most damaging headline number in this memo. A 51% EPS decline on top of -10% revenue compression represents significant earnings destruction and explains why the stock has traded under pressure.
- The EPS decline was driven by four factors: (1) revenue contraction (-$62m YoY); (2) elevated net interest charges on debt taken to fund the Ocado settlement; (3) the final tranche of the $256m Ocado settlement itself flowing through the P&L; (4) higher effective tax rate as losses in some jurisdictions could not offset gains elsewhere.
- Adjusting for the Ocado settlement impact, underlying EPS decline was materially smaller — perhaps 20-25% rather than 51%. The settlement is now closed; this distortion will not repeat.
- Consensus projects 23% EPS CAGR over the next three years. On a 2025 depressed base, this implies: 2026 EPS recovery of approximately 25-30%; cumulative three-year recovery to approximately 1.7x 2025 EPS. The recovery profile is steep and depends critically on the order intake inflection sustaining.
- SS estimate changes: following Q4 2025 results and the order intake beat, sell-side consensus EPS estimates for 2026 have been revised upward by approximately 5-10% across the analyst community. This is a positive revision momentum signal but from a low base.
- EPS delivery versus guidance: AutoStore did not deliver against its own 2025 guidance (which was withdrawn mid-year). The withdrawal itself was an implicit admission that management's own forecasts were unreliable. Any forward EPS projections carry credibility discount.
- No buyback activity in 2025 — management prioritised debt repayment following the Ocado settlement. This is appropriate capital allocation in a high-debt environment but provides no EPS per-share support.
- Leverage: net debt elevated post-Ocado settlement. Management is guiding for progressive deleveraging through 2026-2027 via operating cash flow. The Ocado payments are now complete, so free cash flow should step up materially in 2026.
- Interest rate sensitivity: net debt exposure means the 2024-2025 rate environment has been a meaningful P&L headwind. As rates decline (ECB cutting cycle ongoing), net interest cost should reduce, providing a modest EPS tailwind.
- Dilution risk: SoftBank (~40%) and Thomas H. Lee (~30%) together control ~70% of the register. Neither has indicated an intention to sell, but any secondary offering — which would be rational for a PE/growth equity investor seeking liquidity after multi-year holding period — would be dilutive and would mechanically depress EPS.
- Peer EPS comparison: Kion and Jungheinrich EPS trajectories were similarly negative in 2024-2025 due to the same CapEx restraint environment. Kardex maintained positive EPS growth through the cycle due to lower single-ticket project exposure. AutoStore's EPS cyclicality is structurally higher than peers.
- The 23% CAGR consensus EPS projection for 2026-2028 is achievable if: order intake sustains at +15-20% in 2026; revenue recovers to $650-700m; EBITDA margin recovers to 44-46%; and net debt reduction reduces interest burden. Each of these is plausible individually; the conjunction requires execution.
- Sceptical interpretation: 23% EPS CAGR from a trough year is a recovery story that the market will price optimistically. The risk is that order intake normalisation in Q1-Q2 2026 reveals the Q4 2025 surge was partly seasonal/timing; that AaaS mix transition suppresses reported revenue recognition; that R&D investment for AI capabilities prevents EBITDA margin recovery. In this scenario, 2026 EPS could undershoot consensus by 15-25%, and the stock re-rates lower despite a positive order narrative. The guidance withdrawal history means there is no management anchor to prevent downward consensus drift.
- Structural EPS quality: AutoStore's IP-led model means that once the revenue/margin recovery is underway, EPS conversion from EBITDA should be high (low depreciation on physical assets, minimal maintenance CapEx, high software margin). This quality is hidden in the current trough year numbers.
- The deferred tax position and settlement-related non-cash items have created noise in reported EPS. Normalised EPS — stripping out settlement amortisation and exceptional items — likely shows a shallower decline, but the market trades on reported numbers and the headline is unambiguously poor.

EPS Rating: ORANGE — 51% EPS decline is a serious headline. Recovery consensus of 23% CAGR is achievable but requires sustained order delivery and guidance credibility restoration. No buyback support. Leverage elevated but declining. Flag: SoftBank secondary risk.

5. Guidance & Forecast Changes

- AutoStore's guidance history in 2025 is the primary credibility problem: initial full-year guidance issued early 2025, withdrawn mid-year citing "continued macro uncertainty and project timing variability." This is the second guidance withdrawal or significant downgrade in three reporting years.
- No formal full-year 2026 guidance has been reinstated as of the Q4 2025 results. Management provided qualitative commentary on order momentum and AaaS pipeline but no revenue or EBITDA margin guidance range.
- The absence of formal guidance for 2026 means the market is operating on consensus projections with no management anchor. This creates asymmetric downside risk: consensus revisions can deteriorate rapidly if Q1 2026 order intake disappoints.
- SS consensus divergence: the high-end analyst case (typically the most bullish sell-side houses) models 2026 revenue at approximately $670-690m, implying 24-28% growth. The bear case models $580-600m, implying approximately 8-11% growth. The spread of approximately $90m on a $539m base represents extraordinary uncertainty.
- Guidance change magnitude L12M: for 2025, the starting consensus revenue estimate (early 2024 vintage) was approximately $670-700m. The outturn was $539m — a miss of approximately 20-25% versus the consensus entry point. This is a large magnitude guidance/consensus failure.
- Guidance change magnitude L3M: since Q4 2025 results, 2026 consensus revenue has been revised upward by approximately 5-8%, reflecting the order intake beat and management's constructive commentary on Q4 momentum extending into early 2026.
- Beat/miss patterns: AutoStore has a recent history of missing quarterly revenue consensus. In each of Q1-Q3 2025, reported revenue came in below consensus due to project timing delays. Q4 2025 was closer to consensus, helped by the order intake beat. The beat/miss ratio over L8 quarters is approximately 2 beats vs 6 misses — not a credible beat track record.
- SS revisions by analyst: UBS and Barclays have been among the more constructive post-Q4 revision cycle, raising 2026/2027 estimates. Deutsche Bank and ABN AMRO have been more cautious, maintaining below-consensus estimates with concerns about AaaS transition revenue drag. JPMorgan holds a roughly neutral position.
- The AaaS transition creates a specific guidance complexity: management cannot easily give traditional revenue guidance when an increasing portion of backlog will convert to ratable rather than upfront revenue. This structural issue makes traditional guidance less relevant as a forecasting tool.
- Bookings versus revenue guidance: the most informative forward metric is order intake, not revenue guidance. The $638m full-year 2025 order intake with a Q4 2025 exit rate of $194m (annualised ~$776m) provides the most constructive forward read. If management pivots to bookings-based guidance, it would enhance transparency.
- Sceptical interpretation: the guidance withdrawal was poorly managed. A mid-year withdrawal, rather than a revised range, suggests internal forecasting was materially worse than public messaging implied. Best-in-class management teams (compare Kardex or Jungheinrich) typically manage guidance conservatively with progressive narrowing. AutoStore's approach more resembles a growth-stage company than a mature, professionally-managed industrial business. This is partly a function of the PE/SoftBank ownership structure where quarterly earnings cadence discipline has historically been weaker.
- The 2026 guidance reinstatement — if it comes with Q1 2026 results — will be a significant catalyst either way. A formal guidance initiation with upside bias would be a strong credibility restoration signal; a further "we prefer not to guide given macro uncertainty" response would be a negative.
- Analyst dispersion on EPS is even wider than on revenue, given differing assumptions about AaaS mix, R&D trajectory, and interest cost normalisation. The range between bull and bear EPS estimates for 2026 is approximately 40-50% — unusually wide for a company of this market capitalisation.
- The combination of: no guidance, wide analyst dispersion, recent miss history, and AaaS accounting complexity creates a situation where the stock will be driven primarily by order intake data points rather than earnings guidance management. This makes each quarterly order intake print a binary event.

Guidance Rating: RED — Track record of guidance failure, no 2026 formal guidance, consensus operating without management anchor. Beat/miss pattern 2:6 unfavourable. AaaS transition further complicates forecasting. Requires sustained delivery before credibility is restored.

7. Overall Fundamental Assessment

Synthesis: Company-specific or sector-wide?

The 2024-2025 revenue decline was predominantly sector-wide — a broad pause in warehouse automation CapEx following the 2021-2022 e-commerce pull-forward. Kion and Jungheinrich provide corroborating evidence; both saw similar demand compression. AutoStore's decline was somewhat steeper in percentage terms because its project-cycle concentration in e-commerce/retail exceeded peers, and because its lumpier, higher-ticket system sales amplify cyclicality. This is an important distinction: AutoStore did not lose structural market position; it experienced amplified cyclicality from its end-market mix.

The gross margin expansion through the revenue trough is the clearest evidence of structural quality preservation. A company losing market share or competitive position cannot expand gross margins by 15 percentage points during a revenue contraction. This metric, more than any other single data point, supports the "sector-wide trough, not structural impairment" thesis.

Bull Case Signal Strength: Moderate-High

- Order intake inflection is real and accelerating: Q4 2025 at +35% YoY is the strongest signal in two years
- Gross margin at 72%+ is structurally durable and likely to expand further as AaaS scales
- Ocado settlement closed: $256m cash drag is now removed, freeing FCF for deleveraging
- Consensus EPS recovery of 23% CAGR is achievable from a depressed 2025 base if order intake sustains
- Patent risk materially reduced: cross-licence in place; legal overhang removed
- AI/automation secular tailwind (labour cost inflation, regulatory burden, e-commerce normalisation at a higher baseline) remains intact

Bear Case Risk Level: Moderate

- 23% EPS CAGR consensus assumes simultaneous revenue recovery, margin stability, and leverage reduction — conjunction risk is real
- No formal 2026 guidance: consensus operating without management anchor; downside revision risk if Q1-Q2 2026 disappoints
- AaaS transition may suppress reported revenue recognition for 2-3 years while the economic value builds; market may punish reported revenue miss even if bookings are healthy
- SoftBank secondary offering risk is non-trivial given multi-year holding period; any partial exit would be dilutive and sentiment-negative
- Exotec competitive pressure is intensifying at the mid-market — Pio is AutoStore's response but is subscale
- Management credibility is impaired after guidance withdrawal; rebuild requires multiple quarters of delivery against expectations

Overall Fundamental Assessment:

AutoStore is at a genuine inflection point: order intake has turned, margins are robust, the Ocado settlement overhang has cleared, and the structural automation thesis is intact. The fundamental trajectory has shifted from clearly negative to cautiously positive. However, the EPS trough was severe, guidance credibility was damaged, the AaaS transition adds near-term revenue recognition complexity, and the forward consensus embeds an aggressive recovery that requires sustained execution delivery.

The most important catalysts for upgrading to Green: (1) formal 2026 guidance reinstatement at Q1 results; (2) Q1 2026 order intake confirmation of the Q4 2025 trend; (3) explicit management communication on AaaS pipeline and ARR growth metrics as new disclosure standards.

The most important risks for a downgrade to Red: (1) Q1 2026 order intake disappointment suggesting Q4 2025 was timing-driven; (2) SoftBank secondary placement announcement; (3) AaaS revenue recognition compressing reported 2026 revenue below consensus despite healthy bookings.

Overall Rate: YELLOW — Inflection signal genuine but not confirmed. Margins structurally strong. EPS credibility gap requires repair through multiple quarters of delivery. Watch Q1 2026 order intake and guidance reinstatement as the two binary catalysts.
