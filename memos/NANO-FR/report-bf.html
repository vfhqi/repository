EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 24 findings

Core investment thesis and strategic positioning

1.
Nanobiotix is a late-stage clinical biopharmaceutical company pioneering physics-based nanotherapeutics to expand cancer treatment options, with its lead therapeutic platform JNJ-1900 (NBTXR3) consisting of functionalized inorganic hafnium oxide crystalline nanoparticles administered via single intratumoral injection and activated by external radiotherapy
2.
The nanoparticles function as a physical radioenhancer , amplifying local radiation dose nine-fold inside the tumor without damaging healthy adjacent tissues
3.
The core NBTXR3 asset was globally out-licensed to Janssen Pharmaceutica (a Johnson & Johnson company) in 2023 under a deal structure representing aggregate milestone value of up to $2.6 billion with tiered double-digit royalties ranging from low-teens to low-twenties percent on global sales
4.
Strategic resource reallocation directs the May 2026 offering proceeds toward next-generation assets, with less than 10% allocated to JNJ-1900 development and 50% to 60% dedicated to advancing the Curadigm Nanoprimer platform

Competitive landscape and long-term disruption threats

17.
NBTXR3's functionalized hafnium oxide nanoparticles amplify local radiation energy dose by nine-fold through a biologically inert crystalline core that provides sustained dose enhancement via a single intratumoral injection without changing healthy tissue radiation exposure
18.
Traditional checkpoint inhibitors like Keytruda and Imfinzi face intrinsic or acquired resistance to systemic anti-PD-1 or anti-PD-L1 antibody backbones, whereas NBTXR3-induced cell death physically turns cold tumors hot , reversing biological checkpoint resistance
19.
