  • Sub: Reported revenue growth includes M&A step-up; organic-only figure is the credibility-relevant metric

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Verdict — guidance posture: Asymmetric patterner — management sandbagged on revenue (consistently), over-promised on margin (consistently); this dual signal is more complex than pure sandbagging and requires different treatment [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Organic revenue guidance mid-point has been below actual in 3 of 4 periods; the low end of the guided range has never been breached in the L4P window; management appears to deliberately embed ~100-200bps of cushion into the revenue range mid-point [#A]

  • Sub: This sandbagging discipline on revenue is consistent with management's stated preference for "under-promise, over-deliver" on growth (reiterated by CFO at FY2024 call)

  • Sub: Veterinary market structure (recurring prescription refills, clinic captive customer base) makes revenue more forecastable than manufacturing or cyclical sectors — low-end sandbagging is easier to sustain

  • Over-promiser signal — margin: EBITDA margin guidance has been above actual outcome in 3 of 4 periods; management has been optimistic on the pace of cost absorption and integration completion [#A]

  • Sub: The ~80bps average margin overstatement suggests management is not deliberately setting a low margin bar; they appear to genuinely miscalibrate the cost side

  • Sub: This is a different failure mode from conscious over-promising — it is structural forecasting error on costs, not revenue

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: EPS has the least formal guidance and the most volatile outcome; "directional" framing allows management to shift the goalposts mid-period by pointing to organic vs reported EPS, adj vs IFRS, or one-off reclassifications [#A]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 3/4 beats; within those beats, magnitude has been declining (210bps → 130bps → tracking ~150bps Q1 2025); cushion is narrowing, consistent with a growing company where guidance calibration naturally tightens [#A]

  • Miss consistency — margin: 3/4 misses; within those misses, magnitude has also declined (120bps worst → 55-70bps recent); partial improvement but not enough to declare a structural fix [#A]

  • Pattern shift: H2 2024 showed the first period where FCF conversion beat ">50%" floor AND margin miss was below 60bps; this may signal early improvement in cost control, but one data point [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat vs miss asymmetry: Revenue beat magnitude (~170bps average) is larger than margin miss magnitude (~80bps average) in absolute bps terms; however, the economic translation is approximately symmetric since a 170bps revenue beat at ~20% incremental margin adds ~35bps to EBITDA margin, partially offsetting a 80bps margin miss — management is not leaving investors with a net negative [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: Trend directionally positive but slow; the core bifurcation (revenue beats, margin misses) persists and has not been resolved [#A]

  • Sceptical read — §3: The revenue sandbagging cushion has been narrowing each period; by FY2026-27, when Vimian's organic growth rate should be moderating toward market growth (as the law of large numbers takes hold), the sandbagging dynamic may invert — management may start missing revenue guidance precisely as it is simultaneously being held to its CMD margin targets; the combination of a revenue miss + margin miss in the same period would be materially de-rating [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Verdict — assumption stack: Revenue assumptions credible; margin assumptions require simultaneous delivery on 3-4 cost variables that have not historically co-operated; EPS assumptions weakest due to leverage sensitivity [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Veterinary market assumed to grow ~6-8% pa globally; US companion animal market decelerated to ~4-5% in 2023 but re-accelerated in 2024; EU market more resilient at ~7-8%; assumption is plausible but not conservative [#A]

  • Sub: Any sustained US pet care spending normalisation (post-COVID pet boom) would pressure the market assumption by ~100-200bps; Vimian's EU/global diversification provides partial buffer

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes ~3-4% price contribution within the organic growth figure; specialty pharma pricing power has historically supported this; however, payor (clinic) pushback on pricing is a latent risk as veterinary clinic chains consolidate [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance is organic-only; however, management has historically deployed €50-150m pa in bolt-on M&A; any major acquisition adds reported revenue but creates integration drag on margins — the guidance structure systematically excludes the cost drag [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: ~100-200bps margin expansion per annum assumes that SG&A grows at roughly half the rate of revenue (positive operating leverage); this has not been delivered in any single full-year period post-IPO; FY2024 was the closest at ~55bps miss [#A]

  • Sub: Distribution and logistics costs (material for Vimian's international specialty distribution network) are semi-fixed and have been inflating above revenue growth rates in the post-COVID period

  • Margin assumption — integration: Post-acquisition integration costs have run SEK 50-100m pa (estimated); management excludes these from adj EBITDA but they dilute reported EBITDA and free cash flow; the "adjusted" margin path is thus contingent on sustained M&A cessation or declining integration drag [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Gross margin stability (~55-57% historically) is the one margin assumption management has consistently delivered; gross margin has not deteriorated materially; the miss has been entirely in the SG&A/opex layer [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt/adj EBITDA has been running ~3.0-3.5x; interest cost sensitivity is meaningful; a 100bps rise in average funding cost on SEK 3-4bn net debt = ~SEK 30-40m additional interest per annum = ~5-8% EPS drag [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate has been ~22-25%; no material assumption risk here given Scandinavian/European mix; stable assumption [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: No formal share buyback programme; dilution from management incentive schemes is ~0.5-1.0% pa; manageable [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: SEK reporting with significant EUR/USD revenues; ~1/3 of revenue in non-SEK currencies; 10% SEK appreciation vs EUR would reduce reported revenue by ~3%; guidance does not hedge this [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: No formal GDP assumption; veterinary spend has historically shown low cyclicality (0.3-0.5 beta to GDP); macro sensitivity is low relative to most consumer sectors [#A]
