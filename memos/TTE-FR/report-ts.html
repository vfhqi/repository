Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. TotalEnergies benefits from a structurally advantaged multi-energy portfolio, combining low-cost upstream oil and gas with a rapidly scaling and highly profitable integrated power business.

Segment Operations and Revenue Streams

1.
Diversified segment operations: TotalEnergies divides its operations into five distinct business segments: Exploration & Production, Integrated LNG, Integrated Power, Refining & Chemicals, and Marketing & Services
2.
Transaction-based revenue: Hydrocarbon revenues are primarily transactional, driven by spot and contract sales of crude oil, refined products, and natural gas
3.
Contractual LNG revenue: Liquefied natural gas revenues are heavily contract-based, with over 70.00% of sales covered by long-term oil-indexed contracts mostly in Asia
4.
Regulated and merchant power: Power revenues are earned through a mix of merchant power trading and long-term capacity payments in Europe

Value Chain and Competitive Advantage

5.
Favoured value chain position: The company sits in a profit-favoured position by owning both upstream resource extraction and downstream processing and distribution assets
6.
Low customer concentration: Customer concentration is extremely low due to a massive global retail network and a diversified industrial customer base
7.
Durable cost advantage: The company's primary competitive advantage is its industry-leading low production cost of $4.90 to $5.00 per barrel of oil equivalent
8.
Durable cost advantage (cont.):
9.
Superior reserve life: A peer-leading proved reserve life of 12.20 years provides long-term production predictability well into the 2030s

Industry Structure and Peer Context

10.
Highly consolidated industry: The global integrated energy sector is highly consolidated and dominated by a small group of supermajors
11.
Differentiated transition strategy: Unlike peers like Shell and BP that scaled back power targets, TotalEnergies has consistently grown its integrated power segment
12.
Advantaged scale growth: The company is consolidating its position as a top-three global LNG player, targeting a 50.00% capacity expansion by 2030
13.
Other side of the story: The company's heavy integration in European power markets exposes it to rising regulatory intervention and power price deflation, which could cap power segment returns
