  • Medium-term — N2Y: FY26 targets under 2024–2026 plan: RAB reaching ~€4.0bn (from ~€3.5bn end-2023); EBITDA ~€460–480m; net debt/EBITDA ≤5.5× [#A]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

J-front verdict: Mixed profile: EBITDA guidance shows superficial sandbagging characteristics (consistent modest beats), but this is ERSE-mechanical not management-discretionary; CapEx guidance is consistently over-optimistic (systematic under-forecast); net verdict is cautious-to-honest on revenue/EBITDA, over-promiser on CapEx and therefore FCF [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA guided to lower end of achievable range in L4 periods; beats of +1.9–3.6% are consistent with a mild sandbagging buffer; European regulated-utility peer median EBITDA beat is ~+2–4%, so RENE is within the peer norm, not a standout sandbagger [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — CapEx: CapEx guidance in all L4 periods reflected best-case procurement and execution assumptions; actuals overran by 10–13% each period; this is the dominant pattern and the most investment-relevant one [#A]

  • Sub-note — structural explanation: RENE's CapEx overruns reflect tendering-cycle slippage and regulatory scope additions (ERSE mandates additional investment mid-period); these are partially exogenous but management consistently fails to budget for scope creep [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: Management implicitly over-promises FCF by providing CapEx guidance that is systematically too low; since FCF = CFFO − CapEx and CFFO beats are narrow (+1–2%), CapEx overruns arithmetically translate to FCF misses [#A]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue guidance is well-calibrated to ERSE's tariff schedules; beats of +1.4–2.4% reflect small volume/inflation upside within a mechanistic framework; management is neither sandbagging nor stretching here [#A]

  • Honest tracker — DPS: DPS guidance is fully credible; 100% delivery rate; management treats €0.18 as a hard commitment rather than a target [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — net debt: Leverage guidance was met in FY21–22 but missed slightly in FY23–24; the miss is directionally consistent with FCF underdelivery compounding CapEx overruns [#A]

  • Pattern shift: FCF miss magnitude has worsened across the four periods (−10% → −19% → −26% → FY24 not yet fully resolved); this is a deteriorating trend, not stable variability [#J]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA vs FCF: The divergence between near-perfect EBITDA beat record and near-perfect FCF miss record is the defining guidance-credibility feature of RENE; investors anchoring on EBITDA beats will systematically overestimate FCF delivery [#J]

  • Sceptical lens — §3: The sandbagging narrative on EBITDA is fragile: with 85% regulated revenues, management has very limited discretion in where to set EBITDA guidance; the "sandbagging" is more accurately described as guidance-setting in line with minimum tariff certainty; CapEx over-optimism is the real discretionary call and it is consistently wrong [#J]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Portuguese corporate tax (surtax regime) has introduced variability in effective tax rates; FY23 effective rate ran ~3pp above guidance assumption; management has been slow to revise tax assumptions upward [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: ECB rate cuts in 2024–25 reduce refinancing risk; RENE's debt maturity profile is manageable (average maturity ~6–7 years); this is a modest tailwind vs 2022–24 headwind [#A]

  • Macro assumption — regulatory reset risk: Post-2026 ERSE review could reset allowed WACC downward, tracking lower benchmark rates; this is the single most impactful macro assumption and is entirely outside management's control [#J]

  • CapEx execution assumption: 2024–2026 Plan assumes €1.0bn CapEx delivered on time and on budget; historical pattern of +11–13% overruns means the plan is likely understated by €100–130m at current overrun rates [#A]
