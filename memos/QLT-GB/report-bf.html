Industry Structure and Competitive Dynamics

The UK investable wealth market totals £5.6 trillion, including £2.1 trillion in liquid cash savings and £614 billion in excess cash . Industry forecasts project that retirement and savings assets will grow roughly 70% over the next decade, expanding the addressable market to £5.0 trillion by 2030 . Auto-enrolment has driven workplace pension participation from 47% to 82%, with over 33 million members now enrolled in private-sector defined contribution schemes . Only 9% of UK adults received regulated financial advice in the 12 months to mid-2024, with roughly 90% of the population not typically considering paid advice . 25

Platform market share is concentrating toward scale players, with the eight largest platforms controlling nearly 60% of total adviser platform assets . FNZ serves as the core technology partner for major platforms including Quilter, abrdn, and Nucleus, leading to intense regulatory scrutiny of systemic vendor concentration . Platform MPS assets reached £230 billion at end-2025, growing at a 27% CAGR since 2019 and representing over 30% of all advised platform assets . Platform fee compression is leveling off, with competitive battles shifting from price cuts toward value-added digital tools and adviser services . 30

Regulatory Risks and Remediation Progress

Quilter recognized a £76 million provision for customer remediation in FY24, subsequently releasing £20 million in late 2025 after strong progress, leaving a £42 million closing balance . The Skilled Person Review of historical ongoing advice charges has been finalized, with the FCA approving a structured, risk-based customer remediation strategy involving cohort identification and detailed case sampling . Jefferies originally estimated restitution costs up to £170 million, meaning the smaller £76 million provision removed a major regulatory overhang from the stock . The new FCA Targeted Support regime launched April 6, 2026, allowing firms to provide readymade suggestions to consumer groups, potentially benefiting up to 21.5 million UK savers .

Regulators are implementing stricter due diligence rules for pension transfers, including explicit requirements for SIPP operators and a new Pension Scheme Money and Assets regime . Apparent platform fee stability could rapidly disintegrate if the FCA enforces strict value assessments under Consumer Duty rules or if AI-driven planning tools successfully disintermediate human-led wealth advice networks .
