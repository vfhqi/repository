EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The activist presence assessment reveals no active traditional activist campaigns in the available library for Altri SGPS S.A., yet significant structural governance and strategic friction points exist. Reference family shareholders retain massive capital stakes minimizing agency conflict risks, with board member Ana Rebelo de Mendonça holding a 17.54% stake through Promendo Investimentos, Paulo Fernandes holding a 14.78% stake through Actium Capital, and João Borges de Oliveira holding 16.67% of capital through Caderno Azul. However, independent directors represent only 20% of the board, leaving room for governance improvements despite female directors representing 33% of the board. The most material quasi-activist pressure emerges from regulatory intervention, as Xunta de Galicia initiated procedures to archive Project Gama's industrial status in 2026, while central administration excluded Project Gama from national electricity planning through 2030. This regulatory exclusion of the €1,000M Spanish expansion effectively represents an external constraint on strategic optionality comparable to activist limitations, forcing management to actively prepare arguments to appeal the Galician archiving decision while seeking alternative electrical connections independent of the national grid planning.

The synthesized verdict reveals Altri as a classic self-help transformation candidate navigating severe cyclical compression while executing material portfolio repositioning toward structurally superior bioeconomy segments. The investment case hinges critically on the company's ability to deliver three simultaneous industrial conversions before leverage constraints or South American capacity additions trigger a structural pricing downcycle that would compress margins before specialty premiums fully materialize. Management credibility is high based on historical honest-tracker behavior and operational execution consistency, yet the compressed timeframe for deleveraging combined with elevated cost curve positioning relative to Nordic and LatAm peers creates meaningful downside vulnerability. The bull case requires successful near-term BHKP pricing stabilization, flawless execution of Biotek and Caima startups by late 2026, and sustained USD stability to compound specialty margin expansion, yielding an estimated joint probability near 50% when accounting for cross-dependencies and external market risks that remain beyond management control.
