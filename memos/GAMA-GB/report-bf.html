EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Gamma Communications operates as a critical infrastructure provider, supplying integrated cloud communications and connectivity services to European enterprises, provisioning essential cloud communications, enterprise calling, and secure data connectivity solutions across Western Europe

. The company demonstrates robust structural quality through its highly-recurring business model, successfully offsetting legacy UK SME headwinds via decisive, scale-building German acquisitions . Operations cover the UK, Germany, Spain, and the Netherlands, targeting micro-businesses through to large public sector entities, with a commercial framework relying overwhelmingly on sticky, long-term unified communications subscriptions and voice traffic rentals .

Gamma demonstrates exceptional financial consistency, pairing high returns on capital (27.8% ROCE) with robust cash conversion, significantly outperforming European peer NFON . Group revenue expanded from £191.8m in 2015 to £645.8m in 2025, representing a 13 percent 10-year CAGR , while adjusted EBITDA grew at a 17 percent CAGR from £28.3m in 2015 to £141.7m in 2025 .

Recurring revenue remains exceptionally stable at 89%, materially outperforming broader European telecom sector medians of roughly 75%, yielding an adjusted cash conversion rate of 93% in FY25 .

Gamma delivered a 27.8 percent ROCE in 2025, marginally up from 27.4 percent in 2024, fundamentally outperforming traditional European telecoms through its asset-light, software-centric operating framework .

The balance sheet shifted from £153.7m net cash in 2024 to a marginal £9.3m net debt position in 2025, entirely driven by the £152.2m cash outflow for the Starface acquisition .

The European cloud communications industry offers highly favourable growth economics, with the European Contact Centre as a Service market reaching approximately €1.83 billion in 2025
