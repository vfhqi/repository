Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Stadler Rail offers high forward revenue predictability backed by a record CHF 32.3bn order backlog, though near-term profitability has been severely depressed by natural disasters and delayed acceptances
1.
Custom vehicle focus: Stadler designs and manufactures highly customized regional passenger trains, locomotives, and urban transit vehicles, while rapidly expanding its service and digital signaling divisions
2.
High-growth recovery case: The thesis relies on a massive step-up in revenues to over CHF 5.0bn by FY2026, driven by backlog execution and operational normalization post-flooding
3.
Back-end loaded accounting: Revenue recognition relies on conservative units-of-delivery accounting, booked only upon customer acceptance, creating a multi-year lag
4.
Improved operating result: In FY2025, revenues grew 13% to CHF 3.68bn, and operating profit increased 60% to CHF 160.6m
5.
Execution delay risk: Severe weather events in Spain, Switzerland, and Austria delayed CHF 350m of revenues and disrupted key supplier facilities
6.
Profitable service expansion: High-margin Service and Components order backlog reached CHF 9.3bn in FY2025
7.
Mixed investment verdict: A closer look is warranted because the massive valuation discount to historical multiples is unjustified given structural cash collection expected through FY2026

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Stadler has established a highly competitive niche in regional multiple units and locomotives, enjoying strong pricing power and high customer switching costs via long-term service agreements

Core Product and Segment Mix

1.
Core rolling stock: Rolling Stock represents 80.3% of total third-party revenues, or CHF 2.95bn in FY2025
2.
Expanding service segment: Service and Components contributed 16.5% of FY2025 group revenues, growing 19% to CHF 607.4m
3.
High-growth signaling: The Signalling division contributes 3.2% of sales, CHF 117.5m
4.
Decarbonization product leadership: Stadler holds 50% of alternative-drive trains in Europe, led by battery and hydrogen multiple units
5.
High project visibility: Stadler records only firm orders with no uncalled options; the CHF 32.3bn backlog represents solid, contractually binding commitments

Value Chain Positioning and Customers

6.
Customer concentration risk: Customers consist primarily of state-owned rail operators and regional transit authorities
7.
Favourable niche positioning: Former directors state that Stadler's willingness to customize standard platforms provides a major advantage over Alstom's rigid standardization
8.
Significant switching costs: Long-term service agreements, lasting 10-30 years, create massive switching costs
9.
Highly consolidated industry: Alstom, Siemens, and Stadler control over 90% of deliveries in key regional passenger categories
10.
Other side of the story: Increased bidding competition from low-cost Chinese state-backed suppliers like CRRC threatens to erode Stadler's pricing power

Accelerating top-line recovery: Revenues rose 13% (15% FX-adjusted) in FY2025 to CHF 3.68bn

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is highly compelling given the guaranteed CHF 5bn revenue milestone in FY2026, though execution is vulnerable to supply chain disruptions and delayed acceptances
1.
Clear revenue acceleration: The primary earnings driver is the CHF 32.3bn order backlog, contractually securing the leap in FY2026 revenues to over CHF 5.0bn
2.
Units-of-delivery cash catch-up: The CHF -588.4m cash outflow in FY2025 is expected to reverse into heavy cash inflows in FY2026
3.
Backlog pricing protection: Roughly 80% of major rolling stock and service agreements include price escalation indexation
4.
Highly profitable services: Service and Components revenues grew 19% in FY2025 to CHF 607.4m, with a CHF 9.3bn backlog
5.
Scale-driven cost absorption: Stepping up revenues to over CHF 5.0bn in FY2026 will raise margins toward the 6-8% target
6.
Successful US division: A dedicated North America division supports the USD 500m MARTA project
7.
Other side of the story: If customers delay train acceptances due to administrative backlog, revenue recognition will be deferred for several quarters

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Stadler's valuation is highly attractive compared to historic ranges and European capital goods peers, pricing in excessive pessimism around execution and cash flows
1.
Highly depressed multiples: Market cap of CHF 2.80bn, reflecting significant compression compared to its 2019 IPO valuation of CHF 4.0bn
2.
Low peer multiples: European railway peers trade at a median EV/EBITDA of 5.5x and P/E of 11.3x
3.
Under-priced sector trajectory: Rival Alstom SA trades at an EV/EBITDA of 9.1x
4.
Market mispricing mechanism: The market is pricing in persistent negative free cash flows, failing to recognize that units-of-delivery accounting reverses cash outflows into heavy inflows once deliveries peak
5.
High target upside: Rothschild & Co Redburn maintains a DCF-based target price of €28.00 on peer Alstom SA
6.
Other side of the story: Persistent negative cash flows and delayed deliveries could lead the market to permanently discount the stock

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Extreme weather events and localized supply chain halts represent highly credible operational risks, though Stadler's strong decentralized production mitigation limits terminal portfolio damage
1.
Localized weather disasters: Flooding in Valencia, Valais, and Austria delayed revenues by CHF 350m
2.
Single-supplier bottleneck: Flooding of supplier Constellium's Valais facility halted aluminum profile deliveries for several months
3.
Contractual penalty threats: Late deliveries to major municipal authorities, such as the Berlin Metro, pose risks of contractual penalties
4.
Belarus asset write-down: Geopolitical sanctions forced Stadler to wind down its Belarus plant, shifting assembly lines to Poland
5.
Strong competitor bidding: SBB recently awarded a 200 double-decker train framework contract to Siemens
6.
False friend backlog: Rising order backlog can act as a false friend if legacy, low-margin contracts lack sufficient price-escalation coverage
7.
Other side of the story: Risks are overstated because over 80% of major rolling stock contracts contain robust indexation clauses

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Stadler's governance is undergoing a generational shift on the Board of Directors, while operational management remains highly stable under long-serving industry leaders
1.
Rejuvenated board structure: The AGM elected Danijela Karelse and Niko Warbanoff as new board members
2.
Orderly board transition: Barbara Egger-Jenzer and Kurt Rüegg chose not to run for re-election, reducing the board to eight members
3.
Management structure stability: Group CEO Markus Bernsteiner has held numerous operational management positions since joining in 1999
4.
Strong insider alignment: Executive Chairman Peter Spuhler raised his shareholdings from 42.14m to 42.39m shares in FY2025

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. A deeper look into Stadler Rail is highly warranted given the asymmetric risk-reward of its CHF 32bn backlog and the highly predictable revenue leap in FY2026
1.
Compelling recovery case: The strongest bull argument is the contractually secured leap in revenues to over CHF 5.0bn by FY2026
2.
Other side of the story: The bear case centers on severe weather execution risks and customer acceptance delays that could permanently impair cash conversion
3.
Concrete validation action: Investigate the monthly delivery and acceptance schedule for the Berlin Metro contract
4.
Structured diligence step: Analyze the exact working capital requirements for the FY2026 production ramp-up
