B.1 Business Description 8 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business: monopoly cross-Channel transport and energy infrastructure operator. Getlink SE owns and operates the Channel Tunnel Fixed Link under a concession running to 2086, monetised through four segments:
1.1
Eurotunnel shuttle and rail-toll services (~75% of revenue), ElecLink electricity interconnector (14.1% of revenue), Europorte rail freight logistics (10.8% of revenue), and smaller customs/other services
1.2
Physical moat: The Treaty of Canterbury bars publicly-funded duplication of the tunnel, and a private competing tunnel would require an investment exceeding €20bn; this is an effectively unchallengeable structural monopoly on the shortest sea crossing between France and the UK
2.
Geographic structure: UK-France cross-border with EUR/GBP dual-currency exposure. Revenue splits roughly two-thirds EUR / one-third GBP with ~60% of operating costs in EUR
2.1
The group carries zero financial derivative FX hedging (hedge ratio 0.00%), a genuine outlier versus eurozone-only concession peers
2.2
FX sensitivity: A 10% sterling depreciation reduces reported revenue by €64m and EBITDA by €46m; a 30% dual-shock (sterling plus an ElecLink outage) would breach the minimum debt-service cover ratio of 1.25x
3.
Financial character: high-margin infrastructure compounder with capex-heavy medium-term profile. FY2025 EBITDA €859m at a 53.9% margin (highest in its European transport-infrastructure peer set bar none, versus Vinci 16% and Eiffage 18%)
3.1
Net debt/EBITDA improved to 3.9x from 4.3x
3.2
Free cash flow fell 20.6% to €374m in FY2025 as capex rose to €190m, guided €170-220m annually to 2032
3.3
Segment margin spread: ElecLink 70.2% EBITDA margin, Eurotunnel/Fixed Link 55.6%, Europorte rail freight only 19.8% — group blended margin is a function of segment mix, not uniform operating leverage
4.
Investment style: regulated/quasi-regulated infrastructure concession with event-driven optionality. The core Eurotunnel toll business behaves as a bond-like inflation-linked annuity (Railway Usage Contract indexed to CPI/RPI minus 1.1% to 2052)
4.1
ElecLink behaves as a merchant-energy trading business layered on top, introducing genuine earnings volatility the core concession does not otherwise carry
5.
Ownership structure is a strategic-anchor-dominated register, not a widely-held float. Eiffage holds 29.40% of capital / 29.50% of voting rights
5.1
Mundys holds 19.00-25.00% of capital / 29.90% of voting rights (the two disclosures diverge across reports, flagged in F.2)
5.2
Both sit symmetrically just below the 30% mandatory-offer threshold, gridlocking buybacks
6.
Governance is centralised and long-tenured. Chairman Jacques Gounon has led since 2007 (age limit raised from 70 to 75 in 2025 specifically to retain him)
6.1
CEO Yann Leriche (in post since 2020, reappointed to 2028) requires board sign-off above €10-20m thresholds
7.
**Peer rank on margin is best-in-class
7.1
Peer rank on growth is pedestrian.** Getlink's 53.9% EBITDA margin dwarfs the civil-contractor peer set (Vinci, Eiffage, ACS, Hochtief all sub-20%) and even beats most airport-concession peers (Aena ~59%)
7.2
But underlying volume growth is structurally low-single-digit, a genuine "quality but slow-growth" profile

CEO Yann Leriche: École Polytechnique (1997) plus École des Ponts — the classic French "Corps des Ponts" grande-école infrastructure-executive pedigree
