Section B — Investment case: Executive summary

Section-level BLUF: NKT is a genuine large-positive-change-forces case — the Triaging qualitative test is comfortably met — but it is a change-forces case whose attractiveness as an investment has been substantially recognised by the market in the last three weeks, which moves the centre of gravity at Triaging from "is the change real?" (yes) to "is there still enough change-from-current left to underwrite?" (narrower than it was).
  • ❌ The entry point has moved hard against the buyer, and that is now the single most important fact in the case. The 25-Apr research base read NKT at DKK ~867 with the Street's average target below the price; on 14-May the stock is at a 52-week high of ~DKK 1,050, the Street-high target has been reached, and the Q1 2026 "transition-year" print beat consensus by a wide margin. The change forces are large and real, but a meaningful slice of the change-from-current that a Triaging buyer would want has been paid away in 19 days
  • The change-forces case is large, multi-vector, and structurally rather than cyclically driven. A EUR 10.2bn-plus high-voltage backlog (likely >EUR 12bn post-EGL3 and the SSEN conversion), 95% from quasi-sovereign European transmission system operators, with embedded pricing that has roughly doubled per kilometre since 2019; an EU policy framework (Grids Package, Net-Zero Industry Act, ENTSO-E build-out) that mandates the demand; and a supply-constrained oligopoly that hands incumbents pricing power. This is a strong Pillar I
  • The case is unusually focused and unusually clean, which cuts both ways. NKT is the purest pure-play of the cohort: ~80% of EBITDA from high voltage, a net-cash balance sheet, one dominant site (Karlskrona). That focus is the source of the operating leverage and the reason the case is simple enough to underwrite at Triaging, but it also means the case has almost no diversification buffer and is, in the end, a bet on one factory ramp
  • The fulcrum is binary and singular: Karlskrona. The entire 2027+ inflection (the path from EUR 390m EBITDA toward the >EUR 900m 2030 ambition) runs through one capacity expansion that has already absorbed a EUR ~300m cost overrun. Triaging cannot resolve this; it can only name it as the question Early-Stage Assessment must answer
  • Recommendation: PROGRESS to Early-Stage Assessment, but progress it as a "valuation-and-entry" case, not a "discovery" case. The change forces clear the Triaging test with room to spare. What Early-Stage Assessment must establish is not whether NKT is a good business (it visibly is) but whether, at a 52-week high and after a consensus-beating print, the residual change-from-current is large enough, and the Karlskrona execution risk small enough, to underwrite a concentrated position. E1 — Required input change forces (internal / external) — first pass, ×2 double-weight (×2 double-weight)B

Element judgement — [B]. The change forces acting on NKT are large, structural rather than cyclical, internally and externally sourced, and visible in the financials already — comfortably enough to clear the Triaging qualitative test. The grade is held at [B] rather than [A] because the second Required Attribute — the "robust base," and specifically the mispricing and low-multiple Core Questions — has weakened materially in the 19 days since the research base: the change is real, but a meaningful portion of the change-from-current has been paid away by a 21% re-rating to a 52-week high.

RA1 — Change forces? — summary (6 bullets minimum)A
  • Verdict: strong, dual-sourced change forces, predominantly external but with a real internal leg: the case is driven by an external demand supercycle (HVDC interconnectors, offshore wind, grid reinforcement) and an internal step-change (the completed pure-play transformation plus the Karlskrona-led capacity near-doubling). Having both legs is what makes this an [A]-strength RA1
  • The external change force is policy-mandated, not cyclical: the EU Grids Package (December 2025) frames EUR ~584bn of grid investment and a 40% domestic-sourcing target by 2030; ENTSO-E's development plan identifies 88 GW of additional cross-border capacity needed by 2030 and 108 GW more by 2040. This is binding regulatory demand, not discretionary capex that can be deferred in a downturn
  • The internal change force is a genuine identity change, now complete: NKT divested Photonics (May 2024) and acquired SolidAl (June 2024), finishing a multi-decade conglomerate-to-pure-play transformation, the company is now ~100% power cables and ~80% of EBITDA is high-voltage, versus Prysmian's 20–25%. The "Charging Forward" strategy (November 2025) and the 1-January-2026 reorganisation into Transmission / Distribution / Grid Solutions & Accessories operationalise it
  • The change is already visible in delivered numbers, not just forecast: operational EBITDA compounded from EUR 15m (2019) to EUR 390m (2025), four consecutive record years; the Q1 2026 print (EUR 97m operational EBITDA vs EUR 84m consensus, 16.0% margin vs 13.4% consensus, record order intake) is fresh, post-research-base confirmation that the change forces are converting
  • The pricing leg of the change force is the most underappreciated: DNB Carnegie's contract-by-contract analysis shows revenue per kilometre roughly doubling, EUR ~0.61m/km (2019–21) to EUR ~1.21m/km (2022–23) to EUR ~1.44m/km (2024–26 at 525kV DC); Morgan Stanley models blended backlog execution price up ~80% by 2027. This is a supply-constrained repricing already embedded in a multi-year backlog, so it flows through mechanically as legacy contracts roll off
  • Counter-consideration on durability: Morgan Stanley also models the supply deficit transitioning toward balance by ~2030 as LS Cable and Sumitomo add ~35% to global HV capacity, so the pricing change force is powerful through the late 2020s but not perpetual. At Triaging this does not weaken RA1 (the change-force window comfortably covers a 12–24-month hold) but it is a flag for Early-Stage Assessment

RA1 Core Questions:

E1 RA1 CQ1 — Strong external change forces / tailwinds? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes, and they are unusually well-evidenced and unusually durable: the external tailwind is the European energy-transition grid build-out, and it is mandated by binding EU and national policy rather than driven by a discretionary capex cycle, the strongest possible form of an external change force
  • Demand visibility is quantified and policy-anchored: ENTSO-E's TYNDP identifies 88 GW of additional cross-border capacity by 2030; over 1 TW of renewable capacity sits in European grid connection queues; the EU Grids Package frames EUR ~584bn of grid investment. These are not NKT forecasts, they are regulatory and system-operator commitments
  • The supply side amplifies the tailwind into pricing power: Morgan Stanley estimates EUR ~13bn of necessary HV projects have been deferred to the 2030s because of supply shortage, not demand shortage, the binding constraint in the value chain is manufacturing and vessel capacity, which hands incumbents the ability to select projects and price them up
  • A second, additive external tailwind is data-centre power demand: Jefferies notes AI workloads could require up to ~100 GW of dedicated global power by 2030; hyperscaler demand is largely absent from traditional utility planning, so it is incremental to the policy-driven number and benefits both HV connections and MV grid reinforcement
  • The energy-security framing gives the tailwind political durability: post-Ukraine and post-Iran (March 2026), cross-border interconnectors are treated as national-security infrastructure with cross-party support, which insulates the demand from a change of government or a fiscal-austerity cycle far better than typical industrial capex
  • Counter-consideration: the one genuine external-demand soft spot is German policy favouring overhead lines over underground cables to cut cost, which adds uncertainty to the onshore German land-cable pipeline specifically; offshore and interconnector demand is unaffected, so this is a composition risk within a still-strongly-positive external picture

E1 RA1 CQ2 — Strong internal change forces? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes, and the internal change is a completed structural transformation plus a live capacity step-change, not a vague "self-help" story: NKT has actually finished becoming a different company, and is in the middle of nearly doubling the capacity of its core segment
  • The portfolio transformation is done and verifiable: Photonics sold (May 2024, ~EUR 205–225m), SolidAl acquired (June 2024, ~EUR 192m at ~9.4x pre-synergy EV/EBITDA, ~7x post-synergy per DNB Carnegie), NKT is now a pure-play power-cable company with no conglomerate dilution left to clean up
  • The capacity step-change is the live internal change force: the EUR ~1.3bn Karlskrona programme (third 200m extrusion tower, harbour expansion, a new 4,000 sqm HV test centre) lifts high-voltage output by roughly 50% from 2027; DNB Carnegie projects the single-site concentration will give NKT the highest HV margins in the global sector by 2028
  • A second capacity leg is the NKT Eleonora cable-laying vessel: hull launched April 2026, operational from 2027, doubling installation bandwidth from one vessel to two, internal change on the installation side of the value chain, not just manufacturing, which matters because vessel availability is itself a binding constraint
  • The internal change is being run by a credibly mandated team: CEO Claes Westerlind (appointed May 2023, internal, ex-ABB HVDC background) carries an explicit engineer-operator "execute and scale" mandate; the CFO transition (Michael Yong, internal, effective 1-April-2026, authored "Charging Forward") is low-risk; the LTI is 50% EBITDA / 50% absolute TSR, aligning the team through the ramp
  • Counter-consideration: the internal change force has a blemish, the EUR ~300m (25–30%) Karlskrona cost overrun, plus mid-level departures (the Cologne HV Solutions head and two EVPs) during the critical scaling phase. This does not negate the internal change force but it is the reason the internal leg is "strong with an asterisk" rather than unqualified
RA2 — Robust base? — summary (6 bullets minimum)
  • Verdict: the base is operationally robust but, on valuation, no longer cheap: this is the soft Required Attribute of E1: NKT is well-invested, has delivered congruently with the thesis, and has a structural change-from-current, but the "large mispricing" and "low trading multiple" Core Questions have weakened materially since the research base
  • Operationally the base is genuinely robust: a net-cash balance sheet (EUR 963m net cash at end-2025), customer prepayments structurally funding the capex programme, four consecutive record-EBITDA years, and a backlog providing 5–7+ years of revenue visibility, there is no balance-sheet fragility and no demand-coverage gap
  • The "well-invested base" question is genuinely positive: NKT is not under-invested and playing catch-up, it is mid-cycle on a EUR ~2bn 2025–2028 programme that is largely self-funded, with the assets capitalised pre-revenue (which is exactly why 2025 ROCE optically diluted from ~35% to ~24%)
  • The thesis-congruency question is positive and freshly confirmed: guidance was met or beaten in 2022, 2023, 2024 and 2025, raised intra-year in 2024 and 2025, and the Q1 2026 print beat consensus, the fundamental delivery record is congruent with the change-forces thesis
  • But the mispricing and multiple questions have turned: at the 25-Apr research-base price (DKK ~867) the Street's average target sat below the price; on 14-May the stock is at a 52-week high (~DKK 1,050), the Street-high target has been reached, and EV/EBITDA on 2025 operational EBITDA is ~15–16x, richer than Prysmian (~10–12x) and far richer than Nexans (~7–8x). The "low trading multiple" Core Question can no longer be answered positively
  • Counter-consideration / what saves RA2 from a poor grade: the multiple is high on trough earnings, 2026 is a guided transition year, and on 2028–2030 estimates the same EV falls to a far less demanding figure; so the base is not expensive on a through-the-ramp view, it is simply no longer cheap on a current view. That is enough to keep RA2 at the lower end of acceptable rather than failing it, but it is the single most important reservation in E1

RA2 Core Questions:

E1 RA2 CQ1 — Absence of external headwinds? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes, broadly: the external environment is supportive, with two identifiable but contained headwinds rather than a structural exogenous problem: this Core Question is comfortably positive, which matters because Stage II (Filtering Out) is precisely about side-stepping large or worsening exogenous risks
  • The dominant external forces are tailwinds, not headwinds: EU policy, energy-security politics, the supply-demand imbalance and data-centre demand all push the same way, there is no large, worsening exogenous force acting against the case
  • Headwind one: German overhead-vs-underground policy: German policymakers favouring overhead lines to cut cost adds uncertainty to the onshore German land-cable pipeline; it is a composition headwind within a specific sub-market, not a threat to offshore or interconnector demand
  • Headwind two: Asian capacity additions, but the timing is late: LS Cable (525kV type approval from TenneT, EUR ~1.09bn Port of Tyne factory) and Sumitomo (GBP ~350m Scottish Highlands factory) are real, but Morgan Stanley's own modelling puts the market-balancing effect at ~2030, outside a Triaging-stage 12–24-month horizon
  • Currency is a minor, manageable external factor: NKT reports in EUR with DKK-listed shares and multi-currency project revenues; it is a noise item, not a headwind of any size
  • Counter-consideration: the genuine external tail risk is a European recession coinciding with the late-decade capacity additions, but for a Triaging-horizon assessment the external picture is clearly clear-enough-to-pass, and the recession-plus-capacity scenario is an Early-Stage Assessment bear-case input, not a Triaging disqualifier

E1 RA2 CQ2 — Well-invested base? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes, emphatically: NKT is at the investing end of its cycle, self-funding a transformational capacity programme, with no under-investment to remediate: this is one of the strongest individual Core Questions in the memo
  • The scale of the investment is transformational, not maintenance: EUR ~2bn cumulative capex 2025–2028 (~75% growth, ~25% maintenance) against a EUR ~5.6–7.5bn market cap, this is a company building its future earnings base, not harvesting a tired one
  • The investment is self-funded: no equity-dilution risk: the EUR 963m net-cash position plus customer prepayments fund the programme; there is no rights issue or convertible overhang in the case
  • The investment is in the right place: Karlskrona concentration (three extrusion towers, HV subsea capacity, R&D, the new test centre on one site) is projected by DNB Carnegie to produce the highest HV margins in the global sector by 2028, the capital is going into the highest-return part of the value chain
  • The post-2028 profile is attractive: once the programme completes, maintenance capex drops to ~4% of revenue and free cash flow inflects, the heavy-investment phase has a defined end, which is the right shape for a "well-invested base."
  • Counter-consideration: "well-invested" carries the corollary that the capital is now committed and path-dependent, NKT must fill the new capacity for the investment to earn its return, so the well-invested base and the Karlskrona execution risk are two sides of one coin; CQ2 is strongly positive on the investment itself, with the fill-rate risk captured under E7 and E1 RA2 CQ3

E1 RA2 CQ3 — Past/present fundamental delivery congruency with thesis? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes: the delivery record is congruent with the change-forces thesis and has just been freshly re-confirmed by the Q1 2026 beat: the company has done what the thesis says it should be doing
  • The multi-year record is one of consistent delivery: guidance met or beaten in 2022, 2023, 2024 and 2025, with intra-year upgrades in 2024 and 2025, a serial under-promise / over-deliver pattern that is exactly what a credible change-forces case should show
  • The EBITDA trajectory matches the thesis: EUR 255m (2023) to EUR 344m (2024) to EUR 390m (2025), four consecutive record years, with the Q2 and Q3 2025 quarters being the first-ever EUR 100m-plus quarters, the operating leverage the thesis predicts is visible in the prints
  • The Q1 2026 print is fresh, post-research-base congruency evidence: EUR 97m operational EBITDA versus EUR 84m consensus, a 16.0% margin versus 13.4% consensus, record order intake and ~+20% EBITDA growth, delivered in the middle of the guided "transition" year, which strengthens rather than weakens the congruency read
  • The one genuine incongruency is cash, and it is by design: FCF was −EUR 244m in 2025 and −EUR 92m in Q1 2026, but this is the deliberate capex-burn phase, not a deterioration in earnings quality, and the prepayment-funded model means it is not a solvency concern
  • Counter-consideration: the delivery record was built during "exceptionally favourable demand conditions" (NKT's own framing), the team has not been tested in a downturn, and the real congruency test is 2026 (managing the trough) and 2027 (ramping Karlskrona); the Q1 beat is a good early data point but it is one quarter of the test, not the whole test

E1 RA2 CQ4 — Large change-from-current / mispricing? (6 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict, the change-from-current is large, but the mispricing has narrowed sharply, this is the Core Question that has turned most against the case since the research base: there is still a real change-from-current, but the market has closed much of the gap in 19 days
  • The operational change-from-current is genuinely large: the path from EUR 390m EBITDA (2025) toward the >EUR 900m 2030 ambition is a near-tripling, DNB Carnegie's own model bridges 2025 to ~EUR 742m by 2028 and ~EUR 969m by 2030, so the fundamental change-from-current is not in question
  • But the mispricing has been substantially recognised: at the 25-Apr research base (DKK ~867) the Street's average target sat below the price and the [C] research explicitly judged "the thesis is better than the entry point"; on 14-May the stock is at a 52-week high (DKK ~1,050) having compounded ~+21% in 19 days, and the Street-high target (DNB Carnegie's DKK 1,050) has been reached
  • The Q1 beat is the proximate cause and it cuts both ways: the EUR 97m-vs-EUR 84m print is genuinely good news that justifies some of the move, but it also means the "2026 trough creates a mispricing window" thesis that underpinned the change-from-current argument has been at least partly invalidated by the company delivering above the trough
  • On a through-the-ramp view there is still change-from-current left: if NKT delivers ~EUR 740m EBITDA in 2028, the current EV is a far-less-demanding multiple of that figure than of 2025's EUR 390m, so the change-from-current is not gone, it has simply migrated from "cheap on current numbers" to "reasonable on 2027–2028 numbers, expensive on 2026."
  • Counter-consideration / the honest read: at Triaging the conclusion is that the change-from-current is real but reduced, large enough to keep E1 in [B] territory and to justify progressing the case, but no longer the kind of obvious, wide mispricing that would make this an [A] Pillar I; the Early-Stage Assessment work has to size the residual change-from-current precisely against the DKK ~1,050 price

E11 — Value-chain dynamics — second pass — lightA

Element judgement — [A]. NKT's value chain is supportive on essentially every axis: a structurally pulled demand side, a stable and rational competitive structure, low near-term disruption risk, the most supportive political/regulatory backdrop the industry has seen, and a supplier side neutralised by pass-through and hedging. E11 is the strongest individual Element in the Triaging memo. RA1 — Demand side strong / fragile? (4 bullets minimum)
  • Verdict: strong, not fragile: the demand side is the single most supportive part of NKT's value chain: demand is policy-mandated, supply-constrained, and additive across multiple end-uses
  • The demand is structurally pulled, not cyclically pushed: ENTSO-E's 88 GW-by-2030 cross-border target, the EU Grids Package's EUR ~584bn, over 1 TW of renewables in connection queues, this is mandated demand, the opposite of fragile

  • Counter-consideration: the only demand-side fragility is composition risk (German overhead-vs-underground policy affecting the onshore land-cable mix) and the long-dated risk that a European recession coincides with capacity additions, neither of which makes the demand side "fragile" on a Triaging horizon; RA1 is firmly strong

E11 RA1 CQ1 — Is end-demand growing, stable or declining? (4 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: growing, structurally and at pace: the HVDC/transmission market is described across the research base as growing at a ~10–24% CAGR depending on the sub-segment, driven by mandated grid investment
  • The growth is multi-decade, not a single cycle: EU climate-neutrality-by-2050 commitments, national offshore-wind targets stretching to 2045–2050, and the ENTSO-E build-out plan all point to demand growth well beyond any 12–24-month holding period

E11 RA1 CQ2 — Is the demand captive / switching-cost-protected? (4 bullets minimum)

  • Verdict: yes, strongly: HV cable demand is among the most switching-cost-protected in the industrial world: once a project is designed, manufactured and partially installed, switching supplier mid-project is effectively impossible
  • Qualification barriers lock demand to incumbents before a contract is even bid: TSOs require multi-year pre-qualification and type-testing, a process that takes years and that newcomers cannot short-cut
  • Repeat-business dynamics deepen the lock-in: the [C] research documents the TenneT relationship pattern, initial project delivery leads to framework agreements for future projects, so demand compounds toward the proven incumbent
  • Counter-consideration: the switching-cost protection is at the project and relationship level, not absolute, over a long horizon a TSO can qualify an additional supplier (LS Cable's TenneT 525kV approval shows this), so the demand is captive on a 12–24-month view but the incumbency is not permanent; CQ2 is strongly positive with that long-dated caveat
RA2 — Competitive supportiveness / aggression? (4 bullets minimum)
  • Verdict: supportive, not aggressive: the HVDC oligopoly is behaving rationally: Prysmian, Nexans and NKT are raising margins, not cutting price to gain share
  • The structure is a stable three-player oligopoly: Prysmian ~40%, Nexans ~21%, NKT ~14% of HVDC submarine, with extreme barriers to entry (EUR 1bn-plus factories, 3–5-year build times, 5–7-year qualification cycles, scarce vessels)

  • Counter-consideration: the competitive supportiveness is time-limited, Asian capacity additions (LS Cable, Sumitomo) could add ~35% to global HV capacity by ~2030, and the [AS] sell-side work explicitly warns of vulnerability "if strict pricing discipline breaks down"; for a Triaging horizon the structure is supportive, but RA2 carries the clearest "watch the late-decade" flag in E11

E11 RA2 CQ1 — How concentrated is the competitive structure? (4 bullets minimum)
