Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Technoprobe is a premier pick for semiconductor testing exposure due to its dominant 60% market share in vertical MEMS probe cards and an exponential earnings growth trajectory driven by rising AI test complexity.
1.
Dominant technology: Produces customized vertical MEMS probe cards that serve as critical interfaces for wafer testing, allowing chipmakers to find defects early and avoid expensive scrap costs
2.
Compelling thesis: Leverages a duopoly in high-end testing to capture massive secular tailwinds from AI accelerators, custom ASICs, and High Bandwidth Memory, which require unprecedented testing intensity
3.
Execution swing: Operational execution of its massive capacity expansion, which aims to double capacity by early 2027 to meet surging demand from major customers like Nvidia and TSMC
4.
Accelerated growth: Q1 2026 revenue of €187.0m, up 19.0% year-on-year; EBITDA margin rising to 37.0% from 30.5% in the prior year
5.
Margin turnaround: Recovering from margin trough in 2024 when EBITDA margins fell to 25.1% due to dilutive acquisitions; margins reached 32.1% in 2025
6.
Concentration risk: Top five customers represent 72.35% of total trade receivables – severe customer concentration
7.
Compelling verdict: A deeper look is highly warranted as forward estimates remain highly conservative relative to the company's planned capacity expansion and accelerating market traction in custom HBM

Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. Technoprobe occupies a highly profitable niche in the semiconductor value chain by supplying customized consumable interfaces that prevent high-cost package failure.

Proprietary Technology and Value Chain

1.
Consumable demand: Produces proprietary vertical MEMS probe cards customized for each unique chip design – replaced for every new product run
2.
Value chain position: Wafer-level testing stage – bridges ATE with individual dies before packaging to ensure high yields
3.
Massive IP barrier: Over 600 proprietary patents including flagship TPEG vertical MEMS technology; long customer qualification cycles
4.
High-value integration: Fully integrated business model – manufactures high-value MEMS probe heads internally, guaranteeing short lead times and superior electrical performance
5.
Advanced power capability: Probe cards managing over 1,000 amperes of current and 100 watts of power for advanced packaging thermal challenges
6.
Downstream expansion: Entered $1.1bn final-test board segment through 2024 acquisition of DIS
7.
Innovative substrate design: Invested over €150m in proprietary wafer-based multi-layer organic capacity in Taiwan

Market Structure and Customers

8.
Duopolistic logic market: 60% global market share in vertical MEMS probe cards; duopoly alongside FormFactor
9.
Heavy customer concentration: Top 5 customers = 72.35% of total trade receivables at end FY2025
10.
Deep foundry lock-in: TSMC estimated >70% of direct and indirect sales
11.
Sole-source logic supply: Near 100% share at Nvidia; majority supply at AMD
12.
Broad geographic reach: 21 offices globally for rapid local engineering service
13.
Strategic test ecosystem: Teradyne (10.0% stake) and Advantest (2.5% stake) as co-development partners
14.
Other side of the story: Customers actively enforcing dual-sourcing – long-term market share erosion of up to 20% as FormFactor gains qualification at Nvidia

Section 5 – The possible investment case 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Technoprobe grows significantly stronger as compounding test complexity from advanced packaging shifts test budgets permanently toward high-margin wafer probers.

Advanced Computing and High Bandwidth Memory Drivers

1.
Nvidia GPU volume ramp: Nvidia's transition to Blackwell and Rubin architectures expands the AI GPU probe card addressable market at an estimated 130% CAGR over 2023-2026E
2.
Plausibility of GPU driver: High – near 100% share at Nvidia; 15-20x step-up in test intensity vs. mobile SoCs
3.
Failure mode of GPU: Nvidia successfully qualifying FormFactor as major second source in 2026 → share falls toward 70%
4.
Custom HBM insertion: Custom HBM (at HBM4E) requires high-density vertical MEMS probe cards instead of low-cost cantilever technology due to finer pitches
5.
Plausibility of HBM driver: Medium – cost-sensitive memory manufacturers may extend cantilever cards via sacrificial test pads to delay shifting to MEMS
6.
Failure mode of HBM: Prolonged adoption of cantilever technology at HBM4, delaying expected €250m HBM revenue ramp past FY2028

Silicon Photonics and Custom ASIC Drivers

7.
Silicon Photonics transition: Transition to co-packaged optics in AI data centers requires Technoprobe's Eclipse opto-electronic probe card for optical-electrical wafer testing
8.
Plausibility of SiPho driver: Medium-high – first material revenues expected FY2027
9.
Failure mode of SiPho: Slower global adoption of co-packaged optics → limits Technoprobe's estimated €120m SiPho market size
10.
Custom ASIC share gain: Hyperscalers (e.g., TPUs) require high-end vertical MEMS probe cards; strategic partnership with WinWay enables capture at Taiwanese OSATs
11.
Plausibility of ASIC driver: High
12.
Other side: Bull case requires independent drivers to fire concurrently; slower CoPO rollout limits 2028 potential by €120m

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Risks are primarily focused on customer dual-sourcing demands and execution hurdles as Technoprobe doubles its global manufacturing capacity.
1.
Primary threat: customer-driven dual-sourcing initiatives – both Nvidia and TSMC highly motivated to qualify alternative suppliers. Scale of planned capacity doubling introduces severe execution risks. Other risks: active patent litigation, FX volatility, slower recovery in consumer smartphone/PC markets (still ~50% of revenue)
2.
Nvidia dual sourcing: Nvidia actively driving FormFactor through qualification → TPRO share could fall to 70% by 2027
3.
TSMC pricing pressure: TSMC commands massive purchasing power; could demand steep pricing concessions during unfavorable FX
4.
Low free float: <20% free float → extreme share price volatility; downward swings amplified on negative quarterly updates
5.
HBM qualification risk: Delayed volume qualification at major memory manufacturers → FormFactor solidifies first-mover advantage in HBM testing
6.
Patent litigation friction: Active patent infringement battles with Taiwanese competitor MPI Corp (counterclaims filed US federal court May 2026)
7.
False friend signal: Robust near-term revenue growth could mask underlying structural market share loss if probe card TAM is expanding rapidly
8.
Early-warning indicator: Monitor FormFactor quarterly logic revenue + TSMC advanced node wafer utilization rates as leading indicators of share shifts
9.
Other side: Bear concerns of massive share loss overstated – Technoprobe's advanced thermal solutions provide a unique moat that FormFactor cannot easily replicate in high-power AI GPU testing
