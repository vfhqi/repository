Kempower operates as a pure-play DC fast-charging hardware manufacturer transitioning from Nordic-centric sales toward balanced global exposure across Europe and North America. The company's modular distributed architecture separates power cabinets from satellite dispensers, achieving 50% smaller physical footprints compared to conventional all-in-one chargers and enabling dynamic load-balancing across 12 satellites without grid upgrades. The satellite dispensers require up to 50% less real estate at charging sites, and a single 1,200 kW power unit can dynamically distribute electrical power across up to 12 charging satellites. This technological foundation creates structural switching costs through physical site integration and ChargEye SaaS backend lockin , validated by 99%+ operational uptime across 13 million charging sessions. However, the business model remains 95% hardware point-in-time revenue , with recurring software and lifecycle services representing only 5% of 2025 sales despite management's strategic pivot toward materially higher recurring streams by 2030.
