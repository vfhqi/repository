B.1 Business Description 12 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Cellnex is Europe's largest independent wireless tower operator, leasing passive telecommunications infrastructure (towers, rooftops, distributed antenna systems) to mobile network operators under long-duration Master Service Agreements
2.
Contract structure. The business rests on a EUR 100bn revenue backlog with average contract lives exceeding 15 years, of which approximately 65% of revenues are CPI-linked and the remaining 35% use fixed escalators, a genuinely long-duration base
3.
Scale. FY25 core revenues reached EUR 3,995m, up 5.8% on an organic pro forma basis; Q1 2026 revenue excluding pass-through was EUR 984m, up 4.7% organically
4.
Margin profile. Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA grew 6.4% organically to EUR 832m (an 84.60% margin, top 5% of the global tower universe); EBITDAaL rose 7.2% to EUR 595m, a 170 basis point margin expansion to 60.5%
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt to EBITDA fell to 6.2x in Q1 2026 from 6.3x in Q4 2025, down from a 7.7x base and a 8.4x crisis peak in December 2022
5.1
Detail: 77% of gross debt is fixed-rate and total liquidity is EUR 6bn (EUR 3bn cash, EUR 3bn undrawn facilities)
6.
Cash generation. Underlying free cash flow turned positive at EUR 118m in Q1 2026, from negative EUR 66m in Q1 2025, described in the source report as a "critical milestone" and a "pivotal turning point"
7.
Capital returns. Cellnex initiated an EUR 800m share buyback in 2025, returned EUR 1bn total to shareholders in FY25
7.1
Detail: And has upgraded the FY26 capital return to EUR 1bn including EUR 500m of buybacks and a EUR 500m dividend floor growing 7.5% annually through 2030
8.
Portfolio simplification. The group completed EUR 1.8bn of divestments (Austria EUR 803m, Ireland EUR 971m) and signed a put option to sell its French data-centre business for EUR 391m
8.1
Detail: Alongside capex tapering from EUR 1.9bn to an EUR 700m medium-term run-rate
9.
Geography. Pan-European, not narrow: France generates 21.2% of FY25 revenue, Italy 20.0%, Spain 14.5%, with further material exposure in the UK and Poland and residual Austria/Ireland exposure being unwound
10.
Investment style read. Cellnex reads as a leadership-driven, self-help deleveraging compounder: a capital-intensive infrastructure asset base now pivoting from expansion capex to organic cash generation and balance-sheet repair
11.
Leadership. Chief executive Marco Patuano leads a disciplined, streamlined executive team, the executive committee reduced from 14 to 10 members to accelerate decision-making
12.
Ownership. Two active anchor shareholders exercise governance oversight: TCI holds 10.15% (up from 9.39% in 2024) and Edizione controls 10.25% (up from 9.90%), both with annual director re-election rights
