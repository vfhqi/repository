  • Bear — structural competitive pressure: German retail energy is one of the most competitive in Europe; the Bundeskartellamt is actively monitoring incumbent pricing; digital challenger brands (E.ON Digital, Tibber, Awattar) are taking share in the under-40 demographic [#A]

What if the return of high wholesale prices creates a second switching cycle that further erodes Mainova's retail base faster than the 3–5Y structural decline assumption? [#J]

  • D1 → D2 (sequential dependency): The regulatory WACC reset makes the capex programme more value-accretive (higher allowed return on new RAB); if the WACC reset is weaker than expected, the capex ROIC spread narrows — D1 failure degrades D2 [#A]

  • Correlation: positive; if D1 disappoints, D2 also disappoints; they are NOT independent positive bets

  • D2 → D7 (leverage feedback): Higher capex (D2) increases net debt (D7); if the capex programme is larger or more expensive than planned, leverage rises faster, increasing financing costs and further pressuring EBITDA [#A]
