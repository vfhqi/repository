§6 — Margin + Profitability Outlook

  • Verdict: Margin expansion assumed in FY26-27 is primarily interest-cost-driven, not operational; underlying EBITDA margin trajectory is flat-to-declining in the ServiceCo business, a concern underweighted by buy-rated analysts [#J]

  • Consensus EBITDA margin: ServiceCo FY25 approximately 32-36% (organic, excl. exceptionals); this compares to ~38-40% pre-NetCo on the integrated entity; the restructuring has revealed a structurally lower-margin ServiceCo rump [#A]

  • Consensus EBIT margin: Approximately 10-14% for FY25; recovering to ~15-18% by FY27 in bull case; primarily function of reducing D&A post-NetCo deconsolidation (infrastructure assets removed from balance sheet) [#A]

  • Consensus net margin: Near-zero or marginally positive in FY25; improving to ~3-5% by FY27 in consensus; entirely dependent on interest cost reduction from NetCo proceeds deployment [#A]

  • Margin trend assumed: Buy-rated analysts assume moderate EBITDA margin expansion of ~100-200bps per year FY25-27; driven by cost efficiency programme (Project PACE or equivalent), headcount reduction, and operating leverage on a declining but stabilising revenue base [#A]

  • Productivity assumption: TIM has a documented efficiency programme targeting €0.4-0.6bn in annual cost savings over 3Y; consensus broadly credits ~60-70% of these savings, with sceptical analysts crediting only 40-50% [#A]

  • Operating leverage: ❌ Limited operating leverage on declining revenue; the classic telco operating leverage mechanism (fixed costs as % of revenue falling as revenue grows) runs in reverse here; cost savings must work against a revenue headwind [#A]

  • Fixed cost: Fixed cost structure remains heavy; ~55-60% of OpEx is people/network-related and sticky; restructuring costs (early retirement, etc.) add exceptional items that distort reported margins [#A]

  • One-off vs structural: The efficiency programme is partially one-off (headcount reduction) and partially structural (network simplification post-NetCo); analysts disagree on the structural component — bulls credit ~€300M as structural, bears credit ~€150M [#A]

  • Margin downgrade L12M: EBITDA margin assumptions have been cut ~100-200bps over L12M by the consensus; driven by lower revenue not higher costs; absolute cost assumptions have been broadly maintained [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The EBITDA margin expansion thesis for FY26-27 assumes the efficiency programme lands fully and revenue decline moderates simultaneously; if revenue decline accelerates (Berenberg's bear case) and cost savings underdeliver (historical TIT track record), EBITDA margin could deteriorate 200-300bps below consensus, which would be devastating to FCF and the debt service narrative [#J]

§7 — Narrative + Market Story

  • Verdict: The market story is almost entirely event-driven; absent the NetCo transaction, there is no compelling standalone organic investment case and consensus knows it [#J]

  • Bull case: The dominant bull narrative: NetCo separation unlocks hidden infrastructure value (FiberCop is a regulated monopoly-like asset worth €14-18bn enterprise value); proceeds repay €14-15bn of TIT net debt, transforming the balance sheet; a leaner ServiceCo with €1.5-2.0bn FCF capacity justifies €0.30-0.40/share on a sum-of-parts basis; Brazil provides a free growth option [#A]

  • Bear case: The dominant bear narrative: ServiceCo is a structurally declining fixed-line/mobile operator in a commoditising market; even with debt reduction, interest coverage remains thin; the Italian regulatory environment is not structurally advantageous; TIM Brasil is insufficiently large to offset domestic decline; the stock deserves a liquidation-value discount, not a recovery premium [#A]

  • Bullish theme 1: Balance sheet transformation — virtually every buy-rated analyst leads with this; net debt/EBITDA dropping from ~4x to ~2.5-3x is the headline catalyst; [AS-Citi], [AS-Goldman Sachs], [AS-Barclays] all lead with this theme [#A]

  • Bullish theme 2: Free cash flow inflection — analysts model FCF turning positive (or significantly improving) in FY26-27 post-NetCo, driven by lower interest, lower CapEx (no longer building fibre), and efficiency savings; [AS-Mediobanca], [AS-Deutsche Bank], [AS-BNP Paribas] anchor their bull cases here [#A]

  • Bullish theme 3: TIM Brasil optionality — less prominent than balance sheet, but several analysts (Goldman, Barclays) explicitly flag Brazil as undervalued in the current market cap; sum-of-parts assigns €0.08-0.12/share to Brazil [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 1: ServiceCo is a melting ice cube — Berenberg and HSBC use near-identical language; domestic fixed-line revenue in structural secular decline; no competitive differentiation; enterprise market increasingly won by pan-European operators [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 2: Residual leverage still dangerous — JP Morgan flags that even post-NetCo, TIT ServiceCo will carry €8-10bn net debt on €4bn EBITDA (~2.5x); if EBITDA disappoints by 10%, leverage re-approaches stress territory; [AS-Morgan Stanley] adds regulatory risk to FiberCop wholesale pricing as an underappreciated EBITDA lever [#A]

  • Narrative shift L12M: Pre-NetCo close (H1-24): dominant narrative was "will the deal complete?" — existential uncertainty; Post-close (Q4-24/Q1-25): dominant narrative shifts to "how fast does ServiceCo recover?" — a fundamental rather than event-driven question; this narrative transition has not fully resolved in analysts' PTs [#J]

  • Near-term focus: Most analysts now focused on H1-25 domestic subscriber data as the first post-NetCo acid test; if Q2-H1-25 shows fixed broadband churn stabilising, expect PT upgrades; if it accelerates, expect downgrades [#A]

  • Original insight: [AS-Morgan Stanley] provides the most original analytical content — focusing on FiberCop wholesale regulation as the key valuation swing factor; most other analysts treat FiberCop as a fixed value; Morgan Stanley flags regulatory risk to the wholesale access price as the key unknown [#A]

  • Management echo: [AS-Mediobanca] and [AS-Equita] show the most management-echo pattern; their FY26-27 forecasts are essentially management guidance re-expressed with minimal independent adjustment [#J]

  • Narrative credibility: The FCF inflection narrative is credible IF domestic revenue stabilises by FY26; it becomes non-credible if TIT's domestic revenue decline accelerates to -6% or beyond, as Berenberg fears [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bull narrative's fatal assumption is that ServiceCo revenue trough is near; this is asserted, not evidenced; the structural forces (copper-to-fibre ARPU compression, Iliad mobile price competition, enterprise contract losses) have not demonstrably reversed; analysts are essentially making a timing bet, not a structural thesis, and timing bets on telco recovery have a poor historical track record in European markets (see: Telecom Portugal 2012-2016, Telefónica 2016-2019) [#J]
