Plausible problems to monitor 4 findings

1.
Take-rate compression trajectory: The group net take rate declined from 17.0 basis points to 16.3 basis points in Q1 2026 , underperforming the industry expectation of stable unit economics as volumes scale. While management attributes this to favorable mix effects from larger enterprise merchants, the consistent downward trajectory suggests structural revenue volatility disconnects from volume growth that exceed peer norms. Peer anchor: Stripe maintains more stable blended rates through its opaque pricing model, while Braintree operates on thin but consistent 25 basis point take rates. [Cross-ref: Section 1, Section 2]
2.
North American competitive positioning: Growth was temporarily moderated by U.S. tariffs and slower spending from APAC-headquartered online retailers , exposing Adyen's weaker commercial penetration in its primary investment geography. Intensifying North American pricing pressure from Stripe could accelerate take-rate erosion , as Adyen's US card-not-present market share remains in high single-digits versus Stripe's share in the 30s%. Peer anchor: Stripe commands 3-4x higher US CNP market share and maintains deeper developer ecosystem lock-in. [Cross-ref: Section 1, Section 3, Section 7]
3.
Shopify channel dependency and routing risk: Shopify's upmarket push creates channel friction as the platform acts as a gatekeeper favoring Shop Pay over third-party processors, while its core Shop Pay checkout is powered exclusively by Stripe . Shop Pay's rapid expansion in Europe risks displacing Adyen's direct enterprise e-commerce volumes at checkout , with materially lower take rates for Adyen compared to direct merchant contracts when processing through Shopify Payments. Peer anchor: Stripe maintains exclusive Shop Pay routing agreements, capturing premium branded checkout economics that Adyen cannot access. [Cross-ref: Section 6, Section 7, Section 8]
4.
Acquisition integration execution risk: Combined acquisitions add 1 percentage point to net growth but temporarily dilute FY26 margins by 1 percentage point , representing Adyen's first major M&A execution test after a decade of pure organic growth. Integrating complex acquisitions may dilute Adyen's unified code stack, potentially compromising the core single-stack architectural moat that drives superior authorization rates. Peer anchor: Stripe successfully integrated multiple billing acquisitions (including Metronome capabilities) without materially disrupting its core developer experience. [Cross-ref: Section 1, Section 2] and SMB commercialization lag: Integrating Adyen's unified API requires a demanding one-to-three month technical implementation process for large enterprise developers, while developer complexity hurdles limit Adyen's appeal for nascent, fast-growing startups compared to Stripe's Connect . This structural friction leaves Adyen exposed to systematic adverse selection , capturing enterprise merchants only after they've scaled on competitor platforms and accumulated switching costs. Peer anchor: Stripe's self-serve onboarding enables sub-24-hour integration for startups, capturing high-growth cohorts at formation rather than at maturity. [Cross-ref: Section 5, Section 9]

Extreme customer retention performance: Adyen maintains a volume churn rate of less than 1% annually, which positions the company in the top 5% globally for enterprise software retention metrics and represents a structural outlier relative to legacy payment processors experiencing mid-single-digit annual merchant attrition . This exceptional retention is mechanically driven by the massive technical switching costs embedded in Adyen's unified data architecture and direct global banking license infrastructure.
