§2 — Key Driver #1: Lesjöfors Volume Recovery

Lesjöfors is the highest-leverage driver in the case — as Beijer Alma's largest segment by both revenue and EBIT, spring and wire products volumes directly dictate group EPS trajectory over 24M.

  • Driver definition: Lesjöfors manufactures springs, wire and strip products for automotive, industrial and consumer end-markets; volume recovery means organic revenue growth resuming after L12M softness driven by European auto production weakness and industrial destocking [#J]
  • Mechanism to EPS: Lesjöfors carries a semi-fixed cost base; incremental volume drops through at ~40-50% contribution margin at the EBIT line (estimated from prior segment disclosures); 10% organic volume recovery implies ~15-20% EBIT uplift at segment level [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Higher Lesjöfors EBIT directly drives group EPS; at ~16-18x P/E (current implied), each 10% EPS recovery is worth approximately 10-18% share price; volume recovery also triggers multiple re-rating toward mid-cycle levels [#A]

  • Company guide: Management has guided for volume recovery in H2 2025 / 2026 based on normalisation of customer inventory destocking; explicit quantitative guidance not provided on organic volume growth rate [#A]

  • Management commentary: Management characterises softness as cyclical, not structural — automotive OEM and Tier 1 customers reduced orders through inventory normalisation; management expects order intake to improve as destocking completes [#A]
  • Track record: Lesjöfors has delivered consistent organic growth above European industrial averages through prior cycles (2016-2019 pre-COVID), suggesting genuine competitive positioning; COVID recovery 2020-2022 demonstrated operating leverage exists when volumes recover [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Management guidance on recovery timing reads as optimistic given PMI data through Q1 2026 — H2 2025 recovery call has been progressively pushed; risk that timing slips further [#J]

  • SS consensus: Consensus broadly expects Lesjöfors organic growth to resume +3 to +6% in FY2026 vs flat/negative in FY2024-25; Carnegie and Handelsbanken have flagged recovery timing risk [#A]

  • Bullish analyst: Berenberg flagged Lesjöfors' niche positioning in automotive springs as a structural incumbent advantage that limits share loss even in volume troughs [#A]
  • Sceptical analyst: ABG Sundal Collier noted that European auto production schedules remain under pressure from EV transition disruption, which could delay Lesjöfors automotive recovery beyond consensus timing [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Full delivery of +5% organic Lesjöfors volume in FY2026 estimated to add approximately SEK 1.5-2.5 EPS at group level (c.10-15% on prior normalised EPS); peer-based anchor from comparable spring/industrial component manufacturers (Indutrade, Addtech proxies) suggests 5% organic volume recovery is a credible mid-cycle expectation [#A]

  • Recent delivery: Lesjöfors reported negative organic growth in H2 2024 and Q1 2025; revenue fell approximately 5-8% organically L12M driven by automotive and industrial volume weakness; no meaningful recovery signal visible in most recent reporting [#J]

  • L3Y baseline: 2021-2022 saw Lesjöfors deliver strong organic growth (+8-12% at peak); 2023 marked the inflection to softness; L3Y average organic is approximately flat, masking the trough dynamic [#A]
  • Trend L12M: Decelerating through H2 2024 and H1 2025 — no early-turn evidence visible in public order data or management commentary as of most recent results [#J]

  • Macro tailwind: European industrial PMI has oscillated around 45-48 through Q1 2026 — sub-50, not conducive to volume destocking completion; auto production schedules from VW, Stellantis, Renault remain under pressure from EV transition and affordability headwinds [#J]

  • Macro headwind: Auto OEM capex consolidation and model-year delays reduce Tier 1 spring order visibility; German industrial weakness is structurally more prolonged than consensus embedded [#J]
  • Competitive dynamics: Lesjöfors operates in a fragmented market with limited substitution risk on bespoke springs; however, Asian competitors are pricing aggressively on standard products; margin mix risk if standard products volume recovers before high-value bespoke [#A]

  • Visibility: Low-to-medium — dependent on external PMI, OEM production schedules, and customer inventory positions that are difficult to forecast [#J]

  • Plausibility assessment: If European PMI recovers to 50+ by Q3 2026 and automotive production schedules normalise, Lesjöfors can deliver +4 to +6% organic in FY2026; this is the central scenario embedded in consensus but requires macro conditions not yet visible [#A]

  • Mechanism to EPS: Direct transmission via Lesjöfors volume (Driver #1) and indirect via Group margin (Driver #3); an industrial capex cycle re-acceleration amplifies every other driver's delivery probability [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Industrial cycle recovery triggers sector re-rating for European small-cap industrials broadly; Beijer Alma would participate in any sector multiple expansion [#A]

  • Company guide: Management cannot guide on macro; they note end-market conditions as "stabilising" but not yet recovering [#A]

  • Management commentary: References to "awaiting customer inventory normalisation" are the proxy for macro dependence; management has repeatedly positioned recovery as imminent but timing has slipped [#A]

  • Track record: Beijer Alma participates in European industrial cycles; prior recoveries (2016-2017, 2020-2021) showed revenue and earnings inflection within 2-4 quarters of PMI recovery above 50 [#A]
