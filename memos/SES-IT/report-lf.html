EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Alessandro Fabbroni represents a highly committed internal specialist-turned-operator with extreme leadership continuity as Group CEO and partner since 2008 , demonstrating exceptional long-term revenue scaling from €812.0m in FY12 to €3,356.8m in FY25 through programmatic bolt-on acquisitions. However, expert network insights expose deep post-M&A salary friction and integration bottlenecks across Sesa's decentralized consortium of over 100 semi-independent subsidiaries , challenging the credibility of the group's transition to organic-led growth under the 2026-2027 Industrial Plan.

CEO Track Record and Execution Capability

Sesa scaled EBITDA from €41.3m in FY12 to €240.7m in FY25 with a robust 14.0% CAGR under Fabbroni's leadership . Management completed 80 bolt-on acquisitions between FY15 and FY25, adding €830m of consolidated revenues and 3,120 skilled professionals . Sesa recorded a 987% total shareholder return in the

ten years since its 2013 IPO , substantially outperforming traditional IT distributors. Fabbroni consistently under-promises and over-delivers, with FY26 results conﬁrmed at the upper end of original organic ranges . The 2026-2027 Industrial Plan marks a major strategic transition from M&A-driven expansion to organic growth , with annual M&A investments declining to around €30m . 3 4

Governance Structure and Voting Control Concentration

ITH SpA holds 53.53% of Sesa's share capital but exercises 69.589% of voting rights through increased voting mechanisms . Founder Paolo Castellacci has served as Chairman since inception, with key management exercising control via ITH SpA's 56.84% stake . Sesa maintains a balanced 50.00% independence ratio with ﬁve independent directors, comparing favorably to STAR segment requirements . The group operates a robust Internal Compliance Model pursuant to Italian Legislative Decree 231/2001, with KPMG oversight for 2023 to 2031 . Concentrated family-and-partner voting control of 69.589% may allow principal shareholders to bypass minority dissent on critical asset allocation choices . 8 9
