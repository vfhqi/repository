§8 — CEO Quality Verdict + Animal-CEO Archetype + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict (cross-cutting bear synthesis): Leriche is a credible operator-track CEO; the animal-CEO archetype is a partial match — force and detail-orientation are visible, agency and dynamism are muted; single largest risk is the bounded-strategic-ambition / lifer-conservatism tension

Animal-CEO archetype assessment:

  • Force marker: Partially present — Leriche's operational push on yield management, pricing discipline, and ElecLink ramp is consistent with genuine commercial force within his defined domain; however, the force is channelled narrowly (existing asset optimisation, not business-building)
  • Evidence: explicit yield management language in earnings calls; freight slot pricing assertiveness vs Ro-Ro competition; ElecLink commercial launch on schedule

  • Gap: no evidence of force deployed outside the existing asset boundary

  • Agency marker: Present but moderate — internal promote based on delivery record; willing to make public statements that commit the company on operational delivery (e.g., border-check readiness); owns the EES headwind narrative without deflection

  • Evidence: the "our operational readiness is complete" border-check statement is a clean agency marker — he stated what is within his control and refused to equivocate on external variables

  • Gap: no examples of contrarian or bold decisions against consensus or board/shareholder preference

  • Dynamism marker: Weakest of the five — Leriche has not demonstrated the ability to identify and act on structural inflections; the strategic agenda has been entirely within the inherited asset base; no evidence of imaginative optionality framing beyond the concession extension narrative

  • The one exception: the ElecLink narrative has been developed into a recurring "energy transition / interconnector optionality" frame — this is the closest to a dynamism signal, though the asset was predecessor-built

  • Capital-allocation discipline: Adequate — balance-sheet repair is progressing; no value-destructive M&A; dividend management is prudent; no evidence of capital misallocation

  • Gap: capital-allocation discipline under no-M&A conditions is not a strong positive signal — the harder test (what to do when there is an acquisition opportunity or a capital-heavy adjacent investment) has not been faced
