§3 — False Friends

Definition: Evidence that investors interpret as bull confirmation but which bears should read as confirming failure. False friends are structurally more dangerous than obvious bear cases because they suppress the urgency to act on emerging deterioration. #J

False Friend 1: "FY25 EBIT margin of 16.3% proves the recovery story is working"

What bulls see: Margin expanded from 11.8% (FY24) to 16.3% (FY25). Target of 14%+ achieved and exceeded. Thesis on track.

What bears see: 16.3% was delivered via SEK 279M cost-out run-rate savings — not via revenue growth, not via mix shift, not via ASP improvement. R&D amortisation is now mechanically outpacing R&D capitalisation, creating a growing headwind that is inescapable. A cost-out driven margin is a one-time improvement that exhausts itself within 1-2 years unless the revenue engine accelerates. Consensus has straight-lined this as the floor and assumed further expansion — any revenue shortfall will produce non-linear EBIT disappointment. #J [C+AS]

False Friend 2: "The backlog purge is a positive — management is being disciplined and conservative"

What bulls see: New management cleaning up Salford-era backlog quality. Proactive conservatism = credibility restoration. SEK 34bn remaining backlog is higher quality and more predictable.

What bears see: Two purges in 12 months totalling SEK 7.1bn — 17% of the pre-purge backlog. The first purge was framed as "proactive." The second purge, announced months later, revealed the first purge was insufficient. Backlog conversion rate has deteriorated from 66% (FY16) to the low 40% range. This is demand destruction dressed up as inventory management. The question for the remaining SEK 34bn is not whether it is "higher quality" but whether the same forces that cancelled 17% will cancel another tranche. #J [C+AS]

False Friend 3: "ViewRay's failure validates Elekta's MRI-linac dominance"

What bulls see: Only competitor in MRI-linac bankrupt. Elekta's Unity now has ~50%+ global share in an 18% CAGR market with no competitive threat. Moat has never been wider.

What bears see: ViewRay's failure was financial, not clinical. The MRIdian system worked. ViewRay failed because: (a) $6M per system with $25M installation complexity was too capital-intensive, (b) reimbursement models (CMS) didn't move fast enough to justify the premium, (c) long installation cycles created cash burn. Elekta faces structurally analogous risks: premium pricing ($7-10M), complex installations, reimbursement dependency. The "no competitor" framing obscures a declining market size risk: if hospitals are choosing to defer or not adopt MRI-linac at all, 100% share of a smaller market is not a bull case. #J [C]

False Friend 4: "Service revenue resilience confirms the installed base is stable"

What bulls see: Service revenue remains stable, providing a recurring "annuity" that validates the installed base moat.

What bears see: Service contracts are tied to installed hardware. If the hardware installed base is shrinking or if competitive hardware is replacing Elekta units, the service revenue will eventually follow the hardware decay with a lag — typically 3-5 years. Service revenue "resilience" today is therefore a lagging indicator of hardware market share losses that have already occurred. #J [AS]

Why false friends are more dangerous than obvious bear cases:

Obvious bear cases (China competition, CEO departure) are priced by markets. False friends are not priced because they are actively interpreted as positives. They create a situation where incremental negative information is repeatedly dismissed ("that's already known") rather than acting as the escalating signal it represents. The four false friends above are operating simultaneously — margin story, backlog story, competitive moat story, and service revenue story are all being read backwards by market participants. False friends delay action until the evidence is overwhelming, at which point the stock has already re-rated significantly. #J [C+AS]
