EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Michelin operates a structurally advantaged premium tire franchise that commands superior pricing power, technical moats, and replacement market loyalty despite near-term cyclical pressures in commercial transportation and agricultural segments. The group's partnership governance structure, massive R&D scale, and disciplined capital allocation position it to defend high-teens operating margins while expanding into adjacent high-margin Polymer Composite Solutions targeting a EUR 70 billion addressable market . Financial performance remains resilient, with segment operating income of EUR 2.9 billion in 2025 and free cash flow of EUR 2.1 billion supporting an accelerated EUR 2 billion share buyback program through 2028 . However, the business faces structural headwinds from Indian specialty tire exporters capturing 10 percentage points of offthe-road market share , aggressive Chinese electric vehicle tire penetration , and persistent trade-down pressure in Tier 2 and Tier 3 replacement segments as budget-conscious consumers shift toward imports priced 10% to 15% below Western peers .

Competitive Threats and Market Share Erosion Risks

Global Tier 1 players have defended stable 49% to 53% value share since 2010 through disciplined volume sacrifice, but Tier 3 budget imports have captured 20% to 25% global share , pressuring Michelin's lower-tier brands. Indian off-the-road specialists pose an acute threat, with Balkrishna Industries expanding to 6% global OTR share and pricing agricultural products 10% to 15% below European Tier 1 equivalents while deploying a massive 3,200-SKU product catalog backed by 10.5% capital intensity and 3.4% R&D spending . CEAT's acquisition of Michelin's Camso construction brand for USD 225 million grants immediate access to 40 global OEM accounts and full competitive rights in USD 850 million remaining Camso segments after 2028 , directly challenging Michelin's specialty positioning. Chinese manufacturers rapidly close quality gaps, with modern tires delivering 60% to 70% of Tier 1 performance at half the price , leveraging state-of-the-art Southeast Asian and Eastern European production bases to bypass Western tariffs. However, premium electric vehicle launches above USD 30,000 continue to rely exclusively on Tier 1 tires, while European anti-dumping duties of 30% to 50% drove a 66% contraction in Chinese passenger tire imports in early 2026, temporarily insulating Michelin's replacement pricing. Bridgestone's ENLITEN lightweight technology represents a credible peer threat, optimizing rubber thickness and modular architecture to customize electric vehicle specifications, though Michelin's 89% brand awareness and decades-long innovation leadership provide defensive moats against rapid market share loss.
