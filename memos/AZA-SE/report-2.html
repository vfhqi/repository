§3 — CHANGE IN STRATEGY/STRUCTURE

J-front verdict: The 2030 strategy represents the most significant strategic shift in Avanza's 25-year history — from self-directed Swedish retail brokerage to a multi-vertical, multi-market savings platform. The Denmark expansion is the single most structurally material new development not present in prior research. It opens a SEK 9tn TAM and validates geographic replicability of the model — but at meaningful execution cost and timeline risk. [#J]

  • Private banking buildout [#A]: Avanza launched dedicated private banking (Avanza Private Banking) targeting customers with >SEK 1m in savings. [C]: Swedish private banking market estimated at SEK 600bn+ AuC concentrated at SEB Private Banking, Handelsbanken, Nordea Wealth — Avanza's current share is sub-2%. Digital-first advisory at lower cost than incumbents is the competitive wedge.

  • Occupational pensions (tjänstepension) [#A]: [AS·company]: Savings capital grown to SEK 86bn = 7.5% market share in 2025. Swedish occupational pension market is ~SEK 4,500bn — approximately 4× Avanza's current total AuC. [C]: PPM pension reform (simplification of transfer market) allows Avanza to leverage its platform without additional fees — a regulatory tailwind accelerating this segment. Alecta's 2023 governance scandal (US bank equity losses) created a trust vacuum that platforms including Avanza can fill.

  • Denmark expansion — new strategic vector [#AS·company]: Avanza announced first international market entry: Denmark, planned for H2 2027.

  • Danish TAM: SEK 9tn — increasing Avanza's total addressable market by ~70% from SEK 6.2tn Swedish market.
  • Danish retail penetration: 13% direct shares ownership vs 64% for funds — significantly lower than Sweden, implying large structural growth opportunity with a similar cultural/savings-behaviour profile.
  • [AS·Broker — DNB Carnegie]: Danish market growing at ~8% p.a. Investment: SEK 120–150m initial cost; cloud-native platform purpose-built for Denmark, separate from Swedish core to avoid disruption. Cloud migration completion targeted by 2030.

  • [C]: This is a qualitative step-change: Avanza transitions from a pure-play domestic platform to a multi-market business. The Nordnet multi-market model provides a proof of concept, but also defines the execution bar — Nordnet took 10+ years to build Nordic footprint; Avanza is attempting a compressed entry.

  • M&A posture — organic only [#I]: No change for Sweden. Denmark is an organic greenfield, consistent with the founder-influenced no-acquisition culture.

  • Avanza Global (in-house fund) [#J]: [C]: AuM exceeded SEK 100bn in 2024 — one of Sweden's largest individual funds. Fund commission income (0.08% TER, 100% retained) flows directly to P&L without brokerage-cycle volatility. This is genuine revenue diversification: growing at ~15%+ p.a. on AuM expansion.

  • Cloud migration [#AS·company]: Cloud migration to be completed by 2030. Danish platform will be built cloud-native from day one. [C]: Cost-to-savings-capital ratio improvement (14.5bps → 14.1bps 2024→2025) shows early operating leverage — cloud completion should accelerate this trajectory.

  • §3 Sceptical: ❌ The 2030 strategy requires Avanza to win simultaneously in private banking (high-touch, relationship-driven), occupational pensions (institutional partnerships, regulatory complexity), and international expansion (culture, regulation, brand-building). Avanza's structural moat in low-cost self-directed retail does not automatically transfer to any of these three new arenas. Sequencing risk is real. [#J]

The medium-term disruption risk is the fund kickback ban (MiFID) — this is regulatory disruption, not technology disruption, but could materially compress commission revenue over a 3–5 year horizon. [C]: SAVR's "kickback-free" model already creating competitive pressure — this is the proof of concept that the ban, when it comes, will not destroy fund distribution but will compress Avanza's economics on third-party fund sales.   • Robo-advisory [#A]: Avanza has "Avanza Auto" robo-advisory product. [C]: AuM growing but small % of total AuC. Nordnet's "Automatisk" is the main comparable.   • Open finance / PSD2+ [#A]: [C]: Avanza has positioned itself as aggregation hub rather than switching target — integrating third-party account data to strengthen platform completeness. This limits switching risk from open banking to Avanza specifically.

  • Denmark platform — cloud-native [#AS·company]: New Danish platform built cloud-native from day one. [C]: This creates an interesting structural dynamic: Avanza may end up with a more modern architecture in Denmark than in Sweden (where the core is 25 years of incremental development). If Danish cloud-native performs well, it validates the architectural approach for eventual Swedish platform migration.

  • §8 Sceptical: The fund kickback ban is the single most underappreciated disruption risk in Avanza's revenue mix. Fund commissions have grown to ~19% of income from 14% in 2020 — exactly the line MiFID reform would most directly impair. Avanza Global's 100% TER retention provides partial offset, but third-party fund distribution economics would be structurally altered. [#J]
