§4 Synthesis

4.1 D2C brokerage revenue sensitivity

Nordnet's revenue has three main drivers operating simultaneously. First, net commission income (trading volumes) — this rose 11% year-on-year in Q1 to SEK 909 million, with 18.8 million trades (up 15%), the highest since Q1 2021; this driver fires when markets are volatile and retail participation is high. Second, net interest income on client cash — with Riksbank holding at 1.75% since early 2025 and minutes from May 2026 confirming the rate path is flat through year-end, NII upside from rate cuts is closed off; this was a meaningful tailwind in 2022-24 and its absence caps earnings upgrades. Third, net savings inflows and assets under custody — SEK 28.8 billion net savings in Q1 (one of the highest quarters on record) feeds recurring fee income and a larger asset base, but is a slow-burn driver not a quarterly catalyst.

The combination of record trades and record savings inflows in Q1 set a very high bar for Q2, particularly as global equity volatility (US tariff escalation, Iran risk) makes retail trading behaviour unpredictable from here.

4.2 Candidate ranking

The dominant candidate explaining the peak-to-trough move is Q1 consensus miss on operating profit plus post-print de-rating: the stock had rallied ~5-6% in late April into and after the Q1 report, pricing in a beat that did not materialise on the bottom line (SEK 1,042m actual vs SEK 1,070m consensus). The subsequent unwind is orderly — no sharp single-day break — consistent with institutional re-rating rather than a news shock. Secondary to this, Riksbank's confirmed rate hold removes the last remaining NII upgrade optionality that some bulls had priced. Tertiary is the global risk-off backdrop: Nordic retail brokerages are high-beta cyclicals, and with European equities volatile through April-May 2026, generalist funds have trimmed exposure to activity-sensitive financials. Peer read-across (Avanza's stock fell ~5% on its own record Q1 print) confirms this is a sector-wide pattern, not Nordnet-specific deterioration.
