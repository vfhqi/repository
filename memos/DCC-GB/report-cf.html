EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 7 findings

DCC plc is undergoing a rapid transformation from a diversified industrial conglomerate into a pure-play multienergy provider, having completed two major divestments (Healthcare and Info Tech) within twelve months and targeting completion of its remaining Technology sale (Nexora) by calendar year-end 2026. Management articulates an ambitious vision to double energy operating profits from £407 million in FY2022 to £830 million by FY2030, positioning the group as a product-agnostic multi-energy solutions provider across liquid gas distribution, energy services (solar PV and heat pumps), and mobility fuel retailing. The strategic pivot eliminates structural complexity and concentrates capital in businesses historically generating pre-tax returns on capital employed above 17%, while simultaneously returning £700 million to shareholders via buybacks and tender offers. The financial profile demonstrates resilience despite near-term headwinds, with continuing revenue declining 2.9% to £15.44 billion in FY2026 but free cash flow conversion surging to 108% from 84% in the prior year, driven by portfolio simplification and the removal of working capital volatility from divested technology assets. Operating profit per liter in the Solutions segment expanded 15.1% to 3.8 pence, while continuing adjusted operating profit grew 3.6% to £634.0 million, demonstrating unit-level margin power despite volume pressures from unseasonably mild European winter weather. Leadership transitions have been orderly and internally sourced, with energy division veterans assuming the CFO and newly created COO roles to drive operational execution during the pureplay transition.

The materiality of strategic change is exceptionally high, representing a complete corporate reorientation. DCC has divested Healthcare (£1.05 billion enterprise value, 12.2 times EBITA multiple) and Info Tech (£100 million enterprise value), with the final Technology component (Nexora) actively marketed for sale by calendar 2026 yearend. Post-simplification, operations are streamlined into two energy divisions: Solutions (Energy Products at 68% of group profits plus Energy Services) and Mobility (approximately 1,200 retail forecourts). This represents a fundamental shift from a diversified portfolio spanning healthcare distribution, consumer technology, and energy into a focused downstream energy platform. - Earnings Calls Management's stated ambition to double energy EBITA from £407 million to £830 million by FY2030, combined with completion of £700 million in shareholder returns during FY2026, signals aggressive confidence in the pure- play strategy.
