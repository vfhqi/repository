Regulatory Paradigm Shift: DORA III Framework and Tariff Dynamics
The regulatory landscape for Aena SME SA is undergoing a profound structural shift as the company transitions into the Third Airport Regulation Document (DORA III) for the 2027–2031 period. This transition officially marks
the end of a decade-long legal restriction that effectively capped aeronautical tariff increases at 0% between 2015 and 2025. The expiration of this price ceiling has already materialized in the baseline tariffs leading into the
new framework, with the National Markets and Competition Commission approving a 6.44% average
tariff hike for 2026. This near-term adjustment sets the maximum adjusted annual revenue per passenger at
€11.02 for 2026, establishing a higher financial baseline before DORA III commences. 3 4 • News
Aena's DORA III Proposal: Capital Expansion and OPEX Pressures
To address capacity constraints and modernize its network, Aena submitted its formal DORA III proposal to the Directorate General of Civil Aviation and the CNMC on February 18, 2026. The framework relies on an
unprecedented capital expenditure plan totaling €12.89 billion over the five-year period, of which €9.99 billion is
strictly designated as regulated aeronautical investment. This funding is essential to expand congested terminals,
elevate physical and cybersecurity standards, and advance decarbonization targets. 5 • ARS
Alongside elevated capital investments, Aena projects a significant escalation in its regulated operating expenses , climbing from €1.99 billion in 2027 to €2.31 billion by 2031. This cost ramp-up is largely inflexible,
driven by the maintenance needs of aging infrastructure, heightened security regulations, and the requirement to
bolster service personnel during terminal construction to prevent quality degradation. 7 8 • IRPRESS
To sustainably finance this cycle, Aena's proposal outlines conservative growth metrics and requests higher capital returns. The company forecasts a modest passenger traffic compound annual growth rate of
1.3%, aiming to reach 347 million passengers by 2031. Based on these inputs, Aena is formally requesting a
9.0% pre-tax Weighted Average Cost of Capital . Consequently, the proposed tariff trajectory
incorporates an average annual increase of €0.43 per passenger, which equates to a +3.8% annual tariff hike
through 2031. Management argues that the 9.0% WACC reflects the true financial risk profile of deploying
massive capital amid geopolitical uncertainty and inflationary pressures. 9 10 13 • IRPRESS

Airline Pushback and the Tariff War
The proposed economic parameters have sparked intense friction with airport users, leading to a highly contested negotiation process. Airline associations, including the Airline Association and the International Air
Transport Association , have aggressively lobbied the government to halt Aena's fee increases. Industry
representatives estimate that the 3.8% annual tariff hike would impose a severe €800 million cost burden on
airlines and consumers over the five-year period. 14 15 16 • News
Airlines fundamentally dispute Aena's underlying assumptions, particularly regarding traffic and WACC. Independent studies commissioned by ALA and IATA project a much stronger passenger traffic CAGR of 3.6% for
the period. Furthermore, airline advocates argue that an airport operator functioning without direct local
competition cannot justify a 9.0% WACC, and they are countering with a proposed return on capital of 6.35%. By leveraging higher traffic expectations and a lower WACC, the airlines are demanding a 4.9% annual reduction in
tariffs, arguing that Aena historically underestimated traffic to extract excessive regulatory returns. 17
