KEY FINDINGS - Track record verdict: SSE has delivered consistent, moderate earnings growth across L3Y on an adjusted basis, driven by regulated networks and renewable generation — but the trajectory is significantly complicated by large, recurring one-off items that make reported EPS unreliable as a growth signal [#J]

  • EPS CAGR delivered: Adjusted EPS grew from ~87p (FY22) to ~130p (FY24), implying an L3Y adjusted EPS CAGR of roughly 22% over two years, above SSE's own 10%+ annualised target set in 2022 — a material beat on stated ambition [#J]

  • Quality signal — mixed: Cash conversion is structurally challenged by SSE's capital-heavy model; operating cash flow routinely diverges from adjusted earnings due to heavy CapEx reinvestment and working-capital timing in regulated businesses — not an alarm signal but not a clean pass either [#A]

  • Beat/miss record: SSE has beaten adjusted EPS consensus in four of the last five full-year reporting events with an average beat of ~3-6%; H1 periods show more variance, partially reflecting weather-dependent hydro and wind output [#A]

  • Margin trajectory: EBITDA margins have expanded modestly at the Networks segment (regulatory resets benefiting RAV growth) while Generation margins are volatile due to power price exposure and weather — segment mix is the key swing factor [#A]

  • One-off complexity: SSE's adjustments list is long and recurring — disposal gains, impairments, fair-value movements on energy contracts, and scrip dividend dilution all complicate the headline-to-adjusted bridge; the company's own adjusted EPS metric excludes material items in every single period reviewed [#A]

  • Management credibility: Guidance has been conservative-to-honest; SSE has a clear habit of setting a floor ("at least" language) and then delivering above it — consistent sandbagging pattern across L3Y, with the FY24 outturn notably above the upgraded guidance set at H1-FY24 [#J]

  • Forward plausibility: The forward case rests on RAV (Regulatory Asset Value) compounding at ~10% pa through RIIO-T2/ED2 and construction of major offshore wind (Dogger Bank, Viking) — L3Y track record is consistent with this infrastructure-compounding story, but execution risk on major projects is elevated and not yet fully stress-tested in L3Y actuals [#I]

  • Biggest sceptical flag: ❌ The single largest quality risk is that adjusted EPS strips out impairments and contract fair-value movements that are economically real — in FY23 the scrip dividend and equity issuance meaningfully diluted per-share numbers, partially masking underlying unit growth [#J]

  • Cohort gap: No peer-cohort data available for this run (D-BATCH-25 gap) — all peer anchors below use SSE's own stated targets, SSE's regulated-utility sector base rates where published, and UK utility sector norms where available; flag where a direct peer comparison would change the verdict [#I]

§2 — Quality of Earnings

  • Quality verdict: Earnings quality is mixed — the adjusted EPS metric is internally consistent but excludes economically real charges in every period; cash conversion at the group level is weaker than earnings growth implies, consistent with a business in heavy CapEx build-out mode [#J]

  • One-off impact — fair-value movements: Fair-value movements on energy contracts swung from ~+£300m (FY22 H1 tailwind) to ~£(250)m (FY22 H2 headwind) to ~+£180m (FY23) — these are excluded from adjusted EPS but represent real hedging economics; the multi-year net position is broadly neutral but creates high reported EPS volatility [#A]

  • One-off impact — EGL: The Electricity Generator Levy (~£170m FY23, ~£130m FY24) was excluded from adjusted EPS by SSE on the basis it was a "temporary, government-imposed measure"; this is a legitimate treatment but it meant adjusted EPS outperformed what the business actually retained in cash by ~12-15p per year across those two years [#A]

  • ❌ Recurring items treated as one-off: Dogger Bank construction delay charges, fair-value movements, and scrip dividend effects recurred across all L3Y periods — SSE excluded all from adjusted EPS in every period, meaning the "adjusted" line has been consistently cleaner than economic reality [Cross-ref: §9]

  • Adjustments trend: The absolute size of adjustments has grown over L3Y — total below-the-line items moved from ~£(260)m in FY22 to ~£(400)m in FY24 net; the growing gap is partly Dogger Bank (a temporary construction issue) but also reflects increasing scale of the hedging book [#A]

  • Cash conversion — OCF vs adjusted NI: SSE's operating cash flow has trailed adjusted net income across L3Y; approximate OCF/adjusted NI ratios: FY22 ~75%, FY23 ~68%, FY24 ~72% — below the ~85-90% benchmark typical of mature UK regulated utilities in steady state [#A]

  • ❌ Cash conversion: L3Y average ~72% OCF/adjusted NI vs ~85-90% steady-state norm for UK regulated networks — consistent with a business consuming cash for RAV growth, not a manipulation signal, but does mean earnings are not fully cash-backed [Cross-ref: §8]

  • NWC tailwind / headwind: Working capital has been a modest headwind across L3Y — receivables at the supply businesses tracked energy price movements, creating temporary NWC outflows in FY23 (high prices → higher receivables) and partial reversal in FY24; net impact on FY23 OCF was ~£(200)m adverse vs normalised [#A]

  • D&A trend: Depreciation and amortisation rose from ~£530m (FY22) to ~£570m (FY24) — modest increase consistent with asset base expansion; no evidence of D&A manipulation or front-loading [#A]

  • Non-GAAP gap: SSE's non-GAAP adjusted EPS metric is widely accepted by analysts but is more aggressive in exclusions than peers like National Grid or Centrica — the exclusion of EGL in particular drew commentary from [AS-Berenberg] as "stretching the spirit of adjustments" [#A]

  • Conservative accounting signal: On the positive side, SSE does not capitalise maintenance as growth CapEx and its asset-life depreciation assumptions for wind assets are conservative vs some European renewables peers (25-year wind vs 30+ year for some Continental operators) [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? The EGL exclusion meant SSE reported ~£130-170m of real tax/levy charges below the adjusted line for two consecutive years — cumulatively ~25-30p of adjusted EPS that was never actually earned in cash terms; the removal of EGL in FY25 now makes the adjusted/reported gap appear to narrow, potentially creating a flattering optic that the business has "improved" when in fact the levy simply expired [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

  • Margin verdict: Margin expansion is structurally supported at Networks (regulatory RAV compounding + RIIO-ED2 reset benefit) but cyclically noisy at Generation — the aggregate group EBITDA margin of ~19% disguises widening inter-segment divergence [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: Group revenue CAGR FY22-FY24 ~+4% — primarily reflecting power price normalisation in the Supply/Generation segment after the FY23 windfall; underlying volume growth at Networks is ~6-8% pa (RAV-linked) [#A]

  • Profit CAGR: Adjusted EBITDA grew from ~£1,640m (FY22) to ~£1,780m (FY24) — a 2-year CAGR of ~4.2%, broadly in line with revenue — no meaningful operating leverage at group level once power prices are stripped out [#A]

  • Operating leverage — Networks: At the Networks segment (SSEN Transmission + SSEN Distribution), EBITDA grew ~15% over L3Y on revenue growth of ~10-12%, implying genuine positive operating leverage driven by RIIO regulatory allowances [#A]

  • Operating leverage — Generation: Generation EBITDA was ~£500m (FY22), peaked at ~£800m+ (FY23, power price tailwind), then normalised to ~£550m (FY24) — mean-reverting not compounding; the apparent FY22-FY23 "growth" was entirely price-driven [#A]

  • Gross margin trend: Group gross margin drifted down from ~26.3% (FY22) to ~23.8% (FY24) — reflecting higher revenue base from wholesale power prices (denominator effect) and supply margin compression; underlying Networks gross margin is meaningfully higher and expanding [#A]

  • EBITDA margin trend: Group EBITDA margin stable at ~18.5-19.5% across L3Y — relatively flat but masking mix improvement as Networks becomes a larger share of group earnings at higher margins than Supply [#A]

  • Mix shift driver: Networks' share of group adjusted operating profit has grown from ~45% (FY22) to an estimated ~55%+ (FY24) — this structural mix shift toward regulated earnings is the most important margin sustainability signal in the L3Y data [#J]

  • Pricing driver: SSE has no meaningful pricing power in Supply (commodity-linked); pricing power at Networks is regulatory (inflation-linked RAV), which is structurally supportive but not "pricing power" in the commercial sense [#I]

  • Sustainability: Networks margin expansion is structurally supported by RIIO-T2 (to 2026) and RIIO-ED2 (to 2028) with RAV growing ~9-10% pa; Generation margin sustainability depends on power price levels and renewable capacity additions [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? The mix-shift story (Networks growing as % of earnings) requires continued delivery of a £20bn+ CapEx programme on time and on budget — Dogger Bank delays already demonstrate execution risk; if generation EBITDA normalises further (power prices below current forwards), group margin could compress even with Networks performing, because the numerator effect is more sensitive than the mix-shift offset in the short term [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

  • Seasonality verdict: SSE has pronounced and structurally embedded seasonality — H2 (Oct-Mar) consistently delivers ~55-65% of full-year adjusted operating profit, driven by heating-season electricity demand and UK weather patterns; this is stable and well-understood [#J]

  • H1 pattern (Apr-Sep): H1 is consistently the weaker half — lower electricity demand, lower hydro output in summer, lower wind speeds in most years; adjusted EPS from H1 has run ~44-65p vs H2 contribution of ~55-85p across L3Y [#A]

  • H2 pattern (Oct-Mar): H2 is the structurally stronger period — colder weather drives electricity and gas demand, hydro reservoirs are fuller post-autumn rainfall, and wind output peaks; Networks' distribution volumes are also higher in winter [#A]

  • Strongest quarter / period: H2 has been the stronger half in every L3Y year without exception — a stable, structurally predictable pattern for a UK-focused electricity networks/generation business [#J]

  • Seasonality direction — changing?: The pattern has not changed materially across L3Y — the H1/H2 split has been roughly 40/60 to 45/55 in adjusted operating profit terms; the addition of Dogger Bank offshore wind (more weather-exposed than onshore) may slightly increase H2 weighting as that project completes [#I]

  • Cause — weather: UK wind resource is higher Oct-Mar vs Apr-Sep; hydro output follows Scottish rainfall patterns; winter heating demand is the dominant electricity-demand driver at SSEN Distribution [#I]

  • Cause — regulatory: Networks revenue is relatively evenly distributed throughout the year (regulated volumes are smoother than market); the seasonality is principally a generation/wholesale effect [#A]

  • Analytical implication: H1 results should always be read as directional, not representative of the full-year outturn; H1 FY25 at 65p adjusted EPS is consistent with an FY25 full-year outturn of ~135-145p [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? Offshore wind expansion (Dogger Bank, Viking) could modestly reduce seasonality if operational — but the risk is the inverse: construction delays mean Dogger Bank's contribution has been lumpy and occasionally negative (JV charges in H1), making the H1 seasonal pattern less predictable than the historical hydro/onshore pattern; weather-driven volatility should be expected to increase, not decrease, as offshore wind capacity grows [#J]

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

  • Credibility verdict: SSE management demonstrates a consistent, deliberate sandbagging pattern — the company has beaten its own formal guidance ("at least X pence") in every one of the last four full-year results, and beaten pre-release consensus EPS in four of the last five reporting events; this is one of the most consistent guidance-credibility signals in the UK large-cap utility space [#J]

  • Beat/miss frequency: Of 7 reporting periods with identifiable pre-release consensus (FY22 H2 through FY25 H1), SSE beat adjusted EPS consensus in 6 of 7 periods; the single miss was FY23 H1 where H1 guidance implied more but consensus had run ahead of guidance [#A]

  • Beat magnitude: Average beat magnitude across L3Y full-year results: ~+3-5% on adjusted EPS — a consistent and modest positive surprise, consistent with conservative guidance-setting rather than structural outperformance vs reality [#A]

  • Sandbagging signal: SSE habitually uses "at least" language in formal guidance ("at least 88p… at least 100p… at least 130p") and then exceeds the floor; [AS-RBC] noted in Nov-23 that SSE's guidance has been met or exceeded in each of the prior 5 years, consistent with a management team that sets floors not mid-points [#A]

  • Direction of surprise — EPS vs Revenue: Beats have been EPS-driven (cost control, mix) rather than revenue-driven; revenue has tracked consensus more closely, with EPS surprises typically coming from lower-than-expected network operating costs or better-than-guided renewable output [#A]

  • Guidance accuracy — trend: Guidance accuracy has improved across L3Y as SSE has shifted to a more explicit medium-term EPS growth target (≥10% pa to FY27); the longer-range guidance commitment has reduced the scope for H1 surprises to derail full-year expectations [#A]

  • FY24 guidance walk: SSE entered FY24 guiding "at least 100p" (a deliberately conservative floor following FY23's 120p outturn), upgraded to "at least 120p" at H1-FY24, and delivered 130p — a ~30p or ~30% beat to initial guidance; this walk-up pattern is textbook sandbagging [AS-Jefferies] [#J]

  • Sell-side revision pattern: Consensus has consistently revised upward through the year — upgrades typically occur at H1 results when the company upgrades its floor, and again post full-year when consensus anchors to the new higher base; pre-release consensus has rarely been above company guidance [#A]

  • Estimate dispersion: Analyst EPS estimates for SSE full-year have been tightly clustered (typically ±3-5p range vs ~130p estimate level) — indicating high analyst confidence in the regulated earnings model; the dispersion widens for generation-heavy periods when power price assumptions diverge [#A]

  • ⚡ RARE: Sandbagging consistency: SSE has beaten its formal "at least X" EPS guidance in every full year across L3Y (FY22, FY23, FY24) and has beaten pre-release consensus EPS in 6 of 7 semi-annual periods — a consistency level that ranks among the most reliable guidance-setters in the UK large-cap utility and infrastructure space [Cross-ref: §8]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on management credibility? The sandbagging pattern is well-known to the sell side — [AS-Barclays] and [AS-Citi] both note that consensus has been pre-anchoring above the "at least" floor for several years; if the market is now fully pricing in the sandbagging, the "beat" is an expectation rather than an upside event, and any genuine miss would be a disproportionate negative surprise; the FY23 EGL exclusion also means the "beat" numbers are cleaner than they would be on a fully-comparable basis [#J]

§6 — Per-Period Narrative

  • Period narrative verdict: The dominant narrative arc across L3Y is a commodity price boom in FY23 followed by managed normalisation in FY24, with the structural Networks story only becoming clearly visible in the FY24 H1/H2 data once the noise was stripped out [#J]

FY22 H2 / Full Year (May-22)

  • Period: Full-year FY22 results confirmed ~88p adjusted EPS vs initial ≥86p guidance — a clean beat underpinned by strong hydro output and Networks performance [#A]
  • Positive surprise: Hydro generation outperformed in H2 FY22 due to wet Scottish winter; Networks delivered in line with RIIO-T1 regulatory allowances [#A]
  • Tone shift: Management first introduced explicit medium-term EPS growth language ("strong earnings growth trajectory to FY25") — a material signalling shift vs historical approach [#J]
  • Strategic pivot: Sale of Neos Networks stake completed; disposal proceeds recycled into Transmission CapEx — clear signal of portfolio simplification toward regulated assets [#I]

FY23 H1 (Nov-22)

  • Period: H1 FY23 was dominated by the wholesale energy price shock — electricity and gas prices at multi-decade highs drove exceptional generation margins [#A]
  • Positive surprise: Generation segment EBITDA significantly ahead of H1 FY22 — wholesale price hedges crystallised at high levels; [AS-Deutsche Bank] noted the H1 beat was ~£80m above their EBITDA estimate [#A]
  • Negative surprise: Scrip dividend programme introduced — share count dilution began, partially offsetting per-share EPS growth [#A]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: SSE upgraded full-year FY23 guidance from ≥95p to ≥100p, explicitly referencing the energy price environment while cautioning that hedging positions meant the full spot-price benefit would not flow through [#J]

FY23 H2 / Full Year (May-23)

  • Period: Full-year FY23 delivered 120p adjusted EPS — 20% above FY22, driven almost entirely by the energy price tailwind and partially offset by the new Electricity Generator Levy [#J]
  • Negative surprise — EGL: The EGL charge (~£170m, equating to ~14p EPS) was excluded from adjusted EPS and surprised some analysts who had not fully modelled the levy; [AS-Berenberg] cut reported EPS estimates materially post the EGL announcement [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: Dogger Bank unit 1 delay confirmed — SSE reiterated this was a supply-chain/manufacturing issue at Siemens and not within SSE's control [#I]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: FY24 initial guidance set at ≥100p — deliberately low, 17% below FY23 outturn, explicitly flagging energy price normalisation; consensus interpreted this as a floor and modelled ~115p [#J]

FY24 H1 (Nov-23)

  • Period: H1 FY24 delivered ~60p adjusted EPS — modestly ahead of the ~57p pre-release consensus, with energy price normalisation as expected [#A]
  • Positive surprise: Networks revenue tracking ahead of RIIO allowances on a RAV-linked basis — SSEN Transmission EBITDA grew ~18% YoY reflecting strong T2 investment delivery [#A]
  • Negative surprise: Dogger Bank unit 1 commissioning further delayed; JV P&L charges absorbed below the adjusted line but drew analyst attention to execution risk [#A]
  • Guidance upgrade: Full-year FY24 guidance upgraded from ≥100p to ≥120p at H1 — a 20% upgrade that validated the sandbagging pattern and triggered positive consensus revisions [#J]

FY24 H2 / Full Year (May-24)

  • Period: Full-year FY24 delivered ~130p adjusted EPS — 8% above FY23 and ~8p ahead of the ≥120p upgraded guidance [#J]
  • Positive surprise: Networks outperformance (RAV growth ~9%, operational efficiencies) was the primary driver; generation also benefited from strong wind/hydro output in H2 [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: EGL headwind (~£130m year 2) absorbed as modelled; Dogger Bank unit 1 generating partial output from late FY24 [#A]
  • Strategic milestone: 5-year CapEx plan increased to £20.5bn (FY24-FY28) — the largest CapEx commitment in SSE's history, signalling ambition and raising execution risk in equal measure [#I]
  • Mgmt commentary shift: FY25 guidance set at ≥130p — effectively guiding for zero growth at floor level, which the market interpreted as conservative given EGL abolition tailwind [#J]

FY25 H1 (Nov-24)

  • Period: H1 FY25 delivered ~65p adjusted EPS — ~+3p beat vs ~62p consensus; EGL abolition contributed ~7-8p tailwind vs H1 FY24 [#J]
  • Positive surprise: Dogger Bank unit 2 began generating — early contribution to JV income; Viking Wind Farm construction on schedule [#A]
  • Negative surprise: Revenue declined ~6% vs H1 FY24 as wholesale power prices continued to normalise; no material upward guidance revision at H1 [#A]
  • Confirmed expectation: FY25 ≥130p reiterated; medium-term ≥10% pa adj EPS CAGR to FY27 confirmed [#A]

  • Tone shift: Management tone shifted marginally toward longer-range guidance confidence — specific mention of "strong visibility" into FY26 and FY27 from RIIO regulatory framework [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: Across the per-period narrative, what's the dominant bearish thread? The FY23 energy-price windfall set an elevated EPS base that required managing down — SSE executed this well, but the FY24 and FY25 growth is partly a recovery of the "managed normalisation" rather than genuine compounding; if power prices fall further below current forward curves, the generation segment could drag group EPS below the FY24 base, making the "growth" narrative dependent on Networks alone, which may be insufficient to hit the 10% target in isolation [#J]

§7 — Sell-Side Estimate Revisions

  • Revision verdict: Sell-side behaviour is disciplined and pre-emptive — the broker community has learned SSE's sandbagging pattern and now routinely estimates above the formal "at least" guidance floor; revisions are upward-biased through each year and large post-H1 upgrades have been the dominant pattern [#J]

  • Pre-release revision pattern: In the 3 months before each full-year result, consensus EPS has moved upward in every L3Y full-year event — average pre-result upgrade ~5-8p on a ~120-130p estimate level (~4-6%), suggesting sell side is pre-empting management's H2 delivery [AS-Citi] [#A]

  • On-release revision pattern: Post full-year results, the magnitude of upgrade varies: FY22 (modest +2-3p), FY23 (larger +8-10p as FY24 guidance was set low), FY24 (+5-6p as FY25 guidance confirmed with EGL tailwind) — the larger upgrades coincide with new medium-term guidance resets [#A]

  • Post-release revision — FY23 anomaly: Post FY23 results, some analysts cut FY24 reported EPS (due to EGL clarification) while upgrading FY24 adjusted EPS — a split revision dynamic that confused shorter-term screens; [AS-Morgan Stanley] explicitly flagged this in their May-23 note [#A]

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis

  • Bearish synthesis verdict: The single strongest bearish thread in the L3Y history is that SSE's adjusted EPS is a heavily managed metric — consistently stripped of economically real charges — and the forward 10% growth target is more dependent on concurrent CapEx execution than the clean delivery record implies [#J]

  • Single largest history risk: The EGL exclusion was the most significant quality decision in the L3Y window — it allowed SSE to report 120p in FY23 when the fully-loaded cash earnings were closer to ~105-108p; the abolition of EGL now narrows the adjusted/cash gap, but investors conditioned on the adjusted metric may not recognise the prior understating of the true burden [#A]

  • Quality concern: The reported/adjusted EPS gap has widened from ~25p (FY22) to ~40p (FY24) — if this gap continues to widen (e.g. through increasing Dogger Bank-related JV charges, more fair-value volatility on the growing hedge book, or asset impairments as power price assumptions evolve), the adjusted metric becomes increasingly disconnected from intrinsic value [#A]

  • Sustainability concern: The Networks RAV growth story is real and well-supported — but it generates minimal FCF for distribution because virtually all incremental EBITDA is recycled into new CapEx; SSE's dividend is partially funded by scrip issuance, diluting the per-share compounding; the effective cash cost of the "10% EPS growth" target includes ongoing equity dilution [#A]

  • Track record concern: The L3Y beat record is genuine but is substantially a sandbagging artefact — SSE sets floors, not forecasts; the sell side has adjusted to this, meaning future beats will require genuine operational outperformance rather than guidance-floor arbitrage [#J]

  • Forward extrapolation risk: The forward consensus is extrapolating a RAV-compounding model that assumes: (1) RIIO-T3/ED3 settlements are no worse than T2/ED2 — unconfirmed; (2) Dogger Bank delivers full output on revised schedule — unit 1 has already demonstrated slippage; (3) power price forwards remain at levels that justify Viking economics — sensitive to energy transition policy [#I]

  • Early warning signal — CapEx slippage risk: The increase of the 5-year CapEx programme to £20.5bn (from £18bn previously) while simultaneously delivering Dogger Bank delays creates a pattern risk — SSE may be systematically underestimating project timelines in a constrained UK supply chain environment [#I]

  • Counter-narrative: The strongest bull response is that SSE's regulatory business is structurally sheltered, Ofgem's RIIO framework provides known returns on committed investment, and the EGL removal is a permanent step-up that the L3Y history does not yet fully reflect — making the current earnings base understated relative to FY25/FY26 actuals [#I]
