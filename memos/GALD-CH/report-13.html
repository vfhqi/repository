Management's current formal guidance covers FY2025 as the near-term anchor and a medium-term framework extending to 2028 (set at the March 2024 Capital Markets Day).

  • Verdict — guidance completeness: Management provides explicit FY annual guidance plus a named 3Y framework, making this a structurally well-disclosed guidance regime by European mid-cap standards [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Organic net sales growth of +8% to +10% for FY2025 (constant-currency, organic, excluding Sculptra contribution in the base period where applicable); stated at Q4 2024 results, March 2025 [#A]

  • Sub: absolute FY2024 net sales delivered USD 4.27bn; FY2025 guidance at midpoint implies ~USD 4.62-4.69bn organic

  • Sub: currency caveat explicit — USD/CHF and USD/EUR moves create reported-currency variance of ±1-2pp at current spot levels [AS-JPMorgan]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Core EBITDA margin guided to expand by approximately 50-75bps versus FY2024 (which delivered 25.9% core EBITDA margin); implies ~26.4-26.6% for FY2025 [#A]

  • Sub: "Core EBITDA" excludes share-based compensation, exceptional items, Sculptra step-acquisition costs — definition consistent since IPO [AS-UBS]

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit adjusted-EPS point guidance issued for FY2025; management points to EBITDA margin + revenue range as the implicit EPS proxy; adjusted net income growth modelled by SS at ~15-20% for FY2025 [#I]

  • Sub: leverage trajectory guidance (net leverage moving toward ~2.0x by end-FY2025 vs ~2.5x at IPO) provides partial EPS anchor through interest cost reduction

  • FY current — FCF: Free cash flow conversion guided to "strong double-digit % of revenue" — no hard FCF figure; management defines FCF as cash from operations less capex, before working capital seasonality adjustment [AS-Berenberg]

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided at ~3.5-4.0% of net sales (approximately USD 160-190m for FY2025); consistent with FY2024 actuals of 3.6% [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: Capital Markets Day (March 2024) set 2024-2028 framework: organic revenue CAGR of +8-10%, core EBITDA margin reaching ~30% by 2028 (from ~25% at IPO), implying ~+100bps per year [#A]

  • Sub: 2028 target of 30% EBITDA margin is a 500bps expansion over 4 years — aspirational but not unprecedented for specialty pharma/aesthetics at scale [AS-Goldman Sachs]

  • Medium-term — N3Y EPS: Management targets adjusted EPS CAGR of "high teens to low twenties" percent over 2024-2028, driven by EBITDA margin expansion + leverage reduction + organic volume growth [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Capital Markets Day, Zurich, March 14, 2024 — the primary medium-term framework anchor [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Q4 2024 / FY2024 results call, March 2025 — most recent FY2025 guidance reaffirmation [#I]

  • Update timing: FY guidance updated at each quarterly results release; no mid-quarter updates or profit warnings issued in public-company history to date [#I]

  • Caveat: All guidance stated on organic constant-currency basis; Sculptra milestones and PuraSense pipeline are excluded from base guidance until commercially launched [AS-Citi]

  • Conditional assumption: Injectable aesthetics market assumed to grow at "normalised" +8-10% globally; management explicitly flags that FY2024-H2 US softness was transitory and that normalised growth resumes in FY2025 — this is the key conditional [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that management's "organic constant-currency" framing systematically strips the two largest uncertainties (FX and Sculptra integration costs) from the headline number, making apparent guidance ranges narrower than economic reality; the embedded "normalised injectable market" assumption is an assertion, not a demonstrated reversion, and 2025-H1 data remains mixed [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Verdict — posture: Management exhibits a mild but consistent sandbagging pattern on revenue guidance; EBITDA margin guidance is more honest-tracking with upside optionality; no over-promising pattern identified across L4 periods [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: Revenue guidance consistently uses directional ranges (HSD, MSD+) rather than numeric midpoints in the pre-IPO era; this structural ambiguity creates a mechanical beat tendency when actuals hit the top-third of any reasonable range interpretation [#A]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 4 of 4 periods beat or met revenue guidance; average upside ~100bps above midpoint; consistent direction of surprise is positive — mild sandbagger profile [#A]

  • Sub: comparable mid-cap specialty pharma sandbagger baseline (no cohort injected) typically shows 55-65% of periods beating revenue guidance; Galderma's 100% over L4 is above-baseline

  • Honest tracker — margins: EBITDA margin guidance behaves more like honest-tracking: 3 of 4 periods in-line to modestly positive; the FY2022 miss was an external shock, not a pattern failure; management does not systematically guide margin conservatively [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: EPS guidance is structurally absent (directional only); management is avoiding the reputational risk of an EPS point miss during leverage-reduction transition — a rational but investor-unfriendly posture [#I]

  • Sub: absence of numeric EPS guidance is common for PE-backed newly-listed companies (CVC, EQT-backed IPOs 2022-2024) in the first 12-24 months; this is a pattern, not Galderma-specific [AS-Barclays]

  • Pattern shift: Post-IPO (FY2024), management transitioned to slightly more numeric guidance (explicit "8-10% organic" language) versus the directional pre-IPO framing — a positive credibility signal that more granular beat/miss analysis becomes possible [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Revenue beat magnitudes are modest (+80-200bps above midpoint), not dramatic — this is consistent with genuine conservatism rather than sandbagging-for-optics [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Sole EBITDA miss (FY2022) at -70 to -120bps below midpoint — magnitude contained; management did not miss revenue in the same period [#A]

  • Beat consistency — CapEx: CapEx in-line or underspend in all 4 periods; capex conservatism (guiding 4% then delivering 3.6-3.8%) contributes to FCF beats [AS-Deutsche Bank]

  • Improving accuracy: Post-IPO disclosure framework (explicit numeric ranges, quarterly updates) structurally improves accuracy comparability versus pre-IPO directional language; trajectory of guidance precision is improving [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the sandbagging pattern is that the "conservative" revenue ranges relied on a structurally growing aesthetics market as the safety margin; if the market growth rate decelerates to +4-6% (from assumed +8-10%), the sandbagger cushion disappears and guidance becomes aspirational — improving accuracy may be market-cycle-dependent rather than management-discipline-structural [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Verdict — trend: Guidance accuracy is improving, driven by transition from directional pre-IPO language to explicit numeric ranges post-IPO; however, the trend is masked by the favourable demand cycle FY2021-FY2024, making structural improvement hard to isolate [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY2024 (first public-company year) was the most precisely guided and most accurately delivered period: revenue in-line-to-beat and EBITDA margin effectively in-line at the top of the guided range [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY2021-FY2023 accuracy was directionally correct but harder to score precisely due to range-not-number guidance; the structural ambiguity inflated apparent beat frequency [#I]

  • Accuracy improving: Post-IPO quarterly cadence with explicit %-growth ranges creates a tighter accountability loop; management is now subject to consensus tracking versus a specific number, which incentivises more careful calibration [#A]

  • Sub: IPO-vintage companies typically show guidance accuracy improving in years 2-3 post-listing as management learns investor expectations [AS-Morgan Stanley]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — no evidence: No deterioration trend identified; the FY2022 margin miss is the sole meaningful miss and is isolated to an exogenous shock year [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: FY2022 miss was primarily macro-driven (China lockdowns, FX, input costs); no internal execution failure evidenced in the historical record [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: Sculptra acquisition (closed mid-2024) adds integration complexity to FY2025 guidance — first year with a major acquired asset in the guidance base; this is a new source of potential variance [AS-UBS]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx-to-sales ratio is the most consistently accurate guided metric — within ~20bps of guidance in all 4 periods; capex is within management's direct control, explaining the precision [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Core EBITDA margin is the most volatile guided metric — 100bps miss in FY2022; the mix-sensitive nature of aesthetics (Dysport vs filler margins differ materially) creates inherent guidance difficulty [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that the improving trajectory is entirely an artefact of the post-IPO disclosure upgrade and a benign demand environment; the first meaningful external shock in the public-company era (FY2024-H2 US softness) prompted zero guidance revision — management either had genuine visibility or deferred the problem into FY2025 guidance; the FY2025 year will be the first real accuracy test under normalising conditions [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Verdict — assumption credibility: Revenue growth assumptions are well-grounded in category dynamics; EBITDA margin expansion assumptions are achievable but sensitive to injectable mix and Sculptra cost timeline; EPS assumptions have meaningful leverage optionality that is under-appreciated [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Injectable aesthetics global market assumed to grow +8-10% CAGR (CMD 2024); this is consistent with historical category CAGR of ~9% (2015-2023) but embeds no slowdown from 2024-H2 US softness being permanent [#A]

  • Sub: if injectable market grows at +5-6% (bear case), Galderma would need significant share gains to achieve +8-10% organic revenue — a materially harder task [AS-Goldman Sachs]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Pricing contribution assumed at +2-3% annually across injectables and dermocosmetics; dermocosmetics (Cetaphil) pricing power is better anchored than filler pricing, which is facing generic/biosimilar HA filler competition in EU [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — Sculptra: Sculptra poly-L-lactic acid contribution (~USD 120-150m incremental annualised) layered into FY2025 base; guidance treats Sculptra as "in base" from FY2025; integration risk is timing and channel overlap with Restylane [AS-UBS] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — operating leverage: EBITDA margin expansion guided at ~+100bps/year over 2024-2028 ; ~50bps from manufacturing scale (Reinbek and Fort Worth sites absorbing fixed costs on higher volumes), ~30bps from SG&A leverage, ~20bps from mix toward higher-margin Dysport/neurotoxin [#A]

  • Sub: manufacturing scale leverage is the most credible component — Galderma runs integrated biologics manufacturing that benefits from volume leverage [AS-Deutsche Bank]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Input costs (hyaluronic acid, botulinum toxin fermentation) assumed stable to modestly positive in FY2025; no significant commodity inflation embedded; any energy cost surge would pressure FY2025 margin guidance [#I]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Dysport volume pricing relatively stable; filler (Restylane) pricing under modest pressure from HA filler generics entering EU market (no US generic threat yet) — management has not explicitly guided for pricing pressure, which is an optimistic assumption [AS-Jefferies] [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net leverage decline from ~2.5x to ~2.0x by end-FY2025 reduces interest expense by ~USD 40-60m; this is a structural EPS tailwind that is reliably modellable and conservative [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed at ~18-20% (Swiss-headquartered, IP held in Switzerland); post-Pillar Two global minimum tax implementation, management guides for modest effective rate increase to ~20-22% from FY2025; this is a known headwind now embedded in SS models [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count assumed broadly stable; no material buyback programme in place; dilution from management equity plans (~0.5-1% annually) is modest [#I]

  • Macro assumption — currency: USD/CHF and USD/EUR are the two dominant exposures; guidance given on CC basis; at current spot rates (USD ~0.88/CHF), FX is ~+1pp reported revenue tailwind versus CC guidance — a hidden cushion at current levels [AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: No explicit GDP assumption; but US and China consumer spending are the two most important macro inputs; management assumes US aesthetics spend normalises in H2 2025 without explicit data support [#I]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Verdict — credibility: MEDIUM-HIGH — management has delivered on or above guidance in 7 of 8 major metric-period tests; the sole miss was exogenous; medium-term targets are grounded in verifiable category data, not blue-sky projection [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH credibility — constrained from HIGH by (1) short public-company track record, (2) absence of explicit EPS guidance, and (3) key revenue assumption (injectable normalisation) that is asserted rather than evidenced [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY2024 beat on both revenue and EBITDA margin in first public-company year — a meaningful signal that management set achievable IPO-guidance targets rather than stretch targets [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Revenue guidance framed as organic CC deliberately strips out FX tailwinds and Sculptra contribution at point of guidance — an inherently conservative presentation framework [#I]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Manufacturing scale-up at Reinbek and Fort Worth executed without major disruption; Dysport biosimilar biosimilars have not eroded market share; Cetaphil grew through the 2022-2023 input cost cycle [AS-Deutsche Bank, UBS] [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: ❌ No direct public-company miss yet — but FY2022 bondholder-era EBITDA miss is the relevant data point; management did not pre-warn, which is a disclosure quality concern [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: ❌ The 30% core EBITDA margin by 2028 embeds ~100bps/year improvement every year for 4 years — back-end loaded if years 1-2 deliver only 50-75bps; this creates a credibility cliff if year 3-4 expansion must accelerate to compensate [AS-Jefferies, Morgan Stanley] [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: ❌ US consumer aesthetics sentiment is the single most important uncontrollable variable; 2024-H2 evidence of trading-down from premium fillers is not yet resolved; management's assertion of normalisation is not yet supported by H1 2025 data [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: Sculptra integration (new channel, new consumable, different physician call profile) is an execution risk that management has limited track record on — prior M&A was smaller bolt-ons [AS-Barclays] [#A]

  • Margin of safety — medium: If guidance is off by 10% on EBITDA (i.e., ~26.5% delivered vs ~29.3% in a -10% EPS scenario for FY2025), the investment remains viable but IRR falls to the high-teens rather than 25%+; highly sensitive to the 2028 margin target achievement [#J]

  • Sub: at current valuation (~28-30x FY2025E PE, ~20x FY2025E EV/EBITDA), missing the 30% margin target by 200bps in 2028 reduces intrinsic value by ~10-15%

  • Thesis sensitivity: The 25%+ IRR thesis depends critically on ~30% EBITDA margin by 2028 being delivered; revenue growth alone (without margin expansion) supports only ~12-15% IRR at current entry multiples — margin expansion is not optional for the IRR case [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on credibility is that apparent MEDIUM-HIGH credibility is premature given only 1 year of public-company guidance delivery; the pre-IPO record is directional and unaudited against hard targets; the real credibility test is FY2025-FY2026, and the first sign of a guidance miss will be a disproportionate confidence destroyer given the IPO valuation premium embedded [#J]
