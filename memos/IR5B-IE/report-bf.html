EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Model and Competitive Position

Irish Continental Group operates as a critical infrastructure provider through its Ferries and Container & Terminal segments, delivering highly predictable, diversified booking revenues through dual business-to-customer and business-to-business models. The group completed its strategic transition to full asset ownership of the ferry fleet , strengthening long-term cost discipline and operational flexibility. ICG operates the leading Irish-based maritime transport network connecting Ireland with Britain and Europe, maintaining dominant passenger and freight linkages on Irish Sea routes while expanding direct continental corridors to capture post-Brexit trade flows. The company maintains a significantly higher operating margin profile than capital-intensive freight peers like DFDS, which posted ROIC of just 1.20% in FY25 and leverage at 4.10x NIBD/EBITDA , forcing suspension of shareholder distributions. The Short Straits and Irish Sea corridors show high concentration among four key operators that face chronic overcapacity risks, though capacity rationalization efforts and space charter agreements are stabilizing pricing discipline.

Cost Structure and Operational Risks

The group maintains a consistent policy of not financially hedging fuel prices , purchasing bunker fuel on spot markets and exposing operating costs to short-term oil shocks. Freight tariffs incorporate a wellestablished bunker adjustment factor surcharge calculated on a monthly lagged basis, recovering fuel cost movements above designated base levels. Brittany Ferries hedged 80.00% of its 2026 fuel needs at approximately $70.00 per barrel , enabling a zero passenger fuel surcharge guarantee for summer 2026 that puts pressure on unhedged competitors. Unhedged fuel exposure leaves 23.00% of non-depreciation operating costs directly vulnerable to global crude volatility, with total bunker costs rising 0.90% to €110.50 million in FY25. Days payable outstanding stretched to 84 days in FY25 from 78 days in FY24, providing significant interestfree cash funding compared to 37 days for receivables collection.

Management Quality and Strategic Execution

CEO Eamonn Rothwell has led ICG for 34 years since becoming CEO in 1992, establishing a deep culture of longterm capital discipline and operational agility through multiple market cycles. Management's primary strategic objective of achieving full fleet ownership was executed successfully with the purchase of the James Joyce in April 2025 and scheduled final payment for the Oscar Wilde in May 2026. Capital allocation history demonstrates superior execution , balancing intensive asset reinvestment with substantial returns to equity holders, including highly proactive, opportunistic shareholder alignment through massive €97.70 million in buybacks during FY25. ICG establishes high customer stickiness through critical transport infrastructure

Structural Disruption Forces and Long-Term Growth

Entry barriers remain formidable due to scarce port terminal slots and extremely high capital requirements for next-generation fleet decarbonization, with ICG's embedded port slot access at Dublin, Holyhead, and Belfast Container Terminal concession extended to 2032 blocking new entrants. EU Emissions Trading System inclusion introduces direct, escalating carbon permit liabilities covering 70.00% of emissions in 2025 and rising to 100.00% in 2026, with operators managing these through transparent customer surcharges. Terminal automation through investments in remote-controlled, semi-automated gantry cranes insulates operations from labor shortages, with Dublin Ferryport Terminals deploying nine electric, remote-controlled rubber-tyred gantry cranes and Belfast operating eight fully electric cranes powered by certified green electricity. Long-term growth pivots toward high-margin direct continental freight corridors capturing secular volume growth as hauliers avoid UK customs friction, while reintroduction of duty-free shopping on UK-EU routes supports robust retail margins. ICG's focus on full asset ownership and integrated terminal infrastructure demonstrates strong alignment with the capital-disciplined investor regime, with tightening European carbon intensity rules mechanically penalizing older, inefficient fleets and favoring ICG's modern owned vessels. 25 29
