  • Royalty revenues depend on Exelixis quarterly reports; timing mismatch between Exelixis's US reporting and Ipsen's recognition is a known lag, not a manipulation signal

  • Non-GAAP gap: Ipsen reports "Core operating profit" as its primary profitability metric, excluding amortisation of intangibles from acquisitions and restructuring charges — the gap between Core and IFRS operating profit is material given acquisition-heavy strategy [#A]

  • Quantified: IFRS operating profit is approximately 15-25% lower than Core operating profit in recent years, driven by intangible amortisation post-Epizyme and Albireo acquisitions
  • Peer anchor: European pharma peers (UCB, Jazz, Recordati) show similar 10-20% GAAP/non-GAAP gaps from acquisition amortisation — Ipsen is at the higher end but within range

  • Adjustments increasing: Core adjustments have grown in absolute size since 2021 — consistent with increased M&A but worth monitoring for adjustment creep [#A]

  • No evidence of non-recurring items recurrently included as "one-offs" — a common accounting quality red flag — but sample period is short

  • Goodwill / intangible impairment risk: Ipsen's balance sheet carries significant goodwill and intangible assets post-Epizyme (~€1.4bn acquisition) and Albireo (~€1.6bn acquisition) — if clinical/commercial execution disappoints, impairment charges are a real risk [#A]
