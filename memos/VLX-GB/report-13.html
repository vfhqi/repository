§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net signal: Management is a revenue sandbagger / EPS honest-tracker with a miss bias — a bifurcated pattern, not a clean one [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Consistent use of "£Xm+" language rather than hard-point estimates; "+" construction creates deliberate upside optionality with no downside accountability [#A]

  • Sub: 4 of 4 revenue guidance statements used the "£Xm+" or "broadly in line" framing; no year contained a hard-point ceiling

  • Sandbagger signal — FCF: "Strong cash generation, >80% conversion" language is persistently conservative; FY24 and FY25 both delivered 86-88% versus the 80% floor [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBIT margin: The "approaching 10%" and "double-digit aspiration" language has been repeated since FY22; actual delivery has moved from 8.2% to 9.4% in L4 years — progress but not velocity consistent with the framing [#A]

  • Sub: Four consecutive years of "approaching 10%" language with 10% not yet achieved constitutes aspirational over-framing, not credible annual guidance

  • Over-promiser signal — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance but the margin+revenue framing implies an EPS trajectory that has consistently disappointed in 3 of 4 periods [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: Revenue conservatism and margin optimism coexist; the honest description is that management uses the headline metric (revenue) to build trust and the sub-headline metrics (margin, EPS) to stretch aspiration [#J]

  • Beat consistency: Revenue beat/in-line in 4 of 4 periods; this is the strongest credibility signal and it is genuine [#A]

  • Miss consistency: EPS missed in 3 of 4 periods; the single delivery (FY25) is too thin to constitute a pattern shift [#A]

  • Pattern shift: FY25 marks the first simultaneous revenue + EPS delivery; if repeated in FY26, it would upgrade the pattern assessment materially — but the investor must not extrapolate from n=1 [#J]

  • Magnitude — miss: Average EPS miss magnitude of -6.8% across miss periods is not trivial; in a 12-24 month hold with a 25% IRR requirement, an annual EPS underrun of this size eliminates approximately one-quarter of the return [#A]

  • ❌ Bearish read — guidance pattern: The "revenue sandbagger + EPS miss" bifurcation may reflect a structural feature of the acquisitive growth model — acquired businesses introduce financing costs and integration drag that consistently exceed management's below-the-line assumptions; if acquisitions continue, the pattern repeats by design [#J]
