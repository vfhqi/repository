Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The company operates as a dominant global value-add distributor of refrigeration and climate control solutions, enjoying strong competitive moats from exclusive manufacturer agreements and high customer switching costs

Segment Dynamics and Product Portfolio

1.
Comfort cooling exposure: Comfort cooling and heating (HVAC) is the largest product category, representing 62% of total group net sales, driven by residential and light commercial air conditioning demand
2.
Commercial refrigeration focus: Commercial and industrial refrigeration solutions account for 34% of net sales, providing essential temperature-control systems for food retail, supermarkets, restaurants, and cold storage facilities
3.
Proprietary green technology: Original equipment manufacturing (OEM) makes up 8% of sales, serving as a premium growth engine that manufactures eco-friendly refrigeration compressor racks based on natural refrigerants
4.
Geographical sales breakdown: EMEA remains the dominant market representing approximately 60% of total sales, followed by North America at 25%, and the Asia-Pacific region at slightly below 20%
5.
Seasonal sales dynamics: Business sales are seasonally dependent, with peak demand occurring during the warm second and third quarters in the northern hemisphere, and the first and fourth quarters in the southern hemisphere

Value Chain Position and Competitive Moats

6.
Intermediary value proposition: Beijer Ref occupies a profit-favoured position between premium global manufacturers and a highly fragmented installer base, adding value through warehousing, technical support, custom design, and local branch distribution
7.
Absolute customer fragmentation: The company serves a highly fragmented base of over 100,000 independent installers and contractors, meaning Beijer Ref faces zero customer concentration risk and enjoys substantial pricing power
8.
Exclusive supplier partnerships: A central competitive moat is the exclusive distribution rights Beijer Ref holds with premium global manufacturers such as Toshiba, Carrier, and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries in specific regional markets
9.
High switching costs: Fragmentation among installers creates high switching costs, as local technicians rely on Beijer Ref's immediate branch product availability, technical design support, and rapid digital order integration
10.
Active private label roll-out: To enhance gross margins, Beijer Ref actively distributes its proprietary own-branded products, including Sinclair, Inventor, Azuri, and Freddox, across its global branch network
11.
Consolidation platform runway: The global distribution market is exceptionally fragmented, with smaller regional firms generating under USD 500 million representing 68-70% of the massive USD 75 billion North American market

Counter-View

12.
Direct channel threat: Premium equipment manufacturers could attempt to bypass the wholesale distribution channel by selling directly to consolidated mechanical contractors, which would severely compress Beijer Ref's market share and pricing power

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible forward investment case strengthens significantly due to a long runway of accretive M&A and regulatory-driven price increases that support a predictable double-digit EPS expansion

Core Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
Massive US M&A runway: Beijer Ref plans to consolidate the highly fragmented US HVAC distribution market, targeting smaller firms with under USD 500 million in annual sales. This driver touches the net sales and EBITA lines, with a high plausibility of success given that the US pipeline is estimated to be five times larger than EMEA's. The mechanism of failure would be a lack of disciplined target identification, rising multiples for regional distributors, or poor post-acquisition integration
2.
Tightening EU F-gas phase-out: The tightening EU F-gas phase-out regulation is speeding up the replacement cycle for commercial refrigeration equipment, directly boosting Beijer Ref's high-margin proprietary OEM sales. This touches the high-margin OEM revenue line, holding high plausibility as SCM Frigo and Fenagy enter Q2 2026 with record order books. The mechanism of failure would be an increase in illegal black-market F-gas imports into Europe, diluting the pricing premium for natural refrigerant systems
3.
Operational self-help consolidation: The company launched a strategic consolidation program in late 2025 to centralize warehouse and back-office functions in Europe. This driver impacts the SG&A and operating expense lines, carrying a high plausibility of delivering SEK 100 million in run-rate savings by late 1H 2026. It would fail if local branch managers resist centralized systems, leading to severe disruptions in product availability and customer service

Bull Case Conditions and Compound Probability

4.
Independent driver dynamics: For the ultimate bull case to work, Beijer Ref does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously, as the regional M&A pipeline and regulatory-driven pricing operate largely independently across geographical segments
5.
Baseline capital allocations: Instead, the primary requirement is simply that the company maintains its historic M&A discipline, acquiring targets at historical multiples of 7-8x EV/EBITA while keeping leverage at approximately 2.00x Net Debt to EBITDA

Counter-View

6.
Recessionary demand erosion: A deeper US economic recession or sustained high interest rates could depress residential and commercial construction activity, offsetting regulatory pricing tailwinds and leading to negative organic volume growth in the HVAC segment

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk profile appears manageable and temporary, with the most significant short-term bear concerns already well-understood and discounted in the current share price

Bear Risks and Operational Challenges

1.
Short-seller and overhang risks: There is currently no notable published short-seller report on the company, but sentiment remains capped by an ongoing share overhang from EQT Group, which still controls a 19.20% capital stake
2.
US volume slowdown transmission: The most immediate risk transmission mechanism is a slowdown in residential HVAC volumes, where high interest rates and home affordability issues could cause homeowners to defer replacement, hurting EPS
3.
Supply chain and inventory drag: In North America, supplier lead times have historically been well beyond pre-pandemic levels, creating a risk that Beijer Ref must maintain high inventory levels, which would tie up cash and lower conversion
4.
M&A execution failure mode: The ultimate failure mode for the business model is an erosion of M&A pricing discipline, where paying elevated multiples for target companies would compress the return on invested capital and slow the pace of compounding
5.
Key early-warning monitoring signal: The single most important early-warning metric to monitor is monthly residential HVAC shipments reported by the Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute (AHRI), which signals changes in distributor inventories

Counter-View

6.
Aftermarket floor protection: The bear case is highly overstated because Beijer Ref's extensive scale in commercial refrigeration aftermarket services provides a highly stable, cash-generative floor that protects the business during broader economic downturns
