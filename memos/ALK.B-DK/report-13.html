§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: ALK exhibits a split posture — mild sandbagging on revenue (consistent top-line beats) combined with a structural tendency to over-promise on EBIT margin pace, a pattern that is common in commercial-stage biopharma/allergy-pharma [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Revenue guidance consistently set ~2-3% below realised outcome; in L4 periods management has not once missed the revenue guidance range; this is a credible sandbagging signal on top-line [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBIT: EBIT guidance midpoint missed in 2 of 4 periods; the over-promise pattern on EBIT is driven by insufficient provisioning for launch and scale-up costs in the year of guidance issuance [#J]
  • Beat consistency — revenue: 4/4 revenue on-target or beat; this is consistent sandbagging; not statistically unusual (European specialty pharma peers often exhibit similar patterns per [AS-Berenberg] sector note) but meaningful [#A]
  • Miss consistency — EBIT: 2/4 EBIT below midpoint is at the mid-range for European specialty pharma; not egregiously weak but not sandbagging either [#A]
  • Magnitude — miss: The FY2022 -28% EBIT miss is large in absolute terms; misses of this magnitude in specialty pharma typically trigger rating downgrades and are not easily explained away as conservative guidance conservatism [#J]
  • Pattern shift: FY2023-2024 show improved EBIT delivery (on-target and mild miss respectively); management has visibly tightened its cost guidance process post-FY2022; [AS-Carnegie] notes improved guidance granularity since Q2 2023 [AS-Carnegie] [#A]
  • Improving accuracy: The trajectory from FY2022 (large EBIT miss) to FY2023-24 (near-target) is positive; management appears to have internalised the lesson of the FY2022 event [#J]

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: Because EPS guidance is not explicitly issued, EPS beats/misses are second-order outcomes; the L4 implied EPS record is 2 misses / 2 on-target, matching the EBIT pattern [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The apparent sandbagging on revenue may be running out of cushion: FY2025 guidance of 10-15% revenue growth is already a stretch target (DKK 4.5-5.2bn implied range vs DKK 4.71bn in FY2024); the revenue buffer above guidance is narrowing as guidance becomes more ambitious; management cannot sandbag on revenue AND accelerate EBIT simultaneously without execution risk compounding [#J]

  • Historical accuracy: FY2021 shows the pre-investment-phase pattern — mild revenue beat, slight EBIT miss; this is the structural baseline before US commercial acceleration distorted the EBIT guidance framework [#I]
  • Accuracy improving: The post-FY2022 trajectory is clearly improving; management introduced more granular cost guidance at CMD 2023 including explicit CapEx quantum guidance (previously absent); this structural process change is a positive [#J]
  • Volatility driver — execution: FY2022 accuracy failure was execution-driven (US launch cost escalation, SLIT capacity constraints); not primarily macro-driven, which is a more alarming signal than external miss [AS-Nordea] [#A]
  • Volatility driver — complexity: As ALK moves from a primarily European AIT prescription business to a multi-geography SLIT tablet commercial model, the guidance complexity increases; more moving parts = structural reason to expect continued guidance volatility on margins [#I]
  • Metric — best accuracy: Revenue guidance accuracy is ALK's strongest; 4/4 record; consistent with a business where market growth is gradual and AIT prescription volumes are relatively predictable [#J]
  • Metric — worst accuracy: EBIT margin guidance is the weakest metric; cost control on the US commercial build is the primary source of variance [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — caveat: FY2023-24 improvement coincides with a period of reduced US launch spending growth; if US investment accelerates into 2026-27 as the medium-term plan requires, EBIT accuracy may deteriorate again [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The accuracy improvement may be cyclical, not structural: FY2023-24 improved EBIT delivery reflects a period of investment plateau rather than evidence that management has fundamentally solved its cost forecasting problem; the medium-term plan requires investment acceleration, which is precisely when past accuracy broke down [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front verdict: The medium-term guidance rests on three mutually-dependent assumptions (US SLIT volume, operating leverage, mix shift) that must co-deliver; individual assumption sensitivity is tolerable, but simultaneous stress is a material risk [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: US SLIT tablet patient penetration assumed to grow from c.200k active patients (estimated 2023) to c.600-700k by 2027; a 3x volume increase over 4 years; ambitious but not unprecedented in newly-launched immunotherapy markets [#I]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: US SLIT tablet net pricing assumed broadly stable to slightly declining (formulary pressure from pharmacy benefit managers); management does not assume price increases to drive revenue — this is a volume story [AS-Berenberg] [#A]
  • Revenue assumption — market: European AIT market assumed to grow at LSD-MSD (3-7% annually); relatively conservative; European market is mature and volume-driven; this is the low-risk portion of the revenue assumption [#J]
  • Margin assumption — leverage: EBIT margin expansion from ~10% to 15%+ requires DKK 800m+ of incremental revenue to absorb a roughly fixed-cost base of R&D (~DKK 700m annually) and central SG&A; every DKK 1 of incremental revenue above ~DKK 4.5bn yields approximately DKK 0.35-0.40 of EBIT at current mix [#I]
  • Margin assumption — cost: Management assumes R&D costs plateau at ~DKK 700-750m post-2025 (no new major clinical programmes); this is a critical assumption; any pipeline reinvestment would compress the margin expansion path [AS-Carnegie] [#A]
  • Margin assumption — pricing power: European AIT pricing is essentially regulated/reimbursement-linked; no pricing power assumption is embedded; this is conservative and realistic [#J]
  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate ~22-24% assumed; stable Danish corporate tax environment; limited sensitivity unless geographic revenue mix shifts materially toward higher-tax jurisdictions [#I]
  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count stable; buyback programme modest; no material dilution expected; DKK-denominated EPS not exposed to significant share count changes [#I]
  • Macro assumption — currency: USD/DKK is the key FX exposure (US revenue ~20% and growing); a 10% USD depreciation against DKK reduces revenue by ~2% and EBIT by ~3% (US business is higher-margin than group average) [#I]
  • Sensitivity — US volume ±10%: 10% shortfall in US SLIT tablet volume = ~DKK 40-50m revenue impact = ~DKK 25-35m EBIT impact = ~15-20% FY2025 EPS impact at implied EPS of DKK 11-14 [#I]
