EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Azelis operates an asset-light, specialty-focused distribution model spanning 64 countries, serving over 2,800 chemical manufacturers and 65,000 customers through technical formulation support, though near-term earnings remain highly sensitive to macroeconomic pressures and the company faces structural threats from unexclusive Chinese suppliers bypassing Western principals.

Business model and value proposition

Azelis functions as a pure-play specialty chemical and food ingredients distributor, focusing exclusively on highvalue specialties while avoiding low-margin bulk commodities, acting as an outsourced sales and technical arm for over 2,800 specialty chemical manufacturers. The company provides vital formulation support to over 65,000 customers through more than 70 application laboratories globally, creating deep customer stickiness as specialty formulations prove difficult to replicate. The portfolio splits between resilient Life Sciences segments at approximately 63% of sales and Industrial Chemicals at roughly 37% of sales. Revenue generation remains highly transactional with customers demonstrating hand-to-mouth buying patterns and short order-book visibility of only 8 to 10 weeks. Switching costs are highly structural in regulated segments like pharmaceuticals which require FDA validation, and once a specialty chemical is specified into a customer's

formulation, replacing the distributor becomes extremely difficult.

Customer dynamics and switching barriers

Azelis serves a massive global footprint of over 65,000 customers with highly diversified revenue mix where no individual material customer is present and the largest customers typically represent less than 1% to 2% of total group revenue. Azelis primarily focuses on B and C clients representing small-and-medium enterprises, with small customers heavily depending on Azelis for technical support and medium-sized clients utilizing Azelis to navigate complex global regulations. Sourcing specialty ingredients creates deep customer lock-in once qualified into formulations, as product qualification is a long, expensive process requiring extensive regulatory approvals, making customers highly reluctant to switch suppliers once a specific ingredient is validated.

Asset-light distributors pass chemical cost increases to customers faster than manufacturers, with specialized focus protecting gross margins which reached 23.6% in FY25, as specialty chemical costs represent a negligible fraction of total end-product cost.
