Overview of Helios Towers' Value Chain and Business Model

Helios Towers operates as a leading independent telecommunications infrastructure provider, managing a portfolio of nearly 15,000 mobile tower sites across nine high-growth markets in Africa and the Middle East. The core of the company's business model is rooted in its role as a neutral host, where it builds, acquires, and operates passive infrastructure that is shared among multiple Mobile Network Operator tenants. This colocation strategy allows the company to provide essential site selection, structural maintenance, physical security, and power management services. By consolidating these capital-intensive requirements, Helios Towers establishes a highly visible base of earnings that compounds over time as networks expand.

Upstream Value Chain: Sourcing and Infrastructure Development

The upstream landscape for Helios Towers is defined by its extensive procurement operations and heavy reliance on localized contractor networks. The company manages an annual procurement spend exceeding $500 million, which is distributed across more than 1,200 global and regional suppliers. Demonstrating a commitment to local economic integration, approximately 75% of this supply chain expenditure is directed toward local suppliers within its operating jurisdictions.

Essential upstream inputs primarily consist of heavy capital goods, including telecommunications towers, hybrid power units, rectifiers, solar equipment, batteries, and diesel fuel. Beyond physical components, the upstream model is entirely dependent on an indirect workforce of over 10,000 partner personnel. These contracted partners are strictly categorized into specialized build partners for greenfield site construction, maintenance partners to ensure continuous asset integrity, and security partners to protect remote locations from theft or damage.

Midstream Operations: Asset Management and Power Provision

At the midstream level, the business model transitions into the active management and optimization of site real estate and energy systems. Operating telecom infrastructure across Africa and the Middle East requires navigating significant logistical hurdles, particularly regarding energy reliability. Across the company's portfolio, standard electrical grid availability averages only 17 to 18 hours per day, necessitating highly resilient alternative power architectures.

Despite these infrastructure deficits, the company's operational excellence model consistently delivers a 99.99% power uptime guarantee for its tenants. The economics of the midstream segment are driven by high operating leverage, where the majority of tower operating costs are fixed. Adding secondary or tertiary tenants to a single tower yields Adjusted EBITDA margins of approximately 80% on the incremental revenue. This colocation density drastically reduces the carbon emissions and energy use per tenant compared to traditional operator-owned models.

Downstream Dynamics: MNO Relationships and Monetization

The downstream segment of the value chain represents the monetization of the passive infrastructure through long-term commercial leasing.
