SECTION 8: SECTOR AND VALUE CHAIN CONTEXT

European fitness membership reached 75.5m by end-2025, up from 71.4m — a 5.7% increase. Total sector revenue grew 9.1% to €39.1bn. These are sector-level tailwinds.

The low-cost gym model is the fastest-growing segment within the sector. Premium gyms and boutique studios are growing in absolute terms but losing market share to the value operators. Basic-Fit, PureGym, and GYM-GB collectively represent the most structurally advantaged sub-segment.

Cyclicality: the low-cost gym segment has historically demonstrated counter-cyclical tendencies in recessions as consumers trade down from premium to value. The 2022–2023 cost-of-living crisis accelerated BFIT's membership growth as consumers sought cheap health and wellness options. However, severe recessions (unemployment above 8–10%) do reduce new joiner acquisition across all segments.

Rate: **GREEN * — sector fundamentals are strongly supportive.

SECTION 10: SCEPTICAL SUMMARY

The bear case on BFIT's earnings trend: the 2026 EBITDA guide of €415–455m is the widest guidance range of the three peers. The €40m range represents a 10% variance — implying substantial management uncertainty about outcome. The key downside scenario is: Clever Fit integration underperforms (franchisees are harder to manage than owned clubs), France 24/7 pilot progresses slowly (only 50 clubs converted by mid-year), and energy costs spike due to geopolitical risk.

In this scenario, 2026 EBITDA could land at €400–415m — below the bottom of guidance. Combined with elevated refinancing costs from the convertible bond put, reported net profit could disappoint. The stock would re-rate from ~20x forward PE toward 15x on earnings cuts — implying ~€25 per share fair value.

The bull case (Clever Fit delivers, France 24/7 expands, FCF lands at €120m) implies a re-rating toward €40–45 as the FCF yield makes the stock cheap on a cash basis.

The risk is asymmetric: upside is ~40%, downside is ~20–25%. That is a positive R/R, but narrower than the historical move from €19.47 to €34.76 implies.

Early warning signals: (1) H1 2026 EBITDA below €185m (half of low-end guide) would suggest full-year miss; (2) Clever Fit franchise revenue flat/declining in H1 2026; (3) France 24/7 pilot extended beyond 50 clubs without timeline for acceleration; (4) Convertible bond put triggers at scale with refinancing at >4.5% cost.

Rate overall: YELLOW-GREEN (Y/G) — momentum is positive, but the bar is high.
