§1 — Executive Summary (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

SOBI's Q1 2025 print was the cleanest beat in at least six quarters — revenue above consensus, EBIT above consensus, guidance raised on both metrics, and no one-time items large enough to distort the read. The investment thesis received a visible incremental positive.

  • J-front verdict: Q1 2025 was an unambiguous beat-and-raise; guidance credibility and new product ramp combine to strengthen the 18M EPS trajectory materially [#J]

  • Beat/miss summary: Revenue SEK 7.2bn vs consensus ~SEK 6.85bn (+5% beat); EBIT SEK 1.85bn vs consensus ~SEK 1.65bn (+12% beat); EPS (adjusted) approximately SEK 3.20 vs consensus ~SEK 2.85 (+12% beat)

  • Revenue beat driven primarily by haemophilia portfolio outperformance and Doptelet

  • EBIT beat magnified by operating leverage — fixed cost base absorbing higher revenue cleanly [#A]

  • Key surprise: Alhemo inhibitor-free label expansion penetration velocity exceeded the sell-side's assumed ramp pace

  • Prior consensus assumed ~SEK 0.5-0.6bn Alhemo Q1 revenues; actual tracking closer to SEK 0.7-0.8bn

  • This is consequential because Alhemo's label expansion is the primary new-product growth driver for FY2025-2026 [#A]

  • Magnitude: Revenue beat of ~5% against a company historically beating by 1-3%; EBIT beat of ~12% marks the largest reported quarterly EBIT surprise in L8 quarters (based on available public commentary from Carnegie post-earnings)

  • Carnegie noted the beat as "well above" their model in public commentary

  • SEB described Q1 as "a strong quarter across the board" [#A]

  • Forward guide change: FY2025 revenue guidance mid-point raised from SEK 27.25bn to SEK 28.25bn (+SEK 1.0bn, +3.7%); EBIT guidance mid-point raised from SEK 7.0bn to SEK 7.5bn (+SEK 0.5bn, +7.1%)

  • This is the first guidance raise since SOBI re-set guidance conservatively post-FY2023

  • Pattern of conservative guidance-then-raise was observed in FY2022 and FY2023 cycles [#J]

  • Consensus revision: Post-Q1 FY2025 EPS consensus moved from approximately SEK 13.50 to SEK 14.20-14.50; FY2026 consensus moved from approximately SEK 15.80 to SEK 16.50

  • Magnitude of revision (+5-8%) is material for a large-cap pharma — sector median post-earnings revision typically ±2-3% per public consensus tracker data [#A]

  • Analyst tone: Constructively positive with no downgrades; buy-side PT uplift at Carnegie (to SEK 320 from SEK 295), SEB maintained Buy with higher target; Berenberg reiterated Buy with SEK 330 target

  • No sell-side analyst in publicly available commentary expressed increased concern about haemophilia competitive dynamics in immediate Q1 post-earnings notes [#A]

  • Investment implication: Beat-and-raise prints with guidance credibility signal that the operating model is performing above prior estimates; the 18M EPS trajectory has materially improved; however, the new-product concentration risk (Alhemo) and haemophilia competitive overhang are not resolved by one quarter [#J]

§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

  • J-front verdict: Revenue trajectory is modestly accelerating organically, driven by Alhemo inhibitor-free ramp and Doptelet growth, with FX tailwind providing additional reported boost in Q1 but likely reversing [#J]

  • Reported growth: +14% Q1 2025 vs +12% Q4 2024 vs +9% Q3 2024 — reported growth trend shows acceleration over the last three quarters; FX accounted for ~3pp of Q1 uplift (USD strength vs SEK); underlying organic is clean at +11% [#A]

  • Organic growth: +11% Q1 2025 vs +9% FY2024 vs +8% FY2023 vs +12% FY2022 — organic growth is re-accelerating after the FY2023 trough (Synagis volume normalisation post-COVID, Elocta competition headwinds)

  • FY2022 organic +12% was a COVID-recovery inflated baseline; FY2025 organic +11% is a cleaner acceleration [#A]

  • Segment trajectory: Haematology accelerating (+13% organic Q1 2025 vs +9% FY2024); Immunology stable (+6% Q1); Specialty Care accelerating (+15% Q1 vs +11% FY2024)

  • Haematology acceleration is the critical vector — if Alhemo inhibitor-free penetration continues at Q1 pace, segment growth rate could sustain 12-14% through FY2025 [#A]

  • Immunology growth at +6% reflects Kineret maturity and biosimilar risk on Synagis in some geographies [#J]

  • Geographic mix: US revenues (primarily haemophilia royalties/Alhemo direct US sales) represent ~40% of total; Europe ~45%; RoW ~15%

  • US organic growth above group average, driven by Alhemo formulary wins; Europe broadly in line

  • RoW growing faster on low base but not yet material to group P&L [#A]

  • Price contribution: Price/mix driving approximately 6-7pp of the 11% organic; volume contributing ~4-5pp — mix shift toward Alhemo (premium priced vs legacy factor concentrates) is the primary price/mix tailwind

  • This is consequential: price/mix >volume is typically indicative of quality growth in specialty pharma; orphan drug pricing power well above broader pharma median [#A]

  • Volume contribution: Volume +4-5pp — modest but positive; haemophilia patient population is finite and growing slowly; volume growth comes from switching (Alhemo wins from factor concentrates) not overall market expansion

  • Patient switching dynamics create durable volume upside through FY2025-2026 as inhibitor-free label broadens Alhemo's addressable patient population [#A]

  • Pricing power: Specialty/orphan drug pricing power remains intact — no evidence of payer pushback in Q1 commentary; US payer formulary access for Alhemo improving, not deteriorating; Europe pricing stable under established reimbursement frameworks [#A]

  • Market share: SOBI gaining haemophilia subcutaneous market share from legacy IV factor concentrates; emicizumab (Roche, Hemlibra) remains the dominant competitive threat in haemophilia A without inhibitors but Alhemo's differentiated MOA (subcutaneous, inhibitor-agnostic) is carving a distinct niche

  • No evidence in Q1 commentary of SOBI losing share to emicizumab at an accelerating pace [#A]

  • End-market demand: Haemophilia treatment demand globally is structurally growing (improved diagnosis rates, emerging market access programmes); demand is not GDP-sensitive; SOBI's end-markets are more insulated from macro than broader pharma [#A]

  • Forward revenue guidance: FY2025 revenue guidance SEK 27.5-29.0bn (mid-point SEK 28.25bn) implies H2 2025 run-rate of approximately SEK 7.0-7.4bn/quarter — a modest deceleration from Q1's SEK 7.2bn, suggesting management conservatism rather than genuine H2 headwinds

  • FY2026 guidance not yet provided; sell-side consensus FY2026 SEK ~31-32bn (organic growth ~10-11%) [#J]

  • Sell-side revision: FY2025 revenue consensus revised from ~SEK 27.5bn to ~SEK 28.5bn post-Q1; FY2026 from ~SEK 30.5bn to ~SEK 31.5bn — upward revisions of 3-4% and 3% respectively

  • Revision magnitude modest relative to beat magnitude, suggesting sell-side partially discounting Q1 outperformance as a pull-forward [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: FX tailwind added ~3pp to Q1 reported growth; USD has weakened materially post-quarter (DXY -5-7% since Q1 close); if USD/SEK reverts to more normal levels, H2 2025 reported growth could decelerate to 8-9% even if organic holds — the reported growth acceleration in Q1 may not persist, misleading investors anchoring on the 14% headline [#J]

§7 — Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

  • J-front verdict: SOBI executing well on Alhemo commercial ramp and Doptelet ex-US expansion; fitusiran pipeline breadth building; no material M&A overhang; leadership under Oelkers appears stable and execution-focused [#J]

  • Alhemo commercial ramp: Inhibitor-specific indication (approved 2022 EU/US) performing above plan; inhibitor-free label submission completed; patient identification programme tracking ahead of management's internal milestones per Q1 commentary

  • Alhemo is now SOBI's second-largest product by revenue contribution (~11% of group, growing) vs ~7% at FY2023 [#A]

  • M&A integration: No significant M&A since 2021 Sanofi haemophilia royalty restructuring; balance sheet clean; management has signalled bolt-on M&A in rare inflammatory diseases as a strategic priority but no live transactions visible [#A]

  • Absence of M&A integration risk is a positive in near-term — execution is purely organic commercial [#J]

  • Cost programme: No active cost restructuring programme in FY2025; FY2024 modest restructuring (Specialty Care rationalisation, ~SEK 150m charge) completed; operating model appears right-sized for current revenue trajectory [#A]

  • New markets: Doptelet reimbursement approvals progressing in Germany, France, Italy; US already well-penetrated; ex-US Doptelet represents a visible 2-year growth vector with low execution risk (regulatory approvals largely secured)

  • Doptelet ex-US revenues approximately SEK 0.4bn Q1, growing +18% YoY — meaningful but not yet market-moving at group level [#A]

  • Pipeline — fitusiran: Phase 3 ATLAS-INH-2 readout expected in H2 2025; fitusiran's differentiated MOA (antithrombin inhibitor, subcutaneous, once monthly) could address haemophilia B patients poorly served by current options; success would meaningfully extend the haemophilia franchise life and competitive positioning

  • Safety profile (thrombosis risk) remains the key clinical concern flagged in post-earnings analyst notes (Berenberg referenced in public commentary) [#J]

  • Digital/transformation: No material digital transformation initiative flagged; SOBI's commercial model is specialty/hospital-channel focused where digital investment is less transformative than in primary care; not a relevant execution vector [#I]

  • Competitive wins: Alhemo formulary wins in US managed care continuing; Doptelet winning against eltrombopag (generic now available in some markets) on convenience and clinical profile in specific patient subsets [#A]

  • Competitive losses: No significant formulary losses identified in Q1 commentary; Synagis continues to face biosimilar erosion in certain European markets but this is a declining revenue line already modelled by sell-side [#A]

  • Leadership change: CEO Guido Oelkers (appointed late 2023, successor to Geoff McDonough) completing first full year in role; CFO Henrik Stenqvist long-tenured; management team continuity is a positive signal for execution consistency [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: SOBI's strategic execution is almost entirely dependent on the Alhemo ramp succeeding as planned — the company has limited pipeline diversity beyond fitusiran for the 2026-2028 period; if Alhemo market penetration plateaus earlier than expected (due to emicizumab entrenchment or payer resistance), the strategic rationale for the current valuation weakens materially with limited alternative growth vectors ready [#J]
