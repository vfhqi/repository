Section summary

2.
Sugar tax changes: Evolving soft drink industry levy thresholds reducing to 4.5g of sugar per 100ml in January 2028 require significant reformulation work
3.
Packaging cost inflation: Elevated Extended Producer Responsibility fees starting in late 2025 create a c. £2.00m to £3.00m headwind on profit, which must be offset by manufacturing efficiencies
4.
Short-seller positioning: Research did not surface any public short-seller reports or unusually high short interest, reflecting the company's stable defensive positioning

Early-Warning Metrics

5.
Watch factor: Crucial early-warning indicator to monitor is volume growth in core grocery channels, which would signal if pricing actions are causing lasting brand de-valuation
6.
Sweetener backlash: False-friend risk would be a positive-looking revenue growth signal that masks an underlying consumer rejection of enhanced artificial sweeteners
7.
Other side of the story: Robust defensive characteristics are highly understated because consumer staples historically demonstrate immense volume resilience during weak economic periods as affordable treats
