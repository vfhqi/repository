Brief company descriptions

Kitron ASA is a leading Scandinavian Electronics Manufacturing Services provider that delivers complex electronic solutions and complete end products to international markets. 1 • ARS

The company's business model encompasses the entire product lifecycle, ranging from development, design, and industrialization to sourcing, manufacturing, and aftermarket redesign or repair services. 2 • ARS

Kitron manufactures electronics embedded within customers' proprietary products as well as fully box-built electronic systems and high-level electromechanical assemblies. 3 • ARS

Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) partner with Kitron to increase their manufacturing flexibility, reduce operational costs, and improve overall product quality while allowing them to focus on core competencies. 4 • ARS

The company has successfully built a global footprint across 11 countries, maintaining operations in Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Lithuania, Germany, Poland, the Czech Republic, India, China, Malaysia, and the United States. 5 • ARS

The group employs over 3,000 full-time workers, including more than 300 engineers, and manages over 110,000 square meters of manufacturing space equipped with over 35 surface-mount technology lines. 6 • ARS

Kitron utilizes a "One Kitron" operational model, maintaining common IT platforms, processes, and equipment strategies across all sites to enable rapid scaling and borderless workload transfers. 7 • Transcript - Analyst Transcript/Investor Day

While Kitron formally reports as a single EMS segment, the company derives its revenue from five distinct market sectors: Defence/Aerospace, Electrification, Industry, Medical Devices, and Connectivity. 8 • ARS

Defence and Aerospace currently represents Kitron's primary growth engine, producing mission-critical systems for surveillance, avionics, secure communications, and advanced unmanned aerial vehicles. 9 • Transcript - Earnings Calls

The Electrification business line capitalizes on global green energy trends and massive data center expansions, supplying components for power grid transmission, battery management, and charging technologies. 10 • Transcript - Earnings Calls

The Industry sector serves professional customers with control systems, electronic control units, and automation solutions for robotics, infrastructure, and autonomous systems.

Within the Medical Devices sector, Kitron specializes in high-complexity, highly regulated equipment such as ultrasound systems, life support machines, and in-vitro diagnostics. 11 • ARS

The Connectivity segment focuses on the structural growth of the Internet of Things, providing connected sensors, optical networks, and intelligent asset tracking devices. 12 • Transcript - Earnings Calls

Kitron's underlying revenue model is characterized by long-term, repeat relationships with blue-chip OEMs that demand high-complexity, low-to-medium volume manufacturing capabilities. 13 • Transcript - Analyst Transcript/Investor Day

Over the past three years, Kitron has strategically transformed its portfolio to focus heavily on structurally supported markets, moving away from consumer-driven volatility toward sectors demanding rigorous traceability and reliability. 14 • ARS

Kitron achieved record financial performance in 2025, generating EUR 738.3 million in revenue and approaching its long-term corporate EBIT margin target of 9 percent. 15 • INTRM

The company has actively utilized targeted acquisitions to expand its geographic footprint and technical capabilities over the last three years, notably integrating Denmark's BB Electronics and acquiring Sweden's defence-focused DeltaNordic. 16 • ARS

Looking forward to the next three years, Kitron has set an ambitious medium-term target to essentially double its size, aiming to reach EUR 1.5 billion in annual revenue. 17 • Transcript - Analyst Transcript/Investor Day

Future growth over the next 36 months is expected to be heavily fueled by a European defence investment "supercycle," driven by geopolitical instability and customer requirements for regional, sovereign manufacturing capabilities. 18 • Transcript - Analyst Transcript/Investor Day

Alongside top-line expansion, management intends to leverage economies of scale to maintain an EBIT margin above 9 percent and a return on operating capital exceeding 25 percent over the next three years. 19 • Transcript - Analyst Transcript/Investor Day
