Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Passive tower infrastructure operator. Inwit owns and operates the leading passive mobile-tower network in Italy, with a 45% market share against closest peer Cellnex's roughly 40%, per Morgan Stanley
2.
Revenue model. The business rents tower space to mobile network operators under long-dated Master Service Agreements (MSAs) that Inwit maintains are irrevocably extended to 2038 following a 2022 change-of-control clause
3.
Anchor tenant concentration. TIM and Vodafone/Fastweb together supply approximately 80% of group revenues, a structural concentration now central to the investment case following both anchors' 2026 termination notices
4.
Financial character. First-quarter 2026 EBITDAaL was EUR 189.9 million, down 2.2% year-on-year, on an EBITDAaL margin of 71.9%, down from 72.9% a year earlier
4.1
Cash generation: Recurring free cash flow reached EUR 176.2 million, ahead of the EUR 157.4 million consensus
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt/EBITDAaL runs at 5.20x, inside management's stated 5.0x-6.0x target range and comfortably below Cellnex's 6.20x, though above Helios Towers' 3.4x
6.
Tenancy density. The tenancy ratio reached a record 2.39x in the first quarter of 2026, ahead of Cellnex's 2.25x per the same source, reflecting the density advantage of operating in a single, mature Italian market
7.
Self-help margin lever. Inwit is buying the freehold land under its towers, with ownership at 18.3% of the base in the first quarter of 2026 against a 2030 target above 30%, structurally offsetting rental-cost inflation
8.
Growth mix shift. Forward growth increasingly depends on indoor distributed antenna systems (DAS) and small-cell densification (around 850 dedicated DAS locations) rather than new macro-site tenancies, as roughly 80% of mobile data traffic now occurs indoors
9.
Sponsor ownership. No activist investor is present; instead infrastructure sponsors Ardian (31%) and Oak Holdings (37.6%) dominate the shareholder register, providing both a valuation floor and private take-private optionality
10.
Investment-style read. This is best read as a defensive, inflation-linked infrastructure compounder under acute, event-driven stress, not a cyclical name, and not (yet) a clean special situation, because the outcome of live litigation is unresolved
