Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Implenia has built a high-quality integrated model combining specialized infrastructure execution, premium building construction, and a market-leading recurring property services platform.

Division and Segment Economics

1.
Consolidated Buildings division: Comprising real estate development and building construction in Switzerland and Germany, this division generated CHF 1.63 billion in revenue and CHF 75.60 million in EBIT for 2025
2.
Masterful Civil Engineering: Focusing on tunnelling, bridges, and special foundations, this segment generated CHF 1.93 billion in revenue and CHF 55.00 million in EBIT during 2025

Revenue Mix and Contract Structures

3.
Growing property services: Through Wincasa, Implenia manages CHF 84.00 billion in assets under management, producing stable, counter-cyclic recurring fees that insulate the group from construction cyclicality
4.
Shifting contract frameworks: Implenia is systematically shifting away from bilateral, claim-driven contracts toward collaborative models such as Design & Build and Alliance (IPA/IPD) frameworks
5.
Shared risk models: These collaborative multi-party contracts replace adversarial relationships with shared risk-and-reward mechanisms, de-risking project execution but placing a cap on maximum contract margins

Competitive Advantages and Value Chain Positioning

6.
Dominant data center position: In Switzerland, Implenia holds a commanding 75.00% market share in core & shell execution for data centers, driven by its technical mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) capabilities
7.
Technical tunnelling mastery: The group is a preferred contractor for high-complexity alpine and urban transit tunnelling across Europe, leveraging proprietary tunnel boring machine (TBM) and drill-and-blast capabilities

Customer Concentration and Industry Dynamics

8.
Highly balanced backlog: Implenia's order book is well-balanced, with public sector clients comprising 75.00% of the backlog, and private clients accounting for 25.00%
9.
Fragmented regional structures: European construction markets remain highly fragmented by nature, with local scale and specialized technical credentials acting as natural barriers to entry
10.
Other side of the story: Collaborative contract models limit the potential to extract super-normal margins during periods of high efficiency, as cost savings must be shared with the client
