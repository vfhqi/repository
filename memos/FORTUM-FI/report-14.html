§2 — Key Driver 1: Nordic Hydro + Nuclear Power Margin

The most consequential and most volatile single driver in the Fortum investment case. Bull case and bear case diverge most sharply here.

  • J-front verdict: Medium plausibility — structural Nordic power economics are constructive long-term, but 2026-27 price mean-reversion risk and hedge book rollover are active threats to 18-24M EPS trajectory [#J]

Driver definition:
- Driver definition: EBIT contribution from Nordic hydro (~10-12 TWh annual) and Loviisa nuclear plant (~8-9 TWh annual) via realised power price per MWh achieved after hedging [#A]
- Magnitude required: Bull case likely assumes Nordic spot/realised power price stabilises ≥€55-65/MWh in 2025-26; each €10/MWh move ≈ €180-200m EBIT impact across the ~18-20 TWh combined output [#A]
- Baseline — current state: Nordic system price in H1 2025 has averaged €40-55/MWh range (volatile); 2024 full-year average was weaker than 2022-23 due to hydro reservoir normalisation; Fortum realises hedged prices above spot when hedges were set during 2022-23 high-price environment [#A]
- Target state — required: Normalised EBIT from Generation segment ≥€700-800m to support 25% IRR thesis; implies realised price ≥€55/MWh sustained over 12-18 months [#A]
- Required trajectory: Stabilisation at current hedge book levels through 2025 harvest; no cliff-edge reset as 2025-vintage hedges roll to 2026-27 rehedging at current lower forward curve levels [#A]

Bull case:
- Bull — historical: Nordic system price averaged €70-90/MWh through 2022-23 energy crisis; Fortum locked portions of output at these levels, supporting elevated 2023-24 Generation EBIT vs pre-crisis norm [#A]
- Bull — recent: Finnish/Nordic power prices bottomed in late 2023 (wet hydro year + mild winter); partial recovery in H1 2025 suggests normalisation toward €50-65/MWh range is underway [#A]
- Bull — structural: Nordic electricity demand structurally growing from data centres, EV penetration, green hydrogen; IEA projects Nordic power demand growth 2-3% annually through 2030, vs. historical flat/slight decline [#A]
- Bull — nuclear: Loviisa nuclear provides ~8 TWh of low-marginal-cost baseload; capacity factors typically >90%; nuclear economics superior to gas-indexed peers in current EU ETS environment [#A]
- Bull — hydro: Scandinavia hydro reservoirs fluctuate but mean-revert; 2024 wet year creates base effect tailwind for 2025 generation volumes [#A]
- Bull — mgmt: Fortum management has consistently articulated 70-80% hedge ratios 12M forward; if 2025 hedges were placed in 2023-24 when prices were above €60/MWh, 2025 realised price is partially protected [#A]
- Bull — SS: SEB and Nordea equity research (publicly available summaries) argue Fortum's Generation segment can sustain €600-750m EBIT at normalised prices; implies significant EPS re-rating if balance sheet concurrent [#A]

Bear case:
- ❌ Bear — power repricing: 2026 Nordic forward prices (as of early 2025) are ~€35-45/MWh; as 2025 high-vintage hedges mature, 2026-27 hedges reset at materially lower prices, compressing Generation EBIT by potentially €200-300m vs peak [#A]
- Bear — hydrology: Exceptionally wet Nordic seasons (as in 2023-24) pressure spot prices down; hydro production is weather-dependent in 10-15% range year-to-year [#A]
- Bear — German RE displacement: Rapid German and Danish wind/solar capacity additions displace Nordic interconnected prices downward during off-peak periods; structural price deflation risk in shoulder months [#A]
- Bear — hedge opacity: Fortum discloses hedge ratios but not absolute hedge prices in full detail; actual 2026 realised price is not publicly verifiable, creating an evidence gap [#A]
- Bear — SS skeptic: Barclays and Berenberg (public commentary) flag that Fortum's peak-cycle hedge book advantage dissipates progressively through 2025-26; normalised EBIT guidance implies a step-down [#A]
- Bear — market structure: Nordic power market is increasingly interconnected with Central Europe; episodes of negative spot pricing in Germany transmit to Nordic system via interconnectors, compressing peak-hour rents [#A]
- Bear — macro: European industrial demand remains weak (2024-25 recessionary conditions); reduced industrial load reduces Nordic baseload price floor [#A]

Forward trajectory:
- Quarterly trajectory: 2025 Generation EBIT likely near-peak (high vintage hedges); H2 2025 / FY2026 the critical transition point as next hedge vintage crystallises [#A]
- Acceleration needed: No acceleration needed for 2025; the risk is deceleration in 2026; bull case requires 2026 hedge prices ≥€50/MWh to avoid material EPS compression [#A]
- Key dependency: 2025-26 rehedging price and hedge ratio disclosure; Nordic demand trajectory from electrification; German/Nordic interconnection flows [#A]
- Downside scenario: If 2026 realised price falls to €40/MWh (vs €60/MWh base case), Generation EBIT falls ~€300-400m; EPS impact ≈ €0.20-0.30/share on €0.80-1.00 normalised base — devastating to thesis [#A]
