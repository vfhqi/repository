§6 — Margin + Profitability Outlook

  • J-front verdict: Margin recovery is the entire investment thesis; consensus assumes EBIT margin of 3-4% in FY2025 rising to 8-10% by FY2026-2027 — a near-tripling that requires clean execution with no further cost surprises [#J]

  • Consensus EBIT margin: FY2025E ~3.5-4.5%; FY2026E ~7-9%; FY2027E ~9-11% [#A]

  • Sub: Pre-2022 Vestas normalised EBIT margin was ~8-10%; consensus recovery target simply restores to prior normal, not a step-change

  • Consensus gross margin: FY2025E ~13-15% (Turbines segment dragging); FY2026E ~17-19%; implied gross margin recovery driven by new-vintage project delivery [#A]

  • Consensus net margin: FY2025E ~1-2%; FY2026E ~4-6%; FY2027E ~6-8% [#A]

  • Margin trend assumed: Sustained expansion over 3Y horizon — every Buy analyst models a linear or near-linear improvement; no analyst models a "false start" where FY2026 disappoints before FY2027 recovers [#A]

  • Productivity assumption: Consensus embeds ~2-3% annual manufacturing cost reduction from Vestas's efficiency programme ("Profitable Growth" strategy); this is management-guided and broadly accepted [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Barclays] explicitly doubts this assumption, citing supply chain complexity and steel/logistics cost stickiness

  • Operating leverage: Bulls model positive operating leverage with ~60-70% incremental EBIT margin on revenue above €18bn threshold [#A]

  • Sub: Operating leverage assumption requires stable volume — any project cancellation or delay breaks the leverage calculation

  • Fixed cost trajectory: Consensus assumes fixed cost as % of revenue declines from ~12-13% (FY2024 actual) to ~9-10% by FY2027 [#A]

  • One-off vs. structural: Consensus treats the FY2022-2024 margin compression as one-off (supply chain shock, low-margin order vintage) and FY2025+ recovery as structural normalisation [#J]

  • Sub: [AS-Société Générale] and [AS-Barclays] dispute this framing — they argue that structural cost inflation in nacelles and blades is permanent, not cyclical

  • Margin upgrade: [AS-Goldman Sachs] upgraded FY2026 EBIT margin assumption from 7% to 8.5% in February 2026, the only material upward revision in L3M [#A]

  • Margin downgrade: Broad-based downgrade L12M — approximately 12-15 analysts reduced FY2025 EBIT margin assumptions; average cut of ~100-150bps on FY2025 margin [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The consensus margin recovery model is entirely dependent on no further raw material cost spikes and no project penalty clauses on legacy orders — both have materialised in the prior 24 months; the probability of another adverse cost event is not zero and is arguably higher than consensus is pricing [#J]

§7 — Narrative + Market Story

  • J-front verdict: Bull narrative is coherent and cycle-anchored; bear narrative is equally specific; this is an unusually high-quality narrative disagreement — not just valuation noise [#J]

  • Bull case: Vestas is exiting the worst industrial trough in its history, caused by simultaneous supply chain inflation, low-margin contract vintage, and interest rate headwinds — all of which are now reversing or have reversed; the order book is refilling at higher ASPs and the Service business provides a structural earnings floor [#J]

  • Sub: [AS-Bernstein], [AS-Goldman Sachs], [AS-DNB] most articulate bull-case advocates

  • Sub: Bull case does not require market share gains or new products — simply normalisation of margins

  • Bear case: Legacy cost inflation is partially structural; Siemens Gamesa's operational recovery will increase competitive intensity in Europe; Chinese OEMs (Goldwind, CSSC, Envision) are gaining traction in non-EU markets and could suppress global pricing; FCF generation will disappoint relative to EBIT recovery due to working capital buildup [#J]

  • Sub: [AS-Barclays], [AS-JPMorgan], [AS-SocGen] are the most articulate bear-case advocates

  • Bullish theme 1: Order backlog quality improvement — new orders post-2022 carry meaningfully higher ASPs and Vestas has publicly disclosed improved contract terms [#A]

  • Bullish theme 2: Service segment earnings floor — Service is ~30% of revenue, ~50-55% of EBIT at normalised margins; multiple analysts call this a "private-equity-quality cashflow stream" if separated from Turbines [#A]

  • Bullish theme 3: European policy tailwinds — REPowerEU, German onshore permitting reform, UK offshore expansion — all cited across >10 analyst notes in L12M [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 1: FCF vs. EBIT divergence — working capital requirements for manufacturing ramp are absorbing cash; FCF generation lagging EBIT recovery is a recurring theme in Hold-analyst notes [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 2: Competitive re-entry — Siemens Gamesa's internal restructuring appears to be stabilising; a recovered SGRE would remove a key tailwind (competitor weakness) from the bull case [#A]

  • Sceptical theme 3: US policy risk — IRA rollback or tariff escalation could suppress US demand, reducing the order intake outlook and potentially requiring further estimate revisions [#A]

  • Narrative shift L12M: From "will Vestas survive the cost crisis?" (2023-early 2024 tone) to "when does normalisation arrive?" (mid-2024 to present); subtle but important shift — the existential framing is gone, replaced by a timing debate [#J]

  • Tone shift: More constructive across the pool in Q1 2026 vs. H2 2024 — but constructive does not equal conviction; management must deliver H1 2026 [#A]

  • Near-term focus: H1 2026 order intake and FY2025 EBIT delivery vs. guidance; the short-term catalyst is the Q2 2026 interim results [#A]

  • Medium-term focus: FY2026 EBIT margin confirmation — is 8-10% achievable or is the ceiling 6-7%? [#A]

  • Original insight: [AS-Bernstein]'s energy security demand argument (European governments paying a strategic premium for onshore/offshore wind capacity) is the most differentiated bull thesis — not purely margin normalisation [#A]

  • Sub: [AS-Barclays]'s structural cost argument is the most differentiated bear thesis — explicitly not a consensus view

  • Management echo: ~60% of Buy-analyst narrative language directly mirrors management CMD language from November 2024; a herding signal suggesting analysts are largely re-packaging management slides [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The dominant bull narrative ("normalisation of margins") is not a growth story — it is a recovery story; a recovery story requires management to do less wrong, not more right; but Vestas has a documented track record of underdelivering on margin guidance in each of L4 reporting periods; the narrative coherence does not compensate for the execution record [#J]
