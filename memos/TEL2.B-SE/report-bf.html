EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 23 findings

Section summary
  1. Tele2 operates an integrated connectivity model across Sweden and the Baltics, focusing on high-margin mobile and fixed convergence.
  2. The financial model features highly predictable recurring service revenues amplified by strong scale economies and high operating leverage.
  3. Recent strategic actions have cannibalised legacy business segments while driving a 15% workforce reduction and an abrupt CEO transition.

Within the telecom value chain, Tele2 occupies the connectivity and transport stage alongside retail services delivery, capturing high margin pools through regional retail brand dominance and convergent bundles that lower customer churn. The Swedish fixed broadband market is highly concentrated, with Telia commanding 48.00% share, followed by Tele2 at 23.50%, while the mobile market is structured as a tight four-player oligopoly where Telia holds 32.70%, Tele2 holds 27.50%, Telenor holds 19.80%, and Three Scandinavia holds 19.80%. Independent Opensignal measurements from December 2025 named Tele2 Sweden's fastest 5G download network, sharing the top spot in seven other categories including best 5G video experience.

Competitively, Tele2 maintains a stable market position but faces intensive commercial rivalry and persistent open-access fiber price squeezes in Sweden. Three Scandinavia historically operates as the primary market disruptor in Sweden, maintaining aggressive pricing tactics that pressure mobile voice and data average revenues per user. Tele2 is actively avoiding certain open fiber networks due to rising, unmotivated access fees, where local intermediary operators permanently increase wholesale prices that squeeze retail provider margins. The Swedish Post and Telecom Authority proposed a new framework to regulate local villa fiber monopolies, though PTS has delayed final decision enforcement regarding wholesale local access to fiber networks until late 2026.

On the customer side, implementing fixed-mobile convergence packages reduces customer churn by approximately 30%, while direct sales channels now account for approximately two-thirds of total group transactions following aggressive restructuring to reduce third-party dealer reliance. Price adjustments successfully offset inflationary pressures across Sweden and the Baltic countries, with Baltic repricing driving Latvia and Lithuania first-quarter 2026 service revenues up 7%. Tele2 relies heavily on a highly concentrated pool of upstream technology vendors for key network infrastructure, with active mobile radio network rollouts balanced primarily between market leaders Ericsson and Nokia.

Looking forward, new entrants face massive capital requirements to secure multi-decade radio frequency spectrum licenses in Sweden, while bypassing the Net4Mobility joint network requires astronomical physical replication costs to match modern mobile coverage standards. In late 2025, Tele2 launched a global satellite IoT service to close terrestrial network coverage gaps, making Tele2 the first Swedish operator to market a commercial satellite IoT solution. Modern consumer data demand acts as a highly defensive, non-cyclical utility, with the Swedish telecommunications market expanding at a low-to-mid single digit rate, positioning Tele2 as a premium defensive yield play aligned with current public market regimes favoring non-cyclical assets carrying high cash generation.

The primary foundation risk centers on aggressive workforce cuts and sudden executive turnover potentially impairing long-term network operational execution and consumer relationship stability under new CEO Nicholas Högberg, compounded by sustained regulatory delays regarding local Swedish fiber monopolies leaving Tele2 exposed to ongoing wholesale margin squeezes from municipal operators.
