Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Heijmans presents a highly predictable Dutch construction and infrastructure play with robust double-digit earnings growth and clear valuation re-rating potential over the next 18 months.
1.
Integrated revenue model: Heijmans generates revenues and high-quality margins by operating as a fully integrated builder and developer across Living (residential), Working (non-residential), and Connecting (infrastructure) segments
2.
Lucrative investment case: The potential thesis relies on utilizing its expanded land bank to address severe housing shortages while capturing high-margin public utility and defense infrastructure contracts
3.
Principal swing factor: The primary swing factor for intermediate earnings is the rate of municipal permit issuance, which directly regulates high-margin modular housing delivery volumes
4.
Downside macro threats: High macro interest rates represent the most significant risk, risking potential land impairments and depressing consumer housing demand
5.
Re-rating investment verdict: A deeper research process is highly warranted because Heijmans' conservative risk management and upgraded targets suggest the market is materially underpricing its mid-term earnings power

Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Heijmans operates a resilient, risk-managed construction and infrastructure business model focused on high-margin energy transition, residential housing, and recurring public maintenance contracts in the Netherlands.
  2. The group leverages standardisation and modular building concepts to offset local labour shortages while securing high-value project orchestrator roles with sovereign public entities.

Divisional Segment Breakdown and Operating Economics

1.
Core residential segment: The Living division develops and constructs high-quality residential properties across the Netherlands, utilizing standardisation to build modular, ground-bound homes
2.
Technical services expansion: The Working division delivers complex technical services and sustainability retrofits for non-residential buildings, capitalizing on growing public and private decarbonization demands
3.
Critical civil infrastructure: The Connecting division designs, builds, and maintains Dutch highways, bridges, waterways, and energy networks, shifting its focus from new-builds to long-term asset management. This is the fastest-growing division in public-sector partnerships
4.
Predictable recurring revenue: Contracts in maintenance and technical management represent 35% of group revenues, providing a stable financial foundation from long-term client relationships
5.
Modular housing assets: Heijmans operates a state-of-the-art timber-frame factory in Heerenveen under the Horizon brand, producing scalable 2D and 3D residential building modules
6.
Selective resources allocation: Operational resources are strategically steered away from low-margin transactional tenders toward high-investment sectors like regional grid operators, defense, and airport infrastructure

Value Chain Moats and Competitive Advantage

7.
Premium orchestrator position: Sitting at the top of the value chain, Heijmans acts as a knowledge partner from initial engineering to final realization and maintenance
8.
Strong customer retention: Multi-year infrastructure maintenance and technical installation management agreements create substantial switching costs for public municipalities and private airport operators
9.
Specialized technical expertise: Pricing power is strongly supported by Heijmans employing highly specialized crews, particularly certified workers capable of executing complex high-voltage grid upgrades
10.
Strategic land holdings: The acquisition of developer Van Wanrooij has significantly enlarged the company's land bank, securing long-term suburban construction rights in high-demand regions
11.
Consolidated peer landscape: The Dutch construction sector is highly consolidated, with Heijmans and Royal BAM Group competing as the two dominant integrated civil engineering champions
12.
Intensive pricing pressure: Other side of the story: Extreme cost competition in public procurement tenders can occasionally force margin compression if peers aggressively bid to fill their capacity

  2. The company's historical shift toward margin over volume is proven by its ability to beat medium-term EBITDA targets while maintaining a robust, highly liquid solvency profile.

Revenue and Profitability Trajectory

1.
Revenue expansion: Revenue rose 7.28% in 2025 to €2.77bn, with management guiding for €3.10bn in 2026, representing an 11.83% year-on-year increase
2.
Strategic M&A impact: The guided 2026 revenue of €3.10bn includes the full consolidation of Hegeman Group, which is modeled to contribute €140 million in technical service turnover
3.
Acquired margin dilution: Bolt-on acquisitions like Hegeman initially operate at dilutive 5% to 6% EBITDA margins before being integrated into Heijmans' higher-margin project management blueprints

Capital Efficiency and Liquidity Profile

4.
Strong returns: Normalised ROE reached 25.70% in 2025 and is projected to expand to 27.60% in 2027
5.
Robust cash generation: Free cash flow remained highly positive at €110.40m in 2025, supported by disciplined capital expenditure and strong working capital management
6.
Zero leverage net cash: Ending 2025 with a net cash position of €58 million, Heijmans retains a clean balance sheet with zero leverage risk
7.
High solvency: The company's solvency ratio reached 32.90% at the end of 2025, comfortably exceeding minimum banking covenant requirements
8.
Volatile cash conversion: Other side of the story: Cash conversion could sharply deteriorate if a sudden cyclical freeze in home sales increases unsold residential inventory, locking up working capital

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The possible investment case strengthens significantly based on a structural shift toward high-margin technical public services and Heijmans' massive standardized modular housing capacity.
  2. Suburban housing demand and long-term public energy grid upgrades act as independent, highly visible growth engines that secure robust mid-term earnings power.

Primary Drivers of Forward Earnings

1.
High-voltage grid demand: Driver 1 is the multi-decade investment cycle of Dutch regional grid operators, who are contracting Heijmans to construct and reinforce high-voltage substations
2.
Plausibility of grid upgrade: The plausibility of this driver is high because severe electricity grid congestion is actively delaying both the energy transition and commercial construction
3.
Grid execution risks: This driver would fail if public grid operators delay budget approvals or if persistent local labor shortages prevent contract execution
4.
Standardised timber construction: Driver 2 is the rapid deployment of standardized, low-emissions timber-frame modular housing, which bypasses onsite labor shortages
5.
Plausibility of modular demand: Plausibility is high due to a political consensus to build 100,000 new homes annually to solve a 400,000-unit housing shortage
6.
Permit bottleneck risk: This driver would fail if municipal permitting delays and nitrogen restrictions continue to prevent spatial developments

Public Infrastructure and Valuation Uplift

7.
Defense sector expansion: Driver 3 is the Dutch Ministry of Defense's massive spending cycle, which has already awarded Heijmans a €177 million contract for technical infrastructure
8.
Plausibility of defense spend: Plausibility is high because national security mandates have forced the Dutch government to rapidly upgrade its military real estate assets
9.
Diversified driver independence: These growth drivers are highly independent, meaning that a slowdown in cyclical residential sales would not affect long-term government defense and utility contracts, acting as non-cyclical hedges against any residential volatility
10.
Consumer confidence threat: Other side of the story: If high global inflation forces mortgage rates back up, consumer home-buying confidence will collapse, stalling private development margins

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture is heavily dominated by regulatory bottlenecks in nitrogen permits and grid congestion, although Heijmans' low-risk project portfolio and robust order book offer solid mitigation.

Regulatory and Project Execution Risks

1.
Nitrogen permit bottlenecks: The chief regulatory hurdle is the ongoing nitrogen debate, requiring strict nature permits for many new residential and civil projects
2.
Administrative delay mechanism: Delays in obtaining these permits directly push out project start dates, causing short-term revenue slippage and increasing idle labor costs
3.
Electricity grid constraints: Increasing electrification in mobility and industry has led to massive grid congestion, preventing new buildings from securing energy connections
4.
Labor supply disruption: Stricter rules on freelance employee contracts have restricted crane and lifting operator availability, creating labor shortages in civil infrastructure
5.
Single point failure: The single failure mode that would most damage the investment case is a major design or pricing error on a massive, complex fixed-price civil contract

Monitoring Indicators and Mitigations

6.
Unreliable order backlog: A rising order backlog, which reached €3.70bn in 2025, is a "false friend" if contracts lack strong inflation indexation protections
7.
Primary monitoring indicator: Investors must monitor monthly housing transactions, as a decline below 3,000 units would signal a severe suburban market slowdown
8.
Revamped tender procedures: Other side of the story: The risk of catastrophic contract pricing failures is highly overstated because Heijmans has structurally shifted its tender culture toward cash flow and risk mitigation

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Heijmans strongly warrants deeper work, given its unique combination of predictable public-sector demand, modular residential capability, net cash balance sheet, and highly attractive valuation metrics.
1.
High-margin public demand: The most compelling investment argument is the non-cyclical, high-margin public demand in grid reinforcement, defense, and infrastructure asset management, securing predictable public-sector demand
2.
Permitting spatial blockers: Other side of the story: The strongest counter-argument is that severe municipal permit delays and grid congestion could permanently block modular housing volumes, capping near-term EPS growth
3.
Backlog margin audit: Audit the specific margin and indexation terms within the Connect segment's €3.70bn backlog to ensure complete protection against cost inflation
4.
Spatial analysis verification: Investigate local municipal permitting delays in Utrecht and Brabant to model the exact release schedule of Van Wanrooij's suburban development plots
