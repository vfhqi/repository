Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Systemair holds a leading European market position in commercial ventilation, leveraging its global factory platform and decentralized sales.

Segment and Product Dynamics

1.
Comprehensive HVAC portfolio: Systemair designs and produces a highly diversified range of ventilation products, specializing in modular air handling units (AHUs), high-performance fans, and air distribution systems. These systems are designed to provide healthy, climate-controlled, and energy-efficient air in public and private spaces. The company's brand portfolio includes the core Systemair brand, Frico for heating, Fantech for North American residential, and Menerga for precision pool ventilation
2.
Commercial end-market focus: The largest end-market for Systemair's products is commercial buildings, representing approximately 30.00% of group revenue. The remainder is spread across industrial applications, infrastructure (tunnel ventilation), residential properties, healthcare facilities, and data centers. This diversified end-market mix helps shield the company from severe cyclicality in any single real estate segment
3.
Revenue generation mix: The vast majority of revenue is transactional, driven by direct equipment sales to ventilation contractors and wholesale distributors. A small but highly strategic portion (roughly 5.00% of sales) is derived from high-margin maintenance, installation, and servicing agreements. Management has targeted double-digit annual growth in services to deepen customer lock-in

Competitive Advantages and Moats

4.
Value chain positioning: Systemair sits in a profit-favoured position in the HVAC value chain, working directly with consultants and specifiers to lock in system designs early. By providing highly customized modular AHUs like the Geniox series, the company builds high customer retention
5.
Durable competitive advantages: The company's competitive moats are built on high switching costs and extreme local availability. Integrating custom AHUs with proprietary digital control platforms like the ACCESS system creates strong ties with building operators
6.
Scale and localization moats: Operating 27 manufacturing plants across 19 countries provides a structural barrier that smaller local players cannot replicate. This local presence guarantees rapid delivery times, which is a key buying criterion for contractors

Industry Structure and Peer Landscape

7.

9.

Section 5 – The possible investment case 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Systemair's investment case is strengthened by regulatory compliance tailwinds and targeted commercial execution by its new leadership.

Regulatory and Decarbonization Tailwinds

1.
Stricter building codes: The European Union's updated Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD) is the primary structural growth engine. Stricter EPBD regulations act as a legal mandate that requires substantial energy upgrades in existing commercial buildings
2.
Natural refrigerant transition: Stricter F-gas laws are accelerating the transition from synthetic to natural refrigerants such as R290. Systemair's Geniox HP DFN air handling unit is fully compliant, capturing early premium market share
3.
87 Renovation over new build: While new build construction remains stable at low levels, renovation demand has surged. This shift benefits Systemair, as commercial energy retrofits carry higher margins than initial equipment installations

Operational Turnarounds and Efficiency

4.
Production relocation benefits: The complete relocation of Menerga's production from Germany to Slovenia represents a major margin driver. This shift streamlines European logistics and optimizes capacity utilization in lower-cost manufacturing hubs
5.
91 Quantifiable cost savings: The Slovenian relocation is projected to deliver at least SEK 70.00m in annualized operational savings. These structural savings will directly support margin expansion starting in the current fiscal year
6.
93 Commercial engine upgrade: Under new CEO Robert Larsson, the strategic focus has transitioned to commercial execution. This initiative involves restructuring sales teams in Germany and Sweden to improve pricing discipline
7.
improvement. This expansion is highly visible as the scaled factory footprint is already in place

International Scaling and M&A

8.
Rapid global expansion: Investments to triple production capacity in Saudi Arabia under the "Saudi Made" label secure high-potential projects. Similarly, the Hyderabad factory relocation supports massive volume scaling in India
9.
