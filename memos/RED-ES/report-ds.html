Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 18 findings

Section summary
  1. The introduction of a massive hybrid debt program to fund accelerated investments severely diluted earnings and caught the market off-guard.

FY25 results and strategic plan delivery

1.
Earnings print – in line actuals: Redeia reported FY25 EBITDA of €1.26bn and net income of €506m, which was perfectly in line with the Visible Alpha consensus of €1.26bn and €509m respectively
2.
guidance – minor undershoot: Redeia guided to a 2025-29 EBITDA compound annual growth rate of greater than 5% (implying ~€1,530m), representing a minor deviation below the €1,560m expected by consensus
3.
income guidance – material miss: The strategic plan guided to a 2025-29 net income compound annual growth rate of ~3% (~€570m), which fell 4% below the €592m expected by consensus
4.
growth – positive surprise: Redeia guided to a 2% compound annual growth rate for dividends to €0.87 per share by 2029, surpassing the consensus expectation of €0.84 per share
5.
rating – methodology transition: Management indicated it is targeting an FFO/Net Debt ratio of greater than 14% and expects a BBB+ credit rating, moving away from its previous commitment to an A- rating

Hybrid financing and EPS dilution mechanics

6.
Hybrid debt plans – unexpected scale: Redeia announced plans to issue €1.5bn to €2.0bn in new hybrid debt to fund its €9.4bn total strategic capital plan, exceeding prior buy-side expectations of €1.5bn
7.
dilution – post-hybrid shock: While net income was guided at a ~3% compound annual growth rate, deducting hybrid coupon costs left expected post-hybrid EPS growth at just ~1% CAGR through 2029
8.
headwinds – low-coupon pressure: Management highlighted a substantial headwind from refinancing bonds maturing in 2026-28 that currently carry coupons below 1.5%, which will meaningfully increase the gross cost of debt
9.
raise avoidance – reassuring signal: A positive deviation from some worst-case buy-side fears was that Redeia explicitly ruled out any new equity issuance to fund the strategic plan

Useful life accounting and Q1 results

10.
Q1 EBITDA – surface-level beat: On April 29, 2026, Redeia reported Q1 EBITDA of €339m, which was 3% above the Jefferies consensus of €327m and up 5.8% year-on-year
