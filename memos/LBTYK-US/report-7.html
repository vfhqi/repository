§1 Executive Summary

Liberty Global is a HoldCo-in-transition: the investment case is overwhelmingly event-driven — spin, consolidation, and discount closure — not organic EBITDA growth. The February 2026 Vodafone buyout transforms the structure, giving LG 100% of VodafoneZiggo and 100% of Telenet under the Ziggo Group umbrella ahead of a planned 2027 Amsterdam listing and spin-off. The primary analytical question is whether the commercial and financial prerequisites for that listing can be met on schedule, given persistent NL subscriber headwinds and Belgian competition intensification.

  • Verdict: The thesis is event-driven and calendar-anchored; the key risk is not "will the spin happen" but "will the asset quality at listing support a discount-closing valuation or merely replicate an inferior stub" [#J]

§2 — Key Driver #1: Ziggo Group Spin-Off (H2-27) — HoldCo Discount Closure

  • Verdict: The February 2026 Vodafone buyout and Ziggo Group formation is the most significant structural catalyst in LG's history; a 2027 Amsterdam listing of a €6.6bn-revenue, €2.5bn+ EBITDA Benelux pure-play would be the largest European telecom IPO in years and structurally collapses the LG HoldCo discount [#J]
  • Definition: LG acquires Vodafone's 50% stake in VodafoneZiggo for €1bn cash + 10% stake in newly-formed Ziggo Group; Ziggo Group consolidates 100% VodafoneZiggo (NL) + 100% Telenet (Belgium); LG retains 90% pre-listing; Amsterdam listing + 90% spin-off to LBTYK shareholders targeted H2-27 [#I]
  • Structure detail: Combined entity targets €6.6bn revenue, >€2.5bn EBITDA, 7m mobile subs, 5m broadband subs — a top-5 European cable operator by subscriber count [#I]
  • Synergy target: Management guides €1bn NPV from combined synergies (net of integration costs); €500m combined adjusted FCF by 2028 [#I]
  • Leverage roadmap: Target 4.5x net debt/EBITDA by time of listing; requires mid-term EBITDA growth + capex normalisation in Belgium post-2025 peak [#A]
  • HoldCo discount mechanism: Pre-announcement LG shares traded at an estimated 40-50% discount to sum-of-the-parts NAV; spin-off eliminates the HoldCo wrapper discount by converting LBTYK into a direct Ziggo Group shareholder [#A]
  • Timing gates: Deal close targeted H2-26 (subject to regulatory approval); listing and spin targeted H2-27; 12-month gap allows initial integration and deleveraging [#I]
  • Q1-26 update: LG reiterated all 2026 full-year guidance targets after Q1-26 results (1-May-26); commercial trends at VodafoneZiggo supportive of listing preparation [#I]
  • Peer comparison: Swisscom (SCMN) trades at ~5.5-6x EV/EBITDA as a pure-play incumbent; a Ziggo Group listed entity trading at even a 4.5x multiple with €2.5bn+ EBITDA implies enterprise value of €11-12bn, vs. LG's consolidated market cap of ~$3-4bn pre-announcement — illustrating the magnitude of the discount closure opportunity [#A]
  • Sceptical read: Synergy targets for cable+cable combinations historically deliver 60-80% of stated NPV; Ziggo Group must also IPO into a telecom market where NL cable valuations are under structural compression — there is no guarantee the listing premium materialises [#J]

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — transaction announced, structured, regulatory path clear; execution risk is operational not structural

§3 — Key Driver #2: VMO2 Stake Disposal — Largest Single Asset Monetisation

  • Verdict: LG's ~50% stake in VMO2 (UK joint venture with Telefonica) is the single largest non-Benelux asset; Telefonica has been publicly reported as exploring control options but as of May-25 explicitly stated "no deal on the table" — making this a 2027+ option rather than a 2026 catalyst [#J]
  • Definition: VMO2 is the UK's leading fixed-mobile convergence operator (Virgin Media + O2); LG and Telefonica jointly hold 50% each via a holding company; InfraVia holds the remaining 50% in the nexfibre JV (separate FTTH build entity) [#I]
  • Nexfibre complexity: In February 2026, nexfibre (InfraVia + LG + Telefonica JV) acquired Substantial Group for £2bn, adding further complexity to the LG UK asset map; wholesale client is VMO2 [#I]
  • Telefonica signals: Bloomberg (May-25) reported Telefonica drawing up plans to acquire LG's VMO2 stake; Telefonica publicly downplayed the discussions; no subsequent deal announcement through May-26 [#A]
  • Asset value estimation: VMO2 carries ~$10bn+ enterprise value on a pro-rata basis; LG's 50% economic stake represents a material portion of any sum-of-the-parts NAV calculation for LBTYK [#A]
  • Strategic rationale for disposal: Post-Ziggo Group formation, LG's stated strategy is to become a Benelux-focused pure-play; VMO2 is non-core to that structure and disposal would crystallise value and fund further deleveraging [#A]
  • Structural impediment: Telefonica's 50% co-ownership means any disposal requires Telefonica either as buyer or as co-consenter to a third-party sale; no identified third-party buyer; regulatory risk (CMA) on any consolidation scenario [#A]
  • Buybacks funded by VMO2 proceeds: If disposal proceeds ($2-3bn range) materialised, LG could fund accelerated buybacks or special dividends pre-spin — a secondary capital return upside not in consensus [#A]
  • Sceptical read: VMO2's UK fixed subscriber losses and intense alt-net competition (CityFibre, Openreach FTTH) may be depressing what a buyer would pay; Telefonica may prefer to acquire at distressed multiples — the "no deal on the table" language could be strategic anchoring [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Low-medium plausibility in 2026; higher probability in 2027+ as Ziggo Group listing clarifies LG's long-term structure and Telefonica faces strategic pressure post-Spain

§4 — Key Driver #3: VodafoneZiggo Commercial Inflection — NL Subscriber Stabilisation + ARPU Recovery

  • Verdict: VodafoneZiggo's NL broadband trajectory is improving — four consecutive quarters of narrowing net losses, lowest consumer churn in three years (Q1-26), and the 2Gbps upgrade rollout — but subscriber losses have not turned to net adds and the company cannot yet claim structural inflection [#J]
  • Definition: VodafoneZiggo is the Dutch converged cable operator (7m+ homes passed); delivers LG's primary contribution to the Ziggo Group thesis; commercial trajectory is a direct gating factor on listing quality [#I]
  • Subscriber trend: TV sub losses of 121k in FY25 (improved from 135k in FY24); broadband net losses narrowed for four consecutive quarters; Q4-25 broadband net loss of 11,900 was the best quarterly result in over two years [#I]
  • ARPU performance: Fixed ARPU +1.0% YoY in Q4-25; price indexation partially offset by proactive front-book repricing; "value over volume" commercial strategy explicitly maintained despite Q3-25 weakness [#I]
  • Speed differentiation: VodafoneZiggo became largest provider of 2Gbps broadband in the Netherlands; planning 4Gbps and 8Gbps tiers in 2026 — cable speed proposition counters FTTH narrative [#A]
  • 2026 guidance: VodafoneZiggo 2026 guidance targets improving YoY revenue trends and improving fixed subscriber trajectory under the "How We Win Plan" [#I]
  • FTTH exposure: VodafoneZiggo is gradually adding FTTH capability; planning to cover >2m Dutch households with FTTH by 2026; however, primary infrastructure remains HFC/DOCSIS — cable speeds at 2Gbps are the primary competitive response [#I]
  • FMC bundle economics: FMC penetration is a key ARPU stabiliser; VodafoneZiggo leverages the Vodafone mobile brand (pre-buyout) and post-consolidation will integrate Ziggo mobile brand — FMC cross-sell is central to churn reduction [#A]
  • Sceptical read: Four quarters of improvement in losses is not the same as subscriber growth; KPN is taking structural share and the FTTH coverage gap means VodafoneZiggo is in catch-up mode for the next 3-5 years — "improving losses" may be the permanent state, not a transition to growth [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — trajectory is clearly improving but inflection to net adds remains unproven; adequate for listing preparation but insufficient to claim thesis validation

§5 — Key Driver #4: Telenet/Belgium FTTH Capex Peak Turning — FCF Inflection Post-2025

  • Verdict: Telenet's FCF profile is severely constrained at peak capex (38% of revenue, negative FCF of ~€170-180m in 2025) but the capex curve is structurally declining from 2026 onward; FCF inflection is necessary for the Ziggo Group's €500m combined FCF target by 2028 — timeline is tight and Digi disruption adds revenue-side risk [#J]
  • Definition: Telenet is the dominant Belgian cable operator (Flanders/Brussels); executing a major FTTH overbuild of its hybrid-fibre-coax network; capex peak is 2025 with expected normalisation from 2026 [#I]
  • Capex guidance: Moody's and Fitch independently model capex-to-revenue at ~38% in 2025, declining to ~35-36% in 2026-27; absolute capex guided at ~€1.1bn in 2025 and ~€1.0bn in 2026 [#I]
  • FCF trajectory: Moody's forecasts negative FCF of ~€170-180m in 2025 and ~€100m in 2026; no dividend payments during FCF-negative period; path to positive FCF in 2027 is the base case [#A]
  • Peer context: Proximus (PROX-BE) similarly executing FTTH at elevated capex intensity (~25-27% of revenue) but has a lower leverage base; Telenet's starting leverage (pre-Ziggo Group consolidation) is higher, making FCF inflection more binary [#A]
  • Revenue baseline (Q4-24): Telenet FY24 revenues of €2.85bn; Adjusted EBITDA declined 2.1% YoY; mobile postpaid softening due to intensifying competition from Digi + Orange/VOO restructuring [#I]
  • 2025 guidance: Telenet guided broadly stable revenue and low-to-mid single-digit EBITDA decline for 2025 — consistent with the FTTH investment peak absorption phase [#I]
  • Ziggo Group combined FCF target: €500m by 2028 requires Telenet to contribute positive FCF from 2027 at minimum, with VodafoneZiggo also generating positive FCF trajectory; both conditions are achievable but dependent on no further ARPU compression from competition [#A]
  • Sceptical read: Digi Belgium's fixed-line rollout (launched December 2024) is still in early stages — if Digi captures 200-300k Belgian broadband subs by 2027, Telenet's revenue-side recovery assumption is broken and FCF inflection to 2028 becomes highly uncertain [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — capex trajectory is mechanically declining; revenue-side Digi risk is the key variable that could delay FCF inflection by 1-2 years

§6 — Key Driver #5: Digi Belgium Disruption Risk — Bear Driver

  • Verdict: Digi Belgium launched in December 2024 with hyper-aggressive pricing (€5 mobile, €10 FTTH at 500Mbps), accumulated 78k mobile subs in 9 months, and pledged no price hikes through 2026; the mobile price war is live and the broadband disruption phase has not yet fully arrived — this is a bear driver that, if it fires materially, directly undermines the Ziggo Group listing thesis via Telenet EBITDA compression [#J]
  • Definition: Digi Communications (Romania-HQ) is a low-cost disruptor that has entered Belgium as the 4th MNO; deploys a nationwide 4G network via Citymesh; broadband rollout progressing alongside mobile [#I]
  • Pricing aggression: €5/month for 15GB mobile; €10/month for 500Mbps FTTH at launch; pledged no price hikes in 2026 while all Belgian incumbents (Proximus, Orange, Telenet) implemented annual increases [#I]
  • Market response: Proximus raised data allowances at unchanged prices (defensive concession); Orange Belgium streamlined the VOO brand; Proximus share price dipped on Digi entry fears — competitor responses confirm pricing environment compression [#I]
  • Subscriber ramp rate: 78k mobile subs by end-September 2025 (9 months post-launch); rate of acquisition suggests 150-200k by end-2026 on mobile — mobile revenue impact on Telenet and Proximus is already evidenced [#I]
  • Fixed-line risk: Fixed-line Digi rollout is the next pressure point; early FTTH deployment in Belgium targeting urban areas first; broadband disruption (volume + ARPU) has not yet appeared in Telenet results — it is a forward risk [#A]
  • Cohort comparison: Digi's entry into Spain/Portugal produced 2-3% ARPU compression across incumbents over 18-24 months; applying this to Belgian cable (Telenet Adjusted EBITDA ~€1.3bn) implies €26-39m annual EBITDA headwind at initial impact — material relative to the €100m negative FCF in 2026 [#A]
  • Telenet response options: FTTH investment (in progress) is Telenet's primary structural defence; faster FTTH rollout combined with FMC bundle economics are the key retention levers [#A]
  • Sceptical read: Digi's track record in Central/Eastern Europe shows that disruption compounds over 3-5 years, not just the first 12 months; Belgian regulators (BIPT) have historically supported infrastructure competition; Digi may accelerate fixed rollout faster than Telenet's FTTH completion [#J]

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility as a bear driver — already evidenced in mobile; the forward risk of broadband disruption is not yet in consensus and represents the primary downside scenario for the Ziggo Group thesis

§7 — Key Driver #6: NL Structural Fibre Competition — VodafoneZiggo Cable-to-Fibre Risk

  • Verdict: KPN's FTTH expansion (4.68m homes passed, ~40% NL broadband share, targeting national coverage by 2026) is a persistent structural headwind for VodafoneZiggo; the NL fixed broadband market is undergoing a technology transition from cable to fibre with no equivalent of the Digi tariff disruption — the risk is structural share loss, not price war [#J]
  • Definition: Netherlands fixed broadband market is bifurcating — KPN leads with FTTH (~40% share), VodafoneZiggo defends with upgraded DOCSIS 3.1/3.2 HFC; the technology gap is narrowing (VodafoneZiggo at 2Gbps) but not eliminating consumer preference for "true fibre" [#I]
  • KPN trajectory: KPN has 4.68m homes passed (>50% of NL homes); intentionally slowed rollout in late-2024 to prioritise take-up over coverage; ~40% NL broadband share — versus VodafoneZiggo at ~3.2m broadband subs (est. 35-37% share) [#I]
  • FTTH growing as revenue share: FTTH grew to over 50% of Dutch broadband revenues in 2025 — the first year fibre revenue exceeded cable revenue; this is a watershed structural signal [#A]
  • VodafoneZiggo's structural response: FTTH rollout targeting >2m NL households by 2026; 2Gbps and future 4/8Gbps tiers as speed parity arguments; VodafoneZiggo was the first to offer 2Gbps in the Netherlands [#I]
  • Alt-net competitive set: Beyond KPN, smaller FTTH alt-nets (Delta, Caiway) operate in regional markets; combined alt-net rollout adds further take-up pressure in secondary urban areas [#A]
  • ARPU implication: Fixed ARPU +1.0% YoY (Q4-25) masks the mix shift from full-cable bundles to lower-cost SIM-only as customers churn mobile to MVNO alternatives; net ARPU trajectory is flatter than the headline number suggests [#A]
  • Consumer broadband market size: NL broadband penetration is >90%; the market is zero-sum; VodafoneZiggo cannot grow sub count — it can only retain and upsell [#A]
  • Sceptical read: The technology narrative ("cable beats FTTH on speed") works at 2Gbps but becomes uncompetitive when KPN deploys 10Gbps symmetric GPON — the speed war is won by whichever technology has the longer upgrade roadmap, and FTTH wins at 5-10 year horizon [#J]

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility as a persistent bear driver — the structural NL fibre transition is happening on a known trajectory; the key question is pace of share loss, not direction

§8 — Key Driver #7: HoldCo Buyback Programme Sustainability

  • Verdict: LG's buyback programme has delivered substantial per-share accretion (224m+ shares repurchased since 2021, ~10% of shares per year when active) but is mechanically constrained in H2-26 by the €1bn Vodafone buyout cash requirement; buyback pace likely decelerates while Ziggo Group formation completes, resuming post-close [#J]
  • Definition: LG operates a systematic share buyback programme financed by HoldCo cash, growth portfolio disposals, and structural dividends from VodafoneZiggo and Telenet; buybacks compound per-share value at the LBTYK level regardless of NAV discount [#I]
  • Historical record: 224m+ shares repurchased since 2021; ~$700m programme in 2024 (~10% of shares); up to 10% of outstanding shares authorised for 2025 [#I]
  • Q1-25 pace: 3.2m shares repurchased Jan-Mar 2025 for $38.8m (0.92% of shares); modest pace suggesting buyback activity is episodic and liquidity-constrained [#I]
  • Q1-26 update: Company disposed of half its ITV stake and a portion of EdgeConneX for combined ~$180m in Q1-26 and $300m through April — proceeds funding liquidity for buyout cash and potentially future buybacks [#I]
  • Constraint from Vodafone buyout: €1bn cash payment for Vodafone's VodafoneZiggo stake is the primary H2-26 cash use; during the period between signing and close, buyback pace is likely constrained to preserve cash covenants [#A]
  • Post-spin buyback logic: Post-Ziggo Group listing (H2-27), LBTYK would own Ziggo Group shares + residual growth portfolio; residual LG at HoldCo level could pursue aggressive buyback or wind-down; this is the terminal value mechanism for LBTYK shareholders [#A]
  • Peer context: SCMN (Swisscom) does not use buybacks as a value lever — it operates as an incumbent dividend payer; PROX-BE similarly distributes cash dividends rather than buybacks; LG's buyback mechanism is structurally distinctive [#A]
  • Sceptical read: If VodafoneZiggo and Telenet are both FCF-constrained through 2026-27, the HoldCo receives less structural dividend income; buyback capacity then depends entirely on growth portfolio disposal pace, which is opportunistic and non-guaranteed [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — buybacks will continue but at reduced pace through H2-26; acceleration post-Ziggo Group close is the base case; not a primary thesis driver but a meaningful per-share compounding mechanism

§9 — Key Driver #8: Growth Portfolio Monetisation (AtlasEdge, ITV, Formula E)

  • Verdict: Liberty Growth's $3.1bn portfolio across ~70 companies is a genuine source of NAV optionality but monetisation is opportunistic rather than programmatic; Q1-26 disposals of ITV and EdgeConneX stakes (~$180m) demonstrate execution capacity, but the portfolio's concentration in a small number of core holdings means lumpy, unpredictable realisation [#J]
  • Definition: Liberty Growth is LG's global investment arm with positions in content, tech, and infrastructure; key holdings include AtlasEdge (European edge data centres), ITV plc (UK broadcaster), Televisa Univision, EdgeConneX, Plume, and Formula E [#I]
  • Portfolio scale: $3.1bn fair market value; top 6 investments represent a substantial majority of value; ~75 companies and funds [#I]
  • Q1-26 activity: Partial exit of ITV stake + partial EdgeConneX exit = ~$180m Q1-26; $300m total through April 2026 [#I]
  • AtlasEdge: European edge data centre company; LG is a co-founder alongside DigitalBridge; European edge demand is structurally growing (AI workloads, content delivery); valuation trajectory positive but no IPO announced [#A]
  • Formula E: LG holds a strategic stake in the Formula E electric racing series; formula e media rights and team valuations are rising but the asset is illiquid and minority-positioned; not a near-term monetisation candidate [#A]
  • Portfolio role in thesis: Growth portfolio serves two functions — (1) incremental NAV at LBTYK level, and (2) cash generation via periodic disposals to fund buybacks/Ziggo Group formation [#A]
  • NAV bridge: At $3.1bn portfolio value and ~345m LBTYK shares outstanding, growth portfolio represents ~$9/share in NAV contribution — material relative to any realistic LBTYK market price [#A]
  • Sceptical read: Portfolio FMV is LG's own valuation — illiquid positions (Formula E, Plume, smaller funds) may carry marks that do not translate to market realisable values; ITV's structural decline as a linear TV broadcaster questions the durability of that mark [#J]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility as a thesis contributor — genuine value but lumpy and opportunistic; not a timeline-driving catalyst; best viewed as NAV floor support rather than an active catalyst

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis

  • Verdict: The Ziggo Group spin-off is the thesis-defining event; six of eight drivers either directly support or are gating conditions for that event; the investment case at Triaging stage is analytically tractable — the question is probability-weighting the execution risks against the discount closure upside [#J]
  • Bull case synthesis: Ziggo Group lists H2-27 on Amsterdam at 5.0-5.5x EV/EBITDA; €500m FCF achieved on schedule; HoldCo discount at LBTYK collapses as spin-off mechanics deliver Ziggo shares direct to shareholders; VMO2 disposal crystallises residual value; LBTYK total return 60-80% from announcement [#A]
  • Bear case synthesis: NL subscriber losses re-accelerate as KPN FTTH take-up compounds; Digi Belgium's broadband launch compresses Telenet EBITDA by 5-10% vs base case; Ziggo Group 4.5x leverage target missed; listing delayed to 2028 or downsized; VMO2 disposal stalls at inferior multiples; LG trapped in discount for additional 2 years [#A]
  • Key non-linearity: The listing thesis is binary — if the listing happens at a reasonable multiple, the thesis works; if it is delayed or the asset quality is inferior at listing, the HoldCo discount may persist or widen [#J]
  • Gating conditions to monitor (in priority order): [#A]
  • VodafoneZiggo Q2-Q3-26 subscriber trend — must show continued narrowing losses and at minimum flat ARPU
  • Telenet FY25 EBITDA vs guidance — must hold low-to-mid single digit decline; any material miss flags FCF inflection delay
  • Digi Belgium fixed broadband launch timing and initial take-up — first 6 months of broadband rollout are the key signal
  • Ziggo Group regulatory approval (deal close H2-26) — no listed antitrust obstacles identified but standard merger review applies
  • LG Q3-Q4-26 cash balance — must demonstrate capacity to fund buyout + maintain buyback programme
  • Structural advantage: LG's 100% ownership of both NL and Belgian cable networks post-consolidation creates genuine synergy potential (network sharing, procurement, IT consolidation, shared services) unavailable to either asset standalone — this is a legitimate value creation argument, not financial engineering [#A]
  • Terminal value logic: Post-spin, LBTYK shareholders own Ziggo Group shares (direct) + a residual LG entity (growth portfolio + VMO2 stake); the residual LG entity should trade at or near NAV if management commits to wind-down [#A]
  • Cohort valuation anchor: SCMN trades at 5.5-6x EV/EBITDA as a Swiss incumbent; PROX-BE at ~5x; European cable peers historically at 6-8x; Ziggo Group at 4.5-5.0x at listing would be conservative but achievable given leverage start point and NL subscriber headwind [#A]
  • Sceptical read on timeline: The Sunrise spin-off by Swisscom (SCMN analogue) succeeded because Sunrise had a clean EBITDA growth story; Ziggo Group faces two actively deteriorating sub-markets — VodafoneZiggo NL and Telenet Belgium — which could make the IPO narrative harder to sell to institutional investors at premium multiples [#J]
  • Verdict on confidence: The bull case is coherent and structurally well-constructed; the bear case is also coherent and operationally grounded; the key variable is competitive intensity (Digi Belgium + KPN FTTH) relative to management's ability to deliver commercial inflection in both markets simultaneously [#J]
  • Watson's stage verdict: PROGRESS to ESA — the event thesis is concrete enough to justify deeper work on the listing mechanics, Ziggo Group NAV bridge, and competitive intensity modelling for NL and Belgium; the risk-reward is asymmetric enough to warrant ESA investment but the driver-set composition signals this is an event/catalyst play, not a compounder [#J]

Confidence in case-level thesis: medium plausibility — the event is real and calendar-anchored but operational execution in two simultaneously challenged cable markets creates meaningful downside scenarios

AGGREGATE WEAK SIGNALS / DOWNSIDE ❌

  • ❌ VodafoneZiggo executed 400 job cuts in May 2025 following subscriber losses — restructuring signals management recognises structural under-performance, not cyclical; cost reduction alone does not fix a subscriber trajectory problem
  • ❌ Telenet's Adjusted EBITDA declined 2.1% in FY24 and guided further low-to-mid single digit decline for FY25 — two consecutive years of EBITDA compression entering the capex peak period is a weak starting base for the FCF recovery argument
  • ❌ Liberty Global's Q1-26 revenue of $1.27bn was below the $1.30bn estimate — early evidence that the top-line recovery narrative may be ahead of actual commercial performance
  • ❌ LG's growth portfolio includes ITV plc, which faces secular decline in linear TV advertising; the mark on ITV is a declining-quality asset that may realise at a discount to carrying value over a 2-3 year horizon

AGGREGATE OUTLIERS ⚡ RARE

  • ⚡ RARE If Telefonica agrees to purchase LG's VMO2 stake in 2026-27 at a 7-8x EV/EBITDA multiple (consistent with mid-market UK cable comps), LG would receive $3-4bn+ in proceeds — equivalent to ~$9-12 per LBTYK share — which would be transformative for the capital return capacity and buyback programme, entirely outside current consensus modelling
  • ⚡ RARE Digi Belgium's rollout, if it mirrors the Belgian mobile market disruption pace (78k subs in 9 months), could reach 500k+ broadband subs in Belgium by 2028; at Belgian ARPU levels (~€50/month), this represents €300m+ annualised revenue threat, equivalent to ~23% of Telenet's revenue — a scenario that would structurally impair the €500m Ziggo Group FCF target
