BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front

Two distinct events explain the full spike-and-fade pattern in the week of 12–15 May 2026. First, Gestamp mocion (structural automotive body components — doors, pillars, chassis parts) published Q1 2026 results on 12 May that beat on margin, with earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortisation margin expanding 60 basis points year-on-year to 11% on revenues of €2.83 billion; the market read this as a Phoenix Plan (North American restructuring programme) validation and the stock spiked ~6–7% over 12–13 May. Second, the macro backdrop turned sharply negative on 14–15 May as Trump's threatened 25% tariff on European Union-assembled vehicles came back into focus, driving a sector-wide sell-off that wiped out the earnings-driven gain almost in full. The net result is a stock near €3.16 — barely above the six-week low of €2.93 — with the fundamental delivery of Q1 fully overwhelmed by the tariff-regime overhang.
