  • Over-promiser signal — 2022: FY2022 -31% EBIT miss represents over-promising in a stress environment; no mid-year guidance revision despite visible cost deterioration — pattern B (aggressive guidance held too long) [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — 2023: post-crisis guidance reset was deliberately conservative; midpoint beat of ~14% in FY2023 is mechanically driven by anchoring to a cautious range; not structurally impressive sandbagging — it was a crisis response, not a systematic discipline [#J]

  • Honest tracker — 2024: FY2024 EBIT delivery ~€340m vs midpoint ~€330m is a modest +3% beat; range was still wide; pattern converging toward honest-tracker but without strong conviction [#A]

  • Pattern shift — 2024-2025: The 2025-2027 CMD targets (EBIT >€500m by 2027) look like a return to pre-crisis stretch ambition; the 14% EBIT CAGR from 2025 midpoint to 2027 floor is materially above the 2017-2021 trend CAGR of ~5-7% [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: average miss magnitude when missing is ~20% (FY2021 -10%, FY2022 -31%); miss severity is above European utility peer median of ~8-12% [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: average beat magnitude when beating is ~10% on midpoint (FY2023 ~14%, FY2024 ~6%); below the miss severity — asymmetric risk profile: misses hurt more than beats reward [#J]

  • ⚡ RARE — cyclical re-stretch: The pattern of pre-crisis over-promise → crisis miss → post-crisis sandbag → recovery re-stretch is a specific and unusual guidance psychology; if 2025-2027 repeats the 2019-2022 arc, the current CMD targets are the equivalent of the pre-crisis over-promise phase [Cross-ref: §2 + §7]

  • Margin assumption — cost: management embeds ~2-3% p.a. real cost efficiency improvements in grid and heat networks; this is aspirational — the FY2021 grid OpEx overrun suggests cost control is not reliably achieved [#J]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: EBIT margin expansion from ~12% (FY2024) to ~15% (FY2027) requires operating leverage on a growing revenue base; at current CapEx intensity, incremental margins need to run >20% on new renewables volumes — achievable but tight [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: effective tax rate assumed ~27-28% (consistent with German corporate rate + local trade tax); no identified tax structure optionality; stable assumption [#I]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: management does not provide explicit gas/power price assumptions but states "neutral to prior year" for FY2025; given the 2022 precedent, any commodity spike that is not fully hedged represents a credibility tail risk [#J]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: €2.2-2.4bn CapEx cycle financed partly by new debt issuance; management models 3.5-4% cost of new debt — current market rates for BBB-rated German utility new issuance are approximately 3.8-4.2% (as of Q1 2026), creating modest upside pressure on interest cost assumptions [#A]
