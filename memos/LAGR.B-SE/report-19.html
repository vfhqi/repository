§5 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 4: Free Cash Flow Conversion Structural Gap

Specific failure narrative: Lagardère's reported EBITDA is materially eroded by IFRS 16 lease cash flows (Travel Retail concession operating leases), working capital movements (book advances, inventory), and maintenance capex across both segments. Bull thesis assumes FCF conversion of 55-65% of EBITDA; structural reality may be 35-45%, meaning apparent cheap EV/EBITDA multiples are significantly misleading. Transmission mechanism: FCF miss → leverage stays elevated → re-rating risk → concentration in EV → equity value compression disproportionate to underlying operating performance.

Early warning signals:
- Watch: Operating cash flow vs EBITDA divergence widening over consecutive quarters
- Watch: IFRS 16 lease liability growth outpacing reported EBITDA growth (leverage ratio optically flat but economically rising)
- Watch: Working capital DSO/DIO trends — rising days in either metric = cash conversion deterioration
- Watch: Capex intensity rising (new concession fit-out, IT infrastructure in Publishing) — maintenance vs growth capex split not always disclosed
- Watch: Net debt/EBITDA pre- vs post-lease divergence (tells you the lease-adjusted leverage story)

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — IFRS 16 adoption structurally inflates EBITDA for concession-heavy businesses; this is well-documented across the travel retail sector; the question is magnitude, not existence

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: IFRS 16 mechanics are deterministic — lease payments must be made regardless of EBITDA
- Motive: No behavioural element — this is accounting structure, not intentional obfuscation (though management benefits from EBITDA-based compensation)
- Opportunity: IFRS 16 lease disclosure is complex enough that buyside often uses pre-IFRS 16 proxies incorrectly
- Test result: All three legs present — full M/M/O; accounting-structural failure mode

Disconfirming evidence: Management disclosure of explicit post-IFRS 16 FCF (cash flow after lease payments) consistently above 55% of reported EBITDA; independent verification via FCF yield calculation using actual cash interest + lease payments

Monitoring checklist:
- Annual/semi-annual cash flow statement: operating CF, lease payments (financing section), capex (investing section)
- Adjusted FCF calculation independently: EBITDA minus cash tax, cash interest, lease principal payments, capex
- Net debt reconciliation pre- and post-IFRS 16 (management sometimes discloses both)
- Credit facility documentation for covenant definition of "net debt" — IFRS 16 treatment in covenants varies
- Compare to peer: Avolta/Dufry FCF conversion rate as calibration anchor

Interaction with other failure modes: High correlation with FM3 (concession rent escalation) and FM1 (per-location productivity). FM4 amplifies impact of FM1 and FM3 by making financial position less resilient to operational setbacks.

Sceptical closer: Disconfirming evidence — an independently calculated post-IFRS 16 FCF yield of ≥4.5% on current market cap, sustained over 2 years, would validate the FCF conversion assumption in the bull thesis.
