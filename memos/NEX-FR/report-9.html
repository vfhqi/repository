§2 — Quality of Earnings

  • Quality verdict: Mixed — headline cash conversion is solid and improving, but the absolute size of non-GAAP adjustments has grown in line with earnings, creating a persistent quality discount that is structurally embedded rather than shrinking [#J]

  • One-off impact: Restructuring charges have been present in every reporting period across L3Y — €55m in FY2022, €45m in FY2023, €35m in FY2024 — declining in absolute terms but still ~4-5% of adjusted EBITDA annually [#A]

  • Sub: treating restructuring as one-off is increasingly difficult to justify given 3+ consecutive years of inclusion

  • ❌ Adjustments trend: The gap between reported and adjusted EPS widened from €1.40 (FY2022) to €1.10 (FY2024) in absolute terms — modestly narrowing but still ~8-9% of adjusted EPS; at no point has Nexans produced a clean "no adjustments" year [#A]

  • Sub: M&A-related amortisation (PPA charges from Cenergy, Cavi acquisition-related items) continues to run at ~€30-40m annually

  • Recurring vs one-off: Restructuring and M&A costs have been present in ALL 6 full-year reporting periods across L3Y — these should be treated as operational costs in any honest earnings quality assessment [#A]

  • Sub: stripping these costs would reduce L3Y adjusted EPS CAGR from ~16% to approximately 13-14% — still solid but materially lower

  • NWC tailwind/headwind: Working capital has been volatile but net neutral across L3Y — copper inventory swings drove a €-80m NWC headwind in H1 2022 (copper price spike) and a €+60m tailwind in H1 2023 (copper normalisation) [#A]

  • Sub: HV project milestone billing creates large but episodic positive NWC swings; receivables timing around project completions has inflated OCF in FY2023 and FY2024

  • Cash conversion: OCF/EBITDA averaged approximately 62% across L3Y (FY2022: ~58%, FY2023: ~65%, FY2024: ~65%) — this is acceptable for an industrial with significant project WIP but below typical cable-sector peers at ~70-75% [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx intensity is rising (HV capacity expansion) — net free cash flow conversion after CapEx is lower, approximately 35-40% of EBITDA across L3Y

  • ❌ OCF vs NI: Cash conversion from reported net income to OCF is inconsistent — FY2022 saw OCF below net income (€290m OCF vs €345m net income), a red flag for a period with heavy accrual-based earnings [#A]

  • Sub: FY2023 recovered (OCF €420m vs net income €385m) and FY2024 was similarly positive — but FY2022 anomaly warrants attention

  • D&A trend: D&A has risen from approximately €175m (FY2022) to approximately €195m (FY2024) — roughly in line with gross CapEx additions, suggesting no aggressive acceleration of depreciation schedules [#A]

  • Sub: amortisation of acquired intangibles (€30-40m annually) is included in reported but excluded from adjusted figures — this is the primary non-GAAP gap driver

  • Receivables turn: Receivables DSO has been broadly stable at ~55-60 days across L3Y, with no deterioration trend — reassuring for a project-billing business where receivables quality is a key risk [#A]

  • Conservative accounting: Nexans applies IFRS-15 percentage-of-completion conservatively on HV contracts, recognising revenue only on certified project milestones — this reduces revenue smoothing risk but creates lumpiness [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on earnings quality? Restructuring charges recurring for 3+ consecutive years are best modelled as an ongoing operating cost (~€35-55m annually); if so, "true" EPS is 8-9% lower than adjusted consensus. Additionally, the H1 2022 OCF < NI anomaly suggests earnings quality can deteriorate rapidly in copper-spike environments — a risk that is not fully priced into forward models

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

  • Sustainability verdict: Margin expansion across L3Y is predominantly structural — driven by deliberate mix shift toward HV/submarine cables (higher margin, backlog-secured) rather than cost cuts or transient volume leverage [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: Reported ~4.8% L3Y; organic (copper-adjusted) approximately 8-9% — upper-quartile performance vs European cable industry peers (no direct peer data available; general sector context flagged in QC) [#A]

  • EBITDA CAGR: ~12% L3Y, outpacing revenue CAGR by approximately 700bps — the spread confirms genuine operating leverage rather than revenue-only growth [#A]

  • Sub: EBITDA margin expanded from 7.8% (FY2022) to 8.9% (FY2024), with the most material step-up occurring in H1 2024

  • Adjusted EPS CAGR: ~16% L3Y, outpacing EBITDA CAGR by ~400bps due to: (1) net debt reduction (lower interest cost), (2) absence of major equity dilution, (3) modestly lower effective tax rate in FY2024 [#A]

  • Operating leverage: Revenue grew ~10% between FY2022 and FY2024 (organic basis) while EBITDA grew ~25% — an operating leverage ratio of approximately 2.5x, consistent with mix-driven rather than volume-driven expansion [#A]

  • Gross margin: Expanded from approximately 24.0% (H1 2022) to approximately 26.5% (FY2024) — a ~250bps improvement; the absence of any period of gross margin contraction across L3Y is notable for a company with significant commodity pass-through exposure [#J]

  • Mix shift driver: HV/submarine cables grew from approximately 25-28% of EBITDA in FY2022 to approximately 40%+ in FY2024 — this segment carries EBITDA margins of 12-15% vs 5-7% for distribution cables, explaining the majority of blended margin accretion [#A]

  • Sub: distribution cable margins have been under mild pressure from European construction softness — HV mix shift has more than compensated

  • Cost cut driver: Restructuring savings (~€40-60m annually per company disclosure) have contributed a steady but declining share of margin improvement — management targets structural exit from high-cost European manufacturing, partially complete [#A]

  • Pricing driver: Nexans operates largely on copper pass-through contracts in distribution cables — pricing power is limited; HV/submarine contracts contain fixed-price elements where the company captures pricing upside in a tight-supply market [#A]

  • Scale driver: Submarine cable manufacturing has significant fixed-cost leverage — Halden facility ramp in 2024 provides an incremental fixed-cost absorption benefit estimated by sell-side at ~€30-40m annually at full utilisation (2025-2026 timeframe) [AS-Berenberg] [#A]

  • Sustainability: HV backlog of €5.2bn at FY2024 (approximately 4-5 years of HV revenue at current rates) provides high visibility on mix-sustaining revenue — structural margin sustainability is better supported than in most European industrial peers [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on margin sustainability? HV submarine cable margins of 12-15% assume no major project cost overrun — a single large cable failure or re-lay event could absorb 2-3 years of margin progress in one period. Additionally, as the HV market attracts new entrants (Prysmian capacity additions, new Asian players), pricing power on new contract signings may compress over 2026-2028, reducing the incremental margin of new backlog vs existing backlog

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

  • Seasonality verdict: H2 is structurally stronger than H1 for Nexans, with the H1/H2 skew widening modestly over L3Y as HV project billing (H2-weighted) grows as a share of revenue — this is a real and increasing pattern [#J]

  • H1 pattern: H1 consistently contributes approximately 48-49% of full-year revenue and approximately 45-47% of full-year EBITDA — below-proportional contribution [#A]

  • Sub: H1 2022 EBITDA was €305m vs FY2022 €630m (48%); H1 2023 was €315m vs FY2023 €700m (45%); H1 2024 was €385m vs FY2024 €790m (49%) — stable but margin-dilutive half

  • H2 pattern: H2 contributes approximately 51-55% of full-year EBITDA — the gap is driven by HV project milestone certifications clustering in Q4 and European distribution cable seasonality (construction season peaks Q2-Q3, billing lags to Q3-Q4) [#A]

  • Strongest quarter: Q4 is consistently the strongest (highest revenue recognition and billing milestones) — Q3 trading updates have in each of L3Y guided to an H2-weighted full-year outcome [#A]

  • Weakest quarter: Q1 is structurally weakest — construction activity lowest, no project milestones typically cleared, copper volatility front-loads NWC consumption [#A]

  • Seasonality direction: H1/H2 skew appears to be widening modestly as HV grows — if HV reaches 50%+ of EBITDA by 2026, H1/H2 imbalance will increase further, making H1 results a structurally poor guide to full-year outcomes [#J]

  • Sub: sell-side analysts have repeatedly under-weighted this effect, creating systematic H1 consensus misses that recover in H2 — a persistent pattern rather than individual forecast errors

  • Cause — production: Submarine cable installation is weather-limited (North Sea installation windows open April-October, cable manufacturing and testing clusters pre-installation) — creates natural H1 revenue recognition lag vs H2 delivery [#A]

  • Seasonality changing: The shift toward longer-duration HV submarine projects (5-7 year contracts) is creating multi-year revenue recognition patterns within each project — individual period seasonality is increasingly project-timing-dependent rather than purely calendar-driven [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on seasonal patterns? A widening H1/H2 skew creates increasing risk that H1 misses are misread as trend deterioration rather than mechanical timing — but if any HV project suffers a genuine delay (weather, permitting, vessel unavailability), what looks like seasonality could be a real slip. The bearish risk is that the market accepts H1 misses as "timing" for 2-3 cycles, then discovers one genuine delay that was masked by the seasonal explanation

§5 — Beat/Miss Pattern + Management Credibility

  • Credibility verdict: Nexans management is a moderate sandbagger — guidance is set conservatively at the start of each year, and the company has beaten or matched initial guidance in FY2022, FY2023, and FY2024; intra-year guidance is upgraded at H1 in each case [#J]

  • Beat/miss frequency: 7 of 8 measurable semi-annual reporting periods showed a beat vs pre-release consensus on EBITDA; the single miss was H1 2023 (€315m vs €325m consensus) — a miss attributable to copper timing, not underlying operational weakness [#A]

  • Sub: EPS beat/miss pattern mirrors EBITDA — 7/8 beats; revenue is a slight narrower beat set (5/8 beats, 3 in-line, 0 misses on revenue)

  • Beat magnitude: Average EBITDA beat across 7 beating periods: approximately +€15m (+4-5% vs consensus) — a consistent, modest sandbag rather than dramatic sandbagging [#A]

  • Sub: average EPS beat approximately +€0.20 per share on adjusted basis — approximately 3-4% per period

  • Miss magnitude (H1 2023): EPS miss was €0.20 (adjusted EPS €4.55 vs consensus €4.75) — approximately 4% below consensus; market reaction was muted given management's immediate attribution to copper timing and reiteration of full-year guidance [#A]

  • ⚡ RARE: Sandbagging signal: In each of FY2022, FY2023, and FY2024, Nexans issued initial full-year EBITDA guidance at or below the prior-year consensus-implied trajectory, then upgraded at H1 results — a remarkably consistent pattern across 3 consecutive fiscal years, consistent with deliberate conservative anchoring [#J] [Cross-ref: §6]

  • Guidance accuracy (FY level):

  • FY2022 initial guidance: EBITDA ~€580-620m; delivered €630m — beat initial midpoint by ~€20m (+3.3%)
  • FY2023 initial guidance: EBITDA ~€660-700m; delivered €700m — at top of range

  • FY2024 initial guidance: EBITDA ~€740-780m; delivered €790m — beat midpoint by ~€30m (+4.0%)

  • Direction of surprise: Revenue surprises are smaller and less consistent than EBITDA surprises — suggesting management controls the margin story better than volume delivery, consistent with backlog-driven revenue and controlled cost execution [#A]

  • Trend in accuracy: Guidance accuracy at the EBITDA level has improved marginally across L3Y — FY2022 guidance range was wide (€40m wide), FY2024 guidance range was tighter (€40m wide but tighter % range as EBITDA base grew) [#A]

  • Sell-side revision pattern: Pre-release, sell-side consistently ran estimates below management's stated midpoint in FY2022 and FY2023, catching up post-H1 guidance upgrades — by FY2024, sell-side had partially closed the gap [AS-JPMorgan, AS-Kepler Cheuvreux] [#A]

  • Management commentary tone: Management has progressively increased confidence language around HV backlog execution from FY2022 ("visibility improving") to FY2024 ("high-conviction delivery of EBITDA trajectory") — a positive tone shift consistent with operational maturation [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: What's the bearish read on management credibility? Consistent beats are the most fragile form of credibility — they depend on guidance conservatism being sustained. The sandbag cushion appears to be narrowing: FY2024 delivered €790m vs a midpoint of €760m (beat of ~€30m, or 4%), but FY2025 guidance midpoint of €880m already implies +11% growth embedded in the baseline, leaving less room to underpromise. If HV execution slips in 2025, the sandbagging cover evaporates and the company faces its first FY guidance miss in 4 years

§6 — Per-Period Narrative

  • Period narrative verdict: The dominant per-period narrative across L3Y is consistent operational delivery with one identifiable setback (H1 2023 copper effect) and one step-change inflection (H1 2024 HV ramp) — a narrative arc that supports rather than undermines the forward thesis [#J]

H1 2022:

  • Period — H1 2022: Positive surprise on EBITDA margin (7.4% vs 7.0% consensus) driven by earlier-than-expected HV project billing; revenue above consensus due to copper pass-through (copper prices peaked March 2022) [#A]
  • Sub: restructuring of €30m was in-line with guidance — no shock

  • Sub: management tone: confident; "the Nexans Power Delivery strategy is delivering ahead of schedule"

  • Mgmt commentary shift: First period where management explicitly linked HV backlog size (then ~€3.5bn) to multi-year earnings visibility — a strategic framing that became a key investor narrative anchor [#A]

FY2022:

  • Period — FY2022: Positive surprise on both EBITDA (€630m vs €610m consensus) and adjusted EPS (€9.60 vs €9.20) — full-year beat driven by H2 project completions and copper hedge gains [#A]
  • Sub: €25m copper hedge gains were disclosed separately — excluding these, EBITDA beat narrows to ~€5m (still a beat but mechanically weaker)

  • Sub: guidance for FY2023 was issued at €660-700m EBITDA — below the FY2022 exit rate implied by H2 run-rate, immediately setting up another sandbag

  • Strategic pivot: FY2022 results marked the formal exit from Nexans's commodity cable businesses (sale of Building & Territories North America), accelerating the portfolio quality shift [#A]

H1 2023:

  • Period — H1 2023: Single miss of the L3Y window — EBITDA €315m vs €325m consensus (miss of €10m), EPS €4.55 vs €4.75 (miss of €0.20) — driven by copper pass-through timing (lower copper base vs H1 2022 inflated copper) [#A]
  • Sub: organic volumes were positive (+4%), confirming the miss was mechanical not operational

  • Sub: market reacted negatively intraday but management reiterated full-year guidance; stock recovered within 2 weeks — management credibility intact

  • Negative surprise: Revenue -2.7% YoY alarmed some analysts unfamiliar with the copper pass-through mechanism — Berenberg and JPMorgan both published explainer notes post-results [AS-Berenberg, AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

FY2023:

  • Period — FY2023: Record EBITDA of €700m — beat consensus (€685m) on strong H2 HV project billing; adjusted EPS €10.80 vs €10.55 consensus [#A]
  • Sub: HV backlog increased to €4.6bn (from €3.5bn at end FY2022) — a signal of forward revenue de-risking that drove meaningful post-results upward revisions

  • Sub: management upgraded FY2024 initial guidance above consensus-implied trajectory, confirming the sandbag cycle

  • Tone shift: FY2023 call featured explicit CEO language on "structural margin expansion" for the first time — a deliberate communication choice to shift investor framing from cyclical to structural [#A]

H1 2024:

  • Period — H1 2024: Step-change inflection — EBITDA margin of 8.9% in H1 (vs 7.9% in H1 2023) — the largest single-half margin improvement in the L3Y window [#J]
  • Sub: Halden submarine cable facility ramp credited as the primary driver; production efficiency improving ahead of schedule

  • Sub: EPS beat was largest in absolute terms (€6.35 actual vs €6.10 consensus, +4.1%)

  • Capacity addition: First revenues from Halden facility recognised in H1 2024 — management confirmed facility is booked through 2028, providing multi-year revenue certainty [#A]

FY2024:

  • Period — FY2024: Second consecutive record EBITDA (€790m); adjusted EPS €12.90 vs €12.50 consensus; HV backlog at record €5.2bn — a clean delivery that justified the forward thesis [#A]
  • Sub: FY2025 EBITDA guidance of €860-900m (midpoint €880m) issued above consensus of ~€855m — continuing the pattern of above-consensus initial guidance anchoring

  • Sub: restructuring charges declined to ~€35m (from €55m in FY2022) — a genuine quality improvement in absolute terms even if still present

  • Confirmed expectation: Distribution cable recovery in European markets confirmed (volume growth low-single-digits in H2 2024) — a supportive tailwind that had been flagged by management but doubted by some sell-side [AS-Deutsche Bank, AS-Citi] [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: Across the per-period narrative, the dominant bearish thread is the concentration of positive surprises in the HV/submarine segment — every period where management beat consensus, the HV segment delivered above expectations. This creates a binary risk: if the HV segment stumbles (project delay, cost overrun, backlog repricing), the entire per-period beat pattern reverses simultaneously rather than partially

§7 — Sell-Side Estimate Revisions

  • Revision verdict: Sell-side analysts have been consistently reactive (revising upward after, not before, earnings beats) — this persistent revision lag is a signal that the market has not fully priced the HV execution story, creating ongoing estimate momentum [#J]

  • Pre-release revision: In each of the last 3 FY reporting cycles, consensus EBITDA in the 4 weeks before results was below the delivered figure — sell-side was not pre-empting beats but running at consensus [#A]

  • Sub: pre-release consensus revision direction was flat to slightly upward in H2 of FY2022, FY2023, and FY2024 — analysts anchoring on prior guidance midpoints rather than anticipating upside

  • On-release revision: Post-FY results in all 3 years, consensus for the following year was revised upward by 2-4% on EBITDA within 48 hours of results — consistent with reactive not predictive revision behaviour [#A]

  • Sub: post-FY2023 results, FY2024 consensus EBITDA moved from ~€750m to ~€775m (+3.3%) — the eventual delivery of €790m exceeded even this upgraded estimate

  • Post-release revision: The largest revisions occurred 2-4 weeks post-results as broker models were updated — Berenberg and Kepler Cheuvreux were among the most aggressive in upgrading FY forward estimates after each FY results [AS-Berenberg, AS-Kepler Cheuvreux] [#A]

  • Revision magnitude: Average post-FY revision to the following year's EBITDA consensus: approximately +€20-25m (+2.5-3.5%) — modest in absolute terms but consistent and directionally positive [#A]
