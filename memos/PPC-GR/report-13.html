§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front: PPC exhibits a selective sandbagging pattern — mild and consistent on EBITDA, absent or inverted (over-promising) on CapEx and FCF; the pattern is not clean and is metric-dependent [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA guidance has been at or below eventual delivery in all 4 of 4 recent periods; management consistently guides to the bottom or middle of a range and allows operational performance to close the gap [#A]

  • Sub: Mechanism: RES generation is guided conservatively (P75 load factor assumption per [AS-Eurobank Equities] research note) and actual load factors have run P50-P60 in benign weather years [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — CapEx: ❌ CapEx guidance is systematically optimistic; 5 consecutive overruns; management consistently presents CapEx guidance as if construction pipeline will execute on schedule; it has not [#A]

  • Sub: Mechanism: Greek permitting timelines, Romanian grid-connection delays, and supply-chain lead times on wind/solar components have each extended projects beyond plan [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: ❌ Implied FCF guidance is structurally overoptimistic; the implicit assumption of working capital neutrality has been wrong in 4 of 4 periods; Greek state receivables (PPC is a significant creditor of state-owned entities) accumulate through energy price cycles [#A]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue guidance is directionally honest but imprecise; management has acknowledged commodity price uncertainty and provided ranges rather than point estimates; this is appropriate calibration for a partially merchant utility [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EPS: EPS is not formally guided; implied EPS has broadly tracked EBITDA beats, but the conversion from EBITDA to EPS has been variable owing to interest cost surprises (hedging gains/losses on hybrid debt) and minority interest complexity post-Romania consolidation [#I]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA: 4/4 periods at or above guided range; this consistency is notable for a utility with meaningful commodity exposure; the sandbagging is real but not aggressive — average beat of 5-8% not 15-20% [#A]

  • Miss consistency — FCF/CapEx: 4/4 periods below implied FCF guidance; 5/5 periods CapEx overrun; these are not isolated misses, they are structural patterns [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No observable shift in the sandbagging/over-promising asymmetry; EBITDA sandbagging has been consistent since 2020; CapEx overrun pattern predates FY2021 [#I]

  • Improving accuracy — none: Guidance accuracy on CapEx and FCF has not improved despite repeated overruns; management has not tightened the methodology or expanded the range to reflect realistic upside-downside [#I]

  • Magnitude — beat (EBITDA): Average +5-8% above guided midpoint; modest, not aggressive sandbagging; peer median for European mid-cap utilities is +2-3% beat on EBITDA per [AS-Morgan Stanley] European utilities coverage note [#A]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Management's guidance rests on a layered stack of assumptions across energy pricing, RES execution, regulatory environment, and working capital recovery; multiple assumptions carry meaningful sensitivity.

  • J-front: The assumption stack is internally coherent but execution-dependent at multiple links; the FCF bridge from EBITDA to cash specifically requires simultaneous achievement of CapEx target + receivables recovery + tariff normalisation — joint probability is materially lower than any single assumption [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes normalised Greek wholesale power price of ~€80-100/MWh for FY25 — broadly consistent with forward curves but above the FY24 average of approximately €75-85/MWh; a €10/MWh decline would reduce EBITDA by approximately €40-60m given thermal generation exposure [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Greek retail market share assumed stable at ~60-65%; regulatory reform has reduced exit barriers but PPC has maintained dominant share; incremental churn risk is low in the near term [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Romanian and Bulgarian RES markets assumed to deliver government-supported feed-in tariff contracts on new capacity additions; feed-in premium contracts are contractually locked for initial 15 years, providing high revenue predictability once operational [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Lignite cost rundown is a key FY25-27 margin assumption; management assumes lignite-fired generation declines from ~15% of generation mix to <5% by FY27; each 5pp reduction lowers fuel cost by approximately €60-80m [AS-Goldman Sachs] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage from RES additions is the primary margin expansion driver; RES EBITDA margin is ~65-70% vs group average ~30%; accretion from RES mix shift is mechanically significant if build targets are met [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Distribution and supply margins assume regulatory tariff framework stability; Greek regulator conducts periodic tariff reviews; an adverse review could compress distribution EBITDA by €50-80m [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed ~22-24% (Greek statutory 22% + Romanian/Bulgarian local taxes); no unusual deferred tax reversals or credits assumed; stable assumption, low sensitivity [#I]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Management assumes net debt/EBITDA declines from ~2.8x (FY24 est.) to ≤2.5x (FY27 target); this requires FCF generation to outpace CapEx; given FCF miss pattern, this leverage target may slip [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Input cost exposure (gas for backup/peaking) partially hedged; management assumes gas hedging programme covers 70-80% of FY25 exposure at costs consistent with FY24 achieved rates; no shock assumed [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: EUR-reporting with EUR-denominated revenues and costs; minimal direct FX sensitivity; Romanian/Bulgarian operations in EUR or EUR-pegged currencies; FX risk low [#I]
