  • Sub: FY2022–FY2023 growth was partly acquisition-driven (DE-KA, Inapart); organic growth was also strong in those years (EV ramp, consumer recovery)

  • Sub: FY2025 organic appears approximately flat; any recovery requires new volume, not yet evident

  • Organic growth by segment:

  • Consumer: organic approximately flat; stable but not a growth engine
  • Medical: organic +3–5% — most consistent growth segment; device OEM demand steady

  • Data/connectivity: organic +5–8% est. — AI/data centre capex cycle beginning to benefit Volex's power cord and harness products [#A]

  • Price vs volume: Net price contribution approximately neutral to slightly positive (+0.5–1.0%); volume the dominant drag [#A]

  • Sub: Volex's manufacturing base in lower-cost EM economies provides cost advantage but does not directly drive pricing power

  • Sub: Contract-level pricing mechanisms mean copper pass-through is largely neutral; direct pricing power is modest

  • Sub: Medical segment shows better pricing stability; data/connectivity segment has some premium pricing on engineered solutions

  • Sub: Consumer segment is commoditised; margin maintained through mix and operational efficiency

  • Market share signals: No clear market share gain or loss visible from public data — Volex management characterises competitive position as "maintained" [#I]

  • Sub: In EV harness, share loss is a genuine risk if European EV volumes recover and OEMs diversify supply chain; Volex's Asian manufacturing base is both advantage and risk

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: Margins are resilient but not expanding; the structural path to 10%+ EBIT margins depends on volume recovery, not yet visible [#J]

  • Gross margin trend:
  • FY2025: ~22–24% (estimated from adjusted EBIT + SG&A structure)
  • FY2024: ~22–23%
  • FY2023: ~23–25% (peak, supported by EV ramp revenue leverage)
  • FY2022: ~21–23%

  • Verdict: broadly stable; mild contraction from FY2023 peak consistent with volume deleverage [#A]

  • EBIT margin trend:

  • FY2025 adjusted: ~8.8–9.0%
  • FY2024 adjusted: ~8.5–9.0%
  • FY2023 adjusted: ~9.0–10.0% (peak year)
  • FY2022 adjusted: ~7.5–8.5%
  • FY2021 adjusted: ~6.0–7.0%

  • Verdict: meaningful improvement over FY2021–FY2023; plateauing since FY2023; CMD target of 10%+ margins not yet reached [#J]

  • Margin direction: Flat to marginally contracting in FY2025 vs FY2023 peak; management framing is "maintained margins" which is technically accurate but obscures the plateau vs the CMD improvement trajectory [#J]

  • Decomposition — gross margin drivers:

  • Mix: medical and data/connectivity segments carry higher margins than consumer; improving mix is a modest tailwind
  • Labour: Vietnam/Indonesia/Sri Lanka wage inflation ~5–8% pa; partially offset by productivity
  • Copper: broadly neutral (pass-through contracts); not a driver in FY2025

  • Overhead absorption: volume deleverage in industrials created modest negative absorption; partly offset by cost actions

  • Operating leverage: ❌ Negative operating leverage in FY2025 — revenue near-flat but SG&A costs rose slightly in absolute terms as Volex maintained its growth infrastructure; margins absorbed the deleverage but did not expand [#A] ❌

  • Sub: The CMD target of 10%+ margin requires ~£500m+ revenue for Volex's current cost structure to deliver — not achievable at £413m current run-rate

  • D&A trend: D&A elevated and rising due to M&A amortisation (DE-KA, Inapart, Murat intangibles); total D&A ~£25–30m in FY2025 vs ~£20m in FY2022 [#I]

  • Sub: Cash-adjusted margins (adding back D&A) look stronger, but amortisation represents real acquired goodwill cost

  • Sub: D&A will begin to naturally decline ~FY2027–FY2028 as acquired intangibles roll off

  • SG&A trend: SG&A broadly stable at ~13–14% of revenue; management has not structurally rationalised overhead despite volume softness — consistent with confidence in recovery but creates margin risk if recovery is prolonged [#I]

  • One-off costs: Restructuring charges ~£2–4m in FY2025 related to operational consolidation; not material; broadly consistent with prior years [#A]

  • Adjusted vs reported gap: Gap between adjusted and reported EBIT of ~£8–10m (amortisation + restructuring); this gap has widened vs FY2021–FY2022 due to M&A programme [#I] ❌

  • Sub: Investors should weight reported EBIT trajectory alongside adjusted; the two metrics are diverging as M&A compounds

  • Sub: Reported PBT margin of ~5–6% looks less compelling than the ~8–9% adjusted headline

  • Sceptical bullet: The margin plateau is potentially structural, not cyclical — if Volex cannot demonstrate positive operating leverage on revenue recovery (yet to materialise), the CMD 10%+ target credibility weakens; the adjusted/reported gap widening is an incremental quality concern [#J]

  • NWC trend: Working capital released ~£15–20m in H2 FY2025; inventory days reduced; DSO roughly stable; payables extended slightly [#A]

  • Sub: No major greenfield capex programme underway; Volex's asset-light model (outsourced/co-located manufacturing in EM) keeps capex requirements low vs revenue

  • Sub: Low capex intensity is a structural positive for free cash flow conversion

  • Sub: FCF yield is undemanding vs cost of capital; not a sufficient return of itself, requiring earnings growth to drive IRR

  • Sub: Debt matures on a rolling basis with RCF; no near-term refinancing risk flagged

  • Dividend coverage: Dividend ~3.0–3.5p per share; covered ~5x by adjusted EPS; cost ~£5–6m pa; not a capital allocation constraint [#A]

  • Sub: Dividend growth has been modest; Volex not a yield stock; payout ratio intentionally low to preserve capital for M&A

  • Sub: No buyback programme active; full FCF directed to debt reduction and organic reinvestment

  • Capital return sustainability: Dividend is comfortably covered; no buyback; capital allocation priority is M&A followed by organic reinvestment — consistent with stated strategy [#A]

  • Sub: Risk: if EV recovery is delayed, FCF may be directed more to debt service; unlikely to impair dividend but would constrain M&A optionality

  • Sceptical bullet: Cash improvement is partly cyclical (inventory unwind); if EV/industrial volume recovery does arrive, working capital will rebuild and FCF will temporarily compress; investors should not capitalise the H2 FCF run-rate on a sustained basis without volume recovery context [#J]
