B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What it does. Cenergy Holdings SA is a Brussels-headquartered industrial holding company operating through two wholly owned subsidiaries: Hellenic Cables, a leading European manufacturer of extra-high-voltage subsea and land power cables, and Corinth Pipeworks, a producer of large-diameter steel pipes for energy infrastructure
2.
How it makes money. The group sells engineered, project-specific cable and pipe systems under multi-year bespoke contracts to utilities, grid operators, offshore wind developers and energy majors; Cables generated 71% of FY25 revenue and Steel Pipes 29%
3.
Primary market and geography. Manufacturing is concentrated in Corinth, Thiva and Eleonas in Greece, a telecoms-cable plant in Bucharest, Romania, and a steel-pipe plant in Thisvi, Greece; a new land-cables plant is under construction in Baltimore, Maryland, and a UK pipe mill was acquired at Hartlepool in 2026
4.
Listing structure. Cenergy is secondary-listed on the Athens Exchange (CENER-GR) and Euronext Brussels (CENER-BB); parent Viohalco SA holds a controlling 69.71% of voting rights following two successive dilutions from 79.78% via a 2024 capital increase and 2025 placements
5.
Financial character, scale. FY25 group revenue was EUR 2,061m (up 15% year on year), adjusted EBITDA EUR 348m (up 28%), and net profit EUR 194m (up 39%); net debt was EUR 204m, 0.6x adjusted EBITDA, following a EUR 200m October 2024 capital increase
6.
Financial character, trajectory. Revenue has nearly doubled since FY2021 (EUR 1,054m to EUR 2,061m), adjusted EBITDA margin has expanded from 13.7% in Q1 2024 to a record 19.7% in Q1 2026, and return on capital employed reached 27.6% in 2025, ahead of Prysmian's 14.0% and Nexans' 21.3%
7.
Segment mix and backlog. The order backlog stood at approximately EUR 3.3bn to EUR 3.4bn entering 2026, split across grid interconnections, offshore wind and other programmes; the backlog is 100% of revenue visibility for the group's bespoke contract model
8.
Investment style implications. The profile is a change-and-growth industrial compounder riding a structural grid-investment and electrification supercycle, with a single large greenfield capital project (Baltimore) as the primary forward swing factor, rather than a pure defensive utility-adjacent holding
9.
Balance-sheet character. Leverage is conservative at 0.6x net debt to adjusted EBITDA, down from 1.8x in FY23, funded in part by the 2024 capital raise; free cash flow turned mildly negative in FY25 (approximately EUR -15m reported) on working-capital build and elevated capex
10.
Ownership and liquidity. Free float rose from 20.2% to 28.5% after the 2024 capital increase; Viohalco's residual 69.71% stake means Cenergy remains a controlled subsidiary rather than a widely held independent company
