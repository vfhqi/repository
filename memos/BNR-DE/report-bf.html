EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business Foundation and Divisional Strategy Brenntag operates as the undisputed global market leader in chemical and ingredients distribution , commanding approximately five percent of a highly fragmented global market while maintaining a physical footprint of 600 sites across more than 70 countries serving over 180,000 customers .

The business model captures value through massive logistics scale and repackaging infrastructure that economically services smallorder accounts–averaging EUR 4,000 per transaction –that bulk chemical producers cannot profitably reach directly.

Management formally abandoned divisional separation plans in 2025 , preserving an integrated corporate structure where Essentials (commodity-heavy, representing 70% of gross profit ) and Specialties (formulation-driven, 30% of gross profit ) share logistics infrastructure, ERP systems, and supplier relationships while pursuing distinct commercial strategies.

The strategic reversal avoided estimated annual dis-synergies of EUR 90-120 million and enabled streamlined governance through a two-member Board of Management under CEO Jens Birgersson and CFO Thomas Reisten, accelerating decision-making velocity and commercial execution through a newly established Executive Committee that places regional business units in direct contact with corporate leadership.

The operational division between Essentials and Specialties addresses fundamentally different market dynamics : Essentials relies on asset-heavy last-mile logistics with rapid commodity pricing pass-through across industrial manufacturing sectors, while Specialties leverages 70+ innovation and application centers to provide technical formulation services securing exclusive supplier mandates in life sciences, material sciences, and performance ingredients verticals. Competitive Moat and Structural Advantages Brenntag's competitive position rests on massive physical scale and extreme customer fragmentation , with the top ten customer accounts representing less than 5% of operating gross profit , insulating pricing power from single-account negotiation leverage and credit concentration risk across business cycles.

The extensive warehouse and repackaging network creates steep barriers to entry requiring multi-decade capital investment to replicate, while regulatory compliance burdens–including chemical handling permits, FDA/USP certifications for specialty ingredients, and stringent European CSRD/CSDDD frameworks–filter out sub-scale competitors lacking dedicated compliance infrastructure and sophisticated master data systems.

Customer switching costs remain deeply bifurcated by division : commodity Essentials buyers transition vendors easily based on transactional pricing and immediate availability, whereas Specialties customers face lengthy regulatory re-qualification trials lasting up to twelve months once performance ingredients are formulated directly into product recipes, creating structural lock-in reinforced by exclusive territorial distribution mandates from premium suppliers.

The business benefits from secular outsourcing tailwinds as large chemical producers continuously offload transaction-heavy tail-end accounts to global distributors, with specialty chemical distributors historically outgrowing underlying chemical producers by 2.7% annually through mandate share gains and complexity reduction at manufacturing principals.

the moat exhibits material weaknesses relative to pure-play specialty peers : asset-heavy infrastructure creates high fixed-cost operating leverage during industrial downturns

conversion margins persistently lag competitors like IMCD (41.7%) and Azelis (42.4%) versus Brenntag's trough level of 22.8% in Q1 2026

and capital intensity from warehouse ownership and truck fleet operations permanently constrains return on capital metrics compared to asset-light formulation-focused business models that completely outsource physical logistics to third-party providers. The group maintains conservative financial leverage at exactly 2.0x Net Debt/EBITDA , providing substantial covenant cushion within investment-grade boundaries while supporting disciplined capital allocation through a EUR 1.90 per share dividend proposal (reduced 10% from prior year) and active share buyback execution deploying EUR 250.1 million during the downcycle, both backed by resilient free cash flow generation of EUR 940.8 million benefiting from countercyclical working capital release as normalizing inventory requirements freed up operating cash.

Return metrics remain structurally depressed relative to specialty competitors : ROCE contracted to 12.9% in FY2025 (11.4% after special items including EUR 142 million of goodwill impairments across Latin America and APAC), capital expenditure intensity of approximately 2.25% of sales significantly exceeds asset-light peer models below 0.5%, and working capital turnover declined from 7.6x to 7.3x as geopolitical conflicts forced occasional inventory stockpiling to buffer supply chain risks.

Peer benchmarking reveals Brenntag operates at 3.5x the revenue scale of IMCD yet captures materially lower EBITDA margins (8.6% versus 11.3%) and inferior cash conversion (35% of EBITDA versus specialty peer average of 45%), driven by heavy tangible asset bases that prevent the business from matching pure specialty platforms' mechanical compounding advantages through formula-driven margin expansion and capital-light growth trajectories. Operator Quality and Strategic Execution Management executed a decisive strategic reversal by abandoning divisional separation, streamlining governance to a lean two-member board, and intensifying commercial field time by 15-20% through sales force realignment that successfully reactivated 70 dormant high-margin accounts in targeted markets within concentrated campaign windows while defending profitability through accelerated overhead rationalization.

The Project Brenntag cost-out initiative targets EUR 300 million in annual gross savings by 2027 , supplemented by an additional EUR 200-250 million accelerated program benchmarked against a reset 2025 expense baseline, with Q1 2026 delivering EUR 27 million in structural savings tracking toward approximately EUR 150 million in calendar 2026 alongside organic headcount reduction of 1,007 positions concentrated in corporate headquarters and non-customer-facing back-office functions.

Capital allocation discipline remains intact despite earnings compression, with dividend payout exceeding normal 35-50% boundaries due to non-cash impairments while leverage was actively managed to maintain investment-grade cushion and EUR 700 million remaining share buyback authorization provides counter-cyclical deployment optionality.

Strategic acquisitions enhanced capabilities, notably the Airedale Group purchase for EUR 97.6 million adding valuable UK-based chemical dilution, blending, and formulation infrastructure to narrow the technical capability gap versus specialty pure-plays operating 80+ laboratory networks.

operator execution remains materially constrained by structural business model limitations : financial performance lacks the mechanical tailwinds of pure specialty platforms given heavy commodity exposure directly subjects Essentials earnings to global price and volume cycles

persistent operating cost inflation diluted gross cost-out savings by 80 basis points annually as personnel expenses rose as a percentage of sales

and heroic turnaround reliance on active restructuring exceeds passive industry growth capture compared to competitors benefiting from formula-driven organic expansion in formulation-locked specialty portfolios.
