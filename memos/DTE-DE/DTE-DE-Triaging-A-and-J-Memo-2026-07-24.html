Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Structure. Deutsche Telekom runs five reporting segments: Germany, United States (via T-Mobile US), Europe, Systems Solutions (B2B/T-Systems) and Group Development/headquarters
2.
Scale. Group net revenue reached EUR 119bn in FY25, outpacing Orange's EUR 41bn and Telefonica's EUR 37bn, with service revenues at roughly 83% of total group revenue
3.
Geographic concentration. The United States segment, via the T-Mobile US subsidiary, contributes approximately 66% of group net revenue, making Deutsche Telekom a transatlantic operator in substance, not a European incumbent with a US sideline
4.
Ownership. Deutsche Telekom holds a 53.6% stake in T-Mobile US as of Q1 2026, mechanically creeping toward 55-60% by 2030 through non-participation in T-Mobile buybacks
5.
State backing. The German federal government and state development bank KfW jointly hold approximately 28.3% of shares, a structural stabiliser for the register and a deterrent to activist or short pressure
6.
Financial character. FY25 adjusted EBITDA AL of EUR 44.2bn (37.1% margin) sits well above Vodafone's 29% and Telefonica's 32%, with free cash flow AL of EUR 19.5bn
6.1

7.
Leadership. Chief executive Tim Höttges has had his contract extended to the end of 2028, having delivered a 76% total shareholder return since the 2021 Capital Markets Day versus an 8% European peer average

Balance sheet. Net debt including leases stood at EUR 134bn at Q1 2026 (2.64x adjusted EBITDA leverage), below the company's 2.75x internal ceiling; S&P upgraded the long-term rating to A- in early 2026
