Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Teqnion offers a decentralized serial acquisition platform that has successfully doubled earnings per share over five years, but faces recent headwinds from organic declines and historic portfolio write-downs.
1.
Niche serial acquisition: Teqnion buys stable, profitable niche companies with fine cash flows to own and develop with a perpetual horizon. Highly decentralized management; strategic coaching from parent
2.
Compounding investment case: Dual-engine model – high-return niche acquisitions + organic performance improvement. UK banking facilities expanded to fund higher-margin UK acquisitions; well-positioned to scale earnings trajectory over next 18-36 months
3.
Sourcing execution swing: Critical swing factor = speed and margin quality of M&A execution within Teqnion Väst (UK). Required to offset persistent organic declines in domestic Swedish portfolio
4.
Robust multi-year growth: FY2025 Net Sales +15% to SEK 1,800m; EBITA +36% to SEK 203.10m. But organic sales -5%, organic EBITA -10% – heavy reliance on acquired growth
5.
Rebounding quarterly earnings: Q1 2026 Net Sales +17% to SEK 474.40m; EBITA +106% to SEK 68m. EBITA margin reached record Q1 level of 14.30%
6.
Severe underwriting failure: Costly liquidation of Reward Catering in late 2025 + SEK 73m goodwill write-down Q3 2025 significantly reduced reported EPS
7.
Positive structural verdict: Strong margin recovery in Q1 2026, successful two-region reorganization, and expanded credit facility suggest accelerated profitable growth phase ahead

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Teqnion operates a decentralized "eternal" holding structure across Nordic and UK industrial niches, protecting margins through product leadership rather than price competition.

Decentralized Operating Segments

1.
Two business areas: Teqnion Nord (Nordic operating companies) and Teqnion Väst (British Isles). Parent operates strictly as holding company for capital allocation, M&A, and governance
2.
Product-heavy revenue: Q1 2026 goods sales SEK 446.90m (94.20% of sales); services SEK 26.50m
3.
Diversified industrial products: Subsidiaries include Eloflex (electric wheelchairs), Mitab (industrial cremation systems), Edurus Gravstenar (gravestones), Norlin Polymers (contact lens mold polymers), HT Servo (Challenger 3 battle tank components)
4.
Strong value chain position: Subsidiaries occupy profit-favored positions in narrow B2B niches – typically market leaders in specific product categories with high switching costs
5.
Negligible customer concentration: No single customer >10% of consolidated net sales
6.
UK footprint growing: Sweden largest market SEK 260.70m Q1 2026; UK reached SEK 114.40m

Sourcing Discipline

7.
Global supply support: Dedicated Far East Supply Chain team in Kunming, China supports subsidiaries' procurement
8.
Disciplined acquisition criteria: Daniel Zhang (acquisition general) screens 100-150 companies/year. Criteria: post-tax earnings SEK 10-30m, >10% bottom-line profitability, high ROCE, product-led moats
9.
Pricing power focus: Strictly rejects price competition; subsidiaries target niches where pricing power is high
10.
Other side of the story: Extreme operational decentralization can limit parent visibility – allowed Reward Catering deterioration to go unnoticed until material capital loss realized

Section summary
  1. Teqnion's investment case strengthened by proven ability to double EPS over five years, supported by newly reorganized regional model to scale UK acquisitions.
1.
High-margin UK acquisition engine: Teqnion Väst (UK) delivers EBITA margin of 26.40% vs Teqnion Nord 11.60%. UK acquisitions (Birketts Bogmats, Merridale, HT Servo) highly accretive to consolidated margins
2.
Expanded bank facility: Credit facility expanded SEK 575m to SEK 1,000m in early 2026. Secured and in place
3.
Bolt-on potential: Subsidiaries executing own bolt-ons – Eloflex (French subsidiary), Avelair (acquired Cambs and Powerair), Wallmek (UK subsidiary registered)
4.
Aggressive restructuring: Cutting unprofitable tail – merged two manufacturing units, reduced headcount at 3 struggling companies, installed 4 new CEOs. Organic EBITA grew 13% in Q1 2026 despite organic sales -5%
5.
Other side: Severe operational bottleneck possible if rapid international acquisitions overwhelm central staff of 9 people, leading to underwriting errors

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Risk profile moderated by diversified niche portfolio but Reward Catering liquidation and goodwill write-downs expose underwriting vulnerabilities.
1.
Costly Reward Catering liquidation: Single most significant operational failure – acquired 2022, liquidated late 2025, demonstrating material underwriting risk
2.
Massive goodwill write-down: SEK 73m impairment Q3 2025; non-cash but severely reduced reported EPS
3.
High earnout volatility: Contingent considerations SEK 195.60m at end 2025. Earnout revaluations cause significant quarterly earnings volatility (e.g. SEK 17.90m positive gain Q2 2025)
4.
Persistent organic declines: Organic sales -5% FY2025 and -5% Q1 2026. Core Nordic portfolio exposed to cyclical macro headwinds
5.
Critical early-warning metric: Spread between pro-forma EBITA and actual FCF + organic sales growth trends in Teqnion Nord
6.
Non-cash profit "false friend": Earnout revaluation gains can mask underlying operational weakness
7.
Other side: Cohort payback highly favorable – 2021-2023 cohort companies generating 33-50% of original purchase price in FCF annually
