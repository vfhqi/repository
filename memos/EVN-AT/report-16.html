§4 — Board Composition + Independence

J-front verdict: Mixed — formal governance structure is compliant but dominant-shareholder influence on supervisory board composition structurally constrains meaningful independence

Board structure (Austrian Aktiengesetz two-tier):
- Fact: EVN operates a two-tier board structure under Austrian law — a Vorstand (executive board, §1–3 above) and an Aufsichtsrat (supervisory board); the Aufsichtsrat does not include executive directors, so the independence question applies to the supervisory board
- Board size: EVN's supervisory board comprises approximately 12 members — 8 elected by shareholders + 4 delegated by the works council (employee representatives) per Austrian co-determination law; the employee representatives are a structural feature of Austrian governance and are not evaluated as "non-independent" in the same sense as shareholder appointees
- Independence ratio: Of the 8 shareholder-elected members, independence is structurally limited by the dominant shareholders — NÖ Landes-Beteiligungsholding GmbH (Lower Austrian state holding, ~28.35% stake) and Wiener Stadtwerke (~28.35%) collectively nominate the majority of shareholder representatives; these are not "independent" directors in the Anglo-Saxon sense

Insider directors / dominant shareholder influence:
- Structural constraint: With ~56–57% combined ownership, the two dominant shareholders effectively control the composition of the supervisory board; directors associated with these shareholders are not independent by any mainstream governance definition (ISS, Glass Lewis, UK CGC)
- Consequence: Supervisory board independence ratio is likely in the 25–40% range (independent / total shareholder seats), which is below the 50%+ threshold recommended by most governance frameworks for European listed companies of this size
- Mitigant: The dominant shareholders are both Austrian public-sector entities with aligned, long-term capital interests; they are not financial sponsors seeking a quick exit or extracting related-party value; the capture risk is "governance conservatism" rather than "minority-shareholder expropriation"

Director tenure pattern:
- Long tenure: Consistent with the insider-culture of Austrian Aktiengesetz governance; supervisory board members associated with the dominant shareholders often serve long terms; public record suggests several board members have served 6–10+ years

Recent board changes L24M:
- Limited visible changes: No high-profile supervisory board changes flagged in the public record L24M; EVN's board composition is stable in the dominant-shareholder context

§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Clean-to-mixed — no material red flags; structural risk is shareholder-concentration-driven board capture and compensation opacity, not fraud or aggressive accounting

Voting rights structure:
- Single-class shares: EVN has a straightforward ordinary share structure; no dual-class voting, no founder-controlled super-voting, no golden share with special rights on public record
- Dominant-shareholder concentration: NÖ Landes-Beteiligungsholding (~28.35%), Wiener Stadtwerke (~28.35%), and EVN's own treasury shares (historically ~5–7%) mean the free float is approximately 35–40%; this is a structural governance feature — the two dominant shareholders can block special resolutions (blocking minority) and in practice align to direct the ordinary agenda
- Anti-takeover: The dominant-shareholder concentration is itself the most effective anti-takeover defence; no poison pill or specific anti-takeover provision is documented on public record beyond the structural ownership

Related-party transactions:
- Potential: The relationship between EVN and Wiener Stadtwerke (one of its dominant shareholders) involves commercial transactions — EVN's district heating and energy supply relationships in the Vienna region interact with Wiener Stadtwerke's infrastructure networks; these represent structural related-party relationships
- Assessment: No evidence of egregious related-party extraction or uncommercial transactions on public record; the transactions appear to reflect normal commercial relationships in a structurally interlinked Austrian energy market; the annual report related-party note should be reviewed at transaction level (this is a standard DD verification task)
- Lower Austria government relationship: NÖ Landes-Beteiligungsholding's majority shareholder is the Lower Austrian state government; EVN's concession relationships (electricity distribution network, water) in Lower Austria involve the regulatory/political framework of the same state government — a structural conflict-of-interest that is managed through regulatory oversight but is not zero

Audit history:
- Clean: EVN has not switched auditors in a manner that would signal a governance concern; PwC Austria (or equivalent Big Four) has historically been the group auditor; no documented material weakness or audit qualification in recent years on public record
- No restatements: No documented material financial restatement in EVN's recent public reporting history

Legal/regulatory issues:
- No material class action on record: No evidence of a major securities class action against EVN management or the company in the public record
- Austrian energy regulation: EVN operates under Energie-Control Austria (E-Control) regulatory oversight; the regulatory relationship is arms-length and professional; no evidence of ongoing adverse regulatory investigation targeting EVN management
- SEE operations: EVN's South East European generation operations (Bulgaria, North Macedonia) operate in lower-governance-quality regulatory environments; historical legacy included some political complexity in the Bulgarian power market; no current material legal/political dispute documented at EVN level in public record

Whistleblower / governance complaint signals:
- Not visible: No whistleblower case or governance complaint against EVN management surfaces in public search; Austrian corporate press does not flag governance controversies at EVN in the L3Y window

Sceptical bullet: Bear read on governance: Through the mechanism of structural related-party proximity between EVN, the Lower Austrian state government, and Wiener Stadtwerke — all three operating in interlinked utility and regulatory roles — minority shareholders cannot independently verify that EVN's regulatory outcomes (tariff decisions, network-investment approval, concession renewals) are arm's-length commercial outcomes rather than political settlements that benefit the dominant shareholders at the expense of the free-float; the governance red flag that would invalidate the operator-quality thesis is evidence of discriminatory regulatory decisions benefiting NÖ at EVN's expense — or conversely, of EVN receiving unfair regulatory advantage that then unwinds when political priorities shift
