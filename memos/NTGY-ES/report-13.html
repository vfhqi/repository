§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

J-front verdict: Naturgy is a metric-specific mixed tracker — mild sandbagger on EBITDA, honest tracker on CapEx, structural over-promiser on FCF (implicit guidance) [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA guidance floor set at €3.8-4.0bn range consistently below eventual outcomes in 3 of 4 recent periods — pattern is consistent with mild downside cushioning, not aggressive stretch targets [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average EBITDA beat (ex-COVID, ex-FY2022 windfall) approximately +1-3% — modest, consistent with European regulated-utility sandbagger cohort base rate of +2-5% per Barclays [#A]

  • Honest tracker — CapEx: CapEx guidance is neither sandbagged nor stretched — actuals land within ±8% of midpoint every year; management does not use CapEx guidance as a messaging tool, unlike some peers (Iberdrola has historically guided CapEx at lower end of ambition) [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF (implicit): ❌ Management's implied FCF (derived from EBITDA guidance minus guided CapEx minus stated interest/tax assumptions) has overshot actual FCF in 3 of 4 periods by 15-25%; the mechanism is that management excludes working capital swings and Latam cash repatriation lags from its implicit FCF construct, which is optimistic by design [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — Latam returns: Guided regulated-asset returns in Latam (Colombia, Argentina, Chile) have averaged 150-250bps above actual delivered returns due to tariff-reset timing lags — not disclosed as explicit guidance misses but visible in divisional EBITDA bridges [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — EPS/net income: No persistent bias in net income track record — FY2020 miss (impairments), FY2021-2023 in-line; pattern is honest-tracker with COVID outlier [#A]

  • Pattern shift: Post-2022 CMDay, management shifted to more conservative EBITDA floor guidance (explicitly lowered the range ceiling) and stopped providing annual FCF commitments — this looks like learning from 2022 FCF miss, but may also reflect increasing FCF uncertainty as regulated cash flows become harder to forecast [#A]

  • Beat consistency: EBITDA beats are consistent but not compounding — management does not ratchet guidance upward mid-year even when H1 suggests full-year beat is likely; this is a mild positive sandbagging discipline signal [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that EBITDA sandbagging cushion may be structurally narrowing — the gap between €4.0bn floor and achievable EBITDA has compressed as commodity windfall fades and Spanish network tariffs face reset; if EBITDA guidance is set at approximately the same level as expected delivery, any adverse shock directly translates to a miss rather than absorbing into a comfortable buffer [#J]
