§1 — Current Management Guidance

Centrica's current guidance framework (as of FY2024 results, February 2025) combines a point-in-time FY2025 adjusted operating profit range with a rolling medium-term capital allocation framework anchored to the "Centrica Way Forward" plan first articulated at the November 2022 Capital Markets Day and updated in February 2024.

  • FY current — verdict: Management has provided a qualitative-to-range FY2025 adjusted operating profit expectation; hard EPS and FCF point guidance remains absent, reducing comparability [#J]
  • FY current — EBIT: Adjusted operating profit guided to "broadly in line with FY2024" (£1.0-1.2bn range implied); no hard point-estimate published — range guidance is Centrica's standing practice [#A]
  • FY current — EPS: No formal EPS guidance issued; management references "underlying EPS progression" consistent with capital returns; consensus proxy for FY2025 EPS circa 12-14p (see §5) [#A]
  • FY current — FCF: FCF guided "above FY2024 level" (FY2024 FCF approximately £0.5-0.6bn after working capital); no hard number published; this vagueness is a credibility signal in itself [#J]
  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guidance £600-700m for FY2025, consistent with medium-term envelope of ~£600m p.a. as guided at the February 2024 update [#A]
  • Medium-term — N3Y (to FY2026): Three-year FCF target of £700-900m cumulative as articulated at November 2022 CMD; EPS "mid-to-high single digit CAGR" from FY2022 base; these were refreshed (modestly) in February 2024 [#A]
  • Medium-term targets: Customer services margin target of "6-8%" for British Gas Services ; Centrica Business Solutions EBIT breakeven to positive by FY2025; nuclear fleet availability target circa 70-75% for Centrica's EDF stake contribution [#A]
  • Source — investor day: November 2022 CMD was the primary medium-term guidance event; February 2024 FY2023 results presentation provided a rolling update; next CMD not formally scheduled as of May 2025 [#A]
  • Source — earnings call: FY2024 results call (February 2025) reiterated range guidance; management explicitly declined to provide a hard FY2025 EPS number citing commodity price uncertainty [#A]
  • Update timing: Annual updates at results; no interim guidance updates disclosed (Q1/Q3 trading statements provide customer number updates but not financial guidance revisions) [#A]
  • Caveat: "Subject to commodity price movements, no material acquisitions, and nuclear fleet performance" — these three caveats appear in all guidance statements and serve as broad escape valves [#A]
  • Conditional assumption: Management's FCF guidance conditionally assumes "normalised" commodity prices; the commodity hedge book (85-90% hedged L12M) provides partial insulation but hedge roll-off creates FY+1 exposure [#A]
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that range-guidance without hard EPS or FCF point estimates provides management with maximum optionality; the "broadly in line" language for FY2025 EBIT is not analytically anchored — if FY2024 came in near the bottom of its range (£1.0bn), "broadly in line" could mean £0.9-1.1bn, which spans a meaningful EPS spread [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Pattern verdict: Centrica is a commodity-conditional sandbagger — in high-price environments it appears to sandbag because commodity upsides dwarf guidance ranges; in normalising environments it is an honest tracker on profit but a structural under-deliverer on cash [#J]
  • Sandbagger signal — profit: EBIT guidance ranges are conservatively wide (typically ±20-25% around implicit midpoint), which means delivery within range reads as a beat in market perception even when near the bottom of range [#A]
  • Sandbagger signal — EPS: EPS consensus beats in 3 of 4 periods but the magnitude of "beats" is modest (+3-5%) except FY2022; Centrica is not a serial earnings over-deliverer in the way that genuine sandbagging companies are [#A]
  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: FCF is the metric where Centrica consistently over-promises (implicitly) relative to delivery; the language ("positive FCF", "above prior year") sounds reassuring but delivery consistently falls short of implicit market expectation [#J]
  • Over-promiser signal — customer retention: BGS customer retention language ("stable", "modest growth") has missed in 2 of 2 periods where quantifiable data exists; this is a small sample but directionally consistent [#A]
  • Honest tracker: CapEx guidance is Centrica's most credible metric; in-range delivery across all 4 periods suggests operational planning discipline in spend management [#A]
  • Mixed pattern — margin: Divisional margin guidance (BGS Services "6-8%", Business Solutions "breakeven to positive") is the most variable; Business Solutions EBIT trajectory has repeatedly disappointed versus medium-term language [#A]
  • Pattern shift: Pre-FY2022 (legacy Centrica) guidance posture was more volatile; post-2020 restructuring (exit of NA assets, simplified structure) has narrowed the range of surprises but not eliminated the FCF gap [#A]
  • Magnitude — beat: Excluding FY2022, average EBIT beat versus midpoint approximately +3-5%; genuinely modest — not the hallmark of deliberate sandbagging [#A]
  • Magnitude — miss — FCF: Average FCF miss versus implicit expectation approximately -15-20%; this exceeds normal working capital noise [#A]
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that what looks like "partial sandbagging" on profit is actually management benefiting from commodity price volatility that runs above planning assumptions; when commodity prices are flat-to-declining (as they are in FY2024-2026 planning period), the sandbagging cushion disappears and the FCF shortfall becomes the headline story [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Trend verdict: Guidance accuracy on EBIT has improved since FY2021 as business complexity has reduced (post-NA exit); FCF accuracy has not improved and remains the dominant residual credibility risk [#J]
  • Recent accuracy — EBIT: FY2023 and FY2024 EBIT outcomes were both within or near the guided range; this is genuine improvement versus the FY2019-2021 period when Centrica was managing multi-geography disposals with high execution variability [#A]
  • Historical accuracy — FCF: FCF guidance accuracy has not improved: FY2021 (no hard guide, weak delivery), FY2022 (commodity release flattered), FY2023 (material miss), FY2024 (marginal miss) — the trend is flat-to-deteriorating [#A]
  • Accuracy improving — CapEx: CapEx accuracy has been consistently high across the full L4 period; this reflects post-restructuring operational simplification [#A]
  • Accuracy deteriorating — customer metrics: Customer account guidance accuracy has deteriorated from "stable" (FY2022) to consecutive misses (FY2023, FY2024) — a trend that matters for the medium-term revenue base [#A]
  • Volatility driver — commodity: The FY2022 outlier is entirely macro-driven; in a normalised commodity environment (FY2023-2025), guidance volatility is driven by execution on cost-out and customer retention [#A]
  • Volatility driver — nuclear provisioning: Decommissioning provision charges (Centrica's 20% EDF stake) are irregular in timing and impact FCF; this creates episodic FCF variance independent of operating performance [#A]
  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx — in-range in 4 of 4 periods; a clear standout [#J]
  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF — miss or shortfall in 3 of 4 periods; the persistent gap [#J]
  • Volatility driver — complexity: Business Solutions (B2B energy services) remains a complexity source; the margin guidance for this segment has been consistently optimistic relative to delivery [#A]
  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that the post-FY2022 "improvement" in EBIT accuracy is partly a function of management guiding conservatively after the commodity spike normalised; if energy prices re-spike or collapse sharply (outside the hedge book), the apparent accuracy improvement could reverse rapidly [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Guidance credibility depends on three assumption clusters: commodity price realisation, cost-out delivery, and customer retention. All three have shown historical variability and are not independently controllable by management.

  • Assumptions verdict: The assumption stack behind FY2025-2026 guidance is moderately aggressive — individually plausible, but 2-3 simultaneous adverse movements could produce a 15-25% downside to management's implied EPS trajectory [#J]
  • Revenue assumption — customer: BGS energy supply customer accounts assumed "broadly stable" in FY2025 guidance language; actual trend is -3 to -4% p.a. in FY2023 and FY2024; management's assumption is behind the observed trend [#A]
  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Energy supply pricing assumes regulated tariff cap (Ofgem) tracking at or above cost; management has guided that the price cap environment post-2024 is "supportive" — this is a regulatory assumption not a commercial one [#A]
  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance is explicitly "organic, no material acquisitions" — consistent with all guidance language since the NA disposal [#A]
  • Margin assumption — cost: Medium-term guidance embeds £150-200m cumulative cost reduction by FY2026 ("Centrica Way Forward" efficiency programme); H1 delivery is on-track per FY2024 results but full programme delivery remains an assumption [#A]
  • Opex reset assumption: Business Solutions margin improvement assumes SG&A reduction and contract mix improvement; historical delivery on this segment's cost guidance has been poor (cost-out targets missed in FY2022 and FY2023) [#A]
  • Margin assumption — leverage: BGS Services margin of "6-8%" target assumes volume recovery in home services (boiler install, maintenance); UK housing market slowdown could pressure volume assumptions [#A]
  • EPS assumption — tax: UK Energy Profits Levy remains the principal tax risk; management guides to an effective tax rate of approximately 40-45%; any EPL extension or rate change would materially affect EPS [#A]
  • EPS assumption — share count: Buyback programme (£300-500m authorised annually) reduces share count; management's EPS growth assumption embeds continued buyback execution; suspension of buybacks (e.g., if FCF disappoints) would remove EPS uplift [#A]
  • Commodity hedge assumption: 85-90% of gas/power exposure hedged L12M forward; FY2026+ is less hedged; management's FCF guidance for N2Y+ assumes commodity prices stabilise near current forward curves [#A]
  • Macro assumption — commodity: FY2025 guidance is approximately 85% hedged; FY2026 approximately 50-60% hedged; the unhedged portion creates earnings sensitivity to gas/power price movements [#A]
  • Customer base assumption: Nuclear contribution (20% of EDF UK fleet) assumed at 70-75% availability; unplanned outages (EPR delays, AGR extensions) represent an uncontrolled earnings variable [#A]
