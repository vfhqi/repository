2.
Raised capacity forecasts: Deutsche Bank notes that Low-NA EUV capacity will expand to at least 60 units in 2026 and over 80 units in 2027, driven by robust advanced node ramps
3.
Accelerating logic orders: ING indicates that advanced logic and memory makers face tight capacity and are aggressively expanding fabs to support AI demand

Sustained demand momentum: Morgan Stanley highlights that capacity concerns are overdone as customers are pulling in orders to address a supply-limited market

6.
Defining EUV ASP driver: Increasing average selling prices of EUV scanners directly drives the gross margin line, with Low-NA EUV ASP projected to rise 11.00% in FY2027 and 40.00% from 2026 to 2030

Productive scanner upgrades: This driver would fail if technical bottlenecks in source-power scaling prevent ASML from achieving target throughput specifications, eroding its value-based pricing leverage

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by highly credible geopolitical and regulatory trade restrictions that could limit ASML's Chinese revenues, offset partially by robust structural demand in democratic jurisdictions .

Geopolitical and Regulatory Risks

1.
Tighter Chinese export controls: The proposed MATCH Act represents a direct risk to net system sales, as it threatens to completely ban all immersion deep ultraviolet scanner shipments to Chinese customers, potentially wiping out up to 20.00% of ASML's guided FY2026 revenue
2.
Damaging supply chain disruptions: Further trade restrictions could trigger severe retaliation from Beijing, such as restricting critical rare earth exports beyond the November 2026 window, serving as the single most dangerous failure mode that could permanently impair Chinese system revenues

Technology and Operational Bottlenecks

3.
Postponed leading-edge integration: Technical delays or high tool costs of €400.00M may prompt key customers like TSMC to postpone High-NA EUV integration, forcing them to rely on low-NA systems and limiting ASML's gross margin expansion to its 56.00%-60.00% target
4.
Deceptive Chinese demand: Rapid short-term growth in immersion sales to China in 2026 represents a dangerous "false friend," as it is likely driven by pre-ban stocking rather than structural demand, masking a severe revenue cliff in FY2027
5.
Highly correlated early-warnings: The most critical early-warning signal to monitor is the monthly China and Taiwan customs lithography import data, which have a high 0.89 and 0.75 R-squared correlation with ASML's reported revenues

Market Dynamics and Shorts

6.
Negligible short interest: Research indicates there is currently no published short-seller report or unusually high short interest in ASML, reflecting the market's broad respect for the company's monopoly power
7.
Other side of the story:
8.
Robust regional backfilling: The bear case on China export controls is significantly overstated because massive, accelerating EUV and immersion demand from TSMC, Samsung, and Intel in Taiwan, South Korea, and the United States will fully backfill any lost Chinese revenues over the next 18 to 24 months
