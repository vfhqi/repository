Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Radiotherapy capital equipment maker. Elekta designs, manufactures and services linear accelerators (linacs) and software for cancer radiation therapy, competing head-to-head with Varian (part of Siemens Healthineers)
2.
Duopoly structure, junior partner. The global radiotherapy hardware market is a consolidating duopoly; Varian commands approximately 60% share versus Elekta's low-to-mid 30s, per an industry expert channel check
3.
Revenue scale. Latest-twelve-month net sales of roughly SEK 16,718m, with organic growth averaging near 1% over the last three years, well below the sector's mid-to-high single-digit secular growth rate
4.
Margin profile. Adjusted EBIT margin improved 70 basis points to 12.3% in the most recent full year, but this lags Varian's reported 18.8-19.4% margin by 400-540 basis points
5.
Balance sheet. Net debt to EBITDA of a conservative 1.24x, materially better than Siemens Healthineers' 3.1x; cash conversion at 96% comfortably exceeds management's 70% target
6.
Segment mix. Hardware (linacs, notably the flagship Unity magnetic-resonance linear accelerator and the newer Evo computed-tomography linear accelerator) plus a growing software and services attachment layer at 80% gross margins
7.
Geography. Developed-market exposure concentrated in the United States (recently declining) and China (recovering from an anti-corruption tender freeze); manufacturing footprint split between Crawley, UK and Beijing, China
8.
Leadership. A near-total C-suite turnover completed in the last 12 months: new chief executive (September 2025), new chief financial officer (by March 2026), Chief Commercial Officer role eliminated, Chief People Officer ousted
9.
Investment-style implication. This reads as a change-from-current turnaround case, not a quality compounder: the thesis rests on whether the new team's cost programme and Evo product cycle can outrun an advantaged competitor
10.
Employee base signal. Employee Net Promoter Score collapsed to 8 in FY24/25 from 28 in FY21/22, following a roughly 10% workforce reduction, an unusual signal for a business asking staff to execute a turnaround
