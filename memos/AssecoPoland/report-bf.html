Strong entry barriers: new entrants face formidable obstacles due to complex local regulations and entrenched client relationships across public sectors
2.
Massive system scale: Poland's social security KSI ZUS system handles mission-critical transaction volumes for nearly 30 million domestic citizens daily
3.
Regulatory knowledge lock: replacing proprietary public software requires deep compliance expertise that takes years for new competitors to accumulate
4.
High switching costs: public sector and utility clients experience severe operational risks when attempting to replace deeply embedded custom legacy systems
5.
Long sales cycles: winning new public hospital contracts typically requires three to four years of rigorous business development
6.
Decadelong customer longevity: core relationships with major public administration and healthcare entities typically span 10 to 15 years historically
7.
Limited exit paths: structural client exits are highly constrained because specialized proprietary architectures lack standardized alternatives in Central Europe
8.
Hard database reliance: major domestic commercial and cooperative banking platforms continue to depend on Asseco's back-office database interactions
