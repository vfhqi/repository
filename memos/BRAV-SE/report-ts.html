Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Bravida is a highly cash-generative, late-cycle compounder recovering from cyclical market weakness, offering significant margin self-help potential alongside structural growth drivers.
1.
Essential building life cycle partner: The company earns revenue by acting as a complete technology partner for buildings, splitting its income between slightly cyclical installation projects and highly recurring, predictable service and maintenance contracts
2.
Multi-year compounding potential: The core investment case rests on the company's ability to leverage a stabilising macroeconomic backdrop to re-accelerate organic growth while expanding margins through stringent project selection and aggressive structural cost rationalisation
3.
Project selection and pricing discipline: The single most important swing factor for earnings over the next 12 to 24 months is the company's ability to enforce strict margin-over-volume bidding, thereby avoiding cash-draining, complex fixed-price contracts in a highly competitive pricing environment
4.
Historical compounding track record: The company has historically demonstrated robust compounding, delivering an average 9% revenue CAGR and 10% earnings CAGR in the nine years following its 2015 initial public offering
5.
Recent cyclical compression: During the 2024 and 2025 fiscal years, the company faced severe cyclical headwinds and margin compression, evidenced by a 5% organic revenue contraction in 2025 and an EBITA margin dip to 5.9%
6.
Acute reputational and legal vulnerabilities: The most critical risk to the investment case is potential contagion from recent public sector over-invoicing allegations, which could trigger widespread contract cancellations and severely damage the company's local market credibility
7.
First-look verdict: Yes, the stock warrants deeper investigation because the underlying service business remains highly resilient and the shares offer an attractive entry point into a proven serial acquirer at the bottom of its earnings cycle

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. The business model balances cyclical project installations with highly recurring, defensive service revenues, underpinned by extreme customer diversification and decentralised local branch execution.

Business operations and revenue mix

1.
Dual-engine revenue model: The company operates two distinct but complementary segments, with service accounting for approximately 46% to 49% of group revenue and new installations making up the remainder
2.
Counter-cyclical service stability: The service division focuses on essential, non-discretionary property maintenance, generating highly predictable, recurring cash flows that act as a natural hedge during broader construction market downturns
3.
Comprehensive technical capabilities: Operations span core disciplines including electrical, heating, plumbing, and HVAC systems, alongside higher-margin specialist niches such as building automation, security, fire safety, and electric vehicle charging
4.
Value chain positioning: The company operates strictly as an integrator and installer, sourcing materials from third-party manufacturers and passing those input costs through to clients, thereby minimising direct commodity price exposure and inventory risk
5.
Internal operational standardisation: Operations are governed by 'The Bravida Way', a proprietary, highly standardised management framework that enforces uniform procurement, IT systems, and project execution metrics across all 330 local branches

Market structure and competitive advantage

6.
Extreme customer fragmentation: The business serves a massively diversified base of over 91,000 customers, ensuring that no single customer accounts for more than 5% of total consolidated income
7.
Insulated contract risk profile: The company actively mitigates large-scale project execution risk by keeping its average contract size remarkably low, with approximately 85% to 89% of all orders valued below SEK 50 million
8.
Industry consolidation engine: The Nordic technical installation market remains highly fragmented, creating a target-rich environment for serial acquirers to deploy capital and extract immediate procurement synergies
9.
Durable scale advantages: As the largest integrated multi-technical provider in the Nordics, the company leverages its sheer size to secure preferential pricing from material suppliers and bid on complex, multi-disciplinary national infrastructure projects that smaller local peers cannot handle
10.
Favourable end-market exposure: The company has successfully pivoted away from the depressed residential new-build market, increasingly targeting robust institutional demand in hospitals, prisons, data centers, and defense facilities
11.
Other side of the story: While scale theoretically provides purchasing leverage, the inherently decentralised nature of the installation business means local relationships often trump corporate scale, allowing smaller, nimble competitors to routinely undercut the company on pricing and poach key local talent

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The bull case strengthens on the premise that an impending macroeconomic recovery will amplify aggressive internal cost-cutting, driving substantial margin expansion alongside a reinvigorated M&A strategy.

Margin self-help and structural turnarounds

1.
Danish operational turnaround: The company has successfully executed a massive overhaul of its previously struggling Danish division, rapidly shifting EBITA margins from a depressed 1% in early 2024 to a robust 5% in Q1 2026
2.
Swedish structural rationalisation: The ongoing merger of the company's three Swedish divisions into a single unified entity is projected to strip out SEK 65 million in annual structural costs, directly bolstering the bottom line by 2027
3.
Plausibility read on cost savings: High. The company has a demonstrated track record of executing brutal but effective cost controls during downturns, and the upfront restructuring charges for the Swedish integration have already been fully provisioned

Structural end-market tailwinds

4.
Energy efficiency mandates: The European regulatory push for building decarbonisation creates a massive, non-discretionary pipeline of renovation work, directly benefiting the company's core HVAC, electrical, and building automation segments
5.
Specialist infrastructure expansion: The company is aggressively capturing high-margin, complex work in rapidly expanding niche sectors, specifically securing long-term installation contracts for military defense facilities and hyperscale data centers
6.
Plausibility read on end-market tailwinds: Medium. While the structural demand is undeniable, the company must successfully transition its workforce's skill sets to handle these highly complex technical projects without incurring execution missteps

Capital deployment and consolidation

7.
M&A flywheel re-acceleration: With the balance sheet heavily de-levered, the company is perfectly positioned to resume its role as the premier aggregator in the fragmented Nordic market, targeting an additional 5% to 7% in annual acquired growth
8.
Compounding probability: The drivers are largely independent; even if the macroeconomic recovery stalls, the internal cost-out programmes and the deployment of idle balance-sheet cash into accretive bolt-on acquisitions will mathematically support EPS growth
9.
Other side of the story: The entire investment case requires the company to accurately price complex forward contracts; if inflation unexpectedly spikes or wage demands surge, the fixed-price nature of the installation backlog will quickly incinerate the projected margin improvements

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Attractive absolute multiples: The company currently trades on a reasonably undemanding 12-month forward P/E of approximately 14.0x and an EV/EBITA multiple of 11.3x to 11.5x based on 2026 estimates
2.
Favourable peer benchmarking: At 11.5x EV/EBITA, the stock trades at a slight premium to struggling local construction peers but sits at a meaningful 10% to 15% discount to proven European serial acquirers like SPIE

Historical discount: The current EV/EBITA multiple remains roughly 5% to 10% below the company's own 10-year historical average, indicating that the market has not fully digested the recent operational turnaround
4.
Generous shareholder yields: Investors are currently compensated by an attractive free-cash-flow yield of approximately 8% and a dividend yield approaching 4%, securely covered by recurring service revenues
5.
Broker price targets: Sell-side consensus remains broadly supportive, with median price targets clustered between SEK 100 and SEK 120, implying a healthy 10% to 25% upside from current trading levels
6.
Other side of the story: The market is arguably pricing the stock correctly as a low-growth, late-cycle construction derivative; if the anticipated multi-year installation recovery fails to materialise, the current 14x P/E multiple will quickly look like a value trap

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by acute reputational threats from invoicing scandals and the perpetual danger of massive margin bleed on complex, fixed-price mega-projects.

Public sector reputational damage

1.
Over-invoicing allegations: The company faces severe scrutiny following a 2024 police report filed by Region Skåne, supported by an external EY audit, alleging systematic over-invoicing on public service contracts
2.
Contagion mechanism: If the Swedish authorities find evidence of widespread fraud, the company could face catastrophic exclusion from all municipal and state-level public procurement bidding, instantly wiping out a massive double-digit percentage of its core revenue base
3.
False friend caution: The company's recent internal investigations concluded that irregularities were isolated; however, this is a dangerous false friend, as public sector clients may preemptively blacklist the firm regardless of legal outcomes simply to avoid political optics

Project execution and financial lock-ups

4.
Fixed-price project bleed: The company routinely takes on massive, multi-year installation projects (e.g., hospitals, data centers) on fixed-price terms; any failure to accurately forecast labour hours or material costs translates directly into unrecoverable margin obliteration
5.
Trapped receivables disputes: The balance sheet is currently masking deep operational friction, with the company locked in aggressive legal disputes in Norway and Denmark that are holding up to SEK 550 million in contested receivables
6.
Early warning signal: The most critical metric to monitor is the quarterly ratio of unbilled contract assets to total receivables; any sudden spike indicates that clients are disputing project milestones and withholding vital cash
7.
Other side of the story: Despite the headline noise surrounding the Skåne scandal and trapped cash, the sheer mathematical diversification of 91,000 distinct customers ensures that no single project failure or regional dispute can fundamentally break the wider group's profitability

1.
CFO transition: Petra Vranjes assumed the role of Group CFO in May 2025, replacing long-tenured Åsa Neving; Vranjes brings a background in strategic finance from Telia and Ericsson, signalling a renewed focus on rigorous corporate governance and cash extraction
2.
M&A leadership overhaul: In March 2026, the company appointed Alexandra Johansson as the new Head of M&A, replacing retiring veteran Magnus Liljefors, paving the way for a more aggressive, next-generation approach to market consolidation
3.
Swedish operational consolidation: Lars Täuber was appointed Head of Sweden in January 2026 to lead a brutal restructuring that collapses three regional divisions into one, reflecting management's urgency to arrest margin bleed in its largest market
