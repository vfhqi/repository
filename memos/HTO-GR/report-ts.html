Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

Section summary
  1. OTE represents a highly cash-generative, pure-play Greek telecom incumbent, offering an attractive shareholder remuneration profile alongside robust mobile and ICT growth, despite emerging fixed-line infrastructure competition.
1.
Clear business model: OTE provides integrated fixed, mobile, TV, and enterprise IT services to Greek households and businesses, monetizing connectivity through sticky monthly subscriptions and large-scale public sector tech contracts
2.
Core investment case: The stock offers a highly visible, cash-generative profile supported by premium 5G and FTTH networks, enabling a reliable earnings stream that fully funds a sector-leading shareholder return policy
3.
Critical swing factor: The ultimate severity of the emerging retail fiber overbuild by energy incumbent PPC will dictate medium-term fixed-line pricing power and wholesale revenue resilience
4.
Multi-year performance: The company has maintained remarkable balance sheet discipline, operating with a 2025 net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 0.4x while generating €542.8m in adjusted free cash flow
5.
Recent momentum: First-quarter 2026 results demonstrated a robust 4.9% year-over-year revenue expansion, powered by a 76.3% surge in System Solutions and continued postpaid mobile additions
6.
Primary risk mechanism: A heavily subsidized, aggressive price war initiated by infrastructure challenger PPC could fracture the historically rational Greek telecom market, eroding OTE's high-margin broadband base and stalling earnings growth
7.
First-look verdict: Yes, the company warrants deeper investigation, as its entrenched mobile dominance and unlevered balance sheet provide a thick margin of safety against the widely publicized fixed-line infrastructure threats

Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. OTE dominates the Greek telecom market with superior 5G and FTTH infrastructure, operating a highly profitable, converged fixed-mobile model supported by a rapidly growing IT solutions segment.

Market positioning and segments

1.
Converged service provider: The company operates as the incumbent Greek telecom operator, delivering a balanced mix of mobile voice and data, retail fixed broadband, pay-TV, and wholesale network access
2.
Enterprise ICT expansion: Beyond traditional connectivity, OTE functions as a leading systems integrator for complex public and private sector digital transformation projects, diversifying its revenue base into high-growth technology consulting
3.
Pure-play geographic focus: Following the recent strategic divestment of its underperforming Romanian mobile unit, the group now operates exclusively within the Greek market, simplifying its operational footprint and eliminating external cash drag

Competitive advantages and scale

4.
Superior network infrastructure: The company possesses the country's most extensive fiber and mobile architecture, including Greece's only commercially available 5G Stand-Alone network, which provides a structural quality advantage over local challengers
5.
Structural mobile dominance: Operating within a rational three-player mobile market alongside Vodafone and Nova, OTE retains the leading market share, enabling inflation-linked price adjustments and a lucrative transition from prepaid to postpaid billing
6.
Global parentage benefits: As a majority-owned subsidiary of Deutsche Telekom, OTE leverages the parent's massive procurement scale, common product platforms, and established global brand equity to reduce capital costs and differentiate its offerings
7.
High-barrier TV content: The pay-TV segment benefits from exclusive sports broadcasting rights and recent content-sharing agreements with rival Nova, locking in subscribers through highly desirable, proprietary entertainment bundles

Industry structure and economics

8.
Recurring revenue foundations: The core economic engine relies on sticky, monthly subscription contracts for bundled household communications, generating predictable cash flows that insulate the business from immediate macroeconomic shocks
9.
Wholesale market dynamics: OTE historically monetized its legacy copper network by leasing capacity to competitors, a high-margin revenue stream that is now structurally declining as rivals deploy their own independent fiber architectures
10.
Fragmented IT landscape: The domestic enterprise IT market remains highly fragmented and supported by temporary European Recovery and Resilience Facility funds, providing a lucrative but potentially cyclical runway for OTE's systems integration arm
11.
Other side of the story: The historical monopoly advantage in fixed-line infrastructure is permanently fracturing, as state-backed energy providers and private equity-funded challengers aggressively lay parallel fiber networks that bypass OTE's toll gates entirely

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 12 findings

Section summary
  1. A multi-year trajectory of stable revenues and expanding free cash flow provides a strong foundation, though near-term margins reflect a shift toward lower-margin ICT and wholesale headwinds.
[ A data table from this section could not be extracted cleanly from the source and is omitted; the figures are carried in the surrounding findings. ]

Multi-year revenue and margin trajectory

1.
Resilient historical revenue: The company generated €3,590.8m in 2024 and €3,464.3m in 2025, with the slight contraction primarily reflecting strategic decisions to exit zero-margin international transit traffic rather than core business deterioration
2.
Transitional margin pressure: Reported EBITDA margins compressed slightly from 40.4% in 2024 to 39.6% in 2025, driven heavily by the rapid growth of the structurally lower-margin systems integration and ICT business
3.
Steady forward estimates: Consensus modeling anticipates top-line stability, with projected revenues of €3,411.7m in 2026 and €3,379.1m in 2027, yielding an expected 2026 EPS of €1.62 as operational efficiencies offset legacy declines
4.
Unlevered balance sheet: The company operates with virtually no financial risk, maintaining a 2025 net debt-to-EBITDA ratio of 0.4x, drastically below the European telecom sector average of approximately 2.4x

Cash generation and capital intensity

5.
Exceptional cash conversion: The asset base routinely converts a high percentage of operating profit into liquidity, delivering €542.8m in 2025 adjusted free cash flow even during a period of peak infrastructure deployment
6.
Elevated capital expenditures: Capital intensity remains heavy at roughly €600m annually, or 17% of sales, as the group races to fulfill its ambitious target of passing 3.5 million homes with fiber by 2030
7.
Consistent shareholder returns: Unburdened by debt, management directs nearly 100% of generated free cash flow back to investors, authorizing €451m in combined 2025 dividends and buybacks for an exceptional total return profile

Recent quarterly momentum and guidance

8.
First-quarter revenue beat: The first quarter of 2026 featured a 4.9% top-line expansion to €859.4m, comfortably surpassing analyst expectations on the back of explosive 76.3% growth within the ICT and system solutions division
9.
Mobile service acceleration: The lucrative mobile segment demonstrated remarkable vigor, with first-quarter service revenues climbing 5.5% as consumers increasingly adopted higher-value data packages and 5G-enabled devices
10.
Management guidance reliability: Leadership exhibits a track record of honest, achievable forecasting, successfully reiterating their 2026 target for 3% adjusted EBITDA growth despite intensifying domestic infrastructure competition and the loss of international transit volumes
11.
Other side of the story: The explosive growth in the ICT division acts as a false friend for overall profitability, masking the underlying decay of high-margin legacy wholesale revenues and permanently diluting the group's consolidated return on sales

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The bullish thesis relies on sustained pre-to-postpaid mobile migration and robust ICT expansion, driving predictable free cash flow that fully funds sector-leading shareholder returns.

Prepaid-to-postpaid mobile migration

1.
Favorable mobile market structure: Operating in a consolidated three-player arena provides structural pricing power, enabling the company to push inflation-linked tariff increases without triggering the destructive price wars seen in other European geographies
2.
Structural ARPU expansion: With 56% of the mobile base still on prepaid plans–well above the European average–the systematic migration of these users to higher-yielding postpaid contracts offers a massive, multi-year runway for organic growth
3.
Plausibility and failure mode: High plausibility, as the trend is actively progressing with a record 7.6% postpaid growth in early 2026, though a severe domestic recession could force consumers back to cheaper prepaid alternatives

Fiber upselling and bundled convergence

4.
Subsidized broadband upgrades: The rollout of a state-backed Gigabit voucher scheme is actively accelerating household migration from legacy copper to premium fiber-to-the-home connections, directly boosting fixed-line average revenue per user
5.
Defensive bundling mechanics: By aggressively bundling fiber broadband with mobile lines and exclusive pay-TV sports content, the company dramatically increases consumer switching costs and insulates its subscriber base from emerging infrastructure challengers
6.
Plausibility and failure mode: Medium plausibility, as early adoption metrics show fiber network utilization reaching 39%, but failure would occur if price-sensitive consumers reject the premium speeds and defect to discount fiber alternatives

Romania exit and margin relief

7.
International portfolio rationalization: The recently approved sale of the struggling Telekom Romania Mobile unit structurally de-risks the equity story, immediately eliminating an estimated €20m to €30m in annual cash burn
8.
Plausibility and failure mode: High plausibility, given regulatory approval is secured, though the thesis would marginally weaken if final transaction adjustments or lingering tax liabilities consume the anticipated cash proceeds

Compounding the bull case

9.
Independent growth engines: These drivers operate largely independently of one another, meaning the investment case does not require a perfect macroeconomic backdrop, provided the core Greek consumer continues demanding higher data allowances and faster home connectivity
10.
Other side of the story: The entire upselling thesis assumes competitors remain rational, but if energy giant PPC uses its balance sheet to sustain predatory fiber pricing indefinitely, OTE will be forced to sacrifice ARPU growth to defend market share

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Trading at a material discount to European incumbent peers, the shares appear cheaply valued given the unlevered balance sheet and superior free cash flow yield.
1.
Depressed enterprise multiples: The stock currently trades at a highly undemanding 2026 estimated EV/EBITDA multiple of 5.1x, representing a staggering 22% discount to the European telecommunications sector average of 6.5x
2.
Outsized cash flow yield: Investors access an unlevered free cash flow yield of approximately 8.5%, which comfortably eclipses the sector median of 8.1% and provides a substantial premium over the 3.3% Greek ten-year government bond
3.
Lucrative shareholder distribution: The current valuation supports an aggregated dividend and share buyback yield approaching 8.5%, delivering a highly predictable, tangible return that requires zero multiple expansion to achieve acceptable equity performance
4.
Consensus target upside: Sell-side analysts maintain median price targets clustered between €20.00 and €23.00 per share, implying a robust 25% to 30% capital appreciation potential from current trading levels
5.
Implied market pricing: Current share prices suggest the market is aggressively pricing in a worst-case scenario where new fiber entrants completely destroy fixed-line margins, assigning virtually zero terminal value to the company's surging mobile and IT divisions
6.
Other side of the story: The optical cheapness on an EV/EBITDA basis is heavily distorted by the company's abnormally low debt load; on a pure price-to-earnings basis of roughly 11x, the stock trades much closer to historical sector norms

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The primary threat emanates from the structural erosion of the company's historical fixed-line monopoly, compounded by regulatory headwinds and the unpredictable behavior of well-capitalized, non-traditional infrastructure challengers.

The PPC infrastructure assault

1.
Predatory fiber pricing: Public Power Corporation (PPC) has rapidly deployed a parallel fiber-to-the-home network passing nearly 1.7 million premises, launching retail broadband offers at up to a 50% discount compared to OTE's established pricing
2.
Earnings transmission mechanism: If PPC successfully captures significant market share, OTE will suffer immediate retail revenue attrition and forced margin compression, fundamentally breaking the thesis that consumers will pay premiums for network quality

Wholesale market deterioration

3.
Evaporating toll-gate revenues: The company historically generated lucrative wholesale fees by leasing its copper infrastructure to rivals Nova and Vodafone, a high-margin stream that evaporates permanently as these competitors migrate customers to their own proprietary fiber networks
4.
Earnings transmission mechanism: This structural shift directly degrades the blended EBITDA margin, forcing the company to replace highly profitable, passive rental income with lower-margin IT services and capital-intensive direct retail acquisitions

Capital market technicals

5.
Index reclassification overhang: The impending transition of the Greek equity market from Emerging to Developed status by major index providers introduces severe technical risks, as passive emerging-market funds may be forced to mechanically liquidate their OTE holdings
6.
Early warning signal: The single most critical metric to monitor is the quarterly evolution of OTE's fixed-line broadband subscriber net additions, as sequential losses would explicitly confirm that PPC's aggressive pricing is inflicting terminal damage
7.
Other side of the story: The bear case fundamentally ignores the power of convergence, as OTE's ability to bundle heavily discounted fiber with premium mobile and exclusive sports TV creates a holistic household lock-in that a standalone broadband provider simply cannot replicate

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 4 findings

Section summary
  1. A seamless transition in executive leadership has catalyzed a sharper focus on the domestic market, accelerating digitalization initiatives and concluding a multi-year effort to shed distracting international subsidiaries.
1.
Successful chief executive handover: Kostas Nebis assumed the CEO role in July 2024, successfully succeeding the long-tenured Michael Tsamaz without triggering operational disruption or signaling a radical, value-destructive pivot in corporate strategy
2.
Strategic portfolio cleanup: Under the new leadership, the board decisively executed the sale of Telekom Romania Mobile, proving management's willingness to cleanly amputate structurally disadvantaged assets to protect the group's aggregate return on capital
3.
Stable board governance: Governance remains exceptionally stable with Deutsche Telekom exercising its 54.6% control in a hands-off manner, providing OTE with global technological resources while allowing local management to dictate tactical market responses

Section 9 – Net read and what to check next 5 findings

Section summary
  1. The company warrants an immediate, deep-dive investigation, as the exceptional free cash flow and mobile dominance provide a massive margin of safety against fixed-line infrastructure anxieties.
1.
Strongest argument for: The systematic migration of a massive, legacy prepaid mobile base toward higher-yielding postpaid contracts offers an incredibly reliable, internally controlled earnings engine that is utterly immune to fixed-line fiber competition
2.
Other side of the story: The permanent loss of high-margin wholesale revenues and the fracture of the domestic broadband monopoly could permanently trap the company in a cycle of heavy capital expenditure and structurally lower returns on capital
3.
Next action–verify network utilization: Commission a deep-dive analysis into the actual commercial connection rates of PPC's newly constructed fiber network to definitively test whether their 50% retail price discount is genuinely capturing OTE's customer base
4.
Next action–audit ICT sustainability: Investigate the exact composition and duration of the enterprise systems integration backlog to determine if the recent 76% growth surge is a durable recurring revenue stream or a temporary spike driven by expiring EU pandemic subsidies
