  1. Endesa presents a fully loaded strategic plan with modest EPS growth and demanding valuation compared to European utility peers

2.
Elevated network investment: Net capex is guided at €9.6bn, up 12% from the previous plan, with a heavy focus on regulated networks
3.
Constructive capital allocation: A €2.0bn share buyback program through 2027 provides technical support for the share price and improves per-share metrics

Mid-tier driver: Modest renewable capacity additions of 1.9GW are planned, substantially lower than sector peers, bearing a HIGH plausibility verdict
7.
Bottom driver: A €200m cost-saving initiative is targeted, partly reliant on AI-enabled efficiency which faces execution risks, warranting a MEDIUM plausibility verdict
8.
Bottom driver: Potential life extension of the Almaraz nuclear plant provides blue-sky upside optionality if approved by the government, holding a LOW plausibility verdict

10.
Low-quality beat: The outperformance was largely driven by a €100m positive one-off settlement in the distribution networks business
11.
Maintained forward guide: Management confirmed 2026 guidance, viewing the underlying performance as in-line and de-risking the full-year targets

13.
Weak guidance assumption: The 2028 integrated supply margin targets rely on retaining high retail tariffs despite falling wholesale input costs

Sell-side positioning

Tight price target range: Most broker price targets cluster around €27-€34, implying limited upside from current trading levels
