EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Business foundation and integrated value chain positioning

SCA achieves approximately 60% wood raw material self-sufficiency through its integrated value chain, with over half supplied from the company's own forests and sawmills, creating a protective pricing cushion that insulates industrial mills from rising global wood costs . The company has secured approximately 100% electricity self-sufficiency by the end of 2025 through wind farm integration, while also generating a 2.5 TWh annual surplus of solid biofuels . Proprietary logistics control extends through three dedicated Roll-on/Roll-off transport vessels operating scheduled voyages to Kiel, London, and Western European ports , providing defensive buffering against global freight bottlenecks. 6

Structural risks and regulatory headwinds

The European Union Deforestation Regulation takes effect on December 30, 2026, with strict EUDR compliance expected to decrease regional wood supply and escalate wood prices through extensive value chain traceability requirements . Proposals to restrict Swedish forestry activity pose a severe threat to industrial fiber supply, with converting half of managed forests to continuous-cover methods potentially causing harvesting volumes to decline by 25-30% . The European Commission's planned updates to the Emissions Trading Scheme benchmark raise profound carbon cost concerns, with ten out of eleven paper product benchmarks facing reductions by the maximum 50% update rate .
