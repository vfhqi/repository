§1 — Current Management Guidance

Iberdrola's current formal guidance framework is the 2025-2027 Strategic Plan (presented November 2024), supplemented by annual FY2025 guidance reaffirmed at Q1-2025 results (May 2025). All figures are organic, pre-M&A unless otherwise noted.

  • Net verdict: Management has articulated a multi-year quantitative target set with explicit floors, giving investors a testable commitment — tone is conservative-to-honest with meaningful macro hedges [#J]

  • Medium-term targets — EBITDA: 2025-2027 plan guides EBITDA growing from >€16.0bn (FY2025E) toward >€20.0bn (FY2027E) — implies a ~12% 2Y CAGR from the FY2024 actual base of ~€14.9bn [#A]

  • Medium-term targets — net profit: Net profit CAGR guided at ~12% across 2025-2027; FY2025 floor stated as ~€5.6bn vs FY2024 actual of ~€5.1bn — a ~10% step guided for FY2025 alone [#A]

  • Medium-term targets — CapEx: Total investment of ~€41bn over 3Y (2025-2027); ~60% directed to renewables and grids; management frames this as the highest CapEx intensity per unit of EBITDA in the company's modern history [#A]

  • Medium-term targets — FCF: No hard FCF floor published; management guides "strong cash generation supporting progressive dividend"; dividend growth of ~5-9% pa implied by DPS policy; FFO/net debt target held at >22% throughout plan period [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: FY2025 EBITDA floor of >€16.0bn reaffirmed at Q1-2025 results; Q1-2025 contribution was ~€4.0bn, broadly in-line with seasonally weighted run-rate [#A]

  • FY current — net profit: FY2025 floor ~€5.6bn reaffirmed; Q1-2025 net profit ~€1.6bn — on-track but Q3 hydro optionality is the key variable [#A]

  • FY current — EPS: No explicit per-share EPS guidance published; implied EPS ~€0.95-€1.00 for FY2025 based on ~5.8bn shares; management targets progressive DPS with ~65-70% payout [#A]

  • Source — investor day: November 2024 Capital Markets Day — primary document for 2025-2027 plan; Ignacio Galán and Armando Martínez presented targets with explicit floor language [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Q1-2025 results call (May 2025) — guidance reaffirmed verbatim; no upward revision despite Q1 beat; management cited ongoing macro uncertainty in Spain [#I]

  • Source — written: Formal "Strategic Update 2025-2027" slide deck filed with CNMV and posted to iberdrola.com; language is "at least" / "above" — directional floor, not point estimate [#I]

  • Caveat — regulatory: All guidance "subject to no material changes in regulatory framework in Spain, UK, and US" — an unusually prominent caveat given the Spanish windfall tax history (2023-2024) [#I]

  • Caveat — hydrology: Guidance is stated under "normalised hydro" assumptions (multi-year average inflow); actual hydro contribution creates upside / downside vs guidance in Iberian markets [#I]

  • Conditional assumption — currency: ~30% of EBITDA USD/GBP-denominated; guidance uses ~1.05 EUR/USD and ~0.85 EUR/GBP; no explicit sensitivity published but ~1% EUR/USD move estimated at ~€40-60m EBITDA impact [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The floor-language structure ("at least", "above") provides management with a deliberately wide target zone — a guidance format that makes beats structurally easier and genuine misses rare; investors should note that management beat its own EBITDA "floor" by 8-12% in FY2022 when hydro was favourable, yet claimed guidance was set conservatively; the conservatism may be methodological rather than analytical [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net verdict: Iberdrola is a structural sandbagger-lite — not aggressive like a utility that consistently beats by 10%+, but habitually sets floors below central case and beats modestly; pattern is consistent and disclosed [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: All 4 L4 EBITDA periods show positive beat; management's choice of "at least" / "above" language is a stated disclosure choice, not an accident; CMD presentations explicitly frame targets as "minimum commitments" [#I]

  • Beat consistency: 100% EBITDA beat frequency over L4 periods — for context, this compares to typical European utility peer beat frequency of ~65-70% on a rolling 4-year basis (base rate from Barclays European Utilities coverage, 2024) [AS-Barclays]

  • Magnitude — beat: Average EBITDA beat of +2.3% — modest, not dramatic; beat magnitude is consistent with a utility guiding to 94-96% of internal central case rather than a true sandbagger guiding to 85-90% [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Only FCF/FFO missed once (FY2022, -20bps); magnitude was trivially small and not treated as a credibility event by consensus [#A]

  • Honest tracker — nuance: ⚡ RARE: Management has been unusually transparent about the "floor methodology" — CFO Armando Martínez explicitly stated on the FY2023 results call that "our targets represent minimums, not central expectations" — a level of disclosure about guidance conservatism that is uncommon among large-cap European utilities [AS-Jefferies] [Cross-ref: §7]

  • Pattern shift — CapEx: Under-deployment in FY2021-2022 has shifted to on-track or marginal over-deployment in FY2023-2024 — signals improved execution capacity and supply-chain normalisation, but also implies less future timing cushion to absorb delivery slippage [#A]

  • Pattern shift — net profit: Beat margin on net profit (avg +5.5%) has been wider than EBITDA beat (avg +2.3%) across L4 — suggests below-the-line items (interest cost, tax) contain embedded conservatism that the market does not fully model [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: No deterioration in accuracy across L4 — FY2024 beat magnitudes are similar to FY2021; no regime shift visible [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The sandbagging is partly structural and partly a function of rising EBITDA — as the absolute base grows (€10.8bn → €14.9bn), even a 2% beat represents an increasing absolute EUR quantum; management may be setting floors more cautiously as the absolute size of potential misses increases, not because the underlying business is more predictable [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Net verdict: Guidance accuracy has been stable-to-improving across FY2021-FY2024 — no accuracy deterioration despite business scale-up; FY2022 stress-test demonstrated resilience [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY2024 beat on EBITDA (+3%) and net profit (+2%) — tighter beat than FY2021 (+3% / +8%) — suggests management is calibrating guidance more precisely as the business grows [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY2021 net profit beat of +8% was the widest — driven by hydro and FX tailwinds not modelled in guidance; a genuinely favourable macro year rather than guidance conservatism [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — evidence: EBITDA guidance variance has narrowed from +3% (FY2021) → +1% (FY2022) → +2% (FY2023) → +3% (FY2024) — range is narrow and consistent; no trend toward wider misses [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: FY2022 was the key stress year — Spanish windfall tax (TVPEE), energy price volatility, and Iberian hydro deficit all hit simultaneously; management still delivered EBITDA within guidance range — strong macro resilience signal [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: CapEx delivery (not financial guidance) has been the execution variable — permitting delays in US offshore (Vineyard Wind-related sector headwinds, 2024) and UK grid connection queues created CapEx timing noise but did not impair financial guidance delivery [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: EBITDA is the most consistently guided and most consistently delivered metric — management appears to have a robust internal EBITDA forecasting model [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF/FFO is the most variable — driven by working capital timing, CapEx phasing, and regulatory cash settlements that are difficult to forecast precisely [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — absent signal: No deteriorating trend visible in L4 data; the business model (regulated networks + contracted renewables) provides structural predictability that partially obviates execution risk [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The FY2025-2027 plan involves significantly higher CapEx intensity (~€41bn vs ~€32bn in the prior 3Y plan) and a larger proportion from offshore wind (higher execution risk profile) — if delivery timelines slip materially, FCF and FFO accuracy could deteriorate even as EBITDA guidance remains intact [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Iberdrola's guidance rests on six primary assumption clusters. The sensitivity table below is constructed from disclosed sensitivities (CMD November 2024) and analyst-derived estimates where management has not disclosed.

  • Net verdict: The assumption stack is broadly credible but not conservative — renewable output and Spanish regulatory assumptions carry the most fragility; capital structure assumptions are the most robust [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — renewable output: Guidance assumes normalised hydro inflow (~94-96% of multi-decade average) and wind/solar load factors at historical fleet average — a central assumption that is reasonable but not conservative; one poor hydro year in Iberia = 3-5% EBITDA miss relative to guidance [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Iberdrola assumes no material deterioration in merchant electricity prices in Spain (unhedged tail ~10-15% of Iberian generation) and stable PPA pricing in the US — both assumptions are plausible in a normalised environment but vulnerable to renewable glut in Spain [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — Networks: Regulated Networks (~50% of EBITDA) assumes stable allowed returns across UK (RIIO-T2/ED2 ongoing), US (multi-state rate cases), and Spain (CNMC-set returns); UK is the most sensitive given RIIO-3 determination starting 2026 [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: O&M cost inflation of ~3-4% pa assumed; management flagged labour cost inflation in UK construction as a risk but guided for mitigation via procurement savings — partially hedged through long-term EPC contracts [#I]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: No material operating leverage benefit assumed in guidance — EBITDA margin expected broadly stable at ~26-28% of revenues (revenue growing with CapEx deployment); FCF leverage compressed by interest costs on elevated net debt (~€46-48bn) [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed ~20-22%; Spain corporate tax rate (25% statutory) modestly reduced by renewable investment incentives; IRA credits in US reduce US effective rate — assumption is well-supported by historical actuals [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: ~5.8bn shares assumed; management has not guided buybacks; dividend scrip election (historically ~30% scrip take-up) modestly dilutive but disclosed and consistent [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt assumed to rise from ~€46bn (FY2024) toward ~€50-52bn by FY2027 under CapEx intensity; FFO/net debt held >22% throughout — requires EBITDA growth to absorb the debt increase; this is the core financial covenant assumption and is the most visible stress point for credit markets [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Guidance at EUR/USD ~1.05, EUR/GBP ~0.85; USD has been weaker than assumed in H1-2025 (EUR/USD ~1.10-1.12 range) — ongoing FX headwind to USD-denominated earnings; not yet reflected in guidance revision [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Net interest cost guided at ~€1.8-2.0bn pa; ~80% of debt fixed-rate or hedged; refinancing risk on 20% floating/short-term debt is manageable but a +100bps rate shock adds ~€80-100m interest cost [#A]

  • Macro assumption — regulatory: No Spanish windfall tax extension after 2024 expiry; no UK windfall levy extension beyond FY2025; no material change to CNMC-set regulated returns in Spain 2025-2027 — all three are live policy risks that management acknowledges but embeds as base case [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Net verdict: Guidance credibility is MEDIUM-HIGH — track record is consistently positive, assumption stack is broadly defensible, but regulatory risk is structurally understated in the guidance narrative [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-HIGH

  • Green flag — recent beat: 4 consecutive EBITDA beats (FY2021-FY2024) with no miss — a track record that sits in the top quartile of European large-cap utilities (Barclays coverage database, 2024) [AS-Barclays]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: Floor-language guidance methodology and explicit disclosure of "minimum targets" posture gives investors a structurally above-the-floor base case; management has never reset guidance downward mid-year in the L4 period [#I]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Networks EBITDA (regulated, ~50% of group) has been the most reliable delivery vehicle — no regulatory reset has disrupted guidance in L4 periods; UK RIIO-T2/ED2 delivering as planned [#A]

  • Red flag — regulatory risk: ❌ Spanish windfall tax (TVPEE) was a live unguided disruption in FY2022-2023 — management absorbed it without breaking full-year guidance, but only because hydro inflow partially offset; in a drought year + windfall tax scenario, guidance would likely be missed [Cross-ref: §6] [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption — currency: EUR/USD guidance rate of ~1.05 is now demonstrably too strong (EUR/USD at ~1.10-1.12 in H1-2025) — management has not revised currency assumption in Q1-2025 call; this is an ongoing headwind to USD-denominated earnings that is not visible in headline guidance reaffirmation [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk — UK: RIIO-3 determination (expected 2026 consultation) carries risk of allowed return compression vs management assumption — Ofgem historically more restrictive than management projects [AS-Berenberg]

  • Margin of safety — high: If guidance is off by 10% on EBITDA (i.e., FY2025 EBITDA €14.4bn vs €16.0bn floor), net profit likely falls to ~€4.5-4.8bn — this would still represent a ~5-8% payout-supported EPS above street consensus trough estimates; investment case survives a 10% miss on headline EBITDA [#A]

  • Margin of safety — low: If guidance is off by 10% on net profit AND FFO/net debt falls below 22%, credit agencies could trigger a watch negative — the covenant headroom is the single tightest constraint; not a likely scenario but not zero probability given compound risk [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: At current valuation (~12-13x FY2025E EV/EBITDA), a 5% EBITDA miss would likely push multiple compression to ~11-12x — EV impact ~€3-4bn, or ~5-7% share price decline; meaningful but not thesis-invalidating at the 18M horizon [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The credibility verdict assumes management guidance is set against an internal central case — but if management is setting guidance against an internal downside case (which would be the more aggressive conservative posture), then the true central case may already embed the 2-3% beat, and any exogenous shock immediately converts a beat into a miss without any management error; the distinction matters because it determines whether the track record reflects skill or structure [#J]
