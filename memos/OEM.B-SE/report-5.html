Earnings Summary and Regional Performance
Group Earnings Summary

OEM International experienced a sequential acceleration in demand from the end of 2025 into the first quarter of 2026, setting new record levels for both sales and profitability.

In the fourth quarter of 2025, the company reported mixed top-line results, with net sales decreasing by 2% to SEK 1,327 million. This slight decline was driven by a negative 5% currency impact, which offset 1% organic growth and a 2% contribution from acquisitions. However, underlying demand began to strengthen, evidenced by incoming orders rising 5% year-over-year to SEK 1,371 million in Q4 2025. Operating profit (EBITA) for Q4 2025 fell slightly by 2% to SEK 202 million, though the EBITA margin remained stable at 15.2%. For the full year 2025, net sales increased by 1% to SEK 5,317 million, supported by 3% organic growth and 1% acquired growth, while EBITA decreased by 1% to SEK 795 million at a 15.0% margin.

The momentum from late 2025 accelerated significantly in the first quarter of 2026. Net sales for Q1 2026 grew by 7% year-over-year to SEK 1,472 million. This expansion was fueled by a robust 5% organic growth rate and a 6% contribution from recent acquisitions, which comfortably offset a 4% currency headwind. Incoming orders in Q1 2026 surged by 10% to SEK 1,543 million, outpacing net sales by 5% and driving the order backlog up to SEK 1,126 million by the end of March 2026. Profitability also saw significant expansion in the first quarter. Q1 2026 EBITA rose 16% year-over-year to SEK 238 million. The EBITA margin for the quarter improved notably by 130 basis points to 16.2%, which management attributed to a combination of higher gross margins and strict cost control.

Strategic Growth: M&A Strategy and Capital Allocation
Evolution of M&A Strategy

OEM International prioritizes a structured acquisition strategy to drive long-term value creation and complement its organic expansion. The company primarily focuses on three types of acquisitions: adding complementary products in existing markets, expanding into new geographic markets with existing product lines, and acquiring product-owning companies within established territories. Target companies are typically leading trading or product-owning businesses generating annual sales between SEK 30 million and SEK 250 million. Historically, acquisitions have served as the company's secondary growth engine, delivering a median acquired growth rate of approximately 2% annually between 2013 and 2023.

The strategic framework has recently evolved to accelerate capital deployment following EQT's assumption of principal ownership in March 2025. While OEM historically targeted technical trading companies, the new strategic mandate explicitly broadens the focus to encompass product-owning companies in selected niches to enhance value creation. To execute this accelerated M&A agenda, OEM appointed Henrik Forsberg Schoultz as Deputy CEO in February 2026, tasking him with further developing the group strategy and scaling the decentralized business portfolio through value-accretive capital deployment.

Recent Acquisitions and Top-Line Impact

The intensified focus on M&A materialized significantly in early 2026, driven by a strong pipeline of potential targets. During the first quarter of 2026, OEM completed five acquisitions that collectively contributed SEK 46 million to the quarter's net sales. This M&A activity provided a 6% boost to the Group's total acquired growth for the quarter, comfortably offsetting currency headwinds. An additional acquisition was completed in April 2026, further expanding the company's regional footprint.
