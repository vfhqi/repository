Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Aegon is successfully executing a profound structural pivot towards the US life and retirement market, crystallising value from European assets to fund robust capital returns.
1.
Pure-play focus: Aegon has divested its low-return Dutch assets to ASR Nederland and agreed to sell its UK business to Standard Life, focusing resources exclusively on the US market
2.
US redomiciliation: The primary investment case rests on the company's legal relocation to Delaware and transition to US GAAP by 2028, which should eliminate its historical European conglomerate valuation discount
3.
Remittance catalyst: The single most critical swing factor for Aegon's forward earnings is the capital upstreaming capacity of Transamerica, which relies on maintaining a robust US Risk-Based Capital ratio
4.
Strong financials: Aegon exceeded its 2025 operating capital generation target, delivering EUR 1.3 billion against a EUR 1.2 billion baseline, alongside EUR 829 million in free cash flow
5.
Capital return: The company completed a EUR 400 million share buyback program in December 2025, and proposed a total 2025 dividend of EUR 0.40 per share
6.
Asset divestments: The strategic review of Aegon UK concluded on April 15, 2026, with a planned £2.0 billion sale to Standard Life, simplifying the balance sheet and enabling further buybacks
7.
Downside vulnerability: A severe downturn in US interest rates or credit markets represents the primary risk to statutory capital generation and capital return growth
8.
Idiosyncratic rerate: A closer look is highly warranted because Aegon is poised to achieve a profound valuation rerate as it transitions into a pure-play US life retirement company

Section 3 – What the company does 10 findings

Section summary
  1. Aegon operates a highly focused business model dominated by its US subsidiary Transamerica, supplemented by asset management and strategic minority holdings.

Core US Protection and Retirement Operations

1.
Transamerica market positioning: Transamerica is Aegon's core subsidiary and the largest operating unit worldwide, employing approximately 7,200 people across the United States. The division is strongly positioned to serve the middle-market and mass-affluent segments
2.
Distribution captive power: World Financial Group represents a key strategic asset, operating an affiliated network of over 95,000 licensed agents across North America
3.
Protection solutions trajectory: New life sales increased by 30% in 2025. Growth was driven by entering the instant decision market for Final Expense and expanding Indexed Universal Life
4.
Legacy book run-off: Legacy Financial Assets comprising older, capital-intensive life products including Variable Annuities and Long-Term Care, are managed for rapid run-off. Capital employed was reduced to USD 2.7 billion in 2025, ahead of targets

Global Asset Management and European Holdings

5.
Scalable global asset manager: Aegon Asset Management operates wholly-owned global platforms with EUR 261 billion in assets under management, focusing on fixed income, alternatives, and private debt
6.
Dutch strategic shareholding: Aegon holds a 24% strategic stake in ASR Nederland, following the combination of its Dutch operations with ASR in 2023
7.
Standard Life UK transaction: Aegon concluded the strategic review of Aegon UK by signing an agreement to sell the unit to Standard Life on April 15, 2026, for £2.0 billion. Upon completion, Aegon will hold a 15.3% stake in Standard Life plc
8.
Profit-favoured market positioning: Aegon is one of the few pure-play life insurers in Europe without a volatile non-life property and casualty underwriting business
9.
Other side of the story: Spruce Point issued an 82-page activist short-seller report in December 2024, alleging that World Financial Group operates as a multi-level marketing scheme. If regulatory bodies impose restrictions, Aegon's primary US distribution engine would face significant disruption, reducing cash remittances and severely damaging brand equity

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Aegon is delivering robust financial growth and capital generation, outperforming intermediate targets and maintaining strong balance sheet buffers.
[ A data table from this section could not be extracted cleanly from the source and is omitted; the figures are carried in the surrounding findings. ]
1.
Declining European revenue: Reported insurance revenue declined from EUR 10.39 billion in 2023 to EUR 9.10 billion in 2025, reflecting planned divestment of mature European blocks. This contraction is structural and positive
2.
Rising reported EPS: Basic EPS grew from a loss of EUR -0.12 in 2023 to positive EUR 0.59 in 2025
3.
Exceeding OCG targets: Operating capital generation reached EUR 1.3 billion in 2025, comfortably exceeding the EUR 1.2 billion intermediate target set during the 2023 Capital Markets Day
4.
Upward cash flows: Free cash flow increased to EUR 829 million in 2025, exceeding the target of around EUR 800 million
5.
Capital returns record: During 2025, Aegon returned EUR 1.1 billion to shareholders, including a EUR 400 million share buyback and EUR 0.40 in total dividends per share

Balance Sheet Strength and Guidance Reliability

6.
Solid group solvency: Aegon's Group Solvency II ratio stood at 184% as of December 31, 2025
7.
Transamerica RBC cushion: The statutory Risk-Based Capital ratio for Transamerica was 424% in 2025, exceeding the 400% target
8.
Other side of the story: The disqualification of Perpetual Cumulative Subordinated Bonds as Restricted Tier 1 capital on January 1, 2026, creates a significant statutory capital contraction, reducing group solvency by 7 percentage points

Section 5 – The possible investment case 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Aegon grows significantly stronger as the company simplifies its structure, redomiciles to the US, and exits non-core assets.

US Relocation and Corporate Rerating

1.
Delaware corporate redomiciliation: Aegon's relocation of its legal seat and headquarters to Delaware is scheduled to be voted on in the fourth quarter of 2026, targeting completion by 2028
2.
Rerating play plausibility: The probability of a successful valuation rerating is high because Aegon currently trades at a 27% discount to the European Insurance sector and a significant discount to US peers. Reporting under US GAAP starting in 2027 should pave the way for US index inclusion

Value Extraction from Asset Divestitures

3.
High-premium UK sale: The agreed sale of Aegon UK to Standard Life for £2.0 billion represents a transaction multiple of 1.9x book value and 14.2x operating result after tax
4.
Deleveraging and buybacks: The transaction is expected to close at the end of 2026, delivering £750 million in gross cash proceeds that will fund further share buybacks
5.
Liquidity war chest: The remaining 24% stake in ASR Nederland represents a highly liquid, EUR 3.0 billion asset that Aegon can monetize following the expiration of lock-up periods

Capital Return Acceleration

6.
Structural share buybacks: Aegon's EUR 400 million share buyback program for 2026 will reduce the outstanding share count by several percentage points, amplifying EPS growth
7.
Independent driver probability: The investment drivers are independent, meaning that even if US index inclusion is delayed, shareholders will still benefit from massive cash returns funded by European divestments
8.
Other side of the story: If Aegon experiences a severe US credit downturn, rating downgrades would trigger substantial statutory capital requirements at Transamerica, preventing planned share buybacks

Section 6 – Valuation 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Aegon shares trade at a material discount to both US and European insurance peers, offering an asymmetric risk-reward profile.
1.
Multi-peer discount: Aegon currently trades at 7.8x PER FY26F, compared with US Life peers at 9.4x and European composite peers at 11.7x
2.
High dividend yield: Total dividend of EUR 0.40 per share implies a dividend yield of approximately 6.5%, compared with European composite peers at 5.2% and US peers at 3.0%
3.
Aggressive broker target: ING maintains a Buy rating and increased its target price to EUR 7.40, reflecting the highly positive cash implications of the £2.0 billion UK sale
4.
Price-in consensus: The market is currently pricing in unjustified legacy accounting noise, creating an entry point where the forward rerating catalyst is bought at a deep structural discount
5.
Other side of the story: Standalone P/E multiples remain highly sensitive to quarterly non-operating items, meaning a series of investment or derivative losses could inflate the trading multiple and erase the optical discount

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The principal threat is Aegon's exposure to US macroeconomic headwinds, particularly credit defaults and interest rate volatility, which directly impact statutory solvency.

Credit, Macro, and Legacy Risks

1.
Portfolio default sensitivity: Transamerica's statutory capital is highly sensitive to credit migration, where general downgrades in the fixed-income portfolio would compress the US RBC ratio
2.
Liquidity collateral stress: Rapidly rising interest rates represent a material threat, forcing Aegon to post substantial cash collateral on interest rate swaps, thereby straining holding company liquidity
3.
Statutory US tax risk: The implementation of US tax code Section 899 could disrupt statutory cash flows, potentially reducing annual remittances by USD 150 million to USD 350 million
4.
Governance transition friction: The legal relocation process involves significant complexity, with estimated implementation and consulting costs of EUR 350 million to be incurred through 2028

Technical Mismatches and Early Warning

5.
Remittance early warning: The critical metric to monitor is the US RBC ratio, as any deterioration below the 400% target would prompt US regulators to restrict holding company remittances
6.
Other side of the story: Aegon's comprehensive dynamic hedging program, which covers 25% of Variable Annuity base contract fees, protects statutory capital from sudden interest rate and equity market shocks

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 4 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership is a significant catalyst for corporate change, driving aggressive restructuring, asset sales, and US redomiciliation governance updates.
1.
CEO term extension: Aegon's Board will propose extending Lard Friese's mandate until 2030 to ensure management continuity during the critical Delaware redomiciliation and US GAAP transition phase
2.
US-insurance expert Chairman: David Herzog, former CFO of AIG, succeeded William Connelly as Chairman in November 2025
3.
Elimination of capital structures: The proposed Delaware by-laws will remove the staggered board structure by 2030 and convert all outstanding Common Shares B held by Vereniging Aegon into a single class of common stock with equal voting rights
