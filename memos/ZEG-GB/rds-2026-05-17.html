Zegona Communications is a UK-listed holding company whose sole asset is Vodafone Spain, acquired for EUR5 billion in May 2024. The investment thesis is straightforward: extract margin and revenue growth from a structurally underperforming Spanish mobile network operator (MNO) through aggressive cost reduction, network rationalisation, and customer base stabilisation, then re-rate as free cash flow inflects.

The operational story is running ahead of expectations. The FY25 results (year to March 2025) showed: EBITDAaL of EUR1,249 million with margin up over 2 percentage points to 34%; cash generation of EUR625 million in the twelve months to March 2025, up 55% year-on-year; and Vodafone Spain returning to broadband and mobile customer net-adds for the first time in years. Q1 FY26 (April to June 2025) continued the trend with EBITDAaL margins hitting ~35% and contract mobile lines growing 39,000. Management guided for cash flow margins above 21% for full-year FY26, more than double pre-acquisition levels.

The capital structure overhang was partly resolved in January 2026: shareholders approved a EUR1.8 billion capital return funded by the FiberCo and FiberPass fibre asset disposals. EUR975 million was used to repay Vodafone financing in full, with EUR440 million distributed pro-rata to ordinary shareholders via a special dividend paid January 7, 2026. The share count was reduced by ~69% (from ~759 million to ~236 million), which mechanically inflated the per-share price but also significantly reduced leverage. Post the share consolidation, the per-share metrics are on a materially different base -- a frequent source of apparent price-level confusion among less-attentive market participants.

The next scheduled results event is the Q2/H1 FY26 release, estimated for late May or early June 2026 (consensus estimate: May 25; company calendar: June 3, 2026). This upcoming print is the single largest near-term binary catalyst.
