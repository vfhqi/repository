EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Brembo operates a vertically integrated premium braking business delivering industry-leading margins but faces mounting capital intensity, slowing mechatronic adoption, and structural return compression from aggressive non-organic expansion. The company commands exceptional brand equity and over fifty percent market share in vehicles above €55,000, yet confronts severe software decoupling risks that threaten to commoditize its core caliper hardware as brake-by-wire platforms shift value downstream to tier-one systems integrators. Balance sheet leverage escalated to historic highs of 1.20x net debt to EBITDA following the €366 million Öhlins acquisition, while core return on investment compressed from 16.00% to 10.90% over two fiscal periods. Free cash flow generation remains structurally constrained by capital expenditures exceeding €440 million and expanding working capital absorption across Chinese OEM and aftermarket channels. The Sensify dry brake-by-wire platform entered series production in H1 2026 but currently holds zero awarded commercial business for mass-market applications due to its fifty percent cost premium and unproven long-term reliability concerns. Management's strategic pivot toward integrated mechatronic corner solutions via Öhlins and localized joint ventures in China and India positions Brembo for long-term growth, but near-term execution depends heavily on heroic engineering outcomes rather than mechanical market forces. Premium segment concentration exposes the group to cyclical European luxury vehicle downturns, while intensifying competition from software-heavy tier- ones and emerging Chinese suppliers threatens to erode hard-won pricing power in mainstream applications.

The industry is shifting toward fluid-free, dry brake-by-wire solutions that distribute intelligence, removing centralized hydraulic systems to improve packaging flexibility and response times while supporting stable vehicle dynamics . The Sensify platform entered series production in H1 2026 for a leading global manufacturer fitted as standard on 100% of vehicles in the launch program, confirming Sensify's transition from an R&D concept to industrial reality . However, expert calls indicate dry by-wire adoption faces unproven long-term reliability and high redundant power costs, with Sensify demanding a 50% higher system cost premium and safety validation timelines restricting rapid mass-market penetration over the medium term . Brembo completed the 100% acquisition of suspension specialist Öhlins Group AB in January 2025 for €366 million representing the largest acquisition in history, paying an expensive multiple of 13.1x EBITDA to secure premium mechatronic suspension technology , enabling fully integrated active corner systems combining brakes and dampers with Öhlins supplying Formula 1 and MotoGP teams supporting high- performance motorsport heritage and transferring racing damper algorithms into road-car mechatronic suspension designs . Heavy battery electric vehicles require extra braking power providing opportunities for higher-margin brake configurations, with BEV mass preserving mechanical friction safety requirements despite regenerative systems and heavier vehicles driving OEM demand for advanced lightweight aluminum caliper designs .
