Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Technip Energies represents a highly predictable earnings visibility story supported by a record backlog and capital-light engineering economics.
1.
Business model: Leading engineering and technology contracting giant, generating revenue through long-term project delivery and short-cycle technology licensing
2.
Investment thesis: The primary investment case rests on the company's ability to capitalize on structurally growing global LNG demand and its transition toward high-margin technology licensing
3.
Focus acceleration: This transition is expected to accelerate significantly through the integration of the recently acquired Advanced Materials and Catalysts business
4.
Earnings swing: The single most critical swing factor for short-term earnings remains the resolution of geopolitical disruptions in the Middle East
5.
Recent delivery: In 2025, the company delivered highest-ever revenues of EUR 7.20 billion and EBITDA of EUR 638 million
6.
Backlog protection: Growth visibility is exceptionally high, with the backlog reaching over EUR 20 billion in Q1 2026, poised to exceed EUR 24 billion mid-year
7.
Key risk: The primary near-term threat to the investment case is operational delays from Middle East conflict and logistical bottlenecks around the Strait of Hormuz
8.
First-look verdict: A deeper investigation is highly warranted, as the market's discount undervalues the massive medium-term earnings trajectory

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. Technip Energies leverages its unrivalled process technology licensing and engineering selectivity to secure high-margin project delivery without owning capital-intensive fabrication assets.
1.
Segment structure: Organized into Project Delivery and Technology, Products & Services operating segments
2.
Project segment: Project Delivery provides comprehensive global engineering, procurement, and construction capabilities
3.
TPS segment: Technology, Products & Services provides process technologies, proprietary catalysts, specialized equipment, and plant life-cycle consultancy services
4.
Revenue split: Project Delivery revenue was EUR 5.40 billion in 2025, vs EUR 1.80 billion for TPS
5.
Licensing strength: Greater than 40% global licensing market share in petrochemical ethylene cracker technologies
6.
EBITDA profile: Project Delivery margins around 8-9%; TPS commands EBITDA margins of over 14% bolstered by catalyst licensing
7.
Competitor contraction: Onshore E&C competition has reduced significantly following the financial distress of McDermott and Petrofac
8.
Asset-light structure: Zero direct yard or vessel ownership; operates with highly favorable negative capital employed
9.
Customer concentration: Top five customers account for 50% of adjusted revenue and 86% of the active backlog
10.
Strategic clients: QatarEnergy, BP, and ADNOC serve as core long-term clients
11.
Portfolio diversification: Management is actively diversifying by securing new contracts in sustainable fuels, green hydrogen, and carbon capture
12.
Risk: Extreme customer concentration in Qatar exposes the company to severe project suspension shocks

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Technip Energies strengthens based on its massive backlog inflection and high-margin catalyst growth, despite near-term Middle East disruptions.
1.
Backlog expansion: Commonwealth LNG NTP will book over EUR 1 billion of revenue in Project Delivery backlog during Q2 2026
2.
Replication advantage: Technip's modular SnapLNG solution enables accelerated execution and margin protection through standardized process engineering
3.
Plausibility: High - contracts are already signed and mobilization has officially commenced
4.
Execution failure risk: Local labor shortages or supply chain inflation in the US could erode the embedded profitability of lump-sum contracts
5.
Recurring revenues: Approximately 70% of AM&C revenues are tied to operating expenditures, providing substantial long-term recurring visibility
6.
Incumbency advantage: Technip's historical role building 77 million tonnes per annum of Qatar LNG capacity makes them the natural partner for critical reconstruction work

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 6 findings

Section summary
  1. The bear case is moderately credible due to the Middle East logistical blockade, but strong contract execution history and cost protections limit the threat to long-term earnings.
1.
Logistical disruptions: EUR 500-600 million of revenue deferred from FY26 into outer years
2.
Failure mode: A severe material availability crisis for mid-cycle projects where heavy machinery cannot be rerouted via land transport; if the blockade persists into H2 2026, further revenue deferrals are likely
3.
False friend caution: High order intake and backlog optics can act as a "false friend" if revenue recognition speeds fade due to localized site disruptions
4.
Early warning signal: Investors must closely monitor the timeline of the Strait of Hormuz reopening, currently assumed by management to normalize by end of Q2 2026
5.
Counter-argument: The risks are structurally overstated because any damaged energy infrastructure in the Middle East directly creates lucrative, high-margin reconstruction contracts for Technip as the primary incumbent
