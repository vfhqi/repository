Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Vallourec dominates the highly consolidated premium seamless tube market, leveraging its proprietary VAM connection technology to command strong pricing power.

Business Segments and Product Profiles

1.
Critical upstream applications: The company's principal segment is Oil Country Tubular Goods which supplies casing and tubing to protect the structural integrity of subsurface wells
2.
Specialized joint technology: Vallourec equips its seamless tubes with proprietary VAM premium threaded connections, providing gastight sealing in highly corrosive and high-torque environments
3.
Integrated mining assets: The group owns and operates a major iron ore mine in Brazil, providing a natural cash hedge and low-cost raw materials
4.
Emerging energy transition: Under the Vallourec New Energies brand, the group offers tubular solutions for geothermal wells, carbon capture, and clean hydrogen storage

Value Chain Position and Revenue Mechanics

5.
Domestic integrated operations: Vallourec operates as a fully integrated domestic producer in the US, shielding its local sales from punitive tariffs
6.
Strategic export hubs: The company utilizes low-cost manufacturing bases in Brazil and China to serve global offshore and international land markets
7.
Multi-year contract framework: Revenues are secured via transactional purchase orders underpinned by long-term framework agreements with major national oil companies
8.
Non-eligible taxonomy reporting: Following European Union regulatory adjustments, the group's seamless tube manufacturing and mining revenues are classified as non-eligible under standard green taxonomy

Industry Moats and Competitive Landscape

9.
Oligopolistic market concentration: The high-end seamless tube sector is consolidated, with premium players enjoying structurally higher returns on capital than welded tube producers
10.
Substantial switching costs: Customers face high switching costs due to rigorous qualification procedures and the widespread industry adoption of the VAM standard
11.
Technical entry barriers: Achieving the metallurgic durability required for high-value premium seamless tubes requires advanced heat-treatment and complex threading lines, preventing standard commodity steel competitors from entering
12.
Displaced Western supply: The permanent closure of over 685,000 tonnes of rolling capacity in Germany has significantly reduced industry-wide oversupply
13.
Other side of the story: Standard commoditized products remain exposed to low-cost Asian competition, and during severe macro downturns, customers can temporarily trade down to lower-specification connections, which dampens the average selling price of tubes

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens significantly due to structural self-help tailwinds and the rapid expansion of the geothermal and offshore energy transition segments.

Key Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
Inflection in US spot pricing: Upward momentum in US spot pricing acts as a high-plausibility driver of forward earnings, with tariffs keeping imports low
2.
Bernstein notes that a inflection in North American pricing is driven by low imports and robust booking activity from US onshore operators
3.
Extensive offshore contract awards: International volume expansion is highly likely, underpinned by major contract wins including the Petrobras long-term agreement renewal valued up to $1.00 billion through 2029
4.
Accelerating geothermal breakthrough: The geothermal market provides an emerging medium-term driver, highlighted by the milestone five-year, $800.00 million supply agreement signed with Fervo Energy in April 2026
5.
Sustainable mine extension contribution: The successful start-up of Phase 1 mine expansion in late 2024 has expanded iron ore production, driving volumes to 6.20 million tonnes in 2025

Plausibility and Structural Catalyst Dynamics

6.
Strategic regional positioning: The earnings drivers operate independently, meaning the investment case does not require all geographic drivers to fire simultaneously to achieve a 25.00% IRR
7.
Large-scale contract pipeline: Geothermal demand acts as a structural volume backstop, with Fervo target capacity requiring up to 100,000 tonnes of specialized tubes per gigawatt
8.
Powerful tariff protection: US domestic assets are protected by Section 232 steel tariffs, providing a highly defensible margin buffer
9.
Other side of the story: The forward investment case would fail if a severe global macroeconomic recession caused deep cuts to upstream capital expenditures

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk picture remains moderately balanced, as execution risks from geographical transfers are behind us but macro cyclicality and iron ore volatility persist.

Market and Operational Volatilities

1.
Severe demand cyclicality: The primary risk factor is the inherent cyclicality of global upstream spending, which directly dictates overall demand for seamless steel tubes
2.
Unpredictable mining exposure: Iron ore mine earnings are highly sensitive to Chinese spot market prices, meaning a sharp drop in steel production would immediately impact group EBITDA
3.
Intense regional disruptions: Red Sea logistics disruptions have forced Vallourec to bypass the Strait of Hormuz via trucking through Oman, causing temporary margin dilution
4.
Currency translation volatility: The company's results remain highly sensitive to fluctuations in the EUR/USD exchange rate and Brazilian real translation changes

Ownership and Financial Risks

5.
Unanticipated shareholder overhang: ArcelorMittal's unexpected decision to sell down 10.00% of its stake in May 2026 has introduced near-term overhang from shareholder secondary offerings
6.
Complex warrant dilution: The exercise of 33.00 million outstanding penny warrants on June 30, 2026, will cause a c.15.00% dilution to basic shares, which is only partially offset by recent share buybacks
7.
Key early-warning signals: Investors must closely monitor US horizontal rig counts and monthly seamless imports as early-warning indicators of pricing pressure
8.
Other side of the story: The risk of a collapse in OCTG pricing is mitigated by the highly consolidated, oligopolistic structure of the premium seamless tube market
