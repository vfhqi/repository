Section 1 – Business Model + Organisation 58 findings

Section summary
  1. DEUTZ is successfully executing its Dual+ strategy to transform from a cyclical engine pure-play into a highly diversified, resilient solutions provider.

Segmental Structure and Reorganisation

1.
Segmental reorganisation: In January 2026, DEUTZ implemented a divisional restructure comprising five independent business units to enhance entrepreneurial agility
2.
Divisional autonomy: Each of the new business units carries direct responsibility for its own operations and financial results
3.
Management focus: The slimmed-down central holding structure allows the Board of Management to focus primarily on strategy and capital allocation
4.
Reporting segments: Operating activities transitioned from two reporting segments in 2025 to five distinct reporting segments in 2026
5.
Engines and Service: The classic engines segment comprises core diesel development, while the Service segment consolidates global aftermarket operations
6.
Energy and NewTech: The Energy segment brings together decentralized power generation, while NewTech focuses on decarbonized battery-electric drives
7.
Defense and Other: This newly created segment bundles specialized military propulsion systems alongside exhaust aftertreatment via HJS
8.
Historical division: During 2025, DEUTZ reported under only two segments, namely DEUTZ Engines & Services and DEUTZ Solutions
9.
Revenue dominance: The Engines & Services segment dominated consolidated operations, accounting for 91.1% of total sales in 2025
10.
Solutions exposure: The Solutions segment housed the nascent NewTech and Energy business units, representing 8.9% of consolidated revenue

Revenue Generation and End Markets

11.
Revenue model: DEUTZ generates revenue through a mix of transactional engine sales, long-term supply contracts, and recurring aftermarket service parts

Engine transactions: Conventional diesel and gas engine sales are transactional, recognized at a single point in time upon customer delivery
13.
Contractual supply: Strategic agreements, such as the Daimler Truck partnership, secure predictable multi-year supply volumes and parts revenue. [#g]

Service predictability: Aftermarket services provide highly predictable, recurring revenue, acting as a crucial buffer during economic downturns

Diversified end markets: The company serves highly cyclical industrial applications, alongside rapidly growing, non-cyclical defense and energy markets

Defense expansion: Specialized military vehicle engines, tactical powerpacks, and electric propulsion systems for military drones are growing rapidly. [#g]
