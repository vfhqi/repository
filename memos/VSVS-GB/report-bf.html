Industry Structure and Competitive Positioning

The molten metal flow engineering industry is dominated by a clear global duopoly of Vesuvius and RHI Magnesita, with the top five players controlling a major portion of the high-grade refractory market while the remainder remains highly fragmented. Vesuvius captures an exceptional 50.00% global market share in steel flow control, leading RHI Magnesita and all regional specialists in workflow-embedded ceramic systems. Because continuous casting refractories represent only 2.00% to 3.00% of steelmaking operating costs but are critical to preventing catastrophic plant failures, the margin pool is heavily concentrated in highly engineered, customized flow control products commanding operating margins of 15.00% or higher versus standard refractories at 8.00% to 10.00%. RHI Magnesita achieves over 50.00% raw material self-sufficiency and is 70.00% self-sufficient in basic magnesite, while Vesuvius explicitly avoids upstream mine ownership, choosing instead to procure raw materials through tight, jointly-defined specifications with external suppliers to insulate from high fixed mining costs.

Customer Dynamics and Switching Costs

Vesuvius demonstrates exceptionally low individual customer concentration, with no single account exceeding 10.00% of group revenue, shielding the business from individual defaults compared to RHI Magnesita's top 10 customers accounting for approximately 33.00% of consolidated sales. Customer switching costs are structural and massive, driven by workflow-critical technology secured through over 1,400 active patents globally, with customers facing safety risks and prolonged multi-stage qualification trials requiring simulation and real

operational testing during scheduled maintenance periods. Real pricing pass-through capabilities are proven through-cycle but suffer a near-term lag of one to two quarters, with Vesuvius experiencing a £11.70m net negative profit impact in 1H FY25 from unrecovered cost inflation before successfully re-establishing positive net pricing in 2H FY25.
