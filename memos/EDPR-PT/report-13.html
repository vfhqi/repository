§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

EDPR's guidance posture sits clearly in the over-promiser category at the multi-year Business Plan level, while exhibiting modest conservatism on operational KPIs (MW additions, CapEx). The asymmetry is material for investment case construction.

  • Over-promiser signal: Business Plan EBITDA and net profit targets are consistently set at ambitious levels that embed "stretch" power price assumptions and maximum asset-rotation proceeds; not set to be beaten [#J]

  • Sub: EDPR BP2021 set terminal-year (2025) EBITDA of ~€2.2bn — requiring ~7% CAGR from a ~€1.5bn base; achievable only with both price and volume execution [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — mechanism: Management uses BP targets as capital-allocation signalling tools to justify equity issuance and debt raising; incentive structure biases toward ambitious framing not conservative framing [#J]

  • Sub: this is consistent with EDP parent's guidance culture — EDP itself has a documented history (Citi note, Nov-2023) of BP targets requiring revision downward by ~10-15% by terminal year [#A] [AS-Citi]

  • Sandbagging absent — CapEx exception: CapEx guidance is the one area of modest underpromising — actual CapEx has run ~5-10% below guided spend in FY2021 and FY2022, driven by construction timing deferrals; this is the inverse of the EBITDA pattern [#A]

  • Honest tracker signal — GW additions: GW additions guidance tracks honestly — 3 of 4 years within ±5%, with no consistent over- or under-statement; management appears to have calibrated confidence in its construction pipeline [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — financial vs operational split: A two-speed guidance pattern exists: operational KPIs are honest-to-conservative, financial KPIs are structurally optimistic; this split is diagnostically important — execution is fine, monetisation is the risk [#J]

  • Beat consistency: Zero consistent beat pattern on financial metrics post-2022; any beat during the measurement window was exogenous (power price spike) and thus provides no comfort about management's conservatism [#A]

  • Miss consistency — financial L2Y: FY2023 and FY2024E both missed on EBITDA and net profit; two consecutive years of financial underdelivery post-normalisation is a pattern signal, not noise [#J]

  • Pattern shift: The FY2022 outlier masks what appears to be a structural tendency to over-promise on financials; removing 2022 leaves a 3-year miss streak on net profit vs BP targets [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Average financial guidance miss (ex-2022) is ~-15% on net profit and ~-8% on EBITDA — at the more punishing end of European renewable utility peers (Nordex and Vestas also serial missers but for manufacturing reasons, not EDPR's monetisation reasons) [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the operational credibility (GW adds) provides false comfort — investors may anchor to the clean operational delivery record and discount the structural financial over-promise; the two should not be conflated in IRR modelling [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

EDPR's guidance rests on a layered assumption stack with power price and offshore delivery as the two highest-fragility variables. The sensitivity analysis below quantifies the financial impact of each assumption moving adversely.

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: BP2025 EBITDA guided on ~€80-90/MWh blended realised price Iberia; actual 2023-2024 averaged €65-72/MWh; gap implies €100-180m structural EBITDA shortfall per annum vs plan [#A]

  • Sub: approximately 35-40% of EDPR's generation is uncontracted / short-duration merchant in Iberia; the rest is PPA-contracted at fixed prices averaging €50-60/MWh [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — capacity: 19 GW by YE2025 is achievable based on L4Y additions trajectory; the capacity assumption is the most credible element of the guidance stack [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A/rotation: BP net profit guidance embeds ~€200-300m annual asset rotation gains; these are treated as "other operating income" and are the most volatile revenue line; FY2023 delivered only ~€100m vs plan [#A]

  • Sub: asset rotation timing depends on buyer appetite and yield compression; in 2023-2024, higher interest rates compressed renewable asset transaction volumes by ~30% vs 2021-2022 peak [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Operating cost per MWh (O&M, grid, insurance) tracked within ~5% of BP assumptions in L3Y; cost discipline is a relative strength of EDPR vs peers [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Guidance assumes ~€8-9bn net debt by 2025 at ~4% average cost; actual financing costs have risen to ~4.5-5.0% in 2023-2024 driven by rate environment, adding ~€40-60m annual interest headwind vs plan [#A]

  • Sub: each 50bps increase in marginal borrowing cost on ~€2-3bn of refinancing per year adds ~€10-15m annual interest expense [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided ~20-22%; actual 2023 ETR was ~24% due to higher taxable profits in Spain and lower deferred tax asset usage; +2pp ETR equates to ~€15-20m net profit drag [#A]

  • EPS assumption — FX: ~20-25% of EBITDA generated outside Eurozone (US, Brazil, UK); USD weakness vs EUR adds ~€30-50m translation headwind per 5% USD/EUR move; BP assumed 2021-era FX rates [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: BP2025 built on ~2-3% long-term rate assumption; actual environment 2022-2024 averaged 3-5%; discount rate expansion compressed NAV and increased project financing costs simultaneously [#A]
