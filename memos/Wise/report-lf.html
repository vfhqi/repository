EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Kristo Käärmann is a founder-operator with a mathematics and consulting background who co-created Wise's proprietary peer-to-peer FX model over 15 years , demonstrating exceptional tenure stability versus fintech peers. He holds an 18% equity stake representing approximately 40% of voting rights through dual-class shares , though his lack of external corporate operational experience may leave him poorly equipped to navigate complex regulatory environments independently .

Financial delivery and operational track record

Käärmann delivered spectacular revenue growth to £1,210 million in FY25, up 15% year-on-year , while active customers reached 15.6 million , representing 21% year-on-year expansion proving robust product-market fit . EPS increased by 18% to 40.37p in FY25, demonstrating high operational gearing under his leadership . He scaled share repurchases from £10 million in FY23 to £73 million in FY25 via the Employee Benefit Trust to

neutralize stock-based compensation dilution . Käärmann maintains a strict zero-dividend policy to reinvest all cash into the technology flywheel, outperforming peer capital returns . 2

Regulatory crises and credibility erosion

Käärmann paid a £350,000 FCA fine in October 2024 for deliberate non-disclosure of tax defaults, breaching Senior Management Conduct Rule 4 and compromising corporate governance credibility . The public disclosure caused a material 25% share price decline from its year-to-date high . A Belgian National Bank money-laundering investigation launched in 2025 broke recent share price momentum, focusing on whether Wise accounts laundered half a billion euros in Europe . Wise US paid a $4.2 million penalty in July 2025 following a Multi-State MSB examination, highlighting persistent compliance friction in major markets .

Governance structure and founder entrenchment

Wise utilizes a dual-class share structure where Class A shares carry one vote and Class B shares carry nine votes, renewed for an additional ten-year period expiring in May 2036 . Käärmann holds 18.14% of share capital, translating to 56.28% of total voting power, placing him in the top-decile of UK tech founders . The Articles authorize the Board to issue preferred shares without shareholder approval under a rights plan, representing the single most important defense in Wise's governance scaffolding, allowing active poison pill deployment . PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP has served as Wise's statutory auditor since 2014, representing twelve years of continuous tenure, while management launched a remediation plan after identifying material weaknesses in US GAAP financial reporting controls .
