  • Growth capex: ~80% of total; maintenance/sustaining capex: ~20%

  • Project-finance structure mitigates some risk: debt is non-recourse at asset level; corporate-level leverage lower
  • Corporate net debt (ex. project finance): ~€300-500m est. — more manageable; key question is refinancing risk on project debt

  • Interest cover (EBITDA/net interest): ~3.5-4.0x — adequate but thin; any EBITDA shortfall squeezes coverage

  • Capital returns: No dividend and no buyback — all capital reinvested in growth [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The project-finance structure flatters the leverage picture — consolidating all non-recourse project debt at the group level yields ~6-7x EBITDA leverage, which is aggressive even for a renewable IPP in build-out; rising interest rates in FY 2022-24 have inflated refinancing costs on new project finance tranches; if average cost of project debt on new assets is 5.5-6.5% vs the legacy book at 3-4%, the interest burden will compound faster than EBITDA, pushing FCF inflection further right
