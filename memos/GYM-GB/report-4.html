SECTION 8 — SECTOR CONTEXT

The UK budget gym market is the most relevant context for GYM. Key structural facts:

  • Low-cost segment: 806 clubs, up 8.5% year-on-year, representing 41% of all private sector UK gym members
  • Market value: £986m in 2025, projected to exceed £1bn in 2026
  • Penetration: 16% of UK adults hold a gym membership, below the pre-COVID peak of 18%
  • Growth driver: Millennial and Gen Z health prioritisation, post-COVID increased health consciousness

Post-COVID normalisation is largely complete. The 2022-2024 period of rebuilding member volumes has given way to an organic growth phase driven by pricing power and new site expansion. The "reopening tailwind" is exhausted but the structural demand backdrop remains supportive.

Macro sensitivity: Budget gyms are demonstrating near-counter-cyclical characteristics. During the 2022-2024 cost-of-living crisis, gym memberships grew. Consumer resilience at the £25/month price point is well-evidenced.

Sector Rating: GREEN-YELLOW — Structurally growing market, intensifying competition from PureGym, but GYM's urban positioning and retention improvements create defensible competitive positioning.

SECTION 9 — QUALITY OF EARNINGS

Cash Conversion

GYM converted £56.7m EBITDA LNR to £38.3m FCF — a 68% conversion rate. This is strong for a lease-heavy gym operator. The conversion reflects good working capital management and disciplined maintenance capex of £17.3m (approximately 7% of revenue).

IFRS 16 Impact

IFRS 16 creates significant lease liability recognition on gym operators' balance sheets. GYM's "Non-Property Net Debt" measure strips out lease liabilities to show the underlying financial debt position. At £59.3m, the true financial leverage is low. The gross lease liability is substantially larger but is a matching of future rental obligations — cash already committed to future landlords.

The "Adjusted EBITDA Less Normalised Rent" measure uses a normalised rent charge to remove short-term lease variation. This is the most comparable metric across periods and is the number management guides to (£56.7m in FY2025, targeting £59.6m-£60.7m in FY2026).

NWC

Gyms are inherently negative NWC businesses. Members pay monthly in advance (via direct debit). Costs are incurred as services are delivered. This creates a structural cash inflow bias. GYM's NWC position is not disclosed in detail but the model's negative NWC characteristic is a positive quality-of-earnings indicator.

Maintenance Capex Stability

Maintenance capex of £17.3m in FY2025 vs £51.2m total capex confirms that the majority of capital expenditure is growth-oriented (new sites). Maintenance capex as a percentage of revenue has been stable — a good sign that the existing estate is not degrading.

Quality Rating: GREEN — High FCF conversion, appropriate IFRS 16 disclosure, negative NWC characteristic, and stable maintenance capex confirm earnings quality.

SECTION 10 — SCEPTICAL SUMMARY

Bear Case

The primary earnings risk is a convergence of three negative forces:

1. ARPMM deceleration continues to 2-3%. If the headline Standard membership price is at the ceiling for budget gym positioning, ARPMM growth flatlines. Revenue growth then depends entirely on member count growth, which depends on new sites ramping. The timeline for new-site maturity (18-24 months) means ARPMM deceleration could coincide with a period of sub-mature estate dilution.

  1. New site ROIC disappointment. The 75-site target requires consistently high-quality site selection. If the pipeline includes more second-tier locations than management acknowledges, average ROIC could fall below 25%. This would reduce FCF per marginal invested pound, straining the self-funding narrative.

  2. PureGym competitive squeeze. PureGym's larger estate, superior brand recognition (46% share of search), and international expansion model creates a scale asymmetry. PureGym can out-market GYM on acquisition cost and can be more aggressive on joining fee pricing. If PureGym occupies the best new retail park sites before GYM can execute, the quality of GYM's pipeline deteriorates.

Base Case

ARPMM holds at 3-4% growth. New sites deliver 25-27% ROIC. FY2026 EBITDA LNR reaches the top of the £59.6m-£60.7m guidance range. EPS grows at 15-20% in FY2026 from the FY2025 5.3p base.

Sceptical Rating: YELLOW — Bear case is plausible but not the most likely outcome given management track record, Latchem's property credentials, and structural demand tailwinds.
