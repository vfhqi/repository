Supply-Side Pressures and Operational Headwinds

Vessel procurement is concentrated at China Merchants Jinling Shipyard in Jiangsu , ranking among the top three shipbuilding groups in China , creating shipyard delivery dependency for the 14 dual-fuel Shaper and Shaper+ class vessels scheduled between 2026 and 2028. Net bunker costs surged by USD 12 million quarter-over-quarter in Q1 2026 , driven by escalating Middle East geopolitical conflicts that elevated global marine fuel prices. Red Sea security disruptions force vessel re-routing via the Cape of Good Hope , adding transit days and reducing total fleet capacity by approximately 4 to 6 percent due to longer voyages.

Operator Quality and Strategic Execution

Disruption Forces and Long-Term Structural Evolution

Fleet renewal pivots on the delivery of 14 dual-fuel Shaper-class vessels featuring seven dual-fuel LNG units with ammonia-ready fuel tanks and seven dual-fuel methanol engines, targeting up to 40 percent energy efficiency gains compared to the existing fleet and secured by a two-year bio-methanol agreement with Equinor at the ports of Antwerp and Zeebrugge. The prototype 217-meter Orcelle Wind project targets a 90 percent emission reduction on a single deep-sea voyage, accelerated by a EUR 9 million Horizon Europe research grant shared among eleven partners.

topic-specific reports Long-term near-shoring initiatives by Chinese and Asian OEMs pose a credible drag on ocean tonne-mile demand over a five-to-ten year horizon, with BYD planning to open a 150,000-unit capacity assembly plant in Hungary scheduled for active production by 2026 and the EU's proposed 70 percent local content rules favoring parts suppliers with existing European footprints over exporters. A rapid shift to regionalized automotive manufacturing could permanently shrink deep-sea tonne-mile demand, leaving the expensive Shaper-class fleet underutilized and financially unviable, particularly as heavy vessel orderbooks and near-shoring trends require cautious fleet replacement and asset planning.
