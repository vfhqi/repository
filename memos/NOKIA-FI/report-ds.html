Section 3 – What happened and the consensus deviation 19 findings

Section summary
  1. Nokia's Q1 print and subsequent Google design win completely shattered the consensus baseline across margins, revenue growth, and long-term TAM projections –

Q1 Earnings Delivery and Margin Surprises

1.
Slight revenue undershoot: Q1 comparable revenue printed €4,500m, slightly undershooting Jefferies' consensus of €4,572m by 1.57% due to legacy Fixed Networks softness
2.
Spectacular gross margin beat: Comparable gross margin printed 45.5%, beating Jefferies' estimate of 42.9% by 260 bps due to Infinera synergies
3.
EBIT consensus beat: Comparable operating profit printed €281m vs consensus of €241m, representing a solid 16.60% outperformance
4.
Group guidance tracking: Group level financial outlook for comparable operating profit was held at €2.0-2.5bn, but management noted they are tracking above the midpoint
5.
Guidance raise - Network Infrastructure: Net sales growth guidance for Network Infrastructure was upgraded to 12-14%, up from the prior 6-8% baseline
6.
Guidance raise - IP and Optical: Growth guidance for combined Optical and IP Networks was lifted to 18-20%, up from 10-12% previously raising of the Optical and IP Networks growth guidance
7.
Market growth upgrade - AI and Cloud: Nokia raised its AI & Cloud addressable market CAGR through 2028 to 27%, up from 16% in November

Segment Level Performance and Mix Shift

8.
Optical Networks acceleration: Optical Networks net sales grew 20% on an adjusted basis, beating consensus and accelerating from 17% in Q4
9.
IP Networks acceleration profile: IP Networks grew 3% in Q1 but management guided to sequential acceleration throughout the year
10.
AI and Cloud order boom: High-growth AI & Cloud revenues grew 49% YoY, accounting for 8% of total group sales with €1bn in fresh bookings
11.
Fixed Networks transition: Fixed Networks declined 13% but gross margins improved as Nokia intentionally exited low-margin customer premises equipment
12.
Mobile Infrastructure margin turnaround: Mobile Infrastructure printed solid results with an 8.9% operating margin, expanding 380 bps YoY due to cost discipline

Strategic Hyperscaler Wins and Tech Agreements

13.
Google switching design win: Deutsche Bank's Robert Sanders revealed a material next-gen Google campus switching win within IP Networks, adding €400m in sales
14.
Coherent Lite tailwind: The Google switching win validates traction for Coherent Lite, a power-optimized campus-wide interconnect architecture
15.
FWA business disposal: Inseego Corp agreed to acquire Nokia's Fixed Wireless Access business, allowing Nokia to streamline its AI data center focus
16.
Gemini AI partnership: Expanded Google Cloud partnership to integrate Gemini-powered AI agents into Nokia's Assurance Center for dynamic network automation
17.
NVIDIA GPU partnership: Nokia expanded collaboration with NVIDIA to integrate its switching and optical technologies into future GPU-based AI-RAN architectures
18.
Other side of the story: While the Google switching win is transformative, it is highly concentrated, and failing to secure follow-on hyperscaler wins poses downside risk

Ranked Cause 1 - Google switching contract: High plausibility due to the €400m incremental sales and trebling of the datacenter optical and switching business pipeline
