Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What it does. AddLife distributes and resells medical technology, laboratory equipment and life-science consumables across Northern Europe, operating a highly decentralised model of locally-run subsidiary businesses
2.

3.
Recent portfolio actions. AddLife divested its UK endoscopy distribution business for SEK 158m after a key OEM partner shifted to direct sales, and closed Camanio, removing roughly SEK 15m of quarterly operating cost
4.
Recent bolt-on acquisitions. The company acquired Edge Medical (UK orthopaedics), BonsaiLab (Spanish molecular biology), BioSpectrum (UK urology/gynaecology distribution, GBP 6m annual revenue) and CoaChrom Diagnostica, adding roughly SEK 185m combined annual revenue
5.
Financial character. Adjusted EBITA margin reached 12.1% in FY2025, up from 11.3% in FY2024
5.1

Cash conversion profile. The business converts free cash flow to earnings per share at 124% over the past decade, the top of the European compounder peer group, ahead of Diploma's 97%
7.
Capital allocation. Net investment in non-current assets was SEK 254m in 2025, mostly customer-rental instruments; the board proposed a dividend of SEK 1.50 per share for 2025, within the group's 30-50% payout policy

Geography and sourcing. Over 90% of revenue is generated in Europe with roughly 80% sourced locally; exposure to North America is under 10% and to China under 5%, a low geopolitical footprint
9.
Leadership and governance. Chief executive Fredrik Dalborg has led since late 2022 driving a "back to basics" decentralisation strategy; chief financial officer Christina Rubenhag anchors working-capital discipline; no activist presence is identified in the AlphaSense library
