§1 — Current Management Guidance

Judges Scientific does not issue formal point-estimate numerical guidance in the manner of a large-cap industrial. Management's communications are deliberately qualitative and strategy-framed, consistent with the company's owner-operator culture and small-cap governance posture. The following reflects the most current guidance statements available from the FY23 full-year results release (March 2024) and accompanying RNS and analyst Q&A.

  • Net signal: Management signals continued organic growth + acquisition-led compounding, with no explicit revenue or EPS target — conservatism by omission rather than by sandbagging a number [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Management describes organic revenue trajectory as "satisfactory progress" across subsidiaries; no numerical organic growth target stated; implies continuation of mid-single-digit organic growth consistent with recent history [#A]

  • Sub: FY23 group revenue was £116.9m (FY22: £95.7m, +22% total; organic component estimated by SS at ~6-8%)

  • FY current — EBIT: Management reaffirms commitment to maintaining group EBIT margins "in excess of 20%"; FY23 delivered 21.3% EBIT margin; no explicit forward margin target beyond the ">20%" aspiration [#A]

  • Sub: The ">20%" framing has been consistent since FY17 and functions as a de-facto floor guidance

  • FY current — EPS: ❌ No explicit EPS guidance issued; management does not provide forward earnings per share targets; SS consensus constructs this number independently [#A]

  • Sub: FY23 adjusted EPS was ~435p; SS FY24 consensus (Peel Hunt, Singer, Cavendish) clusters around 470-490p range at the time of FY23 results

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF guidance; management characterises capital allocation as "acquisition-led" and describes FCF generation as strong but secondary to reinvestment priority; FY23 operating cash conversion was ~88% of EBIT [#A]

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guidance is minimal — typically described as maintenance-level for organic business (~£3-4m annually); major capital deployment is via acquisition [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y/N3Y: No formal medium-term numerical targets; management articulates the model as "acquire high-quality scientific instrument businesses, grow organically, repeat" without a specified revenue or EPS destination [#A]

  • Sub: Investor Day (none held in standard format); guidance delivery via Annual Report narrative + results RNS + analyst Q&A only

  • Source — written: Primary guidance vehicle is Annual Report strategic narrative + RNS results releases; no standalone investor day presentations; no formal guidance booklets [#I]

  • Update timing: JDG updates twice annually — Interims (typically September) and Full Year (typically March); no trading updates, pre-close calls, or quarterly guidance revisions [#A]

  • Sub: Guidance opacity is high relative to larger-cap peers; investors receive only two formal data points per year

  • Caveat — M&A timing: Management explicitly caveats that revenue and earnings trajectory depend on acquisition timing, which is unpredictable; "we cannot guide on the M&A pipeline" is the standing caveat [#A]

  • Caveat — organic variability: Management notes that individual subsidiary performance can be lumpy (orders can be large and project-based), creating intra-year volatility that does not reflect trend [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: The implicit assumption underpinning management's positive outlook is that the pool of acquirable UK/European scientific instrument businesses at 5-8x EBIT multiples remains accessible; management has not publicly acknowledged deal multiple inflation as a risk [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The absence of numerical guidance is strategically convenient — it prevents formal miss attribution; "satisfactory progress" and ">20% EBIT" are sufficiently vague that management can claim delivery even in deteriorating conditions; the lack of an investor day or formal targets presentation means there is no accountability anchor for medium-term commitments [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Net signal: JDG management is a systematic sandbagging operator — qualitative guidance is set conservatively, actuals consistently beat directional framing; posture reflects the owner-operator culture and preference for under-promise/over-deliver [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: Management's language ("satisfactory," "continued progress," "healthy order books") is consistently more conservative than subsequent delivery; no instance found of management framing an outcome positively that was not subsequently delivered [#A]

  • Beat consistency: EBIT margin has beaten the ">20%" floor aspiration in 6 of 7 years of available data (FY17-FY23); the pattern is structural not cyclical [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat: Typical beat vs margin floor: +100-200 bps on EBIT margin; typical beat vs SS EPS consensus: +3-10% in non-COVID years — consistent with a systematically conservative communications posture [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal: No evidence of management over-promising on any tracked metric; JDG does not issue stretch targets or investor-day compounding targets that would create over-promise risk [#I]

  • Honest tracker: The "honest tracker" descriptor is not quite right for JDG — management's guidance is not precise enough to track honestly; the posture is better described as "strategic ambiguity + conservative delivery" [#J]

  • Pattern shift: No evidence of a pattern shift in recent periods; FY23 guidance posture is consistent with FY17-FY22 pattern; no drift toward aggressive framing detectable in language analysis of management commentary [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: ❌ Accuracy cannot strictly be said to be improving because precision of guidance has not increased; management has not moved toward more formal numerical guidance despite scale growth from ~£30m to ~£117m revenue [#A]

  • Sandbagging mechanism: The sandbagging is structural rather than strategic — management genuinely cannot predict acquisition timing, so it issues conservative organic framing; the M&A optionality then creates upside vs. any given base expectation [#J]

  • Beat consistency — SS EPS: SS EPS consensus has been beaten in each of FY21, FY22, FY23; magnitude declining (FY21: +17%, FY22: +7%, FY23: +3%) — suggests SS is progressively closing the gap on acquisition impact modelling [#A]

  • Sceptical read: Apparent sandbagging may be approaching exhaustion — SS consensus has been progressively catching up; the FY23 EPS beat was only ~3% vs consensus, the narrowest in the L3Y period; if SS fully closes the modelling gap, the "beat" optionality that underpins positive price momentum diminishes [#J]

  • Margin assumption — cost: JDG's subsidiary model is low-fixed-cost; subsidiaries are lean, owner-operated (post-acquisition), with minimal central overhead; margin assumption of ">20% EBIT" depends on subsidiaries not being burdened with inappropriate cost allocation [#A]

  • Sub: Central cost growth is a watchpoint as JDG's head office function scales to manage 25+ subsidiaries

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage is modest in the JDG model — revenue growth from acquisitions does not generate the same incremental margin uplift as organic revenue growth in a fixed-cost business [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Niche scientific instruments typically face limited commoditisation; pricing power assumed to remain stable at LSD% annually [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: UK corporation tax rate assumed ~25% (post April 2023 increase); no guidance-level sensitivity to tax provided; relevant given JDG is entirely UK-domiciled [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: JDG has minimal share issuance history; M&A is predominantly cash-funded (bank debt + FCF); share count broadly stable; SS models assume dilution risk is low [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt / EBITDA typically maintained at ~1-1.5x post-acquisition; management targets leverage recovery to ~1x within 12 months of each deal; assumption of continued bank facility availability at current terms [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: UK/European R&D spending broadly correlated with GDP growth; 2024-2025 consensus GDP growth ~1-2% for UK; supports organic revenue assumption without requiring acceleration [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: JDG generates approximately 50-60% of revenue internationally (USD/EUR denominated); management does not hedge systematically; sterling strengthening creates EPS headwind not explicitly modelled in management framing [#J]
