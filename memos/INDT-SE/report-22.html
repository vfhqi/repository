§1 — KQ Definition, Mission Command Brief, and Competing Hypotheses

J-front: H2 (partial recovery, structurally impaired) has fewest hard inconsistencies; H1 (full recovery/durable long) fails on governance and infrastructure legs; H3 (pure value-trap) fails on asset quality evidence

KQ Stated

Is Ferrexpo's operational and financial recovery from Ukraine-war disruption a real, durable structural investment thesis warranting a long position, or a value-trap obscured by geopolitical noise and cyclical iron ore dynamics?

Higher Intent

  • Decision on the critical path: whether to advance Ferrexpo to portfolio candidate status or park/kill — the entire investment case rests on whether the asset quality + recovery optionality can overcome governance, geopolitical, and infrastructure constraints within a 12–36 month holding horizon
  • Why this is a load-bearing KQ: Ferrexpo's market cap has compressed dramatically from 2021 highs; the bull case requires normalising operations, the bear case requires internalising that "normalised" may never return — getting this wrong in either direction (premature long OR premature kill) has large IRR consequence

Competing Hypotheses — Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive

H2 — Partial Recovery / Structurally Impaired Ferrexpo recovers to a permanently lower steady-state (4–7 Mtpa) due to infrastructure, governance, and capex-cycle damage — real value exists but full pre-war normalisation is not achievable within the investment horizon Yes — falsified if volumes recover above 8 Mtpa OR if governance is fully resolved with institutional-grade ownership structure

  • Investment horizon: 12–24 months (primary), with optionality horizon extending to 36 months if war-resolution signal emerges
