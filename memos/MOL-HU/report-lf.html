EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

NET VERDICT ON CEO AND DUAL-EXECUTIVE ARCHITECTURE

MOL operates an uncommon dual-executive leadership architecture , dividing corporate and operational responsibilities between Chairman-CEO Zsolt Hernádi , who has served since June 2001, and Group CEO József Molnár , who has led operational execution since May 2011. This structure represents one of the most stable executive configurations in the European downstream sector. MOL's executive stability is exceptionally high, contrasting with the more frequent management turnover observed at major European oil peers. By comparison, Repsol CEO Josu Jon Imaz has served 12 years since April 2014, while OMV CEO Alfred Stern is departing in August 2026 after a five-year tenure. The net verdict on the CEO pairing is cautiously constructive : the tandem delivers deep regional operator credibility and a highly conservative financial posture, but the

concentration of personal authority and the advanced age of both executives – Hernádi at 66 and Molnár at 70 – represent a structurally elevated key-person risk that peers have deliberately engineered away. Hernádi's banking background contrasts with the technical profiles of peer CEOs, while Molnár's 48 years of chemical industry and financial reorganization experience provides operational grounding. 1

GOVERNANCE ROBUSTNESS AND STRUCTURAL VULNERABILITIES

The governance assessment is the single most structurally weak dimension of the MOL-HU investment thesis. Only 60% of MOL-HU's board is independent, representing 6 out of 10 directors, compared to OMV's 100% independence among shareholder representatives and Repsol's 66.67% independence ratio. MOL-HU exhibits extraordinary director tenure entrenchment, led by Hernádi since February 1999, while Deputy Chairman Sándor Csányi has served since October 2000, creating 25 years of continuous board influence. MOL-HU combines its compensation and nomination duties into a single Corporate Governance and Remuneration Committee, and Hernádi himself sits on this committee, breaching standard non-executive recommendations. The combined Board and Supervisory Board is 95.5% male and only 4.5% female, starkly contrasting with Repsol's 40% female representation and OMV's 34% female Supervisory Board. The structural ownership architecture compounds these concerns: MOL-HU is insulated from activism by foundations including MOL New Europe holding 10.5%, while the Hungarian State holds a single Series "B" preferred share that grants a permanent veto over critical corporate decisions including board elections, profit distributions, and amendments to the Articles of Association. The board's extreme length of service creates a captured oversight dynamic, which fails to challenge key executive capital allocations. 11 13 14

INSIDER TRANSACTION SIGNALS AND OWNERSHIP ALIGNMENT

Chairman-CEO Hernádi maintains substantial skin-in-the-game holding 818,549 shares directly as of December 2025, while Group CEO Molnár holds 312,613 shares directly, with this heavy combined direct executive ownership of over 1.13 million shares representing the highest long-term insider alignment among regional peers. Sándor Csányi controls 2.64 million indirect shares, representing the deepest regional skin-in-the-game among board members. These are genuinely constructive alignment signals. However, open-market buying or selling by MOL-HU directors has been extremely thin over the last 12 months which tempers the signal quality. Against peers, Repsol executive directors must hold shares equivalent in value to three times their fixed annual remuneration, and CEO Imaz holds 934,917 Repsol shares , representing over ten times his fixed pay. The absence of active open-market purchases at MOL-HU, in a period of deep valuation discounting driven by windfall taxes and pipeline disruption, may indicate management caution rather than conviction. 19 21

In contrast, PKN Orlen suffered wholesale senior leadership replacements following early 2024 government interventions, and OMV faces leadership changes with bp's Emma Delaney succeeding Alfred Stern in September 2026.
