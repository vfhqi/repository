EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

and Strategic Positioning The group maintains 136 operational facilities situated across 22 countries to support global supply chains locally , creating network effects that allow customer work redirection during equipment breakdowns and leverage shared logistics, trucking routes, and central sales channels to lower unit shipping expenses . End market exposure comprises 34% aerospace and defence, 22% automotive, 22% industrial markets, 11% energy, and 11% medical and other applications , with manufacturers outsourcing to reduce internal capital outlays and

avoid up to 60% of carbon emissions compared to typical in-house atmospheric processing . CEO Jim Fairbairn succeeded Stephen Harris in May 2024, initiated a comprehensive plant footprint review, and rebuilt the leadership with 50% of the executive team joining over the last two years . The Optimise, Perform, and Grow strategy focuses on closing underperforming sites, driving standardisation through the proprietary HEAT framework targeting 100bps margin lift by 2028, and increasing Specialist Technologies to 35-40% of group revenue by 2028 . 5 6 7

19 Dynamics and Industry Structure Bodycote holds a leading 15% share of the £6bn outsourced market within a £30bn total thermal processing sector, operating 136 sites globally far outpacing nearest competitors . The market consists of a consolidated top-tier duopoly with Aalberts NV as global number two generating €1.09bn in Industrial division

revenues, followed by highly fragmented regional family-run shops and specialized coating competitors like Oerlikon and Linde . The North American landscape consolidated when Aalberts acquired Paulo Products for €150m in December 2024, with Paulo generating c.€100m annually, moving Aalberts' US market share closer to leader Bodycote . The threat of customers vertically integrating backward remains low due to substantial upfront furnace capital costs exceeding £500k per high-capacity vacuum furnace, requiring multiple units plus scarce metallurgy talent, strict certifications, and extensive regulatory approvals . Tightening carbon regulations act as a major catalyst driving manufacturers to convert captive operations to outsourced partners, with outsourcing lowering customer process-level emissions by up to 60% via commercial facility aggregation .

and Long-Term Growth Vectors Additive manufacturing represents an opportunity rather than a disruption threat, as 3D-printed metal parts contain internal microporosity that must be eliminated via hot isostatic pressing to prevent premature fatigue failures , while advanced thermal spray processes are actively displacing hazardous traditional plating methods due to tightening environmental standards . The group is expanding its exposure to high-margin adjacencies including food sector adoption supporting hygiene compliance, medical market expansion in orthopedic implants generating margins exceeding 40%, and semiconductor cooling demands from artificial intelligence server deployments requiring vacuum furnace brazing . Decarbonisation acts as a commercial catalyst, with tightening global emission regulations accelerating supply chain migration from inefficient in-house customer ovens, delivering up to 60% carbon emission reductions via commercial outsourcing compared to typical captive manufacturer processing . The group has formalised environmental commitments by linking executive remuneration to validated decarbonisation goals, strengthening its emissions target to a validated 46% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2030, and redesigning its long-term incentive plan with 20% of awards linked

directly to science-based emissions targets . 33
