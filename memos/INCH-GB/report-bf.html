EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 23 findings

Inchcape's transition to a pure-play global distribution model has structural benefits that drive higher-margin earnings, with diversified exposure to high-growth emerging economies offset by competitive pressures in Asia-Pacific, enhanced by proprietary digital infrastructure and strategic leadership transitions under Jerry Buhlmann.

Strategic Positioning and Business Model

1.
Inchcape completed its transition to a pure-play automotive distribution business after selling UK Retail assets in Q3 2024, raising £346m in cash consideration to fund future high-return bolt-on acquisitions while shifting the group mix toward higher-margin, capital-light distribution contracts globally
2.
Inchcape manages the end-to-end value chain from factory logistics to final vehicle delivery in small-to-medium-sized markets, with this independent distributor model highly valued by OEMs in complex regions requiring deep local expertise
3.
The company operates across three core global regions, with Americas generating £3.3bn in revenue (36% of total), Europe & Africa delivering £3.3bn, and Asia Pacific contributing £2.5bn in FY2025
4.
Distribution revenues are split between high-volume new vehicle sales (85% of revenue, 10-15% gross margins) and highly profitable aftermarket spare parts (15% of revenue, 40-45% gross margins)

Financial Profile and Return Characteristics

5.
The pure-play distribution transition drives superior ROCE of 29% and cash conversion of 104%, with balance sheet de-risking compounding excess cash returns, returning £1.3bn to shareholders since 2019 via buybacks and dividends
6.
Pure-play distribution generated £315m of free cash flow from continuing operations in FY2025, achieving exceptional 104% FCF to adjusted profit after tax conversion, with management targeting £2.5bn in cumulative free cash flow between FY2025 and FY2030
7.
Return on Capital Employed increased to 29% in FY2025 from 27% in FY2024, with structural mix benefit from transitioning away from low-margin retail improving capital efficiency from 22% in FY2019, remaining highly consistent with through-the-cycle guidance of 25-30%
8.
Inchcape is a clear return outlier with 29% ROCE, driven by a capital-light, pure-play distribution model

Industry Structure and Value Chain Economics

9.
The global vehicle population in use has surpassed 1.5 billion units, with this expanding global vehicle population guaranteeing stable, long-term replacement demand for aftermarket parts

Independent vehicle distribution achieves highly attractive financial returns through superior capital efficiency, with Inchcape's pure-play distribution model delivering a 6.2% adjusted operating margin in FY2025, supported by negative working capital cycles and capex below 1% of revenues
11.
The aftersales parts and service stage is the single most profitable component of downstream operations, with replacement parts distribution yielding outstanding gross margins between 40% and 45%, generating over 46% of total dealership gross profits
12.
Economic value is shifting away from midstream vehicle manufacturing toward downstream mobility services, with recurring service models like connected services and finance comprising up to one-third of value by 2040
