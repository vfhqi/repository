  • Bear case: organic growth reverts to 2-3% as Autodesk transition headwinds persist through 2026, European industrial capex softens, and M&A multiples rise as deal competition intensifies

  • Single largest risk: The Autodesk perpetual-to-subscription transition is the single largest structural risk to the Design Management business model (~50% of group revenues) [#J]

  • This is not a new risk, but it is a sustained headwind that management's public commentary has downplayed relative to sell-side concerns

  • Early warning signals: Three potential early-warning indicators from available data: (1) Design Management organic growth returning to negative territory, (2) Process Management win rates stalling as Aveva/Siemens PLM compete more aggressively, (3) M&A deal quality deteriorating (paying higher multiples for smaller, less strategic businesses) [#A]

  • Breaking points: The thesis breaks most sharply if adjusted EBIT margins stall below 7% through 2025-26 while the market is pricing a 8-10% medium-term target; consensus will cut forecasts more aggressively than the margin miss itself warrants [#A]

  • Peer-relative downside: If Vitec Software and Hexagon continue to compound at 20-22% EBIT margins while Addnode is stuck at 6%, the relative multiple disadvantage widens; Addnode cannot command a software multiple while carrying reseller economics [#J]
