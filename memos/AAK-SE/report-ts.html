Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. AAK offers a highly predictable earnings compounding model driven by high-margin cocoa butter alternatives and structural cost optimization despite temporary volume headwinds.
1.
Favourable business economics: AAK tailors plant-based oils and fats using a customer co-development approach to replace animal fats and fossil-based ingredients in food products. This model creates highly customized products that are technically integrated into customers' supply chains
2.
Robust investment thesis: The core opportunity lies in the accelerating adoption of cocoa butter alternatives due to high cocoa butter prices, supported by capacity utilization recovery and cost optimization. This is expected to drive double-digit earnings growth over the holding period
3.
Critical earnings driver: The single most important swing factor for earnings is the spread between cocoa butter and specialty shea fats, which dictates chocolate manufacturers' reformulation speed. This spread directly impacts the highly profitable confectionery division
4.
Excellent historical compounding: Operating profit grew at an 18% CAGR since 2020, rising from SEK 2.20 billion to SEK 4.68 billion in FY2025. This was driven by strategic pricing and operational efficiency
5.
Substantial near-term recovery: Volumes returned to 2.4% year-on-year growth in Q1 2026, breaking a five-quarter streak of volume contraction. This indicates that customer destocking and macro headwinds are beginning to wane
6.
Vulnerable downside risk: A sharp, prolonged collapse in cocoa butter prices could reduce the economic incentive for customers to substitute cocoa butter with AAK's alternatives. This would slow down growth in the lucrative CCF segment
7.
Disciplined pricing power: AAK hedges over 98% of its raw material volumes through back-to-back contracts, which completely insulates its operating profit from severe commodity price fluctuations
8.
Compelling initial verdict: AAK's current valuation of 13.0x 2026e EV/EBIT is a significant discount to its 10-year NTM average of 17.5x, offering strong IRR potential. This mismatch represents an attractive entry point

Section 3 – What the company does 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The customer co-development model positions AAK as an essential partner in the food supply chain, generating sticky relationships and premium margins.

Core product segments and operational setup

1.
Favourable business segments: AAK operates in three business areas: Food Ingredients (external sales SEK 28.19bn), Chocolate & Confectionery Fats (SEK 15.52bn), and Technical Products & Feed (SEK 2.32bn) in FY2025
2.
Premium confectionery profitability: The Chocolate & Confectionery Fats segment is the primary profit engine, contributing approximately 40% of group operating profit. This division produces high-value cocoa butter equivalents that replicate the melting profile and mouthfeel of real chocolate
3.
Stable customer integration: The Food Ingredients segment serves bakery, dairy, foodservice, and special nutrition subsectors, offering tailored fat blends that improve product quality and shelf life
4.
Low-margin technical products: Technical Products & Feed operates locally in Northern Europe, selling bypass fats and proteins to agricultural cooperatives. Lower margins but useful synergistic links to primary refining facilities

Sourcing dynamics and value chain positioning

5.
Downstream refining position: AAK operates downstream in the global food value chain, purchasing crude and semi-refined vegetable oils from crushers and refineries. This downstream focus shields the business from the capital intensity of farming and crushing
6.
Highly fragmented sourcing: Company sources a diverse basket of raw materials: 51% palm oil, 12% rapeseed oil, 12% palm kernel oil, 5% shea kernels, and other vegetable oils globally
7.
Extensive customer fragmentation: AAK serves more than 4,000 customers globally, including major multinational FMCG brands and small regional food producers. High customer fragmentation limits individual client bargaining power
8.
Excellent contractual protection: Margins are protected through a sophisticated hedging model where AAK locks in raw material costs and sales prices simultaneously, leaving less than 2% of annual volumes unhedged

Moats, barriers to entry, and industry structure

9.
Durable co-development moat: AAK's primary competitive advantage is its customer co-development approach, acting as an extension of the customer's R&D department to formulate bespoke solutions. This co-development model builds high switching costs that protect against competitor encroachment
10.
Extensive innovation infrastructure: AAK operates 16 customer innovation centers and 19 production plants globally, enabling close collaboration and short time-to-market for new formulations
11.
Consolidating specialty market: While the total edible oils market is vast at 200 million metric tons, the specialty fat segment accounts for only 5% of volumes. AAK maintains a dominant 20% share in this profitable specialty subsector
12.
Other side of the story: Slower customer adoption of plant-based foods or a secular decline in bakery demand could result in permanent capacity underutilization, eroding pricing power and forcing AAK to take lower-margin commodity work

Section 5 – The possible investment case 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case is highly compelling, driven by a structural shift to cocoa butter substitutes, factory optimization, and a SEK 300 million cost-savings program.

Structural cocoa butter alternatives substitution

1.
Accretive product substitution: Elevated global cocoa butter prices encourage chocolate manufacturers to reformulate products using AAK's cheaper cocoa butter equivalents, boosting CCF segment profitability
2.
High probability plausibility: This driver is highly plausible as customers who reformulate their products rarely switch back to expensive cocoa butter, even as commodity prices normalize

Focused commercial volume push

3.
Sustained volume recovery: Management is implementing plant-by-plant commercial initiatives to drive volume growth, utilizing its 15% to 20% available production capacity

Structural productivity and cost optimization

4.
Favourable margin support: The Fit-to-Win cost optimization program launched in 2025 is scheduled to deliver SEK 300 million in annual savings by mid-2026
5.
High cost-savings plausibility: Achieving the cost-savings target is highly plausible as SEK 50 million in savings was delivered in 2025
6.
Independent compound drivers: The forward earnings trajectory does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously; the cost-savings program and cocoa substitution are independent of macroeconomic volume recovery

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 7 findings

Section summary
  1. The overall risk profile is moderate, with short-term cash flow volatility and ESG palm oil compliance acting as the main watch items.

ESG and supply chain sourcing risks

1.
Responsible palm oil sourcing: Palm oil represents 51% of AAK's raw material intake, exposing the company to regulatory and reputational risks associated with rainforest deforestation
2.
Strict regulatory compliance: The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) mandates full traceability of palm and soy crops, requiring AAK to invest SEK 100 million in facility upgrades

Cash flow volatility and product quality risks

3.
Temporary working capital lock-up: Rising commodity prices cause massive working capital drag, with an expected SEK 1.10 billion cash outflow in H2 2026
4.
Devastating food safety failure: A severe contamination incident represents the single failure mode that would destroy customer trust and disrupt long-term co-development relationships
5.
Favourable monitoring signal: The key early-warning metric is the spread between cocoa butter and specialty fats, indicating the economic incentive for chocolate manufacturers to substitute ingredients
6.
Other side of the story: Sourcing and commodity margin compression risks are highly overstated because AAK hedges over 98% of its annual raw material volumes, isolating operating profit from price spikes
