Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. UCB is a high-quality biopharma player with a profit-favoured positioning in high-barrier immunology and neurology therapeutic areas.

Product Portfolio and Segments

1.
Immunology blockbuster lead: Bimzelx (bimekizumab) is a novel dual IL-17A and IL-17F inhibitor approved for five key immunology indications. It generated a remarkable €2.23 billion in net sales in FY2025, making it the largest drug in UCB's portfolio
2.
Legacy anti-TNF revenue: Cimzia (certolizumab pegol) remains a highly stable cash cow treating rheumatoid arthritis, Crohn's disease, and plaque psoriasis. It generated €1.95 billion in FY2025 sales, which was a mild 4.00% decline from the previous year due to net price pressure
3.
Rare disease segment: Fintepla (fenfluramine), acquired via the €1.52 billion Zogenix acquisition, is approved for Dravet syndrome and Lennox-Gastaut syndrome. It posted strong sales of €427.00 million in FY2025, which was a 26.00% increase over the prior year
4.
Myasthenia Gravis franchise: UCB's newly launched generalized myasthenia gravis franchise consists of Rystiggo (rozanolixizumab) and Zilbrysq (zilucoplan). These assets contributed €332.00 million and €217.00 million in sales during FY2025, establishing UCB as a key player
5.
Osteoporosis partnership income: Evenity (romosozumab) is co-marketed globally with Amgen, contributing €137.00 million in direct European sales. It also yields an exceptionally profitable 50/50 global profit-share recognized by UCB under other operating income, which reached €481.00 million in 2024

Value Chain and Moat Dynamics

6.
High-margin value capture: Operating in the discovery and commercialization stages of the value chain allows UCB to capture exceptionally high gross margins. Adjusted gross profit margin expanded from 78.30% in 2024 to 79.20% in 2025 due to a favorable shift toward higher-margin biologics
7.
Durable competitive advantage: The company's durable competitive advantage is secured by deep clinical differentiation and long-dated patents. Bimzelx's patent protection extends to 2036-37 in key markets, providing a long runway of exclusivity
8.
Extreme customer concentration: UCB is highly dependent on major US pharmaceutical wholesalers to distribute its commercial drug products. McKesson represents 17.00% of global net sales, while Cencora and Cardinal Health represent 8.00% each, exposing UCB to distributor pressure

Industry Structure

9.
Crowded immunology landscape: The immunology space is characterized by massive, well-established biologic drug platforms. Bimzelx faces direct competition from AbbVie's IL-23 inhibitor Skyrizi and Novartis' IL-17A inhibitor Cosentyx
10.
Neurology niche leadership: UCB enjoys a strong first-mover advantage in epilepsy and a growing presence in the highly lucrative generalized myasthenia gravis market. The generalized myasthenia gravis market is expected to double to $10.00+ billion, offering substantial room for expansion
11.
Other side of the story: Intense therapeutic crowding from well-capitalized competitors like AbbVie, which generated $17.50 billion in Skyrizi sales in 2025, could limit UCB's market share expansion. AbbVie has set an extremely high bar with its dominant commercial sales force and extensive rebate contracts with payers

Section summary

Key Driver – Bimzelx Global Rollout

1.
Unprecedented market penetration: Bimzelx has captured over 32.00% of US dynamic patient share within 10 months of launch, signaling rapid clinician adoption. The drug has already been rolled out to over 50 countries, serving over 116,000 patients
2.
High clinical plausibility: Clinical trial BE BOLD demonstrated Bimzelx's scientific superiority over market-leading blockbusters, with a 49.10% ACR50 response rate versus 38.40% for Skyrizi at Week 16. Over 58.00% of surveyed rheumatologists expect to increase their use of Bimzelx following these head-to-head results
3.
Rollout failure mechanism: The primary risk to this driver is payer/formulary access, which 40.00% of surveyed physicians cited as the most significant adoption hurdle. High gross-to-net discount rates may erode realized margins if UCB must offer larger rebates to secure formulary access

Key Driver – Myasthenia Gravis Expansion

4.
Dual-asset platform: Launching Rystiggo and Zilbrysq addresses high unmet needs in generalized myasthenia gravis, targeting a market expected to expand to $10.00+ billion. Rystiggo and Zilbrysq net sales reached €332.00 million and €217.00 million in FY2025, showing strong launch velocity
5.
Medium clinical plausibility: Competitors such as argenx's Vyvgart hold over 50.00% share of this market, presenting a severe commercial barrier to UCB's late-entrant assets. Vyvgart's established first-mover advantage has secured deep clinician familiarity and strong patient loyalty
6.
Expansion failure mechanism: The driver fails if daily injections of Zilbrysq face patient compliance issues compared to Vyvgart's cyclical dosing options. Patients may refuse daily self-injection regimens in favor of Vyvgart's subcutaneous prefilled syringe convenience

Key Driver – Evenity Partnership Accretion

7.
Highly lucrative profit-share: UCB's co-marketing of Evenity with Amgen resulted in a massive €481.00 million net operating contribution in FY2024. This partnership represents UCB's most margin-accretive asset, carrying an operating margin estimated at 63.00%
8.
High commercial plausibility: Evenity's global sales momentum is highly durable, with peak global sales expected to exceed €1.80 billion by 2030. Strong osteoporosis prescription trends continue to support upward revisions in other operating income
9.
Accretion failure mechanism: Driver failure would occur if competitor launches or safety concerns, such as cardiovascular warnings, disrupt sales growth. Any regulatory or safety setback would directly hit the co-promotion income reported on UCB's other operating income line

Synthesis of Bull Case Dynamics

10.
Independent growth paths: Crucially, these drivers do not require simultaneous success to yield an highly attractive 25%+ IRR, making the equity story robust. The independent market dynamics of Evenity and Bimzelx provide substantial margin of safety for investors
11.
Other side of the story: The bull case would be materially broken if the US FDA mandates changes to Bimzelx's direct-to-consumer advertising or if safety warnings regarding candidiasis infections depress script volume. Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk picture remains highly credible but manageable, centered primarily on clinical pipeline competition and mature asset price cuts.

Direct Pipeline Threats

1.
Immuno-dermatology competitive threats: In hidradenitis suppurativa, Novartis' selective oral BTK inhibitor remibrutinib represents the most credible threat to Bimzelx, with Phase 3 data expected in late 2026. Remibrutinib has potential to act as an earlier-line oral competitor, disrupting UCB's biologic patient flow
2.
Next-generation biologic entrants: High-efficacy long-acting biologics such as Oruka's ORKA-001 demonstrate high skin clearance in psoriasis, threatening to disrupt Bimzelx's market positioning. ORKA-001's potential for infrequent annual dosing poses a structural threat to Bimzelx's established treatment paradigm

Policy and Patent Exposures

3.
Severe Medicare policy risk: In early 2026, the US government selected Cimzia for Medicare price negotiations under the Inflation Reduction Act, effective in 2028. This price negotiation will severely impact Cimzia's high-margin US revenue, which reached €1.30 billion in 2024
4.
Severe gross-to-net discount headwind: Pricing pressure in the US is intensifying, with Bimzelx's gross-to-net discounts averaging a massive 44.00% to 46.00% throughout 2025. High rebate rates to secure insurance coverage represent a persistent headwind to net realized pricing

Sceptical and Watch Signals

5.
Unusual false friend signal: UCB's recent reported double-digit volume growth in Cimzia is a false friend, masking a net price decline that led to a 4.00% drop in overall sales. This highlights that volume gains are being purchased at the expense of severe price discounting
6.
Early warning monitor signal: Investors must closely monitor weekly IQVIA prescription unit growth, as any sequential deceleration below 0.70% would risk missing FY2026 US consensus. Slow script trends indicate that the commercial launch is proving less dynamic than expected
7.
Other side of the story: The impact of biosimilars on Cimzia is likely to be heavily delayed, with analysts expecting no commercial launch of Cimzia biosimilars before 2028 or 2029. This delay provides UCB with a vital cash-flow buffer to fund its pipeline expansions
