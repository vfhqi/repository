§2 — Key Driver: Subscriber Base Stabilisation + Churn Normalisation

Net signal: this is the primary fulcrum and the most fragile driver — churn has been structurally elevated for 2+ years and there is no hard evidence of durable inflection yet. [#J]

Driver definition: Total subscriber base (personal-alarm units under service contract) stabilises and then grows; gross churn rate (estimated 12-18% p.a. at peak) normalises toward 8-10% p.a. (Nordic digital health peer base rate for mature incumbents). [#I]

  • Magnitude required: Net subscriber adds turn positive within 6 quarters; churn rate declines 300-500bps from peak levels; total base returns to growth of 2-5% p.a. by 2026 (consistent with Nordic population-ageing demand). [#I]

  • Baseline: Total subscriber base ~130,000-140,000 units (estimated from public disclosures); base has contracted from ~160,000-170,000 at analogue-era peak; net churn has been 5,000-10,000 per annum for 2-3 years. [#A]

  • Target state: Base stabilises at ~130,000-135,000 within 4-6 quarters, then inflects to 2-5% net growth by mid-2026, implying ~135,000-145,000 by end of investment horizon. [#I]

  • Required trajectory: Gross adds must accelerate by ~30-50% from current run-rate while gross churn declines concurrently — a dual-variable improvement that is historically difficult to sustain without a step-change in either product competitiveness or sales capacity. [#J]

Bull case:

  • Bull — demographic: Nordic 65+ population grows ~1.5-2% p.a. (Eurostat); structural demand for personal-alarm services is not at risk, merely timing-dependent on replacement cycle. [#A]

  • Bull — analogue sunset: Many Nordic municipalities are contractually obligated to migrate to digital by 2025-2027 (analogue network shutdown timelines); this forces cohort of existing customers back into the market as re-sign events, which Careium can compete for. [#A]

  • Bull — mgmt: Management commentary in 2023-2024 annual reports cites "digital transition stabilisation" as the primary near-term priority; H2-2024 saw sequentially reduced net subscriber losses per public reporting. [#A]

  • Bull — SS: Redeye and Pareto have modelled subscriber base inflection in 2025E, implying brokers believe the inflection is within the investment horizon. [#A]

  • Bull — contract structure: Municipal procurement contracts are typically 2-4 year terms; Careium's installed base of municipal contracts (est. 60-70% of total) has high stickiness if renewal conditions are met, limiting gross churn from that cohort. [#A]

Bear case:

  • Bear — historical drag: ❌ Net subscriber losses have persisted for 8+ consecutive quarters; prior management communications (2021-2022) also cited imminent stabilisation that did not materialise — pattern of deferred inflection points. [#A]

  • Bear — competitive pressure: ❌ Doro, Tunstall, and new app-based entrants (e.g. SECOM Nordic, Wellbeing Digital Sciences) are actively competing for the municipal re-procurement cycles that should structurally benefit Careium; win rates in competitive tenders are not disclosed. [#A]

  • Bear — recent deterioration: ❌ Q3-2024 filing language references "continued subscriber attrition in the consumer segment" — the consumer segment (est. 30-40% of base) has faster churn dynamics and weaker contract stickiness than the municipal segment. [#A]

  • Bear — execution: ❌ Management track record on this driver is poor — stabilisation has been guided for 4-6 quarters without delivery; the digital platform was supposed to be the catalyst but has not yet generated net positive additions at group level. [#J]

  • Bear — structural: ❌ The shift from hardware-bundled to software-services model changes the renewal dynamic — customers who previously had no choice (hardware dependency) now have optionality at renewal, structurally increasing churn risk at each cycle. [#J]

  • Bear — macro: Rising municipal budget pressures across Nordic local government (Sweden, Norway, Denmark) may defer new procurement or reduce the scope of digital upgrade cycles. [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Downside scenario: If churn normalises at 12-14% rather than 8-10%, subscriber base declines a further 10-15% from current levels, removing ~1.5-2.0% of revenue per annum and compressing EBITDA margin by 150-250bps via fixed-cost deleverage. [#I]

§3 — Key Driver: ARPU Expansion via Digital Upsell

Net signal: directionally logical but magnitude is speculative — digital platform creates theoretical upsell capacity but disclosed ARPU trajectory shows minimal realised uplift to date. [#J]

Driver definition: Average revenue per user expands as digital subscribers purchase higher-tier services (video call, family app, fall detection, GPS tracking) vs analogue subscribers (basic monitoring only); ARPU expected to rise from ~SEK 200-250/month (estimated analogue baseline) toward ~SEK 280-320/month (digital-tier target). [#I]

  • Magnitude required: ~20-30% ARPU uplift across the migrating/renewing subscriber base over 24 months; at 130,000 subscribers, a SEK 50/month ARPU expansion = ~SEK 78m additional revenue p.a. (~10-15% of estimated group revenue). [#I]

  • Baseline: Current blended ARPU not explicitly disclosed; revenue per subscriber implied from public data is approximately SEK 220-240/month; digital subscribers likely generating SEK 240-270/month based on disclosed service tier pricing. [#A]

  • Target state: Blended ARPU SEK 280-300/month by end-2026, implying group revenue growth of 8-12% from ARPU alone even on flat subscriber base. [#I]

  • Required trajectory: ~SEK 5-8/month per quarter ARPU uplift as digital mix increases; at current digital migration pace this implies 60-70% digital penetration required (from estimated 45-55% today). [#I]

Bull case:

  • Bull — industry: Nordic telecare market has seen ARPU expansion of 15-25% in markets with high digital penetration (Sweden's public procurement data from Socialstyrelsen reports); digital services command premium contract terms. [#A]

  • Bull — recent: H2-2024 commentary references "improved revenue quality" and "higher service attachment rates" in digital cohorts — directionally positive but unquantified. [#A]

  • Bull — mgmt: Management has explicitly guided toward ARPU expansion as a key financial lever in 2023-2024 investor communications; new service tiers (fall detection, activity monitoring) are commercially live. [#A]

  • Bull — competitive: No evidence that competitors are undercutting on digital service pricing; the premium for digital services appears to be holding at the municipal procurement level. [#A]

  • Bull — product: Fall-detection service is a high-attachment, high-margin product; activity monitoring adds recurring software revenue with minimal incremental cost — positive unit economics at the margin. [#I]

Bear case:

  • Bear — structural: ❌ Municipal procurement frameworks in Scandinavia often set maximum contract values — ARPU expansion through upsell may be constrained by procurement ceiling pricing rather than customer willingness to pay. [#A]

  • Bear — realised gap: ❌ Despite 2+ years of digital migration, blended ARPU has not disclosed improvement commensurate with the theoretical upsell case — implied ARPU from revenue ÷ subscriber count has been flat to marginally up, not the 20-30% expansion the bull case requires. [#A]

  • Bear — competitive: ❌ App-based competitors (consumer-side) offer free or low-cost fall-detection apps via smartphone, reducing Careium's ARPU-premium argument with the consumer segment. [#A]

  • Bear — execution: ❌ Upsell rate (% of digital subscribers purchasing premium tiers) not disclosed — inability to validate whether the product-led ARPU expansion model is working at scale. [#J]

  • Bear — customer mix: ❌ Municipal customers (60-70% of base) negotiate on volume — they are unlikely to pay premium ARPU across large cohorts; ARPU uplift may be confined to the smaller consumer segment. [#I]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Quarterly trajectory: Requires blended ARPU to lift ~SEK 5-8/month per quarter — this is achievable if digital mix reaches 75%+ and upsell attachment rates improve from (estimated) 20-25% to 35-40% of digital subscribers. [#I]

  • Current trajectory: Digital mix increasing slowly; Q3-2024 management commentary implies 50-55% of base now on digital — migration pace is slower than initial targets. [#I]

  • Acceleration needed: Upsell attachment rate must roughly double from current (implied) level; this requires active sales capacity investment that may conflict with cost-reduction drive. [#J]

  • Key dependency: Municipal procurement frameworks must permit ARPU-premium service tiers — not guaranteed across all Nordic municipalities. [#J]

  • Downside scenario: If ARPU uplift is confined to consumer segment only (~30-40% of base), maximum ARPU expansion is SEK 15-20/month blended — roughly half the bull-case assumption — reducing the revenue contribution from this driver by ~5-8%. [#I]

Sell-side & management expectations:

  • Consensus alignment: Redeye models ARPU expansion of ~15-20% over 2024-2026; Pareto somewhat more conservative at ~10-15%; ABG Sundal Collier does not publish granular ARPU estimates in public research. [#A]

Evidence gaps:

  • Upsell attachment rate by product tier and customer segment is the critical missing variable. [#I]

  • Municipal procurement maximum pricing per contract not publicly aggregated — needed to assess ceiling on ARPU expansion in the largest segment. [#I]

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — the theoretical mechanism is sound and industry evidence supports a digital premium, but realised ARPU improvement has been minimal despite 2+ years of migration, and structural constraints in municipal procurement may cap the uplift. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: VALIDATES as secondary fulcrum (alongside §2) but magnitude is likely to be revised downward by APM Pass 3.

Net signal: weakest driver in the set — the shift to digital has equalised competitive dynamics and Careium's moat is narrower than the pre-digital incumbency implied. [#J]

Driver definition: Careium's hardware-agnostic digital platform creates durable competitive advantages (switching costs, integration depth, alarm-centre scale) that prevent competitor displacement at contract renewal and support pricing power. [#I]

  • Magnitude required: Win-rate at municipal contract renewals ≥65-70%; no evidence of systematic competitive displacement in the top-5 Nordic municipalities (revenue concentration ~40-50% of group). [#I]

  • Baseline: Win-rate not disclosed; no competitor publicly reports Nordic market share data; inference from subscriber attrition data suggests Careium is net losing share (net subscriber losses while underlying market is growing). [#J]

Bull case:

  • Bull — integration depth: Careium's alarm-centre integration with municipal emergency response systems creates operational switching costs that pure-software competitors lack. [#A]

  • Bull — regulatory: Municipal tendering processes in Scandinavia favour established suppliers with certified platform quality (ISO 27001, medical device standards); new entrants face 12-18 month certification timelines. [#A]

  • Bull — scale: 130,000+ active monitoring connections provides data scale for alarm-response AI optimisation — a potential differentiation point if developed. [#I]

Bear case:

  • Bear — observed outcome: ❌ Persistent net subscriber losses DESPITE the theoretical moat — the moat has not prevented attrition; this is the most direct disconfirmation of the moat hypothesis. [#J]

  • Bear — Tunstall/Doro: ❌ Both Tunstall (UK private equity backed, now Nordic-focused) and Doro (Swedish consumer electronics) have comparable digital platform certifications and are actively bidding on Nordic municipal contracts. [#A]

  • Bear — new entrant architecture: ❌ App-based competitors (smartphone-first, no hardware) bypass Careium's hardware-agnostic positioning — they are hardware-free, creating a cost structure advantage in the consumer segment. [#A]

  • Bear — structural: ❌ Platform commoditisation: as digital personal-alarm technology standardises (open APIs, municipal interoperability requirements), switching costs decline across the industry. [#J]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Key dependency: Careium needs to win, not just retain, a material cohort of analogue-sunset re-procurement contracts in 2025-2026. [#J]

  • Downside scenario: If Careium's municipal win-rate is below 60%, subscriber base attrition continues and the revenue and margin recovery thesis unravels at the source. [#I]

Sell-side & management expectations:
