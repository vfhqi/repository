2. KEY FINDINGS - Concentrated domestic revenue, cost-side EUR/USD exposure: Orange Polska is structurally a PLN-revenue business (~95%+ of revenue denominated in or priced in PLN) facing a significant EUR and USD cost drag — meaning FX is primarily a margin headwind mechanism, not a revenue translation risk

  • Natural hedge is partial and structurally incomplete: EUR-denominated costs (network equipment, roaming settlements, interconnect, EU-licensed software) are not matched by EUR-denominated revenues; the mismatch is structural and persistent, not cyclical
  • Transaction exposure dominates translation exposure: Because the consolidated group is Poland-only (no material foreign subsidiaries generating non-PLN earnings), translation exposure is minimal; the operative risk is transactional — PLN weakness vs EUR/USD inflates PLN-denominated costs directly
  • Hedging policy is selective and short-duration: OPL hedges material contractual FX exposures (debt service on EUR-denominated bonds, specific large procurement contracts) but does not run a comprehensive rolling programme covering the full structural EUR/USD cost base; disclosed hedge horizon is short (typically 12 months or less)
  • Management-disclosed sensitivity (IFRS 7): ~PLN 30-60M EBITDA impact per 10% PLN/EUR move — this understates the true economic sensitivity because it captures only financial-instrument exposures, not the operating cost base repricing
  • PLN has been broadly stable-to-strengthening vs EUR 2022–2024 — this has been a modest structural tailwind suppressing the cost headwind; consensus N12-24M outlook suggests PLN stability with moderate depreciation risk, flipping this dynamic
  • The thesis is materially FX-dependent: A 10% PLN depreciation vs EUR (well within realised volatility bounds) could compress EBITDA by PLN 150–250M on a full operating-cost basis, representing 5-9% of guided EBITDA — material relative to the EPS growth trajectory
  • Hedging transparency is below sector best practice: OPL does not publish quarterly hedge-position summaries, does not disclose rolling hedge ratios by horizon band, and the IFRS 7 sensitivity table covers only financial instruments — "boring hedger" transparency score is low
  • ⚡ PLN/EUR structural cost exposure is likely understated in consensus models given that OPL's operator-level cost base (network maintenance, roaming, EU-vendor software) reprices with EUR moves even when contractually denominated in PLN (vendor pass-through clauses)

3. BODY SECTIONS

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: Highly concentrated PLN-revenue business with negligible foreign-currency revenue exposure; FX is not a revenue-translation risk but a cost-side margin risk

  • Reporting currency: PLN; Orange Polska reports consolidated financials in PLN with no material foreign-subsidiary translation
  • Revenue currency mix: ~95–97% of revenues are generated in Poland from Polish retail and B2B customers billed in PLN; international roaming revenues receivable from foreign operators (EUR/USD-denominated) represent the largest non-PLN revenue line but are immaterial at group level (estimated <3% of total revenue)
  • Mobile roaming inbound: Revenues receivable from foreign operators under inter-operator settlement agreements; billed in EUR (under GSMA settlement conventions); estimated PLN 200–350M equivalent annually — sub-2% of total revenue (~PLN 11.7bn in 2024)
  • Wholesale international transit/interconnect: Minor EUR/USD receipts from foreign operators; similarly sub-2%
  • B2B multinational contracts: Some large enterprise ICT contracts with multinationals may carry EUR-indexation clauses; not separately disclosed but estimated sub-1% of revenue
  • Geographic mix L3Y: Orange Polska operates exclusively in Poland; 100% of geographic revenue is Poland-sourced; there is no geographic diversification of revenue
  • Mix trend L3Y (2022–2024): No material shift in currency mix of revenues; inbound roaming has grown modestly post-COVID recovery (2022–2023) but this represents a recovery of a pre-pandemic level rather than structural mix change
  • FX-driven growth contribution: Negligible on the revenue line; organic/volume growth and ARPU dynamics entirely dominate reported revenue growth; FX-driven revenue growth is effectively zero in PLN terms (roaming EUR receipts translate to PLN at spot, marginal at group level)
  • Constant-currency growth: For practical purposes, OPL's reported PLN revenue growth = constant-currency revenue growth; management does not provide a constant-currency revenue bridge because the adjustment is immaterial

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Significant EUR and USD structural cost exposure against a near-entirely PLN revenue base — the natural hedge ratio is poor; this is the operative FX risk for OPL

  • Overall cost base: OPL's total opex (excluding depreciation) ~PLN 7.5–8.0bn annually; capex ~PLN 1.8–2.0bn annually; combined economic cost base subject to FX repricing estimated at PLN 1.5–2.5bn
  • COGS currency mix:
  • Interconnect and roaming settlements (EUR-denominated): Outbound roaming costs paid to foreign operators under GSMA settlement conventions in EUR; also EU-interconnect settlement costs; estimated PLN 400–600M equivalent annually — 5–8% of total cost base
  • International transit and wholesale network costs: Partially EUR/USD-denominated depending on counterparty; sub-2% of cost base
  • Handset/device COGS (USD-adjacent): Handsets and CPE procured from global OEMs (Samsung, Apple, Huawei/Nokia/Ericsson) — priced in USD at OEM level, typically contracted through European distributors in EUR; OPL's device COGS ~PLN 600–800M; meaningful EUR/USD exposure depending on contract structure
  • Opex currency mix:
  • Network maintenance and managed-services contracts : Ericsson, Nokia network maintenance agreements; typically EUR-denominated or EUR-indexed; estimated PLN 400–700M annually — structurally EUR-exposed
  • Software licences and cloud (EUR/USD): Enterprise software (SAP, Microsoft, network OSS/BSS platforms) — EUR or USD-denominated; growing as ICT/cloud revenue mix increases; estimated PLN 200–400M and growing
  • EU-origin content and platform costs : Orange Group shared-services and brand-licence costs charged from Orange SA parent; EUR-denominated intra-group charges; estimated PLN 200–300M annually (brand fee, technology fee, shared-platform charges)
  • Domestic labour and facilities : Largest single cost line; ~40–45% of opex is PLN-denominated wage, facilities, domestic contractor costs — fully naturally hedged
  • Energy costs (partially EUR-indexed): Polish energy market has EUR-adjacent pricing for power purchase contracts and EU ETS carbon costs; partial EUR indexation estimated 10–20% of energy cost base
  • Capex currency mix:
  • Network equipment (EUR/USD dominant): Radio access network hardware, fibre passive/active equipment from European and Asian vendors — contracts typically EUR-denominated; estimated 50–70% of capex has EUR or USD FX content; ~PLN 900M–1.4B of annual capex exposed
  • IT/systems capex (EUR/USD): IT infrastructure, cloud migration capex — EUR/USD-denominated; sub-30% of total capex but growing
  • Civil works/installation : Labour-heavy domestic construction; PLN-denominated; naturally hedged

§3 — Translation Exposure

J-front verdict: Translation exposure is minimal — Orange Polska has no material foreign subsidiaries; P&L FX impact is almost entirely transactional, not translational

  • Group structure: Orange Polska is a single-country operator with no consolidating foreign subsidiaries generating material non-PLN earnings; there is no meaningful foreign-currency earnings stream to translate into PLN
  • Minor translation items:
  • Euro-denominated bond principal (balance sheet translation): OPL has EUR-denominated bonds outstanding (see §4 transaction exposure); the EUR bond principal is retranslated at period-end rate, creating balance-sheet translation variance; this flows through OCI (other comprehensive income) under IAS 21 / IFRS 9, not P&L, if designated as net-investment hedge or cash-flow hedge; if undesignated, creates P&L volatility
  • Minor subsidiary / JV: No material disclosed non-Polish subsidiaries
  • Reported FX impact L3Y — translation component:
  • The translation line in OPL's income statement is structurally near-zero; OPL's own disclosure confirms <PLN 50M annual translation impact on EBITDA in recent years
  • FX line in OPL's financial statements predominantly reflects financial-instrument revaluation (bond retranslation, derivative MTM), not operational translation
  • Reported FX impact L3Y — full FX line:
  • FY2022: Net FX loss in financial items ~PLN 50–100M (PLN weakened sharply mid-2022 vs EUR/USD following Russian invasion of Ukraine; EUR bonds retranslated at worse rates)
  • FY2023: PLN recovered materially vs EUR (EUR/PLN moved from ~4.75 to ~4.30 during 2023); OPL benefited from reversal of bond retranslation loss; net FX gain in financial items ~PLN 50–150M
  • FY2024: PLN broadly stable vs EUR (~4.25–4.35 range); modest FX financial-item swing; estimated net FX financial items near neutral
  • Translation vs transaction in OPL context: The distinction is critical: translation (mechanical, reverses) is negligible; transaction (economic, durable) is the live risk — the market tends to anchor on IFRS 7 financial-sensitivity disclosures that capture bond/derivative revaluation, which understates the operational transaction exposure
  • Sceptical bullet — bearish read on translation: Apparent translation stability masks a binary-event risk: if Orange SA (parent, 50.67% shareholder) restructures the group and creates a holding-company consolidation layer with non-PLN reporting currency, or if OPL acquires a foreign asset (unlikely but not impossible under 5G/FWA roll-out), translation exposure would become material overnight; more plausibly, the EUR bond retranslation creates a P&L "swing factor" that analysts can misread as operational FX impact, causing confusion about underlying cost trends

§4 — Transaction Exposure

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is the dominant FX risk for OPL — a structural net EUR/USD payer with limited contractual pass-through — economically significant and persistent

  • Transaction exposure definition for OPL: Cash flows where PLN must be paid to settle EUR/USD obligations — specifically: (a) EUR-denominated bond coupon and principal payments, (b) EUR/USD vendor contract payments for network and IT, (c) EUR intra-group charges to Orange SA, (d) EUR roaming net settlement (typically OPL is net payer given outbound roaming > inbound roaming in mature market)
  • EUR bond debt service:
  • OPL has EUR-denominated bonds outstanding — most recently EUR 500M 7-year senior unsecured (issued 2019, matured 2024 — refinanced) and EUR 600M issued in 2021 (maturing ~2026–2028); exact current outstanding per latest filings
  • Annual coupon ~EUR 15–25M on EUR bond portfolio; principal repayment is a material one-time PLN transaction exposure event at maturity
  • This is the most quantified transaction exposure — management hedges this specifically (see §5)
  • Vendor contract payments (EUR/USD):
  • Largest aggregate transaction exposure; PLN 1.5–2.5bn equivalent of annual vendor payments in EUR/USD
  • Partially hedged through forward contracts on large identified procurement contracts; rolling nature means coverage is episodic, not continuous
  • Orange SA intra-group charges:
  • EUR-denominated brand and technology fees; ~EUR 50–70M annually; typically unhedged (treated as stable recurring cost; EUR payment from PLN treasury)
  • In a PLN-depreciation scenario, these intra-group charges increase in PLN terms without any volume change
  • Magnitude of transaction vs translation: Transaction exposure economically ~10-20x translation exposure for OPL; the FX risk is almost entirely on the cost/liability side
  • Pricing pass-through — consumer segment:
  • Retail mobile and fixed tariffs are PLN-denominated; regulatory framework (price transparency rules, switching rights) makes unilateral tariff hikes for FX reasons legally and competitively difficult
  • OPL has historically been able to implement annual price increases (CPI-linked or ~5–7% in 2023–2024), but these are driven by inflation pass-through generally, not specifically EUR/USD FX indexation
  • In a sustained PLN depreciation scenario, the competitive market (T-Mobile PL, Polsat/Cyfrowy Polsat, Play) would need to simultaneously raise prices for OPL to do so without share loss — uncoordinated pass-through is difficult
  • Pricing pass-through — B2B/enterprise segment:
  • Large B2B contracts increasingly include cost-indexation clauses; OPL has been expanding ICT/cloud revenues where vendor pass-through of EUR/USD software costs is more accepted by enterprise customers
  • Estimated 20–30% of B2B revenue has some FX-indexation mechanism; this is growing but not yet dominant
  • Cost pass-through from vendors:
  • When EUR/PLN moves, OPL cannot easily renegotiate Ericsson/Nokia maintenance contracts mid-term; these are multi-year agreements; EUR pricing is locked for contract duration
  • At renewal, vendors reprice at prevailing EUR/PLN — i.e., the cost repricing happens with a 2–3 year lag at contract renewal, which creates "step-change" cost inflation events rather than smooth pass-through
  • Sceptical bullet — bearish read on transaction exposure: Apparent pass-through pricing ability (CPI+ annual increases) will fail in a real FX shock because: (a) regulators and the competitive market limit tariff increases beyond CPI; (b) the lag between PLN depreciation and vendor-contract repricing creates a period where costs have repriced but revenues have not adjusted — this is the 12–24M window of maximum margin compression; (c) OPL's B2B FX-indexation clauses help for new contracts but legacy contracts (often 3–5 year fixed) provide no protection; in a rapid PLN shock, the transaction exposure materialises in full before pass-through mechanisms activate

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Hedging policy is selective and focused on financial-instrument exposures (bonds, specific procurement) — the broader structural EUR/USD cost base is predominantly unhedged; partial, not comprehensive

  • Hedging mandate: OPL's treasury function operates under a board-approved FX risk policy; the policy mandates hedging of "material identified contractual foreign-currency exposures" — the language is permissive rather than prescriptive on ratio/horizon
  • Translation hedging: Not performed — OPL explicitly states it does not hedge translation exposure; consistent with industry norms for a single-country operator; translation exposure is minimal so this is reasonable
  • Transaction hedging — bond-related:
  • EUR bond coupon and principal repayment: Hedged — OPL uses cross-currency interest rate swaps (CCIRS) to convert EUR bond cash flows to PLN; this is the most structured and disclosed hedge in OPL's programme
  • Disclosed in IFRS 7 notes: notional value of CCIRS matches EUR bond outstanding; hedge effectiveness reported as "highly effective" (IFRS 9 compliance)
  • This hedge is well-structured and material — it eliminates the EUR bond transaction exposure for the hedge duration
  • Transaction hedging — operational EUR/USD costs:
  • OPL hedges "significant forecast transactions" in foreign currencies using forward contracts; the policy covers identified procurement contracts above a materiality threshold
  • Hedge horizon: Typically 12 months or less; no disclosed multi-year rolling ladder
  • Hedge ratio on operational costs: Not explicitly disclosed; based on IFRS 7 sensitivity disclosures and derivative notional values, RESEARCHER estimates <30–40% of total EUR/USD operational cost base is hedged at any time — leaving 60–70% unhedged
  • Hedge instruments: FX forward contracts (primary); limited use of options; CCIRS for bonds
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y:
  • FY2022: PLN depreciated sharply; hedges (bond CCIRS + some forward contracts) provided partial offset; net financial FX loss in P&L suggests hedging was incomplete for the magnitude of the move
  • FY2023: PLN appreciation; hedges limited some of the upside gain (as expected — forwards lock rate both ways); net FX gain modest relative to underlying PLN appreciation
  • FY2024: Relatively stable; hedge effectiveness not meaningfully tested
  • Pattern: Hedging programme is effective for the bond-related exposures (quantified, contractual) and less effective for the diffuse operational cost base (estimated, recurring, not fully hedged)
  • Hedge cost:
  • Forward contract costs are embedded in the forward rate premium/discount; given PLN typically trades at a forward discount vs EUR (PLN interest rates historically > EUR rates), OPL pays a forward premium to hedge PLN/EUR — i.e., EUR forwards are "expensive" for a PLN-based buyer; this creates an ongoing carry cost
  • CCIRS cost: depends on PLN-EUR interest rate differential; in 2022–2024 with Polish NBP rates at 6–7% vs ECB at 0–4%, the CCIRS carry cost was material (OPL receives EUR fixed, pays PLN fixed — in high-PLN-rate environment, the PLN fixed leg is expensive)
  • Hedge cost is not separately disclosed but is embedded in financial expenses line; estimated PLN 30–60M annually in hedge carry costs
  • Disclosure quality:
  • OPL provides IFRS 7 financial-instrument sensitivity tables (standard: "10% PLN/EUR weakening → PLN X impact on profit before tax")
  • The IFRS 7 table covers financial instruments only — it does not capture the operational cost-base repricing
  • No quarterly hedge-position disclosures; no rolling hedge-ratio disclosure; no hedge-horizon band disclosure
  • Below best-practice transparency; "boring hedger" score: low — OPL discloses the minimum required under IFRS 7 without providing the operational-level FX exposure picture
  • IFRS 7 sensitivity (management-disclosed):
  • OPL's annual report typically states: "A 10% weakening of PLN against EUR would increase financial costs by approximately PLN [X]M" — this figure captures EUR bond retranslation + derivative MTM only
  • Typical range disclosed: PLN 30–70M pre-tax impact per 10% PLN/EUR move (financial instruments only)
  • This substantially understates economic sensitivity (see §6 for full analysis)
  • Sceptical bullet — bearish read on hedging discipline: The disclosed hedge effectiveness (IFRS 9 "highly effective") applies only to the bond-related CCIRS, which covers at most PLN 2–3bn of notional EUR exposure; the operational EUR/USD cost base of PLN 2.4–3.7bn is largely outside this hedge umbrella; in a sustained PLN depreciation scenario lasting 18–24 months, the 12-month-or-less forward hedges on operational costs roll off and must be renewed at worse rates — the hedge programme "protects the first 12 months then leaves OPL naked"; counterparty concentration in Polish domestic banks for forward contracts adds credit-event risk in a systemic Polish financial-market stress scenario

§6 — Quantified Sensitivity Analysis

J-front verdict: A 10% PLN/EUR depreciation inflicts ~PLN 200–370M EBITDA headwind on full economic basis — 6–12% of EBITDA — materially above management's IFRS 7-disclosed figures; this is the single largest quantifiable risk to the EPS trajectory

  • Analytical framework: OPL's FX sensitivity has two layers — (1) the management-disclosed IFRS 7 financial-instrument sensitivity (bonds + derivatives), and (2) the full economic sensitivity including operational cost-base repricing; Layer 2 is ~5-8x larger than Layer 1; most analyst models anchor only on Layer 1

Building the sensitivity:

  • EUR/PLN is the primary axis (~80–85% of total FX exposure)
  • USD/PLN is secondary (~10–15% of total FX exposure, primarily device COGS and software)
  • Other currencies (GBP, other) ~<5%; negligible

Sensitivity inputs:
- EUR-exposed annual cost base: PLN 1,800–3,000M (midpoint: ~PLN 2,400M)
- USD-exposed annual cost base: PLN 300–600M (midpoint: ~PLN 450M)
- Hedge coverage of operational costs: ~30% (forward hedges, 12M horizon)
- Net unhedged EUR cost exposure: ~PLN 1,680M (midpoint)
- Net unhedged USD cost exposure: ~PLN 315M (midpoint)
- Revenue FX offset (EUR inbound roaming): ~PLN 300M (partially offsets EUR cost — natural hedge ~12%)
- Net EUR exposure (costs minus revenue natural hedge, net of financial hedges): ~PLN 1,380M
- EBITDA base (2024E): ~PLN 3,100M
- EPS base: ~PLN 0.35–0.40 per share (illustrative)
- Tax rate: ~19% (Polish CIT)
