§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The VWS-DK recovery thesis is fragile in one specific way: it requires simultaneous continuation of (1) commodity cost stability, (2) pricing discipline holding vs competitive pressure, (3) permitting reform delivering project volumes, and (4) no new fixed-price contract mispricing — the 2021-23 crisis proved all four can break simultaneously [#J]

  • Single largest risk: Contract pricing discipline — the core structural lesson of 2021-23 is that Vestas (and all wind OEMs) signed fixed-price contracts at ASPs that could not survive commodity inflation; the sector has repriced, but competitive pressure to win share could erode that discipline in the next boom cycle [#J]

  • Sub: GE Vernova and Nordex both face the same incentive to discount to grow — if any competitor blinks, rational repricing becomes unstable

  • Early warning signals: Three signals to watch actively across 2025 reporting:

  • Sub: Gross margin on newly booked orders — if ASP per MW stagnates while input costs re-inflate, margin will compress in 2026-27 before showing in reported numbers
  • Sub: Service revenue growth deceleration — service is the high-quality margin anchor; any deceleration is a mix deterioration signal

  • Sub: Order intake ASP (EUR/MW) disclosed in quarterly data — watch for downward drift as volume pressure increases

  • Breaking point — commodity re-inflation: Steel and logistics costs re-accelerating (e.g. tariff-driven in US, conflict escalation-driven in Europe) would compress margins by an estimated 100-300bps; given current EBIT margin of only ~5.4%, even a 200bps headwind returns Vestas to near-zero — the margin safety buffer is structurally thin [#J]

  • Breaking point — permitting: If German/EU permitting reform stalls, revenue recognition on the EUR 29bn backlog slips by 12-24 months; EPS trajectory 2025-2027 de-rates materially without operationally fundamental change [#A]

  • Breaking point — Chinese OEM entry: Trade policy reversal (e.g. EU-China trade deal removing anti-dumping measures) or Chinese OEMs finding European manufacturing partners to sidestep trade barriers — this is a 2-4 year risk horizon, not immediate, but structurally significant [#J]

  • Peer-relative downside: If GE Vernova executes faster on US IRA tailwind and captures disproportionate Americas share, VWS-DK's global share may stagnate despite European growth — creating a scenario where revenue growth is regionally concentrated and event-risk is higher [#A]

  • Valuation context: VWS-DK shares have recovered ~200-250% from 2023 lows; consensus FY2026 P/E approximately 20-25x on EUR 3-5 EPS estimates — not cheap for a company with a 37% guidance beat ratio over L8 periods and structurally thin margins; the 25% IRR thesis requires either significant EPS beats vs consensus or multiple expansion from a base that already prices in recovery [#J]

  • Synthesis — what makes the bear case coherent: The bear case is not "VWS-DK's fundamentals are bad" — they are clearly improving.
