§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: EBITDA AL margins stable but structurally flat; D&A step-up from fibre activation is compressing reported EBIT margins — the headline EBITDA story flatters the underlying earnings trajectory. [#J]

  • Gross margin: OPL-PL does not separately disclose gross margin in the Anglo-Saxon sense; direct costs (interconnect, content, energy) as a proportion of revenue broadly stable — estimated gross margin c.55-60% range [#A]

  • EBITDA AL margin: Q1 2025 c.25-26% — consistent with FY2024 full-year average of c.25%; no margin contraction at EBITDA AL level [#A]

  • Peer context: Western European incumbent telcos (Deutsche Telekom, Orange SA, Proximus) report EBITDA AL margins in 30-35% range — OPL-PL is below sector leaders, reflecting smaller scale and higher relative capex intensity

  • Polish telco incumbent margin at 25-26% is consistent with regional CEE/smaller-market comparators

  • Margin direction: EBITDA AL margin broadly flat for 3 years (FY2022: c.25%; FY2023: c.25%; FY2024: c.25%); no structural expansion trend evident [#A]

  • Expansion thesis depends on post-fibre-build capex reduction + operating leverage; neither yet visible in margin line

  • Decomposition: Margin stability driven by: (1) revenue growth offsetting cost inflation, (2) workforce restructuring savings, (3) energy procurement hedging — offset by (1) higher lease costs (IFRS 16), (2) rising interconnect/content costs [#A]

  • EBIT margin: EBIT margin under pressure — estimated c.8-9% in Q1 2025 vs c.10-11% two years prior; D&A step-up from fibre network activation is the primary driver [#A]

  • ❌ D&A as % of revenue rising: c.17% in 2022 → c.18-19% in 2024-25; this is the structural earnings headwind

  • Operating leverage: Operating leverage is weak — revenue +2-3% is insufficient to generate meaningful margin expansion at EBIT level when D&A is growing faster [#A]

  • D&A trend: D&A increasing as fibre network assets are activated; management guided D&A to peak in 2025-26 and then stabilise as the build completes — this is the key forward assumption for EBIT margin recovery [#J]

  • Operating expenses: SG&A broadly controlled; headcount reduction programme (c.10% workforce reduction announced 2022-24) delivering savings on schedule; energy cost headwind partially offset by hedging [#A]

  • One-off costs: Restructuring charges c.PLN 50-80m annually in recent years — material relative to reported EBIT of c.PLN 250-280m per quarter; adjusted EBITDA AL strips these, but they are recurring in nature [#A]

  • "Recurring" restructuring charges are a non-GAAP quality concern common across European incumbents (cf. BT Group, KPN, Proximus)

  • Adjusted vs reported: Gap between EBITDA AL and reported EBIT is the key quality dimension; investors using EBITDA AL as the primary metric are abstracting away D&A and lease costs that are real cash obligations [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish margin read: EBITDA AL "stability" masks a deteriorating EBIT trajectory driven by D&A; if D&A does not peak and decline as management projects (because fibre rollout extends or wholesale revenue ramp is delayed), EBIT margins could compress further through 2026-27; restructuring charges are recurring despite being "exceptional" — adjusted margins are flattering; the 25-26% EBITDA AL margin plateau is c.5-10ppt below Western European peer leaders, and there is no credible path to convergence [#J]
