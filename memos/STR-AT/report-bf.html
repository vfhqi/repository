EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

STRABAG SE operates as a vertically integrated European construction technology leader, achieving record

self-supply, and a structural shift toward public infrastructure and high-tech industrial facilities . This margin performance positions STRABAG significantly above European peer medians and exceeds its own

horizon . The group maintains an unlevered balance sheet with €3.52 billion net cash and an investment-grade BBB+ S&P rating , providing exceptional bidding capacity for complex public-private partnerships .

Business Model Resilience and Revenue Mix STRABAG's diversified construction footprint spans the entire building lifecycle from materials production through planning, construction, and building operations across more than 50 countries . The company achieved historic output volume of €20.42 billion in 2025, representing solid 6.00% year-on-year growth and keeping Strategy 2030 volume expansion goals firmly on track . Public sector contracts dominate the revenue mix at 69% of total output volume , providing powerful cyclical protection against residential construction downturns that have severely impacted European housing-exposed competitors . This public concentration has expanded consistently from 63% in 2021, reflecting deliberate strategic repositioning toward infrastructure-heavy project portfolios . Conversely, residential building exposure is successfully contained at under 10% of group output , insulating STRABAG from high interest rate-driven private market collapses . Strategic high-tech expansion is accelerating, with next-generation semiconductor fabs and data centers representing 6% of order backlog equivalent to €2.0 billion , supported by proven delivery of over 15 major European chip fabs .

Vertical Integration and Value Chain Dominance STRABAG operates one of Europe's densest construction materials networks with 556 proprietary production facilities , producing a substantial 86% of asphalt needs in-house , creating an effective pricing and supply security buffer against external inflation . The group controls 146 proprietary quarries and gravel pits , protected by astronomical environmental permitting barriers and restricted 30-40km economic transport radius that creates highly defensive local oligopolies . This upstream heavy materials positioning captures premium EBIT margins of 15-20% versus downstream general contracting's structurally thin 2.5-5.0% margins, allowing STRABAG to retain high-margin value pools internally . Despite operating in commoditized contracting segments, STRABAG achieved an outstanding 6.7% EBIT margin significantly outperforming typical sector operating margins of 3.5% . The integrated value chain extends from upstream aggregates through project development, general contracting, facility management, and closed-loop recycling, with STRABAG maintaining presence across all five value-chain stages .
