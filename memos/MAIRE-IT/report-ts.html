Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The group's innovative licensing-centric division NEXTCHEM de-risks E&C volatility by delivering high-margin technology packages with robust competitive moats.

Segment Dynamics and Revenue Mix

1.
Balanced segment division: MAIRE operates through two main reporting divisions comprising Sustainable Technology Solutions and Integrated E&C Solutions, a structure that effectively hedges high-margin technology licensing revenues against the high-volume but volatile execution of physical engineering construction projects
2.
High margin STS: The Sustainable Technology Solutions segment is headed by NEXTCHEM and delivers technology licensing, proprietary equipment, and catalysts with significantly high margins and low volume
3.
High volume E&C: The Integrated E&C Solutions segment is headed by Tecnimont and executes large-scale downstream energy projects, generating high volumes of revenue at lower margins
4.
Strategic acquisition boost: Integrating the newly acquired Ballestra Group adds over 30 proprietary technologies, widening inorganic chemistry, sulfuric acid, and fertilizer capabilities from Q2 2026 onwards
5.
Deep technology footprint: Stamicarbon, a core NEXTCHEM subsidiary, possesses dominant global market share in urea fertilizer licensing, reinforcing MAIRE's high-end engineering credentials
6.
Expanding adjacent markets: Leveraging its Stami Urea technology, NEXTCHEM is aggressively entering green ammonia and circular carbon transition markets globally

Moats and Customer Concentration

7.
Extreme customer concentration: Backlog remains concentrated with 86% of H1 2024 backlog stemming from the ten largest contracts, raising individual project cancellation risks
8.
High regional exposure: Middle East contracts represent roughly 38% of the group backlog as of Q1 2026, highlighting geographic reliance on OPEC national oil companies
9.
Durable competitive moats: High customer switching costs arise because clients cannot construct licensed plants without NEXTCHEM's proprietary know-how and basic engineering design packages
10.
Favorable value chain: Operating as an asset-light licensor allows MAIRE to capture high-margin economics early in the CAPEX cycle, avoiding downstream asset ownership drag
11.
Differentiated scale advantage: Global execution capabilities let Tecnimont handle multi-billion dollar lump-sum turnkey petrochemical complexes that few European peers can execute
12.
Crowded peer landscape: Industry structure remains competitive, though Technip Energies maintains a stronger LNG focus compared to MAIRE's dominant downstream chemicals focus
13.
Other side of the story: Large-scale lump-sum turnkey contracts expose MAIRE to severe margin slippage if cost inflation or execution delays occur on multi-billion projects

Section summary

Multi-year Financial Trajectory

1.
Consistent revenue acceleration: Group revenue expanded by 38.5% in FY24 to €5.90 billion and 20.3% in FY25 to €7.10 billion, highlighting structural demand
2.
Improving margin profile: EBITDA margins expanded from 6.4% in FY23 to 7.0% in FY25, driven by growing high-margin technology licensing within the product mix
3.
Exceptional return on equity: MAIRE boasts an overwhelmingly high three-year average ROE of 31% compared to peers, stemming from its de-risked licensing focus
4.
Asset-light capital efficiency: Operating with an asset-light model limits tangible asset requirements, driving outstanding return metrics with minimal capital employed

Guidance Credibility and Recent Beats

5.
Highly conservative guidance: Management consistently sets realistic targets, raising its FY24 guidance mid-year and subsequently beating it at year-end results
6.
Highly visible backlog: Future revenues are secure with the order backlog growing 23.6% in Q1 2026 to €15.70 billion, covering over two years of production
7.
Capital expansion financing: Refinancing activities remain robust with the issuance of a €185 million medium-term loan in April 2026 to support R&D
8.
Reliable historical forecasting: Management's conservative approach was demonstrated in FY25 when they exceeded upgraded revenue and EBITDA targets despite regional supply frictions
9.
Other side of the story: Negative working capital dynamics could reverse if clients delay payments on massive projects, creating sudden cash drain

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens dramatically as high-margin green transition licensing transforms MAIRE into a highly profitable, capital-light technology leader.

Critical Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
NEXTCHEM profitability expansion: The first primary driver is the growth of high-margin licensing and catalyst sales which carry margins of 24% to 25%
2.
High execution plausibility: Plausibility for STS margin expansion is high because NEXTCHEM's technology portfolio has grown to over 80 commercial solutions including Ballestra
3.
Mega project execution: The second driver is the ramp-up of the $8.70 billion Hail & Ghasha project, which reached 70% progress in Q1 2026
4.
Medium execution risk: While Hail & Ghasha execution is on track, the driver would fail if severe supply chain disruptions occur in the Persian Gulf
5.
Accretive Ballestra integration: The third driver is the integration of Ballestra Group from Q2 2026, adding €235m in high-margin adjacent revenue
6.
Sizable cross-selling opportunities: Ballestra cross-selling plausibility is high as its inorganic chemistry technologies fully cover the fertilizer value chain, enabling turnkey integrations
7.
Geographical market expansion: The fourth driver is entering the massive United States LNG and refinery market via new strategic partner agreements
8.
Compound bull case: Achieving the ultimate bull case of a 25%+ IRR requires both the E&C execution and STS licensing engines to fire simultaneously
9.
Other side of the story: If national oil companies freeze final investment decisions due to lower energy prices, the bidding pipeline collapses

Section 6 – Valuation 5 findings

Section summary

1.
Undemanding absolute multiples: Shares currently trade on an estimated 17x PE on FY28 earnings, representing only peer average levels
2.
Sizable peer discount: MAIRE trades at a c.15% discount to closest peer Technip Energies on both forward EV/EBITDA and EV/EBIT measures
3.
Mispriced growth prospects: Current market pricing appears to underestimate NEXTCHEM's high-margin transition potential, valuing MAIRE as a low-margin contractor
4.
Other side of the story: Should backlog growth decelerate below a 1.0x book-to-bill ratio, multiples will compress toward historical E&C averages

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by Middle East geopolitical exposure, though aggressive supply corridor de-risking and contractual protections substantially mitigate margin downside.

Backlog Concentration and Geopolitics

1.
High regional concentration: Geopolitical risk remains prominent with roughly 38% of the group's total backlog concentrated in the Middle East
2.
Direct transmission mechanism: Regional escalation would directly impact EPS by causing project suspensions or severe logistic delays at Hail & Ghasha
3.
Critical project concentration: Backlog concentration is high as the Hail & Ghasha project accounts for nearly half of overall backlog
4.
Inflationary margin pressure: Raw material inflation represents a risk, though price adjustment clauses typically pass cost increases directly to clients
5.
Potentially false signal: Sizable order intakes could act as a false friend if low-margin, high-risk contracts are secured to win volume
6.
Primary warning signal: Investors must closely monitor the quarterly book-to-bill ratio of NEXTCHEM to ensure high-margin technology adoption is continuing
7.
Other side of the story: Geopolitical risks are overstated because 98% of procurement for Middle East projects is already completed
