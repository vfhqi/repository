§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Clean on conventional red flags — no dual-class structure, no related-party concerns, no restatements, no enforcement actions; structural governance weakness is the majority-public-sector-ownership dynamic, which is disclosed and priced, not concealed.

Dual-class structure:
- No dual-class share structure identified; single class of registered shares (Namenaktien) in the Swiss tradition
- The majority shareholders exercise control through economic ownership, not through special voting rights — a cleaner governance design than dual-class structures

Founder control:
- No individual founder-control dynamic; the controlling shareholders are institutional public bodies (Badenova AG, Vorarlberger Illwerke AG, potentially others)
- Control is exercised through the supervisory board representation, not through special veto rights; this is governance-standard for Swiss municipal utility holdings

Voting rights:
- One-share-one-vote appears to be the structure; no poison pill or golden share identified
- ⚠️ Share transfer restrictions: Swiss Namenaktien often carry board-approval requirements for share transfers; this is standard but does reduce liquidity and could inhibit a takeover that might benefit minority shareholders

Related-party transactions:
- Transactions with Badenova AG (energy supply, grid, distribution) and Vorarlberger Illwerke (operational coordination, hydro dispatch) exist as part of normal operational arrangements
- These are disclosed in annual reports under related-party transactions; the terms appear commercially reasonable (market-rate energy supply contracts, management service agreements)
- No obviously enriching related-party transactions identified; no director receiving personal benefit from related-party arrangements

Audit history:
- Statutory auditor: Deloitte AG (Zurich/Basel); multi-year relationship; no auditor change in the publicly available period
- No audit qualification identified; clean audit opinions on available annual reports
- Audit committee appears functional; committee reports included in annual reports meet minimum Swiss disclosure standards

Material weakness:
- No material weakness disclosure identified in available annual reports
- No restatement of financial results identified

Restatement history:
- No restatements identified; this is a clean signal

Legal/regulatory issues:

- Regulatory exposures: Swiss electricity market regulation (ElCom), German BNetzA (for wind/grid), Austrian E-Control; these are sectoral regulators, not enforcement-adverse actors

Whistleblower / governance complaints:
- No public whistleblower disclosures identified; no employee complaint campaigns visible on public media

Anti-takeover provisions:

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on governance is that the "clean" record may reflect the absence of scrutiny rather than genuine governance quality. naturenergie's limited public profile, non-SPI-index status, limited analyst coverage, and German-language primary disclosures mean that governance issues could persist without being caught by the market.
