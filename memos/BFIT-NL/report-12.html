Customer and Member Dynamics

Membership Growth and Scale

In 2025, Basic-Fit demonstrated robust customer acquisition, ending the year with 4.82 million memberships
across its owned clubs. [1] This performance represented a 13% year-over-year increase, adding 564,000
members compared to 2024. [2] When factoring in the newly acquired Clever Fit franchise network, the company's
total reach expanded to 5.8 million memberships across Europe. [3] At the unit level, the average number of
memberships per owned club grew from 2,701 in 2024 to 2,902 in 2025. [4] For its mature estate, the company
continues to target an average of 3,250 memberships per club over the medium term. [5] To accelerate initial
membership density, Basic-Fit successfully utilizes a "Founding Member" campaign that grants lifetime
discounted rates to individuals who join during a new club's opening week. [6]

Evolving Pricing Strategy and Premiumization

Basic-Fit operates a transparent, tiered membership model that has been actively optimized to balance volume
with higher unit economics. [7] In most core markets, the standard offering consists of a Comfort membership at
€24.99 per four weeks, a Premium tier at €29.99, and an Ultimate tier at €34.99. [8] Following the implementation
of this updated pricing structure, approximately half of all new joiners have opted for the higher Premium and
Ultimate tiers. [9] The Ultimate membership in particular has seen exceptional traction, with over 40% of new
joiners selecting this top-tier option. [10]

Supplier and Partner Relationships

Equipment Manufacturers and Sourcing Leverage

Basic-Fit's ability to maintain its low-cost, high-value proposition relies heavily on its supply chain and partner
ecosystem. [30] The company primarily partners with Johnson Health Tech to supply its clubs with Matrix ﬁtness
equipment. [33] Because Matrix equipment is manufactured in China, it represents a highly cost-effective
alternative compared to the premium, European-made equipment utilized by competitors like McFIT. [34] Basic-Fit
leverages its position as the largest buyer of ﬁtness equipment in Europe to secure highly favorable purchasing
terms. [35] These volume-based agreements provide the company with extended warranties, protective pricing,
