EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Metsä Board has executed a complete structural renewal of executive leadership and launched its 2026-2030 Lead the Pack strategy, pivoting from heavy capital expenditure to disciplined cash-driven steering while permanently closing the Tako mill and suspending dividends to preserve capital. The investment thesis rests on a narrow margin of safety against a backdrop of severe cyclical headwinds, structural Nordic wood cost inflation, and European folding boxboard overcapacity. Comparable operating performance collapsed to a historic trough of EUR -80.2 million in FY2025, though Q1 2026 showed sequential recovery with a comparable operating loss narrowing to EUR -10.8 million. The company faces high execution risk as it attempts to deliver a EUR 200 million transformation savings programme while navigating US tariff headwinds, depressed European consumer demand, and intense peer competition from Stora Enso's massive Oulu capacity expansion.

Activist Presence

Metsäliitto Cooperative controls 52% of shares, resulting in no active activist presence in AS library for Metsä Board. The concentrated ownership structure with majority control by the cooperative parent eliminates the probability of external activist campaigns targeting capital allocation or strategic direction. This governance structure provides strategic stability but removes a potential catalyst for accelerated restructuring or asset monetization that could unlock shareholder value in a distressed cycle. The absence of activist pressure may allow management to pursue a longer-term turnaround horizon without near-term earnings pressure, though it also reduces external accountability mechanisms during the transformation programme execution phase.
