Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Alleima offers highly resilient margins through unique metallurgy, but near-term cyclical headwinds in its flagship Tube division obscure the long-term earnings trajectory.
1.
Specialized innovator: Advanced materials innovator manufacturing high value-added seamless tubes, electric heating systems, and precision strip steel for demanding industrial applications
2.
Investment thesis: Strategic transformation is driven by high-margin reopening of nuclear tube capacity and expansion of medical wire globally, shifting the mix away from low-margin industrial steel
3.
Flagship swing: Demand fluctuations and capital expenditure levels in oil and gas represent 24% of group sales and remain key swing factors
4.
Historical record: Revenues peaked close to SEK 20.7 billion in FY 2023 but destocking pulled FY 2025 sales down to SEK 18,630 million
5.
Recent compression: Operating profit declined 28% to SEK 386 million in Q1 2026, hit by significant currency translation headwinds of SEK 97 million
6.
Primary risk: Prolonged industrial stagnation in Europe that could restrict capital expenditure budgets of chemical and petrochemical customers
7.
Valuation profile: Trades at 10.8x EV/adj. EBIT for 2025e, reflecting a cyclical low before late-2026 capacity starts delivering
8.
First-look verdict: Deeper work is highly warranted because underlying structural tailwinds provide a clear multi-year thesis despite macro headwinds

Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Alleima dominates premium material niches using highly integrated metallurgical capabilities that create substantial barriers to entry, though divisional performance remains highly uneven.

Divisional Structure and Value Chain

1.
Tube operations: Core division Tube represents ~70% of sales, fabricating advanced seamless tubes and long products for energy and chemicals
2.
Kanthal operations: Niche division Kanthal accounts for ~21% of revenues, delivering electric industrial heating elements and ultra-fine medical wire
3.
Strip operations: Precision Strip division generates ~9% of revenues, producing high-value compressor valve steel, shaving razor steel, and bone saw steel
4.
Revenue streams: Mostly transactional product sales where control transfers to customers at point-of-delivery, governed by long-term framework agreements
5.
Integrated chain: Upstream operations utilize two owned steel mills and five extrusion presses, ensuring complete control over material purity and quality
6.
Value chain positioning: Deeply integrated metallurgy provides a profit-favoured position, enabling unmatched pricing power relative to non-integrated competitors

Moats, Customers, and Industry Structure

7.
Moat mechanism: Extremely high customer switching costs because critical tube or wire failure leads to expensive, hazardous industrial shutdowns
8.
Materials IP: Proprietary portfolio containing over 900 active alloy recipes developed through continuous investment across 5 global R&D centers
9.
Customer mix: Extremely diversified exposure across ten customer segments, with oil/gas representing 24% and industrial markets making up 17%
10.
Low concentration: Customer list spans 80 global markets, ensuring that the five largest geographic markets represent just over 50% of revenue
11.
Industry consolidation: Consolidated global specialty alloys market where Alleima holds leading positions in steam generator and umbilical tubing
12.
Competitor landscape: Primary peers in advanced steel and heating elements include ATI, Carpenter Technology, Haynes, and specialized medical players like Fort Wayne Metals
13.
Other side of the story: Flagship Tube division remains heavily exposed to structural capital expenditure declines in oil and gas, which could severely depress mill volumes

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens based on key structural tailwinds in nuclear and heating, though execution risks and delayed capacity ramp-ups represent intermediate bottlenecks.

Structural Growth Drivers and Plausibility

1.
Nuclear capacity driver: Reopening of the second nuclear steam generator tube facility in Sandviken adds 60% capacity to nuclear revenues
2.
Nuclear plausibility: High probability driver because global nuclear activity is rising faster than anticipated, with long backlogs already contracted
3.
Furnace electrification: Kanthal division captures the rapid transition from gas to electric heating element modules within steel plants
4.
Medical wire growth: Global expansion of the high-margin medical wire business is supported by a new greenfield footprint in Penang, Malaysia

Secondary Options and Compound Feasibility

5.
Medical plausibility: High probability driver underpinned by aging global demographics and continuous glucose monitoring expansion
6.
Medical failure mode: Certification delays or local regulatory hurdles in Malaysia could delay high-margin medical volume contributions
7.
Secondary drivers: Aerospace titanium tubing and hydrogen fuel cell coated strip provide strong long-term optionality
8.
Compound probability: Bull case does not require simultaneous success of all drivers, as each operates in separate independent niches
9.
Other side of the story: Aggressive capital expenditure budgets could depress capital returns if commercial ramp-ups are deferred

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderate but highly visible, primarily driven by cyclical raw materials exposure and European economic weakness.

Key Risk Transmission Pathways

1.
Recession risk: Direct transmission of industrial slowdowns to capital budgets of chemical and petrochemical customers, depressing orders
2.
Commodity exposure: Fluctuations in nickel and oil prices affect surcharges and cause volatile inventory revaluations
3.
Currency risk: Most sales are in foreign currencies while production costs are SEK-denominated, creating significant transaction volatility
4.
Short interest: Research indicates no published short-seller campaigns or elevated short interest currently active
5.
Energy supply: Intense energy use makes furnaces highly sensitive to electricity price surges and supply disturbances
6.
Environmental risks: Pollution risks at Sandviken and Hallstahammar sites expose the company to potential cleanup liabilities and licensing costs

Performance Indicators and Mitigations

7.
False friend: Improving book-to-bill in Strip can mask continued structural margin decay in the flagship Tube division
8.
Early warning: Purchasing Managers' Indexes and large orders above SEK 300 million represent the best indicators
9.
Other side of the story: Pricing power and comprehensive energy surcharges largely insulate the company's gross profit from inflationary shocks
