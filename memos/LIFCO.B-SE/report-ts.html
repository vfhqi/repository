Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Lifco represents a premium compounding vehicle whose recent de-rating provides an attractive entry point to capture a highly predictable EPS trajectory and outstanding capital efficiency
1.
Highly decentralized model: Lifco generates earnings by acquiring small-to-medium-sized niche businesses and letting them operate independently with zero synergy extraction, maintaining their local brands and entrepreneurial culture
2.
Compelling investment case: The opportunity lies in stable earnings compounding, where Lifco reinvests robust free cash flow into acquisitions at disciplined multiples of approximately 6.5x EV/EBITA, generating an EBITDA CAGR of 18% and EPS CAGR of 16% over 2015-2025
3.
Volatile swing factor: The principal swing factor for near-term earnings is the pacing of acquisitions combined with a cyclical recovery in European industrial demand, which represents over half of group EBITA
4.
Exceptional historical compounding: Over the past decade, Lifco has achieved a 19% EBITA CAGR from 2015 to 2025, driven by 6% organic growth and a stable 12% annual contribution from acquisitions, while remaining virtually unleveraged
5.
Severe sentiment overhang: The primary near-term risk is the sentiment overhang from a short-seller report by The Analyst in January 2026, which alleged structural organic growth deterioration and triggered a 16% share price decline
6.
Compelling first-look verdict: A closer look is highly warranted, as the short-seller de-rating has priced the stock at a 20% discount to its 5-year average EV/EBITA multiple, creating an attractive entry point for an asset with a proven capital allocation model

Section 3 – What the company does 18 findings

Section summary
  1. Lifco manages a highly diversified portfolio of market-leading niche companies characterized by high EBITA margins and excellent pricing power
  2. The group's operating model relies on exceptionally decentralized autonomy that preserves the local entrepreneurial culture of acquired subsidiaries
  3. The European M&A space provides a vast and fragmented consolidation runway for Lifco to compound capital at high rates of return

Segment Breakdown and Offerings

1.
Highly profitable segments: Lifco is organized into three historic segments: Dental, Demolition & Tools, and Systems Solutions, each focusing on specialized B2B niches
2.
Stable dental consumables: The Dental business area (SEK 6,331 million net sales in 2025) provides consumables, medical equipment, and software, operating as a stable, non-cyclical cash-cow
3.
Global demolition leadership: Demolition & Tools (SEK 6,760 million net sales in 2025) sells remote-controlled demolition robots and crane attachments, holding the global number one market position under the Brokk and Kinshofer brands
4.
Scalable industrial holdings: Systems Solutions (SEK 15,160 million net sales in 2025) operates a wide array of niche industrial businesses spanning contract manufacturing, environmental technology, and vehicle transport interiors
5.
Upcoming structural reporting: Reflecting strong structural growth, Lifco is reorganizing Systems Solutions in Q2 2026 to elevate Environmental Technology and Transportation Products into standalone reporting segments
6.
Highly specialized subsidiaries: Lifco's subsidiaries operate across specialized niches including dental materials, demolition tools, contract manufacturing, environmental technology, and vehicle interiors

Revenue Model and Value Chain

7.
B2B transaction focus: Revenue is earned entirely through B2B transactions, combining contractual distribution in Dental with transactional machinery sales in Demolition & Tools
8.
Superior value chain: Lifco sits in a highly profitable position in the value chain by focusing on highly specialized, proprietary products while outsourcing basic component manufacturing to contract partners, keeping capital intensity very low
9.
Fragmented customer base: The customer base is exceptionally fragmented across thousands of industrial and dental clinics, with no single customer accounting for more than 10% of consolidated net sales

Competitive Advantages and Industry Structure

10.
Durable decentralized moat: Lifco's durable competitive advantage is its decentralized model, offering a "safe haven" where founders sell their businesses but remain to run them with total operational autonomy
11.
High customer switching: High customer switching costs are driven by the specialized nature of its products, such as proprietary dental software and highly integrated crane attachments
12.
Vast market fragmentation: The European M&A market remains highly fragmented, particularly in medtech and niche industrial niches, giving Lifco a vast and consolidating runway for acquisitions
13.
Structurally superior margins: Lifco stands out against peers like Indutrade and general Swedish capital goods by maintaining structurally superior EBITA margins of over 22% compared to the sector average
14.
High peer premium: International peer Halma PLC, which also operates a decentralized roll-up model, trades at a premium EV/EBITA of over 21.50 times, showcasing the premium multiples achieved by successful compounders
15.
Other side of the story: Increased competition from Swedish serial acquirers has turned the sector into an asset class, which could drive up acquisition multiples and degrade returns on capital if pricing discipline slips

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Lifco boasts a stellar multi-year financial track record of consistent margin expansion and superior capital returns versus its peers
  2. Excellent cash generation and minimal maintenance capex enable the group to fund continuous self-generated compounding with zero equity dilution
  3. The balance sheet remains highly conservative and unleveraged, providing immense financial flexibility to execute on the forward acquisition pipeline

Long-Term Growth and Profitability

1.
Steady margin expansion: Lifco has structurally improved its EBITA margin over the long term, rising from approximately 14% at its 2014 IPO to 22.4% in FY25, which restates the long-term margin improvement trend of the group
2.
Outstanding EPS growth: Adjusted EPS has compounded at a 16% CAGR from 2015 to 2025, rising from SEK 3.67 in 2020 to SEK 7.27 in 2024 and further expanding to SEK 8.00 in 2025
3.
Balanced growth composition: Acquisitions consolidated in FY2025 added SEK 899 million to consolidated net sales and SEK 268 million to EBITA, which would have risen by a further SEK 1,155 million and SEK 329 million, respectively, if consolidated from January 1, 2025
4.
Peer returns outperformance: Lifco consistently delivers superior capital returns, achieving a Return on Equity of 19.8% and an average Adjusted Return on Equity of 27% over 2015-2025

Capital Efficiency and Cash Conversion

5.
Staggering return on capital: Excluding goodwill, Lifco's Return on Capital Employed reached a staggering 132% in FY2025, showcasing the extreme asset-light nature of its operating business model
6.
Stellar cash conversion: Cash generation is highly efficient, with cash flow from operating activities growing 10.7% to SEK 5,124 million in 2025, while free cash flow per share grew from SEK 5.50 in 2020 to SEK 9.80 in 2025
7.
Asset-light operational profile: The business model requires extremely low maintenance capital expenditure, as subsidiaries mostly assemble purchased components

Balance Sheet and Recent Results

8.
Extremely conservative leverage: Despite completing 16 acquisitions for a total cash spent of SEK 3.36 billion in 2025, interest-bearing net debt to EBITDA remained at a very comfortable 1.1 times, well below the 3.0 limit
9.
Decent first quarter: In Q1 2026, Lifco delivered net sales of SEK 7,186 million and EBITA of SEK 1,588 million, representing a 6.2% EBITA increase that was aided by the consolidation of Karl Kaps and Ethoss Regeneration adding SEK 180 million
10.
Habitual target outperformance: Management consistently under-promises and over-delivers, setting a long-term target of Net Debt to EBITDA of 2.0 to 3.0 times, yet habitually operating around 1.1 times
11.
Other side of the story: Near-term organic growth has slowed, with organic EBITA growth collapsing to 0.1% in FY2025 due to a weak construction sector hurting demolition robot sales

Section 5 – The possible investment case 13 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case for Lifco is compelling and strengthens following the recent short-seller de-rating of the stock
  2. Disciplined acquisition execution and margin-accretive targets drive a highly predictable forward earnings trajectory
  3. The upcoming reporting separation of key high-performing divisions acts as a major catalyst for structural re-rating

Core Value Creation Drivers

1.
Ample long-term runway: The primary driver of the forward earnings trajectory is the acquisition of high-margin niche businesses, which Lifco expects to continue at a pace of 10 to 20 additions annually
2.
Proven M&A pacing: We assess the plausibility of this driver as high, because Lifco's M&A capacity has scaled 10% annually over the last seven years, and the European small-cap market is heavily fragmented
3.
Inherent acquisition multiple: This driver could fail if Lifco loses pricing discipline, paying inflated multiples above its historic ~6.5x EV/EBITA average, or if target integration fails
4.
Structural margin expansion: The second driver is the structural expansion of the EBITA margin toward 23% to 24%, driven by the acquisition of targets with higher margins than the group average
5.
Highly supportive average: We assess the plausibility of the margin driver as medium, because the 17 acquisitions consolidated in FY2025 boasted a superior average EBITA margin of 29%
6.
Adverse product mix: This driver could fail if adverse product mix shifts, such as lower sales of highly profitable demolition robots, offset the benefits of high-margin acquisitions

Plausibility and Structural Catalysts

7.
Valuable upcoming spin-out: The third driver is the Q2 2026 reorganization, which will expose the high-performing and highly specialized Environmental Technology and Transportation Products business areas
8.
Solid separation plausibility: We assess the plausibility of the Q2 2026 reorganization driving a re-rating as high, because both divisions generate close to SEK 1 billion in EBITA with margins well above 20%
9.
Non-correlated compounding math: For the bull case to work, these drivers do not need to fire simultaneously, as the decentralized business model relies on independent, non-correlated business lines
10.
Other side of the story: The bull case could be derailed if macroeconomic demand in European industrial production remains depressed, preventing any organic volume recovery and leaving earnings entirely reliant on acquisitions

Section 6 – Valuation 7 findings

Section summary
  1. Lifco's shares look unusually cheap on a relative basis following the short-seller de-rating, trading at a significant discount to peers despite superior historical growth
1.
Depressed current valuation: Lifco trades at an EV/EBITA of 20-21x, representing a 20% de-rating below its 5-year average EV/EBITA and a 21% discount to historical peers Addtech and Lagercrantz
2.
Rare valuation discount: Historically, Lifco has traded on par with Addtech and Lagercrantz, with its current valuation offering a significant 21% discount that represents a rare entry point into a high-performing compounder
3.
Significant multiple contraction: Based on RedEye's early 2026 data, Lifco's P/E multiple has contracted to approximately 30.9x from its historical post-pandemic peak of over 45.0x
4.
Massive price target: The sell-side median price target of SEK 385 implies an attractive 26.5% upside to the current share price level of SEK 304.40
5.
Clear pricing mismatch: The market currently appears to be pricing in prolonged organic growth deterioration and structural margin decline, ignoring the highly resilient cash flows of the group
6.
Other side of the story: If interest rates remain elevated or rise further, the entire Swedish compounder sector could face a structural de-rating, depressing Lifco's multiple regardless of its operational performance

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is moderate but manageable, with short-seller sentiment and cyclical industrial exposure representing the main headwinds to near-term earnings growth

Operational and Sentiment Risks

1.
Severe short-seller report: A prominent short-seller report by the British firm The Analyst in January 2026 caused a 16% share price collapse and a severe de-rating of Lifco
2.
Valuation macro sensitivity: Higher base rates directly pressure Lifco's valuation multiple and increase the cost of debt financing for future M&A transactions
3.
High cyclical exposure: Over half of Lifco's earnings come from cyclical industrial production niches, making it highly sensitive to macroeconomic downturns in Europe
4.
Negligible key-person risk: The business depends on CEO Per Waldemarson and key capital allocators, though decentralization significantly minimizes overall key-person risk

Early Warnings and Mitigations

5.
Temporary organic growth: High organic revenue growth in contract manufacturing represents a false friend, as it was driven by lumpy deliveries in late 2025 and is highly likely to face tough comparisons in 2026
6.
Useful early-warning metrics: Investors should closely monitor organic order intake and book-to-bill ratios across the Systems Solutions and Demolition segments as an early-warning signal
7.
Other side of the story: The risks are likely overstated because Lifco's interest-bearing net debt to EBITDA is an incredibly low 1.1 times, leaving massive liquidity to fund earnings-accretive M&A through any cyclical downturn

Section 8 – Management: what is changing 5 findings

Section summary
  1. Leadership is a source of highly positive alignment and governance evolution, marked by a new institutional director and a major synthetic option program
1.
Stable executive leadership: CEO Per Waldemarson (appointed 2019) and CFO Therese Hoffman (appointed 2011) maintain exceptional stability and are deeply aligned with the decentralized culture
2.
Highly attractive option: In May 2025, Carl Bennet AB issued 428,115 synthetic call options to 23 directors and executives with a strike price of SEK 504.12, representing a strong incentive to exceed current share price levels
3.
Stronger institutional governance: At the April 2026 AGM, Anders Oscarsson, former Head of Equities at AMF, was newly elected as a director, replacing long-standing member Axel Wachtmeister
4.
Minor regulatory deviation: Lifco deviates from the Swedish Corporate Governance Code as Chairman Carl Bennet also chairs the Nomination Committee, a setup justified by his dominant ownership stake

Section summary
  1. Deeper work is highly warranted because the short-seller de-rating has created a significant valuation disconnect for one of Europe's highest-quality compounding assets
1.
Unmatched capital efficiency: The most compelling argument for Lifco is its unmatched capital efficiency, boasting a 132% Return on Capital Employed ex-goodwill that enables continuous self-funded compounding of EPS
2.
Other side of the story: The strongest counter-view is that the short-seller allegations might indicate structural organic growth limits, making the group entirely dependent on finding larger volumes of M&A targets to sustain its compounding rate
3.
Immediate structural due: Investors should immediately commission deep-dive work on the organic growth trajectory of the newly separated business areas, Environmental Technology and Transportation Products, in Q2 2026
4.
Urgent quantitative capital: Research must verify the exact cash conversion rate and purchase multiples across the 17 acquisitions consolidated in FY2025 to confirm capital allocation discipline
