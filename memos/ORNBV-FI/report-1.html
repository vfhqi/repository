Revenue source: Three revenue pillars — (1) Proprietary Products including Nubeqa royalties, Easyhaler, Simdax; (2) Branded Generics (Nordics-focused); (3) Animal Health [#I]
- Sub: Nubeqa (darolutamide) royalties estimated at ~€120-150m pa (2023-24) — approximately 10-12% of total revenue but ~35-40% of EBIT given near-100% incremental margin [#A]
- Sub: Easyhaler DPI platform revenue approximately €250-300m pa (2023-24) — organic growth ~5-8% pa as market converts from MDI to DPI inhaled therapies [#I]

Revenue mix (geography): Finland + Nordics approximately 38-42% of total; rest of Europe ~45-50%; rest of world ~10-15% (primarily animal health and API sales) [#A]
- Sub: Nordic concentration creates home-market defensibility (reference pricing, formulary inclusion, long-standing HCP relationships) but limits upside scaling [#A]
- Sub: Germany is a key Easyhaler market; IQVIA data suggests Orion's DPI share in Germany has grown from ~12% to ~18% over 2019-24 [#I]

Cyclicality: Low cyclicality — prescription pharma + animal health are among the most defensive revenue categories; COVID impact in 2020 was mild (-1% revenue) [#A]
- Sub: Easyhaler is a maintenance therapy (COPD, asthma); once a patient is device-trained, switching costs are high — near-zero revenue cyclicality for existing patient cohorts [#A]

- Sub: No meaningful NPS data publicly available; proxy: market share stability in Nordic DPI markets (Orion has held ~30-40% Nordic DPI share for 5+ years per IMS/IQVIA) [#I]

§3 — Industry Context (Peer Comparison, Structure, Macro + Non-Macro Change)

Net signal: European specialty pharma + branded generics operates in a moderately concentrated, stable-margin industry; Orion's niche inhaler + royalty structure insulates it from the worst generic pricing pressure, but pricing reform across EU markets (reference pricing tightening, HTA harmonisation) is a 3-5 year headwind that the consensus may be underweighting [#J]

Market structure: European branded pharma + branded generics is a fragmented-to-moderately-concentrated industry; top 10 players hold approximately 30-40% of European branded prescription market by volume [#I]
- Sub: Inhaled therapy (Orion's core specialty) is more concentrated: top 5 inhaler companies (AstraZeneca, Boehringer Ingelheim, GSK, Orion, Cipla) hold ~75%+ of European DPI market share [#A]
- Sub: Branded generics (Orion's generic segment) faces structural pricing pressure in Nordic markets — Norwegian and Swedish reference pricing models ratchet downward annually [#A] ❌

Competitive intensity: Inhaled specialty: moderate-high; large global players (AZ, Boehringer, GSK) have superior marketing scale but struggle to match Orion's formulary incumbency and device training networks in Nordics [#A]
- Sub: Prostate cancer oncology (Nubeqa's space): Johnson & Johnson's enzalutamide (Xtandi) and apalutamide (Erleada) are direct competitors; Nubeqa differentiated by CNS penetration profile (lower CNS side effects) — clinically meaningful differentiation [#I]
- Sub: DPI generic entrants: Hikma, Teva, and Sandoz (Novartis spinoff) have announced or progressed DPI generic pipelines; these represent medium-term formulary-switch risk, particularly post-2026 as Easyhaler budesonide/formoterol patents in some markets approach expiry [#A]

New entrant risk (inhaler): Device complexity is a meaningful barrier — inhalation API bioequivalence studies are expensive (~€5-15m per molecule) and regulatorily demanding (FDA/EMA device + drug combination rules) [#A]
- Sub: No transformative new entrant in Orion's inhaler niche in the last 3 years; Cipla (India) is the most likely longer-horizon challenger given its global DPI manufacturing capability [#I]

Disruption force: Digital inhaler devices (GSK's Ellipta series with connectivity features, Propeller Health integration) are a nascent disruption vector — could shift formulary preference in progressive health systems that reward adherence data [#A]
- Sub: Orion has not publicly announced a digital inhaler strategy — risk that Easyhaler's analogue device design becomes formulary-disadvantaged in 5-10 year horizon as health systems digitise adherence monitoring [#A]

Regulatory shift: EU Joint Clinical Assessment (EU HTA Regulation, effective Jan 2025 for oncology) mandates harmonised HTA assessment for new medicines — could commoditise Nubeqa's market-specific pricing advantages in some EU markets [#A]
- Sub: Nordic reference pricing reform (particularly Denmark and Sweden tightening generic reference bands) is an ongoing headwind for Orion's Generic Products segment, where annual price erosion of 3-5% is the baseline [#I]
- Sub: Finnish domestic pricing: Orion benefits from FIMEA (Finnish Medicines Agency) familiarity and established relationships, but EU HTA harmonisation reduces the value of purely national reimbursement strategies [#A]

Macro shift (L12M-N36M): EUR/USD stability broadly neutral for Orion (primarily EUR-denominated); Finnish cost base has faced inflation pressure in energy and labour (Finland wage inflation ~4-5% pa 2022-23) [#A]
- Sub: Interest rate environment: near-zero net debt means rate environment is largely neutral to Orion; company actually benefits marginally from higher cash deposit rates [#I]
- Sub: Broader European pharma pricing reform (IRA analogue discussions in EU, EFPIA negotiations) is a medium-term headwind for branded products — estimated 2-5% pricing pressure over 3-5 years across European specialty pharma [#A]

Thematic trend: Aging European demographics are a structural tailwind for Orion's core therapy areas (respiratory disease, prostate cancer, cardiovascular — all strongly age-correlated) [#A]
- Sub: COPD + asthma prevalence growing ~1-2% pa in Europe; shift to maintenance DPI therapy (rather than rescue MDI) structurally benefits Orion Easyhaler [#I]
- Sub: Prostate cancer incidence expected to grow ~2-3% pa to 2035 in Europe (GLOBOCAN projections) — directly supportive of Nubeqa royalty duration [#I]

Supplier dynamics: Active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) for key Easyhaler molecules are manufactured in-house (Finland) — lower input cost volatility than peers dependent on Asian API supply [#A]
- Sub: API self-sufficiency is a competitive differentiator vs Almirall, Hikma, and generic competitors with India/China API dependency; relevant post-COVID supply chain repatriation narrative [#I]

Political shift: Finland NATO membership (2023) is immaterial to Orion's business; broader Nordic trade stability positive; no sanctions/geopolitical risk to Orion's current markets [#I]

Sceptical bullet: The structural moat narrative (device switching costs + Nordic incumbency) could erode faster than consensus implies if (a) Sandoz or Hikma launch Easyhaler-generic DPI versions in Germany/UK post-2026, triggering formulary substitution at scale, and (b) EU HTA harmonisation commoditises Nubeqa's country-specific pricing strategy; these are medium-term (3-5 year) risks that current SS models do not explicitly stress-test in their base cases [#J]
