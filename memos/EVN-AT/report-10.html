§9 — Risk Assessment + Sceptical Synthesis

The strongest bear case is a compounding of Red Lake normalisation failure + Cowal Stage H delay + gold price mean-reversion — a scenario where all three disappoint simultaneously would imply 35-50% valuation impairment from current levels. [#J]

  • Downside scenario #1 — Red Lake permanent impairment: If Red Lake throughput fails to normalise above 850ktpa (vs nameplate ~1.2Mtpa) by FY27, the asset's NPV contribution approaches near-zero; EVN paid ~US$375m for what was then guided as a path to 200kozpa; a 100kozpa steady-state dramatically reduces the acquisition's contribution [#A]

  • EPS downside: Red Lake normalization failure removes approximately A$0.05-0.08 EPS from FY27 consensus (rough estimate based on ~50kozpa miss at ~A$1,500 AISC margin); consensus EPS for FY27 is approximately A$0.30-0.35, so this is a 15-25% EPS impairment

  • Downside scenario #2 — Cowal Stage H denied or delayed to post-FY30: Cowal currently contributes approximately 30-35% of group production; if Stage H is denied, Cowal production begins declining materially post-FY28 [#A]

  • EPS downside: Cowal production decline scenario (no Stage H) removes approximately 80-90kozpa of gold by FY30; at current AISC margins and gold prices, this implies ~A$0.08-0.12 EPS impairment

  • Valuation re-rate: Combined with production-profile risk, a Cowal Stage H denial would likely trigger a 15-20% stock re-rate independently of earnings changes
