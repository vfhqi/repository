§7 — Competition Changes

(a) Chinese entrants to EU public charging:

  • Chinese manufacturers (BYD charging arm, CATL-adjacent, Huawei) are dominant in China — BYD deploying 500+ megachargers domestically, targeting 1,500kW speeds. [I]
  • European public tenders for major CPOs (IONITY, Fastned, Mer): no confirmed Chinese wins reported as of 2025. [I]
  • Barriers persist:
  • Cybersecurity concerns: Chinese national security law obliges companies to share data with Beijing on request; EU/UK infrastructure operators increasingly sensitive to this. [I]
  • No formal EU regulatory ban (unlike US where NEVI programme has domestic content provisions), but soft exclusion in sensitive corridor tenders is evident. [J]
  • AFIR interoperability requirements (ISO 15118-2 from Jan 2026) create specification hurdles for non-European manufacturers. [I]
  • ❌ Risk: as price pressure intensifies, lower-tier CPOs (smaller municipal operators, retail forecourts) may accept Chinese equipment. Price is a genuine differentiator for Chinese suppliers. [J]

(b) European incumbents — ABB E-mobility, Alpitronic, Siemens, Heliox:

  • ABI Research (July 2025) top-4 commercial EV charging vendors: Siemens, i-charging, ABB E-mobility, Kempower. [I]
  • Kempower: "strongest expansion trajectory with 22% energy growth" and "market-leading dynamic load management solution." [I]
  • ABB E-mobility: complete portfolio, added MCS1200 (1.2MW continuous, 1,500A) for truck charging — direct head-to-head with Kempower on MCS. [I]
  • Alpitronic: 4.5+ TWh delivered by Sep 2025 — scale player, likely stronger in South/Central Europe. [I]
  • Heliox (Hitachi): strong in depot/bus charging; less exposed to public network cycle. [I]
  • ❌ Siemens (broad industrial base, government relationships) entering commercial EV charging is a notable structural competitive development. [J]

(c) Tritium — exit creating opportunity:

  • Tritium filed for voluntary administration April 2024; declared insolvent after 6 buyers backed out. [I]
  • Exicom (Indian company) acquired Tritium out of receivership for USD 37M (August 2024). [I]
  • Tritium's exit removed a meaningful competitor, particularly in UK and Australia. [I]
  • ⚡ Tritium's insolvency is the clearest single positive structural change for Kempower in the competitive landscape — fewer credible European alternatives for CPOs requiring third-party equipment. [J]

(d) Kempower product differentiator:

  • Modular satellite architecture: power unit centralised, satellites at stalls — allows flexible deployment, lower per-stall cost, easier maintenance. [I]
  • ABI Research explicitly cites dynamic load management as market-leading. [I]
  • ❌ Advantage is real but not permanent — ABB and Siemens are investing in similar architectures. [J]

§9 — Political and Regulatory

- 2025: 150kW charging pool every 60km on TEN-T core network — effective now, compliance enforcement ongoing. [I]
- 2025: 1.3kW capacity per registered BEV per member state (capacity sufficiency test). [I]
- April 2025: Operators must make static and dynamic charge point data freely available. [I]
