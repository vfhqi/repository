§6 — Governance Structure + Red Flags

J-front verdict: Clean on standard red flags; structural governance concerns are the municipal-control concentration and modest minority protection mechanisms — not fraud signals, but genuine governance discount items

Voting rights structure:
- Share structure: Single class of ordinary shares; no dual-class or multi-vote structure; Iren is a standard Italian listed company (S.p.A.) with one-share-one-vote
- Municipal control concentration: The majority of voting shares are held by a consortium of municipalities (Comune di Torino and related municipal entities ~19%; Comune di Genova and related ~15%; Reggio Emilia and related entities ~10%; Iren Foundation and others ~5%) — aggregate municipal bloc approximately 45-50% of capital; de facto control without a formal majority but sufficient for slate-list dominance under Italian list-voting system
- Anti-takeover provisions: The list-voting system for board elections (voto di lista) means the controlling municipal bloc can elect the majority of the board unilaterally as long as their combined stake exceeds ~30-35% under standard Italian AGM quorum rules — effective entrenchment without formal poison pills or golden shares
- Free float: ~50-55%; meaningful institutional ownership (Italian pension funds, European utility funds, domestic retail)

Related-party transactions:
- Nature: Iren provides energy supply and infrastructure services to the municipalities that own it; these contracts are disclosed as related-party transactions in annual reports; the governance framework requires Comitato Parti Correlate (Related-Party Committee) approval for transactions above materiality thresholds
- Scale: Disclosed RPTs are in the range of €50-150m per annum in aggregate; not negligible but not anomalously large vs. revenue scale; terms are disclosed as at-market
- Assessment: RPTs are a structural feature of Italian municipal utility ownership — they exist for operational reasons (the municipalities commission services from Iren) rather than as extraction mechanisms; no evidence of RPTs being used for value extraction at the expense of minorities
- Red flag assessment: Not a red flag — standard disclosure, standard governance controls; the RPT committee process is functional

Audit history:
- External auditor: Deloitte & Touche S.p.A. (Italian audit engagement) — major international firm; consistent engagement, no recent auditor change
- Material weaknesses: None disclosed in the L3Y annual reports; no qualified audit opinion; no significant going-concern language
- Restatement history: No material restatements in the public record

Legal/regulatory issues:
- Litigation: Standard utility sector litigation (environmental, land, contractual disputes) disclosed in annual report notes; no material class action or regulatory investigation visible in public record
- ARERA regulatory relationship: ARERA (Italian energy/water regulator) sets tariff frameworks; no Iren-specific regulatory investigation or enforcement action in public record; compliance culture is standard Italian utility — constructive rather than adversarial
