EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

InterContinental Hotels Group operates as a global lodging franchise and management powerhouse with over 7,000 open hotels across more than 100 countries , generating adjusted operating profit of $1.27 billion in FY25 through a highly scalable, asset-light business model that licenses brand intellectual property to independent third-party hotel owners. The group's core fee revenue of $1.90 billion positions it as the third-largest pure- play hotel franchisor globally, though it remains 45% smaller than Hilton and 65% smaller than Marriott in absolute fee scale. IHG's pure franchise-led revenue model drives structurally superior returns on capital employed exceeding 20% , outperforming Marriott's 14.2% ROIC and Hilton's 17.1% ROIC in FY25. However, the company's EBITDA margin of 56.1% trails Marriott's 77.1% and Hilton's 74.3% due to lower central overhead recovery from its smaller scale base.

Asset-Light Business Model + Revenue Architecture

IHG operates as a brand franchisor and management platform , licensing intellectual property and centralized commercial systems to third-party hotel owners while earning fee revenue driven by two structural levers : net system size growth and revenue per available room. The franchise model insulates IHG from hotel operational cost inflation , though rising key money capital expenditure escalated from $57 million in 2017 to $206 million in 2024 , dragging down cash conversion below peer levels.
