Brief company descriptions
AT&S (Austria Technologie & Systemtechnik AG) operates as a leading global supplier of highly complex technological components, undergoing significant capacity and strategic transformations to meet next-generation computing demands.

Core Business Identity: AT&S is a premier global technology leader specializing in the development and large-scale manufacturing of high-end printed circuit boards (PCBs) and integrated circuit substrates. The company's sophisticated interconnection modules serve as the foundational infrastructure for data processing in high-end data centers, 5G base stations, autonomous driving systems, and advanced consumer electronics. RS efferies Research

Recent Structural Reorganization: Recognizing the divergent needs of its end markets, AT&S reorganized its overarching operational structure on April 1, 2023, shifting away from its legacy device-centric reporting. The company now operates through two primary technology-based segments: Electronics Solutions and Microelectronics. efferies Research NTRM

Electronics Solutions Segment: The Electronics Solutions business unit comprehensively bundles the company's printed circuit board and advanced module activities across the entire global group. This segment caters heavily to the mobile devices market, automotive electronics, industrial applications, and medical technology sectors. NTRM RS

Microelectronics Segment: The Microelectronics segment is intensely focused on the rapidly growing market for IC substrates. These highly complex substrates are absolutely critical for bridging the gap between high-performance processor modules and traditional PCBs, serving as the backbone for modern PCs, enterprise servers, and artificial intelligence compute clusters. NTRM efferies Research

Global Manufacturing Footprint: To serve its international client base, AT&S operates a highly sophisticated and globally distributed manufacturing network consisting of six primary production locations across Europe and Asia. This footprint allows the company to balance regional supply chain resilience with the mass-production efficiencies offered by Asian hubs. RS RS

European Production Centers: Within Europe, the company's manufacturing presence is anchored by its global headquarters in Leoben-Hinterberg, Austria, alongside a supplementary Austrian production plant located in Fehring. The Leoben facility stands out as the first and only IC substrate plant in Europe, serving as a critical hub for advanced prototyping, Ultra High Density applications, and technological R&D. RS nvestor Presentation

Asian Production Network: The company's high-volume manufacturing is predominantly located in Asia, featuring massive legacy facilities in Shanghai and Chongqing, China, as well as a plant in Nanjangud, India. Crucially, AT&S has recently expanded its Southeast Asian semiconductor network by constructing a massive, state-of-the-art IC substrate production facility in Kulim, Malaysia. RS nvestor Presentation

Global Sales and Distribution: To seamlessly support its manufacturing base and interface with global technology giants, AT&S maintains an extensive network of nine specialized sales and support offices worldwide. These critical commercial outposts are strategically located in major technology hubs including San Jose, Chicago, Düren, Seoul, Tokyo, and Taipei. 15 RS

Value Chain Positioning: Within the broader technology hardware value chain, AT&S operates primarily as an advanced Tier-1 and Tier-2 supplier. The company directly distributes its highly customized electronic components to leading original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and contract electronic manufacturers (CEMs) who assemble the final consumer or enterprise products. Customer Concentration Risks: Due to its relentless strategic focus on the absolute high end of the printed circuit board and substrate markets, the company's active customer base is inherently limited to a select group of premier technology leaders. This concentration is structurally high, with the top five largest customers accounting for an overwhelming 71% of total group revenue. RS RS

Key Client Relationships: According to analysis by Jefferies, AT&S maintains an outstanding reputation among pivotal global computing OEMs, specifically highlighting deep relationships with Intel, Apple, and AMD.

Forward-Looking Revenue Ambitions: Looking ahead over the next three years, AT&S has established a highly ambitious medium-term financial guidance framework targeting total revenue between €2.1 billion and €2.4 billion for the 2026/27 financial year. This growth trajectory is heavily reliant on the successful ramp-up of the new Kulim and Leoben facilities, which are just beginning to contribute to top-line results. RS NTRM

Medium-Term Profitability Goals: Alongside aggressive top-line expansion, the company's management team is targeting a structural improvement in profitability over the next 36 months. The stated corporate goals include achieving a sustainable EBITDA margin in the range of 24% to 28%, coupled with a long-term return on capital employed exceeding 12%. 24 RS
