B Executive Summary 23 findings
Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.
Judgement summary
Bravida is a competently run Nordic technical-installation consolidator riding genuine electrification and EU building-retrofit change forces, with a real, separable self-help margin recovery underway in Denmark and Sweden. The reservation is price: the stock sits at all-time highs on a premium multiple, with negative sell-side earnings-revision momentum and a competitor (Assemblin-Caverion) that is now both larger and higher-margin. On the Triaging test, whether the case carries large positive change forces, it passes clearly. The call is to progress to Early-Stage Assessment at medium-low conviction, with valuation and the ASCA scale threat as the questions that decide the case.
B.1 Business Description 7 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Multi-technical building-services installer and maintainer. Bravida installs and services electrical, heating, plumbing, HVAC, cooling, security and fire systems across ~206 locations and ~13,000 staff in Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Finland
2.
Mid-cap Nordic scale, revenue roughly doubled in a decade. FY2025 net sales SEK 28.2bn (~USD 2.79bn), up from SEK 14.7bn in 2016; the largest listed pure-play Nordic technical installer
3.
A defensive service book under a cyclical installation book. Installation 52% / service 48%; service grew +8% in the 2024 downturn while installation fell 3%, the structural buffer that limits the cycle
4.
Diversified geography and end-market. Sweden 47-48%, Denmark 24-25%, Norway 19-21%, Finland 8%; end-market spread across construction 26%, public 21%, commercial, industrial and property, with the largest client under 5% of revenue
5.
Asset-light, modest-return, conservatively financed. FY2025 EBITA margin 5.9% (versus a 7% target), ROE 13.8%, ROIC ~8.2%, capex under 1% of sales, net debt 1.1x EBITDA, cash conversion temporarily 79%
6.
Margin-over-volume operator with a roll-up engine. Strategy is project selectivity plus bolt-on consolidation (166 deals since 2014 at 5-6x EV/EBITA), though the engine slowed to four deals in 2025
7.
Investment style. A self-help margin-recovery and capital-allocation compounder on a cyclical-recovery inflection, with an electrification and building-retrofit thematic overlay
