Brief company descriptions

Roko AB is a perpetual owner of profitable, small and medium-sized niche businesses that are primarily headquartered in Europe.

The company was founded in 2019 by Fredrik Karlsson and Tomas Billing, and it was publicly listed on Nasdaq Stockholm in March 2025.

By the end of 2025, the group had grown to consolidate 30 different business units operating across approximately 20 countries.

For the full year 2025, Roko generated net sales of MSEK 6,452 and an adjusted EBITA of MSEK 1,339.

The fundamental business idea is to acquire companies with proven, sustainable business models and a history of continuous profit growth to create long-term shareholder value.

Unlike traditional private equity firms, Roko applies a perpetual investment horizon, meaning subsidiaries are acquired with the intention of never being sold.

Roko employs a highly decentralized governance structure where subsidiaries operate independently and retain their unique brand identities.

The company ensures aligned incentives by having selling founders and local management teams retain minority ownership stakes in their respective subsidiaries.

The parent company operates with minimal bureaucracy, employing only a handful of individuals at its headquarters to provide strategic guidance, capital allocation, and internal control.

Roko divides its operations into two primary reporting segments based on end-customer classification: Business to Business (B2B) and Business to Consumer (B2C).

The B2B segment is the largest division, accounting for 66% of total group revenue in 2025.

This B2B segment consists of 20 business units as of 2025, offering a highly diversified range of products and services across various industrial and commercial sectors.

The B2C segment accounted for the remaining 34% of total revenue in 2025 and includes 10 distinct business units.

Companies within the B2C segment are primarily niche manufacturers, brands, and distributors that target private consumers, featuring shorter sales cycles and a strong focus on marketing.

The vast majority of the group's revenue is derived from the sale of physical goods recognized at a specific point in time, with a smaller portion coming from service-related revenues like maintenance and support.

The United Kingdom is Roko's largest single geographic market, representing approximately 32% to 34% of total group sales.

Other significant markets include Norway, Sweden, Denmark, the Netherlands, and the USA, providing a broadly diversified revenue base.

Over the last few years, Roko has scaled rapidly by maintaining a steady acquisition pace of approximately five platform companies per year.

DNB Carnegie projects that Roko can sustain an EBITA growth rate of over 15% annually in the near future solely through continued acquisitions and solid cash conversion.

Looking forward, DNB Carnegie assesses that the company is well-positioned to potentially double its earnings over the next five years without increasing its financial leverage.

FULCRUM - M&A

Röko AB operates as a perpetual owner of profitable European niche businesses, employing a strategy where acquired subsidiaries are intended to be held indefinitely.

The fundamental business idea is to acquire mature companies that have demonstrated a continuous track record of profit and positive cash flow generation over several years.

The company's most stringent acquisition criterion is this requirement for continuous profit growth, typically evaluated over the five to seven years preceding the transaction.

Röko specifically targets majority stakes in founder-led companies, structuring deals that allow the selling founders and management to retain a minority interest.

These retained minority stakes are governed by put and call options, which provide the parent company with the contractual right to eventually acquire 100 percent of the subsidiary over time.

Over the five-year period spanning 2020 to 2024, the company maintained a consistent and disciplined acquisition pace, averaging five to six platform deals annually.

Since its inception in 2019, the group has successfully scaled its highly decentralized portfolio, acquiring more than 30 individual business units across various European geographies.

During the 2025 financial year, the group finalized three acquisitions, which included two new platform business units and one strategic add-on transaction.

The total cash consideration deployed for these subsidiary acquisitions during the 2025 fiscal year amounted to approximately SEK 946 million.

The pace of deal-making has visibly accelerated into early 2026, highlighted by the recent purchase of Lambda S.p.A., an Italian manufacturer of electronic components and lasers.

The company also executed a significant transaction in March 2026 by acquiring an 80 percent stake in NH Norsk Handel AS, a Norwegian sports equipment retailer.

KEY - Profit margins

Over the past three years, Röko has demonstrated a consistent ability to expand its profitability, establishing a robust margin profile that ranks highly among Swedish compounders.

The group's reported EBITA margin has steadily increased from 18.5% in 2023 to 19.6% in 2024.

This positive trajectory continued into the most recent financial year, with the adjusted EBITA margin expanding by another 100 basis points to reach 21.0% for the full year 2025.

At the segment level, the Business-to-Business (B2B) division serves as the primary engine for profitability, generating an adjusted EBITA margin of 22% in 2025, up from 20% in the previous year.

Meanwhile, the Business-to-Consumer (B2C) segment maintained a stable, high-level adjusted EBITA margin of 21% throughout both 2024 and 2025 before the allocation of central costs.

The company's overarching profitability target dictates that the adjusted EBITA margin must remain above 15% at the group level after accounting for central headquarters functions.

DNB Carnegie analysts emphasize that the company consistently acquires high-quality businesses that inherently boast strong standalone EBITA margins of approximately 20%.

During 2025, the addition of these higher-margin targets to the portfolio was a primary driver of the group's aggregate 100 basis point margin expansion.

Beyond favorable mix effects from M&A, underlying subsidiaries successfully improved their operating margins on a comparable, organic basis throughout 2025.

This organic margin improvement is underpinned by a decentralized strategy that continually encourages subsidiaries to raise prices on their goods and services.

A former Head of Acquisition at Lifco highlighted that strategic pricing adjustments and hands-off management are critical components to unlocking value and increasing margins post-acquisition.

DNB Carnegie notes that this margin-accretive playbook directly mirrors the highly successful strategy previously executed by Röko's current CEO, Fredrik Karlsson, during his tenure at Lifco.

To further optimize profitability, subsidiaries are actively encouraged to prioritize their internal resource allocation toward higher-margin products, specific customer segments, and more profitable end-markets.

Additional margin protection initiatives across the portfolio include the strategic phase-out of unprofitable legacy products.

At the group level, gross margins are exceptionally healthy, resting at approximately 43% as of 2024.

Because the cost of goods sold constitutes roughly 57% of sales, these strong gross margins provide a significant degree of operating leverage that should further boost EBITA margins as organic revenue growth accelerates.

During 2024 and 2025, robust management performance at the local level allowed subsidiaries with heavy exposure to the struggling European construction sector to successfully protect their margins despite declining sales volumes.

Sell-side forecasts from DNB Carnegie project that Röko's EBITDA margins will reliably expand from 22.6% in 2025 to 23.7% in 2026, and ultimately reach 24.1% by 2028.

DNB Carnegie anticipates the unadjusted EBITA margin will concurrently rise from 20.0% reported in 2025 to an estimated 21.5% by the 2028 financial year.

Group-level margins in 2025 successfully absorbed minor headwinds from negative exchange rate differences and approximately SEK 41 million in one-off transaction costs related to the company's IPO, indicating that underlying operational profitability is even stronger than reported statutory figures suggest.

KEY - Cash conversion

DNB Carnegie highlights that a company's cash conversion, defined as free cash flow divided by EBITA, dictates the share of profits that can be effectively reinvested into new acquisitions each year.

Historically, Röko's reported cash conversion has averaged approximately 53% over the three-year period spanning from 2022 to 2024.

Looking forward, DNB Carnegie projects that the group's cash conversion will naturally improve to an average of roughly 61% during the 2025 to 2027 forecast period.

This projected structural improvement in cash conversion expectations is primarily driven by structurally lower working capital requirements and suppressed capital expenditures relative to total sales.

The primary structural drag on Röko's reported free cash flow conversion stems from its decentralized, aligned-incentive ownership model, where the parent company typically only owns about 75% of its subsidiaries.

Because the group consolidates 100% of the underlying subsidiary EBITA but distributes roughly 25% of the net income as dividends to minority founders, this cash outflow significantly reduces the retained free cash flow available for parent-level reinvestment prior to M&A.

To illustrate the underlying portfolio's true standalone cash generation capability, DNB Carnegie estimates that if Röko owned 100% of its subsidiaries, its cash conversion would average a highly robust 79% over the 2025 to 2027 period.

In an effort to structurally enhance cash retention, management explicitly aims to gradually increase its ownership stakes in existing subsidiaries to a target level of approximately 85%.

Successful execution of this step-up in subsidiary ownership will mechanically strengthen the group's cash conversion profile and narrow the performance gap relative to fully consolidated industry peers.

From an absolute reporting perspective, the group generated a strong operating cash flow less capital expenditures of MSEK 1,006 for the full year 2025.

This 2025 cash generation figure represented a slight 2% decline compared to the MSEK 1,027 in post-capex operating cash flow generated during the prior 2024 financial year.

The underlying asset-light nature of the portfolio is safeguarded by strict acquisition criteria, mandating that target companies exhibit historical capital expenditures below 5% of their net sales.

DNB Carnegie notes that the group has maintained a highly efficient capital expenditure profile in practice, with pure capex-to-sales averaging just 1.2% over 2023 and 2024.

This asset-light efficiency marks a tangible operational improvement from the 2020 to 2022 period, when capital expenditures inclusive of leases consumed a higher 3% to 4% of total revenues.

Alongside disciplined capital expenditures, working capital intensity is a crucial component of the group's pre-M&A free cash flow generation.

The net working capital to sales ratio for the group averaged a highly efficient 15% across the 2023 and 2024 financial years.

DNB Carnegie points out that this 15% working capital intensity is notably superior to the 22% average ratio observed among proven Swedish compounder peers during 2022.

This competitive advantage in working capital efficiency is predominantly driven by the Business-to-Consumer segment, illustrating the structural cash flow benefits of Röko's sector-agnostic acquisition strategy.

Within the B2C division, subsidiaries such as Snowminds and AJAT operate with highly seasonal business models that structurally generate significant upfront advances from customers.

These customer prepayments form a substantial current liability on the balance sheet, actively depressing the net working capital requirement and boosting overall cash conversion.

Management cautions that the timing of these substantial customer prepayments, alongside standard corporate tax payments, can introduce meaningful volatility into the group's quarterly operating cash flow results.

For the full year 2025, total changes in working capital resulted in a minor net cash outflow of MSEK 24, primarily driven by an MSEK 51 increase in operating receivables.

KEY - Leverage

Röko AB maintains a strict and clearly defined capital structure policy, establishing a long-term target that its financial net debt should not exceed 3.0x adjusted EBITDA.

Management notes that this leverage ratio may temporarily exceed the 3.0x ceiling in the short term depending on the size and timing of new strategic acquisitions.

The group defines its financial net debt comprehensively, including short-term and long-term liabilities to credit institutions, bond loans, and interest-bearing pension provisions.

Crucially, this financial net debt calculation also incorporates the substantial liabilities related to mandatory put and call options for non-controlling interests, as well as contingent considerations from past transactions.

As of December 31, 2025, the group's total financial net debt stood at MSEK 3,040, representing a modest absolute increase from the MSEK 2,944 recorded at the end of 2024.

Despite this absolute increase in debt volume, the group's total financial net debt to LTM adjusted EBITDA ratio successfully declined to 2.0x at year-end 2025, down from 2.1x in the prior year.

This robust de-leveraging trajectory was primarily driven by strong internal cash generation and solid organic profit growth across the portfolio throughout the 2025 financial year.

The vast majority of the company's leverage consists of non-interest-bearing put and call option liabilities owed to minority subsidiary founders, which amounted to MSEK 2,602 at the end of 2025.

When isolating purely interest-bearing liabilities, Röko operates with an exceptionally conservative leverage profile.

The group's interest-bearing net debt amounted to just MSEK 438 at the end of 2025, an increase from MSEK 208 at the end of 2024 but still remarkably low in a historical context.

Consequently, the ratio of pure interest-bearing net debt to LTM adjusted EBITDA remained negligible at just 0.3x at the close of the 2025 financial year.

From a liquidity standpoint, the group maintains a highly secure position, holding available cash and cash equivalents of MSEK 407 as of December 31, 2025.

To further support its capital structure, the parent company has access to a dedicated overdraft facility of SEK 350 million, which remained entirely unutilized at the end of the year.

Additionally, the company possesses substantial undrawn borrowing capacity under its formal financial arrangements, totaling SEK 790 million as of early 2025.

Management has explicitly stated that the primary purpose of these loan agreements and undrawn facilities is to provide immediate financing for general corporate purposes, specifically including the funding of future acquisitions.

Since the third quarter of 2023, the company has successfully transitioned to being entirely self-financed, funding all new acquisitions through its own cash flows and external bank debt rather than relying on capital injections from its shareholders.

Looking forward over the next 12 to 18 months, management has clearly signaled its intention to actively raise additional external financing in various forms to support an accelerated acquisition agenda.

Regarding interest rate exposure, approximately 99 percent of the group's interest-bearing liabilities to credit institutions carry a variable interest rate.

Management calculates that a hypothetical 1 percent increase in the interest rate applied to the group's current debt load would have a minimal negative impact on net profit of approximately MSEK 4 over a twelve-month period.

DNB Carnegie notes that Röko's explicit target to keep net debt below 3.0x EBITDA is a fairly standard and prudent benchmark shared by several successfully listed Swedish compounder peers.

DNB Carnegie forecasts that the company's leverage ratio will comfortably remain within a manageable band of 2.1x to 2.6x through the 2025 to 2031 period, even while aggressively pursuing its M&A pipeline.

DNB Carnegie assesses that Röko possesses the structural capacity and balance sheet strength required to sustain an annual EBITA growth rate of over 15 percent solely through acquisitions without dangerously inflating its net debt to EBITDA ratio.

DNB Carnegie also highlights that while Röko's internal definition of net debt conservatively includes minority interests and earn-outs, it excludes IFRS-16 leasing liabilities, meaning standardized broker models may reflect slightly higher total leverage figures.

Over the last two years, the company has successfully de-levered its total financial net debt to just 2.0x EBITDA (down from 2.1x), comfortably below its 3.0x maximum target. Change in strategy/structure

Roko AB's core corporate strategy is to operate as a perpetual owner of profitable, small and medium-sized niche businesses primarily located across Europe.

A defining characteristic of this strategy is the total absence of asset sales, as the company acquires all subsidiaries with the explicit intention of holding them indefinitely.

Consequently, the group does not structurally classify its businesses into "core" or "non-core" assets.

The overall portfolio remains strictly sector-agnostic, though operations are administratively divided into Business to Business (B2B) and Business to Consumer (B2C) segments to facilitate relevant internal knowledge sharing.

A fundamental pillar of Roko's strategy is its highly decentralized organizational structure.

The parent company purposefully operates with minimal bureaucracy, employing only eight individuals at the central headquarters as of the 2025 financial year.

Local management teams within the subsidiaries are fully empowered with the autonomy to make independent operational decisions.

The central headquarters team functions primarily as a strategic sounding board and capital allocator rather than intervening in daily operational execution.

To ensure structural alignment within this decentralized model, Roko utilizes an equity partnership strategy where selling founders and local management retain minority ownership stakes.

Regarding financial objectives, the group's primary goal is to achieve continuous adjusted EBITA growth every single year.

Strategically, Roko targets a consolidated adjusted EBITA margin consistently above 15 percent, even after accounting for central group costs.

The company's capital structure policy strictly targets a financial net debt to adjusted EBITDA ratio below 3.0x, though temporary deviations are permitted following major acquisitions.
