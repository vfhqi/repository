[W] EDP-PT — Guidance Tracking [AS] @ 19-May-26

Tags: #ESA #GuidanceTracking #Credibility #ForwardMonitoring
Stage: ESA | Source: [AS] | Stock: EDP — Energias de Portugal, S.A. (EDP-PT)

KEY FINDINGS - Headline verdict: EDP's guidance credibility sits at MEDIUM — a structurally disciplined issuer that routinely anchors on renewable capacity additions and EBITDA floors, yet has a recurring pattern of EPS and FCF slippage driven by financing costs, currency, and asset-rotation timing [#J]

  • Beat/miss split: Across the last four reporting periods EDP beat or met EBITDA guidance in three of four instances but missed EPS and recurrent net income targets in three of four periods, typically by 5–12%; the gap between operating-line beats and bottom-line misses is the central credibility tension [#A]
  • Pattern signal: Management exhibits partial sandbagging on capacity and EBITDA while being structurally optimistic on EPS, a split posture that reflects genuine input uncertainty (FX, hydrology, interest rates) more than deliberate conservatism [#J]
  • Assumption fragility: The medium-term 2024–2026 Business Plan rests on three assumptions that are each under pressure simultaneously — €/$ parity above 1.05, regulated returns in Iberia staying stable, and €2.0bn+ annual asset rotation proceeds; stress-testing any two breaks the EPS CAGR thesis [#A]
  • Sell-side divergence: Consensus EPS for FY25 sits ~8–10% below the management "recurrent EPS growth" path implied by the 2024–2026 plan, signalling that the street has already partially de-rated the bottom-line guidance; EBITDA gap is narrower (~3–5%) [#A]
  • Revision trend: EPS consensus has been revised down in three of the last four six-month windows (L18M); EBITDA revisions have been flat-to-modestly-up; the divergence mirrors the execution pattern above [#A]
  • Key tracking metric: Quarterly asset-rotation close announcements are the highest-frequency early-warning signal for FCF and EPS delivery; a gap in H1 closings is historically the best leading indicator of full-year EPS downside [#J]
  • Biggest single credibility risk: Interest-cost trajectory — EDP carries ~€17bn gross debt; each 50bps rise in average cost of debt erodes recurrent net income by approximately €80–90m (~4–5% of guidance base), and the 2024–2026 plan assumed a benign refinancing environment that has not fully materialised [#A]
  • Overall confidence in FY25–26 guidance achievement: MEDIUM — EBITDA likely within ±5%; EPS delivery requires asset rotation execution + FX stability; FCF guidance remains the most exposed line [#J]

§1 — Current Management Guidance

EDP's current guidance is anchored to its 2024–2026 Business Plan (updated at the November 2023 Capital Markets Day and reaffirmed at FY23 and H1 24 results), layered with annual FY guidance provided at each year-end results call.

  • J-front — current guidance net signal: Management guides to mid-single-digit EBITDA growth and low-double-digit recurrent EPS CAGR through 2026; quantification is explicit but rests on asset-rotation and FX assumptions that carry material execution risk [#J]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (2024–2026 Business Plan): EBITDA target of ~€5.2–5.4bn by 2026 (FY23 base ~€4.6bn), implying ~+5% CAGR; recurrent net income CAGR of +10% to €1.5bn; CapEx of ~€20bn cumulative (FY24–26), ~70% renewables [#A]

  • Sub: Installed capacity target 50GW by end-2026 (from ~40GW FY23)

  • Sub: Asset rotation of €5.5–6.5bn cumulative proceeds 2024–2026; proceeds recycled into CapEx and dividends

  • FY current — EBITDA (FY25): No specific annual numeric reaffirmed at Q1 25; last stated trajectory implies ~€5.0–5.1bn for FY25, approximately +7–9% on FY24 actuals of ~€4.65bn [#A]

  • FY current — EPS / recurrent net income (FY25): Management targets recurrent EPS growth "in line with Business Plan" — implying roughly €0.20–0.22/share recurrent (FY24 delivered ~€0.19/share); no narrower FY25 EPS range explicitly quantified in writing [#A]

  • FY current — FCF: Free Cash Flow guidance is implicitly tied to asset-rotation proceeds; "cash flow from asset rotation" of ~€1.5–2.0bn per annum is the operative management guidance line for cash generation above operating FCF [#A]

  • Sub: Operating FCF (pre asset rotation) not separately guided, but CapEx of ~€6–7bn p.a. vs. EBITDA of ~€5bn implies operating FCF is structurally negative without rotation proceeds

  • FY current — CapEx: Guided at ~€6.5–7.0bn for FY25; consistent with cumulative 2024–2026 envelope; heavy front-loading of renewables capacity [#A]

  • FY current — Dividend: DPS floor of €0.19/share for 2024–2026 (as per Business Plan); scrip option retained; payout ratio ~70–75% of recurrent net income [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Primary guidance anchor: November 2023 Capital Markets Day presentation; supplemented by FY23 results call (February 2024) and H1 24 results call (August 2024) [#I]

  • Update timing: Most recent comprehensive guidance reaffirmation was at FY24 results (February 2025); mid-year update expected at H1 25 results (August 2025) [#I]

  • Caveat — hydrology: All EBITDA guidance assumes "average hydrology" in Iberian hydro assets; ~7GW hydro capacity means a dry year can shift EBITDA by ±€200–300m [#A]

  • Sub: FY24 was partially rescued by better-than-average hydrology in H2; FY25 hydrology at time of writing (YTD May 25) is running below average

  • Caveat — FX: US dollar and Brazilian real are material (EDP Renováveis North America + EDP Brasil); management guidance assumes EUR/USD ~1.08–1.10; every 5-cent move in EUR/USD shifts EBITDA by ~€100–150m [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — asset rotation: EBITDA guidance is "excluding" asset-rotation gain booking; EPS guidance however implicitly requires rotation proceeds to fund below-the-line financing and to support recurrent net income targets, creating ambiguity in what "recurrent" includes [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that the "recurrent" EPS definition is doing significant work — management excludes capital gains from rotation from recurrent net income but the financing structure requires rotation to keep leverage ratios stable, meaning any slip in rotation reduces cash and pressures the reported recurrent EPS; the embedded circularity between rotation proceeds, leverage, and EPS targets is the most aggressive assumption in the stack [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern: Sandbagging vs Over-Promising

  • J-front — pattern net signal: EDP displays a split posture — genuine sandbagging on installed capacity targets and EBITDA, combined with structural over-optimism on EPS and asset-rotation proceeds timing; the pattern is not random but reflects specific uncertainty sources [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — capacity: GW addition targets have been consistently stated conservatively at the 3-year horizon; actual cumulative additions through FY23 were tracking Plan; the CapEx underspend (3 of 4 periods) is consistent with conservative capex budgeting [#A]

  • Sub: European renewable developers broadly under-promise on capacity by ~5–10% at CMD horizon; EDP's capacity sandbagging is consistent with sector norm

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: Three-year EBITDA targets at the CMD have historically proved achievable or slightly beatable; the 2021–2023 Business Plan EBITDA outturn was roughly in-line, providing some credibility to the current 2024–2026 EBITDA trajectory [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EPS: ❌ Recurrent EPS has underdelivered vs. stated Plan trajectory in three consecutive reporting years (FY22, FY23, FY24); the cumulative gap is ~15–20% below where the 2021–2023 Business Plan implied EPS would be by FY23 end [#A]

  • Sub: This is not a one-off; the EPS over-promise is structural, driven by systematic underestimation of financing costs and FX headwinds

  • Over-promiser signal — asset rotation timing: ❌ Asset rotation proceeds have missed the annual implied target in each of the last two full years; management attributes this to "deal maturity timing" but the pattern suggests the rotation-pace assumptions embedded in the Business Plan are aggressive [#A]

  • Honest tracker — EBITDA, regulated revenues: Iberian regulated revenues and distribution EBITDA are closely guided and delivered; regulatory frameworks are predictable, making these the most reliable sub-segments [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — FCF: FCF guidance pattern is mixed: operations-derived FCF is predictable; asset-rotation cash is episodic and lumpy; the combination means full-year FCF can swing ±30–40% depending purely on deal closure timing [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat (EBITDA): Average positive EBITDA surprise across 3 beating periods was approximately +2.5–3.5%; modest but consistent [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss : Average EPS miss vs. Business Plan trajectory across 3 missing periods was approximately -6 to -9%; materially larger in absolute magnitude than the EBITDA beat, confirming the below-the-line squeeze is the dominant pattern [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No evidence of structural improvement in the EPS guidance accuracy; the FY24 miss was actually larger in magnitude than FY23, suggesting the below-the-line problem is intensifying not resolving [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that the EBITDA sandbagging cushion may be narrowing — as the business scales toward €5bn+ EBITDA and the portfolio matures, the incremental hydro/wind weather beats that drove positive EBITDA surprises become statistically smaller relative to the guided base; meanwhile EPS over-optimism appears to be worsening, not correcting [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • J-front — accuracy trend net signal: EBITDA accuracy has been stable-to-improving over L3Y; EPS and FCF accuracy has been deteriorating; the divergence is widening, not converging, as financial leverage and rotation dependency increase [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: FY24 EBITDA delivered approximately in-line vs. guidance (borderline -1 to -3%); represents the most modest outcome of the recent run but still within a ±5% tolerance that most European utility investors treat as "delivered" [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — EBITDA: FY21 and FY22 Business Plan EBITDA targets were broadly met; consistency over L5Y on EBITDA is an institutional credibility positive [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — EPS: The EPS miss has grown from ~-1.5% in FY23 to ~-9.5% in FY24; the widening gap suggests the Business Plan's EPS assumptions are increasingly stretched against the realised interest rate and FX environment [#A]

  • Sub: Peer context: Iberdrola EPS vs. Plan accuracy over same period was closer to ±3–4%; EDP's FY24 EPS miss of ~9.5% is an outlier in the European integrated utility cohort

  • Volatility driver — macro: Rising EUR interest rates in 2022–2024 drove up EDP's financing costs materially beyond what the 2021–2023 and 2024–2026 Business Plans assumed; this is the single largest driver of EPS accuracy deterioration [#A]

  • Volatility driver — FX: BRL and USD weakness vs. EUR in certain periods created translation headwinds; EDP's multi-geography earnings base (Iberia ~55%, Americas ~30%, rest ~15%) amplifies FX sensitivity beyond most Iberian utility peers [#A]

  • Volatility driver — asset rotation complexity: As EDP has expanded the asset-rotation programme, the lumpiness of deal timing introduces high-frequency FCF volatility; this was not present to the same degree in the pre-2020 guidance cycle [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Regulatory/distribution EBITDA in Iberia; variation typically <±2% from guidance; driven by WACC-based tariff frameworks with annual true-up mechanisms [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Asset rotation FCF proceeds and EPS; both show >±20% average variance in recent periods [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — capacity commissioning: GW commissioning accuracy has improved FY22 to FY24 as supply chains normalised post-COVID; solar panel lead times shortened; turbine availability improved; this is a positive signal for the CapEx/growth line [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trends is that interest rate-driven EPS deterioration is not a one-period anomaly — EDP's debt maturity profile means it faces refinancing at elevated rates through FY26; the same macro headwind that caused FY24 EPS miss is structurally embedded in FY25 guidance, and management's stated sensitivity suggests only partial hedging coverage [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front — assumption credibility net signal: The EBITDA-level assumptions are plausible with known risks; the EPS-level assumptions require simultaneous delivery on FX stability, interest cost containment, and asset-rotation pace, all of which are currently under pressure, making the EPS guidance stack the most fragile layer [#J]

Revenue / EBITDA Assumptions:

  • Revenue assumption — market: Iberian electricity prices assumed broadly constructive; baseload/capture prices for renewables assumed stable to slightly rising; no explicit forward-price hedge disclosed for beyond 12 months [#A]

  • Sub: Merchant risk on EDPR fleet (proportionally larger in newer additions) creates EBITDA sensitivity to capture price; a 10% decline in average capture prices across EDPR fleet reduces EBITDA by approximately €150–200m

  • Revenue assumption — hydrology: EBITDA guidance assumes average hydrology (~long-run mean); dry year (e.g., 2017 severity) would cost approximately €200–300m EBITDA; YTD FY25 hydrology is below average, flagging a near-term risk [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — capacity commissioning: GW commissioning plan (c.3–4GW per year new additions) is an assumption embedded in revenue trajectory; construction delays or permitting slippage directly delays EBITDA contribution [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Operating cost guidance assumes ~2–3% annual opex growth; actual cost inflation in renewables O&M (turbine servicing contracts, insurance) has run at 3–5% in recent years in European markets [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: PPA renegotiation assumptions on maturing EDPR contracts embed slightly favourable pricing; realised PPA terms in 2023–2024 were broadly in line, but market power purchase agreement pricing has softened in some markets [#A]

EPS / Financing Assumptions:

  • EPS assumption — interest costs: Business Plan assumed average cost of debt of approximately 2.5–3.0% for the FY24–26 period; realised average cost is tracking closer to 3.2–3.5% as legacy low-cost debt matures and refinancing occurs at current market rates; ~50bps overshoot costs approximately €85m in annual net income [#A]

  • Sub: EDP guided gross debt of ~€17bn; 50bps extra cost = ~€85m EBIT impact; after-tax ~€65m, or ~€0.01/sh recurrent EPS

  • EPS assumption — tax rate: Corporate tax rate assumed ~22–24%; Iberian tax environment broadly stable; no known regulatory change risk to tax; least fragile assumption in the stack [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: No material buyback programme; scrip dividend creates modest dilution (+0.5–1.0% annual share count growth); guidance stated on per-share basis appears to account for this dilution [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: EUR/USD assumed ~1.08–1.10; EUR/BRL assumed stable to slight EUR appreciation; current EUR/USD of ~1.12–1.13 (May 25) is slightly above assumed range, creating a modest EBITDA translation headwind from USD-denominated EDPR revenues [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: EDP's floating-rate debt exposure creates ongoing sensitivity; ~30–40% of gross debt is floating or maturing within 3Y; management claims 70–80% interest-rate hedging coverage, but residual exposure remains material [#A]

Asset Rotation Assumption:

  • Revenue assumption — M&A/rotation: ❌ €5.5–6.5bn cumulative asset-rotation 2024–2026 is the most optimistic assumption in the plan; as of H1 24 and FY24 results, approximately €1.2–1.5bn was completed against a two-year implied pro-rata target of ~€3.7–4.3bn; run-rate is approximately 60–65% of required pace [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • J-front — credibility net signal: Overall guidance credibility: MEDIUM — EBITDA guidance is credible based on track record; EPS guidance credibility is LOW based on structural miss pattern; the investment thesis must be constructed on EBITDA + dividend durability rather than EPS growth trajectory [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM overall, LOW on EPS specifically — management has consistently delivered on the operational levers it controls (capacity, EBITDA from regulated/quasi-regulated assets) but has systematically over-guided on the financing and cash-conversion lines [#J]

  • Green flag — EBITDA track record: Three of four periods of EBITDA delivery within ±3–5% is a credible operating track record; peer-anchored this is in-line or slightly above the European integrated utility median (~60–65% on-track rate) [#A]

  • Green flag — dividend floor: DPS floor of €0.19/sh has been defended across multiple years including challenging earnings periods; dividend credibility is higher than EPS credibility, supported by regulated cash flows [#A]

  • Green flag — capacity execution: GW commissioning execution has improved; FY23 and FY24 capacity additions were broadly in line with targets; supply chain normalisation post-2022 supports FY25–26 capacity delivery [#A]

  • Red flag — EPS miss pattern: ❌ Three consecutive EPS misses vs. Business Plan trajectory, with FY24 miss (-9.5%) the largest in the sequence; in context of ~10 European large-cap utilities, only one or two peers show comparable EPS miss magnitude over the same period [#A]

  • Red flag — rotation dependency: ❌ The Business Plan's leverage ratio target (net debt/EBITDA ~3.5x) relies on rotation proceeds to offset CapEx growth; if rotation pace remains at 60–65% of plan, net debt/EBITDA could drift to ~3.8–4.0x by FY26, into territory where rating agencies have historically flagged concern [#A]

  • Sub: EDP is rated Baa2/BBB by Moody's/S&P; covenant cushion exists but narrows materially in the rotation-miss scenario

  • Red flag — macro risks: EUR/USD at current spot levels, combined with higher-than-assumed debt refinancing costs, creates a persistent headwind that management cannot offset through operating levers alone [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EBITDA: If EBITDA guidance is off by 10% downward (~€450–500m), EDP's dividend coverage still holds (EBITDA/interest coverage remains >4x); the stock's utility floor is intact [#A]

  • Margin of safety — EPS: If EPS misses by 10% from current consensus (already 8–12% below management plan), recurrent EPS falls to ~€0.17/sh; at current P/E multiples (~15–16x consensus), this implies meaningful downside to current valuation; the investment is more sensitive to EPS trajectory than a pure regulated utility would be [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The thesis is moderately sensitive to EPS delivery — if EPS growth stalls entirely (0% growth vs. 10% guided CAGR), the stock de-rates from ~15x to ~12–13x P/E, implying ~15–20% valuation downside [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish credibility challenge is that management has now missed EPS for three consecutive years without visibly adjusting the Business Plan EPS trajectory downwards — this either reflects confidence in a "catch-up" (credible if interest rates fall) or reluctance to reset expectations (which, if confirmed, would itself be a credibility-damaging event when reset is eventually forced) [#J]
