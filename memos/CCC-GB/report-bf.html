EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Computacenter operates a highly scaled, resilient IT reseller and services model combining Technology Sourcing, Professional Services, and Managed Services to support end-to-end technology lifecycles for large corporate and public sector organizations. The business targets the largest 500 to 1,000 enterprises in each operating country, with almost a quarter of major customers maintaining relationships exceeding ten years. Geographic concentration has shifted dramatically, with North America expanding from 24% to 39% of Group adjusted operating profit in 2025, driven primarily by US and UK hyperscaler investments in AI-centric data center infrastructure. The business delivered 32% constant currency revenue growth in 2025, substantially outperforming the 7% average IT market growth base rate, with revenue scale directly driving gross profit conversion and 11.3% adjusted operating profit growth. However, rapid pivot toward US hyperscalers creates concentration risk; if AI infrastructure spending moderates, the sourcing revenue model provides minimal downside protection.

Financial Profile and Capital Returns

Group revenue reached £9,193.9m in 2025, up 32.0% reported, with North America Technology Sourcing growing 62.0% in constant currency. Group gross margin compressed by 242 basis points to 12.4% in 2025, reflecting high-volume, lower-margin US hyperscale business, with adjusted operating margin of 3.0% significantly trailing CDW's 8.9% and Softcat's high-teens levels. Adjusted diluted EPS increased 9.5% to 175.1p in 2025, while the group ended with £606.0m of adjusted net funds. Management distributed over £1bn to shareholders since 2013 via dividends and special returns, raising 2025 dividend 5.5% to 74.6p following a £200m share buyback in 2024. Return on capital employed averaged 49.3% over the four years to 2023, demonstrating exceptional capital efficiency driven by agency-like software sales and efficient hardware pass-through. Headline growth heavily relies on lower-margin US hardware sales that mask deteriorating profitability metrics; cyclical downturn in hyperscaler data center spending would expose bloated inventory and compress operating margins.

Value Chain Position and Margin Distribution

Computacenter occupies a defensible but structurally low-margin value-chain node, offsetting upstream supplier power and downstream hyperscaler concentration through scale-driven integration services. Computacenter's core VAR stage captures relatively low gross margins of 10-18% compared to upstream semiconductor components at 50-70%, with upstream vendors capturing vast majority of economic rent through IP moats. The business operates across two distinct downstream stages combining lower-margin Technology Sourcing with higher-margin IT Services, with no vertical integration into proprietary hardware manufacturing, bridging gaps via massive proprietary integration centers in Hatfield and Atlanta that enable high-volume configuration of complex liquid-cooled AI racks. Upstream technology vendors wield significant pricing power dictating channel economics, though Computacenter dampens supplier power by remaining strictly vendor-independent, partnering with over 3,000 technology brands. Hyper-concentration of AI investments among few tier-1 cloud providers grants customers immense negotiating leverage, with Computacenter acting strictly as agent in many transactions, capturing only predetermined commission rather than dictating end pricing. Value-chain position vulnerable to disintermediation; if hyperscalers or hardware OEMs transact directly for AI infrastructure, Computacenter's volume would compress severely.

Customer Concentration and Competitive Positioning

Extreme top-1 concentration creates severe downside revenue risk, with largest North American customer accounting for £2,731.2m or nearly 30% of 2025 Group revenue, more than doubling from £1,095.5m in 2024. Managed Services division historically demonstrated elite 94% contract renewal rate, with approximately quarter of major customers maintaining relationships exceeding ten years. Pricing pass-through achieved in Managed Services via Cost of Living Adjustment clauses to mechanically pass through underlying wage inflation, though inflation protection imperfect with onerous contract provisions tripling to £14.8m in 2025. Massive proprietary Integration Centers grant structural cost advantage over smaller regional VARs, with firm focusing on complex deployments like liquid-cooled AI racks, though US operations rapidly gained global share swelling from 4% of EBIT in 2018 to over 35%. Mega-vendors like Microsoft and Salesforce increasingly bypassing VARs to sell cloud software directly to large enterprises, with sustained reduction in vendor incentives posing structural challenge to traditional software reselling.

Foundation Quality and Operator Assessment

Computacenter pairs highly advantaged infrastructure sourcing model with generational CEO Mike Norris serving since 1994, orchestrating unmatched 19-year track record of consistent EPS growth, shifting seamlessly from traditional resale to complex hybrid-cloud integration. Succession planning major board focus acknowledging intense difficulty of replacing successful three-decade leader, though senior operating team boasts exceptional institutional knowledge with most direct reports serving over ten years, and management explicitly targeted international scaling, successfully growing North American division from 3% to nearly 40% of profits. Business delivered exceptional 16% CAGR in adjusted EPS from 2005 to 2023, matching premium profit trajectory of Softcat, while returning over £1 billion to shareholders since 2013, with £200 million buyback executed during 2024 and $352 million total investment across four US acquisitions producing £95 million in 2025 estimated profit. Massive proprietary Integration Centers in Hatfield and Atlanta create insurmountable physical barriers against smaller regional VARs, with firm commanding 5% UK market share, while roughly one-third of total enterprise customers utilize all three main offerings creating severe structural integration and switching costs, and global addressable IT market estimated at £1.6 trillion providing massive structural runway for mid-single-digit organic growth. Asset-light, cash-generative model consistently produces Return on Capital Employed metrics averaging nearly 50% over recent years, vastly outpacing broader European IT services average, while business historically converted roughly 100% of cumulative adjusted net income into free cash flow. Computacenter's historical exceptionalism inextricably linked to Mike Norris's 30-year heroic tenure; his eventual departure could expose lack of underlying mechanical advantage against nimbler software-focused peers.

Structural Evolution and Long-Term Disruption

Computacenter benefits from generational AI hardware infrastructure tailwinds, with massive physical integration centers creating high barriers against smaller regional VARs and hyperscale rack integration requiring significant working capital and balance sheet strength protecting incumbents. Generative AI tools structurally lowering coding costs and compressing traditional IT service volumes, with AI agentic automation potentially reducing global services market size by $400-500 billion by 2030. Highly fragmented IT reseller market consolidating around scaled players capable of managing complex AI deployments, with hyperscalers remaining dependent on partners for rack-level physical integration, though value and pricing leverage continue accruing to upstream component IP owners and direct-to-customer cloud software models posing ongoing structural threat to traditional software reselling volumes. The $92m acquisition of Government Acquisitions Inc provides direct access to lucrative US federal sector broadening addressable market with balanced civil and defence portfolio, while North American hyperscale demand driving unprecedented volume growth for high-performance computing infrastructure and broader addressable business IT market forecast to grow over 7% annually through 2029, with targeted acquisitions like AgreeYa aiming to accelerate higher-margin professional services penetration. Computacenter perfectly aligns with current market focus on AI-centric infrastructure and data center capital expenditure, widely viewed as primary picks-and-shovels play for AI infrastructure supercycle, though current paradigm fit relies heavily on cyclical hyperscaler hardware boom; once data centers built, value shifts to AI software where Computacenter lacks structural moat.
