Plausible problems to monitor 5 findings

1.
Legacy Business Erosion: Ambu's aggressive repricing initiatives in the Anesthesia & Patient Monitoring segment forced the surrender of exclusive supply agreements to non-exclusive contracts, driving a 4.4% revenue decline and exposing the business to accelerating customer churn . Combined legacy division growth is expected to slow to just 1% compared to higher historical rates . [Cross-ref: Section 1 + Section 5 + Section 9]
2.
Urology Commoditization Risk: Chinese manufacturers like Pusen offer disposable flexible ureteroscopes at highly disruptive price points, depressing average selling prices in standard urology procedures . Boston Scientific dominates single-use ureteroscopy with 70% share, while Ambu faces increased competition in cystoscopy from low-cost entrants . [Cross-ref: Section 3 + Section 7 + Section 9]
3.
Malaysian Tariff Exposure: The Penang plant produces approximately 55% of global output, creating material US import tariff penalties with an estimated 2 percentage point EBIT margin headwind in FY25/26 . Major rivals manufacturing in China face tariffs of up to 54% compared to Malaysia's 24%, yet Ambu's geographic sales mismatch remains acute . [Cross-ref: Section 1 + Section 2 + Section 6]
4.
Working Capital Deterioration: Net working capital expanded to 22% of revenue in Q2 2025/26, exceeding the 20% long-term target due to deliberate inventory stockpiling . Cash conversion dropped to 35% in FY25 from 52% in FY24, pressured by elevated inventories and capex rising to DKK 384m . Peer Boston Scientific maintains superior working capital discipline at higher scale. [Cross-ref: Section 2 + Section 6 + Section 8]
5.
Gastroenterology Adoption Barrier: Gastroenterology represents a massive DKK 120bn TAM but remains under 1% single-use penetrated, and Ambu previously deprioritized high-resource GI colonoscopes after initial efforts were not successful . Clinicians view single-use GI scopes as prohibitively expensive and display high resistance due to established sterilization infrastructures . [Cross-ref: Section 1 + Section 5 + Section 10]

Superior Value Chain Margin Capture: Ambu's device manufacturing stage captures 60-70% gross margins , positioning at the top 5% of medtech value chain profitability relative to upstream component suppliers (30-40% gross margin) and downstream distributors (8-15% gross margin), demonstrating exceptional value accrual concentration in final assembly and sterilization operations.
