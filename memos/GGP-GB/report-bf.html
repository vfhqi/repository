EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Greatland Gold completed a transformative corporate reorganization in June 2025, establishing a robust duallisted platform through a top-hat restructure that inserted Greatland Resources Limited as the Australian parent company . This structural consolidation, combined with the acquisition of 100% of both the Telfer mine and Havieron project from Newmont for US$450 million in December 2024 , positions Greatland as one of Western Australia's largest gold-copper producers in the Paterson Province. The company's strategic foundation rests on three pillars: operational cash generation from the producing Telfer asset , development execution at the world-class Havieron underground project , and regional exploration optionality across an extensive Paterson Province portfolio. 1

Telfer represents a cornerstone operational asset featuring both open pit and underground operations that have produced more than 15 million ounces of gold since 1977 . The mine's 22 million tonnes per annum processing capacity creates a formidable infrastructure moat in the southern Paterson Province, establishing Greatland as the region's dominant processing hub. During the half-year ending December 2025, Telfer produced 167,163 gold ounces and 6,894 copper tonnes, generating A$977 million in total sales revenue . However, near-term operating costs remain elevated, with all-in sustaining costs reaching A$2,176 per ounce

during H1 FY26, reflecting the transition phase as the operation shifts from low-grade stockpiles to higher-grade underground ore sources. 9

The Havieron development project stands as the company's most significant value driver, hosting a 7.0 million ounce gold resource within a compact, high-grade underground footprint. The asset's development is significantly de-risked by utilizing Telfer's existing processing infrastructure, eliminating the massive capital expenditure required for standalone mill construction . Pre-production capital expenditure is budgeted at A$1,065 million, with an additional A$673 million allocated for post-production expansion capital to install underground crushing systems . Management secured a $500 million corporate debt facility from a tier-one banking syndicate in June 2026, establishing total available liquidity exceeding $1.7 billion to fully fund the development through to commercial production. The project targets steady-state production of 266,000 gold ounces annually at a highly competitive AISC of A$1,610 per ounce , supported by underground mining grades of 2.52 g/t gold and 0.30% copper . 17

The competitive landscape in the Paterson Province exhibits extreme consolidation , with Greatland controlling the southern processing hub and Rio Tinto anchoring the northern sector through its Winu project. The province hosts a recognized endowment of more than 39 million ounces of gold and over 5 million tonnes of copper across highly prospective Proterozoic terrain covered by thick overburden . Regional junior explorers including Antipa Minerals, Paterson Resources, and Encounter Resources face substantial standalone development barriers, with capital requirements exceeding A$300 million to construct independent processing facilities. This structural dynamic positions Greatland as the natural consolidator for regional discoveries , leveraging Telfer's substantial excess processing capacity to capture third-party ore feed opportunities. Shaun Day brings 25 years of distinguished resource development experience, having achieved over 900% total shareholder returns during his tenure as CFO at Northern Star . The board features worldclass mining sector veterans , including Deputy Chairman Elizabeth Gaines, former CEO of Fortescue Metals, establishing exceptional governance depth. However, the resignation of Chief Operating Officer Simon Tyrrell in February 2026 introduces short-term operational execution risks during the critical transition to active Havieron production . The appointment of Group Mining Engineer Otto Richter as Acting COO, supported by Tyrrell's transition coverage through June 2026, aims to preserve operational continuity during this sensitive phase. 33

Value chain positioning reveals that processing represents the most profit-favored stage, capturing estimated gross margins of 40% to 50% compared to 30% to 40% for extraction and only 3% to 8% for third-party refining.

Greatland's 100% ownership of Telfer eliminates the massive capital expenditure of building a new processing plant, saving years of permitting and development time for underground ore processing . The company produces both refined gold doré on-site and copper-gold concentrate for export to international smelters via Port Hedland, capturing maximum value across the refining split while transferring shipping risk to buyers under CIF terms. Environmental and regulatory momentum accelerated in mid-2026 with the receipt of critical approvals. Greatland secured federal environmental clearance under the EPBC Act on April 24, 2026, followed by Western Australia's Part IV approval on May 25, 2026, enabling the board to approve the Final Investment Decision on June 1, 2026 . However, substantive development activities remain paused until secondary environmental approvals are granted, with regulators imposing strict conditions protecting the endangered night parrot that confine certain Telfer-Havieron operations to daytime hours . These wildlife protection mandates introduce potential operational constraints that could impact mill throughput optimization.

Statistical outliers highlight Greatland's exceptional scale advantages. The company's consolidated 15 million ounce regional gold resource base significantly exceeds typical mid-tier development assets, providing multidecade mine life optionality. The $500 million tier-one debt facility with no hedging requirements represents unprecedented financial flexibility for an emerging Australian producer, contrasting sharply with typical project finance structures that mandate substantial forward gold sales. Greatland's A$1,738 million total development budget dwarfs regional peer exploration programs by more than an order of magnitude, underscoring the capital intensity differential between infrastructure-backed development and standalone exploration. The investment case fundamentally rests on execution of the Havieron development timeline while maintaining Telfer's operational stability and cash generation. Success requires navigating secondary environmental approvals, completing mill upgrades on schedule and budget, managing labor relations during enterprise agreement negotiations, and recruiting permanent senior operational leadership to replace transitional appointments. The company's formidable infrastructure moat, world-class resource endowment, and fully-

funded development pathway establish a robust foundation, though near-term operational integration risks and elevated transitional costs demand careful monitoring. Greatland's structural position as the Paterson Province's dominant processor creates a natural gravitational pull for regional consolidation, offering substantial long-term optionality beyond the core Telfer-Havieron asset base.
