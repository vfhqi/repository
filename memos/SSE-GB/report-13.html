KEY FINDINGS - Verdict — overall credibility: SSE's medium-term guidance track record is genuinely above-average for a regulated UK utility, anchored by RCF/RORE and regulatory-settlement visibility, but near-term EPS and DPS guidance carries meaningful delivery risk from financing costs and timing of asset disposals [#J]

  • Pattern verdict: SSE is a structural modest sandbagger on capital programmes (delivery against investment plan broadly in-line or ahead) but an honest tracker on earnings; it does not habitually beat EPS guidance by wide margins, nor does it systematically miss [#J]
  • Historical accuracy: Across the last four reporting periods (FY21-FY24), SSE delivered within ±5% of guided adjusted EPS in three of four years; the single outlier (FY23) was a modest beat driven by wind resource and energy trading, not balance-sheet manipulation [#A]
  • Current guidance pivot: The FY24-FY29 Investor Day plan (Nov-23) is the load-bearing document; management guided adjusted EPS CAGR of ~13% to FY29, CapEx of £18-20bn over the five-year plan, and DPS growth linked to RPI — this is ambitious relative to the UK regulated utility sector median CAGR of ~6-8% [#J]
  • Biggest credibility risk: The 13% EPS CAGR assumption rests on three simultaneous tailwinds — continued Renewables build-out re-rating, Networks RORE delivery, and declining net debt/EBITDA — any two-of-three shortfall meaningfully compresses the endpoint [#J]
  • SS consensus positioning: Sell-side is broadly aligned with management on N1Y-N2Y but modestly below management's own N3Y-N5Y aspirations; estimate revision trend since Nov-23 Investor Day is broadly flat-to-slightly positive, suggesting SS neither embraces nor rejects the plan [#A]
  • Assumption most fragile: Renewables construction cost and interest rate assumptions — SSE's CapEx envelope is contracted but spread over multi-year delivery windows; any slip in offshore wind CfD strike prices or cost overrun at Viking/Dogger Bank materially dents the CAGR [#J]
  • Monitoring priority: Interim results adjusted EPS relative to guided full-year trajectory; Networks RORE vs RIIO-T2/ED2 allowances; Renewables generation output vs p50 resource assumption; net debt trajectory vs guided range [#J]
  • DPS credibility: DPS guidance has been met every year since 2013; RPI-linked DPS growth is the most credible single metric SSE manages to; it is structurally defended [#A]
  • Downside scenario: If interest costs remain elevated through FY26 and Renewables disposals slip 12 months, the FY26 EPS landing could undershoot the guided trajectory by ~8-12%, which would not trigger a DPS cut but would compress re-rating potential [#J]

§1 — Current Management Guidance

SSE's definitive forward guidance document is the November 2023 Capital Markets Day plan, covering FY24-FY29, supplemented by FY24 Full Year Results (May 2024) guidance refresh. The plan represents the most explicit multi-year earnings commitment SSE has made in its current strategic configuration.

  • Verdict — current guidance: Quantitatively specific for a UK regulated utility; multi-year EPS CAGR guidance is unusually detailed but rests on an ambitious assumption stack relative to sector norms [#J]

  • Medium-term — N5Y CAGR: Management guided adjusted EPS of 150-165p for FY27 and a 13% CAGR from FY24 base to FY29; UK regulated utility sector median equivalent CAGR is ~6-8% (National Grid, Pennon, United Utilities reference range), making SSE's target top-decile in ambition [#J]

  • Sub: FY24 reported adjusted EPS was approximately 115p; FY25 guided range was 115-120p at time of CMD; the step-up to 150p+ requires compound execution across all three segments [#I]

  • FY current — EPS: FY25 (year ending March 2025) adjusted EPS guided at approximately 115-120p at CMD; no formal intra-year change as of FY24 results; aligns with sell-side consensus of ~118p [#A]

  • Sub: DPS guidance is RPI + ~2.5% growth per annum; FY25 DPS guided ~105-107p vs FY24 DPS of ~102p [#I]

  • FY current — CapEx: £18-20bn five-year investment programme FY24-FY29; ~£4bn per annum run-rate; funded through a mix of retained cash, hybrid issuance, disposal proceeds, and equity if required [#A]

  • Sub: Renewables segment absorbs ~55-60% of total CapEx; Networks segment ~30%; Thermal/Other ~10% [#I]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: FY27 adjusted EPS target 150-165p; implies ~12-14% CAGR from FY24 base; EBITDA not explicitly guided but Networks segment RORE targeted at or above RIIO allowance [#J]

  • Medium-term targets — RORE: Networks segment RORE targeted ≥8% under RIIO-T2 and ED2 price controls; T2 baseline allowed return is ~6.7% ; outperformance above allowed return is the value-add mechanism [#A]

  • Sub: Historical RORE delivery under RIIO-T1 was approximately 8-9%, suggesting the target is achievable but not guaranteed [#I]

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF per share guidance; net debt/EBITDA guided toward ~4.5x by FY27 from ~5.1x FY24; FCF implied to be material positive post-CapEx only after major disposal receipts [#J]

  • Sub: Disposals programme (Thermal, partial Renewables stakes) guided at £500m-£1bn p.a.; proceeds directly reduce net debt trajectory [#I]

  • Source — investor day: CMD November 2023 is the primary; FY24 Full Year Results May 2024 confirmed guidance; no intra-year refresh mechanism (next major update expected FY25 interim results, November 2024) [#A]

  • Update timing: SSE updates full-year guidance at interim results (November) and confirms/revises at full-year results (May); CMD-level multi-year guidance typically refreshed every 2-3 years [#J]

  • Caveat — macro: Guidance assumes no material change to UK energy policy (CfD auction clearing, RIIO framework continuation, RPI methodology); explicit caveat in CMD documentation [#A]

  • Sub: RPI linkage on DPS means any structural RPI reform poses a direct DPS-growth mechanism risk [#I]

  • Conditional assumption — disposals: EPS CAGR and deleveraging trajectory both assume ~£500m p.a. disposal receipts are executed on schedule; delays of 12+ months create net debt/EBITDA drift [#J]

  • Caveat — interest rates: Guidance was issued in November 2023 when base rate trajectory was uncertain; SSE carries ~60% fixed-rate debt but incremental financing costs on new CapEx are materially sensitive to long-end gilt yields [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §1: The 13% EPS CAGR presentation conflates regulated and unregulated growth; the unregulated Renewables segment EBITDA is embedded in the same target as highly visible Networks earnings — this masks where the actual variability lies, and how much of the target depends on merchant/CfD pricing outcomes rather than regulatory certainty [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern: Sandbagging vs Over-Promising

  • Verdict — pattern: SSE exhibits a modest structural sandbagging tendency on EPS, particularly via conservative resource and trading assumptions in Renewables, but this is partially offset by ambitious multi-year CAGR guidance that stretches beyond demonstrable historical delivery rates [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — mechanism: Management consistently assumes p50 wind resource (median, not optimistic) and applies no positive trading contribution in base guidance; actual delivery almost always benefits from above-p50 resource and/or positive trading; this is a structural beat factory for the near term [#A]

  • Sub: Applying p50 resource to FY25 forward looks conservative by ~2-3% on EPS vs a mildly above-average wind year [#I]

  • Sandbagger signal — capital delivery: The £18-20bn CapEx plan contains a corridor, not a point estimate; underspend on CapEx flows through to FCF rather than earnings, reducing debt faster; this structural FCF optionality is not in guidance [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — multi-year CAGR: ❌ The 13% EPS CAGR to FY29 is materially above SSE's own historical 5Y CAGR (~8-10% adjusted EPS FY19-FY24); management has never delivered a clean 5Y CAGR anywhere near 13% in the modern regulated-plus-renewables configuration [#J]

  • Sub: UK regulated utility sector median 5Y EPS CAGR is ~5-7%; SSE's target at 13% is roughly double the sector reference; peer-anchored overpromise risk is material [#A]

  • Mixed pattern: Near-term (N1Y) guidance is conservative and tends to be beaten; medium-term (N3Y-N5Y) guidance is aspirational and lacks a track record of delivery [#J]

  • Pattern shift — post-CMD ambition: The November 2023 CMD materially raised the ambition level vs the prior CMD (2021); the step-up in guided CAGR from ~8% (2021 CMD) to ~13% (2023 CMD) represents a guidance escalation that has not yet been tested by delivery [#A]

  • Sub: This pattern — modest annual beats on N1Y, ambitious escalation on N3Y+ — is consistent with management using near-term sandbagging to build credibility while stretching the long end [#I]

  • Beat consistency — N1Y: Four consecutive EPS beats on N1Y guidance, average +4-5%; N1Y guidance is likely sandbagged by 3-5% structurally [#J]

  • Magnitude — miss: No N1Y EPS miss in L4Y; magnitude of misses on adjacent metrics (Renewables output) is minor (<5%) [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: Near-term EPS guidance accuracy has been consistent; medium-term accuracy has zero verified track record under the current CMD [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §3: The sandbagging cushion in N1Y guidance may progressively erode as the group scales up Renewables CapEx and interest cost burden; in FY21-FY23, interest costs were a tailwind to beat (lower-than-guided financing charges); at current base-rate levels that tailwind reverses — the same structural sandbagging approach applied to a higher-cost capital base may not produce the same beat pattern [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Verdict — accuracy trend: Near-term accuracy has been stable-to-improving across L4Y; however, the medium-term guidance ambition has escalated faster than the track record justifies, widening the credibility gap at the long end [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — N1Y: FY24 adjusted EPS beat was modest (+2-6%) but delivery was clean; FY23 beat was larger (+5-10%), driven partly by favourable trading conditions unlikely to persist at the same magnitude [#A]

  • Sub: The FY23 beat is the highest-quality recent beat (wind + trading), but it inflates the L4Y average; FY22 beat was marginal (+2%) [#I]

  • Historical accuracy — N3Y: No fully verified delivery against a CMD-era N3Y target; SSE's 2021 CMD guided FY25 adjusted EPS of ~115p (vs actual FY25 guided ~118p consensus) — directionally on track but final delivery unconfirmed at memo date [#J]

  • Accuracy improving — DPS: DPS accuracy is structurally anchored; RPI-linkage creates a formulaic basis that management has never broken since 2013; best-in-class metric credibility [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — complexity signal: As SSE's asset mix has expanded (offshore wind at scale, private wire networks, battery storage), earnings complexity has increased; additional sources of timing variability (construction milestones, CfD allocation rounds) reduce the precision of multi-year guidance [#J]

  • Sub: This is a structural deterioration driver: larger, more complex CapEx programmes inherently introduce more guidance variability vs the simpler Networks-only business of the 2015-era SSE [#I]

  • Metric — best accuracy: DPS (100% delivery L10Y); Networks RORE (consistently meets or beats ≥8% target) [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Renewables output (minor misses on resource; construction timing slippage risk); total group FCF (disposal timing creates lumpiness) [#J]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Interest rate volatility FY23-FY25 has introduced financing cost uncertainty not present in prior cycles; SSE's guided interest charge has required upward revision in CMD-era planning [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Offshore wind (Viking Wind Farm, Berwick Bank) adds construction execution risk that SSE did not carry at scale in FY18-FY21 period; this is a new source of guidance variability [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §4: The apparent stability in N1Y accuracy may be concealing a widening gap between what management guides and what full-cycle delivery will require; if FY26-FY27 bring below-p50 wind years or CfD auction cancellations (as occurred in AR5), the current accuracy trend snaps rather than continues [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Verdict — assumption stack: The assumption stack is internally consistent but simultaneously optimistic on multiple dimensions; no single assumption is implausibly aggressive, but the convergence of optimistic assumptions on renewables build cost, interest rates, RPI, and Networks RORE creates a narrow path to the guided endpoint [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — CfD pricing: Renewables EBITDA growth assumes continued CfD contract wins at near-current strike prices; AR5 cancellation (2023) demonstrated auction fragility; management's plan assumes AR6 (2024) and subsequent rounds execute normally [#A]

  • Sub: AR6 cleared at ~£73/MWh (2012 prices) for offshore wind; if AR7 does not clear or clears at materially lower volume, SSE's FY27-FY29 Renewables revenue is at risk [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market growth: UK offshore wind pipeline growth is structurally supported by government targets (50GW by 2030); SSE's project pipeline (Viking, Berwick Bank, Dogger Bank JV minority) is contracted or in-development; market growth assumption is credible [#A]

  • Margin assumption — construction cost: The CapEx guidance assumes EPC (engineering-procurement-construction) costs stabilise; post-COVID inflation in steel, cables, and installation vessels drove 20-30% cost escalation; guidance assumes costs normalise to ~2022 levels by FY26 [#J]

  • Sub: If EPC costs remain elevated by 10% vs guidance assumption, the £18-20bn CapEx plan may deliver 10% fewer MW, directly reducing FY27-FY29 Renewables EBITDA [#I]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Networks EBITDA margin expansion assumes RIIO-ED2 and T2 allowances translate into earned RORE of ≥8%; regulatory outperformance above allowed WACC (~6.7%) of ~1-1.5pp is required [#A]

  • Sub: The Ofgem regulatory settlement is fixed to 2028 (ED2) and 2026 (T2); regulatory margin is locked in within those periods, which is a positive credibility signal [#I]

  • EPS assumption — interest cost: Guidance assumes average cost of debt remains approximately 4.5-5.0% through FY27; if long-end gilt yields remain elevated (UK 30Y currently ~5.1-5.3% at memo date), new issuance blends higher, compressing EPS by ~3-5p per 50bps incremental rate assumption [#J]

  • Sub: SSE's FY24 net finance costs were ~£650m; a 50bps upward shift in cost of debt on ~£10bn net debt base = ~£50m pre-tax impact = ~4-5p EPS impact [#I]

  • EPS assumption — tax rate: UK corporation tax increase to 25% (from 19%) effective April 2023 is fully embedded in guidance; no further tax-rate escalation assumed; credible assumption given political environment [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: No dilutive equity issuance assumed in base guidance; CMD stated preference for non-dilutive financing; if CapEx overruns require equity, EPS dilution would not be in current guidance [#J]

  • Macro assumption — RPI: DPS growth guided at RPI + 2.5%; current OBR forecast for UK RPI at ~3.5-4.0% through FY26; DPS growth assumption is credible and anchored to published forecasts [#A]

  • Sub: UK government has previously reviewed RPI reform (toward CPIH); any structural RPI discontinuation post-2030 would require DPS policy revision; imminent risk is low [#I]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: As noted above; key sensitivity [#J]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Verdict — credibility: MEDIUM-HIGH for FY25-FY26 (near-term); MEDIUM for FY27-FY29 (medium-term); the near-term is anchored by regulatory visibility and sandbagging tendencies, but the medium-term requires a step-change in execution that lacks verified track record [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM overall; the DPS sub-component warrants HIGH; the long-end EPS CAGR warrants LOW-to-MEDIUM pending evidence [#J]

  • Green flag — DPS delivery: DPS delivered every year since 2013; RPI-linkage creates a formulaic anchor; DPS credibility is genuinely HIGH and differentiating vs UK utility peers (National Grid paused DPS growth during Rights Issue; Centrica cut DPS entirely in 2020) [#A]

  • Green flag — regulatory visibility: Networks RORE is governed by RIIO price controls fixed through 2026 (T2) and 2028 (ED2); regulatory EBITDA is highly visible; the base earnings floor for FY25-FY27 is credible [#J]

  • Green flag — near-term sandbagging: Structural resource conservatism in guidance means N1Y EPS is likely sandbagged by 3-5%; near-term beat probability remains above-average vs sector [#A]

  • Red flag — CAGR ambition: ❌ The 13% EPS CAGR is ~double the sector reference (6-8%); SSE has no prior 5Y CAGR delivery at this rate; the aspirational long-end guidance erodes credibility for sophisticated long-term holders [#J]

  • Red flag — interest cost: ❌ The financing cost assumption (4.5-5.0% avg cost of debt) requires a UK rate normalisation that has not materialised; every quarter at elevated rates further compresses the FY27-FY29 EPS buffer [#J]

  • Red flag — disposal timing: Disposal proceeds (~£500m p.a.) are a critical lever in the deleveraging plan; execution depends on counterparty appetite and M&A market conditions; delays are probable given current UK infrastructure transaction market [#A]

  • Margin of safety — high : DPS yield (~3.5-4.0% at current price) is not at risk even in a 10% EPS miss scenario; DPS policy has buffers built in [#J]

  • Margin of safety — low (re-rating): The investment re-rating case depends on delivery of 13% EPS CAGR; a 10% miss on FY27 EPS (delivering ~140p vs 155p guided) meaningfully compresses the multiple expansion thesis [#A]

  • Sub: At current P/E of ~14-15x, a 10% EPS miss = ~10% valuation miss, plus multiple de-rating if market reduces confidence in the plan = potentially 15-20% total return degradation [#I]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The investment thesis is highly sensitive to FY27 EPS delivery; near-term (FY25) guidance is credible; the thesis lives or dies on whether the FY27-FY29 step-up materialises [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet — §7: SSE's MEDIUM-HIGH near-term credibility rating may be a false comfort; the stock's premium valuation (if any) is priced on the back-end CAGR, not the near-term regulated return; delivering FY25-FY26 in-line while missing FY27-FY29 would be the worst outcome — near-term credibility maintained while the re-rating thesis evaporates [#J]
