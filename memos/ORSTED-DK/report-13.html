§1 — Current Management Guidance

Orsted's current financial framework was reset at the November 2023 Capital Markets Update following the US impairment episode, with further refinement at Q4 2023 and Q1-Q2 2024 results. The framework is explicitly "de-risked" vs the pre-2023 vintage — management's own framing.

  • Verdict — current guidance: Nominally de-risked vs 2022 vintage, but still contingent on US project execution timelines and European power price realisations that remain materially uncertain [#J]

  • FY current — EBITDA (2024): DKK 20-23bn guided range for FY2024, subsequently narrowed toward the lower end in H1 2024 commentary; compares to DKK 20.4bn reported in FY2023 on restated basis [#A]

  • Sub: guidance implies roughly flat-to-modest growth, a sharp step-down from the pre-impairment trajectory of DKK 30bn+ that was in the 2025 framework as recently as 2022

  • Medium-term — EBITDA N3Y: DKK 23-26bn for 2025-2026 per the Nov 2023 CMD framework; management characterised this as achievable from committed, under-construction projects without reliance on new US offshore awards [#A]

  • Sub: DKK 23-26bn range implies 13-27% cumulative growth from 2024 base; plausible if European offshore and onshore pipelines execute on time

  • Medium-term — CapEx: DKK ~DKK 100bn gross 2024-2030, net of farm-downs and partner contributions approximately DKK 60-70bn; management flagged capital discipline as a priority post-2023 impairment [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx guidance represents a ~25-30% reduction vs the pre-2023 CMD framework that had assumed heavier US build-out

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF guidance provided; management guides to EBITDA + CapEx ranges and implies FCF via dividend policy; FCF-to-EBITDA conversion historically compressed by construction spend [#A]

  • FY current — DPS: DKK 9.00/share for FY2024, reflecting a ~14% cut from pre-impairment levels; management targets 5-8% annual DPS CAGR from 2024 base [#A]

  • Sub: the 2024 DPS level follows the December 2023 cut from DKK 10.50; cover is thin at current EBITDA guidance midpoint

  • Source — investor day: November 2023 CMD (post-impairment reset); supplemented by Q4 2023, Q1 2024, H1 2024 results presentations [#A]

  • Update timing: Most recent guidance update was H1 2024 results (August 2024); no material changes to FY2024 or medium-term ranges at that point, consistent with management signalling stabilisation [#A]

  • Caveat — power prices: Management guidance explicitly assumes "normalised" European power prices; no explicit spot/forward curve assumption disclosed; creates asymmetric downside if Nordic/UK power prices remain depressed [#A]

  • Caveat — US execution: Framework excludes any new US offshore awards; relies on existing commitments (Sunrise Wind, South Fork, Revolution Wind, Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind [CVOW] partnership) proceeding on schedule [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — farm-down proceeds: DKK 10-15bn of asset rotation / farm-down proceeds assumed in 2024-2026 period; failure to execute reduces net CapEx coverage and tightens FCF [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that "de-risked" guidance still embeds four contingent assumptions simultaneously — European power price stabilisation, US project execution on time, farm-down proceeds crystallisation, and no further regulatory/BOEM cost inflation — any one of which has already individually triggered a guidance reset in the L36 months; concurrent stress is not modelled [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (sandbagging vs over-promising)

  • Verdict — pattern: Chronic over-promiser with deteriorating pattern; no evidence of sandbagging discipline; management has not once guided conservatively low in L4 periods to create a beat cushion [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal: CMD-vintage guidance routinely set at aspirational mid-cycle power price + optimistic project timeline assumptions; the 2022 CMD EBITDA DKK 30bn+ target for 2025 was never achievable on the actual cost curve that materialised [#A]

  • Sub: the pattern is consistent with management teams of capital-intensive energy developers globally; regulatory/political pressure to show ambition incentivises aggressive CMD targets

  • Miss consistency: 3 of last 4 measured periods produced negative surprises on at least one major metric vs CMD-vintage guidance; even the FY2022 "in-line" concealed early US cost-escalation signals that surfaced within 12 months [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: FY2023 EBITDA miss of DKK 2.6-5.6bn vs CMD guidance represents 11-22% shortfall; in the context of a DKK 145bn (peak) market cap company, this is a peer-bottom-quintile guidance event [#A]

  • Pattern shift: Post-2023 management language has shifted markedly toward "capital discipline," "de-risked pipeline," and "committed-only projects"; this is a vocabulary change, not yet a demonstrated delivery record under the new regime [#A]

  • Sub: language shift is a necessary but not sufficient condition for credibility restoration; needs 4-6 quarters of in-line delivery to shift the observable pattern

  • Sandbagger signal: Absent; Orsted has not exhibited the conservative-guide/beat behaviour of, for example, European integrated energy majors (Shell, TotalEnergies) where CMD targets are routinely set at P90 not P50 assumptions [#A]

  • Mixed pattern note: FY2024 is the first test of the new regime; if H2 2024 EBITDA tracks in-line or above the DKK 20-23bn range, it would represent the first clean beat in 3 years; early signals from H1 2024 results are equivocal [#A]

  • Improving accuracy — conditional: Only improvable if the new CMD framework genuinely excludes speculative US new-build; the structural source of over-promising (US development optionality priced into guidance) must be absent for the pattern to shift [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that the post-2023 "conservatism" may reflect the absence of a large new addressable market (US offshore contracted pipeline is exhausted for now) rather than a genuine change in guidance culture; if Orsted re-enters aggressive US bidding or European floating offshore, the same over-promise mechanism reactivates [#J]

  • Margin assumption — cost: O&M cost assumptions embed ~3-5% annual efficiency improvement per management; achievable for mature assets (Anholt, Borssele) but less evidenced for newer offshore assets where warranty periods are ending [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage positive as new capacity comes online (Revolution Wind, CVOW); but leverage is one-sided — any delay in commissioning removes the revenue without removing the fixed cost base [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed ~22-25%; largely anchored to Danish corporate tax (22%); stable assumption; low variance risk vs peers [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count stable; no material buyback programme in guidance; dilution risk from potential equity raise if FCF deteriorates is not in base case [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Leverage is elevated post-2023; net debt/EBITDA approximately 4.0-4.5x; guidance embeds a path to 3.5x by 2026 via EBITDA growth + farm-down proceeds; if either leg slips, leverage stays elevated and refinancing risk increases [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: USD/DKK exposure is material for US project valuations and revenue; management hedges project construction costs but commodity exposure is partly unhedged; USD weakening would be EBITDA-negative for US segment [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Higher-for-longer rate environment increases WACC for uncontracted development projects and farm-down valuation; DKK 10-15bn farm-down proceeds assumption is IRR-sensitive at >5% discount rates [#A]

  • Farm-down assumption — execution: DKK 10-15bn cumulative 2024-2026 farm-down proceeds is a critical free cash flow assumption; precedent (2023: limited execution) raises execution risk; buyer universe for large offshore wind stakes has narrowed [#A]
