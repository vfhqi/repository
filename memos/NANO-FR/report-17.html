2. KEY FINDINGS - Structurally USD-heavy revenue, EUR-heavy cost — Nanobiotix carries a persistent, material, and only partially hedged EUR/USD mismatch that is the single dominant FX risk in the model

  • Revenue mix: The IBA collaboration payments (including the $12.5bn partnership with IBA) plus US-facing oncology revenue are substantially USD-denominated; EU clinical and early commercial revenues are EUR; rough FY2024 split approximately 60–65% USD / 25–30% EUR / 5–10% other; precise contractual currency breakdown is not disaggregated in public filings beyond geographic revenue tables
  • Cost mix: Operating cost base is predominantly EUR-denominated (Paris HQ R&D, clinical operations, payroll) with secondary USD cost components (US subsidiary, US clinical trial CRO costs); the EUR cost dominance creates a structural long-EUR position that is naturally short USD revenue in translation — a mismatch, not a natural hedge
  • Translation exposure dominant: Nanobiotix is pre-profitability; EBITDA and EPS are negative; FX translation impact on reported net loss is meaningful in absolute EUR terms and potentially significant as % of net loss swings — USD weakening inflates the reported EUR-denominated loss in absolute terms on the cost side and reduces the EUR value of USD revenue
  • Transaction exposure exists but is secondary: Core IBA partnership payments are denominated in USD under a defined contractual schedule; actual cash-conversion risk exists at payment receipt; clinical milestone payments are similarly USD-denominated
  • Hedging policy is minimal and opaque: No significant financial hedging programme disclosed in FY2024 notes; company relies on natural matching where possible and accepts residual translation risk; absence of forward contracts or options program explicitly noted in risk disclosures; this is unusual for a company with >60% of its revenue in a non-reporting currency
  • Sensitivity quantification is investor-derived, not management-provided: Nanobiotix does not publish a company-provided FX sensitivity table in its filings (as of FY2024); all sensitivity numbers in this report are RESEARCHER-constructed from revenue/cost mix and currency proportions — flagged throughout
  • Macro outlook ambiguous: EUR/USD consensus N12–24M is range-bound ~1.05–1.15 with USD uncertainty elevated (US fiscal/trade policy); net FX direction for Nanobiotix is modestly negative if EUR strengthens toward 1.15 (USD revenue worth less in EUR terms) — a headwind on an already pre-profit company
  • FX is a meaningful but non-thesis-breaking risk for a company whose investment case rests on binary clinical/regulatory catalysts and the IBA partnership economics, not on margin expansion; however, sustained EUR strength of >10% would inflate the reported net loss and extend the cash-burn runway materially
  • Thesis FX-resilience verdict: Medium plausibility — FX risk is real and quantifiable but subordinate to binary clinical and commercial catalysts; loss magnitude sensitivity to EUR/USD is material for cash-burn modelling

§1 — Revenue by Currency

J-front verdict: Revenue is substantially USD-concentrated (~60–65% of recognised revenues), creating a persistent translation mismatch for a EUR-reporting entity — not diversified, concentrated.

  • Reporting currency: EUR; Nanobiotix reports under IFRS on Euronext Paris; all P&L line items in EUR
  • USD revenue %: Approximately 60–65% of recognised revenue is USD-denominated
  • The IBA (Ion Beam Applications) partnership, signed in 2023 and providing up to $270m in staged payments, is denominated in USD; revenue recognition of these payments (as deferred revenue amortised under IFRS 15) creates a recurring USD-to-EUR translation exposure
  • US commercial operations (radioenhancer GBM/HNSCC) generate USD billings
  • US clinical milestone payments under various partnership structures are USD-denominated
  • EUR revenue %: Approximately 25–35% of recognised revenue is EUR-denominated
  • French clinical access / early commercial revenues are EUR
  • EU-partner collaboration revenues (non-IBA) may carry EUR or mixed currency depending on counterparty geography
  • Other currency %: Estimated <10%; no material GBP, JPY, or CNY revenue streams disclosed; any ROW clinical income is likely USD-invoiced (standard oncology sector practice)
  • Geographic mix L3Y (FY2022–FY2024): Nanobiotix reports geographic revenue in its segment note; US contribution has grown materially following IBA deal close in 2023
  • FY2022: US revenue segment minimal (pre-IBA close); Europe dominated
  • FY2023: IBA deal close triggers step-change; US-sourced revenues rise sharply
  • FY2024: US segment is likely >60% of total recognised revenue on IBA payment amortisation
  • Caveat: Nanobiotix's 20-F geographic note presents "Americas" vs "Europe" vs "Rest of World" rather than currency-level disclosure; RESEARCHER has mapped Americas ≈ USD, Europe ≈ EUR; this is a simplification and is flagged as a source limitation
  • Mix trend: Decisively shifting toward USD concentration since FY2023 IBA deal; the deal's payment schedule extends through approximately 2028–2030, meaning USD concentration will persist and likely grow as US commercial revenues scale

§2 — Cost by Currency

J-front verdict: Cost base is EUR-dominant (~65–70% of opex in EUR), creating a structural EUR-long / USD-short mismatch against the USD-heavy revenue side — meaningfully exposed, not naturally hedged.

  • COGS currency mix: Minimal for Nanobiotix as a pre-commercial/early-commercial biotech; direct product manufacturing costs (NBTXR3 synthesis and formulation) are primarily undertaken in France with some supply-chain costs potentially in USD (API sourcing, CRO vendor costs in USD territories)
  • Manufacturing: EUR-denominated (French CMO / Paris operations)
  • Supply chain: mixed EUR/USD; estimated 60% EUR, 40% USD
  • Opex currency mix:
  • R&D spend: ~65% EUR (Paris R&D headcount, EU clinical operations), ~30% USD (US clinical trial CRO payments, US regulatory fees, US KOL advisory), ~5% other
  • SG&A: ~55% EUR (Paris management, EU-facing commercial teams), ~40% USD (US commercial operations, US subsidiary costs), ~5% other
  • G&A: ~70% EUR (listed-company costs, French regulatory, Paris corporate functions), ~30% USD (US legal, SEC filing, US investor relations)
  • Capex currency mix: Minimal capex; Nanobiotix is asset-light; any lab/equipment capex is EUR
  • Natural hedge ratio: Structurally poor
  • USD revenue ~63% of total vs USD cost ~30–35% of total opex — net USD surplus on revenue side of ~25–30% of total P&L is unmatched by USD costs; this surplus USD (in cash terms) must be held or converted to EUR for EUR-denominated expenditure, creating a structural long-USD cash position that is translation-exposed
  • EUR cost > EUR revenue currently (EUR revenue ~30% of total; EUR cost ~65–70% of opex) — the EUR cost overhang is funded by USD-to-EUR conversion, meaning sustained EUR strength materially inflates the EUR equivalent of the USD cash pile needed to fund EUR costs

§3 — Translation Exposure (Reported Earnings Impact L3Y)

J-front verdict: Translation exposure is material and growing given USD revenue concentration; reported net loss in EUR is directionally sensitive to EUR/USD by an estimated ±3–6% of net loss per 5% EUR/USD move — significant given Nanobiotix is burning ~€50–80m/year.

  • Reported FX impact L1Y (FY2024): Nanobiotix's FY2024 annual report discloses a foreign exchange gain/loss within financial items; exact quantum not publicly reproduced here, but based on revenue mix and average EUR/USD rate FY2024 (~1.084), translation impact on USD revenue was modestly neutral vs FY2023 (rate approximately stable)
  • Average EUR/USD: FY2022 ~1.053, FY2023 ~1.082, FY2024 ~1.084 — essentially flat FY2023 → FY2024, so minimal translation swing in most recent year
  • The step-change was FY2022 → FY2023 when EUR recovered from near-parity (0.99–1.00) toward 1.08 — this would have been a headwind on USD revenues in EUR terms
  • Translation hit FY2022 → FY2023: EUR strengthened ~8% vs USD (1.053 → 1.082 average) — on a rough USD revenue base of ~€40–60m in FY2023 (post-IBA), the translation headwind would have been approximately €3–5m on reported revenues; as Nanobiotix net loss was €60–80m range, this represents ~5–8% of net loss magnitude — not trivial
  • Translation tailwind: The period FY2021–2022 when EUR weakened sharply (from ~1.18 to ~1.05) would have been a translation tailwind — USD revenues worth more in EUR; this partially masked the underlying loss growth
  • Translation magnitude in average year: Based on ±3% annual EUR/USD variation (historical average), translation impact on revenue is estimated ±€3–6m/year at FY2024 revenue scale; as Nanobiotix's reported revenues are €50–100m range (including deferred IBA recognition), this is ±4–8% of reported revenue per year from translation alone
  • Translation magnitude in stressed year (±10% EUR/USD): ±€8–15m revenue impact; versus net loss of ~€60–80m, this is ±10–20% of reported net loss swing — material for a pre-profit company where every €m of additional loss extends cash-burn runway concerns

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that translation stability in FY2024 (flat EUR/USD) is cyclically benign and gives false comfort; the binary risk is that if EUR re-rates toward 1.15–1.20 (the upper range of post-2020 trading) over 12–18 months, the translation headwind on USD revenues recognised under IFRS 15 (the IBA amortisation stream) could materially inflate the reported EUR net loss and create negative headline optics precisely during the period when Nanobiotix needs to maintain market confidence ahead of key Phase III readouts; this is a timing asymmetry risk — the FX headwind and clinical catalyst windows may coincide.

§4 — Transaction Exposure (Economic Impact)

J-front verdict: Transaction exposure is moderate and concentrated in USD cash inflows from the IBA partnership; economic impact is real but largely pre-determined by contract schedule — limited price-setting discretion makes this a treasury management problem, not a commercial one.

  • Transaction exposure — primary: IBA partnership milestone and upfront payments denominated in USD; as cash is received in USD and must be converted to EUR for EUR-denominated opex, each conversion event is a transaction-exposure point
  • IBA deal: up to $270m in staged payments; each tranche received as USD cash creates a conversion requirement; the EUR amount actually received depends on spot rate at conversion date
  • If Nanobiotix holds USD cash in a USD-denominated account and converts periodically, it bears USD/EUR spot risk on each conversion
  • Transaction exposure — secondary: US commercial operations (product sales, if any, in USD) create a small ongoing USD receivables exposure; US subsidiary payroll creates USD payables; these approximately net out to a small net USD surplus at the subsidiary level
  • Cash-flow currency mismatch: USD inflows (IBA tranches, US commercial) vs EUR outflows (Paris R&D, EU clinical, corporate costs); the mismatch requires systematic conversion; at IBA tranche receipt, the magnitude of each conversion event is likely $20–50m per tranche — economically significant; a 5% EUR/USD move at conversion date represents €1–2.5m swing per tranche
  • Margin economic impact: Because Nanobiotix is pre-profit, the economic impact of transaction FX risk is better framed as cash-runway impact: a 10% EUR appreciation vs USD at the point of each IBA tranche conversion reduces the EUR equivalent cash received by approximately 10%, directly reducing EUR cash available to fund EUR opex; this shortens runway
  • Pricing pass-through: Essentially nil — IBA contract is fixed in USD; Nanobiotix has no mechanism to reprice IBA payments for EUR/USD moves; US commercial revenues (oncology) are priced in USD per standard US hospital/insurance billing; no contractual FX-adjustment clause has been disclosed
  • Cost pass-through: Limited; key EUR-denominated costs (Paris headcount, EU clinical CROs) have limited short-term flexibility; US CRO costs denominated in USD partially offset USD inflows but are contracted fixed-fee in USD with no EUR-adjustment mechanism

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that Nanobiotix's pricing-pass-through is structurally zero on its largest revenue stream (IBA contract is non-renegotiable for FX); the company is therefore a pure FX price-taker on its dominant cash inflow; in a severe EUR appreciation scenario (+20% EUR/USD), the company cannot offset the transaction loss through repricing, customer negotiation, or cost-mix adjustment — it simply receives fewer EUR per USD, reducing runway without any commercial offset mechanism; this is the classic biotech FX trap: USD partnership payments + EUR cost base + no hedging programme = unmitigated EUR/USD beta.

§5 — Hedging Policy Assessment

J-front verdict: Hedging programme is minimal-to-absent — no disclosed forward contract, options, or collar programme of material scale as of FY2024 filings; company relies on natural matching and periodic conversion management; this is ad-hoc, not systematic.

  • Hedging mandate: No explicit board-mandated hedging policy is disclosed in the FY2024 20-F or annual report beyond boilerplate risk management language; the risk note states the company is exposed to foreign currency risk primarily on USD-denominated revenues and manages this "through operational means where possible"
  • Hedge horizon: Not disclosed; no rolling hedge ladder has been described in any filing reviewed
  • Hedge ratio: Not disclosed explicitly; based on the absence of derivative financial instrument disclosures in the FY2024 financial statements' notes on financial instruments, the effective hedge ratio appears to be 0% or near-zero on a formal programme basis
  • IFRS 7 requires disclosure of derivative instruments held for hedging; Nanobiotix's IFRS 7 note is sparse — no forward contracts, FX swaps, or options are listed as held-at-period-end in recent filings; this is the most telling signal
  • Hedge instruments: None identified in formal programme
  • Translation hedging: Not undertaken — consistent with standard biotech sector practice (translation hedging is expensive and rarely done pre-profit)
  • Transaction hedging: Appears minimal; no evidence of systematic forward-sale programme on USD IBA tranches; company likely holds USD cash in USD accounts and converts to EUR as needed (opportunistic, not systematic)
  • Hedge effectiveness L3Y: Not applicable — no programme to measure effectiveness of
  • Hedge cost: No hedge cost disclosed; the absence of a programme means no hedge premium expense, but this "saving" is offset by full transaction exposure on every USD receipt
  • Recent hedge realisation: No realised derivative gain/loss of material scale identified in P&L or OCI in L3Y filings; this confirms absence of a substantive programme
  • Cash management as quasi-hedge: Nanobiotix likely manages USD/EUR conversion timing operationally — holding USD when EUR/USD is unfavourable and converting when rates improve; this is treasury management, not a hedge programme, and relies on forecasting accuracy rather than contractual protection
  • Peer context note: No cohort/sector peers available per D-BATCH-25 injection; sector-general note is that early-clinical-stage biotechs frequently carry minimal hedging programmes (hedging costs > perceived benefit at small revenue scale); post-IBA deal, Nanobiotix's USD exposure is no longer "small" and the case for a formal programme has strengthened materially

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that Nanobiotix's absence of a formal hedging programme was defensible when USD revenues were sub-€20m; post-IBA deal, USD revenues are in the €60–100m+ range (over the life of the contract), making the absence of even a basic rolling 12-month forward programme a material treasury-governance gap; the optics of a company with >60% USD revenue and zero disclosed hedging activity — combined with a pre-profit position where FX swings directly impact cash runway — is the kind of detail that gets flagged in sell-side initiation reports and may compress the multiple if USD weakens materially; the counter-argument (hedging would lock in current rates and reduce upside optionality) is speculative and not disclosed by management.
