Section A — Company and financial snapshot

(Carried forward from Triaging memo — substantively unchanged. Updated with Q4 FY2025/26 data where available post-results.)

Elekta AB (publ.) is a Swedish medtech company manufacturing radiation therapy and radiosurgery equipment for oncology and brain disorders. Founded 1972 by Lars Leksell. Listed on Nasdaq Stockholm. Dual-class share structure: A-shares (1 vote) and B-shares (1/10 vote). Laurent Leksell (son of founder) holds 30.24% of voting rights from 1.85% of economic capital via A-share dominance.

Core product categories:

  • Linear accelerators (Versa HD; Elekta Evo — FDA-cleared January 2026): the main revenue driver (~57% of net sales as Solutions); external beam radiation therapy
  • Unity MRI-linac: high-field magnetic resonance imaging guided linear accelerator; ~150 installed globally; Elekta's key technological differentiator but commercially narrow
  • Leksell Gamma Knife: stereotactic radiosurgery; Elekta holds ~97% market share in this niche
  • Brachytherapy: ~65% global market share
  • Elekta ONE software suite (Monaco treatment planning + MOSAIQ oncology information system + RayCare): ~20% of total business; recurring revenue base; SaaS now 25% of new OIS orders

Financial snapshot (FY2024/25, ending April 2025):

  • Net sales: SEK 18,016M
  • Adj. EBIT margin: 11.6% (FY2024/25 full year); Q3 FY2025/26: ~11.9%
  • Adj. gross margin: 37.8% (FY2024/25); Q3 FY2025/26: 38.3%; Q4 FY2024/25: 40.3% five-year high
  • FCF: ~SEK 1.25bn FY2024/25; FCF conversion ~80%; improving YTD FY2025/26 (+SEK 443M YTD)
  • Net debt: ~SEK 3.8bn; Net debt/EBITDA: ~1.35x
  • Dividend: SEK 2.40 per share
  • Order book: SEK 34.15bn post two write-down rounds totalling SEK 7.1bn
  • Q3 FY2025/26 book-to-bill: 1.17; group gross orders +3% CC

Competitive context: Global radiation therapy duopoly. Siemens Healthineers (via 2021 Varian acquisition) holds ~55-60% linac market share globally; ~65-70% in North America. Full brand unification completed August 2025. ARPA-H $83M FLASH award to Siemens May 2026. Elekta holds ~42% globally, falling to ~25-30% in North America. UIH (United Imaging Healthcare) overseas revenue growing 42% YoY with established commercial presence in UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain.

[B] Change force 1 — oART secular shift and oncology demand: deepened confirmation at solid pass

  • The WHO structural demand driver (1 in 5 individuals will develop cancer; RT used in ~50% of patients) is confirmed as secular, not cyclical. Key deepening: the oART clinical shift is specifically Elekta-relevant in a way that the Triaging analysis established but did not fully stress-test. The AlphaSense Sector Primer confirms that online adaptive radiotherapy — where AI re-optimises the treatment plan while the patient is on the treatment table — is the primary upgrade driver for hospital procurement of next-generation linacs. Both the Evo (CT-linac, AI imaging) and Unity (MRI-linac) sit on this clinical migration, making Elekta's product roadmap structurally aligned with the direction of the market. This is not a surface-level thematic fit; it is a product-cycle alignment.
  • The global radiation therapy market growth rate ($13.3bn 2024, projected $19.1bn 2029 at 6.8% CAGR) is underpinned by the oART shift in the premium sub-segments where Elekta competes. MRI-guided RT growing at 18.1% CAGR; AI-enabled adaptive planning at double-digit growth. Elekta's hardware and software roadmap is pointing into the growth segments, not the commodity volume segments.
  • The contra-evidence that must be held alongside this positive: Varian's Ethos CT-guided adaptive system reached 100 orders in 18 months vs Unity's ~36 months to the same milestone. The oART trend is real, but Siemens/Varian is executing on it more commercially successfully than Elekta at this point. The change force is structural; the competitive execution of the change force is Elekta's weakness, not its strength.
  • The hospital procurement cycle (10-12 years for linac replacement) creates a staggered, lumpy demand release pattern. When the next generation of oART linacs enters the replacement cycle seriously (now, via Evo and Ethos), it hits simultaneously across the installed base that was procured in the 2012-2015 wave. Elekta's ~7,500-unit installed base means approximately 30-40% is entering the replacement-eligible window in the 2024-2028 period. This is a demand wave, not a steady trickle.
  • Downside qualifier on change force 1: the oART premium segment (Unity, Evo) is the market share battleground. If Elekta loses the adaptive RT software narrative to Siemens/Varian's more integrated Ethos/ARIA ecosystem, the structural demand growth accrues to Siemens, not Elekta. Growth in the market does not automatically accrue to #2.
  • Hospital procurement committee structure is also deepened at ESA: US hospital decisions on $3M CT-linacs involve the CFO, CMO, Radiation Oncology Department Head, and frequently a Group Purchase Organisation (GPO). This is a 12-36 month procurement cycle, fundamentally different from the 6-12 month B2B software sales cycle that Just-Bomholt managed at 3Shape. The CEO learning curve risk is highest in the US commercial sales process.
  • The EMEA market procurement cycle is characterised by centralised national tenders or regional NHS/sickness-fund frameworks that can be 18-36 months from framework to delivery. The Polish tender activity (cited as a positive by Jefferies) is real but volatile — tender win does not mean fast revenue recognition. EMEA procurement is a lumpy, long-duration, budget-sensitive process.
  • Confirmation at ESA: the oART secular shift is the highest-conviction change force. It is not market-specific and it is not dependent on Elekta management execution. The risk is Elekta's execution on it, not the change force itself. ESA rating on this sub-force: [B], maintained.

[D] Headwind 1 — FX structural mismatch: deepened confirmation, quantitative precision added

  • SEK reporting base, USD (~21%) + EUR (~27%) + CNY (~15%) + JPY (~4%) revenue concentration, GBP-heavy cost base (~20% of COGS from Crawley manufacturing). Structural short-revenue-SEK, long-cost-GBP/EUR position.
  • ESA-level quantitative precision: Q3 FY2025/26 FX gross margin drag = 130bps in a single quarter. Tariff gross margin drag = 100bps in the same quarter. Combined 230bps external headwind in Q3 alone — equivalent to approximately SEK 200M of gross profit in one quarter. At that run-rate, a full year of combined FX + tariff headwind exceeds SEK 700M gross profit impact, nearly equal to the entire SEK 500M cost savings programme.
  • The Jefferies research (Q10/Short Sellers) notes that Elekta's tariff exposure is increasing as North American sales grow. Hardware manufacturing remains heavily reliant on UK, Netherlands, and Sweden — all non-US jurisdictions subject to US tariff surcharges on hardware shipped to US customers. Every incremental US Evo order exacerbates the tariff drag absent a US manufacturing decision, which is a multi-year strategic commitment the current management has not made.
  • The USD/SEK structural dynamic: Elekta's cost base does not shrink when USD weakens. Revenue translates down; costs stay fixed in SEK terms. The asymmetry is structurally hostile. The bears correctly note that FX is frequently cited by management as "transitory" or "exogenous" — but a structural short-revenue-strong-currency position with manufacturing in a structurally stronger currency (GBP, EUR) is not transitory. It is architectural.
  • [D] maintained at ESA. The FX headwind is a load-bearing disqualifying concern, not a marginal adjustment.

[D] Headwind 2 — R&D amortisation headwind: deepened confirmation, mechanically material

  • The R&D capitalisation-to-amortisation mechanics are confirmed at ESA solid depth. Elekta historically capitalised up to 60% of annual R&D spend (~SEK 1.2-1.4bn annually). New management is intentionally reducing the capitalisation rate toward ~40% to improve earnings quality.
  • Q3 FY2025/26: the combination of lower capitalisation and higher amortisation on recently launched products (including the Evo and Unity software platforms) created a 150bps drag on reported EBIT margin in a single quarter. DNB Carnegie (Q9/Earnings History) confirms: "underlying earnings quality is steadily improving, but the R&D drag is mechanically weighing on reported EBIT." Jefferies confirms: "aligning capitalisation with amortisation effectively reduces reliance on capitalised R&D."
  • The SEK 1,064M non-cash R&D impairment in FY2024/25 (abandoned cloud software platform) was the first visible evidence of this structural accounting normalisation. The impairment accelerated the amortisation schedule on the abandoned projects, adding to the reported EBIT drag without any change in underlying operating performance.
  • The 14% EBIT target therefore requires clearing not just the operational improvement bar but also the R&D accounting normalisation headwind — roughly 150bps. The "real" operational target to achieve 14% reported EBIT is closer to 14% + 150bps accounting drag + 230bps FX/tariff overlay = approximately 300bps of genuine operational improvement required above the current Q3 FY2025/26 11.9% level. This is achievable if the SEK 500M savings fully land (290bps) — but it requires no further FX deterioration and no revenue shortfall. The margin for error is thin.
  • [D] maintained at ESA.

[D] Headwind 3 — Backlog quality: deepened confirmation, pattern risk now fully evidenced

  • The order book write-down pattern (SEK 4.9bn Q1 FY2025/26 + SEK 2.2bn Q2 FY2025/26 = SEK 7.1bn total) is confirmed as a pattern, not a one-time event, by the ESA and DD research. The pre-mortem (Q19) assigns ~70% probability to a third material write-down over the next 12-18 months. The backlog conversion rate (66% FY2016 → low 40% range current) has not recovered; there is no confirmed mechanism for this to reverse with the cleaner post-write-down backlog.
  • ESA deepening from Short Sellers (Q10): the accounting structure of Elekta's revenue recognition (percentage-of-completion on long-cycle contracts) means that percentage-of-completion revenue may have been pulled forward before the underlying contract risk was recognised. This is not an outright fraud allegation — PwC audits with clean opinions. But it raises the question of whether the revenue recognised in FY2023/24 and FY2024/25 on now-cancelled orders was booked appropriately at the time or slightly optimistically.
