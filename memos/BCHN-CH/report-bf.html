EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Burckhardt Compression operates a high-moat, vertically integrated reciprocating compressor business spanning initial systems manufacturing and decades-long aftermarket servicing, capturing a 30% to 35% global market share within a consolidated four-player oligopoly. The company demonstrates exceptional capital productivity , achieving a 40.40% Return on Net Operating Assets in FY2025 versus peer medians in the midteens, driven by customer-funded working capital and asset-light capacity expansion . Financial delivery remains robust, with FY2025 sales reaching CHF 1,057.10m and net income exceeding CHF 110.10m , supported by Services Division margins of 24.40% that structurally outperform the lower-margin Systems Division at 10.70%. However, near-term order intake volatility presents material headwinds, with H1 2025 orders collapsing 34.85% year-on-year due to US tariff uncertainties and deferred Chinese petrochemical investments, while Systems orders alone declined 45.75% . Structural vulnerabilities include extreme geographic concentration in China representing 40.85% of sales, rising payment delays with over-90-day receivables climbing to 36.40% of the net book, and the risk of localized Chinese competitor substitution eroding both new-build Systems backlog and highmargin aftermarket services penetration. The business model leverages formidable customer switching costs anchored by mission-critical downtime penalties of €1-2m per day, proprietary digital monitoring platforms that cement multi-decade relationships, and highly recurring service contracts covering equipment lifespans of 20 to 50 years. Burckhardt's competitive positioning benefits from extreme engineering barriers to entry requiring one-to-three-year project evaluations,

concentration creates a plausible scenario where Burckhardt's exceptional return profile deteriorates rapidly if sovereign-backed buyers extend payment terms indefinitely while local rivals simultaneously capture service market share.
