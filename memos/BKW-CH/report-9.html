§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Mixed quality — grid segment earnings are high-quality (regulated, cash-generative, predictable); energy supply and solutions segments introduce one-offs, revaluations, and acquisition noise that make reported EPS a poor proxy for recurring earning power without adjustment [#J]

Recurring vs one-off: FY-2022 energy windfall (~CHF 2.5-3.5 EPS equivalent) was management's own "exceptional year" disclosure — but the company did not formally adjust for this in its "adjusted" EPS definition, which itself warrants scrutiny [#A]

  • One-off impact FY-2023: Net disposal gain ~CHF 40m (+~CHF 2.50 EPS equivalent) offset by restructuring ~CHF 15m; net ~+CHF 1.50 EPS one-off benefit to reported [#A]
  • One-off impact FY-2024: Acquisition costs ~CHF 12m (-~CHF 0.75 EPS) offset by pension credit ~CHF 18m (+~CHF 1.10); net approximately neutral to slightly positive [#A]

Adjustments trend: ❌ The company's adjusted EPS definition is not consistently applied period-to-period — FY-2022 energy windfall was not stripped from "adjusted" figures, while FY-2023 disposal gain was partially adjusted; adjustment methodology lacks published policy document [#A]

  • Non-GAAP gap: The gap between reported and adjusted EPS has ranged from <1% to >15% depending on period — unusually wide range for a regulated utility where European peers (Terna, Red Eléctrica, Swissgrid comparables) typically show <5% gap [#A]

Cash conversion: Grid segment OCF conversion estimated at ~90-95% of grid EBIT — strong, consistent with regulated utility norms; group-level OCF conversion is lower (~70-80%) due to working capital volatility in energy supply [#A]

  • OCF vs NI: Group OCF/Net Income ratio estimated ~1.1-1.3x over L3Y — above 1x, indicating non-cash charges (D&A) exceed non-cash income, a net positive quality signal [#A]

NWC tailwind: Energy supply working capital was a significant tailwind in FY-2023 as commodity collateral requirements fell; this benefit was partially reversed in FY-2024 as solutions segment build-out consumed NWC [#A]

  • Receivables turn: Not materially deteriorating based on available disclosures; grid segment receivables are by definition short-duration (regulated tariff billing) [#I]

D&A trend: D&A increasing moderately (~3-5% annually) reflecting grid infrastructure investment programme; this is structurally normal for regulated networks and does not signal aggressive accounting [#I]

Conservative accounting signal: BKW uses IFRS; grid assets are carried at cost less depreciation (not fair value); no mark-to-market flattery on fixed assets [#I]

Bearish read on quality: What's the bearish read? Through what mechanism might margin growth be flattered by recurring items dressed as one-off? BKW's energy supply revaluation gains on commodity positions could recur if power markets stay volatile — management could repeatedly book these as "exceptional" while benefiting from them in adjusted figures selectively; the absence of a published adjustment policy is the key vulnerability [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: EBITDA and EBIT margins are structurally stable at the group level, but FY-2022 peak margins were energy-windfall-inflated; the post-normalisation margin (~20% EBITDA margin) is sustainable but modest relative to pure regulated-grid peers that typically run 35-45% EBITDA margins [#J]

Revenue CAGR vs profit CAGR: Revenue CAGR FY-2022 to FY-2024 approximately -8%; EBITDA CAGR approximately -10% — slight negative operating leverage on the way down, consistent with energy supply fixed-cost base partially exposed to lower revenues [#A]

  • Normalised base: Revenue CAGR from a normalised FY-2022 (~CHF 3,200m equivalent) to FY-2025E (~CHF 3,450m) implies ~+2-3% CAGR — modest but positive [#A]
  • EBITDA CAGR normalised: Similarly, normalised EBITDA CAGR ~+2-4% — inline with low-growth regulated utility norms [#A]

Gross margin: Gross margin stable at ~29-31% L3Y — no deterioration; energy supply margin decline offset by solutions segment gross margin improvement [#A]

EBITDA margin: Group EBITDA margin compressed from ~21% (FY-2022) to ~20% (FY-2024/2025E) — minimal compression once FY-2022 windfall is contextualised; structurally, the ~20% margin is the steady-state [#A]

  • Grid segment margin: Regulated grid EBITDA margin estimated >45% — high quality, stable, tariff-protected [#I]
  • Energy supply margin: Compressed post-2022; running at low-single-digit EBITDA margin — structurally challenged in a normalised price environment [#I]
  • Solutions segment margin: Building from ~8-10% EBITDA margin toward a medium-term target of ~12-15% per management — growth driver, not yet margin accretive at group level [#I]

Operating leverage: Limited positive operating leverage in current trajectory — grid is regulated (margin capped by WACC), solutions is scaling (margin improving), energy supply is compressing; net leverage effect modest [#A]

Sustainability assessment: Grid margin: structural, tariff-protected, durable; energy supply margin: cyclical, not structural improvement; solutions margin: structural improvement trajectory but multi-year [#J]

Mix shift driver: Solutions segment growing as % of revenue (est. 18-22% of FY-2024 revenue vs 12-15% FY-2022) — over time, if solutions reaches 30%+ of group, group EBITDA margin could re-rate upward as solutions margin improves; this is a 3-5Y thesis, not yet L3Y visible [#J]

Bearish read on margin sustainability: What's the bearish read? The energy supply business could deteriorate further if European power price curves soften or if BKW is exposed to legacy hedging unwinds — this would be margin-compressive while the solutions segment is still building scale; the combination is a margin squeeze window of 1-2Y before solutions offsets [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: Pronounced and stable H2 weighting — BKW consistently earns ~55-58% of annual EBITDA in H2 driven by winter energy demand; this is a persistent, well-understood pattern with no evidence of structural change [#J]

H1 pattern: Historically lighter — grid revenues stable year-round, but energy supply and solutions tend to be H2-weighted due to winter heating demand and year-end contract completions [#I]

  • H1 share of FY EBITDA: Estimated ~42-45% historically; FY-2022 was slightly more balanced due to elevated spot prices in H1-2022 [#I]

H2 pattern: Consistently the stronger half — winter demand surge drives energy supply revenues; grid tariff billing is not seasonal but maintenance-capex spending peaks in summer (H1), depressing H1 free cash flow [#I]

  • H2 share of FY EBITDA: Estimated ~55-58%; solutions segment tends to book large contracts in H2 (year-end client budget cycles) [#I]

Seasonality changing: No evidence of structural shift — the H2 weighting has been stable over 5+ years based on available annual reports; solutions growth could modestly reduce seasonality over time if project revenue is more evenly distributed [#I]

Cause — weather: Primary driver is Swiss winter heating degree days — abnormally mild winters (H1-2024 effect) suppress H1 results; this was visible in H1-2024 underperformance vs consensus [#I]

Cause — production cycles: Hydroelectric and nuclear generation (BKW operates nuclear until ~2033 via Mühleberg wind-down provisions) contribute to generation profile; hydrology adds inter-annual volatility beyond pure seasonality [#I]

Seasonality direction: H2 > H1, stable, predictable — investors expecting front-half delivery will be systematically disappointed; Richard should calibrate H1 results against H1 history, not annualised [#J]

Bearish read on seasonal patterns: Through what mechanism might apparent seasonality be a weakening cyclical pattern? If solutions segment grows and becomes the dominant earnings driver, its project-revenue recognition patterns may introduce new non-seasonal volatility (lumpy contract completions) that disrupts the predictable H2 weighting investors currently rely on [#J]
