  • Management has signalled appetite for further M&A to reach £300m+ revenue target; any future deal would restart M&A contribution questions

  • Sceptical bullet: The revenue recovery assumption rests almost entirely on (a) Components destocking reversal and (b) Power defence programme delivery timing; if either slips — which is plausible given programme delays are endemic in UK defence procurement — FY26 growth guide evaporates and the thesis timeline extends materially [#J]

§3 — Margin Profile & Evolution

  • Verdict: SOLI-LON margin trajectory is directionally positive but recovering from a trough that was worse than most identifiable peers; the pace of recovery is the critical unknown [#J]

  • EBIT margin: Adjusted EBIT margin for FY25 is estimated at approximately 5.0-5.5%, up from a FY24 trough of ~4.5%; management medium-term target is 7-8% [#A]

  • For context, discoverIE operates at ~7-8% adjusted EBIT margin; Volex at ~6-7%; XP Power historically ~15%+ but currently recovering from ~10% trough

  • SOLI-LON's current 5-5.5% sits at the bottom of this comparator range ❌

  • Gross margin: Group gross margin is estimated at approximately 28-30%, blending Components (~22%) and Power/Systems (~38%); this is broadly stable year-on-year [#A]

  • Gross margin trajectory is slightly positive as mix shifts toward Power; this is the mechanism by which EBIT margin recovery is expected to occur

  • EBITDA margin: Approximately 7-8% on an adjusted basis, reflecting ~£18-21m EBITDA on ~£264m revenue; depreciation and amortisation runs ~£6-7m annually post-Pacer acquisition [#I]

  • Direction: Expanding, but slowly; the step from 4.5% to 5.5% EBIT margin over FY24-FY25 is in the right direction but falls well short of the 200-300bps gap to the 7-8% target [#A]

  • At the current pace (~100bps per year), the medium-term target is 2-3 years away — which the current valuation may or may not be pricing

  • Decomposition — mix contribution: Positive; the shift toward Power/Systems (higher-margin) and away from Components (lower-margin) is the primary structural driver [#A]

  • Each 1pp of revenue mix shift from Components to Power contributes approximately 10-15bps to group EBIT margin

  • Decomposition — volume contribution: Negative in FY25 due to Components volume decline; operating deleverage in the Components division partially offset Power division operational leverage [#A]

  • Decomposition — input costs: Neutral to mildly positive; electronic component costs have normalised post-supply-chain crisis; SOLI-LON benefits as a buyer of components for its Power assembly operations [#A]

  • Decomposition — productivity: Management has cited cost reduction initiatives post-Pacer integration; headcount rationalisation in FY24 contributed ~50-100bps to EBIT margin [#A]

  • This is a one-off contributor; sustainable margin expansion must come from revenue growth and mix, not repeat restructuring

  • Operating leverage: The business has operating leverage at the division level, particularly in Power/Systems; however, the group-level effect is muted while Components (the deleverage drag) remains a large revenue share [#A]

  • A 10% volume recovery in Components alone would likely add ~30-50bps to group EBIT margin through fixed-cost absorption

  • Peer margin trends: discoverIE has maintained margins in the 7-8% range through the downturn, suggesting stronger mix and pricing power than SOLI-LON in the current environment ❌ [#A]

  • XP Power experienced a sharper margin collapse (from ~15% to ~10%) but from a much higher starting point; its recovery trajectory is also faster, reflecting semiconductor-cycle dynamics

  • Sell-side outlook: Zeus Capital and Singer both model margins recovering toward 6-6.5% by FY27 — below management's 7-8% target, implying the sell-side is sceptical the full target will be achieved on schedule [#A]

  • Cost programmes: No material new restructuring programme announced; post-Pacer integration costs are largely behind SOLI-LON; incremental cost discipline is embedded in guidance but not separately quantified [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The margin recovery thesis depends on mix shift and volume recovery simultaneously — but if Components volumes recover faster than Power (which is programme-gated), the mix benefit partially reverses and EBIT margin recovery undershoots the trajectory the model projects [#J]

  • The risk of being squeezed between large distributors (scale) and specialist design-in manufacturers (value-add) is real

  • The key analytical question is whether the cyclical recovery is timing-dependent or durable — the structural trend supports the thesis, the cyclical component creates execution risk

  • Sceptical bullet: If UK defence programme delays extend (endemic in MoD procurement) and industrial end-market recovery is slower than the 2024-25 consensus expects, SOLI-LON faces the uncomfortable position of being too early in the transition — still largely a components distributor without the scale in Power/Systems to offset continued distribution weakness [#J]

§9 — Sceptical Synthesis (Cross-Cutting)

  • Verdict: The strongest bear case is a prolonged cycle extension in industrial electronics combined with UK MoD programme slippage — either alone stresses the thesis; both together would likely cause consensus to cut FY26 estimates by 20%+ [#J]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: The thesis depends on three simultaneous improvements: Components destocking reversal, Power programme delivery, and group margin recovery; all three must happen broadly on schedule for FY26 EPS consensus to hold [#J]

  • Bear scenario: Components recovery is 12M later than consensus, MoD programme slips one cycle, and margin is flat; FY26 EPS of ~50p vs consensus ~75p — a ~33% miss

  • Single largest risk: UK MoD programme timing is the single highest-conviction risk; it is endemic, systemic, and outside SOLI-LON's control; every quarter of programme delay directly defers Power division revenue recognition [#J]
