EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The senior executive team exhibits deep institutional knowledge and stable divisional leadership, with the Executive Committee comprising the CEO, CFO, four Business Unit Heads, and two major Function Heads, emphasizing succession planning and job rotation to build a robust internal talent pipeline while filling almost 60% of management vacancies internally, with Patrik Grande promoted to Head of Business Unit Healthcare in January 2026 to replace veteran leader Bijay Singh who executed a planned transition into retirement, Natale Capri becoming the sole head of Performance Materials in 2026 after Thomas Sul's transition while maintaining over 26 years of continuous service at DKSH.

Board composition demonstrates independent leadership with residual family influence, where seven of the nine board members are fully independent while Adrian T. Keller and Andreas W. Keller are considered non-independent as representatives of the Diethelm Keller Group AG which controls 45.0% of corporate voting rights, resulting in 77.8% board independence that exceeds Givaudan's board independence level, though an active board refresh occurred at the March 2026 Annual General Meeting where three directors retired including founder-linked director Andreas W. Keller who did not stand for reelection and Dr. Wolfgang Baier and Suwannee Ratthayabandith also retired, with shareholders electing Valerie Diele-Braun, Corine Tap, and Julie von Wedel-Keller to successfully establish a majority of female representatives on the board. Director skin-in-the-game shows Chairman Marco Gadola increased his holdings to 18,100 shares in 2025 while anchor family member Adrian T. Keller holds 60,526 shares directly, though other independent directors hold very small or zero equity positions with five directors holding a combined total of under 3,000 shares, no activist investor holds a seat on the board, and UBS Fund Management holds a stable 5.0% passive stake.

Management commentary reveals cautious tone shifts and selective topic avoidance, with executive tone shifting from high optimism to defensive caution as chemical industry demand remained persistently muted throughout 2025 while commentary shifted in mid-2025 to address macroeconomic tariff shockwaves before returning to cautious optimism, leadership admitted Consumer Goods growth was soft and underperforming expectations in early 2025 though by early 2026 management expressed being highly encouraged by second-half acceleration in Malaysia and Vietnam, repeatedly avoiding quantifying the exact financial impact of highly publicized AI pilot initiatives while noting it was too early to tell the full productivity potential of machine learning, consistently deflecting regional operational disruptions in Myanmar as being completely out of corporate control, minimizing geopolitical risks by highlighting Mainland China represents only 2.5% of group revenues, and responding to analyst frustration over slow chemical acquisitions by reiterating strict valuation discipline.

Key-person risk assessment indicates low CEO dependency but elevated CFO concentration, with Stefan P. Butz leading DKSH as CEO for nine years while establishing highly predictable and conservative corporate standards, the 58-year-old CEO facing no immediate statutory retirement age limits or operational horizons with no public indications of executive health issues that could threaten leadership continuity, though CFO Ido Wallach manages dual executive responsibilities covering corporate finance, group supply chain management, and transformation such that a sudden departure would be highly disruptive by leaving a temporary vacancy across several programs, while DKSH utilizes an annual Talent Review and Succession Planning campaign to identify high-performing and high-potential internal leaders and successfully filled approximately 55% of its management vacancies through internal promotions, with Patrik Grande promoted internally to Head of Healthcare in January 2026 to succeed Bijay Singh who completed a planned transition into retirement after eleven years of service.
