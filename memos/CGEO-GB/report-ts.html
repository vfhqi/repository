Section 3 – What the company does 20 findings

Section summary
  1. The business operates a dominant and defensive private portfolio of market-leading assets complemented by a large equity stake in a leading, highly profitable listed banking group.

PORTFOLIO SEGMENTS AND REVENUE MODEL

1.
Segregated portfolio structure: The portfolio is split between listed assets, representing 47.2% of total value, and private businesses, representing 52.8% of total value. Large portfolio companies make up 41.9% of total portfolio value
2.
Premium banking asset: The listed portfolio is composed entirely of a 16.6% equity stake in Lion Finance Group, a LSE-listed FTSE 100 universal banking group. Lion Finance Group dominates the universal banking sectors of both Georgia and Armenia
3.
Market-leading pharmacy retailer: Retail pharmacy is the largest private segment, representing 18.3% of portfolio value and operating 458 stores in Georgia and Armenia. It is also expanding wholesale operations to capture wider distribution channels
4.
Comprehensive healthcare network: Healthcare services represent 12.3% of the portfolio, comprising 7 large hospitals, 30 regional hospitals, and 16 outpatient clinics. This segment is the largest healthcare market participant in the country
5.
Highly defensive insurance: Insurance represents 11.3% of the portfolio, providing property, casualty, and medical insurance with rising premium rates. Underwriting performance has improved consistently due to tariff adjustments
6.
Emerging business incubation: Emerging and other businesses represent 10.9% of the portfolio, spanning renewable energy, private education, and auto services. These assets represent a platform for future monetization and cash generation
7.
Predictable recurring revenue: Portfolio revenues are highly defensive, with retail pharmacy sales and recurring medical insurance premiums providing excellent cash predictability. This defensive mix insulates the platform from cyclical downturns
8.
K-12 private education: The education business holds a 9.8% market share in Georgia's private K-12 market, operating four school brands across seven campuses. Growth is driven by organic capacity increases and strong student intakes
9.
Renewable energy operations: The renewable energy segment operates five commissioned assets with a combined capacity of 71MW, though pipeline projects were recently discontinued. The business provides highly predictable, long-term utility-like cash flows
10.
Healthcare segment leadership: The healthcare business is the largest healthcare market participant, captured across outpatient polyclinics and referral hospitals. This absolute leadership delivers strong pricing power when negotiating with corporate and state insurance clients
11.
Diagnostics Caucasus market share: Megalab operates the largest laboratory in the entire Caucasus region, offering highly advanced diagnostics services. The laboratory business is a key driver of high-margin clinics and diagnostics segment revenue
12.
Regional hospital services: Regional and community hospitals provide basic outpatient and inpatient services to approximately 80,000 capitation patients in Georgia. These hospitals act as an essential, high-volume referral network for specialized tertiary hospital services

COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE AND VALUE CHAIN

13.
Unmatched retail dominance: The pharmacy business is Georgia's largest retailer, holding an unrivalled 34% market share in the organised retail market. Scale advantages allow the business to secure favorable supplier terms
14.
Vertically integrated healthcare: Georgia Capital is the largest healthcare market participant, capturing high value across inpatient, outpatient, and diagnostic services. This integration delivers strong cost efficiencies and patient referral capture
15.
High customer diversification: The customer base is exceptionally granular, spanning millions of individual pharmacy consumers and corporate and retail insurance policyholders. No single customer represents a material concentration risk for the private portfolio
16.
Strict capital-light focus: Future growth is directed solely at capital-light sectors to enhance returns on capital and reduce holding company capital requirements. This strategy supports a rapid deleveraging trajectory
17.
Consolidating industry structure: Services industries in Georgia are consolidating, enabling Georgia Capital to execute highly profitable bolt-on acquisitions at attractive multiples. This allows the platform to expand without heavy greenfield execution risks
18.
Universal healthcare partnership: Historical collaborations between private providers and the government on Universal Health Coverage have successfully delivered high-quality, affordable healthcare. This public-private cooperation has driven substantial market expansion
19.
Other side of the story: Heavy exposure to domestic regulatory changes in healthcare reimbursement rates or pharmacy price controls could severely compress portfolio profit margins. Regulatory shifts represent the primary failure mode of the healthcare investment case

Section 5 – The possible investment case 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case strengthens significantly due to a fast-growing domestic economy, high-quality asset compounding, and highly accretive share buybacks.

MACRO AND BANKING CONVERGENCE DRIVERS

1.
High macroeconomic compounding: Georgia's economy grew by a strong 9.1% in Q1 2026, and is projected to expand by 7.0% in 2026. This high GDP growth provides a highly supportive environment for consumer and healthcare spending
2.
Stable policy environment: The implementation of the Digital Economy and Information Society Development Strategy 2025-2030 provides a supportive policy framework. Plausibility is high, failing if execution capacity lags
3.
ICT sector acceleration: Georgia's ICT sector GDP increased from GEL 1.30 billion in 2019 to GEL 7.40 billion in 2025, driving high-end consumer demand. Plausibility is high, failing if technical workforce talent exits
4.
Listed banking valuation: Lion Finance Group represents 47.2% of total portfolio value and acts as a direct driver of Georgia Capital's NAV. Its share price appreciation is highly likely to continue compounding NAV
5.
Universal banking expansion: LFG's expansion into Armenia and premium segment LSE listing provide long-term earnings predictability. Plausibility is high given a targeted 20%+ return on equity, failing only on severe regional shocks
6.
Resilient external flows: Despite geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, Georgia's economic momentum remains supported by strong exports and remittances. This macro buffer helps maintain structural lari resilience
7.
Universal banking capitalization: LFG is well-capitalized, maintaining its dividend payouts while executing highly profitable universal banking strategies. This high capitalization supports continued cash distribution to Georgia Capital

PRIVATE PORTFOLIO AND CAPITAL RETURNS DRIVERS

8.
Private pharmacy growth: Pharmacy segment revenues are driven by same-store sales growth, average bill size expansion, and margin expansion. Plausibility is high as margins improved by 1.7 percentage points in Q1 2026
9.
Healthcare services optimization: Healthcare profitability is driven by inpatient specialized services and the onboarding of reputable doctors. Plausibility is medium, with risk of failure from severe skilled nurse shortages
10.
Share buyback compounding: Capital returns conducted at a deep NAV discount compound NAV per share growth. Plausibility is high as long as the discount remains attractive, failing only if Holdco cash flow is constrained
11.
Non-core business monetization: Management seeks to monetise its private investments over a 5-10 year period. Plausibility is medium, with risk of failure if market liquidity dries up
12.
Compounding bull case: The bull case does not require all drivers to fire simultaneously, as share buybacks and banking appreciation act as independent compounding engines
13.
Other side of the story: If the Georgian Lari undergoes major depreciation, the sterling-denominated NAV per share growth will fade even if local currency EBITDA growth remains robust

Section 6 – Valuation 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Georgia Capital's shares trade at a compelling discount to NAV that provides a substantial margin of safety and highly attractive compounding opportunities.
1.
Record-low NAV discount: Shares trade at a 16.2% discount to the reported March 2026 NAV per share of GBP 43.34, representing a significant narrowing from the 66.6% discount in September 2022
2.
Private portfolio discount: The standalone discount to the private portfolio NAV was 30.2% as of March 31, 2026, offering a highly accretive environment for share buybacks
3.
Historical multiple comparison: Predecessor assets like Georgia Healthcare Group were valued by Jefferies at a high 27.0x P/E multiple, suggesting that CGEO's private healthcare assets are conservatively valued within the NAV
4.
Standalone Holdco equity value: JSC Georgia Capital's equity value is estimated with reference to its underlying portfolio value offset by Holdco net debt. This valuation methodology aligns closely with global private equity best practices
5.
Record cash distribution: Lion Finance Group maintains a targeted 30% to 50% capital distribution payout ratio, delivering predictable cash flows to CGEO. This cash flow is critical for backing ongoing Holdco buyback funding
6.
Other side of the story: The market continues to apply a holding company discount, pricing in persistent geopolitical tensions and the illiquidity of frontier market private assets. This creates a highly attractive entry point if the holding company discount persists or narrows further
7.
Accretion multiplier potential: As long as the market prices CGEO below its underlying NAV, every pound spent on buybacks delivers disproportionate NAV per share growth. This compounding effect accelerates NAV per share expansion beyond organic portfolio growth
8.
Other side of the story: If the NAV discount narrowed to a record-low 16.2% continues to compress to single digits, the high-accretion benefit of share buybacks will diminish, shifting the compounding burden entirely onto organic EBITDA growth

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk profile is dominated by elevated geopolitical and domestic political uncertainties in Georgia, which could impact the local currency and investor confidence.

POLITICAL AND MACROECONOMIC VULNERABILITIES

1.
Severe country risk: Recent political polarization and protests over disputed elections represent a primary risk to economic and financial stability
2.
Geopolitical market shocks: Geopolitical shifts away from the West have historically triggered sharp share price declines due to frontier market sentiment
3.
Currency transmission risk: A major depreciation of the Georgian Lari would immediately inflate import costs for the retail pharmacy business, compressing margins and sterling-denominated NAV
4.
Structural growth vulnerabilities: High short-term GDP growth, driven by temporary hard currency inflows, could reverse rapidly, exposing structural economic weaknesses
5.
Trade openness vulnerability: Due to high trade openness and reliance on tourism, the Georgian economy remains highly vulnerable to unexpected external shocks
6.
Early warning signal: Investors must closely monitor the National Bank of Georgia's monetary policy rate and foreign currency interventions as indicators of mounting exchange rate pressures

PORTFOLIO AND CONCENTRATION RISKS

7.
High asset concentration: Georgia Capital's NAV is highly concentrated in two assets, with Lion Finance Group alone representing 47.2% of total portfolio value
8.
Severe healthcare shortages: A persistent shortage of skilled healthcare workers and doctors represents a primary constraint to hospital EBITDA growth
9.
Climate change physical risks: Severe heat stress and water scarcity present long-term threats to agricultural and hydropower assets in the emerging portfolio
10.
Systemic banking concentration: The domestic financial sector is dominated by two massive private banks, increasing the risk of systemic contagion during economic stress
11.
Concentrated asset volatility: Because Lion Finance Group represents 47.2% of the portfolio, any domestic bank run would immediately impair CGEO's NAV. Such banking sector volatility represents a direct transmission mechanism to CGEO's share price
12.
Discontinued pipeline projects: Discontinuing renewable projects led to a GEL 25.70 million value reduction in Q1 2026, demonstrating execution volatility. These asset write-downs highlight the execution risk in capital-heavy infrastructure projects
13.
Other side of the story: Georgia's sovereign balance sheet is highly resilient, with public debt declining to a record-low 34% of GDP, providing a substantial fiscal buffer against macroeconomic shocks
