Section 3 – What the company does 15 findings

Section summary
  1. The company operates a highly defensive, diversified business model with leading positions in structural growth markets like sports medicine and negative pressure wound therapy.

Business Segments and Product Portfolio

1.
Segment revenue: S&N's consolidated revenue of $6,164 million is organized into Orthopaedics at $2,437 million, Sports Medicine & ENT at $1,934 million, and Advanced Wound Management at $1,793 million. The Orthopaedics business is the largest segment by sales, representing 39.00% of group revenue
2.
Orthopaedic solutions: The Orthopaedics segment develops primary and revision knee systems like JOURNEY II, hip systems like CATALYSTEM, and EVOS plating systems for trauma. S&N has expanded its trauma presence with the AETOS shoulder system and EVOS plating
3.
Sports Medicine solutions: The Sports Medicine & ENT segment focuses on joint repair platforms like the REGENETEN bioinductive implant, arthroscopic enabling technologies like COBLATION, and ENT systems. Sports Medicine has grown to become larger than orthopedic reconstruction and robotics combined
4.
Wound care solutions: The Advanced Wound Management segment sells Allevyn foam dressings, Santyl bioactives, and PICO single-use negative pressure wound therapy systems. Advanced Wound Devices continues to see double-digit growth from products like the LEAF patient monitoring system
5.
Consumable revenue mix: Revenue is heavily driven by repeat-use consumables in wound care and sports medicine, whereas orthopedic reconstructive systems require heavy initial set investments. This transactional versus contractual mix supports cash flow stability during capital budget cycles

Value Chain Positioning and Customer Dynamics

6.
Upstream profit favored: S&N occupies a high-margin upstream position by developing IP-protected, clinically-differentiated medical devices directly to healthcare providers. This allows the company to capture robust gross margins above 70.00%
7.
Direct selling model: In established markets, S&N sells directly to hospitals and ambulatory surgery centers, while relying on distributors in emerging markets. Direct selling ensures close relationships with surgeons and hospital purchasing departments
8.
Low customer concentration: S&N's customer base is highly fragmented across thousands of acute hospitals and post-acute homecare agencies, mitigating buyer power. No individual customer accounts for a material share of revenues, protecting the firm from volume disruptions
9.
Capturing surgical shifts: S&N has focused on ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs), placing 40.00% of its CORI robotic systems in ASCs to capitalize on lower cost settings. This position allows S&N to outpace traditional hospital-focused joint reconstruction growth

Barriers to Entry and Market Structure

10.
Technical switching barriers: Entry barriers are exceptionally high due to specialized surgeon training and high switching costs once a surgeon adopts a specific implant platform. Surgeons develop deep familiarity with S&N's surgical instrumentation and representative support
11.
Clinical data moats: S&N's key competitive advantage is its extensive clinical evidence library supporting products like REGENETEN and PICO negative pressure dressings. This extensive clinical validation serves as a significant hurdle for generic medtech entry
12.
Consolidated peer group: The advanced wound care market is highly consolidated with S&N holding a 14.00% market share, competing directly with Solventum at 14.00% and Mölnlycke at 10.00%. S&N acts as the primary challenger in negative pressure therapy
13.
Orthopedic joint market: S&N acts as a smaller challenger in the $33bn joint reconstruction market with a 7.00% share, lagging behind Stryker and Zimmer Biomet. This limited scale historically restricted S&N's pricing power in standard joint implants
14.
Other side of the story: Surgeons emphasize that competitor joint implants are clinically equivalent, making S&N's sales highly vulnerable to aggressive representative poaching by Stryker

Section summary
  1. The financial profile has significantly inflected since 2022, exhibiting consistent mid-single digit revenue growth, expanding trading margins, and a fifteen-fold increase in free cash flow.

Growth and Profitability Performance

1.
Revenue turnaround: S&N's historical low-single digit revenue growth has shifted to a consistent mid-single digit profile with a reported CAGR of 5.70% over 2022-2025. Revenue grew from $5,549 million in 2023 to $5,810 million in 2024, and reached $6,164 million in 2025
2.
Underlying sales growth: Organic revenue growth remained highly consistent, tracking at 5.30% in 2024 and maintaining 5.30% in 2025. Growth excluding China was even stronger, tracking at 7.00% in 2025
3.
Profitability expansion: Trading profit margins expanded by 240 basis points to 19.70% in FY25 from 17.30% in 2022, driven by operational efficiencies. S&N expanded its margin from 18.10% in 2024 to 19.70% in 2025 despite overcoming some 1,000 basis points of macro headwinds
4.
Free cash generation: Free cash flow achieved a fifteen-fold increase, rising from $56 million in 2022 to $840 million in 2025 due to working capital improvements. FCF grew from $325 million in 2023 to $551 million in 2024, and surged to $840 million in 2025
5.
Cash conversion strength: Trading cash conversion surged to 102.00% in 2025, compared to 95.20% in 2024, representing exceptionally high earnings quality. Adjusted EPS grew from $0.83 in 2023 to $0.84 in 2024, and reached $1.02 in 2025
6.
Divisional margins: S&N's segment profit margins in 2025 were 14.90% in Orthopaedics, 23.80% in Sports Medicine, and 24.90% in Advanced Wound Management. Orthopaedics margins surged in H2 2025 to 21.50% compared to 17.70% in H1 2025

7.

10.
Conservative guidance history: S&N's management has consistently met or exceeded guidance, such as raising its FY25 free cash flow target from $600 million to $750 million. Management's provisional 2026 guidance calls for 6.00% underlying sales growth and $800 million free cash flow
11.
Other side of the story: Redburn analysts note S&N's 2024 orthopedic revenue fell far short of Stryker, which posted 11.00% knee growth while S&N's knee growth fell by 1.00%

Section 5 – The possible investment case 11 findings

Section summary
  1. The forward investment case strengthens significantly based on S&N's structural transition to faster-growing segments and highly visible margin self-help under the RISE strategy.

Key Structural Margins and Product Drivers

1.
Divisional margin targets: S&N aims to expand its Orthopaedics trading margins from 14.90% in 2025 to over 20.00% by 2030 through direct anterior anterior hips and SKU streamlining. S&N is consolidating its primary knee SKU footprint by nearly 40.00% to drive margin expansion
2.
Orthopedic margin failure: This critical expansion would fail if surgeon pushback on SKU consolidation disrupts the sales channel or if price erosion offsets volume gains. Product rationalization can lead to near-term disruption in clinic relationships
3.
Product launch pipeline: S&N is introducing the cementless LANDMARK primary knee in Q3 2026, creating a highly competitive solution for the shift to cementless implants. This launch will round out the knee portfolio and drive growth reacceleration in H2 2026
4.
Landmark failure mechanism: The Landmark launch would fail if competitors' cementless knee technologies maintain superior clinical tracking and capture account loyalty. Competitor products like Zimmer's Oxford cementless knee have built a very strong track record in joint procedures
5.
ASC robotic penetration: CORI handheld robotics position S&N to lead in ASCs, as CORI's small footprint and lower capital cost are highly tailored to ASC budgets. S&N placed 40.00% of its CORI systems in ASCs in 2025, capturing above-market penetration
6.
Robotics failure mechanism: This driver would fail if clinicians choose fully automated, larger robotic platforms like Stryker's Mako for their higher accuracy. Handheld platforms can be difficult to learn and are perceived as less accurate than static robotic units

Portfolio Transition and Strategic Execution

7.
Accelerating market exposure: S&N's long-term portfolio shift has repositioned the company into structural growth markets, with sports medicine and wound care now representing over half of group sales. These divisions have consistently delivered above-market growth over the past decade
8.
Portfolio driver failure: This transition would fail if Medicare skin substitute reimbursement resets in 2026 severely compress advanced wound bioactive margins, where pricing reform is expected to be a major headwind
9.
Independent growth dynamics: S&N's growth levers operate independently, allowing the investment case to succeed on sports medicine and wound strength even if US knees recover slowly. Strong joint repair demand offsets temporary US orthopedic knee launch lags
10.
Other side of the story: Surgeons warn S&N's handheld CORI system is difficult to learn and less accurate than larger competitor platforms, risking slow commercial adoption. This could limit the pull-through of highly profitable reconstructive knee implants in critical accounts

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk picture is heavily tied to execution and reimbursement, but recent operational stabilization suggests these risks are highly manageable.

Regulatory and Reimbursement Headwinds

1.
Reimbursement reset risk: S&N's Advanced Wound Bioactives segment faces a 20.00% to 25.00% price reduction in skin substitutes, risking a $20 million to $40 million profit headwind. Management expects pricing to decline but aims to remain volume-neutral
2.
Inflation and tariff headwinds: S&N faces a compound tariff headwind of $15 million to $20 million in 2026, which creates a 65 basis point margin drag. S&N is implementing manufacturing cost savings to mitigate these ongoing headwinds
3.
Volume-Based Procurement pressure: S&N's ENT and Sports Medicine segments face ongoing price cuts in China from VBP policies. However, China now represents only 3.00% of S&N's sales, reducing its impact

Commercial Execution and Early Warning Signals

4.
Salesforce attrition (The single failure mode): S&N's single failure mode would be sales representative turnover, as joint reconstruction is highly representative-dependent and sensitive to service quality. Loss of key sales representatives can instantly lead to market share losses to peers
5.
False friend robotic volumes: Investors should caution against strong initial CORI capital sales, as robotic placements can mask a decline in highly profitable joint implant consumable volumes. The core driver of joint revenue remains high-volume consumable implant procedures
6.
Primary monitoring signal: US knee implant market share tracking remains the key early-warning metric to monitor before S&N's expected H2 2026 recovery. Softness in US knee sales must recover as Landmark is deployed
7.
Other side of the story: The bears overlook S&N's insulation from macro risks and backing from activist Cevian Capital, which has built an 11.00% stake. S&N has limited exposure to broader currency volatility and tariffs compared to international medical supply peers
