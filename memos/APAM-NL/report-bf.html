EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 1 findings

Section summary
  1. Aperam has successfully transformed from a cyclical European stainless steel producer into a diversified specialty materials operator with formidable competitive barriers, though elevated leverage and persistent European demand weakness represent near-term headwinds.

Business Model and Operational Footprint

Aperam operates a diversified 2.5 million tonne flat stainless and electrical steel capacity across Europe and South America, uniquely positioning it as the sole integrated flat stainless producer in South America. The company has expanded beyond commodity steel through its Alloys and Specialties division, which manufactures high-value nickel alloys and superalloys for critical aerospace applications across France, India, China, and the United States. Vertical integration is substantial, with the Recycling and Renewables segment securing secondary raw materials through Aperam Recycling–established as a global leader in superalloy recycling following the December 2021 ELG acquisition–and captive charcoal-based forestry operations in Brazil. The BioEnergia subsidiary manages 150,000 hectares producing 450,000 tonnes of biochar annually, enabling 100% coke-free blast furnace operations since 2012 and providing structural insulation from fossil fuel volatility. Downstream integration through the Services and Solutions segment distributes tailored products through 14 steel service centers to more than 6,000 active customers globally, capturing approximately 40% of mill output and reducing exposure to speculative wholesale intermediaries. The company maintains strong direct-to-end-user sales channels, with approximately 70% of stainless products supplied directly, enhancing customer lock-in and pricing visibility while the proprietary eAperam portal operates as the largest direct e-commerce platform for stainless steel outside China. 1 4 7 10 13

Financial Profile and Through-Cycle Resilience

Aperam generated revenues of €6,080 million in 2025, representing a defensive 2.80% contraction compared to

2025, substantially outperforming Outokumpu's adjusted EBITDA of €167 million, with margins defended by €100 million in Leadership Journey Phase 5 self-help gains despite severe European pricing pressure. Return on capital employed reached 1.90% in 2025, meaningfully ahead of Outokumpu's negative ROCE of -3.20%, though this represents cyclical trough performance compared to the peak ROCE of 22.60% achieved in 2022. Net financial debt expanded to €978 million in 2025 from €544 million in 2024, driven entirely by the fully cash-funded €415 million acquisition of Universal Stainless, with management targeting a net debt-to-EBITDA ratio below 1.00x by year-end 2027. Working capital management delivered a strong €176 million cash release in 2025, while free cash flow before dividends turned negative €167 million due exclusively to the Universal acquisition, contrasting with positive €125 million in 2024. The company proposed maintaining its base dividend at €2.00 per share for 2026, representing a €145 million outflow and demonstrating superior cash stability versus Outokumpu's slashed dividend of €0.13 per share.

Value Chain Position and Margin Capture

Aperam's vertical integration spans from upstream inputs through downstream distribution, with captive BioEnergia forests providing 100% of charcoal requirements for South American operations and ELG Recycling securing over 80% scrap inputs for European mills, insulating the company from primary raw material volatility. The value chain analysis reveals stark margin divergence, with downstream service centers capturing the highest sustainable returns at estimated 22.00% gross margins and 6.00% EBIT margins, compared to highly volatile melting operations at 12.00% gross margins and 4.00% EBIT margins. Services and Solutions operates with half the net working capital requirements of independent distributors, maintaining 60 days of inventory versus 120 days for peers, while delivering mid-cycle EBITDA of €80 per tonne and generating 9% ROCE in worst-performing years escalating to 42% ROCE during inventory upcycles. The integrated supply chain distributes 40% of mill output to more than 6,000 active clients across 36 countries, providing real-time market intelligence that optimizes mill utilization and mitigates severe cyclical swings. 28 31

Competitive Landscape and Strategic Positioning

Aperam operates within a highly consolidated European oligopoly alongside Outokumpu (28.00% cold-rolled market share) and Acerinox, facing aggressive volume-oriented Asian producers including Tsingshan, Delong, Baosteel, and POSCO that leverage low-cost nickel pig iron to erode European commodity margins. Customer concentration risk is minimal, with the company serving over 10,000 active clients globally and maintaining approximately 70% direct-to-consumer shipments that minimize wholesale distributor bargaining power, though exposure to spot pricing in Europe offsets technical alloy switching barriers. High-nickel aerospace alloys face lengthy five-to-seven year qualification cycles that create prohibitive customer switching costs, with the €415 million Universal Stainless acquisition instantly capturing established certified customer accounts and securing direct entry into the U.S. aerospace specialty metals market. Cross-value-chain signals indicate that downstream integration cushions Aperam against scrap pricing volatility, with working capital analysis revealing Days Sales Outstanding rising to 26.11 days in 2025 from 22.42 days in 2024, signaling modest trade term extensions granted during industrial recessions.

Foundation Quality and Operator Strength

Leadership transitioned seamlessly on January 1, 2026, with Sudhakar Sivaji assuming the CEO role after 25 years of engineering and financial experience, while the Leadership Journey Phase 5 efficiency program realized €195 million in cumulative gains by year-end 2025, finishing a year ahead of schedule. Phase 6 of the Leadership Journey launched with ambitious €150 million gain targets by 2028, already capturing €18 million in gains during Q1 2026 and focusing on recycling automation, Brazilian input optimization, and U.S. alloy supply chain integration. Capital allocation discipline remains robust, with organic growth projects required to meet minimum 15% internal rates of return, while the Universal Stainless acquisition was secured at 6.9x EV/EBITDA versus peer valuations of 11.1x and targets €27 million in annual synergies driving estimated post-synergy ROCE of 16.10%. Aperam maintains formidable competitive moats as the sole integrated flat stainless steel producer in South America with 100% coke-free operations, while integrated ELG Recycling positions the company as a global leader in superalloy recycling operating over 50 locations across 18 countries. Financial outputs demonstrate resilient profitability, with adjusted EBITDA of €339 million substantially outperforming Outokumpu's €167 million and ROCE of 1.90% exceeding Outokumpu's negative 3.20%, while Alloys and Recycling segments generated a combined 53% of total group EBITDA in 2025, successfully dampening European stainless steel cyclicality.

Disruption Forces and Long-Term Structural Position

Entry barriers remain formidable, with heavy upfront capital requirements, specialized metallurgical equipment with high exit sunk costs, and lengthy five-to-seven year aerospace certification cycles that the Universal acquisition strategically bypassed through pre-existing customer relationships. The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism established its first reference price at €75.36 per ton of CO2 in April 2026, with carbon price assumptions of €100 per tonne raising average steel import costs by approximately 14% and helping domestic European steelmakers defend regional pricing against low-cost Asian imports. Aperam's green steel credentials are industry-leading, with European furnaces operating with approximately 90% scrap inputs, captive BioEnergia producing 450,000 tonnes of biochar annually from 150,000 hectares of sustainable eucalyptus forestry, and innovative bio-oil by-products replacing traditional fossil fuels while targeting 0.30t CO2e per ton crude steel emissions. Long-term growth adjacencies include a €160 million European investment package for advanced vacuum induction melting at Imphy, strategic capacity upgrades targeting high-growth niches in hydrogen, LNG, and e-mobility, and the April 2026 Magnetec acquisition establishing the new Aperam Magnetic Components unit to target EV sectors. Confidence in BB#2 strength: HIGH–the company demonstrates exceptional self-help execution, formidable integration barriers, and successful specialty diversification. Single largest risk: delay or failure in harvesting the €27 million Universal Stainless synergies. Early warning signal: sequential margin compression in Alloys & Specialties during flat macro cycles.
