§2 Business Model and Revenue

Net signal: Three-segment business with Online as the primary growth engine and margin driver; Sports Franchise and Gaming Franchise are the managed-decline cash cow segments. The online mix shift is structural — iGaming EBITDA estimated to pass 50% of total group EBITDA in FY2026, vs <30% at IPO. Every percentage point of mix shift adds ~200bp to blended EBITDA margin. [#J]

2.1 Online Segment

The highest-growth and highest-margin division. Comprehensive digital product suite through Lottomatica, GoldBet, Better, Betflag, and Planetwin365 brands.

Source: INTRM (7 May), ARS (16 Apr)

  • Products: iSports (sports betting, virtual, horse racing); iGaming (online casino, slots, poker, bingo, betting exchange, skill games). Revenue basis: Gross Gaming Revenue = total bets minus player winnings. [ARS • 18 Mar]
  • Online EBITDA margin: 57.5% (Q1 2026, up from 53.6% in Q1 2025). Materially above blended group margin of 39%. Every shift in revenue mix toward online directly expands the group blended margin. [INTRM • 7 May]
  • Market share: 32.2% online market share in Q1 2026 — highest on record. iGaming share +1.9pp YoY; iSports share +0.7pp YoY. Total online bets +15% YoY to €8.49bn. [Deutsche Bank Research • 6 May; Jefferies Research • 6 May]
  • Brand portfolio: Multi-brand strategy (Goldbet, Betflag, Lottomatica, Totosì, Planetwin365) allows targeted demographic segmentation across sports betting profiles. [Expert Call • 25 Jun 25; Rothschild & Co Redburn • 23 Jan]

2.2 Sports Franchise Segment

Physical retail betting network operating 3,804 operating PoS as of March 31, 2026.

Sources: INTRM (7 May), Jefferies Research (22 Apr)

  • Revenue model: Fixed-odds sports betting, virtual sports, horserace betting through terrestrial franchise network. Secular headwinds from online cannibalisation but managed through in-shop digital migration (tablet-based betting terminals). [#I]
  • Margin: EBITDA margin 24.6% (Q1 2026, down from 30.4% in Q1 2025) — payout-driven volatility plus secular volume decline at retail level offsets any revenue growth at the margin line. [#A]
  • Omnichannel advantage: Physical retail locations function as customer acquisition funnels for the more profitable online platforms. Omnichannel customers generate 3.6x higher lifetime value than pure online players. [Deutsche Bank Research • 1 Mar 24]

2.3 Gaming Franchise Segment

Concessionary management of AWP gaming machines and gaming halls.

Source: INTRM (7 May)

  • Assets: 50,785 owned AWP machines; 19,831 VLT rights; 123 gaming halls under direct management. [INTRM • 7 May]
  • Revenue model: State-mandated ADM concession basis. Revenue legally licensed and regulated; contractual floor. AWP machines provide a quasi-utility revenue stream that is structurally predictable. [#I]
  • Secular pressure: Italian AWP machine volumes declining ~2-3% pa. Total gaming machine GGR has declined ~25% since pre-COVID levels to approximately €7.8bn (2025). Secular managed decline — well-understood and priced in. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Margin: EBITDA margin 24.8% (Q1 2026) — stable contribution through fixed-fee ADM concession structure. [#A]

2.4 Lottery Operations Context

While "Lottomatica" is historically synonymous with Italian lotteries, the exclusive management of Italian Lotto and Scratch & Win (Gratta e Vinci) is held by Lottoitalia s.r.l., in which Brightstar Lottery (formerly IGT) is majority shareholder. Allwyn holds a 32.5% stake. Lottomatica Group SpA focuses on the Online, Sports, and Gaming Franchise segments — not the direct management of state lotteries.

Sources: INTRM (6 May), ARS (29 Apr, 7 May)

2.5 Competitive Advantage — Proprietary Technology

  • "Lottomatica Core": Unified technology and data platform allowing superior innovation and scalability. Includes internal game studio development. Proprietary tech = no "value leakage" to third-party technology providers (unlike peers). Enables high online EBITDA margins vs sector average. [Transcript - Earnings Calls • 7 May 25, 30 Oct 24; Jefferies Research • 6 Aug 23]
  • 17,000+ physical PoS network: Irreplaceable customer acquisition channel in Italy's advertising-restricted environment (Decreto Dignità 2018 bans gambling advertising). New entrants and online-only operators (bet365, William Hill) are structurally disadvantaged without this network. [INTRM • 7 May; Rothschild & Co Redburn • 23 Jan]
  • Brand recognition: "Lottomatica" brand has near-universal recognition in Italy (40+ years as the country's lottery operator). Brand = lowest-cost customer acquisition channel. Harder to quantify as a moat but observable in customer stickiness and market share trajectory. [#J]

Sceptical bullet (§2): Italy-only operator has no geographic buffer against regulatory or competitive shocks. If Italian regulatory policy tightens materially (advertising further restricted, tax rates raised, AWP machine count reduced), LTM has no geographic offset. The 2018 Decreto Dignità advertising ban was passed as a surprise policy tightening; a repeat is not inconceivable. Additionally, Flutter's April 2025 Snaitech acquisition and SEA platform migration potentially gives the #2 operator global AI/data analytics capabilities that LTM's Italian-built stack may not match at scale over a 3-5 year horizon. [#A]

§3 Industry Context

Net signal: Italian online gaming market is the largest in the EU and is undergoing structural consolidation that permanently favours scale operators. Post-November 2025 ADM overhaul: 52 licensed domains, 46 operators, 9-year concession terms. Online market growing +15.8% in 2025. LTM + Flutter now control >60% of the online market in an effective regulated duopoly. [#J]

3.1 Italian Market Structure

  • Total market scale: Italian regulated gaming total GGR ~€20bn+ (land-based + online); online segment ~€5.1bn GGR (2025). Online growing at +15.8% casino, +20%+ sports betting. Mobile penetration driving younger demographic adoption. [#A]
  • Post-November 2025 overhaul: 407 licensed domains → 52 concessions. Effective operator count: ~46. Drastic consolidation in one regulatory step — this is a structural market redefinition event with no comparable precedent in European gaming history. Customers from delisted operators migrating to licensed platforms — structural demand transfer to LTM. [#A]
  • Duopoly formation: LTM #1 online (32.2% share, Q1 2026); Flutter Italy (Sisal + Snaitech) #2 (~26.5% combined). Top 2 players hold >58% of online market. Effective duopoly in practice — entrenching annually. [#A]
  • Online penetration gap: Italian online gaming penetration ~35-36% vs European average >50%. Structural convergence toward EU average represents a multi-year demand tailwind independent of market share gains. [Rothschild & Co Redburn • 17 Apr]

3.2 Regulatory Framework and Online Concessions

A new licensing regime became effective November 13, 2025, valid for nine years.

Source: 10Q (6 May), Morgan Stanley (20 May)

  • Licence cost: €7m per brand — a step-change from ~€200,000 previously. Filters out operators with revenues below €50m. Acts as a near-prohibitive barrier to new entry for the 9-year concession term to 2034. [Morgan Stanley • 14 May 25; Expert Call • 14 Apr 25]
  • Concentration caps: Draft framework introduces explicit ownership limits. LTM expected to bid for maximum permitted share (up to 40% for sports franchises; 34% for gaming machines). [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Lottomatica position: Secured 5 concessions through GBO Italy, Betflag, PWO, and Totosì — the full complement of its brand portfolio. [ARS • 16 Apr]

[AS·Morgan Stanley]: "New online licensing tender in 2025. The tender for online concessions is already established, with bids expected between 1st March and 30th May 2025, approximately one month expected for processing and a go-live date no later than 17 September. The new licences cost €7m per brand, with a maximum of 5 brands per operator, along with other technical guarantees and requirements." — Morgan Stanley, 14 May 25

3.3 Retail Reorganisation Outlook (2028)

  • Timeline: New retail concessions expected in 2028, with staged payments over 2028-2029. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Betting concessions: ~400 lots of 25 rights each at approximately €1.40-1.45m per lot. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Gaming machines: Auctioned in €25m lots (4,000 AWPs + 920 VLTs each). Nationwide machine count reducing to ~200,000 AWPs and 46,000 VLTs. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May]
  • Projected LTM spend: Analysts estimate approximately €600-690m to maintain leading market position under the new retail framework — a significant FCF headwind in 2028-2029. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May; Expert Call • 8 Apr]

§4.1 TC1 — Operator Quality

  • CEO Guglielmo Angelozzi: Since 2014 (11+ years at time of writing). Survived Apollo's full M&A reconstruction, 3 major acquisitions, IPO, and PE exit. Unusual longevity in PE-backed gaming sector where CEO turnover is common. [#A]
  • CFO Laurence Van Lancker: Former Credit Suisse TMT and Gaming investment banker; instrumental in the group's listing and major acquisitions. Financial structuring expertise is directly relevant to LTM's complex capital allocation environment. [ESG reports • 17 Apr]
  • Disciplined M&A: 57 targets screened over 5 years, 3 completed = ≤5% deal completion rate. Unusually disciplined for a sector prone to empire-building. Each deal added Italian online scale — no conglomerate diversification. [#J]
  • Guidance posture: Four consecutive guidance beat/raise events since IPO (May 2023). Conservative guidance buffering is established. FY2024 EBITDA outturn 1.2% above top of range; FY2025 beat original guidance; Q1 2026 EBITDA +2.2% vs consensus; FY2026 guidance raised to top of range post-Q1. [#J]
  • Capital allocation discipline: Post-leverage (3.7x → 2.4x), now returning capital. Buyback activation (12.5% shares, 18 months) at 2.4x leverage signals confidence in FCF durability. Priority sequence: leverage maintenance → organic growth → capital return → selective M&A. [#A]
  • Governance concern — CEO/Chairman combination: From July 2025, Angelozzi holds both CEO and Chairman roles. Non-standard structure in European corporate governance context. Reduces board-level independence and oversight. Regulatory pressure from Borsa Italiana or institutional investors to separate roles is a credible medium-term risk. Flag for ESA investigation. [#A]

§4.2 TC2 — Advantaged Business + SRCA

  • Regulatory moat — hard barrier: ADM concession holder. Post-November 2025 overhaul, new licenses require €7m fee, 9-year term, stringent compliance. 8+ years remaining on LTM's current concession. This is the most durable moat in the business — not replicable by capital alone. [#J]
  • Brand moat: "Lottomatica" brand = Italy's most recognised gambling brand (40+ years as lottery operator). Customer account-based stickiness; loyalty points; multi-product cross-sell. Hard to quantify NPS publicly; churn rate not published; observable in market share trajectory. [#A]
  • Scale moat: Processing €44.7bn in bets = infrastructure, data, and risk management scale advantages that accrue naturally. Largest Italian book provides best-odds capability in sports betting. Scale operating leverage means fixed compliance costs spread across larger revenue base than competitors. [#J]
  • TAM: Italian online gaming projected €5.5bn+ GGR by 2026. Land-based adds ~€14-15bn. Total TAM ~€20bn. LTM captures ~11-12% of total Italian regulated GGR — meaningful room for online share growth. [#A]

§4.3 TC3 — Value Chain (IG Summary Level)

  • Customer side: Strong digital retention for online (account-based, bonus-funded, loyalty-driven). Post-November 2025 regulatory framework strengthens online by reducing operator count and reducing player confusion across legitimate operators. Omnichannel customers (3.6x LTV) are the highest-quality cohort. [#A]
  • Competition side: Duopoly dynamic (LTM + Flutter) strengthening. Moderate competitive intensity in iGaming; higher intensity in sports betting where Flutter's global capabilities (Flutter Edge data analytics) are a genuine advantage. Smaller competitors (Entain, Evoke) losing share structurally. [#A]
  • Supplier side: Technology vendors have meaningful power in online segment; LTM has partially internalised through Betflag/SKS365 product stacks and "Lottomatica Core." AWP machine capex managed through equipment manufacturers (Aristocrat, IGT). [#A]

6.3 Key Risks Forward

  • Single-country exposure: 100% Italy concentration creates vulnerability to localised regulatory changes or macroeconomic downturns. Morgan Stanley applies an explicit single-country discount in relative valuation methodology. [Morgan Stanley • 6 May]
  • Retail concession renewal costs: Estimated >€600m upfront for retail concessions in 2028-2029, creating FCF headwind in those years. This will substantially consume the free cash flow generation that is currently being deployed for buybacks. [Morgan Stanley • 20 May; Expert Call • 8 Apr]
  • Flutter competitive sharpening: SEA platform migration completed April 2025 gives Flutter a globally competitive tech stack in Italy. The 3-5 year risk of Flutter's AI/analytics advantage eroding LTM's online market share lead is real. [Expert Call • 8 Apr; #A]
  • Revenue growth deceleration: +23% (FY2024) → +10% (FY2025) → guided +6-9% (FY2026). Step-down each year. If organic growth approaches 5% pa, EPS growth becomes primarily leverage-driven (not sustainable). [#A]
