Section summary
  1. 1&1 is navigating a high-risk transition to a network operator, offering massive equity upside if consolidation or wholesale pivots occur.

1.
Transformative network build: 1&1 is shifting from an asset-light MVNO to constructing Europe's first fully virtualised OpenRAN 5G network
2.
Structural simplification: The recent internal acquisition of 1&1 Versatel integrates a 68,500km fibre-optic network to backhaul new mobile sites
3.
Ownership consolidation: Parent UTDI increased its stake to 86.46% following a tender offer, fortifying control ahead of peak network investments

Top key drivers

4.
Top driver: German market consolidation: A potential merger with Telefonica Deutschland would unlock up to €7bn in synergies, forming the highest-impact upside catalyst
5.
Mid-tier driver: Vodafone wholesale agreement: Shifting national roaming to Vodafone secures interim coverage, but high capacity-based wholesale payments caused a recent EBITDA downgrade
6.
Mid-tier driver: Low-band spectrum access: The lack of sub-1GHz frequencies remains unresolved, presenting a material hurdle for competitive indoor network coverage against incumbents
7.
Bottom driver: Broadband commercial momentum: Management expects broadband net additions to turn positive, aided by FTTH expansion through city carriers and Deutsche Telekom
8.
Bottom driver: Capex normalisation: Network deployment costs are peaking, but projected €500-550m cash capex for 2026 leaves room for robust free cash flow generation

10.
Pressured margin delivery: Q1 2026 EBITDA of €192.4m fell 8.0% short of Deutsche Bank estimates, hampered by elevated network rollout expenses

Forward credibility

11.

12.
Aggressive mid-term targets: The company targets €800m EBITDA for 2026, alongside commitments for €100m annual EBITDA growth in 2027 and 2028
