  • Operating leverage: Tower businesses have strong theoretical operating leverage — incremental tenancy additions at near-100% contribution margin; consensus models ~50-70bps EBITDA margin expansion per 1% revenue growth [#A]

  • One-off vs structural: EBITDA margin improvements are structural (operating leverage + power cost management); FCF improvement is path-dependent (requires debt paydown or refinancing) [#J]

  • Sceptical close: The bearish read on margin outlook: the EBITDA margin story is broadly credible, but it is irrelevant to equity valuation when net leverage is 5x+ and FCF barely covers interest. The margin debate that matters is FCF margin, not EBITDA margin. Consensus FCF projections ($0-20m in FY26E turning to $50-100m in FY28E) are achievable on paper but require interest burden reduction — which requires either debt paydown (from cashflow that doesn't yet exist in scale) or refinancing at lower rates (difficult in the current rate environment).
