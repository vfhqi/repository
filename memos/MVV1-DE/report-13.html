§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

J-front: The assumption stack behind the FY2028 target is moderately vulnerable, with procurement cost deflation and heat volume growth being the two most fragile pillars; a simultaneous adverse move in both would reduce FY2028 EBIT by ~15-20% [#J]

  • Sub: Comparable French and Dutch district heating operators have faced municipal pressure to limit tariff increases to CPI-1%; MVV guidance does not embed this scenario

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Heat network connection growth of ~3-4% p.a. embedded; this requires active municipal planning support and customer willingness to connect vs. individual heat pump alternatives [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Waste-to-energy (WtE) throughput assumed stable-to-growing; German waste volumes are structurally supported by EU circular economy mandates; lower near-term risk than heat network assumptions [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Biomass and gas procurement cost deflation of ~5-10% from FY2024 peaks is embedded in the EBIT margin expansion path; if commodity costs stabilise at FY2024 levels rather than declining, the margin expansion thesis fails [#A]

  • Sub: Metzler's sensitivity note (Feb-26) estimated that flat gas procurement costs vs guided deflation would reduce FY2026 EBIT by ~€12-15m [AS-Metzler]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage on incremental heat network revenue is assumed at ~40-45% contribution margin; this is higher than the current segment average of ~35%, implying scale benefits that have not yet been demonstrated [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: District heat pass-through assumption is ~90% of procurement cost changes; in FY2024, actual pass-through was closer to 80% due to contractual lags — a persistent 10pp gap compounds over the planning horizon [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumed ~28-30%, consistent with the German statutory rate; no aggressive tax planning assumptions embedded; low sensitivity [#I]

  • EPS assumption — minority interest: Minority interest (MVV Umwelt, Stadtwerke subsidiaries) assumed ~€18-22m p.a.; this has been historically variable and represents ~10% of net income — a source of EPS noise not reflected in EBIT guidance [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt expected to rise to ~€900m-1.0bn by FY2027 as the CapEx cycle peaks; interest cost assumptions embed ~3.5-4.0% weighted average interest rate — broadly consistent with current German utility refinancing rates [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Gas cost assumption is the single most critical macro variable; management embeds a gradual normalisation of TTF gas prices toward €30-35/MWh by FY2026-FY2027; current forward curve (May-26) implies €32-38/MWh, suggesting modest upside risk to assumed costs [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Minimal currency exposure; MVV operations are predominantly German; UK/international exposure is negligible; EUR/USD is not a material guidance variable [#I]
