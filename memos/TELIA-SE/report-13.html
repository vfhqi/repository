§1 — Current Management Guidance

Telia's current guidance framework anchors the FY25 year and the medium-term CMD corridor through FY26, issued progressively across the FY24 full-year results (February 2025) and the Q1 2025 update (April/May 2025). Management has maintained the FY25 guidance corridor without revision as of the most recent update.

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management guides FY25 EBITDA (excl. IFRS 16) to grow "low-single-digit %" YoY in constant currency, implying approximately SEK 18.5-19.0bn vs FY24 reported ~SEK 18.1bn [#A]
  • Sub: Constant-currency framing excludes SEK/NOK/EUR translation noise — a meaningful caveat given ~35% of revenue is non-SEK

  • Sub: "Low-single-digit" provides a corridor rather than a point estimate, limiting precision

  • FY current — revenue: Revenue guidance for FY25 targets "stable to modest organic growth" in service revenue — management explicitly avoids a percentage range, instead anchoring narrative on Swedish market stabilisation and Nordic B2B momentum [#A]

  • Sub: Absence of a revenue percentage range is a deliberate management communication choice, reducing accountability

  • Sub: Total reported revenue will be depressed by ongoing wholesale contract attrition and handset revenue mix shift

  • FY current — FCF: Management guides FY25 equity free cash flow (eFCF) to "improve vs FY24" — FY24 eFCF was approximately SEK 5.2-5.5bn; implied trajectory suggests SEK 5.5-6.0bn target range [#A]

  • Sub: eFCF definition excludes spectrum payments, lease payments, and pension contributions — definition diverges from headline FCF

  • Sub: Q1 2025 eFCF run-rate was weak (seasonal pattern); H2-loading of FCF delivery is structurally embedded

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx-to-revenue guided at approximately 15-16% for FY25 (consistent with FY24 ~15.7%), implying continued 5G rollout investment without a step-down; management targets below-15% CapEx intensity by FY26 [#A]

  • FY current — dividend: Dividend guidance of SEK 2.00/share for FY25 maintained — yield approximately 6.5-7.0% at current price; management frames this as a "floor not ceiling" post the FY22-FY23 restructuring period [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y: FY26 CMD targets include: EBITDA margin (excl. IFRS 16) in the "low-to-mid 30s %" range — current FY24 margin approximately 31-32%; implies 150-300bps expansion over two years [#A]

  • Sub: EBITDA margin expansion relies on: Swedish convergence uptake, B2B enterprise growth, cost efficiency programme SEK 1.0bn+ savings

  • Sub: No explicit FY26 revenue growth target stated — management avoids top-line commitment beyond current fiscal

  • Medium-term — N3Y: Management has not issued explicit FY27+ guidance; medium-term narrative references "sustainable mid-single-digit EBITDA growth" post-FY26 CMD delivery — this is aspirational framing, not a guided target [#A]

  • Source — earnings call: Primary source: FY24 full-year results earnings call (February 2025) + Q1 2025 results call (April/May 2025) — guidance maintained at both; no in-year upgrades or downgrades issued [#A]

  • Source — investor day: CMD 2023 (held November 2023) established the multi-year EBITDA margin and CapEx corridor that underpins current FY25-FY26 guidance; next CMD not scheduled as of May 2025 [#A]

  • Caveat — M&A: All guidance is stated "excluding material M&A" — Telia has been a net portfolio simplifier (exiting Eurasian assets FY19-FY22), but any bolt-on Nordic acquisition could reset CapEx and integration cost assumptions [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — Swedish market: Revenue guidance explicitly conditioned on Swedish telecom market "not deteriorating further" from current levels — management's own language acknowledges material top-line sensitivity to domestic competitive dynamics [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §1: The bearish read: "stable to modest organic growth" for revenue is so wide as to be unfalsifiable within a single fiscal year — management can claim guidance delivery across a wide range of outcomes; the deliberate absence of a percentage range for revenue may be designed to avoid accountability rather than reflect genuine uncertainty about the Swedish market trajectory [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Verdict bullet: Management exhibits a split personality: mild sandbagger on EBITDA (guides conservatively, delivers at or above), but honest-tracker-to-slight-miss on revenue and a structurally optimistic (over-promiser) framer on FCF — the FCF over-optimism is the most actionable pattern for investors [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: EBITDA corridor guidance has been set at or slightly below what management's internal plans target — consistent with the European telco incumbent playbook where EBITDA is the primary sell-side anchoring metric and management protects credibility on the metric most directly linked to dividend sustainability [#A]

  • Sub: 75% EBITDA guidance achievement rate is above the peer median but not statistically remarkable — Proximus and Swisscom achieve comparable rates

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: FCF guidance language ("improvement vs prior year", "above SEK X.Xbn") consistently sets a positive directional expectation that is then underdelivered; management's eFCF definition excludes items (spectrum, leases, pensions) that are economically real — the gap between guided eFCF and true shareholder FCF is systematically understated [#A]

  • Sub: FY2021 and FY2022 FCF misses both originated from items explicitly excluded from the eFCF definition — suggesting the definition itself is the over-promising mechanism

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue guidance language has evolved to progressively vaguer framing ("stable to modest", "returning to growth") precisely because management missed concrete revenue guidance in FY2021-FY2022; the widening of the corridor is a credibility management strategy, not a reflection of genuinely higher uncertainty [#A]

  • Pattern shift: Pre-FY2021, Telia issued more quantified revenue guidance corridors (e.g., "+2-4% organic"); post-FY2022 misses, management shifted to qualitative framing — this is a deliberate pattern shift that reduces near-term miss risk while providing less investor information content [#A]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA: 3 of 4 periods beat or met EBITDA guidance; the one miss (FY2022, -1.2%) was driven by a specific Swedish consumer repricing cycle and was acknowledged as a one-time structural adjustment period in management commentary [#A]

  • Miss consistency — FCF: 3 of 4 periods missed or underdelivered eFCF guidance; management's stated reason shifts across periods (spectrum in FY2021, working capital in FY2022, timing in FY2024) — the rotating explanation for FCF misses suggests a structural FCF modelling weakness rather than a series of independent one-off events [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat (EBITDA): EBITDA beats where they occurred averaged approximately +SEK 0.2-0.4bn above guidance midpoint — these are not large beats; management is not leaving substantial excess on the table, suggesting genuine corridor guidance rather than aggressive sandbagging [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss : FCF misses where they occurred averaged approximately -7-10% below guided trajectory — meaningful relative to the absolute FCF quantum and dividend coverage arithmetic; a sustained -10% FCF miss would compress covered pay-out ratio from ~2x to ~1.8x [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §3: The bearish read on pattern: EBITDA sandbagging cushion is thinning as cost programme savings diminish post-FY25 — the easy opex wins (Eurasian overhead removal, workforce rightsizing) are largely banked; without a new structural cost lever, EBITDA guidance will shift from mild-sandbagger to honest-tracker, increasing miss probability; meanwhile FCF over-optimism shows no sign of structural correction despite repeated misses [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Verdict bullet: Guidance accuracy trend is modestly improving on EBITDA and revenue since the FY2021-FY2022 nadir, but FCF accuracy has not structurally improved — the trend is directionally positive but fragile, driven more by management's deliberate shift to vaguer framing than by improved predictive capability [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: FY2023 and FY2024 EBITDA both delivered at or above guidance midpoint after the FY2022 minor miss — a two-period improvement streak that analysts cite as evidence of improved cost discipline; this is consistent with the cost efficiency programme delivering as targeted [#A]

  • Historical accuracy — FY2021-FY2022: The FY2021-FY2022 period represents the nadir of Telia's guidance credibility — both revenue and FCF missed materially, management was managing through Eurasian asset exits, and the Swedish market entered a sustained downcycle; this period should be contextually discounted but not ignored as a stress-test scenario [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — mechanism: Post-FY2022 improvement in guidance accuracy driven by three factors: (1) completion of Eurasian portfolio exit (removes the largest source of guided-vs-actual FCF noise), (2) CMD 2023 establishing a more conservative guidance corridor, (3) management's shift to qualitative revenue framing reducing measurable miss events [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: FX translation volatility (SEK/EUR, SEK/NOK) has been an underappreciated accuracy driver — guidance issued in constant currency but reported in SEK; a 5% SEK strengthening vs. EUR can create a 1.5-2% reported EBITDA deviation from constant-currency guidance without any operational performance difference [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: Execution-driven misses (B2B enterprise deal slippage, fibre rollout cost overruns) contributed to FY2021 and FY2022 misses — FY2023-FY2024 have been cleaner on execution, though B2B deal timing remains lumpy and creates quarterly noise [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: CapEx intensity — consistently within 20-30bps of guided range across all 4 periods; reflects management's high degree of control over discretionary investment phasing [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Equity FCF — widest variance across all 4 periods; reflects the multiple items excluded from management's eFCF definition that nonetheless consume cash; FCF accuracy is structurally impaired by the definition gap [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — caution: The apparent FY2023-FY2024 improvement coincides with management progressively widening guidance language — it is partially a statistical artefact of moving goalposts rather than genuine forecasting improvement [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet — §4: The bearish read on accuracy trend: management's communication shift to qualitative revenue guidance (avoiding a percentage corridor) means the "improvement" in revenue accuracy since FY2023 is partly manufactured by removing the precise target that could be measured as a miss; if management were required to issue a ±1pp revenue corridor in FY25, the miss probability would be meaningfully higher than the current vague guidance framing implies [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Verdict bullet: The guidance assumption stack carries three independently fragile pillars — Swedish ARPU recovery, Nordic B2B enterprise momentum, and FCF conversion — any two of which deteriorating simultaneously would push group EBITDA below the guided corridor and eFCF materially below the guided trajectory [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing (Sweden): Management assumes gradual Swedish consumer ARPU stabilisation and modest recovery through convergence product uptake; Swedish ARPU has been declining ~1-2% annually since 2021; the assumption requires the competitive environment to stop intensifying — Tele2 and Three Sweden have been pricing aggressively in mobile, and there is no structural sign this abates in FY25 [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — customer (B2B): Nordic B2B enterprise pipeline assumed to close at mid-single-digit growth in managed services and connectivity — B2B deal timing is inherently lumpy; management has guided this segment at mid-single-digit every year L3Y and delivered low-to-mid single digit; the assumption is directionally credible but not precisely so [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market (Baltic): Baltic region (Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania) assumed to grow mid-single-digit organically — this has been the one segment where management guidance has consistently been met or exceeded; Baltic growth assumption is the least fragile in the stack [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: SEK 1.0bn+ cost efficiency programme assumed to deliver in FY25-FY26 — programme targets headcount reduction, network sharing, IT transformation; SEK ~0.6bn delivered cumulatively through FY24; remaining SEK 0.4bn+ dependent on IT system migration success and union negotiations in Sweden [#A]

  • Sub: IT system migration risk is the least visible cost assumption — delays could defer savings by 12-18 months

  • Margin assumption — operating leverage: EBITDA margin expansion from ~31-32% to low-mid 30s% requires revenue growth to outpace opex growth; if revenue is flat (as SS models), margin expansion requires pure cost reduction rather than operating leverage — a harder path that management guidance implicitly conflates [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Telia's effective tax rate approximately 20-22%; Swedish corporate tax regime stable; no unusual tax assumption embedded in guidance — this is the least fragile assumption in the EPS stack [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count stable — no active buyback programme beyond scrip dividend mitigation; dilution from LTIP is modest (~0.2-0.3% annually); share count assumption is clean and conservative [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt / EBITDA guidance implies maintaining ~2.0-2.5x leverage — management has committed to not pursuing leveraged M&A; leverage assumption is contingent on EBITDA delivery and no material spectrum auction outflows beyond FY25 [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Guidance issued in constant currency — SEK has been volatile vs EUR and NOK; every 5% SEK appreciation vs EUR reduces reported EBITDA by approximately 1.0-1.5% vs guided constant-currency figure; currency is a systematic reporting-vs-guidance noise factor [#A]

  • Macro assumption — interest rates: Telia carries approximately SEK 45-50bn gross debt; a sustained 50bps increase in blended funding cost adds ~SEK 220-250m annual interest expense; partially mitigated by fixed-rate hedging (~60% of debt fixed) but refinancing risk exists as maturities roll in FY26-FY28 [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Verdict bullet: Overall guidance credibility: MEDIUM — management reliably delivers on EBITDA and CapEx guidance but carries a demonstrated history of FCF over-optimism and revenue over-confidence; current guidance framework is better-calibrated than the FY2021-FY2022 era but has not fully addressed the structural FCF definition gap that is the primary credibility damage vector [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — credible on EBITDA and cost programme delivery; less credible on FCF and revenue top-line; significantly less credible on the precise timing of margin expansion vs the general direction [#A]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY2023 EBITDA delivery (SEK 17.9bn vs SEK 17.5-18.0bn guided) demonstrated that management can use cost lever to deliver when top-line disappoints — this is a genuine credibility positive: management showed willingness to offset revenue softness with cost action [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption (CapEx): CapEx intensity guided at 15-16% has been delivered within range for 4 consecutive periods — management has strong grip on the most controllable part of the FCF bridge; this is the cleanest credibility positive in the guidance set [#A]

  • Green flag — portfolio simplification: Completion of Eurasian asset exits (2019-2022) eliminated the largest structural source of guidance uncertainty — FY2023-FY2024 guidance is materially cleaner than the multi-geography era; credibility baseline has genuinely improved structurally [#A]
