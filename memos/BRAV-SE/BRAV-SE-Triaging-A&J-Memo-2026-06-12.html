Executive Summary -- We are looking for a clear, conviction-carrying statement of the investment thesis, key ratings, and actionable conclusion; avoiding vague or overly hedged language.

BLUF

1.
Market-turn timing call: Bravida is a multi-technical installation and service consolidator sitting at what DNB Carnegie characterises as the start of a multi-year Nordic construction recovery. The organic growth inflection has already occurred (+1% in Q1 2026 versus consensus -0.5%), Denmark has been successfully turned around to a 5% EBITA margin, and the Swedish restructuring is on track to deliver SEK 65m in annualised savings. The investment case is real, but the timing component carries genuine risk: Deutsche Bank holds at SEK 106 precisely because organic recovery in late-cycle installation lags macro improvements by 6-12 months, and Southern Sweden plus Finland remain weak.
2.
The organic inflection is real but narrow: Late 2025 net sales stability relied on acquired growth to mask a circa -3% organic contraction (confirmed in the Risks report ACH analysis). Q1 2026's +1% organic therefore represents an inflection from -3% to +1% — genuine but early-stage. The FALSE FRIEND profile (M&A masking organic decay) is explicitly rated by the Risks report as the ACH winner with the fewest hard inconsistencies. The recovery thesis requires quarterly confirmation, not a single data point.
3.
Structural tailwinds are durable and unambiguous: EU Green Deal mandates, electrification, data-centre build-out, defence infrastructure, and Nordic energy renovation requirements provide a multi-decade demand backdrop broadly independent of cyclical construction. The 49% service mix (rising toward 60% in Norway) cushions installation troughs and provides recurring base-load earnings that grew 8% even during the 2024 downturn.
4.
Governance risk is the primary discount factor: The Region Skane over-invoicing scandal (April 2024, validated by EY audit, resulting in criminal convictions) exposed a structural flaw in the decentralised branch model. The Swedish divisional merger addresses the symptom but the underlying tension between local autonomy and central oversight is intrinsic to Bravida's operating model. SEK 1.2bn in disputed receivables across Denmark and Norway (arbitration 2028) represent a material, unresolved tail risk.
5.
Self-help programme is the near-term earnings lever: The SEK 65m run-rate savings from Swedish centralisation and the SEK 200m ABEKA EL divestment proceed entirely within management control. Combined with the share buyback initiation, these actions signal management is using the trough period to structurally improve the cost base. Cash conversion normalisation from current 77% back toward the historical 99% average is the single largest near-term catalyst.
6.
Cohort peer BRKN-CH trades at 20.7x EV/EBITDA versus Bravida at 9.5x: Burkhalter commands a massive premium for Swiss domestic-only, ESG-mandated execution. Bravida's discount suggests material upside if the organic recovery thesis plays through; it also reflects the governance and late-cycle overhang that peers without decentralised risk profiles do not carry.

E1 Required Input Change Forces
Required Input Change Forces -- We are looking for credible external tailwinds (regulatory, structural, or cyclical) AND active internal change programmes (turnaround, restructuring, re-acceleration); both must be real and sufficiently strong. Avoiding cases where change is incremental or forced.
RA1 Change Forces Present?

E1 RA1 — Change forces present summary: Bravida has a credible set of both external structural tailwinds and internal self-help change forces that can plausibly drive above-market EPS growth over a 3-5 year horizon. The external forces are regulatory and structural in nature rather than merely cyclical, and the internal forces are management-initiated and already partly evidenced in reported numbers.

Strong External Change Forces / Tailwinds?
1.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive tailwind: The EPBD legally mandates progressive energy efficiency upgrades across European building stock with specific phase-in deadlines creating a non-deferrable, multi-year installation pipeline. Nordic properties are disproportionately affected given existing building stock age and climate-driven heating requirements, directly loading Bravida's service backlog with structurally guaranteed demand that cannot be deferred without regulatory penalty.
2.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — Electrification and grid upgrade tailwind: The structural transition to electric vehicles, heat pumps, and renewable energy generation requires massive ongoing electrical installation and grid reinforcement work across all four of Bravida's Nordic markets. This demand is independent of the residential construction cycle and tied to energy policy trajectories with a decade or more of runway, providing structural decoupling from the construction cycle trough.
3.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — Data-centre build-out tailwind: The AI computing boom is driving documented shortages of data-centre capacity across Northern Europe. These facilities require specialist electrical, cooling, and HVAC installations at a complexity level that rewards scale operators like Bravida over local generalists. Management explicitly identified data centres alongside defence and public infrastructure as the three structural demand vectors offsetting residential weakness in current earnings commentary.
4.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — Defence and public infrastructure tailwind: Nordic defence spending has risen sharply following geopolitical developments. Military facility upgrades, hardened infrastructure, and public-sector capital programmes provide a growing project pipeline that is politically driven and largely non-discretionary, providing multi-year forward demand visibility decoupled from consumer and business confidence cycles.
5.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — Nordic market stabilisation and incipient volume recovery: Third-party forecasts project 4% annual installation market volume growth in both 2026 and 2027, following a period of severe contraction. Q1 2026 organic growth of +1% (versus consensus -0.5%) provides the first empirical confirmation the volume trough has passed. Order intake grew 5% year-on-year in Q1 2026, pushing the total backlog up 7% to SEK 16.7bn.
6.
E1 RA1 CQ1 — Fragmented market consolidation opportunity provides a structural growth overlay independent of organic cycles: With over 25,000 operators and the top five players controlling only 18% of the Nordic market (versus 39% in mature markets like France), Bravida operates in a structurally under-consolidated sector. This provides a multi-decade M&A compounding runway at attractive multiples of 5-6x EBITA pre-synergies, generating growth independent of organic demand cycles.
Strong Internal Change Forces?
1.
E1 RA1 CQ2 — Danish turnaround already evidenced in reported numbers: The single most important internal change force is the restoration of Danish EBITA margins from near-zero in 2023 to 5.0% in Q1 2026 with 10% organic growth. DNB Carnegie estimates this single driver contributed roughly half of the 2025 EPS improvement. A new CEO (Christian Also, installed May 2024), tighter contract negotiations, and completion of legacy loss-making projects were the mechanisms. The turnaround is not speculative; it is visible in current quarterly delivery.
2.
E1 RA1 CQ2 — Swedish organisational consolidation targeting SEK 65m in run-rate annual savings: The merger of three Swedish divisions into a single national entity effective January 2026 under Lars Tauber targets SEK 65m in annual run-rate savings once fully implemented. Upfront charges of SEK 20m in Q4 2025 and SEK 70-90m in 2026 are already guided and being charged. Underlying Q1 2026 margin improved +40bps ex-restructuring, confirming savings are beginning to flow through the P&L on schedule.
3.
E1 RA1 CQ2 — M&A flywheel re-acceleration with exceptional balance sheet underpinning: Acquisition pace deliberately dropped to four deals in 2025 amid poor target quality. Management now signals a return to historical acquisition volumes in 2026 with an improved pipeline, newly appointed Head of M&A (Alexandra Johansson, March 2026), and 1.1x net debt / EBITDA providing massive headroom under the 2.5x ceiling. Over 166 deals executed since 2014 at 5-6x EBITA multiples confirms the repeatable nature of this compounding engine through multiple cycles.
4.
E1 RA1 CQ2 — ABEKA EL divestment sharpens portfolio and recycles capital: The April 2026 sale of non-core ABEKA EL for SEK 200m sharpens the portfolio around Bravida's core multi-technical competency and recycles capital to higher-return segments. It demonstrates management willingness to prune as well as acquire, a signal of capital allocation discipline.
5.
E1 RA1 CQ2 — Share buyback initiation signals board confidence in intrinsic value: The initiation of a SEK 100m buyback in May 2026 alongside the SEK 3.80 dividend signals board confidence that the balance sheet is over-capitalised at 1.1x leverage. E1 RA1 CQ2 — Service mix expansion towards higher-quality recurring revenue compounds over time: Norway service mix has reached 60% of regional net sales, and group-wide service penetration at 49% is actively rising. Each percentage point of mix shift toward service improves through-cycle margin floor and reduces earnings volatility, representing both a current-period quality improvement and a structural change force that compounds with time.
