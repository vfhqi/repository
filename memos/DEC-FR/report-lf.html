EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 29 findings

Co-CEO career trajectory and prior operational track record

1.
Both co-CEOs followed parallel operator paths through decades of hands-on geographic expansion. Jean-François Decaux joined the group in 1982, establishing the critical German subsidiary as his first operational role, subsequently directing expansion into Northern Europe, North America, and Australia. Jean-Charles Decaux entered the family business in 1989, was appointed CEO of JCDecaux Spain in 1991 at age 22, and successfully established JCDecaux's presence across Asia, South America, and the Middle East
2.
Both brothers possess over 35 years of continuous operational tenure within the global outdoor advertising sector
3.
Their early international subsidiary setups demonstrated entrepreneurial market entry force, with Jean-François establishing JCDecaux Germany in 1982 as a greenfield startup and Jean-Charles building JCDecaux Spain from its 1991 CEO appointment into a top-tier regional operator, scaling it into a regional market leader via organic growth prior to sector consolidation
4.
Jean-Charles has served on the board of French private equity firm Eurazeo since June 2017, became Chairman of Eurazeo's Supervisory Board in April 2022, and oversaw Eurazeo's transition into a diversified third-party asset manager reaching €31bn in assets, monitoring record fundraising of €6bn in sustainability assets while maintaining a 100% attendance rate across board and committee sessions in 2025
5.
Unlike JCDecaux's operator-founders, major international peers rely on non-media, consultant-track executive talent. Clear Channel Outdoor CEO Scott Wells previously served as an Operating Partner at Bain Capital and holds an MBA from the Wharton School, while Outfront Media CEO Nick Brien built a 35-year career on the advertising agency side, leading Dentsu, McCann, and IPG Mediabrands. JCDecaux's highly specialised family operator model represents an extreme outlier versus the institutionalised corporate peer universe

10

Co-CEO operational delivery and capital allocation discipline

6.
JCDecaux delivered resilient organic growth of 9.7% in 2024, reaching €3,953m total adjusted revenue, with group revenue reaching €3,967m in 2025 representing a 1.8% organic increase. Adjusted operating margin expanded to 20.9% in 2025 up from 19.4% in 2024. Management sold APG at 13x EBITDA while acquiring Clear Channel Italy at 6.7x EBITDA, proposed a €0.65 dividend per share for 2025 after a five-year suspension, held capex to 7.5% of sales in 2025, and improved net debt to €587m by end of 2025
7.
Digital Out-of-Home revenues reached 41.7% of total revenue in 2025, programmatic digital OOH revenues expanded by 27.2% in Q1 2026, VIOOH programmatic revenues reached €75m in 1H 2025 representing 10% of digital revenues, while only 5% of JCDecaux's 1m locations are digital, leaving a long growth runway
8.
Co-CEOs successfully renegotiated minimum guarantee rents in China during late 2024, explaining one-third of the operating profit expansion in 1H 2025. Management preserved cash in 2020 by slashing capex and canceling dividends, and has clauses to suspend minimum guarantees if major airports close. JCDecaux generated positive free cash flow throughout the pandemic
9.
The co-CEOs manage with long-term capital preservation, holding a family stake of 65.42% of group voting rights. Jean-Charles Decaux explicitly stated that management prefers understatement over overstatement in financial forecasting, demonstrating operational conservatism and integrity. Strategic organic expansion targets a disciplined internal rate of return of 15% or more
10.
JCDecaux routinely secures street furniture contracts of 10 to 25 years versus seven-year typical billboard contracts. The group secured an exclusive 10-year street furniture contract in Barcelona in late 2025, announced a 12-year street furniture renewal in Reims in 2024, won an 11-year Rome bus shelter contract, secured a critical 15-year renewal of the Shanghai Metro concession, and operates an exclusive 20-year municipal advertising and bicycle contract in Valencia

Board member Alexia Decaux-Lefort is the daughter of co-CEO Jean-François Decaux, former Executive Board member Jean-Sébastien Decaux joined the Supervisory Board directly after resigning his operational roles, and Vice-Chairman Jean-Pierre Decaux is the uncle of both co-CEOs leading the company's executive team. The 55.56% board independence ratio exceeds the 33.33% recommended threshold for family-controlled entities under AFEP-MEDEF guidelines

All three committees achieved an absolute 100% attendance rate across 2025 meetings. The Ethics and CSR Committee is 100% independent, headed by independent director Bénédicte Hautefort. There is currently no active activist investor or hedge fund representation on the board

Executive compensation alignment and unique equity waiver

20.
Both co-CEOs have waived all performance shares and stock options since the 2001 IPO, with co-CEO compensation structured entirely around fixed salary and annual cash bonuses. For 2025, Jean-François and Jean-Charles Decaux received identical fixed base salaries of €1,096,938 each and €1,637,180 each in annual variable bonuses representing 99.50% of their variable cap, resulting in a total 2025 compensation of €2,754,452 each for the rotating co-CEOs
21.
Variable bonuses are determined by 60% financial, 15% ESG, and 25% strategic criteria, with financial metrics tied to group-level adjusted consolidated EBIT and segment operating margin metrics. Non-financial metrics require retaining presence in ESG indices and reducing carbon emissions, while strategic goals emphasize the renewal of major contracts, M&A execution, and digital scaling. All financial performance targets are calculated on an adjusted operational basis to reflect joint ventures
22.
The co-CEOs' zero-equity model is the single most unique alignment feature in the global OOH sector. Clear Channel Outdoor CEO Scott Wells' 2024 compensation was 185 times their median employee pay, with peer Clear Channel relying primarily on Adjusted EBITDA as the core executive incentive target. Because the Decaux family owns 65.42% of the share capital, dividends represent their true long-term incentive. The waiver of performance shares prevents any dilutive pressure on minority shareholders over time

Governance red flags and antitrust friction

23.
JCDecaux maintains a one-vote-per-share structure across all outstanding shares, with no double voting rights. The founding family controls 65.44% of the share capital via JCDecaux Holding, with the extended family concert holding 66.47% of total voting rights. This block provides insurmountable voting control over all extraordinary general meetings and represents the ultimate defense against hostile acquisitions
24.
The Audit Committee reviews all related-party agreements annually to ensure arm's-length pricing. JCDecaux sold a tangible asset to JCDecaux Holding for €30.20 million in June 2023, with near-zero impact on operating EBIT. Related-party IFRS 16 lease liabilities with family entities totaled €96.80 million in 2022, with right-of-use amortization with related parties costing €12.80 million during fiscal year 2025
25.
The group faces regular legal disputes over municipal contract awards and terminations. Spanish competition authority CNMC imposed strong structural remedies on the Clear Channel Spain acquisition, with JCDecaux agreeing to divest regional assets to preserve the €60.00 million deal

Overall management quality verdict and key vulnerabilities

28.
The Animal-CEO archetype assessment reveals strong match demonstrated by Jean-François Decaux establishing the German greenfield startup in 1982, partial match shown via rapid operational adjustments to secure minimum guarantee rent relief in China during late 2024, partial match through successful expansion of programmatic digital out-of-home platforms like VIOOH, strong match anchored by a strict 15% minimum internal rate of return requirement on all organic growth capital investments with co-CEOs reinstating a proposed €0.55 dividend per share for fiscal year 2024, strong match through an explicitly stated management posture of conservative understatement, and strong match evidenced by securing historically long street furniture concessions of 10 to 25 years in major European municipalities. However, expert insights suggest that centralized family control slows initial localized decisions, requiring deep consensus across the board, while expert network disclosures indicate historic internal resistance among senior executives to transitioning physical advertising inventory into programmatic systems
29.
The rotating annual co-CEO model risks strategic paralysis if the brothers diverge on key technological investments. High-barrier street furniture concessions are becoming hyper-competitive globally with peers pushing up minimum rent guarantees. Regulatory watchdogs prevented consolidation in core European markets by blocking the acquisition of Clear Channel Spain, forcing reliance on high-risk organic bidding rather than accretive, large-scale regional consolidation. Group Managing Director Finance David Bourg now supervises R&D, purchasing, and operations alongside traditional financial duties, creating a massive operational bottleneck if Bourg departs

The final verdict assigns medium plausibility confidence in management quality and operator-thesis execution capability, acknowledging exceptional capital discipline and municipal contract track record while recognizing structural decision-making friction and European consolidation barriers that constrain near-term strategic optionality.
