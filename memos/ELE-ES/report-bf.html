EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 30 findings

Endesa operates a vertically integrated Iberian utility model spanning electricity generation, regulated distribution networks, and retail commercialization, with operations concentrated almost entirely within Spain and Portugal . The company leverages a highly competitive, 90% carbon-free peninsular generation fleet anchored by low-cost nuclear and hydroelectric assets that deliver best-in-class ROIC of 11.00% , outperforming Iberdrola's 7.00% and EDP's 6.00% . Regulatory tailwinds emerged through CNMC Circular 9/2025, which raised the electricity network remuneration rate to 6.58% nominal pre-tax for 2026-2031 , representing a 100 basis point increase from the previous 5.58% rate . However, the retail customer base faces significant erosion , with freemarket electricity customers declining from 6.9 million in 2023 to 6.2 million in 2025 amid system-wide annual churn rates exceeding 25% . Merchant generation economics deteriorated sharply as rapid solar capacity additions deflated average 1Q26 Iberian spot power prices by 48% to €44.20/MWh , with Spanish solar capture prices collapsing to €25.86/MWh .
