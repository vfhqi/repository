B.1 Business Description 11 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Sole Spanish electricity transmission operator. Redeia (formerly Red Eléctrica) holds the exclusive statutory mandate under Law 24/2013 to own, operate and system-manage over 44,000km of high-voltage transmission lines across mainland Spain and the islands
2.
Revenue is fully regulated, not volume-based. The Spanish regulator sets a financial remuneration rate applied to the Regulated Asset Base
2.1
Mechanism: revenue is decoupled from the volume of electricity actually transmitted, giving exceptionally high earnings visibility but a hard regulatory ceiling on returns
3.
Newly purified, near-pure-play profile. Redeia completed the EUR 725m sale of its satellite unit Hispasat to Indra on 30-Dec-2025, taking regulated networks to approximately 90% of group EBITDA from 80% previously
4.
Two smaller non-regulated legs remain. Reintel (54,700km dark fibre, 49% owned by KKR) leases wholesale fibre capacity built onto power infrastructure at EBIT margins above 55%; Redinter runs 7,600km of concession-based transmission lines in Peru, Chile and Brazil
5.
Financial scale is material. FY2025 revenue EUR 1,659.5m, EBITDA EUR 1,258.3m at a 75.8% margin, net profit EUR 505.6m, net debt EUR 5,474.2m (4.4x EBITDA at Q1-2026)
6.
Capital-intensive growth phase. The 2026-2029 Strategic Plan commits EUR 6.5bn of capex, over 90% domestic transmission, targeting an 8% RAB CAGR to EUR 12.0bn by 2029
7.
Financing is deliberately non-dilutive. The plan is funded without equity issuance via hybrid bonds (EUR 1.5bn planned, first EUR 500m green hybrid priced at a 4.4% coupon in April 2026) plus Hispasat proceeds and targeted opex outperformance
8.
Credit profile shifted deliberately weaker. S&P and Fitch downgraded Redeia to BBB+ from A- in 2025; management has explicitly accepted the one-notch downgrade as the trade-off for executing the plan without equity issuance
9.
Investment style read. This is a regulated-yield, capex-driven RAB compounder, not a cyclical or event-driven EPS-upgrade story; the relevant comparison set is the European transmission-operator cohort, not the broader industrials or consumer universe
10.
Governance is stable and state-anchored. Spanish state holding company SEPI owns 20% (three board seats), Pontegadea 5%; the board is 58.3% independent and 58.3% female, both above Ibex-35 and Spanish Parity Act norms
11.
Recent overhang defines the current narrative. An April 2025 peninsular blackout has triggered a formal CNMC sanctioning procedure into Redeia's system-operator conduct
11.1
Exposure: a capped EUR 60m regulatory fine but an unprovisioned, uncapped private-litigation tail estimated up to EUR 400m in a worst case
