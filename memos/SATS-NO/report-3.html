SECTION 1: STAGE HISTORY

SATS AS listed on the Oslo Stock Exchange in 2019 at IPO. The COVID pandemic of 2020 was catastrophic for gym operators globally: Norway and Sweden imposed various lockdowns and restrictions, forcing temporary club closures. SATS's revenue collapsed in FY2020, EBITDA turned negative, and the share price fell sharply from IPO levels (which were themselves in the mid-to-high 20s NOK range) to distressed levels.

The post-COVID recovery began in earnest from late 2021 / early 2022 as restrictions lifted and members returned to clubs. However, the initial recovery was uneven: cost inflation (energy, wages), Sweden restructuring charges, and ongoing pandemic volatility created multiple false starts. The stock staged a Stage 2 advance attempt in 2022 that was interrupted by the global equity sell-off and the specific headwinds in consumer discretionary / leisure.

The sustained Stage 2 advance from a multi-year base began in the 2022–2023 period as SATS demonstrated operational leverage in its quarterly results. The 52-week range in the current data shows a low of NOK 32.57 and a high of NOK 44.85. Given the current price of NOK 43.1, the stock is near its 52-week high — consistent with a stock in an active Stage 2 advance, not one in distribution.

Accumulated advance from post-COVID lows: Based on available data, SATS appears to have advanced substantially from its 2020–2021 distress lows (likely in the NOK 15–25 range) to current levels of NOK 43.1.
