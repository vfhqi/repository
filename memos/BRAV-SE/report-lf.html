EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

CEO QUALITY AND TRACK RECORD Mattias Johansson is a definitive operator-track CEO with pure-play industry continuity, demonstrating 100% industry focus and rising through every operational level over 17 years to a clean internal succession . Since his January 2015 appointment, he has scaled total revenue from SEK 12bn at IPO to SEK 29.6bn in 2024 , though EBITA margins compressed from 6.4% in 2020 to 5.2% in 2024, reflecting severe Nordic construction market headwinds . His track record demonstrates exceptional 99% average cash conversion since 2015, an industry- leading working capital management achievement versus peers . He executed a relentless margin-over-volume doctrine, successfully turned around the 2017 Oras acquisition from heavy losses to a 5% EBITA margin, and completed approximately 160 acquisitions since 2014 . His leadership style is highly disciplined and margin- obsessed, enforcing relentless strategic focus on profitable growth through the unified "Bravida Way" across 330 branches , though extreme margin-over-volume dogmatism may institutionalise top-line stagnation and erode

structural market share . 1

GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE AND RED FLAGS The board maintains a fully independent, 50/50 gender-balanced composition with 100% of AGM-elected directors independent of the company, management, and major owners . Total CEO compensation reached SEK 17.6m in 2025, a restrained figure for a SEK 30bn revenue business , with variable payouts overwhelmingly linked to operational execution (EBITA) and acquisition activity , though no component of short-term bonus is linked to TSR or EPS, decoupling executive payouts from direct per-share returns . However, governance is severely undermined by systemic over-invoicing scandals, with Region Skåne and Örnsköldsvik Municipality filing police reports for deliberate over-invoicing , revealing critical weaknesses in local branch oversight despite centralised control mechanisms .
