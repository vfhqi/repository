Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Core business. Rai Way is Italy's national broadcast-transmission infrastructure operator, owning and operating over 2,300 telecommunications towers that carry the signal of parent company and 65%-controlling shareholder RAI, Italy's state public broadcaster
2.
Revenue model. 84.0% of consolidated turnover (EUR 237.4m in FY25) derives from a single turnkey Service Contract with RAI, running to June 2028 with a tacit seven-year renewal clause extending the relationship structurally to 2035
3.
Indexation. Almost all customer contracts, including the anchor RAI contract, carry 100% CPI-linked pricing with no annual caps; management estimates every 1.0% of inflation adds roughly EUR 2.0m of incremental EBITDA
4.
Scale and growth. FY25 core revenue reached EUR 282.8m, up 2.4% (organic core growth 4.4%)
4.1
Margin detail: Adjusted EBITDA reached EUR 191.8m, up 3.3%, at a 67.8% margin, up 60 basis points from 67.2% in FY24
5.
True EBIT. Reported operating profit (EBIT) fell 1.3% to EUR 130.2m in FY25 on rising depreciation from the accelerated industrial-plan capex programme, a 46.0% EBIT margin on core revenue
5.1
Detail: Net income of EUR 88.6m fell 1.4% year-on-year; ex-one-offs net income would have risen 0.4%
6.
Balance sheet. Net debt stands at EUR 136.5m, a 0.7x Net Debt/EBITDA leverage ratio, a genuine outlier against Cellnex's 6.3x and Inwit's 5.2x-5.7x, with EUR 67m of unused committed credit lines
7.
Cash generation and payout. Recurring free cash flow to equity was stable at EUR 118m in 2025 (a 7.3% yield)
7.1
Payout detail: A 100% (99.8%) net-income dividend payout policy delivers a proposed EUR 0.33/share dividend, a 5.7% yield
7.2
Detail: Cumulative distributions since the 2014 IPO exceed EUR 761m, roughly 95% of IPO proceeds, alongside 60% organic EBITDA growth over the same period
8.
Diversification strategy. The 2024-27 Industrial Plan targets a 4.0% revenue/EBITDA CAGR via up to 18 edge data centers, a 35.2MW Rome-Pomezia hyperscale campus and a 40MW solar programme, funded by EUR 240m of development capex
8.1
Detail: Execution is genuinely thin so far: the five operational edge data centers generated just EUR 0.30m in Q1 2026 (versus EUR 0.10m a year earlier)
8.2
Delivery lag: Pomezia and solar capex have both been delayed a year by Italian permitting bottlenecks, pushing full contribution beyond the original plan window

The dead catalyst. The long-discussed consolidation with domestic peer EI Towers (approximately 2,400 sites, EUR 285m FY23 revenue) definitively collapsed on 1 July 2026 when the non-binding Memorandum of Understanding expired unresolved, ending an on-off process dating to 2015

Leadership. CEO Roberto Cecatto (appointed April 2023) has executed an orderly, delivering transition from predecessor Aldo Mancino, reorganising the group into three specialised business units in 2024 and overseeing ten consecutive years of guidance-beating delivery

Governance and ownership. RAI's 65% controlling stake structurally limits activist entry and caps the CEO's unilateral transaction authority at EUR 2m per deal

Board detail: The nine-member board is 66% independent and 44% female, and government decree caps RAI's minimum stake at 30%
