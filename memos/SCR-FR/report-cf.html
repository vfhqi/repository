EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

SCOR SE presents a stabilized reinsurance execution story anchored by leadership continuity under CEO Thierry Léger, a compressed combined ratio of 80.2% in Q1 2026, and a strengthening solvency ratio of 220% supported by robust reinvestment yields of 3.6%. The investment case is underpinned by an imminent Solvency II regulatory reform expected to deliver an additional 15 to 20 percentage points of capital relief in January 2027, positioning SCOR as the largest beneficiary among European reinsurance peers. The net signal of change reflects a deliberate strategic pivot toward margin prioritization over volume growth, evidenced by traditional reinsurance premium contraction of -8.7% at April renewals as management aggressively trims US Casualty exposures to mitigate social inflation risks.

Forward guidance credibility is assessed as moderate to improving following historical Life & Health reserving volatility. Management has demonstrated transparent adjustment behavior by strengthening solvency to 220% at Q1 2026–matching the absolute ceiling of the 185% to 220% optimal range–and benefiting from upcoming Solvency II regulatory reforms. However, the balance sheet remains highly sensitive to actuarial assumptions, with 5% claims reserve increases impacting net income by EUR -79 million and 1% mortality rate increases reducing net income by EUR -110 million. The exceptional EUR 300 million reserve buffer provides near-term cushion, but Morgan Stanley characterizes SCOR as an "honest tracker" adjusting reserves transparently rather than risking further negative revisions.

Activist presence is absent, with no active activist stakeholders holding greater than 3% stakes or conducting public campaigns against SCOR SE.
