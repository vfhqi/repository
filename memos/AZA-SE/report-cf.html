EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Avanza has undergone a near-total executive management reconstitution over the last twenty-four months, fundamentally shifting leadership from long-tenured insiders to external banking and fintech veterans, elevating execution risk amid high transition velocity during critical strategic initiatives including Danish expansion and cloud migration. The company announced an organic launch into Denmark targeting late 2027, marking its first international expansion into a digitally mature market that increases addressable savings capital by approximately seventy percent. Management guides a pre-launch investment of SEK 120-150 million alongside approximately SEK 140 million in annual running costs, expecting profitability five years post-launch while diluting near-term earnings by an estimated two percent annually. Traditional Swedish incumbent banks are aggressively reinvesting in digital platforms to defend market share, while Nordnet remains the primary competitive threat, boasting a fifty basis point revenue margin in Denmark versus Avanza's forty-four basis points locally.

Riksbank policy rate cuts act as a severe mechanical headwind to net interest income generation across the Swedish financial sector, with a one percentage point decline mechanically eroding Avanza's net interest income by SEK 450 million. A 100-basis-point policy rate cut mechanically erodes Avanza's net interest income by SEK 520 million annually, while Nordnet conversely expects its own NII to stabilize throughout 2026 due to growing customer deposit bases. Avanza's gross brokerage margin compressed to 10.6 basis points in early 2026, noticeably lagging Nordnet's 13.1 basis points, driven by higher turnover per note within the fixed-price tier mechanically lowering income per traded krona. Fund income margins deteriorated to 23.3 basis points as Swedish customers rotated aggressively into low-fee index funds, which now constitute 52.5 percent of Avanza's fund capital, directly dragging on overall yield.

Total operating income accelerated from SEK 3,437 million in 2023 to SEK 4,495 million in 2025, driven heavily by macro interest rate tailwinds throughout the period. The Q1 2026 earnings print marked an all-time record operating profit of SEK 879 million, up seven percent year-over-year, with the operating margin hitting seventy percent reflecting immense scalability as transaction volumes surged during heightened geopolitical volatility. Operating profit reached an all-time high of SEK 879 million, with reported earnings per share amounting to SEK 4.71, marginally beating the consensus estimate of SEK 4.69. Avanza added 55,400 net new customers in Q1 2026, though customer intake trailed Nordnet's exceptionally high 77,500 new funded accounts in the same period. Net inflows totalled SEK 16.5 billion, hampered by the ongoing phase-out of external savings accounts, significantly lagging Nordnet's SEK 28.8 billion net savings recorded during the quarter. 13
