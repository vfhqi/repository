§1 — GENERAL

Swissquote is Switzerland's dominant online brokerage bank, holding a full FINMA banking licence since 2000, offering a uniquely diversified platform spanning equity trading, crypto custody and exchange, eForex, robo-advisory (LEON/Invest Easy), ePrivate banking, and multi-currency savings — a revenue model materially richer than any direct peer in the neobroker cohort.

  • Verdict: Best-in-cohort revenue diversification and highest AuC per core account; pre-tax margin (~58% in 2025) is best-in-cohort; but earnings quality is mixed — ~53% transactional, rate-sensitive, and crypto-cycle-exposed. [#J]

1.1 Corporate History and Founding Logic

Founded in 1996 by Marc Bürki and Paolo Buzzi with the original thesis of democratising financial information by providing free, real-time access to security prices on the Swiss Stock Exchange. [AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank (Aug 2025) documents the key milestones:

  • 2000: Swissquote becomes a bank and obtains banking licence from FINMA
  • 2001: Listed on the SIX Swiss Exchange (ticker: SQN)
  • 2003: Launch of online forex trading
  • 2005: Acquisition of Consors (Switzerland)
  • 2008: Launch of online mortgage platform with BLKB
  • 2010: Acquisition of Advanced Currency Markets , becoming a leading forex provider
  • 2013: Entry into institutional business with Swissquote Solutions
  • 2015: Partnership with PostFinance to launch Yuh announced
  • 2016: Launch of eBanking platform
  • 2018: Expansion to Asia: opening of Swissquote Singapore Ltd.
  • 2019: Acquisition of Internaxx Bank in Luxembourg
  • 2021: Launch of crypto and staking services
  • 2022: Launch of mortgage platform in partnership with several cantonal banks
  • 2023: Integration of Luxembourg subsidiary under EU passporting framework
  • 2025: Swissquote acquires full ownership of Yuh (previously 50/50 JV with PostFinance) for CHF 90m

[C]: The 2010 ACM and 2013 MIG Bank acquisitions are underappreciated structural decisions — they explain why Swissquote has a top-10 European eForex franchise, not just a retail FX offering. Bürki built the institutional FX desk via acquisition before competitors recognised its value, then embedded it as a revenue pillar. This is the same first-mover logic that drove the 2021 crypto custody and 2022 SQX exchange decisions. Pattern: Bürki consistently invests ahead of the revenue cycle.

1.2 Current Corporate Structure

The group operates as a SIX-listed holding company with primary operations centred in Swissquote Bank Ltd, a FINMA-regulated entity. Key subsidiaries as of year-end 2025:

  • Swissquote Bank Europe SA: 100%-owned subsidiary in Luxembourg regulated by the CSSF; serving as the EU hub for passporting services.
  • SQX: Proprietary crypto exchange, launched 2022, providing institutional-grade liquidity and matching services.
  • Yuh Ltd: Formerly 50/50 JV with PostFinance; Swissquote acquired remaining 50% on July 4, 2025, for CHF 90m; now fully consolidated.
  • International hubs: Wholly-owned entities in London, Dubai (DIFC DFSA Cat-3A since 2012), Hong Kong, Singapore, Bucharest, Cyprus, and Cape Town.

2.2 Net Interest Margin Mechanics

Net interest income became a central pillar following the SNB's exit from negative rates in 2022, with NII expanding from roughly CHF 218m in 2024. [AS·company]: Swissquote's own sensitivity disclosure: a parallel shift of 150bps in CHF rates impacts pre-tax income by CHF 52.18m. Despite SNB cuts in 2024 and 2025, NII remained resilient, declining only 3% in FY2025, as higher loan volumes and deposits partially offset rate compression. [AS·Broker]: Jefferies (Mar 2026). SNB went negative in 2015, returned to positive in 2022, cut to 0.00% by mid-2025.

[C]: NIM analysis by pillar:
- NIM source: Spread between client cash deposit rates paid and reinvestment in Swiss money market and bonds. FINMA banking licence enables Swissquote to act as direct banker, not intermediary, capturing full NIM.
- Multi-currency hedge: Client cash held in CHF, EUR, USD, GBP — USD rates still 4%+ in 2024 buffered CHF rate decline. Structurally different from Avanza (SEK-only NIM) and Nordnet (SEK-dominated).
- Rate cut sensitivity (100bps CHF): Rough estimate — every 100bps CHF cut reduces CHF-leg NIM by ~CHF 30-40m; USD/EUR legs partially offset. Net impact maybe -CHF 15-25m, or ~4-6% of pre-tax profit — manageable but not trivial.
- CHF at floor: SNB cut to 0.00% by mid-2025; further negative rates are possible given Swiss deflationary tendencies and CHF safe-haven pressures. CHF NIM effectively at floor. ❌
- Bear scenario: SNB returns to -0.50% (CHF safe-haven appreciation in geopolitical shock). CHF-leg NIM drops to zero or negative; USD/EUR legs only partially offset. Net NIM headwind ~CHF 20-30m. ❌
- Bull scenario: USD Federal Reserve rate cycle inflects higher; USD deposit leg NIM expands. Swissquote uniquely positioned among European retail brokers to benefit from higher-for-longer USD. [#J]

[AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank (Aug 2025) characterises Swissquote's "hybrid model" as more resilient than NIM-heavy Nordic peers — diversification allows it to thrive in different market conditions: high volatility drives brokerage and eForex income, while rising rates fuel NII, and crypto bull markets provide "embedded optionality" for disproportionate upside through operating leverage.

2.3 eForex and Trading Business

The eForex business is a distinctive differentiator vs Nordic peers, though it remained pressured by low volatility in 2025, with income down 3.8% YoY to CHF 91.1m. [AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank (May 2026). The segment primarily serves active retail FX traders and institutional/corporate clients, providing access to OTC foreign exchange markets. Net trading income of CHF 119.5m is largely derived from client transactions in non-CHF currencies.

[C]: Swissquote built the eForex institutional desk via the 2010 ACM and 2013 MIG Bank acquisitions — two strategic bolt-ons that installed 90+ institutional bank partner relationships. These are long-contracted, technology-embedded relationships where switching requires an IT integration project plus regulatory re-approval — multi-year lock-in. Revenue CHF 91m (2025) from this network is semi-captive; competitors would need to undercut on both price and service quality simultaneously to win clients. No cohort peer has an institutional FX desk of this scale. eForex dynamics: revenue highly sensitive to FX volatility; 2022 was a strong year (Ukraine/SNB shock). Revenue can halve in low-volatility years vs high-volatility years — essential to model across-cycle, not point-in-time. ❌

2.7 Hybrid Model — Flywheel vs Treadmill Assessment

[AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank characterises Swissquote as a "hybrid model" more resilient than NIM-heavy Nordic peers — diversification allows it to thrive across market conditions: high volatility drives brokerage and eForex income, rising rates fuel NII, crypto bull markets provide embedded optionality.

[C]: Disaggregating the flywheel vs treadmill dynamic:
- eForex institutional desk: Flywheel — each new institutional partner improves liquidity → better pricing → more partners
- SQX crypto exchange: Flywheel — liquidity flywheel; moderate network effects (more traders → tighter spreads → more traders); institutional crypto allocation via MiCA tailwind could accelerate
- Core retail brokerage: Treadmill — continuous marketing, product improvement, and service investment to retain clients facing Saxo/IBKR alternatives
- Crypto custody: Flywheel potential if MiCA drives regulated consolidation — Swissquote benefits disproportionately as unregulated crypto venues are squeezed

4.1 Swiss Online Brokerage Market Structure

Swissquote is the undisputed leader in Swiss digital banking. [AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank (Aug 2025):

  • Direct competitors: Saxo Bank and Interactive Brokers compete on pricing; Cornertrader targets active traders
  • Traditional incumbents: Cantonal banks (ZKB etc.) and UBS remain dominant in total assets but are generally more expensive for self-directed traders
  • Neobanks: Neon is a serious domestic competitor for the Yuh segment, reaching 235,000 users by early 2025; Trade Republic expanding pan-European including Switzerland

[C]: Swissquote's estimated 60-70% online brokerage market share in Switzerland is stronger than Avanza's ~40% share in Sweden or Nordnet's ~25% Nordic share. However, the total market is small (~650k accounts pre-Yuh). Growth to 1m+ core accounts requires penetrating lower-wealth segments (lower revenue/account) or converting traditional bank customers notoriously sticky. Both paths compress revenue-per-account metrics. ❌

4.2 FINMA Regulatory Moat

[AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank (Aug 2025, Apr 2026): Swissquote's primary competitive advantage is its dual status as a fully licensed Swiss bank and a technology company. Its FINMA-regulated crypto custody and exchange is significantly harder to replicate than standard EU neobroker offerings, providing a "Swiss quality premium" that attracts international clients seeking regulatory certainty.

[C]: FINMA banking + FINMA crypto custody is the most significant regulatory moat — no EU bank has the equivalent crypto + banking + exchange trifecta. Switzerland enacted the DLT Act (2021) recognising crypto as a distinct asset class with clear legal ownership — 3-4 years ahead of EU regulatory clarity. This enabled Swissquote to build SQX and obtain FINMA crypto custody approval before EU peers could act. EU MiCA (live Jan-25) begins closing the gap, but Swiss domicile preference for custody quality and legal certainty remains durable for 3-5 years. [#J]

4.5 Barriers to Switching

[AS·Broker]: Deutsche Bank: switching costs reinforced by complexity of transferring multi-asset portfolios and regulatory trust embedded in Swiss banking relationship. Swissquote's integration of "all-in-one" banking (multi-currency debit cards, mortgages) increases stickiness compared to pure-play brokers.

[C]: Switching cost quality by pillar:
- Multi-currency accounts: Mission-critical for Swiss expatriates (salary receipt, FX hedging, daily banking infrastructure) — switching requires rebuilding multi-currency infrastructure at a new institution
- Crypto custody: FINMA-regulated custody is high-friction to transfer — Swiss legal framework, tax documentation requirements, custody transfer compliance; higher switching cost than equities custody
- eForex institutional: Partner banks have technology-embedded FX infrastructure — switching requires IT integration project plus regulatory re-approval; multi-year lock-in
- Core equity custody: Substitutable — Saxo, IBKR, flatexDEGIRO all offer comparable service; lowest switching cost pillar ❌
- Overall: Above-cohort average due to crypto, eForex, and multi-currency elements; Saxo Bank is the only realistic substitute with comparable regulatory standing

[C] Analytical disagreement flag: [C] frames switching costs as "genuine but not best-in-class" and flags Saxo as removing the regulatory moat argument. [AS·Broker] (Deutsche Bank) presents a more positive view — "all-in-one banking" integration increases stickiness materially. The reconciliation: Saxo matches on regulation but not on product breadth (no mortgage, no full banking); Deutsche Bank's "all-in-one" framing is likely more accurate for the core affluent segment. Saxo is a valid threat for pure traders but not for full-service banking clients. [#J]

§6 — INDUSTRY STRUCTURE & SECULAR BACKDROP
