B.1 Business Description 16 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Pure-play transmission manufacturer. NKT A/S designs, manufactures and installs power cables from low-voltage building wire through extra-high-voltage subsea and underground interconnectors, following the 2024 divestment of NKT Photonics
2.
Three-segment structure since January 2026. Transmission (formerly "Solutions") covers complex extra-high-voltage subsea and underground interconnectors; Distribution (formerly "Applications") covers medium-voltage grids, data centres and low-voltage building wire; Grid Solutions is a new segment merging AC onshore projects, installation services and accessories
3.
Earnings concentration. Transmission generates roughly 75-80% of group EBITDA on Jefferies' segment framing, the highest high-voltage concentration of the three listed European cable peers against Prysmian and Nexans' 20-25% each
4.
Geography. Core European and Nordic markets; Germany alone is 28.6% of FY2025 revenue by currency-exposure proxy, the single largest national market; over 95% of the transmission order backlog sits with European transmission system operators
5.
Manufacturing footprint. Concentrated single-site model centred on Karlskrona, Sweden, where NKT is expanding a third high-voltage extrusion tower, contrasted with Prysmian's four-site European footprint
6.
Scale. FY2025 group revenue EUR 2,722m (standard-metal basis), up from EUR 1,927m in 2023 and EUR 945m in 2019; FY2025 operational EBITDA EUR 390m at a 14.3% margin per company filings, though a 15.2% figure also appears in the same source, see F.2
7.
Balance sheet. Net cash position of EUR 963m at end-2025 (leverage of approximately -2.5x), against Prysmian's roughly 1.3x net debt; no dividend paid since 2016
8.
Working capital. Net working capital of negative EUR 1,524m at Q1 2026 (-35.8% of last-twelve-month revenue), structurally driven by customer prepayments on long-cycle transmission system operator contracts, not receivables stress
9.
Capital programme. A EUR 2.0bn group capital-expenditure programme runs 2025-2028, including a EUR 300m cost overrun on the Karlskrona expansion disclosed in late 2024; capital intensity peaked in 2025 at 27% of standard-metal revenue
10.
Order book. Record transmission backlog of EUR 13.5bn at Q1 2026, underpinned by the Eastern Green Link 3 contract win and an eight-year Alliander medium-voltage framework agreement running mid-2026 to mid-2034
11.
Leadership. Chief executive Claes Westerlind, in role since May 2023, an internal promotion with a decade at ABB (2006-2017) in high-voltage direct current sales before joining NKT in 2017
12.
CFO transition. Chief financial officer Michael Yong took the role on 1 April 2026, promoted internally from NKT's own Chief Strategy Officer post he had held since 2021, not an external hire as an earlier memo draft stated; see Element E9 for the correction detail
13.
Competitive structure. The extra-high-voltage cable market is a global oligopoly of five to six manufacturers capable of the highest voltage classes, with NKT, Prysmian and Nexans as the three listed European players and Sumitomo Electric, LS Cable and Hellenic Cables the most credible challengers
14.
Investment style implication. This is a capacity-expansion compounder at a capital-intensive inflection point: heavy 2025-2026 investment and negative free cash flow are guided to convert into structurally higher margins and positive free cash flow from 2027 as the Karlskrona tower and the NKT Eleonora cable-lay vessel come online
15.
Segment margin profile. Distribution (medium-voltage) margins are thinner and more competitive than Transmission, with a 290 basis point margin dilution in early 2025 attributed to fragmented Central European price competition, since partly recovered
16.
Secular backdrop. The company sits inside a structurally growing addressable market, with ENTSO-E guiding a 41% uplift in 2031-35 offshore grid investment versus 2025-30 and a persistent European high-voltage total addressable market above EUR 10bn a year through 2030
