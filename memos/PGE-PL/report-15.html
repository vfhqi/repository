  • Highest-conviction concerns: (1) Ørsted-vs-PGE commissioning language divergence; (2) CMD financing gap unresolved 12+ months post-event; (3) management tone hedging trajectory across L4 quarters; (4) coal-exit timeline avoidance pattern; (5) State Treasury non-action as a non-signal

  • Multiple-truths read: The bull case and bear case can genuinely coexist — PGE has real assets, real capacity market revenues, and a real transition asset pipeline; the narrative-reality disconnect is about pace and financing certainty, not about whether the assets exist; a patient investor with a 3-5Y horizon could be right on the destination while being surprised by the journey

Confidence in management narrative: medium plausibility — the strategic direction is credible and consistent with Polish energy policy; the execution timeline and financing confirmation are not yet at the evidential standard that would support high plausibility
