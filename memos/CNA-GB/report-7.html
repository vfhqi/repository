  • Mechanism to SP: Adverse regulatory determination would reduce earnings visibility, compress multiple (utilities re-rate on regulation news), and potentially force management to revise capital return commitments [#A]

  • Company guide: Management does not provide explicit guidance on Ofgem outcomes — appropriately, given regulatory independence; characterises regulatory environment as "constructive but watchful" [#J]

  • Sub-bullet: Embedded assumption: current price cap methodology persists; no retrospective windfall clawback; Energy Profits Levy not extended to retail supply margins [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Consensus modelling of regulatory risk is optimistic — most brokers assume cap methodology unchanged; probability of adverse reset is systematically underpriced in SS estimates [#A]

  • SS consensus: Deutsche Bank and Barclays explicitly flag Ofgem methodology review as "key risk" in their coverage; neither has quantified the downside scenario into base case; Berenberg includes a bull/bear regulatory scenario table [#J]

  • Sub-bullet: Consensus changes L12M: regulatory risk has been flagged more frequently post-2023 UK general election cycle; heightened attention from analyst community but not yet in numbers [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: Historical base rate: Ofgem has made adverse methodology changes in 3 of the last 8 annual cap reviews; ~37% adverse determination frequency [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Political pressure on energy bills is reduced at lower gas prices (less consumer anger = less regulatory pressure to compress margins) [#J]

  • Sub-bullet: Macro headwind — UK Labour government has historically been more interventionist on energy market regulation; 2024 election outcome increases regulatory pressure probability [#A]

  • Visibility: Low — Ofgem is independent, decisions are not pre-signalled, and political environment is variable [#J]

  • Bear case: Labour government legislates windfall tax extension to retail supply; Ofgem simultaneously tightens cap methodology; combined impact £300-400m EBIT headwind; buyback suspended; multiple compresses [#A]

  • Sub-bullet: Probability of this combined adverse scenario: ~15-20% in Watson's base assessment; low probability but severe magnitude [#J]

  • Red flag: UK 2024 election outcome (Labour majority delivered) increases probability of regulatory intervention; several Labour manifesto commitments reference energy market reform [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: What if the sell-side is systematically under-pricing regulatory risk because UK analysts have been conditioned by a decade of incrementalist Ofgem decisions, and the step-change risk from a new political economy post-2024 is not captured in any broker model? [#J]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility for adverse reset — base rate ~37% for adverse methodology change, elevated by political cycle; magnitude large enough to be case-defining [#A]

  • Bear case: Fleet availability falls to 55-60% (vs 70-75% normalised) due to unplanned outages; nuclear EBIT drops to £80-100m; market re-rates the stake as a liability rather than an asset [#J]

  • Sub-bullet: EDF operational control risk — Centrica has no ability to influence outage decisions; passive stakeholder in a technically complex, aging fleet [#A]
