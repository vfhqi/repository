  • EBIT margin: FY2022 approximately 12-13%; FY2023 approximately 11%; FY2024 approximately 10.4%; Q1 2025 approximately 10.0-10.5% — a clear multi-year compression trend of ~200-250bps from peak [#A]

  • Adjusted vs reported margins: Adjusted EBITA margin approximately 100-150bps above EBIT margin due to intangible amortisation; non-GAAP adjustments are modest and stable — not a quality concern [#A]

  • Sceptical read: Management's decision to hold the cost base intact while volumes are weak is strategically defensible but creates a risk: if volumes recover only partially, the cost base represents a permanent drag on margins; the "recovery leverage" story requires full volume normalisation, not a partial bounce [#J]
