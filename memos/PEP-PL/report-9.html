§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Earnings quality is mixed — RES segment EBITDA is clean and cash-generative; Trading segment introduces MTM noise; adjustments are modest but directionally concerning in their frequency [#J]

  • Recurring vs one-off: MTM derivatives featured in every L3Y period — swings ranged from ~PLN -35m (H1 2023) to ~+PLN 40m (H1 2022), representing 6-13% of half-year EBITDA [#A]

  • Sub-point: the company treats MTM as non-recurring in adjusted EBITDA, but energy trading is core to the business model — calling it non-recurring is structurally arguable [#J]

  • Adjustments trend: Adjusted EBITDA exceeded reported EBITDA in 3 of 6 semi-annual periods (FY2022 H1, FY2023 H1, FY2024 H1) — frequency of upward adjustments rising slightly [#A]

  • Sub-point: development cost expensing (FID preparation for offshore) added ~PLN 25-30m of apparent one-offs in H1 2024 [#I]

  • Sub-point: green certificate reclassification in H2 2022 was a ~PLN 25m boost not repeated — suggests some period-specific inflation of base EBITDA [#I]

  • Non-GAAP gap: Adj EPS vs reported EPS gap averaged ~PLN 0.15-0.30/share L3Y — relatively contained vs peers [#A]

  • Sub-point: gap widest in H1 2023 (~PLN 0.10 downward adj) due to MTM loss [#I]

  • NWC tailwind: NWC movements were broadly neutral to modestly positive in FY2022-FY2023; FY2024 shows receivables building as capacity addition pipeline grows — early headwind signal [#A]

  • Sub-point: payables turn appears stable; receivables turn lengthening slightly (energy contract settlement timing) [#I]

  • Cash conversion: ❌ OCF/EBITDA averaged ~55-65% L3Y — below the 75-85% range typical for mature European renewable operators (Vestas, RWE renewables, Energix comparators) [#A]

  • Sub-point: suppression is structurally explainable by heavy development CapEx; sustaining CapEx conversion is cleaner [#I]

  • Sub-point: interest capitalisation on development assets inflates EBITDA vs OCF — unwinding this on project completion will be a positive quality inflection [#J]

  • D&A trend: D&A growing at ~18-20% annually as new assets commissioned — faster than revenue, compressing EBIT margins relative to EBITDA margins [#A]

  • Sub-point: this is expected in a capacity build-out phase; it implies EBIT is a less useful metric than EBITDA until offshore wind delivers [#I]

  • OCF vs NI: OCF/Net income ratio ~1.2-1.4x L3Y — acceptable; NI is genuinely cash-backed at reported level [#A]

  • Conservative accounting signal: Development costs for Bałtyk II/III are predominantly capitalised on balance sheet rather than expensed — appropriate under IFRS and not aggressive [#I]

  • Aggressive accounting concern: ❌ Green certificate revenue recognition timing differs across periods — ~PLN 20-30m of FY2022 H2 and FY2023 H1 certificate income may reflect recognition of prior-period entitlements rather than current-period earnings [#J]

  • Receivables turn: Receivables days edging from ~35d (FY2022) to ~42d (FY2024E) — modest lengthening; worth monitoring if it continues [#A]

  • Sceptical quality bullet: Bearish read: MTM derivatives, green cert timing, and FID cost classification are individually small but collectively mean that in any given half, ~PLN 50-75m of reported EBITDA could be period-specific noise — equivalent to ~10-12% of half-year EBITDA; this makes quarterly tracking less reliable than it appears [#J]

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: Margin expansion from ~16.6% to ~18.9% EBITDA margin over L3Y appears structurally driven by RES mix shift; however, it remains below what the business will achieve post-offshore commissioning — current margins understate terminal potential [#J]

  • Revenue CAGR: ~15% L3Y (FY2022-FY2024E) — driven ~60% by volume (new MW commissioned) and ~40% by price/tariff [#A]

  • Sub-point: price/tariff contribution declines post-FY2023 as energy wholesale normalises [#I]

  • Profit CAGR: EBITDA ~23% > Revenue ~15% — 800bps annual spread implies meaningful operating leverage [#A]

  • Sub-point: operating leverage is genuine: fixed costs (network O&M, grid connection) spread over growing GWh base [#J]

  • Gross margin: Expanded from ~19.7% (FY2022) to ~22.8% (FY2024E) — 310bps over 3 years [#A]

  • Sub-point: gross margin improvement driven by onshore wind portfolio economies and PV yield improvements [#I]

  • EBITDA margin trajectory: Consistent improvement each year; no single-period reversal [#A]

  • Sub-point: FY2022 base was inflated by energy price spike — the "true" starting margin for the business in a normalised environment is closer to FY2023's ~17% [#J]

  • Operating leverage: Each 1% revenue growth has delivered ~1.5x EBITDA growth L3Y — consistent with a scaling infrastructure business [#A]

  • Cost cut driver: Limited — margin expansion is capacity-mix driven, not cost-cut driven; headcount has grown with the business [#I]

  • Mix shift driver: Key driver: RES segment (wind + PV) as % of group EBITDA grew from ~55% (FY2022) to ~70%+ (FY2024E) — higher-margin regulated/quasi-regulated revenue displacing lower-margin trading [#J]

  • Pricing driver: Auction prices for Polish RES capacity (CfD equivalents) locked in FY2020-2023 rounds remain supportive through FY2026-2027 — limited spot price risk on existing portfolio [#I]

  • Scale driver: Offshore wind (Bałtyk II + III, ~1.5 GW combined) represents the next margin step-change — EBITDA margins on offshore wind typically 45-55% at asset level vs 25-35% for onshore (peer data: Ørsted, Vattenfall offshore comps) [#A]

  • Sustainability: Structural drivers (mix shift, auction pricing protection, scale) are more durable than cyclical drivers; key vulnerability is energy price assumption embedded in unhedged volumes [#J]

  • Sceptical margin bullet: Bearish read: the mix-shift argument requires offshore wind to deliver on schedule; every 12-month delay to Bałtyk FID/commissioning removes one year of the highest-margin revenue layer, leaving margins stuck at current 18-19% rather than stepping to 25-30%+ — construction risk is the margin sustainability risk [#J]

§4 — Seasonal Patterns

Seasonality verdict: PEP-PL shows a modest but consistent seasonal pattern: H2 > H1 for wind (Polish wind regime), H1 > H2 for PV; net effect is mild H2 EBITDA bias that the market understands and prices in [#J]

  • H1 pattern: H1 EBITDA consistently ~43-45% of full-year EBITDA — modest structural H1 underweight; Polish wind resource peaks in autumn/winter [#A]

  • Sub-point: PV generation offsets some wind seasonality in H1 (peak irradiance May-August) [#I]

  • H2 pattern: H2 EBITDA ~55-57% of full-year — driven by higher wind capture factor (Q3/Q4 wind speeds in northern Poland) [#A]

  • Strongest quarter: Q4 (within H2) is typically strongest for wind generation; no quarterly breakdown publicly available given semi-annual cadence [#I]

  • Weakest quarter: Q2 (within H1) weakest for wind; partially offset by PV peak [#I]

  • Seasonality direction: Seasonality mildly intensifying as PV portfolio grows — increasing H1 PV contribution partially closes the H1/H2 EBITDA gap [#A]

  • Cause — weather: Wind regime dominates; PEP-PL's portfolio concentrated in northern/central Poland (Baltic coastal proximity advantageous) [#I]

  • Cause — production: RES dispatch is weather-dependent; Trading segment adds some counter-seasonal demand (energy price volatility typically higher in winter peak demand) [#I]

  • Seasonality changing: PV additions are structurally moderating the H1/H2 split — positive from a cash flow predictability standpoint [#J]

  • M&A timing: No M&A seasonality detected L3Y; all growth is organic greenfield [#I]

  • Sceptical seasonality bullet: Bearish read: if Polish wind resource declined persistently (climate trend) or if new offshore capacity is H1-biased (Baltic offshore wind more variable), the assumed H2 EBITDA delivery could become unreliable — weather normalisation is not a trivial assumption for a ~55% H2-weighted earnings model [#J]

§8 — Forward Plausibility

Forward plausibility verdict: L3Y track record supports continuation at ~15% revenue / ~20% EBITDA growth; the forward thesis implies acceleration to 25-35% EBITDA CAGR post-offshore FID — this requires a step-change not yet evidenced in historical data [#J]

  • Track-record support: L3Y delivered ~23% EBITDA CAGR and ~19% adj EPS CAGR on organic RES build-out — demonstrates execution capability on greenfield infrastructure at scale [#A]

  • Forward EPS implied: Consensus FY2025-FY2027 adj EPS CAGR ~25-30% if Bałtyk II begins contributing EBITDA from FY2027 [#A]

  • Required CAGR: For a 25%+ IRR over 3 years from current valuation (est. ~12x FY2024E EV/EBITDA), EBITDA must grow at ~25-30% — requiring offshore contribution [#J]

  • Achievable from L3Y: L3Y CAGR of ~23% EBITDA is close to the "without offshore" continuation scenario but below the 25-30% required for 25%+ IRR — offshore is not optional for the thesis, it is load-bearing [#J]

  • Acceleration required: The forward thesis requires material acceleration from ~23% to ~28-32% EBITDA CAGR from FY2026 onwards — contingent on Bałtyk II FID (targeted H1 2025) and first power (c.2028-2029) [#A]

  • Deceleration acceptable: A 3-5% CAGR deceleration is acceptable if the current 12x EV/EBITDA multiple re-rates toward 15-18x on offshore FID confirmation — valuation re-rating can substitute for near-term earnings acceleration [#J]

  • Margin runway: Offshore wind EBITDA margins (45-55% asset level) will structurally lift group margins from current ~19% to potentially ~28-32% by FY2028-FY2029 assuming ~800-1,000 MW offshore contribution — significant margin runway remains if project executes [#A]

  • Quality runway: Cash conversion will improve post-offshore commissioning as capitalised development costs begin depreciating and producing OCF — the quality concern (§2) is partially self-correcting on project delivery [#J]

  • Inflection plausibility: The L3Y track record on capacity delivery (on-time commissioning, cost control) is the strongest evidence base for believing offshore will be similarly executed — management has not missed a major onshore milestone [#A]

  • Sceptical forward bullet: Bearish read: the L3Y track record was built on onshore wind and utility-scale PV — technologies with well-established Polish supply chains and permitting precedents; offshore wind in Poland has no comparable precedent, different risk profile, and is an order of magnitude larger capital commitment — extrapolating onshore execution track record to offshore is a category error in risk assessment [#J]
