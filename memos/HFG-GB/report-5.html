1. BRIEF COMPANY DESCRIPTION

Hilton Food Group is a UK-listed outsourced protein and fresh food processing company, operating manufacturing facilities on behalf of major supermarket retailers across Europe, Australasia, and increasingly North America. The business manages approximately 90% revenue from meat and fresh prepared foods (beef, pork, lamb, poultry, convenience products), with ancillary operations in seafood (Seachill), plant-based processed foods (Foppen, Dalco), and a recently-exited supplier platform (Foods Connected sold to Apax; Fairfax Meadow sold to Sysco).

Geographic footprint: Six core manufacturing sites across UK, Netherlands, Denmark, Poland, Belgium, Australia, New Zealand. New greenfield investment underway in Canada (Ontario; Walmart partnership; launch early 2027) and Saudi Arabia (49% JV with Nadec; launch H2 2026).

Revenue composition (2024-25): ~£4.0bn annual revenue. Customer base anchored on Tesco and Ahold Delhaize (major supermarket retailers with extended contract terms of 5-15 years). Tesco represents material proportion of revenue; customer concentration remains a structural risk.

2. COMPANY'S FOCUS AT MOST RECENT EARNINGS (FY 2025)

Management's presentation of full-year 2025 results (announced 31-Mar-26) focused on the following 20 key messages:

### Positive / Resilience Messages

1. Core meat and convenience business delivering above-market growth. Retail meat and fresh convenience volumes grew 3.1% in H1 2025, significantly above market volume trends (~1.8% market growth). Attributed to strong retailer partnerships, new product launches, well-aligned range in inflationary environment. This segment represents ~90% of group revenue and is operationally resilient.

2. Volume growth maintained amid severe inflation. Group volumes grew 2.5% in H1 2025 and 0.2% for full year 2025, achieved in a "highly inflationary environment," particularly acute for beef and protein. Management highlights this as evidence of pricing discipline and value proposition strength.

3. Revenue growth through pricing and mix. Total revenue up 11.9% on constant-currency basis (full year 2025), and 7.6% on reported basis in H1 2025. Growth driven primarily by raw material cost pass-through and product mix improvements, not volume expansion (core message: pricing power exists but limited to input cost recovery).

4. Adjusted profit margin stability in 2025 core operations. Adjusted PBT of £73.2m maintained flat vs 2024 (£73.2m), despite inflationary headwinds and exceptional costs at Seachill/Foppen/Dalco. Signals underlying operational efficiency and cost control in legacy operations.

5. Strategic portfolio simplification realized value. Disposal of Fairfax Meadow to Sysco (£54.4m cash proceeds) and Foods Connected stake to Apax (£21.8m cash + £24.3m equity instruments) generated £66.5m gains, producing £29.3m net exceptional benefit. Illustrates management's willingness to exit non-core, lower-growth businesses and redeploy capital.

6. Balance sheet remains resilient; covenant headroom. Net bank debt of £126.7m (~0.9x adjusted EBITDA, FY25 year-end). Leverage ratio of 1.3x adjusted EBITDA mid-year 2025. Banking covenants (3.0x leverage, 4.0x interest cover) maintained with headroom. New £450m RCF secured with 5+ year term, supporting future investment.

  1. Canada partnership (Walmart) on track for early 2027. $192m investment in Brantford, Ontario facility for North American market entry. Described as "step-change growth opportunity," centered on 10-year Walmart supply agreement. Viewed as unlocking secular growth in North American food processing, diversifying from UK/EU exposure.

  2. Saudi Arabia JV progressing. 49% stake in NADEC joint venture, initial investment £6.5m (subject to regulatory approval), targeting launch H2 2026. Positioned as entry into high-growth Gulf market and geographic diversification.

  3. Customer relationship extensions secured. Renegotiation/extension of key contracts in Netherlands and Denmark confirmed in 2025. These renewals support contract visibility and revenue stability through mid-cycle (5-15 year terms typical).

10. Sustainability credentials improving. Achieved A-score on CDP climate change assessment; 32% reduction in Scope 1/2 emissions vs 2020 baseline; deforestation elimination targeted end-2025. Positioned to meet retailer ESG requirements and emerging regulatory demands (EPR scheme, etc.).

  1. Operating efficiency focus. Despite inflationary environment, adjusted operating profit margin held at ~2.4% (H1 2025: 2.2%). Reflects automation investment, labor productivity gains, and supply chain optimization in legacy operations.

### Negative / Headwind Messages

12. Profitability outlook deteriorating in 2026. Management guidance for FY2026 adjusted PBT: £60m–£65m, implying mid-teens percentage decline from £73.2m (FY25). Biggest single headwind: continued losses/disruption at Seachill, Foppen, Dalco. Guidance reset suggests loss of confidence in near-term recovery of these units.

13. Seachill in loss-making position. Volumes fell ~7% in FY25; business moved to loss-making state. White fish shortages and supply chain constraints cited as ongoing drivers. No specific recovery timeline articulated; characterization has shifted from "turnaround" to "limited investment, separate dedicated leadership."

  1. Foppen severely disrupted by regulatory/logistics constraints. Regulatory restrictions on US shipping and Greece production halt (post-2024) impacted smoked salmon exports. Relocation and airfreight cash costs: £9.2m. Reorganization/restructuring costs: £9.6m (double prior year). Business characterized as having limited optionality; management limiting future capex.

15. Dalco remains loss-making despite volume growth. Volumes up 8.5% but business still unprofitable. Plant-based segment faces structural margin compression and weak end-market demand. Unlikely to achieve profitability without significant portfolio action.

16. Margin pressure from raw material inflation unabated. Protein inflation (beef +20.4%, lamb +10.2% in mid-2025) passing through revenue but not translating to PBT growth. Gross margin expansion offset by operating cost inflation (labor, energy, logistics). Pricing power limited to input cost recovery; little upside in net margin.

17. Volume growth decelerating. Group volume growth (0.2% FY25, 2.5% H1 2025) below retail market trends and well below historical norms (3-5%). Signals: retail volumes mature/flatlining; customer inventory discipline high; volume growth will require geographic/product expansion (Canada), not organic UK/EU market share.

18. Free cash flow deterioration. Adjusted FCF negative in 2025 (outflow, after disposal proceeds), vs positive in prior year. Driver: higher inventory holdings (partly inflation-driven, partly working capital build for Canada/Saudi projects) + elevated capex. 2026 capex guidance £50m–55m (core) + elevated spend on Canada completion and Poland expansion start.

19. Exceptional benefits mask underlying earnings pressure. Reported profit includes £29.3m net exceptional gain (Fairfax, Foods Connected disposals). Adjusted PBT (excluding exceptionals) flat vs 2024, indicating no earnings leverage in core business. Growth investments (Canada, Saudi) not yet contributing; cannibalization risk if Canada volumes pull from APAC/Europe.

20. Guidance conservatism suggests high execution risk. Management framing of 2026 (mid-teens PBT decline, seafood/plant turnarounds on "separate dedicated leadership," limited investment) indicates low confidence in timing of recovery. Implies downside optionality if Seachill, Foppen, Dalco deteriorate further or if macro/retail volumes weaken materially.

Rating Assessment

COLOUR: RED / ORANGE — Major deterioration in forward guidance, profitable near-term recovery unlikely. Core meat resilience is genuine but masked by loss-making ancillary units. Cash flow headwinds and capex intensity rising. Momentum negative; volatility elevated.

Change from 6 months prior: Downgrade from "cautious hold, watch execution" to "hold, downside risk."

For a fundamental equity picker targeting 12-24 month performance, HFG offers negative risk/reward at current levels. Core meat resilience does not offset portfolio drag, execution risk, and financial pressure. Better opportunities exist in peers (Cranswick if poultry supply stable) or non-food manufacturing with better margins and clearer growth paths.
