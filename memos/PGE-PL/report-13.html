§1 — Current Management Guidance

PGE's current guidance framework combines near-term FY2025 financial targets issued at the FY2024 results (March 2025) and the medium-term Strategy 2030 targets reconfirmed at the Capital Markets Day in late 2023 / early 2024.

  • J-front verdict: Management has issued quantitative but wide-banded near-term guidance and ambitious medium-term targets; both sets carry material conditionality around coal phase-out, regulatory environment, and green financing access [#J]

  • FY2025 — EBITDA: Management guided PLN 7.5–8.5bn EBITDA for FY2025 at the FY2024 results call; midpoint ~PLN 8.0bn; represents approx. flat-to-modest growth vs FY2024 reported EBITDA of ~PLN 7.8bn [#A]

  • Sub: guidance range width (~13% band) is wider than Western European utility norms (Enel, RWE typically guide ±5–7% band); signals management uncertainty, not conservative discipline [#I]

  • FY2025 — Revenue: No explicit revenue guidance issued for FY2025; management references "stable wholesale electricity revenue supported by capacity market payments" without quantification [#A]

  • Sub: absence of revenue guidance is consistent with PGE's historical practice and reflects Polish electricity price volatility; comparable to CEZ and Tauron in the CEE utility universe [#I]

  • FY2025 — EBIT: Not explicitly guided; EBIT implicitly derivable only after D&A which management does not guide separately [#I]

  • FY2025 — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance; management references "continuation of dividend policy" (target payout 25–35% of net profit) as a soft proxy; consensus-implied EPS ~PLN 1.20–1.50 for FY2025 [#A]

  • FY2025 — FCF: No explicit FCF guidance; management acknowledges elevated CAPEX of PLN 9–11bn for FY2025, implying FCF will be negative or marginally negative before financing [#A]

  • Sub: negative FCF guidance in absolute terms is a structurally unusual signal for a utility of PGE's scale; Enel and E.ON both guided positive FCF through their heavy investment cycles via asset disposals [#I]

  • FY2025 — CapEx: Guided PLN 9–11bn for FY2025; represents continued ramp vs historical ~PLN 6–7bn; primarily driven by offshore wind (Baltic Power JV with Orlen), distribution network upgrades, and gas peakers [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y (2027): Strategy 2030 targets include EBITDA of PLN 12–14bn by 2027; implies ~50–75% growth from FY2025 midpoint; driven by renewables ramp, distribution regulated returns, and coal asset retirement savings [#A]

  • Sub: 2027 EBITDA target is consensus-regarded as aspirational; PKO BP Research and Wood & Co both apply ~20–25% haircut to the 2027 midpoint in their base case models [#I]

  • Medium-term — 2030 targets: Full Strategy 2030 plan targets ~11 GW renewables capacity, net-zero coal by 2036, PLN 75–80bn cumulative CAPEX 2023–2030 [#A]

  • Sub: coal phase-out by 2036 is conditional on Polish government policy support; management explicitly flags this as "state-supported transition" [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: FY2024 results and Q3 2024 results calls; FY2024 guidance reiterated in annual report filed March 2025 [#A]

  • Source — investor day: Strategy 2030 CMD (Nov 2023); not updated since; over 18 months stale at time of this report [#A]

  • Update timing: Next scheduled update is H1 2025 results (August 2025); watch for any mid-year guidance revision given Polish electricity price volatility in Q1–Q2 2025 [#I]

  • Caveat — macro: All guidance explicitly conditional on "stable Polish regulatory framework, no extraordinary windfall taxes, and EU ETS CO₂ prices in PLN 250–350/tonne equivalent range" [#A]

  • Caveat — coal: Near-term guidance explicitly excludes "extraordinary impairments on coal assets" — this is a recurring below-the-line carve-out that has historically been large and frequent [#A]

  • Conditional assumption — renewables financing: Medium-term CAPEX plan assumes access to EU Just Transition Fund, EIB green lending, and KfW-equivalent Polish state financing at sub-market rates; none are contractually locked [#A]

  • ❌ Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that management's EBITDA guidance band is so wide (~PLN 1bn range for FY2025) that it is almost trivially achievable in normal conditions but provides no real forecast discipline; the medium-term targets embed financing and regulatory assumptions that are structurally outside management's control, and the explicit coal impairment carve-out means management can repeatedly beat "guided" EBITDA while EPS disappoints through non-guided below-the-line charges [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • J-front verdict: Split personality — EBITDA guidance is mildly conservative (sandbagger posture) but net profit / EPS guidance is systemically over-optimistic driven by persistent below-the-line misses that management does not adequately provision for [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — EBITDA: Management consistently guides EBITDA with wide ranges skewed slightly below consensus; beats 3 of 4 periods by modest margins (+2–8%); classic mild sandbagging [#A]

  • Sub: the sandbagging is not aggressive — beats are in the +2–8% range, not the +15–20% beats seen from Orsted or Terna at their most conservative periods [#I]

  • Over-promiser signal — EPS/net profit: Net profit guidance embeds an implicit assumption that below-the-line charges (impairments, exceptional CO₂ costs, financing) will be absent or minimal; this assumption has been wrong in all 4 observed periods [#A]

  • Sub: management appears to guide net profit on a "normalised" basis without adequately discounting for the base rate of Polish coal impairment charges, which are structurally recurring [#J]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue is not guided, which is arguably the most honest signal — management effectively admits it cannot forecast Polish electricity wholesale prices reliably [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — CapEx: CapEx guidance is systematically low-biased (always undershoots); this is not sandbagging but rather consistent optimism bias in project cost estimation; mirrors pattern seen at CEZ and Tauron during their respective heavy investment cycles [#A]

  • Pattern shift — post-2022: There is a tentative improvement in EBITDA guidance accuracy post-2022 (FY2022–FY2023 beats are larger and more consistent) — possibly reflecting management's adoption of wider guidance bands following the volatility of COVID and gas crisis [#A]

  • Sub: the improvement in EBITDA accuracy coincides with worsening FCF misses; the EBITDA beats are partially a function of capitalising more costs into the investment programme [#J]

  • Magnitude — beat (EBITDA): Average EBITDA beat +4.5%; below the +8–12% beats that characterise true sandbagging discipline in the European utility sector [#I]

  • Magnitude — miss : Average EPS/net profit miss -40%; far worse than the European utility peer miss magnitude average of ~-10 to -15%; classified as structurally poor EPS guidance [#A]

  • Beat consistency — EBITDA only: If an investor scored PGE solely on EBITDA guidance, the credibility verdict would be Medium-High; adding EPS and FCF collapses this to Low-Medium [#J]

  • ❌ Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that apparent EBITDA sandbagging is a definitional artefact rather than genuine conservatism — management guides "adjusted EBITDA" excluding impairments and treats each impairment as non-recurring; as coal assets age and EU taxonomy pressure mounts, the frequency and scale of these "non-recurring" charges will likely increase, not decrease, further widening the gap between guided EBITDA and realised EPS [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • J-front verdict: EBITDA accuracy modestly improving post-2022 on wider guidance bands; EPS and FCF accuracy not improving — structural not cyclical drivers dominate the miss pattern [#J]

  • Recent accuracy — EBITDA: FY2022 and FY2023 show more consistent EBITDA beats vs FY2020–FY2021 which were noisier; wider guidance ranges introduced post-COVID appear to mechanically improve "beat" frequency [#A]

  • Accuracy improving — EBITDA band discipline: From ~FY2022, PGE adopted explicitly wider EBITDA guidance bands (PLN 1bn range vs ~PLN 0.5bn previously); this statistical change inflates apparent accuracy improvement [#J]

  • Sub: wider bands reduce the precision of guidance as a forecast signal; a PLN 1bn band on ~PLN 8bn EBITDA (12.5%) is wide vs RWE's €0.5bn on ~€9bn EBITDA (~5.5%) [#I]

  • Accuracy deteriorating — EPS: No improvement in EPS accuracy over L4 periods; average miss consistent at -30 to -50% in each period; driven by persistent coal impairments and volatile below-the-line items [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: Polish electricity price volatility (spot prices ranged PLN 200–800/MWh across the sample period) is a primary driver of earnings unpredictability; comparable to Verbund's exposure to Alpine hydro hydrology [#I]

  • Volatility driver — execution: CapEx overruns are an execution issue, not a macro issue; 4-of-4 overshoot pattern at consistent +10–13% suggests a systemic project estimation bias, not random error [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: The coal segment adds structural complexity — asset values are impairment-sensitive to both CO₂ prices and political timeline changes, creating binary valuation events that are structurally unpredictable [#J]

  • Metric — best accuracy: EBITDA (adjusted) — management can forecast its own adjusted earnings definition relatively well [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: FCF — 4-of-4 miss, including two sign reversals; FCF is the least reliable metric management guides toward [#A]

  • ❌ Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on accuracy trend is that any EBITDA improvement is a mechanical artefact of wider band adoption rather than genuine forecast discipline improvement; and as PGE enters the most capital-intensive phase of its transition (2025–2028), CapEx overrun risk and balance sheet complexity will increase, not decrease, making a real improvement in EPS/FCF accuracy unlikely without structural changes to guidance methodology [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • J-front verdict: Guidance assumptions are internally coherent but rest on several external variables that are highly volatile and partially outside management's control; the most fragile single assumption is the CO₂/capacity market revenue stack [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Near-term EBITDA guidance assumes Polish baseload electricity prices ~PLN 350–450/MWh for FY2025; spot prices have ranged PLN 200–750/MWh over 2020–2024; a 20% price miss wipes approximately PLN 0.8–1.2bn of EBITDA [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — capacity market: Management models capacity market payments of ~PLN 2.0–2.5bn per annum; these are contractually committed for existing capacity auctions through 2027 but future auction results are uncertain [#A]

  • Sub: capacity market revenues are a near-term floor but not a long-term growth driver; the European Commission is scrutinising Polish capacity mechanism design under state aid rules [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — renewables ramp: Medium-term EBITDA growth is ~60–70% dependent on renewables capacity scaling from ~2 GW current to ~11 GW by 2030; assumes Baltic Power (1.2 GW offshore) operational by 2027–2028 [#A]

  • Sub: Baltic Power is a JV with PKN Orlen; permitting, cable connection, and turbine supply chain delays have already pushed original timelines; management's 2027 commissioning assumption is tight [#J]

  • Margin assumption — coal cost: Coal segment cost assumptions embed ~PLN 400–450/tonne domestic hard coal; Polish coal prices are partially administered but globally linked; a sustained +20% coal cost shock reduces EBITDA by ~PLN 400–600m [#A]

  • Margin assumption — CO₂ cost: EU ETS CO₂ price assumption ~EUR 60–70/tonne; current market ~EUR 55–65/tonne (in range); management retains ~40% free allocation through 2025 under Phase IV EU ETS; from 2026, free allocation steps down — this is a structural margin headwind not fully priced in guidance [#A]

  • Sub: every EUR 10/tonne CO₂ price move above guidance impacts PGE EBITDA by ~PLN 300–500m given ~20Mt annual coal-generation CO₂ exposure [#J]

  • EPS assumption — impairment provisioning: Management's guided net profit explicitly excludes "extraordinary" impairments; the base rate of coal impairment charges over L4Y is ~PLN 1–2.5bn per annum; treating these as extraordinary is analytically questionable [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Polish CIT rate 19%; no material tax guidance uncertainty at current rates; EU minimum tax impact (~15% for large entities) marginally positive for PGE vs current Polish effective rate [#I]

  • EPS assumption — share count: ~1.87bn shares outstanding; no share buyback guidance; dilution risk from any equity financing for CAPEX programme is real but unquantified [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: PLN/EUR rate relevant for CO₂ costs (EUR-denominated); management assumes stable PLN in guidance; a 10% PLN depreciation vs EUR increases CO₂ cost burden by ~PLN 200–300m [#A]

  • Macro assumption — Polish GDP: Distribution segment growth tied to Polish industrial electricity demand; Poland GDP consensus ~2.5–3.5% for 2025–2026; a recession scenario is not priced in guidance [#I]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • J-front verdict: MEDIUM overall guidance credibility — EBITDA guidance is Medium-High credibility; EPS/net profit guidance is Low credibility; FCF guidance is Low credibility; the investment case must rest on EBITDA trajectory, not EPS [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM — with important segmentation: adjusted EBITDA guidance is credible at ±10%; EPS and FCF guidance are not credible as standalone forecast inputs [#J]

  • Green flag — EBITDA consistency: 3 of 4 EBITDA beats with modest positive variance (+4.5% average) is a genuine positive signal for EBITDA-anchored valuation [#A]

  • Green flag — distribution floor: Regulated distribution EBITDA (~PLN 3.0–3.5bn, ~40% of total) is highly predictable and provides a credible earnings floor; consistent with European distribution utility regulated return stability (Enagas, National Grid, Elia) [#I]

  • Green flag — capacity market contracts: Capacity market revenues (~PLN 2.0–2.5bn) are contractually committed through 2027; near-term EBITDA has a contractual backstop [#A]

  • Red flag — EPS chronic miss: 4 of 4 EPS misses averaging -40%; this is not a cyclical pattern but a structural one driven by recurring "non-guided" below-the-line charges [#A]

  • Red flag — FCF sign reversal: Two of four periods saw FCF sign reversal (guided positive, delivered negative); this level of FCF inaccuracy makes dividend and balance sheet modelling unreliable [#A]
