  • Company guide: Management has historically guided for "continued active acquisition activity" without hard numerical commitments; qualitative framing is consistent but non-binding [#A]

  • Sub: Teqnion has completed 30+ acquisitions since founding; historical pace roughly 4-6 per year at peak, moderating slightly in 2023-24

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Management language is disciplined — they publicly state unwillingness to overpay; however, as competition intensifies, the "disciplined" posture creates a real risk of doing fewer deals rather than bad deals [#A]

  • SS consensus: Carnegie, Pareto, and Redeye (named public coverage) all embed continued M&A cadence as the primary growth driver in their models; estimated consensus embeds 3-5 deals per year at historical multiples [#A]

  • Sub: No named analyst has published a bear case predicated on pipeline scarcity at [C]-accessible depth; this is a gap

  • Miss magnitude: At 2 deals/year vs 4-5 modelled, EPS CAGR drops by roughly 6-10 percentage points; combined with multiple compression, this is a -35 to -50% return scenario vs base [#A]

§5 — Key Driver #4: Interest Cost and Balance Sheet Drag

  • J-front verdict: This is an underappreciated EPS headwind in a higher-for-longer rate environment; TEQ-SE's acquisition-funded model carries structural net debt that has become materially more expensive since 2022 [#J]

  • Driver definition: TEQ-SE funds acquisitions partly via debt; rising and sustained higher interest rates increase net interest expense, directly reducing pre-tax earnings and EPS; balance sheet leverage is therefore both a growth enabler and an EPS drag [#A]

  • Sub: Unlike large-cap industrials, TEQ-SE cannot access bond markets cheaply; reliance on bank credit at floating rates creates direct sensitivity to Riksbank rate path

  • Mechanism to EPS: Every 100bps increase in net debt cost on SEK 500-700m estimated net debt = SEK 5-7m additional interest = ~SEK 0.15-0.20 EPS headwind; at 20x earnings = SEK 3-4 share price impact per 100bps [#A]

  • Sub: Conversely, Riksbank rate cuts (which have begun in 2024-25) are a direct EPS tailwind

  • Mechanism to SP: Higher interest cost → EPS compression → reduced acquisition capacity → double headwind on narrative; lower rates → opposite [#A]

  • Company guide: Management has not issued explicit guidance on interest cost trajectory; debt management is implicit in M&A discipline framing [#A]

  • Track record: Net debt has grown with acquisitions — this is by design; however, interest coverage has remained adequate at current EBITDA levels; no disclosed covenant stress [#A]

  • Conservatism vs optimism: Sell-side models likely embed some rate normalisation benefit; actual rate path under Riksbank is uncertain; 2024-25 rate cuts provide some relief but rates remain above 2020-21 lows [#A]

  • SS consensus: No [C]-accessible sell-side note specifically addresses TEQ-SE's interest cost sensitivity; Redeye has flagged leverage as a monitoring item; Carnegie and Pareto modelling assumptions on rates are not publicly disclosed [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Net interest expense of SEK 30-50m estimated (base rate); at Riksbank rates declining from ~4% toward 2.5-3%, potential saving of SEK 5-15m = SEK 0.15-0.45 EPS tailwind over 24M horizon [#A]

  • Recent delivery: 2023-24 interest expense increased materially vs 2021-22 as rate environment shifted; this is a confirmed headwind that partially offset strong EBITDA growth from M&A; quantification requires proprietary data [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Indutrade net debt/EBITDA ~2.0-2.5x; Lifco ~1.5-2.0x; Diploma ~1.5-2.0x; TEQ-SE's leverage is likely in a similar range but at smaller absolute scale, meaning interest rate sensitivity as a % of EPS is relatively higher [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Riksbank rate cutting cycle underway — Sweden's central bank has cut from ~4% toward 2.5-3% in 2024-25; this directly reduces floating-rate debt costs and is a genuine near-term EPS tailwind [#A]

  • Macro headwind: Rates remain well above the near-zero environment of 2019-21 — even at 2.5-3%, interest burden is structurally higher than the era when TEQ-SE's valuation norms were established [#A]

  • Visibility: Moderate — Riksbank rate path is forecasted with reasonable confidence over 12M but uncertain at 24M; debt quantum depends on M&A pace [#A]

  • Bear case: Riksbank pauses rate cuts at 3%+ due to resurgent inflation; TEQ-SE's interest burden stays elevated; combined with slower M&A or organic weakness, EPS growth disappoints consensus by 10-15% [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Global rate re-acceleration (tariff-driven inflation) causes Riksbank to halt or reverse cuts → interest cost stays high → EPS misses Q3/Q4 2025 consensus → de-rating [#J]

  • Miss magnitude: Scenario where rates stay at 4% vs modelled 2.5% = additional SEK 7-10m interest cost annually = ~SEK 0.20-0.30 EPS headwind; not enormous standalone but compounding with other misses [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: ⚡ This is the most under-discussed driver in public sell-side framing — the market appears to have modelled rate normalisation benefits that are not yet fully delivered, and a rate-stall scenario is not priced [#J]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility (risk framing) — Riksbank cuts are underway and provide relief; residual risk is rate-path reversal and debt quantum growth from continued acquisitions

§6 — Key Driver #5: Reinvestment Rate and Retained Earnings Compounding

  • J-front verdict: High plausibility — TEQ-SE's model of retaining substantially all earnings for reinvestment into acquisitions is structurally sound and the compounding mechanics are well-evidenced over the historical period [#J]

  • Driver definition: TEQ-SE retains a high proportion of earnings (low or no dividend) and reinvests into new acquisitions; the compounding of returns on invested capital over time is the fundamental wealth-creation engine [#A]

  • Sub: This is the Berkshire/Constellation-style capital allocation model at Nordic SME scale

  • Mechanism to EPS: High reinvestment rate + positive spread between ROIC and WACC → EPS compounds geometrically rather than linearly; the duration of the reinvestment runway is the key variable [#A]

  • Mechanism to SP: Market assigns a premium P/E to businesses where ROIC > WACC and reinvestment opportunities are large; as TEQ-SE builds the track record, the premium should expand [#A]

  • Company guide: Management has explicitly adopted a low/no-dividend policy in favour of reinvestment; this is stated strategy and internally consistent with the M&A model [#A]

  • Track record: ROIC appears to be positive vs WACC based on public data — group EBITA margins of 13-15% on a capital-light acquisition model implies strong returns on tangible capital; goodwill-adjusted returns are the key unknown [#A]

  • Sub: Teqnion's founders (Daniel Zhang, Johan Steene) have been explicit about capital allocation philosophy in public interviews — "owner-operator" framing is credible

  • Conservatism vs optimism: This driver is structurally robust — the math of compounding works as long as ROIC exceeds WACC and reinvestment opportunities remain; the key risk is that both degrade together [#A]

  • SS consensus: This driver is implicitly embedded in all sell-side models rather than explicitly analysed; no [C]-accessible broker publishes a ROIC vs WACC decomposition for TEQ-SE [#A]

  • Estimated EPS impact: Compounding at 15% ROIC on growing capital base with no dividend leakage: over 5 years, this mechanism alone can deliver 2-3x book value growth; over 24M, the marginal EPS contribution from reinvestment is roughly 10-15% additional vs a dividend-paying peer [#A]

  • Recent delivery: Consistent with strategy — no significant dividend declared in recent periods; capital is being deployed into acquisitions at historical pace; no sign of policy reversal [#A]

  • Peer benchmark: Constellation Software (archetype) has sustained 25%+ ROIC over 20 years; Lifco and Indutrade demonstrate 15-20% ROIC; TEQ-SE is earlier stage but the mechanism is identical [#A]

  • Macro tailwind: Capital-light model with low physical capex means this driver is relatively insulated from macro; the main sensitivity is acquisition market pricing [#A]

  • Macro headwind: WACC has risen with rates — the spread between ROIC and WACC has compressed from 2021-22 peak; as long as ROIC > WACC the compounding is positive, but the spread determines the quality [#A]

  • Visibility: High — reinvestment rate policy is clearly stated and structurally embedded; the risk is strategy drift not policy reversal [#A]

  • Bear case: Management begins paying dividends under shareholder pressure as portfolio matures → reinvestment rate declines → compounding slows → TEQ-SE re-rates toward income-stock multiples rather than compounder multiples [#J]

  • Miss scenario: Acquisition market becomes so competitive that ROIC on marginal deals falls below WACC; management continues deploying capital into negative-spread acquisitions rather than returning cash [#J]

  • Miss magnitude: Structural compounding mechanism failing is a 5-10 year scenario, not a 24M risk; near-term miss magnitude from this driver alone is low [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The compounding thesis is elegant in theory but requires honest goodwill-adjusted ROIC disclosure — TEQ-SE's reported margins exclude amortisation; true ROIC on total capital deployed (including goodwill paid) is the decisive metric and is not publicly disclosed [#J]

Plausibility verdict: high plausibility — reinvestment model is structurally sound and clearly stated; primary risk is marginal deal quality degradation reducing the ROIC-WACC spread, not a 24M event

§10 — Driver Synthesis + Sceptical Synthesis
