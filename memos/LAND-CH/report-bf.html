EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 116 findings

Section summary
  1. management has successfully simplified the corporate structure into two dedicated segments focused entirely on smart grid infrastructure
  2. the business model leverages Connected Platforms for scale and Grid Intelligence for high-margin recurring monetization
  3. divesting the complex EMEA business to AURELIUS removes historically margin-dilutive, capital-intensive operations
  4. long-duration utility contracts and a massive USD 3.9 billion backlog provide multi-year revenue and cash visibility
  5. secular demand tailwinds from AI data centers, electrification, and grid edge sensing drive utility capital plans
  6. the transition to a highly experienced, action-focused leadership team under Peter Mainz accelerates strategic execution

Landis+Gyr has executed a decisive corporate transformation by divesting its margin-dilutive EMEA business and reorganizing into two focused segments: Connected Platforms (representing approximately 76% of FY25 revenue) and Grid Intelligence (representing approximately 24% of FY25 revenue). This strategic pivot concentrates resources on high-value ANSI-standard markets in North America and Asia Pacific, eliminating historically complex and capital-intensive European operations. The company serves over 2,000 utilities across 40 countries with more than 127 million deployed intelligent devices, anchored by a massive USD 3.9 billion contracted backlog that provides over three years of revenue visibility. Secular demand tailwinds from generational electricity load growth–driven by AI data center expansion, electrification, and grid edge sensing–are fueling unprecedented utility capital investment, with large investor-owned utilities projected to spend USD 1.4 trillion over five years and US annual load growth reaching approximately 2.8% through 2035. The transition to highly experienced, action-focused leadership under CEO Peter Mainz (previously CEO of peer Sensus) and CFO Davinder Athwal accelerates strategic execution and positions the company to capitalize on this infrastructure investment cycle.
