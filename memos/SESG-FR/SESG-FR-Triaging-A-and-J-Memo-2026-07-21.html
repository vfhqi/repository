B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
Satellite fleet operator, multi-orbit. SES S.A. (SES) leases satellite capacity across geostationary and medium-earth-orbit (MEO) fleets, serving video distribution, government, and aviation/maritime mobility customers
2.
Transformational scale event. The mid-2025 acquisition of Intelsat nearly doubled the combined entity's revenue base and is the single dominant fact governing the current investment case
3.
Segment mix, post-merger. Revenue splits across legacy Video (declining, ~30% of group revenue), Networks (government and enterprise connectivity, +106.0% year-on-year to EUR 556m in the most recent quarter), and Mobility (aviation, +207.8% reported)
4.
Financial scale. Pro forma combined revenue of EUR 3.7bn, backed by an EUR 8bn backlog; adjusted EBITDA margin compressed to 45.4% from a pre-merger 51.4%
5.
Balance sheet. Net leverage expanded from 1.1x to 4.1x net debt to EBITDA following the debt-funded Intelsat acquisition, more than double Eutelsat's 2.0x
6.
Geography and ownership. Luxembourg-headquartered; the Luxembourg Government holds 33.3% of voting rights against only 16.7% of economic interest, a dual-class structure that recurs through the memo
7.
Investment style implication. This is an event-driven, high-leverage turnaround case, not a steady compounder: the thesis rests on cost-saving delivery and multi-orbit demand offsetting video erosion and low-earth-orbit (LEO) price deflation before the balance sheet constrains flexibility
8.
Competitive backdrop. SpaceX's Starlink (10.3 million subscribers, USD 11.4bn revenue, 63.0% EBITDA margin) has driven a roughly 77.0% five-year collapse in satellite bandwidth costs, the single largest external force acting on the whole cohort
9.
Sovereign optionality. SES leads the SpaceRise consortium for the EU's EUR 10.6bn IRIS2 sovereign satellite program, a genuine but multi-year, capex-heavy revenue stream
10.
Leadership reset. Chief executive Adel Al-Saleh (since February 2024) and chief financial officer Elisabeth Pataki (since June 2025) both post-date the activist pressure from Appaloosa LP (a 7.0% economic interest)
