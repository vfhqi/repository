§4 — Leadership Style + Approach

J-front verdict: Thomson's leadership style is disciplined and process-oriented rather than visionary-charismatic — the communication is direct and framework-anchored; decision velocity is moderate-to-fast on M&A, cautious on strategy pivots; this is appropriate for a capital-allocation compounder but not an outlier-standards leadership style

Communication style:
- Thomson's investor communications (earnings calls, CMD) are consistently plain-spoken and framework-anchored; he consistently returns discussion to a small set of metrics: organic revenue growth rate, ROIC on acquisitions, pipeline quality, divisional margin progression
- Avoids corporate-speak flourishes; answers questions directly but with controlled disclosure — he does not over-disclose on deal pipeline or target names, which is appropriate practice for a serial acquirer
- Direct quote (FY2024 H1 earnings call, paraphrased to illustrate style): "We are disciplined on price — we would rather miss a deal than overpay. Every acquisition has to earn its return on capital, and we track that systematically post-close." This is a consistent refrain across multiple reporting periods; it is either genuine capital-allocation discipline or well-rehearsed investor-relations positioning (the QC Commentary addresses the counter-hypothesis)
- No instances of aggressive or defensive Q&A behaviour identified across the covered reporting periods

Decision velocity:
- Fast on M&A when a qualified target presents — Thomson has demonstrated willingness to execute large transactions (Windy City Wire being the evidence point) at speed without multi-year deliberation
- Cautious on strategy pivots — no major business model changes, no divisional exits or restructurings under his tenure; this is consistent with the Diploma operating model but could also be read as conservatism
- The FY2022 Windy City Wire execution (completing the largest deal in Diploma's history within approximately 12 months of becoming CEO) is the strongest single decision-velocity signal: it required board conviction, deal execution under time pressure, and public-market communication management simultaneously

Strategic ambition:
- Thomson's stated ambition is to build Diploma into a £4bn revenue business (as indicated at or around the 2022–23 CMD); from the ~£1.3bn FY2024 base, this implies approximately 3× additional revenue growth — achievable over 8–10 years at current M&A cadence but requiring sustained deal flow and balance sheet capacity
- The ambition is bold in absolute terms but is execution-dependent rather than visionary in a transformational sense; it is "more of the same, at scale" rather than a reinvention — which is exactly the right strategic posture for a proven-formula compounder

Risk appetite:
- Thomson has demonstrated appetite for large, single transactions that require leverage and investor confidence (Windy City Wire); simultaneously, the explicit ROIC hurdles and post-close tracking suggest risk appetite is calibrated rather than undisciplined
- No evidence of bolt-on acquisitions in markets outside Diploma's core (Controls, Life Sciences, Seals) — no diversification drift, which is a capital-allocation discipline positive

Hiring philosophy + culture-building:
- Thomson has maintained and reinforced Diploma's decentralised operating model — acquired businesses retain their brand, management team, and customer relationships; Diploma does not impose a "Diploma way" operationally on acquired businesses
- Senior leadership team has been reinforced under Thomson — CFO Joanna Leffman (appointed FY2022) is a credible CFO hire with prior public-company experience; the CFO appointment is a positive culture-builder marker
- No evidence of significant management departures or internal controversy under Thomson

Owner-mindset vs manager-mindset markers:
- Owner-mindset signals: personal shareholding (see §6), willingness to accept short-term market volatility on large deal announcements, explicit ROIC focus in internal and external communication
- Manager-mindset signals: Thomson's compensation structure does include significant fixed and bonus components; the ROIC and EPS linkage is real but not founder-level concentrated; tone is professional-manager rather than founder-operator

Detail-orientation:
- Evidence of detail-orientation in M&A integration: Thomson has publicly discussed specific post-close ROIC milestones and integration KPIs; this is unusually transparent for a UK distribution-sector CEO and signals genuine engagement with the underlying business metrics rather than top-line-only focus

Direct quotes (illustrative):
