§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

Scatec exhibits a split-metric pattern: a mild sandbagging tendency on physical/operational metrics (production, MW) paired with a consistent over-promising tendency on financial metrics (EBITDA, net profit), creating a misleading impression of guidance credibility when assessed on the more visible operational lines.

  • Pattern — operational: Sandbagger signal on production and capacity — management has beaten or met production guidance in 3 of 4 periods; the operating teams appear to build weather/availability buffers into production estimates [#J]
  • Sub: Production sandbagging is genuine but low-stakes — production upside does not automatically translate to financial upside if FX or interest costs move adversely [#A]
  • Pattern — financial: Over-promiser signal on EBITDA and net profit — management's financial guidance embeds optimistic FX rates, optimistic refinancing assumptions, and minimal impairment provisions; all three have proven structurally aggressive [#J]
  • Sub: Over-promising on financials is more consequential to equity valuation than production sandbagging [#I]
  • Mixed pattern — split-metric: The combination of operational sandbagging + financial over-promising creates a specific investor risk: management appears credible on the metrics it highlights, while the metrics that drive equity value are systematically optimistic [#J]
  • Beat consistency — production: 3/4 periods met or beat; beat magnitude ~2-5% vs midpoint; consistent but not large [#A]
  • Miss consistency — EBITDA: 3/4 periods missed midpoint; miss magnitude ~3-14%; largest miss (H1 2024) driven by interest costs, suggesting the miss magnitude is growing, not shrinking [#J]
  • Magnitude — beat (production): Average ~3% above midpoint; statistically modest; consistent with genuine operational buffer-building [#A]
  • Magnitude — miss (EBITDA): Average ~-6% below midpoint across L4 periods; H1 2024 at -14% represents a step-change in miss magnitude; directionally worsening [#J]
  • Pattern shift — FY2024: H1 2024 is the first period where the EBITDA miss fell below the stated guidance range, not just below midpoint; this is a qualitative step-change from "in-range miss" to "guidance-range miss" [#J]
  • Sub: A guidance-range miss (vs. midpoint miss) is more damaging to credibility because it invalidates management's own stated uncertainty bounds [#I]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

The guidance assumption stack carries three fragile embedded assumptions — EM FX stability, project debt cost, and construction timeline — any two of which deteriorating simultaneously would reduce guided EBITDA by 20-30% and guided equity IRR by 200-400bps.

  • Revenue assumption — power price: Long-term PPAs (power purchase agreements) are the dominant revenue driver; most projects are contracted at fixed USD/EUR/local currency prices; this provides revenue visibility and is the strongest element of the assumption stack [#J]
  • Sub: PPA counterparty risk (government/utility off-takers in EM) is underweighted in guidance; payment delays in Ukraine, Egypt, and Pakistan have been recurring [#A]
  • Revenue assumption — FX translation: Guidance embeds mid-year spot FX rates for ZAR, EGP, PKR, UAH, BDT; no disclosed buffer or hedging offset; EGP spot at time of FY2023 guidance was ~30% stronger than the rate prevailing through most of FY2024 — an assumption that was structurally wrong at issuance [#J]
  • Sub: EGP component alone represented ~NOK 300-500m EBITDA sensitivity in FY2024; management did not signal this adequately [#I]
  • Revenue assumption — production: Management uses P50 (50th percentile) solar irradiance forecasts; industry-standard but means a 50% probability of production shortfall in any year [#A]
  • Sub: Using P50 rather than P75 is a mild sandbagging restraint; legitimate industry practice but creates systematic downside probability [#I]
  • Margin assumption — cost: O&M cost guidance assumes 2-3% annual inflation on contracted O&M; actual EM cost inflation in some markets (Pakistan, Uganda) has exceeded 10-15%; the O&M cost assumption is relatively benign but not zero-risk [#J]
  • Margin assumption — interest cost: This is the single most fragile assumption; project finance debt is predominantly at variable rates in EM markets; management guidance for 2024-2026 appears to assume refinancing at blended 7-9% vs. prevailing EM project finance costs of 9-12%+; a 200bps upward shift adds ~NOK 400-600m in annual interest costs [#J]
  • Sub: LIBOR/SOFR transition + EM risk premium widening post-2022 has structurally lifted the rate assumption baseline; management has been slow to reset [#A]
  • EPS assumption — interest at HoldCo level: HoldCo bonds (Scatec corporate-level debt) carry fixed coupons; this layer is reasonably guided; the miss source is project-level debt, not HoldCo debt [#J]
  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate assumption ~20-25%; EM withholding taxes and royalties have intermittently pushed the effective rate higher; not the primary miss driver but adds noise [#A]
  • EPS assumption — share count: Stable; no disclosed buyback or dilution plan; not a material source of guidance error [#A]
  • Macro assumption — EM construction costs: Steel, copper, and logistics costs for solar construction have partially normalised from 2022 peaks; management guidance for 2025-2027 capex per MW appears to assume ~USD 750-900k/MW (utility-scale solar); this is achievable in best-case procurement but assumes no supply chain disruption [#J]
  • Macro assumption — financial close pace: Medium-term guidance assumes 800-1,000 MW of financial close annually; historical average has been ~600-700 MW; the financial close assumption is arguably the most aggressive in the medium-term plan [#J]
  • Sub: Lower financial close volume delays revenue, defers EBITDA contribution, and may force HoldCo leverage upward to fund the equity commitment pipeline [#I]
