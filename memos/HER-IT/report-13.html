§1 — Current Management Guidance

Hera's current guidance framework is anchored by the 2024-2028 Business Plan presented at the November 2023 Capital Market Day; all forward targets reference that plan unless subsequently updated at quarterly results.

  • Overall §1 verdict: Management has provided a quantified multi-year BP through 2028 with clear metric targets; guidance is comprehensive but inherently dependent on regulatory continuity and macro normalisation [#J]

  • Source — investor day: 2024-2028 BP presented 15 November 2023 at CMD; most recent update at FY24 results (March 2025) confirmed BP trajectory intact with no material revision [#A]

  • FY current — revenue: FY25 revenue guidance not explicitly quantified as a point estimate; management references ~4-5% CAGR organic growth through the plan period implying FY25 revenues of approximately €5.0-5.1bn (FY24 base ~€4.8bn) [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management targets ~€1.22bn EBITDA for FY25 (implicitly derived from BP slope); FY24 reported EBITDA was approximately €1.14bn, requiring ~7% YoY growth [#A]

  • Sub: FY25 EBITDA step-up partially depends on waste margin recovery from depressed 2023-2024 levels — mechanically achievable if spot waste disposal prices normalise

  • FY current — EPS: Net profit guidance not issued as a point FY25 figure; BP implies net profit of ~€285m for FY25, up from ~€260m in FY24, representing ~10% YoY growth [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: FY28 EBITDA target ~€1.35bn; net profit ~€320-330m; FCF generation sufficient to cover dividend with progressive growth (DPS +5% per annum implied) [#A]

  • Medium-term targets: Total CapEx 2024-2028 ~€4.4bn, heavily weighted to regulated networks (~65%); net debt/EBITDA target maintained at ≤3.0x throughout plan period [#A]

  • Sub: CapEx programme is substantially committed — ~40% contracted at CMD date; execution risk on uncontracted portion

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit free cash flow guidance issued; management communicates that operating cash flow will comfortably cover CapEx and dividends given regulated revenue visibility [#A]

  • Update timing: Quarterly earnings releases provide BP progress commentary but no intra-year guidance upgrades are standard practice; management reserves BP revisions for CMD events (annual cadence) [#A]

  • Caveat: All targets stated as "organic, excluding bolt-on M&A"; Hera has historically made small bolt-on acquisitions (~€50-100m per year) that add 1-2% to reported revenue but are excluded from BP organic baseline [#A]

  • Conditional assumption: BP assumes ARERA regulatory framework stable through 2027 rate period; any reset of distribution WACC or waste treatment tariffs would require BP revision [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that EBITDA targets embed a waste-segment recovery that has been delayed twice already — if waste volumes and margins remain compressed through FY25, the €1.22bn EBITDA implied midpoint becomes a stretch rather than a floor; management has not explicitly disclosed the sensitivity of the BP to a no-recovery waste scenario [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Overall §3 verdict: Hera exhibits a mild sandbagging posture on EBITDA and net profit guidance; the pattern is consistent but not extreme — management tends to set BP targets at moderate conservatism then confirm or modestly upgrade at annual CMD [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal: BP targets for EBITDA have been set below subsequently-achieved actuals in 3 of 4 recent periods; the average initial guidance conservatism buffer is approximately 3-5% below eventual delivery — consistent with a "commit to achievable, outperform modestly" culture [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal: Revenue guidance carries an over-promising risk at the BP level — FY23 and FY24 organic revenue growth was lower than BP long-run CAGR implied, with headline revenues inflated by pass-through energy pricing rather than genuine volume/mix improvement [#A]

  • Honest tracker: On regulated network metrics (RAB growth, connections, infrastructure investment), management is an honest tracker — actuals closely mirror BP schedules, consistent with externally-determined regulatory frameworks that reduce internal optimism bias [#A]

  • Beat consistency: EBITDA beat in 3 of 4 periods with positive but small magnitude (+1.8% to +7.1%); no period of large positive surprise; this is characteristic of a sandbagger with moderate cushion rather than a structural outperformer [#A]

  • Miss consistency: Only one EBITDA miss in L4 periods (FY23); context was sector-wide (Italian waste peers A2A and Iren also missed waste EBITDA targets in FY23) — pattern is not company-specific execution failure [#A]

  • Sub: When adjusted for the waste sector miss, underlying networks/services EBITDA beat guidance in all 4 of 4 periods

  • Magnitude — beat: Average EBITDA beat +€31m on a ~€1.0bn base; not large in absolute terms; suggests conservatism is a feature but not extreme sandbagging [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss: Single EBITDA miss of ~−€20m; magnitude modest; no evidence of significant earnings quality decline around the miss [#A]

  • Pattern shift: The beat magnitude is compressing: FY21 beat +7.1%, FY22 beat +6.1%, FY24 beat +1.8%; if this is a structural trend, the sandbagging cushion is thinning as the BP baseline becomes more efficiently calibrated [#A]

  • Improving accuracy: Accuracy on the EBITDA line has improved: FY23 miss was isolated and partially pre-disclosed; FY24 delivered close to plan; management's transparency around waste-margin risk has improved across BP cycles [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that compressing beat magnitudes (from +7% to +2%) may signal the sandbagging cushion is nearly exhausted — the FY25-FY28 BP may be more tightly calibrated, leaving less buffer for the beat-and-raise dynamic that underpins analyst confidence; if the next negative macro shock occurs (waste markets, energy prices), there is less cushion to absorb it without missing [#J]

§4 — Trend in Guidance Accuracy

  • Overall §4 verdict: Guidance accuracy has been broadly stable but the beat buffer is shrinking; no deterioration in accuracy per se, but reduced margin of outperformance makes the current BP more sensitive to execution [#J]

  • Recent accuracy: FY24 EBITDA delivered +1.8% above BP Year 1 target — tight but positive; net profit +2.0%; leverage in-line; recent accuracy is high in directional terms but the magnitude of outperformance is near-zero [#A]

  • Historical accuracy: FY21 and FY22 period saw meaningful EBITDA beats (+7%); those periods benefited from energy market tailwinds and RAB growth acceleration that was not fully anticipated in the 2019-2023 BP baseline [#A]

  • Accuracy deteriorating: Not deteriorating in miss frequency, but deteriorating in beat magnitude; the gap between initial BP and delivery has compressed from ~7% to ~2% across L4 periods — a 70% compression of the outperformance buffer [#A]

  • Volatility driver — macro: The FY23 miss was entirely macro-driven (waste market); the broader trajectory of European waste-to-energy economics and secondary commodity prices introduces macro volatility that management cannot fully control or predict [#A]

  • Volatility driver — execution: No evidence of execution-driven misses in L4 periods; regulated network segment (the largest profit contributor) has been highly accurate across all periods — execution volatility is concentrated in the market-facing waste segment [#A]

  • Volatility driver — complexity: Hera's multi-segment structure (networks, waste, energy, water) means cross-segment diversification historically absorbed volatility; this acts as a natural accuracy buffer for overall EBITDA even when individual segments miss [#A]

  • Metric — best accuracy: Regulated revenue metrics (distribution tariffs, water tariffs) — ARERA-determined, low-volatility, guidance accuracy near-perfect [#A]

  • Metric — worst accuracy: Revenue (energy pass-through), and waste EBITDA (market-sensitive) — both are high-variance relative to BP estimates [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the accuracy trend is that as the waste segment normalises (waste EBITDA recovering toward BP), the remaining upside relative to current BP is limited — the structural diversification buffer has already been "used" in FY23; a second consecutive waste sector miss in FY25 would produce a net EBITDA miss because the networks segment is already delivering close to its maximum achievable trajectory [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Overall §6 verdict: The BP assumption stack is coherent but carries a critical regulatory dependency; the most fragile single assumption is ARERA tariff continuity; the most underappreciated risk is simultaneous waste margin and regulatory disappointment [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — market: BP assumes Italian energy distribution volumes grow ~1-2% pa and waste treatment volumes recover to 2019-2021 norms (~7-8m tonnes treated pa by Hera group); current spot levels of ~6.5-6.8m tonnes represent a ~3-5% volume gap to BP assumption [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: ARERA-determined tariff increases are the primary revenue pricing driver for networks; BP assumes modest real tariff growth in line with regulatory cost recognition; this is a conservative assumption given ARERA's historically supportive methodology [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: BP explicitly excludes bolt-on M&A contribution; historically Hera adds €50-100m revenue pa from small tuck-ins; if this continues, reported revenue will modestly exceed BP [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: BP assumes operating cost inflation of ~3-4% pa with offsetting productivity gains; given Italian wage agreements and energy input costs, this is achievable but tight — any resurgence of energy cost inflation could erode the margin expansion assumption [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: The networks segment is expected to provide operating leverage as RAB grows ~6-8% pa; this is structural and regulatorily-guaranteed — the most reliable margin lever in the BP [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Waste-to-energy segment margin recovery from ~12% to ~15%+ EBITDA margin relies on waste gate-fee pricing improving as waste volumes normalise; this is market-sensitive and the least defensible margin assumption in the stack [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: BP assumes effective tax rate ~28-30%; Italian IRES/IRAP rates are stable; no significant tax assumption risk visible [#A]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Hera does not run buyback programmes; share count is stable; no dilution risk from equity issuance given strong FCF [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt grows in absolute terms as CapEx is deployed but net debt/EBITDA stays ≤3.0x; finance costs assumed broadly stable given fixed-rate hedging on debt portfolio (~70% fixed rate) [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: Italian GDP growth assumed ~1% pa (broadly consensus); Hera's regulated revenues are largely GDP-insensitive; waste volumes have some cyclical sensitivity but muted [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: EUR-denominated; no material FX exposure [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity: Energy commodity price assumption: BP models normalised energy prices (not 2022 crisis levels); a resurgence of energy price inflation would increase revenue (pass-through) but could also inflate operating costs in the waste segment (fuel, electricity) [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Overall §7 verdict: Guidance credibility is MEDIUM-HIGH; management has delivered on major BP metrics in 3 of 4 recent plan periods, the assumption stack is largely anchored to regulated frameworks, and the one notable miss (FY23) was sector-wide rather than idiosyncratic [#J]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY24 delivered at BP Year 1 targets; FY24 marks the second consecutive year (FY22, FY24) of at or above BP delivery on EBITDA; the 2024-2028 BP has started on-track [#A]

  • Green flag — conservative assumption: ARERA tariff assumptions in the current BP are modelled conservatively vs the actual FY24 tariff outcome — ARERA's distribution tariff review was broadly neutral to slightly favourable; management did not "bank" the upside but retained it as buffer [AS-Mediobanca] [#A]

  • Green flag — execution track record: Networks segment (regulated, ~55-60% of EBITDA) has never missed a BP guidance target in L4 plan periods — the most reliable and material segment is structurally credible [#A]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FY23 EBITDA miss (−1.8%) was the first in multiple BP cycles; even though macro-driven, it broke a clean beat streak and revealed that waste segment guidance has limited buffer [#A]

  • Thesis sensitivity: The 25%+ IRR case requires the 2028 BP net profit (~€320-330m) to be achieved; at a 15x P/E exit and current entry, ~€310m net profit minimum is needed — BP guidance has ~€10-20m buffer above minimum required; not comfortable [#J]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on credibility is that the MEDIUM-HIGH verdict is heavily dependent on the networks segment continuing to mechanically deliver, and implicitly assumes the waste recovery will occur even though it has now been delayed for two consecutive years; if the credibility of the waste-recovery assumption is stripped out, the BP credibility reduces to MEDIUM — and the EPS CAGR of 8-10% becomes 5-6% (closer to inflation than real growth) [#J]
