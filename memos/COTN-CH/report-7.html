Driver 1: WFE Cycle Recovery (Weight: ~65% of earnings case)

The WFE cycle inflection is confirmed in the order book — this is not a projection. The primary uncertainty is duration into FY2027, not the fact of the recovery itself.

Nature of the Driver

PCT (Plasma Control Technologies) generated CHF 247m revenue in FY2025, approximately 61% of group revenue. This division supplies power generators, plasma control systems, and RF power amplifiers to WFE OEMs — principally ASML (for EUV), Lam Research (etch), Applied Materials (CVD/PVD), and Tokyo Electron. PCT revenue is directly correlated with global WFE spending. #I

Analysis: WFE spending cycle: Global WFE spending peaked at approximately $105bn in FY2022, troughed at approximately $80bn in FY2023, and recovered to approximately $90bn in FY2024. SEMI forecasts FY2025 WFE at $100bn+ and FY2026 at $108-115bn. The primary drivers of the current cycle are AI infrastructure buildout (HBM, advanced DRAM, logic for AI accelerators) and Gate All Around node transitions at TSMC and Samsung. #A

Signal Strength

Judgement: Q1 2026 orders: CHF 147.6m (+22.3% YoY). Book-to-bill: 1.4. This is the strongest quarterly order intake since the cycle peak in 2022. The backlog build is consistent with OEM commentary from ASML (record 2025 orders), Lam Research (strong H1 2026 WFE outlook), and Applied Materials. #I

Judgement: The WFE cycle recovery is not a projection — it is in the order book. The question is the duration and amplitude of the upcycle. #J

Bull/Bear Scenario

Bull: WFE spending reaches $115bn in FY2026 and sustains into FY2027 driven by AI capex. PCT revenue grows to CHF 280-300m in FY2026, CHF 330-360m in FY2027. EBITDA margin approaches 15%. EPS of CHF 20-25 by FY2027. Stock could trade at CHF 450-500 on 20-22x. #A

Judgement: Assessment: Yellow-Green. The cycle inflection is confirmed. The bull scenario is achievable but requires sustained WFE spending into FY2027. The bear scenario is not bearish per se — it just implies less upside than the current price implies. #J

Driver 4: Synertia RF Platform — Structural Growth Driver (Weight: Future PCT growth)

Synertia is strategically compelling but commercially unproven at volume. It is an option on structural PCT share gain, not a confirmed driver. Conviction requires AS evidence.

What Is Synertia?

Synertia is COMET's proprietary RF power and plasma control platform, designed specifically for EUV lithography (ASML systems) and Gate All Around transistor manufacturing (TSMC, Samsung, Intel). It integrates RF power generation, plasma monitoring, and process control into a single system — replacing multiple components that were previously sourced separately. #I

Analysis: The strategic rationale: as semiconductor nodes shrink toward 2nm and below, plasma process control becomes increasingly critical. The tolerance for variation in RF power delivery narrows. COMET's Synertia platform offers higher precision than incumbent solutions. #A

Market Opportunity

COMET estimates the Synertia addressable market at CHF 400-600m annually by FY2027, based on assumed WFE spending levels and Synertia attach rates in EUV and GAA applications. Current Synertia revenue is small (CHF 20-30m estimated) — the platform is in early commercial deployment. #I

Analysis: Attach rate thesis: If Synertia achieves 35-40% attach rates in EUV/GAA applications (i.e., specified by ASML or used in 35-40% of relevant WFE tools), PCT revenue could sustainably exceed CHF 320-340m at the next cycle peak — approximately 30-40% above current trough levels — even without WFE spend growth. #A

Risk Assessment

Synertia is an unproven platform at volume. COMET has historically competed against Cohu, AIMM, and AEIS in RF power delivery for WFE. AEIS in particular has a strong incumbent position in power delivery subsystems. Synertia's differentiation (integrated plasma monitoring) is technically compelling but the displacement of established AEIS relationships at key WFE OEMs will take time. #A

The AS research should probe: (1) customer qualification status of Synertia at ASML and key US OEMs, (2) competitive response from AEIS, (3) timeline to material revenue contribution. #OQ

Judgement: Assessment: Orange. Synertia is strategically important but not yet commercially demonstrated at scale. It is an option on structural share gain, not a confirmed driver. Conviction on Synertia requires AS evidence of customer qualifications and design wins. #J

Driver 5: IXS Recovery — Free Option or Continued Drag?

IXS is a free option if European industrial end-markets stabilise — but the probability is only 50-60%. The cost of failure is continued EBITDA headwind the market already knows about.

IXS Overview

IXS (Industrial X-ray Systems) generated approximately CHF 145-155m revenue in FY2025 (the division breakdown is estimated — COMET does not fully disaggregate in public filings). IXS serves automotive (non-destructive testing of cast parts, EV battery modules), aerospace (structural inspection), and electronics (PCB inspection). Revenue is predominantly European. End-markets are cyclical with automotive and industrial production as the primary drivers. #I

Current State

IXS EBITDA margin: -6.8% in FY2025. Management has taken CHF 15-20m in restructuring charges over FY2024-FY2025. Headcount reductions, facility consolidations, and product rationalisation are complete or nearly complete. The cost base has been reset. #I

Management guidance: IXS EBITDA breakeven in FY2026. This requires European automotive and industrial production to stabilise (not recover — just stop deteriorating), and the restructured cost base to be fully effective. #I

Bull/Bear on IXS

Bull: European automotive production stabilises in H1 2026 (new model launches, hybrid ramp). EV battery inspection demand grows (COMET has specific EV testing products). IXS reaches breakeven in FY2026, positive margin in FY2027. The 200-250bps consolidated EBITDA drag disappears. #A

Bear: European automotive continues to contract (EV transition disruption, geopolitical tariff impacts). IXS remains loss-making in FY2026 requiring further restructuring or write-downs. IXS becomes a disposal candidate — which would crystallise a loss but remove the drag. #A

Judgement: Assessment: Yellow — conditional on end-market. The probability of IXS reaching breakeven in FY2026 is approximately 50-60%. This is a free option if it materialises; the cost of it not materialising is continued 200-250bps EBITDA headwind, which the market already knows about. #J

Driver 6: Penang Manufacturing — Medium-Term Optionality

Penang is strategically sound but a FY2027+ story. Near-term effect is capex intensity (net negative). From FY2027, adds ~250-370bps margin and CHF 20-25m additional FCF.

The Strategic Rationale

COMET is building a new manufacturing facility in Penang, Malaysia. The strategic logic is threefold:

  1. Cost reduction: CHF-denominated manufacturing in Switzerland is expensive given CHF appreciation. Shifting production to Malaysia (USD-denominated costs) reduces the structural FX headwind. #A

  2. Asian customer proximity: WFE manufacturing is increasingly concentrated in Asia (TSMC in Taiwan, Samsung in Korea, SMIC in China). Penang reduces lead times and serves the supply chain localisation preference of Asian OEMs. #A

  3. Capacity for cycle upswing: The Penang facility adds production capacity beyond current Swiss capacity — enabling COMET to capture volume in a strong WFE cycle without capacity constraint. #A

Timeline and Costs

Capex: CHF 52m in FY2025 (includes Penang). Peak Penang capex is FY2025-FY2026. Annual maintenance capex post-Penang buildout: CHF 25-30m (vs CHF 45-55m during construction phase). The capex normalisation alone will contribute approximately CHF 20-25m to annual FCF from FY2027. #I

Operational benefits: CHF 10-15m annual cost savings from Penang manufacturing vs Swiss equivalent (management guidance). Phased realisation: approximately 25% in FY2026, 75% by FY2027. #I

Judgement: Assessment: Yellow. Penang is a strategically sound investment but a mid-term, not near-term, driver. The near-term effect is capex intensity and startup costs (net negative in FY2025-FY2026). From FY2027, Penang adds approximately 250-370bps of EBITDA margin and CHF 20-25m of additional FCF. #J

Driver 7: CHF/FX — The Structural Headwind

This is not a temporary risk. It is a permanent, unhedgeable drag on reported results and a recurring source of guidance miss risk. Any entry must be stress-tested at CHF 0.95-1.00 vs USD.

The Problem

COMET reports in CHF. Approximately 60-65% of PCT revenue is USD-denominated (US WFE OEM customers; Asian customers also billed in USD). CHF has strengthened materially since 2021:
- CHF/USD in 2021: approximately 0.91
- CHF/USD in 2025: approximately 0.93-0.94
- CHF/USD trajectory: historically strong CHF bias vs USD

The translation headwind on USD revenue to CHF reporting: every 1% CHF appreciation vs USD is approximately CHF 2.5-3.0m annual revenue headwind and approximately CHF 1.0-1.2m EBITDA headwind at current margins. #I

FY2025 impact: approximately CHF 15-18m revenue headwind and CHF 5-7m EBITDA headwind from FX translation vs a flat-rate assumption. #I

Structural Nature

The CHF/FX headwind is a permanent feature of owning COMET. SNB (Swiss National Bank) policy historically maintains a strong CHF as anti-inflation insurance. There is no mean reversion thesis for CHF weakness available to a long-only investor. #A

Analysis: Penang partially mitigates this by shifting some costs to USD-denominated currencies, but the structural revenue translation headwind does not disappear. #A

Judgement: Assessment: Red. This is not a temporary risk — it is a permanent drag on reported results and a source of guidance miss risk whenever CHF appreciates unexpectedly. Any investment case must be stress-tested against CHF at 0.95-1.00 vs USD. #J
