Section summary
  1. UPM is executing a profound strategic transition to divest structurally declining graphic paper assets and scale low-cost pulp and advanced packaging platforms

UPM-Kymmene is executing a profound strategic transition to divest structurally declining graphic paper assets and scale low-cost pulp and advanced packaging platforms, shifting from a traditional graphic paper producer to a diversified high-margin material solutions provider. This executive summary synthesizes the core analytical findings across business model quality, financial profile, value chain positioning, and operational foundation to deliver a decision-ready assessment of UPM's competitive standing relative to Nordic peers including Stora Enso, SCA, Holmen, and Metsä Board. Business Model Quality and Strategic Positioning UPM operates a bifurcated pulp platform with world-class low-cost southern assets in Uruguay and higher-cost northern operations in Finland, managing total annual pulp capacity of 5.8 million tonnes split 60% in Uruguay and 40% in Finland, with northern mills processing local conifers and birch while southern operations rely on fastgrowing eucalyptus. The Paso de los Toros mill achieves highly competitive cash costs of USD 280 per delivered tonne , placing UPM in the top quartile of the global pulp cost curve. In stark contrast, Fibres North faces structurally tight wood supply and high pulpwood costs in the Finnish market, forcing temporary production

curtailments at the Pietarsaari and Kaukas pulp mills.

UPM demonstrates a highly defensive customer profile with the largest single customer representing only 3.00% of external group sales in 2025, and the ten largest customers accounting for approximately 12.00% of total sales. UPM's WISA-Birch plywood maintains a dominant position supported by high technical qualification barriers, with UPM extensively co-developing and testing its LNG spruce plywood with sector leader Gaztransport & Technigaz, enabling UPM to supply more than half of the global LNG fleet. 23

UPM is the world's third-largest market pulp supplier and largest multi-fiber producer, with the southern platform in Uruguay delivering world-leading cost competitiveness through highly productive plantations, targeting Paso de los Toros delivered cash costs of USD 280 per tonne, supported by owned Montevideo port terminals and direct rail links. UPM Energy enjoys a unique structural hedge through zero-carbon power co-ownership under the Mankala principle delivering baseload hydro and nuclear power at production cost, with highly profitable power

assets delivering a record comparable EBIT of EUR 100 million in Q1 2026.

In conclusion, UPM-Kymmene exhibits a bifurcated competitive foundation anchored by world-class Uruguayan pulp economics and unique Finnish power assets, offset by structurally challenged Nordic pulp operations and delayed biochemical ramp-ups. The strategic portfolio transformation through graphic paper de-consolidation and plywood demerger represents decisive capital discipline, though near-term returns remain pressured by commissioning-phase drag and volatile commodity pricing. UPM's transition into a renewable materials platform positions the company favorably for long-term decarbonization trends, contingent on successful Leuna execution and sustained pulp price recovery.
