§1 — Current Management Guidance

Careium's most recent formal guidance was issued alongside FY 2024 results (reported Q1 2025) and reiterated at the Q1 2025 interim. The guidance posture is characterised by deliberate vagueness on margins and more concrete signalling on revenue direction.

  • Overall verdict: Current guidance is directionally constructive but quantitatively under-specified on margin and EPS; revenue guidance is the only hard number management is willing to defend [#J]

  • FY current — revenue: Management guides for "stable-to-modest organic growth" in FY 2025, consistent with the 1-3% organic revenue growth achieved in FY 2024 (SEK ~700m revenue base); no explicit % target provided [#A]

  • FY current — EBITDA: Management targets "continued improvement" in EBITDA margin in FY 2025; no specific margin percentage is given for the current year, a deliberate hedging strategy observed since the 2022 target miss [#A]

  • FY current — EBIT: No explicit EBIT guidance issued for FY 2025; EBIT is derivable from EBITDA guidance only with D&A assumptions [#I]

  • FY current — EPS: No EPS guidance provided — management consistently avoids EPS-level guidance, citing ongoing restructuring charges and non-cash items as distorting; this prevents direct guidance-vs-delivery comparison on the metric most relevant to the investment thesis [#A]

  • FY current — FCF: No explicit FCF guidance; management qualitatively targets "positive free cash flow" in FY 2025, consistent with the FCF break-even achieved in H2 2024 [#I]

  • FY current — CapEx: CapEx guided at approximately SEK 30-40m for FY 2025, broadly flat with FY 2024 actuals of ~SEK 35m; this is one of the few hard-number commitments in the current guidance set [#A]

  • Medium-term — N2Y/N3Y: Management has referenced a medium-term EBITDA margin target of "above 10%" without specifying a year; this target has been carried forward from prior CMD communications without a hard delivery date [#A]

  • Medium-term targets: Subscriber base growth to "approximately 200,000+ subscribers" from the ~185,000 base at end-2024; no explicit CAGR stated [#I]

  • Source — earnings call: Primary guidance delivery vehicle is the quarterly earnings press release plus Swedish-language teleconference; formal investor day last held 2022 [#I]

  • Source — written: Written guidance statements in quarterly interim reports are the definitive record; qualitative language ("continued improvement") predominates over hard numbers [#I]

  • Update timing: Guidance updated at each quarterly reporting event (Q1, H1, Q3, FY); no mid-quarter trading updates issued [#I]

  • Caveat: All FY 2025 guidance issued under caveat of "excluding material M&A"; Careium's footprint is predominantly organic post the 2021 Doro combination [#I]

  • Conditional assumption: Revenue stability assumption presupposes no material churn acceleration in the Nordic public-sector customer base, which accounts for ~60% of revenue; this is an unstated but load-bearing assumption [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on current guidance is that the deliberate absence of hard margin or EPS numbers for FY 2025 is itself informative — management learned from the 2022-23 target miss cycle that specific margin guidance creates accountability it cannot currently sustain; the "continued improvement" language is precisely vague enough to claim success at any margin level above prior year [#J]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Overall verdict: Careium exhibits a split personality: a cautious sandbagger on near-term annual revenue, and a persistent over-promiser on medium-term margin and profitability; the two patterns co-exist and must be tracked separately [#J]

  • Sandbagger signal — revenue: Annual revenue guidance is consistently set at "stable" or "low single-digit growth" — a sufficiently modest bar that actual performance has met or beaten it in 3 of 4 periods; this is genuine sandbagging on the top line [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — margin: The medium-term EBITDA margin target (10%+) has been reiterated across CMD 2022, FY 2022 results, FY 2023 results, and FY 2024 results without material delivery progress; this is structural over-promising on profitability [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — integration timeline: The FY 2022 guidance that integration costs were "largely complete" proved incorrect by approximately two quarters, indicating management's tendency to front-load optimism on cost-reduction milestones [#J]

  • Mixed pattern: The pattern is not random — there is a systematic division: management is reliably conservative on revenue (because the subscription base provides visibility) and reliably optimistic on margins (because cost reduction requires execution that has lagged) [#J]

  • Beat consistency — revenue: 3/4 periods beat or met; a sandbagger profile on this metric [#A]

  • Miss consistency — margin (vs CMD target): 4/4 periods missed vs medium-term margin target; a structural over-promiser profile on this metric [#A]

  • Magnitude — beat (revenue): Beats are small (±2%); management does not appear to be deliberately setting revenue guidance at deep discounts to actual capability [#A]

  • Magnitude — miss (margin): Misses are large (300-550bps); the gap between guided profitability aspiration and delivered reality is not noise — it reflects a structural execution challenge [#A]

  • Pattern shift: No evidence of pattern shift; the bifurcation has been stable across all 4 assessed periods; there is no observable trend toward margin target credibility improving faster than the gap is closing [#J]

  • Improving accuracy: The absolute margin level is slowly improving (FY 2022 ~4.5% to FY 2024 ~7.2%), but the medium-term target has not been revised down to reflect this, maintaining the appearance of ongoing miss [#I]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on this guidance pattern is that the sandbagging on revenue is running out of cushion — subscriber growth has slowed materially in recent periods, and the subscription base that provides revenue visibility may itself be at risk of churn acceleration if public-sector contract renewals become contested; the sandbagger profile could flip to miss profile on revenue if the base begins to shrink [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Overall verdict: The medium-term guidance case rests on multiple simultaneous assumptions that are individually plausible but collectively demanding; the probability that all deliver on schedule is low given the track record [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — customer: Management assumes the subscriber base grows from ~185,000 to "approximately 200,000+" — implying ~8% cumulative growth; the actual net add trajectory in FY 2023-24 was approximately 2,000-4,000 subscribers/year, which at current pace reaches 200k in 4-7 years, not the 2-3 year medium-term implied window [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Modest ARPU stability assumed; no explicit price-increase assumption beyond inflation indexation in public-sector contracts; ARPU growth has been approximately flat in real terms over L3Y [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Underlying telecare market is a genuine structural growth market (ageing demographics, healthcare digitisation); the demand environment is not the problem — execution of subscriber capture is the variable [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: The path from ~7% to 10%+ EBITDA margin requires approximately 300bps of cost reduction as a percentage of revenue; management attributes this to platform migration to a unified technology layer (reducing per-call handling cost) and personnel productivity improvement [#A]

  • The platform migration has been in progress since 2022 and remains incomplete; each quarter of delay represents ~50-75bps of margin upside deferred [#I]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage is assumed to contribute as the subscriber base grows; but at 1-3% annual subscriber growth, the leverage is modest — volume alone cannot close the 300bps gap [#A]

  • Margin assumption — pricing power: No pricing power assumption is explicit; public-sector contracts typically have inflation-indexed adjustments but do not allow above-inflation increases; pricing is not a meaningful margin driver [#I]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Careium operates across multiple Nordic and European jurisdictions; effective tax rate has been approximately 20-25%; no explicit tax guidance, but no material change assumed [#I]

  • EPS assumption — share count: Share count has been broadly stable; no material buyback programme or dilutive issuance planned; EPS sensitivity to share count is low [#I]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt position is modest; interest cost is not a material EPS variable at current leverage [#I]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Careium reports in SEK but has material revenue exposure in EUR (Finnish and German operations) and GBP (UK operations); a 5% SEK appreciation against EUR/GBP would reduce reported revenue by approximately SEK 20-30m (approximately 3% of revenue) [#A]

  • Macro assumption — commodity/energy: Energy and facilities cost inflation impacted FY 2022-23 margins materially; management assumes normalisation in FY 2025 guidance — a reasonable but not certain assumption [#A]
