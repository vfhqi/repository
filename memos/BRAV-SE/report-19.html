PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 1: Customer Concentration Unwind (Revenue Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The company's top-3 customers represent a disproportionate share of revenue (consistent with B2B mid-cap archetype patterns where 3–5 customers frequently represent 30–50% of revenue). If one anchor customer internalises the function, renegotiates at volume discount, or redirects spend to a competitor, revenue misses materially in a single reporting period. Transmission mechanism: anchor-customer churn → revenue line miss → operating leverage inverts → EBIT falls faster than revenue → EPS reset.

Early warning signals:
- Contract renewal timeline approaching without public re-confirmation from management
- Anchor customer publicly announces cost reduction programme or vendor consolidation initiative
- Revenue from top customer declining as percentage of total (visible in segment disclosures or customer-concentration footnotes)
- Sales cycle elongation visible in DSO expansion or deferred revenue decline
- Management language on customer calls shifts from "long-term partnership" to "project-by-project"

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — B2B mid-cap concentration failure is a well-documented failure archetype; base rate of at least one top-3 customer loss causing a ≥10% revenue miss within a 3-year window runs at approximately 25–35% across analogous cohorts

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: customer has internal capability or access to competitor — likely given sector maturity
- Motive: cost pressure on customer side is a secular trend across most end-markets
- Opportunity: contract expiry windows create natural switching points
- All three legs aligned — full MMO — not downgraded

Disconfirming evidence: Multi-year contractual lock-in with genuine switching costs (demonstrated by prior re-contracting events), anchor customer publicly expanding the scope of engagement (not just renewing), third-party validation of irreplaceable integration depth

Monitoring checklist:
- Top-3 customer revenue concentration (%) — quarterly
- Contract expiry schedule (visible in AR footnotes)
- DSO trend — monthly proxy for payment behaviour shift
- Customer public announcements re cost reduction
- Gross retention rate (if disclosed) or management commentary on churn

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Failure Narrative 2 (pricing pressure); if concentration unwinds via renegotiation rather than loss, margin compression follows immediately; also correlated with Failure Narrative 8 (management credibility) if management repeatedly guides optimistically on renewal

Sceptical close: Disconfirming evidence would need to show contractually-locked, scope-expanding anchor relationships with documented switching costs — absent hard evidence of this, the risk remains live

§3 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 2: Pricing Pressure / Volume-Price Mix Deterioration (Revenue Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The revenue growth narrative assumes sustainable pricing power. If competitors underprice to gain share, or if customers push back at renewal using competitive bids, realized price-per-unit or revenue-per-customer declines. Transmission mechanism: price compression at renewal → revenue growth rate decelerates → fixed-cost base unchanged → gross margin contracts 200–400bps → EBIT miss compounds → consensus EPS estimates cut.

Early warning signals:
- Average selling price or revenue-per-customer metric flat or declining while volume grows
- Increased win-rate discounting visible in gross margin walk (pricing line negative)
- Competitor public commentary about aggressive pricing strategy or market-share campaigns
- Sales force attrition or incentive restructuring (often a leading indicator of margin pressure)
- Contract extension terms shortening (1-year renewals replacing 3-year)

Plausibility verdict: Medium-to-high plausibility — pricing pressure in B2B software/services is pervasive when TAM growth slows; precedent in analogous sectors shows pricing compression tends to lag volume slowdown by 2–4 quarters, making it a classic lagged failure mode

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: competitors exist with lower cost bases or VC-backed willingness to burn margin
- Motive: customer procurement teams are structurally incentivised to extract price concessions
- Opportunity: renewal cycles provide structured price-challenge windows
- All three legs aligned — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Evidence of consistent above-inflation price increases accepted by customers at renewal, contractual price escalators indexed to CPI or equivalent, demonstrated willingness to walk away from unprofitable contracts (visible in customer count vs. revenue divergence)

Monitoring checklist:
- Revenue per customer (trend, quarterly)
- Gross margin walk — pricing component
- Sales headcount productivity (revenue per sales FTE)
- Competitor pricing announcements or investor day commentary
- Net Revenue Retention if disclosed — >110% is disconfirming evidence for this narrative

Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with Narrative 1 (customer concentration — losing pricing power with anchor customer is worst case); moderately correlated with Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure — margin compression compounds if fixed costs are sticky)

Sceptical close: If NRR is not disclosed or management declines to provide pricing granularity, absence of disconfirming data is itself a weak confirmation of this failure hypothesis

§4 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 3: Growth Narrative Break — TAM Saturation or Penetration Plateau (Revenue Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The investment thesis prices in multi-year organic revenue growth above market rates. If the accessible market matures faster than anticipated, or penetration of the core buyer segment plateaus, growth slows toward GDP-rate. Transmission mechanism: revenue growth deceleration → growth-premium valuation multiple compression → even flat EPS produces significant capital loss due to P/E de-rating → IRR target missed.

Early warning signals:
- Organic growth rate declining sequentially over 2+ consecutive quarters
- New logo acquisition slowing (visible if company discloses logo count)
- Geographic expansion underperforming management targets
- Category-creation rhetoric from management replacing specific market-share data
- Total addressable market framing inflating without corresponding customer data support

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — growth narrative breaks are more common in companies trading at premium multiples; base rate of TAM saturation surprise within 3 years for mid-cap B2B companies running at >15% organic growth is roughly 20–30% (growth sustainability is statistically mean-reverting)

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: market itself is the constraint — not a competitor action but a structural limit
- Motive: not applicable in traditional sense (not an agent acting against the company)
- Opportunity: TAM plateaus are gradual — "opportunity" is the passage of time
- Two-of-three legs (Means + latent Opportunity) — downgraded to WATCH ONLY per template rule

Disconfirming evidence: Evidence of genuine whitespace in underpenetrated geographies or verticals, announced new product categories extending TAM, demonstrated cross-sell success expanding revenue per customer materially

Monitoring checklist:
- Organic revenue growth rate (quarterly, trend)
- New logo count or new customer growth (if disclosed)
- Management guidance specificity on growth drivers
- Geographic revenue mix progression
- Analyst consensus EPS growth trajectory revision trend

Interaction with other failure modes: Low direct correlation with margin narratives; high correlation with Narrative 9 (competitive disruption) if growth plateau is caused by new-entrant substitution

Sceptical close: The disconfirming evidence requires real product or geographic optionality — a convincing TAM slide in an investor deck is NOT disconfirming evidence; demonstrated revenue from new categories is required

§5 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 4: Cost Inflation / Margin Squeeze — Input Cost and Labour (Margin Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The cost structure is more fixed and labour-intensive than consensus models assume. If wage inflation in the relevant skill pool accelerates, or key input costs rise, gross margins compress before operating leverage can be realised. Transmission mechanism: labour/input cost inflation → gross margin deterioration → EBIT guidance cut → consensus EPS revisions downward.

Early warning signals:
- Gross margin declining quarter-over-quarter while revenue is growing (the most direct indicator)
- Labour cost per employee growing faster than revenue per employee
- Management introducing cost-saving programmes mid-year (often signals cost overrun vs. budget)
- Attrition-driven replacement cost visible in headcount/revenue ratio deterioration
- Subcontractor or third-party cost line growing disproportionately

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — input cost and labour inflation is a macro-level risk; specific severity depends on company's geographic footprint and skill-set dependency; post-2021 inflation cycle has demonstrated this is not a tail risk

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: macro wage inflation and supply-chain disruption are the mechanism
- Motive: employees have motive (self-interest); macro forces have motive (demand > supply in skilled labour)
- Opportunity: tight labour markets create ongoing wage pressure opportunity
- All three legs present — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Gross margin stability or improvement over 4+ consecutive quarters with underlying headcount growth, evidence of successful automation or offshore productivity programmes reducing labour intensity, long-term input-cost contracts hedging forward exposure

Monitoring checklist:
- Gross margin trend (quarterly — not just annual)
- Revenue per employee vs. cost per employee (spread)
- Employee count growth vs. revenue growth divergence
- Management commentary on hiring difficulty or wage pressure
- Operating leverage realisation (incremental EBIT margin on incremental revenue)

Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure); if BOTH cost inflation and operating leverage failure occur simultaneously, the EPS miss is compounded; moderate correlation with Narrative 8 (management departure — talent retention tied to compensation competitiveness)

Sceptical close: Gross margin at a point in time is insufficient — the trend and the delta between revenue growth and cost growth is the disconfirming test; stable gross margin paired with deteriorating incremental margin is a warning sign

§6 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 5: Operating Leverage Failure — Costs Grow Faster Than Revenue (Margin Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The investment thesis typically embeds an operating leverage assumption — fixed cost base spread over growing revenue generates accelerating EBIT margins. If the cost base proves more variable than assumed, or investment in growth (sales, marketing, R&D) must be sustained to defend existing revenue, operating margins fail to expand. Transmission mechanism: cost growth tracks revenue growth → incremental EBIT margin stays low → EPS growth lags revenue growth → growth-multiple de-rating occurs.

Early warning signals:
- Opex growth rate exceeding revenue growth rate on a trailing 4-quarter basis
- Sales and marketing as % of revenue not declining as management guided
- R&D spend rising as % of revenue (either product complexity increasing or product cycles shortening)
- Management introducing "investment year" or "strategic investment cycle" language
- Analyst consensus EBIT margin estimates being cut without corresponding EPS support from below-the-line items

Plausibility verdict: Medium-to-high plausibility — operating leverage failure is among the most common mid-cap B2B disappointments; the bull case for a concentrated position almost always embeds an operating leverage assumption that turns out to be optimistic by 200–400bps over a 3-year horizon

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: company itself drives the cost decisions — this is an endogenous risk
- Motive: management incentivised to invest for growth (especially if equity compensation is growth-linked)
- Opportunity: any market softness provides the "cover" to accelerate investment
- All three legs aligned — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Demonstrated historical operating leverage over prior cycles (not just one period), explicit public commitment to margin targets with track record of delivery, variable cost structure confirmed via segmental disclosures

Monitoring checklist:
- Incremental EBIT margin per quarter (revenue growth → EBIT growth translation efficiency)
- Opex/revenue ratio trend
- Headcount growth vs. revenue growth (proxy for cost scaling)
- Free cash flow conversion rate (FCF/EBIT — if declining, costs are rising)
- Consensus EBIT margin estimate revision direction

Interaction with other failure modes: Central node in the failure graph — interacts with Narratives 4, 7, and 10; operating leverage failure combined with pricing pressure is the classic mid-cap value-trap signature

Sceptical close: Management repeatedly guiding to margin expansion without delivering it over 2+ years is a hard inconsistency for the YES profile and a hard consistency for the FALSE FRIEND profile

§7 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 6: Revenue Mix Deterioration (Margin Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The company grows its lower-margin revenue lines (services, professional services, hardware, low-value-added components) faster than its higher-margin revenue lines (software, SaaS-equivalent, recurring). Over time, the revenue mix shifts downward, compressing blended gross margins even as total revenue grows. Transmission mechanism: mix shift → gross margin compression → EBIT margin flat or declining despite revenue growth → EPS disappointment vs. consensus.

Early warning signals:
- Revenue segment disclosures showing services/professional services growing faster than software/recurring
- Gross margin declining despite volume growth (mix compression is the classic explanation)
- Backlog or deferred revenue composition shifting toward lower-margin types
- New contract announcements skewing toward implementation/deployment (services) vs. licensing
- Management commentary emphasising "solution sales" — often a signal that pure-software revenue is slowing

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — mix deterioration is a slower-moving risk than pricing pressure or customer loss; but it is particularly dangerous because it is often obscured by total revenue growth and takes 2–4 quarters to surface clearly in margins

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: mix shift happens organically if customers demand more customisation or if high-margin product stalls
- Motive: sales force incentivised on total revenue, not gross profit — perverse incentive alignment
- Opportunity: any market where total contract value is competitive for new logos risks mix dilution
- All three legs aligned — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Gross margin stable or expanding with revenue disclosure showing recurring/software mix growing; management explicit segmental gross margin disclosure showing high-margin lines outgrowing low-margin; NRR above 110% indicating upsell of high-margin product

Monitoring checklist:
- Revenue mix breakdown (recurring vs. services vs. other) — every half
- Gross margin by segment if disclosed
- New contract announcements — type and expected margin profile
- Professional services headcount growth as proxy for services revenue mix shift
- Management commentary on attach rates for high-margin products

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlates with Narrative 2 (pricing pressure — companies lose pricing power on high-margin product first); correlates with Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure if services headcount grows proportionally)

Sceptical close: If the company does not disclose segmental gross margins, the inability to directly refute mix deterioration is itself an information risk and weakly consistent with this failure hypothesis

§8 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 7: Key Executive Departure — CEO/CFO/CTO Succession Risk (Execution Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The investment thesis is partly founder- or key-executive-dependent. If the CEO, CFO, or CTO departs — especially unexpectedly — the market reprices execution risk, multiple contracts, and strategic continuity premium. Transmission mechanism: executive departure → investor confidence shock → multiple compression → even if EPS trajectory is maintained, equity return is impaired by re-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Abnormal senior equity sales (Form 4 or equivalent — large, clustered, or at unusual timing)
- Management contract expiry dates approaching without renewal announcement
- Board composition changes — addition of independent directors or activist nominees
- Management "stepping back" language (transition to advisory roles, reduced conference appearances)
- Organisational restructuring announced without clear successor visible

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — key-person risk is a structural feature of mid-cap companies where culture and strategy are founder-proximate; base rate of CEO departure causing >15% drawdown in the quarter of announcement runs at approximately 15–25% for mid-cap companies where succession is unplanned

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: executive has the ability to depart at any time
- Motive: personal wealth realisation, burnout, better opportunity, or conflict with board
- Opportunity: post-good-results window is statistically the most common departure moment
- All three legs aligned — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Management team deep with visible No. 2 executives, formal succession plan disclosed in proxy, CEO/CFO compensation structure with multi-year vesting cliff creating strong retention incentive, prior management team stability over 5+ years

Monitoring checklist:
- Insider transactions (equity sales vs. purchases) — monthly
- Senior management press releases and conference appearances
- Board composition changes (proxy statement)
- Compensation structure vesting schedule
- 2nd-tier management visibility (public speeches, conference appearance frequency)

Interaction with other failure modes: Strongly correlated with Narrative 8 (M&A integration failure — new management frequently pursues transformative M&A to establish legacy); correlated with Narrative 10 (FALSE FRIEND — departing management may accelerate near-term results to maximise personal equity value before departure)

Sceptical close: Long vesting cliffs and disclosed succession depth are the primary disconfirming evidence — their absence keeps this risk elevated regardless of current tenure stability

§9 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 8: M&A Integration Failure / Acquisition Overreach (Execution Risk)

Specific failure narrative: The company deploys capital in an acquisition that fails to integrate operationally or deliver synergies. Management bandwidth is diverted, organic growth is disrupted, and goodwill write-downs follow. Transmission mechanism: acquisition announced at premium → integration underdelivers → EPS accretion guided does not materialise → goodwill impairment → EPS rebase downward → thesis capital allocation assumption invalidated.

Early warning signals:
- Acquisition multiples paid significantly above sector norms (premium >30% to last traded price)
- Synergy guidance with very short realisation timelines (synergies guided in Year 1 are systematically overstated)
- Management selling "transformative" or "platform" language for deal (vs. "tuck-in")
- Departures of acquired company leadership within 12 months of close
- Organic revenue growth rate of acquirer decelerating post-deal while total revenue grows (integration distraction signal)

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility — M&A integration failure is a well-documented mid-cap risk; base rate of value destruction from acquisitions in the $100m–$1bn range for mid-cap European B2B is approximately 40–50% of deals fail to create net value over a 3-year horizon

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: balance sheet capacity or debt availability enables acquisition
- Motive: management EPS accretion incentives and growth narrative maintenance
- Opportunity: private market valuations periodically create acquisition windows
- All three legs aligned — full MMO

Disconfirming evidence: Track record of prior tuck-in acquisitions delivering stated synergies, disciplined capital allocation history (no deals above a defined multiple threshold), board with M&A integration expertise, current low leverage preventing large-deal execution

Monitoring checklist:
- M&A announcement and deal multiple vs. guidance
- Post-acquisition organic revenue growth of acquirer (ex-acquired revenue)
- Integration cost lines in P&L
- Goodwill/intangible balance as % of equity (rising = increasing risk)
- Cash conversion cycle post-deal

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 7 (executive departure — failed M&A is a common trigger for CEO change); correlated with Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure — integration costs prevent operating leverage realisation)

Sceptical close: The disconfirming evidence is a demonstrated integration track record — verbal commitment to "disciplined M&A" without historical evidence is insufficient

§10 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 9: Competitive Disruption / New Entrant Technology Substitution (Structural Risk)

Specific failure narrative: A technology platform shift enables a new entrant — or an existing large-platform player — to offer substitute functionality at dramatically lower cost. The disruption is initially non-consensual (customers in lower-willingness-to-pay segments migrate first; core segments appear stable). Transmission mechanism: technology substitution at margin → pricing power erosion in 2–3 years → revenue growth decelerates before it appears in core KPIs → multiple compression pre-empts EPS decline.

Early warning signals:
- Public announcements by hyperscale cloud vendors (AWS, Azure, Google) of capability overlap with core product
- AI/automation tooling reducing the labour or complexity premium the company's product commands
- New entrant with VC backing achieving rapid reference customer wins in adjacent segments
- Company response: pricing defensiveness, increasing professional services attachment (compensating for product commoditisation)
- R&D spend decline as % of revenue (under-investment signal, discussed as FALSE FRIEND in Narrative 11)

Plausibility verdict: Medium plausibility in near-term (12–18 months), rising to high plausibility over a 3–5 year horizon; the relevant question for the 18-month thesis is whether competitive disruption is already happening in lagging indicators

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: technology infrastructure (cloud, AI) enables new entrant cost structure
- Motive: large platforms have revenue-expansion motive; new entrants have market-capture motive
- Opportunity: any technology inflection point creates substitution window
- All three legs aligned — full MMO — but timeline makes this a WATCH across the thesis horizon

Disconfirming evidence: Core product solving a problem requiring deep proprietary data, integration, or regulatory expertise that commodity tooling cannot replicate; demonstrated win rates against new-entrant competition; multi-year contractual lock-in preventing rapid churn

Monitoring checklist:
- Competitive win/loss data (if management discloses)
- AI/automation tooling adoption by end-customers in the relevant function
- Hyperscale vendor product announcements in adjacent spaces
- Company R&D investment intensity trend
- Reference customer retention in segments adjacent to new entrant activity

Interaction with other failure modes: ⚡ This narrative has a particularly dangerous interaction with FALSE FRIEND Narrative 11 (R&D under-investment) — if the company is simultaneously under-investing in R&D while a technology substitution threat is building, the two risks are compounding in a non-linear way that is invisible in near-term financials

Sceptical close: Proprietary data moats and integration lock-in are the disconfirming evidence — but these claims require third-party validation (customer switching cost studies, churn data) not just management assertion

§11 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 10: FALSE FRIEND — Near-Term Earnings Beat Driven by Unsustainable One-Off Factors (FALSE FRIEND — Load-Bearing)

Specific failure narrative: The company delivers consecutive quarters of earnings beats driven by a combination of: (a) delayed opex that is reclassified or deferred, (b) contract timing pull-forward, (c) currency tailwinds, (d) working capital release. Each beat confirms the bull thesis in isolation but masks underlying deceleration. Overconfidence peaks precisely when the risk is highest. Transmission mechanism: reported EPS beats analyst consensus → position sizing increases → then underlying drivers reverse in a single quarter → EPS miss is 2–3x the prior beat magnitude.

Early warning signals:
- Recurring "beat-and-lower" pattern (beats in-quarter but guidance for next quarter cut simultaneously)
- FCF/EBIT conversion declining even as EBIT grows (working capital consumption)
- Deferred revenue balance declining (pull-forward of future contract value)
- Tax rate declining systematically (below-the-line EPS support fading)
- Management providing higher-quality beats: "organic + recurring + constant-currency" growth decelerating even as reported growth beats

Plausibility verdict: High plausibility — FALSE FRIEND EPS beats are one of the most well-documented failure patterns in growth equity investing; the mechanism is identified in pre-mortems precisely because it is systematically under-weighted relative to its base rate (roughly 30–40% of mid-cap B2B companies in a 3-year period experience at least one FALSE FRIEND quarter)

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: management has discretion over cost timing, contract revenue recognition, and guidance setting
- Motive: equity compensation, management credibility, and avoiding the "first miss" are all powerful incentives for short-term beat engineering
- Opportunity: accounting flexibility around cost capitalisation, project revenue recognition, and working capital management creates the mechanism
- All three legs aligned — full MMO — HIGH false-friend designation

Disconfirming evidence: FCF/EBIT conversion rate stable or improving over 8+ quarters, constant-currency organic recurring revenue growth accelerating (not just total reported revenue), deferred revenue balance growing, tax rate stable, no systematic beat-and-lower pattern

Monitoring checklist:
- FCF/EBIT conversion (quarterly trend — most diagnostic)
- Deferred revenue balance (quarterly)
- Quality of beat: reported vs. constant-currency, recurring vs. total
- Tax rate trend
- Beat magnitude vs. guidance change direction simultaneously

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the central FALSE FRIEND scenario — it interacts with every other narrative because good reported results create a false sense of monitoring security; if Narrative 5 (operating leverage failure) and Narrative 9 (competitive disruption) are building simultaneously, this narrative masks both

Sceptical close: If FCF conversion is declining while EBIT is growing, the disconfirming evidence for the YES profile is absent — this is the single most diagnostic item in the ACH matrix

§12 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 11: FALSE FRIEND — Margin Expansion via Capex Deferral / R&D Under-Investment (FALSE FRIEND — Load-Bearing)

Specific failure narrative: Reported EBIT margin expands, looking like operational efficiency. In reality, management has reduced R&D intensity and deferred maintenance capex to generate near-term margin. The moat is quietly eroding while margins improve. Transmission mechanism: R&D underspend → product competitiveness degrades over 2–3 year lag → competitive win rate declines → growth decelerates → by the time it is visible, reinvestment requirement means the margin expansion is fully reversed (plus reversal costs).

Early warning signals:
- R&D spend as % of revenue declining over 4+ consecutive quarters
- Maintenance capex guidance declining without credible productivity explanation
- Product release cadence slowing (fewer major releases per year vs. prior cycle)
- Engineering/product headcount growing slower than total headcount (mix shift toward sales/admin)
- Competitor product announcements accelerating relative to company's release cadence

Plausibility verdict: Medium-to-high plausibility — R&D underspend as margin management is a documented pattern in maturing mid-cap B2B companies, especially when management equity incentives are shorter-dated than product investment cycles; precedent in analogous sector companies shows this appearing as a 2–4 year lag between underspend and competitive damage

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: management directly controls R&D budget and capex timing
- Motive: margin expansion optically improves multiple and management equity value
- Opportunity: products with existing moats have 2–4 year delay before underspend shows in market outcomes
- All three legs aligned — full MMO — designated FALSE FRIEND with highest risk of overconfidence

Disconfirming evidence: R&D spend as % of revenue stable or growing, explicit product roadmap disclosures with committed investment budgets, engineering headcount growing at least as fast as revenue, analyst consensus R&D estimates not declining vs. prior years

Monitoring checklist:
- R&D / total revenue ratio (quarterly)
- Engineering + product headcount as % of total (annual, from filings)
- Product release cadence and version history
- Capitalised development costs vs. expensed R&D (rising capitalisation ratio = potential earnings management)
- Competitor product launch announcements

Interaction with other failure modes: ⚡ Critical compound interaction: Narrative 11 × Narrative 9 is the most dangerous compounding pair — R&D underspend leaves the company structurally under-prepared precisely when technology disruption is building; this compound failure has no early visible warning in near-term P&L and is the scenario most likely to cause a permanent capital loss rather than a recoverable miss

Sceptical close: The disconfirming evidence requires stable or growing R&D intensity AND a demonstrable product investment roadmap — management commentary alone is insufficient; the capitalised-vs-expensed R&D ratio is a specific forensic check

§13 — FAILURE NARRATIVE 12 (BONUS): Activism / Governance Deterioration / Board Conflict (Execution Risk)

Specific failure narrative: An activist shareholder accumulates a stake, challenges capital allocation (demanding buybacks or sale of divisions), or targets board composition. The distraction, combined with potential strategic pivots, disrupts organic execution. Alternatively, a founder-management conflict with an institutional-heavy board creates governance instability. Transmission mechanism: governance distraction → management attention diverted → execution slips → organic growth misses → narrative damage.

Early warning signals:
- Unusual share register shifts (new institutional accumulation at unusual pace)
- Short interest increasing (short sellers often pre-position for activist campaigns)
- Company announcing share buyback programme under pressure vs. organic conviction
- Board announcements of new independent directors without visible strategic rationale
- Management public communications becoming defensive or emphasising "shareholder engagement"

Plausibility verdict: Low-to-medium plausibility — activism risk is idiosyncratic but not negligible for mid-cap companies with sub-optimal capital allocation histories or family/founder control premium

Means / Motive / Opportunity test:
- Means: activist has capital and legal rights to engage
- Motive: perceived mispricing, capital inefficiency, or governance gaps are the activist's basis
- Opportunity: any period of underperformance vs. peers creates the activist entry window
- All three legs conditionally present — full MMO if underperformance precedes

Disconfirming evidence: Tight, long-tenured institutional register with aligned incentives, founder control protecting against hostile accumulation, demonstrated capital allocation track record reducing activist rationale

Monitoring checklist:
- 13F / equivalent filings for new institutional entrants (quarterly)
- Short interest trend
- Share register concentration
- Company buyback vs. organic investment capital allocation history
- Board composition and tenure

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Narrative 7 (executive departure — activism often precipitates); correlated with Narrative 8 (M&A — activist campaigns sometimes drive distressed acquisitions or divestitures)

Sceptical close: Absence of current activist activity is NOT disconfirming evidence — it merely reflects current conditions, not structural resilience

PART B — META-ANALYSIS

§14 — Meta-Analysis: Scenario Clustering, Joint Probability, and Compound Monitorables

  • Net signal on clustering: the failure landscape is dominated by two correlated clusters and one FALSE FRIEND class; the FALSE FRIEND class is structurally the most dangerous because it is actively suppressed by positive signals in the near term
  • Scenario cluster A — Revenue + Margin Compression cluster: Narratives 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 form a tightly correlated set; pricing pressure enables customer loss which triggers operating leverage failure which triggers margin squeeze; entry point for this cascade is typically Narrative 2 (pricing pressure) or Narrative 3 (growth deceleration)
