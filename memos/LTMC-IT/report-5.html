§2 Segment Performance

Net signal: Online clearly outperformed; Gaming Franchise was stable; Sports Franchise underperformed on a reported basis. The channel mix shift story continued as expected. [#J]

  • Online segment: Strong double-digit growth. iGaming market share 32.2% (+1.9pp YoY); iSports share 32.5% (+0.7pp YoY). Total online bets +15% YoY to €8.49bn. [Deutsche Bank Research • 6 May]
  • Sports Franchise: Revenue -5% reported (+11% normalised). Sporting event calendar headwind in Q1 2026 vs Q1 2025. Sports Betting Protocol 5.0 migration commenced March 2026. [#A]
  • Gaming Franchise (AWP/VLT): Broadly stable — bets €2.74bn, -1% YoY. Consistent with secular managed-decline thesis. Concession structure provides floor. [#I]
  • Market context: Total Italian GGR grew only ~2% YoY in Q1 2026 — well below the full-year 2025 run-rate of +8%. LTM's normalised +22% EBITDA growth in a +2% GGR market is remarkable, but the market deceleration is a watch point. [#A]
  • Tail operator attrition: Small operators saw collective iGaming market share drop from 15.5% to 13% in Q1 2026. LTM captured >50% of share lost by tail operators. [Deutsche Bank Research • 6 May]

Sceptical bullet: The +2% Italian GGR market growth in Q1 2026 is below the run-rate needed to support LTM's revenue guidance. If this reflects structural deceleration rather than sports calendar seasonality, FY2026 revenue guidance (+6-9%) is at risk. [#A]

§5 Beat Quality + Sustainability

Sceptical bullet: Four consecutive guidance beats since IPO creates consensus expectation of a fifth.
