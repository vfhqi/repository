§2 — Quality of Earnings

Quality verdict: Cash conversion is genuinely strong and NWC is well-managed; however, the non-GAAP adjustment stack is large and growing, and recurring M&A costs dressed as one-offs are a persistent concern — net quality signal is mixed [#J]

One-off impact: M&A/integration costs have run at NOK 35-120m per period across L3Y, totalling approximately NOK 350-400m over FY2022-FY2024 — these are excluded from adjusted EBITDA in every period [#A]
- Sub: A company making 2-4 acquisitions per year will structurally generate M&A costs every year; calling these one-off is aggressive by any standard

Adjustments trend: The PPA amortisation add-back has increased from NOK 280m (FY2022) to NOK 345m (FY2024), a +23% rise over two years, while the M&A cost add-back has remained in the NOK 70-120m range — the total non-GAAP gap is widening, not stabilising [#A]

Recurring vs one-off: ❌ Management classifies M&A transaction and integration costs as non-recurring in every single reporting period L3Y; this is a structurally recurring item labelled as non-recurring — peer European distribution compounders (Bunzl, Diploma) make a similar move but typically disclose cumulative non-recurring costs as a % of revenue (Bunzl: ~0.3% annually over 5Y); Asker's ratio is ~0.5-0.8% of revenue [#A]

Non-GAAP gap: The gap between reported EBIT (~NOK 870-980m FY2023-FY2024) and EBITDA adjusted (~NOK 1,810-2,010m) is approximately NOK 940-1,030m, representing 7-8% of revenue — a substantial wedge driven primarily by PPA amortisation and D&A on acquired assets [#A]

NWC tailwind: Working capital appears broadly neutral to slight tailwind across L3Y — Days Sales Outstanding estimated at 35-40 days, Days Payable Outstanding at 45-55 days for a healthcare distribution model; net working capital cycle is favourable versus typical distribution peers [#J]
- Sub: No evidence of receivables stuffing or aggressive inventory drawdown in available semi-annual data

Cash conversion: OCF/adjusted EBITDA is estimated at 88-95% across L3Y periods — strong for a distribution-plus-services model; base-rate for European healthcare distribution is ~80-90%; Asker is in-line to slightly above [#J]
- Sub: CapEx is relatively light (~NOK 100-150m annually, <1.2% of revenue) consistent with asset-light distribution; free cash flow conversion is therefore high

§3 — Revenue vs Profit Growth (Margin Sustainability)

Margin verdict: Margin expansion is modest and non-structural at the gross level; EBITDA margin improvement is real but small (~50bps over L3Y), driven by SG&A leverage rather than gross margin expansion — sustainability is medium-conviction [#J]

Revenue CAGR: ~11-13% total L3Y (FY2022 to FY2024); organic component is estimated 6-7%, consistent with stated 5-8% organic guidance — management is not systematically under-delivering on organic [#J]

Profit CAGR: EBITDA CAGR ~9-10%, slightly below revenue — no operating leverage at the EBITDA line over L3Y; profit growth is volume-driven not margin-driven [#J]

Operating leverage: Revenue grew ~NOK 1,900m from FY2022 to FY2024; EBITDA grew ~NOK 330m — incremental EBITDA margin on that revenue increment is approximately 17%, below the current EBITDA margin of ~14.7%, suggesting slight negative mix from lower-margin acquired businesses [#A]

Gross margin: Gross margin has been essentially flat at ~20.2-20.3% across all L3Y periods — no expansion despite scale; this is consistent with a distribution model where supplier pricing resets annually and mix shifts toward pharma offset specialist margin improvement [#J]
- Sub: European distribution peer base-rate gross margins range 18-25%; Asker at ~20% is in the middle, appropriate for its mix

EBITDA margin: FY2024 EBITDA margin of 14.7% is the L3Y high; improvement from 14.2% (FY2022) is real but modest at ~50bps over two years — pace of margin expansion is not accelerating [#J]

Cost cut driver: No evidence of significant restructuring-driven margin expansion; SG&A leverage (fixed cost spread over larger revenue base) is the primary driver — this is a real but slow-moving mechanism [#A]

Mix shift: Asker's strategic emphasis on higher-value segments (homecare, rehabilitation, wound care) vs pure pharma distribution should theoretically expand gross margin over time; this mix thesis is not yet visible in the L3Y gross margin data [#A]

Scale driver: Central procurement/logistics leverage is the management-articulated margin expansion driver; evidence of delivery is modest so far — CMD 2024 target of 15-16% EBITDA margin by 2026-2027 requires ~100-150bps further expansion from FY2024 [#J]

Sustainability assessment: Margin expansion to 15-16% is plausible but not assured; it requires mix improvement (more specialist, less distribution) and SG&A discipline without acquisitive dilution; the L3Y record provides weak-positive support [#A]

Bearish margin bullet: What's the bearish read? Gross margin has not expanded despite 2+ years of scale gains; if specialist/homecare mix shift is the thesis driver and gross margin remains flat, the path to 15-16% EBITDA margin requires SG&A cuts that may erode service quality; any acquisition of lower-margin distribution assets would further dilute the margin trajectory [#A]
