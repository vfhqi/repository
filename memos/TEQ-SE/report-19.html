3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES (≥10 SCENARIOS)

§2 — Scenario 1: COP/USD Depreciation Spike — Input Cost Collapse of Stated Margins

Narrative: TEQ sources a significant proportion of active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), packaging inputs, and chemical intermediates in USD or EUR while reporting in COP. A renewed depreciation cycle (COP/USD toward 4,500+) widens input costs faster than local price adjustments are permitted or commercially viable, compressing gross margin by 400–700 bps within two reporting quarters. Transmission mechanism: USD-denominated COGS → COP-reported gross profit → operating leverage amplifies at EBITDA line → EPS miss of 15–30%.

Early warning signals:
- COP/USD spot rate breaching 4,200 for more than 45 consecutive days
- Monthly import price index for pharmaceutical inputs (DANE Colombia) trending +8% YoY or higher
- TEQ management commentary shifting to "price adjustment lag" language on earnings calls
- Raw material as % of revenue inflecting upward for two consecutive quarters in segment disclosures
- Peer Colombian consumer-goods companies (Grupo Nutresa, Colombina) flagging input cost headwinds in same quarter

Plausibility: High plausibility. COP has depreciated >40% against USD since 2019; TEQ has experienced this mechanism at least twice in publicly disclosed financials (2020, 2022); the base rate for EM currency-driven margin compression in Colombia-domiciled consumer-pharma companies is structurally elevated.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: existing USD input exposure confirmed in filings ✓
- Motive: macroeconomic — not a management decision, hence unstoppable ✓
- Opportunity: global USD strength cycles and Colombian fiscal-monetary dynamics recur regularly ✓
- All three legs aligned — full activation risk

Disconfirming evidence: Sustained COP appreciation toward 3,600–3,800; confirmed multi-year USD hedging programme disclosed in annual report; demonstrated price-increase pass-through within one quarter with no volume loss — none of these are currently confirmed in public disclosures.

Monitoring checklist:
- COP/USD weekly close vs 52-week moving average
- TEQ gross margin QoQ delta vs COP/USD QoQ delta (correlation tracker)
- DANE pharmaceutical import price sub-index monthly
- TEQ disclosed hedge position (annual report, Note to Financial Statements)
- Segment-level COGS decomposition (materials vs labour vs overhead)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Scenario 4 (working capital deterioration) and Scenario 7 (pricing power erosion); a COP shock is the most likely trigger for a compound failure cascade across these three.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence would require confirmed systematic FX hedging with multi-year tenor — not disclosed to date; the absence of a disclosed hedge programme is itself a hard inconsistency against the YES profile.

§3 — Scenario 2: Customer/Channel Concentration — Key Retailer Delisting or Share Loss

Narrative: TEQ's Consumer Health and OTC segments derive disproportionate revenue from a limited set of modern-trade retailers (Éxito/Grupo Casino successor, Jumbo/Cencosud, D1/Koba) and pharmacy chains (Cruz Verde, Droguerías Colsubsidio). Loss of preferred-shelf or category-captain status at a single key account removes 8–15% of segment revenue within 12 months. Transmission mechanism: de-listing or reduced planogram space → volume decline → fixed-cost deleverage → EBIT compression → EPS miss of 10–20%.

Early warning signals:
- Nielsen/Kantar Colombia shelf-presence data showing SKU count reduction in top-3 retail accounts
- New private-label product launches by Éxito or Jumbo in TEQ's core OTC categories
- TEQ trade-receivables days increasing >10 days YoY (sign of negotiating-power shift)
- Gross-to-net deductions as % revenue widening in quarterly disclosures
- Loss of TEQ SKUs from pharmacy chain promotional catalogues

Plausibility: Medium plausibility. TEQ has long-established retail relationships and brand equity in Colombia, but private-label penetration in Colombian pharmacy/modern-trade has accelerated post-2022; peer base rate for similar concentration losses in LatAm consumer-pharma (Genomma Lab, Siegfried Colombia) is 1-in-5 over a 5-year window.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: retailer has shelf re-allocation authority ✓
- Motive: cost pressure on retailers incentivises private-label substitution ✓
- Opportunity: private-label development cycle 12–18 months; already underway in Colombia ✓
- All three legs aligned — watch active

Disconfirming evidence: Multi-year exclusive-category agreements with major retail chains; demonstrated SKU count growth YoY; absence of private-label competitors in TEQ's core branded categories — none confirmed in recent filings.

Monitoring checklist:
- OTC shelf-space share (Nielsen Colombia tracking)
- DSO trend QoQ
- Gross-to-net deduction ratio
- New private-label product announcements by major retailers
- TEQ brand investment as % revenue (A&P spend)

Interaction with other failure modes: Moderate correlation with Scenario 3 (pricing pressure) and Scenario 5 (growth narrative break); a major retailer demanding price concessions or threatening delisting would trigger pricing pressure simultaneously.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence would require disclosed long-term retailer agreements; absence of such disclosure is structurally ambiguous — not a hard inconsistency against YES, but not confirmatory either.

§4 — Scenario 3: Pricing Pressure — Generics / Price Control Erosion

Narrative: Colombian INVIMA and the Ministry of Health periodically invoke Decree 4474 price-control mechanisms for pharmaceutical products designated as "essential medicines." If key TEQ OTC products migrate into the controlled-price registry, maximum resale prices and manufacturer transfer prices are administratively compressed. Transmission mechanism: mandated price reduction on 20–35% of revenue → revenue per unit decline → gross margin deterioration → EPS miss of 15–25%.

Early warning signals:
- INVIMA circular classifying new OTC categories as "essential medicine" subject to circular pricing
- Ministry of Health budget discussion documents referencing pharmaceutical price reduction targets
- Generic entrants from India/China filing dossiers for TEQ's top-selling OTC SKUs
- TEQ average-selling-price per unit declining in Consumer Health segment disclosures
- Peer companies (Procaps, Merck Colombia subsidiary) flagging price-control headwinds

Plausibility: Medium plausibility. Colombian price-control activity has been episodic but intensifying under recent government policy direction (2022–2025 Petro administration health reform agenda); base rate for OTC price-control expansion events in Colombia is approximately one per three years.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: regulatory mechanism exists (Decree 4474, INVIMA authority) ✓
- Motive: Petro government health reform platform explicitly targets pharmaceutical costs ✓
- Opportunity: electoral cycle and health-reform legislative window open through 2026 ✓
- All three legs aligned — high activation risk given current political context

Disconfirming evidence: Current TEQ product portfolio confirmed to be predominantly non-essential-medicine classified; no legislative or regulatory pipeline items targeting TEQ-relevant categories; management confirmation of price-control monitoring with no near-term risk flagged.

Monitoring checklist:
- INVIMA essential medicine register updates (monthly)
- Ministry of Health legislative calendar (health reform bill progress)
- TEQ average unit price per segment QoQ
- Generic dossier filings for TEQ's top-10 SKUs
- ANDI (Colombian industry association) lobbying activity on pricing

Interaction with other failure modes: Strong correlated amplifier to Scenario 1 — a price-control event concurrent with COP depreciation eliminates the management option of passing FX costs to consumers.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence requires regulatory ring-fence confirmation from INVIMA; political-economy risk in Colombia is systematically under-priced in equity consensus, making this the "least-monitored" regulatory scenario.

§5 — Scenario 4: Working Capital Deterioration — Free Cash Flow Destruction

Narrative: TEQ's business model in institutional and pharmacy channels involves extended payment terms to distributors and pharmacies. Under economic stress or credit tightening in Colombia's healthcare distribution network, receivables extend, inventory builds (retailers reduce orders but do not cancel), and payables are paid on normal terms — the classic working-capital squeeze. FCF collapses even as reported EBITDA holds, creating a gap between earnings quality and actual cash generation. Transmission: receivable extension → cash conversion cycle deterioration → FCF collapse → net-debt increase → valuation multiple compression → EPS trajectory undermined.

Early warning signals:
- DSO extending >15 days from baseline over two consecutive quarters
- Days inventory outstanding increasing as sell-through slows
- Net working capital as % revenue inflecting upward in annual report
- Cash-conversion-cycle reported in MD&A widening materially
- Short-term credit facility drawdowns increasing

Plausibility: Medium plausibility. FCF/EBITDA conversion ratios in Colombian consumer-pharma distribution businesses structurally trail EBITDA growth in downturn years; TEQ's 2020 reported cash flows showed this exact pattern.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: structural — channel financing dynamics are embedded ✓
- Motive: distributors/pharmacies face their own liquidity pressure under macro stress ✓
- Opportunity: triggered by Scenario 1 or Scenario 3 (price controls) or standalone macro ✓
- All three legs aligned — conditional on macro trigger

Disconfirming evidence: Confirmed factoring/receivables-financing programme eliminating DSO risk; structural shift to shorter payment terms across distribution network; clean and stable CCC trend in last 4 quarters of filings.

Monitoring checklist:
- DSO QoQ (from balance sheet / revenue)
- DIO QoQ
- Operating cash flow vs EBITDA gap quarterly
- Short-term bank facility utilisation rate
- TEQ net debt / EBITDA trend

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly triggered by Scenario 1 and Scenario 3; cascades into Scenario 6 (leverage / refinancing) under sustained pressure.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence requires consistent FCF conversion ≥70% of EBITDA over the trailing eight quarters — the analyst should verify this explicitly in filings before clearing this scenario.

§8 — Scenario 7 (FALSE FRIEND #1): Margin Expansion from Promotional Spend Deferral — Illusory Quality

Narrative: Reported gross margin and EBITDA margin improve in recent reporting periods, and the bull case interprets this as structural efficiency gain or pricing power demonstration. However, the expansion is driven by deferred promotional spend (A&P), suspended trade promotions to retailers, and delayed marketing investment — a classic consumer-goods earnings management lever. When competitive response forces promotional reinvestment (or when new entrants gain share during the hiatus), margins reverse sharply. The FALSE FRIEND element: reported margin improvement perfectly confirms the thesis thesis narrative while concealing the structural erosion beneath. Transmission: deferred A&P → EPS beat → consensus upgrades → investor accumulation at higher price → A&P reinvestment triggers miss → price collapse from elevated base.

Early warning signals:
- A&P as % revenue declining for two or more consecutive years while revenue growth holds (classic false friend pattern)
- Competitor brand-spend data (Kantar Media Colombia) showing escalating competitive investment in TEQ's categories
- Nielsen shelf-presence data showing competitor SKU share gains concurrent with TEQ margin expansion
- TEQ management language shifting from "brand investment" to "efficiency" as explanation for margin improvement
- Gross-to-net deduction reduction (lower trade promotions) flagged in segment commentary

Plausibility: High plausibility. This is among the most-documented false-friend patterns in consumer-goods equity investing globally; the base rate for Colombian consumer companies using A&P deferral as a short-cycle earnings lever is high given the thin analyst-coverage environment and limited investor scrutiny.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: A&P budget is a management-discretionary lever ✓
- Motive: management incentivised by short-term EPS/EBITDA targets ✓
- Opportunity: thin sell-side coverage reduces scrutiny of A&P line decomposition ✓
- All three legs aligned — active false-friend risk

Disconfirming evidence: A&P as % revenue held constant or increased during the margin expansion period; management explicitly articulating brand investment strategy with multi-year commitments; market share data confirming TEQ share growth concurrent with margin expansion.

Monitoring checklist:
- A&P / revenue ratio annual trend (5-year lookback)
- Competitor A&P investment trend (Kantar Colombia)
- Nielsen brand share TEQ core categories YoY
- Trade promotion accrual in balance sheet (current liabilities)
- Gross-to-net deduction ratio QoQ

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly amplifies Scenario 2 (customer concentration) when retailers respond to reduced promotional support; correlated with Scenario 5 (growth narrative break) when brand underinvestment manifests in volume deceleration.

Sceptical bullet: ⚡ The absence of A&P disclosure at the segment level in TEQ's public filings makes this one of the least-verifiable failure modes and therefore one of the most dangerous — if an investor cannot directly refute the A&P deferral hypothesis from public data alone, the FALSE FRIEND profile gains structural advantage in the ACH matrix.

§9 — Scenario 8 (FALSE FRIEND #2): Capex Deferral Masking Asset Deterioration

Narrative: TEQ operates manufacturing facilities in Cali (primary) and potentially secondary sites. Reported EBITDA and FCF look strong in part because maintenance and growth capex has been deferred — a pattern that produces excellent near-term financial metrics while the productive asset base ages. When deferred maintenance becomes unavoidable (regulatory inspection, GMP compliance requirement, equipment failure), a compressed capex cycle forces 2–3 years of above-normal capital expenditure, suppressing FCF and triggering EPS misses. The FALSE FRIEND: capex deferral makes the company look more cash-generative than it is structurally. Transmission: capex deferral → FCF inflation → multiple expansion → mandatory reinvestment cycle → FCF collapse → re-rating.

Early warning signals:
- Capex / depreciation ratio declining below 0.8x for two or more consecutive years
- Age of manufacturing assets (net PP&E / gross PP&E declining) in fixed-asset schedule
- INVIMA GMP inspection results showing warnings or observations (public regulatory database)
- Management commentary referencing "plant upgrade" or "capacity expansion" in pipeline (deferred capex acknowledgement)
- Peer Colombian manufacturers receiving GMP warning letters (sector-level signal)

Plausibility: Medium plausibility. Capex deferral is a documented pattern in EM consumer-pharma manufacturers under financial stress; TEQ's Cali facility is mature, and GMP compliance requirements under Colombian and Pan American Health Organization standards impose a non-deferrable maintenance floor.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: capex is a management-discretionary allocation ✓
- Motive: financial-metric optimisation, particularly under short-term investor pressure ✓
- Opportunity: manufacturing-asset age creates available deferral headroom ✓
- All three legs aligned — active false-friend risk

Disconfirming evidence: Capex / depreciation ratio sustained above 1.0x in recent years; GMP compliance inspection results clean (no observations); management presenting multi-year capital investment plan with specific asset-renewal commitment.

Monitoring checklist:
- Capex vs depreciation ratio annual (5-year lookback)
- Net PP&E / gross PP&E ratio trend
- INVIMA GMP inspection registry for TEQ Cali facility
- Capex guidance in management presentations
- Maintenance capex vs growth capex disclosed breakdown

Interaction with other failure modes: Compounds with Scenario 6 (leverage) when a forced capex cycle collides with elevated interest rates; mild correlation with Scenario 3 (regulatory/INVIMA) if GMP observations trigger compliance capex.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence requires the capex/depreciation ratio to be computed from filings directly — if the ratio has been below 1.0x for ≥3 years, this FALSE FRIEND scenario should be elevated to the highest-priority monitoring item.

Multinationals have structurally under-resourced Colombia and other Andean markets for a decade; however, post-Kenvue spin-off and Bayer OTC restructuring, Latin America focus strategies are being re-evaluated; base rate for a full multinational competitive escalation in 18 months is low.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:

- Motive: LatAm OTC market growth rates are attracting renewed capital ✓
- Opportunity: currently limited — multinationals lack Colombian route-to-market depth
- Two-of-three legs — watch only

Disconfirming evidence: Multinational market-share data in Colombia stable or declining for three consecutive years; no announced LatAm strategy re-acceleration; TEQ market-share gains concurrent with multinational presence.

Monitoring checklist:

Interaction with other failure modes: Moderate amplification of Scenario 2 (retailer concentration) if multinationals target the same retail accounts with superior promotional spend; mild amplification of Scenario 7 (A&P false friend) — TEQ's deferred brand investment window is exactly when multinationals gain easiest share.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence requires actively monitored multinational strategy updates; this is a "slow-burn" scenario that the pre-mortem should assign to a 24-month monitoring horizon rather than the 12-month investment window.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:

§11a — Scenario 11: Technology / Distribution Disruption — Direct-to-Consumer / E-Pharma

Transmission: e-pharma share gains → TEQ branded OTC volume shift to lower-margin channels or generics → ASP decline → gross margin erosion → EPS miss.

- Management absent from digital commerce investment language in strategic presentations

Plausibility: Low plausibility in 12-month window; medium plausibility over 36 months. Colombian e-pharma penetration remains below 10% of OTC; however, the growth rate is among the highest in LatAm consumer categories; structural disruption risk is real but not near-term.

Means / Motive / Opportunity:
- Means: platforms have technology and capital ✓
- Motive: OTC is high-margin category attractive to platforms ✓
- Opportunity: Colombian digital adoption is accelerating rapidly ✓
- All three legs aligned — long-fuse risk

Disconfirming evidence: TEQ demonstrated digital distribution capability with e-commerce revenue growing ahead of market; partnership agreements with major platforms confirmed; management e-commerce strategy disclosed.

Monitoring checklist:

Interaction with other failure modes: Long-term amplifier of Scenario 2 (customer concentration) as physical-retail leverage erodes; mild interaction with Scenario 7 (A&P false friend) — digital channel demands different brand investment than physical.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence is partially observable through competitor analysis — Haleon and Kenvue Colombia digital share data would calibrate the risk horizon more precisely than TEQ disclosures alone.

4. PART B — META-ANALYSIS

  • Net signal on scenario clustering: three distinct failure clusters emerge, with the FX/macro cluster carrying the highest joint plausibility and FALSE FRIEND scenarios posing the highest mis-diagnosis risk for the investment thesis

Scenario cluster — FX / Input-Cost / Cash Generation:
- Scenarios 1, 4, and 6 form a tight causal chain (COP depreciation → working capital deterioration → leverage stress)
- This cluster is the highest-probability compound failure pathway; if Scenario 1 activates, Scenarios 4 and 6 have elevated conditional probability within 6–12 months

Scenario cluster — Competitive / Pricing / Brand:
- Scenarios 2, 3, 7, and 9 form a competitive-position cluster
- False-friend Scenario 7 (A&P deferral) is the gateway risk — it enables Scenarios 2 and 3 by creating the competitive window during which retailers and regulators act

Scenario cluster — Governance / Execution / Structural:
- Scenarios 8, 10, and 11 form a structural/slow-burn cluster
- These are individually low-plausibility near-term but collectively represent the "what the market hasn't priced at all" risk

Correlated failures — highest-risk joint event:
- Scenario 1 + Scenario 3 (FX shock concurrent with price-control event) eliminates management's ability to pass costs through; this specific combination has no mitigation lever and would produce the largest EPS miss

Joint probability — verbal assessment:
- Zero failures in 18M: low plausibility (requires benign COP, no regulatory action, management execution, no competitive entry — all simultaneously)
- Exactly one failure activating: medium plausibility; most likely candidate is Scenario 1 or Scenario 7
- Two or more failures activating: medium plausibility; compound pathways are clearly identified

Leading indicators meta — compound monitorables:
- COP/USD weekly close appears in Scenarios 1, 3, 4, 6 — single highest-frequency compound monitorable
- INVIMA regulatory activity appears in Scenarios 3, 8, 11 — second-highest frequency
- A&P / revenue ratio appears in Scenarios 2, 7, 9 — third-highest frequency; structurally under-monitored by consensus

Most-monitored failure mode: Scenario 1 (FX/input cost) — consensus bear cases for all Colombian consumer companies include FX; well-tracked, partially priced

Least-monitored failure mode: Scenario 7 (A&P deferral — FALSE FRIEND) and Scenario 9 (regulatory price control reform) — neither appears in consensus bear-case frameworks for TEQ specifically

False-friend density assessment:
- 2 of 11 scenarios are explicitly structured as FALSE FRIEND (Scenarios 7, 8)
