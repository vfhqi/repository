4. ACCOUNTING & GOVERNANCE RED FLAGS

NII Sensitivity to Rates

Avanza's NII is a function of client cash balances x deposit spread. With Riksbank at 1.75% (flat guidance), management says NII is "near the bottom." But this is a binary risk: any resumption of easing would directly compress the largest income line. Income from lending already fell 7% in Q1 2025 as average lending rates declined. The company's own 15% annual savings capital growth target through 2030 implicitly requires rate stability.

The Audit score of 10 (worst decile) is a material flag. ISS does not publicly disclose the specific drivers, but typical triggers include: auditor tenure, audit committee independence, related-party transactions, or restatement history. This warrants further investigation.

Chairman Tenure

Sven Hagstromer has been Chairman since 1997 — 29 years. He is also Avanza's founder and simultaneously chairs Creades AB (an investment company). Long founder-chairman tenure can correlate with governance entrenchment, though Avanza's Shareholder Rights score (1) and Compensation score (3) partially offset.

GDPR / Data Breach

In Jun 2024, IMY fined Avanza SEK 15M for unlawfully transferring personal data of 500K-1M customers to Meta via the Meta pixel (Nov 2019 - Jun 2021). Data included personal ID numbers, loan amounts, securities holdings, and credit limits. Fine was modest relative to revenues, but the breach scope was severe and raised questions about internal controls — consistent with the ISS Audit red flag.

Regulatory Environment

EU MiFID III and MiFIR PFOF ban (phase-out by Jun 2026) — Sweden already banned PFOF, so direct impact on Avanza is limited. However, broader MiFID III transparency requirements could compress fund distribution margins over time.

Credibility: Orange — ISS Audit 10 is a genuine flag. GDPR fine is fact. Rate sensitivity is structural. Chairman tenure is notable but not disqualifying alone.

10. SCEPTICAL REBUTTAL — STRONGEST BULL RESPONSES

  1. Market share trajectory is the wrong metric to watch — it's savings capital. Avanza grew savings capital to SEK 1,079bn (+34% implied from market share gain). Even if customer growth slows, assets compound via market returns and net inflows. The 15% CAGR target is achievable in a normal market environment.

  2. NII trough is already here. Management explicitly stated NII is near bottom at current rates. The Riksbank has paused. Further cuts are not consensus. And even if rates decline modestly, savings capital growth provides a volume offset.

  3. Q1 2025 was a record quarter — this is not a business in decline. Net profit +28% YoY, +13% QoQ. The business model works across multiple income lines, not just NII.

  4. Sweden-only is a feature, not a bug. Concentrated market dominance (8.3% share, #1 position) yields brand power, regulatory simplicity, and operating efficiency that multi-market players sacrifice for scale.

  5. Valuation is reasonable in context. At UBS's SEK 320 PT, the stock trades at 17.7x 2027E P/E with 9-10% EPS growth. For a capital-light, high-ROE platform with structural tailwinds (savings digitalisation), this is not excessive.

  6. ISS Audit score requires context. ISS methodology can flag long auditor tenures or specific committee structures that are standard in Swedish corporate governance. A score of 10 does not mean fraud risk — it means ISS's framework flags something. Requires investigation, not alarm.

  7. Hound Partners' position is tiny and tactical. Oscillating around 0.5% is not a conviction short. If the best-known short is running a sub-1% position, the short thesis lacks institutional backing.

Credibility: Green — these are the strongest counter-arguments, backed by recent financial data.
