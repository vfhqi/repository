[W] GAW — Key Question Research Memo: New Product Launch Pipeline (Next 24-36 Months) [C] @ 28-Apr-26

KEY FINDINGS

  • WH40K 11th Edition is the most significant financial event in GAW's near-term history. The June 2026 launch of the "Armageddon" starter box ($299, "largest ever") falls entirely within H1 FY2027 (GAW fiscal year runs June–May). FY2027 will be the first full fiscal year capturing this tailwind across all channels — this is the year the investment case turns measurably positive.

  • The FY2026 licensing base is dramatically depressed, which makes FY2027 comparisons misleadingly flattering. H1 FY2026 licensing dropped £14.1m to just £16m as Space Marine 2 royalties normalised. Total War: WH40K (likely CY2027 launch) and the Amazon series (2028-2029 at earliest) are the next material licensing catalysts; neither falls in FY2026.

  • Core business momentum is structurally sound independent of edition cycles. H1 FY2026 core revenue hit not less than £310m (vs £269.4m prior H1), delivering record half-year PBT of not less than £135m despite the SM2 royalty drop. This demonstrates that the base business — tabletop miniatures, paints, terrain — is growing without edition tailwinds. The 11th Edition launch should be additive to an already-strong base.

  • Factory 4 is the crucial operational constraint. The new 63,000 sq ft Nottingham facility was targeting spring/summer 2026 completion and specifically expands injection moulding and tooling capacity. If it comes online ahead of the June 2026 launch, GAW has headroom to meet demand for the largest-ever starter box. If delayed, this becomes the single biggest operational risk to launch.

  • Total War: WH40K is the correct licensing catalyst for FY2027-FY2028, not the Amazon series. Creative Assembly has confirmed only "lots more to talk about in late 2026" — a 2027 launch is the base case, which would fall within FY2028 for GAW. Given SM2 generated ~£30m of licensing in a single half-year at 70-90% drop-through, a comparable console/PC launch could be transformative.

  • Amazon series is firmly a 2028-2029 story. Pre-production began June 2025, creative guidelines agreed December 2024, Henry Cavill confirmed as star and executive producer — but no cast beyond Cavill, no showrunner confirmed, no episode count, no release date. Even optimistic assumptions imply a 3-year runway from pre-production to air. Investors pricing this in for FY2027 are running ahead of the facts.

  • Age of Sigmar 4th Edition is performing acceptably but is structurally a secondary line. AoS contributes an estimated ~20% of core revenue. The mid-cycle faction release schedule (Cities of Sigmar May 2026, Nurgle already Jan 2026, Fyreslayers/Ogors H2 2026) provides steady revenue throughput but is not a transformative driver. Reception is constructive, not electric.

  • 11th Edition community sentiment is positive but not unanimous. Multi-detachment mechanics and re-roll changes are contested. Critically, GW's decision to keep all 10th Edition codexes valid removes the "forced codex repurchase" uplift that drove revenue in prior transitions — a deliberate accessibility choice that may moderate the per-player average spend spike at launch, even as it broadens the addressable audience.

  • 3D printing threat is accelerating at the margin — AI tools now compress the timeline from GW preview image to printable file dramatically. GAW has filed legal action (against MyMiniFactory, January 2025) and softened tournament policy. This is a slow-burn erosion risk, not a near-term cliff; edition launches historically accelerate it because new previews generate new files.

  • Overall pipeline rating: GREEN. The 24-36 month pipeline is the strongest in GAW's history. Two major edition launches (WH40K 11th in FY2027, potential 5th Edition AoS further out), a confirmed video game sequel in the franchise (TW:WH40K), Factory 4 expanding capacity, and a media licensing platform with Amazon that — even if it arrives late — is a genuine option value. The FY2027 investment case is clearly positive; the FY2026 year is a transitional trough.

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pipeline Cadence Overview

The 24-36 month launch pipeline running from mid-CY2026 through CY2028 is structurally the most loaded in GAW's history. Three distinct revenue levers operate on overlapping timelines: (1) tabletop core — WH40K 11th Edition from June 2026, AoS 4th Edition mid-cycle faction releases throughout 2026-2027, Horus Heresy 3rd Edition (Saturnine) ongoing; (2) video game licensing — Total War: WH40K (Creative Assembly, likely CY2027) and the longer-dated Space Marine 3 (~CY2029); (3) media licensing — Amazon Prime Video WH40K series (Henry Cavill, pre-production since June 2025, realistic launch CY2028-2029) plus a confirmed AoS animated episode and ongoing Secret Level cultural momentum.

FY2026 (ends May 31 2026): Transitional trough year. The WH40K 11th Edition launch falls entirely outside FY2026 (it launches June 2026, the first day of FY2027). Core business is strong — H1 FY2026 core revenue hit a record £310m+ — but licensing is £16m vs £30m in the SM2-boosted prior H1. Full-year FY2026 revenue will likely be below FY2025's £617.5m on a licensing drag but core-supported basis. No major new edition releases within the year to drive a step-change.

FY2027 (June 2026 - May 2027): The inflection year. WH40K 11th Edition launches at the very start of the fiscal year and provides a full 12 months of tailwind across starter boxes, codexes, faction models, paint sets, and terrain. AoS mid-cycle faction releases continue at pace. If TW:WH40K launches in H2 CY2026 (early in FY2027), there is a licensing revenue contribution. Factory 4 expands manufacturing capacity. This is the year the investment case crystallises.

FY2028+: Second-cycle monetisation of 11th Edition (codex updates, campaign books, model refreshes), Space Marine 3 development approaching completion, Amazon series potentially entering late pre-production / production, and the first structural evidence of whether TW:WH40K has a DLC monetisation model comparable to TW:WH Fantasy.

Thesis: Does the pipeline justify a positive investment stance?

Yes, clearly and on multiple dimensions. The tabletop core is demonstrating structural quality growth independent of edition cycles (FY2026 H1 core revenue up ~15% YoY despite being an off-year). WH40K 11th Edition should drive a meaningful revenue step-change in FY2027. The video game licensing pipeline extends revenue visibility through FY2029-2030. The Amazon option, while early, represents unpriced asymmetric upside. The combination of a world-class IP platform, asset-light licensing economics at ~70-90% drop-through, and clear near-term catalysts makes a positive stance well-supported.

Key Risks

Three risks warrant monitoring: (1) Factory 4 delivery timing relative to June 2026 launch — if capacity doesn't come online in time, supply constraints cap the revenue opportunity; (2) US tariff impact on gross margins — GAW has flagged a ~2% gross margin headwind from tariffs in FY2026/2027, requiring operational mitigations; (3) community engagement with the multi-detachment system — if competitive players resist the 11th Edition ruleset, tournament attendance and competitive ecosystem spending could lag.

Overall Rating: GREEN

2. WH40K 11TH EDITION ARMAGEDDON — THE CENTREPIECE LAUNCH

Rating: GREEN

  • Official reveal at AdeptiCon, March 2026. Games Workshop unveiled 11th Edition to a packed live audience. The title is "Armageddon," the iconic war-world from WH40K lore, centring on a Blood Angels and allied Space Marine chapters vs Orks conflict in the aftermath of the "Return of Yarrick" narrative. The reveal included a cinematic trailer, live model reveals, and the first rules previews — the reception was enthusiastic.

  • Starter box: $299, "largest ever." GW itself has described the Armageddon box as the biggest in the game's history. Pricing at $299 mirrors the Horus Heresy Saturnine box, which serves as a useful internal precedent. A May 1, 2026 livestream unboxing was confirmed at time of announcement, suggesting a full June (likely June 20th) retail launch. The high price point is deliberate — it positions the box as a premium purchase and a collector/gift item while still being competitive with equivalent models bought à la carte.

  • Blood Angels vs Orks — significance of faction choices. Blood Angels received minimal support in 10th Edition — a widely noted grievance in the community. Featuring them as the launch faction is both a commercial signal (pent-up demand for new BA models) and a community management move. Blood Angels have a large, passionate player base, particularly in the UK and North America. Orks are perennially popular and high-unit-count, supporting starter box value. Neither is a niche faction.

  • 70+ detachments at launch is the key accessibility signal. Keeping all existing 10th Edition codexes valid and launching with 70+ detachments means players do not face mandatory repurchase to participate. This lowers the barrier to re-entry for lapsed players and reduces the "edition tax" friction that historically caused churn. The trade-off is that per-player spending at launch may be lower than in transitions requiring full codex replacement — but addressable audience is wider.

  • Rules changes: evolution not revolution. 11th Edition does not start over. Key changes include: no stratagem stacking on one unit; objective terrain footprints replace objective circles; cover shifts to Hit-roll impact rather than damage mitigation; melee streamlined across the charge/pile-in/consolidation sequence; hiding mechanics simplified but now penalised if you shoot or get within 15". These are meaningful quality-of-life improvements, not a wholesale reset that would alienate the existing playerbase.

  • Fiscal year mechanics: 100% of the launch benefit falls in FY2027. GAW's fiscal year runs June 1 to May 31. The June 2026 launch is the first day of FY2027. The entire launch window — starter box sell-through, codex releases, the wave of faction model kits — will flow through FY2027. This is the single most important timing fact for FY2027 estimates.

  • Comparable editions — the 10th Edition precedent. 10th Edition (launched June 2023) gave GAW "its best sales month ever" and drove FY2024 revenue to a then-record £460m (vs £427m in FY2023 — approximately a 7-8% uplift in the launch year, before the full-year effect). The next comparable data point is 8th→9th Edition (launched July 2020): FY2021 revenue of £353m was up from £256m in FY2020, though COVID-period demand dynamics muddy the comparison. The consistent pattern: a major edition launch creates at minimum a ~10% revenue uplift in the launch fiscal year.

  • Revenue pipeline timeline:

Quarter Revenue event
FY2027 H1 (Jun–Nov 2026) Armageddon box launch; Blood Angels/Orks codexes; first 11th Ed terrain range
FY2027 H2 (Dec 2026–May 2027) Faction wave releases; campaign supplements; potential TW:WH40K contribution
FY2028 H1 Second wave faction refreshes; 11th Ed competitive season

  • Trade channel dynamics. GAW allocates major launches across three channels: own retail stores (highest margin), trade (independent retailers/FLGS), and online direct. For the Armageddon box, trade pre-order windows are typically brief and quantities limited — artificial scarcity historically drives secondary market premium and community buzz. GAW has refined this mechanic over multiple editions. Own-store exclusives (paints, accessories) drive footfall.

  • Manufacturing risk is the principal execution risk. Producing the "largest ever" starter box requires significant injection moulding capacity — more sprues, more box components, more packing throughput than any prior launch. This is precisely why Factory 4's spring/summer 2026 completion timing is critical. If F4 is late, GAW could face supply constraints in the launch window, potentially damping revenue by £10-20m or shifting it into H2.

  • Community risk: multi-detachment mechanics are the contentious point. While overall sentiment is positive, the most vocal criticism at time of writing (April 2026) centres on multi-detachment army building — players worry it will create balance problems and complexity in competitive play. GW has a track record of iterating rapidly on FAQs and balance updates, so this is likely manageable, but it bears watching as the launch draws near.

3. AGE OF SIGMAR 4TH EDITION — MID-CYCLE EXPANSION

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN

  • 4th Edition launched July 2024 (Skaventide box). The edition is now approaching the end of its first full year. The battletome rollout — approximately one per month across all factions — is expected to complete by mid-2026. The overall reception to 4th Edition has been constructive: cleaner rules, the Spearhead quick-play format has been well-received as an AoS equivalent to Combat Patrol (notably more so than Combat Patrol's current iteration), and the Skaventide launch box was commercially successful.

  • Cities of Sigmar: May 2026. The Cities of Sigmar battletome and model refresh arrives in May 2026, meaning it falls within FY2026's final month. Cities is one of AoS's most popular "order" factions (human soldiers + elves + duardin) with broad hobbyist appeal. A model refresh for this faction is commercially meaningful — Cities players have waited several years for updated miniatures.

  • Nurgle: first major H2 2026 release. Maggotkin of Nurgle released January 2026, bringing Festus the Leechlord and new Nurgle cavalry (a "slug the size of your car" per community coverage). This is a significant model refresh for a popular chaos faction. Nurgle players tend to be committed hobbyists with high-spend profiles.

  • Fyreslayers, Ogors: H2 2026 - H1 2027. These factions are queued in the battletome rollout. Fyreslayers (dwarf berserkers) and Ogors (giant mercenaries) have smaller but dedicated player bases. Each battletome release generates a 4-6 week commercial window — new rules drive existing players to update/expand armies, and new model kits attract new entrants.

  • Revenue scale: AoS is estimated at ~20% of core tabletop revenue. GAW does not break out revenue by system, but analyst estimates and community convention broadly agree that WH40K accounts for approximately 60-65% of core tabletop revenue, AoS ~20%, with Horus Heresy, Old World, Kill Team, and specialist games comprising the balance. At FY2025 core revenue of roughly £560m (stripping out licensing), AoS is approximately £100-110m annually.

  • AoS vs 40K release cadence asymmetry. AoS has no major new edition catalyst in the 24-month window (4th Edition will not be replaced until ~2027/2028 at the earliest — assuming a typical 3-year edition cycle, 5th Edition would be CY2027). Mid-cycle faction releases provide steady revenue throughput, but there is no box-set catalyst equivalent to the WH40K 11th Edition launch. This is why AoS is a supporting act to the 40K centrepiece in FY2027.

  • Community reception: constructive, not transformative. The Spearhead format has been the standout success — it gives AoS a credible entry-level game mode. The Skaventide box was positively reviewed. However, there is community commentary that AoS struggles to attract new players at the rate WH40K does, partly due to lore accessibility and partly because competitive play for AoS has a smaller global tournament footprint. GW appears aware of this and has invested in AoS animated content (confirmed Amazon Prime episode) as a visibility driver.

  • Key risk: AoS mid-cycle lull between major faction waves. If two or three months pass without a significant AoS release, revenue from the system dips. GAW's release scheduling has become more consistent over the last two years (roughly a 4-6 week cadence per release window), but gaps still occur. This is normal course-of-business risk for a product-line-driven company.

  • AoS animated episode on Amazon Prime. GAW has confirmed a standalone AoS episode for Amazon Prime Video (separate from the live-action WH40K series). No release date confirmed. This is meaningful for brand visibility in a market where AoS is perceived as secondary to 40K — if the episode captures an AoS character with broad appeal, it could drive new player acquisition.

4. VIDEO GAME PIPELINE — TOTAL WAR WH40K AND SPACE MARINE 3

Rating: GREEN (medium-term); YELLOW (near-term)

  • Total War: Warhammer 40,000 — confirmed December 2025. Creative Assembly and Sega announced the game in early development on December 12, 2025. Four confirmed playable factions: Space Marines, Orks, Aeldari (Eldar), and Astra Militarum (Imperial Guard). The announcement trailer was well-received and generated significant community interest.

  • Console-simultaneous launch is a structural upgrade. TW:WH40K will be the first Total War game to launch simultaneously on PC, Xbox Series X/S, and PlayStation 5. This meaningfully expands the addressable audience beyond the traditional PC-only Total War fanbase. Console RTS games have historically underperformed, but the 40K IP may drive crossover from the console gaming audience that engaged with Space Marine 2.

  • Release timeline: likely CY2027. Creative Assembly has stated "lots more to talk about later in 2026" — implying the game will not release in CY2026. A late-2026 launch is theoretically possible but is not the base case. CY2027 is consensus, which is also notable as the 40th anniversary of the original Warhammer 40,000 Rogue Trader rulebook (published 1987). Marketing around a 40th anniversary tie-in would be commercially rational.

  • GAW fiscal year implications. A CY2027 launch maps to H1 or H2 FY2028 (depending on whether the game launches before or after June 1, 2027). This means TW:WH40K's licensing revenue contribution is a FY2028 event, not FY2027 — an important distinction for near-term modelling. If the game somehow launches in H2 CY2026, a small H2 FY2027 contribution is possible, but the bulk of royalties (typically earned over the first 6-12 months post-launch) would still fall in FY2028.

  • Licensing revenue model: high drop-through economics. GAW receives royalties as a percentage of game revenue, with approximately 70-90% flowing to operating profit (near-zero incremental cost). Space Marine 2 generated approximately £30m of licensing revenue in H1 FY2025 alone (the half-year including its September 2024 launch). If TW:WH40K achieves comparable commercial scale, the licensing impact is material — £20-35m in the first year post-launch.

  • TW:WH Fantasy precedent is instructive but imperfect. The Total War: Warhammer Fantasy trilogy (2016-2022) sold over 17 million copies cumulatively. However, WH Fantasy (now the Old World) does not have the same mainstream cultural penetration as WH40K. TW:WH40K starts from a stronger brand position but enters a more competitive market for AAA strategy games. The 40K IP advantage (Space Marine 2 showed the franchise can generate genuine mainstream excitement) is a meaningful positive.

  • Space Marine 3: confirmed, ~CY2029 expected. Saber Interactive (developers of SM2) have confirmed they are working on Space Marine 3. SM2 sold over 7 million copies and generated the largest single-game licensing windfall in GAW's history. SM3, if it achieves comparable commercial scale, represents a FY2030 licensing catalyst — a multi-year anchor to the investment case. No release date confirmed.

  • Other video game titles. The WH40K licensing portfolio is deep: Darktide (Fatshark), Mechanicus sequel in development, multiple mobile titles. None appear to be near-term material catalysts on the scale of SM2 or TW:WH40K. The licensing income from the broader portfolio likely provides a baseline of £10-15m annually independent of major launch spikes.

  • Risk: is the Total War franchise itself aging? Total War's last major commercial success was Troy (2020) and Three Kingdoms (2019). Pharaoh (2023) underperformed. Creative Assembly has undergone significant management changes and studio contraction. There is a legitimate question about whether the team can deliver a compelling RTS at the quality level required to maximise the 40K IP. The WH40K franchise can attract players who have no prior TW engagement; it cannot rescue a technically poor product.

5. AMAZON MEDIA LICENSING — TV/FILM PIPELINE

Rating: YELLOW (timeline risk) / OPTION VALUE

  • Current production status: early pre-production. As of GAW's own H1 FY2026 financial report (January 2026), the Amazon MGM Studios WH40K series remains "in development," with pre-production formally beginning June 2025. The project involves Amazon MGM Studios, Henry Cavill (executive producer and confirmed on-screen lead), Roy Lee of Vertigo Entertainment, and Natalie Viscuso. GAW is described as an active creative partner to protect authenticity.

  • December 2024: creative agreement milestone. The deal between GAW and Amazon was finalised in December 2024 following months of negotiation. Henry Cavill described agreeing on "a fantastic place to start our Universe, which has been agreed upon by those up on high at both Amazon and Games Workshop" — the starting point remaining secret (likely a specific character or campaign arc). This milestone — creative alignment between IP owner and studio — is genuinely significant; many licensed adaptations collapse at this stage.

  • Henry Cavill: status confirmed. As of January 2026 (most recent available data), Cavill is confirmed as star and executive producer. He has publicly described the project as "wonderful" and "going well." No other cast members have been announced. Cavill's personal passion for the Warhammer hobby (he is a known lifelong hobbyist) is a genuine qualitative differentiator — he will resist creative dilution in a way that a purely commercial attachment would not.

  • Realistic release timeline: 2028-2029 at earliest. Pre-production began June 2025. Assuming 12-18 months of pre-production (casting, script finalisation, location/studio prep), principal photography likely commences late 2026 or early 2027. A production period of 12-18 months for a high-budget sci-fi series is standard. Post-production (CGI is extensive — 40K's "grimdark" aesthetic is almost entirely VFX-dependent) adds another 12 months minimum. The arithmetic points to a CY2028 release at the optimistic end and CY2029 for a realistic base case.

  • Licensing economics: the financial upside is transformational if the series succeeds. GAW's licensing segment drops through at 70-90%. Amazon series licensing fees for a major franchise are typically structured as upfront guarantees plus backend royalties and merchandise royalties. A successful Amazon series on the scale of The Boys or Rings of Power could plausibly generate £50-100m+ in licensing income (upfront + ongoing) over a multi-year window. At 87% drop-through, this is almost pure profit. The option value is substantial even discounted for execution and timeline risk.

  • Secret Level episode (December 10, 2024): community proof of concept. The 15-minute animated WH40K episode ("And They Shall Know No Fear") in Amazon's Secret Level anthology series aired December 10, 2024 — a sequel to SM2's Titus narrative, produced by Blur Studio (Tim Miller) with Syama Pedersen (creator of the acclaimed fan-made Astartes series) involved. IMDB rating: 8.7/10 — exceptional for the genre. The trailer generated 2.1 million views before release. Community consensus: the best WH40K animation since Astartes, and a credible proof of concept that the IP can translate to high-quality screen content.

  • Age of Sigmar animated series for Amazon Prime: confirmed, no date. GAW has confirmed a standalone AoS animated episode for Amazon Prime Video. This is separate from the live-action WH40K series. It expands the Warhammer IP footprint on the platform and continues the relationship-building with Amazon that precedes the major live-action project.

  • Sceptical view — three specific risk vectors:

  1. Amazon Prime budget and delivery risk. The Rings of Power (£250m for season 1) overran budgets and received mixed audience reception despite exceptional production values. WH40K will be at minimum a comparable budget given CGI requirements. Amazon does not have a clean track record of delivering 40K-equivalent world-building on schedule and on-budget.

  2. Henry Cavill — Amazon relationship history. Cavill's departure from The Witcher (Netflix) involved documented creative conflicts. He left specifically over creative direction of the show. Amazon is a different studio, and Cavill has executive producer status (more control), but the pattern of talent-studio conflict on beloved IP adaptations is well-established.

  3. The WH40K "accessibility problem." 40K's lore is 40+ years deep, with thousands of named characters, dozens of factions, and an entirely original cosmology. No prior attempt to adapt it for mainstream audiences has fully cracked the accessibility problem. The series must be comprehensible to viewers with zero 40K knowledge while satisfying the existing fanbase. This is an extraordinarily difficult creative brief.

  • D&D: Honor Among Thieves comparison. The D&D film (2023, $150m budget, $208m global box office) is the comparable cautionary tale for beloved-IP mainstream adaptation. Strong critical reception, genuine fan enthusiasm, box office disappointment. WH40K's IP advantage is that it maps more naturally to existing popular sci-fi/action genre conventions than D&D's fantasy, making mainstream accessibility more achievable — but the risk of middling commercial performance persisting for what is an expensive production is real.

6. HORUS HERESY, OLD WORLD, KILL TEAM — SECONDARY LINES

Rating: YELLOW

  • Horus Heresy 3rd Edition (Saturnine): launched 2025, ongoing 2026. The third edition of the Horus Heresy specialist game (30K — the war between Space Marine legions before the current WH40K timeline) launched in 2025 with the Saturnine box containing Saturnine Terminators, Saturnine Dreadnought, MkII Crusade Space Marines, and the Araknae Quad Accelerator Platform. The 2026 HH release roadmap has been published by GW, confirming ongoing faction releases throughout the year.

  • Horus Heresy revenue scale: niche but passionate. HH does not have the broad accessibility of 40K or AoS — it assumes pre-existing WH40K knowledge and is aimed at more experienced hobbyists. Its scale is materially smaller than the main lines. The community skews older (30+ demographic), has higher average spend per player, and is notably tolerant of premium pricing. GW's strategy with HH is "depth not breadth" — high-quality specialist releases for a committed audience rather than volume releases.

  • The Old World: Grand Cathay launches May 16, 2026. GW revealed new Grand Cathay and Solar Auxilia pre-orders for May 2, 2026, with a shelf release date of May 16, 2026. Grand Cathay is one of the most anticipated Old World factions — a Chinese-themed empire of celestial dragons and disciplined warriors, never before available as physical miniatures. Its debut in Total War: Warhammer III created substantial demand; GW is finally fulfilling it.

  • The Old World's trajectory. Launched January 2024 with The Old World returning WH Fantasy Battles (the original Warhammer tabletop game) to the product line after its 2015 End Times discontinuation. The initial community reception was strong from returning veterans. The system runs on 10-15mm scale larger bases than Age of Sigmar and plays a distinctly different game. GW's release cadence has been slower than some community members expected, and the system's revenue contribution is genuinely niche — estimated at 3-5% of core tabletop revenue.

  • More Old World factions planned 2026-2027. Beyond Grand Cathay, further Old World factions have been teased including Kislev and potentially further Empire updates. Each faction release opens a commercial window for the Old World range, but unit economics remain small relative to 40K or even AoS.

  • Kill Team: Nemesis Operatives — a flexibility expansion. Revealed at AdeptiCon 2026, the Nemesis Operatives book provides flexible "build your own" nemesis operative rules for Kill Team, plus standalone releases for the Ambull and The Archivist (Zoat) — two creatures from Warhammer Quest: Blackstone Fortress. Kill Team continues to operate as GAW's "elite skirmish" entry-level format for WH40K — lower miniature count, faster games, lower cost of entry. Its commercial function is partly funnel: Kill Team players frequently expand into full-scale 40K armies.

  • Kill Team and 11th Edition synergy. A major WH40K edition launch historically revitalises Kill Team interest — the two games share lore and models, and new 40K releases often seed new Kill Team operatives. The 11th Edition launch period is likely to be accompanied by Kill Team releases using the new Blood Angels and Orks miniatures from the Armageddon box.

  • Underworlds, Warcry, Necromunda. These three specialist games continue at a steady pace. Warcry (AoS skirmish) and Underworlds (card-driven miniatures game) provide regular quarterly releases. Necromunda (WH40K gang warfare) has maintained a loyal niche. No transformative releases flagged in the 24-month window; these lines represent a combined ~5-7% of core tabletop revenue on current estimates.

  • Secondary lines: steady-state, not growth drivers. In aggregate, HH, Old World, Kill Team, and the specialist games provide consistent revenue throughput estimated at £50-70m annually (combined). They are not pipeline transformation drivers for FY2027, but they reduce revenue volatility during periods between major edition launches. Their strongest commercial function is diversification of the tabletop fanbase — HH appeals to older collectors, Old World to returning veterans, Kill Team to entry-level players.

7. WARHAMMER+ SUBSCRIPTION SERVICE

Rating: YELLOW

  • Current subscribers: 248,000 (as of H1 FY2026 report, January 2026). This is up from 207,000 in November 2024, 176,000 in 2024, and 136,000 in 2023 — a consistent 20-25% annual growth rate. The trajectory is positive but the absolute number remains small by streaming industry standards.

  • Pricing: approximately £4.99/month or £34.99/year . Regional pricing varies. At 248,000 subscribers paying an average of ~£28/year (blended between monthly and annual subscribers, and across geographies), the annual revenue from Warhammer+ is approximately £6-7m. This is not a material revenue contributor on a £600m revenue base — it represents approximately 1% of total revenue.

  • Content pipeline: Astartes sequel and more in 2026. GAW hired Syama Pedersen (creator of the acclaimed Astartes fan series) in March 2021. A sequel to Astartes — one of the most-watched pieces of WH40K content ever created — is "coming sometime in 2026" per a February 2025 teaser. Two other teased series ("Altar of Wrath" and "High Lords") remain without release dates. The content pipeline is real but slow; consistent with GAW's quality-over-speed approach to animation.

  • Warhammer+ as community tool, not growth driver. The more honest framing of Warhammer+ is not as a streaming competitor to Netflix or Disney+ but as a community retention and loyalty tool. The app includes army-building tools, vault access to out-of-print content, hobby tutorials, and exclusive subscriber miniature offers. At 248,000 subscribers, it is reaching a dedicated core that would be disproportionately disappointed if the service were discontinued — a retention asset, not an acquisition engine.

  • Is subscriber growth meaningful or stalling? 20-25% annual growth sounds impressive but needs context. The installed base of active WH40K players is estimated at 3-5 million globally. Warhammer+'s 248,000 subscribers represent approximately 5-8% penetration of the active player base. Reaching the next tier (500,000+) would require either significant content investment, meaningful price reduction, or a major marketing event (an Amazon series driving casual fans to subscribe is plausible, but premature). The growth rate has been consistent but not accelerating.

  • Sceptical view: content quality has been uneven. Outside Astartes (fan-made) and Secret Level (Amazon, not directly Warhammer+), the original animated content on Warhammer+ has received mixed reception. "Hammer and Bolter," the original animated anthology, was criticised for inconsistent quality. The production pipeline is slow — this is not a content powerhouse. For a subscription service competing for viewer time against Netflix, Disney+, and Apple TV+, the content volume and quality ceiling may limit growth.

  • FY2027 upside scenario. If the Astartes sequel lands in 2026 and generates significant community engagement, it could drive a subscriber spike. More meaningfully, the Amazon series (even at announcement stage in 2027 before it airs) could cause a sympathetic subscription uplift as casual fans seek to engage more deeply with the Warhammer universe. This is option value, not base case.

8. FACTORY 4 & MANUFACTURING CAPACITY

Rating: YELLOW-GREEN

  • 63,000 sq ft facility, Lenton area, Nottingham. Factory 4 is GAW's fourth manufacturing facility on or near its Nottingham campus, adding approximately 25-30% to existing factory footprint. The facility is housed in the same geographic cluster as Factories 1, 2, and 3, which simplifies logistics and workforce management.

  • Primary purpose: packing consolidation to unlock injection moulding expansion. The key operational insight is that F4 is not primarily adding new injection moulding capacity directly — it is releasing capacity in Factories 1 and 2 by consolidating packing operations (currently split between F1 and F2) into a single F4 facility. This allows F1 and F2 to expand their core competency (injection moulding and tooling) rather than being partially occupied by packing operations. Additionally, GAW plans to double tool storage capacity via an extension to the existing tool store.

  • Completion timeline: spring/summer 2026. The facility was targeting spring 2026 operational readiness as of available reporting. "Spring 2026" is ambiguous relative to a June 2026 launch — March-May 2026 is spring in the UK. If F4 reaches operational capacity by May 2026, it provides the full benefit for the June launch. If the timeline slips to summer (July-August), the opening months of the 11th Edition launch window will be served by the existing factory configuration, with new capacity coming online mid-launch.

  • Five factories now, with a sixth for paint. GW has also announced plans for a fifth factory specifically for Citadel paints manufacturing. This underscores the breadth of the capacity expansion programme — the current factory infrastructure appears to be a binding constraint on growth, and GAW is investing to relieve it across multiple product categories simultaneously.

  • Capital expenditure context. GAW does not provide granular capex guidance at this level, but the combined investment across F4 and the paint factory represents a significant commitment. In FY2025, GAW's cash position and debt-free balance sheet provide ample headroom for this investment without impacting dividend capacity. The company paid £2.48 per share in FY2025 core dividend — comfortably covered — and factory investments are revenue-capacity investments, not restructuring cost.

  • The critical supply chain question for June 2026. GAW has committed to the "largest ever" starter box at $299. This implies substantially higher volume requirements for plastic sprues, box components, rulebooks, and accessories versus prior editions. The production run for the Armageddon box will have begun many months before launch. If factory capacity was the binding constraint, GAW will have had to either limit initial print run (risking supply shortages at launch) or delay the product (not commercially viable given the AdeptiCon announcement). The very public $299 commitment and May 1 unboxing livestream suggests management is confident in supply.

  • Broader manufacturing strategy. GAW manufactures the vast majority of its plastic miniatures in the UK, which provides some protection against US and EU tariff risk (which primarily affects China-origin goods). The tariff headwind GAW has flagged (~2% gross margin impact) relates primarily to China-manufactured terrain, accessories, and some packaging components — not core plastic miniatures.

10. RISKS TO THE PIPELINE

Rating: YELLOW (manageable, monitored)

  • Manufacturing and supply chain risk — the principal near-term risk. The "largest ever" Armageddon box demands production volumes GAW has not previously managed. Factory 4's spring/summer 2026 completion is the critical path item. Even a 4-6 week delay in F4 becoming fully operational could result in below-optimal stock allocation at launch, creating secondary market price spikes, consumer frustration, and deferred rather than lost revenue (GAW typically re-stocks within 6-8 weeks). Probability: moderate. Impact: moderate.

  • Community engagement with 11th Edition ruleset. The multi-detachment system and changes to re-rolls are the most contested rules revisions. If competitive players (who drive significant spending on faction-specific kits) disengage from the tournament ecosystem, it reduces the "ecosystem spend" uplift historically associated with edition launches. GW has shown willingness to FAQ rapidly (10th Edition received frequent balance patches), which mitigates the risk. Probability: low-moderate. Impact: low-moderate.

  • 3D printing competitive risk: acceleration not cliff. AI tools (Meshy AI and equivalents) can now convert GW preview images into printable starting points rapidly. The timeline from "GW shows new model" to "printable proxy available" is compressing. GAW's legal action (against MyMiniFactory, January 2025) and tournament policy updates are responses, not solutions. The 3D printing risk is highest for: (1) large, expensive kits (the $299 Armageddon box is a target); (2) faction-specific accessories that casual hobbyists might proxy. This is a slow erosion risk, not a sudden disruption, but it compounds over 3-5 years. Probability (of material impact): low in 24-month window; rising thereafter.

  • Amazon series: delays and creative conflict. The 2028-2029 timeline is already built on optimistic assumptions. Amazon has a track record of budget overruns (LOTR: Rings of Power), and the creative challenge of 40K's lore accessibility is genuinely formidable. Henry Cavill's Amazon relationship is new (his prior tension was with Netflix/Witcher showrunners); his executive producer status provides more creative protection. A 2030+ launch is possible. Financial impact if delayed: purely an option value that does not materialise on schedule, not a negative event for the core investment case.

  • Total War franchise health. Creative Assembly has undergone significant studio changes and its recent non-Warhammer Total War titles have underperformed. TW:WH40K is a franchise-defining bet for Creative Assembly. If the product disappoints — on review scores, on commercial performance, or on community engagement — the licensing royalties are materially smaller than the SM2 precedent. The console-simultaneous launch is both an opportunity (larger audience) and a risk (CA has no prior console experience with Total War; technical execution could be compromised).

  • US tariff impact on gross margins. GAW has flagged approximately 2% gross margin headwind from tariffs in FY2026-2027, primarily affecting China-manufactured terrain, packaging, and accessories. The operational plan to recover through efficiencies is referenced but not detailed. At GAW's revenue scale, 2% gross margin = approximately £12-15m of annualised profit impact. This is meaningful but not existential — GAW's balance sheet is ungeared and cash-generative. Price increases are possible; GAW has successfully implemented above-inflation price increases multiple times in the 2020s with minimal demand elasticity impact.

  • Competitive threat from other miniature wargames. Corvus Belli (Infinity), Mantic Games (Kings of War), Para Bellum (Conquest), and emergent 3D-print-native games are all competing at the entry level. None have the IP depth, retail infrastructure, or community flywheel of GAW. Competitive risk is real but structurally mild in the 24-month window. The 11th Edition launch is precisely the event that re-activates lapsed players and pulls in new entrants before competitors can.

  • Price sensitivity at $299 for the starter box. The Armageddon box at $299 is the highest-priced launch box in 40K history. In an environment of consumer spending pressure (particularly in the UK/Europe), there is a risk that price sensitivity limits initial sell-through below GW's expectations. Historically, GAW's launch boxes sell through quickly regardless of price — the SM2/Saturnine £200+ box sold out. But $299 is a new ceiling, and the macroeconomic environment in 2026 is more uncertain than 2023's launch context.
