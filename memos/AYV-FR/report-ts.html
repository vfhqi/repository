Section 3 – What the company does 12 findings

Section summary
  1. Ayvens leverages its dominant 3.2 million vehicle fleet to generate highly visible recurring leasing margins and sticky service fee revenues.

Segment Economics and Customer Profile

1.
Product segmentation: The company's principal activity is operational leasing, which comprises 96.00% of earning assets with average contract terms of 47 months. Operating leases represent the vast majority of the company's vehicle portfolio, ensuring stable multi-year contract assets
2.
Recurring margin base: Revenue is composed of leasing contract margins reflecting financing spreads and services margins covering maintenance, repairs, tyres, and insurance. These services are billed as part of the monthly lease payment, creating highly stable recurring streams
3.
Customer demographic: The funded fleet is split between corporate accounts representing 67.00% and retail customers representing 33.00% of the total. The retail segment is split between small-and-medium enterprises at 65.00% and private individuals at 35.00%
4.
Negligible concentration risk: Customer concentration is low with the top ten corporate accounts representing just 5.60% of the fleet. This wide client dispersion insulates Ayvens from specific default risks of single corporate accounts
5.
Global operations: The company operates in 41 countries directly, capturing global cross-border corporate contracts. This extensive footprint allows Ayvens to serve large international clients across multiple jurisdictions

Competitive Moat and Value Chain Positioning

6.
Unmatched scale: Ayvens purchases approximately 700,000 vehicles annually, securing immense procurement discounts from tier-1 original equipment manufacturers. This buying power allows the group to secure massive volume discounts on car parts, maintenance services, and tyres
7.
Multi-brand leadership: Managing 3.2 million vehicles as of end-2025, Ayvens is the absolute leader in multi-brand full-service leasing. Its massive fleet provides significant operational leverage across the full-service leasing value chain
8.
Consolidated structure: The leasing industry is highly consolidated with top players Ayvens at 3.2 million and Arval at 1.9 million dominating the market. This consolidation raises barriers to entry, as new competitors lack the capital and scale to match pricing
9.
Robust customer stickiness: International corporate contracts are highly sticky due to the complex administrative switching costs of transitioning fleets to new lessors. Corporate clients prefer to minimize relationships to simplify operations
10.
Integrated value chain: Ayvens controls the downstream lifecycle through Ayvens Carmarket, its proprietary B2B online auction platform present in 38 countries. This platform optimizes resale prices by increasing bids per vehicle
11.
Other side of the story: Original equipment manufacturers are bypassing lessors by establishing captive financial institutions, limiting independent lessors' competitive rebates and direct customer access. This vertical integration could erode Ayvens' procurement advantages

Core Profitability Drivers

1.
Synergy capture driver: The realization of €440.00 million in cost synergies by end-2026 directly reduces operating expenses and represents a highly plausible driver. This represents significant operational leverage for the combined entity
2.
Synergy failure mechanism: IT system and office integration bottlenecks in key markets like the UK, Germany, and the Netherlands could stall the capture. This would keep operating expenses above targeted levels

4.
Repricing failure mechanism: Renewed price competition from Arval or captive automotive financial arms could compress leasing margins back below 5.00%. High price elasticity from customers would limit further margin pass-through

5.

6.
Funding failure mechanism: A steepening of wholesale interest rates or a rating downgrade would quickly increase financing costs and pressure margins
7.
This could turn the funding cost tailwind into a headwind
8.
Compound probability condition: For the full bull case to work, used car sales must stabilize at €400 per unit while cost-to-income declines to 52.00%. This requires all drivers to fire simultaneously, representing a compound probability

Independent driver structure: Key profit drivers are independent, meaning cost synergy capture can still succeed even if used car prices normalize faster. Operational efficiency gains do not depend on used car market dynamics

Other side of the story: Used car prices could collapse far faster than expected, as battery technology obsolescence drives electric vehicle residual values down. This would force massive non-cash impairment charges on returning fleets

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. The risk landscape is moderately elevated due to electric vehicle residual value volatility, though core integration risks have largely stabilized.

Asset Volatility and Operational Integration Risks

1.
Asset residual risk: Volatility in secondary market pricing for battery electric vehicles represents the most significant risk, directly accelerating non-cash vehicle depreciation. This risk is driven by subsidy reductions and high new vehicle prices
2.
Interest rate risk: Rises in wholesale interest rates could immediately increase financing costs before Ayvens can pass them onto new lease contracts, compressing the margin. Benchmark rate volatility has created significant funding spread variance
3.
Single failure mode: A drop in used electric vehicle residual values below expected contractual targets represents the single most damaging failure mode. This would force accelerated depreciation charges across the fleet
4.
False friend caution: Sluggish fleet growth is currently a false friend, masking underlying profitability as the company deliberately defleets unprofitable business. This restructuring of corporate accounts in the UK has supported margins
5.
Negligible short interest: There is currently no notable published short-seller report or unusually high short interest reported in the open market. This indicates that the bears are not aggressively positioning
6.
Primary warning signal: Investors must closely monitor the average monthly used car sales gross result per unit on the proprietary Carmarket auction platform. Falling auction prices provide an early warning of residual value pressure
7.
Other side of the story: Risks to residual values are heavily overstated because lease contracts are highly diversified, and Ayvens actively mitigates risk via second leases. Extending contracts reduces the residual risk at final end
