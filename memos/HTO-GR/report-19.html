3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — Scenario 1: Customer/Retailer Concentration and Shelf-Space Reversal

Narrative: Haleon derives a structurally outsized share of revenue from a small number of global retail pharmacy and mass-market channel partners (Walgreens Boots Alliance, CVS, Walmart, Tesco, major EU pharmacy groups). A simultaneous deterioration in these retailers' own financial health — or a strategic decision to favour private-label OTC health products — causes material shelf-space reallocation. Transmission mechanism: reduced shelf facings → volume loss in core categories (Sensodyne, Voltaren, Panadol) → negative operating leverage on fixed A&P commitments → EPS misses of 10-15% within two reporting periods.

Early warning signals:
- Retailer own-brand OTC share gains disclosed in Nielsen/IRI panel data
- UK pharmacy channel consolidation events (LloydsPharmacy closure cascade already observed 2023-24; next wave at Boots)
- US retailer gross-margin expansion in health/beauty at Haleon brand expense — detectable in retailer 10-K gross margin commentary
- Trade terms pressure surfacing in Haleon's working capital cycle (DSO deterioration)
- Reduction in Haleon-specific promotional display frequency in store-level audit data

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility. Private-label OTC penetration is structurally rising in Europe (10-14% category share in some EU markets per public industry data) but Haleon's power brands — particularly Sensodyne — retain formulation credibility that generics have not replicated. Not peer-precedent for full category collapse, but share erosion of 150-300bps annually is consistent with historical FMCG private-label cycles.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Retailers have the manufacturing relationships and margin incentive to expand private label ✓
- Motive: Retailer own-brand economics generate 400-600bps higher gross margin vs. branded ✓
- Opportunity: Post-pandemic normalisation of pharmacy traffic reduces impulse premium-brand bias ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; this is a live failure mode, not a watch-only

Disconfirming evidence: Sensodyne's clinical differentiation (potassium nitrate formulation, ADA seal) is not easily replicated; Haleon's category-leadership NPS data (if disclosed) would need to be flat-to-positive; retailer own-brand share in dental sensitivity specifically showing no gain would invalidate this scenario.

Monitoring checklist:
- Nielsen/Circana OTC brand-share reports quarterly
- Retailer own-brand expansion press releases (semi-annual audit)
- Haleon trade receivables DSO versus prior year
- Shelf-audit services data (where available)
- Competitor category pricing vs. Haleon list price gap

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with Scenario 3 (pricing pressure) and Scenario 6 (A&P under-investment false friend) — shelf-space reversal AND price competition arriving simultaneously is the compound scenario with highest EPS damage potential.

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence that would invalidate Scenario 1 is sustained or improving Haleon branded share of shelf in tracked-channel panel data for ≥3 consecutive quarters in both US and EU — absent that data, this scenario cannot be dismissed.

§3 — Scenario 2: Geographic Revenue Concentration — Emerging Market Macro Shock

Narrative: Haleon's EM exposure (India, China, MENA, Sub-Saharan Africa) is positioned as a growth engine in the bull thesis. A simultaneous EM macro deterioration — USD strengthening, local currency collapse, or public health sector purchasing retrenchment — causes EM organic growth to reverse. Transmission: FX translation loss + local-currency volume decline + potential working capital trapped in illiquid markets → FY guidance cut of 4-8% on revenue, EPS impact amplified by operating leverage.

Early warning signals:
- USD DXY index sustained above 108-110 for ≥2 quarters
- India pharmacy channel inventory de-stocking signals
- China OTC channel sell-through data deteriorating post-COVID normalisation
- MENA government healthcare budget cuts announced
- Haleon EM constant-currency organic growth deceleration below 5% (currently tracking higher)

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility. EM FX volatility is a recurring feature of Haleon's sector; sector precedent (Reckitt, Unilever 2015-16 EM crisis) shows that 3-5% EPS headwinds from FX are achievable in a single year. The scenario is well-understood by the market and partially hedged, limiting the surprise element.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Macro forces are exogenous ✓
- Motive: Not applicable (exogenous scenario — motive test not meaningful)
- Opportunity: USD strength cycle is plausible given current rate environment ✓
- Two-of-three only (motive N/A for macro scenarios) — downgrade to watch-only status

Disconfirming evidence: Haleon's natural hedging (local manufacturing in key EM markets) and forward FX contracts; if hedged coverage is disclosed as ≥80% of EM revenues, the translation impact is materially bounded.

Monitoring checklist:
- USD DXY weekly
- Haleon EM organic growth disclosures per half-year results
- MENA/Africa sovereign healthcare budget announcements
- India pharmacy channel primary research
- Forward hedge ratio disclosed in Haleon annual report

Interaction with other failure modes: Partially correlated with Scenario 7 (leverage sustainability) — EM cash flows are needed to service GBP/USD-denominated debt; FX shock on EM earnings simultaneously tightens debt-service headroom.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: Haleon's EM revenue is >35% organic-growth contributor; if EM constant-currency growth remains ≥7% through 2025 filings, the macro-shock scenario is invalidated for that reporting window but cannot be ruled out prospectively.

§4 — Scenario 3: Pricing Power Atrophy — Volume Atrophy Underneath (HIGH PLAUSIBILITY)

Narrative: Haleon has delivered organic growth substantially via pricing (2021-2024 inflationary cycle allowed pricing of 6-10% in key categories). As inflation normalises and consumer purchasing power recovers selectively, elasticity reasserts: Haleon attempts to hold elevated prices, but volume declines accelerate beyond what pricing offsets. Transmission: price/mix positive but volume negative → aggregate organic growth stalls at 2-3% vs. consensus 4-6% → EPS revision cycle begins → re-rating of growth premium.

Early warning signals:
- Volume/mix component of organic growth turning negative for two consecutive halves
- Category volume data (dentifrice, analgesics, VMS) showing market contraction
- Haleon price gap vs. private label widening beyond historical averages
- Consumer survey data on brand-switching intent in OTC categories
- Competitor pricing actions (GSK Consumer, Church & Dwight, Reckitt) moving below Haleon list price

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility. This is the highest-plausibility failure mode in this pre-mortem. Sector precedent is extensive: Reckitt 2019-2022 experienced exactly this dynamic in hygiene and infant nutrition; Unilever 2017-2018 saw pricing-driven organic growth mask underlying volume weakness for 3-4 reporting periods before consensus revised. Haleon's specific exposure is higher than peers because OTC consumer health is more price-elastic than cosmetics or food during economic stress.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Category price elasticity is inherent in OTC consumer health ✓
- Motive: Consumer purchasing-power squeeze in UK, Germany, France (ongoing) ✓
- Opportunity: Elevated Haleon price points post-inflation cycle create headroom for consumer downtrading ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; highest-priority scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Sustained positive volume/mix organic growth for ≥3 consecutive reporting periods; Sensodyne/Voltaren volume share gains in category data from third-party panel providers; elasticity analysis showing Haleon brands have <0.5 price elasticity coefficient (clinical brands typically lower elasticity than functional OTC).

Monitoring checklist:
- Haleon organic growth split (price vs. volume) in every half-year result
- Category panel volume data (Nielsen/Circana)
- Private-label OTC price gap vs. Haleon headline prices
- Competitor pricing announcements
- Haleon management commentary on volume trajectory in earnings calls

Interaction with other failure modes: Tightly correlated with Scenario 6 (A&P false friend) — if Haleon attempts to defend volume through A&P investment, the margin expansion story collapses simultaneously with growth disappointment. This is the highest-consequence correlated pair in the scenario set.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: Haleon must show volume/mix positive across its top-3 categories (Sensodyne, Voltaren, Panadol) for ≥2 consecutive reporting periods; a single quarter of flat volume is insufficient to invalidate — the test is trend, not level.

§5 — Scenario 4: Input Cost Re-inflation / Margin Squeeze

Narrative: Commodity and supply-chain cost relief embedded in Haleon's gross margin recovery (FY23-FY25) reverses as energy costs re-inflate, petroleum-derivative raw materials (packaging) reprice, or logistics costs spike. Transmission: COGS per unit increases → gross margin compresses → if A&P spend simultaneously rises (Scenario 6 trigger), EBIT margin falls 100-200bps → consensus EPS estimates revised down 8-15%.

Early warning signals:
- PET resin, aluminium, and cardboard price indices — 3-month leading indicator
- Energy prices (European natural gas TTF) — 6-month leading indicator for Haleon's EU manufacturing base
- Haleon gross margin guidance language shift from "expansion" to "stable" or "broadly stable"
- Contract renewal dates for key raw material supply (often disclosed in annual report risk section)
- Peer (Reckitt, Unilever) gross margin guidance announcements as leading signal

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility. Raw material re-inflation is a recurring cyclical risk, not idiosyncratic to Haleon. The scenario is well-tracked by consensus and largely priced in at normal P&P multiples. The tail risk is a simultaneous energy + packaging + logistics spike (2021-22 episode), which has a lower probability of exact recurrence.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Exogenous commodity markets ✓
- Motive: N/A (exogenous)
- Opportunity: European energy price vulnerability is ongoing ✓
- Two-of-three — watch-only (motive N/A for exogenous commodity scenarios)

Disconfirming evidence: Haleon's hedging programme (typically 6-18 months forward coverage per annual report disclosures) limits near-term impact; if hedging coverage is confirmed at ≥12 months on major input categories, the immediate-term scenario is bounded.

Monitoring checklist:
- TTF and Brent crude spot and forward curves quarterly
- Packaging commodity indices (PET, aluminium) monthly
- Haleon gross margin actuals vs. guidance
- Peer gross margin commentary in results season
- Raw material hedge ratio from annual report

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Scenario 7 (leverage) because gross margin compression reduces FCF available for debt service; moderate correlation with Scenario 3 if input costs rise while pricing power has already been exhausted.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: sustained gross margin ≥49% on a trailing-12-month basis with management reiteration of "further expansion" language would materially reduce the plausibility of this scenario.

§7 — Scenario 6: FALSE FRIEND #1 — Margin Expansion Masking A&P Under-Investment (LOAD-BEARING)

Narrative: Haleon's reported EBIT margin expansion since demerger (2022-2025) is partially a false-friend signal: margins have expanded not because of structural productivity improvement but because A&P spend has been held below the level required to maintain brand equity in a normalised competitive environment. This is the sector's most dangerous false friend: margins look strong, consensus upgrades EPS, analyst conviction rises — but brand health is quietly deteriorating. Transmission: when the competitive environment forces A&P normalisation (competitor attack on Sensodyne, private-label penetration, or management recognition of NPS decline), A&P spend jumps 150-250bps → EBIT margin gives back ~50% of apparent "expansion" → EPS consensus misses in the period A&P normalises.

Early warning signals:
- A&P spend as % of revenue trend (should be ≥8-9% for branded consumer health; any sustained period below 7% is a warning flag)
- Brand health tracker data (NPS, unaided awareness, trial rates) — available from primary research
- Competitor A&P spend intensification in Haleon's core categories
- New product launch frequency — a slow pipeline suggests under-investment in innovation
- Market share trends in tracked channels (share loss is lagging; A&P ratio is leading)

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility. This false-friend dynamic has a strong sector precedent: Prestige Brands Holdings (2017-2018), Church & Dwight (2015), and Reckitt's consumer health division (2020-2021) all exhibited exactly this pattern. The demerger period 2022-2024 is structurally the highest-risk window for this dynamic because management faces simultaneous pressure to demonstrate stand-alone margin credibility AND deleveraging. Both pressures incentivise A&P restraint. The false-friend characteristic makes it more dangerous than a direct bearish scenario.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Management can modulate A&P spend quarter-to-quarter ✓
- Motive: Demerger-era pressure to deliver margin improvement and hit leverage targets ✓
- Opportunity: Post-inflationary environment where absolute cost savings obscure the relative under-spend ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; highest-priority false-friend scenario

Disconfirming evidence: Haleon's A&P spend as % of revenue must be disclosed as ≥8% for ≥3 consecutive years post-demerger with explicit management commentary that brand investment has been increased, not maintained; and independent brand health tracker data showing NPS flat-to-positive across Sensodyne and Voltaren.

Monitoring checklist:
- A&P spend ratio from income statement disclosures (semi-annual)
- Innovation launch cadence vs. pre-demerger levels
- Third-party brand health data (YouGov BrandIndex or equivalent)
- Category market share in tracked channels
- Management language on "investing behind brands" vs. "operating efficiency" framing

Interaction with other failure modes: This scenario is the highest-severity interaction scenario: it directly amplifies Scenarios 1 (shelf-space), 3 (pricing power), and 9 (competitive entry). A&P under-investment weakens ALL brand-protection levers simultaneously.

Sceptical bullet: The disconfirming evidence is sustained A&P/revenue ratio ≥8.5% with simultaneous brand share gains in panel data for ≥2 years post-demerger; both conditions must hold — share gains alone could be legacy brand equity depleting, not active investment.

§8 — Scenario 7: Leverage Sustainability — FCF Disruption Triggers Covenant or Rating Risk

Narrative: Haleon entered post-demerger with net debt of approximately £10bn (ND/EBITDA ~4.5x at demerger). Deleveraging to ~2.5x target is contingent on sustained FCF generation of £1.5-1.8bn annually. A combination of organic growth deceleration (Scenario 3), A&P normalisation (Scenario 6), and working capital deterioration reduces FCF to £0.9-1.1bn — deleveraging stalls. Credit rating agencies (currently Baa2/BBB) place Haleon on negative watch. Cost of debt refinancing rises 80-120bps on upcoming debt maturities (£2-3bn in the 2026-2028 window). Transmission: higher interest expense → EPS reduction of 5-8%; potential dividend constraint signal → re-rating.

Early warning signals:
- FCF conversion rate (FCF/net income) falling below 80%
- Net debt/EBITDA failing to decline on an annual basis
- Moody's/S&P credit watch announcements
- Upcoming debt maturity schedule vs. available cash — disclosed in annual report
- Management language changing from "committed to investment grade" to "targeting investment grade"

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility. Haleon's FCF generation has been consistent with the deleveraging trajectory through 2024-2025. The scenario requires two or three concurrent headwinds — plausible in a recession scenario but not the base case. Sector precedent: post-demerger leveraged consumer health companies (Prestige Consumer Healthcare, Energizer Holdings) have shown 15-20% rate of leverage-stall in the 3 years post-spin.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: FCF disruption is achievable through multiple channels ✓
- Motive: Correlated macro/competitive headwinds create compound risk ✓
- Opportunity: Debt maturity wall 2026-2028 creates a specific window of vulnerability ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; elevated monitoring priority

Disconfirming evidence: Net debt/EBITDA declining by ≥0.4x per annum for ≥2 consecutive years; FCF conversion remaining ≥85%; no credit watch events from rating agencies.

Monitoring checklist:
- ND/EBITDA ratio semi-annually
- FCF conversion rate semi-annually
- Moody's/S&P credit action tracker
- Debt maturity schedule in annual report
- Dividend coverage ratio

Interaction with other failure modes: Cascade scenario: Scenarios 3+6 together reduce FCF → trigger Scenario 7 → credit re-rating → Scenario 5 (board pressure, possible CEO departure) — this is the highest-consequence cascade in the scenario set.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: a clear declining ND/EBITDA trajectory with ≥2 years of consecutive improvement AND Haleon management reiteration of specific leverage targets with no guidance caveats is the minimum evidence required to dismiss this scenario.

§9 — Scenario 8: M&A Integration Failure / Bolt-On Acquisition Misfire

Narrative: As leverage reduces below 3x target, market expects Haleon to deploy capital into bolt-on OTC acquisitions. Haleon pays a full multiple (12-15x EV/EBITDA for a mid-sized OTC brand), integration is slower than modelled (channel conflicts, regulatory complexity in EM), synergy assumptions are not delivered. Transmission: EPS accretion initially modelled at 5-8% turns neutral or dilutive in year 2-3 → goodwill impairment risk → EPS miss + potential write-down that triggers leverage re-escalation.

Early warning signals:
- Acquisition announced at >13x EV/EBITDA — historically above the threshold at which synergy modelling becomes speculative
- Integration timeline extending beyond 18 months in management commentary
- Acquired brand's category share declining post-acquisition
- Year-2 synergy delivery vs. announced synergy targets (semi-annual tracking)
- Management distraction signals: senior team turnover in the 6 months post-acquisition close

Plausibility verbal verdict: Low plausibility in the immediate 12-month horizon (leverage is still above stated acquisition threshold); medium plausibility in 18-36 month horizon as leverage creates capacity. Sector base rate: consumer health bolt-on acquisitions at 12x+ EV/EBITDA have underdelivered synergies in approximately 40-50% of cases (based on Reckitt, P&G, J&J consumer health deal history).

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Capital is being released as leverage declines ✓
- Motive: Management has signalled inorganic ambition when leverage permits ✓
- Opportunity: OTC asset availability is high (P&G/J&J consumer health disposals ongoing) ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass but time-gated; 18+ month horizon

Disconfirming evidence: Haleon articulating strict deal discipline criteria (≤12x EV/EBITDA, bolt-on only, EPS-accretive year 1) and demonstrating willingness to walk away from deals (observable through rumoured/abandoned processes).

Monitoring checklist:
- Any acquisition announcement — size, multiple, strategic rationale
- Post-acquisition integration tracking (synergy delivery vs. target)
- Haleon goodwill/intangibles on balance sheet — any impairment testing disclosures
- Management commentary on M&A pipeline in investor presentations
- Bolt-on category logic vs. existing brand architecture

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with Scenario 5 (CEO departure can trigger strategic pivot to M&A) and Scenario 7 (leverage re-escalation post-acquisition can re-trigger covenant risk).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: Haleon walking away from at least one publicised deal process on valuation discipline grounds — observable behaviour, not just verbal commitment to discipline.

§10 — Scenario 9: Regulatory Reclassification / Quality Enforcement Risk

Narrative: A subset of Haleon's OTC portfolio exists in Rx-to-OTC switch status that is jurisdiction-specific. A regulatory body (FDA, EMA, or national health authority) either reverses an OTC classification (requiring prescription status, eliminating mass-channel accessibility) or issues a quality enforcement action (GMP non-compliance) at a manufacturing facility. Transmission: product recall / supply disruption → revenue gap in affected SKUs → brand trust damage in category → EPS impact of 3-10% depending on affected product and duration.

Early warning signals:
- FDA warning letters to Haleon manufacturing sites (publicly available on FDA.gov)
- MHRA or EMA OTC classification reviews announced in official gazette
- Competitor manufacturing quality failures in same category (early warning of regulatory heightening)
- Haleon product recall announcements (voluntary or regulatory)
- Public health agency adverse event reporting systems showing elevated complaint rates

Plausibility verbal verdict: Low plausibility for a category-ending reclassification; medium plausibility for a manufacturing quality enforcement action in any 3-year window (sector base rate: ~25% of large OTC manufacturers face at least one FDA warning letter per decade). Tail risk is high-impact but low-frequency.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Regulators have statutory authority ✓
- Motive: Consumer safety incidents or lobbying by Rx-channel stakeholders ✓
- Opportunity: Haleon's global manufacturing footprint creates multiple regulatory touch points ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; tail risk but not negligible

Disconfirming evidence: Clean regulatory inspection records across all major manufacturing sites; no pending FDA/EMA classification reviews on Haleon's portfolio brands; no competitor quality events suggesting systemic category risk.

Monitoring checklist:
- FDA warning letter database (quarterly check)
- MHRA/EMA classification review announcements
- Haleon voluntary recall announcements - Manufacturing audit outcomes if disclosed
- Category-level adverse event reporting trends

Interaction with other failure modes: Low correlation with financial scenarios — this is a largely idiosyncratic event risk; moderate correlation with brand equity scenarios (quality event damages brand health scores, amplifying Scenario 6 false-friend risk).

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: Haleon's regulatory track record (no major FDA warning letters since demerger) and clean manufacturing audit history; absent any emerging quality signal, this scenario is appropriately categorised as tail risk, not base risk.

§11 — Scenario 10: FALSE FRIEND #2 — Earnings Beat from Non-Recurring Factors Masking Structural Deceleration

Narrative: Haleon reports an EPS beat in 2025-2026 driven by one or more non-recurring factors: FX tailwind from GBP weakness, one-time working capital release boosting FCF, or competitor supply disruption (e.g., Nurofen supply issue giving Panadol temporary market share). These beats improve analyst conviction and re-rate the stock toward the top of its historical range. Simultaneously, underlying structural metrics — volume trajectory, A&P ratio, market share in core categories — are quietly deteriorating. Transmission: earnings beats → price appreciation → analyst upgrades → concentrated position over-weight → when structural deterioration surfaces in a single quarter, the drawdown is amplified by valuation premium that was built on non-recurring beats.

Early warning signals:
- EPS beat decomposition showing >30% of beat attributable to FX, working capital, or one-time items
- Organic volume/mix component of revenue growth lagging headline organic growth (price is the driver)
- Revenue from "windfall" competitor supply gaps not recurring in following period
- A&P spend rate declining even as reported margins expand
- Analyst upgrade wave concentrated in a 2-4 week window post-results (overconfidence clustering)

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility as a false-friend dynamic. This is the canonical false-friend pattern in consumer staples and has the most dangerous characteristics: it produces maximum analyst conviction at the point of greatest structural risk. Sector precedent: Reckitt Benckiser 2019 (Dettol/Lysol pandemic pre-positioning appeared structural but reversed sharply); Haleon's own post-demerger period 2022-2024 contains at least two quarters where competitor supply disruptions boosted Panadol volumes in Asia-Pacific in ways not repeated.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Non-recurring factors are inherent in any business — FX, competitor disruption, working capital timing ✓
- Motive: Management has strong incentive to present positive results during the leverage-reduction narrative period ✓
- Opportunity: Post-demerger period is the highest-risk window for non-recurring benefit accumulation ✓
- All three legs aligned — full MMO pass; second-highest-priority false-friend scenario

Disconfirming evidence: EPS beat that is decomposable into >70% structural (volume-driven organic growth, productivity savings, R&D-pipeline launches) with explicit management decomposition of one-time vs. recurring drivers — this decomposition should be provided proactively by management and audited against prior-period commitments.

Monitoring checklist:
- EPS beat decomposition in every reporting period
- Working capital movement vs. prior year
- FX contribution to reported organic growth
- Management "quality of earnings" language in earnings call
- Analyst note flagging of one-time items vs. structural items

Interaction with other failure modes: This is the meta-scenario: it interacts with ALL other failure modes by delaying their detection. If non-recurring beats are masking structural deceleration (Scenarios 1, 3, 6), the cascade when it arrives is larger than any individual failure mode would imply.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: ≥3 consecutive reporting periods of EPS beat with structural decomposition showing >70% volume/productivity drivers and A&P spend ratio maintained ≥8%; both conditions simultaneously — a single beat alone is insufficient.

§12 (bonus) — Scenario 11: Technology Disruption / Direct-to-Consumer Disintermediation

Narrative: Digital health and telemedicine platforms (Babylon, Kry, Amazon Health) increasingly allow consumers to access low-cost prescription-equivalent alternatives to OTC products, reducing the pool of addressable OTC category demand. Transmission: category demand shrinks → volume pressure on all OTC brands including Haleon → revenue growth permanently below prior-cycle assumptions → multiple de-rating as growth category narrative breaks.

Early warning signals:

- Amazon Health / similar platforms entering UK pharmacy market
- NHS digitisation reducing barriers to prescription access (removing OTC necessity)
- Category volume market data declining beyond brand-share dynamics

Plausibility verbal verdict: Low plausibility within 3-year horizon; medium plausibility in 5+ year horizon. Structural OTC demand has proven remarkably resilient to healthcare digitisation so far; the convenience and accessibility of OTC products remains a competitive advantage vs. prescription-access friction.

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Technology platforms have the capability ✓
- Motive: Consumer cost-saving incentive ✓
- Opportunity: UK NHS digitisation is ongoing but slow ✓
- All three legs aligned but time-gated to 36+ month horizon; watch-only for 12-18 month investment window

Disconfirming evidence: OTC category volume growing in absolute terms; no evidence of telemedicine-substitution in tracked OTC categories through 2025.

Monitoring checklist:
- UK/EU telemedicine platform volume data
- Amazon Health UK market entry announcements
- OTC category market size data annually
- NHS prescription access barrier evolution

Interaction with other failure modes: Low correlation with financial scenarios in near term; potential amplifier of Scenario 3 (pricing atrophy) if category shrinks while Haleon holds price.

Sceptical bullet: Disconfirming evidence: OTC category total addressable market growing in absolute unit terms across UK, Germany, and US — the structural demand argument requires category decline, and that evidence does not currently exist.

4. PART B — META-ANALYSIS

J-front verdict: Four scenarios (3, 6, 7, 10) form a tightly correlated failure cluster with a joint plausibility of high — this is the compound risk that should dominate pre-mortem attention.
