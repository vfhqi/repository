Report Title

Croda International: Navigating Post-Peak Destocking and Portfolio Transformation in Specialty Ingredients

Key Takeaways

  • ~75% share price collapse from 2021 peak driven by severe post-pandemic customer destocking, unwinding of highly profitable COVID-19 lipid sales, and asset under-utilization from heavy growth capex made ahead of demand that depressed ROIC.
  • Portfolio rationalisation via £665m PTIC divestment to Cargill (June 2022) eliminated low-margin industrial drag, reducing industrial exposure from ~40% to 12% of revenues and creating a less cyclical, lower carbon intensity pure-play specialty chemicals business.
  • Margin compression from 24.7% (2022) to 17.2% (2024) reflects capacity utilization collapsing to 65-67%, with ROIC falling from 14.4% to 7.1%, requiring ~20% volume improvement to restore optimal 80% operating rates and historical mid-20% margins.
  • Industry-leading 82% exposure to Local & Regional customers in Consumer Care structurally supports growth premium versus peers over-indexed to multinational FMCG giants, as smaller brands rely heavily on Croda's formulation support and regulatory expertise.
  • Destocking cycle largely complete across crop protection (fully recovered by late 2025) and consumer health pharma segments, though Consumer Care required tactical price reductions in less differentiated categories; shared site utilization recovered to 92% of 2019 baseline by mid-2025 with full normalization targeted for H2 2026.
  • Durable competitive moat anchored by customer stickiness: over 90% retention of larger customers over five years, reinforced by extensive claims testing/validation data, 1,700+ patents, and co-creation model making ingredient substitution extremely difficult.
  • Medium-term framework (2026-2028) targets 3-6% organic sales CAGR, margin expansion beyond 20%, ROIC recovery to at least 10%, and free cash flow conversion above 12% of sales, supported by £100m cost savings program and £50m working capital release.

Business Model and Revenue Architecture

Croda International operates a highly differentiated business model focused on creating value through direct selling and customer-focused innovation. The company uses predominantly natural, bio-based raw materials and proprietary downstream processes—such as derivatisation, refinement, and purification—to provide approximately 6,000 specialty ingredients to over 17,000 customers globally. Croda's core value proposition relies on deep customer intimacy, extensive formulation expertise, and a direct sales approach that tailors ingredients for specific applications. Following a multi-year portfolio transition, Croda has repositioned itself as a pure-play specialty chemicals business focused exclusively on high-value niches across consumer and life science markets.
(ARS, 28 Feb 23; ARS, 24 Feb)

Divisional Architecture and Margin Profile

The Group's sales, marketing, and research activities are organised into three global market sectors: Consumer Care, Life Sciences, and Industrial Specialties.

Consumer Care

Consumer Care is Croda's largest division, generating £972.7 million in revenue for FY2025, representing approximately 57% of total Group sales. The division delivered an adjusted operating profit of £169.8 million in 2025 (margin: 17.5%). The portfolio comprises four main sub-segments: Beauty Care, Beauty Actives, Fragrances and Flavours (F&F), and Home Care. Beauty Actives focuses on premium ingredients such as peptides and ceramides, while the F&F business acts as a fast-growing emerging market specialist.
The strategic trajectory for Consumer Care centres on reinvigorating the Beauty franchise globally and deepening proximity to Local and Regional (L&R) customers. These L&R customers are innovating faster and winning market share from multinational companies, and now account for 82% of Croda's Consumer Care sales. Because Croda provides more formulation and testing support to these smaller brands, the company can command higher prices and secure attractive margins.
- Jefferies highlights that this industry-leading exposure to L&R customers structurally supports growth and provides vital downside protection compared to peers who are heavily over-indexed to global FMCG giants. (Jefferies Research, 23 Apr)
- Rothschild & Co Redburn notes that the F&F business—acquired via Iberchem—has been a stellar performer that delivers unique diversification benefits through its exclusive exposure to emerging markets. (Rothschild & Co Redburn, 18 Oct 24)

Life Sciences

Life Sciences generated £532.2 million in revenue in FY2025, representing roughly 31% of the Group total. The division posted an adjusted operating profit of £116.5 million (margin: 21.9%). The segment provides advanced delivery systems and is structured around three units: Pharma, Crop Protection, and Seed Enhancement. Pharma focuses on high-purity excipients, lipid delivery systems for mRNA vaccines, and consumer health ingredients. Crop Protection and Seed Enhancement develop delivery systems that improve the yield and sustainability profiles of agricultural formulations.
The strategic trajectory for Life Sciences is focused on rebalancing the Pharma portfolio:
- Pharma Ingredients (>70% of Pharma sales): core consumer health ingredients and advanced excipients for established drugs
- Pharma Solutions (<30%): volatile segments like lipid technologies and vaccine adjuvants

In Agriculture, Croda is applying AI modelling and focusing on bio-based crop care solutions to drive niche differentiation.

Industrial Specialties

Industrial Specialties posted £194.5 million in revenue and £9.0 million in adjusted operating profit in FY2025 (margin: 4.6%). This division primarily exists to support the efficiency of Croda's integrated manufacturing model. Sales are derived exclusively from shared manufacturing sites, leveraging available capacity and monetising co-streams and by-products. While its standalone margins are structurally lower, filling capacity with Industrial Specialties volumes ensures fixed-cost absorption across Croda's global asset base, thereby protecting the profitability of Consumer Care and Life Sciences.
(ARS, 24 Feb; ARS, 16 Mar; ARS, 15 Mar 24)

Divisional Mix Evolution and Portfolio Rationalisation

Over the last 3-5 years, Croda's divisional mix has undergone a profound structural shift away from industrial chemicals and toward higher-growth, specialized health and consumer markets. In 2019, approximately 73% of total sales were directed toward consumer, pharma, and agriculture end-markets. By the end of 2025, following aggressive capital reallocation and M&A activity, this figure had increased to 89%.
(ARS, 24 Feb; INTRM, 24 Feb)

The most transformative event was the June 2022 divestment of the majority of the Performance Technologies and Industrial Chemicals business to Cargill for gross proceeds of €775 million (£665 million). The PTIC business was highly exposed to the cyclicality of broader industrial and automotive markets. The divestment removed four manufacturing sites and numerous sales offices from Croda's network, dramatically shrinking the industrial footprint.
(INTRM, 28 Feb 23; ARS, 8 Mar 23; ARS, 19 Nov 22)

Rothschild & Co Redburn observes that prior to 2019, the industrial segment routinely constituted nearly 40% of Group revenues, but this share fell to just 12% following the Cargill transaction.
(Rothschild & Co Redburn, 16 Sep 24)

The financial and strategic impact of the PTIC divestment:
- Eliminated a low-margin, capital-intensive drag on the portfolio
- Created a less cyclical business with lower carbon intensity
- The significant cash proceeds enabled reinvestment of over £175 million into high-return Pharma capacities (nucleic acid and protein delivery systems)
- Enabled strategic bolt-on acquisitions like South Korea's Solus Biotech (ceramide capabilities in Asia)
(ARS, 28 Feb 23; ARS, 8 Mar 23; INTRM, 29 Jul 22)

Competitive Position, Moat, and Industry Structure

Croda International's competitive moat is fundamentally built on its formulation expertise, proprietary downstream processing capabilities, and a highly integrated direct-selling model. The company leverages its smart science and core technologies to transform bio-based alternative feedstocks into highly specialised ingredients. Approximately 58% of Croda's raw material volumes are derived from organic, bio-based resources.

Rather than operating large continuous-operation plants, Croda relies on flexible, capital-light manufacturing sites that produce approximately 6,000 specialty ingredients in small batches. This agile manufacturing setup is complemented by a direct sales team that works closely with R&D colleagues to co-create bespoke solutions for over 17,000 customers.
(ARS, 12 Mar; ARS, 18 May; ARS, 12 Mar 25; ARS, 24 Feb)

Moat Anchors

  • Customer stickiness: Over 90% retention of larger customers over a five-year period. Stickiness reinforced by extensive claims testing and validation data, making it extremely difficult for customers to formulate Croda's ingredients out of their final products. (ARS, 24 Feb; ARS, 12 Mar; Transcript — Earnings Calls, 24 Feb)
  • IP portfolio: 1,700+ patents, with sales of patented ingredients continuing to grow steadily.
  • Pricing power: Specialty ingredients typically represent only a small, single-digit percentage of a customer's total production costs, providing significant pricing power and absolute profit protection. (Morgan Stanley, 25 Mar)
  • Pricing agility: Superior ability to pass through input cost inflation within one quarter (vs. peer average of two quarters), closely aligned with Croda's internal inventory cycle of approximately 2.6 months.

Consumer Care: End-Markets and Market Share

  • Global beauty ingredients market: ~$8 billion addressable opportunity; Croda holds a top-three global position with an estimated 10% market share.
  • F&F market: ~$25 billion addressable; Croda holds approximately 2% market share (small but rapidly growing).
  • L&R customer shift: from 73% in 2019 to 82% of Consumer Care sales by 2025, providing structural growth premium.

Jefferies Research emphasizes that smaller L&R brands rely much more heavily on external formulation partners like Croda because they possess limited in-house R&D capabilities. Consequently, suppliers equipped with local application laboratories and regulatory expertise are structurally positioned to outperform the broader end-market.
(Jefferies Research, 23 Apr)

Life Sciences: End-Markets and Market Share

  • Advanced pharmaceutical excipients, adjuvants, and delivery systems: ~$2 billion addressable market; Croda holds a top-three position with approximately 15% global market share.
  • Agriculture and crop protection: ~$4 billion addressable market; Croda is a leading supplier of adjuvants, co-formulants, and microplastic-free seed coatings.

Morgan Stanley Research notes that the pharmaceutical delivery systems market is dominated by a select group of specialized players: Croda, Evonik, Ashland, Roquette, and Merck. Croda is widely recognized for its robust market position in high-purity lipids and small molecule excipients, competing closely with Evonik in the lipid nanoparticle space. Pharmaceutical customers strongly prefer long-term agreements with suppliers who can seamlessly scale production from early-stage pilot quantities to full commercial volumes within a single facility.
(Morgan Stanley, 24 Mar)

Broader Industry Structure

The broader specialty ingredients sub-sector is characterized by extremely high barriers to entry — intensive R&D, complex formulation capabilities, continuous generation of novel safe molecules, and complex regulatory compliance (IFRA for fragrances, REACH in Europe, FDA approvals for pharmaceutical components).

Specialty chemical companies sit in a highly defensive position within the value chain, acting as critical intermediaries between upstream bulk commodity producers and downstream consumer and healthcare brands. Their value is derived entirely from application performance, technical integration, and sustainability validation.
(Earnings Presentation, 12 May; Press Release, 22 May)

§C1 — Granular FY2025 Financial Data

  • FY2025 adjusted PBT: £276.2m, up 8.4% on FY2024 (£260.0m); constant currency equivalent £282.0m. Source: Croda FY2025 Results.
  • IFRS operating profit anomaly: IFRS operating profit FY2025 only £110.1m (2024: £227.5m), depressed by £107.3m impairment charges, £44.6m lipids capacity optimisation costs, and £15.9m onerous contract provision — all related to post-COVID LNP overcapacity. The £107.3m impairment formally crystallises the write-down of excess mRNA manufacturing infrastructure; management is acknowledging the COVID investment was structurally over-scaled for base demand. Source: Croda FY2025 Results.
  • IFRS PBT anomaly in 2022: IFRS PBT was £780m in FY2022, inflated by the £356m gain on the PTIC divestment to Cargill; the underlying continuing operations profit was materially lower.
  • ROIC trajectory: FY2022 peak ROIC approximately 14–15%; FY2024 restated ROIC 7.7% (new peer-comparable definition: adjusted operating profit net of tax divided by average adjusted invested capital); FY2025 ROIC 8.2% — slowly recovering toward the 10% target. New definition removes goodwill add-back to improve peer comparability.
  • Net debt FY2025: £523.8m (2024: £532.3m); leverage ratio 1.3x EBITDA (2024: 1.4x; target range 1–2x).
  • Dividend payout ratio: FY2025 full year dividend 111p per share (2024: 110p), a 1p token increase; payout ratio 76% — well above the stated 40–50% policy because earnings have not recovered proportionally to the maintained dividend. This is unsustainably above policy; the Board has maintained the dividend despite earnings compression for three consecutive years, creating material risk of a cut if recovery stalls.

§C2 — Competitive Landscape: Named Competitors and Market Sizing

  • Consumer Care competitive intensity: Top-tier competitors include BASF SE (Care Chemicals division), Evonik Industries AG (Nutrition and Care, Personal Care actives), Ashland Inc. (specialty ingredients), Clariant AG (personal care), Dow (consumer solutions), Wacker Chemie, and Innospec. No single player has market-defining share; the field is fragmented above approximately 5% individual share. The personal care ingredients market is approximately $13–17bn globally (MarketsandMarkets estimates $17bn by 2030 at approximately 4.4% CAGR).
  • Life Sciences excipients competitive intensity: Narrower field; major players are Evonik (Lipoid), BASF Pharma Solutions, Ashland (excipients), Lubrizol (drug delivery polymers), and Croda/Avanti (LNP lipids). Pharmaceutical excipients is a separate approximately $8–10bn market growing faster at 6–8% CAGR driven by biologic drug delivery complexity.
  • Crop Protection competitive intensity: BASF, Evonik, Nufarm, and several regional Indian and Chinese specialty chemical producers; Croda holds strong positions in adjuvants and seed enhancement but faces pricing pressure from Chinese generics.
  • Moat caveat: The moat in Consumer Care is real but not impenetrable; BASF, Evonik, and Ashland are aggressive in the same markets; Croda's premium pricing strategy means it loses bids to lower-cost alternatives in volume-sensitive formulations; where sustainability differentiation erodes, switching costs fall.

§C3 — Pharma Excipient Switching Costs: Regulatory Mechanism

  • Drug delivery excipients are embedded in regulatory filings (drug master files, NDA and MAA references); switching supplier requires new validation studies, batch manufacturing records, and regulatory authority notification — a 12–24 month process per product that makes switching economically irrational in most cases. This is the structural mechanism underpinning Croda's pharma moat and is distinct from, and materially stronger than, the switching costs in Consumer Care.
  • Pfizer has a multi-year lipid supply agreement established via the Avanti relationship — a critical anchor contract for Croda's LNP revenue base.

§C4 — Avanti Acquisition: Full Detail and Aftermath

  • Avanti Polar Lipids acquired July 2020 for initial $185m plus up to $75m earn-out (contingent on commercial opportunities); thesis was LNP excipients as an emerging platform for mRNA therapeutics and gene therapies beyond COVID-19. Fully integrated into Life Sciences Pharma under the "Lipid Systems" brand.
  • COVID-19 lipid revenues peaked in 2021 at approximately $190m; the subsequent collapse in COVID vaccine demand left Croda with excess LNP manufacturing capacity. Subsequent capex to build GMP LNP capacity elevated total invested capital significantly above the headline acquisition price.
  • Croda via Avanti now offers 2,000+ lipid grades for drug research; LNP excipients for mRNA therapeutics require GMP-grade manufacturing and regulatory clearance — barriers limiting the field to Croda, Evonik (Lipoid), and a handful of specialist CDMOs.
  • mRNA platform therapies beyond COVID (oncology, rare disease, influenza, HIV) represent a decade-long structural growth opportunity for LNP excipients — this is the long-term thesis that remains intact despite the near-term overcapacity write-downs.

§C5 — Share Price Decline: Causes and Valuation Context

  • Peak-to-trough decline: Croda shares peaked at approximately 10,397p in December 2021; as of May 2026 the share price is approximately 2,991p — a decline of approximately 71% over 4.5 years, among the worst large-cap UK specialty chemicals drawdowns of the cycle.
  • Profit warnings: Croda issued multiple profit warnings in 2023 and 2024; in October 2023 alone the company cut full-year adjusted PBT guidance from £370–400m to £300–320m, destroying investor confidence in management visibility.
  • Heavy capex ahead of demand: Capital expenditure to scale LNP manufacturing, expand Asian Consumer Care capacity, and integrate Avanti, Iberchem, and Solus Biotech peaked while revenues fell — FCF conversion deteriorated and the balance sheet was stressed.
  • Valuation context: At approximately 2,991p the stock trades on approximately 20–22x FY2026 adjusted EPS (consensus); the P/E multiple appears elevated relative to the trough earnings base but not relative to the 2028 earnings recovery if management's targets are achieved — a classic trough valuation investment question.
  • 2028 margin stretch: The FY2025 recovery is real but 17.4% adjusted operating margin remains 250–300 basis points below the 2028 target of >20%; with the dividend payout ratio at 76%, there is very limited balance sheet headroom if the recovery stalls.

§C6 — Sustainability as Genuine Structural Advantage

  • Croda's chemistry originates from oleochemical feedstocks (natural fats and oils); this bio-based foundation predates sustainability as a marketing concept and provides genuine feedstock differentiation vs synthetic alternatives facing increasing regulatory scrutiny — this is structural, not greenwash.
  • Croda provides carbon footprint data for over 1,500 Beauty Care product codes and 750+ Home Care codes, enabling customer sustainability claims — a measurable service differentiator vs competitors with less granular sustainability data provision.
  • Vertical integration advantage: Croda controls quality from renewable raw material to finished specialty ingredient; this supply chain depth is unavailable to commodity chemical converters and creates cost and consistency advantages in high-purity applications.

§C7 — NPS Trajectory and Customer Co-Creation

  • Consumer Care NPS increased to +42 in FY2025 (FY2024: +31); Life Sciences NPS +49 (FY2024: +41); improving NPS on a recovering revenue base suggests commercial relationships are strengthening post-destocking. Source: Croda FY2025 Results.
  • Average pipeline value of each customer co-creation project increased 12% in FY2025 — a structural lock-in mechanism as customers embed Croda scientists in their formulation labs. Source: Croda FY2025 CEO Statement.
  • Consumer care formulators typically use 15–30 ingredients per product; reformulating to replace one Croda ingredient requires new stability testing, consumer trials, and potentially new claims validation — switching costs are real though lower than pharma.

§C8 — Manufacturing Footprint Rebalancing (Asia)

  • New low-emissions production centre commissioned at Dahej, India (lower cost per unit than existing Indian facility).
  • New F&F and Actives facility under commissioning in Guangzhou, China — shifting capacity toward higher-growth Asia markets.
  • Solus Biotech (ceramides, acquired 2023 in South Korea): commercialisation initially slower than anticipated; ceramide sales up 36% in FY2025 at constant currency (albeit from low base); upgraded data packages and global sales network roll-out now underway.

§C9 — Listed Entity and Company Provenance

  • Listed entity: London Stock Exchange , founded 1925, headquartered Snaith, East Yorkshire; ~£3.2bn market cap as of May 2026. The company's 100-year history in oleochemistry underpins its raw material expertise and institutional customer relationships — not a recent re-positioning.
  • Revenue scale in context: FY2025 group sales £1,699.4m, up 4.4% on FY2024 (£1,628.1m); modestly recovering from the 2022–24 trough. On a like-for-like Consumer Care plus Life Sciences basis, FY2025 has essentially recaptured the 2022 continuing-ops revenue base of approximately £1.7–1.8bn, meaning the multi-year destocking cycle has returned the business to its pre-COVID specialty revenue level.
  • Iberchem acquisition: Acquired 2020 for approximately $820m. FY2025 Fragrances and Flavours grew 15%, now a standout contributor to Consumer Care and the strongest sub-segment performer in the division during FY2025. Source: Croda FY2025 CEO Statement.

§C10 — Sceptical / Risk Flags Summary [C]

  • Consumer Care adjusted operating margin at 17.5% in FY2025 remains meaningfully below management's own >20% medium-term aspiration; four years post-peak and margin recovery is gradual, not V-shaped.
  • The 2028 margin target of >20% requires approximately 260 basis points of margin expansion from FY2025's 17.4% over three years; with £100m cost savings contributing approximately 5–6% of sales, the residual 1–2% of margin improvement must come from volume leverage and mix improvement — achievable but dependent on macro conditions management cannot control.
  • The combined effect of Chinese generic competition in crop adjuvants, US pharma regulatory uncertainty (referenced by management in FY2025), and potential AI-assisted formulation tools reducing customer dependence on supplier co-creation are structural threats to monitor — not currently material but directionally relevant.
  • Residual macro risks: US trade tariff uncertainty affecting customer order patterns; Chinese generic crop adjuvants pressuring Crop Protection pricing; pharma regulatory environment creating uncertainty for Pharma customers.

§C11 — Industry Pricing Power and Sector Positioning

  • Specialty ingredients are largely price-inelastic in pharma (regulatory lock-in) and moderately price-inelastic in Consumer Care (formulation embedding); during the 2022–24 destocking Croda maintained prices while volumes fell — demonstrating that pricing power held even when demand was collapsing. This is the key structural characteristic distinguishing specialty ingredients from commodity chemicals.
  • Specialty ingredients typically price at 5–10x commodity chemical equivalents; the sector is characterised by high switching costs, long qualification cycles, and formulation expertise that is tacit and hard to replicate.
  • The personal care ingredients market (approximately $13–17bn) and pharmaceutical excipients market (approximately $8–10bn, growing at 6–8% CAGR) together represent Croda's primary addressable markets. Growth is structurally supported by aging demographics increasing pharmaceutical demand and premiumisation in personal care driving demand for high-performance actives.
  • Local and Regional customers now represent 56% of Crop Protection sales (up from lower bases in 2019) — the same structural mix shift driving Consumer Care prem
