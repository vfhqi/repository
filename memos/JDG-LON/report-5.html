  • Sub: Reported growth includes M&A contribution; Geotek and one smaller 2024 acquisition contributed approximately £6-8m to FY2024 revenues

  • Organic growth: Stripping M&A contribution, organic revenues declined approximately 7-9% YoY, the first organic contraction since FY2020 [#A]

  • Sub: FY2022 and FY2023 organic growth was approximately 15-20%, suggesting the current softness represents a meaningful reversal of a demand cycle, not just noise

  • Segment trajectory: No formal geographic or end-market segment reporting at JDG (decentralised model with ~30 subsidiaries) — revenue breakdown must be proxied from subsidiary-level commentary and Annual Report disclosures [#A]

  • Sub: Vacuum science, cryogenics, and scientific instruments sub-groups were weakest; materials testing and environmental monitoring were relatively more resilient

  • Geographic mix: JDG derives approximately 70-75% of revenue from export markets (US, Europe, Asia-Pacific), with UK accounting for 25-30%; the organic decline was broadly distributed rather than concentrated in one geography [#A]

  • Sub: US academic spending softness and European research funding caution were cited contributing factors

  • Price contribution: JDG subsidiaries retained pricing discipline — list price increases of approximately 2-4% were realised across most subsidiaries, consistent with prior years [#A]

  • Market share: No direct market share data available from [C] sources; however, management commentary did not reference competitive losses, suggesting volume decline is demand-driven rather than share-driven [#A]

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

J-front verdict: Margins compressed modestly but held above 18% adjusted EBIT — decentralised model provided resilience, but negative operating leverage at lower volumes is a forward risk if revenue recovery is delayed [#J]

  • Gross margin: Gross margins held broadly stable in FY2024, approximately flat to slightly down (-0.5-1pp) versus FY2023; JDG's niche product positioning and pricing discipline insulated gross margins despite volume softness [#A]

  • Sub: FY2022/FY2023 gross margins were approximately 46-48%; FY2024 likely settled at 45-47% range — marginal compression, not structural deterioration

  • EBIT margin: Adjusted EBIT margin compressed from approximately 20.8-21% in FY2023 to approximately 18-19% in FY2024, a ~2pp contraction [#A]

  • Sub: This is within the historical volatility range for JDG (FY2019 margins were approximately 18-19%); not a new low, but it reverses two years of expansion

  • Margin direction: Trend is contracting — FY2022 ~19%, FY2023 ~21%, FY2024 ~18-19% — the expansion phase has reversed as revenue softened [#A]

  • Decomposition: Margin compression driven primarily by negative operating leverage (fixed overhead not proportionally reduced as organic revenues fell ~7-9%); pricing contributed positively; product mix was broadly neutral [#J]

  • Sub: JDG's decentralised model means subsidiaries retain independent cost structures — central overhead leverage is minimal, limiting both the upside on scale and the downside on deleverage

  • Operating leverage: Negative operating leverage observed — revenue fell ~5%, EBIT fell ~14-16%, implying approximately 3x earnings leverage on revenue movements in the downward direction [#J]

  • Sub: In the upward direction (FY2022-2023), EBIT growth exceeded revenue growth — the leverage is symmetric and a key feature of the JDG model

  • D&A trend: Depreciation and amortisation elevated by approximately £1-2m versus FY2023 due to acquired intangible amortisation from Geotek and the 2024 bolt-on; this is a structural drag that grows modestly with each acquisition [#A]

  • Sub: IFRS 3 amortisation adjustments are the primary reconciling item between reported and adjusted PBT — adjust quality is high, but the absolute D&A drag grows over time

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A and central overhead as % of revenue likely edged up approximately 0.5-1pp as revenues fell but central costs held flat; JDG's central headcount is deliberately minimal (~20-25 people at plc level) [#A]

  • One-off cost: No material restructuring charges disclosed; JDG's model does not pursue headcount restructuring in downturns — subsidiaries retain autonomy, including cost decisions [#A]

  • Sub: The absence of restructuring charges is philosophically consistent with JDG's culture but means fixed costs are not dynamically reduced when revenue falls — margin pressure is absorbed, not eliminated

  • Adjusted vs reported: Non-GAAP gap of approximately £2-3m (IFRS amortisation of acquired intangibles) — this is clean, predictable, and does not suggest earnings quality concerns [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: If FY2025 revenue recovery is weaker than consensus assumes (3-5% miss scenario), EBIT margins could compress further to 16-17%, implying adjusted EPS of 155-165p — at that level, the stock's valuation support weakens materially and the recovery thesis requires further time [#J]
