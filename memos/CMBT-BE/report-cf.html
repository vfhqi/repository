EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

CMB.TECH has undergone a transformative corporate reorganization following the Saverys family takeover of Euronav, consolidating executive control under Alexander and Ludovic Saverys while strengthening supervisory board oversight and initiating a corporate culture pivot toward green technology and fleet diversification.

The appointment of Alexander Saverys as Chief Executive Officer on November 22, 2023, marked a complete departure from the prior pure-play tanker management strategy, bringing over twenty years of shipping experience to lead the newly diversified platform.

External value-chain forces present a mixed picture, with Red Sea disruptions forcing costly detours around the Cape of Good Hope while environmental regulations accelerate fleet phase-out of older vessels, dry bulk enters its seasonal upcycle, and macroeconomic slowdown in China drags down commodity imports.

The net signal of change for CMB.TECH is decisively positive in the near term, driven by extraordinary tanker spot market conditions and successful fleet diversification, though burdened by structural capital intensity and elevated geopolitical risks.

Operating revenues reached USD 1,666 million in FY25, representing a 77.23% increase compared to USD 940 million in FY24, while clean EBITDA excluding book gains rose to USD 751 million in FY25, up 40.90% from USD 533 million.

The most recent quarter demonstrated explosive growth, with Q1 2026 revenues reaching USD 519.63 million, improving by 121.08% compared to USD 235.04 million in Q1 2025, while net profit reached USD 368.83 million representing a massive surge from USD 40.37 million in the prior year period.

However, this strength is heavily distorted by non-recurring capital gains from aggressive fleet rejuvenation, and the underlying operational cash generation remains highly exposed to volatile spot shipping cycles across both tanker and dry bulk segments.

The five to eight key forward drivers shaping CMB.TECH's earnings trajectory present a mixed plausibility profile with significant inter-dependencies.

Tanker segment spot rates represent approximately 45% of estimated EPS impact with HIGH plausibility and high confidence, dependent on geopolitical shipping routes and OPEC+ export cuts, while dry bulk spot rates account for roughly 30% impact with MEDIUM plausibility and medium confidence, tied to Chinese property and industrial commodity demand.

Management secured 81% of Q2 spot VLCC days at USD 182,731 per day and Suezmax Q2 bookings achieved 83% coverage at USD 122,147 per day, reflecting extreme geopolitical tailwinds that stabilize short-term cash flows.

The dry bulk integration expanded Bocimar's fleet to 119 modern vessels with Q2 bookings securing 80% of Newcastlemax days at USD 44,100 per day, outpacing seasonal historic benchmarks.

Strong driver dependency exists as tanker cash generation is fundamentally critical to fund the outstanding capital commitments of USD 1.50 billion, though the mathematical joint probability of achieving simultaneous multi-year tanker and dry bulk upcycles remains moderately low.

Recent delivery performance demonstrates strong consensus outperformance in headline metrics but reveals underlying execution volatility tied to integration complexity and spot market swings.

Unaudited Q1 2026 EBITDA reached USD 558.28 million, representing a substantial beat against the USD 279.70 million consensus estimate, though this financial beat was heavily supported by USD 267.35 million in capital gains from fleet rejuvenation sales.

Actual adjusted EPS missed prior consensus estimates during Q4 2025, coming in at USD 0.22 versus USD 0.37 expected, as higher-than-expected operating and administrative expenses during integration dragged down near-term earnings.

Strategic execution achieved scale objectives by integrating 89 modern dry bulk vessels from Golden Ocean to create a leading commodity shipping platform, with the dry bulk division now representing 55% of total fleet value, while management successfully sold ten older tankers during 2024 to recycle capital into eco-friendly solutions and crystallize significant profits.

Net cash from operating activities improved to USD 167.35 million from USD 33.44 million in Q1 2025, while net cash from investing activities was positive USD 204.41 million due to asset recycling sales, though financing cash outflows reached USD 324.55 million reflecting debt repayments and capital restructuring.

Forward guidance credibility places management firmly in the honest-tracker archetype, though the absence of formal EPS targets and discretionary dividend policy reduces visibility relative to pure-play peers.

CMB.TECH does not provide explicit quarterly or annual EPS guidance, choosing instead to publish spot booking updates and cash flow scenarios, and management operates as an honest-tracker archetype, delivering operational updates transparently without attempting to aggressively sandbag expectations.

A 10% downward variance in spot rates would lower projected FY26 EPS from USD 2.05 to USD 1.82, preserving a compelling 25%+ IRR case, while the outstanding capital commitment includes fully secured financing leaving only USD 216 million in unfunded requirements, and the transition to a discretionary payout policy provides an immediate cash buffer to absorb potential spot market downturns.

The base case models FY26 adjusted EPS at USD 2.05 reflecting current elevated tanker rates and moderate dry bulk demand, the bull case assumes prolonged Red Sea re-routing and peak asset values lifting projected adjusted EPS above USD 3.41, while the bear case models a sharp Chinese import collapse and rapid spot rate normalization dragging adjusted EPS down to USD 0.27.

Stated guidance is consistently delivered without aggressive sandbagging, and dividend payouts remain aligned with capital allocation priorities.

Sell-side positioning reflects cautious consensus with concentrated Hold ratings from key regional brokers, driven by concerns over capital intensity and dry bulk cyclicality despite near-term tanker tailwinds.

The broker pool contains a total of twelve named individual analysts providing active or recent coverage, with consensus recommendations across the historical database standing at 59% Buy, 38% Hold, and 3% Sell, though currently the active analyst pool is highly consolidated at Hold recommendations from both key regional research houses.

Analyst Jørgen Lian of DNB Carnegie maintains a Hold rating with a target price of USD 16.20, while analyst Quirijn Mulder of ING maintains a Hold rating with an adjusted target price of EUR 11.00.

Fundamental peer momentum ranking places CMB.TECH in a strong second position among maritime comparables, demonstrating superior revenue growth and near-term earnings revisions despite margin volatility and dividend visibility constraints.

CMB.TECH occupies a leading peer position following its massive dry bulk expansion outpacing peer averages in fleet scale, though earnings quality remains highly dependent on spot rates as heavy debt service offsets near-term cash generation of the fleet, and dividend visibility lags behind pure-play peers due to front-loaded capital intensity of the dual-fuel newbuilding program.

CMB.TECH has expanded its diversified fleet to 243 vessels establishing unparalleled scale, operating revenues surged by 121.08% to USD 519.63 million in the first quarter of 2026, strategic dry bulk consolidation integrated Golden Ocean's 89 dry bulk vessels shifting segment exposure, and Suezmax spot rates rose 121.91% to USD 91,849 per day.

Peer Okeanis achieved the top momentum rank with superior margins and strong consensus revisions, while Star Bulk ranked third with modest but steady growth, DHT Holdings placed fourth with defensive leverage but declining revenue, and Frontline ranked fifth with limited available metrics but strong historical cash returns.

Activist presence was historically material during the Euronav takeover transition but has been fully resolved, with no ongoing activist blocks currently threatening the Saverys family control structure.

Severe concentration of family power under the Saverys brothers could marginalize independent shareholder interests, creating material transaction conflicts.

FourWorld Capital Management filed claims to declare the entire CMB.TECH takeover and Euronav restructuring null and void, though the Belgian Market Court largely rejected all claims in September 2024, allowing the mandatory public takeover bid to secure 80.51% ownership and enabling CMB NV to control 61.59% of total voting shares.

The clearance of this litigation removed the primary structural overhang, though ongoing legal uncertainty from dissenting shareholder appraisals in Bermuda could still disrupt cash allocations.

The two to three most material plausible problem to monitor demanding close monitoring include front-loaded capital commitments colliding with cyclical market volatility, severe concentration of family control creating governance blind spots, and over-exposure to speculative green technology premiums with unclear regulatory timelines.

Outstanding capital commitments fell to USD 1.50 billion in January 2026 down from USD 2.20 billion, though the next 12 months require USD 1.20 billion in yard payments demonstrating highly intense near-term cash commitments, while own cash requirements for the outstanding yard program are limited to USD 216 million with remaining debt fully secured.

Unfunded yard commitments and heavy asset leverage could trigger covenant breaches if volatile dry bulk and tanker rates collapse simultaneously.

Bureau Veritas experts suggest dual-fuel vessels currently yield zero near-term return on investment, while green ammonia fuels carry a 38% cost premium over conventional heavy marine diesel fuel.

Slower global regulatory momentum could leave dual-fuel vessels unchartered, leaving CMB.TECH burdened with massive high-cost asset leverage.

A rapid geopolitical de-escalation in the Middle East could immediately reopen the Suez Canal, releasing up to 10% of global vessel capacity and collapsing spot TCE rates below cash breakeven.
