Section 1 — Stage Identification and History

Stage Framework

Minervini's four-stage model: Stage 1 (neglect/accumulation), Stage 2 (advancing/markup), Stage 3 (topping/distribution), Stage 4 (declining/markdown). The goal is to own stocks in Stage 2 and avoid everything else.

COM-IT Stage History

Stage 4 / Late Stage 1 (pre-Apr 2025). The stock traded in the €25-30 range. The 200DMA was flat to declining. Volume was negligible. The stock was invisible to momentum screens. This was the neglect phase.

Stage 1 to Stage 2 transition (~Apr-May 2025). Price broke above the 200DMA decisively. The 200DMA began turning upward. The 50DMA crossed above the 200DMA (golden cross). Volume expanded on the breakout. This marked the beginning of the current advance.

Stage 2 — Current (May 2025 to present, ~12 months). The stock advanced from ~€27 to a 52W high of €52.40. That is a 94% gain from the Stage 2 entry point. The 200DMA has been rising for 9 consecutive months. Four distinct bases have formed during the advance, each producing a successful breakout until the current pullback.

Percentage advance analysis. A 94% Stage 2 advance is substantial for an industrial micro-cap. Minervini's research shows the median Stage 2 advance for big winners is 100-200%, but for non-tech industrials the range is narrower. A 94% move with four bases suggests the stock is in the later innings of this particular Stage 2 run. That does not mean the advance is over — it means the risk/reward of each incremental entry is worse than it was at base 1 or 2.

Current position within Stage 2. The stock is classified as "Capital" stage in the filter system. All 11 MM99 criteria pass. The 200DMA is rising. The 150DMA is rising. Price is above both. This is unambiguously Stage 2. However, the declining 5D, 10D, and flat 50D signal deceleration within the stage. The stock is in Stage 2 but not in the strongest part of Stage 2.

What would signal a Stage 3 transition?

Three things to watch: (a) the 50DMA rolls over and begins declining, (b) price breaks below the 50DMA on expanding volume and fails to reclaim it, (c) the 200DMA flattens and price begins oscillating around it. None of these have occurred yet. The 50DMA is flat, not declining. Price is above the 200DMA by 11.2%. The stock is not in Stage 3. But the flat 50DMA is the earliest possible warning that the character of the advance is changing.

Ideal Entry Zone

There is no entry signal now. The conditions for an entry would be:

  1. 50DMA turns higher. This is the prerequisite. No entry while the 50D is declining.
  2. Price reclaims the 100DMA (€45.82) on expanding volume. This would confirm the pullback is resolving.
  3. A VCP or tight consolidation forms near the 50DMA. This would create a defined risk point (below the consolidation low).
  4. Volume expands on the breakout above the consolidation. Confirmation of demand.

If all four occur, the stock would present an Uptrend Retest entry with the stop below the 50DMA or the consolidation low. Risk per share would be approximately €2-3 (4-7% of price). Given the liquidity constraint, the position would need to be small.

Alternatively, if the stock pulls back to the 150DMA (€42.72) and holds on declining volume, then reverses on expanding volume, that would be a deeper-value entry with a wider stop (below the 150DMA or 200DMA) but a better risk/reward setup.

Bullish shift: 50DMA turns higher, price reclaims 100DMA and then the prior swing high (€46.70 from pullback monitor), volume expands. This would suggest the consolidation is a base 5 forming, and the advance has more legs.

Bearish shift: Price breaks below the 50DMA (€44.63) on volume, fails to reclaim it within 1-2 weeks, 50DMA accelerates downward, 150DMA is tested. This would suggest Stage 2 is ending and a transition to Stage 3 is underway.

Neutral continuation: Price oscillates between the 50DMA and the 100DMA for several more weeks. The 50DMA flattens but does not roll over. Volume contracts further. This would extend the consolidation without resolving it — a waiting game.
