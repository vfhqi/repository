Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 8 findings

1.
Unrivaled niche positioning: The company operates as the global leader in white cement with 3.3 million tonnes of capacity, accounting for 25% of traded flows
2.
Predictable investment thesis: The core thesis relies on structural regional construction recoveries, the low-carbon roll-out of FUTURECEM, and the strategic reallocation of a huge cash pile

Section 3 – What the company does 14 findings

Section summary
  1. The company leverages a unique global oligopoly in white cement to extract premium margins, supported by vertically integrated regional grey cement and concrete platforms

Global Product and Segment Footprint

1.
Crown jewel product: White cement is a specialty product with high aesthetic and structural properties, allowing the group to maintain a massive 3.3 million tonne capacity
2.
Vertically integrated networks: The group operates integrated operations in Denmark, Belgium, and Turkey, controlling the entire supply chain from limestone quarries to ready-mixed concrete plants
3.
Regional revenue composition: The largest regional market is the Nordic and Baltic region with EUR 736.80 million in 2025 revenue, followed by Turkey at EUR 314.80 million
4.
Downstream concrete assets: Ready-mixed concrete sales reached 4.3 million cubic metres in 2025, operating primarily in the Scandinavian region and Belgium
5.
Local quarry networks: Aggregate sales reached 10.4 million tonnes in 2025, supported by the ownership of 34 quarries across Europe and North America
6.
Turkey waste integration: The Turkish operations include industrial waste management subsidiary Sureko, which processes waste into alternative fuels to reduce reliance on imported petcoke

Economics and Competitive Advantages

7.
Premium oligopolistic pricing: The white cement market operates as a niche global oligopoly, protecting Cementir's average selling prices from commodity grey cement price wars
8.
Natural geographic monopolies: Cementir is the sole producer of cement in Denmark and operates key import terminals across major global trade routes, blocking new competitors
9.
High switching costs: Customer relationships in the specialty white precast and architectural segments are highly sticky, as builders face structural risks when changing cement suppliers
10.
Raw material ownership: Long-life limestone quarries located adjacent to its primary kilns deny competitors access to essential high-quality raw inputs
11.
Low customer concentration: The group serves a highly fragmented base of over several thousand builders, contractors, and local distributors, reducing counterparty pricing power
12.
Strategic trade network: A global trading setup operated through Spartan Hive allows Cementir to dynamically redirect clinker exports to markets with the highest margins
13.
Other side of the story: Prolonged regional construction downturns and localized ready-mix pricing pressure in competitive markets like Sweden can temporarily erode downstream segment margins

Section 4 – Financial profile and performance 14 findings

Section summary
  1. Multi-year profitability shows resilient EBITDA margins and exceptional returns on capital, supported by a rapid transition to a net cash position

Profitable Growth and Cash Dynamics

1.
Margins expansion trajectory: EBITDA margin improved from 23.7% in 2024 to 26.8% in 2025, driven by stable pricing and lower fuel expenses
2.
Exceptional return efficiency: Return on capital employed increased by 280 basis points to 19.5% in 2025, representing an industry-leading return level
3.
Organic capital reinvestment: The group prioritizes upgrading existing assets, as seen in the upgrade of Kiln 4 in Belgium to boost alternative fuel usage to 70%
4.
Consistent cash generation: Operating cash flows reached EUR 336.95 million in 2025, demonstrating excellent cash conversion from underlying earnings
5.
Reliable volume growth: Clinker and cement volumes rose 3.1% to 11.05 million tonnes in 2025, helping offset cyclical European housing weakness
6.
Under-leveraged capital structure: The company has built a net cash balance of EUR 465.10 million by 2025, representing a complete deleveraging

Balance Sheet and Capital Allocation

7.
Strategic asset pruning: Management actively optimizes its footprint, completing the sale of Kars Cimento in Turkey for EUR 51.00 million on December 1, 2025
8.
IFRS lease obligations: Net cash calculations include lease liabilities of EUR 71.70 million in 2025, down from EUR 90.80 million in 2024
9.
Solid dividend policy: The board increased the dividend per share by 7.1% to EUR 0.30 in 2025, reflecting robust underlying profitability
10.
Conservative guidance history: Management consistently issues prudent forecasts, beating its initial 2025 EBITDA projection of EUR 415.00 million with EUR 439.50 million reported
11.
Recent Q1 slowdown: Sales revenue fell 7.1% in Q1 2026 to EUR 344.07 million, impacted by the coldest European winter in 20 years
12.
Confirmed annual target: Management reconfirmed its full-year 2026 guidance, targeting EUR 1.70 billion in revenue and EBITDA of EUR 400-420 million
13.
Other side of the story: The persistent application of IAS 29 hyperinflation adjustments in Turkey introduces substantial accounting volatility, making reported EBIT figures less representative of cash generation

Section 5 – The possible investment case 10 findings

Section summary
  1. The investment case rests on steady volume recoveries, high-margin low-carbon product rollouts, and capital reallocation from a massive net cash buffer

Forward Earnings Drivers

1.
Nordic housing rebound: A structural recovery in Nordic and Baltic residential construction is expected from 2027, reversing a multi-year stagnation caused by high interest rates
2.
Carbon transition premium: The commercial rollout of low-carbon FUTURECEM cement is expected to reach 51% of European sales by 2030, commanding high pricing
3.
Egyptian export expansion: Reactivating the second kiln line at El Arish in Egypt has unlocked a 68% surge in high-margin white cement exports
4.
Active M&A deployment: Deploying its EUR 465.10 million cash buffer, Cementir signed a EUR 120.00 million agreement to acquire Nymølle Stenindustrier in March 2026
5.
Scaled operational savings: The group targets EUR 30.00 million in variable and fixed cost reductions under its "Maintenance 4.0" digital process standardisation
6.
White cement expansion: Global demand for premium white cement is projected to expand across the US and Southeast Asia, where Cementir holds market-leading shares

Bull Case Conditions

7.
Independent growth drivers: The bull case does not require all regions to fire together, as drivers are structurally independent, meaning the 2028 revenue target of EUR 1.95 billion is highly plausible
8.
Massive cash redeployment: The ultimate bull case relies on successfully redeploying its projected EUR 800.00 million in net cash by 2028 into high-ROIC bolt-on acquisitions
9.
Other side of the story: High interest rates could structurally suppress European construction demand for another 24 months, stalling the volume recoveries necessary to absorb rising carbon taxes

Deeply protected downside: The group's massive net cash balance of EUR 465.10 million represents a large percentage of its market cap, offering strong downside protection
5.
Depressed market expectations: The current stock price of EUR 18.78 reflects overly pessimistic expectations regarding Turkey's inflation and European housing demand
6.
Sustainable cash yield: Solid operating cash flow ensures a dependable, high free cash flow yield that fully supports future dividend growth

Other side of the story: The steep multiple discount could remain permanently entrenched if the Caltagirone family's 70% shareholding continues to deter institutional investors due to low free float liquidity

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 9 findings

Section summary
  1. Regulatory carbon costs and FX volatility in emerging markets represent the primary threats to Cementir's earnings trajectory

Key Structural Risks

1.
Carbon allowance reduction: The tightening of EU ETS free CO2 allowances will cause a shortage of 130,000 tonnes of carbon credits annually, starting in 2027
2.
Emerging market currencies: Deep devaluations of the Turkish Lira and Egyptian Pound have previously hit consolidated revenues by EUR 97.00 million, representing a persistent headwind
3.
Decarbonisation execution delay: Reaching 2030 emissions targets depends on the Danish ACCSION carbon capture project, which relies entirely on third-party pipeline and storage infrastructure
4.
Technical carbon penalties: If the ACCSION project is delayed, Cementir faces a potential loss of its EUR 100.00 million investment and massive ETS carbon penalty exposure
5.
Severe construction cyclicality: The grey cement and concrete segments are structurally tied to highly cyclical regional interest rates and residential construction housing starts
6.
Under-reformed emerging markets: Despite the sale of Kars Cimento, Turkey still represents a highly volatile market with elevated country-specific political and hyperinflationary risks
7.
False friend caution: Temporary volume spikes in post-disaster areas can sometimes mislead investors if they are accompanied by price controls that compress underlying profit margins
8.
Other side of the story: Government backing is strong, as demonstrated by the Danish Energy Agency's positive subsidy award for ACCSION, which significantly reduces execution risk

Strongest investment thesis: Cementir combines an absolute global oligopoly in high-margin white cement with an unleveraged, cash-rich balance sheet returning an exceptional 19.5% return on capital
2.
Other side of the story: Sceptics will argue that the Caltagirone family's tight control and resulting low stock liquidity could lock the company in a permanent small-cap valuation discount
