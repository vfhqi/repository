Section summary

Core Business + Operational Structure

1.
Organisational structure: The company manages its operations as a single, unified operating segment focused on Integrated Site Management across Italy. Inwit owns and manages a macro grid of approximately 26,000 towers, maintaining an average density of one tower every 3 kilometres across Italy. The macro tower footprint is geographically split, with 65.00% classified as raw-land (greenfield pylon) sites and 35.00% located as rooftop installations. The asset base is further distributed by municipality size, with 25.00% in small municipalities under 5,000 residents and 40.00% in urban centers over 35,000 residents. The micro-grid business unit supports densification with 12,000 micro-coverage systems, including Distributed Antenna Systems (DAS) and small cells in 800 indoor locations. Revenue model – contract-backed : Revenue is generated via long-term hosting fees paid by Mobile Network Operators (MNOs) and Other Than Mobile Operators (OTMOs) to place equipment on sites. Revenues are highly predictable, governed by long-term Master Service Agreements (MSAs) that are structurally protected by inflation-indexing mechanisms. Inwit’s primary anchor contracts are tied to its co-founders, TIM and Vodafone Italia, which was acquired by Swisscom and integrated with Fastweb
