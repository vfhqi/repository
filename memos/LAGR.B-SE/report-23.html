KEY FINDINGS (5-10 bullets — written last, placed first)

Purpose: The sharpest version of the thematic's conclusions. Each bullet is 1-2 sentences. J-front: judgement leads. These are not summaries of the sections below — they are the portfolio-actionable punchlines.

Format in live output: - [Verdict word] — claim + supporting evidence in one sentence - [Verdict word] — what's surprising relative to consensus - [Verdict word] — the most important at-risk signal - [Verdict word] — the single most important beneficiary signal - [Verdict word] — what the historical parallel tells us about timing

§1. DEFINITION (50-100 words)

Purpose: Refine the submitted {THEMATIC_DEFINITION}. State what the thematic IS (2-3 sentences), identify the primary transmission mechanism to European corporate earnings, specify the time horizon (6-month / 1-year / multi-year), and assign a confidence level in persistence (High / Medium / Low).

Analytical standard: The definition must be falsifiable. A good definition names the mechanism precisely enough that a student could test whether it is operating by looking at public data. "Inflation" is not a thematic definition. "Persistent above-CPI services wage inflation compressing labour-intensive European consumer and retail sector operating margins through fixed-contract pricing structures" is.

What will be challenged: If {THEMATIC_DEFINITION} is too broad, too narrow, or conflates first-order and second-order effects, the Researcher will say so explicitly and offer a revised framing.

§2. BENEFICIARY SUMMARY (30-60 words)

Purpose: The elevator pitch for who wins. One to two sentences. Written for a PM who has 30 seconds.

Format in live output: Direct assertion of the beneficiary archetype, the mechanism, and the magnitude of benefit. No hedging. No "could potentially." A claim with a number or a directional anchor.

§3. AT-RISK SUMMARY (30-60 words)

Purpose: The elevator pitch for who loses. Same format discipline as §2.

Format in live output: Names the at-risk archetype, the attack vector, and the earnings consequence. Written to trigger recognition — the PM should immediately think of specific names in the pipeline.

§4. BENEFICIARY ATTRIBUTES (300-600 words)

Purpose: The scoring engine. Every attribute must be matchable to an individual stock by an APM. "Companies with pricing power" fails the test. "Companies with contractual price escalators tied to CPI or input cost indices, renegotiated annually or more frequently, and representing >60% of revenue" passes.

Structure per attribute:

Field Content
Attribute name One line, specific
Transmission mechanism Input → margin/revenue → EPS, 1-2 sentences
European examples 2-3 listed companies, $5-50bn market cap where possible
Importance rating Critical / Important / Supportive

Four categories required:

Revenue/Demand Attributes — demand uplift, pricing power, new market creation, volume acceleration Cost/Margin Attributes — input cost advantage, operating leverage, fixed-cost base benefit Strategic/Competitive Attributes — regulatory tailwind, market share gain opportunity, M&A optionality Financial Attributes — balance sheet strength, cash conversion, dividend resilience, re-rating potential

Analytical standard: Each attribute must trace a complete chain from the thematic to an EPS line. Where the chain is probabilistic, the Researcher will state the break point and its likelihood. Where a European company has disclosed specific sensitivity data (e.g., "every 1% move in X costs €Ym of EBITDA"), that data will be cited.

Historical parallel discipline: Each category will carry at least one historical parallel (2008, 2014, 2020, 2022 are the most commonly relevant). The parallel must name what worked, not just that something happened.

§5. BENEFICIARY PROBABLE SETUPS (200-400 words)

Purpose: Stock-level investment pattern recognition for beneficiaries. This section answers: what does the ideal beneficiary look like at entry, and how does the thesis crystallise?

Required components:

  • Entry profile: valuation anchor (absolute and relative), momentum condition, earnings trajectory requirement
  • Catalyst map: what specific events cause the market to recognise the beneficiary status (earnings beat, guidance raise, contract announcement, regulatory decision, macro data point)
  • Risks to beneficiaries: over-earning (cycle peak), valuation stretch (consensus already reflects the thematic), crowding (every active manager already long)
  • Standard setup mapping: where natural, the Researcher will identify which of the standard investment setups this resembles (demand-driven earnings acceleration, margin expansion from input cost advantage, defensive premium re-rating, FCF inflection)

Analytical standard: The setup description must be specific enough that a stock screener could filter for it. "Cheap quality" is not a setup. "Trailing EV/EBITDA <12x, revenue growth accelerating from <5% to >10% over the prior two quarters, and consensus EPS estimates revised up in each of the last three months" is a setup.

§6. AT-RISK ATTRIBUTES (300-600 words)

Purpose: The mirror of §4. Same structure, same specificity standard, same four categories.

Format: Identical table structure. Each attribute names the vulnerability, the attack vector to EPS, two to three European examples, and an importance rating.

Critical discipline: At-risk ≠ cyclical. The thematic must specifically attack this company's earning power through an identifiable channel. A company that is merely sensitive to macro conditions is not at-risk in the thematic sense unless the thematic specifically amplifies that sensitivity.

Four categories required:

Revenue/Demand — volume loss, pricing pressure, demand destruction, substitution risk Cost/Margin — input cost escalation, margin compression, operating deleverage Strategic/Competitive — regulatory headwind, competitive displacement, stranded asset risk Financial — balance sheet stress, dividend vulnerability, refinancing risk at adverse terms

§7. AT-RISK PROBABLE SETUPS (200-400 words)

Purpose: Stock-level pattern recognition for at-risk names. Answers: what does the typical at-risk stock look like, what triggers crystallise the thesis, and what separates temporary from structural vulnerability?

Required components:

  • Earnings vulnerability profile: what the consensus model assumes that the thematic will disprove
  • Trigger map: specific events that crystallise the at-risk thesis (earnings miss, guidance cut, margin compression commentary, covenant breach, rating action)
  • Temporary vs structural distinction: the most important judgement call in at-risk analysis — a temporarily pressured company can be a buy at the right price; a structurally at-risk company is a short or avoid regardless of valuation
  • Standard setup mapping: earnings deterioration, multiple compression, liquidity trap, value trap (appears cheap because EPS denominator is inflated)

Analytical standard: The temporary vs structural distinction must be answered with a specific test. "The company is structurally at-risk if X happens; it is temporarily at-risk if Y happens instead." The test must be observable in reported data within 12-18 months.
