Section 2 – The 80/20 (bottom line up front) 6 findings

Section summary
  1. Lundin Mining offers premium pure-play copper exposure with peer-leading organic growth, though near-term capital demands and full valuations require a highly disciplined approach.
1.
Streamlined asset base: Pure-play copper vehicle with ~85% of revenues from copper and 10% from gold following divestment of Eagle mine
2.
Transformational growth case: Investment thesis centers on development of the massive Vicuña District in partnership with BHP, targeting peak attributable production of over 250,000 tonnes of copper annually
3.
Demanding market valuation: Trades near its 10-year high at 2.90x book value versus historical average of 1.20x
4.
Substantial execution risk: Core risk involves potential capex overruns at the massive $7.1bn Josemaria project, situated at high altitude with complex regional logistics
5.
Balanced preliminary verdict: Deeper research highly warranted given the world-class Vicuña District optionality, though investors must remain cautious regarding capital allocation and entry valuations

Section 3 – What the company does 11 findings

Section summary
  1. Lundin's streamlined South American portfolio places it in a highly profitable, pure-play copper position to capture the global electrification trend.
1.
Copper pure-play vehicle: ~85% of total revenues from copper and 10% from gold, following disposal of US nickel operations
2.
Dominant Chilean assets: Operating mines in Chile (Candelaria and Caserones) represent the bedrock – jointly contributing 77% of Q2 2025 continuing revenues
3.
Stellar Caserones performance: Caserones has emerged as a cash engine, generating 44% of total company revenues in Q1 2026 on robust grade performance
4.
Steady Candelaria baseline: Candelaria produced 145,471 tonnes of copper in FY2025, operating with high mill availability and secured by extended environmental permit to 2040
5.
Gold-rich Chapada mine: Chapada in Brazil operates as a highly cost-efficient open-pit mine with low C1 costs of $0.45/lb supported by robust gold by-product credits. Unrivaled Vicuña scale: Vicuña JV aims to develop a giant multi-generational district with 70+ year mine life, capable of producing over 500,000 tonnes of copper annually
6.
Advantaged cost position: Q1 2026 consolidated C1 costs of $1.66/lb trending well below FY2026 guidance range of $1.90–$2.10/lb
7.
Customer diversification strength: Concentrates and cathodes sold via long-term contracts to creditworthy smelters in Japan, China, and Europe – minimal customer concentration risk
8.
Formidable infrastructure moat: Candelaria's desalination plant possesses 30%–40% excess capacity to support regional development
9.
First-mover consolidating advantage: Uniquely positioned as a regional consolidator in the Vicuña District, supported by its deep corporate relationship with 50% partner BHP
10.
Other side of the story: Heavy geographic concentration in Chile and Argentina exposes earnings to escalating South American resource nationalism and environmental permitting hurdles

Section 5 – The possible investment case 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Lundin's investment case strengthens dramatically due to the world-class organic growth optionality of the Vicuña copper district.
1.
Josemaria Stage 1 timeline: The Josemaria project in Argentina heading toward a final investment decision in 4Q26, targeting attributable Stage 1 capacity of ~65,000 tonnes of copper annually
2.
De-risked execution plan: 50:50 JV partnership with BHP significantly de-risks construction and funding; detailed engineering guided to reach 40% prior to final sanctioning
3.
Highly accretive Caserones increase: Agreement to purchase additional 5% interest in Caserones for $215m consolidates ownership to 75% and immediately expands attributable copper volumes
4.
Synergistic Los Helados deposit: Caserones transaction includes a 30.9% stake in nearby Los Helados deposit, providing long-term feed synergy with Caserones' operating mill. Saúva gold-rich deposit: Chapada's Saúva deposit tracking toward a 2H2026 sanctioning decision – expected to add 10,000–15,000 tonnes copper and 35,000–45,000 ounces gold annually
5.
Independent execution risks: Lundin's brownfield drivers are independent – Saúva and Candelaria can deliver robust earnings growth even if Josemaria is delayed
6.
Favorable cost curves: Combined expansion projects likely to position Lundin in the first cost quartile globally
7.
Other side of the story: Significant capital overrun risks at Josemaria – a modeled $2bn capex escalation would trigger severe project IRR compression

Premium multiple versus history: Trades at forward 2026 EV/EBITDA of approximately 10.0x versus 10-year historical average of 5.70x
2.
Elevated book value premium: Trades at 2.90x book versus 10-year historical average of 1.20x
3.

Other side of the story: A severe global economic downturn depressing copper prices to $4.00/lb would trigger a 25% downward SOTP re-rating across core South American assets

Section 7 – Key risks and the other side 8 findings

Section summary
  1. Lundin's near-term operational risk is moderate but mitigated by strong capital backing and world-class asset quality.
1.
Significant water stress risk: Core assets in Chile and Argentina situated in high-baseline water stress areas – exposed to stringent regulatory water-use restrictions
2.
Argentina political instability: Operating Josemaria project exposes Lundin to Argentina's volatile fiscal environment; tax stability negotiations ongoing
3.
Geotechnical wall failures: Past pit wall failures at Candelaria have disrupted mine sequencing and production
4.
Severe local road blockades: Recent provincial access disputes in La Rioja, Argentina caused temporary legal blockades – illustrating potential regional transport risks for the Vicuña project
5.
Arsenic penalty risk: Filo del Sol's copper-gold deposits exhibit high arsenic concentrations, which may trigger severe smelter penalty charges
6.
False friend signal: Rising gold prices act as a false friend by masking underlying copper grade degradation or cost inflation across core Chilean assets
7.
Other side of the story: Partnership with BHP significantly mitigates capital risks – BHP provides immense balance sheet capacity and deep technical expertise for project execution
