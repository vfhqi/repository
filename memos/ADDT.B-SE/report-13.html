  • FY current — revenue: Management guided "continued organic growth" for FY26 (Apr 25–Mar 26), without stating a precise percentage; context of recent results implies an organic range of ~5–9% consistent with prior-cycle framing [#J]
  • Sub: The absence of a point estimate is structural, not episodic; Addtech has never issued a formal revenue point-estimate for any fiscal year in its public record [#I]
  • FY current — EBITA: EBITA margin guided to be "maintained or improved" versus FY25's delivered 13.2%; implying a ≥13.2% floor with upside optionality framed as "operating leverage from volume" [#J]
  • Sub: FY25 EBITA margin of 13.2% compares favourably to the 3Y average of 12.7%, meaning the "maintained" baseline is already elevated vs trend [#A]
  • FY current — EPS: No explicit EPS guidance issued; management has never provided EPS point guidance in any earnings call or investor day presentation reviewed in the AlphaSense corpus [#I]
  • Sub: EPS trajectory is implicitly communicated via EBITA margin + acquisition cadence + capital structure commentary, not a stated number [#A]
  • FY current — FCF: Management states a target of cash conversion ≥85% of EBITA over rolling 3-year periods; no single-year FCF commitment given [#I]
  • Sub: L3Y actual cash conversion has averaged ~88%, modestly above the stated floor but with year-to-year volatility of ±7pp [#A]

  • Medium-term — N3Y: Addtech's CMD-stated ambitions target doubling of EBITA over a business cycle (~7 years, implying ~10% annual EBITA CAGR); no explicit 3-year revenue or EPS figure issued [#J]
  • Sub: The doubling-EBITA target is the closest the company comes to medium-term quantitative guidance; it has been stated consistently since at least FY19 [#I]
  • Medium-term — M&A: Acquisition ambition is "4–8 acquisitions per year, average size SEK 100–400m revenue"; at historical multiples of 6–8× EBITA, this implies SEK 800m–2.4bn deployed annually in M&A [#A]

§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Sandbagger signal: Addtech's structural guidance posture is consistent with sandbagging; "continued growth" framing at the low end of achievable range has historically understated organic delivery in 3 of 4 years [#J]
  • Beat consistency — organic: Organic EBITA beats consensus in most periods; management's refusal to give a point estimate means the "beat" is always versus sell-side reconstruction, but the directionality is consistently positive [#A]
  • Magnitude — beat: Typical beat magnitude is modest (+30–70bps margin, +1–3pp organic revenue) in normal years; FY22 was an outlier at +8–11pp organic [#A]
  • Sub: Modest beat magnitude is consistent with genuine sandbagging discipline rather than lucky timing; management is not building in heroic cushions [#I]
  • Miss consistency — revenue: The one clear miss (FY24) was exogenous; destocking at industrial customers is not a management forecast error but a demand-environment error [#J]
  • Sub: Even in FY24, EBITA margin was missed by only 10bps, suggesting cost discipline is reliable even when top-line disappoints [#A]
  • Honest tracker — margin: EBITA margin guidance is more honest-tracker than sandbagger; the "maintained or improved" language is a real floor, not a cushion, given FY24 nearly breached it [#J]
  • Pattern shift: No structural shift in guidance posture detected over the L5Y corpus; management has consistently used the same vague but directionally-conservative language since at least FY19 [#I]
  • Improving accuracy: Not applicable as a trend; accuracy is cyclically-driven rather than structurally improving (high-cycle beats big, low-cycle misses modestly) [#J]
  • Mixed pattern — M&A contribution: M&A-driven EBITA contributions are not guided at all; the "pattern" is therefore incomplete — one cannot assess sandbagging vs over-promising on the 35–45% of EBITA that comes from M&A [#I]
  • Sub: This is the structural limitation of Addtech's guidance framework; the M&A engine is the most powerful value driver and the least disclosed [#J]
  • Peer comparison — Indutrade: Indutrade (closest Swedish serial-acquirer peer) issues similarly vague directional guidance; Addtech's organic beat frequency at 3/4 fiscal years is comparable to Indutrade's L4Y record of 3/4 organic beats [#A]

Sceptical bullet: The bearish read on the guidance pattern is that management's sandbagging advantage may be eroding; as Addtech's scale increases and Swedish/German industrial PMI remains subdued in FY26, the organic revenue cushion that generated historical beats narrows, and "maintained" margin guidance shifts from conservative to aspirational [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

Management's "maintained or improved" EBITA margin and "continued organic growth" stance rest on several embedded assumptions that are not explicitly stated but reconstructable from results commentary and historical financials.

  • Revenue assumption — market: Guidance implicitly assumes Swedish and Northern European industrial capex and automation markets grow at ~3–5% in underlying volume terms; consistent with long-run trend but above CY2023–2024 subdued levels [#J]
  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Addtech's niche industrial distribution model embeds ~1–2% annual price increases via supplier pass-through and value-add pricing; this is not explicitly guided but is embedded in organic growth assumptions [#A]
  • Sub: Pricing power has held across L4Y (no year showed negative pricing); but volume is the swing variable [#I]
  • Revenue assumption — M&A: Guidance implicitly assumes 4–8 acquisitions annually at 6–8× EBITA, contributing ~3–5% additional annual revenue growth; management explicitly declines to forecast this [#I]
  • Sub: At 6–8× EBITA entry multiples, each SEK 100m EBITA acquisition costs SEK 600–800m; 4–8 deals = SEK 2.4–6.4bn deployed annually [#A]
  • Margin assumption — cost: EBITA margin guidance assumes personnel costs grow broadly in line with revenue (no significant operating deleverage); Swedish wage inflation at ~3–4% in FY25 creates a ~0.3–0.5pp headwind at flat volumes [#A]
  • Sub: Management's "maintained" margin language implicitly assumes pricing/volume can offset wage inflation; this is not a given if organic volumes soften [#J]
  • Margin assumption — leverage: Operating leverage from volume growth of ~7% would add ~30–50bps EBITA margin; this is the primary mechanism for "improved" vs "maintained" [#A]
  • Margin assumption — pricing power: Embedded assumption of ~1–2% price increases holding through FY26; challenged if European industrial customers push back on price as capex budgets tighten [#J]
  • EPS assumption — tax: Effective tax rate guided implicitly at ~22–23% (FY25: 22.7%); stable Swedish corporate tax regime makes this assumption low-risk [#I]
  • EPS assumption — share count: No share buybacks guided; dilution from employee share programs ~0.3% annually; EPS arithmetic assumes flat-to-modest dilution [#A]
  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt/EBITA of ~1.5–2.0× post-M&A assumed; above 2.5× would start constraining deal pace [#I]
  • Sub: FY25 year-end net debt/EBITA estimated at ~1.6×; headroom exists for current guidance period [#A]
  • Macro assumption — GDP: Guidance implicitly consistent with Eurozone GDP growth of ~1.0–1.5% in CY25–26; below this threshold, industrial volumes likely drop below organic growth guidance [#J]
  • Macro assumption — currency: Addtech reports in SEK; ~30% of revenues from outside Scandinavia (Germany, UK, Netherlands); a 5% SEK appreciation cuts reported revenue by ~1.5% [#A]
  • Macro assumption — interest rates: M&A assumption depends on acquisition financing at ~3–4% cost of debt; if ECB/Riksbank rate path shifts materially, deal economics at 6–8× EBITA tighten [#J]
