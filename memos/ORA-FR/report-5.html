§7 — Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

J-front: Orange OMEA is executing well on four growth pillars — mobile data, fixed broadband, Orange Money, B2B — with the Max It super app as a fifth digital-services leg. Execution quality is above average for an African telco incumbent, though Orange Money competitive disruption is a genuine headwind requiring attention. [#J]

Mobile Data + Network

  • 4G/5G rollout: Targeting >40m additional 4G/5G users by 2028; mobile data revenue +19.4% in Q1 2026. [#I]
  • Rural sites: 15,000 new fully solar-powered sites over 3 years — locking in rural subscribers before competition arrives. [#I] ⚡
  • [AS·Earnings Presentation]: 5G spectrum secured in Egypt (2.6 GHz, €159m, major urban centres within 5 years), Morocco (700MHz + 3.5GHz, 74% population coverage by 2030), Ghana, and Jordan. Sonatel launched satellite internet offering to pre-empt Starlink entry in Senegal; Guinea's first national data centre initiated (B2B pivot).

Infrastructure Sharing JVs

  • [AS·ARS]: Orange-MTN continent-wide active infrastructure sharing JV — reduces duplicate capex, accelerates rural coverage without ROIC destruction. Orange-Vodacom DRC joint venture — shared build-and-operate of next-generation infrastructure in DRC (100m+ population, one of Africa's highest-growth markets). These JVs reduce capital intensity and improve ROIC trajectory across the OMEA footprint. [#I]

Orange Money / Fintech

  • Scale: 93.7m monthly active users (+14.1% YoY) across 16 countries. [AS·ARS]: Transaction volumes >€160bn in 2024. [#I]
  • Revenue deceleration: Q1 2026 +15.7%, down from FY2024 +20.4%. [AS·ARS]: Wave Mobile Money deployed zero-fee transfer model in Senegal and Ivory Coast; Orange responded by repricing to flat 1% fee — maintained user base but compressed revenue per user. This is structural competitive disruption, not cyclical. [#J]
  • "Max It" counter-strategy: Pivoting from P2P transfer fees toward savings, insurance, credit, and merchant payment cross-sell to rebuild ARPU. [AS·ARS]: Orange retains advantage via existing GSM subscriber base for fintech cross-selling vs bank-led models; but "Max it" pivot execution is unproven and monetisation per user has declined. [#A]
  • JUMO partnership: Orange Money Group x JUMO extending into micro-credit for unbanked populations — structural TAM expansion beyond payments. [#I]
  • Competitive position: Orange Money leads in Francophone West Africa (Senegal, Ivory Coast, Cameroon, Mali) — geographic advantage vs pan-African competitors. [#A]
  • Sceptical bullet: ❌ Orange Money is the most important fintech question for the OMEA thesis. If growth drops below 10% within two quarters, the moat narrative weakens materially and OMEA EBITDAaL growth guidance becomes difficult to sustain without pricing action elsewhere.

Fixed Broadband - Growth: +14.5% in Q1 2026; +19.1% in FY2025. Fibre base to be doubled in OMEA by 2028. [#I]

  • Significance: High-ARPU, low-churn product; penetration in OMEA still in single digits across most markets — long runways. [#A]

Max It Super App

  • Current scale: ~25m users across 16 OMEA markets. Target 75m by 2028 — 3x in 2 years. [#I]
  • [AS·ARS]: Moving from P2P fees toward ecosystem cross-sell (savings, insurance, credit, merchant payments). Risk: execution unproven; M-Pesa/Safaricom benchmark has a 20-year head start in East Africa. [#A]

B2B / Orange Business in Africa

  • Growth: +8.7% in Q1 2026. [AS·ARS]: Submarine cable network and international backbone are structural differentiation for corporate connectivity — pure-play consumer mobile operators cannot replicate. Enterprise revenues are more resilient to retail fintech disruption. [#I]

Country Highlights

  • 11 of 18 OMEA countries posted double-digit growth Q1 2026 — geographic breadth unusual; not a Nigeria-or-nothing story. [#I] ⚡
  • Sonatel (Senegal): IFC partnership ($75m equivalent for rural 4G); dominant position; OMEA's highest-quality franchise. [#I]
  • [AS·Earnings Presentation]: Morocco (highest-margin North African market), Egypt (top-3 operator, strong enterprise), Jordan (5G active, MEA anchor). [#I]

Conglomerate Discount

  • [AS·ARS]: Orange OMEA consistently delivers pure-play-quality growth (12.7%, 39.2% margin, CFA peg) but the parent's consolidated valuation is capped by mature French domestic. Market applies structural discount: (a) investors seeking African growth prefer pure-plays; (b) France is an anchor with limited growth; (c) no near-term catalyst to separate OMEA. This discount persists and is unlikely to close without corporate action. [#J]

  • ❌ Max It execution risk: 75m user target by 2028 from 25m requires 3x growth in 2 years in markets with low smartphone penetration. If this fails, the €1bn incremental revenue target by 2028 evaporates and OMEA EBITDAaL growth moderates below high-single-digit guide. [#A]
  • ❌ MasOrange + Africa capex collision: If €5bn OMEA capex and MasOrange integration costs land simultaneously in FY2026-2027, FCF may disappoint before recovering to €5.2bn 2028 target. [#A]
  • ❌ French state overhang: ~23% government stake constrains capital allocation flexibility — no OMEA IPO, dividend floor is partly political — and creates headline regulatory risk in the domestic business. [#J]

  • ❌ Orange Money competitive disruption is structural: Wave Mobile Money's zero-fee model forced permanent repricing in Senegal/Ivory Coast; revenue yield per user declining; "Max it" pivot execution unproven; if growth drops below 10%, OMEA EBITDAaL guidance at risk.

  • ❌ French state overhang + conglomerate discount: ~23% government stake constrains capital allocation; OMEA embedded value (~€2.5bn towers + full segment upside) remains permanently locked without corporate action that management has not signalled.
