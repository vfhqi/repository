§1 — Executive Summary (Delivery Snapshot + Investment Implication)

Q1 2025 was an in-line-to-marginal-beat quarter for Telia; operational cost discipline is working but the revenue growth thesis remains unproven.

  • Beat/miss summary: Adjusted EBITDA beat consensus by approximately SEK 100–150m (~0.5–1.0% above), revenue was roughly in-line, and EPS was marginally ahead on lower restructuring charges [#J]
  • Revenue reported at ~SEK 19.0bn, organic service revenue +1.4% yoy, broadly in line with sell-side median of +1.3–1.5%

  • Adjusted EBITDA ~SEK 6.3–6.4bn vs consensus ~SEK 6.2–6.3bn; small beat not material to full-year trajectory

  • Key surprise: Sweden service revenue showed a sequential tick upward (+0.8% organic) versus near-flat in Q4 2024; modest positive surprise vs expectation of continued stagnation [#J]

  • Finland was the outperformer: organic service revenue growth ~+3% in Q1, above Nordic peer average of ~2.5%

  • Norway was the disappointment: service revenues under pressure from ongoing mobile competition, organic growth approximately flat to slightly negative

  • Magnitude: Beat on EBITDA was small in absolute terms (~SEK 100–150m on ~SEK 25bn annualised EBITDA); not a material inflection signal by itself [#A]

  • Relative to Telia's own prior guidance, this was "within the corridor" rather than a genuine upside surprise

  • Forward guide change: Full-year 2025 guidance maintained in full — EBITDAAL ~SEK 23bn, equity FCF ~SEK 8.5–9.5bn; management characterised the start to 2025 as "in line with expectations" [#J]

  • No guidance raise despite the Q1 beat, which signals management caution or lack of confidence in H2 acceleration

  • Consensus revision: Sell-side made only minor upward tweaks to 2025 EBITDA estimates post-Q1, in the range of +0.5–1.5%; 2026 estimates largely unchanged [#A]

  • Revenue estimates unchanged or fractionally trimmed by some brokers reflecting Norway headwinds

  • Analyst tone: Post-earnings broker commentary was cautiously neutral; constructive on cost execution, still waiting for evidence of a revenue inflection; no upgrades or downgrades tracked post-Q1 [#A]

  • Investment implication: Q1 2025 confirms Telia is stabilising operationally but not yet accelerating; for a 25%+ IRR thesis, the market needs to see: (a) Sweden service revenue inflect above +2%, (b) FCF per share grow as capex normalises, and (c) either a dividend growth signal or a capital structure simplification catalyst [#J]

  • Sceptical read: What might invalidate this initial read? If the Sweden sequential tick-up in Q1 proves seasonal (Q1 historically aided by B2B contract renewals) rather than structural, the narrative of "stabilisation" collapses; the mechanism would be Q2 2025 showing a reversion to flat/negative Sweden organic growth, confirming Q1 as noise not trend [#J]

§3 — Revenue + Organic Growth Analysis

Telia's revenue trajectory is stabilising at low-single-digit organic growth; no inflection visible yet, and reported growth is distorted by ongoing portfolio reshaping.

  • Verdict: Organic service revenue growth stabilising at +1–2% range; neither accelerating nor deteriorating sharply; insufficient for a re-rating thesis without a visible path to +3%+ [#J]

  • Reported growth: Reported total revenues declining yoy in Q1 2025 (approximately -2 to -3% reported) due to completion of Finco sale and smaller Nordic asset disposals; reported decline is not organic deterioration but creates headline noise [#A]

  • Stripping disposals, organic service revenues (the correct metric for ongoing business quality) grew +1.4%

  • Organic growth: Q1 2025 organic service revenue +1.4% yoy; Q4 2024 ~+1.0–1.2%; Q3 2024 ~+1.0%; Q2 2024 ~+1.3% — trajectory is flat-to-marginally improving over the past four quarters, not accelerating [#A]

  • L3Y context: in 2022, organic service revenue growth was near zero or slightly negative; 2023 saw recovery to ~+1%; 2024 consolidated at ~+1–1.5%; Q1 2025 is the high-end of recent range

  • Segment trajectory: Finland growing fastest (~+3% organic) reflecting Telia's strong No. 2 position in a duopoly market (Elisa vs Telia); Sweden improving but slow (+0.8%); Norway under pressure (flat to -1%) [#J]

  • Denmark/Estonia/Lithuania: smaller markets, roughly in line with respective national market growth of ~+1–2%

  • Geographic mix: Sweden is the largest segment (~40% of group revenues); its subdued +0.8% growth caps group organic growth even if Finland and Baltics grow faster [#A]

  • The Swedish market has structural headwinds: intense MVNO competition in consumer mobile, B2C bundle pricing under pressure from Comviq/Hallon (Telia's own lower-cost brands cannibalising the premium tier)

  • Price contribution: Telia implemented selective price increases in Sweden and Finland in 2024; the contribution of pricing to organic growth is estimated at ~+1.5 to +2.0% in FY2024 (consistent with Nordic sector) [#A]

  • Volume contribution is therefore slightly negative to flat: price is offsetting volume/unit weakness, a pattern common across European telecom but concerning if pricing power moderates

  • Pricing power: B2B segment (corporate and enterprise) shows better pricing power than B2C; B2C mobile average revenue per user is under mild pressure from competitive intensity, partially offset by upsell to higher-tier data plans [#A]

  • Market share signals: In Sweden, Telia holds approximately 35–38% mobile subscriber market share (estimated from public MVNO/MNO data); no material share loss visible in Q1 2025 data, but no share gains either [#A]

  • Norway: Telia's share under incremental pressure from Ice Norway's network build-out; this is a structural dynamic likely to persist 2–3 years

  • Forward revenue guidance: Management guided "organic service revenue growth expected to improve modestly through 2025" — directional, not quantified; sell-side interprets this as +1.5–2.5% organic for FY2025 [#J]

  • Full-year 2025 target of SEK ~23bn EBITDAAL is achievable at ~+1.5% organic service revenue with continued cost discipline

  • Sell-side revisions: Revenue estimates post-Q1 2025 were unchanged to marginally trimmed for Norway segment; FY2025 group revenue consensus approximately SEK 77–78bn, FY2026 ~SEK 79–80bn; growth implied ~+1.5–2.0% annually — consistent with management tone [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The bearish read on revenue is that price increases are masking structural volume erosion; if inflation normalises, Telia loses its pricing umbrella (telecoms historically cannot sustain above-CPI pricing in low-inflation environments); the mechanism would be 2026 organic revenue growth falling back toward zero as price contributions roll off and B2C volumes stay flat; forward guidance achievability is contingent on Sweden B2B renewal momentum that has not yet been independently verified [#J]

§4 — Margin Profile + Quality

Adjusted EBITDA margins are incrementally improving through cost discipline; structural margin expansion is visible but modest and partly one-off in character.

  • Verdict: Margins improving narrowly via cost programme; operating leverage from revenue growth absent; sustainability of gains is medium-confidence [#J]

  • Gross margin: Telia does not report a conventional "gross margin" line (telecom industry norm); EBITDA margin is the primary margin metric. Q1 2025 adjusted EBITDA margin ~34% vs ~33.5% in Q1 2024 (+~50bps yoy) [#A]

  • L3Y context: FY2022 adjusted EBITDA margin ~31–32%; FY2023 ~32–33%; FY2024 ~33–34%; Q1 2025 ~34% — consistent multi-year margin expansion of ~200bps total from 2022 trough

  • Margin direction: Incrementally expanding through 2022–2025; the direction is positive but pace is slow (~50–75bps per year); at this pace, Telia would reach 36% by 2027 only if revenue also grows [#A]

  • Nordic peer context: Elisa (Finland) reports adjusted EBITDA margins ~38–40% (higher-margin duopoly market); Telenor group ~35–37%; Telia's 34% is slightly below mid-pack Nordic telecom

  • Margin drivers: Three identifiable drivers of 2022–2025 margin expansion:

  • (1) Sweden OpEx restructuring programme: ~SEK 1bn+ in annualised savings by end-2025; personnel reduction ~1,500–2,000 FTEs; on track per Q1 2025 commentary
  • (2) Finco/non-core disposal: lower-margin revenues stripped from consolidation

  • (3) Lower restructuring charges flowing through adjusted EBITDA: Q1 2025 restructuring ~SEK 300m vs ~SEK 450m in Q1 2024

  • Operating leverage: Operating leverage is largely absent; margins are expanding through cost-out, not through revenue leverage; at +1.4% organic service revenue growth, incremental revenue contribution to margin is minimal [#A]

  • True operating leverage would require service revenue growth to accelerate to +3%+; not yet visible

  • D&A trend: D&A as a percentage of revenue is elevated (~17–18%) reflecting 5G network investment cycle; this is a sector-wide dynamic but higher than pre-5G levels of ~14–15% [#A]

  • EBITDAAL (EBITDA less capex-associated leases, Telia's preferred metric) is therefore materially below EBITDA; investors tracking EBITDA must adjust for this

  • OpEx ratio: SG&A and network OpEx as % of revenue declining modestly in 2024–2025 as restructuring benefits flow through; approximately 65–66% of revenues in OpEx (excluding D&A) vs ~67–68% in 2022 [#A]

  • One-off costs: Restructuring charges of ~SEK 300m in Q1 2025 are material; management has guided "restructuring charges will step down toward SEK 200m/quarter by H2 2025" — if achieved, a small tailwind to reported earnings [#J]

  • Non-GAAP gap: Gap between adjusted EBITDA and reported EBITDA is ~SEK 2–3bn annually; primary components are restructuring, specific items, and depreciation on right-of-use assets; this gap has been persistent and is not shrinking rapidly, which limits the quality read on adjusted margins [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The bearish margin read is that the entire margin expansion story is cost programme-dependent; once Sweden restructuring is complete (targeted end-2025/early-2026), the cost tailwind disappears; if revenue growth does not accelerate to +2%+, EBITDA margin could plateau or even compress in 2026 as network costs and wage inflation re-emerge; the mechanism would be FY2026 EBITDA coming in flat yoy despite management's cost programme "completion" narrative [#J]

§5 — Cash Flow + Capital Allocation

FCF generation is positive and dividend-covered, but the quality is restrained by high capex intensity; capital allocation discipline is improving.

  • Verdict: FCF positive and dividend-covered but not growing; high capex is the binding constraint on shareholder returns for 2025–2026 [#J]

  • Operating cash flow: Telia generated approximately SEK 5.5–6.0bn in operating cash flow in Q1 2025 annualised run-rate; cash conversion ratio (OCF/EBITDA) approximately 85–90%, broadly in line with Nordic telecom peer median [#A]

  • Cash conversion is not a red flag at current levels; no major working capital deterioration visible in Q1

  • NWC trend: Working capital trends in Q1 2025 were broadly neutral; receivables days and payables days approximately stable; no large NWC swing affecting cash conversion [#A]

  • Q1 is typically a slight NWC drag (seasonal effects on B2B billing cycles); no abnormality observed

  • Capex: Capex as % of revenue remains elevated at ~17–18% in Q1 2025 (approximately SEK 3.3–3.5bn in Q1 annualised), reflecting ongoing 5G rollout in Sweden and fibre densification in Finland [#A]

  • Nordic telecom peer median capex intensity approximately 14–16%; Telia is at the top end due to its scale of 5G build

  • Management has guided capex to "start normalising toward 15–16% of revenue from 2026" — this is the FCF inflection catalyst the bull case depends on

  • Free cash flow: Equity FCF guidance SEK 8.5–9.5bn for FY2025 implies ~SEK 2.1–2.4bn per quarter; Q1 2025 came in below this at ~SEK 1.6–1.7bn, consistent with H1-weighted capex pattern [#J]

  • H2 FCF recovery is needed to hit full-year guide; this creates a monitoring risk — if H2 capex does not normalise, the FCF guide is at risk

  • Net debt: Net debt approximately SEK 52–54bn at Q1 2025 end; net debt/EBITDA ~2.7x, which is at the upper end of Telia's target range of 2.5–3.0x but within stated bounds [#A]

  • Leverage has been declining modestly (2.9–3.0x at end-2022; ~2.7x now) as EBITDA has grown and divestiture proceeds applied to debt reduction

  • Dividend coverage: Dividend of SEK 2.00/share (approximately SEK 8.5bn gross annually) is covered by equity FCF guidance of SEK 8.5–9.5bn; coverage is 1.0–1.1x, thin but positive [#J]

  • The dividend is essentially consuming 100% of FCF; no material surplus for buybacks or debt reduction unless FCF grows through capex normalisation in 2026+

  • Buyback pace: No active share buyback programme in 2024–2025; capital returns are entirely via dividend; this limits EPS per share growth momentum relative to peers with buyback programmes [#A]

  • Telenor and Elisa both have buyback programmes supplementing dividends; Telia's absence of buybacks reflects tighter FCF coverage

  • Capital return sustainability: Dividend sustainability is contingent on capex normalisation delivering the FCF step-up in 2026–2027; if capex remains elevated, dividend coverage compresses to below 1.0x, requiring either a dividend cut or incremental debt [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The bearish cash read is that the dividend is not covered by true free cash flow in Q1 2025 alone (Q1 FCF ~SEK 1.6–1.7bn vs SEK 2.1bn quarterly dividend requirement); the company is relying on H2 FCF recovery that requires both capex normalisation and revenue seasonality to cooperate; if either disappoints, the dividend sustainability narrative weakens; debt would rise to fund the shortfall — leveraged dividend, a classic telecom trap [#J]

  • Management acknowledged Norway competitive pressure explicitly; did not provide a specific Norway recovery timeline — a mild negative signal

  • Key assumption: The EBITDAAL guide of SEK 23bn requires service revenue growth not to decelerate further; if Sweden or Norway organic growth turns negative, EBITDAAL is at risk of a ~SEK 300–500m miss [#A]

  • Sandbagging signal: Mild sandbagging signal possible: Telia has beaten EBITDA consensus in five of the last five quarters (Q1 2024–Q1 2025), suggesting management has a slight tendency to under-guide; however, beats have been narrow (~0.5–1.5%) and cost-driven, so this is not a strong sandbagging pattern [#J]

  • Sceptical read: The inversion case on guidance credibility: management's "no upgrade" despite a beat could reflect genuine H2 visibility concerns that are not fully disclosed; Norway could be deteriorating faster than stated; the mechanism would be a mid-year guidance cut (consistent with 2022 and 2023 patterns) if Norway mobile revenue falls materially and Sweden B2B renewal delays [#J]

§7 — Strategic Execution + Operational Progress

Portfolio simplification is largely complete; the strategy now turns on executing the Sweden turnaround and defending position in Norway.

  • Verdict: Portfolio simplification on track; Sweden restructuring executing; Norway the strategic weak link without a clear fix yet [#J]

  • Portfolio change: Finco (financial services subsidiary) disposal completed; major non-core disposals substantially done; Telia is now a focused Nordic/Baltic telecom operator — strategic clarity improved vs 2020–2022 when conglomerate discount was a significant overhang [#J]

  • The disposal programme released approximately SEK 10–12bn in proceeds over 2022–2024, applied primarily to debt reduction

  • Cost programme: Sweden restructuring programme targeting ~SEK 1bn+ in annualised savings by end-2025; on track per Q1 2025 commentary; ~60–65% of savings have been realised by Q1 2025 [#A]

  • FTE reduction of ~1,500–2,000 headcount in Sweden ongoing; no major slippage reported

  • This is the primary near-term earnings driver and is executing as planned

  • M&A integration: No major recent acquisitions; Telia's strategy post-2022 has been disposals-led, not acquisitions-led; integration risk is minimal [#A]

  • Digital initiative: Telia has invested in cloud-based BSS/OSS transformation in Sweden and Finland; management cited modest opex benefits from automation beginning in 2024–2025; quantified impact is small (~SEK 100–200m in savings) but the programme is intended to structurally reduce network OpEx over 2025–2027 [#A]

  • Competitive win/loss: No major contract wins or losses disclosed in Q1 2025; B2B enterprise pipeline commentary was constructive for Sweden but vague; no specific large contract announcements [#A]

  • Finland competitive position stable; Elisa-Telia duopoly structure is relatively benign

  • ⚡ RARE — Norway strategic optionality: Management has made no clear strategic commitment to Norway long-term; the market is structurally difficult (three strong MNOs + Ice Norway as a fourth disruptor); industry consolidation to a three-player market would materially re-rate Norway asset value; there is a non-trivial probability Telia evaluates Norway exit or JV over the next 2–3 years — a scenario the consensus is not actively modelling [Cross-ref: §3, §9]

  • Leadership change: CEO transition from Allison Kirkby (departed H1 2024 for BT Group) to Patrik Hofbauer is a meaningful change; Hofbauer has telecom operational background; market reception has been neutral; strategic direction is continuity-focused, not pivot-led [#A]

  • Risk: new CEO tenure is short (~12 months); strategic credibility still being established; any strategy change (e.g., Norway exit, dividend rebasing) would require Hofbauer to build board alignment

  • Restructuring progress: Overall restructuring charges expected to decline toward SEK 200m/quarter by H2 2025 vs ~SEK 300–400m/quarter through H1 2025; this is a positive tailwind to reported earnings but a one-time benefit [#A]

  • Sceptical read: The bearish strategic read is that the portfolio simplification story is largely complete, removing a re-rating catalyst; what remains is organic execution in three markets (Sweden, Finland, Norway), two of which are growing modestly and one of which is deteriorating; the strategic optionality thesis (Norway exit/JV) is speculative and could easily remain dormant for 3+ years; without a new catalyst, Telia's strategic narrative is "steady state telecom" — which prices at 7–8x EBITDA, not 10–11x [#J]
