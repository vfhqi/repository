§8 — Cross-Driver Interactions + ACH

  • Sub-point: Multiple-creep is the single variable that can cause both drivers to deteriorate simultaneously — high correlation risk
  • Driver B → Driver C: Organic growth → Margin trajectory: organic leverage is the primary margin expansion mechanism; if organic slows to 3–4%, fixed cost absorption weakens and margin plateaus [#J]
  • Independent drivers: Regulatory tailwinds and balance sheet capacity are largely independent of acquisition pace — they are enabling/floor drivers rather than co-dependent [#A]
  • Correlated drivers: Acquisition engine + organic growth + margin are all positively correlated through the "niche market quality" thesis; a macro shock to niche industrial demand could hit all three simultaneously [#J]
  • Joint probability: If each of the 3 fulcrum drivers is independently ~70% likely to deliver over 18–24M, joint probability ~34%; positive correlation between drivers reduces this further — possibly 25–30% probability of all three delivering at high end [#J]
  • Sub-point: This is the central IRR risk — the HQI archetype requires compounding of multiple good outcomes simultaneously
  • Sequential dependency: M&A pipeline depth must be established before deal quality can be maintained; regulatory tailwinds must sustain before organic floor holds — neither is acutely at risk in the near term [#A]
  • Compound risk: If organic softness triggers margin plateau AND management responds by accelerating acquisition pace (to maintain EPS growth), deal quality risk rises — a dangerous feedback loop documented in other serial acquirers (cf. Spectranetics, Danaher pre-2008) [#J]
