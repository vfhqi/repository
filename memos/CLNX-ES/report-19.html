3. PART A — FAILURE NARRATIVES

§2 — Failure Narrative 1: Refinancing Wall / Rate-Reset Risk (Revenue/Profitability — debt cost transmission)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex carries gross debt of approximately €18–19bn with a weighted-average cost of debt locked at circa 2% during the zero-rate era; as maturities roll from 2026 onwards and new money is raised at 4–5%+ rates, the incremental interest burden compounds against an AFFO base that is already thin relative to enterprise value. Transmission mechanism: rising interest cost → AFFO per share compression → equity re-rating as the market prices a lower terminal distributable yield → share price decline of 20–35% before any operational deterioration occurs.

Early warning signals:
- Weighted-average cost of debt in each quarterly refinancing disclosure rising above 2.5% on a blended basis
- New bond issuances priced with spreads >150bps above Bund/OAT benchmarks
- Maturity schedule acceleration — any refinancing of >€2bn in a single calendar year at materially higher rates
- Credit agency outlook revisions toward negative from BBB/Baa2 equivalents
- Leverage covenant headroom disclosure narrowing in investor presentations

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility — European IG-rated infrastructure issuers with >5× net debt/EBITDA have historically seen AFFO yield compression of 150–250bps in rate-reset cycles; Cellnex's leverage profile (circa 7× net debt/EBITDA) is at the more exposed end of the peer spectrum

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Debt market conditions exist — IG credit at 4–5% is structural, not transitory
- Motive: No motive for Cellnex — this is an exogenous risk, not management-driven; the "motive" leg belongs to the market mechanism
- Opportunity: Maturities are scheduled; refinancing is unavoidable
- Assessment: Two-of-three (Means + Opportunity without internal Motive); downgraded to watch-priority but mechanistically compelling — the lack of internal motive does not reduce impact probability

Disconfirming evidence: Cellnex successfully executes liability management at sub-3% blended cost through refinancing into long-duration instruments; or ECB rate cuts to 2026 lows sufficiently suppress European IG spreads; or disposal proceeds are used for net debt reduction at scale before the refinancing wall hits

Monitoring checklist:
- Blended cost of debt (quarterly; flag if >2.5%)
- Net debt / run-rate EBITDA ratio (flag if >7.5×)
- Maturity schedule weighted average life (flag if <5 years)
- New issuance spread vs. Bund benchmark
- Credit rating outlook (Moody's / Fitch)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with FN3 (AFFO stagnation) and FN8 (disposal programme failure); a refinancing adverse outcome amplifies FN3 and may force FN8 to accelerate at sub-optimal prices; null interaction with FN6 (management departure)

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex's 2025–2026 bond issuances consistently price inside 3% all-in cost and the weighted-average cost of debt on total gross debt remains below 2.8% at year-end 2026, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated

§3 — Failure Narrative 2: Organic Co-Location Growth Deceleration (Revenue — growth narrative break)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex's bull thesis depends on European MNOs progressively adding tenancies to existing towers — the tenancy-ratio path from circa 1.5× toward 2.0×+ drives the step-change in incremental EBITDA margins. If MNO capex cycles slow (network sharing agreements, spectrum efficiency, 5G densification via small cells bypassing macro towers), the new-tenancy flow on the existing portfolio stalls. Transmission mechanism: tenancy addition shortfall → below-consensus revenue per-site growth → EBITDA margin expansion fails to materialise → EPS misses consensus by 10–20% for 2–3 consecutive years.

Early warning signals:
- MNO capex guidance (Vodafone, Orange, Deutsche Telekom) showing macro-tower investment declining as % of total network capex
- Cellnex annual report tenancy additions on existing portfolio (excl. newly-acquired sites) — a flat or declining trend
- Network sharing (MORAN/MOCN) agreements announced between MNOs in Cellnex's core markets reducing per-tower MNO count
- Order intake / signed amendment agreements disclosed in investor day presentations flattening
- 5G small-cell densification spend by MNOs rising as a % of total RAN capex (structural bypass risk)

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility — European macro-tower tenancy ratios have historically grown slowly (CAGR of tenancy ratio improvement across European towercos typically 0.03–0.06× per year), and the 5G small-cell thesis has been repeatedly delayed; the risk is real but the timeline is extended

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Technology alternatives exist (small cells, network sharing)
- Motive: MNOs have strong cost-reduction incentives
- Opportunity: Spectrum positions and network sharing frameworks are in place in Germany, Italy, Spain
- Assessment: All three legs present — full M/M/O; classify as active watch

Disconfirming evidence: Signed MNO co-location pipeline for 2026–2028 shows meaningful contracted amendment volume; tenancy ratio on pre-2020 portfolio (organic cohort) improving at >0.05× per year; MNO capex guidance for 5G densification confirming macro-layer dominance

Monitoring checklist:
- Tenancy ratio on organic (pre-2019) portfolio — disclosed or derivable from segment data
- Amendment revenue per site per year (organic estate only)
- MNO macro-tower capex as % of total RAN capex (industry data)
- Network sharing announcements in Spain/Italy/France/UK/Germany
- 5G rollout progress vs. regulator commitments (proxy for urgency of macro-tower demand)

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with FN4 (pricing pressure) — if tenancy growth stalls, MNOs gain negotiating leverage on lease rates; semi-correlated with FN5 (margin compression); null interaction with FN7 (M&A integration)

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex's organic tenancy-ratio improvement on the pre-2019 portfolio exceeds 0.05× per year in 2025–2026 disclosures, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated

§4 — Failure Narrative 3: AFFO-Per-Share Stagnation / Distributable Cash Flow Trap (Profitability — operating-leverage failure)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex presents adjusted EBITDA growth as the headline metric; AFFO (recurring free cash flow after maintenance capex, lease payments, and interest) per share has been persistently negative or near-zero relative to what the bull thesis projects. If growth capex is reclassified or maintenance capex is understated in management definitions, reported AFFO inflates — but the real FCF available to equity holders is materially lower. Transmission mechanism: AFFO definition diverges from true distributable cash → equity investors price a higher distributable yield than is achievable → re-rating when reality surfaces, or dividend initiation is delayed indefinitely.

Early warning signals:
- Growth vs. maintenance capex split — any upward drift in disclosed maintenance capex as a % of revenues
- Lease liability growth outpacing EBITDA growth (IFRS 16 liabilities on ground leases escalating)
- Difference between reported AFFO and operating cash flow after interest + lease payments
- Analyst consensus AFFO-per-share estimates revised downward for 2026–2028 in two consecutive quarters
- Management guidance language shifting from "AFFO positive" to "path to AFFO positive" — timeline elongation

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility — European towercos including INWIT and Vantage Towers have demonstrated that AFFO conversion is structurally lower than EBITDA conversion due to ground lease obligations; Cellnex's lease intensity is above the sector median due to legacy urban portfolio characteristics

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Lease cost escalation is contractual and structural
- Motive: Management has incentive to define AFFO favourably; limited motive to disclose unfavourably
- Opportunity: Definitional flexibility exists in non-GAAP AFFO reporting
- Assessment: All three legs present — full M/M/O; classify as active watch

Disconfirming evidence: Cellnex publishes a reconciliation showing AFFO per share turning positive in 2026 on a strictly-defined basis (post all lease payments, post maintenance capex, post cash interest) confirmed by two consecutive quarters of positive reported operating cash flow after all obligations

Monitoring checklist:
- Operating cash flow vs. reported AFFO (management definition) — quarterly gap
- Ground lease cost per tower as disclosed in notes
- Maintenance capex intensity (% revenues) — flag if >8%
- AFFO per share consensus trajectory (Bloomberg/FactSet)
- Lease liability balance growth rate vs. EBITDA growth rate

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly amplified by FN1 (rate-reset increases interest burden, shrinks AFFO further); correlated with FN5 (operating leverage failure); false-friend interaction with FN9 (disposal proceeds look like AFFO improvement but are one-time)

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex's 2026 AFFO per share (strictly defined as post-lease, post-maintenance-capex, post-cash-interest) is positive and growing, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated

§5 — Failure Narrative 4: MNO Pricing Pressure / Lease Renewal Risk (Revenue — pricing pressure)

Specific failure narrative: A significant proportion of Cellnex's master lease agreements (MLAs) with anchor MNOs are long-dated (10–15 year terms) with CPI-linked escalators; however, the renewal cycle for older agreements in Spain and Italy exposes the portfolio to renegotiation pressure as MNOs consolidate (e.g. Spain: Orange/MásMóvil merger) and customer concentration increases. Transmission mechanism: MNO consolidation → fewer independent tenants per market → renewal negotiating leverage shifts to MNO → lease rate compression of 5–15% on renewal → per-site revenue decline → EBITDA margin contraction.

Early warning signals:
- MNO M&A activity in Cellnex's top-3 markets reducing the number of independent anchor tenants
- Lease renewal terms disclosed in annual reports showing real escalator erosion (below CPI)
- Revenue per site declining in markets where MNO consolidation has completed
- Churn rate on secondary tenants (non-anchor MNOs) rising
- Regulatory decisions (NCA / EC) attaching tower-access conditions to MNO mergers that reduce Cellnex pricing power

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility — the Orange/MásMóvil consolidation in Spain is a live and material example; precedent from US towercos (American Tower post-T-Mobile/Sprint) shows 2–4% near-term revenue headwind from consolidation churn; European regulatory conditions make this more likely to include explicit tower-sharing mandates

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: MNO consolidation is underway (Spain)
- Motive: Consolidated MNO has strong cost-reduction motive
- Opportunity: Lease renewal windows in Spain portfolio circa 2026–2028
- Assessment: All three legs present — full M/M/O; classify as active watch

Disconfirming evidence: Orange/MásMóvil integration confirms no material change to existing Cellnex MLAs; Cellnex discloses weighted-average lease term remaining of >8 years post-renewal cycle; revenue per site in Spain grows at or above CPI in 2025–2026

Monitoring checklist:
- Revenue per site by country (Spain, Italy, France — segment disclosures)
- MNO M&A regulatory decisions with tower-sharing conditions
- Weighted-average lease term remaining (annual report)
- Churn / early termination disclosures
- CPI escalator realization vs. contractual rate

Interaction with other failure modes: Correlated with FN2 (tenancy stagnation compounds pricing pressure); amplifies FN3 (lower per-site revenue = lower AFFO); partial false-friend interaction with FN9 (disposal of Spanish towers might reduce exposure but also removes revenue)

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If revenue per site in Spain/Italy grows at CPI or above through the 2026–2028 renewal cycle with no disclosed lease concessions, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated

§6 — Failure Narrative 5: Cost Inflation / Ground Lease Escalation Outpacing Revenue (Margin — cost inflation)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex's cost structure is dominated by ground lease obligations — escalating with local CPI or fixed uplifts — and by field maintenance/energy costs that are energy-price-sensitive. If European energy costs re-spike or structural labour cost inflation in Southern Europe persists, the EBITDA margin trajectory (currently guided toward 60%+ adjusted EBITDA margin) is challenged. Transmission mechanism: ground lease CPI escalation + energy cost spike → EBITDA margin 200–300bps below guidance → EPS misses → de-rating.

Early warning signals:
- European wholesale electricity prices (TTF proxy) re-spiking above €80/MWh
- Spanish / Italian CPI remaining structurally elevated above 3%
- Cellnex hedging disclosures showing unhedged energy exposure >30% of total consumption
- EBITDA margin guidance cut in Q-reports by >100bps
- SG&A as % of revenues drifting upward (integration-cost drag from acquisitions)

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility — energy cost spikes are episodic rather than structural post-2023; ground lease CPI escalation is a known and priced-in cost but at 3%+ inflation, the symmetry of CPI-linked revenues vs. CPI-linked costs is imperfect (not all revenue is fully indexed)

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Energy market volatility remains possible
- Motive: No internal motive — exogenous cost risk
- Opportunity: Hedge maturities create windows of exposure
- Assessment: Two-of-three — watch only

Disconfirming evidence: Cellnex discloses fully-hedged energy position through 2027; EBITDA margin guidance maintained or raised at H1 results; ground lease escalation shown to be fully offset by MLA revenue escalators

Monitoring checklist:
- European energy price forward curve (quarterly mark)
- Cellnex energy hedging disclosure (annual report note)
- Adjusted EBITDA margin by segment (quarterly)
- SG&A / revenues ratio trend
- CPI in Spain, Italy, France, Poland (quarterly)

Interaction with other failure modes: Semi-correlated with FN3 (cost inflation compresses AFFO further); partial correlation with FN1 (all-in cost pressure makes debt refinancing timing critical); low interaction with FN7/FN8

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex's adjusted EBITDA margin expands 100bps+ year-on-year in 2025–2026 with disclosed full energy hedge, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated

§8 — Failure Narrative 7: M&A Integration Failure / Stranded Acquisition Value (Execution — M&A integration)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex made >€15bn in acquisitions between 2019 and 2022 across 12 European markets; the integration of acquired portfolios (particularly in markets like Poland, Austria, Ireland, and the UK) is still ongoing. If anchor MNO relationships in acquired markets deteriorate, or if the assumed tenancy-ratio improvement on acquired portfolios fails to materialise in 5 years post-acquisition, the implied acquisition IRRs (guided at 6–8%) degrade. Transmission mechanism: below-plan tenancy addition on acquired portfolios → capital allocated at 6–8% IRR is actually earning 3–4% → goodwill impairment → EPS hit + balance sheet question.

Early warning signals:

- Post-acquisition tenancy-ratio improvement below 0.05× per year in the 3rd/4th year post-close

Plausibility verbal verdict: Medium plausibility — M&A integration risk at this scale is historically significant; comparable European infrastructure roll-ups have seen 15–20% of acquired assets underperform implied acquisition IRRs; Cellnex's geographic and counterparty concentration in acquired markets creates cluster risk

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:

§9 — Failure Narrative 8 (FALSE FRIEND): Disposal Programme Masking Deteriorating Portfolio Quality

Specific failure narrative (FALSE FRIEND): The Cellnex disposal programme (sale of Spanish towers to Asterion, Italian tower conversations, potential further country exits) is read by the market as: (a) a NAV-crystallisation event, (b) deleveraging confirmation, and (c) capital-discipline proof. Each of these reads is partly correct — but the false-friend signal is that the disposed assets are often the highest-tenancy-ratio, highest-margin, most-mature legacy portfolios, not the underperforming ones. Post-disposal, the retained portfolio has a lower average tenancy ratio, lower margin profile, and slower organic growth trajectory. Transmission mechanism: disposal-driven bull signal → analyst upgrades → investor inflows → price appreciation → but remaining portfolio is structurally weaker → EPS trajectory from retained portfolio misses expectations 12–18 months after disposal

Early warning signals:
- Disclosed tenancy ratio of disposed assets vs. remaining portfolio (if available)
- Adjusted EBITDA margin in retained portfolio post-disposal declining vs. pre-disposal level
- Revenue per tower in retained portfolio flat or declining in disposal years
- Proceeds deployed into lower-quality or lower-tenancy acquired towers (Ireland, Eastern Europe)
- Sell-side EPS upgrades immediately post-disposal followed by quiet consensus downgrades 2–3 quarters later

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility — comparable infrastructure asset recycling (sale of mature assets, retention of growth assets) is a documented pattern in European infrastructure; the specific risk that mature assets are the highest-margin assets in a towerco is structurally well-supported; this is a high-plausibility false-friend scenario

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Disposal programme is actively underway
- Motive: Management needs to demonstrate deleveraging; motivated to show NAV crystallisation
- Opportunity: Market conditions for tower asset sales remain constructive (private infra capital seeking yield)
- Assessment: All three legs present — full M/M/O; classify as active watch, false-friend category

Disconfirming evidence: Disposed asset tenancy ratios disclosed as below the portfolio average (i.e. non-core disposals); retained portfolio margin guidance maintained post-disposal; management explicitly discloses that disposals are of non-core or sub-scale market positions

Monitoring checklist:
- Tenancy ratio of disposed vs. retained (disclosed or derivable)
- Adjusted EBITDA per tower post-disposal vs. pre-disposal
- Revenue per tower — organic retained portfolio (strip acquisitions and disposals)
- Management commentary characterisation of disposed assets ("non-core" vs. "mature core")
- Analyst note language on disposal quality (signal extraction)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with FN3 (AFFO stagnation post-disposal); amplifies FN2 (retained portfolio has lower organic growth if mature assets sold); directly relevant to FN7 (underperforming acquired markets are NOT what is being sold)

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex discloses that disposed asset tenancy ratios were below the portfolio average and that retained-portfolio EBITDA per tower is growing post-disposal, this false-friend narrative is substantially invalidated

§10 — Failure Narrative 9 (FALSE FRIEND): Earnings Beat From CPI Escalator Tailwind Masking Volume Stagnation

Specific failure narrative (FALSE FRIEND): Cellnex's MLA revenues carry CPI escalators; in 2022–2024, elevated European CPI delivered above-historical revenue growth on the existing portfolio. This mechanical tailwind has been partially mis-attributed by both management and consensus as evidence of strong "organic" growth momentum. As CPI normalises toward 2–2.5% (ECB target), the mechanical escalator contribution decays — and if underlying volume growth (new tenancies, amendments) has been insufficient to offset, consensus revenue estimates built in the high-CPI era will miss in the normalisation era. Transmission mechanism: CPI normalisation → escalator tailwind decays → organic revenue growth slows below consensus → EPS miss of 5–10% for 2–3 consecutive years without any operational failure.

Early warning signals:
- Organic revenue growth disclosure broken down between CPI-driven and volume-driven components
- Consensus revenue growth estimates for 2026–2028 that embed CPI above ECB target
- Management guidance language moving from "strong organic growth" to "organic growth in line with expectations" — a subtle downgrade
- Actual CPI in Spain/Italy/France tracking toward 2% or below
- Revenue per site growth declining below CPI (volume component negative)

Plausibility verbal verdict: High plausibility — this mechanism is documented in every towerco cycle where CPI escalators overstate organic performance; the risk is specifically elevated in 2025–2027 as European inflation normalises; it is a false-friend because reported growth continues to look strong even as the underlying volume engine is decelerating

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: CPI normalisation is the ECB's explicit policy objective
- Motive: No management motive to fail — mechanical/exogenous
- Opportunity: Consensus estimates already embed the CPI-escalator period; revisions happen at results
- Assessment: Two-of-three (Means + Opportunity; no internal motive); still classify as high-priority false-friend watch given false-friend character

Disconfirming evidence: Management provides explicit CPI vs. volume decomposition of organic growth showing volume component >2% in 2025–2026; consensus revisions post-CPI-normalisation remain stable or positive; new tenancy additions accelerate offsetting escalator decay

Monitoring checklist:
- Eurozone/Spain/Italy CPI monthly prints
- Organic growth decomposition in management commentary
- Consensus revenue estimates — track vs. CPI assumption embedded
- Revenue per site trends (strip new builds)
- Amendment volume per quarter (if disclosed)

Interaction with other failure modes: Directly correlated with FN2 (tenancy stagnation is the mechanism behind volume component weakness); amplifies FN3 (lower revenue growth = lower AFFO); interacts with FN4 (pricing pressure) — CPI normalisation + lease renegotiation = double revenue headwind

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If Cellnex explicitly breaks out CPI-driven vs. volume-driven organic growth and shows volume component growing at >2% CAGR through 2027, this false-friend narrative is substantially invalidated

§11 — Failure Narrative 10: Regulatory / Political Risk in Core Markets (Structural/Competitive)

Specific failure narrative: Cellnex operates under regulatory frameworks that govern tower access, pricing, and MNO obligations in each of its markets; in markets where Cellnex holds dominant towerco position (Spain, Italy, France), regulators have the tools to mandate open-access pricing or impose remedies that constrain lease-rate setting. Additionally, political pressure in Southern Europe around MNO infrastructure costs could accelerate mandatory tower-sharing regulations. Transmission mechanism: regulatory intervention → pricing cap on tower access → lease revenue per site capped at below-CPI growth → valuation multiple compression (infrastructure assets re-rate lower when pricing power is structurally curtailed).

Early warning signals:
- European Commission consultation documents on towerco market structure / open access
- NCA investigations in Spain or Italy (AGCOM) into towerco pricing
- Political party platforms (Spain: Podemos/PSOE; Italy: M5S) proposing infrastructure nationalisation or pricing regulation
- Comparable regulatory actions in other markets (India towerco regulations as precedent)
- MNO lobbying disclosures citing towerco pricing as a competition concern

Plausibility verbal verdict: Low plausibility on 12-month horizon — no active regulatory investigation is known; medium plausibility on 3-5 year horizon given the pattern of EU digital infrastructure regulation expanding scope

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: Regulatory frameworks exist; EU Connectivity Toolbox gives member states tools
- Motive: MNOs have political motive to lobby for pricing relief
- Opportunity: Spectrum auction renewals and MNO merger approvals create regulatory windows
- Assessment: All three legs present but timeline-extended — classify as structural watch, low near-term urgency

Disconfirming evidence: No active NCA investigation; EU regulatory agenda focused on MNO consolidation facilitation rather than towerco constraint; Cellnex proactively engages with regulators and receives neutral/positive outcomes

Monitoring checklist:
- EU / NCA regulatory consultation calendar
- MNO lobbying disclosures and public statements on tower costs
- Political party manifestos in Spain/Italy/France (election cycle monitor)
- Cellnex regulatory affairs disclosures (annual report)
- Comparable towerco regulatory actions globally (India, US)

Interaction with other failure modes: Semi-correlated with FN4 (pricing pressure from MNO consolidation may be amplified by regulatory facilitation); low interaction with FN1/FN3

Disconfirming evidence sceptical bullet: If no NCA or EU investigation into Cellnex pricing is initiated through 2026 and MNO merger approvals do not include towerco pricing conditions, this failure narrative is substantially invalidated for the 24-month hold period

§11B — Failure Narrative 11: Competitive Disruption via MNO In-Sourcing / New Entrant Towerco (Competitive/Structural)

Specific failure narrative: A key structural assumption is that European MNOs will not re-insource tower management and will remain anchor tenants for the life of Cellnex MLAs. However, a sustained period of high tower-lease costs (relative to declining MNO revenue per user) creates a structural motive for MNOs to acquire competing towercos, build new infrastructure outside MLAs, or in the extreme, attempt to buy back tower assets. A new-entrant private infrastructure fund acquiring a competing European towerco portfolio (e.g. a large institutional fund aggregating smaller markets) could also introduce price competition on new build/amendment contracts. Transmission mechanism: competitive pressure on new-build contracts → Cellnex loses incremental tenancy revenue to competitor → below-plan tenancy ratio improvement → EPS miss.

Early warning signals:
- MNO strategic announcements about tower strategy (in-sourcing, JV formation)
- Private infrastructure fund acquisitions of European tower portfolios
- New-build tender wins by Cellnex declining as a percentage of MNO new-site programmes
- MNO capex guidance showing increased "owned infrastructure" allocation
- Analyst commentary on competitive dynamics in towerco sector

Plausibility verbal verdict: Low plausibility — MNO tower divestiture trend is structural and well-established; in-sourcing would require MNO balance sheet capacity that current leverage levels prohibit; private fund competition is real but focused on smaller markets

Means/Motive/Opportunity test:
- Means: MNOs lack balance sheet capacity for in-sourcing at current leverage
- Motive: Cost-reduction motive exists in theory
- Opportunity: MLA lock-ins reduce near-term opportunity for structural change
- Assessment: One-of-three on near-term basis — watch only, low priority

Disconfirming evidence: European MNO divestiture pipeline continues; no announced in-sourcing; private fund acquisitions of European towers confirm asset class demand (not supply competition threat)

Monitoring checklist:
- MNO strategic announcements (annually)
- Tower asset transaction market (M&A pipeline)
- New-build tender award disclosures
- MNO leverage ratios (proxy for balance sheet capacity)
