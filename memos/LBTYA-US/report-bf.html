EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Liberty Global Ltd. operates as a highly sophisticated telecommunications holding company that is systematically dismantling a persistent valuation discount through aggressive corporate restructuring, strategic asset spinoffs, and structural network de-layering across its European footprint. The company commands substantial

major fixed-mobile convergence champions and a $3.4 billion growth portfolio of approximately 70 digital infrastructure and sports investments. However, the equity trades at a severe discount to sum-of-the-parts fair value, with management arguing the current stock price implies near-zero or negative valuation for core telecom businesses. This disconnect stems from complex joint venture structures, elevated subsidiary leverage ratios near 5.5x at Virgin Media O2 and VodafoneZiggo, and restricted cash upstreaming mechanisms that limit parent- level liquidity. CEO Mike Fries has executed a bold strategic pivot, completing a Bermuda re-domiciliation to enable friction-free spinoffs, shrinking corporate overhead by 75% , and successfully demerging Sunrise in Switzerland as a proof-of-concept for unlocking stub equity value. The core investment thesis rests on Liberty's ability to mechanically expose billions in hidden infrastructure value through continued portfolio separations, particularly the planned 90% spinoff of the newly formed Ziggo Group in Amsterdam by 2027. This Benelux consolidation combines VodafoneZiggo and Telenet into a €6.6 billion revenue champion serving 13 million customers, targeting €500 million in adjusted free cash flow by 2028 after capturing €1.0 billion in net present value synergies . Simultaneously, the group is executing a structural de-layering

strategy that separates high-margin wholesale network assets from lower-margin retail operations, exemplified by the €4.35 billion Wyre NetCo carve-out in Flanders and the 50-50 nexfibre joint venture in the UK. These infrastructure platforms command premium transaction multiples of 18x EBITDA in private markets versus depressed 5.9x multiples for integrated telecom peers, creating a powerful arbitrage opportunity. Fixed-mobile convergence penetration has reached 55.3% at Telenet and 51% at VodafoneZiggo , delivering critical churn reduction benefits that cut subscriber losses by approximately 50% in bundled households. Despite these structural advantages, Liberty faces formidable operational headwinds including persistent organic fixed-line relationship losses across all major markets, aggressive fiber overbuild competition from incumbents like BT Openreach, and the disruptive impact of UK One Touch Switching regulation that has materially lowered consumer switching barriers . Virgin Media O2 lost 110,000 consumer fixed relationships year-over-year in early 2026, while VodafoneZiggo shed 90,500 organic relationships amid intense Dutch fiber competition from KPN. Mobile performance diverges sharply, with VodafoneZiggo adding 24,700 postpaid subscribers in Q1 2026 while VMO2 lost 60,400 postpaid customers following controversial price increases. The group's financial returns remain structurally depressed, with consolidated return on invested capital of just 4.4% versus peer leader KPN's 15.0% ROIC , driven by elevated capital intensity of 25.3% to fund DOCSIS 4.0 upgrades and fiber migrations. Corporate cash flows are highly restricted by joint venture partner veto rights and mandatory leverage maintenance covenants , with VMO2 distributions collapsing to £200 million from historical £800 million averages and VodafoneZiggo guiding to zero cash distributions in 2026 . Liberty's capital allocation strategy has undergone a significant transformation, with management completely suspending share buybacks for 2026 despite historically retiring 60% of outstanding equity since 2017 . This pause preserves parent liquidity ahead of the €1.0 billion cash buyout of Vodafone's 50% VodafoneZiggo stake and supports the ambitious Benelux consolidation timeline. The company targets maintaining approximately $1.5 billion in holding company cash by year-end 2026, replenished through $500 million in planned Liberty Growth divestments including the successful €95 million UPC Slovakia sale at 7.0x EBITDA . Corporate overhead has been aggressively rationalized through 40% headquarters headcount reductions and the commercialization of back-office platforms into the standalone Liberty Blume services brand , which generated over £100 million in revenue during 2025 with a £400 million commercial pipeline . Management introduced a new 1.5% annual management fee on Liberty Growth's asset base, generating approximately $50 million annually to offset central losses and target $50 million in net corporate expenses by 2026 . The competitive landscape reveals intensifying pressure from multiple vectors including incumbent fiber overbuilds, disruptive low-cost mobile entrants like Digi Belgium with 99,000 mobile subscribers captured at 70% discount pricing , and the vertical integration threat from wholesale NetCos like nexfibre extending downstream into retail. Market concentration exceeds critical thresholds with VodafoneZiggo commanding 42% Dutch fixed share and top-three Dutch operators controlling a combined 91% market share , yet pricing discipline remains fragile amid aggressive promotional discounting. The UK wireless market achieved structural repair through the Vodafone Three merger creating a 57 million connection giant targeting £700 million in annualized synergies , while European regulators are shifting toward more supportive consolidation frameworks that favor scaled fixed- mobile national champions. Adjacent growth opportunities include the 50-50 AtlasEdge data center joint venture securing €253 million in green financing for edge computing expansion and Formula E sports platform investments valued in excess of $1 billion .

Supply chain analysis reveals critical dependencies on concentrated network equipment vendors including Teleste for 1.8 GHz amplifiers and CommScope for virtualized Distributed Access Architecture , while strategic partnerships with Infosys delivered 50% fewer network outages year-over-year and a landmark five-year Google Cloud alliance embeds Gemini AI models for autonomous operations. However, the group faces material geopolitical exposure through Sunrise's long-term Huawei dependency , with European Commission proposals threatening binding high-risk vendor bans that would require massive unbudgeted capital expenditures for equipment replacement. Upstream optical fiber pricing has experienced severe inflation with bare fiber spot prices doubling from $5 to $12-18 per fiber-kilometer due to global preform capacity constraints and surging hyperscaler demand, exemplified by Corning's $6.0 billion Meta supply agreement . Working capital management remains efficient with negative 15-day net cycles versus peer averages, though affiliates increasingly extend supplier payment terms and rely on structured vendor finance programs to defer repayments beyond customary 90-day windows . Looking forward, Liberty's success hinges on flawless execution of the Ziggo Group spinoff, continued NetCo- ServeCo de-layering to unlock premium infrastructure valuations, and the ability to stabilize retail subscriber trajectories through aggressive fixed-mobile bundle penetration gains. The investment case offers asymmetric upside if management can replicate the Sunrise precedent where the Swiss business captured 45% of combined market capitalization despite representing only 20% of proportionate EBITDA . Critical risks include the potential for joint venture partners to block strategic transactions, severe retail price wars permanently impairing cash generation and trapping debt-servicing burdens inside isolated credit silos, and the structural threat that rapid altnet consolidation could permanently dilute infrastructure advantages . The company operates in a favorable paradigm characterized by formidable capital barriers to entry , complementary rather than disruptive satellite competition from Starlink partnerships, and regulators actively encouraging market-repairing consolidations that support massive infrastructure investments. However, technological disruption vectors including AI-driven disintermediation and the failed Open RAN movement have largely validated incumbent network vendor positions while enabling software-upgradeable transition pathways to future 6G architectures.
