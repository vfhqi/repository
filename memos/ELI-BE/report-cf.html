EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

This comprehensive analysis of Elia Group reveals a company executing a transformative €31.6 billion capital expenditure program that positions it as the fastest-growing regulated transmission operator in Europe , delivering projected 12-14% compound annual EPS growth through 2029 –approximately double the peer median . The investment thesis rests on three fundamental pillars: sector-leading regulated asset base expansion driving 17-20% RAB CAGR , substantially outpacing Terna's 9% and Redeia's 8% ; constructive regulatory tailwinds in both Belgium and Germany, with CREG's draft methodology proposing increased maximum incentive caps to

3.75% from 2.43% and Morgan Stanley estimating Belgian changes could boost realised ROE by 50-100 basis points ; and the decisive resolution of a 16-month C-suite overhang through Bernard Gustin's CEO appointment and Marco Nix's permanent CFO role, establishing experienced leadership capable of executing the

massive infrastructure rollout . However, this growth narrative carries material execution risks that warrant careful monitoring: the aggressive capex profile drove free cash flow to negative €3.8 billion in 2025 , with positive inflection not anticipated until 2034, requiring sustained reliance on external capital markets ; severe supply-chain bottlenecks have extended transformer lead times from 6-8 months to 3-4 years

trading at a 30% P/E premium to its network peer group . The fundamental investment question centers on whether regulatory frameworks will adequately compensate for inflation and supply-chain cost escalation while the company navigates a decade-long period of massive negative cash conversion –if capital markets tighten unexpectedly or regulators balk at affordability concerns, the entire growth trajectory becomes structurally unfinanceable despite the legally mandated nature of the underlying grid expansion.

Net signal on change materiality

Elia resolved its 16-month interim leadership period through Bernard Gustin's January 2025 CEO appointment, bringing immediate familiarity with strategic rollout and equity funding requirements given his prior role as Independent Chairman , while Marco Nix's permanent Group CFO appointment in April 2025 brought a decade of 50Hertz CFO experience to centralize financial oversight .

The strategic ambition has fundamentally broadened , with corporate vision evolving from a top-five European TSO to a fully integrated international transmission energy group, backed by the massive 2024-2028 capex plan reaching €31.6 billion that drives sector-leading 17% RAB compound annual growth versus a 9% peer average .

This expansion is fully funded through a transformative €2.2 billion equity package via private placement and rights issue , though execution pragmatism has increased following severe offshore cost inflation that doubled the Princess Elisabeth Island budget and forced HVDC infrastructure re-evaluation .

The regulatory environment is materially improving , with Belgium's draft 2028-2031 methodology proposing increased maximum incentive caps of 3.75% from 2.43%, potentially boosting realised ROE by 50-100 basis points , while Germany intends to shift transmission from rigid revenue-cap to a more dynamic WACC-based cost-plus model with uniform remuneration for both new and existing assets .

However, the massive cost of grid expansion creates growing public and political pushback regarding energy affordability , representing a material political risk to future regulatory generosity. The financial profile transformation is dramatic, with top-line grid revenue expanding 15.8% to €4.4 billion and consolidated EBITDA increasing 17.2% to €1.79 billion , driving adjusted net profit surging 39.8% to €716.5 million in 2025 , though this comes at the cost of deeply negative €3.8 billion free cash flow and consolidated net financial debt reaching €14.1 billion .

Recent delivery track record and earnings quality

Elia demonstrates a consistent sandbagging archetype , with FY25 adjusted net profit reaching €716.5 million and beating the €490-540 million guidance range, representing an 8.1% beat at the mid-point , while FY24 net profit of €421.3 million comfortably exceeded the upwardly revised €395 million guidance, representing a 12.3% beat .

The company exhibits a perfect beat ratio against net income guidance over the last four reporting periods , with beats consistently driven by strong operational incentive capture and early project commissioning by the 50Hertz German division . However, earnings quality concerns are material, as underlying quality is deteriorating due to massive negative free cash flow and reliance on tax one-offs, with recent FY25 earnings beats heavily relying on non-cash capitalised interest of €90 million in Germany, flattering the reported bottom line .

Additional one-off boosts include a €46.5 million deferred tax revaluation in Germany linked to planned federal corporate tax rate reductions and a €19.2 million uplift through tax consolidation in Belgium for fiscal year 2024 .

The negative side saw reported FY25 net income suffer a material €99 million non-cash impairment on the US energyRe Giga portfolio due to regulatory delays .

On cash conversion , the picture is severely negative , with aggressive front-loaded capex driving free cash flow to negative €2.88 billion in the latest period despite robust EBITDA delivery , though Elia's cost-of-debt pass-through mechanism effectively shields operating cash flow from rising rates .

The operational delivery was strong, with the group successfully executing a record €5.2 billion in capex during FY25, tripling its historical annual average , while critical domestic infrastructure like Ventilus and Brabo III progressed through key regulatory and construction milestones without material delays , though severe supply chain bottlenecks forced postponement of Princess Elisabeth Island HVDC contracts, triggering a potential multi-year delay .

Forward guidance credibility and sensitivity analysis

Management demonstrates HIGH credibility , exhibiting a highly credible, consistent sandbagging archetype, routinely guiding conservatively on net income and beating expectations via accounting effects , with FY26 net

income guidance of €690-740 million confirming the massive investment plan's trajectory . The guidance relies on a Belgian 10-year OLO base rate assumption of 3.2% mechanically driving allowed return calculations

and a German base regulatory ROE of 2.9%, slightly upgraded from the prior 2.8% assumption . The company's sensitivity profile is highly unusual for a utility, with regulatory frameworks in Belgium and Germany fully isolating Elia from volume risk, ensuring zero EPS impact from demand fluctuations , while top-line revenue variances reflect direct pass-throughs of energy costs, meaning revenue beats do not mechanically drive EPS

beats . The real sensitivities lie in actual equity returns relying heavily on generating incentive bonuses above the base allowed regulatory margin ceiling , with inflationary operating cost spikes fully passed through to

future customer tariffs under cost-plus structures . The consensus view is constructive, with the FY26 mid-point guidance of €715 million sitting comfortably above the pre-release consensus estimate of €644 million , and Morgan Stanley modelling a 14% EPS CAGR through 2030 as highly achievable .

Critical forward tracking metrics include monitoring capitalised interest closely as €90-100 million of FY26 net income relies on this non-cash item , with a warning threshold triggered if the Belgian 10-year OLO yield drops below 2.5%, which would severely depress allowed returns through mechanical ROE calculations .

Sell-side positioning and consensus fragility

The sell-side exhibits overwhelming bullish positioning , with 86% Overweight ratings according to Morgan Stanley consensus distribution , though ING's terminal pull shows a wider consensus of 59% Buy and 41% Hold . Price targets show wide dispersion from a €120 low to €155 high, with consensus targets implying 14-20% total return potential over the next 12 months .

The bear case notes while RAB growth is sector-leading, actual equity returns remain fundamentally constrained, with the lack of obvious near-term catalysts limiting further multiple expansion .

Recent estimate revisions have been uniformly positive, with near-term earnings upgrades reflecting the integration of higher capitalised interest and improved regulatory visibility , including ING upgrading to Buy from Hold with price target increasing from €110 to €154, citing solid capex execution and earnings guidance beats . The consensus fragility lies in the fact that the overwhelming bullish consensus ignores the fundamental fragility of Elia's deeply negative cash flows, where a sudden macro shock could abruptly close funding markets, rendering the sector-leading growth thesis immediately uninvestable .

Fundamental peer positioning and relative momentum

Elia leads its fundamental peer group on long-term growth metrics, with analysts projecting a 14% EPS compound annual growth rate through 2029, significantly outpacing the sector average , and securing the top peer rank for projected growth, driving an estimated 14-18% RAB CAGR into 2030 .

National Grid ranks second with 11% estimated RAB CAGR as UK transmission investments scale up , while Terna holds third with a robust 9% estimated RAB CAGR and highly visible output-based regulatory incentives .

However, cash conversion lags materially , with Elia suffering from deeply negative free cash flows, expecting no positive inflection until 2034 due to capex , and offering a meagre 1.6% dividend yield, starkly underperforming Terna and Redeia which offer yields above 5% .

The valuation disconnect is notable, with Elia's sub-10% RAB premium trailing Terna's 25% premium, reflecting investor concern over funding needs , despite Elia benefiting from highly visible cost-plus remuneration models in Belgium and Germany, shielding earnings from direct power-price volatility .

The sector tailwinds are universal, with the European energy transition mandating massive infrastructure upgrades to integrate offshore wind, lifting capital deployment across all peers , while the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence data centres drives accelerating electricity demand, necessitating further grid reinforcement . The material risk is that Elia's sector-leading growth is entirely dependent on continuous debt and equity issuance; if capital markets tighten, peers with stronger free cash flow generation like Terna or National Grid will rapidly overtake Elia's momentum position .

Activist presence and governance concerns

The governance structure appears stable following recent transitions, with Pieter De Crem assuming the Board chairmanship in February 2026 following the sudden passing of former Chairman Geert Versnick in late 2025 , and three new independent directors appointed in May 2025, broadening the board's financial and operational expertise for a four-year term .

The culture and retention metrics are strong, with 2025 turnover rate decreasing to 5.2% from 5.7% and a 96.6% employee health rate consistently beating the 95% internal target .

However, a potential governance weakness exists, as the executive suite is heavily recycled from internal ranks; a lack of external hires might limit fresh strategic thinking during this critical capex phase .

Material risks and bear case synthesis

The primary structural risk centers on perpetual capital market dependency , with the entire growth profile depending on continuous access to massive external funding, where an unexpected tightening in debt markets could halt the RAB expansion , while the entire growth thesis represents a fragile house of cards built on perpetual capital market access; if interest rates spike unexpectedly or supply chains break down, the massive capex plan becomes unfinanceable, stalling RAB growth and triggering immediate, severe equity dilution .

The supply-chain risk is severe and escalating, with transformer lead times drastically extended from 6-8 months to 3-4 years across the industry, while high-voltage transformer prices experienced staggering 400-900% increases from 2020 baseline levels , and supply chain scarcity forcing the Princess Elisabeth Island budget to double, triggering severe redesign and multi-year delays .

The regulatory risk involves affordability pushback , with the massive cost of grid expansion creating growing public and political pushback regarding energy affordability , potentially forcing regulators to cap allowed returns despite rising inflation and debt costs due to political intervention . The earnings quality risk is material, with the entire earnings growth narrative representing an accounting illusion masked by IFRS capitalisation rules; while reported EPS hits double-digits, the underlying business burns billions in cash annually, meaning the "beats" are merely non-cash interest capitalisation while real shareholder value is diluted by constant equity raises .

The execution risk manifests in severe supply constraints and soaring costs colliding with a softening macroeconomic demand picture; if regulatory returns fail to match inflation, the required €31.6 billion capex plan becomes unfinanceable, stalling the entire super-cycle . The international M&A risk has already materialized, with the acquisition of a 35.1% stake in US-based energyRe Giga facing regulatory hurdles, triggering a €99.1 million non-cash impairment , demonstrating that strategic M&A outside core regulated European networks consistently underperforms initial expectations and destroys shareholder value .
