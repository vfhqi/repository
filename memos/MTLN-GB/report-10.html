§9 — Risk Assessment + Sceptical Synthesis

J-front verdict: Single largest risk is energy-market regulatory reversal + aluminium price decline occurring simultaneously — a plausible 2-3Y scenario that could compress group EBITDA 25-35% [#J]

  • Downside scenario — base bear case: Macro regime change — Greek energy prices normalise to long-run equilibrium (net pool price -30% from 2024 levels), LME aluminium declines to $2,000-2,100/t (from ~$2,400 current), and SES margin compresses on competitive pressure [#A]
  • EPS downside: if Power segment EBITDA declines 30% and Aluminium 20%, group EBITDA falls ~25%; applying 10-15% valuation de-rating (regulatory risk premium), EPS downside is 35-45% [#I]

  • Peer anchor: European integrated utility/commodity conglomerates in a price-normalisation scenario (cf. Verbund AG post-2023, Salzgitter aluminium/steel division) have experienced 30-50% EBITDA compression in comparable cycles [#A]

  • Downside scenario — severe bear case: Regulatory + cyclical + execution triple fault — windfall levy reimposed, LME aluminium falls to $1,800/t, and a major SES EPC project suffers cost overrun requiring a large provision [#I]

  • Fair value downside: in this scenario, EV/EBITDA de-rates to 5-6x (Athens-peer multiple), implying 45-55% downside from current London pricing [#I]

  • Margin compression: High operating leverage — Power segment is high-fixed-cost (generation assets, grid balancing obligations); EBITDA margin is highly sensitive to price change with limited variable cost offset [#A]

  • Peer anchor: high-fixed-cost European generators (Engie, RWE) show 2-3x revenue EBITDA leverage; MTLN-GB's Power segment likely exhibits similar characteristics [#A]

  • Valuation re-rate: Real risk — current pricing appears to embed a partial re-rating to London-peer multiples; any earnings disappointment that triggers a reversion to Athens-peer multiple applies a double-whammy (earnings down + multiple down) [#I]

  • Probability — bear correct: ~25-30% probability that the base bear case (energy normalisation + aluminium pressure) plays out materially within 24M [#I]

  • Probability — bear partially correct: ~45-55% probability that at least one of the two core macro drivers (Power pricing, LME) disappoints materially within 24M [#I]

  • Single largest risk: Greek energy regulatory reversal — this is the highest-impact, politically plausible single event; unlike LME price (global market), Greek regulatory action is a concentrated political risk that MTLN-GB cannot diversify away [#J]

  • Reverse-engineered bear: What would have to be true for the stock to halve? — Power EBITDA halves (regulatory + price collapse), Aluminium turns loss-making (LME <$1,700), SES has a major contract write-off; each individually unlikely, collectively possible if a Greek sovereign or energy crisis reprises [#I]

  • Invalidation trigger #1: Regulatory — Greek government reimposing windfall energy levy or introducing price cap mechanism on Power generators; watch for RAE regulatory consultation announcements [#J]

  • Monitor: Greek energy ministry announcements, RAE consultation documents, EU DG Energy policy on energy market intervention

  • Invalidation trigger #2: Aluminium — LME aluminium price sustained below $2,000/t for >2 quarters; watch LME forward curve [#J]

  • Monitor: LME monthly average, Chinese aluminium production data, US tariff policy on aluminium imports

  • Invalidation trigger #3: EPC execution — SES segment margin contraction >300bps in consecutive half-years, or disclosure of a major contract provision >€50m [#J]

  • Monitor: SES segment margin in H1/H2 reporting; order book backlog quality disclosures

  • Invalidation trigger #4: Governance — founder health/succession event, related-party transaction requiring shareholder vote, or audit committee disclosure of accounting concern [#J]

  • Confidence in bear thesis being materially correct: MEDIUM — the macro bear thesis is structurally coherent and is not obviously priced in, but requires a specific combination of macro conditions that are plausible but not base-case within the 12-24M holding period [#J]
