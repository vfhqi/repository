2. NII Guidance vs Delivery

Net interest income is Avanza's single largest revenue line, contributing approximately 40% of group operating income in 2024.

Rate environment context: The Swedish Riksbank cut its policy rate by 100bps during 2024 into early 2025. This was widely expected to be a significant headwind for Avanza's NII.

What management communicated: Management acknowledged the NII headwind from lower rates but expressed confidence that volume growth in deposits and lending would partially offset the rate impact. They did not provide explicit NII guidance in SEK terms but framed the outlook as "manageable" given volume tailwinds.

What actually happened: Full-year 2025 NII remained broadly stable versus 2024 -- a strong outcome given the 100bps rate cut. The key offsets were:
- Growth in internal deposits moving onto the balance sheet
- Increased lending volumes
- Higher average deposit balances as savings capital grew

Verdict: Management was arguably conservative in framing NII as facing a headwind. The actual outcome -- stability despite 100bps of cuts -- was better than the market feared. This was a credibility-enhancing outcome. The Riksbank is now guiding for stable rates in 2026, which should provide a more benign backdrop.

Forward context: With the Riksbank signalling unchanged rates unless inflation outlook shifts materially, NII in 2026 should benefit from continued volume growth without rate drag. This is a tailwind.

3. Customer Growth Execution

Long-term target: Avanza's strategic framework targets savings capital growth of 15% per annum through 2030, with customer acquisition as a key input.

2025 delivery:
- Net new customers: 171,000 (total base: 2,242,700 at year-end)
- Net inflow: SEK 54bn
- Savings capital at year-end: SEK 1,079bn (+13% YoY)
- Market share of Swedish savings market: 8.3%

Assessment: The 171,000 net adds represent solid growth for a platform that already has ~2.2m customers in a country of ~10.5m people. However, the pace is clearly decelerating from the pandemic-era surge (Avanza added 300,000+ customers in 2020-2021). More importantly, the 13% savings capital growth fell short of the 15% annual target. The shortfall was driven by weaker market performance in late 2025 rather than customer acquisition failure.

Quarterly trajectory (2025):
- Q3 2025: 41,000 net new customers (strongest quarter)
- December 2025: 10,300 net new customers (typical seasonal slowdown)

Structural view: Avanza is approaching saturation risks in Sweden. At 2.24m customers, they serve roughly one in five Swedish adults. Future growth increasingly depends on:
1. Deepening wallet share with existing customers (AUM per customer)
2. New product verticals (Private Banking, occupational pensions, discretionary portfolio management)
3. Potentially international expansion (CEO has stated this is deferred -- "focus remains on Sweden")

The customer acquisition engine remains healthy but the company is transitioning from a pure-growth phase to a growth-plus-monetisation phase.

2025 revenue mix observations:

Full-year 2025 operating income of SEK 4,495m (+15% YoY). The drivers by line:

  1. Net brokerage income -- strong. Benefited from higher trading activity and rising equity markets. Brokerage is cyclical but Avanza's market share gains in trading provide structural support.

  2. Net currency-related income -- star performer. Foreign securities trading reached 30% of brokerage-generating turnover in Q4 2025, the second-highest absolute level ever. This line has been growing structurally as Swedish retail investors diversify internationally. It is becoming a meaningful and more durable revenue stream.

  3. Fund commissions, net -- growing but margin-compressed. Fund volumes grew with AUM, but the average margin is declining as customers shift toward lower-fee index funds and Swedish index funds in particular carry lower margins. This is a classic volume-vs-margin dynamic. Net effect: commissions grew but underperformed topline growth.

  4. Net interest income -- resilient. As discussed in Section 2, NII held broadly stable despite 100bps of rate cuts. Volume growth offset rate impact. This was the biggest positive surprise relative to expectations entering the year.

  5. Other income -- declined. Not material at ~5% of the mix but directionally negative.

Revenue diversification assessment: The revenue mix is healthier than it was five years ago. The growing FX income line provides a natural hedge against periods of lower domestic trading activity. The NII base provides stability. Fund commissions offer AUM-linked growth. The key risk remains brokerage cyclicality -- in a severe bear market, both brokerage and FX income would contract while NII would face customer withdrawal pressure.

The 2030 target of SEK 2,000bn savings capital is ambitious and requires roughly doubling the current base in five years.
