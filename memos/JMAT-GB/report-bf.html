EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

Johnson Matthey is transitioning into a streamlined, cash-focused business centered on circular PGMs and emissions control, having divested Catalyst Technologies to Honeywell. CEO Liam Condon launched a strategic refresh to create a highly focused, lean and cash-generative business , simplifying what was historically a complex conglomerate into a three-segment operator anchored by Clean Air autocatalysts , PGM Services refining and recycling , and nascent Hydrogen Technologies . The group occupies highly advantaged positions within a disciplined oligopoly structure, though it faces structural headwinds from the long-term decline of internal combustion engines and severe near-term setbacks in hydrogen commercialization.

Business Model and Strategic Positioning

Air operates on point-in-time product sales of bespoke catalysts to global automotive original equipment manufacturers. Pricing incorporates a pass-through of precious metal costs, meaning the group is insulated from direct commodity price volatility. Revenue model resilience is secured by metal pass-through clauses and multi-year OEM contracts , though the group remains structurally exposed to terminal ICE decline.
