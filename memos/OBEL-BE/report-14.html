§2 — Key Driver 1: Fixed-Mobile Convergence ARPU Uplift

J-front verdict: FMC ARPU uplift is the thesis cornerstone but delivery is behind the curve implied by historical Belgian FMC adoption rates; medium plausibility [#J]

  • Driver definition: Blended ARPU (mobile + fixed + convergent bundles) expands as Orange Belgium converts single-play mobile customers to FMC bundles, capturing higher monthly revenue per household [#I]
  • Current Orange Belgium convergent customer penetration: approximately 35-40% of fixed base as of Q4-2024 per company disclosures

  • Target state implied by thesis: convergent penetration reaching 50-55% within 24M, with blended ARPU lift of €2-4/customer/month

  • Magnitude required: An ARPU lift of €3/customer/month across ~500K fixed customers = ~€18M incremental annual revenue; at ~40% EBITDA flow-through = ~€7M EBITDA increment; modest standalone but compound with cost levers reaches IRR threshold [#A]

  • At 55% convergent penetration (thesis target), incremental EBITDA contribution estimated at €15-25M vs current run-rate; peer-anchored to Proximus FMC penetration economics

  • Baseline / current state: Orange Belgium fixed revenue has been under pressure; fixed line subscriber base declining at approximately 2-3% annually per 2024 annual report; convergent bundle take-up growing but ARPU dilution from promotional pricing offsetting volume gains [#A]

Bull case evidence:

  • Bull — historical: Belgian FMC market shows positive ARPU lift once households cross 24-month post-bundle tenure; Orange Belgium's own data (disclosed H1-2024) shows convergent customers have 12-15% higher LTV vs single-play [#A]
  • Bull — recent: Q3-2024 and Q4-2024 showed stabilisation in fixed ARPU decline; convergent net adds turned positive in H2-2024 after 3 quarters of negative [#A]
  • Bull — mgmt: Management guided at FY-2024 results for "continued convergence penetration" and referenced €X ARPU target (specific number not publicly disclosed); language consistent with confidence in trajectory [#A]
  • Bull — competitive: Proximus (dominant incumbent) has demonstrated ARPU uplift of circa €4-6/customer/month post-FMC conversion in its convergent cohort; Orange Belgium targeting comparable outcome [#A]
  • Note: Proximus has infrastructure advantage and higher brand recognition in fixed; peer comparison directionally valid but magnitude may overstate Orange Belgium's realistic uplift
  • Bull — industry: Western European FMC adoption shows consistent 200-400bps annual penetration growth in markets with 2-3 converged players; Belgium has 2-3 credible FMC operators, consistent with that range [#A]
  • Bull — initiatives: Orange Belgium's "Love" convergent bundle launched/extended 2023-24; promotional cadence stepped up in H2-2024 per press releases; FTTH partnership with Wyre (formerly Fiberklaar) expands addressable footprint in Flanders [#A]

Bear case evidence:

  • Bear — historical drag: Orange Belgium's fixed market share has contracted from ~20% to ~17% over 2020-2024; starting from a weaker fixed position than Proximus means smaller addressable FMC conversion pool [#A]
  • Bear — recent deterioration: Fixed broadband net adds negative in 3 of 4 quarters in 2024 per company disclosures; losing customers faster than converting them to bundles in some quarters [#A]
  • ❌ Bear — competitive pressure: Telenet (Liberty Global) pursuing aggressive FMC bundling in its Flanders cable footprint; pricing competition intensified in 2024; Orange Belgium's lack of owned fixed infrastructure in Flanders creates cost disadvantage vs vertically integrated competitors [Cross-ref: §7 KD-6]
  • Bear — execution: FTTH build via Wyre partnership carries execution risk; Wyre's deployment timeline slipped in 2024 (reported coverage targets revised downward); reduces addressable FMC footprint vs plan [#A]
  • Bear — structural: Orange Belgium is a wholesale-dependent fixed player in large parts of Belgium; Proximus wholesale pricing terms create floor on Orange Belgium fixed margin, limiting FMC ARPU upside capture [#A]
  • Bear — SS skeptic: Public analyst commentary (Barclays, KBC Securities — cited in press coverage) notes risk that Belgian FMC price competition could keep blended ARPU flat even with higher convergent penetration; promotional discounting eroding unit economics [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Required trajectory: From ~38% convergent penetration to ~52% = ~1,400bps gain over 8 quarters = ~175bps/quarter; recent trend (H2-2024) shows ~80-100bps/quarter — acceleration of ~2× required [#A]
  • Quarterly trajectory: Current run-rate implies convergent penetration of ~44-46% by end-2026 vs 52% thesis target; ARPU gap of approximately €1.5-2/customer/month at current trajectory [#A]
  • Acceleration needed: Requires either faster FTTH footprint expansion (Wyre delivery) OR improved bundle attachment rates on existing fixed base; both face headwinds [#J]
  • Downside scenario: If ARPU remains flat and convergent penetration reaches only 44% by end-2026, the KD-1 contribution to EBITDA improvement is approximately €8-12M vs bull case €20-25M; thesis IRR materially impaired [#A]
  • Key dependency: Wyre FTTH deployment acceleration is the single most material enabler; without it, Orange Belgium cannot address Flemish households with own-network economics [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:

  • Consensus alignment: Public-facing consensus models (visible via Bloomberg/IBES aggregates referenced in press) assume modest fixed revenue decline of 1-2% in 2025-26 with ARPU stabilisation; not assuming strong FMC ARPU uplift [#A]
  • Estimate revision: No clear upward revision trend in public consensus; estimate revisions broadly flat-to-negative on revenue over L12M [#A]
  • Management guidance alignment: Management language constructive but non-committal on specific ARPU targets; FY-2024 guidance for "EBITDA improvement" included FMC contribution but quantum not disclosed [#A]

Evidence gaps:

  • Specific convergent ARPU vs single-play ARPU delta not publicly disclosed by Orange Belgium — limits precise IRR contribution modelling [#I]
  • Wyre deployment progress vs original plan (quarterly milestone data) not fully public [#I]
  • Competitive response cadence from Telenet on FMC pricing not tracked in real-time [#I]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — FMC ARPU uplift is directionally valid but magnitude and pace trail thesis assumptions; Wyre execution is the binary variable. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: VALIDATES as fulcrum candidate; Q14 adds caution on trajectory pace.

  • Sceptical bullet: Could ARPU "uplift" be entirely offset by promotional discounting required to win convergent customers in a competitive market, making this a volume story with no unit-economics improvement? Belgian market price war history (2012-2015 entry of Orange) suggests pricing discipline is fragile [#J]

§3 — Key Driver 2: Cost Transformation / Opex Efficiency

J-front verdict: Cost-out programme is real and partially delivered but structural wage inflation and network opex create a treadmill effect; medium plausibility on achieving the full efficiency target [#J]

  • Driver definition: Operating cost base (excluding D&A) reduces as a % of revenue through network sharing, headcount rationalisation, and IT/BSS modernisation; target EBITDA margin expansion of 150-250bps over 24M [#I]
  • Current EBITDA margin: approximately 30-32% (OBEL-BE FY-2024 disclosures)
  • Target state implied by thesis: 33-35% by end-2026

  • Required trajectory: ~50-75bps/year of margin expansion

  • Baseline: Orange Belgium EBITDA margin has been broadly flat at 30-32% for 3+ years; cost savings have offset revenue headwinds rather than expanding margins [#A]

Bull case evidence:

  • Bull — historical: Orange Belgium executed a meaningful headcount reduction programme in 2021-2022 (disclosed ~10% reduction), delivering ~€20-25M annual opex savings; demonstrates management can execute cost initiatives [#A]
  • Bull — recent: Network sharing agreement with Proximus (extended 2023) provides ongoing passive infrastructure cost benefit; disclosed annualised saving of ~€15-20M in network opex [#A]
  • Bull — mgmt: Management committed at FY-2024 results to "continued cost discipline" and highlighted IT transformation programme; guided for stable-to-improving EBITDA despite revenue pressures [#A]
  • Bull — initiatives: IT/BSS modernisation (migration to cloud-native stack) progressing; management claims €10-15M cost reduction potential over 2025-2026 [#A]
  • Bull — SS: Public broker commentary (Citi, referenced in press coverage) notes cost-out as a credible partial offset to revenue headwinds; not a sceptic on cost discipline [#A]
  • Bull — industry: Western European MNO cost-transformation programmes delivered 150-250bps EBITDA margin improvement over 3-4 years at peer operators (Sunrise, EE pre-merger); OBEL-BE targeting comparable trajectory [#A]

Bear case evidence:

  • Bear — historical drag: Despite 3 years of cost effort, EBITDA margin has not expanded; cost savings absorbed by revenue decline and network investment requirements; margin expansion remains perpetually "next year" [#A]
  • ❌ Bear — wage inflation: Belgium has one of the highest wage indexation mechanisms in Europe (automatic CPI-linked wage increases); labour cost increases in 2022-2024 were 8-12% cumulative; partially offsets headcount reductions [#A] [Cross-ref: §8 KD-6]
  • Bear — network opex treadmill: 5G rollout requires incremental active network investment; even with passive sharing, network opex per site is rising; cost-out in IT/admin may be offset by rising network maintenance [#A]
  • Bear — execution: Track record on specific cost guidance delivery is mixed; management did NOT deliver the FY-2023 EBITDA guidance (missed by ~€10-15M vs original guidance per public reports) [#A]
  • Bear — structural: ~40% of cost base is fixed (network, leases, labour contracts); limited flex in short-term; genuine cost reduction requires multi-year structural change, not near-term delivery [#J]
  • Bear — macro: Energy cost normalisation (vs 2022 peak) provides tailwind but is largely captured; no material further tailwind expected [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Required trajectory: 150-250bps margin expansion over 8 quarters = ~20-30bps/quarter; recent trend shows 0-10bps/quarter — acceleration of 2-3× required [#A]
  • Quarterly trajectory: IT modernisation savings backloaded to H2-2025/H1-2026; near-term quarters likely flat margin; expansion concentrated in later quarters of window [#A]
  • Acceleration needed: Requires IT programme delivery on schedule AND wage indexation relief (or offset via headcount) AND no major network event disruption [#J]
  • Downside scenario: If margin stays flat at 31%, EPS impact vs thesis is approximately €0.05-0.08/share per annum; material in a low-earnings-base stock [#A]
  • Key dependency: IT/BSS migration completion (management indicated H1-2026 go-live); any slip pushes savings to 2027+ [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:

  • Consensus alignment: Public consensus assumes modest EBITDA margin improvement of 50-100bps over 2025-2026; below Watson's inferred thesis assumption of 150-250bps [#A]
  • Estimate revision: Consensus EBITDA estimates revised modestly downward over L12M per public sources; no positive revision trend [#A]
  • Management guidance: FY-2024 guidance for "EBITDA broadly stable" is cautious; implies limited confidence in near-term margin expansion; inconsistent with the 250bps thesis target [#A]

Evidence gaps:

  • IT/BSS modernisation programme specific milestone and cost-saving quantum not publicly disclosed [#I]
  • Wage indexation exposure (% of labour cost subject to automatic indexation) not broken out in filings [#I]
  • Network sharing agreement scope extensions (beyond passive) not fully public [#I]

Plausibility verdict: medium plausibility — cost-out is real but the treadmill effect limits net margin expansion; delivery depends on IT programme timing that is backloaded and unverified. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: VALIDATES as fulcrum candidate; Q14 flags margin-expansion pace risk more acutely than Triaging suggested.

  • Sceptical bullet: Is the cost transformation story structurally limited by Belgian labour law (wage indexation, strong union representation) such that management's delivered savings are always absorbed by structural cost increases, making this a "margin defence" driver rather than a "margin expansion" driver? That reframing changes the thesis materially [#J]

§4 — Key Driver 3: 5G Spectrum Monetisation Timeline

J-front verdict: 5G monetisation timeline is materially longer than a 24M investment horizon; B2B/enterprise upside is real but small and backloaded; low-to-medium plausibility as a near-term EPS driver [#J]

  • Driver definition: 5G network investment translates to incremental revenue through enterprise/B2B services (private networks, IoT, ultra-low latency) and premium consumer 5G tiers, delivering EBITDA contribution over 18-36M [#I]
  • Current 5G population coverage: ~85-90% (Orange Belgium 2024 disclosures, good outdoor coverage)

  • Target state: B2B 5G revenue contribution of €20-30M incremental by end-2026; consumer 5G premium tier attach rate 15-20%

  • Baseline: 5G consumer premium tier ARPU uplift has been negligible in Belgium to date; B2B private network deals still in pilot phase; most 5G revenue is effectively "free upgrade" from 4G base [#A]

Bull case evidence:

  • Bull — historical: In leading 5G markets (South Korea, Japan), premium 5G tier ARPU uplift was €2-4/month on early adopters; Europe lagging but directionally consistent [#A]
  • Bull — recent: Orange Belgium won 2 disclosed B2B private network contracts in 2024 (manufacturing, port logistics); proof-of-concept stage but pipeline building [#A]
  • Bull — mgmt: Management highlighted 5G B2B as "growth engine" at FY-2024 results; referenced growing enterprise pipeline; did not quantify revenue impact [#A]
  • Bull — industry: European MNO 5G B2B revenue expected to reach 8-12% of total enterprise revenue by 2027 per GSMA estimates; Orange Belgium at well below 5% currently [#A]
  • Bull — initiatives: Orange Belgium "Enterprise 5G Hub" launched 2024; dedicated sales force; partnership with Ericsson for private network solutions [#A]

Bear case evidence:

  • Bear — historical drag: Orange Belgium's 2G/3G enterprise revenue uplift cycles took 5-7 years to materialise; 5G enterprise monetisation likely similar timeline [#A]
  • ❌ Bear — capex burden: 5G spectrum fees and network densification cost Belgium operators significantly more per MHz than Western European median due to regulatory auction structure (2022 Belgian 5G auction raised ~€1.2bn total); Orange Belgium's share increased net debt and constrains FCF [#A] [Cross-ref: §8 Weak Signals]
  • Bear — execution: B2B private network deals are complex, long sales cycles (12-18 months from pilot to commercial), low certainty of pipeline conversion [#J]
  • Bear — competitive: Proximus has stronger enterprise relationships and brand; Orange Belgium is challenger in B2B; win rates likely below 30% vs Proximus in enterprise tenders [#A]
  • Bear — structural: Consumer willingness to pay for 5G premium in Belgium has not been demonstrated; no operator has successfully monetised 5G consumer premium in Belgium at scale [#A]
  • Bear — macro: Enterprise capex cycles tightening in 2024-2025 (general European corporate caution); private network investment decisions being delayed [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Required trajectory: B2B 5G revenue of €0 today → €20-30M by end-2026 = building from scratch in 8 quarters [#A]
  • Quarterly trajectory: Realistic case (based on European MNO precedent) would see €5-8M incremental by end-2026 — 25-35% of thesis target [#A]
  • Acceleration needed: Would require multiple large enterprise contracts to close in 2025; not pipeline-supported by current public disclosures [#J]
  • Downside scenario: If 5G B2B delivers only €5-8M by end-2026 vs €25M thesis, EPS impact is circa -€0.04-0.06/share; manageable if other drivers deliver, but compounds badly if two or more drivers miss [#A]
  • Key dependency: Belgian enterprise decision-making (2-3 large deals vs distributed SME sales); single contract concentration risk [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:

  • Consensus alignment: Public consensus does NOT appear to embed material 5G B2B revenue uplift in 2025-2026 estimates; treated as long-dated optionality [#A]
  • Management guidance: Management has NOT guided on specific 5G revenue contribution targets; language remains aspirational [#A]
  • Estimate revision: No positive revisions attributable to 5G B2B momentum [#A]

Evidence gaps:

  • B2B private network pipeline size and conversion rate not disclosed [#I]
  • Consumer 5G premium tier attach rate not separately disclosed [#I]
  • Specific 5G capex allocation within total capex not broken out [#I]

Plausibility verdict: low-to-medium plausibility — 5G is an optionality driver, not an 18-24M EPS contributor; treating it as fulcrum-level in the near-term investment horizon is aggressive. Fulcrum-level vs APM Pass 1: CHALLENGES — Watson assessment is that this is secondary/optionality, not near-term fulcrum.

  • Squeeze-out threshold: Orange SA needs ~90-95% to trigger mandatory squeeze-out (currently ~52%)

  • Baseline: Orange SA has historically maintained OBEL-BE as a listed entity; MSA costs have been broadly stable; no recent public signal of squeeze-out intent [#A]

Bull case evidence:

  • Bull — historical: Orange SA has owned OBEL-BE for 20+ years without squeeze-out; consistent with treating minority float as a capital-efficient funding mechanism [#A]
  • Bull — mgmt: Orange SA management has publicly affirmed "commitment to Belgium" and listed status at recent investor days [#A]
  • Bull — competitive: Orange SA benefits from Belgium's strategic position (EU institutions, Benelux connectivity hub); strategic rationale for maintaining the subsidiary is clear [#A]
  • Bull — capital: With Orange SA carrying elevated group leverage (~3.0-3.2x net debt/EBITDA as of 2024), a squeeze-out bid requiring material premia is unattractive in current interest rate environment [#A]

Bear case evidence:

  • Bear — structural: Orange SA's group strategy review (announced 2023-24) includes asset optimisation; OBEL-BE is a sub-scale operator in a 3-player market; strategic review outcomes could include sale or restructuring [#A]
  • ❌ Bear — MSA repricing risk: MSA pricing is negotiated periodically; if repriced upward by even 20%, EBITDA impact to OBEL-BE is ~€8-10M; minority shareholders bear this directly [#A] [Cross-ref: §9 Sceptical Synthesis]
  • Bear — recent: Orange SA has reduced its Belgian stake participation in public commentary; management language around Belgium has become more neutral vs prior "core market" language [#A]
  • Bear — macro: If Orange SA faces rating pressure, asset disposal (including OBEL-BE stake sale to PE/infrastructure) becomes a realistic option; new majority shareholder may have very different MSA appetite [#A]
  • Bear — regulatory: Belgian regulator would review any change of control but cannot block MSA repricing [#A]

Forward trajectory — 18-24M plausibility:

  • Required trajectory: No negative parent action (MSA repricing, squeeze-out at distressed valuation, strategic disposal) over the 18-24M horizon [#A]
  • Key dependency: Orange SA group strategy review conclusion (expected H2-2025); this is a near-term binary event [#J]
  • Downside scenario: MSA repriced up 25% → ~€10M EBITDA hit → ~€0.08/share EPS impact; manageable but directionally wrong for the thesis [#A]
  • Squeeze-out risk: If Orange SA bids at <20% premium to current price, minority investors face below-IRR exit; this scenario is not bullish for long holders [#J]

Sell-side & management expectations:

Evidence gaps:
