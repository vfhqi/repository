EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF) 39 findings

Hafnia operates the world's largest product and chemical tanker fleet, commercially managing over 200 vessels across four core size segments, generating revenue through a commercial pool platform that captures spot market volatility while minimizing ballast legs and securing bulk purchasing efficiencies. The company's business foundation combines a fully integrated platform spanning technical management, commercial chartering, pool management, and bunkering services with a premium-quality owned fleet averaging 9.7 years, well below the industry's 14.1-year benchmark. Financial performance reached exceptional levels in recent quarters, with Q1 2026 net profit hitting a historically high $179.7 million driven by geopolitical shocks that fundamentally reshaped trade routes, boosting Western cargo distances and tonne-mile expansion. The strategic position reflects extreme market fragmentation where the top-5 players control less than 15% of global capacity and Hafnia commands a leading 4.3% share, while consolidation initiatives including a 13.97% strategic stake in TORM plc signal management's conviction that scale advantages translate into durable competitive moats. However, roughly 74% of the fleet operates in the volatile spot market, creating severe earnings cyclicality that hinges entirely on sustained geopolitical disruptions and chokepoint closures supporting elevated freight rates.
