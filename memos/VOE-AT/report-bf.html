EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

voestalpine operates as a downstream-focused steel and technology group specializing in customized materials and engineered system solutions across four operational divisions: Steel Division (high-tech flat products), High Performance Metals (tool steel and aerospace alloys), Metal Engineering (railway infrastructure systems), and Metal Forming (automotive components) . The group's strategic pivot toward high-value processing segments mechanically insulates margins from commodity steel cyclicality, though persistent European automotive weakness necessitates painful structural restructuring of German assets while management aggressively expands into high-growth markets like India and railway infrastructure .

Value-chain positioning and power dynamics

voestalpine restructured its upstream footprint by selling an 80% stake in its Texas HBI plant to ArcelorMittal while retaining a 20% stake secured by a long-term contract for 420,000 tonnes of HBI annually, fully covering the high-grade metallic feedstock required for Phase 1 of greentec steel . Strategic downstream assets represent approximately 45% of voestalpine's business profile, with the Metal Engineering Division leading globally with a 35% market share in premium switches and turnout technology, while High Performance Metals utilizes advanced smelting in Kapfenberg to supply critical nickel-base and titanium aerospace alloys integrated with diagnostic sensors and monitoring software to provide high-margin predictive maintenance solutions . Tailored engineering contracts in automotive and rail allow voestalpine to pass through raw material cost changes, with extremely high qualification standards in aerospace and rail turnouts creating high customer switching costs . Power is shifting downstream toward specialized engineering segments due to intense customization and digital integrations, with digital diagnostic rail systems creating long-term customer lock-in and high-value turnouts and aerospace forgings capturing premium margins that uncouple voestalpine from commodity cycles .

Customer diversification and end-market exposure

The premium automotive sector remains the largest end market accounting for 30% of total group revenue, with energy infrastructure representing 16% and railway infrastructure systems generating 15% . voestalpine employs a tiered contract framework to balance pricing stability and spot market responsiveness, with annual and semiannual contracts governing the majority of sales to premium automotive manufacturers while approximately 40%

of the steel division's sales are negotiated quarterly . High qualification barriers in specialized downstream niches insulate voestalpine from low-cost import substitution, with aerospace metallurgy requiring extensive material traceability and multi-year customer audits, and the group's specialized alloys integrated directly into critical civil aviation production lines . Structural demand weakness in the European automotive sector continues to pressure downstream volumes, with persistent profit warnings from major European car manufacturers restricting component call-off rates and the Metal Forming division's automotive components segment recording low capacity utilization .

Decarbonization transition and long-term paradigm fit

The €1.5 billion greentec steel program aims to replace two coal-based blast furnaces with electric arc furnaces by 2027, targeting a 30% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2029 compared to 2019, with management already investing approximately 60% of the budget as of June 2026 . New steel safeguard measures effective July 1, 2026 cut import quotas by 50%, while the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism effective January 2026 establishes equal carbon pricing for imported steel, shifting procurement toward lower-carbon European producers and limiting cheap non-EU imports . Transitioning to EAF routes intensifies competition for high-quality scrap risking raw material cost inflation, with high scrap ratios potentially affecting mechanical properties of advanced steel grades, though the 20% Texas HBI stake secures 420,000 tonnes of direct reduced iron annually bypassing scrap deficits . Downstream niche expansion allows voestalpine to exceed stagnant European GDP growth through specialized engineering, with management aiming to grow sales beyond €20 billion by 2030 up from €15.1 billion in FY25/26 .
