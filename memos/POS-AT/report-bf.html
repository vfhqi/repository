EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

business model centers on highly specialized civil engineering capabilities rather than commoditized general contracting. The AT/CH home market segment generates approximately 49% of group revenues and contributes a disproportionate 68% of EBIT, reflecting superior pricing power in Austria's stable public infrastructure markets . Proprietary patented systems like "Slab Track Austria," co-developed with national railway operator ÖBB, create un-replicable technical moats that shield the backlog from general builder

entry . High prequalification barriers for mega-infrastructure projects demand verified balance sheet capacity, deep track records, and significant bank guarantees that exclude under-capitalized mid-sized contractors . Access to qualified technical personnel represents the single most critical industry constraint, with public clients prioritizing bidders with permanent localized workforces and tier-one operators enforcing strict no-team, no-bid policies .

The industry structure presents both opportunity and vulnerability through dependency on public funding timelines. Eastern European public authorities are committing exceptional long-term budgets, including Poland's €48bn for rail expansions through 2029 and Austria's €19.7bn scheduled through 2030 . The looming 2026 RRF eligible expenditure deadline creates a powerful "use it or lose it" incentive that accelerates project tendering but compresses delivery windows, boosting demand for scale-advantaged contractors . Despite massive tender scale, CEE markets remain structurally fragmented with market leaders controlling less than 10% of national volumes and Strabag holding just 1.1% of Romania's total construction market . Large-scale project success requires robust contractual pricing safeguards, with Poland's GDDKiA raising price indexation caps to 15% and successful builders combining indexation with targeted materials hedging to insulate project margins . 33 35

The investment paradigm positions PORR as a levered play on European public infrastructure acceleration rather than a structural compounder. The company lacks the self-reinforcing competitive advantages and pricing power of true economic moats, instead relying on heroic management execution to capture episodic margin expansion during cyclical public funding surges. Technical barriers and specialized capabilities provide temporary protection
