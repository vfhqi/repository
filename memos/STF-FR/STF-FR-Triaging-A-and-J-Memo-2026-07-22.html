B.1 Business Description 10 findings
Business Description -- A plain-English description of what the company does, how it makes money, and its primary market.
1.
What STEF does. Europe's leading temperature-controlled food logistics network, operating 325 multi-temperature platforms spanning 15.24m cubic metres of storage across three temperature ranges, -25C to +18C
2.
How it makes money. Two divisions, STEF France (nine specialised business units by product/temperature) and STEF International (seven countries), moving and storing chilled, frozen and ambient food for over 24,000 customers
3.
Ownership model. Asset-heavy, owns real estate via IMMOSTEF and operates nearly 4,000 rigid trucks and tractor units, a structural contrast with the asset-light contract-logistics peers in its own sector
4.
Financial scale. FY25 revenue EUR 5,119.5m, +6.6% reported / +4.7% like-for-like; operating margin compressed to 3.4% from 4.8% a year earlier
5.
Segment mix. STEF France EUR 2,458.6m (+2.5% reported, 6.0% operating margin, flat year-on-year); STEF International EUR 1,973.6m (+9.7% reported, now 44-46% of business-line revenue, operating margin collapsed to 1.2% from 4.4%)
6.
Balance sheet character. Net debt EUR 1,533.2m, gearing 1.17x versus a 2.0x covenant ceiling; free cash flow negative for a second consecutive year, -EUR 66.7m in FY25
7.
Customer base. No single client exceeds 5% of group revenue; national key accounts 38%, middle market 35%, international accounts 27%; named relationships include Perfetti Van Melle, BEL Portugal and Carrefour
8.
Ownership structure. Concert party controls 74.10% of capital and 82.98% of voting rights via double-voting shares; employees and management combined hold over 72%, an unusually concentrated, activist-proofed register for a listed European logistics name
9.
Investment style read. Best characterised as a capital-intensive, family-controlled compounder mid-integration on five recent acquisitions, not a pure cyclical and not a pure event-driven special situation
10.
Non-core asset. Owns La Meridionale, a passenger-and-cargo maritime subsidiary running the Corsica public-service concession (2023-2029), historically loss-making on its non-concession routes
