Section 3 – External Change: Value-Chain Forces (Demand, Competition, Disruption, Regulation, Supply, Cycle, Macro) 59 findings

Section summary
  1. customer demand is experiencing a bifurcated pulse with near-term recycling and waste recovery delays offsetting greenfield DRS deployment volumes in Collection.
  2. the competitive landscape remains highly concentrated in reverse vending but faces intensifying price competition at the low end from nimbler entrants.
  3. technology disruption is limited but emerging digital deposit alternatives and alternative sorting formats threaten to reduce total RVM capacity.
  4. regulatory drivers are highly supportive but plagued by execution setbacks and policy dilutions across major European and global markets.
  5. supply chain economics remain highly exposed to component availability and geopolitical trade tensions that pressure gross margin structures.
  6. cyclicality is highly pronounced in the Recycling segment with orders experiencing a prolonged multi-year standstill due to low virgin plastic prices.
  7. macroeconomic pressures like elevated interest rates and US import tariffs continue to delay customer capital investment decisions globally.
