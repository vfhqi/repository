  • Sub: evidence — EMA has approved multiple generic DPI substitutes for Budesonide/Formoterol combinations; Orion has not demonstrated pricing power maintenance in the face of generic entry at the documented level bulls claim

  • Bear rebuttal #2: Phase II/III binary risk is systematically underweighted by ORNBV bulls; seltorexant is a partnered asset (Johnson & Johnson/Janssen) where Orion is a royalty/milestone recipient, not the driver; ORM-6151 (Parkinson's) is in early Phase II with no efficacy data public; levocetirizine is a reformulation of a well-genericised molecule
  • Sub: the partnership structure for seltorexant means Orion has limited control over development timelines, trial design, or commercialisation decisions

  • Sub: base rate evidence — Phase II-to-approval success rate in CNS indications (Parkinson's: ORM-6151) is approximately 10-15% historically; option value should be discounted accordingly

  • Sub: management's defence (capital efficiency in R&D) requires sustained pipeline success that the existing track record does not fully validate
