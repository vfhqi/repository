EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (BLUF)

The materiality of change assessment concludes that Naturgy has executed a governance and ownership transformation of high strategic significance by resolving multi-year shareholder misalignment, but the operational and financial transformation remains incomplete and contingent. The base case depends on highly probable self-help efficiency gains and Spanish regulatory rate step-ups delivering low-single-digit EPS growth, while the bull case requires successful balance sheet deployment into hard-currency electricity assets that management has not yet demonstrated the capability or willingness to execute at scale. The risk-reward setup favors patient capital willing to capture the certain near-term operational improvements while retaining optionality on inorganic acceleration, but lacks the visibility and conviction to support aggressive positioning ahead of concrete M&A announcements. Activist presence has successfully transitioned from disruptive break-up pressure to supportive industrial ownership, removing a persistent overhang but also eliminating the external forcing function that historically drove strategic urgency.
