Financial Performance & Earnings Delivery WH Smith's financial performance over the Last Twelve Months ending February 28, 2026, reflects a period of resilient top-line expansion that has been significantly offset by profitability headwinds and margin compression. While the Group continues to benefit from long-term structural tailwinds in global travel, near-term earnings delivery has been restricted by geopolitical uncertainty, sustained cost inflation, and planned disruptions from a major store development program.

Top-Line Growth and Revenue Dynamics

The Group has maintained positive sales momentum, driven by its ongoing transition toward higher-yielding Travel Essentials. For the first half of fiscal 2026 (H1 2026), total Group revenue increased by 5% year-over-year to £748 million. This followed a strong close to FY 2025, where total group revenue reached £1.55 billion, an increase of 5% compared to the prior year.

On a like-for-like basis, Group revenue grew by 2% in H1 2026. This marks a deceleration from the 5% LFL growth achieved across all divisions in FY 2025. The moderation in LFL performance is largely attributable to the UK division, which reported flat LFL revenue growth in the first seven weeks of the second half of 2026. The Air segment, which accounts for over 70% of total revenue, saw total revenue increase by only 1% and LFL revenue up 2% in H1 2026. Management noted that this restricted top-line growth was expected due to temporary store closures required for the division's largest-ever store development program across all Heathrow Airport terminals.

Profitability and Margin Compression

Despite revenue gains, WH Smith experienced a material contraction in profitability metrics during the LTM period. Total LTM Headline EBITDA for the 12 months ending February 28, 2026, was £173 million. In H1 2026 specifically, Headline EBITDA fell to £48 million, down from £62 million in the restated prior-year period.

Headline Group trading profit for H1 2026 declined to £32 million, compared to £47 million in the first half of 2025. This decline was visible across multiple reporting segments, with the UK dropping by £6 million to £34 million, and North America decreasing by £3 million to £2 million. Furthermore, Headline Group profit before tax and non-underlying items plummeted by £18 million year-over-year, landing at just £3 million for H1 2026. Group loss before tax, including non-underlying items, widened to £25 million in the period.

The contraction in operating profit heavily impacted earnings delivery. For H1 2026, Headline diluted EPS before non-underlying items fell into negative territory at (0.8) pence, a stark contrast to the 11.5 pence generated in the same period last year. On a statutory basis, basic and diluted loss per share for continuing operations was reported at (20.0) pence for the half-year. This represents a significant deterioration from the end of FY 2025, where Headline diluted earnings per share before non-underlying items stood at a robust 43.4 pence.

Macroeconomic Factors and Passenger Dynamics

Margin delivery and overall financial performance have been heavily influenced by several external and macroeconomic challenges.

Geopolitical Disruption and Passenger Volumes: The conflict in the Middle East has created a highly uncertain environment for global travel. Disruption to flight schedules and weaker consumer confidence have negatively impacted passenger numbers, particularly softening the UK Air segment. Management has adopted a cautious outlook for the balance of the year, assuming no immediate improvement in consumer sentiment and warning that much will depend on the stability of jet fuel supplies during the peak summer trading period.

Inflationary Headwinds: The Group continues to battle sustained inflationary pressures across its operations. Management cited cost inflation of 4% to 5% across most major cost lines, particularly impacting the UK business. While WH Smith successfully delivered substantial operational efficiencies in FY 2025, the annualized cost benefits of approximately £9 million are insufficient to fully offset the ongoing inflation headwinds expected in FY 2026.

Labor and Operational Costs: In addition to general inflation, margin compression in regions like North America was driven by increased costs associated with new logistics setups and the annualization of structural labor cost increases. To combat these pressures, the Group is enforcing tighter cost controls and optimizing its estate to protect medium-term profitability.

Segmental Performance Analysis

WH Smith's fundamental delivery across its three primary reporting segments highlights a divergence between resilient top-line growth and the localized pressures impacting profitability. The Group has increasingly leaned on its Travel Essentials format to drive revenue, while actively restructuring underperforming channels and regions to protect long-term margins.

United Kingdom: Core Market Resilience and Investment Disruption

The UK remains WH Smith's largest and most profitable division, though near-term earnings have been restrained by strategic investments and inflation. For the first half of fiscal 2026, total UK revenue reached £392 million, representing a 2% increase on both a total and like-for-like basis. However, Headline trading profit in the UK declined to £34 million, down from £40 million in the prior-year period. This margin compression was primarily driven by expected disruptions from the division's largest-ever store development program and ongoing inflationary headwinds.

Performance across the UK's distinct channels varied significantly:

Air: The Air channel reported a modest 1% total revenue increase and a 2% LFL increase in H1 2026. Growth was artificially suppressed by temporary store closures required to roll out the new "one-stop-shop" format across all Heathrow Airport terminals. This flagship format combines health and beauty, food-to-go, and travel essentials to drive higher average transaction values.

Hospitals: The Hospitals channel delivered the strongest fundamental growth in the UK estate, with total revenue rising by 8% and LFL revenue up 4% in the first half. Management attributes this outperformance to a flexible multi-format approach that incorporates partnerships with M&S Food and Costa Coffee, alongside the rollout of the proprietary Smith's Family Kitchen café concept.

Rail: The Rail segment continued to battle a challenging consumer environment, posting a 1% increase in total revenue but a 2% decline on an LFL basis. To counter these headwinds, the Group recently opened a flagship one-stop-shop at London Bridge station to maximize convenience and basket sizes for commuters.

In terms of footprint, the UK division operated 589 stores as of February 28, 2026. During the half-year, the Group opened 7 new stores while closing 11, aligning with the broader strategy to optimize the estate and exit less lucrative locations.

North America: Travel Essentials Boom vs. Format Rationalization

The North American division presents a mixed narrative, with exceptional growth in the core business offset by the structural decay of specific legacy formats. Total revenue in NA increased by 5% to £204 million in H1 2026, which translates to a 10% gain on a constant currency basis. Headline trading profit, however, fell to £2 million from £5 million in the prior year, hampered by new logistics setups and annualized structural labor cost increases.

The segmental performance within North America is starkly divided by store format:

Travel Essentials: This segment now accounts for over 55% of NA revenue and is the division's undisputed growth engine. In H1 2026, Travel Essentials revenue surged 22% on a constant currency basis, with LFL sales growing 6%. These stores operate with an attractive Headline trading profit margin of approximately 10%.

InMotion: The technology accessories brand experienced a 4% LFL revenue decline during the first half. Due to shifting consumer behaviors, WH Smith is severely restricting new InMotion openings to strategically important tender packages only. Management expects the InMotion estate to contract by 20% to 30% over time, eventually falling below 100 stores.

Resorts: The Resorts channel suffered a 6% LFL revenue decline, driven heavily by reduced visitor traffic in Las Vegas. In response, the Group has initiated a controlled exit from loss-making fashion and specialty stores, closing 3 fashion locations in the period with more planned before year-end.

The North American footprint remained flat overall during the half-year, as the opening of 18 new stores—including new formats in Albuquerque and Portland—was perfectly offset by 18 store closures.

Rest of the World : Strategic Pivot to Franchise Operations

The Rest of the World and Other division experienced robust top-line growth but struggled with profitability constraints in specific markets. ROW revenue grew by 8% on a constant currency basis in H1 2026, with LFL revenue advancing by a strong 6%. Despite this, the division swung to a Headline trading loss of £4 million, down from a £2 million profit in the prior year, primarily due to staff and logistics inflation in underperforming regions.

To rebuild margins, WH Smith is actively restructuring its ROW footprint. The Group closed four uneconomic stores at Dusseldorf airport and entirely exited its operations in Norway. Conversely, targeted direct investments are being channeled into high-performing core markets such as Ireland, Spain, and Australia, where the brand has established scale. For future international expansion, management is shifting aggressively toward a less capital-intensive franchise model, recently opening new franchise locations in Saudi Arabia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. During H1 2026, the ROW division opened 11 stores and closed 12, leaving an active estate of 324 locations.

Capital Allocation, Cash Flow, and Balance Sheet

WH Smith has materially shifted its near-term financial strategy to prioritize balance sheet stability and liquidity preservation amid ongoing macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainties. Management's rebalanced capital allocation framework focuses strictly on debt reduction, disciplined capital expenditure, and tighter cash controls.

Rebalanced Capital Allocation and Dividend Suspension

The most significant change to the capital allocation policy is the immediate suspension of the shareholder dividend. This prudent decision was taken by the Board as a first step to ensure the right foundations remain in place to support long-term value creation and protect the business against weaker consumer confidence. The Group has stated it will look to reinstate returns to shareholders only when excess cash becomes available. Historically, the company had returned surplus capital via a 2.5x dividend cover policy and recently completed a £50 million share buyback program during the prior fiscal year.

Cash Flow Generation and Working Capital

In the first half of fiscal 2026, WH Smith reported a free cash outflow of £61 million, a slight improvement from the £72 million outflow in the prior-year period. Despite the headline operating profit contractions, the underlying business demonstrated its cash-generative nature by delivering £48 million in Headline EBITDA for the half-year. The cash flow profile was heavily impacted by an expected working capital outflow of £54 million. This working capital drag was primarily attributed to the seasonality of the travel retail business, the setup costs associated with new store openings, and a one-off payables timing headwind linked to a large franchisor partner at the end of the previous year.

To support future growth and protect existing business assets, the Group spent £50 million on capital expenditure during H1 2026. This capital was actively deployed toward opening 36 new stores globally and funding the transformational one-stop-shop developments at Heathrow. Looking ahead, management anticipates full-year FY26 capex to total approximately £90 million.

Balance Sheet Health and Leverage Multiples

At the close of H1 2026 (February 28, 2026), Headline net debt stood at £496 million. This represents an increase from the £390 million reported at the end of August 2025, driven largely by the seasonal working capital outflow and continuing investments in the North American store estate. Consequently, the Group's leverage multiple rose to 2.9x.

Refinancing Activities and Debt Maturity

The Group maintains access to approximately £550 million of total liquidity. To further protect the balance sheet, WH Smith recently executed comprehensive refinancing activities designed to smooth and extend the maturity profile of its debt stack. In March 2026, the company fully drew down £200 million in committed US Private Placement notes. Concurrently, the Group cancelled its £200 million 12-month syndicated term loan facility.

Additionally, the company exercised the first of two one-year extension options on an existing £120 million term loan, pushing its maturity out to March 2029. This term loan is expected to be drawn down ahead of the scheduled repayment of the Group's £327 million convertible bonds, which mature in May 2026. The Group also holds a revolving credit facility maturing in June 2030, ensuring robust operational liquidity for the medium term.

Strategic Initiatives and Future Outlook

Following the divestiture of its High Street and Funky Pigeon businesses, WH Smith has solidified its strategic positioning as a pure-play global travel retailer. Management's forward-looking framework is now strictly anchored around five key pillars: increasing the mix of Travel Essentials, deploying capital with rigorous discipline, strengthening the operating model, rationalizing low-margin space, and exiting loss-making locations.

The "One-Stop-Shop" Rollout Strategy

The cornerstone of the Group's organic growth strategy is the accelerated rollout of its "one-stop-shop" format. This highly scalable model transitions stores away from traditional news, books, and convenience retailing toward a comprehensive travel essentials proposition. By integrating health and beauty ranges, technology accessories, and the proprietary Smith's Family Kitchen food-to-go brand under a single roof, the format significantly drives average transaction value and space productivity. The compelling economics of this strategy are evidenced by the Birmingham Airport flagship store, which delivered a 20% increase in ATV and a 30% uplift in sales per square foot following its refit.

North American Portfolio Optimization

In North America, strategic initiatives are heavily focused on remediation and aggressive portfolio rationalization. The Group aims to increase the proportion of highly profitable Travel Essentials stores to over 70% of the division's revenue mix in the medium term, up from the current 55%. Conversely, management is initiating a controlled exit of its unprofitable Resorts fashion and specialty stores, aiming to largely complete the closure of fashion locations within the current year. Additionally, the InMotion electronics portfolio is undergoing an active review, with future openings strictly limited to strategically important tender packages. As a result of shifting consumer behaviors and landlord redevelopments, the InMotion estate is expected to contract by 20% to 30%, eventually falling below 100 locations over the medium term.

International Franchise Pivot

For the Rest of the World division, WH Smith is pivoting toward a less capital-intensive franchise-led model to drive future international expansion. Direct capital investments will be exclusively channeled into existing core markets where the Group already possesses operational scale, such as Australia, Ireland, and Spain. In parallel, management is actively exiting sub-scale or uneconomic markets to protect divisional profitability. This franchise-oriented approach allows the Group to partner with experienced local operators, mitigating operational complexity while accelerating the delivery of stronger capital returns.

Forward-Looking Guidance and Medium-Term Targets

Looking ahead to the balance of fiscal 2026, management has adopted a cautious stance due to geopolitical uncertainties in the Middle East and their potential impact on consumer confidence and passenger volumes. Factoring in these headwinds, the Group expects to deliver FY26 Headline Group profit before tax and non-underlying items in the range of £90 million to £105 million.

On a segmental basis, FY26 planning assumptions project total revenue growth of 1% to 3% in the UK, 6% to 8% in North America, and 2% to 4% in ROW. Headline trading profit margins for FY26 are targeted at 13% to 14% for the UK, 7% for North America, and 4% for the ROW division. To optimize the overall estate quality and support these margin targets, WH Smith anticipates closing approximately 50 to 60 underperforming stores across the Group while simultaneously opening an equivalent number of new, higher-yielding locations during the year.
