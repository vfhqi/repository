§3 — Guidance Pattern (Sandbagging vs Over-Promising)

  • Pattern verdict: TIT-IT management is a consistent over-promiser on FCF and EBITDA; there is no credible sandbagging pattern on the metrics that matter most to the investment thesis [#J]

  • Over-promiser signal — FCF: Four consecutive years of guided FCF positivity vs. actual FCF negativity constitutes a structural optimism bias, not cyclical variance; this is qualitatively different from a single miss or a macro-event miss [#A]

  • Sub: European telco precedent for 4-year FCF miss streaks: Vodafone hit a 2-year FCF miss streak in FY20-21 but recovered; TIT-IT's streak is longer and more consistent [#A]

  • Over-promiser signal — EBITDA: EBITDA guidance has been set at or above eventual outturn in 3 of 4 years; average guided margin ~30% vs. average delivered ~28-29% EBITDA-AL, a persistent 100-200bps optimism gap [#A]

  • Mixed pattern — CapEx: CapEx guidance is closer to accurate (within ±7% in recent years) and showed a minor beat in FY23; however, FY20 and FY22 CapEx overshoots of +6-7% contributed materially to FCF miss [#A]

  • Honest tracker — revenue: Revenue guidance, while directionally modest, is the most accurate metric on a relative basis; FY21 was essentially on-target after the FY20 reset; this is the one dimension where management does not appear structurally biased [#A]

  • Pattern shift: Management revised guidance methodology in FY23/FY24 by (1) explicitly excluding restructuring from EBITDA guidance and (2) defining equity FCF more narrowly post-NetCo to reduce the scope for miss; these changes may improve apparent accuracy without improving underlying delivery [#A]

  • Sub: This is a methodology-masking behaviour rather than operational improvement; should be treated as a yellow flag [#A]

  • Beat consistency: The only consistent partial beat is CapEx in the post-NetCo period, a structural artefact of the asset disposal rather than a sandbagging discipline [#A]

  • Miss consistency: EBITDA misses are consistent in direction (guided too high) and moderate in magnitude (-2.6% to -13%); FCF misses are consistent in direction and large in magnitude [#A]

  • ⚡ RARE: Guidance redefinition frequency: TIT-IT management has materially redefined what "FCF" or "EBITDA" means in guidance at least twice in four years (FY20 to FY24); among European Tier-1 telecoms, Deutsche Telekom and Orange have maintained consistent metric definitions across the same period — TIT-IT's redefinition cadence is a top-quartile negative outlier in definitional stability [#A] [Cross-ref: §2 + §7]

  • Improving accuracy: CapEx guidance has improved post-FY22; EBITDA guidance appears tighter in FY23; but the FCF definition change in FY23/24 makes improvement claims unreliable [#A]

  • Deteriorating accuracy: The structural ARPU decline, which management each year guides as "stabilising," has not stabilised; this creates a recurring revenue guidance optimism that flows through to EBITDA and FCF misses [#A]

  • Sceptical bullet: The bearish read is that apparent pattern improvement in FY23 (minor EBITDA beat, CapEx beat) was methodology-driven; if management again guides FCF positivity in FY24-26 using a redefined metric, apparent accuracy may improve while underlying cash generation remains poor [#J]

§6 — Key Assumptions Behind Guidance

  • Assumption verdict: The guidance assumption stack is fragile at multiple simultaneous stress points; ARPU stabilisation, MSA income timing, Galileo cost savings, and financial charge trajectory are all independently uncertain and partially correlated [#J]

  • Revenue assumption — pricing: Management assumes Italian consumer mobile ARPU stabilises in FY24 and turns modestly positive by FY25-26; this requires the Italian mobile market to end a 4+ year pricing war that has no visible structural catalyst [#A]

  • Sub: Italian mobile ARPU declined at ~3-5% annually FY19-FY23; European peers that have successfully stabilised ARPU (e.g., Deutsche Telekom in Germany) did so via market consolidation (3+4 merger in 2022) — no equivalent structural change is planned in Italy [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — market: Fixed broadband market assumed to show continued FTTH take-up acceleration, with TIM maintaining 35-40% retail share of FTTH lines; this is contested given Open Fiber's aggressive wholesale pricing [#A]

  • Revenue assumption — M&A: No further M&A assumed in domestic guidance; Brazil (TIM Brasil) excluded; any incremental M&A activity (enterprise, cloud) would be additive but not guided [#A]

  • Margin assumption — cost: Project Galileo cost transformation (headcount reduction of ~4,000-5,000 FTEs) assumed to deliver €600-700m of run-rate annual savings by FY26; approximately 40% of savings are assumed to be realised by FY24 [#A]

  • Sub: Galileo has achieved approximately 60-65% of target headcount reductions as of Q1-FY24; remaining reductions face higher union negotiation complexity in Italy than initial plan assumed [AS-Bernstein] [#A]

  • Margin assumption — leverage: EBITDA-to-revenue leverage assumed to expand through fixed-cost dilution as revenue stabilises; if revenue continues to decline, operating leverage works in reverse [#A]

  • Margin assumption — MSA: MSA income from NetCo assumed at approximately €1.5-1.7bn annually in FY24, rising modestly to €1.8bn by FY26; this is the single largest new income line in the post-NetCo P&L [#A]

  • Sub: MSA unit economics and escalation mechanisms are not fully disclosed; TIM has stated MSA is CPI-linked, but the interaction between MSA pricing and TIM's own wholesale costs to NetCo creates a partial offset that management's guidance may understate [AS-JPMorgan] [#A]

  • EPS assumption — capital structure: Net debt post-NetCo approximately €8-9bn (domestic, post-proceeds); management assumes no further refinancing costs above currently guided interest expense of approximately €600-700m annually [#A]

  • Sub: Debt maturities in 2025-2027 could require refinancing at materially higher spreads if Italian sovereign spreads widen; every +50bps on €8bn net debt = ~€40m incremental annual interest cost [#A]

  • EPS assumption — tax: Italy's corporate tax rate is approximately 24% IRES + 3.9% IRAP; management's guided effective tax rate incorporates Italian patent box and R&D incentives; any legislative change would be incremental [#A]

  • Macro assumption — GDP: Italy GDP growth assumed ~0.5-1.0% annually FY24-26; below-forecast GDP would reduce enterprise and SME IT spend but TIM's consumer exposure dominates [#A]

  • Macro assumption — currency: Domestic guidance is EUR-denominated; no currency sensitivity in core guidance [#A]

§7 — Credibility Assessment

  • Credibility verdict: Overall guidance credibility is MEDIUM-LOW; FCF and EBITDA guidance lack credibility based on L4-period track record; revenue guidance is MEDIUM credibility; the investment case credibility rests more on asset/debt restructuring logic than on management's operational guidance delivery [#J]

  • Credibility — verbal verdict: MEDIUM-LOW — management consistently overestimates FCF improvement speed and underestimates structural competitive headwinds to ARPU; the NetCo restructuring is a genuine strategic improvement but guidance built on its back contains unverifiable assumptions [#J]

  • Red flag — recent miss: FCF missed guidance in all four of FY20-FY23; the miss in FY23 (guided positive, delivered negative) is particularly damaging as it was set after two prior misses and management claimed greater confidence in FY23 delivery [#A]

  • Red flag — aggressive assumption: The FY24-26 FCF ramp from ~€0 to €1.0-1.5bn in 2 years is an aggressive embedded trajectory with no comparable European telco precedent at this pace without large asset sales [#A]

  • Red flag — execution risk: Project Galileo headcount reduction programme is at approximately 60-65% completion but facing increasing union friction in Italy; cost savings may be delayed into FY25-26, creating FY24 EBITDA risk [AS-Bernstein] [#A]

  • Red flag — macro risk: Italian mobile pricing competition shows no structural signs of abating; Iliad Italy and WindTre continue aggressive SIM-only discounting; ARPU stabilisation assumption is not supported by competitive dynamics data [AS-Exane] [#A]

  • Green flag — recent beat: FY23 EBITDA minor beat (+2.6%) is the first EBITDA beat in four years and may signal cost discipline improving; CapEx beat in FY23 is also a positive signal on capital efficiency [#A]
